Windows Store Sees 1.7 Million Average Daily Downloads, Grows Nearly 40% in 4 Months

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 19:25:30

According to information provided by Microsoft to developers, the Windows Store selling Windows 8 and Windows 8.1 apps had a daily average download volume (including both free and paid apps) of around 1.7 million in October, representing a 38.56% increase from June.

Data provided by Microsoft on its developer portal indicates that throughout October, the total downloads for both free and paid apps amounted to approximately 51.2 million. For Microsoft, this figure represents significant growth; however, compared to its main competitor Apple, Microsoft is still far behind.

In this dataset, Microsoft categorized Windows Store app downloads by category, subcategory, whether they were paid or free, as well as by country and gender. TechCrunch compared data from January, February, June, and October, reasoning that January is the peak season for app downloads (since it follows the holiday shopping season, during which PC sales tend to increase significantly); February's data measures performance after the holiday season; June provides insights into summer app download trends; and October is the most recent full month with available data.

Below are the data comparisons listed by TechCrunch:

| October | June | February | January |

|---------|------|----------|---------|

| Paid App Downloads: 1,355,593 | 1,036,456 | 1,046,896 | 1,283,975 |

| Free App Downloads: 49,796,691 | 35,880,344 | 34,604,172 | 41,867,366 |

| Total Downloads: 51,152,284 | 36,916,800 | 35,651,068 | 43,151,341 |

| Daily Average Downloads: 1,705,076.133 | 1,230,560 | 1,188,368.933 | 1,438,378.033 |

From this chart, it can be seen that after the holiday shopping season, the download volumes showed strong resilience compared to the decline in February; and throughout the summer, the download volumes remained relatively consistent.

TechCrunch pointed out that comparing each app category (free and paid), one can conclude that the app download performance on Windows Store has improved recently. Although there are significant gaps between different app categories and their popularity, overall, over the past few months (especially in October), Windows Store's app downloads have shown a positive growth trend.

Microsoft recently claimed that during the holiday shopping season, app downloads and developer revenue increased, and the above chart confirms this.

Comparison with Apple OS X

TechCrunch noted that it is difficult to compare app download volumes from the Windows Store with those from Apple’s Mac Store because the two platforms do not offer direct comparability in this data. The Windows Store covers various hardware devices including tablets, laptops, and desktops, spans across Windows 8 and Windows 8.1 operating systems, and controls 9.25% of the PC market share (by active installed base). In contrast, all versions of OS X combined hold a market share of 7.23%.

However, Windows 8.X (referring to Windows 8 and Windows 8.1) has broader coverage than OS X since the latter is not an operating system used on tablets. Therefore, although one might claim that Windows 8.X surpasses Apple's Mac Store, the evidence supporting such a claim based solely on progress made in the tablet market is insufficient.

Regarding relevant information for the OS X platform, TechCrunch provided the following data:

- On June 10, 2013: App Store downloads reached 5 billion;

- On October 22, 2013: App Store downloads reached 6 billion;

- Growth from June to October: 1 billion;

- Days between June and October data: 134 days;

- Daily average downloads: 74,626,865.

By comparing these figures with Windows Store download data, it can be observed that Apple’s daily app downloads are equivalent to 43.8 times that of Microsoft. Moreover, since this comparison uses Microsoft's best-performing month historically against Apple's average over the last few months, it is slightly unfair to Apple.

Techcrunch pointed out that while there is currently no download data for Windows Phone apps and a lack of related information for the Mac OS X platform, making precise comparisons between total app downloads for Microsoft and Apple difficult, it can still be said that Windows is a platform in its early stages. Assuming Windows Phone app downloads also continue to grow and reach around 250 million in October, then the monthly download volume for the entire Windows ecosystem would reach 300 million, or approximately 10 million daily downloads, which is still less than one-seventh of Apple's App Store.
