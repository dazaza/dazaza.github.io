Initial Strategy for the Left Area of Blackdale in X360 "The Last Remnant"

by lk0gamec on 2009-07-08 09:33:15

After completing the story mission "Blackdale," you can enter the area to the left of Blackdale. The monsters there are quite tough, causing trouble for many players. However, after some research last night, I discovered that it's entirely possible to conquer this area early on — the key lies in team composition and formation.

Pre-battle status confirmation:

- The maximum number of parties (teams) is 3.

- The maximum number of units that can participate in battle is 9.

- The maximum number of leaders that can participate in battle is 4.

- You can hire up to 10 Asylum soldiers (hire them in the central area).

- The maximum number of party leaders (including mercenaries) is 10.

Here, the term "leader" refers to the main character, mercenaries, and companions from the storyline. At the point when you can access this area, only 4 people can participate in battles at most. However, you've probably noticed that the maximum number of participants is 9. So where do the remaining 5 come from? You need to hire soldiers, which you can do in the central area. These soldiers are generic characters. For this mission, you should hire at least 5 soldiers (choose based on their abilities; mages should have high intelligence, while warriors should have high dexterity and strength). It's recommended to hire 2 mages and 3 warriors.

P.S.: We still don't know the exact role of each character's fourth status ability, such as "favor" or "big eater"...

Although you can split into 3 groups for battle, each group averages only 3 members with HP not exceeding 1000. Entering the area to the left of Blackdale will likely result in a quick death. Therefore, we'll split into 2 groups: each group will have 2 leader characters. One group will consist of the main character and 3 hired soldiers, while the other group will consist of 2 hired soldiers. Each group should have at least 1200+ HP (even so, it's normal for one group to die during the battle).
