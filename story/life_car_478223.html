Tips for Choosing the Right Tint for Your Car! Make Your Car Look Brand New!

When car owners decide to tint their cars, they should pay attention to the following points:

1. Find a decoration company with an enclosed workshop because dust and sand are the biggest enemies of tinting. Street-side operations find it hard to maintain a clean environment.

2. Choosing branded window films from reputable stores can reduce the risk of buying inferior or counterfeit products.

3. Ask the store staff and observe if the backside of the film has an anti-counterfeiting mark. Regular brand-name films usually have such marks on the back. If possible, ask the shopkeeper for a sample piece. Peel off the backing film to expose the adhesive layer, then rub it on the ground. If discoloration or scratches appear, it's likely a fake film.

4. Request that the front and rear windshields be installed without cutting, ensuring the film is applied in one piece. Otherwise, it will lose its anti-explosion properties and affect aesthetics.

5. After the entire car is tinted, the owner should sit inside the car and check if the color is uniform, there are no bubbles, and there aren't too many particles. Check visibility from various angles outside the car.

6. Test the film personally. Most auto decoration shops have a box with glass and infrared light sources inside. The film is applied on the glass to simulate a sunlit car cabin environment. High-quality films can significantly block heat radiation, which can be easily felt by hand.

7. Good quality films typically last 5 to 6 years under normal use. Some brands offer warranty cards, so remember to request one from the seller. For films without warranty cards, keep all receipts and invoices for future reference.

Comprehensive Guide to Selecting, Installing, and Maintaining Car Window Films

The car window film industry is mixed with good and bad products, so car owners must be cautious when choosing a film for their vehicles. Attention must be paid at every stage: selecting, installing, and post-installation care, to avoid being scammed and ensure quality installation.

1. Selecting the Film: Observation, Smell, Inquiry, and Diagnosis Are Essential

Currently, explosion-proof films are the best choice. They offer excellent transparency regardless of color depth, providing clear views even at night or during rainy weather. These films are colored using special coating techniques and combined with metal mesh for heat insulation, achieving up to 80-90% heat and UV protection. When selecting car films, consider clarity and transparency first. Regardless of the color depth, transparency should be high, avoiding foggy appearances. Side window films, especially those for the front passenger windows, should ideally have over 85% light transmittance. This way, side window films don’t require holes and won’t obstruct vision. At night, these films help reduce glare from headlights reflected on the rearview mirror, making driving more comfortable. Additionally, they provide good visibility during rain, reversing, or turning around.

How to Differentiate Between High-Quality and Low-Quality Films?

Feel it with your hands. High-quality films feel thick and smooth, while inferior ones are soft and thin, lacking sufficient flexibility and prone to wrinkling. Smell it with your nose. Inferior films often have a strong odor due to high levels of benzene in the adhesive, which can seriously harm the health of the driver. High-quality films offer up to 90% clarity, with excellent transparency regardless of color depth. On the other hand, low-quality films rely on ordinary dyeing processes for heat resistance, resulting in darker colors and a foggy appearance when viewed from inside the car. Additionally, always check the manufacturer’s warranty card when selecting a film. Reputable brands have anti-counterfeiting marks.

2. Installation: Evaluate the Software and Hardware Conditions of the Installation Shop

Choosing the right film is important, but selecting the right shop is equally crucial. The technology and conditions required for proper installation are very high. Factors like dust-free environments, specialized equipment, standardized procedures, and skilled technicians are essential. Therefore, avoid street-side small shops for installation.

When choosing an installation shop, start by checking the hardware: Films are sensitive to dust and sand, so look for a shop with an enclosed workshop. Then check the software: Verify if the shop has valid brand authorization certificates and whether the authorization period has expired. Also, check the training certificates of the installation personnel.

Moreover, the skill level of the installer is critical. The front and rear windshields of a car, due to their large size and curvature, test the limits of film application craftsmanship. It is recommended to insist on having senior technicians perform the work. After the entire car is tinted, the owner should sit inside and check if the color is uniform, there are no bubbles or particles, and verify visibility from various angles.

Good quality films generally last 5 to 6 years under normal use. Remember to obtain the warranty card and all relevant invoices and receipts to address any issues with the seller.

3. Post-Installation Care: Prevent Ruining Previous Efforts

After completing the car tinting, follow these steps:

- Keep the side windows closed for 24 hours in summer and 48 hours in winter.

- Avoid using nails or sharp objects to lift the edges of the film to prevent dirt from entering.

- If bubbles form, visit a professional car beauty and maintenance shop within 24 hours for handling.

- Do not stick adhesive labels directly onto the film.

- After 2-3 weeks, you can clean the car. Use lint-free cloths or soft items dipped in soapy water to clean. Avoid including sand or sharp particles to prevent scratching the film surface.

- Do not clean with ammonia-based liquids.

- If there is a misty water vapor between the film and the glass, it will naturally dry and disappear within 2-3 weeks.
