A Brief Analysis of Suning Appliances

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 12:10:30

"Come to Suning, bring happiness home" - it was this slogan that drew my attention to Suning Appliances. Yesterday, I bought another 3,100 shares of the company. This appliance retail giant once drove the entire capital market wild, and its popularity was entirely justified.

By the end of 2010, six years after its IPO, Suning's stock price had increased 37 times, with an average annual compound growth rate of 84%. However, Suning's stock price increase has been supported by solid company performance. In 2004, the first year after Suning went public, its net profit was 1.81 billion yuan. The company estimated that its net profit would reach 4 billion yuan in 2010, representing a compound annual growth rate of 75%, almost in line with the stock price increase.

These figures illustrate an often overlooked investment truth: good growth companies need long-term high-speed growth to demonstrate their characteristics, not just data from half a year or one year used to mislead people.

I admit that due to the correlation between home appliance sales and real estate sales, as well as the diversion of online shopping channels, Suning has faced rare skepticism in recent years. Is the company’s high growth still intact? This is the crux of the issue.

Currently, Suning has over 1,300 stores, and its sales are expected to reach 75 billion yuan in 2010. China's total home appliance sales volume in 2010 is estimated to be between 950 billion yuan and 1 trillion yuan. If it grows at an annual rate of 10%, conservatively estimated, it will exceed 4 trillion yuan in ten years. If Suning captures 15% of the market, its sales will reach 600 billion yuan, indicating potential for more than seven times growth.

From a macro perspective, China's GDP growth remains above 8% annually. According to data from the Ministry of Commerce, the home appliance industry is growing at an annual rate of 16%. Even with government regulation of the real estate market, the growth rate of home appliances should still exceed 10%. Currently, Suning's share in the home appliance market is less than 10%, but in the future, its market share could reach over 15%, possibly even higher.

In the short term, Suning's physical stores will continue to expand rapidly, with plans to open 370 new stores of various types in 2011. More importantly, Suning is directly addressing the online shopping market. In its recently announced 2011 business strategy, Suning outlined strategic breakthroughs in online sales.

Suning will comprehensively adjust its YiGou e-commerce platform, establishing an independent e-commerce operation system controlled by the listed company, operating in a corporate manner. Based on sharing the backend service system of its physical network, the e-commerce system will develop in parallel with the store system. Suning YiGou aims to quadruple its sales in 2011, reaching 8 billion yuan, with some predicting this figure will reach 30 billion yuan in three years.

Moreover, the essence of the retail industry is high-tech logistics. Suning already possesses large-scale warehousing and distribution capabilities, along with a relatively mature backend control system, giving it a late-mover advantage compared to pure online retailers. Therefore, online sales will become Suning's second engine for future performance growth and a guarantee for its success.

The American home appliance retailer Best Buy saw its stock price rise 100 times in the last decade of the 20th century, while Walmart's stock price increased 500 times in the 1980s and 1990s. Currently, Suning's stock price is around 14 yuan, corresponding to a predicted P/E ratio of only 18 times for 2011. Are we being overly pessimistic about the growth and valuation of such a great company?
