Introduction:

Celine Dion often releases live albums, including various English and French live albums. However, this one is different. This is a live album from her massive "A New Day..." concert in Las Vegas. After its release, the live album sold over 58,000 copies in its first week and ranked tenth on the US Billboard 200 chart, setting a new record for the Canadian diva's live albums in the United States.

Choosing to perform in Las Vegas was an adventurous decision. Deciding to perform there continuously for 600 shows spanning three years was even more of a feat. Probably only someone like Celine Dion, a superstar of today's pop music scene, could have such a bold idea. With an audience of 4,000 people per show and five performances each week, large-scale scenes and multimedia stage settings over a period of three years, these super performances are not just a test of singing ability but also of physical strength, perseverance, and spirit.

For a star with immense talent like Celine Dion, holding concerts isn't an issue. Selecting over a dozen singles from the already ongoing "New Day" concert series to compile into a live album is a piece of cake. Being able to propose such a grand plan means that Celine Dion must be very confident and sure of herself. Besides the large recording engineering team, stage supervisors, backup dancers, and typical instrumental accompaniment teams, the lineup for this grand concert also includes a dedicated choir and the Stockholm Orchestra. Through such a long-lasting performance, Celine Dion's expertise in singing will be fully displayed.

This is an impressive live album born out of a marathon concert. On this live album, Celine Dion sings classic songs like "My Heart Will Go On," "It's All Coming Back to Me Now," "Because You Loved Me," as well as recent singles like "I Drove All Night." Of course, these are only part of Celine Dion's many classic songs. Although these may not necessarily be the songs fans want to hear most, they represent Celine Dion's brightest moments during the concert series that has been ongoing for over a year. Perhaps only at live performances or through live albums can fans truly experience Celine Dion's enchanting and ethereal voice. It can be said that this is a gift from Celine Dion to fans who cannot attend the live performances.

This album is not entirely composed of live singles; it also includes two previously unreleased studio singles, "You And I" and "Ain't Gonna Look the Other Way." Compared to the outstanding live performances, although these two singles are beautiful, they are slightly inferior compared to some of Celine Dion's classic singles.

The concert series, which has been ongoing for over a year, is clearly just beginning. In the nearly two years that follow, Celine Dion will continue to perform on that stage in Las Vegas. Perhaps soon, there will be a second or even third volume of the live album from this concert series. Having the capability ensures that the released live album will be substantial.

In 2007, she delivered a deeply touching performance during the three-year-long concert, deeply moving every fan both on and off the stage. The double-disc Blu-ray released in 2007 is a grand and generous gift to all fans. Fans of Celine Dion should not miss this celestial voice.

Behind-the-Scenes Production [Basic Information]

Celine Dion was born on March 30, 1968, in Quebec, Canada. She is a famous French and English female pop singer known as the Queen of Pop. As one of the best-selling female singers globally, Celine Dion has sold more than 200 million albums worldwide.

Her mother opened a small club when she was 40 years old, where the whole family gathered and sang folk songs every weekend. Celine entered the music world at the age of 12 and released her first French single at 15. Her debut English album "UNISON" launched her onto the global market, with the single "WHERE DOES MY HEART BEAT NOW" reaching fourth place in the U.S. charts.

She is a Grammy, Juno, and Oscar-winning pop singer who occasionally participates in songwriting. After her manager and later husband René Angélil mortgaged his property to support Celine's career, she became a teenage star in the French-speaking regions of Canada. She won the Yamaha World Song Festival competition held in Japan in 1982 and the Eurovision Song Contest in 1988, gaining significant recognition in parts of Asia and Europe. Her first English album "Unison," released by Sony in 1990, secured her a place in the English-speaking music markets.

Celine's music is influenced by various styles, including pop, rock, gospel, classical, etc. Similarly, she is renowned for her exceptional vocal techniques. Before announcing her temporary retirement from the entertainment industry in 1999, she released numerous hit songs in English and French, including "I’m Your Angel" and "My Heart Will Go On." In 2002, Celine Dion returned to the music scene with her new album "A New Day Has Come." In 2004, she won the Diamond Award at the World Music Awards, recognizing her as "the best-selling female artist of all time." At the end of 2007, Celine concluded her "A New Day...Live In Las Vegas" show at Caesar's Palace in Las Vegas and released the English album "Taking Chances," which quickly became a global bestseller.

[2003-2007 Residency in Las Vegas]

As early as 2002, Celine had announced a contract for 600 performances over three years, with weekly performances titled "A New Day" at Caesar's Palace in Las Vegas. This move was considered "the smartest decision made by artists in recent years" due to her declining new album sales. The idea came to her while watching Dragone's O during her hiatus. The concert officially began on March 25, 2003, on a specially designed stage accommodating 4,000 people. Designed by Franco Dragone, the show combined music, dance, and visual effects, with Celine performing her most popular songs amidst a large group of backup dancers and visual effects.

Reviewer Mike Weatherford believed that Celine wasn't as relaxed as she should have been, making it sometimes hard to find her among the vast stage and numerous dancers. Nonetheless, he still thought her stage presence had improved, and simple costumes and sets turned her concert into an entertaining performance. The show was widely accepted and praised by audiences despite the expensive tickets. Since 2003, almost every night's tickets were sold out. According to Pollstar, Celine sold 322,000 tickets in the first half of 2005, generating $43.9 million in box office revenue, ranking second on Billboard's six-month summary of box office rankings. By June 2005, out of 384 performances, 315 were sold out. Based on the concert's success, her contract was extended until 2007, creating over 700 sell-out performances. Specific compensation was undisclosed. Recordings from her live performances were compiled into an album for commercial release, achieving great success.

[2007: A New Beginning]

At the end of 2007, Celine ended her "A New Day...Live In Las Vegas" show at Caesar's Palace in Las Vegas and released the English album "Taking Chances," which quickly became a global bestseller, entering the TOP 12 in at least 13 countries and becoming NO.1 on the Billboard European Music Chart. On February 14, 2008, Valentine's Day, she started her 2008 "Taking Chances" world tour in Africa. On March 15, she arrived in Macau, China, and performed successfully in Shanghai on April 13, marking her first visit to China. In May 2008, Celine began her European tour.

On May 22, 2008, French President Nicolas Sarkozy held a ceremony at the Elysée Palace to award Celine Dion the Knight of the Legion of Honour medal. Accompanied by her mother, husband-manager, son, and other relatives and friends, Celine expressed her honor in receiving the knight's medal.

On August 29, 2008, the Canadian-born singer Celine Dion (40 years old) will sing in Japanese for the film "The Phantom of Yamatai Country" (directed by Masahiko Tsugawa, released on November 1), starring Yoko Yoshinaga (63 years old).
