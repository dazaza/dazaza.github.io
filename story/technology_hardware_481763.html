See how foreigners ensure drinking water safety

by jifugs2539 on 2011-08-26 09:41:52

The Germans drink tap water

Although the Germans claim "drink beer more than water", however, according to Shenzhen Nanshan District Dream Home water delivery telephone, data shows that what the Germans drink the most is still water. When the Germans are thirsty, they will casually take a glass of tap water, without heating or filtering, it's hygienic and safe.

Although there are more than 200 different brands of mineral water in Germany, the Federal Ministry of Health of Germany still advises consumers that drinking tap water is actually the safest choice. Because the Stuttgart Health Office once tested 1,171 different brands of mineral water produced by different manufacturers, and found that 6.3% of them had excessive bacteria problems, mainly caused by pollution during production and transportation.

But there are no such problems with Germany's tap water. Whether it is the water used for cooking in the kitchen, the water used for bathing in the bathroom, or the water used for flushing the toilet in the restroom, all are within the standard range of raw drinking water. However, the hot water tap water cannot be drunk directly because high copper content may occur during the heating and transmission process.

"Tap water can be drunk directly first thanks to strict source control," said Mr. Younail, a water health expert at the German Federal Ministry of Health, told reporters, the sources of tap water are divided into surface water and groundwater. Among them, surface water accounts for the majority, such as river, lake water etc., all belong to surface water. In order to ensure the safety of water sources, various places in Germany have established water source protection zones. For example, the water resources in the Berlin area come from the Berlin-Warsaw underground aquifer, which is an underground spring with abundant water volume and quite good water quality. Berlin also divides three levels of water source protection zones around the aquifer at different distances. The first-level protection zone within 10 meters around the water extraction point has the strictest requirements, prohibiting all polluting substances from seeping into the ground, violators will be fined heavily. These well-protected waters, after collection, must go through a series of treatments such as large-scale purification, sedimentation, filtration, disinfection, etc., before entering residents' homes. The German Federal Ministry of Health stipulates that water companies must issue water quality reports every year, and residents can request them by phone at any time. In densely populated large cities, water quality monitoring is not once a day, but once an hour.

Germany not only measures the pH value and bacterial content of tap water, but also measures the mineral content, so drinking this water is naturally reassuring. However, the price of tap water in Germany is also the most expensive in the world. If a user uses 10,000 cubic meters of water per year, the price per cubic meter is as high as 1.91 euros (1 euro is approximately equivalent to 8.8 RMB), while in the United States, the water fee converted into euros is only 47 cents. Such a high water price is mainly to make people save water.

The British love filter jugs

The "national drink" of the British is tea. The British Tea Association claims that the British drink 62 billion cups of tea every year, accounting for 40% of the British liquid intake. With the recognition of the value of plain water, drinking plain water in the UK is gradually becoming fashionable.

In British households and office premises, it is rare to see bottled water, and people like to carry kettles when they go out. At home, water filters are very common. According to statistics from the largest water filter manufacturer in the UK, "Brita", among the 24.5 million households in the UK, 6 million households use water filters, and the current annual sales amount of water filters in the UK reaches as high as 1 billion pounds (1 pound is approximately equivalent to 10.5 RMB). Many Britons use water filtration devices not because they do not trust the safety of tap water, but to soften the water quality, because the water quality in most areas of the UK is quite hard, prone to scale formation, which reduces the thermal efficiency of appliances and consumes more cleaning products. For example, 1.6 mm of scale in an electric kettle reduces thermal efficiency by 12%. Therefore, the British love to use convenient water filters. However, the Drinking Water Inspectorate in the UK does not recommend the public to use water filtration devices, because improper use of water filtration devices may instead pollute the water quality and alter the taste of the water. The agency reminds the public using water filtration devices to store the filtered water in the refrigerator and finish drinking it within 24 hours.

In addition, the main pipes of the UK tap water are mostly made of iron, which is prone to rusting, and some areas have high iron and manganese content in the water source. If not handled properly, sediment will form in the pipes and mix into the tap water. Therefore, the Drinking Water Inspectorate in the UK reminds the public that if they find that the color of the tap water at home has changed, they should immediately notify the tap water company and have the right to claim compensation.

Americans resist bottled water

When I was in China, I often heard people say that the tap water in the United States is very clean and can be drunk without worry. After arriving in the United States, I found that many people were also worried about the safety of tap water. Therefore, the US Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), the Natural Resources Defense Council (NRDC) and some health organizations have all provided some drinking water safety guidelines.

However, none of the major American drinking water organizations promote bottled water. On the contrary, they have all consistently launched a large-scale "resist bottled water movement". "Many people think that bottled water is safer and more hygienic than tap water, but that's not true," said a senior official of the American Environmental Working Promotion Association. He said that 45% of the bottled water sources on the US market are not as advertised, coming from natural springs or purified water, but are actually purified urban water or even tap water. They once tested the top 10 best brands of bottled water in the United States, and found more than 200 types of pollutants, including bacteria, fertilizer components, industrial chemicals, etc., which have lost the meaning of pure water. In addition, a large number of plastic bottles have a great impact on the environment, and less than 15% of plastic bottles are recycled. Therefore, advocates of environmental protection and conservation have also joined the "resist bottled water movement".

"Actually, safe tap water tastes better than bottled water," pointed out an energy expert from the University of California, Davis. It is often seen on American TV stations that there are programs testing water quality. Participants are blindfolded to identify water quality, and the result is often that tap water wins. "Drinking water in big cities is tested for bacterial content at least 100 times a month, but the US [tray] and Food and Drug Administration (FDA) only requires bottled water companies to test once a week. Which one is safer is obvious."

The American Safe Drinking Water Association also promotes drinking knowledge to the public. For example, when I was in China, I thought boiled water was safe, but after coming to the United States, I learned that boiling only eliminates microorganisms in the water. If there are nitrates and lead in the water, boiling the water would increase the concentration of nitrates and lead, which is harmful and counterproductive. In addition, before using water, you need to let the water run first to flush the pipes and remove some harmful substances such as lead produced in the pipes.

Japanese pipelines are all stainless steel

Tokyo tap water is often poured into beverage bottles and placed in vending machines, sold together with oolong tea, afternoon black tea, etc., priced at 100 yen (1 yen is approximately equivalent to 0.07 yuan). This is because the source of Tokyo tap water is the spring of Mount Fuji, and the flowing process passes through volcanic lava soil, making its mineral content quite rich.

To ensure that tap water meets drinking standards, the Japanese have put a lot of effort, especially in the protection of pipelines, their technology and level are leading in the world. A person in charge of the Tokyo Waterworks Bureau told reporters: "Pipeline pollution is the most common factor in tap water contamination. The water originally flowing out of the purification pool is clean, but it has to go through a long pipeline transport to reach users, and it is easy to have problems during this process." It is understood that Japan has 51 standards for water quality testing, including E. coli, mercury content, water hardness, and taste. If there are problems during the transportation process, then the previous monitoring is useless. Since stainless steel is more corrosion-resistant and stronger in hygiene compared to other materials, therefore, most of Japan's tap water pipelines use stainless steel, and the Japan Waterworks Association has formulated material usage standards for pipelines and valves as early as the 1980s.

There are two ways to supply water to buildings in Japan, one is direct water supply, and the other is tap water entering a water storage cabinet first, then being sent to the water consumption endpoint by different water supply methods. The former is aimed at buildings with two floors or less, and the latter is aimed at high-rise buildings. Since the water flows down from the water tank rather than from the pipe, people turn on the tap every morning and let the water flow for two minutes before drinking to ensure safety. For water supply pipelines and water tanks, the water company has a special maintenance department, which cleans them every six months, stopping water supply for two to three hours during maintenance. If aging problems are found in the pipeline at any time, they will be repaired or replaced immediately.

In some mountainous areas in Japan, some places have good environmental protection, and people prefer to drink well water, but recently, there have been frequent cases of gastrointestinal diseases caused by wells, so the government calls on people to trust tap water the most.
