Follow-up report: Was Ling Xiaosu's Weibo hacked? Analysis of the twists and turns of Yao Chen's divorce case

The evolution of Yao Chen and Ling Xiaosu's divorce case should have greatly exceeded the expectations of both the parties involved and netizens. From the initial announcement of their peaceful breakup, to the exposure by the media that Tang Yifei was the third party, and then Ling Xiaosu's admission on Sohu Weibo that "Tang Yifei is indeed my woman," this modern melodrama with its twists and turns has thrilled the onlookers, making them shout in excitement. Sohu Entertainment exclusively analyzed the twists and turns of Yao Chen's divorce case, giving netizens a more comprehensive understanding.

First Wave: Not even a tenth person can make a difference

On January 28, 2012, Yao Chen and Ling Xiaosu issued a statement through the media, ending their seven-year marriage. They stated that after the split, they would still consider each other as the most trusted family members in their lives, and they would still believe in love and remain steadfast for life. On February 5, Ling Xiaosu updated his Sohu Weibo: "I know our affairs have saddened many friends, but I really don't want everyone to lose faith in marriage because of this. We are actually very serious and conscientious about life, but we belong to a very special group, walking on tightropes, while you stand on solid ground, relatively safer. Anyway, I'm sorry for troubling everyone!"

At this point, this matter should have come to an end, but it was reignited due to media exposure that Tang Yifei was the third party. On February 22, Ling Xiaosu updated a sincere Weibo post on Sohu, expressing regret over the end of his marriage and firmly denying the existence of a third party, hence "If there were no irreconcilable issues within us, even if there were ten outsiders, wouldn't it be useless?" Who would abandon a partner who has been together for ten years for someone else? Now we are both already in the depths of despair, and our families are extremely sad. Why are there so many cruel words? These words are like sharp knives!

The full Weibo post on February 22 read as follows:

"I remember a writer once said that life is like a performance that cannot be rehearsed. I also feel that although I may not be satisfied with playing other roles in my life, the role I should play well is myself, Ling Xiaosu, which I realized through acting. The failure of my marriage is now a certainty, and the sense of defeat almost crushed me. But when both sides have done their best and still cannot give each other the life they desire, we have no choice but to change the nature of our relationship, otherwise we would just be delaying each other, because if neither side can give the person they care about most happiness, it is truly unbearable! Hence, if there were no irreconcilable issues between us, even if there were ten outsiders, wouldn't it be useless? Who would abandon a partner who has been together for ten years for someone else? Now we are both already in the depths of despair, and our families are extremely sad. Why are there so many cruel words? These words are like sharp knives!"

Second Wave: Pinocchio Turns Out to Be a Woman

Ling Xiaosu's Weibo post on February 22 could not stop the doubts from netizens, and people condemned Tang Yifei in various forms, including harsh language. At 21:10 on February 23, Tang Yifei posted on Weibo saying she was innocent and hoped not to be dragged into it, bearing unfounded accusations. However, many netizens still held a skeptical attitude.

At noon on February 25, Yao Chen posted on Weibo: "Pinocchio, Pinocchio, turns out you're a woman," which many netizens pointed out was a satire on Tang Yifei for lying. And Yao Chen's friend Ning Caishen almost simultaneously posted a comment saying, "Is this thing not embarrassing enough? Doesn't everyone in the circle know your glorious deeds? Don't you fear hurting yourself by telling such a big lie?" This led many netizens to speculate that it was a supportive act for his friend.

Third Wave: Tang Yifei Is Indeed My Woman

At 23:50 on February 25, Ling Xiaosu finally admitted his romantic relationship with Tang Yifei on Weibo and made a harsh statement to his ex-wife Yao Chen, only hoping that his ex-wife Yao Chen would take care of herself and live a peaceful and happy life forever! From now on, let us look at each other from afar in the world and never contact each other again until death. This sensational Weibo post brought the divorce to its climax, prompting numerous netizens to repost and comment, with some even starting to write micro-novels based on it.

Just as many media outlets were ready to dive deeper and netizens were eagerly awaiting developments, Ling Xiaosu's Weibo post was deleted. Afterwards, his agent claimed in an interview that the Weibo account had been hacked and impersonated. By 3 a.m. on February 26, Ling Xiaosu's Weibo posts had been cleared.

Was the Weibo account really hacked, or was it just an excuse? Sohu Entertainment will continue to follow the development of this matter. As of the time of writing, Ling Xiaosu and his agent's phones were constantly busy, with no response yet.
