With the bite marks, Chen Xinmin and his students took turns guarding the dog, recording the changes in the bite marks from 0-96 hours. They took a photo every 10 minutes, made a record, and conducted a digital analysis. After taking 576 photos and making dozens of pages of records, they concluded that "after 72 hours, the bite marks on the dog's body only leave a faint trace, and after 96 hours, they almost disappear..." - up to now, the world's unique "bite mark morphology regression formula" was born in their hands. Using this formula, you can get the state of the bite mark at any time between 0-96 hours after it appears.

Behind the scenes>>>

A thousand-year-old finger, a ten-thousand-year-old tooth. Forensic dental science mainly uses the uniqueness of teeth for same identification. As Chen Xinmin puts it, tooth prints are the "life password that never disappears".

It is not common for bite marks to occur in homicide cases, so after the establishment of the forensic dental science research group, they only handled the identification of four cases, but the last seemingly simple case caused Chen Xinmin a lot of trouble.

In 1999, about two years after the establishment of the forensic dental science research group, Professor Chen Xinmin and his students created the world's only "bite mark morphology regression formula" so far.

In 1997, experts from the Stomatological Hospital of West China involving restoration, joint, anatomy and physiology, under the leadership of Professor Chen Xinmin, established the forensic dental science research group, which was the first forensic dental science research group in China at that time. Subsequently, responsible Professor Chen Xinmin became a specially hired expert of the Southwest Judicial Appraisal Center.

Bite marks lock down suspected murderers

Not long after, Chen Xinmin received a call from the investigator, and false teeth were found at the foot of the victim's bed. The dental model of the criminal suspect wearing false teeth was soon delivered, and the results of the bite mark same identification quickly came out: three lower teeth matched, seven upper teeth matched, reaching 100% same identification.

Chen Xinmin carefully examined the suspect's dental model again and finally found: the suspect had many missing lower teeth, and there were many residual roots on the right side of the lower teeth, with obvious signs of artificial grinding on the residual roots, "indicating that the suspect had undergone false teeth repair." Seeing this, Chen Xinmin felt confident: "I estimate that the suspect committed the crime while wearing false teeth, and during the biting process, the false teeth fell off and were not picked up in the chaos. When the mold was taken, the suspect did not wear false teeth!" Professor Chen Xinmin called the investigators: please find the false teeth at the crime scene.

Chen Xinmin found that the bite marks on the victim's face were overlapping, repeated bite marks. He carefully separated the overlapping bite marks and began to conduct bite mark same identification. He made bite marks on wax sheets using the suspect's dental model, scanned them, and all the other tooth impressions matched the bite marks on the victim's face, but there were always three missing tooth impressions. "4-5 teeth match, the error of same identification is less than eight in ten thousand, and 8 identical teeth can achieve 100% same identification." These three "missing" tooth impressions left Chen Xinmin very confused.

After the establishment of the forensic dental science research group, they focused on the biomechanical research of bite mark same identification. "Forensic doctors cannot arrive at the scene in time, the bite marks will change significantly more than 24 hours after the homicide occurs, so we must find a way to restore them to their original state." However, this process cannot be simulated on humans.
