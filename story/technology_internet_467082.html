The focus of home decoration has always been indoors, and outdoor decoration is easily overlooked by people, especially the outdoor balcony. Some more particular people would choose some colorful tiles, but no matter what, tiles always give people a feeling of being too cold, lacking a sense of comfort and ease. In fact, there is another option now - laying the floor on the balcony as well. Outdoor anti-corrosion wood is much more ideal than tiles in creating a warm home atmosphere. It can really last for 30 years.

Anti-corrosion wood has an ultra-long life span to withstand outdoor wind and sun exposure, which is related to its processing process. How is anti-corrosion wood made? Simply put, it's when the wood is processed and placed into a sealed pressure anti-corrosion tank, using vacuum pressure to press the anti-corrosion agent into the interior of the wood. The anti-corrosion agent penetrates into the wood's cell tissue and tightly combines with the wood fiber, thereby achieving the purpose of anti-corrosion. In order to allow the anti-corrosion agent to effectively enter the wood, the wood itself has also gone through special selection. For example, Russian pines and European red pines can achieve a closely combined effect with the anti-corrosion agent, so their usage time is relatively long, up to 30 years.

Anti-corrosion agents are the key

Anti-corrosion wood undergoes processing with anti-corrosion agents, and whether these agents are harmful to the human body, and choosing which type of anti-corrosion agent processing is safer, are probably the issues that consumers are most concerned about. Early anti-corrosion wood used CCA-C, B wood preservatives. Although this preservative has a certain level of toxicity, since it is only used outdoors, the harm is relatively small. Nowadays, ACQ-D, C, B wood preservatives have appeared. Anti-corrosion wood processed with this preservative meets environmental protection standards, significantly reducing its toxicity, allowing everyone to purchase it with peace of mind.

Advantages of anti-corrosion wood

As the name suggests, anti-corrosion wood naturally has anti-corrosion properties, thus it is often used as outdoor flooring material. Anti-corrosion wooden floors can effectively prevent microbial erosion and insect infestation, while also being waterproof and anti-corrosive, allowing them to withstand harsh outdoor environments without requiring much care.

However, as outdoor anti-corrosion wood, its own thermal expansion and contraction are not specially controlled, so deformation is relatively severe. When laying anti-corrosion wooden floors, they are usually made with gaps between the planks and are elevated like hollow ones, able to be flipped open at any time for cleaning or retrieving dropped items.
