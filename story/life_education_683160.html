How to achieve the optimal combination of manpower and equipment in fire fighting and rescue 18671079399

by xfxybk on 2012-02-29 12:16:57

In firefighting and rescue operations, advanced equipment is crucial, as are human factors. However, only through the organic combination of people and equipment can their maximum efficiency be achieved, thereby minimizing casualties and property damage.

I. Familiarity with and mastery of equipment performance, strengthening maintenance and care, are the prerequisite conditions for achieving the organic combination of people and equipment.

In recent years, the General Team has paid particular attention to fire equipment construction, taking effective measures to strongly promote it. Firefighting equipment construction in various places across the province has made great strides, and special service team equipment allocation has also increased day by day. The performance and usage methods of the equipment vary. If the performance, technical parameters, and applicable scope of the equipment cannot be proficiently mastered, then it will be difficult to play its due role in practical firefighting and rescue operations, leading to missed opportunities.

Firstly, serious study must be conducted to understand and master the performance and basic principles of various types of equipment.

Using a variety of methods, combined with the personnel situation of the troops, an in-depth study of the performance, technical parameters, and applicable scope of various types of equipment should be carried out. The basic principles of various types of equipment must be truly understood, applying knowledge from one area to another, integrating and comprehending thoroughly. Avoid "not seeking deep understanding, rote memorization," and combine the use of equipment to familiarize yourself. Brigades and battalions should combine position training exercises and assault group competitions to further strengthen supervision and inspection of the troops' mastery of equipment performance, technical parameters, and applicable scope. Categorize and organize the unit's equipment situation, create manuals, frequently learn and master common knowledge about equipment to achieve the purpose of complete understanding and familiarity.

Secondly, strengthen the cultivation and retention of professional backbone personnel, and do a good job in the "transmission, help, and lead" of professional technology.

For some special service equipment and parts of the technically demanding equipment, on the basis of understanding its basic principles and common knowledge, hire technical personnel or experts for guidance lectures to deeply understand and master the equipment's performance, technical parameters, and applicable scope. On the basis of general understanding, designate specific personnel, assign responsibilities, conduct post-specific training, and focus on organizing key breakthroughs for some technically demanding and difficult-to-master equipment, mastering them categorically. This not only cultivates a batch of technical backbone personnel but also stirs up enthusiasm for studying technology and participating in large-scale position training exercises. In cultivating the backbone team, attention should be paid to maintaining a hierarchy, ensuring smooth transmission, helping, and leading, avoiding the occurrence of "disconnection" or "inadequate succession" among the backbone personnel.

Thirdly, strengthen the maintenance and care of equipment to ensure that the equipment remains in excellent condition and fully functional. First, establish and improve all management systems, implement responsibility, regularly inspect, and frequently maintain, ensuring that the equipment can be mobilized, used, and operated when needed. Second, when purchasing equipment, coordinate and cooperate with multiple parties. Equipment purchase must be practical and useful. Additionally, choose manufacturers with high-quality products, good after-sales service, and reliable training and maintenance, establishing long-term cooperative relationships to avoid situations where purchased equipment cannot be operated or malfunctions immediately upon purchase, becoming useless.

II. Based on actual needs, earnestly conducting equipment training is the basic requirement for achieving the organic combination of people and equipment.

To maximize the efficiency of people and equipment, apart from efforts in familiarizing with equipment and maintenance, training on the use of equipment is equally indispensable. Training not only helps understand the performance, technical parameters, and applicable scope of the equipment but also tests whether the equipment can play its due role during application, promoting mutual learning and improvement among officers and soldiers, enabling them to comprehensively master and apply the equipment.

First, determine training content based on actual needs.

There are two methods to determine training content: one is to follow the regulations in the "Firefighting and Rescue Business Training Outline," comparing training subjects and arranging types of training equipment accordingly. For example, reconnaissance subjects can involve training with toxic gas detectors and flammable gas detectors; demolition subjects can include hydraulic cutters, electric shears, and mobile chainsaws. The second method is to select training equipment types based on the actual needs of firefighting and rescue. For instance, underground building fires can involve smoke extraction machine training and guide rope simulation training.

Second, strictly adhere to the training procedures.

Training should proceed according to the principle of progressing from easy to difficult, simple to complex, low to high, step by step. Generally, start with theoretical learning to understand basic knowledge, then proceed to operational practice. After proficiently mastering basic techniques, gradually change training conditions, showcasing various terrains, fire scenarios, weather conditions, or simulating various faults and dangerous situations, allowing trainees to handle them, improving their application skills under various conditions.

Third, actively create conditions for effective simulation training.

Firstly, according to the thirteen types of simulation training facilities listed in the Ministry of Public Security Fire Bureau's "Guidance Plan for Simulation Training Facility Construction at Fire Training Bases (Centers)," unify planning, implement in phases, adapt to local conditions, simplify where necessary, prioritize basic construction, and focus on key constructions to build good simulation training venues. Secondly, make full use of existing equipment to give full play to and explore the roles and potential of current equipment, effectively combining regular training with simulation training. Lastly, newly purchased equipment must undergo actual training. All equipment should promptly be converted into combat effectiveness and not remain unused in storage, wasting resources.

Fourth, training should be close to practical use, focusing on results.

How fires are extinguished determines how soldiers are trained; how rescue operations are conducted dictates how training is organized. Train in firefighting tactics, equipment use, organizational command, synthesis enhancement, etc., choosing appropriate means around the desired effects, avoiding formality, gimmicks, and superficiality.

III. Scientific battle formations are the realization form of the organic combination of people and equipment.

Due to the arduousness and complexity of modern firefighting and rescue tasks, to meet new situations and problems faced by fire brigades, especially in cross-regional and large-scale complex firefighting and rescue operations, if equipment functions poorly, tactics and methods cannot be flexibly applied, and coordinated operations fail to form synergy, the integrity and timeliness of firefighting and rescue combat systems will be severely disrupted, slowing down overall firefighting battles or rescue activities and reducing efficiency, causing unnecessary losses to the public. Therefore, it is essential to carefully study battle formations suitable for actual needs, strengthen training, and continuously improve.

Firstly, clarify that vehicle and equipment are the foundation of battle formations.

That is to say, all levels of commanders must understand and master the most basic battle formations and adjust or reduce vehicles and personnel based on existing vehicles and equipment, forming several practical firefighting battle formations, which is very important to cultivate habits in training and actual combat. For example, water tankers can be formed into water tanker battle teams, foam vehicles can be either water tanker teams or foam vehicle teams. Similarly, aerial ladder vehicle formations, high-spray vehicle formations, rescue vehicle formations, lighting vehicle teams need to be studied and formulated. In summary, whatever type of equipment available can be compiled into certain forms of battle formations. Battle formations are not limited to one form; they can break the boundaries between teams, using a specialized vehicle of one team as the main force and combining with nearby teams to form one or several formations; or according to the actual vehicles of each team, form one formation or a water supply system, compiling large-tonnage water tankers or special vehicles as the main combat fleet, compiling high and low-pressure pump fire trucks as the water supply fleet, and then using conventional fire trucks to occupy nearby water sources to form supplementary water supply fleets, etc., to achieve the optimal combination of personnel and equipment and achieve scientifically adapted battle formations.

Secondly, fully play the role of every combatant, reflecting specialization in multiple areas.

Currently, non-commissioned officers are the main force in firefighting and rescue operations, and their management and utilization are particularly important. Non-commissioned officers should be strengthened in training to become professionals who can operate, maintain, repair, and manage well. Company commanders should understand the characteristics of various types of personnel and, according to different situations, compile them into reconnaissance, firefighting, rescue, leakage sealing, decontamination groups with independent combat capabilities yet mutual cooperation, fully playing their sharp edge role in firefighting and rescue. Strengthen the study of firefighting tactics and technology, so they can master the characteristics and tactical methods of various types of fires, understand and grasp the characteristics and disposal procedures of common disaster accidents, and play a sharp edge role at critical moments. Regular drills of combat vehicles, personnel, and combat formations should be conducted to strengthen coordination and cooperation between groups, classes, and companies, achieving the best combination of personnel and equipment in firefighting and rescue operations and maximizing efficiency.

IV. Scientific decision-making in actual combat is an important guarantee for realizing the organic combination of people and equipment.

In firefighting and rescue operations, only through scientific decision-making by participating officers and soldiers, based on the understanding of personnel and equipment, can the maximum benefits of people and equipment be realized to achieve success in firefighting operations. However, the disaster scene often changes rapidly, thus it is necessary to timely grasp relevant information on the scene to provide reliable basis for scientific decision-making.

Firstly, carry out situation reconnaissance at the accident site well.

Fully leverage the role of detection instruments in accident handling and run situation reconnaissance throughout the entire accident handling process. This is the premise for commanders to formulate action plans.

Secondly, the action plan must be thorough and feasible.

During the action, commanders should seek opinions from professionals, pool ideas, and decisively make decisions based on the personnel and equipment situation of the troops. According to changing circumstances, deal with emergencies appropriately, avoiding blind actions, ensuring the safety of rescue operations, and ensuring that rescue actions are implemented in one go without detours.

Thirdly, constantly summarize the experiences and lessons learned in actual combat.

Carefully summarize successful experiences, and regarding any issues that arise, do not fear exposing shortcomings. Develop a good habit of doing post-action reviews, which is a shortcut to continuously enhancing firefighting and rescue capabilities in practice. It is also an important measure to formulate targeted measures for strengthening and rectification, avoiding past mistakes from recurring in future firefighting and rescue operations.

In summary, strengthening research on equipment, mastering its performance and patterns while focusing on its training and application, and achieving the organic combination of equipment, personnel, and tactics will continuously enhance the firefighting and rescue combat capabilities of fire brigades, making them a civilized army capable of truly "being mobilizable, rushing forward, saving lives, and winning battles," laying a solid foundation for protecting national and personnel life and property safety.
