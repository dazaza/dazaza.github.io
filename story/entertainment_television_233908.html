The Birth of the Black Warrior

Mak Tik Cheung has always possessed a sharp business mind since he was young and understands the principle that doing business leads to prosperity. Thus, he worked hard and successfully completed his MBA at Stanford University in the United States. He then sought opportunities in the foreign land.

After graduation, Tik Cheung saw the potential of Ho Man Tin's company and proactively approached him, clearly stating that he didn’t want to be an employee but rather a partner, even if it meant owning only 0.01% of the shares. Ho Man Tin, seeing Tik Cheung's uniqueness, allowed him to join as a partner. Their relationship became both mentorship and friendship, working seamlessly together, specializing in acquiring companies and selling them off piecemeal, becoming renowned snipers in the American business world.

Later, Tik Cheung became known as the ruthless Black Warrior in the business world, showing no mercy. Gradually, his influence grew, and so did the differences of opinion between him and Man Tin. For the sake of the company's interests and development, Tik Cheung decided to fully acquire Man Tin's company. Unable to bear the blow, Man Tin drove under the influence of alcohol. Tik Cheung learned that Man Tin's car had lost control and fell into the sea. After the incident, Man Tin's body could not be found, but Tik Cheung firmly believed that Man Tin was dead, feeling immense guilt. Was the value of a deal really greater than friendship, or even life? Tik Cheung reflected on everything from the past and determined to stop being the Black Warrior, deciding to withdraw from the business world. From the newspaper, Tik Cheung learned that Man Tin's widow and child were struggling financially in Hong Kong.
