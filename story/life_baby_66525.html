Living observation: who benefits from the milk price increase?

by kk2040 on 2008-05-02 23:08:23

On March 27, Guangming Dairy and Shijiazhuang Sanlu Group Corporation respectively submitted applications to the National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC) to raise the prices of some pure milk products. According to relevant provisions of the "NDRC Implementation Measures for Temporary Price Intervention on Some Important Goods and Services", the NDRC accepted the price increase requests from both companies. After review, the NDRC officially issued a document approving the content of the two companies' submissions on March 27.

Since January this year, with the rise in national prices, finished milk and raw milk prices have increased to varying degrees, increasing the profits of dairy product manufacturers and restoring farmers' enthusiasm for dairy farming. However, recent interviews revealed that after the milk price hike, the benefits to dairy product manufacturers and farmers were not significant, consumer enthusiasm for purchasing milk was affected, and milk stations, supermarkets, and other intermediaries became the biggest beneficiaries.

The milk price increase did not result in optimistic gains for farmers and enterprises.

At several finished milk retail stores in Hohhot, Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region, it was observed that Yili and Mengniu-produced 0.5 kg paper-packaged pure milk boxes (12 packs per box) were priced at 40 yuan, while 0.25 kg paper-packaged pure milk boxes (12 packs per box) were priced at 25 yuan. These prices had risen by 2 yuan and 1 yuan per box respectively since the beginning of the year. The price of bucket-packed fruit yogurt also increased from 5.5 yuan per bucket at the start of the year to 6.5 yuan.

However, this finished milk price did not significantly benefit the farmers. In some areas, the condition of dairy farming has not improved, and farmers' incomes remain uncertain. According to calculations in Qinghe Town,科尔沁区of Tongliao City, Inner Mongolia, the current highest milk price is about 2.7-2.8 yuan per kilogram. At this price, each dairy cow can generate up to 2000 yuan profit annually. But with feed price increases, recent profits have been around 1000 yuan.

In Daxipeng Village, Qinghe Town,科尔沁区of Tongliao City, farmers calculated the cost of raising a dairy cow: an average daily feeding cost of 30 yuan during the lactation period of 8 months amounts to 7200 yuan; a dry period of 4 months costs 15 yuan per day per cow, totaling 1800 yuan; additionally, treatment costs are 200 yuan per year, and breeding fees are 50 yuan. The total cost comes to 9250 yuan.

A dairy cow averages 20 kilograms of milk per day, producing 4800 kilograms over 8 months. Subtracting the 300 kilograms consumed monthly by calves, there remains 4500 kilograms of milk. At 2.4 yuan per kilogram, this totals 10800 yuan. Adding the income from calf sales of 500 yuan, the total comes to 11300 yuan. Subtracting the costs, each cow nets 2050 yuan.

The increase in finished milk prices similarly did not bring obvious benefits to dairy product manufacturers. Li Xiang, head of the finance department of Mengniu Dairy科尔沁Limited Liability Company, told reporters: "The price increase of finished milk reduced the cost pressure on dairy product manufacturers and helped them out of losses. If the product prices do not rise, according to the current raw milk prices, the company would incur losses."

However, with the rise in finished milk prices, production volumes have decreased. Despite a decline in sales volume, the sales revenue continues to rise. From Mengniu Dairy, we learned that despite a 20% increase in finished milk prices, sales and profit margins have declined, particularly evident in mid-to-low-end milk products like pure milk.

The largest beneficiaries of the milk price increase are the intermediaries.

The intermediaries benefited the most from the milk price increase. According to reports from various regions in Inner Mongolia, neither farmers nor dairy product manufacturers gained much from the milk price increase; most of the price increase was "appropriated" by intermediaries such as milk stations. Shan Wenhai, vice president of the Tongliao Dairy Association, said: "After the milk price increase, neither farmers nor dairy product manufacturers made much money; the money went to feed producers and milk stations."

From Tongliao, we learned that compared to the end of last year, the original milk purchase price in Tongliao region rose from 1.9 yuan per kilogram to 2.4 yuan. However, since last year, the price increase of major feeds such as corn exceeded 60%, with full-price feed rising to about 2.4 yuan per kilogram, far exceeding the milk purchase price increase.

Currently, Yili and Mengniu companies do not directly collect milk from farmers or households; seventy to eighty percent of their operations involve milk stations, which have become a point of conflict in the industrial chain. We learned from Yili and Mengniu companies that the average raw milk price delivered to these companies is about 3 yuan per kilogram, but the price received by farmers is only 2.4 to 2.6 yuan, with the difference entirely taken by milk stations.

Additionally, Yili and Mengniu and other dairy product manufacturers are heavily exploited by large shopping malls, especially large supermarkets like Carrefour. To enter these large supermarkets, they must pay various fees such as protection fees, entry fees, anniversary fees, etc., resulting in 10% to 20% of the product value being taken by supermarkets.

As a response, whether it's Mengniu or Yili, in recent years, they have increased the development and marketing of premium milk. Pan Gang, chairman of Yili Group, said: "Due to continuous increases in raw milk prices and 'exploitation' by intermediaries, liquid pure milk production by dairy product manufacturers does not make money. Therefore, Yili Group adjusted its product structure, increasing the share of high-value-added products to 40%. Since the consumption group for premium milk is relatively stable, this has led to an overall upward trend in sales revenue in the dairy industry, avoiding losses for the enterprise."

After the price increase of finished milk, consumers' enthusiasm for purchasing was affected.

After the price increase of finished milk, consumers benefited the least. From several finished milk retail stores in Hohhot, we learned that after the price increase, consumer enthusiasm for purchasing was affected, and the sales volume of liquid pure milk dropped significantly.

The owner of a Mengniu and Yili finished milk retail store located on Yingbin South Road in Hohhot said: "Previously, ten boxes of liquid pure milk sold out in three to five days, now it takes ten days to sell ten boxes."

To promote healthy and orderly development of the dairy industry, standardize the dairy market order, and protect consumer interests, the NDRC published the "Implementation Measures for Temporary Price Intervention on Some Important Goods and Services" on January 15, implementing price intervention on dairy products and four key enterprises. As a necessity of life, the stability of milk prices must be guaranteed.

However, from the current situation, the regulation of finished milk prices by relevant national departments has kept the original milk prices relatively stable. But due to natural disasters, feed price increases, rising production costs, overall supply shortages, and other reasons, factors leading to finished milk price increases are difficult to control.

For this reason, responsible persons from the Inner Mongolia Dairy Office, Yili, Mengniu, and other dairy product manufacturers suggest the need to improve supervision mechanisms for the entire process of dairy production. This includes comprehensive supervision of feed prices, original milk purchase prices, and retail prices to control unreasonable price increases. There should also be increased efforts to clean up unreasonable charges by large supermarkets. Meanwhile, through market leverage, an appropriate price for milk should be regulated, prompting dairy product manufacturers to enter a virtuous cycle of development through technological innovation and industrial upgrading.
