Essential Tips for Understanding and Using the Android Operating System (Part Five)

by dream2012 on 2012-02-21 17:52:09

The Android operating system may seem complex and confusing to many novice users who are just getting started. Here, we have compiled some essential tips for understanding and using the Android operating system, hoping they will be helpful. Please note that these tips mainly target the most mainstream Android 2.1 system, but most of them also apply to older or newer versions of Android.

41. Make your phone speak in a pure British accent

You might find the English pronunciation strange when using Google Maps for navigation. You can check the "Use my settings" option in Menu > Settings > Text-to-Speech. You'll then notice that the navigation voice switches to a pure British accent, which sounds much more comfortable.

42. Delete those bulky apps

Users of Android phones that don't support the APP2SD function may often face the dilemma of insufficient phone memory, necessitating the deletion of some applications to free up space. In the Settings > Applications > Manage Applications interface, you can press the Menu key to sort by app size, prioritizing the deletion of those that consume a lot of space.

43. Activate the speakerphone

Sometimes, during a call, the environment might be too noisy to hear clearly. In the call interface, press the Menu key and then select the "Speaker" option. This ensures that not only can you hear clearly, but people around you can also hear what the person on the other end is saying.

44. Delete entire SMS conversations with someone

If there are secrets hidden in your phone's text messages that you don't want others to know, and you don’t want your wife to discover them at home, long-press the SMS conversation list, then select delete. All messages exchanged with that person will be deleted.

45. Use your own pictures as wallpapers

If you've taken an excellent landscape photo or want to use a family picture as wallpaper, simply long-press the phone screen, choose wallpaper, and then select the image you want to use as wallpaper. You can even crop a portion of it before setting it as the final wallpaper.

46. List of recently used programs in Android

Long-pressing the Home key brings up a list of the most recently used programs in the Android system. If you accidentally close an application, you can reopen it using this method.

47. Turn off phone animations

Although various animation effects in the Android system make your phone look cool, they also consume a lot of battery power and affect performance. Normally, you can turn off related options in Settings > Sound and Display > Animations. When demonstrating to other users, you can always turn them back on to impress them.

48. Integrated process management in Android

The Android system actually has a task killer feature, though it is deeply hidden. In the phone's Settings > Applications, you can view running services. Clicking on relevant processes allows you to force-close them. However, this feature is less user-friendly compared to third-party process management tools.

49. Set up an unlock pattern for your phone

Setting an unlock pattern for your Android phone can enhance its security, at least preventing it from being snooped on by ill-intentioned individuals. Just make sure you don't forget this unlock pattern yourself, as the consequences could be serious.

50. Prevent the phone screen from sleeping while charging

Having the phone screen stay on all the time without considering power consumption can be a good thing, especially when your phone charges overnight. With the help of software, your phone can transform into an electronic clock, allowing convenient time checks and functioning as a night light. To enable this, check the "Stay Awake" option in System Settings > Applications > Development.
