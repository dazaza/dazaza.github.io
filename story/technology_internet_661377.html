This afternoon in Beijing time, Shenyang Dongjin Club currently doing spring training at the Hongta Base in Kunming, Yunnan had a teaching match with Tianjin Songjiang Club. Ultimately, Shenyang Dongjin drew 4-4 with Yanbian through Zhu Dayi's brace (Weibo), Wang Qingfeng's goal and another goal by a South Korean trial foreign player.

In the previous teaching matches, Shenyang Dongjin consecutively played three teaching matches against Fujian Junhao, the newly promoted team in China League One (Chinese second division), Gwangju FC, and Qingdao Zhongneng. Shenyang Dongjin embarrassingly achieved a result of three consecutive losses, and the team still has many problems. Since the China League One will reignite battles on the seventeenth day of next month, the team's head coach Chen Bo still has some time to solve the current issues within the team.

It is worth noting that while other teams preparing for the new season of China League One are recruiting soldiers and buying horses, Shenyang Dongjin’s pace in introducing domestic and foreign players still lags behind other competitors. Among the already confirmed signed domestic players for the team, there are only two veteran players: Xiao Zhanbo and Li Yinan (Weibo). Although midfielder Zou Peng from Chengdu Blades (Weibo) has been on trial with the team for several days, the Dongjin coaching staff is still in the observation phase regarding Zou Peng, and whether they can sign this midfielder still needs to be tested in internal matches and teaching matches.

Regarding foreign players, Shenyang Dongjin's trial foreign players have changed in batches recently. Apart from these foreign players failing to impress the coaching staff, there are also players that Dongjin wanted but had to regrettably part ways with due to their high prices. However, today, according to local media in Shenyang, South Korean central defender Song Taeyoon, who once played for Henan Jianye (Weibo) in the Chinese Super League (Weibo), has already signed with Shenyang Dongjin Club. Song Taeyoon's joining makes Shenyang Dongjin's backline more solid.

In addition, Brazilian forward Rodrigo, who shone brightly for Shenyang Dongjin in 2009, was rumored to have the possibility of returning to his former club. Regarding Rodrigo's skills and technical features, both the coaching staff and media fans are well aware. However, since the Brazilian has been away from Dongjin for two years, whether he can maintain his 2009 season form remains unknown. Therefore, Shenyang Dongjin Club is still cautious about re-signing Rodrigo.

Since this match is of a friendly nature, head coach Chen Bo used different lineups in the first and second halves. It is reported that Shenyang Dongjin Football Team will have another teaching match tomorrow afternoon with another China League One team, Tianjin Songjiang.
