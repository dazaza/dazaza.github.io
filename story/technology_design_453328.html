The Environmental Protection Department's compliance building has received a three-star green building certification - Technical Information - Design Group Network

by anonymous on 2011-06-29 08:55:37

Not long ago, the Environmental International Convention Compliance Building of the Ministry of Environmental Protection (MEP), which adopted Ingersoll Rand's Trane HVAC system and security hardware system solutions, was awarded the three-star Green Building Label Certificate by the Ministry of Housing and Urban-Rural Development (hereinafter referred to as MOHURD).

The Green Building Certification is currently the only most authoritative building energy-saving certification formulated and managed by the relevant government authorities in China. The three-star rating represents the highest level of this certification. So far, the Compliance Building is the only public building in Beijing that has passed the design and usage stages of the three-star green building certification. And across the country, only five public buildings have passed this certification. Successfully obtaining the three-star green building certification not only marks another successful example of Ingersoll Rand’s efforts to create and maintain a safe, comfortable, and efficient environment but also highly affirms the company's long-term commitment to energy conservation and environmental protection and its active promotion of building energy efficiency and green building development.

As an important corporate partner of the National MEP, Ingersoll Rand began strategic cooperation with MEP as early as 2007 and actively participated in the construction of the Compliance Building project, providing a series of HVAC system energy-saving solutions. This review mainly focused on aspects such as energy saving, water saving, land saving, and material saving for strict examination of both the design and usage phases of the Compliance Building. It lasted several months, during which experts from Tsinghua University School of Architecture, designated by MOHURD as a third-party professional institution, collected data, held expert evaluation meetings, and issued authoritative assessment reports.

For many large buildings, the chilled water unit is the most critical part of the air conditioning cooling system. The Compliance Building's air conditioning system uses Trane's three-stage centrifugal chillers, which are currently the most energy-efficient large-scale chillers in the world. Their maximum operating efficiency coefficient can reach up to 7.0, which is 13.5% more energy-efficient than other similar large-scale chillers in the market. According to Tsinghua University's report, the actual annual electricity consumption of the Trane air conditioning system used in the building is only 17.75 kWh/square meter, and even in July when the air conditioning power consumption peaks, it is only 5.23 kWh/square meter. These figures are significantly lower than the conventional electricity consumption of air conditioning systems and other energy-saving buildings participating in this green building selection. These two indicators were the most important bonus points in the energy-saving aspect of this review, playing a key role in the final approval of the Compliance Building and receiving consistent recognition from the Tsinghua expert group and MEP officials. Meanwhile, the security hardware system used in the Compliance Building has greatly improved the safety performance of the building itself and the evacuation speed of personnel in emergency situations, demonstrating humanistic care that has been highly affirmed by the experts.

MOHURD initiated the management work of green building evaluation labels in 2008, aiming to implement national strategies for resource conservation and environmental protection and guide the healthy development of green buildings. Based on the evaluation indicators specified in the "Green Building Evaluation Standard", such as building energy efficiency rate, residential area green space rate, renewable energy utilization rate, non-traditional water source utilization rate, and recyclable building materials usage, the green building label levels are divided into one-star, two-star, and three-star from low to high. It is reported that apart from being awarded the three-star green building label certificate, the Compliance Building also participated in the MOHURD Green Building and Low-Energy Building Double Hundred Demonstration Project Group and was included in the double hundred project demonstration directory, serving as a model of green public buildings promoted nationwide by MOHURD.

As a globally leading diversified industrial company, Ingersoll Rand consistently adheres to the concept of making technological progress more environmentally friendly and energy-efficient. Through continuous technological innovation, it promotes building energy efficiency and green building development, actively contributing to the sustainable development of the enterprise itself and its customers. Besides successfully assisting the Compliance Building in obtaining the green building certification this time, the Taicang factory of Ingersoll Rand also recently received the LEED Global Green Building Certification Gold Award from the U.S. Green Building Council. Moreover, green buildings that have obtained LEED certification due to the use of Ingersoll Rand products and technologies include GE Shanghai Factory and Suzhou Bentley Factory, which won the gold award, Shenzhen Excel Century Center, Harbin LeSong Shopping Mall, and Shenzhen Hushan Apartments, which won the silver award.
