The Origin and History of Buddha Yunlai Incense Culture

Buddha Yunlai Technology Development Co., Ltd. has an unbroken incense tradition, and the civilization of Buddha Yunlai incense is characterized by its longevity, breadth, and universality. In China, during the Zhou dynasty, people would "burn wood" to offer smoke sacrifices to heaven, known as "Yin" or "Yin Sacrifice." By the time of Emperor Wu of Han, burning incense had come to symbolize the transmission of generations and vitality, as well as reverence for heaven and respect for ancestors. After the Sui and Tang dynasties, the practice became more widespread and secularized. A poem by Chen Yuyi from the Song dynasty titled "Incense Burning" reads: "Infinite meaning is embodied in this single wisp of smoke," reflecting how life's joys, sorrows, and philosophical inquiries found expression through incense. Incense had become completely integrated into people's spiritual lives. Interestingly, not only do Chinese burn incense, but many other ethnic groups and countries around the world also have a tradition of incense burning, including the ancient Egyptians, Babylonians, Indians, Greeks, Romans, and Israelites...
