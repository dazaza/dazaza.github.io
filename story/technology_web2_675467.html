UK 5-Year-Old Drives 6 KM Alone, Only Stops After Crashing Into a House

by anonymous on 2012-02-25 09:34:56

According to Xinhua News Agency, on the morning of August 18th, British 5-year-old boy Thomas Chatfield drove a SUV out while his parents were still asleep. He drove more than 6 kilometers and only stopped after colliding with a house. Fortunately, no one was injured in the incident.

Multiple people reported the event. The police of West Sussex received several calls around 7 o'clock reporting seeing a little boy driving a car.

"Almost at the same time, a man with an anxious tone reported that his 5-year-old son had driven away with the family car," said the police spokesperson.

According to the report by The Times, the vehicle driven by the boy Thomas was a Mitsubishi Pajero 7-seater SUV. If he sat in the driver's seat, his feet could not even reach the gas pedal.

One of the witnesses, Nathan Parkinson, is 22 years old. On his way to work, "I saw a car approaching, and it seemed like there was a child sitting in the driver's seat," the car was swaying unsteadily.

"I couldn't believe what I was seeing, so I looked again and saw a forehead and a pair of eyes behind the steering wheel," he said, "He wasn't wearing a seatbelt, it seemed like he was standing."

Several workers from a nearby auto repair shop saw the car passing through an intersection, and all the surrounding vehicles were honking their horns.

A route map made by The Daily Mail showed that Thomas drove from a two-way section of A27 road to A285 road, passing by the Goodwood Racecourse along the way. Later, the car turned onto a country road, climbed up a grassy slope, and finally collided with an old house and stopped.

The boy was scared to tears.

A man noticed the abnormal driving situation of the Mitsubishi and followed it in his own car. After the front car stopped, the man immediately went forward to check. The boy was crying at that time, so the man took him out of the car, then called the police and ambulance.

The boy appeared unharmed, but the paramedics still took him to the hospital for a check-up.

The Mitsubishi hit at least three cars on the road and grazed a pedestrian.

After this adventure, the car body had scratches, some parts were dented, the wheel on the passenger side and the glass on the driver's side were damaged.

The house that suffered from the "car accident" is a heritage building worth 800,000 pounds (approximately $1.224 million).

The homeowner, 70-year-old Cynthia Rivett, said: "I'm so glad he is safe."

The police said that this event was quite peculiar, and they did not plan to hold anyone accountable.

Thomas' mother Jessica was extremely frightened by her son's behavior.

"She was terrified, I kept comforting her," said a neighbor who wished to remain anonymous.

The neighbor said that Thomas took the car keys from his mother's bag while she was still asleep. "He often rides in his mother's car... Thomas is a smart child, he observed his mother's actions and learned how to drive."

It is unclear exactly how Thomas operated the car, but according to The Daily Mail, most advanced Pajeros can be started by just pressing a 'start' button. Thomas might not have needed to press the gas pedal because the vehicle can automatically move slowly in driving mode.
