What to sell in an online store is the most profitable?

One) No matter what kind of goods should have uniqueness or fashion:

Two) The price should be reasonable: If you can buy it offline at the same price, no one will buy it online.

Three) Do not sell single items online that cost less than shipping, this way you'll only lose money.

Four) The volume should be small, so it can be easy to transport and reduce costs.

Five) Not available offline, but only online, such as foreign trade order products or products brought directly from abroad.

When you choose a category or several products, you need to start understanding customers and competitors through market research to determine if your choice is correct, because most of the time your choice is based on your own experience or asymmetric information for judgment, which is often wrong and may eventually lead to your entrepreneurial failure.

Six) Everyone can look online to see what others are selling. If there are many such products, it indicates that these products have a good market. But whether such a market is your market depends on whether your product has competitive advantages. If it's the same brand, mainly look at whether the price is advantageous. If it's different brands, then look at the influence of your brand and the cost-performance ratio of the product.

Seven) If you're still not sure whether the product you want to sell has a market or is popular among Internet users, you can take out your idea or sample and show it to your friends or family, let them give you some advice. Don't think that people who don't go online are not my customers and they won't buy. This idea is wrong because they are likely to be your potential customers! Because opening an online store is just a marketing channel. It doesn't mean that products no one wants, when put online, someone will want. Only products that people want offline, when placed online with a good cost-performance ratio, will be sold.

Eight) The last point is not to sell counterfeit goods. You might think that once you sell the goods and make money, everything is OK. In fact, it's not. If you think like this, you're completely wrong (though I think none of you are so foolish). We don't want one-time customers; if we do business, we want to do it permanently.
