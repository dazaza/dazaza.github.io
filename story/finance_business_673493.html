Chronic rhinitis is a relatively serious disease, and it must not be treated as a minor ailment. Severe cases of chronic rhinitis require surgical treatment. Additionally, there are certain precautions for treating chronic rhinitis that we need to pay attention to.

The precautions for chronic rhinitis are very important to us. Understanding these precautions can lead to effective treatment and prevention.

1. Preventing nasal infections while swimming: Rhinitis patients should be cautious about water entering the nasal cavity during summer swims, which may cause infection. Therefore, before submerging your head underwater, take a deep breath, and exhale through your nose while underwater. After coming out of the water, if there is water in your nose, avoid forcefully blowing it out. Instead, you can perform jumping movements on the ground while making short exhalations through your nose to expel the water. This is a particularly important precaution for chronic rhinitis.

2. Do not forcibly blow your nose when congested to avoid rupturing nasal capillaries, which could result in nosebleeds. It also prevents bacteria-laden mucus from traveling back into the nasopharynx and causing otitis media. This is another precaution for chronic rhinitis that we should know.

3. Avoid drastic temperature changes: Do not stay in overly cold air-conditioned rooms for long periods. When entering and exiting air-conditioned spaces, prevent severe temperature fluctuations that might cause a cold. Minimize consumption of stimulating foods such as fried, spicy, or pickled items. All of these are aspects of the precautions for chronic rhinitis that require our attention.

Our living environment must be kept clean. Windows should be opened regularly to enhance airflow between indoor and outdoor spaces, and regular cleaning should be maintained. Doing so helps prevent chronic rhinitis, and the aforementioned precautions for chronic rhinitis should be remembered well, especially for children with rhinitis.
