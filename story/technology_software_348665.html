Identification and Maintenance of Designer Bags

by wanglina on 2010-04-20 09:59:48

Louis Vuitton is one of the most outstanding leather goods designers in French history. In 1854, he opened his first leather shop named after himself in Paris. A century later, Louis Vuitton became one of the top two brands in the field of luggage and leather goods, and also became a symbol of high society. Nowadays, the Louis Vuitton brand is not limited to designing and selling high-end leather goods and suitcases; it has become a giant trendsetter involving fashion, accessories, leather shoes, suitcases, jewelry, watches, media, fine wines, and more. LV is probably the most counterfeited brand on the market. Some counterfeit products look almost identical to the real ones at first glance, but with careful observation, it's not hard to spot the obvious quality difference between genuine and fake items. There are usually seven methods for identifying the authenticity of an LV handbag:

1. Look at the leather texture.

A genuine LV bag has deeper leather patterns, with clearly defined gaps between the small bubble-textured granules and average width. The feel is soft yet resilient. To illustrate, it's as if there's a flat surface underneath, and then irregular bubble textures are laid on top, with evenly spaced and deep seams between each bubble, and the height of each laid bubble is uniform, giving a textured appearance.

Counterfeit LV bags may have similar-looking embossed patterns, but they tend to feel oily with prolonged use, lacking the fine and resilient texture, and feeling harder. Although they may also have irregular patterns, the gaps between the patterns are smaller, making the bubbles appear crowded together, less three-dimensional. Upon closer inspection, discrepancies can be seen. Some counterfeit bags might have prominent grooves, but often the particles on top seem overly smooth and lack texture. Therefore, by simply looking at the leather texture, one can basically determine the authenticity of an LV bag, regardless of how convincingly the counterfeiters describe their product. The texture of the LV monogram leather is a hurdle that counterfeit manufacturers cannot overcome.

2. Look at the color and composition of the four-leaf flowers on the monogram leather.

The typical Monogram pattern from afar appears to be painted in one stroke of yellow-green, but upon closer inspection, you'll notice that the print consists of small dots forming diagonal lines. Up close, you'll see that the printed colors are actually made up of parallel diagonal lines, with the flower color being yellow-green, and the overall tone being very distinct.

For counterfeit goods above the super-A level, the flower logo is generally composed of diagonal stripes, but the colors are often incorrect, mostly leaning towards brown. Some colors are closer, but due to the overall pattern, from afar, the colors always appear blurred. Please note that this second method can be used as one of the identification criteria, but should not be solely relied upon because current counterfeit goods replicate the patterns quite realistically, and the flower colors are relatively close to those of genuine bags.

For example, some people take pictures with their phones or under evening lights, which may result in color discrepancies. That is to say, if you buy an LV bag and see the color on the right in bright light, it can be determined as a counterfeit. The left image is authentic, the right image is counterfeit.

3. Look at the hardware components.

Typically, the hardware on genuine LV bags from the store, such as the zipper pull, is not a very bright gold yellow but rather a greenish-golden copper color, slightly darker, with a matte-like texture. Of course, this cannot be generalized since many new models now feature dazzling bright hardware. Here we discuss the most common old-style classic bags. Below, using a super-A zipper pull as an example, we illustrate the differences between the two. The left image shows the hardware piece of a counterfeit bag, where the color is already quite close to the greenish-golden copper color touted by super-A sellers, but it's still too shiny. The one on the right, which is darker, is genuine. Due to lighting conditions in the photo, the actual item doesn't have such a heavy matte feel; it has better brightness but isn't as glaringly bright as the one on the left. The biggest difference lies in the "LV" letters, which on genuine items are more three-dimensional and neat, with sharper edges, while on counterfeit items, besides being too shiny, the engraved "LV" feels overly smooth and lacks definition.

Fake hardware can never compare to genuine items. Locks especially reveal this. The left is a counterfeit, the right is authentic.

4. Look at the matching leather.

Some suggest checking the anti-counterfeiting line and the water-drop darkening test. This point is actually achieved by super-A level and above counterfeits. So details must be examined. First, consider the white original leather thin roll border around the bag connecting to the monogram (such as pillow bags, shell bags, etc.). Genuine LV products typically have fine, straight, round, and three-dimensional borders, uniformly neat without breaks, no visible seams, no twisting or deformation issues.

Secondly, the genuine leather shoulder strap. Even for cross-body bags, LV shoulder straps are seamless throughout, requiring high-quality leather and resulting in significant leather waste, making it difficult for general counterfeits to achieve this. Additionally, the handles of handbags. Genuine handles are smooth and wrinkle-free, while slightly inferior counterfeits often have wrinkles at the bends, though some super-A bags have managed to eliminate these wrinkles. Some suggest judging by color, but this is the most misleading point. Since LV boutiques of the same model may not achieve exactly the same leather color at different times, and oxidation over time results in varying shades, it's known that this type of leather changes color even if unused and stored for long periods.

Matching leather isn't particularly easy to identify from photos, especially when it has turned honey-colored. However, for new bags, especially the handles of handbags, judgment can be made by touch. Genuine new bags' matching leather feels somewhat like raw silk, slightly rougher, with visibly prominent pores and no shine. With regular use, the leather pores close, proper maintenance gives it a sheen, and it becomes more supple. On the other hand, counterfeit bags' matching leather, even if wrinkle-free when new, tends to be shinier, having a resilient but not soft feel. The image below shows the detailed leather texture and neatly stitched lines on a new pillow bag.

5. Look at the stitching and edge sealing details of the matching leather.

Not only are LV's stitching lines neat and symmetrical, but they are also waxed hemp-cotton threads, serving as an identifiable detail. All LV products have thick and resilient stitching at equal intervals. Flipping open the inner handle closure of an LV handbag, the fixed position's leather attachment is also correctly placed. Current counterfeits may manage the exterior well, but it's difficult for them to achieve such meticulousness inside. Previously, it was said that stitches could differentiate, with fake bags typically having straight lines, while genuine bags have angles on each stitch resembling connected willow leaves. However, some super-A and above counterfeits have now managed to replicate this angle, so this point alone isn't sufficient and can only serve as a standard for assessing the precision of genuine and counterfeit bags. From the comparison of used genuine and counterfeit bags below, the difference in stitching is minimal, but the feel is slightly different. The counterfeit leather feels harder and shinier, while the genuine leather is softer. Additionally, observing the stitching of new genuine and counterfeit bags reveals differences, though this applies only to new items and serves as a reference. Genuine items are yellow (refer to the fourth point's image), while counterfeit items are goose-yellow or very bright yellow. This distinction is typically more apparent in new items, but once used, it becomes difficult to distinguish by thread color alone.

Additionally, observe the edge sealing of the matching leather, though this point is best observed on newer items. New genuine and counterfeit items show more noticeable differences. Genuine bags use brown-red edge sealing paint, while some denim styles or more limited editions have slightly lighter sealing colors. Counterfeit bags always appear too light and orange. Besides color, the depth of the sealing paint is even and does not overflow. Pay special attention to the connection between the leather handle and metal buckle; the sealing and stitching remain even despite changes in thickness and shape. Counterfeit items often expose flaws in this detail.

Counterfeit edge sealing uses a very red color or is too pale, even if brown, the color always seems awkward. Additionally, some areas may have uneven sealing, with color spilling out. However, if a bag is used for a long time, distinguishing by color becomes difficult, revealing that older bags can obscure many details. Thus, particular attention should be paid to some online second-hand bags that pass off fakes as genuine.

From the counterfeit bag shown below, it's evident that this monogram leather bag is meticulously replicated, but the color of the edge sealing exposes its counterfeit nature.

6. Look at the serial number.

LV handbags print production numbers indicating the factory and year of manufacture in inconspicuous positions, usually consisting of two letters and four digits. Each LV handbag has one, and production years differ. Bag styles vary, so serial numbers differ accordingly. Counterfeit items may have serial numbers, but often the same serial number is used across multiple items from the same counterfeit factory. Note that some genuine items produced before 1987 do not have these numbers. Not all LV products are made in France; Spain, Italy, the United States, and Germany also produce LV items. Therefore, even if the product is not made in France, it does not necessarily mean it is a counterfeit. Regardless of origin, the quality of genuine LV bags remains consistent. Moreover, not all French-made items have serial numbers starting with "SP." Generally, serial numbers consist of two letters and four digits, representing the place of origin and production date. The first and third digits indicate the month, while the second and fourth digits indicate the production year. An exception is that recently, many new bags have changed the numbering sequence. For bags purchased after the end of 2006, the second and fourth digits still indicate the production year; however, the first and third digits indicate the week of production within that year. For example, "1097" does not indicate the 19th month of 2007 but instead indicates the 19th week of production.

Below is a list for reference to identify based on serial numbers (letters represent the country of origin):

France: A0.A1.A2.AA.AN.AR.AO.AS.Al.AC.BA.BU.CA.CI.CT.ET.FA.FH.FL.FR.LM.LO.MB.MI.MO.MX.NO.ND.OS.OU.RA SD. SL SN..SP.SR.TH.VI.VR.VX etc.

Spain: CA(EPI etc)LM.LO.MO(M-color)

Italy: SA usually makes clothing, shoes, and accessories

USA: FH.FF.OS.SD

LV serial numbers vary by series but are usually found in the following locations:

At the stitching seam (e.g., bag bottom, upper-left or upper-right seam)

On the foldable LV leather inside

On the back of the trademark

At the base of the shoulder strap

For example, the serial number on this large bucket bag indicates it was produced in April 1923 in France.

Some people claim their bags are genuine because they have serial numbers. However, super-A and higher-level counterfeit bags also display serial numbers in the same position as the originals. For instance, this counterfeit denim backpack also has a stamped serial number. It's been observed that counterfeit manufacturers favor the number "SP0012."

7. State the bag size.

The final secret, which many may not know, is that on the outside of bags labeled "Louis Vuitton Paris Made in France," the genuine LV will always indicate the bag's size. For example, the back of this 30-inch bag displays the number "30." If it's a counterfeit, this detail will be missing.

Identification Tips:

The "F" in "Made in France" is always capitalized; some counterfeit items use lowercase "f."

All accessories or lock heads bear the LV logo.

LV's Monogram fabric is custom-made canvas with fire-resistant material, and touching it provides a textured feel. Counterfeits have a dull, smooth texture.

LV items have hidden serial numbers at the edges of the leather seams, indicating the manufacturing location and date.

Chanel handbags are simple and elegant, pairing perfectly with outfits, but their high prices deter many consumers. Exploiting this psychology, counterfeiters have produced numerous inexpensive fake Chanel handbags.

1. Leather Quality

Most Chanel bags are made from lambskin (or calfskin), with a darker, textured feel. Chanel bags maintain a stiff form without collapsing. Especially lambskin bags, they offer a good tactile experience and carry an original leather scent. Fake Chanel bags don't use lambskin; their leather reflects more light and has poorer elasticity. The double-C logo on the Chanel Cambon series is crafted from snakeskin, providing a strong texture.

2. Logo

Genuine Chanel logos are mostly gold-plated and thickly coated. Fake logos have thinner plating, appearing brighter but prone to fading.

3. Zippers

All genuine Chanel zippers have a jewel-themed hook at the chain end, usually either a lion's head or the famous double-C design. Genuine zippers are heavier, with lamb leather separating the closures and double-stitched seams. Fake Chanel zippers show glue marks at the closure.

4. Interior

Genuine Chanel bags display gold-embossed "Chanel" and "Made in France/Italy" inside. The bag has a small sticker with a computer-printed number matching the card and original box. Fake stickers show signs of tampering, with numbers typed by a typewriter.

Tips for Identifying Genuine Chanel Bags:

1. Check the leather smell and stitching consistency of Chanel lambskin bags.

2. Ensure the anti-counterfeiting laser sticker number on the interior matches the guarantee card.

3. Lambskin and crocodile-pattern leather are the most commonly copied Chanel materials. Both diamond-pattern bags and CoCo classic bags have smooth, non-abrasive metallic parts.

4. Genuine Chanel bags maintain a stiff form without collapsing, especially lambskin ones, offering a good tactile experience and an original leather scent. Counterfeits aren't made from lambskin.

5. The strongest proof of authenticity involves a fluorescent anti-counterfeiting sticker inside the bag. The sticker number must match the guarantee card number for the bag to be genuine.

Gucci's most counterfeited item is the GG LOGO patterned denim tote bag. Any item with the GG LOGO pattern, whether a tote or wallet, is a popular target for counterfeiters.

1. Craftsmanship

Genuine Gucci craftsmanship is extremely precise, from the weave pattern to stitching and lining, everything is neat and clean. Counterfeit Gucci workmanship is subpar; check if the G pattern weaving is neat, all stitching is precise, endings are clean, and the leather folds or cutting lines are flat. These are areas where fakes often fail.

2. Material

Genuine Gucci nylon fabric feels stiff and substantial. Counterfeit Gucci nylon fabric is thinner and lacks texture.

3. Serial Number

Genuine Gucci labels inside the lining will print "Gucci" and "Made in Italy," with a clear serial number behind the label, which is the identity mark of genuine Gucci.

Genuine Gucci handbags come with the following attachments:

1. Product manual

2. Dust bag

3. Purchase certificate

4. GUCCI paper bag or 5. Paper box (with a long product model barcode on the side)

"The Devil Wears Prada" indeed added considerable charm to the PRADA brand. However, the market is flooded with Prada counterfeits. Below are five points for consumers to distinguish authenticity:

1. Nylon

Genuine Prada nylon is made from parachute material, which is heat-resistant, thick, stiff, and flexible, with low reflectivity and visible diagonal weave under a magnifying glass. Fake Prada uses ordinary chemical fibers, either too light and thin without texture, or too thick and hard with poor feel. Their surfaces are either very smooth or extremely dull, showing dot patterns under a magnifying glass.

2. Leather

Genuine Prada uses cowhide, lambskin, or even deerskin and crocodile skin, with obvious leather grain and unique leather scent. Fake Prada leather is harder and has fewer grains.

3. Triangle Logo

The metal triangle logo of genuine Prada is centrally located on the bag, with evenly spaced stitching around the edges. The font on the logo has a tactile impression. The metal logo of fake Prada may not be central, with rough stitching and uneven stitches.

4. Zippers

The metal zippers of genuine Prada are heavier, while those of fake Prada are much lighter.

5. Straps

The nylon straps of genuine Prada are elastic, with nylon surfaces resistant to pilling and featuring diagonal weave. Fake Prada straps are thin, lacking elasticity, and have no diagonal weave.

Additional tips for identifying genuine Prada:

1. New genuine items have a distinctive leather scent due to Prada's special leather preservative, impossible to replicate due to its high cost. You can verify the scent at a boutique.

2. Prada stores do not sell factory-leaked goods or parallel imports. Prada's president insists on "Made in Prada," allowing no service or channel errors, thus eliminating factory leaks.
