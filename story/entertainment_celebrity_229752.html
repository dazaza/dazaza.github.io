Cinematic Origins

Cheng Kui-An (February 1, 1955 –), nicknamed "Big Fool" (Da Sha), was an original resident of Nam Wai in Sai Kung, Hong Kong. He was a film and television actor known for his tall stature and talent in playing villainous gangster roles. Cheng dropped out of school at the age of 13 and worked as a hired thug. In 1978, he was discovered by Stephen Chow and began acting in films. Cheng once ran as a candidate in the Hong Kong District Council elections in Sai Kung but was not elected. In recent years, he was diagnosed with nasopharyngeal cancer and underwent chemotherapy and radiation therapy.

Since being discovered by Stephen Chow in 1978, over the course of 23 years in the entertainment industry, Cheng has acted in over 350 films and more than 600 episodes of television dramas. In most of these productions, he predominantly played antagonistic characters. Even when he attempted roles such as a police officer or a father—roles that were more positive and amiable—he remained indelibly associated with the image of a "villain." Due to the nature of his roles, the nickname "Big Fool" became widely recognized, albeit reluctantly.

More than ten years ago, in John Woo's classic film "The Killer," Cheng's portrayal of the triad boss evoked intense hatred from viewers. At the end of the movie, his deliberate act of shooting out the eye of the assassin Xiao Zhuang (played by Chow Yun-fat) shattered Xiao Zhuang's final wish—donating his cornea to a singer named Jenny, whom he had accidentally injured during his career as a hitman. It’s safe to say that at this point, all viewers would have wanted to tear him apart. Thus, when Chief Inspector Li Ying (Stephen Chow) opted to be charged with murder rather than let "Big Fool" escape justice in front of the police, many in the audience applauded enthusiastically. Cheng's portrayal of the ruthless villain was so vivid and lifelike that the audience's applause was the highest form of praise for his masterful performance as a negative character.

In 1983, Cheng Kui-An first portrayed the role of "Big Fool" in the movie "Fortune Has Its Reasons," leaving a deep impression on the audience. To this day, "Big Fool" remains his synonymous name. Regarding his deeply ingrained image as a triad boss, Cheng once publicly clarified: "One should not judge a person by their appearance; I have been re-elected as the village chief of our village, which proves that I am a good person! Moreover, when I'm not filming, I stay at home, making me a 100% 'good man.'"

In movies, the leaders of the underworld are always surrounded by a swarm of underlings, projecting an air of authority. As a "man of the underworld," Cheng Kui-An was no exception. However, it turned out that the so-called friendships were built on money. Cheng enjoyed lavish gatherings with large groups of friends, generously spending his own money, which quickly depleted his finances. To maintain the demeanor of a "gangster boss," Cheng indulged in alcohol and smoked incessantly, often sharing cigarettes with his friends, consuming nearly two packs a day. Back then, Cheng lived a life filled with revelry and drinking, enjoying every moment.

In 1996, Cheng Kui-An started earning money on the mainland, reaching the peak of his career in 2003 and 2004, earning over a million yuan annually. However, a serious illness wiped out years of savings, but Cheng took it in stride, even feeling grateful to have survived. Reflecting on how he is still employed in his forties, he feels quite content. Cheng has a positive attitude, accepting everything with equanimity. Even though his current income is less than before, he faces it calmly, believing that as long as one's conscience is clear, that is enough. His father left behind a dilapidated house, which Cheng tore down and rebuilt into a three-story building where he lives happily with his family.
