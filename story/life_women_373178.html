1. Hot Compress

A hot compress is a direct and effective way to relieve discomfort. When you feel breast tenderness, you can wrap a hot water bottle with a towel and apply it to your chest. If the effect is not ideal, you can also prepare a basin of cold water, soak the towel in it, and then alternate between hot and cold compresses.

Additionally, you can use a castor oil hot compress because it contains substances that enhance lymphocyte function. Drop some castor oil onto gauze, then apply it to your face. To prevent staining your clothes, it's best to cover it with plastic film, and then use a hot water bottle or warm towel together for about an hour.

2. Well-fitted Bra

An undersized bra will press on the breasts, causing poor blood circulation and leading to tenderness, while an oversized bra won't provide enough support, potentially causing injury during movement.

3. Salt Reduction Campaign

In the 7-10 days before menstruation, high-salt foods should be avoided as they cause breast swelling. People who often eat at restaurants need to pay attention because restaurant food often has a lot of seasonings, making its salt content much higher than home-cooked meals. If you eat very salty stewed dishes or snacks, don't drink large amounts of water immediately because this won't neutralize the salt but instead allow it to seep into the bloodstream through liquids, putting pressure on the blood vessels.

4. Vitamins, Reducing Prolactin

Prolactin causes breast expansion, so you can consume more foods rich in Vitamin C and B vitamins to regulate prostaglandin E production and help suppress prolactin generation.

5. Avoiding Caffeine

Medical experiments have proven that giving up the daily habit of drinking coffee significantly improves breast tenderness. Of course, it's not just coffee; you should also stay away from chocolate, cola, tea, and even painkillers containing caffeine to alleviate the pain.

6. Low-fat High-fiber Foods Intake

In the week before menstruation, try eating more coarse grains, whole wheat, and other low-fat, high-fiber foods to improve the body's nutritional balance and alleviate tenderness.

7. Breast Massage to Relieve Pain

Stimulating around the breasts can promote blood and lymph circulation. In the 7-10 days before menstruation, massaging every night can help alleviate tenderness.

Method: First, gently enclose one breast with both palms from either side. Then, tighten both hands, using the thumbs at the base of the breast to lift it upward from below. Repeat 10 times on each side. Note that when the breasts are tender, it’s best not to massage them to avoid excessive stimulation.
