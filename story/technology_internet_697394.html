Sinopec's Guangxi branch responds to reports of problematic gasoline

by lovec4f9 on 2012-03-08 15:17:20

Following the emergence of the "stalling scandal" in Yunnan and Guizhou, car owners in some areas of Guangxi have recently experienced engine weakness, vibration, unusual noises, and even stalling after refueling at certain independent gas stations. Once again, the quality of refined oil has become a hot topic of social concern. Relevant experts and responsible persons from Sinopec Guangxi Petroleum Branch were interviewed regarding this issue.

From the symptoms of the vehicles involved in the "stalling scandal" in Guangxi, they are basically consistent with those in Guizhou. According to the sampling test results announced by the industrial and commercial departments of Guizhou Province and Guiyang City at the end of February, the 93-octane gasoline met the national standard GB17930-2011 for vehicle gasoline, meaning that it passed the inspection according to the national standard. However, if it meets the national standard, why did hundreds or even thousands of vehicles experience the same malfunction? Relevant experts stated that through testing the fuel extracted from the affected vehicles, they found that the main cause of the incident was: first, the unwashed gum content in the "problematic gasoline" was too high, with some gasoline having an unwashed gum content as high as 200mg/100mL, whereas the "National Fuel Standard" stipulates that the unwashed gum content should not exceed 30mg/100mL; secondly, the "problematic gasoline" contained chemical raw materials such as N-methylaniline, isobutyl acetate, pentane, ethylbenzene, and cyclopentadiene. These substances are not only toxic and harmful to the human body but can also cause rubber to swell, leading to vehicle malfunctions. Based on this analysis, it can be determined that these "problematic gasoline" are chemical blended oils. By visual inspection, light color is one of the characteristics of chemical blended oil. When gasoline containing these chemical raw materials burns, it can form adhesive gums or asphaltene on the car's intake valve, tappet, spark plug, and inside the cylinder, depositing on them. This causes the car's intake valve to close improperly or fail to open normally, clogs the spark plug, resulting in difficulties in starting the car, vibrations, weak acceleration, and other malfunctions.

To prevent chemical blended oil that meets the current national testing standards but still poses actual harm to consumers from entering the market, Sinopec issued a ban on external procurement of oil products in May 2011, known as the "three non-procurement" order. That is: no procurement if the oil product's consuming enterprise cannot be confirmed, no procurement if the oil source traced back three levels cannot clearly identify the consuming enterprise or if the consuming enterprise is not within the specified directory, and no procurement if the supplier does not provide a qualified quality inspection report. At the same time, based on the 20 detection indicators in the current national gasoline standard GB17930-2011, Sinopec added seven internal quality detection indicators, including "unwashed gum" and "N-methylaniline," which are newly added detection indicators by Sinopec itself. In this way, source management has been achieved, strictly ensuring the quality control of oil products.

According to relevant responsible persons from Sinopec Guangxi Petroleum Branch, during this period, they did not receive any valid complaints due to oil quality issues, and their operations were not affected in any way. This responsible person said that Sinopec Guangxi Company regards oil quality as the lifeblood of the enterprise and fully implements the ISO9000 quality management system. In the links of oil product warehousing, storage, outbound, transportation, and sales, they implement full-staff, full-process, and all-round monitoring and management. In every oil quality supervision and random inspection conducted by government departments, the company's oil quality was entirely qualified.

It is reported that after the recent emergence of "problem oil" in some areas of Northeast China, PetroChina also urgently suspended the external procurement of blended oil.
