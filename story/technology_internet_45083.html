08 Internet Market Has a Broad Prospect, Spring Festival Promotion Warms up for the Olympic Year

by now on 2008-01-26 10:06:13

According to a forecast released by the consultancy Sedai on the 22nd at the "2008 Annual Conference of China Internet Market", China's internet market scale will reach 163.5 billion yuan in 2008, an increase of 40% compared to last year. For China's internet industry, this is encouraging news. With the year coming to an end and Spring Festival approaching, all internet merchants are taking advantage of this opportunity to launch a series of New Year promotional activities, warming up the internet market for 2008.

Spring Festival is China's traditional festival. Therefore, every year at this time, businesses conduct large-scale promotional activities. The internet industry is no different. Many businesses have launched a series of promotional activities to attract consumer attention. Recently, after launching successive lottery events with cash prizes and partnering with Kuaiqian to give New Year gifts, Now Interconnect (www.now.cn) has grandly launched a "Spend 300 Get 100 Free" activity, increasing the level of discounts and becoming a highlight of the entire internet market.

In recent years, with the rise of enterprise informatization construction and personal website building trends, China's internet has developed vigorously, forming a huge market for internet basic service industries. It is reported that China has more than 42 million small and medium-sized enterprises, but those with websites do not exceed one million households. The website ownership rate for enterprises is less than 2%. Additionally, with the deepening development of Web 2.0 and the rise of personal grassroots sites, the prospects for the website construction market are broad. Among these, the demand development momentum for virtual hosting and enterprise mailboxes is the most rapid. After conducting market research, businesses clearly saw this point, thus making their discount promotions very targeted. Regarding the "Spend 300 Get 100 Free" activity by Now Interconnect, the scope of use for the discount coupons targets the market's hot demand products - virtual hosting and enterprise mailboxes. Therefore, it is highly attractive to a wide range of customers.

With the Beijing Olympics drawing near, China's internet market is gradually heating up. A series of promotional actions by internet merchants before the year also carries a very clear signal: the internet market in 2008 will undoubtedly be booming, and competition will be even more intense. All merchants will use all their skills to strive for the high ground in the 2008 internet market.
