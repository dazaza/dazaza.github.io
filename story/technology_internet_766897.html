Xu Liangjie: The adjustment is part of Sina Weibo's IPO plan

by anonymous on 2013-10-02 10:16:17

After its alliance with Alibaba, Sina Weibo reached a new climax, and recently rumors of an "IPO" have surfaced again. Abroad, social media platform Twitter has recently announced its intention to go public via its official website and secretly submitted an S-1 form containing its IPO plans to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). What will be the future direction of Sina Weibo?

Yesterday afternoon, Charles Xu, Co-President and Chief Technology Officer of Sina, told South China Morning Post and other media that Twitter's IPO is a "positive" factor for the industry. If no company in the industry succeeds (in going public), everyone would question whether its business model is recognized. As for the internal business adjustments within Sina since the beginning of this year, they are part of the (IPO) plan, but the specific timing is not convenient to disclose.

The IPO rumors have been rampant

Since the beginning of this year, news about Sina's internal business adjustments to prepare Weibo for its IPO has been circulating in the industry. The latest reports suggest that Sina Weibo is already preparing for its IPO, such as starting to transfer employee relations, with plans to officially launch the IPO agenda early next year.

A reporter from South China Morning Post learned from inside Sina that the relationship between Sina Portal and Sina Weibo employees was unclear in the past, but since the beginning of the year, some employees have been required to clearly define their employment contracts under the Microdream Creative Network Technology (China) Co., Ltd.

It is understood that Microdream Creative Network Technology (China) Co., Ltd. was established on October 11, 2010, with David Cao as the legal representative, and it is one of the two independent subsidiaries Sina created for Weibo. The other is Beijing Microdream Creative Network Technology Co., Ltd., with a registered capital of 10 million yuan, established on August 9, 2010, and Peng Shaobin as the legal representative.

In addition, at the beginning of this year, the Music Division of Sina was also merged into the Weibo Division. In the newly announced recruitment ratio for fresh graduates in 2014, Weibo accounts for about 75%, while the portal accounts for about 25%. Sina will comprehensively strengthen its technical talent reserve to promote the overall transformation into an internet platform.

The establishment of an independent corporate structure, adjustments to divisions, and clear division of personnel have led the industry to speculate that the spin-off listing of Sina Weibo is approaching.

Yesterday, Charles Xu, who attended the Sina 2014 campus recruitment event at Sun Yat-sen University in Guangzhou, told South China Morning Post that Sina wants to transition from a first-generation portal to a product-driven platform company. Currently, the business is divided into portal and Weibo sectors. He denied the layoffs in the portal business rumored by outsiders, believing it to be just an internal business adjustment within Sina. "Some employees of the portal have participated in the construction of Weibo over the past few years. What people see is just a business adjustment. Currently, Sina has more than 5000 employees, and the number is increasing, with Weibo and the portal each accounting for half."

Regarding the rumors of the IPO, Charles Xu stated that the business adjustment is part of the (IPO) plan, but the specific time and detailed plan are not convenient to announce, and the IPO will be a "natural progression".

However, during the interview, Charles Xu proactively mentioned the imminent IPO of Twitter. He believed that Twitter has been around longer than Weibo, with good revenue growth and successful commercialization attempts. Twitter's IPO is a "positive factor" for this industry. "If no company in the industry succeeds (in going public), then everyone would question whether its business model is recognized. You can see that in 2000, the first-generation portals Sina, Sohu, and NetEase all went public within one or two months. At the time, people said only one could survive, but now all three are doing well."

Sina Weibo secures its mobile ticket

In April this year, Alibaba and Sina Weibo announced a strategic cooperation agreement. The Alibaba Group purchased preferred shares and common shares issued by Sina Weibo Company for $586 million, accounting for approximately 18% of the fully diluted post-IPO shares of Weibo Company.

According to both parties' forecasts, this strategic cooperation is expected to bring Sina Weibo approximately $380 million in marketing and social e-commerce revenue over the next three years. However, based on Sina Weibo's current revenue situation, its loss-making state has not changed due to Alibaba's entry.

Charles Xu believed this is because Weibo is still in the "investment phase." He emphasized that Weibo's revenue is considerable, but the data has not yet been disclosed.

"We are very fortunate to secure our mobile ticket, although I agree with Pony Ma that no company has secured standing or sitting tickets, but in terms of installation volume, Weibo is indeed one of the most installed APPs, and WeChat and Weibo will coexist," he said.

He proposed that currently, 70% of Weibo traffic comes from mobile devices, so the group strategy since the beginning of the year has been mobile-focused. "In the future, the portal and Weibo should play complementary roles," Charles Xu denied that the portal has entered a difficult period, stating it is merely "facing a crossroads, the next generation's challenge opportunity." "What we least want to see is competition between the two, transferring the portal's customers to Weibo doesn't add much commercial value to the company."

Written by: Xie Rui, South China Morning Post
