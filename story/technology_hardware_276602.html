Engineer Old Hu's Eco-home

by tfbgfnbv on 2009-11-25 09:06:42

Last week, Engineer Mr. Hu called Hangbao and expressed his wish to renovate a newly acquired 90 square meter affordable housing unit into an ecological home. Regarding the functions of this ecological home, Mr. Hu provided a very specific description: installing a water circulation system in the sunroom to conserve water resources and achieve water recycling; transforming the balcony into an air-regulating sunroom by planting some green plants to absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen; if possible, installing solar panels on the roof to achieve environmental protection and energy saving effects...

Many people told Mr. Hu that such an ecological home is too technically challenging to be realized. However, after interviewing several energy-saving professionals, the reporter found that although it's currently impossible to fully meet Engineer Hu's requirements, some of his ideas for energy-saving renovations in the sunroom are still feasible with slight modifications.

For instance, sandstone relief fiberglass products can regulate the air in the sunroom. Ren Weizhong from Anji, who last year used his self-developed "ecological house" to help rebuild areas in Sichuan affected by the earthquake, stated that the function of the sunroom is not limited to using plants to adjust indoor air quality. Making the balcony fully transparent with glass and sealing it, such as using double-glazed windows or lockable windows, the sunroom can completely transform into a constant temperature room for collecting sunlight heat. This is because large-area glass increases the lighting area, allowing the sunroom to absorb sunlight heat during the day and supply warm air to the room at night.
