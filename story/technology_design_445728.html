The automotive industry is one of the pillar industries of China's economic development. In recent years, both automobile production and sales have maintained rapid growth; the overall market share of the domestic automobile industry has also shown an upward trend. At the same time as the rapid development of the automobile market, China's bus market has also shown a steady forward momentum.

In the first half of 2008, despite being affected by major natural disasters, due to the high-speed growth of the macroeconomy, the overall demand for the bus market in our country maintained stable growth, and the monthly sales volume of buses in June set a new historical record. After entering the second half of the year, the international financial crisis spread globally, and the production and sales of buses significantly decreased, and the overseas export market suffered a significant impact. From January to December 2008, China produced a cumulative total of 337,100 buses (including chassis), and sold a cumulative total of 340,600 units, with both production and sales showing a declining trend.

In the first half of 2009, the bus industry faced severe challenges. On the one hand, the domestic tourism and rural passenger transport market demands were not smooth, and on the other hand, the improvement in railway operational efficiency occupied a considerable portion of the road passenger transport market share. At the same time, the significant decline in exports exacerbated the sluggish state of the industry. Affected by this, the demand for buses significantly dropped. After entering the third quarter of 2009, the bus market began to gradually warm up. Judging from the sales volume of July and August, China’s bus market has returned to the level of 2007.

The public bus market continues to see large-scale order tenders, becoming a major force driving the warming of the bus market. With economic development, more and more cities need to update their public buses to improve air quality and living conditions for residents. Additionally, after the implementation of the "Classification and Rating of Operating Buses" in 2002, passenger transport companies updated a large number of vehicles. Now, these vehicles are gradually entering the phase of scrapping and updating.

The recovery of demand for public buses and road buses will drive the improvement of domestic bus demand. With the gradual recovery of the international economic situation, bus exports will also gradually warm up. As the development environment changes, the trend of technological upgrades, and further intensification of competition require the bus industry to perfect its industrial structure and accelerate the pace of transformation and upgrading. In the future, China's bus industry will enter a new adjustment period.

Compared with other细分markets, intercity short-distance passenger transport markets, rural passenger transport markets, public transport markets, unit reception bus markets, and tourist bus markets are all markets with traditional advantages. Bus companies should focus on these markets as key areas for enterprise development, invest more marketing costs, and develop products suitable for the needs of segmented markets. Especially for the rural passenger transport market, national policy support is one aspect, and another aspect is that there are many old vehicles in the rural passenger transport market, with strong replacement requirements, offering broad market prospects.
