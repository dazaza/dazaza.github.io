Suit Pairing Doesn't Have to Be Rigid

Two or Three Buttons Are Safest

The Beijing News: What kind of suits do you think suit Chinese men?

Gustavo: The body types of men in northern China are very similar to those of Europeans, so someone looking for a suit in an international brand store can also find a well-fitting suit in a brand store in China. Nowadays, China's equipment and craftsmanship are all imported, and many Chinese brands have improved the details of Chinese suits such as shoulder width, sleeve length, and back design. Therefore, I believe that Chinese-made suit brands would be more suitable for the Chinese physique.

The Beijing News: Some men are confused about how many buttons a suit has. Is this just a matter of design, or are there different considerations for different occasions?

Gustavo: From the perspective of traditional suit culture, there are many versions of the origin of the button when it comes to suits. However, from a contemporary fashionable standpoint, we can discard all the rigid traditional rules because clothing is also a part of you. Conservatively speaking, two or three buttons are the safest options, especially suitable for job interviews. This applies regardless of whether you're in China or Europe and America, and in any occasion.

A Good Suit Feels Unrestricted

The Beijing News: How do you judge if a suit is a "good" suit?

Gustavo: Judging the quality of a suit mainly depends on its cut, craftsmanship, and fabric. A good cut is comfortable and fits well, for example, when you stretch your elbow forward, you won’t feel any pulling sensation under the armpit, and when your hands are down, the suit won't feel bulky. Any change in the sleeve length or shoulder width will affect the overall fit, so a good suit makes everything feel comfortable. Personally, I have particularly strict requirements for stitching and lining quality. So talking about a "good suit", it requires an excellent production line, but without mature handcraft skills and good equipment processes, it's impossible to fully achieve this. Although China's wool industry is very developed, and many fabrics are imported from abroad, China can produce suits of quality similar to foreign ones at a lower cost. However, regarding the maintenance and care of suits, China still needs some awareness.

When pairing suits, you don't necessarily have to be so "rigid". Besides pairing with shirts, you can boldly pair them with sweaters or T-shirts. You don't have to wear a tie with a suit; wearing a scarf can also look great. In fact, people can borrow more from runway styling and street photography abroad, or innovate and try themselves. Every combination will have its own characteristics and trends.
