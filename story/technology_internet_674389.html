Here we would like to remind car owners, especially those who like to follow trends, to focus on practicality, pay attention to the quality of materials and construction when decorating your car.

Points to note:

1. Use environmentally safe materials

Laying down floor mats is originally for keeping the car clean. If you use cheap mats to save money, the toxic substances can exceed standards, causing air pollution inside the car. It would be better to use the original carpet and buy a set of foot mats to lay on top, with the same effect. Moreover, if the size of the floor mat is not suitable, it can easily get dirty, and over time, bacteria may grow, making it even harder to clean.

When using foot mats, one should also be cautious. Foot mats can easily pollute the car environment. Cotton and linen foot mats in spring and summer if not cleaned on time, can easily produce bacteria. Some cheap plastic foot mats have strong odors, and their quality is hard to guarantee. Therefore, we suggest that car owners choose these items from reputable manufacturers that produce qualified products.

2. Decorating the car should be moderate

Cartoon characters are popular, so we often see some new cars, especially those owned by women, with cartoon figures hanging all over the dashboard, rear windshield, and interior rearview mirror. These things may look cute, but they could very likely obstruct the driver's view. When driving, hanging small decorations will sway with the movement of the vehicle, affecting the driver's ability to judge and respond to emergencies, possibly leading to traffic accidents. The cartoon items placed on the seats might roll around with the car's movements, distracting the driver. So Liangdun Car Products suggests that new car owners should decorate their cars moderately with cartoons and dolls.

3. Maintain performance, new installations should surpass the original ones, otherwise, it’s better not to install them at all

The fabric of a new car's interior [http://www.taobis.com] has been carefully designed and manufactured. The original fabric usually has anti-static, wear-resistant, breathable, flame-retardant, and fireproof properties. Therefore, when choosing replacements, they should be better than the original. If you don't use the original parts and instead install something that doesn't meet the standards, it would be a waste.

For example, the steering wheel. In designing a car, its size, material, and comfort when gripped, etc., all consider ergonomic requirements. If it still feels uncomfortable, adding a light and thin steering wheel cover is acceptable. However, never add thick or large steering wheel covers that look pretty, as they might cause safety issues. Therefore, when decorating the car, new car owners should keep in mind that the main idea should be to enhance the performance while maintaining the original components' functions rather than just for aesthetics. If the added items don't meet the standards, it's better to directly use the original ones, which are safer. After all, safety is the primary concern we need to ensure.

4. External decoration, safety and legality

Decorations that alter the original appearance structure of the car should be prohibited. One reason is that it buries potential safety hazards, and another is that it might violate relevant automobile safety regulations. As for decorative car stickers, they shouldn't be too flashy; they should only serve to beautify and embellish.
