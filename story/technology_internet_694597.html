The Ministry of Railways responds to the resumption of work in the railways by saying that there is no arrears of construction payments

by king3h0a on 2012-03-07 10:37:08

At the Guangzhou group discussion meeting yesterday, in response to representatives' proposals regarding "[railway projects resuming work each month resulting in a loss of 10 billion yuan]" and "[railway investment shrinking from 700 billion yuan per year to 400 billion yuan]", Wu Xun, a national people's congress representative and executive deputy director of the Railway Engineering Management Center of the Ministry of Railways, responded that last year's 406 billion yuan investment included 41 high-speed rail projects, and the overall infrastructure investment in railways increased by 75% compared to the [Eleventh Five-Year Plan].

Resumption of work results in monthly losses exceeding 1 billion yuan

National People's Congress representative Mao Guifen had previously conducted a series of surveys on high-speed rail enterprises. At yesterday's group discussion meeting, she indicated that after the [July 23 Yongwen EMU accident] last year, railway infrastructure investment shrank from the previous standard of 700 billion yuan per year to 400 billion yuan. Currently, railway funds have been drastically reduced, with individual arrival rates being only 30% to 40%. Data shows that domestic projects suspended due to broken capital chains have exceeded 10,000 kilometers, and the entire industry incurs losses of over 1 billion yuan per month due to the suspension of work. She appealed: "[For projects already under construction, there should be a guarantee of adequate funding]."

No arrears in engineering payments

In response to this, Wu Xun, a representative and executive deputy director of the Railway Engineering Management Center of the Ministry of Railways who recently transferred to the Guangzhou delegation, responded that his suggestions brought forward this year include "[increasing central government fiscal investment in public-interest railway projects]", "[establishing a subsidy mechanism for public-interest factors in railways]", etc.

He also suggested studying the establishment of a stable long-term mechanism for ensuring the source of funds for railway construction and development. Following market-oriented and commercial principles, he encouraged and guided various social and private capitals to invest in railway construction to broaden the channels for raising funds for railway construction.

He admitted that there was indeed a period of financial difficulty in the second half of last year and at the beginning of this year, with some projects experiencing suspension of work. Currently, through coordination and support from the State Council, National Development and Reform Commission, Ministry of Finance, and other departments, all construction funds that should have been paid before the Spring Festival have been fully disbursed, and there are no issues of overdue engineering payments.

He stated that this year's 406 billion yuan compared to last year's 600 billion yuan may seem less, but overall investment has increased by 75% compared to the [Eleventh Five-Year Plan]. "[This is still a relatively large investment amount for a country.]"

Building 41 high-speed rail projects this year

Representative Wu Xun introduced that high-speed rail is constructed according to the national railway [Twelfth Five-Year Plan] development plan. This year, following the principle proposed by the new Party Leadership Group of the Ministry of Railways of "[ensuring ongoing projects, implementing necessary ones, and emphasizing supporting facilities]," high-speed rail construction will maintain an "appropriate scale," and by the end of the [Twelfth Five-Year Plan], China's railways will reach 120,000 kilometers.

Regarding this year's scale of railway investment, he cited data showing that the total railway construction investment for the year is 406 billion yuan, of which 405 billion yuan is for large and medium-sized projects, involving a total of 249 projects, including 41 passenger-dedicated lines (high-speed rail) projects.

It is reported that China plans to complete and put into operation 3,500 kilometers of high-speed rail this year, accounting for 55% of the total mileage of new lines planned for operation throughout the year. By then, China's high-speed rail mileage will exceed 10,000 kilometers. Previously, media reports indicated that due to limited and narrow financing channels, this year's high-speed rail construction might fall into a debt crisis and significantly "slow down."
