Austriamicrosystems introduces new rhythm LED driver.

by ledlamp8 on 2012-02-26 23:17:05

Austriamicrosystems (AMS) has launched the AS3665, which supports mobile phones and other consumer applications, showcasing amazing lighting effects. The AS3665 will achieve sharper colors, smoother color transitions, and high brightness with minimal design effort. This new rhythm lamp LED driver enhances the performance of mobile phones, MP3 players, laptops, energy-saving lamps, portable game consoles, LED bulbs, speakers, toys, and any audio device, making them even more eye-catching.

The AS3665 features 9 LED channels, with each channel capable of up to 25mA current. Each channel has 12 independent pulse width modulation (PWM) controls, offering unparalleled smooth color effects and achieving linear effects based on the number and proportion of changes. The PWM generator has three sortings and 1.5Kb of memory, allowing for the storage and processing of astonishingly complex light patterns without requiring an external processor or interactive intervention.

AS3665 directive-based programming makes it easy to create a variety of light patterns. To maintain true color, the AS3665 uses various color correction features, such as automatic RGB color correction according to temperature.

The AS3665's audio synchronization feature enables colorful, programmable lights. With this feature, color LEDs can utilize the device’s audio input, such as music or ringtones, to produce smooth and timely flashes of light and shade changes or color shifts. The AS3665 is based on music input synchronized LED. Audio synchronization light modes can be freely adjusted to achieve different effects, defining unique color responses that fit any type of music, from heavy metal rock to gentle melodies.

To extend battery life, the AS3665 design includes various power-saving features. With a drive current of 10mA, the pressure difference in each channel is less than 50mV. An efficient 150mA charge pump power supply can automatically switch between 1:1, 1:1.5, and 1:2 modes. To further optimize power consumption, there are nine channels with three programmable power supplies for driving RGBLEDs, allowing selection between battery or CP supply for higher efficiency, i.e., using separate power sources for red and blue/green LEDs.

To accelerate system implementation, the AS3665 offers pre-design capabilities with minimal software programming. Additionally, provided software tools support rapid optical mode command development, making development as simple as drawing a line on paper. The software output is a compiled file, ready for immediate use with the AS3665.

The AS3665 uses a small 2.6 × 2.6mm 25-bump WL-CSP package, requiring minimal external components.
