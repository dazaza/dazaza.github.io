Property management withdraws causing nearly 500 households to lose water supply, citing significant unpaid fees by the owners.

by hj1718004 on 2011-08-15 21:06:21

Yesterday, there was a water outage in buildings 19 and 20 at Fadou Jinchani village, and residents were seen carrying various containers to fetch water. This photo was taken by reporter Han Meng.

Beijing News (reporter Lu Man) - Starting yesterday, the property management of buildings 19 and 20 in Chaoyang District's Jinchani village ceased the operation of water pumps, causing nearly 500 households to lose water supply. The residents had no choice but to carry buckets and queue up to get water. The property management stated that due to unpaid property fees, the company is struggling financially and decided last year to withdraw from managing the area, officially pulling out yesterday.

Residents left their homes to queue for water.

At 17:00 yesterday, in buildings 19 and 20 of Jinchani village, many residents could be seen entering and exiting with water buckets. In front of the tap located in the property office on the first floor of building 19, a long queue of residents waiting to fetch water formed. A resident surnamed Wang from building 20 said that at 10:00 yesterday morning, the water pump and elevator stopped running. After an emergency call from a sick resident living on a higher floor, the elevator temporarily resumed operation. "Now, there's no water above the fourth floor, how are we supposed to live?"

Inside the property office, a notice read: "This community has a severe issue with unpaid property fees, leaving our company in dire straits and unable to continue providing property services; it has been decided to terminate services at 10:30 AM on May 23, 2011." Signed by Beijing Hengte Property Management Co., Ltd.

Regarding this notice, several owners expressed that due to dissatisfaction with the quality of property services, indeed some owners have not paid their property fees, "but cutting off water to the entire neighborhood is unreasonable," they said.

Years of losses lead to property management withdrawal

Mr. Cui Fulin, responsible for the daily management of the community, stated that since 2008, the collection rate of property fees has been decreasing in a trapezoidal manner, reaching only 30% by the end of 2010. "In four years, the company has lost over one million yuan." Regarding the issues of service quality raised by the owners, Cui Fulin said that the main items of property services have basically met the standards. For the specific service projects where owners have complaints, coordination meetings have been held with the owners but no consensus was reached.

Cui Fulin said that due to operational difficulties, the company decided last year to withdraw from management and has processed relevant procedures with the appropriate departments. At the end of November last year, the decision to withdraw was publicly announced in the community. According to the plan, from March 1 to May 22 this year, the community entered a low-cost operation phase, and starting from May 23, formal withdrawal began.

Regarding the inconvenience caused by the water outage, Cui Fulin stated that due to the lack of new property management taking over and no homeowners' committee in place, there was no way to hand over the responsibilities. "We have already done all we could."

The residents' committee will look for a new property management.

Yesterday, the director of the residents' committee, Zhou Xuan, stated that a meeting was held to initiate the establishment of a homeowners' committee, but currently, no owners have registered.

At around 18:30 yesterday, the secretary of the residents' committee, Sun Jingrong, stated that after multiple rounds of coordination, the property management company has agreed to temporarily restore water supply and elevator operations. Moving forward, the residents' committee will find a new property management company to take over or implement self-governance by the residents.
