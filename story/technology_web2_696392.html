by zhst64934 on 2012-03-08 02:42:37

On March 7th, Beijing time, the first round of Group G in the 2012 AFC Champions League season saw Tianjin Teda (Weibo) held to a 0-0 draw by Central Coast Mariners from the A-League at their home ground. The stadium, with temperatures as low as zero degrees Celsius, seemed even colder due to a dull match. For Teda, not achieving the expected opening victory was somewhat frustrating, but Cao Yang's (Weibo) post-match interview still revealed the players' determination for future games.

Cao Yang frankly stated after the match that the entire team had hoped for a good start in their first home game of the new season, but the lack of cohesion made the result somewhat regrettable: "Everyone gave it their all during the game, but clearly, the new domestic and foreign players need more time to gel with everyone. Due to issues with the pitch and coordination earlier on, the overall performance of everyone today wasn't ideal. As the matches progress, we believe we will perform better in subsequent games."

Despite missing the opportunity to secure all three points at home, the draw between Seongnam Ilhwa and Nagoya Grampus in the other group match provided Teda with an opportunity. "Everyone was very much looking forward to this victory, especially on our home ground. Our head coach conducted a detailed analysis of the opponent before the game, and during the match, we closely marked relevant players. However, we ended up drawing with the opponent. Although it wasn't ideal, not losing isn't too bad a result either. After this game, we need to reflect more on our regrets."

In past encounters with AFC opponents, Australian teams have always been known for their rugged style, which has indeed caused Teda some trouble. Despite having faced them in the 09 season, the Central Coast Mariners now exhibited a more refined style. As a participant in both encounters, Cao Yang also noticed this change: "This Central Coast Mariners team is more focused on footwork coordination and overall passing compared to the past. Actually, this is somewhat similar to our team's characteristics, but the difference is that they are physically stronger. Even though it was our home ground, the opponent's style restrained ours, preventing us from fully showcasing our strengths. Additionally, without a tall center-forward to serve as a focal point upfront, it was difficult to control the ball in the front field, resulting in insufficient threats and attacks."

Although they continued to use the 4-5-1 formation, the absence of a striker like Luciano certainly affected Teda's offense. Notably, Coach Kuzé moved starting forward Alves to the wing in the second half, indicating he was still exploring different options upfront. Regarding the awkward situation on the offensive line, Cao Yang said: "The club recently signed a black striker who, although unable to participate in the AFC Champions League, will be available for the upcoming league matches, so you can look forward to his performance."

Not being able to seize the opportunity to win the first game was undoubtedly regretful for Teda, but Cao Yang believed it wouldn't affect the upcoming preparations: "Winning the first game would have been great, but fortunately, we didn't lose. There are still five matches in the AFC Champions League group stage, and every subsequent match requires all of us to strive together to achieve good results."
