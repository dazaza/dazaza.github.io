AC Milan's big move exposed: Twin stars, Berlusconi allocates 30 million euros specifically for signing him.

by fevqqkvv on 2012-02-09 10:11:39

AC Milan's transfer advisor, Bronzetti, emphasized the difficulty of the deal: "AC Milan indeed wants Hamsik, but they are facing a club that is growing into a major power. They need 24 top-class players, so this deal is extremely difficult." However, last summer Bronzetti also said this about Ibrahimovic's transfer, but Raiola is someone who specializes in completing difficult deals. Even though he is not Hamsik's agent, we can still see what tricks he has up his sleeve to bring the Slovakian player to AC Milan. Nevertheless, Italian media almost unanimously believe that the "big move" mentioned by Raiola, which started preparations in January, refers to Hamsik.

What new information have the media revealed? Scognamiglio from the *Roma Sport* told Sky TV: "The names AC Milan is now pursuing have become very clear: Asamoah and Hamsik. The Ghanaian player is a versatile midfielder who can play on the left, while Hamsik is an attacking midfielder who scores goals. I think he represents modern football, and he can score many goals."

The *Milan Sport* disclosed the bottom-line figure AC Milan has prepared for Hamsik: 30 million euros. With this amount of money to buy Hamsik, Berlusconi has already approved it with Galliani. This sum is said to be allocated specifically by Berlusconi, but in reality, it comes from the savings made by letting Pirlo go, releasing Janakulovski, and reducing salaries for Inzaghi, Nesta, Ambrosini, and other veteran players. This amount should be enough to bring in Hamsik.

It is worth noting that when discussing the transfer market, Allegri particularly emphasized: "We need a strong midfielder right now, and he must have a very good scoring ability." Clearly, this is not referring to Asamoah, who only scored two goals in a season, whereas Hamsik and Pastore both scored 11 goals. AC Milan's coach also expressed: "I am going on vacation, but I will talk to Galliani every day to understand the progress of our transfers."

Galliani also talked about Pastore, Kaka, Ganso, and Lamela, saying: "We already have Seedorf and Bojan, so we don't need Kaka or Pastore as shadow forwards. Ganso? We don't have any non-EU slots left, and he is also a 'number 9.5' player, but Brazil is a beautiful country, so we'll take a look. Lamela? Braida has evaluated him; he is a very talented player, but for a young player, his price is too high..." In fact, there isn't much difference between Hamsik's position and these few players, but Kaka, Ganso, and Pastore are defined as 'number 9.5' players while Hamsik is considered capable of playing left midfield because his defensive abilities are stronger than the aforementioned players, which is exactly what Allegri values.

On the other hand, during interviews yesterday, both Galliani and Allegri mentioned Hamsik's name. Allegri stated: "Hamsik is a good player, but there are many good players. Do I like Hamsik and Asamoah? Who doesn't like them? Both are young players with potential." Galliani then said: "We need a left midfielder. Asamoah is definitely a left midfielder, and Hamsik can also play this position, just like Fabregas and Bale, but Pastore cannot. Van der Vaart? Maybe勉强can!" Hamsik is clearly an attacking midfielder, but here Galliani forces him into the category of a left midfielder. A few days ago, after publicly denying the possibility of Hamsik joining, Galliani is "gradually getting closer." Galliani even admitted: "Hamsik's price is completely negotiable, but De Laurentiis is right; I have never asked him to buy Hamsik. I am more concerned about my 11 players whose contracts will expire next summer. My relationship with De Laurentiis is good, and I respect him." At the same time, Galliani ruled out the possibility of Fabregas being able to play left midfield, stating: "Fabregas is too expensive, we cannot snatch players from European giants."

"Hamsik won't leave, he will stay. We will participate in the Champions League next season, and we will retain all key players." Yesterday, Napoli's chairman, De Laurentiis, made such comments, indicating that AC Milan's pursuit of Hamsik faces significant challenges. According to the latest news brought by *TuttoMercatoWeb*, Hamsik did not change his agent, meaning that Raiola, who is closely related to AC Milan, ultimately did not become the Slovakian attacking midfielder's new agent as claimed by Italian media. For AC Milan's acquisition of Hamsik, these are all negative developments.
