So-called nocturnal emission refers to a phenomenon where the reproductive organ discharges sperm and semen outside the body without sexual intercourse. There are two types of nocturnal emission: one is called emission during dreams (梦遗), and the other is called spontaneous emission (滑精). A person in a sleep state who experiences nocturnal emission while dreaming about sexual activities is referred to as having emission during dreams. If during the day, when the person is conscious, they see sexually suggestive books, magazines, or films that overly excite the sexual center of the brain, then even without sexual activity, sometimes there can be leakage of semen, which is known as spontaneous emission. Some teenagers (including their parents) are very nervous about emission during dreams. They believe that the occurrence of emission during dreams is an indication of "kidney deficiency." Is this really true? The truth is, when the essence is full, it overflows.

In fact, the appearance of nocturnal emission is a "marker" for males entering puberty (for girls, the marker is the onset of menstruation). The testicles produce hundreds of millions of sperm every day, which are stored in the epididymis. The capacity of the epididymis is limited, yet sperm continues to be produced constantly. When the epididymis cannot hold any more sperm and semen, it sends a signal to the brain. If there is sufficient sexual stimulation to excite the "ejaculation center," all the "ejaculation organs" contract rhythmically, and thus the sperm is ejaculated outside the body. During adolescence, when sexual functions begin to mature but before reaching the legal marriage age, economic conditions, social status, and responsibility for future families do not allow them to establish a family or start sexual activity. Therefore, some people relieve "sexual hunger" through masturbation. Additionally, during sleep, because the brain receives the signal that "the epididymis cannot hold any more sperm," the brain causes the semen to be discharged through dreams involving sexual content; or during the day after seeing sexually suggestive materials, the excitement of the ejaculation center causes the semen to be discharged. This apparent emission during dreams and spontaneous emission is essentially still a manifestation of "when the essence is full, it overflows." This situation is just like how water overflows from a cup when it's too full, and the principle is exactly the same. Therefore, nocturnal emission is not an indication of "kidney deficiency," on the contrary, it is precisely a sign of sufficient kidney energy.

If you pay a little attention, you'll find that most people who experience emission during dreams or spontaneous emission are unmarried or adults who have been separated from their spouses for a long time and are "full of vitality." It's not due to kidney deficiency. So, where does the claim that "emission during dreams equals kidney deficiency" come from? Originally, this was influenced by classical medical texts that overly emphasized the importance of semen. Classical medical texts believed that "essence, qi, and spirit are the three treasures of man," placing "essence" first. They thought that "essence" is the most precious substance in the human body, usually stored in the kidneys. If there is insufficient kidney energy, the kidney gate will not close tightly, causing the outflow of semen, which is called "kidney deficiency." In reality, this is a one-sided understanding that arose due to historical limitations in ancient medicine. However, because this view has been around for too long, it still affects many people today. Many also mistakenly believe that semen is more valuable than blood, and there's even a saying that "one drop of semen equals ten drops of blood." Through modern scientific analysis, the main component of semen is water, accounting for more than 90%. Other components are largely similar to plasma, and it is not true that one drop of semen equals ten drops of blood. If a teenager becomes immersed in pornographic literature or watches films with sexual content, the sexual center remains in a constant state of excitement. As the saying goes, "what you think about during the day, you dream about at night," so excessive excitement of the sexual center during the day makes it easy to dream vividly at night, leading to emission during dreams. If nocturnal emissions occur too frequently, feelings of tension, fear, anxiety may arise, affecting sleep. These negative emotions and mental states will, in turn, make one feel fatigued, dizzy, tinnitus, palpitations, soreness in the waist and legs, listlessness, memory loss, and lack of concentration, leading to the belief that one has "kidney deficiency." In reality, the overflow caused by "when the essence is full, it overflows" is akin to needing to urinate when the bladder is full, entirely a physiological phenomenon. Why create unnecessary troubles? People don't feel anxious about losing bodily essence when they urinate, so why should occasional nocturnal emissions cause alarm? The key issue lies in our insufficient understanding of human sexual physiology, thereby blindly carrying the "spiritual cross." Therefore, it's important to correctly address nocturnal emissions, avoiding magazines, books, films, and videos with sexual stimuli. If there are too many instances of nocturnal emissions within a period, Chinese medicine diagnosis and treatment can help. After a period of treatment, the frequency of emission during dreams will decrease. Thus, there's no need to bear undue mental burdens regarding emission during dreams.
