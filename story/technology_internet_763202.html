MIIT: 280 million mobile phone users not registered under real name, difficult to implement supplementary registration

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 17:04:24

On July 24, it was reported that at the press conference on the development of industrial communication in the first half of 2013, Zhu Jun, deputy director of the Communication Development Department stated: "According to the MIIT statistics, as of March this year, about 280 million users have not registered under their real names. These users account for around 20% of the total users, mainly prepaid mobile phone users."

Zhu Jun said: "How to register for those users who have not registered under their real names is also a complex issue, which requires a gradual approach over time. Various measures need to be taken to encourage, support, and guide these users to gradually register under their real names for telephone services."

Regarding the real-name registration system for telephones, Article 6 of the "Decision on Strengthening the Protection of Online Information" passed by the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress last year clearly stipulates that when network service providers handle website access services for users, or provide fixed-line telephone, mobile phone network access procedures, or information release services, they should require users to provide true identity information when signing agreements or confirming service provision. The Telecommunications Regulations also have relevant provisions stating that registration under false names is prohibited.

In addition, the MIIT has issued the "Regulations on the Registration of True Identity Information of Telephone Users." These regulations clearly state that for new users, when handling telephone network access procedures, they only need to show valid identification to telecommunications enterprises, provide true identity information, and cooperate with the relevant information registration work.

For existing users, telecommunications enterprises are required to notify them through various means and provide convenient conditions and channels to facilitate the completion of real-name registration procedures for existing users. If they do not complete the registration, telecommunications enterprises will require users to register when confirming the provision of services.

Furthermore, the regulations also require telecommunications enterprises not to take the opportunity to arbitrarily increase user responsibilities or infringe upon user rights and interests during the registration process.

Regarding the issue of agents, Zhu Jun believes: "This is both a weak link in management and a challenge in implementing the real-name registration system for telephones. However, the overall principle required by the regulations is: whoever entrusts, manages, and takes responsibility. As telecommunications operators, if they entrust agents to develop business, they must ensure supervision responsibilities; agents that do not meet the conditions cannot be entrusted to develop business. The premise is to ensure user information security and prevent leakage."
