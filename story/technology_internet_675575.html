What does Tieguanyin taste like?

by meitaocha123 on 2012-02-25 10:38:10

Tieguanyin is a type of Oolong tea, and Oolong tea is semi-fermented. Anxi Tieguanyin belongs to the top grade of Oolong teas, classified under green teas, with its origin in Anxi County, Quanzhou City, Fujian Province. The taste of Tieguanyin can mainly be divided into five categories: light aroma Tieguanyin, strong aroma Tieguanyin,韵aroma Tieguanyin, carbon roasted Tieguanyin, and fresh aroma Tieguanyin, each having different tastes. Tieguanyin tea can be evaluated from aspects such as appearance, soup color, aroma, and taste. Although good tea doesn't necessarily have a beautiful appearance, a good appearance often gives a refreshing feeling. However, for friends who often drink Tieguanyin tea, the taste and soup flavor are the most important, which is to appreciate the unique charm of Tieguanyin tea. A tea that feels fragrant and smooth upon entry, rich and refreshing, with endless aftertaste, is the standard for good tea.

Light Aroma Type: Belongs to the trendy lightly fermented tea category. "Clear soup and green water" is the most representative characteristic of the light aroma Tieguanyin, also suitable for market taste. Light aroma tea emphasizes clear soup, freshness, and the currently popular "skewed sour" and "green sour" represent this light aroma tea. The leaves look good, with a bright green color, making them very appreciable. The aroma can be smelled immediately, looking just like when freshly picked. When brewed, it results in clear soup and green water, without branches or foam, with high aroma and pure taste, very suitable for appreciation. It suits friends who prefer a light taste and enjoy the color of tea. The light fermentation requires a lighter degree of roasting, retaining more moisture in the tea leaves. The taste is light and suitable for daily brewing and appreciation. Generally, the tea can be brewed 5-12 times, depending on the quality of the tea. One point to note is that it needs to be refrigerated for better preservation.

Fresh Aroma Type: Belongs to the trendy lightly fermented tea category. Fresh aroma tea emphasizes the freshness and fresh taste of the tea, along with the market's "dragged sour" and "fresh sour," representing this fresh aroma tea. Tieguanyin price [http://www.teaguest.com/] is also relatively expensive and not easily available in the general market. The leaves look good, with a bright green color, making them very appreciable. Upon picking up the tea, you can smell a fresh aroma, appearing more vibrant in color compared to other teas. When brewed, it results in clear soup and green water, without branches or foam, with high aroma and pure taste, very suitable for appreciation. Fresh aroma tea uses a light fermentation process requiring a lighter degree of roasting, keeping the moisture content in the tea leaves full. The taste belongs to the fresh and light category, suitable for daily brewing and appreciation. This tea is suitable for personal consumption and helps nourish the spirit. Fresh aroma Tieguanyin is suitable for friends who are new to tea.

Strong Aroma Type: Belongs to the traditionally semi-fermented Anxi Tieguanyin tea, suitable for experienced tea enthusiasts to drink because the taste is heavier. The color of the leaves appears green behind which there are slightly black and yellowish leaves, meaning the color isn't the best, but the taste is the most authentic. Deep golden yellow soup water represents the strong aroma tea. Traditional Tieguanyin tea requires a longer and heavier semi-fermentation time, resulting in a darker tea soup color when brewed. The drinking experience is rich and smooth, meaning it is genuine. We ourselves often drink traditional strong aroma tea and aged tea. After traditional Tieguanyin is stored for a long time and becomes aged tea, it undergoes further processing such as re-roasting, charcoal roasting, etc., making the taste and health benefits more significant.

Charcoal Roasted Type: Based on traditionally semi-fermented Anxi Tieguanyin tea, it is roasted with wood charcoal for about 5-12 hours, belonging to the category of traditionally authentic good tea. The taste is more assured, smooth, and carries a natural fire aroma. Now, traditionally charcoal roasted strong aroma Anxi Tieguanyin is increasingly popular in Anxi. Charcoal roasted tea leaves have special aftertaste, unique taste, and make the throat feel particularly comfortable after tasting. They carry a strong fire aroma. A particular point to mention is that after brewing, the tea soup color is deep yellow, completely different from the commonly seen clear soup and green water of light aroma Tieguanyin. Only experienced tea enthusiasts should choose this type; generally, newcomers to tea won't get used to it. Aged Tieguanyin is also made by annual charcoal roasting based on charcoal roasting. The taste of charcoal roasted tea is unique and cannot be imitated by others.

Yun Aroma Type: Is a newly introduced tea between light aroma and strong aroma types. More friends will accept Yun aroma type because it lies between light aroma and strong aroma types, offering more flavors to enjoy. It has both the fragrance of light aroma and the pure taste of strong aroma, making it durable for brewing. It certainly possesses the essential qualities of good tea.
