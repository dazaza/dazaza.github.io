Dr. Ling Zhipeng's Profile:

More doctors like Dr. Ling Zhipeng, male, Chief Physician, Professor. Born in September 1962, graduated from Anhui Medical University in 1984 with a bachelor's degree. Obtained a master's degree in neurosurgery from Wannan Medical College in 2002. From 1984 to April 2005, he worked in clinical and research at the Department of Neurosurgery and Stereotactic Neurosurgical Research Institute of Anhui Provincial Hospital. In 1996-1997, he studied abroad in France as a visiting scholar at the Henri Mondor Hospital Neurosurgery Center of Paris XII University, focusing on research in deep brain stimulation for Parkinson's disease, radiofrequency and microsurgical treatment of trigeminal neuralgia, and epilepsy surgery. From October 2000 to April 2005, he was the Deputy Director and then Director of the Department of Neurosurgery and Stereotactic Neurosurgery at Anhui Provincial Hospital, Chief Physician; from May 2005 to May 2007, he served as the Director of Functional Neurosurgery at Beijing Sanbo Xingfu Brain Hospital; from May 2007 to November 2008, he was the Director of Functional Neurosurgery at the Second Affiliated Hospital of Tsinghua University; since December 2008, he has been the Chief Physician and Professor at the Department of Neurosurgery of the General Hospital of the People's Liberation Army (301), responsible for stereotactic and functional neurosurgery. He was the first in China to use deep brain stimulation (DBS) for the treatment of Parkinson's disease. He specializes in minimally invasive surgical treatments for Parkinson's disease, essential tremor, dystonia, spasmodic torticollis, chorea, cerebral palsy, Tourette syndrome, evaluation and surgical treatment of refractory epilepsy. He uses electrical stimulation or micro-ablation methods to treat refractory obsessive-compulsive disorder, depression, and other mental illnesses. He has unique skills in minimally invasive treatments for facial muscle spasm, trigeminal neuralgia, glossopharyngeal neuralgia, cancer pain, central neuropathic pain, brain cysticercosis, intracranial metal foreign bodies, etc.
