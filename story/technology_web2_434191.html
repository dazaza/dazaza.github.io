Shenzhen second generation rich qualifies for subsidized housing, says father's assets are irrelevant to himself

by kfjro082 on 2011-05-20 17:39:41

It was recently exposed by a netizen that Li Shixiong, the son of a former senior executive of a listed company worth tens of millions yuan, had passed the final review of Shenzhen's second affordable housing program, which has aroused controversy and doubts. Li Shixiong said in an interview: "My father does indeed have assets, but they are his, not closely related to me."

>> Net post: The son of a former executive passed the final review

After the final review list of Shenzhen's second affordable housing program was announced, a netizen posted saying that applicant with ID SL1080081000185, Mr. Li (nickname used), had passed the final review. Mr. Li’s family currently lives in a house of over 150 square meters and owns no less than two properties, two cars. His father is the former union chairman of a listed company, Tianjian Group Co., Ltd., with assets of no less than 30 million yuan. Mr. Li once resigned from a bank and made substantial income from stock trading between 2006 and 2007. "After knowing this afternoon that he passed the final review for the affordable housing application, Mr. Li boasted to my friend about having many connections." This post has drawn much attention. Some citizens think that if the father is wealthy, the son should not apply for affordable housing; others believe that the "rich second generation" who start from scratch deserve encouragement.

The final review list of Shenzhen's second affordable housing program shows that Li Shixiong, born in 1977, had a "bank deposit and financial product value" of 31,899.99 yuan, "stock market value" of 11,748.41 yuan, and "commercial insurance market value" of 5,946.39 yuan as of December 31, 2009; no real estate or vehicles; an income of 12,133 yuan in 2008 and 7,529 yuan in 2009. The final review conclusion was: eligible for affordable housing application.

It is understood that according to the application conditions for Shenzhen's affordable housing, adult children can apply independently for affordable housing, and assets only calculate those of the applicant and co-applicants. From a legal policy perspective, regardless of how rich Li Shixiong's parents are, as long as Li himself meets the eligibility criteria, he can apply.

>> The party concerned: Houses and cars all belong to the father

Around 18:00 on January 6, at the municipal company compound in Futian District, Shenzhen, the reporter knocked on Li Shixiong's door. Li Shixiong frankly admitted that his father had once been the union chairman of Tianjian Group, "but my father's assets have nothing to do with me."

Regarding the complaints mentioned in the online post, Li Shixiong said: "If they come to investigate, there will be no problem. I have nothing to hide." "(Houses and cars) all belong to my father, the car is parked downstairs, it also belongs to my father." Li Shixiong showed a bitter smile, "My father does have assets, but they have nothing to do with me!" He said that he has always lived with his parents, and others might misunderstand when they see him owning houses and cars. In fact, the only assets truly belonging to him are things like computers, and everything that should be declared has been declared.

"Tell lies, my relationship with my father is not good," Li Shixiong said. He hasn't worked for a long time and just does some small transactions on Taobao; regarding job hunting, "my father hasn't helped me (find a job)." Li sighed, saying he had changed jobs many times and once worked as a temporary employee in a bank without a base salary.

Regarding the claim of "making millions from stock trading," Li Shixiong firmly denied it, saying he doesn't trade stocks, only his wife trades a little, which is reflected in the securities market value in the final review public information. Regarding the claim that his father's assets exceed ten million, Li Shixiong did not explicitly deny it.
