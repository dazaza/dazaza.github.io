Zhejiang Higher People's Court: Accurately grasp the boundary between official lending and illegal fundraising

by loved7k9 on 2012-02-29 16:13:29

Qi Qi, president of the Higher People's Court of Zhejiang Province said in an interview with Legal Daily today that since this year, courts at all levels in Zhejiang province have been paying attention to changes and trends in local economic and social development. They timely communicate major situations involved in cases, ensure smooth information flow between higher and lower levels, form a judicial synergy, follow the central government's general tone of [seeking progress while maintaining stability], deeply implement proactive judiciary, people-oriented judiciary, and continuously improve the level of trial services for the overall situation.

Currently, courts at all levels are closely monitoring four issues related to capital chains: first, the capital chain issue of small and micro enterprises; second, the capital chain issue of private lending and guarantees; third, the capital chain issue of real estate developers and home mortgage buyers; fourth, some central government debt capital chain issues. The focus is on properly handling five types of disputes involved in cases:

Firstly, the differentiated treatment of small and micro enterprises involved in cases. Indiscriminate assistance may lead to opposite results. [Those who can survive] should be legally helped, [those who should die] should exit in an orderly manner. On one hand, actively help small and micro enterprises with markets and prospects mediate disputes, maintain production, and overcome difficulties. Legally protect the effective productivity of enterprises with orders and more employment, avoiding adverse impacts on enterprise production and operation activities due to improper justice; on the other hand, increase publicity, guide eliminable small and micro enterprises to take the path of market-based bankruptcy, especially high-pollution, high-energy-consuming backward enterprises that should exit the market, actively explore difficult bankruptcy methods, force economic transformation and upgrading, eliminate bubbles, and achieve legal reorganization and redistribution of effective assets as much as possible.

Secondly, wage arrears disputes involving migrant workers and employees. In recent years, the provincial higher court has issued notices every year-end to legally prevent and handle disputes over enterprise wage arrears. Local courts actively respond effectively, legally protect the survival and legitimate rights of employee groups involved in cases, achieving early warnings, early suggestions, and early coordination, preventing accumulation and intensification of conflicts. Trial enforcement has accumulated rich experience and achieved good results. This year, we will also legally punish the crime of refusing to pay labor remuneration newly included in the criminal code, strengthen judicial publicity for typical cases.

Thirdly, disputes involved in private lending. Private lending involves both enterprises and people's livelihoods, and also relates to regional stability, with strong judicial policy implications. Courts across the province need to increase judicial synergy. Courts in Wenzhou and other areas with numerous cases involved must rely tightly on local party and government coordination, legally and appropriately handle widespread and complex issues such as civil-criminal crossover, prudently grasp the boundaries between illegal private lending and illegal fundraising crimes, and legally recognize the effectiveness of private lending contracts. Regarding the legality and illegality of private lending, the scope of legality is larger than illegality because according to the judicial interpretation of the Contract Law, as long as it does not violate the prohibitive provisions of laws passed by the National People's Congress and its Standing Committee, the contract can be recognized as valid. There are at least two large space zones between this and illegal contracts: one is situations where the law does not stipulate, and the other is situations where the law has advisory provisions rather than prohibitive provisions. These two situations can be recognized as legal but cannot be recognized as illegal.

Courts should dare to act first, be good at innovation, expand new countermeasures for the trial of private lending disputes in courts across the province, summarize new experiences, jointly maintain the stability of the market economy and social order in various places. Closely follow the new round of financial reforms in our province, legally support the central government's financial innovation actions, promote the transparent and standardized operations of private lending and private capital, alleviate financing difficulties for small and micro enterprises. This year, on the one hand, we need to carefully explore and summarize the practical experience of the trial of private lending disputes in Zhejiang courts, and on the other hand, actively propose revision suggestions for the formulation of relevant judicial interpretations for the reference of the Supreme Court.

Fourthly, disputes involved in forced demolition of key projects in various places. This is actually a difficult problem of how judicial intervention balances public interests and collective interests, economic development and social stability in accordance with the law. The new "Regulations on the Expropriation of Houses on State-owned Land and Compensation" issued last year by the State Council abolished administrative forced demolition, requiring judicial review by the court. For this, the mainstream opinion of the court is to implement the [separation of adjudication and execution] forced demolition model, that is, in principle, the court only reviews the request for forced execution, and the specific forced execution is still mainly implemented by the administrative agency. However, because relevant judicial interpretations are unlikely to be issued in the near term, the issue of forced demolition in various places cannot wait or be bypassed. Especially after the completion of the municipal and county-level party and government leadership changeovers, the momentum of starting and promoting new key projects will significantly increase, which may lead to more frequent forced demolition conflicts and disputes. For this, courts across the province should adhere to the principles of [strict judicial review standards, more application of hearing procedures, and trying to promote self-execution], relying on local party and government authorities, cautiously carrying out judicial review and enforcement work for forced demolition, balancing the protection of the legitimate rights of expropriated persons with the objective needs of promoting key projects and urbanization development in various places, guarding the bottom line that human life value is higher than property value, and preventing extreme incidents and serious events.

Fifthly, disputes involved under real estate regulation. Closely monitor disputes and social unrest that could be triggered by capital chain problems among real estate developers and home mortgage buyers. Higher courts should carefully study the impact of real estate regulation policies on the validity of housing transaction contracts and promptly unify judgment standards. As real estate regulation policies continue to be firm, housing prices may further decline, and breaches of housing transaction contracts may significantly increase. Hangzhou and other places have already shown signs of group events similar to ["house riots"]. For this, courts at all levels should base their decisions on the actual circumstances of individual cases, respect the judicial principle of the validity of housing transaction contracts, and rely on local party and government authorities to appropriately coordinate and resolve these issues.
