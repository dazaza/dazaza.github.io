I am an English teacher, and perhaps because I live in Shijingshan, I often pay attention to the English training circles around me.

Below are some of my personal views, and I hope to share them with parents and English enthusiasts.

The Aging Dale English - Part 1

Address: Shijingshan Hualian

Dale English was established in 1999. In order to quickly expand its reputation, the owner Wang Zhongwei had a brilliant idea and registered the trademark for "Dale English." But what commercial value does this name have?

"Using force against itself" – long before Dale English was founded, the internationally renowned company Dell Computers had already taken root in China. Its advertising strength and creative planning were unmatched by domestic companies. Dell Computers quickly became an internationally recognized brand in Beijing, just like IBM. At this time, suddenly there appeared a "Dell International English."

Each consumer's associative function was incredibly powerful. With their immense admiration for foreign brands, they flocked into the American-flagged training classes.

A grand entrance, coupled with media hype, made "Dale" rapidly expand, becoming a banner in China's English training industry. Opening teaching points, setting up branch schools, franchising – truly thriving business across the seas, wealth flowing from all directions! But is this really how schools should be run??? The ancient saying "it takes ten years to grow trees but a hundred years to educate people" has gone out of fashion? Can education, this most ancient industry, be hyped like this? Can "behavior as a model teacher, learning as an example" be simply franchised? (To be continued...)

The Aging Dale English - Part 2

Siddhartha Gautama – the founder of Buddhism. The original meaning of Buddha in ancient India was a teacher or master, which later became deified when it was transmitted to China. Buddhism provides great spiritual comfort through its transcendent Buddhist philosophy! This is why temples have become the largest "franchise chain" in the world!

Confucius' Analects educated the Chinese masses and laid the foundation for the entire nation's character. Because of the heights he reached, they are unparalleled.

Tsinghua University: Wood Bell Golden Sound! It has weathered a century of vicissitudes in modern China. This is not only due to the era in which they were founded but also because of the batches of people who relentlessly pursue truth, willing to worry about the affairs of the world before others and enjoy after others.

Renda High School, Beijing No.4 Middle School, No.101 Middle School, Jingshan School – their reputations echo throughout Beijing. The reasons are self-evident!

The reason for New Oriental's success lies fundamentally in its Peking University heritage and overseas team. Combined with the then-outbound fever and universities lacking relevant outbound English courses, plus Yu Minhong's random choice of a Haidian training location, these elements created a myth that could only happen in China!

"Dale English" – what do you rely on? (To be continued…)

The Aging Dale English - Part 3

The core of education is still teaching, like any business. If I grow watermelons and they're small and not sweet; if I sell tofu and it's bitter and sour. Initially, you might get away with deceiving consumers, calling small watermelons a new variety or claiming bitter tofu lowers blood pressure, but can such pure deception really fool kind-hearted consumers forever...

Opening Dale English's promotional brochure, "a hundred foreign teachers, an American specialty," "international textbooks costing millions."

Looking at the main projects, it's still the never-tiring New Concept English. To "promote," package deals are offered: buy two, get one free; sign up for two books, get one book free. At first glance, it seems generous, like McDonald's combo meals where you eat more and save more… I say this is complete nonsense. The gap between the first and third books of New Concept English is enormous, spanning from English phonetics to graduate school entrance exams. Completing such a huge leap in just half a year or a year is utterly self-deceptive. If this were true, Dale could apply for intangible cultural heritage protection. These teachers would be revered as ancestors like Lu Ban and Bian Que!

Don't make a mockery of the world anymore. Returning to the essence of education and studying teaching well is the right path!

Shijingshan District Training School

Author: Chaoran

Foreign Talk Galore - Shijingshan Library, Bayjiaonan Road

At the beginning of the 21st century, following the successful bid for the Olympics and entry into the World Trade Organization, there was a nationwide excitement, especially in Beijing. "Go learn English" became a common topic among everyone from grandpas to grandchildren! A large number of "excellent educational teams" rose instantaneously, presenting a scene of "a hundred flowers blooming, a hundred schools contending." Each seemed to possess a secret recipe hidden in their ancestors' chamber pots, making people feel there was no other place like it.

One of these was Foreign Talk Galore.

In 1978, reform and opening-up finally came. With the rush of "Western winds," many people were at a loss. At this time, CCTV introduced the first TV English teaching program – Follow Me 《跟我学》. Before the news broadcast every day, old and young, men and women, intentionally or unintentionally began to learn how to use chopsticks to savor Western cuisine.

Subsequently, Family Album U.S.A 《走边美国》 and its sister series 《欢乐美语》 achieved great success in China. Mr. Sun Zhen, the boss of Foreign Talk Galore, saw the opportunity at this time. (To be continued…)

Mr. Sun, leveraging his relationship with a Beijing TV station, took on a big project, responsible for producing a TV English program. Overly excited, Mr. Sun started to doubt himself – did he have the capacity for such a big job? To reduce risks, he brought in two close friends to enrich their fortunes together. Although "one hero needs three assistants," when difficulties arise, they may scatter like swallows. Soon, due to only investment and no returns, his iron-clad buddies couldn't bear it anymore and left without saying goodbye. At this point, Mr. Sun was like an ant on a hot pot, confused! However, the overall enthusiasm for English learning in society was still very high, and frankly speaking, the shows Mr. Sun produced weren't complete disasters. Suddenly, an opportunity arose – Hongen Software Company was planning a set of English software products. Upon hearing the news, Mr. Sun's eyes lit up. The days afterward were like a honeymoon, extremely pleasant!

Having tasted the sweetness, Mr. Sun's confidence greatly increased. Plus, with the help of Hongen Software's distribution, Mr. Sun decided to continue with TV English, creating his own brand "Foreign Talk Galore," personally leading the charge. Suddenly, a unique landscape appeared before Beijing TV viewers – a chubby white man, speaking English with a strong local accent, joking around with beauties and foreigners. Regardless, and undeniably, the brand value improved. With fame came simplicity, Mr. Sun became Old Sun; the TV show became the school's name; the program host became a guest professor; dividends began rolling in!

Everything seemed to go smoothly, and Foreign Talk Galore was seen as a major player in Beijing's education and training.

But ultimately, everything converges to one point – being a teacher is not child's play. Can simply renting a venue, finding a foreigner, and cobbling together some materials qualify as a school? Old Sun realized this and took some measures, such as "Chinese-foreign teachers teaching together" and "Foreign Talk dedicated textbooks." But these external glitz couldn't hide the inner emptiness. Students failing to study well has consequences far worse than buying a defective TV! That anger comes from within, feeling deceived. Up to now, "Foreign Talk Galore" has seen 10 franchise schools in Beijing alone go bankrupt!

What's real will always remain real, what's fake will never be true!~
