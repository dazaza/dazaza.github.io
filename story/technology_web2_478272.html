Review Background:

In today's article, netizens will see the performance of Samsung G810 in various functions.

Picture: Samsung G810

Operating System:

The Samsung G810 is a standard smartphone using the Symbian 9.2 operating system on the S60 third edition FP1 platform. Samsung has directly adopted an interface similar to Nokia’s S60 phones, with only slight modifications to the system theme and some icons. Therefore, netizens familiar with Nokia S60 phones, especially N-series models, can comfortably use the Samsung G810. The only thing that might be unfamiliar for users is that the G810 does not have a clearly labeled "menu" key but instead places it below the left soft key, which may require some time to get used to.

Picture: Samsung G810

Picture: User Interface of Samsung G810

On the standby screen, we can clearly see that Samsung G810 includes a Google search toolbar. Entering keywords here and confirming will open the browser and display Google search results. For example, searching "ZOL" yields links and introductions to ZOL and its mobile phone channel.

Picture: Google Search Plugin of Samsung G810

Phone Functionality:

As a standard S60 phone, the contact list of the Samsung G810 is a type we are very familiar with. The G810 allows users to add details such as name, mobile number, telephone number, email, pager, internet phone, push-to-talk, company, and position for each contact. These are default items; other fields can be added by the user. Contacts can also be managed through groups. Additionally, users can set whether a particular contact card should be public during synchronization, protecting user privacy.

Picture: Contact List of Samsung G810

The G810 can store up to 30 days of call records, showing recent calls, call duration, and grouped data details. It can also display detailed types and specifics of various data transmissions. Users can query the details of each data connection and filter them based on outgoing, incoming, voice/video, messages, grouped data WLAN connections, or specific numbers.

Picture: Call Records of Samsung G810

Messaging Functionality:

The Samsung G810 supports SMS, MMS, emails, service messages, and broadcast messages, capable of storing up to 999 messages. In terms of input methods, the pinyin input method offers 6 selectable characters and 6 associated words, supporting copy, paste, and cut. Our test unit was the Asia-Pacific version, supporting simplified Chinese, Malay, and English input. The domestic version, G818E, equipped with Intelligent Video, will also be launched within days.

Picture: Messaging Function of Samsung G810

Music Playback:

Pressing the multimedia key below the right soft key allows you to choose between launching the music player, RealPlayer, or the radio. The music player supports songs arranged by entire track, artist, album, track list, genre, and composer, supporting random playback and automatic loop playback. It supports MP3/AAC/AAC+/e-AAC+/WMA formats, though this music player cannot manually add song covers.

Picture: Music Player of Samsung G810

Other Multimedia Functions:

For other multimedia functions, the Samsung G810 comes with Adobe Flash Lite 2.1 and RealPlayer. Testing showed that RealPlayer can play QVGA resolution mp4 files. There is also a recorder (which can use inserted storage cards for unlimited recording) and a radio (requires headphones to function).

To meet personalized user requirements, the Samsung G810 also includes image and video editing software. The image editor can adjust overall effects, partial effects, brightness, contrast, color, crop, and insert frames, images, clip art, emoticons, and text. The video editor can edit videos, create photo films, and produce storyboards.

Practical Tools:

Common tools found in Nokia S60 phones, such as calculators, clocks, calendars, file managers, Quickoffice, Adobe PDF, notepads, unit converters, and storage card information, are all preserved. From the screenshots, we can see that the Samsung G810 comes with Quickoffice version 3.8.12.0 and Adobe Reader LE version 1.5.0.

GPS Testing:

The Samsung G810 is a GPS phone with a built-in GPS chip. The model we received did not come pre-installed with any GPS navigation software but instead had a "GPS+" program that can download the latest almanac to speed up positioning. However, while using this program to update the almanac, it indicated network settings were incorrect, even though our internet settings were fine. Thus, the GPS star search test was conducted without updating the almanac.

We tested the GPS star search and positioning capability of the Samsung G810 in an open area using the "GPS Data" option in the tool menu. It took about one minute for the Samsung G810 to complete the star search and positioning process. If the almanac could be updated normally, its positioning speed would likely increase significantly.

Camera Software:

The Samsung G810 features a 5-megapixel autofocus camera that also supports 3x optical zoom. Opening the lens cover automatically enters the camera interface. The phone supports program-controlled shooting, scene modes (portrait/landscape/sports/indoor/beach/sunset/dusk/autumn/color/waves and snow/night/shooting/backlight/fireworks/text), and video recording. It also supports shooting modes (single shot/continuous burst/frame shooting/matrix shooting/panorama shooting), macro, red-eye reduction flash, 3/5/10-second self-timer, face detection, white balance (auto/daylight/incandescent/fluorescent/cloudy), effects (no effect/black and white/sepia/sharp/negative), and brightness adjustment.

The Samsung G810's face detection feature was demonstrated using a single photo. You can clearly see that the G810 identified 8 faces in the photo. We estimate that in real scenarios, the G810's performance would be better than just recognizing from a static image.

In basic camera settings, the Samsung G810 supports anti-shake, extended digital zoom, wide dynamic range, contrast, saturation, sharpness adjustment, and can adjust shutter sound and preset filenames, taking photos with GPS information. Additionally, it can adjust metering mode, ISO (auto/50/100/200/400/800), and video playback resolution. The maximum video resolution supported by this device is VGA (640x480 pixels).

Photo Samples:

From the photo samples, the 5-megapixel camera of the Samsung G810 performs well in wide-angle imaging quality, realistically reproducing different environments under various weather conditions. The sharpness and contrast of the photos are satisfactory, and the detail representation in the macro samples is quite complete, demonstrating the advantages of a 5-megapixel camera. Netizens can click to enlarge the images.

3x Optical Zoom:

The Samsung G810 uses a periscope lens that supports 3x optical zoom, ensuring the body thickness isn't too large. After performing 3x optical zoom, we found that the quality of the photo samples did not significantly decrease. Color reproduction and sharpness were maintained quite well, indicating good telephoto imaging quality, making it highly practical.

Review Summary:

Although the Samsung G810 does not use a processor that supports 3D acceleration, it still retains the powerful expandability and rich features of S60, particularly the 5-megapixel autofocus camera with 3x optical zoom, giving it a say in photography. Its relatively low price makes it a cost-effective S60 phone. If you can accept a slightly thicker body, the G810 is a good choice for consumers looking for a "camera + smart" phone.
