Six online merchants submitted a petition for a hearing, but the Beijing Industry and Commerce Bureau refused to accept it by shirking responsibility at all levels.

by longyi on 2008-08-31 14:23:21

At 2 p.m. on August 27th, six representatives of Beijing online merchants gathered at the XX Industrial and Commercial Office in Beijing (at the request of the parties involved, the reporter has withheld the name of the unit). They were holding a prepared "Hearing Application Document."

This "Hearing Application Document" begins with: "We request that the Beijing Administration for Industry and Commerce hold a hearing regarding the stipulation in the 'Opinions on Implementing the Beijing Information Promotion Regulations to Strengthen the Supervision and Management of E-commerce' which requires all e-commerce operators to obtain a business license."

Online Merchant Representatives: Delayed submission of application due to gathering public opinion on Tianya Forum and to welcome the Olympics.

This application was not written solely by these six online merchants but is an aggregation of opinions from thousands of merchants on the Tianya forum. The Tianya forum is currently the most popular Chinese language forum (BBS), and it serves as the golden megaphone for grassroots classes in the internet era. Cases such as the BMW hit-and-run case and the fake South China Tiger case were exposed here and achieved just results.

On July 19th, a netizen named "Urban Drunken Swordsman" posted on Tianya saying: "Regarding the fees for online stores, I have decided to sue the State Administration for Industry and Commerce or initiate administrative reconsideration, please help." This post quickly became a focus of attention, and as of now, it has been viewed 229,367 times and received 4,695 replies.

"Urban Drunken Swordsman" is an ordinary Beijing online merchant who told the reporter that this seemingly simple application is a summary and simplification of suggestions from tens of thousands of online merchants, representing the collective effort of merchants nationwide.

Originally, this application was planned to be submitted on August 1st, the implementation day of the new online store regulations, but to welcome the once-in-a-lifetime Olympic Games for the Chinese nation, after discussion, everyone unanimously agreed to submit the document after the Olympics.

Chaoyang Branch: We have no authority to accept it nor obligation to forward it.

The six online merchants first chose a nearby Beijing XX Industrial and Commercial Office. The leadership there explained that their level was too low to accept the submission of the document. However, this leader pointed out a way forward - the branch could accept the materials.

Following the suggestion of the industrial and commercial office leadership, the six online merchants went to the Chaoyang Branch of the Beijing Administration for Industry and Commerce. But they were told: "The administration has neither the power nor the qualification to participate in the formulation and amendment of these regulations, so we cannot accept this material. You need to submit it yourself to the municipal government or the People's Congress. The Administration for Industry and Commerce is merely an institution that enforces regulations and has no power or obligation to accept this material."

Around 6 p.m., the six online merchants returned home without any resolution, but they did not give up. "Urban Drunken Swordsman" told the reporter: "Whether it's going to the Beijing Municipal Government, the People's Congress, the State Administration for Industry and Commerce, or the State Council, we must submit this hearing application that embodies the efforts of hundreds of thousands of online merchants."

New Online Store Regulations: Someone dares to issue fee-charging regulations but no one dares to take responsibility.

A legal expert told the reporter that the reasons given by the Chaoyang Branch of the Beijing Administration for Industry and Commerce are baseless. The new online store regulations, namely the 'Opinions on Implementing the Beijing Information Promotion Regulations to Strengthen the Supervision and Management of E-commerce,' is just an administrative announcement and holds the lowest status among all levels of law. This new regulation was not formulated by the Beijing Municipal Government and passed by the Beijing People's Congress, but rather was self-formulated by the Beijing Administration for Industry and Commerce.

This expert pointed out that the Chaoyang Branch of the Beijing Administration for Industry and Commerce intentionally confuses the relationship between the new online store regulations and the 'Beijing Information Promotion Regulations.' The latter was formulated by the Beijing Municipal Government and submitted to the Beijing People's Congress for approval. For reconsideration or other requests regarding this administrative regulation, one should approach the Beijing Municipal Government and the People's Congress.
