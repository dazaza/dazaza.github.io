The days of DNF cheats being rampant are not over yet.

by dnfmeimei on 2009-12-01 00:53:20

According to reports (by reporter Tao Ying), Beijing has been "fallen" into a haze day, and next week will continue the record. At 11:00 am today, the latest report from the city meteorological station stated that there would be no significant cold air entering Beijing in the coming week, stable weather conditions would persist, and this year's longest hazy weather would continue.

Beijing will still be under heavy haze next week according to the latest meteorological data. The overall strength of the cold air affecting Beijing will remain weak. It is expected that around Wednesday next week, there will be a cold air moving across the country from west to east, which may help reduce the fog in Beijing. However, this cold air seems powerless against the strong haze, so the haze will not immediately dissipate, and the days shrouded in fog will continue.

This morning, the Central Meteorological Observatory continued to issue an orange fog warning, with nearly half of China covered in fog. Among these areas, the southern part of Beijing, Tianjin, and other regions in North China are the "severely affected areas." The average visibility is less than 1 kilometer, with local visibility being less than 200 meters.

The director of the municipal meteorological station, Guo Hu, stated that this is a clear case of localized dense fog, mainly concentrated in the southeastern areas, while the northern areas have visibility above 1 to 2 kilometers. This is due to Beijing's high northwest and low southeast terrain, combined with south winds increasing the humidity at lower altitudes in the southeastern region, causing water vapor to accumulate and making it difficult to disperse. However, as the sun rises higher, visibility will improve somewhat.

Since there is no significant cold air entering Beijing in the near future, Beijing will continue to experience city-wide or localized smoggy weather. Citizens traveling, especially towards the southeastern direction, should pay attention to traffic weather forecasts.

So far, Beijing's continuous smoggy weather has lasted for 5 days, setting the longest record since the beginning of this year.

At the same time, the weather in the city next week will generally remain stable, with little change in temperature. The daytime highest temperature will stabilize between 6 to 7°C, and the nighttime lowest temperature will be between -4 to -3°C, with no wind or snow weather expected.

There will be no significant cold air entering Beijing in the coming week, with the highest temperature maintained between 6°C to 7°C.
