N This Paper reporter Peng Jianwen Yang Jiong. At the age of 48, Meng from Inner Mongolia once committed a crime due to drug addiction, which led to the destruction of his family and loss of loved ones; after being released from prison, he embarked on a path of anti-drug advocacy. Over the course of three years, he cycled through 700 counties and cities, successfully helping more than 30 people quit drugs. These past few days, Meng has arrived in Quanzhou, searching for drug users who need help.

Sample One: Addicted to Cough Medicine like Life Itself, Uninterrupted for Six Years

"Cough medicine turned me into a slave to drugs, I would get tremors in my hands if I didn't drink it" "My brother-in-law's alcohol addiction is like drug addiction, drinking eight or nine bottles of alcohol daily but rarely eating meals"... Yesterday, as soon as the news of 'Anti-Drug Knight Meng coming to Quanzhou' spread, many citizens called the Haibu Hotline 968111, hoping to receive Meng's help.

"I hope more users of traditional drugs will actively contact me," said Meng. Some drug users, having lost confidence in themselves and fearing exposure of their privacy, are reluctant to contact him. Next, he plans to get involved in community-based drug rehabilitation efforts in Quanzhou, hoping that more people can truly reintegrate into society with his help.

According to Xiao Yu, her brother-in-law's business had always been smooth sailing, and there were no emotional traumas, yet somehow he became addicted to alcohol. Initially, her brother-in-law only drank a little "light alcohol", but later developed the habit. He could down a small bottle of 38-degree alcohol in one gulp, drinking eight or nine bottles daily.

□ "The Anti-Drug Knight Has Arrived in Quanzhou! Urgently Seeking Drug Users" Follow-up

"One day later, my brother-in-law’s mental state returned to normal, and he started demanding to go home," said Xiao Yu. After returning home, he began drinking again.

Xiao Long is 23 years old. Six years ago, while still in middle school, he started drinking "cough syrup." At the time, he heard others say that drinking it was "extremely stimulating," so he and his classmates went to the pharmacy to buy it, drinking it every few days. After graduating from high school, he realized he was addicted and had to drink daily without interruption. "If I don’t drink, I feel weak all over and unable to work," he said. As a result, he frequently lost jobs and when out of money, he borrowed from relatives and friends everywhere.

Xiao Yu said, for Meng, drug addiction can be overcome, and alcohol dependency should also have a way to quit, "I hope he can help my brother-in-law break free from this predicament."

Ming (a pseudonym) is Xiao Yu's brother-in-law, aged 38.

Sample Two: Alcohol Addiction, Eight or Nine Bottles a Day

"I'm worried that one day, drinking too much will cost me my life," said Xiao Long. He wants to seek Meng's help to truly kick the "medicine water addiction."

Among yesterday's seekers, two cases stood out uniquely: Xiao Long (a pseudonym), who had long been addicted to "cough syrup"; and another seeker, Ming (a pseudonym), who had an unrelenting passion for alcohol and was diagnosed with "moderate alcohol dependence" at the hospital. Last night, Xiao Long briefly described his situation to Meng via text message. Meng said these two seekers had fallen victim to "alternative drugs": one being medication dependence, the other alcohol dependence. On the road to breaking dependence, he is willing to provide them assistance. From his experience, compared to true drug addicts, these two cases won't find quitting too painful. They just need to be convinced psychologically, then start a forced detox period at home before proceeding with relevant treatment, which would be more effective.

Xiao Yu said recently, whenever Ming drinks, he gets into arguments with others and smashes things at home, exhibiting poor drinking behavior. A few days ago, the family took him to the hospital, where the test results showed "moderate alcohol dependence," leading to isolation and mandatory treatment.

"I've just gotten married, and all the money in the house comes from my wife's earnings. I want to quit," said Xiao Long. Nowadays, he spends 100 yuan daily on "medicine water," which places a heavy burden on his family. Because of this, he also wants to quit the "medicine water addiction," but each time he tries, he cannot last half a day before his hands start trembling.
