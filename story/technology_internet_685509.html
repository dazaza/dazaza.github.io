Pudong Company Registration Tax System Reform Pilot Program: Policy Guidelines on Service Levy Management

(1) Regarding Mixed Operations

If the main business is not within the scope of the Business Tax to VAT reform, but includes taxable services from the pilot program, VAT should only be paid for the pilot taxable services, while the main business continues to pay Business Tax.

(2) Calculation of Taxable Amount

1. General VAT Taxpayers

Typically use the general tax calculation method, Pudong company registration [http://www.shanghaijingshi.com/] calculates output VAT at three tiers: 17%, 11%, and 6%. The formula for calculating the taxable amount is:

Taxable amount = current period output VAT - current period input VAT; Output VAT = tax-excluded sales × rate; Tax-excluded sales = tax-inclusive sales ÷ (1 + rate).

Currently, projects where general VAT taxpayers can deduct input VAT mainly include purchasing raw materials, fuel, machinery equipment, etc., as well as receiving taxable services and labor.

2. Small-Scale VAT Taxpayers

Use a simplified tax calculation method, paying VAT according to the tax-excluded sales multiplied by a 3% collection rate. The formula for calculating the taxable amount is:

Taxable amount = tax-excluded sales × collection rate; Tax-excluded sales = tax-inclusive sales ÷ (1 + collection rate).

3. General VAT Taxpayers Implementing Simplified Taxation

During the [Business Tax to VAT] pilot period, general VAT taxpayers providing public transportation services such as ferry passenger transport, bus passenger transport, rail transport, taxi services, etc., may choose the simplified tax calculation method, paying VAT at a 3% collection rate based on tax-excluded sales. The formula for calculating the taxable amount is the same as that for small-scale VAT taxpayers.

(3) Regarding Sales Amounts Pudong company registration [http://www.shanghaijingshi.com/]

1. If taxpayers provide freight forwarding services and make payments abroad, the provisions regarding sales amounts in the "Pilot Matters Regulations" apply, [if pilot taxpayers provide taxable services and are subject to differential Business Tax collection under national Business Tax policies, they may calculate their sales amounts as the balance after deducting payments made to non-pilot taxpayers from the total receipts and extra charges received.]

2. As confirmed by the State Administration, the scope of [sales amount deduction full expenditure] has been adjusted to [income and expenditure same scope deduction].

For general taxpayers, the calculation formula is:

Tax payable = (sales revenue - payment amount) ÷ (1 + rate) × rate - input VAT

For small-scale taxpayers, the calculation formula is:

Tax payable = (sales revenue - payment amount) ÷ (1 + collection rate) × collection rate

In the declaration form, the invoice amount for the current period's taxable reduction items in the "Current Period Sales Amount Deduction Project Amount Detail Table" (Column 3) is the full invoice amount; the actual deduction amount for the current period (Column 4) = the current period's taxable reduction project invoice amount (and less than or equal to the corresponding inclusive sales amount) - the actual deduction tax amount for the current period (Column 5); the actual deduction tax amount for the current period (Column 5) = the current period's taxable reduction project invoice amount (and less than or equal to the corresponding inclusive sales amount) ÷ (1 + rate or collection rate) × rate or collection rate.

3. If turnover or sales amount is settled in foreign currency, it should be converted into RMB for calculation. The conversion rate can be chosen as the RMB exchange mid-price on the day of occurrence or on the first day of the month (the Business Tax regulations and the post-pilot VAT regulations are consistent). 4. Payments made by taxpayers providing transportation services or financial lease services to pilot small-scale taxpayers, if they meet the original Business Tax differential taxation policy, are allowed to be deducted from the sales amount.

(4) Regarding Input VAT

1. If the pilot general taxpayer was not a general taxpayer before 2012, the input VAT obtained before becoming a general taxpayer cannot be offset.

If the pilot general taxpayer was already a general taxpayer before 2012, they can offset the input VAT included in goods and services consumed in the sale of goods and processing repair services obtained in 2011 according to the current VAT regulations, but cannot offset the input VAT included in goods and services used in projects included in the pilot scope that year.

(5) Regarding Tax Payment Location Pudong company registration [http://www.shanghaijingshi.com/]

1. If an enterprise registered elsewhere registers for tax in this city, its branch will be considered a fixed business entity in this city and should be included in the pilot scope as per regulations; if it does not register for tax in this city, it will be considered a fixed business entity elsewhere and should declare and pay Business Tax at the location of the institution according to the current Business Tax regulations.

2. Individuals who have not registered for tax should be treated as non-fixed business entities and declare taxes to the competent tax authority where the taxable service occurs. However, for non-fixed business entities whose institution location or residence is in the pilot area and provide taxable services outside the pilot area, they should declare and pay VAT to the competent tax authority at the institution location or residence.

(6) Regarding Non-Trade Certification

In non-trade certification, if a general taxpayer in the pilot area uses a general payment receipt for input tax offset and the overseas enterprise being withheld requests the original general payment receipt, the current non-trade procedure operations can continue to be followed, with the general payment receipt serving as the input tax offset voucher for taxpayers and issuing relevant tax completion certificates to the overseas enterprise.

(7) Regarding Cross-Year Lease Contracts

[For lease contracts signed by pilot taxpayers on December 31, 2011 (inclusive) or earlier that have not been fully executed, Business Tax shall continue to be paid according to the current Business Tax policy until the contract expiration date.] The term [signed on December 31, 2011 (inclusive) or earlier] refers to the official signing date of the lease contract being December 31, 2011 (inclusive) or earlier. If the renewal date is also on December 31, 2011 (inclusive) or earlier, it will be regarded as a lease contract signed on December 31, 2011 (inclusive) or earlier that has not been fully executed, and Business Tax shall continue to be paid according to the current Business Tax policy until the contract expiration date.
