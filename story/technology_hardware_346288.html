["Retweet"] The life of overseas students born in the 80s and the post-80s in China

by zhiyun0972 on 2010-04-15 19:20:18

I just saw an interesting post online and I've re-posted it here... Personally, I think this post is more entertaining than anything else. Just take it as entertainment, no need to take it too seriously... But there are many truths indeed. :) Let me use myself as an example for comparison... The red words are my comments.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Academic qualifications and language skills.

Domestic Post-80s: Except for a few prestigious universities in China, the rest are basically just going through the motions. After four years of undergraduate study, students basically learn nothing. (This might vary from person to person... I didn't attend university in China, so I don't have much to say about this.) Some families with less favorable financial conditions have to prepare tens of thousands of yuan for their children's university tuition, only to find that after graduation, their children may need 2-3 years to earn back what their parents invested. As for foreign language abilities, if one isn't self-motivated, whether studying Japanese or English, it won't be useful without personal effort. There's also not much of an environment for learning; attending a foreign teacher training course will cost even more money.

A four-year undergraduate degree == Tens of thousands of hard-earned yuan from parents

Foreign teacher training course == Thousands of yuan spent

Chinese universities == Strict entrance, lenient exit, wasting time. (In some ways, I agree with this. At least, this is true for most cases.)

Overseas Post-80s: Overseas university education systems are relaxed but also strict. It's not easy to easily obtain a diploma, Chinese students have to work twice as hard. Additionally, overseas universities demand comprehensive development from students, which is beneficial for one's overall quality. The foreign language environment overseas is very conducive to improvement, and it's common for someone to speak three or more languages fluently, some can even speak five or more. (Most of this is true, except for the last point, I'm unsure. People who can fluently speak three languages or more are extremely rare, at least I haven't seen many, and I certainly can't. I've just barely started learning Korean... As for those who can speak five languages or more... Are they gods? )

A four-year undergraduate degree is proportional to the amount of money parents spend. (I disagree... In today's global economic downturn and high employment pressure, I don't feel that graduates from domestic universities are particularly favored... Of course, I'm referring to the general situation, excluding top-tier schools.)

Overseas universities == Easy entrance, strict exit, working hard with everything on the line!

2. Catchphrases.

Domestic Post-80s: Damn it. Shit. Hell.

Overseas Post-80s: Shit... What the hell...

(For some reason, I feel the opposite. Among the overseas students I know, very few curse in English, while most of them use extremely fluent and proud Chinese curses like "Your mother," "SB," "Grandpa," "Damn," etc... Conversely, in China, I've heard and seen people on the streets shouting "FUCK" and "SHIT" fluently and proudly many times... Even my younger brother knows these words, and he tells me they're popular in his school... Wow...)

3. Marriage.

Domestic Post-80s: Graduate, work, get married, have kids, worry, get laid off, argue, divorce.

Overseas Post-80s: Study, graduate; graduate, study; internship, work, make money; further study, read master's degrees; play around, don't fall in love.

(I haven't reached this stage yet, so I'll refrain from commenting.)

4. Friends.

Domestic Post-80s: Good friends selected from elementary school, middle school, high school, and university, maintained until entering the workforce, still close friends.

(Seems like most friends made in university become lifelong friends...)

Overseas Post-80s: Good friends selected from elementary school, middle school, high school, and university, maintained until going abroad for studies; later friends are those met overseas who become close friends, but it's hard to make good friends overseas. Domestic friends, over time, seem to develop an invisible barrier, making one feel uncomfortable.

(It's indeed hard to make close friends overseas, at least I haven't made any yet... Undoubtedly, as time passes and contact decreases, along with differing experiences, it's inevitable to feel a sense of estrangement between domestic friends... Until eventually, no one understands each other anymore... This is truly a sad thing... Therefore, to avoid this, maintaining regular contact is key...)

5. Topics.

Domestic Post-80s: Changing jobs, promotions, changing boyfriends/girlfriends, lack of money, wanting to go abroad, favorite phrase is 'My husband/wife, my son/daughter' how they are doing.

Overseas Post-80s: Changing jobs, gaining experience, reading master's degrees, reading PH.Ds, how to loan for schooling, scholarships, lack of money, wanting to return home, favorite phrase is, I broke up again (for the Nth time), returning home for arranged marriages, returning home to work, obtaining foreign status, settling down.

(I only agree with the first part... The latter depends on the individual.)

6. Health.

Domestic Post-80s: Resting at home when sick, taken care of by parents, comforted by boyfriends/girlfriends, unwilling to go to the hospital, loving to be sick (don't have to go to school or work).

Overseas Post-80s: Can't afford to rest when sick, no one takes care of you, want to go to the hospital but have no money. Don't like being sick (can't go to class or work, no money).

(This part seems a bit heart-wrenching... But it's very true... People who haven't experienced it will never understand...)

7. Love.

Domestic Post-80s: Wealthy ones find N playmates, then get married; poor ones also find N playmates, then break up. Normal ones fall in love and get married. Those who haven't had enough continue their romantic escapades.

Overseas Post-80s: Rich or poor, both play around, don't talk about love. Loving others is too troublesome, investing emotions is too exhausting. Normally, after falling in love, they break up, continuing endless romantic escapades because everyone doesn't know where each other's future lies. Many pair up for company, conveniently solving physiological needs.

(I strongly disagree with this part... At least most of the overseas students I know date due to the emptiness of their material and spiritual lives abroad, using it to pass the time and indulge in infinite yearnings... Of course, as a new era socialist youth, I've remained single all along. I'm determined to contribute to our country's socialist construction after returning home... Sweat... The original poster starts daydreaming again... Actually, my ambitions aren't that great, nor am I that enlightened... I just want to solve the basic needs problem, then marry and have a child, living happily every day... Staying in this barren place, without a single friend, the whole school empties out during holidays, the people around me are either vacationing with their partners or enjoying their own little worlds... It's a lie to say I don't want a girlfriend like them... But the harsh reality forces me to remain single... Who would want me anyway?)

8. Parents.

Domestic Post-80s: More and more啃老族 (living off parents), asking parents for houses and cars when getting married; leaving children with parents after marriage to save trouble.

Overseas Post-80s: There are also many啃老族, but more people are self-reliant. A strange environment and culture cultivate our resilience; we rarely talk to our parents about anything, solving problems ourselves, except when asking for money. Living without anyone taking care of us, we actually miss being able to act childish and spoiled in front of our parents. We are the Post-80s who don't want to grow up. (This accurately reflects the heartaches of many overseas students... Strongly agree.)

9. Future.

Domestic Post-80s: Live day by day, whether rich or poor, life goes on, be happy, don't give yourself unnecessary trouble, there are plenty of people worse off than me.

Overseas Post-80s: Each day is annoying, when will it end? Afraid to think about the future, every day feels helpless, living a life of confusion, moving between work -- school -- home, that's it. Why am I doing this? Don't want to return home. Waiting, enduring, and persisting for foreign identity and qualifications.

(I think this is the absolute truthful reflection of my current psychological state...)

10. Personality characteristics.

Domestic Post-80s: Selfish, capricious, helpless, often complain about depression, dependent, rely on connections, parents, power, admire brands.

Overseas Post-80s: Silent, arrogant, independent, selfish, depressed every day, rely on oneself, admire brands.

(In this section, "silent, independent, depressed every day, relying on oneself" applies to me, the rest varies from person to person.)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PS: It's not that we are unfriendly, it has nothing to do with arrogance.

Please understand overseas students, we are helpless, suffering and enduring.

Not explaining doesn't mean I haven't suffered.

Not smiling doesn't mean I'm not nice.

Not talking doesn't mean I won't fight back.

Not dating doesn't mean I'm not pursued.

Not returning home doesn't mean I don't miss home.

Not crying doesn't mean I'm strong.

Being silent doesn't mean I'm weak.

Do you understand? Do you get it?

You don't understand, you don't get it.

(The author's heartfelt words, also the heartfelt words of countless overseas students...)
