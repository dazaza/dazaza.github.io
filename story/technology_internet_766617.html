Two key Facebook managers quit, one of whom is set to join Alibaba

by anonymous on 2013-09-24 20:59:16

Video Advertising Project Manager Justin Shaffer

According to the Wall Street Journal, two key managers at Facebook are set to leave: Justin Shaffer, the product manager leading video advertising products, and Ashley Zandy, manager of the corporate communications team.

This is the latest in terms of personnel departures for the still rapidly growing company. Facebook plans a significant expansion of its workforce this year.

Informed sources say that Shaffer has yet to announce his next move. The video advertising product, led by Shaffer and Product Marketing Director Jim Squires, has experienced repeated delays in launch, greatly disappointing some advertisers.

Shaffer is a well-known manager at Facebook who has helped lead several projects, including the "Groups" feature and the original check-in tool "Places." He joined Facebook in 2010 when his live event check-in service startup Hot Potato was acquired by the company. He also worked for several years in the digital department of MLB Advanced Media (Major League Baseball), where he was responsible for creating MLB.com.

Sources revealed that Shaffer is considering forming a venture capital fund.

Ashley Zandy

Zandy was a trusted aide to Zuckerberg. She joined Facebook in April 2012 as Corporate Communications Manager, overseeing earnings disclosures, information dissemination, and other matters related to the finance department, corporate development, and legal affairs. She also served as a spokesperson for Facebook, assisting with the day-to-day management of the company.

Prior to joining Facebook, Ashley worked for over five years at Runswick Group (the photo above shows her with colleagues and a Wall Street Journal reporter during her time at Runswick). Runswick Group is an international consulting firm specializing in crisis public relations. Earlier, Ashley worked for Brainerd Communicators, providing investment relations consulting and support services to international clients across various industries.

With dual professional backgrounds in corporate consulting and crisis PR, it's not hard to see the value of this career manager.

In addition, Ashley is currently a board member of the Silicon Valley chapter of the Public Relations Society of America (PRSA). She maintains close ties with PRSA and the New York, San Francisco, and Silicon Valley chapters of the National Investor Relations Institute — which essentially vouches for her extensive network.

This accomplished female executive graduated with honors from the University of Florida with a Bachelor’s degree in Public Relations, focusing on business management.

Notably, Doug Serton, former corporate PR experience at AOL (America Online), gave the following evaluation on Ashley Zandy's public profile:

Ashley is an energetic person, straightforward and easy to get along with. As a seasoned expert in corporate PR consulting and PR strategy, her long-term professional experience has earned her immense trust and respect from peers, managers, and external partners alike.

In the industry, she is a role model; she brings the same passion to both her work and everything she pursues in life. Moreover, her energy is infectious, her talent seems inexhaustible, and her integrity is impeccable.

To put it simply in the most colloquial terms, "She's incredibly talented, and her character is top-notch!"

Can the hiring of foreign executives smooth the way for Alibaba's overseas expansion? Regardless, considering the recent hires like Hugo Barra from Android by Xiaomi and Ashley Zandy by Alibaba, it's undoubtedly a positive sign that Chinese local companies are beginning to attract international talent. This could be beneficial in many ways.
