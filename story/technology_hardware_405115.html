Love, as it is said, is that you still care about the other when all the feelings, enthusiasm and romance are gone.

by vnsdct441 on 2011-04-05 15:01:07

Love is when you take away the feeling, the passion, the romance, and you find out you still care for that person. Love is when, after removing all feelings, passion, and romance, you still care for that person.

Walking hand in hand on the street for one day is a one-night stand; walking hand in hand for one year is a romance; walking hand in hand for five years is an emotion; walking hand in hand for ten years is family affection. If you can still walk hand in hand thirty years later, that's true romance.

Be who you are and say what you feel because those who mind don't matter and those who matter don't mind. Be yourself and express your feelings, because those who care too much don't matter, and those who truly matter won't mind.

You're gone, and I cannot see you off. Your path, I've paved with my gaze. When I miss you, I just stand here and look far into the distance because life may not have paths, but it must have genuine feelings. Those rays of sunshine, those raindrops, those eastern winds, those warm mists, those flowers, those blessings, have all dissipated into thin air since your departure. But from now on, there will be a tearful star in the horizon, forever gazing at the back you once left.

One day someone will walk into your life, then you will realize that love is always worth waiting for. One day, when someone enters your life, you'll realize that true love is always worth waiting for.

Perhaps the best version of oneself is to be a little quieter. Traveling through one city after another, walking down one street after another, looking up at one sky after another, witnessing one farewell after another. But I wonder, why do we grow up so quickly in the wind? Those blossoms, those sunsets, those pure and clear moments, that crystalline youth, and that youthful sorrow, how do they pass through my body and flow so cleanly?

The love you give, you don't know if the other person will accept it! Even so, you still need to give your sincere heart! Because love and giving are proportional. A person might prefer fewer sweet words and more practical actions... That's how it is. You give a lot, but whether you get real affirmation depends on yourself! Loving someone is hard, requiring complete understanding of the other person... Pondering their psychology, relieving their worries... Even giving up everything precious for them!

It has less blind devotion and more appreciative smiles; less torment of longing and more graceful composure; fewer unnecessary entanglements and more harmonious trust; less heavy responsibility and more romantic ease; less devastating harm and more rational serenity.

A person has only one heart, but it has two chambers. One houses happiness; the other houses sorrow. Don't laugh too loudly, or you might wake up the sorrow next door.

Emancipate yourself from your past. The only way to move forward is to stop looking back! Free yourself from the past, as the only way to progress is to stop looking behind.
