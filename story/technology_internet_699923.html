What are the causes of bloody stool? Is rectal bleeding caused by hemorrhoids? How to check? Experts from Shenzhen Yuan Da Anal Hospital pointed out that blood coming out of the anus, or mixed with stool, with feces containing blood or presenting as bloody stool, is referred to as bloody stool. Bloody stool in children is often caused by rectal polyps, with bright red blood, painless, and blood not mixed with stool. In adults, if mucus-like bloody stool appears along with lower abdominal pain, increased bowel movements, etc., it is generally a symptom caused by ulcerative colitis. Is rectal bleeding caused by hemorrhoids? Then why does bloody stool occur, and what are the causes?

Experts from Shenzhen Anal Specialty Hospital [http://www.zhichuang360.com/] explained that rectal bleeding is not necessarily caused by hemorrhoids, and there are five main causes:

1. Digestive tract diseases: The stool may appear tarry or black-red, indicating bleeding from the upper digestive tract; if the blood is red, it could be due to bleeding from the lower digestive tract.

2. Anal fissure: Bright red blood, dripping or on toilet paper, with severe anal pain after defecation for fresh anal fissures.

3. Hemorrhoids: Rectal bleeding occurs during or after defecation, with bright red blood, not mixed with feces, and the amount can vary.

4. Rectal and colon polyps: Bright red blood, painless, not mixed with stool.

5. Rectal cancer: Bright red blood, dripping on the surface of the stool; in late stages, accompanied by a feeling of heaviness in the anus and rectum, significant weight loss, increased bowel movements, alternating constipation and diarrhea.
