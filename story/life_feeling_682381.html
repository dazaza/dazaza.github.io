That Christmas love, is unforgettable

That year's Christmas, my girlfriend and I were in love. The girlfriend at that time has now become my wife. We went to the church for the first time.

We first went to a Christian church. That church had the architectural style of a dream western one, with a huge cross on top.

That day, this church happened to invite a "famous mouth" patriarch from Wenzhou, Zhejiang to give a lecture. The lecture was about love, family, and filial piety. The patriarch's deep baritone was so magnetic, we unconsciously listened for an entire afternoon, feeling both excited and moved. The best part was when everyone sang religious songs together accompanied by an old church piano. One reason was that it was embarrassing to leave rudely, and secondly, that Christmas song was indeed beautiful.

Thirdly, the atmosphere of everyone singing together accompanied by a piano was extremely warm. The one playing the piano accompaniment was a young girl, but I didn't know if she was a nun. I originally wanted her to critique a song called "Merry Christmas" that I wrote the lyrics and composed the music for. However, because she was busy, she recommended a hymn book to me, which I still keep to this day. That day, the church provided free communion. We knew the principle of "there's no such thing as a free lunch," and of course there wouldn't be a free dinner either. After eating, naturally, one would have to "voluntarily" make offerings.

So we didn't take the communion. We heard that there was a Catholic church near that Christian church, so we asked those Christians, but they all shook their heads and said they didn't know.

We ended up asking for directions ourselves and found it. This place was only separated from that Christian church by a narrow road, but it was located in a secluded alley. We gently pushed open the dark and creaky old door. Here, it was just a small farmhouse courtyard. Compared to the bright, spacious, and bustling Christian church not far away, this place could truly be described as dark, narrow, and deserted.

There was only an elderly farming nun here. In the middle of the church hung a portrait of the Virgin Mary. Everywhere were religious paintings, and of course, there was also a large "cross," creating a unique atmosphere and scenery. From this nun, we finally learned that Christians believe in Jesus, while Catholics believe in the Virgin Mary.

We also finally understood that even though they are Western religions with the same origin, they can be "within hearing distance but never interact." The relationship between the two was no different from commercial competition between Western companies.

Before that day, we had spent a romantic Christmas Eve. We went dancing at a dance hall where she lived, at "Hebei Normal University." It was the kind of dance hall where a one-yuan dance ticket could bring a female dance partner, very simple and rudimentary.

Inside were spirited university students who were officially enrolled, except for my girlfriend and me. Without dazzling neon lights or captivating sound equipment, the vast "dance hall" only had a few large incandescent light bulbs and an old dual-cassette recorder. But like those university students, we crazily sang and danced on the ground filled with rain pits and garbage, following Wang Luobin's "Youth Dance Song." Until everyone together welcomed the exciting moment of Christmas Eve — midnight, when everyone cheered and jumped together. After the romance came peace, and the most disappointing part of the holiday was after I saw her off, I had to go home alone, feeling lonely.

Christmas is a big party and carnival. It doesn't belong only to Christians and Catholics, but also to us who love life and enjoy life. We seriously discussed and dreamed of having a wedding ceremony like Prince Charming and Snow White's in a church. Finally, we heard that only believers could do that. In the end, we had to have a traditional Chinese one.

To this day, we (perhaps only I) still can't forget that year's Christmas and that romantic love story that wandered inside and outside the church. Everything back then is vividly remembered, as if it were right before our eyes.
