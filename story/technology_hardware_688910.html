Metersbonwe Denies Being Trapped in "Inventory Crisis", Claims Discount Sale is Promotion - NetEase Finance

by anonymous on 2012-03-03 16:07:55

Recently, China's fashion giant - Metersbonwe was rumored to be caught in an "inventory crisis". According to the third quarter report of Metersbonwe as of September 30, 2011, its inventory reached 2.9 billion yuan, accounting for nearly half of the net profit. After visiting the largest Metersbonwe flagship store on Chunxi Road in Chengdu yesterday, it was found that apart from the first floor where spring clothing new arrivals were displayed, the second to fifth floors were all undergoing a clearance sale with prices starting at 39 yuan. In response, relevant personnel from Metersbonwe headquarters denied the claim that the "clearance" was due to high inventory pressure during an interview with Chengdu Business Daily, stating that this was just a form of promotion by the company. From the third quarter report of Metersbonwe as of September 30, 2011, Chengdu Business Daily learned that the inventory of Metersbonwe was 2.9 billion yuan, while in the same period of 2009, Meibang's inventory was only 900 million yuan. The securities issued report showed that Metersbonwe's inventory had dropped to 2.5 billion yuan by the end of 2011, with over 1.5 billion yuan worth of goods being off-season products. The report pointed out that Metersbonwe's net assets were approximately 3.2 billion yuan, and the off-season clothes in the warehouse already accounted for nearly half of its net assets. "We have always been dealing with the pressure of inventory, but it's not as serious as the media reports," relevant personnel from Metersbonwe headquarters told reporters. They mentioned that in 2011, Metersbonwe's operating revenue increased, and the sales scale was growing rapidly, so the stock preparation kept increasing, leading to a rise in inventory volume. Yesterday, Chengdu Business Daily visited the largest Metersbonwe flagship store on Chunxi Road. It was observed from the second to the fifth floor that there was a large amount of off-season clothing piled up, including coats, skirts, shirts, short-sleeved T-shirts, etc. Sales staff informed that Metersbonwe has been continuously conducting promotional activities aimed at providing good quality at low prices. Regarding this, relevant personnel from Metersbonwe headquarters told reporters that this should not be called "inventory", but rather "stock". There are two reasons for the "stock": First, the winter clothing sales cycle was extended when winter arrived in 2010, thus the data compiled in the third quarter financial report of 2011 appeared to show a large stock volume; Second, every winter, companies prepare goods in advance to deal with the labor shortage in the following year, which also increases the stock. She indicated that with the arrival of the peak season for spring and summer goods, the stock volume would significantly decrease. Yesterday, Gao Jianfeng, General Manager of Shanghai Bogai Consulting Co., Ltd., stated in an interview with Chengdu Business Daily that after going public, Metersbonwe expanded its stores and increased investment in direct-operated stores, which increased asset income. However, once sales slowed down, inventory pressure would increase.
