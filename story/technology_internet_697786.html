Xiaomi smartphone's sky-high repair fees draw controversy, service promises hard to honor

On March 1st, according to a comprehensive report by China News Service, Xiaomi phones, priced below 2000 yuan, had repair costs reaching up to 1800 yuan. Such exorbitant fees made the Xiaomi phone, which had already been controversial due to "hunger marketing" and the "restart gate," once again become the focus of public opinion. Recently, negative news about this popular phone has been frequently exposed. A consumer complained to the media that the repair process for his Xiaomi phone took 10 days, far exceeding Xiaomi's promise of a "five-day limit."

A 1999-yuan phone cost 1800 yuan to repair

Recently, reports about Xiaomi's "astronomical repair fees" have appeared in the press. It was learned that this incident originated from a Xiaomi phone user named "Bad Stone," who posted on a forum that his phone had gotten wet but still functioned normally, except for a small spot in one corner of the screen. When he sent it back to the factory for repairs, Xiaomi's after-sales service stated that the repair fee would be 1800 yuan, a price only 199 yuan cheaper than the 1999-yuan Xiaomi phone. "Bad Stone" also mentioned that aside from the "astronomical" repair fee, Xiaomi's after-sales service was also "troublesome": the customer service hotline could only be reached once after over 100 attempts; online customer service required waiting in line for an entire morning just to get through.

Liang Wanqiang, one of the founders of Xiaomi (Weibo)[http://weibo.com/wanqiangli?zw=tech], publicly responded to the issue, stating that "this 1800-yuan price is not the price of the repair parts, but rather the price of replacing with a brand new bare unit (excluding battery and charger packaging)." He also stated that after-sales service adheres to the principle of operating close to cost. According to a report by the Beijing Business Today, Liang Wanqiang, co-founder and vice president of Xiaomi Company, said yesterday that "Xiaomi plans to invest 2 billion yuan throughout the year to continuously strengthen the construction of its online shopping after-sales service system," focusing mainly on the construction of Mi Home stores, customer service centers, authorized repair points, and warehousing logistics.

It is understood that the quoted prices for some components of Xiaomi phones are not low, with the motherboard material quoted at 999 yuan, and the replacement material for a damaged touch screen quoted at 220 yuan. The actual price may fluctuate based on the engineer's inspection.

Repair or replacement takes at least 10 days

Unlike traditional mobile phone manufacturers, Xiaomi's sales channels do not need to enter traditional mobile phone markets or distribution channels, selling exclusively through online reservations and operator contract phones. In this regard, Lei Jun, chairman of Xiaomi (Weibo)[http://weibo.com/leijun?zw=tech], admitted frankly that sales, supply chains, and production are not the hardest problems; the real challenge lies in service issues.

How difficult is it to repair Xiaomi phones? According to a report by the Daily Economic News, currently, Xiaomi has only a few service stations and contracted repair points. Users in other cities can only return their phones via courier to the factory for replacement or repair. This means users might engage in a "prolonged battle" for replacements or repairs. Recently, a consumer reported that the repair process for his Xiaomi phone lasted 10 days. Customer service personnel from Xiaomi admitted that under normal circumstances, returns and exchanges take only 3 to 5 working days, but going through the steps of "applying for an exchange, cross-regional phone delivery, phone engineer inspection, and re-delivery by courier" might require at least 6 to 8 working days. This makes it difficult for Xiaomi to meet its claim of completing repairs within five days.

Google may serve as a cautionary example

The model of relying on online sales channels was not pioneered by Xiaomi; Google phones were the first to adopt this method. However, Google phones ultimately failed. Industry insiders pointed out that the inability for consumers to see physical units, the lack of distributors leading to no grassroots marketing efforts, and reliance solely on the internet for after-sales service were important reasons for Google's failure.

Now, similar situations are recurring with Xiaomi phones. Analyst Geng Yan from CCID Consulting stated: "Compared to other mobile phone manufacturers with years of accumulated after-sales service outlets, Xiaomi's rapid coverage of 120 cities is too fast, making it difficult to ensure quality. Moreover, from a financial perspective alone, it is extremely challenging." Recently, Xiaomi has indeed been busy raising funds. According to foreign media reports, due to increased demand for working capital following sales growth, Xiaomi, which just raised 90 million USD, is now considering plans for its next round of financing, although specific timing and forms have yet to be determined.
