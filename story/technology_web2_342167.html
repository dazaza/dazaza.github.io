Marketing Planning: What kind of online marketing strategy does your website really need?

by zhanggjcg on 2010-04-08 13:29:14

I believe most of you have your own websites or are doing online marketing for other websites. However, many of you may be troubled by the same question: how should I formulate my online marketing strategy? Many people directly choose SEO because it can bring free traffic. But is it really what you need the most?

The industry often debates which online marketing strategy is the most effective. So, what's the answer? The truth is that every website says something different. Each website has its own unique goals and customer base. The most important thing is to understand what your potential customers want and what their internet habits are. At the same time, you need to know what stage your website is in and what capabilities it has. If you deeply understand your customers and yourself, you will be able to easily formulate different strategies for different stages. Whether you're doing e-commerce, writing a blog, managing an enterprise website, or building a small site for Google Adsense, you can find corresponding strategies to achieve success. Of course, success comes from a lot of practice. Don't stick to just one marketing strategy. Only through trying multiple strategies can you know which ones are most effective for your website.

SEO - Search Engine Optimization

Many people think SEO is simply about choosing keywords and posting links on various sites to get free traffic. In reality, it's much more complicated than that. SEO is the most complex online marketing tool I've encountered so far. It involves understanding your customers and industry, researching keywords, deploying network structures, creating and optimizing articles, building external links, tracking data, analyzing results, and then re-analyzing and optimizing again. All of this consumes a great deal of time and money. If a small business doesn't have the appropriate budget or tries to save costs, the final result might not be worth the effort. On the other hand, if a website has a long-term vision and invests heavily in resources, it may eventually receive significant returns from search engines, making SEO one of the most successful investments for the past decade. For example, Luoyang Web Design Company currently offers SEO services.

At the same time, don't forget to investigate whether your potential customers predominantly use search engines. If your target audience is older, you should carefully consider whether it's worth it.

PPC - Pay-Per-Click Advertising

PPC is much easier to understand compared to SEO. Search engines or websites provide a platform where you pay when a customer clicks on your ad. Although PPC algorithms are also quite complex, it's easy to intuitively see how well your ad performs. For many websites, they only need to rank for a few less competitive keywords. Why not spend some money on PPC? You won't have to worry about long-term investment without returns, and you can instantly see problems and make adjustments.

If your website is small and just starting out but already has products to sell, why not try PPC to earn your first pot of gold?

Email Marketing - Email Advertising

Many people may have forgotten about our old friend, email. Although email has been around for a long time, it remains one of the most efficient advertising methods today. A well-planned email advertising strategy can effectively establish a connection with your existing customers, turning them into loyal customers.

If the majority of your website's potential customers are middle-aged elites, then an email advertising strategy is absolutely crucial for you. They may not have time to go on Google, but they will definitely check their emails.

Affiliate Marketing - Affiliate Online Marketing

Money makes the world go round, and the power of commissions drives people to work hard for you. Many large companies have affiliate programs, such as Amazon. But don't assume that only big companies can successfully use affiliate marketing. Many small companies, like the WordPress theme design company WooThemes, have achieved great success with this method. Affiliate marketing also plays a key role in their SEO strategy.

If your product is related to IT products or services, you can certainly try affiliate marketing. The diffusion effect of this method is quite powerful. Of course, you can also use your own website to promote products for others and earn commissions. How did John Chow and Jeremy Schomaker become cash kings? Isn't it thanks to the power of affiliate marketing?

Social Media - Community Online Marketing

This is a relatively new field, and many companies have joined the community online marketing bandwagon with a follow-the-trend attitude. But does the return on the time and energy invested justify the effort? This is hard to determine, as this is widely recognized as the hardest online marketing strategy to measure. Although Avinash and other experts have summarized some measurement methods, they are still very limited. Even Twitter and Facebook struggle to make money themselves, so using them to make money for yourself won't be easy either. Moreover, in communities, the power of the crowd is the greatest. If you mishandle something, you could end up in serious trouble. A classic example is "United Breaks Guitars," which resulted in estimated losses of millions of dollars.

Community networks in China are still in a relatively primitive stage, but I actually like it because it provides a platform for netizens from all over to exchange ideas, right? If your potential customers spend a lot of time on platforms like Digu, Fanfou, or Xiaonei, then go ahead and give it a try.

CRO - Conversion Rate Optimization

I'm not sure if this counts as an online marketing strategy, but it is definitely one of the most important strategies in my opinion. All the strategies mentioned earlier focus on attracting potential customers to your website. However, if your SEO is perfectly done, you rank first and attract a large number of visitors, but only a small percentage buy your products, then your website is simply a failure. Let me give you a math problem: 10,000 people visit your website but only 10 buy your products, versus 100 people visiting your website and 20 buying your products. Who earns more? The ultimate success of the marketing strategies mentioned earlier all depend on the success of CRO.

You might say, I don't know if my conversion rate is good. Here's some data for you. In 2009, the top-ranked online flower shop in the US had a conversion rate of 38.6%, meaning that out of 10 people who visited their site, 4 decided to buy something.

Goal conversion applies to all kinds of websites. Even if you're running a blog, you can set the goal of how many people subscribe to your website as a standard for measuring success. Therefore, regardless of what kind of website you have, you should strive to research how to improve your conversion rate. Specific methods and ideas will be covered in a future post.

After reading this article, has Zhang San's thinking become clearer? There are many other colorful marketing strategies in this ever-changing online world that I haven't listed here. Before implementing any marketing strategy, please thoroughly understand your own website and customers. Only then can you accurately grasp the direction of the future, increasing your chances of success significantly.
