Hello, common methods for caring for patients with axillary bromhidrosis include:

1. Pay attention to personal hygiene and keep the skin clean. Maintain cleanliness and hygiene by frequently washing the affected areas with soap, taking regular showers, and changing clothes often to keep the skin dry. Especially focus on keeping areas like the armpits, groin, and breasts clean, washing them daily with soapy water.

2. Patients with axillary bromhidrosis should wear loose-fitting underwear. Young men and women often prefer tight clothing and jeans, which cause the skin to adhere closely to the clothes, preventing the evaporation of secretions. This is especially true in summer when these areas become warm and humid, serving as a "culture medium" for bacteria, promoting bacterial growth, decomposition, and resulting in special odors.

3. Local medication can be used in the daily care of axillary bromhidrosis. External medications can inhibit and stop sweating, preventing the sweat glands from producing sufficient unsaturated fatty acids or stopping bacterial growth, but this only temporarily avoids the occurrence of odor.

4. Shower after exercising. During intense activities or excessive exercise, heavy sweating is unavoidable, creating favorable conditions for bacterial growth. Therefore, it is important to shower after exercising, clean the skin, and change underwear regularly to reduce bacterial proliferation.

5. Reduce stimulation to the armpit skin. Avoid long-term chronic stimulation to the armpit area, such as frequent shaving or plucking underarm hair, to prevent follicular hyperplasia and enlargement. If the apocrine glands enlarge, they can secrete excessive sweat and increase lipid secretion. Additionally, the warm and moist environment of the armpit, combined with poor ventilation, encourages bacterial growth and reproduction, breaking down excessive unsaturated fatty acids and increasing the odor. Therefore, in the care of axillary bromhidrosis, avoid frequently plucking underarm hair.
