What are the tricks to learning English grammar?

by lk0cswk on 2009-08-13 16:39:33

Learning English is not as difficult as it seems, nor is it as easy; in fact, it can be quite challenging. The question remains: what preparations are necessary to learn English well?

There are two foundations for learning English: vocabulary and grammar. My method for memorizing words is to look at them every day, not using large chunks of time to memorize, but breaking the time into smaller segments, reviewing the words multiple times a day. With repetition, you will gradually remember them. I never force myself to memorize all the words in one go, to avoid feeling discouraged if I don't succeed. Our classmates mostly have college degrees or above, so we should all have a vocabulary of over three thousand words. For the CET-4 exam, having a vocabulary of five thousand words is more than enough. Memorizing two thousand words isn't too heavy a task, especially since the adult level three test is even simpler than CET-4. As for grammar, it's just some language rules. We cannot learn a language like children learning to speak; as adults, we have logical thinking and creative thinking abilities, so we need to study grammar to apply it broadly. My learning method is to do lots of practice problems because exams usually focus on fixed grammar points, so I target these grammar points when doing problems.

**Key Points for Learning English:**

1. You need to remember a certain amount of vocabulary, as it is the foundation of your conversation. Otherwise, how can you start speaking? Therefore, memorizing words is essential. However, my habit of memorizing words is not according to the order in a word book or dictionary. Personally, I find that memorizing words in sequence can be confusing, so my memory habit is to extract unfamiliar words during reading, which helps me remember more structures and usage.

2. You need to turn what you've learned from textbooks into your own knowledge, which requires you to use structures effectively. For example, if you remember the structure "spend some time doing something," you can express the idea of spending time doing something. The key to learning is to be able to apply knowledge broadly, which is what I often tell my students.
