This Wednesday, AC Milan will challenge Tottenham Hotspur in the away match of the UEFA Champions League Round of 16. Due to a two-footed tackle on Spurs defender Vedran Corluka in the first leg, which led to a serious injury for Corluka, Milan midfielder Mathieu Flamini has been heavily criticized by the British media, especially by Tottenham's manager Harry Redknapp. In response, Flamini once again denied being a dirty player.

Even though Flamini had boldly declared his intention to eliminate Tottenham for Arsenal before the first leg, he insisted that he has never been a messy player: "In my four years in the Premier League, I've never injured an opponent. I've never been sent off with a red card in England, never. I am a defensive midfielder, and naturally, I have to do the toughest interception work, but this is the first time I've injured an opposing player. I'm not a dirty player, I had no intention of doing so."

Regarding Redknapp's comments, Flamini said: "I know what people are saying. I respect Mr. Redknapp very much, but I think he cleverly fabricated a story beyond that, so everyone is focusing on it and forgetting about the second leg. Tottenham only has a one-goal advantage. The game in London will be very tough. To be honest, I am very confident, especially after our performance in the second half of the first leg. I believe we can go there and reverse the situation to advance."

"All I did was aimed at the ball, not the player. I didn't plan to use a two-footed tackle. I shouldn't have done it, but it wasn't intentional. I can understand people's complaints, but I think it's part of the game. I apologize for what I did. Unfortunately, he got injured, but I went to their dressing room to apologize to him, and he accepted it. I'm not saying what I did was right, but don't make it more complicated. I've never hurt anyone before. It really hurts to hear people say I intentionally hurt him, but it's not true at all."
