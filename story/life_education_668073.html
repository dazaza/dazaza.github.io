Nurture children to become part of the sports population

by hudazikao on 2012-02-17 14:04:30

Teachers at the Hunan University Self-study Examination Office noticed a research finding: if a person did not like to exercise during childhood, it would be difficult for them to develop an exercise habit as they grew up. This obviously has adverse effects on the quality of life. I feel ashamed that my daughter didn't form an exercise habit, which is related to my dereliction of duty because fathers bear especially important responsibilities in their children's sports. I sincerely hope that all fathers take me as a warning and start educating their children with physical education. According to experts, people who exercise at least three times a week and each time no less than 30 minutes are considered part of the sports population. Fathers should set an example and cultivate their children to become part of the sports population.

Teachers at Hunan University Continuing Education believe that wise parents all place physical education first in their children's growth process. The great thinker Mr. Lu Xun did this. He believed that "wild cattle becoming domesticated cattle, wild boars becoming domesticated pigs, wolves becoming dogs, their wild nature disappeared, but only made enemies happy, without any benefit to themselves."

Hunan University Self-study Examination Network teachers think that in the process of a child's growth, physical education should be the premise of all education, the foundation of all education. How do parents value their children's physical exercise and choose suitable sports for them? The educational advice from Hunan University Self-study Examination teachers is:

1. If you find your child doesn't fit in well and isn't used to interacting with peers, you can suggest they choose team sports such as football, basketball, volleyball, and relay races for exercise.

2. If you find your child often hesitates and is indecisive, then frequently bring your child to participate in activities like table tennis, badminton, tennis, boxing, hurdles, long jump, high jump, fencing, etc. These sports have significant effects on cultivating decisiveness. If you find your child has an irascible temperament and is impulsive, it's best to bring them to participate in activities that test control, such as playing chess, practicing Tai Chi, slow cycling, and shooting. These activities help stabilize emotions.

3. If you find your child lacks confidence, you can bring them to participate in activities such as skipping rope, push-ups, and doing radio calisthenics. After persisting in exercise for a period, you will find that your child's self-confidence has been enhanced.

4. If you find your child cannot perform normally and tends to get nervous easily, bring them to participate in more public and intense sports competitions to train their ability to calmly deal with competition.

5. If you find your child likes to show off and is overconfident, you can choose some more difficult and complex sports such as gymnastics, diving, swimming, and marathons for training. You can also find several opponents for your child so they understand the principle of "there’s always a better person out there."
