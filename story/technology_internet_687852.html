Kobe Bryant turns into a masked flying knight, Lakers easily slaughter the Timberwolves 104-85.

by king9x9u on 2012-03-02 19:31:50

Kobe Bryant Transforms into Masked Flying侠 as Lakers Defeat Timberwolves 104-85

On the evening of February 29th (Thursday), the Los Angeles Lakers defeated the visiting Minnesota Timberwolves with a large score of 104-85, unveiling the curtain for the second half of the season with an exhilarating victory. Their ranking in the Western Conference also leaped to fourth place. Playing injured, Kobe Bryant was diagnosed with a fractured nasal bone and concussion, but he took the court wearing a mask, scoring 31 points, grabbing 7 rebounds, and dishing out 8 assists, astonishing everyone.

Multi-point Offensive Explosion Seals Victory in Three Quarters

The entire game saw the Lakers achieve a season-high tying 30 assists. Under the leadership of Kobe Bryant, the team's offense flowed smoothly, with eight players scoring at least seven points. The team's shooting percentage approached 50%, and substitutes like Steve Blake, Matt Barnes, and Kwame Brown all had commendable performances. Veterans Metta World Peace (Ron Artest) and Derek Fisher also contributed steadily. Relying on a comprehensive offensive explosion, the Lakers had already sealed their victory by the end of the third quarter. Especially in the third quarter, the Lakers outscored the Timberwolves 33-15, establishing a 24-point lead, causing the fourth quarter to effectively enter garbage time.

Paul Gasol, who has been frequently plagued by trade rumors recently, once again performed excellently, scoring 15 points in addition to grabbing 5 rebounds and dishing out 4 assists. In the third quarter, the 2.13-meter tall Gasol participated in fast breaks twice; the first was thwarted by a foul from his Spanish national teammate Ricky Rubio. On the second fast break, Gasol didn't give Rubio a chance to foul him again, faking out the defense before finishing with a one-handed dunk.

After half a season of adjustments, the Lakers' offense finally began to take shape, and the All-Star break provided the veteran Lakers players with a much-needed rest, allowing them to rebound in their performance on the court. After winning this game, the Lakers moved up to fourth place in the Western Conference rankings, meaning that if they can maintain this position, they will have home-court advantage in the first round of the playoffs.

Of course, the second half of the season has just begun, and there are still many uncertainties ahead for the Lakers. Several key players remain deeply affected by trade rumors, and with only half a month left until the trade deadline, the team's roster is likely to undergo significant changes. The road ahead for the Lakers in the second half of the season will not be smooth sailing.

TT Entertainment Kobe: Wearing the Mask Feels Like Drinking My Own Sweat

Due to being fouled by Dwyane Wade during the All-Star Game, Kobe was diagnosed post-game with a fractured nasal bone and mild concussion, temporarily placed on the Lakers' daily observation list. However, Kobe insisted on playing in this game. After a pre-game re-examination confirmed that his concussion was no longer an issue, doctors approved his participation. To protect his fractured nose, Kobe had to wear a specially designed mask during the game.

This was not only the first time in Kobe's NBA career that he wore such a specially designed facial protection mask during a game but also the first time a player from the Lakers wore a mask while playing. This kind of mask affects breathing comfort, especially during intense physical activity. Despite being the first time wearing it, Kobe did not show much discomfort, instead delivering impressive stats of 31 points, 8 assists, and 7 rebounds. Under Kobe's leadership, the Lakers played beautiful team basketball. At one point in the game, the Lakers led by as much as 27 points, completely dominating their opponent throughout.

After the game, Kobe said in an interview: "It feels like having a face sauna, except it's like drinking my own sweat." Despite the discomfort, Kobe must continue wearing the mask. "There's nothing I can do about it. I'll have to keep wearing it until my nose isn't so sensitive anymore."

As for the instigator of the injury, Dwyane Wade, Kobe generously stated: "He's my good friend. I believe Wade didn't intentionally hurt me; he's not that kind of person."

Kobe's perseverance despite injuries has long earned admiration. In recent years, whether it's finger fractures, ankle sprains, or wrist ligament tears, none have stopped Kobe from soaring on the court. Basketball legend Michael Jordan once privately praised Kobe to his biographer: "The guy is simply not human!" A reporter asked Kobe after the game: "What kind of injury would make you sit out?" Kobe laughed and replied: "Don't curse me, alright?"
