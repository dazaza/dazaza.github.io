Li Wei, Deputy Director and Deputy Secretary of the Party Committee of the State-owned Assets Supervision and Administration Commission of the State Council. (Image source: SASAC website) Click here to view all financial news images Li Wei: Seriously study and practice the scientific development concept and do a good job in managing state-owned property rights under new conditions - Speech at the Enterprise State-owned Property Rights Management and Property Rights Trading Institution Work Conference Today, we convene the annual enterprise state-owned property rights management and property rights trading institution work conference. The main task of the meeting is to deeply study and practice the scientific development concept, implement the spirit of the national state-owned assets supervision and administration work conference, summarize the work of 2008, analyze the current economic situation, and study the work of 2009. 2008 was an extremely unusual year. China experienced rare natural disasters such as southern snow and ice freezing and the Wenchuan super earthquake, facing severe challenges brought by the international financial crisis; it successfully hosted an unparalleled Olympic Games and smoothly completed the first spacewalk of astronauts. The past year will inevitably leave a heavy mark in history because of a series of major events. 2008 was also a year for our various levels of SASAC to seriously study and practice the scientific development concept and steadily advance the management of state-owned property rights. The property rights management system has been further improved, all kinds of basic management work have been further strengthened, the transfer of state-owned property rights has become more standardized and orderly, and the informatization level of property rights management has steadily increased. The effective conduct of property rights management work has played a positive role in stabilizing and recovering the national economy. Under very difficult external circumstances, we could achieve the above results, which was not easy. I would like to take this opportunity to express my heartfelt gratitude and sincere greetings to comrades engaged in state-owned property rights management and property rights trading at all levels of SASAC across the country on behalf of the State-owned Assets Supervision and Administration Commission of the State Council and Comrade Rongrong! Below, I will discuss three points regarding how to carry out property rights management work in 2009 in combination with the current economic situation. First, correctly analyze and recognize the current economic situation and adjust the thinking of property rights management work in time. Property rights management work is closely related to the economic situation. To do a good job in property rights management, we must make correct analysis and grasp of the domestic and international economic situation. From the perspective of the international economic situation, it has now entered a phase of global economic recession. This US subprime crisis first turned into an international financial tsunami, then into a global economic recession, and has now evolved into a global economic crisis. The speed of spread, the strength of impact, the wide range of involvement, and the depth of influence of this financial crisis are rare in history, and can be said to be a real global economic crisis affecting the whole world. The Great Depression that erupted in the United States in 1929 lasted for seven years, but mainly impacted the economies of Western countries such as the US and Europe, and was generally still a regional issue, having little impact on many underdeveloped countries. The Asian financial crisis in 1997 and 1998 and the bursting of the dot-com bubble in 2000 also caused some damage to the development of the world economy, but their impact was also local. Historically, no shock wave has had such a large and wide impact on the entire world economy as this financial crisis. The reason is mainly that this financial crisis occurred against the backdrop of the accelerated development of economic globalization in the last decade. This financial crisis originated in the United States. After the exposure of the subprime problem in June 2007, some Western economists were still optimistic about it. Former Chairman of the Federal Reserve, Alan Greenspan, said in December 2007 that the impact of the subprime crisis was getting smaller and would be over by the first quarter of 2008. Former US Treasury Secretary Paulson also said during his visit to China in February 2008 that the subprime crisis would bottom out in the second quarter, and the economy would see light in the third quarter. Until September 2008, the majority of Western economic figures held an optimistic attitude. But after October 2008, the Baltic Dry Index (BDI) plummeted sharply, oil, copper, iron, and other energy and resource prices dropped significantly, marking the entry into a period of global economic depression. Affected by this, the international manufacturing industry shrank dramatically. In December 2008, the US manufacturing index fell to its lowest point in 28 years, and the three major automobile companies, General Motors, Ford, and Chrysler, faced the risk of bankruptcy. The UK manufacturing industry saw its largest drop in 27 years. Japan's manufacturing sales plummeted, and Toyota Motor Corporation expected a loss of 350 billion yen (about 3.9 billion USD) in 2008. The employment situation in major European and American countries was very severe, with unemployment rates continuously rising. In 2008, the US lost 3 million jobs, and the unemployment rate in January 2009 reached 7.6%, the highest level since 1992. Among them, 30 states in the US sought federal government assistance of 140 billion USD due to their inability to pay unemployment benefits. In the UK and France, there were worker strikes due to dissatisfaction with hiring foreign workers. The growth rates of major economies declined sharply. The GDP growth rate of the US in 2008 was 1.1%, better than the World Bank's previous forecast of -0.5%, but compared to the average growth rate of 2.77% in the previous three years, there was a significant decline. Japan's GDP growth rate in 2008 was -1.6%, the first negative growth in nine years. Recently, visiting Japanese guests revealed that Japan's GDP is almost certain to experience consecutive quarterly declines in the first quarter, which would be the first time Japan experienced four quarterly declines in one fiscal year since the war. The EU region also faces numerous difficulties. The economies of Eastern Europe, hit hard by historical massive debts and the financial crisis, are struggling, with the situation far worse than Japan. Currently, Eastern European countries owe more than 4 trillion USD to the West, with 400 billion USD due this year, and some governments face the risk of fiscal collapse. Since the fourth quarter of 2008, the international economic situation has become even more severe. According to Standard & Poor's, in the fourth quarter of 2008, major US listed companies suffered their first overall quarterly loss since 1935. Data shows that the economic contraction in major European and American countries in the fourth quarter of 2008 was the worst in 30 years, with the US economy contracting by 3.3% on a quarterly basis. Excluding inventory factors, operating contraction reached as high as 5.1%. Fed Chairman Ben Bernanke believes that the impact of the US financial crisis on the economy is still unknown. Currently, the general consensus is that the economic recession in major Western countries has not yet hit bottom. As for when it will hit bottom, opinions vary, with some predicting the end of this year, others next year, and still others predicting that the US may follow Japan's path in the 1990s, entering a prolonged period of slow decline. Regarding when a true recovery will occur, it is difficult for political, theoretical, and practical circles to reach a conclusion, with more moderate views suggesting a recovery in 2-3 years. However, the time span of 2-3 years will have a significant impact on the world and China's economy, bringing us many unimaginable difficulties and problems. In the past two days, news reports indicated that the Federal Reserve claimed it would purchase 300 billion USD worth of treasury bonds, including other bonds, equivalent to central bank printing money, essentially shifting the crisis to the whole world, undoubtedly leading to the depreciation of the dollar and laying the groundwork for global inflation, thereby illustrating the severity of the US economic situation. From the perspective of the domestic economic situation, affected by the international financial crisis and its own structural contradictions, China's economic development will face the greatest difficulties and challenges since the beginning of this century. For a long time, China's economic growth has had a high dependence on exports, with net exports contributing 2 percentage points to economic growth, and export dependency reaching 36%. With the deepening of this financial crisis, China's export enterprises were severely hit first. From November 2008 when exports began to decline until February this year, there were four consecutive months of negative growth, at -2.2%, -2.8%, -17.5%, and -25.7%, with the decline expanding month by month. Major industries in the national economy, such as power, coal, petrochemicals, steel, non-ferrous metals, light industry, textiles, building materials, and real estate, are increasingly finding it difficult to operate, with some industries already experiencing overall losses. Against this background, China's GDP growth rate slowed down significantly in 2008, with GDP growth rates for the first, second, third, and fourth quarters being 10.6%, 10.1%, 9%, and 6.8%, respectively, and the full-year GDP growth rate being 9%, a decrease of 2.3 percentage points from the previous year. In January this year, the CPI rebounded slightly, increasing year-on-year by 1%, but in February, the CPI fell again, decreasing year-on-year by 1.6%; the PPI for January and February both decreased, with a year-on-year decrease of 1.6% in January and 4.5% in February. From the perspective of state-owned enterprise operations, the operating profits of state-owned enterprises have dropped significantly. In 2008, central enterprises achieved sales revenue of 11.9 trillion yuan, an increase of 17.9% year-on-year; they realized a total profit of 665.29 billion yuan, a decrease of 34.1%. Provincial regulatory enterprises achieved sales revenue of 6.1 trillion yuan, an increase of 18.9% year-on-year; they realized a total profit of 276.68 billion yuan, a decrease of 24%. In the first two months of this year, the operating situation of state-owned enterprises became even more severe. According to financial express statistics, central enterprises achieved sales revenue of 1.4265 trillion yuan in the first two months, a decrease of 11.3% year-on-year; they realized a total profit of 56.5 billion yuan, a decrease of 52.9% year-on-year. According to statistics, industrial added value grew by 3.8% in the first two months of this year, while state-owned enterprise industrial added value decreased by 1%. Facing the sudden deterioration of the international and domestic economic situation, the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and the State Council have taken a series of measures to promote stable and rapid economic development, quickly launching ten measures to further expand domestic demand and promote economic growth, determining a 4 trillion yuan investment plan to boost domestic demand, and formulating adjustment and revitalization plans for ten major industries. Social consumer goods retail sales increased by 15.2% year-on-year in the first two months, investments increased by 26.5%, and industrial added value rebounded to 11% in February. The Manufacturing Purchasing Managers' Index (PMI) data for January and February were 45.3% and 49%, respectively, rebounding for three consecutive months. On March 13, the BDI index rose to 2201 points, up 232% from the low point of 663 points. From the above data analysis, China's economy has initially shown signs of stabilization. However, from an overall perspective, the situation remains far from optimistic. Generally speaking, 2009 will be the most difficult year for China's economic development since the new century, with great difficulties, many problems, complex situations, and many variable factors. We need to fully estimate the difficulties, consider countermeasures more carefully, prepare fully for a prolonged period of global economic downturn, and adjust the thinking and measures of state-owned property rights management under the premise of correctly recognizing the current severe economic situation. The new economic situation brings challenges and difficulties to our property rights management work, and we need to remain confident and actively respond. But we should also see that the new situation provides new opportunities for improving the level of property rights management, and we need to turn "crisis" into "opportunity" and further enhance the overall level of property rights management work. It can be foreseen that this crisis is a historical opportunity for China's economy to implement the "two transformations," as well as a historical opportunity for the state-owned economy to deepen reforms and adjust structures. We need to seize this rare historical opportunity, accelerate the reorganization of state-owned enterprises, deepen the reform of state-owned enterprises, strengthen the management of state-owned enterprises, and accelerate the pace of state-owned enterprises going global. This will bring a large amount of property rights management affairs and challenges. Under the new situation, doing a good job in property rights management and actively responding to the above challenges in property rights management requires correctly handling several relationships: First, the relationship between consolidation and adjustment. In the six years since the establishment of the State-owned Assets Supervision and Administration Commission of the State Council, we have established a complete set of property rights management systems and explored effective practices for property rights management, and we need to cherish these hard-won achievements, continue to consolidate and strengthen all aspects of property rights management work. However, we should also see that some of our systems and practices cannot meet the needs of economic and enterprise development under the new situation, or even the needs of the operation of China's market economy, and some are even blank spots. Therefore, adjustments need to be made according to changes in the situation, following the requirements of the scientific development concept, adjusting thoughts, practices, systems, and regulatory methods accordingly. Second, the relationship between development and standardization. Standardization is the premise and foundation of property rights management work, and standardization is for better development. In the past, we have done a lot of work in standardizing property rights management, effectively safeguarding the legitimate rights and interests of state-owned assets. But standardization is just the premise, foundation, and means, not the goal. The ultimate goal of property rights management work is to promote the reform and development of state-owned enterprises and the growth of the state-owned economy within the framework of standardization. Under the new situation, we must strive to achieve this fundamental goal by all means, providing strong support for state-owned enterprises to better cope with the economic crisis. Third, the relationship between innovation and risk prevention. Innovation is an inherent requirement for doing a good job in state-owned asset supervision, and an important guarantee for property rights work to continuously adapt to new situations, improve, and reach new heights. We need to continuously innovate in regulatory systems and methods, achieving "dual driving," and striving to elevate property rights management work to a higher level. Property rights trading institutions need to continuously innovate in trading rules, trading methods, trading varieties, etc., to achieve sustainable and healthy development. At the same time, we also need to learn from the lessons of this financial crisis in the process of innovation, accurately grasp the positioning of investors, strengthen risk prevention, innovate within the legal and compliant framework, and effectively prevent possible legal risks, market risks, and public opinion risks in the process of innovation. Fourth, the relationship between regulation and service. Property rights management work is an important foundation and key link in state-owned asset supervision work. In actual work, we must strengthen regulation, standardize operations, and act according to law. But we should also firmly establish a service-oriented mindset, improve service levels, and sincerely serve enterprises. The current economic situation has brought serious difficulties to the reform and development of state-owned enterprises. We need to actively and proactively think of ways and offer suggestions to help enterprises solve various problems involving property rights management, improve work efficiency, and not only talk about regulation without service. We need to continuously improve work quality in the process of service and explore new ideas, models, and methods for property rights management in the process of service.
