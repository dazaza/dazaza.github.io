Girls, don't cry when you read this.

by lwf225 on 2009-10-09 11:43:44

I told you, "I almost fell down the stairs while sweeping them today." I thought you would comfort me and say, "Honey, be careful." But you said, "Sweep slower, that's all."

I was sad, I thought you didn't love me at all, and you didn't care about me.

Later, I found our stairs were exceptionally clean, so clean that they didn't need to be swept; a month later, I realized that this was the result of your taking 5 minutes every day.

I told you, "My bike is broken, it took me half an hour to walk to the bus stop." I thought you would care and say, "Why didn't you take a taxi? Are you tired?" But you said, "Anyway, it's not far, you can lose weight in the meantime."

I was angry, I thought you didn't love me, and you didn't care about me.

The next day, I found your car keys left on the table, as well as the rich breakfast prepared for me.

I told you, "I want to go to Hokkaido, Netherlands, and other places to admire the magnificent sea of flowers." I thought you would care and say, "Where do you want to go? Let's plan it." I was angry, I thought you didn't love me, and you didn't understand me.

Later, I found in the travel magazines at home, whether domestic or foreign reports, as long as there was an introduction to flower viewing on that page, the corner of the page had creases, and there were your notes inside.

I told you, "I'm going out with friends, I'll be back late tonight." I thought you would care and say, "Who are you going out with? Be careful, remember to call or come home earlier." But you said, "Whatever, just be happy."

I got angry, I thought you didn't love me, and you didn't care about me. Later, I came home at 3 in the morning in a huff, and I saw you sleeping on the sofa.

I told you, "My period has come, my stomach hurts so much." I thought you would comfort me and say, "Bear with it, one day will pass." But you said, "Women are so troublesome, can't stand it."

I was sad, I thought you didn't love me, and you didn't spoil me.

Later, there were a lot more chocolates and red beans in the snack cabinet at home, which you bought, but you never ate them. Until a month passed. In the week before and after my period, you cooked red bean soup every day.

I told you, "I am so glad to have married you, you are the best husband." I thought you would happily answer me, "I feel the same way, you are the best wife." But you said, "Since we're already married, what else do you want?"

I was angry, I thought you didn't love me, and you didn't understand me.

Later, I accidentally discovered that you wiped the 40-inch wedding photo by the bedside with tissue before sleeping, and then stared at the photo and smiled foolishly for a long time.

I think I finally understood, under your indifferent appearance, there is a heart that is not good at expressing itself with words, a heart that loves me the most. Originally, you do love me. You just don't say it. This is your way of love, different from others.
