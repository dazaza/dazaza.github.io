The first urination in the morning can indicate the condition of liver function.

by lucky6105 on 2012-03-02 00:59:53

Core tip: Urine is the best indicator of whether your lifestyle and diet are normal. Most people wake up in the morning mainly because of the urge to urinate. Of course, some people urinate drowsily and go back to sleep, so they often don't know the color of their urine. The condition of the liver can be observed from the first urination in the morning. In fact, the first urination is very important and must be carefully observed. Check if the color is very dark, or if there is blood in the urine, or if it is as turbid as rinsed rice water, or if there are many bubbles, with various different phenomena. If there are a lot of bubbles and it is as turbid as rinsed rice water, it means that there is a problem with kidney function, indicating that you ate too salty food or too much food with preservatives yesterday. If the urine is red, the first thing to consider is whether you have a cold or fever, because a cold or fever can affect the blood vessels in the lower abdomen, causing congestion. Some blood vessels are more fragile, leading to microvascular rupture, which will cause hematuria. Secondly, consider the possibility of having stones, because diamond-shaped or multi-angular stones can break microvessels when sliding, making the urine red. Some people may be serious enough to make the entire toilet bowl red. Staying up late, smoking heavily can also easily lead to hematuria, because smoking causes microvascular contraction, affecting oxygen supply, so the face turns cyanotic. Smoking makes the face turn cyanotic, drinking alcohol makes the face turn red. At that time, drinking alcohol while smoking will not change the facial expression. Therefore, I advise everyone to carefully observe urine and feces every morning, then adjust your lifestyle and eating habits, which can prepare for the future and prevent problems before they occur. Among them, the most severe is liver cancer. Once suffering from liver cancer, the patient's survival rate is extremely low, and the disease progresses quickly, leading to death rapidly. The frightening aspect of liver disease is that patients do not have particularly significant symptoms. Patients may experience abdominal distension, chest tightness, decreased appetite, common cold, fever, nausea, etc., but ordinary people usually do not take these phenomena seriously and buy medicine from the pharmacy themselves, leading to deterioration of the condition and even death. Experts explain that general fatigue, abdominal distension, stomach pain, jaundice, loss of appetite, etc., do not indicate poor liver function; but even if one can eat, drink, run, jump, or is an athletic person, it does not mean everything is fine. Because most liver diseases have no symptoms, this is why doctors call liver disease the "biggest hidden killer", so it cannot be taken lightly.
