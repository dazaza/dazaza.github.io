Syncing Control Exclusive Interview: Chris, the Founder of YUNIO Cloud Storage Service Team

by xdash on 2011-11-20 12:30:29

The newly introduced cloud storage service — Yunio (original article here) has garnered widespread attention shortly after the news was released. We also conducted an interview with one of Yunio's founders, Chris, to discuss the questions that everyone is interested in. He shared his product vision and some behind-the-scenes stories about the team.

Q1: First, introduce your team.

I am Chris, and my CTO/partner Rick are from California, USA. I've been an entrepreneur since high school and founded my first internet company while in college in the U.S., which was back in 2001. After graduation, I came to China for the first time and knew this was where I wanted to stay. Rick started coding at the age of seven, became a hacker in his teens and got punished, but later big companies offered him high salaries during high school. He has also experienced many internet startups. More than two years ago, I invited him to move to China and start a business with me, and he really came. After more than two years of ups and downs, we finally successfully obtained angel investment and began developing YUNIO. Over these years, I have met quite a few talented people, and after founding YUNIO, I invited them all over. These people are basically the best in their previous company departments.

Q2: How did you come up with the idea to start a cloud storage project?

The original inspiration came from Fetch.io’s offline download feature (editor's note: see our previous introduction). At that time, I personally experienced how a 700MB video could be downloaded in 2 minutes and begin playing, which was like magic. However, during the research on YUNIO, we found that the offline download feature wasn't a product that could grow big, and it didn't belong in YUNIO. So at the time, we separated it and released Fetch.io to sustain the team. The features you see in YUNIO now were determined after countless reflections.

Q3: Your project has been in preparation for 3 years, during which similar products have appeared on the market. How do you view the changes and development in this market, and how do you view the market competitiveness (differentiation) of your product (launched relatively late)?

We have been paying close attention. I don’t just focus on all domestic and international synchronization products; I pay more attention to user feedback, check their forums, join their QQ groups, and listen to what they are complaining about. Along the way, although many products have emerged, none of them have been good enough quality-wise to make us feel that YUNIO isn’t needed anymore. The only exception is Dropbox, which is slow in China and may be blocked at any time. I remember when the Dropbox founder was looking for investors, the investors mentioned the issue of there already being many cloud storage products on the market. Drew countered by asking if they used those products?

Q4: I can see that a lot of effort has been put into product details, UI, promotional videos, and Weibo operations. Did you hire specialists to handle these aspects?

Thank you! Except for the animations in the video, all design and art work was done by our team (we even designed our office ourselves!). I originally started as a designer, couldn’t stand ugly or poorly experienced products, so I have strict requirements for design and experience. Of course, a large part of the credit goes to our design director, King, whose rich imagination and skills brought the YUNIO robot to life. Also, our product director, Ethan, strictly ensures that nothing less than perfect gets released.

Q5: For cloud storage products, the team's background and strength are important factors in ensuring continuous and stable service. How do you ensure users can use your service without worrying about data security and stability?

We haven't cut costs on hardware and bandwidth, ensuring users don't experience slowdowns. The rest is handled by our CTO. YUNIO stores all files in three distributed copies, and the backend uses Erlang, developed by our development director Fisher for high performance. In terms of technology, all I can do is provide sufficient resources and trust my team. Of course, I also occasionally cause trouble and try every method to break YUNIO ^^

Q6: Can you share any thoughts or plans regarding the future development of the product?

Our goal is to make YUNIO the most convenient file-sharing channel under any circumstances. It's important to emphasize that we are doing file sharing, not file dissemination. File dissemination is another kind of product. We believe that YUNIO's one-click download link feature is convenient, but still has room for improvement. Next, we have a cool idea under study that will make file sharing and team collaboration very easy.

Q7: What is the current financing situation, and what help do you need?

Currently, we have received angel investment and are preparing for round A [...].
