E Ink, a electronic paper manufacturer, got badly affected by the impact of tablet computers.

by anonymous on 2013-08-20 15:49:29

E Ink manufacturer, Prime View International (PVI), recently released its revenue report for the last quarter. The company reported a net loss of 1.01 billion New Taiwan dollars, approximately 33.63 million US dollars. This represents a decline of about 46% compared to the same period last year.

This is not the first time PVI has incurred losses, but this quarter's loss is the worst in four years. During a conference call, the company stated that it expects to sell between 10-15 million electronic screens this year, which is also lower than last year's figures.

In the previous quarter, PVI spent 500 million New Taiwan dollars on severance packages during the spin-off plan implemented at its South Korean subsidiary, Hydis. Nearly 50% of the employees were laid off, reducing the workforce from 800-900 people to 400.

Meanwhile, through the licensing of Hydis patents, PVI received around 400 million New Taiwan dollars in licensing fees from screen manufacturers such as Sharp and LG. The licensed Hydis patents will be partially used to produce high-resolution screens for Apple and Samsung.

"There will be significant growth momentum in the third quarter, you will feel a strong increase," said PVI's CFO, Chen Yansong, who remains optimistic about the company’s future. He told investors that customers purchasing E Ink screen orders will launch new products in the second and third quarters, improving the company's revenue. Chen Yansong estimates that the revenue at that time will be at least twice that of the previous quarter, as users begin preparing for holiday gifts, boosting the sales of popular e-readers.

The impact of tablet computers is the main reason for PVI's declining revenue. Tablet computers with LCD panels have higher refresh rates, more vibrant colors, and richer content. PVI has been trying to expand into new application areas, such as digital magazines, smartwatches, and luggage tags as electronic screens, but the effects have not been very noticeable. E Ink screens account for 70% of PVI's quarterly income, but by the end of the year, revenue from new application areas is expected to be less than 5% of the company's total income.
