Beijing Outward Bound Training - Progress a Little Every Day

by chandadada on 2012-02-18 20:23:35

Everyone admires successful people. When we reflect on ourselves, why do we fall short of others? In fact, we are all the same; it's just that successful people make progress every day and persist in good habits. No matter when, Beijing Outward Bound training is about achieving small successes every day. If you're still not satisfied with your fate, then try to learn from good experiences daily, turning them into habits that help you improve a little each day. Just like brushing your teeth and washing your face every day, if you can maintain good learning habits consistently, giving yourself small achievements every day, over time, we will become successful individuals. This awareness of making progress every day should never be lost.

In outward bound training courses, coaches often talk about improving mental models. How do we improve? When we understand information that helps us progress, we shouldn't just store it in our brains but immediately put this method of improvement into action and continue doing so. Only then will we find satisfaction in what we do.

There’s a saying: "Today's you is the result of past habits; today's habits will determine tomorrow's fate." Change all the habits that make you unhappy or unsuccessful, and your destiny will change. The larger the scope of your habits, the freer and more energetic your life will be, leading to greater success.

Success isn't always as difficult as we imagine. Developing a good habit every day and sticking to it might make success imminent. It's easy to develop a good habit every day, but the difficulty lies in maintaining it. This requires a combination of belief and perseverance, which is why there are so few successful people.

"A person must have small achievements every day to achieve great accomplishments." The difference between the poor and the rich isn't merely financial. Here's a small story: A rich man gave a poor man a cow, and the poor man began to work with hope. But the cow needed grass, and the man needed food, making life difficult. So, the poor man sold the cow and bought some sheep. He ate one and kept the rest to breed lambs. However, since the lambs were slow to be born, life became tough again. The poor man sold the sheep and bought chickens, hoping to earn money from their eggs. But life didn’t change, and eventually, he slaughtered the chickens, causing his dreams to collapse completely. This is the habit of the poor. On the other hand, according to an investment expert, the secret to the rich man's success is: no matter how hard times get, don't touch your investments or savings. Pressure will drive you to find new ways to earn money and pay off your bills. This is a good habit. Character determines habits, and habits determine success.

Some say that one of God's fairest gifts to humanity is that everyone has only 24 hours in a day. As children, we once recited the phrase, "An inch of time is worth an inch of gold, but an inch of gold cannot buy an inch of time." Although we may not know exactly how long "an inch of time" is, its value compared to gold shows its preciousness. Therefore, utilizing these 24 hours well, managing our time effectively for maximum efficiency, is crucial for both individuals and groups.

The founder of Franklin Time Management Company, Hyrum Smith, proposed ten natural laws in his book "Opening the Door to Success":

1. Mastering life through mastering time.

2. Establish core values as the foundation for self-actualization and personal achievement.

3. Prioritize tasks; when daily life reflects your core values, you'll experience inner peace.

4. Set clear and achievable goals; to reach important goals, you must leave your comfort zone.

5. Plan your daily work; good planning leads to ample time and high efficiency.

6. Review behaviors aligned with beliefs; actions reflect true beliefs.

7. Adjust behaviors to meet requirements; when beliefs align with facts, needs are naturally met.

8. Reopen the window of faith; change wrong thoughts and overcome negative behaviors.

9. Base self-respect on personal values.

10. Achieve self through dedication; the more you give, the more you gain.

Whether someone has clear daily goals and reasonable time management, rather than chaotic living, undoubtedly affects how close they are to success. With the development of the times, especially in modern big cities, life becomes increasingly hurried and tense. Everyone's time is like traffic congestion on highways, filled with things that need to be done and cannot be avoided, leaving little room for what truly matters. "Busy, blind, and bewildered" aptly describes their lives. Only by maintaining good living habits and clear time management concepts can one find a sense of ease amidst the rush.

From books, life, work, or even outward bound training, we can learn wisdom that helps us progress. Seize every opportunity around you to become innovative and pioneering individuals. Everyone understands the principles, but the key is to start now, stick to good habits, learn new knowledge every day, and gain something every day. Make this habit as necessary as eating. Life involves survival and progress, neither of which can be missing. Only by doing so can our lives be free of regret and lack, making them more fulfilling!
