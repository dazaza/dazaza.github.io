Chutian Jinbao (Weibo) [http://weibo.com/chutianjin?zw=news] reported: □Text and photos by our reporter Li Guangzheng, correspondent Liu Ling

At around 12:30 AM on February 23, an astonishing scene occurred at the Huaxiang Police Station in Huarong District, Ezhou City: three blood-covered men were tightly holding onto another man as they stumbled into the station. After learning the situation, the on-duty officers were deeply moved. A veteran officer with over 30 years of experience excitedly handed them towels and emotionally said, "I've never seen policemen as fearless as you."

The three men were Officer Tang Xingbo, Rao Haifeng, and Wen Xing from the Macheng Public Security Bureau's Criminal Investigation Division. When treating their wounds, the doctor discovered that Tang Xingbo's left cheek had been bitten through, and there was a large gash on his forehead; Rao Haifeng's right wrist had been slashed by a dagger; Wen Xing's left finger was fractured... The female nurse treating their wounds was moved to tears, imagining how intense the arrest operation must have been, how brutal the suspect was, and how courageous our police officers were.

A gunshot shocked the small town of Macheng

In Chengma Township, Macheng City, people live peacefully with simple folk customs. However, the gunshots at midnight on December 29 last year broke the decades-long tranquility of this small town. At around 10 PM that evening, a serious case of gun homicide and robbery occurred at the home of a Hu family in Hujiadun Village. More than 20 men armed with several shotguns, machetes, and fish forks stormed into the Hu residence, injuring seven people and stealing more than 70,000 yuan in cash.

The next morning, the Macheng Public Security Bureau received a report from the Wuhan police stating that "several men were being treated in Wuhan for gunshot wounds sustained in Macheng." Subsequently, the Macheng police quickly formed an investigation team. After several days of arduous investigation, they learned that the incident was actually caused by two rival criminal gangs from Xinzhou competing for control of gambling dens, inviting other smaller gangs involved in organized crime to join the fight.

On February 4, the suspect who initiated the incident, surnamed Li, surrendered to the Macheng Public Security Bureau. According to Li's confession, he had once operated a casino with Guo and Lei, but later Lei opened a separate casino in Chengma Township. Li invited Jiang and others to carry out reprisals against the Hu family. The police subsequently confiscated three homemade shotguns used by Jiang and others during the attack in Xinzhou. Jiang, who was still at large, was listed as the top target for capture by the police.

Jiang, 21 years old, is from Huaxiang, Ezhou. His father died early, and his mother remarried shortly after, leaving him to be raised by his grandfather. From a young age, he has had a violent temper and has been handled multiple times by the police. He has criminal records in Ezhou, Wuhan, Xiaogan, and other places. Jiang uses extremely cruel methods in committing crimes and possesses some ability to evade detection and capture. In this case, Jiang personally invited many people to participate, carrying a five-shot shotgun and leading the charge into the scene, directly shooting at people in an attempt to kill.

Following clues leads to the emergence of the "gunman"

To capture Jiang, on February 10, the special task force formed a working group consisting of Chief Rao Haifeng of the Criminal Investigation Brigade's Anti-Black Group, Deputy Chief Wen Xing, and Deputy Chief Tang Xingbo of the Cyber Supervision Brigade. On February 21, the police determined through technical investigative means that Jiang had appeared at someone surnamed Xia's house in Ezhou. The three officers quickly arrived in Ezhou and, with the assistance of local police, located Xia. Xia stated that at 9 AM on February 19, Jiang had driven a green Mitsubishi jeep in Huaxiang, Ezhou, and left the next afternoon.

The officers decided to "follow the vehicle and monitor the person," staking out places where Jiang was most likely to go.

At around 11 PM the next day, the three officers set up surveillance near Jiang's home when they spotted a green Mitsubishi jeep parked in a corner of an alley. "Jiang might be at home!" As the three officers were about to approach the Mitsubishi jeep, Rao Haifeng suddenly had a premonition, "He wouldn't be sleeping in the car, would he?" Rao Haifeng immediately signaled to his teammates with gestures, and the three formed a combat formation, surrounding the Mitsubishi jeep from both sides. Looking up, they saw a person lying on the passenger seat playing with a phone. Using the faint light from the phone, the officers identified the person as Jiang.

A bloody struggle, victory goes to the brave

At the same time, Jiang also noticed the officers outside the car. Bravery wins in close encounters, and the three officers shouted while pulling open the car door, "Don't move! We are the police!" Enraged, Jiang pulled out a dagger and forcefully pushed open the passenger side door. Tang Xingbo and Wen Xing quickly moved to intercept him. Jiang stabbed Wen Xing in the chest, but Wen Xing dodged and, along with Tang Xingbo, tackled Jiang to the ground. But Jiang was physically strong, rolled on the ground, and quickly got up, fleeing blindly toward the rice paddies beside the road. The three officers pursued him closely.

"Absolutely cannot let the suspect escape!" Rao Haifeng and the other three kept their eyes on Jiang's blurry figure, relentlessly chasing him. After nearly a kilometer, the three officers finally cornered Jiang against a fence. Seeing no way out, Jiang brandished the dagger and yelled, "Whoever comes closer, I'll stab them to death!" None of the task force members were afraid. Tang Xingbo rushed forward and hugged Jiang's waist, while Rao Haifeng and Wen Xing moved to grab the dagger.

The almost frenzied Jiang fiercely resisted, stabbing the two officers in front of him. Rao Haifeng and Wen Xing's arms and wrists were successively slashed. The four wrestled and tumbled in the muddy soil. Unable to break free from the officers' control, Jiang viciously bit Tang Xingbo's cheek and grabbed a brick from the fence wall, smashing it onto his head. Blood streamed down Tang Xingbo's face and head, blurring his vision, but he endured the excruciating pain and firmly held onto Jiang until Rao Haifeng and Wen Xing managed to handcuff Jiang and completely subdue this ferocious criminal.
