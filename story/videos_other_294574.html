When will the problem of taking a taxi during rush hours be solved?

by caoying520 on 2009-12-05 22:57:42

For a long time, there has been a phenomenon of difficulty in taking taxis during the peak hours of commuting in the urban area. Many citizens who often need to take taxis at this time due to work, study and other reasons are very headache about this, but they feel helpless. Then, why does such a strange phenomenon form in the taxi market of our city? When will the problem of difficult taxi-taking be alleviated? Let's see the investigation of our reporter. Speaking of the phenomenon of difficulty in taking taxis during the peak hours, many citizens, especially those who often take taxis, have quite a lot of complaints.

During this period, what are the city's taxis busy with, how can they not do business?

It turns out that taxis are all busy changing shifts, but bringing one more passenger won't take too much time, and they can earn more money. What are our taxi drivers thinking?

This way, it is naturally difficult for citizens to take taxis, but the shift change time is also set by people. Why don't taxi companies think about citizens and change this time? Or stagger this shift change time so that not all rush to change shifts at the same time, making our citizens anxious!

This master even pointed out the crux of the problem.

So, how did this precedent come about? According to reports, there are currently 810 taxis in the urban area of our city, and these 810 vehicles can no longer meet the needs of our city's development.

This demand for taxis three or four years ago was definitely not as large as now. During the peak hours of that time, taxi drivers changed shifts, and I guess everyone could understand a bit. But now the place is bigger, the population is more, and people's feelings are naturally different.

The good news is that the municipal government has also noticed this situation! Not long ago, the news that 100 more taxis would be added to the urban area is now widely known. And here we will reveal another good news: these 100 new taxis will hit the road in early January. However, the issue of taxi shift management cannot be ignored. As for the long-standing practice of shift changes during peak hours in the taxi industry, the city's vehicle operation management department, the Municipal Passenger Management Office, will naturally not sit idly by.

From the perspective of our passenger management department, we will make reasonable scheduling and arrangements for the working hours of existing taxi companies. We initially plan to stagger the shift change times of several companies, not concentrating during peak hours. In principle, in the morning around 10 o'clock, and in the afternoon around 5 o'clock, to avoid the shift change time.

Moreover, for this adjustment, the passenger management department will not be lenient in management.

If not (during the specified) shift change time, requests for shift changes without carrying passengers will be considered as refusal to carry and will be punished accordingly.

Tonight's commentary: Breaking industry old rules and customs, making it no longer difficult for citizens to take taxis.
