Polyethylene closed-cell foam board

Reference diagram for polyethylene closed-cell foam board

One. Product features:

- Low density, high recovery rate, with independent bubble structure.

- Low surface water absorption rate, good anti-seepage performance.

- Resistant to acid, alkali, salt, oil and other organic solvent corrosion, excellent aging resistance.

- Does not flow at high temperatures, does not crack at low temperatures.

Two. Application scope of the product:

- Joint plates for concrete road expansion joints;

- Bridge joint waterstop plates;

- Expansion joint plates for embankments, protective dams, energy dissipation steps, slopes, and retaining walls in water conservancy projects;

- Waterstop joint plates at the bottom of hydropower and thermal power projects, water towers;

- Settlement joint fill plates for buildings, frame structures;

- Waterstop fill plates for water plants and sewage treatment plant pools;

- Joint plates for airport runways;

- Joint plates for ports, docks, and concrete structures;

- Water-carrying joint plates for tunnels and water passages;

- Underground channel concrete waterstop joint plates for subways.

Because chemically cross-linked polyethylene foam has unique advantages such as strong restoration ability, no water absorption, impact resistance, climate resistance, chemical resistance, and aging resistance that other materials do not have, the application fields of PE foam are becoming increasingly extensive.

Three. Product specifications:

Original plate specifications: (1600--1950) × (800--950) × 50mm. According to user needs, plates, strips, and sheets of different thicknesses ranging from 5 to 50mm can be processed and produced, with cutting errors within ±0.2 to ±1 range.
