Don't underestimate the ordinary soap and toothpaste. When purchasing a water sprinkler truck, check the pictures of the water sprinkler truck first. These can be used to clean small dirt spots on the car with pretty good results; we're talking about sanitation water sprinkler trucks here. Let's first take a look at using soap to clean leather seats. Leather seats are afraid of being scratched by hard objects and even more so of being corroded by chemical cleaning agents. Using transparent soap with minimal corrosiveness not only cleans well but also leaves the leather soft and shiny after drying. The price of water sprinkler trucks should be considered when buying a van; you can apply an appropriate amount of soap evenly on a wet towel and then gently wipe the seats, followed by wiping twice with a cleaned wet towel.

Next, let's talk about using toothpaste to remove scratches. Over the years, I've been using the method of polishing with toothpaste to remove minor scratches, and it works quite well. The method is as follows: First, clean the scratch area with clean water, then take a clean cloth or towel, apply a small amount of toothpaste, and repeatedly and gently rub the scratch until it disappears or becomes less visible, then wipe dry with a wet wipe.

Finally, let's discuss using menthol oil to remove sticky labels. Apply some menthol oil (a bit concentrated) on the back of the label, wait for it to soak through for a while, then scrub vigorously with a dry cloth, and the label will come off without leaving any traces.

Although these methods may seem a bit old-fashioned, they are still very effective. Most importantly, they are low-cost and convenient. Is driving better or repairing cars better? Answer: Honestly, driving is better, provided it's for large trucks or above.
