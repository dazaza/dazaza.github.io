What to eat during menstruation

by dd24561514j on 2012-02-27 17:39:07

What should you eat during your period? Although menstruation is a normal physiological phenomenon for adult women, it is also quite important to know what vegetables are good to eat during this time. For example, you should not eat blood-nourishing foods like donkey-hide gelatin or red dates intentionally, nor should you indulge in sweets. Additionally, avoid eating raw and cold foods, as well as spicy and stimulating foods. Women should drink more brown sugar water, which can warm the uterus and help expel menstrual blood.

What should you eat during your period? First, let's look at food categories, which mainly fall into three types:

1. Raw and cold foods: such as pears, water chestnuts, stone flowers, bananas, stone ears, and earth ears.

2. Spicy foods: such as cinnamon, Sichuan pepper, cloves, and black pepper.

3. Foods that affect sexual function: such as water caltrops, mustard greens, rabbit meat, Chinese water chestnuts, winter melons, fiddlehead ferns, black fungus, and hemp seeds.

Dietary rules during menstruation: As the menstrual cycle approaches, women may experience mild to severe "premenstrual syndrome," such as restlessness, emotional instability, conflict with others, breast or abdominal swelling, weight gain, general bloating, changes in appetite, mouth ulcers, acne breakouts, headaches, etc. These symptoms usually appear 10 to 14 days before the start of menstruation and end within 24 hours after the onset of menstruation. The severity and occurrence time of the symptoms vary from person to person. In addition, women may experience abnormal conditions during their periods, such as lower abdominal distension, back pain, irregular flow, or unpredictable cycles.

A few dietary precautions during the period:

1) Avoid indulging in sweets like beverages, cakes, brown sugar, and candies. This prevents blood sugar fluctuations and reduces various menstrual discomforts.

2) Eat more high-fiber foods such as vegetables, fruits, whole grains, whole wheat bread, brown rice, and oats. Consuming enough high-fiber foods can promote the excretion of estrogen, increase magnesium levels in the blood, regulate menstruation, and calm the nerves.

3) Eat some foods rich in vitamin B group between meals, such as walnuts, cashews, and dried beans.

4) Consume enough protein. Eat more high-protein foods like meat, eggs, tofu, and soybeans to replenish nutrients and minerals lost during menstruation.

5) Eat on a regular schedule. This can prevent blood sugar spikes and drops, reduce rapid heartbeat, and alleviate dizziness, fatigue, and emotional instability.

6) Avoid caffeinated beverages such as coffee and tea, as these drinks can increase anxiety and restlessness. Instead, try barley tea or mint tea.

7) Avoid eating extremely hot or cold foods with large temperature variations.

8) Women who experience heavy bleeding should consume more high-fiber foods such as spinach, red dates, red vegetables (with red broth), and raisins to help replenish blood.

9) Women approaching menopause should consume more calcium-rich foods like milk and dried small fish. It is essential to follow the above precautions to ensure a pain-free menstrual period.
