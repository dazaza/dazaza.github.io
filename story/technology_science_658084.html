How much do you know about Interactive Voice Response?

by jojozou on 2012-02-01 16:09:38

Interactive Voice Response (IVR) is a software application that can accept voice telephone inputs or keypad selections and interact with users in the form of appropriate responses (such as voice, fax, callback, email, or other media formats). In a complete call center solution, the proper positioning, allocation, and coordination of functions and resources by the IVR system are very important for leveraging the strengths of each component to fully achieve the powerful functions of the entire call center. IVR is an important part of the call center, and its main function is to provide users with interactive voice services, transferring calls to live agents when necessary. This automatic telephone information service system uses fixed voice menus and real-time data from databases to respond to incoming callers with voice answers and fax replies. It can complete signaling connections with the switchboard, handle user key presses, and provide corresponding voice services, obtaining pre-recorded digits or synthesized voice information from the system. Interactive Voice Response is an important component of the CTI (Computer Telephony Integration) system, providing interactive voice navigation and information inquiry functions. It has been widely applied in telephone stock trading systems, telephone banking systems, audio information services, recharge card systems, call centers, unified messaging platforms, and other systems. By using Interactive Voice Response, users can enjoy 7x24-hour information services anytime and anywhere. Adding an IVR system to a call center allows most calls to be handled automatically, reducing the burden on customer service representatives so they can handle calls that require human intervention. Due to the real-time and convenience provided by the IVR system, it has gained widespread recognition from many users, and currently, many companies are dedicated to using this advantage of IVR to attract users. The IVR development environment is the key to the IVR self-service system. If your IVR application development is both simple and easy to use, you will quickly reach profitability and ultimately achieve greater profits. A good IVR self-service solution should have an intuitive graphical IVR business process editing interface, allowing customers to complete IVR application development simply by clicking. This makes it easy to develop and modify application processes to meet your ever-changing IVR service needs, reduce costs, and increase profits. The Interactive Voice Response system provides automatic voice services and is the main equipment for enterprises to provide self-service to customers. The system uses user-oriented voice catalogs, completing corresponding information inquiries and command executions based on customer selections (through the telephone keypad or voice), which can be described as controlling computers through telephone key presses. By connecting to a database at the backend, the IVR system can provide customers with dynamic real-time information. If used in a call center, the IVR system can automate most calls, saving approximately 60% of costs while also reducing the burden on customer service representatives, allowing them to focus on more important customer service tasks. With continuous breakthroughs in voice recognition technology, today's IVR systems can also be integrated with voice recognition, enabling computer systems to be operated through direct language input. This undoubtedly expands the scope of applications for IVR, such as dynamic voice queries, voice navigation, and automatic business processing. In summary, the IVR system is no longer limited to voice processing but can also provide fax sending and receiving services to customers.
