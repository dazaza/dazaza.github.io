Current Status and Development of Broadband IP Satellite Communication Technology

by weixing123 on 2009-09-10 18:21:36

The satellite IP network structure is the evolution and application of terrestrial broadband IP technology in the field of communication. It is an attempt to adapt to satellite packet services and reduce system complexity, aiming to provide users with satisfactory high-volume packet data services at a low cost without the intervention of ATM. The adoption of this non-ATM IP over Satellite solution has the following benefits compared to the IP over ATM solution:

• Lower overhead. Bell Labs' simulations show that if the IP over ATM over SONET structure is used, there will be approximately 22%-29% overhead, with about 4% of that being SONET overhead. Therefore, we consider removing the ATM layer to achieve IP over SONET or IP over SO-CDMA/TDMA.

• Easy to implement gigabit packet networks. Currently, experimental trunk rates for multimedia satellites using ATM technology have reached 622 Mbps. However, when moving to gigabit speeds, the issue of large ATM overhead constrains further increases in line speed.

• Reduces system complexity. With the support of technologies such as RS block coding, interleaving, and FEC, satellite links can reach quasi-fiber quality. Thus, it is unnecessary to use ATM's complex QoS assurance mechanism to resist wireless channel errors. In Japan, KDD explicitly proposed in its "KDD Terabit (Terabit) Highway Plan (KTH21)" that high-speed networks can be achieved without ATM switching and SDH terminal equipment.

Specific research directions include:

(1) Providing LAN-based IP interconnection

By adopting the developing IPv6 and RSVP, LAN-based IP can support real-time services with varying QoS levels by reserving resources in each transit router along the path of the service flow. Because IPv6 can provide two-way interactive services such as video conferencing and web browsing, which have transmission requirements different from those of services requiring only "best-effort" transmission (such as ftp), the data link layer protocol of the wireless interface must include a functional block that can convert QoS requirements into appropriate satellite MAC PDUs. That is, the dynamic capacity allocation (DCA) on the star must be coordinated with the QoS of the IP layer and control the end-to-end QoS of the wireless LAN.

(2) MAC layer protocols suitable for satellite IP services

MAC layer protocols ensure that communication nodes located in different places have the ability to control packet switching between them and effectively manage network bandwidth to make full use of resources. In recent years, a series of MAC layer protocols have emerged to meet the requirements of various application environments. However, these MAC protocols often satisfy some application requirements while failing to meet others. When we only consider the MAC layer protocol for satellite communications, first, a considerable number of MAC protocols are not considered due to the limitation of satellite delay, such as LAN and WAN MAC layer protocols. Second, some MAC layer protocols already used in terrestrial networks need to be re-evaluated in satellite networks due to the different transmission delays between satellite and terrestrial links. Third, changes in satellite physical devices are limited, so a simple and feasible control mechanism is needed. Fourth, the MAC protocol of the satellite network should have fault tolerance and survivability, allowing normal operation when the network topology changes or the system is reconstructed. Fifth, it helps to reduce power consumption and buffer capacity and increase channel capacity. The design goal of the MAC layer protocol for broadband satellite IP networks is to achieve high channel traffic, low transmission delay, channel stability, protocol implementability, channel reconstructability, and low complexity of control algorithms. Currently, MAC layer research targeting multimedia satellite systems mainly involves performance comparisons of overlay models between the MAC layer and wmATM (wireless mobile ATM). Hassan Penyravi divided existing MAC layer protocols into five categories and compared their support for multimedia under the application background of NASA Mars regional networks. Janez Bosic studied the scheme of using priority-based MAC layer protocols in TDMA satellite networks to ensure ATM transmission.

These studies indicate that currently no MAC layer protocol has been optimized specifically for satellite multimedia services. Therefore, it is necessary to propose a MAC layer protocol suitable for large-capacity IP packet services in satellites. We drew inspiration from BELL Lab's Simple Link Protocol (SDL) for IP over WDM and made appropriate modifications for use in the satellite environment. In 1997, ITU-R proposed the satellite SDH protocol stack for backbone networks. Here, SDH refers to satellite SDH, which differs slightly from terrestrial SDH.

It is necessary to study the advantages and disadvantages of various MAC protocols, especially their support for multimedia services. Important parameters affecting the MAC layer include transmission delay, bandwidth, packet size, and message frame length distribution. During analysis and simulation, specific service models need to be incorporated into the MAC layer protocol to study its performance.

(3) Research on the structure of onboard IP switches

Third-layer switching is essential for IP networks to adapt to the rapid growth of optical transmission capabilities and bandwidth, and is also the result of the development of technologies such as ultra-large-scale integrated circuits and distributed processing. The adoption of third-layer switching technology for onboard switching makes it possible to construct broadband IP networks under new transmission regimes.

To improve the throughput of IP switches and reduce the queuing time of packets within the switch, it is necessary to study the switching architecture (Switching Fabrics) of the switch. This problem is similar to previous ATM switch problems. In actual operation, it is possible for cells from several input/output ports to arrive at the same output port simultaneously, a situation known as port contention. Sometimes this competition occurs within the switching structure, referred to as internal blocking (Internal Blocking). For each port of the switch, there may be three data queues: input queue, output queue, and broadcast queue, managed by the port controller. Each port has a certain amount of buffer memory to cache the above three data queues. It is necessary to study internally non-blocking switching architectures. Additionally, other functions of the switch need to be researched: priority setting, broadcasting, and multicast functions.

(4) Mobile multicast management

In terms of mobile management using the mobile IP protocol, mobile IP multicast is a new research direction. IP multicast services already account for 20% of commercial backbone network services and are becoming increasingly important with the development of web-caching and "push" services. When an IP site sends the same data to multiple IP sites on the Internet, IP multicast can reduce unnecessary overlapping transmissions, effectively utilizing network bandwidth. Although it increases the processing of IP multicast-related protocols, it still reduces the processing time of a large number of multicast routers and hosts for IP packets relative to multiple identical point-to-point transmissions. The unique topological simplicity and suitability for broadcasting in satellites determine the application prospects of mobile IP multicast technology in broadband IP satellite systems. The current technical issue is how to integrate asymmetric broadcast-type satellite networks with the symmetric and point-to-point cellular interconnection structures of the Internet.

3.3 Free-space optical code division multiple access technology (SO-CDMA)

Space optical code division multiple access technology is a key technology in wireless transmission technology (RTT). The multi-access method of interstellar links is very important and directly affects the performance of the system.

Currently, in interstellar free-space optical communication, the TDMA method is generally used. However, due to the long transmission distance, the delay is relatively large, making it difficult to achieve the strict clock synchronization required by TDMA systems. If the WDM method recently proposed internationally is adopted, it is difficult to meet the requirement of continuous frequency adjustment of the light source due to the difficulty in producing continuously adjustable light sources. We suggest using space optical code division multiple access technology in satellite IP networks. Space optical code division multiple access technology uses mutually independent optical pulse sequences as address codes for each light source. These address codes are orthogonal to each other, and each signal source is modulated with its own address code, and demodulated with the corresponding address code at the receiver. Space optical code division multiple access technology has the following advantages:

(1) The inherent resistance to background noise of code division multiple access is particularly useful for interstellar communication. In free-space communication systems, the receiver not only receives the signal light but also light emitted by other users and background light. The background light is much stronger than on the ground, thus having a significant impact on system performance. Theoretically, optical filters can be used to filter out background light noise, but obtaining extremely narrow-bandwidth and highly stable filters (e.g., atomic filters) is very difficult. Since correlation reception is performed using the correlation characteristics of the address code, the system has strong noise resistance.

(2) Code division multiple access does not require full-network synchronization and can well support asynchronous communication and bursty services. Also, since only one wavelength light source is used, there is no need for a tunable frequency light source.

Its only disadvantage is the wide occupied bandwidth, but this can be ignored for optical communication with abundant wideband resources.

Currently, research on space optical CDMA technology mainly focuses on the following aspects: selection of spread spectrum sequences, modulation methods, signal detection methods (including multipath reception and multi-user reception), power control, and synchronization technology. Space optical CDMA is one of the most important ways for future interstellar links.

Four, Conceptualization for constructing China's communication satellite backbone network

When constructing China's communication satellite backbone network, it is necessary to consider current business development trends and the needs of small and medium-sized commercial enterprises. The characteristics of this communication satellite network are:

(1) Two-layer satellite network structure. The satellite IP network consists of a two-layer satellite network, with the lower layer being various communication LEO satellites and the upper layer being GEO satellites used for data relay and control, employing onboard processing. LEO satellites send data to GEO satellites, which then exchange and route data from various satellites before sending it to several ground control stations. Ground control centers send control information to GEO satellites, which then send the necessary information to various LEO satellites.

(2) The business traffic between LEO satellites and GEO satellites is asymmetrical. This asymmetrical burst data method closely resembles multimedia services, with static images, dynamic videos, burst data, and voice on the forward link, and mostly control information on the return link. Therefore, the results of international research on multimedia satellite systems can be fully utilized.

(3) Given the requirements for small investment and quick returns in China's satellite systems, we recommend that the satellite system adopt IP technology.

(4) Establish interstellar links based on space optical CDMA between LEO and geostationary orbit satellites. Space optical CDMA has the characteristics of anti-interference and good confidentiality.

(5) Interconnection with terrestrial communication systems. It can form an integrated system with terrestrial communication networks, facilitating the realization of an integrated sky-ground system. Therefore, we can view the conceptualized communication satellite network as the core network of a broadband multimedia satellite network.

With the rapid growth in demand for multimedia services and Internet services, people have begun to pay more attention to satellite broadband communication systems. 3GPP Release99 indicates that the third-generation mobile communication system will be unified under an IP core network structure. This also illustrates the development trend of future communication networks. The new market opened up by broadband IP satellite systems may bring about another major development in satellite communications.
