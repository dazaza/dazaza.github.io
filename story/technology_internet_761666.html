Exclusive interview with Jack Ma: Investing in Weibo is an important step for Alibaba, and he will not return to the spotlight again.

by geekzhang on 2013-05-06 11:08:25

Ma Yun Participated in the Dialogue with Silicon Valley Elites at Stanford University, California, USAMa Yun, Chairman of the Board and CEO of Alibaba, participated in a dialogue event with Silicon Valley elites at Stanford University, California, USA last Saturday. After the event, he accepted an exclusive interview with Sina Tech, sharing his views on the strategic cooperation between Alibaba and Sina Weibo, rumors about Alibaba's IPO, and the selection of the next CEO.Ma pointed out that investing in Weibo aims to make it healthier and more positive, which is a crucial step for Alibaba's future development. Alibaba will definitely go public, but the exact timing and location are still uncertain. He fully trusts the next CEO, Jonathan Lu, and stated that he will not reassume the position of CEO.Ma's main purpose for visiting the US was to attend The Nature Conservancy (TNC) Global Meeting in Los Angeles, where he serves as a director on the global board. On May 10th, Ma will officially step down as CEO of Alibaba but continue as Chairman of the Board. On May 11th, he will take over as Chairman of the China Board of TNC.An Important Step for Alibaba's Future DevelopmentOn April 29th, Sina Weibo, a subsidiary of Sina Corporation, announced a strategic cooperation agreement with a subsidiary of Alibaba Group. Leveraging their respective advantages in social media and e-commerce, they aim to create a more active Weibo platform and explore a more imaginative Weibo open ecosystem and business model. Alibaba acquired approximately 18% of Sina Weibo's shares through its wholly-owned subsidiary for $586 million.Regarding this strategic cooperation, Ma told Sina Tech that Weibo is an excellent product and a remarkable company that has contributed significantly to the positive progress of Chinese society, especially in terms of communication between people and community building, enhancing the positivity and transparency of Chinese society. Alibaba's collaboration with Weibo aims to support Weibo's success, making it healthier and more positive.He emphasized that Alibaba does not intend to turn Weibo into an e-commerce platform but wants to explore how e-commerce can assist Weibo, working together with Sina to build a new type of Weibo platform for the future. If Weibo were used for e-commerce, I would be cursed by netizens and criticized by the times.Ma noted that Alibaba currently holds 18% of Weibo's shares; if Weibo succeeds, so will we. First, we should focus on making Weibo better before discussing what it can bring to Alibaba. It’s Sina's responsibility to do well with Weibo, but if there’s anything we can help with, we will support it. Only with this mindset can the cooperation continue. Investing in Weibo is a very important step for Alibaba's future development, and I am proud to participate in Weibo's construction.Ma's personal Weibo account has not been updated for one and a half years. He explained that he has actually been paying attention to and using Weibo all along, and if he didn't like Weibo, he would never invest in it. He revealed that his personal Weibo account will soon resume updates, and he has been pondering what content to post these days.Unclear About IPO TimingThe upcoming IPO of Alibaba Group has always been a hot topic of external interest. As of September last year, Alibaba generated revenues of $4.08 billion over a 12-month period, surpassing Baidu. According to The New York Times' estimation, the overall valuation of the soon-to-be-listed Alibaba Group will approach $100 billion, potentially becoming the largest market capitalization company in China's internet industry.Ma did not avoid the topic of going public. He joked that everyone knows Alibaba will go public, but when exactly is unclear to him. He likened going public to a wedding and delisting to a divorce, noting that Alibaba is someone who has been married and divorced before. Alibaba's B2B subsidiary listed in Hong Kong in 2007 but was privatized and delisted in 2012.Ma further elaborated that going public is certainly important for Alibaba, but since we have been married and divorced, we understand that marriage is more important than getting married. We know what a wedding is and what a marriage is. Marriage requires time; we don't care when the wedding will be held, but we care about how beautiful and lasting the marriage can be, and how much happiness it can bring to others. Where and when the wedding ceremony takes place is not too important to us; we are more concerned that after the wedding, the marriage might become the grave of love.Message to Successor: Be HimselfOn January 15th, Ma announced in an internal email that he would resign as CEO of the company on May 10th and continue as Chairman of the Board. On March 11th, Ma again announced via internal email that Jonathan Lu, the group's Chief Data Officer, would become the next CEO.Talking about stepping down as CEO, Ma said that choosing to step down this year during Taobao's tenth anniversary is because he wants to retire before the age of 50. He mentioned that he has been looking forward to this day for many years, a decision he has thought about for a long time, and he hasn't spent as much time on the CEO role as before. However, Ma emphasized that he is still the Chairman of the Board of Alibaba and bears significant responsibility for Alibaba, and he deeply loves this company.He frankly admitted that he has full trust in the new CEO, Jonathan Lu, and will not interfere with the CEO's work or come out of retirement. Ma explained that he sincerely feels that he is somewhat old for the internet industry. You love your child, you need to let them stand on their own feet. Loving your company means letting someone who understands the company better take control. Today, I still bring positive energy to the company, but I am getting older, and I don't want to become a negative force tomorrow.Ma indicated that he has no expectations for his successor, Jonathan Lu, telling him to just be himself and do what he believes is right. Retired personnel should not meddle in the affairs of the next generation, believe in them, and let them act freely. I didn't choose just one person, but a group of people to take over. I believe that these people are far smarter and more capable than I was ten years ago, and they love this company as much as I do. Even if Alibaba faces challenges, could I really save it by returning? I think I lack the ability of Liu Chuanzhi (who returned to Lenovo), as the internet industry changes too quickly; even being away for six months makes a difference.This founder of Alibaba firmly said: Even if there is a pit ahead for Alibaba, I will let him jump. This is trust. Young people are better than us; at my age, I may not be able to overcome it, but perhaps he can.
