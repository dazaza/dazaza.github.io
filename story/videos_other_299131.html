The car owner won the lawsuit after 15 years and was awarded 210,000 in compensation.

by j0371zxy on 2009-12-08 16:03:24

The hub's sudden rupture caused a terrible car accident, resulting in 2 deaths, 4 minor injuries, and 11 serious injuries. The vehicle owner compensated the families of the deceased and the injured for an economic loss of 179,000 yuan. Since then, the vehicle owner embarked on a long road to defend his rights. This lawsuit lasted for 15 years. Last night, after receiving the judgment document from the Jintang Court, Huang Zuhua exhaled a deep breath - the 15-year-long hub case was finally settled. The hub seller was ordered to compensate 215,532.34 yuan.

In May 1994, Wang Daohao and Huang Zuhua bought a hub at Jiang Yuzhi's "Wanliu Auto Parts Store" in Huixin Town, Jintang, and installed it on their main transport bus. On the afternoon of July 17th of the same year, due to the right front hub suddenly rupturing during travel, the bus flipped into a 13-meter-deep river ditch. Among the 17 passengers, 2 died on the scene, 4 had minor injuries, and 11 sustained serious injuries. After the incident, the Jintang County Traffic Police arrived at the scene to investigate and handle the situation, storing the broken hub in the garage. Subsequently, the traffic police determined that Huang Zuhua and Wang Daohao were fully responsible. For this reason, the couple compensated the families of the deceased and the injured with an economic loss of 179,000 yuan.

"Although this long legal battle lasted 15 years, my rights were ultimately protected," said Huang Zuhua. Through this lawsuit, he learned a lot: consumers must preserve evidence and actively request receipts. Because if he had the receipt as evidence, this lawsuit would not have taken so much effort.

The court eventually ruled that the defendant should compensate the plaintiff 215,532.34 yuan, deducting the 79,159.46 yuan paid by the insurance company, leaving an actual compensation of 136,372.88 yuan.

Is the hub submitted for inspection the one involved in the accident? The court believed that there is physical evidence proving consistency. The preserved fragments and screw cap photos were identified as being purchased from the plaintiff's store through metallographic testing.

Subsequently, Huang Zuhua, through the Jintang Technical Supervision Bureau, sent the hub involved in the accident to an authoritative department for testing. The conclusion was that the hub had severe quality issues. Huang Zuhua and his wife then sued Jiang Yuzhi in court, demanding compensation for losses.

Court Focus:

Consumers should actively ask for receipts

Was the hub involved in the accident sold by Jiang Yuzhi? The Jintang Court believed that although there was no purchase invoice for the hub transaction, there is evidence proving that Huang Zuhua once bought four rear hubs from the defendant. The defendant admitted that the plaintiff bought and installed the hub from them. The court investigation confirmed that the hub involved in the accident was indeed purchased from the plaintiff's store.

"The first person to arrive at the scene after the accident was someone who had been a traffic police officer for 40 years, and they had never seen such an event," said He Jianzhi, a former educator at the Jintang Traffic Police Team. The scene showed that the right rear wheel hub of the bus broke while making a left turn. Such a hub is unique, and even if money was spent, it would be impossible to find another identical hub, thus proving that the hub submitted for inspection was indeed the one involved in the accident. Traffic police officer Jiang Yan said that it was he who personally handed the hub over to the technical supervision personnel, but since it was just an auto part, no formal handover procedures were done at the time.

The representative of Jiang Yuzhi argued that without written handover procedures, it cannot be proven that the Technical Supervision Bureau retrieved the hub from the traffic police. At the same time, the written statement from the Technical Supervision Bureau insisted that they did not retrieve the hub from the traffic police or eat at any restaurant, but instead picked up the hub from a pharmacy, thereby failing to prove that the hub involved in the accident was purchased from Jiang Yuzhi's store.
