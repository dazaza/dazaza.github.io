Grief in Brightness

I am not an outgoing person, I can even say that I am a little cold! However, recently, a friend said: "I finally realized that you are just an ordinary person with emotions too, it's just that your overly resolute exterior hides the grief in your brightness!"

Is this true? In this world, is there really a me who exists with a touch of sadness amidst brightness? I don't know!

I long to live forever in my self-imposed confined space until death.

I long to be carefree under the blue sky and white clouds until I rest peacefully.

I long to be like a blank sheet of paper, without a single impurity in life.

The reason for such longing is that I understand these are merely longings, one could even say unreachable dreams. And I have to continue living according to the current trajectory, unable to deviate!

In the past, my life was nothing but ordinary, and now, my life remains common. However, those irretrievable pasts, whether beautiful, joyful, or sorrowful, make me so nostalgic. I do not wish to grow up, at least not want to live aimlessly like now, it seems as if living is all there is to do.

Perhaps, I am truly a melancholy person, hiding the real me beneath my cheerful exterior, a me that I refuse to acknowledge. Am I happy? I keep asking myself, and I do not know.

My life is very monotonous, monotonous to the point of being only white. I live this monotonous life, without brilliance or complexity. I once yearned for Tao Yuanming's pastoral life, working from sunrise to sunset. But I do not have my own piece of pure land, nor do I have a peaceful abode. I also yearned for the shepherd's life, under the blue sky, on the green grass, herding sheep, leaving long footprints on the earth; I even yearned to be a homeless wanderer, drifting from one place to another, never staying, just being an ordinary passerby, simply passing by, then continuing to drift.

All my past yearnings, those unattainable dreams, remain in my heart. My grief in brightness, my past.

Tides rise and fall, clouds roll and stretch, I still walk hand in hand with my white until old age. It has always been with me, through countless years, telling me about the colorful beauty, telling me about the lonely pain, telling me about the grief in brightness. We live together, living a simple life.

I still long to live forever in my self-imposed confined space until death, only my confined space is getting bigger day by day.

I still long to be carefree under the blue sky and white clouds until I rest peacefully, only my dreams carry a faint unease.

I still long to be like a blank sheet of paper, without a single impurity in life, only I forgot that the traces left by the brush on the white paper cannot be wiped away!

It can be seen that I still long, only I have gotten used to the grief in brightness!
