A design style that primarily uses wood as its main vocabulary doesn't have to be limited to a light and simple natural style.

Secret Technique 1/ Reduce Oppression with Transparent Glass

The most taboo aspect of designing a raised mezzanine is the feeling of crowding and narrowness. The designer paid special attention to the planning of the stairs, using transparent glass in the handrail area to maintain visual openness, making the staircase not feel narrow; combined with the design of bookshelves and the color contrast of black and white, it brings out a perfect sense of layering.

Secret Technique 2/ Transform Staircase into an Atmosphere Bar Counter [Staircase Renovation Effect Picture](http://www.china-kewen.com)

To make full use of irregular spaces, the designer cleverly designed the space next to the mezzanine staircase as a dining bar counter, and the staircase itself became the most beautiful decoration. Placing twinkling candlelight creates an immediate atmosphere, making it not only aesthetically pleasing but also functional.

Secret Technique 3/ Combine Staircase Design with Storage Space

This high-ceiling mezzanine space has only 8 pings available for recovery of tobacco and alcohol [http://www.163huishou.com]. Therefore, all the space must be carefully planned and utilized. To create more adequate storage space, the designer combined the staircase with storage space, using the shape of the steps to create storage cabinets and display cabinets of different sizes, making the use of space more versatile.

Secret Technique 4/ Carefully Designed Every Step of the Staircase

Since the staircase space itself is relatively narrow [Staircase Renovation Effect Picture](http://www.china-kewen.com) and the number of steps is less than that of a regular staircase, the designer meticulously calculated and used triangular shaped steps, ensuring that each step is steady and safe while walking, reducing the risk of falling. Combined with the clean texture of wood, it provides the most comfortable and thoughtful experience.

Secret Technique 1/ Create a "Breathable" Home with Solid Wood

Not only pursuing natural interest visually, the designer also used green design techniques to take care of the space's lighting, ventilation, and airflow. By using unpainted solid wood materials wrapped around columns to regulate indoor temperature and humidity, and by setting window openings according to air guidance principles, the space can breathe easily surrounded by natural materials.

Secret Technique 2/ Use High Ceilings to Create Layered Changes

Using high ceilings, even a twenty ping personal home can cover all aspects! In this case, a steel structure was used to create a wooden staircase that appears to float in the air, paired with a high ceiling full-wall bookshelf, fully utilizing the irregular space. Through the wooden staircase, the overall space is organically connected into a clear path, maximizing the efficiency of the space.

Secret Technique 3/ Borrow Green Beauty from Nature

To ensure the bedroom space retains pure sleep quality, the designer deliberately omitted excessive decoration, laying the ground with dark wood material, naturally forming a spacious and comfortable bed. Since lush greenery lies just outside the window, large windows were opened in the space to introduce natural scenery, becoming the most beautiful artwork in the room.

Secret Technique 4/ Highlight Style Vocabulary with Main Wall Design

Creating a home space with wood can bring a fresh and natural feeling, but if the entire space uses wood, it may appear monotonous and have higher material costs. Therefore, the main wall can be used to highlight the spatial style, such as the TV wall in this case which is made of engineered wood, instantly presenting a natural style.

Secret Technique 5/ Mix Lighting and Plants for a Wooden House Texture

A design style mainly expressed through wood does not have to be merely light and natural, it can also be fashionable and mixed! For example, in this case, the bathroom space combines indirect lighting, plants, and black mirrors on wooden furniture, paired with stone flooring, allowing the space to exhibit a simple yet fashionable and naturally comfortable feeling.

Secret Technique 5/ Penetrating Design Creates Grand Quality

The staircase connecting the upper and lower spaces of the high-ceiling mezzanine was intentionally left open on the side walls by the designer, maintaining an open and penetrating view, making the overall space feel more complete and consistent. Adding windows at the top and bottom of the staircase introduces natural light, bringing warmth and pleasantness with sunlight.
