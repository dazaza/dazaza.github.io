A fire broke out at a sofa factory in Xiaoli Village, Chengxi, Xi'an city yesterday, trapping a boy inside the factory - factory, fire smoke exhaust.

by jianssanl on 2011-03-21 15:56:18

--->

At around noon yesterday, after the workers of Shanghai Chunmu Industrial Co., Ltd. Xichun branch finished their work, they all went to have lunch. "Around 2 PM, we returned to the factory and found smoke coming out from the southern part of the factory." At that time, there were only a few workers inside, who immediately evacuated and helped rescue the unburned wood and fabrics.

At 2:03 PM, after receiving the alarm, the fire trucks rushed to the scene. "Initially, three fire trucks arrived, but due to the large fire, they requested support from the command center," said the on-site rescue personnel.

Looking south from the rooftop of the third floor on the north side of the burning factory, the entire factory was engulfed in thick smoke. The color steel roof had collapsed due to the fire, with flames continuously leaping out beneath the steel. The thick smoke made it hard for people to breathe. "My child is inside the factory, he's only seven years old, at the corner of the second floor," said an anxious female worker.

Due to the color steel blocking the way, the water couldn't flow down, and the firefighters couldn't enter the factory. At 3:50 PM, the rescuers brought in an excavator to push down the brick wall on the north side of the factory and used a fire smoke exhaust vehicle to remove the thick smoke, ensuring that the firefighters could approach the fire scene. By 4 PM, the fire was basically under control.

Deputy Director Jing Yuan of the Fire Prevention Department of the Xi'an Public Security Bureau said that the excessive burning area reached 2000 square meters. Because the stored materials such as sponge, fabric, and wood are flammable, the fire was very intense. The fire department mobilized more than 30 fire trucks and over 190 firefighters to participate in extinguishing the fire.

By 6 PM when the reporter left, the fire had been controlled, but the trapped boy was not found. The boy’s mother looked at the factory and burst into tears. As of 8 PM when this article was published, the work of extinguishing the remaining fire and search and rescue was still ongoing.
