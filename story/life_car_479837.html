Several Methods of Car Washing in Car Beauty

If you want to give your car a thorough cleaning, it's best to start from the inside of the car because there is more dust and dirt inside, and it takes more time. If you first wash the exterior and wax it, it will be easy for the dust created during the interior cleaning to stick to the wax that has not dried yet. Besides, when cleaning the interior, you should start from the top down to the bottom because some dust will fall down while cleaning the top, so it's better to clean from top to bottom. Well, let's start washing the car happily now.

Method One: Scrape Glass Stains with a Knife

Many people like to stick various types of stickers on the car windows, or stick driving licenses, road fees, etc. No matter what kind of adhesive material, after being exposed to sunlight and wind, these stickers will turn yellow or get dirty in the gaps over time. The longer they are not cleaned, the harder they are to clean. In this case, we need to use a small knife blade to help. When using a blade to clean stubborn stickers, it's best to use a single-edged type for safety. When scraping, the flat edge must be tightly attached to the glass, and you should gently scrape slowly. Too much force and too fast speed not only cannot completely remove the stains, but also easily break the blade, causing danger.

Method Two: Turn Air Conditioner Odor into Fragrance

Whether you smoke in the car or not, as long as you frequently use the air conditioning system, after long-term operation, the car's air conditioning system will inevitably have an unpleasant smell. Even if you place car fragrances, perfumes, or scented ointments in the car, since they are chemicals, they will eventually reach their expiration date or mix with body odor, sweat, or gasoline smell, turning from fragrance to stench. After the air conditioner sucks in and expels the air, it naturally accumulates many different odors. To thoroughly clean it is a big project, but there is still a simple method, which is to use a machine that sprays steam. By spraying steam into the air conditioning ducts, the heat emitted by the water vapor decomposes the substances causing the odor as much as possible, minimizing the bad smell.

Method Three: Sweep Cup Holders and Trash Bins

For car owners, cleaning the soda racks, ornament boxes, handrest boxes, or door-side storage boxes inside the car is the most headache-inducing problem. To clean these hidden places, you need to use a paintbrush to sweep, combined with a car-specific vacuum cleaner, which is the simplest and fastest method. If these places are stained with soda or oil, you inevitably need to use cleaning agents or water to rinse them. After rinsing, you can use a dry cloth to absorb the moisture, then use a car vacuum cleaner to suck away the stains. The vacuum cleaner produces suction, utilizing the principle of air flow to evaporate the moisture that cannot be wiped dry. Of course, using a hairdryer is also possible.

Method Four: Use a Hairdryer to Soften Stickers

It was mentioned earlier to use a blade to clean stickers on the glass, but for body stickers, this method absolutely cannot be used because it will scrape off the car's paint, with very serious consequences. Some people have tried using turpentine, alcohol, gasoline, thinner, etc., to clean them, but the results are the same as using a blade to scrape stickers, damaging the car's paint because all of these are chemical solvents. If you want to remove stickers without damaging the paint, you can use the heat from a hairdryer to soften the adhesive of the sticker, then slowly peel it off by hand.

Method Five: Clean Carpet and Velvet

In addition to the leather cleaning method mentioned earlier, there is also a velvet cleaning method for cars with mainly velvet interior decoration. It's best to use the previously mentioned steam spray cleaner to brush, but there are also some specialized cleaning agents on the market for cleaning velvet. Using a velvet cleaning agent works best when combined with a brush of moderate hardness, which will yield better results. As for the carpet, how should it be cleaned? First, you need to take the carpet out of the car for treatment, the further away from the car the better, to avoid dust blowing back into the car. The carpet can be washed with water mixed with cleaning agents and brushed with a hard-bristle brush. After cleaning, the carpet must be completely dry before it can be put back into the car, because moisture in the carpet can easily breed mold.

Method Six: Apply Wax to Leather Seats to Prevent Cracking

For friends who don't have time to take care of their cars regularly, if it happens that the car they drive has a leather interior, under long-term friction or frequent exposure to dry air conditioning environments, the leather will crack. If the situation is not severe, simply buy some wax specifically designed to moisturize leather and apply it. If there are flakes falling off the leather, then you need to use some specialized leather pigments for spraying, followed by applying a thicker layer of wax on the leather surface to try to re-moisturize the leather. Remember! Before applying the wax, you must clean the leather thoroughly. You can wipe it with a damp cloth, but you must wait until it is dry before applying the wax.

Method Seven: Distinguish Between Fine and Coarse Wax

Finally, it's the car's exterior wash! Washing the car with water and cleaning agents is very simple, no need to say more. On the contrary, there are some things to note about waxing. First, the place where you wax should not have dust flying around. Second, the car being waxed should not have too much water; a few droplets attached are fine. Additionally, the technique for waxing is to apply it gradually in circular motions. After application, allow sufficient time for the wax to adhere to the body, then use a dry cloth to wipe away the wax powder. For newer cars, fine wax can be used. For older cars that haven't been waxed for a long time, coarse wax can be used. After waxing, it's best not to drive immediately because the wax has not yet firmly adhered to the body and can easily be blown off by strong winds.
