Villa construction drawings; A girl fell down and a key stabbed into her head by 4 centimeters.

by maojin95700 on 2011-11-22 10:18:10

When the girl was taken to the hospital, her parents recalled the shocking incident to the doctor. It was reported that 9 people were sentenced for producing and selling substandard roast duck, with 12,000 ducks already entering the market. On the night of the incident, the family had already finished dinner. The parents were watching TV while the girl was happily playing at home. The key in her hand was her temporary toy, along with the villa construction plan. The girl ran back and forth on the floor with the key. These actions seemed too normal to her parents.

Currently, the girl is still under treatment in the hospital. (This report by journalist Xia Haijun)

At around 8:30 PM, the reporter arrived at the Second People's Hospital. The doctor on duty in the emergency department introduced that the appearance of the girl when she was brought in was very frightening - he had never seen such a special patient before. "A key was inserted into her head, almost entirely entering the girl's skull." The girl was still conscious and able to speak when she was brought in. Her family held her tightly to prevent her from moving her hands and hitting the key, thus avoiding further harm. After an emergency treatment, the girl was sent to the neurosurgery department for treatment.

A key can open a door, but it can also nearly cost someone their life. At around 8 PM last night, the Second People's Hospital of Hefei received a special patient - a 5-year-old girl whose forehead was pierced by a key! The key was almost completely inserted into the girl's head, making the scene hair-raising and almost collapsing her family emotionally.

At around 9 PM, the seriously injured girl was rushed into the operating room of the hospital. The reporter saw several family members, including her elderly grandfather, anxiously waiting outside the operating room. Every time the operating room door opened, they would rush to the door to inquire about the surgery situation. The neurosurgeon explained that the girl's injuries were severe. The key had penetrated 4 cm deep, breaking through the cranial cavity and causing cerebral bleeding. "The condition can be described as 'life-threatening'."

The injury came too suddenly. The girl accidentally tripped and fell face down. The key she was holding pierced directly into her forehead. Her screams shocked her parents who clearly saw that their beloved child had suffered terrible harm. After the girl was injured, her family was overwhelmed with guilt. The parents could not hide their inner remorse, admitting they had overlooked the potential danger of the key in their daughter's hand.
