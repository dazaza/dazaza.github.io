Warm underwear for severe winter: Yunnan's Luliang integrates more than 60 million yuan of funds to promote the development of sericulture industry.

by uyahd9mjik on 2012-02-14 16:41:54

Through the projects of supporting sericulture industry that were completed last year and are currently being implemented, Luliang County is gradually transforming from a large county of sericulture resources to a strong county of silk industry.

1. The ongoing provincial integrated fund support for agricultural industrialization project supports the leading enterprises in the county to promote the deep processing of silk, carry out technical transformation of traditional handicraft workshops, reach an annual production capacity of 20,000 branded silk quilts, which can add annual tax revenue of 800,000 yuan. The project funds mainly rely on provincial financial support of 1.4 million yuan and enterprise self-raised funds of 5 million yuan for implementation.

2. The central fiscal poverty alleviation fund project that is currently being organized and implemented. Through the assistance channel of industrial poverty alleviation, the central finance invests 500,000 yuan, integrates 1.12 million yuan of agricultural funds in the project area, and will plant 2,500 mu of mulberry trees in three main mulberry parks in Majie Town.

3. Through the funds for supporting farmers' cooperative organizations. Subsidizes 300,000 yuan of fiscal funds to two farmers' organizations involving the sericulture industry in the county, focusing on the demonstration and promotion of grid cocoon technology and training of mulberry planting and silkworm breeding practical techniques.

Currently, more than 50,000 households and nearly 150,000 people in Luliang County are engaged in the sericulture industry. The county has 140,000 mu of mulberry garden area and produces 9,500 tons of fresh cocoons annually. The entire industry has formed a circular economic industrial chain integrating mulberry tree planting, silkworm seed production, cocoon purchasing, silk production, silk weaving dyeing, clothing processing, and cocoon sand utilization.

1. Through the implementation of provincial sericulture industry development projects. The total investment in the project is 31.5 million yuan, including: 6.8 million yuan of provincial special fiscal funds, 9.2 million yuan of integrated departmental funds, and 15.5 million yuan raised by the masses. It has completed the construction of 310 new and expanded small silkworm communal rearing rooms, built 2 collection and drying stations, planted 21,000 mu of new mulberry gardens, and renovated 12,000 mu of low-yield mulberry gardens, conducting specialized training for silkworm farmers reaching 10,000 person-times.

2. The ongoing agricultural industrialization project. With subsidies of 6.8 million yuan from provincial and municipal finances, integration of departments and labor investment of 15.36 million yuan, the project plans to invest 22.16 million yuan. It has completed the renovation of 3,100 standardized small silkworm feeding rooms, built 4 sets of drying stoves. Currently, it is accelerating the variety improvement of 10,000 mu of low-yield mulberry gardens, implementing unified prevention and control of mulberry diseases and silkworm diseases in contiguous mulberry gardens, promoting 2,000 sets of labor-saving silkworm platforms in the new silkworm areas of Shiba Village and Huangguanying Village, and demonstrating and promoting 6 million pieces of grid cocoons in the high-quality mulberry and silkworm bases throughout the county.

Facing the new opportunities and challenges of sericulture industry development, Luliang County in Yunnan Province adheres to a scientific development view, continuously innovates development models, follows the pattern of government-led, supporting leading enterprises, and mass participation. Towns and villages organize the construction of high-quality mulberry gardens and silkworm houses according to unified standards, increase the promotion and application of new technologies, strengthen mulberry garden management, achieve quality improvement and efficiency enhancement, and coordinate the implementation of agricultural projects. Through the integration of project funds, it increases production investment efforts, laying a solid foundation for the new development of the county's sericulture industry.
