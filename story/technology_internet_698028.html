At the 2012 National Two Sessions, food safety once again became one of the top ten issues of public concern. How can we ensure that ordinary people eat safely and with peace of mind? For this purpose, our newspaper conducted exclusive interviews with national committee members Wen Simei and Cheng Ping, as well as academician of the Chinese Academy of Engineering Chen Junshi, to interpret the root causes and response measures of food safety issues.

Key: Numerous loopholes in the industrial chain

Food safety has long been a key focus of government efforts. But why do food safety incidents keep happening? Wen Simei believes this is determined by the food industry chain. He said that with the development and deepening of the economy, the food industry chain has extended, and correspondingly, more vulnerabilities have emerged. Problems may occur at any stage, whether it's production, transportation, processing, or sales. In addition, he thinks that the imperfect market leading to "bad money driving out good" is also one of the reasons.

Chen Junshi believes that China's agricultural production by farmers has yet to reach scaled and standardized production, with insufficient scientific knowledge and unscientific use of fertilizers being objectively present. Driven by the interests of the market economy, some people incite farmers to engage in illegal production. We have over 500,000 small and medium-sized food production enterprises, many of which are small producers. The hygiene conditions and microbial infection indicators of these enterprises always have a part that does not meet standards.

Contradiction: The more effective the regulation, the more problems arise?

The state's regulatory and rectification efforts on food safety have increased year by year, yet the common people feel that food safety problems have become more numerous. What is the reason for this? Chen Junshi explained that the reason why China's food safety problems seem numerous is because people equate issues that are not related to food safety with food safety problems, such as the "colored steamed bun" issue which is actually a counterfeit and inferior product problem.

He added that another reason is the enhanced awareness of food safety among consumers and the media, even exposing some long-standing but previously undiscovered issues, such as the Shuanghui "lean meat powder" incident, which created a focused effect within a short period of time.

Solution: Adjusting the agricultural production structure

Chen Junshi believes that the fundamental solution to food safety problems lies in adjusting the agricultural production structure. Agricultural production should be large-scale and standardized with rules. The production of edible agricultural products should have a certain level of standardization, including the use of pesticides and fertilizers, which need corresponding norms. However, standardizing scattered farmer production will require some time. He emphasized that safe food is produced rather than regulated or inspected. Therefore, addressing potential hazards from the source of production is the key to solving the problem.

Wen Simei stressed that solving food safety issues requires focusing on the management of the entire process, all links, and multiple nodes, establishing corresponding standards and traceability systems. Whichever link encounters a problem should be penalized accordingly, leaving no place for illegal behavior to hide.

Proposal: Be rational consumers

While the state and industry are making efforts, ordinary people can also take action from small steps to prevent unsafe food. For this, Chen Junshi proposed first to diversify food consumption. Second, branded food is more reassuring. Third, one should learn some scientific knowledge to enhance self-protection.

Wen Simei emphasized that one should be a rational consumer, learning more about food safety knowledge, avoiding food without an "identity certificate," and purchasing food from large supermarkets which have higher costs of violating laws and thus a smaller probability of selling unsafe food. Additionally, he suggested that ordinary people further increase their rights protection awareness, regardless of the cost, daring to report, cracking down on counterfeit and inferior goods, and showing no tolerance for illegal merchants.

Dialogue with National Committee Member Cheng Ping:

99% Safety in Guangdong Province's Livestock Products

National Committee Member Cheng Ping, Deputy Director of the Guangdong Provincial Department of Agriculture, told our reporter that the provincial department of agriculture has made a series of efforts and innovations in controlling the production source of food and the testing system, ensuring 99% safety in livestock products and 90%~95% safety in vegetable products, with the quality and safety of agricultural products gradually improving.

Reporter: What measures has Guangdong Province taken in responding to food safety issues?

Cheng Ping: Safe food is produced. Grasping the safety of the source of agricultural products is crucial. For input items used in agricultural product production, such as fertilizers, pesticides, and feed, we conduct relevant registration and testing. We also provide training to farmers, guiding them to plant scientifically, thereby enhancing the safety of the food source. Secondly, strengthen control at each link, optimize the production environment, promote production bases and farmers' cooperatives, and guide scaled and standardized production. Thirdly, further improve the regulatory system, standardize market access mechanisms, implement labeled management, conduct regular or random inspections of enterprises and production bases from the provincial to the municipal and county levels, and conduct special sampling tests of agricultural products.

Reporter: What innovative points does Guangdong Province have in solving food safety issues?

Cheng Ping: We pioneered labeled management, focusing on the "three products and one mark + brand-name products." The three products refer to green food, organic food, and pollution-free food, while the one mark refers to geographical indication. Special rectifications are carried out on these items, starting from these brand-name products to strengthen labeled management and establish a linkage mechanism. Once the food industry leaders are handled well, other problems will naturally be resolved.

In addition, we implemented information management of the testing system relatively early, effectively monitoring the province's food safety information. At the same time, we attach great importance to the training of the testing team, establishing two provincial expert groups for emergency response and agricultural product expertise. These groups provide scientific suggestions to address agricultural product safety hazards and include the training of expert teams below the provincial level.

Reporters Li Hua, Li Dong, Ren Chaoliang
