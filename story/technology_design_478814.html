Policy "stabilizes" the market, light rare earth over supply

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-08-17 15:42:54

After a long period of increase, the prices of all kinds of rare earth products began to turn downward, and a panic over the price crash began to spread in the August market. Many sellers who had stockpiled goods began to sell off, and everyone was unsure about the prices. On August 8, a person in charge of a Jiangsu rare earth smelting enterprise told our newspaper.

On August 11, the new round of inspection and rectification of the rare earth industry by six ministries became the hope for stabilizing prices. If the enforcement is very strong and does not allow illegal rare earth products to enter the market, the prices will stabilize. Zhang Zhong, general manager of Baotou Steel Rare Earth Company, said.

However, how long can the practice of controlling prices through the execution of directive plans last? With foreign mines starting production one after another, in three to five years, at least in terms of light rare earth supply, China's absolute advantage position will face challenges.

Price without demand?

The sharp rise in rare earth prices in the past six months, some people in the industry believe, is not a normal phenomenon. It's useless if the price rises this high; there must be market transactions. A person in charge of the Chinese Rare Earth Society said at the Third China Baotou Rare Earth Forum. In his view, the current market is a market with prices but no demand.

By the end of June, the total amount of China's rare earth exports this year was only about 7,000 tons or so (calculated as oxides), compared to the same period in 2010, the decline exceeded 60%. The first batch of rare earth export quotas issued by the Ministry of Commerce this year, however, was 14,446 tons. Prices have been hyped up too high, and foreign countries can't accept it for now. Zhang Zhong explained that from July, foreign demand sides gradually began to accept the existing prices. Contracts that couldn't be signed due to price issues in the past few months also started signing in July.

But a company in Jiangsu with rare earth export quotas held a different view. Demand is still very weak, everyone is watching, especially since prices started falling in recent weeks.

A common view within the industry is that the skyrocketing of rare earth prices in the past few months is an abnormal situation, and behind the soaring trend is the influx of large amounts of speculative capital into the rare earth industry. Some unrelated people also come in to buy and hoard. A director of a Shanghai rare earth trading company told reporters like this, he believed that the current stockpile volume is even enough for the market to use for two years. The implementation of national rectification actions is very important. If it continues as before, a sharp drop in prices is inevitable.

In fact, the decline in prices has already begun, and the first to change the upward trend were praseodymium-neodymium products. According to statistics from RuiDao Metal Network, the price of praseodymium-neodymium oxide started to fall from July 8. And by August 12, the market had already seen a neodymium oxide quote of 1.3 million yuan per ton, while in early July, the price of neodymium oxide was still 1.5 million yuan per ton.

And entering late July, the prices of other rare earth products such as cerium oxide and lanthanum oxide also entered the falling period. By August 12, in just two short weeks, the decline reached about 20%.

The aforementioned person in charge of the Jiangsu rare earth enterprise said that there are currently a large number of sellers in the market who are selling off their accumulated goods. Many of them are outsiders who came to speculate before, without sales channels, they start dumping when prices fall.

Policy stabilizing the market

However, starting from last week, all rare earth product prices have once again entered a relatively stable period. The market attributes the cause to the notice posted on the departmental website by the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) in the afternoon of August 8.

This "Notice on Carrying Out Special Rectification Actions for the Order of National Rare Earth Production" issued by six ministries including MIIT, Supervision Department, Environmental Protection Department, etc., requires special rectification of the order of rare earth production starting from August 1, focusing on various behaviors without or exceeding directive production plans. The behavior of rare earth smelting and separation enterprises purchasing illegal rare earth ore products, as well as the behavior of circulation enterprises purchasing illegal or non-directive production plan rare earth products, are also included.

At the same time, Director Jia Yinsong of the Rare Earth Office under the Raw Materials Industry Department of MIIT said that in the second half of the year, MIIT will soon introduce measures such as management methods for directive production plans, access regulations for the rare earth industry, and management methods for rare earth enterprise production permits.

Despite this, smuggling of rare earths, over-standard production, and other behaviors have continued despite repeated treatments, mineral market conditions. Taking mining quotas as an example, over-mining of light rare earths generally occurs around 10% each year, and heavy rare earths sometimes exceed mining quotas by nearly double in some years.

Some experts believe that driven by the high-price benefits of rare earths earlier, the actual mining and production indicators for rare earths this year were already used up by June and July, which is also the reason why China Minmetals advocated immediate voluntary shutdowns starting from early August 2011 and the special rectification actions by the six ministries. As a responsible enterprise, production stability should be ensured; you cannot use up the indicators given by the state in the first half of the year. Zhang Zhong didn't think much of China Minmetals' initiative. According to him, the indicators allocated by the state to Baotou Steel Rare Earth have only been consumed by about 50%.

According to insiders, many of the rare earth products stockpiled by buyers belong to illegal products outside the directive plan. If the planned thorough rectification can be carried out, the supply and demand situation in the rare earth market may undergo a complete transformation, and the current trend of price declines will be curbed.

But will this year's rectification actions really be effective? The market prices have temporarily returned to calm, but there is still a considerable distance from true market stability, and everyone is watching. The aforementioned person in charge of the Jiangsu enterprise said.

Light rare earth surplus

As long as the directive production can be fully implemented, under the current absolute advantage of China's rare earth production in the world, rare earth prices can remain at the current high level. However, what about in a few years?

In May this year, Goldman Sachs released a report stating that with Western companies opening new mines, light rare earths may experience oversupply by 2013.

In fact, the current sharp rise in rare earth prices has already stimulated some foreign mines to restart mining. Both Molycorp of the United States and Lynas of Australia have formulated and implemented rare earth mining plans. According to information disclosed so far, Molycorp's Mountain Pass mine in the U.S. plans to reach a production capacity of 20,000 tons by the end of 2012, and the expected production capacity of Lynas-controlled Mount Weld mine will also exceed 20,000 tons. Additionally, Japan's rare earth mining in Vietnam and India is also underway.

However, whether these mines can complete their production plans on schedule remains controversial. Zhang Zhong believes that Goldman Sachs' timetable is set too early. The irreplaceability of rare earths determines that the high growth of rigid demand for rare earths in the next five years will continue to be maintained. Zhang Zhong believes that more than 10 years of blank space in the rare earth industry in Western countries has lost a complete industrial system, and it will be difficult to restore, plus environmental pressure, which will delay the release of effective rare earth production capacity abroad.

Before overseas rare earth mines extract large-scale rare earths, the contradiction between supply and demand will continue to intensify, and the overall rare earth prices will still remain at a high level.

Unlike light rare earths, China's current reserves of heavy rare earths still hold an absolute advantage. Overseas mines are basically light rare earths. Lin Dongru, Secretary-General of the Chinese Rare Earth Society, said that there is currently no danger of oversupply in heavy rare earths.
