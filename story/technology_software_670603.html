Man demands 5000 yuan breakup fee, rejected and kills his lover's family of three

by foot2050 on 2012-02-21 09:01:44

On February 9, a man was rescued from an elevator and then assaulted a maintenance worker. The suspect, Luo Wen, under police escort, went to the crime scene for identification.

Murderer killed three members of a family in the middle of the night. Police conducted a five-day and four-night mountain encirclement search and captured the suspect.

A murder case triggered by a 5000 yuan breakup fee.

Reported by Jiang Gewei and Luo Changhong of the Legal System Weekly with text and photos.

"Why did you carry a kitchen knife and a wooden stick if you just wanted her to repay the money?"

"I was very angry at that time." "If she had agreed to return me four or five thousand yuan, there would have been no problem."

On February 9, in the interrogation room of the Changsha County Public Security Bureau, Luo Wen, with his awkward Mandarin, evasively defended himself. His facial expressions alternated between anger and frustration, responding nervously to various media questions. However, his trembling hands, bound in handcuffs, revealed his inner terror.

Five days ago, Luo Wen brutally murdered Li's family of three with a wooden stick. Afterwards, he fled fearing punishment, attempting suicide by drinking pesticide and drowning, but failed. He then hid in the mountains, beginning a five-day and four-night escape.

At two o'clock in the afternoon on February 9, Zeng WeiGuo, Director of the Changsha County Public Security Bureau, informed the media that the special major murder case of "February 4" in Shuangjiang Town, Changsha County, had been successfully solved.

On the day of the press conference, the psychological motives and escape details of suspect Luo Wen became the focus of media attention. After several days of continuous cold weather searches, the Changsha police eventually captured suspect Luo Wen, earning applause from nearly a thousand villagers within a twenty-kilometer radius who had heard the news.

Incident: Refusal of Breakup Fee

Luo Wen, male, 33 years old, dropped out of junior high school, unmarried, a native of Longhua Village, Shuangjiang Town, Changsha County.

From December 2010 to December 2011, Luo Wen had emotional disputes with Li Mou. Later, Li proposed ending their improper relationship. Luo then demanded that Li refund him the money spent on her, which Li refused and insulted him. Multiple negotiations ended in arguments, causing Luo Wen to harbor hatred.

Luo Wen confessed that at one o'clock in the morning on February 4, after leaving someone else's house, he returned home, took a wooden stick and a kitchen knife, and walked straight to Li's house, entering through the window. At this time, Li and her six-year-old son were already asleep, as was Wang, Li’s 66-year-old mother-in-law, in the adjacent room.

Luo Wen woke up the sleeping Li and once again requested her to return the 5000 yuan he had spent on her. Li refused again, saying, "I don't have any money to give you, do whatever you want!"

"At that time, I put the wooden stick and the kitchen knife in the living room. If she had agreed to refund me four or five thousand yuan, it would have been fine. She said if I wanted to kill her, then kill her whole family... these words drove me crazy..." Luo Wen said that last year, he didn't give his parents a single penny from his earnings, spending most of it on Li. When Li later didn't want to be with him anymore, he decided to get back what belonged to him.

Luo Wen said that he was provoked by Li's words, ran into the living room, and hit Li on the head several times with the stick. Due to losing control of his emotions, the enraged Luo Wen then picked up the kitchen knife and slashed both Li and her six-year-old son several times. At this point, Li's mother-in-law in another room woke up. Afraid of being exposed, Luo Wen, red-eyed with rage, swung the knife at the elderly woman...

Escape: Surviving by digging up radishes in the deep mountains

After the killing, seeing the blood all over the room, Luo Wen immediately fell from anger into panic.

Luo left behind his bloody outerwear, slippers, and underwear at the scene and turned to leave. Knowing he had nowhere to run, Luo Wen returned home and drank pesticide, "preparing for myself."

After drinking the pesticide, Luo Wen jumped into the village pond, "the water was too shallow, couldn't drown, drank a lot of water." Luo Wen struggled to climb out of the pond.

"Later, I went back to my old house, took a quilt, and slept in someone else's pigsty," Luo Wen said. As dawn approached, he grabbed a bundle of straw and ran up Longhua Mountain. In his hurried escape, Luo Wen only wore a few thin clothes.

During the few days of escape, Luo suffered physically and mentally. Either he would lay down some straw on the ground to sleep for the night or find abandoned old houses to rest in. For five days and four nights, he survived by eating radishes from the mountains. He dared not close his eyes at night, fearing discovery by villagers and police searches, while images of the massacre constantly replayed before his eyes.

Investigation: Five-day and Four-night Encirclement Search Captures Suspect

After receiving the alarm on February 4, the Changsha County Public Security Bureau quickly organized investigators to rush to the scene to conduct investigation work. Upon learning of the case, Li Jiande, Director of the Changsha Municipal Public Security Bureau (Weibo), required full assistance from the Criminal Investigation Brigade and other departments to solve the case as soon as possible. Deputy Director Ou Yike and other leaders also successively arrived at the crime scene to direct the investigation work.

After the incident, the criminal investigation team of the Changsha County Public Security Bureau quickly rushed to the crime scene. During the investigation, the officers discovered the bloody clothing, slippers, and other items left by the perpetrator. Through comparison and identification, the police identified Luo Wen as the prime suspect.

Longhua Mountain, located at the intersection of Changsha, Pingjiang, and Liuyang counties, has perilous terrain and steep roads, providing a natural barrier for Luo Wen's hiding place and also posing significant difficulties for the police investigation.

After identifying Luo Wen, the police issued a reward notice to local residents. Simultaneously, more than a hundred police officers and local cadres and villagers worked around the clock, braving the cold to set up checkpoints, conduct searches, and hunt for the suspect in the mountains.

While searching the surrounding forests, the police found a pile of radish peels, seemingly left by someone chewing them. The police immediately took the radish peels back for identification, confirming they were left by Luo Wen. The remaining radish peels confirmed that Luo Wen was hiding nearby, gradually narrowing down the search area, and organizing teams to ascend the mountain to search for the suspect.

In the morning of February 8, the police received a tip-off from a villager, reporting that when checking a chicken coop on the mountain, they found that items inside had been tampered with. A quilt originally placed next to the chicken coop had been moved to the loft floor, and there were obvious signs of someone having slept on it.

Upon receiving the villager's lead, the Changsha County Public Security Bureau immediately reported to the municipal bureau and promptly deployed a large number of police forces. Some officers guarded the various routes down the mountain, while others pursued the suspect in the direction of his escape.

"In 15 minutes, the police surrounded the slope where the suspect was hiding and cut off the connection between the slope and the surrounding mountains," said Zhou Gangqiang, Deputy Director of the Changsha County Public Security Bureau. Luo Wen made a wrong choice in fleeing, not heading deeper into the mountains but trying to break out of the small slope, "he inadvertently entered the police's encirclement."

Deputy Chief of the Criminal Investigation Brigade of the Changsha Municipal Public Security Bureau, Long Wenjin, along with Zhang Qiang and Zhang Wu of the Changsha County Public Security Bureau, were the first to catch up with the suspect. Under Long Wenjin's command, the two officers leaped forward and tackled the suspect to the ground, successfully capturing him.

At two o'clock in the afternoon on February 9, the villager who provided the tip-off, Mr. Wu, received a cash reward of 20,000 yuan.

"The Expansion of Desire" is the Root Cause of Crime

"To kill the person he once loved for 5000 yuan, how could he bring himself to do it?" This became something the public, including the journalists present, could not understand.

Faced with reporters' questioning, Luo Wen either avoided answering or gave irrelevant responses, "I was very angry at that time."

Psychologists see these behaviors as predictable and preventable. Liu Lijing, the executive vice secretary-general of the Psychological Society of Changsha, stated that Luo Wen's case is a typical example of falling into the depths of crime due to "desire expansion" because he could not obtain what he wanted.

Liu Lijing analyzed that Luo Wen, 33 years old and unmarried, was certainly unwilling to easily give up on his relationship with Li Mou. Therefore, when Li proposed breaking up, Luo Wen was very reluctant. To seek psychological balance, Luo Wen demanded compensation for the 5000 yuan expenses.

After multiple unsuccessful negotiations, Luo Wen impulsively committed extreme acts, resulting in both loss of person and property.

After committing the murders, Luo Wen's first psychological reaction was, "I've killed people, and not just one, I will definitely be sentenced to death..." Thus, the fear after the killings led him to attempt suicide. In the confusion, the various methods of suicide he chose could not result in his death. After his initial reaction period passed, the instinct for survival made him choose to flee. Luo Wen's killing behavior was merely the result of impulsiveness due to not being able to swallow his pride.

"When conflicts and frictions occur with others, we should all learn to lower our desires; tolerance and concession are the best ways to resolve conflicts!" Liu Lijing said that disputes and conflicts between people are inevitable. We must remain calm first and preferably seek help from our friends to give ourselves space and time to relieve negative emotions, striving to solve problems through proper channels.

"Unacceptable relationships often stem from unacceptable psychology." National second-level marriage and family consultant Hu Junlian analyzed that the relationship between the unmarried Luo Wen and the married Li Mou contained elements contrary to ethics and morality, destined not to bear the fruit of love. From Luo Wen's perspective, a rural young man who remains unmarried at 33 years old must have reasons for failing to marry. Perhaps due to poverty and poor conditions, he has always lacked self-esteem and a sense of self-worth. People with personality defects are prone to act emotionally. Coupled with poor economic conditions, facing abandonment makes them feel deceived and manipulated, leading to a loss of reason.

"The bloody case once again warns the world to establish a correct view of marriage." Hu Junlian indicated that marriage comes with conditions. Having feelings with a married person is futile and unproductive, a situation that occurs frequently.

At three o'clock in the afternoon on February 9, the arrival of three police cars broke the tranquility of the Xinyiwu Group in Longhua Village again.

The handcuffed Luo Wen was escorted off the police car to identify the crime scene. The news spread quickly, and villagers from a twenty-kilometer radius gathered, nearly a thousand people surrounding the scene tightly.

"You beast, you're not human! How could you bring yourself to do it? The child is only six years old..." Relatives of the victims, standing at the center of the circle, angrily cursed Luo Wen, with some even wanting to rush forward to attack him.

At this moment, Luo Wen kept his head down, silent in the face of accusations and curses, even devoid of expression.
