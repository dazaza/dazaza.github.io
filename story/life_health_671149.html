What are the methods to prevent bedsores? Currently, bedsores remain one of the main causes of death for paraplegic patients. This shows that small bedsores, if not properly treated, can potentially lead to fatal consequences. Next, Director Zhao Jianlin from Shanxi Taiyuan Traditional Chinese Medicine Hospital for Bedsores will introduce the methods to prevent bedsores.

The methods to prevent bedsores are:

1. Change positions frequently, lying on the left or right side of the body. Generally, turn over every 2-3 hours, not exceeding 4 hours. When assisting the patient in turning over, avoid dragging, pulling, and pushing actions to prevent skin abrasions.

2. Tighten the sheets and pay attention to frequent changes and washes to maintain cleanliness, dryness, smoothness, and no debris.

3. Maintain body cleanliness, use hot water to clean at least once a day, then use 50% alcohol to massage the patient's waist, back, and pressure areas for about 3-5 minutes to prevent bedsores. In the early stages of bedsores, the skin may turn red, and massage must be started immediately. However, when massaging, do not add extra pressure on the area. You can use the thumb to perform circular motions from near the sore area outward.

4. Place cushions such as air rings, cotton rings, sponge pads, or water mattresses on bony prominences. Note that the air ring should not be inflated too much, two-thirds full is ideal, with the valve facing down, placed between the legs.

5. Pay attention to consuming various nutrients relatively evenly to enhance resistance and tissue repair capabilities.

6. Do not use damaged bedpans. When using a bedpan, do not force it in or out; if necessary, place soft paper or cloth on the edge of the bedpan to avoid skin abrasions. In the initial stage of bedsores, symptoms such as swelling, numbness, or tenderness may appear. If infection is prevented from further pressure, moisture, and friction, and circular massage is performed to improve local blood circulation, development can be stopped, and recovery can occur.

Timely prevention of bedsores can effectively prevent their occurrence. Of course, if bedsores occur, timely diagnosis and treatment at a regular hospital are necessary.
