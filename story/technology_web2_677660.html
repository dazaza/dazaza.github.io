How to Choose an Abortion Method for Unplanned Pregnancy? Experts from Xi'an Cai'ai Gynecology Hospital introduce the principle of choosing an abortion method for unplanned pregnancy: choose a method that causes minimal harm to the uterus, has no aftereffects, and truly protects the uterus.

How to choose an abortion method for unplanned pregnancy? Experts point out that common methods of artificial abortion include medical abortion, painless abortion, and superconducting visual abortion. Now, we will introduce the characteristics of these three methods:

1. Medical abortion: This involves taking medication under medical supervision to induce an abortion. The greatest benefit is that there is no mechanical intervention, avoiding damage or infection to the reproductive organs. Drawback: Lower success rate, approximately 75%. If problems occur, such as incomplete abortion, residual fetal membrane tissue can cause excessive bleeding, requiring immediate hospitalization for curettage; otherwise, it could be life-threatening. Individuals with heart disease or anemia should use caution.

2. Regular Painless Abortion: This is currently one of the more commonly chosen methods, where the patient is put under general anesthesia during the procedure. The greatest advantage is its high success rate, lack of pain, and quick duration. However, in addition to the risk of infection similar to regular abortions, there is a fatal weakness: if uterine perforation occurs during the procedure, the patient, being under anesthesia, would have no reaction. Sometimes, by the time the doctor notices, it may be too late, posing a life-threatening situation.

3. Superconducting Visual Abortion: Based on painless abortion, a hysteroscope is inserted into the uterine cavity via the cervical canal. Cold light illuminates the uterine cavity, making the location of the gestational sac clearly visible. Through optical magnification, the internal condition of the uterine cavity is transmitted and displayed on a computer monitor screen, allowing the surgeon to see clearly inside the uterus, including the villi and decidua. In this case, the gestational sac can be easily removed without any pain to the patient. Safe, minimally invasive, and truly protective of the uterus.
