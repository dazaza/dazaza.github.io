Hongcun: A Picturesque and Beautiful Village Trip

by jj0505 on 2012-02-18 14:23:42

Hongcun, a World Heritage Site, is known as the "village in a Chinese painting". It is the most outstanding and typical representative of Huizhou culture. There are more than 100 well-preserved ancient dwellings from the Ming and Qing dynasties here. Not only is it the perfect combination of natural scenery and cultural landscape, but also an excellent embodiment of Feng Shui theory in Huizhou. Some scenes of the Oscar-winning movie *Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon* were filmed here.

We need to take the tourist shuttle bus at the Huangshan City Tourism Distribution Center. After about one hour and fifteen minutes, we arrive at Hongcun, with tickets priced at 14 yuan per person. We then purchase the entrance tickets (full price 80 yuan per person, students, seniors over 60 years old, and military personnel can get discounted tickets for 40 yuan with valid identification). Following this, our true Hongcun journey begins.

The Hua Bridge in the center of Nanhu Lake is the first scene from *Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon*. After crossing Nanhu Lake, turn left to reach the Nanhu Academy, which was a private school in ancient times. All descendants of the Wang family in Hongcun had to receive their early education here before being allowed to attend lectures at Zhidaotang. Famous figures such as Wang Daxie, the acting premier during the Republic of China era, and Wang Dinggui, the richest man in the village, both received their education here.

After visiting the Nanhu Academy, we return to Hua Bridge and enter the village in the opposite direction. Along the way, we can see that the entire village is surrounded by water flows. This is the unique charm of Hongcun. These water systems were originally constructed for fire prevention purposes. In the past, before the Wang family relocated here, they lived elsewhere and suffered a fire, prompting them to move en masse. "Once bitten, twice shy," hence they built the water system. If you ever get lost in the village, don't worry—just follow the direction of the water flow to find your way out. Aren't you amazed by the ingenious design of the Huizhou people?

Walking through some houses and following the "Proceed for Sightseeing" sign, we arrive at another beautiful spot in Hongcun—the Moon Pond. This is the center of the village. Due to its unique cultural landscape and picturesque scenery, students from various art colleges come here to paint. Most of the time, they gather around the Moon Pond, sketching the white walls and black tiles of Huizhou-style architecture along with the pond itself, making this place one of the most scenic spots in Hongcun.

After passing the Moon Pond and following the signs, we reach the other end of the village. Here, there are two towering trees in front of us—one is a ginkgo tree, also known as the white fruit tree, and the other is a red poplar tree. The ancient customs of Hongcun dictate that when there is a joyous occasion, the groom must carry the bride around the red poplar tree three times. However, if there is a funeral, one must circle the ginkgo tree three times.

After seeing these two trees, we arrive at the last attraction, Chengzhi Hall, which is also the most representative building among the Huizhou cultural sites of Hongcun and even the entire city of Huangshan. Known as the "Folk Forbidden City," it was the private villa of Wang Dinggui, the richest man in the village, covering an area of 2,100 square meters. There are three unique features of this house: First, it uses the village's water system for live-water fishkeeping, eliminating the hassle of changing water. Second, it has complete entertainment facilities, including the "Paisang Pavilion" for playing mahjong and the "Tunyun轩" for smoking opium. Third, and most astonishingly, the wood carvings on the door lintels and pillars are exquisitely crafted, painted in gold and vivid colors, making it a masterpiece of Huizhou wood carving and representing the highest craftsmanship of Huizhou artisans at the time.

These are the regular routes for touring Hongcun. Of course, there are also some less important attractions not mentioned here. If time permits, I strongly recommend that you visit all of them!
