2010 Chinese Super League: 14 Teams Competing, Alternative Plan for 4 Teams to Battle in Play-offs for Substitution Slots

by yinding999 on 2009-12-18 04:35:12

In 2010, 14 teams will compete in the Chinese Super League, with another proposal suggesting a play-off among four teams for relegation spots. Delegates from various parties who will attend the new session of the Football Congress of China have received the official notification from the Chinese Football Association (CFA). The Football Congress will be held in Beijing on the 21st and 22nd of this month. "Currently, the CFA has brainstormed several opinions regarding possible changes in the new season's Super League, which may be discussed in a closed-door small group setting during the Football Congress," a source within the CFA informed the reporter last night.

Will fourteen teams compete in the Super League?

It is currently almost undisputed that Guangyao and Chengdu, two teams confirmed to have played rigged matches, will definitely be relegated. Despite media claims that these two teams might continue in the Super League, starting the new season with negative points as per the Italian Serie A precedent was also considered as one of the plans; however, according to relevant regulations of the CFA, such a possibility does not exist.

Due to implications from the General Administration of Sport hinting at reducing the number of teams in the Super League, after Guangzhou and Chengdu are relegated, the successful promotion teams from the previous season, Liaoning and Nanchang, along with the current 12 Super League teams, making up a total of 14 teams, is one of the key schemes being considered by the CFA currently. However, this plan would lead to a reduction in the scale of the Super League, and given that the market and social influence of the Super League had gradually recovered in the 2009 season, this scheme faces significant external resistance.

Maintaining the status quo with four teams competing for relegation spots?

After Guangzhou and Chengdu are determined to be relegated, if the CFA considers adding teams to the Super League, there are currently two proposals: the first is for Hangzhou and Chongqing, the two relegated teams from the 2009 season, to remain in the Super League; the second is for Hangzhou, Chongqing, as well as the third and fourth place teams from the previous season's China League Two, Shenyang Dongjin and Shanghai East Asia, to participate in a play-off, with the two winning teams entering the Super League.

At this Football Congress, the CFA will gather opinions from all parties regarding the size of the new season's Super League and communicate with related clubs to seek advice.

Chen Hong, the general manager of Chongqing Lifan, who will represent the team at the Football Congress, stated: "If such a situation arises, Chongqing Lifan will make its own choice based on the overall interests of Chongqing football and the calls of the fans."
