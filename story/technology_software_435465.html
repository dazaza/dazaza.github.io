Understand latex paint, know how to choose building materials

by 888maggic on 2011-05-23 10:06:12

Latex paint is the common name for emulsion paint, which was born in the mid-to-late 1970s. It refers to a class of synthetic resin emulsion paints represented by acrylic ester copolymer emulsions. Latex paints can be categorized as follows: by application, into interior wall latex paint, etc.; by gloss effect, into matte, satin, semi-gloss, silk gloss, and glossy latex paint, etc.; by base material, into pure acrylic, styrene-acrylic, vinyl-acrylic, and tert-carbon paint, etc.; by decorative effect, into flat coating, stippling, and textured coatings, etc.; by solvent, into water-soluble latex paint, water-soluble paint, and solvent-based latex paint, etc.; by decorative function, into general-purpose latex paint and functional (antibacterial, anti-stain, etc.) latex paint.

Characteristics

① Water-soluble Interior Wall Latex Paint

Water-soluble interior wall latex paint is pollution-free, non-toxic, and poses no fire hazard. It is easy to apply, dries quickly, and has good water resistance and washability. The color tone is soft. Water-soluble interior wall latex paint uses water as the dispersing medium, avoiding environmental pollution issues caused by organic solvent gases. Its good breathability prevents blistering caused by temperature and pressure differences between the inside and outside of the film, making it suitable for newly constructed walls that have not fully dried.

② Water-soluble Paint

Water-soluble paint is inexpensive and offers a certain level of decorative and protective properties. The production process is simple, with abundant resources and easily obtainable raw materials. However, its washability is inferior to latex paint, generally less than 10 times. It tends to peel, crack, bubble, and has poor weather resistance. It holds a significant share in rural markets.

③ Solvent-based Interior Wall Latex Paint

Solvent-based interior wall latex paint primarily uses high molecular synthetic resin as the film-forming substance and requires organic solvents as diluents. This type of paint is made by mixing pigments, fillers, and additives, and it is a volatile paint. Its price is higher than water-soluble interior wall latex paint and water-soluble paint. Since this type of paint uses flammable solvents, there is a risk of fire during construction. However, its performance under low-temperature construction conditions is better than water-soluble interior wall latex paint and water-soluble paint. It has good weather resistance and stain resistance, along with thickness, gloss, water resistance, and alkali resistance. However, when applied on damp substrates, it is prone to peeling, bubbling, and falling off.

④ General-purpose Latex Paint

General-purpose latex paint represents a large category of general-use latex paints, catering to different consumer levels. It is currently the product with the largest market share. The most common type is matte latex paint, which appears white without gloss, ensuring clean and tidy walls while providing some degree of scrub resistance and good hiding power. A typical example is silk wall paint, which feels as smooth, fine, and comfortable as silk fabric. The gloss can be seen on the side walls but is not very noticeable from the front. This type of latex paint is quite demanding on the walls; if it's an old wall being refurbished, even slight unevenness in the substrate will show up under lighting. Therefore, the construction requirements are also relatively high, necessitating meticulous workmanship to achieve its elegant, delicate, and refined effects.

⑤ Anti-stain Latex Paint

Anti-stain latex paint is a latex paint with certain anti-stain functions. It can easily remove water-soluble stains such as water-based pens, fingerprints, and pencil marks, and oil stains can also be wiped off with cleaning agents. However, for some chemical substances like ink, it cannot be completely restored, only showing better stain resistance. It has a certain anti-stain role but is not absolutely stain-proof.

⑥ Antibacterial Latex Paint

People's pursuit of healthy, clean ecological living environments is becoming increasingly strong, and their attention to antibacterial products is also increasing. In addition to having a detailed and full coating, water resistance, mildew resistance, and weather resistance, antibacterial latex paint also features antibacterial functionality. Its emergence has promoted the development of architectural coatings. Currently, ideal antibacterial materials are inorganic antibacterial agents, including metal ion-type inorganic antibacterial agents and oxide-type antibacterial agents. These agents have killing and inhibitory effects on common microorganisms, Staphylococcus aureus, E. coli, Candida albicans, yeast, and mold. Choosing antibacterial latex paint can improve the living environment to a certain extent.

⑦ Tert-Carbon Paint

The base material of tert-carbon paint is based on the copolymer of tert-carboxylic acid vinyl ester. Due to the presence of a large multi-branched tert-carbon group on the tert-carboxylic acid vinyl ester, it protects the base material molecular chain and has a plasticizing effect similar to solvents. Therefore, tert-carbon paint has excellent film properties, outstanding endurance, decorative properties, construction properties, and environmental health properties. It contains no formaldehyde, and the VOC content is extremely low.
