Tencent and Guangdong Unicom launch "WeChat data plan", available on August 8th

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 14:48:14

Yesterday's rumors about the cooperation between WeChat and Guangdong Unicom "Good Buddy" have been verified today. The timing was carefully chosen - at 8:08 AM, Tencent announced on its YiXun website a partnership with China Unicom to launch the "WeChat Wo" package. According to YiXun's announcement, the official launch date of this collaboration is August 8, 2013.

In the official introduction, it is stated that "WeChat Wo is more than just a SIM card." Users who choose the "WeChat Wo" package will receive additional data allowances, exclusive WeChat privileges, and the option to select premium Unicom numbers ending in "88".

Globally, there are many internet companies (referred to as "OTT companies" in China) collaborating with telecom operators, such as Line partnering with Docomo in Japan, Google Voice with Sprint in the U.S., and Spotify with Deutsche Telekom in Germany, among others. However, "WeChat Wo" sets a precedent: allowing users to choose the form of collaboration between the internet company and the telecom operator.

Tencent did not announce specific details of its collaboration with the telecom operator but instead allows users to select their desired "WeChat privileges." Among the available privileges are:

Group chat limit expanded from 40 to 60 people

Additional data allowance

WeChat Pay discounts

Free games

Free emoticons

Free music downloads

Free HD movie viewing

HD image sharing

Easier visibility in the "People Nearby" feature (if you wish)

Currently, approximately 23,000 people have participated in customizing their WeChat privileges. Based on this model, the final "WeChat Wo" package launched by Tencent and Guangdong Unicom will provide WeChat privileges based on popularity. It is expected that Tencent may offer multiple privileges rather than just one. Of course, users can also request all privileges.

The "WeChat Wo" package will be launched 22 days later on August 8th. From today until August 4th, the YiXun page will allow users to "like" and choose their desired privileges; from August 5th to 7th, reservations will be made; and sales will begin on August 8th.

Regarding the specific package format, according to previous reports from Phoenix News, WeChat might provide WeChat privileges on top of Guangdong Unicom's existing 36 yuan Wo Pai package targeted at student users. However, it remains unclear whether the "WeChat package" is just a small-scale trial or if it will be extended to more users after enthusiastic participation.

This Monday, we exclusively reported that WeChat would collaborate with mainland operators to launch customized WeChat services. At the time, based on overseas cooperation cases, we speculated that the collaboration between WeChat and operators could involve data packages, exclusive services, call discounts, etc., but we were unsure about the depth of their collaboration. Currently, we still need to wait some time to know what kind of package they will base their collaboration on and whether they will introduce a standalone "WeChat package."

Given that the "WeChat Wo package" news was released through Tencent's e-commerce channel - YiXun, it implies that both parties will cooperate in specific business sales. The official statement claims that "WeChat Wo" will issue physical SIM cards, leading us to believe that future buyers of this SIM card will not only include student users but also many loyal WeChat users. After all, WeChat data usage might already constitute a significant portion of their data expenses.

To reiterate, as a user, I am pleased to see WeChat collaborating with operators. While the privilege functions may not be personally urgent for me, bundling WeChat data into a single package is something I urgently need.

Finally, there's one detail worth discussing: among the selectable "WeChat privileges," there is an item for "free emoticons." Although the emoticons shown on YiXun's official Weibo account are traditional free ones, the mention of "free" suggests the existence of paid emoticons. This indicates that the WeChat 5.0 version, which will support paid emoticons, will not be released later than August 8th. The rest, everyone can go dig for updates themselves.
