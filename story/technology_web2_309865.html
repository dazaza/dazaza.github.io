Arsenal's Narrow Victory Triggers Wenger's Outburst Over One Player; A Time Bomb Forces the Gunners to Act Again

By Reporter Ke Han: At Anfield, Arsenal came from behind to win. Amidst the joy, there was also a sense of relief: without Andrei Arshavin, would Arsenal have faced another embarrassing loss? Especially disheartening is that although Liverpool dominated in the first half, their goal was not an absolute certainty but rather a gift from goalkeeper Manuel Almunia's mistake.

Before the match, Arsenal legend Ian Wright had predicted worriedly in his Sun column: "Almunia is a good second-choice goalkeeper, but he lacks the quality of a number one for a title-contending team. Occasionally, he looks completely out of his depth. Arsenal needs a top-class goalkeeper, but the problem is that such a keeper is hard to attract to the Emirates."

Almunia's deputy, Lukasz Fabianski, who participated in last season’s 4-4 draw when he returned from injury, said: "I hope to put as much pressure on Almunia as possible. What else can I do? Only train hard every time and show my abilities when I get the chance. I also hope Almunia will enjoy this competition."

But Almunia insisted: "It has been tough for me recently (sickness, injury, and the unexpected death of my mother-in-law in a car accident). I wasn't feeling great at the start of the season, and everything else made things even harder. Psychologically, I wasn’t fully recovered, but now my confidence is back. The old Manuel is back!" However, in the 9th minute, he directly kicked a goal kick out of play, drawing boos from the crowd.

In the 12th minute, Almunia did indeed catch Torres' one-on-one shot firmly. This wasn't surprising, as Torres, after returning from injury, clearly hadn't regained his match fitness. Upon receiving a brilliant pass from Gerrard, instead of rushing into the box to try and dribble past his compatriot, he attempted a long-range shot outside the box with insufficient power, making it easy for Almunia to save.

But Almunia's weakness lies in his tendency to make wrong decisions when the opposition plays high balls from set-pieces into the box. In the first half of this game, there were several instances of this: in the 23rd minute, Gerrard's corner kick into the box left Almunia somewhat bewildered; two minutes later, Gerrard sent another free-kick into the box, which Almunia merely punched away; in the 32nd minute, Gerrard's diagonal cross found its way into the box, and although Almunia got the ball, he immediately gave it away during the subsequent counterattack!

In the 41st minute, Aurelio's free-kick posed no real threat, but the advancing Almunia failed to secure the ball, instead hitting it directly to Kuyt on the edge of the area. The Dutchman, naturally, didn't hesitate to thank him with a swift long-range shot that found the net! In a challenging away fixture, the key is to avoid conceding first, but Almunia's amateurish error once again brought shame to the team.

Fairly speaking, Lucas might have been offside on this goal, and Arsène Wenger on the sideline expressed his anger over it. But as the BBC website pointed out: "Even if Almunia was barged by Lucas, he should still have done better. This is a goal that Almunia will want to forget. Those fans who are still chanting 'Almunia for England' can now take a break."

At halftime, the professor blew up in the dressing room. Vermaelen later said: "I've never seen the boss so angry, so in the second half, we all started to work harder." The considerate Belgian didn't mention whether Wenger's primary target of anger was Almunia, but many believe the Spanish goalkeeper must have received the full force of Wenger's wrath.

The second half saw a dramatic turnaround. Arsenal scored two goals within ten minutes to level the score, and Liverpool, unexpectedly slipping into a lull, couldn't test Almunia properly again, allowing the ticking bomb to temporarily fall silent. This game taught Arsenal a lesson: with Arshavin in the squad, they may not lack forwards, but sooner or later, they will need to buy a goalkeeper better than Almunia.
