Male infant suffered from a strange unconfirmed skin disease, Weibo users posted for help

by o6nihao8h on 2012-03-06 18:36:28

At the beginning of October, Ms. Chen took her child to the Zhujiang Hospital. At that time, the child's body was covered with grain-like rashes, red blisters, and occasional fever. On October 6th, due to an abnormal heart rate, the child was admitted to the pediatric intensive care unit. "Currently, the baby's skin is bright red, shedding in patches. After shedding one layer cleanly, another layer comes off after a few days. The attending doctor still doesn't know what kind of skin disease it is, and the dermatology department of the hospital can't figure out the cause either."

Multiple hospitals unable to diagnose the cause

On August 3rd of this year, the male infant Li Haoran was born into a rural family in Lizhi Village, Taiping Town, Luoding City. He weighed over six pounds at birth, dark and plump, very cute. "He was very active when he was born, but I didn't expect him to end up like this." Yesterday morning, in the pediatric ward of Zhujiang Hospital, the child's mother, Ms. Chen, couldn't help but shed tears as she talked about her child.

Information Times News (Reporter Xiong Xufan) "In Luoding, there is a 2-and-a-half-month-old baby suffering from a strange skin disease. His whole body is red with blisters, and large patches of his skin are peeling off. He also frequently has high fevers. Despite visiting numerous hospitals in Guangzhou, no cure has been found. Now we hope to use the power of Weibo (http://t.sina.com.cn) to find doctors who can help this unfortunate baby! Thank you!" The day before yesterday, an urgent post on Sina Weibo caught the attention of many netizens. Following the clues from the Weibo post, reporters located the child's parents yesterday. It was learned that the baby is currently hospitalized in the pediatric ICU of Zhujiang Hospital in Guangzhou, and the doctors have yet to confirm the cause of the illness. Anxious parents have decided to try every possible method to seek medical experts.

Enthusiastic netizens post on Weibo for help

Ms. Chen said that she had tried posting for help on some forums initially, but there was little response. However, after a netizen posted a plea for help on Weibo the day before yesterday, she received many condolences from netizens. Many netizens today forwarded the plea for help on Weibo.

Ms. Chen recalled that the child started getting sick 20 days after birth. "It started with diarrhea, lasting two or three days, with the buttocks always red. Then the neck turned red, and later even the corners of the eyes were a bit red." When Ms. Chen took the child to see the doctor, they said it was eczema, but the condition did not improve after medication. They then visited the Luoding Maternity and Child Health Hospital and Huigui Hospital, but the condition remained unresolved. Instead, the child's skin began to peel, and red rashes resembling heat pimples appeared on the face, legs, and back, spreading in patches.

The reporter contacted the Weibo user @Cyu3 who posted the message. She claimed that she didn't know the baby's mother personally but discovered the mother's hospital diary online, feeling sorry for the baby, so she posted on Weibo hoping to help them.

To treat the child, the couple, who were originally not wealthy, had already spent all their savings and borrowed a large sum from relatives and friends. "After seeking treatment for over a month, we've already spent tens of thousands of yuan. Now, daily hospitalization costs us two to three thousand yuan, and inviting expert consultations costs another one to two thousand yuan. We really don't know what to do," Ms. Chen said. She and her husband are temporarily staying at her sister's home in Guangzhou, relying on donations from friends to get by.

During their medical journey in Guangzhou, Ms. Chen and her husband also consulted Zhongshan First Hospital, Zhongshan Second Hospital, the Provincial Traditional Chinese Medicine Hospital, and the Municipal Dermatology and Venereal Disease Prevention and Treatment Institute, but the exact cause of the illness was never identified. The day before yesterday, they sent an invitation letter through Zhujiang Hospital to Zhongshan Second Hospital, asking relevant experts to come for consultation, "hoping to quickly determine what the disease is and start targeted treatment as soon as possible."

Suffered from a strange illness 20 days after birth

It was understood that starting from the afternoon of the day before yesterday, many people began calling and sending text messages to the child's parents, "Some just expressed concern, while others gave advice for reference, such as suggesting we find an old TCM doctor or recommending we go to certain hospitals. In any case, we will record all useful information." Ms. Chen said she was grateful to these netizens and hoped that the cause of her child's illness would be determined and treated as soon as possible, "No matter what, I will not give up on him. I believe there must be a doctor who can cure him."

On September 19th, Ms. Chen's family came to Guangzhou for medical treatment. However, both the Children's Hospital and Dongsheng Hospital were at a loss regarding the strange illness afflicting the baby. The diagnoses varied: some said it was a fungal infection like candidiasis, others suggested psoriasis, and some thought it was eczema.
