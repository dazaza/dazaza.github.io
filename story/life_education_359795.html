Study Abroad Handbook: Choose Overseas Institutions Based on College Entrance Exam Scores

http://www.sina.com.cn July 1, 2010, 16:31 Evening News

The results of the 2010 college entrance exam have been announced, and many students and parents are troubled by unsatisfactory scores. Should they choose to retake the exam after a year of review? Attend a regular domestic university? Or study abroad? Study abroad experts will use their rich experience to guide you.

College Entrance Exam Scores Above the First Tier University Admission Line:

These students mostly possess strong learning abilities, and overseas institutions have a high level of recognition for them. They have a broad range of choices for study abroad destination countries and also have a significant chance to apply for scholarships.

Study abroad experts suggest that students can apply to the top 50 universities in the United States, but they need to provide language test scores such as TOEFL or IELTS. In addition, they can choose well-known Canadian institutions like UBC, SFC, and the University of Toronto, or apply to British universities ranked between 10th and 20th. Students who score more than 50 points above the first tier line can apply to the National University of Singapore. If choosing to study in Japan, after some language study, there is hope to enter top Japanese institutions like Waseda University and the University of Tokyo.

Scores Above the Bachelor's Degree Admission Line:

Such students can choose to apply to universities ranked within the top 100 in the United States, the top 30 in the UK, and well-known institutions in Australia and Canada. Studying at a regular domestic undergraduate program still leads to a tough job market after graduation. If they can enter prestigious overseas institutions, they will not only receive a high-quality education but also greatly benefit in terms of career choice and employment prospects. As long as preparations are thorough, even if they cannot enter Fudan University or Jiaotong University domestically, there is still a chance to aim for renowned schools in the UK and the US. On the other hand, recent exchange rate changes have been very favorable for Chinese students studying abroad, with annual costs in the US potentially saving 50,000-100,000 RMB, in the UK around 50,000 RMB, and in Canada about 60,000 RMB. Moreover, these countries' higher education institutions offer generous scholarships and financial aid. If hoping to keep study abroad expenses under 100,000 RMB, one can apply to the ADIUT French University Institute of Technology Presidents Association project, which waives the French visa interview, and the degrees are mutually recognized by both countries.

Scores Below the Bachelor's Degree Admission Line:

Study Abroad Recommendation: American Community Colleges, Australian Fast Track Programs, etc.

Unsatisfactory college entrance exam results do not necessarily mean a student is not smart or lacks learning ability; it could be that China’s "generalist" education system and selection process are not suitable for them. The foreign higher education system is more flexible than the domestic one, allowing students to fully utilize their strengths and highlight their individuality and specialties. American community colleges are cost-effective (annual tuition ranging from $5,000-$8,000 USD), easy to apply to (no TOEFL or SAT scores required), have short application times, and flexible entry options. After completing two years of study, one can directly apply to transfer to a regular four-year university to continue for another two years to obtain a bachelor's degree.

The Australian IBT University Fast Track Program is also an excellent choice. Application does not require college entrance exam scores or language test results. IBT certificate courses and diploma courses are equivalent to first-year university courses, and upon completion, one can directly connect to second-year university courses, usually completing an undergraduate degree in three years.
