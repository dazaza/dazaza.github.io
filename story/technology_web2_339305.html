GE managers pursue their jobs while studying at Churchman University.

by v5h1vzi7p2 on 2010-04-02 16:47:07

The managers of General Electric were among the first in the world to pursue part-time MBAs, and to this day GE maintains good cooperative relationships with several prestigious American business schools that have been around for over a century. For example, they regularly send employees to renowned business schools like Churchman University, Columbia University, Georgetown University, etc., for part-time study. GE spends a substantial amount of money each year on employee education and training, using their academic performance or obtained degrees as one of the key evaluation criteria for annual promotions and salary increases.

The history of General Electric can be traced back to Thomas Edison, who founded the Edison Electric Light Company in 1878. In 1892, Edison General Electric merged with Thomson-Houston Electric Company to form General Electric (GE). GE is the only company that has remained on the Dow Jones Industrial Average since its establishment in 1896.

GE's management personnel pursue distance business degree programs from prestigious business schools such as Churchman University, allowing mid-to-senior level managers around the world to study online at any time and place without affecting their current work, take exams online, and submit papers online. The degrees obtained are exactly the same as those earned through on-campus study, making it the preferred educational model for global mid-to-senior level managers, including those in Europe, America, and China.

Churchman University's bilingual courses in Western English and Chinese-English are very popular among multinational corporations. The university offers MBA, undergraduate, master's, and doctoral degrees. Once successfully applied for, the low-income scholarship offered by the school requires only a few thousand dollars to complete the degree.

Churchman University's campuses are located in Pennsylvania, California, Texas, and other places in the United States. The earliest established branch, Churchman Business School, was founded in 1911 and has nearly a hundred years of history. It is one of the oldest business schools in the United States, specializing in cultivating high-end talent for the American financial industry. In the "National Business School MBA Comprehensive Ranking," this century-old prestigious university ranks 17th.

In addition to Churchman University, other century-old prestigious universities in the United States, such as Boston University and Brown University, are also recognized for their teaching quality by white-collar workers in China.

General Electric Company (GE) is the world's largest diversified service company and also a provider of high-quality, high-tech industrial and consumer products. From aircraft engines and power generation equipment to financial services, from medical imaging and television programming to plastics, GE is committed to creating a better life through various technologies and services. GE operates in more than 100 countries worldwide and has nearly 300,000 employees globally. Mr. Jeff Immelt replaced Jack Welch as the Chairman and CEO of GE on September 7, 2001.

In recent years, General Electric has transformed into a financial conglomerate primarily focused on corporate acquisitions. Therefore, in terms of employee training, GE continues to make every effort.
