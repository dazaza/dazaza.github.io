Sina Technology News, January 18 evening (Beijing Time)

by qiaodie7236 on 2012-02-25 21:48:35

An analyst mentioned that although Samsung might be interested in acquiring RIM, the hardware of BlackBerry would not be a correct choice, which is in line with Samsung's strategy of weakening and reinforcing functions.

Shared to: Related reports: Samsung expressed no interest in acquiring RIM; RIM plans to launch two BlackBerry PlayBook tablet computers within the year; rumors of Samsung's intention to acquire caused RIM's stock price to plummet nearly 7%; Motorola, RIM, etc., emulate Apple.

The message stated: "We have our own platform, so RIM is an interesting option, and we maintain a flexible attitude towards any event." Based on historical experience, Samsung mainly relies on its strength to expand business. In recent years, it has not carried out small acquisitions. In 2008, Samsung abandoned the acquisition of chip manufacturer SanDisk. Later, Samsung took an open stance on mergers and acquisitions, mainly to strengthen its software business to compete with Apple and Google.

Product delays and profit erosion have severely impacted RIM's confidence. Despite this, its valuation still reaches $9 billion. A message from a major Asian mobile phone manufacturer said that RIM remains highly attractive to Asian mobile phone manufacturers such as LG, HTC, and ZTE because these manufacturers currently do not have their own mobile platforms.

In comparison, Samsung already has its own mobile platform Bada. Samsung spokesperson James Chung said on Wednesday: "We have never considered acquiring RIM, and we are not interested in it." The spokesperson also said that RIM did not actively contact Samsung to discuss selling matters, and Samsung has no interest in RIM's mobile platform.

Investment bank Jefferies analyst Peter Misek said: "RIM may license the BlackBerry 10 operating system, charging $10 per device. Samsung and HTC could become licensing targets, so they do not have to rely solely on the Android platform, thereby achieving personalization."

Tech news reported that according to sources from an Asian mobile phone manufacturer, despite Samsung's lack of interest in acquiring RIM, Asian mobile phone manufacturers such as LG, HTC, and ZTE might be interested in RIM. Analysts believe that RIM could reach a deal within three months.

Misek believed that RIM has the potential to reach an agreement within three months. Appointing a new chairman could accelerate this process. RIM currently adopts a non-co-CEO structure, where the two co-CEOs, Mike Lazaridis and Jim Balsillie, are also co-chairmen of the board, a structure that has been criticized by investors.

Later, there were reports that RIM had rejected offers from Amazon and other potential buyers, as RIM still hoped to resolve issues through its own efforts.

Subsequently, there were reports that RIM planned to sell the company to Samsung. Stimulated by this news, RIM's stock price surged by more than 10%. However, Samsung denied it on Wednesday, causing RIM's stock price to retreat again.
