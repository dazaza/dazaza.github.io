Apple's Latest Earnings Report: Decline in Sales in China

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 16:30:58

July 24th news: Apple today released its third quarter financial report for the fiscal year 2013, ending on June 29th. The report shows that in Q3 of fiscal year 2013, Apple's revenue was $35.3 billion, slightly higher than last year's $35 billion; net profit was $6.9 billion, down 22% year-on-year. Although these figures were slightly higher than analysts' expectations, for Apple CEO Tim Cook, the biggest challenge is the decline in revenue in multiple markets including China, both sequentially and year-on-year.

Sales Decline in China May Be Affected by the 3.15 Scandal

Although Apple's financial data this quarter was slightly better than analysts' previous expectations, for Apple CEO Tim Cook, the declining trend in core markets like China has become an issue that must be addressed. For a long time, Apple's sales in China maintained significant growth. During the last earnings call, Cook even pointed out separately that Apple had its best quarter ever in China. However, this quarter Apple's development in China was not ideal, with revenue declining both year-on-year and sequentially, and iPhone sales dropping by 4% year-on-year.

Cook stated that despite weak iPhone sales in China, it remains a huge opportunity and he would not be discouraged by 90 days of sales data. Notably, this quarter's earnings report came exactly three months after the 3.15 Evening Gala in 2013. According to reports from CCTV's 3.15 Evening Gala team, Apple violated the "Regulations on Repairing, Returning, and Replacing Mobile Phone Goods" and the service standards for several products breached the national three guarantees policy.

Growing Sales of iPhone 4 May Boost the Launch of a Cheaper Version of iPhone

In this earnings call, Apple disclosed several figures about the iPhone. The average selling price (ASP) of Apple phones dropped from $624 a year ago to $580, causing a decrease in profit. This also resulted in iPhone sales exceeding analyst expectations by 5 million units, reaching 31.2 million units. Apple officials said that the drop in ASP was partly due to the iPhone 4. This means that the iPhone 4 essentially played the role of a cheaper version of the iPhone within the product line, competing with mid-to-low-end smartphones.

In this earnings call, Cook specifically pointed out that iPhone sales in India grew by 200%, which was due to recent strategies such as installment payments for the iPhone 4 and iPhone 4S. Clearly, the strong sales of the iPhone 4 in low-income regions increased the possibility of launching a cheaper version of the iPhone. Currently, product images of the cheaper version of the iPhone have already flooded every corner of the internet, but Apple has neither confirmed nor denied related rumors so far.

Still Dominating the U.S. Market, Samsung Struggles to Break Into Apple's Home Turf

According to the data disclosed by Apple, Apple's overall revenue performance in the third quarter was unsatisfactory. Revenue from the Americas reached $14.4 billion, increasing 3% sequentially and 12% year-on-year. Revenue from the Japanese market was $2.54 billion, decreasing 19% sequentially but increasing 27% year-on-year. Outside the U.S. and Japan markets, including China and Europe, revenue declined both year-on-year and sequentially.

Despite overall poor performance, for Apple, achieving continuous revenue growth in its home turf, the U.S. market, is a good thing. This suggests that Samsung's surpassing of Apple's monthly sales in May this year was merely a rare occurrence. As Samsung Galaxy sales weaken globally due to aesthetic fatigue, Apple's position in its domestic market will become more solid. According to data published at the end of last month by the market research company comScore, from March to May this year, Apple held a 38.9% market share in the U.S., far ahead of Samsung's 23% market share.

Poor iPad Sales Confirm Issues with Last Year’s Product Iteration Schedule

According to the financial report, Apple sold 14.6 million iPads in the third quarter, lower than last year's 17 million units. Apple's previous forecast was between 16 million to 17 million iPads. Regarding the iPad sales issue, Cook explained that it was related to channel inventory and reiterated that Apple launched three versions of the iPad in the third quarter last year.

This explanation once again confirms that there were issues with Apple's product iteration planning for the iPad last year. In 2012, Apple for the first time released three similar products within one year. Overseas media SlashGear believed at the time that the update of iPad 4 should have waited until early 2013 to align with Apple's annual product update cycle. TechCrunch, on the other hand, thought the improvements from the third generation to the fourth generation were not significant enough. Clearly, the explosive sales of the iPad mini could not offset the decline in sales of the traditional-sized iPads, and the iPad 4 was not a product designed to drive sales.
