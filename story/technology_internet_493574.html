Thus, in a career of fast-paced times

by wenjlblove on 2011-09-22 19:08:17

I should focus on studying now; diving into it is a way to break free from emptiness. Even if I immerse myself in something wrong, it’s still better than sitting around and wasting my life. I’m grateful for that passage by Muctong.

Then, Yongbin, my high school desk mate, also abandoned his studies to join me, followed by Hailong (Hailong was someone I painted with, someone I personally feel close and familiar with. It's really hard to express this feeling here. It seems like I know, and you might know too, but of course I know you don’t. Ha ha, here I realize I’ve laughed heartily for the third time, even if it only appears in my heart. I guess the gloomy mood of the morning has already been relieved and vented through these words, but I still have to continue). The three of us worked together for a while. I omitted two romantic relationships in between, as I think those stories can be told separately and written independently. Those days when we worked hard and tried our best to make money always felt fulfilling yet boring. In youth, we always thought about how to surpass others. Last April, we started our own business. Without discussion, without talking to our parents, without preparation—no, we did have courage and boundless enthusiasm!

Last night, I read a novel until 12 o'clock, until I felt sleepy. Because I fear insomnia, I always end up thinking about things that make me feel helpless, make me hesitate, or things that once hurt me before I fall asleep. So I think books are a kind of numbness, a mental hypnotism. A long time ago, I planned to quit reading books, at least those third-rate novels. Masterpieces may carry profound meanings and inspire deep reflection, but they always lack the courage to pick up, desiring something fictional inside. So I never quit, just like the cigarette in my father's hand. Perhaps what he smokes is just loneliness... Now many such words appear in the mouths of young people: emptiness, loneliness, boredom, confusion. A teacher's words this morning explained the reasons behind these words.

It happens that I'm feeling this way today, mixed with self-reproach and helplessness.

This morning, I came to work at the company and posted on the blog for the first time. You might find my experiences confusing, but I think it's necessary to analyze a person and feel an event behind the experiences. (Perhaps someone who shares similar interests will see my words, ha ha.) I had a blurred childhood, with only the memory of the two ducks I raised by hand left in my mind; I had a colorful youth, where there were my beloved friends and a beautiful first love. But I feel that the beginning of my life was after I graduated from high school. I spent most of my firsts in life, including my virginity, ha ha. After graduation, due to my own grades and family circumstances, I didn't continue to go to college. Because I had studied art, I don’t know whose suggestion it was, but I went to study graphic design. Regarding this experience, I seem to have bragged to many people. It was some hard-earned honor, going from a student who knew nothing to becoming their teacher. This process is still worth being proud of now, but it's no longer something worth boasting about. Afterwards, I started working at an advertising company, constantly changing jobs because I felt there was nothing left for me to learn in the previous company. Actually, salary was the main reason. Every time I changed jobs, there was a significant increase in my salary. There’s no one who doesn’t care about money.

Quote: Thus, in the fast-paced era of life, people become restless. They merely admire idols without being willing to work hard or settle down to do the tasks at hand. As a result, disappointment, emptiness, and boredom dominate their mental world. They rise early and retire late, wasting time and youth. Worse still, some have already lost the courage to face themselves. —Muctong

Looking back now, I was indeed young. Although the company has been operating for more than a year, there hasn't been any outstanding performance. One of us has already left, and we've all been decisively abandoned by our girlfriends. We are still struggling to hold on. Someone said that falling down means moving forward physically. Facing the survival of our own company, I really don't know what to do, I can only try my best and explore slowly. I'm not afraid of hardship, but I'm afraid of being like a bull that has lost its direction, charging ahead without knowing what's ahead—it could be a wall, or it could be a ray of light. But I have no way back, nor have I left myself any other options. Recently, business has been bad, and my heart has become restless and uneasy. I don't know how to change it. For business, the first thing that comes to mind is promotion, so I plan to learn website design, and we'll promote ourselves. We've already bought a domain name. I always like to leave myself no way out when doing things, and after buying it, I always have to make it work. Wasting money is, to me, a waste of life.
