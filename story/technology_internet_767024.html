During the National Day holiday, 360 may attack Sogou again, targeting mobile input methods.

by anonymous on 2013-10-02 17:14:31

The seven-day National Day holiday is approaching, but for the search market, these seven days will definitely not be peaceful.

According to multiple sources, 360 will launch a second round of attacks on Sogou during the National Day holiday, mainly targeting Sogou's mobile input method, and may also affect Sogou's ally - Tencent's mobile browser.

"This is a carefully planned three-step plan. The first step was to attack Sogou during the Mid-Autumn Festival, the second step was to attack hardware manufacturers like Xiaomi. The effects of the first two steps were good, and the third step is to attack Sogou on mobile devices during the National Day holiday. 360 has already mobilized internally." A source said. She has a relative whose work is related to this, who mentioned that their National Day travel plans have been affected by work commitments.

Another Attack on Sogou

The source said that 360's main target during the National Day holiday will be Sogou's mobile input method, mainly to increase its own share in the mobile market. This is 360's recent central task.

For 360, the importance of mobile entry points goes without saying. One of Sogou's important assets is its input method, which stands out on mobile devices. To defeat an opponent, one must first eliminate their advantage; taking down Sogou's input method would severely damage Sogou.

360 has quietly acquired an input method team, which can take over once Sogou is weakened.

Sogou's model is the three-stage rocket: input method-browser-search engine. Sogou's input method is very popular with users and has a high market share. The input method brings traffic to the browser, the browser brings traffic to the search engine, and monetization occurs through the search engine.

Another source said that since 360 is a low-level software, it has extremely powerful capabilities. Once it launches an attack, Sogou's mobile input method will be defenseless.

360's main weapons on mobile devices are 360 Mobile Security Guard and 360 Mobile Assistant. 360 has put a lot of effort into making these softwares deeper as low-level software, giving them strong capabilities on mobile devices. Many users have installed its one-click Root function, giving even more control over the phone. The former is equivalent to a locksmith who can easily open your door, while the latter is equivalent to handing over your key to the security guard. Therefore, it is easy for 360 to eliminate ordinary mobile applications. Sogou's mobile input method is actually in a dangerous position.

This source also said that to increase the market share of 360 Mobile Assistant, some very effective strategies have been employed, such as many people having 360 Security Guard or other antivirus software installed on their computers. As long as users connect their phones to the computer for charging, 360 Mobile Assistant can be quietly installed directly onto the phone.

He also said that because Tencent has strategically invested in Sogou, this time Tencent's mobile browser is also likely to be attacked.

Hype around Mobile Assistant

On the eve of the National Day holiday, on September 27th, 360 just launched an attack on several smartphone manufacturers, allowing users to root their phones with one click and block pre-installed software from phone manufacturers. Subsequently, Xiaomi and Lenovo angrily announced that they would remove 360 software from their app stores.

An industry insider said that on the surface, 360 seems very passive, but in fact, 360 is quite satisfied with the results of this action. Internally at 360, this is considered a very successful operation because it successfully hyped up 360 Mobile Assistant. This is typical "war marketing," with only one goal: to make 360 Mobile Assistant famous.

He said that outsiders might think 360 suffered a loss this time and won't act again during the National Day holiday, but the truth is exactly the opposite. 360 is preparing to take advantage of this external sentiment and launch another disruption during the National Day holiday.

This action by 360 has raised the awareness of other internet companies, such as Baidu, Xiaomi, and Tencent. Some media reports suggest that 360 has been surrounded and beaten. However, insiders say that such large-scale PR battles are nothing new to 360. As long as there is no technical deterrent against 360, it doesn't really matter to them.

Holiday Offensive

Some people may wonder why 360 always launches attacks during holidays. On the second day of this year's Mid-Autumn Festival holiday (September 20), after failing to acquire Sogou, 360 attacked Sogou Browser. 360 Security Guard modified the default settings of users' browsers, so when users opened Sogou Browser, they were actually opening 360's own browser or IE Browser.

Industry insiders explained that this is because holiday attacks are more covert and allow for hit-and-run tactics. Notaries do not work during holidays and evenings, which increases the difficulty for Sogou to gather evidence.

360 has always tried to acquire Sogou. In the search market, Baidu's market share is about 63%, far ahead, while 360's is around 18%, and Sogou's share is 10%, placing it third. If 360 successfully acquires Sogou, its market share will rise to 28%. At that point, it would have a significant chance to challenge Baidu.

But just when 360 thought acquiring Sogou was a done deal, suddenly a competitor emerged. On September 16th, Tencent quickly invested in Sogou, merging Soso with Sogou into the new Sogou. Soso's market share is around 4%, bringing the new Sogou's share to 14%. 360 is now squeezed between Baidu and Sogou. During the Mid-Autumn Festival, 360 launched a surprise attack on Sogou, both to vent frustration and to weaken its competitor.

On the evening of September 25th, Sogou formally sued Qihoo 360 for unfair competition at the Xi'an Intermediate Court, demanding 45.5 million yuan in compensation. Previously, 360 had engaged in 13 lawsuits with Tencent and Baidu among others, losing all 13 cases. However, the penalties were minor and had no adverse effect on 360.

Industry insiders believe that 360 Security Guard is a low-level software, akin to a nuclear weapon. The only way for other companies to restrain 360 is to possess a similar nuclear weapon capable of taking down 360. Otherwise, there is no other way.
