2012 Indoor Air May Harm Kidney Function

by lovew2t7 on 2012-03-09 12:08:15

Many diseases are influenced by environmental factors. For example, the prevalence of pollen allergies occurs during the season of spring blossoms, while the terrifying disease malaria is related to rainfall. As for the more familiar influenza, it begins to spread widely due to a drop in temperature. In fact, the relationship between houses and human health is even closer than the previously mentioned environmental changes. As early as 40 years ago, the medical field had already proven that bad housing conditions could potentially lead to the occurrence of cancer. At the time, foreign research discovered that chimney sweeps were very likely to develop bladder cancer. It turns out that coal tar in soot is a carcinogen. Since at that time, cleaning chimneys was done by chimney companies and ordinary people wouldn't clean their own chimneys, these workers became the biggest victims.

From the studies conducted 40 years ago up to recent times, concerning the connection between houses and diseases, there have been worrying phenomena. Let's take nephrology diseases—focal segmental glomerulosclerosis (FSGS) as an example. Glomerulosclerosis is a type of glomerulonephritis, which mainly presents symptoms such as proteinuria, anemia, and renal function decline. Patients will experience edema, foamy urine, dizziness and blurred vision caused by anemia, dark yellow skin color, nausea, and vomiting. This often leads to uremia. About 20 years ago, this could be considered a rather rare pathological report, but now the global case reports of this disease have increased from 2.8% to 20.2%, which is not something to be taken lightly.

In the past, we didn't fully understand the reasons behind the increase in this type of nephritis. Some even suspected whether there was any mutation in genes that led to such a rapid increase in this disease. In fact, modern research has proven that human genes are quite stable. Humans have existed on Earth for 4 million years, yet the genetic similarity between modern humans and humans from 4 million years ago remains as high as 99%. Therefore, it's impossible for any gene to cause a certain disease to increase nearly 10 times within just 20 years. The only explanation lies in environmental changes.

In fact, recent clinical research has found that many cases of focal segmental glomerulosclerosis are related to exposure to total volatile organic compounds in the air. Volatile organic compounds include carcinogens such as formaldehyde, benzene, xylene, and styrene. In the last few decades, we have begun to extensively use various composite boards, adhesives, paints, carpets, plastic windows, wallpapers, etc., which contain volatile organic compounds (TVOCs), to decorate our houses. Thus, indoor volatile organic compounds have increased significantly. Behaviors like dry cleaning clothes also frequently expose us to toxic volatile organic compounds.

Therefore, I deeply feel that one of the most important ways to avoid getting this kidney disease is to prevent indoor environmental toxicity.
