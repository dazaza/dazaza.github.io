Chinese Tourists Boost Japan's Tourism Development

According to the Chinese Guide in Japan website, under the impact of the financial crisis and the new influenza, Japan's tourism market, like most countries around the world, is undergoing a severe test. However, according to recent figures released by some regions in Japan, the number of foreign tourists visiting these areas last year reached new highs, with Chinese tourists leading the way. In some regions, the growth rate has exceeded 50%, making the Chinese tourism market naturally the battleground for Japan's tourism industry. Many regional governments and tourism departments have already adjusted or are adjusting their strategic direction, focusing on China as the main promotional market. Chinese tourists will become an important driving force for supporting and promoting the development of Japan's tourism economy.

According to the figures released by Wakayama Prefecture, the number of tourists visiting Wakayama Prefecture in 2008 was 31.343 million people, a decrease of 2.3% compared to the previous year. Among them, the number of overnight guests decreased by 2%, about 5.455 million people, while the number of day-trip visitors decreased by 2.4%, approximately 25.888 million people. However, the number of foreign overnight guests increased by 168,351 people, setting a historical high. Notably, the number of Chinese tourists increased significantly by 52%.

Meanwhile, on May 12, the Kyoto Municipal Government announced that the number of tourists visiting the city last year reached 50.21 million people, breaking historical records for eight consecutive years and achieving the goal of attracting 50 million tourists set in 2000. Among them, the number of foreign overnight guests increased by 1.1%, approximately 940,000 people. The city analyzed that measures such as organizing school trips and lighting up scenic spots at night achieved good results. At the same time, the "Japan fever" centered on Europe and America and the steady increase in the number of Chinese tourists were also key factors.

Due to the impact of the global financial crisis, many countries' economies have been severely hit. Especially those countries with fewer resources, smaller areas, and heavy reliance on foreign trade are struggling. Japan is one of them. To develop its economy, Japan has stepped up its publicity efforts in tourism, striving to revitalize Japan's economy. Meanwhile, China, less affected by the financial crisis, saw many citizens not even noticing the impact of the crisis. People continued to travel as usual. Due to the outbreak of swine flu in Europe and political instability in Thailand, traveling to Japan, South Korea, and Singapore became fashionable.
