Hefei disfigured girl went to Beijing yesterday to receive treatment; the hospital has made an initial plan

by lovei0c1 on 2012-03-06 13:52:59

■ Xiao Zhou boarded the train to Beijing

After the media reported on the disfigurement of Xiao Zhou, a teenage girl from Hefei, it drew widespread attention from all sectors of society. Many plastic surgery hospitals across the country extended their helping hands to Xiao Zhou, offering free treatment. On March 5th, after careful consideration, the Zhou family decided to take Xiao Zhou to Beijing for rehabilitation treatment [http://news.sina.com.cn/s/2012-03-06/024924064226.shtml]. At noon yesterday at 12:00, accompanied by her family, Xiao Zhou left home, traveled by ambulance from Hefei to Nanjing, and then took the high-speed train departing at 16:41 to Beijing for treatment of infection control and scar hyperplasia.

■ Worsening condition

Decision to seek treatment in Beijing

It was learned that considering the relatively advanced medical facilities and medical level in Beijing, the Zhou family accepted the medical assistance from Jiangsu Satellite TV's "Dream Comes True" program and prepared to go to Beijing for treatment. The program's producer introduced that they originally planned to go to Beijing on March 4th. However, Xiao Zhou's condition began to worsen, making her unsuitable for long-distance travel, so the plan was put on hold. Yesterday, Xiao Zhou's parents finally decided to accompany her to Beijing. Given her condition, which made air travel unsuitable, they decided to take the three-hour high-speed train to Beijing.

■ Excitement the night before departure

The disfigured teenager could hardly contain her excitement

At 9:30 am on March 5th, Xiao Zhou was resting on the sofa at home. Xiao Zhou’s mother (Weibo)[http://weibo.com/u/2629306111?zw=news] said that Xiao Zhou knew she was going to Beijing for treatment and was excited all night, “She didn’t sleep until around two or three o’clock this morning.”

"We will first take an ambulance to Nanjing Railway Station and then take the afternoon high-speed train to Beijing," Xiao Zhou's mother said. In recent days, due to frequent contact with outsiders, Xiao Zhou's wounds had become infected. To prevent further infection during the journey from excessive contact with people, the railway department arranged an entire carriage for Xiao Zhou and her family to rest in.

■ Bumpy journey

One hour of protective work

From Hefei to Beijing, the road was bumpy, and since Xiao Zhou suffered extensive burns, even lying still at home caused her pain. How to safely reach Beijing while reducing physical suffering became a major concern for Xiao Zhou's mother these past few days.

At around 11:00 am yesterday, medical personnel from Nanjing's emergency services and a local hospital came to Xiao Zhou's house and spent nearly an hour implementing safety precautions. The doctor said, "First, we apply disinfectant to the wound, then a layer of pain-relief ointment. The burn areas will be wrapped in gauze, preventing infection during the trip and reducing pain caused by bumps."

■ Neighbors come spontaneously

To see off the young girl

At around 12:00 pm yesterday, supported by nurses and her mother, Xiao Zhou walked out of her home. Hearing that Xiao Zhou was going to Beijing for treatment, neighbors who had gathered spontaneously gave her a warm round of applause. An elderly man in his seventies or eighties approached Xiao Zhou and encouraged her, saying, "Girl, cooperate well with the hospital in Beijing. Don't be afraid; be strong. You will get better."

It was learned that this was the first time Xiao Zhou stepped out of her home since February 24th when she went out for injury assessment. Xiao Zhou said, "When I return, I want to be a completely new person."

■ Hospital has formulated

A preliminary medical plan

It was learned that Jiangsu Satellite TV (Weibo)[http://weibo.com/jsbcjstv?zw=news] established a rescue special action team for Xiao Zhou's trip to Beijing, preparing ambulances, doctors, stretchers, and other necessary medical equipment. Before leaving, the doctors from the ambulance coming from Nanjing were treating Zhou Yan, who endured the pain with tears held back.

Beijing Yimei Hospital promised to provide free medical assistance to Xiao Zhou and would arrange a double room for her, solving the accommodation problem for Zhou Yan's parents. The hospital specially formed an expert group, which would organize the first consultation as soon as Xiao Zhou arrived in Beijing. They have already given the treatment steps as follows: first eliminate unstable scars on the skin surface, then assess functional loss, and ultimately develop a comprehensive treatment plan based on the situation.

Mr. Liu from Beijing Yimei Plastic Surgery Hospital stated that, under the current circumstances, the possibility of complete recovery is small. Burn injuries are a long-term treatment process, possibly lasting two years or even requiring lifelong treatment.
