Obama's three sentences, all about eating

by alexsgxu on 2009-11-30 23:53:49

1. The successful people I admire most not only think about themselves, but they also think about things beyond themselves. They want to make a contribution to the world; they hope to contribute to their country and city. Beyond impacting their own lives, they wish to influence others' lives as well.

2. Those who truly leave their mark in history do so because they have greater aspirations - like figuring out how to help more people have enough food to eat, ensuring more children receive education, or resolving conflicts in peaceful ways, etc.

3. Only these kinds of people can make contributions to the world, and I believe that if all of you here present work hard, you too can make such contributions.

The above three sentences were mentioned by U.S. President Obama during his dialogue with Chinese youth. Phrases like "hope to influence others' lives while impacting their own," "those who truly leave their mark in history do so because they have greater aspirations - like figuring out how to help more people have enough food to eat..." and "I believe that if all of you here present work hard, you too can make such contributions" reflect Obama's passionate words encouraging Chinese youth to contribute more to the people, the nation, and the world. Fundamentally, it is still about solving the problem of feeding the people. Therefore, these three sentences from Obama are all closely related to eating.

These three sentences spoken by Obama are not just spur-of-the-moment inspirations, but should be words coming from deep within him. According to reports, the number of hungry people globally has reached 1.02 billion, the highest peak since 1970. As the largest developed country in the world, the number of hungry families in the United States is not decreasing but rather increasing. A recent annual survey report released by the U.S. Department of Agriculture shows that about 49 million people across the country, or one in every seven households, are "struggling due to lack of food." The situation for American children is even worse, with one in every four children "going hungry due to lack of food." The report also points out that approximately 17.3 million people face food shortages. Under such tense circumstances, Obama voiced this warning, saying "this advice has been very useful to me," which is not only encouragement for Chinese youth but also a self-reminder, as Obama promised during his campaign to eradicate child hunger in America by 2015.

Food is fundamental to people’s survival. Regardless of the country, basic sustenance remains the most essential need for ordinary people. Food issues are the root cause of all contradictions, and only when the food issue is resolved can development be discussed. Compared to the past, the lives of our farmers have greatly improved, achieving leaps from poverty to subsistence, and from subsistence to a moderately prosperous life. Even though our living standards have improved now, we must maintain a sense of vigilance and not let this blind us. We need to see that currently, most Chinese households are still at the level of semi-subsistence or subsistence, and some families even struggle to afford meals. Therefore, solving the food and clothing problems for our people and the global population is a long and arduous task. Especially in an era where the world is entering a "food crisis," it is particularly important to pay high attention.

According to surveys, the amount of food wasted by humans each year is shocking. In the UK alone, about 7 million tons of food are wasted annually, which is heart-wrenching. In a certain sense, waste is also a form of "crime." Waste leads to pollution; if we could avoid wasting this food, not only would the carbon dioxide emissions in the atmosphere be significantly reduced, but more importantly, it wouldn't lead to some people going hungry or even starving to death. Since its establishment, GreenTree Inn has always focused on resource conservation and environmental protection. Therefore, I call on everyone here to establish a sense of thrift, recognizing that "every grain is hard-earned," and consciously resist extravagance and waste. Moreover, we should lend a helping hand and take practical actions to contribute to the increasingly severe global food crisis.

This is the enlightenment I gained from President Obama's three sentences.
