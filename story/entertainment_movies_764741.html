The baseball team members of Futakotamagawa Academy have all moved up to the third year, and their team advisor, Mr. Kawafuji, has returned to the academy. In the spring of 2009, with the arrival of a new semester, two new players joined the baseball team. One is Akahoshi Shoichi, who was already famous in junior high school. The other is Hamanaka Taiyo, who mistakenly believed that he had joined the baseball team to follow his idol, but later learned otherwise. The original expectation was that the team would be more energetic with these new additions; however, Akahoshi, proud and confident, thought his goal was professional leagues, so practicing high school baseball seemed unnecessary and unenthusiastic. Meanwhile, after understanding the real character of his idol, Hamanaka became disillusioned and started causing trouble. To protect Akahoshi from delinquent students, Mikoshiba suffered a leg fracture and will definitely not be able to participate in the summer regional preliminaries. Everyone at Mikoshiba's bedside vowed to make it to the national tournament, and their passion infected Akahoshi and Hamanaka.

Futakotamagawa Academy smoothly passed the first and second rounds and performed well in the semifinals. In the final, they faced off against Sasano High School, whose ace pitcher, Kawakami Sadaharu, was the only one who had defeated Anijima in junior high school. Facing the clever strategies and Kawakami's strong abilities, Futakotamagawa Academy struggled. Suddenly, Wakana met with an accident.

Will Futakotamagawa Academy enter the dream Koshien?

Behind the Scenes

The Last Fiery Youth of Baseball Boys

"Rookies" (also known as "Coach for Life") is a long-running manga written by Maho Morita, serialized from 1998 to 2003 in "Weekly Shonen Jump". In 2008, TBS turned it into a TV series broadcast in the spring. This baseball campus drama, led by Ryota Sato and a group of young and unique actors, did not initially perform well in terms of ratings, but as the story developed, its popularity gradually increased, with the final episode reaching a rating of 19.5%, and an average rating of 15%.

The TV series, themed around chasing dreams, maintained a strong style of sports-themed shonen manga. The dramatic transformation of delinquent students, passionate and emotional lines, and intense battle scenes became the biggest features and highlights of the show. Before the TV series finished airing, TBS began considering making a sequel - not a new season of the series, but a movie version as the grand finale. In September 2008, while the TV series was still popular, the script for the movie version began production.

Producer Akira Ishimaru hoped the movie would depict the graduation of the baseball team members, concluding the entire story with everyone leaving Mr. Kawafuji and going their separate ways. Thus, the movie was named "Rookies: Graduation," with an original main storyline, telling the story of the protagonists' efforts to reach Koshien before graduation. At the same time, many famous scenes from the manga were incorporated, creating a magnificent final story.

Reunion of the Futakotamagawa Academy Baseball Team!

The movie continued with the cast and crew from the TV series. Directed by Yuichiro Hirakawa and scripted by Kiyohiro Izumi, all the main actors including Ryota Sato, Hayato Ichihara, Keisuke Kobushi, Yu Takada, Mitsunori Nakao, Akihiro Takagaki, Kentaro Tokugawa, Takeshi Sato, Shunsuke Igarashi, Yosuke Kawamura, Hiroshi Onoue, and special guest appearance by Haruka Ayase, participated.

In addition, the movie version introduced two new members to the Futakotamagawa Academy baseball team: Akahoshi Shoichi and Hamanaka Taiyo, played by the then-popular Yudai Yamamoto and Tatsuya Ishida. Producer Ishimaru revealed, "We believe that if Yudai Yamamoto plays Akahoshi, the movie will surely succeed. Tatsuya Ishida originally had a full schedule, but he made time to participate in this film."

The old members of the baseball team had already formed deep friendships during the filming of the TV series, and the two new members, similar in age, quickly integrated into this big family. Not only did they shoot together, but they also often ate and chatted during their free time. The camaraderie portrayed in the film was three parts acting and seven parts genuine emotion. The actors shed tears of excitement while filming the final climactic scene.

Fun Facts:

- Ryota Sato was once a fan of Maho Morita, and he really liked Morita's first long manga, "Iron Fist vs Steel Fist." Later, when the new series "Rookies" began, Sato was in his senior year of high school, preparing to enroll in the Art Department of Nihon University to become an actor. At that time, he hoped to play the role of Mr. Kawafuji in the future film adaptation of "Rookies," which eventually came true.

- Filming took place from early November to mid-December 2008. The short days and cold rain of winter posed significant challenges for the production team. Staff often prepared before dawn, and the actors rehearsed as the sun rose. Winter rain occasionally disrupted schedules but brought good effects, such as the scene where the baseball team trained dragging tires in the rain, which was filmed in real rain without artificial rain, adding a sense of realism.

- The production team rented out an abandoned high school, renovating it to resemble Futakotamagawa Academy High School for filming. Fans often waited outside the gates marked "No Trespassing" hoping to catch a glimpse of their idols.

- The last scene of the movie shows the baseball team expressing their feelings to Mr. Kawafuji on the school playground, a touching moment. Each person's lines were carefully discussed and determined by the actors themselves and the producers. On the day of filming, five cameras were used simultaneously, capturing the entire scene in uninterrupted long takes, recording the heartfelt words of the students and Mr. Kawafuji's moved expression.

- The final regional championship was filmed at the Meiji Shrine Outer Garden Stadium. The audience consisted of recruited extras. Despite the cold winter, since the scene required summer clothing, everyone removed their thick coats and wore summer outfits to cheer on the players in the cold.
