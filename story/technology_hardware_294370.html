Coal Sector: The King Returns - Stock Knowledge

by fj0953210 on 2009-12-05 21:36:57

Based on our observations of the market this week, we believe that the trend of the market is transforming in the way we predicted last week when the market began to crash. The appeal of small and medium-sized cap stocks to investors will decline in the future, while large-cap blue-chip stocks will gradually gain favor among investors. The Shenzhen Stock Exchange's action against the bubble in the Growth Enterprise Market (GEM) on Friday serves as a strong warning for the current bubble in small and medium-sized cap stocks, which may push these high-valued small and medium-sized cap stocks into a consolidation period. On the other hand, the several intraday rallies of the large-cap blue-chip stocks this week have sent a signal of activation to the market. Especially the roller-coaster trend on Friday leads us to believe that the main force institutions are taking advantage of the external force of the management's move to prick the GEM bubble to suppress the small and medium-sized cap stocks and test the willingness of small and medium-sized cap stocks to go long. Institutions must attract funds from small and medium-sized cap stocks to large-cap stocks if they want to initiate a blue-chip rally. Combining with the intraday trends of large-cap and small and medium-sized cap stocks this week, we judge that the strategy of the main force institutions is gradually taking effect.

There are three important events next week that will affect the market trend: First, the annual Central Economic Work Conference will be held. The tone of this conference is basically in line with market expectations. What needs special attention is the top leadership's positioning of real estate market policies for next year. Although the conference may send some signals of partial structural fine-tuning, it does not really constitute a bearish factor in the current environment. Instead, the end of the conference will be interpreted by the market as the clarification of uncertain factors, thus being bullish for the market; Second, the economic data for November will be released at the end of next week. It can be expected that these data will further confirm the consolidation of the macroeconomic recovery trend, so it is also bullish for the market; Third, the much-anticipated global climate conference will be held next week. Since before the opening of the conference, China, the United States, India and other major carbon-emitting countries have made commitments to reduce emissions, specific results from the conference can be expected, which will help increase the market's bullish hotspots.

Comprehensively analyzing the above factors, we believe that after making moderate fluctuations around 3300 points for consolidation, the index will regain its upward momentum and launch an attack on the August high point. The conditions for setting new highs in the next stage have basically matured.

In terms of strategy, we suggest that investors maintain an optimistic view, closely observe the changes in the market style, and choose the right investment sector and individual stocks. Although we are optimistic about the future performance of blue-chip stocks, we do not believe that the market will repeat the clear-cut blue-chip rally seen in June and July. In the future, it cannot be ruled out that some small and medium-sized cap stocks and related theme stocks will remain active and strengthen.

Firstly, we recommend that investors focus on the blue-chip sector. Investors can take advantage of the oscillation period of the market to accumulate these blue-chip stocks and increase the proportion of positions in this area. Secondly, with the possible rise of large-cap blue-chip stocks, the index will maintain a good upward trend. However, if stock selection is inappropriate, it may be difficult to make profits. Therefore, we suggest that investors avoid small and medium-sized cap stocks and related theme stocks with excessive gains in the previous period and do not participate in their rebounding rally. Thirdly, for some small and medium-sized cap stocks and related theme stocks that have not yet broken through the highs of July and August, the market will still leave opportunities for such types of stocks to perform. For example, the new energy sector and other low-carbon economy sectors, which have been quiet for more than half a year, will still have profit-making opportunities in the future.

In terms of sectors, we recommend that investors pay attention to the coal sector. The coal sector has multiple investment values: Firstly, the continuous depreciation of the US dollar has kept international oil prices high, indirectly supporting coal prices to remain firm. As a major resource product, coal is undoubtedly one of the main beneficiaries of inflation expectations. Secondly, under the background of economic recovery, downstream production in the coal industry has entered an accelerated recovery channel, which will fundamentally drive the demand for coal consumption. As one of the most sensitive basic departments to the prosperity of the macroeconomy, the coal sector has already sensed the warmth of spring and entered the recovery stage first, which will inevitably reflect on the capital market. Thirdly, with the arrival of winter, the country enters the peak electricity consumption period, and the demand for coal has reached a seasonal peak. Coal inventories in various places have declined, and there is a requirement for coal price increases, which forms a seasonal stimulus for the coal sector. Fourthly, the performance of relevant listed companies in the coal sector has a stable growth prospect. According to reports, at this stage, funds are busy preparing for next year's market, and next year's market will mainly revolve around the 2009 annual report and the 2010 Q1 report. It is expected that industries and stocks with earnings pre-increases or beyond expectations will become the focus of market funds chasing. From the reports of each quarter this year, the performance of listed companies in the coal sector has maintained a relatively high growth rate. Therefore, optimistic expectations can be held for the performance of 2009 and the first quarter of next year, so the attraction of the coal sector to institutions is increasing. With the strategic reduction of institutions in sectors and stocks with large gains in the previous period approaching the end, increasing holdings in the coal sector is an inevitable choice, and even chasing high to build positions may occur.

In the secondary market, after a long period of accumulation and consolidation, the enthusiasm for buying most stocks in the coal sector has become uncontrollable this week, resulting in a rise in prices. Their performance is significantly stronger than the overall market, with many stocks leading the charge and breaking through the August high point. With the gradual rise of blue-chip stocks, more stocks in the coal sector will break through previous highs in the future, bringing the entire sector into a strong upward channel, and the sector effect will appear, regaining prominence in the market in a high-profile manner.
