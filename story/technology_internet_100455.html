With an unfavorable financial environment, the criteria for job hunting have also been lowered.

by hophi on 2008-11-02 21:16:34

Boluo ZhiKe website reported that yesterday, a large-scale talent exchange fair in the six central provinces was held at the 4th floor exchange hall of Jiangxi Talent Market. As the first large-scale job fair in our province after the US financial crisis, it was found by reporters that many job seekers lowered their standards for employment.

According to statistics, there were nearly 200 recruiting units entering the venue and more than 5000 positions offered. The total number of people who entered the job fair reached 12,114, with 2,160 people reaching an intention to hire, accounting for 17.8% of the total number of people who entered the venue. Among them, 534 people had a diploma degree, 762 people had a junior college degree, 858 people had a bachelor's degree, and 6 people had a master's degree or above.

In this job fair, the "Job Demand Ranking Table" showed that human resources/administrative management accounted for 28.5%; sales accounted for 26.8%; public relations/secretarial work accounted for 5.9%; production/manufacturing accounted for 5%; finance/audit accounted for 4.3%; electronics/electrical/appliances accounted for 3.9%; advertising/business planning accounted for 2.7%; services accounted for 2.5%; machinery accounted for 2.2%; others accounted for 18.2%. Affected by the bleak financial environment, the employment standards of college graduates have significantly dropped. Mr. Chen, a graduate of East China Jiaotong University, said that he majored in international finance. Nowadays, the employment situation in the financial industry is much worse than before. Therefore, he just hopes to find a position suitable for himself, accumulate experience, and wait for development opportunities.
