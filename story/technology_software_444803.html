July 25, 1978

by kdje3873 on 2011-06-12 19:10:26

The world's first "test-tube baby" was successfully delivered in 1978. In 2010, the in vitro fertilization technology developed by Professor Robert Edwards of Cambridge University in the UK won the Nobel Prize in Medicine. In 2002, the first patient in Jilin Province to receive "test-tube baby" treatment successfully conceived. However, at that time, the total number of "test-tube babies" treated at Bethune Second Hospital of Jilin University throughout the year was only more than ten cases. But by 2010, there were about 30 cases per month, and the total number had increased more than 20 times.

Five reasons for the increase in "test-tube babies"

On March 31, reporters contacted Dr. Xu Yaohong, director of the Reproductive Medicine Center of Bethune Second Hospital of Jilin University. Dr. Xu said that the high incidence of infertility is the main reason for the multiple increase in "test-tube babies."

Artificial abortion can easily damage reproductive organs

Liu Ying (a pseudonym), 34, has been married for nearly seven years. Because she underwent two abortions before marriage, she has been unable to conceive ever since. Later, after undergoing a fallopian tube radiography examination at the hospital, it was discovered that both of her fallopian tubes were blocked, and she could only conceive through "test-tube baby" treatment.

Dr. Xu introduced that premarital sexual behavior among young people nowadays is relatively open, and the proportion of unintended pregnancies is also increasing. There are also many cases where couples fail to use contraception properly after marriage, leading to unplanned pregnancies. Often at this point, the parties do not want the child and choose to undergo an abortion at some medical institutions. If they go to unlicensed medical institutions, even though it may be painless abortion, improper surgical procedures or lack of strict sterile operations may cause damage to reproductive organs, induce pelvic infections, and other diseases, which can lead to infertility and other long-term complications. In severe cases, the only option is to conceive using "test-tube baby" technology.

Environmental changes lead to reduced fertility

In April 2009, Wang Rui (a pseudonym), 35, and his wife Li Jie (a pseudonym), 34, came to the hospital for "test-tube baby" treatment. "I've been married to my wife for almost six years, but we haven't been able to have children. Later, after checking at the hospital, we found out that our reproductive functions have severely declined, so we haven't been able to have children," Wang Rui said with distress.

Dr. Xu introduced that he has encountered many couples like Wang Rui and Li Jie. Due to changes in living environments, poor lifestyle habits from earlier years, and various additives in the food they eat every day, changes in living environment and lifestyle have led to a decline in people's fertility. Women experience premature ovarian function decline, and men experience a decrease in sperm quality, which can lead to infertility.

Excessive work pressure leads to endocrine disorders

Wang Jing (a pseudonym), 36, works as a project manager at a large company in Changchun. Although she has been married for nearly five years, she almost always works overtime for her job. To others, she appears as a relentless "iron woman" who sacrifices everything for her career. However, as she grows older, her husband becomes dissatisfied, and her parents urge her. All sorts of pressures come crashing down on her. Wang Jing decides to put aside her work responsibilities and wants to have a child to make her family complete. However, after a year, Wang Jing still hasn't become pregnant. When she goes to the hospital for a check-up, the doctor tells her that she has severe endocrine disorders and has almost lost her ability to bear children.

"She is a typical working woman. Excessive work pressure and a fast-paced life have caused her to suffer from serious endocrine disorders. Conventional assisted reproduction treatments have been ineffective. However, when she and her husband chose 'test-tube baby' treatment, they successfully conceived. Now their child is already in kindergarten," Dr. Xu said.

Missing the optimal childbearing age before 30

"I really should have listened to my family earlier. If I had married and had children sooner, I wouldn't be in this situation now," said Meng Xiao (a pseudonym) in June 2008. At the age of 39, accompanied by her husband, Meng Xiao left the reproductive center feeling heavy-hearted. Just moments ago, the doctor informed Meng Xiao that she had missed the optimal childbearing age. Meng Xiao got married late and only married at the age of 38. "After getting married, we wanted a child, but no matter what we did, we couldn't conceive. We took a lot of medicine and injections, and even tried many 'folk remedies,' but nothing worked. Finally, we had no choice but to go to the hospital for a check-up. The doctor diagnosed that because I got married and had children late, I had missed the optimal childbearing age. My ovarian function is now very poor," Meng Xiao said sorrowfully.

Dr. Xu pointed out that a woman's ovarian function is most vigorous before the age of 30, and it gradually declines after 30. "Eventually, Meng Xiao and her husband underwent 'test-tube baby' treatment, and the results were good. They successfully conceived and gave birth to a daughter," Dr. Xu said.

Older parents wanting to conceive again face difficulties

In August 2005, Jiang Tao, aged 52, left Bethune Second Hospital of Jilin University with her newborn baby. Jiang Tao is from Liaoning Province. On July 25, 2005, she successfully gave birth via cesarean section to a boy weighing 3150 grams. She became the oldest woman in Jilin Province to successfully give birth to a "test-tube baby" and one of the older mothers in China to have a "test-tube baby." Four years ago, her 21-year-old son passed away due to illness. To heal the wounds of losing a child in middle age, she and her husband decided to have another child. Since she was already advanced in age and natural conception was difficult, they came to the Reproductive Medicine Center of Bethune Second Hospital of Jilin University and successfully conceived through "test-tube baby" treatment.

"The child unexpectedly passed away, and the older parents wanted to have another child, so they eventually chose the path of 'test-tube baby.' Although the pregnancy rate for older women is extremely low, and complications such as premature birth are very common, Jiang Tao, at the age of 52, successfully conceived after just one 'test-tube baby' treatment. There were no abnormalities during the preservation treatment, and she smoothly delivered a healthy baby boy. She safely passed through the postpartum bleeding and heart failure stages, and there was no infection in the wound after surgery," Dr. Xu said.

Most patients remain discreet; related information must be kept confidential

At the hospital, reporters noticed that most couples waiting in the hall remained silent, some even wearing masks. Each face revealed anxiety and helplessness. "Patients coming to the hospital for treatment are generally very discreet and sensitive," Dr. Xu said. According to reports, protecting the personal privacy of infertile couples is the responsibility of doctors, and the hospital has strict confidentiality policies. Due to insufficient understanding of "test-tube babies," most patients do not wish to disclose this information to others. "Sometimes, even during our hospital's follow-up health visits after childbirth, we encounter refusals from the parents," Dr. Xu explained.

No difference from naturally conceived children

Ms. Sun, a 36-year-old resident of Changchun, always believed that "test-tube babies" are different from normal children. "I heard that 'test-tube babies' are less intelligent compared to those born naturally, so I have always been worried," Ms. Sun expressed her concerns. In response, Dr. Xu stated, "There is no difference between 'test-tube babies' and normal children. Neither physical development nor intelligence is affected." "This is because many people do not understand 'test-tube babies.' We have helped many 'test-tube babies' conceive successfully. For example, a pair of twins, a boy and a girl, were successfully conceived at our center in September 2002. They are now in school and are very healthy," Dr. Xu said.

Gender selection is possible but requires medical indication

"In 2009, a couple came for 'test-tube baby' treatment. At that time, they were capable of conceiving naturally but wanted a boy. However, since they did not meet the medical criteria for gender selection, we were unable to grant their request, as it would be illegal," Dr. Xu explained. Originally, "test-tube babies" can indeed determine the baby's gender in advance, but this must be based on relevant medical indications, such as sex-linked genetic diseases. "For example, if a certain genetic disease will definitely occur in girls but not in boys, then gender selection is allowed," Dr. Xu clarified.

Success rate for patients under 35 is 40% to 50%

Dr. Xu said: "'Test-tube baby' involves using artificial methods to allow oocytes and sperm to fertilize outside the body, undergo early embryonic development, and then be transplanted into the mother's uterus for further development until birth. Female factors include severe fallopian tube diseases or issues with the fallopian tubes. Male factors include oligospermia, asthenospermia, and teratospermia. Additionally, there are some unexplained cases of infertility. Suitable candidates are legally married couples aged between 20 and 50. It usually takes about three weeks, and the cost varies depending on individual circumstances, generally ranging from 15,000 to 20,000 yuan."

Dr. Xu added: "The success rate for patients under 35 is typically 40% to 50%. For those over 35, the success rate decreases significantly. The success of 'test-tube baby' mainly depends on factors such as age, ovarian function, sperm quality, uterine lining, psychological status, and laboratory culture conditions. Therefore, couples who have been married for more than five years without children should undergo relevant examinations as soon as possible."

Reporter's direct observation of "test-tube baby" surgery

Oocyte retrieval takes only three to five minutes

Around 1 p.m. on March 31, in the corridor of the Reproductive Medicine Center of Bethune Second Hospital of Jilin University, there were six or seven couples waiting. Ge Chong (a pseudonym), 39, and his wife Li Xin (a pseudonym), 36, appeared more anxious. "My wife is having surgery this afternoon for 'test-tube baby,'" Ge Chong explained. It was understood that Ge Chong comes from a rural area in Dehui City. He married his wife seven years ago, but they have not had any children. As the saying goes: Among the three forms of filial impiety, none is greater than being without offspring. Although Ge Chong does not appear anxious on the surface, he feels worried inside, and the elders often remind them about having a child.

"We have visited at least ten hospitals, taken countless medications, and received numerous injections. I don't blame my wife, but our entire family hopes to have a child. Eventually, we came here, and the doctor said that my wife has severe fallopian tube blockage. To have a child, we must undergo 'test-tube baby' treatment," Ge Chong said. At 1:30 p.m., the operating room door opened, and Ge Chong watched as his wife entered the operating room.

With the doctor's permission and thorough disinfection, the reporter entered the hospital laboratory and operating room wearing sterilized clothing. According to the doctor, the process of "test-tube baby" actually changes the way and place where oocytes and sperm meet. Through artificial means, they are fertilized outside the body, undergo early embryonic development, and are then transplanted into the mother's uterus for further development until birth. Therefore, controlled ovarian hyperstimulation, oocyte retrieval and sperm washing, in vitro fertilization, embryo transfer, and subsequent luteal support treatment are all crucial steps.

Preparation: Controlled Ovarian Hyperstimulation

Before the surgery, Li Xin had undergone a series of routine tests and had injected ovulation-inducing drugs for more than ten days. After monitoring with ultrasound, it was determined that the number and size of the oocytes met the requirements, thus confirming the timing for oocyte retrieval. "Through drug stimulation, multiple follicles develop to maturity, increasing the success rate of oocyte retrieval and in vitro fertilization. This is known as controlled ovarian hyperstimulation. Simultaneously, we monitor follicle development and determine the timing for oocyte retrieval," Dr. Xu explained.

Surgery: Oocyte Retrieval and Sperm Washing

According to the doctor, oocytes are highly sensitive to external environmental conditions. The incubator temperature is maintained around 37°C, with appropriate humidity, and they must not be exposed to light. Therefore, during the operation, direct exposure to light must be avoided. During oocyte retrieval, the doctor uses ultrasound guidance to aspirate oocytes through the posterior fornix. Based on the ultrasound screen showing the puncture guiding line, each black follicle is sequentially aspirated, extracting all the fluid within the follicle. The follicular fluid flows through the puncture needle tubing into test tubes, which are then placed in a constant-temperature test tube rack on the workbench in the embryo culture room. Laboratory operators assist in selecting oocytes and transferring them to culture medium, where they are cultured for 4 to 6 hours before fertilization. On the same day as oocyte retrieval, semen is collected, optimized, and processed to select sperm with strong motility and good fertilization ability. Within 3 to 5 minutes, Li Xin's oocyte retrieval procedure was completed.

Cultivation: In Vitro Fertilization and Embryo Transfer

"After oocyte retrieval, the oocytes are mixed with processed sperm in a specific ratio in the culture medium for fertilization. The fertilized eggs need to be continuously cultured for 2 to 3 days. If the oocytes and sperm 'cooperate well' and successfully develop into multiple embryos, then embryo transfer can proceed," the doctor explained. During embryo transfer, the doctor places 2 to 3 well-developed embryos into the uterine cavity through a transfer catheter. Remaining high-quality embryos can be cryopreserved. "After the transfer, you need to lie flat for 2 to 3 hours and avoid strenuous activities within 24 to 48 hours," the doctor advised.

Around 2:30 p.m., Ge Chong left the hospital temporarily with Li Xin. Although the surgery itself was short, the waiting time felt long. "I believe we can definitely succeed. As long as we can conceive a child, we are willing to wait however long it takes. We just want a complete family," Li Xin said softly.
