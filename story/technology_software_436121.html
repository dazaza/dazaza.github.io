College girl village official starts left-behind children tutoring class (picture)

by iface003 on 2011-05-24 10:26:54

The deepest impression on Huang Pan was that one day it rained heavily and her good friends came to invite her to play. Thinking of the tutoring class, Huang Pan refused. She rode an electric scooter to the tutoring class. At a downhill section, she fell off the scooter and sprained her foot. Her friend asked in puzzlement: "What do you want?" But when she saw five children who were taking shelter under the eaves and got wet by the rain shout in surprise "Teacher Huang has come", her eyes became moist. This made her keep doing it. "Children's happy smiles and parents' warm greetings when they pick up their children make me feel that my efforts are worth it and should be done!" Huang Pan said that children's healthy growth was the result she wanted. Now it is winter vacation and she hopes to let children learn more knowledge through the winter vacation homework tutoring class.

At the end of February 2009, there was a meeting at the village office of Laijia Village. However, there were four or five children playing and making noise in the yard. Huang Pan went out to see what was going on and saw a scene that made her "love": these children brought their homework after school and put it on the table tennis table. They would crawl up to write sometimes and play sometimes. Because the weather was still cold, the students' hands were all frozen red.

Later, the village built an "agricultural book house" and she served as the administrator of the book house, which also provided a suitable place for her tutoring class. In May 2009, the tutoring class "moved" into the village reading room. Every time, she would boil water for the children early and warm the house with coal in winter.

Happy

Female college student village official Huang Pan is tutoring students in their homework (Photo by Li Biao)

This tutoring class has students from first grade to eighth grade. The questions raised by the students vary, but as a former teacher, she can still provide the greatest help. Last "Children's Day", she personally spent money to buy pens, notebooks, erasers and other school supplies for left-behind children as holiday gifts for the children.

Through tutoring, the children's grades have improved significantly, and three students have even been admitted to key middle schools. After receiving the final exam score report, the mother of first-grade girl Lai Xiaoru specially went to the village office to show her score report to Huang Pan.

Transition from teacher to village official

In July 2007, Huang Pan graduated from Jiankang College and successfully became a teacher at a school in Foshan, Guangdong. There, she quickly rose from an ordinary substitute teacher to become the head of the grade and the deputy editor of the literary society, becoming a "backbone" of the school. If it weren't for a recruitment announcement for university student village officials online in June 2008 that "moved" her, Huang Pan's life would have continued quietly. Huang Pan, who is from Xinzhi Town, Nanzheng County, hopes to contribute her youth and intelligence to her hometown. Although the school repeatedly tried to retain her, she resigned from her enviable job in the south and resolutely returned to her hometown to take the village official exam. In October of that year, she came to Laijia Village, Hujiaying Township, Nanzheng County, and took on the role of assistant to the village party secretary. "Huang Pan is a good girl, very dedicated and hardworking, and the work in the village is done very well." This was the evaluation of her more than a year of work by village party secretary Luo Haichun yesterday. The change of roles made Huang Pan more mature. Although she often needs to deal with various trivial matters, she feels very fulfilled and thinks that she "has learned a lot from it, very meaningful."

Noisy children gave birth to the "tutoring class"

"Thank you very much, Teacher Huang!" This is the common feeling of the parents. Yesterday, village party secretary Luo Haichun said that because the parents of the students work outside all year round, they are very worried about leaving their children at home. The tutoring class solved the parents' worries. Many student parents often buy some fruit to give to Huang Pan to express their gratitude. Some parents, seeing that the windows of the tutoring class were broken, would come over to fix them.

In order not to conflict with her daily work, when the tutoring class started, Huang Pan would ride an electric scooter to Li Xue's house every Monday and Thursday afternoon after work to tutor the students collectively. As the news of the free tutoring class spread, many students joined the tutoring group after school. The number of students increased from the initial six to fourteen, and currently reached twenty-six.

From a naive college student to a responsible village official, Huang Pan, 24 years old this year, has gradually grown up. And her free "Left-Behind Children Homework Tutoring Class" has written the youthful style of this contemporary college student contributing her intelligence to the construction of a new countryside during this period.

Suddenly, Huang Pan had the idea to establish a free "Left-Behind Children Homework Tutoring Class". After the meeting that day, she found the village clerk to understand the situation of left-behind children in the village, then conducted door-to-door investigations in the village to grasp the number of left-behind children in the village. On March 12, 2009, with the strong support of the village, she established the "Left-Behind Children Homework Tutoring Class".
