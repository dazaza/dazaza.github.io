Jimmy Choo and the like all say goodbye to Mahjong.

by jimmy87jl on 2010-03-31 17:10:29

First of all, I must admit to this shortcoming: my self-control regarding mahjong and related activities seems inadequate. I've always thought of myself as someone with strong self-discipline—independent, not easily influenced by others in making decisions—but in this area, it's like I'm under a spell. In a group of friends, I don't usually consider myself one of those people who are easily swayed, but perhaps due to recent boredom or loneliness, or the stock market’s fluctuations that don’t concern me much, I’ve started thinking backwards and seeing things more clearly, yet still feeling some confusion.

Playing mahjong, although up to now there have been wins and losses, I think I should remain detached both inside and out. In fact, no wonder a friend’s phone calls go unanswered, no wonder friends pull away; if your own decisions can be influenced by others, how can you maintain yourself or avoid losing what truly matters? At the card table, the weaknesses of human nature gradually come to light. Why is that? Winning money, yes, but sometimes it’s because of the cards causing friction at the table that hurts friendships and understanding. Playing cards with a group of brothers and friends for over ten years, when they lose money, they feel unlucky and adopt a mindset to regain their luck through playing mahjong again and again, leading to more friction...

This again makes me think of Liu Bei and Zhuge Liang from the Three Kingdoms era. They could have achieved the unification of China, but the cause failed because Liu Bei couldn’t control himself, ending in regret and loss. This incident also led to Luo Ji's death and buried the grand cause of reunifying China under Zhuge Liang and Luen Wu. While Zhuge Liang was also guilty of not controlling his own mistakes after Liu Bei's death, he ignored warnings and simply wanted to attack Wei, eventually failing and dying during the Northern Expedition.

Similarly, if one still cannot control themselves, they will ultimately become a loser.

Today, with this article on my blog, I will go do something I should do, stay detached from them, and return to my own horizons.
