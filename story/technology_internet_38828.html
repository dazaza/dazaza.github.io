Bonlun.com, there is no best, only better.

by impark on 2007-12-11 11:00:01

Recently, I saw someone on the internet questioning the fairness of an event organized by Bonlun, and there were anonymous comments criticizing Bonlun, which reminded me of a lot. Those who have visited my blog also know that I participated in Bonlun's activities before. Here are the articles I contributed: "Bonlun Website, My Favorite Movie, Grandma's House" and "Bonlun Website, Rising Housing Prices and Inflation, Everything is Increasing!" For the second article, due to my own efforts in soliciting votes, it ranked first in terms of voting numbers. However, because the difference in votes between the first and second place was within 15 votes, the final winning article was selected by the staff of Bonlun Website, and unfortunately, my article didn't make the cut. At that time, I was indeed very disappointed because I had put in a lot of effort to solicit votes and wrote the article with great care. So, I also wrote some articles expressing complaints and suggestions - Thoughts on Bonlun's Thousand-Yuan Event. Some people said that there were underhanded dealings in the events currently organized by Bonlun Website. Seeing these accusations, all I wanted to do was laugh at those petty-minded individuals. In fact, I've also visited the office of Bonlun Website, so I have some understanding of their staff. Writing this article now isn't because I know them and want to stand up for them, but as a blogger, I just want to share my thoughts. If you participate in an activity, write an article with effort, and solicit votes, yet your article is not selected, and you don't win the thousand-yuan prize, anyone would feel disappointed and sad. After all, you've put in the effort, and when your efforts don't pay off, you're bound to feel let down. However, what's important is that even if you're disappointed, you shouldn't blame the organizers of the event, because you participated in the competition knowing the rules. Regardless of the outcome, don't anonymously criticize the organizers with harsh words. There are also many people online who say that participants in Bonlun's events, in order to make others' articles appear as cheating, will go to great lengths to vote for their opponents, ensuring that their own articles seem fair. It's indeed difficult to measure and prevent such cheating behaviors. Everyone knows that although cheating is not allowed during exams, many still use special methods to cheat.
