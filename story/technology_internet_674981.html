Introduction to Hong Kong:

Hong Kong is located at 114°15′ East longitude and 22°15′ North latitude, along the south coast of China. It consists of Hong Kong Island, the Kowloon Peninsula, the inland New Territories, and 262 large and small islands (offshore islands).

Hong Kong is a free port; except for tobacco, strong liquor, and fuel (gasoline, diesel, etc.), no other import items are subject to tariffs in Hong Kong. Hong Kong's economy is renowned for free trade, low tax rates, and minimal government intervention. The financial industry, real estate, tourism, and manufacturing are the four pillar industries supporting Hong Kong’s economic development. The top ten Hong Kong products include ready-made garments, electronic products, textiles, watches, printed materials, plastic products, metal goods, food and beverages, industrial machinery, and jewelry. For many years, Hong Kong has been rated by international prestigious rating agencies and organizations as having the world's freest economic system. Hong Kong is an international financial center, international shipping hub, regional trade center, and enjoys an irreplaceable superior location close to many countries and regions.

Advantages of Registering a Hong Kong Company:

1. No industry restrictions for Hong Kong companies. Any legal business can be operated without requiring additional licenses;

2. Hong Kong is one of the freest trading ports globally with unrestricted logistics: most goods entering and leaving do not incur tariffs; there is free movement of capital in Hong Kong, allowing instant conversion and transfer of various currencies;

3. Tax policies and rates for Hong Kong companies: few tax types and low tax rates;

Hong Kong companies only have two types of taxes: one is a one-time registration capital stamp duty, with a rate of 1/1000; the other is profits tax, with a rate of 16.5%.

Special consumer goods (such as tobacco and alcohol) are subject to customs duties when entering or leaving Hong Kong.

4. Utilizing Hong Kong's reputation to create a Hong Kong brand and enhance the company's international image;

5. Allows shell companies to exist and allows offshore business operations;

Conditions for Registering a Hong Kong Company:

1. Company Name

The general provisions regarding company names under Hong Kong's Companies Ordinance are as follows:

(a) A company may use an English name, a Chinese name, or register both English and Chinese names simultaneously, but it cannot use a name that combines English and Chinese.

(b) An English company name must end with [Limited], and a Chinese name must end with [有限公司].

If the company name includes any of the following words/terms, prior approval from the Chief Executive is required before usage:

(01) Building society (02) Chamber of commerce (03) Cooperative (04) Kaifong (05) Mass transit (06) Underground railway (07) Municipal (08) Tourist association (09) Trustee (10) Savings (11) Municipal (12) Underground railway (13) Metro (14) Cooperation (15) Trustee (16) Housing cooperative (17) Trustee (18) Chamber of commerce (19) Trust (20) Tourism association (21) Neighborhood (22) General Chamber of Commerce (23) Savings

2. Company Shareholders (at least one)

3. Company Directors (at least one)

Shareholders and directors can be the same entity, which can be either a natural person or a limited company.

A natural person can be of any nationality but must be at least 18 years old (excluding undischarged bankrupts or those with mental disabilities).

A limited company can be registered anywhere.

4. Statutory Secretary (must be a Hong Kong company or Hong Kong resident)

5. Business Registration Address (must be a Hong Kong address)

Used for receiving government correspondence and notifications. The company does not need to specially rent or purchase dedicated property, but it cannot solely use a post office box as the business registration address.

6. Registered Capital (as per Hong Kong company law: standard registered capital is HKD 10,000)

Documents Required from Clients:

1. Company name;

2. Registered capital (as per Hong Kong company law: standard registered capital is HKD 10,000);

3. Copies of identity proofs of shareholders and directors;

4. Shareholding ratio of each shareholder;

5. Permanent addresses and contact details of shareholders and directors.
