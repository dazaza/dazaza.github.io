The stain removal process of a dry cleaner 

 

The stain removal methods used by dry cleaning shops should be flexibly applied according to the characteristics of the stains. Different stains require different chemical agents, and different properties of stains call for different stain removal methods. Proper application is helpful for removing stains. Otherwise, it could either reduce the efficiency of stain removal or damage the fabric fibers. There are usually several methods for removing stains: 

1. Spray Method of Stain Removal Process

The spray gun equipped on the stain removal table has functions such as spraying pure steam, steam with air, and compressed air, providing a kind of mechanical force. The spray method utilizes this force to remove water-soluble stains (or partially remove them) from fabrics. When using this method for stain removal, attention should be paid to:

(1) Whether the fabric structure can withstand a certain amount of mechanical force.

(2) Whether the stain can withstand without solidifying or dissolving deeply into the fabric.

(3) The distance and angle of the spray gun's action, as well as the duration of the spray.

2. Wiping Method of Stain Removal Process

Wiping is a commonly used method during stain removal. It involves applying a certain amount of mechanical force to the stain on the fabric, causing the stain to detach due to the wiping action. There are usually the following forms of wiping:

(1) Brushing Style

After applying stain remover to the surface of the stain, gently tap the surface of the stain with a brush to loosen it, then lightly brush until the stain detaches from the fabric.

(2) Scraper Wiping Style

After applying stain remover to the surface of the stain, let it penetrate and dissolve, then gently scrape the stain with a scraper to detach it from the fabric fibers.

(3) Front-side Wiping Style

Form a ball with cotton wrapped in fine cloth, dip it in stain remover, and gently wipe the surface and surrounding area of the stain to remove the stain.

(4) Back-side Wiping Style

Form a ball with cotton wrapped in fine cloth, dip it in stain remover, and gently wipe the backside of the stain to remove it.

3. Soaking Method of Stain Removal Process

For some stains that are tightly bonded to the fabric and cannot be smoothly removed by dripping stain remover and wiping, the relevant stain remover can be placed in a container to soak the stained part of the fabric, allowing sufficient time for the stain remover to react with the stain. After an appropriate period of action, use a brush to gently brush to help the stain detach.

4. Absorption Method of Stain Removal Process

For certain fabrics, such as delicate fabrics or loosely structured fabrics, or stains with severe discoloration pollution, such as large areas of ballpoint pen paint, the absorption method can be used to assist in stain removal. This involves applying an appropriate amount of stain remover to the stain on the fabric, waiting for it to dissolve, and then using a cotton cloth bundle to gently absorb from the backside of the stain, gradually transferring the dissolved stain onto the cotton cloth bundle. A clean cotton cloth bundle should be replaced after each light absorption to avoid secondary contamination.

5. Strong Washing Method of Stain Removal Process

For large-area stains where gentle mechanical action does not achieve good cleaning results, the strong washing method can be used. Apply stain remover to the surface of the stained fabric, wait for it to penetrate, then strongly brush with a brush or forcefully scrape with a scraper, utilizing strong mechanical action to make the stain detach from the fabric.

When using the strong washing method, a prerequisite must be followed: the structure of the fabric being brushed should be tight and wear-resistant. If it is a colored fabric, its dye fastness should also be considered before strong washing. Using this method to handle stains on fabrics will inevitably cause some damage to the fabric, reducing its lifespan. Therefore, unless absolutely necessary, the strong washing method should not be used for stain removal.
