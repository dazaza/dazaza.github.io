Learning Leadership from the President Part One

In 1964, several rather important political figures made a suggestion to President Lyndon Johnson: the country should provide young people with the opportunity to work directly in the highest administrative body of the government. This idea was clearly not out of urgent need. Not only were cabinet members busy with official duties, but bringing inexperienced youths into the White House to participate in serious national political activities was already a risky move. However, whether it was fate, providence, coincidence, or God's will, some key individuals almost simultaneously had the same idea at the same time.

At first, Goben did not want to work at the Department of Agriculture, but after meeting Harding himself and further understanding the affairs of the Department of Agriculture, Goben gladly accepted the position and put in great enthusiasm. "I personally have no background related to agriculture. In my mind, the Department of Agriculture is just a department that pays farmers not to grow crops," Goben said, "But after meeting Harding and talking with him, I fell in love with this job. It was then that I learned that the U.S. Department of Agriculture has the largest financial institution in the world - because the Commodity Exchange Department also belonged to the Department of Agriculture at the time, and the Department of Agriculture also oversees the largest welfare system and housing rental department. I knew so little about the Department of Agriculture before!"
