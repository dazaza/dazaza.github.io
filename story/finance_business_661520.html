Gree and Midea air conditioning - dual oligopoly - is expected to form

Gree and Midea Air Conditioners: "Dual Oligopoly" Likely to Form – Corporate Dynamics - Industry News - Building Materials Network

When two companies together monopolize or nearly monopolize the market for a particular product, this is referred to as a dual oligopoly. According to statistics from the Information Resource Development Department of the National Information Center, 27 air conditioner brands disappeared in China in 2005. Among the remaining 69 brands, 29 have a market share below 0.01%, and these 29 are likely to struggle to survive in 2006. If the main theme of the air conditioning industry in 2005 was the exit of Kelon and LG from the first tier, then the main theme for 2006 may very well be the preliminary formation of a dual oligopoly between Gree Electric (000651) and Midea Electric (000527). Currently, Haier has already ceded its leading position in Shandong's market to Gree.

Similarities Between Gree, Midea and Maotai, Wuliangye:

We do not intend to directly compare Gree, Midea with Maotai, Wuliangye. In the short term, it is difficult to determine which of Gree or Midea has a more promising future.

Industry Situation: Overall, the number of consumers of baijiu is decreasing but increasingly concentrated on high-quality baijiu; similarly, while the overall growth space for air conditioners is limited, production and sales are increasingly concentrated in leading enterprises.

Brand Advantage: Under the pressure from upstream and downstream industries, products without a strong brand find it increasingly difficult to gain trust from consumers, upstream suppliers, or banks.

Quasi-monopoly Advantage —— Cost Control Capability: Although Maotai and Wuliangye possess natural monopoly advantages, the cost control capabilities of Gree and Midea represent quasi-monopoly advantages built on the market. Under the same selling price, these companies can make profits, whereas others lacking such capabilities are gradually eliminated or relegated to regional brands.

Specific Advantages of Gree and Midea: Strong capital chains, sales channels, integrated upstream and downstream operations.

Materials accelerate industry consolidation, highlighting the importance of capital chains. 2005 was one of the most challenging years in the history of the air conditioning industry. The pressure came from rising raw material prices, which led to intense conflicts especially between home appliance chains and manufacturers, resulting in large-scale industry integration.

Discussing capacity alone is meaningless. One reason is that all countries' consumer goods experience overcapacity, necessitating factors like brand, sales channels, cost control, and capital application ability. Focusing solely on capacity leads to misleading conclusions and pessimistic projections about the industry. Another reason why capacity isn't important is that for the air conditioning industry, most fixed asset depreciation is nearing completion and doesn't significantly impact costs, making manufacturers insensitive to whether capacity is utilized. Additionally, setting up assembly lines for every 1 million air conditioners requires an investment of less than RMB 5 million.

The sales model advantages of Gree and Midea are evident, and the integration of upstream and downstream industries significantly reduces costs. A notable feature of the 2005 air conditioning market was the tight supply of compressors. Midea successfully passed on the increased raw material costs and even achieved higher returns. Due to its complete industrial chain, including self-produced compressors and motors, Midea benefited significantly from steel price reductions, prompting us to upgrade our rating. As compressor production and sales reach balance, Japanese companies producing compressors still maintain strong pricing power and aren’t likely to drastically reduce prices. Gree and Midea have started strengthening cooperation with upstream suppliers, such as Gree signing agreements with Baosteel and Midea forming a strategic alliance with Jiangxi Copper Group. Establishing stable alliances with upstream suppliers, combined with increased scale and capacity, integrates the entire industrial chain, opening up larger profit margins for Gree and Midea. Superior cost control ensures stable profits, laying a solid foundation for further adjustments and development in marketing strategies and brand building.

Other manufacturers lack the ability to challenge the "dual oligopoly." Haier struggles to stop its declining trend. Its domestic sales channel remains uncertain, and its own-brand exports face awkward situations. The integration of Hisense and Kelon still requires time. After Hisense took over Kelon, they have yet to fully understand the situation, and issues with corporate culture and management style integration remain unresolved, so there won’t be any essential impact on the market for now. While struggling to defend its microwave oven business and facing financial constraints, Galanz has zero opportunities for good performance in air conditioners. Midea adopted strategic losses, forcing Galanz's market share to drop from 76% to 37%. Skyworth is preparing for a Hong Kong IPO, but its advantage lies in price wars, and it cannot match Gree and Midea in terms of technology, quality, and brand. Aoxiang finds it hard to escape its low-end image, and its marketing events remain at a tactical level rather than part of a strategic marketing plan.

Huge Growth Potential in Central Air Conditioning

Central air conditioning is a natural extension of the industry. In the US, usage exceeds 70%, in Japan over 50%, while in China, household central air conditioning usage accounts for only around 5%, indicating vast growth potential. Moreover, the gross profit margin of these products is above 40%.

The development potential of Gree and Midea mainly lies in production management, cost control, and after-sales service. As technology continues to break through, their manufacturing advantages will soon become apparent. It is believed that these two companies will ultimately emerge as the winners.
