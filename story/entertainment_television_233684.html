The Birth of the Dark Knight

From a young age, Mai Ti Shuang was full of business acumen and well understood the principle that doing business leads to prosperity. He studied hard and successfully completed his MBA at Stanford University in the United States. While there, he also looked for opportunities.

After graduation, Shuang recognized the potential of He Wentian's company and took the initiative to approach Wentian. He bluntly stated that he didn't want to be an employee but rather a partner, willing even if it meant owning just 0.01% of the shares. Impressed by Shuang's uniqueness, Wentian allowed him to join as a partner. Their relationship was both mentor-mentee and friends, working seamlessly together to specialize in acquiring companies and selling them off. They became renowned snipers in the American business world.

Later, Shuang became known as the infamous "Black Knight" in the business world, ruthless and merciless, gradually gaining prominence. Similarly, the differences in opinions between Shuang and Wentian increased. For the sake of the company’s interests and development, Shuang decided to fully acquire Wentian's company. Unable to bear the blow, Wentian drove under the influence of alcohol. Shuang learned that Wentian lost control of his car and fell into the sea. After the incident, Wentian's body could not be found, but Shuang believed Wentian was dead, feeling immense guilt. Was the value of a deal really greater than friendship or life? Shuang reflected on everything from the past and decided to no longer be the Black Knight, choosing to retire from the business world. From the newspaper, Shuang learned that Wentian's wife and child were living in difficult conditions in Hong Kong. Believing the responsibility lay with him, Shuang decided to return to Hong Kong to take care of Wentian's family.

Miao Miao Pursuing Debts

Upon returning to Hong Kong, Shuang sought out Wentian's wife, She Mu Lian. Coincidentally, he stayed at a thug hotel run by Tang Ji, meeting Tang Ji who was troubled by issues related to the hotel's operations. Tang Ji, a middle-aged skirt-chaser and second-generation heir, was born into a commercial family and had a notorious reputation outside but was filial to his father, always surrounded by beautiful women.

By chance, Shuang met Lin Miao Miao at a luxury store, thus creating animosity between them. Miao Miao was a compulsive shopper, owing a large amount on her credit cards, which forced her to become a debt collector. With a "no success, no fee" spirit, she became the ace of the debt collection company. Mu Lian owed Miao Miao's financial company a large sum due to her grandmother Zhou Xiao Qiao being scammed. Miao Miao was responsible for collecting the debt, leading to another encounter with Shuang. Shuang took on Mu Lian's debts after finding her family, causing Miao Miao to mistakenly believe Shuang was Mu Lian's ex-husband who abandoned his wife and children. This left a terrible impression of Shuang on Miao Miao. Mu Lian was unaware that her husband's death was indirectly caused by Shuang and still firmly believed that her husband, Wentian, was alive. Mu Lian's grandmother would often accuse Shuang of being the murderer of her son Wentian. Initially shocked by the truth being discovered by Mu Lian, later it was found that the grandmother suffered from early-stage dementia, leading everyone to believe her words were nonsensical and not credible.
