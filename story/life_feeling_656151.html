Late at night, don't say you love me anymore.

by xiaoxiao67 on 2012-01-06 22:22:01

In the deep night, please stop saying you love me.

When flowers bloom and rain falls in dreams, when lotus leaves are newly stained but no sparrows come, a lute sits by the window.

Alone, I feel this sentiment, somewhat melancholic.

Self-pitying, without any special charm, all of this is caused by my solitary nature leading to self-isolation, with no trace of anything else.

The slanting sun sets in the west, gradually pressing the sky around me. Clouds dark as ink gather tightly, not letting a breath through. The cool dampness begins to condense into droplets; tiny water beads float in the air, transparent and visible, mist-like delicacies hovering around me. That cold beauty sticks to my eyelashes and hair ends, quietly dispersing the heat waves, bringing a refreshing sensation from the outside of my body all the way inside, indescribable with specific words to express such heartfelt comfort.

Silent surroundings, no sound around, nothing else visible.

Being alone, it's really good, walking so light and unique.

Gradually, a kind of tranquility seeps out from within my body, as if everything about me has turned into mist, evaporating in the ink-colored horizon until there’s no trace left. Otherwise, how could I feel that I no longer exist in this world, nor can I sense other lives?

Silently breathing in the damp and cool air, the distinctive natural fragrance and light colors blend into my thoughts, an illusion floating up, a different taste spreading in my heart. It's as if the crowds under the other sunshine have suddenly evaporated, those troubling interpersonal relationships and complex social interactions are suddenly disconnected from me. This feeling of being detached from worldly troubles, tangled and unable to leave, is completely erased from my memory.

It's terrifying, my mind has been cleansed by the ink-colored night sky pressing down on me.

If I can't clearly return before dawn, how will I be able to walk the path shared by everyone in that chaotic world? I don’t want to be a trouble in the crowd or live an alternative life. I console myself, stop my steps, and look for the way back, but there’s no trace left to touch or caress.

A crooked dead tree, sitting on it brings joy. The smell of decay makes me feel close, like meeting a friend whose whereabouts were unknown. It feels like it's waiting for my arrival in such an environment and weather.

Thinking, fortunately, I lie down on it. The sky seems right against my face, lighting a cigarette, letting its faint glow warm my fearless soul, pulling my sight away to create some space between my heart and the world.

An inexplicable sense of loss follows the extinguishing of the cigarette towards me. Your figure becomes clearly visible. This isn't a dream, yet it's more perfect than one. I can't believe my own eyes and illusions. Please don't wander into my sight again, and don't say you love me so seriously.

I don't want to hear your breath, especially in such a night, nor do I want your comfort. This kind of bewilderment is very comfortable, letting me experience another world through stillness.
