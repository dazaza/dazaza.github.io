Moratti Publicly Rejects Mourinho's Request to Buy a Striker: "He Got It Wrong"

Sina Sports - Mourinho publicly stated, "I hope to sign a striker in the winter," and news websites like Football Market confidently reported that Inter Milan had already reached a transfer agreement with Toni, just waiting for the transfer market to open to complete the formalities. However, on Thursday afternoon, Inter Milan President Moratti attended a scheduled meeting of the professional league in Milan and told reporters waiting outside that Inter Milan would not buy a striker.

Speaking about the possible actions Inter Milan might take in the upcoming transfer market, Moratti said, "What are the chances of Pandev or Toni joining? Currently, the chances of Pandev and Toni joining are zero. We are fine as we are now, and we will continue moving forward with our current squad. This year, we do not intend to make too many significant signings. Mourinho said that another striker would be helpful? He is right in saying that, but he has already seen what the club achieved in the transfer market this summer, and he assumed we would do the same in January. However, the club does not have that intention. I don't think we can make acquisitions of the same magnitude in January as we did in the summer, so we do not plan to take any action in January."

Regarding the recent widely publicized conflict between Mourinho and La Gazzetta dello Sport journalist Rammazotti, Moratti said, "Despite some incidents, his attitude towards me and the players within Inter Milan is completely different from what the newspapers say. On Sunday in Bergamo, he was just a little too anxious, which I regret, but I have already apologized for it. The ruling on Monday regarding the football scandal case was only the first step; let's see what happens next."

Although Mourinho recently stated, "I don't think my relationship with Moratti has deteriorated," in the media's view, Moratti's statement was actually a public rejection of Mourinho's transfer demands. While Mourinho made excessive requests in the transfer market, there was no improvement in European competitions, which already annoyed Moratti. Moreover, Mourinho's tough stance towards the media and his conflict with Rammazotti further exacerbated the situation. Moratti's words today proved that Mourinho had not consulted him before throwing out the so-called "buy a striker" claim, making Moratti's tone filled with dissatisfaction.

In response, CEO Bauliro spoke in an official tone: "I don't think there is any need for a coaching change. We are currently at the top of the league, and we are very happy about it. It is normal for the head coach to face controversies. The Champions League is an important competition, but we all know that achieving goals is very difficult. Only one of the top European teams can ultimately lift the trophy. Therefore, we cannot tie the success or failure of this tournament to the dismissal or retention of the team's head coach. Decisions about whether the head coach stays or leaves must consider all comprehensive factors."

Regarding Mourinho's recent state of anxiety, Bauliro said, "I think Mourinho is very relaxed now, and we should regain our composure. The team is currently at the top of the league, and we have also advanced to the next round of the UEFA Champions League. I don't know why we should be anxious. We have every reason to feel happy and peaceful: we highly appreciate our head coach, and we could not be more satisfied than we are now."

Speaking about Inter Milan's moves in the January transfer market, Bauliro said, "Will Pandev join? In early January, we will calmly discuss matters related to the transfer market. These players are all strong, and I think we just need to remain calm. Whether it is Pandev, Toni, or someone else, it will ultimately be Mourinho who decides who Inter Milan needs."
