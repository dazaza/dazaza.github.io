Kingsoft Kuaipan New Version Preview: Will Adopt a New "Cloud Bridge" Interface Technology

by xdash on 2011-09-23 11:32:28

On September 19, Kingsoft's cloud synchronization software, Kingsoft KuaiPan (www.kuaipan.cn), made a grand announcement at the new product communication meeting of Kingsoft Office Software and its partner conference in Beijing. The announcement was about a "globally pioneering" new technology called the Cloud Bridge Technology, which is an embedded integrated operation interface technology.

(Above image: Preview of the new version of KuaiPan's "Cloud Bridge" interface. Click to view full-size image.)

It is reported that in the soon-to-be-released new version of KuaiPan, both local and server files will be managed under a unified visible interface. This interface achieves seamless integration with the resource manager through an embedded method. When users perform regular operations on a file within KuaiPan, they can see the progress and effects of the server's operations on this file via this interface. This kind of integration is indeed a first for domestic and international cloud storage services. This technology will significantly improve the current situation where using a cloud disk is not intuitive and users find it hard to understand server operations. Furthermore, it will promote the popularization of synchronized cloud disks among ordinary users.

Yang Gang, the head of KuaiPan, stated: "Kingsoft KuaiPan has always been focused on product technology refinement and user demand mining. After analyzing the reasons for Dropbox's high valuation, we can easily find that user numbers, profitability, user experience, and innovation are the key elements for cloud storage products to grasp the market pulse. Innovation provides the source power for the other three aspects. The Cloud Bridge Technology launched today is a milestone in KuaiPan's development. We have sufficient confidence to win in the long run."

Background Information: In 2007, Kingsoft established the cloud storage laboratory. According to the initial plan, KuaiPan was only meant to provide personal document storage for tens of millions of active WPS Office users. Later, the acceptance and recognition of Dropbox abroad proved the broad prospects of personal cloud storage services, leading to KuaiPan being spun off as an independent entity. The release of the internal beta version in March 2010 made KuaiPan China's first multi-terminal synchronized cloud disk. More than a year after its launch, KuaiPan's user base has approached 5 million, with over 2 billion uploaded files, a total volume exceeding 1 PB, and nearly 50 million files processed daily.

Additionally, it is understood that the KuaiPan team has already split off to operate independently within Kingsoft. Yang Gang mentioned that the primary goal of current business development is to increase the user base. The company plans to start providing online storage services in the European, American, and Japanese markets after National Day (October 1st).
