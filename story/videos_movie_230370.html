Story Outline

The Birth of the Black Warrior

Mak Tai Cheung has always had a sharp business mind since he was young, understanding well that only through business can one achieve success. Thus, he worked hard and completed his MBA at Stanford University in the U.S. Afterward, he sought opportunities there.

After graduation, Cheung saw the potential in Ho Man Tin’s company and proactively approached him, offering not to work as an employee but rather as a partner, even if it meant owning just 0.01% of the shares. Impressed by Cheung's uniqueness, Man Tin allowed him to join as a partner. Their relationship became both mentorship and friendship, seamlessly collaborating in acquiring companies to break up and sell, becoming renowned snipers in the American business world.

Later, Cheung became known as the ruthless Black Warrior in the business world, merciless in his dealings. Gradually gaining more power, the differences in opinions between Cheung and Man Tin also increased. For the benefit and development of the company, Cheung decided to fully acquire Man Tin’s company. Unable to bear the blow, Man Tin drove drunk, and Cheung learned that Man Tin’s car lost control and fell into the sea. After the incident, Man Tin's body couldn’t be found, but Cheung firmly believed that Man Tin was dead, feeling immense guilt. Was the value of a deal greater than friendship, or even life? Cheung reflected on everything from the past and decided to no longer be the Black Warrior, withdrawing from the business world. From the newspaper, Cheung learned that Man Tin’s wife and child were struggling financially in Hong Kong. Cheung felt responsible and decided to return to Hong Kong to take care of Man Tin’s family.
