Inadvertently, another semester is approaching the beginning, which means that we and the children will put a period to the small class. The children who cried when they came to the kindergarten last year are about to be promoted to the middle class and become older brothers and sisters. Looking at these lovely children, I can't help but recall the bits and pieces of our life and harvest together in this semester... In order for every child to make progress, this is the motivation for our unremitting efforts. During the semester, we have exhausted our best efforts, poured our highest enthusiasm, and solidly and diligently completed a semester's work.

One: Self-care ability:

The development of young children is realized in activities. Children are the masters of life. Small class life activities provide rich content for all-round development of children. Life activities not only enable children to obtain living skills, but more importantly, experience their own value and gain self-confidence.

1. We use various teaching activities, games, rewards of fruit dolls, etc., to actively guide children to finish their food without leaving any rice grain. And we separate the capable children from the less capable ones for meals, avoiding feeding them in front of many children. For the less capable children, we patiently guide them step by step to eat on their own. We also ask the children with good eating habits, who eat fast and clean, to distribute the spoons, thereby encouraging other children to eat cleaner and faster. Now the dining order and habits of the children in our class have improved greatly.

2. As the weather gradually warms up, the children wear fewer clothes, so we encourage them to take off and put on pants and shoes by themselves. When going to the toilet, they try to pull up their elastic waistband pants by themselves, then let the teacher check after pulling them up. Gradually, the children have all learned to pull up their pants. During nap time, they also know how to take off their pants by themselves. While the children are napping, we also turn their pants the right way and place them beside their beds. After waking up, we guide each child to put the side with beautiful patterns in front and encourage them to wear their pants by themselves. For the less capable children, the teacher helps them put on their pants step by step. Now when the children wake up, as soon as they hear the signal to get up, they quickly put on their pants and shoes, and some even dress themselves.

3. At home, children usually have their fruits peeled by their parents and are fed by them, so they face challenges in group life. To help children overcome this technical obstacle, we take advantage of the lunchtime fruit-eating opportunity, following the principle of education originating from life, through nursery rhymes, demonstrations, and hands-on learning, enabling children to quickly master this life skill, achieving good results. Now when we eat fruits like apples and bananas with peels, the children can peel them by themselves and put the peels in their bowls, eating fruits neatly and no longer needing help from teachers or assistants.

Children in the baby class are just in the key stage of "I do it myself". Teachers should be good at letting go and allowing children to try, explore, and experience. Letting children do what they want to do, though it may be troublesome and not as convenient as adults doing everything for them, this opportunity is precisely the process of their learning and development.
