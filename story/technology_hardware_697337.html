Correction Method: Approximately 75% of clubhead speed during the downswing comes from the rotation of the wrists, with another 11% coming from shoulder rotation. Therefore, 86% of clubhead speed originates from the rotation of the shoulders and wrists. Let your hands rotate the wrists during the downswing. This will unleash more power and improve hitting accuracy.

Right Figure: Moving your head slightly toward the back foot places your chest behind the ball, preventing reverse weight transfer, resulting in powerful hits with good contact.

Swing Practice: Tie a tie. When aiming, if the tie hangs vertically downward, it should point directly to your toes. When you reach the top of your backswing, the tie should point to the inside of your right foot. If your head remains stationary, the tie will point to the inside of your left foot because your chest won't move behind the ball. If you feel this weight transfer, your hitting power will significantly increase.

Correction Method: When you reach halfway through your backswing, move your head toward your right foot. This will allow your chest to move behind the ball without needing to rotate.

Right Figure: Allowing your left arm to naturally bend during both the strike and reaching the top of the backswing will make it easier for your shoulders to rotate without needing to rotate your hips and legs.

Swing Error: Stretching the left shoulder towards the chest can stop the upper body from rotating and lifting the arms. Conversely, this causes the right shoulder to drop during the downswing. More importantly, the chest's position means that the arms are in front of the chest. When the left arm is straight and the club is behind your back, the chest cannot fully rotate, limiting the range of motion and weight transfer. Typically, this results in a weak swing and inability to cleanly strike the ball.

Correction Method: Keep the left arm naturally bent while addressing the ball and reaching the top of the backswing. This allows the shoulders to avoid dropping or lifting and prevents hip and lower limb instability. Instead, the chest rotates during the strike, helping fully release the club.

Swing Practice: Start aiming at the ball. From the address position, rotate the club until it rests above your right shoulder. Now, complete the backswing so your upper body is behind the ball. This indicates the correct position at the top of the backswing. Simply lift the club slightly off your shoulder to perform the swing and you'll be able to hit the ball.
