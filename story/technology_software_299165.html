Arsenal Lose Five More Players to Injury in Weekend Win; Wenger Calls Current Season Worst for Injuries in 13-Year Tenure

According to Sina Sports, Arsenal ended their losing streak with a 2-0 victory over Stoke City last weekend. However, during the match, Arsène Wenger's already injury-hit squad suffered further losses with five more players succumbing to injuries. Wenger complained that his team’s injury crisis has put them at a disadvantage in the title race, saying their competitors are “ahead from the starting line.”

After 15 rounds of Premier League matches, Wenger's youthful side remains aesthetically pleasing but finds themselves eight points adrift of leaders Chelsea. In Saturday's home game against mid-table Stoke City, Arsenal secured a win but seemed to lack fortune as Cesc Fabregas missed a penalty and Eboue cleared an own-goal off the line. After reviewing the casualties, Wenger discovered five more players had been added to the growing list of injured personnel. According to the Daily Mail, William Gallas and Franck Dja Djédjé suffered hamstring injuries, Eboue strained a muscle, Andrey Arshavin hurt his ankle, and Tomas Rosicky sustained a groin injury.

Before Saturday's match, Arsenal's official website reported that half the first team was already sidelined with eight injured players: Eduardo (thigh), Mathieu Flamini (calf), Nicklas Bendtner (groin), Theo Walcott (hamstring), Gael Clichy (back), Kieran Gibbs (foot), Johan Djourou (knee), and leading striker Robin van Persie who is out for the season due to a serious ankle injury.

Wenger expressed his frustration, noting that Arsenal have been much unluckier than their rivals, stating: “We have far more injuries than other big clubs. There may be reasons for this, but it’s hard to pinpoint one common answer. For example, Rosicky and Eduardo were hampered by long-term injuries, Van Persie picked up his injury on international duty, and Theo Walcott hasn’t played since being tackled in Birmingham. This is the worst season for injuries since I’ve been here (13 years). What makes it worse is that the positions affected overlap. Last year, we lost many defenders to injury, and this year almost every attacking player has been hit.”

Rosicky's latest setback evokes sympathy for his misfortune. The Czech midfielder had already been out for a year and a half due to knee and thigh issues before the start of the season. After rigorous rehabilitation, the 29-year-old returned to the Emirates Stadium, only to face another month-long absence. According to the Daily Mail, Wenger revealed: “He's devastated. It's a vicious cycle. Eduardo didn't play for a year and a half after breaking his leg, and recovery is difficult. Your first or second game back demands a lot physically, and no one can guarantee everything will go smoothly.”

Discussing the January transfer window, Wenger denied rumors of Ruud van Nistelrooy joining and ruled out former captain Patrick Vieira's return to North London: “Vieira is not a forward, and we don't lack midfielders. Song's suspension ends next week, and I believe our midfield can cope. What we lack is firepower upfront.” Uncharacteristically, Wenger admitted they needed reinforcements up front: “I don't know if our situation in January will remain the same. But don't expect me to buy a world superstar to solve all our problems. We need someone eligible to play in the Champions League (i.e., not having played in the competition for another club this season) and preferably available on a free transfer. Looks like we'll have to rely on Santa Claus for a miracle.”

The financially prudent Wenger prefers cost-effective yet high-quality players, but he doesn't need to scour the globe to address his attacking woes. As per Arsenal's official website, new defensive signing Thomas Vermaelen believes teammate Andrey Arshavin can fill the void left by Van Persie. The Russian, playing out of position as a central striker against Stoke City due to a lack of forwards, performed exceptionally well. Originally a contingency plan by the professor, Arshavin opened the scoring and created a penalty, earning the highest post-match rating.

After the game, Vermaelen acknowledged: “Van Persie is an excellent striker, and his absence is tough for us, but we have another top player in Arshavin. He's incredibly fast and scored a beautiful goal today. Our style of play relies on passing, and with him, we can send balls behind the opposing defense and let him exploit those spaces.”

With the league break over, European competition resumes as Arsenal travels to Athens for their final Champions League group-stage match. Having already secured qualification and the top spot in the group, Wenger plans to use the game against Olympiacos as an opportunity to give younger players experience, akin to a League Cup outing. Arsenal's official website reports that 32-year-old Sylvain Wiltord will act as a "silent leader" for the young squad.

Wenger praised his compatriot: “He doesn’t receive enough credit for his attitude and quality. His commitment in last week's League Cup match against Manchester City was absolutely commendable. He's a respected figure in the dressing room. Though quiet, he's a positive leader who always helps the younger players. Of course, the best help is winning games.”

Wednesday's Champions League fixture won't be the first for the former Manchester United player but could be a debut experience for some Arsenal youngsters. Wenger disclosed: “You’ll see Wilshere involved. Last week, we fielded our youth team against Manchester City's first-choice lineup, and for the first hour, we held our own despite a drop in performance later. For this game against Olympiacos, I'll select another young squad. It will be thrilling for the youngsters because the opponent will push hard to secure second place. I hope to prove that our young players can perform better than they did against Manchester City.”
