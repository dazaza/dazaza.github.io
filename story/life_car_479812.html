Car window tinting can cool down your "oven"

"Ovens are all weak! This is real energy saving and pollution-free! Leave it there when you go to work, and it's ready to eat when you get off work!" This is a popular Weibo post that has been wildly re-posted by car owners recently.

In this creative Weibo picture, a car parked in the parking lot is exposed to direct sunlight. The owner placed two boxes of chocolate cookies behind the front windshield. Obviously, the owner wants to use the heat from the car being exposed to the sun, turning the car into an oven.

"Too reliable... next time I'll park my car outside the garage... let's see if it works!" "Will beggars break the window?" In 10 days, the Weibo post was forwarded 8,225 times, and netizens left thousands of comments like these.

The temperature inside the car during summer has always been a long-standing problem for car owners. Although the high temperature may not be enough to bake cookies, it is certainly enough to make people sweat profusely.

During the dog days of summer, apart from the rising temperatures, car sunshades, heat-insulating sun protection covers, car window films, and quick cooling agents have also become popular. According to Ms. Wu, the manager of Jinhua Cap Auto Accessories Store, car window tinting actually helps reduce the interior temperature of the car. In the past two months, many car owners have come for window tinting, and during busy periods, they tint over 60 cars per month.

Experts say that the burning sensation and ultraviolet radiation that drivers feel inside the car mostly come from the front windshield. Over time, driving a car without a front windshield film will easily cause sunburn, tanning, and even increase the risk of skin cancer due to prolonged exposure to ultraviolet rays from the sun. Moreover, under constant exposure to ultraviolet rays, the car’s interior gradually fades, ages, and cracks.

First, what are the functions of car window tinting?

Mr. Wang from Jinhua Cap Auto Service Co., Ltd., who has been working in car window tinting for more than ten years, introduced that car window tinting generally has several functions:

1. Change the color of the glass, enhancing its aesthetics.

2. Reduce the harm caused by shattered car windows. It also has a certain theft prevention effect.

3. Provide heat insulation. The heat insulation rate of car window films can reach over 50%, effectively reducing the usage of car air conditioning, saving fuel, and improving air conditioning efficiency.

4. Block harmful ultraviolet rays. Car window films can effectively block ultraviolet rays, protecting passengers' skin from UV damage and slowing down the aging of the car's interior.

5. Side windshield films have a one-way light transmission function, increasing privacy for passengers.

Second, why can car window tinting provide heat insulation?

Sunlight is divided into the strongly penetrating ultraviolet spectrum, the visible light spectrum that does no direct harm to the human body, and the infrared spectrum that contains the most energy. Various bands of near and far infrared rays constitute 53% of solar energy, while ultraviolet rays account for 3% and visible light accounts for 44%.

Car window films are made by vacuum spraying or magnetron sputtering technology, creating multi-layered, dense, highly heat-insulating metal film layers from metals such as aluminum, gold, copper, and silver. When illuminated by light waves, the electric field of the light wave causes free electrons in the metal material to absorb the light's energy and produce oscillations at the same frequency as the light, then reflect light with the same frequency as the original light.

High-quality car window films can reflect infrared rays, so the temperature inside the car is much lower, which reduces the load on the air conditioner and saves fuel.

Third, how to choose car window tinting?

"The simplest way is to just block the sun with the glass that has the film, and use your face or hand to feel its heat insulation effect." Mr. Wang recommends this simple method to car owners.

Additionally, the higher the transparency of the film, the better; the better the heat insulation effect, the better; the stronger the anti-ultraviolet ability, the better; the thicker and harder the film, the relatively better quality. These four criteria can also serve as references for choosing car window films.

After clearly understanding the methods to judge the quality of the film, car owners can select suitable car window films for their beloved cars based on their own needs within their acceptable price range.
