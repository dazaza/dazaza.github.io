Drinking with the boss - the tricks of drinking with your leader

Trick 1: Although at the dinner table, "if you have deep feelings, drink it all in one gulp; if you have shallow feelings, just lick it", you should never say this phrase while drinking.

Trick 2: Conceal your abilities and wait for the right moment to act. Never try to show off as soon as you sit down at the table.

Trick 3: After the leaders have finished drinking among themselves, it's then your turn to toast them.

Trick 4: It is acceptable for multiple people to toast one person, but never for one person to toast multiple people unless you are the leader.

Trick 5: If you're toasting someone else and not clinking glasses, how much you drink can depend on the situation, such as the other person's alcohol tolerance or their attitude towards drinking. You must never drink less than the other person since you are the one offering the toast.

Trick 6: If you're toasting someone and do clink glasses, say, "I'll finish mine, you drink as you please," to show magnanimity.

Trick 7: If you hold a lower position, remember to frequently refill the leader's glass. Don't blindly offer to take drinks for the leader. If you do, make sure the leader really wants someone to take the drink, and pretend that you want to drink rather than taking the drink for the leader. For example, if Leader A cannot handle their liquor, you can subtly stop those who intend to toast Leader A.

Trick 8: When raising your glass (beer glass), hold the glass with your right hand and support it from below with your left hand. Remember that your glass should always be lower than others'. If you are the leader, know your place and don't hold your glass too low, otherwise, how can those below you maintain propriety?

Trick 9: If there are no special guests present, when clinking glasses, follow the clockwise order to avoid favoritism.

Trick 10: When clinking glasses and toasting, have something to say.

Trick 11: Do not talk business at the table. If everyone drinks well, the deal is almost done, and everyone knows it. Otherwise, why would they drink openly with you?

Trick 12: Do not act arrogantly, say the wrong things, or do the wrong things. Do not argue; instead, voluntarily punish yourself by drinking.

Trick 13: Suppose, purely suppose, there isn't enough alcohol. Place the bottle in the middle of the table and let people serve themselves. Don't foolishly go around pouring drinks one by one, otherwise, what will those who come later do without any alcohol?

Trick 14: There will definitely be one last silent toast at the end, so don't let your glass be empty. It's inevitable.

Trick 15: Be mindful not to lose control after drinking. Do not talk nonsense, boast, lose composure, spit everywhere, wildly throw your chopsticks around, point fingers recklessly, slurp your soup loudly, or fart and burp. If you can't help it, excuse yourself to the restroom, no one will stop you.

Trick 16: Do not repeatedly say, "I can't drink" (if you actually can). This way, others won't accuse you of being insincere. Whether you believe it or not, whether someone can drink or not is quite obvious.

Trick 17: If the leader drinks with you, it's giving you face. No matter how much the leader asks you to drink, finish your drink first as a sign of respect. Remember: use both hands, and keep your glass lower.

Trick 18: Peanuts are a good thing for drinkers. Staying sober and showing concern after drinking is essential. Offering a cup of yogurt, a cup of hot water, or a warm towel shows your thoughtfulness.
