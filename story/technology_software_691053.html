Woman坚持searching for her separated son for 19 years

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-05 14:03:01

According to our agency, reported by Luosujing and intern Yuxuan: "I was forced to be separated from my son for 19 years. I really want to see him again. Regardless of whether he recognizes me as his mother or not, I want to know how he is doing now." Yesterday, Ms. Zhang, who rushed to our agency from Ningde City, Fujian Province, told the reporter about her experience of searching for her son.

Ms. Zhang told the reporter that in 1992, her ex-husband Mr. Xiao brought their 3-year-old son to work at Jiangxi Wire Factory in Nanchang City. When Ms. Zhang came to Nanchang to visit the two, she found that her son had disappeared and her ex-husband did not tell her whether the child was given away or sold. Ms. Zhang said that after that, her ex-husband never returned to his hometown in Fujian, and the Jiangxi Wire Factory had also moved away, making it impossible for Ms. Zhang to contact her ex-husband. It was not until 2003 that Ms. Zhang heard that someone might know the whereabouts of her child, so she found this informant, but the informant denied knowing anything. CCTV's "Touching China" paid special tribute to Bai Fangli. Ms. Zhang stated that during these 19 years, she has never given up searching for her child. Whenever she has time, she rushes from Fujian to Nanchang to look for her son.

"The child's name before the age of three was Bao Sijun (the same surname as Ms. Zhang's father), born in 1989, should be 22 years old this year, with no birthmarks or obvious characteristics on his body," Ms. Zhang said that she has no other purpose now, only wanting to see her child and find out how he is doing.
