1. Eyes: The main component of the cornea is collagen, which decreases with age causing dryness and fatigue in the eyes, poor transparency of the cornea, hardening of elastic fibers, clouding of the lens, and cataracts.

2. Teeth: Teeth contain calcium components, and collagen allows calcium to bind with bone cells preventing loss. With age, collagen loss in teeth causes calcium loss leading to loose teeth, cavities, gum disease, and eventual tooth loss.

3. Cardiovascular System: Collagen loss reduces vessel elasticity affecting blood pressure stability; thickened blood can cause fatty liver, high blood pressure, slow circulation, angina, chest pain, inability to exercise strenuously, poor absorption and metabolism, and cardiovascular diseases; memory loss, dizziness, forgetfulness, insomnia, and vivid dreams may also occur.

4. Breasts: The role of collagen in breast enhancement is well known. Breasts are mainly composed of connective and fat tissues, and firm breasts rely heavily on the support of connective tissue, of which collagen is a major component.

5. Stomach and Intestines: Collagen in the stomach inhibits the coagulation of food proteins caused by gastric acid. Collagen loss leads to symptoms like heartburn, bloating, belching, gastric spasm, pain, stomachache, gas, constipation, poor digestion, retained stool, gastritis, intestinal noise, acne on the forehead, thin body shape, or obesity.

6. Detoxification and Metabolism: Lymph nodes contain large amounts of collagen, and its loss results in toxin accumulation causing yellowish skin, roughness, flakiness, fat granules, swelling, itching, pain, spots, pimples, severe acne, decay, and even skin cancer; poor metabolism can also lead to constipation, obesity, acidity, organ decline, kidney and liver metabolic disorders, nephritis, and even renal failure.

7. Bones: 70-80% of organic matter in bones is collagen, referred to as "bones within bones." New collagen formation supports breast enhancement. Collagen loss or deficiency can cause osteoporosis. Tendons connecting bones and muscles are 80% collagen, and their weakness results from collagen deficiency.

8. Joints: 50% of joint cartilage is collagen. As collagen decreases with age, joint pain, arthritis, reduced flexibility, stiffness, back pain, shoulder rigidity, bone growths, blocked meridians, thickened back fat, weather-related bone pain, deformity, rheumatism, muscle atrophy, body shape changes, cold hands and feet, limb numbness, restricted movement, and ligament elasticity loss occur.

9. Nervous System: The nervous system belongs to connective tissue (mainly collagen). Collagen loss leads to memory decline, lack of concentration, insomnia, anxiety, depression, irritability, menopausal symptoms, decreased reaction ability, and neuralgia.

10. Hair: Hair health depends on scalp nutrition supplied by collagen in the dermis layer, controlling hair thickness, elasticity, and moisture. Collagen loss causes dryness, breakage, baldness, split ends, gray hair, and increased dandruff.

11. Skin: 80% of the dermis is collagen. Its loss with age causes skin laxity, more wrinkles, drooping cheeks, jawline, and eye corners, double chin, ear area fat accumulation, dryness, sensitivity, broken collagen fibers, decreased elasticity, rough keratin, enlarged pores, excessive oil, severe pigmentation.

12. Nails: Collagen in the dermis supplies nutrients to nails. Its loss makes nails brittle and dull.

13. Organs: All internal organs are affected by collagen. Lack thereof impacts organ health and can cause various diseases. Supplementing collagen assists organ nutrition and function, effectively preventing illness. Major organs and fascia contain abundant collagen, whose loss with age causes organ prolapse and weakens cardiac pumping ability leading to anemia and decreased physical function.

14. Endocrine System: Women are prone to hormonal imbalance due to higher collagen consumption. After menstruation, the uterus needs repair involving collagen fibers. Childbirth and abortions further deplete collagen, potentially causing hormonal imbalance and premature aging. Studies show collagen peptides regulate hormones restoring balance, enhancing metabolism, promoting vitality, delaying aging, postponing menopause, improving microcirculation, clearing burdens, and inhibiting fat pigment increase.

15. Reproductive System: Both male and female reproductive systems contain significant collagen. Loss with age causes uterine prolapse, urinary incontinence, ovarian shrinkage, low reproductive immunity, vaginal looseness, pelvic pain, lack of sexual activity, abdominal fat, stretch marks, poor anal muscle contraction, painful defecation, and hemorrhoids.

16. Immune System: Lymphatic channels composed of collagen transport lymph fluid. Collagen loss with age slows lymph circulation causing low immunity, susceptibility to infectious diseases, muscle soreness, and fatigue.
