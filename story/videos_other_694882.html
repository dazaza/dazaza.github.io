The "Doomsday Plane" of the United States, with the military designation E-4B, belongs to the 55th Flying Team of the U.S. Air Force. There are currently a total of four aircraft. Its main function is to protect the President, Defense Secretary and other military and political leaders in the event of a catastrophic disaster facing the United States. It can even temporarily move the government and military headquarters' capabilities into the air. The plane costs $223 million and is modified from a Boeing 747 passenger aircraft. It can carry 112 people. Moreover, the fuel tank capacity of this aircraft is astonishing, reaching 185 tons, allowing it to stay airborne for several days without needing refueling. The "Doomsday Plane" also has electronic equipment weighing 165,000 pounds and a tightly sealed protective shell, designed against nuclear radiation, which is sufficient to ensure that the personnel inside the cabin will not be affected by a nuclear bomb blast. In an interview, Captain Scott Red said that there is more than one "Doomsday Plane," each of which is on standby 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Senior Master Sergeant Joey Stoltz of the U.S. Air Force stated: "Give us any phone number, no matter when or where, as long as it's on Earth, we can go and pick up specific personnel." "We can even maintain contact with submarine crew deep underwater and deploy a five-mile-long rope from the rear compartment to connect with the submarine." Although the ride may not be very comfortable, the U.S. military developed this type of aircraft as early as January 1980 but never disclosed it to the public.
