Missing You: My Beloved Grandpa

Once again, it's the third day of the second lunar month. Once again, it's a season when spring begins to sprout. And once again, it's a time that brings sorrow. On this very day last year, I lost my beloved grandpa. The trees didn't even have time to bud before he left us for another paradise.

His departure was so sudden that I didn't even have the chance to see him off. The air was filled with the sorrowful atmosphere of parting. No one noticed the peach blossoms in the courtyard were about to bloom. Relatives were all hugging each other and crying, grieving over the loss of their dear loved one. That heart-wrenching pain made it hard for me to breathe, and my uncontrollable tears drowned my heart.

Today is the anniversary of Grandpa's passing, and my heart is aching again. I can't help but remember Grandpa's appearance, his white hair, his kind face, his rough and shriveled hands... In my memory, Grandpa was always so tall, but I don't remember which time I went home and suddenly found that Grandpa wasn't as tall anymore. His back began to curve, and his steps became slow and unsteady... At that moment, I realized that Grandpa had grown old.

I still remember the day Grandpa was laid to rest. It was probably around dawn when light drizzle started falling from the sky. By morning, the rain grew heavier, and by the time the coffin was sealed, tears and rainwater were mixed together, indistinguishable. I cried out in my heart, "Heaven, please rain harder! Let me keep Grandpa here. I beg you, let Grandpa stay with us a little longer. I only have one grandpa; let me stay with him a while longer." We were always busy while Grandpa was alive, and the number of times we went home during the year could be counted on one hand. The time we spent with him was so scarce. Now we're all back, but he has left us. He couldn't hear our calls; he lay there peacefully, without any response. The mud soaked my clothes and froze my heart. I was afraid to see the fresh soil on Grandpa's grave. Beneath that soil lies my beloved and respected grandpa. At that moment, I hid in the corner of the room and cried. No amount of crying could bring Grandpa back to us. He has left us forever; I no longer have a grandpa. After Grandpa was buried, the rain stopped. Looking through the window of the room where Grandpa used to live, I saw peach blossoms scattered on the ground, petals drifting with the wind. All the petals seemed to be crying. They seemed to lament why their lives were so short, why they couldn't smell the fragrance of spring, experience the heat of summer, taste the flavors of autumn, or see the whiteness of winter. But these were just their dreams, like Grandpa's reluctance to leave.

As flowers fall and bloom again, it's once more the season of blooming and fading. Grandpa will never see the peach blossoms fill the yard again, and our calls will receive no response. All that remains is the empty house. The owner of the house will never return. Thick layers of dust slowly cover those traces, and no one wants to touch these sorrowful memories.

Looking at Grandpa's portrait, the faint smile reveals the wrinkles so clearly. Suddenly, I realize that these are the marks of an elder. I forgot that Grandpa was an elder; in fact, he had been an elder for a long time. However, his robust body didn't seem like that of an elderly person. Still, he couldn't withstand the ravages of time. After enduring storms and hardships, he succumbed to the passage of years. No one could hold onto him, and he left us just like that.

Facing Grandpa's portrait, I ask: "Grandpa, are you doing well? Are there peach blossoms like the ones in our family's courtyard in heaven? Is it spring where you are? Are there people to chat with you there? Are you lonely there? Are you comfortable there... Do you know, we all miss you. Today is your anniversary, and we've all come back, but you are still gone, just like last year today. We can only stand by your grave and look at your name on the tombstone to think of you. Grass has already grown on the mound of earth. Grandpa, do you know that we've all come to see you? Uncle still lights a cigarette and places it in front of your grave, your favorite smoke. Can you smell that familiar scent?"

Grandpa, please remember us in heaven and don't forget us. When many years pass, we will come to find you. At that time, you will still be my grandpa, and we will still be a family.

Grandpa, we pray for you together. May you rest in peace in heaven!
