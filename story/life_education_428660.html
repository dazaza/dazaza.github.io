Wisdom Education ---- A Detailed Explanation of the Percentage of SSAT Scores

by thatseed123 on 2011-05-10 22:04:12

To enter an excellent American private high school, there's no doubt that SSAT is the most powerful "stepping stone". The SSAT exam (Secondary School Admission Test) is a very important entrance exam for private middle schools in the United States, Canada, and other countries, also known as the American Middle School Entrance Exam. It is independently selected by private schools in the United States, Canada, and other countries. The SSAT includes four major parts: language arts, mathematics, reading, and writing. The writing section is not scored (some schools may consider this score during admission), while the rest are all multiple-choice questions. For each correct answer, 1 point is awarded, and for each wrong answer, 1/4 point is deducted; unanswered questions receive 0 points. Since the score is only related to the number of correct and incorrect answers, knowing when to appropriately skip a question is also a form of wisdom. The Advanced SSAT is aimed at students currently in grades 8-11, with section scores ranging from 500 to 800. The lowest total score is 1500 (i.e., 1500 is the base score), and the highest score is 2400.

So, does a higher SSAT score mean better? The answer is: not necessarily. In fact, the "SSAT percentile" on the SSAT score report, which represents the SSAT percentage, is the key factor for American high schools to admit a student. So, what is the SSAT percentage? What does it signify? Let's first look at a student's score report:

What Does My SSAT Percentile Mean?

Your SSAT percentile is a score between 1 and 99. Your score compares your performance on the test to the performances of other students of the same grade and gender who have taken the SSAT within the past three years. For example, if seventh-grader Sue Smith received a 65 SSAT percentile, she did as well as or better than 65% of the other seventh-grade girls who took the SSAT in the past three years.

The SSAT percentage actually indicates where the student stands compared to all other candidates of the same age and gender globally over the past three years, with a higher percentage indicating greater excellence (1%-99%). As shown in this score report, you might think that scoring over 2000 is good, but in fact, her SSAT percentage is only 48%, meaning that compared to other candidates of the same age and gender over the past three years, her score is only ahead of 48% of them. Similarly, a score of 2000 might result in vastly different percentages for an eighth grader (male/female) versus a tenth grader (male/female). For top American private high schools, the SSAT percentage is generally required to be no less than 60%, and some even require it to be no less than 85%. Conversely, sometimes a student's total score is only 1900, yet their SSAT percentage can reach as high as over 70%. Therefore, do not jump to conclusions based solely on the total score, but rather check your SSAT percentage before making decisions about school selection.

SSAT Exam Precautions:

1. Standard Exam Fee: $215 (Mastercard / VISA), no refunds.

2. Exam Time: 9:00 AM - 12:15 PM (must sign in 30 minutes early and be assigned a seat by the exam center teacher).

3. Score Reports: Free delivery to the candidate’s home (approximately 10 business days after results are available), or an additional $20 fee for the exam center to email the results to the registered email address, or an additional $36 fee for FedEx delivery to your home. Note: SSAT scores, unlike TOEFL, are not published online. For anxious candidates, it might be better to choose one of these two options.

Additionally, the SSAT exam center can send scores to ten schools for free, which can be added or canceled before or after the exam, but must be modified no later than 5 PM EST on the Tuesday of the first week after the exam.

4. Items to Bring: Original passport, 1 copy of the passport (the first page with the photo), 2 identification photos, 2 pencils (HB), eraser, drinking water.
