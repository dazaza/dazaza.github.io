It's just that

by nfhlvpug on 2012-02-21 11:07:50

This is the how manyth one that we've left, do you still remember? Every day after you left, I would draw a big smile on the foggy glass. Maybe if you see it, you will come back to find me.

Written by Mo Xiaoxi

Passing by occasionally, I saw the table and chairs where we used to sit together. The peeling wooden paint. The spot by the window. And the faint marks carved on the table.

Passing by occasionally, I saw the corridor we once walked through together. The fallen leaves. The sun-facing stone bench. And the quiet osmanthus flowers in the distant corner.

All of this is still here. Wrapping around our memories. Quietly. Quietly.

But, you have left. Leaving me all alone. Guarding our memories.

Do you remember, we said we would plant a money tree together in the future, wearing safety helmets and shaking it under the tree to make it drop lots of money.

Do you remember, we said we would stand together in the future, no matter what happens, standing together even if we get beaten up.

Do you remember, we said we would have a lot of children, and I would walk up the stairs with you during your pregnancy so that giving birth wouldn't hurt as much.

In each lonely night, I would look far away at the city where you are, without tears in my eyes, but they were a little red...

I miss the mornings we walked through in summer, shivering as I pass by our familiar lotus pond, imagining the lotus flowers blooming under the dazzling summer sun; every time I reach the doorstep, I would instinctively turn around to wave goodbye to you, but when I turn around in the dark night, I can't see you.

Some flowers bloom over and over again.

Some melt repeatedly.

Some scenes are seen again and again.

Some people, however, cannot pass by again.

Those leaves, withered and withered again.

Those camphor trees, green and green again.

That autumn took a long time to come.

Yet, those things never stopped.

The ageless years.

Lilac fills the entire heaven.

Who is flying?

And who touched whom?

Opening a long-sealed book, I suddenly found a piece I once loved inside.

At the end of the story, Doraemon still left Nobita.

Do you remember?

If, my hand really gets held by someone else's hand, following his steps to wander restlessly, will your spirit dim?

In a certain year and month, I really did seriously search for your unvanished footprints.

Perhaps, at a certain turn. That has already gone far away.

We truly loved deeply once.

Now, there's no trace left to be found.

I'll lock those old stories as you wish and won't remember them anymore.

For a while after you left, my life fell into trouble, and thus, the diary was also quite neglected.

Afterward, I really didn't write about you anymore.

I don't long for you calling me "treasure" over and over again, because, some habits can indeed be changed.

I no longer miss you lightly pulling my arm, because I've already gotten used to shopping alone.

I no longer mourn that small city that once had you.

Just, inexplicably, I suddenly miss so much,

the taste of corn porridge from the shop downstairs.
