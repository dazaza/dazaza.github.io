Sina CEO Charles Chao: Social Media Revolution Still in Early Stage

by anonymous on 2013-10-08 18:19:12

Sina Chairman and CEO Charles Chao delivered the closing remarks of the conference

Beijing, morning news on October 6th - At the Stanford University Graduate School of Business "China 2.0" annual meeting held in California, USA, Sina Chairman and CEO Charles Chao delivered the closing keynote speech of the conference. He stated that Sina Weibo has brought profound changes to Chinese society and the media industry, but this social media revolution is still in its early stages.

Chao mentioned that since its launch four years ago, Sina Weibo has provided the public with a platform to express their own views, and thus has been welcomed by Chinese Internet users. The number of registered Weibo users has grown from zero to over 500 million currently, making it China's most influential social media platform, and it continues to grow.

He pointed out that unlike previous media, Weibo has changed the production, distribution, and sales model of digital media, bringing about a social media revolution. As a social media platform, Weibo has also had a profound impact on Chinese society. Below are some key points from his speech:

Driving Social Transparency and Progress

Weibo has given every netizen their own microphone, allowing them to directly report, publish, and share information. Everyone can become a communicator, expressing their own voice, revealing and exposing the beauty and ugliness of society. With the popularization of mobile Internet, everyone has a smartphone connected to the Internet, and they can all report through Weibo. This makes information control increasingly difficult.

Taking the high-speed train accident two years ago as an example, in the initial stage after the accident, almost all the news came from the Weibo platform, while official media had no related news or pictures. Traditional media such as newspapers and magazines learned about the situation development through Weibo.

In addition, Weibo has provided the public with a platform for participation, forming public opinion through extensive discussions and actively participating in social affairs. Last year's air quality issue gained widespread attention across society through the spread on Weibo, ultimately prompting the government to focus on environmental issues as a top priority.

Likewise, through Weibo, the Guo Meimei incident has pushed Chinese charity organizations to operate more transparently. The micro-public welfare platform launched by Sina Weibo allows Weibo users to create, fund, and supervise public welfare and charity projects, encouraging more public participation in charitable causes.

On the other hand, the Chinese government is also accepting these changes and gradually interacting with the public through the Weibo platform. Currently, there are already over 60,000 government Weibo accounts on Weibo, changing the way the government communicates with the public. Sina is also helping government officials understand, familiarize themselves with, and use Weibo.

Deep Impact on the Media Industry

As a new type of media, Weibo has had the greatest impact on the media industry itself. Currently, more than 60% of breaking news in China is first published on the Weibo platform. Since anyone can become a reporter and communicator, the role of traditional media has gradually shifted to follow-up reporting and analysis. They have also gradually turned to Weibo for information release.

Social media will directly change the generation, distribution, and sales of digital content, bringing a significant impact on the existing model of the digital media industry.

Due to Weibo, the live broadcasting function of television will gradually weaken in the future, but people will participate more in program discussions through social media. Similar to the situation in the United States, popular TV shows like "The Voice of China" and "Happy Boys" have sparked heated discussions on Weibo, generating 500 million Weibo posts and comments related to these programs in the past two months.

Social media also provides a marketing and crowdfunding platform for digital media. The low-budget film "Lost in Thailand" achieved a box office record-breaking 1 billion yuan mainly due to its extensive and active use of the Weibo platform for promotion. In the future, movies and TV series can raise funds through Weibo, making viewers shareholders of digital content.

In the future, films, music, and TV series can also be distributed and sold through Weibo. The newly released micro-reading platform on Weibo allows authors to directly publish and sell book content to readers, changing the existing model of the publishing industry. In the future, Weibo users will not only consist of individuals, businesses, or institutions, but can also include books, movies, music, or locations.

In the concluding part of his speech, Chao emphasized that Weibo is still in the early stages of the social media revolution, and there will be more exciting developments to look forward to in the future. Sina hopes to be a part of this revolution, and the goal of Weibo is to help people access and share information more easily, thereby promoting social progress.
