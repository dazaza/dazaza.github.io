Shengda First Promotes Gamified Enterprise Management: Employees Get Promotions and Salary Increases by Levelling Up

by keyingi16 on 2010-04-18 08:05:45

Editor's note: China's biggest company has emerged. According to revelations from employees inside Shanda, the company has started promoting a "game-style management" model internally. In the future, Shanda employees will be like characters in online games who gain experience points by "leveling up". When they reach a certain level, they will "automatically" get opportunities for promotion or salary increase.

"All future salary adjustments and promotions for Shanda employees will be determined by experience points," explained Zhang Yanmei, senior vice president of Shanda. "In the past, when to promote or give a raise was decided by supervisors or the HR department. Now, it is decided by 'experience points'." She said that as long as you are truly excellent talent, you can stand out under this system, which is unique within the industry.

According to introductions, the core of Shanda's "game-style management" is that all employees will have their own "experience points" within the company. "Just like characters in a game, employees' daily performance and work achievements will be faithfully recorded by their experience points."

Gee Hui, spokesperson for Shanda, stated that the idea of "game-style management" has been brewing internally at Shanda for nearly a year. "We spent six months building the mathematical model for calculating experience points and have now formed dual development sequences for professional and managerial positions, with each level corresponding to different experience points."

Moreover, Gee Hui indicated that to align with game-style management, Shanda's internal information system has also been fully integrated with game-style management. All employees can now check and manage their own experience points in real time.

Shanda's internal employees introduced that experience points are divided into two parts: "time experience points" and "project experience points". The former is like leveling up in a game where, as long as no mistakes are made, experience points naturally increase over time. The latter is akin to "completing quests" or "running dungeons" in games, where more experience points are earned on a project basis.

"This not only strengthens employees' sense of responsibility but also encourages them to fully utilize their talents. The more projects they participate in, the greater their contribution to the company, and the faster their experience points increase," said Zhang Yanmei.

Chen Tianqiao, chairman of Shanda, once pointed out in an internal meeting: "We should manage our company like we manage games, and serve our employees like we serve users." It is reported that Shanda is the first company in the world to propose "game-style management", and several domestic and international universities have expressed interest in conducting follow-up studies.
