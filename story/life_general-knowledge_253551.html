Grand View of Wooden Material Decoration

by longersing on 2009-10-29 16:56:37

A grand view of wooden material decoration, including ceiling, wall decoration, raised floor, plywood, screens, wooden cabinets, etc., are all made of wood. As a prelude to decoration, you must understand this knowledge.

1. Door set (door panel, door frame)

Select a flat door panel with melamine board and match it with the finished door frame. Hanging a painting on the door is an economical, beautiful, and personalized approach. The best way to maintain the door panel is to dust it with a feather duster or wipe it with clear water. It is not recommended to use cleaning agents for maintenance as the chemical components can easily cause discoloration of the surface.

2. Ceiling

The most common materials for ceilings can be divided into plywood, calcium silicate board, oxidized board, and light steel frame ceilings. Their shapes are mainly divided into flat, flat shape, irregular surface, irregular shape, etc. Indirect lighting-style ceilings are very common nowadays, and a few people may choose to apply veneer or solid wood treatment. The price difference can be quite significant.

3. Wall Decoration

Many people like to use woodwork to decorate or embellish walls, such as bedheads, TV cabinet backs, sofa backs, and corridors. Woodwork decorative walls are mainly divided into: flat shape, irregular surface, irregular shape, exterior veneer, or embedded calligraphy/paintings/glass/mirrors, etc.

4. Raised Floor

Whether in the bedroom, study cum guest room, children's room, or even the balcony extension, raised floors have become the mainstream of many home designs. They not only have practical functions but can also replace traditional partitions with different heights to achieve space segmentation. Raised floors can be divided by complexity into: flat raise under 15 cm, stepped shape, irregular shape, additional storage compartments under the raised area, and raised floors with adjustable tables. The surface can be veneered or fitted with flooring, etc.

5. Partition (Plywood, Calcium Silicate Board)

Unlike concrete partition walls, woodwork partitions are thinner and more affordable. For small spaces or spaces with less flexible dimensions, they are a good choice. Since the main function of partitions is to separate different spaces, soundproofing is crucial. Therefore, whether to add soundproof cotton should be given special attention. Most woodwork partitions are flat. Materials can be divided into plywood, calcium silicate board, calcium oxide board, light steel frame partitions, cement boards, etc.

6. Screen

Whether used to separate spaces or as a feng shui barrier, compared to regular partitions, screens have a lightweight characteristic. They were initially used at entryways and before bathrooms, so the screen design often leaves the first impression upon entering. In terms of design, it occupies a very important position. Screens are mainly divided into flat shapes, irregular shapes, woodwork with embedded glass, or surfaces decorated with calligraphy/paintings, etc.

7. Woodwork Cabinets

Cabinets made by carpenters belong to those with better quality and more varied shapes in decoration. The biggest difference in woodwork cabinets lies in the treatment of the cabinet surface, which not only affects the shape but also the price. According to functionality, they can be classified as wardrobes, bookshelves, dining cabinets, storage cabinets, desks, seat cabinets, shoe cabinets, and shaped cabinets. Appearances can be further divided into veneered, non-veneered, flat, and shaped cabinets, etc. The interior is usually made of smooth-surfaced so-called "polyboard." If veneering is required, it will be labor-intensive, and the price will increase one to two times accordingly. Regardless of whether there is a special shape, the function of cabinets remains the same. To achieve cost-effective and aesthetic results, consider designing shaped cabinets in the living room and entrance hall while opting for flat cabinets with good storage functions inside the rooms. This way, you can have both appearance and practicality.
