After experiencing a crazy round of signings in the summer,

by fevqqkvv on 2012-03-09 07:57:50

Ibrahimovic, after joining the Rossoneri, quickly became a pillar of the team's front line. Before the winter break, Ibrahimovic (only including league matches, same below) played in 16 games, all as a starter, and completed the full match in 13 of them. He scored 9 goals and provided 7 assists. Especially when Pato was injured and his condition was uncertain, Ibrahimovic led the team to continuous successes with his personal ability, making significant contributions to AC Milan becoming the first-half champion of Serie A this season. Compared to the Swedish forward, Robinho had less playing time but performed equally well, scoring 6 goals in 15 appearances for 972 minutes. Another summer signing, Bojan Krkic, also performed excellently, appearing in 15 games and contributing 2 goals and 1 assist. Looking at the entire season, the combination of transfers involving Bojan Krkic + Ibrahimovic + Robinho brought a total of 29 goals and 16 assists to AC Milan, accounting for nearly half of the team's current goal tally of 61! After 7 years, AC Milan successfully reclaimed the title, winning the club's 18th league championship. This success is not only due to coach Allegri's balanced tactics but also related to the decisive command of the Rossoneri's management in the transfer market. It was precisely the successful signings by Berlusconi, Galliani, and others this season that enabled the rebirth of the Milan dynasty.

The shining success of AC Milan's recruitment operations has made Inter Milan feel ashamed. Berlusconi achieved great things with minimal spending. After a tough period with zero investment, Berlusconi finally opened the long-sealed wallet and injected new energy into AC Milan this season. In late August last year, AC Milan signed Zlatan Ibrahimovic from Barcelona on loan. Soon after, "Bicycle King" Robinho also arrived at San Siro, and Ghanaian international Kevin-Prince Boateng, who shone brightly at the South Africa World Cup, joined AC Milan via a loan-to-buy deal. These three transfer deals orchestrated by Berlusconi ultimately brought about transformative changes for AC Milan.

After experiencing the frenzy of summer signings, holding the victory of the first-half league championship gave Berlusconi a taste of the benefits. When the winter transfer window opened, AC Milan once again followed the same strategy, successfully snatching Cassano from their city rival Inter Milan, then recruiting winger Emmanuelson from Ajax. Additionally, Dutch midfielder Mark van Bommel joined for free from Bayern Munich. The arrival of the bad boy further solidified AC Milan's dominant position this season. Especially when Pato was injured and unavailable, and Ibrahimovic was banned from playing, the partnership of Cassano and Robinho delivered surprising performances, securing key victories to help the team maintain its lead in the standings. Furthermore, AC Milan achieved its first five-game winning streak this season, successfully claiming the championship trophy. Meanwhile, Van Bommel quickly won over the San Siro crowd with his performances; his addition was like providing an unprecedented iron gate for the Rossoneri. The Dutch wing's performance was equally outstanding, and although head coach Allegri did not give him much playing time, it is believed that Emmanuelson will find his place in the near future.

The six major recruitment strategies across the summer and winter seasons (Ibrahimovic, Robinho, Boateng, Cassano, Van Bommel, Emmanuelson) brought about a qualitative change in AC Milan, allowing them to successfully ascend to the throne and win the club's 18th Italian top-flight league title. Apart from these six key players, Berlusconi also brought in Lerrotaile, Amelia, Papastathopoulos, Yepes, and Dida among others, bringing the total number of signings to 11, with a total expenditure of just 42 million euros, relatively economical. Undoubtedly, after tasting the success of these signings, Berlusconi should continue to make moves this summer. After all, AC Milan's midfield configuration has severely aged, and many veterans such as Pirlo have chosen to leave upon the expiration of their contracts to join other teams. Therefore, if everything goes according to plan, Berlusconi will bring in some new players to ensure the team's lineup completeness, thereby thoroughly putting the Rossoneri back on the path to revival. On the contrary, Inter Milan, after being inactive last summer, only introduced Ranocchia from Genoa for 12.5 million euros when the winter transfer window opened. If Moratti does not take any action this summer, the once glorious Inter Milan could potentially start declining, facing mockery from their city rivals AC Milan.

It is worth noting that these three signings did not cost Berlusconi too much money. Boateng was borrowed from Genoa, and Ibrahimovic's 24 million euro transfer fee is only payable next summer. The only expense was the 18 million euros spent on Robinho from Manchester City, but before this transaction, Milan already had 14 million euros from the sale of Huntelaar, meaning Milan only actually spent a mere 4 million euros on this deal.

AC Milan's extensive winter recruitment has allowed them to dominate the league henceforth.
