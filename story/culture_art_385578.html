English Remedial Study Method for Score Improvement and Final Sprint Close to the College Entrance Examination

Students with poor English scores who want to catch up before the college entrance exam should refer to the score-improving techniques summarized by experts on the ks5u College Entrance Exam Resource website. The best method is to thoroughly review the key grammar points of high school. In fact, these are not only the focus points for single-choice questions but also frequently appear in error correction and cloze tests, so they cannot be overlooked. However, given the weak foundation and limited time, a more clever method is to buy a set of real college entrance exam English papers from previous years, complete all the single-choice questions, then correct them yourself. Write down the incorrect ones in a dedicated error notebook, striving to understand each question thoroughly. If you don't understand, it's recommended to ask teachers or classmates with good English skills. It's not enough to just know the answers; the key is to understand the knowledge points being tested. Since the college entrance exam doesn't present identical questions, but the test points remain the same, it’s important to grasp the essence. If there is extra time, doing some mock exams can also help, similar to the methods used by top scorers. Additionally, if conditions allow, hiring a tutor can be very effective as teachers can highlight and analyze the key test points, potentially leading to twice the results with half the effort. For error correction, it's recommended to practice regularly, much like finding differences in QQ games. With more practice, inspiration will come naturally, and your eyes will become sharper. Reiterating again, an error notebook is really effective, as ks5u College Entrance Exam Resource website experts have done this and benefited greatly. If there are truly incomprehensible parts, just memorize the correct options and ignore the wrong ones. For understood questions, no further effort is needed. Keep going!

For the error notebook, just record the correct answers to avoid confusion. You can also highlight the correct option among the four choices with a fluorescent pen for emphasis. Depending on how well you understand the analysis of each question, you can add small notes such as the sentence pattern or fixed collocations used, making everything clear. Detailed or brief notes depend on your preference. ks5u College Entrance Exam Resource website experts suggest creating some small icons, such as asterisks or checkmarks, to indicate the importance or mastery level of each question. Make markers that you can easily understand. Once fully mastered, you can cross them out with a pen, saving time when reviewing later.

It's best to memorize the verbs in the relatively important textbook during this holiday period. This holiday should be enough time to memorize them. After the holiday, continue to allocate time daily for memorization until the college entrance exam, gradually accumulating a significant vocabulary. Words are crucial, and cannot be memorized in one day, so it’s essential to learn a little every day. Especially for verbs, their usage must be firmly grasped.

The key to achieving high scores in the college entrance exam lies in reading comprehension.

Carefully and meticulously complete the reading comprehension exercises provided by the school and supplementary materials assigned by the teacher. Be sure not to look up unfamiliar words while doing the exercises, focusing instead on answering correctly. After completing the exercises, check the answers, carefully analyzing where mistakes were made and why the correct answer was chosen.

Then, transcribe the previously marked words into a notebook. While re-memorizing all the words may be difficult now, the words appearing in the readings are likely to be tested, making this process somewhat focused. Here, ks5u College Entrance Exam Resource website experts emphasize prioritizing the memorization of verbs, though adjectives are also important due to their influence on sentence meaning.

Cloze tests primarily involve grammar and vocabulary. The knowledge gained during the vocabulary memorization process will be helpful here. Moreover, understanding and transcribing the knowledge points involved in the cloze tests you've done, followed by regular review, will also be beneficial.

For single-choice questions, maintain an error notebook, recording all incorrectly answered questions from now on. There's no need to note which option (A, B, C, D) was selected; simply write the correct answer directly into the book. Review these notes when you have time, avoiding writing down the wrong answers to prevent deepening their impression. Single-choice questions require consistent effort, so recording errors from now on is key. If a question involves particularly important knowledge points, note them beneath the sentence. Only record what you consider important, not every question.

In summary, the key is to focus on the frequently appearing important test points encountered in problem-solving. Complete the tasks arranged by teachers seriously and ensure full understanding. The questions in the college entrance exam are simpler than usual. Never give up; hopefully, what I've said will be helpful. Persisting until the college entrance exam will definitely lead to improvement, as the key points are limited.

ks5u College Entrance Exam Resource website experts say that English proficiency cannot be improved in just a few days; it requires cumulative effort over time. First, maintain a certain vocabulary size. Second, read more articles, ideally memorizing some excellent articles, paragraphs, and sentences. Most importantly: boldly speak English, don’t be shy, and don’t refrain from speaking due to fear of making mistakes, which is a major taboo in practicing oral skills. Don’t fear making mistakes, even if the grammar is incorrect, still speak boldly.

Persistence is key, keep going without giving up!
