Due to the off-season for tourism - price cuts in the tourism industry before and after the Spring Festival

by longersing on 2010-02-03 16:07:38

Due to the off-peak season for tourism - the period around the Spring Festival sees a wave of price reductions in tourism, but it is also the best time for conference tourism. Before the Spring Festival, there is a peak in annual incentive travel for large enterprises, so there will inevitably be a new wave of opposing sides in tourism. The off-peak season for traditional tourism has led to price cuts by various travel agencies, while at this time, conference tourism reception is booming.

The hot registration for the Spring Festival tours caught many travel agencies off guard, and many routes had to urgently add more flight seats, causing the prices of some popular routes to soar to record highs. According to reports from tourism experts yesterday, the price of Spring Festival tours this year will reach its peak around Valentine's Day on February 14th, and after the festival, the maximum price drop could be up to 60%. In particular, the decline in post-festival airfare and hotel prices is most significant, making it the best time for travelers to go out.

"Why are the prices rising again? Ms. Zhu, who was preparing to go on an island tour, found that the price of her trip kept skyrocketing. "Look at this one going to Cebu; it was originally around 5000 yuan, but now it's over 6000!"

It is understood that the enthusiasm of citizens for Spring Festival tours this year is extremely high, which caused the pre-purchased flight seats by travel agencies to be sold out two weeks earlier. To accommodate the continuing influx of registrants, travel agencies had to temporarily add more flight seats. At this time, airlines saw the "business opportunity" and raised the discount prices of group tickets, leading to a rise in travel costs.

I saw that the price of Cebu and Boracay lines during the off-season was only over 2000 yuan, but this year during the Spring Festival, it soared to over 6000 yuan, with an increase of up to 200%; the usual price of Australia's Great Barrier Reef is about ten thousand yuan, but this year during the Spring Festival, it rose to over 16000 yuan.

Domestic travel prices are no exception. For example, the room rates of five-star hotels in Sanya have increased by 500%; due to too few normal flights to Beijing, travel agencies had to charter planes, and the return charter flights arrived in Guangzhou at around 2:00 AM.

I found out that the price of flights departing from Guangzhou before the Spring Festival is generally above 80% of the full price. For example, most flights from Guangzhou to Chengdu this week only offer 80% discounted tickets, and all flights will be fully booked a week before the Spring Festival. Flights from Guangzhou to Sanya only offer full-price tickets or first-class/business-class tickets starting at 800 yuan; there are even fewer discounts on flights from Guangzhou to Beijing, with only a few flights offering 85% and 90% discounted tickets.

But avoiding the peak travel period, the ticket prices drop significantly. After the Spring Festival, the discounts on flights departing from Beijing to Changchun, Shanghai, Guiyang, Chongqing, and Chengdu are all within 30% of the original price. Uniquely, routes flying from Shanghai to Shenyang, Qingdao, Dalian, etc., after the Spring Festival even offer 99-yuan super-low tickets.

Hotel prices are similar. For example, after the Spring Festival, Beijing offers special-rate rooms ranging from international youth hostels costing dozens of yuan to four-star hotels costing less than 200 yuan. Over 500 hotels and inns provide accommodations costing less than 200 yuan per night.

It is reported that the Spring Festival travel quotes this year formed two price nodes around February 10th and 17th. Most route quotes began to rise significantly after February 10th, reaching their highest point on February 13th, 14th, and 15th, then dropped significantly after February 17th, and returned to normal levels after February 20th.

For example, a 3-day self-guided tour to Yalong Bay in Sanya departing from Guangzhou: If you depart on February 13th, the minimum cost is nearly 3500 yuan, and on February 14th, it directly rises to close to 4500 yuan. The highest quote appears on February 15th and 16th, starting at 4828 yuan. Starting from February 17th, the price gradually decreases by a thousand yuan per day, and by February 20th, it returns to a starting price of around 1800 yuan.

A 5-day self-guided tour to Phuket departing from Guangzhou: The quote for departure on New Year's Eve is around 4600 yuan, and if you depart on the first day of the New Year, the same route increases by 300 yuan, while the regular price of this route is approximately 2500 yuan.
