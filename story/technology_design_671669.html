Another goldmine of online shopping

by wgyuvs8a on 2012-02-22 11:42:28

It's easy to open an online store, but selling products is a real science. As a result, a large number of new professions have emerged that rely on online shopping, such as online models (internet beans), online store decorators, and store management services, all of which are highly sought after in the market. According to statistics, the transaction volume of Taobao's online shopping service market will reach 12.5 billion yuan in 2012. With such a huge market demand, a large number of "gold prospectors" have flooded into the online shopping supporting service market.

Doing online store "decoration" allowed Xiao Lin to drive a 600,000-yuan Mercedes-Benz sports car back home in Wuhan after five years. Xiao Lin, who graduated from the art department, was like many college students; she worked in a small company after graduation and later opened a small design company, which closed down due to poor performance after half a year. By chance, she heard a colleague say that doing store "decoration" for sellers on Taobao could earn a good income, so Xiao Lin and her computer-savvy husband together opened a decoration shop on Taobao. "At first, we were making three or four thousand yuan a month, now even in bad times we can make over ten thousand yuan a month, and in good times it can reach 200,000 yuan," said Xiao Lin, adding that their family life has changed greatly because of this.

"A lot of people doing online store decoration run husband-and-wife stores, both full-time and part-time," said Xiao Lin. Mr. Liu, a PhD student at a university in Wuhan, is one of those part-timers, "We just come to Taobao for casual work, taking jobs when we have time." Since 2006, Mr. Liu and his girlfriend's income has quadrupled, reaching over 20,000 yuan a month in good times.

According to statistics, the daily transaction volume of Taobao's online store decoration is currently 1.5 million yuan, with an expected total transaction volume of 200 million yuan this year.

Online shopping has given rise to new service professions.

In fact, greater demand lies hidden in every aspect of the seller's sales process. According to the first report released by Taobao titled "Development Report of China's Online Shopping Service Market", the third-party service industries currently used more frequently already cover tool software, store management, quality inspection, marketing, photography models, consulting training, supply chain finance, human resources, etc.

The person in charge of the Han Du Yi She online store on Taobao Mall said that the store needs to launch 50-60 new clothing items every day, and relying solely on its own photographers and models is simply not enough, necessitating the use of "external support". "Finding suitable online models is not easy, it's not just about being pretty; for example, our store needs girls who look cute and friendly," he added. Due to the massive demand, a large number of internet beans and internet bean agents have emerged.

The Express Lane promotion window on Taobao is a tool that helps sellers sell their products. How to convert the large influx of traffic brought by Express Lane into effective purchasing power has become a headache for sellers. This has led to the emergence of Express Lane optimization services, where such service providers set precise keywords for sellers to generate profits. The head of a company specializing in Taobao Express Lane optimization introduced that the company currently has 300 clients, with service fees ranging from 4,000 yuan per quarter to 30,000 yuan per year.

A netizen named Gongxi Facai mentioned that some software programs that help Taobao sellers analyze and retrieve customer data are also very popular. Although they are personalized and have low development value, the income is "still okay." The truly profitable ones are commercial software, which can be widely used, but promoting them is extremely difficult. "First, you need to be accepted by everyone, then you can talk about making money," he added. Therefore, specialized distributors promoting various products have emerged.

In addition, some store owners entrust their entire store to third parties for operation. A company named Da Yuzhou provides similar services for Uniqlo, outsourcing all customer service of Uniqlo's flagship store on Taobao Mall to Da Yuzhou. The company employs more than 70 people dedicated to working for Uniqlo, providing services such as Taobao Wangwang, call center, E-mail, and customer relationship management.

Another gold mine of online shopping

The "Development Report" shows that the transaction volume of Taobao's online shopping service market was 2 billion yuan in 2010, with an estimated transaction volume of 5 billion yuan in 2011, representing a year-on-year growth rate of 150%. At this growth rate, the scale of Taobao's online shopping service market next year will reach 12.5 billion yuan. Among these, tool software is the most used service by sellers, with currently 1.5 million sellers paying for tool software subscriptions, averaging 2.75 subscriptions per seller. These sellers are mainly concentrated between 1 diamond and 4 diamonds, with 4-diamond sellers spending 150 yuan per month using online subscription software tools. Photography and modeling are also among the larger service industries on Taobao.

Large online merchants have smelled the business opportunities in the online shopping service industry. Last year, JD.com, Dangdang.com, and Amazon China successively opened up third-party platforms. According to a platform merchant of JD.com, the main role of the platform merchant is to provide goods, take pictures of the goods, and once the goods enter JD.com's warehouse, all other matters including sales, customer service, after-sales service, and delivery are completed by JD.com. The "decoration" and marketing software services required on Taobao are "basically not needed" on JD.com.

However, the subsequent services provided by JD.com to merchants are not free. To open a flagship store on JD.com, an annual platform usage fee of 6,000 yuan must be paid, along with a certain percentage of category commission, as well as warehousing, delivery, and marketing service fees. JD.com Chairman and CEO Richard Liu publicly stated at the beginning of this year that there is not much difference in profit between JD.com's open platform and self-operated business. After receiving $1.5 billion in financing this year, JD.com stated that almost all of the funds would be used for logistics and technology research and development projects, which are the main services provided by JD.com to its platform merchants.

Regarding this, Zhao Jingqiao, an e-commerce expert at the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, said that there are many small and medium-sized sellers on Taobao, and the price of the accompanying online merchant service products is relatively cheap. Moreover, Taobao's product categories are quite diverse, so the forms of these services are also relatively flexible. Compared to JD.com and Dangdang.com, Taobao involves more online merchant services, which are also more challenging.

The online shopping service industry is favored by capital markets.

The online shopping service industry has also caught the attention of capital markets. Song Anlan, a partner at Softbank China Venture Capital Co., Ltd., pointed out that compared to online merchants themselves who sell goods, the capital market pays more attention to industries that provide services to online merchants, thus "we believe that doing platforms may be more attractive to venture capitalists."

Song Anlan explained that a large portion of e-commerce is so-called online marketing, with approximately 10%-15% of sales revenue allocated to product promotion and other forms of online marketing. Even if some people think there is a bubble, VC money invested means these e-commerce companies will inevitably spend on marketing, and the people on these platforms still make real money. In the future, logistics and payment will all be industries that the capital market focuses on.

It is understood that the online marketing company Yi Ma Union received its third round of financing in July this year, led by Lanxin Asia, with tens of millions of dollars in funding secured. Hangzhou Xi Lang, an e-commerce outsourcing service provider established in April 2009, also completed a new round of financing amounting to tens of millions of yuan this year. Prior to this, Xi Lang had received an investment of tens of millions of dollars from a well-known American private equity fund, as well as angel investments from the heads of four listed companies inside and outside China, including Supor and Hengsheng Electronics. E Dian Bao and Zhejiang Zhongfu, among other e-commerce service providers, have also received funding, expanding investments in the e-commerce industry to all links of the industrial chain.

Industry insiders said that most of the current online merchant service providers are "husband-and-wife stores," largely dependent on Taobao's ecosystem. With the continuous development of e-commerce and the increasing demand of traditional brands for the online market, domestic online merchant service industries will gradually see the emergence of enterprises with scale and strength.
