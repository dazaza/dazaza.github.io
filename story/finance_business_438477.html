Mr. Dewey, an American educator, once said: "Giving a child a certain kind of education symbolizes giving them a certain kind of life!" Yes, the majority of a child's childhood is spent in school. As a teacher, I have always regarded Ye Shengtao's words as my career pursuit: "Teaching aims to reach a point where teaching is no longer necessary; explaining aims to reach a point where explaining is no longer needed." (Friends who want to exchange teaching ideas, please call this number, do not disturb if you're not interested.)

Compilation of Accumulated Wisdom from Grade 1 to Grade 6 in People's Education Curriculum Standard Edition:

Fourth Grade:

1. Read and Memorize (Scenic Couplets):

Green willows stretch for a thousand miles, spring wind warms ten thousand homes.

Yellow orioles sing among emerald willows, purple swallows cut through the spring breeze.

Spring wind dares to comb the willows, night rain secretly waters the flowers.

A gentle breeze turns a thousand mountains green, southern swallows return to bring spring to ten thousand homes.

2. Read and Remember:

Thunder roars, heavy rain pours, sunshine radiates, dances with the wind, fragrance fills the air, black and white intermingle, the sun shines brightly.

Surrounded by mountains, beautiful scenery, lush trees, emerald lake water, historical sites, blurry outlines, twinkling lights.

Mist drizzles, night falls, bright lights illuminate, dazzling gold, shining silver, vivid colors.

Magnificent diversity, renewed appearance, falling from the sky, every possible means.

3. Read and Memorize (Mutual Assistance Proverbs):

Flowers need leaves to support, people need each other's help.

Giving roses leaves a lingering fragrance on your hands.

Sincerity can make stones shed tears, earnestness can make dead wood sprout.

Helping others leads to receiving help in return.

4. "The Twenty-Four Solar Terms Song":

Spring rain awakens the spring, clears the valley and heaven,

Summer fullness connects with Mangzhong and heat,

Autumn begins with dew, autumn chills, and frost descends,

Winter snows, small cold, and great cold.

5. Read and Memorize (Diligence Proverbs):

Knowledge is the wings we use to fly towards the sky.

Thinking can build a bridge that leads us to new knowledge.

Genius is one percent inspiration plus ninety-nine percent perspiration.

The future of science belongs only to the diligent and humble youth.

Fifth Grade:

1. Read and Memorize P32:

Wisdom comes from learning, genius from accumulation. (Lenin)

Nothing in the world is difficult for one who sets his mind to it. (Mao Zedong)

Study for the rise of China. (Zhou Enlai)

Books are the ladder of human progress. (Gorky)

Any achievement is the result of hard work. (Soong Ching-ling)

2. Read and Memorize P49 "Lisheng Du Yun":

Heaven pairs with earth, rain with wind, vast ocean with boundless sky.

Mountain flowers pair with sea trees, red sun with vast heavens.

3. Read and Memorize P64:

In the first month, spinach starts to turn green, in the second month, scallions are planted;

In the third month, leeks grow vigorously, in the fourth month, bamboo shoots sprout after the rain;

In the fifth month, pomegranates bloom like fire, in the sixth month, lotuses fill the pond;

In the seventh month, eggplants hang downward, in the eighth month, chili peppers turn red;

In the ninth month, persimmons glow like fire, in the tenth month, radishes are weighed;

In winter, cabbage is found in every home, in the twelfth month, garlic sprouts are fresh and green.

4. Excerpts from "Analects" P69:

Reviewing old knowledge leads to new insights.

When three people walk together, one of them can be my teacher.

Studying regularly brings joy.

To know is to know; not to know is not to know—that is true knowledge.

5. Read and Memorize P98:

Bright moonlight filters through the pines, clear springs flow over the rocks. (Wang Wei)

Amidst green hills, birds appear even whiter; amidst blue mountains, flowers seem to burn. (Du Fu)

For thousands of miles, orioles sing against a backdrop of green and red; by riverside villages and mountain towns, wine banners flutter in the wind. (Du Mu)

Mountains repeat and waters multiply, seeming to block the way; but then willow-shaded paths reveal another village. (Lu You)

Willows sway around lakes and rivers, plum blossoms bloom everywhere on hills before and behind. (Wang Anshi)

6. Read and Memorize P114:

A journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step. Even when reaching the top, there is still room for improvement. Hearing is unreliable, seeing is believing. No man is perfect, no gold is pure.

7. Read and Memorize P131 Excerpt from "Three-Character Classic":

At birth, human nature is inherently good. Natures are similar, habits differ.

If children don't study, it is inappropriate. If they don't learn young, what will they become when old?

Jade unpolished cannot become a fine object. A person unstudied does not know righteousness.

As a child, while young, respect teachers and friends, practice propriety.

Sixth Grade:

1. Read and Memorize (Poems) P32:

Among countless valleys, towering trees reach the sky; across many mountains, cuckoo calls echo. (Wang Wei)

Over mist-covered rice fields, white egrets soar; under dense summer woods, orioles sing. (Wang Wei)

In the rain, chickens crow at a few houses; bamboo-lined village paths lead to slanted plank bridges. (Wang Jian)

Through blossoming flowers, butterflies flit deep within sight; dragonflies touch the water surface gracefully flying. (Du Fu)

On the pool, there are three or four dots of green moss; beneath the leaves, one or two yellow orioles sing. (Yan Shu)

2. Read and Memorize (Proverbs) P49:

Ropes break at the thinnest part; ice cracks where it's weakest.

Only by personally entering the river do you know its depth; only by tasting the pear yourself do you know its flavor.

Do not trust the calm surface of the river; underneath lies its great depth.

No mighty pine grows in a flower pot, no eagle flies out of a birdcage.

Traveling daily, you won't fear traveling thousands of miles; doing often, you won't fear doing thousands of tasks.

3. Read and Memorize (Time Proverbs) P55:

Youth wasted brings regret in old age. Flowers may bloom again, but people do not get younger.

Plan your day in the morning, plan your year in spring.

Dark hair knows not to study early, white-haired regrets reading late.

4. Read and Memorize (Weather Proverbs) P104:

Red sunset indicates wind without rain. Bright evening stars promise fair weather tomorrow.

Heavy dew tonight means a red sunrise tomorrow. Rain clouds covering the mountains predict heavy rain.

Long sunny spells followed by fog indicate rain, long rainy spells followed by fog indicate clear skies.

5. Read and Memorize (Friendship Proverbs) P120:

True friends are close even if apart. (Wang Bo)

We share the same moon though separated by distance. (Zhang Jiuling)

Meeting a friend in a foreign land during drought feels like meeting rain. (Wang Zhu)

Cold weather reveals the resilience of pines and cypresses; adversity shows true friendship. (Anonymous)

Sending geese from afar, though light, carries deep sentiment. (Xing Junchen)

6. Read and Memorize (Idioms) P137:

Eight immortals crossing the sea - each displays their unique abilities.

Monkey King causing chaos in heaven - panic ensues.

Han Xin counting soldiers - more benefits from more.

Zhang Fei threading needles - rough yet detailed.

Bao Gong judging cases - impartial and selfless.

Jiang Taigong fishing - those willing to bite will take the bait.

Seventh Grade:

1. Daily Accumulation P19 (Antithetical Couplets):

Fog locks the mountain tops; mountains lock the fog.

Green water worries not, wind ripples its surface; blue mountains remain young, silver makes their heads white.

Mountains and waters everywhere are clearly beautiful; sunny days and rains always feel wonderful.

Layer upon layer of mountains, winding roads; tinkling springs, trees high and low.

2. Daily Accumulation P38:

In the first month, plum blossoms fragrant; in the second month, orchids in pots;

In the third month, peach blossoms spread for miles; in the fourth month, climbing roses lean against short walls;

In the fifth month, pomegranates red as fire; in the sixth month, lotus ponds full of flowers;

In the seventh month, gardenias worn on heads; in the eighth month, osmanthus branches covered in gold;

In the ninth month, chrysanthemums begin to bloom; in the tenth month, hibiscus dressed up;

In winter, narcissus placed on altars; in the twelfth month, plum blossoms hidden in snow.

3. Daily Accumulation (Couplets) P98:

A path lined with bamboo casts shadows on the ground, half-curtained flower reflections drape over the gauze window. (Yiheyuan Moon Wave Pavilion, Beijing)

Red trees contrast with green trees, distant smoke and near autumn blend harmoniously. (Chengdu Qingcheng Mountain True Wu Hall)

Lotus flowers surround all sides, willows line three; city views of mountains cover half the lake. (Daming Lake, Jinan, Shandong)

Moonlight and gentle breezes come without price, nearby water and distant mountains have affection. (Canglang Pavilion, Suzhou, Jiangsu)

4. Daily Accumulation (Famous Quotes) P141:

Where there's a will, there's a way. (History of the Later Han Dynasty)

Do not refrain from doing good because it seems small; do not commit evil because it seems insignificant. (Liu Bei)

Proficiency comes from diligence, neglect from play; success stems from thoughtfulness, failure from carelessness. (Han Yu)

Prime years do not come twice; a day cannot dawn again. One should seize the moment and strive, for time waits for no one. (Tao Yuanming)

5. Daily Accumulation (Famous Quotes) P158:

Without bold conjectures, no great discoveries can be made. (Newton)

In the new scientific palace, success belongs to the novel brave ones, who possess courageous scientific imaginations and a passion for exploring new things. (A. Fersman)

Both daydreaming and chasing shadows are characteristics of scientific workers. Let us explore infinite truths in the boundless universe! (Guo Moruo)

Eighth Grade:

1. Daily Accumulation P16:

In the vast desert, a solitary wisp of smoke rises straight; over the endless river, the sun sets round. (Wang Wei)

Several lines of red-leafed trees, countless sunset-covered mountains. (Wang Shizhen)

Endless falling leaves signal distant skies; a clear river reflects the moon distinctly. (Huang Tingjian)

Floating waters carry boundless trees; clouds shrouded in rain bury half a mountain. (Xin Qiji)

Spring river tides connect level with the sea; the bright moon rises together with the tide. (Zhang Ruoxu)

2. Daily Accumulation P33:

Say what you mean, mean what you say. (The Analects of Confucius - Zi Lu)

With friends, keep your word. (The Analects of Confucius - Xue Er)

Do not impose on others what you do not wish for yourself. (The Analects of Confucius - Yan Yuan)

With sincere intentions, even metal and stone can be moved. (History of the Later Han Dynasty - Guangling Thought King Jing Biography)

Those who love others will be loved by others; those who respect others will be respected by others. (Mencius - Li Lou Xia)

Respect elders as you would respect your own elders; cherish children as you would cherish your own children. (Mencius - Liang Hui Wang Shang)

3. Daily Accumulation (Weather Proverbs) P50:

Around the Qingming Festival, sow melons and beans.

Seeing the afterglow, don't leave home; seeing the evening glow, travel far.

Sky speckled with fish scales, drying grain doesn't require turning.

Late roosting chickens, happy ducks - storms aren't far off.

Ants moving nests, snakes crossing paths - heavy rain is sure to come tomorrow.

Spring fog means wind, summer fog means clear skies, autumn fog means cloudy skies, winter fog means snow.

4. Daily Accumulation (Famous Quotes) P97:

Life is limited, but serving the people is limitless. I shall dedicate my finite life to the infinite service of the people. (Lei Feng)

I have always held onto this belief throughout my life: the meaning of life lies in giving and contributing, not in taking or competing. (Ba Jin)

For me, the meaning of life is to empathize and put oneself in others' shoes, sharing others' sorrows and joys. (Albert Einstein)

5. Daily Accumulation (Ancient Poetry) P117:

Picking chrysanthemums beneath the eastern hedge, leisurely I see the southern mountain. (Tao Yuanming)

Idle people watch osmanthus fall, quiet nights let spring mountains empty. (Wang Wei)

Beyond the bamboo, peach blossoms here and there; the ducks know first that the spring river water is warm. (Su Shi)

In the season of plums, every household experiences rain; frogs croak everywhere in the green grass pond. (Zhao Shixiu)

Under Goose Lake Mountain, the rice and millet are abundant; pig pens and chicken coops have half-open doors. (Wang Jia)

Alone stepping out the front gate to gaze at the wild fields, the moon illuminates buckwheat flowers white as snow. (Bai Juyi)

6. Daily Accumulation (Idioms) P161:

Spreading like wildfire - not achieved in a day.

Early blooming plum blossoms - standing alone.

Brick used in building walls - surpassing the original.

Losing Jingzhou - pride leads to downfall.

Wang Xizhi writing - penetrating insight.

Zhou Yu beating Huang Gai - one willing to beat, one willing to be beaten.

Ninth Grade:

1. Daily Accumulation (Famous Quotes - Reading) P18:

A day without books, a hundred things go to waste. (Chen Shou)

Reading extensively sharpens the pen. (Du Fu)

Books are like medicine; good reading can cure ignorance. (Liu Xiang)

Not realizing the importance of studying diligently when young, regretting it when old. (Yan Zhenqing)

Reading has three essentials: heart, eyes, and mouth. (Zhu Xi)

2. Daily Accumulation (Homesickness Poems) P37:

Vast is the universe, profound is the longing for hometown. (Zhang Jiuling)

Clouds drift all day, wanderers stay away for too long. (Du Fu)

Falling leaves belong to alien trees, lonely lamps to lone travelers. (Ma Dai)

If the moon has feelings, it should recognize me; year after year, I meet her in a foreign land. (Yuan Mei)

In dreams, how far is home? In spring, how many people return along the river? (Lu Lun)

How many times have plum blossoms bloomed in the south, while I, far away, have turned gray. (Liu Zhu)

3. Daily Accumulation (Seasonal Winds) P55-56:

(Spring) Spring winds thaw frozen lands, warmth urges farming. Skirts gently sway, floral scents waft.

(Summer) Summer winds perfume plants, vitality flourishes. Standing by the pond side, lotus fragrance crosses the shore.

(Autumn) Autumn winds mix with autumn rains, adding coolness. Whistling sounds never cease, fallen leaves dance gracefully.

(Winter) North winds roar like tigers, study rooms have closed windows. Roaring all day, birds and beasts hide quietly.

4. Daily Accumulation (Proverbs - Diligence) P74:

Nothing in the world is difficult for one who sets his mind to it.

To see the ultimate truth, examine everything carefully.

Humility achieves all things, arrogance ruins nine out of ten.

Drops of water can penetrate stone; efforts bear fruit naturally.

Sharp swords emerge from honing, plum blossoms gain fragrance from enduring bitter cold.

5. Daily Accumulation (Idioms - Homophones) P79:

An orphan holding a lantern - remains the same (uncle).

Confucius moving house - all losses (books).

Green onions mixed with tofu - crystal (green) clarity.

Shoe repair without an awl - truly (needle) good.

April's icy river - started moving (frozen).

Blowing a trumpet through a door crack - famous (sounding) outside.

6. Daily Accumulation (Famous Quotes) P112:

Brothers encourage harmony, friends emphasize sincerity. (Chen Ziang)

Filial piety lies in substance, not in appearance. (Huan Kuan)

Loving relatives means not hating others; respecting relatives means not being disrespectful to others. (The Classic of Filial Piety)

Without tranquility, one cannot clarify their ambitions; without peace, one cannot achieve greatness. (Zhuge Liang)

7. Daily Accumulation (Poetry) P154 [Work] "Divination Sonnet: Ode to Plum Blossoms" [Author] Mao Zedong

Winds and rains bid farewell to spring, snow greets its arrival.

Cliffs draped in ice, yet some blossoms remain elegant.

Not competing for springtime glory, merely heralding its arrival.

Awaiting the profusion of flowers, she smiles among them.

Nature moves vigorously, gentlemen strive for self-improvement. (The Book of Changes)

The road ahead is long and arduous; I will seek high and low. (Li Sao)

Without accumulating small steps, one cannot reach a thousand miles; without gathering small streams, one cannot form a mighty river. (Xunzi)

Emerald greens, reds everywhere, orioles chirping swallows singing, winds and rains, year after year mornings and evenings. (Repetitive character couplet)

Outside the pavilion, green mountains, beyond the mountains white clouds, clouds floating beyond the sky; beside the pool green trees, beside the trees red rain, rain falling into the stream. (Chain-link couplet)
