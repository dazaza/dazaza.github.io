Bosheng's new type of controller can control 6 UV sterilization boxes at the same time.

by ditan360 on 2010-06-17 17:02:13

Berson's newly launched UV-Tronic+V5 PLC controller, combined with the SCADA control system of ultraviolet disinfection systems, allows users to conveniently set and process the operating parameters of ultraviolet disinfection systems according to their needs. Berson's new UV-Tronic controller, UV-Tronic+V5, is based on a robust PLC and uses an RS485-based Modbus interface.

The older version of UV-Tronic already had the ability to control multiple water flows and multiple treatment tanks, which was particularly useful for treatment plants with multiple water-related parameters (such as flow or velocity). This feature could turn off independent ultraviolet disinfection tanks when they were not in use, thereby reducing energy consumption.

The new UV-Tronic+V5 has enhanced features, with the power to simultaneously control up to six ultraviolet disinfection tanks, ensuring that each water flow conforms to the flow conditions, thus further reducing energy consumption.

In addition, the controller can be programmed to calculate the required power settings based on whether it receives validation from DVGW*, UVDGM**, or Berson's default dose control settings. Currently, the software is being developed to enable the controller to operate according to NWRI*** parameters. The device can control both traditional electromagnetic ballasts and Berson's electronic ballasts.

Some new features have also been added, including enhanced monitoring and control through Modbus, enhanced manual control functions to reduce maintenance and after-sales service times, and enhanced independent calibration of ultraviolet sensors during operation under DVGW mode. A modem can also be used to provide remote monitoring, diagnostics, and software upgrades.

Moreover, when the ultraviolet detector inside the ultraviolet disinfection tank shows a decrease in ultraviolet levels, the UV-Tronic+V5 can activate the automatic wiper of the quartz sleeve to protect the ultraviolet lamp. The wiper removes deposits accumulated on the sleeve, ensuring it is free from microbial contamination. The greatest benefit of automatic wiping is that no chemicals are needed for cleaning, which is an extremely important feature for drinking water disinfection.

"UV-Tronic+V5 is a very useful feature in Berson's ultraviolet system," commented Bob Clifford. Clifford is an electrical engineer at Cambridge Water in the UK and one of the users of this system. "It allows us to independently control each ultraviolet disinfection tank, greatly simplifying operations and reducing operating costs."

*DVGW (German Scientific Association for Gas and Water) is an organization responsible for self-regulation in Germany's gas and water supply industries. Its technical rules are the basis for ensuring safety and reliability.

**U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (USEPA) Ultraviolet Disinfection Guidance Manual (UVDGM), this verification approves the use of the system under the Long-Term 2 Enhanced Surface Water Treatment Rule.

***National Water Research Institute (http://www.nwri-usa.org) / American Water Works Association Research Foundation (http://www.waterresearchfoundation.org).
