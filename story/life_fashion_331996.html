001. Ride a bike on the road and enjoy the scenery.

002. Take a one-day trip to a nearby town. You don't have to go with friends; it's also nice to stroll around while listening to CDs and carrying a camera.

003. Go shopping, specifically in small shops you normally wouldn't enter. Or go to the mall, where girls can look at men's clothing and boys can look at women's clothing.

004. Take a bus route that you rarely or never take, from the starting point to the end.

005. Read a book, daydream, and feel the breeze by the lake when the sun is shining brightly.

006. Go fishing with the elderly.

007. Under the cover of night, visit the kindergarten, primary school, and middle school you once attended to reminisce. Write in the classrooms you used to be in.

008. Play games you played as a child, like hopscotch, sandbags, and rubber bands.

009. Go to a traditional market to buy groceries and experience the satisfaction of haggling.

010. Keep walking in one direction until you realize you should stop.

011. Play bottle message by the sea, then spend the remaining boring time wondering if anyone has received your letter.

012. Go to an amusement park and repeatedly ride the most thrilling attractions.

013. Get up early in the morning, go to the park to practice Tai Chi with the elderly, then watch them play with their birds.

014. Go to another city to visit a long-unseen friend and give them a surprise.

015. Visit all the places in your city that you haven't been to yet.

016. Spend a day volunteering on the street.

017. Visit your grandparents and great-grandparents.

018. Sneak into your parents' workplace for reconnaissance.

019. Fly a kite, a particularly large one.

020. If conditions allow, go diving!

021. If conditions allow, go bungee jumping!

022. Visit a history museum; history might not be so dull after all.

023. Play rock-paper-scissors with friends in the busiest area. The loser must do the most boring thing, such as singing while squatting next to a trash can or plastering themselves against a store window...

024. Visit a teacher who once helped you a lot.

025. Stand in a busy area or in front of a mall and play a game of item exchange with passersby.

026. Find some beautiful insects or leaves to make specimens.

027. Patiently attend a serious concert or watch a play.

028. Buy a stack of newspapers early in the morning and sell them as a newspaper boy for the day.

029. When bored, pretend to be someone else's fan and go out to canvass votes.

030. Ride a horse; many cities have small horse farms.

031. If you're old enough, why not learn how to drive?

032. Go to a bookstore or library to read, bring enough bread and water to last all day.

033. Try on clothes at expensive counters, just try without buying.

034. Search for CDs or DVDs; bringing a bunch home can pass the time.

035. Go out with a friend and act as each other for the day, following each other's habits in behavior, actions, and tone.

036. Work as a temporary salesperson at a supermarket or mall for the day.

037. If you're brave enough, join a group hug and embrace strangers.

038. Specifically help the elderly or those with mobility issues cross the road beside the road.

039. Climb a mountain and have a picnic on top. (Note: Not cooking!)

040. Rural areas around the city have developed agritourism projects. Why not experience life in the countryside, do some farm work, and eat some home-cooked meals.

047. Permanently perm your hair, trying strange hairstyles you've never tried before.

048. Buy hair dye and color your own hair.

049. Trim your split ends every day.

052. Take out your mom's or sister's clothes and play dress-up in front of a full-length mirror.

053. Hold a mirror in front of yourself and perform a situational drama with the person in the mirror.

054. Take out old unwanted clothes and make an exaggerated costume for yourself.

055. Turn the home sound system up to maximum volume, hold a microphone and lip-sync like a superstar. It’s best if someone records you with a DV.

068. Raise some pets that require careful care.
