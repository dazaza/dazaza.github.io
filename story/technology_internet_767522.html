Beijing Issues First Fine for False Advertising on Weibo: Penalty Amount is 150,000 Yuan

by anonymous on 2013-10-20 18:34:01

The Beijing Administration for Industry and Commerce (AIC) yesterday announced the results of a special anti-counterfeiting campaign, issuing the first penalty for false advertising via Weibo: Beijing Fangwanyuan Company was fined 150,000 yuan for making false claims on Weibo that damaged the business reputation of others. The AIC stated that in the future they will focus on monitoring goods promoted and sold through Weibo and WeChat, particularly targeting false advertising.

Merchants are required to remove substandard products from shelves and face maximum fines

According to reports, all city-level branches of the AIC have been conducting a special campaign against counterfeit goods since late August. Key targeted products include clothing, children's toys and products, indoor decoration materials, small household appliances, shoes, beauty and hair care products, auto parts, electric bicycles, etc. During this special operation period, the AIC increased product sampling by over 2,000 items, mainly focusing on products with frequent issues found during regular inspections and those with prominent complaints from consumers. Inspection results indicate that clothing, communication equipment, electric bicycles, toys, and other products sold in the market have significant quality problems not meeting mandatory standards. Yesterday, the AIC published the first list of substandard products, involving 70 products produced by 61 manufacturing enterprises.

For substandard products, the AIC, besides publicizing them externally, also incorporates illegal operator information into Beijing’s credit information system, requires merchants to remove substandard products from shelves, and recalls already-sold items. At the same time, merchants are subject to maximum fines, and severe cases may result in the revocation of their business licenses.

Green Source and other electric bikes found to be substandard

Inspection results show that well-known electric bike brands such as Green Source, Giant, Urban Wind, Tala, Little Bird, etc., were all found to be substandard, mostly due to issues like maximum speed and overall weight. The AIC stated that according to regulations, electric bicycles should not exceed 20 km/h and weigh no more than 40 kilograms. Problems with these indicators can lead to significant safety hazards.

The AIC indicated that in the next phase, they will initiate investigations into lawbreakers and impose strict penalties, concentrating efforts on handling major influential cases.

Eight Major Cases

1. Case of False Advertising on Weibo Damaging Business Reputation

Beijing Fangwanyuan Company compared its "Big K Leek Edition" mobile phone with others on Weibo and claimed superiority. Due to false advertising damaging others' business reputations, it was fined 150,000 yuan. This is the first penalty issued by the AIC for false advertising on Weibo.

2. Case of Infringing on the "Crocodile" Trademark

The Qianmen branch of Beijing Hengchao Jewelry Co., Ltd. infringed on the "Crocodile" trademark and was fined over 210,000 yuan.

3. Case of Selling Counterfeit "Adidas" Brand Clothing

Yun Mou was fined 89,000 yuan for selling counterfeit "Adidas" brand clothing.

4. Case of Using Counterfeit XYPEX "Sibers" Products

Beijing Third Construction Engineering Company was fined 126,000 yuan for using counterfeit XYPEX "Sibers" cement-based crystalline waterproofing material concentrate.

5. Case of Selling Infringing "Yalu" Clothing

Beijing Tianlan Olai Trading Co., Ltd. was fined over 147,000 yuan for selling infringing "Yalu" clothing.

6. Case of Selling Infringing "Tasty Point" Food Trademark

Beijing Jinliangping Food Co., Ltd. and Du Mou were fined over 102,000 yuan by the Fengtai District AIC for selling infringing "Tasty Point" food trademarks.

7. Trademark Infringement Case

Through online collection of fake-selling clues, the Tongzhou branch dismantled three dens selling counterfeit "Niangshan" liquor and infringing "BURBERRY" bags and clothing, confiscating 71 infringing items.

8. Online Sale of Counterfeit Well-Known Brand Goods Case

The Changping branch investigated an online sale of counterfeit well-known sports brand goods case, with a case value reaching 410,000 yuan.
