Tencent QQ "Happy Beans" gambling controversy: players lose five thousand weekly

by nuannuanleng on 2009-11-09 23:04:04

Gambling, gambling, anyway, in the end, the winners are always the house and the casino owners. So Shi Yuzhu wants to have "open boxes" in "The Journey Online", and Tencent wants to come up with something like "Happy Beans" in QQ Games. After all, everyone likes to make money quickly. Of course, if the casino is too obvious, it is still illegal.

Recently, some players have found that "Happy Fight Landlord" and "Happy Upgrade" in QQ Games, which require "Happy Beans" to participate, seem a bit different from ordinary games. At the end of each game, players will be deducted a certain amount of "Happy Beans". This is a typical gambling "rake" - the house also gets a certain percentage of the winner's winnings, known as "rake" in jargon.

It is understood that in QQ Games, the exchange rate between RMB and Q Coins is 11, and 1 Q Coin can roughly get 10,000 or so "Happy Beans". In each game, the loss is at least several thousand "Happy Beans", and at most hundreds of thousands of "Happy Beans" - this is just for one game. This situation can also be seen on the Lianzhong Chess and Cards platform. Gambling games such as "Hong Kong Five Cards (Poker)" and "Win Three Cards (Three Flowers Fraud)" are prominently displayed in the game list.

A player surnamed Liu who was once involved also said, "You can gamble while working. I originally thought 'a little gamble is enjoyable', but later I gambled more and more, unable to stop." Liu even had an experience of losing more than 5,000 yuan in a week, and according to his disclosure, "There are more than ten kinds of gambling games in some gaming halls, and the gameplay is constantly enriched. To be honest, it's not uncommon to lose or win tens of thousands of wealth values in a day."

Damn Tencent, deceiving our hard-earned money, what a broken Happy Beans game
