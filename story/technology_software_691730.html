Best study abroad options in Quebec, Canada for humanities majors?

by dingyi947279 on 2012-03-05 17:27:28

There are many liberal arts majors in China who, if they choose to study abroad, can opt for the cost-effective and prestigious option of studying at the University of Quebec in Canada. Studying at the University of Quebec is affordable, costing only 70,000-80,000 RMB per year. It offers bilingual English and French instruction, enhancing future employability by breaking personal limits. Some programs provide paid internship opportunities, offering good assurance for employment. Many programs offer government scholarships, reducing your financial burden. Immigration policies are relaxed and comprehensive, making it easy to transition status after completing studies. So, what preparations should students going to Quebec for study make?

Canadian universities generally have an intake in September each year, with some universities also accepting students in January and May. Considering university admission policies, program choices, and adaptation for new students, September is the best time to start. To seize the golden opportunity of starting in September, both students and parents need to prepare well in advance.

Firstly, I would like to remind students interested in studying in Quebec that attending university in Quebec will not be any easier than in China, so it's essential to lay a solid foundation before entering university to attend a renowned institution and study a good major. Laying a solid foundation means achieving good results in Chinese high school courses. We don't demand being top exam scorers, but we need to at least prove that we're not falling behind. A high school score between 70-80 is the basic preparation every high school student should aim for. Now, with the liberalization of the study abroad market, it's no longer difficult for students in grades 10 or 11 to study in Quebec, Canada. Additionally, studying high school or pre-university courses in Quebec does not require TOEFL or IELTS scores, making it very convenient. Here, I sincerely recommend that students applying to schools in Quebec should prepare thoroughly in advance and start preparing application materials and visa documents early.

Secondly, I would like to remind parents planning to send their children to study in Canada to prepare financially in advance. Many people mistakenly believe that having money alone is enough to study abroad, which is not entirely correct. To study at the University of Quebec in Canada, you must not only prove that you have the funds but also clearly explain the deposit history of these funds, proving that you have sufficient economic strength to cover all expenses during your child's study in Canada. Start preparing the funds early, and specific methods can be consulted with friends who already have children studying abroad or professional study abroad agencies.

Lastly, both students and parents should pay attention to the application timeline. Students hoping to enter undergraduate programs directly or language/pre-university programs must grasp the registration deadlines to secure enrollment in their ideal university in September of the same year or September of the following year.
