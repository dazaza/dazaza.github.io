If you are keen to help your child with reading then this article is for you. Tell me, what is phonics? There is a special relationship between letters and sounds that is at the same time both complex and simple, and this is what phonics is all about. It is the pairing of the spoken and the written. The connection between the written or printed letter and the corresponding sound is the essence of phonics, which means teaching children the relationships in the system of language used for reading. Creating words by mixing sounds together to form words and in turn from mixing sounds, to make letters etc mix letters "test" the word is the strategic goal of early readers using phonics. Let's look at an example: Phonics teaches the letter P makes the "P" sound, it is the beginning sound of the word 'pool'. Now the student blends the sounds to form the word. Now this all sounds pretty simple, doesn't it? However, there are 26 English letters and they form a total of 44 different sounds. From phonics schemes, children learn how to decode regular words, and also spell them even if they haven't seen the word, before. Tell me more about the importance of phonics? The whole purpose of reading is to be able to understand text. The key to being able to read fluently without stumbling is comprehension, and this is the core strength of phonics teaching. Students need sufficient exposure to phonics skills and practice them while recognizing letters, and writing skills, such as leading to reading competence.What forms, make phonics important? Let's consider the fundamental purpose of reading: comprehension. Reading fluently without stuttering or getting stuck, is where the reader needs to go to understand what they are reading. This is where phonics comes into its own. Combined with other skills such as writing and letter identification, voice and production leads the reader on the correct road How do you teach phonics? Use a system, and in a sequence. Teachers need to let children practice extensively at each level before moving on to the next stage. If children can decode books at the right level for them, they will be consolidating their work. The key here is to frequently provide children with opportunities to read with an adult who can guide and correct, and then let them take some words home to really drill the lesson, which is a high quality way to help children.
