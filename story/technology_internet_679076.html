Man spends money to hire people to pretend to be his parents to defraud notarization for raising start-up funds

by peexzi1127 on 2012-02-27 19:15:30

On that day, a young man in his twenties led two middle-aged people in their fifties into the Chongqing Notary Office.

"My name is Cheng Yu. These are my parents. They want to entrust me to handle the house mortgage loan," the young man said. He then provided the notary with his identity information, property ownership certificate, and his parents' identity information. The notary warmly received Cheng Yu, while preliminarily reviewing the written materials and chatting casually with his parents.

"Where did your son Cheng Yu work before?" the notary asked. "A second-hand housing company," his father replied. "Which company?" the notary casually asked again. "Uh..." His father paused for a moment, looked at Cheng Yu's mother, and awkwardly smiled at the notary.

Unaware of the Son’s School

It was All an Act Paid for

"How could parents not know where their son works!" The notary became suspicious. "Which high school did Cheng Yu attend?" The notary suddenly asked Cheng Yu's parents. "This..." Both parents were once again speechless.

To be cautious, the notary proposed to visit the house that was preparing to apply for the mortgage. At this point, all three showed signs of panic on their faces, wanting to register as tax consultants.

"The property certificate is here. There's no need to conduct an on-site inspection, right?" Cheng Yu started making various excuses. His parents hurriedly stood up, flustered and wanted to leave.

"Speak up, what's really going on?" the notary seriously said. Seeing that the act couldn't go on any longer, Cheng Yu had no choice but to lower his head and tell the truth: it turned out that these two people were not his parents but actors he hired for 500 yuan each to pretend to be his parents.

Young Man Wants to Start a Business but Lacks Funds

Got the Wrong Idea from Certificate Ads

Cheng Yu explained that after graduating from university, he began working at a real estate company's second-hand housing transaction center. Having worked there for more than six years, he was very familiar with the second-hand housing transaction process and had accumulated some clients and social resources. This year, he decided to start his own company and needed to raise start-up funds, so he discussed with his parents about mortgaging their house to take out a loan.

Cheng Yu's parents owned a more than 100 square meter commercial apartment in Yuzhong District. Worried about the risks of their son starting alone, they firmly refused. After a big argument with his parents and storming out of the house, while wandering around, he saw a roadside advertisement for certificates and suddenly had the idea to get fake certificates and hire people to pretend to be his parents to obtain a power of attorney notarization.

Cheng Yu and the two fake parents have violated the law, and the notary office could report it to the police. However, considering that Cheng Yu resorted to such measures for entrepreneurship and has a good attitude towards recognizing his mistakes, the notary office decided to give him a chance to correct his mistake and did not report it.

"I will never take chances and do such things again!" Cheng Yu promised before leaving the municipal notary office.

Last Friday (the 17th), a pair of middle-aged "husband and wife" accompanied by their "son" walked into the Chongqing Notary Office to handle the power of attorney notarization. "Which high school does your son attend?" The notary casually asked, and the two showed difficulty, stammering and unable to answer —— originally, the young man Cheng Yu (a pseudonym) wanted to secretly mortgage his parents' house to raise start-up capital and found two people to pretend to be his parents for notarization.

300 Fake Notarizations Detected Last Year

Yesterday, the head of the Municipal Notary Association said that every year, our city's notary institutions encounter cases where parties impersonate others or provide false materials to defraud notarial certificates. This requires notaries to significantly enhance their awareness of prevention and identification. Currently, for a 200-yuan commission power of attorney notarization, notaries must carefully review the materials provided by the parties, strengthen questioning, and conduct on-site investigations to prevent fake and erroneous certificates.

According to statistics, in 2011 alone, the city's notary institutions uncovered nearly 300 applications involving impersonation or providing false proof materials.

The head of the Municipal Notary Association stated that according to the provisions of the "Notary Law", those who provide false proof materials to defraud notarial certificates and constitute a crime will be held criminally responsible.

By Wang Ming of the Chongqing Evening News, with contributions from Zhou Lei.
