AdSense Check Cashier Oddities: Is Google Running Out of Cash?

by jagger on 2007-07-19 20:54:44

After painstakingly building a website, testing ads, and waiting for months or even a year, your Google AdSense revenue finally reaches $100! I believe every webmaster who has placed AdSense ads on their site can relate to the excitement of that moment. Next, of course, is waiting for Google to send the dollar check. In China, before Google fulfills its promise of localized payment methods, we still have to go to the bank and process the collection of the AdSense checks sent in dollars. In most cases, all we need to do is wait for the bank to handle the collection; there won't be any issues. But what if, when you're at the bank processing the collection, they tell you that your check cannot be cashed because the issuer, Google, doesn't have sufficient funds in their account? "What?! Google doesn't have enough money to cash my small check?! They made $3.66 billion just in the first quarter of this year!"

Is this a joke? If you were in this situation, you'd probably feel as astonished as mojo. An AdSense user abroad named mojo had previously changed his address, but due to technical issues, Google AdSense failed to process it correctly, causing mojo's AdSense check to be delayed by six weeks. That wasn't the worst part, though. With the help of Matt Cutts, mojo finally received his check last week and quickly took it to the bank to cash it. However, the bank clerk informed him that his check couldn't be cashed because the issuer’s account didn't have sufficient funds.

At first, mojo naturally thought the bank was joking, since the check was issued by Google—how could such a large company not have enough funds to cash his insignificant check? But the bank confirmed the fact again. This was already a strange story, but the funniest part was yet to come.

The bank suggested that mojo call Google. Since mojo didn't know Google's contact number, he had no choice but to dial the phone number printed on the check. As you might imagine, mojo heard an automated system prompting him to enter an extension number. Not knowing the extension, mojo chose to input a name directly. Following the prompts step by step, he entered Matt Cutts' name because Matt Cutts was aware of the issue and would be a good person to contact. Unexpectedly, the system informed him that the name "Matt Cutts" was invalid—Google didn't recognize Matt Cutts. After much effort, mojo finally managed to reach Matt Cutts' extension, only to hear a pre-recorded message from Matt: "This is not a frequently used voicemail box. Please do not leave a message." Still determined, mojo continued navigating the phone system, hoping for further assistance, but the system eventually told him that since Google doesn't have customer service, he should visit Google's website for more information.

So the situation became clear: mojo received an AdSense check from Google that couldn't be cashed, and when he called hoping to learn more about the situation, he found that Google's phone system was essentially useless and couldn't provide him with any help. Now, mojo is left powerless, holding the check and waiting for Google's response. Fortunately, since mojo wrote about the entire process, it caused quite a stir, and Matt Cutts himself saw mojo's situation again. He apologized and stated that Google was investigating the matter internally. For the full description of the incident, you can read it here—it's vividly written. (Note: If you have doubts about the authenticity of the event or the bank procedures, it's recommended to first read the comments section on mojo's blog, where these questions are addressed.)
