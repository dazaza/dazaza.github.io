Cohabiting couple's argument leads to woman's fall from building, man sentenced to 1.5 years in prison

by lovei0c1 on 2012-03-06 13:54:03

Our agency reporter Yang Bo - A man quarreled with his girlfriend due to an ambiguous text message in her mobile phone, which eventually led to the girlfriend climbing onto a window of the shower room and accidentally falling from the building, suffering serious injuries. The case of Liao's argument with his girlfriend Ruan that happened last February in Sanshui District has had new developments. Recently, the People's Court of Sanshui District sentenced Liao to one and a half years in prison for negligent infliction of grievous bodily harm and ordered him to pay 310,000 yuan in compensation. It is reported that Liao has appealed against the first-instance judgment.

The cohabiting couple quarrelled, causing the woman to fall from the fourth floor.

Liao is 28 years old and works as a public official at the Land and Urban-Rural Development Department of Sanshui District. The victim Ruan is also 28 years old and works at the People's Property Insurance Company of China. They have been in love for over a year, and the incident occurred in their cohabitation home.

On February 5, 2011, after drinking and having snacks at a KTV, they returned home at around 4 o'clock in the morning. In the room, an ambiguous text message on Ruan's mobile phone triggered an argument. During the quarrel, Liao pushed Ruan, who then fled into the shower room. Liao followed her inside and locked the door. He continued questioning her and said if she wanted to leave, she could only climb out through the window. Ruan then sat astride on the window and accidentally fell from the building, fracturing her waist vertebrae and suffering nerve damage.

According to the indictment, Liao used language such as "if you want to go, just go through the window," which prompted Ruan to climb onto the windowsill, indicating a certain degree of negligence. The prosecution believed that Liao's actions constituted negligent infliction of grievous bodily harm, requiring corresponding criminal responsibility and compensation of more than 1.44 million yuan.

The first-instance judgment ordered a compensation of 310,000 yuan, but Liao disagreed and appealed.

Regarding the injury appraisal of Ruan, there were three different appraisal results, once causing controversy. On February 17, 2011, the Judicial Appraisal Center of the Sanshui Public Security Bureau determined that Ruan's injuries were minor. On March 17 of the same year, the Forensic Clinical Appraisal Institute of the First People's Hospital of Foshan determined that Ruan's injuries were severe. On November 25 of the same year, the Forensic Appraisal Center of Sun Yat-sen University determined that Ruan's injuries were minor.

In the first-instance judgment, the court of Sanshui accepted the appraisal conclusion from the First People's Hospital. Liao believed that this appraisal procedure was suspected of being illegal.

After the court's review, it was found that Ruan's act of sitting on the windowsill was closely related to Liao's previous pushing, questioning, and locking of the door. On February 10, 2012, the People's Court of Sanshui District determined that Liao's actions constituted negligent infliction of grievous bodily harm, sentencing him to one and a half years in prison and ordering him to bear 80% of civil compensation responsibility (including half the maintenance fee for Ruan's daughter, amounting to 64,000 yuan), i.e., compensating Ruan with 310,000 yuan.

It is understood that Liao disagreed with the first-instance judgment and has already filed an appeal.
