by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-09 08:21:53

If Manchester City does not make it to the top four this season, Hughes will definitely be sacked, with Mourinho and Benitez on the shortlist to take over.

After beating Chelsea, Manchester City has climbed to sixth place despite having played one fewer match. With their strong progress in the League Cup, things seem to be going relatively smoothly for Manchester City. However, they have not reached a state of complete smooth sailing yet. In the game against Chelsea, the absolute main left-back Bridge was injured by Belletti's tackle. According to the Daily Mail after the match, Bridge will be absent from at least three matches due to an injury to the medial collateral ligament of his knee.

From the match footage, it is clear that although Bridge mostly avoided Belletti's tackle, his knee still got hit. He fell down clutching his knee immediately and was carried off the field on a stretcher. Hughes expressed his hope after the match that his key player wasn't too seriously injured. From the current situation, resting for three weeks is considered a lucky outcome.

In this match, goalkeeper Given's performance left a deep impression. Combined with the sympathetic scene that happened to the Irish team not long ago in the World Cup qualifiers, many people's evaluations of this goalkeeper have increased significantly these days. A columnist for The Mirror, McDonnell, even said: "Given is not only Manchester City's best acquisition but also the best goalkeeper in the league!"

Below are the highlights of McDonnell's article: “Among all players with big-name reputations bought with large sums of money, there is one person who Mark Hughes has used most efficiently, but he is also the cheapest acquisition. Shay Given, costing only 6 million pounds, represents an extremely significant value. It can be argued that his worth exceeds that of Robinho, Tevez, Adebayor, Leskot, and Toure by far. Saving Lampard's penalty kick greatly helped Manchester City defeat Chelsea, and this was just the latest heroic act by the Irish goalkeeper.”

McDonnell compares him to Schmeichel, saying: “Hughes admitted after the match that Given shares some similarities with his old teammate Schmeichel. They create a certain atmosphere on the pitch and convey the message to the defenders that as long as they have them guarding the goal, the last line of defense is secure. Ferguson once said that Schmeichel, in terms of points, was worth about 10 to 12 points per season, and Given is no different. When Manchester City's newly assembled defensive line was still lost, the 33-year-old Given helped his team pass countless times. Thanks to his performance, it can be fairly said that he has earned Manchester City 7 to 8 more points.”

McDonnell concludes: “In modern football, a reliable goalkeeper is truly a godsend, so Hughes signing Given was a correct decision. As a goalkeeper, at 33 years old, he is at the peak of his career. Few goalkeepers can control the will of penalty takers, but in the penalty duel with Lampard, Given won the mental battle. Therefore, without a doubt, based on his current form, he is the best goalkeeper in the Premier League.”

Finally, there is a rumor that might disturb Mark Hughes slightly. The People newspaper revealed that the Manchester City equipped with plenty of money, what the Middle Eastern consortium hopes to see most is to achieve results like Chelsea did in the 04-05 season this season. At the very least, they want to enter the Champions League by the end of the season, and this bottom-line goal is also the premise for whether Hughes can continue in his coaching position. If this cannot be achieved, his job will be in jeopardy immediately.

In fact, Manchester City's management already has a potential list of coaches, all non-British. Among them are Hiddink and Mourinho, who have proven their abilities in the Premier League, and even the current Reds coach Benitez is on the list, along with Rijkaard.

Strongly entering the semi-finals of the League Cup, defeating Chelsea in the league, and having already faced each of the four major clubs at least once this season, Manchester City only lost to United in a game with excessive stoppage time, which temporarily ensures Hughes' safety. In the short term, his position is guaranteed. Of course, achieving these goals is still insufficient. The previous seven consecutive draws in the league annoyed the upper management greatly. Whether home or away, they cannot draw with every team, and currently, they are only ranked sixth, still with a gap to the top four Champions League positions. Manchester City needs to learn how to earn 3 points from weaker teams. Simply winning against a few major clubs does not play a decisive role in the final ranking, and Hughes' status will still be precarious at that time.
