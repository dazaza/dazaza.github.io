Prostatitis almost accompanies a man's entire life. Now, let me introduce in detail the prostatic problems at various stages.

Children rarely suffer from prostatitis. Pediatric prostatitis is similar to adult prostatitis and can be divided into acute and chronic types. The causes of pediatric acute prostatitis, apart from conditions like phimosis, redundant prepuce, and poor external genital hygiene leading to ascending urinary tract infections, may also involve bacteria entering the prostate through the bloodstream due to issues such as cavities or respiratory infections. Of course, pediatric prostatitis is quite rare.

As boys enter puberty, hormones cause curiosity and admiration for their own bodies and those of girls on one hand; on the other hand, they cause repeated congestion and continuous growth in the internal prostate and seminal vesicle glands, as well as external organs such as the penis, testicles, and epididymis. After repeated congestion and erection, ejaculation forms, and the pleasure brought by ejaculation leads most boys to pursue it. During this process, some boys start to masturbate (formerly known as self-abuse) and become indulged in it. Some boys carry heavy burdens based on this, while others try to suppress and avoid it. These either excessive or scarce masturbation habits cause some boys to experience discomfort in the lower abdomen, scrotal heaviness, or difficulty in urination initiation, or urinary retention, or frequent urination, or incomplete urination, and even back pain. These are symptoms of prostatitis. At this stage, prostatitis caused by unclean factors is extremely rare.

For middle-aged and young adults, regular sexual activity allows some men's prostates to be properly and appropriately used. However, for some weekend couples or couples who live far apart for long periods, when they reunite, sexual activity is dense and frequent, and when they separate, it becomes scarce. Without proper guidance and appropriate release, prostatitis symptoms are very likely to appear.

On the other hand, men at this stage often suffer from excessive smoking and drinking, overconsumption of spicy food, prolonged sitting at office desks, computer tables, dining tables, gambling tables, or in vehicles, insufficient water intake, and lack of exercise, all of which can lead to prostatitis. Furthermore, at this stage, men have more opportunities for extramarital affairs and unclean sexual activities, which cannot be overlooked.

In old age, some elderly men, due to decreased libido and erectile ability, or lack of cooperation from their spouses, or widowhood, result in a lack of sexual activity for some individuals in this group. This often leads to prostatic inflammation accompanying prostatic hyperplasia.

The inducing factors of prostatitis at different age groups vary, but the common factors causing prostatic inflammation are mainly these: excessive smoking and drinking, overconsumption of spicy food, prolonged sitting, insufficient water intake, lack of exercise, irregular sexual activity, and unclean sexual activity.

Understanding these factors and paying attention to them in daily life should reduce the chances of your prostate becoming inflamed.
