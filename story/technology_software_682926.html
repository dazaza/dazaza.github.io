Sick girl on hunger strike against her mother's opposition to her donation of cornea (Photo)

by xue94fwsh on 2012-02-29 10:34:57

In the hospital ward lies a smiling girl (as shown in the picture). She is Yueyang, and in a few days, it will be her 20th birthday. From her smile, you can't sense any pain at all, but when you see the condition of her lower body, the reporter is shocked - below the knee joint is a thumb-sized ulcerated wound, where even white tendons are visible. But she clutches the blanket tightly and continues to smile.

As her condition worsens, Yueyang knows that her time is limited. In a few days, it will be her 20th birthday. She told her mother that she didn’t want to stay in the hospital; instead, she wanted to wear a pretty dress and celebrate a happy birthday at home with her mother and younger brother (who was conceived by her mother while her father had left home for the first time).

**Fever causing cerebral palsy leads to father's abandonment**

□ Chief Reporter Zhang Zhili, Intern Song Kun (Text and Photos)

Not long ago, Yueyang saw a news report on TV about human body donation, which sparked her idea of donating her corneas. Initially, her mother strongly opposed this idea. However, little Yueyang started a hunger strike, and after seven or eight days, her mother finally agreed. On September 19th, Ms. Yuan went to the Red Cross Society of Anyang City to handle the relevant procedures for her daughter.

**For the donation of corneas, she fought with hunger strike**

When the reporter asked Yueyang if she had any unfulfilled wishes, she said she wanted to wear a beautiful dress like other girls. Ms. Yuan said: "My daughter has spent nearly 20 years in bed, always wearing patient clothing. She mentioned several times that she wanted to wear pretty clothes and skirts like other girls."

**Mom, I'll take care of you next life**

According to the doctor’s introduction, Yueyang suffers from complications of cerebral palsy. Since August this year, her condition began to deteriorate, with swelling in her legs and feet, and multiple joints becoming ulcerated. This disease causes unbearable pain, but Yueyang doesn’t want others to worry about her, so she always remains silent and shows everyone her sunny side. However, the tears flowing down her cheeks and the sweat on her forehead betray her true feelings.

Even though the pain makes her sweat profusely, she always greets everyone with a smile because she doesn't want others to worry about her. She wants to leave her smile for every person who cares for and loves her.

**Core Tip**

Ms. Yuan said that her daughter's 20th birthday is approaching, and she is preparing to donate all usable organs from her body to celebrate her birthday. "Now I have thought it through. If my daughter wants to donate, then let her fulfill her wish. This may be the only thing I can do for her."

According to Ms. Yuan, in the summer of 1992, one-year-old Yueyang suffered from high fever, leading to oxygen deprivation in the brain and resulting in cerebral palsy. Over time, Yueyang's father grew to detest the paralyzed Yueyang, eventually leaving home. Yueyang's grandmother had suffered from a stroke for many years and was also bedridden. Ms. Yuan not only had to take care of little Yueyang but also her own mother-in-law, an immense burden that weighed heavily on her.

In 1996, Ms. Yuan sold their family home for tens of thousands of yuan to raise money for Yueyang's medical treatment. They traveled south to Zhengzhou and north to Beijing, making countless trips, but Ms. Yuan never gave up on her daughter's treatment.

**Although tormented by pain, she always smiles brightly**

Because her mother opposed her donating her corneas, she went on a week-long hunger strike as a protest and ultimately got her way.

**Smiling Girl**

Yueyang has another wish: to be mother and daughter with her mom in the next life. She misses her father but feels disappointed by his cruelty. In the next life, what she wants to do is serve her mother well. She tells her mother: "This life has caused you too much suffering. In the next life, I will repay you."

The only tool for the long-term bedridden Yueyang to understand the outside world is an old TV set. The colorful screen taught Yueyang what love and kindness are.

**Tip-off provided by Mr. Zhang**

"Every day now, I have to take sleeping pills to fall asleep, otherwise, the pain would make me unable to sleep," said Ms. Yuan, Yueyang's mother, with tears in her eyes, standing by the bedside.

On her sickbed, as her 20th birthday approaches, she once again prepares to donate all usable organs from her body.

In 1998, Yueyang's grandmother passed away, and since the house was sold, the family could only rent a flat in the old district. During this time, Yueyang's father returned home once, but soon left again. In 2004, Ms. Yuan divorced her husband.
