The anal-rectal fistula mainly invades the anus and rarely involves the rectum, so it is often called an anal fistula, which is a granulomatous duct communicating with the perineal skin. The incidence rate ranks second only to hemorrhoids and is more common in young and middle-aged men, possibly related to the vigorous secretion of sebaceous glands, one of the sex hormone target organs in men.

According to Professor Peng Peng of the Anorectal Center of Yangzhou Armed Police Hospital, an anal fistula has a primary internal opening, a fistula, branches, and a secondary external opening [http://www.wjgc.net/zzcx/gmss/bx/]. The internal opening is the entrance of the infection source, mostly located inside the anal sinus and its vicinity, commonly seen on both sides of the posterior median line, but it can also be in any part of the lower rectum or anal canal. The fistula can be straight or curved, and a few have branches.

The external opening is where the abscess ruptures or is incised for drainage, mostly located on the skin around the anal canal. Due to the continuous entry of the primary lesion through the internal opening into the duct, plus the tortuous course of the duct near the internal and external sphincters, the duct wall is composed of fibrous tissue, and there are granulation tissues inside the duct, hence it does not heal easily.

Professor Peng Peng of the Anorectal Center of Yangzhou Armed Police Hospital introduced that generally, a simple anal fistula has only one internal opening and one external opening, which is the most common type. If the external opening is temporarily closed and local drainage is not smooth, redness and swelling will gradually occur again, forming another abscess. The closed external opening may break again or form another external opening elsewhere. This repeated occurrence will expand the scope of the lesion or sometimes create several external openings communicating with the internal opening, such an anal fistula is called a complex anal fistula, i.e., having one internal opening and multiple external openings.

Some people believe that the complexity of an anal fistula should not be divided by the number of external openings in anorectal hospitals [http://www.njgcyy.com], but refers to the main duct involving the anal-rectal ring or above. Although this kind of anal fistula has only one external opening and one internal opening, the treatment is relatively complex, hence it is called a complex anal fistula. Conversely, sometimes an anal fistula may have multiple external openings, but treating the anal fistula is not complicated.
