----------------------------------- Style Conversion: A New Round of Attacks Officially Launched?

Summary: In the current environment where valuations of thematic stocks are generally high, capital entering the undervalued and steadily performing blue-chip sectors is an inherent requirement for market development. With the cooling down of various thematic stock speculations, the heavyweight blue-chip sectors represented by banks, insurance companies, brokers, and oil-petrochemicals have emerged strongly. While the rotation of hotspots has intensified the volatility of the index, a new round of upward momentum may have officially begun.

[Hotspot Focus and Market Opportunities]

This week, the Shenzhen-Shanghai stock market exhibited a strong upward trend with oscillations, achieving weekly gains of 7.83% and 7.13%, respectively. The weekly K-line charts closed with medium-sized bullish candles, recovering from the previous week's adjustment caused by the Dubai debt crisis. Notably, the Shanghai Composite Index successively reclaimed the key levels of 3100, 3200, and 3300 points. As of now, the overall upward trend remains intact. It’s worth noting that as the speculative fervor around various thematic stocks cools down, the heavyweight blue-chip sectors represented by banks, insurance companies, brokers, and oil-petrochemicals have emerged strongly. Amidst the hotspot rotation causing increased index volatility, a new phase of upward momentum might have officially commenced.

Observing Friday's hotspot performance, while the ChiNext board experienced a collective plunge and the speculative fervor around various thematic stocks rapidly cooled down, the heavyweight sectors such as banking, insurance, brokerage, and oil-petrochemicals showed significant fluctuations. Most heavyweight stocks like China Pacific Insurance, China Life Insurance, Ping An Insurance, CITIC Bank, Ningbo Bank, China Merchants Bank, and Sinopec all surged significantly. Furthermore, it was noted that the substantial rise in these heavyweight sector stocks accelerated the adjustment of various thematic stocks, showcasing a typical "seesaw" effect, which led to the re-emergence of the "2-8 phenomenon" and triggered severe index fluctuations. However, after the intense fluctuations, the strengthening of the heavyweight sectors had a positive impact on the index. Considering that the speculation around various thematic stocks has been relatively sufficient in the earlier stages, whether the abnormal movements in the heavyweight sectors can effectively promote a shift in market style and ultimately drive the market to successfully break through the previous high-resistance level clearly deserves close attention from investors.

I believe that factors such as the anticipated launch of financial innovations like stock index futures and margin trading, along with the expectation of November's CPI possibly turning positive, could all contribute to the switching of market hotspots. Stock Forum. Investors should pay attention to the fact that this week, Zhu Yuchen, General Manager of China Financial Futures Exchange (CFFEX), stated at the Fifth China (Shenzhen) International Futures Conference that CFFEX is exploring the establishment of an appropriateness system for stock index futures and setting three strict thresholds for investor entry, including requiring clients with over 500,000 yuan to open accounts first. The stock index futures will be launched in a steady and appropriate manner. Former CSRC Chairman Zhou Daojing pointed out that we should genuinely advance financial innovation reforms, resolve deep-seated market contradictions, utilize risk management, accelerate the development of the financial derivatives market, and call for the prompt launch of stock index futures. Clearly, the expected launch of stock index futures and other financial innovations will have a significant impact on the market style conversion.

Additionally, the November CPI data will soon be released, and the market generally expects this data to turn positive, thereby reinforcing the potential inflation expectations. Blue-chip sectors such as finance, real estate, coal, and nonferrous metals will benefit significantly. Therefore, from the perspective of preparing for potential inflation, the adjustment direction of main funds will facilitate a smooth shift in market style.

In fact, the transformation of market style has become inevitable. On one hand, according to the rules of market style rotation, due to the relatively full exploitation of thematic stocks in the short term, valuation constraints will inevitably inhibit further upward space. Meanwhile, as the index enters a relatively sensitive high stage, mainstream funds will inevitably choose blue-chip sectors with valuation advantages and relatively stable performance, effectively promoting the market style to revert back to the blue-chip sectors. Therefore, under the current environment where valuations of thematic stocks are generally high, capital entering the undervalued and steadily performing blue-chip sectors is an inherent requirement for market development.

It's worth mentioning that reviewing the market conditions in the first half of the year, after entering June, due to the temporary rest period of thematic stocks, the heavyweight blue-chip sectors represented by financial real estate, coal, and nonferrous metals once directed a phase of accelerated upward momentum. The current market situation is similar to the circumstances before and after June, suggesting that the market may replicate the accelerated upward momentum driven by blue-chip sectors seen in June and July. Therefore, in terms of strategy, investors should closely monitor the dynamics of hotspots and adapt to the market style conversion.

From the perspective of expiring funds, the central bank still faces considerable hedging pressure.

The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you're buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. Additionally, this commission is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price at which you intend to buy or sell the stock. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is six digits long. The code and abbreviation of the stock must match when placing a commission. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks being bought or sold: the quantity of stocks intended for purchase must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks intended for sale does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place a commission: counter submission, telephone automatic commission, computer automatic commission, and remote terminal commission.
