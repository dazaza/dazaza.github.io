WeChat Show-off: Obsessive-compulsive disorder or "small certainty happiness"?

by anonymous on 2013-11-25 11:22:37

In recent years, an internet term "food-flaunting tribe" has become popular, mainly referring to those who take pictures of their food before eating and upload them to share on social platforms like Weibo and WeChat Moments. A media survey reported that 40% of netizens admitted they belonged to the "food-flaunting tribe". Some said that members of the "food-flaunting tribe" might have obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD), while others mentioned that there is a sense of "small but certain happiness" in cooking fresh dishes and enjoying delicious food. Experts suggest that the "food-flaunting tribe" should be cautious about becoming addicted to flaunting food.

Food-flaunting OCD: "Let me take a picture first before I eat!"

"Let me take a picture first before I eat!" Nowadays, whether it's in a high-end fashionable restaurant or a small food stall by the roadside, or even at home with the otaku crowd, this scene is not uncommon.

Major social networking sites are the "main battlefield" for the "food-flaunting tribe" to show off their food and broadcast "food-teasing photos."

Yu Juan, who lives in Wuhan, Hubei Province, modestly flaunts her homemade weekend lunch in her WeChat Moments: "Carrot braised chicken leg, mustard greens with shredded meat, coarse grain staple food - not much to look at, but the taste isn't bad!"

In Yu Juan's WeChat "Moments," there are also pictures of the various delicious foods she has eaten in different places. Like Yu Juan, "let the phone eat first, take a picture whenever you encounter good food, and post every picture" are common characteristics of the "food-flaunting tribe."

On Sina Weibo, searching for "food-flaunting tribe" yields nearly 100,000 related posts, and these Weibo users do not hesitate to admit they belong to the "food-flaunting tribe." Some "advanced food-flaunters" use Tencent Weibo to broadcast their "food-flaunting diaries" under the topic of "food-flaunting tribe."

A media survey reported that 40% of netizens admitted they were part of the "food-flaunting tribe," with some choosing to "only take pictures for big meals," while others "take pictures for every meal." One netizen said, "If I don't take a picture, I always feel like I haven't enjoyed the meal well." After reports surfaced about the tendency of the "food-flaunting tribe" to have OCD, many netizens joked that they had been "hit while lying down."

Confessions of Small Happiness: "Cooking fresh dishes is like governing a great nation"

Yu Juan's "modest flaunting" of "food-teasing photos" quickly drew comments and likes from friends. In the back-and-forth chat with her friends, Yu Juan arranged several gatherings with her "foodie" friends, naturally earning praise for her "good cooking skills." The ancient saying "governing a great nation is like cooking fresh dishes" was altered by many "food-flaunting tribe" members who enjoy cooking to "cooking fresh dishes is like governing a great nation," revealing their self-showing-off intentions completely.

Yu Juan told reporters that she is a newly graduated "white-collar worker." It's rare for her to take the time on weekends to carefully prepare a delicious meal, and being able to share her insights with friends and interact through casual chats brings her a sense of happiness amidst her busy life.

"A dead-of-night knock on the door startled me. I opened the door to find a delivery guy holding a lot of hairy crabs outside. I said, 'You must have made a mistake; I didn't order takeout.' The delivery guy replied, 'This is your WeChat friend asking me to let you see what he's having tonight. His phone is broken, so he can't post on his Moments.'" This joke about "hardcore food-flaunters" quickly went viral, resonating with the "food-flaunting tribe," who all gave it a thumbs-up.

For many "food-flaunting tribe" members, flaunting food is a fashion statement, a form of social interaction, and more importantly, a "small but certain happiness."

Netizen "Strong Si Jia" said: "The 'food-flaunting tribe' isn't showing off wealth like rich people. What they flaunt isn't just delicious food; it's the lonely and bored heart that yearns for attention!"

Netizen "Yan Potato" said: "I used to dislike people who liked taking pictures of food, thinking it was vanity and pretentiousness. Later, as I hustled through life, I realized that being able to make food look both delicious and beautiful in pictures is also a sign of loving life."

Excessive "food-flaunting" may lead to OCD

Wuhan Kangde Tongji Psychological Counseling expert and national level one psychological counselor Shen Meng said that living in a fast-paced urban environment, the "food-flaunting tribe" uses "showing off food" to alleviate inner anxiety and irritability, release stress from life and work, record the little happiness of daily life, and achieve interpersonal interaction through online social platforms, which is worthy of affirmation.

However, Shen Meng also pointed out that if someone excessively flaunts food, such as "taking pictures for every meal" or "always feeling like the meal wasn't good enough without taking a picture," they could fall into a state of dependence on their phone, leading to OCD. Moreover, it might also cause annoyance or disturbance to others.

Experts suggest that the "food-flaunting tribe" should pay attention to the occasion, consider others' feelings, and reasonably flaunt food. At the same time, they should increase face-to-face interactions and sharing with their "foodie" friends in real life.
