March economic data shows mild interest rate hike is not helpful

by huaxun892 on 2010-04-15 13:16:55

Last month, the CPI growth rate was as high as 2.7%, and PPI at 5.4%. When the market saw these data, countless experts and scholars created a public opinion on interest rate increase. At that time, the author pointed out that the February data mainly came from seasonal factors, carry-over effect and imported factors rather than endogenous factors, so we must carefully look at the problem of data rise and cannot generalize.

The March data is more reasonable. According to the CPI data statistics released today, the CPI increased by 2.4% year-on-year in March, while the PPI was at 5.9%. Although the CPI fell slightly on a monthly basis, the PPI still maintained an upward trend.

CPI is mainly affected by food prices. The March data showed that the food price increase was 5.2%, which decreased compared to 6.2% in February. However, non-food prices remained steady at 1%. Why do we pay so much attention to the price changes of food and non-food? This is mainly because the demand for food is rigid. No matter how the benchmark interest rate changes, the demand for food will not decrease. In fact, the change in interest rates affects industry the most. Since non-food prices remain stable at 1%, it is difficult for inflation to be endogenous under the current situation.

The central government's No. 1 document has repeatedly addressed issues related to agriculture, rural areas, and farmers. Regarding the promotion of rural consumption and increasing farmers' income, the central government's attitude is very clear: increase farmers' income and investment in rural areas. The rise in food prices is beneficial to farmers. This is reflected in the data, where urban consumer prices increased by 2.3% in March, while rural prices increased by 2.6%.

And last year's price dropped by 1.2%, so the carry-over effect still exists. But the current CPI does not consider the real situation after excluding the carry-over effect, this point must be clear. The current rise in consumer goods prices is due to the low base last year, not normal and reasonable price increases. For example, a product priced at 100 yuan in 2008, the price dropped by 1.2% in March 2009, making it 98.8 yuan. In March 2010, the price rose by 2.4% on the basis of 98.8 yuan, only reaching 101 yuan. Therefore, when dealing with CPI data, one must not just read the surface, as that would lead to distortion of data and misinterpretation.

In terms of PPI, there are also such problems. Last year, the PPI fell by 6% during the same period, but in March this year, it reached as high as 5.9%, with a fluctuation of nearly 12% in between. Such serious carry-over effects would undoubtedly greatly reduce the authenticity of the PPI data. The influence of imported factors is more serious compared to February. The production material output price increased by 6.8% in February, and reached 7.4% in March, a significant increase within just one month. Moreover, the purchase price of raw materials, fuel, and power increased by as high as 11.5%. Such serious imported factors are the reasons for the rise in PPI.

After resource-related commodity prices are aligned with international markets, international bulk commodity prices will be transmitted to the domestic market faster. In March, international crude oil and London copper prices rose significantly again compared to February, so resource prices naturally appear high.

Therefore, under the current environment, raising interest rates is not beneficial. Under the influence of imported and carry-over factors, raising interest rates at this time would make the environment worse. Resource commodity prices are driven by the rise in international commodity prices, and international bulk prices are controlled abroad. Can our country's interest rate increase curb the rise in bulk commodity prices? Can it resist the influence of imported factors? It cannot. So under the circumstances where enterprises need to spend more money to purchase raw materials while maintaining the same production quantity, this would increase the capital demand of enterprises. If an interest rate increase occurs, it would undoubtedly cause double blows to enterprises.

From the trade data, a trade deficit appeared in March, with import growth faster than export growth, import prices continuously rising, but export prices unable to rise, frequently encountering anti-dumping investigations, and export volume continuously declining, proving that China's trade conditions are gradually changing. General trade exports have plummeted sharply, while processing trade shows growth. The March export data shows that our overall trade surplus depends on processing trade, while general trade exports have rapidly decreased. The current export trade environment in our country is also very unfavorable.

In the monetary market, the growth rates of M0, M1, and M2 continue to fall. This year's tightening policy is a normal situation, but it absolutely does not mean a reduction in liquidity. This is mainly due to the excessively large amount of currency issued last year, which inevitably leads to the continuous recovery of excessive liquidity. Last year, the M2 growth value was nearly 30%, a number that would definitely trigger hyperinflation in previous years. Therefore, continuously recovering excessive liquidity is inevitable, but the overall situation still maintains growth.
