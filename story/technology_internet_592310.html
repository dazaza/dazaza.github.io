Vanke's Wang Shi stated on Weibo that the company's refusal to bribe has become a competitive advantage for Vanke.

by edivista on 2011-11-28 17:03:03

Click to enter Wang Shi's Weibo

Just before the Spring Festival of 2011, Wang Shi, chairman of Vanke, finally embarked on his long-planned study tour in the United States. The industry generally believed that this move represented Wang Shi's gradual retirement from his position as the helmsman of the industry leader. However, a Vanke spokesperson clearly stated that being able to study at a world-renowned university had been Wang Shi's dream since he was young.

During his studies in the United States, the board of directors held meetings via video conferencing, and Wang Shi's identity as chairman of Vanke has never changed. He recorded what he saw and heard during his study tour on Weibo, including many beautiful pictures of plants and trees. In his view, paying attention to and highlighting the details of life is not contradictory to having a broad vision and social responsibility, but rather complementary.

The following is a partial compilation of some of Wang Shi's classic quotes:

1. Q: How do you manage a business while studying?

A: Managing a business was not affected even while climbing Mount Everest, and the environment here is much better than in the snow mountains.

Q: What do you think about the severe real estate regulation?

A: It is starting to take effect. If the policy is adhered to, a soft landing is possible.

Q: Are you not worried about Vanke's performance?

A: When house prices soar, all companies in the industry perform well, making it hard to distinguish Vanke. It's only when the market tightens that true skills are revealed. Isn't it?

2. Q: What will you study at Harvard?

A: Corporate ethics. The foundation of corporate systems is a product of capitalist culture. Tracing back to its roots, the influence of Protestant Christianity on the capitalist mindset... further down the line, it leads to Jerusalem. My next plan includes Europe and Israel.

Q: Buddhism is vast and profound, encompassing all kinds of knowledge.

A: In terms of modern corporate systems, there isn't much relation to Buddhist thought.

3. Q: Why does the modern corporate system originate from Christian civilization?

A: The modern corporate system is a corporate form under the modern economic system, originating from countries like Britain and the Netherlands in the 17th century. During this period, Christianity dominated the ideological landscape of European society. The religious reform initiated by Calvin in the mid-16th century provided a theoretical basis for the bourgeois revolution. Weber's "Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism" discusses this relationship.

4. Q: Do you cook at Harvard, Mr. Wang?

A: Before sleeping, put a few grains of rice into a thermos, fill it with boiling water, and it becomes congee the next morning; then boil it in a small pot for a few minutes, and breakfast is ready.

Q: Isn't that too simple?

A: There's also toast and grapefruit slices. I need to read materials before class and won't waste time cooking. Leftover congee can be reheated in the evening and still tastes good...

5. Q: Are there many social engagements at Harvard?

A: No. If I accepted all invitations, my time would be wasted. Here, time is not enough; learning language takes up half a day, following two main courses requires at least two hours of reading relevant books and materials; I also need to keep track of the dynamics of the industry, Vanke, and NGOs, which means at least one hour of domestic calls every day. Calculating it, where is there time for social engagements? Besides, people at Harvard are very busy and have no time for socializing...

6. Q: Will you return to Vanke after finishing at Harvard?

A: I haven't left, so how can I talk about returning.

Q: My meaning is to play a greater role in society.

A: The purpose of systematic study is twofold: personal cultivation; secondly, considering devoting more energy to teaching in universities in the next ten years, so after countless battles, one must read. As for playing a greater role in society, life is like a parabola, my peak has already passed, the future belongs to the younger generation. The younger generation represents the future!

7. Q: You once said if you had to choose, you would choose Christianity?

A: It's not about believing or not believing in God, but rather a comparative preference at the cultural level such as architecture, music, painting, etc. For example, Gothic Christian architecture is tall and soaring, sunlight passing through the rose window shines into the hall, giving warmth, uplift, and upward momentum; Buddhist temples unfold parallel, their exteriors are splendid and secular, but the interior lacks light transparency and gives a sense of oppression...

8. Q: It's hard to be a good person when social morals are declining.

A: Is it easy to be a bad person? Both parties involved in bribery and corruption will suffer from guilt, why should they drink themselves drunk in social gatherings, numbing themselves.

Q: You say no bribery now, what about before? Also, some say the chairman doesn't need to bribe, but what about subordinates?

A: Not bribing is the bottom line, Vanke did not allow any employee to engage in bribery in the past or present.

Q: That's hard.

A: Actually doing it, it's not as difficult as imagined.

9. Q: Can you get good land without transactions under the table?

A: No. Vanke's plots are far from the city center with inconvenient transportation, and the land price is expensive. In the late 1980s, there was a company that issued stocks at the same time as Vanke and also engaged in real estate. Its ability to acquire land through agreements was ten times that of Vanke, and the price was cheaper. But this company delisted many years ago. While Vanke, which acquired expensive and remote land, survived well. Not bribing enhanced competitiveness.

10. Q: How does not bribing become Vanke's competitiveness?!

A: Not bribing is a basic requirement for enterprises. If over 90% of enterprises comply, it naturally does not constitute competitiveness. But if bribery and corruption form a social trend, non-bribing enterprises will be at an unfavorable operating position. What to do? Either go with the flow, or find a way out in the market, how to create products and services that consumers like on plots that are remote and expensive. Moreover, operating without bribing gives one confidence!

11. Q: You emphasize that Vanke does not accept bribes, how to ensure no bribery occurs?

A: It's impossible to completely prevent bribery, but once discovered, we will pursue responsibility without leniency. There were several cases where bribery occurred, and Vanke's personnel handed over the suspects to the procuratorial organs, demonstrating Vanke's stance. Regular and irregular financial audits are conducted for frontline management, avoiding the use of relatives, etc., to minimize the occurrence of bribery. Establishing good professional ethics among employees is the responsibility of the management.
