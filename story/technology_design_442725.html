To further enrich the monetary policy regulation tools and restrain blind expansion of money credit

The 2011 credit management, under the clear condition that the increment of credit needs to fall back from this year, it is understood that the central bank has recently passed a plan for dynamic adjustment of differential reserve requirement ratio. Through dynamically adjusting the differentiated deposit reserve ratio, it aims to explore further improvement in the targeting, flexibility, and effectiveness of macro-control. From the source, it will control the total credit valve, which will be significantly different from the current direct loan limits or loan stoppage management methods.

Market-based regulation must be gradual

A credit target may still exist next year

The central bank has recently convened a meeting with key commercial bank executives to discuss reforms in the credit management system. The central bank will attempt to determine the speed of credit issuance based on economic growth speed, while guiding credit issuance according to each bank's capital adequacy ratio, liquidity ratio, dynamic provisioning rate, and loan-to-deposit ratio indicators.

Market analysts believe that if this action is implemented, it means that the central bank may no longer set credit targets. The use of differentiated deposit reserve ratios, targeted central bank bills, and other tools will become more frequent. Market analysts generally hold positive attitudes towards this.

However, regarding the effects of the new credit management system being discussed, industry insiders have some doubts.

For example, administrative regulation's effect is immediate, like separating chaff from wheat. However, the effect of guiding credit issuance based on capital adequacy ratio, liquidity ratio, and loan-to-deposit ratio indicators needs to be observed over a longer time cycle," said a relevant person in charge of the risk management department of a commercial bank yesterday.

The market probably can't do without administrative regulation. Another business executive from a major state-owned bank also frankly stated as such.

In fact, according to Xinhua Overseas Finance report, People's Bank of China advisor Zhou Qiren expressed yesterday that China might cancel the annual bank loan quota as part of a long-term reform plan but would likely still set a lending target for next year.

Understand the people

Real estate dynamics articles provided by www.lin23.com

The news that the real estate industry might catch the last bus of the revitalization plan stimulated a full-scale rise in real estate stocks. However, after the market closed, there were reports suggesting that the hope of including real estate in the revitalization plan might be lost. According to data tracked by various institutions, however, the trading volume of the real estate market in major cities has generally increased, undoubtedly adding confidence to the rise in real estate stocks.

The message indicates that the real estate industry might catch the last bus of the revitalization plan, among which the most eye-catching part is the possible cancellation of the "second home" restriction policy. This policy was once called "the last straw that broke the camel's back." If this policy is canceled, it will have a significant promoting effect on releasing improved housing demand. Some industry insiders indicated that under the strong stimulation of domestic demand by the state, the cancellation of the second home policy should be a natural result, but it will not necessarily lead to an increase in housing prices. The rise and fall of housing prices ultimately depend on the economic fundamentals. After the market closed yesterday, another report claimed that "the government might not include the real estate industry in this industrial revitalization plan due to its particularity and sensitivity."
