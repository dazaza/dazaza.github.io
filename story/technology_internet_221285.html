Village-to-village live broadcast satellite TV project in Jiangjin

by weixing123 on 2009-08-11 14:42:13

Grandma Li Shengkai is a beneficiary of Jiangjin District's vigorous implementation of the village-to-village live satellite TV installation project. Reporters learned yesterday that the district has already sent 9,435 sets of live satellite TV receiving equipment to 9,435 households in 629 natural villages across 26 towns (streets, management committees) in the entire district, solving the problem of difficulty in watching TV, and becoming the first district or county in our city to complete the construction of the live satellite TV project.

According to reports, in order to completely eliminate blind spots for radio and television, and allow villagers in remote areas to normally watch TV programs through the reception of live satellite TV, in March this year, Jiangjin officially launched the village-to-village live satellite TV project. It promptly established live satellite TV engineering work institutions at the district, town, and village levels, and implemented an engineering accountability system. The district Party committee and government clearly listed this work as a key project and a supervised project for serving the people, incorporating it into the annual assessment. To promote the installation progress, the district formed 20 installation teams responsible for installation and debugging, and various places hired more than 10 satellite TV technical personnel who understand technology and are familiar with local conditions to guide farmers in using the equipment. To strengthen supervision, both the district and town levels have established engineering supervision groups, forming a supervisory mechanism of irregular spot checks and regular inspections.

"With this 'precious thing', the house has become much livelier than before..." A few days ago, Li Shengkai, 59, from Yowan Village, Zhongshan Town, Jiangjin District, happily told reporters while turning on the TV. The "precious thing" mentioned by Grandma Li refers to the newly installed live satellite TV receiving equipment.

Grandma Li's home is located in a remote mountainous area in the south of Jiangjin. Due to geographical restrictions, it was unable to normally receive broadcast satellite TV installations. She said that before buying this color TV, her family was still watching a black-and-white TV from over 20 years ago, which could only receive two or three channels, and the signal often interrupted. At the end of June, after installing a live satellite TV receiving device with a diameter of about 45 centimeters, the newly purchased color TV can now receive 48 TV channels and listen to 43 radio channels, with stable signals and clear images.
