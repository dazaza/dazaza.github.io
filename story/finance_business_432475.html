Centipede Breeding Precautions and Benefit Analysis

What is a centipede? The centipede, also known as Tianlong, Hundred-footed Bug, or Few-spine Centipede, etc., common centipedes come in three varieties: red-headed, green-headed, and black-headed. Red-headed centipedes have backs that are reddish-black in color, abdomens that appear light red, and legs that are either light orange-red or yellow. Green-headed centipedes have blue backs and legs, with light blue abdomens, smaller bodies, and lengths about half that of the red-headed variety. Black-headed centipedes have black backs and legs, light yellow abdomens, and even smaller bodies. Among these three types, the red-headed centipede is considered the best due to its larger size, higher yield, gentle temperament, strong adaptability, and rapid growth, making it suitable for artificial breeding.

The Chinese people, through their long struggle against disease, have made comprehensive use and processing of the medicinal functions of centipedes. The famous Ming Dynasty physician Li Shizhen provided a thorough introduction to the medical uses of centipedes in his work "Compendium of Materia Medica." According to incomplete statistics, there are dozens of medicinal preparations using centipedes.

With the development of pharmaceutical sciences, the medical applications of centipedes have expanded, leading to an increasing demand for medicinal centipedes. Additionally, with the need for export and foreign exchange earnings, the market supply cannot meet the demand.

Centipedes have traditionally been despised by people, who consider them pests. Some people react strongly upon seeing one, exclaiming that it must be killed on sight. This prejudice based solely on appearance has mistakenly harmed this insect, which is highly beneficial to humanity, which is very regrettable. Centipedes are indispensable in medicine and have high value. In the Yueyang centipede recycling market, each centipede is worth around 1.5 yuan. With the growing demand for centipedes and their production reaching unprecedentedly low levels, what can be done?
