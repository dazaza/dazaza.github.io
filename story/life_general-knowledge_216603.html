**Stone Renovation Services: Marble Crystal Surface Restoration, Terrazzo, Granite Polishing**

Service Content: Waxing, polishing, renovation, repair, waterproof treatment, crystallization treatment, disease treatment, and joint leveling for marble, terrazzo, granite, and other stones.

---

1. **Waxing and Polishing**

Stone is a natural and environmentally friendly decorative material with vibrant colors and luxurious appeal, so its maintenance is crucial. Waxing seals the tiny pores on the stone surface that are invisible to the naked eye, preventing dust contamination and stain penetration, thereby extending its lifespan.

(1) **Cleaning the Floor:** Use a multi-functional floor cleaning machine in combination with vacuum and water suction machines, as well as stone-cleaning agents and tools for thorough cleaning.

(2) **Waxing:** Use professional waxing machines along with high-grade stone care wax for waxing treatment.

(3) **Polishing:** Use a high-speed polisher to achieve a clean, bright finish.

---

2. **Stone Disease Treatment**

The main diseases of stone include oil stains, water stains, coffee stains, and other colored stains that penetrate the stone, causing discoloration. Professional imported chemicals are used to remove these stains, restoring the stone to its original color.

---

3. **Stone Renovation**

For stones affected by pollution or disease, such as uneven surfaces, deep scratches, or heavy stains, an imported stone renovator is used in conjunction with multiple sets of grinding discs and pads. The process involves coarse-to-fine grinding and polishing, allowing the stone to regain its original appearance.

---

4. **Crystallization Hardening Treatment**

After several years of use, the surface of the stone may age and lose its luster. Crystallization hardening treatment involves using a powder grinder to chemically react with the calcium components in the stone, forming a new crystallized layer on the surface. This prevents stains from penetrating deeper into the stone, enhances wear resistance, and restores the natural shine of the stone, achieving a certain level of hardness and brightness.

---

5. **Stone Protection**

To prevent future water stains, rust spots, or other contamination on marble, we implement protective measures after grinding. Learning from past experiences where protection caused discoloration, we tested several top-tier waterproofing agents on identical stone samples to check both their protective effects and whether they altered the stone's color. Based on the water absorption rate, we determined the appropriate application dosage. Only after completing these preparations did we proceed with large-scale protective treatment on the floor.

---

6. **Crystal Treatment**

After the above processes, crystal treatment can be performed. We use Spanish crystal hardeners and gloss enhancers, following their usage instructions. Using equipment under pressure, low-speed grinding generates a thermal reaction, creating a non-slip, hardened mirror surface on the floor.

---

7. **Joint Leveling**

For newly installed stone floors with uneven surfaces or cracked joints, we perform multiple rounds of grinding and use German stone filler with strong adhesion for crack sealing and crystal surface treatment. This ensures a smooth, flat, and seamless surface overall.

---

8. **Old Solid Wood Flooring Renovation**

(1) **Grinding:** Use large-scale wood flooring renovation machinery to grind off the paint layer, surface layer, and about 0.5 millimeters of the wooden layer. Then carefully sand the floor to make it smooth and fine, restoring it to a brand-new state. Use small corner grinders for edges and corners to ensure overall smoothness.

(2) **Sealing Joints:** Remove the original transparent putty from the gaps, reseal them, and apply a primer to the entire floor surface. Allow it to dry.

(3) **First Coat of Finish:** After the primer dries, apply the first coat of finish and allow it to dry.

(4) **Sandpaper Smoothing:** Use wet sandpaper to carefully polish the surface until slightly rough, removing any dust.

(5) **Repeat Coating:** Apply three to four coats of finish according to the above procedure, without sanding on the last coat.

(6) **Final Step:** After the final coat dries completely, apply floor wax.

---

**Situations Where Full Renewal May Not Be Achievable:**

1. Moisture at the edges causing mold and discoloration.

2. Swelling of boards due to moisture exposure.

3. Severe damage to the floor.
