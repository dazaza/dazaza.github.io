It is bound to make us unforgettable. What is it exactly?

by atijvnzd04 on 2012-03-08 10:45:18

Touching others' feelings, browsing through others' experiences, and silently reading others' stories. Always giggling foolishly, wondering if I was just as foolish back then. Remembering the solemn promises of never breaking up, the selfless giving for each other, and the vows of eternal love. Then why did someone break the promise first? Whose giving became humble first? Who lost those vows first? Always pondering whether it's because I'm not good enough, or you stopped loving me, or you fell in love with someone else. Some people always say that words spoken are like water spilled, they can't be taken back. So what about love? Once given, can it never be reclaimed? There are always too many things we shouldn't have said, too many memories collected and mourned. But we still miss them, yet missing them brings us no closer to the past. Letting go is too hard for everyone, time moves slower and slower. We try our best to erase the figure in our minds, but why does it never work? Forget, forget, is it truly forgetting or remembering? What exactly makes us unable to forget? Is it the once said lifetime commitment, the beautiful memories, or the people we can't let go? Is it the deep regret, or that heart-breaking phrase "I don't love you"? Not loving, not loving, is it because love has ceased, or there was never love at all? Unable to forget, we cry our hearts out, torment ourselves, tear open wounds to look at them, emotions pulling us aimlessly. In our moods, it's you; in our words, it's you; in our diaries, it's you; in our messages, it's you; in our tears, it's you; in our hearts, it's you. Endless memories flood out like a tidal wave, overwhelming yearning leaks out bit by bit. Can you see how much sorrow your unforgettable presence has brought? And the farewell sentence you wrote yourself, turning around and holding someone else's hand, walking further and further away. No time for a "why", no waiting for a reply, who left too hastily? Is the unforgettable sorrow caused by you? Is it your phrase of not loving? Is it your warmth? Is it your memory? Is it the scenes of our laughter? Is it our exclusive stage? But the stage has changed people, you mercilessly expelled me, even begrudging me the identity of a bystander. You said I'm not the main character in your life, and those youthful vows were just a mirage. Those most beautiful vows have already drifted far away with the wind. The vicissitudes of time prevent sadness from playing out. The passionate moth-to-flame passion was extinguished by your final hurtful words. Remember in the pre-marriage trial there was a line like this: "To love someone, you must hurt her/him first, because guilt is the best way to maintain love." Why did mutual harm lead to our parting ways, never to meet again? What exactly is the reason, who should be blamed, who should be hated? Love and hate are like two ends of a rope, constantly pulling and tugging back and forth. But why not let go of both ends, when what truly holds us back is our own hearts. An obstinate thought keeps us unforgettable.
