Driver hits and kills pedestrian, flees and lets younger brother take the blame

by wangxintong3 on 2012-03-03 01:04:00

Through on-site visits, a witness spotted the license plate number of one of the hit-and-run vehicles, which was identified as a red SUV. Following this lead, the police obtained the contact information of the vehicle owner, Mr. Yi Zhang, and made contact with him. Zhang claimed he was on a business trip in Changde and not in Yiyang, but his speech was hesitant and evasive, leading the police to determine that Zhang was a major suspect.

Soon after, technical investigation departments provided feedback indicating Zhang's location near Heshan Temple in the city. The police immediately informed their unit leaders of the situation and advised Zhang's family to cooperate, requesting Zhang to surrender himself. Not long after, Zhang’s nephew called to inform that Zhang agreed to surrender within an hour. However, when the police attempted to call Zhang again, his phone was already turned off.

Officer Guo Daohong immediately rushed to the scene and reported to the team leader. Team Leader Yu Xiang and Deputy Team Leader Yuan Yiping subsequently arrived at the scene to guide the investigation and interview work, reporting the accident situation separately to the Municipal Bureau and the Brigade duty leaders. Deputy Director of Yiyang Public Security Bureau, Zhou Jianjun and Guo Luwu, respectively issued important instructions requiring full efforts to solve the case. Deputy Brigade Commander Tang Jianwen personally supervised the command center for case-solving operations.

A pedestrian was struck and killed, and the driver fled the scene.

At 2:45 AM on February 27th, someone came to the station to surrender, claiming to be the driver of the red SUV involved in the hit-and-run. However, upon questioning by the police, the "suspect" revealed numerous inconsistencies. Testing showed his alcohol content was zero, and his physical features did not match the description given by witnesses, suggesting he was attempting to take the fall for someone else.

At 3:15 AM, when the police arrived at the hotel, the staff said the room had already been vacated. The police immediately conducted checks around Heshan Temple, including hospitals, hotels, inns, streets, as well as Zhang's previous residences and workplace.

At approximately 8:40 PM on February 26th, the traffic accident handling team of Taoyang County received a directive from the 110 Command Center: A member of the public reported an accident on Longzhou Road at the Yi'er Kang section where a pedestrian was struck and killed by two cars, both of which fled the scene.

After repeated ideological work by the police, the "suspect" finally confessed the truth: He was Zhang's younger brother, and the actual perpetrator was Zhang, who was currently at a certain hotel.

Upon learning this, Deputy Brigade Commander Tang Xiwen decisively decided: Dispatch two groups of police forces along the Yi-Taoyang Expressway towards Taoyang for pursuit and notify the Taoyang emergency response team to assist in setting up roadblocks. He immediately reported to the Municipal Bureau leadership, requesting the technical investigation department to cooperate in applying technical investigative measures against the suspect, personally going to Zhang's home to communicate with his family.

At around 4:00 AM, with no way out, Zhang had no choice but to turn himself in to the station and admitted without reservation to driving under the influence and fleeing the scene after causing the accident. Currently, the suspect Zhang has been criminally detained. Another hit-and-run car driver, Zhang Moumou, did not voluntarily surrender until two hours later.

On February 27th, through close cooperation between the Technical Investigation Division of Yiyang Public Security Bureau and Taoyang County Traffic Police Brigade, the Taoyang County Directly Affiliated Brigade solved a major traffic accident escape case within 8 hours.

Subsequently, a taxi driver at the accident scene reported to the police that he heard about a vehicle similar in model and color to the hit-and-run vehicle asking for tire replacement at the train station. He described the driver's appearance and suspected drunkenness. When the police arrived at the train station, the suspect had already driven away. Through questioning taxi drivers and other witnesses near the train station, they learned that the vehicle was heading towards Taoyang.
