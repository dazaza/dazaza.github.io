1. What are the procedures for opening an online store?

Just follow the website's instructions to register. Generally, people who want to sell things need to register their ID numbers, then scan or fax their ID to the website. After verification, the website allows you to sell things.

2. What are the costs of opening an online store?

The main cost is the source of goods, and other things can be borrowed if you don't have them.

For example, you can go to an internet cafe to surf the web, borrow digital cameras and scanners from friends.

If you want to do it more formally, you need to prepare a computer, broadband, digital camera, and scanning equipment.

A second-hand computer costing over 2000 RMB can get you a pretty good one.

A digital camera should be of better quality so that it can take good pictures of detailed products, a 3000 RMB digital camera generally meets the requirements. This cannot be compromised because pictures are very important.

A scanner costing 400-500 RMB is sufficient.

3. How do you prepare and organize your sources of goods?

Sources of goods are very important.

Offline, you can go to large wholesale markets to purchase goods, or negotiate agency agreements with manufacturers or brands.

Online, you can find websites, many small shopping websites have wholesale businesses, generally if you purchase a certain amount in one go, you can become an agent.

You need to investigate whether the supplier is honest and whether they can offer a certain return policy (for example, if there are quality issues or unsold goods within a certain period, they can be returned), which greatly reduces your risk;

If you can negotiate with the supplier to first post some pictures online, and only take the goods when a customer wants them, that would be even better. This can reduce your investment to the minimum, but sometimes it happens that online customers want something that has already been sold offline, leaving no goods available. At this point, it gets a bit awkward, or constantly getting single items from the supplier may annoy them, so having stock on hand is also necessary.

To see if a certain item sells well, you can first search on the website, see how many people are selling similar items, including identical or similar ones, how their sales are going, how product prices are positioned, how the store is designed, how products are introduced, etc. This is also the time to study and analyze the market, truly "knowing oneself and knowing the enemy."

Generally, items that sell well online are mainly:

Items available offline but much cheaper online, such as digital products;

Items available offline but with few agency points, unavailable in many places, with good quality assurance, such as our nutritional weight loss products. Currently, the company has very few agents nationwide, only available online, and service is very important. Even if someone can buy it offline, if our service is better at the same price, they are willing to buy from us. Some local specialty crafts and foods also fall under this category.

Items not available offline but only online, such as many foreign trade order products or famous products directly brought back from abroad.

Operating Secrets

Managing an online store, perhaps we don't have much experience, so it is particularly necessary to constantly summarize and exchange ideas with others, thus gaining something every day.

1. Frequently add new items. Let people have something to look at, surprises. More items mean more choices for buyers. However, don't increase quantity without considering quality. Don't give up on stocking up because of poor business! If your items always remain the same, it will bore others. Buyers will get tired of seeing the same items repeatedly.

2. Set different price levels for items. It's best to have both low-priced and high-priced items in your store. Adjust the properties and quotes of items in a timely manner. For example, if you're selling jade, because it's genuine and expensive, initially, no one looks at it, let alone buys it! What to do? Suggestion: Purchase some cheap jade! Without changing the nature of your store, set up one-yuan bids or discounted sales! Gaining popularity is the fundamental goal of Taobao stores! As long as you have transactions regularly! As long as you receive good reviews! More friends will trust your store and come to buy items from you. When launching new items, it's best to simultaneously launch a new signature, letting this moving advertisement promptly inform everyone that you have new items!

3. Offer discounts to buyers. Second-time visitors can get a 10% discount, third-time visitors can get a 20% discount, fourth-time visitors can get a 30% discount, and all subsequent purchases can get a 30% discount (you can design according to specific situations). Or if the purchase exceeds a certain amount, gifts can be given, discounts can be offered, and shipping fees can be waived, etc. In this way, you might earn less per transaction, but considering the long term, retaining many old buyers has many benefits.

4. Regularly hold promotional events. Regular discounts or gifts. Choose one or two items, mainly to attract others to your store. In conjunction with the event, change your signature to introduce the event, and also announce this "good news" in other places. Whatever event you organize, any dynamics you have on Taobao (such as being featured on the homepage, recommended on the homepage, etc.), you should promptly inform your friends around you and your online friends, etc. Make full use of every resource to promote yourself.

5. Start bidding from one yuan. This trick is very effective and can gather popularity in a short time. But be prepared to sell the item for one yuan. Therefore, it's not recommended to bid one yuan on too valuable items. In any case, think carefully, choosing one yuan means accepting the risk of selling for one yuan. While conducting a one-yuan auction, it's best to run some advertisements, letting everyone know your "discount" policy. The purpose of a one-yuan auction is to attract buyers to casually browse your other items through this item. Prepare for the worst, if it really ends up being sold for one yuan, you must definitely sell it, otherwise, your credit will be affected!

6. Use Alipay. It's best to have a bank card from Industrial and Commercial Bank of China, as it's the fastest way to collect payments. Although using Alipay is not as fast as direct transfers from buyers, it gives buyers greater confidence. Especially for sellers with low credit ratings, to make buyers confidently buy items from your store, use Alipay! Using Alipay for payment, the handling fee for remittances is covered by Taobao, and it can also save shipping costs for buyers.

7. Give small gifts. You can keep the buyer unaware beforehand, and when they receive the items you sent along with the gift, they will surely be happy. Gifts don't have to be expensive, it's the thought that counts. Regardless of whether your buyer will continue shopping in the future, treat them as friends, face them sincerely. There will certainly be good returns.

8. Calmly deal with buyers who bid but don't buy. When selling items online, you will inevitably encounter buyers who bid but don't buy, which is quite normal. It was painful the first time it happened, but later, you shouldn't worry too much about it. Because Taobao is huge, with buyers from all over the country, and even many foreign buyers. Many reasons can lead to buyers bidding but not buying: for example, they changed their mind, forgot their password, started bidding just for fun, suddenly had something urgent and couldn't go online for a short period, etc. Sellers need to maintain a peaceful mindset. Only then can they face more intense competition among online stores in the future.
