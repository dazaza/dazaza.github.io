After being defrauded 16 times and losing 60,000 yuan, the woman became the girlfriend of the fraudster

by xue94fwsh on 2012-02-29 14:23:38

Excuse for helping her find a job

By Tu Jing, Chongqing Evening News

Ms. He stated that after being deceived by Tian, SiSi was in tears every day. Even though the Spring Festival is approaching, SiSi still chose to go far away to calm down.

He: He said he was a civil servant, his father ran a mine, and his family had connections. As adults, we couldn't manage our children's affairs. We just trusted him too much and wanted him to help.

A mouth that can talk endlessly

The police investigation revealed that Tian's identity as a civil servant, his father running a mine, and his wealthy family background were all false. In fact, he was a temporary worker at a local unit. When Tian was caught by the police, there were only two bills in his wallet - one 50 cents and one 10 cents.

When he first met her, he claimed he could find her a teaching job, deceiving her 16 times and swindling 60,000 yuan from her. After the matter of the job went unresolved, she didn't become suspicious but instead was moved by him and became his girlfriend.

SiSi's family was impressed. Tian lied about needing to pay a deposit and took 20,000 yuan from SiSi's parents on October 24, 2010. Two days later, Tian took another 40,000 yuan from SiSi's parents, claiming it would be used as a down payment along with the previous 20,000 yuan.

Yesterday, Chongqing Evening News communicated with Ms. He, SiSi's mother.

16 times swindled 60,000 yuan

The female protagonist: SiSi (pseudonym), born in 1987, from Dadukou, with a junior college education, an employee at a decoration company in Dadukou.

The male protagonist: Suspect Tian, born in 1988, from Xiushan, with a high school education. Claimed to be a civil servant, his father running a mine, and his family wealthy. In reality, he was a temporary worker at a local unit.

Afterwards, Tian took between 10,000 to 20,000 yuan multiple times from SiSi's parents under the pretext of treating guests to dinner and giving travel expenses to those handling the matter.

On the day Tian was arrested, SiSi sent a text message to Tian's phone: I have confirmed that all the certificates are forged.

SiSi still couldn't become a teacher. Tian told her that because he had frequently asked favors for her job, his superior found out. The superior was very angry and fired him.

SiSi felt guilty and also developed feelings for Tian, eventually becoming his girlfriend.

"We trusted him too much"

In 2010, SiSi's family received a compensation payment due to demolition, and the whole family was busy buying a house.

SiSi's family wanted to buy a house of seventy to eighty square meters. Tian told SiSi's parents that this type of apartment in the housing development would cost seven or eight hundred thousand yuan, but he could get it for 500,000 yuan.

This story, which seems like a TV drama plot, actually happened in Dadukou District.

To become a teacher, one must first have a teacher qualification certificate. Tian made SiSi pay three times: the first time 3,000 yuan, the second time 4,000 yuan, and the third time 7,000 yuan.

After obtaining the "teacher qualification certificate," Tian took money from SiSi each time under various pretexts such as "thanking the school principal Mr. Yang" and "going to the school to pay respects," ranging from 3,000 yuan to over ten thousand yuan each time.

At this point, they had already paid more than 200,000 yuan for this house.

Reporter (hereinafter referred to as "Rep"): What was your impression of Tian before knowing he was suspected of fraud?

Ms. He (hereinafter referred to as "He"): He was very good at talking, sweet-talking, but gave an unreliable feeling.

Rep: At the time, Tian said he would help SiSi change jobs, but failed to do so. Why did you still believe him?

When he was arrested, he only had 60 cents.

In May 2011, Tian handed over a property deed to SiSi's parents, claiming everything was settled. This time, he received 30,000 yuan in gratuity from SiSi's parents.

In August 2011, when the housing development delivered the house, SiSi's parents happily brought the property deed to the sales office to see the house, only to find out that the apartment on the property deed had not been sold.

SiSi said that after discovering the property deed was fake, she went to Chongqing University, where Tian claimed he obtained her teacher qualification certificate, and learned that the certificate was also fake.

Chongqing Evening News learned that Tian, approximately 1.7 meters tall, with a high school education and average looks, neither talented nor wealthy, how could he repeatedly succeed in deception? "The key is that he has a mouth that can talk endlessly," said the police officer handling the case.

Tian told SiSi that he was a civil servant, originally from Xiushan, and his father ran a mine in his hometown. Tian voluntarily offered to help her work in her major, teaching at a certain experimental primary school in Dadukou. Tian confidently claimed that his family had connections, but some fees would be required. Both SiSi and her family expressed understanding.

In 2009, SiSi met Tian. At that time, SiSi graduated from a normal school and worked at a decoration company in Dadukou, working very hard.

Excuse for being able to buy a house at a low price

Defrauded her family of over 200,000 yuan

Tian told SiSi's parents that his father and the developer "Mr. Liu" of a certain housing development in Dadukou were close friends, and he could buy a house at a low price.

Yesterday, Chongqing Evening News learned from the Dadukou police that Tian was suspected of fraud and had been criminally detained.
