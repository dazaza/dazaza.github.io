Perspective One: Timing Grasping

We predict that the upcoming rally will span across years, and the selection of time and sectors/individual stocks are two areas worth paying attention to.

In terms of timing, it needs to be understood from both the time period and intra-day turning points. From the weekly chart trend, the most important pressure facing the stock index above is the 120-week moving average, which we have mentioned multiple times in our previous analysis. Based on historical experience, breaking through this moving average largely depends on the help of events. Next week is the peak period for the release of November data, and the release of positive data such as CPI is expected to form a positive stimulus.

For the short-term Friday's movement, considering the weekend effect, our prediction of the intensity of fund inflow tends to be conservative. However, this does not affect the sector rotation and the accumulation of strength before the market makes its final push.

Technically speaking, the recent 5-day moving average is turning upwards. The consolidation trend of the daily K-line on Thursday can be judged as waiting for the upward movement of the 5-day moving average. Once the 5-day moving average crosses above the 10-day moving average, the strength of the market will be further strengthened. The turning point during trading mainly lies in the activation of heavyweight stocks and the year-on-year increase in trading volume. But it can be predicted that before the overall start, due to the existence of previously trapped chips, there is still a possibility of significant fluctuations in the market. Investors should seize the opportunity for portfolio adjustment and stock replacement.

Perspective Two: Sector Hotspots Grasping

We believe that the layout for the next phase of the market should closely revolve around the important clue of economic data. In the soon-to-be-released November economic data, the biggest highlight is the "CPI" turning positive. After the financial crisis, the monthly CPI index has been in a negative state, which once triggered concerns about deflation in the market. Following the successful breakout of PMI above 50, the CPI index turning positive is another important indicator for the market to examine the results of economic recovery. Once successfully turned positive, it not only removes the concern of deflation but also further verifies the authenticity of economic recovery.

Under the current CPI data statistical system, resources, agricultural products, and real estate are the three major data source regions. The turning positive of the CPI is simply due to the stimulating effect brought by the price increase of products in these three directions. An appropriate positive value of the CPI data is conducive to healthy economic development. Therefore, we expect that the subsequent values will still have room for further growth. On this basis, related sectors such as agriculture, metals, and chemicals have enterprise profit growth space due to price increases, which is worth considering as an important reference for cross-year layout.

Overall, under the premise of a positively developing large wave trend, the recent fluctuations in the market will provide better opportunities for adjusting positions and replacing stocks for the next stage layout. Operationally, focus on sectors benefiting from economic data and actively wait for the start of the general offensive.

The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. Moreover, this order is only valid on the day the order is placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price at which you are buying or selling. The abbreviation of a stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is six digits long. The code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent when placing an order. At the same time, there are certain rules regarding the quantity of stocks being traded: the quantity of stocks being purchased must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks being sold does not need to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place an order: counter submission orders, telephone automatic orders, computer automatic orders, and remote terminal orders.
