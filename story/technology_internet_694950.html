Taxi driver runs three red lights to send a pregnant woman in labor to the hospital

by hdj4vjfbd3 on 2012-03-07 13:10:17

By Huang Tao, Intern Reporter for Legal System Weekly, with photos and text

On the morning of February 28th, near the Nankou vegetable market in Changsha, in a small winding alley, a pregnant woman named Xiao Xi (a pseudonym), who was about to give birth and alone, endured waves of pain. With one hand clutching her stomach and the other gripping her phone, she anxiously sought help outside. At only 21 years old, she wouldn't have imagined that with this phone call, a legendary "love relay" would unfold around her and the child in her womb in Xingcheng.

The plight of a struggling worker on the verge of labor

Kang Shi'e, at 54 years old, came from her rural hometown in Shaoyang to seek a living in Changsha. She worked as a cleaner in an internet cafe near the pedestrian street. In early February, Kang Shi'e learned from a friend of Xiao Xi: Xiao Xi, a girl from Xiangxi without parents, worked in a restaurant in Changsha to make a living. After becoming pregnant, the father of the child died in a car accident not long ago. Xiao Xi was about to give birth, but due to financial difficulties and hearing that a 120 emergency ambulance cost hundreds of yuan each time, even though she felt she was about to go into labor, she didn't dial the emergency number.

As a mother, Kang Shi'e decided to help this pitiful girl. On February 28th, at that critical moment, Kang Shi'e's phone rang, and on the other end, Xiao Xi's plea for help was heard. Kang Shi'e quickly left the internet cafe and rushed to Xiao Xi's residence.

Seeing that Xiao Xi wasn't feeling particularly painful, Kang Shi'e let her rest at home. Then she hurriedly called a friend with birthing experience for help. On the phone, her friend told her to prepare a pair of scissors, and if they couldn't get to the hospital in time and the baby was born, use the scissors to cut the umbilical cord... Subsequently, while on her way back to the internet cafe to ask for leave, Kang Shi'e also bought a pair of scissors just in case.

Upon learning that Kang Shi'e had taken on such a "task," her family and friends were worried she might get into trouble and kept calling to remind her. "Helping is fine, but don't take it all on yourself. If something happens, you won't be able to take responsibility."

"She's too pitiful. If I don't help her, what will she do?" Kang Shi'e, tears streaming down her face, explained to her family over the phone.

When she returned to Xiao Xi's residence again, it was already past 4 PM. The signs of labor were becoming more evident for Xiao Xi, accompanied by intermittent convulsions, she often groaned "aiyo" in discomfort. Kang Shi'e quickly helped Xiao Xi move slowly from the house towards the roadside.

Several cars were flagged down but none stopped. In a rush, Kang Shi'e told a young man driving a bread delivery van nearby about Xiao Xi's situation. The driver quickly let them both onto the vehicle and drove them to the nearest Changsha Traditional Chinese Medicine Hospital.

Upon arriving at the TCM hospital, Kang Shi'e found out they did not offer birthing services. Thus, she supported Xiao Xi back to the roadside...

A taxi driver speeds through three red lights safely delivering them to the hospital

At 5:30 PM on the 28th, Zhou Bin, having just finished his shift, started his evening shift driving the blue-yellow taxi with license plate number Xiang AT8232. He still planned to stay up late after his shift to watch over his mother who had just undergone surgery, allowing his father to rest. In 2005, Zhou Bin began working for the Hunan Changsha Xiangtu Urban Taxi Company, becoming a "taxi driver." Not long ago, at 27, he became a father.

Just as he prepared to turn left, Zhou Bin saw an elderly person helping a pregnant woman flagging down a car on the right side of the road. He drove over, it was his first fare of the day.

The car door opened, and the elderly person helped the pregnant woman inside. Only then did Zhou Bin realize there was no one else besides the anxious elder and the weak pregnant woman. "Where's the baby's father?" Having just become a father himself, he sensed something was off and asked. "This sister is an orphan, and the baby's father also passed away recently," the elder quickly said. "Baby, baby..." The pregnant woman, in severe pain, kept repeating these two words. To Zhou Bin, it seemed Xiao Xi was expressing a determination: "I must keep this child safe." Hearing this, it felt like Zhou Bin's heart was being squeezed, and the sense of responsibility grew heavier.

"Where are you going?" Faced with Zhou Bin's question, the elder and the pregnant woman looked bewildered. Someone outside suggested taking them to the Changsha Maternal and Child Health Care Hospital. Considering the traffic jam during this time at the city maternal health care center, Zhou Bin thought for a moment and finally decided to go to the provincial maternal health care center. "If we encounter serious traffic congestion, we'll call and request a 'green channel.'"

Not even 2 minutes after getting in the car, Zhou Bin heard the pregnant woman say, "The baby is coming." Zhou Bin quickly turned around and saw the baby's head faintly visible below the pregnant woman, but no cries could be heard. The white seat cover in the backseat was dyed bright red with the blood flowing from the pregnant woman...

Zhou Bin turned on the taxi's warning lights and proceeded along the route he had planned towards the provincial maternal health care center. Red light! Just as the car had started moving, they encountered a red light. Without much thought, seeing no cars around, Zhou Bin sped through. To reach the destination in the shortest time possible, Zhou Bin drove along the non-motorized vehicle lane, running through three red lights. Passing West Lake Bridge and Du Fu Pavilion... It seemed like they were approaching their destination when suddenly traffic appeared to be blocked ahead! Delaying even a minute could mean life or death, making Zhou Bin even more anxious. Seeing a police car nearby, he thought, if the traffic doesn't move, he'd simply tell the police and ask them to help clear the way. Fortunately, about a minute later, the traffic flow resumed. Zhou Bin managed to drive all the way to the provincial maternal health care center.

Successful delivery within 3 minutes

At 5:20 PM that day, at the provincial maternal health care center, the staff at the triage desk were tidying up various forms, some preparing to leave work. Group leader Gan Bei and nurse Deng Hongzhi were patrolling the first-floor lobby. Outside the door, a tall man rushed out of the taxi and shouted urgently to the triage desk, "Doctor, come quick! There's a woman in labor in the car, the baby's head is already out!"

Hearing the cry, Gan Bei and everyone quickly went over. A taxi was parked at the entrance to the inpatient department, with an elder crying for help and a young pregnant woman groaning in pain inside.

"Two lives!" Gan Bei quickly leaned into the car to observe; half of the baby's body was already exposed, its whole body purple, still connected to the umbilical cord. The pregnant woman started to convulse, and the backseat and around the car doors were stained with blood...

At this moment, the head nurse of the emergency department, Long Yueping, also arrived. While Gan Bei consoled the pregnant woman, with Long Yueping's help, they quickly opened the delivery kit and successfully delivered the baby and cut the umbilical cord. Subsequently, the mother and daughter were separately sent to the delivery room for placement. It's hard to imagine that from the taxi stopping to the pregnant woman completing childbirth and being sent into the delivery room, it took only 3 minutes.

15 minutes later, good news came, "The baby has regained spontaneous breathing! The mother temporarily fainted, but her vital signs are normal!" Everyone present finally sighed in relief...

After Xiao Xi gave birth smoothly, Zhou Bin quietly returned to his taxi, slightly cleaned the bloodstains inside and outside the car, and then quietly drove away. That day, he only accepted this one "fare."

"I just did what I should do"

Besides working, Kang Shi'e spent her days looking for the kind-hearted taxi driver while accompanying Xiao Xi and her daughter. Every time she talked about Xiao Xi with people, she couldn't help but shed tears, "I'll continue to take care of them during this period." Said "temporary mother" Kang Shi'e.

Although colleagues knew that Zhou Bin had taken a pregnant woman to the hospital, it wasn't until March 1st that they realized the kind-hearted taxi driver everyone was looking for was him. Before that, to find Zhou Bin, people had even used the taxi GPS positioning system.

After finding Zhou Bin, every time Kang Shi'e met him, she wanted to give him 100 yuan, but Zhou Bin always refused to accept it. Upon learning that Zhou Bin was the kind-hearted taxi driver, the leadership of Xiangtu Taxi Company decided to reward Zhou Bin, call on other employees to learn from him, and assist him in coordinating the traffic violations that occurred at the time with the traffic authorities. Regarding the praise and rewards, Zhou Bin always appeared very shy, saying that he merely did a very ordinary little thing. "In our company meetings, we're encouraged to do more good deeds. I just did what I should do," Zhou Bin said he also wanted to visit Xiao Xi and the baby at the hospital.

At noon on February 29th, Xiao Xi woke up. She gave her daughter a nickname, "Di Di," a homophone for the "Di" in "taxi," wanting her daughter to remember Uncle Zhou Bin. She said that although she had experienced many misfortunes, she had also encountered many kind-hearted people. "Thank you all, there are still more kind-hearted people in the world. When she grows up, I will tell her the stories of these kind-hearted people who helped us." Xiao Xi teased her daughter, softly muttering.
