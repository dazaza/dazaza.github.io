Pan East New Thinking OA strikes again, offering free downloads of the official version of OA.

by wesiey on 2008-12-08 13:27:03

The leading collaborative OA vendor, Pan East Xin Sicuang, has once again launched a free version that has left the market gasping for breath - the genuine Xin Sicuang OA3000 Lifetime Free Edition with no functional or time limitations and 15 built-in licenses.

According to the official website of Pan East Xin Sicuang (http://www.alleast.com.cn), participating in the lifetime free version is simple; after downloading and installing the program, users can complete their own registration and start using it. The user end does not require any software installation and can directly access office applications via the network, with free telephone support services available.

This move has evidently caused many collaborative OA vendors to feel uneasy. Some vendors have even directly criticized Xin Sicuang's actions as "a foolish act that disrupts the market," while others have claimed on their blogs that this is a "short-term behavior." Some representatives from other vendors believe that although launching a free version for market promotion is acceptable, there should be restrictions on functionality and time. However, the permanent free version introduced by Xin Sicuang is not only a fully open-functional version but also can be used permanently, which will undoubtedly have a negative impact on the overall market, especially on some small software vendors.

Xin Sicuang, however, has refuted these claims. They argue that the reason why the collaborative OA market lacks intimacy with users and causes confusion among them is directly related to the lack of necessary transparency in the market. Many users simply cannot access collaborative systems, and when they try to find a software for experience, they are treated like criminals during reviews, making it hard for them to generate purchasing motivation and benefit the market vendors. This re-launch of the permanent free version is precisely aimed at allowing users to experience the product comprehensively and is an activity to popularize collaborative OA. Due to the limitation of 15 genuine licenses, it will not impact the overall market but will increase public awareness of the collaborative OA market.

While vendors who offer free and non-free versions have engaged in verbal sparring, most users have warmly welcomed this move. Mr. Zhang, the director of the sales company Liaoning Benxi Tianyi Steel, said that the company is currently in the preliminary preparation phase for selecting collaborative office software. Due to the multitude of products in the market, some of which do not even provide trial opportunities, it is difficult to form a clear understanding of collaborative products. Xin Sicuang's initiative has given them a chance to experience collaborative OA. This point is worth acknowledging, though the authorization of 15 accounts clearly cannot meet the requirements. The responsible person stated, "If everyone democratically votes and finds it acceptable, we will prioritize considering it."

Experts from the Beijing Software Industry Association believe that due to the certain lock-in nature of the software industry, the launch of multiple versions is very beneficial for sales. Utilizing free versions to conduct market actions has long been a common tactic used by software companies in developed Western countries, known as the very popular "experiential marketing" in recent years. IBM and Microsoft both have successful precedents, so this should not be surprising. In China, however, due to the requirement for comprehensive strength from vendors, this approach is still not widely applied in the market.

The subtext of the experts' remarks reveals a problem: free versions, once seen often enough, lose their novelty. Xin Sicuang's cleverness lies in taking the lead by half a step. According to intelligence we've gathered from Baidu, Xin Sicuang has played the free card three times: In July 2006, Xin Sicuang launched a free distribution campaign themed "Let’s OA," and in December 2006, Xin Sicuang launched the permanent free version. With this latest one, Xin Sicuang's strategy resembles Cheng Yaojin's three ax blows.

Speaking of Cheng Yaojin, the "trick" becomes easier to understand - if it weren't for tasting the sweetness of free offers, how would software vendors have such enthusiasm to promote? Cheng Yaojin was part of the Wagang Fort group, certainly not a Lei Feng.
