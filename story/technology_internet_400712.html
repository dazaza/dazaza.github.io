People who have experience in online shopping, come and have a look to prevent from being cheated

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:29:52

Nowadays, many people have the experience of online shopping, and so do I. But today, I almost got scammed. The following is my personal experience, and I hope to give you all some warnings.

Last Monday, I ordered two pieces of clothing on Taobao for less than 300 yuan in total, and I chose the payment method of "cash on delivery". It should have arrived within 5 to 7 days, but today is the 8th day, Guangzhou VIPshop Information Technology Co., Ltd., and I was wondering why it hasn't been delivered yet. Since I can't use my phone during class and there's no one at home during the day to receive the goods, I decided to find out the shipping status from the seller. However, the online customer service couldn't solve the problem, so I had to consult by phone. So I searched for "Taobao customer service phone number" on Baidu. There were many results, and I picked the first one and called it. A man with a foreign accent answered the phone. He didn't even say "Hello, I'm Taobao, how may I assist you," which is something that customer service should definitely say. Then I told him about my general doubt, and he actually said, "If you haven't paid online, how could we possibly ship your order?" At this point, I confirmed that he was a complete scammer, and probably part of an organized gang. Think about it, would Taobao's customer service really say that the "cash on delivery" method requires you to pay first before they ship? They don't even have basic knowledge of business practices! But I decided to see what other tricks they might have up their sleeve. Sure enough, then he asked me if I had a bank card or anything like that, saying they needed to check my consumption record online. Here and there, everywhere!!! So I just went along with him and said yes, and then I asked if there was a bank nearby where I could make an online payment at a nearby ATM, saying that after the payment, they would ship immediately. So I didn't bother talking to him anymore and hung up the phone directly.

Here, I want to remind everyone that now everyone is afraid of unsafe online shopping, so they choose big shopping websites like Taobao, Mecox Lane, Vancl, etc. But these websites generally don't have comprehensive telephone customer service, so a lot of hateful scammers take advantage of this situation, posting some phone numbers online, and specifically waiting for those customers who have questions, trying every possible way to get you to pay again using your bank card~ In my case today, it only shows that the other side is not only a scammer but also a complete fool. How could "cash on delivery" require an online payment via bank card first! Here, I suggest that when shopping online, try to choose a payment method that you feel comfortable with, which is safer. If you still have unresolved questions that need to be addressed by telephone customer service, it's best to spend a few cents calling 114 to ask for the website's official phone number. This is more secure. I write this article to warn everyone and express my feelings.

May all the scammers in the world and those shameless Uighurs who openly rob people on the streets die. May our country flourish!
