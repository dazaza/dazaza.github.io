In a vast and resource-rich China with profound cultural heritage, batches of talents and literary works have emerged. In ancient times, we had the Four Great Classical Novels; today we have "Gouji". Why does "Gouji" hold such significant influence and status in Chinese literature? Since Gouji has become an indispensable part of leisure time for ancient people, from government officials to commoners, they would all play a few rounds during their free time. So, what exactly is Gouji? Gouji is a type of card game. As its name suggests, Gouji means being exciting and passionate. Playing Gouji can bring incomparable joy and unprecedented excitement. Where did such a wonderful thing originate? And who was the genius behind it? Let us trace back through history to explore the wonders of Gouji.

When we talk about Gouji, we must mention its origin and birthplace. In Shandong Province, there is a village named Juanpenglou renowned for its rich culture. The ordinary villagers here are simple and kind-hearted. This village is adjacent to the hometown of Confucius and Mencius, so everyone here is highly talented, not necessarily erudite but well-read. Moreover, they are all very intelligent. Some have become famous entrepreneurs, like the well-known Nan Huan Qingzhong Steel Market, which has now become one of the world's top 500 companies and a pillar of Chinese enterprises. The people of this village have made tremendous contributions to the country and nurtured numerous talents. Most notably, they invented a fun activity suitable for all ages—Gouji. Who was the inventor of such a great project? It wasn't the work of just one person but a group of unmarried young people. They were up early and worked late every day, playing cards continuously. They were familiar with various card games like Run Fast, Landlord Fight, Bullfighting, and Pai Gow. However, over time, they found these games monotonous and boring. Also, the number of participants couldn't be too many, yet the game should remain engaging. Thus, they embarked on a grand plan to create a card game that everyone could understand and enjoy. Every night, they gathered together to study and experiment personally, working overtime until midnight while others were already asleep. Sometimes, three or four people would share a pack of instant noodles or a bottle of cheap Mirinda soda, truly neglecting food and sleep. Their efforts paid off when they eventually created a unique card game called Gouji. This added a brilliant touch to China's entertainment industry, marking a historical milestone. Now, Gouji has been widely spread and become a major national entertainment project. Especially in several counties and cities around Shandong, it has gained popularity. This project has been recognized as "World Intangible Cultural Heritage" by the World Intangible Cultural Organization and received praise from central party leaders and State Council members. The creators of this project have been awarded "Top Ten Youth Nationwide," collectively honored as "National Advanced Group" and "Most Creative Award," even receiving the "Nobel Entertainment Award." This village has been personally granted the titles of "First Village of China" and "Hometown of Gouji" by the President, among other prestigious awards. The village has also established the Gouji Culture Propaganda Department, Gouji Organization Department, and the Literary Editing Department of the "Gouji Outline" and other authoritative Gouji departments. Every September 15th, the village hosts Gouji competitions and Gouji Culture Festivals. The most outstanding contestants are rated as the year's most promising Gouji players. These young inventors have now become authorities and experts in China's Gouji circle, laying a solid foundation for China's cultural development. We should remember and respect those who have contributed to China's entertainment industry and continue this legendary project for the benefit of our future generations. One day, wherever we are in the world, we can proudly say, "I know how to play Gouji," leaving foreigners envious and amazed.

This concludes the introduction to the origins of Gouji. Next, let me introduce the basic gameplay and steps.

One: Items required. To play Gouji, you need six people and four decks of poker cards. Participants should be smart and, most importantly, have good etiquette and poker ethics. Poker cards should be wear-resistant (here I recommend a special brand of poker cards which are not only wear-resistant but also durable against impact. These are available at major supermarkets).

Two: Drawing cards. Since you're playing cards, you need to draw them. For the first round, you need to select who goes first. Whoever gets selected plays first. Except for the first round, the player with the largest drop will deal the cards. You take out threes and fours, keeping only six threes and six fours, one set per person. If you don't have enough, you need to buy them (money represented by twos). If you don't have money, you can use flowers. Threes must be bought because they need to be cleared at the end. Fours can be optional. If you think your hand is good, you can buy them; otherwise, it doesn't matter. If you want to buy threes and fours, you should buy from your partner first. If your partner can't afford it, you can ask the federation (three-person corner is the federation). If the federation doesn't have any, you need to buy from the other two players.

Three: Tribute. The tribute in Gouji includes circles, points, burning, clearing, and falling. If you get circled, you don't fall, and if you fall, you don't get circled. Both cannot happen simultaneously.

Four: What is a circle? A circle refers to when none of the three players have gone out. Each of the three players must give one circle tribute to their partner. At the same time, all three must put down their fives, known as pooling the fives, allowing any one of the other three players to play. If they can't play, any of the three players who were circled can start the next round.

Five: Points. Points involve interactions between partners and are unrelated to the other four players. Speaking of points, we must explain what Gouji cards are. Gouji cards refer to cards valued at 10 or above (including 10). The basic Gouji cards consist of five tens, four jacks, three queens, two kings, and two aces. Scoring points includes announcing points. Announcing points means declaring that your partner won't open points. This is only done when your Gouji cards are strong. Normally, the "Gouji Outline" discourages announcing points. If neither of the two partners opens points, then you need to switch points. Once any two of the six players have gone out, points cannot be opened anymore. Even without four cards, you still need to contribute point tributes.

Six: Burning cards refers to the cards immediately following Gouji cards where the two neighboring players can burn cards. After burning, you need to progressively add flowers when going out. If you are burned, it's not hopeless. Your partner, or your federation, can help you unburn, and you can also unburn yourself, known as counter-burning. If successful, you can at least claim two burning tributes. Here, I must emphasize an important point about burning cards. Sometimes, when your cards are good, you might get excited and impulsively decide to burn. But upon dealing the cards, you realize you can't afford to burn. Sorry! For such careless behavior, we must impose strict penalties without mercy. You'll just have to accept the worst fate.

Seven: Clearing. Clearing refers to when the final three cards are stuck in someone's hand with no other cards left. Clearing also includes burning clearing, which is the same as burning cards.

Eight: Falling refers to the last two players, known as Big Fall and Second Fall. Only these two players have the honor of falling, divided into Big Fall and Second Fall. They receive corresponding head and second tributes. Big Fall receives two, and Second Fall receives one.

The above is an introduction to the basic gameplay and steps of Gouji. Below, let's discuss the benefits of playing Gouji.

Playing Gouji can exercise the brain, promote cerebral blood circulation (Note: People with insufficient brain blood supply should avoid this activity), enhance memory, and train the coordination of both hands. It also cultivates personal qualities and poker ethics.
