[Original] Confucius Interprets the "Outline" in the Gen Chapter

(Analects Miscellaneous Talks No. 90)

The "Education Development Plan Outline" has been issued. When we read the "Outline" and have not yet understood its essence, the major key universities have already raised the banner of independent recruitment, gathering forces, shouting slogans, marching into the Ministry of Education's college entrance examination domain with high spirits, occupying and governing territories, and showing a strong tendency to stage a martial arts TV drama version of the "Seven Heroes Struggle for Dominance" adapted by Zhang Jizhong. First came the "Huayue School" led by Tsinghua University, followed by the "Beiyue School" with Peking University as the leader, then Beijing Institute of Technology self-proclaimed as the "little third", hastily assembling the "Engineering School". For a time, the Chinese education kingdom showed a situation of Zhou dynasty's weakening power and the rise of feudal lords.

The "Outline" pointed out that "schools independently recruit students according to law, students make multiple choices. Form a multi-dimensional admission system." The universities took advantage of the word "independent", thus acting on their own, impacting the little remaining "fairness" in China's college entrance examination under the grandiose slogan of "independence", filling the originally somewhat "transparent" college entrance examination in China with thick gloom, truly making it "gloomy and heavy". Countless students taking the college entrance examination are now unsure of "where they will wake up after drinking tonight".

The "Outline" proposed "independent recruitment according to law", but I cannot understand the word "according to law". I ask, which law should universities follow when recruiting students independently? Is it the big law or the college entrance examination recruitment law? (Does China even have a recruitment law?) If China does not have laws supporting "independent recruitment", then the word "according to law" in the "Outline" is just empty talk, or can be said to be a sleight of hand to deceive students taking the college entrance examination. In fact, it points to "independence", allowing so-called key universities to act on their own without any basis, turning the "college entrance examination" concerning students' fate and higher education aimed at improving national quality into an "inter-school warlord chaos".

Moreover, the "Outline" not only gave the universities "independence" ("independence" means doing whatever they want), but also used this to "form a multi-dimensional admission system". One word "multi-dimensional" implies so much. It seems to tell people that the fairness of the "college entrance examination" no longer exists. We know that the traditional meaning of the "college entrance examination" still has some "fairness". Its fairness lies in preserving the humanistic survival view of the imperial examination system, providing fair competition opportunities for the lower class and poor citizens to enter the upper society. During the imperial examination era, many poor scholars changed their status through "passing the exam". Even today, students from impoverished areas see passing the college entrance examination as the best way to get rid of poverty. However, the creators of the "Outline" blocked the already narrow path for the poor to enter the upper society with the two bricks of "independence" and "multi-dimensionality", instead building multiple detours for the rich to further their studies and gain prestige. Official data shows that the majority of students who registered for Tsinghua University's independent recruitment this year come from urban areas, which sufficiently proves that so-called "independence" is just a shortcut opened by the Chinese Ministry of Education for wealthy officials' children to enter top universities, and so-called "multi-dimensionality" is nothing more than a surefire way for officials holding recruitment power to take bribes and accumulate wealth.

There is no need to hide the fact that today's China's Gini coefficient has far exceeded the internationally recognized warning line, and the gap between the rich and the poor, as well as the urban-rural gap, can be considered one of the highest in the world. Using the concepts of "independence" and "multi-dimensionality", the makers of the "Outline" reforming the traditional college entrance examination system undoubtedly adds insult to injury for the poor descendants, while the offspring of the rich become even better off. If the "independent" and "multi-dimensional" recruitment model becomes a reality, several years later, the poor will become poorer, the rich richer, leading to what sociologists call the "fracture" phenomenon. Poor people will lose the possibility of changing their fate and may follow the path of Chen She and Wu Guang in rebellion, which is the source of social unrest.

Higher education is currently still elite education. "Elite" is rooted in all college entrance examination students. The traditional college entrance examination model, with "one test for all", shines brightly because it treats everyone equally regardless of their social status or wealth, and regardless of whether they are from urban or rural areas. All students compete fairly before "one test". It gives students hope and makes them accept success or failure gracefully. However, the "independent" and "multi-dimensional" recruitment model deprives students who do not have the material conditions to participate in "independent" recruitment and cannot enter the "multi-dimensional" pattern of the opportunity to compete, and these students are precisely the poor masses. It can be said that the "independent" and "multi-dimensional" recruitment reform plan proposed in the "Outline" is a mistaken policy that harms the poor and benefits the rich.

The Analects records a dialogue between Zi Gong and Confucius about "helping the masses", which can be used to criticize the "independent" and "multi-dimensional" theory. The Analects once said: "Zi Gong said: If there is someone who widely distributes benefits to the people and can help the masses, how is it? Can it be called benevolence? Confucius said: What does it have to do with benevolence, must it be sainthood!"

Widely distributing benefits to the people and helping the masses is the embryo of Confucian political thought, and Mencius' "benevolent governance" theory originates from this. Confucius taught the people and founded private schools, achieving the social function of widely distributing benefits to the people and helping the masses. Since ancient times, whether it is government-run education or privately run schools, their social functions are to widely distribute benefits to the people and "help" the poor masses. But today's makers of the Chinese "Outline" let the balance of education tilt towards the rich and the noble. I wonder if they are going to reform the college entrance examination, or use the name of "reform" to harm the poor again.

Poor students, you have lost another (not just one) chance to change your fate. You have once again become a sacrifice to "reform". Is it fate, destiny? No... This is caused by the political system. Think about it, when our path to change our fate is blocked, what should we do?
