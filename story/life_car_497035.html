**English Translation:**

Use cleaning agents to thoroughly clean oil stains, mud stains, and dust from the surface of the engine, restoring its original appearance and enhancing the engine's heat dissipation. Engine cleaning and quality requirements:

Basic tools: High-pressure water gun, toothbrush, old towels, plastic bags.

Supplies: Engine cleaner, tire shine agent.

Time requirement: 1.5-2 hours (for one person), depending on the situation.

1. **High-pressure Water Gun Cleaning Method**

- **Operation Steps:**

**Step One: Waterproof Preparation**

Open the hood, cover components such as the generator, distributor, and brake oil reservoir with cloth or seal them with plastic bags.

Key points: Make sure everything is covered or sealed properly, especially the generator and distributor.

Quality standard: Ensure that all parts are tightly covered or sealed.

**Step Two: Rinse Process**

(1) Spray engine cleaner onto all components, focusing more on particularly dirty areas.

(2) When the cleaner begins to dissolve the dirt, use a high-pressure water gun to rinse the engine and its dirty spots.

(3) Use the high-pressure water gun to spray under the front windshield at the air intake vents.

(4) Spray and clean leaves, mud, and dust in the space between the windshield and the engine compartment's heat insulation area.

(5) Use the high-pressure water gun to rinse leaves, insects, and dust from the radiator, condenser, and cooling fins, starting from the inside out.

(6) Use the high-pressure water gun to rinse the wheel fenders and drain holes inside the engine compartment, removing leaves and debris.

(7) Use the high-pressure water gun to clean dirt or dust from the support bars inside the engine compartment.

Key points: Rinse the engine after it has cooled down. Be careful not to use too much pressure from the water gun.

Quality standard: After rinsing, the engine should be clean and free of dirt.

**Step Three: Drying and Inspection Process**

(1) Use an air spray gun to blow sand particles out of the spark plug holes and dry them.

(2) Blow dry the battery terminals.

(3) Dry other parts to prepare for spraying the engine shine agent.

(4) Check if there is any water on the engine and distributor, and handle accordingly.

Key points: Before starting the car, make sure to check that no water remains in these areas.

**Step Four: Spraying Shine Agent**

(1) Remove the covering cloth and use a clean cloth to thoroughly wipe all parts of the engine compartment, especially the underside of the hood.

(2) After all components are completely dry, spray the tire shine agent, which is only suitable for plastic and rubber parts.

Key points: Make sure all components are thoroughly dry before spraying the shine agent.

Quality standard: Clean, glossy, and shiny black.

2. **High-pressure Air Cleaning Method (Brief Introduction)**

- **Operation Steps:**

(1) Use a high-pressure air gun to blow away oil stains from the raised strips on the inner side of the engine compartment.

(2) Use a high-pressure air gun to remove dust from the air filter housing, especially around the central fixing bolt flange.

(3) Use a high-pressure air gun to blow away dust from the spark plug recesses.

(4) Use a high-pressure air gun to blow away dust from accessories around the engine, such as the battery and splash guards.

(5) Use a high-pressure air gun to remove dirt from the condenser and radiator.

(6) Use a high-pressure air gun to remove dirt from the drainage holes on both sides.

(7) Use an air gun to clean dirt from the fan.

(8) Open the air filter cover for cleaning work. When spraying, use a cloth to block the top of the carburetor to prevent dust from entering.

(9) Remove dust from the air core.

**Key Points:**

- Pay attention to safety and report issues promptly.

- 1. When the engine is hot, avoid burns by handling carefully.

- 2. When washing other components inside the engine head box, be cautious of connectors and wires to avoid hand injuries.

- 3. Do not open the hood when the engine is running.

- 4. Close the hood gently; do not slam it.

- 5. Certain engine parts must be protected from water ingress.
