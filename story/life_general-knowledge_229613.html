Shanghai Home Renovation: 10 Secrets for Kitchen Renovation

by longersing on 2009-08-27 18:07:39

Don't underestimate the decoration of Shanghai city. Kitchen decoration is a big problem. Today, let's take a look at the top 10 secrets of kitchen decoration and learn how to reasonably and correctly decorate your home.

1. Scientifically set up the ventilation system

The kitchen space is relatively small, but the density of kitchen facilities is extremely high, including cabinets, kitchen appliances, stoves, and pipes. In addition, we cook daily in the kitchen, making it a place full of smoke and fumes. Whether considering the comfort of working in the kitchen or safety, ventilation is very important for the kitchen. The arrangement of supply and exhaust air outlets in the kitchen should be considered according to the specific location of the stove to avoid reverse suction of grease when exhausting air. For safety reasons, the kitchen's exhaust system should be divided by fire zones, trying not to cross the firewall. If it needs to cross, a fire valve should be installed.

2. Choose kitchen configuration based on lifestyle

Choosing what kind of kitchen configuration means choosing what kind of lifestyle. What can the kitchen be used for? Most Chinese people think it's just a place for cooking, while families in Europe and America believe that the kitchen should have entertainment functions such as TV and parties, which directly reflects the huge difference in lifestyles. An open kitchen makes the kitchen space larger and more spacious, allowing the kitchen to accommodate more spatial functions. You can set up a professional wine cabinet here, turning the kitchen into a wine tasting area; you can reserve a small reading area here, reading for leisure; reserve network cables to meet your mobile office needs. Nowadays, we can see more complete kitchen configurations. Choose some that suit you and customize your own life.

3. Wall tiles with easy cleaning as the first choice

Kitchen tiles not only need to consider aesthetics and fashion, but more importantly, cleaning issues. Kitchen tiles come in various materials, divided into glazed tiles, through-body tiles, polished tiles, vitrified tiles, mosaics, etc. In kitchens and other spaces with larger areas, the first choice for wall tiles is smooth or matte glazed tiles.

4. Customize the correct countertop height

The correct countertop height not only makes kitchen work smoother but also effectively prevents back diseases. From a humanized perspective, considering individual situations, the reasonable height of the sink is usually 0-5 cm below the elbow; the reasonable height of the operating table is 10-15 cm below the elbow, avoiding bending over for long periods; the stove is generally 25 cm below the elbow. There should be at least 40 cm of lateral distance between the stove and the wall to provide enough space for the operator to work freely. This free space between the operating table and the stove can be connected by the countertop, becoming a convenient and useful work platform.

5. Increase the usage rate of small kitchen appliances

As an auxiliary tool to improve the quality of life, the development of small kitchen appliances is closely related to the living standard. In developed countries in Europe and America, each household owns 30 to 40 small appliances. In domestic households, egg boilers, toasters, juicers, bread machines, deep fryers, grills, and other emerging small kitchen appliances can provide a lot of convenience for our daily lives, but their actual usage rates are not high.

6. Consider safety issues for floor tiles

In terms of materials, ground load-bearing capacity needs to be considered. Materials like stone slabs, ceramics, or terracotta bricks generally exceed the weight-bearing capacity of regular floors, so they should be used cautiously. If there is no problem with ground load-bearing capacity, materials such as granite, ceramic tiles, and wood can refresh the feel of the kitchen. In terms of surface treatment, it is best to choose floor tiles with matte processing, along with stain-resistant colors and slip-resistant materials, to prevent the kitchen's liquid spills from making the floor too slippery.

7. Plan electrical and plumbing renovations in advance

Generally, in water and electricity renovations, the following issues should be noted: design water and electricity circuits according to the use of sinks and appliances, reserving openings in corresponding positions of the kitchen cabinets; determine the direction of water pipelines according to the use of cold and hot water; fully consider safety issues, such as solutions for high-power appliances, weak current safety tests, and water pipeline pressure tests.

8. Appropriately reduce swing doors

Swing doors are the most common way to open kitchen cabinets, but both base cabinets and upper cabinets require a certain amount of door opening space. For upper cabinets, upward-opening doors can be used, which show particular advantages in narrow and inconvenient-to-open spaces. When the cabinet doors open, they move parallel upwards, so even if installed at a lower position, they won’t hit your head. The damping opening method allows the cabinet doors to stop at any point, making operations more convenient. Items stored deep in the base cabinets are usually difficult to retrieve. Using drawers can eliminate unnecessary squatting and avoid having to empty most items in the cabinet to retrieve something. However, installing drawers costs more than installing cabinet doors, so this should be considered when choosing configurations.

9. Proper maintenance keeps the kitchen shiny as new

Although kitchen design is important, regular thorough cleaning and maintenance are essential to keep the kitchen clean and fresh. Cabinets: everyday cleaning can be done with a slightly damp cloth, and for harder-to-clean areas, neutral detergent and a scrub sponge can be used. Regular maintenance and disinfection can be done with a 1:1 diluted solution of bleach and water. Pots, pans, and other objects should be dried as much as possible before being placed in the cabinet, while sharp objects should be avoided from scratching the surface directly, and steel brushes should not be used for cleaning.

10. Set up auxiliary lighting in appropriate positions

Good kitchens generally have higher requirements for lighting. Good kitchen lighting not only improves work efficiency and safety but can also affect appetite and mood. To facilitate kitchen operations, recessed ceiling lights, spotlights, and ceiling lights can be used primarily. Auxiliary lighting can be set up at the intersection of overhead cabinets and walls where chopping and cooking take place. Install embedded or semi-embedded diffuse-type ceiling lights above the operating table, with transparent glass or plastic covers at the embedded openings to keep the ceiling simple and reduce the trouble caused by dust and grease. Generally, warm white incandescent bulbs are suitable as light sources for kitchens, while cold fluorescent lights are not recommended because lighting has a significant impact on the appearance of food, which can influence one's appetite. Since people spend a considerable amount of time in the kitchen, the lighting should be pleasant and attractive, which can increase enthusiasm for cooking.
