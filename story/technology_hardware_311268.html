2010 Chinese Super League: 14 Teams Competing, Alternative Plan for 4 Teams to Battle in Play-offs for Substitution Slots

by yinding999 on 2009-12-16 19:04:21

In 2010, 14 teams will compete in the CSL. Another proposal is that four teams will compete in a playoff for promotion. Delegates who will participate in the new session of the Football Congress of China have received formal notification from the Chinese Football Association (CFA). The Football Congress will be held in Beijing on the 21st and 22nd of this month.

"Currently, the CFA has conceived several opinions about the possible changes in the new season of the CSL. These may be discussed privately within a small scope during the Football Congress," a source inside the CFA revealed to reporters last night.

Fourteen teams to compete in the CSL?

It is currently almost non-controversial that Guangyao and Chengdu, two teams confirmed to have played rigged matches, will definitely be relegated. Despite media claims that these two teams will continue to compete in the CSL, starting the new season with negative points as per an Italian Serie A precedent is also one of the plans, but according to relevant regulations of the CFA, such a possibility does not exist.

Because there are hints from the General Administration of Sport to reduce the number of teams in the CSL, after Guangzhou and Chengdu are relegated, having Liaoning and Nanchang, the two teams that successfully promoted last season, join the current 12 CSL teams to form a total of 14 teams in the CSL is one of the key options currently under consideration by the CFA. However, this plan will lead to a reduction in the size of the CSL. In the 2009 season, the popularity of the CSL and its social influence had gradually recovered, so this plan faces significant external resistance.

Maintain the status quo with four teams competing for promotion?

After Guangzhou and Chengdu are confirmed to be relegated, if the CFA considers promoting teams to the CSL, there are currently two proposals: first, Hangzhou and Chongqing, the two relegated teams from the 2009 season, remain in the CSL; second, Hangzhou, Chongqing, as well as ShenYang Dongjin and Shanghai East Asia, the third and fourth place teams from last season's Chinese Second Division League, will compete in a playoff, with the two winning teams entering the CSL.

At this Football Congress, the CFA will gather opinions from all parties regarding the scale of the new season's CSL, communicate with relevant clubs, and solicit opinions.

Chen Hong, the president of Chongqing Lifan, who will represent Chongqing Lifan at the Football Congress, stated: "If such a situation really occurs, Chongqing Lifan will make its own choice based on the overall interests of Chongqing football and the voice of fans."

The new session of the Football Congress will be held on the 21st. It is a foregone conclusion that Guangyao and Chengdu will be relegated. Lifan might participate in the CSL promotion playoffs, which could bring the CSL back to Chongqing again.
