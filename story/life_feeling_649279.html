Life is like a dream, and the falling words turn into sorrow.

by lili1 on 2011-12-30 19:16:17

The afterglow of the setting sun suddenly made my heart feel a deep sense of emptiness. My wandering thoughts had nowhere to settle, hiding in the lethargy and laziness of spring.

In black and white annals, there was an orderly sorrow. I often felt that I couldn't even understand myself, and would lose myself unconsciously. My heart didn't know which direction it should fly towards, unable to find the way forward. I once longed so much to set my heart free, to go to a distant and secluded place, or perhaps to wander aimlessly and capriciously across the horizon, unencumbered by anything.

Actually, I always understood that just because one has strong aspirations in their heart, it doesn't mean they will reach the paradise of dreams. Even if one never asks for much, they may still inadvertently deviate from the right path. Each of us wants to work hard for ourselves, wanting to create a stage of our own, and we continue on the road without wronging the direction. But in this long journey, only a few can reach the end and enjoy the infinite scenery at the top of the perilous peak.

Perhaps, the end of life is the ultimate goal of our pursuits. Life always seems to come and go in a rush. Everyone works tirelessly during their lifetime, wanting to fully interpret the brilliance of life, no matter what form or method they use. Perhaps, when we grow old, we will realize that we cannot take anything with us, but we will still have no regrets. Even if there are sighs, regrets, attachments, and unwillingness, as well as many unfulfilled wishes, life still feels rich and fulfilling.

Perhaps, life is destined to be filled with too many entanglements. There will be hustle and bustle, hardship, and too much disorder. Just like me, there are so many moods and tangled intestines in my heart that words alone can never fully express. Therefore, it is also predetermined that I will be inseparable from writing throughout my life. Regardless of whether words will eventually bring harm, I cannot abandon them. Resolutely, I want to rely on them, letting them listen to all the joys and sorrows of my life!

Suddenly, for a while, I felt especially busy, with too many things to handle at work, making every day busy and full. I wanted to grow and harvest in this busyness and fullness. More often, I was willing to become exhausted after such hard work and chaos, then fall into a deep sleep at night, not pondering those unreachable pasts in silence, not always placing my heart on the edge of sorrow and hesitation, touching that sadness and melancholy that reaches deep into the soul.

But, with more busyness, there might be less writing, yet my mood seemed unchanged. Just like this spring, everything was still related to sadness and loneliness. The homepage remained its usual black color, allowing sorrow to bloom luxuriantly in the ink. Fanzi said, don't always be so oppressed, let me change to a warmer tone. Looking out the window at the harmonious spring scenery, I still read its sorrow so acutely. The light blue sky could not hide my heart's affairs like a spring river.

It was also during this period that I got used to not rushing home after work, staying alone in the office, either accompanied by work or lost in thought within the words, willing to sit quietly in front of the computer, with a cup of tea, letting my mind wander freely and capriciously. I would indulge in boundless imagination, and suddenly bursts of melancholy would arise, for the past and for the confusing future. My heart, wandering without support, homeless. In this vast red dust world, I didn't know where I could find solace for all my grief and coldness.

Returning home bathed in moonlight, I often turned on the radio, listening to 99.5 FM's "Harbor of the Heart", and at that time, my heart always felt a deep sense of emptiness. Although I admit I am not a sentimental woman, my heart is always very soft. In the words accompanied by music, my heart would mistakenly fall into a state of sorrow, generating a faint melancholy. Most of the time, I taught myself to be strong. I learned to be optimistic and magnanimous, calmly facing all aspects of life, keeping my heart's affairs to myself, reflecting alone when no one was around.

Walking along the road, remembering along the way. Words are my way of expressing sorrow. Every station recorded, every journey, is a landscape. On the road of life, because words listen, the soul finds its most solid support. Not caring about format, not caring about rhythm, a free and unrestrained mindset extends within the imagery of words.

Some pasts, some memories. Some stories belonging to youth. In the fleeting life, words turn into sorrow. My sorrow resides in a lonely castle, using words to write a naive reflection on the passing years.

... Sparse memories.
