Build a lakeside cottage and live there with her - "The Lake House" US Blu-ray review

by diglog on 2010-10-06 12:35:09

In 1994, a movie gave birth to two film stars. The movie was "Speed," and the two people were Sandra Bullock and Keanu Reeves. More than a decade has passed since then, and the two actors have transformed from their once slightly inexperienced on-screen personas into accomplished film stars. Sandra starred in mainstream works like "Miss Congeniality" and "Crash," while Keanu became synonymous with "The Matrix" series as the savior. What would happen if these two collaborated again? The 2006 Warner Bros. Pictures production, "The Lake House," answers that question. Even more heartwarming is that the reunion of this on-screen couple came in the form of a pure love story—though it may be somewhat melodramatic.

This film is a remake of a South Korean movie, one I haven't seen, so I won't attempt any comparisons. However, I've indeed heard many claims about how outstanding the original is, something I haven't yet had the chance to verify. From this Hollywood version, however, love stories and happy endings have become standard formulas. The only thing that really makes you think—or keeps your brain engaged throughout the viewing process—is the time-traveling storyline. It's indeed very interesting, even though it’s not a rare concept in movies. What's commendable is that this isn’t the kind of abstract consciousness or complex time-space theory that makes you want to pull your hair out like in "Donnie Darko." Instead, it uses such a plot device to weave together a love story—who doesn’t like love? And who wouldn't be interested in "time travel"? (There might actually be some people.)

At the climactic moment of life and death in the film, there’s indeed a touch of excitement and emotion. As for other parts, I can only say this is a movie that requires patience and a tranquil mindset to watch. It's not an action flick, so don’t expect dazzling visuals; it's not half a romance either, so don't expect gratuitous chest-touching or nudity. A comfortable afternoon, curled up on the couch quietly watching, would be a good idea, or cuddling with your sweetheart—maybe prepare her a box of tissues, she might get moved. But then again, if you're truly a tough guy, tough enough to find it hard to endure a plot-driven romance movie, and can't stand a single shot lasting over 10 seconds, then you should stay far away from this movie and others like it. If you're forced to watch it with your woman, then my sympathy goes to you.

This Blu-ray disc from Region A in the U.S. is just an average work. To be harsh, it simply isn't worth the price of a Blu-ray. But the issue is, I still really like this movie, and I was driven mad by the lackluster DVD versions issued in Region 6, so I grabbed this disc at a discount. The film's visuals are very beautiful, whether in terms of framing or color adjustments. The quality of the Blu-ray basically restores the excellent cinematography of the movie, far surpassing DVD, but still seems to have much room for improvement. The details in the picture are less than satisfactory, with an overall soft feeling, showing significant gaps compared to some other Blu-rays. The colors aren't overly artificially adjusted, relatively natural, but there's a slight tendency toward being too saturated and reddish in some places. The black levels perform decently well, with decent depth in dark scenes.

Of course, I wouldn't expect stunning sound effects, but I do hope that there would be an HD audio track to support this movie. Unfortunately, this Blu-ray only has three Dolby Digital 5.1 surround sound tracks. The English track generally achieves clear dialogue, although there are still some areas that seem a bit muffled. The movie's music is rather beautiful, but unfortunately, this audio track doesn't give it much chance to shine. Most of the time, you'll barely notice the presence of the music. In the final scene where the female lead realizes that the person who died in a car accident right in front of her two years ago was her beloved, she rushes to tell the male lead "not to go to her." At this tense moment, a piece of music named "Wait For Me" should have been very effective in setting the mood, but instead, it feels flat and uninspired. The surround sound channels have few opportunities to shine. One scene where everyone is celebrating New Year's, and the sound of fireworks outside briefly wakes me up—it’s one of the few times the surround sound reminds me of its existence.

The bonus features on the disc are utterly useless. Besides some SD-quality deleted scenes, bloopers, and a movie trailer, there's nothing else. Like Warner's other Blu-rays, the movie plays directly without a main menu, only with pop-up menus. So, if you don't like this movie—and I imagine not many do—you really don't need to buy it. Conversely, if you do, you can grab a copy to keep. Just note: there are no Chinese subtitles.
