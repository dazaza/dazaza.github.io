Thank our parents

by cyyaya1638 on 2011-06-16 20:02:31

-- Written on the Occasion of Father's Day 2011

Under the dense shade of green trees, with the sun blazing overhead, the summer breeze carries a familiar scent that stirs up boundless nostalgia within me. Isn't this the feeling of my father walking into the house with his shirt drenched in sweat? Father's Day is here, and it makes me think even more about our selflessly contributing parents.

Each one of us came into this world alone, but our births brought lifelong joy to our parents. Because of our existence, our parents find life more meaningful and strive harder, working to provide us with a little patch of sky. For our growth, our parents have given their whole lives in love, hoping to use their fragile lives to shield us from the wind, snow, and ice of the world.

We grow up, but our parents grow old. While we are busy striving and growing strong, we may not notice that our parents' backs have become hunched, their steps faltering. Their hair has turned white, wrinkles have gathered on their faces, and their former handsomeness and beauty have melted away in the river of time. We keep rushing around, perhaps too preoccupied to remember our parents' special days. Even a simple greeting would be enough to make them emotional.

Our parents are the source of life, and we thank them for giving us life and raising us with unspoken hardships. They ask for nothing and expect no return. As time slowly passes, you might find yourself reflecting: Is there already an irreparable regret that I cannot mend?

Touching the Chuan Ye smart electronic shoe cabinet and footbath basin, do you recall the toys of your childhood? Our parents have always given without expecting anything in return, but how can we forget their kindness? Who will care for their weary feet?

The heartfelt words of Mr. Zhu Ziqing resonate repeatedly in our ears. Do the stooped figures of our parents come back into your view? White clouds drift near home, colorful halls echo with laughter, lambs kneel to suckle, and crows feed their parents. A grateful heart is a tradition as vast as the blue sky and earth, as deep as the Yangtze River and Yellow River flowing endlessly. Silent is paternal love, yet children have feelings.
