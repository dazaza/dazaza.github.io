Report: Clenching his teeth has become a habit for Ma Baolin these days. His son's life is facing a critical juncture, with treatment costs reaching hundreds of thousands of yuan, and he feels unable to overcome this obstacle. On the 2nd day of this month, his three-year-old son Xiaoli accidentally fell into a pot of boiling water, resulting in 70% third-degree burns on his skin, and he is still not out of danger. So far, the treatment has cost nearly 100,000 yuan. The attending doctor introduced that Xiaoli's upcoming treatment will cost close to 200,000 yuan (as reported by our newspaper on January 15).

Yesterday, the "Angel Mom" Love Foundation expressed that it would allocate funds for Xiaoli.

Feeding the child with a syringe

Yesterday, outside the special care room of the Burn and Plastic Surgery Department of the East District of the Beijing Military Region General Hospital, Ma Baolin would take out his mobile phone every few minutes. He was eagerly awaiting visiting hours.

Not seeing his son makes him anxious; seeing his son breaks his heart. On the hospital bed, his son stared at him with big eyes, while he instinctively widened his own. He said he didn't dare to look at his son's skin because each glance made his nose tingle. He clenched his teeth and endured.

Ma Baolin's father repeatedly paced back and forth outside the special care room, sighing continuously. Since being admitted, the grandfather hasn't heard Xiaoli speak a single word.

The grandfather said that Xiaoli was active and mischievous, "he ran fast, I couldn't catch up; he talked a lot, incessantly babbling." "For 10 days now, (the child) has hardly said a word."

"The child lacks energy but can't sleep due to discomfort," Ma Baolin said. The child often squints his eyes, frowning when in pain. The mother who stays with him day and night said that the child understands, only calling out 'mom' when crying in pain. But this one call of 'mom' always brings tears streaming down the adults' faces.

Ma Baolin said that Xiaoli's mouth and tongue were ulcerated, and he hadn't eaten anything for three days, "the child used to be able to eat five or six small bowls (of food) a day." Helplessly, the family could only use a syringe to inject the prepared milk formula into the child's mouth, but most of it was vomited out.

Still not out of danger

"Great kindness, never forgotten." Ma Baolin said that starting yesterday, kind-hearted people have been contacting him, asking about Xiaoli's condition, and some have even donated money. Yesterday, Ma Baolin's notebook recorded the names of kind-hearted people and their corresponding amounts. "I will record every person's donation, and repay them well in the future." Currently, Xiaoli has received nearly 4,000 yuan in donations.

"If we can't get through this, my son will be gone." Ma Baolin is borrowing money from all directions, and after each phone call, he falls silent for a while.

■ Hospital Statement: Treatment fees waived, nursing fees reduced

Xiaoli is a premature baby, weak in constitution, although his condition has improved somewhat after treatment, he is still not out of danger. Currently, Xiaoli urgently needs surgery to remove the scabs on his side waist area, otherwise it may endanger his life. According to the attending doctor, Xiaoli still needs to undergo five or six surgeries, costing nearly 200,000 yuan.

Xiaoli's attending doctor Dr. Wen said that Xiaoli suffered from 70% third-degree burns, and his skin has lost its regenerative capacity, requiring skin grafting.

"We (the hospital) have done our utmost." Attending doctor Dr. Wen expressed, "Currently, the entire department is mobilizing efforts to help Xiaoli, all treatment fees have been waived, and nursing fees have been reduced to the lowest possible level." Dr. Wen said that due to severe fungal infection, Xiaoli requires medication costing over a thousand yuan daily, plus blood transfusions and protein supplements, making the costs very high, and the hospital really cannot afford this expense. "The drugs are purchased by the hospital from external sources, and the hospital does not profit from them." Dr. Wen said that currently the department is also shouldering the responsibility to ensure Xiaoli's basic treatment, "but we don't know how long we can bear this."
