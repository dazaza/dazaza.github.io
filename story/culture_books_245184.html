"Hurried Years" - The Bloody Romance of the Post-80s Generation

by jqrxass on 2009-10-04 20:56:13

Zhang Nan, a man from the 80s generation, went to study in Australia because he couldn't find a good job after graduation. There, he met Fang Wei who was also studying abroad. Just as Zhang Nan was drawn to Fang Wei's mysterious aura, fate had it that they ended up living under the same roof. Through other friends, he learned that Fang Wei had once been deeply hurt and had a unforgettable past. One day, by chance, in Zhang Nan's room, Fang Wei told him her story...

"Hurried Years" humorously narrates the love story of Fang Wei and Chen Xun, portraying the emotions and life journey of the post-80s generation. Fang Wei's reminiscences transport readers back to Beijing at the end of the 90s. Spanning over a decade, this narrative includes beautiful memories of youth and campus life, explores teenage delinquency, mentions historical events such as the 50th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China, welcoming the new millennium, and Beijing's successful bid for the Olympics, describes the decadence and confusion of university days, the arduous struggles after entering the workforce, and reflects on current marriage life. From a unique perspective, it truthfully records the growth trajectory and era-defining marks of the post-80s generation. It is another masterpiece by Jiuye Fen following "Brother, Love Me Again" and "Wind Does Not Drift, Cloud Does Not Drift". Upon its release, "Hurried Years" quickly gained popularity on various websites, with its click rate skyrocketing in a short time, being hailed by netizens as the "Bloody Romance of the Post-80s Generation".

Volume 1 of "Hurried Years" - Never Forget

Fang Wei said: "Perhaps everyone has something they want to forget but can't."

Volume 2 - Like

Fang Wei said: "Back then, we didn't say 'love'. 'Love' was such a distant, heavy word. We only said 'like', and even liking was done secretly."

Volume 3 - Past

Fang Wei said: "I think the reason why 'meeting is not as good as remembering' is because meeting only makes people helplessly mourn their pain in reality, while remembering can turn already predetermined lies into fairy tales."

Volume 4 - Keep Moving

Fang Wei said: "That day I had a dream, dreaming that we were still in high school. It was probably dusk, the sky was dark yellow, everyone was running on the playground, and at that moment, I just wanted to keep running forever..."

Volume 5 - Grow Up

Fang Wei said: "We all thought that after growing up, we could truly be together forever, so we spared no effort to grow up quickly. But when we really grew up enough to say goodbye to our youth, we suddenly realized that growing up would only make us separate..."

Volume 6 - Farewell

Fang Wei said: "Goodbye... let's meet again."

Volume 7 - Encounter

Fang Wei said: "After growing up, we will always learn different lessons, walk different paths, and meet different people. We can't avoid the fate of parting ways."

Volume 8 - Separate

Fang Wei said: "When we were young, we always did things that hurt each other. In the end, we were both desperate because we knew that the only way to save each other was to choose to separate."

Volume 9 - Hurried

Fang Wei said: "I really loved, and I really hated, but those loves and hates passed by so quickly. Now thinking about it, I actually don't regret it. If I had to choose again, I would do it all over again..."
