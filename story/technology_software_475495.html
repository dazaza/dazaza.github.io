The "new national standard" for the express delivery industry has finally been released after much anticipation.

by gtobalmf on 2011-08-12 17:18:47

The long-awaited new national standard for the express delivery industry has finally been released. Yesterday, the State Post Bureau issued the "Operational Guidelines for Express Delivery Services." According to the guidelines, after receiving an order, courier companies should ideally collect the parcel within 2 hours; after collection, they should ideally deliver the parcel to the business premises within 3 hours. When collecting parcels at the customer's door, the safety of the collected parcels must be ensured, and it is strictly forbidden to leave collected parcels in unattended places.

In addition, when couriers collect parcels at the customer's door, they should use scales, tape measures, and other measuring tools that comply with national and industry standards to determine the correct billing weight and calculate service fees based on the billing weight and type of service.

The newly introduced guidelines specify quantitative indicators for sorting operations. Parcels weighing less than 5 kilograms should be placed in sorting trays, while those weighing more than 5 kilograms should be stacked in designated positions. Stacking should follow the principle of larger items not pressing smaller ones, heavier items not pressing lighter ones, and fragile items being stored separately. The guidelines also require that sorting operations be conducted under video surveillance.

The guidelines stipulate that before delivery, dispatchers should contact the recipient by phone to confirm the address and schedule a delivery time.

Courier Companies Worry About Lack of Conditions for Speed

A courier company manager told reporters that since the guidelines are not mandatory and most courier companies can already meet or exceed these standards in their operations, the impact of the guidelines is not significant. However, he also raised concerns about some provisions in the guidelines. For example, Article 9 of Chapter 2 requires that after receiving an order, courier companies should ideally collect the parcel within 2 hours and deliver it to the business premises within 3 hours after collection. Since the transportation methods used by couriers for collecting and delivering parcels are not standardized, and some roads restrict passage, most courier companies find it difficult to meet this requirement. Therefore, he suggests that relevant departments establish a unified national standard for couriers' transportation methods and implement special policies for their passage. Additionally, the requirements for facilities and equipment in courier company business premises listed in Appendix I are only general principles and should have quantifiable standards. Fire protection facilities and video surveillance equipment, as required by relevant regulations, should also have quantifiable requirements, such as specifying one video surveillance device per certain square meters, making the operation more feasible.
