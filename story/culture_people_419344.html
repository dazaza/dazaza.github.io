Three years without making mistakes is mediocrity.

by imadin on 2011-04-21 13:58:38

During the Spring and Autumn Period, Yanzi had a subordinate named Gaoliao. He worked under Yanzi for three years, always being very cautious and prudent. For three years as an official, he never made any mistakes. But suddenly one day, without any reason, Yanzi dismissed him. Yanzi's aides were curious and asked him: "Gaoliao has served you for three years, and you have not only failed to promote him but also dismiss him. Isn't this too harsh?" Yanzi replied: "I am a person with limited knowledge and need timely advice from others. It is like the four corners of a fishing net that need strong ropes to be stable. However, Gaoliao has been with me for three years and has never pointed out or corrected my mistakes, so I dismissed him." From Yanzi's words, we can easily understand some commonly known yet often forgotten truths.

Hearing criticism with joy is a kind of cultivation. Chinese traditional culture advocates the philosophy of "do not gossip about others' faults, always reflect on your own mistakes in private", and there is also the saying "honest advice may be unpleasant to hear but beneficial to act upon, just as bitter medicine cures disease". These reveal the attitude towards others' suggestions and opinions. From the incident of Yanzi dismissing Gaoliao, it can be seen that Yanzi did not have the stubbornness and hatred of some leaders, but was happy to accept corrections. In real life, some people like to hear flattery and cannot tolerate criticism or advice. The root cause is ideological rigidity, clinging to the existing nature, ignoring right and wrong, and having a self-centered and arrogant mindset. No one is perfect, who can avoid making mistakes? If one turns a deaf ear to sincere advice, it will only lead to the unchecked growth of personal defects, eventually resulting in unforeseen disastrous consequences. Hearing criticism with joy shows one's broad-mindedness, humility, and integrity. With such cultivation, one can clearly see shortcomings, gain insight into problems, win respect and trust, eliminate obstacles to development, and achieve self-improvement.

Speaking frankly is a virtue. As the prime minister of a country, Yanzi had many responsibilities and was prone to exhaustion and errors. He needed subordinates who could provide good advice and support, especially those who could point out mistakes at critical moments. However, Gaoliao only cared about protecting himself, being overly cautious, showing no initiative to contribute positively. It can be seen that although Gaoliao followed rules strictly, what he lacked was the courage to speak frankly and give bold advice. This courage allowed Wei Zheng to faithfully advise Emperor Taizong of Tang Dynasty, leading to the famous Zhenguan Era; it also let Ji An dare to challenge authority and become a renowned minister of the Western Han Dynasty despite ups and downs; and it enabled Peng Dehuai to present his ten-thousand-word petition during the Lushan Conference, facing unjust treatment but never regretting. Whether we are subordinates or in high positions, we all need such courage to speak frankly. Because speaking frankly comes from sincerity and deep concern, out of great responsibility for collective honor and work, and loyalty to one's position, it is a noble virtue worthy of respect. Based on this, frank speech shows zero tolerance for errors, keen insight into right and wrong, and promotes innovation through deep reflection.

Self-awareness is a wisdom. Yanzi's precious quality lies in his self-awareness even when he was at the peak of power and in the chaotic times of the Spring and Autumn Period. Self-awareness can be regarded as foresight, with a clear understanding and concern for oneself. It constantly warns oneself not to ignore personal flaws and not to wait until disaster strikes to take remedial actions. This is a rare and desirable strategic quality for leaders. Can leaders today conduct self-reflection like Yanzi? Self-awareness is not only foresight but also discernment. Yanzi did not hastily dismiss Gaoliao but spent three years observing him. When Yanzi himself noticed obvious mistakes, and Gaoliao still ignored them, dismissal became inevitable. This should serve as a lesson for current leaders who favor and protect their close associates. Self-awareness is also a higher vision. Yanzi had a unique understanding of officials' responsibilities. He did not equate following rules with fulfilling duties, nor being mistake-free with competence, nor closeness with trustworthiness. He judged everything based on the future of the state and the essence of responsibilities. This is worth learning from by leaders at all levels. History has proven that due to Yanzi's self-awareness, the State of Qi maintained long-term stability despite its decline.
