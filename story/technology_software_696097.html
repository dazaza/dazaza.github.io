International Herald Guide: Wenzhou property speculation groups temporarily disband

by y3y9v1k7p3 on 2012-03-07 20:35:20

After the introduction of the new real estate policy, many groups of home buyers who were planning to "set out" for Wuhan, Zhuhai, Hangzhou, Dalian and other places have automatically disbanded. However, the vast majority of Wenzhou property investors have not withdrawn from the market.

Reporters Zhang Heping and Zhang Ran of the International Herald Tribune reported from Wenzhou and Hangzhou: "I am still observing the strength and sustainability of the national regulation." Recently, a Wenzhou native surnamed Wang, who has bought multiple investment properties across the country but wished to remain anonymous, said this. Mr. Wang's mindset is quite representative among Wenzhou property speculators.

According to information from the Wenzhou Small and Medium Enterprises Promotion Association, although a small number of people have sold off their properties in Beijing and Shanghai, the vast majority of Wenzhou property investors have not exited the real estate market. Some even believe that after some time, there may be those who cannot withstand the pressure and will "tearfully" sell off their properties, making it a good opportunity to "buy at the bottom." They believe that under the increasingly severe expectations of inflation and the increasing pressure of RMB appreciation, investing in real estate remains an effective investment channel.

However, under the state of observation, several Wenzhou property speculation groups have automatically disbanded.

Temporary Abandonment

Wang Guangsen, with over ten years of experience in property speculation, works in a remote village in Ruian, Wenzhou, yet he has a clear understanding of the national real estate market conditions. Not long ago, he and a group of like-minded property buyers judged that based on the current "satellite cloud chart" of China's real estate market, new "cloud clusters" had formed in Wuhan and Zhuhai. The reasons were that Wuhan would be developed into the central city of Central China, and the central government and the Hong Kong-Macau Special Administrative Regions were investing in the construction of the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macao Bridge. In the future, there would be significant appreciation potential in property prices in Wuhan and Zhuhai, making it a good time to enter the market. For this reason, he and a group of "companions" confidently went to Wuhan for inspection with their bank cards.

The day after arriving in Wuhan, the State Council issued a notice requiring a 50% down payment and a loan interest rate of 1.1 times for second homes.

This made Wang Guangsen regretful: "If we had left a day later, we wouldn't have rushed here so urgently." A few days later, the State Council issued stringent measures including suspending third-home loans in certain areas, leading to his return without success.

Another group of dozens of property speculators from Ruian originally planned to go to Zhuhai for a quick profit but ended up dispersing unhappily.

It was learned that after the introduction of the new real estate policy, many enthusiastic groups of home buyers who were originally planning to set out for Wuhan, Zhuhai, Hangzhou, Dalian, and other places have automatically disbanded.

Before the introduction of the new policy, developers from Zhuhai, Hangzhou, Dalian, and Huzhou in Zhejiang Province successively came to Wenzhou to hold property exhibitions. Stimulated by the "one-day-one-price" phenomenon, these properties saw very promising transaction volumes. After the introduction of the "Ten National Measures," the transaction volumes of these properties were immediately "frozen." Some people who were celebrating the previous day for having paid a 200,000 yuan deposit and securing a profitable opportunity were lamenting the next day. Even some were afraid of losing big and were preparing to abandon the already paid deposits.

However, many investors expressed that currently, the adjustments such as a 50% down payment and a benchmark interest rate of 1.1 times are "bearable." What they fear most is the central government imposing a powerful personal housing consumption tax, such as property holding taxes or transaction taxes, "which would truly hurt."

The general manager of a well-known Wenzhou real estate company told reporters that Wenzhou people place great emphasis on the leverage effect of funds. For example, with only a 40% down payment, one can mobilize 60% of the funds, or amplify the capital by 1.5 times. If it cannot be amplified, and the purchase fully relies on personal funds, then one must consider whether there is a hundred percent appreciation potential. Currently, under the strong new policy, it is difficult to see any appreciation potential, so naturally, Wenzhou people choose to temporarily abandon.

Flow of Capital to Second-Tier and Third-Tier Cities and Upstream and Downstream Sectors

However, the desire for "money making money" won't let the money sit idle.

Chen Hong, head of Wenzhou Tianhao Real Estate Co., Ltd., analyzed that now, it's impossible to buy houses in Beijing and Shanghai, so Wenzhou people will turn to buying locally, which will exacerbate the amplification of demand, leading to rising house prices.

The day after the introduction of the "Ten National Measures," the last phase of the "Platinum Mansion" residential complex located in the suburbs of Wenzhou "successfully concluded," with nearly a hundred units averaging 45,000 yuan per square meter being fully subscribed. A buyer surnamed Ge said: "I have seen its appreciation potential. As long as the annual appreciation rate reaches 20%, it is better than letting the money sit dead in the bank or suffer depreciation due to inflation."

Zhou Dewen, president of the Wenzhou Small and Medium Enterprises Promotion Association, introduced that currently, many Wenzhou people are secretly investigating various opportunities, looking towards second-tier and third-tier cities. "The largest possibility for Wenzhou private capital is to flow into second-tier and third-tier cities. Some private capital might also enter the upstream and downstream sectors of the real estate industry," Chen Hong believed.

According to the Wenzhou Small and Medium Enterprises Promotion Association, two new trends have recently emerged: First, domestic and international venture capital companies, investment funds, private equity funds, asset management companies, etc., have been contacting the Wenzhou Small and Medium Enterprises Promotion Association, hoping to connect and help Wenzhou private funds find new outlets. Second, real estate developers from second-tier cities such as Chengdu, Shenyang, and Wuhan have quietly come to Wenzhou to promote their advantages, hoping that Wenzhou people will help them stabilize the market and seek financial support from Wenzhou people.

"Regulation will last until around June next year"

During interviews, many Wenzhou people expressed doubts about the long-term effects of the new policies.

Mr. Wang believes that the real estate industry can drive fifty to sixty related industries, contributing significantly to the national economy and solving employment for many people. If it is "cracked down on" too harshly, GDP in the second half of the year, especially next year, will definitely decrease somewhat. "I am more concerned that after each round of regulation in the past, property prices have rebounded sharply after the regulation," www.qqzajinhua.com.

Chen Hong analyzed for reporters that due to mortgage loans being restricted, but the demand for housing still exists, a group of people will sell the houses they are paying off through mortgages at a higher price to meet the desires of those eager to buy houses. "You get what you pay for, and this will inevitably lead to rising house prices," qqzajinhua www.qqzajinhua.com.

"This strict policy faces the test of 'how long can it last,'" Zhou Dewen said, "it can obviously curb in the short term, but from a long-term perspective, it is a double-edged sword. We think it cannot permanently suppress the market."

Zhou Dewen told reporters that many Wenzhou property speculators share similar views. He believes that the current regulation has initially shown results and will continue until obvious results are achieved. It is estimated that by around June next year, this policy will make corresponding adjustments.
