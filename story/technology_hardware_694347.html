8-Year-Old Girl Lives with Sick Grandfather after Relatives Passed Away (Photo)

by baimao55537682 on 2012-03-07 09:15:10

She will be 9 years old soon and really wants to go to school. Cao Li's eyes are filled with confusion, and she mutters: "I want to study." Cao Li also misses her grandmother and hopes that her grandmother can walk back from the prayer flags, even if it's just lying in bed. As long as she can hear her grandmother's call, she would respond to anything. Her father passed away early, and his appearance is even unclear now. Now there is only her grandfather left. His dark hands sometimes pick up the rotten vegetable leaves on the ground and put them directly into his mouth. When her grandfather has a seizure, Cao Li is very afraid and runs to her great-aunt's house to hide. Recently, she has been eating at her great-aunt's house every day, but her great-aunt is already old. Her great-grandmother (the mother-in-law of her great-aunt) is almost 90 years old. The great-aunt takes care of her own grandchildren, and Cao Li is afraid that the great-aunt will say to send her elsewhere, so she can only cry with her mouth open.

The neighbors said that Cao Li's family is in such a situation that even with the government's low-income subsidy, they have no ability to take care of themselves and it's hard to survive. And the future of this angel-like little girl is unimaginable. Due to lack of money for preschool, Cao Li cannot enter elementary school, and a person without normal life and learning, her future is destined to be a modern "primitive person"!

Li Fujie, an elder, said that although Cao Qibing has two half-brothers who often come to visit, they moved down from Cao Family Village to be close to their land. Cao Li's great-grandparents are still alive, both nearly 80 years old, and need their care. Plus, their son and daughter-in-law have gone out to work, leaving them to take care of the grandchildren who stay behind. They are already struggling to take care of themselves, let alone taking care of Cao Li and her grandfather!

In the village, when people hold banquets, the leftovers are often sent over to this grandfather and granddaughter.

Cao Li says, "I want to go to school."

When Cao Li's grandmother passed away, the government allocated 1500 yuan for funeral expenses, plus the money from the low-income subsidy, which was just enough to bury her grandmother.

Neighbor Li Fugjie said that for many years, Cao Li and her grandparents relied on the low-income subsidy to get by. Originally, with her grandmother managing the household while she was mentally sound, everything was fine. Now that her grandmother is gone, her grandfather is crazy and behaves erratically. When he has a seizure, he chases after Cao Li, hitting and scolding her, which is quite terrifying.

The neighbors told reporters that sometimes the neighbor's dogs also often come to visit Cao Li and her crazy grandfather. When her grandfather is calm, Cao Li quietly stays with the dog, watching the sun rise and set, watching the stars come and go, watching the village under the moonlight. When she gets tired, she goes back to sleep in her small house. In the dim and messy room, Cao Li dreams of herself carrying a new schoolbag, walking on the sunlit road like other children. Sometimes, she even dreams of her mother's warm big hand holding hers, celebrating the New Year and wearing new clothes.

Five years ago, when Cao Li was less than four years old, her mother left her silently and disappeared forever. At home, there were only her seriously ill grandmother and crazy grandfather left. Two months after her mother left, her father died unexpectedly. Two months ago, her grandmother, who had been paralyzed for six years, also passed away...

Li Fugjie said that when Cao Li's father, Cao Dexun, was still alive, I used to grow some watermelons in the field, but later due to owing others fertilizer money, the land was taken by the creditor for a year to offset the debt. When it was returned, Cao Dexun was already dead, and the land had lost all meaning for this family that had completely lost its labor force. Although Cao Qibing has another son, due to genetic reasons, this younger son is also crazy, working aimlessly, and no one knows where he has gone.

The villagers all know that these two depend on each other and cannot live apart. If the old man is missing, the young one searches everywhere, and if the young one is missing, the old man looks for her. When neighbors come to visit, only Cao Li can understand her grandfather's words and translate for him. Cao Qibing talks to himself, not knowing what he is saying. Sometimes he laughs loudly, and sometimes he cries loudly, tears streaming down his face.

From then on, this family only had a paralyzed grandmother who was mentally sound. Lying in bed, the grandmother directed the young Cao Li to do housework. Cao Li is more than 8 years old now and hasn't started school yet. When the children in the village went to school, this little girl wandered around the village alone, looking very pitiful.

Li Fugjie said: "Cao Li's grandfather often picks up the yellow cabbage leaves and rotten fruits that others don't want and brings them home to eat. The low-income account is now managed by a relative named Tian Hua, who withdraws the money every month and gives it to Cao Qibing. Cao Qibing's mind doesn't function properly, and he spends the money randomly on things."

Tian Xinghui is a kind-hearted person in the village who often visits this poor family. She said: "When Cao Li's grandfather goes crazy, he smashes pots and pans, grabs knives, and chases after Cao Li, hitting and scolding her. Since her grandmother passed away, because of fear, Cao Li runs to her great-aunt's house to eat and play, returning home only to sleep late at night."

Currently, Cao Qibing has about a hundred pounds of coal and half a bag of rice left. It looks like they will soon run out of firewood and food.

Zhang Feiqin is Cao Li's neighbor. She told the reporter: "When Cao Li was four years old, her mother, seeing the impossible life ahead, left cruelly and quietly, leaving behind a crazy grandfather and a paralyzed grandmother. Two months after his wife left, Cao Li's father, Cao Dexun, suddenly fell off the bed one noon, hitting his head and creating a small wound. After convulsing, he left the world."

The gloomy clouds have collapsed this small family.
