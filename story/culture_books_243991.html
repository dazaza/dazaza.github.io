Happiness Regained: Can You Hear My Heart Beat? Author: Li Li Xiang

by jqrxass on 2009-09-29 11:39:00

Eighteen-year-old Zhong Bi was in her third year of college when her mother was diagnosed with breast cancer. She spent her days running back and forth between school and the hospital, where she eventually met successful businessman Zuo Si. Zuo Si had ill intentions towards Zhong Bi, but she rejected his advances. Unfortunately, Zhong's mother's condition worsened as her breast cancer metastasized to bone marrow cancer. In her most difficult time, Zhong Bi had no choice but to succumb to Zuo Si's demands. Upon learning of this, Zhong's mother stopped taking her medication and committed suicide. By this point, Zhong Bi was pregnant and gave birth prematurely due to overwhelming grief. She left her child with Zuo Si, fled from him, and returned to school to continue her unfinished studies. Zhong Bi worked hard to start anew and met a talented young man named Zhang Shuo, who was manipulative and brooding. Despite her growing affection for Zhang Shuo, Zuo Si relentlessly pursued her and threatened her with their child. Out of guilt towards her son, Zuo Xue, she agreed to marry him.

Five years later, Zhang Shuo, now a successful man, made a heartfelt declaration of love to Zhong Bi in front of thousands of spectators: "It is not the wind that moves, nor the pennant that moves, do you hear my heart moving?" At this moment, Zhong Bi was planning to divorce Zuo Si. A highly publicized and lengthy custody battle over their son Zuo Xue shook the whole of Hong Kong, with numerous media outlets following and reporting on the case, making it the talk of the town. Exhausted mentally and physically, Zhong Bi struggled through it all...

**Part 1**

On a summer night without stars or moon, the shadows of the trees swayed, and the lights were dim. The night breeze caused the red banner in front of the century-old auditorium to flutter loudly, with four golden words written on it: "Graduation Party." After the crowd dispersed, Zhang Shuo and Zhong Bi walked out one after another. The branches moved, leaves rustled, and the wind howled, like whispers in the dead of night. Zhang Shuo looked up, slightly furrowing his brows, "The wind is really strong tonight, don't you hear?" His tone was casual and polite, just small talk. It was the sound of the flag being blown. Zhong Bi stood under the tree, hands in her trouser pockets, gazing intently at his tall silhouette, her heart racing. Zhang Shuo turned around, raising an eyebrow in question upon seeing her standing still.

**Part 2**

Waking up early, it was another new day. In her dream, vague memories of youthful events lingered, a fleeting pleasure. She rubbed her slightly sore eyes, feeling a splitting headache. Why did she remember such distant things so clearly? Every detail was vivid and clear as if it happened right before her eyes. Like a wandering soul, she got out of bed. Zuo Xue was already dressed neatly, enjoying fresh milk, cream puffs, and cheese sauce at the dining table, his face covered in food. Without looking up, he asked, "What time did you come home last night?" Zhong Bi felt dizzy, her mind still somewhat dazed, ignoring him. He threw down his fork and hummed, "Where are my mango sticky rice and durian pastries?" How could she leave him alone?

**Part 3**

Every working day, Zhong Bi felt like she was being squeezed dry, filled with mixed feelings of love and hate for Zhang Shuo, along with resentment and anger. She felt like Huang Shiran and Xi'er reincarnated, yet she didn't dare to complain openly and could only make sarcastic remarks indirectly, "Without tilling or weeding, why do you take three hundred measures of grain? Without hunting or trapping, why do I see your courtyard full of game? O gentleman, you do not eat without working!" Zhang Shuo couldn't understand what she was muttering about, assuming it wasn't anything good, so he ignored her. Intellectuals are always sour and moaning without cause. On their way to work together, seeing her exhausted and sleeping in the back seat, Zhang Shuo said, "Actually, you might want to consider changing jobs." Aibaiseng wasn't a place for slacking off, and he wouldn't give her any special treatment either.
