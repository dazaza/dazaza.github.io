Abstract: A research report named "2010 China Green Development Index Annual Report - Inter-Provincial Comparison" was released by a joint research team composed of Beijing Normal University, Southwestern University of Finance and Economics, and the China Economic Climate Monitoring Center of the National Bureau of Statistics. The report compared and ranked the green development levels of provinces across the country.

According to the introduction, the China Green Development Index includes three categories of indicators. The greening degree of economic growth reflects the extent to which production affects resource consumption and the environment; the resource and environmental carrying potential shows the potential that natural resources and the environment in the region can carry; government support reflects the level and strength of social organizers in resolving the contradictions between resources, the environment, and economic development. Based on this, the index is further divided into 55 indicators such as per capita regional GDP, labor productivity, per capita water resources, per capita industrial solid waste emissions, environmental pollution control investment as a percentage of regional GDP, and the ecological restoration and management rate of mining areas.

Except for Qinghai Province, the top 10 provinces and cities in the comprehensive ranking of this index are all located in the eastern region. Members of the research team stated that this indicates that the eastern region has a relatively high level of economic development, relatively strong financial resources, relatively fast industrial upgrading, a higher proportion of the tertiary industry, and relatively higher efficiency in resource utilization and environmental protection efforts. Among them, Beijing ranks first overall due to the increased environmental management efforts by the government during the Olympic Games period and the heavy investment in environmental construction.

However, the report also shows that the eastern region has a low per capita resource possession, and the ranking of resource and environmental potential indicators is relatively backward.

Although the western region lags behind in economic development, many provinces have prominent resource advantages, laying the foundation for green development, and are at a medium level in the overall ranking. The green development index of the six central provinces is lower than the national average, with most provinces except Anhui and Jiangxi ranking in the last ten. Among them, Shanxi Province ranks at the bottom due to losing points in the resource and environmental carrying potential. The three northeastern provinces show a "two-way differentiation" trend. Heilongjiang has good resource and environmental conditions but very low government support; Liaoning and Jilin rank high in economic greening but have very low resource and environmental potential.

The research report points out that the inland central region, as the "sandwich layer," currently faces the greatest pressure in green development. On the one hand, it bears a large amount of manufacturing transfer from the east, further compressing resource and environmental space; on the other hand, its economic strength is far less robust than that of the east, limiting the investment in resources and environmental protection.
