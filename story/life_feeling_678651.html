I Have Always Been Surrounded by Happiness

In the long river of memory, those years that should not be forgotten are like a gentle stream flowing through my heart. In the mysterious course of fate, I always set the white sails of memory back to that warm journey, feeling the happiness and sweetness of love. That feeling is sweet, a little sour, without any impurities.

Ru is my wife now. When we first met, it was my eighth year in the army, and she was teaching at a middle school in the city.

She entered my life because of a blessing text message. Seeing the unfamiliar number and the warm wishes, I politely replied, after all, receiving someone's blessings is also a kind of happiness. Not long after, she replied: "If there is an edge, we will meet for thousands of miles, why not become friends?" I gladly agreed.

That's how we met. During our leisure time, we would send text messages, make phone calls, and talk about our work, study, and lives. As time went on, I found there was an inexplicable rapport between us, and we spoke without restraint, as if we were old friends reunited after a long separation. The topics of discussion also increased, slowly touching upon love...

For a period of time, sending text messages became our mandatory course. Every morning, I would send her a timely message: "Little lazy bug, the sun is shining on your butt, get up quickly, be careful not to be late for class." She would reply to me: "I know, be safe during your training, don't overwork yourself." We often chatted until very late at night before reluctantly going to sleep, and as soon as we woke up the next day, we would immediately pick up our phones. At that time, the sky was always blue, and our hearts were always warm.

Thus, we were immersed in immense joy. Sometimes, when I saw those caring text messages, my heart would suddenly race, my face would turn red, and I would feel something unusual in my heart. Could this be what they call dating? I didn't know, but one thing was certain: my life could no longer go on without her.

On the seventh day of the seventh lunar month, I told her the idea I had cherished in my heart: "Be my girlfriend." She paused for a moment and replied: "Let's talk about it after we meet..." Was she unwilling? Did she only see me as a chat friend, or had she never thought of being my girlfriend? Or was there another reason? My heart became restless.

We agreed to meet a month later. A month may not seem like much to others, but for me, it felt endless. In the uneasy wait, I painfully passed this month, and we finally met at the agreed-upon location.

Her hair flowed like a waterfall, soft and smooth, emitting a faint fragrance. A well-fitted black windbreaker outlined her full and slender figure. Her bright eyes were like a pool of water, clear and transparent, making one's heart flutter. Her mature face was filled with charming smiles, especially her glasses which perfectly reflected her profound cultural cultivation. Her speech and behavior exuded the dignity and tranquility unique to modern women.

According to common sense, such an excellent girl should not be missed under any circumstances. But my strong self-esteem reminded me: she came from a wealthy family, while I had nothing; she had an outstanding appearance and a stable job, while I was just a poor soldier; she lived in a beautiful city, while I was in a remote mountain village thousands of miles away. All these things plunged me into deep conflict. Could I give her the happy life she wanted? Could I make her happy for a lifetime? I hesitated, I wandered, I even dared not imagine that she could be my girlfriend anymore. Perhaps everything before was just a dream.

Walking on the street, beside us was a constant flow of vehicles rushing in their respective directions. My heart was in extreme conflict, not knowing where to go. At a crossroads, she said she had to return to school. By some strange impulse, I failed to understand her meaning and let her return to school alone, although her school was only a few steps away from that intersection.

Back home, I lost my center of gravity, feeling dazed. I always felt the sky was gray, dark, without any brightness. For a long time, I locked myself in the room, thinking about those beautiful memories. I was extremely disappointed, feeling as if the whole world had collapsed. One evening, she suddenly called and said: "Do you remember what you said on the seventh day of the seventh lunar month?"

"I, I... don't remember..." I didn't know what was wrong with me, and I blurted out that inappropriate sentence.

There was a pause on the other end of the phone: "Oh, is that so! It's getting late, I won't bother you anymore, get some rest."

On the day I returned to duty after my leave, she came to the station to see me off. We quietly sat in a small restaurant next to the station, neither of us speaking. Time slipped by second by second, and it was getting closer to the time to board the train. She took out a letter from her bag and handed it to me, saying: "This is all my thoughts from these months spent with you. After you finish reading it at the base, burn it, pretend this never happened, and we never met..." After finishing, she got up and walked out of the restaurant, disappearing into the night.

My mind was in fierce turmoil, whether to chase her back or to give up on this relationship. I couldn't hesitate anymore. I believed she could understand me, no matter what kind of life we led, no matter how much hardship we endured, she would be with me... I rushed out of the restaurant and grabbed her who was about to get into a taxi. At that moment, I held onto my own love.

The speeding train tore through the night towards the distance. The sound of the wheels colliding with the tracks continuously struck my heart, unable to calm down. I found a quiet place, with a nervous heart, and opened the letter. When I finished reading the letter, I felt as if I had been hit by something hard. I tried to hold back, but it was impossible, completely beyond my control. I just felt my eyes becoming hotter and more swollen, and before I could reach out to rub them, something surged out of my eyes.

Every sentence in the letter was her heartfelt words, every word was her sincere expression. In fact, she had already regarded me as the person she had been waiting for, and she was fully prepared to endure hardships. Yet I stubbornly clung to so-called false pretenses and social status. I didn't know how many tears she shed while writing this letter, how many times her heart ached. I realized I was terribly wrong, every word after we met deeply hurt her. Reading her letter, I felt incredibly insignificant and weak.

I carefully placed the letter in my bosom, as if holding her heart. Let her wounded heart be closer to me, let me give it warmth, let her lie in my arms forever and never separate.

After getting off the train, I urgently found a public telephone booth and dialed her mobile phone. "Will you be my girlfriend?"

There was a pause on the other end of the phone, followed by her crying voice, each tear falling on my heart, piercing pain like needles.

...

My girlfriend has always hoped that one day, I would suddenly appear at the gate of their school, holding a bunch of fiery red roses. At the moment of dismissal, I would kneel in front of her, passionately declaring: "Marry me!" Then, amidst everyone's cheers, she would take the roses from my hand and say: "Love me forever."

I will never forget that day when my girlfriend and I walked together on the red carpet. Surrounded by numerous relatives and friends, I held a bunch of fiery red roses and knelt in front of her, saying: "I will love you forever!" Looking at my wife, who was as beautiful as a flower, I finally said the words I should have said long ago. My wife was dazzlingly beautiful, captivatingly charming, and she shed tears of happiness at that moment. An elder nearby hurriedly said: "Happy tears, happy tears!"

A year later, we had a beloved son. Every time I watched my son and I playing around, my wife's face was always filled with smiles. She always said: "It's great to be together as a family!"

On the night before I returned to duty after my leave, my wife and I sat at the bedside, enjoying the rare moonlit night. The bright moonlight gently bathed the earth, soft and smooth. My wife quietly leaned against my chest, her body soft and warm.

My wife suddenly said: "Do you still remember that text message when we first met?"

I said: "Of course I remember, I will never forget it in my life. What's the matter?"

My wife suddenly smiled mischievously: "That text message was actually sent intentionally. After seeing your picture, I decided to contact you..." My wife looked quite proud.

It turns out I've been deceived all along by my wife, but that feeling of being deceived is really good.

Looking at our sleeping son beside us, I hugged my wife tighter.

In fact, I have always been surrounded by happiness.
