A New Socialist Countryside Based on Science and Technology

Using modern scientific and technological achievements to develop traditional agriculture is an important direction for building a new socialist countryside. The application of science and technology, such as the Internet, in agricultural production can be transformed into great agricultural productivity. Agricultural technologization is one of the important tasks in building a new socialist countryside.

Overview of Xihuangfu Village

Xihuangfu Village is adjacent to the Jingkai Expressway. There are currently 283 farming households with 2,500 mu (about 167 hectares) of arable land. The entire village mainly engages in planting, breeding, and services. In recent years, Xihuangfu Village has fully utilized its transportation, location, and resource advantages by developing facility agriculture and implementing diversified operations, which have promoted the rapid development of rural economy. For three consecutive years, the village has been rated as a "National Civilized Village," and now more than 150 groups and over 7,000 people visit and conduct research in Xihuangfu Village each year.

She doesn't need to drag watermelons around the streets shouting to sell them; she only needs to "holler" online.

This "hollering" turned out to be very effective. Without leaving her village, within less than two months of the watermelon sales period, all her family's watermelons were sold out, and at an unprecedentedly good price: making 200,000 yuan!

Her name is Cao Demei, a watermelon farmer from Xihuangfu Village, Yufa Town, Daxing District. She led the whole village in selling watermelons online, creating an unprecedented new atmosphere in Xihuangfu Village: in previous years, villagers would leave the village to sell watermelons, which was both frustrating and busy; this year, trucks come into the village, and both buyers and sellers are happy.

Busy Selling Watermelons at Midnight

On September 27th, in Xihuangfu Village, red doors, wooden window frames... elegant and imposing imitation ancient courtyards are neatly arranged. One of these is Cao Demei's home.

Cao Demei graduated from high school and is considered a "cultured person" in the village. In the village, she isn't considered a top watermelon grower, having only grown watermelons for five years. However, like all watermelon farmers, she has many bitter memories of selling watermelons.

"It's not easy! A few years ago when we sold watermelons, every day at two or three o'clock in the morning, no matter how tired or sleepy, we had to get up, drive cars loaded with watermelons, and take them to wholesale markets like Xinfadi and Shawo. We had to finish shipping the goods before dawn." Cao Demei said that back then, after taking the watermelons to the market, they would wait for customers to come, and also had to walk around the market a few times to understand the day's watermelon market situation, then set their own prices based on the quality of the watermelons.

Selling watermelons was like gambling on luck

"Really, a few years ago, selling watermelons was like gambling on luck!" Cao Demei said, there were always unlucky times. After a bumpy ride to take the watermelons to the market, sometimes they couldn't sell them and had to take them back, which was a common occurrence to make two or three trips back and forth, and finally had to sell them at a low price.

When encountering such bad luck, farmers suffered "three losses": loss of fuel money, loss of booth fees, and loss of management fees. What hurt even more was that after two or three trips back and forth, good watermelons got damaged and had to be painfully thrown away!

Often tricked by watermelon dealers

Like Cao Demei, villager Wang Fenglan also encountered many problems when selling watermelons in the past. Xihuangfu Village used many new technologies for growing watermelons, multi-layer coverage being one of them. Although this technology allows watermelons to hit the market more than ten days earlier, it also brought difficulties to the villagers.

"Every year around this time, I feel quite intimidated," Wang Fenglan said, "not knowing what the market price really is. Although the harvest is good, we farmers stay in the fields all day and don't know the outside situation, fearing to encounter those cunning watermelon dealers. If they 'trick' us, even with a good harvest, we won't get a good price."

Learning computers felt like wasting time

Unexpectedly, this traditional way of selling watermelons ended so quickly.

"In fact, before this year, I was still a computer illiterate, only able to type!" Cao Demei said, "I previously learned some knowledge about online sales through TV, but always thought it was something far away from me, something only big bosses did when doing big business."

In March this year, Daxing District launched the "Digital Home" project, and 20 brand-new computers were delivered to the village committee. Seeing computers enter the village, many villagers said, "What can learning that thing do? Isn't it a waste of time?"

This is exactly what the leaders of Yufa Town in Daxing District were worried about. Indeed, before this year, more than 70% of the villagers in Xihuangfu Village had never touched a computer, and even fewer could use them proficiently. "We mustn't let these advanced devices become just showpieces! At the very least, we should teach farmers to use computers to sell watermelons."
