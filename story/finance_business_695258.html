by hoouk260 on 2012-03-07 15:04:31

After she left me, a traveler passed by. Silently, invisibly - he took her away with a sigh.

This is from William Blake's "The Secret of Love". After a breakup, what should one do? This is perhaps the first question many people face after losing a romance. Some can take it calmly, smiling and telling themselves that what's past is past; others will be heartbroken, crying endlessly and desperately wanting to go back to the past; some lovers will politely say goodbye and cherish each other, then become two skew lines in different directions; some will harbor resentment, remembering all the mistakes of the past, telling themselves that they are just saying goodbye to an idiot; some remain silent, some cry loudly, some seek comfort from friends, some fall into loneliness. Too many reasons lead to breakups, and after breaking up, there will be millions of different situations. Which have you experienced?

The British poet William Blake wrote in "The Secret of Love":

Soon as she was gone from me

A traveler came by

Silently, invisibly -

He took her with a sigh

Zhu Guangqian translated this literally as:

She left me

Not long after, a passerby came

Without a sound, without a trace -

With a slight sigh, he took her away

Li Ao's translation has more poetic flavor:

After my beloved left, the traveler came.

In silence, he sighed deeply, and both disappeared.

When a relationship reaches its end, lovers can't help but imagine how their other half is doing. Maybe you once loved him/her very much, or maybe he/she once loved you very much, or maybe you were inseparable, or maybe your relationship was as bland as water. However, after separating, the two individuals become independent entities. Or, while you are suffering immensely, your he/she may have been "taken away with a sigh" by someone else. If the relationship has completely ended, the best way is to get out of the shadow of the breakup as soon as possible, not overthinking, not worrying, not being sad, not feeling sorry for yourself, not being angry, not hating, not remembering, and not forgetting.

"Five Girlfriends Before Me": Love Has No Rules

Have you ever had thoughts of self-pity or self-abandonment when you felt hopeless about love? Have you ever wanted to end it all because of the pain you've endured in love? The movie "My Five Ex-Girlfriends" begins with a man who attempts suicide after a failed relationship. He writes farewell letters to his five previous girlfriends, recounting the harm the relationships caused him.

However, this is not a tragic story but rather a light-hearted comedy. We follow the memories of the disheartened man as we meet his five ex-girlfriends, from acquaintance to intimacy, from intimacy to love, and finally from love to separation. The entire process is presented through imagery. Moreover, this adds many spices, and the constantly changing scenes make one doubt if this is a movie about heartbreak. But as you continue watching, you reach the climax and begin to resonate emotionally with the concept of love. For example, the movie says: "Despite the incredibly small probability, we still found each other. Doesn't this prove that everything isn't just coincidence? Perhaps God is also playing a matchmaker game." Reflecting carefully, how did you and the person in your life who made you sad, moved you, hated you, and deeply loved you come together? Wasn't it slightly careless, and you would have missed each other for life? Even possibly just waking up a minute late, running a red light, or losing a coin could have made you miss each other.

Duncan in the movie, after experiencing failure in love again and again, has lost faith in love. He said: "I decided I wouldn't play this game anymore. I don't understand the rules, and this kind of hurt is too painful." But before long, another girl entered his life. What love is, the movie doesn't give a clear explanation, but at the end, it provides an imaginative conclusion.

"500 Days of Summer": What is Love?

When this movie came out, everyone was impressed. Such a fresh, romantic, slightly sad, and warm little movie hadn't been seen for a long time. Thus, Zooey Deschanel became a popular sweetheart, and Joseph Gordon-Levitt began to shine.

Watching "500 Days of Summer" is a heartbreaking experience because many people can find a bit of themselves in this movie. It feels like Mark Webb hit the nail on the head, willingly dissecting his own long-suppressed heart. At least, that's how I feel. I constantly compare myself to Tom, viewing Summer as the benchmark for my dream lover or perfect partner, feeling joy and sorrow alongside the film, and then smiling contentedly after it ends. Yes, even now, rewatching this movie, I still unconsciously empathize.

Autumn has arrived, leaves are falling, and hearts are filled with melancholy. Unfortunately, in this lonely and desolate season, I lost my Summer, and I became a heartbroken person. Unlike in the movie, this loss was entirely my fault. I once said, "Even though I am enslaved by fate, I still want to fight with all my might. I want my love to be conveyed to the person I want to love, not the person I must love. Maybe one day, under the thousand cuts of reality, I will change this original intention, but before I take my last breath, I still hope to meet my Summer and fulfill my fantasy about love."

She came into my world in winter, and I always considered her my spring. But only after losing her did I realize how much it hurt. She was my Summer, and she left me in this autumn...

"Chungking Express": Warmth Amidst Steel and Concrete

In the city, skyscrapers stand tall, forming a cold forest of steel and concrete. Each person lives woodenly in this forest, loves woodenly, and feels sadness woodenly. Wong Kar-wai's portrayal of the urban men and women's state of mind in this movie touches the heart, simple yet luxurious, gentle yet stern, fragmented yet profound, full of despair yet hinting at hope.

In the movie, Takeshi Kaneshiro's character 223 says: "Everyone experiences heartbreak. Every time I break up, I run. Running can evaporate the moisture in your body so that I don't cry easily. How can I cry? In May's heart, I am a cool guy."

In the morning, I went jogging, a habit since summer started, which was interrupted due to weather conditions for a while. Today, I got up early again to run. The sky doesn't brighten as early as before. Getting up at five, it was pitch black outside. After running for a while, it gradually lit up. I realized that running in the dark gives me a strong sense of security. I thought sweating during running would prevent me from crying, but facts proved me wrong. Dear, in your heart, I am a cool guy, right? So why do I keep crying for you?

Unlike 223, Tony Leung's character 663 comforts every object in his house after a breakup. He comforts the soap, the towel, the doll, but never himself. After a breakup, the best comfort doesn't come from others but from oneself. Only by getting past this hurdle can one truly move out of the shadow.

I tried to move out of the shadow, but I couldn't. I wear the clothes you gave me, smell the perfume you gave me, look at the sticky notes you posted behind my photos, read the letters you wrote me, miss everything in the past, and regret my own foolish actions, yet I can't find any appropriate words to comfort myself. Dear, how can I forget you?

"Cashback": This Moment, Pause and Love You

People who lose love often find themselves trapped in a helpless and confused state, unable to eat or drink, unable to concentrate on anything, and constantly thinking about the past. Ben in "Cashback" is such a person. Losing love is like being drained of energy. Nights are sleepless, spirits are low, time seems to slowly drag forward in pain, and pushing it to go faster makes it even slower. Actually, people who have broken up fear the night. Silence, emptiness, loneliness, depth, darkness—these emotions are all poisons that torment the heart of those who have broken up.

I've been insomnia for several days, waking up startled around three or four o'clock every night with all sorts of strange dreams. Then I'm drenched in cold sweat and cannot fall asleep again. I stare at the ceiling, not knowing what I'm thinking about, not knowing what I need to think about, just sitting there dumbfounded, at a loss, waiting until dawn breaks, but my eyes are still blurred. Calculating carefully, I sleep only two or three hours a day, and even these few hours are spent in nightmares, without a moment of peace. Should I also find a part-time job at night like Ben?

I wish time could pause, and I wish I could control time. I wish I could freeze the second when I love you most, without having to think about complicated problems, just quietly and peacefully loving you, without doubts. But I can't do it. Time flows like sand, impossible to grab or stop. I can only give up resisting and wait for it to swallow me whole.

Sleepless nights, sleepless nights, every night is hard to sleep.

"Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind": How Can I Forget You?

In Alexander Pope's "Eloisa to Abelard," he wrote:

How happy is the blameless Vestal’s lot.

The world forgetting, by the world forgot.

Eternal sunshine of the spotless mind.

Each prayer accepted, and each wish resigned.

The destiny of the sacred goddess is so blissful.

Forgetting the world, forgotten by the world.

The pure mind shines with eternal sunshine.

Every prayer is accepted, every wish is abandoned.

After a breakup, should I forget the past? Can I forget the past? Remember the essay topic for the college entrance exam one year—it was trendy—"If memory can be transplanted," this science fiction-themed essay topic surprised everyone, as even the eight-part essay began to have new trends. Then for the movie "Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind," it raises such a hypothesis: if memory can be erased.

If those memories could be removed, how many people would willingly do so? At least, I absolutely would not. Even if the memories contain pain, even if they contain sorrow, even if they contain disgrace, even if they contain bitterness, I want to remember them clearly, without missing a single detail. People without memories are like empty vessels, forever thin, forever cold. With memories, life becomes colorful, and joys and sorrows are all precious wealth.

For those who have loved, memories are even more precious things. To remember how you once loved each other, to remember how you once stayed together, to remember how you once supported each other, to remember how you once relied on each other, those sweet moments far outweigh the bitter ones, bringing warmth to the heart.

I can't forget the past days, I can't forget your smile, I can't forget your tenderness, I can't forget your shyness, I can't forget your grievances, I can't forget your sadness, I can't forget your understanding, I can't forget how you loved me, I can't forget how I loved you, everything about you, I can't forget, because I still love you.

"A Chinese Odyssey": Love You Forever

As a child, watching "A Chinese Odyssey" made me laugh stupidly and smile foolishly; later as I grew older, I began to understand what love is, so watching "A Chinese Odyssey" again, laughter was mixed with some inexplicable sadness; later on, encountering true love, understanding true love, and giving true love, I understood the phrase spoken by Zi Xia in the movie: "Loving someone can be so painful," turning "A Chinese Odyssey" from a crazy funny nonsensical movie into a thorough healing movie. Inside, there can be unrestrained happiness, but after the happiness comes long-lasting sadness.

When the melody of Lu Guan-ting's "Love of a Lifetime" slowly plays, anyone who resonates will not be able to hold back their tears. "There was once a sincere love placed before me, but I didn't cherish it. When I lost it, I regretted it bitterly, and the most painful thing in the mortal world is none other than this. If heaven could give me another chance, I would tell that girl three words - I love you. If I must add a deadline to this love, I hope it is - ten thousand years!"

Heaven won't give anyone another chance, and neither of us has a moonlight box to travel through time and space. So when you say "What's lost cannot be regained," my heart is completely shattered. Without strength to support, without strength to rally, without strength to console myself, and without strength to forget the past.

"Once lovers part, they will never return. Silent and alone, gazing beyond the mundane world. Flowers may wither, but they will bloom again. The one I love most is vaguely beyond the clouds." That vast desert, stirring up yellow dust, and at this moment, I am hesitating in the barren desert of my heart. Looking around, all I see is desolation, and the one I love most is vaguely beyond the clouds...

"Lee's Adventure": Through Everything to Find You

Originally, I wasn't optimistic about this movie because I didn't like the original 20-minute animation, and disliking it doesn't require a reason. But I tried to watch this movie with a calm and unfamiliar attitude, and in fact, the movie is more exciting than the original work. In this absurd yet real world, the act of a heartbroken person going to great lengths to retrieve their lover is surely enough to become the root cause of touching heaven and earth, plus the magnificent story structure and the Cult-like image style, making "Lee's Adventure" possess qualities that make people look twice.

Li Xianji says: "In this lonely world, only one person sincerely accompanied me, and I have lost her forever." Hearing this line of dialogue, I already felt a touch of heartfelt sorrow. My world has always been lonely, I don't like socializing, I don't like interpersonal relationships, I don't like networking, I don't like flattery, I don't like hypocritical exchanges, I don't like dancing with masks, thus, my world has become a huge transparent prison, with only one prisoner, which is myself. I wanted to lock myself up, not caring about anything, just being happy in my own happiness, grieving in my own grief, but you broke into my world. In this lonely world, you were the only one sincerely accompanying me, giving me sincere love, and I lost you.

In this messy yet clear movie story, it conveys a valuable spirit called life. Even the most absurd stories, if completely detached from real-life experiences, would be empty and annoying. What touches people most is always genuine feelings, just like the relentless search after a breakup.

"Comrades: Almost a Love Story": Circle Round and Round

In essence, this is a love movie with a pitiful process and a sweet ending. Two young people away from home developed an indescribable affection for each other, yet lost themselves in this indescribable sentiment. They kept searching for what they called themselves, kept searching for what they called life, but never realized that this search was flawed from the start. Because they themselves didn't know what was themselves, what was life.

Watching "Comrades: Almost a Love Story" doesn't feel sweet at all. Regarding love, this movie's answer is that no matter how many times you miss each other, no matter how far apart you are, lovers will eventually be together. I think this concept is nonsense, or overly idealistic. When Leon Lai and Maggie Cheung watch the news about Teresa Teng together through the glass window, it puts all the previous buildup at a disadvantage, and this ending is somewhat too dramatic. I prefer the movie to stop earlier, with Maggie Cheung chasing Leon Lai but losing him in the end, standing alone in the vast city, feeling a sense of loss. The camera pulls back, revealing the oppressive atmosphere of the urban jungle made of steel and concrete. Stopping here is just right. ——Some people, once missed, can never be retrieved; being retrieved is an extremely rare event.

I believe everything is fate, but also believe nothing is fate, which is contradictory. Because I've never understood what fate really is. Is it a predestined arrangement, or our own choices? Is it a supreme control, or the butterfly effect after a minor deviation? The ending of "Comrades: Almost a Love Story" carries a tone of fatalism. Originally, the two were closely connected, taking a big circle and coming back together again. Isn't this fate?

"High Fidelity": Come On, Let Me Take You Away

A man who runs a record store, full of random thoughts, living without any main thread, seemingly carefree but actually close to exhaustion, talking incessantly about his past romances, former girlfriends, and the joys and sorrows of memories, constitutes this "High Fidelity".

Watching this movie is like listening to someone chat with you. His words are fragmented, his values are absurd, but you still want to listen to him talk, and sometimes laugh, cry, be at a loss, and feel sentimental with him. John Cusack's character Rob is a "breaking the fourth wall" role. This is his story, his movie. He occasionally jumps out and talks to you face-to-face about his insights, as if no one else can hear or see. Coincidentally, some of these words can completely capture your heart and make you empathize.

For instance, he says: "I always have distractions, which makes me fail to do many things, such as planning my future. Perhaps it would be better not to insist on loving someone, so I can freely love others. But such behavior is suicidal—chronic suicide." He appears to be doing what he likes, living freely, but inside, he is a mess, and life is terrible. All stems from his inner uncertainty, always doubting everything, and unable to focus on one thing, one relationship, one person. It's not that his heart is bad, but he can't control his thoughts, or doesn't know how to control them. He is always lost, not knowing where to go, and at this fragile moment, he wishes someone would come beside him and say: "Come on, let me take you away, let's go." I wish the same.
