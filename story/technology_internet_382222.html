Summary of XOOM User Experiences

by diglog on 2011-02-25 04:22:53

As the first tablet to use Android 3.0 Honeycomb — a system specifically customized for tablets — the XOOM has captured the attention of countless enthusiasts. Several foreign media outlets also provided reviews promptly after its release. Overall, Honeycomb introduced numerous optimizations and changes tailored for tablets. Although some details are still imperfect, Motorola ensures the quality of the hardware. The combination of both guarantees that the XOOM is a competitive and attractive product, making it a strong rival to the iPad in the tablet market. Let's take a look at what several foreign media outlets have to say about the XOOM:

BGR:

In terms of hardware, whether it's the touch-coated shell, Gorilla Glass, or sturdy plastic, everything feels premium. The design of the notification light and camera indicator light is also considerate and practical. In terms of battery life, during BGR editors' daily usage, the standby time over a few days was comparable to the iPad. However, one inconvenience is the need for a dedicated charger, as it cannot be charged via microUSB unless using the official dock accessory.
