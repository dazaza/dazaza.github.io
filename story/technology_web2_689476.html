Woman hides drugs on children to traffic (Picture)

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-04 09:47:40

The police officers who were handling the case checked the children's diapers.

In the past few days, every day, there were six or seven women carrying babies and some men on the east side of Xi'an train station square hurriedly exchanging items... Since January 10th, the reporter has been continuously stationed at the train station square for three days. From some abnormal actions of these women carrying babies, some clues were found.

■ Women carrying babies often secretly "trade"

During the stakeout, the reporter discovered that these women carrying babies are approximately between 20 to 40 years old. There are generally around six or seven of them. Each one is wearing a large scarf, with a child aged between one to three years old strapped to their back. Except for eating and going to the bathroom, they spend the entire day walking back and forth on the square. From morning till night, every so often, a man would come up, after some negotiation, the man would hand over money, and the women would immediately give him a small paper package. The other party would then quickly leave, the entire process lasting just a few seconds. If it weren't for continuous observation over several days, an ordinary person wouldn't notice anything. Near these women, there would also be some men waiting to receive items. According to incomplete statistics, every day at least six or seven men come to purchase items, mainly concentrated between 2 PM to 4 PM.

■ Carrying children is a form of cover

"They frequently appear here, always up to no good," according to informants. This group of women carrying babies have been appearing at the train station square for some time. Previously, they were selling drugs on the square and had been dealt with by the police. Now, carrying children is a form of cover.

"These children are too pitiful, being so young and already suffering on the square, and they're exposed to all sorts of bad things," said members of the public. After observing on-site, the reporter noticed that these children were very obedient. Even in cold weather, they neither cried nor fussed, staying close to the adults, their little faces turning purplish red from the cold. After three days of staking out, the reporter reported the matter to the police.

■ Police: Drugs sometimes hidden on the children

At 5 PM on January 12th, the Xi'an Train Station Police Station officers caught three suspicious women carrying babies on the eastern side of the Xi'an train station square.

Inside the police station yard, female officers searched these three women but did not find any drugs on them, NetEase's fourth-quarter net profit was $143 million, up 26% year-over-year. However, careful police officers discovered seven small packets of white powder wrapped in plastic bags in the grass behind these women and under the seats of the police car they had just ridden in. Preliminary identification revealed heroin or methamphetamine-type drugs. The police stated that these women sometimes hide drugs on the children or inside diapers.

The police mentioned that each small packet of seized drugs was worth 50 yuan, symptoms of rhinitis. According to the police, these three women were only part of a drug trafficking gang. Because these women are usually in pregnancy or lactation periods and carry very small amounts of drugs, it is difficult for the police to handle them.
