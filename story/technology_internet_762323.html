Who says we're dull and lacking in sex appeal? Seven reasons to choose big companies

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 14:55:32

　　English Original: 7 Reasons Why You Need To Work For A Big Company

　　This article is written by Mark Stephen, the founder and CEO of IDR Solutions.

　　Large companies are often seen as slow and lacking in excitement, while startups are viewed as flexible, efficient, and full of fun. I am currently running a startup, but to be honest, I am glad that I once spent some memorable time working for a large company. Below are the reasons why I think big companies have their own merits:

　　1. Witnessing Excellence and Absurdity

　　The good and bad coexist, the beautiful and ugly intertwine. You will witness extraordinary insights about source code control and also experience empty talk that leads to disengagement. While large companies may not be as dynamic, they still have their reasons for growing into giants. Even in the most ordinary institutions, there are bound to be talented individuals. Discovering the logic behind large companies and identifying employees who excel in both technical expertise and political acumen can be a great asset for you.

　　2. Working Alongside Talented Individuals

　　In some departments of large companies, you will find clusters of smart people. In my view, working with a group of talented individuals is an incredibly wonderful experience, as if intelligence permeates the air around you. These intelligent people choose large companies mainly due to considerations of family and responsibility, unwilling to take too many risks by joining startups. Experienced core employees can teach you a lot, whereas colleagues in startups are often around your age and less experienced.

　　3. Leaning on a Strong Tree for Shade

　　Twelve years ago, I worked for a large company. To this day, I still maintain contact with my former colleagues and frequently run into them in various places, which is a very precious bond. In today's era of social media, it remains crucial for people to know who you are and what you can do. Your employment history at a large company is documented, and when you need to conduct business cooperation with large companies, this background will provide you with a green channel.

　　4. Enjoying Quality Benefits

　　I miss the cafeteria and gym of the large company, and that summer in Amsterdam... The Oracle training courses funded by the company, the fully reimbursed MBA while working, just thinking about it feels great! However, in a startup, getting your book purchases reimbursed would already be considered generous. Would you rather use someone else’s money to gain knowledge while working at a large company or work 100 hours a week at a startup to chase a distant future?

　　5. Learning Political Skills

　　At large companies, you learn not only technical skills but also "diplomatic" and "political" skills. When one day you transition from being an employee to a partner of a large company, these skills will come in handy. Large companies like to buy things and spend money generously, understanding their operational rules can help you better conduct business and uncover more potential customers through them.

　　6. Slower Pace, Better Time

　　Startups have a fast-paced rhythm akin to meteors racing against the moon, while large companies often move at a slower pace. Although it lacks some of the thrill and excitement, it provides more leisure time for learning, recharging, and self-reflection. Sometimes, this "slower pace" can lead to unexpected positive effects. I won’t tell you how a major media company avoided spending millions on a brainless web project simply because the decision-making was too slow, allowing the dot-com bubble to burst first…

　　7. Sticking to Principles for Dreams

　　When you first enter the workforce, you are full of confidence and fearless. After experiencing the ups and downs of working at a large company, you will understand what is feasible and what is not, realize that some things are crucial while others are trivial, and clarify what dreams you pursue throughout your life and what principles you cannot compromise. Defining your professional creed can help you perform better.

　　These are the reasons why I like working for large companies. What are your reasons?
