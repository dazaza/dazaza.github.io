Weibo invites the wolf into the house, will Sina give it all to Ali?

by anonymous on 2013-10-02 17:24:30

By Wang Liyang

In the past few days, rumors have been circulating on Weibo about Aitao and Weitao merging. After seeing this, I was left cold-sweating. Is Alibaba trying to destroy Sina Weibo? First, it invested in Sina Weibo to secure the most important social commerce entry point. Then, it vigorously developed its own social commerce products, Weitao and Aitao. Finally, it merged the mobile Weitao with the PC-based Aitao, creating another major social commerce product that rivals Sina Weibo. Since Alibaba is an investor in Sina Weibo, Sina Weibo can only watch as Alibaba gradually cuts into its market without being able to fight back. In the end, including Weibo, all of Sina could be swallowed up by Alibaba.

First, invest in Sina Weibo to stabilize the social commerce entry point

It's common knowledge that Alibaba has never given up on SNS, especially under the trend of social commerce. They are determined to carve out a path in the SNS field. The rapid development of WeChat in the mobile SNS sector has made Alibaba feel very uneasy. Therefore, Alibaba chose to invest in Sina Weibo, leveraging Weibo to stabilize its position in the social commerce market, leaving room and energy to develop its own SNS products.

The traffic-driving effect of Sina Weibo on Taobao is very direct, especially after the launch of the "Weibo Taobao Edition" jointly released by Sina and Taobao. This significantly improved the traffic-driving effect. Previously, media reports stated: "The interaction volume of product information posted by test sellers is six times that of sellers not using the Weibo Taobao Edition; sellers using the Weibo Taobao Edition saw a fivefold increase in traffic driven by Weibo."

Nowadays, compared to WeChat's traffic-driving ability on mobile devices, Weibo's traffic-driving effect on PCs is more noticeable. Alibaba's $586 million for 18% equity in Sina Weibo was a very good deal, already securing the largest social commerce entry point. But Alibaba's ambition doesn't stop there.

Then strongly promote Weitao, try Aitao, merge and compete with Sina Weibo

In May this year, Taobao Wireless launched Weitao. After a round of media exposure during the initial launch, it entered a silent internal testing phase. Later, taking advantage of the release of Taobao posts on Weibo, it was heavily promoted again, bringing Weitao fully into public view. Since Weitao is an auxiliary tool for Taobao Wireless, it has similar social media marketing functions to Sina Weibo and is not a pure mobile IM application, so it does not directly compete with WeChat. Therefore, the industry is quite optimistic about Weitao's growth space and development potential.

On the PC side, Sina Weibo drives traffic to Taobao from the outside, while on the mobile side, Weitao drives traffic internally to Mobile Taobao. These two products previously had little overlap and coexisted peacefully. However, when Taobao launched its own PC-based SNS product Aitao in August, Sina Weibo's nightmare began. When rumors emerged today that Aitao will merge with Weitao, the nightmare turned into bad news.

Taobao's e-commerce resources are not suitable for pure SNS products, and previous efforts in pure SNS products have all failed. The conclusion drawn is that Taobao is better suited to create a social media tool similar to Sina Weibo. The direction of Weitao's development is also moving towards social media. Previously, Sina Weibo was responsible for driving traffic to the PC-based Taobao, which did not lead to conflicts due to the quiet development of Weitao. However, now that Taobao's own PC-based SNS product Aitao has appeared in the form of social media and is merging with Weitao, Sina Weibo cannot ignore this anymore.

Since Weitao and Aitao are closer to Sina Weibo, all conducting e-commerce marketing in the form of social media, Taobao SNS's direct competitor is not Tencent's WeChat or QQ, but Sina Weibo. Sina Weibo accepted Alibaba's investment because of its trillion-dollar e-commerce transaction scale, hoping to increase advertising revenue through Alibaba. Now, after Alibaba invested in Sina Weibo, it is vigorously developing e-commerce advertising business that directly competes with Sina Weibo, which Sina Weibo should not have accepted. Alibaba is completely trying to kill Sina Weibo.

Finally, completely consume Sina Weibo, buy out all of Sina for $586 million

Sina Weibo, as the leading social media site, is an irreplaceable presence in the internet industry. Even if Aitao and Weitao merge, they cannot fundamentally replace Sina Weibo. However, what Aitao and Weitao can do is to compete with Sina Weibo for the social commerce advertising market. Sina Weibo's monetization capability has always been questioned. It wasn't until this year, after accepting Alibaba's investment, that it was considered to have found a larger advertising market. However, before Sina Weibo could gain substantial e-commerce advertising revenue from Alibaba, it is facing competition and diversion from Taobao's Weitao and Aitao. This will inevitably lead to a significant decrease in Sina Weibo's e-commerce advertising revenue.

Alibaba's $586 million investment for 18% equity has tied Sina Weibo's hands, making it unable to resist. Facing Alibaba's SNS development rhythm of first Weitao and then Aitao, Sina Weibo is indeed unable to respond. It cannot create conflicts with Alibaba and cut off cooperation, nor can it prevent Alibaba from developing Weitao and Aitao. It can only watch helplessly as Alibaba slowly cuts into its flesh. When Sina Weibo loses its most promising e-commerce advertising revenue business, what economic value does Sina Weibo have left?

When Sina Weibo's monetization ability is restricted, its corresponding market value will also become lower and lower. At that time, Alibaba can increase its holdings in Sina Weibo at a low point, eventually achieving full control. Sina's most valuable business is Sina Weibo. Once Sina Weibo belongs to Alibaba, Sina itself will not be worth much. In the end, Alibaba can swallow up Sina altogether. Recently, there were rumors that Alibaba was considering investing in Sina Video. It seems that Alibaba is methodically eating away at all of Sina.

Conclusion

Sina invited the wolf into its home, putting itself in a dangerous situation for just $586 million. However, even if Alibaba hadn't invested in Sina Weibo, it would still have launched Weitao and Aitao. This is a natural development trajectory and not a trap specifically designed for Sina Weibo. It's just that Sina didn't see Alibaba's intentions clearly at the time, leading to today's embarrassing situation where it can neither fight back nor retaliate. Perhaps being fully incorporated into the Alibaba Group wouldn't be such a bad outcome for Sina.
