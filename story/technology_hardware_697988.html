China's furniture market is gradually segmenting, and the competition among children's furniture brands is intense.

by seo17519 on 2012-03-08 22:40:27

The development trend of children's furniture over the past five years has been generally upward. A few years ago, out of 100 companies, only a few were making children's furniture. Now, more than ten out of 100 companies are producing children's furniture because many companies have seen the business opportunity and the potential for the development of children's furniture, and have joined this industry which is moving from its initial stage to a more advanced one. There are more than 300 million children under the age of 16 in our country, accounting for about one quarter of the national population. The degree of emphasis placed on children by families in our country also affects the development of this industry. With the rapid development of our country's economy, people's quality of life has started to improve significantly. In many families, parents' investment in setting up children's rooms for their children is also increasing, making children's furniture popular in the market. Currently, furniture products such as DuoXihuan (多喜爱), YiKas (益卡斯), and Seven Color Life (七彩人生) have become well-known brands in the market. Some traditional furniture companies have also keenly discovered the enormous business opportunities contained within the children's furniture market, and brands such as QuMei (曲美) and HongPingGuo (红苹果) have also gradually started testing the waters with children's furniture products, leading to an increasing number of children's furniture brands, and fierce competition in the market.

Merchants all play the environmental protection card. After visiting several furniture malls, I found that almost all furniture markets have dedicated counters for children's furniture, with some brands having as many as ten or more varieties. These children's furniture items vary in material and price. However, they all unanimously claim to be "natural and environmentally friendly". From the materials perspective, there are those claiming to use imported Nordic pine wood, Russian pine wood, Brazilian pine wood, and Northeast China pine wood. From the price perspective, there are both high-end imported goods priced over ten thousand yuan, as well as "special offer children's beds" available for just over a thousand yuan, with prices differing by as much as tenfold.

In the showroom, salespeople from the YiKas youth mattress brand introduced me to and showed me the environmental certificate issued by the International Ecological Nature Foundation: "Our products are all solid wood bed bodies, using North American Canadian imported pine wood as internal material, and nine-layer ashwood solid wood multi-layer boards as panels, with zero formaldehyde paint."

I Love My Home (我爱我家) and Seven Color Life (七彩人生) also showed me various quality inspection reports. Salespeople from Seven Color Life stated that all children's furniture uses E1 grade panels according to national standards, and the surface paint used is also environmentally friendly PU paint, "Even our hardware accessories are from well-known Italian brands."

Unreasonable industrial structure and narrow coverage

In multiple children's furniture brand stores, I saw that the most common type was furniture suitable for children aged 7 to 14. Although most merchants claimed that their furniture could meet the needs of children aged 0 to 18, there were fewer options for furniture suitable for infants aged 0 to 1 year, toddler furniture for children aged 1 to 3 years, and youth furniture for children aged 4 to 6 years and 16 to 20 years, resulting in a limited consumer choice range. This formed a jujube kernel-shaped structure in the children's furniture market, large in the middle and small at both ends.

Mr. Zhong, the relevant person in charge of Spain's YiKas professional youth children's growth mattress, stated that the main reason for the imbalance in the structure of children's furniture lies in unclear product classification and lack of in-depth research on market demand. From the situation of some furniture exhibitions in recent years, it can be seen that age segmentation of children's furniture is an inevitable trend in the development of the industry. Some manufacturers have already begun to differentiate between infant furniture, large child furniture, and youth furniture, indicating that the children's furniture market is moving towards maturity. Currently, the children's mattresses on the market are simply smaller versions of adult mattresses, completely ignoring the characteristics of spinal growth and development in children. At different stages of development, the length and weight of the spine change, and one type of support cannot meet these needs. The market inevitably requires specialized youth children's mattresses that conform to the developmental characteristics of children.

Variable levels of product design

In recent years, the popularity of children's furniture has attracted many furniture manufacturers to follow suit and produce, but most manufacturers have not regarded children's furniture as an independent research and development field, merely viewing children's furniture as a scaled-down version of ordinary furniture, distinguishing it solely through cartoon patterns or bright colors, leading to insufficient depth in the research and development of children's furniture and low design standards.

During my visits, I found that most products remain similar, with no obvious differentiation. Common features include slight differences in appearance, but single internal functions of the furniture. Additionally, the colors are overly bright, not conforming to the scientific application principles of color, focusing only on the visual impact of color while ignoring the hazards of color, especially the adverse effects on children's vision, nerve development, and emotions. Insufficient safety considerations in the design, focusing on shape while neglecting safety and convenience, such as ladder design and the rounding of board edges, have not been fundamentally resolved or paid attention to. Insufficient scientific consideration, especially in terms of scale and operational convenience, has not received detailed consideration.

Furthermore, there are currently no full-time children's furniture designers domestically, most transitioning from adult furniture design to children's furniture design. Therefore, lacking experience, it is not surprising that designs are similar. Growth requires time, even more so through trials and failures. I believe that talented children's furniture designers will emerge after a few years.

National standards for children's furniture are about to be issued

In recent years, the pass rate of children's furniture inspections has not been high, mainly due to the majority of manufacturers focusing on product styles and colors, trying only to attract children's attention in the first instance, and not paying enough attention to quality issues, especially product safety performance. Moreover, the children's furniture industry does not have its own independent standards, instead adopting adult standards, further exacerbating the insecurity of children's furniture.

The newly formulated General Technical Requirements for Children's Furniture standard is expected to be approved and released within the year. This standard is the first national mandatory standard for children's furniture in the country. It clearly specifies that children's furniture applies to children aged 3-14, including nine parts such as limits on toxic and harmful substances and the use of safety warning signs. Besides detailed regulations on sharp corners, protruding parts, hole sizes, gap sizes, and stability performance of children's furniture, the standard also strictly restricts toxic and harmful substances. The limits on toxic and harmful substances mainly target formaldehyde, soluble heavy metals, etc., covering ten types, with requirements similar to those for children's toys.
