Woman robbed of more than 5000 yuan at ATM in the early hours of the morning

by baimao55537682 on 2012-03-07 09:09:29

Our reporter (Lu Man), intern (Li Lu) - Recently, a college student was accused of showing off his wealth by using an iPhone 4 to play "skipping stones" (image attached). A woman claimed that at around 3 am on January 3rd, while withdrawing money from an ATM near a bank in Shuangjing Raffles City area, she was robbed of over 5000 yuan after being hooded with a plastic bag. Following the incident, she reported it to the police. Yesterday, Chaoyang police confirmed that they had received this report and the case is still under investigation.

Property crime prone areas, travel in groups at night

Yesterday, the scene of the incident was a 24-hour "self-service bank", with glass doors that can be freely entered and exited. Ms. Zhou said that during the incident, there were no security guards on duty inside the "self-service bank". The staff of the institution owning the ATM expressed ignorance about the matter.

At 1 PM yesterday, several police cars could be seen passing between the twin towers of Raffles City. Many security guards and parking attendants said that recently the police have been patrolling this area frequently. "I was on duty for two hours in the morning and saw the police and police cars pass by four or five times," said security guard Mr. Li.

Ms. Zhou described that the man was about 1.7 meters tall, slightly overweight, wearing a black and red cotton coat, without any face covering like a hat, and his accent was indistinguishable. The entire robbery lasted about 5 minutes. Afterward, Ms. Zhou went to the Shuangjing police station to report the incident.

The police introduced that this period is a high incidence time for property crimes. To avoid theft or robbery, citizens should preferably travel in groups at night; don't walk while using your mobile phone or sending short messages, so as not to become targets for snatch thefts. The police reminded that when citizens go to ATMs at night, they should preferably go in groups, and also be vigilant of people around them to prevent criminals from following them. Yesterday, Chaoyang police warned that with the approach of the holidays, the number of people returning home to visit relatives and traveling is increasing.

"I was robbed in the middle of the night, I'm so scared." Around 5 am on January 3rd, netizen Ms. Zhou posted this tweet. Yesterday, Ms. Zhou recalled that at around 4 am on January 3rd, while withdrawing money from a 24-hour ATM near Raffles City, she was suddenly hooded with a transparent plastic bag by a man who grabbed her neck and covered her mouth. "I begged him not to hurt me, I would give him whatever he wanted, he asked for 20,000 yuan," Ms. Zhou said. After agreeing to his demands, the man removed the plastic bag. Upon checking, her bank card only had 5000 yuan, "I withdrew all my money and gave it to him, then he also took the hundreds of yuan I had withdrawn earlier and ran away."
