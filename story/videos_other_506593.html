Key Point Two of Foreign Trade: Detailed Explanation of the Tax Rebate System

by fubu88482 on 2011-10-12 10:09:35

Tax rebates are an important concept in foreign trade and currently one of the main sources of profit in foreign trade operations. For ease of administration, the state assumes that all products are for domestic circulation and consumption; therefore, VAT is generally levied at a rate ranging from 6% to 17% of the selling price. Under normal circumstances, the domestic purchase price or pre-export price includes taxes, meaning it is the price after VAT has been paid. However, if the product is for export, this tax should not have been levied, and the portion already collected can be partially or fully refunded to exporters according to procedures.

For example, if you purchase a batch of color TVs from a domestic factory at a tax-inclusive price of 1170 yuan, where 1000 yuan is the net price and 170 yuan is the paid VAT. According to national regulations, the export tax rebate rate for color TVs is 17%, which means that after exporting the TVs, the tax bureau will refund 170 yuan to the exporter. In this way, even if the exporter exports at cost price, i.e., 1170 yuan, they can still receive a rebate of 170 yuan as profit income. In such a case, if the exporter considers competition and uses part of the 170 yuan to subsidize a price reduction, selling below the purchase price of 1170 yuan would still yield a profit.

Foreign trade transactions usually involve high values, and the corresponding tax rebate amounts are also substantial. Of course, the state strictly manages tax rebates, closely integrating them with foreign exchange management. Before export, an "Export Tax Rebate Verification Form" must be obtained from the Foreign Exchange Administration Department, declaring the total export amount. The verification form must also be stamped by customs to confirm that the goods have indeed been exported. After receiving payment from overseas buyers, with bank receipts, together with the verification form, go to the Foreign Exchange Administration Department to complete the verification process, then proceed to the tax bureau with the VAT invoice to handle the tax rebate matters and collect the rebate funds.
