Mr. Wang Shu was specially interviewed by the Beauty & Hairdressing magazine on the event of hair salon street solicitation:

by moulinty2 on 2010-05-31 10:49:12

In the January 9th edition of the Beijing News, there was a report titled "Xidan Cracks Down on Street Solicitation and Forced Consumption". The specific content is as follows: Four departments have initiated a special rectification campaign in Xidan to address street solicitation and forced consumption. Individuals found repeatedly disturbing public order through street solicitation will be detained.

Scenes often occur on the streets of Xidan where strangers aggressively persuade pedestrians to receive so-called free beauty and hair services. This phenomenon of street solicitation leading to forced consumption is about to face severe measures. Yesterday, the Xidan Regional Management Committee, together with the Xicheng Industry and Commerce Bureau, Public Security Bureau, and Urban Management Department, launched a special operation. Individuals frequently found soliciting customers on the street and whose actions are serious will be penalized or detained.

Currently, there are over 40 beauty and hair salons in the Xidan area. Some idle individuals often solicit customers on pedestrian walkways, subway passages, and in front of mall entrances, luring consumers into stores with false promises of free trials and even resorting to forced charges.

Deputy Director Zhang Ying of the West Chang'an Street Industry and Commerce Office pointed out, "Due to the large number of street solicitors, fierce competition has arisen, leading to aggressive and deceptive practices that pose significant risks to public security." In just the last quarter of last year, the industry and commerce department received 26 related complaints from the Xidan area, with one instance resulting in a refund of 50,000 yuan to a consumer.

According to reports, during this special operation targeting street solicitation and forced consumption as well as consumer fraud, the Xidan regional police station will quickly investigate and gather evidence against those disturbing public order. Repeat offenders and those with serious offenses will be penalized or detained. Meanwhile, urban management will crack down on the distribution of beauty and hair salon advertisements on the street.

Upon receiving clues from these two departments, the West Chang'an Street Industry and Commerce Office will issue administrative recommendations to beauty enterprises and publicly list those frequently complained about. "Once confirmed cases of illegal solicitation and false advertising are found, we will impose penalties, require business suspension for rectification, up to and including revocation of business licenses."

A security officer at Xidan Mall said that there are many people soliciting customers for beauty and hair services. He indicated that yesterday's rectification efforts indeed reduced such occurrences. However, at around 7 PM last night, reporters noticed that near the south side alley of Hua Wei Building, some beauty and hair salon personnel began to solicit customers again.

Based on the above information, here are our specific questions:

1. What are your thoughts after reading this news?

2. How do you view the phenomenon of street solicitation?

3. What do you think is the root cause of this phenomenon?

4. How do you evaluate the current rectification measures?

5. Do you have any good suggestions for promoting healthy competition in the hairdressing industry?

6. What practical actions would you take?

Thank you for supporting our work. Please answer my questions as soon as possible and kindly send me your photo and a brief resume. Finally, wishing you good health and smooth work!

Editor Wang Fang of "Beauty Hairdresser" asked: What are your thoughts after reading this news? Actually, the phenomenon of hair salons soliciting customers on the street is not new. Several years ago, some individual beauty shops, tattoo parlors, or insurance companies were already intercepting passersby under the pretext of free trials or market surveys, either forcibly promoting their products and services or collecting personal information. Personally, I believe this behavior can be considered an act of fraud or forced consumption, constituting unfair competition. It disrupts social order and people's normal lives and severely tarnishes the image of our city. In today's increasingly complete legal system, such behavior will inevitably be punished accordingly. This time, the municipal government's decisive rectification action to thoroughly clean up the situation is indeed a wise move.

How do you view the phenomenon of street solicitation? Street solicitation is actually a very crude marketing tactic. Not only does it make customers feel uncomfortable, but it also damages the overall image of our entire industry in the eyes of consumers. Hairdressing, as a comprehensive industry centered on technology, service, and design, should provide customers with professional technical skills, comfortable consumption environments, considerate services, and incorporate the most fashionable global trends into our creations, giving each customer extraordinary experiences. However, some operators fail to focus on improving employee competence, enhancing service awareness, or mastering professional techniques. Instead, they resort to opportunism and violate market rules by taking advantage of their prime commercial locations and high foot traffic to forcibly solicit and exploit customers. By doing so, they not only harm consumers but also create serious obstacles for their own future development. This侥幸attitude focused solely on short-term benefits is truly regrettable.

What do you think is the root cause of this phenomenon? In fact, everyone knows that our industry, with its unique characteristics, differs from other technical industries. For example, workers in medical, repair, and manufacturing fields must study specialized theoretical knowledge and gain long-term practical experience before becoming independent professionals. However, most of our workers switch careers midway and start working after short-term technical training. Their family backgrounds, growth environments, educational levels, overall qualities, and personal understandings of aesthetics vary greatly. To become an excellent hairdresser, all these factors play a crucial role, which is precisely the difference between artisans and artists. Therefore, I believe that to change the overall status quo of the industry, we must start from the root; strengthen cultural reviews of newcomers, set educational requirements, raise industry thresholds, and improve the education system. However, some educational management institutions emphasize military-style management for employees, encourage external customer acquisition, and use lottery incentives to stimulate consumer spending. Such methods completely contradict the fundamental value of the hairdressing industry. This almost brainwashing educational concept and misplaced operating strategy indicate that if an industry wants to develop, it first needs a healthy model.

What do you think of this rectification measure? The upcoming Beijing Olympics will be a window for the world to understand China's traditional and contemporary cultures. Beijing will become an irreplaceable stage for the world to recognize China and Chinese civilization. Therefore, I strongly support the Beijing government's special rectification action this time. I hope that through this special rectification, we can resolutely eliminate such unlawful behaviors as street solicitation. Purify our cities and fully protect the interests of consumers, welcoming the Olympics with a harmonious Beijing.

Do you have any good suggestions for standardizing healthy competition in the hairdressing industry? First, we need to establish unified and practical industry norms and implement them strictly. We should pay attention to the cultural level of practitioners. Since the country popularizes nine-year compulsory education, technical training schools should deny admission qualifications to those without junior high school graduation certificates. Industry associations should strictly review the teaching staff of various training schools and provide unified and practical training education for newcomers. Training schools must ensure strict graduation standards and guarantee the delivery of high-quality graduates to employers. At the same time, hairdressing institutions should avoid hiring scattered interns from society and uniformly recruit employees from professional training schools, ensuring they work with certification. There should also be mutual supervision within the industry, and regulatory agencies must enforce laws strictly and increase penalties for rule violations. This can improve the overall quality of our practitioners and lay a solid foundation for their further studies.

The hairdressing industry involves multiple disciplines such as art, color, geometry, architecture, combined with good verbal communication skills and full comprehension abilities, plus rich personal imagination, to highlight inspiration and unlimited creativity. However, the hairdressing industry differs from general artistic industries due to its special nature. While perfectly completing a work, we must also provide the best service to our customers. Therefore, I believe that competition within the same industry is necessary. When aspects like price, technology, product, environment, and service are at the same level, what we compare is who can give customers more value-added enjoyment and a better mood. This not only allows consumers to benefit meaningfully but also broadens merchants' thinking, making the entire hairdressing market more lively and vibrant. It also enhances the social status of our practitioners fundamentally. But this competition must be based on healthy grounds; otherwise, it may backfire.

What kind of practical actions will you take? I currently serve as the Technical Education Director of GuBei Hair Art in Beijing and am also part of L'Oreal's China ID Technology Team. Managing technical education for our company's employees, I am responsible for assisting L'Oreal in promoting seasonal hairstyle trends across different regions in China. Starting today, I will dedicate myself more sincerely and professionally to educating young hairdressers in China, contributing my modest efforts to the development of our country's hairdressing industry and striving for early international alignment.

Wang Su
