How Samsung Redefines the New Model of App Stores from the Perspective of Samsung Hub

by anonymous on 2013-09-04 14:10:39

Samsung Hub by Samsung aims to be a one-stop content shop where you can consume any content of interest. We can understand it as the Samsung version of iTunes Store.

Samsung Hub is translated into Chinese as "Samsung Service Hub" (hereafter referred to by its Chinese name), which includes five categories: music, video, games, reading, and learning. Corresponding content can be downloaded under each category. However, the available categories vary in different countries and regions. For instance, on the Galaxy S4 domestically, only reading, games, and learning are visible.

The Samsung Service Hub generally adopts a magazine-style layout with large images on the homepage and featured recommendations, looking very elegant. After entering different categories, each category is divided into two sections. For example, upon entering the reading category, it is then divided into bookstore and my books, and you can freely switch by swiping your finger left or right on the screen. The game and learning categories have similar designs.

The reading category has a wide range of content, currently mainly offering books. It includes literature, novels, business/economy, biographies, history, romance, etc. The bookstore's homepage is divided into large image recommendations, bestsellers, recently published, staff picks, and popular free sections, meeting diverse reading needs for different users.

Swipe left on the screen to enter my books, which displays all books purchased within the Samsung Service Hub. If you have bought many books, it also supports a search function. The design of the reading interface is like a paper book, with a page-turning effect when flipping pages. You can customize text size, foreground and background colors, add bookmarks, etc.

The Game Hub and Reading Hub have similar design styles, but the homepage is divided into premium games, social games, independent games, and staff picks.

Each game here is actually an independent app, meaning that after selecting a game, you need to download it, and ultimately, it will redirect you to the Samsung App Store (Samsung Apps) for downloading. When playing, what opens is actually an independent application. However, such games offer a better experience.

A characteristic of Samsung is to benchmark against top companies in the industry. Apple's iTunes Store has already brought a significant portion of revenue to Apple, and Samsung may also hope that the Samsung Service Hub can become something akin to the iTunes Store.

Going a step further than the mere applications offered by iTunes Store, Samsung Hub is Samsung's digital entertainment store. It is not just a simple app store but a content platform that includes music, videos, games, reading, and learning, making its resources more abundant than those of iTunes Store. This is likely to be a major trend in future mobile distribution—distribution of not just apps but also other mobile resources.

We can say that Samsung Hub redefines the new model of software and content stores in terms of design philosophy. Additionally, due to the well-known Android fragmentation issue, the apps and content on Samsung Hub go through unified adaptation via Samsung Apps, ensuring they run smoothly on Samsung terminal devices. Users don't need to worry about any software or hardware compatibility issues, bringing unprecedented user experience improvements.
