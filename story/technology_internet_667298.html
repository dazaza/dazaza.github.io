Dance Drama Works:

Starring in the large-scale dance drama "Mèi", showcasing the beauty of Eastern women. The plot transitions through four eras: "Tang Dynasty", "Modern", "Qing Dynasty", "Future", giving the audience a perspective on the unique beauty of Chinese women across different times through traditional Chinese songs and dances and costume changes. It doesn't overly pursue historical accuracy but rather expresses these concepts through artistic forms.

Dance Theory Works:

The paper "Origins and Development of Classical Dance" was published in the 1997 Issue 3-4 of the Beijing Dance Academy Journal.

Film and Television Performances:

In the TV series "The Kangxi Dynasty", she played Zi Yun, a famous prostitute who inadvertently meets Emperor Kangxi and earns his deep affection. She ultimately faces a dilemma between loyalty and filial piety and is forced to take poison to save Kangxi's life.

In the TV series "Silver Rat", she played Mrs. Qiu, gentle and virtuous, deeply understanding of righteousness, she gets involved in palace killings while supporting her husband's anti-corruption efforts.

In the TV series "Yangtze River Police", she portrayed Shen Jingjing, a public relations manager skilled in manipulation for personal gain and emotional needs, eventually fleeing alone to France.

In the TV series "Antique Prince", she performed as Na Ren Shi, kind and adorable, talented in singing and dancing, she was the favorite concubine of the grand eunuch. Upon discovering a conspiracy, she voluntarily informed the royal family and met a tragic end.

In the movie "Dragon and Phoenix Fight at Panlong Workshop", she played Xiao Feng, a fallen aristocrat from the Beggar Gang, skilled in martial arts, hating evil, she ultimately sacrifices her family for justice and wanders the world.

Additionally, she participated in the filming of other TV dramas and films such as "Princess Huan Zhu 3" (as Zhi Qin), "The Legend of the Condor Heroes" (as Han Ji), "Dashilan" (as Liu Yao), "Li San Enters the City" (as Tang Laoshi), "Wind and Moon" (as Su Qin), "Seven Fairies in Joy" (as Chang'e), "Heroic Women of the Han Dynasty" (as a mysterious dancer).

Tong Jiali Participated in the Play "Moonlight"

As a dancer, Tong Jiali seems to prefer the stage of plays. On July 23, 2008, after participating in the play "Pretzel", she once again joined the cast of "Moonlight" and also crossed over to become a singer.

The play "Moonlight" tells the story of two men and one woman concerning art and love. In the play, Tong Jiali portrays a dancer. From the character perspective, it seems like she is playing herself. However, at the rehearsal site yesterday, the lead actress Tong Jiali introduced: "This is my third time performing in a play. In the play, apart from dancing, I will also sing a song for the first time accompanied by a live band. The theme song uses a popular style to sing classical music. Additionally, there are many performances involving entering and exiting the scene, and many symbolic physical performances. This is a big challenge for me."

The director of the play, Shang Wei, said, "Many plots in this play are tailor-made for Tong Jiali. Audiences can see various art forms in the play, including modern dance, rock music, classical music, art exhibitions, etc. This play explores more art forms. The stage surrounds the audience instead of the traditional setup where the audience surrounds the stage."

The play will be presented to the audience at the TNT Theater of Nine Theaters in Chaoyang District from July 23 to August 3, 2008.
