Joy in reunion, sorrow in parting

We've parted ways... it's something we have to go through...

I still remember when I was in kindergarten, it was such a happy time. Back then, the older sisters were in fifth or sixth grade, and we all lived together every day. We played house, pretended to be teachers and students, doctors and patients. Thinking about it now, it seems so naive. But it was so joyful! Now, the sisters have grown up and are no longer like they used to be. When we meet again, it's as if we're strangers - unsure what to say, unsure how to start a conversation, having to think carefully before speaking. We're no longer together, and with growing up, there's less innocence and more maturity. We can never return to those naive games of the past because we no longer play together. The distance has made our conversations less frequent. As we've grown up, we understand more, but we've lost that innocence. This is something we must experience, yet it feels like something is missing, and it's hard to let go...

I still remember when I started first grade. At that time, you couldn't call it foolishness, just innocence. We were so happy and excited, though we didn't know why. Perhaps it was because we could make new friends, or perhaps at that time we didn't understand the word "farewell." I still remember during registration, I asked a classmate for her name repeatedly. Her name was four characters long and hard to remember, so I kept asking her, and she patiently told me each time. How cute!

Now, things will never be the same. Among classmates, there's less trust and more suspicion. If you make one mistake, you might not notice, but your classmates will always remember it. In the past, I always thought classmates got along well. However, during the last semester, I realized how little trust there was between us. There was a barrier between everyone, thin but very strong, impossible to cross. Our thoughts have changed too, becoming impure. We often think, do, or say things we shouldn't. I used to think I would never insult others, but now I see that we can never return to that original, pure self. We've changed so much that we barely recognize each other anymore. It's so sad.

I still remember when I was graduating from sixth grade. By that time, it was already a critical period; studying further wouldn't have helped much. I would play some silly, even nauseating games with Watermelon that could annoy our desk partners. Before graduation, we talked about our hopes for the future. I always hoped to make new friends, and saying goodbye seemed so easy. But when the real farewell came, it was so painful. I think of Watermelon, Chicken Feather, Little Fatso, Ren Beauty, and you, my desk partner. I miss you so much. The last time we were together, I cried that night. It was the first time I truly understood the pain of parting, the heart-wrenching ache, and I regretted not having the courage to give you the gift I had prepared for you. That was my first regret.

We've only been apart for less than 20 days, yet when I see my classmates, we act as if we haven't seen each other. In less than 20 days, we've gone from seeing each other every day to being strangers. Why? Parting only makes us forget each other. Slowly, the happiness we once shared turns into a faint memory in our minds. In the future, if we meet on the street, we'll just pass by each other without a word.

The past we can't return to, the memories we can't hold onto...

Joy in reunion, sorrow in parting, pain in reminiscing, difficulty in separation, forgetting in growing up, strangeness in meeting again...
