"Divorce Midlife" - A twenty-year divorce battle between a married couple

by jqrxass on 2009-09-14 11:03:53

Using the lower half of the body to seek pleasure, and the upper half to find pain. This is the history of sexual desire over thirty years for two ordinary Chinese men, and also a factual record of the absurd divorce battles between two couples spanning more than a decade. It is another excellent novel after "Chinese Style Divorce" and "Ma Wen's War," which describes the divorce battle of ordinary middle-aged people.

Instant passion, permanent pain. No matter how legendary the marriage, no matter how beautiful the love, once it falls into the mire of life, it still leads to irreversible consequences.

A seemingly legendary marriage, yet lasting twenty years of conflict. What are the underlying reasons? A shrewd and capable businessman, yet always plagued by misfortune. Is there any inevitability?

This book uses the divorce battle of a couple and the close friendship between two men as a thread, unfolding a thirty-year history of the lives of urban underdogs. Startling marital life, unimaginable family relationships, inexplicably arduous struggles, intricate political games, all reveal the profound changes in urban lifestyles since the reform and opening up, the new connotations of social ethics, and the struggles and sense of disillusionment of ordinary people under new lifestyles and ethical relationships. The whole text is filled with fascinating anecdotes and wise sparks that elicit admiration.
