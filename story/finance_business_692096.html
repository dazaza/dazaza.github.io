Love each other but live apart at the ends of the earth

by ybnalal7688 on 2012-03-05 22:35:23

Walking alone on the path, looking at the familiar scenery, I am reminded of past memories and the former beauty. Now, where have they all gone? The cold wind howls, leaves fall in profusion. Is this a sorrowful desolation or a romantic beauty? Between the leaf and the tree, who has abandoned whom?

The wind cruelly assaults my pale face, my black hair flutters like a broken-winged butterfly in disarray. The scattered crowd reveals my sadness. Perhaps because my heart is fragile, I choose silence for many words.

Focusing on one passing scene after another, leaving behind heartbreak after heartbreak. A bitter figure echoes in this street corner, familiar yet strange antics, torn illusions, eroding the past. My thoughts are trapped in a black nightmare, unable to find an exit, fearfully accepting the venting of surrounding complaints. Perhaps the innermost part is the most barren desert, all surrounding illusions defeated by reality, such a fallen disappointment.

Gradually, I've become accustomed to a tearless facial expression, no matter how complex the mood, I pretend to be calm. Unretained silhouettes can only bring memories of the vicissitudes of life.

The entangled emotions are mixed with a few strands of faint sorrow, weaving a beautiful dream, carrying a thin and tranquil heart, stepping lightly, discarding the complicated worldly desires. Believing in vows for a whole year, fragile feelings come to an end, shredding all memories and tossing them into the abyss. The lights go out, love has walked away. Closing my eyes, not thinking, not looking, my frail body stands beside a precipice.

I try to feel the residual warmth of memories; your innocence, forever frozen, the twilight of your turning back. The pain in my heart, like the initial beauty, is sealed in the deepest heart's abyss. If everything is predestined, why should I be too attached? The past is just a fleeting glimpse of a night-blooming cereus, leaving only futility.

Confiding emotions? How many can understand? Pulling a indifferent smile, softly laughing, the air is filled with a chill. The falling leaves cover the ground in prosperity, people remember only how to grieve, mourning the lost years in vain sorrow, using slightly profound language to write their own deep melancholy.

Time interprets vicissitudes, rain and snow are all sadness. No one can understand what I am thinking. Just as now, I sit in front of the computer, writing down my full chest of sorrow, and no one would expect if I might happily change to a smiling face in the next moment, walking out the door, into that unfamiliar yet familiar drunken prosperity. It becomes increasingly desolate, tears unknowingly slip in some unnamed corner, leaving ripples in the heart. How to get rid of the sadness tangled in the heart. How to laugh and tell myself to be happy. How to make tears not look so weak.

On both sides of the road, neon flashes float, the lights, though once bright, have dimmed, obscured under a cloud of drunken opulence. Everything has already become so dim in a certain place on a certain day, month, and year.

The gray sky, dark background, bleak streetscape, gloomy heart, all fade away unknowingly. The time left behind is vast and desolate.

To forget is the sorrow of two people. Pain is the aftermath when promises turn into lies, heartbroken. When the splendor fades, will we still adhere to the vows we made before? Perhaps only the scars will remain, etched deeply in our hearts.

There is a trace of longing in the heart, some love must be parted far apart across the seas.
