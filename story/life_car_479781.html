Experts Recommend Ten Tricks to Save Money and Worry on Car Maintenance

Nowadays, buying a car is not a difficult task for many people. What truly burdens car owners are the large expenses that come with maintaining and repairing their vehicles after purchase. The industry has summarized money-saving tips for car maintenance and repair, which can help you avoid unnecessary costs. Not only does it save money, but it also saves worry, extending the life of your beloved vehicle and keeping it running strong.

1. Replace Necessary Filters and Liquids

Even the most mechanically clueless drivers know to regularly change their car's oil and oil filter. However, other liquids and filters also require regular maintenance. This is necessary because over time, they lose important properties—such as the ability to dissipate heat, lubricate, and resist rust and cold.

Replacing your air filter helps your car breathe easier and extends the life of your engine. To function, an engine needs a precise mix of fuel and air, and all air enters the engine system through the air filter. Its purpose is to prevent dirt and other external objects from entering and damaging the engine. If the air filter is clogged, your engine will not operate properly. This will also harm your car's fuel efficiency, as it may lead to incomplete fuel combustion.

2. Inspect the Cooling System

Ensuring that your car's cooling system works properly and has sufficient coolant can potentially save you thousands of dollars in maintenance. A failed cooling system can even cause your engine to melt.

3. Take Care of Your Tires

Tires are often the most neglected part of a car, yet they are among the least expensive to maintain. For instance, check the tire pressure. Most people do not pay much attention to keeping their tires at normal inflation pressure, which is not only bad for the car, tires, and fuel economy but also poses a safety issue. Maintaining proper tire pressure is a simple measure that remains highly effective and essentially costs nothing. Additionally, don't forget to rotate your tires, preferably every 5,000-8,000 kilometers, even if there are no obvious signs of wear. This will help ensure your car drives and brakes normally.

4. Pay Attention to the Dashboard

If there is an issue with your oil pressure, cooling system, or any other major system, the car's dashboard will inform you. Most people overlook this, which is why manufacturers use automatic warning lights to give clear prompts when problems arise. When the warning light comes on, find a technician as soon as possible to address the issue.

5. Find a Trusted Repair Shop and Technician

Find a repair shop and technician you trust, and try to ensure the same person services your car. If you go to different shops each time, every technician will need to gradually understand your car, conduct detailed inspections each time, identify potential issues, and then fix them for you. This way, it takes longer and you may end up paying more. Moreover, maintaining a good relationship with your technician will help you make wise decisions when problems arise, without having to doubt the authenticity of the advice.

6. Ensure Regular Inspections

The owner's manual has a maintenance schedule. Another benefit of having the same technician service your car is that he can ensure you adhere to this schedule and think of things not included in the manual. If you go to different shops each time for repairs or maintenance, the technicians there won’t know what happened to your car last time. As a result, you may end up paying for unnecessary repairs. If you consistently get your car serviced at one shop, since most shops keep electronic records, the technician knows what services to schedule when.

The periodic maintenance table in the owner's manual only tells part of the story. Staying in touch with a service advisor who knows your car and understands what kind of maintenance is needed when, is also a good idea.

7. Drive More Smartly

Your driving habits affect the lifespan of your car and your fuel consumption. By maintaining good driving habits, you can reduce wear and save fuel. Gentle driving, gradual acceleration, and pre-planned braking allow you to avoid abrupt stops. In cold weather, warm up your car first so the lubricating oil can flow freely throughout the system and fully lubricate internal parts. All these actions reduce wear on your car and may enhance fuel efficiency.

Additionally, minimize short trips. Trips less than ten minutes are very harmful to cars because the engine never gets a chance to really heat up, leading to condensation in the engine and exhaust system. When iron, oxygen, and water mix, rust forms, which is detrimental to the car. Condensation in the engine dilutes the lubricating oil, which is also harmful.

8. Reduce Weight

Extra weight creates additional demands on the car’s power and causes suspension and brake problems. Therefore, avoid driving with a bunch of unnecessary items in your car. Also, remove items that create extra air resistance, such as roof racks. These have the same effect as adding weight—they increase the demand on the engine, causing premature wear and reducing the car's fuel efficiency.

9. Keep the Vehicle Clean

Take care of the exterior of your car by washing and waxing it periodically, and don't neglect the interior cleanliness. This is often overlooked. Spend some time cleaning, and don’t just rely on air fresheners to adjust the smell inside the car. Use a vacuum cleaner to regularly clean the carpets. Clear out small debris promptly, as if you don’t, it will be harder to remove later. A car with a dirty interior not only makes driving uncomfortable but is also detrimental to vehicle maintenance.

10. Store the Vehicle in a Safe Place

Park your car in a garage or under a car cover. Keep your car away from direct sunlight, bird droppings, leaves, and areas where bells ring. There was once a Chongqing blogger whose engine hood had a speck of bird droppings, which he ignored. After some time, it cost him 800 yuan to clean it.
