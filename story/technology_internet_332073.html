In 2010, I had 16 major wishes for people's livelihood. Who can help me spread the word?

by alexsgxu on 2010-03-09 15:24:51

In 2010, I have 16 major livelihood wishes. Who can help me voice them?

By Xu Shuguang, Chairman of GreenTree Hotels and 519.com Wine Direct Sales Network

Preface: Wishes are always beautiful, but they are also relatively difficult to achieve. However, in our lifetime, having a wish is better than having none. Although I am not a member of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC) or a representative of the National People's Congress (NPC), just a foreign "grassroots" individual, taking advantage of the grand occasion of the two sessions being held, I want to share my 16 major livelihood wishes for 2010. I hope to leverage some forces to help me voice these livelihood wishes, which is also the responsibility that GreenTree Hotels should fulfill as a corporate citizen. At the same time, I believe that these wishes are what everyone is eagerly concerned about.

---Xu Shuguang, GreenTree Hotels

First Wish: The "snail house" lifestyle of ordinary people comes to an end. Housing prices should rationally return, no longer skyrocketing, truly achieving "housing for all." For a long time, whether in coastal areas or inland cities, housing prices have been continuously rising. To buy a house, to find a three-foot foothold in the city, many people have had to persistently strive for decades. Buying a house has undoubtedly become the biggest wish in the lives of most people. High housing prices also restrict the development of the local economy, significantly increasing the cost of local production and operation. Moreover, ordinary people cannot benefit from it, as was the case in Japan over the past 30 years, which serves as a very significant lesson, one we must not repeat. I wish that "housing for all" will no longer be relegated to a utopian dream, no longer becoming a self-mocking smile in our sleep.

Second Wish: Income distribution becomes more fair, reasonable, and transparent. According to surveys, 70% of people believe that the current social distribution is unfair. It is reassuring that our national leaders have recently clearly pointed out that work in this area needs to be done well. The "cake" of social wealth is large, but if it is not distributed well, such as excessive income disparity leading to severe wealth inequality, it will inevitably affect social fairness and justice, and also cause social unrest and instability, becoming an obstacle to sustained, stable, and rapid economic development. We should adjust the income distribution structure and reform the income distribution system through policy measures to gradually make income distribution fair and reasonable. This is the foundation of social harmony and stability, and also the long-standing aspiration of the people.

Third Wish: Education sheds the "official-centric" label and achieves "fairness." Our universities should return to the essence of education and not let the "official-centric" phenomenon distort our value orientation, thereby fostering restlessness and corruption, which seriously affect the development of higher education institutions. We should ensure that every child can afford schooling and receive quality education. However, phenomena such as school selection fees, borrowing fees, and unequal distribution of educational resources still exist, all of which become stumbling blocks on the path to education for poor children. Although tuition fees for compulsory education have been abolished, other arbitrary charges still persist. For low-income groups, being able to afford schooling and receive quality education remains a legend. At the same time, I hope that quality-oriented education can truly be implemented, allowing students to develop comprehensively in morality, intelligence, physical fitness, aesthetics, and labor.

Fourth Wish: "Medical care for all" and the problems of difficult and expensive medical treatment are fundamentally resolved. For low-income families, being able to eat enough food and wear warm clothing is already a happy thing. What they fear most is probably falling ill. Although various regions have already implemented medical insurance, which can cover part of the medical expenses, due to high drug prices and expensive treatment costs, we still feel an invisible fear towards illness.

Fifth Wish: Green environmental protection and low-carbon living continue to receive attention. In the past, factories that polluted the environment could make money, but now this way is no longer viable because it causes harm to everyone. We need to avoid repeating the Western model of "polluting first and then treating later." We only have one Earth, and it needs everyone's joint maintenance. Every few dozen seconds, someone dies of lung cancer, and the incidence of stomach cancer ranks first in the world. There is a close relationship between the environment and stomach cancer. We should quickly address environmental issues, advocate nationwide smoking cessation, and leave fewer potential risks and pressures for our future social security and medical insurance systems. In this regard, our GreenTree Hotels always advocates that guests do not smoke, drink less alcohol, do not litter, and lead healthy ecological lifestyles and work styles, without causing greater burdens to society. At the same time, our management and design consistently adhere to the green environmental protection and low-carbon road.

Sixth Wish: No more tears on the journey to employment. Employment is the foundation of people's livelihood and the source of social harmony. We should broaden employment channels and achieve low unemployment rates (zero is impossible, so don't expect it). Each year, millions of university graduates either consider the salary too low or lack suitable professional work opportunities, remaining unemployed for a long time. Tens of thousands of migrant workers wait anxiously in the labor market for job offers. They are habitually referred to as idle laborers. However, this issue requires universal education. We should despise those who have the ability but refuse to work, relying on social relief or parental support, unwilling to earnestly do something. If necessary, we should consider lowering the minimum wage in some industries to ensure that our labor force remains competitive globally, bringing lost jobs back to China. Our country's per capita output is still relatively poor, and we should recognize this fact, encouraging everyone to strive hard and improve the living standards of nine hundred million farmers. To shoulder the responsibility of a corporate citizen, this year our GreenTree Hotels plans to add ten thousand new employees, and we are willing to become a place for graduates to "train" and "grow."

Seventh Wish: "Returnees" no longer become "waiting returnees." In recent years, the phenomenon of "returnees" becoming "waiting returnees" has become increasingly serious, with multiple reasons. For example, after returning, they cannot integrate into Chinese society, or their learned specialties have no use, or they are overly eager for quick success, wanting to achieve everything at once. To change the "waiting returnee" phenomenon, first of all, as "returnees," one must look to the future, lower one's stance, start from zero, and work diligently. As for the government, I think policies can be broadened to create a good employment and entrepreneurship environment for our returned overseas students. Only by doing so can we retain talent and contribute to building our motherland.

Eighth Wish: Migrant workers' rights are better protected. The issue of migrant workers has always been our top priority, and we have always attached great importance to it. Although some achievements have been made, there are still many difficulties. Many enterprises, especially some construction companies, hire migrant workers when they are incapable, exploiting their weak legal awareness, failing to pay wages timely according to national laws, using delayed payments to undertake projects. These companies often fail in management and cost control, undertaking projects unethically and irresponsibly, resulting in serious triangular debts. Ultimately, they accumulate wealth by delaying payment of migrant workers' wages and inciting trouble to achieve illegal profits. I hope that the legal authorities can effectively revoke the operating qualifications of these companies, legally protecting the rights of migrant workers, while correctly guiding them to use legal means to protect their rights when infringed upon.

Ninth Wish: Our fiscal deficit does not become too "deficit!" Many non-renewable resources are converted into fiscal revenue, and some simply sell land continuously to offset fiscal deficits. If this continues, our deficit will only grow larger. The accumulation of these fiscal deficits is the most frightening. Year by year, it may seem to have little impact, accounting for only 2% of GDP, but cumulatively, it becomes very alarming. Borrowing is easy, but repaying is hard—this is a fact everyone knows. When non-renewable resources like land are almost all sold, population aging and social burdens will also become more severe. How will we bear the future pension insurance? How can we let future generations bear such heavy burdens? If fiscal deficits cannot be controlled, it may result in cuts in necessary livelihood-related expenditures, such as healthcare, education, and pensions. Ultimately, it will hurt our ordinary people, making it difficult for the social economy to develop healthily, stably, and sustainably.

Tenth Wish: The monopolistic status of monopolistic industries is broken. For example, the reason why our current housing prices are so "high fever" is largely due to the monopoly of land, thus facing criticism from many representatives and the public. In other industries, such as tourism, there are monopolists who gain huge returns through monopolistic behavior, which is extremely unethical. We encourage college student entrepreneurship, but if we don't give them a fair and reasonable environment, allowing these monopolistic industries to control industry development, the final outcome will be the destruction of the entire industry's healthy development. Although some monopolistic enterprises bring tax revenue to certain local economies, they are very harmful to the overall Chinese society and economy! We hope that the anti-monopoly law issued by the government can be effectively implemented. Breaking monopolies and creating an open, fair, just, and standardized market competition order is beneficial to economic development.

Eleventh Wish: Household registration no longer makes us bump into walls everywhere in the city; wherever we are, we can have the right to legal residence. At the same time, household registration is also an important way to solve the problem of excessively high urban housing prices. "Sun Zhigang" might have become a mark of an era, but it is still struggling to shake the deeply rooted household registration system. Nowadays, the household registration system is still the dividing line between urban residents and farmers in terms of identity and feelings, and "tragedies" continue to unfold.

Twelfth Wish: "Equal voting rights" for urban and rural residents. If this wish can be realized, it would be a milestone of historic turning point, representing a big step forward in equality and further progress in China's democracy. Of course, this does not mean that elections in the past were unequal; it only means that elections with equal rights are more equal than before.

Thirteenth Wish: Supporting the elderly is no longer just a legend. A monthly pension of 3 yuan may sound unfamiliar, but it is a real story that happened in our society, revealing a harsh reality. The task of the pension insurance system is arduous, and realizing "supporting the elderly" will require a long process. This requires the full efforts of all citizens and relies on everyone practicing frugality. Now, we have no money to balance the deficit, so how can we increase these benefits? Where will they come from? This relates to the comprehensive employment system I mentioned earlier, where basic salaries in some industries can be reduced, and as long as one has the ability, they should work, which may resolve these issues.

Fourteenth Wish: Officials remain clean, and corruption no longer hurts our fragile emotions. For many years, people have paid attention to the two sessions, focusing primarily on official corruption issues because it severely harms social fairness and the public's trust in the government. Corrupt officials are undoubtedly a major pest endangering the development of the nation and society. At the same time, I hope that the law enforcement actions of law enforcement departments can receive strong legal protection, and behaviors such as beating and insulting law enforcement officers should be severely punished by legislation.

Fifteenth Wish: No more innocent people shedding tears during demolitions. In the past year, violent demolition has been a hot topic of public opinion, and "tragedies" have occurred from time to time, challenging the emotional bottom line of the people. Chengdu Jinniu District female entrepreneur Tang Fuzhen resisted violent demolition by self-immolation, but ultimately could not stop its advance. However, a new demolition regulation began to take shape thereafter, and we look forward to it with great anticipation. At the same time, we hope that the public, regarding the issue of demolition, considers the overall situation and, under reasonable compensation, must not hinder the development of a community or the interests of the vast majority, even all, of the people. We hope that the demolition law to be introduced can balance the interests of all parties and achieve a win-win for the public and society.

Sixteenth Wish: Some seats of CPPCC representatives can be opened to overseas Chinese participation. This is also the common expectation of many overseas Chinese I have communicated with. Because looking at China from the outside compared to looking from within, there will certainly be some more rational and innovative proposals. Many overseas Chinese are also paying attention to the "two sessions," and quite a number of people are watching the TV news and reports of the "two sessions." For instance, as an American, I am also very concerned about China's development and hope to offer advice to our central government.

Wishes are always beautiful, but they are also relatively difficult to achieve. However, in our lifetime, having a wish is better than having none. Although I am not a member of the CPPCC or a representative of the NPC, just a foreign "grassroots" individual, taking advantage of the grand occasion of the two sessions being held, I want to share my 16 major livelihood wishes for 2010. I hope to leverage some forces to help me voice these livelihood wishes, which is also the responsibility that GreenTree Hotels should fulfill as a corporate citizen. At the same time, I believe that these wishes are what everyone is eagerly concerned about.

We must rationally view these issues from a balanced perspective. Some may take some time to become reality, but I think our leaders should act in the best interest of the whole, be selfless and fearless, dare to make big decisions, face challenges, educate and guide, setting a correct image and concept for society. Only in this way can the entire society develop healthily, stably, and sustainably.

Fortunately, this session of the two sessions has positively responded to the expectations of every ordinary citizen. According to a report by the People's Daily Online on March 8, the objective conditions for implementing the election of people's representatives based on the same population ratio in urban and rural areas are now available. From now on, residents of urban and rural areas have the hope of "equal voting rights," which is undoubtedly a spring breeze sent by the two sessions to ordinary citizens. The true democratic and equal elections we have long awaited may soon be realized. Each positive response made by the power department to ordinary people reveals their sincerity in addressing livelihood concerns, and therefore, I am confident in these 16 major livelihood wishes!
