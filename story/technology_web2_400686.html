Choosing the Right Online Shopping Platform for Your Store: Insights from CNNIC Report by Wang Yijian

In early December, CNNIC released its latest report, which many people used to study the changing landscape of China's online shopping market. However, it reminded me of another topic: if you are a store owner looking to open a shop, where should you choose to open your store?

Firstly, according to CNNIC's report, China's online shopping penetration rate is 26%. Many people think this is low. However, for sellers, a low penetration rate isn't necessarily a problem; what matters more is the absolute number - China has 87.88 million online shoppers, with an annual growth rate of 38.9%, which sounds very attractive.

Now let's get into the main topic:

Keyword 1: Competition

When doing business and opening a store, there is a basic principle: don't go where competition is too fierce, and definitely avoid places where there are no customers. This rule applies equally to online businesses, if not more so. The competition in online business mainly manifests in two aspects: price and credibility. Taobao is undoubtedly the most competitive place, and new sellers do not have an advantage in terms of credibility.

However, this does not mean that new sellers should abandon Taobao. The average traffic per individual shop on Taobao is still quite high. According to CNNIC's data, Taobao's penetration rate is 81.5%. I would recommend a strategy of opening shops on both Taobao and Youa, but gradually focusing more energy on Youa, with an eye on the future.

Key 2: Shopping Loyalty

In the vast sea of the internet, it is not easy to capture a returning customer, but once you do, the wealth will flow. CNNIC provides two data points reflecting this metric: primary users and user exclusivity.

Among the primary users, Taobao accounts for 76.5%, Paipai 6.1%, followed by Youa at 1.5%; while "user exclusivity" refers to users who only choose a specific platform, with Taobao at 79.3% and Youa at 55.4%.

Based on these indicators, Taobao comes first, followed by Youa, making them the preferred choices.

Key 3: Purchasing Power

According to CNNIC's data, Taobao and Paipai users tend to be younger, with users under 24 years old accounting for 66.9%; Yi趣 has more "older" users, with those aged 25-30 making up 40%, directly related to the brand's aging; Youa is relatively balanced, with those over 30 accounting for 34.2%.

From the comparison of these numbers, Youa users have higher purchasing power, belonging to the category of "wealthy" and "relatively insensitive to price."

Key 4: Game Theory Issue

The relationship between sellers and platforms is delicate, a win-win situation, but also involves game theory. Platforms seek their own revenue, which can somewhat deplete the resources of sellers. This is particularly evident in Taobao, which now functions as a media platform, with many resources being sold as materials.

This becomes a balancing act, taking into account the expectations and costs of opening a store to reach the most suitable conclusion. If you are financially well-endowed, consider Taobao more; if you prefer low-cost operations or more platform support, consider Youa. Youa has its own "Phoenix Cry Plan," targeting B2C merchants, with good policies and support currently available.
