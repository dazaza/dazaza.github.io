3. Are you sure that management work will bring you greater psychological satisfaction?

4. Do you have a comprehensive understanding of the situation of your company? Are you familiar with the different demands and management methods of various departments within your enterprise? Can you easily transfer from one department to another?

5. Have you established your goals for the next 5 to 10 years? Are you sure that your current work can help you achieve these goals? Are you aware of the more intense competition at the management level? Are you confident that you can fully face this competition?

6. Do you value people more than work? Do you prefer working with others? Can you easily find collaborators? Are you willing to help others? Do you truly understand why people behave the way they do in society?

7. Do your colleagues and friends think you are friendly and easy to follow? If you realize that helping others requires sacrificing personal interests, would you still insist on doing so? Do people seek your advice? Are you willing to accept help from others?

8. Can you handle situations flexibly under unpredictable circumstances? Can you remain calm when things get chaotic? Can you still be happy even if everything doesn't go as planned? Do you feel anxious about the results of your decisions before they are finalized?

9. Do you trust others and feel trusted by them? Can you easily break down barriers?

10. Do you focus on people and subjective factors in your work? Do you value utilizing others? Do you also value your superiors?

11. Do you pay attention to your own behavior and try to explain it? Do you sometimes hear your opinions sounding like someone else's ideas? Have you tried to see things from others' perspectives?

12. Do you believe you are good at interacting with all kinds of people and maximizing their potential when utilizing them?
