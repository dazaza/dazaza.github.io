US issues zombie alert guideline to guard against "zombie apocalypse"

by hdghu19056 on 2012-02-27 14:39:13

Beijing Delivery Network reported that a U.S. government agency posted an apocalypse written by a physician assistant on its website, titled "How to Prevent the Zombie Apocalypse," which has been wildly popular among netizens with no shortage of humorous "joining in the fun."

The report stated that the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) was not joking. It seriously pointed out that these suggestions were aimed at preparing Americans for the upcoming hurricane season. The agency declared online that many emergencies could be prepared for by the American public. "Take the 'Zombie Apocalypse' as an example; you may laugh now, but when something really happens, you will be glad you read the apocalypse."

On May 22nd, according to Hong Kong's Wen Wei Po website, recently, a U.S. government agency posted an apocalypse titled "How to Prevent the Zombie Apocalypse" written by a physician assistant on its website, which has been wildly popular among netizens, with no shortage of humorous "joining in the fun." Officials from the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) claimed that this was all to remind everyone to be prepared for emergencies.

What if one day, the zombie attacks on humans often depicted in films and TV shows were to actually happen in real life? Should we shoot them? The U.S. federal government does not think so. The U.S. CDC, headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia, launched the "Zombie Apocalypse" on its blog, issuing guidelines on how the public should respond to a zombie attack, which immediately sparked heated discussions and popularity among netizens, causing a surge in website traffic and even temporarily crashing the site.

According to reports, a CDC assistant doctor suggested in a web log that in case the zombie crisis seen on TV becomes "reality" in the future, people should always have an emergency kit ready, containing water, food, medicine, battery-powered radios, multi-purpose knives, and important documents, etc. Additionally, they should pre-establish an emergency plan and two predetermined meeting locations, and promptly head to refugee camps to take shelter.
