Nutritional Components of Potatoes

Potatoes have high nutritional and medicinal value. In general, the components found in fresh potatoes include:

Potatoes contain a large amount of carbohydrates, as well as proteins, minerals (such as phosphorus and calcium), and vitamins. They can be used as a staple food, eaten as a vegetable, or processed into auxiliary foods like French fries and chips. They are also used to make starch, vermicelli, and can even be brewed into alcohol or used as livestock feed.

Potatoes contain some toxic alkaloids, mainly solanine and chaconine. However, these toxins generally break down when cooked at 170°C. Wild potatoes tend to have higher toxicity, and solanine poisoning can lead to headaches, diarrhea, convulsions, coma, and in severe cases, death. However, cultivated potatoes typically have very low toxicity, and potato poisoning incidents are rare. Cultivated potatoes usually contain less than 0.2 mg/g of alkaloids, and it generally takes over 200 mg to cause poisoning, which is equivalent to eating 1.4 kg of raw potatoes at once. When stored, if exposed to light, potatoes can turn green and their toxic substances increase. Additionally, sprouted potatoes with purple spots around the eyes accumulate more toxins, making them more likely to cause poisoning. Therefore, care should be taken when consuming them.

Starch content ranges from 9% to 20%, protein from 1.5% to 2.3%, fat from 0.1% to 1.1%, and crude fiber from 0.6% to 0.8%. In 100g of potatoes, the nutritional components include: 66-113J of energy, 11-60mg of calcium, 15-68mg of phosphorus, 0.4mg-4.8mg of iron, 0.03-0.07mg of thiamine, 0.03-0.11mg of riboflavin, and 0.4-1.1mg of niacin. In addition, potato tubers contain carotene and ascorbic acid, which are not found in cereal grains. From a nutritional perspective, potatoes have more advantages compared to rice and flour, as they can provide the human body with a large amount of energy and can be considered "perfect food." A person can sustain life and health solely on potatoes and whole milk because the nutritional composition of potatoes is very comprehensive, and its nutritional structure is relatively reasonable, though the amounts of protein, calcium, and vitamin A are slightly lower. These deficiencies can be compensated for by whole milk. Potato tubers have high water content, low fat, and a relatively low calorific value per unit volume. The vitamin C content is ten times that of apples, B vitamins are four times that of apples, and various minerals are several to dozens of times higher than in apples. Potatoes are known to be hypotensive foods. An imbalance in certain nutrients in the diet can lead to disease, and similarly, adjusting the diet can help "eat away" corresponding diseases.
