Apple Event Summary: The Price of iPhone 5C is Hard to Bear

by anonymous on 2013-09-11 14:35:34

At 1:00 AM Beijing time on September 11, Apple held its new product launch event at its headquarters in Cupertino, California. The much-anticipated iPhone 5s and iPhone 5c were unveiled as scheduled. During the event, aside from the two most anticipated new phones, iOS 7, which was first introduced at WWDC 2013, also received an update.

iOS 7

Cook began by discussing how the iTunes Festival has been running for seven years, and next month, the cumulative sales of iOS devices are expected to exceed 700 million units.

iOS 7 already features over 200 new features, supporting the fourth generation and newer iPhones, second-generation and newer iPads and iPad minis, as well as the fifth-generation iPod touch. The new system will be pushed out on September 18.

A piece of good news is that Apple announced that the iWork suite, along with iPhoto and iMovie, will be freely provided on devices activated after September 1 and compatible with iOS 7.

Prior to being free, the three iWork software applications were priced at RMB 68 each, while iMovie and iPhoto were both priced at RMB 30. Apple CEO Tim Cook stated that the reason for making them all free is "considering that almost all users of Apple devices wish to use this software."

It is understood that the iWork series of software is Apple's mobile productivity tool, with Keynote, Pages, and Numbers all optimized specifically for mobile devices. Meanwhile, iMovie and iPhoto are tools for users to create beautiful videos and images.

iPhone 5c

The first to be launched, the iPhone 5c comes in five color options for its back shell: green, white, blue, pink, and yellow, but the front panel is only available in black. The back shell material is polycarbonate, and Apple simultaneously released a flexible silicone case. Other features include:

A6 processor, 4-inch 1136 x 640 Retina display, 326 ppi

8-megapixel backlit camera, using a five-element lens group and hybrid infrared filter, aperture size f/2.4, supports 30fps shooting of 1080p video, supports video stabilization

Front-facing camera is 1.2 megapixels, also uses a backlit sensor element, supports 720p video shooting

Supports dual-band Wi-Fi at 2.4GHz and 5GHz (802.11 a/b/g/n)

Supported LTE frequency bands are the most globally among smartphones, with a maximum download speed of 100 Mbps

Other: Bluetooth 4.0, Lightning cable, EarPods headphones

Two-year contract price is $99 for 16GB, $199 for 32GB; silicone case costs $29.

iPhone 5s

The iPhone 5s followed the iPhone 5c with the following specifications:

64-bit A7 processor, doubling the processing speed. The iPhone 5s is the world’s first smartphone with a 64-bit chip, while remaining compatible with 32-bit apps

M7 motion coprocessor

4-inch 1136 x 640 Retina display, 326 ppi

8-megapixel backlit camera, using a five-element lens group and hybrid infrared filter, aperture size f/2.2, individual pixel size 1.5 microns. Supports 30fps shooting of 1080p video and video stabilization

Main camera sensor viewfinder area increased by 15% compared to previous models, supports 120fps slow-motion video shooting

Uses True Tone dual LED flash, one tone is whiter/colder, another tone is yellower/warmer

Front-facing camera is 1.2 megapixels, also uses a backlit sensor element, supports 720p video shooting

Battery life improved: 10 hours of 3G calling, 10 hours of LTE browsing, standby time exceeds 250 hours—"better than the iPhone 5"

Touch ID fingerprint sensor, thin at 170 microns, scanning density 500ppi. Sensor is embedded within the Home button, supports fingerprint scanning for unlocking the phone, suitable for different individuals

Available in white, gold, and gray colors

Two-year contract price is $199 for 16GB, $299 for 32GB, $399 for 64GB, no 128GB option

Also features multiple cases, each priced at $39.

Detailed Explanation of iPhone 5S Touch ID Fingerprint Recognition

Friends who watched tonight's event should know that the biggest innovation of the iPhone 5S is likely this “Touch ID” function, which is completed through the Home button.

First, let's look at the construction of Touch ID. Its sensor is built into the Home button, right next to it (flattened instead of concave like before) is a ring of stainless steel sensors. When the user's finger approaches, it can sense it, and the sapphire layer on the surface of the Home button acts as a lens, so the user does not need to press the button for the phone to sense the finger's presence.

It is worth noting that the thickness of Touch ID is 170 microns, capable of real-time detection of 500ppi images. Apple even claims that it can perform 360-degree scans, allowing users to add multiple fingerprints, which are stored in the A7 processor and not uploaded to the cloud.

Users can more securely conduct iTunes purchases and unlock the screen through Touch ID. Phil Schiller also mentioned in a media interview that Apple currently will not open up this feature to third-party developers.

The iPhone 5s will be available for pre-order on the U.S. official website on September 13, and will be sold comprehensively in Japan, Canada, and other countries a week later. China, after 7 years since the birth of the iPhone, finally enters the list of first-batch sales countries.

Apple will open domestic online pre-orders for the iPhone 5c on September 13, while the iPhone 5s will only be orderable starting September 20. The Apple China official website lists the bare unit prices for the iPhone 5c and iPhone 5s as follows:

iPhone 5c: RMB 4488 for 16GB, RMB 5288 for 32GB

iPhone 5s: RMB 5288 for 16GB, RMB 6088 for 32GB, RMB 6888 for 64GB

During this event, Apple did not update the iPod product line, and the iPod player, long overshadowed by the iPhone, may be abandoned by Apple in the future. The iPhone 5, which was released just a year ago, has also been discontinued by Apple.
