A Comprehensive Review of the 12th Five-Year Plan for Seven Emerging Industries in 2011

December 28, 2011, 03:48

Source: Securities Daily

Author: Shen Ming

Editor's Note: On October 18, 2010, the Chinese government website published "The Decision of the State Council on Accelerating the Cultivation and Development of Strategic Emerging Industries," which identified seven emerging industries. These include energy conservation and environmental protection, next-generation information technology, biotechnology, high-end equipment manufacturing, new energy, new materials, and new energy vehicles. These industries are considered four pillars and three leading industries of the national economy. Ministries such as MIIT, MEP, and MOST immediately took action and successively launched the 12th Five-Year Plans for related industries in 2011. Our newspaper is launching a special edition today to review these plans one by one.

On October 18, 2010, the Chinese government website published "The Decision of the State Council on Accelerating the Cultivation and Development of Strategic Emerging Industries." By 2020, energy conservation and environmental protection, next-generation information technology, biotechnology, and high-end equipment manufacturing will become pillar industries of the national economy, while new energy, new materials, and new energy vehicles will become leading industries of the national economy. This decision marked the finalization of the framework for the seven strategic emerging industries.

From the introduction of the ten industrial revitalization plans in 2008 to the future focus on developing seven emerging industries, China's pace of upgrading production methods is accelerating. Adjustments to industry priorities are mainly based on the current state of industrial development.

From the new industry definitions, each industry includes several sub-industries. For example, the new energy industry includes nine sub-industries such as nuclear power, wind power, solar power, clean coal, and biomass energy; the next-generation information technology industry includes eight sub-industries such as triple network convergence, Internet of Things/sensor networks, next-generation communication networks, high-performance integrated circuits, and cloud computing; the energy conservation and environmental protection industry includes six sub-industries such as building energy conservation, emission treatment, environmental protection, and smart grids; the new material industry includes special functional materials, high-performance composite materials, chemical new materials, and nanomaterials; the new energy vehicle industry includes electric vehicles, hybrid vehicles, hydrogen-powered vehicles, charging equipment, lithium batteries, and new energy vehicle components; the high-end equipment manufacturing industry involves five sub-industries such as intelligent equipment, high-end power equipment, aerospace, marine engineering, and advanced transportation equipment; the biotechnology industry involves six sub-industries such as innovative drugs, biopharmaceuticals, advanced medical equipment, biological breeding, stem cells, and genetic modification.

"Chains of the seven strategic emerging industries will gradually form and improve over the next few years, having a positive effect on the formation of China's economy and new industrial cycles," said Zhou Zixue, chief economist of MIIT. According to recent GDP growth rates and scales in China, it is estimated that by 2015, the added value of strategic emerging industries will be approximately 4.3 trillion yuan, and by 2020, it will reach 11.4 trillion yuan. To achieve the above development goals set by the State Council, it is expected that strategic emerging industries will need to achieve an average annual growth rate of 24.1% from 2011 to 2015, and 21.3% from 2016 to 2020.

Environmental Protection and Energy Conservation

On December 20, 2011, the State Council announced the "Twelfth Five-Year National Environmental Protection Plan." The plan shows that the total environmental protection investment demand during the "Twelfth Five-Year Plan" period is about 3.4 trillion yuan. In the future, efforts to reduce energy consumption and emissions will expand further, and the State Council has signed responsibility agreements for reducing energy consumption and emissions with eight central state-owned enterprises through the Ministry of Environmental Protection.

According to the "Twelfth Five-Year National Environmental Protection Plan" published on the official website of the Central Government, the total environmental protection investment demand during the "Twelfth Five-Year Plan" period is about 3.4 trillion yuan. Among this, priority will be given to implementing eight key environmental protection projects, conducting a series of environmental basic surveys and pilot demonstrations, with an investment demand of about 1.5 trillion yuan.

The plan clearly defines the main indicators of environmental protection during the "Twelfth Five-Year Plan" period, including an 8% reduction in chemical oxygen demand (COD) emissions, a 10% reduction in ammonia nitrogen emissions, an 8% reduction in sulfur dioxide emissions, a 10% reduction in nitrogen oxide emissions, and an increase of 8% in the proportion of cities at or above the prefecture level meeting secondary air quality standards by 2015 compared to 2010.

In terms of solid waste treatment, by 2015, the comprehensive utilization rate of industrial solid waste will reach 72%, the harmless treatment rate of urban domestic garbage nationwide will reach 80%, and all counties will have the ability to handle domestic garbage harmlessly. By 2015, the intensity of dioxin emissions in key industries will decrease by 10%.

In terms of sewage treatment, by 2015, the country will add approximately 160,000 kilometers of urban sewage pipelines, increase daily sewage treatment capacity by 42 million tons, basically ensuring that all counties and key towns have sewage treatment capabilities, raise the load factor of sewage treatment facilities to more than 80%, and ensure that the urban sewage treatment rate reaches 85%. More than 50% of large-scale livestock farms and breeding areas will be equipped with solid waste and sewage storage and treatment facilities, and basically ensure that medical waste in cities at or above the prefecture level is treated harmlessly.

The plan points out that major pollutant reductions should be promoted. Backward production capacities should be eliminated quickly, especially in industries such as steel, non-ferrous metals, building materials, chemicals, electricity, coal, papermaking, printing and dyeing, and leather. Efforts should focus on reducing chemical oxygen demand (COD) and ammonia nitrogen emissions. Reductions in sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxide emissions should be strengthened, continuously promoting pollution reduction in the power industry and speeding up the process of desulfurization and denitrification in other industries.

The plan indicates that the investment demand is approximately 3.4 trillion yuan, which is 13% higher than the previously expected 3 trillion yuan. Major pollutant reduction projects will benefit significantly. We believe that during the "Twelfth Five-Year Plan" period, the goal of accelerating industrial transformation through strengthening environmental protection has been clarified. With the country's increased investment in energy conservation and emissions reduction, related industries will enter a developmental stage.

Next-Generation Information Technology

In May 2011, at a forum held in Beijing, relevant officials from the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) stated that the draft of the "Twelfth Five-Year Plan for the Development of Next-Generation Information Technology Industry" (hereinafter referred to as the "Plan") had been completed and was ready to be submitted to the State Council.

According to MIIT officials, the next-generation information technology industry will focus on developing new mobile communications, the next-generation internet, triple network convergence, the Internet of Things (IoT), cloud computing, integrated circuits, new display technologies, high-end software, high-end servers, and information services.

"On the current basis, during the Twelfth Five-Year Plan period, China will achieve the integration of the three networks," said Zhu Hongren, Chief Engineer of MIIT. Since the State Council decided to accelerate the work of integrating the three networks, MIIT, the State Administration of Radio, Film, and Television, and other relevant departments have actively promoted the pilot work of integrating the three networks and have made positive progress.

He indicated that related enterprises are strengthening collaboration and innovation in industrial forms and market promotion models, driving the development of IT PV, mobile TV, online video, and other businesses related to the integration of the three networks. At the same time, they are enhancing the research and development and manufacturing of information technology products and proposing a standard system suggestion for the integration of the three networks.

According to a research report from the Electronics Information Industry Development Research Institute, by 2015, the overall scale of China's IoT market will reach 750 billion yuan, with a compound annual growth rate exceeding 30% within five years. The market prospect will far exceed the single markets of computers, the internet, and mobile communications.

According to the "Plan," during the Twelfth Five-Year Plan period, China will formulate and promote its own independent coding system, break through core technologies and major key common technologies, initially forming a complete industrial chain from sensors, chips, software, terminals, whole machines, networks to business applications, and cultivating a group of IoT industry leading enterprises with strong international competitiveness.

At the same time, it will focus on promoting the development of the smart logistics industry characterized by IoT. From 2013 to 2015, a logistics informatization system will gradually be formed, and by 2015, a coordinated development logistics informatization system adapted to the national modern logistics system will be preliminarily established.

In terms of cloud computing, MIIT officials stated that after the release of the "Plan," domestic cloud computing standardization policies are expected to be issued by the end of the year, with the initial draft of cloud computing standardization released in June.

He further explained that domestic cloud computing standardization mainly involves standard subjects, entry points, operations, and construction aspects. The focus will be on managing internet data centers (IDCs), measured by IDC energy, security, user experience, etc., divided into four levels. Major operators and equipment manufacturers will obtain certification certificates, with the certificate level determining the IDC construction level, making IDC construction more standardized.

According to authoritative institutions, from the end of 2009 to the end of 2013, cloud computing will bring $800 billion in new business revenue globally, of which China will receive more than RMB 1.105 trillion (approximately $159 billion) in new net business revenue.

The "Plan" points out that a major breakthrough in the core technologies of the global information technology industry is imminent. The computing architecture faces profound changes; network technology develops towards broadband, wireless, intelligence, and ultra-high-speed systems, ultra-large capacity directions; software technology accelerates towards networking, systematization, servicing, and high reliability directions; sensing technology breaks through towards intelligence, integrating and extending the industrial value chain.

Biotechnology

On November 29, 2011, according to a message on the Ministry of Science and Technology website, the Ministry of Science and Technology recently released the "Twelfth Five-Year Biotechnology Development Plan." The development goal of the "Plan" is that during the Twelfth Five-Year Plan period, the independent innovation capability of biotechnology will significantly improve, the overall level of biotechnology will enter the forefront of the world, and some fields will reach world-leading levels.

The "Plan" points out that biotechnology is the main driving force behind the development of international science and technology today, one of the fastest-growing high-tech fields in the world, and has become a focal point of international competition. It will become a breakthrough point for solving major problems facing human social development, such as health, food, energy, environment, and biosafety.

The development goal of the "Plan" is that during the Twelfth Five-Year Plan period, the independent innovation capability of biotechnology will significantly improve, the overall level of biotechnology will enter the forefront of the world, and some fields will reach world-leading levels. The rapid rise of industries such as biopharmaceuticals, bio-agriculture, bio-manufacturing, bio-energy, and bio-environmental protection will lead to the basic formation of the overall layout of the bioindustry, promoting the bioindustry to become one of the pillar industries of the national economy, making China a biotechnology powerhouse and a bioindustry giant.

The "Plan" points out that closely following the frontiers of international biotechnology development, aiming at the major strategic needs of the country, according to the focus of various national scientific and technological programs, balancing current and medium-to-long-term development, integrating resources covering medicine, agriculture, manufacturing, energy, environmental protection, and other fields, combining original innovation with integrated innovation, combining introduction, absorption, digestion, and re-innovation, strengthening collaborative innovation, forming core independent technologies, cultivating original innovation results, forming sustainable development capabilities. Integrating the forces of various departments and localities, overall planning, step-by-step implementation, using points to drive surfaces, reflecting the connection between basic research, applied research, and industrialization.

Orient Securities pharmaceutical industry chief analyst Li Shuhua believes that research and development is the top priority. From a mid-to-long-term perspective, the "Twelfth Five-Year Biotechnology Development Plan" encourages independent innovation, which will significantly promote the currently low-level imitation stage of the bio-pharmaceutical industry, benefiting leading companies in sub-industries.

High-end Equipment Manufacturing Industry

On November 16, 2011, Zhang Xiangmu, Director of the Equipment Department of MIIT, at the mechanical industry economic situation report meeting, stated that the High-end Equipment Manufacturing Industry Twelfth Five-Year Plan would soon be issued, focusing on five areas: aviation equipment, marine engineering equipment, rail transit equipment, satellite manufacturing equipment and application, intelligent manufacturing equipment. Among them, the national subsidy funds for the Intelligent Equipment Development Special Program and the Satellite and Application Development Special Program have already been implemented. The first-time subsidies for intelligent equipment account for 25-50% of the product sales price.

Zhang Xiangmu mentioned that the special funds for intelligent manufacturing equipment and satellite manufacturing equipment and application have been confirmed. The first batch of 19 projects in the direction of intelligent manufacturing equipment received a national subsidy of 9.5 billion yuan. Products with the nature of being the first unit or set were particularly valued, with national subsidies accounting for 25%-30% of the product sales price, reaching up to 50%. Both project development units and users of the first unit (set) products were supported. Fiscal support for aviation equipment and marine engineering equipment is currently being implemented. Aviation engines will also be included in the relevant major special plans of the High-end Equipment Manufacturing Industry Twelfth Five-Year Plan.

In terms of financial support, MIIT will organize local enterprises to apply for innovation products, and loans can receive preferential treatment. Currently, Industrial and Commercial Bank of China, Export-Import Bank of China, and others are actively cooperating.

Currently, the scale of China's intelligent equipment industry is about 300 billion yuan, with most of the domestic market dominated by foreign manufacturers. Domestic manufacturers occupy 10% of the market share in intelligent instruments and control systems, 20% in industrial robots and specialized equipment, 20% in mid-range CNC machine tools, and 1% in high-end CNC machine tools.

CICC's research report believes that by 2015, the sales revenue of China's intelligent equipment industry is expected to reach 1 trillion yuan, with the domestic market share of high-end intelligent equipment and basic manufacturing equipment needed by key industries in the national economy reaching 50%. By 2020, the sales revenue of the intelligent equipment industry is expected to reach 2 trillion yuan, with the domestic market share reaching 70%.

Key sub-industries supported in the satellite and application industry include satellite manufacturing, launch services, satellite operation, and satellite ground equipment manufacturing.

It is understood that the current scale of the satellite manufacturing and launch industry is 7 billion yuan, and the scale of the satellite application industry is 60 billion yuan. The manufacturing and launch industries are monopolized by the Fifth Academy of Aerospace and the First Academy of Aerospace, respectively. In the satellite application industry, the GPS navigation system occupies more than 95% of the market share, while the domestically produced Beidou navigation system only accounts for 1%.

CICC's research report mentions that China has already launched eight Beidou navigation satellites. It plans to launch more than ten satellites by 2012, building a Beidou satellite navigation system covering the Asia-Pacific region. It is expected to build a global satellite navigation system consisting of five geostationary orbit satellites and 30 non-geostationary orbit satellites by 2020. The Beidou satellite navigation system is expected to form an industry worth 150 billion to 200 billion yuan by 2015, and could reach 400 billion yuan by 2020.

New Energy

On December 15, 2011, the National Energy Administration announced a series of renewable energy Twelfth Five-Year Plan targets. By 2015, wind power will reach 100 million kilowatts, generating 190 billion kilowatt-hours annually, including 5 million kilowatts of offshore wind power; solar power generation will reach 15 million kilowatts, generating 20 billion kilowatt-hours annually; combined with biomass energy, solar thermal utilization, and nuclear power, the total amount of non-fossil energy developed by 2015 will reach 480 million tons of standard coal.

These planning data were released at the summary meeting of the achievements of the China Large-Scale Renewable Energy Development Project held on the same day.

Deputy Director Liu Qi of the National Energy Administration emphasized in his speech that during the Twelfth Five-Year Plan period, management of the wind power industry will be strengthened, focusing on grid connection and consumption of wind power, raising technical and quality requirements for wind power, and implementing annual development plan management for wind power to ensure orderly development of wind power. Photovoltaic power generation subsidy policies will be improved, supporting the application of distributed photovoltaic power generation; rural renewable energy utilization will be promoted, and by 2015, 200 green energy demonstration counties will be built nationwide.

Meeting information showed that after years of development, China has become a global leader in renewable energy. By the end of 2010, China's installed hydropower capacity reached 213 million kilowatts, ranking first in the world; wind power capacity grew rapidly, with grid-connected operating capacity exceeding 31 million kilowatts; solar photovoltaic battery production reached 8 million kilowatts, accounting for more than 40% of global production; cumulative use of solar water heaters exceeded 168 million square meters, accounting for more than 60% of the total global usage; biomass energy, geothermal energy, and other renewable energy sectors also achieved varying degrees of development.
