It depends on how you practice.

1. Judgement Branch: Focus on the Judgement skill, with a side focus on Shield or Riding skills. This way, your power is immense but requires high INT. For example, 30 INT leads to level 3 Judgement dealing 1700x3 damage to Orc Corpses in one hit, though it also injures yourself, making it not cost-effective. However, this build is less vulnerable to mages compared to other classes.

2. Shield Branch: Focus mainly on Shield skills. Since you need to max out shield techniques, you can't ride birds. But you can activate all skills simultaneously, providing defense and reflection, ensuring absolute survival. However, the attack power is relatively low; run when you face a mage. When facing a monk's AB, there's a 50% chance of mutual destruction.

3. Knight Type: Focus on practicing riding and lance acceleration, suitable for those with high AGI.

Ultimately, how you train depends on how you allocate your stats; otherwise, you might regret it T_T

Max out the Shield skills, don't bother with Judgement—it's not worth it:

1. You can't hit people because your DEX is too low.

2. The damage isn't impressive.

There are several types of Paladins (I prefer the first type, so I'll explain it in more detail):

1. A Paladin focusing on Holy Judgement, with Sacrifice and Divine Pressure as secondary skills.

Stat allocation: STR 30, AGI 12, INT 105, VIT 90, DEX 62, LUK 4 (this includes enhancements but excludes equipment).

There are two detailed articles that introduce this well, which you can use as references:

First article:

After the update, the Sacrifice skill works similarly to the Knight's Spiral Thrust, meaning it's no longer an additional effect on normal attacks. It's now related to DEX rather than AGI. After using Spiral Thrust, your HP decreases by 3% * 3 = 9%, and you make five attacks on the enemy at 3% * 1.65 of your attack power. If your DEX is high, you can take down high-VIT classes with enough potions; it's not a pipe dream.

Therefore, high VIT and high DEX are the directions for the Sacrifice stat allocation. As for Grand Holy Sound and Divine Might, they've changed significantly now. Level 10 Holy Sound used to have a 37% activation rate, but after the update, it's 55% at level 1 and 100% at level 10.

Since S hasn't been increased, carrying capacity has become an issue, but since S attack speed isn't needed, riding a bird that adds 1000 carrying capacity and movement speed boost becomes the preferred choice. This is why we ride birds.
