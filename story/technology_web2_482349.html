The Feast of Brand Building in "Golf" Sports

By Zhang Zhiwei, Journalist for this publication, Intern Liu Hua

Nobility and Elegance

Wisdom and Challenge in Golf

Golf is an ancient aristocratic sport. It's a challenging project rooted in nature, a noble life element that pursues a cultural taste melted into the natural world and a leisurely lifestyle. Originating from Scotland in the 15th century, it evolved from a game played by shepherds to one of the most popular sports in the world today. With a long history and deep roots, according to the information disclosed by "World Rankings", www.ttiioo.com, among the top 15 activities Americans love, sports rank high, with golf being the first choice over tennis and swimming. In China, due to its participants having enviable careers, status, reputation, and economic backgrounds, golf has become a coveted domain for a select few. Furthermore, golf contains philosophy akin to Go (Weiqi), which also embodies a philosophy of life and attitude. Cultured elements always attract people to participate and pay attention, making golf tournaments and players the focus of media pursuit.

When this sport gathers a certain amount of popularity, it forms an industrial platform. Utilizing golf as a medium, brand building becomes a thriving marketing force on this platform.

Golf Sport and Car Brand Building

Due to the prominence of golf participants, golf events have become the target of luxury car manufacturers. The 2006 Volkswagen Masters started in Sanya, inviting two-time U.S. Open champion Retief Goosen and China's top player Zhang Lianwei, with a total prize money of $300,000. In this Masters event, the five luxury sedans provided by Volkswagen, the Phaeton, became another major highlight of the event. Symbolizing nobility, elegance, wisdom, and challenge, golf and the intelligence, progressiveness, and transcendence of its players align with the characteristics of Phaeton users—leaders of wealth in the new century. Through this sport, the precision, technology, and elegance showcased effectively promote Volkswagen's consistent car-making philosophy and brand essence.

Compared to Volkswagen, Volvo appears more frequently beside the "greens." The noble elegance, return to nature, relaxed self-discipline of golf harmonizes perfectly with Volvo's brand concept of respecting life, safety, and environmental protection. Since 1995, Volvo has sponsored the China Open Golf Championship. One of Volvo's goals is to give more Chinese players the opportunity to excel in their country's open championship, nurturing more professional champions like Zhang Lianwei. Since entering China, Volvo Trucks, with its high-quality, high-value product features, has greatly promoted the development of China's logistics industry. As the China Open progresses, Volvo Trucks' market in China continues to achieve remarkable results. Its "All Touching" service and "All Journey" comprehensive logistics solutions create more wealth and dreams for China's elite class. The 2007 Volvo China Open will start on April 12th, with a record-breaking total prize money of $2 million, five times higher than in 1995, and specially providing 50 XC90 vehicles as VIP cars for this competition. Through the China Open, Volvo has closely integrated with the rapidly developing golf movement in China, deeply rooting its brand in the soil of Chinese golf development.

BMW has a tradition of hosting golf events. Starting in 1988, BMW established the BMW International Golf Tournament, which has developed into the largest amateur golf tournament in the world today. Mercedes-Benz named the China Golf Finals, with the concept of "sponsoring and organizing classic sports events, advocating an active and healthy lifestyle," as its sponsorship philosophy. Mr. Henning Leschke, President of Mercedes-Benz (China) Auto Sales Company, stated that golf's pursuit of high-quality living resonates with Mercedes-Benz's essence and core values, showcasing a spirit of positive progress and innovation. Through sponsoring golf events, www.yuchang.net.cn/js/default.asp, car manufacturers enhance user loyalty and build brand culture.

By integrating the prestigious golf sport with luxurious cars, automobile manufacturers gain excellent reputations and brand recognition, leaving deep positive impressions of products and enterprises in consumers' minds. As more and more people in China begin to experience golf, understanding the efforts made in trials and tribulations, mastering more precise skills, and eventually enjoying the joy of victory in competition, if these spiritual connotations resonate with the values of car manufacturers, the benefits are undeniable.

Golf Sport and Bank Brand Building

Currently, the homogenization problem of credit card issuance in domestic banks is quite severe, with similarities in transaction processing networks, policies, regulations, process management, and credit policies. In terms of customer positioning, influenced by each bank’s credit policy and development strategy, the target groups for credit cards show a high degree of convergence. In December 2006, the Everbright Bank Golf Celebrity Invitational was held as scheduled, www.dbyhk99.com, inviting numerous social elites and business leaders. Taking advantage of this invitational, Everbright Bank jointly launched the first "UnionPay Golf Credit Card" with Bank of China. This card offers various value-added services related to golf, including discounts at some city golf courses. This credit card, using golf as an entry point, repeatedly uses credit cards as a link to provide high-end customers with a refined and extraordinary life experience, building a high-class exchange platform of "one card to make friends, use the ball to convey feelings," thus promoting the development of credit card business. In credit card brand operations, accurate brand positioning and brand personality building combine perfectly with golf sports.

CITIC Bank sponsors the Youth Golf Match Play Championship, spanning Beijing, Shanghai, and Shenzhen. CITIC Bank's VIP customers not only let their children enjoy the fun of swinging clubs in the match but also feel the superiority brought by their VIP financial management identity. "Wisdom Creates Wealth" is CITIC Bank's VIP financial management service philosophy, where wisdom is the source of wealth management, a concept similar to the intelligent sport of golf.

HSBC's attention to golf is continuously increasing. Besides sponsoring the current three top-tier tour events, HSBC also sponsors the Sheshan International Golf Championship. Its significant sponsorship fee in golf is eye-catching, offering a $5 million prize pool, showcasing its royal demeanor in naming rights for golf events. HSBC globally sponsors over 300 golf-related events.

Golf and Luxury Brand Building

Famous liquor Johnnie Walker, whose parent company Diageo comes from the UK and is the world's largest spirits company. Its consistently promoted brand image is gentlemanly, high-end, and positive. In brand promotion, Johnnie Walker often uses high-end sports event marketing. In 2005, the Johnnie Walker Golf Elite Tournament was held in Beijing, effectively spreading and expanding brand culture. After the Chinese New Year, Johnnie Walker launched the "Golf Edition" advertisement. The ad shows a group of men playing golf; after several attempts, the ball still doesn't go into the hole, yet they maintain an optimistic attitude and continue playing, even when the ball lands near an alligator's mouth. The screen again displays "Keep Walking." This ad continues to promote Johnnie Walker's cultural connotation and brand philosophy while highlighting its ongoing sponsorship and promotion of golf. Its image immerses viewers in the green golf course scene. It is dedicated to the most discerning and visionary people in the world, rewarding them for their progress and success.

Rongsheng Wang = "Successful People". Rongsheng Wang aims to establish a "successful person" brand image, initially choosing a golfer as a brand spokesperson and symbolizing high-end brands through Western golf and equestrian competitions. Although Rongsheng Wang later sponsored fishing tournaments, canoeing competitions, and the torch relay of the Five Cities Games, and its brand slogan changed, golf remains the most tightly bound and longest-used element, significantly contributing to establishing Rongsheng Wang's premium brand image.

Due to its ability to evoke personal emotional attachment, transcend cultural, faith, and racial barriers, and simultaneously possess media functions, along with its noble, fashionable, and wise characteristics, there are currently very few true elite, end-of-line, or luxury brands in China. Golf sports will become a feast for companies to leverage in shaping their brands.
