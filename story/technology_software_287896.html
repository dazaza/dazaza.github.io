"When doing part-time jobs outside, you must be low-key as a person but high-profile in your work," said Xiao Deng. Xiao Deng really wanted to turn and leave, but after gritting his teeth, he put on a smile again: "I'm sorry for taking up your precious time, but I'm not here to sell anything; instead, I hope to provide you with quality service...". Through trials and tribulations, his business acumen and psychological resilience continuously improved, and he finally succeeded, creating a miracle that year: as a part-timer, he outperformed all full-time employees and became the sales champion.

He is the fourth of five siblings. When he was little, Xiao Deng had already made up his mind to study hard and become a useful person.

Xiao Deng coordinated and balanced his schedule by opening a small tutoring class during holidays, which brought him substantial income. During school days, he found collecting used study materials from graduates and reselling them to new students was also a profitable venture. So, after school, he set up a stall on campus.

Later on, part-time jobs were not just a means of making a living for Xiao Deng, but also a window to understand society.

He often encountered others' disdainful looks while selling products, but Xiao Deng always greeted them with a smile. Once, as he approached the client's office door, the client pushed him away, saying, "You keep coming, aren't you tired of it?" He almost fell, and the委屈in his heart was unimaginable.

Dean Deng gave advice to college students: Part-time jobs are good, but they need to be reasonably arranged according to one’s own actual situation. Before each college student chooses a part-time job, they should consider clearly the nature of the part-time work and their purpose of studying at university. Let their college life be rich and efficient.

Because of his hard work and willingness to endure hardship, he always received recognition and appreciation from his employers. In three years, he saved 80,000 yuan.

Deng Yongfa's home is in Dongtianpu Village, Lijia Town, Shaoyang County, Hunan Province. This is a remote and impoverished small mountain village. His parents worked far away in Shaoxing, Zhejiang to support the education of their five children.

Part-time jobs require a great deal of time and energy, but absolutely cannot sacrifice academics for money-making. This has always been a firm principle upheld by Deng Yongfa. After entering university, Xiao Deng set long-term goals and plans, aspiring to pursue postgraduate studies starting from sophomore year. In his senior year, facing the triple pressure of professional exams, inter-disciplinary postgraduate entrance exams, and earning money, he insisted on balancing studying and working. But if there was a conflict, no matter how much money he could earn, he wouldn't go. So how did he find the balance point between the two and manage both simultaneously?

The former director of the Provincial Academy of Social Sciences, Deng Jianqiu, believed that college students doing part-time jobs outside school can earn a certain amount of economic income, gain work experience, understand society, and learn knowledge that cannot be learned in school. However, it is very easy to pick up peanuts and lose watermelons.

A financially struggling rural college student started working part-time from his sophomore year, having worked as a salesperson for a company and as a tutor for English, Chinese, and mathematics... His monthly income exceeded 10,000 yuan at its highest, earning 80,000 yuan in three years. Not only did he cover all the expenses for his junior brother who was in his third year of university, but he also took out 20,000 yuan to help classmates start businesses in Chongqing. This year, he successfully passed the entrance exam for a fully-funded postgraduate program in Administrative Law at Hubei Academy of Social Sciences with the top score in his major.

In 2005, Xiao Deng was admitted to Sichuan Foreign Languages Institute (now Southwest University of Foreign Studies). Except for the tuition fees for his first year, Deng Yongfa never asked his family for money again. Starting from his sophomore year, he joined the ranks of those doing part-time jobs. "Doing private tutoring is the most profitable method," Xiao Deng smiled. That summer vacation, he tutored five students every day, earning more than 10,000 yuan in over a month.

Yesterday, at the dormitory of Hubei Academy of Social Sciences, we met the new graduate student, Deng Yongfa, who transferred from Sichuan Foreign Languages Institute (now Southwest University of Foreign Studies).

"Part-time work is very tough. But through it, I not only gained economic income and solved my practical difficulties, but also honed my abilities and gained valuable social experience. However, it must be reasonably arranged and comprehensively considered. After all, academics are the most important, as they relate to one's long-term development in life," said the simple Xiao Deng.

Comrade Deng Yongfa has given us a good example to follow. His "two-point theory" (earning money while studying) and "focus theory" (placing study as the main focus) well resolved the contradiction between part-time work and study. When doing part-time jobs, the key lies in time management and self-control. One should quickly switch roles and do what needs to be done at the right time.

Before going to bed at night, he also stipulated that he must read books for half an hour. With this persistence, his academic performance consistently ranked at the top. In the midst of busy part-time jobs, he won multiple awards: scholarships for two consecutive semesters from 2007-2008, the "Glorious Senlin Scholarship" in 2007-2008, and the "Science and Technology Innovation Advanced Individual" award at the institute level in the 2007-2008 academic year...

Method Three: Reasonable Arrangement, Smart Part-time Work

To avoid affecting his normal studies, Xiao Deng set several rules for himself: only do part-time jobs on weekends, push off some work when exams are approaching, and try internships in large companies during vacations... The reasonable arrangement allowed him to handle everything with ease. At the same time, he focused on continuously increasing the value of his part-time work. From initially running around giving private lessons to now collaborating with classmates to develop educational institutions, Xiao Deng laughed and said that the intellectual content of his part-time work was getting higher and higher, moving towards the "boss" level. This way, the time and energy needed for part-time work would be greatly reduced, allowing more energy to be spent on studying.

During his junior year, he set a goal for himself - to get into a master's degree program in law. From Monday to Friday, whether eating or walking, he seized every moment to read books. "Time is really like water in a sponge, squeezed out." He also wrote down English words on small pieces of paper, putting them in his pocket to memorize one or two while waiting for the bus.
