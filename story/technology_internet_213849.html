1.1. Cisco GSR Series Routers vs. Port Bay PowerHammer Series Routers

**Cisco GSR Router Description:**

The Cisco 12400 Internet router provides a high-performance, high-capacity switching platform with dense ten-gigabit routing capabilities for deployment in service provider and Internet Service Provider (ISP) applications. The Cisco 12000 Internet router is a key component of Cisco's IP+Optical strategy.

**Main Features:**

- **Scalable Platform Capacity**: The Cisco 12400 Internet router offers slots with 20Gbps per board, providing up to 320Gbps aggregate switching capacity.

- **Distributed Architecture**: Distributed packet forwarding occurs over a simple crossbar switch matrix (without storage function) and a single line card forwarding engine (with packet buffer), ensuring line-rate performance for each slot. This line card supports multicast replication and broadcast/multicast video streaming services without affecting the service level of the line card, supporting services that require minimal latency and jitter.

- **Various Service Interfaces**: The Cisco 12400 Internet router supports a variety of interfaces, platform interchangeability, and legacy card support, ensuring the necessary interface technology is provided. ASIC-based packet forwarding and value-added features like Class of Service (CoS) support full-line speed performance.

- **Carrier-grade Reliability**: The Cisco 12400 Internet router fully complies with Network Equipment Building System (NEBS) requirements for carrier-grade network devices, meeting industry safety rules and standards. The chassis includes redundant route processors, switch fabric cards, clock scheduler cards, and power supplies.
