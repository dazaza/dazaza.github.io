LG Electronics Japan announced on November 10 the release of the LED monitor "E2241VG-BN" equipped with an energy-saving mode called "SUPEREnergySaving." During regular operation, it limits power consumption to 24W. The product is set to be released in mid-November with an estimated retail price of around 17,000 yen. The E2241VG-BN is a 21.5-inch LCD monitor that uses a glare panel and white LEDs for backlighting. Combined with the video engine "f-ENGINE," it claims to achieve a contrast ratio of 5 million to 1.

The energy-saving mode "SUPEREnergySaving" reduces backlight brightness while amplifying the input signal and increasing the aperture ratio of the LCD panel, thus achieving low power consumption without significantly lowering brightness. The on-screen display can also show cumulative carbon dioxide reduction amounts. The monitor is equipped with HDMI, D-Sub 15-pin, and DVI-D ports and supports VESA mount 75mm x 75mm.

In other news, Paperboy&Co. has released an Android app for their ebook creation and sales platform "Puboo." Through this app, users can purchase, download, and view ebooks available on Puboo. These ebooks can be browsed via a browser or reader apps compatible with PDF and EPUB formats. Downloaded files can be saved in the app's "bookshelf" for offline viewing. Purchase history for paid ebooks is automatically saved, allowing access through either the app or PC.

Puboo is a platform where users can create, publish, and sell ebooks in formats like PDF and EPUB, suitable for various devices including tablets and smartphones. Since its launch on June 22, 2010, approximately 18,000 works have been published on the platform.
