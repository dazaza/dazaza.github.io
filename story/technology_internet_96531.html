How much do you know about cell phone radiation? Get close to you, make it hard for me!

by zdj625 on 2008-10-15 11:26:35

Xinhua News Agency reported on April 27 in Life Times that recently, American lawyer Peter Angelos is representing neurologist Professor Christopher Newman, who suffers from glioma, in a lawsuit against 10 domestic and international mobile phone manufacturers and network operators for $800 million in compensation. If this case has not drawn the attention of domestic mobile phone users, then a rare malignant brain tumor - glioma - recently diagnosed by Beijing Liberation Army 304 Hospital cannot fail to attract national attention. Neurosurgery expert Professor Li Anmin, who has been engaged in research and treatment of brain gliomas for many years at the hospital, believes that it may be related to long-term high-frequency use of mobile phones...

After eight years of using a mobile phone, a brain cancer developed in the left brain.

According to Professor Li Anmin, last year at the end of the year, the Department of Neurosurgery at the Liberation Army 304 Hospital treated a patient with a large-scale brain glioma in the left cerebral hemisphere. The patient had been misdiagnosed as having a stroke at several famous hospitals in the country. The 304 Hospital identified it as a clinically rare type of brain glioma, which was related to the patient's long-term use of mobile phones.

There are three reasons: First, the shape of the tumor; common gliomas have a lump-like proliferation center and infiltrate into surrounding normal brain tissue like crab claws, but this patient's tumor grew diffusely and scattered, with unclear boundaries around it. Second, the tumor occurred in the parieto-occipital-temporal junction area, which is extremely rare in clinical practice. Third, the patient had used a mobile phone for 8 years and was accustomed to answering calls with his left hand, and the tumor area coincided with the radiation area of the mobile phone antenna.

Professor Li believed that the occurrence of this case was closely related to mobile phone radiation. This is the first case reported domestically suspected of being related to mobile phone radiation among malignant tumors.

Mobile phone electromagnetic wave radiation may induce malignant brain tumors

Professor Li Anmin introduced that electromagnetic wave radiation is divided into two categories: ionizing radiation, such as high-energy rays, X-rays, α-rays, β-rays, γ-rays used in physics and nuclear medicine, and cosmic rays; and non-ionizing radiation: microwave radiation. Mobile phones belong to the category of microwaves with relatively high radiation.

British scientists found after radiating nematodes with an electromagnetic field of mobile phone frequency that the body protein of the nematodes produced effects similar to heat shock, meaning they were slightly cooked. If the radiation stopped at this point, the nematode protein could return to normal. However, if the radiation continued, the protein would undergo irreversible denaturation. The human body has an active immune system responsible for immune regulation, immune surveillance, immune killing, and immune repair functions. Whether damage at the cellular molecular level caused by ionizing radiation or non-ionizing radiation, it will be accurately identified and precisely repaired by a healthy immune system, maintaining the integrity of human cells and organs. However, due to the decline of immune function, ultra-high doses of ionizing radiation or long-term low-dose radiation both exceed the monitoring and repair capacity of the human immune system, resulting in irreversible changes in human cells—biologically called mutations. Mutated cells proliferate uncontrollably, leading to the formation of malignant tumors.

The measurement results of mobile phone electromagnetic wave radiation are worrying

At the end of 2000, the Swiss Institute of Technology published test results for 28 brands of mobile phones sold in the European market. The radiation amount of the highest brand was six times higher than that of the lowest brand. In February 2000, a survey by the Guangdong Consumer Association found that the electromagnetic wave radiation of 11 mobile phone models sold on the market exceeded the national standard limit by more than 18 times under maximum power.

Professor Li took out a light-emitting diode and asked the reporter to make a call with a mobile phone. While the mobile phone was transmitting, the diode lit up. Professor Li said that the diode should emit light only when powered, but a single call could make it glow, indicating how strong the electromagnetic waves of the mobile phone are. The harm of electromagnetic wave radiation to the human body goes through a process from quantitative change to qualitative change. Although there is controversy over whether mobile phone electromagnetic wave radiation can cause harm to the human body, prevention before disaster strikes and reducing the "electronic fog" surrounding the human body is always beneficial to health.

Professor Li reminded that when dialing a mobile phone, do not rush to bring it close to your ear, wait until the call is connected, to reduce the high-intensity electromagnetic wave radiation generated by the mobile phone during the instant of transmission; try to reduce the frequency of mobile phone use.

The controversy over whether mobile phone electromagnetic wave radiation is harmful to human health

American lawyer Peter Angelos is currently representing neurologist Professor Christopher Newman from Maryland, who suffers from brain glioma, in a lawsuit for $800 million in compensation against 10 domestic and international mobile phone manufacturers and network operators.

On July 27 last year, the British Ministry of Education issued a notice banning students under the age of 16 from using mobile phones. In the attached research report, it was stated that children's nervous systems are in the development stage, making them particularly vulnerable to mobile phone radiation, thus absorbing more energy from the radiation.

In January this year, Dr. Andre of Essen University in Germany compared 593 patients with retinal melanoma and found that the incidence rate among frequent mobile phone users was three times that of less frequent users.

Professor Gale of London University, who studies human brain nerves and cells, wrote an article pointing out that normal white mice continuously exposed to four hours of radio wave radiation with frequencies similar to mobile phones showed signs of brain cell and DNA structure damage.

However, some mobile phone manufacturers, telecommunications experts, and some medical and biological experts believe that mobile phones are harmless to the human body.

Vodafone, a giant in mobile phone production in the UK, stated that the most comprehensive study currently conducted on mobile phone radiation shows that both mobile phones and transmission stations are harmless to the human body, but he never refutes the association between mobile phones and brain tumors, claiming further research is needed.

Singapore telecommunications experts strictly tested mobile phone radiation and showed that the temperature rise caused by mobile phone radiation on surrounding objects does not exceed 0.15°C. WHO's strictest thermal injury temperature rise standard is 0.25°C, so mobile phones will not cause serious harm to the human body.

The German Institute of Communication Science explained that long-term mobile phone use causing facial heat, numbness, memory loss, dizziness, and blurred vision on the side of the antenna is a sign of physical fatigue and unrelated to mobile phone use. Motorola's professional research institution clearly stated that each mobile phone model is designed according to the strict requirements of the International Telecommunication Union, undergoes rigorous testing, and biochemical radiation is one of the main items tested.

At the end of 2000, the authoritative international medical journal "Journal of the American Medical Association" published a survey report completed by eight medical experts over five years, stating that after long-term follow-up of 900 mobile phone users, it was concluded that the incidence of brain tumors was unrelated to mobile phone use.

Harmful? Harmless? Will we know in three to four years?

The debate over whether mobile phones are harmful to human health has attracted the attention of the World Health Organization. The International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) under WHO initiated a two-year study involving 13 countries on the relationship between mobile phone radiation and human brain tissue damage, with final results expected to be published in 2004.

France is conducting comparative research on 1,500 cases of brain tumor patients and healthy individuals to monitor the relationship between mobile phone use and brain tumor incidence, with results expected to be announced by the end of 2003. It seems that authoritative arbitration results will take another 3-4 years.

It is said that the brain tumor patient at the 304 Hospital no longer dares to use a mobile phone. After using it for 8 years, did he realize too late? And will more of our consumers develop diseases related to mobile phone radiation in the next four years? We don't know. (Reporter Ye Runxia, Correspondent Zhang Xianhuai)
