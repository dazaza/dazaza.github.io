Consumers say they found flies in their dumplings. The manufacturer agreed to compensate, but refused to apologize.

by lpoihjkmkk on 2012-03-01 17:38:21

Why is the company willing to offer a "return one, compensate ten" deal but unwilling to apologize to the customer? Mr. Zhao explained in an interview with Rednet's "Consumer Rights Protection" hotline that the production line for the Sim恋 brand dumplings is fully enclosed, so it is impossible for flies to enter. Additionally, the meat filling in the dumplings is all minced, so it is also impossible for there to be whole foreign objects. "However, the customer did indeed find a foreign object while eating. But since the source of the foreign object cannot be confirmed, the factory naturally cannot issue an apology and can only provide appropriate compensation."

The manufacturer responded that the appearance of foreign objects in food could be due to multiple factors. Currently, they are unable to attribute specific responsibility to any party, so they cannot offer a formal apology to the customer but are willing to provide a "return one, compensate ten" compensation.

Mr. Ma rejected this plan from the manufacturer, stating, "I don't need any economic compensation from the manufacturer; what I need is a formal apology from them. Due to their handling of this matter, it has wasted years of my trust in this brand!"

Linked lawyer Li Yanhui from Hunan Renren Law Firm frankly stated that regarding current domestic regulations related to food safety, it is difficult for consumers to protect their rights when they find foreign objects in packaged foods.

Lawyer: Difficult to protect rights after opening packaged problematic foods

He mentioned that "not testing opened items" is a regulation for all food testing institutions. The National Food Quality Supervision and Inspection Center once told the media: "If the packaging or beverages have been opened, we cannot determine whether the product is originally produced by the factory or whether it has been contaminated after being opened."

"Without the identification results from national quality inspection institutions, it is very difficult for consumers to win lawsuits through legal means." However, Lawyer Li reminded numerous food manufacturers to treat consumer complaints kindly: "When consumers buy your products and discover quality issues and complain, it is often because they like and trust your product."
