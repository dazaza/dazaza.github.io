● A rap MV uploaded online attracted nearly ten thousand netizens within an hour. The singers were not stars but ordinary police officers from Qionglai.

Following the viral success of the rap song "People in My Heart" in 2010, which made it to CCTV, yesterday afternoon, the second Sichuan dialect rap "Qionglai Police・Xiongqi" created by Qionglai Public Security Bureau officers Liu Jin, He Jiajing, and others once again garnered praise from netizens.

Regarding this Sichuan dialect rap, Liu Jin said, "Our idea is very simple, we just want everyone to understand the police and our work. It has nothing to do with hype."

The hip-hop rap video created by the police has gained immense popularity online.

At 5 PM yesterday, Ms. Tian was browsing the web when a friend sent her a video link saying, "The rapping police have released a second one, they're really talented, take a look."

Ms. Tian opened the video and saw a police badge accompanied by the chorus "Devotion, Duty, Fairness, Livelihood" gradually zooming in. Then, traffic police directing traffic, community police handling documents for the public, and frontline firefighters all appeared. More interestingly, after a segment, the lead singer would shout with his comrades, "People's police, Xiongqi! Qionglai's police, Xiongqi!"

"This is quite fashionable, even rapping, keeping up with the times." After watching the 4 minute and 6 second video, Ms. Tian exclaimed and quickly shared it on her Weibo. At this point, the video had been uploaded for less than an hour and already had nearly ten thousand views.

This marks the second time the Qionglai police have gained fame, showcasing the spirit and demeanor of the police force.

Ms. Tian didn't know that this was already the second rap produced by the Qionglai police. Despite having become famous once before, Liu Jin, the main creator of "Qionglai Police・Xiongqi," didn't expect the second rap to receive such a strong response.

Liu Jin introduced that this video was prepared for the bureau's Spring Festival party, "We wanted to use the video to showcase the spirit and demeanor of the Qionglai police."

However, when the lyrics were submitted, everyone liked them, and the leadership felt that it could serve as the basis for a sequel to the previous rap. Subsequently, Liu Jin began revising the lyrics repeatedly, "Highlighting the atmosphere and enhancing the rhythm." Meanwhile, He Jiajing was busy arranging for colleagues to make cameo appearances and finding external cameramen.

In their spare time, it took them less than a week to complete the project. Just after the New Year, "Qionglai Police・Xiongqi" was born.

Afraid of being accused of hype, they waited a month before uploading it online.

In mid-January, this unique video aired at the Qionglai City Spring Festival Gala and the Qionglai Public Security Bureau's Spring Festival gathering, receiving widespread acclaim.

But He Jiajing and Liu Jin did not upload the video online, "Nowadays, more emphasis is placed on police image building, and we also feared being accused of hype."

It wasn't until 3 PM yesterday that He Jiajing finally uploaded the video online. At 4 PM, the Qionglai Public Security Bureau's official Weibo account "Safe Qionglai" reposted it. Despite maintaining a low profile, the video still sparked a viewing frenzy on Sina Weibo and various forums.

"As with the previous one, we still want to use an easy-to-understand method to promote the Qionglai police, get closer to the people, and it's absolutely not hype," He Jiajing reiterated, otherwise they would have uploaded the video much earlier.

Interview with the Main Creators

Rapping is a waste of time? Created during off-hours without affecting work.

Yesterday afternoon, the reporter interviewed two of the main creators of "Qionglai Police・Xiongqi" - police officers Liu Jin and He Jiajing.

Reporter: Why did you choose to sing in Sichuan dialect again?

He Jiajing: This is closest to our real work and life, and it also has more characteristics of Qionglai.

Reporter: Some say that police doing rap is idling away time, what do you think?

Liu Jin: I am an ordinary criminal investigation police officer, writing songs and composing music is just a hobby, done during my spare time, it hasn't affected my work. Actually, when I was studying at Xihua University, I formed a rap band with classmates.

Reporter: Will the next work have to wait until next year to see?

Liu Jin: Work is usually very busy, so new songs come out slowly. I am currently conceptualizing a new song called "Police Also Miss Home," which is expected to be completed in the first half of the year. The previous two works showcased the demeanor of the police, while this one hopes to let everyone understand the ordinary life of the police.
