Crackdown on scalpers by multiple parties creates order in ticketing affairs

by jssscjzwds on 2009-12-01 22:40:21

Voice-over: In the past, when the Spring Festival travel rush begins, it is also the time when some scalpers start to stir. How is this year's ticket market order? Zhang, a student from Yancheng Technician College, came to Yancheng Railway Station early in the morning to buy tickets home to prevent harassment from scalpers. However, the situation this year was out of Zhang's expectation.

同期声：Zhang Student: "We came here to buy tickets and it was quite smooth. There were no special circumstances in between. After queuing up, we could buy tickets immediately."

Like Zhang, many citizens have the same feeling. The rampant "scalpers" of the past seem to have disappeared this year.

同期声：Citizen: "Now there is no selling of high-priced tickets at the train station. It is very regular and well-organized."

This is inseparable from the multiple preventive measures of the station police.

同期声：Police Officer Ma Gangqiang on duty: "The station police station mainly takes the following aspects to crack down on ticket scalpers and yellow cows. First, increase the intensity of publicity, set up a hotline for reporting, so that the majority of passengers recognize the importance of cracking down on ticket scalpers from all aspects. We have established a ticket source investigation point at the station. As long as passengers buy high-priced tickets, we will definitely find out."

With these two "treasures" - the ticket source investigation point and the hotline for reporting, scalpers naturally have nowhere to hide. Of course, railway police also remind the majority of passengers to actively cooperate with the station work and jointly maintain the ticket order.

同期声：Police Officer Ma Gangqiang on duty: "For the high-priced tickets you bought, you must report to us or call the reporting hotline to notify us. We will certainly protect their privacy. For those who report meritoriously, we will give certain rewards. We hope that the majority of passengers will actively cooperate with our work."

Editorial: With the close cooperation of passengers and stations, passenger travel will be more convenient.
