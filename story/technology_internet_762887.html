Tencent and Guangdong Unicom launch "WeChat data plan", available on August 8th

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 17:55:02

Yesterday's rumors about the cooperation between WeChat and Guangdong Unicom as "good buddies" have been verified today. The timing was carefully chosen - at 8:08 AM, Tencent announced on its YiXun website a collaboration with China Unicom to launch the "WeChat Wo" package. According to YiXun, the official start date for this collaboration is August 8, 2013.

In the official introduction, it is stated that "WeChat Wo is more than just a SIM card." Users choosing the "WeChat Wo" package will be gifted more data, enjoy WeChat privileges, and can select premium Unicom numbers ending in "88".

Globally, there are many internet companies (referred to as "OTT companies" in China) collaborating with telecom operators, such as Line with Docomo in Japan, Google Voice with Sprint in the US, and Spotify with Deutsche Telekom in Germany, etc. However, "WeChat Wo" sets a precedent: allowing users to choose the form of service provided by the internet company and the telecom operator.

Tencent did not announce specific details of the collaboration with the operator but instead let users choose the required "WeChat privileges." Among the available privileges are:

Group chat limit expanded from 40 to 60 people

Additional data provided

WeChat Pay discounts

Free games

Free emoticons

Free music downloads

Free HD movie viewing

HD image sharing

Easier visibility to "people nearby" (if desired)

Approximately 23,000 people have already participated in customizing their WeChat privileges. Based on this model, the "WeChat Wo" package launched by Tencent and Guangdong Unicom will provide WeChat privileges based on popularity. It is expected that Tencent may offer multiple privileges rather than just one. Of course, users can also request all privileges.

The launch of the "WeChat Wo" package is scheduled for August 8, 22 days from now. From today until August 4, the YiXun page will allow users to "like" and choose their desired privileges; August 5 to 7 will be for pre-orders; and sales will begin on August 8.

According to previous reports from Phoenix News, WeChat might offer WeChat privileges on top of Guangdong Unicom's existing 36 yuan Wo Pai package aimed at student users. However, it remains unclear whether the "WeChat package" is just a small-scale trial or if it will be extended to more users after enthusiastic participation.

This Monday, we exclusively reported that WeChat would collaborate with mainland operators to launch customized services. At the time, based on overseas cases, we speculated that the collaboration could involve data packages, exclusive services, or call discounts, but the depth of the collaboration was unclear. Currently, we still need to wait some time to know what kind of package they will collaborate on and whether a separate "WeChat package" will be launched.

Given that the announcement of the "WeChat Wo package" was made through Tencent's e-commerce channel - YiXun, it implies that both parties will cooperate in specific business sales. Officially, "WeChat Wo" will issue physical SIM cards, and we have reason to believe that buyers won't be limited to students but will also include loyal WeChat users. After all, WeChat data usage has likely become an important part of their data expenses.

To reiterate, as a user, I am pleased to see WeChat collaborate with operators. While the privilege features may not be something I urgently need, bundling WeChat data into a package is something I do need.

Finally, there is one detail worth discussing: among the available "WeChat privileges," there is a "free emoticon" option. Although the emoticons shown on YiXun's official Weibo account are still traditional free ones, the mention of "free" suggests the existence of paid emoticons. This means that the WeChat 5.0 version, which will feature paid emoticons, will be released no later than August 8. The rest is up to everyone to uncover.
