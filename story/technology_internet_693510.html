How to treat thickening of endometritis?

by mofh587 on 2012-03-06 16:21:59

What are the treatment methods for thickened endometritis? 1. Remove residual materials and other foreign bodies in the uterine cavity: If there is suspicion of placental tissue residue, it should be removed immediately while using antibiotics. However, it is preferable to gently remove the residual material in the uterine cavity rather than performing a curettage. A curettage can be performed when the antibiotics have reached a certain dosage and the inflammation is under control, so as to prevent the spread of inflammation. If there is active bleeding in the uterus, the uterine cavity can be cleaned under the application of large amounts of antibiotics.

What are the treatment methods for thickened endometritis? 2. Cervical dilation, drainage, and estrogen therapy: For thickened endometritis and senile thickened endometritis, cervical dilation can be used as a complementary treatment method, which helps with the drainage of secretions from the uterine cavity and removes the cause. Elderly patients can also use a small amount of estrogen during this treatment method.

What are the treatment methods for thickened endometritis? 3. Patients with acute thickened endometritis should rest in bed, preferably in a semi-reclining position, which helps localize the inflammation and drain secretions from the uterine cavity; patients with chronic thickened endometritis can apply heat to the lower abdomen to promote the absorption of inflammation and relieve pain. It's important to maintain smooth bowel movements to reduce pelvic congestion and help with toxin excretion.
