I want to compose a song about a youth without regrets.

by huiming on 2012-01-08 22:55:32

I want to compose a song of youth without regrets

When the ending is indifferently laid before my eyes

Tears gently stream down pale cheeks

The book of youth has nowhere to write

Is the afterglow of the sunset, when it's fading away

Will it leave behind a tear of helplessness?

- Inscription

Time stumbles, hiding how many vows, overthrowing how much fiery passion? The distant scenery is as beautiful as flowers; how many people willingly get drunk in front of it and refuse to wake up. Tossing and turning under the moonlight by the flowers countless times, but never paying attention to whether our decadent youth can match even a single blade of grass among the myriad flowers?

Watching the sun gradually approach the horizon, dyeing a patch of air on the coast red. For no reason, I feel short of breath. Is it fear of the night invading the sky or dread of memories filling my mind? Truly, I have no strength left to struggle. In front of society, I may just be an insignificant slave. The only comfort is that my thoughts are still free.

The wind lightly brushes against my face, while tears quietly spread. There will always be a rainbow after the rain, so will this tear of mine, after being dried by the wind, gain the sublimation of the soul? "Now I know all about sorrow, wanting to speak but holding back. Wanting to speak but holding back, and saying instead what a cool autumn it is." How can the sorrow at this moment be explained with just one word of regret?

Singing a song of youth, what comes out is a mottled sense of regret. Searching and searching, looking back at the past, I realize that in those good times, I did so many foolish things that I regret for life. Now, knowing well that time cannot be reversed, how does regretting not help?

At this moment, in this silent and soundless night, too many regrets are active in my heart, gathering deep within my soul into a poster of youth, outlining every little mistake I've made. Kneeling before regret, cawing like a crow, but no matter how, I can't call back the colors of youth.

Through the passage of time, drifting and bumping along, I've missed so many beautiful dreams. In the mountains and fields, the gentle sound of a flute rises, its rhythm coming from afar, embracing the breeze, carrying the night, flowing drop by drop into my body. In the distance, who else is also lamenting their lost youth alone?

Only when dusk fills the sky do I realize that youth is also gradually growing old. At this point, even a calm mindset becomes silently disordered. Leaving behind those bitter tears, when will they be buried by the dust of time, when will they disappear from my memory? Is that last tear one of helplessness or regret?

With my stranded youth, I want to use my last tear to commemorate those regrets. If time could turn back, I would also want to compose a song of youth without regrets!
