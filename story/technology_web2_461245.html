The retired Jianjiao-6 aircraft shell was temporarily seized by relevant departments. Mr. Qin provided the photo.

It's not uncommon for cars on the street to be stopped and inspected by the police. However, if a real airplane is stopped on the street, it’s quite rare. The night before last, the Fengdu traffic patrol police received a report from the public and intercepted a long-distance truck from another province on Binjiang Road in the county town. The truck was carrying the fuselage of a retired and scrapped Jian-6 trainer aircraft. What exactly happened?

Police received a report from the public

Someone was smuggling an airplane under cover of darkness

At around 7 p.m. the previous evening, Fengdu's 110 emergency service received a report that a Henan-registered extra-long trailer truck was transporting an airplane from Shuanglu Town in Fengdu County towards the county town, with its destination unknown. Not long after, the traffic patrol police intercepted the large truck near Hui Feng International Travel Agency on East Binjiang Road.

Witness Mr. Qin said that the truck, registered as Yu B66318, was marked with the words "Kaifeng Second Transport Corporation," and the trailer had no covering. With the help of streetlights, one could clearly see the full appearance of the airplane: the plane had its tail wing and both side wings removed, measuring over 10 meters in length and about 3 meters wide, without visible landing gear or engines. The cockpit's control equipment positions were all empty, just an empty shell of a fuselage.

Mr. Qin said that aviation enthusiast friends identified this airplane as the long-retired Jian-6 trainer.

Where did the plane come from? Both the driver and the accompanying security personnel claimed that the shell of this scrapped airplane had legal procedures, but the documents were in the hands of the owner. Later, when the airplane's owner arrived on the scene, they provided the relevant documentation. Due to exceeding their jurisdictional authority, the traffic patrol police handed the matter over to the local armed forces department for handling.

Seven years parked in a farmhouse courtyard

The plane was transported away that day

In the morning yesterday, following directions from informed individuals, reporters located a farmhouse courtyard at No. 4 Social Group, Duanyue Village, Shuanglu Town, Fengdu County, by the roadside.

"The plane was already transported away yesterday." The homeowner Ms. Xiang pointed to the rusted steel pipes piled up in the yard and told the reporter: This plane had been parked in her courtyard for more than seven years, earning her 200 yuan per month as a caretaking fee. In May 2003, the plane was delivered by a crane and three large trucks. The owner of the plane was a local person, a retired soldier, who had legitimate holding procedures.

Ms. Xiang said, "Having the plane parked here has caused me quite a bit of trouble; the police and the armed forces department have both come to investigate." People coming to gawk even knocked down a section of her wall.

Ms. Xiang said that in the afternoon of the previous day, the plane's owner, Mr. Zhang, brought a crane and large trucks to haul the plane away. "He said he would transport it to another place to continue exhibitions, allowing it to serve further purposes. You should quickly contact him; the plane may have already left Fengdu." Ms. Xiang provided the reporter with Mr. Zhang's contact information.

Legally purchased from the military

Someone offered 4 million yuan to buy it

The plane's owner, Mr. Zhang, told reporters that he had spent 18 years in the military, always working in ground crew services, maintaining and managing fighter jets, forming a deep bond with J-6 fighters and other combat aircraft. After retiring, he settled in his birthplace of Fengdu, with the intention of establishing a museum themed around military aircraft. He submitted multiple reports, gaining support from relevant departments in Fengdu County and Chongqing Municipality, and through complex procedures, obtained support from the military. It took him two years to finalize the procedures and acquire this retired J-6 trainer aircraft shell from the military.

Mr. Zhang said that he used three large trucks and spent over 7,000 yuan in transportation fees to bring the plane from Dazu to Fengdu, where it was exhibited for several months on Fengdu's Binjiang Road.

"From Wulong, Wushan, Jiangbei, and other places, people have come to me to buy the plane, with the highest offer reaching 4 million yuan," Mr. Zhang said. His child has already started a family, and he himself receives a retirement pension, so he is not short of money. Therefore, he has never agreed to sell it.

Prepared to transport to downtown Chongqing

Wash, assemble, and exhibit

The person who persuaded Mr. Zhang to transport the plane was a retired soldier settled in Chongqing. This unnamed retired soldier said he planned to transport the plane to downtown Chongqing, first cleaning and reassembling the wings, then painting it to create a patriotic education base. Additionally, he had legally acquired shells of decommissioned tanks and other equipment.

His idea resonated with Mr. Zhang. Mr. Zhang said, "I didn't expect there to be so many troubles during the transportation process. We are continuing to seek support from relevant departments, hoping for early clearance." Regarding the final location of the patriotic education base, both parties stated that it had not yet been finalized.

In the afternoon, reporters went to the Fengdu County Armed Forces Department to inquire about the procedures required for holding decommissioned military aircraft and related transportation. Relevant individuals said that this was the first time they encountered the transfer of an airplane, and they were currently seeking instructions from higher-level departments. Before a decision is made, the plane cannot be released for now.

Reporters consulted relevant departments and learned that in general, special equipment like airplanes being transported on city roads requires prior application to the transportation management department, followed by transportation according to designated times and routes.

Jianjiao-6:

China's first supersonic trainer aircraft

The Jianjiao-6, based on the supersonic J-6 fighter jet, was developed by the Shenyang Aircraft Manufacturing Plant. Research began in 1966, with its first flight in November 1970 and production ceasing in 1986. A total of 624 units were produced. It was China's first supersonic trainer aircraft in history, with performance far superior to previously used jet trainers. Currently, the Jianjiao-6 has been honorably retired.

Baxian Middle School once received a gift of a Jianjiao-6

In September 2007, a container truck carrying a Jianjiao-6 fighter jet arrived at Baxian Middle School. This aircraft was transported from a military airport in Kunming and was a "school anniversary gift" donated by a certain air force unit to Baxian Middle School for exhibition and enlightenment teaching.
