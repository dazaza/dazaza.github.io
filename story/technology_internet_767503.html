Amazon reportedly plans to team up with HTC to develop a smartphone

by anonymous on 2013-10-19 11:56:22

According to well-informed sources, Amazon is planning to collaborate with HTC to develop a series of smartphones, aiming to better compete with Apple and Google.

The Financial Times website reported on Tuesday that one source said Amazon and HTC are discussing the development of three smartphones, one of which has already entered an advanced stage of development. However, another source warned that the release schedule for this smartphone has been changed, and Amazon may decide not to release it.

Rumors about Amazon entering the smartphone market have been circulating for nearly two years. If Amazon decides to proceed with this project, this smartphone is unlikely to be released this year but rather possibly in 2014.

Amazon declined to comment on this news. In response to another recent report about its mobile business plans, Amazon stated that it "has no plans to launch a phone this year." HTC also refused to comment on the news, but the company's Chief Marketing Officer, Jerry Shi, said that HTC "is always exploring new opportunities."

Shi remarked: "We have always focused significantly on building our brand, but we are also very open to collaborations with carriers and other tech brands."

Selecting HTC to help develop smartphones means that Amazon's strategy largely resembles Google's, which has collaborated with brands like Asus and LG to develop its own Nexus tablet and smartphone series.

Industry observers pointed out that selling smartphones will help Amazon foster higher user loyalty because consumers are increasingly using smartphones and tablets for shopping activities. Currently, Apple and Google dominate the smartphone market, and both companies have launched online book and music stores.

In 2011, Amazon first introduced the Kindle Fire tablet, which was upgraded last month. This product runs on a customized version of Google's Android mobile operating system.

Amazon has been developing Kindle devices at its secret design laboratory, Lab 126, located in California, USA, but production is handled by contract manufacturers. For this Seattle-based online retailer, collaborating with renowned device manufacturers like HTC would be a first.

HTC’s history of collaboration with Amazon dates back to when the company was designing mobile devices for others such as Palm. Later, HTC became an independent brand, but recently it has struggled with marketing and retail skills, making it difficult to compete with companies like Apple and Samsung and capture consumer attention. Earlier this month, HTC reported its financial results showing the company's first quarterly loss.

This is not HTC's first collaboration with a more prominent internet brand in hopes of increasing its own recognition and sales. Earlier this year, HTC launched the so-called "Facebook Phone" HTC First, but despite its deep integration of Facebook features, this relatively affordable hardware did not become a hot-selling product.

However, analysts and sources close to HTC revealed that collaborating with Amazon would be a good strategy as it would allow HTC to focus on product design, where it relatively excels.
