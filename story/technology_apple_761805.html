by geekzhang on 2013-05-10 18:22:17

The iPhone and iPad are global bestsellers. However, after the CCTV 315 Gala exposed Apple Inc.'s "back cover gate" incident this year, problems hidden behind its glossy sales performance came to light.Yesterday afternoon, the China Consumer Rights Protection Law Research Association (hereinafter referred to as the Consumer Law Research Association) held a seminar on Apple's illegal business practices. President He Shan revealed that the back cover gate was just one of Apple's after-sales service issues, but according to investigations by relevant departments, Apple's App Store had multiple illegal problems such as tax evasion, infringement, and involvement in pornography.Accused of Tax Evasion: Never Paid Taxes for Hundreds of Thousands of Chinese ApplicationsMentioning the App Store, many Apple fans are familiar with it. It is a service platform created by Apple Inc. for its iPhone, iPad, and other products, also pioneering a new business model for mobile software. Users who purchase iPhones or iPads must download relevant applications from the App Store if they want to post on Weibo, play games, or watch online videos.Due to the hot sales of iPhones and iPads, the application downloads from the App Store are equally astonishing. On January 8, 2013, Apple announced that the number of App Store downloads had exceeded 40 billion times, half of which were completed in 2012. President He Shan of the Consumer Law Research Association uncovered another serious illegal business practice by Apple: tax evasion.China has a large number of Apple users, and Apple not only makes money through hardware sales but also earns a considerable amount through paid downloads on the App Store. There are hundreds of thousands of Chinese applications on the App Store, yet the revenue from these sales has never been taxed by Chinese customs, said He Shan. He Shan further explained that according to civil law, the application software on the App Store falls within the scope of goods. According to China's Customs Law, import duties must be paid on imported goods.Apple Inc. is the taxpayer for customs duties, but Apple uses the internet to bypass Chinese customs and directly reach Apple mobile phone users, extensively selling Chinese fee-based applications within China, thereby evading taxes. This essentially constitutes a serious smuggling act, said Cao Sanming, Vice President of the Consumer Law Research Association and Deputy Dean of the National Judges College. Cao Sanming stated that while Apple's products allow Chinese consumers to experience advanced technology and innovative consumption models, we cannot turn a blind eye to the suspected criminal activities involved.Cao Sanming called on administrative and judicial authorities to investigate Apple's illegal business operations and even suspected criminal acts based on Chinese laws, to protect the rights of Chinese consumers and uphold the dignity of the law.Infringement of Copyright: Online Store Frequently Sued, Apple Continuously Loses CasesMeanwhile, the infringement and illegal issues of the App Store have begun to surface, leading to Apple being repeatedly sued. In November 2012, Apple Inc. was first ruled by a Chinese judicial authority to constitute an infringement due to an App Store application. The Encyclopedia of China Publishing House discovered numerous unauthorized applications containing content from the "Encyclopedia of China" available for download on the App Store, allowing iPhone and iPad users to read them. Thus, they sued Apple for infringement and claimed compensation.The Beijing No. 2 Intermediate People's Court ruled in the first instance that Apple should cease the infringement and compensate the Encyclopedia of China Publishing House for economic losses amounting to 520,000 yuan. Not long ago, Apple lost another case due to infringement. Plaintiffs Motie Shu Meng Company, Mai Jia, and Yu Zhuo claimed that they held the copyrights for works such as "Those Things About the Ming Dynasty," "The Whisper," "The Sound," "Decryption," and "Cadre on Assignment," but found pirated versions on the App Store. The court determined that Apple was at fault and sentenced them to pay tens of thousands of yuan in damages.These cases are just the tip of the iceberg; the Apple App Store contains a large number of infringing books. Due to the high cost and long duration of litigation, only a small number of authors have filed lawsuits in court. He Shan stated that Apple must abide by international copyright conventions, comply with China's Copyright Law, protect the rights of Chinese authors, and remove infringing pirated content.Pornography: A Large Number of Pornographic Applications Suspected of Criminal OffensesBesides piracy, some books provided for download on the App Store contain a large amount of pornographic content. Some books have consistently ranked in the top ten on the Apple Store charts, such as "Collection of One Hundred Ancient and Modern Erotic Novels," "Yufang Secrets," and "Erotic Novels Teacher Series." said He Shan.Li Weimin, Deputy Secretary-General of the Consumer Law Research Association, told reporters that teenagers make up a significant proportion of Apple users, and the harm caused by obscene and pornographic content to their physical and mental health is evident. Applications are developed by third parties, and profits are obtained through user-paid downloads, generally divided between Apple and developers in a 3:7 ratio. Books containing pornographic content have a higher rate of paid downloads, and Apple adopts a laissez-faire attitude, said Li Weimin.Kang Shuhua, professor of law at Peking University, said that refusing to spread obscene, pornographic, and violent content to minors is a consensus among all countries around the world. According to China's Criminal Law, Apple's actions go beyond the concept of illegal business operations and are suspected of constituting the crime of profiting from the dissemination of obscene materials. Li Weimin believes that although the App Store server is located in the United States, the place where the crime was committed and the location where the consequences occurred are within China, so Chinese police have criminal jurisdiction. Apple is suspected of corporate crime, and the police have the authority to conduct investigations.
