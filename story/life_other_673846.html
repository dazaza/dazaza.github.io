Proliferate like flowers, who can share my sorrow of parting without complaint

1

Every day, different people come and go around me,

　　some familiar, some not.

　　Wearing earphones, standing in the middle of a crowd, everyone rushes by.

　　I look up at the gloomy sky in confusion.

　　It's going to rain soon, isn't it?

　　I slowly move my steps towards the opposite street.

2

　　Hua always asks me one question.

　　She says: Are you still half-asleep?

　　It takes me several seconds each time to focus my eyes and then I slowly reply: No.

　　She holds my face and says: Why are you always so slow? Just like you haven't woken up yet.

　　I rub my cheek against her palm, lower my eyelids, and mutter: I don't know either. Then my eyes regain their extremely tired and lifeless appearance.

　　She hugs me and whispers something I didn't hear clearly. Then I quietly fall asleep in her arms.

3

　　My left hand holds Hua's, while my right hand is sipping carbon roasted pearl milk tea.

　　I drag Hua slowly into the park.

　　Then we sit on a stone bench.

　　I like Hua. Hua is always gentle. She is kind to everyone.

　　Hua has many companions and many admirers.

　　But I only have Hua as my companion. Only Hua likes me.

　　Perhaps, Hua doesn't even like me.

　　I lean my head on Hua's shoulder, lower my eyelids halfway, hiding the trace of mockery in my eyes.

4

　　Sitting on the floor of the balcony, I recall a question I once asked Hua.

　　I asked Hua, will you leave me too? Like they did.

　　Hua said, no.

　　I pick up my phone and call Hua.

　　"Sorry, the number you dialed cannot be connected temporarily, please try again later..."

　　My gaze slowly focuses as I search for another person's number.

　　"Hello." "This is Xiao Shu, where is Hua?" "...You don't know yet?" "What should I know?" "Something happened to Hua, she's in XX Hospital..."

　　Something happened to Hua, something happened to Hua, something happened to Hua... The phone falls to the floor, my pupils suddenly contract, my mind repeatedly echoing Xiaolei's voice, unable to believe what I've heard.

　　I stumble down the stairs, hailing a taxi to the hospital where Hua is.

5

　　Looking at Hua lying on the hospital bed as if sleeping,

　　with a pale face, I slowly walk to Hua's bedside and hold her hand,

　　feeling somewhere in my chest blocked, making it hard to breathe,

　　I press Hua's cold hand against my face, softly murmuring,

　　You promised not to leave me, you promised to always stay with me, you promised to always be with your Xiao Shu.

　　How could you, leave me first.

　　How could you, break this promise so easily.

　　Darkness gradually envelops my vision, then everything goes blank in my memory.

6

　　Standing in front of a stone bench in the park,

　　struggling to focus my scattered gaze,

　　frowning, a scene flashes through my mind but I can't grasp it, trying to recall, unexpectedly causing a hammer-like pain in my head.

　　A boy runs over, Xiao Shu, Xiao Shu, what's wrong with you,

　　No, I just have a terrible headache. Did I come here with someone before? I feel like I've forgotten something important...

　　Don't worry, let's go home.

　　Alright, good.

-Epilogue.

　　I have always been standing on the edge of collapse.

　　Just waiting for someone to give me that final push,

　　and I would collapse forever.
