Humorous Website Cheezburger Hires Ex-Yahoo, Microsoft Executive as President

by anonymous on 2013-09-08 19:42:05

The American humor website Cheezburger reported that the company has hired the industry-renowned former Yahoo and Microsoft executive Scott Moore as its new president and COO (Chief Operating Officer).

Cheezburger issued a statement saying that after joining the company, Moore will "be responsible for expanding the Cheezburger brand, which is increasingly becoming an important participant in the digital media and entertainment industries." Moore will be in charge of products, technology, sales, marketing, and account management, directly reporting to Ben Huh, the founder of Cheezburger and current CEO. Interestingly, Moore will also serve as a director of the company. It is speculated that Moore will also be responsible for enhancing the business prospects of this company.

Cheezburger is headquartered in Seattle and is known for its "I Can Has Cheezburger?" website. Although Cheezburger has garnered industry attention, it hasn't grown significantly so far. Cheezburger claims that users currently upload more than 15,000 pieces of humorous content daily, but its monthly unique user visits on desktop and mobile in the United States are only 7 million. However, Cheezburger has raised $69 million in funding from companies such as Foundry Group, SoftBank Capital, Avalon Ventures, and Madrona Venture Group.

Moore's most recent position was at the business service provider Hibu, where he was responsible for the company's digital business. Prior to this, he was in charge of operating Microsoft's MSN portal and also served as the media director of Yahoo. Additionally, Moore has served as the president of MSNBC.com and the publisher of the American online magazine Slate. In an interview Wednesday evening, Huh stated that Moore possesses deep professional experience in the digital and media industries, which is the reason Cheezburger hired him as president and COO.

Huh said, "Cheezburger has always aimed to become a mainstream entertainment site. For us, having a leader who understands both the digital and media industries is very important."

Moore expressed that Cheezburger's humor content is what interested him. He believes that Cheezburger's user-generated content, with its social, mobile, and family-oriented nature, will attract advertisers' attention. He pointed out, "Cheezburger's content is highly suitable for promotion via mobile devices, offering great potential for growth."
