What to do if toothache prevents you from eating?

Author: Station Master Source: Internet Date: November 27, 2011 Time: 5:16:18 PM Clicks: 2362

What to do when encountering a toothache? The first thing to solve is the eating problem. If you skip one meal, it doesn't matter much, but going a whole day without eating can be unbearable, let alone having to endure the pain of a toothache! Therefore, don't tough it out when you have a toothache; try to eat as much as possible. Having proper nutrition boosts your resistance and helps promote the body's immune system to fight off diseases.

If the toothache makes eating difficult, consider the following options for drinks:

One, Porridge

Porridge is easy to chew and digest, making it an ideal food for someone with a toothache. It's also very convenient to cook at home. Make sure to simmer it over low heat for longer to make it thicker. If possible, add ingredients like Job's tears, lotus seeds, and red beans along with rice to increase the nutritional value and help the body recover faster.

Recommended: Nutritious Job's Tears, Lotus Seeds, and Red Bean Porridge

Job's tears is a great supplement that strengthens the spleen and stomach, nourishes the lungs, clears heat, removes wind-dampness, and benefits oral health without causing harm.

Lotus seeds are good for replenishing deficiencies in the five internal organs, promoting smooth blood flow in the twelve meridians, ensuring uncorrupted circulation of qi and blood.

Two, Silver Ear Fungus Soup

Silver ear fungus is often called the "common man's bird's nest." When toothache makes eating inconvenient, drinking more silver ear soup can be beneficial.

Three, Radix Adenophorae Stewed with Eggs

Use 30 grams of radix adenophorae and two eggs. Add two bowls of water and cook together. After the eggs are cooked, remove the shells and continue cooking for another half hour. Add an appropriate amount of rock sugar or white sugar, then drink the soup and eat the eggs.

Even though toothaches are uncomfortable, it's important to keep eating to strengthen your resistance and aid in recovery. If the toothache is caused by excessive heat (fire) in the body, you can eat more preserved duck eggs.
