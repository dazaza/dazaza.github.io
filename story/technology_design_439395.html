This means that spleen deficiency is the pathological mechanism of peptic ulcers in TCM, which is the essential factor causing ulcers. Based on this, experts, adhering to the principle of TCM that treatment must address the root cause, treat peptic ulcer patients not only with Western medicine for anti-ulcer and anti-Helicobacter pylori but also add spleen-strengthening and qi-enhancing Chinese herbs based on TCM differentiation. The clinical application results show improved quality of ulcer healing and reduced recurrence rates.

Combining advanced diagnostic technologies from Western medicine, it emphasizes the contradictions of yin-yang deficiency and excess in the body, identifies the causes and lesions, providing scientifically reliable diagnostic evidence for clinical treatment. Through balancing yin and yang, adjusting deficiencies and excesses, unblocking meridians, nourishing blood and qi, eradicating the source of the disease, curing the lesion, regulating immunity, and restoring normal bodily functions.

Since TCM treats diseases with an overall concept, according to the holistic view of TCM, the individual's disease is organically combined with nature, environment, climate, and different manifestations of individual diseases.
