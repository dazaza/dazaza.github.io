8-Year-Old Girl Lives with Sick Grandpa after Relatives Passed Away Sequentially (Photo)

by foot2050 on 2012-02-21 09:01:12

Xiaocaoli and Her Mad Grandpa, Xiaocaoli's Simple Home

Key Points:

Five years ago, when Xiaocaoli was not yet four years old, her mother left her quietly and disappeared without a trace. The family was left with a severely ill grandmother and a mad grandfather. Two months after the mother's departure, the father died unexpectedly. Two months ago, the grandmother who had been paralyzed for six long years also passed away...

On February 17th, in Liangzi Village of Shuangshan Village, Zhaile Township, Nayong County, there was an "extraordinary" household. The house was messy and dirty, with two beds placed inside. On a short stool by the wall between the beds sat a man in his fifties, his hands pitch black, his face covered in filth, his clothes so dirty they were indescribable, like armor. His pupils emitted a strange light, his lips twitched as he mumbled incomprehensible words. This man was Caoli, an eight-year-old girl's deaf, mute, and mad grandfather, Cao Qibing.

The Gloomy Collapse of a Small Family

Zhang Feiqin is Xiaocaoli's neighbor. She told reporters: "When Xiaocaoli was four years old, her mother, seeing no way out of their dire situation, cruelly left quietly, leaving behind a mad grandfather and a bedridden grandmother. Xiaocaoli's father, Cao Dexun, two months after his wife's departure, one noon, suddenly fell off the bed and hit his head, creating a small wound. After convulsing, he passed away."

From then on, this family only had a paralyzed but mentally sound grandmother. Lying in bed, she would direct the young Xiaocaoli to do household chores with her mouth. Now, Xiaocaoli is over eight years old and still hasn't started school. When the children in the village go to school, this little girl wanders alone in the village, pitiful indeed.

Neighbor Elder Li Fujie said that for many years, Xiaocaoli and her grandparents relied on low-income assistance to get by. Originally, with the grandmother managing the home while being mentally sound, now that the grandmother has passed away, the grandfather behaves erratically, sometimes chasing Xiaocaoli around, hitting and scolding her, which is quite terrifying.

The Fire Goes Out, Food Runs Out

Elder Li Fuijie said that when Xiaocaoli's father, Cao Dexun, was still alive, he planted watermelons in the fields. However, later, due to owing fertilizer money, the land was taken by the creditor to be farmed for a year as debt repayment. By the time it was supposed to be returned, Cao Dexun was already deceased. For this family, which has completely lost its labor force, the land has become meaningless. Although Cao Qibing has another son, due to genetic issues, this younger son is also disoriented, unable to work or follow instructions, and no one knows where he has gone.

Tian Xinghui is a warm-hearted person in the village who often visits this unfortunate family. She said: "When Xiaocaoli's grandfather goes mad, he smashes pots, bowls, and even grabs knives, chasing Xiaocaoli around hitting and scolding her. Since the grandmother passed away, this child, out of fear, runs to her great-grandmother’s house to eat and play, returning home only at night to sleep."

Villagers who host banquets remember to send leftovers to this grandfather and granddaughter pair.

When Xiaocaoli's grandmother passed away, the government provided 1500 yuan to handle funeral arrangements, along with the low-income assistance money, barely enough to bury the grandmother.

Currently, Cao Qibing has about a hundred pounds of coal and half a bag of rice left. It won’t be long before the fire goes out, and food runs out.

Grandfather and Granddaughter Supporting Each Other

Elder Li Fuijie said: "Xiaocaoli's grandfather often picks up leftover yellow vegetable leaves and rotten fruits from others' stalls to eat. The low-income savings book is now managed by a relative named Tian Hua, who withdraws the money every month and delivers it to Cao Qibing. However, Cao Qibing's mind doesn't function properly, and he spends the money indiscriminately, without any plan."

Villagers all know that these grandfather and granddaughter are mutually dependent. If the elder one disappears, the younger one searches everywhere, and if the younger one disappears, the elder one does the same. When neighbors come to visit, only Xiaocaoli can understand her grandfather's words and acts as a translator. Cao Qibing mutters to himself, laughing loudly sometimes and crying loudly other times, tears streaming down his face.

Elder Li Fuijie said that although Cao Qibing has two half-brothers who often visit, they moved down to Caojiazhai near the land. Xiaocaoli's great-grandparents are still alive, both nearly eighty years old and needing care. With their sons and daughters-in-law working outside, they must take care of their own grandchildren. They are struggling just to manage themselves, let alone taking care of this grandfather and granddaughter pair!

Xiaocaoli Says, "I Want to Study"

Neighbors told reporters that sometimes the neighboring dogs also come to visit Xiaocaoli and her mad grandfather. When the grandfather is calm, Xiaocaoli quietly stays with the dog, watching the sun rise and set, watching the stars come and go, watching the moonlit village. Tired, she returns to her cramped dwelling to sleep. In the dim and chaotic room, Xiaocaoli dreams of herself carrying a new schoolbag, walking on sun-drenched roads like other children. Sometimes she even dreams of her mother's warm hand holding hers, celebrating New Year and wearing new clothes.

Xiaocaoli misses her grandmother, hoping that she could walk back from the prayer flags, even lying in bed would suffice, as long as she could hear her grandmother's call, calling her anything. Her father passed away early, and his appearance has become blurry. Now, there is only her grandfather, whose dark hands sometimes grab rotten vegetable leaves from the ground and put them directly into his mouth. When her grandfather gets sick, Xiaocaoli is very afraid and hides at her great-grandmother's house. Recently, she eats at her great-grandmother's house every day, but her great-grandmother is already old. Her great-grandmother's mother-in-law is almost ninety years old. The great-grandmother takes care of her own few grandchildren, and Xiaocaoli fears that her great-grandmother will send her away somewhere else, so she can only cry with her mouth open.

She is almost nine years old and really wants to study. Xiaocaoli's eyes are filled with confusion, murmuring: "I want to study, I want."

Neighbors say that Xiaocaoli's family, under these circumstances, even with government low-income assistance, cannot manage independently or survive easily. The fate of this angelic little girl is unimaginable. Due to lack of funds for preschool, Cao Li is unable to enter elementary school. A person without normal life and learning is destined to be a modern "primitive person"!
