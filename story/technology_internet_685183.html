The concept of solid wood stairs is an art at the corner of the home.

by lovea6o4 on 2012-03-01 14:00:03

The architectural element of the staircase is becoming increasingly prominent in its unique position during the process of reconstruction, which also brings a new design challenge to interior designers. A staircase is simply a passage that allows people to move smoothly between two spaces. It must be designed fairly. According to standards, each step of the staircase should be 15 cm high and 28 cm wide. However, in real life, such ideal conditions are extremely rare. Therefore, designers need to have a thorough understanding and control of the dimensions to ensure convenient passage while occupying minimal space. Based on practical circumstances, the height of the staircase steps should be less than 18 cm, and the width should be greater than 22 cm; otherwise, it will be difficult for us to walk.

There is also a misconception among designers that staircase design is merely about calculation and dimensioning. In reality, this understanding is very limited. Staircases encompass many aspects, ranging from overall spatial style and form, to smaller details like handrail styles, step forms, and types of lighting. Missing any one of these elements might result in a failed staircase renovation.

Currently, compared to the industry standard, Qingdao solid wood staircases mainly come in several types: based on form, there are switchback, straight-run, curved, and same-form staircases. For top-floor residences with attics and relatively small spaces, switchback and straight-run staircases are generally suitable. For villas and duplexes with larger spaces, more grand curved staircases can be chosen. As shown in the figure, this is a duplex residence with an attic on the top floor. The original staircase location was not ideal. After some adjustments by the designer, a larger space was obtained. The designer installed a large curved steel frame staircase between the first and second floors, and a straight-run staircase from the second floor to the attic. The bas-relief on the staircase background adds a unique charm, making the entire staircase lively. Based on materials, there are mainly wooden staircases, steel frame staircases, and concrete poured staircases. Considering cost, wooden and concrete poured staircases are more economical, while steel frame staircases offer convenient construction and diverse mid-range options.

An ordinary residence can appear majestic and full of vitality due to a well-designed staircase. One could say that staircases are the essence of multi-level residential design. Although staircases may seem like a common architectural feature, they can become a highlight in decorating a house through thoughtful design, turning them into an art form.
