1. A child doesn't need to be too smart.

by was65752 on 2011-06-06 14:07:14

1. Don't be too direct; I feel that's not a good thing. The key is to cultivate good habits.

(Parents should not expect their children to become prodigies, but people who can survive and stand on their own in future society.)

2. Environment is very important, and one must act properly.

(Parents and family members should set an example!)

3. Don't easily form a bad habit, because you will find that the time required to correct a bad habit will be two or three times longer than forming a good habit; don't compromise for short-term benefits by casually introducing new bad habits to your child, such as turning on the TV when the child doesn't eat, just to achieve the purpose of feeding a few more bites.

(Habit formation happens in every small detail of daily life, perseverance is key to seeing results. Cultivating good habits requires a lot of time, so correcting bad habits requires even more time. Don't expect our children to all be saints who show immediate results.)

4. Respect your child, get used to perspective-taking.

(Stand in your child's shoes to listen, watch, and think about this world. If you want your child to respect you, then first you need to learn how to respect your child!)

5. Tell the truth, and when you realize some truths might have negative consequences, pay attention to your speaking skills. It's better to say half the truth diplomatically than tell lies.

(A lie needs a hundred more lies to cover it up, why bother! Today, if you deceive your child with rumors because they are young, consider that when you're old, your child might also deceive you with lies!)

6. Speak language your child can understand. Mothers who are accustomed to explaining things should be concise and clear, avoid being like Tang Seng (from Journey to the West).

(Knowing how annoying it is to listen to others nag, don't bring that annoyance to our children!)

7. Pay attention to techniques when educating your child, progress gradually, don't try to make a fat person in one bite, take it step by step; understand the characteristics of today's children, and don't force growth unnaturally.

(Respect individual differences among children, don't use other children's strengths to compare with your child's weaknesses. Respect the developmental features of children, don't use rigid models to create high IQ but low EQ "prodigies".)

8. Be prescient, if you know doing something might cause strong resistance from your child and you might end up compromising, then don't interfere with your child, otherwise the result will only be worse, not better.

(If you know your child will see snacks and want to eat them, then don't take out snacks in front of your child and eat them yourself. Don't expect our children to have good self-control.)

9. When eating, pay attention to a relatively balanced ratio, no need to be too refined; before 1 year old, ensure milk intake; after 1 year old, focus on three meals; if you can't achieve a variety of balanced foods in a day, then balance it over a week, taking everything into account is also acceptable.

(Know the principle of excess leading to harm, don't blindly supplement what you think is nutrition, but formulate reasonable balanced nutrition based on the individual needs of your child.)

10. A child won't starve themselves to death.

(Good eating habits are not formed through your nagging and scolding. Whether the child is hungry or not, they know. Let the child understand that food isn't for parents, but for their own survival needs.)

11. Eating must be done in a high chair, if not eating, please leave.

(Don't always say: "My child doesn't eat, always likes to play while eating and run around." That's because you willingly chase after your child to feed them, don't shift the blame onto our children.)

12. When playing outside and cannot eat as comfortably as at home, there's no need to worry too much, within the ability range, slightly pay attention, such as choosing a clean restaurant; other factors like whether there is monosodium glutamate in the dishes, whether the dishes are organic, etc., there's no need to worry too much.

(The cleaner things are, the easier they get dirty.)

13. For calcium supplementation, be confident and supplement only if needed; get more sun exposure.

(Currently, hospitals offer trace element tests. Don't supplement your child based on your assumptions, but rather supplement based on scientific evidence, lacking what's necessary, and pay attention to the balance of daily nutrition.)

14. Dressing is not particular, not intentional.

(Beauty does not equal comfort, dress your child cleanly and comfortably.)

15. Dress similar to the mother.

(Flowers grown in greenhouses wilt quickly when taken outside. If you dress your child in a certain way, dress yourself similarly, check the flexibility of movement, if the child feels comfortable moving, then you should know.)

16. When dressing your child, having fewer clothes is better than having more, especially in schools. Children have a large activity volume, sweat during play, and catch colds easily when the wind blows, making recovery harder.

(Children sweat more easily than adults, take off a layer when active, put it back on when resting.)

17. If the child has a runny nose, add another layer of clothing.

18. Determine whether the child is cold or hot by touching the back of their neck.

(When sweating on the head, it means the child is already too hot, making them more susceptible to catching a cold.)

19. Do not frequently give your child medicine. In cold seasons, occasionally give vitamin C, or eat fruits rich in vitamin C daily.

(Kiwifruit, oranges, etc., eat them one hour before meals!)

20. When the symptoms of a cold are mild, such as only having a runny nose, there's no need to start medication immediately, drink more water instead; most colds are viral, there are no specific drugs for viruses, taking medicine improves in 14 days, not taking medicine improves in 2 weeks; many times it's psychological comfort for the adults.

(Parents always hope their children recover quickly, remember this saying: illness comes suddenly, but leaves slowly. Everything has a process, so does illness, which has its course.)

21. When going to the hospital for a cold or fever, try to request a blood test.

(Colds are divided into viral and bacterial. Viral colds have normal or lower white blood cell counts, 90% of infant colds are viral, antibiotics are ineffective against viruses! Bacterial colds increase white blood cells, at this point, a certain amount of antibiotics should be used, oral administration is preferred over injections, injections over IVs, and IVs only when really necessary. Parents must understand the consequences of antibiotic abuse!)

22. Can someone not get sick? Impossible; therefore, when your child catches a cold or has a minor illness, don't panic, look at it dialectically, getting a cold is like getting a preventive shot. For these macroscopically unavoidable things, there's no need to be overly concerned.

(The human immune system and disease resistance are built early in common colds and fevers, 38.5°C to 39°C is moderate fever, don't artificially disrupt the establishment of the immune system.)

23. Play without fear of dirt.

(Not afraid of dirt, but pay attention to hygiene after playing!)

24. If it doesn't endanger oneself or infringe on others' interests, don't intervene too much in the child's behavior.

(Let the child experience the world on their own~ don't deprive the child of the right to accumulate experience!)

25. Let the child play freely, crawling and rolling around, as long as there's no serious injury.

(Free your mind, seek happiness~ a happy childhood is the best gift parents can give their child!)

26. Not all dangerous objects should be forbidden to the child; within controllable limits, teach the child where the danger lies and how to avoid it.

(Let the child perceive danger themselves, guide them after they perceive it, pass on your experience to strengthen the child's daily awareness of danger and self-protection.)

27. Give the child as many opportunities as possible to figure things out themselves, don't directly tell them the result; let them explore, bump, and fail.

(Without the process, the result loses its educational meaning!)

28. Teach the child to wait.

(Waiting is a manifestation of cultivation!)

29. Teach the child to take responsibility for their actions.

(Sense of responsibility is a good foundation for future success!)

30. Teach the child that giving and receiving are mutual, and learn gratitude.

(As the saying goes: "Heart exchanges heart!" To survive in future society, children need to cooperate with others. Selfish people are those we are unwilling to collaborate with. Don't raise your child to be someone who harms others for personal gain, they will eventually lose your protection, and it will be hard for them to survive in society!)

31. Try to accompany your child to sleep.

(Self-reliance is not forcing a young child to face darkness alone to fall asleep, but guiding the child to face darkness bravely!)

32. Protect the child's attention and interest.

(Don't use adult preferences to choose the path of life for the child!)

33. Cold training.

(Unable to change the weather, adapting is most important!)

34. Many "early education" methods exist in society, when unsure what to do, don't do anything, know when to act and when not to; believe in moderation, excess is as bad as deficiency.

(Rationally choose methods suitable for your child to educate them, respect individual differences. Read parenting books critically~ never copy them verbatim to educate your child!)

35. Children belong to society, don't regard yourself as the sole savior of your child.

(Borrowed from Yu Dan's interpretation of The Analects: "In this world, all kinds of love aim for unification, except one kind of love that aims for separation, and that is the love of parents for their children. Truly successful parental love is letting the child separate from your life as an independent individual as early as possible. The earlier this separation, the more successful you are.")

36. Don't dwell excessively on the 'why' and theoretical basis of your parenting details. Once done, it's a fact, no need for so much fuss.

37. Ensure that one parent plays with the child for a certain amount of time each day.

(Good daily parent-child interaction lays a solid foundation for parent-child relationships in adulthood. Don't use busyness as an excuse, the cost later is irreplaceable by money.)

38. Find ways to make the child laugh heartily several times a day.

(Laughter relaxes the mood, while laughing, parents should also laugh happily a few times, emotions can influence each other! Optimism starts with laughter!)

39. Make life gamified, make games lifelike.

(Games are the work of children, Montessori's words explain where the joy of children lies!)

40. The most important sentence: I only need a 70-point child, so I only need to be a 70-point mother; not strictly demanding of myself, just getting by is fine, after all, it's my first time being a mom.

(I disagree with this point, wanting a 70-point child, but I want to be at least a 90-point mother, because the child only has one chance to grow! Missed opportunities are hard to make up!)

41. Teach the child to face failure directly, I may be不服 (unyielding), but I can afford to lose, I can choose to give up or choose to try again, but I cannot cry in front of my opponent.

(Parents should guide the child to analyze the reasons for failure when failing, find ways to deal with failure, summarize lessons learned from failure. Only in this way can failure be the mother of success!)

42. If a friend attacks, learn to assess the situation, warn once, and the basic principle is, if you can fight, fight, if you can't, escape; in kindergarten, shout to attract others' attention.

43. Do not attack first.

44. Regardless of who strikes first, you cannot be the first to cry.

45. You have the right to decide about your own things.

(Do not expect the child to endure giving away their favorite things, just like you wouldn't share your beloved items with others.)

46. Plan to introduce a weekly allowance mechanism after age 3—start learning to manage limited funds to do what you want most, learn to generate income, learn to save.

47. Frequently take the child on outings, go to the countryside; do not fear wind and rain.

(Let the child return to nature, urban concrete jungles make children lose much, we should help them regain the fun of nature!)

48. Strive to visit the rural hometown once a year to experience a different lifestyle.

49. Do not bring work troubles home.

(Children are not relievers of our negative emotions, do not vent your negative emotions on your children, the damage to their hearts is too deep!)

Add a few of my own:

50. Do not always say "no" to the child. Instead, tell the child why they cannot do this or that, let the child know the reason behind "no."

51. Fulfill the child's reasonable requests, and firmly resist unreasonable ones, even if the child cries. Let them know that crying won't solve problems, so they won't use crying as a long-term tool to achieve their goals.

52. Allow the child to vent dissatisfaction, just like you vent when dissatisfied (of course, rationally and without harming others' interests). The mood gets better this way, our children also have times when they need to vent their bad moods.

53. In daily life, do not always focus on the child's study, IQ, nutrition, etc., pay more attention to the mental health of our children. Children are the least likely to disguise themselves, in the small abnormalities of children, parents should learn to grasp the direction of the child's psychological growth, and guide the child to face life optimistically and positively.

54. Do not always demand the child follow rules while relaxing your own standards. Remember: if you want the child to reach a certain level, you must first reach that level yourself! Demand politeness from the child, but if you yourself do not use polite language, our children will never truly learn to treat others politely.

55. When the child asks "why," do not respond with "where do so many whys come from!" This kills the child's interest in exploring the unknown world, and you still expect the child to become a scientist exploring the world?

56. Remember the most important rule: the behavior, manners, thinking patterns, mental health levels, etc., of parents are all models for the child. Good children definitely come from good parenting. Think about whether your own behavior, thoughts, and mannerisms resemble those of your parents? Your behavior, manners, thinking patterns, and mental health levels will all affect your child's entire life!

57. Do not easily label the child as lying. Children are not as false as adults, perhaps it is their imagination that makes them say things inconsistent with reality. Parents need to find the reason before concluding about the child's speech!

58. Do not expect the child to naturally like reading. The habit of reading is cultivated through long-term guidance from parents.

59. Being an only child does not mean the child owns everything exclusively, but is a unique child. Uniqueness, respect the child's individuality, guide the child to learn cooperation with others.

60. Do not tell the child "behave! Listen!" Instead, listen to the child's ideas, rationally analyze the problem, whoever is right, listen to them. Do not force the child to live according to your wishes just because you are the parents!

61. Help the child form good living habits from the first day of birth. Confucius said: "Forming habits early is like nature, habits are natural!"

62. Give the child time to be alone, do not always ask questions around them. How will the child learn concentration this way!

63. There should be rules in the family, establish your own family rules, which both children and parents should follow! Let the child know that in social survival, not everything can be done according to one's own wishes, but certain rules must be followed.

64. Tell the child that life is precious, once lost it cannot be recovered, learn to cherish life!

65. When the child asks about physiological issues, do not think it is "lewd" behavior. The child does not yet distinguish between boys and girls, how would they know what "lewd" behavior is? Tell the child where they came from using language they can understand, do not deceive them by saying they were found.

66. Choose media programs suitable for the child, control the exposure time, do not let the child be prematurely influenced by inappropriate media and lose their innocence.

67. Hug and kiss the child every day, do not think that because the child is growing up, they no longer need affection and hugs. Children with autism first need continuous hugs and kisses!

68. Let the child learn to do their own things, help with household chores. Do not just talk, take action, that is the most basic skill for the child's future survival. After all, under normal circumstances, we will leave this world earlier than they will. Even animal parents teach their offspring survival skills, yet we, as highly intelligent beings, discard the most essential things we should teach our children!

69. Without a second language learning environment, do not expect the child to speak a second language as fluently as their native tongue. First master the native language fluently before learning a second language!

70. Do not always clean up after the child's mistakes, let them know they must pay a price for their actions, only then will they truly avoid making the same mistakes repeatedly.

71. When disputes arise between children, as long as there is no severe physical attack, let the child solve the problem themselves. After all, the child cannot always live under your protection!

72. Do not mock mature questions from the child, that is the beginning of their cognition. Think about how you started from knowing nothing, and had childish motives in childhood.

73. Do not ridicule the child for not doing things well, helping with housework makes things more chaotic. As long as they are willing to do it themselves and actively help you, they deserve praise. After all, they are children, think about how you started from making things more chaotic and progressed to proficiently doing housework! One ridicule, one scolding, will eliminate the child's enthusiasm and initiative to do housework!

74. Shouting loudly at the child is ineffective, it only provides an example of shouting when angry or unable to control one's temper, and makes the child accustomed to noise!

75. Always remember that if you want the child to respect you to a certain degree, you must first respect the child to that degree! Respect is not unconditional indulgence!

Educating children is not something that shows immediate results, bit by bit, when the child grows up, the results of your education will gradually appear. Is it sweet? Is it bitter? Is it sour? It's all in your control!
