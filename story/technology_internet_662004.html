Liu Shishi was rushed to hospital for pancreatitis after working overnight on a drama set, which then suspended filming much to the relief of fans

by chinameinet4 on 2012-02-09 16:08:22

Liu Shishi

Yesterday afternoon, the reporter of Huaxi Metropolis Daily contacted Ms. Teng, the contact person for Liu Shishi. She said, "Shishi's condition is stable at present. She has just finished a medical examination, and the report results will be out tomorrow. The preliminary diagnosis suggests that surgery should not be necessary for now, and she mainly needs rest and intravenous fluids."

Liu Shishi Suddenly Falls Ill with Pancreatitis, Shocking Everyone on the Set

When Being Famous Makes Time Precious: Rushing Through Nights to Film, Collapsing on the Scene

Liu Shishi, who skyrocketed to fame last year, has been frequently sought after by film companies this year, leaving her extremely busy. On July 7th, the crew of "Sad Fairy Tale," which was originally preparing to accept media visits, suddenly canceled their plans due to Liu Shishi falling ill and vomiting on set. The production team immediately sent her to a hospital in Guangzhou.

It is understood that the movie "Sad Fairy Tale" tells the story of Da Zuo and Hu Xia both falling in love with the female lead, Liu Shishi, choosing two entirely different methods to pursue her, thus creating a touching love triangle. Since Liu Shishi’s role in the film was very demanding, while shooting in Dongguan, she worked through the night continuously until eventually being rushed to the hospital due to exhaustion. Her close friend Da Zuo also showed support for his "girlfriend" from the drama on Weibo, saying, "We who endure all insults, really nothing is more important than health. Refusing is something we will never learn how to do."

Awaiting Test Results, Recovery Date Still Unknown

Yesterday afternoon, reporters from Huaxi Metropolis Daily contacted the production team, who confirmed that Liu Shishi had acute pancreatitis. According to medical experts, pancreatitis is a disease caused by the pancreas digesting itself due to pancreatic enzymes. It often occurs in middle-aged men, usually preceded by binge eating or a history of gallbladder disease. The production team revealed that this incident happened very suddenly, and since she is the leading actress, the shooting has been temporarily halted. When the shooting resumes will depend on Liu Shishi’s recovery status.

Had Discomfort Earlier, Endured it for Three to Four Days for Work

"In fact, the company did not schedule her to handle many interviews in one day; it’s mainly because she sets high standards for herself. Even when there were no scenes for her, she would still rehearse with others." Yesterday afternoon, Ms. Teng from Liu Shishi’s side told reporters that Liu Shishi had already felt unwell three to four days earlier but endured it silently.

"She didn't want to delay everyone's process, and ultimately, she could bear the pain no longer and informed us." Ms. Teng added that usually, Liu Shishi enjoys eating to relieve stress, so what made this illness especially painful was that she couldn’t indulge her cravings anymore.

No One in the Palace Drama World Feels the Strain

Wu Qilong Spends a Day "Traveling" Across Four Cities

The popularity of "Bu Bu Jing Xin" (Step Into the Heart) not only brought attention to "Ruoxi" but also crowned "Fourth Prince" Wu Qilong as an undisputed star.

Faced with rising fame and a flood of work opportunities, Wu Qilong’s image as a hardworking model has deeply resonated. "Take one day as an example: waking up at 6:30 am, arriving at the set for makeup at 7 am, flying somewhere else in the afternoon for events, getting haircuts in the evening for endorsements, then doing micro-interviews... ending work around 1 am is Wu Qilong's normal routine." Yesterday, Wu Qilong's agent Ren Yue told reporters that even during the Spring Festival, Wu Qilong was shooting in Paris, hardly taking any breaks. "At our busiest times, we flew across four cities in one day."

Preparations in Place for Palace Drama Stars

Lin Gengxin and He Shengming Regularly Get Health Check-Ups

"During filming, I don't arrange too much extra work for myself. A regular lifestyle is crucial for actors." Yesterday afternoon, the currently popular "Yongzheng" actor He Shengming told reporters that simply getting dressed in period costumes takes two to three hours, which is quite normal. "A standard day of filming lasts around ten hours, and sometimes there are situations where we need to rush through scenes. My way of relieving pressure is swimming—it's convenient and easy to achieve. Also, not just ordinary people but actors need to take regular health check-ups seriously."

The new generation "lady-killer" Lin Gengxin also stated that self-adjustment is essential. Additionally, Lin Gengxin revealed that he will once again visit Chengdu on the 11th to promote a certain brand.
