By Chen Yiquan

In 1968, Pascal Clemen was born in Toulouse, a small city in the south of France. Due to his mother Chantal Clemen's poor figure and her use of chemical drugs during pregnancy to preserve the fetus, the side effects of the drug greatly affected the child in her womb, resulting in Pascal Clemen being born with congenital disabilities.

When it was time for him to go to school, his grandfather took him there, but the principal was reluctant to accept this child. Later, with the help of a priest, he entered a smaller school. His parents hired a nanny to assist him with daily life tasks, including turning pages and writing.

Pascal Clemen decided to break free from this state; he yearned for growth and independence. He asked his father to install two prosthetic limbs for him. However, due to the limited technology at the time, these prostheses were clumsy and not flexible, offering little more than allowing him to imagine having arms in front of a mirror. From a young age, he loved music, but without arms, he could only play a whistle as an instrument. One summer, while sitting on a small chair playing the whistle, he accidentally dropped it on the ground. His prosthetics couldn't pick it up, but then he noticed his tender feet in sandals. Subconsciously, he used his toes to pick up the whistle and realized that his toes were more effective than the prosthetics. At that moment, he had an idea: to use his feet as hands!

From then on, he began practicing self-care using his feet—reading books, writing, playing video games, and even playing the guitar and keyboard. It was manageable in summer, but in winter, the cold was unbearable. Yet, he always kept barefoot indoors, and even when going out, he wore only cotton slippers because his feet were often used for shopping, grabbing food, and flipping newspapers. Despite his toes turning purple and the skin breaking from the cold, he refused to stop training. Day by day, year after year, his feet gradually became his "hands," and he could independently dress himself and cook.

At the age of 21, Pascal Clemen achieved good results in his legal studies and obtained a degree. He had already adapted to living a life where he read and wrote with his feet, and working in the legal service industry posed no significant obstacles for him. If obtaining a law degree was an invisible milestone for his future, what truly changed his life was music and an indomitable spirit.

It happened during a campus concert before his graduation. Some classmates who played DJ music made the entire concert exciting with their self-made DJ music, causing people to dance to the rhythm. The earth seemed to tremble with the beauty of the music, and he longed to be part of it! At that moment, he decided to become a DJ musician.

His mother, Chantal Clemen, strongly opposed his decision. She argued that he had spent over a decade studying law, obtained a degree, and entering the legal service industry was a natural progression. Suddenly deciding to become a DJ meant abandoning everything he had worked for. His mother told him, "I believe in your musical talent, but being a DJ requires hands. Can you compete with other musicians? Moreover, you already have a promising future in law, something many people struggle for years to achieve. You can't give it up so easily!"

Pascal Clemen replied, "I don't compare myself with others; I just want to do what I love."

Her opposition was ineffective, and the family could only support him. His parents bought him a computer and a turntable, and he started practicing beat juggling with his feet at home. With determination and musical talent, a few years later, his DJ music records began entering the local market on a small scale. In Toulouse, many parties and bars started using his DJ music. Soon, his works caught the attention of Laurent Garnier, a famous French DJ, who found Pascal Clemen and invited him to join his music group for a performance in Paris.

On the day of the performance, when the audience learned that a DJ musician without arms would perform, they expressed strong dissatisfaction and demanded his program be replaced. Even as Pascal Clemen stepped onto the stage, the protests continued. Pascal Clemen said, "I am happy living without arms because I never compare myself with others. I chose to abandon my legal career for DJ music because I never compare myself with others. I'm here today because I've never compared myself with anyone else. I simply love DJ music, so I hope everyone stops comparing me with others!"

As music lovers, Pascal Clemen's words struck a chord with the audience, evoking admiration and emotion. They no longer opposed his performance. As he passionately performed, the entire concert hall was captivated, and applause erupted repeatedly from the audience. People danced enthusiastically, casting appreciative glances at him. After the performance, everyone present thanked him, and all previous dissatisfaction vanished.

Nowadays, Pascal Clemen is a highly influential DJ musician in France. Besides occasional performances, he spends most of his time in his music studio, operating sound equipment, mixers, computers, discs, and various electronic devices with his feet. Excellent DJ music pieces continuously emerge and gain popularity in various music markets. In 2009, his new album "VA-Psylove" set a record of 5 million copies sold globally, an unprecedented achievement among French DJ musicians!

Pascal Clemen has no hands, but he possesses wings that cannot be seen with the naked eye. With these wings, he freely flies in the paradise of DJ music. Some say his feet are his wings because he creates countless excellent DJ works with them. However, this is only partially correct; the true name of his wings should be—faith!
