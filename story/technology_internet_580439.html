The Difficulty of Choosing Home Broadband: Who Took Away My Rights?

by diglog on 2011-11-23 12:10:18

The aftermath of CCTV's exposure of China Telecom and China Unicom's alleged broadband monopoly has turned more into a "mess" battle between media outlets. In fact, consumers, who have the most authority to speak about monopolies, say "it doesn't matter whether it's a monopoly or not, as long as I can freely choose." Setting aside distant issues for now, even in Guangzhou, there is no freedom in choosing broadband services for residential areas. Some netizens pointed out that Guangzhou Tietong, E Home Broadband, and Pearl River Broadband have signed exclusive broadband service agreements with many residential areas, making it impossible to choose other broadband services. Additionally, some netizens reflected that communication consumers must bundle their mobile services to obtain better broadband services; otherwise, they will be charged higher fees. "Is this a monopoly or unfair competition? Who took away my right to choose?" Case 1: "Last Mile" Monopoly - Residential Area Broadband "Being Chosen". Mr. Cheng, who lives in Roma Homeland, stated that his home can only install E Home Broadband.
