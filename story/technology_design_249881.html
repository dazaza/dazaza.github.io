Practical bedroom design

by longersing on 2009-10-20 15:31:57

Practical bedroom design: eliminate the large wardrobe and use a big cabinet to remove the clutter in the bedroom out of sight, achieving a wider space effect visually. If conditions do not allow, you should still try to hide various single-piece or temporary storage furniture with curtains or various screens.

The dressing room is what the female owner needs most

Converting a room into a dressing room is far more suitable than designing other functional spaces. The master bedroom, dressing room, and bathroom are all connected and hidden behind sliding doors. When the sliding doors are closed, all three belong to the overall space of the master bedroom; when opened, they become independent spaces.

Wall design (including horizontal surfaces) should be simple as a principle

Decoration is necessary, but materials with strong reflection or overly carved graphic treatments are unnecessary. Otherwise, it's easy to produce hallucinations when sleeping, and overly complex designs can easily lead to insomnia. Don't create shapes just for the sake of creating them; appropriate decoration should be done under the premise of convenience. The master bedroom is a place for leisure and should not be too elaborately decorated, which might give people a sense of instability.
