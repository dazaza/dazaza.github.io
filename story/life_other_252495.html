by gaocan12345 on 2009-10-27 15:16:03

At around 2 PM in the afternoon, two boys aged about 12-13 drowned by the Yangtze River in Shashi District, Jingzhou. Several freshmen students noticed the situation and formed a human ladder to rescue them, including those who couldn't swim and female classmates. When the second child was almost saved, due to exhaustion and undercurrents, the human ladder broke and nine university students fell into the water. Cries for help filled the air when several elderly members of the winter swimming team, all over sixty years old, managed to save six university students while three others unfortunately drowned. There were two fishing boats nearby with people on board but not a single person came to help. The fire department was the first to arrive at the scene, but they said that they didn't have diving suits or other rescue equipment so they only searched along the riverbank (as shown in the news footage) before returning ashore. They were stopped by several crying university students and went back to the riverbank, but without any intention of diving in to search for people. The Maritime Bureau's boat just looked around and then left. It took two hours for the school leaders to arrive at the scene and negotiate the cost of retrieving the bodies with the owners of the previously mentioned two fishing boats - 12,000 yuan per body. Only then did the fishermen start retrieving the bodies from the water. When the first body was brought up, cries of grief could be heard from the crowd. The location where the body was retrieved was less than three meters away from the fishing boats. At that time, if the people on the boat had just thrown an oar, the drowning victims could have been saved. I watched this happen and felt deeply distressed. Even when the bodies were brought up, the doctors still pretended to try resuscitating them with oxygen (as shown in the news footage). By this time, it had been over three hours since the incident occurred. When the second university student's body was brought up, the owner of the fishing boat stopped the operation, claiming that the agreed amount of 24,000 yuan hadn't been paid and refused to retrieve more bodies. Accusations arose from the crowd. In the end, the teachers knelt down and negotiated a deal of 11,000 yuan per body. It wasn't until 6 PM that the third body was retrieved. To this day, the two children who were saved have disappeared and cannot be found. However, the news reported that it was the fire department and the Maritime Bureau who retrieved the bodies. After reviewing the news footage carefully, you can see that there were five or six members of the fire department walking in the shallow water, while no trace of the Maritime Bureau was seen. What are these government departments being maintained by the state for? Retrieving bodies is their responsibility, yet no one from the government showed up to do it. I swear by my integrity that everything I said is true. It is truly disheartening and I can't bear to watch. The parents wouldn't have died if the fishing boats nearby had come to help. Those students cried and knelt down to beg the fishermen to go down and rescue, but the fishermen remained indifferent. It is said that there is often drowning in that area and there is a retrieval team locally which charges 12,000 yuan per body, generally ignoring the cries for help because it's a profitable path!
