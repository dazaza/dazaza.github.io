"Unmarried" is a significant work on marriage by Wang Hailing.

by jqrxass on 2009-09-05 14:50:06

Author Wang Hailing became famous due to her collaboration with Wang Shuo on "If I Love You", and gained widespread recognition because of the sensation caused by "Holding Hands." "Rather Not Marry" changes the single-threaded narrative style of "Holding Hands," attempting to analyze the myriad aspects of marriage through five representative stories of married couples. A thousand marriages, a thousand feelings—Is harmony the ultimate pursuit? Wang Hailing believes that after practicing with multiple works and learning from Wang Shuo, she has greatly benefited.

Chapter One

Xu Liang was honest and indeed said again: "From head to toe, inside and out, from clothing to hairstyle, to walking, sitting, and lying down..."

Tao Ran screamed: "You still dare to say!" Without thinking, she crossed her hands and grabbed the hem of her T-shirt, "—If you say it again, I'll take off my clothes right now!"

Chapter Two

Tan Xiaoyu stood there motionless, completely stunned, thus not noticing Tao Ran's arrival at all. When she sensed someone's presence, Tao Ran was already standing in front of her, giving her a big scare.

Chapter Three

In the women's changing room, the nurses were getting dressed before their shift. One girl had already taken off her coat and was standing by the door in shorts and a bra when suddenly the door was pushed open with a "bang," exposing the girl to the corridor. The girl screamed, "Close the door!"

Chapter Four

With an innocent face, neither Xiaoyu's mother nor Professor Tan thought she knew anything. Professor Tan left. Xiaoyu's mother calmly reached under her pillow for her wallet, took out some money, and said, "Remember to buy some tofu, no plaster. ..."

Chapter Five

Liu Huiyang wanted to work not only for more income each month—these additional two hundred yuan, compared to his fixed monthly expenses—since he had to pay 5000 yuan per month for his house even without eating or drinking—could be considered meaningless. But, if not this job, then what?

Chapter Six

The ward was quiet. Night-shift nurse Tan Xiaoyu was making medical records at the nursing station when suddenly, she felt someone in front of her. She looked up—it was Xu Liang. She had been avoiding him, but he still came. She greeted him reluctantly.

Chapter Seven

Everyone fell silent, unable to think of anything to say. After a few seconds of extreme silence, Liu Huiyang walked towards the female worker, lifted the front part of the bike, and started walking with the worker. Xiaoyu’s gaze was not without worry; Tao Ran and Dian Dian unconsciously held her arms from both sides.

Chapter Eight

Professor Tan and Xiaoyu saw Xiao Zheng and Dian Dian out. As the door opened, they just happened to meet Liu Huiyang who had come to pick Xiaoyu up to go home. After exchanging greetings, Xiao Zheng bid farewell. At this point, Professor Tan said, "Xiao, you are a young man dedicated to your career."

Chapter Nine

How could Xiaoyu have the leisure to listen to Tao Ran's grandiose talk? She pondered, "He said he wanted me to be his secretary..."

"A secretary is a polite way of saying 'mistress,' just like 'lady' is a refined term for 'prostitute.'"

Chapter Ten

She turned over and buried her face in his warm chest. His heart thumped heavily against her eardrums, tears flowed down, and she quietly wiped them away. At this moment, she didn't want to explain.

Chapter Eleven

The company was eerily quiet, perfect for working. Shen Ping was fully focused on his desk. Suddenly, there was a loud knock on the door. He went to open it curiously. Outside the door stood a drenched Xiaoyu.

Chapter Twelve

No one heard the gate sound. Huiming, wearing a work uniform and covered in sweat, appeared at the dining room entrance. For a moment, the room was dead silent, and everyone was stunned.

Chapter Thirteen

Xiaoyu stared at her father, her hands swiftly tearing a plate into two halves, then four... Professor Tan closely watched his daughter. After a while, he suddenly turned to the girl, "Let's go. I'll send you. It's getting late."

Chapter Fourteen

Liu Huiyang delivered water to Shen Ping's company and knocked on the door of Shen Ping's office, entering upon permission. Inside, Shen Ping was talking with several well-dressed people. Liu Huiyang carried the water in, causing Shen Ping to stop speaking immediately.

Chapter Fifteen

The company agreed to Liu Huiyang's request, and Liu Huiyang also fulfilled his promise on time, allowing Tan Xiaoyu to stand out among six newly hired real estate salespeople and sell the first house.

Chapter Sixteen

Xiaoyu no longer argued with Lingzhi, trying to push her away. But she was no match for the strong Lingzhi, who shoved her back in one move, causing her to stumble backward several steps to avoid falling.

Chapter Seventeen

At the memorial hall, large black-and-white characters, black-and-white portraits of the deceased, wreaths, and couplets. Instead of funeral music, the melody of "The Hawthorn Tree" played in the hall. The farewell ceremony had yet to begin.

Chapter Eighteen

Xiaoyu returned to the room and quickly packed things into a large box, essentially grabbing her clothes and throwing them into the box. Then, bang, she closed the lid and dragged it out.

Chapter Nineteen

Upon hearing this, Dian Dian burst into tears and drank the wine in her glass in one gulp. Tao Ran worriedly said, "Dian Dian, take it easy!"

Chapter Twenty

Somewhere far away, an unknown family's speaker was playing the Shaanbei folk song "Blue Flower." The distant and ethereal singing echoed: Blue thread, blue thread so fine, gave birth to a Blue Flower, truly loved to death. ...
