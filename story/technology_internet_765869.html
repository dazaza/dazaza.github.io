Weibo "Big V" Xue Manzi May Be Deported

by anonymous on 2013-08-26 14:19:51

On August 23, Weibo "Big V" Charles Xue was administratively detained for soliciting prostitution, causing a stir in public opinion. Those who had once been helped by him exclaimed that they couldn't believe it, but online public opinion still believed that making mistakes is making mistakes, and breaking the law is breaking the law. However, one thing is clear. Experts believe that if Xue Manzi indeed holds American nationality, he might be deported after being subjected to re-education through labor.

At 11:12 AM yesterday, the official Weibo account of Beijing Public Security Bureau released a message stating that on August 23, 2013, according to a tip from the public, the Chaoyang police caught Xue (male, 60 years old) and Zhang (female, 22 years old, from Henan) engaging in prostitution at an apartment complex in Anhui Beili. After investigation, both confessed to their acts of prostitution. Currently, the police have legally administratively detained both individuals. After multiple confirmations by reporters, Xue is indeed Xue Manzi, whose real name is Xue Biqun, a well-known investor.

Yesterday afternoon, on Xue Manzi's Sina verified Weibo homepage, his last posted Weibo was at 17:41 on the 23rd, which was a forwarded Weibo about the "seventh day memorial for Northeast China flood." A Weibo post regarding the collection of children's supplementary rice powder has been pinned to the top. As of that day, he had posted a total of 85,012 Weibos. According to rough estimates by the reporter, Xue Manzi averaged 78 Weibos per day. Corresponding to his number of followers, the number of forwards and comments on each of Xue Manzi's Weibos generally ranged from hundreds to thousands.

However, the reporter found that a Weibo account named "Manzi Wenzhai" was still updating normally yesterday, and Xue Manzi's last Weibo was forwarded from "Manzi Wenzhai." The reporter attempted to contact "Manzi Wenzhai" via private message, but as of the time of publication, there was no response.

Interpretation

Foreigners involved in prostitution may be deported

According to public reports, Xue Manzi holds American nationality. Regarding the legal consequences of his arrest for prostitution, Professor Hong Daode of China University of Political Science and Law believes that according to Chinese law, Xue Manzi could possibly be deported.

Hong Daode told reporters that if his act of prostitution does not involve criminal offenses, then it will be handled according to the Public Security Administration Punishment Law, resulting in a detention of more than 10 days but less than 15 days, with a possible fine of up to 5,000 yuan; for lighter cases, a detention of less than 5 days or a fine of up to 500 yuan. According to Beijing's regulations, anyone involved in prostitution will be placed under custody education for 6 months. "This practice applies equally to Chinese citizens and foreigners. Given that he is a foreigner, it is possible that after being placed under custody education, he will then be deported," said Hong Daode.

Xue Manzi's Multiple Identities

Angel Investor

Xue Manzi's real name is Xue Biqun. He once summarized his life trajectory like this, "From the age of 1 to 13, I was a privileged young master, after the 'Cultural Revolution' I spent two years in the countryside, came back and worked at the Forbidden City, during that period I met many great scholars. When I took the graduate school entrance exam, I accidentally became the national champion, and then I went all out and rushed to the United States. After returning, I even started a listed company."

However, before 2011, Xue Manzi's most famous identity domestically was "angel investor."

He was one of the founders of UTStarcom, gaining a high return of $125 million from an investment of $250,000 in just a few short years. Since then, he has become one of China's most active individual investors.

Xue Manzi once served as the former chairman of China e-commerce website 8848 and the former chairman of China Learning Network. His investments include PCPOP, Autohome founded by Li Xiang, Xueqiulicai founded by Fang Sanwen, and Huayi Baichuang founded by Du Zijian.

His latest investment is in the currently hot agricultural product e-commerce sector. Starting with笨鸡蛋 (stupid eggs), the supply comes from 600 poor counties, undergoing trial sales in Beijing, while prioritizing the care of poor populations and left-behind children, also enabling them to be self-reliant, allowing ordinary people to eat safe food.

Philanthropist

In mid-2011, Xue Manzi opened a Weibo account, but by yesterday, his Weibo followers had exceeded 12.12 million. "Weibo public intellectual" is another of his identities.

On February 2, 2011, an "initiative to completely eliminate large-scale child trafficking and forced begging crime groups nationwide" became a hotspot on Weibo, initiated by Xue Manzi.

After being forwarded by Cai Wensheng, Yao Chen, Feng Xiaogang, Pan Shiyi, Zhao Wei, Han Hong, and other celebrities, "anti-child trafficking" became a topic discussed by over a million people. The ONE Foundation Children Rescue Project was officially launched in Beijing, with initial funding of 200,000 yuan from the ONE Foundation, and 520,000 yuan contributed by Xue Manzi, Yu Jianrong, Xu Xiaoping, and others. The founding council members included Shen Nanpeng, Xiong Xiaoguo, Zhang Yaqing, Zheng Yuanjie, and more than forty others.

Subsequently, Xue Manzi participated in a series of public welfare and livelihood topics and Weibo events such as fighting cancer, free lunches, and rescuing girls with leukemia. His Weibo frequently reposted a large number of public welfare and help-seeking information.

The media once depicted Xue Manzi's simple life. "Most of his clothes were bought at Xiushui Market, costing no more than 200 yuan in total. He never takes first-class cabins when traveling. His socks often have holes."

Cancer Warrior

On May 20, 2011, Xue Manzi posted a Weibo revealing that he had been diagnosed with rectal cancer. Due to continuously documenting his battle against cancer on Weibo, Xue Manzi became a cancer-fighting celebrity, also known as the "first person to fight cancer on Weibo."

He reported his condition on Weibo, suggesting the public change their lifestyle and eating habits, not smoking, drinking less alcohol, not staying up late, exercising more, and having regular check-ups. The day before surgery, he still strongly urged on Weibo, "Take care of your physical health." Through surgery, doctors removed thirty-one lymph nodes from Xue Manzi's body, all benign. Tests showed no spread.

Xue Manzi's Weibo cancer-fighting campaign once sparked controversy over hype. In response, Xue Manzi said, "As long as I post one Weibo, reminding more people to have a colonoscopy examination annually after the age of 40, saving one person is worth it, so this Weibo was not posted in vain."

Degree Forgery?

Last year, an article titled "Ten-Thousand-Word Review of Xue Manzi, Revealing Your Nakedness" went viral on the Internet, questioning Xue Manzi's family background, educational experience, public welfare charity, business model, and more.

Among these, the article questioned Xue Manzi's degree forgery, pointing out that the resume of "Xue Manzi" on Baidu had been edited three times, with the most substantial edit occurring on November 14, 2011, where Xue Manzi himself changed "In 1977, directly admitted to the Chinese History Postgraduate Program at the Palace Museum in Beijing, obtained a master's degree in 1979, and received a scholarship from the University of California, Berkeley in 1979 to pursue a Ph.D. in American history" to "In 1979, directly admitted to the postgraduate program in Sino-foreign relations history at the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, in 1980, before completing his master's studies, received an American scholarship, went to the East Asian Department of the University of California, Berkeley to continue studying for a master's degree in Sino-foreign relations history. Later, met Masayoshi Son, abandoned his studies to start a business, and did not complete his studies." Some netizens joked, "In 1978, the country resumed graduate student admissions, yet in 1977 he was already admitted to the graduate program at the Palace Museum, did he time travel?"

Recent Public Activities

August 10th Network Celebrity Social Responsibility Forum

Xue Manzi: Social Responsibility is Conscience

On the afternoon of August 10th, the Network Celebrity Social Responsibility Forum was held at the new CCTV building. Weibo "Big V" Xue Manzi and multiple official Weibo account managers discussed topics such as the influence and responsibilities of network celebrities in the Internet era. Lu Wei, Director of the State Internet Information Office, attended and exchanged ideas with network celebrities.

At the forum, Weibo "Big V"s reached a consensus that network celebrities should adhere to the "seven bottom lines," namely the legal bottom line, the socialist system bottom line, the national interest bottom line, the citizen's legitimate rights and interests bottom line, the social public order bottom line, the moral fashion bottom line, and the information authenticity bottom line.

Facing the question of whether to delete posts or refute rumors, Xue Manzi said, "Morality and culture cannot be achieved overnight, the most important thing is to have laws to follow. The reason why there are so many people cursing and spreading rumors on Weibo now is mainly due to the lack of cost. Spreading rumors has no cost and can attract many fans, who wouldn't spread rumors?" He said, if what you say is wrong, a fine of 1 million yuan should be imposed, and the next day no one would dare to spread rumors.

About social responsibility, he said, "I think it's very simple, it's conscience. Everyone's true social responsibility is to tell the truth and not lie, not speak empty words, not speak clichés, not speak official jargon. If necessary, if you can't tell the whole truth, make sure not to tell lies. This is my understanding of social responsibility."

The content of the forum was broadcast on CCTV Finance Channel's "Dialogue" program on August 18th.

August 13th Entrepreneurship Open Class

Xue Manzi: Now is the Golden Age for Entrepreneurship

On August 13th, Xue Manzi (see picture below) personally attended Tencent's entrepreneurship open class held at the National Convention Center, sharing insights and experiences with numerous entrepreneurs. Xue Manzi stated that great companies like Google and Facebook all have the shadow of angels, entrepreneurs need angels, and he is willing to carry on the "foolish" spirit of taking risks to the end.

Xue Manzi described the current entrepreneurial environment in China as the golden age for entrepreneurship. He indicated that thanks to the continuous expansion of the middle class brought about by reform and opening up, and the rapid rise of Weibo, WeChat, and mobile Internet, it brings many opportunities to entrepreneurs, and the past marketing model that relied on burning money has also been overturned.

When discussing the issue that entrepreneurs are most concerned about, "What kind of entrepreneurs do angel investors like the most," Xue Manzi pointed out incisively that being smart, honest, and having high emotional intelligence are crucial, and the most important point is to be obsessed with one's own career. For newcomers who have just graduated from school, he suggested not rushing into entrepreneurship, but first going to start-up companies to learn and accumulate experience, and then acting after growing and maturing. In addition, Xue Manzi also advised entrepreneurs to look more at traditional industries, study O2O, and explore how to find entrepreneurial opportunities and expand entrepreneurial space in cross-border fields.

Xue Manzi emphasized that today is an era where talents are selected without restrictions. The purpose of the China Internet Innovation and Entrepreneurship Competition is similarly to select talents without restrictions. As the largest scale, lowest threshold, newest form, and richest awards entrepreneurship competition in 2013, it is believed that it will change the fate of many entrepreneurs present.
