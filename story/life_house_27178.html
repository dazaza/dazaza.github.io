Renovation Tips: Ensure Good Ventilation to Prevent Dampness and Avoid Sun Exposure - 5 Summer Renovation Points to Note

by kjp1982 on 2007-08-10 14:46:53

In summer, the temperature is high and the climate is dry. There are a lot of materials piled up at the construction site. From the perspective of safety, the materials should be placed in a cool place as much as possible to avoid fire caused by poor air circulation and excessively high temperatures inside. The materials used for summer renovation should also be reduced on the construction site as much as possible, which can reduce accident risks. Additionally, electrical appliances and tools used during operations should maintain a certain distance from the materials. When working with electricity, stay as far away from the materials as possible. It's best to make a reasonable division between the work area and the material storage area.

Ensure good ventilation to prevent pollution from renovation.

To avoid being harmed during the "high incidence period" of summer renovation pollution, besides choosing non-toxic or low-toxic decoration materials and hiring reputable home renovation companies, it is also necessary to ensure ventilation and air purification in the renovated rooms. Whenever possible, let the room ventilate as much as possible; if not feasible, indoor ventilation equipment and air purification devices that reduce harmful gases indoors can be selected.

Prevent dampness and exposure to sunlight.

When purchasing wood products in summer, including main materials like large core boards and auxiliary materials such as white wooden strips, door edges, window frames, and baseboards along the wall, it is essential to check the moisture content of the wood products. In the building materials market, most specialized wood product stores should have wood moisture content measuring instruments. According to national regulations, the moisture content of standard panels cannot exceed 12%. Experts suggest that when purchasing materials during the rainy season, you can choose drier ones appropriately. Once the materials arrive on-site, they can be left for one or two days to adapt to the surrounding humidity, making them more suitable for use.

Additionally, wooden boards should be protected from strong sunlight. If the humidity is high during rainy weather, measures should be taken to prevent dampness and deformation. Do not place wooden boards or timber in rooms directly exposed to sunlight, as sunlight can accelerate the aging of paints and adhesives used in wood product decorations. Rainwater can cause wood products to swell, deform, or even mold, so special attention should be paid during renovation.

Wallpaper and paint should naturally dry in the shade.

Paint and wallpaper are commonly used wall decoration materials. In spring, due to high humidity, people often open windows and doors to let the walls dry quickly. However, in summer, due to dry conditions, wallpaper is usually soaked in water before laying, then brushed with glue and pasted. If windows and doors are opened at this time, the just-laid wallpaper will be blown by crosswinds, causing rapid dehydration and shrinkage deformation.

Eliminate moldy smell.

In home renovations, there is often an issue with latex paint drying slowly and developing mold and odor in humid and hot weather. A common solution used by renovation companies is to use air conditioning to dehumidify, removing moisture from the air; furniture cabinet doors are left open to maintain ventilation. After the renovation, various odors may linger, affecting people's health. Renovation companies recommend placing more green plants after the renovation, or putting two or three lemons, oranges, or bananas in the room, which can effectively remove odors quickly.

Do not paint during rainy days.

For wood products, whether brushing clear varnish or mixed paint, remember not to paint on rainy days. On rainy days, ask the construction team to do other tasks first, or even temporarily stop work for two days. Do not rush to repair.

In spring, the air humidity is high, and the moisture content in walls, floors, and wooden furniture is relatively high. Entering summer, the air gradually becomes dry, and seasonal changes may cause some issues due to differences in temperature and humidity, such as floorboard contraction and increased gaps between boards; wall and door frame seams appearing due to different shrinkage rates of various materials; wall cracking... These are all normal phenomena associated with seasonal transitions. Repairing at this time allows continued moisture evaporation, and walls may continue to crack. Repairs should be carried out when the wall moisture matches the external climate, resulting in better outcomes.
