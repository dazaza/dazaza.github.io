Madonna Cheats on Guy Ritchie, Who Skips Cash for Divorce Attorney

by kouhong on 2009-02-10 22:31:35

The marriage of US pop queen Madonna and her British director husband Guy Ritchie has hit the rocks. The Sun reported that Madonna has hired Fiona Shackleton, the divorce lawyer of Prince Charles, to protect her interests. However, this marriage seems to have left unpleasant memories for Guy Ritchie, who has publicly announced that he does not want Madonna's money, but just wants a divorce to free himself from her shadow.

At 50, Madonna is 10 years older than Guy Ritchie. She married him at the end of 2000 and moved to the UK, living quietly as a housewife for a few years before returning to the stage. Having been in the entertainment industry for over 20 years, she owns nearly 300 million pounds (about 765.87 million new dollars) worth of assets. Moreover, Madonna is a "real estate expert" with a total of 8 properties in the UK and the US, valued at over 35 million pounds (about 89.35 million new dollars), four of which are residences. As their marriage comes to an end, due to the lack of a prenuptial agreement, theoretically Ritchie could sue for up to half of her assets, setting a record for the highest alimony in UK history.

Madonna and Guy Ritchie have three children, but only Rocco is Ritchie's biological child. In order to gain an advantage in the divorce case, Madonna specifically hired Fiona Shackleton, a renowned British divorce lawyer who had previously handled the divorce case of British singer and former Beatles lead vocalist Paul McCartney.

On Wednesday evening, Ritchie and his lawyer began negotiating the divorce agreement presented by Madonna. It is reported that this agreement includes a provision: Ritchie and his family are not allowed to publicly discuss the details of the divorce. Meanwhile, there was a preliminary result regarding the division of their properties: Ritchie will obtain the Escot Estate in Wiltshire, but all the artworks and antiques within the estate — especially two Picasso masterpieces — will not be under his name.

Additionally, Ritchie will receive subsidies for multiple mansions owned by him and Madonna in London and New York. Any property that Madonna gifted to Ritchie during their marriage does not need to be returned; similarly, any gifts Ritchie gave to Madonna will remain in her storage room.

With the split becoming a fact, Madonna and Guy Ritchie began exposing each other's secrets. In comparison, Madonna finds herself in a difficult situation because her family also expressed dissatisfaction with her decision to divorce again. Madonna once had a tumultuous marriage with Hollywood actor Sean Penn.

An insider said: "Madonna's plan to divorce again has been severely criticized by her father, who angrily believes that Madonna and Guy Ritchie did not make more efforts to resolve their differences or consider their children." On October 15th, local time in the US, the legendary diva Madonna and her director husband Guy Ritchie officially announced the end of their nearly eight-year marriage. Thus, the rumors about their divorce finally reached a formal conclusion.

Madonna's spokesperson Liz Rosenberg told the media that the couple had agreed to divorce and requested the media to respect their privacy. However, Ritchie's spokesperson indicated that the couple had not yet fully reached an agreement on issues such as asset division. "Guy Ritchie bears the main responsibility for the divorce," an insider revealed: "He couldn't wait to announce the news, as their marriage had lasted for a long time." Another insider said: "Almost all of Madonna's friends and relatives knew about it, they thought Madonna found it hard to spend life with anyone. But thank goodness, she still has her children." They wanted to finalize the divorce procedures before their 8th wedding anniversary.

On the morning of October 15th, British media had already spread the news of their separation. According to The Sun, an unnamed source close to the matter revealed that they planned to complete the divorce procedures before Christmas, as this year's Christmas would mark their 8th wedding anniversary, a date they no longer wished to celebrate together. At 50, Madonna and 40-year-old Guy Ritchie "could not bear to continue living together anymore."

This source said: "It's very sad, they were a great couple and wonderful parents, they just couldn't live together anymore. Despite previous hopes to mend these gaps, both realized that divorce was inevitable, just a matter of time. Both parties tried hard in the past few months, but ultimately lost control." It is understood that Madonna wanted to focus on her work and discuss marital issues after her world tour ended, but Guy Ritchie refused to continue pretending. Eventually, Madonna compromised. "Guy Ritchie said if they were going to separate, it had to be now. Madonna initially didn't know whether they should try separation or divorce. But now she agrees, divorce is the best course of action."

Although Madonna and Guy Ritchie hoped to finalize their divorce this year, divorce lawyers stated that such cases are difficult to handle and the earliest resolution would be around February next year. Fiona Shackleton (right), who helped McCartney retain 100 million pounds worth of assets, is representing Madonna.

Even though rumors about pop queen Madonna and British director Guy Ritchie getting a divorce had been circulating for quite some time, neither party had confirmed them until now. Just turning 50 in August, Madonna couldn't escape the 'seven-year itch'. Reuters, People magazine, Daily News, The Sun and several other media outlets published reports yesterday (the 16th) indicating that the couple had formally announced the end of their less-than-eight-year marriage.

They no longer wish to pretend to be a loving couple. Madonna met Guy Ritchie through the introduction of Sting, a British rock star. They got married in an ancient Scottish castle in October 2000. Previously, she was married to Hollywood bad boy Sean Penn in 1985 and divorced four years later.

Currently holding the "Sticky & Sweet" world tour in Boston, Madonna herself hasn't explained the reasons for the divorce. However, her female spokesperson announced: "Both Madonna and Guy Ritchie agree on the divorce. During this special period, they hope the media can show respect towards them and their family." According to The Sun, since their recent heated arguments over the time spent on their respective careers and Madonna's intention to adopt another African girl, it became difficult for them to continue living together.

Rumors about Madonna and Guy Ritchie's divorce have surfaced from time to time. In July, it was even rumored that Madonna had an affair with Alex Rodriguez of the New York Yankees, leading to his divorce. However, Madonna denied it publicly and firmly stated: "I and my husband absolutely have no plans to get divorced." Afterwards, she took time off from her tour to attend the London premiere of Guy Ritchie's new film "RocknRolla." Considering Madonna's ongoing tour and Guy Ritchie's busy schedule filming the new movie "Sherlock Holmes" starring Robert Downey Jr. and Jude Law, they originally planned to officially announce the divorce next year. However, due to their recent busy schedules and lack of time to handle various issues between them, their relationship deteriorated further. With Guy Ritchie's urgent desire for divorce, Madonna finally decided not to pretend to be a loving couple anymore.

The end of the marriage also marks the beginning of a war, as the wealthy Madonna is bound to face a divorce lawsuit. As one of the richest female artists in the world, Forbes magazine estimated her income between June 2006 and July 2007 at $72 million. It is reported that the joint assets accumulated during their marriage amount to $521 million, and they did not sign any prenuptial agreement. A friend of Madonna revealed: "She wasn't unaware of a prenuptial agreement before marriage. Someone suggested it to her, but she rejected it. Like many women in love, Madonna believed her marriage would last forever."

Despite Guy Ritchie previously telling friends that he didn't care about Madonna's money, hiring Fiona Shackleton, the famous divorce lawyer, indicates that Madonna is prepared to defend her assets. It is reported that Guy Ritchie hopes to secure half of the assets from this divorce lawsuit. However, according to the Daily Mail, Madonna is only prepared to give him a property worth $12 million and $15 million in cash.

"She is feminine, successful, and uncompromising in the face of difficulties. Whether as a negotiation partner or opponent, she deserves respect." This is how Madonna's divorce lawyer Fiona Shackleton evaluates her new client, and this description applies equally well to herself. As a mother of two, Shackleton earns $900 per hour handling lawsuits, making her arguably the best divorce lawyer in the UK — comparing her to the "Madonna" of her field is no exaggeration. She was employed by Prince Charles to handle his divorce from Princess Diana; last year, Shackleton served as Paul McCartney's divorce lawyer, reducing his alimony payment from £125 million to £24.3 million. Heather Mills, McCartney's ex-wife, once slandered her, calling her "the enemy of all women." This time, Shackleton and Madonna have formed a "tough woman alliance."
