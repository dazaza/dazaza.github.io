TCM辩证论治 Strict prevention of the outbreak of H1N1 influenza pandemic

by bbeij62s on 2009-12-03 16:26:43

Due to the fierce development of the current epidemic, it is imperative to be vigilant against the second wave of H1N1 influenza outbreak in the community. Residential areas, with a large number of migrant population, see droplets as the main culprit for the spread of flu outbreaks. When patients speak or cough, they can easily transmit the virus to others through droplets, which may lead to a rapid increase in cases and a batch of severe cases.

With the arrival of autumn and winter, the domestic transmission speed of the A/H1N1 influenza virus has accelerated, and the second wave of the H1N1 influenza epidemic has already begun. Experts recommend using Baihu Decoction for high fever clearance, and prescriptions like "Sangju Drink" and "Dihuang Decoction" for heat-clearing and detoxifying.

In response to the second wave of H1N1 influenza, the principle of TCM syndrome differentiation and treatment is the general guideline for treating infectious diseases. Focusing on removing pathogens, "removing pathogens" is the common method of treatment, which can be considered the ideology behind TCM's treatment of infectious diseases. Professor Shen Qingfa, a nationally renowned expert in traditional Chinese medicine for infectious diseases and a member of the Shanghai H1N1 TCM Prevention and Control Expert Group, pointed out that TCM has made significant contributions in preventing and controlling epidemics. For example, Daguanyin and Shengjiangsan can be used for dispelling turbidity; "Sangju Drink" and "Dihuang Decoction" for clearing heat and detoxifying; Baihu Decoction and Qingwen Baidu Decoction for clearing high fever; and Angong Niuhuang Pill, Zixue Dan, Zhibao Dan, and Shexi Dan for opening the orifices and awakening the spirit.

There are also health preservation methods for prevention. Traditional Chinese medicine has accumulated many excellent health preservation methods, such as medicinal diets, acupuncture, massage, scraping, cupping, qigong, Jing Chan, Wuqinxi, Baduanjin, Tai Chi, Yijinjing, etc., which help adjust the internal balance of the human body, achieving the state where "yin and yang are balanced, and the spirit is regulated." Additionally, preventive measures should include spreading knowledge about infectious disease prevention into communities.

Prevention-oriented is an effective method of TCM in treating infectious diseases. The main measures include: fumigation disinfection, as recorded in multiple places in "Compendium of Materia Medica": whenever an epidemic spreads, drugs such as Atractylodes, mugwort leaves, Angelica, cloves, and sulfur can be burned indoors for air disinfection. Disinfection can also be achieved by making drugs into smoky agents or aerosols; vinegar disinfection, a method still used today. Boiling disinfection, for items touched by patients, Li Shizhen proposed that they should be steamed in a steamer or boiled in boiling water for disinfection, ensuring that "no one in the family gets infected."
