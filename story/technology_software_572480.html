Yun.io - A Powerful and Thoughtful Domestic Cloud Storage Service Supporting All Platform Terminals (Exclusive Review + Video + Multiple Images)

by xdash on 2011-11-20 12:30:29

Yun.io is a newly launched domestic cloud storage service that supports all platforms, including Windows, Android, iOS, MacOS, Linux, and Web. It is feature-rich and offers an excellent user experience.

Cloud storage products are becoming increasingly popular, but inevitably suffer from severe homogenization, to the point where I am no longer inclined to write dedicated articles introducing new services, preferring instead to include them in my weekly product newsletters. However, Yun.io stands out as one of the rare excellent products in this field that I've seen recently. Its product design, interface, performance, and strategy are all commendable. Notably, Fetch.io, a tool for extracting and downloading foreign network disk resources that we introduced previously, was also developed by the same team. After three years of quiet development, Yun.io has finally been made public.

Storage Space

Yun.io provides free users with 5GB of initial cloud storage space. Additionally, users can obtain an extra 5GB of space (for a total of 5GB + 5GB = 10GB) through methods such as binding devices, verifying email addresses, inviting friends, and assisting with promotion.

Platforms

Unlike other cloud storage products that delay consideration of Mac/Linux platforms, Yun.io released clients for these two platforms on its launch day, showing great sincerity. The iPhone and Android versions are unsurprisingly included in the list, although it may take some time before they become available for download (@XDash has already obtained the Android beta version, see below).

Web Interface

The main interface after logging into the web version is shown in the figure below. It supports operations such as previewing, downloading, renaming, moving, copying, deleting uploaded files, creating new folders, generating external links with one click, and sharing to Sina and Tencent Weibo.

File uploads via the web version are extraordinarily powerful. Not only does it support selecting multiple files at once for simultaneous upload, but the speed is also impressive. During testing, I uploaded a total of 118MB of PDF e-books, which took only about 1 minute, averaging around 2MB per second, with the upload progress numbers rolling rapidly.

When viewing files shared by others, the web version also supports saving the shares "to your own Y drive" with one click. This is a demonstration of an e-book sharing external link.

PC Client

After the initial installation of the PC client, there is a relatively detailed introduction, and an icon is generated in the system tray (even learning from Dropbox's big arrow indicating the tray icon, except Yun.io uses its own cartoon robot image):

After installation, a Y drive will be mapped locally on your computer, and file operations in this folder will be synchronized to the cloud, similar to how tools like Dropbox work.

I tested the upload synchronization speed of local files, reaching nearly 1MB/s at its highest, with an average speed of over 600K/s.

Additionally, the PC version supports pausing synchronization, local area network synchronization, and screenshot functionality. Just like QQ screenshots, selecting a screen region automatically sends it as an image to the cloud storage space and generates an external link address, making it easy to quickly exchange information that cannot be easily described with words. This is a demonstration of a screenshot sharing external link.
