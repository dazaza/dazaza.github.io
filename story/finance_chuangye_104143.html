Hangzhou rental properties can be used as business premises for entrepreneurship

by huangxin27 on 2008-11-17 14:14:29

Rented houses and family residences can be used as business premises; a rent subsidy mechanism will be implemented; market access conditions will be relaxed... This afternoon, the Hangzhou municipal government held its 36th executive meeting. The meeting reviewed "Several Opinions on Encouraging and Supporting College Students to Start Their Own Businesses in Hangzhou" (hereinafter referred to as "Opinions"). Hangzhou will encourage and support college students to start their own businesses in multiple ways.

At the meeting, the most debated topic was whether rented houses and family residences could be used as business premises. Many representatives present expressed that using rented houses as business premises might affect the normal lives of surrounding residents.

"Ma Yun of Alibaba started from a rented house, and GOOGLE also went out from a garage. For the IT industry, all you need at the beginning is a table and a computer, which will not affect the surrounding residents. I suggest the mayor consider restricting industries that can start businesses in rented houses," said Fang Yi, a college student, who made a video suggestion to Mayor Cai Qi of Hangzhou.

After listening, Cai Qi said that neighbors should communicate more and understand each other. The enterprises starting in rented houses must be strictly controlled, and they must not affect the lives of surrounding residents.

College students' entrepreneurship achieves zero registration fees

The "Opinion" proposes to open a green channel for industrial and commercial registration and implement free industrial and commercial registration. For college students who start their own businesses in individual operations, the industrial and commercial departments will exempt administrative and institutional charges such as registration, management, and certification categories; for college students who start their own enterprises, the industrial and commercial departments will exempt administrative and institutional charges such as registration and certification categories.

The minimum registered capital for entrepreneurship is 30,000 yuan

For enterprises established by college students who start their own businesses, except as otherwise provided by laws, regulations, and administrative licenses established in accordance with the law, the minimum limit of enterprise registered capital shall be reduced to 30,000 yuan across the board, relaxing market access conditions; encouraging college students to value intellectual property rights, physical objects, scientific and technological achievements, etc., as priced contributions; allowing college students' enterprises to finance through equity pledges.

Expanding the scope of college students for entrepreneurship

The "Opinion" also expands the scope of college students who can enjoy supportive policies. In the originally implemented college student entrepreneurship assistance policy in Hangzhou, the scope of college students was defined as university graduates after 2007 within two years after graduation.

Now, the scope of college students has been extended forward to university graduates after 2005, and backward to five years after graduation.

Universities should offer courses to cultivate entrepreneurial talents

At the meeting, some netizens also proposed that what college students lack most in entrepreneurship is experience, hoping that the municipal government can create a platform for college students, with specialized legal, financial, and strategic investors helping college students to start their own businesses.

Cai Qi said that universities need to make up for lost time - entrepreneurship courses. Universities are not just about academic education but also about entrepreneurial education, helping and encouraging college students to start their own businesses.
