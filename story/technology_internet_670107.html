Qixi Festival flower ordering, buying flowers, loving flowers and growing flowers

by kissxh770l on 2012-02-20 15:24:24

I love flowers, but I don't like to see them fresh in the vase for a few days and then wither forever in sorrow. I like to grow flowers because I want to see the growth of life. Many colleagues, classmates, and friends find it unremarkable that I love growing flowers. They often ask me for advice on flower care or give me some good seeds, such as my American Aloe Vera, which is tall, beautiful, and very practical. I am happy to inform them one by one because knowledge itself is interactive and there's nothing worth keeping secret. The art of flower care is just the combination of knowledge and experience!

I remember liking to grow flowers when I was very young. At that time, our family lived in an apartment building, and there was no place to put flower pots. As a child, it seemed there were not as many potted plants as there are now. I remember growing Aglaonema mostly. With a little soil and water, placed indoors, it would always be green! Every winter, when there were few flowers, I would use a bottle with a larger opening, fill it with a little soil, break off a few branches of Aglaonema, and place it on my desk. When they turned yellow, I would replace them with new branches.

After having my own space, my passion for growing flowers has never diminished. There is no garden in the city apartment, so my husband had a row of flower racks made for me. Actually, the flower racks are too limited; how many potted plants can they hold! However, something is better than nothing! I enjoy buying flower pots, seedlings, seeds, and fertilizers. In the first year, I spent hundreds of RMB. Perhaps this is a matter of mindset. If someone does not like growing flowers, hearing about spending so much money on it in a year would seem incredible. In the flower market, when I see the flowers sold by farmers, bright and beautiful, I buy whatever is pretty. I vowed to make the flower rack have flowers blooming all four seasons, and each pot would have a different variety. For this reason, I almost went to the flower market every day to look at flowers, admire flowers, and buy flowers. Many flowers brought me and my family a lot of happiness. Soon, the flower rack could not bear the load, so I placed the flower pots in the garden of the community. Wow! There were forty or fifty pots of various sizes. This was not the problem. What saddened me was that the flowers I bought home would wither or die within a few days. The mentality of a flower grower! It was really heart-wrenching. Empty flower pots were quickly filled with newly bought flowers, but still could not be well cared for, leaving me feeling depressed! I was extremely upset inside, but I did not want to say it out loud. Each time I bought flowers, I would ask the flower farmer for some advice on flower care, but it was very limited. My husband reminded me, "Aren't you a bookworm? Why don't you buy a couple of books on flower care and read them." Hehe! This method really worked, and I no longer had to deliberately please the flower farmers and beg for a little pitiful advice on flower care when buying flowers.

Books are silent teachers. With books, I learned a lot about flower care. For example: many flowers couldn't survive because of the balcony. When buying flowers, you need to consider the nature of the flowers - do they prefer sun or shade? Balconies are generally semi-shaded and semi-sunny, so don't plant flowers that like sunlight on the balcony. For example, roses bloom all four seasons, have large and beautiful flowers, and come in many colors and varieties. I especially like fragrant roses with a light scent. You smell the fragrance before seeing the flower, but these flowers like sunlight and fertilizer. They need plenty of sunlight all year round. Also, Dahlia belongs to the chrysanthemum family but is not a chrysanthemum. Its flowers resemble what we used to call "sweet potato flowers" when we were children. Only its flowers are large and beautiful, as big as sunflowers, and the stems have many roots shaped like sweet potatoes. The beauty of this flower is loved by everyone! It seems to be a foreign variety cultivated in Kunming, Yunnan Province in our country. This flower is not delicate, but it likes sunlight. I bought it many times, but every time the flowers withered and the roots died, so I had to give up growing it. Also, Hibiscus Syriacus seems to be a cultivated variety. It has large and beautiful leaves, and many people like it. But it also naturally prefers sunlight, making it difficult to grow on balconies.

The best flowers to plant on the balcony are some cactus plants, which are lazy-person flowers. They do not require too much watering or management. Then there is spider plant, which is more elegant; orchid grass, which is refined but harder to grow; Noble Orchid, whose flowers resemble rooster combs, noble and beautiful; and then there is the hanging bellflower, which blooms for business openings and has flowers all year round. The flowers resemble inverted golden bells, very beautiful! There are so many kinds... If you want to grow flowers on the balcony, you should definitely choose those that are easy to grow and live well.

Of course, growing flowers has many benefits. They purify the air, beautify the environment, and provide enjoyment for people. If you also like growing flowers, or if you don't have a private garden, you might as well grow a few beautiful potted plants. For example, you can grow spider plants and asparagus ferns in the bedroom; they are experts at clearing carbon dioxide and purifying the air. In rooms with computers, it's best to place two small pots of cactus, which are great at cleaning computer radiation. However, some plants should not be placed indoors because they emit gases harmful to the human body, such as rubber trees and night-blooming jasmine. Although night-blooming jasmine has mosquito-repellent effects, it is harmful to the human body. There are currently about twenty types of plants that are harmful to humans. So if you want to buy potted plants for your home, don't do it blindly! There are specialized books introducing this kind of knowledge, and you must not only focus on the beauty of the flowers without considering other factors.
