Science fiction novel: 3-year-old boy drowns, parents donate his corneas

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-03 10:54:58

Yesterday morning, in the intensive care unit of the Jiaxing Armed Police Hospital, Xiaolong, a boy under the age of 3, passed away. Those who had met this boy said that he was very cute, especially his bright and lively eyes.

Xiaolong's parents firmly believe that their young son will surely become an angel. In their grief, this couple decided to donate all of his usable organs to those in need.

Yesterday afternoon at 3 o'clock, in the Zhejiang Provincial People's Hospital, one of Xiaolong's corneas was transplanted to an uncle whose left eye was almost blind; the other cornea will be transplanted today to a 1.5-year-old little sister.

The parents of Xiaolong, Zhou Changchun and Xiao Kunying, come from Neijiang, Sichuan. About seven or eight years ago, they came to Haiyan, Jiaxing, to work as ship operators for a local brick factory. Their elder daughter is already in university in Chengdu. The birth of their son Xiaolong in February 2009 added much vitality and laughter to the family.

On December 19th in the morning, while Xiao Kunying was doing housework inside the ship, she suddenly heard a "splash" sound outside the cabin. When she ran out, she was terrified - her son had fallen into the water! She lost her mind and kept shouting for help. A man from the opposite ship took off his coat and jumped into the river to save the child.

When father Zhou Changchun arrived, he saw his son lying motionless on the deck, soaked through and unresponsive. He lifted the child upside down to drain the swallowed water and then performed artificial respiration.

After the on-site first aid, even before the ambulance arrived, Zhu Mingguang, the construction site manager, immediately drove the child to the Jiaxing Armed Police Hospital. After emergency cardiopulmonary resuscitation by the doctors, the child's heartbeat and breathing were restored, but he remained in a deep coma and was soon transferred to the intensive care unit.

The child may never wake up again, so his grieving parents decided to donate his body.

Xiaolong's parents are both over 40 years old. This drowning incident reminded them of another painful memory - their original son, who was 12 years old in 2007, unfortunately lost his life to the river while swimming.

It can be imagined how tormented their hearts must have been when Xiaolong met with another accident.

From the doctors, the couple learned that the situation was not optimistic, and they should prepare for the worst - "Even if he could be saved, he would likely end up in a vegetative state."

Finally, they made a difficult decision: since their child might never wake up again, let him go peacefully and donate all of his usable organs, so that his short life would not have been in vain.

They contacted the Jiaxing Red Cross Society, which assessed that the child's corneas could save lives!

Through the Jiaxing Red Cross Society, Xiaolong's parents yesterday contacted corneal donation volunteer Zhu Qiangrong, willing to first donate the child's corneas to help those who have lost their sight. Zhu Qiangrong arrived early yesterday morning at the Jiaxing Armed Police Hospital to handle the cornea donation procedures for Xiaolong.

At 10:30 in the morning, Xiaolong's parents entered the ICU for a final visit with their child. The father's dark and rough hands touched his son's white tender face, and tears uncontrollably streamed down. The couple changed their son into a new set of clothes: "Treasure, you used to wear clothes altered from adult sizes, today you can finally wear a new outfit, go well..."

Subsequently, the ophthalmologist stepped forward, bowed deeply, and removed the child's precious corneas.

Almost simultaneously, the blindness prevention guidance center set up in the Provincial People's Hospital was also busy: the head nurse opened the register of patients urgently needing corneal surgery and called the two patients at the top of the list - Xiaolong's diamond-like clear corneas would bring them light.

The first one in line was Zhang Xingyue from Xiaoshan. At the age of 48, more than ten years ago, his left eye was infected with a virus, leading to keratitis that turned into corneal leukoma opacity, almost blinding his left eye. Yesterday afternoon at 3 o'clock, everything was ready, and Xiaolong's cornea was successfully transplanted to Zhang Xingyue. The chief ophthalmologist Xu Zhineng estimated: if there were no unexpected incidents, today he could see a clear world again.

The other cornea will be donated today to a 1.5-year-old baby named Xiaoyue (a pseudonym).

Xiaoyue suffers from a congenital abnormality similar to a tumor in her right eye known as corneoscleral dermoid. As she grows older, it might cover her pupil and cause blindness. Removing the tumor and performing a corneal transplant is the ideal treatment method. The earlier the corneal transplant, the less harm it does to her.

Yesterday noon, after Xiaoyue's parents received the call, they immediately rushed to the Provincial People's Hospital from Xiaoshan. Because the child had eaten lunch and the operation under general anesthesia required fasting for 8 hours, the surgery was postponed until today.
