There was a girl named Qian, whose father had already passed away before she was born. She lived with her mother, grandmother, and stepfather, but neither her grandmother nor her stepfather liked her. Therefore, when she started high school, her mother sent her to a private school. The private school was full of children from wealthy families, and among these aristocratic heirs, Qian stood out as an oddity. Her monthly allowance was only 123.5 yuan, which was just enough for her to eat every month. Thus, she would wait until her classmates had finished eating, then hide in a corner to eat steamed buns and pickled vegetables. But the girl's strong-willed nature made her excel academically; she always ranked first in the entire grade. Many parents of wealthy children hoped she could tutor their own children, but she disliked the attitudes of rich kids, so she refused them all.

Not long after, a boy named Gong in her class asked Qian, "Can you help me with my studies?" Qian gave a disdainful laugh and said, "Why should I tutor you?" Gong replied earnestly, "Because my grades are poor!" This statement made Qian look at him again, and this glance made her feel that Gong was different. Unlike other wealthy children, he always wore an old overcoat and lacked the arrogance of the nobility. Thus, she agreed to tutor him. Every day, Qian tutored Gong, which also increased her income. Gradually, they got to know each other better. They learned that Gong's birthday was on December 31st, the last day of the year, while Qian's birthday was on January 1st, the first day of the year. Gong joked, "I am exactly one year older than you!" Qian responded indifferently, "Only by one day!"

That day after school, a BMW was parked at the school gate, which many wealthy children had never seen before. It was Gong's family coming to pick him up. As Qian watched Gong walk towards the car, she thought, "Why can they be so rich? Why can they drive a BMW?" At that moment, Gong turned around and gave her a faint smile before getting into the car.

Qian shook off her thoughts, thinking, "Why should I envy him? He isn't better at studying than me!" Days went by like this. Despite Qian tutoring Gong every day, his grades did not improve significantly. The college entrance exam approached, and they were both focused on their final reviews, no longer having time for tutoring. Qian was determined to get into Tsinghua University because she didn't want to be looked down upon by others. When the exam results came out, Qian successfully enrolled in Tsinghua University with a score exceeding the second-highest student in the entire school by 50 points.

Since school hadn't started yet, Qian wanted to go home to see her mother, as they hadn't seen each other for years. However, as soon as she entered the house, her stepfather beat and scolded her. Her mother protected her from behind, but Qian left the house in frustration. When she reached the building's balcony, she wished to see her mother one last time before leaving, but instead, her grandmother threw all her luggage down. Heartbroken, Qian left home and returned to Beijing.

Back in Beijing, Qian prepared to find a place to live. Just then, she saw an old woman tidying up a warehouse. She walked over and asked, "Old lady, is this house for rent?" The old woman smiled and said, "Young miss, do you want to rent a room?" Qian nodded, "Then it will be 100 yuan!" The old woman replied. While Qian was tidying up the house, she met Gong, whom she hadn't seen for a long time. Not wanting others to see her in such a pitiful state, she angrily asked, "Why are you here?" Gong still dressed casually as usual and said with a peculiar expression, "I'm visiting my aunt!" Qian felt as if she had been exposed and remained silent. Gong asked, "I heard you got into Tsinghua, congratulations!" Qian wasn't ungrateful, so she said, "Thank you! What about you?" Gong unhappily replied, "My dad wants me to study abroad!"

Gong continued, "Would you help me with something?" Qian asked curiously, "What is it?" Gong said, "Would you work as a typist at my mom's company?" Qian immediately became angry and said, "Are you pitying me?" Gong hurriedly explained, "No, my mom's company has recently had poor performance, many employees have left, and there's currently a shortage of staff. You can consider it as helping me, alright?" Qian agreed and worked at Gong's mother's company every day, earning a monthly salary of 600 yuan, which satisfied her greatly.

As Gong was about to leave for abroad, Qian invited him for a meal before he departed.

After Gong left, Qian continued to work hard, balancing her studies and job. By now, her monthly salary had reached 2000 yuan, and Gong's mother, considering her convenience, provided her with a laptop. In others' eyes, she seemed quite wealthy, though Qian neither admitted nor denied it.

In Qian's dormitory, there was a Beijing girl who was rather spoiled but never bullied Qian. Qian remained as competitive as ever, unable to tolerate anyone surpassing her academically. However, this time, she encountered a rival. A boy named Lin consistently scored higher than her, making her very upset and determined to surpass him. During a review class, Qian thought, "Next exam, I must surpass him!" At that moment, the teacher asked a question and called on Qian to answer, but since she wasn't paying attention, she shook her head. The teacher then asked, "Does anyone know the answer?" ...Lin stood up and answered correctly, receiving praise. Qian thought resentfully, "You think I don't know? Let me tell you!" After class, Qian stopped Lin and said, "Next exam, I will definitely surpass you!" The classmates present were surprised.

When the exam results came out, Qian indeed achieved first place. She searched for Lin's name on the score sheet but found that all his scores were zero. She was very angry: "If you don't want to compete with me, just say so!" Her roommate informed Qian that Lin was waiting for her by the lake on campus. Furious, she ran over and said, "What do you mean? Is this some kind of joke?" Lin remained silent and handed her the test paper. Confused, Qian asked, "How did you get the test paper?" Lin replied, "My aunt works in the academic affairs office..." Before he could finish, Qian interrupted, "Rich people are all like this! No wonder your scores are always so high!" Calmly, Lin explained, "But I've never used such connections. I've already completed the test; you can check the score!" Qian calculated the total score and found it was still higher than hers. She was very puzzled... Suddenly, Lin hugged her and said, "I like you!" Holding a boy in her arms, all her pride disappeared, and thus they began dating. Soon, they became intimate. Over time, she would regularly give Lin pocket money, which became a normal occurrence between them.

Qian's birthday was approaching, and she imagined what gift Lin would give her, thinking, "The pocket money I've given him is enough to buy me a necklace or a diamond ring." She felt happy just imagining it!
