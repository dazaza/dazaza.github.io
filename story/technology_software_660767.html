Steel consumption off-season after holiday, steel price experiences first adjustment

by lklkkgfeg5 on 2012-02-08 10:48:04

The steel consumption peak season is not as strong as expected, and the steel price has seen its first adjustment after the holiday - Today's Focus - Industry News - Building Materials Network. According to the latest monitoring data provided by a well-known domestic steel spot trading platform, Xiben New Dry Line, in the past week (4.19-4.23), domestic steel prices saw their first adjustment after continuous increases for 8 weeks, indicating that a short-term turning point in the steel market may have arrived.

Last week, the regional steel price changes according to Xiben New Dry Line were as follows: Steel prices in the Shanghai area continued to fall, with a weekly reduction of 60 yuan/ton, reaching 4290 yuan/ton by April 23; Steel prices in the Beijing area rose and then fell, reaching 4700 yuan/ton by April 23, still showing an increase of 60 yuan/ton from the previous Friday; Steel prices in the Guangzhou area slightly decreased, with a weekly price reduction of 30 yuan/ton, reaching 4600 yuan/ton by April 16.

Data from the Xiben New Dry Line Steel Spot Trading Platform showed that by April 23, the average price of high-quality secondary rebar 25mm in major domestic markets was at 4600 yuan/ton, down 19 yuan/ton from the previous Friday; the average price of 6.5mm high line was at 4592 yuan/ton, down 28 yuan/ton from the previous Friday. The data indicates that domestic steel prices have been continuously rising for nearly two months, with some cities seeing increases close to a thousand yuan. Such rapid and sharp increases are facing resistance from demand-side negotiations and also significant pressure for adjustment.

Feedback from various trading merchants on the Xiben trading platform indicates that last week, demand in Shanghai further weakened, mainly due to restrictions caused by the World Expo, rainy weather, and downstream market observation. Data disclosed by the spot trading platform shows that last week, the daily average trading volume of steel products continued to decline by 21.39%, specifically manifested by a continued slowing of procurement rhythms by downstream terminal users of the steel industry, and extremely low transactions among traders on the spot trading platform. Notably, this marks the third consecutive week of declining daily average sales on the spot trading platform, meaning that the Shanghai steel market has fallen into an awkward situation of being "not strong during the peak season," making further price hikes increasingly difficult.

Factors affecting the domestic construction steel market last week included the following:

Firstly, a sharp reduction in the number of manufacturers adjusting prices. According to Xiben New Dry Line monitoring data, last week, a total of 13 manufacturers of rebar and wire rod adjusted their ex-factory prices, with a sharp decrease in the number of manufacturers adjusting prices. Since the latter part of this month, although leading large domestic steel mills still announced price increases, the rate of increase without exception declined, and Baosteel's main product hot-rolled coil variety surprisingly remained flat, significantly lower than market expectations.

Secondly, mixed rises and falls in domestic raw material prices. According to Xiben New Dry Line monitoring data, by April 23, the price of 20MnSi billets in the Shanghai area was 4220 yuan/ton, down 110 yuan/ton from the previous Friday; scrap steel prices in Jiangsu province were 2950 yuan/ton, unchanged from the previous Friday; coking coal prices in Shanxi province were 1820 yuan/ton, up 40 yuan/ton from the previous Friday. Additionally, it is worth noting that international scrap steel prices, after nearly two months of continuous increases, recently showed signs of adjustment.

Thirdly, national building materials inventory has fallen for seven consecutive weeks. Xiben New Dry Line monitoring data showed that the total inventory of construction steel in major domestic cities this period was 8.36871 million tons, down 75,310 tons from the previous week, with a much weaker decline compared to the previous week. Moreover, the total inventory in East China region even increased by 0.77%, and the inventory of rebar in Hangzhou increased significantly by 4.8% week-on-week. These signs indicate that as prices continue to rise, the stage of supply-demand negotiation is intensifying.

Xiben New Dry Line Chief Analyst Liu Qiuping introduced that there are several factors that will affect future market trends, which deserve attention:

Firstly, unprecedented government regulation of the real estate market. Following the new policy on second home loans, the State Council quickly launched a new policy on third home loans and proposed accountability for curbing excessively fast-growing housing prices for the first time. On April 22, the CBRC fundamentally overturned the recognition of second home loans to be limited by property ownership. Furthermore, real estate companies' financing plans in the capital market have been suspended, and housing consumption taxes and purchase restrictions will soon be piloted in some cities. All these signs indicate that the "combination punch" of the government's control over housing prices is much stronger than expected, which will inevitably have a certain impact on future demand for construction steel.

Secondly, unexpectedly low interest rates on three-year central bank bills. Last Thursday, the central bank issued 90 billion yuan worth of three-year central bank bills in the open market. However, despite this issuance volume being six times higher than the previous one, it did not cause the issuance interest rate to rise. Instead, the winning bid interest rate of 2.74% was 1 basis point lower than the initial 2.75%, indicating that under conditions of abundant funds and lack of negative news in the future market, banks' configuration-type demands have been somewhat released.

Thirdly, domestic spot ore prices approaching historical highs. After the China Iron and Steel Association and the China Chamber of Commerce of Minerals, Metals & Chemicals Import & Export requested restrictions on iron ore imports with grades below 60%, the number of new contracts for low-grade iron ore in the domestic market has significantly decreased, exacerbating the tight supply situation for high-grade ores, pushing spot ore prices further upward. By April 23, mines reported prices of 63.5/63 Indian fines at $193-195/ton, with domestic mainstream quotes at $185-187/ton, nearing the historical high of $200/ton set in 2008. However, such high prices have already led to a noticeable weakening of steel mills' willingness to accept bids, posing a risk of spot ore prices peaking and then falling.
