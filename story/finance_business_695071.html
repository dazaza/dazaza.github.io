Night Travel in Brocade: A 14-Year-Old Girl Gives Birth to a Baby Boy, Parents Were Completely Unaware Due to Being Busy with Work

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-07 14:07:25

(By reporter Zhi-Jia Chen and correspondent Xing Liu) Xiaohua (a pseudonym), a 14-year-old girl still in her growth and development phase, recently gave birth to a 3.6-pound baby boy at the Second People's Hospital of Zhuhai. Her parents were completely unaware of her pregnancy beforehand. According to medical staff, in recent years, teenage pregnancies have become increasingly common, leaving hospitals in a difficult position when it comes to handling illegal births by minors.

"Abdominal Pain" Leads to Emergency Childbirth

Yesterday, it was reported from the Second People's Hospital of Zhuhai that the gynecology department had recently admitted a 14-year-old regular patient—Xiaohua. After an urgent examination by the on-duty doctor revealed abnormalities, an obstetrician was called in.

Upon examination, the obstetrician found: "Premature labor, she is about to give birth." This was the first time the department had treated such a young mother—due to her young age, the surgical risks were relatively high, so various emergency measures were immediately prepared.

The doctors immediately spoke with the family. Xiaohua’s mother was shocked upon hearing the news, unable to believe it, clearly having been completely unaware prior. Not long after, Xiaohua delivered a baby boy weighing 3.6 pounds.

Busy Mother Knows Nothing

The obstetrician told reporters that Xiaohua had almost no knowledge of sexual physiology. When questioned about her condition, she was unaware of details such as "when was her last period, when did she get pregnant, and whether she experienced any early pregnancy reactions."

Thus, Xiaohua unknowingly became pregnant and subsequently went into labor without any preparation.

Parents Busy With Work, Unaware of Situation

The parents, busy with their work, especially Xiaohua’s mother who often worked long hours, lacked communication with Xiaohua and failed to notice her special circumstances. Xiaohua didn’t tell her mother many things. Her first menstruation began at the age of 12. As her body was still in its developmental phase, her menstrual cycle was irregular at first—sometimes one month, sometimes two months.

During the early stages of her pregnancy, due to "amenorrhea," her mother had taken her to see a doctor and had her take Chinese medicine for regulating menstruation for a period of time. "But it never worked," and later, Xiaohua’s belly began to grow slightly larger. Her mother thought it was due to her being in the developmental stage, and with the added bulkiness of winter clothing, she didn't pay much attention.

Recently, Xiaohua complained of "abdominal pain," and her parents took her to a nearby clinic where treatment proved ineffective. They then visited another hospital which immediately recommended transferring her to a different facility. Under the accompaniment of her parents, Xiaohua was admitted to the Second People's Hospital of Zhuhai.
