Bromhidrosis can be inherited by children

Bromhidrosis is not contagious but it is hereditary. According to surveys, if both parents have bromhidrosis, the likelihood of passing it on to their children is 80%. If only one parent has bromhidrosis, the chance of inheritance drops to 50%. Pregnant women who suffer from bromhidrosis need to pay extra attention and take proper measures to treat it promptly to avoid passing it on to the next generation.

The harm of bromhidrosis to children

Bromhidrosis usually starts during puberty in children. It may lead to the growth of various bacteria and other skin diseases. For teenagers, it might bring significant stress and psychological pressure. Teenagers are prone to developing bromhidrosis, and many of them experience troubles caused by it in daily life.

Xiaomei learned about many cases of teenage bromhidrosis patients from her doctor friend. The mother of one patient told the doctor that her daughter was in the second year of high school at a key middle school in the city. She used to be very lively and outgoing when she was in elementary and middle school, but since she developed bromhidrosis three years ago, she has gradually become more isolated and unwilling to socialize with others.

Some bromhidrosis patients have even more severe conditions, showing tendencies towards obsessive-compulsive disorder. A parent of a male patient told the doctor that his son’s bromhidrosis wasn’t actually very serious, yet he always believed that the smell from his body was too strong. Regardless of whether it was summer or winter, he would spend over an hour showering each time. Moreover, he didn't want to interact with others and had almost no friends.

Some college student patients expressed that bromhidrosis significantly affected their social interactions. One male patient said that no girl was willing to accept him because they disliked his body odor. He also had few friends as everyone avoided him. Now, he doesn't dare to initiate conversations with people anymore.

The age when bromhidrosis appears coincides with the psychological development stage of young boys and girls. If not treated in time, it can cause a psychological burden, and in severe cases, may lead to mental illness. Nowadays, children mature earlier, so the age of onset for bromhidrosis has also advanced. Once bromhidrosis symptoms appear, timely treatment is necessary.
