Sad stories are only told to the person themselves.

by hilfeifeig on 2011-09-18 19:45:22

What has been missed are what we want. What hasn't been missed are what we can accept quietly.

As time flows between my fingers, I feel my star slowly falling from its orbit...

Only when love is gone do people realize its preciousness; many don't know how to cherish what they have until it's lost, and then they see that what was most familiar was actually most valuable.

I've decided to stop crying, just as firmly as you've decided to leave me.

If you truly love, why not say it? Some things, once lost, can never be regained.

When a kite is tired of the sky, will it unhesitatingly fall into the sea?

Even without happiness, there's still a smile. No, there's still childhood.

If heaven is too crowded, then let's go crazy in hell together.

When you truly wait for someone, that person will never appear.

Whose smile fades from whose sight first? Who understands happiness?

You've left, taking all my love with you, leaving only a word of farewell. I hold back tears watching your retreating figure, wanting to embrace you one last time, wanting to tell you I love you one more time.

The air smells of rain, and sorrow is exhausted; the memories in my mind are slowly melting away.

When I cry, you cry too. I thought you weren't as lonely as me. When I smile, you're still crying.

Happiness and sadness are stories we tell ourselves.

When I truly want to protect someone, I realize I've been silently protected by that person all along.

When I most want to say something, it's often when I'm most silent.

When the weather clears, maybe I'll love you all over again.

Talking to loneliness is just because I don't want to believe you've already gone.

The more you try to hold on to something, the easier it is to lose it.

Time will gradually settle; some people will become blurred in your heart. Learn to let go; your happiness needs your own completion.
