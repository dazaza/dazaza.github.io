Pregnancy Knowledge - Four Preparations: Let You Conceive a Healthy Baby!

by gege on 2012-02-27 20:02:32

In addition to the physiological preparation before pregnancy, psychological preparation for both husband and wife is also quite necessary. First of all, expectant mothers must be mentally prepared for the changes during pregnancy. If pregnant, their body shape will inevitably change, causing fluctuations in their psychology. Some women cannot accept the changes in their body shape during pregnancy and thus refuse nutritious diets. This is not conducive to the normal development of the baby.

Moreover, expectant fathers should prepare mentally to take on many responsibilities. Before the wife becomes pregnant, they must consider that after having a child, the romantic moonlit walks and peaceful mornings and evenings will drastically decrease. The wife's care and love for the husband will also be significantly divided and focused more on the child.

Therefore, both husband and wife must be psychologically prepared before becoming pregnant. They should care for and understand each other from before conception and jointly experience this special stage of life.

Supplementing folic acid 3 months before pregnancy

To achieve optimal birth, apart from making physical health and good psychological preparations before pregnancy, nutrition before pregnancy is also very important.

The nutritional supply plan before pregnancy should refer to the principle of a balanced diet and arrange meals according to the physiological characteristics of conception.

In fact, a woman's pre-pregnancy weight is related to the newborn's birth weight. Many low-birth-weight babies are often due to the mother having a lower pre-pregnancy weight or gaining less weight during pregnancy. Therefore, proper nutrition before pregnancy should not be overlooked. First, one must cultivate good eating habits. Food intake should be varied, avoiding picky eating, and paying attention to the intake of proteins, minerals, and vitamins. A large amount of medical data confirms that if a woman lacks proper nutrition before pregnancy, she is prone to anemia during pregnancy. If a pregnant woman is anemic, her fetus may also suffer from malnutrition, resulting in low birth weight, premature birth, or even stillbirth, which is very detrimental to optimal birth.

Dr. Zhang Yuan reminds expectant mothers: Supplementing 0.4mg of folic acid daily from 3 months before pregnancy to 3 months after pregnancy can prevent fetal neural tube defects. Research has found that the lack of folic acid in early pregnancy is the main cause of neural tube defects in fetuses. Supplementing folic acid before and after pregnancy can prevent 70% of neural tube defects.

Choosing the best time for conception

To conceive a healthy baby, it's not only necessary to make the above preparations, but also to grasp the best time for conception. Achieving the following six "bests" will lay a solid foundation for having a healthy, smart, and lively baby.

1. Best conception environment: including living and working environments.

(1) Living environment: Clean and quiet, with sufficient sunlight, suitable temperature, and fresh air circulation. There should be no cigarette smell in the house, and it's best not to conceive in newly renovated homes.

(2) Work environment: Before preparing for conception, both expectant mothers and fathers should temporarily avoid occupational hazards and promptly transfer away from jobs involving lead, mercury, benzene, nickel, ammonia, radiation, and other harmful substances that can cause abnormal sperm, miscarriage, stillbirth, premature birth, and fetal malformations.

2. Best conception age:

The best age for women is around 25-29 years old, and for men, it's around 26-35 years old. Below 20 years old, the body has not fully developed, increasing the incidence of premature birth, difficult labor, and malformations. Above 35 years old, the incidence of congenital idiocy significantly increases, and difficult labor is more likely to occur.

3. Best conception season:

Generally, late summer to early autumn is the best season. This is the harvest season when various fresh fruits and vegetables are available, allowing expectant mothers to obtain rich nutrition, beneficial for fetal growth. Also, the incidence of infectious diseases such as rubella, influenza, and meningitis is lower during this time.

4. Best conception date:

To successfully conceive, you need to grasp your ovulation period. If the menstrual cycle is normal, it's around 14 days before the next menstruation; if the menstrual cycle is irregular, you can measure basal body temperature or monitor ovulation through ultrasound.

5. Best conception location:

A place familiar, warm, and comfortable for both expectant mothers and fathers, where they can completely relax. Therefore, the best location is at home.

6. Best conception state:

When both partners' health and emotions reach their best state, it's the best time for conception. Emotionally, both should focus on emotional communication, letting deep love flow between them. Do not force conception under unstable emotional conditions, as it is not conducive to optimal birth and childbearing.
