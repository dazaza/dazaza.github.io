2011 Singapore Self-guided Tour

by feif82358 on 2012-02-20 10:32:02

Self-guided Tour in Singapore | Christmas Food Plaza "The food in Singapore is really delicious! I am full after every meal," Daniel praised. Last time, he almost fled back from Hong Kong because "there was nothing to eat, only Western food." Singapore is an immigrant country, like a hot pot that gathers various ethnic groups from Southeast Asia. The taste is sour and spicy, heavy, which suits Daniel's taste. The food stalls are arranged by the government in shopping malls or public facilities, clean like restaurants. The food looks good, making people drool. There are Thai dishes, Vietnamese dishes, Malay flavors, Korean and Japanese cuisines... The choices are so many that it's hard to decide. (Similar to Shanghai's Grand Food Court, but more densely distributed and hygienic.) No wonder many citizens in Singapore don't cook at home and solve their three meals at the food plaza. (However, recently, the newspapers in Singapore discussed: the price of goods has soared, 50 yuan can hardly buy anything at the supermarket, so some taxi drivers' wives also cook again, preparing breakfast and lunch boxes for their families to save money. "The government said" this is all due to the chain reaction caused by the rise in oil prices. Some citizens said: "After the children go to university, we will live in China, where the cost of living is low." They are too naive, the cost of living in China is not low either.) However, the clean food plaza opens at 10:30, before 10:00 they are still cleaning up and preparing, if you want to eat breakfast, you have to find another way. We found a small street behind the hotel to eat breakfast, with slightly poorer hygiene conditions, selling fried rice vermicelli, roasted meat, etc., with even lower prices, similar to the big food stalls in Hong Kong. The mainland girls working here can't speak English, they can't even say the name of the street they are on, just cutting roasted meat day after day with a sad face, although they have gone abroad, their situation hasn't improved. It is said that schools in Singapore generally implement bilingual education, without English, it's hard to find a good job! It is also said that "Singaporeans are very particular about diplomas, even if you have many years of work experience, if you are not from a prestigious school, it won't be recognized." This is hearsay, I don't know if it's true or not. The cost of living in Singapore is not expensive, which can be seen from eating at the food plaza. Each person spends about 6-10 Singapore dollars per meal (the exchange rate between Singapore dollar and RMB is approximately 5:1), 10 Singapore dollars include fruits or juice, so it's about the same price as the business lunch in Shanghai. The food is mainly fast food, plus small hot pots. Here, you can eat a lot of papaya, pineapple, kiwi, common pears and apples are also particularly sweet and juicy. The fruits are cut into pieces neatly lined up for selection, each piece costs about 0.5-1 Singapore dollar, after selecting, the waiter will cut them into small pieces, put them on a plate, and hand them over to you with bamboo skewers, I originally thought she would pack them in a bag and let me figure it out myself! Unsuccessful search for Bak Kut Teh, still remembered until now The most authentic cuisine in Singapore is probably Bak Kut Teh. We didn't do our homework beforehand, so we rushed to Pagoda Street in Chinatown according to the travel guide to eat Bak Kut Teh at Wong Ah Sua, but we walked around Pagoda Street three times and couldn't find this restaurant! Later, we found the address by comparing the door numbers, but there was a foot masseuse soliciting customers at the door. People don't know where they have gone, peach blossoms still smile in the spring breeze. After returning home, I found that the shop had moved to Enggor Road. Later, when I went to Little India, I didn't encounter the Bak Kut Teh restaurant run by Wong Ah Sua either, leaving regrets. These two old brands are very famous, run by the children of Wong Ah Sua respectively, the taste must be similar, both are light with pepper flavor, but what do I know, I haven't tasted them. And Bak Kut Teh is not sold in ordinary food plazas, it's really not considerate to tourists! High population density Singapore has a population of more than 4 million, 77% of whom are Chinese. Apart from Monaco, Singapore is the country with the highest population density in the world, but it is said that the government is still vigorously introducing talents, hoping to catch up with Hong Kong and reach 6 million. Singapore lacks labor force, there is a whole page of recruitment information in Lianhe Zaobao, however, these are jobs with no technical content. Except for taxi drivers, waiters and cleaners are mostly elderly people. (This indirectly reflects the polarization of the Lion City, many poor people cannot retire peacefully when they are old.) The government encourages childbirth, giving allowances for having three children. Therefore, there are lively and cute children everywhere, having three children in a family is very common. Once while eating, I sat next to a big family, one little girl, two little boys, taken care of by a Filipino maid. That mother has a loud voice, "Sit properly!" (I guess having many children forces her to have a loud voice.) Then the mother went to order food. With the tiger away, the monkeys play. The two little monkeys were rubbing against each other on the table. At this time, a staff member from the adjacent Japanese restaurant called out in a tone to solicit customers, "いらっしゃいませ!" The older brother thought it was interesting and imitated loudly, making the younger brother giggle. The Filipino maid kept telling them to be quiet, but no one listened to her. After repeating a few times, the younger brother loudly said: "Everyone keep quiet, freeze, listen to me!" After getting the attention of the neighboring tables, he also imitated in a strange tone, the staff member became embarrassed, lowered his head, and sighed with a smile. The chef was also attracted to peek out. Seeing that they were being noticed, the children became even more proud and shouted louder. Until we finished our meal, they were still energetically helping the Japanese restaurant attract customers. These children speak multiple languages, it's hard to imagine how their little brains switch between them. Language, learned a lesson. Newcomers are taught English from a young age, so there is no problem with English thinking, but their accents are heavy and their speaking speed is fast, listening carefully, it sounds like they are speaking Fujianese or Cantonese, causing me serious hearing problems, sometimes I even need Daniel to translate for me, (which makes him very proud). So foreigners (whether from Europe or America) also need to adapt for a period when they arrive in Singapore. English is the written language of Singapore, (so although the majority are Chinese, there is rarely any Chinese on the streets.) The official language is diverse, Mandarin for Chinese, Tamil for Indians, and so on. English has been localized in Singapore, besides strange pronunciation, the vocabulary also incorporates characteristics of Indian, Malay, and Fujian dialects, forming a unique Singlish, introduced on the official website. To hear ordinary citizens argue in English at the food stalls made me feel greatly stimulated, after returning home, I vowed to only watch satellite TV and listen to English broadcasts, to align with the newcomers.
