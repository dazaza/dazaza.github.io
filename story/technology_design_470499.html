Cisco layoffs begin in the US, China branch yet to receive notice; employees remain calm

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 09:47:18

Sina Technology News, early morning of August 4th: On July 19th, Cisco announced the layoff of about 6500 people. At that time, Cisco said that employees being laid off in the U.S., Canada, and a few other countries would be notified during the first week of August. Yesterday, some American employees of Cisco claimed they had already received layoff notices, with August 19th set as the final deadline for leaving Cisco.

American Cisco Employees Have Already Been Notified

An American employee working at Cisco said yesterday, "Some of our colleagues have already received layoff notices after being called in for discussions with their bosses. According to previous notifications, the list would be announced between August 2nd and 4th."

Unlike previous layoffs, Cisco's decision this time is firm: "In the past, Cisco used to give three months' time to find another job internally, but this isn't a regular layoff (lay off), it's an axe (cut)." This means that all employees on the layoff list must go through the procedures to leave, and there will be no internal adjustments possible. August 19th will be the final deadline for leaving Cisco.

Regarding severance packages, Cisco stated that accumulated pre-tax restructuring fees, including severance pay and other one-time departure benefits, would not exceed $1.3 billion. The specific amount of severance pay for each individual remains unknown so far. "Previously, it was six months' salary; we don't know what it will be this time," the aforementioned employee said.

This employee also mentioned that the basis for this round of layoffs mainly involves adjustments according to products and business lines. Previously, Cisco CEO Chambers had indicated that to restructure Cisco's business, some new product development departments would be cut, non-core businesses would be exited, and the sales department structure would be improved.

Chinese Employees Remain Calm

In contrast to the anxiety felt by Cisco's employees in the U.S., Chinese employees remain relatively calm.

It is understood that from the announcement of layoffs on July 19th up until now, Cisco China has yet to receive any layoff notices.

However, an employee working at Cisco Shanghai said, "From the current trend of these layoffs, the China region cannot escape unscathed; layoffs are inevitable."

As for the situation faced by their American colleagues, this employee commented, "We do have internal discussions, but we're not in a state of constant panic regarding the layoffs."

"We've gone through several rounds of layoffs due to both economic crises and internal adjustments within Cisco; we're used to it," the above-mentioned employee said. "Compared to our American colleagues who face more difficulty finding jobs, we find it relatively easier. Moreover, many are experienced engineers, so moving to other companies in the industry isn't too difficult."

The Largest Layoff in Cisco's History

Cisco's last large-scale layoff was in 2001 when the company laid off 8500 people, which accounted for 18% of its total workforce at the time.

On July 19th, Cisco announced the layoff of 6500 people, and its set-top box production plant in Mexico was sold to Foxconn, transferring 5000 employees along with it. This round of cumulative layoffs will reach 11,500 people.

According to statistics, as of the end of April this year, Cisco had a total of 73,408 employees. This layoff may become the largest in Cisco's history, accounting for 15.7% of its total workforce. (Xiuqian)
