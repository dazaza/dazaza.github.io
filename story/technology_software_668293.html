The helplessness of "Bento Brothers"

by hdghu19056 on 2012-02-17 16:28:40

At 11:35 yesterday morning, a reporter wanted to take the elevator from the 12th floor of an office building in Jiangnan District downtown. A middle-aged man reminded him that at this time, one should either choose to walk down the stairs or first take the upward elevator to secure a spot; otherwise, it's quite normal to wait five to six minutes and still not get on the elevator.

This Mr. Yang told the reporter that there were four to five hundred staff members on the upper floors of the office building, all finishing work at 11:30 AM, so taking the elevator down became very crowded. "To go downstairs for lunch, including finding a restaurant, waiting for food, and waiting for the elevator, it takes about an hour. To save time for eating out at noon, I generally choose to order takeout, but nearby fast-food restaurants or snack shops require half-hour advance reservations. Sometimes when I call to order at 11 o'clock, it may not be delivered until 12 o'clock, and sometimes it might not even be delivered."

Li Xi, a foreign trade clerk working at Yiwu Guomao Building, jokingly referred to himself as a "legendary box meal brother." Over the years, Li Xi has almost eaten all the boxed meals around the office building. When his colleagues bring back boxed meals, he knows which shop they are from without asking. Yiwu Guomao Building is just across the street from the International Commerce City. The set meals in the surrounding upscale restaurants are expensive. For Li Xi and his colleagues with monthly incomes of three to four thousand yuan, these restaurants can only be occasional treats. Li Xi kept more than ten business cards sent by "mobile fast food" vendors. After checking, the reporter found that these "mobile fast food" options ranged from boxed meals to ramen, from stir-fried dishes to dumplings and wontons, with varieties available for everything. Each meal generally costs 8 to 15 yuan. "These business cards are all handed out by them. Now, people like me who work in office buildings mainly rely on these mobile fast food stores to deliver meals," Li Xi said.

"The troubles of 'Canteen Fatty'"

In a planning company on the 9th floor of an office building downtown, Miss Zhou was having lunch with several colleagues around a coffee table. Their lunch was brought from home, and the company specifically prepared a microwave oven for the employees. Miss Zhou envied her friend who worked in a company downstairs because she could eat in the company canteen. "Not only is it free, but it's also more hygienic than the boxed meals from fast food shops. If friends of employees need to have lunch, they are allowed. I've eaten there, and it tastes pretty good, but it's not polite to always freeload."

Under Miss Zhou's guidance, the reporter went to her friend's company. The cook hired by the company told the reporter that there were about a dozen employees in the company, and they all knew each other. Strangers would never be able to sneak in for a meal. If a friend of an employee needed to eat in the canteen at noon, they must give notice in advance. She indicated that no cases of employees frequently bringing friends to eat had been discovered yet.

"I have gained about 15 kilograms since I graduated from university," said Xiao Jing, a friend of Miss Zhou. During her university days, due to financial difficulties, she was very frugal and extremely slim. After starting work, when she first came to the company canteen, she was very happy: there were over a dozen dishes on the table that she could eat freely. As a meat lover, she unhesitatingly picked two chicken legs. After two years, her weight exceeded 60 kilograms, and she was teased by her colleagues as "Fatty." "I guess if I don't switch jobs, I won't be able to lose weight," Xiao Jing complained about her weight while showing a hint of happiness.

During the interview, the reporter found that few canteens served food that employees called "delicious." In popular rumors, canteen food was mostly too salty. "The saltiness of the food could kill someone; I guess they bought too much salt last time!" "Our chef cooks every dish as if it comes straight out of oil!" Such complaints were endless. Some even said, "Every time I eat in the canteen, I'm not full, and I'm starving before work ends."
