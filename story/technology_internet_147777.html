Horse racing development process

by chang79059 on 2009-05-10 17:48:28

From 1842 to 1844, the annual horse races were held in Macao. In 1845, Hong Kong began to hold weekend Grand Prix horse races. By October of 1884, due to the increase in the number of horse races, the "Hong Kong Jockey Club" was officially established. The first preparatory meeting of the club was held at the Hong Kong Town Hall, and its members included foreigners from the German Club, American Club, Western Club, and various foreign firms. At that time, Chinese people were not allowed to join the club but could only be guests. After World War I, the club began to widely absorb members, and wealthy Chinese could also become members and horse owners. In 1960, the club changed its name to "The Hong Kong Jockey Club by Royal Charter."

In its early days, the horse racing activities were amateur in nature. After 1971, horse racing in Hong Kong became a professional activity. Nowadays, the club holds about 700 races annually, which take place at the racecourses in Sha Tin and Happy Valley. Besides betting inside the racecourse, the club also accepts bets outside the racecourse, via telephone, and through self-service terminals. There are now more than 100 off-course betting stations and over one million telephone betting accounts. During the 2001-2002 horse racing season, the club had 1,144 horse owners, 24 trainers, 35 jockeys, and 1,435 racehorses. In the 2002-2003 fiscal year, the total betting amount for horse racing was 71 billion Hong Kong dollars. After deducting 58 billion for prize payouts and 9.5 billion for gambling taxes, the club's revenue was approximately 3.9 billion Hong Kong dollars. The gambling tax paid by the club accounted for 11.7% of Hong Kong's tax revenue. After deducting operating expenses, the remaining profits of the club are managed by the Hong Kong Jockey Club Charities Trust Fund and are mainly used for sports, culture, education, social services, and medical purposes.
