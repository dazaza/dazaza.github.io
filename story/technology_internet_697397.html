Ma Jue, Minister of Supervision, stated that sending children abroad is not an exclusive right of officials.

by lovec4f9 on 2012-03-08 15:18:16

Minister of Supervision Ma Wei received interviews this morning at the north hall of the National People's Congress Hall, answering questions such as [naked officials].

Supervision Minister: Difficult to provide [naked official] data

Sending children abroad is not a special right for officials; property declaration of officials in some areas is under investigation.

How many [naked officials] are there in China? Apparently, it is still difficult to provide statistical data now. This morning, Ma Wei, Deputy Secretary of the Central Commission for Discipline Inspection, Minister of Supervision, and Director of the National Bureau for the Prevention of Corruption, made the above statement when receiving an interview at the north hall of the National People's Congress Hall.

Regarding the situation of implementing property declarations for officials in places like Altay in Xinjiang and Liuyang in Hunan, Ma Wei said that it is not yet possible to evaluate the pros and cons.

Sending children abroad is not a special right for officials.

[Naked officials] refer to public servants whose spouses and children all settle abroad or acquire foreign nationality or obtain permanent residence abroad without work necessity. Regarding the phenomenon of high-ranking officials sending their children abroad, Ma Wei responded that she personally believed that under the situation of reform and opening up, officials and ordinary citizens are the same, and officials do not have any special rights.

For the question of [how many naked officials are there in China], Ma Wei indicated that "it might be difficult to provide this statistical figure now." "The media pays attention, various regions are taking measures, and it is reasonable for everyone to pay attention." She stated that she had also expressed her views on the issue of naked officials for three consecutive years.

It was understood that during last year's Fourth Session of the Eleventh National People's Congress, Ma Wei stated in an interview with the media that China would conduct registration management of [naked officials] for the first time in 2011; at the same time, basic systems would be established to support property declarations for officials and social governance.

During this year's opening of the CPPCC meeting, Ma Wei again revealed that two reports on significant matters concerning officials had been conducted previously. In the first report, a very important summary and evaluation were done, which is also an important basis for the property declaration of officials. Especially regarding the issue of [naked officials], officials took it very seriously during the second report and provided detailed summaries.

The system for reporting significant matters concerning officials will be perfected.

Ma Wei stated that significant matters concerning officials are being summarized. "Promoting this matter, everyone can imagine, should be a gradual process." She stated that there is no exact timetable for the disclosure time.

She said that they are closely monitoring the implementation status of the already launched significant matters reporting system. Last year's report was completed by January 31st this year, and further summarization is required. "Monitoring the implementation status of this system and improving it in a timely manner. This is the measure we currently need to take."

Regarding some central attempts to implement property declarations for officials, Ma Wei stated that some regions are conducting explorations, including places like Altay in Xinjiang and Liuyang in Hunan, each having their own practices. It is currently not easy to evaluate which ones are the best, welcoming the in-depth research of the media and providing relevant situations for the supervision department.

Former Minister of Industry and Information Technology Li Yizhong:

Implementing the broadband project will solve the slow internet problem.

According to reports (Wang Nan, Li Jie, Chen Si), the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology will implement the [broadband] project to solve issues such as slow internet access. This morning, Li Yizhong, Vice Chairman of the Economic Committee of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference and former Minister of Industry and Information Technology, made the above statement during an interview.

This morning, as soon as Li Yizhong appeared at the north hall of the National People's Congress Hall, he was discovered by reporters. Li Yizhong walked behind the reporters, smiling and greeting everyone first, then saying: "I have retired and am no longer in my position, so I cannot answer some questions well, but feel free to ask any questions."

Regarding the issue raised by reporters about the slow domestic internet speed, Li Yizhong said that China has become an information powerhouse, whether in terms of average mobile phone numbers or the number of people online, China is already the world's first.

"We admit that in the setting coverage of networks and mid-to-high-end manufacturing levels, especially in the field of triple network convergence, we have achieved certain results, but indeed there are problems, such as the issue of slow internet speed." Li Yizhong said that implementing the [broadband] project by the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology can effectively solve this problem.

Regarding the situation where some energy-saving and emission reduction targets were not achieved last year, Li Yizhong said that the national energy work conference had clearly set the goal that by 2015, China's total energy consumption target would be around 4.1 billion tons of standard coal. To achieve this goal, Li Yizhong frankly admitted that the pressure is great.
