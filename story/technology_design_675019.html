When it comes to used car insurance, there are industry secrets you can't afford not to know. When purchasing a used car, one should first ascertain whether the original owner had compulsory traffic insurance and commercial vehicle insurance. If not insured, timely supplementary insurance should be arranged; if still within the insurance validity period, one should promptly go with the original owner to the insurance company to transfer the policy. This way, the buyer can continue to enjoy the vehicle's insurance coverage until the end of the insurance period.

Huangjiang Used Cars reminds: Although consumers focus on various performance indicators when conducting used car transactions, used car insurance is equally important for both buyers and sellers. Not only should one check if the vehicle has adequate coverage for vehicle damage insurance, third-party liability insurance, glass breakage insurance, scratch insurance, but also whether it includes spontaneous combustion loss insurance. Timely transfer of ownership is necessary, and unexpired insurance can be refunded.

According to the standard vehicle transaction process, if the car insurance hasn't expired, the remaining balance of the premium can be refunded after the vehicle is sold. Therefore, when selling a car, the original owner should go to the insurance company to reclaim the unexpired car insurance premium, or simply include the unexpired car insurance in the car price, which would be more worthwhile. It is worth noting that although the various performance indicators of the vehicle become the focus of attention during used car transactions, used car insurance is equally important for both buyers and sellers.

Buyers of used cars should not only look at whether the vehicle has adequate coverage for vehicle damage insurance, third-party liability insurance, glass breakage insurance, and scratch insurance, but also whether it includes spontaneous combustion loss insurance. Because used cars are more prone to problems such as aging oil and electrical circuits, once spontaneous combustion occurs and spontaneous combustion insurance is not purchased, losses will be suffered without compensation. In addition, if the used car has additional equipment and the original owner did not purchase additional equipment loss insurance, in case of an accident, the insurance company will only compensate for the loss of the original vehicle, and the repair costs of the additional equipment will have to be borne by the buyer themselves.
