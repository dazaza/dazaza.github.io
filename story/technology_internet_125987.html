Online shopping: Tips to prevent scams

by xlight on 2009-02-20 11:34:35

First trick: Check the company/person's business qualifications

1. See if a formally registered company is operating. Is the company qualification certificate and registration number published? Many provinces and cities' Administration for Industry and Commerce provide online query methods or telephone queries. If no company registration information is disclosed, there must be a problem!

2. Check the contact details left by the company. Since illegal fraud always leaves an escape route, either the address is false or vague, and the contact number is mostly mobile phones or landlines. A merchant who doesn't dare to give you a fixed location or fixed telephone, can you trust his credibility?

3. If it's an individual operating, be careful. Is there a binding mechanism (such as Baidu's Baifubao)? Because the industrial and commercial department has very little binding force on individual commercial behaviors.

Second trick: Check if there are any bad records

A regular operating company should be able to search for a lot of relevant information on the Internet. Including introductions, news, registration information, also including information about being investigated and complained about.

If the store has credit evaluation in Baidu, carefully check the evaluations of customers who have already made transactions.

Third trick: Check if it's a price trap

1. The temptation of profit is always the beginning of deception. Reasonable profit is the basis for a company's survival. If the quote is much lower than the general quote, even half-price, it must be a trap. Any customs detained goods, gray market goods, clearance goods, etc., cannot possibly have such low prices. Recently, several "companies" selling mobile phones and digital products almost at half price in "Shenzhen Shatoujiao China-Britain Street" have been targeted by law enforcement agencies.

2. Online shopping is convenient, with symmetric information. Cheap prices are also one aspect. One strategy of promotion is losing money here but making profits elsewhere. Supermarkets sometimes sell cabbage for five cents per pound, which everyone must have seen. So individual low prices are not surprising, but if all items are low-priced, then it's a trap.

Fourth trick: Check if there are recent transaction and delivery records

Any company that operates normally will have courier or postal delivery waybills. Require the website to provide the express numbers of the recent few customers, or EMS. According to this number, you can check online whether there are successful sending records. Because customer purchase information will not be displayed, there is no reason to refuse this query request.

Fifth trick: Check if payment upon delivery is possible

Payment upon delivery is the Achilles heel of scammers. We don't expect all current Chinese e-commerce companies to offer nationwide payment upon delivery, but the city or region where the company is located should be able to provide payment upon delivery services. This also proves that the company has sufficient strength and is serious about its operations. If this cannot be achieved, don't take the risk.

Sixth trick: Check if product authenticity, gray market/genuine identification methods are informed

Fraudulent companies use tactics such as switching important components, faking, substituting inferior ones for better ones, and confusing genuine and gray market goods to make profits, so they will never clearly inform customers of the identification methods. For example, Sony digital cameras sold online at particularly low prices, below cost price. After investigation: the original battery costs more than 300 yuan, but if replaced with a fake Sony battery, it only needs tens of yuan cost!

Seventh trick: Check transportation safety and after-sales service

The consumption concept and habits of Chinese people focus on price before payment. However, the mature consumption concept in Western countries focuses on services after payment. Online shoppers are all high-quality people, so it is necessary to improve their own consumption concepts, paying attention to issues such as compensation for lost goods during transportation and commitment to after-sales warranty services. Pay special attention to certain brands like Sony digital cameras which require invoices + warranty cards for nationwide warranty services.

Eighth trick: Transfer money to the company account

All formally registered companies have basic company accounts, and the account name is absolutely the full company name. If the so-called "company operation" dodges and refuses to accept transfers to the company account for various reasons, then be careful. If the company does not exist or has been deregistered, the bank will return the transfer.

Ninth trick: Entrust local friends to conduct transactions

Scammers can deceive online because consumers cannot find them, and even if they do, the cost is very high. Entrusting local friends to pick up goods at the door or conduct face-to-face transactions is also a good method.

Tenth trick: Make the first deal with a low-priced item

Practice is the sole criterion for testing truth! If none of the above judgments have problems but you're still a bit worried, you can try buying low-priced goods to gain more understanding of the company's sales, quality, shipping, and services. If satisfied, you can gradually buy some other higher-value goods. Credit is accumulated through long-term integrity. But scammers are just "fishing for big fish" and don't have time to bother with your "small orders".
