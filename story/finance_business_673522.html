**AAPEX 2012 - Las Vegas, USA**

**Exhibition Date:** October 30 - November 1, 2012

**Exhibition Venue:** Sands Expo Center

**Exhibition Cycle:** Once a year

**Scope of Exhibits:**

Various automotive parts, air conditioning systems, car audio equipment, anti-theft products, electronic devices, batteries, seat belts, brake components, chassis, clutches, cooling systems, transmissions and gearboxes, rims and tires, metal components, heavy vehicle parts and accessories, lifting equipment, service equipment, calibration devices, vehicle repair tools and equipment, workshop equipment, automotive lighting, and car beauty products, etc.

**Exhibition Introduction:**

The International Automotive Parts and Aftermarket Service Exhibition in Las Vegas, USA is jointly organized by the American Parts Distributors Association (APAA), the American Service Industry Association (ASIA), and the Motor & Equipment Manufacturers Association (MEMA). It is held annually in Las Vegas and is divided into two exhibition areas: AAPEX and SEMA.

AAPEX stands for "The Automotive Aftermarket Products Expo" and is the largest professional exhibition for the global automotive aftermarket. It gathers users of automotive parts and aftermarket service providers from North America, South America, Europe, and the Middle East. It is one of the largest trade fairs in the global automotive manufacturing industry, focusing on showcasing the automotive aftermarket parts and maintenance products. As the exhibition with the best trade results in the North American automotive parts industry, AAPEX provides an opportunity to connect with buyers from around the world, especially from North America, that are hard to reach at other exhibitions.

**Market Introduction:**

The North American market is the largest demand market for automotive parts globally. The market capacity for automotive parts in the U.S. alone is $60 billion, with more than half relying on imports. Automotive bearings, axles, and hydraulic components are also advantageous products in the North American market, presenting significant development potential. According to statistics, the U.S. market accounts for one-sixth of the global market's annual sales.

The size of the U.S. automotive parts market is astonishing. For example, based on the current number of vehicles in use in the U.S., Americans currently change their oil and perform routine maintenance every 5,000 miles (approximately 8,000 kilometers). If this mileage is reduced to an average of 4,900 miles, the market capacity could increase by $65 million annually. The U.S. automotive aftermarket has evolved over nearly 100 years, from initial car washing and waxing to today’s satellite navigation and performance modifications, witnessing the emergence of countless new products and technologies, which have created wealth miracles for millions of Americans.
