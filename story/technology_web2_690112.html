While violent anger will soon lead you to failure

by affdqwf2 on 2012-03-04 23:51:47

1. Good psychological quality is half of success. Many people tend to have this kind of mentality when they are doing things: they want others' help but fear being rejected; they want to express their own ideas, but fear being looked down upon. Although they may prepare well beforehand, once it comes to practical application, they can't say anything, feel a blank in front of them, and don't know what to do. However, once they leave such occasions, all the feelings return to normal. This phenomenon often appears in people with strong inferiority complex. People with strong inferiority complex are more likely to fail when they are doing things. Therefore, before doing things, one should analyze oneself and the other party more, find out confidence through comparison. In daily life, one should communicate more with others, participate more in social activities, enrich oneself in social activities, establish self-confidence, and possess good psychological quality.

2. Confidence is the driving force for successful matters. To overcome inferiority, one needs to build confidence because confidence is the driving force for successful matters. If a person always thinks that he is not capable and the things he wants to do are too difficult, then he will definitely not succeed; on the contrary, if he thinks he can do it and the things he wants to do are within his ability range, then he will use all his strength and eventually succeed. In short, what is needed when doing things is boldness and meticulousness, full of confidence.

3. Patience is required when doing things. No matter how difficult the situation is when doing things, one must be patient. If you feel abnormally restless, you can tell yourself "There is nothing to be restless about, calm down." At the same time, think about some very peaceful pictures or things, take your thoughts away from the current situation, and you will be very patient, keep calm, and increase the chances of success. Remember, restlessness makes people deviate from correct judgment, and easily gives others the impression of being hard to approach. When you lose patience, you also lose the support of others. Do not always be irritable. People who are irritable and angry will have fewer and fewer friends.

Another key to maintaining a calm mindset is having a sense of humor. People who are good at turning embarrassment into humor are not only smart but also likable. Patient people not only show calmness but also a kind of cultivation.

4. Extensive knowledge and interests help communication during matters. The specific performance when doing things is often varied. People with extensive knowledge and interests can leave a deep impression on others during the process of doing things and increase others' affection towards them. If a person does not know common social skills, such as dancing, light conversation, etc., it will be difficult to communicate with others during the process of doing things, thus making people feel distant and prone to failure. For example, if you go sing Karaoke with everyone, and everyone is singing and chatting enthusiastically, but you sit there alone in a daze, then you will definitely seem strange in everyone's mind. Therefore, learning normal social skills is necessary.

In addition, knowing certain social etiquette is also necessary. For example, how to greet and chat with people; how to participate in some usual ceremonies, how to sit, stand, and walk, are all very important. Mastering more knowledge in this area can make you look elegant and free when doing things.

5. Stay calm in the face of changes and control your emotions. Emotions are the catalysts for the success of doing things. A relaxed and steady attitude will bring unexpected successful results. However, irritability and anger will make you fail quickly. Doing things successfully requires strong self-control and the quality of remaining unchanged.

How to learn self-discipline? The most important thing is to often put yourself in others' shoes and think; sometimes you get angry not because the other party did it intentionally, but unintentionally, and at this time, if you get upset, it shows that you are hard to get along with. If your temper is relatively irascible, then at least when doing things, you should consciously restrain yourself, and after finishing the task, find a way to vent, which must not damage your relationship with others. When you feel angry, you must try to avoid impulsiveness leading to adverse consequences. You might try drinking a sip of tea to suppress your anger.
