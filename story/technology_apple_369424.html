Women who wish to add a "green coat" to their breasts may soon have the opportunity to experiment with a solar-powered bra charger. The solar bra charger, created by Japanese designers, generates enough power to charge an iPod or a mobile phone. It generates electricity from a solar panel worn on the user's abdomen. But the problem with such a design is also obvious - if women are dressed up for travel instead of wearing a short dress, how will the sunlight pass through the clothes and reach the TV board?

"Considering that it is usually impossible for women to go out without clothes, the charger may be difficult to get practical application," said Miko Masuda, a spokeswoman for Triumph International Japan Co., which makes the solar bra charger. The availability of the charger is likely to be limited to a limited number of places such as balconies or gardens." Not surprisingly, with its attractive green design, the solar bra can either convert solar energy into electricity to charge mobile devices, or be directly plugged in and used as a regular charger.
