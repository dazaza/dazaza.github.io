I've been in the process of starting a business for more than half a year and have always wanted to document this journey in words. Unfortunately, too many things happened during the previous period, and now I finally have a bit of spare time to record these bits and pieces of my entrepreneurial experience. At the same time, I will share some of my thoughts on entrepreneurship. My writing skills are not good, so please bear with me!

At the beginning of the year, filled with beautiful imagination and anticipation for the future, I started my entrepreneurial dream. The first step was finding a place. I searched online for many real estate agents and called each one to inquire. Then came the part where I followed their salespeople to look at places. After seeing many that were unsuitable, a female salesperson joked with me, "You must be very picky when choosing a girlfriend!" After an entire week, I finally found a commercial-residential integrated place. It was on the 27th floor, quite high up, with a great view, and even better night views! The location was nice, and transportation was convenient. Opposite were Baishu Steel Market and Yixian Steel Market. Then I negotiated prices with the landlord and signed the contract. Next came ordering office desks, setting up telephones and broadband, assembling computers, and purchasing office equipment...

After dealing with these tedious preparations, more than half a month had passed. By then, the available funds I had for operations were almost depleted, so I had to borrow from here and there again.

Now the most important thing was the company name. Decision-making time! Should I register a new one or transfer an existing one? Registering a new one was more troublesome, as it required a probationary period, and the invoices wouldn't be enough. Also, being a new company would make it harder to attract clients since they might perceive us as inexperienced. In the end, I decided to temporarily pass accounts through a friend's company while looking for a suitable company name. Later on, I really did find a company registered in 1998 whose boss was from Guangdong. The financial crisis made it impossible for him to sustain his company which he had run for 11 years. Of course, there were also issues related to personal management. But this was enough to show how terrifying this financial storm could be!

Before leaving work, she voluntarily shared her feelings about her first day on the job, expressing her happiness to be part of our team and liking the work atmosphere. The second round of the award ceremony began at 15:10, and the final award was for Outstanding Employer, and fortunately, we were on the list. Xiao Liu went up to receive the award, and I captured a few moments.

Finally, this reminded me of what I thought about the world's top 500 companies. Despite coming from different countries with different cultural backgrounds, they not only emphasize investment in employee training but also excel in retail training methods.

Every year, in the Fortune magazine's list of the world's top 500 companies, global renowned retail companies always make appearances, such as Walmart from the United States, Carrefour from France, and Metro from Germany, which we are all familiar with. What are the reasons behind the continuous brilliance of these retail empires? Following the growth footprints of these retail giants, we may discover and summarize many secrets to their success, such as Walmart's consistent "everyday low price" strategy, Carrefour's strict internal management system, Metro's rigorous and scientific membership management, etc. But have you noticed that despite coming from different countries with different cultural backgrounds, they not only attach importance to investing in employee training but also excel in retail training methods.

This also made me think about the two years I worked at Dicos. Many friends say I wasted two good years of youth. But I don't think so. Although Dicos is not as well-known as KFC or McDonald's, as the first domestic Western fast-food brand, their business philosophy, customer service awareness, and employee on-the-job training are quite commendable. This led me to associate with the steel industry I am currently in. Even though it is a sales industry, many companies rarely provide systematic training to new employees. Often, they require new hires to have ** years of work experience or bring their own customers. Or there is a lack of on-the-job training for new employees.

Training, as an important part of enterprise development, is not only a direct tool for enhancing employee skills but also a form of motivation. Through training, employees' professional levels can be rapidly improved, bringing direct benefits to the company. Training also allows employees to feel valued by the company, experience the joy of personal growth obtained through training, and envision a bright future for themselves, inspiring them to work hard positively. Simultaneously, it fosters a learning-oriented organization atmosphere within the company where everyone values learning, enhancing organizational performance and achieving the company's sustained profitability and growth.

Accountant Aunt Li told me that I have to pay a lot of corporate income tax this month. Later, I consulted a friend knowledgeable in accounting and learned that general taxpayers are divided into two types:核定征收 and 查账征收. For 核定征收 companies, the corporate income tax is calculated as 营业额 x 4% x 25%, meaning the income tax is 1% of the turnover. Fortunately, last month, I couldn't buy invoices temporarily, otherwise, I would have issued an additional 500,000 yuan worth of invoices and had to pay an extra 5,000 yuan in taxes. And I didn't even know that the company name I transferred was under 核定征收. The lack of tax knowledge makes me feel ashamed, and I had to buy this lesson at a cost. Here, I warn my friends that if you're transferring a company name, be sure to ask clearly. If you don't understand, consult those with experience. I suffered from being too confident, and everything I didn't know, I looked up on Baidu. Well, learn from mistakes, gain wisdom from setbacks. I must intensively study accounting knowledge. However, hiring a capable accountant is necessary, and reasonable tax planning is essential.
