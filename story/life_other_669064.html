I love you, for an entire past.

by gege on 2012-02-18 20:42:34

I love you, for an entire past.

After the breakup, I still know you, but I no longer want to see you. If you're doing well, I won't bless you; if you're not, I won't mock you either, because from now on, we are strangers. Your world no longer has me, and my world no longer has you. I can no longer cherish you. Sorry, what I've lost is also what you've lost.

Occasionally, you may reach out to me, your sudden appearance still stirs my heartstrings. But I've learned to put up a front with you - neither too cold nor too warm, indifferent and carefree in laughter, and I won't shed those cheap tears anymore. Then I hear you softly say: "You've changed."

It always takes a long time, until there's no retreat left, before we realize that what we once willingly gave up can never be met again in the days to come.

Farewell, I loved you so much, though clumsy, I did try hard. So I have no regrets. Now, I return my love to you. Would you give back the little pride I have left?

I will still believe in love, just no longer in its eternity.

You suddenly reminded me that our acquaintance could be measured in years. You found the one you love, while I am still wandering at the starting point.

We will never be like we used to be, irreplaceable to each other; we will never love as fiercely as before, until tears stream down our faces.

One day, I finally stopped missing him, because he's been gone for too long. My habit is no longer a habit.

I always stubbornly believed that I could face everything with a calm smile, but when you turned around and decided to leave, my tears gushed out uncontrollably. The happiness of the past mocks the pain in my heart. It turns out that the greatest pain in the world is parting.

The most romantic words are when an ex-lover calls and asks, "Are you okay?" You casually reply, "I'm fine," but deep down, you still love them, and you're not fine at all.

When you see through everything, you realize that loss feels more solid than possession.

There was someone who taught me how to love, but then they stopped loving me.

Instead of making excuses everywhere, it's better to simply say, "I don't love you anymore."

Forgetting that person is harder than forgetting yourself. Tell yourself, it's not that you're afraid they'll forget you, but that they might remember you someday. Time takes away memories, but recollections become clearer. If one day they turn around and tell you they've been thinking of you, don't believe it, because they are no longer who they were, and you are no longer who you were.

I've always had you in my heart, just in different proportions.

Now that it's finally time to part ways, he leaves before me, and I feel a bit relieved. This kind of sorrow will eventually be experienced alone by one of us, so let me bear it.

There's nothing unforgettable; sooner or later, I'll forget you - first your appearance, then your voice, and finally the things you said. If not now, then maybe later.

It seems like waiting for a hundred years, and suddenly realizing that even if we meet again, a mature performance is worse than not meeting at all.

In my heart, you've always been there, just in different measures.
