Today, I'll first talk about makeup removal, then another day I'll discuss facial cleansers and skincare routines. This is because it's my first time using Rayli Blog, and I'm still learning a lot of its functions.

In my personal skincare routine, what I value most is thorough cleaning + makeup removal. Pores must be kept clean to accept subsequent nourishing products and essence nutrients.

Many skin problems are caused by improper cleansing:

Over-cleansing —— Peeling, dryness, fine lines

Under-cleansing —— Acne, blackheads, oiliness

Cleansing techniques —— 1. Gently washing your face every day for ten years can make your skin as tender as a baby's. For the first 18 years, I wasn't gentle enough when washing my face. What exactly counts as gentle? You can find out by watching the recommended videos in my posts.

2. Being rough on your skin every day for ten years... No matter how many anti-aging products you use, wrinkles will stick with you, increasing rather than decreasing.

The above are some insights I've summarized personally. If anyone has more good methods, let's chat together :)

One, Makeup Removal (See the first picture)

1. FANCL Cleansing Oil, a cost-effective makeup remover. When the Japanese counter has a promotion, buying two bottles is even cheaper. It emulsifies well, feels fresh without being greasy. Almost everyone has tried this product. ★★★★☆

2. Dior Makeup Remover Milk for Face, Lips and Eyes, one of the first CD products I used. Bought a full set of Snow Fairy for my mom and got this mini sample. The milk-like texture suits winter/dry skin types. Because it’s mild, waterproof eye makeup isn't removed easily, but it claims to be anti-allergic. It leaves the face feeling clean and smooth. ★★★★

3. Clarins Gentle Foaming Cleanser/ Peach Water - Recommended. If you don’t like using oil-based makeup removers and are particular about smell and usage feel, this bottle of peach water would suit you perfectly. One cotton pad soaked in it can remove all makeup from your face, including waterproof mascara! It claims to be a no-rinse cleanser, meaning you could skip the cleanser, toner, and makeup remover. However, I usually wash my face after using it. In winter, when in the dormitory, I fear dryness and cold, so I just apply it and spray some Avene without using a cleanser, without worrying about pimples. It smells good, somewhat like L'Oreal's primer. The price is also reasonable. Make sure to use good-quality cotton pads. ★★★★★

4. ALBION EXAGE Turnaround Cleansing Oil, one of my favorite makeup removers. Persistent use really removes blackheads. Its emulsification is excellent, making your complexion appear brighter. It's also a very reassuring brand. Many makeup removers aren't suitable for acne-prone skin, and using quality makeup removal cream might be safer. However, this product cured a friend's acne-prone skin, which led me to become an Albion fan. Buying the large bottle is cheaper than Shu Uemura, and it often wins awards in the makeup removal category. Highly recommended. ★★★★★

5. Impress IC Cleansing Cream, haven't tried it yet. A certain skincare expert recommended it to me. It's said not to cause acne like oil-based ones, and it cleans gently and thoroughly, causing no irritation or damage to the skin. Very tempting, I want to try it. If anyone has used it, please give some advice. ★★★★★

6. Kose Makeup Remover, a drugstore-level makeup remover, pretty good. May not work for heavy makeup. Adequate for daily wear and sunscreen, slightly expensive and runs out quickly. ★★★

7. DHC Cleansing Oil, although it has many good reviews, I think it's okay. Emulsification isn't complete, a bit greasy for me. I prefer almost complete emulsification. It cleans quite well, so later I liked using it to wash my powder puff. A drop makes a section of the sponge clean...

It's important to keep your powder puff clean.

There's not much else to write. Girls, if you want to test different makeup removers, mainly look at their emulsification, whether they clean thoroughly, and if they're irritating.

1. Clinique Eye & Lip Makeup Remover, cleans well but isn't very gentle. ★★★★

2. Lancome Bi-Facil Rapid Eye Makeup Remover, my favorite and the best eye makeup remover. Whether it's Love Mirror Mascara or waterproof eyeliner, the harder the makeup is to remove, the better it performs. After finishing a big bottle, this is a small sample of 30ml, perfect for travel. Some may say it might be irritating, but imagine using a remover that doesn't clean properly, rubbing back and forth can hurt your eyes and cause eyelash loss. ★★★★★

3. CHANEL Hydrating Cleansing Milk for Face and Eyes, basically only suitable for the face, super durable. The fragrance is the usual light scent of their products. It's a bit hard to press out, feels dry initially, then emulsifies. Don't wash it off immediately; wipe it off with a cotton pad first. Gentle makeup removal, persuaded by BA to buy it. Since I don't usually wear full makeup, it's sufficient for daily light makeup or sunscreen. Often used, but not high in terms of cost-effectiveness. ★★★★

4. Maybelline Eye & Lip Makeup Remover, average performance. Regular eyeliners and mascaras are okay, but waterproof ones are hard to handle. Lip removal is acceptable. ★★★

5. Avon Cleansing Oil, cheap and abundant. Although emulsification isn't great, since we always use a cleanser afterward, it's still pretty good in terms of cleaning. After all, the price is very affordable. ★★★★

6. Shu Uemura Sakura Cleansing Oil, a beloved bottle of cleansing oil. Even though I don't use foundation or powder in summer, I still carefully remove sunscreen, otherwise closed comedones can be troublesome. Excellent emulsification, the face becomes very soft. The fragrance is also nice. ★★★★★

7. Bioderma Micellar Water, a large 500ml bottle, reasonably priced but very effective. Occasionally used for eye makeup removal, soak a cotton pad and wipe the face, makeup is almost completely removed. As it's water-based, there's no need to worry about emulsification. Highly recommended! ★★★★★
