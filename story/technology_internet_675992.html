An excessively high temperature in the external genitalia, irritant foods such as chili peppers, and tobacco or alcohol can cause local congestion and worsen itching. Poor hygiene of the vulva, tight underwear, or wearing synthetic underwear may all stimulate the vulva, causing itching and skin reactions. Overpaying attention to it may create a conditioned reflex, especially repeated scratching, which will more easily exacerbate symptoms.

Its common causes include:

1. Chronic local stimulation: The most common cause is excessive discharge from vaginitis. A reduction in the acidity of secretions promotes changes in the bacterial flora within the vagina, making it easier for local inflammation to occur. There are also many people whose vulvar skin becomes thinner, subcutaneous fat disappears, and atrophic changes appear, possibly extending to the entire vulva and anal area. This manifests as skin shrinkage, hardening, whitening, and on this basis, concurrent vulvar dermatosis occurs. These changes continuously stimulate the nerve endings in this area, resulting in unbearable chronic vulvar itching, which is difficult to distinguish from lichen sclerosus and vulvar leukoplakia and other dermatoses.

2. Irritation from urine, sweat, and anal secretions.

3. Vulvar varicose veins: Skin nutritional disorders and changes in the excitability of peripheral nerves lead to itching; repeated scratching may result in secondary chronic eczema, simple lichenoid sclerosis, thick skin disease, and even progress to vulvar leukoplakia.

4. Vulvar dermatoses secondary to systemic dermatoses: Such as psoriasis, seborrheic dermatitis, chronic eczema, and lichen planus.

5. Systemic diseases: Such as deficiencies in vitamin A and B vitamins; jaundice, leukemia, and diabetes can cause local and systemic itching when severe.
