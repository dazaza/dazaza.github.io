Xiao Yajun said that they had already spent more than 200,000 yuan on their daughter's treatment, and the high cost of treatment made them, who were already in financial difficulty, lose sleep and appetite. However, how to make her daughter face the reality bravely and build confidence in life was what he and his wife worried about most at present.

At present, Xiao Yajun said that he almost sent one or two inspiring text messages to his daughter every day. Once, Xiao Xi replied to him via text message: "Dad is really good", and he cried with happiness at that time. Xiao Yajun said that he was now worried about not having good inspiring text messages to send to his daughter. "My daughter needs confidence most now." Xiao Yajun pleaded with the reporter while crying: "If citizens have good inspiring text messages, I hope they could send one to my daughter in their busy schedule, which will be the best gift for her daughter." Xiao Yajun said that Xiao Xi could move almost only her hands. If she has time to look at the inspiring text messages sent by well-wishers, she would definitely be very happy.

Lawyers help victims for free and sue responsible parties recently

"My daughter needs confidence most now, and I hope kind citizens can send a text message to encourage her!" Yesterday, Xiao Yajun, the father of Xiao Ruoxi, a female college student from Chongqing Real Estate Vocational College, pleaded with this newspaper "to help him out". On October 25 last year, a woman committed suicide by jumping from the sixth floor of a residential building in Sandu Square, Shapingba District, and fell on Xiao Ruoxi walking on the pedestrian street, resulting in Xiao Ruoxi's high-level paralysis. Yesterday, Xiao Ruoxi, who was still being treated in the Sixth People's Hospital of Chongqing Municipality, spoke out to the reporter for the first time: "I want to learn from Zhang Haidi and Sang Lan and live a strong life!"

In addition, Xiao's father thought that there were mistakes and management omissions in the business office of Sandu Square and the property management company of the building, and they were also difficult to get rid of.

In this regard, Hu Chaowan, the lawyer representing Yuan Mei, said that he would still sue the owner who had rented out his house, because the owner was the first person responsible for this case. That night, when he arrived at the hospital, Xiao Xi's spirit was obviously much better, and she even took the initiative to tell him that she received the text messages.

Skyfall fork stick case opens court today, 61 building owners become defendants

"I want to support myself"

In November 2008, Yuan Mei (a pseudonym), 22 years old, was setting up a stall outside Yuzhou New City as usual when a fork stick about 1.3 meters long suddenly fell from the sky, and the metal hook pierced into her head. After timely rescue by the doctor, Yuan Mei was out of life-threatening danger. However, after the incident, the owner of the fork stick did not show up, and no one paid for Yuan Mei's huge medical expenses.

Lawyer Chen Ye said that since the woman who committed suicide by jumping from the building (direct infringer) was an adult, it was rumored that she did not have much inheritance available for execution. If this was true, it would leave a huge compensation gap, and who should bear such supplementary compensation responsibility was still relatively vague in the relevant regulations, and there was controversy in judicial practice. Lawyer Chen Ye said that in foreign countries, in the event of such circumstances, charity organizations often intervened and ensured that the victim's subsequent medical expenses would not be interrupted.

"There was a week when she cried when she woke up." Xiao Yajun said tearfully that during that period, Xiao Xi was contrary to her usual self, annoyed by seeing the light, and they even dared not turn on the light when taking care of her at night.

Sue: Hope the "murderer" shows up

Yesterday, Xiao Ruoxi accepted an interview with the reporter.

Defense: Only one defendant hired a lawyer

In desperation, Lin Lei, Yuan Mei's husband, hired lawyer Hu Chaowan to sue 61 households in the building who were the most suspicious. Regarding the fact that 61 households were sued, but only one was the real "murderer" and the rest of the owners would be wronged, Lin Lei helplessly said that if he was the innocent owner upstairs, http://www.vdisk.cn/down/index/7710106A7758, he would also feel angry and aggrieved, but in order to treat his wife, he could only resort to this strategy. He hoped that the "murderer" could have some public morality, take the initiative to admit the mistake and compensate for the medical expenses when seeing other neighbors being wronged.

Lawyer's statement

"My daughter met with a disaster out of the blue, but now no one pays for her treatment." Yesterday, Xiao Yajun said that the treatment costs for his daughter were almost all donated by all sectors of society. Yesterday, lawyers Chen Ye and Dai Houkui from Chongqing YuanYe Law Firm said that they would provide legal aid to Xiao Ruoxi for free, and planned to sue the family of the woman who jumped from the building, the business office of Sandu Square, and the property management company of the building where the incident occurred to court soon to claim compensation.

News face-to-face

According to Xiao Xi's attending doctor, Xiao Ruoxi's waist was severely hit and broken. Unless a miracle happens, it will be difficult for her to stand up in the future. He said that after multiple surgeries, it would be quite good if she could sit up in the future.

As of yesterday when the reporter went off press, no owner came forward to take responsibility for the matter, and most owners said they would not attend today's trial. Most owners said that even if the court ruled against them (the 61 households), they would not pay compensation, because they felt "guilt-free", but they were willing to donate money to Yuan Mei out of sympathy and help Yuan Mei recover. Source: Chongqing Business Daily

Yesterday, the reporter came to the building and learned that some of the 61 households that had been listed as defendants had rented out their houses before the incident. Therefore, they thought that even if they were pushed onto the defendant seat, it was the tenant rather than them. And most of the owners complained orally to the reporter that they were either working or not at home on the day of the incident, so they would not touch the fork stick, so the fork stick absolutely did not fall from their own balcony. But they all couldn't produce evidence and didn't know how to clear themselves in today's trial. It is understood that among the 61 defendant households, only a hotel on the third floor of the building hired a lawyer to defend itself, and prepared sufficient evidence that the fork stick did not fall from their place.
