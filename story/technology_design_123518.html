Plot Introduction:

Since his mother's suicide, Guangzi (played by Eric Tsang) has been unhappy and hasn't grown taller. Guangzi blames his father (played by Aaron Kwok) and stepmother (played by Karen Mok) for everything and hates this family. One day, Guangzi meets a vagrant (played by Feng Xiaogang), who claims to have refined a potion that can cause rapid growth. By a twist of fate, the potion turns Guangzi into an adult overnight. After growing up, Guangzi (played by Andy Lau) is both shocked and delighted, and he decides to pursue his dream girl Miss Lee (played by Charlene Choi). However, Guangzi’s age increases dramatically every night... In the film, Andy Lau plays a child growing up in a troubled family. One day, while in the park, he encounters a vagrant and drinks the magical potion given by him, subsequently transforming into an 80-year-old man within a short period. Besides being portrayed by young actors during his childhood, Andy Lau himself must play from age 20 to 80, which he admits is a significant challenge for his acting skills. According to the investor, Emperor Motion Pictures, there are two key figures responsible for making Andy Lau instantly old. The first is Feng Xiaogang, who plays a mysterious vagrant whose magical potion turns Andy Lau into someone over 80 years old; the second is Mark, a top Hollywood special effects makeup artist hired at great expense, who previously worked on "Artificial Intelligence" and "Mission Impossible 2".

However, this process of aging is not as simple as it seems. First, one must remain motionless with the same expression for several hours, enduring multiple layers of silicone fake skin being glued onto their face. What's hardest to bear is applying and removing 90% alcohol on the face during the makeup and removal process. Moreover, for authenticity, the stylist prepared wigs and fake ears for Andy Lau when portraying someone over 60, and even modified his prominent nose by adding layers of fake skin. Additionally, to make the old-age makeup more realistic, the makeup artist prepared different sets of contact lenses for various ages, as the gleam in one's eyes changes with age. When asked about his experience with the old-age makeup in the movie, he joked: “I think I look most like myself at 20. At 20, I didn’t need any makeup, just my current face, no processing, real and cheap; starting at 40, some processing was needed, bleaching hair, growing beards; at 60, it cost the production company millions in makeup fees. Next time, let’s all call me to play 20 again, cheaper and handsomer.” Though he speaks lightly, he actually suffered greatly for the old-age makeup. The production company hired the top Hollywood special effects makeup artist Mark, who previously worked on "Artificial Intelligence", to design the look. First, they used frozen gel to mold Andy Lau's face, then wrapped multiple layers of plaster bandages, binding him tightly, and then poured the gel over his head while maintaining the same expression. He described the "burial alive" process as lasting less than an hour but feeling like an eternity.
