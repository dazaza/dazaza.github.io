Respiratory allergies are the most common diseases in children under 5 years old. Recurrent respiratory infections, especially those that respond poorly to antibiotic treatment, should raise concerns as to whether they are caused by allergies. Allergy is a disease caused by immune system dysfunction.

The main types of respiratory allergies include hay fever, allergic rhinitis, allergic cough, and allergic asthma. Substances from outside the body that cause allergies are called allergens. When allergens invade the body, the immune system releases a large amount of inflammatory chemicals, causing capillary dilation, increased vascular wall permeability, and resulting in allergic reactions and symptoms. Common food allergens mainly include eggs, milk, seafood, etc., while common inhalant allergens mainly include dust mites, cockroaches, pets, pollen, and fungi.

Allergic reactions are systemic immune abnormal responses; therefore, the clinical manifestations of allergies are complex and varied. Mild cases of respiratory allergies may present with repeated coughing, sneezing, runny nose, nasal congestion, similar to colds; severe cases may present with chest tightness, difficulty breathing, leading to asthma. Often, when children encounter cold air, cold wind, or after visiting parks or someone's home, they suddenly start coughing, wheezing, or feeling short of breath.

Treatment for respiratory allergies: For children with recurrent respiratory allergies who respond poorly to antibiotic treatment, parents need to be vigilant about the presence of any allergic factors after ruling out immunodeficiency diseases. It’s best to seek a clear diagnosis at a local hospital. If an allergy is confirmed, desensitization treatment should be carried out to avoid delaying the child's condition.
