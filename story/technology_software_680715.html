As part of a long line behind revered golf makers, Callaway Golf recently added a great magazine to its roster created by golf products. The Callaway Golf Journal first went into print in the pre-spring of 2004. This magazine was meant to complement Callaway’s line of golf merchandise, but also to provide avid and recreational golfers with tips and concepts on how to improve their game. The magazine quickly went on to become an award-winning trade and sports publication and has since gone on to become a top-quality golf magazine. The magazine is sent out to Callaway customers and distributed for free at local golf clubs, specialty stores, and online as a PDF. Over 800,000 copies are distributed each quarter, and this small trade magazine has developed a strong cult following due to its top tips and insider articles. By showcasing articles, unique content, and interviews from golf pros like Olin Browne, Morgan Pressel, Johnny Miller, and Phil Mickelson alongside industry experts and celebrity golfers like Dennis Quaid, Nike, and Jordan, this magazine offers a well-balanced approach for golf enthusiasts.

However, it's the tips that keep readers coming back for more. Using their in-house staff professionals and pro team members like Annika Sorenstam, the journal consistently provides useful tips and easy-to-follow how-to articles. For example, during its thirteenth issue (October 2007), Julieta Granada gave a complete how-to piece on the best way to play every short Par-3. 1. Right from the tee, Julieta tells you that the shorter distances found on some Par 3's make the entire group much harder.

2. Julieta says further, suggesting that Par-3 holes are frequently rife with hazards. Thus, it’s a hole like this where you need to genuinely plan a simple method right from the tee.

3. Due to the awkward and sometimes deceiving distances on Par-3 holes, golfers should sometimes find themselves off one green on a less-than-perfect tee and needing a superb chip shot. Julietta suggests maintaining a straight line between your leading arm and your club shaft when making the best chip shot while letting your hand lead the ball where you want it to go.

4. Julieta also stresses studying the break on a chip shot, trying to get exclusive understanding of where the ball’s going to hit and how the gravity of the break will affect where it goes.
