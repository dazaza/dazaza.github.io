Acceptance criteria for exterior wall paint

by fangfuv09 on 2010-04-19 11:12:07

1 Introduction: The construction of exterior wall coatings serves both decorative and protective functions for buildings. Compared to traditional exterior cladding materials such as exterior wall tiles and mosaics, these coatings have the advantages of energy saving, environmental friendliness, rich colors, and difficulty in alteration, thus gaining rapid development in our country over recent years. Many municipal governments have issued relevant documents to restrict or reduce the use of exterior wall tiles, making the promotion of building exterior coatings a positive development for the coating industry. In the past few years, imported latex paints not only impacted the domestic market but also stimulated consumption, promoting the improvement of product quality by domestic enterprises and driving the progress of our country's architectural coatings. With the rapid development of building exterior coatings, some high-quality products have entered the market, yet issues with exterior paint application quality have arisen. If these problems are not completely eliminated, they will significantly limit the development of exterior coatings. It is necessary for construction management departments and quality supervision departments to strengthen quality control. While production enterprises strive to improve product quality, construction companies should scientifically and reasonably organize construction, enhance quality management, and ensure the quality of painting.

Currently, there is no unified national standard specifically for the construction specifications and acceptance criteria of building exterior wall coatings. Only in the "Construction and Acceptance Standards for Building Decoration Engineering" industry standard (JGJ 73-91) are standards and requirements specified for exterior wall painting projects, which are not implemented in practical engineering. As the production and application technology of building coatings develop, parts of the standards have become outdated. Regions like Shanghai, Jiangsu, and Fujian have successively revised their local standards for exterior wall coating construction and acceptance procedures, but these are not strictly enforced in actual engineering management and project acceptance, making it difficult to ensure construction quality. In developed countries, building painting is treated as a specialized industry where contractors must possess construction qualifications, workers must hold certificates, and there are strict, practical management methods and acceptance standards for construction quality and safety. To ensure the long-term healthy development of the building exterior wall coating industry, strengthening scientific management of exterior wall coating construction has become an urgent necessity.

2 Requirements and Treatment for the Substrate of Building Exterior Wall Coatings

2.1 Adequate Curing Period

Old cement mortar concrete surfaces must go through a reasonable curing period to allow them to "swallow alkali" and fully dry before construction can begin. The moisture content should be greater than 10%, and the pH value should be less than 10. However, measuring the moisture content and pH of wall surfaces is not convenient. Practically speaking, under conditions of 25 degrees Celsius temperature and 65% relative humidity, cement mortar walls need to dry for more than 21 days. In the same season, based on experience, winter usually requires more than two weeks, while summer usually requires less than four weeks. Currently, some high-rise buildings often remove scaffolding after exterior plastering completion for maintenance for over two months before using baskets for exterior wall coating construction. This not only reduces the moisture content and pH value but also allows timely handling of any compression cracks or other defects on the wall surface, ensuring the quality of exterior wall coatings to a large extent. However, due to schedule reasons and scaffold issues, many multi-story buildings cannot guarantee sufficient curing periods, leaving potential hazards for exterior wall coatings.

2.2 Proper Substrate Treatment

The substrate surface should be dry, compact, and sturdy, without loose sand, cracks, or looseness. The exterior wall coating substrate is divided into general, intermediate, and high-grade plaster substrates or concrete substrates according to the requirements of the coating. Before construction, the plaster and concrete substrates must be inspected. Walls that have not been accepted or failed inspection cannot undergo coating construction. If there are loose sand, cracks, or looseness on the wall, they should be repaired by the wall repair unit. Loose layers, cracks, damaged edges, and corners should be removed and repaired until they pass inspection.

2.3 Clean Substrate Surface

The substrate surface must be clean, free from soil, frost, oil stains, and formwork agents. If these contaminants are found, they should be removed using a scraper, steel brush, sandpaper, detergent, etc., then washed with clean water and dried before coating. After wall treatment, construction should proceed as soon as possible to avoid re-contamination.

2.4 Division Strips on Exterior Walls

Exterior wall coatings directly depend on division line treatments. Division strips come in various forms; previously, wooden strips were often embedded in plastering mortar, then removed after painting to create light-colored paint joints. In recent years, plastic division strips have been widely used, offering diverse forms and good results. However, attention must be paid during painting to protect these strips from contamination. Whether wooden or plastic, the strips must be firm and resistant to deformation.

2.5 Treatment of Plaster Layers

Exterior wall coatings should not involve large-scale putty scraping. It is recommended to roughen the plaster layer, then apply primer and topcoat directly. If putty scraping is indeed necessary, polymer cement putty is suggested, avoiding regular low-strength interior wall putties, including 107 glue mixed with cement-based putties. The putty layer should not be too thick. The wall substrate should not be too rough, as excessive roughness affects decoration quality, but it also shouldn't be too smooth, as excessive smoothness reduces paint adhesion.

3 Construction Plan and Methods

3.1 Thick Layer Exterior Wall Coating Application

Includes synthetic resin emulsion interior wall coatings and solvent-based exterior wall coatings. Synthetic resin emulsion exterior wall coatings (latex paints) come in high, medium, and low-grade products suitable for different consumer levels. Solvent-based exterior wall coatings belong to high-end exterior wall coatings used for higher-demand coating projects.

◆ Thick-layer exterior wall coatings are especially suitable for roughened walls. If putty application is needed, polymer cement putty should be selected, and it should not be too thick.

◆ Generally, one coat of primer and two coats of topcoat are applied. The number of topcoat applications can be appropriately increased based on project quality requirements.

◆ Primer function: Seals the wall's alkalinity, improves the adhesion between the topcoat and the wall, prevents excessive absorption of the paint due to wall dryness, ensures paint quality consistency, and increases the strength of the substrate material. However, the function of the primer is limited. Even with the use of primer, the pH and moisture content of the substrate must meet requirements before construction can begin.

◆ Construction methods: Roller application, brush application, air spraying, and airless spraying can be used.

◆ Color selection: Fading of exterior wall coatings is a common issue. High-quality exterior wall coatings fade slowly but not permanently, so it is generally recommended to choose lighter or darker colors for exterior wall coatings. On one hand, this makes buildings appear lively and generous, and on the other hand, darker colors are usually made from organic pigments, which have better weather resistance than inorganic pigments, thus fading more slowly. Additionally, the same batch of coatings should be selected for the same building to ensure color consistency.

◆ Building coating construction should start from top to bottom, with each stroke bounded by horizontal lines, wall angles, or downspouts.

◆ Follow the product instructions strictly for construction intervals (re-coating time).

◆ Stir the paint thoroughly before use. If the viscosity is too high, adjust the application properties by adding a thinner (for latex paint, usually water) according to the instructions. Pay attention to the requirements, especially for latex paint, as excessive water can make it difficult for the dried coating to form a film and reduce its gloss, durability, and hiding power.

◆ Temperature and humidity influence: Paint instructions generally specify the highest temperature for coating construction. Among them, latex paint has the highest requirement for construction temperature. If the temperature is below a certain level during construction, it will lead to poor film formation. There is a misconception: some users believe that high-grade latex paint can be constructed at lower temperatures, but this is exactly the opposite. Because high-grade latex paint is usually made with high glass transition temperature emulsions, and the proportion of emulsions is relatively high, even if a large amount of film-forming agent is added, a higher film-forming temperature is still required, meaning the construction temperature.

◆ In northern China, humidity also frequently affects construction quality, especially for solvent-based exterior wall coatings. Construction is generally not recommended when humidity exceeds 85%. Under high-humidity conditions, the drying speed of the coating film is very slow, which not only affects the adhesion of the coating to the wall but also makes it easy for the wall to bloom. Therefore, painting construction should not be carried out under humid conditions, such as foggy weather, and after heavy rain, construction should only begin once the wall is dry.

3.2 Multi-layer Coating Construction

Multi-layer exterior wall coatings are also known as convex-concave pattern coatings or sculptured spray plastic coatings. Here is a concept that needs to change: in emphasizing multi-layer coating construction, the particles should be large, and the convex-concave feeling should be strong. However, due to current environmental pollution and other factors, now popular are large and uniform particles with slightly convex-concave feelings, also called orange peel-like exterior wall coatings.

◆ Multi-layer coatings do not require the top layer to be extremely flat. Choosing multi-layer coatings for rough plastered walls is more reasonable. For obvious defects such as damaged edges and corners, repairs with polymer cement putty should be made before proceeding with coating.

◆ A primer coat must be applied because the base coating not only seals the wall's alkalinity and adjusts the substrate's permeability but also enhances the adhesion of the subsequent layers. Skipping or omitting the primer application is unacceptable.

◆ Secondary layer construction: The secondary layer is also called the aggregate. If it belongs to a reaction-cured type, it should be used immediately after preparation and not stored for long periods. Mixing should be handled by a designated person to ensure accuracy. During construction, mechanical spraying is generally used, so test spraying should be conducted beforehand. Parameters such as paint viscosity, spray gun type, nozzle diameter, air pressure at the nozzle, distance and angle between the spray gun and the wall should be adjusted appropriately. Test samples should be sprayed first, and only after passing inspection should large-scale construction begin.

◆ At corners and dividing lines, shielding should be applied. The movement of the spray gun can be up-down or left-right, and uneven areas can be resprayed to ensure uniformity. After the main layer spraying is completed, according to requirements, rolling with a plastic roller or rubber roller dipped in solvents such as kerosene can be performed to press and roll, ensuring no obvious joint marks at each roll intersection.

◆ Orange peel-like wall coatings can also be constructed using methods similar to those of high-viscosity thixotropic latex paints, akin to decorative mortars. Selecting rollers of different patterns can create various designs. The techniques used during construction are crucial, such as the amount of material dipped each time and the force applied, all of which affect the design of the patterns. Therefore, construction personnel should repeatedly try rolling until they master the skills before starting small-scale construction.

◆ Topcoat: Generally, two coats of topcoat are applied on the surface of the main layer, either solvent-based exterior wall coatings or synthetic resin emulsion exterior wall coatings (latex paints). The application of the topcoat is similar to that of thick-layer interior wall coatings.
