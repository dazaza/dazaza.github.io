IFM Proximity Sensors

　　Inductive Sensors

　　Currently, inductive sensors are widely used in the industrial field. Compared to mechanical switches, inductive sensors have perfect prerequisites: contactless and wear-free operation with high switching frequency and switching accuracy. In addition, they are anti-vibration, dustproof, and moisture-proof. Inductive sensors can detect all metals without contact.

　　Magnetic Sensors

　　In control technology, magnetic sensors use a contactless and wear-free working method for position detection. They can be used in all fields where inductive sensor applications are limited. Since magnetic fields can penetrate all non-magnetized materials, magnetic sensors can identify objects such as non-ferrous materials, stainless steel, aluminum, plastic, or wooden walls.

　　Capacitive Sensors

　　Capacitive sensors can detect any object in a contactless manner. Compared to inductive sensors that only detect metal objects, capacitive sensors can also detect non-metallic materials. Typical application fields include the wood industry, paper industry, glass manufacturing, plastic manufacturing, food industry, and chemical industry.

　　Infrared/Red Light Sensors

　　In all areas where the precise position of an object needs to be identified safely and without contact, photoelectric sensors should be used. Regardless of the material of the detected object, this product can function normally. Compared to proximity sensors, photoelectric sensors have a larger detection range.

　　Through-beam Sensors

This system consists of two separate components: a transmitter and a receiver. The transmitter emits light in the infrared or red wavelength range. The receiver identifies the arriving beam and immediately converts the interruption caused by an object within the detection range into a switch signal.

　　Retro-reflective Sensors

The transmitter and receiver are located in one housing. A reflective mirror reflects the emitted beam back to the receiver. When an object appears in the beam path and interrupts it, a switch conversion occurs. Retro-reflective sensors without polarization filters work with infrared, while systems with polarization filters use visible red light.

　　Diffuse Reflective Sensors

Used for direct object detection. Like retro-reflective sensors, the transmitter and receiver of diffuse reflective sensors are in one housing. The transmitter emits light, which is reflected by the detected object and recognized by the receiver. This system does not estimate beam interruptions but estimates the light reflected back from the object. It's important that the switch function is adjusted according to the light-on/dark-on mode. The system emits pulsed light.

　　Fork/Corner Photoelectric Sensors

Similar to through-beam sensors composed of facing transmitters and receivers. Both components are fixed fork-shaped or corner-type components and do not need to be aligned face-to-face.

　　Fork and Corner Photoelectric Sensors

If an object enters the detection area, the beam will be interrupted, and a switch signal will be generated. Fork and corner photoelectric sensors use particularly fine beams suitable for detecting small objects.

　　Infrared Sensors

Detect thermal radiation in the &；amp；micro；m range and convert it into electronic switch signals. The sensor works as an infrared receiver. Thus, the object itself works as the sensor's transmitter. The spectrum of the emitted infrared rays directly depends on the temperature of the object. Compared to temperature sensors (such as PT100), the biggest advantage of this sensor is the lack of mechanical contact between the object and the sensor.

　　Sensors operating according to calorimetric and ultrasonic principles are especially suitable for monitoring gas and liquid media.

　　IFM offers various sensor models to meet different applications and requirements. All systems are carefully designed so that the signal can be directly transmitted to the control system for further processing.

　　Flow sensor devices provide an analog output signal and display flow changes on-site via an LED bar.

　　Airflow sensors are specifically designed to monitor airflow in air conditioning equipment and air used to cool switch cabinets. DC and AC specifications are suitable for other application areas.

　　Sensors are made from different materials, such as stainless steel, ceramic, and titanium with plug-in connectors or cable connections, specifically designed for connection to individual processing displays.

　　DC and AC specification control displays process the signals from flow sensors and emit two-position output signals for further processing. Features wire break protection and temperature monitoring.

IFM Temperature Sensors

　　Temperature Sensors and Temperature Sensor Devices

　　Temperature regulation and monitoring are primary tasks in automation technology and process technology. For example, in piping process equipment, the correct temperature plays a decisive role in the quality and economic efficiency of the process.
