The first urination in the morning can indicate the condition of liver function.

by lucky6105 on 2012-03-02 01:01:07

Key tip: Urine is the best indicator of whether one's lifestyle and diet are normal. Most people get up in the morning because they feel an urgent need to urinate. Of course, some people may urinate while still half asleep and then go back to bed for a nap, so they often don't pay attention to the color of their urine.

The condition of the liver can be observed from the first urination in the morning. In fact, the first urination of the day is very important and should be carefully observed. Check if the color is too dark, if there is blood in the urine, if it looks like cloudy rice water, or if there are many bubbles, each indicating different conditions. If there are a lot of bubbles and the urine looks like cloudy rice water, this suggests that there might be a problem with kidney function, showing that you ate too salty food or too much food with preservatives yesterday.

If the urine is red, first consider if you have a cold or fever, as catching a cold or having a fever can affect the blood vessels in the lower abdomen, causing congestion. Some blood vessels are more fragile, leading to microvascular rupture and thus blood in the urine. Secondly, consider the possibility of having stones, because when sharp stones move, they can break microvessels, making the urine appear red. In severe cases, the entire toilet bowl may turn red.

Staying up late, smoking heavily, also easily leads to blood in the urine, because smoking causes microvessels to contract, affecting oxygen supply. So the face turns bluish when smoking, and red when drinking alcohol. When both smoking and drinking happen at the same time, the face remains unchanged.

Urine that looks like coffee definitely has something to do with the liver and gallbladder. Therefore, I advise everyone to carefully observe their urine and stool every morning, and then adjust their living and eating habits accordingly, which can help prepare for potential issues and prevent them before they occur.

Early examination: Learn to diagnose or speculate if you have liver disease

The liver is prone to diseases such as type A or B hepatitis, toxic hepatitis, liver cancer, or cirrhosis. Among these, the most serious is liver cancer. Once afflicted with liver cancer, the survival rate is extremely low. The condition progresses quickly, and death occurs rapidly. The frightening aspect of liver disease is that patients usually don't have particularly noticeable symptoms.

Patients may experience bloating, chest tightness, reduced appetite, common colds, fever, nausea, etc., but ordinary people often don't take these symptoms seriously and buy medicine from the pharmacy themselves, leading to worsening conditions and even death.

Experts explain that general fatigue, bloating, abdominal pain, jaundice, and loss of appetite do not necessarily indicate poor liver function. But even being able to eat, drink, run, jump, or being an athletic person does not guarantee good health. Since most liver diseases have no symptoms, this is why doctors call liver disease the "biggest hidden killer," so it must be taken seriously.
