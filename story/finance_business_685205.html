Recently, the Leizhou Court rejected the lawsuit of Tang Rqing and her two younger brothers. At the same time, the court approved the plaintiff's exemption of 2300 yuan in case acceptance fees.

In their civil complaint, the orphan Tang Rqing and her two younger brothers claimed that from 2003 to 2009, Tang Se, under the guise of raising them, illegally occupied various government relief funds, other properties, and exploited labor compensation totaling 82,892 yuan. After deducting the 5,280 yuan paid by Tang Se for food expenses, he should return the illegally occupied orphan subsidy, low-income allowance, five-guarantee funds, and other subsidies and properties totaling 77,612 yuan. Tang Rqing once said that she started working in Tang Se's orchard during holidays when she was 11 years old, but Tang Se never gave her a penny.

Facing the charges, Tang Se claimed that he managed a total of 52,532 yuan of various subsidies, donations, and moving congratulatory money for Tang Rqing and her two younger brothers. After offsetting the construction and food expenses, he currently still owes 9,468 yuan to the three siblings. The investigation by the Leizhou Discipline Inspection Commission also recognized this figure.

According to reports from the Southern Rural Newspaper (reporter Xian Wei Feng), the orphans from Chenjiqiao Village in Baitown Town, Leizhou City, Guangdong Province - Tang Rqing and her two younger brothers - pushed the village committee director who raised them for 10 years, Tang Se, onto the defendant's seat in court. On October 20th last year, after the Southern Rural Newspaper reported on the front page about this incident, it drew widespread attention from society. Recently, the Leizhou People's Court made a first-instance judgment, ruling that Tang Rqing and her two younger brothers lost the case.
