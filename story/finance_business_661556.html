Wallpaper vs Paint: A Detailed Comparison of Decoration Materials (1)

Currently, in China, indoor wall decoration still primarily relies on paint, but the impact of wallpaper is growing, especially in high-end home decoration, hotels, and shopping malls where its influence on interior wall coatings is becoming more evident. Between wallpaper and interior wall coatings, which is stronger and which is weaker?

Step 1: Price

Price is an intuitive data point that is very easy to compare. According to industry insiders, the price of a mid-range roll of wallpaper is approximately 400 yuan per roll. If calculated based on each roll covering 5.2 square meters of wall, while also considering a waste rate of 3% to 10% (larger patterns or those requiring alignment have higher waste rates), the cost of mid-range wallpaper is about 79.4 to 84.6 yuan per square meter. Currently, at the mid-range level of painting, the cost is between 40 to 60 yuan per square meter (including labor costs and auxiliary materials). Generally speaking, at the same level of decoration, wallpaper is clearly more expensive than paint. Of course, today's wallpaper comes in a full range of high, medium, and low-grade varieties, with frequent discount promotions offering many cost-effective products.

Step 2: Environmental Friendliness

It is well known that excessive emissions of pollutants during renovation can cause various diseases. Therefore, environmental friendliness has become an important factor for renovators when selecting decorative materials. To ensure renovation safety and maintain market position, all wall decoration product companies prioritize "environmental friendliness". So, between wallpaper and paint, which one has a greater advantage in terms of environmental friendliness? Overall, both high-quality wallpapers and paints from reputable brands are very environmentally friendly. In high-end wallpapers, whether they are made from paper, PVC, fabric, flocking, or natural materials, they all possess strong environmental performance. Reputable paint brands (especially latex paints using water as a diluent) are mostly safe materials, odorless, non-toxic, and pollution-free during use. However, low-end wallpapers and paints often come with safety hazards. For example, low-end PVC wallpapers contain large amounts of formaldehyde, and due to poor breathability, formaldehyde is not easily released, and wall moisture does not evaporate easily. Low-end paints, aside from possibly having excessive formaldehyde levels, may also contain organic solvents such as dichloromethane, chloroform, benzene, toluene, and heavy metals like lead, cadmium, chromium, and mercury, which can cause chronic long-term pollution in living spaces, endangering residents' health. It is worth noting that within the same brand, there are different quality tiers, and their environmental performance naturally varies.

Step 3: Effectiveness

Consumers generally believe that wallpaper offers better visual effects than paint. Although many branded paints now have increasingly powerful functions and richer colors, compared to wallpaper, they still seem monotonous and less impactful. As a general decoration material, even with the help of color-mixing machines and professional designers, paint lacks the vibrancy and expressiveness of wallpaper. Compared to paint, wallpaper has texture, rich colors, diverse patterns, a three-dimensional feel, and styles ranging from luxurious to elegant, classical to modern, providing the most direct visual effect and better reflecting the owner's taste and emotional elements. However, while wallpaper enhances the home's style, extensive use of a single type of wallpaper may not yield good results and could be inconsistent with the overall room and furniture style. Therefore, wallpaper selection should start with the overall design, determining your preferred style first, then choosing based on the wallpaper's color, pattern, and characteristics, tailored to individual preferences and room types, ensuring coordination with furniture styles. Industry experts suggest that when choosing wallpaper, you should test your favorite options by holding them two meters away under indirect light. When applying wallpaper, select different patterns and colors based on the room's function.

Step 4: Construction

Comparing the ease of construction, paint is relatively easier. However, if considering the construction period, paint takes longer. According to industry insiders, wallpaper installation requires higher technical expertise; otherwise, issues like peeling can affect its lifespan. For instance, poor quality adhesive can cause curling and peeling, shortening the wallpaper's life. Additionally, insufficient technical skills of workers can affect the aesthetic appeal of the wallpaper, such as excessive adhesive spilling over and not being promptly cleaned with a sponge dipped in water, leading to yellowing at the seams after several days. Moreover, inadequate waterproofing of walls can cause wallpaper to turn yellow due to prolonged dampness. In contrast, paint application is much simpler. Ensuring smooth plastering, clean base layers, and avoiding overly thick paint prevents problems like bubbling, wrinkling, and watermarks. In terms of construction period, wallpaper is generally more convenient and faster. With wallpaper, only a layer of clear varnish or primer needs to be applied on a smoothed wall before installation. A professional team can complete the wallpapering of a 100-square-meter house in a day with three people. With paint, walls need multiple layers, spaced a day apart, taking about a week to finish, with an additional requirement of at least 10 days of ventilation before moving in.

Step 5: Lifespan

Generally, pure wallpaper lasts about 8 to 10 years, PVC wallpaper about 5 to 8 years, and metal wallpaper about 3 to 4 years. The most commonly used interior wall coating, latex paint, if well maintained, can last around 10 years. Of course, these estimates assume normal conditions. If the base or environment is too harsh, both wallpaper and latex paint will have reduced lifespans. Wallpaper may bubble, peel, or curl, while latex paint may crack or flake. Under the same conditions, the lifespan of different grades and materials of wallpaper and paint also vary, with better quality lasting longer and poorer quality lasting shorter. Moreover, the lifespan of walls depends not only on the material but also significantly on the skill level of the workers. Both wallpaper and paint require meticulous craftsmanship.

Conclusion:

Wallpaper and paint each have their strengths, with no absolute winner. Industry experts state that traditional decoration concepts still favor paint, while trend-conscious young people prefer wallpaper. Whether to choose wallpaper or paint for wall decoration depends on individual needs. In fact, wallpaper and paint are not mutually exclusive but can complement each other. During renovation, you can selectively use wallpaper on top of a single-colored paint base for matching and supplementation. This avoids the monotony of using only paint and reduces the high cost of using only wallpaper, enhancing theme clarity.

For example, use simple abstract wallpapers for the TV background to emphasize ambiance; choose straightforward patterns for the sofa background to extend the living space visually; apply yellow wallpaper behind the dining table to stimulate appetite; and use cartoon wallpapers in children's rooms to enhance fun.
