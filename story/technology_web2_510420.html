Seven Olympic Weapons of China's Event Clothing Brands_6365.www.zp-nmg.com

by atonyfsu69 on 2011-10-18 10:32:15

With less than 400 days remaining until the 2008 Beijing Olympics, it is clear that the Olympic Games in modern society are no longer just a sports competition between countries, but also a brand and market competition. From Adidas' partnership with the Olympics for 80 years, to Samsung's rapid rise through the Seoul Olympics, we see how the Olympics have perfectly integrated with modern commerce. The two arenas of competition are equally exciting and full of suspense.

For the 2008 Beijing Olympics, as the first batch of 48 sponsoring enterprises (including sponsors, suppliers, exclusive suppliers, international Olympic partners, international sponsors, and international suppliers) made their brand appearances, the face of licensed products, including clothing, badges, toys, stationery, luggage, crafts, silk products, ceramics, gold and silver products, jewelry, among ten major categories and over 4,000 varieties, gradually became clearer. Many strong brands obtained entry tickets to this grand Olympic commercial event by using intellectual property barriers to drive smaller brands out of the field. Most Chinese sports apparel brands lack the strength, yet they face this perfect opportunity for their own brand positioning, and being the host country of the Olympics, can only sit in the audience?

Our answer, of course, is no. In the face of intellectual property barriers, in this market monopolized by brand oligarchs, many sponsors have already begun their Olympic strategies. For example, Adidas has established an "Asian Design Center" for the Beijing Olympics, developing sports apparel souvenirs with dual logos of both the Beijing Olympics and Adidas, which will undoubtedly leverage the Olympics to significantly promote its brand share in China. So, how can our domestic sports apparel brands make a difference?

We believe that for Chinese sports apparel brands to ride the Olympic winds into the sea, they must achieve the following:

1. Re-examine market opportunities. The Olympics can be said to be the best commercial platform in modern society because it integrates the broadest relationship marketing, the most comprehensive media promotion, and the deepest public relations marketing. On average, more than one billion eyes focus on the Olympics every day during the 17-day event. No other event in modern society can compare. Do not assume that sponsors' brands monopolize all the attention; any brand can create a blue ocean in the Olympic economy through creative marketing, deep marketing, potential marketing, and invisible marketing. If the chosen method is correct and innovative, the ripple effect can amplify brand awareness several times, becoming a dark horse during the Olympics.

2. Recognize your company’s conditions. We do not agree that all companies should blindly engage in Olympic projects. After all, Olympic marketing is high-output, high-return, and high-risk. During the Olympics, the noise level in communication is very high, and market costs can easily reach hundreds of millions. Chinese sports apparel brands must recognize their brand growth stage and whether it is necessary to join the competition. If joining, it must be strategically important to analyze investment returns and use necessary market monitoring tools for continuous evaluation.

3. Identify the brand growth model. Is it an online strategy (above the line) aimed at establishing an international brand strategy, choosing broader media promotion and more public welfare-oriented Olympic event marketing? Or is it an offline strategy (below the line) focused on achieving product promotion through the Olympics, timing promotional efforts effectively? Or is it finding a good balance between focusing on one and balancing the other?

4. Find the right entry point. We say that modern Olympics is no longer just a sporting event. It softens human rational values, reflects modern society's aesthetic views, national competitiveness, citizens' national sentiments, individual psychological dependencies on sports, and entertainment, fashion, experience, and emotional transfer. Any of these elements can be a potential entry point. Chinese sports apparel brands must align with their brand strategies to find the right entry point to stand out in this Olympic competition.

As for how Chinese sports apparel brands should participate in the Olympics, we believe there are seven strategic weapons available:

Weapon One: Sponsor participating teams. Nike did not secure the sponsorship qualification for the Olympics, but we know that in the track and field team led by Liu Xiang, the basketball team anchored by Yao Ming, the tennis team led by Zheng Jie and Yan Zi, and the weightlifting team supported by Zhang Guozheng... It is said that 21 Chinese national teams are sponsored by Nike's competition uniforms. This method of potential marketing reaches a situation where it rivals Adidas. This method is the most common and effective during the Olympics.

Weapon Two: Package potential star players. During the Olympics, big-name stars are often courted by various brands. Gaining endorsement opportunities is difficult and costly. The last Athens Olympics saw the Chinese delegation win 32 gold medals, and this time, as the host country, the Chinese delegation is expected to surpass this number, meaning there are still many potential Olympic champions who could be discovered and packaged before the Olympics, bringing unexpected results.

Weapon Three: Seize media high ground. The leading brand in Chinese sports apparel, Li Ning Company, also missed out on becoming an Olympic sponsor, but Li Ning took a step ahead by signing with CCTV, securing the sponsorship of all host and on-camera journalists' uniforms from January 1, 2007, to December 31, 2008. During the Olympics, China's major strong media outlets will certainly have considerable coverage, making it timely to seize this opportunity.
