It is important to conduct a patent legal status search when introducing technology.

by keyizhang1 on 2012-03-06 15:00:07

**Patent (www.k8008.com[http://www.k8008.com]) Legal Status Search**

The patent legal status search refers to the inquiry into the current state of a patent or patent application. Its purpose is to determine whether the patent application has been granted, whether the authorized patent is still valid, whether the patent owner has changed, and other information related to the patent's legal status. For key target patents, the main goal of conducting a legal status search is to confirm whether the patent is currently valid and whether there have been any changes in the patent ownership. Especially in technology引进s, many foreign companies offer bundled patent licenses, but a significant number of these patents may have already expired or become invalid. Additionally, differences in the remaining protection period can also affect the value of the patent. Therefore, it is very necessary to conduct a legal status search on key patents before introducing technology or seeking licensing. So what specific information does a patent legal status search provide?

1. **Patent Authorization**

On the date of the search or prior to it, if the searched patent has been granted and remains valid until the next fee payment deadline after the search date, this legal status is referred to as "patent authorization."

2. **Publication of Patent Application**

China implements an early publication and deferred examination system for invention patents. After passing the preliminary examination, the invention text is published, followed by a substantive review within three years. If the legal status shows "authorization," it means the patent has obtained patent rights in the applied region. After filing an invention patent application, 18 months after passing the preliminary examination, the National Intellectual Property Administration will publish the patent and comprehensively review its novelty, inventiveness, and utility. If no objections are raised within three years, the patent will be granted.

3. **Expiration of Patent Validity Period**

On the date of the search or prior to it, if the searched patent has been granted but its validity period has exceeded the term specified by the patent law (including any extended term), this legal status is referred to as "expiration of patent validity period." Once the patent validity period expires, the patent is no longer protected by patent law.

4. **Patent Application Not Yet Authorized**

On the date of the search or prior to it, if the searched patent application has not yet been published or has been published but not yet granted, this legal status is referred to as "patent application not yet authorized."

5. **Withdrawal of Patent Application**

On the date of the search or prior to it, if the searched patent application has been voluntarily withdrawn by the applicant or deemed withdrawn by the patent authority, this legal status is referred to as "withdrawal of patent application." A patent deemed withdrawn does not enjoy patent rights and lacks exclusivity.

6. **Rejection of Patent Application**

On the date of the search or prior to it, if the searched patent application has been rejected by the patent authority, this legal status is referred to as "rejection of patent application."

7. **Termination of Patent Rights**

On the date of the search or prior to it, if the searched patent has been granted but becomes prematurely invalid due to non-payment of patent fees before the expiration of its validity period, this legal status is referred to as "termination of patent rights."

8. **Invalidation of Patent Rights**

On the date of the search or prior to it, if the searched patent has been granted but subsequently declared invalid due to valid reasons for invalidation by the patent authority, this legal status is referred to as "invalidation of patent rights." An invalidated patent is considered invalid from the beginning.

9. **Transfer of Patent Rights**

On the date of the search or prior to it, if the searched patent or patent application undergoes a change in ownership or applicant, this legal status is referred to as "transfer of patent rights."

10. **Abandonment of Patent Rights**

After the Patent Office issues a notice granting the patent, if the applicant fails to complete registration procedures within the specified time frame, it is considered as abandoning the right to obtain the patent.
