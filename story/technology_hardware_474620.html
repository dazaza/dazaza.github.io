Man detained for 10 days for spreading H1N1 flu rumors online

by zzf000zxyn7 on 2011-08-11 16:04:53

According to the news (intern reporter, Jianjin Huang), recently a man from Chang Le posted false information about an H1N1 flu outbreak online and was administratively detained for 10 days.

Recently, Li Mou, a 28-year-old man from Yutian, Chang Le, posted on a forum claiming: "Yesterday at Chiyu Middle School and Tangyu Primary School in Shouzhan Town of Chang Le City, over 30 cases of H1N1 influenza were discovered, causing students from both schools to be suspended." After receiving the report, the Chang Le police highly valued the situation. Following extensive investigations, they found that this information was false. They subsequently arrested the suspect, Li Mou, and sentenced him to 10 days of detention.

Zhang Kexiang, legal advisor to the Chang Le Municipal Government, told reporters that deliberately spreading flu rumors could result in detention or fines. According to China's Public Security Administration Punishment Law: Spreading rumors, falsely reporting emergencies, outbreaks, or police situations, or using other methods to intentionally disrupt public order will result in detention for five to ten days, possibly accompanied by a fine of up to 500 yuan; if the circumstances are relatively minor, the penalty will be detention for less than five days or a fine of up to 500 yuan. Additionally, Article Ten of the "Interpretation of Several Issues Concerning the Specific Application of Laws in Handling Criminal Cases Hindering the Prevention and Control of Sudden Infectious Disease Outbreaks and Other Disasters" by the Supreme People's Court and the Supreme People's Procuratorate stipulates that fabricating terrifying information related to sudden infectious disease outbreaks and other disasters, or knowingly disseminating such fabricated terrifying information, thereby seriously disrupting social order, will be punished under Article 290-1 of the Criminal Law for the crime of fabricating and deliberately spreading false terrifying information.

Zhang Kexiang reminded citizens that upon encountering such false information, one should not easily believe it but instead promptly report it to relevant departments such as the police, education authorities, or village (residents') committees; secondly, one should not continue spreading or disseminating false information.
