Who Stole My Car Door Open: Unveiling the Grey Chain of Automotive Decoders

by anonymous on 2012-02-29 20:18:28

On the noon of March 6, in a shopping mall parking lot in Daliang, Shunde, within one hour, nine cars were consecutively "plundered," with over ten thousand yuan worth of items stolen from five of them. Strangely, there were no visible signs of forced entry or damage on the car doors and trunks. Additionally, the remote controllers that the nine car owners carried did not issue any alarms. This left the victims thoroughly baffled.

Why did the car doors open without the owners' knowledge? Why did the remote locking fail? Why didn't the electronic anti-theft equipment work? What allowed the thieves to succeed repeatedly within an hour?

In fact, car thefts inside vehicles are not limited to shopping mall parking lots; cars parked in parks and hotels have also frequently fallen victim. So, how exactly do these thefts happen inside cars? And how can car owners protect themselves against this seemingly mysterious method of theft? Yesterday, a knowledgeable individual faced our reporter and revealed the secrets behind these "effortless" door-opening methods.

[Case] Pocket-contained jammer: Nine cars plundered in one hour

Thieves used an electronic jammer to interfere with the signal, making it so even if the owner used the remote control to lock the car and heard the locking sound, in reality, the car was not locked.

"I wish I could sleep in my car!" said a Honda owner from the Dong district of Daliang to our reporter, "Parking by the roadside feels like leaving things on the side of the road, with no sense of security at all!"

Even though they had pressed the remote lock, why did the car doors still open? Yesterday, our reporter interviewed the police handling the case. The officer explained that the thieves used an electronic jammer to interfere with the signals sent by the remote controller. Even if the car owner used the remote to lock the car and heard the locking sound, in reality, the car was not locked. In the case solved by the Shunde police, the "electronic jammer" was used to intercept the signals from the car owners' remotes. Four men successively intercepted the remote signals from nine cars, completing the process of opening the car doors, disabling the anti-theft system, and stealing within just one hour.

The incident occurred on the noon of March 6 in a parking lot of a mall in Daliang. Four men drove a black BYD vehicle with a Guangzhou license plate pretending to park. After getting out of their car, they immediately followed any car owner who was parking nearby. When a car owner left after parking, one member would use the "jammer" hidden in his pocket to intercept the signal from the car owner's remote, preventing the car doors from locking. Another member would follow the car owner, while two others would approach the target vehicle. One of them pretended to be "making" a phone call near the car as a lookout, while the other entered the car to steal, quickly closing the car door and leaving after succeeding.

Officer Zhou Hong from the Daliang Criminal Investigation Team, who handled this case, said that the suspects usually interfered with the signal when they were about 3-4 meters away from the car owner, "This way, the signal will be very strong."

According to the police, there are many different types of "electronic jammers" available on the market. The function of the jammer is to interfere with the car owner's remote signal by frequency interference, causing the car owner to mistakenly believe that the car has been locked, but in reality, it hasn't, effectively shielding the remote signal.

In addition to using jammers to intercept signals, the Shunde police revealed another tool called a decoder: "After intercepting the signal, it automatically opens the car door by emitting a signal, equivalent to a car key. As long as the car owner uses the electronic lock to open or close the door within its effective range, the decoder can automatically 'remember' the frequency of the electronic lock. After the car owner leaves, the car thief only needs to press the transmission button once to open the car door."

[Revelation] Car decoders and jammers sold openly online at marked prices

An announcement on a network store read: "Car decoder, car jammer, lock-picking tool, open the door to your wealth."

The police believe that thieves generally don't target high-end cars because they have better anti-theft systems, but insiders revealed that if both a jammer and a decoder are used, high-end cars can also be stolen.

What exactly are jammers and decoders? How do they break the car's anti-theft system? Where do these devices come from? According to the police, the electronic jammers used for crimes are purchased online, with prices ranging from several thousand to tens of thousands of yuan.

Yesterday, through an online search engine, we queried electronic jammers and decoders, finding millions of web pages, including tens of thousands of websites selling "car decoders." Clicking into the web pages, there were hundreds of types of related equipment such as "car decoders," "jammers," "lock-picking tools," "central control decoders," with pictures showing the goods and marked prices below. Some websites even included explanatory introductions.

On the webpage of "Guangzhou Jia'an Electronics," we saw the network store's announcement: "Car decoder, car jammer, lock-picking tool, open the door to your wealth." The webpage also provided a 24-hour purchase hotline, customer service QQ number, and email address, with the "product classification" section categorizing cars. Besides the aforementioned two tools, the website also sold "lock-picking guns," "quantum locks," and other quick car-opening tools.

The sales price displayed on the webpage ranged between 3680 yuan and 4800 yuan, with many websites pricing decoders according to the type of car. A cursory browse revealed that professional decoders for Honda cars were priced around 3800 yuan, Toyota car decoders around 1100 yuan, and small car-opening tools for Honda Accords around 120 yuan.

The highest-priced decoder on some websites targeted Mercedes-Benz brands. On a website named "Hot Wave," the decoder labeled "remote key automatic pairing for Mercedes-Benz use" was priced at 9020 yuan. The lowest-priced one was a quick car-opening tool for Mazda, priced at 80 yuan.

Even if the car is already locked, can the decoder still open it?

"The jammer can only interfere with the signal; if the car door is locked, it cannot be opened. However, the decoder can automatically remember the frequency of the electronic lock, allowing it to open even after the car is locked."

We found online that these decoders are no larger than an ordinary mobile phone at most, and as small as a motorcycle key at minimum. The smallest decoder can easily be operated in the pocket without being noticed.

[Prevention] Carefully confirm whether the car is locked; do not leave valuable items in the car.

The car theft incidents have caused concern among many citizens. According to the investigating police officers, the car theft gang on March 6 mainly targeted mid-to-low-end sedans priced under 300,000 yuan. Whether the vehicle was domestically produced or imported, both types were involved. Previously, such cases often happened when car owners went to supermarkets or malls. "Many car owners did not realize they had been stolen until they returned home and prepared to retrieve their belongings, only to find that the items were missing."

The police reminded car owners to pay attention to safety precautions and offered theft prevention advice, suggesting that car owners should confirm whether the car doors are truly locked when using the remote lock and avoid placing valuable items in the car.

On the local forum in Shunde, a netizen offered advice: "For safety reasons, it's best to park your vehicle in a place with dedicated supervision, install more anti-theft switches connected to the alarm system, accelerator, and power lines in the car. This way, even if the car's password is cracked, the thief won't be able to steal the car, minimizing the loss."
