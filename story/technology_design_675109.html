My 21st Birthday

Today is my 21st birthday! If we assume an average lifespan of 80 years, then in the blink of an eye, I have already lived through a quarter of my life. Standing at the start of the second quarter, I look around at the dusty construction sites and can't help but smile, as if I've truly grown up. Thinking back several years ago when I was still a child, I began to yearn for my future, never imagining that I would end up so destitute.

On my 21st birthday, I stand in the place where the mountain leaves accumulate the most, laughing happily yet feeling such loneliness. Turning around, I look at Yao and tell her how much I want to go home; she says the same, and after a moment of silence, when the noon sun becomes warmer, Yao looks at me and wishes me a happy birthday! And I, smile brilliantly. Yes, today is my 21st birthday.

It's already the third day since I came to this ghostly place. The autumn wind keeps blowing towards one direction. It's bearable during the day, but at night, the cold lingers in the room, making it hard to sleep. In these mountains without any heating measures, a simple stove is lit in the crude flat house, reminiscent of when I was very young. As night falls, just returned from the construction site, my colleagues who are stuck here with me prepare a birthday celebration for me.

The pork bought thirty miles away during the day is slowly stewing in the pot, and the dishes borrowed from the rudimentary canteen are sizzling under the skilled hands of Uncle Enu. After cleaning the dirt off my hands, I join others in wrapping dumplings with the few pounds of white flour we have. The northern night sky is deep and daunting. When a table full of rich dinner is set, when I blow out the candles on the cream cake, and when I receive small gifts and blessings from my colleagues, I cry. The warmth revealed in this desolate mountain moves me.

I raise my cup, drink it all down with tears, it's spicy and bitter. On my 21st birthday, I don't laugh.

After the warmth fades, it's already late at night. I put down my cup because there's fill-in work at pit number six, and I have to go to the construction site. Wrapping myself tightly in a coat, I walk out.

That night, on my 21st birthday, I didn't rest. At midnight, I was still sitting on a rock chatting endlessly with a Liu-surnamed technician or standing by the excavator calling for more cement or holding a flashlight checking the depth of the soil pile... When I return, it's already past two o'clock. Feeling extremely tired, I lie down in the icy bed, hugging a hot water bag, but toss and turn unable to sleep.

Closing my eyes, another world opens up. There's endless confusion, hesitating at a small crossroads. When I finally choose a path, countless hardships await me. But in my youth, even after being battered and bruised, I still try to move forward, looking quite foolish!

I remember before, at home, often arguing with my mother over trivial matters, thinking I was so wronged, eager to leave home, arrogantly declaring that I had grown up. But once outside, I realize that home is the warmest place. Without the care of home, it's like a bird with broken wings, so pitiful.

Friends, classmates, those who cared about me and paid attention to me, have all disappeared, living, studying, working, each striving for their own dreams. The numbers in my phone book that are now disconnected, do I still remember they were once frequently contacted? In today's society, parting may mean forever goodbye. Even if we meet again someday, everything has changed, and when we pass each other, we feel strangely unfamiliar.

Now, love follows you, reaching out its hands in your nightly solitude. Once manipulated and tortured by love until bloodied and scarred, because we were too young, we recklessly gave our all. Even while hurting each other and bleeding, we pretend to comfort each other. In this game of chasing and evading, we both play tragic roles. Later, as we grow older and mature, looking back, everything seems scattered.

My 21st birthday has passed, I get up from bed, pick up a pen, and under dim light, write "Long live youth" again.

Putting down the pen, I always feel overly melancholy. Watching the footsteps of youth deepen in the flow of time, there have been laughter, tears, abandonment, and persistence. Actually, the result no longer matters. Years later, looking back, it's like tea, bitter yet sweet.

Looking out the window, the first snowfall of winter has begun, blending beautifully with the star-filled sky!
