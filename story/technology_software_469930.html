2. Deer Placenta Ointment

As soon as we walked out of Tonghua train station, we saw signs at the entrance of the underground mall that read "Ginseng, Deer Products." This turned out to be a market mainly dealing in Changbai Mountain specialties. According to the vendors here, there are two or three markets like this in Tonghua City, and this one is relatively small. Here, we saw that almost every counter sold deer placenta ointment, most of which were simply packaged, and some had no packaging at all. In the medicinal building and specialty counters of the Tonghua market, the situation we observed was basically the same. The vendors told us that deer placenta ointment is a special drug for various gynecological diseases, and its special efficacy largely comes from the medicinal value of the deer placenta as a special ingredient.

When the boss saw we were strangers, he first introduced the high-quality deer placenta ointment. After some conversation, he took out a few blocks of low-priced deer placenta ointment and started recommending them to us.

However, when we asked why there was such a big price difference between the two types of deer placenta ointment, the boss did not answer our question. Under our repeated requests, he began to explain the production process of these cheap, inferior deer placenta ointments. He told us that these ointments had tampered with their ingredients.

Lamb placenta powder was placed in a dirty bag where its color was unclear, soaked in a bucket of water. (Scene: adding water), the fishy smell of lamb placenta powder attracted several small flying insects that drowned in the water. Workers poured the water used to soak the lamb placenta powder into this container and stirred it forcefully. The boss told us that after some time of boiling, the deer placenta ointment made from lamb placenta would be produced. These so-called "deer placenta ointments," once packaged, could then be put on the market.

4. Green Dezhou Roast Chicken

Dezhou roast chicken is renowned worldwide for its tender meat and rich aroma. Its delicious taste and fame have been passed down for over three hundred years. Dezhou roast chicken is a Chinese time-honored brand and one of China's four traditional famous chickens. Last May, our program reported on individual factories in Dezhou producing substandard roast chickens illegally. Following this, the local government conducted a comprehensive rectification campaign on all roast chicken production enterprises in the city and implemented strict policies for production, processing, and sales. More than a year has passed since then. How is the quality of Dezhou roast chicken now? Recently, our reporter conducted undercover investigations.

Roast chicken is a city card for Dezhou, Shandong Province. Walking through the streets and alleys of this city, you can see numerous advertising signs and shops selling roast chicken everywhere. Recently, while interviewing in Dezhou, reporters found that some roast chicken shops prominently displayed "green food" signs.

Reporter: Is this roast chicken green food?

Salesperson: Yes.

Reporter: What does green food mean?

Salesperson: Our company has a dedicated breeding base. The chickens eat pesticide-free, pollution-free feed, and the production workshop processes according to strict green food standards.

Reporter: Is this the broth for cooking roast chicken?

Technician: This is a 300-year-old soup.

Reporter: 300 years?

Technician: Yes, 300 years. The flavor of our roast chicken is achieved by cooking in this old soup.

Host: This small notch is a detail and can also serve as a method of identification. Among the four traditional famous chickens in China are Shandong Dezhou Roast Chicken, Henan Dao Kou Roast Chicken, Anhui Fu Li Ji Roast Chicken, and Liaoning Jin Zhou Gou Bang Zi Smoked Chicken. Are these four famous chickens currently facing some common dilemmas and issues?

Expert: They face the same issue. Some legitimate companies, after decades of development, continuously arm themselves with new technologies and modern techniques, establish strict standards, strengthen management, introduce modern processing technology, and produce high-quality products. However, some small workshops and illegal merchants use dead chickens as raw materials, process any type of chicken without careful control of safety, quality, and nutrition. Thus, their production costs are relatively lower, and they pass off inferior products as genuine ones, impacting the market. This affects the development of legitimate companies in the market.

Secondly, consumers may not know these are counterfeit products. After eating them, they find the taste poor and the quality bad, eventually losing confidence in the product. Therefore, how to inherit our excellent traditions and historical brands, products with hundreds of years of excellent history and renowned domestically and internationally, is indeed a major challenge we currently face.

5. Instant Noodles

Whether instant noodles are safe, hygienic, and nutritious directly relates to the interests of numerous consumers. According to national grain and oil quality inspection center checks, the passing rate of instant noodles in 2003 was 70%, but if preservatives were not tested, the passing rate would rise sharply to 99%. Preservatives have become the biggest factor affecting the quality of instant noodles. For this reason, reporters recently visited large instant noodle factories like Kang Shi Fu and Hua Long for investigation.

Instant noodles consist of noodle cakes and seasoning packets. To produce good instant noodles, one needs good flour first. If the flour contains excessive bleaching agents, it may decompose to produce preservatives like benzoic acid, significantly affecting the quality of instant noodles. In the noodle-making workshop of Wuhan Kang Shi Fu Instant Noodle Factory, reporters saw more than ten mixing machines working loudly, with workers occasionally scooping some powdered substances from a box in the corner of the room and pouring them into the mixers.

Frying serves two purposes: to enhance the texture of the noodles and extend their shelf life. Some irregular enterprises often use inferior oils combined with artificial preservatives instead of premium palm oil, seriously affecting the quality of the noodle cakes. In the frying workshop of the instant noodles, reporters saw a worker adding a liquid to the oil tank.

Host: I'd like to learn first whether preservatives can be added to the noodle cakes and seasoning packets of instant noodles.

Expert: Adding additives, according to regulations, such as preservatives, modifiers, antioxidants, is allowed in instant noodles and their seasoning packets. However, in the production of instant noodles in our country, there are quite a few problems in the seasoning packets or the production process itself, mainly involving excessive or non-compliant use of additives.
