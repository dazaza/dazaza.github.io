How to Write a Good Sponsored Article

by longersing on 2009-07-09 17:58:48

What is a soft article? I think everyone has already got some understanding through checking online materials, and I often see some soft articles on the Internet. And I will summarize it in a few simple sentences: Articles that can be obviously identified as soft articles are at most third-party advertising media. However, what we aim to do today is to make you, as a browser, have the following feelings after reading the article:

1. This is an authoritative report related to social, economic and other factors;

2. It relates to problems I may encounter in certain aspects in the future;

3. It's really worth my time to read;

4. After reading, I will still view issues based on the viewpoints mentioned in the article;

5. When I encounter problems in the future, I might come back to consult some authoritative institutions mentioned in this article.

Actually, soft articles are just that simple. You may need them as a webmaster. As an SEOer, you must know how to write them.

Soft articles favored by portals include review analysis and product evaluation articles which are quite popular. Moreover, such articles usually have a high click-through rate, due to the fact that the products involved in these reviews indeed have a large number of "information seekers" on the Internet. Just like when we need to buy something, we first go online to search for it and find some detailed parameters of the product. The same principle applies: Soft articles are created for this search result, promoting themselves and showcasing themselves in front of demanders.

Portals prefer analytical articles that focus on specific events. These articles have a broad audience and contain fewer ad components, so they can fit most portals and some medium and small comprehensive websites.

Nowadays, there are still many soft articles that are highly favored by merchants: For example, embedding product names into articles with storylines; replacing and modifying certain keywords from official information of a product in bulk; using mythological stories to highlight the importance of modern products; pretending to belittle a product under the guise of discussing the impact of certain regulations on an industry (actually taking advantage of readers' curiosity "those who don't understand will search for the product"). However, these soft articles do not perform well in actual promotion, especially those articles where links are added to the keyword parts. Unless written smoothly, they may instead damage the product image because many people, in order to meet the keyword density and links, even write articles that are incomprehensible like a "Chinese dictionary".
