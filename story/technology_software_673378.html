This has caused headaches for webmasters who receive tens of thousands of articles per day, keeping their websites in a state of reduced weight. Now let me share my experience in building a novel website.

First, you need to consider whether you have the determination to operate this novel website for a long time, rather than giving up after a month if the website doesn't perform well. You need to have a balanced mindset; otherwise, you might as well not do it at all, considering the intense competition in the novel website field (in several QQ groups I am in, there are over 500 people doing novel websites, and some webmasters even have more than ten novel websites). If you have the determination to persist in the long term, please continue reading.

The reason why building a novel website is costly lies mainly in the high requirements for server configuration. Wealthy friends use servers, while those who want to save a bit use VPSs, along with using Guan Guan's data collection tool. Therefore, the minimum construction cost for a new novel website (domain name plus server plus Guan Guan) is around 1000 yuan. Of course, the server I mentioned is paid monthly, excluding your template-related expenses. You also need to consider the long-term persistence, calculating the expenses for three months to half a year, not counting your human and mental resources.

A good novel website should have its own characteristics and an easy-to-remember domain name, allowing more people to remember your website. The keywords of the website should ideally include explanations of your domain name. For example, my www.hanshufang.com is named "Han Study Room," making it easier for people to remember. Other keywords should not be too popular. Novel websites focus on repeat rates. As long as you collect and update quickly, have fewer pop-up ads, the repeat rate will be quite high, leading to a high PV and quick ranking.

In terms of external links and promotion, it's best to participate in Baidu Knows, SouSou, and other question-and-answer websites, adding your website address. Your answers can directly convert into loyal readers. For instance, when someone asks where they can read the VIP chapters of "Stealing Heaven," it's best to know how to guide others to click on your website rather than simply adding your URL. An answer like "Hello, I am also a fan of 'Stealing Heaven.' The website I often visit is (http://www.hanshufang.com.cn), which has fewer pop-up ads and allows free viewing of VIP chapters" would make others likely to visit your website. In the early stages, it's best not to put up advertisements to give readers a good experience rather than making them leave immediately. Let your website be memorable to them.

To make them remember your website, one personal trick is to add a banner logo at the top of the website displaying your URL. Write something like "Don't worry if you can't remember our website address, Han Study Room - the latest free original romance novel reading and download." Because it's a new website, people won't search for your site using keywords like "novel reading." The only possible way is through your unique website name. The advantage of this method is that you're simultaneously creating your own keywords for your website. Remember to be a keyword creator, not a follower, because your website can't surpass the rankings of older sites unless they stop or get penalized, but the chances are slim.

Secondly, don't waste your server resources. You can use PT thievery to create a few more novel websites, changing the keywords of each site. Spend a few dozen yuan to buy space and purchase weighted domain names, with your own site as the target. This way, you don't have to worry about your thievery program causing issues such as loss of article inclusion leading to reduced weight. Remember, fate is in your own hands, not controlled by others. This method isn't used by me alone; many friends are doing it. Writing another person's site as the target is too risky, and all the hard-earned rankings could vanish instantly.

To summarize, novel websites have large traffic, with tens of thousands of people reading novels every day. To outperform others, you must provide readers with a true experience, reduce advertising, take a long-term approach, and focus on repeat customers. Such websites are the way forward, and all major search engines will favor your site due to its high PV and user engagement depth.
