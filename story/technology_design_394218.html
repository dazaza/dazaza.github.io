Knowledge of online stores

Best Answer:

The process of starting an online store begins not on the internet, but in your mind. You need to decide what kind of store you want to open. In this aspect, opening an online store is no different from a traditional brick-and-mortar shop - finding a good market and ensuring your products are competitive are the keys to success.

Step Two: Choose a platform or website for your store. You need to select a website that offers a personal store platform and register as a user. This step is crucial. Most websites will require registration using your real name and valid identification documents such as an ID card. When choosing a website, factors like high traffic, whether it's free or paid, and the cost structure are all important considerations. Many platforms now offer free store-opening services, which can save you quite a bit of money.

Step Three: Apply to open a store on the website. You must carefully fill out the categories of products your store will provide. For example, if you're selling fashion watches, they should be categorized under "Jewelry, Watches, Glasses" > "Watches". This ensures your target customers can find you easily. Then, you'll need to come up with a catchy name for your store. The more appealing the name, the more likely netizens are to click on your store in the listings. Some online stores display personal information, which should be filled out truthfully to build trust.

Step Four: Procurement. You can source your inventory from familiar channels or platforms. Controlling costs and sourcing at low prices are key.

Step Five: Product Listing. You need to list each product’s details on the website, including its name, origin, location, nature, appearance, quantity, transaction method, and transaction deadline. It's best to include pictures of the products. Names should be as comprehensive as possible, highlighting the advantages, because when others search for similar items, only the name will appear in the list. To increase appeal, the quality of the images should be as good as possible, and descriptions should be detailed. If shipping is required, it's best to clarify who is responsible for the shipping fees.

An extremely important part of listing is setting the price. Typically, websites allow sellers to set starting prices, reserve prices, and fixed prices. Suppose a seller wants to sell a piece of clothing bought at 100 yuan and plans to sell it for 150 yuan. If the seller were a traditional shopkeeper, they would just start by marking it at 150 yuan, then gradually lower the price if it doesn't sell. However, online bidding works differently. Sellers first set a starting price, and buyers bid upwards from there. A lower starting price tends to attract more interest; some sellers even start bids at 1 yuan to draw attention.

However, setting too low a starting price carries the risk of ending up with a final sale price that's also too low. Therefore, sellers should simultaneously set a reserve price, for instance, setting 105 yuan as the reserve price to ensure the item isn't sold below cost. Another downside of a very low starting price is that it may imply you're willing to sell at a very low price, causing the auction to linger at a low price point. If the seller feels waiting for the auction to finish takes too long, they can set a fixed price. Once a buyer agrees to pay this price, the deal is immediately done. The downside is that if multiple buyers are interested, the price won't be driven higher. Sellers should use these settings according to their specific situations.

Step Six: Marketing and Promotion. To boost your store's popularity, especially in the early stages, appropriate marketing and promotion are necessary. However, relying solely on online methods isn't enough; you need to promote through various channels both online and offline. For example, you can purchase ad space on high-traffic pages of the website for “hot product recommendations,” make product names bold in category lists, add pictures to grab attention. You can also utilize free advertising, such as exchanging links with other stores and websites.

Step Seven: Mid-Sale Service. When customers are deciding whether to buy, they may need much more information than what you've provided. They might ask questions online at any time, and you should respond promptly and patiently. However, note that many websites prohibit buyers and sellers from sharing any personal contact information online (such as email or phone numbers) to prevent private transactions that evade transaction fees, otherwise penalties will apply.

Step Eight: Transaction. After a successful deal, the website will notify both parties of the contact details, and transactions can proceed as agreed, either through face-to-face meetings or via remittances and mail. Transactions should happen quickly to avoid doubts about your creditworthiness. Whether to offer additional after-sales services depends on prior agreements between the two parties.

Step Nine: Evaluation or Complaint. Credit is a very important factor in online transactions. To help build a trustworthy environment, if the transaction is satisfactory, it's best to give the other party a positive review and earn good reviews through excellent service. If the transaction fails, give a negative review or file a complaint with the website to minimize losses and warn others. If you receive a complaint, handle it promptly to avoid tarnishing your own credit.

Step Ten: After-Sales Service. There's no need to elaborate on this point. Providing thorough and considerate after-sales service is a crucial factor in maintaining enduring business success. Keep in touch with different customers and manage customer relationships well.

Currently, there are over a hundred large shopping websites in China offering online store services, but only a few have significant influence. Here are introductions to a few main relevant websites:

EachNet (www.eachnet.com): Founded in Shanghai on August 18, 1999, by Shao Yibo and Tan Haiyin, it is the largest Chinese-language online trading platform globally, providing C2C (individual-to-individual) and B2C (business-to-individual) network platform construction and services. In March 2002, EachNet received a $30 million investment from eBay, the largest US e-commerce company, and formed a strategic partnership. In June 2003, eBay added a $150 million investment in EachNet. To date, EachNet has attracted nearly $220 million in foreign investment, making it the domestic enterprise attracting the most foreign capital among overseas student entrepreneurs. EachNet (www.ebay.com.cn) was one of the earliest shopping websites in China to offer online store services. Registering an online store incurs charges, including bottom price setup fees, item listing fees, transaction service fees, and advertising value-added service fees.

Taobao (www.taobao.com): Taobao is a leading individual transaction (C2C) online platform domestically, founded on May 10, 2003, with a 450 million yuan investment from Alibaba, the world's best business-to-business (B2B) e-commerce company, aiming to become the world's largest personal transaction website.

Taobao currently offers free registration, free certification, and free store-opening services.

Yipai (www.1pai.com.cn): Yipai is a newly established, fully functional, high-quality online buying and selling community created by a joint venture between Sina and Yahoo Internet brands in early 2004 — Beijing Xinyah Online Information Technology Co., Ltd., providing smooth trading platforms and various transaction models for small and medium-sized enterprises and individual users in China.

Yipai currently provides free online store-opening services.

8848 Network (www.8848.com): Established on May 18, 1999, 8848 was once the flagship of China's e-commerce enterprises. In September 2003, it began preparing new business development plans, shifting its focus to establishing China's first professional shopping search engine — 8848 Shopping Engine, serving small and medium merchants through 8848 Online Stores, and providing e-commerce solutions for corporate users to expand their businesses. 8848.com officially launched on January 1, 2004.

8848 currently provides free online store-opening services.
