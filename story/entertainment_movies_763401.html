Introduction:

Yellowstone National Park is located on the lava plateau between the northern and central Rocky Mountains in the western part of the United States, with the vast majority in the northwestern part of Wyoming. It has an elevation of 2,134-2,438 meters and covers an area of 8,956 square kilometers.

The Yellowstone River and Yellowstone Lake run through it, featuring canyons, waterfalls, hot springs, and geysers, presenting picturesque scenery that captivates visitors. Among these, the "Old Faithful" geyser, which erupts every hour, is the most famous. The park is densely forested and also houses some surviving wildlife such as American bison for public viewing. There is also a historical museum within the park.

Since its establishment in 1872, over 60 million people have visited Yellowstone. Visitors come from all over the world, with diverse backgrounds, leading to a wide range of experiences and impressions: admiration, awe, introspection, excitement, understanding of nature's power and tranquility, and even mixed emotions of joy and sorrow.

Yellowstone Park is the world's oldest and most primitive national park. According to the U.S. Congressional Act of March 1, 1872, Yellowstone Park was "approved as a public park and recreational site for the benefit of the people," and it is also "to preserve all its trees, mineral deposits, natural wonders, landscapes, and other objects in their existing natural state without destruction."

Within the vast natural forest area of Wyoming, Yellowstone National Park covers approximately 9,000 hectares. Impressive geothermal phenomena can be observed there, along with more than 3,000 geysers, steam vents, and hot springs. Yellowstone National Park is also renowned worldwide for its wildlife, including grizzly bears, wolves, bison, and elk (not the Chinese species).

The distinctive geological feature that initially attracted interest and led to Yellowstone becoming a national park is the geothermal activity. Here, you will find more geysers and hot springs than anywhere else in the world, along with the colorful Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone, fossil forests, and Yellowstone Lake.

Based on cultural relics found here, the history of civilization in Yellowstone Park can be traced back 12,000 years. More recent history can be seen in the historical architecture, as well as the preserved facilities used by park administrators and visitors from various periods. Ninety-nine percent of the park remains undeveloped, allowing for the proliferation of numerous biological species. It hosts the largest number and variety of mammals on land.

Yellowstone Park is a true wilderness, one of the few large-scale natural environments preserved in the 48 contiguous states of the United States. Here, you can experience nature. June, July, and August are the peak months for visits, during which the park enforces various rules and regulations to ensure visitor safety and protect natural and cultural heritage.

Yellowstone National Park is the world's first national park and was one of the earliest entries in the World Heritage List in 1978. The main part of the park is located in the northwest corner of Wyoming, extending partially into southwestern Montana and eastern Idaho. It covers an area of 7,988 square kilometers (according to data from the book "Exploration Journey Series - American National Parks"; while the June 2002 issue of "Man and Nature" magazine provides data of 2,219,833 acres; the www.whgx.library.sh.cn website gives data of 3,400 square miles).

Yellowstone Park, this world of ice and fire, this labyrinth of jagged peaks, was born nearly two million years ago from a volcanic eruption. Ninety-nine percent of the park remains undeveloped, making it a vast and pristine natural area spread across the highest peaks of the Rocky Mountains. Abundant rainfall makes it the source of many major American rivers. On this expansive igneous plateau averaging 8,000 feet in height, there are geological wonders such as mountains, stone forests, eroded lava flows, and obsidian hills.

Yellowstone National Park is the oldest and largest national park established in the United States. It is renowned worldwide for preserving the natural environment in its original form.

Another scenic feature within Yellowstone National Park is the Yellowstone River, which bursts forth from the Yellowstone Canyon, running through the entire park and reaching into Montana. The Yellowstone River carved through the mountains to create the magnificent Yellowstone Grand Canyon. Under the sunlight, the canyon walls transition in color from orange-yellow to reddish-orange, resembling two winding ribbons. Due to the high elevation of the park, the Yellowstone River and its tributaries deeply cut into the canyons, forming many spectacular waterfalls.
