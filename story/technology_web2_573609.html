Domino DJ: A Social Music Recommendation and Interaction Product

by daodao on 2011-11-21 05:11:11

Domino DJ is a new music discovery website that helps people find great music through graphical, social, and simulated live DJ experiences. On Domino DJ, everyone can become a real DJ, allowing users to decide the playlist for the party. By playing and sharing your own music, you can meet like-minded friends, discuss music experiences, and share feelings. Additionally, everyone in the room has the right to vote for or against the songs played by the current DJ. By playing excellent songs, you can earn points from listeners, which will help you gain higher honors in the Domino DJ community growth system.

Domino DJ was launched two days ago by the Domino Music team as a product tagged with "social music recommendation and interaction." Its creative inspiration comes directly from Turntable, which recently completed a $7 million Series A round. As for this product that has achieved a certain level of success (favor from capital markets and startup competition judges) abroad, how likely is it to succeed if brought directly to China, and how large is the user base it can cover? — I find it hard to be optimistic.

Looking back at the domestic internet market over the past five years, there are recorded entrepreneurial and innovative projects related to Avatar-based social products (non-social gaming categories), and their lifespans generally last between six months to one year. For entrepreneurs, the painful lessons of failure were attributed to the product's user experience, technical solutions, and the network bandwidth not keeping up with the growing demands of users at the time; however, when we look back today, the reasons for failure do not lie in these aspects. On the contrary, compared to five years ago, the needs of the general public have never undergone significant changes. Simply put, 90% of internet users we don't come into contact with cannot accept or understand the significance of this form of interaction within the browser; and the greatest value of Avatar—interactivity and entertainment—is considered terrible and boring due to users' lack of understanding.

BTW: Domino DJ is currently in the beta testing phase, with five beta invitation codes available in the comment section for interested students to claim.
