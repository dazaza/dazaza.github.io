Murder fugitive went blind due to eye injury that he dare not seek medical treatment for during the escape

by yani091lxue on 2012-03-07 09:31:41

Early Report: Eighteen years ago, a 26-year-old man from Cao County, Wu Mou, along with his friend, heard that his friend was overcharged by a motorcycle taxi driver by 10 yuan. In order to help his friend vent his anger, he, fueled by alcohol, conspired with his friend to beat up the motorcycle taxi driver and ended up beating him to death. After the incident, seeing his friend arrested by the police, he started an 18-year escape under the false name of his younger brother. During his escape, even after accidentally injuring his eye, he didn't dare to seek medical treatment, ultimately resulting in blindness in his left eye. Recently, the Jiaozhou police caught him during a routine inspection of temporary residence permits. Yesterday, it was learned by reporters that Wu Mou has been criminally detained.

Wu Mou killed the motorcycle taxi driver to help his friend vent his anger.

On October 20, 1993, at noon, Wu Mou and his friend Wei Mou were eating at a small restaurant in their hometown in Cao County. During the meal, Wei Mou told Wu Mou that on that day, he rented a farm tricycle to visit a friend, agreeing with the driver Li Mou on a fare of 20 yuan. However, upon arrival, the driver forcibly charged him 30 yuan. Seeing his friend Wei Mou very angry, Wu Mou, who usually liked to stand up for others, after finishing the meal, pulled Wei Mou to "teach this driver a lesson." That afternoon, the two, with ropes and sticks, tricked Li Mou into going to a remote area under the pretense of renting a car. They first hung Li Mou on a tree and subjected him to "severe torture." Seeing that Li Mou would not admit his mistake, the two beat him up with sticks, eventually causing his death. After receiving the alarm, the local police captured Wei Mou on the same day. Seeing his friend arrested, Wu Mou embarked on an 18-year escape.

Routine checks revealed the fugitive murderer.

However, just as the Cao County police were racking their brains on how to capture Wu Mou, on December 10, officers from the Yunxi Police Station in Jiaozhou City unexpectedly found that a left-eyed disabled man named "Wu Qixin" had identity information that matched very closely with the wanted fugitive Wu Mou suspected of intentional murder. Subsequently, the officers quickly contacted the Cao County police and found out that there indeed was a man named "Wu Qixin" in a certain village in Suzi Town, Cao County. This man had always lived in his hometown and had not gone out, and his left eye was also not blind. It was only his brother Wu Mou who, due to a murder case 18 years ago, had been on the run ever since. Based on the photo of Wu Mou passed by the Cao County police, after comparison, the investigating officers confirmed that the eye-disabled man was indeed the fugitive Wu Mou. After confirming Wu Mou's identity, on December 12, the investigating officers captured Wu Mou in a rented civilian house in Jiaozhou.

Injuring his eye, he dared not seek hospital treatment.

According to Wu Mou's confession, after the incident, he took his younger brother's ID card and fled to Beijing, impersonating his younger brother's name to work as a laborer on a construction site in Beijing. During his escape, Wu Mou never dared to mention his family to his co-workers. Afraid of revealing the truth after drinking, he also quit drinking. In November 2000, while working on a construction site, Wu Mou accidentally injured his left eye with a steel bar. Although Wu Mou was immediately sent to the hospital by his co-workers, fearing exposure, he hurriedly checked out without treatment after receiving a 5,000-yuan compensation from the construction site, ultimately leading to the blindness of his left eye due to delayed treatment. After his left eye became disabled, Wu Mou came to Yantai, Weihai, Qingdao, and other places to work odd jobs. In October this year, he fled to Jiaozhou and worked in a furniture company.

Yesterday, it was learned by reporters that the Jiaozhou police will hand over Wu Mou to the Cao County police in the near future.

(Reporters Mu Chengzi, Correspondent Zhao Jingmei)
