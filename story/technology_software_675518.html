The openness and upgradeability of systems are widely concerned by customers. Starting from this year, the openness and upgradeability of systems have begun to attract the attention of a large number of customers. Can more devices be connected to achieve the desired functions? Can it be compatible with every product on the market? Will upcoming electronic products be recognized by the smart home system? These are questions that customers often ask. Moreover, all customers do not want their expensive smart home systems to be replaced every year like mobile phones. Therefore, the openness and upgradeability of the system has become another focus for all suppliers in 2012. It takes a certain amount of strength to make one's smart home system have the same integration capability as big brand smart home systems to integrate tens of thousands of products. The upgradeability is a major challenge for bus-based systems realized through comprehensive wiring. Wireless solutions have inherent advantages in terms of upgradeability, as corresponding devices can be added without damaging the decoration, easily realizing extended functions. In addition, remote automatic upgrades of system software like Wulian's is also a major trend in the future, where the system and devices can automatically upgrade to the latest functions without requiring management by customers or dealers. The usability after system crashes is included in the design.

We should pay attention to an issue: what if our smart home system breaks down or crashes? Many comprehensive wiring solutions route all the strong electric wires of the lamps to the integrated electrical box, which controls them via relays. The switch panel actually controls the weak electric wires that control the relays. Once the relay fails or there is a problem with the line, the lights cannot be turned on, and the customer can only spend a night in the dark or take a flashlight to the electrical room to try their luck.

This issue is believed to be completely resolved in 2012. A new solution has emerged, and the impact caused by system failures will be minimized. For example, most of the switches of smart home systems currently on the market can operate independently without the host. Even if the system crashes, they can still function as ordinary switches!
