Tencent and Guangdong Unicom launch "WeChat data plan", available on August 8th

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 17:15:42

The rumored collaboration between WeChat and China Unicom Guangdong, dubbed as "good buddies," was confirmed today. The timing was carefully chosen - at 8:08 AM, Tencent announced on YiXun (its e-commerce platform) the launch of the "WeChat Wo" package in partnership with China Unicom. According to YiXun's announcement, the official launch date for this collaboration is August 8, 2013.

In the official introduction, it states that "WeChat Wo is more than just a SIM card." Users who choose the "WeChat Wo" package will receive additional data, exclusive WeChat privileges, and can also select China Unicom premium numbers ending with "88".

There are numerous examples worldwide of internet companies (referred to as "OTT enterprises" in China) collaborating with telecom operators, such as Line partnering with Docomo in Japan, Google Voice with Sprint in the U.S., and Spotify with Deutsche Telekom in Germany. However, "WeChat Wo" sets a precedent by allowing users to choose the form of service provided by the internet company and the operator.

Tencent did not announce the specific details of its collaboration with the operator but instead allows users to select their desired "WeChat privileges." Among the available privileges are:

Group chat limit increased from 40 to 60 people

Additional data allowance

Discounts on WeChat Pay

Free games

Free emoticons

Free music downloads

Free HD movie viewing

Free exchange of HD images

Increased visibility in the "People Nearby" feature (if desired)

Approximately 23,000 people have already participated in "customizing" their WeChat privileges. Based on this model, the "WeChat Wo" package launched jointly by Tencent and China Unicom Guangdong will provide WeChat privileges based on popularity. It is expected that there may be multiple privileges rather than just one. Of course, users can also request all privileges.

The "WeChat Wo" package will be launched on August 8, 22 days from now. From today until August 4, users can "like" and select their desired privileges on the YiXun page; from August 5 to 7, pre-orders will be accepted; and sales will begin on August 8.

Regarding the specific package format, according to earlier reports from Phoenix New Media, WeChat might offer WeChat privileges on top of China Unicom Guangdong's existing 36-yuan student-oriented Wo Pai package. However, it remains unclear whether the "WeChat package" is just a small-scale trial or if it will be extended to more users after enthusiastic participation.

Last Monday, we exclusively reported that WeChat would collaborate with mainland operators to launch customized WeChat services. At that time, based on overseas cooperation cases, we speculated that the collaboration between WeChat and operators could take forms such as data packages, exclusive version services, call discounts, etc. However, our understanding of the depth of their collaboration was limited. Currently, we still need to wait some time to know what kind of package they will base their collaboration on and whether a separate "WeChat package" will be launched.

Given that the "WeChat Wo package" news was released through Tencent's e-commerce channel - YiXun, it implies that both parties will collaborate on specific business sales. Officially, the "WeChat Wo" will issue physical SIM cards, and we have reason to believe that buyers of these SIM cards will not be limited to students but will also include more loyal WeChat users. After all, WeChat data usage may already constitute a significant portion of their mobile data expenses.

To conclude, as a user, I am delighted to see WeChat collaborate with operators. While the privilege functions may not be something I urgently need, bundling WeChat data into a single package is certainly something I require.

Finally, there's one detail worth discussing: among the available "WeChat privileges," there is an option for "free emoticons." Although the free emoticon screenshots from YiXun's official Weibo account are still traditional ones, the mention of "free" suggests the existence of paid emoticons. This indicates that the WeChat 5.0 version, which will introduce paid emoticons, will not be released later than August 8. The rest is up to everyone to dig further.
