The Definition of Smart Home is Changed by the Internet of Things

Today, if you still think that the augmented reality technology currently enhanced by the Internet of Things (IoT) is just a castle in the air, then you'd better change this view.

Smart home refers to a home automation network platform used for remote control and monitoring. When it comes to the impact of China's IoT development on the smart home industry, IoT is a broad and macroscopic concept. An important application or feature of smart homes is to achieve interconnectivity. In this sense, smart homes are encompassed within the larger concept of IoT.

With the introduction of IoT-enabled appliances in the market, it becomes more evident that IoT technology will inevitably accelerate the development of smart homes. However, "augmented reality" technology is moving beyond the conceptual and practical stages that only interest IT enthusiasts; it is now extending its reach to the next generation: today's internet-savvy children.

Currently, after years of consumer education and demand guidance in China, the concept of smart homes has become deeply rooted in people's minds. However, the market has not yet truly taken off. It can be said that China's smart home industry is currently in a transitional phase leading to mature applications. Developed countries have higher overall levels of informatization, and consumers in these regions have accepted the concept of smart homes earlier. Countries such as the United States, Germany, Singapore, and Japan already have relatively widespread applications of smart homes!

We can see that, in fact, home automation products, home networking products, networked appliance products, and information appliance products are just one type within the smart home system product category. They each have distinct product characteristics and cannot simply be equated. Of course, there are also many overlapping concepts among products, such as the set-top boxes that have gained popularity with the convergence of three networks. These can be understood as both home networking products and information appliance products.

Extending to the use of products in smart homes, it should be noted that many domestic media outlets and manufacturers intentionally or unintentionally confuse the concepts of smart homes and information appliances (or networked appliances). Some manufacturers simply amplify the functions of one or two information appliances and claim that having these information appliances at home means achieving a smart home. This is clearly an immature and laughable action that has brought some adverse effects to the industry, causing consumers to eventually doubt smart homes. Some manufacturers should stop this shortsighted approach.

Smart homes mainly include three aspects of application: security, entertainment, and office work. Some people believe that China’s smart home development can start with smart security, which is indeed an important application of smart homes. The development of smart homes allows each enterprise to integrate more intelligent applications into their own products based on their own products, heading towards the direction of smart homes, and ultimately achieving the same goal through different paths.

Isn't it true? Early media revelations about the exploration of Bill Gates' smart mansion found that the number of vendors and organizations claiming to develop smart homes domestically and internationally is relatively large. For smart homes, the very important aspect of interconnectivity requires certain standards. Otherwise, inconsistent home product standards will greatly trouble users. If purchased products fail to work together due to differences in communication or interface standards, each product operates independently. What kind of digital family would that be?

Recently, the author learned from the Yizhuang Management Committee that cooperation between smart home control system integrators and domestic famous IT enterprises has preliminarily established a complete set of smart home systems! Moreover, some research institutions sensitive to technological development trends and market trends, along with capable companies, have also seen the vast prospects of this market. They have begun preliminary deployment and planning for researching and developing related systems and products, and have ventured into the entirely new field of smart family networks. The successful debut of the Yizhuang Smart Home Experience Hall will gradually develop residential decoration and renovation from local renovation teams. From this, we can see that IoT technology will certainly drive the rapid development of smart families and welcome a new era of intelligence.

In addition, the author corrects the terminology regarding smart homes: smart homes are not equivalent to luxurious homes; smart homes are not the same as smart neighborhoods.

Smart homes consist of three parts: integrating security protection, convenience and fashion, and healthy living into three major lifestyle home products. This breaks the previous mode of indoor switch remote control, making home safety and family health smarter, thus creating a truly smart and healthy life. Smart homes uniquely adopt an experience consumption + terminal advisor model, using open scenes where all products can be directly touched by consumers, turning wealth's barren land into a place where renowned heroes emerge.

When choosing home appliances, apart from considering brand and function, health is gradually becoming an important factor influencing consumer purchasing decisions. With the improvement of people's living standards, and with various electrical brands constantly entering consumers' sights in recent years, the increase in electrical brands has intensified competition in this market. Manufacturers have started to focus more on the functionality of electrical appliances, striving to attract more consumers. Low-carbon, environmentally friendly, and health-oriented therapeutic home appliances often receive more attention from consumers.
