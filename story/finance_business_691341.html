Beijing Xidan driver crashes into 9 people due to mental breakdown, sentenced to 7 years in prison

by cozfio76 on 2012-03-05 15:28:11

It was reported that after Li Honghai hit 9 people, he was stopped by a SWAT vehicle that was on duty. After getting out of the car with his hands up, he said: "I want to report a crime." A forensic appraisal showed that Li Honghai suffered from schizophrenia and had limited criminal responsibility at the time of the crime. The court determined that Li Honghai turned himself in after committing the crime, and thus made the above ruling.

When this case was adjourned, Li Honghai's lawyer introduced to the reporter that Li Honghai's mental state was abnormal before the incident. He often muttered about someone keying him, having a bug in his house, and saying that there was mercury in the milk he drank and poison in his blood. He also claimed that there was a group of 3000 people in Beijing conducting experiments with human organs, and that his daughter and other family members were controlled by this killing organization. On the day of the incident, Li Honghai said he was driving to Fuwai Hospital for medical treatment. On the way, he called 110 to report the killing organization. The police officer who received the call asked him to wait for processing. Li Honghai thought that the police did not take his report seriously, so he decided to cause an incident by hitting people in Xidan to attract the attention of the police.

According to reports, Li Honghai, a person from Heilongjiang who came to Beijing, drove a silver-gray Jetta sedan and hit 9 innocent pedestrians on Xidan Street. In the morning yesterday, the Beijing No. 1 Intermediate People's Court made a first-instance judgment on this case. The court determined that Li Honghai suffered from schizophrenia and had self-surrender circumstances at the time of the crime. In the first-instance trial, Li Honghai was sentenced to 7 years in prison for endangering public safety by dangerous means, and was ordered to compensate the 9 victims a total of more than 350,000 yuan.

After the verdict was announced, the victims who walked out of the courtroom expressed to the reporters that they believed the sentence for Li Honghai was too lenient. Additionally, although Li Honghai agreed to compensate for the victims' losses, according to his own statement, he only had some stocks and no other assets. The real estate he purchased in Tongzhou was registered under his parents' names, making it difficult for the court to enforce execution. They were worried whether they would be able to receive compensation or if they would have been hit for nothing.

Early yesterday morning, several victims who were injured by Li Honghai gathered at the court waiting for the announcement of the verdict.

In the morning of November 22, 2010, on Xidan North Street, a silver-gray Jetta suddenly accelerated and ran over pedestrians without warning. Afterwards, amidst shouts of "Stop the car! It's hitting people!", the Jetta continued to accelerate northward. In front of the Xidan Shopping Center, Ms. Fan, a retired journalist from Guangming Daily, was the fourth person to be knocked down. The Jetta finally crashed into a man in his 60s at the turning point of Lingjing Alley, where it was intercepted and stopped by a SWAT vehicle. Along the scene were injured people lying on the ground and severely deformed bicycles. Nine injured people were sent to the hospital for treatment after the incident.
