In today's society, modern women are very independent and self-reliant. In proportion to their independence, they also face significant work pressures. Therefore, many sexually active women often neglect changes in their physical condition, such as early pregnancy. However, many women do not even realize that they are pregnant. Hence, the importance of early pregnancy checks for women goes without saying. Not only can it prevent the effects of gynecological diseases on children, but it can also detect ectopic pregnancies, protecting the physical health of women.

So, what is the purpose of early pregnancy checks? What is its significance?

Early pregnancy checks usually involve the first examination around 40 days after the cessation of menstruation. The doctor will inquire about the patient's history and perform a gynecological examination to confirm the pregnancy. If necessary, prenatal consultations and genetic consultations may be conducted to determine whether the pregnant woman can continue with the pregnancy. Early pregnancy checks can confirm whether the size of the uterus corresponds with the duration of amenorrhea, thereby understanding the development of the embryo and discovering abnormalities or gynecological diseases in the reproductive organs.

Another aspect of early pregnancy checks involves blood, urine, and liver function tests, which can help identify various diseases affecting pregnancy at an early stage. If the examination reveals that continuing the pregnancy would endanger the life of the pregnant woman or if the baby has severe congenital malformations, the pregnancy should be terminated as soon as possible.

Early pregnancy checks are very necessary because this examination can discover many potential conditions. Some people skip these checks to reduce the cost of abortions, which is very dangerous. Women who unexpectedly become pregnant must not ignore this check during an abortion. When necessary, this check can save lives!
