Many of our enterprises have already felt the charm of a brand and seem to understand the value of branding. Relevant data shows that this is well known in the industry: China produces 9 billion pairs of shoes annually, but the entire shoe industry's profit does not surpass that of a single Nike company; we are the birthplace of tea culture and a major tea exporter, yet the entire tea industry’s revenue doesn't match Starbucks'. But how exactly should we operate a brand? How do we build one? There are two crucial points, as evidenced by the launch of the Patriot Ge Kiln camera.

Firstly, product development—what we mean when we say it starts with the product and succeeds through branding (communication). The Brand Seed Theory suggests that building a brand must involve finding its unique seed, which is the core concept in the consumer's mind, while emphasizing its manifestation in product and service development. Product innovation is the most critical part of brand marketing, where effective brand communication leads to the sale of products or services. In essence, it boils down to what you really want to do?

Currently, in many brand marketing innovations, people focus on breakthroughs in marketing communication methods, including packaging and promotion of brands, but there isn’t enough emphasis on product development and innovation. Only products or services can provide direct brand experiences, and establishing a true brand requires innovative products or services. Having only perfect functionality and quality is not enough.

In product development, we generally follow the three-tier product strategy framework proposed by Philip Kotler, which includes value perspective, tangible product perspective, and added-value perspective. However, due to the internet, everything is changing. Consumers' definitions of products are gradually shifting away from their core benefits. For instance, Xiangshan red leaves are sold in various forms, and in Luogu Alley, there are stores selling creative bottles labeled with hopes, struggles, love, cuteness, etc. So, what would you say the utility value of these products is?

Consumers define products or services as a mental choice, processing, or even restructuring, moving away from the conventional core benefits and functions of products, referred to as "mimetic products." This decision-making power lies with the consumers. Many so-called brand scientists' logic and theories often ignore the inner desires of consumers.

If the Patriot digital camera were to compete with traditional Japanese and Korean digital giants based on industry fundamentals, what would be the result? But what if we turn the camera into an art piece? The Patriot Ge Kiln was born, combining traditional Ge Kiln with fashionable digital technology, creating a fascinating reaction. The technological art piece strongly impacts everyone's inner being. Even if you already own an international-brand digital camera, it doesn't matter. You can still get a unique, strong personality-driven Patriot Ge Kiln. Digital products are a trend, and traditional culture is a throwback; the stark contrast between the two is extremely striking. It serves as a way to express your extraordinary individuality and convey emotions. At this point, does the camera's function itself still matter? Therefore, it's no surprise that the Patriot Ge Kiln could sell for 400,000 RMB at a Singapore President's dinner or fetch 660,000 RMB at an Olympic auction.

Of course, it is especially important to mention here that traditional culture is like fertile soil. What kind of modern commercial fruits grow in this rich soil requires wisdom. Many cultures have era-specific characteristics. Despite our Tang suits being great, few people wear them on the street. Despite our yellow robes being excellent, they're more appropriate for television stages. As I mentioned in "Blind Pursuit of 'Old' Culture Detrimental to Brand Marketing," Coca-Cola is a genuine century-old shop, but do you think it's old? On the contrary, it's fashionable. The Patriot Ge Kiln shouldn't pursue traditional culture merely for tradition's sake but must adhere to the established Chinese style series, continuing to creatively develop digital tech products. The space for technological art pieces is vast.

Secondly, brand communication. As previously mentioned in the issue of product development, more attention within the industry is focused on marketing and communication. It's also important to note that many within the industry don't pay enough attention to communication and fail to understand the importance of brand communication. Otherwise, how could the saying "channels reign supreme" still be so prevalent! We often say that property is in the spread of communication. Bai Yansong once said that dragging a dog onto CCTV for continuous television appearances for a month would make it a nationally famous dog. Why? As I discussed in "Also Taking Ye Maozhong to Say Industry Trends," communication is the fundamental value of brand marketing. The research results published by Communication University of China under the National Administration of Radio, Film, and Television's Humanities and Social Sciences Project, "Effective Brand Communication," suggest that marketing and communication have become unified in some sense.

But how do we carry out brand communication? Is it still about making various media budgets and then placing ads in the media? In the fragmented media age, broad coverage has far exceeded the capacity of individual companies, let alone the fact that thousands of pieces of information have already numbed consumers. Nowadays, business and finance newspapers casually have over 100 pages, set-top box TVs can watch over 200 channels, and there are many community-based media online discussing proper communication. So what about the dissemination of your brand information?

The Brand Seed Theory emphasizes continuous growth in communication, stressing the creation of communication channels. Communication is no longer just unidirectional embellishment done by manufacturers, nor is it about false flower arrangement arts. I've repeatedly emphasized that while flower arrangement is intricate, it regrettably lacks vitality. Instead, we should allow consumers to water your brand seeds, letting governments, investors, upstream and downstream stakeholders nurture these seeds. This is an interactive growth process. Although the internet now provides us with good interactive opportunities, unfortunately, more manufacturers still haven't changed their "manipulative control" mindset. The recent disputes between two large dairy giants are a clear example. They haven't realized that the consumer era has arrived, and even the best disguises can easily be exposed due to the internet. Therefore, giving power to consumers, engaging in honest communication, and fostering responsible consumer culture will lead consumers to vote with their money.

Thus, it seems that Patriot hasn't spent much on advertising, without big-budget highway billboards or CCTV top spots, yet they have been flourishing with their own unique style. This aligns with current communication rules, genuinely creating word-of-mouth. President Feng Jun has also become a star like Liu Xiang, appearing on subway lightboxes, endorsing banks. His every move attracts media attention, which is an innovation in media channels. Corporate leaders become the main channel for communication. Every event Feng Jun participates in, whether the Olympic torch, visiting multiple countries and gifting products to foreign leaders, or participating in various entrepreneurial and charity forums, enriches and nurtures the Patriot brand, growing this excellent brand seed. In this process, I believe Feng Jun has undoubtedly experienced richer life insights, as if traveling, meeting all sorts of wise individuals, searching, sharing, and resonating. Isn't this the best form of brand communication? Not absolutely avoiding hard-sell advertising, but rather, this mindset and trend are becoming irreversible in the industry, operating at a level far beyond some insights and skills within the industry.

Let's take a look at Starbucks, Apple, and others. Did they have the kind of advertising we talk about before? The owner of The Body Shop in the UK has talked about environmental protection for decades, and The Body Shop's business has reached worldwide. But how did you know? All experienced brand scientists please return to simplicity and use your right brain to think.

Of course, we must also consider that Patriot's communication method represents an industry trend, and widespread communication will have more influence. Starting from the essence of life and business, the focusing characteristic of information requires Patriot to be cautious and preserve attention and influence growth. It's essential to maintain societal attention acuity and ensure the penetration ability of information in a phase, leading to cumulative influence. When Mr. Feng becomes a social activist, will people overlook Patriot's product information? How can the seemingly free-spirited knight lifestyle combine with high-quality commercial culture? How can Patriot's numerous product information penetrate the hearts of countless fans following Mr. Feng's influence? This is becoming a key issue for Patriot.

Finally, I want to say, if Patriot can do it, how about you? Brands make China more respected, and brands ensure the continuous survival and growth of enterprises. Let's work passionately together to revive the intelligence for Chinese enterprise brand building!
