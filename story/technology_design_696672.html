As a mother and housewife

by muoehtprer on 2012-03-08 09:52:43

This saying has moved me, as a mother and housewife, for a long time and taught me countless lessons; Mom said: Honest people often suffer losses but gain blessings in return; Good deeds bring good rewards, evil deeds bring evil rewards; If you don't do things that hurt your conscience, you won't be afraid of ghosts knocking on your door; You should not have the heart to harm others, but always be cautious. My parents only know that we had conflicts, they don’t know about this matter. If they did, who knows what would happen. You must keep it confidential for me. As for me, the daughter-in-law, I will take care of washing dishes, mopping the floor, and cleaning the house all by myself. We two work together! After marriage, I still continued working. Anyway, I was bored with nothing to do, and going to work allowed me to chat with my colleagues, which made me feel much better. But you kept encouraging me, telling me not to worry about money.

Ha ha, in the blink of an eye, I am already three years old. You should come see me more often; I'll be very excited when you do. Mother, you don't want us children to spend money extravagantly. It's not that we don't know how to save when living our lives, it's that we don't want to endure the pain of "wanting to support our parents but they are no longer around." In your limited years, let us take good care of you! As long as you are healthy, that is the greatest blessing for us as your children.

Later, when the "man" had gradually developed muscles on his arms and a faint stubble above his lips, I bid farewell to home and the sour jujube tree. Every day, my son lived up to his parents' expectations. My father always returned home with a tired body. Regarding the news of Uncle Qiang becoming a vegetative state patient, his eyes were deeply sunken, evidence of his working day and night. When he met acquaintances or friends, he took pride in these shoes.

"Hey, Juan, what's wrong with the 3M lightbox fabric?" I just cried, as if wanting to pour out all the hard work of these few days as grievances. "Speak up, is everything okay at home? Is the baby alright?" "Mom, everything is fine, when will you come back? The baby misses you." Thus, quietly watching youth fade away, tear marks have long cooled. When I looked at my father again, his hair and eyebrows were all dyed white by frost. That year, when digging the Leap Forward River, because the weather suddenly turned cold, my mother only had one quilt, too cold to sleep. A kind aunt from the neighborhood said: Let's sleep together on one bed, two quilts for two people will be warmer. At this moment, my father really seemed like someone who fell into the river, grabbing any grass to save himself.

Mother, when I was young, you went into business, leaving me in the care of an old woman collecting rags, making me sit in the rag cart every day, enduring the wind and frost with the rags.

A rice pillow always rested by your bedside, because that year when you were building houses for others and fell, suffering a compressed fracture of the bone marrow, the doctor suggested surgery, but you refused. The reason was that it meant resting in bed for two months, yet you were still worried about us three sisters studying outside, right? Why are you always so selfless? The doctor said that the consequence of not having surgery might be paralysis. This was the result that both you and I heard. When your daughter pleaded with you to have the surgery, your resolute refusal left all my reasons pale.

Buy some meat! Don't say it's too expensive. In the haze, someone gently stroked my hair. I opened my eyes and saw it was my mother. Thinking about Guo Fu now makes me angry. No matter what, he casually says, “It’s just a matter of spending a little money.”

As an old nurse, Mother clearly knew her condition was beyond saving, yet she paid such close attention to that trumpet vine blooming brilliantly for just one day. I knew she was using her last strength to fight against the illness.
