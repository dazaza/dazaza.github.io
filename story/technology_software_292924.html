Healthy people don't need to wear masks all the time.

by bbeij62s on 2009-12-04 17:04:45

Recently, some media reported that an anti-virus mask which can effectively inhibit the influenza virus has been successfully developed and launched. This special mask has a 92% inhibition rate against the H1N1 influenza virus. In response to this, the city's Center for Disease Control stated yesterday that the National Health Ministry has not yet publicly recommended the use of such masks. The online report claims that this new type of anti-virus flu mask can kill large amounts of harmful bacteria to the human body, especially with a killing rate of over 99% against E. coli, Staphylococcus aureus, and Candida albicans. It has long-term antiviral and antibacterial effects, and does not fail even after being washed.

"Citizens should not listen to one-sided reports," the experts said. Citizens can purchase regular common medical masks. Experts also specifically reminded that wearing a mask cannot completely block the spread of the virus among people. After wearing a mask, citizens must not let their guard down and ignore important disease prevention measures such as frequent hand washing. Currently, it is the high season for H1N1 flu outbreaks, and many citizens have started wearing different styles of masks. How should masks be worn to most effectively isolate the virus? Yesterday, experts from the Municipal Center for Disease Control specially issued a reminder about wearing masks, reminding citizens that there are methods to wearing masks. If masks are not used correctly, they will not only fail to provide protection but may also increase the possibility of virus transmission.

Reminder One: Healthy people don't need to wear masks all the time

Many citizens overly worry about catching seasonal flu and H1N1 flu, and regardless of where they are, they fully arm themselves, never taking off their masks, even in open areas with few people. "Even if it is the commonly recommended regular medical surgical mask, prolonged wearing will more or less cause discomfort, therefore, healthy people do not need to wear masks at all times in daily life," the expert reminded. The purpose of wearing a mask is to prevent contact with droplets containing the H1N1 flu virus. Healthy people only need to wear masks when caring for patients with flu-like symptoms under home quarantine, having close contact with people who have cold symptoms; going to hospitals, stations, and other crowded public places with high infection risks; or traveling to areas severely affected by H1N1 flu.

Reminder Two: Disposable medical masks should be changed every day

Some citizens like to reuse the same mask repeatedly without cleaning it, even when it gets dirty, thinking that cleaning the mask will eliminate its ability to block viruses. Experts suggest that disposable medical masks should be changed daily, and reusable gauze masks should be cleaned daily. The H1N1 flu virus is sensitive to heat, so boiling the gauze mask before washing makes it safer.

Reminder Three: Suggest buying regular medical masks

Recently, a uniquely designed and colorful mask with distinct personalities has gained popularity among citizens. These masks with various patterns usually have only two layers, and their materials vary widely. Experts from the Municipal Center for Disease Control remind that masks with unknown materials and thin structures cannot effectively block droplets. According to relevant requirements from the Ministry of Health, the material of flu prevention masks must be medical cotton fabric, strictly disinfected, and come with individual packaging. "Clearly, these personalized masks lack disinfection marks and independent packaging, especially the loose personalized masks sold in public places, which are prone to secondary contamination." Experts say that some masks with strange designs cannot fully cover the mouth and nose, thus failing to provide protection. They suggest citizens buy regular medical surgical masks from reputable pharmacies instead of blindly pursuing fashion.
