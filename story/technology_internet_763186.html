Apple's total revenue in the third quarter was 35.3 billion US dollars, and its net profit decreased by 22% compared with the same period of the previous year.

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 17:01:43

On July 24, according to foreign media reports, Apple released its financial report for the third quarter of fiscal year 2013 ended June 29. The report showed that Apple's net profit in the third quarter was $6.9 billion, a year-on-year decrease of 22%; total revenue in the third quarter was $35.3 billion, slightly higher than $35 billion in the same period last year.

Performance in the third quarter of 2013:

In the third quarter, Apple's net profit was $6.9 billion, down 22% from $8.8 billion in the same period last year; diluted earnings per share were $7.47, lower than $9.32 in the same period last year; total revenue reached $35.3 billion, slightly higher than $35 billion in the same period last year, but decreased by 19% compared to $43.6 billion in the previous quarter; operating profit in the third quarter was $9.2 billion, lower than $11.6 billion in the same period last year; gross margin was 36.9%, compared to 42.8% in the same period last year; international market revenue outside the U.S. accounted for about 57% of the company's total quarterly revenue.

By product category, in the third quarter, iPhone sales reached 31.2 million units, exceeding 26 million units in the same period last year, marking the highest third-quarter iPhone sales in Apple's history; iPad sales reached 14.6 million units, lower than 17 million units in the same period last year; Mac sales were 3.8 million units, lower than 4 million units in the same period last year; iPod sales were 4.569 million units, down 32% year-on-year, compared to 6.751 million units in the same period last year; iTunes, software, and service total revenue was $3.99 billion, up 25% year-on-year, compared to $3.203 billion in the same period last year; accessory business total revenue was $1.179 billion, down 4% year-on-year, compared to $1.227 billion in the same period last year.

By geographic region, in the third quarter, Apple's revenue from the Americas region reached $14.4 billion, up 12% year-on-year from $12.8 billion in the same period last year, up 3% quarter-on-quarter from $14 billion in the previous quarter; revenue from the Europe region reached $7.6 billion, down 8% year-on-year from $8.2 billion in the same period last year, down 22% quarter-on-quarter from $9.8 billion in the previous quarter; revenue from Greater China was $4.6 billion, down 14% year-on-year from $5.4 billion in the same period last year, down 43% quarter-on-quarter from $8.2 billion in the previous quarter; revenue from the Japanese market was $2.54 billion, up 27% year-on-year from $2 billion in the same period last year, down 19% quarter-on-quarter from $3.1 billion in the previous quarter; revenue from other regions in Asia-Pacific was $2.05 billion, down 18% year-on-year from $2.5 billion in the same period last year, down 35% quarter-on-quarter from $3.2 billion in the previous quarter.

Financial Position:

As of June 29, 2013, Apple's cash and cash equivalents totaled $112.5 billion, compared to $107.5 billion in the same period of the previous fiscal year; holdings of short-term convertible bonds amounted to $31.4 billion, compared to $18.4 billion in the same period of the previous fiscal year; total assets held amounted to $199.956 billion, compared to $176.1 billion in the same period of the previous fiscal year.

Apple's board also announced a cash dividend plan, with dividends of $3.05 per common share, which will be paid out by August 15, 2013, mainly distributed to shareholders on record before the stock market closes on August 12, 2013.

In addition, Peter Oppenheimer, Apple's CFO, said: "Apple generated $7.8 billion in cash flow from operations in the third quarter, and we are pleased to return $18.8 billion in cash to shareholders through dividends and stock repurchases."

Outlook for the fourth quarter of 2013:

Apple expects that in the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2013, total revenue will range between $34 billion and $37 billion, slightly lower than the average estimate of $37.1 billion by market analysts; gross margin will range between 36% and 37%; operating expenses will range between $3.9 billion and $3.95 billion; other profits will be $200 million; tax rate will be 26.5%.
