Amazon's internal job level exposed: only Bezos at level 12

by anonymous on 2013-10-15 21:39:45

On the evening of October 15th, Beijing time, Bloomberg Businessweek senior reporter Brad Stone's new book, "The Everything Store: Jeff Bezos and the Age of Amazon," will be published this month. Stone said that getting promoted at Amazon is very difficult, and there are several reasons for this.

On one hand, Amazon CEO Jeff Bezos believes that company managers should uphold high standards when recruiting and promoting employees; only outstanding geniuses can develop within Amazon. The internal evaluation process at Amazon is called "OLR (Organizational and Leadership Review)."

OLR is a series of meetings held twice a year where senior leaders from various departments of Amazon gather to discuss the strengths and weaknesses of their subordinates, approve promotion candidates, and sometimes identify the poorest performers for dismissal. Discussions in these meetings are intense, and Amazon promotes only a very limited number of employees each year. Through this method, Bezos hopes to avoid political struggles and unnecessary promotions within the company. However, some employees believe that OLR precisely brings about the political struggles that this mechanism aims to prevent.

On the other hand, it is difficult to get promoted at Amazon due to its flat corporate structure, which is divided into only ten levels within the company. Here’s how the different levels break down:

Level 2 and Level 3 employees are primarily manual laborers working in logistics centers, earning hourly wages. It seems that Amazon does not have any Level 1 employees. Level 4 employees are mainly newly hired regular staff, possibly recent graduates with bachelor's degrees. Project managers or product managers are at Level 5. Senior product managers are at Level 6; these lower-level managers are responsible for a specific project or function. Level 7 employees are likely to hold graduate degrees and serve as senior managers leading multiple Level 5 or Level 6 employees. Starting from this level, Amazon employees usually spend some time every few years participating in customer service call center or logistics center work to understand the tasks of lower-level employees. Level 8 employees are various directors, vice presidents are at Level 10, and senior vice presidents are at Level 11. These employees are members of one of Amazon's leadership teams, such as the recently formed "D Team" responsible for digital business. Level 12 has only one person—Bezos himself.

Interestingly, there is no mention of Level 9 employees, and Amazon employees are unclear about the reason. Some employees think it might be a historical oversight. Others speculate that it could be a symbolic gesture. Bezos and Amazon's early executives may not want a rigid corporate structure, so they deliberately skipped a level throughout the grading system to reduce bureaucracy within the company. (Zhang Fan)
