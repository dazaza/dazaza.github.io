According to the different causes and courses of thyroiditis, it is generally divided into three categories: acute, subacute, and chronic thyroiditis.

● Subacute thyroiditis is also known as giant cell thyroiditis, granulomatous thyroiditis, and De Quervain thyroiditis. The cause is unknown, but it is generally believed that the onset may be related to viral infection.

● Chronic lymphocytic thyroiditis is also called Hashimoto's disease or chronic autoimmune thyroiditis, which is an autoimmune disease. TG-Ab, TPO-Ab, and TSH receptor antibodies exist in the patient's serum. It can occur at any age, but is more common in middle-aged and elderly women.

● The onset is insidious with slow progression, often accidentally discovering thyroid enlargement or nodules, accompanied by throat discomfort, mild swallowing difficulty, and a feeling of neck compression. There are also cases diagnosed due to symptoms of hypothyroidism.

● Thyroid enlargement is usually bilateral and diffuse, with an uneven surface, lobes, or nodules, and has a relatively hard texture. Generally, there is no tenderness, although there are also cases with no obvious swelling or even shrinkage.

● In the initial stage, thyroid function is normal or accompanied by symptoms of hyperthyroidism. In the later stage, thyroid function decreases, leading to myxedema.

● It can also be accompanied by other autoimmune diseases such as pernicious anemia and connective tissue diseases.
