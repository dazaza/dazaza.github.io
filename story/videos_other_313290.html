Inter Milan Suddenly Reaches Lightning Transfer Agreement! Toni to Join in Winter with Half the Salary

Sina Sports News - Not long after rumors emerged that Inter Milan intended to invite Italian striker Luca Toni, currently playing for Bayern Munich in the Bundesliga, back to Serie A, this Wednesday "Football Market" broke the news exclusively that Toni has already reached an agreement with Inter Milan. There is a possibility that he will join Inter Milan in January, and his requested salary will be around 2.5 million euros. "Football Market" stated that it is still unclear whether Toni will join Inter Milan on loan or as a permanent transfer, and the economic terms agreed upon between Inter Milan and Bayern Munich regarding this transfer are also unknown.

It is reported that there are still some economic treatment-related details between Toni and Inter Milan that need to be confirmed. It is rumored that Toni's agent will come to Milan for negotiations next Monday. Currently, Toni's net annual salary at Bayern Munich is approximately 4.8 million euros. If the above rumors are confirmed, it would mean that Toni will return to his homeland after many years. Now, due to his continuous ideological differences with team coach Van Gaal, Toni has gradually been marginalized and lost his starting position.

In fact, even before the start of the 2006 World Cup, Inter Milan had already shown interest in inviting then highly sought-after striker Luca Toni from Fiorentina to join them. Although not officially confirmed, it was widely believed that Inter Milan offered Fiorentina a very tempting bid of 25 million euros at the time. However, despite facing the threat of relegation due to football scandals, Fiorentina, who was enjoying great success in Serie A, felt that 25 million euros did not match the status of Toni, who had become the best scorer in Europe. Subsequently, under the strong stance of club president Della Valle, Fiorentina held onto Toni for one more year before letting him go. In 2007, Toni transferred from Fiorentina to Bayern Munich in the Bundesliga, with an annual salary of 6 million euros, but his transfer fee was much lower than the rumored price offered by Inter Milan a year earlier, only 11 million euros. At Bayern Munich, Toni experienced glory, but now he has completely taken a downturn.

Now, once again, Toni has set his sights on Serie A, hoping to restart his career at any cost. Due to his differences with Bayern coach Van Gaal, he has gradually been squeezed out of the starting lineup and finds it difficult to continue his journey in the Bundesliga. For Toni, returning to Serie A is not just a last resort; several clubs, including Napoli, Roma, and even Inter Milan, are eager to sign him. With the upcoming Africa Cup of Nations, Inter Milan's forward Eto'o will be absent from club matches in January, prompting the Nerazzurri to seek a capable replacement to fill Eto'o's position. However, according to "All Market," Toni is not a good choice. "All Market" believes that unless Inter Milan cannot obtain any players more likely to strengthen the team, they should not sign Toni. Given his current age and form, it will be challenging for him to compete with Milito or Balotelli for the starting position, and he might even become a burden to the team immediately upon joining. "All Market" believes that Toni and Inter Milan should have joined forces in the summer of 2006, and now, things have changed too much, making it too late to rekindle their past connection.

However, during an interview with Sky Television, FIFA-registered agent Fabio Parisi expressed optimism about Luca Toni's potential move when commenting on Inter Milan's upcoming transfer actions in January. He said: “The rumor of Cavani joining is undoubtedly the most attractive, but since this involves a player still under contract, I doubt Zapallini would be willing to sell him. Additionally, the Uruguayan’s price might be high, and I don’t think Palermo would let him go. As for the possibilities of Pandev and Toni joining, the situation differs from Cavani's case. Since these two players are already marginal figures in their respective teams, it makes it easier for Inter Milan to acquire them. Regarding Pandev, we must wait for the arbitration committee's decision, and I am unsure if there will be enough time to complete the deal by January. Once the Macedonian successfully terminates his contract with Lazio, he will become a target for most European teams, not just Italian clubs.”

Therefore, according to Fabio Parisi, Inter Milan's easiest option is to sign Toni. Parisi said: “Toni has completely fallen out with Van Gaal now. To preserve his position in the national team and retain the world champion title he earned in Germany, he wants to leave Bayern and find a new club. I believe he can secure free transfer rights at a low cost. I think Inter Milan could offer him appropriate conditions by extending the contract term. Roma is also looking for a forward, and someone like Toni should be very appealing. However, unless they sign him for free, his salary is still quite high compared to their club standards.”
