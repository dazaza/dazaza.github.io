At 7:20 PM last night, a fire broke out on the 12th floor of Building 6, Anyuan, Anhui North Community, Chaoyang District. Eighteen fire trucks rushed to the scene for firefighting. The apartment on fire was a rental unit where a three-bedroom-one-living-room apartment had been illegally divided into five smaller rooms by a real estate agency and rented out to five different families, housing a total of nine people. Fortunately, no casualties were reported in the incident. It is suspected that the fire was caused by high-power electrical appliances igniting the wiring.

The residential building became a "waterfall cave"

Building No. 6 in the Anyuan community of Anhui North consists of 24 floors, with the fire starting in Room 1201. At 8 PM last night, more than ten fire trucks were still spraying water onto the fire site. The entire unit of the residential complex turned into a "waterfall cave." Property management staff were clearing wastewater using brooms and large buckets, while a pungent smell of smoke lingered at the scene.

The windows of Room 1201 were shattered, and the walls were blackened. Items inside the room were charred beyond recognition, and the structure of the house was indistinguishable. Several pairs of shoes salvaged from the debris were placed outside the door. The police were evacuating the crowd, sealing off the area, and conducting an investigation.

As the burning building was adjacent to a road, fire vehicles were parked on the main road. As of 9 PM last night, Datun Road was under temporary traffic control, causing congestion in both directions.

Firefighting efforts involved the use of aerial ladders

According to reports, the burning apartment was a rental unit, with five rooms housing a total of nine people. A young couple living in one of the rooms recounted that around 7:20 PM last night, after returning home from work, they smelled smoke coming from a neighboring room. Initially dismissing it, they soon noticed black smoke emanating. "We realized there was a fire, but we didn't have the key to enter, so we chose to escape. There was thick smoke and flames shooting out accompanied by crackling sounds, so I called the emergency services," they said.

After the incident, eighteen fire trucks arrived at the scene. Firefighters used aerial ladders to break through the windows and enter the fire scene to extinguish the flames. About half an hour later, the fire was brought under control. No casualties were reported in the accident.

The unregulated actions of the real estate agency almost caused a major disaster

At 9:30 PM last night, the fire department gathered the nine tenants, the landlord, the real estate agency, property management, and street office personnel to understand the situation.

According to the landlord, two years ago, the house was entrusted to an unknown small real estate agency for rental purposes. The original three-bedroom-one-living-room apartment, totaling 139 square meters, was somehow partitioned into five rooms by the agency, accommodating nine people. "It is suspected that the use of high-power electrical appliances ignited the wires, requiring further investigation by the fire department."

Last night, the gas company conducted an inspection and ruled out the possibility of a gas leak causing the fire. Currently, the fire department is investigating the specific losses and cause of the fire. According to the property management, the 13th floor of the building where the incident occurred is a mechanical equipment floor, which was unoccupied; otherwise, the water used for firefighting would have caused even greater damage.
