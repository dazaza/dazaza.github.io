CN domain name registration goes global, but reputation effect hard to save domestic market

by yyy852456 on 2012-02-18 14:47:57

Admin5 February 16 news, not long ago Singapore domain name registration company IP Mirror said that individuals and organizations from the UK, Hong Kong, Singapore, Malaysia and Australia will be able to directly register China domains, using .CN, .中国 (China), .公司 (Company) and .网络 (Network), without having to pay for a local presence certification fee. According to CNNIC's approval process, these domains will then be released from the server-hold status, allowing them to be resolved to websites and enable email services. This means that foreign entities from these regions registering domains will also need to go through CNNIC's approval, and during use, must comply with CNNIC's policies for managing website content. If any violations are found, the domain may be banned by CNNIC.

At the end of 2009, relevant national departments targeted illegal and inappropriate internet content, leading to a major cleanup of the domestic internet environment. A batch of websites with issues such as illegal content and incomplete filing information were sealed in this cleanup. The severe one-size-fits-all policy also affected many legitimate websites to varying degrees. Subsequently, CNNIC tightened the CN domain registration policy, stipulating that starting at 9:00 on December 14, 2009, personal user domain registration would stop, also closing the channel for overseas CN domain registration. This became a turning point for CN domains, with the total number of CN domains falling from tens of millions at its peak to around three million now. In just two years, CN domains plummeted from heaven to hell, making it difficult to recover after the sharp decline.

This time, the re-opening of overseas CN domain registration has led many industry insiders to speculate that it could inject a strong stimulus into the long-depressed CN domain market, potentially sparking a new wave of CN domain registration fever. Admin5 editors believe that CNNIC opening up overseas CN domain registration can be considered a rather meaningless "self-redemption." Promoting CN domains internationally, for CN domains, is more symbolic than practical; domain registration, reputation effect outweighs market effect, and while it might enhance the image of CN domains in the long term, it is unlikely to bring about substantial numerical growth. The reasons are mainly as follows:

First, we all know that the development of CN domains started around 2006 when the domestic internet recovered from the bubble. The development of the internet economy spurred the emergence of a new batch of websites, with both individual webmasters and large websites gaining some development. In 2007, CN domains launched a "one yuan registration" campaign, drastically lowering the threshold for CN domains. This populist policy promoted the rapid popularization of CN domains, making them the first choice for webmasters to build their sites. As a result, CN domain registrations exploded in a short period, becoming the most used country-level top-level domain globally, rivaling COM domains. However, the explosive growth of CN domains also brought certain problems due to the special environment of the domestic internet and the wild growth of webmasters, resulting in various illegal and infringing behaviors, with pornographic and spam websites rampant. The subsequent one-size-fits-all cleanup policy led to the demise of many CN sites and the implementation of a new CN domain policy prohibiting personal registration. Webmasters lost confidence in CN domains, even though the terminal value for enterprises increased instead of decreasing, but it was difficult to drive overall growth in CN domains again. Even with the opening of overseas registration, there may be some growth, but trying to make amends for the astronomical drop over the past two years is unrealistic.

Second, for foreign institutions, the application significance and value of CN domains are limited. Although this time the regions opened are places where there are many Chinese people, with numerous Chinese enterprises and Chinese-language websites. Opening CN domain registration in these areas can indeed drive some growth, but in terms of quantity, it is unlikely to form a scale. On one hand, CN domains belong to country-level top-level domains, and their application scope generally focuses domestically. Even if there are a large number of Chinese enterprises and websites abroad, to take root locally, their own country-level top-level domain and COM domains are still the preferred choice. Unless foreign organizations enter the domestic market and launch websites, CN domains cannot demonstrate their own value. The true meaning of country-level top-level domains lies in domestic widespread application. With the domestic market in disarray, seeking breakthroughs in overseas Chinese regions is akin to avoiding the heavy and taking the light, failing to find the real solution to the problem. Going global may give CN domains some reputation and image promotion, but the debt accumulated over the past two years is not something that can be repaid easily.

Third, from the perspective of the open policy, overseas CN domain registration requires compliance with CNNIC's policies for managing website content during use. If any violations are discovered, the domain may be banned by CNNIC. The website and domain management policies abroad differ greatly from those in China, with almost no common ground. Foreign institutions registering CN domains to build websites will certainly adapt to local website and domain policies. If the website does not violate local policies but conflicts with CNNIC's management policies, it will inevitably be banned for this reason. From previous CN domain cleanups, it is unlikely that CNNIC will be able to formulate management methods suitable for overseas website content and local internet policies and environments in the short term. Governing overseas website domains with domestic policies will inevitably lead to conflicts, and various troubles and problems will definitely arise.
