New York Times Square China Screen Declassified: $10 Million Contract Signed in 5 Years

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 09:27:05

Xinhua News Agency takes "leading role" in media and city image ads, long-term contract amount may reach tens of millions of dollars

By Zhou Jia, Li Xiuzhong, Wang Weijia

At just before 6 a.m. on Monday morning, as the first rays of sunlight spread across New York, a large liquid crystal "China Screen" located in Times Square also lit up.

This approximately 240 square meter large advertisement screen is located on Building No. 2 of the square. It's the first time that a Chinese company has rented a large outdoor liquid crystal display screen for a long term in this prime location known as the "Crossroads of the World."

The screen broadcasts promotional videos from Xinhua News Agency and its subordinate institutions, as well as image advertisements for the cities of Shanghai and Chengdu.

Brian Turner, President of Sherwood Outdoor (the tenant of the ad screen), told the reporter from The First Financial Daily that this screen was originally used by HSBC.

Turner indicated that after the ten-year lease contract with HSBC expired, they did not plan to renew it. Thus, they contacted Xinhua News Agency and signed a long-term contract several months ago.

The advertisement screen is rented by Xinhua Image Gallery (Beijing) Culture Communication Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as "Xinhua Image Gallery"), a wholly-owned subsidiary of Xinhua News Agency.

Although Turner was unwilling to disclose the amount of the contract, industry insiders told our reporter that the contract amount for such a prime advertising position generally reaches the level of millions or even tens of millions of dollars.

New York Outdoor Advertising Economy

This liquid crystal display screen, which is about 19 meters high and 12 meters wide, is located at the upper part of the entire giant advertising pillar, directly above the advertising screens of South Korea’s Samsung Electronics, America’s Coca-Cola Company, and South Korea’s Hyundai Group.

Our reporter observed on-site that the display screen was playing a short video introducing Xinhua News Agency. In the title bar, the Chinese and English names of Xinhua News Agency appeared repeatedly in blue letters on a white background. Meanwhile, images of Beijing, Shanghai, and other places were shown below. Finally, the logo of Xinhua News Agency appeared.

The advertisement screen also broadcast promotional videos for Xinhua News Agency and its subordinates, including Xinhua Net (CEC), “Xinhua 08” financial information service platform (CFC), China Xinhua News Television Network (CNC), and China International Culture Image Company (CIC).

In fact, corresponding to this "China Screen" nearby is the Reuters building.

Regarding Xinhua News Agency's major move, Reuters even made a special report, considering it "possibly the most conspicuous step in (Xinhua's) recent expansion in the United States."

It is understood that since 1971, Xinhua News Agency has set up an office in New York, with its headquarters located in Queens, where rents are relatively cheaper. In May this year, Xinhua's headquarters moved to the expensive Times Square area in Manhattan.

Due to New York's unique commercial geographical location, an extremely valuable outdoor advertising industry has formed here.

Turner told our reporter that the parent company of Sherwood Outdoor is Sherwood Equities. The parent company owns three buildings and 25 outdoor advertising boards in Times Square, equivalent to one-sixth of all the outdoor advertising spaces around the square.

Turner said that 95% of their advertisements are from long-term clients who have contracts lasting more than five years, including world-renowned brands like Coca-Cola and Samsung.

In fact, Coca-Cola's advertisement has been "settled" here since 1932.

"The business is very healthy. Our advertising spaces have been 100% sold out this year, and there will only be a few empty spots next year." Turner told our reporter, "Our business is composed of long-term contracts, which is a very stable business model."

Xinhua Image Gallery makes its appearance

Turner did not reveal the details of the "China Screen" lease agreement.

Even when interviewed by The New York Times, Turner and executives from Sherwood Capital still did not introduce the details of the commercial terms.

However, The New York Times reported that the monthly rent for similar-positioned advertisement screens could be as high as $300,000 to $400,000.

Therefore, if the lease contract for this "China Screen" lasts for 5 years, the amount would be counted in tens of millions of dollars.

Rick Willard, President of Rule 7 Media LLC, an electronic advertising information company, told our reporter that there are no more than five main outdoor advertising companies occupying the best geographical locations near the square.

According to his introduction, these companies' business models are largely similar: they are private enterprises owning these real estates, thus they can profit by renting out outdoor spaces.

"These advertising companies are all small in scale, usually just a small company with a few people. Advertising contracts generally last from 10 to 20 years," Willard told our reporter. "And different from the past, they now mainly directly contact companies to conduct advertising business."

Turner told our reporter that their clients come from various fields globally, but Xinhua News Agency is the first media giant among them. They started contacting Chinese institutions and companies several years ago, and Xinhua News Agency was the first to take the opportunity.

Our reporter found through industrial and commercial records that as the tenant of the advertisement screen, Xinhua Image Gallery was just registered on December 16, 2010. Its business scope involves cultural promotion activities such as literary creation, photography, videography, and printing services. At the same time, its business scope also includes import and export of goods, technology, and agency.

The registered capital of this company is 1 million yuan.

Public data shows that Xinhua Image Gallery is registered in Room 1719, No. 57 Xicheng District, Xuanwu Gate West Street, Beijing. The legal representative is Wang Yao.

No. 57 Xuanwu Gate West Street is precisely the location of Xinhua News Agency.

An informed source revealed that Wang Yao is currently the Deputy Editor-in-Chief and Director of the Photography Department of Xinhua News Agency.

Our reporter learned yesterday through the general number of Xinhua News Agency that "Room 1719" is registered as a "picture operation company" there.

Zhang Xiaojun, General Manager of Xinhua Image Gallery, stated in an interview with Xinhua News Agency that leasing the display screen in Times Square is a measure for brand promotion and image display by Xinhua Image Gallery.

It is worth noting that the image advertisements and commercial advertisements of various regions and enterprises in China are also organized by this company.

Interaction between cities and enterprises

The Chengdu city image promotional video that has landed in Times Square includes elements such as pandas and Du Fu Thatched Cottage.

"At the end of June this year, after we learned that Xinhua News Agency had obtained this resource, we contacted the headquarters through the Sichuan branch of Xinhua News Agency and expressed our intention to play the Chengdu city image video on it," Huang Lin, Director of the News Liaison Office of the Chengdu Municipal Committee Foreign Propaganda Office, told our reporter.

"By mid-July, we had reached an agreement," Huang Lin said, adding that the fee paid by Chengdu was very low, "much less than when the national image video was played."

Currently, Chengdu has rented two time slots for four months, i.e., from August 1 to September 30, and from December 1 to January 31 of the following year, rolling 20 times a day, each time for 30 seconds.

The consideration for choosing this time slot is that August and September are during the European tourism season, giving more opportunities for audiences from various countries to see it. The end of the year is also around Christmas and the Spring Festival, which have relatively higher viewing rates.

It is worth noting that among the enterprise image videos mentioned by Zhang Xiaojun, Wuliangye is included.

It is understood that Xinhua News Agency has selected a batch of enterprises domestically that can represent China's cultural and economic achievements for display, and Wuliangye has obtained the first entry ticket to Times Square in New York.

Tang Bochao, Director of the Office of Wuliangye Group, confirmed to our reporter that Xinhua News Agency hopes to promote some key domestic enterprises, so they approached Wuliangye, but he is unclear whether a fee was paid to Xinhua News Agency.

According to him, Xinhua News Agency has rented this display screen for 5 years.

According to official statistics, Times Square in New York attracts about 40 million tourists from all over the world every year.

Our reporter encountered a couple from Virginia and their daughter taking photos as souvenirs in the square.

They said they carefully watched the entire advertisement of Xinhua News Agency.

"At first, I only saw the words 'Xinhua' and images from various places in China, and I thought it was a tourism promotion for China. It wasn't until the end that I realized it was an advertisement promoting China's news agency," they said.

Ava Seave, visiting associate professor at Columbia Business School, told our reporter that purely from a business perspective, Xinhua News Agency advertising in Times Square might have two intentions: to establish the image of a mainstream media outlet to attract more advertisements, and to attempt to expand its footprint in English-language news reporting.

At the beginning of this year, China's national image video was already aired in Times Square in New York.
