11 little-known new ways to make money online

by yinyin on 2008-11-05 11:44:20

Social sites and web applications are becoming more and more popular rapidly. Young entrepreneurs or entrepreneurs make a lot of money by connecting people with simple ideas. It's no secret that great ideas transform the web. Are you interested in becoming one of them? The dream is simple: With careful planning and some free time, someone like you can create a smart idea, add a twist, and get rich. But where are these great ideas? Why do we always see clutter instead of great new ideas? Where is the sapphire in the rust?

Actually, they're right under your feet. You just need to look a little more closely. Here are 11 unpublished ideas to learn today and be rich tomorrow. Doin 'it! The alarm has gone off.

1. Inverted work board

Mini Market: "Buy and sell jobs online."

Specific meaning: Do you know the work board on the network? Employers don't advertise for jobs, job seekers advertise for applications.

Trading principle: Designers, developers, writers, and anyone who needs a job can ask for a job, and how much commission to give the person who connects.

Why it works: More and more people are working online, and sometimes jobs are hard to find and time-consuming. Wouldn't it be nice if someone else could help you find a job? Especially if you happen to know someone who needs a website or logo design, and you happen to know someone who is looking for a design job.

Source of income: transaction commissions, or monthly fees for demand information or contact information.

2. Community consultation

Mini Market: "Yahoo is responsible for webmasters."

Specific meaning: a website where webmasters pay for advice and get opinions from experts from all walks of life.

How it works: The webmaster pays gold (or whatever it is) and then publishes an article with a screenshot of his website and the goal he hopes to achieve. Community counselors offer advice and collect gold coins. Gold coins can be cashed or used to ask questions.

Why it works: Ideas and suggestions are important to improve your website. This approach allows you to avoid paying someone a huge consultation fee, and you only need to pay the team a small fee. It will also increase your value in the long run.

Source of income: Stationmaster buys gold coins. For example, a community advisory service to a submission site costs 25 gold coins, you can collect 5 gold coins, and the remaining 20 gold coins are paid to those who provide the advisory service.

3. Internet debate

Mini Market: "Debate Wikipedia."

What it means: Debates fill every forum and website. They have little control and lack structure, depth, certainty, etc. A well-managed debate site is bound to have a market. The most recent site I've seen is Squidoo's Hey Monkey Brain, but I think there's room for more.

Trading principle: Propose a debate topic, choose the pros or cons, state your reasons, and then encourage others to debate.

Why it works: Everyone likes to express their opinion, your job is to let them express their opinion and control the process of debate, and then you can make some money.

Source of revenue: People may pay a small fee to argue, but your source of revenue should mainly be advertising affiliates or other matching ads.

4. Network Butler service

Mini Market: "Your web steward."

What it means: Have you ever thought about shopping online, but don't want to spend too much time searching or filling out long forms for airline tickets and movie tickets?

How the transaction works: You request, for example, "Two tickets to the Die Hard movie at 8 p.m.," via email, SMS, or voice to your web steward. Your web manager faithfully follows your instructions. There are already automated services for this sort of thing, but an effective human butler service will set you apart.

Why it's possible: Outsourcing and personal virtual secretaries have become hot topics at present. Leading online butler services will have a large market. There are a lot of busy people in the world. You can have one standard of service for people who have a lot of money and another for people who don't have a lot of money.

Source of income: A monthly fee or a monthly fee.

5. Live auction website

Mini Market: "Buy it now."

What it means: Online auction sites are great, but rarely do they do as well as live auctions. I think the live auction site still needs to be improved.

Trading principle: For example, to sell an iPhone, you can only bid on the top bid first, and the bid is only valid for a few minutes. Bids are live and secured, and when the top bid is auctioned, the next bid is auctioned.

Why it works: As a salesperson, it's getting harder and harder to list, resonate, and sell quickly, and a simple live Internet auction site will solve these problems.

Source of income: Only receiving commissions for successful trades. You must find a way to ensure that all auctions are genuine. You may require users to put in some money before bidding, or you may use other methods.

6. Skill trading

Mini-market: "Exchange skills, not money."

What it means: a network center for exchanging skills with others. There is no need to pay for services, you simply need to exchange your skills.

Trading Principles: What needs to be done? Post a "Help" AD with your skills and examples of how your skills can be used.

Why it works: There are thousands of technically talented designers, developers, writers, illustrators, musicians, and more on the Internet. But there isn't a good way to get in touch. By mediating arguments and ensuring that transactions are fair and guaranteed, a skills exchange site can become a big business.

Source of income: A monthly fee, or a fee per exchange, or a fee for contact information.

7. Bestsellers, DVDS, games and CDS for sale

Mini Market: "Quick sale of anything with a barcode."

What it means: Do you have any books, games, DVDS, or CDS that you will never use again? It's a bit shy, isn't it? A web page or a piece of software can scan barcodes through a web camera to quickly list the catalog, saving time and making a lot of money.

Trading principle: Log in, scan your books and price. Job done! This can operate as a website, or it can be a standalone application, such as linking to Amazon Marketplace.

Why it works: Millions of people have tons of useless books, games, and other stuff. But they are lazy to sort these things out! This is your chance.

Source of income: Earn a commission from book sales, or charge a flat fee for software if you go standalone.

8. Read the website later

Mini Market: "Internet reading list."

Specific meaning: an online community reading list. RSS browsers are great, but not social enough for some people to be comfortable with. What we need is something everyone can use and understand - a web reading list.

A website that aggregates reading, archiving, and sharing never expires, especially for people who don't understand technology.

Trading Principles: Have you ever seen a good article, but don't have time to read it? You can add it to your favorites, but you might soon forget about it. Wouldn't it be better to mark the page "Read later" and put it on an online reading list?

Then, you can set aside an hour each week to read all the articles on the list, categorize the articles, and see which ones you read the most.

Why it works: RSS browsers are hot, but socially shared links are even hotter. It would be nice to combine the two, and there's a Firefox add-on that does that, but it needs to be expanded.

Revenue source: Make money through advertising, advertising is a good basic business model.

9. Real estate Price Index

Mini Market: "How much is your house worth now"?

What it means: A live house price website needs to have a powerful data model behind it, so that the house prices are displayed on a map. To some extent, it's already been done, and the market is crowded. But if you can provide the right price on the site of the house, you will make a fortune.

Trading principles: Data models can be very complex. You need to rely on a social factor to adjust your prices and build a reliable valuation system based on previous rates, local trends, market influences, and customer views.

Why it works: Every landlord wants to know how much his or her home is worth. For large real estate investors, it is important to track and know when prices are rising. In addition, the labor market index may be useful for sales and valuation by real estate agents.

Sources of revenue: The possibilities for making money from this idea are endless, including commissions on home sales, affiliate sales, advertising for real estate agents and real estate investors, community advertising, etc.

10. Real-time public billboards

Mini Market: "Your ads can be placed anywhere in no time."

What it means: A network of billboards, newsstand projectors linked to a website that enables ordinary people like you to place ads in public.

Transaction principle: You register and upload your AD, choose a place, and then you can see live web photography of your AD in any public place in the world.

Why it works: The cost of booth advertising is high for many small businesses and self-employed people, and a public, global, mini billboard for ordinary people and small businesses could make millions with just a mouse click to update it. If you succeed, Google will work with you.

Revenue source: Advertising at a fixed price or at a monthly cost. You can divide the electronic billboard/projector into many smaller units, or sell the entire space or network to the same advertiser.

11. Website for sale

Mini market: "A place to buy and sell websites."

What it means: If you had a blog to sell, where would you sell it? There is no dominant player in the market for selling websites. What is preventing you from developing this market?

Trading principle: Based on a fixed price or auction minimum price, list the characteristics of the website you want to sell, including screen shots, traffic, PageRank value, revenue status, and then wait for buyers to come to the door!

Why it works: Selling a domain name is a big deal. But it is still more profitable to sell the site at a reasonable price, or to buy the web property through a trusted means.

Source of revenue: a commission is charged for the successful sale of the website, or a fee is charged for each service.
