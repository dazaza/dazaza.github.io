Suspect who killed a mother and her child in cold blood smirked during the trial, said that he had attempted suicide and was seeking the death penalty

by dahslkh6 on 2012-03-03 20:41:27

The suspect who brutally murdered a mother and her son was seen smirking during his trial, stating that he had long wished for death and requested the death penalty. The 20-year-old laborer Ma was charged with killing Ms. Yin and her 2-year-old son because of dissatisfaction over being asked to help take care of the child. During the trial at the Beijing No. 2 Intermediate People's Court, the victims' family held up their portraits in extreme grief, while Ma showed no remorse and even smiled. He claimed he had long wanted to die and only hoped the court would quickly sentence him to death.

A trivial argument led to the brutal attack. At 9:50 AM yesterday, Ma was brought into the courtroom, and the victim’s family immediately took out pictures of the deceased mother and son, showing strong emotions. However, Ma seemed completely unconcerned, sitting slouched on the chair, shaking his head and smirking occasionally. The prosecutor and judge reminded him several times to sit properly.

Ma, born in 1991, is from Heilongjiang Province. Before the incident, he worked as a laborer in a trading company in Chaoyang District. The victim, Ms. Yin, was the company's accountant and the boss's daughter-in-law. Ma said there were no previous conflicts between them. On May 22nd last year, which was a Sunday, he originally planned to sleep in but was woken up by the boss at around 6 AM to unload goods.

"I usually don't get so upset, but I don't know why, I was particularly angry that day," Ma said. Around 8 AM, he went to the company kitchen and put about twenty to thirty sleeping pills into a pot of porridge. "I thought everyone would want to sleep after eating it and wouldn't have to work." About an hour later, the boss and several employees began experiencing dizziness and nausea and sought medical attention.

Ma said that in the evening when he wanted to sleep, Ms. Yin, who was washing clothes, asked him to fix the light and also to look after her child. "I got really mad and said I didn't want to babysit. She replied, 'You're just a worker; if I tell you to do it, you should.' " Their argument escalated into a physical altercation. "I picked up an axe from the corner and hit her on the head, thinking that would be enough to teach her a lesson," Ma said. Unexpectedly, Ms. Yin hugged her child and shouted, "Murder! Help!" The child was also crying. "I panicked then and struck her repeatedly."

After killing them, Ma learned how to drive and fled in a high-speed car chase. Ma said that in the darkness, he closed his eyes and slashed approximately twenty times until both mother and child fell silent, breaking the axe in the process. Afraid of being seen by colleagues, he dragged Ms. Yin to another courtyard, during which he heard her repeatedly saying, "Why?" "I told her I didn't know why, my legs were trembling, my whole life was ruined," Ma said. He pulled Ms. Yin under a truck intending to run her over but abandoned the attempt because he didn't know how to drive. He then returned to the dormitory and threw Ms. Yin's son into a warehouse. After changing his clothes, he stole more than 3,000 yuan, phones, bank cards, and other belongings from the office and Ms. Yin's room. Subsequently, he drove off in the boss's Buick car.

Ma admitted, "I didn't know how to drive before; I learned on the spot." He kept driving towards his hometown and engaged in a high-speed chase on the highway. "I didn't fully understand what I was doing and crashed into the barrier." He then abandoned the Buick and took a taxi for over 2,000 yuan to Wuchang City in Heilongjiang Province. He planned to visit relatives and commit suicide but was arrested by the police the next afternoon.

During the trial, Ms. Yin's husband and parents each filed civil lawsuits against Ma for compensation of 1.39 million yuan and 870,000 yuan respectively. Her husband knelt before the judge, requesting the immediate execution of the death penalty for Ma. The prosecutor stated that Ma, after evaluation, possessed full criminal responsibility, with extremely high personal danger and social harm, without any mitigating circumstances, and deserved severe punishment, the death penalty.

Ma's defense lawyer argued that Ma suffered from parental divorce at a young age and lived with his father. His father was sentenced to death for murder when Ma was four years old, and Ma endured abuse from his stepmother. These extraordinary experiences caused personality defects and radical character traits, deviating his worldview from normalcy. Although the evaluation showed Ma possessed full criminal responsibility, the lawyer hoped the court would consider his growth background when sentencing. Ma gave up self-defense. Regarding his childhood, he refused to elaborate, only mentioning that past experiences often caused insomnia and suicidal thoughts since childhood. The prosecutor believed that Ma showed indifference to his actions in court, disregarded others' right to life, and even displayed contempt for the court, making leniency inappropriate.

Throughout the entire trial, Ma did not express any apologies or regrets to the victims or their families. In his final statement, he said, "I hope the judge can quickly sentence me to immediate execution, thank you!" and bowed to the judge. The verdict was not announced in court.
