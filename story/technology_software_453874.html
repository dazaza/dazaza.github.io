Preliminary Discussion on the Dance Arrangement of China Sports Dance Troupe - Published in the Medical Center Journal

Abstract: Research methods such as questionnaire surveys, record statistics, and comparative analysis are primarily used. Since 2000, the national sports dance championship (group dance) and the top six action sets have been statistically analyzed from aspects of difficult actions, formation, action choreography, and characteristics and trends, introducing other aspects of the complete set of dances performed by various dance groups.

Keywords: Sports Dance Group, Dance Choreography

In recent years, the Blackpool Dance Festival, Latin dance professional group in Guangdong, Latin Wei Dong, Long Wei Min dance group competitions, have been won for three consecutive years by Coach LED in the top three. Our group project teams are increasingly on par with world-class strength. In this study, according to comparative analysis between China and foreign countries, we uncover the gaps in our collective project scheduling and also glimpse the trends in collective project scheduling.

2 Methods

2.1 Documents and materials focus on entering international sports dance competition rules, referring to books on dance and gymnastics since 1993. Expert interviews and applications throughout the province during training sessions to participate in national competitions have facilitated exchanges with international sports dance experts Wei Xiao Ying, Yin Wei Dong, Long Wei Min regarding dance choreography and judging. Through video analysis, the national sports dance championship dance groups, a portion of the world sports dance championship, and the top six complete action sets are analyzed via video recordings.

The discussion on difficulty scheduling reveals an increasing number of high-difficulty rotational actions, resulting in the following forms: continuous level switches (single), double-supported rotation with foot contact on the ground, foot rotation assisted by the ground, and twisting directional changes assisted by completion. Space is a dense material existence with its breadth, height, and three-dimensional depth. Difficulties are not only beginning to increase the basic difficulty of flat rotational twists but also incorporating spatial transformations that assist in completing twists at high, medium, and low gravity centers through three different movements using high, medium, and low postures. This reflects the movement and changes of the body in different directions and hierarchical lines, creating a more comprehensive and diverse action structure, enhancing the attractiveness of the complete set of actions while also increasing dual difficulties. Therefore, increasing the difficulty of movements is not merely a quantitative overlay but involves adding higher-level actions to increase spatial difficulty and achieving near-perfect quality in completing rotational actions. Comparing modern dance and Latin dance groups, the difficulty in operating space (low-to-high space) has relatively smaller changes. In terms of action choreography, the difficulty in the complete set increases with techniques such as crotch flips and ground motion difficulty flips.

Regarding the action climax and structural balance model of dance groups, it is generally either 5 parts: start, development, climax, counter-climax, climax peak, end; or three parts: beginning, middle, end. The most difficult tasks occur during the action climax, peak, or middle climax stage. For example, in 2006, the world Latin dance champion team utilized the intimate and emotional characteristics of Rumba music, leading the male partner to perform a full leg half-rotation for nine weeks, followed by each move being guided by double twirls, showcasing spatial changes and immediate stops, highlighting the action as the highlight of the entire movement's scheduling difficulty, pushing the emotions of the audience and judges to a climax. The complete transformation of domestic games forms a diversified action set statistical analysis, presenting a trend towards diversification in the completion of difficult actions. In competitions both domestically and internationally, continuous rotations and sliding movements are applied after 1-3 formations change, causing variations in the upper and lower spatial hierarchy and changes in different directions accompanied by the same actions continuously, with males and females completing various forms of difficult actions (including alternating completions of different spatial levels) and partner alternations performing the same actions.
