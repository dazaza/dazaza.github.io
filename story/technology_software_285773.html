Cupola Furnace Principle

Qingdao Lujin Wang General Dust Removal Equipment is a professional manufacturer specializing in the research and development and production of cupola furnaces. Our factory produces a series of cupola furnace products, including hot blast cupola furnaces, environmental protection cupola furnaces, cold blast cupola furnaces, high-temperature iron chip furnaces, and dust removal equipment for cupola furnaces. Examples include integrated wet scrubbers, reverse-blow flat bag dust collectors, and reverse-blow fabric bag dust collectors. With the proposal of the scientific development concept and the "Three Represents" regarding "combining economic development with environmental protection to follow a sustainable development path," our factory has developed heat treatment furnaces utilizing residual heat from cupola furnaces based on the working principle of the cupola furnace in heat treatment processes. This not only improves efficiency but also protects the environment.

1. The air infiltration coefficient of the cupola furnace refers to the ratio of the amount of air entering the furnace body through the charging port and other areas to the theoretical air requirement for the cupola furnace process.

2. The air infiltration coefficient for the cupola furnace (hot blast furnace, blast temperature > 400°C) is specified as 2.5.

3. The main feature of the heat treatment furnace using residual heat from the cupola furnace is the addition of an annealing furnace above the cupola furnace charging port, replacing the large chimney of the cupola furnace and the wet scrubber. It utilizes the residual heat and the heat released by the complete combustion of flammable gases from the cupola furnace to meet the heat treatment needs of castings. This not only improves the thermal energy utilization rate of the cupola furnace, saves dedicated heat sources for heat treatment, but also reduces environmental pollution.

4. A type of heat treatment furnace composed of a heat source device and an annealing furnace, characterized in that the heat source device adopts the melting chamber of the cupola furnace, and the annealing furnace is set above the charging port of the cupola furnace, replacing the large chimney of the cupola furnace. Temperature adjustment holes are provided on the wall of the annealing furnace, forming a heat treatment furnace that utilizes residual heat from the cupola furnace.
