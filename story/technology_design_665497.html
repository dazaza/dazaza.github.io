Zhengzhou wedding celebration held hostage by bandits who drove off in a BMW with the woman and demanded 15 million yuan

by siyimu009 on 2012-02-14 17:38:50

Beijing Morning Post (reporter Fei Yan) - Ms. Wang was driving her BMW home one night and was kidnapped in a bread van at the community gate when she was parking, and taken to Hebei. Her BMW was also hijacked. The kidnappers then used Ms. Wang's mobile phone to send a text message to her husband demanding an urgent transfer of 1.5 million yuan, but it was identified as a scam and the husband reported it to the police. Two days later, Ms. Wang was successfully rescued. Recently, the five kidnappers were tried in Chaoyang Court.

According to the indictment, Chen Mou conspired with Sun Mou and three others, around 22:00 on January 11, 2011, forcibly took Ms. Wang away on the roadside east of Chaoting Apartment Complex in Chaoyang District, Beijing, and drove away the BMW car she was driving (worth 300,000 yuan). Later, these five individuals demanded 1.5 million yuan from Ms. Wang's family and stole cash of 2,000 yuan along with her mobile phone, rings, and other items. They also withdrew 69,000 yuan from Ms. Wang's bank account.

During the trial, all defendants admitted their guilt. According to defendant Sun Mou, initially Chen Mou had asked him to bring some people to Beijing to help collect a debt. It wasn't until they got Ms. Wang into the van that they realized it was a kidnapping. Another defendant, Zhu Mou, said he regretted his actions while still in the vehicle and secretly wrote "Sister, I regret it" on the victim's hand. Zhu Mou stated that when he was left alone with Ms. Wang, she begged him to let her go, but he was afraid of Chen Mou's retaliation and therefore did not agree.
