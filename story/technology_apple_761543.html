Are Apple fans really more loyal?

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 17:07:48

Every survey result seems to indicate that the user loyalty of Apple products is higher than other brands. However, some people believe that users are not really loyal; their loyalty stems from insufficient temptation. If competitors like Samsung and Google were to launch more dazzling new products, would Apple's fans still be as tolerant of Apple and pledge lifelong allegiance?Loyalty of Apple FansTrusting others is like trusting that your bus will arrive on time. Fundamentally, people are fickle. In today’s world where everything is shared, people have become increasingly selfish because they must spend more time considering how to publicly display themselves.Yet, Apple continues to enjoy unwavering loyalty from its fans, which is somewhat surprising. A casual visit to any Apple retail store will reveal it is always packed with people.Whenever a brand loyalty survey is conducted, Apple appears to consistently rank at the top. Not long ago, a survey aimed to identify which brand people were least willing to purchase. BlackBerry seemed to be the least favored brand, while Apple ranked last in this regard.This week, market research company Yankee Group stated that by 2015, the number of iPhone users in the U.S. will far surpass Android users. It claimed that the number of people indicating an intention to buy an iPhone in the future was double that of those intending to buy an Android phone.Moreover, it argued that such loyalty is not just due to the influence of Apple products themselves, but also because of the entire Apple ecosystem. According to relevant statistics, only 9% of Apple smartphone users plan to switch to Android for their next phone, whereas 24% of Android users indicated they would switch to another phone, with 18% specifically interested in switching to an Apple device.It’s not hard to imagine that such conclusions seem to make sense. Those who genuinely fall for Android phones seem to be mostly tech-savvy insiders who see advanced technology in Android. It's difficult to find anyone who believes Android surpasses Apple in aesthetics or any other emotional factor.Loyalty and Its LimitationsHowever, the more fascinating question is how far this loyalty can go.When you think about people who are loyal to you or whom you are loyal to, you often feel that in such relationships, people tend to overlook certain minor flaws. Yes, she tends to become aggressive when nervous, but I can forgive her because I am loyal to her. Indeed, he behaves rudely in political matters, but in times of crisis, he always helps me.The entire Apple ecosystem may have its own limitations, but it is relatively easy to navigate. Moreover, the company has rewarded loyal fans, such as having cheerful employees in blue uniforms ready to assist customers with questions. Yes, they are indeed waiting in the store.The current test is whether such loyalty can bring even a hint of tolerance. Numerous cases of human nature analysis have found that people always enthusiastically pursue novel things. Their expectations are irrationally driven by various fresh possibilities allowed by technology, such as meeting in a vacuum or drawing with their fingers.However, Apple has not satisfied this nearly mad desire—at least in the eyes of some. If a competitor releases a product that makes Apple instantly pale in comparison, people might no longer be as tolerant of Apple as they are now. Their expressions would resemble the look of disdain shown by a wife in movies towards her aging husband before running off with someone else.The Temptation of Samsung and Google GlassesSamsung's introduction of larger displays is clearly a step forward. However, it isn't enough to dim Apple's shine, though it does pose a certain threat.Some believe that Google Glasses could completely transform the market. They envision a future where everyone wears their portable computer on their nose, freeing their hands to do more things.This undoubtedly represents a different mindset.Ultimately, user loyalty to Apple may follow the same path as loyalty in any relationship. You stay because it still makes sense to do so, or because you haven’t found anything sufficiently attractive elsewhere to make you leave.You stay because you still believe things will get better and you still feel comfortable. You might think people are trapped in Apple's ecosystem, but if they don't feel that way, then they aren't trapped.Should I stay or should I go? This question may depend on the shocking product that Apple CEO Tim Cook mentioned during the earnings call last Tuesday, set to be launched in the fall.However, I believe that people's loyalty to Apple will not fade away quickly.
