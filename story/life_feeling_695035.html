The Love of 100 Lollipops

The story goes like this: Long ago, there was a boy who would see a girl he fancied every day on his way. This went on day after day. Finally, one day, the boy mustered up the courage to buy the girl her first lollipop. The girl put the lollipop in her mouth and handed the wrapper to the boy. She said, "If we are destined..."

The boy opened the wrapper and found a piece of paper inside. Unfortunately, it was a blank sheet. The boy was taken aback as he pondered over what the girl had said. He was disappointed but did not give up...

A few days later, the boy bought the girl another lollipop. As before, the girl put it in her mouth and handed the wrapper to the boy. Once again, she said, "If we are destined..." The boy received another blank sheet...

And so, the boy continued to buy the girl lollipops.

Months passed, and the boy had given the girl 99 lollipops. However, each time, the girl's response was always the same: "If we are destined..." along with a blank sheet of paper. Eventually, the boy grew impatient. He no longer bothered to look at the wrappers the girl gave him. He felt that he could never win her heart. When he received the 99th wrapper from the girl, he chose to marry another girl who had been pursuing him for some time.

On their wedding night, the boy, to demonstrate his loyalty to his wife, took out all the wrappers he had collected from the girl and told her the whole story.

His wife examined each wrapper one by one. Just as the boy had said, they were all blank sheets. When she reached the last dozen or so, she noticed they hadn't been opened and asked the boy why.

The boy replied, "Since they're just blank sheets anyway, does it matter whether I open them or not?"

With the boy's permission, his wife opened the last dozen or so wrappers that the girl had given him.

When she opened the 99th wrapper, she saw a line of writing. She fell silent and handed the paper to the boy.

The boy took the paper and was astonished to find these words written on the 99th sheet: "I have prepared my wedding dress. When you send me the hundredth lollipop, I will become your bride."

The boy stood there, stunned, dazed, dumbfounded, and lost. He couldn't believe this fact. He took the 99 wrappers outside and let the bone-chilling wind carry them away one by one. His heart flew far, far away with the flying white papers.

Perhaps this is just a story, but I believe that the love of those lollipops was so romantic, so pure, and so unforgettable...

After reading it, I cried,

Suppose you were that boy,

Would you hold onto that love...
