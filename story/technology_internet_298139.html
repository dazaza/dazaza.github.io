Radio and Television Notice May Become a Catalyst: Guxi TV Shopping Officially Goes Out of Business

by sjfdns12 on 2009-12-07 18:11:42

By Morning Post reporter Bao Hua Li - The collapse of the television shopping company Guxi Industrial Development Co., which had previously bombarded multiple TV stations across the country with advertisements, has caused a great stir. Yesterday, at Zhangjiang Geti Port, I saw that all of "Guxi's" offices were locked up tight, and a posted "Notice" revealed that the company officially collapsed last Friday, and a post-closure liquidation team was established.

Analysts believe that the Notice on Strengthening the Management of Television Shopping Short Videos and Home Shopping Programs, which will be enforced starting next year, will act as a "catalyst" for some medium and small television shopping enterprises lacking in strength to be eliminated from the market, and could also be one of the reasons for "Guxi's" collapse.

Suppliers owed up to 5 million yuan

Yesterday at the Zhangjiang Geti Port building, I saw that the plaque for Guxi Industrial Company can still be found on the floor directory. After exiting the elevator on the 9th floor, I could see a notice signed by "Guxi Shopping." Both the offices on the 9th and 10th floors are locked up tight, deserted. While I was lingering outside "Guxi's" door, two men who claimed to have business dealings with "Guxi" also came to "Guxi's" office but left empty-handed. When they tried contacting relevant personnel at "Guxi," their phones were either turned off or already disconnected.

The notice posted by "Guxi" stated, "Due to poor company performance and severe losses, it can no longer continue." According to the notice, after bankruptcy, a liquidation team was formed to handle issues regarding unpaid employee salaries and supplier goods payments. However, a supplier told reporters that the liquidation team mainly handles internal settlements within "Guxi," and to retrieve the payment, they would need to find "Guxi's" chairman Yuan Jianda. Mr. Wu from Ningbo Baode Electric, one of "Guxi's" suppliers, told reporters that his company began supplying goods to "Guxi" in June this year, and so far, "Guxi" owes Baode Electric over 300,000 yuan in unpaid goods. It is understood that the suppliers owed by "Guxi" provided products including GPS devices and laptops, with amounts ranging from 50,000 to 5 million yuan, distributed across various regions of the country.

As suppliers suspect that "Guxi's" founder and chairman Yuan Jianda has "disappeared," I managed to reach him via his mobile phone. Yesterday, Yuan Jianda's assistant specially came to the Morning Post to explain the situation, stating that due to the impact of the financial crisis, there was a break in the capital chain, and the company has now entered a state of suspension and cleanup. A post-closure work team has been established, and lawyers have been entrusted to participate in asset and creditor-debtor settlement matters, with a dedicated creditor reception office set up. Currently, the post-closure work team is evaluating the company's original assets, striving to come up with a solution to the problems between the suppliers as soon as possible, unlike what some media outlets previously reported about the "chairman disappearing" or "suspected fraud."

A "winter" for television shopping may be coming

Although related responsible persons at "Guxi" claim that the company's collapse is due to the impact of the financial crisis, we learned that the Notice on Strengthening the Management of Television Shopping Short Videos and Home Shopping Programs issued by the State Administration of Radio, Film, and Television will officially begin enforcement on January 1st next year, becoming a "catalyst" for some medium and small television shopping enterprises lacking in strength to be eliminated from the market.

The relevant notice issued by the State Administration of Radio, Film, and Television requires that television shopping short videos truthfully introduce the products being sold, clearly mark the name of the sales enterprise, and announce commitments for unconditional returns and cash-on-delivery within a certain period, strictly prohibiting content that is false, illegal, vulgar, exaggerated, or misleading. Additionally, companies allowed to broadcast shopping advertisements on radio and television must have "a registered capital of no less than 10 million RMB," "a call system with no fewer than 100 seats, logistics delivery, and settlement systems," etc.

Furthermore, different from the model used by Hunan Happy Purchase and Oriental Shopping Channel, which collaborate with their own television stations, "Guxi's" model involves purchasing airtime on local television channels to broadcast product advertisements, a practice common among most television shopping enterprises, but with the disadvantage of high costs for purchasing time slots, directly leading to a shortage of funds for television shopping enterprises.

A person familiar with the television shopping industry told reporters that the regulations stipulated in the "Notice" have reduced the range of products that television shopping can sell, with stricter reviews, thus venture capital will not blindly enter the market. Combined with the exorbitant costs of television advertising, this directly leads to insufficient funds for some medium and small television shopping enterprises, ultimately resulting in bankruptcy. From this perspective, "Guxi's" collapse may just be the tip of the iceberg for the "winter" facing television shopping enterprises.
