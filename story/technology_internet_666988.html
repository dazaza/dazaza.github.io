Analysis of the Characteristics of Internet Advertising

by hebei5602i on 2012-02-16 12:25:39

The Extensiveness of Online Advertising Dissemination

Online advertising can be spread around the world through the international Internet network, 24 hours a day without interruption. This could be described as an all-weather dissemination method. Internet users can browse advertising information at any time and place on the Internet, which is an effect that traditional media cannot achieve. In fact, this is one of the key advantages of online media over traditional media. When it comes to online advertising, it means that advertisements can be released globally. However, for many clients, this advantage does not seem particularly attractive.

It is well known that companies hope their advertisements target specific regional audiences rather than everyone, otherwise it would lead to reduced appeal and effectiveness. For instance, health supplement manufacturers wish their advertisement audience consists of people who need health supplements. Such requirements have also led some traditional industries to misunderstand online advertising. Take real estate as an example, since real estate developers mainly serve residents and businesses within a particular city, advertising in local newspapers, TV stations, or billboards will undoubtedly be more effective than "facing the whole world" websites. To put it simply, no matter what kind of enterprise, its marketing strategy ultimately targets a certain area or group of people. Although CCTV generates substantial advertising revenue, compared to numerous local media across the country, it only accounts for a small portion. Therefore, the extensive nature of online advertising has yet to fully translate into an advantage for online advertising.

The Countability of Advertising Audience

A mainstream view we often see is: "When doing advertising on traditional media, it is difficult to accurately know how many people receive the ad information. However, on the Internet, through authoritative and impartial visitor traffic statistics systems, the number of users who viewed each client's ad can be precisely counted, along with the time distribution and geographical distribution of these users. This helps advertisers correctly evaluate the ad effect and determine the advertising placement strategy."

However, in reality, it is impossible to statistically measure the absolute number of advertising audiences. Using click-through rates to measure the effectiveness of banner ads is fundamentally wrong because the role of online advertising is not solely about whether you click on it. Therefore, evaluating advertising effectiveness based on this so-called statistical number leads to absurd conclusions.

The Sensory Nature of Online Information Dissemination

Online advertising allows consumers to personally "experience" products, services, and brands comprehensively. They can also make reservations, conduct transactions, and settle payments online—things that traditional media cannot achieve. By comparison, regardless of whether it's television shopping on TV or mail-order via newspapers and magazines, none of them can compete with the Internet in terms of sales activities. From this perspective, compared to traditional media, online advertising indeed demonstrates its unique advantages.

The Interactivity of Online Information Dissemination

For online advertising, if the audience is interested in a particular ad, they just need to click the mouse to learn more detailed and vivid information. The most illustrative example of interactivity in online dissemination is e-commerce websites. These websites categorize goods in detail, have clear hierarchies, and allow direct transactions online.

However, current network bandwidth imposes various restrictions on the interactivity of online advertising, making it impossible for consumers to "comprehensively experience" products. Although online advertising claims to be multimedia with images, text, video, and audio, it cannot truly achieve this in practice. Currently, although online advertising content is richer than newspapers and television, its dissemination effect lacks the impact of traditional media like television advertising. It’s evident that the interactive advantage of online advertising has been somewhat offset by the limitations of network conditions. For online advertising to develop further, there must be groundbreaking advancements in network technology.
