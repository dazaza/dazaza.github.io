Introduction: Communication is everywhere; skills determine success.

Main Body:

No matter the size of an online store, customer communication is essential. Communication is omnipresent, and skills decide success or failure. When customers flood in, how do we communicate? When they wield their big knives to bargain with us, how do we handle it? And when they place an order but delay payment, how do we deal with it?

We typically divide customer communication into three stages: pre-sale, during-sale, and after-sale. This article focuses on during-sale communication, which refers to the interaction between buyers and sellers from the time they browse until they pay. This mainly happens through instant messaging via Wangwang but can also occur through product page comments, store messages, internal emails, emails, text messages, or phone calls.

During-Sale Communication: The Seven Sword Techniques

During-sale communication can be broken down into seven steps: greeting, inquiry, recommendation, negotiation, verification, farewell, and follow-up. That is, "Greet first, ask twice, recommend thrice, negotiation and verification are crucial, and after bidding farewell, keep following up" (These are famous sayings that must be memorized thoroughly).

Num 1, Greeting: Timely response, polite and enthusiastic.

Buyers may simultaneously reach out to multiple sellers. Whoever responds faster gains the advantage. We shouldn't sacrifice politeness and warmth just for speed. Our goal should be: one expression of words, politeness and enthusiasm warm hearts, starting strong to retain customers' attention (this is too classic, remember it).

Num 2, Inquiry: Guiding enthusiastically, listening attentively.

Ask leading questions to help buyers quickly express their purchasing information so that we can more accurately recommend suitable products.

Inquiry Example: [This was originally a picture, but I manually typed it out for better effect]

Buyer: I want to buy a bracelet, can you help me select one?

Customer Service: Sure, is it for gifting or personal use?

Buyer: I want to give it to my girlfriend as a birthday present.

Customer Service: What price range are you looking at?

Buyer: Around 200 yuan.

Customer Service: Alright, I'll pick the most suitable one for you, please wait a moment.

Num 3, Recommendation: Demonstrating professionalism, precise recommendation.

Being familiar with the product demonstrates professionalism, and professionalism builds trust. Based on the collected buyer information, recommend suitable rather than the most expensive products to the buyer. Make the buyer feel they are receiving sincere assistance rather than being sold something.

Num 4, Negotiation: Retreat to advance, close the deal.

The most exciting part for customer service is negotiating prices. Buyers who negotiate fall into two categories: one seeks fair treatment, and the other looks for special deals. For the former, emphasize standardization, fairness, clear pricing, value for money, and no cheating regardless of age. Eliminate the buyer’s concerns, these customers generally won’t haggle endlessly. For the latter, guide them to purchase promotional items or offer small gifts to satisfy their desire for discounts.

Num 5, Verification: Timely confirmation, buyer's approval.

With the groundwork laid by previous communications, verify transaction details while the buyer is still online to effectively avoid issues like wrong or missing shipments. Verification corrects errors in buyer remarks and records personalized requests emphasized during Wangwang conversations. Helping buyers verify also helps oneself stay organized.

Num 6, Farewell: Enthusiastic thanks, welcome back.

Both parties are happy when a deal is made, so farewells naturally become enthusiastic.

Even if a sale doesn't go through, goodwill remains, and farewells should still be warm. Not buying now doesn't mean they won't buy later. Especially for those who didn't make a deal due to price negotiations, their expectations for discounts weren't met, but they understand the seller's non-negotiable business model. These buyers have a high probability of returning and making purchases smoothly.

Num 7, Follow-up: Treat as completed, timely communication.

This step involves following up on transactions where the buyer has placed an order but hasn't paid yet. According to the rules, placing an order is a commitment to purchase. As a seller, it should also be treated as a completed sale, prepare the goods accordingly, and communicate with the buyer at the right time to confirm and understand the reasons for non-payment, thereby promoting payment.
