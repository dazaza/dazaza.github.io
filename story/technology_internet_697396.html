Persistent rain in many places in the north breaks the record of the past 50 years

by lovec4f9 on 2012-03-08 15:17:39

Since 2012, persistent drizzle has lingered over much of southern China and northern Guangxi. Many netizens have humorously commented on this weather: ["Ask the north what 'clear' is, it makes people 'wet'!], [Mr. Sun is probably on vacation], [This weather is all about who has the most underwear and socks!] These comments are ubiquitous on Weibo. [High-definition map: Northern China's rainy areas]

From the perspective of cumulative rainfall, the rainfall in most parts of southern China and northern Guangdong has generally exceeded 100 millimeters. In southeastern Jiangsu, southern Anhui, western Zhejiang, and southeastern Fujian, the rainfall has even exceeded 200 millimeters. Compared to normal years, the rainfall has increased by 50-200%. In February, more than 10 days of rain were recorded in 11 provincial capital cities in the south. Representative cities include Guiyang, Fuzhou, and Hangzhou, where out of 29 days, the number of rainy days were 23, 19, and 19 respectively. The long rainy periods have made sunshine a scarce commodity.

Zhejiang’s precipitation is the highest in 60 years

From January 1 to March 5 this year, the average rainfall in Zhejiang Province reached 310 millimeters, which is 80% higher than usual, making it the highest in nearly 60 years. The province's average sunshine duration was 95.4 hours, which is 56% less than the usual period, setting the lowest record in nearly 60 years, with 40 counties (cities, districts) breaking historical records for the least sunshine.

Hunan’s sunshine duration hit the lowest since the founding of the People's Republic of China

Since January this year, Hunan Province's average temperature has been 2.0℃ lower than usual, ranking as the ninth lowest value since 1951. The total sunshine duration from January to February this year was 38.6 hours, hitting the lowest since the founding of the People's Republic of China, nearly 18 hours less than the least sunny year in history (1990).

In Jiangxi, 64 counties (cities) have the highest number of rainy days in history

Since January this year, Jiangxi Province's average sunshine duration has been 60% less than usual, setting the lowest historical record. The number of no-sunshine days ranges from 42 to 59 days, 10 to 22 days more than usual. In 73 counties (cities), the sunshine duration has set the lowest record since meteorological records began. The number of rainy days ranges from 46 to 61 days, 10 to 20 days more than usual, with 64 counties (cities) having the highest number of rainy days in history.

From March 4 to 6, Ganzhou City experienced heavy to torrential rain. The water level at the Gongshui Xiashan hydrological station was 109.48 meters, exceeding the warning level by 0.48 meters, marking the first flood situation in early March since 1998.

Guangzhou’s continuous rain is rare in nearly ten years

In February and March this year, Guangzhou experienced a prolonged rainy period. Throughout February, there were fewer than four clear days. Just entering March, Guangzhou experienced short-term thunderstorms on the evening of March 1st and hail on the morning of March 7th. Such prolonged rainy weather is rare in the past decade. Similar continuous rainy weather occurred in 2005 and 2009, where the rainfall was greater but did not last as long as this year.

Guizhou’s sunshine duration is the lowest in 50 years

From December 2011 to February 2012, Guizhou Province's seasonal sunshine duration was 46.1 hours, 117 hours less than usual, making it the year with the least sunshine since 1961. The seasonal precipitation was 8.5% less than usual, considered normal. Guiyang, the provincial capital, only saw the sun for 7 days in three months, and in February, which usually marks the arrival of spring, there was not a single sunny day. Sunshine in Guiyang this winter became a "luxury."

Fujian’s January sunshine duration is the lowest in 50 years

Since January this year, Fujian Province has experienced prolonged high temperature, drizzle, and low sunshine. According to statistics, the monthly average sunshine duration in January this year was 78.5 hours less than usual (108.2 hours), making it the lowest since 1961. Entering February, the monthly average sunshine duration was 37.6 hours less than usual, making it the third lowest since 2000. It is expected that rainy weather will continue to accompany Fujian in March and April this year.

Jiangsu’s precipitation is 10-30% higher than usual

From January 1 to February 28, Jiangsu Province’s average temperature was 0.1 to 1.3℃ lower than usual. The precipitation was 10-30% higher than usual in southern Jiangsu, with 21 to 29 rainy days, 3 to 8 days more than usual. From early January to mid-February, the province’s sunshine duration was 150 to 320 hours, slightly more than usual in Huabei, but 10-30% less in other regions.

Anhui’s temperature is the lowest since 1989

Since February, Anhui Province has shown significant "cold and wet" characteristics, with precipitation 10-70% higher than usual, the lowest temperature since 1989, and sunshine 30-60% less than usual. This gloomy and low-temperature weather will continue into early March.

Guangxi’s high temperature and drizzle have lasted for two months

The high temperature and drizzle in northern Guangxi have lasted for two months, with increasing intensity. It is expected that there will be another round of precipitation from the 9th to the 12th.
