Continued: Music professor applies for forced demolition of illegal balcony - Hunan Online

by qiyouchax64 on 2011-05-01 09:22:13

Xinhua News Agency, July 14 - "I don't just want him to dismantle that corner now; I want all the illegally constructed balconies to be dismantled." This morning, Professor Yuan from ChangTai HaoYuan in Kaifu District, Changsha, called our reporter at this newspaper. Professor Yuan said he had already submitted a forced demolition application to the Furong North Road Team of the Kaifu District Urban Management Brigade, requesting that the urban management law enforcement officers quickly dismantle the illegally built balcony.

Professor Yuan: "Demand for Full Demolition"

Yesterday afternoon, I conducted a telephone interview with Professor Wu and conveyed his opinions to Professor Yuan. In response to Professor Wu's statement, Professor Yuan expressed disbelief. Professor Yuan said that Professor Wu specializes in building structures and is an expert in this field. The original blueprint for removing the edge of the balcony was drawn by Professor Wu, and the idea of removing the edge also originated from him, which inherently indicates that removing the edge of the balcony is feasible. Now, he turns around and says it’s not possible— isn’t that like slapping his own face? “He doesn't really want to dismantle it; drawing up blueprints and signing documents are completely fooling me!” Professor Yuan said.

"On June 24, we both signed the agreement, and there were urban management personnel present at the time," Professor Yuan said. "Now, the agreed-upon demolition date has passed half a month, and Professor Wu hasn't dismantled the edges according to the commitment in the agreement. The initial agreement has become invalid. Now, I don't just want him to remove that corner; I want all the illegally constructed balconies removed." Professor Yuan told reporters that he had already submitted a demolition application to the Furong North Road Team of the Kaifu District Urban Management Brigade and the ChangTai HaoYuan property management company, requesting their prompt removal of the illegally constructed balconies.

Property Management and Urban Management: Support Yuan's Rights Protection

Yesterday afternoon, I interviewed Ren Lin, the person in charge of the ChangTai HaoYuan property management company. Ren Lin told reporters that Professor Wu did not obtain approval from the planning department before constructing the balcony, making it the largest illegal construction in the neighborhood, which has had a negative impact. The property management company supports Professor Yuan's rights protection efforts.

The head of the Kaifu District Urban Management Brigade stated that Professor Wu did not dismantle the agreed-upon portion as per his original commitment. The balcony he set up itself belongs to illegal construction, and neighbors have raised significant complaints. They will dismantle the illegal construction according to laws and regulations at the first available opportunity.
