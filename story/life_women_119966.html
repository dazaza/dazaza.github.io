What is eyeliner?

Eyeliner uses traditional tattoo techniques to beautify the edge of the eyelid. It has become a popular beauty technique in recent years. The purpose of eyeliner is to achieve a more perfect eye shape. After applying eyeliner, it not only changes and enlarges the eye shape and increases the thickness of the eyelashes, but also creates a vivid color contrast between the black eyeliner and the white sclera, making the eyes appear brighter and more spirited.

For most women, eyeliner can produce good results. Those with big eyes and double eyelids will have more radiant eyes after applying eyeliner; those with sparse eyelashes will have brighter and more spirited eyes; small eyes and double eyelids will look larger; overly wide double eyelids can visually reduce the width of the upper eyelid. However, for single eyelids, due to the inward curling of the eyelashes, the applied eyeliner may not be easily visible, so it is recommended to undergo double eyelid surgery before applying eyeliner. People with large eye fissures, protruding eyeballs, blepharitis, skin diseases on the eyelids, and women during menstruation are not suitable for eyeliner.

Suitable candidates for eyeliner:

1. Loss of eyebrows or eyelashes caused by illness or other reasons.

2. Sparse or light-colored eyebrows or eyelashes.

3. Damage or scars to eyebrows or eyelashes caused by trauma or surgery.

4. Those who need no time for makeup due to their profession.

5. Asymmetric eyebrow shapes, unsatisfactory or undesirable original eyebrow shapes.

6. Individuals over 18 years old who desire eyeliner, especially small eyes and double eyelids that can enhance eye appearance and beautify facial features.

Who should avoid eyebrow or eyeliner tattoos:

1. Local inflammation or rash.

2. Recent trauma to eyebrows or eyelids.

3. Infectious diseases such as hepatitis or sexually transmitted diseases.

4. Allergic or scar-prone constitutions.

5. Abnormal mental state or psychiatric patients.

6. Diabetics and those with severe heart or brain diseases.

7. High expectations from surgery, or indecision about eyebrow or eyeliner tattoos, with family disagreement.

8. Eyelids with inversion, obvious outward protrusion of the eyeball, loose upper eyelid skin, or noticeable bags under the eyes.

9. Single eyelids. However, single eyelids can obtain satisfactory results after undergoing double eyelid surgery followed by eyeliner.

Precautions related to eyeliner:

1. Upper eyeliner should be applied at the root of the eyelashes, finer at the inner end, gradually widening outward, with a certain angle at the tail, slightly elongated and slightly upward to increase the length of the eye fissure.

2. Lower eyeliner should be applied inside the root of the eyelashes, slightly wider on the outside than on the inside, with fine and straight lines.

3. The width ratio of upper to lower eyeliner should ideally be 7:3. The upper and lower eyeliner should not connect at the outer corner of the eye, otherwise it will form a black circle, affecting the aesthetic effect.

Principles of eyeliner:

Eyeliner not only changes and enlarges the eye shape and increases the thickness of the eyelashes, but also creates a vivid color contrast between the black eyeliner and the white sclera, making the eyes appear brighter and more spirited. When applying eyeliner, note that the upper eyeliner should be applied at the root of the eyelashes, finer at the inner end, gradually widening outward, with a certain angle at the tail, slightly elongated and slightly upward to increase the length of the eye fissure. The lower eyeliner should be applied inside the root of the eyelashes, slightly wider on the outside than on the inside, with fine and straight lines. The width ratio of upper to lower eyeliner should ideally be 7:3. The upper and lower eyeliner should not connect at the outer corner of the eye, otherwise it will affect the result.

Possible complications of eyeliner:

Local infection can occur if doctors do not strictly disinfect before and during surgery, or if patients do not carefully protect themselves afterward.

Cross-infection can occur due to viruses like hepatitis or AIDS spreading through blood, tears, saliva, etc. This often happens when needle sterilization is not strict, and needles are shared among multiple people. Some without medical knowledge perform eyebrow tattoos without sterilizing needles. Some beauticians only use traditional alcohol or new germicides which have no effect on hepatitis B virus or AIDS virus, causing cross-infection.

What to do if eyeliner goes wrong:

If asymmetry occurs in the eyebrows or eyeliner post-surgery, or if the eyebrows or eyeliner appear strange, patients can go to the hospital to remove the pigments embedded in the skin via laser. If the laser cannot remove the pigment, then surgical removal of the pigmented skin is required. For those who have removed pigments or had pigmented skin surgically removed, they can undergo another tattoo, but this should be carefully considered. If local infection, cross-infection, eye injury, or drug allergy complications occur, one should promptly seek treatment at a reputable hospital.
