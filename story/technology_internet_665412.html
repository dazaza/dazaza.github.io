6 Super Personalized Fashion Outfits, the Style Loved by Avant-Garde Women

1. Lace's Cool and Sexy Charm

The allure of lace transparency can be very feminine with just a short-sleeved suit that has a strong design sense. If you're still not confident in your legs, add a slightly wrinkled, close-fitting knee-length skirt under your denim hot pants.

2. The Elegant Yuppies Look of Jumpsuits

Retro suits worn head-to-toe have greater occasion limitations and may seem somewhat rigid. When paired with the recently popular jumpsuit shorts, it creates an effect that is both formal and casual. Black and white two-tone suit shoes are classic items for showcasing neutral beauty; medium to high heels are more suitable for Asian women's height. A conservative black and white color combination can be elevated with a colorfully bold fluorescent green scarf, enhancing the overall look and exuding modern female yuppie charm.

3. The Madness of Princess Dresses

Pairing princess dresses with suits is no longer exclusively Kate Moss's domain and has even become less fresh. So, break free from the constraints of the suit and try a vest instead. To avoid looking bulky or pregnant, add one or two belts to the vest for layers and definition of the waistline.

4. The Love Song of the 70s with Oversized Suits

This khaki-colored loose-collared suit symbolizes the 1970s. If you want to create a nostalgic touch of sadness, simply pair it with wide-leg pants and suspenders. The illustrated T-shirt underneath uses modern illustration techniques to reminisce about the past. The pale pink hue adds a sense of melancholy.

5. PVC Punk Attitude

The highlight of the outfit lies in the contrast between the high-waisted pleated skirt and the PVC suit jacket in terms of color and texture. The striped tank top continues the visual effect of internal coordination, making the entire line very complete.

6. Slip Dress Meets Cycling Moments

Soft straps resembling nightgowns are no longer symbols of sloppiness. With a cool suit and fitted jeans mix-and-match, the edgy vibe becomes apparent immediately. The great Karl Lagerfeld invented leg cuffs in recent years, and you can DIY like in the picture above to make your jeans more playful and suitable for cycling.
