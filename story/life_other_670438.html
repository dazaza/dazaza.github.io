Licking one's own wounds

Submitting online

On the dimly lit street, I staggered along, my mind a blank, my heart shattered, numb, devoid of any sensation of pain, without tears, wandering aimlessly.

The horns behind me honked, but I was deaf to them, blind as well. I was merely a shell, a soulless shell, telling myself that I no longer felt pain.

Finally, I fell, crashing hard onto the ground. The kind driver didn't call me a fool; instead, he asked if I needed to go to the hospital. Shaking my head and waving my hand, I said it was fine, truly fine.

The blood seeped through my white skin, yet I felt no pain. Standing up, I brushed off the dust, still loving cleanliness as always, and continued my aimless walk.

I didn't want to see these drops of bright red blood. I had to clean it off. Knocking on the door of a clinic, from the surprised eyes that greeted me, I knew I must look disheveled, even terrifying. Finally, I managed a faint smile and said, "I'm injured. Could you bandage me?"

The gentle doctor, an angel, carefully cleaned my wound, occasionally asking me, "Does it hurt? If it does, scream it out, or cry if you need to."

I kept smiling, my eyes half-closed. I knew only by doing this could I pretend to be strong.

Finally, the cleaning was done. My legs were wrapped in white gauze, glaringly bright, but certainly more comfortable than the vivid red.

Standing on the street, everything spun around me. I didn’t know where I should go, I didn’t know why I was so disheveled, completely exhausted. I wanted to cry so much, but I really had no tears left. I had grown cold.

Shaking in the wind, I knew I had become the kite whose string had been cut, finally severed from the wheel that held me...
