Samsung shows off a foldable phone, but it's far from reality

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 18:15:29

At the recent Analyst Day event, Samsung showcased two foldable devices with flexible screens to the attending analysts. However, hands-on experience was not allowed on-site, and photography was strictly prohibited.

"The first device they demonstrated was a phone similar in size to the Galaxy S III, which could fold in half, somewhat resembling a lady's compact powder case," described Jae H. Lee, an analyst from Daiwa Securities who attended the meeting.

"The other device was like a wallet-shaped device that could also fold in half, akin to a tablet computer." According to Lee, both devices looked impressive, and other analysts also found them novel.

However, a Samsung spokesperson told The Wall Street Journal that the two foldable devices shown yesterday were merely conceptual products. Currently, there is a lack of necessary components to form a complete device.

As for commercialization? That’s even further away. Samsung CEO Kwon Oh-hyun said that foldable screens would likely not be available until at least 2015.

When Samsung launched the curved-screen phone Galaxy Round last month, many people questioned whether such innovation was necessary. However, the significance of the Galaxy Round should now be clear: it represents Samsung's attempt at mobile phone screens, with plans to eventually transition to foldable screens in the future.

Currently, foldable screens require glass components to be replaced with more flexible plastic components while ensuring that the latter can withstand environmental factors such as temperature, humidity, and pH changes. Additionally, achieving a bendable angle that allows the screen to fold together poses significant challenges. Even these aspects are difficult to realize, let alone color accuracy and viewing angles — foldable phones are still quite far from becoming a reality.
