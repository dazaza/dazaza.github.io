GM Is Starting to See Tesla as a Threat

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 15:32:29

On July 21, it was reported that Dan Akerson, CEO of General Motors (GM), had started to research how Tesla might impact GM's business. This move sent a clear signal to the industry: major automakers are beginning to worry about the threat posed by startup electric vehicle manufacturers.

Bloomberg reported that studying Tesla is just one way Akerson hopes to change GM's corporate culture following the financial difficulties of 2009. The global economic crisis and U.S. government bailout measures have led to some major U.S. automakers no longer being complacent, a trait that once caused them to lag behind their overseas competitors. However, this economic crisis and government assistance also highlighted significant progress made by the three giants of the U.S. auto industry — GM, Ford, and Chrysler — particularly in terms of quality, performance, and efficiency.

Since Akerson took the helm, some internal mechanisms at GM have changed. However, improving the company’s future means enhancing its products as well. From a technological standpoint, the most crucial point for GM remains the continued development of its Volt series.

Akerson stated that the next generation of Volt vehicles could hit the market in 2015 or 2016. Reducing costs remains a significant obstacle. GM engineers are working hard to cut $10,000 off the cost of each car. Despite the Volt taking price-cut measures to compete with Tesla's Model S, from a cost perspective, consumers find Tesla's prices more accessible, making it impossible for GM to ignore. In the first quarter of this year, Model S sales surpassed those of the Volt.

GM Vice Chairman Steve Girsky remarked, "In the past, they might have said, 'It's just a bunch of laptop batteries, don't worry about it,' but that's not the case now. I don't know if the Model S will pose a threat to us. What I do know is that if we ignore it and claim it's just a pile of laptop batteries, then that would be a disgrace."
