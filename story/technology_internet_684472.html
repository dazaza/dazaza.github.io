In-depth Analysis of Children's Wear Strategic Marketing Model in 2009

September 10, 2009 - China Children's Fashion Network

Every crisis is an opportunity for change in business and strategic marketing models. The transformation of the strategic marketing model for children's wear enterprises has risen to an unprecedented level of importance.

Under the impact of this financial storm, compared to other types of clothing, due to the relatively high necessity of children's wear consumption, its sales decline has been much slower than that of other clothing industries. However, children's wear brand enterprises are relatively small in scale, with low capital accumulation rates and immature channel construction, making their ability to resist risks relatively weak. For many small children's wear enterprises, 2009 was a severe test. With the serious challenges of the export market, some export-oriented children's wear enterprises will find it difficult to survive this crisis. However, for large children's wear brand enterprises, this is the best opportunity for value chain integration. Therefore, the transformation of the strategic marketing model for children's wear enterprises has risen to an unprecedented level of importance.

Strategic Prototype of Children's Wear Enterprises

The new commercial competition brought about by the crisis is no longer based on single products, prices, channels, or promotions, but rather on systematic competition based on core competitiveness. The idea behind changing the strategic marketing model is to elevate marketing from the sales level to the strategic level, and strategy should focus on strengthening and building core competitiveness. Before changing the strategic marketing model, all children's wear enterprises must first transform their commercial thinking and find the source of their core competitiveness, using core advantages for marketing.

To find the basis for strategic positioning, enterprises must clearly understand the forces from customers (Customer), the company itself (Company), and competitors (Competitor). In an uncertain environment, they need to find a certain path that can enhance their capabilities and build system competitiveness around this foundation.

According to Michael Porter's competitive theory, this construction mainly considers three ways: cost leadership (overall cost leadership), differentiation (differentiation), and focus (focus). The strategic marketing of enterprises is a process centered on customers as the core function, with marketing as an overall function elevated to the strategic level. In this process, marketing is not just a single department for value realization, nor is it a simple link in the value chain, but rather marketing DNA exists in every process link, and this DNA revolves around the above three competitive strategies.

Looking at the existing Chinese children's wear industry, we must first clarify the enterprise's strategic prototype identity (i.e., the original identity before engaging in the children's wear business) to find its core competitiveness. Based on the different strategic prototypes of Chinese children's wear enterprises, we have found three relatively obvious strategic marketing models (see Table 1).

1. Original practitioners of the children's wear industry, focusing their main business on the children's wear industry, with the key point of their strategic marketing model being the vertical integration traction model, which means controlling design upstream and terminal downstream, such as Hong Huang Lan and Hao Haizi.

2. Originally engaged in adult clothing, thereby deriving the children's wear business, but with brand awareness mainly concentrated in adult clothing, with the key point of their strategic marketing model being the horizontal integration traction model, which means leveraging the original advantageous links, such as Senma and Bailide.

3. Engaged in other children's industries, such as catering and entertainment, entering the children's wear industry through brand positioning, with the key point of their strategic marketing model being the concentric diversification traction model, which means focusing on satisfying the same type of target customers and continuously integrating the consumption chain, such as Wahaha and Taiziniu.

Three Models of Children's Wear Strategic Marketing

Vertical Integration Strategic Marketing Model

The core competitiveness of the marketing model formed by vertical integration focuses on its uniqueness in a certain link of the value chain, integrating upstream or downstream through the core resources of the enterprise, gradually controlling the vertical value chain of the children's wear industry. This model either highlights its advantage in design, forms an advantage in product development, or occupies an advantage in terminals. Most enterprises adopting this model are brand enterprises with children's wear as their main business. Representative models include:

1. Design-led Model

Design is the soul of the garment industry and also the most core link in the value chain. Children's wear design generally considers several elements: style, color fabric, and concept, which permeate the unique cultural and technological connotations of the brand. Good children's wear design is more complex than adult clothing, not only keeping up with fashion trends but also considering the psychological and physiological characteristics of children. It must have fashionable styles, bright colors, soft textures, and comfortable wearing, as well as being dirt-resistant and wear-resistant. Health is the first factor parents consider when buying clothes for children. With the awakening and upgrading of consumer awareness, green clothing is becoming a development trend for children's wear, especially infant and toddler clothing.

Compared to the social significance given to adult clothing by fashion, education should be the social significance given to children's clothing in the future. The "education through clothing" design philosophy deserves increasing attention from enterprises. During the 0-4 years of infancy, approximately 50% of learning ability is developed, 30% during the 4-8 years, and the remaining 20% will be completed between 8-17 years. How to use children's clothing to help develop intelligence and establish aesthetic cognition is the main topic currently facing professional children's wear designers.

The design philosophy and works of Chinese children's wear enterprises have always lagged behind in the international market. There are no fairy tales left in the adult world in China, so the children's wear world is basically a miniaturized version of adult clothing. Xiamen "Lida Star" Clothing Cup promotes the China Children's Wear Design Competition, not only enhancing the brand's reputation in the industry but also discovering a large number of excellent designers and design works for enterprises to use. Finding professional, civilian, or even grassroots forces through competitions, networks, etc., is a good model during this period when the professional design industry in China has not yet been fully established.

2. Product Portfolio Model

The crystallization of design is the product, and the product is also the carrier of the brand. Most children's wear enterprises originate from manufacturing, and their core competitiveness often comes from the product. Price and quality are no longer the sources of product competitiveness; continuously finding niche markets and developing product lines around them transforms into competitiveness. There are usually three common operational modes.

The first way is to horizontally integrate along the children's wear industry to become a professional supplier of infant and young child products. For example, Linyingfang, which is based on infant and children's wear products and extends to include breastfeeding, cleaning and care, packaging, toys, growth training, and a series of products that meet the needs of young mothers. Through membership and events, these customers are locked in. After more than ten years of development, Linyingfang has over 1,000 stores nationwide, with annual revenue exceeding RMB 1 billion, achieving a compound growth rate of more than 20% annually, successfully occupying the domestic infant and child product market.

The second way is the regular product line development model. From a single piece of children's wear to style combination matching, gradually extending to shoes, hats, accessories, bags, scarves, and other series of clothing combinations. This model is familiar to most enterprises, seemingly unremarkable on the surface, but there is much room for innovation. For instance, T100 once launched a "parent-child clothing" series, where parent-child clothing is produced for adults and children to match. Although the styles and colors may not be exactly the same, the design concept is consistent, thus increasing the product line combination and forming multi-angle sales promotion.

The third way is the specialization of细分products. For example, in the adult sportswear world, there are brands like Adidas and Nike. So, what about in the children's wear world? r100, a German sports children's wear brand, is sweeping through the Chinese market with great momentum. r100 considers the needs and physical development of children and teenagers, combining sports and leisure well, integrating the spirit of sports, cultivating the habit of participating in sports from a young age, and continuously segmenting the product line to find the customers they need.
