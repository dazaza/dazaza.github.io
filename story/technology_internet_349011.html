Food Therapy Series - Blood Nourishment

As the saying goes, "Medicinal supplementation is not as good as food therapy." Nutrition experts point out that paying attention to a reasonable combination of nutrients in daily diet and appropriately supplementing with blood-nourishing and qi-enhancing foods can help women prevent and combat diseases. Below are a few blood-nourishing sweet soups that I often consume:

Silver Ear Fungus and Red Dates Soup:

Silver ear fungus is also known as "the poor man's bird's nest." Bird's nest may be nutritious, but it is expensive. Silver ear fungus resembles bird's nest in color, texture, and efficacy, but at a much lower price, hence its name. Bird's nest being too nourishing might cause internal heat, whereas silver ear fungus provides cooling nourishment and helps with dryness. Dryness and internal heat are like acute and chronic diseases; internal heat comes quickly and goes quickly, but if it persists for too long without relief, it transforms into dryness. Therefore, eating silver ear fungus is most effective.

Ingredients: Dried longan, goji berries, red dates, and silver ear fungus. Boil these ingredients together to create a delicious breast-enhancing snack that can also give your complexion a rosy glow and keep you light and slim.

Black Glutinous Rice Blood-Nourishing Porridge:

Black glutinous rice, longan, and red dates are all well-known blood-nourishing ingredients. Adding highly nutritious yam enhances the qi and blood-nourishing effects significantly. Remember to consume this porridge monthly. Ingredients include black glutinous rice, red dates, longan, yam, and brown sugar. A small reminder: Longan can nourish blood and qi and promote blood circulation, but because it is hard to digest, it’s best to limit consumption to about 7-8 pieces per serving (those with cold stomachs can add 2 slices of red ginseng).

Angelica Sinensis, Red Dates, and Spare Ribs Soup:

This soup helps alleviate seasonal skin dryness and roughness, as well as skin itching. Ingredients: One spare rib, goji berries, 12 red dates, 4 slices of Angelica Sinensis. Parboil the ribs, clean off the blood water, then place them in a clay pot along with goji berries, red dates, Angelica Sinensis, scallions, and ginger slices. Bring to a boil on high heat, then simmer until the ribs are tender. Season with salt and chicken essence before serving.

Oatmeal Beauty and Blood-Nourishing Porridge:

A wise woman once said, "Women should always cherish themselves!" Instead of cherishing oneself constantly, just remembering to do so every now and then is already commendable. Soak walnuts first. Clean the oats and place them in an electric rice cooker with an appropriate amount of water and start cooking! Be mindful of the time! Once the porridge boils, switch to the porridge mode. Add some brown sugar before serving.

Double Red Blood-Nourishing Soup:

Ingredients: 500g of sweet potatoes, 10g of red dates, and an appropriate amount of brown sugar. Add 2000ml of clear water. Cook everything together until the sweet potatoes are cooked, then add brown sugar. This summer dessert can also be made with pumpkin instead of sweet potatoes.

Papaya and Silver Ear Fungus Beauty Soup:

Ingredients: Silver ear fungus, papaya, red dates, rock sugar. After soaking the silver ear fungus, bring it to a boil, then add the red dates and simmer on low heat for half an hour. Finally, add the rock sugar and papaya, bring to a boil again, and serve.

Soybeans, Snow Pear, and Pig Trotter Soup:

Regularly drinking pear soup benefits women. Pears have been praised as the "King of All Fruits" since ancient times. Making soup with snow pears can relieve coughs, moisten the lungs, clear the mind, and beautify the skin. Drinking this snow pear soybean pig trotter soup can visibly smooth the skin and relieve hoarseness and dry mouth. It also lowers blood pressure and nourishes with coolness.

Health and beauty through proper food combinations can lead to improved health and appearance. Regularly consuming such soups can provide auxiliary benefits for nourishing yin, moisturizing, and skincare.
