" This article's beginning → [Abstract] Based on the analysis of the characteristics and business content that differentiate human capital management from traditional personnel management, this article points out that human capital management can no longer adopt the traditional departmental professional personnel management model. Instead, it must explore a human capital management model suitable for the enterprise's own characteristics. Subsequently, according to the features of small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), a three-dimensional human capital management model is proposed, which establishes within the enterprise a comprehensive three-dimensional human capital management system involving decision-making levels, the human capital department, and frontline managers who both divide responsibilities and coordinate with each other.

The transition from traditional personnel management to modern human capital management is a key issue in enterprise management reform in China at this stage. To adapt to this major transformation, the primary challenge facing enterprises is to establish an overall human capital management model suitable for their own characteristics. Only in this way can enterprises systematically and organizationally carry out various aspects of modern human capital management, truly achieving people-oriented practices and fully utilizing the role of human capital in creating wealth, thereby genuinely realizing the shift from personnel management to human capital management.

1. The traditional departmental professional personnel management model cannot meet the requirements of human capital management features and business

The reason why human capital management can no longer continue to use the traditional departmental professional personnel management model lies in the characteristics and business content that differentiate human capital management from traditional personnel management.

1. The characteristics of human capital management determine that enterprises cannot continue to use the traditional departmental professional personnel management model

Human capital management refers to the series of management processes by which an organization scientifically and reasonably updates, configures, utilizes, develops, and incentivizes its human capital to achieve its strategic objectives for a certain period. The object of human capital management is the entire labor resource capital of the enterprise, and the characteristics of human capital management are determined by the characteristics of human capital.

Compared to other material, technology, and financial resources, human capital has the following features: First, human capital has agency. The agency characteristic of human capital is mainly manifested in people's thinking and analytical abilities as well as their subjective needs, which makes human capital management more complex and difficult than any other functional management; Second, human capital has complexity. The complexity of human capital arises from individual differences, which manifest as vast disparities between people in terms of quality, ability, attitude, and performance, further intensifying the complexity and difficulty of human capital management; Third, human capital has sociality. The sociality of human capital is mainly reflected in interpersonal interactions and the resulting intricate relationships. This means that we must consider not only individual traits but also team relationships and their impact on the organization during the human capital management process.

Due to these characteristics of human capital, human capital management differs from other functional management in three major ways compared to Inner Mongolia Xing'an recruitment network: ① People-oriented management. People-oriented management is the most fundamental characteristic of human capital management, reflecting the essence of modern human capital management. The essence of people-oriented management is to place employees at the center, treating people as the most valuable asset of the enterprise, establishing the dominant position of people in management, and focusing the core of enterprise human capital management on motivating the initiative and proactivity of employees. People-oriented management adapts to the agency characteristic of human capital, enabling human capital management to fully reflect the individual characteristics and needs of employees. People-oriented management should be embodied in all aspects and specific business processes of human capital management, such as talent recruitment, configuration, promotion, training, and compensation management, which must fully reflect the principles of people-oriented management; ② Differentiated management. Differentiated management is also a key feature that distinguishes human capital management from other functional management in the enterprise. As mentioned earlier, there are vast differences among enterprise employees in terms of quality, ability, attitude, and performance, which increases the complexity and difficulty of human capital management. This requires us to fully implement differentiated management in our human capital management work. Differentiated management adapts to the complexity characteristic of human capital, and differentiated management should also be reflected in all aspects and specific business processes of human capital management; ③ Team management. Human capital has sociality, mainly manifested in mutual interaction, comparison among employees, and employee demands for fairness, etc., requiring human capital management to focus on stimulating team enthusiasm rather than just individuals or a few talents. Team management should also be reflected in all aspects of human capital management (such as talent recruitment, configuration, promotion, training, compensation management).

In summary, the three major characteristics of human capital management—people-oriented management, differentiated management, and team management—show that human capital management is a systematic project. The tasks of human capital management are challenging and complex, and cannot be achieved by a professional human capital department alone. On one hand, to implement the three major principles of people-oriented management, differentiated management, and team management, frontline managers must directly participate in this systematic project of human capital management because they are on the front lines and have the best understanding of employees' abilities, qualities, work status, and motivational needs, thus they must be endowed with the functions and authority of human capital management; On the other hand, for the three major principles of people-oriented management, differentiated management, and team management to be truly and thoroughly reflected, the decision-making level must also pay high attention to and participate in human capital management, especially by closely linking organizational growth with employee career development and making human capital strategy an important part of organizational strategy. High attention and participation from the decision-making level also provide excellent support for the human capital professional department and frontline managers to fulfill their human capital management responsibilities.
