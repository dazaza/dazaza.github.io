Eat radishes in winter and ginger in summer, and you won't need a doctor to write a prescription.

by uefbp54187 on 2012-02-20 14:57:29

Since ancient times, Chinese medical scientists and the general public have said that "ginger cures all diseases". In summer, bacteria grow and multiply abnormally active, easily contaminating food and causing acute gastroenteritis. However, eating appropriate amounts of ginger or drinking dry ginger tea can play a role in prevention and treatment. In summer, people like to indulge in coolness, enjoying electric fans and air conditioners, which makes it easy to catch colds. Drinking some ginger sugar water at this time will help expel wind-cold from the body.

There are many ways to eat ginger. For example, drinking ginger soup, eating ginger porridge, adding some ginger threads when cooking with hot oil, adding ginger slices when stewing meat or frying fish, and adding a little ginger crumbs when making dumplings or wonton fillings, not only make the taste delicious, but also help refresh the stomach, stimulate appetite, promote digestion, and assist the gastrointestinal tract in absorbing nutrients.

However, since ginger has pharmacological effects, attention should be paid to its usage methods and taboos. The following issues should be noted:

1. Do not peel: Some people like to peel ginger before eating it, but doing so cannot fully utilize the overall efficacy of ginger. Generally, fresh ginger can be washed clean and then cut into strips or slices.

2. Certain disease patients should not consume ginger: Those who are yin-deficient with excessive internal heat, or suffer from boils, carbuncles, pneumonia, lung abscesses, pulmonary tuberculosis, gastric ulcers, cholecystitis, pyelonephritis, diabetes, or hemorrhoids, should not consume ginger for a long time.

3. Use caution when consuming ginger red sugar water: From a therapeutic perspective, ginger red sugar water is only suitable for patients with wind-cold感冒 or those who feel cold and feverish after being caught in the rain. It is not suitable for patients with summer-heat感冒 or wind-heat感冒, nor can it be used to treat heatstroke. Fresh ginger juice can be used to treat vomiting caused by exposure to cold, but it is not suitable for other types of vomiting.

4. Do not eat rotten ginger: Rotten ginger produces a highly toxic substance that can cause liver cell degeneration and necrosis, thereby inducing liver cancer or esophageal cancer. The saying that "rotten ginger doesn't lose its flavor" is incorrect.

5. Eating ginger is not always better in larger quantities: In summer, the weather is hot, and people tend to experience dry mouth, thirst, sore throat, and excessive sweating. Ginger is pungent and warm in nature, belonging to the category of hot foods. According to the principle of "treating heat with cold," it is not advisable to consume too much. A few slices of ginger can be added when cooking or making soup.
