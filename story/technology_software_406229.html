The World Health Organization has announced that approximately 93% of people die from various diseases rather than the natural end of life. Due to limitations in medical technology, many diseases such as diabetes and malignant tumors cannot be completely cured at present. The treatment goal can only be to prevent disease progression, improve patients' quality of life, and extend survival time.

Western medicine's treatment for diabetes mainly focuses on lowering blood sugar levels. If fasting blood glucose is reduced to 7.0 mmol/L and postprandial levels to 10.0 mmol/L, it can be determined that the treatment is qualified. Lowering blood sugar is a confrontational treatment; it cannot fundamentally restore pancreatic function or prevent the death of islet cells. Over time, antidiabetic drugs need to be taken in increasing amounts as blood sugar levels continue to rise.

The essence of Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) theory is about treating the person, not just the disease. This "person" refers to the self-healing functions of the human body. Treating the person means creating conditions to restore cellular function. For example, when skin is cut, new skin grows back, and colds can resolve without medication, indicating that human cells have self-healing abilities. Islet cells also have self-healing capabilities and can be repaired; we just haven't found reliable methods yet.

Unlike antidiabetic drug treatments, the core of the 1+1 Repair Technology is not to lower blood sugar but to repair diseased islet cells and maximize the recovery of damaged pancreatic function, thereby inhibiting islet cell death and the occurrence of complications.
