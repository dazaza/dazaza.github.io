National People's Congress representative Zhou Hongyu suggests that the National Administration of Safety Supervision take the lead; school buses should enjoy certain privileges during operation.

Yesterday, Zhou Hongyu, a national people's congress representative and professor at Central China Normal University, stated that the "National School Bus Safety Project" should be led by the State Administration of Work Safety, with a special coordinating body established to take charge. During their operation, school buses should enjoy certain privileges.

The State Administration of Work Safety should take the lead

During this year's session of the National People's Congress, Zhou Hongyu submitted a proposal titled "On Implementing the School Bus Safety Project," suggesting the nationwide promotion of the "School Bus Safety Project." He suggested that a dedicated coordinating body should be responsible for the "National School Bus Safety Project," with the State Administration of Work Safety taking the lead and the Ministry of Public Security, Ministry of Education, Ministry of Transport, General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine, Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, Ministry of Finance, and Ministry of Justice as supporting departments. This specialized committee could use the "Safety Technical Conditions for Dedicated Elementary School Buses" implemented last year as a basis to formulate various technical standards suitable for primary and secondary school students' needs and establish school bus production access systems, manufacturing buses that meet standard requirements.

Government guidance combined with market operations

He suggested that the "National School Bus Safety Project" rely on both government guidance and market operations. The state would subsidize student bus fees, with local government finances sharing the burden according to proportions, such as eastern developed regions solving it themselves, central moderately developed regions sharing costs equally between central and local governments, and western underdeveloped regions having 70% covered by the central government and 30% by local governments. Policy preferences would be given to school bus manufacturers and operators, such as tax reductions or exemptions for major components of school buses and partial income tax reductions for school bus operations.

Uniform purchase by the state

Zhou Hongyu indicated that school buses would be uniformly purchased by the state, with routes agreed upon by local public security, education departments, and transport companies through bidding processes, with the state providing fiscal subsidies. Detailed procedures and norms for safe school bus operations, as well as maintenance and repair standards, would be supervised by the public security department.

Privileges during operation

He also advocated that school buses enjoy certain privileges during operation, such as all vehicles stopping when a school bus is picking up or dropping off passengers at the roadside while maintaining a safe distance. Gradually, citizens should recognize school buses as equal to fire trucks and ambulances, even enjoying priority passage rights, voluntarily yielding to school buses.

Dialogue: "Government-funded school buses focus on rural areas"

Person: He Yan Guang

Identity: Renowned photojournalist who has encountered overcrowded school buses multiple times during interviews.

On the night of the Gansu school bus accident, renowned photojournalist He Yan Guang posted via Weibo a photograph of an overloaded school bus taken last year in Yunnan, drawing attention.

Beijing News: Could you talk about the situation when you took this photograph?

He Yan Guang: On November 11 last year, in Eryuan County, Yunnan, we were interviewing village doctors at a health center. Next to the health center was a kindergarten with dozens of children. It happened to be dismissal time, and the children were all squeezing into one vehicle, so I took this photograph.

Beijing News: What investigations have you conducted regarding school bus issues?

He Yan Guang: In my residential complex, there are several international schools, and I often see their school buses. I paid attention to the school bus of a Canadian international school, which used to be just a regular color but is now bright yellow, very internationalized, and operates very standardly, such as requiring the child to be handed over to the parent. If the child's home is across the street, the driver will turn around to deliver the child to the parent's side, not letting the parent and child cross the road.

Beijing News: What suggestions do you have regarding school bus issues?

He Yan Guang: The government can completely manage (school buses), providing strong support in terms of funds and policies. School bus fees should not be passed on to schools and parents but should be borne by the government. Especially rural areas should be emphasized, as they are remote with inconvenient transportation and weak educational investment. Some netizens want to fundraise and provide assistance through public welfare actions, but I don't quite agree. What the government should do should not be replaced by public welfare.

"School bus safety supervision should be clearly defined by legislation"

Person: Yuan Gui Lin

Identity: Professor at the Education Department of Beijing Normal University

Beijing News: Can you discuss the issue of government funding for purchasing school buses?

Yuan Gui Lin: Now many children travel far to school and need to take transportation to return home. This is the result of school layout adjustments and mergers. The government is unwilling to set up so many schools because setting up more schools requires more spending, and the government wants to spend less on education, or even no money on education.

Purchasing and operating school buses require funds, and they lack sufficient funds for school bus investments. During the compulsory education stage, financial security is mainly implemented at the county level, and in many counties in central and western regions, most of the county-level finances are primarily used for compulsory education.

Beijing News: Which department should be held accountable for school bus accidents?

Yuan Gui Lin: There is no law that clearly states where these responsibilities lie, lacking any legal basis. Currently, school bus management is jointly managed and jointly responsible, but joint management and joint responsibility mean no one manages, no one takes responsibility, lacking a responsible entity. There is no clear division of responsibilities among the public security, education, and transportation departments regarding which department is responsible for school buses.

Beijing News: Is school bus safety mentioned in relevant laws and regulations?

Yuan Gui Lin: Both the "Compulsory Education Law" and the "National Medium- and Long-Term Education Reform and Development Plan" mention school safety but do not mention the word "school bus." "School safety" refers to safety within the campus, but school bus operations occur outside the campus, which is not mentioned, and it shouldn't be. The education department does not know if it is their responsibility because there is no legal basis, and they cannot find the responsible entity.

If an accident occurs on the road, it is handled as an ordinary traffic accident, which is unfair to the children. If ordinary traffic accident rules are applied to school bus accidents, I believe this is the lowest standard. School bus accidents frequently occur, yet the accountability object is unclear, and this current situation should change, with relevant legislation reflecting this.
