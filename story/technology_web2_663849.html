What criteria should we use to build a website?

by hebei5602i on 2012-02-12 17:35:29

I. Website Style/Creativity

Style is abstract and refers to the overall impression a site gives to its visitors. The overall image includes page layout, color, font, browsing method, etc. Every website leaves different impressions on people. What we need to do here is to create a unique style for the website based on its positioning. In addition, we also need to maintain consistency in style, which is no different from traditional printed publications. All images, text, including background colors, dividing lines, fonts, titles, footnotes, etc., on the web pages should have a unified style throughout the entire site. This way, users will feel comfortable and smooth, and they will leave with an impression of "very professional."

II. Website LOGO

Logo, by definition, is the symbol of the site. The most important aspect of a logo is to convey the website's positioning and business philosophy in a graphical manner, while also making it easy for people to identify. Generally, there are three approaches to designing a website logo: 1. Directly use the website URL as the logo. 2. Design the logo based on the characteristics of the products/services provided by the website. 3. Feature the transmission of the website operator's business philosophy.

III. Visual Flow

When people read certain information, their vision always has a natural flow habit, looking at things in a specific order: first this, then that, and so on. Psychological research shows that the general browsing habit is from top to bottom, from left to right. Similarly, the left side of a plane feels more relaxed, while the right side feels more stable. Therefore, the visual impact on the upper part of the plane is stronger than the lower part, and the left side is stronger than the right side. Thus, the upper and middle-upper parts of the plane are called the "optimal visual field," which is the best place. In web design, some prominent or recommended information is usually placed in this position. Of course, this visual flow is just a feeling, not a fixed formula. As long as it conforms to people's psychological sequence and logical sequence, it can be used more flexibly in web design. Flexible and reasonable application directly affects the accuracy and effectiveness of conveying information.

IV. Web Page Framework and Layout

Web page layouts can generally be divided into "Square" type, corner type, "T" type, "L" type, comprehensive framework type, Flash type, and variable type. Actually, when we are doing design, we don't overly consider what form to use. For example, if we see a round object on a piece of paper, it's easy to associate it with the sun, while others might think of the moon, etc... These are all form metaphors. We believe the most important thing is to capture the client's needs, grasp the website's positioning, and create a reasonable framework layout.
