Shandong Haihua: The leading company in basic chemical industry benefits from the price increase expectation＿Stock Forum

by fj0953210 on 2009-12-05 21:36:33

On Friday, the stock index experienced significant fluctuations. In contrast to the strengthening of large-cap blue-chip stocks, theme stocks began to plummet significantly. Regardless of how the "2-8" phenomenon in the market plays out, the concept of price increases will remain the eternal theme throughout the fourth quarter's market trend.

This week, under the expectation of price hikes, the chemical sector surged significantly. Among them, United Chemicals achieved continuous daily limits in the first half of the week due to institutional fund involvement. According to a report by Shanghai Securities News, "The chemical industry, which is still in the early stages of recovery, has been generally favored by institutions recently. The entire chemical sector on the A-share market has remained strong. Based on the expectation of an impending nitric acid bull market, United Chemicals, which mainly produces concentrated nitric acid, ammonium nitrate, and liquid ammonia, repeatedly hit the limit. Bullish institutions snapped up 2 billion yuan worth of shares, causing this chemical dark horse's stock price to soar 36.4% within four days. The enthusiasm of institutions chasing higher prices is quite rare in the recent market."

Similarly, in the chemical sector, Shandong Haihua also possesses the nitric acid and ammonium nitrate concept. Shandong Haihua is one of the world's largest nitrate manufacturers. The 100,000-ton/year concentrated nitric acid project of Shandong Haihua's Hualong Nitrammonium Co., Ltd., has been completed and put into operation. Not long after its commissioning, the quality of dilute nitric acid and concentrated nitric acid reached the requirements of GB/T 337.2 and GB/T 337.1 respectively, setting a new record among similar projects nationwide. As a leading basic chemical raw material enterprise with significant influence worldwide, Shandong Haihua has formed production capacities including 3 million tons of soda ash, 220,000 tons of calcium chloride, 72,000 tons of melamine, 15,000 tons of bromine, 160,000 tons of sodium nitrate and sodium nitrite, 100,000 tons of caustic soda, and 100,000 tons of PVC. Among these, the production capacities of soda ash and sodium nitrate/sodium nitrite rank first globally, calcium chloride and melamine rank first in Asia, while bromine and bromides have the highest production capacity in China. Entering a prosperous cycle in the chemical industry has become a consensus among institutions, and the chemical sector became one of the key sectors for funds, including China Asset Management (Huo Xing), to build positions in the third quarter.

In the nitric acid sector, United Chemicals' share price soared dramatically under the favor of institutional funds. From November 27 to December 3, institutions appeared on the buying list of United Chemicals' trading chart as many as 11 times, only twice on the selling list, accumulating a total purchase of 2 billion yuan and sales of merely 210.9 million yuan. The net inflow in institutional seats over three days reached 1.8 billion yuan, and the mass buying by institutions dominated the emergence of the biggest dark horse in this wave of chemical stock fever. Shandong Haihua (000822) (enter the stock forum, new version of the market conditions), as one of the world's largest nitrate manufacturers, has an annual production capacity of 100,000 tons of concentrated nitric acid. The stock shows clear signs of breaking through, and as one of the leading stocks in the basic chemical sector, it will substantially benefit from the arrival of the chemical industry’s prosperous cycle.

The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. This order is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is six digits. The stock code and abbreviation must match when placing an order. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks bought or sold: The quantity of stocks ordered to buy must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks sold does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four types of orders: counter submission orders, telephone automatic orders, computer automatic orders, and remote terminal orders.
