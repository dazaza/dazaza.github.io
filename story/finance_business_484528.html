Dear partners of all agents,

First of all, congratulations on your resilience in the ceramics industry after so many years of fierce洗礼 (baptism). As a poet would say: suddenly looking back, I can still find your heavy shadow in the place where the lights fade.

Every fighter in the business tide is a traveler, moving forward every day for their own dreams. In this journey, I have recorded some images and thoughts about the characteristics of the ceramics industry, and I would like to discuss them with you all.

Do not blindly believe in so-called "masters." Terms like "breaking the deadlock," "winning marketing," "the remaining ones are kings," "decisive victory at the terminal," "execution power," etc., my conclusion is: the words are very fancy, but the content is shocking. A fact we cannot ignore is: have they been to ceramic terminals? Have they had conversations and exchanges with ceramic terminal agents and salespeople? Do they truly understand the problems existing in the ceramic terminal channel market? Have they actually participated in a ceramic industry holiday promotion? They simply take PPT files from fast-moving consumer goods industries, home appliances industries, etc., and tell you that doing it this way is correct. Kindly reminding some ceramic companies and agents, don't become obsessed or even worship these "masters." If you have the time, you might as well visit more agents who operate the same brand as you, see how others handle price differentiation, personnel management, employee motivation, and how they open up home decoration channels, etc. Those are the real "masters." The word "solidify" has always been one of my favorite terms. Borrowing this, I also want to send a message to some so-called masters still lingering in the ceramics industry: "Marketing masters, your mom calls you home for dinner!"

Clarifying three questions—"what do you have," "what do you want," and "what are you willing to give up"—will make your work twice as effective or determine how far you can go in the ceramics business circle. "What do you have": Where is your advantage channel? Where is the potential growth channel for you? What is your store location advantage? What is your decoration quality advantage? What is your team's competitiveness? What is your team's creativity advantage? Are they all united with you?... "What do you want": How high do you want to bring the brand you operate? What position do you want to achieve in the local ceramics industry business circle? What kind of recognition do you want to gain? Do you want long-term recognition across the entire ceramics industry or short-term operational profits? Are you prepared to treat the ceramics industry as a lifelong business or as a speculator?... "What are you willing to give up": Are you willing to give up channels that you are not good at? Are you willing to abandon a certain brand due to overlapping product strategies and brand strategies? Are you willing to abandon stores with no advantages? Are you willing to abandon your rigid salary system and implement an effective salary incentive system so that every loyal employee can buy a car and live in their own house? Are you willing to give up the intention to buy a BMW and instead renovate your store? Are you willing to completely follow the ideas of the manufacturer's designer to decorate your store? Are you willing to give up a few days of business and travel with your employees? Courage to give up effectively will allow you to win decisively; otherwise, you'll only have short-sightedness and become a victim of industry reshuffling.

Remember, Foshan is your forever home. Don't always focus solely on hard work. Why not occasionally return to your "home"? Most of the industry information obtained by some agents comes from their own agency brands.
