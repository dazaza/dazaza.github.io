1. Clothing

First trick: Buy discounted clothes during seasonal sales.

All girls know this trick, but there are certain things to keep in mind when buying discounted clothes.

First is timing. You need to do market research to know which days are the discount season, and you should roughly have an idea in your mind. Don't miss it.

If you like newer styles, you should buy at the beginning of the discount season. Otherwise, the good clothes will all be sold out. On the other hand, if you prefer cheaper options, go towards the end of the discount season; that’s when prices are the lowest.

Second is location. During the discount season, it's best not to go to large commercial centers like Huaihai Road or Xujiahui. There are too many people, the shopping environment is poor, the service attitude of the staff may not be great, and most importantly, many attractive clothes have already been sold out.

I once had an experience where I liked a pair of discounted boots. In several malls on Huaihai Road, my size was no longer available. A few days later, I found them in a mall in Hongqiao. Moreover, there were fewer people, and the service staff was particularly friendly.

Second trick: Hunt for foreign trade stores.

My chubby husband has one advantage: he loves browsing small shops. He gets dizzy in big malls because they are too crowded and chaotic with too many people. But small shops are different, especially in the small streets in downtown Shanghai. They are quiet, with fewer pedestrians, and lined with charming little houses and plane trees. You can walk leisurely, chatting along the way.

Small shops can be divided into two types.

One is avant-garde shops, which specialize in trendy items (there are many such shops along Huaihai Road in Shanghai). Generally, their prices won't be cheap. If you want to buy something from these shops, aim for high-quality pieces and bargain hard. This piece of clothing should be the highlight of your entire outfit. If you can find a shop with relatively better value-for-money and a friendly owner, take note and visit often to explore more.

The other type is inconspicuous small shops. These shops don’t have fancy decorations and might even look shabby on the outside. However, they often hide versatile basic items, and the prices are super cheap. For example, single-color pencil skirts (also known as step skirts), jeans, and single-color long pants.

In short, the key to the second trick is to adopt a thorough search strategy. Take out a Shanghai map, find some promising small streets in the city center, and browse them one by one.

Third trick: Visit foreign trade warehouses.

Foreign trade warehouses are the latest innovation in Shanghai. Some bosses travel to foreign trade factories around Shanghai, buy their inventory goods by weight, and then sell them piece by piece in a store.

The quality of the items is generally good, though the styles may vary.

These warehouses are mostly concentrated in suburban residential areas. Sometimes the prices are really super cheap! I once bought Uniqlo pants for 20 yuan each. Some unbranded clothes are even 5 yuan or 10 yuan per piece.

Fourth trick: Buy fabric and have clothes custom-made.

Shanghai has many light textile markets that sell fabrics, and they also have tailors. You can buy their fabric and have them make clothes for you. One of my colleagues, who is on the shorter side and can't find suitable sizes, told me this trick.

Many of the beautiful dresses she wears are made there. She also mentioned that if you like a design from a magazine, you can bring the picture and have them make it according to the design. Of course, the design shouldn't be too bizarre.
