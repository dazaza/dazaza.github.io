Taobao draws a national "insomnia map": Shanghai is the place where people are most likely to lose sleep

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 16:29:44

"Every day, I go to bed around 11 o'clock, then watch videos and browse online stores on my phone. I don't feel sleepy until around 1 o'clock." Mr. Ding, a 30-year-old white-collar worker who works in a commercial building on Moganshan Road in Hangzhou, complained. In fact, more and more people are beginning to have trouble sleeping. "People who sleep are all similar, but those who can't sleep have their own reasons," the owners of Taobao shops take advantage of this trend to sell "sleep aid" products.

Yesterday, a set of data published by Taobao was also quite interesting. People who can't sleep have very obvious regional characteristics. In the top 5 cities with the highest per capita purchases of eye masks and earplugs in the last 5 months, the four first-tier cities of Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen all made the list. Hangzhou is the only non-first-tier city in the top five, ranking third.

The most active "insomniacs":

23 million people shop online at midnight

"At this point, is anyone still awake?" Nowadays, such midnight insomnia-related Weibo posts are becoming more common. "Insomniacs" instantly transform into "night shoppers," using their mobile phones to shop on Taobao while lying in bed. The passion for shopping does not diminish due to the quietness of the night; instead, late at night becomes one of the times with a high rate of mobile online shopping orders.

"In such hot weather, I definitely won't go out shopping during the day. When I can't sleep at night, I use that time to shop." Miss Li from Hangzhou is a typical night owl. She said she often doesn't feel sleepy after 12 o'clock, so she opens her mobile phone and browses Taobao. "Yesterday, I bought two pairs of sandals and one dress within an hour." Finally, with the satisfaction of shopping, she finally fell asleep. However, her money flows out every day.

There are quite a few such "night shoppers." According to data published by Taobao, in 2012, 22.832 million people shopped on Taobao between 11 PM and 5 AM. Among them, many men also stayed up late shopping on Taobao, accounting for 48% of the total, while women accounted for 52%.

The most insomniac province:

Jiangsu, Guangdong, Zhejiang

What to do if you really can't sleep? More and more people are starting to rely on external tools like earplugs and eye masks to fall asleep. Clever online store owners have obviously already spotted the business opportunity. A search for earplugs on Taobao revealed more than 200,000 items for sale, and nearly 90,000 eye masks.

The "sleep aid market" is quite promising. Just the product of eye masks comes in various types such as 3D, cartoon patterns, ice packs suitable for summer, silk eye masks, bamboo charcoal eye masks, etc. Since summer began, ice pack eye masks have become very popular. I saw that the monthly transaction volume of a single item in a Taobao store exceeded ten thousand pieces.

Many sellers are also making great efforts with pillows, launching calming lavender pillows, buckwheat pillows, etc. Even foods like millet and jujubes have been labeled as promoting sleep.

The "insomnia map" shows that according to Taobao's transaction data over the past 5 months, from the proportion of people buying earplugs and eye masks among the total population of the city, all the first-tier cities of Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen made the list in the top five. Hangzhou, which ranks third, is the only non-first-tier city among them.

Top 10 cities for purchasing earplugs: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chongqing, Ningbo.

Top 10 cities for purchasing eye masks: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, Guangzhou, Chongqing, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chengdu.

Shanghai can be called the most severely insomniac city. Jiangsu, Guangdong, and Zhejiang are the three provinces with the worst sleep.

"The vicious cycle of 'can't sleep at night, can't wake up in the morning.' Cities where people have more trouble sleeping at night are also the cities where people find it hardest to wake up in the morning. In the past 5 months, the top five cities in terms of total population purchasing alarm clocks are the same: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, and Guangzhou."

"I have to wear earplugs every night to fall asleep, but then I can't hear the alarm on my phone in the morning. After setting my phone alarm to vibrate, I'm worried it won't be powerful enough to wake me up, so I bought another vibrating alarm clock." Xiao Mao's "sleep induction" and "wake-up" equipment has become essential for many insomniacs.

The most helpless reason for buying earplugs:

Husband snores

So, who are the insomniacs? Online store owners know best. "The number of people who need earplugs to sleep increases every year, there are more consulting customers, and the age range is getting wider. Basically, students and urban white-collar workers account for 90% of total sales," said the owner of a store selling earplugs.

Indeed, the store's flagship product - a 19-yuan earplug - sold 19,000 pieces in just one month.

Why can't everyone sleep? There are actually many reasons. It is understood that students who buy eye masks and earplugs may live in school dormitories. Mobile devices are now very common among students. After bedtime, some classmates may still be playing games. To avoid being disturbed, they simply "seal themselves off."

White-collar workers, on the other hand, have relatively high work stress during the day, which affects their nighttime sleep. Some buyers even humorously state their reason for buying earplugs as "my husband snores too loudly."

Taobao data shows that most of the buyers of earplugs and eye masks are from the "post-90s" and "post-80s" generations. For example, in the case of eye mask sales over the past 5 months, the "post-90s" are the largest demand group, with 36% of eye masks purchased by people aged 18-24. Additionally, people aged 25-29 make up more than 30% of the buyers.

From the purchase ratio of various products, men are more likely to have trouble sleeping than women, with about 20% more male insomniacs than female.
