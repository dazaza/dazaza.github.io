One, Determine Surface or Reverse Printing

Before printing, it is necessary to determine whether the printing is surface or reverse printing. The material used should also be determined as either surface or reverse printing, including film, plate cylinder, and ink. The most critical part is the ink because the differences between types of ink are significant, and each type requires different diluents. Using the wrong diluent can lead to adverse consequences or economic losses. There are many types of inks, such as benzene-soluble, alcohol-soluble, and other specialized ones. Choose the appropriate ink and diluent based on the determined sample; the film should also be determined according to its type, such as BOPP, PET, PA, PE, PVC, etc. During the plate-making process, the plate manufacturer determines the plate-making and tension requirements. Therefore, determining whether it is surface or reverse printing before printing is crucial.

Two, Temperature, Humidity, and Dryness Are Very Important

In plastic gravure printing, many manufacturers do not pay enough attention to the temperature, humidity, and dryness of the machine, failing to take timely effective measures, resulting in many problems by ignoring the temperature and humidity requirements of the printing machine. If the temperature of the printing machine is too high and the humidity is too large, problems such as tackiness, fading, color transfer, sticking, poor adhesion, ink decomposition, inability to print colors, etc., may occur. If it is too dry, fast machine speed may generate static electricity, causing the ink to move on the film; slow machine speed may result in dull prints, dried plates, blocked plates, etc. Generally, the machine temperature should be controlled between 18°C-25°C, and the humidity should be maintained at 55%-75% for optimal results, especially when making high-end steaming packages and pharmaceutical packages, where environmental requirements are more stringent. A slight oversight can cause serious losses to the enterprise.

Three, Carefully Balance the Plate Cylinder

Generally speaking, the balance of the printing plate cylinder depends on the printing machine and the plate-making cylinder. However, when installing the plate cylinder, it is essential to set the position of each color and the balance on both sides to achieve the best balance. The positioning of the plate cylinder should be determined according to the alignment lines and length of the plate cylinder. For keyless plate cylinders, the screws must be tightened. When installing the plate cylinder, care must be taken not to damage it, avoiding unnecessary trouble and loss.

Four, Master the Doctor Blade Angle Skillfully

The doctor blade plays a very important role in the entire gravure machine, scraping off excess ink from the plate cylinder. Therefore, when installing the doctor blade, it is necessary to maintain good balance and adjust the angle between the doctor blade and the plate cylinder. Generally, the angle between the doctor blade and the plate cylinder should be between 45°-65°, further adjusted based on the depth of the plate's screen cells and machine speed. During adjustment, ensure that the distance between the doctor blade and the pressure roller matches the machine speed. At the same time, apply appropriate pressure so that the doctor blade penetrates the ink layer and contacts the plate cylinder, performing the ink-scraping function. The size of the doctor blade pressure can be adjusted based on the depth of the screen cells on the plate, the sequence of overprinting, etc. Preventing doctor blade streaks is also important; generally, hard blades tend to produce more streaks than soft blades. The hardness of the blade should be determined based on the hardness of the plate cylinder.

Five, Adjust Ink Viscosity at Any Time

During the printing process, ink needs to be diluted with a solvent to reach the required working viscosity. First, adjust the ink viscosity and drying speed according to the machine speed. High machine speed requires fast-drying solvents, while slow machine speed requires slow-drying solvents. Second, during normal printing, due to the evaporation of the solvent, more solvent needs to be added. When adding solvent, it is best to mix it with the ink and add it to the ink fountain together, ensuring consistency in the printed items and reducing waste rates. Since high ink viscosity corresponds to higher color concentration and low viscosity to lower color concentration, changes in ink viscosity can directly affect the printed color. Therefore, adjusting the ink viscosity is very important.

Six, Remember the Pressure of the Rubber Roller

The impression rubber roller is a key component that determines printing quality. Poor-quality rubber rollers make it difficult to produce good prints. If the rubber roller is uneven, problems such as missed prints and uneven coloring may occur. Excessive pressure on the rubber roller can cause ghosting (double eyelid), unclear layers, lack of gloss, etc. Insufficient pressure may cause shallow screen versions to fail to color properly and text parts to appear unclear. The softness and hardness of the rubber roller should be determined based on the thickness of the printed film and the depth of the plate cylinder. The pressure requirement for the rubber roller is generally determined by the ability to print small characters clearly or allow shallow screens to color properly. In high-end printing machines with pressure gauges, the pressure should be controlled within 2-3 units.

Seven, Coordinate Printing Speed with Drying Boxes Well

The drying system determines the printing speed of the gravure machine. From low-end to high-end printing machines, their drying systems differ. From single drying boxes to double drying boxes and multifunctional drying boxes and tunnels. The main purposes are to increase the printing machine speed and thoroughly dry the printed pattern, enhancing the adhesion of the ink on the film and allowing the ink's solvent to dry completely. Prevent false drying, which causes tackiness or odors after bagging. Therefore, the drying system should be well-coordinated with the printing machine speed, adjusting the drying box temperature and airflow based on the printed area size. The drying box temperature should also be determined based on the material of the printed substrate. For example, the drying box temperature for printing OPP film differs from that for PET film.

Eight, Neglecting Tension Causes Color Deviation

The performance of the gravure machine largely depends on the tension. Tension is automatically or manually adjusted by the tension control roller. To facilitate printing and increase the printing machine speed, manufacturers install magnetic powder tension controls on both the upper and lower films for automatic control. Some high-end printing machines install computer tracking and automatic control systems for each color to control tension and align colors. For printing machines without computer-controlled color matching, the tension of each color must be manually adjusted. Adjustments should be made based on the machine speed and the size of the tension, ensuring accurate color alignment and satisfactory printing. It is evident that the accuracy of color alignment largely depends on tension control, requiring strict attention to tension.

In summary, to print well, one should master the above eight key factors, ensuring that no step is overlooked. Adjust these elements based on your own printing machine's performance.
