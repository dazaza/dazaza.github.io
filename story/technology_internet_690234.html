I. Grasping the Key Points of Product Presentation

Customers are concerned about fundamental benefits when purchasing any product, and these benefits have a consistent direction. For example, when customers choose freight transportation services, they are most concerned with whether the goods can be safely and accurately delivered to the destination. Therefore, the sales representatives in the transportation industry must focus their presentation on safety and accuracy. If they can address the customer's problems and doubts in this regard, they will surely win over the customer.

Each industry has some key selling points that resonate most with customers. Following these points will yield twice the result with half the effort. Below are some examples for reference:

1. Real Estate Sales Focus

- Investment: Purchasing a house can preserve and increase value.

- Convenience: Ease of commuting, schooling, and shopping.

- Quality of living: Fresh air, quiet environment.

- Safety: Security facilities, building management personnel configuration, and a certain level of residents.

- Social status: The neighborhood is inhabited by political and business elites, representing personal social status.

Although individuals may have different motives for buying a house, such as convenience for work or seeking a tasteful living environment, the key points of the sales presentation still revolve around the combination of the aforementioned elements.

2. Toy Sales Focus

- Educational: Should possess a certain educational enlightenment.

- Safety: Will not cause accidental harm to children.

- Fun: Must be enjoyable to play with for a long time.

Even though the toy market relies on TV cartoons and comic books to promote sales, influencing children’s choices, if parents are buying toys for their children, the three sales focuses mentioned above still resonate most with parents.

3. Production Equipment Sales Focus

- Productivity: The purchase of production equipment is a rational act; the level of productivity is the key factor in selection.

- Return on Investment: Even if productivity is high, if market demand isn’t large enough, it will affect the return on investment. Therefore, the height and risk of investment returns are important indicators.

- Stability: Line supervisors are most concerned with the stability of production equipment because they are responsible for daily output. Instability in production equipment directly impacts their performance.

4. Office Machine Sales Focus

- Operability: Whether it is convenient to operate and whether it requires a specialist are all key points affecting office efficiency.

- Size: With the high cost of office rent, almost every office lacks sufficient space. Therefore, overly large office machines will not be welcomed.

- Contribution to Rationalization: Office machines aim to improve the efficiency of official document processing and promote rationalization. Thus, efficiency and rationality are the starting points for office machines.

- Function, Price, and Practicality: Although having many functions is a benefit, too many functions that aren't used only add costs, turning the benefit point into a weakness.

For other products like cosmetics, health supplements, beauty and fitness, the main appeal lies in painting a hopeful vision to move the customer. In contrast, products like building materials, computers, measuring instruments, and molds focus more on providing adequate consulting services to solve customer problems.

Grasping the big picture allows you to quickly identify the key points and know how to prepare. For instance, there are no less than a thousand types of building materials. To meet customer needs and solve their problems, sales representatives must enhance their knowledge of various building materials and keep up with their evolution. Life insurance sales representatives must have a compassionate heart and take the initiative to care about people around them. Understanding the big picture and preparing accordingly enhances your sales ability subtly.

II. Convincing Your Customers Through Evidence

How do you make your customers believe what you say? Provide evidence! Evidence is the most powerful tool for persuasion. How do sales representatives provide evidence to convince potential customers? You can let customers feel the evidence through physical and sensory experiences. A car sales representative was recommending a luxury car to a client. He guided the client to view the car from different angles, allowing the potential customer to visually confirm the grandeur of the car's design. He invited the client to sit inside the car to experience its spaciousness, comfort, and luxury. He also showed the client signed purchase contracts from several well-known business figures. As a result, they began discussing the price of the car and the delivery procedures. Before long, the potential customer signed a contract for a nearly 1.2 million yuan car.

This case might seem incredible or purely luck-based, but I want to tell you that even if it were luck, this car sales representative did at least two things right: First, he fully grasped the needs of the potential customer. He knew that the potential customer didn't drive himself but had a dedicated private driver, so he wasn't very familiar with cars. The client's need was simply for "grandeur." Therefore, the sales representative focused solely on this selling point. Second, he proved that the recommended car could meet the customer's needs. For example, he guided the customer to look at the car's exterior from different angles, showing how it looked majestic. He let the potential customer personally experience the car's comfort, spaciousness, and luxury. He also showed purchase contracts from well-known business figures, proving that this car was the best choice for people of status, thus adequately proving the car's exceptional value.

From the above case, we can see that convincing customers through evidence involves only two steps:

1. Identify the selling points that satisfy customer needs.

In fact, regardless of whether your sales process is long or short, the amount of time spent, the real reason for closing a deal always comes down to a few points. Customers absolutely do not buy because of all the selling points of your product, nor do they refuse to buy because your selling points are fewer than others by one or two. The true focus is on whether one or two of your selling points can be sufficiently proven to meet their needs.

2. Prepare evidence targeting the selling points.

Since the selling points that satisfy potential customers vary, you must find the best way to prove that each selling point is factual.

There are many ways to provide evidence, and here are nine methods for your reference:

(1) Physical demonstration

Physical demonstration is the best proof method. All the selling points of the product can be proven through physical demonstration.

(2) Expert testimony

You can collect expert opinions to support your claims. For example, ergonomic chairs prevent spine curvature caused by poor posture.

(3) Visual proof

Photos, pictures, and product catalogs all have visual proof effects.

(4) Recommendation letters

Recommendation letters from other well-known customers are highly persuasive.

(5) Guarantees

Guarantees can be divided into two categories: one provided by the company to the customer, such as free maintenance for one year; another for quality assurance, such as obtaining ISO9000 quality certification.

(6) Customer thank-you letters

Some customers write letters expressing gratitude for the company's service or help in solving special problems, and these thank-you letters are effective proof tools.

(7) Statistics and comparative data

Mathematical statistical data and comparative data with competitors effectively support your statements.

(8) Success cases

You can provide potential customers with some successful sales cases, proving that your product is popular and providing them with verification information.

(9) Public reports

Newspapers, magazines, and other publications featuring reports about the company and products can serve as proof material, instilling trust in potential customers.

In summary, verifying your selling points to potential customers is an indispensable step in the sales activity. Therefore, you must prepare the most advantageous proof methods in advance.
