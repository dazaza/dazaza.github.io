There are many online software applications on the Internet, and when it comes to online software, one cannot fail to mention online operating systems (WebOS). Compared to individual online software, WebOS offers more comprehensive functionality with all features browser-based (if MSN is blocked in your location, you might try using WebOS to log in). Some people believe that webOS will become mainstream in the future because, just like online software, you can connect to your work platform and file storage from anywhere. Here, we introduce more than 20 WebOS options, quite a few of which offer Chinese interfaces. (From cnBeta)
