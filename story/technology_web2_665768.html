2012 Cultural Relics and Art Auction Market: Passion Returns to Rationality

All art auctions in 2011 have ended, presenting a situation of first rising and then declining throughout the year. The curtain of the spring auction in 2012 is about to open, how will the cultural relics and artworks market perform?

Soaring High

Since being affected by the financial crisis from the second half of 2008 to the first half of 2009, Chinese cultural relics and artworks auctions started to grow continuously from the autumn auction in 2009. By the spring auction in 2011, after four seasons, each season's growth rate was more than doubled. The spring auction in 2011 was another historical peak after the autumn auction in 2007. The performance of major auction companies all set new historical records, with a total turnover of 20.509 billion yuan for ten companies.

In 2011, Chinese calligraphy and painting still occupied an absolute share of the entire market. Among the 348 pieces of Chinese calligraphy and painting auction items over 10 million yuan from ten companies, there were 207 pieces of modern and contemporary calligraphy and painting works, with a total transaction amount of 5.749 billion yuan. Among these, Zhang Daqian, Qi Baishi, Xu Beihong, Li Keran, Fu Baoshi, Lu Yanshao, and Huang Zhou stood out, accounting for the majority.

In 2012, the absolute market position and high growth rate of Chinese calligraphy and painting will not change, mainly due to the following reasons: First, Chinese calligraphy and painting are the most important traditional artworks in our country, with a large number of surviving pieces, wide recognition of famous artists across generations, and high artistic value; Second, compared to artifacts, the trading market of Chinese calligraphy and painting is concentrated domestically; Third, compared to porcelain or miscellaneous artworks, Chinese calligraphy and painting are easier to appreciate, with a broad audience base, and new buyers usually start from Chinese calligraphy and painting.

In 2011, the ten companies held 52 special auctions for ceramics, jade, and treasures, with 4,396 pieces (sets) sold, accounting for 7.71% of the total number of auction items, and a transaction amount of 4.371 billion yuan, accounting for 11.75% of the total market. Among these, including China Guardian's "Shenden Mingdao Wutaishan Ren Collection of Qing Daoguang Imperial Porcelain", "Dongbozhai Collection of Porcelain", "Royal Prosperity Court Imperial Porcelain Essence"; Beijing Poly's "Overseas Collection of Song, Yuan, Ming, and Qing Jade", "Tianhan Yaoqiong Overseas Collection of Chinese Jade Carving Art Masterpieces", "Great Ming • Ge Gu"; Xiling Seal Engravers' Society's "Chinese Contemporary Jade Masterpieces" etc., all achieved remarkable results. It is estimated that in 2012, bronze ware, porcelain, and other exquisite cultural relics will still be the main focus pursued by collectors besides calligraphy and painting.

Furthermore, on various auction stages, categories such as furniture, guqin, classical furniture, philatelic coins, purple sand tea wares, etc., have all made breakthrough developments. Categories that used to only fill numbers in certain special auctions now shine as highlight special auctions, and some items that were previously unseen or rarely included also frequently appear in the auctions of various companies.

Steady Transactions

In recent years' cultural relics and artworks auction markets, masterpieces and special auctions have always been "hard currencies", with strong anti-fall capabilities. In the spring auction of 2011, the number of auction items sold by ten auction companies exceeding one million yuan reached as many as 5,726 pieces, increasing by 2,260 pieces compared to 2010. Among these 5,726 pieces, 533 exceeded 10 million yuan, increasing by 188 pieces compared to 2010; 45 exceeded 50 million yuan, increasing by 11 pieces compared to 2010; 13 exceeded 100 million yuan, increasing by 2 pieces compared to 2010. This was the year with the largest number of high-end artworks in the history of China's cultural relics and artworks auction market in 19 years.

Special auctions of renowned collections were highly sought after. Such as China Guardian's "Zhongxing Hall Collection Special Auction", "Qiu Zhai Collection Special Auction", "Singapore Collection of Five Pieces of Lu Yanshao's Masterpieces Special Auction", "Zhenru Studio Collection Special Auction", "Zini Community Collection of Modern and Contemporary Purple Sand Masterpieces Special Auction", "Luming Room Collection of Ming-style Furniture Special Auction", "Wutaishan Ren Collection of Qing Daoguang Imperial Porcelain (I)(II) Special Auction", and "Shisunzhai Collection of Modern and Contemporary Chinese Calligraphy and Painting Special Auction"; Beijing Poly's "Good Collection Dr. Guo Ruitan's Traditional Chinese Calligraphy and Painting Night Auction", "Jicui Building Collection of Modern and Contemporary Chinese Calligraphy and Painting Night Auction", "Zhenru Studio Collection of Modern and Contemporary Calligraphy and Painting", and "Dr. Guo Xiuyi's 100th Birthday Celebration Rare Collection Calligraphy and Painting Night Auction"; Beijing Hanhai's "Sun Peicang Family Collection of Xu Beihong Oil Painting Masterpieces Special Auction" and "Zhao Qingwei Collection of Illustration and Picture Book Manuscripts Special Auction"; Beijing Kuangshi's "Xuhuai Collection of Modern and Contemporary Calligraphy Special Auction"; Beijing Huachen's "Bamboo Rhythm Studio Collection of Chinese Calligraphy and Painting Special Auction", "Ran Family Study Collection of Chinese Calligraphy and Painting Special Auction"; Shanghai Duoyunxuan's "Liaotian Building Calligraphy and Painting Special Auction"; Xiling Seal Engravers' Society's "Leisure Dwelling Hall Collection of Chinese Calligraphy and Painting Special Auction" and so on.

In 2012, the reliability effect and brand effect of renowned collections, historical masterpieces, and special auctions of fine collections will become increasingly prominent in the cultural relics and artworks auction market. In the art market, more and more art investors and investment institutions face the primary task of risk control before them. Under the current situation of immature authentication technology and chaotic authentication system, they will inevitably choose works with reliable sources as their purchasing objects. Moreover, all purchasing behaviors aimed at investment hope that their purchased items and purchasing behaviors themselves possess certain brand effects and news effects, which is conducive to the further enhancement of the item's value and the resale of the work.

Strong Rise?

In recent years, whether in terms of transaction volume or the level of high-end auction items, the international dominance of Chinese cultural relics and artworks auctions has shifted to China. Beijing is also one of the four major global art auction centers after New York, London, and Hong Kong. The Global Art Market Report released by the European Art Fair (The European Fine Art Fair) in 2011 stated that the Chinese art market is strongly rising and has already ranked second globally. With the growth of China's economy and the arrival of the cultural economic era in our country, more and more international collectors, investment experts, and international auction houses will focus on Chinese art transactions. It can be affirmed that China's cultural relics and artworks auction will play an increasingly important role in the international market.

Although the Chinese cultural relics and artworks auction market has maintained rapid growth each season in recent years, in fact, the growth rate has always shown a downward trend. In 2012, there are many uncertain factors in China's economy, and the expected GDP growth rate may decrease. M1 growth rate may also decrease accordingly as monetary policy tends to be prudent. Facing the unclear economic situation and capital situation at this stage, it is difficult for China's cultural relics and artworks auction market to achieve significant growth in transaction volume in 2012. We believe that with the deepening understanding of the value and credit system of artworks by the whole society, China's cultural relics and artworks auction market will enter a market adjustment period where passion returns to rationality, investment and collection are unified, and the construction of the integrity system is further improved.

Under the background of national policies promoting the great development and prosperity of the cultural industry, and the trend of gradual standardization in the art market, the medium and long-term trends of China's cultural relics and artworks auction market will remain optimistic.
