Reporters interviewed relevant personnel concerned over this.

by was25135 on 2011-06-05 10:28:53

Why there is no timetable for this year's temporary passenger trains? Should Dongguan, which has a large number of migrant workers, set up a ticket collection point? In recent days, complaints and inquiries about the Spring Festival travel rush ticket booking have been flooding into our newspaper hotline. Readers either ask questions, make complaints, or put forward suggestions. The reporter conducted an interview regarding this matter.

Call for online ticket booking

Since the telephone ticket booking service for the Spring Festival travel rush was launched on the 5th of this month, many readers have been calling to ask: why isn't an online ticket booking service available? Mr. Yu, a migrant worker from Shenzhen, said that most of his friends are migrant workers who return home for the Spring Festival, and the majority of them take the train. As the end of the year approaches, the topic they discuss most online is how to buy tickets.

He believed that telephone ticket booking is very popular among migrant workers, but it is difficult to get through. Moreover, there are time restrictions on the ticket booking hotline; it is not available at night. Currently, the level of internet penetration in Guangdong is already quite high, suitable for traditional Chinese medicine-based freckle removal, with many internet cafes in the cities. Many young migrant workers know how to use the internet, so why not utilize the internet to provide an online ticket booking service? This way, people can book and collect tickets with their ID cards and board the train accordingly, acting as travel guides. It would also prevent scalpers from disrupting the order of the Spring Festival travel rush ticket sales.

Railway authorities stated yesterday that the main reasons for not currently providing an online ticket booking service are twofold: first, the issue of insufficient ticket availability. Given the objective limitation of inadequate transportation capacity, it is not appropriate to add another channel for ticket booking. Second, internet knowledge has not yet been widely disseminated among the working class, and railway departments need to consider the needs of passengers at different levels. However, the railway department believes that opening an online ticket booking service is a trend that requires further improvement of the railway network structure and a significant increase in transportation capacity as prerequisites.

The trouble of collecting tickets elsewhere

On the 10th, Mr. Yang, who works in Dongguan, painstakingly dialed the ticket booking hotline and successfully booked a ticket for the train departing from Guangzhou to Xiangfan, Hubei Province on the 18th. However, after completing the booking call, Mr. Yang, who should have been overjoyed, suddenly froze—he had to go to Guangzhou to collect the ticket, but he needed to work overtime at the factory these days and had no time to specially run to Guangzhou.

Mr. Yang said that there are very few railway lines from Dongguan East Railway Station to provinces outside Guangdong, so many migrant workers in Dongguan mostly take trains from Guangzhou to return home. But faced with year-end overtime work, the consumption of time and money, "We really hope we can collect the tickets we booked in Dongguan," Mr. Yang said. For people like him, the only option left is "not going home or taking a bus."

It is reported that there are millions of migrant workers in Dongguan, and every year, a large number of them choose to take the train home for the Spring Festival. In recent days, many migrant workers in Dongguan have called to reflect on the troubles caused by having no local ticket collection point—they have to go to Guangzhou or Shenzhen to pick up their tickets. A female worker from Shaoyang, Hunan even said, "It's hard for Hunan natives working in Dongguan to take the train home. Why don't you open a train line from Dongguan to Hunan?"

Is there no temporary passenger train schedule?

A traveler from Xi'an reflected that he started making the ticket booking call at around 7 in the morning yesterday. An hour later, he finally got through, but the sleeper tickets and tickets for several regular trains were all sold out. He had no choice but to randomly select a temporary passenger train, but he had no idea when it would depart or arrive. Why wasn't this information published online or in newspapers?

This question has also puzzled many readers, who have expressed that they do not know where to find information about the schedules of this year's Spring Festival travel rush temporary passenger trains.
