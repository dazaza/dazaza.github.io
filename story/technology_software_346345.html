GEM retreats, while the main board experiences broad fluctuations and consolidation.

by xw0972406 on 2010-04-15 22:32:10

On Thursday, the overall trend of the stock indexes in Shanghai and Shenzhen was characterized by a slight high opening, followed by a wide-ranging consolidation. The trading volume was basically flat compared to the previous trading day, indicating that investors were cautious about the future market. In terms of sectors, large-cap blue-chip stocks performed well today. Petrochemical giants both rose against the trend. Due to its relatively smaller tradable shares and larger weight, with the introduction of stock index futures, the petrochemical giants have received significant attention from institutional investors and are still worth watching for the future market. Brokerage stocks also showed strong performance today, Northeast Securities, Southwest Securities, and Guojin Securities all surged over 2%. Brokerage stocks will directly benefit from the development of margin trading. The coal sector also performed strongly today. With further increases in international energy prices, coal prices will also show an upward trend, and the performance of listed companies will continue to grow. The ChiNext board plummeted significantly today, with evident capital outflows, showing that investors' enthusiasm for high-priced small-cap stocks has temporarily come to an end. Additionally, ceramics, steel, material foreign trade, aircraft manufacturing, and brewing sectors performed relatively well today, while instrument manufacturing, shipbuilding, electronic information, media entertainment, power generation preparation, and medical devices adjusted significantly. Overall, the Shanghai and Shenzhen stock markets experienced a wide-ranging consolidation. The Shanghai Composite Index will continue to rise along the 5-day moving average line, and the market still has room for further gains, allowing continued participation.
