According to the 2005 annual survey data from iResearch, online media has become the primary channel for users to learn about online education companies or schools. Among these channels, online advertising accounts for 48.1%, ranking first. This is followed by online forums at 43.9% and search engines at 41.5%.

However, most institutions that cultivate this main force—schools—are still enthusiastic about traditional promotional methods. The image of their websites and their promotional techniques are out of sync with the times, unable to attract the attention of the youth group, and even creating a rift between students and schools. They fail to integrate with the open, equal, and free spirit of the Web 2.0 era.

From this, we can see that in today's increasingly market-oriented education industry, to stand out in competition and gain the favor of students and parents, forward-thinking principals should use the internet as the most important marketing tool for schools.

Combining several core aspects of operating in the education industry, we believe that online marketing in the education sector can be summarized into the following four parts:

1. Online Recruitment - Expanding School Enrollment Sources

With the breaking of the traditional unified recruitment model, schools have begun to enter the track of market competition. How to find sources of enrollment has become the most headache-inducing issue for various schools. Opening newspapers reveals dazzling advertisements with various titles and honors. Schools are anxious, and so are the students—one side struggles to find students, while the other struggles to find suitable schools. The difficulty of school recruitment and student school searches stems from information asymmetry, making it hard for schools and students to resonate and attract student sign-ups.

The internet is the most comprehensive and rich carrier of information display. To gain the favor of students, schools must showcase as detailed and abundant information as possible on their websites to enhance students' understanding of the school. Below are points suggested by Shang Hengtong for information to be displayed on school-type websites:

1) School Overview: Detailed introductions about the history of the school’s founding, its educational culture, leadership figures, campus environment, etc., allowing students to understand the school more deeply and comprehensively.

2) Faculty Team: Introductions about the school’s quality faculty team, an important step in boosting confidence among students and parents.

3) Majors and Curriculum Settings: This part of the introduction is also a core concern for students. A simple search function can be set up to help students quickly find their favorite courses.

4) Teaching Advantages: Besides self-introduction by the school, recommendations from already graduated students or student parents can enhance the school's credibility.

5) Online Consultation: If students have any questions about the school, they can raise them online. Timely responses from the school help build trust among students.

6) Online Registration: As an important channel to understand student intentions, online registration allows timely understanding of student registration intentions, enabling quick contact with students to answer their questions.

2. Online Campus - Opening New Spaces for Communication Between Schools and Student Parents

The network has become an indispensable tool for students every day. Utilizing the interactivity of the network to communicate with students and parents not only enhances mutual feelings but also helps understand the thoughts of students and parents. It showcases the rich creativity and imagination of students, enlivens the school atmosphere, and elevates campus culture.

1) Online Communication: Establishing a communication platform among students, parents, and teachers to discuss issues equally.

2) Campus News: Timely reporting on the latest news and activities of the school, arranging student correspondents to report on topics of interest to students, attracting student attention.

3) Online Bulletin Board: Moving the traditional blackboard newspaper online, fully utilizing students' intelligence and talents, providing space for students to display their skills.

4) Online Surveys: Conducting research on issues of concern to students on the website, listening to opinions from student parents to enhance the school's credibility.

3. Employment (College Entrance) Guidance - Paving the Way for Students’ Future

College entrance (employment), as a topic of common concern for students and parents, is also an important focus for schools. On the website, a special section for college entrance employment guidance can be established to provide beneficial support for students' futures.

1) Job Recommendations: For some outstanding students, recommendations can be made on the school website, reflecting the school's people-oriented educational philosophy.

2) Employment/College Entrance Information: Searching for the latest employment and college entrance information for students, attracting browsing by student parents, showing the school's concern for students.

3) Employment/College Entrance Guidance: Answering problems encountered by students in the process of college entrance/employment, helping students find their direction in employment/college entrance.

4. Alumni Home - Accumulating Long-Term School Branding

The accumulation of alumni resources can bring the best word-of-mouth promotion for the school. By forming an online alumni association, not only can alumni recognize and exchange ideas, accumulating social resources, but alumni can also become the "living signs" of school promotion, attracting student participation.

1) Alumni Directory: Recording the contact information of each graduating class, facilitating alumni searches;

2) Alumni Testimonials: Allowing successful alumni to express gratitude to the school, enhancing alumni sentiment towards their alma mater, giving student parents full confidence.

3) Alumni Exchange: Establishing a space for alumni exchanges, maintaining regular contact among alumni, bringing more social resources to the school.
