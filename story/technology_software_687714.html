Hyperbaric oxygen in the treatment of 58 cases of sudden deafness

by ncpimqudxsx on 2012-03-02 17:29:08

Hyperbaric oxygen in the treatment of sudden deafness in 58 cases. There were 32 cases of unilateral deafness out of 51 cases, and 7 cases of bilateral deafness. The onset time was the shortest of half a day and the longest of 3 months, with tinnitus and dizziness in 8 cases, and a history of hypertension in 12 patients treated by the following methods: Hyperbaric oxygen therapy: using the old factory medium high-pressure gas tank for pressure therapy at 0.22MPa (2.2ATA), vasopressin for 20 minutes, voltage for 30 minutes, wearing a mask for pure oxygen inhalation, intermediate rest for 10 minutes reduction, 30 minutes, once a day, 10 times as a course of treatment. Conventional treatment: a. Vascular dilatation treatment: according to the ENT doctor's treatment; b. Psychotherapy: encouraging the patient and family to build confidence in overcoming the disease, and communicating with the patient about the knowledge of hyperbaric oxygen in the treatment of sudden deafness. Results: 2.1 Efficacy criteria: Cure: hearing recovery normal, tinnitus, dizziness symptoms disappeared; Effective: listening improved by more than 30 dB, but did not reach the normal level, symptoms significantly improved; Slightly effective: hearing improved by 10-30 dB, symptomatic improvement; Ineffective: listening improved less than 10 dB, no improvement in symptoms. 2.2 Evaluation of curative effect in 58 cases, 29 cases were cured (50%), 18 cases (31.1%) markedly effective, 7 cases (12.1%) effective, and 4 cases (6.8%) ineffective, with a total efficiency of 93.2%. Discussion: Common pathological changes in patients with sudden deafness include spiral organ atrophy, decreased auditory hair cells, perilymphatic space infiltration caused by inner ear ischemia and hypoxia, weakened aerobic metabolism leading to hair cell degeneration and hearing loss. Hyperbaric oxygen treatment can effectively improve this condition, with the mechanism as follows: hyperbaric oxygen can rapidly increase arterial oxygen content, expand capillary blood oxygen diffusion distance, increase the inner ear and perilymphatic oxygen partial pressure content, correct hypoxia in inner ear cells, enhance aerobic metabolism, accelerate the repair of hair cells and cochlear and vestibular nerve fibers. High-pressure oxygen can improve local hypoxia conditions, cause local vasoconstriction, reduce capillary endothelial permeability, decrease exudation, and improve inner ear edema caused by hypoxia. High-pressure oxygen improves blood rheology, reduces cell numbers, lowers blood viscosity, enhances inner ear circulation, improves metabolism, promotes auditory function recovery, making hyperbaric oxygen an ideal treatment method for sudden deafness.
