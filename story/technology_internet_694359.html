Skin tones vary from person to person, not just white or black, but also yellow or red. In short, choosing a foundation that suits your skin tone is best. Although it's troublesome to try on makeup every time you buy cosmetics, this is the correct way to purchase.

2. Which type suits your skin better: liquid, cream, or powder?

Foundations are generally divided into three types. Choosing a foundation that suits you not only makes it last longer but also looks natural after use.

For dry skin, liquid is preferable because the lotion has more moisture and transparency, looking more natural. For even drier and less lustrous skin, cream is more appropriate. For normal or oily skin, powder is best and can absorb excess oil on the skin.

Using appropriate cosmetic products not only enhances beauty but also keeps the makeup lasting longer.

3. The right foundation color for your skin

White skin:

Bright pink tones can make white skin look lively and vibrant.

Bright ochre red tones, people with pale faces can use this color series to add a rosy charm.

Bronze skin:

Dark ochre red tones. If it's not naturally dark skin but sun-tanned, the neck is still white, so special attention must be paid to extend the makeup color series to the neck.

Normal skin:

Middle tones of pink, dark ochre red, and olive green. Neutral skin can show different qualities through middle tones of light and dark colors. Pink tones easily create a fresh atmosphere, while darker tones produce a dignified feeling.

When the face looks yellow:

Middle tones of pink. A yellowish complexion directly gives an impression of unhealthiness. At this time, colors stronger than yellow, though suitable for the skin, should be avoided. Generally, pink tones can express the healthy beauty of the skin.

Reddish skin:

Middle tones of ochre red and olive green. Although both are red, the natural skin tone of Asians is white with a touch of red. Olive green tones have a balancing effect on redness. Ochre red and olive green tones are excellent blending colors, showing health.

Many women focus more on eyebrow drawing, eye makeup, and lipstick application during professional makeup sessions, neglecting the role of foundation. Experienced women and professional makeup artists know that the key to good makeup lies in applying the base color well.

Applying base color uses a product called foundation cream. The basic components of foundation cream are "oil" and "moisture," which are essential for the skin, along with pigments that enhance the makeup effect. Using foundation cream can make your skin tone look better, smoother, fuller, more hydrated, and cover minor flaws on your face. This is like painting; if you paint on a smooth, flat, clean, and glossy paper, the result will be better.

For oily skin, choose water-based liquid foundation; for dry skin, choose oil-based liquid or cream foundation; for combination skin, water-based foundation is best. If you use a dual-purpose foundation, oily skin should choose dry powder foundation, and dry skin should choose wet foundation.

Generally speaking, foundations come in three basic colors: deep skin tone foundation, light skin tone foundation, and pink-toned foundation. People with darker skin tones use deep skin tone foundation, and those with fair skin use light skin tone foundation, making the basic base color of the face look natural, real, and fitting. Pink-toned foundation is more suitable for young girls.

Before applying light skin tone foundation, first apply a thin layer of green skin corrective fluid on your face (this can be bought in stores), then apply a small amount of light skin tone foundation. It can quickly whiten dark skin. Fair-skinned friends can also use deep skin tone foundation to achieve certain makeup effects. In short, in modern society's aesthetic consciousness, fair skin is no longer the only standard of beauty. Emphasizing individuality in makeup techniques has increasingly gained people's appreciation and admiration.

When applying water-based or cream foundation, first take a small amount of foundation on a cotton swab and place it on the back of your hand. Then use your middle finger and ring finger to evenly "press and wipe" the foundation on your face. Here we say "press and wipe" rather than just "wipe." The biggest difference between the two is that the former allows the foundation to adhere better to the skin, making it last longer.

When applying dry or wet powder foundation, use a powder puff to directly pick up the dry powder and rub it a few times on the back of your hand to ensure the powder on the puff is evenly distributed before pressing and wiping it onto your face. When using wet foundation, dip the powder puff in a little water, then pick up the dry powder, similarly rubbing it a few times on the back of your hand, then press and wipe it onto your face. Used powder puffs must be washed with soap and dried in a cool place before reuse. If you have long hair, it can be styled in various ways, wrapping the hair around a pen and twisting it upwards onto your head. Also:

Hair knots —— Instead of always letting your hair hang down, you can tie it into a knot. First, comb your hair back, then divide it in half at the middle, place the left side over the right, then wrap it around from the middle, just like tying a real knot, then smooth down the ends and secure it with a hairband.

Pigtails —— This is the simplest kind of braid, giving a strong and solid look. First, comb your hair back, then part it down the middle, place the left side over the right, gently twist them together by crossing them over each other until only one inch of the ends remain, then secure it with a hairband.

Loose braids —— These braids are loose and natural, giving a very casual feel. Comb your front hair back and gently brush it to one side, dividing it into three parts: one thin strand and two thicker ones, then loosely braid them together and secure with a hairband.

Trendy hairstyles —— Make stiff hair look fluffy. While your hair is still damp, braid it into several medium-length braids and secure them with hairbands. Once your hair is dry, gently untangle the braids and slowly comb your hair into waves (be careful not to braid them too tightly, otherwise, the hairstyle will look fried).

Before applying blush, to make its color look natural and beautiful, often first wrap a few ice cubes in a towel, then wipe your face and neck (to shrink pores). After completely drying, then apply the blush. Some people prefer powdered blush because it is easily absorbed by the skin, looking soft and natural. And this powdered blush is suitable for all types of skin. But choosing which blush depends on personal experience. Don't choose blush that contrasts greatly with your skin tone. For example, a pale face with bright red blush will make people uncomfortable, and if you have olive-green skin, applying pink blush will be very disharmonious. No matter how evenly applied, if the skin and blush color are not harmonious, there won’t be a good effect. There’s another point to note:

People with smaller faces should apply it more intensely.

People with larger faces should apply it more lightly.

In summary, when using eyeshadow, blush, and lipstick together, —— be sure to pay attention to the coordination of various colors. Make the colors harmonious and unified. If using peach, rust, and brown eyeshadow and lipstick, then peach-colored blush should be used. If selecting light purple, pink, purple, gray eyeshadow and lipstick, the blush should preferably be pink rose or deeper blue-purple.

Generally, mascara is applied in 3-4 layers, fully coating each layer. After the first layer dries, apply the second layer, and after each layer dries, use a small brush to comb through. This ensures the mascara is evenly applied and both upper and lower lashes are coated uniformly. When applying, it’s best to place the mirror below your forehead so your eyes look downwards, allowing better control.

Apply Vaseline on your eyelashes at least three times a week for maintenance. As for colored mascaras, such as green, red, violet, blue, and crimson, they are used for disco dancing. You can also draw lines on your face with green eyebrow pencils to appear funny and lively.

After applying the base color and finishing the eyes, it's time for lipstick —— on the lips and cheeks. Red symbolizes passion and fire. The effect red has on the spirit can also affect the cheeks. Perhaps due to the simple connection between blood and red, we associate red with passion and courage. Therefore, when you apply lipstick and blush, you can express a bit of passion —— of course, subtly.

If the passion you want to express on your lips needs to be restrained rather than fiery! Avoid overly vivid or deep red colors, as everyone will only focus on your mouth. Lipstick color should be chosen based on your skin tone. If your lip color is naturally deep, using a purple-toned lipstick will make your lips look exaggerated and scary. On the other hand, a mix of pink and beige will look fresh and beautiful on your lips.

Pay attention to the intensity of the color and match it with your clothing before applying lipstick. However, there's no need to overly emphasize the consistency between clothing and makeup, but some colors, such as certain reds and oranges —— when paired together, can be quite jarring. Many people keep a palette for both clothing and makeup, resulting in a much more natural look.

Some people apply lipstick directly, never using a lip brush. Occasionally, they outline their lips with a slightly redder copper pen. Whether or not you use a pen, following your natural lip line when applying lipstick works well.

Most women, when thinking about lipstick, imagine a closed mouth that appears gentle and pleasing, forgetting that the lipstick color must coordinate with the teeth color. This might sound trivial, but when you see a woman with bright red lipstick and yellow teeth, you'll believe it. Tooth color can vary in many ways, ask any dentist. If your teeth are yellow, avoid using similar purplish-red or coral orange shades. The simplest method is to smile after trying a new lipstick. This way, you can accurately determine if the color suits your teeth, and it will make the salesperson behind the counter happy!

Some people frequently use special lipsticks as blushes. They enjoy the slight sheen, so they often dab a bit of lipstick on their cheekbones. It makes the cheeks look rosy and complements the base color, as well as harmonizing with the lipstick. Sometimes, they mix in a bit of another color to make the cheeks brighter or darker. But after tanning, only a thin layer is needed.

In general, powder brushes are suitable for dry skin, while gel colors last longer on oily skin. Some modern gels and powders make the skin look convincingly sun-kissed. A friend once said, if you apply blush to areas where the sun easily hits —— like the forehead, cheeks, bridge of the nose, and chin —— the effect is very satisfying. However, the blush should be light and evenly blended.

Before starting makeup, it's important to check if the lighting in the makeup area is appropriate. If you're doing makeup for daytime activities, then applying makeup under daylight yields the best results. Daylight is harsh, and if you apply makeup under artificial light and then go outside, it will look glaringly obvious.

In the morning, you might see a woman rushing to work, dressed beautifully and appropriately, but with two pink circles on her cheeks. Clearly, she applied makeup under dim lighting, perhaps even under the bathroom fluorescent lights. If you apply makeup under daylight, it will look beautiful and natural under most other types of lighting. Therefore, whenever you travel, carry a small mirror with you. This way, you can use natural daylight by the window instead of applying makeup in a dimly lit bathroom. If your office uses fluorescent lights, it's best to keep a small hand mirror in your drawer. That way, you can check if the makeup colors on your face are moderate. Remember, artificial lighting can alter the colors of blush and lipstick; colors that appear blue or brown under fluorescent lights can turn black or yellow-brown, despite looking attractive under daylight.

In most regions of the world, the light in winter and summer differs significantly, and you must adjust your makeup accordingly. In the pale light of winter, you can apply brighter makeup to make your face look radiant. In summer, with intense light, you should use more natural colors and apply lighter, softer makeup on your face and eyes.

1. Clean facial skin: Before applying the base color, you must remove any impurities from your face to start the makeup process. Removing facial oil can be done with either oil cleansing or water cleansing. If possible, it's best to use oil cleansing, i.e., using products like cleansers or cleansing creams. Its advantage is that it removes facial oil, cleanses the skin, and protects it from the adverse effects of alkaline substances like soap.

2. Use toner to gently press on your face and neck, then add a layer of tinted moisturizer, keeping the unmade-up face clean, fresh, and hydrated. This tinted moisturizer not only benefits the skin but also enhances the effectiveness of cosmetics, making the makeup last longer, more even, fine-textured, and less likely to change color. Especially in summer, using moisturizer can give the skin a natural sun-kissed look, helping to protect it.

Apply foundation: Use a small amount of foundation on your face, then use a cotton ball or sponge to carefully spread it evenly, extending all the way to the temples and jawline to avoid visible traces. Then use a small amount of oil-based eyeshadow cream as a base, enhancing the true color of eyeshadow powder. You can also use a small amount of oil-based eyeshadow cream on your cheekbones, gently smoothing it with your fingertips. To cover dark circles above the eyes or imperfections on your face, apply concealer first and blend it with a sponge. Be careful not to apply it under the delicate skin beneath your eyes.

4. Sweep eyeshadow powder: Use a brush to sweep eyeshadow powder, making the different colors more evenly distributed. Then, apply a deeper shade of eyeshadow on the inner corners of the eyes to highlight the nose lines, a common technique for Asian face shapes.

5. Draw eyeliner: Use black eyeliner to draw along the upper and lower lash lines, making the eyes appear more spirited and adding vitality.

6. Curl lashes: Use a lash curler to sweep the lashes from the underside twice, then let them dry. When curling the lower lashes, use the lash curler once, then gently sweep with a clean lash brush.

7. Apply blush: Applying blush makes the entire face look soft and natural and highlights the cheekbones. Then, lightly sweep the same color blush on the temples to make the facial colors appear harmonious.

8. Outline lips: First, apply foundation on the original lip line, then powder, and use a lip pencil to draw the designed lip shape. Add pearl gloss in the center of the upper and lower lips for added shine.
