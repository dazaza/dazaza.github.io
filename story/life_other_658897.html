The morning light flows through the sadness.

by zhouzhou56 on 2012-02-04 17:15:29

Dawn, flowing in sorrow

Stepping out the door, I realize too much time has passed; the mountains in the dawn light are hazy and melancholic. Vaguely, one can see their rugged outlines and hear the sorrow flowing within their bodies. Who has left behind so much of this springtime sorrow, spreading in the cool morning light?

It seems as if I've slept for too long in a house filled with selfishness, narrow-mindedness, and suspicion - a house that belongs solely to myself, entangled for far too long. I always thought I had a broad mind, but in such a house, I gradually fell into decline, like a leaf stained by wind and frost, becoming increasingly withered and estranged.

Long ago, I was an excellent listener, hearing that enchanting voice narrate an unforgettable emotion. A faint sigh, a long silence, a tranquil companionship - I was moved, jealous, and envious of it all. Unknowingly, I would also yearn for such a relationship in my heart, not intense but fragrant and enduring, not grand like a river but able to merge into the vast ocean. Yes, I longed for encounters, listening, understanding, and companionship. These words filled a certain summer of a certain year. The warm breeze thinned your longing but greened the seedlings in my heart. It was just like a warm, serene, and loving poem, singing of our evergreen youth.

Standing alone in the wind, I heard the rustling of falling leaves. My heart's little window opened slightly in the deep autumn water, lighting up a streak of brightness in the drizzle. Perhaps, it was just a noon hour, just one of your casual glances, or an unintentional pause, which planted sweet thoughts in my heart. Since then, we counted the stars together, got drenched in the autumn rain, and walked over the four limbs of Father Time. We prayed to a certain star and let a drop of rain moisten the lines of poetry in our hearts. Since then, I wrote line after line of text for you, sang for you every night, and planted a sunflower for you in a certain corner. Not for anything else, I only hoped that one day, the light in my heart could illuminate every corner of yours and warm each of your winters.

But, from when did the creases in your past take away all my sunshine? Jealousy and suspicion built a house for me in my heart, white walls, white doors and windows, white manuscript paper. I locked myself inside, thus falling into an inexplicable prison, unable to extricate myself. Every time I got drunk, I couldn't control myself, hurting both myself and you in my heart. In the heavy nights, endless darkness always swallowed up brilliance and the warmth of mutual dependence. Love with wounds is like the mythical firebird in legend, flying recklessly, endlessly!
