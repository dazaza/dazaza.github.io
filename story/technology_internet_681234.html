What is the difference between the two?

1. Question: Hot stamping can be divided into rotary-on-rotary and flat-on-flat hot stamping.

Answer: The difference between rotary-on-rotary hot stamping and flat-on-flat hot stamping is that generally, rotary-on-rotary hot stamping can be suitable for both large-area and small-area hot stamping. The reason lies in that rotary-on-rotary hot stamping is a line-contact hot stamping method, which allows for higher-speed hot stamping. Flat-on-flat hot stamping belongs to face contact, making it difficult to expel air, leading to problems with incomplete hot stamping. Flat-on-flat hot stamping is suitable for small-area patterns, lines, or text, and its hot stamping speed is low.

2. Question: What are the differences between hot stamping on composite cardstock and white cardstock?

Answer: The main difference lies in adhesion. The adhesion layer of the hot stamping foil (electrolytic aluminum) has an affinity with the material being stamped. Since the surface tension on white cardstock is higher, the hot stamping effect on white cardstock is better than on composite cardstock.

White cardstock usually uses regular ink, while composite cardstock has a smooth and dense surface, allowing the use of UV ink. Composite cardstock typically uses ink for composite material printing (such as Hanghua Ink Chemical Co., Ltd.'s offset JP series ink) or UV ink. Choosing flat-on-flat hot stamping makes it hard to expel air, so it's best to use rotary-on-rotary line contact hot stamping machines.

3. Question: What should be noted when printing on hot stamping foil?

Answer: It's important not to apply varnish if possible. Careful research should be done on the properties of the material and ink. 1. When hot stamping first and then printing, ensure that the electrolytic aluminum and the ink are compatible; otherwise, the ink may not transfer properly. Please test print before proceeding. If allowed, consider leaving the hot stamping area hollow. 2. Choose electrolytic aluminum with a surface tension between 4.0-4.6×10^2 N/m to ensure good adhesion of the ink.

4. Question: What should be noted when performing electrolytic aluminum hot stamping on UV varnish?

Answer: Generally, solvent-based and water-based varnish products do not cause issues. This is closely related to the surface tension of the substrate material. UV varnishes often encounter problems mainly due to compatibility issues.

1. Regular electrolytic aluminum hot stamped on UV varnish ensures that the electrolytic aluminum's surface tension is greater than the UV varnish's surface tension; otherwise, incomplete hot stamping may occur.

Or hot stamp first and then apply UV varnish; 2. If allowed, consider leaving the hot stamping area hollow.

Then determine the hot stamping process and materials. 3. It's best to test print first.

5. Question: What are the causes of inaccurate hot stamping registration? What are the solutions?

Answer:

One, paper deformation occurs. Solution: Lower the drying temperature or UV curing temperature during printing (high temperatures can cause paper shrinkage).

Two, expansion of the hot stamping plate due to heat. Solution: Determine several hot stamping points on one hot stamping plate. Compensate beforehand during production. Understand the expansion rules of the hot stamping plate with temperature changes.

Three, no consideration given to shrinking or enlarging the hot stamping patterns, text, or lines during plate making.

Ensure consistency in front gauge and side gauge (distance from paper edge to waste line). Four, previous process issues. Solution: Address issues arising from prior processes.
