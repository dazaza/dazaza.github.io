3. Throughout the process from the start of drilling until completion, sufficient cooling should be maintained without interruption. Short cooling can easily cause chipping of the tool.

4. At the beginning of drilling, the feed should be slow and steady. After cutting 1~2mm, the feed speed can be increased. When retracting the tool, appropriately reduce the feed speed, and maintain uniform feeding during the middle cutting process.

5. When drilling with hard alloy steel plate drills, the reasonable cutting speed should be above 40 meters per minute, and at least not less than 30 meters per minute. Meanwhile, as hard alloy is a high hardness material, care should be taken to prevent damage during storage and distribution, and impact should be avoided during use.

6. The initial sharpness of a new blade can lead to micro chipping due to shock. Magnetic drill [http://www.hanyue-sh.com/] suggests that special attention should be paid to the first few dozen holes when using a new blade, with slow feeding to prevent chipping. After the edge naturally dulls, it can be used normally, which can significantly extend the life of the tool.

7. If the machine stops due to overload during the drilling process, cut off the power first, manually turn the tool slightly in reverse to disengage the blade from the cutting area, then lift the motor and remove the tool. After checking for no abnormalities, you can restart the operation. If too many chips wrap around the tool body, you can withdraw the tool and remove them with a hook.
