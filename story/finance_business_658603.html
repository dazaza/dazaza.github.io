Improper maintenance of cars

by chituma222 on 2012-02-04 10:49:56

All car owners hope that their beloved cars can run for a long time and reduce maintenance costs. To achieve this, it is necessary to learn how to scientifically maintain your car. However, in the process of car maintenance, there are often such situations: thinking you're maintaining the car but actually damaging it unintentionally. Some may even damage the paint protection layer, leading to peeling and rusting. After reading the following incorrect maintenance methods, car owners can check if they have any such improper behaviors.

Misconception Review

1. Washing the car under the scorching sun harms the paint.

Many car owners like to wash their cars under the blazing sun, believing that the water on the body will dry quickly after washing. In fact, it's not the case. Under the scorching sun, the convex lens effect formed by water droplets causes localized high temperature on the topmost layer of the car paint. Over time, the car paint will lose its luster. If waxing at this time, it can easily cause uneven coloration of the car body. Therefore, it's best to wash and wax the car under sheltered conditions. If that cannot be guaranteed, it's better to choose an overcast day or early morning/evening on a sunny day.

2. Washing the car like taking a bath damages the air conditioning.

When the weather gets hotter, dust increases, and the car body gets dirty more easily, so the frequency of car washing also rises. Many car owners want to wash their cars thoroughly like taking a bath themselves. But here's what you need to pay attention to: make sure the car air conditioner remains dry. If the car air conditioner gets wet accidentally, it will affect its lifespan.

3. Acid rain harms the car paint; correct washing prevents damage.

The sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and suspended particles in urban polluted air mix with rainwater to form acid rain with a pH value between 4-5.0, which greatly damages the car body coating. After the car has been exposed to acid rain, a layer of acidic deposits covered with dust and dirt forms on the body. When these acidic substances combine with morning dew, they will cause acid corrosion again. The car body paint keeps being harmed by acidic substances until the gold oil layer is completely corroded, and the vehicle will then lose all its luster. Car owners should regularly wash and maintain their cars with the correct method to reduce the time that acidic substances stay on the car body, thus providing the most complete protection for your beloved car.

4. Frequent washing also harms the paint; be cautious with polishing and beautification.

Under normal sunny conditions, the car can be washed every 5-8 days if it’s not very dirty. If washed every 1-2 days, the life span of the car paint will only be 50%. Wash the car promptly after rain or snow to prevent corrosion. Polishing itself harms the paint. Choose a reputable place for car beauty services; small shops' services equal disfigurement.

5. Over-tightened bolts cause permanent deformation.

There are many fasteners connected by bolts and nuts on the car, and it's important to ensure they have sufficient pre-tension, but they shouldn't be tightened too much. If tightened too much, on one hand, it will cause the connecting parts to deform permanently under external force; on the other hand, it will cause the bolt to stretch permanently, or even result in slipping or breakage.

6. Too tight fan belts increase bearing load.

When the temperature is too high, some drivers think increasing the tightness of the fan belt can improve the engine cooling effect, so they keep tightening the fan belt. However, doing so is harmful. The fan belt should be kept appropriately loose or tight. If too tight, it will increase the bearing load, consume more power, bend the water pump shaft, stretch and deform the belt, and shorten its lifespan.

7. Imported tires are not suitable.

Some people particularly emphasize "imported" when buying tires. New foreign-made tires are not very suitable for domestic users. There are significant differences between European roads and domestic ones, so tires suitable for European roads may not perform well domestically, just like wearing Italian brand-name leather shoes on gravel roads—inevitably causing bulges on the tire side. After some foreign brands produce tires jointly in China, they add a layer of curtain fabric on the tire side according to domestic road conditions, greatly enhancing the anti-impact ability of the tire side. Tires produced through joint ventures have performance that can fully replace imported ones.

8. Circular waxing produces poor results.

Many people habitually use a circular motion when waxing the car body, which is an incorrect method. The correct way to wax is in straight lines, alternating horizontal and vertical lines, and finally applying in the direction of water flow. This can reduce the effect of producing concentric circle halos on the car paint surface.

9. Laundry detergent is the most harmful to car paint.

Seeing the staff wash diligently, bubbles flying everywhere, many car owners think that no matter how dirty the car is, as long as it's taken to the car wash, everything is "OK". After washing, the car looks clean from the outside. If you want to know what the car wash uses to wash your car, you can pinch and smell those bubbles with your fingers. You'll find it's laundry detergent! Laundry detergent is an alkaline cleaning agent that severely damages the car paint. If you consistently use laundry detergent to wash your car, the car surface will gradually lose its luster. By the time the owner realizes that the car has been damaged by washing, it's too late to regret.

10. Correct car washing makes your car shinier.

Washing the car might be the simplest step for us in daily car maintenance, but this seemingly ordinary process, if improperly operated, can lead to serious consequences, and over time will harm your beloved car.
