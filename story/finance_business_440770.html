Four members of a British Chinese family were killed in a suspected quadruple murder-suicide in Smethwick, West Midlands. The suspect left a farewell message to his family before the crime.

by anonymous on 2011-06-01 17:47:50

A Chinese British family of four was killed

May 7 (ECNS) -- According to UK's Chinese Herald, the police found on May 1st a family of four Chinese British people dead in their home. The victims included Ding Jifeng, a senior lecturer at Manchester Metropolitan University, his wife, and two daughters. They had multiple knife wounds. The police were making every effort to find the murderer and did not rule out that the case might be related to commercial disputes.

According to the police, the four victims were 46-year-old Chinese lecturer Ding Jifeng, his 47-year-old wife Xu Ge (who taught Chinese in a local middle school), their 18-year-old elder daughter Ding Xing, and their 12-year-old younger daughter Alice. Neighbors nearby had not seen this family since April 29th and thus reported it to the police.

Glyn Timmins, the detective sergeant in charge of the case in Northampton, pointed out that Ding Jifeng's younger daughter Alice posted comments about the royal wedding on social network Facebook on the afternoon of April 29th, which was the last record of this family before their death. The police suspected that the suspect was Du Anxiang, a 52-year-old business partner who co-operated in the herbal medicine business with the female homeowner. According to the police tracking data, Du Anxiang left the herbal medicine store at 10:30 am on April 29th in Coventry, central-west England, and left a farewell note for his family saying: "It's time to say goodbye."

He then took the train to Northampton and finally escaped driving the FIAT car of the victim's elder daughter Ding Xing. Detective Sergeant Glyn Timmins said: "The police believe that this murder is unrelated to organized crime. Current evidence shows that there were long-term economic disputes between Du and Mr. Ding's family, and the murder may have been caused by this reason. On April 28th, Du lost a lawsuit related to a commercial dispute with Mr. Ding's family. In Du's view, the Ding family owed him hundreds of thousands of pounds."

Du Anxiang was last seen on April 29th at the Pallisades Shopping Center, wearing a white baseball cap, a brown short coat, a blue wool shirt, gray pants, slim in stature, 5 feet 9 inches tall, slightly bald. The police stated that Du Anxiang's mental condition was unclear, and anyone who saw him should not approach him easily but immediately call 999 or 08000961011 to report.

In addition, after the murder case, a five-door Vauxhall Corse small car registered as BG60PMO belonging to the Ding family was missing, and it was suspected to be used by the murderer when escaping. The police appealed that if anyone sees this car, they should report it immediately. It was learned that Ding Jifeng had taught at Manchester Metropolitan University for 15 years. His colleagues all praised him as very friendly, helpful, and having a strong sense of family responsibility. His students called him the best chemistry teacher.

Ding Jifeng's 18-year-old elder daughter Ding Xing was a well-known violinist in her school, excelling both academically and morally, and was expected to attend Cambridge University. The university students set up a website on Facebook to mourn, and the school expressed shock and grief over this tragedy and was assisting the police in the investigation.
