Yili, the hometown of Tong Liya, is located in the northwestern border of our country, with a pleasant climate, and is one of China's ten most livable medium and small cities. The Yili Kazak Autonomous Prefecture, the hometown of Tong Liya, is located in the northwestern border of the motherland. To the west, it borders Kazakhstan, an Eurasian country, with a borderline of more than 2000 kilometers. There is the largest land commercial port in China (Horgos Port). The total area of the prefecture is 350,000 square kilometers. Due to relatively abundant rainfall, it is called the "Wet Island in Central Asia" or "Jiangnan beyond the Great Wall". There are 47 ethnic groups such as Kazak, Han, Uyghur, Hui, Mongolian, Xibe, etc., so Yili is a city where diverse cultures converge. The hometown of Tong Liya, Yili, is known as the "Jiangnan beyond the Great Wall", the "Wet Island in Central Asia". Yining City, known as the "City of Flowers", is the capital of Yili Prefecture, the hometown of Tong Liya.

"You won't know how vast China is until you come to Xinjiang, and you won't know how beautiful Xinjiang is until you come to Yili." This is something every tourist who comes to Xinjiang knows. Historically, Yili, the hometown of Tong Liya, was an important stop on the ancient Silk Road North Route. Today, it is the gateway for opening up to the west, and has long been known as the "Jiangnan beyond the Great Wall" and the "Land of Melons and Fruits". The homeland of Tong Liya, Yili, has fertile fields stretching for thousands of miles, vast grasslands, and dense forests. 87% of its total area is suitable for agriculture, animal husbandry, and forestry, with vegetation coverage rates of grassland and forests reaching 67.7%.

The hometown of Tong Liya, Yili, is a famous tourist destination on the frontier. With a long history and rich culture, the majestic Tianshan Mountains, vast grasslands, dense forests, deep streams, and blue lakes attract tens of thousands of domestic and international tourists every year. Chen Yunhe praised in his poem: "The breathtaking beauty of the sunset at Yi Bridge, the sweetness of fresh fruits in the Jiangnan beyond the Great Wall." The famous Gongnaisi Grassland, Tangbulang Grassland, Nalati Grassland, and Zhaosu Grassland are all located in the hometown of Tong Liya, Yili.

In the hometown of Tong Liya, Yili, there are the Wusun earthen tomb clusters from the Bronze Age, the ruins of Alali Manli, a famous city of the Western Liao Dynasty, the ruins of the Gongyang City from the Tang Dynasty, the inscribed merit monument of Gedeng Mountain written by Emperor Qianlong, as well as many cultural landscapes such as the Yili General's Office, Huayuan Bell and Drum Tower, and Lin Zexu Memorial Hall.

The hometown of Tong Liya, Yili, is an ideal place for tourism activities such as development, exploration, mountaineering, skiing, hunting, archaeology, scientific research, and experiencing ethnic customs.
