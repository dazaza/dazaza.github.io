When the Gates of Memory Open

Deep within everyone's heart lie secrets, known to none or unwilling to be revealed. These secrets may be bitter, sour, or self-indulgent, carefully buried in a corner of our hearts, rarely touched for fear of the pain they bring. However, sometimes when we are moved by a scene or find ourselves alone, the floodgates of memory open, and memories from the past come rushing back. The scenes of the past replay before our eyes, a mix of joy, sorrow, bitterness, and sweetness surge to our hearts, overwhelming us with emotion, blurring our vision with tears.

The memories take me back to that distant era, to that bitter childhood, to those moments of disappointment and helplessness, to that cry for help, to that solitary bewilderment, to an age filled with tears. They say that childhood is beautiful, carefree, full of laughter and joy. But my childhood was painful, filled with tears. Family upheavals meant that I faced abandonment even before I was born (or perhaps even before that). I quietly came into this world without blessings, without the company of loved ones. My birth did not bring joy to my family but rather sighs.

And so began my life of wandering, homeless, eating irregularly, growing up silently amidst cries ignored by all, alternating between hunger and satiety, in a love that was incomplete. When I saw other children growing strong under the protection of their parents, I cast countless envious glances. How many times, with the moon on the treetops, I ran to the village entrance alone, hoping to see my mother’s familiar figure; how many times I shouted at the top of my lungs, my voice and cries echoing in the valley, only the dark night and hunger keeping me company; how many times I waited anxiously, worried, ... This special living environment made me experience the myriad aspects of human nature, made me understand the hardships of life too early, forming my independent and self-reliant character, burying the pain deep inside, striving to be the best version of myself.

Perhaps it was fate, or perhaps just coincidence, but this life has destined me to go solo, wandering far from home. Struggling alone on unfamiliar lands, without the help of relatives or friends, without the support of extended family, without anyone to shield me from the wind and rain, without anyone to wipe away my tears. I have always strived, for the ideals in my heart, for the dreams in my heart, for the responsibilities on my shoulders, hiding all the pain, letting the tears flow inward. Setbacks, disappointments, and blows have accompanied me along the way... Effort, tolerance, and endurance have been my chosen path in life.

The bewilderment during times of disappointment, the helplessness during solitude, the powerlessness during confusion, and the uncertainty of the road ahead have all led me to choose silence. Do not complain about heaven or earth, do not lament the unfairness of fate, do not grumble about being born at the wrong time. Face reality; the road lies beneath your feet. "When Heaven is about to place a great responsibility on a person, it first tests his resolve, strains his muscles and bones, starves his body, leaves him destitute, and confounds his actions. Thus, his mind is tempered, his nature strengthened, and his incompetencies improved." (I know well that I am small and ordinary, and this is merely a way to console myself).

Everything has two sides, and God is fair to everyone. While closing one door, He opens another window. Hardship is a treasure, setbacks make me more mature. Through storms and hardships, after the storm comes the rainbow, sunshine always follows the rain. Even if there are thousands of mountains and rivers ahead, I will face them calmly.
