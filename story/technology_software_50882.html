First Experience with Pidgin (a multi-IM login software, a great tool for keeping QQ online)

by e_wuqing on 2008-03-05 01:07:41

Introduction:

Pidgin (formerly Gaim) is a modular instant messaging client. Pidgin is based on GTK+ and released under the GPL license. It supports multiple platforms, multiple languages, multiple services, and multiple plugins.

Supported IMs:

AIM

Bonjour

Gadu-Gadu

Google Talk

Groupwise

ICQ

IRC

MSN

MySpaceIM

QQ

SILC

SIMPLE

Sametime

XMPP

Yahoo!

Pidgin 2.4.0 has been out for a few days now, I downloaded it early but just installed it recently. Here are my thoughts.

Advantages:

1. Integrates many IM features with numerous plugins and prioritizes speed. Nowadays, many people no longer use only QQ or MSN software. The market share of MSN in China is increasing, and there are also many users of Gtalk. At this time, you can feel the power of Pidgin. Additionally, many excellent plugins make chatting more convenient.

2. For GTalk and MSN-based emails, there is timely email notification. In this sense, it can also be used as an email checking tool.

3. Minimal memory resource usage.

Disadvantages:

1. Regarding QQ usage, there are no user groups, and it cannot display alias names, making it difficult to search for friends. Although it can use its own grouping system, this still causes some inconvenience.

2. QQ group messages pop up automatically, and there's no way to disable the automatic pop-up.

3. Cannot display custom emoticons and other QQ-specific services.

Conclusion:

Despite its many shortcomings, I can't help but love it. Multiple IMs bring a more harmonious life.

PS: Logging into multiple IMs simultaneously is a great idea. I think it shouldn't be difficult for different IM software to achieve interoperability; it's just a matter of interests. Hopefully, that day will come soon.

The emoticons in Pidgin are great, I like them.
