Italian media reported that Manchester City intended to bid 50 million Euros for Edinson Cavani this summer, yet the player claimed he was very happy at Napoli. According to Italian media 'Turin Sports', Premier League team Manchester City planned to offer 50 million Euros for Napoli's forward Edinson Cavani.

It was reported that Manchester City owner Sheikh Mansour hoped to sign the Uruguayan international in the summer and was ready to prepare a generous offer, with Cavani's annual salary potentially reaching 4-5 million Euros.

Napoli's outspoken president De Laurentiis had repeatedly stated that he did not intend to cash in on the Uruguayan star, but whether Napoli could resist under the financial temptation from Manchester City remains unknown.

Cavani himself was very satisfied with life in Naples. He looked forward to joining a big club in the future, but currently, Napoli was moving towards becoming one of the elite clubs.

In an interview with a Uruguayan television program, Cavani said: "Everyone wants to be part of a major club, but I think Napoli is becoming one. There are excellent players here, it's a perfect team. I hope to fight even harder wearing Napoli's jersey."

At the age of 25, Cavani has played 33 matches for Napoli this season and has already scored 23 goals. He was very satisfied with his current situation, "Being the top scorer for Napoli in Europe makes me feel very proud. I am very satisfied with my current life, it is one of the most wonderful moments in my career, and I hope to continue."

Of course, loyalty to the team isn't just talked about; when Manchester City launches its offensive this summer, whether he can resist will be the most important thing.
