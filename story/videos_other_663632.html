Advantages and Characteristics of Hong Kong Tax-Inclusive Express Import

For goods import without documents, the following method can be used: express import declaration is a customs clearance method in which the express company submits an express customs declaration form (such as KJ1, KJ2, KJ3 type declaration forms) to the customs on behalf of the express company and clears the goods in the form of express delivery.

What are the features of express declaration? The express declaration has the following characteristics:

1. Simple procedures

According to national regulations such as the Supervision Measures for Inbound and Outbound Expresses, compared with general trade declaration, the supervision conditions and customs clearance procedures are simplified, and the customs clearance is faster. Therefore, in most cases, the cargo owner does not need to provide a special customs declaration form, customs clearance documents, license/approval (such as 3C national mandatory certification, environmental protection certificate, import quota, etc.).

2. Lower costs

According to national regulations such as the Supervision Measures for Inbound and Outbound Expresses, express imports and exports can enjoy certain tax and fee reduction benefits. Moreover, due to Shenzhen's proximity to Hong Kong, an international platform, Hong Kong's geographical advantage as a free port makes operations convenient, with low tax rates, low operating costs, and naturally lower costs.

3. Faster speed

Due to fewer supervision conditions and simpler procedures, plus the customs clearance and inspection processes being carried out in the form of express delivery, time is naturally saved.

What are the advantages of personal items and small item express imports? Advantages of express import:

1. Small scattered goods, small quantities of samples and advertising materials, etc. Such as: IC, display, glue, thinner water, etc., difficult and high-value products.

2. Various products lacking certain documents (such as no 3C certification). Such as: old molds, machinery, electrical appliances, etc.

3. Products that want to save costs but do not require VAT invoices.

4. If it is bulk cargo, it can also be declared and imported in batches through separate vehicles. Although the customs clearance speed may be affected to some extent, the advantages of simple procedures and lower costs are still significant.

Note:

1. For tax-inclusive imports, the dimensions of each piece of cargo, the weight of the cargo, and the value of the cargo must be known.

2. The real cargo value must be declared.

3. If it is a full container, you need to know whether it will be transferred in Hong Kong before entering mainland China or directly enter mainland China as a full container, with the empty container returning to Hong Kong.

4. Full containers take longer, about a week.

5. This import method does not issue VAT invoices.
