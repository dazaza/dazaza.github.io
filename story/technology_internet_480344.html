Did you know? None of this is HTML5.

by diglog on 2011-08-21 12:42:51

Are the technologies often discussed by students that claim to be HTML5 really within the scope of HTML5? People hope that the all-encompassing HTML5 can natively support more technologies that currently require JavaScript, and even Apple has inadvertently promoted this idea. It is a simple approach towards modern web standards. However, for us, while it may just be semantics, it is important to accurately understand what is not part of HTML5.

- **SVG**: This is not an HTML5 technology; it has existed for at least 5 years.

- **CSS3**: This is not an HTML5 technology; it is simply CSS3, nothing else.

- **Geolocation**: This is absolutely not an HTML5 technology.

- **Client Storage**: This is not an HTML5 technology; it has been removed from the HTML5 specification because many people were concerned that it would become too complex as a whole. It now has its own specification.

- **Web Sockets**: This is not an HTML5 technology; it has its own specification.

However, the most important thing is whether these technologies can be supported by browsers and applied to the modern web. In fact, the management of these branch specifications is still handled by the same people.
