Thousands of corporate threads, a team to string them together. If the foundation of a company is not solid, it will crumble at the slightest shake. Outstanding companies focus on their grassroots levels; owning a consistently well-trained and highly motivated grassroots employee team is a characteristic of all remarkable enterprises. Currently, some industries in China have realized the importance of grassroots development, even elevating "three fundamentals" construction to a strategic level, such as the petroleum and petrochemicals, power, transportation industries, as well as government departments like taxation and public security. However, the actual results are far from satisfactory.

If the problem is wrong, the solution will definitely be wrong. Identifying the correct issue can lead to significant improvements, while missing the mark can result in wasted effort. What is the real reason behind the unsatisfactory results in grassroots team building within companies? The 89 Point Management Training Research Network, through surveys and combining successful experiences from providing team leader competency enhancement and team-building training for large well-known enterprises such as China Mobile, China Petroleum, China Petrochemical, China Power, Mercedes-Benz, etc., has summarized seven major pitfalls in grassroots team building that exist in most Chinese companies:

1. **Team Building with Requirements but No Atmosphere**

Most companies adopt a top-down approach, pushing layer by layer with strict evaluations for team building. However, grassroots managers and employees lack a deep understanding of the purpose and significance of team creation, focusing only on formalities. Employees show low enthusiasm, unclear direction, and minimal participation, often just going through the motions for evaluations, failing to create an atmosphere where everyone participates and strives.

2. **Heavy Investment in Hardware but Little in Software**

A lot of financial resources are spent on hardware配套设施 for team creation, providing necessary hardware conditions for team work. However, there is a lack of systematic thinking and methods in how to scientifically plan and evaluate the effectiveness of these efforts.

3. **Emphasis on System Construction but Neglecting Cultural Management**

Companies emphasize the establishment of team management systems and evaluation mechanisms, monitoring daily team activities, standardizing team creation work and various systems' execution, identifying problems, and researching solutions. However, they neglect how to use culture for management. Cultural management is the highest level of management, influencing and assimilating employees' thoughts and behaviors through systematic and environmental forces, transforming passive employees into active ones.

4. **Stress on Material Rewards but Neglecting Spiritual Cultivation**

During team evaluations, companies focus on material rewards but lack a systematic, scientific, and effective method for spiritual cultivation and motivation, leading to a certain degree of imbalance in the spiritual domain among employees. Humans are spiritual beings, so spiritual and honor-based incentives are indispensable.

5. **Focus on On-site Management but Neglecting Human Quality Management**

Companies emphasize on-site management methods like 5S, requiring proper placement of items and tools. However, there is a lack of necessary methods and means to form employees' daily work habits and quality.

6. **Strong Business Skills Among Team Leaders but Weak Management Skills**

Most team leaders are highly conscious, disciplined, energetic, and skilled in their work, but generally lack management experience and scientific management methods, knowing how to do the work but not how to manage effectively.

7. **Team Leader Leading Alone While Members Lag Behind**

In work, when encountering problems, team leaders may take the initiative to step forward, but they do not know how to motivate members to participate actively, resulting in the leader doing the work while members spectate.

Due to these misconceptions in team building and management, a series of problems arise within companies:

- Employees lack a sense of belonging and cohesion, leading to high turnover rates. On one hand, this causes significant losses in talent recruitment and training; on the other hand, it becomes difficult for companies to retain excellent grassroots employees, significantly reducing overall work efficiency and performance.

- Employees lack enthusiasm and initiative, working passively, perfunctorily, and carelessly. They do not fully engage in their work, failing to maximize efficiency or tap into their creative potential.

- Grassroots teams lack execution capabilities. Without a solid foundation at the grassroots level, corporate strategies cannot be implemented, product quality cannot be guaranteed, customer satisfaction cannot be achieved, and production costs become uncontrollable...

Grassroots team building must involve full participation from all employees, transitioning from simple system and material management to emphasizing cultural environment construction, humanistic concern, and spiritual motivation. Only by truly making team building a spiritual home and a prosperous field for grassroots employees can the vitality and enthusiasm of the grassroots be effectively stimulated, enhancing the overall level of team organization.
