Introduction

When the mobile phone integrates the functions of a radio, MP3, camera, camcorder, TV, PDA, etc., into one device, it has long surpassed its original simple definition as a communication tool and has transformed into an important part of people's daily lives. The rapid advancement of communication technology has further accelerated the arrival of the 3G era. Compared to the previous 2G, 3G brings the internet to the mobile phone, allowing users to handle images, music, browse web pages, hold teleconferences, and conduct e-commerce on their phones. On the other hand, mobile phone manufacturers seize the huge business opportunities brought by 3G, continuously increasing R&D investment, resulting in the emergence of large-screen, full-keyboard, WIFI and WAPI-supported smartphones. Meanwhile, the appearance of counterfeit smartphones has significantly reduced the average selling price of smartphones, greatly lowering the user threshold for high-performance phones, leading to a surge in the number of smartphones, providing a complete hardware foundation for users to use mobile internet services.

According to data from the International Telecommunication Union, there were around 10 million minicomputers globally in 1980, over 200 million terminals connected to the internet in 2000, and by the end of 2010, the number of global internet users exceeded 2 billion, with over 5 billion mobile phone registrations. The rapid growth in the number of users in the mobile internet era is an inevitable trend. The rapid increase in the number of users implies enormous potential and will create significant commercial value.

In the mobile internet era, terminals become one of the key focuses of mobile internet development. Around the demands of mobile internet development, the development of terminals in the mobile internet era shows a clear trend: closely revolving around user needs, providing users with comprehensive services and experiences, i.e., user experience is king. Today, as mobile internet begins, user experience has gradually become the ultimate pursuit of terminal development. Apple's introduction of the iPhone elevated user experience to a new level, winning the favor of global mobile phone users, and the application experience based on Android (Google mobile operating system) has also reached the best level.

Based on 3G networks and smartphone platforms, various needs of internet users based on PCs are slowly shifting to mobile terminals. The trend of 3G encompasses the integration of desktop internet and WAP content, and the development of 3G in China is making mobile phones continuously surpass PCs. According to DCCI's release of the "2010-2011 China Internet Market Data Report," the growth rate of mobile internet in China is significantly faster than PC internet. It is expected that by 2013, the number of mobile internet users in China will reach 721 million, exceeding the 716 million PC internet users, truly entering the mobile internet era.
