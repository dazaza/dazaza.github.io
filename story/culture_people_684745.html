Qing Dynasty Culture】Emp

ress Dowager Cixi's Diet

Empress Dowager Cixi was known as the "behind-the-scenes emperor." After she began ruling from behind a curtain, she enjoyed great power and luxury within the palace. Her diet alone was quite different from others. She had three major preferences when it came to food and drink:

Firstly, she loved drinking flower tea. Cixi was addicted to tea and extremely particular about it. The tea she drank wasn't the usual jasmine or rose that had been fire-cured; instead, it was made with freshly picked flowers mixed with dried tea leaves before being steeped in her teacup. This way, the tea carried both the fragrance of tea leaves and flowers. The water used for brewing was fresh spring water transported daily from Jade Spring Mountain. Cixi's teacups were made of white jade, while the saucers were golden. Three cups would be placed on the saucer: the middle one filled with tea, and the two sides with flowers. When Cixi was ready to drink her tea, two eunuchs would carefully hold the saucer with both hands and rush in front of her, shouting: "The Old Buddha is here to enjoy her tea!" Only then would Cixi partake.

Secondly, she loved eating golden carp. When eating fish, there must be two palace maids who, after washing their hands three times, would meticulously remove all the bones piece by piece and feed her directly.

Thirdly, she loved stinky tofu. Wang Zhihe, a scholar from Anhui, was unsuccessful in his official career despite repeated attempts at the imperial examinations. He later turned to selling stinky tofu. Once, Empress Dowager Cixi tried Wang Zhihe's stinky tofu and found it very delicious, so she requested it daily. By the end of the Qing Dynasty, Wang Zhihe's stinky tofu had become a delicacy in Cixi's royal kitchen. It was a staple in every meal and had to be bought fresh daily from "Wang Zhihe South Sauce Garden."
