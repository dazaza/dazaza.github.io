A few days ago, on that afternoon, I accompanied a few young men from the company in a rented truck to Beijing to collect waste oil. Six people were squeezed into that small cab, and the sunlight was as venomous as a snake. It was very hot, so we suggested the driver turn on the air conditioning, but he said turning on the air conditioning would consume more fuel. The driver was hired long-term; I thought, even if we were steamed like Peking ducks in this small cab, he probably wouldn't care. Fortunately, it was only an hour's drive. After arriving at Yizhuang, the driver drove the car into an industrial zone. In Beijing, Yizhuang is the place I am most familiar with. However, after the driver twisted and turned through those industrial zones, I lost my sense of direction. It seems that even in the places I thought I knew best, there are still many roads I have never walked.

The car stopped outside the gate of a printing factory. This was a large factory, and it looked quite sizable. There were several large workshops in the factory area, which appeared quite impressive. At a relatively remote location behind the factory area, there was a power generation room as big as a large workshop. The waste oil they were to collect came from the large generators inside this power generation room. Behind the power generation room, there were dozens of oil drums filled with heavy oil. Each drum weighed four hundred catties (approximately 266 kilograms). They had to tip these drums over and roll them out before loading them onto the rented truck. This was a rather arduous task, requiring substantial physical strength. In no time, sweat flowed from their faces and bodies, and their clothes were covered with grease stains. Every day, they worked closely with these oil drums. When working, they needed to be highly focused; a moment’s carelessness could result in a drum weighing hundreds of catties falling heavily, with unimaginable consequences.

I learned from a worker clearing waste oil at this factory that factories like theirs almost produce thirty tons of waste heavy oil every month. That means three to four hundred tons annually for one factory alone. If we consider the whole country, what would be the total amount of waste oil produced in a year? It would be an astonishing number.

If these waste oils are properly regenerated and reused, it will make a tremendous contribution to alleviating China's energy crisis. However, if such a massive amount of waste oil is not recycled but wasted, dumped on the ground, or contaminates water sources and seas, polluting our environment upon which we depend for survival, it would become a great disaster for humanity.

Our waste oil workers do the hardest and dirtiest work in the world but fail to receive recognition and respect from society. Many people look at us who handle waste oil with the same disdainful eyes as they view gutter oil, which is extremely unfair.
