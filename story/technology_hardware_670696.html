What are the advantages of silicone implant rhinoplasty?

by bacuomen512 on 2012-02-21 10:18:48

There are many methods for rhinoplasty, such as implant rhinoplasty and injection rhinoplasty. Among these, silicone implant rhinoplasty is a type of implant rhinoplasty method. Many beauty enthusiasts choose silicone implant rhinoplasty, so what are the advantages of this procedure? Let's take a look at the introduction by the experts from Shanghai Bidal Beauty Hospital!

When performing implant rhinoplasty, using medical-grade silicone implants has numerous benefits. Since they are non-toxic and harmless, and have relatively stable tissue compatibility, they are one of the main materials used in medical implants. In recent years, their usage methods have become increasingly standardized, resulting in fewer side effects and complications, hence their widespread clinical application. This material is currently one of the most commonly used materials for rhinoplasty. Its shape is basically pre-formed, and during surgery, slight adjustments can be made according to each individual’s situation before use. The color is similar to skin, providing a natural appearance without any artificial feel. Moreover, it is easy to insert and even easier to remove. If issues like infection or rejection occur, the implant can be completely and promptly removed, leaving no hidden risks. Therefore, this type of rhinoplasty material is currently the most widely used internationally.

Experts from Bidal Beauty Hospital explain that silicone implant rhinoplasty has many advantages. The base and implant bed should fit closely together. This enhances the relative stability between the implant and the surrounding tissues, reduces movement, promotes healing, and minimizes the formation of thick fibrous capsules. However, when performing implant rhinoplasty, attention must be paid to selecting and adjusting the appropriate size and shape of the implant. If the implant is too large and forcibly inserted, it can cause excessive tension on the nasal bridge or tip. Combined with postoperative swelling and poor blood supply, this could lead to skin necrosis or the implant protruding through the nasal tip or incision.

The subperiosteal tunnel should be appropriately sized and symmetrical on both sides. If the tunnel is too large, there will be no fixation effect, causing the implant to easily shift; if too small, the implant may easily penetrate the skin or cause skin necrosis; asymmetry on either side directly leads to a crooked nose after the procedure. It is important to minimize surface contamination of the silicone implant. Silicone rubber carries static electricity, making it prone to adsorbing dust and fibers which are difficult to remove. Any contaminants increase the body's foreign body reaction to the implant. Therefore, during the surgery, the implant should be handled primarily with instruments to minimize hand contact. Attention should also be given to cleaning, preventing talcum powder, fabric fibers, thread ends, shavings from trimming, or tissue residue from being introduced into the surgical wound.
