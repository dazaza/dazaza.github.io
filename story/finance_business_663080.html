On the morning of the 24th, they went to the Shitiantiao apartment complex for investigation. (Source: Guangxi News Network) Huang said that in order to delay the police investigation and increase the difficulty of the investigation, they once used others' ID cards to open bank accounts. Just as they reached the 13th floor, they received a message from downstairs saying that Wei with the high nose had just carried a bundle of purified water into the elevator, which went up to the 9th floor and then only stopped at the 11th floor. After investigation, the four criminal suspects including Huang and Wei confessed to more than ten cases of fraud through QQ over the past three months, involving an amount of more than 200,000 yuan.

At around 16:00 on the 22nd, it was learned that the suspects had moved to a nearby technical college. Huang was instructed to invite Wei out for entertainment at a KTV, but Wei was very cunning, he said he wasn't in Nanning and immediately hung up the phone.

Incident: Family of a girl studying abroad was defrauded of 20,000 yuan

Wei, who sells computers in Nanning, after discovering that stealing QQ accounts could make money, used a Trojan procedure to steal the QQ account of a female student studying abroad, and then together with his accomplices, defrauded more than 20,000 yuan from her family through this QQ account.

After investigating the IP address of Ms. Xu's daughter's QQ number, the police learned that when Ms. Xu was defrauded, her daughter's QQ number was logged in from Nanning, Guangxi. The Xiangxiangtang police assisted the police in a continuous four-day and four-night battle, capturing the four criminal suspects including Wei.

Through the private information, space albums, and friends' comments on the suspect's QQ, relevant information about the owner of this QQ was obtained, further confirming that the owner of this QQ, Wei, and three other men, Huang, Wei, and Tang, were the criminal suspects who defrauded Ms. Xu. In order to avoid attention, the gang rarely went out and did not socialize with neighbors. But there are thousands of units in the apartment complex, so they had to investigate each resident one by one.

According to Wei's confession, he had just graduated from university and usually engaged in computer sales in Nanning, having a good understanding of computer technology. The gang mainly communicated through QQ, rarely using telephone communication. On November 21st, the provincial police found that Huang and others might be living in Xiangxiangtang District of Nanning City, thus collaborating with the Xiangxiangtang Public Security Brigade to conduct an investigation. On November 28th, Wei and the other three were escorted back by the provincial police from Nanning, and the case is still under further review. However, after waiting until 3:00 AM the next day, the suspects were still nowhere to be seen. The police thereby confirmed that the suspects were in Nanning, and another QQ was likely the suspects' own frequently used QQ. As long as the other party clicked on the Trojan, Wei and others would obtain the password of the other party's QQ, log into their QQ, and then impersonate them to defraud their relatives and friends on QQ.

Tracking: QQ data locks down online scammers

In mid-November, the provincial police listed the four men, including Wei, as fugitives on the internet. After investigation, it was found that Huang was from Binyang, and he admitted to the fact that Wei committed fraud through QQ, and disclosed that the ringleader Wei lived in the Shitiantiao apartment near the university, but the specific room number was unknown. More than ten hours later, Wei was finally located between the 9th and 13th floors.

After confirming Wei's exact residence through online chats with the girl, the suspects Wei, Wei, and Tang were immediately captured, and three wireless network cards and three laptops used in the crime were seized in the room. Among them, Wei's prominent feature of a high nose left a deep impression.

Buying Trojans, defrauding 200,000 yuan in three months

In August this year, after Wei and several other fellow villagers from Binyang bought a set of Trojan procedures online, they randomly added others as QQ friends and sent Trojans to newly developed QQ friends. If accounts were opened in places like Guangzhou, withdrawals were made in different locations such as Nanning. Even when going out, they didn't carry large amounts of cash to prevent being caught red-handed by the police.

At around 18:00, during the investigation in You'ai area, the suspect Huang was finally captured near a restaurant. At around 19:00, one team waited on the 13th floor while the other waited near the elevator entrance on the first floor.

This year, on November 3rd, citizen Ms. Xu from the province reported to the local police that she had just been defrauded of more than 20,000 yuan. It turned out that Ms. Xu's daughter was studying in Singapore. Around 14:00 on that day, "mother and daughter" were chatting on QQ. During the chat, "the daughter" said that a close friend needed to return to China urgently for some matters but lacked funds, so she asked the mother for help. Believing her daughter, Ms. Xu quickly transferred 24,500 yuan to the bank account left by "her daughter" on QQ. After making the transfer, Ms. Xu called her daughter to confirm, but her daughter said she never asked her mother for money! In this way, Ms. Xu was defrauded of more than 20,000 yuan.
