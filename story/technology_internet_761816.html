Yahoo's acquisition of game company Loki Studios aims to recruit talents.

by geekzhang on 2013-05-11 10:42:09

May 11, Beijing Time - According to foreign media reports, Yahoo! announced on Friday that it had acquired LokiStudios, a location-based gaming company. The details of the transaction have not been disclosed yet, but this deal is also seen by outsiders as an acqui-hire, meaning that Yahoo! values the talent at LokiStudios more than the company itself.A Yahoo! spokesperson said: "LokiStudios has rich experience in community and location mobile services, and we welcome LokiStudios to join Yahoo!'s mobile team."LokiStudios was founded by a group of computer science students from Stanford University, with the goal of integrating game design and unique features into the next generation of mobile devices. The company mainly focuses on iOS game development, with its flagship game product named Geomon. Currently, the company has eight employees, all of whom, except for UI/UX designer Jessica Lee, will join Yahoo!.LokiStudios uses games to determine users' geographic location information, which will be directly applied to the game settings, such as weather, temperature, day length, season, regional geography, etc. Therefore, LokiStudios games can provide users with different gaming experiences in different locations, and any changes in the user's surrounding environment can affect the game itself.
