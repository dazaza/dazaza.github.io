Construction noise at the site triggered a mass fight among pigs, resulting in the death of 40 of them.

by anonymous on 2012-02-15 10:06:17

The noise generated by the construction of a highway caused pigs in a farmer’s home beside the road to fight with each other, and more than forty pigs were bitten to death. Conflicts arose when the farmer went to reason with the construction party, resulting in both sides having injured people. After identification, the son of the farmer had reached minor injuries and still needed someone to take care of him until now.

Last year, a certain expressway in Nanjing started construction. By the end of the year, the construction had progressed to a small area in Yuhuatai District. One day, Pan Xingfa, who lived beside the highway, found that his pigs in the pigsty at home, due to the influence of construction noise, kept fighting. They bit each other, and nearly 40 pigs had already been bitten to death. Pan Xingfa quickly approached the village leader, hoping the leader would intervene to stop the construction party from continuing the work. However, the intervention of the leader was only effective for the first two days. On the third day, the excavator of the construction party began working again. With the noise coming out simultaneously, the pigs in Pan Xingfa's pigsty began to bite tails and fight again. This time, Pan Xingfa became anxious and immediately found the village leader: "Village Chief, my pigs are fighting again. Out of 200 pigs, 40 have already died. It's almost the New Year, we depend on selling these pigs for money. What should I do if this continues?" The village chief let Pan Xingfa go to the construction site himself and talk with the construction party.

Pan Xingfa immediately ran to the construction site. He saw an operator driving the excavator, and the loud noise was coming from the machine. Without saying anything, Pan Xingfa rushed up and shouted: "Teacher, the noise from the excavator has killed my pigs. Please stop it quickly." The operator was a man in his thirties or forties named Ge Qingsheng. Ge Qingsheng was an impatient person. Seeing Pan Xingfa rush over to "order" him to stop the machine, he got angry: "You say stop and I will? I only follow orders from above!" Thus, the two hot-tempered people started fighting. The workers on the construction site also came to help beat up Pan Xingfa when they saw someone causing trouble. At this moment, the commotion on the construction site caught the attention of Pan Wei, Pan Xingfa's son. Seeing his father being beaten, Pan Wei didn't hesitate to start hitting anyone around. Soon after, Pan Xingfa's wife also arrived. The whole family fought with the workers on the construction site. Initially, it was just hand-to-hand combat, but later they picked things up off the ground and threw them at each other—pebbles, scrap metal all became weapons. The outcome was predictable. The Pan family, outnumbered, lost the battle. Pan Xingfa's toes were broken, and Pan Wei suffered from transverse process fractures in his lumbar vertebrae. After hospital identification, his injuries were classified as minor injuries. Until now, he is still unable to move freely and needs someone to take care of him. Recently, Ge Qingsheng has been reviewed and prosecuted by the Yuhuatai District Procuratorate on suspicion of provoking trouble.

Animal husbandry experts stated that this behavior of pigs fighting and biting tails is called "anti-discomfort syndrome," which occurs when pigs are affected by stress factors causing discomfort, leading to fights and tail-biting within the group. Experts said that this anti-discomfort syndrome could very likely be caused by noise, combined with the cold and damp winter ground, making the pigs restless while standing or sleeping, thus leading to irritability and a tendency to attack other pigs.
