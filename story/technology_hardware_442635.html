I. Preparation of Export Documents

A. Drafts: In international trade, the main instrument used is the documentary draft, which serves as proof of payment request by the exporter. When preparing a draft, the following issues should be noted:

Under a deferred payment letter of credit (LC), the term of the draft must align with what is stipulated in the contract. Some LCs require the issuance of a future-dated draft but allow for immediate payment.

Within the terms of an LC, third-party bills of lading can also be accepted, such as those issued by freight forwarders.

B. Consignee (Consignee): This field is also known as the bill of lading consignee. It should strictly follow the specifications outlined in the LC. If collection is used for settlement, it is generally made out to order.

C. Bill of Lading: "Advanced B/L" is a practice within the international shipping industry. If exposed, it could lead to claims or even refusal of delivery, causing significant losses.

D. Proof provided by the Chamber of Commerce must be handled according to the LC's stipulations.

Flowchart of Letter of Credit Operations

The simplified flow of operations for a documentary letter of credit is as follows:

1. Both parties agree on using a documentary letter of credit for payment in their trade contract.

2. The buyer instructs a local bank (issuing bank) to open a letter of credit in favor of the seller.

3. The issuing bank requests another bank to notify or confirm the letter of credit.

4. The notifying bank informs the seller that the letter of credit has been opened.

5. After receiving the LC and ensuring they can comply with its conditions, the seller ships the goods.

6. The seller submits documents to the designated bank, which could be the issuing bank or the bank specified in the LC for payment, acceptance, or negotiation.

7. The bank reviews the documents against the LC. If they are in compliance, the bank will proceed with payment, acceptance, or negotiation as per the LC.

8. Banks other than the issuing bank send the documents to the issuing bank.

9. After verifying the documents, the issuing bank reimburses the bank that has already paid, accepted, or negotiated under the LC as agreed upon beforehand.

10. After the buyer pays, the issuing bank releases the documents, allowing the buyer to claim the goods.
