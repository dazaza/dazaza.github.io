The government did not show the due sincerity.

by mjzd6713 on 2010-04-18 13:19:26

The office building of the Nan Village Committee in Hailing Town, Yangjiang City, Guangdong Province has been blocked for two months. The government considers the villagers who request to check the accounts as "causing trouble".

The office building of the Nan Village Committee in Hailing Town, Yangjiang City, Guangdong Province has been sealed off by the villagers for two months. Villagers have posted satirical cartoons on the walls. The committee office building is surrounded with feces-filled buckets placed on the steps leading up to the entrance.

Southern Rural Newspaper reported on April 17 that the gate was locked tightly, crisscrossed with nailed wooden planks and welded iron grilles on the doorframe. In front of the steps were several buckets filled with feces which the villagers used as a "resistance weapon".

Since being locked down by the villagers on February 11, the gate of the Nan Village Committee Office in Hailing Town, Hai陵Island Economic Development Experimental Zone, Yangjiang City, Guangdong Province, has remained closed. Despite the government organizing manpower three times to forcibly open it, all attempts failed.

Villagers defended the village committee site with feces. Government's three strong attacks ended in failure.

At 9 a.m. on February 25, there was a "cloud of soldiers" outside the gate of the Nan Village Committee Office.

Outside the locked gate stood dozens of elderly people and women. Some held onto the iron grilles while others had scoops of feces at hand. The steps in front of the gate had buckets full of what the villagers used as their "resistance weapons"—feces. Outside the villagers, there were police officers, auxiliary police, and other government personnel surrounding them.

To pull away the villagers guarding outside the gate, use a cutting machine to cut the iron grilles, then open the committee office gate which was nailed with wooden planks—under the command of a district leader at 9:30 a.m., the government attempted to launch a strong attack according to this "blueprint".

The government's action did not go as smoothly as expected. Officials found that after pulling away one woman guarding outside the gate, another elder came up. Most government personnel dared not step forward due to fear of the villagers' "weapons" because "whoever would be afraid of being splashed with feces."

The conflict was not as intense as the government had imagined, so much so that the two ambulances parked at the entrance of the Nan Village Primary School were never put into use, nor did the government personnel blocking the three exit roads from the village play any role.

At 4 p.m., the personnel gradually dispersed, and the government's first attempt ended in failure. 20 days later, the government again tasted the bitterness of failure, different from the first time, this time there were fewer government personnel. At the end of March, after the last action failed, the government no longer organized large-scale personnel to carry out a strong attack.

"We hope the government comes to solve the problem, not to escalate the conflict." The villagers said that their purpose of sealing the committee office gate was to force the government to help the villagers check the accounts, but they did not expect the government to act this way.

According to the villagers, since the committee office gate was sealed on February 11, the government has not negotiated with them regarding the issues raised by the villagers. The only time Secretary of the Town Party Committee Liang Xiuzhong came to the village was to warn the villagers "not to cause trouble," when (February 20) the villagers were holding a meeting to discuss how to verify the village accounts. Those who also believed the villagers were causing trouble included the secretary and director of the Nan Village Branch Wu Junsong and some officials of the Hailing Island Development Zone. Their consistent statement was "there are more than ten people in the village leading the trouble-making."

"Trouble-making? The whereabouts of 1.66 million yuan compensation is unknown, and the accounts for three quarters have not been disclosed. Is this trouble-making?" The villagers could not agree with the notion of trouble-making. According to the villagers, the so-called more than ten "leaders" mentioned by the government have been placed on a "blacklist."

1.6 million yuan compensation delayed allocation

Last year's village account only published 1/4

"The compensation for Dajiaojing Road has come down." In August 2009, this news gradually spread among the more than 280 households in Nan Village. After hearing the news, the villagers of Nan Village were looking forward to getting the compensation soon.

"Was the money embezzled?" Villagers who haven't seen the distribution of money started guessing the direction of the compensation. In early November 2009, the treasurer of the Village Committee, Wu Maiwei's statement "there have been no vouchers after April, unable to disclose the village account" made the villagers even more confident about their guess.

"No wonder only one quarter was disclosed, we must figure out the accounts clearly." Recalling Wu Maiwei's words and the reality of the village accounts disclosure, the villagers became more determined to clarify the direction of the compensation and investigate the accounts. On April 7, Southern Rural Newspaper reporter saw on the bulletin board of the Nan Village Committee account disclosure that only the first quarter of the four quarters of 2009 was disclosed, and the disclosure date was April 19, 2009.

Subsequently, the villagers began to report relevant issues to the Village Committee and the Town Government and requested an early resolution. On February 11, 2010, the villagers who received no response for a long time nailed the Village Committee gate with wooden strips. A week later, the villagers added iron grilles outside the gate.

"Finding them doesn't work, we can only make them find us." The villagers explained that such measures were out of desperation, "only by making things bigger can we attract the attention of the government."

"The compensation has always been in the Village Committee account, who dares to touch it?" On April 13, Wu Junsong told Southern Rural Newspaper reporter that after the government compensation arrived in August 2009, it has always been in the Village Committee account, untouched until now. The reason for the delay in distribution is that the various village groups have not reached a consensus on the distribution plan. In addition, due to the unsettled accounts of the village's water supply project, it has also caused the inability to disclose the village accounts.

"What distribution plan hasn't been decided? Afraid of problems being uncovered, right?" The villagers were very dissatisfied with Wu Junsong's statement. For this, Nan Village Group Leader Wu Zuwai confirmed to the reporter: "The distribution plan was already decided during the cadre meeting last year."

It is understood that the Dajiaojing Road from East Asia (a place name) to Wanghaiting was built at the end of the 1980s, serving as the main road connecting Nan Village to Zhaipo. In the early 1990s, the villagers contributed funds and labor to harden the road, but due to financial issues, the road renovation was incomplete and became a half-finished project. At the beginning of the century, after the Dajiaojing Road was renovated by the government, it was nationalized. As compensation, the district government promised to give Nan Village 1.66 million yuan as compensation.

No results from the Discipline Inspection Commission's investigation

Motorcycles of petition representatives were seized

On April 10, the villager representatives received a call from the Discipline Inspection Commission of the Hailing Island Development Zone to negotiate the account checking.

The next morning at 10 o'clock, a coordination meeting was held at the Nan Village Primary School involving nine people including members of the Discipline Inspection Commission and Audit Bureau of the Hailing Island Development Zone and representatives of the Nan Village villagers. At the meeting, the District Discipline Inspection Commission proposed two ways to check the accounts: one is to form an account-checking group consisting of the District Discipline Inspection Commission, Audit Bureau, and villagers, and conduct an account verification under the supervision of the District Discipline Inspection Commission; the second is to conduct an account verification at the Village Committee by the same personnel.

At the meeting, the villager representatives did not object to the plan proposed by the Discipline Inspection Commission, but only requested that the Village Committee convene a villager meeting as soon as possible to vote on the Discipline Inspection Commission plan and select members of the account-checking group.

Three days later, a similar meeting was held again at the Nan Village Primary School, with Village Committee cadres also attending the meeting. At the meeting, the villager representatives once again requested the Village Committee to convene a villager meeting as soon as possible to select members of the account-checking group. Representatives also hoped that the government would stop arresting people in the village to avoid escalating conflicts.

Regarding the villagers' requests, the District Discipline Inspection Commission did not raise objections. However, Wu Junsong, the secretary of the Nan Village Branch, proposed that the selected members of the account-checking group must be approved by the Village Committee before they can check the accounts, which was rejected by the villager representatives—"if there is no problem with the accounts, why be afraid of someone checking?"

The villager representatives stated that the government did not show the necessary sincerity, and the planned villager meeting scheduled for the evening of April 12 or the morning of April 13 has yet to be convened, "the meeting negotiation might be a delaying tactic by the government."

Now, several villager representatives are deeply troubled inside, on one hand waiting for the convening of the villager meeting and the start of the account checking, on the other hand worrying about their personal safety.

On March 2, villager representatives Wu Ruren and Wu Yuanwei were "almost caught" when they went to Yangjiang for petitions. Wu Ruren said that at 7 a.m. that day, he and Wu Yuanwei rode their bikes to Xialangxi, then took a vehicle to Yangjiang. After finishing the petition and preparing to go home, Wu Ruren was informed by his family on the phone that "Wu Dengjia had been arrested, you temporarily don't come back." Therefore, Wu Ruren and Wu Yuanwei did not dare to return home until they received notice from the District Discipline Inspection Commission on April 10.

On the afternoon of April 10, at the request of Wu Ruren, villager Wu Guangwei tried to drive Wu Ruren's motorcycle home, but was questioned by four strangers on the way. Later, he was released, but the motorcycle was towed to the Hailing Town Police Station. A few hours earlier, Wu Ruren's second brother Wu Jicai was detained by the Hailingdao Public Security Bureau in Xialangxi for "intentional destruction of property." Wu Ruren revealed that on the afternoon of April 13, the deputy director of the Hailing Police Station asked him to retrieve the vehicle, but he feared being arrested and did not go. Currently, there are still district and town staff coming to "inquire" about him. (Source: Southern Rural Newspaper)
