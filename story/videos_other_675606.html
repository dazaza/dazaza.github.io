Mr. Zhang, a counselor of a department at Jia Geng College, thought that the boy's behavior was more likely to be a prank rather than showing off wealth. If it was for the purpose of showing off wealth, then this boy might have some psychological problems.

In the video, the correspondent of the Dao Bao observed that the "water-skimming guy" in the video seemed to be somewhat drunk. Although there was only one boy in the video all the time, there was always a female voice. At first, the girl beside him thought the boy was just joking and suspiciously asked, "You wouldn't really throw it, would you?" After the boy actually threw the phone into the lake, the girl burst out laughing with surprise and joy, and found the boy's behavior incomprehensible.

Prediction: It might be a phone model

@Qiu Shi Da Bai Ke believed that the boy had been drinking. He posted on Weibo: when you wake up from your drunkenness, you will cry. The water-skimming guy used iPhone4 and HTC G10 to play water-skimming games.

"A mobile phone is not only useful in software but also in its streamlined function." In this video, a boy in a blue sweater played the role of an advertising salesman in front of the camera. With a parody tone, he said he would use the water-skimming function of the mobile phone to test which mobile phone was "awesome". Then, he showed an iPhone4 and an HTC G10 in his hand, briefly introduced the system configuration of these two models, counted to three, and threw the iPhone4 into the Lotus Lake. The mobile phone bounced six times on the water surface before sinking to the bottom of the lake. The boy happily turned to face the camera and said, "Remember, there are six bounces." Then, he threw the HTC G10 into the lake, where the phone bounced twice on the water surface before sinking to the bottom. The boy announced: iPhone4 defeated HTC.

A student surnamed Chen from the Department of International Trade of Jia Geng College told the Dao Bao reporter that he thought this was done to create hype, too showy and should not be imitated.

Netizen "Song San" expressed: I think he was pranking. But even if he wanted to entertain everyone, there was no need to throw two expensive phones into the water. I don't agree with such a way of entertainment.

It was understood that the "water-skimming guy" in the video was a male student surnamed Wei from the Class of 2008 of the Art Design Department of Xiamen University Jia Geng College. Many netizens and students in the Zhangzhou campus also expressed their opinions after watching the video.

Recently, a video named "iPhone4 and HTC G10 playing water-skimming!" has become wildly popular online. The boy in the video tested the streamlined functions of the two mobile phones by throwing them one by one into the Lotus Lake near the Student Activity Center of the Zhangzhou Campus of Xiamen University. The protagonist in the video was humorously referred to as the "water-skimming guy" by netizens. His actions left netizens confused as to whether it was a display of wealth or a prank.

Some students guessed that what was thrown into the lake was not a real mobile phone, but a model.

Relevant person in charge of the Information Planning Department of Jia Geng College told the Dao Bao reporter that today's society is a personalized society. Whether it is a prank or showing off wealth, everyone has their own ideas, but individual behavior cannot represent the group.
