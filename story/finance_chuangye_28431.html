by fenqu on 2007-08-29 15:01:22

Can websites that sell people really sell people?

Who would dare to go to a people-selling website? Would you? You definitely wouldn't dare, right? If you dared, could such a website really sell people? The internet is truly a vast world where all kinds of websites exist. Today, a friend recommended a people-selling website (www.taocaibao.com) made by his friend. After looking at the website, I found it quite interesting. The website treats people as commodities that can be sold. Each time a person is "sold," the website pays a 5 yuan reward.

In fact, this website is a talent recruitment site, but it differs from regular recruitment sites. It treats people as commodities, allowing headhunting companies, schools, and individuals to register as members and open online stores. Whenever a company invites a talent for an interview, the website pays a 5 yuan reward. Essentially, the concept of the website is online headhunting, and it represents the relationship between talents and companies in the form of online trading, fully embodying the free trade principle of market economy. However, I personally wouldn't want to be "sold" on this website.

Changing a talent recruitment site into a people-selling site, how many talents would willingly register and be "sold"? In my opinion, the ones who might get "sold" are likely to be recent university graduates or those who have worked for a few years but lack any skills. Truly capable individuals don't worry about finding jobs; instead, many good companies often approach them. Often, companies aren't short of talents but satisfactory talents. There are too many people in China, but there are too few true talents.

For recruitment companies, what they care about is whether they can recruit satisfactory talents through the Taocaibao website, which is also the basis for the survival and development of the website. So, with the 5 yuan reward, will talents really register? Speaking for myself, I wouldn't register just for these 5 yuan. I don't care about these 5 yuan. After working for many years, I usually find jobs through friends' networks, so the jobs and companies I find are much better than those from talent markets. Then, what is the core value of Taocaibao? Is it giving 5 yuan to talents? Definitely not. It's the effect. The internet is a tool, but a tool that cannot bring results is a bad tool and will surely be abandoned by netizens. So, the first thing Taocaibao needs to solve is how to help companies recruit satisfactory talents. Although the bold innovation of the 5 yuan reward stimulates certain talents, this stimulation is still too small to move someone like me. Analyzing the thoughts of talents, it all comes down to finding a good company, a well-paying job, and enjoying work! So, how can one use the internet to find a satisfactory job? It's not just the stimulation of 5 yuan!

To make the Taocaibao recruitment website effective, the most important thing is to let job seekers register as members, let companies recruiting register as members, and ensure information symmetry between both parties. Only solving this problem is key. Without talent data and recruitment company data, everything is useless. So, how can we make companies recruiting register as members and how can we make job seekers register as members? This is a secret that many recruitment companies urgently need to know! I once participated in the development of a talent website, so I know a bit of the method, though it may not be entirely accurate. I'll share it with everyone. Everyone knows the method of spending money on advertising for promotion, but the key is how to promote the website without money and let others know about it. For a talent website, the most important thing is to have recruitment companies. Therefore, I suggest Taocaibao hire a group of people to call companies currently recruiting on 51job and within Shanghai, send mass faxes, emails, and visit them in person to inform them of the existence of Taocaibao, encouraging them to register and use it for free in the early stage. Free usage in the initial stage is necessary! Otherwise, who would bother? At the same time, distribute flyers at the talent market to encourage talents to register! A talent website should learn from Zhongqi Power and use a sea of people tactics to achieve real effects. This is also the sorrow of Chinese internet companies, which claim to be internet companies but are essentially harassment companies with a large number of people!
