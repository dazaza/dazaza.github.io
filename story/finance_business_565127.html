Tibetan Mastiff gives birth to 18 pups, estimated value exceeds 2 million (photo)

by gogbjijang on 2011-11-17 15:10:35

The little Tibetan Mastiffs together are very cute. Photo by reporter Lu Bin.

Morning Post report: Tibetan Mastiffs usually give birth to litters of five, with the most ever reported in the media being 15. However, recently, a resident of Dangba Town in Gaochun County of Nanjing City raised a pair of Tibetan Mastiffs that successfully gave birth to a litter of 18 little Tibetan Mastiff puppies with varying colors, all of which survived, which is quite rare.

The whole family helped the Tibetan Mastiff give birth.

Yesterday morning, at Mr. Zhang's home in Xiaomaoshan Village, Dangba Town, Gaochun County, I saw the Tibetan Mastiff mother and her children. As soon as strangers entered the yard, the Tibetan Mastiff in the iron cage began to roar and used its paws to repeatedly hit the iron fence. "Be careful, don't get too close, Tibetan Mastiffs are very fierce!" Mr. Zhang quickly led the reporter to a place a bit further away.

Subsequently, he bypassed the iron cage at the entrance and led the reporter to the iron fence in front of the doghouse. At this moment, more than ten little Tibetan Mastiffs were playing happily on the ground, biting each other and pushing around in a heap. Since one litter produced 18 puppies, the owner could not distinguish who was the eldest and who was the youngest, and could only identify some of the more recognizable ones by their characteristics. "Look at the one bullying his brothers on top, it eats the most and grabs anything... The pure black one likes sleeping the most, ignoring the others' play... The gray-furred one, although just over a month old, is very mischievous. If there's even a crack in the door, it will run all over the yard and refuse to go back to its nest."

When talking about this litter of Tibetan Mastiffs, "Mastiff enthusiast" Mr. Zhang smiled broadly as he held a large and small puppy in his hands. The larger brother was much bigger than the smaller brother. Mr. Zhang told the reporter that the parents of these little ones were purebred original Tibetan Mastiffs. The heroic Tibetan Mastiff mother is named Jingjing, who is two years old and was transferred to Mr. Zhang over a year ago by a friend who also loved Tibetan Mastiffs for 30,000 yuan. After buying Jingjing, he specially went to Hebei and spent 170,000 yuan to buy a male Tibetan Mastiff to mate with Jingjing. "The father dog is called Dashuai, doesn't he look very handsome?"

"Our whole family loves raising dogs. One day over a month ago, from the early morning, I stayed by the nest and didn't go anywhere. Until late at night at 11 PM, the whole family realized Jingjing was going to give birth, so we all got up to help the Tibetan Mastiff give birth. We helped until the next morning when she first delivered 10 puppies. Thinking she was done, everyone rested because they were tired. 'I was so excited that I couldn't sleep, so I went to check on the dogs again, and found another 5 had been born, then later 3 more were born, making a total of 18 in one litter."

Not enough milk, the owner finds a way.

Mr. Zhang introduced the issue of milk for the 18 puppies: "People often say, the crying child gets the milk, but for Tibetan Mastiffs, it's the grabbing child that gets the milk. This smaller one can't compete with its siblings, so its body size is much smaller." With one litter producing 18 puppies, the Tibetan Mastiff mother couldn't take care of all the puppies, so Mr. Zhang had no choice but to feed the hungry puppies with cow's milk. However, cow's milk could only fill them up, but definitely lacked the nutrition of breast milk. So among the siblings in the same litter, there wasn't much difference in body size at birth, but after a month, their weight differed by nearly double. As the puppies grew, their appetite increased daily, and breast milk was definitely not enough. Helpless, Mr. Zhang bought baby bottles and mixed rice porridge and milk together, heated it, and poured it into the bottle for the puppies to drink.

Are the 18 little Tibetan Mastiffs worth over 2 million?

"A few days ago, a Tibetan Mastiff in Changzhou gave birth to 10 puppies, only 9 survived and were said to be worth 1 million yuan. My Tibetan Mastiff gave birth to a litter of 18 puppies, and all survived, isn't that worth 2 million yuan?" Yesterday, citizen Mr. Zhang couldn't stop laughing about his 18 little Tibetan Mastiffs, as his 2-year-old Tibetan Mastiff mother heroically gave birth to 18 puppies in one litter.

Not only was Mr. Zhang happy, but for nearly a month, all the villagers in Xiaomaoshan Village, Dangba Town, Gaochun County have been happily discussing this "heroic Tibetan Mastiff mother."

Author: Lu Bin / Source: Nanjing Morning Post
