58-Year-Old Cleaner Moves Netizens by Studying Characters on the Street

by zzf0zxyh00 on 2011-06-13 10:46:58

Recently, a post titled "The Impression Brought to Me by a Cleaner" on a Shaoxing forum has drawn much attention from netizens. The post talks about an aged cleaner aunt who, during her rest time, sits on the street with reading glasses, and tries to recognize characters with borrowed light.

Netizen lanweiyu said: "I was really shocked. In this prosperous city full of material wealth, I can still see such a determined heart for learning. Despite her old age, she is still selflessly studying and recognizing characters, and such a scene moved and impressed me."

Both husband and wife are cleaners

The nearby residents all know her

Afterwards, the reporter learned from the Yuecheng District Environmental Sanitation Department that this cleaner aunt is named Zhang Yafen. She is 58 years old, comes from Suihua City in Heilongjiang Province, and has been working as a sanitation worker for six years.

"She and her husband are both cleaners, taking turns to be responsible for the sanitation work in the middle section of Xinjian North Road," said Mr. Zhou from the Yuecheng District Environmental Sanitation Department.

Yesterday, when the reporter came to Xinjian North Road, the nearby residents all knew Aunt Zhang who sweeps the road here.

An old man said: "She works at noon every day. During mealtime, she will find a place with good lighting to read books and recognize characters, and everyone knows her."

Another boss who runs a store nearby also said: "This aunt is amazing. At such an old age, she still has the perseverance to keep recognizing characters. It's heard that her three children are all university students, one of whom is even a graduate student at a medical university."

Under the guidance of Mr. Zhou, the reporter found Uncle Hua, Aunt Zhang's husband, on Xinjian North Road.

Uncle Hua is also a cleaner. He said: "We both sweep this road. I start working at 4 in the morning until noon, and my wife takes over at noon and works until 10 in the evening."

5 PM is her character recognition time

Uncle Hua took us to his home near Qianwang Temple, saying that Aunt Zhang should be at home at noon.

"My house isn't very clean, please don't come," Uncle Hua kept saying along the way.

Turning into a small alley, the reporter saw Aunt Zhang. Her hair was messy, her skin was dark, and she had two red chilblains on her cheeks. Aunt Zhang felt shy when she saw us and combed her hair with her hand.

"Come in quickly," Aunt Zhang warmly invited us inside. In the small room of more than 20 square meters, there was only a bed and a small dining table. Other places were filled with picked-up waste items, and a foul smell hit our faces.

"Usually, I pick up some waste items to sell for money and supplement the household expenses," Aunt Zhang explained while feeling embarrassed.

Aunt Zhang told the reporter that every afternoon at 5 PM is her character recognition time.

"We have 45 minutes for eating, so after having a quick meal, I take the opportunity to recognize a few characters," Aunt Zhang said, "In the evening, the lighting is not good, so I sit at the entrance of someone's shop and borrow some light."

A Xinhua Dictionary, a pair of reading glasses, a practice book, and a pen are the four items always in Aunt Zhang's pocket.

Aunt Zhang said that she started recognizing characters long ago, but bringing the dictionary out on the street started last June.

"I only went to school for half a year and only learned a little pinyin. As I got older, I felt my brain was empty. I wanted to learn some knowledge, be able to read books, and broaden my horizons," Aunt Zhang said. The dictionary is her teacher. She has already finished reading more than half of it. She remembers some, forgets some, but now reading books and newspapers is no problem. Writing is just a bit slow.

For the children to attend university

Selling ancestral property and moving south

Aunt Zhang has two daughters and one son. She said that all three children are studious and have good academic performance, making her proud.

"My eldest daughter graduated with a master's degree and is now a doctor in Tianjin. My second daughter is an interior designer, and my youngest son has already graduated from the Civil Engineering Department of Zhejiang University and is now working at a design institute in Hangzhou." Talking about their three children, Aunt Zhang and Uncle Hua's faces both showed pride, "To let them study, my husband and I sold our house and farmland back home, went out to work, and accompanied them in their studies."

Uncle Hua said that to accompany their eldest daughter's studies, they lived in Harbin for two years. In 2003, after their son graduated from university, they moved to Shaoxing.

"In the village, my husband and I were very diligent, saved some money, and originally bearing the tuition fees for two children shouldn't have been a problem. But unexpectedly, when our eldest daughter was in high school sophomore year, she fell seriously ill, spending all our savings and delaying her studies for a year," Uncle Hua said, "Later, our eldest daughter and youngest son both passed the university entrance exam, and we had to bear the tuition fees for two children to attend university. Those days were really hard."

Also because of this reason, Uncle Hua's second daughter did not continue her education. "I feel sorry for my second daughter. To not add to the family burden, she started working after graduating from high school. But I never lowered my requirements for her. When we were in the countryside, I required her to learn agriculture and tailoring. Now in the city, I require her to continue studying. Later, she studied interior design and works at a decoration company," Aunt Zhang said.

Children have already settled down, why do Aunt Zhang and her husband continue to do such hard work? Aunt Zhang said: "We cannot rely on our children. We have the ability to work, so we continue. We cannot depend on them, and when we get old and can't work anymore, then we'll rely on them."

The first letter written by the mother

The son has always kept it

In the afternoon yesterday, the reporter contacted Aunt Zhang's son Xiao Cheng.

Xiao Cheng read a letter to the reporter: "Son, everything is fine at home, don't worry. Take care of yourself when you're alone outside, and study well. If you run out of money, tell us..."

Xiao Cheng said this was the first letter his mother wrote him after learning to recognize characters, and he has always kept it. Talking about this letter, Xiao Cheng's voice became somewhat choked, "Although it's only a few hundred words and the handwriting is very childish like a primary school student's, I've always kept it. Every time I see it, I feel both happy and particularly heartbroken."

According to Xiao Cheng's recollection, when he was in junior high school, he bought a Xinhua Dictionary for his mother. At the time, whenever she had free time, she would study together with him. His mother's spirit has always inspired his growth.

Xiao Cheng said that raising them three siblings was really tough for his mother. Now they have all settled down and have stable jobs, he has always persuaded his parents to take a break and not work so hard.

"In a few days, I'll be able to go see my parents. I'll buy some New Year goods and spend the Spring Festival with them," Xiao Cheng happily said like a child. "I originally wanted to bring them to Hangzhou for the Spring Festival, but my father gets carsick, so we decided to go back. I wish my elder sister could also join us. Our whole family hasn't spent the Spring Festival together for six years."

Let others go home for the Spring Festival

We two will continue working

Uncle Hua said that today is the Spring Festival, and they will continue working.

"Many colleagues from Sichuan have gone home for the Spring Festival, and the company is short-staffed, so we two will stay on duty," Aunt Zhang said, "Our children are all close to us, and we can see them anytime we want. Let's leave the rest days for others."

Mr. Zhou from the Yuecheng District Environmental Sanitation Department said: "As the Spring Festival approaches, many workers have gone home, and there is a big shortage of manpower. Luckily, they are still here. Uncle Hua is a good employee and has been selected as an excellent sweeper twice in a row."
