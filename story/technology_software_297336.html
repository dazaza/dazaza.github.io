The three levels of finance in DNF Xiaoxian county jointly invested nearly 2 billion yuan.

by dnfmeimei on 2009-12-07 00:29:01

Nanjing, December 5th - Wu Xiwen and special reporter Shao Min report: In the past four years, 44,000 retired soldiers in Jiangsu Province have participated in free vocational skills training. Among them, 35,000 people have obtained vocational qualification certificates, and more than 3,500 people have taken up positions as village branch secretaries and village committee directors, accounting for about 21% of the "village officials" in the whole province.

As a large province of military personnel, Jiangsu Province needs to settle approximately 15,000 urban retired soldiers and transferred non-commissioned officers every year. In the past four years, the provincial, municipal, and county-level finances of Jiangsu have jointly invested nearly 2 billion yuan, promoting a new method of retired soldier employment training and settlement with "no personal cost, full range of professional types, and consistent training and employment." They selected 110 key vocational schools, determining over 40 majors such as manufacturing, processing, services, and planting according to market demand to train discharged soldiers. At all levels, civil affairs departments have established information databases for retired soldier students, relying on human resources markets and public employment introduction agencies to prioritize recommending trained retired soldiers for employment. Various places have also introduced preferential policies in taxation and industry access, encouraging retired soldiers to start their own businesses.

Free vocational skills training has laid a foundation for retired soldiers' employment and entrepreneurship. In 2008, Hu Jibai, a retired soldier from Sucheng District, Suqian City, co-founded an ecological cattle breeding base with eight other contemporarily retired comrades-in-arms, earning over 400,000 yuan that year.

(Liberation Army Daily, December 6, 2009, Sunday, Page 1: Important News) (Source: Liberation Army Daily)
