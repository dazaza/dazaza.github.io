The after-sales service shortboard in the group-buying industry has yet to be rectified.

by beetgk2958 on 2011-11-30 09:54:37

Group buying, in essence, is a model of localized service for electronic commerce. In response to the increasingly rational trend among current consumers, the previous extensive strategy of group-buying websites that sought completeness and scale has already become limited. Taking a differentiated route and providing distinctive services has become the core competitiveness for group-buying websites in the future. Lahshou.com innovated new rules in China's group-buying market by adopting a "multiple deals per day" and "product + service" model. It subsequently developed services such as lunch flash sales, group-buying triple guarantees, call centers, and offline experience stores, creating a service model that differentiates itself from other group-buying websites. Wowo Group positioned itself as specializing in serving merchants in the lifestyle services category. Sales in the lifestyle services category (including tourism, photography, dining, movies, etc.) account for 90% of its total sales, while physical goods only make up 10%. At the same time, it launched six products, including regular group buying, nearby group buying, and flash sale group buying.

From simply cloning Groupon's "one deal per day" model to the "multiple deals per day" model, Beijing group buying, to LBS concepts, to real-time group buying (flash sales), etc., the service in China's group-buying industry continues to extend forward, creatively meeting the diverse needs of consumers. As users experience various novel experiences, they also increase their recognition and trust in group buying. However, issues such as poor delivery timeliness due to logistics problems, complaints about the quality of group-buying products and services not being answered promptly and effectively, etc., remain high. The after-sales service shortcomings of the group-buying industry have yet to be resolved.

In the long-term development perspective, none of the business activities such as expanding website promotion, improving service quality, deepening localization, and exploring market demand can proceed without financial support. One could say that group-buying websites must raise funds to achieve rapid development.

Currently, after a year of rapid explosion, the market structure of China's group-buying market has become preliminarily clear. Websites with resources such as capital, users, and merchants have become market leaders, while most websites that started quickly but lacked subsequent resource replenishment have gradually fallen behind. The continuous localization and differentiation of the operational models of major group-buying websites is essentially a competition for user resources, control over high-quality merchant resources, and service innovation capabilities. The key to the survival or demise of group-buying websites lies here.

Group-buying websites are basically the product of venture capital. A group-buying website requires several rounds or even hundreds of millions of yuan in venture capital to operate normally. Currently, the financing of group-buying websites mainly comes from two aspects: one is accelerating the establishment of sub-stations in second- and third-tier cities in China to secure territory; the other is using capital to fund advertisements to gain favor with the next round of capital. In 2010, China's group-buying websites received a total of 13 rounds of financing involving 9 group-buying websites, with a total financing amount nearing $7 billion.

In April 2011, Lahshou.com completed its third round of financing, raising a record-breaking $111 million; in May, Wowo Group announced with great fanfare that it had secured an expected $200 million investment from CDH Investments, Shanghai Jiashe, and others, and initiated the IPO process; in June, Mansuo.com completed its second round of financing with $50 million; in July, Meituan.com also raised $50 million in its second round of financing.

According to statistics from the independent navigation website Tuangou800, in October, there were a cumulative total of 1,117 group-buying complaints received, an increase of 6.8% compared to September. The capacity of a bucket does not depend on the height of the tallest plank but rather on the height of the shortest plank. Although various group-buying services continue to innovate and extend forward, the shortfalls in logistics and after-sales services are still obvious. How to improve service quality and address these shortfalls remains the top priority for the development of the group-buying industry.

The characteristics of low gross profit margins and large scale in the group-buying industry require group-buying websites to manage meticulously, using technical means to improve operational efficiency and achieve cost control. Currently, mainstream group-buying websites no longer compete on advertising or price, nor do they expand recklessly. At the same time, they strictly control the development pace of local stations, and even actively close some fourth-tier city sub-stations. These meticulous operational models ensure that group-buying companies maintain stable capital chains.

It is generally believed in the industry that the market's rigid need for group buying still exists. Group-buying companies need to change their previous extensive operating model, focusing their efforts on providing marketing services to small and medium-sized local life-service merchants, controlling their own group-buying rhythm and content, and achieving meticulous operations.
