Seven Types of Tableware Babies Should Not Use

Tableware made from materials such as glass and ceramics may be aesthetically pleasing, but because young children's hands are still very clumsy, "accidents" can happen repeatedly. On one hand, this leads to waste, and on the other, it might injure the child. Additionally, it could easily cause the child to lose confidence in learning how to eat independently.

Tableware that is too large is difficult for small hands to control, while tableware that is too small may result in food spilling when the child eats, artificially increasing the difficulty of learning to eat independently.

Adult tableware

Most tableware is designed for adults and is unsuitable for children in terms of both size and weight. Another disadvantage of sharing utensils with adults is: it can easily transmit adult diseases to the child.

Western tableware

Currently, Western tableware is very popular. Some families want their children to get acquainted with it early. However, Western utensils like knives and forks are both sturdy and sharp, which can easily cut a child’s lips. If the child falls, it could lead to more serious injuries.

Chopsticks

Under normal circumstances, children should only start practicing chopstick use at 3-4 years old. If they are required to use chopsticks too early, not only will it be difficult for them to learn, but due to uncoordinated movements, they may spill food. If parents become impatient and scold or criticize the child at this time, the child’s enthusiasm for eating will be hurt, and their appetite may also be affected.

Certain chemical material-made tableware

Although all manufacturers swear their products are absolutely safe, reality isn't always so. Therefore, when purchasing tableware for children, parents must choose qualified products from reputable manufacturers.

Hard-to-clean tableware

Some tableware is often difficult to clean thoroughly due to its shape, design, or texture, or cannot withstand high-temperature disinfection. As a result, grease and bacteria can easily adhere to it, making it unsuitable for children.
