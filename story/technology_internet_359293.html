Riding Diary: Wandering with the lakes and marshes, without praising the beauty of Xi Zi

by ditan360 on 2010-06-28 09:57:26

Exclusive to the Low Carbon Network: After a good rest, we set off.

It was an extremely comfortable day, so I mounted my iron steed and started galloping. The streets of Heima River have become longer and more prosperous. Leaving Heima River, there was a fork in the road where I almost took the wrong path, turned back, and then got on the west loop road to Bird Island. The west loop road starts from Heima River and runs close to the lake water, winding along the shore.

The headwind made cycling abnormally difficult, and we had to stop and start again frequently, taking photos whenever we encountered a good view.

The blue lake water churned with white waves, washing over the stones on the beach, repeatedly striking the lake shore. Riding here, I couldn't resist the allure of the clear lake water, so I carried my bike down to the lakeside. The water was warm, and I splashed some on my face, enjoying the baptism of the holy water. Standing by the lake, I felt that Qinghai Lake was so vast, just like the sea, an endless world. Amidst the vast expanse, it exuded mystery, serene except for the eternal sound of the waves.

In the distance, there stood an island in the middle of the lake - Heart of the Sea Mountain, about one square kilometer in size. It is said to be the cap of the spring at the bottom of the lake. If it weren't for the deployment of Queen Mother Wang of the West Sea, the water of Qinghai Lake would surely overflow this basin. I found this legend quite interesting - Tibetan land has legends from Han land; do the Tibetans also believe in Queen Mother Wang?

I had once appreciated the charm of the West Lake, seen the vastness of Dongting Lake, and heard the paddle sounds of Erhai Lake. But now, I was awestruck by the simplicity of Qinghai Lake. All those previous elaborate impressions were overturned by the charm of nature. I imagined that when the true creator of nature created Qinghai Lake, facing such a large canvas, he must have unhesitatingly discarded all delicate carving knives and wielded the largest brush, creating it in one go. Thus, leaving behind a lake without any embellishment or refinement, a rugged beauty, a natural beauty, a simple beauty. I truly wished to turn into a yak, leisurely strolling on the grassy banks of the lake.

Lingering by the lake, not wanting to leave, many experiences in life only happen once. This departure might be forever, so I caressed the charming lake water well, reluctantly bidding farewell to the deep blue lake water, and set out on our journey.

I remounted my iron steed and galloped endlessly down the road.

At 4 o'clock, I passed Shinaihai without stopping, directly heading to climb a pass at an altitude of 3430 meters. Going too fast, I almost lost my breath. There was only a little oxygen in each breath, and my head felt dizzy. I slowly rode under the prayer flags hanging above me, sliding slowly, wishing I could lie down by the side of the road and take a nap.

The headwind made my stomach churn with acid, feeling as if the food I ate in the morning was still stuck in my throat.

At 4:50, I reached Buha River, having cycled 130 kilometers today, and my legs were stiff. Standing on the bridge over Buha River, I admired its grandeur. It was strange how the river, filled with sand and mud, remained so turbid yet did not pollute the clarity of Qinghai Lake?

This is Bird Island Town, where we stayed in a self-built hotel.

When we woke up, we originally planned to visit Bird Island to see the birds. The owner of the hotel said that there aren't many birds left on Bird Island now. We would regret going. Again, it was the fault of humans, so these words led us to abandon the idea of seeing the birds. We cycled in the wind, pedaling hard, breathing laboriously, heading towards the next destination.

At 12:13, we arrived at Quanji Township and had lunch here. There were many mountain bikes outside the restaurants by the roadside. I bought some soda water and Gastric Relief for my companion, and after drinking the soda water, my stomach immediately felt much better.

Quanji Township is at an altitude of 3225 meters, with a street full of shops and restaurants, and many large trucks parked there. Apparently, this place is ideal for drivers and travelers to rest their feet.

We had lunch at Lama Zhuoma's home, a small Tibetan restaurant. However, the taste of Sichuan cuisine was indeed authentic, very spicy and delicious.

While eating, my companion frowned, saying he had a headache. The boss saw and came over to give him manual traction and massaged him for a while. He finally sighed in relief. Perhaps because of his love for spiciness, I ate a lot at noon. My companions also thought this was the most delicious meal of the trip.

After a brief rest post-lunch, we continued on our way. On National Highway 315, we climbed a long hill and then saw ahead a vast prairie covered with a sea of purple-red flowers stretching endlessly. The blooming purple flowers are called lamb flowers. As for why they're called lamb flowers, even the Tibetans don't know. Lying among the flowers, smelling the fragrance, and enjoying the wind's caress,

On the road, we met a girl selling watermelons. We asked her what place this was, and she covered her mouth with her sleeve and softly said, "This place is called New Spring Water Lamb Flower Beach." Not long after riding, we saw a white city at the foot of the distant mountains - Gangcha County. We reached the county at 4:50, which is at an altitude of 3300 meters.

We stayed at the "Plateau Hotel". What is called a hotel is actually a regular guesthouse, where you can manage to take a shower. However, this is indeed the plateau, and every movement makes you feel short of oxygen. My lips cracked severely, so I took Vitamin C. I bought lip balm and applied it, finding the moisturizing sensation quite comfortable. I also bought several bottles of soda water to prevent acidity. Today was quite comfortable, as we only cycled 74 kilometers.
