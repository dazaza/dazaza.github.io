Hospital robots can sweat and shed tears, and they can also die if they take the wrong medicine (Picture)

by aiipii12987 on 2012-02-29 15:30:38

The simulated man, just like a real person, can talk, sweat, and shed tears. (Photo by Zhao Bin, our staff reporter)

According to a report by our staff reporter Li Lin: "The patient's heart has suddenly stopped, his lips are turning purple, his eyes are closed; immediate rescue is required." Several doctors swiftly moved forward and respectively administered external cardiac compression and artificial respiration as emergency measures for the "patient." After about ten minutes, the "patient's" heartbeat gradually returned to normal.

This is not an ordinary patient; his name is "simMan3G" — the third-generation simulation man. He recently made an appearance in Xi'an, mainly to assist in teaching and clinical doctor training at Xijing Hospital.

He is very capable, able to sweat, shed tears, and even bleed.

Yesterday afternoon, at the Clinical Skills Training Center of the Medical Education Department of Xijing Hospital, simMan3G, a foreign male beauty around 1.8 meters tall and in his thirties, lay quietly on the hospital bed wearing a blue-and-white checkered patient gown. His eyes blinked occasionally, his heartbeat was strong, and his breathing steady. The monitoring equipment beside him displayed his vital signs: pulse 80, body temperature 37°C, heart rate 80... Suddenly, his breathing became rapid and his heart stopped. After "resuscitation," he gradually returned to normal. "The imported simMan3G costs millions and is our most expensive and smartest simulation man. He can sweat, produce tears and snot, even bleed, and he can also speak Shaanxi dialect," said Yang Guitao, Director of the Training Center. Speaking in the local dialect, sweating, and other skills are all controlled by the background doctor through a computer. According to the introduction, "simMan3G" is currently the most advanced product in international simulation medical education. Not only does he look like a real person, but he can also simulate various physiological and pathological characteristics of patients, producing corresponding physiological reactions to the treatment operations performed by the "doctor," displaying various physiological data on the monitoring instrument connected to the simulation man.

If the wrong medicine is used, he will feel pain and may die.

"Through the computer, any clinical case can be inputted, regardless of age. After inputting the data, he can suddenly develop heart disease or high blood pressure. If the doctor uses the wrong medication or makes a mistake in the rescue procedure, he will feel pain and 'die'; conversely, if treated appropriately, he will recover, just like a real patient," Yang Guitao explained. There is a chip intelligent recognition device under the robot's right arm, which can automatically identify drug components, concentration, and dosage during medication administration and then provide feedback based on the medication situation.

It is understood that more than 700 simulation men "live" in the clinical skills training center, covering all kinds of diseases and surgeries you can imagine. Just for heart sounds alone, one simulation man can simulate 130 types.
