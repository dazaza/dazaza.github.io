Microsoft Announces Discontinuation of External HD-DVD Player for Game Consoles

by eeq on 2008-02-25 15:28:13

According to foreign media reports: A heavyweight member of the HD-DVD camp - US software giant Microsoft Corporation issued a statement on Sunday announcing that it will exit the HD-DVD business and cease production of external HD-DVD players for the Xbox 360 gaming console. This marks that Microsoft has also followed Toshiba in abandoning the HD-DVD standard.

Microsoft issued a statement on Sunday, which read: "Influenced by decisions from Toshiba, Hollywood studios, and retailers, Microsoft plans to exit HD-DVD."

The statement from Microsoft continued: "The Xbox division will no longer produce HD-DVD players for the Xbox 360 gaming console. However, we will continue to offer standard gaming consoles and provide warranty services for HD-DVD external players currently on the market."

Microsoft also stated: "As previously declared by Microsoft, the company believes that Toshiba's decision to abandon HD-DVD technology will not have a substantial impact on the Xbox 360 gaming platform or Microsoft's position in the gaming console market."

The Xbox 360 gaming console does not support high-definition gaming or movie playback, hence the introduction of an external HD player later on. It remains unclear whether Microsoft plans to launch a Blu-ray HD player for the Xbox 360 gaming console in the future. Currently, Microsoft is not a member of the Blu-ray camp.

This announcement by Microsoft was already anticipated by the industry. After Toshiba, the leading figure of the HD-DVD camp, announced the abandonment of related technologies and products, Universal Pictures and Paramount Pictures have indicated they will switch to the Blu-ray standard.

Currently, another well-known member of the HD-DVD camp - US computer chip giant Intel Corporation - has yet to make any statements regarding HD-DVD. The industry generally expects that Intel will also support the Blu-ray HD disc format.

In the format war between HD and Blu-ray, HD generally received support from the personal computer industry, especially from companies like Microsoft and Intel. HD was considered closer to computers and aligned with the trend of home media center PCs. On the other hand, the Blu-ray format was favored by Hollywood due to its more stringent copyright protection measures.
