Wenger praises Arsenal for regaining their most precious wealth, reveals another key player is seriously injured

by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-07 08:40:12

Wenger praises Arsenal's recovery of their most precious wealth, reveals another key player is seriously injured. After a 2-0 home victory over Stoke City, Arsenal finally stopped their losing streak. Coach Wenger believes this was an important win.

In this match, Arshavin's goal opened the scoring for the Gunners. Unlike before, today the Russian played in the center. Regarding Arshavin's position, Wenger said: "I don't think he likes playing in the middle, but I believe he can, because his strength, movement, and technique are all good. His runs were very clever, and that played a role today. He could have scored two or three goals, overall he performed exceptionally well."

With Van Persie out due to injury, Arshavin has been entrusted with high expectations by Wenger. When asked if the Russian could make up for the loss caused by Van Persie's absence, Wenger replied: "I don't know yet, I need to try again, see if this style of play is effective both at home and away, and find a balance, because there are many technically skilled players around him, like Rosicky, Nasri, Fabregas, Eboue, they all pass accurately in the front field. When you have a player like Arshavin, it's essential. We will know the answer, but at this moment, with Walcott, Bendtner, and of course Van Persie absent, we don't have many options. But Walcott and Bendtner may return in two or three weeks. Today, our Diaby and Song were also absent, they should be able to return next week."

The three points from this match were crucial for the Gunners. With this victory, Arsenal returned to third place. Wenger said: "We needed these three points. I feel we performed well in the first half, we should have been leading by two or three goals, then in the second half, you can see the impact of our recent losses, because the score was only 1-0, we were a bit nervous. But what made me happy is that we didn't give the opponent any chances, we scored the second goal, securing the win. You can see how we play when we dominate, if we lose confidence, then we're in danger. That's why we can't lose confidence."

Wenger believed that returning to the path of victory gave the team a sigh of relief. "We sighed with relief, especially for the team. I want this team to succeed, we've recently experienced two big regrets. I'm not talking about the League Cup, but the away loss to Sunderland and the home loss to Chelsea were hard to accept. If we hadn't won today, you could doubt whether our spirit was affected, but we won, and that fact is very important."

Today, Fabregas missed a penalty. In response, Wenger said: "I think such situations can happen, especially when you face Stoke City, they are well-organized and tenacious, they don't need many chances to score, because they rely on powerful throw-ins and set pieces. If you make mistakes, they will take advantage. Ultimately, we seized one of the hardest chances in the first half and scored."

Wenger talked about Arsenal's current injury situation. "Today, Eboue had a muscle problem, Rosicky had a groin injury, Traore and Gallas had Achilles tendon injuries. We hope Eboue, Traore, and Gallas' injuries aren't too serious, but Rosicky will be out for a while. Dos Santos had a thigh issue, he had to stop training yesterday. It's a recurrence of an old injury, the doctor told me he'll be back next week," Wenger said.
