Reserve requirement ratio hiked for the 6th time this year; 380 billion yuan of funds to be frozen

by shootero on 2011-06-15 11:21:46

The central bank decided yesterday that, from June 20th, the RMB deposit reserve ratio of depository financial institutions would be raised again by 0.5 percentage points. This is the sixth time for the central bank to raise the reserve requirement ratio this year. Since the beginning of this year, the reserve requirement ratio has been increased at a pace of once per month. Since last year, the central bank has raised the reserve requirement ratio 12 times. After this increase, the deposit reserve ratio of large and medium-sized financial institutions reached a high level of 21.5%. Interpretation: The main reason is the new high in inflation. Du Zhengzheng, a PhD in finance from the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, believed that the new high in inflation was the main reason for raising the deposit reserve ratio. More than 600 billion yuan of funds will expire on the open market this month, plus about 3000 billion yuan net injection by the central bank in the previous four weeks, so although liquidity was not abundant in the short term, it was not tight either; at the same time, the market's expectation of interest rate hike by the central bank led to low demand for central bank bills, and positive repurchase could only lock up funds in the short term, so it was reasonable to raise the reserve requirement ratio. Caused by difficulties in open market collection. E Yongjian, a researcher at the Bank of Communications Financial Research Center, pointed out that the reason for this increase in the reserve requirement ratio was mainly due to the larger volume of funds expiring on the open market in June and the possibility of new foreign exchange purchases remaining at a relatively high level. In addition, due to the reversion of primary and secondary market interest rates on central bank bills, open market collection faced difficulties again. It means the policy tone has not changed. Professor Guo Tianyong of Central University of Finance and Economics indicated that the slowdown in economic growth and the aggravation of financing difficulties for some small and medium-sized enterprises had triggered controversy over whether the policy was over-tightened and needed to change direction. The central bank's decision to raise the reserve requirement ratio again also calmed down this controversy. The CPI in May reached a high point of 5.5% for the year, but it might not be the end of the line yet, and the future trend remained uncertain. Moreover, the amount of funds expiring on the open market in June was also relatively large. The central bank's decision to raise the reserve requirement ratio again meant maintaining a prudent monetary policy tone, effectively managing liquidity, and controlling inflation. Impact on banks: reduce net interest income by 5 billion yuan. After the adjustment, the deposit reserve ratio for large financial institutions reached 21.5%, and for small and medium-sized financial institutions it was 18%. E Yongjian pointed out that this increase froze 380 billion yuan in funds, reducing the net interest income of banks by approximately 5 billion yuan. Its cumulative effect would constrain the loan issuance of banks with higher loan-to-deposit ratios. He predicted that the amount of funds expiring on the open market in the second half of the year would decrease, and with some banks facing greater liquidity pressure, the frequency of adjustments to the reserve requirement ratio would significantly slow down. Real estate: Banks may increase mortgage down payments. Regarding the impact of this increase in the reserve requirement ratio on the real estate market, Beijing-based professional individual loan institution Weijia Anjie believed: First, the increase in the reserve requirement ratio would tighten the loan quotas of banks again. Under the current situation where banks' loan quotas are already tight, this further increase in the reserve requirement ratio would make life even harder for banks, making it more difficult for them to issue housing loans. Second, the increase in the reserve requirement ratio might force banks to raise mortgage down payments. Weijia Anjie analyzed that under the circumstances of another reduction in the scale of bank credit, considering operating profits or risks, it was possible that the down payment ratio for first-time homebuyers would be increased, which might increase the upfront purchasing pressure on first-time homebuyers. Finally, the increase would increase the market's expectations of an interest rate hike. Weijia Anjie advised potential homebuyers and those currently repaying mortgages to prepare in advance to avoid their lives being disrupted by interest rate hikes. Prediction: A very likely interest rate hike this month. Li Kang, chief economist of Xiangcai Securities, said that an interest rate hike was imminent, and the possibility of an interest rate hike in June was very high. Li Kang believed that overall, monetary policy would continue to tighten. According to the central bank's usual practice, there would still be interest rate hikes, and these would be passive, coping-style interest rate hikes, mainly aimed at curbing inflationary pressures, especially inflation expectations. At the same time, there was still room for further increases in the reserve requirement ratio. The increase in the reserve requirement ratio showed caution regarding interest rate hikes. Shenwan Hongyuan Securities Research Institute held different opinions. They believed that the adjustment of the statutory deposit reserve ratio would create a tightening effect on funds, leading to a flattening rise in the interest rate curve, with a greater impact on short-term interest rates. But this adjustment reflected the prudence of the management layer in introducing interest rate hike policies, which instead provided safety protection for long-term interest rates. In addition, Shenwan believed that the psychological impact of the recent increase in the reserve requirement ratio on institutions would be significantly stronger than its actual tightening effect on liquidity, thus providing corresponding opportunities for speculative funds. Interest rate hikes two to three times in the second half of the year. Senior economist Lu Zhengwei of Industrial Bank believed that although the economy was slowing down now, the pressure of rising prices was greater, so future policies would tend to continue tightening to prevent excessive liquidity from further driving up prices. He predicted that in the second half of this year, the frequency of increasing the reserve requirement ratio would decrease, and there would be two to three more interest rate hikes, with interest rates possibly reaching 3.75%-4%.
