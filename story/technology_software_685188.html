A woman reported the withdrawal of money from her bank card, but the police said that they were unable to investigate for lack of evidence.

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-01 14:02:08

A woman in Zhengzhou forgot to take her bank card after withdrawing money from an ATM. A man who followed her took 3000 yuan from her card within about 27 seconds and then fled. After the woman discovered this, she quickly reported it to the police. However, the police said that 3000 yuan did not meet the standard for initiating a credit card fraud case, so they would not investigate. Yesterday, the woman called our newspaper and said that as of now, more than two months had passed since she reported the incident, and the police still had not initiated an investigation. Not meeting the standard for initiating a case, unable to investigate.

Reporter Ning Tiantian with text and pictures.

At around 10:11 am on November 20, 2011, Ms. Bai, a citizen of Zhengzhou, went to an ATM at a credit union branch near the intersection of Xidajie and Nanshunchengjie to withdraw money. After withdrawing the money, she forgot to take out her card and turned to stand next to another ATM nearby to organize her wallet.

Ms. Bai said, "I will not give up on this matter. Now, it is no longer just about the money for me. I want to know if something like this happens again, which department should I go to for resolution? Even if it does not meet the standard of fraud as mentioned by the police, shouldn't the police help investigate?"

Yesterday afternoon, Officer Li Daji of Xidajie Police Station in Zhengzhou who was handling this case told reporters that this case did not meet the standard for a criminal case, it belonged to the economic investigation aspect of credit card fraud. The standards vary in different places, and in Henan, the standard for initiating this type of case is 5000 yuan. Therefore, this incident cannot be investigated, and without initiating a case, the surveillance cannot be retrieved. However, the day after the incident, they also used personal connections to have the bank take photos of the surveillance video for Ms. Bai to see. But Ms. Bai was not satisfied and still wanted to retrieve the complete video, which they were unable to do. "Later, we retrieved the surveillance under the name of another case for Ms. Bai," the officer said, "Even if this case was initiated, there are no clues at all, where would we investigate?"

[Event]

At this moment, Ms. Bai had not yet left. About three minutes after the man left, Ms. Bai found that her bank card was gone when she checked her wallet. She quickly ran to the ATM, but at this point, the card was already gone. She immediately blocked the card and reported it to the police. By this time, 3000 yuan had already been taken from the card. When the police arrived at the scene, they said that 3000 yuan did not meet the standard for initiating a credit card fraud case, so they could not investigate.

[Police]

Out of desperation, Ms. Bai went to the superior department of the police station to report the matter. It wasn't until more than a month later, on December 31, 2011, during the reception day revisit by the superior department, that the officers of Xidajie Police Station helped her copy the surveillance video from the bank at the time of the incident.

After reporting the case, the police said that it did not meet the standard for initiating a credit card fraud case, so they could not investigate.

Up to now, Ms. Bai has visited the Xidajie Police Station no less than six times, and the matter has still not been investigated.

3000 yuan was taken from the bank card, and after reporting the case, the police did not initiate an investigation.

The surveillance video provided by Ms. Bai yesterday showed that just after she turned around, a middle-aged man with a black pull-along suitcase, wearing a red and black cotton coat and black pants, quickly walked to the ATM where she had just withdrawn money. He looked back at Ms. Bai, who was standing not far away, and then began operating the machine. He then quickly left with his suitcase. This entire process lasted only about 27 seconds.
