The woman who donated her kidney to save her husband unfortunately developed stomach cancer, and her husband wanted to excise his own stomach to save his beloved wife.

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-02 08:29:30

He hoped so much that his wife would recover after taking the medicine. There were chicken, duck, fish, and meat in the fridge sent by neighbors, but his wife couldn't eat them, and neither could he bring himself to eat them. His wife had once donated a kidney to save him, but now there was nothing he could do to save her. The couple held hands tightly, encouraging each other to live on. The wife had once saved her husband by donating a kidney, but now she was suffering from stomach cancer with her life hanging by a thread; all the husband wanted was to give her part of his stomach.

Yu Qiaoni and Li Fu Kai wrote this story. Yesterday, before dawn, Zhang Deqian left home as usual, wearing a mask and rushing out. He started from his home at the foot of Zipei Mountain, took a bus, and then rode an electric bike borrowed from his wife's younger sister. "Before seven thirty, I can probably get to the hospital and pick up my test report," he told himself.

He touched his waist where a kidney originally belonging to his wife had been functioning normally for five months inside him. She was willing to donate her kidney and saved his life. But now she was sick, with advanced stomach cancer.

"I beg the doctor, take my stomach, all of it, and give it to her, will that work?" A simple and taciturn man was powerless in front of his sick wife, only able to let out a sorrowful cry in his heart.

That year, he married the most kind-hearted woman. He is Zhang Deqian, 41 years old this year. She is Zhu Aiyun, 44 years old this year. She is his wife now, but twenty years ago, they called each other uncle and sister-in-law.

Zhang Deqian's older brother passed away from uremia many years ago. At that time, Zhang Deqian was also at the age of marriage, but he couldn't find a bride.

"She didn't mind our poverty, saying she was willing to stay and take care of our family." In Zhang Deqian's eyes, his wife Zhu Aiyun was the kindest person in the world. Not long after their marriage, Zhu Aiyun gave him a son, and they also adopted a girl. A family of four lived happily together.

"I used to be a driver for a courier company, and she worked in housekeeping. Together we earned about three thousand yuan a month. Although it wasn't much, we were very happy. She was especially kind and good, and everyone who knew her said the same."

That day, she donated her kidney to her husband. In the spring of 2009, Zhang Deqian, who had always been physically strong, felt something was wrong with his body. "I lost more than ten kilograms in no time, the change was significant. Look at my palms now, they are red, but back then, they were as white as a wall, without any blood color."

Soon, Zhang Deqian was diagnosed with uremia, "either dialysis or a kidney transplant." Those were the only two choices before him, and there was another possibility that Zhang Deqian didn't dare or want to think about.

"I even drank stones, heavy stones weighing several kilograms, just swallowing them down." To save his life, Zhang Deqian tried every possible method. A nearby barefoot doctor told him that drinking stones could save people with uremia, and he decided to try.

"I drank, and she cried." Zhang Deqian drank stone powder for ten days, and Zhu Aiyun cried for ten days.

"Drinking stones" not only failed to help Zhang Deqian, but almost cost him his life.

But even knowing these folk remedies were mostly harmful and not beneficial, Zhang Deqian had no choice but to try again and again, waiting for a miracle to happen to him.

"Husband, my kidney is for you."

Seeing her husband getting thinner day by day, suffering unbearable abdominal pain, after this year's Spring Festival, Zhu Aiyun voluntarily proposed going to the hospital for matching tests and donating her kidney to her husband.

"Six indicators, three and a half matched, which the doctors said was rare." The matching went smoothly, but because of financial difficulties, they didn't enter the hospital until the end of July for the kidney transplant surgery.

"It was the surgery on July 29th, I remember it clearly. From her chest to her waist, there was a cut so long that it took nineteen stitches."

"When matching, the doctor took X-rays of her, showing one large and one small kidney. She even smiled and said to me, 'Husband, the larger one is for you.'" Zhang Deqian kept talking about how good his wife was to him.

This year, he basically recovered, but his wife developed stomach cancer.

They were looking forward to returning to their happy family life of four after the surgery. "After falling ill, both children suffered too, dropping out of school to work."

Just as the couple was expecting life to move forward along the path they desired, Zhu Aiyun's health deteriorated day by day. "She vomited severely and had unbearable stomach pain."

Starting from November this year, Zhu Aiyun was admitted to the Provincial Hospital for further physical examination. "About twenty years ago, she had stomach ulcers, but she never vomited this badly before, she could hardly move."

"I felt something was wrong, but none of us dared to think the worst." Zhang Deqian said this time his wife's illness was unusual.

On December 1st, after the pathological analysis, the doctor called Zhang Deqian and delivered the news he least wanted to hear.

"It turned out to be poorly differentiated adenocarcinoma, the doctor said the tumor was located in the pylorus region, and it had already spread. Only chemotherapy could help her." Zhang Deqian said, hearing this news, he collapsed on the spot.

That day, he fabricated the lie that it was "just a stomach ulcer."

Hearing the diagnosis from the attending doctor, Zhang Deqian was heartbroken. But he decided to hide the truth from his wife.

Soon, Zhang Deqian calmed down, holding back his tears, and calmly told his wife: "We'll be going home soon. The doctor said it's just a stomach ulcer, not serious."

The doctor told Zhang Deqian that besides needing chemotherapy, Zhu Aiyun also needed to undergo a "gastrojejunostomy" surgery.

Because Zhu Aiyun's condition was severe, apart from continuous vomiting, her stomach had swollen so much that she couldn't eat, requiring surgery to "bridge" between her intestines and stomach. Creating this "bridge" would allow her to eat.

On December 2nd, Zhu Aiyun was admitted to the Provincial Hospital for surgical treatment.

During the surgery period, Zhang Deqian stayed by her side daily. At night, he managed to sleep partially at the head of his wife's bed, with his legs resting on a nearby table.

A nursing bed cost five yuan per night, and he couldn't afford it. Due to the high hospitalization costs, his wife had to check out before her stitches were removed.

At this moment, he was eager to cut off part of his stomach and give it to his beloved wife.

"This won't save her life. I beg the doctor, she could donate a kidney to me, and I could cut my stomach and give it all to her, will that work?"

"One session of chemotherapy costs at least eight or nine thousand yuan, where am I supposed to get this money?" Again, cruel and realistic problems confronted Zhang Deqian, and this time, he felt utterly helpless and desperate.

Uremia had already drained all his savings, leaving him with a debt of 320,000 yuan.

If his wife began chemotherapy, she would need treatment once a month, costing nearly ten thousand yuan each time.

"She saved my life, but why can't I help her?" Zhang Deqian blamed himself, saying he was willing to give everything for his wife, but the astronomical medical expenses left him no room to maneuver.

"You must not let her know when you see her. She doesn't know she has stomach cancer, she thinks it's a stomach ulcer." On the way back to Zhang Deqian's home, he repeatedly reminded the reporter.

After his wife was diagnosed, Zhang Deqian actually considered telling her the truth. "Everyone in the family, including neighbors, knows except her and her mother, because we're afraid to tell her." Zhang Deqian said, fearing she might collapse mentally upon learning the truth.

But now, the illness could no longer be concealed...
