"LeShang" is still a new concept and is increasingly valued.

Unlike IQ and EQ which have been widely publicized and known to everyone, "LeShang" is currently a brand-new concept. There are no experts yet who have expounded on this concept, and it has not been included in Wikipedia. However, it cannot be denied that more and more people are consciously or unconsciously mentioning it. These real and natural expressions from celebrities and netizens may better prove that this kind of "Shang" is becoming increasingly important in work and life.

Zhao Yurun, the current CEO of Huayin Shengshi, is one of the earlier individuals to mention "LeShang". He once wrote on Weibo: "In the workplace, besides IQ and EQ, there is also LeShang. Making yourself happy, making others happy, the source of happiness, that's when a person becomes charming. If not happy, then why bother?" An earlier Weibo post by Yuan Yue, chairman of Zero Point Research Consulting Group, also sparked discussions about "LeShang".

He wrote: "Many men focus only on individual feelings when it comes to happiness, while women are better at feeling happiness from the perspective of partner relationships." Yang Lan then asked: "People have IQ and EQ, can the ability to gain happiness and joy be measured? What 'Shang' would you call it?" Subsequently, many netizens suggested: "Call it LeShang." Netizen QIZEUS also remarked on Weibo: "LeShang is very important; some people can accidentally become happy, while others just can't seem to find happiness." Netizen "Ting-JULY" wrote: "It seems like this world welcomes carefree people with higher LeShang more." There are many similar reflections about LeShang on Weibo.

High EQ does not necessarily mean high LeShang; need to prioritize others' feelings

Although LeShang belongs to the same "Shang" family as other kinds of "Shang", they are not entirely the same. People with high IQ might be stubborn and stuck in a rut, and may not possess the ability and mindset to create a pleasant atmosphere for others, whereas LeShang requires prioritizing others' feelings; people with good EQ might be too accustomed to controlling their emotions and overly protecting themselves, and may not be skilled at self-deprecating humor to make others happy, whereas LeShang requires self-deprecation to please others; Financial Quotient (FQ) and Adversity Quotient (AQ) are even further from the definition of LeShang because money doesn't easily buy happiness, and people who grow up in adversity rarely get used to being happy. Therefore, LeShang is a harder-to-acquire personal charm, and it is more indispensable to have interactive abilities that require feedback from others. After all, enjoying happiness alone is not as good as sharing happiness with others, and achieving shared happiness is not easy.

In the workplace: being an interesting person is more valuable

In the workplace, everyone can easily notice that those who chat and joke around, who are humorous and intelligent, often successfully gather popularity, while those who are rigid and serious, who don't smile easily, always make people keep their distance. Therefore, being an interesting person sometimes has more value than being a useful person.

Xiao Yang, a project manager at a certain cultural media company in Guangzhou, has extremely strong professional sensitivity in design, and the solutions he proposes always surpass the thinking of many colleagues. His talent and sharpness initially earned admiration, but slowly began to attract more resentment, due to his perfectionism akin to a Virgo and his male chauvinism like a Leo, which deeply tormented many colleagues: as long as it was a design solution he had decided on, colleagues hardly had any room for discussion; if partners didn't meet a detail properly, it would lead to Xiao Yang's dissatisfaction or even scolding. "It's impossible to communicate with him," many colleagues said. In contrast, another project manager, SUNNY, although slightly inferior in business ability, could lower her stance, discuss work with colleagues, seek opinions from all parties, creating a free atmosphere that stimulates creativity; during leisure time, she could also mingle with colleagues, occasionally eating together, playing mahjong, playing "Three Kingdoms Kill," singing karaoke, and even shopping online together, showing great cohesion and receiving much appreciation from superiors. Although both were promoted to project managers at the same time, SUNNY was faster promoted to department supervisor than Xiao Yang.

In life: living in the present is most important

People with high LeShang are easier to find happiness in life, and these people generally have one characteristic - living in the present. They neither sigh over yesterday's regrets nor worry about tomorrow's uncertainties. They just need to live well today, and that's enough.

A Dong is one of the members with "high LeShang." After graduation, he found a job in Guangzhou, with a salary not as high compared to other university classmates, but he lives happier than most of them. He fully invests in work during office hours, and enjoys himself after work, playing ball, games, watching various movies and web novels, instead of worrying about major life issues like buying a house like other classmates.

"If you can't enjoy the present, even if you own a house, it will still be hard to be happy," A Dong said.

Netizen "Eat Fruit Without Peeling" lost 3000 yuan in cash but remained happy: "Zhongguancun is really rampant with thieves. For the wallet that was eventually found discarded in the trash can, I am deeply moved by the recovery of my ID card and bank card." She also lamented: "I really have high LeShang, now I think losing a wallet and having meals provided by hundreds of families is actually quite a happy thing." This kind of Ah Q spirit is also one of the manifestations of high LeShang.

In the workplace, bosses with high LeShang will always consider others' feelings and respond flexibly according to different situations, thus more successfully gathering subordinates.

How to cultivate LeShang?

1. In the workplace, first handle the relationship between yourself, the company, colleagues, and work. Don't think the company will stop running without you, and don't feel that colleagues owe you, should listen to you, or admire you. Each colleague is someone you met by fate, and work is the process of completing tasks with these fated people. Don't overly care about who took whose cheese, but rather let your happiness spread to others, giving them pleasant and interesting interactions, which is far more valuable than scheming for negative impressions.

2. In life, understand contentment, cherish blessings, and "move forward step by step, look down while walking upward," meaning to have ambition and strive for progress, tasting the joy of life in the struggle, but don't blindly compete with those stronger than you. When you place your gaze on those less fortunate than you, you can feel a peaceful mind.

3. LeShang is also a high-level ability. Modern people cry easily, often feeling sentimental and nostalgic, but laugh less easily. If the topic isn't substantial enough or your actions aren't interesting enough, you'll only get indifference from observers. Therefore, broaden your horizons and learn to discover topics in life at any time.
