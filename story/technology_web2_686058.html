If you don't want to be a CAM engineer for the rest of your life, this is a must-read.

by htpxpcb7 on 2012-03-01 21:07:19

If you don't want to be a CAM engineer for your entire life, this is a must-read:

1. **Plan Your Path Carefully**: Don’t follow your feelings blindly! Based on personal ideals and decisions, most people don't aim to become academicians or professors but hope to live comfortably and enjoy life more. Therefore, it's important to carefully plan your career trajectory. Choose an industry to start with and gradually gain a deep understanding of it. Avoid frequent job-hopping, especially not just for a little more salary. In the long run, that money doesn't matter much. Once you have several years of experience in an industry, money won't be an issue. Frequent changes are not a good strategy; eventually, you'll end up being a novice in every field.

2. **Engage in Technology, But Don't Be Obsessed With It**: Absolutely do not solely focus all your energy on technical skills! If you put all your thoughts into this, you're destined to become someone like Kong Yiji (a character who symbolizes impractical scholars)! Know when to stop because technology is only one of the pillars of your future prospects, and it’s not even the biggest one unless you're content with being an engineer forever!

3. **Don’t Aim to Be a Technical Expert, Aim to Be a Comprehensive Quality Expert**: In companies, we often look down on someone saying, "He doesn't know anything, why does he earn so much? Why did he get promoted?" This is a typical and stubborn view of engineers. Is 8051 really that impressive? If someone can succeed, they must have their own strengths that you lack. Think about it, the boss has been running the business for many years. Could their insight be less than yours as a newcomer? They may excel in management, understanding the boss's intentions, departmental coordination, etc. Therefore, it is necessary to cultivate various abilities, including management, affability, interpersonal skills, problem-solving ability, etc. To become a comprehensive quality expert, the future will be limitless; otherwise, you’ll only be stuck in a corner looking at an oscilloscope! Skills beyond technology are more important! From ancient times to the present, whether in the U.S. or Japan, it's always been this way!

4. **Make Friends from All Walks of Life**: Don’t just socialize with engineers who share common languages. It's more important to interact with other types of people. If you hope to be a boss or high-level manager someday, then these are the people you will face daily. Understand their experiences, thinking habits, hobbies, learn how they handle problems, and understand the phenomena and issues in every corner of society. This is a huge capital for future development. Without these, you will be clumsy, stumble, encounter numerous difficulties, pay a lot of tuition fees, and significantly reduce your chances of success!

5. **Your Knowledge Should Not Necessarily Be Specialized, But It Must Be Broad**: Read more books in other fields such as finance, accounting, import-export, taxation, law, etc., accumulate knowledge for the future, which will be more useful later. Genesis2000! You'll avoid paying a lot of unnecessary tuition fees!!

6. **Seize the Opportunity to Transition from Technical Roles to Management or Market Sales**: If you want to have a promising future, you can't keep doing development work forever. At the right time, you should transition to management or sales, where the prospects will be greater. The technical experience you gained before won't go to waste and will still be useful. Management can help cultivate leadership skills, while sales can help develop market concepts and thinking, and also accumulate a vast network for future development! This should be considered the real pillar of future prospects.

7. **Gradually Overcome Your Psychological Weaknesses and Character Flaws**: Suspicious, sensitive, naive (in a negative sense, not cute), indecisive, timid, overly worried, too thin-skinned, not ruthless enough, dogmatic thinking... These are common personality flaws among engineers that must be changed! Is it difficult? Just lying in bed and thinking about it certainly won't work. Try helping a friend guard a stall for a month, and it will surely be effective. Practice rather than just think! If you don't overcome these shortcomings, everything will be impossible, and you might not even be able to be a decent project manager - despite possibly having good technical skills!

8. **Prepare for the Future While Working**: Establish your own working environment! Equip yourself with a computer, oscilloscope (you can buy a second-hand one), emulator, programmer, etc., early. You can take on some freelance work during your free time, get in touch with the market, develop a market sense, accumulate funds, and more importantly, prepare your own products. For us in the technical field, without money, only technology, the representation of technology isn't academic qualifications or certificates but products. Showcasing commendable products allows for technology transfer or cooperation in starting a business! Prepare first, wait for opportunities, otherwise, you'll miss them when they come!

9. **Learn to Promote Yourself Effectively**: Not only should you be capable, but you should also be able to communicate and write well. Take advantage of every opportunity to promote yourself and establish your brand image, which is very necessary! Create conditions for others to understand you, otherwise, how will the boss know you're competent? How will external investors trust you? Promoting yourself early ensures that opportunities will naturally come to you! Creating a personal homepage is a great idea!! Especially cultivating a reputation in your industry, with fame, high-paying opportunities will be easy to come by, and more importantly, there will be collaboration opportunities...

10. **Act When You Should Act**: There will never be a 100% guarantee!!! If the conditions are roughly right, take bold action and carve out your own career. Don't hesitate, don't wander aimlessly. Acting doesn't guarantee success, but at least it accumulates experience for the next attempt. Not acting means no progress, and to succeed, you must experience failure. Without experiencing storms, how can you see the rainbow? No one succeeds easily!
