Time easily ages, and past memories can indeed be cast aside.

by mimi456 on 2012-02-03 21:39:15

Youth is fleeting, and past memories can indeed be cast aside.

Gently brushing off the dust of the mundane world, in silent years, restless fingers often wander over Tang poetry, Song lyrics, ancient prose, and old words during quiet nights, waiting only to pass the desolate long night. A gaunt face slightly shows signs of age, drunk and lying by the riverside, watching the bright flames on the other shore, extremely enchanting, gazing at the moon in the vast sky like a disc, evoking thoughts of distant places.

A light white shirt flutters gracefully in the cool breeze. In the silent night, I always like to wander by the tranquil lakeside, counting the years that have quietly passed in the morning and evening, feeling the sorrow of youth fading... Silently observing the myriad forms of human life, melancholy in expression, the long-nurtured sentiments, that thread of emotion, entrusted to the pen under my fingertips, allowing it to play the melody of my heart's song... Spreading out a plain sheet of paper, writing about prosperity or inscribing sorrow, I dance drunkenly with my pen amidst the text. Sighing at the falling flowers drifting into the stream, lamenting the parting of people and the waning and waxing of the moon. The flowing water carries away red leaves, youth lightly paints the vicissitudes of life, and in my heart, there arises an involuntary sadness. Which of the thousand pieces of glass has disrupted my entire life? Bit by bit, these emotions have now all fallen asleep in the misty heart lake...

There have been many joyful gatherings and high songs, and many painful partings. Having idled away twenty-one years, I do not fully understand the Zen meaning of "flowers blooming on the branches of spring, the moon full in the heart of heaven," but I know that life is full of hardships, and the endings of partings and reunions often leave one speechless. Mourning for spring, grieving for autumn, how can gentle and serene words describe a transparent and delicate heart, and how can a naturally sorrowful inkstone depict the emotions hard to let go in this mortal world? Lonely and joyless, lingering between lines of text, laughing and crying foolishly.

Time flows like water, leaving silently. As the brush falls from my fingertips onto the long scroll, there are already ten thousand feelings in my heart... Frowning in deep thought: seeing only the land facing the vast sky, the bright moon facing the boundless heavens, maple leaves facing the bleak courtyard, lonely shadow facing the dim lamp...

Flipping through ancient poetry collections in my hands, three thousand years of storms have faded the glories of six dynasties and the peaceful dances and songs, and the sounds of war and clashing horns have receded. Although I do not know the height of the blue sky or the thickness of the yellow earth, I see the wind, frost, rain, dew, cold moon, and warm sun wear away human life, and the joys and sorrows of parting and reunion torment the seven emotions.

People gone, buildings empty, like startled swans, not knowing whose company will guide me back home. Facing the stars and moon, treading upon the earthly realm. Outside the ancient pavilion, by the long road, waving a sleeve to dance with the pale moon in the cool breeze, sipping a pot of aged wine to intoxicate under the vast heavens, through the solitary shadow of the ancient pavilion, watching the misty water vapor steam up the night.

The past is like smoke, and the poetic spring river flower moon night, its soft strings singing in the dreams of former lives. All the charm leaves behind only regret and sorrow, waiting outside the broken string for the eternal wandering of a crescent moon.

Rain and dew wash away clouds and smoke, cool winds cleanse vanity. A mournful tune disturbs the originally tranquil pool of thoughts. Today, drunk, I drink bitter wine, sighing with sorrow, the wine and worries sinking deeper, how can this entanglement of love and hatred in the mortal world be severed?

Youth is fleeting, time most easily casts people aside, let the worries rest. Gently laying down the sorrow in my penmanship, I transform the deeply hidden threads of emotion into cup after cup, a lonely and drunken libation. Through the cycles of time, so much beauty comes and goes silently, leaving only a handful of residual dew to seep in. Why burden oneself with a dim smile that endlessly transforms through the cycles of spring and autumn? It would be better to send forth a sense of composure, carry a jug of tranquility, and remain calm amidst the purple paths and fine dust of life.
