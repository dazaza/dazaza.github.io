Talk about Organic Glass Acrylic (PMMA): Commonly known as organic glass, also called acrylic, PMMA is an important thermoplastic plastic developed earlier, with good transparency, chemical stability, weather resistance, easy coloring, easy processing, and a beautiful appearance, widely used in construction. Organic glass products are usually divided into cast plates, extruded plates, and mold plastics.

2. Production and Market Situation of Acrylic PMMA in China: China's current total production capacity of acrylic products is around 1.3 million tons per day, with many manufacturers but small production scales.

China's acrylic production and processing capacity is large, but the product structure is not entirely reasonable, only able to produce ordinary varieties, lacking high-value-added products needed by the construction industry. In terms of variety and quality, they cannot meet market demands. For example, the large demand for extruded plates in construction all needs to be imported in large quantities from abroad.

In recent years, China's consumption of acrylic has grown rapidly, but due to the monotonous variety, smaller quantity, and poorer quality of China's acrylic PMMA products, they are far from meeting domestic market demands, leading to a significant increase in imports year after year. In 2000, China's import volume of PMMA mold plastics reached 77,000 tons, and at the same time, nearly 4,000 tons of organic glass plates were imported. From the perspective of import regions, the combined import volume of Guangdong, Shanghai, Zhejiang, and Jiangsu provinces accounts for approximately 95% of the national total import volume. Jiangsu and Shanghai are the main production areas for organic glass cast plates in China. As early as the 1920s, Rohm and Haas Company first invented the method of synthesizing acrylic sheets from MMA, which were called "oroglas/plexiglas." However, this company failed to find a relatively economical production route for MMA, so acrylic sheets did not become widespread at the time.

In 1931, scientists from ICI Company invented the acetone cyanohydrin method for producing MMA, which greatly reduced the cost of MMA, allowing acrylic sheets to become popularized. At the same time, ICI Company successfully developed acrylic sheets for applications such as fighter aircraft canopy hoods, and subsequently built the world's largest acrylic sheet factory in the UK.

Comparison between Acrylic Sheets and Organic Glass: Organic glass originated from the trade name "oroglas," meaning "organic glass." As mentioned earlier, oroglas is actually a PMMA sheet, i.e., an acrylic sheet. Some people in China believe that transparent plastic is organic glass. To avoid confusion and misunderstanding, now most refer to PMMA sheets as acrylic sheets, while various PMMA materials are generally referred to as acrylic.

Types of Organic Glass Acrylic: Acrylic sheets can be classified by polymerization process into cast sheets and extruded sheets, where cast sheets have higher molecular weights, providing greater rigidity and crack resistance, making them suitable for processing large-sized signs. Moreover, the casting process is better suited for producing small batches of different colored sheets. Extruded sheets have lower molecular weights, higher flexibility, suitable for vacuum forming, and the extrusion process can better control sheet thickness, making mass production of single-colored sheets more economical.

Acrylic sheets can also be categorized by transparency into pure transparent sheets, colored transparent sheets, semi-transparent sheets (such as colored sheets); by surface gloss into high-gloss sheets, silk-gloss sheets, and matte sheets (also known as frosted sheets); and by performance into regular sheets, impact-resistant sheets, UV-resistant sheets, flame-retardant sheets, and highly wear-resistant sheets.

Characteristics of Organic Glass Acrylic: Organic glass has crystal-like transparency, with a light transmittance of over 92%. Colored acrylic has excellent color presentation effects. Additionally, acrylic sheets have excellent weather resistance, higher surface hardness and gloss, and good high-temperature performance. Acrylic sheets have good processing properties, capable of thermal forming (including molding, blow molding, and vacuum forming) and mechanical processing methods like drilling, turning, and cutting. Microcomputer-controlled mechanical cutting and engraving not only significantly improve processing accuracy but also allow for patterns and shapes that traditional methods cannot achieve. Furthermore, acrylic sheets can be cut and engraved with lasers, creating unique products.

Transparent acrylic sheets have light transmission rates comparable to glass, but with only half the density. Besides, it is not as fragile as glass, and even if damaged, it does not form sharp fragments like glass. The wear resistance of acrylic sheets is similar to aluminum, resisting corrosion from multiple chemicals. Acrylic sheets have good printability and sprayability, and appropriate printing (such as screen printing) and spraying processes can give acrylic products ideal surface decorative effects.

Special modified acrylic sheets can absorb a large amount of ultraviolet light. Flame-retardant acrylic can help overcome fire hazards in specific venues. High-impact acrylic has an impact strength six to ten times that of regular acrylic, and super-wear-resistant acrylic treated on the surface can withstand rigorous tests.

Advantages of Acrylic Bathtubs: The formal name of acrylic bathtubs is fiberglass-reinforced plastic bathtubs (acrylic is the phonetic translation of ACRYLIC). Its surface material is methyl methacrylate, covered with fiberglass on the back and coated with specialized resin for reinforcement. The entire bathtub should have a uniform color, a smooth surface, no layers or bubbles. The surface layer thickness is generally above 3 mm, firmly bonded with fiberglass without peeling.

Acrylic bathtubs transmit heat slowly, thus providing good insulation and a non-"cold" feeling when in contact with the skin. Compared to cast iron or steel bathtubs, they offer a "warm, soft" sensation, not hurting the body. Due to acrylic's good reprocessing properties, luxury massage bathtubs are best made from it.

When installing acrylic bathtubs, the bottom should be fully covered with sand-cement slurry, not left empty or supported by just a few points to avoid cracking. During use, sharp objects should be avoided for scratching and friction, and cleaning should be done with soft cloths and cleaners, avoiding scrub sponges. If scratches appear, they can be restored by wiping with a soft cloth and toothpaste.

How to Identify Sanitary Acrylic Sheets:

Currently, a new type of acrylic bathtub is becoming popular domestically and gradually gaining consumer acceptance. However, there are three types of acrylic sheets on the domestic market: sanitary-specific acrylic sheets, regular acrylic sheets, and ABS-acrylic composite sheets. The latter two types of sheets have certain material defects and are actually not suitable for making bathtubs, basins, and other sanitary ware.

The chemical name of acrylic is methyl methacrylate, i.e., "organic glass". Specialized acrylic sheets used for sanitary ware have excellent surface smoothness, with wear resistance equivalent to aluminum, not prone to scratching, and easy to clean. If accidental surface scratches occur, they can be quickly and conveniently repaired using polishing agents. Due to the elasticity of the sheets themselves, even if the surface is impacted to some extent, it is not easy to crack or break. Additionally, specialized acrylic sheets for sanitary ware have strong corrosion resistance and transparency. Since pigments can dissolve into the sheets, their colors remain stable, not fading easily even with prolonged exposure to sunlight and rain. Therefore, specialized acrylic sheets for sanitary ware are not only used for indoor and outdoor decoration but are also widely applied in other industries.

Regular acrylic sheets, due to their low hardness, poor heat resistance, and low extensibility, are prone to cracking, breaking, and deforming, and their colors are unstable. During use, they easily develop a fuzzy appearance. The bathtubs formed from these sheets usually have large stretches at the bottom, which can easily lead to color changes, typically showing "whitening" or "yellowing" at the corners of the bottom.

ABS-acrylic composite sheets are made by bonding acrylic sheets to both sides of ABS sheets. This can be easily observed at the edges of the sanitary ware's flanges (cutting edges). Since ABS-acrylic composite sheets are made by bonding two different materials, their shrinkage rates are inconsistent, and after long-term temperature changes, they are prone to delamination. Again, since the surface of ABS sheets only has a few millimeters thick layer of acrylic, their stability and wear resistance are both poor, and during formation, they can easily produce "whitening" or other discoloration phenomena.
