Leftover yuanxiao should not be eaten oil-fried, seek medical advice from the website.

by lokes350 on 2012-02-22 14:43:43

On the 15th day of the first lunar month, people eat tangyuan and yuanxiao which symbolize reunion. However, because of their high sugar, high fat, and indigestible characteristics, some consumers should be mindful when eating them. The main ingredient of tangyuan is glutinous rice. In traditional Chinese medicine, glutinous rice is considered warm in nature. The fillings added to tangyuan provide a large amount of energy, so eating tangyuan can warm the stomach and intestines. For those who are thin, weak, or have cold stomach and intestines, it is a good health-preserving food. However, experts point out that it is not suitable to eat tangyuan in the morning. The early morning is precisely the time when the human gastrointestinal function is weakest. Eating tangyuan, made from glutinous rice which is sticky and hard to digest, as breakfast can easily cause indigestion and acid reflux. Similarly, tangyuan is not suitable as a midnight snack, to avoid increasing the digestive burden on the stomach and intestines at night.

In addition to not being suitable for breakfast, tangyuan should not be eaten as a main meal. You can eat some tangyuan around nine or ten o'clock after breakfast or during an afternoon snack, but each time you eat one or two ounces (50-100 grams) is enough. While eating tangyuan, you should not consume other sugary foods; you also need to reduce the intake of other staple foods. Generally speaking, if you eat three tangyuan, you should reduce your staple food by half an ounce (25 grams).

If the night snack is too close to bedtime, it's best not to eat tangyuan. These foods are difficult to digest and empty slowly, which can increase the burden on the gastrointestinal tract. Some people with weak gastrointestinal functions may experience acid reflux or heartburn after eating tangyuan. Therefore, you shouldn't eat too much tangyuan at once. It's better to eat small amounts multiple times, drink more soup, and not lie down immediately after eating. It's best to stay active for more than two hours before sleeping.

"Since tangyuan is difficult to digest, people with poor digestive systems, the elderly, and children should eat less. People with gastrointestinal diseases are more likely to experience heartburn and acid reflux, so they should only take a small taste on an empty stomach. Diabetics should ideally avoid sweet tangyuan," said Yu Renwen, the chief nutritionist of the Senior Cadre Ward at Beijing Military General Hospital. He pointed out that the leftover tangyuan from the first meal should not be fried and eaten again, as this method increases the oil content, posing a health risk especially for patients with gallbladder or pancreas problems.

In fact, yuanxiao and tangyuan are not without benefits. Some trace elements and nutrients dissolve in the soup used to cook them. Therefore, it's best to drink the soup after eating tangyuan, which can promote digestion and absorption, as the saying goes: "the original broth resolves the original food." However, when drinking the soup, be sure not to add sugar. If you're eating salty tangyuan, you can add some small shrimp and vegetables to the soup to supplement nutrition.

Whether eating yuanxiao or drinking the soup, it's important not to eat or drink anything too hot. Medical research has proven that food burns can lead to related diseases such as esophageal cancer or gastric cancer. Some people swallow the hot tangyuan quickly when scalded, which is even more harmful.
