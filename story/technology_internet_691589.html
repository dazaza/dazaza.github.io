Macolm shifts its business model to prepare for the 2012 Chinese e-commerce market.

by maikaolin0111 on 2012-03-05 16:47:17

China's largest online apparel enterprise Maecolin is committed to providing customers with fashionable, children's shoes and clothing, products worth more than their cost, and a pleasant shopping experience. Maecolin not only provides its own brands Euromoda (Oumengda) and Rampage (Lan Peiqi), but also carefully selected third-party brands, including well-known international brands and some distinctive local brands. China's e-commerce industry is still booming. Investors from all over the world are rushing in, and the purchasing enthusiasm of Chinese consumers is also rising. Maecolin is one of the witnesses of this wave. Among Maecolin's three retail channels, the development momentum of online business has the potential to replace the other two. This can be seen in the company's third-quarter financial report: Maecolin's offline direct-operated stores (excluding franchise stores) have decreased from 143 last year to 115 this year. Although the total net revenue was $53.13 million, a decrease of 4% compared to the same period last year, the average monthly visitors to Maecolin's website increased by 23% compared to the same period last year. "This trend also verifies our strategy," said Maecolin CEO Gu Beichun in a public statement. As a veteran e-commerce company, how to reposition itself in fierce competition and shape core competitiveness? Maecolin CEO Gu Beichun said that some companies adopt a multi-product sales platform strategy. On the issue of doing brands or platforms, after considering and balancing market competition patterns, economic development trends, Maecolin's own strengths, styles, and scale, etc., we have adjusted our strategy: return to the positioning of mainly doing brands. We will go deep into the vertical field of clothing, provide unique style fashion products for a specific demand group, and differentiate ourselves from platform e-commerce with our own style. Platforms and brands each have their advantages and disadvantages. Platform enterprises can reach a considerable scale, especially for maternal and infant products. However, the current competitive landscape of domestic platform e-commerce is already relatively clear, with Taobao having more than 80% of the market share. To win in this market requires substantial capital investment and preparation for making no profit for 5-10 years. Secondly, there is a winner-takes-all phenomenon in the platform e-commerce market, where only 3-4 platforms may succeed in a certain category. Therefore, positioning as a platform e-commerce must face significant risks. At the same time, in such a competitive landscape, the requirements for operational capabilities on the platform and capital investment are high, including technical platforms and logistics platforms, etc. As a brand e-commerce, more advantages can be obtained. The Internet can become one of your channels. You can use online channels, such as Taobao's Singles' Day, or other platforms to expand brand influence. Meanwhile, we can focus more on enhancing our style and products to attract consumers. Moreover, the specialties and capabilities required for brand operators and platform operators differ greatly, as do talent reserves. Teams doing platforms focus on operations, while doing brands requires a strong creative and design team. Therefore, the current strategic positioning is a decision made based on the company's style and strengths, combined with market competition conditions. With the positioning and style of the brand, a fixed customer base will be obtained. We hope to provide better fashion products for Maecolin's customers, allowing more customers to choose the products they like.
