Agent reveals: how does 360 compete with Baidu?

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 15:07:02

After causing a stir online, 360 Search ultimately still needs to focus on monetization. Thus, building a search marketing system has become one of 360's main tasks this year. Under the immense pressure from Baidu's extensive nationwide channels, how is 360, as a latecomer, managing to break into the market?

The reporter interviewed some local agents of 360 Search to uncover the expansion of 360’s search marketing system. We learned that 360’s rapidly growing market share made the agents smell an opportunity for profit, and Baidu’s tightening of agency rights over the years indirectly contributed to agents switching sides. Both the number of 360’s agents and order volume have seen significant growth compared to the beginning of the year.

Specific details are as follows:

1. In fact, the agency rights for 360 Search are highly sought after by agents. To date, 360 Search agents cover 16 provinces and cities, totaling 27 agencies, up from 13 four months ago. Moreover, many local agents have started developing sub-agencies. The agency rights for 360 Search are quite popular locally, "just like during Baidu's early development phase when many agents reaped substantial benefits. Therefore, with 360 entering the search market, they were eager to secure the agency rights as soon as possible," said an agent from Henan to Sohu IT, who obtained the agency rights last month.

2. The development of 360 agents also benefited from the gap created by Baidu reclaiming its agency rights. The aforementioned Henan-based agent stated, "After so many years of development, Baidu has reclaimed many local agency rights and set up branches or provincial chief agents." 360 does not have branches in various places but still primarily relies on agents. Some of these agents previously handled Baidu ads, others handled Sogou or Sou Ads, and some even handled 3721 ads. However, currently, they have shifted their focus to 360, with most agents establishing independent companies specifically to handle 360 ads.

3. Low prices are a significant advantage of 360 Search ads at the local level. A Wuhan-based agent believes that the current advantages of 360 Search ads are: less competition, lower prices, and higher conversion rates. "The price of some 360 Search keyword ads is less than one-fifth of Baidu's," he said. Leveraging its reputation as the "second-largest search engine" and the groundwork laid by Baidu in educating the market, this agent claims promotion isn't too difficult. However, many clients remain on the fence because 360 just entered the market, and they "also want to see if 360 is truly committed to making its search engine successful."

4. Over the past six months, the number of completed orders for 360 Search ads has grown rapidly. In March, we reported that 360 Search had completed about 1,000 orders in Beijing within three months. A salesperson from Tian Di Online, one of 360’s Beijing agents, told us that now they can exceed 600 orders per month alone. There are two agents for 360 Search in Beijing: Tian Di Online and Ju Tao Culture, the latter being newer and smaller in scale. Henan San Bai Liu, which just obtained the agency rights last month, has already completed more than 1,000 orders.

5. The average order value for completed transactions is around 10,000 yuan, which is not low. The minimum opening fee for a 360 Search ad account is 4,600 yuan, including a 600 yuan service fee and a 4,000 yuan prepayment for ad placement. The Wuhan agent revealed that the current average order value is approximately 10,000 yuan. For comparison, Baidu’s average customer revenue last quarter was 14,500 yuan.

6. 360 Search ads now cover all industries except healthcare. Recently, 360 opened up previously restricted industries such as health supplements and adult products. Notably, 360 only accepts corporate clients and does not accept individual clients (Baidu accepts individual clients), and each company can only place ads for one site. Many agents are developing offline customers in sectors such as education and consulting.

But can 360 threaten Baidu? No.

Although 360's agent system has initially taken shape, its competition with Baidu is still in a very early stage. One Baidu agent frankly stated, "Currently, 360 does not pose a threat to Baidu."

Based on feedback from various 360 agents, the current competition is not yet intense. "Right now, 360 Search is mainly giving clients a new market and a new choice," one agent said, noting that many clients advertise on both platforms.

However, this situation is likely temporary. 360's search volume is still small and requires more subsequent investment. Nonetheless, monetizing through search ads will undoubtedly be its most imaginative revenue growth point moving forward. When 360's client base truly grows and prices rise, another inevitable war between 360 and Baidu may ensue.
