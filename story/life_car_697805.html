When you think of Mexico Acapulco, some of the first things that may come into your mind. You might start thinking about a fantastic honeymoon destination. You can consider it a great place to spend spring break. Acapulco you may think, as a great place to go cliff diving, you may think it is a wonderful tropical vacation spot. Do not worry, all these answers are correct, and of course all these things about Acapulco are true. However, do you know that Acapulco also has a great, rich, magical history? That's right, Acapulco's history is filled with fascinating stories, legends, myths, and interesting facts.

Scientists believe that the history of Acapulco can be traced back to 3000 BCE. Evidence supporting this claim can be found in numerous pottery, clay figurines, and tools discovered during archaeological excavations in the surrounding areas. There are also artifacts indicating that the early residents of Acapulco were Nahua Indians who were skilled fishermen, agriculturists, and hunters, using animals as a means of livelihood. These discoveries are quite fascinating, but it really gets interesting when we bring in the idea of Chinese visitors to Acapulco.

It is speculated that Chinese explorers and merchants reached the mainland of Mexico at Acapulco as early as 420 CE. In China, there are records showing that these voyages crossed the Pacific Ocean to reach the American coast. However, the actual evidence to support any contact between the Chinese and the Nahua Indians is not impressive enough, and without sufficient concrete evidence to lend credibility to this claim, it is difficult to say for sure that these events indeed occurred. Nonetheless, it is at least quite intriguing.

Spain established an early foothold in Mexico and parts of South America by the early to mid-16th century. The Spaniards came to the New World for many reasons, but one of the most important was to attempt to open new trade routes to Asia. Thus, in the process, the Spanish ravaged the eastern coast of Mexico and eventually made their way to the western side where they established Acapulco, extracting the entirety of the ancient Aztec civilization. Before establishing a trading outpost in Acapulco, the Spanish conqueror Hernán Cortés used the bay of Acapulco as a port for his ships, where he was able to build vessels in order to further take over even more parts of the country, increasing Spain's presence in the New World.

After hundreds of years of Spanish rule, Mexico won its independence from Spain in 1810. By 1930, Acapulco had gained attention, fame, and status as a premiere vacation hotspot for royalty, celebrities, and social elites. Today, Acapulco is a favorite destination for students on spring break, honeymooners, or anyone who wants to enjoy a great tropical vacation. People flock to its shores to bathe in the warm waters, rest on its beautiful beaches, and perhaps watch the famous La Quebrada cliff divers.

With its beautiful beaches, clear waters, vibrant atmosphere, and Acapulco's rich history, it's no wonder why it is a beloved vacation destination for so many people around the world. If you haven't yet taken the admirable trip to Acapulco, make sure it is on your list of places to see.
