Usage experience of Ctrip.com

by impark on 2007-11-04 22:26:34

To be honest, as I wrote in my last diary, I haven't traveled to many places. However, if I need to travel, the first website I refer to is Ctrip (www.ctrip.com). I check hotel prices and flight prices there. Just now, I saw a TV program on Shanghai Television called "Managing China," which interviewed Ctrip's CEO, Min Fan. Don't underestimate the fact that I haven't been to many places, but I really enjoy traveling. I always hope for an opportunity to go on a business trip from my company, but it never happens, except for the recruitment trip to Yanji last year. Actually, everyone knows that there are countless websites in China that provide travel services like Ctrip, such as eLong, Mangguo.com, Qunar, and many other regional travel service websites. Most of these websites mainly offer hotel reservations and flight reservations. Every time I get off a plane, I receive a lot of business cards for hotel and flight reservations, but eventually, they all end up in the trash can. This is because I still trust big companies like Ctrip. What if my ID number is misused by some bad actors? The key is that large enterprises are more efficient and trustworthy. Here are the reasons why I use Ctrip: 1. Relatively cheaper prices. Although the price might be slightly higher than some other local websites, it is still cheaper compared to market prices. 2. Convenient booking. Even during peak tourist seasons, if you book through Ctrip, there will usually be rooms available. If you call the hotel directly, you may not be able to make a reservation. 3. Efficiency and reliability. You can either call their free hotline or book through their website, which is very convenient, whether you're booking or canceling.
