A sawtooth wave voltage identical to the ID waveform is generated across R821.

by ufg81qt25 on 2011-09-18 16:00:53

After turning on the Changhong 51PT28A TV, there is no raster, no sound, and no image.

Technical data for Beijing Changhong TV repair hotline.

Solution: After replacing R833 with the same model, the fault was eliminated. Beijing Changhong TV Repair Center.

Analysis and inspection: No output voltage was detected in the +B circuit, indicating that the fault lies in the power supply circuit. Analyzing its working principle: after the power is turned on, the 220V voltage charges C823 through R833 and V806. The voltage at pin 4 of N821 rises accordingly. When it reaches 17V, the control circuit inside N821 (STR-6658B) starts to work, and the speed of the power startup is determined by the size of R833 and C823. When the voltage at pin 4 of N821 drops below 10V, the low-voltage lockout circuit (UVLO) inside N821 activates, stopping the operation of the control circuit and returning the power supply to its pre-startup state. After the control circuit starts working, the current consumed by N821 increases, causing the voltage at pin 4 of N821 to drop due to the discharge of C823.

At the same time, the control circuit causes the internal switching transistor of N821 to operate in a switching state, generating an induced voltage on the drive winding (T804 6, 7 windings) due to mutual induction from the self-induced voltage on the switch transformer T804 (1, 4 foot winding). This voltage is rectified by D828, filtered by C825, and stabilized by the voltage stabilizing circuit composed of R829, D827, and Q821, providing the working voltage for pin 4 of N821. After the control circuit starts, the oscillation frequency and pulse width of the oscillation circuit are determined by the charging and discharging speed of C1 inside N821 and the positive feedback voltage at pin 1 of N821. When the switching transistor is conducting, C1 inside N821 is quickly charged to 6.5V. At the same time, the current ID (sawtooth wave) generated when the switching transistor conducts flows through R821 at pin 2 of N821, producing a sawtooth wave voltage identical to the waveform of ID across R821. This voltage is fed back to pin 1 of N821. When this voltage rises to the limit voltage of 0.73V at pin 1, the comparator circuit inside N821 flips, controlling the oscillator to flip and turn on the switching transistor. After the switching transistor turns on, C1 is again quickly charged to 6.5V, and the oscillator begins the next cycle of work, driving the switch transformer to output pulsating DC voltage. Based on the above principle analysis, the focus was on checking the power start-up circuit and the oscillation circuit, and it was found that the start-up resistor R833 was open-circuit.
