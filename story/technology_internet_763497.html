Google China Smartphone User Survey: 60% Willing to Give Up TV

by anonymous on 2013-08-02 10:30:12

Today, Google collaborated with the market research firm IPSOS for the third time to release a report on smartphone usage, providing deeper insights into how people are using their smartphones this year and the characteristics of that usage.

The penetration rate of smartphones in Chinese cities has significantly increased, rising from 33% in 2012 to 47%. Nearly half of urban residents in China now own a smartphone. Among these users, 69% use their smartphones daily to access the internet, a higher proportion than smartphone users in the U.S.

People are using their smartphones anytime and anywhere—whether at home, on the road, or while commuting. Smartphones have become an indispensable part of our daily lives.

Smartphones are so important to consumers that 60% of users would rather give up their television than their smartphone. In the U.S., only 36% of users feel the same way.

Data shows that user activities on smartphones are diverse. Mobile search, video watching, app usage, and social networking are highly active. A staggering 97% of users engage in entertainment activities on their smartphones. The smartphone is the primary access point for searching information, with 60% of users conducting searches daily via their smartphones.

Notably, 85% of users multitask while using their smartphones, such as listening to music, watching TV, reading newspapers or magazines, playing video games, watching movies, or reading books. The widespread adoption of smartphones has also spurred rapid development in mobile applications. On average, a smartphone has 26 apps installed, with 10 apps used in the past 30 days, and an average of 2 paid apps installed.

Additionally, 69% of smartphone users in China have made purchases via their phones, compared to only 46% in the U.S.

For more details of the report, please visit: http://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/mobileplanet/zh-cn/.
