Mr. Liu Taige's main achievements:

In 1969, Mr. Liu joined the Housing & Development Board (HDB) of Singapore as an architectural planner and was promoted to Chief Executive Officer of HDB in 1979, a position he held for 10 years. During his 20-year tenure, HDB completed over 500,000 residential units. The concept of public housing planning in Singapore evolved from large housing estates to today's fully-equipped new towns - self-sufficient residential areas accommodating 200,000 to 300,000 people. The quality of public housing also progressed from simple dwellings to today's high-quality residences. In structural engineering, he encouraged engineers to introduce and experiment with new structural design systems to improve efficiency and reduce costs. From 1989 to 1992, he served as the Director and Chief Planner of the Urban Redevelopment Authority (URA) of Singapore. Under his leadership, URA completed the adjustment plan for Singapore's Concept Plan, which was regarded both domestically and internationally as a forward-looking and feasible development blueprint guiding Singapore towards becoming an outstanding tropical city.

Currently, Mr. Liu serves as the Chairman of the National Arts Council of Singapore, dedicated to promoting Singapore into an international art city. He also acts as a director for multiple institutions under the Singapore government and as a visiting professor at the School of Design and Environment, National University of Singapore. In China, he has been hired as a planning consultant for several provinces and cities including Shandong, Beijing, and Tianjin, and served as the chairman of the Planning Evaluation Committee for the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games facilities.

The urban planning art museum has been successfully completed.

Weifang News Network reported on December 23rd that the completed group of buildings - the Citizens' Art and Culture Center - has become a gathering place showcasing Weifang's historical features, local customs, and regional characteristics. Located south of the People's Square, this dynamic architectural complex resembles an unfolding scroll capturing the attention of the general public. Recently, the reporter traveled to Shanghai to listen to Mr. Liu Taige, the renowned international architect and chief planner of this architectural project, interpret and explain its elements of light, shadow, water, plants, and color. Mr. Liu expressed his passion and dedication to this set of buildings, infecting every interviewer.

A Guideline Building for a City: Poetic Expression of Urban Grace

As a display area for Weifang's historical appearance, local customs, and regional characteristics, the completed group of buildings - the Urban Planning Art Museum - showcases the unique charm of Weifang kite art against the backdrop of clear skies.

"I believe in architecture with emotion. The 'life' of architecture is its beauty, which is very important for humans," said Mr. Liu sincerely about his affection for this set of buildings. As a cluster of buildings, the total area of the Citizens' Art and Culture Center is the largest nationwide. Mr. Liu devoted all his artistic inspiration and perfect planning to sketch a vibrant and charming urban picture for the people of Weifang.

He informed us that building just an art and culture center might not require such a large area. However, due to its unique location advantage, his design philosophy had already transcended this limitation. The Citizens' Art and Culture Center is located at the southern end of the People's Square, aligned on a central axis with the municipal government office complex and the People's Square, naturally extending and expanding the citizens' leisure and entertainment experience. Therefore, from the beginning, Mr. Liu made an assumption: when citizens come here, they are not entering a city building but embracing the embrace of a garden square.

This garden square is not a flat horizontal structure but is deliberately divided into five or six small squares of unequal size and shape, providing a sense of enclosure while being open, comfortable, and lively. To match the atmosphere of this public building cluster, Mr. Liu chose a grand style for the surrounding gardens. No flowers, only trees and lawns, the grand greenery and vast buildings express the dignified and broad connotation here. For the originally merely construction material soil piles, Mr. Liu utilized them effectively by packaging them into undulating green spaces. He told us that this not only conforms to the traditional Chinese architectural design concept of imitating and getting close to nature but also aligns with the current international trend of creating gardens. These design concepts and architectural elements make this space more approachable and full of life, brimming with natural harmony and beautiful emotions.

As a flat plain city, Mr. Liu incorporated the theme of landmark buildings throughout the design. Two 120-meter-high observation towers embody his design ideals. He hopes that more surrounding cities will recognize these observation towers, recognize this group of buildings, thereby recognizing Weifang, the Kite Capital, fostering more communication and exchanges with this beautiful city, allowing Weifang to showcase its development achievements and urban charm in broader fields and higher levels.

Kites Throughout the Design: The Leaping Theme Notes of the City

Mr. Liu informed us that before starting the planning and design of the Citizens' Art and Culture Center, he did not rush into brainstorming but began a long period of "seclusion," fully absorbing the essence of Weifang's kite culture. A large number of books and materials on Weifang kite culture provided him with comprehensive raw materials on history, background, inheritance, development, and art. When he presented the first colored rough draft of the general plan design, everyone, whether laymen or experts, exclaimed in unison: "What a beautiful butterfly!" This exclamation gave him immense joy because it coincided perfectly with his design, meaning he had achieved his initial ideal - using the city as a backdrop, adopting an expansive symmetrical layout style, symmetrically aligned with the Citizens' Leisure Square and the Government Office Complex along the north-south axis, incorporating the meaning of Weifang as the World Capital of Kites, using the "kite flying" composition technique to perfectly shape a landmark building cluster symbolizing "leaping urban development."

After setting the tone for the entire building, how could the cultural charm of the Kite Capital be interpreted more delicately and perfectly? He chose closer integration with more local cultures. Zheng Banqiao calligraphy, woodblock New Year paintings, paper cutting, shining with Weifang's local characteristics, continuously entered Mr. Liu's design vision.

Among them, the most prominent was the bold use of color. Mr. Liu informed us that to determine the color of this building cluster, he specially examined the buildings around Weifang. He found that the most commonly used building material, granite, is typically used in four different shades of gray in Weifang. Zheng Banqiao's former residence is also predominantly gray-white, consistent with the traditional gray-white tones of Chinese cities. On this basis, he added bright colors like yellow and green that conform to international trends, aiming to better integrate the inner qualities of kites with the outer appearance of buildings, making the entire building cluster soar and leap, displaying the elegance and freedom of kites and the vastness of the sky. Meanwhile, this lively and natural color makes the geometricized simple structures convey warmth and adds magical poetic effects to the space, expressing the city's emotions and spirit without restraint.

In the design, Mr. Liu also cleverly embedded local characteristics into every detail. At the entrance of each individual cultural building, he designed glass walls, utilizing folk art paper cutting to convey the function of the building. At the entrance of the concert hall, he will design various instrument shapes like cellos and violins, using paper-cutting techniques to express the essence of music; at the library entrance, he will use calligraphy to welcome every person entering the ocean of books. The overall and detailed consideration allows Mr. Liu's design for this building cluster to achieve a unified style that echoes near and far, appropriately sized. In this beloved "kite garden" of his, he deeply named each group of buildings: the opera house and concert hall cluster is called "wild geese"; the workers' cultural palace is called "Peng"; the science museum is called "dragonfly"; the library is called "swan"; the urban planning museum is called "bluebird"; the two observation towers are called "phoenix", like a father cherishing each of his children, these lovely names carry Mr. Liu's good wishes for this building cluster and for the beautiful city of Weifang.

Citizens' Cultural Living Room: Sharing Happy and Joyful Lives

In recent years, the increasingly rich large-scale cultural and artistic activities held in our city have constantly given us one surprise after another, broadening the artistic horizons of the citizens of Weifang, shaping cultural brands, and establishing the city's image.

With the improvement of the city's taste, the growing demand for spiritual culture among the general public, and the increasing exposure of the weak foundation of cultural facilities, the Municipal Party Committee and Municipal Government, after much deliberation, decided to build the Weifang Citizens' Art and Culture Center. Located south of the Municipal Government Comprehensive Office Building, adjacent to Victory Street to the north, the total area covered is 430,000 square meters. Among which, the northern Citizens' Leisure Square occupies 160,000 square meters and was built and opened in April 2003. To improve the square's functions, in 2005, the Municipal Party Committee and Municipal Government decided to plan and construct the Weifang Citizens' Art and Culture Center occupying 270,000 square meters in the southern part of the square. In October 2007, the Weifang Citizens' Art and Culture Center project smoothly broke ground amid the eager anticipation of the general public.

As this group of buildings carries the cultural life aspirations of the general public, Mr. Liu continuously excavates and absorbs valuable things from worthy foreign architectural planning designs and combines them with the local lifestyle of Weifang to find an appropriate expression method. The five groups of buildings with their respective functional characteristics bear the cultural aspirations and needs of the general public, meeting their different needs.

Even in the design of auxiliary facilities, Mr. Liu embodies the "people-oriented" feature. Between the main buildings and the northern People's Square, irregular winding pathways are designed, accompanied by lush greenery on both sides, allowing people to cross a bridge to reach the vast and magnificent People's Square. At the top of the two observation towers, there are observation restaurants, enabling people to enjoy a beautiful distant view of their own city.

For the facilities surrounding the building cluster, Mr. Liu also provided a perfect design. He hopes that office buildings, restaurants, underground malls, and other high-end complexes will be planned and constructed around the entire square as soon as possible, making the facility construction here more complete, providing the best living services for those enjoying art and culture, and laying the groundwork for the future usage rate of this area, writing an enduring article of the perfect combination of culture and commerce.

A Platform for Sino-Singapore Cultural Exchange: The Ideal Extension of a Building

Regarding Sino-Singapore cultural exchange, Mr. Liu envisions that the Citizens' Art and Culture Center will be an effective platform and a good starting point. Mr. Liu highly appreciates the active communication and proactive exchanges of the Weifang Municipal Party Committee and Municipal Government. As the current Chairman of the Singapore Arts Council, Mr. Liu feels a necessary responsibility and finds his role positioning.

From the start of the planning of the Citizens' Art and Culture Center, during negotiations with the Municipal Party Committee and Municipal Government, Mr. Liu deliberately integrated elements and signals of Sino-Singapore cultural exchange into the construction. For the construction of the library, he specifically exchanged ideas with the designer of the Singapore National Library and adopted the other party's good suggestions; for the construction of the science museum, he listened to the opinions of relevant professionals within Singapore.

Mr. Liu expressed with great anticipation the following wish: Sino-Singapore cultural exchange has a uniquely advantageous emotional foundation, and he is willing to be the "bluebird" delivering messages, realizing and conveying the emotions and information of Sino-Singapore cultural exchange through down-to-earth work. Singapore has many excellent arts groups, and specific cultural exchange activities will interpret this friendly theme.

Mr. Liu particularly mentioned the famous Marina Bay Sands Arts Center in Singapore. The Marina Bay Sands Arts Center is Singapore's premier performing arts venue. Its uniquely shaped dome has earned it the nickname "durian." Inside the arts center, there is a 1600-seat concert hall, a 2000-seat theater, several rehearsal halls and practice rooms, as well as outdoor performance spaces. Additionally, there are shopping centers, international cuisine restaurants, and Singapore's first arts library - the Marina Bay Sands Arts Center Library, which houses a large collection of literary classics and multimedia materials about music, dance, film, and drama. Various forms of performances presented at the Marina Bay Sands Arts Center, ranging from musicals, concerts, dances, dramas to outdoor performances and lunchtime concerts, etc., are abundant.

Mr. Liu particularly introduced to us that the experiences in planning, performance, organization, and operation of the Marina Bay Sands Arts Center can be referenced by Weifang's Citizens' Art and Culture Center. Mr. Liu also represented the people of Singapore in expressing admiration and anticipation for Weifang's unique folk arts and culture. He believes that the Citizens' Art and Culture Center should shoulder the responsibilities of bilateral exchanges between China and Singapore, organizing Weifang's excellent cultural and artistic teams to go to Singapore for exchange performances through this platform, and regularizing and institutionalizing this process, making it as common as visiting relatives. Through continuous cultural exchange activities, achieving exchanges and cooperation in more fields and deeper levels such as economy, technology, society, etc., between China and Singapore, and on the basis of drawing lessons from Singapore's experience in external cultural exchanges, achieving exchanges with more countries and regions around the world. That is the most beautiful common aspiration of both China and Singapore.

As the interview was about to end, Mr. Liu especially expressed his future vision for the city of Weifang. Well-organized roads, rich greenery, and good urban management continue to elevate the status of Weifang, known both inside and outside China as the Kite Capital. And this leading-edge, world-class architectural work - the Citizens' Art and Culture Center - will become one of Weifang's most valuable artistic spaces. It will not only become a new landmark and city card for Weifang but also a gathering space for urban culture, an important venue for social and cultural activities of the general public. Here, it will be the most public, artistically infectious, and representative modern urban beautiful urban space in our entire city. Let's look forward to it together.

The total construction area of the Citizens' Art and Culture Center project is 290,000 square meters, consisting of five clusters and eight single buildings:

Cluster One - Urban Planning Art Museum, located in the eastern part of the planning area, with three floors above ground, covering a total construction area of 24,000 square meters, focusing on showcasing urban art, urban planning, urban master plan models, historical and cultural cities, etc.

Cluster Two - People's Cultural Palace, Youth Palace, located in the central position of the planning area. The People's Cultural Palace covers a total construction area of 48,000 square meters, with four floors above ground, mainly for cultural and entertainment, sports and fitness, etc.

Youth Palace, covering a total construction area of 25,000 square meters, with three floors above ground, equipped with a comprehensive children's rehearsal hall, dance hall, academy, children's sports and entertainment center, and artistic comprehensive classrooms, etc.

Cluster Three - Library, Science Museum, located in the western part of the planning area, with a corridor connecting the two single buildings. The library covers a total construction area of 30,000 square meters, with four floors above ground, mainly for book reading and exhibition of fine documents. The design focuses on creating multi-layered landscape effects through indoor and outdoor interactive spaces; the roof design strives to create a feeling of "flying and lightness," as if kites are floating in the air.

Science Museum, covering a total construction area of 19,600 square meters, with three floors above ground, mainly for popularizing scientific knowledge and displaying scientific and technological achievements.

Cluster Four - Grand Theater, located in the southwestern part of the planning area, covering a total construction area of 40,000 square meters, with four floors above ground. The Grand Theater's planning and design layout can connect directly to the science museum and commercial belt through corridors on the first and second floors, and the underground part connects with commerce, catering, youth palace, and cultural palace.

The Grand Theater consists of three functional systems: a 1,500-seat theater, supporting backstage facilities for the theater, and a conference center. The south facade of the Grand Theater uses glass as the main architectural language, creating good visual interaction between indoors and outdoors. The horseshoe-shaped large auditorium displays a noble and elegant demeanor. Every evening when the lights are lit, the majestic and splendid indoor scenery is displayed before everyone, making it a landmark architectural landscape in the region.

Complementary to the Grand Theater are commercial corridors and observation towers. The two commercial corridors connect the building flow lines north and south of the Zhangmian River in the planning area and run through the underground commercial center, ground commercial area, and observation tower. The two observation towers, approximately 120 meters high, serve as landmarks for the entire art and culture center, resembling organic plants growing out of the commercial belt, presenting a unique effect of vertical takeoff with dynamic lines and frameworks. The functions of the observation tower include restaurants or coffee shops and observation platforms.

Cluster Five - Concert Hall, located in the southeastern part of the planning area, covering a total construction area of 25,000 square meters, with four floors above ground.

The concert hall includes two functional systems: a 900-seat concert hall and supporting backstage facilities. The overall shape presents a dynamic and graceful impression, with horizontal and vertical lines forming an orderly changing facade effect; the horizontal and delicate sunshades and roof settings enhance the light and soaring momentum of the building, making it one of the lightest buildings in the entire art and culture center, just like the Grand Theater.

The underground construction area of the Citizens' Art and Culture Center project reaches 130,000 square meters, mainly providing commercial outlets, equipment and management rooms, and underground parking lots as supporting facilities for each cluster.
