Regarding the origin of the spin bike, there are two versions. One says that it was invented by Australians who love cycling, based on the unique terrain of Australia. Its English name "Spinning" (rotating) aptly summarizes its characteristics - it requires you to keep the wheel rotating through exercise, thereby achieving the effects of fat reduction, body shaping, and improving cardiovascular function. Another version claims it originated in the United States and is currently most popular in France, Italy, Israel, and South Africa.

The "bike" in spin biking is very different from a regular bicycle. Although it also has handlebars, a seat, and wheels, it is fixed to the ground. When you sit on it and pedal like you would on a regular bike, you can make the wheel of this bike turn, but its base remains fixed. The most unique feature of this bike is that you can freely adjust the resistance of the wheel. The seat and handlebars (the bike handles) can be easily adjusted according to your body size. During the workout, everyone moves together, changing the speed and difficulty level of pedaling according to the rhythm of music and lights, simulating natural cycling actions such as going uphill and downhill. Since it has safety foot straps that are always fixed to the pedals, the safety factor is relatively reliable.

Next, let's introduce the situation of spin bike training:

A single spin bike class lasts 45 minutes (in foreign countries, a spin bike class is 60 minutes, but since it's just starting in China, the time is shorter). The first 5 minutes are for warming up; the next 35 minutes are for the main exercise. The coach will adjust the resistance and revolutions of the bike according to individual effort, simulating uphill and downhill movements and walking-in-place actions. While exercising endurance, a large amount of fat is burned. The final 5 minutes are for relaxation exercises, making the lines more attractive.

Finally, let's talk about the cost of the exercise:

It is reported that riding a spin bike has a beautifying effect on the buttocks, thighs, and other muscles, increasing the strength of the lower limbs. Its stability and non-displacement increase the safety factor of the training, making it the best way to treat knee joint problems. Riding a bike for 45 minutes every day consumes 4-5 thousand calories, and you can lose about 1 pound per week, with very noticeable results.

Spin biking significantly improves the human body's cardiovascular function. It burns a lot of fat on average per hour. A 45-minute spin bike class burns 400 to 500 calories, equivalent to running for an hour and a half! This exercise has an especially good shaping effect on the buttocks and thighs.
