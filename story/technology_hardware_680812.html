Steps/Methods:

- The "triangular positioning block" on the table should be adjusted to the middle. There are four screw holes on the table for fixing the "triangular positioning block", two in front and two at the back. Secure the "triangular positioning block" with screws in the front screw holes.

- Place a frame strip against it. If the frame strip is too large, then the two screws of the "triangular positioning block" should be secured in the rear screw holes. For small frames, add two rods to prevent the clamps from damaging the small frame strips. After adjusting the positioning block front and back, tighten the screws and adjust the table according to the position where the frame needs to be nailed. The method is: move the table along with the frame strip forward and backward. For example, to nail the outer part of the frame strip, adjust the large screw rod below the rear of the table. To nail the inner part of the frame strip, adjust the large screw rod below the front of the table. Adjust according to your needs and place the required nails into the nail groove.

- If the mold head height does not match, loosen the screws of the mold head and replace it with a matching one. Place the corner nail with the glue side facing up and the convex side toward the back of the machine. After placing it correctly, install the "nail feeder" and put the rubber band on the back of the "nail feeder" (to squeeze the nails). After the nail and adjustment work is done, the two frame strips can be placed on the table for corner assembly.

- A person sits in front of the machine to work, stepping on the pedal with their left foot, causing the machine's clamps to grip the frame strips while the pressing rod comes down simultaneously (be careful not to get your fingers caught in the clamps). First, nail the inside of the frame strip. Hold the middle of the frame strip or the two round rods on the table, push the table forward, do not release the left foot, then step on the nail frame with the right foot.
