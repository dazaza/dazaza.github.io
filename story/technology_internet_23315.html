Is Yahoo "one page, all available" or "all available in one night"?

by chill_man on 2007-06-25 15:15:21

Occasionally, I heard that Yahoo! launched a service called "One Page到位". Out of curiosity, I looked it up and tried it. Isn't this just placing various search results on one page? Is this what "One Page到位" means? Still unsure, I continued to look it up... In the end, I couldn't find more detailed explanations, so I guess that's all there is to it.

It reminded me of a long time ago when Yahoo! China changed its pure search version and promoted an advertisement campaign that was everywhere in the subway. They were all very interesting questions like, "Are eyebrows unnecessary?", "How to remove sticky residue?", "Do plants feel pain?", and on the other side, there was another question, "Which animal has its nose in its mouth?" Everyone who saw this question said, "This is interesting, I'll go check it out on Baidu." I suspect that during that period, the number of people searching for these questions on Baidu was no less than on Yahoo!... So I really doubt whether Yahoo! can truly achieve "One Page到位", or if it will turn into a shortsighted "overnight success", only to end up doing the groundwork for others.

Yahoo.cn and yisou.com have long been using a tabbed form for search results, with 5 results per page. Some colleagues particularly like this format, finding it very comfortable to use. Unfortunately, the results are limited to only 5 per page, fixed and unchangeable. This might make the first position in each tab even more valuable.
