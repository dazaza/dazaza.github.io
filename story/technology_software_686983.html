**Reasons and Solutions for Common Issues in Sound Amplification Systems**

1. **Cause**: The sound system and lighting system share the same single-phase power supply. The thyristor of the lighting system generates a large working current, which is pulsed AC, and is directly coupled to the input end of the amplifier through the power lead, producing significant AC noise.

**Solution**: Carefully inspect the shielding lines between devices; weld connections wherever possible.

2. **Cause**: Insufficient mid-frequency energy in the microphone.

**Solution**: Boost the 800Hz frequency band.

3. **Cause**: Excessive mid-frequency energy.

**Solution**: Attenuate 1K-1.25KHz.

4. **Cause**: Excessive mid-high frequency energy.

**Solution**: Attenuate 2K-4KHz.

5. **Cause**: Excessive low-frequency energy at the lower limit.

**Solution**: Increase the lower limit frequency of the bass.

6. **Cause**: Some audio equipment uses two-prong plugs, while some require three-prong plugs. Improper connection may cause AC induction, resulting in AC noise.

**Solution**: Invert the power plug of the two-prong audio equipment when operating. If the AC noise in the sound system remains significant, ensure proper grounding by reversing the neutral and live wires on two-wire input sockets (left pin for live wire, right pin for neutral).

7. **Cause**: Poor contact or cold soldering of the shielding lines connecting various channel devices.

**Solution**: Carefully inspect all channel device connections and re-solder if necessary. If "clicking" sounds persist after inspection, check for broken wires using a multimeter and replace cables with internal breaks.

8. **Cause**: Excessive high-frequency energy.

**Solution**: Attenuate 6KHz.

9. **Cause**: Excessive ultra-high-frequency energy.

**Solution**: Attenuate 12K-16KHz.

10. **Cause**: Excessive sub-bass upper-limit energy.

**Solution**: Attenuate 125-160Hz.

11. **Cause**: Harsh high-frequency tones lacking smoothness.

**Solution**: Attenuate 8K-10KHz.

12. **Cause**: Severe AC noise when the sound system is silent.

**Solution**: Use separate single-phase power supplies for the sound system and lighting system. Additionally, use an isolation transformer for the sound system's power supply, ensuring its capacity exceeds the system's power consumption.

13. **Cause**: No sound during playback due to improper power connections of one or more connected audio devices.

**Solution**: Ensure all power plugs are securely connected.

14. **Cause**: Thin or dry sound quality due to insufficient mid-low frequency energy.

**Solution**: Boost 250Hz.

15. **Cause**: Muddy bass due to excessive low-frequency central frequency energy.

**Solution**: Attenuate 60-80Hz.

16. **Cause**: Devices with three-prong power plugs connected to two-wire input sockets with reversed live and neutral wires.

**Solution**: Check the power strip to ensure proper socket connections. Use three-prong outlets where the top center pin is grounded to ensure electrical safety.

17. **Common Issue**: Persistent "clicking" sounds during music playback due to poor or cold soldering of connection lines between devices in the sound amplification system.

**Solution**: Thoroughly inspect all connections and replace faulty cables if necessary.

By addressing these issues systematically, the sound quality and performance of the sound amplification system can be significantly improved. Proper maintenance and regular checks are essential for optimal results.
