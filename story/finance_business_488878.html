At the age of eighteen

by cudtaozro on 2011-09-13 21:22:49

At the age of eighteen, my favorite song to listen to was "Butterfly Flower" by Waterwood. The lyrics were so poetic, the melody so gentle and beautiful, and the rhythm so touching that every time I heard this song, it would evoke endless sighs within me.

When I first listened to this song, I only thought it was beautiful but struggled to understand its deeper meaning and emotions. However, after watching many romantic dramas by Qiong Yao, reciting countless lines about love, and memorizing sentences describing romance, I gradually became more sentimental—not because of myself, but because of the characters in those dramas. Too often, we become emotionally involved with a character's ups and downs, laughing heartily one moment, shedding tears the next, or even crying uncontrollably at times. When these emotional scenes accumulate in our hearts, singing that song "Butterfly Flower" again, I found myself unknowingly shedding real tears.

Do you still remember the butterfly flower in the sunlight of your childhood? It bloomed beautifully on your head, spreading innocence... When I was very young, there was a small garden in our courtyard where I planted flowers transplanted from other places. Before going to school each day, I would take a glance at them, and after school, I would water them with a basin of water. That feeling was so beautiful—it was even better than getting a perfect score on an exam.

On weekends or holidays, I loved hiding in the garden, talking to the flowers, squatting down, and quietly observing each bloom... Years later, I realized that one of the flowers in my garden was indeed a butterfly flower. Now, that garden no longer exists, but I still often miss the days spent with the butterfly flowers, for their beauty and purity.

As time passed and I grew older, things changed. The innocence of childhood gradually faded, and so did the memories of the butterfly flowers. When love came knocking and swept through like clouds, my once-pristine garden of memories was covered in dust. Youth is a lazy season; too often, we prefer chasing and playing around rather than reminiscing about past beauties. We spend much of our time listening to songs and watching romance dramas, neglecting our studies, losing the admiration of classmates and teachers' praise, and becoming increasingly impatient and forgetful of time. In that era, we lost so much...

The most beautiful memory is undoubtedly the first love—a vague beauty. We didn't fully understand what love meant or what feelings truly entailed, but in our naivety, we felt dependent on each other, believing all our thoughts were focused on the other person. The mutual feelings we shared were wonderful, like unripe fruits—tasting slightly bitter at first but sweet upon closer reflection.

It was during this age of not understanding love that we could be so carefree. Only during this time did we chase and play recklessly, running after a butterfly for miles without thinking twice. Tired, we would lie down on the grass, the green juice staining our pink dresses, yet we would laugh and say it was perfection. These are just some snapshots from countless moments, impossible to count in a short while.

One day, you suddenly asked me, "Will you still love me tomorrow?" I gently pinched your nose and told you, "I will love you until the end of time, until the seas dry up and the rocks crumble." You insisted we make a promise with a hook, sealing a lifetime commitment in our hearts. As we grew apart over time, the only way to express our longing was through a simple note. In my letters, I liked calling you "Silly Girl," and you affectionately called me "Doggy."

Perhaps, the taste of missing someone is truly bitter and distant. You often ask me, "Do you still love me?" Having seen too many adult love games and experienced the bittersweet nature of love, I constantly question myself: "Do I still love her?" Time has passed, butterflies have flown away. Can we still remember that year, that month, that day? Nowadays, people change easily, and beautiful lies abound. How many promises can withstand the test of wind and rain? Love seems less genuine, vows seem less binding, and memories seem blurred. It appears we've forgotten the unwavering devotion of our youth. Indeed, who can guarantee that their heart won't change, seeing through the vicissitudes of life?

One day, I dreamed of you, dreaming that you had become someone else's bride. I screamed in agony, but you remained unmoved. Watching you walk down the aisle in a bright white dress, about to become another man's bride, my heart truly ached. I cried until I woke up, realizing then that I still genuinely loved you! When you appeared before me, stroking my gradually thinning face, you couldn't breathe due to pain. You softly confessed to me, sharing how nothing about you had changed, promising undying devotion until the seas dried up. Looking at your weary face, I held you tightly in my arms, knowing deep down that you are the perfect love in my heart.

Perhaps, too much tragedy has trapped us in despair, or perhaps the dazzling allure of city life has made us lose faith in love. Maybe the unpredictability of life has led us to turn left or right. I once told you, "Only when the mountains lose their peaks and the heavens and earth merge will I dare to part with you." And you told me, "If you don't abandon me, I will never leave you." In that passionate embrace, we intertwined our lives into a beautiful, brilliant, and colorful dream. You told me that many had pursued you, but you turned them all away. At that moment, I realized how radiant you were, shyly lowering your head, resembling a lotus flower emerging from the water, delicate and bashful.

Because I dreamed of your departure, I woke up sobbing. Feeling the night breeze pass through the window, do you feel my love? On the day we grow old, will you still be by my side, watching as those vows and lies drift away with the past? Many have admired your youthful beauty, but who can bear the relentless passage of time? Many have come and gone in your life, but know that I will always be by your side... Yes, many have fallen in love with your youthful beauty, but how many are willing to endure the merciless changes of time? Time passes quickly, youth fades easily, and beauty, though like a seven-colored ray of sunlight in the sky, is fleeting. More often than not, we need to support each other, to live together through thick and thin...

Beauty fades, oceans become fields, such is fate, resisting the flow of time is impossible. Darling, don't cry, don't cry. There are no eternal beauties in this world. When we grow old, looking back at our youthful beauty, having you in my life, what regrets could I possibly have in this lifetime? Heaven above, I wish to be with you, to share an unending bond. Only when the mountains lose their peaks, rivers dry up, winter thunders roar, and summer snow falls will I dare to part with you! With this vow in this lifetime, if you do not abandon me, I will surely stay by your side...

(Emotional Memories)
