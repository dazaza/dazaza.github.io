Qingdao Liangxin Housekeeping Company reminds you: Whether it's a newly renovated or an old cracked wooden floor or laminate floor, you need to clean the floor first and then wax it after electrostatic treatment.

The regular care and maintenance of the floor will affect its service life and home effect. More and more homes are laying floors indoors, making the living room elegant and clean; however, if not properly maintained or cleaned improperly, over time, it can cause the floor to lose its luster and the paint surface to be damaged. Renovating the floor is not an easy task after all. The purpose of waxing is to protect the floor, so how do we clean and maintain the waxed floor?

(1) After the floor is laid, clean its surface and apply a layer of protective wax. Use liquid wax, not solid wax. Wax every 2-3 months.

(2) In daily floor cleaning, a dust mop should be used. Spray the dust mop with a dust-attracting agent, which can be used for a week after one spray.

(3) The role of spraying the dust-attracting agent is to adsorb the dust on the floor surface onto the dust mop. After pushing, take it outside and shake it to remove the adsorbed dust. This is where the name "dust-attracting agent" comes from.

(4) A dirty dust mop can be washed, dried, and then sprayed with a dust-attracting agent again.

(5) Wooden floors must not be wiped with water because water is easily absorbed by wooden floors, causing them to swell and deform. Furthermore, wiping wooden floors with a damp cloth or mop causes the dust to grind the floor like sandpaper. Over time, the gloss of the wooden floor surface will be worn off and the paint surface will be damaged.

(6) The material properties of wooden floors determine that they cannot be cleaned with water or solvents, otherwise, it will cause the wooden floor to swell, deform, the paint surface to fall off, and the surface to become mottled. It must be cleaned with a dedicated wooden floor cleaner to achieve the effect of cleaning and protecting the floor.

How to clean and restore a wax-damaged floor?

Waxing the floor is not a simple task that anyone can do well. You must not make the wax look "blotchy." Here, the term "blotchy" refers to unclear waxing, uneven depth of the wax solution, etc. At this point, you can only ask a professional cleaning company to use special wax remover to remove the wax first, and then re-wax it after electrostatic treatment. [Our company's technical personnel have learned through long-term experience that painted wooden floors that have been waxed with solid wax cannot be treated with wax remover to prevent future problems.]

There are generally two types of waxing: one is liquid wax; the other is solid wax. In daily life, people often use liquid wax, which is both economical and practical for consumers. Our company uses a waxing machine for large-scale waxing in enterprises and institutions, and a wax drag for small-scale waxing. For homes, manual waxing is used. Manual waxing has the best effect. Under the force of manual application, the wax solution can be quickly and fully absorbed by the floor, thus achieving the best result. The effect of hand-waxing wooden floors is more obvious, bright, and realistic.
