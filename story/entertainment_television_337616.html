The "Blue Classic • Tianzhi Blue" Cup 14th CCTV Young Singer Television Grand Prix will start the semi-finals on March 26, group finals from April 12 to May 6, individual finals from June 12 to 29, and an award ceremony gala on July 3. This year's 14th edition of the Young Singer Contest is divided into the same categories as the previous edition: bel canto, folk, pop, original ecology, and choir. The competition is still divided into semi-finals and finals. After the semi-finals, 27 representative teams will be selected to participate in the group finals. Each match of the semi-finals and finals will be broadcast live nationwide on CCTV-1 and CCTV-3.

On the evening of March 5, after the preliminary and qualifying rounds of the "Blue Classic • Tianzhi Blue" Cup 14th CCTV Young Singer Contest, a total of 16 outstanding contestants stood out. Among them, there are 5 in the bel canto category, 6 in the folk category, and 5 in the pop category. They will compete for gold, silver, and bronze awards in the final round of the Youth Singer Television Grand Prix.

Compared with previous editions, this year’s contestants have three major characteristics:

1. High academic qualifications, with over 72% of the entrants having undergraduate or postgraduate degrees.

2. Strong capabilities, with more than 70% being professional singers from various institutions, cultural units, and performance groups.

3. Youthfulness, with most entrants aged between 21 and 26, and the youngest just 18 years old. Many "post-85" and "post-90" contestants are beginning to make their mark, including several who are still university students and not yet graduated.

To evaluate the comprehensive abilities of the contestants, the final scoring criteria are divided into two parts:

1. Singing evaluation, with a maximum score of 99 points. Each contestant's singing score is the average after removing the highest and lowest scores.

2. Comprehensive quality evaluation, which includes knowledge questions and imitation singing, each lasting 30 seconds, with a maximum score of 0.5 points for each section. The sum of the singing and comprehensive quality scores constitutes the final score for each contestant, with a maximum of 100 points.

Based on the initial and qualifying rounds, the judges are generally satisfied with the contestants' performances. Although many are quite young, some may still be inexperienced in terms of singing techniques or image presentation, but they show great potential with a strong foundation and diligent attitude. With time, they are expected to become future stars and achieve greater success on larger stages.

Since its inception in 1984, the CCTV Young Singer Television Grand Prix, held every two years, has gone through thirteen editions. It has become an important platform for promoting ethnic arts, popularizing music knowledge, discovering and nurturing vocal talents, and leading and driving the development and prosperity of China's vocal music industry.
