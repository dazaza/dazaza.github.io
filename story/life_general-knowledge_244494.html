How to Maintain Furniture: Maintenance Secrets Revealed

by longersing on 2009-09-30 18:25:01

The following 5 maintenance methods have all been verified by us, and we also guarantee that you've never heard of them before. For example, using egg whites to wipe a dirty leather sofa, toothpaste to clean the refrigerator shell, milk to clean wooden furniture, or white radish to clean the kitchen countertop, etc. Now let's reveal these secret formulas for maintaining furniture.

1: Use egg whites to clean dirty leather sofas. You can use a clean piece of flannel dipped in some egg whites to wipe it. This will not only remove stains but also restore the original luster of the leather.

2: Use toothpaste to clean the fridge shell. For general dirt on the fridge shell, you can use a soft cloth dipped in a little toothpaste and slowly wipe it. If the stain is more stubborn, you can squeeze more toothpaste and use a cloth to repeatedly wipe it. The fridge will then be restored to its original brightness. Because toothpaste contains abrasive agents, it has a very strong stain-removal power.

3: Use milk to clean wooden furniture. Take a clean cloth and soak it in expired milk that cannot be consumed anymore, then use this cloth to wipe tables, cabinets, and other wooden furniture. The stain removal effect is very good, and finally, wipe it once with clear water. For painted furniture that has accumulated dust, you can use wet gauze wrapped around tea dregs to wipe it, or wash it with cold tea water, which will make it even brighter.

4: Use white radish to clean countertops. Kitchen countertops wiped with cleaning agents combined with sliced white radishes will produce unexpected cleaning results. Sliced cucumbers and carrots can also be used as substitutes, but the effect of white radishes is the best.

5: Use alcohol to clean velvet sofas. Velvet fabric sofas can be brushed with a hairbrush dipped in a little diluted alcohol, then dried with a hairdryer. If there are juice stains, mix one teaspoon of baking soda with water evenly, then use a cloth to dip it and wipe it, and the stains will fade.
