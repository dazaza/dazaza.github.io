It's not easy to get a bargain when shopping online. Here are some tips to avoid online shopping traps and prevent fraud.

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:16:57

In order to combat inflation and save time, more and more people have joined the ranks of online shoppers. The general perception is that online shopping saves money, time, and effort, but this isn't always true. In the commercial world, situations like passing off inferior goods as good ones or products not matching their descriptions do occur. The saying "you get what you pay for" also applies in online shopping. Therefore, to truly become a savvy online shopper, we need to practice and practice again.

### Story Section

#### ● Don't Trust "Loss-Leader Prices" Too Much When Doing Group Buying

**Name:** Huang Huiying

**Gender:** Female

**Occupation:** Civil Servant

"The fact proves that the saying 'you get what you pay for' never goes out of style," said Ms. Huang after participating in a home furnishing group-buying event. Since she had just purchased a new house, she often lingered on some home furnishing forums online, hoping to find some good experiences to learn from. "I did find something useful. Many posts on the forum were discussing how cheap it was to buy furniture through group buying. Some netizens even specifically posted their purchasing experiences, saving thousands of yuan each time, which sounded very tempting."

Influenced by these posts, Ms. Huang began paying attention to group-buying collection threads on some forums, and she indeed found some useful information. "I saw a fabric bed originally priced at around 6000 yuan in a group-buying thread, now being sold at a loss-leader price of 60% off. I thought it was quite attractive at the time. After looking at the pictures, I thought the bed was indeed beautiful, so I participated."

However, the result of this group-buying experience left Ms. Huang quite disappointed. Since the merchant involved in this group buying was from another place, Ms. Huang didn't see the actual product. But due to her trust in other group-buying participants, she eventually placed an order. "When I received the goods, I found that there was a significant difference between the actual product and the photos. The material was definitely not as good as described. At that moment, I felt that I had lost out. Moreover, I had to pay the logistics fee myself. The seller said that the loss-leader price couldn't include freight charges, and I carelessly agreed at the time, which cost me several hundred yuan more! Since the logistics company couldn't deliver the goods to my door, I had to find a car to go to the logistics company's business department to pick up the goods, which was really not cost-effective."

What truly drove Ms. Huang crazy was that when she bought other furniture later, she actually saw the same product in a building materials market, where the price was hundreds of yuan cheaper than the group-buying "loss-leader price," and could still be discounted by 85%. "They even included free delivery and installation. This time, I lost big." Ms. Huang became particularly indignant when mentioning this point.

**One-sentence insight:** Don't group-buy things if you're not familiar with the market situation; otherwise, you'll be scammed in no time.

#### ● Snatching Deals Can Be Profitable, But You Might Lose More

**Name:** Wu Xiaojia

**Gender:** Male

**Occupation:** Freelancer

Due to his relatively flexible work schedule, Xiao Wu started online shopping early. "All my gear basically comes from online purchases," Xiao Wu proudly told reporters. He loves sports and leisurewear, and when he has spare time, he enjoys browsing various online shopping platforms, especially those he frequents almost every day. "I prefer some niche brands from Japan and South Korea, especially hand-painted skate shoes, which are my favorite. Now the shoes I've bought almost fill up a whole shoe cabinet. It's indeed wasteful because many of them haven't been worn, just sitting there gathering dust." Although Xiao Wu occasionally feels guilty about this, he continues to enjoy it.

This year, the rise of "flash sales" on online shopping platforms gave him a new way to play. "To be honest, I think flash sales are a great game. For sellers, it's a good opportunity to increase popularity and traffic, and for us buyers, it's a great chance to grab deals. Flash sale prices are generally much cheaper than usual. Sometimes, board shoes that usually cost hundreds of yuan can be bought for less than one hundred yuan. For someone like me who has a collecting habit, it's perfect."

However, after participating in flash sales for half a year, Xiao Wu's thoughts quietly changed. "It's nice to get discounts through these activities, but it's really tiring. Now, sellers organize flash sales with limited supply and strict time restrictions. If you leave for a while, you might miss out entirely. As a result, to be able to snatch something, I often have to wait for a long time, constantly refreshing the page. It's really exhausting. Later, I felt that I was participating in flash sales just for the sake of snatching, regardless of whether I really liked the items. I even missed important matters because I was waiting for a flash sale. Now thinking about it, playing flash sales may give me price advantages, but I lose more in terms of wasted work time and disrupted sleep schedules. It's not worth it."

**One-sentence insight:** Flash sales are process games; don't get addicted. What really matters is the outcome.

#### ● Half-Price Goods Are Often Cheaper Than Half-Price

**Name:** Tang Yifei

**Gender:** Male

**Occupation:** Teacher

Teacher Tang, born in the 70s, only recently joined the ranks of online shoppers, making him a newcomer with little experience. However, this didn't stop him from diving in enthusiastically. On November 11th, known as "Singles' Day," many mall sellers on Taobao held half-price sales. Being single himself, Teacher Tang joined in to comfort his lonely heart by going online and shopping crazily. "I just bought a few clothes. To be honest, I was attracted by the half-price deals. I saw the advertisement for half-price store-wide sales on Taobao's homepage, and since I liked the brand, I clicked in and ended up buying several pieces."

According to Teacher Tang's recollection, when checking the details of one item, it clearly showed the original price at 450 yuan, now discounted to 229 yuan, which seemed very attractive. "Moreover, the seller provided free shipping, so I bought it without hesitation." Later, Teacher Tang had to wait over a week to receive the item. "The shipping speed was too slow. While waiting, I wanted to return the item. The seller explained that they sold too many items at half-price that day and couldn't handle all the shipments on time, so I let it go. After all, half-price is so cheap, and many people were grabbing it, so it was understandable."

After receiving the package, Teacher Tang was relatively satisfied with the quality. He thought this online shopping experience at half-price would end perfectly. However, unexpectedly, a promotional email from the seller shattered his self-satisfaction. "The email said that a new round of promotions had begun, and the clothing I bought was included in the promotion. The key was that if ordered via a mobile app, it only cost 188 yuan, with free shipping included. This was really frustrating. I had only bought it a few days ago, and now it was already 40 yuan cheaper. How fast is that? Buying at half-price turned out to be more expensive than buying regularly. Why did I bother to compete with so many people, and why did I wait so long?" After suffering this "blow," Teacher Tang said that in the future, he would carefully research before participating in half-price promotions. "I think it's best to search with keywords first, regardless of whether it's half-price or not. Choose the cheapest one in the search results, and avoid being deceived by half-price offers."

**One-sentence insight:** Half-price doesn't matter; what matters is whether it's worth the price.

### Tips Section

#### Shipping Costs Stable Products Worth Buying

The rise of online shopping benefits from the selling point of "good quality at low prices." However, under the current inflationary conditions, the trend of rising prices has begun to affect online shopping platforms, especially the increase in logistics costs, adding extra pressure for those enthusiastic about online shopping. In such a situation, is online shopping still worthwhile? How can online shopping be made economical? Let's calculate!

Firstly, consider the structural cost. In the process of online shopping, the costs include the payment for the purchased items and the express delivery and logistics fees. The cost of purchasing items can be compressed to a certain extent through bargaining, flash sales, group buying, etc., but the express delivery and logistics fees often have a smaller room for negotiation.

Faced with this situation, we suggest prioritizing products with stable and low express delivery and logistics fees when shopping online, or considering sellers who offer free shipping. Especially when purchasing large-sized and heavy items, choosing sellers who provide free shipping can save a lot of real expenses, sometimes even more than the discount obtained through bargaining with the seller.

So, what kind of items have relatively stable and low shipping costs? Currently, in the online shopping market, clothing and beauty products have certain advantages in terms of shipping costs. Moreover, these products generally have higher profit margins, providing more room for bargaining during online shopping, making them worth considering. On the other hand, digital products like digital cameras, gaming consoles, laptops, DIY accessories, etc., have generally lower profit margins, so their average prices on online platforms are quite stable. If the seller provides free shipping, it's worth buying, provided the seller has a solid reputation, ensuring no losses in terms of price. Especially for those living near areas without large digital markets, online platforms are a very economical procurement channel. As for mobile phones, MP3 players, MP4 players, HD players, etc., due to personalization and brand effects, there are inconsistent quotations, and the quality of products varies greatly. If you're not very knowledgeable about these products, it's best not to buy them online, as the chances of being scammed are relatively high.

### Problem Section

#### How to Deal with Online Shopping Traps?

The rise of online shopping has fulfilled many people's dreams of staying at home and having everything they need. However, it has also brought about many new types of commercial traps. Just like the uneven quality among traditional merchants, online sellers are not all trustworthy. Issues such as mismatched goods, substituting inferior goods for better ones, and replacing old goods with new ones, which we have encountered in physical stores, also happen on online shopping platforms. Unlike physical stores, during online shopping, buyers rarely have the chance to see the actual products, let alone conduct any "inspection," and the back-and-forth through express delivery and logistics means that goods often travel long distances across provinces, increasing the likelihood of unexpected problems. Handling after-sales service is also more difficult. Therefore, we need to pay more attention during the mid-sale and post-sale processes of online shopping. Here's a simple analysis:

**Reminder One:** Always Remember to Check the Goods Before Paying

As mentioned earlier, since most online shopping goods need to go through express delivery and logistics procedures before reaching the buyer's hands, the chances of problems occurring are much higher than in traditional physical stores. Besides issues caused by the seller substituting inferior goods or mismatched goods, damages or losses during transportation can also cause a lot of trouble for both parties. For such situations, as a buyer, it's essential to fully utilize the delay effect of third-party payment platforms, keeping the final decision-making power of payment firmly in your own hands, so that you can take the initiative in case of disputes.

Simply put, never "confirm payment" until you confirm that there are no problems with the online shopping goods. Specifically, there are a few aspects to note: Firstly, while waiting for the goods to arrive, keep track of the status of the waybill in real-time. This can be done by querying the waybill number provided by the seller on the corresponding courier company's website. If you find that the waybill number is invalid, contact the seller immediately to understand the relevant situation. If you discover that the seller is playing tricks or deliberately delaying shipment, initiate a refund application decisively. Generally, the system of online shopping platforms automatically transfers the funds to the seller after several days without disputes. If you're not careful, the seller might easily scam you.

Secondly, when receiving the goods, try to check the package before signing the delivery receipt, especially for fragile items. If you sign without inspection and then find cracks upon opening, it can be very troublesome as the seller and the courier company may blame each other for the damage. Thirdly, if you find quality issues after receiving the goods, take photos with a digital camera or mobile phone as evidence right away, and contact the seller to report the problem or initiate a refund application proactively. This is to gain the initiative, as delays may give the seller a chance to shift the responsibility for quality issues onto the buyer, leading to a situation where you have no recourse.

**Reminder Two:** Keep Evidence in Case of Disputes

Since online shopping is a form of business, disputes between buyers and sellers are inevitable. Misunderstandings caused by differences in interpretation of images and text descriptions, or discrepancies due to angles and color differences in pictures, are not uncommon. Of course, more disputes arise from unscrupulous merchants cutting corners on product quality or services. How should buyers avoid these potential risks?

The best method is to request mediation from the administrators of the online shopping platform. According to the established rules of platforms like Taobao, Paipai, and Youa, administrator mediation often has a decisive effect in resolving disputes between buyers and sellers, and generally favors the buyer's interests. Therefore, mediation by administrators is often the most effective way for buyers to protect their rights. However, this does not mean that administrators will arbitrarily indulge buyers in groundless complaints. To reasonably resolve disputes through administrators, buyers must provide sufficient evidence proving that their rights have been infringed upon or not adequately protected. For example, if the goods sent by the seller are damaged during transportation, to convince the administrator, the buyer needs to provide relevant pictures as proof. Making unsubstantiated claims will not receive support.

Retaining evidence is even more important for disputes arising from service issues. For example, if the seller fails to honor previous commitments, the buyer should ideally find prior conversation records and submit them to the administrator to obtain full support. To preserve conversation records, it is recommended to set the installation directory of chat tools like Wangwang on a non-system partition of the computer's hard drive. This ensures that evidence is preserved even in cases of system crashes or reinstallation. Additionally, make full use of the network storage function for conversation records provided by online shopping platforms to retain sufficient evidence for future rights protection.

### Risk Section

#### Beware of "Phishing" in Online Shopping

Nowadays, as a new lifestyle, online shopping has entered thousands of households and is indeed very popular. As we all know, although online shopping is convenient and fast, cash-on-delivery only supports a small number of websites, and the security risks associated with online shopping cannot be ignored. With the booming online shopping market, criminals have already targeted the growing population of online shoppers, ready to commit crimes. Even if you are an experienced user, it's hard to avoid falling into traps. Staying vigilant against "phishing websites" is the real key.

"MP3 disappeared from the shopping order after just paying," "Game points cards bought are nowhere to be found," "After completing the transaction, the shopping cart is still empty"... Most of these phenomena are due to "phishing" websites. Nowadays, "phishing" websites have URLs that look authentic, highly realistic web pages, and even professional payment processes. A slight oversight by consumers can lead them into traps. According to our investigation, almost all payment platforms are affected by "phishing" to varying degrees. Netizens must be aware that "online shopping carries risks, be cautious to avoid phishing."

Additionally, with the approach of the Christmas consumption peak season in December, the rapidly emerging group-buying websites are likely to spark a new wave of online shopping fever. Due to the low entry threshold of group-buying websites and the prepayment settlement model, netizens cannot choose cash-on-delivery or other transaction methods that favor protecting their rights. Therefore, group-buying websites may become new targets for "phishing."

**Anti-Fraud Tips:**

1. Always remember the principle that "there's no such thing as a free lunch" and don't fall for small discounts that could lead to big losses.

2. Fake payment platforms used by phishing websites do not display account balances. If you don't see the balance when using Alipay, you should be cautious.

3. Phishing websites lack identity verification functions. If entering incorrect usernames and passwords still allows you to log in successfully, it indicates that the website might be trying to steal your password.

4. Changing the domain name prefix of online banks from http:// to https:// will display a lock icon after opening the URL, indicating that the website encrypts browsing data. Conversely, if you don't see the icon, it suggests you might have entered a phishing website.
