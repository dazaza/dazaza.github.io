How does radio frequency facelift work to remove wrinkles from different parts? The specific operation involves lifting from the center of the forehead towards both sides, repeating each stroke continuously 5-8 times until redness appears. If the wrinkle lines are deeper, use the probe along the wrinkle lines with slight force, repeating 2-4 times for local enhanced effect. The area within 1 cm above the eyebrows is a pain-sensitive zone, so the energy should be appropriately reduced.

2. Eyes: The optimal energy setting is within the range of 3-8. The specific operation involves working outward from around the eyes. The wrinkle-removal electrode starts at a position 1 cm away from the eye (as shown in the facial treatment planning diagram) and gradually moves toward the temple. At the outer corner of the eye and the tip of the eyebrow, follow the arrow direction indicated in the facial treatment planning diagram for lifting. During the radio frequency facelift operation, use your left hand to gently lift the skin of the temporal and outer eye corners to assist in the upward lifting operation. If crow's feet or other deep wrinkles are present, use the probe along the wrinkle lines with slight force, repeating 2-4 times for local enhanced effect. The N nerves around the eyes are abundant, making them sensitive to pain, so care must be taken during the operation to protect the area.

3. Nasolabial Cheeks: The optimal energy setting is within the range of 6-10. The specific order of the facelift surgery operation is lifting from bottom to top. Since the cheek area is less sensitive than the nose and lip areas, the energy can be increased as appropriate, repeating the lift 5-8 times until redness appears. The sensitivity of the nasolabial area is stronger, so attention should be paid to controlling the strength and energy.
