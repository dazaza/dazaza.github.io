Superman of Pickles Part Four: Pickles Become a Monk

Column: Fairy Tales Add Time: February 8, 2011 1:46:25 PM Source: Admin Clicks: 2178

Since that time when the spiders fought with pickles, they have been keeping an eye on him. Every few days, they ask pickles to bring them some tasty food from home. If pickles doesn't bring anything, they intercept him on his way to school and beat him up. Pickles is afraid that his mother, Mabu Bing, will worry about him, so he doesn't tell her. Instead, he secretly takes delicious food from home and gives it to the spiders while starting to secretly practice martial arts.

When the spiders bully him, the ants see the benefits the spiders gain and also start threatening pickles, demanding that he show respect by giving them some advantages. Pickles becomes extremely angry. One day, he who never hit anyone before, actually starts fighting back, releasing all the anger he has built up against the spiders onto the ants. He breaks the ant's leg.

Seeing the trouble he has caused, pickles simply stops going to school and doesn't go home either. He goes straight to Shaolin Temple. He feels that no matter what, he must learn skills; this is no longer about whether he is ambitious or not.

Pickles arrives at Shaolin Temple, knocks on the temple door, and a little monk comes out, bows and asks, "Which temple do you come from master?" This question confuses pickles. He touches his bald head and says, "I come from school. I want to become a monk. I haven't been to any temple." Then the little monk asks, "Then why are you bald?" Pickles replies, "I was born bald." The little monk understands and says, "Ah, this is what Buddha has already arranged for you." Saying this, the little monk leads pickles inside.

The master looks at pickles and says, "You don't need to shave your head. It's quite convenient, and these bumps are perfect for practicing Iron Head Kung Fu." Thus, the master accepts pickles.

Pickles practices kung fu very diligently. After practicing Iron Head Kung Fu, he treats his head not as a head but as a ball or as the spider's head. He forcefully collides with it, resulting in several trees in Shaolin Temple being broken by him.

One day, someone's cow runs to the front of Shaolin Temple and eats all the Chinese cabbage planted there. Seeing this, pickles gets very angry and charges towards the cow. Unexpectedly, the cow doesn't back down and also charges at him. As a result, they start pushing each other, going back and forth for a few rounds. Suddenly, pickles exerts a great force and pushes the cow onto a tree branch. The cow sits on the branch, mooing and crying, "Master Pickles, please let me down. I will never dare to steal Shaolin Temple's Chinese cabbage again."

The master sees that pickles' Iron Head Kung Fu has reached perfection and says to pickles, "Pickles, you have completed your training and can return home now." So pickles returns home.

Upon returning home, pickles finds the family even poorer. His mother, Mabu Bing, has aged significantly. Originally, after he left, the ants came to their home demanding compensation. Mabu Bing had to compensate them with all the family's property.

Pickles feels sorry for his mother and works hard to earn money. Gradually, the family's situation improves. Because pickles has skills, not only does no one dare to bully him anymore, but people constantly seek his help. When the spiders hear that pickles has learned many skills, they fear pickles' retaliation and specially invite him for a drink, insisting on becoming friends with him. Seeing her son become a capable person, Mabu Bing is very happy.
