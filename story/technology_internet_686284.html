Frequent red-light running by vehicles and pedestrians at the entrance of the community has caused 4 deaths in 8 days

by qqlove99 on 2012-03-02 03:36:30

Taoyu Garden Community Entrance Intersection Diagram - On February 18th, a car accident occurred where the entire front of a black Passat was destroyed, trapping the driver inside the vehicle who unfortunately perished. A reader provided photos.

According to reports by journalist Liu Yang, the Taoyu Garden Community entrance on Zhangcai Road in Tongzhou District, notorious for frequent traffic accidents and nicknamed the "fatal intersection" by local residents, will soon undergo safety facility renovations. Yesterday, the Tongzhou traffic management department stated that this intersection will have added surveillance and speed reduction facilities in an effort to eliminate major traffic fatalities.

At the Taoyu Garden Community entrance on Zhangcai Road, community residents often cross the road recklessly with high traffic flow. At 4 PM yesterday, I observed that as Zhangcai Road leads directly to the Jing Shen Expressway, there are many box trucks passing through, with over ten vehicles per minute.

On February 10th and February 18th, two consecutive fatal accidents occurred near this intersection, resulting in four deaths. On February 10th at 7:37 AM, an accident happened at the eastern side of the neighborhood's entrance when Cui Xiujie, Qiao Limin, He Meirong, and Zhang Wanping, four cleaners waiting at the southwest side of the intersection for the traffic light, were hit by an out-of-control gray Jetta, causing two immediate deaths, one death after unsuccessful rescue, and one injury.

On February 18th at 2:50 PM, another accident occurred when a black Passat, due to rear-ending, swerved into the oncoming lane and collided head-on with a heavy-duty box truck, resulting in the immediate death of the car driver.

"Last year, there were four or five incidents; already two since the start of this spring," said residents of Taoyu Garden. In 2007, a traffic light was installed at the neighborhood entrance but lacked a monitoring system, leading many vehicles to run red lights and pedestrians crossing without looking, thus causing frequent traffic accidents.

Yesterday, the Tongzhou Traffic Brigade announced that the traffic safety facility renovation measures for this section have been approved by the Traffic Management Bureau and will be implemented shortly. The police plan to add another traffic light at the intersection, increase surveillance cameras, mark pedestrian crossings and speed bumps, aiming to prevent major traffic casualties.

Additionally, the Tongzhou Traffic Brigade will conduct specialized order rectifications around Taoyu Garden on Zhangcai Road. Residents and drivers are reminded to follow traffic regulations, not run red lights, and use pedestrian crossings.

[Renovation Measures]

Add Signal Lights on the North Side

Wu Haiguang introduced that after the "February 10th" accident, this renovation includes adding another signal light at the north side of the T-intersection, setting up warning signs for steep slopes, marking pedestrian crossings in all four directions, installing red-light violation detection equipment and pedestrian warning signs on both sides.

Secondly, red-light running and speeding violation detection devices will be added at the T-intersection. Speed reduction coatings will be marked on the road surface on both sides of the intersection to remind vehicles to slow down, while a north-south pedestrian crossing will be marked on the west side of the intersection. Accident-prone area warning signs will also be added along the sections where the two accidents occurred.

It is understood that these measures have been approved and will be gradually implemented soon.

[Follow-up]

Heavy Traffic Flow and Pedestrian Crossings at the Incident Site

Why do accidents frequently occur at the Taoyu Garden Community entrance? Deputy Director Wu Haiguang of the Tongzhou Traffic Brigade analyzed that the large volume of traffic and the number of people crossing the road, coupled with poor adherence to traffic rules, are the main causes of frequent accidents.

Wu Haiguang explained that this section is a key traffic route from Zhangjiawan to Caiyu, with peak hours seeing over 900 vehicles per hour, averaging about 15 vehicles per minute. Taoyu Garden West Area is adjacent to the road, and across it lies a large farmers' market. With a population of 10,000 households in the West Area, many people cross the road to go to the market. Additionally, due to low awareness of traffic safety among this group, accidents frequently occur.

[Deceased]

A Kind-hearted Cleaner Who Helped Her Paralyzed Husband Stand Up

Mr. Wang, who suffered from hemiplegia, could finally move and even drive a motorcycle thanks to the care of his wife, He Meirong. However, their good days were short-lived as his 47-year-old wife passed away, plunging their already financially struggling family into further hardship.

More than ten years ago, Taoyu Garden was still a rural area, and Mr. Wang's family were farmers here. In 2002, after the village was demolished, the family of three moved into a small building in the West Area of Taoyu Garden. Due to the lack of income sources, He Meirong applied for a cleaning job at the property management office, responsible for cleaning the entrances and corridors of buildings in the neighborhood.

Separated by a road, the East and West Areas of Taoyu Garden see Mrs. He getting up at 6 AM every day, preparing meals for her family, then arriving at the warehouse at 7:30 AM to prepare cleaning tools, pushing her cart across Zhangcai Road to clean in the East Area. On the morning of February 10th, while Mr. Wang was accompanying his father at the old county hospital, he suddenly received a call from his son saying, "Mom has had an accident!" Before Mr. Wang could get home, he learned of his wife's passing.

Mr. Wang said his wife was very healthy and hardworking. Besides her cleaning duties, she never refused to help any neighbors with wedding or funeral arrangements, readily available whenever needed. She would work tirelessly washing dishes for two days. In 2008, when Mr. Wang suffered from hemiplegia, He Meirong cared for him day and night, enabling him to stand up and walk again, and now he can even drive a motorcycle.
