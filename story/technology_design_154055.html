What is a Logo?

A logo, emblem, or trademark is a product of modern economies, distinct from ancient imprints. Modern logos represent a company's intangible assets and are media for comprehensive information transmission. As the core part of a company's CIS strategy, a logo is the most widely used, frequently appearing, and crucial element in transmitting a company's image. The company's overall strength, perfect management mechanisms, quality products, and services are all encapsulated within the logo, leaving a deep impression on the audience through constant stimulation and repeated exposure.

Logos fix specific objects, events, scenes, and abstract spirits, philosophies, and directions into special graphics. When people see the logo, they naturally associate it with the company, leading to brand recognition. Logos are closely related to a company's operations, being indispensable elements in daily business activities, advertising, cultural construction, and external exchanges. As the company grows, so does the value of its logo. It has been said, "Even if a fire burns all of Coca-Cola's assets, Coca-Cola could rise again with just its trademark." Therefore, companies with long-term vision place great emphasis on logo design and understand the role of a logo. In the early stages of a company's establishment, good logo design is undoubtedly an important carrier for accumulating future intangible assets. Without a logo that objectively reflects the company's spirit and industry characteristics, with a scientifically and aesthetically pleasing design, changes and adjustments made as the company grows will cause unnecessary waste and loss. After China Bank changed its logo, the cost of dismantling and replacing outdoor media nationwide amounted to 20 million yuan.

The Role of a Logo

1. Identifiability

Identifiability is one of the essential functions of a corporate logo. Under the market economy system, competition intensifies, and the public faces complex and diverse information. With countless logos and trademarks, only those that are distinctive, easy to recognize and remember, have profound meanings, and are aesthetically designed can stand out among competitors. They distinguish the company from others, leaving a deep impression on the audience and enhancing the importance of logo design.

2. Leadership

A logo is the core element of a company's visual communication and the dominant force in disseminating information. In the visual identity system, the shape, color, and application methods of the logo directly determine the forms of other identification elements, which are built around the logo as the center. The leadership position of the logo embodies the company's business philosophy and activities, permeating all business activities and playing an authoritative leadership role.

3. Uniformity

A logo represents the company's business philosophy, cultural characteristics, value orientation, reflecting the company's industry features and operational thinking. It is the concrete symbol of the company's spirit. Public recognition of the logo equates to recognition of the company itself. A logo cannot be detached from the company's actual situation or violate its purpose. A logo that only focuses on surface form work loses its inherent meaning and may even negatively impact the company's image.

4. Comprehensiveness

As the company operates and its information spreads, the connotations represented by the logo become increasingly rich. The company's business activities, advertising, cultural construction, and public welfare efforts are accepted by the public, who memorize these through the logo symbols in their minds. Over time, when the public sees the logo again, they associate it with previously purchased products or received services, connecting the company with the audience and becoming a bridge between the two.

5. Innovativeness

Once determined, a logo is not immutable. As times change, historical trends evolve, and social backgrounds shift, the original logo may no longer suit the current environment. For example, the evolution of logos for Shell Oil and Pepsi Cola are vivid examples. Changes in the company's direction or target audience also necessitate logo updates. In summary, a logo always fits the company and is closely tied to its business activities.

Logo Design Process

1. Research and Analysis

A logo is not merely a combination of graphics or text; it is a standardized visual symbol created based on the company's structure, industry category, business philosophy, while fully considering the target audience and application environment. Before designing, it's essential to conduct a comprehensive and in-depth understanding of the company, including its business strategy, market analysis, and the basic wishes of top leadership—these are crucial bases for developing the logo design. Understanding competitors is also an important step, as the primary function of a logo—identifiability—is based on a thorough grasp of the competitive environment.

2. Element Mining

Element mining is further preparation for the design development process. Based on the analysis of research results, extract the structural type and color orientation of the logo, list the spirit and characteristics the logo should embody, explore relevant graphic elements, and identify the direction of the logo design. This ensures that the design work is targeted rather than aimlessly combining text and graphics.

3. Design Development

With a comprehensive understanding of the company and full mastery of design elements, the design development work can proceed from different angles and directions. Through the designer's understanding of the logo, imagination is fully utilized, employing various representation methods to integrate design elements into the design. The logo must achieve profound meaning, distinctive features, grand design, stable structure, and appropriate color matching suitable for the company, avoiding clichés or commonality. Different logos reflect different emphases or appearances. Through discussion, analysis, or modifications, find the logo suitable for the company.

4. Logo Refinement

The logo proposed during the proposal stage may still have some imperfections in detail. By standardizing the drawing of the logo, adjusting sizes, black-and-white applications, line applications, and other different presentation formats, the use of the logo becomes more standardized. At the same time, the logo's characteristics and structure remain intact when used in different environments, achieving unified, orderly, and standardized communication.

Characteristics of a Logo

1. Functionality

The essence of a logo lies in its functionality. Although artistically designed logos have aesthetic value, their primary purpose is practical use. A logo is an indispensable intuitive tool for people to engage in production and social activities. There are logos for public use, such as public venue signs, traffic signs, safety signs, operation signs, etc.; there are logos exclusive to nations, regions, cities, ethnic groups, families, such as flags and emblems; there are logos for social groups, enterprises, individuals, activities, such as meeting emblems, meeting marks, factory marks, community marks, etc.; there are trademarks for specific goods; and there are personal property-specific logos, such as seals, signatures, monograms, inscriptions, brands, etc., each having unique, irreplaceable functions. Legal logos especially have the special mission of safeguarding rights.

2. Identifiability

The most prominent feature of a logo is its unique appearance, making it easily recognizable. It displays the inherent characteristics of things, distinguishes the meanings between them, and indicates differences and affiliations, which are the main functions of a logo. Various logos are directly related to the fundamental interests of nations, groups, or individuals, so they must not be identical or confusing, to avoid causing misunderstandings. Therefore, a logo must be distinctly characteristic, allowing people to recognize it at a glance and never forget it.

3. Prominence

Prominence is another important feature of a logo. Except for hidden logos, the vast majority of logos are set up to attract people's attention. Therefore, strong, striking colors and simple, clear graphics are common characteristics of logos.

4. Diversity

There are numerous types of logos with extensive uses. Regardless of their application format, compositional form, or expressive means, logos exhibit extremely rich diversity. Their application formats include flat surfaces (almost any material surface can be used), and three-dimensional ones (such as reliefs, sculptures, arbitrary three-dimensional shapes, or using packaging, containers, etc., in special shapes as logos). Their compositional forms include direct use of imagery, composition with characters or symbols, composition with realistic, symbolic, or abstract graphics, or composition with colors. Most logos are composed of several basic forms combined together. In terms of expressive means, their richness and diversity are almost impossible to summarize comprehensively, and they continue to innovate with the development of science, culture, and art.

5. Artistry

All non-natural logos that have been designed possess a certain degree of artistry. They meet both practical requirements and aesthetic principles, providing people with a sense of beauty, which is the basic requirement for their artistry. Generally speaking, logos with strong artistic qualities can better attract and touch people, leaving them with a strong and profound impression. The high level of artistry in logos is a need of the times and civilization's progress, reflecting people's ever-increasing cultural literacy and psychological needs for aesthetics.

6. Accuracy

Regardless of what a logo aims to explain or indicate, whether it is metaphorical or symbolic, its meaning must be precise. First, it must be easy to understand, conforming to people's cognitive psychology and capabilities. Second, it must be accurate, avoiding unintended multiple interpretations or misunderstandings, paying particular attention to taboos. People should be able to instantly understand and accurately grasp its meaning without error, which is precisely the advantage of logos over language, faster than language.

7. Durability

Unlike advertisements or other promotional materials, logos generally have long-term usage value and are not easily changed.

Design Principles of a Logo

Logo design is not only the design of practical objects but also a form of graphic art design. While it shares similarities with other graphic art expression techniques, it also follows its own artistic rules. It must embody the aforementioned characteristics to better fulfill its functions.

Due to the extremely strict requirements for simplicity, generalization, and perfection, it must succeed to the point where it's hard to find a better alternative solution, making its difficulty much greater than that of any other graphic art design.

1. Design should be carried out under the premise of a detailed understanding of the design object's purpose, applicable scope, and relevant regulations, deeply comprehending its functional requirements.

2. The design must fully consider the feasibility of its realization, adopting corresponding design methods according to its application format, materials, and production conditions. It must also take into account the visual effects when applied to other visual communication methods (such as printing, advertising, imaging, etc.) or when enlarged or reduced.

3. The design must cater to the intuitive acceptance ability, aesthetic awareness, social psychology, and taboos of the target audience.

4. The conception must be carefully considered, striving for profundity, subtlety, novelty, uniqueness, and accurate expression, capable of withstanding the test of time.

5. The composition must be concise, beautiful, and adaptable to the form of its application.

6. Graphics and symbols must be simple and generalized while maintaining artistic qualities.

7. Colors must be pure, strong, and eye-catching.

8. Following the artistic rules of logos, creatively exploring appropriate artistic expressions and techniques, refining the precise artistic language, ensuring the designed logo possesses highly integrated aesthetic appeal and achieves optimal visual effects, is the principle pursued in logo design art.

Representation Techniques of a Logo

1. Representation Method: Adopting images directly associated with the logo object and possessing typical characteristics, clearly stating the purpose of the logo. This method is direct, clear, immediately understandable, and easy to quickly comprehend and remember. For example, using a book image to represent the publishing industry, a train head image to represent railway transportation, and a coin image to represent the banking sector as logo graphics.

2. Symbolic Method: Using things' graphics, texts, symbols, colors, etc., that have a certain meaningful connection with the content of the logo, symbolizing the abstract connotation of the logo object through metaphors, descriptions, etc. For instance, using crossed sickles and hammers to symbolize the worker-peasant alliance, using vigorous seedlings to symbolize the healthy growth of children, etc. Symbolic logos often adopt socially agreed-upon and recognized associated images as effective representatives. For example, using doves to symbolize peace, lions or eagles to symbolize bravery, the sun and moon to symbolize eternity, pine cranes to symbolize longevity, white to symbolize purity, green to symbolize life, etc. This technique contains profound meanings, adapts to social psychology, and is well-received by the public.

3. Implication Method: Adopting images similar in meaning to the logo or with suggestive implications, hinting at and indicating the content and characteristics of the logo through allegory, suggestion, and indication. For example, using an umbrella image to imply moisture prevention, a glass cup image to imply fragility, and an arrow image to indicate direction.

4. Simulation Method: Using images of things with similar characteristics to mimic or analogize the features or meanings of the logo object. For example, Japan's All Nippon Airways uses a crane spreading its wings to simulate flight and auspiciousness, and Japan's Saguwa Express uses a running figure to simulate express delivery.

5. Visual Sensation Method: Using simple yet uniquely shaped abstract graphics, texts, or symbols without special meanings, giving a strong sense of modernity, visual impact, or comfort, attracting people's attention and making them unforgettable. This method does not rely on the meaning of the graphics but mainly on the visual impact of the graphics, texts, or symbols to express the logo. For example, Japan's Isuzu uses two rhombuses as its logo, and Li Ning sportswear horizontally exaggerates the pinyin letter "L" as its logo. To help people identify the marked objects, this kind of logo is often accompanied by a small amount of text. Once people recognize this logo, removing the small text still allows them to identify it.

Significance of a Logo

In today's rapidly developing science and technology, the roles of printing, photography, design, and image transmission are becoming increasingly important. The development of non-verbal transmission has gained competitive strength against verbal transmission. A logo is a unique mode of transmission among these. People seeing smoke rising think there is fire below. Smoke is a natural mark of fire. In the era of underdeveloped communication, people used smoke (beacon smoke) as a special way to transmit information related to fire (such as urgency, emergency, alarm, rescue, etc.). This artificial "smoke" is both a signal and a logo. It rises high, dissipates slowly, has a vivid image, and distinct features, visible from far away.

The speed and effect of this non-verbal transmission were unmatched by verbal and written transmission at the time. Today, although verbal and written transmission methods are highly developed, logos remain instantly recognizable to the public, fast-acting, and unrestricted by the language and writing barriers of different ethnicities and countries. This intuitive transmission method suits the increasingly fast-paced lifestyle, and its unique role remains irreplaceable by any other transmission method.

A logo is a sign indicating the characteristics of something. It uses simple, prominent, and easily recognizable objects, graphics, or characters as an intuitive language. Beyond marking and substituting, it also has the functions of expressing meaning, emotion, and instructing actions.

As a special way of human intuitive communication, logos are omnipresent in social activities and production activities. Moreover, they are increasingly showing their crucial and unique functions for the fundamental interests of nations, social groups, and even individuals. For example, national flags and emblems serve as symbols of a country's image, conveying special significance that words and languages find difficult to express exactly. Public place signs, traffic signs, safety signs, operation signs, etc., guide people in conducting orderly normal activities and ensure life and property safety with intuitive and quick effects. Trademarks, store signs, factory signs, etc., have dedicated purposes.

Logos have significant practical value and legal protection in developing the economy, creating economic benefits, and safeguarding the rights of enterprises and consumers. Major domestic and international events, conferences, sports meetings, postal transportation, financial trade, agencies, groups, and even individuals (seals, signatures) almost all have logos representing their characteristics. These logos play communication, exchange, and promotional roles from various angles, promoting the advancement of society, economy, politics, science, and culture, and protecting respective rights. With increasingly frequent international exchanges, the intuitiveness, imagery, and lack of language barriers of logos make them very advantageous for international communication and application. Therefore, international logos are rapidly promoted and developed, becoming one of the most effective visual transmission methods and a universal intuitive communication tool for humanity.
