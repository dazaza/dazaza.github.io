Early arcade games run on arcade machines called arcade games. wSM Arcade Nest generally consists of two parts: cabinet and motherboard. wSM Arcade Nest cabinets come in two types - upright cabinets and horizontal cabinets. Upright cabinets stand vertically on the ground, with height greater than width, and the monitor forms an angle with the ground but is not parallel, usually suitable for standing gameplay; horizontal cabinets lie flat on the ground, with width greater than height, and the monitor is parallel to the ground, suitable for sitting gameplay. Cabinets consist of the machine's outer shell (usually wooden products) and internal electronic components. The shell of an upright cabinet can be divided into frame head, frame face, control panel, frame belly, frame side panels, and frame back panels. wSM Arcade Nest Frame head is the highest visible part of the game machine's front, a rectangular translucent horizontal board, possibly with lights or speakers inside, with a sticker on the surface indicating the game name and content. In the concave part between the frame face and the frame head, speakers are generally installed. wSM Arcade Nest Frame face is used to display the monitor, located at the middle cross-section or outside vertical position of the front of the frame body, internally placed either an inclined or upright monitor, covered externally with transparent acrylic glass, possibly with stickers related to the game on the board. wSM Arcade Nest Control panel is a flat panel equipped with game joysticks, game buttons, game knobs, game trackballs, or game light guns, located on the front outer side of the frame face. Below it is the frame belly, integrating the control functions of the game, serving as the input method for interactive gameplay. wSM Arcade Nest Frame belly is the lower half of the front of the game machine, in the center of which there is a coin slot or (and) a coin return slot, used to insert or return coins. Below the coin slot is the coin box, where inserted coins fall. These three devices all have iron doors installed externally with hidden locks, ensuring the commercial interests of arcade owners and completing the process of game consumption and reproduction. wSM Arcade Nest Frame side panels are two whole boards on both sides of the game machine's shell, firmly combined with the entire front part to form a stable whole, serving as the main supporting part of the entire game machine. wSM Arcade Nest Frame back panel is a whole panel at the back of the game machine's shell that can be opened or removed, combining with other parts of the shell to form a closed space to protect the internal components of the game machine. At the same time, the frame back panel also has the function of dissipating heat generated during machine operation, which is very important for the safety of the game machine. wSM Arcade Nest Horizontal cabinets have a simpler structure than upright cabinets, resembling coffee tables, with a very low height for the game machine, a top-down perspective for the game, so there is no frame head, control panels are located at the edges of the frame face, and there are coin slots and coin boxes, without frame back panels but frame bottom panels. Due to their small size, these parts of the cabinet are compressed smaller. This kind of horizontal cabinet has a special function, allowing game graphics to rotate or be bidirectional, so in early single-player games, the two players can sit facing each other and play together.

The motherboard is an electronic motherboard made by specialized game manufacturers, similar to the motherboard of a personal computer, with CPU, ROM, RAM, video memory, input/output interfaces (I/O), etc. Without the motherboard, the game machine is just an empty shell, and one motherboard only carries one game, so if it breaks, this game might disappear forever. Arcade emulators usually refer to software that runs arcade game programs on other computer platforms by converting the program instructions of the arcade platform into instructions recognizable by the local machine. Since 1997, various types of arcade emulators have rapidly developed, becoming the hope for saving and preserving these precious popular cultures, among which the most important is the large cabinet simulator M.A.M.E.. Today, we can replay these old games on our personal computers, but the reality is that arcade games are declining. Following Capcom's announcement of exiting the arcade market in 2003, the opportunities for arcade development have become dimmer.

An endless unknown where players assume one or several roles in the game, with a complete storyline. Players may confuse it with adventure games, but the distinction is simple. RPGs emphasize plot development and personal experience more. Generally speaking, RPGs can be divided into Japanese-style and American-style, mainly differing in cultural background and combat methods. Japanese RPGs mostly adopt turn-based or semi-real-time combat, excelling in emotional subtlety, touching plots, and rich character images, such as the "Final Fantasy" series. Most domestic Chinese RPGs can also be categorized under Japanese RPGs, like the familiar "Chinese Paladin" and "Jianxia". American RPGs like the "Ultima" and "Might and Magic" series feature high freedom, open maps and plots, and strong entertainment value. The appearance of ARPG ancestor "Diablo" in 1997 pioneered a new era for RPGs, disrupting traditional patterns and becoming the blueprint for future graphical online games.

CT = Action Game: Players control game characters using various weapons to eliminate enemies and pass levels, not pursuing storyline, such as the familiar "Super Mario", cute "Kirby", and splendid "Prince of Persia". Computer action games mostly evolved from early arcade games and action games like "Contra" and "Romance of the Three Kingdoms". The design principle is aimed at ordinary players, purely for entertainment and relaxation, generally containing some simple puzzle elements, simple operations, easy to start, tense and stimulating, belonging to "popular" games.

AVG = Adventure Game: Players control game characters for virtual adventures. Unlike RPGs, AVGs feature stories often presented as completing a task or solving certain puzzles, emphasizing the importance of puzzles in the game process. AVGs can further be subdivided into action and puzzle types. Action AVGs can include fighting or shooting elements, such as the "Resident Evil" series, "Tomb Raider" series, "Dino Crisis", etc.; while puzzle AVGs rely purely on solving puzzles to drive the plot development, with higher difficulty, represented by the super-classic "Myst" series.

Strategy Game SLG = Strategy Game: Players use strategies to compete with computers or other players to achieve various forms of victory, whether unifying the nation or establishing extraterrestrial colonies. Strategy games can be divided into turn-based and real-time types. Turn-based strategy games include favorites like the "Romance of the Three Kingdoms" series, "Sakura Wars" series; real-time strategy games include the "Command & Conquer" series, "Empire" series, "Dune" series, etc. Later, some media classified simulation management games, i.e., SIM (simulation) games, such as "The Sims", "SimCity", "RollerCoaster Tycoon", "Theme Park", and raising games (TCG, such as "World Soccer Manager", "Asuka 120% Raising Plan", etc.) under SLG.

RTS = Real-Time Strategy Game: Originally a branch of strategy games SLG, due to its rapid popularity worldwide, it slowly developed into a separate category, gaining even more recognition than SLG, somewhat like the current relationship between FIFA and IOC. Representative works include the "Warcraft" series, "Age of Empires" series, "StarCraft", etc. Later, from it emerged so-called "real-time tactical games", mostly involving controlling a small team to complete tasks, highlighting tactical roles, represented by "Commandos".

Fighting Game FGT = Fighting Game: Players manipulate various characters to fight against characters controlled by the computer or another player. By drawing techniques, it can be further divided into 2D and 3D types. 2D fighting games include famous ones like the "Street Fighter" series, "Samurai Spirits" series, "King of Fighters" series, etc.; 3D fighting games include "Tekken", "Mobile Suit Gundam Fight", etc. Such games lack much of a storyline, at most having a simple scene setting or background display, with relatively single scenes, characters, and controls, but with greater operational difficulty, mainly relying on players' quick judgment and micro-operations to win.

STG = SHOOTING GAME: Shooting games here do not refer to simulation shooting (gun battles) similar to "VR Cop", but pure airplane shooting, where players control various flying objects (mainly airplanes) to complete tasks or pass levels. SSG = Science-Simulation Game: Sci-fi flight simulation games based on non-reality, imagination space, such as "FreeSpace", "Star Wars" series, etc. RSG = Real-Simulation Game: Realistic flight simulation games based on the real world, winning by authenticity, aiming for a sense of presence, such as the "Ace Combat" series, "Su-27" series, etc. Additionally, some other simulation games can also be categorized under STG, such as submarine simulation "Silent Hunter", tank simulation "Steel Division" etc.

FPS = First Personal Shooting Game: Strictly speaking, it is a branch of action games, but like RTS, due to its rapid popularity worldwide, it has developed into a separate category, with typical examples including DOOM series, QUAKE series, "Unreal", "Half-Life", "CS"... too many to list. PZL = Puzzle Game: Puzzle originally referred to jigsaw puzzles used to cultivate children's intelligence, extended to various interesting puzzle games, generally suitable for leisure, the most classic being the well-known "Tetris".

Racing Game RCG = Racing Game: [Also known as RAC] Games simulating various racing sports on the computer, usually taking place in a race scenario, highly technical in terms of graphics and sound effects, representing the cutting-edge technology of computer games. Exciting and realistic, deeply loved by car enthusiasts, representative works include "Need for Speed", "Ridge Racer", "Motorbike Heroes", etc. Another view calls them "Driving Game". Currently, the connotation of RCG is getting richer, appearing in other modes such as boat racing, horse racing, etc. SPT = Sports Game: Games simulating various competitive sports on the computer, varied and highly simulated, widely welcomed, such as "FIFA" series, "NBA Live" series, "Winning Eleven" series, etc. TCG = Raising Game: Previously commonly used in GB series, now generally replaced by EDU (education) to distinguish it from "Online Trading Card Game". Simply put, it is a game where players simulate raising, such as "Star Agent", "Princess Maker", etc. CAG = Card Game: Players control characters through card battle modes. Rich card varieties make the game highly variable, giving players infinite fun, represented by the famous "Nobunaga's Ambition" series, "Yu-Gi-Oh!" series, including card online games "Wuxia Online". Broadly speaking, "Kingdom Hearts" can also be categorized under this type. TAB = Table Game: As the name suggests, it is a game derived from traditional table games onto computers, such as various monopoly games (games where dice rolls determine movement spaces), the classic "Rich Man" series; card games also belong to TAB, such as "Tractor", "Hearts", "Mahjong", etc.
