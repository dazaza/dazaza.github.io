Morning Post reporter Song Jie. Yesterday, law enforcement officers of the Quality and Technical Supervision Inspection Team in Shanghai Holiday Inn uncovered a large number of imitation brand kitchen small appliances.

It was revealed that these imitation brand home appliances have very low prices, sometimes less than half the price of regular brands. But behind this so-called "rock bottom price", there are hidden dangers such as too thin power lines and missing parts. They may short circuit or leak electricity after being used for a while. The inferior materials used in imitation brand home appliances are one-third cheaper than those of the genuine ones. Some rice cookers even use recycled aluminum from scrapped car pistons for their inner pots. Long-term use could lead to the intake of toxic substances; induction cookers don't have energy efficiency labels or even falsely report energy efficiency levels. Such products not only consume more electricity, but also cannot guarantee the life of the panel. It is understood that these home appliances are mainly sold in some urban-rural fringe wholesale markets, and the victims are mostly migrant workers.

At first glance, the appliances in the warehouse are all well-known brands such as Supor and Midea. Under the reminder of the law enforcement officers, the reporter found that except for packaging and trademark identification which are very similar, they are actually completely different. Their real names are "Supor Appliances" and "Midea International". "These are all imitation brand small appliances, with slight changes in wording to confuse the public," said Xiong Xinjian, director of the Municipal Quality and Technical Supervision Inspection Team. For example, the English name of "Supor Appliances" is SUPER, which differs from the regular "SUPOR" by only one letter.

The power line quality is so poor it breaks easily

Law enforcement officers randomly inspected a "Supor Appliances" rice cooker on site, unscrewed the bottom plate with a screwdriver to carefully examine the internal structure, and measured the wire diameter with a micrometer. It was found that the rice cooker's power cord not only lacked a ground wire, but also the power cord was surprisingly thin, breaking easily when pulled. Such power cords can easily cause circuit failures and fire hazards. In addition, this rice cooker lacks the spring washer required by national mandatory standards, and without the safety protection of the anti-loosening spring washer, the rice cooker is also prone to catch fire.

If a home appliance is marked as "SUPER Supor Appliances", can you identify its difference from the regular "SUPOR Supor"? Yesterday was the "3?15" International Consumer Rights Day, and law enforcement officers of the Municipal Quality and Technical Supervision Inspection Team discovered an underground den near Jiasong Highway, seizing more than 5000 units of such imitation brand small appliances as "SUPER Supor Appliances".

According to on-site statistics, the den hid nearly 30 types and over 5000 units of imitation brand home appliances, basically produced by several small factories in Lianjiang, Foshan, Zhanjiang, etc., in Guangdong.

More than 5000 counterfeit home appliances piled up in the warehouse

Following the swift action of the law enforcement officers of the Municipal Quality and Technical Supervision Inspection Team, we arrived at the underground den. This den had no doorplate number or any sign, looking like an abandoned warehouse from the outside. However, when the warehouse door was opened by the law enforcement officers, they were surprised to find that the 1000-plus square meter warehouse was filled with various kitchen small appliances including rice cookers and induction cookers. The two "staff members" of the den both claimed to be workers, saying that the boss was in Guangzhou and they knew nothing about it.
