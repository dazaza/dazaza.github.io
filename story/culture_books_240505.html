"Chinese Style Burning" - A Long Love Novel in Mobile SMS Diary Format

by jqrxass on 2009-09-20 13:27:06

"Chinese Style of Burning" is the first long romantic novel in diary form written via mobile phone text messages. The story takes place in a large city in southern China and a coastal city on the South China Sea bay. One day in September 2004, the male protagonist Da Zhihui met by chance the woman he would later call his "little wife", and it was love at first sight. After that, they pursued their romance by sending each other daily messages, knowing nothing about each other's backgrounds - not about life, education, feelings, family, or economy. "Chinese Style of Burning" records their personal experiences of life and love during the year they got to know each other.

Part One of "Chinese Style of Burning"

A: Would you reject the call of an unexpected romance? To be honest, apart from our first encounter, I don't know anything about your background. I'm someone who follows their feelings.

B: Emotions are noble, but following one's feelings can be dangerous. I respect you, both for your writing and your character.

A: I'm a very picky person, yet also extremely grateful in life. How could I blame you for holding onto another kind of commitment? I really thank you; your appearance made me feel like I've been reborn. Please don't feel guilty, otherwise, I will feel even sadder. Alright?

B: Humanity shares many beautiful emotions. Our exchange under the phoenix tree was one such beauty. May your trip to Xinjiang be filled with similar beauty.

Part Two of "Chinese Style of Burning"

Da: Form supports content, and content blends with form. How I wish I could always be by Xiang girl's side, even if only as a quiet beetle. A lack of conditions forces me away, unable to comfort Xiang girl in her time of need.

Xiang: This love comes from deep within the soul. It is the purest essence of existence. Through this, we have confirmed each other's presence. Being together or not being together is merely a formality. What matters isn't the form, but the love resonating within our souls. It stands independently of any form, and conversely, it can manifest in any form.

Part Three of "Chinese Style of Burning"

Da: Previously, I mentioned a couple of friends - an older husband and a younger wife - who invited us to dinner. Actually, it was also their wedding anniversary feast. Their age difference is more than half, with a ratio of 55:25. They've been together for three years and their relationship is great. True love always has the power to move people.

Xiang: Love has nothing to do with age, but achieving legendary stories definitely requires courage, wisdom, and responsibility.
