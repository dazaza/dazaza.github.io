A new perspective on bugs from an old web enthusiast!

by seo175110 on 2012-03-06 11:15:15

My name is Long Lin, and I am a super veteran internet user with over 13 years of online experience. I have more than 10 years of experience in building web pages (sites). I still remember when NetEase first launched; we would go there to learn HTML tutorials. Back then, websites were mostly independent, and unless introduced by magazines or newspapers, you wouldn’t even know about the existence of other websites. Nowadays, websites primarily rely on search engine traffic, which compared to before, is an enormous change or something unimaginable. Perhaps this is why Google is so respected. I believe the emergence of search engines is as great as the appearance of Windows.

I studied Nuclear Geology for my undergraduate degree. Whether it was luck or misfortune, at the end of my second year, the country approved the establishment of the Software Engineering undergraduate program. Due to my personal interest, I applied to "switch" to the first batch of Software Engineering undergraduate programs in the country (approved by the department head). To this day, many friends still ask me why I suddenly changed my major so drastically. Maybe this is what you call fate. After graduation, my first job was at a subsidiary enterprise of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, stationed at IBM, and responsible for its official website. Later, due to outstanding performance, I was transferred to become a regular employee at IBM. However, this was not the kind of work I dreamed of, nor was it the kind of work someone of my age, having just graduated from university, should be doing. So, I resolutely quit and went to another job that I liked a little more, allowing me more time to study the things I liked.

In 2007, I spent over 2000 USD to buy www.conghua.com from abroad to make it into a local portal for Conghua. Gradually, I started to get involved in SEO. Unfortunately, during the operation process, due to several domestic registration issues and server problems, my site was often suspended for half a month to two months. As a result, I lost my top ranking for the keyword "Conghua," and now I'm stuck on the second page. This has prompted me to study SEO knowledge and hope to return to the first page. During this period, I also engaged in the page design of another relatively large-scale website. However, due to some unpleasant matters later, I don't want to mention the name of this website here. Because of these reasons, through my studies, I realized that external links are very important for a website.

One day, while chatting with a friend, I learned that A5 was holding a group-buying event for ChongChong (a software tool). After understanding the functions of ChongChong, I felt that it would be quite suitable for my target operations. Quickly, I made the payment and bought ChongChong. Of course, in the first two weeks after purchasing ChongChong, my mood changed from the original excitement to a bit disappointed because although ChongChong had powerful functions, it seemed a bit complicated, and I didn't know where to start. But after two weeks of learning, I finally understood ChongChong somewhat, and at this point, I began to crazily collect resources.

However, possibly because the things I collected were basically what all the users of ChongChong had already collected and used, I found that there were very few resources that could be successfully published without being deleted. Moreover, I personally felt that publishing information in bulk on other people's websites was not moral. So, I considered using some of my own domains to operate a network of sites. I established seven websites, installed programs supporting ChongChong's publishing interface, set permissions so that only my account could publish, and others who registered couldn't post information. This was mainly to prevent other ChongChong users from posting information on my sites. In fact, although my new sites have been online for only over a month, there have already been many applications for new accounts, so when we build websites, we should also consider how to prevent others from bombarding our websites with information when we bombard other websites with information.

During the operation of the network of sites, I kept an eye on the inclusion status of the websites every day. Initially, Baidu enthusiastically included hundreds of pages within about a week. However, less than half a month later, it suddenly reduced my inclusion to just over twenty articles, and even two sites had no pages included at all. At this point, I thought it might be because I set the publication frequency of ChongChong too high, basically sending out thousands of articles at fixed points every day. Then I considered adjusting it and used ChongChong's interval setting for publication times, randomly publishing each article between 30 seconds to 10 minutes. This should seem more like human operation to search engines. I don't know if it's because my operation method is correct or if Baidu was just joking with me, but now the inclusion of all seven websites has recovered, and each has included hundreds of articles. I estimate that because they are all low-weight websites, the inclusion is temporarily not ideal, but I think this result is already good for new websites. I feel it will gradually improve. However, since the content is all collected, the quality varies, and it's hard to categorize. Therefore, in the next step, I will slowly adjust it to better serve the websites I want to do external links for.

When these seven websites gain certain weight, I plan to give full-site links to the websites I want to do external links for. This way, I should have decent external link resources and won't interfere with others' websites. Currently, due to the rush to launch the websites without proper planning of the website structure, I haven't installed Baidu Statistics yet, because I heard it analyzes your website experience. My content is all collected, so the experience is not good. Also, because I set one account to post information, it created a situation where all the information came from one user. However, this will change as I continue to improve. When the website inclusion is good and some keywords rank well, I can further conduct SEM operations, redirecting traffic to my valuable websites or conducting targeted marketing to convert traffic into revenue.

Of course, the portal blog function of ChongChong software is also good. I copied another copy of ChongChong as a specific program for publishing portal blogs and persisted in updating these blogs every day. If I keep posting these blogs, the future weight will be good. Since I posted on too many sites, I actually didn't monitor the inclusion status of these sites. However, I just conducted a search for my specific keywords and found that quite a few articles were included in the search engines, proving it was effective, and it didn't waste my electricity bill.

Actually, I've used less than 20% of ChongChong software's functions. Recently, I've started using ChongChong to properly optimize a structured website http://www.smf.cn, learning from previous shortcomings and effectively leveraging ChongChong software to serve my website. So, I constantly research the interactive experiences shared in the group. Friends who know me understand that although I am also a user, I help answer others' questions in the group. This is due to my previous PMP course; the technology or experience you master, if you only keep it yourself, you will forget it quickly in the future. However, if you organize it and share it with others, you will remember more experiences for a long time. I forgot the exact theory, but I remember this experience.

For a software, I think utilizing 40% of its functions is already quite good. For example, Word, we may not even use 5% of its functions regularly. Therefore, using tools in the most suitable place for oneself is the right way. Of course, if you need to widely publish a lot of content, I continuously use the built-in functions of ChongChong to collect resources. Resources are not something you can pull down completely in a day or two; neither Google nor Baidu rose overnight. At the beginning, everyone knows that even if your hands get tired from entering verification codes, the success rate is less than 1%. But as you gradually collect new resources and accumulate your resources, you can increase the success rate. My current experience is to delete all places that require entering verification codes. If a website shows login errors after publishing for a period, discard it; this means an administrator is watching you, and publishing will only waste everyone's time. Slowly accumulate your resources, don't fear failure. Each time I collect hundreds of resources, I can only successfully publish resources from 2 or 3 of them. And I don't collect resources every day. If I did, the accumulated resources would be astonishing.

After buying ChongChong, my favorite part is the customer service QQ group, where experts frequently share various experiences, including regular weekly shares. These aren't just about the software itself but also other special uses of ChongChong or other SEO-related experiences. This is an over-value place, and I must especially thank Le Xianyao and Chen Mo. In general, it's about using the software in the places where you need it most and adapting it to your actual situation.
