Hefei, September 25th (reporter Lan Wu) - On September 25th, it was learned from the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) that a joint research team from USTC and Tsinghua University has successfully achieved Measurement-Device-Independent Quantum Key Distribution (MDI-QKD). This is also the first time MDI-QKD has been realized internationally, successfully addressing the security risks in single-photon detection systems that are vulnerable to hacker attacks in real-world environments.

Quantum key distribution (QKD), based on quantum physics and information science, is considered the most secure encryption method. However, despite QKD theoretically offering unconditional security, the original scheme required the use of ideal single-photon sources and single-photon detectors, which are hard to achieve under real-world conditions. This leads to various potential security vulnerabilities in practical QKD systems.

In 2007, the joint research group from USTC and Tsinghua University first achieved decoy-state quantum key distribution at the hundred-kilometer level internationally. This successfully resolved the security loopholes brought by non-ideal single-photon sources. However, the imperfections of detectors subsequently became the main attack point for "quantum hackers." Several groups internationally proposed attack schemes targeting detection systems, such as "time-shift attacks," "dead-time attacks," and "blinding attacks" using strong light. Although all known quantum hacking attacks can be defended against by appropriately modifying existing quantum cryptographic systems, theoretical security risks still exist.

Is there a quantum key distribution system that can fundamentally and perfectly solve all known and unknown attacks targeting detection systems? Based on this concept, Jian-Wei Pan's group developed interference technology using independent laser sources and jointly developed the world’s most advanced room-temperature communication-band single-photon detector with Stanford University. On this basis, combined with theoretical analysis provided by Professor Xiongfeng Ma from Tsinghua University, they have, for the first time in the world, achieved measurement-device-independent secure quantum key distribution. This experiment is inherently immune to any attack targeting detection systems, perfectly resolving the security risk issues of detection systems.

It is reported that these research results were published in the latest issue of the authoritative international physics journal *Physical Review Letters*, where reviewers praised it as "a significant contribution to the field."

For many years, Jian-Wei Pan's group has conducted systematic forward-looking research on metropolitan and inter-city quantum secure communications based on fiber optics, achieving several pioneering results: In 2007, they first achieved hundred-kilometer decoy-state quantum key distribution in the world. In 2008, based on realizing a prototype system of decoy-state-based fiber optic quantum communication, they successfully built the world’s first interconnected photonic quantum telephone network in Hefei.
