Cannes Directors' Fortnight lineup unveiled, Coppola's new film to open it

by zhlhmntk80 on 2011-12-09 03:13:55

Photos from Francis Ford Coppola's new film "Tetro" have been released. After the main competition and "A Certain Regard" sections' entries were announced for the 62nd Cannes Film Festival, Oliver Pere, the artistic director of the Directors’ Fortnight section, recently revealed the list of films selected for this year's Cannes Directors’ Fortnight section. Among them, renowned director Francis Ford Coppola's new film "Tetro" will open the Directors’ Fortnight section. Previously, Coppola had publicly stated that he declined the invitation from the organizing committee, but Fu Mao hoped that "Tetro" could join the official section as a non-competitive screening.

Coppola has won the Cannes Palme d'Or twice with "The Conversation" and "Apocalypse Now." In the past decade, he has become relatively quiet. "Tetro" and his previous work "Youth Without Youth" form Coppola's comeback chapter. This black-and-white film incorporates Coppola's own autobiographical experiences, telling the story of a pair of brothers in Buenos Aires and their family, with the charismatic actor Vincent Gallo playing the role of the older brother. Coppola believes that the style of the film is close to European cinema of the 1960s, but many people associate it with a series of independent productions he made in the 1980s. The film will be released in North America on June , and the creative team will then travel to San Francisco and other places for promotion.

Compared to the competitive section, there are more American films in the Directors’ Fortnight section, with a total of five. Chinese mainland director Liu Jiayin's "Leather II" also successfully made the cut. From Asia, Hong Sang-soo's "Even the Rain," Nobuhiro Suwa's new work "Yuki & Nina," and the faces of new directors from Singapore and Malaysia are also included. At the opening ceremony of the Directors’ Fortnight section, Naomi Kawase will be awarded the Carriage of Gold Award. A comprehensive attack by Asian directors can also be seen in the "A Certain Regard" section, where Bong Joon-ho, Hirokazu Koreeda, and Lav Diaz, among others, are also listed.
