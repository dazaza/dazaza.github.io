Love Knows No Waiting

Today, on my way to the cafeteria for a meal, I saw you and your current girlfriend. The two of you were walking side by side, talking and laughing as you went, looking very happy together. You happened to look up and caught sight of me, then quickly lowered your head and continued with your happiness. Do you know that this small, unintentional gesture of yours pierced me deeply, making me feel breathless all at once.

Why have we become like this? Even if we couldn't be boyfriend and girlfriend, we could still have been very good friends, I stubbornly believed so all along.

Looking back, we also met through someone else's introduction. At the time, when I first saw you, I was captivated by the unique aura you exuded. You didn’t talk much, but the equally introverted me kept searching in my mind for topics you might find interesting, just to get to know you better. I didn't want this beautiful moment to be ruined by silence.

I talked about my dad, mom, and sister, gradually drawing you into my conversation. You started telling me about your grandfather and grandmother, about your family history. I was very happy at the time because I felt like I knew some secrets about you that others might not know.

I remember our first and only date was set in a café. We both ordered medium-rare pepper steak (from which I fell in love with its taste), eating the steak while chatting, feeling very warm and cozy. You talked about your interests and hobbies, saying that when you had nothing to do, you'd play the guitar or go out with friends to bars. But neither of these things were something I could do. At the time, I regretted not learning more things in college, at least some musical instruments, so we could have another common topic to talk about.

Looking back now, it seems silly at the time, but I was incredibly happy — the happiness you brought me. After finishing the steak, we ordered a dessert, red bean paste. The two of us took turns eating from the same dish, spoonful by spoonful. The red bean paste was sweet, but it wasn't sweeter than my heart.

When we parted ways in the evening, you said we should hang out again sometime. Do you know, because of those words, I practically glued my phone to my body, fearing I would miss any of your calls. But then you suddenly disappeared without a trace. Because of a woman's modesty, I didn't dare to call you first, afraid you might think I lacked depth. So, I waited, hoping for your possible call. Later, a colleague told me that you had already found someone to marry through an introduction by your family.

I refused to believe it, I didn't want to believe it. But fate is cruel, letting me witness this cruel fact with my own eyes. That day, as I went downstairs, I suddenly saw you, hand in hand with your current girlfriend, passing right by me. It seemed like you had already forgotten about me, or maybe you were pretending not to recognize me to avoid your girlfriend’s misunderstanding. Even if we can't be lovers, we could still be very good friends, right? At least I could stay by your side, quietly watching over you, stubbornly thinking so all along.

Actually, in the eyes of others and you, there was never a real start between us. Since there was no beginning, there was no end, nor any process to speak of. Then where does this supposed wound come from? In reality, it has always been my one-sided wish, voluntarily jumping into this non-existent whirlpool of emotions, allowing myself to sink deeper and deeper. Friends say I'm foolish, and I find it laughable too, yet I can't pull myself out.

Maybe I owe you too much from a past life, needing to repay it all in this one. But why must it be repaid in this manner, Heaven?

It's been a long time since I've seen you, haven't thought about you either, and haven't deliberately waited for your call. I thought I had let it go.

But when I saw you again, the pain buried deep inside resurfaced, piercing my heart once more. It turns out I’ve been deceiving myself all along; you are the scar in my heart that cannot heal.

I ask myself: How much longer will the wound you left take to heal?
