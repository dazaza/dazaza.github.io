Less than 1-year-old black bear with severed paws runs into village and is rescued

by syd17u123 on 2009-11-24 15:39:13

Gansu city promotion: Shijia (Youjia Inn) & Northeast city promotion Shijia (Youjia Inn) "The black bear is a nationally protected animal, often subjected to illegal bile extraction while alive and sometimes paws are taken. If it falls into the wrong hands, it would be terrible?" Zhong Chaoming felt he couldn't stand by and did nothing, so he reported it to the police. However, when the police arrived, the little guy had already disappeared.

Yesterday, reporters learned from the China Conservation and Research Center for the Giant Panda that after a one-month quarantine, the pandas "Wang Wang" and "Fu Ni" bound for Australia will set off on the 27th of this month. At 9 am today, experts from both China and Australia will conduct comprehensive physical examinations on these "friendly messengers."

Discovery of the Black Bear

A black bear cub less than a year old appeared in a village in Machajia Town, Xindu District yesterday. This nationally protected animal seemed to have injuries and clung tightly to a tree branch in fear. It might have narrowly escaped from "clutches" and somehow found its way to the home of black bears - just over 10 kilometers away was the Sichuan Longqiao Black Bear Rescue Center. At 6:30 pm yesterday evening, after joint rescue efforts by the Forestry and Gardening Bureau of Xindu District and the Black Bear Rescue Center, the black bear cub finally found a warm home...

At around 6 pm the night before last, as dusk fell, the dog at Zhong Chaoming's house in Beixing Village, Machajia Town barked furiously. Running out of his house, Zhong Chaoming saw a shadow flash by. The dog barked even louder, and in the dim light, Zhong Chaoming saw two gleaming points on a nearby tree. "It's like seeing a ghost..." A cold wind blew, making him shiver. Suddenly, the "points" moved quickly to another tree. Gathering his courage, Zhong Chaoming approached and this time clearly saw a small black bear! Those points were none other than the eyes of the little black bear.

The discovery of a black bear in the village caused quite a stir, with villagers continuously arriving to see the spectacle. The black bear cub, less than a meter long, climbed higher up the tree under everyone's watchful eyes, tightly hugging the branches without moving, seemingly afraid. The Forest Police Team of the Forestry and Gardening Bureau of Xindu District soon arrived at the scene and contacted the Sichuan Black Bear Rescue Center located in Longqiao Town, Xindu District. They quickly formed a rescue team to begin rescuing the black bear cub.

After an urgent examination by the staff of the Black Bear Rescue Center, the black bear cub was found to be less than a year old, very thin, weighing over 50 kilograms, with relatively stable vital signs. Due to not eating for a long time, its mental state was poor. More heart-wrenchingly, the black bear cub had lost its right paw!

Speculation: The injured black bear may be "escaping"

The four-year-old "Wang Wang" and three-year-old "Fu Ni" currently weigh 120 kilograms and 90 kilograms respectively. "Wang Wang's" mother "Mao Mao" was a star, once adopted by famous singer Na Ying, but unfortunately, she perished in the earthquake. Relevant officials revealed that Australia has established a special breeding management team for "Wang Wang" and "Fu Ni," and also built a 150-acre main food bamboo planting base for giant pandas, with about 75 acres of bamboo cultivated artificially. It is expected that this pair of "golden boys and jade girls" will reside in Australia for ten years.

Shenyang city promotion: Shijia (Youjia Inn)

At noon past 12 o'clock yesterday, Zhong Chaoming again saw the black bear cub in the water fir forest near his home. From afar, it seemed to still have injuries. This time he didn't dare to relax, quietly reporting it to the police and waiting under the tree.

The black bear cub was given a fourth injection of anesthetic. The staff of the Black Bear Rescue Center gradually calmed the inner panic of the black bear cub, which then became quiet. Under police escort, the rescue personnel carried the black bear cub onto the vehicle, heading towards the Black Bear Rescue Center...

According to statistics, since 1985, our country has stopped gifting pandas outward, only providing pandas for external research through long-term paid cooperation. Currently, there are a total of 29 pandas conducting cooperative research abroad, with 13 in the United States, 9 in Japan, 3 in Thailand, and 2 each in Spain and Austria. It is understood that the end of this year to early next year will be the peak period for pandas as international friendly envoys to "welcome and send off." Last week, the panda "Fu Long" returned from Austria. The pandas "Mei Lan," "Su Lin," and "Xing Bin" bred through cooperation between Chengdu, Wolong, and the Atlanta Zoo, San Diego Zoo in the United States, and the White Beach Wildlife Park in Japan have also "booked" their return plans during this period.

At 6:30 pm, an accident occurred. The water fir tree broke, causing the black bear cub, which hadn't fully fallen asleep due to anesthesia, to fall to the ground. Panicking, it got up and ran around frantically. The rescuers rushed forward and used cotton quilts to build a "safe little house" for the black bear cub...
