If loving you could be like my weapon 'never wearing out', then no matter how time passes, the affection I have for you will not lose its durability. But reality has told me that between us, we lost to time.

...

During those three years of high school, I was always searching for the meaning of freedom. In the autumn of 2009, I graduated successfully. Although my college entrance exam results were far from ideal, the whole grade failed completely, which was somewhat comforting. At that time, I understood the sighs our teachers often gave us: "You kids today, each generation is worse than the last."

When studying, I always felt there was no freedom, but after graduation, I realized that having too much free time with no plans was a terrifying thing. Later, I decided to continue my education and enrolled in a junior college.

In the blink of an eye, it was October, and I should go to school for registration. After registration, we freshmen were supposed to undergo several days of military-style management. Maybe because I'm not good at enduring hardship, after receiving the camouflage uniform, I went home and slept. After waking up, I had already forgotten all about the arduous and unrewarding military training.

Suddenly, a classmate called me. It turned out he wanted to invite me to play "Dream Westward Journey" again. I had played this game before, but only superficially. After some classmates' equipment was reset, I quit. Now that we are college students, in my impression, college students always have endless time to surf the internet. If I don't find something to play, I would feel I am betraying this identity. I replied: "Alright, see you at the net cafe."

In less than half the time it takes to burn a stick of incense, we gathered at the net cafe, recharged our cards, and turned on the computers.

"You look for the server, tell me when you're done, and I'll create the character," I said and started playing with my ball.

After messing around for a while, he chose Liangfengjiang in Guangxi's second district.

After deciding, it took just a few minutes to create two new NetEase accounts and two new characters in "Dream Westward Journey."

His shameless nickname was 'One Night of Passion'. Fine, if you can have one night of passion, I'll have a passionate night. I also chose the same role and profession as him - Swordsmen, apprenticing under Cheng Yaojin of the Tang Dynasty.

Because we had played before, we leveled up quite fast. In just two days, we reached level 59, confirming that college life is indeed carefree. The game is divided into stages, generally every 9 levels: 59, 69, 89, 109, 129, 138, 144, 155 (top level). Each stage has its own group of enthusiasts; the higher the level, the more expensive it gets. Those at the top are mostly RMB warriors or proxy groups.

For people like us, reaching level 69 is satisfying enough, and I liked level 69. There are many people here, making it easy to form teams for activities. This is a very conflicting stage, where both the proletariat and the bourgeoisie are relatively numerous, making it a rather bizarre phase. Because many weapons and equipment without level restrictions are in this stage, these items are luxury goods. A piece of equipment costing tens of thousands of RMB isn't uncommon, but since it's still a low-level stage, many proletariats linger here.

We belong to the proletariat, so we shouldn't aspire for too high a level; entertainment is enough. We do small activities every day and catch little ghosts. We stayed at level 59 just to save experience to reach level 69, which was our destination. He had already started looking for equipment suitable for level 69, while I focused on gaining experience. Our commonality was that we had become addicted, living a life 'isolated from the world.' I wasn't sure whether to thank this college life or hate it. Perhaps at the time, we thought it was worth thanking.

A week passed quickly, and I felt it was time to start getting equipment. On a quiet night, we still couldn't find a reason to sleep and continued fighting online. He bought the first set of books and iron for level 60 weapons.

"I know someone who can forge weapons," I grinned mischievously at him.

"Call him to help me!" He looked at me eagerly.

I thought, this guy even wants to save on forging fees. Wait until he makes a junk weapon, and I'll make him regret all night. To get a professional to do it still costs a bit of money. This friend I knew online learned weapon forging. Once, I posted a world announcement asking for someone to forge me a weapon for free, and he eagerly volunteered. I let him forge it for me, and thus became friends. That time, he forged me a standard national product (the most ordinary), leaving me 'regretting all night.' His purpose in forging weapons for free was to improve his forging proficiency. Many say the higher the proficiency, the greater the chance of forging a good weapon. I messaged this ordinary weapon forger, who happened to be online, and he readily accepted this unprofitable deal. After a few seconds, he set up a stall outside the blacksmith shop with the words "Free Forging" prominently displayed. My buddy couldn't wait to click on the stall, select the corresponding forging level, and place the prepared books and iron in the right positions. Surprisingly, he chose enhanced forging. His hand trembled slightly but clicked anyway. Forging is divided into normal forging and enhanced forging. Normal forging involves the forger simply creating the weapon, whereas enhanced forging means the forger creates the weapon, but the system automatically places it in the blacksmith shop. You need to buy enhancement stones as materials and then exchange them with the blacksmith shop owner to retrieve your weapon, which costs a bit of money. However, from the names alone, it's clear that the probability of forging a good weapon through enhanced forging is much higher than normal forging, though miracles aren't impossible. Occasionally, normal forging might produce something better than enhanced forging, depending on your luck.

"Haha, looks like you're going to regret all night tonight," I smirked, looking at him eagerly.

"Whatever~" He rolled his eyes nonchalantly.

He quickly posted a world channel message to collect enhancement stones, and within seconds, a few stones were traded into his bag, along with his hundreds of thousands of MHB (Dream game coins) disappearing into others' bags. He couldn't wait to give the enhancement stones to the blacksmith shop owner, exchanging them for his Dragon Sword. He casually bought a scroll to appraise the weapon, capturing my attention. I wondered what kind of attributes this weapon would have once he clicked. His eyebrows were slightly furrowed, perhaps due to excitement, anticipation, or even fear. His hand trembled slightly and missed the weapon upon clicking.

"Shit, click already!" I urged impatiently, eager to see what trash would come out.

He clicked the scroll again, stabilizing this time. Then he clicked the sword, immediately moving his mouse away. I didn't have time to see the attributes, and I guessed he didn't either. Perhaps this time, he was afraid, fearing disappointment.

"Shit, quickly check the weapon, see what kind of trash it is," I urged anxiously.

That guy slowly moved his mouse towards the weapon. When he clicked, we both widened our eyes. The damage and accuracy combined made it an extremely rare weapon.

"Shit, a man rode a motorcycle 6,000 kilometers to escort a stranger deaf-mute elder home, please treat me to dinner." was my first reaction.

"Haha..." He was incredibly excited, grabbing my shoulders and nearly shaking me into a concussion.

"Shit, treat me to dinner quickly," I said irritably.

"Okay..." He happily agreed, which hadn't happened in ages.

We left the net cafe and headed to a late-night snack stall. Along the way, he chattered incessantly like he had found gold, while I was distracted, barely hearing a full sentence. I hadn't felt this sourness in years, possibly due to envy and jealousy. It was so unfair, really damn unfair. But what could be done? Reality was right there, and we had to accept it. "A good weapon makes a good Tang Dynasty warrior," he cheerfully said, which I heard clearly. It might be the most complete sentence I heard from his rambling and also the one I shouldn't have heard. At that moment, I surprisingly backed down, easily giving up.

"I've discovered that Tang Dynasty isn't fun, I won't play it anymore, I want to try something else," I said, lacking confidence.

I was glad he smiled silently, maybe he didn't hear clearly, or maybe I didn't say it out loud.

Regardless of whether it was spoken or not, since I had no confidence in playing Tang Dynasty, it was better to switch professions early. Early next morning, I rushed to the net cafe alone. The phrase "a good weapon makes a good Tang Dynasty warrior" echoed in my mind. Tang Dynasty is purely a violent class, mainly relying on high-damage weapons to empower its violence. I wanted to avoid it, so I chose the class that frustrates this profession the most—Sealing Class! Sealing classes generally have high defense and speed, restricting opponents into abnormal states, thereby slowly torturing them to death. However, it's not to say sealing classes can easily kill high-output types; a single failure in sealing can lead to death under the opponent's blade. Sealing classes include Daughter Village, Fangcun Mountain, Spider Cave, Heavenly Palace, and Wu Zhuangguan. Daughter Village and Spider Cave only accept women, and I didn't want to be teased as a transvestite, so I never considered joining these sects. The best team formation usually includes Wu Zhuangguan. Using their skills, they can treat sealed monsters as dead. I decisively chose Wu Zhuangguan, facilitating future cooperation with that guy in team activities. Thinking about it, I seemed to regain a bit of confidence. Within less than half a minute, a new NetEase user was born, and within a minute, a new character in "Dream Westward Journey" came into existence. Characters that can apprentice under Wu Zhuangguan can choose between Dragon Prince and Divine Soldier. From the public perspective, Dragon Prince is millions of times handsomer than Divine Soldier. Perhaps due to some inferiority complex or wanting to appear unconventional, I insisted on choosing Divine Soldier. Nicknames in the game cannot be duplicated, yet my nickname remained 'Passionate Night,' just with an unfamiliar character inserted in the middle.

In the following two weeks, there seemed to be a generation gap between us. I played mine, and he played his, water didn't interfere with fire. However, being different levels naturally meant water wouldn't interfere with fire. Two days after obtaining that weapon, he reached our ultimate goal of level 69. I, however, started anew, but leveling up to 69 isn't difficult in this game. Two weeks later, we were side by side again, just with my role changing from output to support.

I usually browsed stalls during idle times, looking for suitable equipment. My sect doesn't depend on a single weapon for strength, so I leveled up without equipment, planning to gradually acquire it later.

On a leisurely noon, after eating to satisfaction, I wandered aimlessly through the streets and alleys in the game, hoping for something intriguing to appear. As I walked, I stopped at a "Massive Clearance Sale" stall. Without much expectation, I casually clicked and checked each item one by one. When I reached the seventh item, my eyes lit up. It was a necklace. Judging from its attributes and price, it was well worth purchasing immediately. However, a psychological habit persisted—I believed that if bargaining was possible, saving a coin or two would prevent wasting half a coin. Immediately, I added the vendor's ID. It turned out to be someone of the same level, with the nickname 'Dumb Melon.'

"Can you lower the price of your necklace?" I eagerly sent him a message.

"Eating, wait a bit," an automatic reply chilled my heart.

I held off impulsively snapping up the necklace.

Waiting was painful. Five minutes passed, ten minutes, fifteen minutes...

Half an hour later, I glanced at the stall again. The necklace was still there, untouched by anyone. I felt a peculiar joy, and sent another message to 'Dumb Melon': "Are you back?"

The automatic reply remained unchanged, responding identically.

Just as I was about to leave, the chat bar flashed.

"Yeah, how much are you willing to pay?" the other side finally responded.

"What's your lowest price?" I seized the opportunity to bargain after waiting so long.

"You name it."

"Reduce it by 200,000, I'll snap it up for 1,800,000."

"Nope, it's too low."

"It's fair, you're not losing anything."

"Alright, but I have a condition."

"Say it." I was thrilled, ready to celebrate.

"Hmm, you be my slave for three days."

I sent a wide-eyed expression, adding, "What do you plan to do?"

"Just follow my orders for these three days, do what I tell you."

I pondered, feeling strange but unable to pinpoint why. Anyway, it's virtual, nothing to fear. Grabbing that necklace was the priority.

"Fine, as long as I can do it, it's acceptable. Adjust the price!"

Within half a minute, the necklace was changed from 2,000,000 to 1,800,000 (game currency), and I instantly snapped it up. Holding it in my bag, I felt both secure and excited. At that moment, the chat bar flashed again.

"Starting tomorrow, you're my slave."

"Alright." I resolutely replied. Though embarrassing if revealed, keeping silent saved me 200,000. Reflecting on it, I felt secretly pleased. (Later, I realized I wasn't her slave for just three days; how long afterward I remained her slave remains unclear, and how long I will continue to be her slave is unknown.)

Early the next morning, I woke up aimlessly wandering in the dreamlike game, forgetting yesterday's incident. Unknown how long had passed, she logged in, though I was unaware. Suddenly, the chat bar flashed, revealing her presence.

"Hi, what are you doing?"

"Bored."

"Come take me and my friend ghost hunting."

"Okay, wait a sec." I quickly organized my thoughts, preparing to be a professional ghost-hunting captain.

"Let's meet at the gathering spot."

I found a place for group activities, calling her to gather there.

"Alright, join my team." She formed a team with her friend, inviting me to join.

I searched for her team and applied to join. Upon application, she promptly added me to her team. I noticed her friend was only level 65, a newcomer.

She handed over the captaincy to me, which I accepted, becoming a cart driver pulling them. If being a slave was this easy, it seemed trivial.

I diligently pulled my team, while they engaged in lively conversation. What intrigued me was their flirtatious tone, words I couldn't utter, likely beyond any man's capability. Her friend was also a female character, the same as hers, but from a different sect. Dumb Melon followed Pan Silk Ridge, while her friend belonged to Devil King Sect.

"Are you girls?" I curiously asked.

"Guess."

"..." I was speechless.

They continued their flirtations.

Actually, encountering real girls in games is as rare as finding pandas. Most female characters are played by men. I noticed Dumb Melon, despite following Pan Silk Ridge, a sealing sect, added all her points to strength, making her quite violent. This realization instilled a slight sense of respect.

"Dumb Melon, what's your QQ number?" I couldn't resist asking.

"None."

"..." I was speechless again. In this era, lacking QQ seems alien.

"Let me give you one!" I must have been possessed.

"Sure!" She played along perfectly.

Thus, I handed over a newly registered premium QQ number to her. Actually, it contained no contacts except my main QQ, which I often kept online, reaching a decent level with a couple of moons.

Soon, the QQ lit up, presumably her logging in. Excited, I sent her a smiley face.

"Let's chat in-game!" She dismissed my gesture coldly.

"..." I was speechless.

Returning to the game, they continued their sweet talk, while I silently fulfilled my "slave" duties, diligently pulling their cart. Gradually, I grew familiar with this 'Dumb Melon,' liking her despite knowing neither her gender nor identity. My intuition suggested she was likely a girl.

Before I knew it, three days as a cart driver passed, yet I had grown accustomed to involving her in every activity. She never refused, tagging along wherever I went. Slowly, I discovered my friendship with her was the highest among all my friends, surpassing even 'One Night of Passion.' Despite meeting Dumb Melon, I still accompanied 'One Night of Passion' in activities and dungeons. Gradually, he became familiar with her. Later, the three of us frequently appeared in the same team, with me inevitably remaining the cart driver. There's no helping it; auxiliary classes are often discriminated against in many activities due to lack of output. Thus, our fate was to pull carts, leading groups of high-output players to accomplish tasks.

Many things, once you get used to them, remain so. Getting used to them, you slowly learn to love the habit. Therefore, I pulled carts happily, meticulously guiding my team through every task. Moreover, my team included both people I admired and envied, as well as someone I liked.

One day, I realized my friendship with Dumb Melon was nearing 1000. In this dreamlike game, each battle completed adds one point to friendship, accumulating over time. Crucially, in this game, 1000 friendship points allow marriage.

"Dumb Melon, let's get married!" I calmly sought her opinion.

"Alright!" She calmly provided the answer I desired.

Thus, I eagerly dragged her to the Elder Moon (NPC) to formalize the procedure, fearing she might suddenly change her mind. This was my first marriage in gaming, and I truly knew nothing. Only upon reaching Elder Moon did I realize marriage required owning a house and a fee of 500,000 Dream game coins. My scalp tingled... I still didn't own a house.

"I'll give you 500,000, go buy a house!" Dumb Melon must have known my dilemma, tossing 500,000 Dream game coins into my bag.

"I want to build our own home!" I needed an excuse to step down gracefully.

"Can you?"

"Let's try!"

After brief exchanges, I rushed to the Housing Officer (NPC) to commence construction of our wedding house. Clicking on the Housing Officer, I realized I could only build the smallest house—a cottage! Houses can be categorized as cottages, mansions, or villas. With my current capabilities, I could only construct a cottage. More importantly, I was clueless about building houses! Nonetheless, I proceeded stubbornly, unaware of material properties, providing whatever he requested. Within five minutes, I somehow managed to build a cottage, albeit with poor feng shui—wind ominous, fire strange! Feng shui categories include Purple Qi from the East, Blessings Overflowing the Door, Suitable Home and Family, Water Withered Stone Marsh, Wind Ominous Fire Strange. Clearly, my construction skills were abysmal! But it would suffice temporarily, considering it was my own creation.

"The house is done, come to Elder Moon, we can get married now!" I excitedly informed her, barely containing my eagerness.

"Okay!" She remained composed.

In no time, under Elder Moon's witness, we became husband and wife. As fireworks burst across the sky, I experienced a fleeting moment of emotion amidst years of virtual existence. Few attended our wedding, given my limited circle of friends, but 'One Night of Passion' had waited half an hour beforehand for me to distribute wedding candies.

...

Days passed one by one. Every day, I led my Dumb Melon wife on quests and dungeons. Those days, my entire life revolved around this dreamlike world.

Until one day, she suddenly announced she was going to enlist. My brain momentarily exploded, leaving me dazed. She briefly explained, merely informing me that a friend would continue playing her account, then logged off. Even the QQ I gifted her went dark and never lit up again. She remained as sharp and calm as ever!

Carrying a sense of loss, I also logged off. For the next two days, I lacked the courage to reopen "Dream Westward Journey," hiding in my dormitory sleeping endlessly.

On the third day, I realized that apart from this game, I had nothing left in my life, unsure where to go or whom to seek. Still, I opened the game and was surprised to find our Dumb Melon wife online.

"Greetings, Dumb Melon wife!" I couldn't resist greeting her, perhaps out of habit.

"...I'm her friend."

"Oh...what about her?" I still couldn't believe she had really enlisted.

"She has matters and won't be playing."

"Oh, will you keep playing?"

"Don't know, will play if I have time! Lead me in activities."

I suddenly felt a warm, almost touching sensation.

"Alright."

Then, as usual, I led her around the world, and she shamelessly followed me everywhere. The only difference was that she now enjoyed flirting with male characters but paid little attention to female ones.
