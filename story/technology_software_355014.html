Nanjing's real estate market sees a price drop trend, with the maximum decrease approaching 13% (Part I)

by oeerryy05 on 2010-05-12 17:24:13

Nanjing's Jiangbei real estate market suddenly hit by price cuts yesterday

With super-large scale developments and consistently leading in both development volume and sales in Nanjing, the Jiangbei real estate market suddenly became aggressive yesterday! Following the Xuri Shangcheng development offering a fixed price of 4500 RMB/sq m for over 300 units, Suning Properties launched another fixed-price strategy for its three developments in North Bridge: The Tianrun City development set a fixed price of 4088 RMB/sq m for 350 units, which is about 600 RMB/sq m cheaper than before.

The continuous discounting and promotions by the North Bridge developments—does their collective price drop signify that Jiangbei housing prices can no longer hold up? Is this a precursor to an overall decline in Nanjing housing prices?

[Price Drop] Three more developments offer fixed-price units

The super-large Tianrun City development, which surprisingly offered a fixed price of 4088 RMB/sq m, has a total of 350 special-priced units spread across the development. These 350 special-priced units account for 16% of the total available units for sale.

Coincidentally, two other developments in the same Jiangbei sector of North Bridge also implemented fixed prices: Venetian Water City’s 150 units are uniformly priced at 4480 RMB/sq m, while Tianhua Lvgu’s 50 units are uniformly priced at 4188 RMB/sq m. However, it was learned that these two developments have strict restrictions on the floors of the units, such as: Development B targeting only the first floor and top floor units, and Development C targeting only the 1st to 6th floors of high-rise buildings, the first floor and sixth floor of low-rise buildings.

In total, there are 550 special-priced units, what proportion do they represent of the total units currently on sale? According to Nanjing Online Real Estate data, these three developments currently have a total of 3360 units available for sale. Calculating from this, the 550 special-priced units account for approximately 16% of the total available units.

An industry insider stated that for a major player like Suning in North Bridge, 550 units spread across three developments is just a drop in the bucket, but considering the current total number of available units, a ratio of 16% is still relatively significant.

Sold to specific groups? Sales office says anyone can buy

Interestingly, Suning Properties' unified statement yesterday was: The 550 units across the three developments are specifically set aside to give back to society and show social humanitarian care, particularly selecting 550 classic collection units to thank provincial and municipal model workers, advanced workers, soldiers, and other groups who have contributed to society.

However, when reporters asked each of the three developments yesterday, the salespeople all indicated there were no such restrictions, and buyers could purchase normally.

Why is the developer being so evasive? An industry insider speculated that the developer might be concerned that previous buyers would feel they bought at a loss and demand refunds or returns.

[Citizen Reaction] Both those who want to buy and those who don't have reasons

Citizen Mr. Shi expressed that if the fixed-price discounts only apply to the first floor and top floor units, it doesn't mean much, but he noticed that some fixed prices weren't entirely targeted at the first and top floors. For example, Development C offers fixed-price discounts for the 1st to 6th floors of high-rise buildings, with units on the 4th and 5th floors selling for 4480 RMB/sq m, which is worth considering.

However, Mr. Shi also believed that these fixed-price developments are generally hundreds of yuan per square meter cheaper than the prices he had been following, and with five to six hundred units involved, the scope of the price reduction is quite extensive. He decided to check out the properties over the weekend.

But some citizens are unimpressed by the price drops in Jiangbei developments. Citizen Ms. Lu said that recently, while taking a bus to look at properties in Jiangbei, she was stuck in traffic on the bridge for over two hours. By the time she reached the development to view properties, she had lost all motivation. With such a transportation environment, living there in the future would be too inconvenient. Even though prices have dropped significantly, she remains cautious and will not consider buying in Jiangbei for now.

[Industry Analysis] North Bridge housing prices reaching five thousand Yuan becomes a mirage

Developments in Jiangbei last year aimed to break five thousand Yuan per square meter, but now it seems they've returned to their original state. Vice General Manager Shi Dong of Nanjing Wangshang Real Estate Research Institution analyzed that from a transaction average price of less than 3500 RMB/sq m in February last year to struggling to maintain at 4500 RMB/sq m now, breaking through 5000 RMB/sq m in Jiangbei housing prices has clearly failed.

This can also be seen from the comparison of sales volumes over the same period: In February this year, the sales volume of developments in Pukou decreased by 74% month-over-month, creating pressure for developers.

According to forecasts by the Wangshang Real Estate Research Institution, this year, new listings of commercial residential properties in Jiangbei will reach 3.313 million square meters (slightly higher than 3.21 million square meters in 2007), with new approvals for developments amounting to 2.865 million square meters, leading among all sectors in Nanjing. This year, the added supply of residential units from the 45 ongoing developments in the Jiangbei sector will reach 2.8 million square meters, and the concentrated and large-scale supply of commercial residential properties will inevitably make the competition in the North Bridge area of Jiangbei even more intense.
