Nokia WP 8.1 models exposed, "suspended operation" is coming

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 20:44:39

Today, the renowned tipster @evleaks revealed on Twitter that Nokia is preparing two Windows Phone 8.1 devices, codenamed "Goldfinger" and "Moneypenny".

@evleaks did not disclose detailed information about these two devices, but leaks like this typically suggest that the official release of the devices is not far off.

The Verge obtained information from sources familiar with Nokia's plans, revealing that Goldfinger will be the flagship of the two devices and will feature some technologies Nokia has been developing for years, including "3D Touch".

In simple terms, 3D Touch allows users to control the phone without touching the screen (fingers/palms need to hover above or near the sides of the screen), enabling gesture-based control. This was hinted at by former Nokia design head Marko Ahtisaari in an interview two years ago, who called it "a key area of our ongoing innovation." In fact, we've already experienced similar technology on Nokia Glance (Lumia 925) — by waving a hand over the screen while it's locked, the time can be displayed.

The aforementioned insider stated that Goldfinger will be able to detect gestures above the screen and on both sides of the device. Currently, gestures such as screen scrolling and flipping are under consideration.

Currently, there are smartphones on the market that support features akin to 3D Touch, such as Samsung's Galaxy S4 and Galaxy Note series. The former allows for hovering gestures to answer calls, switch music, and scroll web pages, while the latter supports stylus-based previewing functions through hovering.

It remains unclear whether 3D Touch will be a gimmick or a game-changer for Nokia's future models, as similar features can manifest differently depending on the manufacturer. The comparison between Apple's Touch ID and HTC's fingerprint scanner is a good example.

Perhaps it’s time to have more expectations for Windows Phone 8.1.

The WP 8 GDR3 update already supports Qualcomm's quad-core Snapdragon 800 processor and 1080p screens, helping to narrow the hardware gap between WP and Android. According to information obtained by Win Supersite, WP 8.1 will bring significant changes to the current WP system. As ZDNet puts it, WP 8.1 "will serve as a stepping stone towards unifying the API, NT core, and user interface across Windows, Windows RT, and WP." Based on the description, the changes brought by WP 8.1 include:

- WP 8 and Windows RT share 33% of their APIs; after upgrading to WP 8.1, this proportion will increase to 77%.

- Addition of a notification center and improved multitasking management.

- Removal of the back button (unlikely).

- Possible support for screens larger than 7 inches.

Additionally, multiple sources indicate that WP 8.1 will likely include the Cortana intelligent voice assistant. Besides performing routine voice operations, Cortana has adaptive and learning capabilities to provide users with an intelligent experience.

The exact launch date of WP 8.1 has not been confirmed yet. Some say it will be introduced at MWC 2014 (February), while others suggest it will come in the second quarter of next year.
