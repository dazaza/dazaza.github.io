What is thermal insulation film?

by shengyilx on 2012-03-05 09:53:34

Many modern buildings adopt glass curtain walls, large glass windows, and floor-to-ceiling glass doors (balconies). While this improves the room's view, it also increases the amount of solar radiation heat entering the interior, thus increasing the electricity usage for room air conditioning. Currently, the number one energy consumer in building energy consumption is air conditioning, with air conditioning energy consumption accounting for 20% to 50% of total building energy consumption.

In the 1960s, in order to solve the aforementioned problems, the United States developed heat-insulating film [http://www.020cm.cn]. It has functions such as heat insulation, energy saving, UV protection, aesthetics, comfort, safety, and explosion prevention, effectively and conveniently solving the problems caused by glass.

**Principles of the structure and performance of heat-insulating film:**

The most basic components of the film are: polyester base layer (PET), one side coated with an anti-scratch layer (HC), and the other side being the installation adhesive layer and protective film.

PET is a durable material that is sturdy, moisture-resistant, and performs well at both high and low temperatures. It is clear and transparent. After undergoing various processes such as metallization coating, magnetron sputtering, and lamination synthesis, it becomes a film with different characteristics.

Professional manufacturers of films typically use their patented adhesives for lamination synthesis and the installation adhesive layer. These mainly include pressure-sensitive adhesives and water molecule-activated adhesives.

The main types of heat-insulating films include: transparent thermal mirror, heat-reflective insulating film, low-reflection insulating film, and low-emissivity (Low-E) film.

Considering safety and ease of installation, the thickness of the base material of the heat-insulating film should be above 2MIL.

The installation method of heat-insulating film is usually similar: after removing the protective layer, apply installation liquid to the clean glass surface and the adhesive layer of the film; lay the film flat on the glass, cut off the excess edges, and use installation tools to remove excess moisture.

Heat-insulating film can be referred to as a "amphibious" product in the construction industry, as it can be used for both old building renovations and new building constructions. Although it is just an inconspicuous thin film, it can filter out 80% of infrared rays with heat and over 98% of ultraviolet rays, and also prevent glass breakage. Directly applying the film on ordinary transparent glass turns it into safety insulating glass with both heat insulation and safety explosion-proof functions.
