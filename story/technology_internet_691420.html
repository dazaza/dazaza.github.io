The decline in coal prices is slowing, currently experiencing the darkness before dawn, with prices expected to reach their lowest point in mid-March. In March, the supply will be constrained by the convening of the [Two Sessions] and early maintenance on the Dalian-Qinhuangdao railway line, which will support coal prices. For example, the price of high-quality Shanxi mixed coal (5500 kcal) at Qinhuangdao port, 750 yuan/ton, is expected to be the low point of this round of adjustments.

It is predicted that coal prices in 2012 will still remain at a historically [second highest] level. The average annual coal price is expected to be 770 yuan/ton, down about 6% from the 2011 level of 820 yuan/ton, but still higher than the average price in 2010. Historically, this still belongs to a [second highest] level price, with strong profitability. The supply side has begun to contract: Shanxi and Inner Mongolia have shown clear intentions to limit production.

The economy is gradually stabilizing, a soft landing is expected, and liquidity expectations are improving sequentially. According to Ping An Macro forecasts, the economy will stabilize at the bottom in the first quarter. Currently, PMI is gradually rising, CPI has peaked and is declining, M2 growth rate has hit bottom and is rebounding, and liquidity expectations continue to improve.

Pay attention to the fine-tuning of real estate policies, which will help repair the investment industry chain. Fine-tuning of real estate policies in some places will improve the real estate-led investment industry chain, repairing the valuation of upstream investment products (coal, cement).

Continuously rising oil prices are boosting the trend of coal stocks. With crude oil prices exceeding $100/barrel, it influences investor behavior, boosting the trend of alternative energy - coal stocks. Historical evidence shows that during the stage where crude oil prices exceed the [$100] mark, coal stocks can all achieve positive returns.

Coal stocks are piercing through the fog before dawn, and the second wave of opportunities has arrived. It is suggested to prioritize the allocation to thermal coal.

In order to speed up the process of resource integration in the Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region and further strengthen and expand the coal business, the company plans to increase capital by 8.5 billion yuan RMB to its wholly-owned subsidiary, Jizhong Energy Inner Mongolia Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as [Inner Mongolia Company]), to meet the funding needs of resource integration, increase the scale of resource integration, and accelerate the process of resource integration. After this increase in capital, the registered capital will be increased to 2.209 billion yuan, and the current debt-to-asset ratio is only 18.7%, with strong financing capabilities.

Inner Mongolia coal resource integration requires consolidation entities to have a production capacity scale of more than 5 million tons. It is expected that Jizhong Inner Mongolia Company will strive for the qualification of resource integration entity. According to the Inner Mongolia integration plan, the whole region will comprehensively consider the asset status, production scale, management level, and industrial development conditions of coal production enterprises to establish merger and acquisition entities.

Among them, enterprises with raw coal production capacity of more than 5 million tons, or already own at least one underground mine with a single well scale of 1.2 million tons or more, open-pit mines with a single mine scale of 3 million tons or more, and have not had major accidents involving more than 10 people within three years, with excellent assets, can be prioritized as merger and acquisition entities.
