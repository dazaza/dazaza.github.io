Why did Premier Wen's handwritten apology letter move hundreds of millions of netizens?

------ What should we learn from Premier Wen?

By Xu Shuguang, Greentree Inn

On October 12th, Premier Wen Jiabao wrote a letter to Xinhua News Agency correcting an error in his speech and offering an apology. The full text is as follows: "Dear General Editorial Office of Xinhua News Agency, in the article 'Education Prioritizes Teachers' broadcasted by your agency yesterday, the classification of rock science should be sedimentary rocks, igneous rocks (which can also be called volcanic rocks), and metamorphic rocks. I hereby correct this and extend my apologies to the wide readership." (People's Daily, October 13th, 2009)

Starting on the evening of October 12th, a handwritten apology letter from Premier Wen Jiabao of the State Council of China began circulating widely on the Internet, moving hundreds of millions of readers. (Xinhua News Agency)

Premier Wen's Handwritten Apology Letter

Upon hearing the news that Premier Wen had personally written a letter to Xinhua News Agency to correct an oral mistake in his speech and offer an apology to the wide readership, it immediately became the focus of media attention. This short apology letter made me read it multiple times, think deeply, and ask why Premier Wen's handwritten apology moved hundreds of millions of netizens? To my knowledge, there has never been any leader in history who personally wrote an article for "correction" because they said something wrong. Yet our premier, nearing 70 years old, achieved this, touching people deeply and making them reflect.

There are two key details I would like to interpret: First, the word "apology". Some people said the premier didn't need to specifically correct it, or could have found some reason, such as saying it was caused by the editor's oversight, etc., but this goes against the premier's consistent style; others said that the premier exposing his own "scar" damages his dignity, but actually not so at all. Looking at the netizens' comments, the support is overwhelming, with over 99.8% praising the premier. Second, the word "handwritten". Common thinking suggests that the premier is busy with myriad state affairs, and this "small matter" could have been delegated to someone else to convey his meaning without him having to do it himself. However, our premier chose to write the apology personally, showing even more his sincerity in apologizing and his attitude towards his own mistakes. This makes the world feel even more the greatness of the premier's image and his pragmatic spirit towards science. When the premier was studying and formulating the "National Medium- and Long-Term Education Reform and Development Plan Outline", he emphasized that "teachers should possess a scientific attitude of seeking truth, being innovative, and self-disciplined, striving to promote excellent academic atmosphere and academic morality," and he acted accordingly through his own example.

"Education is the communication between hearts, the fusion of souls, and the dialogue of personalities," Premier Wen reminded educators to "set an example and lead the way." Not long ago, he demonstrated leadership with "correct sitting posture" and pointed out errors in textbooks, setting an example through action. Now he mercilessly criticizes himself, courageously admitting his mistakes and daring to criticize himself. Indeed, this requires courage, and more importantly, Premier Wen conveys this spirit: whether one is a person or a scholar, one must pursue truth and reality, and science does not tolerate any falsehood. The premier's approachable image and servant-like sentiment have long been imprinted on the hearts of the Chinese people. At this moment, the image of a great premier who studies rigorously, adheres to the truth, and practices realism becomes vivid and touches the heart, making people respect him deeply.

No one is perfect, who can avoid making mistakes? Premier Wen is first a human being, then the premier. It's inevitable for a person to make some mistakes in their lifetime, but how one treats those mistakes reflects one's breadth of mind and demeanor. As Einstein once said, "Anyone who is careless about the truth in small matters will not be trustworthy in big ones." In front of the truth, everyone is equal, mistakes are not divided by size, what matters is whether one can admit and correct them. Premier Wen's apology is a respect for common sense and admiration for science, demonstrating his spirit of criticism and self-criticism. Our party and country have gone through decades of storms and still maintain strong vitality and combat effectiveness, one important reason being that we have the Marxist-Leninist weapon of criticism and self-criticism.

Premier Wen's apology gave us deep inspiration, a person possessing the spirit of criticism and self-criticism is rare and precious. I once wrote a letter to our regional managers titled "Greentree Inn's Training = Randy Pausch's Last Lecture = Expectations for Oneself" where I mentioned: "If you make a mistake in your work and someone points it out to you, don't get discouraged, don't complain, remember that it means people still care about you. Randy Pausch said 'if you're doing poorly and no one criticizes you, it means they've given up on you.' If we make a mistake and the company points it out to us, we shouldn't complain, give up, escape, or spread gossip, but instead face our mistakes with a sense of shame and gratitude, so we can improve our work with a positive attitude." Bitter medicine cures disease, and harsh words help conduct. A person committing a mistake is not terrible, what's terrible is not knowing they've made a mistake, or knowing but refusing to admit it.

Premier Wen's handwritten apology is like a mirror, warning the world that not only should we study and spread the truth, but also publicly abandon errors and dare to confront them, which is a noble behavior! From this, I associate with the current prevalence of superficiality in academia and officialdom. Although China's economy has developed rapidly, it has bred impatience and a desire for quick results among the people. This restless mindset has also spread to the academic community. The critical spirit, questioning spirit, and dedication to learning, continuous breakthroughs, and pure devotion to academics that universities should have, have become rare in many institutions of higher education, where fame and profit have become the constant pursuits. When exposed by the media, they try every method to cover up their shortcomings, which contrasts sharply with Premier Wen's rigorous scholarly style. Nowadays, the prevalence of superficiality and the lack of "pursuing true knowledge" have become widespread social problems. Hopefully, the premier's "leading by example" can set a good precedent, allowing the spirit of seeking truth and being pragmatic and rigorous in scholarship to return to campuses and society.

Premier Wen moved hundreds of millions of netizens because he dared to criticize and self-criticize; because of his "truth-seeking and meticulous" scientific spirit; because of his "self-discipline and diligence" serious attitude; because of his immense personal charm; because of his tireless efforts, dedication, people-oriented governance, and selfless contribution; because of... Here, let us say to the premier: "Our dear premier, you've worked hard!"
