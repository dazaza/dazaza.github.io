The ranking of the top 100 private enterprises in Zhejiang Province in 2009 has been determined.

●Hangzhou Wahaha Group, Wanxiang Group, and Guangsha Holdings remain firmly in the top three.

●In the financial crisis, private enterprises maintain good growth potential: business scale, sales revenue, and economic benefits show promising trends.

●37 private enterprises from Hangzhou make the list, gradually becoming the main headquarters for Zhejiang's private enterprises.

●Emerging private enterprises are showing strong momentum, with 35 newly established after 2000 making it to the list.

●Capital-intensive and technology-intensive industries are growing fastest, while labor-intensive industries are declining the most.

●The service sector is accelerating development; net profits of service enterprises among the top 100 private enterprises have increased by 3.6 times year-on-year.

●All Zhejiang top 100 private enterprises are expected to enter China's top 500 private enterprises.

Zhejiang Business Network July 1st news (reported by Yao Enyu, correspondent Shen Yan): On July 1st, the "Top 100 Private Enterprises in Zhejiang Province for 2009" was completed and released by the Zhejiang Provincial Administration for Industry and Commerce and the Zhejiang Private Enterprise Development Federation. It is understood that this year’s ranking focuses on reflecting the overall strength and development potential of enterprises. Based on continuing to optimize data collection and analysis methods, the initial single-sales sorting has been transformed into a statistical method using four comprehensive indicators - sales, net assets, tax payments, and net profit - analyzed according to different weights, better reflecting the overall strength and development potential of enterprises, more accurately reflecting their operational status. The list shows that as the leading enterprises of Zhejiang, the leading development of the top 100 private enterprises remains stable. Hangzhou Wahaha Group Co., Ltd., Wanxiang Group Corporation, and Guangsha Holding Venture Investment Co., Ltd. continue to rank in the top three of the top 100 rankings.

2009 was the time when the global financial crisis had a severe impact on Zhejiang, and how Zhejiang's private enterprises developed became a focal point of attention. Analysis of changes in the new round of top 100 private enterprises shows that Zhejiang's private economy grew against the trend in a crisis environment, stabilized and improved, showed good growth, and the characteristics and trends of transformation and upgrading became increasingly evident.

Private enterprise scale reaches new heights

The latest results of the new round of Zhejiang's top 100 private enterprise list show that Zhejiang enterprises grew against the trend in the financial crisis environment, maintaining good growth potential, with some enterprises reaching record highs in sales revenue and asset growth rates, and sales revenue and net assets showing an upward trend.

Private enterprise operating scale continues to expand: In 2009, the total assets of Zhejiang's top 100 private enterprises amounted to 284.8 billion yuan, up 110% year-on-year, with 88 enterprises having asset growth exceeding the previous year. Among them, Huasheng Construction Group Co., Ltd.'s asset growth rate reached 603.32%. Industry insiders said that cases of some enterprises achieving high-speed growth in adverse environments indicate that the adverse effects of the financial crisis in Zhejiang have been contained, and production is showing signs of stabilization and improvement.

Sales rebound significantly: In 2009, the combined sales revenue of Zhejiang's top 100 private enterprises totaled 1.0411 trillion yuan, up 72% year-on-year, with 90 enterprises having sales revenue exceeding the previous year. Hangzhou Wahaha Group Co., Ltd., Wanxiang Group Corporation, and Guangsha Holding Venture Investment Co., Ltd., ranked first, second, and third respectively in the top 100 private enterprise rankings, with sales revenue growth rates reaching 31.59%, 13.13%, and 15.03% respectively. Some enterprises achieved record-high sales revenue growth rates, with Fangyuan Construction Group Co., Ltd. reaching 118.56%. The lowest sales revenue of enterprises in the top 100 private enterprises was 2.898 billion yuan, an increase of 4.02% compared to 2.684 billion yuan in 2008.

Overall corporate economic benefits are promising: The overall economic benefits of the top 100 private enterprises show a clear trend of stabilizing and improving. In 2008, the total net profit of Zhejiang's top 100 private enterprises was 19.411 billion yuan, while in 2009, the total net profit was 52.131 billion yuan, up 168% year-on-year. Net profits of 87 enterprises increased, with 2 remaining flat.

From the perspective of Zhejiang private enterprise scale in 2009, all of Zhejiang's top 100 private enterprises are expected to enter China's top 500 private enterprises. The threshold for declaring China's top 500 private enterprises in 2009 was annual sales revenue of 2.3 billion yuan, and all of Zhejiang's top 100 private enterprises met the declaration criteria.

Transformation and upgrading become intrinsic demands

In the top 100 private enterprises in 2009, manufacturing enterprises' total sales accounted for 60% of the total, down 24 percentage points year-on-year. In the previous round of top 100 private enterprises, 14 traditional manufacturing enterprises did not make it into the new top 100 private enterprise list, including 3 metal products manufacturers, 3 machinery manufacturers, and 2 textile enterprises.

Among the 14 new entrants to the top 100 private enterprises, there were 4 construction enterprises, 3 automotive parts manufacturers, 2 real estate enterprises, 1 accommodation and catering enterprise, 1 investment and asset management enterprise, and 3 other manufacturing enterprises. Enterprises with significant rank improvements include: Tianjie Group Co., Ltd., rising from 125th last year to 51st; Huasheng Construction Group Co., Ltd., rising from 104th last year to 56th; Fangyuan Construction Group Co., Ltd., rising from 119th last year to 83rd; Lugang Group rising from 120th last year to 93rd; Hangzhou Nobel Group Co., Ltd., rising from 123rd last year to 100th.

From the perspective of the output value ratio of top 100 manufacturing enterprises, capital-intensive and technology-intensive industries are growing fastest, while labor-intensive industries are declining the most. The top three industries with the largest year-on-year increases in total output value are: chemical industry, metal smelting and rolling industry, and communication equipment, computer, and other electronic device manufacturing. The top three industries with the largest declines are: textile industry, wood processing and furniture manufacturing, and papermaking, printing, and cultural education product manufacturing. This indicates that the industrial structure of Zhejiang's private enterprises is gradually optimizing.

Manufacturing still accounts for half of the market. The industry distribution of Zhejiang's top 100 private enterprises mainly focuses on nine industries including manufacturing, construction, investment and asset management. In 2009, manufacturing accounted for half of the top 100 enterprises, with 51 enterprises, 19 in construction, 10 in investment and asset management, 8 in real estate enterprises, 7 in wholesale and retail enterprises, 2 in accommodation and catering enterprises, 1 in financial trust and management, 1 in warehousing, and 1 in other services.

The concentration level of the manufacturing industry is relatively high. The top five industries by scale in the manufacturing sector are sequentially: electrical, mechanical, and equipment manufacturing (20 enterprises, total sales 258.009 billion yuan), textile and clothing leather feather and its products industry (10 enterprises, total sales 113.791 billion yuan), chemical industry (5 enterprises, total sales 82.969 billion yuan), food manufacturing (3 enterprises, 53.523 billion yuan), and communication instrument and cultural office equipment manufacturing (5 enterprises, 42.345 billion yuan). Their proportions are: 41.06%, 18.11%, 13.20%, 8.51%, and 6.73%, respectively, with an industry concentration level as high as 87.61%. Industry insiders stated that the higher concentration level of Zhejiang's manufacturing industry maintains a relatively stable state, which will be beneficial for the continuous, stable, and healthy development of Zhejiang's manufacturing industry, thereby forming stronger competitive advantages.

High-tech industries are accelerating development. In 2009, among Zhejiang's top 100 private enterprises, there were 14 high-tech enterprises such as special material manufacturing and metal and synthetic material manufacturing, an increase of three compared to last year, with total sales reaching 116.552 billion yuan, up 62.26% year-on-year.

Industrial restructuring accelerates

In 2009, many private enterprises accelerated their own industrial structure transformation and upgrading, adjusting the proportion of industries, enhancing production technology, and continuously promoting industrial upgrading within and between industries, achieving a splendid turnaround in secondary entrepreneurship. From changes in the scope of business operations, the industrial structure continues to optimize, including extension of the industrial chain upwards and downwards, improvement in socialization and specialization division levels, enhancement of production process technology, optimization of business methods, etc., showing obvious trends towards diversified operations and inter-industry transformation and upgrading.

Zhejiang Zhongnan Construction Group Co., Ltd., boldly transitioned from the second industry property construction to becoming a leading company in the national animation industry, with products sold worldwide, achieving industrial transformation and upgrading, with sales revenue increasing by 41.57% year-on-year to reach 5.101 billion yuan, net assets increasing by 11.03 percentage points year-on-year to reach 2.496 billion yuan, and net profit increasing by 60.91% to reach 140 million yuan. In 2009, 10 enterprises had net profit growth rates exceeding 200%, among which Zhongshe Jian Gong Group shifted from simple construction to diversified operation models including market management, international project bidding, labor, and equipment export, resulting in profit growth of 26 times. After Tianjie Group Co., Ltd. transitioned from environmental pollution control dedicated equipment manufacturing to warehousing services and other secondary and tertiary industries, profit increased by more than 14 times. Fuli Da Group extended its textile and raw auxiliary materials technology both upstream and downstream, and expanded into diversified directions such as real estate development and operation, property management, industrial investment, enterprise asset management, with net profit increasing by 12 times. Xizi United Holding Group upgraded its internal industrial structure and was the first in the province to enter the large aircraft parts manufacturing sector, resulting in profit growth of 8 times. Other companies with rapid profit increases include Hailiang Group Co., Ltd., with profit growth of 6 times, and Zhejiang Haitian Construction Group Co., Ltd., with profit growth of 5 times. Companies like Zhejiang Rongsheng Holding Group Co., Ltd., Ningbo Jintian Investment Holding Co., Ltd., Xinshiji Holding Group Co., Ltd., Zhongtai Holding Group Co., Ltd., and Ningbo Cixi Import and Export Co., Ltd. saw net profit growth of more than 2 times.

Textiles remain Zhejiang's traditional dominant industry. For many enterprises within the province that do not want to leave their long-term textile bases, many have chosen to seek breakthroughs by shifting upstream and downstream along the industrial chain. Zhejiang Xiangsheng Group has advanced its production core toward the upstream raw material market of the industrial chain, vigorously developing high-value-added upstream raw material products such as viscose short fibers and high molecular polyethylene fibers. Despite the financial crisis causing losses for many textile enterprises, its output value still increased by 20%.

Hangzhou accounts for over one-third of the top 100

The geographical distribution of private enterprises initially reveals the clustering tendency of headquarters economies, particularly with Hangzhou's advantage as a hub for Zhejiang's headquarters economy becoming increasingly prominent. In the latest top 100 private enterprises, Hangzhou has 37 private enterprises, accounting for over one-third of the province. Hangzhou has the highest proportion and also the fastest growth. In 2006, only 25 private enterprises from Hangzhou made the top 100, increasing by 12 over three years, with more than half moving from elsewhere to Hangzhou. Hangzhou is gradually becoming the main base for Zhejiang's private enterprise headquarters.

The concentration of regional distribution of the top 100 further strengthens. In 2009, Zhejiang's top 100 private enterprises were mainly distributed across Hangzhou (37 enterprises, including 4 provincial enterprises), Ningbo (14 enterprises), Wenzhou (13 enterprises), and Shaoxing (20 enterprises) in four cities, with Hangzhou, Ningbo, Wenzhou, and Shaoxing accounting for 84% of the total number of provincial top 100 private enterprises, an increase of 9 percentage points from last year. Other top 100 enterprises are distributed across Jinhua (5 enterprises), Taizhou (4 enterprises), Huzhou (3 enterprises), Jiaxing (2 enterprises), Lishui (1 enterprise), and Zhoushan (1 enterprise). The concentration trend of top 100 private enterprises in Hangzhou, Ningbo, Wenzhou, and Shaoxing is gradually strengthening.

Relevant officials from the Zhejiang Provincial Administration for Industry and Commerce pointed out that the spatial structure of Zhejiang's private enterprise development is shifting towards node-based agglomeration centered around major cities, achieving co-development of industrial agglomeration and urbanization. Vigorously promoting the development of central cities is a strategic choice for our province's economic development to advance to a higher level and maintain long-term intensive growth, and also an important condition for realizing the transformation and upgrading of our province's economy.

Rising stars show strong momentum

As the leading enterprises of Zhejiang's private enterprises, the top 100 private enterprises steadily lead the way. Hangzhou Wahaha Group Co., Ltd., Wanxiang Group Corporation, and Guangsha Holding Venture Investment Co., Ltd. remained firmly ranked first, second, and third respectively, the same as in 2008. Hangzhou Wahaha Group Co., Ltd.'s annual sales revenue was 43.204 billion yuan, up 31.59% year-on-year; Wanxiang Group Corporation's sales revenue was 51.48 billion yuan, up 13.13% year-on-year; Guangsha Holding Venture Investment Co., Ltd.'s sales revenue was 50.851 billion yuan, up 15.03% year-on-year. Yaguo Group Co., Ltd., Hailiang Group Co., Ltd., Zhejiang Hengyi Group Co., Ltd., Zheng Tai Group Co., Ltd., Zhejiang Geely Holding Group Co., Ltd., Zhongtian Development Holding Group Co., Ltd., and People's Electric Group Co., Ltd. entered the top 10 of the top 100 private enterprises, with rankings basically unchanged from last year. Due to a 21.36% year-on-year decrease in sales revenue, Ningbo Jintian Investment Holding Co., Ltd., ranked eighth last year, dropped to twelfth this year, while People's Electric Group Co., Ltd., moved from thirteenth last year to tenth this year. Industry insiders pointed out that the top 10 of Zhejiang's top 100 private enterprises remain largely stable, indicating that these large private enterprises have the strength to develop stably and sustainably in complex economic environments.

Among Zhejiang's top 100 private enterprises, there are eight evergreen enterprises over 20 years old, namely Wanxiang Group Corporation (founded in 1990), Huali Group Co., Ltd. (1970), Wolong Holding Group Co., Ltd. (1984), Orient Construction Group Co., Ltd. (1989), Wu Yang Construction Group Co., Ltd. (1962), Wenzhou Zhongcheng Construction Group Co., Ltd. (1988), Huatong Electromechanical Group Co., Ltd. (1986), and Ruili Group Co., Ltd. (1988). These leading and evergreen enterprises have become benchmarks and backbone forces for Zhejiang's private economy.

Emerging private enterprises also demonstrated strong development momentum, with dynamic changes in rankings. Since 2000, newly established enterprises occupied 35 seats in Zhejiang's top 100 in 2009, accounting for more than one-third of the total, indicating the potential and development power of emerging private enterprises.

Real estate and automobile industries show significant growth

In the new top 100 private enterprises, the fastest-growing sectors were house construction and real estate, increasing from 4 enterprises in 2002 to 27 enterprises in 2009. The total sales revenue of the 10 real estate enterprises reached 146.3 billion yuan, up 66% year-on-year. Automobile sales, automobile and parts manufacturing enterprises increased from 4 in 2008 to 10 in 2009, with total net profit reaching 4.8 billion yuan, up 82% year-on-year.

The production-oriented service industry in the province is currently in a growth stage, with rapid development speed and huge potential. In this year's top 100 private enterprises, the total sales revenue of service enterprises increased from 13.2 billion yuan last year to 24.2 billion yuan, up 83% year-on-year. Net profit increased from 121 million yuan to 587 million yuan, up 3.6 times year-on-year. Industry insiders pointed out that as large manufacturing enterprises gradually transform into groups and diversify, the proportion of financial services in the top 100 is gradually increasing. Other modern production-oriented service industries such as leasing and business services, warehousing, technical research and services are also beginning to appear on the top 100 list.
