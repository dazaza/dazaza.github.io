"Interview" Man Socks: A Professional Men's Sock Delivery Service

by ybcz0519 on 2010-12-19 12:12:55

This article is simultaneously published on Mobinode. Business media please do not reprint!

Nanrenwa (Men's Socks) is a professional men’s sock delivery service aimed at those who dislike wearing socks with holes but have no time to buy new ones. After users place an order, Nanrenwa will regularly send new socks. Currently, Nanrenwa offers three types of Men’s Sock Sets: 28 yuan (3 pairs), 88 yuan (12 pairs, delivered in four installments over one year, 3 pairs each time), and 168 yuan (24 pairs, delivered in eight installments over two years, 3 pairs each time). The purpose is to relieve users from the hassle of purchasing socks regularly.

Similar Services:

Watsonxu's Perspective: The e-commerce field is a sector I personally find very promising. Despite the existence of large domestic e-commerce sites like Taobao, Taobao Mall, and JD.com where you can almost buy everything, there's still something many e-commerce websites haven't researched well or are missing—service. To help everyone understand Nanrenwa better, we conducted an exclusive interview with its founder. Below is the content of the interview with the founder of Nanrenwa (conducted via email).

1. Please give us a brief introduction about your website and team?

Strictly speaking, there isn’t a team yet. The idea for Nanrenwa came from a personal idea I had a month ago. From preparation to launch, it took only two weeks. Currently, my spouse and I are doing this part-time. I’m responsible for technology and operations, while my spouse handles the product sourcing and pre-sales and after-sales services. If any friends in Wuhan like this project and wish to join our team for entrepreneurship, we warmly welcome you.

2. What is the main target user group for the services you provide?

"Nanrenwa" means "men's socks," so first and foremost, it's for men, haha. Then, it's for lazy men—I myself am a programmer by background, and programmers are representative of lazy men. Currently, Nanrenwa’s socks are positioned as business socks, meaning we mainly target business professionals who are too lazy to buy socks. You can also consider Nanrenwa as a special gift for the man you care about.

3. E-commerce is a domain many people look favorably upon. What factors made you consider entering the e-commerce field through socks?

Men are naturally lazy, which is why most men don't enjoy shopping. Nanrenwa started from this point, uncovering a niche market within the sock industry.

I've been involved in the e-commerce industry for quite some time now. The e-commerce market has developed over the years to its current state, gradually gaining acceptance from the general public, showing explosive vitality. For small enterprises, startups, or individuals, it's difficult to compete with the large, comprehensive e-commerce giants without sufficient funds. Therefore, we chose a smaller, more niche market.

4. There should already be similar websites abroad, such as the UK's "Black Socks," which sells 10 million pairs annually. There are also similar services domestically. Compared to them, what do you think are your advantages or characteristics?

The subscription model for socks is not a new concept abroad, and there are already pioneers domestically. Although our current website design may resemble our foreign counterparts', we aim to offer a sock subscription service more suited to local conditions.

I’ve personally experienced both foreign and domestic sock services. Long shipping cycles, high prices, and insufficient personalized services are points that, from a final user perspective, I find hard to accept.

Nanrenwa's current advantages include:

1. Price: We directly cooperate with manufacturers, ensuring a weekly supply and passing the savings on to end-users. Domestic competitors' socks cost around 20 yuan per pair, whereas Nanrenwa offers the same quality for just over 7 yuan, cheaper than buying from a supermarket yourself. This doesn't mean we're engaging in a price war. Personally, I believe that only when users truly benefit can a project sustain long-term development, and users will actively spread the word.

2. Service: On the homepage of Nanrenwa, we prominently display our service commitment: unconditional refunds, no questions asked. This reflects our confidence in our products and responsibility towards customers.

3. Personalization: Currently, Nanrenwa offers only three package options, which, based on sales and user feedback, can meet most users' needs. After ordering, users can contact us at any time to modify the delivery cycle and quantity. However, this isn't enough. Our goal is to make life easier for the lazy. Nanrenwa will soon launch more personalized "DIY packages." Stay tuned.

5. Will focusing solely on the sock niche limit the future development of the website?

Yes, but we’re not afraid. The current team and resources of Nanrenwa are limited, and right now, we want to focus on doing one thing well. We believe good things will always be recognized.

6. Can you introduce the future direction of Nanrenwa?

Same as the answer above, talking about the future is all theoretical right now. The primary goal of Nanrenwa is currently to focus on socks and do them well. We prefer to follow the feelings of our users, so we've set up a public QA platform: http://nanrenwa.com/qa/. What Nanrenwa's future looks like should be decided by the users.
