Plummeting appears, seize structural opportunities

by huaxun924 on 2010-04-19 15:00:39

Today, the two city stock markets continued the downward trend from the previous weekend and both opened significantly lower. The Shanghai Composite Index opened at 3,096.78 points, while the Shenzhen Component Index opened at 12,248.02 points, down 33.52 points and 168.84 points respectively. Subsequently, under the leadership of the real estate, banking, and coal-petroleum sectors, which were heavily hit by negative news, the two city stock markets experienced continuous fluctuations and a downward trend. Currently, deeply affected by the real estate sector, the Shenzhen Component Index has fallen more than 3%, while the Shanghai Composite Index's decline has exceeded 2%, showing signs of short-term weakness.

From today's news perspective, although there were slightly positive messages such as an insider revealing on April 18th that the second batch of margin trading pilot brokers list is expected to be announced in early May, with five brokers who did not make it into the first batch directly entering the second batch, there were no significant positive news. On the contrary, there were more major negative impacts, making it a clearly bearish situation. First, over the previous weekend, due to the famous investment bank Goldman Sachs being charged by the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission for alleged securities fraud, the U.S. stock market and international commodities futures market saw sharp declines, which brought considerable adverse effects to today's domestic securities market, especially affecting the coal-petroleum and non-ferrous metals sectors among the weighted stocks. According to the trends of the three major U.S. indices, although the U.S. stock market does not yet show clear signs of a medium-term top, it has likely entered a short-term peak region, which could form a significant psychological impact on some investment institutions. Second, following the announcement on April 14th to increase the down payment ratio and interest rate by 1.1 times for second-home loans, the State Council issued a notice on April 17th requiring banks in areas where housing prices are excessively high, rising too quickly, and supply is tight to suspend issuing loans for the purchase of three or more homes. A series of new measures to control housing prices have led to new developments showing signs of price reductions, accelerating the exit of property investors. This news will continue to bring significant adjustment pressure to real estate stocks, and because of their relationship with banking, steel, and other weighted stock sectors, along with their special weight and popularity in the market, it will also have a major negative impact on the entire market.

Under the influence of major negative news, the market experienced a significant decline today. From the 15-minute K-line chart of the market, although there are no obvious signs of a bottom yet, there is potential for retesting the support near the annual line at around 3,000 points. However, after continuous declines, the probability of further sustained large declines at the current level is not high. Therefore, aggressive investors with better technical analysis skills can consider taking about 60% positions to buy low and sell high in potential hot sectors like pharmaceuticals and agriculture, focusing on short-term operations, while paying attention to possible mid-short term buying opportunities in sectors like insurance stocks.
