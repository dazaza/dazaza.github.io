She currently serves as the deputy director of the Zhuhai Wall Material Reform and Building Energy Saving Office and is a standing committee member of the Zhuhai Municipal Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference. 　Thinking back to that courage and persistence, she still feels gratified today; even a slight retreat might have caused the truth that should have come to light to be lost in the mists of history. 　　——— You Haiyan 　Today's Luoxi Bridge in Guangzhou, with its busy traffic flow, seems no different than usual. The difference is that there is no longer a need to pay tolls when crossing the Luoxi Bridge. And this monumental change is inseparable from the efforts of the current You Haiyan. This outwardly elegant and somewhat delicate female "Don Quixote," along with many others who advocated for the people, advanced hand in hand through eight years of questioning, finally putting an end to the 17-year toll collection history of the Luoxi Bridge. Time passes quickly, and now serving as the deputy director of the Zhuhai Wall Material Reform and Building Energy Saving Office and a standing committee member of the municipal CPPCC, she still rationally focuses on public sentiment and her spirit of "advocating for the people" remains unchanged. 　Being entrusted with the task of investigating the Luoxi Bridge 　"Do you have any good ideas for popularizing building energy-saving knowledge?" Upon arriving at her office, this "resolute rose" who once became famous across southern Guangdong with the vow "if I don't stand up for the people, then please impeach me", was still preoccupied with how to promote energy-saving work in construction. When talking about the experience of questioning the toll collection of the Luoxi Bridge, she was very humble, downplaying her role with the words "all the results are due to the collective wisdom and efforts". 　The issue of toll collection on the Luoxi Bridge has been a focus of the provincial and municipal two sessions in Guangdong Province for many years. During the period of 1999 and 2000, representative Wang Zecu of the Guangzhou Municipal People's Congress questioned the toll collection on the Luoxi Bridge multiple times and investigated the flow of bridge funds together with the provincial audit bureau, but they were never able to contact the project's contractor, resulting in no outcome. In 2000, then-provincial representative You Haiyan was "entrusted" by the Standing Committee of the Provincial People's Congress to conduct the investigation. The reason was that "she not only has extensive overseas background but also possesses independent and unconventional qualities and the ability to investigate and analyze sensitive issues." Wang Zecu also stated in an interview with reporters that he chose You Haiyan because she had excellent insight. 　Gaining confidence by personally meeting with Ho Ying Dong 　Starting in 2000, You Haiyan began investigating the toll collection issue on the Luoxi Bridge with full confidence. However, before the investigation could delve deeper, she encountered some resistance. She said that once, while conducting research in Panyu with credentials, someone played games with her, and when pressed, even said: "Why don't you go manage some cat and dog issues?" She responded: "Even 100 Don Quixotes can change society. For such large projects involving people's interests and government image, representatives of the people cannot turn a blind eye." 　"Thinking back to that courage and persistence, I still feel gratified today; even a slight retreat might have caused the truth that should have come to light to be lost in the mists of history." You Haiyan still sighs over many things today. 　With setbacks at the start, You Haiyan changed her approach and personally sought out Ho Ying Dong. In 2000, after much difficulty, she finally met Mr. Ho Ying Dong in Guangzhou. Talking about the misunderstanding of donation behavior being regarded as investment behavior by his fellow townspeople, Ho Ying Dong was very indignant. 　"Meeting with Ho Ying Dong strengthened my determination to pursue the matter to the end." You Haiyan said. In April 2001, during the provincial session of the Two Sessions, she submitted an urgent proposal to the Standing Committee of the Provincial People's Congress, asking the provincial government to investigate the toll collection on the Luoxi Bridge, and questioned the toll collection issues on the Luoxi Bridge in nine questions with a rapid-fire tone. Her clear thinking and tight logic were admirable. It was precisely under the repeated questioning of her and representatives like Wang Zecu that starting from midnight on July 1, 2005, the history of toll collection on the Luoxi Bridge came to an end. 　She continues to advocate for the people today 　In 2003, You Haiyan ended her term as a provincial representative, and in 2004, she was re-elected as a standing committee member of the Zhuhai Municipal Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, shifting her focus to Zhuhai. What has changed is her way of participating in public policy, but what hasn't changed is her commitment to "advocating for the people." 　In 2004, she proposed policies such as "establishing a special fund similar to science and technology three-item funds, providing appropriate subsidies to enterprises actively developing a circular economy." In 2005, she conducted her own survey in Yongxing County, Hunan Province, a "poor county," and subsequently proposed the famous proposal "Establishing an Indicator System for an Energy-Saving Society and Improving Relevant Laws and Regulations." In March 2009 at the Zhuhai Two Sessions, she often replied to citizens' emails until one or two o'clock in the morning, eventually collapsing. At the following Two Sessions, she again made headlines by questioning the supervision of housing provident funds, calling it "robbing the poor to help the rich," winning applause. 　Although she has been criticized by some as a "Don Quixote" and "out of touch"; although hurried people only occasionally remember this "female Don Quixote," her persistence in believing that "even 100 Don Quixotes can change society" will surely become a driving force for social progress. 　Written by: Southern Metropolis Daily reporter Hu Mingfa
