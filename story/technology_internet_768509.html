TIOBE Programming Language Rankings for November: C#, Transact-SQL, and VB.NET Show Steady Growth

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 19:04:49

The TIOBE Programming Language rankings for November 2013 have been released, and the top three languages remain unchanged: C, Java, and Objective-C.

Interestingly, among the top 20 programming languages, there are three languages with notable upward trends that were developed by Microsoft, indicating their growing popularity. These languages are C#, Transact-SQL, and Visual Basic.NET. Although it might just be a coincidence to see such a trend in the same month, it is worth noting that Windows Mobile's market share has indeed increased compared to Android and iOS mobile operating systems.

Another point of interest is the upward trend of JavaScript, which is unsurprising given its status as one of the most popular scripting languages on current websites.

Additionally, it’s worth mentioning that Google's Go language (which is now four years old) has successfully climbed from being ranked outside the top 50 last month to rank 49 this month. Whether Go will continue to rise remains to be seen.

The TIOBE Programming Community Index is an indicator of the popularity of programming languages, updated monthly. Rankings are based on the number of experienced programmers, courses, and third-party vendors available on the internet. Rankings are calculated using famous search engines such as Google, MSN, Yahoo!, Wikipedia, YouTube, and Baidu, among others. Please note that this ranking only reflects the popularity of a programming language and does not indicate whether a language is good or how much code is written in it.

This ranking can be used to assess whether your programming skills are up-to-date and can serve as a reference when choosing a language for developing new systems. For more detailed definitions of the rankings, please refer to the official site (in English).
