The low success rate of startups tells us that we should start with a small cost.

by yangliu435sh on 2012-02-18 15:43:32

"We surveyed 2,000 college students and 1,000 graduates who are entrepreneurs in Chengdu," a relevant official from the Youth League Committee's Entrepreneurship Office said. The survey found that 41% of entrepreneurship among college students was survival-oriented, aimed at sustaining and making a living, while 59% was opportunity-driven, aimed at self-actualization, enhancement, and exploration. College student entrepreneurship mainly concentrated in the service and IT industries, with 32.9% choosing the service industry, 19.5% choosing IT, and 14% opting for biopharmaceuticals. "The service industry has relatively low professional technical requirements, entry barriers, and entrepreneurial risks, while the IT industry has a relatively mature industrial development environment, hence many college students choose to start businesses in these two industries." Profitability was not ideal. In Chengdu, 10.99% of college student entrepreneurial enterprises were in the initial operation phase, with a success rate of 5.13%, higher than the national average for graduates. "Entrepreneurial success is defined as having obtained at least one round of external financing, currently operating smoothly with slight profitability, and being able to further develop in the future," the relevant official said. For Chengdu graduates, 32.7% of their entrepreneurial ventures had no profits yet, with 48.4% earning less than 0.3 million yuan per month, 16.1% earning between 0.3 to 1 million yuan, and only 0.8% earning more than 5 million yuan. Among current students, 63.2% reported no profits, with 24% earning less than 500 yuan. This indicates that the profitability of Chengdu college student entrepreneurship was not very ideal.

The report by the Youth League Committee of Chengdu marked the city's first analysis of college student entrepreneurship. According to statistics, there are approximately 35,000 to 40,000 college graduates starting businesses in Chengdu, with a success rate exceeding the national average but with poor profitability. To address this, the Youth League Committee suggested establishing a failure rescue system for college student entrepreneurs and developing entrepreneurial insurance products.

Key words: Strategy – Compiling an entrepreneurial industry directory. During the survey, it was found that college student entrepreneurial projects showed some homogeneity, such as a preference for e-commerce. To guide project selection, the Youth League Committee proposed guiding entrepreneurial enterprises into segments of the value chain where gaps exist. "For example, the animation industry is one where the value chain is loose or even broken, so it would be timely to guide college students into underdeveloped areas of research, production, and supply-sales in this industry."

The Youth League Committee recommended compiling a "Chengdu Entrepreneurial Industry Guidance Directory" based on Chengdu's industrial priorities and the tendencies of college student entrepreneurship. Currently, it could encourage college students to start businesses in digital entertainment, IT, cultural creativity, modern services, and other industries. At the same time, compile a Chengdu Entrepreneurial Investment Field Guidance Directory to promote private capital entering key entrepreneurial sectors; attempt to establish the country's first regional entrepreneurial integrity system to curb improper competition among college student entrepreneurs.

Chen Mao (pronounced "Meng"), a graduate of Communication University of China in 2007, majored in multimedia/journalism, specializing in film post-production, is a young entrepreneur with great aspirations. Despite his young age, he has rich experience, having worked in a model agency and television station designing and editing films. Even before graduation, he had the idea of entrepreneurship, aiming for long-term personal achievement and brand creation. He interned at Beijing Television during his studies.

Expert: Ding Junjie, Vice President of Communication University of China, Doctoral Supervisor, Chairman of the Expert Committee of the Research Center for Cultural and Creative Industry Development at Communication University of China.

Cultural and creative industries belong to emerging industries. According to Principal Ding, cultural and creative industries were proposed under the guidance of China’s sustainable development concept. They involve creating wealth and increasing employment opportunities through intellectual property rights based on individual creativity, talent, and professional technology. Typically, they include advertising, art, antiques, comics, design, film and television, software, music, performing arts, publishing, and information technology services; Principal Ding emphasized: "Cultural and creative industries do not depend on the scale of investment but rather on creativity, personality, and market concepts. In the context of global material and energy shortages, it is necessary to vigorously develop the creative economy. Developing cultural and creative industries is a good way to increase the total national economic output. It also helps build a resource-conserving and environmentally-friendly harmonious society in our country."

Currently facing good development opportunities. According to Principal Ding, Beijing's cultural and creative industries face favorable development opportunities now. As the capital city, Beijing bears the social responsibility of cultural dissemination and the growing domestic and international market demand for excellent Chinese culture, providing strong impetus for the development of cultural and creative industries. Moreover, relevant national departments have introduced a series of preferential policies to encourage the development of cultural and creative industries. "The current industrial situation has changed significantly. People interested in starting businesses in the field of cultural and creative industries will no longer rely on traditional media institutions but mainly on their own ideas and fresh creativity, combined with their technical expertise, to comprehensively use the Internet stage to promote their works and accept market testing," Principal Ding added. He also pointed out that the space for entrepreneurship in cultural and creative aspects is vast, particularly emphasizing the need to make full use of the Internet platform for creation and 'production' of unique things.

Policies safeguarding entrepreneurship. Cultural and creative industries have become a highly focused and supported industry by both the government and society in our country. In recent years, both central and local governments have introduced many support policies and reward conditions for cultural and creative industries. One important clause in the "Several Policies for Promoting the Development of Cultural and Creative Industries in Beijing" encourages enterprises and individual creative personnel to utilize all business models and organizational forms that conform to the production rules of cultural and creative industries to develop cultural and creative industries. Additionally, it encourages and supports the creative R&D and product exports of cultural and creative enterprises, exempting them from income tax and business tax. Income earned by units and individuals in Beijing engaged in technology transfer, technology development, and related technology consultation and technical services in the cultural and creative industries is exempt from business tax. The policy also clearly states that during the "Eleventh Five-Year Plan" period, methods for protecting and promoting intellectual property rights in cultural and creative industries will be studied and formulated; a copyright resource information center and an international copyright trading center will be established; and the Beijing Cultural and Creative Industry Promotion Center will be founded. For patent application fees of cultural and creative enterprises, support can be provided from municipal patent application funding.

Suitable groups for entrepreneurship:

1. Art-related university students. Graduates majoring in fields related to cultural and creative industries have ideas, creativity, and knowledge, easily accepting new things, forming the main force of the future cultural and creative market.

2. Painters, artists. Beijing, as the capital city, leads fashion and art trends, gathering many painters, artists, and art enthusiasts, forming the backbone of the current cultural and creative market.

3. Cultural brokers. With the prosperity of China's cultural and creative market, various cultural brokerage activities have become active. In the coming years, especially around 2008, cultural companies specializing in various performances, film and television production, and program planning will emerge in large numbers, making the profession of cultural broker widely recognized by society.

Preparations before entrepreneurship:

1. Funding. Entrepreneurs need to prepare corresponding funds for their chosen development areas. Generally speaking, engaging in music composition, anime design, and software development requires purchasing specialized equipment, which are high-tech scientific achievements requiring maintenance and repair, thus necessitating certain funds.

2. Professional knowledge. Possessing certain professional knowledge oneself is essential, not necessarily comprehensive but definitely "specialized," excelling in a particular area, such as in fashion design or anime production.

3. Innovation awareness. Cultural and creative industries themselves are emerging industries that "start" with knowledge, ideas, and fresh creativity, requiring entrepreneurs to have forward-looking vision and innovative ideas consistent with the laws of development.

Expert advice:

Principal Ding suggests that people preparing to start businesses in the field of cultural and creative industries should pay attention to some details:

1. Fully utilize the Internet platform. When talking about using the Internet platform, Principal Ding specifically mentioned a female student from their school's animation department who created a series of situational comics, assigning various actions and expressions to these comics, transforming them into a rabbit named "Tu斯基" with varied expressions.

Have you noticed? "Tu斯基" is the expression we often use when chatting on QQ. If you can also use your skills to create another "斯基" and put it on the Internet, you will also gain significant benefits.

2. Protect copyrights, insist on originality. While fully utilizing the Internet platform, it is crucial to pay attention to copyright issues and avoid parody. Principal Ding introduced that with the rapid development of the Internet in modern society, the Internet has extended to every corner of the world, and copyright issues involved in Internet dissemination have attracted widespread attention. The scope of copyright protection has gradually expanded to the Internet because copyright protection directly relates to the vital interests of relevant right holders, disseminators, and netizens. Therefore, it is necessary to insist on originality.

3. National dignity issues. Our country's cultural resources are extremely rich. When engaging in cultural and creative activities, it is essential to respect our traditional culture, uphold national and ethnic dignity, and absolutely not act arbitrarily or do anything detrimental to the national image.

When culture combines with economy and technology to form creativity, the future commercial world will exude a cultural flavor. Cultural and creative industries are becoming one of the most attractive terms. The foundation for developing cultural and creative industries in our country is currently very good, but it is still in its infancy, with a promising future. The industries included in cultural and creative industries are very broad. Most young entrepreneurs are at the early stages of entrepreneurship and should choose industries based on their professional strengths. Regarding specific projects, they should choose those with relatively smaller investment amounts for operation.

Limited funds and experience affect the progress of entrepreneurship.

Chen Mao has now co-founded his own studio with two classmates, mainly producing promotional videos for government agencies and enterprises. Due to studying a media-related major during his schooling and gaining some work experience beforehand, the studio continuously receives some work. However, during the interview process, Chen Mao expressed to the reporter that compared to large film and television production companies, their biggest difficulty was insufficient funds and lack of experience.

Chen Mao carefully analyzed the reasons: they were all newly graduated students, constrained by limited funds and experience. The studio's promotional efforts were relatively small, with few staff members, restricting the further growth and expansion of their studio.

Entrepreneurial insight: maintain a peaceful mindset and be patient.

Chen Mao emphasized that accumulating experience during the entrepreneurial process was quite important: "Since we are all young and just entering society, we don't consider matters thoroughly enough. Therefore, in the early stages of entrepreneurship, we often hit walls. I remember once our studio received an enterprise promotional video. We worked overnight to complete the plan and sent it to the client, but then there was no news. Later, we learned that the client gave our plan to another company. Coincidentally, since the head of that company happened to be my friend, he called me after seeing my name on the plan, and that's how I found out we had been duped."

"Another time, I received a promotional video. Due to insufficient experience during the initial negotiations, many unexpected factors were not fully considered, resulting in us completing the video according to the client's requirements, but then the client raised even higher demands. We had to make multiple revisions, causing a significant increase in costs, losing a lot of money, time, and manpower."

Now, Chen Mao feels deeply, "I am currently in the process of accumulation. My older brother once told me that one should do appropriate things at different ages, not act impulsively, and maintain a peaceful mindset. Actually, entrepreneurship itself is a long process, requiring timing, including favorable conditions, geographical advantages, and harmony among people, needing more accumulated experience."
