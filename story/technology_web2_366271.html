A media platform that uses Web2.0 thinking to create a Web1.0 model

by ybcz0519 on 2010-09-27 10:21:25

This article was first published in the September issue of Star Observation: Building a Web1.0 mode media platform with Web2.0 thinking - Looking at Demand Media's Innovation. Demand Media, a company that hasn't been much noticed by domestic industries before, is now going to IPO, raising $125 million as a new form of social media platform. What are its unique characteristics that make it stand out? Can its model be replicated in the domestic market?

A new media following the route of traditional media

In the Web2.0 era, everyone has become accustomed to the UGC (User Generated Content) model, where users generate and create content. However, due to the arrival of the Web2.0 era and the emergence of personal blogs, the amount of content on the internet has greatly increased. The increase in content also means that the quality of the content is no longer guaranteed. In comparison, the traditional 1.0 editorial model shows its advantages in this aspect. Combining the editorial model with the UGC model is an approach that Demand Media tries. On one hand, it allows the public to participate in writing through its own writing platform, while on the other hand, it ensures the quality of the content through full-time editors who review the content. Authors whose content passes the review will receive a certain amount of remuneration. At the same time, the editor + UGC model is also a way to mass-produce content while ensuring the quality of the content to a certain extent. One can say that Demand Media is a new media following the route of traditional media!

A sound content generation system

A sound content generation mechanism includes both a sound content production mechanism and a search engine-based topic selection. Demand Media, as its name suggests, is a media based on demand, and this demand is met through the selection of popular topics. Another aspect is the crowdsourced writing platform, which recruits a group of writers, editors, and directors to ensure the quality of user-generated content. By allowing the public to write and editors to screen, a bridge between long-tail content and premium content may be built. Furthermore, a series of vertical content websites categorize these premium contents, generating significant traffic and thus earning large advertising fees. Additionally, Demand Media owns a complete supporting system from domain names to website building, enabling it to quickly establish a batch of vertical content sites based on the content.

Strong content operation capabilities

All websites lack premium content, so a content generation platform with quality assurance could possibly operate the content on other platforms for profit. Currently, many premium contents of Demand Media are delivered to websites like YouTube and AOL that need premium content. It is essentially operating content copyrights, building a relatively complete content operation platform from content construction through its own system to copyright operation based on content. Demand Media is a content operation platform, relying on its content generation mechanism and content operation system to form a sound and virtuous cycle system for content from production to operation. Actually, Qidian domestically is a website very similar to Demand Media's structure, and Qidian's operation based on copyrights will have a lot of imaginable space. Of course, whether Demand Media's model can succeed in other fields is a topic worth discussing.
