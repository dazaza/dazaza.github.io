Cherish life

That summer, she, Xiao Si, 16 years old, had just graduated from junior high school. Due to her disappointment in the high school entrance exam and her parents' lack of understanding, she began to give up on herself, started to live a dissolute life, became tired of living, and became decadent. She spent all day with some kids outside. With them, she learned to smoke, drink, fight, take drugs, and develop all kinds of bad habits. Even her originally black long hair also changed drastically, becoming uneven and colorful; she got piercings on her nose and mouth. In her view, there was nothing left in this world worth her attachment, and no one would miss her anymore. Thus, she continued to deteriorate even more.

One night in July, when she and a few of her so-called good buddies were staggering out of a bar drunk, they met their class president, Xiao Yue, the person she loved. Xiao Yue recognized her from afar - the girl holding a wine bottle, dressed strangely, walking unsteadily and unable to stand firmly was her. Seeing her with a group of hoodlums, Xiao Yue was very worried about her, felt heartbroken, and blamed herself. He wanted to try to bring her back on track and let her return to her original life. He quickly walked up to Xiao Si and softly said: "Xiao Si, you're drunk. Do you need me to send you home?" Laughing, Xiao Si coldly replied: "Who are you? What right do you have to interfere with your elder sister! Little boy, go play somewhere else!" Afterwards, those hoodlums around her also joined in and drove him away, not allowing him to approach Xiao Si. However, he persisted and said: "Xiao Si, you said you were afraid of the dark. Let me send you home!" For some reason, upon hearing this sentence, Xiao Si actually agreed.

They walked quietly on a peaceful road, without any passersby, only a few weak beams of light from other people's rooms piercing through the pitch-black night. It was very quiet, very quiet. The whole way, neither of them spoke first. The boy simply followed silently behind the girl, looking at her haggard, weary back, feeling very sorry for her. The road wasn't long, but it seemed like they walked for a very long time. When the girl finally arrived home, as she was about to enter, the boy finally spoke up. He said to the girl: "Xiao Si, can you please stop behaving like this? Even if it's for me, don't continue down this path, okay?" The girl remained silent for a while before laughing and answering: "Do you think who you are? For you? That's laughable! Then who is for me? Don't we have anything to do with each other, as you said?"

This happened a month ago, on June 1st, which was the girl's birthday. The girl was very happy because she received many gifts that day, making her feel very satisfied and giving her 16th birthday significant meaning. But she still felt something was missing because she had fallen in love with their class president, Xiao Yue. However, Xiao Yue seemed indifferent to all girls, leaving her uncertain about his feelings towards her. She hoped Xiao Yue liked her too.

She really wanted to know how Xiao Yue felt about her, so after school, she slipped him a note that read: "Xiao Yue, come to the playground after evening study hall. I have something to tell you, see you then!" Xiao Yue went to the playground as agreed, but when Xiao Si confessed her feelings, Xiao Yue rejected her and said some very harsh words: "You say you like me, but tell me, what exactly do you like about me? What kind of relationship do we have?"

These words were an unexpected response by Xiao Si, and she was dumbfounded, unsure of how to answer, choosing instead to remain silent. Xiao Yue continued: "Alright, you don't know, right? I'll tell you. Besides being classmates, there's nothing else between us. We have no relationship." This sentence completely shattered Xiao Si's heart. Crying, she silently left the playground alone. Her departing figure looked so lonely and sorrowful in the darkness, yet she didn't know that after she left, Xiao Yue also cried.

After crying, Xiao Yue prepared to leave and head home, but in the dark corner of the playground under the pitch-black night sky, he heard a girl crying. He knew that the girl hiding in the corner crying was Xiao Si. He walked over and said to her: "Are you alright? It's not safe for a girl to be out so late. Come on, let me send you home!" Xiao Si didn't speak, but that night, he still sent her home. The scene that night was the same as tonight, and the silence between them on the way was the same. On the way, Xiao Si only said one sentence: "Do you know? I'm really afraid of the dark, I wish... I could..."

Before she finished her sentence, Xiao Si cried again. Xiao Yue understood her meaning - she wished he could always send her home. Xiao Yue deeply regretted saying such harsh words to her, regretted saying those insincere words. In fact, he also loved Xiao Si all along, never changing his feelings. He said those harsh words hoping that Xiao Si would focus all her attention on studying, as the high school entrance exam was approaching, and he hoped Xiao Si could get into a good school.

However, contrary to his expectations, from that day on, Xiao Si never focused on her studies again. During class, she always spaced out. During this period, Xiao Yue wanted to explain to Xiao Si, but whenever Xiao Si saw Xiao Yue, she would sneak away like a rat seeing a cat, not giving him a chance to explain or even speak. Later, the scene described at the beginning of the story occurred - Xiao Si failed the high school entrance exam and began her decadent life.

That night, he couldn't sleep, staying awake all night. He thought a lot. He loved her and didn't want to see the person he loved continue to live such a decadent life. He was thinking of a way to bring Xiao Si back to her original life. Alone, he stared at the crescent moon in the pitch-black night sky, not seeing its glow but only feeling its coldness.

The next morning, Xiao Yue was already waiting at the crossroads that Xiao Si passed every day, holding a bouquet of roses. After waiting for a long time, the person he loved finally came. Blushing, Xiao Yue walked up to her, handed her the roses, and confessed his feelings. Xiao Si couldn't believe that what the person in front of her was saying was true. In her eyes, his confession of liking her was only because she was now hanging out with a group of hoodlums and living decadently, and he was just sympathizing with her, pitying her.

She didn't want anyone's sympathy, so she threw the bright red roses to the ground and walked away, leaving only him standing lonely at the crossroads surrounded by passing vehicles. He also experienced the taste of rejection, which was truly unpleasant, making his heart ache unbearably. He began to understand her pain, so he didn't intend to give up. Instead, he quietly told himself: "I need to keep trying."

Every day since then, he would send her roses, following her closely every single day. When she went to the bar to drink, he would drink for her. When she smoked, he would snatch the cigarette from her hand and throw it away. When she fought, he would silently watch from the side, knowing he couldn't persuade her. But if anyone tried to hurt her, he would immediately rush forward to protect her, shielding her with his body so she wouldn't get hurt. Wherever she went and whatever she did, he was always there. Days passed like this, unchanged.

One day in August, the girl went out to fight again, and the boy still followed her closely. This fight was different from usual. In the past, when they went out to fight, only a few people carried knives. But this time, everyone had a knife, including him. The blade was very sharp and heavy, but his heart was heavier. He tried to stop the girl from going, but she wouldn't listen.

The fighting began. The boy stood helplessly in the corner, extremely scared because this time was really different from before. This time, they were risking their lives. Everyone was armed with real weapons, and soon blood began to stain the ground. Many people were injured, with blood streaming from their wrists, faces, foreheads, arms, abdomens, and legs. But they refused to stop the war, still fighting fiercely, determined to either kill or be killed. The girl was also injured, her wrist dripping with fresh red blood, yet she continued to fight alongside the hoodlums. He couldn't bear to see it. Having never fought before, he picked up the heavy knife in his hand and rushed to the girl's side to help her fight. Later, he noticed someone behind the girl preparing to attack her. The knife gleamed with a white light. He tried to shout to warn her to look behind her, but she didn't hear him. With no other choice, as the knife approached her, he quickly pushed her aside. The girl fell to the ground, injuring her arm and bleeding. But when she got up and turned around to scold the boy, she was speechless, stunned by the sight in front of her. The boy lay in a pool of blood, a red knife piercing his chest, with blood still dripping from the tip of the blade. The girl screamed "Help!" hysterically, but everyone ran away upon seeing the situation.

The girl cried again, sobbing uncontrollably. The boy weakly raised his right hand to wipe away the tears on the girl's face and said to her: "Xiao Si, please don't cry, okay? I truly love you, I've always loved you. My love for you has never changed. If I'm not around anymore, take care of yourself. Every day, remember that everyone loves you. Don't cry, I like seeing you smile..."

Before he could finish speaking, Xiao Yue passed away. The girl didn't cry; she kept smiling, kept smiling. Because the boy said he liked her smile.

On the day of the boy's funeral, the girl didn't attend. She dressed herself beautifully and went to the crossroads where he first gave her flowers. Facing the flowing traffic, she said a long passage: "Dear, I've come. I'm used to having you walk behind me. Without you, my days are unhappy, and I'm very sad. This time, I won't let you walk behind me anymore because this time, we will walk hand in hand, shoulder to shoulder. I've come, wait for me."

When people found the girl, she was already lying at the crossroads, covered in blood like the boy when he died. But the corners of her mouth were always upturned; she kept smiling. She left smiling because the boy said he liked her smile, so she wanted to find him smiling.

Why does love always end so tragically? Why do lovers always part ways? That's because when we love, we don't know how to cherish. If you love him (her), let him (her) know. If you love him (her), be nice to him (her). If you love him (her), don't hurt him (her). Not every story has a perfect ending, but every story can be made beautiful. Love your him (her) well, tell him (her) you love him (her), make sure he (her) knows. Cherish what you have now and make your love more beautiful.
