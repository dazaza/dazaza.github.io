Famous Prostitute of the Song Dynasty, Li Shishi: Hiding Bangyan Under the Bed, Composing Poems to Joke with Emperor Huizong

by jacklewman on 2008-02-24 12:05:02

The famous courtesan of the Song Dynasty, Li Shishi, caused countless heroes to vie for her favor. Not only was Emperor Huizong of Song one of her patrons, even Song Jiang, the leader of the 108 heroes of Mount Liang, risked his life to secretly enter Bianjing for a rendezvous with her. The number of ministers and literati who were captivated by her was countless, making her a renowned figure of her time.

Famous at thirteen, the emperor constantly on her mind

Li Shishi was originally the daughter of Wang Yin, who operated a dye shop in Bianjing city. Her mother passed away early, and she was raised by her father, who fed her gruel instead of milk. When Li Shishi was four years old, her father was imprisoned for a crime and later died in prison from illness. She was then raised by her neighbors, growing up to be a beauty with an understanding nature. Madame Li, who ran a brothel, adopted her, hired teachers to educate her, and trained her in singing and dancing. At the age of thirteen, she began to receive guests as a young courtesan, soon becoming famous throughout Bianjing.

High-ranking officials, literati, princes, and people from all walks of life considered it an honor to visit her door. As Li Shishi's fame grew, it became increasingly difficult for ordinary people to meet her.
