Please watch while holding the wall - A cursed Warcraft account

by nuannuanleng on 2009-11-09 22:33:58

When creating a character and reading the user manual, I suddenly remembered an article written by someone that said "Dear user, I am your father", so I lamented about social injustice and angrily typed in the name "Your Father" for my Draenei Hunter. From that moment on, this account was cursed. From level 1 to 70, almost no one grouped with me, because no one wanted to team up with "Your Father". When I died, some plugins would pop up on the screen saying "Your Father has died"; when I dealt the final blow, it would pop up again: XXX has died, killed by Your Father!

Thus, a great yet cursed character name was born: "Your Father"...

Of course, this kind of entertainment spirit is not unique to me, it's purely coincidental, stay calm, stay calm.

And there is more than one "Your Father"...

Once, I went to the Internet cafe to run an instance with a friend's alt: My friend's computer kept ringing nonstop, with constant notifications popping up on the screen: "Your Father is in critical condition, heal quickly." Luckily, hunters are inherently a solitary profession, needing only their pet's company. To show repentance, I named my pet: "I Am Not Your Father."

But I got killed even more severely in the wilds. Later, I realized that in the wild, a gorilla would charge at you, and "I Am Not Your Father." Just as I was puzzled, I saw a Draenei uncle far away with "Your Father" above his head all the way to level 70. Almost no one wanted to add me as a friend, and no guild wanted to recruit me, because no one wanted to frequently see the notification "Your Father has logged on."

"Your Father" quietly asked: Does KLZ still need people?

You quietly replied to "Your Father": Roll!

And once grouped into a party, I would get very excited, "Hello everyone, I'm very glad to group with you all." "Hello everyone, I'm Your Father, if you find this name hard to call, you can affectionately abbreviate it as Dad." Luckily, German hunters are not common, so in TS and UT, when calling for a hunter to pull monsters, it was clear, otherwise it would be awkward.

Later, I realized that being without a guild definitely wouldn't work, so I talked to the guild leader with my main character:

Main: Can you add a friend of mine to the guild?

Leader: Sure, what's his name?

Main: Your Father

Leader: What?

Main: Call you father

Leader: Roll!
