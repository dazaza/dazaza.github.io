Wei Li, also known as Binbin Li, stated on Weibo that he will cooperate with QQ products and clarified the rumors about Tencent's investment.

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 17:15:24

On July 25, news broke that Lei Wanqiang, Vice President of Xiaomi Inc., posted on Weibo at noon today that next week they will announce a product collaboration with QQ, while denying recent rumors about Tencent's potential shareholding in Xiaomi.

In the Weibo post, Lei Wanqiang stated, "We have a cooperation with QQ, which will be announced next week. However, it is limited to products, so as for the rest, there's no need for further speculation." After this post, Lei Jun reposted and commented, "There are too many rumors about Xiaomi."

Recently, it was reported by the media that Xiaomi would soon finalize its latest round of financing, led by Russian venture capital firm DST, with an amount exceeding $2 billion USD, but in reality, the actual investor behind this financing round was Tencent. Additionally, it was revealed that Lei Wanqiang had recently traveled secretly to Shenzhen to meet with Pony Ma.

In response to these rumors, Lei Wanqiang's Weibo post seems to provide indirect answers, while also sparking netizens' speculations on the content of this cooperation. Speculation includes possible collaborations between Mi Talk and WeChat, Xiaomi launching custom phones with integrated Tencent products, and Tencent releasing a special QQ Xiaomi edition client.

In regard to this, some industry insiders analyzed that Xiaomi’s current businesses mainly include Xiaomi smartphones, Mi Talk, MIUI, and Xiaomi boxes. Therefore, this product collaboration between Xiaomi and QQ would likely involve cooperation between Tencent and Xiaomi in these areas. Recently, Alibaba partnered with Wasu to launch the Wasu Rainbow TV box, so it cannot be ruled out that Tencent and Xiaomi may collaborate on TV set-top box products.
