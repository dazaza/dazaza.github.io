Introduction:

Formed in Sydney, Australia in 1974, the heavy metal powerhouse AC/DC successfully launched from Australia and conquered the American rock scene. The band members, wearing school uniform shorts, performed hard rock music which cemented their place in fans' hearts. With their explosive energy, their music primarily fused blues, rock, and contemporary pop into becoming one of the representatives of hard rock. This BD captures AC/DC's live concert performance on August 17, 1991, in Donington.

Band Profile:

AC/DC: Perhaps what distinguishes AC/DC from all other hard rock bands is the fact that they care about nothing other than playing exciting and thrilling rock music. Hard rock is indeed a strange industry; a band that has made the heaviest THRASH METAL and never follows conventions can suddenly release an album filled with short, sweet, polished songs ready for radio airplay. Greed and arrogance often drive musicians into this peculiar industry. Many bands aim to showcase the individual talents of a guitar virtuoso, a vocal prodigy, or a master songwriter. Of course, some bands also try to express intelligence, knowledge, and even philosophical reflections through their songs.

However, AC/DC is an exception to all of that. They don't care about any of those things mentioned above; they simply play the most straightforward rock music. Their songs are pure, deeply impactful rock anthems that are indispensable at any party or sporting event. Despite limiting themselves to the purest form of drum, bass, and guitar rock, their music still exudes a unique charm, solid rhythm, cool lead guitar riffs, and the singer’s chainsaw-like voice delivering ordinary melodies.

Even though AC/DC's songs are often covered by amateur bands, no band can match the incredible feel AC/DC brings to their live performances, nor can anyone replicate their uncompromising attitude. AC/DC's original lead vocalist, the legendary Bon Scott, once proudly said a famous quote: "Don't mention the bands around us... None of them amount to anything according to their moms." In reality, AC/DC truly possesses a unique look and vibe, especially Angus Young, who makes his skills shine on stage with his signature short-legged school uniform and crazy one-legged jump. His noisy riffs and screeching solos are exactly the kind of sounds high school teachers wouldn't want students to hear.

In the mid-70s, after Angus and Malcolm formed the band, it was Bon Scott who started turning up the volume and invented that unprecedentedly harsh sound in rock history. Unfortunately, in 1980, Scott passed away due to alcohol poisoning, putting the band in a life-or-death crisis. The band recruited a new lead singer, Brian Johnson, and their first album with him, "Back in Black," featured a simple all-black cover as a tribute to Scott. Unexpectedly, this album became a massive success and ultimately one of the greatest classic albums in rock history. It appears on every magazine's list of the top 100 best rock albums ever, without fail. Brian Johnson continued Scott's mission, continuing to make simple and direct rock music with the Young brothers until the late 90s.

The 1997 album "Bonfire" was undoubtedly a feast for the loyal fans of AC/DC who had followed them for over two decades. The album includes unreleased tracks, B-sides, and even early live recordings from their Sydney days. These are the sounds fans wanted to hear the most and serve as the band's best tribute to Scott's unforgettable performances.

For a band with countless classic tracks like "Back in Black," "You Shook Me All Night Long," "Big Balls," "For Those About to Rock (We Salute You)," "Money Talks," and albums such as "High Voltage," "Back in Black," "Powerage," "Who Made Who," among nearly 20 fantastic albums, AC/DC proves the best fact: a good band doesn’t need to change itself or conform to trends to withstand the test of time. Let the record industry go to hell.
