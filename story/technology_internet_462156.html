The plan has not been released yet.

by njubaxgk on 2011-07-13 16:44:31

In the past month, the sharp rise in coal prices at the Qinhuangdao Port has caused power generation companies to suffer greatly.

On October 22, the flat warehouse price of Datong premium mixed coal with a calorific value of 5500 kcal was between 760-780 yuan per ton. On September 25, the flat warehouse price of Datong premium mixed coal with the same calorific value was between 710-720 yuan. In less than a month, the price increased by approximately 7%.

This trend still appears strong for now. Cheng Zhi Liu, Executive Director and Deputy Head of Hong Kong Research Department at BOC International, predicts that domestic thermal coal spot prices will remain firm during the upcoming winter peak coal consumption season.

"Since the beginning of this year, coal prices have been higher than expected. We have respectively raised our predictions for domestic thermal coal benchmark prices for this year and next year by 9% and 14%," said Liu Chengzhi.

However, Chen Liang, the chief analyst of the coal industry at GF Securities, believes that the market's previous prediction for the coal price increase in 2011 was generally within 10%. But GF Securities estimates that the coal price increase in 2011 could be more than 15% (thermal coal 10%-12%, coking coal 15%-20%).

The loss-making area of the five major power generation companies is expanding.

According to multiple sources, the loss-making area of the five major power generation companies is gradually worsening, and most have already exceeded a 50% loss ratio.

Previously, during a regional special seminar held by China Tangshan Group, its key responsible person expressed that despite realizing profits from May to July and overall profit turning around in August, they still maintained slight profits even though the electricity market was in a low period in September.

After October, whether it is the origin places like Shanxi and Inner Mongolia or the transshipment place Qinhuangdao, coal prices have risen to varying degrees. Judging by the coal price increase in October, it is certain that China Tangshan Group will incur losses again.

A senior executive of a power generation group told our newspaper that currently, the loss-making ratio of the power plants under his group is 58%, and the loss-making ratios of other power generation groups should not differ much. According to the current coal price and its increase, it makes sense that the entire power generation industry will fall into losses again by the end of this year.

Previously, five independent power generation enterprises have all announced their third-quarter 2010 power generation data. Huaneng International’s net power generation in the third quarter increased by 23.0% year-on-year, higher than expected. Datang Power Generation announced that its third-quarter net power generation increased by 17.7% year-on-year to 44.9 billion kilowatt-hours.

In addition, China Resources Power’s third-quarter net power generation rebounded by 17.0% year-on-year to 26.1 billion kilowatt-hours. Huadian International also announced that its third-quarter net power generation rebounded by 20.3% year-on-year to 30.1 billion kilowatt-hours.

This means that entering the fourth quarter, power generation will continue to rise rapidly, while the pressure on power generation costs will gradually increase. The current expectation of coal price increases is also growing. The industry generally analyzes that the increase in coal prices not only stems from the upward trend in international bulk commodity prices but also recently has a significant relationship with the monetary market.

An official stationed in Qinhuangdao from a power generation company, when interviewed by our newspaper, also expressed that although many power generation companies have locked in coal contracts with coal companies in advance, in fact, there is still a portion of electric coal that needs to be supplemented through the market. On the other hand, the contract fulfillment rate of coal companies directly determines the cost of power generation companies.

There is hope for an adjustment in the grid-connected electricity price in the near future.

Last month, relevant departments of the National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC) convened a seminar with the five major power generation companies, consulting opinions on issues related to coal-electricity prices and power generation costs. Power generation companies generally complained about the inverted relationship between coal and electricity prices, resulting in severe losses for thermal power. The NDRC is also considering adjusting part of the electricity prices at an appropriate time.

Subsequently, Citigroup released a research report stating that the NDRC had proposed raising the grid-connected electricity price for coal-fired power plants in seven provinces by 15-25 yuan per megawatt-hour and uniformly increasing the grid-connected electricity price for gas-fired power plants by 30 yuan per megawatt-hour.

Despite the five major power generation companies repeatedly reflecting the issue of coal-electricity costs through various channels to the NDRC, we have learned that the NDRC has devised multiple adjustment plans. However, up until now, these plans have yet to be implemented.

"The latest data published by the National Bureau of Statistics shows that the CPI rose by 3.6% year-on-year in September, setting a new high in nearly 23 months. The inflationary pressure has forced the NDRC to be very cautious, as any changes in coal and electricity prices are interconnected," said an official from a power regulatory department when interviewed by our newspaper.

Jingyuan Yao, Chief Economist of the National Bureau of Statistics, expressed at the "2010 Forum on the Growth of China's State-Owned Economy" on the 23rd of this month that the driving effect of food and housing on price increases currently exceeds 90%. The CPI growth in October will be higher than in September. This statement has made the five major power generation companies quite disheartened, implying in the short term that the transmission of rising coal-electricity prices can only be borne by the power generation companies themselves.
