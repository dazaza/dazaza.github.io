Bali & nbsp, Golf Mall; & nbsp; & nbsp; & nbsp; Sohu Sports News & nbsp; Bali is a famous tourist area in Indonesia. It is an island in the small Sunda Islands to the east of Java, covering an area of more than 5560 square kilometers with a population of about 2.8 million. The island has a tropical climate and is influenced by the ocean, so it is warm and rainy, with very fertile soil. It is green and hilly all year round, with colorful flowers and towering trees. The Balinese love flowers and decorate everywhere with them, hence the island is called the "Island of Flowers" and enjoys the reputation of "Paradise of the South Sea" and "God's Island". Most of the island is mountainous, with crisscrossing mountains and an east high west low terrain. There are four or five conical complete volcanic peaks, among which Agung Volcano (Bali Peak) has an altitude of 3142 meters, which is the highest point on the island. Nearby, there is the active Batur Volcano that erupted in 963. The beaches at Sanur, Nusa & #8226; Dua and Kuta are the most beautiful seaside bathing places on the island, with fine sand, wide beaches, and deep blue clear sea water. From April to October is the dry season, which is relatively cool and is the best time to climb the peaks or visit nature reserves. Every year, tourists from all over the world come here one after another.

Special Introduction to Golf Courses: New Kuta Golf

New Kuta Golf

The latest completed golf course in Bali. Located in the southeast of the Kuta area, near Bali's most famous Dreamland Beach, overlooking the Indian Ocean with beautiful scenery. It takes only 15 minutes by car to get to the airport and the city center of Kuta. Establishment Time: 2007 Standard Stroke: 72 strokes Course Length: 6812 yards Number of Holes: 18 holes Designer: Ronald Fream, David Dale & amp; Kevin Ramsey, USA. The course successfully hosted the Indonesian Golf Open in March, attracting many golfers to participate.

Bali Golf and Country Club Course

This is an 18-hole international-level competition course designed by Robin Nelson and Rodney Wright, rated as one of the top 5 courses in Asia by Fortune magazine. It is adjacent to the sea, located in the most concentrated high-end hotel resort area of Nusa Dua in Bali. If you stay in this lush mini peninsula, it would be the most convenient. The fairways here are quite flat, mostly using trees as obstacles. Without having to worry too much about how to hit the ball onto the green, just relax and enjoy the tropical rainforest atmosphere and the unbeatable sea view of the Indian Ocean!
