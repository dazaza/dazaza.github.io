He will hear your voice whenever you call his name to the sky.

by xiaoxiao67 on 2012-03-01 16:01:14

Whenever you call his name to the sky, he will hear your voice.

She is 23 years old and a beautician. I am 35 years old and an after-sales technician in a company. Due to the time difference and a coincidental circumstance, she intruded into my personal life, and at the same time, I also took a big step into her personal living space. This might be a kind of injury to me, or it might be a beautiful memory. For her, it might be a kind of pain, or it might also be an unforgettable love experience.

She is young and pretty, understanding, and sometimes like a tame little cat. We can talk about everything together. We can chat from the past to the future, from heaven to earth. No matter what topic we talk about, it always happens with laughter. Being with her makes everything in the world seem beautiful and happy. Troubles and worries all vanish.

I had a fracture in my right hand in a car accident. She bathed for me, undressed for me, and uncomplainingly did everything I couldn't do. It made me feel shocked and deeply memorable. Because in my memory, no woman has ever bathed for me, let alone a girl who is 12 years younger than me!

Because of my work, I could only rest on New Year's Eve and had to go back to work on the fourth day of the first lunar month. In this situation, we got up at six in the morning on New Year's Eve, had breakfast, and then fully equipped ourselves.

(Because it was cold, we wore four or five layers of clothes inside and out, plus raincoats, and our heads and necks were all wrapped up, leaving only our eyes exposed) Then we rode nearly 300 kilometers by bike and didn't arrive at her house until five in the afternoon. (Since motorcycles are not allowed on highways, we had to take detours and the road conditions were very bad.) Maybe because she was in my heart, and I was there for her, so after riding for such a long time, we didn't feel tired at all! I spent three short days of Spring Festival with her family and then went back to work. (It was the first time I went to her house, and her parents were very friendly and nice to me.)

On the third day of the Spring Festival (with drizzle and snow, minus three degrees Celsius), I had to start my journey home again. She could have chosen not to come with me, but she didn't. Riding in the drizzle and snow is probably one of the worst things in the world.

On the way, she clung tightly to my back, and our bodies still felt no warmth at all. All the way, I kept talking to her to prevent her from falling asleep! Every time we stopped, I was worried that she would freeze. So I asked her to get off the bike and run to warm up, and I ran with her. When we both felt warm, we continued our journey. Now I don't remember how many times we stopped to exercise and increase body heat.

However, there is one thing I remember clearly: when we had ridden about a hundred kilometers, a minibus came. Seeing that she was freezing badly and I felt heartbroken, I told her to take the bus first.

I would continue alone. But she refused. Thus, although our bodies were cold outside, our hearts were burning hot. Neither of us abandoned each other, and we finally returned home together. Despite enduring such hardships under such weather, she never blamed me in later life. We both regarded it as a kind of fun in our lives and remembered this extraordinary journey!

Now every time I ride along that road, when I see the place where we stopped to run, I think of her, her every move at that time was so innocent and romantic. Everything seems just within reach. I think no matter how much time passes, I will never forget this unusual journey...

Due to the age gap, our love was under pressure (from both her family and mine). However, we are both trying hard to work on our respective families' thoughts, and through our joint efforts, the basic issues have been resolved.

There is one problem that may still be a secret in her heart, and that is marriage. I usually don't mention marriage to her. I think; she is still very young, and some things may not be well considered, sometimes making impulsive decisions leading to mistakes. I want to give her more time and more choices. Now thinking about it... This might actually be a wrong decision of mine!

One day, she finally left, and I personally saw her off on the bus. This parting made me realize clearly that it might mean we couldn't be together anymore, and many shared things would remain in the past and memories, unable to bring joy again.

When the bus started, I tried hard not to cry, but my heart was bleeding. Because I knew if she saw me crying, she would cry too. The bus left, and tears involuntarily slid down my face. For a long time afterward, I was like a soulless person, lacking energy to do anything. Alas! That kind of inner pain is something I cannot express in words!

After that, we still kept in touch by phone. Later, her mother suffered a leg injury in a car accident, and I visited her house. During that meeting, she told me that someone had come to arrange a blind date for her. I didn't respond. I just felt heartache. But I also thought, because of her youth, there must be people coming to propose. If not, that would be strange.

Whether she really likes me is a good test. If she truly likes me, she would tell those who come to propose and those around her about us, and others wouldn't bother her. On the contrary, it means that her liking for me was just a momentary impulse. (Since we have never been apart since we met, this separation also became a test of the strength of our love.) So I said nothing.

This was our last meeting. On the same day I left her house, she also went out to work. After that, we could only keep in touch by phone.

During a phone conversation, she told me to find someone else, and at that moment, I almost fainted. Although I was mentally prepared, when reality hit, I still couldn't accept it...

Love to a certain extent should not be possession. This sounds so noble, so selfless. But in practice, it is so painful, so sorrowful.

Yesterday evening at 11 pm, I called her. She asked me if it had rained here these few days. I said yes, and I asked her how she knew. She said she checks the weather forecast here every day. At that moment, my heart trembled, and I was exhilarated.

Here, I want to say to her: Dear Ying, when you wander outside, many people only care if you fly high enough, but few ask if you're tired.

If one day you feel tired, come home and rest! This will always be your best shelter from the storm. There is someone who will silently care for you as much as you care for him, silently waiting for your return, and he will forever support you without hesitation.

No matter where you are, whenever you call his name to the sky, he will hear your voice...
