Prevent lumbar disease

by fjdke534 on 2011-06-09 12:33:48

Often, there is only the effort to withstand and no leisure to relax in folk tourism. Although going to the gym or sightseeing is not a wrong choice for relaxation, busy work and life always make it impossible for white-collar workers.

In fact, besides choosing more professional fitness methods, ancient and fun-filled games are also an option for fitness. For example, games like throwing sandbags, hopscotch, and paper airplanes help exercise the waist. During these games of dodging and maneuvering, one unknowingly exercises the waist muscles, and frequent play can effectively prevent lumbar diseases. Lumbar disc herniation

◆Throwing Sandbags

Sandbags made of scraps of cloth and thread sewn together and filled with fine sand are used as weapons to "attack" the opponent. There are different ways to play sandbag throwing in different places.

Within a designated area, there is one thrower at the front and back who throws the sandbag at the opponent. If hit, the person is penalized and must leave the game. If the sandbag is caught by the opponent, that person gains an extra "life", or a previously "eliminated" teammate can re-enter the game. For white-collar workers, cute hanging decorations can be used as "sandbags".

Warm reminder: Throwing sandbags can exercise the waist muscles and prevent lumbar diseases.

◆Hopscotch

Hopscotch is undoubtedly the most economical game, requiring only a piece of chalk and a stone to play. Draw grids of various sizes on the ground and then move forward while kicking the stone into the correct grid according to the sequence. Going out of bounds or stepping into the wrong grid counts as a failure. Warm reminder: Hopscotch helps to exercise foot control and effectively prevents varicose veins in the lower limbs.

◆Playing Cat's Cradle

In some places, this game is called flipping cotton threads, flipping ox trenches, flipping sticks, flipping ropes, flipping strings, or climbing supports. It's a two-player game requiring a rope about three feet long. One player stretches the string circle with both hands, and through hooking and twisting the string, various shapes can be created. The most common ones include: two doors, chicken anus eyes, cross flowers, well characters, houses, scissors stocks, noodles, and flower handkerchiefs. If your office has a space for activities, you can use lunchtime to play with a few colleagues.

Warm reminder: Playing cat's cradle can exercise finger flexibility and is a great health-preserving mini-game for office workers who often hold a mouse, effectively preventing "mouse hand."

◆Throwing Paper Airplanes

For netizens of the post-80s generation, paper airplanes carried the childhood dream of flying towards the blue sky. Paper airplanes belong to the most common form of aviation-related origami crafts because they are the easiest type of folding paper to master, thus being loved by beginners and experts alike. The simplest way to fold a paper airplane requires only six steps, and white-collar workers can easily fold paper airplanes using discarded A4 paper.

Warm reminder: During the process of throwing paper airplanes, the shoulder muscles of the human body can get fully stretched, thereby effectively preventing the occurrence of periarthritis of the shoulder.
