Solution for Winter and Spring Skin Care Problem Areas

by anonymous on 2011-02-28 22:29:41

In this season, the body is wrapped in thick layers of clothing. Some neglected areas of skincare become even worse due to excessive friction from clothing and being overlooked. You need to have the skills to urgently rescue these skincare trouble spots!

Trouble Spot 1: Nail edges

In winter, hand cream is almost indispensable, but it still can't prevent hangnails or dead skin buildup because the nutrients in the hand cream may not necessarily reach the nail edges. Frequent manicures also harm the cuticles around the nails.

Solution:

DIY Hand Mask: Before sleeping, just use the hand cream you normally use, plus a secret weapon. After applying hand cream, use a Vitamin E capsule on your hands, apply the oil around the nails, massage until fully absorbed, then put on a regular cotton glove and go to sleep.

Hand Gloves: Similar to foot masks, they can both help with skin metabolism, remove excess dead skin cells, and deeply nourish the skin. Just 15 minutes a day, simple operation.

Trouble Spot 2: Front of the calf

The shinbone area, commonly known as the tibia, is the part of the body with the least sebum secretion. Therefore, even if you apply a layer of body lotion, it's hard to avoid flakiness.

Solution:

Double Care: In winter, you can switch to a more oily bath oil. After bathing, while the pores are not completely closed, apply and massage until fully absorbed. And, care for it daily because being able to stay hydrated for 24 hours is already a very good result.

DIY Leg Mask: You can regularly make a leg mask with cling film. First, heat the cling film to 40°C, then wrap it around the calves that have been coated with massage oil. This will accelerate the absorption of the massage oil. After 10 minutes when the heat has completely dissipated, open it up, and the skin will become tight and shiny.

Trouble Spot 3: Back of the neck

Neck lines most easily reveal age, so we are already familiar with the principles of cleaning and massaging the front of the neck, but we forget about the back of the neck because it's hard to observe. It’s only when its texture is no longer smooth and its color differs from the front of the neck that we realize there's a problem. Especially in winter, the increased friction from collars and hair can make the problem worse.

Solution:

Exfoliation: When you perform full-body exfoliating cleanses, don't forget to clean the back of the neck. If you need to handle this issue urgently, you can refer to the 28-day skin metabolic cycle rule and choose gentle scrub products, such as scrubbing facial cleansers, gently massage, and continue cleaning for 28 days.

Moisturizing Massage: Compared to other parts of the body, the skin on the back of the neck does not obviously show discomfort with skincare products, so simple maintenance can meet the needs of the skin. Every day, when applying face cream to the face and front of the neck, gently massage the back of the neck with the cream 10-20 times. This not only moisturizes the skin but also appropriately relaxes the neck.

Trouble Spot 4: Feet

Foot care, especially the skin on the heels, is the hardest to tackle. Most of the de-scaling scrubs sold on the market can only do surface work. Soaking feet can effectively soften keratin and assist in removing dead skin, but it's hard to achieve uniform cleaning.

Solution:

Foot Mask Exfoliation: A new way of foot care - foot masks have become popular. The reason for this is not only because of their simple and convenient operation, but mainly because after about ten minutes, you can clearly see the foot skin becoming tender. The dead skin on the heels, which is the hardest to care for, can also show signs of metabolism with daily care.

Moisturizing: Skincare experts point out that if you apply a thick layer of lotion to your feet before using foot masks, it can significantly improve the condition of the foot skin.
