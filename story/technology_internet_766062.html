Microsoft to buy Nokia's devices and services business for $7.2 billion

by anonymous on 2013-09-04 14:42:43

September 3rd news: Microsoft is acquiring the majority of Nokia's mobile phone business for €3.79 billion, and in addition, will be purchasing Nokia’s patent licenses for €1.65 billion, making the total amount €5.44 billion (or $7.2 billion).

Nokia will retain Nokia Solutions and Networks (formerly NSN), HERE maps business, its research and development department (CTO Office), and its patent portfolio. As of last Friday, Nokia's market value was $14.6 billion.

It is reported that Microsoft will use its overseas cash resources to complete this transaction. The deal is expected to close in the first quarter of 2014 and requires approval from Nokia shareholders and regulatory bodies.

In addition, according to agreements reached between the two companies, Nokia's current CEO Stephen Elop will become Nokia's Executive Vice President, overseeing Devices & Services until the completion of the transaction. Risto Siilasmaa, Nokia's current chairman, has been appointed as Nokia's interim CEO.

After the deal concludes, some Nokia executives including CEO Stephen Elop will join Microsoft.

In February 2011, Nokia and Microsoft partnered to launch the Lumia series of smartphones, capturing a certain share of the smartphone market. Regarding this transaction, Microsoft aims to accelerate innovation in mobile devices to increase their share and profitability, enhance synergies, unify branding, and consolidate marketing efforts.
