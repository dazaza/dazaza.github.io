Plot Summary:

In the early Song Dynasty, Emperor Renzong had a great love for inkstones. Every year, outstanding inkstones with excellent craftsmanship would be selected from all over the country and offered as tributes to the imperial court. Duanzhou in Guangdong Province has always been famous for producing Duan inkstones. The old pit in Duanzhou was sealed as the royal tribute pit, and the stones mined there would be carved into inkstones by the best craftsmen. The best ones were selected as tribute inkstones and offered to the imperial court.

Every year, the inspection and delivery of tribute inkstones to the capital became a major event in Duanzhou, a big day for the people of Duanzhou. Therefore, those fortunate enough to be appointed to make tribute inkstones were often called the "best in the world." There were many inkstone studios in Duanzhou, with countless craftsmen. Among them, Jimo Studio was the most renowned. The stone-carving skills of Xiao Jingzhu, the owner of Jimo Studio, were unparalleled, and almost all the tribute inkstones from Duanzhou every year were made by him, greatly enhancing Duanzhou's reputation. However, due to their prominence, many inkstone officials harbored resentment towards the Xiao family. Yet Xiao Jingzhu's only son, Xiao Fei, his adopted son Le Feichang, and Yang Feng were known as the three rich young men of Ruizhou, notorious wastrels known far and wide.

On this particular day, it was the grand occasion for Jimo Studio to present the tribute inkstone. Xiao Jingzhu respectfully presented the tribute inkstone, but the censor supervising the tribute inkstone from Shezhou, Changxiaohou, pointed out that this inkstone was a forgery. Xiao Jingzhu was arrested and thrown into prison. Prefect Bao Zheng of Duanzhou presided over the trial of the case, and the expert invited to authenticate the authenticity of the inkstone turned out to be Xiao Jingzhu's long-lost younger sister, Lu Min. Lu Min firmly identified the inkstone as being crafted by none other than Xiao Jingzhu, thus inexplicably dragging the Xiao family into a legal disaster. Although Xiao Fei appeared indifferent on the surface, he was deeply worried about his father and constantly opposed him. He rashly stormed the court to take the blame for his father but failed to save him. Xiao Fei was heartbroken. Just before Xiao Jingzhu was to be escorted to the execution ground, an imperial decree pardoning Xiao Jingzhu arrived, ordering him to carve an extraordinary inkstone within half a year for use in negotiations. It turned out that Xiao Jingzhu's good friend Zhuo Tianyi had once been the emperor's teacher and secretly helped him. Around this time, Xiao Fei met Lu Min's mute daughter Ju Ban, and the two got along well.

Xiao Jingzhu arranged for Xiao Fei to marry Zhuo Tianyi's daughter Zhuo Yuer, who had a prearranged marriage contract with him, in order to correct Xiao Fei's impetuous nature. Xiao Fei refused. One problem after another arose as Xiao Jingzhu worked tirelessly day and night to carve the tribute inkstone but unfortunately damaged the imperial gift stone. The Xiao family was stripped of their wealth, Jimo Studio was sealed, and Xiao Fei instantly fell from a wealthy young master to a street beggar, experiencing the warmth and coldness of human society and the humiliation of lowliness.

To resolve the border situation, the emperor ordered Xiao Fei to complete the tribute inkstone for Xiao Jingzhu within half a year. In order to save his father and restore the glory of Jimo Studio, Xiao Fei had no choice but to shoulder the responsibility of carving inkstones and embark on the path of seeking the way of inkstones. In fact, what Xiao Fei hated most was carving inkstones because he had seen since childhood how his mother died tragically in the inkstone pit due to his father's obsession with carving inkstones, which caused years of estrangement between them.

When Xiao Fei set out alone to learn the craft, he repeatedly encountered the mysterious woman Lu Rongrong. After several encounters, they progressed from acquaintances to friends and eventually decided to travel together. Xiao Fei visited numerous masters and endured great hardships, while Lu Rongrong provided full assistance, showcasing remarkable talent and proving herself to be just like Xiao Fei, a person of deep emotion. Gradually, the two developed feelings for each other.

Through learning from three master carvers—Li Ming, Zhou Shiyou, and Ling Lin—Xiao Fei profoundly understood the profound principles of inkstones, research, and ink. They are interconnected yet form their own systems, showcasing the vast depth of inkstone culture. Upon returning from his studies, the inkstone carved by Xiao Fei received great praise from the emperor, allowing Jimo Studio to reopen. At this point, Xiao Fei became somewhat arrogant.

Bao Zheng promptly uncovered Changxiaohou's plot, leading to his demotion and confiscation of his assets.
