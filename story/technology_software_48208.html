Break through IE with Opera's disguise feature

by mylovehisa on 2008-02-18 10:19:53

At the beginning of time, when chaos was just beginning to take form, two great deities ruled over the internet: one was Netscape, and the other was IE. In the cyber world, they looked almost identical, and life was good.

One day, Netscape developed a small feature that allowed web developers to distinguish whether the browser accessing a website was Netscape or IE. When developers felt that their webpages had features unique to Netscape that were not supported by IE, they could deny access to IE users. Thus began the brief era of NETSCAPE-ONLY.

However, as the saying goes, "Heaven wishes to destroy someone, it must first drive them mad." With Netscape's stagnation, the further refinement of IE's functionality, and Bill Gates' near-thuggish bundling of IE with Windows, the great deity fell! This marked the start of the widely criticized IE-ONLY era.

Recently, Aunt Liu from the neighborhood committee mysteriously told me she had scooped up some insider information – Youpai (a Chinese image hosting service) is about to change its policy on external image links.

What I initially feared has now come true. Using domestic services means living in constant fear because they might suddenly turn their backs on you or shut down. Using foreign services also keeps you on edge because the Emperor might get excited one day and block them. Long live the Heavenly Dynasty!

Given this situation, it's better to make plans early. Being so disheartened by the Heavenly Dynasty's service providers, I'd rather risk being blocked and break out of Asia. That’s why I chose PicasaWeb.

I love the Heavenly Dynasty, and I also love Google, but the Heavenly Dynasty always blocks Google. I love Google, and I also love Opera, but Google and Opera don’t see eye-to-eye—PicasaWeb doesn't fully support Opera.

In reality, many websites claiming to be IE-ONLY are simply indulging in the delusions of their developers. Browsing with other browsers usually doesn’t cause any major issues. So, can we force our way through?

Actually, I want to strongly voice public opinion here: IE fans, the deity you adore deeply is nothing more than an unlucky thing that still dares not admit who it really is. Look at what IE's identification says:

Mozilla/4.0 (compatible; MSIE 7.0b; Windows NT 6.0)

Since most websites identify browsers by checking this ID, it’s entirely possible to fool them by forging it. Just look at the pervasive advertisements for fake IDs on the streets of the Heavenly Dynasty. Long live the Heavenly Dynasty!

Data packets sent by browsers to web servers are divided into two parts: the message header and the message body. In the message header, there's a line called "User-Agent" which contains the browser's ID. All we need to do is modify this. In fact, most popular browsers today can perform identity spoofing, and Opera is no exception.

When opening PicasaWeb in Opera, you'll see a pink warning bar at the top of the page:

"You are using a browser that is not fully supported. Some functions may not work properly, but you can still browse around."
