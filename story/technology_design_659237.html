Drilling a Hole, drawn by Zhang Hao

Drilling Holes - Zhang Hao/Artwork. Due to dispatching employees to drill three holes on the second-floor platform, billionaire boss Mr. Liu and his two employees stood together as defendants. On October 14th, prosecuted by the Xiaguan District People's Procuratorate of Nanjing City, Jiangsu Province, the three not only faced charges of "intentional property damage," but also a civil lawsuit demanding compensation of over 7.12 million yuan.

Holes in the Bank's Roof

Mr. Li is an employee at the Dinghuai Gate branch of the Construction Bank of China's Gulou Branch in Nanjing. He recalled that at around 12:00 AM on March 31st, it was raining heavily outside when he was suddenly awakened by a phone call from the branch manager: "The alarm has been ringing non-stop. The police have already arrived. You need to go to the branch quickly and see what's going on!"

Mr. Li didn't dare delay, so he hurriedly got up and called a colleague to rush to the unit. By the time they arrived, the police were already there. Before even opening the door, the two heard water leaking inside with a 'splash' sound. After opening the door and disabling the alarm, the two began searching for the source of the leak while praying that it wasn't coming from the 'back area.'

This back area mainly housed computers, machinery equipment, vouchers, and was where non-counter staff worked. If there was a leak causing machine damage or circuit short-circuit, the consequences would be unimaginable.

In this branch's back area, there are four rooms, with the largest one being used for office work and voucher storage. Adjacent to it is the monitoring room, which primarily houses surveillance equipment, UPS power supplies, switches, routers, and air conditioners.

Unfortunately, their fears came true. When the two turned to the back area, they indeed found three holes in the roof of the voucher storage office and the monitoring room. Water was continuously flowing through these three holes, dripping onto the ground and desks with a 'splash' sound. There was also some water flowing down the walls from the ceiling, resembling a waterfall... The accumulated water on the floor exceeded one centimeter, and many machines were soaked.

The Cause of the Holes - Property Dispute

"How could there be three more holes?" Mr. Li suddenly remembered another incident.

About a week earlier, also during the rain, someone at the branch noticed water leaking from the roof. Since the entire roof had suspended ceilings, the "leak points" couldn't be identified clearly, so workers were hired to remove the ceiling to check for leaks. Mr. Li vaguely remembered finding three holes in the ceiling then.

However, those three holes had already been sealed with cement by the repair workers on the afternoon of March 30th. Now, before the ceiling could be reinstalled, how did three new holes appear?

"Could it be intentional sabotage?" Mr. Li suspected and immediately led the police up to the rooftop platform in the rain. They found about one centimeter of standing water on the platform. Additionally, they discovered a strange plastic pipe on the platform, which extended from the 8th floor. There was a small crack in the pipe, and water was leaking out from the crack, mixing with the rainwater and flowing into the bank through those three holes.

Upon closer inspection of the three holes, the police determined that they were the same three holes that had been sealed with cement, but had now been intentionally reopened.

Based on this evidence, the police concluded that someone was deliberately causing damage and quickly identified the suspect —— Mr. Liu, the owner of a certain fiberglass products company in Nanjing.

It turns out that the Dinghuai Gate branch bought its current office location from this company four years ago. Later, due to various reasons, disputes arose regarding this real estate transaction, leading to a legal battle. As a result, Mr. Liu harbored resentment towards the Dinghuai Gate branch.

In fact, after the first three holes appeared on the roof of the Dinghuai Gate branch, the police were alerted. After determining that it was Mr. Liu's doing, the police warned him not to do such "foolish things" again. However, he refused to heed the advice.

Mr. Liu admitted that the first three holes were drilled by two of his employees, and the second reopening of the three holes was also done by the same two employees. But Mr. Liu insisted, "The ownership of this platform still belongs to me. Who can stop me from drilling holes on my own platform?"

Three Holes Result in a Claim of 7.12 Million Yuan

Mr. Liu and his team never considered the serious consequences of their actions.

In fact, on March 31st, shortly before 3:00 AM, the Dinghuai Gate branch experienced a power outage due to a short circuit, preventing the roller shutters from opening. Despite Mr. Li and his colleagues' best efforts to save the equipment, it was estimated that equipment worth over 300,000 yuan was damaged due to water immersion. More severely, the "leakage" caused the Dinghuai Gate branch to suspend operations for several months.

The prosecution statement from the Xiaguan District People's Procuratorate recognized that Mr. Liu's "drilling" actions caused direct economic losses totaling 290,000 yuan to the Dinghuai Gate branch. However, during the trial, the Gulou branch, acting as the "criminal-attached civil plaintiff," filed a criminal-attached civil lawsuit against Mr. Liu and the other two individuals, requesting the court to order them to jointly compensate for direct economic losses of 312,000 yuan, indirect losses from business suspension amounting to 6.807 million yuan, for a total of 7.12 million yuan.

However, just for drilling three holes, a compensation claim of 7.12 million yuan? Mr. Liu, who has assets exceeding hundreds of millions, expressed that he could not accept it, and his defense lawyer requested the prosecution to present detailed evidence regarding the losses.

The court did not make a ruling on the spot, and the case is still under review.
