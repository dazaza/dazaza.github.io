Comment on the data of the home air conditioner industry in June: domestic sales unaffected by the cancellation of the energy-saving people's livelihood policy H9

by cvz81tk2 on 2011-07-27 16:58:56

Both production and sales growth rates have rebounded, while export volume has basically remained at the same level year-on-year. In June, the home air conditioner industry produced 11.57 million units, a seasonal decline of 70% month-on-month, but an increase of 30.2% year-on-year. Sales reached 11.39 million units, a drop of 10% month-on-month, but an increase of 25.9% year-on-year, with the growth rate showing some recovery compared to May. In June, air conditioner exports amounted to 4.66 million units, a decrease of 17% month-on-month, but an increase of 2.1% year-on-year. June is traditionally the off-season for air conditioner exports, as last year’s base was relatively high due to developed countries replenishing their inventories. This year, inventory replenishment was largely completed by the end of April, resulting in continued declines in growth rates in May and June. We expect that the growth rate will remain low in the second half of the year.

Domestic sales growth exceeded 50%, bouncing back significantly. In June, domestic air conditioner sales reached 6.73 million units, increasing by 50.3% year-on-year, a substantial improvement from the 30% year-on-year growth rate in May. On one hand, the reduction in energy-saving subsidies last year led to a lower base in June (this year’s air conditioner sales were less affected by the withdrawal of energy-saving subsidies), and on the other hand, despite mild weather, increased rigid demand drove industry growth. Since June, we have conducted multiple surveys on domestic home appliance channels and found that air conditioner sales in third- and fourth-tier markets have grown rapidly, with some towns in certain provinces experiencing a boom. We believe that the popularization of air conditioners among rural residents will continue to support rapid growth in domestic air conditioner sales.

Inventories have slightly increased in the short term due to weather factors but remain normal. In June, air conditioner inventories totaled 9.94 million units, up 31% year-on-year and up 180,000 units month-on-month. As June marks the latter part of the peak domestic sales season, inventories usually decline slightly month-on-month. This year's increase partly reflects the impact of weather on inventories in the short term, and also due to dealers' optimistic expectations for next year, leading to more active restocking. The inventory-to-domestic sales ratio in June was 1.5, still at a relatively low level (the lowest being 1.4, equivalent to 1.4 months of domestic sales).

Gree and Midea continue to experience strong domestic sales growth. In June, Gree sold 3.2 million units, including 2.27 million domestically and 0.93 million for export, with respective year-on-year increases of 41%, 68%, and 1%. Midea sold 3.1 million units, including 1.95 million domestically and 1.15 million for export, with respective year-on-year increases of 40%, 63%, and 13%. Domestic sales for both Gree and Midea continue to grow strongly. In the first half of the year, cumulative sales growth for Gree, Midea, and Haier were 52%, 50%, and 24%, respectively.

Risk factors: 1) Extension of property purchase restrictions to second- and third-tier cities, delays in construction and distribution of affordable housing; 2) Slowing economic growth in developed countries; 3) Appreciation of the RMB exchange rate.

Investment recommendation: Air conditioner industry sales growth in the second quarter (25%) showed a slight decline compared to the first quarter (39%), while domestic sales growth in the second quarter (38%) increased compared to the first quarter (29%). This aligns with our mid-term strategy report's forecast that domestic sales will support industry growth, which has been confirmed by our ongoing channel surveys in the home appliance sector. Given that domestic sales are the main source of profit for air conditioner companies, good domestic sales performance will support company earnings. The current valuations of Qingdao Haier, Gree Electric, and Midea Electric relative to 2011 are 13x, 12x, and 15x, respectively, within a reasonable range on the lower side. We maintain our "buy" investment rating.
