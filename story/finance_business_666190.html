Symptoms of Early-Stage Hemorrhoids:

1. Bleeding during bowel movements: The primary symptom of early internal hemorrhoids, which may include jet-like bleeding, dripping blood, or blood on toilet paper. The blood is bright red, and external hemorrhoids do not cause bleeding.

2. Prolapse: The main symptom of mid-to-late stage internal hemorrhoids. The primary reason is the enlargement of internal hemorrhoid nodules, causing the mucosa and submucosal layer to separate from the anal layer. During bowel movements, the internal hemorrhoid nodules may descend below the dentate line and protrude outside the anal canal.

3. Falling pain: The main symptom of painful external hemorrhoids. Internal hemorrhoids do not cause pain unless there is inflammation. Falling pain often occurs when internal hemorrhoids become infected, strangulated, or undergo necrosis, leading to severe falling pain.

4. Itching: Repeated prolapse of late-stage internal hemorrhoids can lead to relaxation of the anal sphincter muscle and increased secretions, resulting in dampness and uncleanliness around the anus, causing itching and eczema. Severe cases may even lead to friction pain and itch pain. Internal hemorrhoid bleeding can also cause anemia, dizziness, loss of appetite, and dry stools.
