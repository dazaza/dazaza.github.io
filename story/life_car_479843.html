Car Beauty: Car Washing and Cooling

Can a car engine be washed with water immediately if it's too hot?

Many people like to wash their car engines while cleaning their cars. Although the intention to keep it clean is good, this method is not recommended. Nowadays, many cars use aluminum air conditioning radiators, engine water tanks, and engine blocks, etc. If these parts are still quite hot and you wash them directly with water, it could cause these components to deform and shorten their lifespan. Moreover, after washing the engine, many cars experience issues with starting. This is mainly due to the ignition system getting damp. In such cases, the car should be placed in a dry and ventilated area, allowing sunlight to directly shine on the engine to dry it, or using clean high-pressure air to blow on the damp areas. If necessary, contact a repair station for assistance. Additionally, if water enters electrical connectors, it can cause electrical system failures, leading to corrosion of electrical parts and potential fault hazards.

Car Cooling Methods:

A When the temperature inside the car is high, vent the hot air before closing the windows. After starting the vehicle, do not close the windows and turn on the air conditioning immediately. Open all the windows first, start the outside circulation, and expel the hot air. Once the temperature inside the cabin has dropped, then close the windows and turn on the air conditioning. Or, when turning on the air conditioning, open the windows for a while until the cold air drives out the hot air, then close the windows. This not only improves the cooling effect but also reduces the pressure on the engine during initial operation.

B The temperature inside the car should ideally be adjusted to between 20°C-25°C. If it's a non-constant temperature air conditioner, set the temperature to the lowest first and increase the fan speed to the maximum. When you feel cold, adjust the temperature higher and reduce the fan speed.

C When using the air conditioning inside the car, try not to smoke inside. If you must smoke, adjust the air conditioning ventilation control to the "outside circulation" position. If the car has a sunroof, open it. Stay in a closed air-conditioned environment for no more than half an hour without airing out for a few minutes to prevent carbon monoxide poisoning. Do not use the air conditioning for too long after parking.

D Pay attention to the air conditioning's intake and exhaust vents. Do not pile items near these vents to prevent them from being blocked, which would impede the air conditioning system's airflow.

E Pay attention to whether the engine load is too heavy. Some mini-cars with engines of 1 liter or less may have poor air conditioning effects and even experience overheating after driving a certain distance, especially in summer. Therefore, if the engine load increases due to the air conditioning causing excessively high water temperatures, pause using the air conditioning until the water temperature returns to normal.

F Replenish the refrigerant. Original air conditioners generally need replenishment every three years. However, most cases of reduced air conditioning efficiency are not necessarily due to a lack of refrigerant; check if the refrigerant is leaking.

G For repairs related to poor cooling, the involved knowledge is relatively professional. Among various faults of poor cooling, the most common and simplest one is poor heat dissipation of the air conditioning system. Poor heat dissipation prevents the refrigerant from circulating in the system pipes according to the designed pressure, temperature, and state, resulting in poor cooling of the air conditioning system and potentially causing high-pressure pipe explosions or leaks. Under normal circumstances, the refrigerant (134A or R12) will not be consumed, and the main maintenance items are the cleanliness of the condenser and the working condition of the cooling fan.

H Reasons for refrigerant leakage include aging of the sealing rings in the pipes, abnormal refrigerant pressure, and damage to system components. After repairing the leakage fault, it is necessary to replenish the refrigerating oil, evacuate the system, and recharge the refrigerant. During the repair process, ensure the quality of the refrigerant and refrigerating oil. Using inferior or different types of refrigerants can lead to compressor abnormalities and poor cooling.

I Nowadays, the seals on car doors and windows of family sedans perform well. Since the air inside and outside the car cannot easily circulate when the car is parked, carbon monoxide emitted by the engine running for a long time may gradually accumulate inside the car. Combined with the oxygen consumption and carbon dioxide exhalation of the people inside, over time, the oxygen inside the car will gradually decrease, causing people inside to unconsciously be poisoned and lose consciousness, which can severely result in loss of life.

J When parking, if there are many vehicles in the same area, the front of your car should avoid facing the exhaust pipe of the vehicle in front, maintaining at least an 8-meter distance to prevent inhaling the exhaust fumes from the previous car.

K When the car is parked, do not keep the air conditioning on for a long time. Even during normal driving, frequently open the windows to allow air circulation between inside and outside the car. If the driver or passengers feel dizzy, heavy-headed, or weak in the limbs, promptly open the windows to breathe fresh air.

L Harmful gases like formaldehyde and benzene inside new cars are released in large quantities when the outdoor temperature is high in summer. If the car windows are closed tightly for a long time while the air conditioning is on, it can increase the pollution level of the car's interior air, harming the health of the occupants. Therefore, newly purchased cars should pay more attention to ventilation and choose effective measures for indoor air purification.
