Is tinea manuum and pedis contagious?

Experts from Shanxi Huimin Dermatology Hospital point out that tinea manuum and pedis is indeed contagious. If left untreated or without proper isolation, it can easily lead to self-contamination or transmission to others. Tinea manuum and pedis refers to superficial fungal infections on the palms, soles, fingers, and toes. It is a contagious disease, as fungi can easily infect through direct and indirect contact. Therefore, direct contact with tinea manuum and pedis patients, or using public baths, towels, shoes, etc., previously used by infected individuals can easily result in contamination. Like other infectious diseases, its spread also follows three basic links: source of infection - tinea manuum and pedis patients and carriers; route of transmission - direct and indirect contact; susceptible individuals - elderly, infants, sick people, etc., with low resistance. To avoid the spread of tinea manuum and pedis, once diagnosed, timely and proper treatment measures should be taken. Personal items should be used individually, and one should avoid touching public facilities in public places. Additionally, personal resistance should be enhanced.
