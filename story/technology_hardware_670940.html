http://www.niugu8.com Youth is a polished pickaxe head Memories fade, years go by like songs, and before I knew it, I had spent nearly 11 years in the position of line worker. World-class photography can capture unforgettable moments in life, but the hour hand and minute hand on clocks are like flowing sand, taking away pieces of our stories, whether they be ordinary or vivid. So would it leave something for me? Like a pen, recording the process of growth; or like an ageless song, constantly singing in life... At the start of my career, in the most basic and fundamental position of switch maintenance, the harsh labor environment, along with daily severe physical exhaustion, once made me have great resistance and aversion to the road maintenance work. I just went through the motions every day, truly spending a period of muddled days, and my state was like a broken and rust-covered pickaxe, throwing all ideals, beliefs, goals, and pursuits into the "scrap pile" along with the abandoned pickaxe. Seeing my listless and low-spirited working condition every day, out of concern and care for young employees, my foreman often found me during his spare time for communication and exchange, and awakened my almost lost passion for work with his own personal experiences and actions, timely helping and guiding me to correct the course of my work. He entered the railway industry 8 years earlier than me, and with his diligent and down-to-earth work style and relentless research spirit of "paying attention everywhere is knowledge" in business technology, he quickly grew into a production backbone of the team and won excellent results many times in technical and business competitions organized by the sub-bureau and the road bureau. I remember that during the preparation work for the first major speed-up, he worked hard with everyone on the scene, K-line chart, and during one replacement of severely damaged equipment while carrying the turnout heart, he sprained his waist due to excessive force. He lay on the gravel slope by the line for more than two hours, insisting on staying at the construction site until he directed everyone to complete the day's production tasks. Then he was carried back to the work area by his colleagues and reluctantly went to the hospital for treatment under the "rebuke" of the workshop leader, but refused to be hospitalized, insisting on leading everyone in the various tasks of the speed-up during the day and going to the hospital for electrotherapy during his spare time...
