DIDINI Warm Reminder: Handling a Baby's Fever

Baby fever is a common situation that parents often encounter. There are several different types of fever, and parents should adopt different treatment methods based on the different causes and types of fever. Extra attention should be paid to high fever, as persistent high fever can harm the health of children.

Types of Fever

Fever is a common symptom in pediatric diseases. Usually, an axillary temperature of 37-38°C in children is considered low-grade fever, 38-39°C is moderate fever, over 39-40°C is high fever, and over 41°C is hyperpyrexia.

Causes of Fever

◆ Infectious fever: This is the most common type and is a defensive response of the body to infection. Fevers caused by infectious diseases can be bacterial or viral.

◆ Non-infectious fever: Fevers caused by non-infectious diseases are also common, such as rheumatic fever, drug-induced fever, vaccine reactions, extensive burns, leukemia, hyperthyroidism, and hyperfunction of the adrenal cortex.

Purpose of Treating Fever

◆ Reduce high fever and reduce physical exhaustion

◆ Prevent babies from having febrile seizures

◆ For some critical cases (such as Japanese encephalitis, toxic dysentery, severe pneumonia, etc.), active antipyretic treatment helps stabilize the condition.

Avoid Misusing Antibiotics

Some parents immediately give their children antibiotics once they have a fever. This is actually unscientific. Antibiotics mainly kill or inhibit the growth of certain bacteria, but they are ineffective against most viral infections, let alone those infectious diseases. Therefore, patients with fever should go to the hospital for examination to identify the cause and take targeted medication. The misuse of antibiotics not only brings no benefit but may also lead to some toxic side effects and even "secondary infections," making the condition more serious. Additionally, a few people show a special reaction to certain antibiotics - drug-induced fever, which means taking antibiotics will cause a fever, worsening the condition.

Handling High Fever

◆ Physical cooling works quickly and safely, especially suitable for high fever.

Cold wet compress method: Use warm water-soaked towels or gauze and apply them to the baby’s forehead, back of the neck, both sides of the groin, armpits, and behind the knees. Change every one minute. Note that for babies with high fever, the water temperature should not be too cold; it should be slightly lower than body temperature.

Alcohol rub: Use alcohol to focus on wiping the above cold wet compress areas and limbs' skin, but avoid the chest and abdomen.

Cold saline enema: Infants use about 50-100 ml of cold saline solution, with the temperature around 28°C-30°C.

Warm water bath: Suitable for poor limb circulation (pale face, cold limbs). Water temperature 36°C-37°C, soak the baby using a large towel wetted in warm water or place the baby in warm water for 5-10 minutes, or extend the time according to the body temperature, dry the baby after completion.

When performing physical cooling, note: Take the temperature every 10-15 minutes, while observing changes in the baby’s breathing, pulse, and skin color.

◆ Give antipyretics, drink plenty of cool boiled water, add some salt and sugar to prevent dehydration.
