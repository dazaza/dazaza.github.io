by rrr68595 on 2010-05-27 10:51:06

It is said that hatred arises from love, the deeper the love, the deeper the hatred. Florentino Perez, the president of Real Madrid, has already held a press conference to appoint Mourinho as the new head coach of the team. However, Moratti, who was celebrating the Champions League title with Mourinho just a few days ago, is now taking a tough stance in negotiations with Real Madrid. Regardless, the most realistic thing for Moratti to consider might be to quickly find a suitable candidate for Inter Milan.

Perhaps Capello is the most suitable choice. He, like Mourinho, is filled with a desire for victory and also has a hard-line approach to discipline. The Milan Sports Daily insists there are at least three reasons why the champion iron coach Capello would answer "Yes" to Inter Milan's invitation.

Firstly, after England's friendly match on Monday, Capello said: "My name is always linked with Italian teams, it was Juventus before, and now they are talking about Inter Milan." These words seem to avoid any connection between himself and Inter Milan, but Capello left himself an out: "This suits me well, but I still want to clarify that I am currently only focused on England's World Cup matches."

In the view of the Milan Sports Daily, Capello's statement is sending a message to Moratti, implying that if Inter Milan wants to invite him, it is completely possible. However, this is a critical moment before the World Cup, and the English Football Association definitely does not want the issue of the head coach's departure to become an unstable factor for the team. According to previous reports, the English Football Association is closely monitoring the talks between Capello and Inter Milan. Therefore, the Milan Sports Daily believes that Capello is using this way to subtly convey his intention to Inter Milan.

The second piece of evidence listed by the Milan Sports Daily dates back to 2006, during Mancini's tenure at Inter Milan, when Capello had already reached a coaching agreement with Inter Milan. However, due to the outbreak of the phone-tapping scandal in Serie A, Capello had to go to Spain and re-coach Real Madrid. Capello, who is already 63 years old, has said that he regrets not having coached Inter Milan and hopes to take over at Meazza Stadium before he retires.

Capello and Mourinho are both coaches who are filled with a desire for victory and are constantly seeking new challenges. So far, Capello has led Milan, Juventus, and Roma, three Serie A powerhouses, to win league championships. If he can lead Inter Milan to dominate Serie A, then he will have the opportunity to achieve the grand slam of leading all four Serie A powerhouses to championship glory. In the past five years, Inter Milan has been invincible in Serie A, and this year they have even dominated the European stage. The Nerazzurri are indeed strong, and if Capello can take over from Mourinho and lead the team, Capello's Serie A grand slam or even defending the Champions League title is not a distant dream.

The new coach of Inter Milan has become the focus of attention in Italy. After expressing his longing for Mourinho, Ranieri also shared his views on Inter Milan's new coach, saying: "Inter Milan's new coach should be Capello or Hiddink. Both of their coaching performances are excellent, but the national teams they each coach probably won't let them go easily."

Indeed, as Ranieri said, Moratti needs to quickly determine the team's coach to start the summer transfer work, but England needs to ensure its performance in the World Cup. They definitely don't want Capello to end up with Inter Milan before the World Cup ends. If Capello doesn't come, Inter Milan has a second choice, which is Hiddink, the current coach of Turkey. Hiddink just signed with the Turkish national team a few weeks ago, and his agent said: "Hiddink's annual salary for coaching in Turkey is 3 million euros." The Italian media believe that Hiddink's salary is not a problem for Moratti, and Hiddink's situation is different from Capello's. Turkey did not make it to the World Cup, so it is acceptable for Hiddink to leave. If the Dutchman coaches Inter Milan, people like Fulham coach Hodgson could possibly take over his position in Turkey.
