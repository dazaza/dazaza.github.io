The SPA Paradigm of "One-stop Shopping" - SPA (Special Stores with Private Label Apparel), or "private label apparel specialty stores," refers to specialized retail shops that design, manufacture, price, and sell their own products directly to customers through their own retail outlets. In short, SPA is a business model that encompasses all functions from product planning to retail sales under one entity.

This concept has gained increasing attention in Japan. A typical example of a company pursuing the SPA concept is Fast Retailing. This company primarily targets Japan's younger generation, researching and designing styles that appeal to them. Through nearly a hundred production bases in places like Hong Kong, Shanghai, and Guangzhou in China, they mass-produce common casual wear such as trench coats, jackets, sweaters, woolen jackets, down jackets, and jeans. These items are sold at low prices in over 550 branches across Japan, making it one of the fastest-growing companies in recent years.

The entire process from production to sales is uniformly managed by one company, saving on waste in the middle stages and achieving cost reduction. This type of business model is known in the primary wholesale industry as manufacturing retailing. In the field of casual fabrics, American companies like GAP are the most famous. In fact, Fast Retailing itself has a relatively short history; originally, it was a retail company that purchased goods from primary wholesalers and trading companies. In 1996, President Tadashi Yanai set the goal of "becoming number one in the casual clothing industry" and decided to transition to the SPA model to catch up with advanced companies like GAP.

Initially, Yanai considered opening stores with local characteristics similar to The Limited or GAP in the U.S., such as the Uniqlo Taobao flagship store. The first UNIQLO store opened in a small alley in Hiroshima, followed by branches in suburban areas of Okayama and Shimonoseki. At the time, it was the peak of the DC brand era, and most of the customers frequenting UNIQLO stores were older people or entire families.
