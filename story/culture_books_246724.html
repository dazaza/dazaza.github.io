"The Forgotten Golden Age" - The Eighteen Years Under the Reign of Ming Xiaozong

by jqrxass on 2009-10-12 16:18:53

This was a period of prosperity forgotten by history.

It was sandwiched between the dark years of the Chenghua era of the Ming dynasty and the absurd times of the Zhengde era, yet it brought the Chinese a life that was sufficiently happy and memorable. This era was the eighteen years of the Hongzhi reign.

It was an era of national peace and people's well-being: the fires of war at the border had been extinguished, the inland economy developed, the people lived in peace and contentment, and officials were honest and self-disciplined. Before it, there was the absurdity of Emperor Chenghua Zhu Jianshen, the rampant corruption of officials, the chaos caused by eunuchs meddling in politics, the reality of the national economy on the brink of bankruptcy, and the suffering caused by natural disasters ravaging both the north and south of China...

Who, with what kind of magic, changed all this?

It was a man named Zhu Youchang, known as "Ming Xiaozong," whose era name was "Hongzhi," and he ushered in a great era of prosperity.

Chapter One: Conspiracy and Love - The Path to Crown Prince of Ming Xiaozong Zhu Youchang

Emperor Yingzong was captured and taken to the Mongolian Plateau to gnaw on raw mutton. Someone had to clean up the mess. With the emperor captured, the enemy at the doorstep, we certainly couldn't emulate the Southern Crossing of the Song Dynasty! Fortunately, his younger brother took over to clean up after him; Brother Zhu QiYu inherited the throne and changed the era name to Jingtai, finally bringing an end to the troubling Zhengtong reign. The Jingtai Emperor trusted capable minister Yu Qian, successfully organizing the defense of Beijing and defeating the enemy. Then through diplomatic pressure, he forced the Oirats to release the Yingzong Emperor back to serve as the Grand Emperor, thus avoiding the fate of Emperor Huizong dying far from home. After all the turmoil, not only did they nearly lose the ancestral lands, but also the imperial throne itself. Such a farce can't be blamed on others.

Chapter Two: The Reforms of Hongzhi - The Road to Restoring Order in the Hongzhi Era

As the saying goes, when the tree falls, the monkeys scatter. More than two thousand so-called "commission officers" were subsequently dismissed, Li Zisheng was arrested, Liang Fang was arrested too. Some were expelled, some imprisoned — in short, no more room for swindling.

Why start with them? First, these people had notorious records, causing immense public and official resentment. After Zhu Youchang ascended the throne, the first to be impeached by censors were precisely these individuals. Second, these people were parasites under the royal power system, without deep roots. In short: those who most deserved punishment and were easiest to punish — if not you, then who else should be sacrificed as a warning?

Chapter Three: Golden Spears and Iron Horses - The Record of Wars During the Hongzhi Era

The situation was already humiliating enough, but looking at the entire process of the coup d'état, it was even more humiliating — all because of marriage.

Hanshen wanted to marry, which wasn't unusual in itself, and the impending enfeoffment was doubly joyous. Moreover, after decades of hard struggle, he deserved a bit of enjoyment. However, the bride's identity was rather special: she was the daughter of the leader of Turpan.

Turpan and Hami were sworn enemies; it was they who once occupied Hami, driving out Hanshen's family to live off the wind. Although Hanshen had since reclaimed Hami, Turpan still held considerable power and continued to have designs on Hami.
