I love you, but it was just once upon a time.

Deleting your number from my contact list, we will no longer have any intersection unless something unexpected happens. Anyway, you haven't taken the initiative to call me for a long time, or even send me a text message. So, that's all. That's it, I don't love you anymore.

I won't catch your voice in the noisy crowd anymore.

I won't chase after your figure in the busy streets anymore.

I won't carefully consider my words, actions, or even expressions when you appear anymore.

I won't be moved by you anymore.

You've never found out that when I called your name, it was the most gentle moment for me, and my voice even trembled slightly; when I passed by you, I always took light and slow steps; I dared not look straight into your eyes, fearing that I would reveal too much emotion; when I daydreamed unconsciously, the draft paper was full of your name, and I could write your name more fluently than mine; when we parted ways, my mind was full of your figure, and my memories were only about you bit by bit; whether I was eating, sleeping, shopping, or spacing out, I was always thinking about what you were doing, and whether you would think of me as much as I thought of you...

Such a pure and calm life, such a life centered on you, became rich because of memories, but withered because of reality. Distance lengthened the longing, but separated the meeting; time settled the unspoken love, making you so far away from me. Watching people around me one by one have their passionate romances, I think it's time to let you go. Memories are just for remembering.

Don't say that I can't stand loneliness, because I've been lonely for a long time; don't say that I'm fickle, because you've never belonged to me; don't say that I give up easily, because you didn't even give me a tiny response. Entanglement.

At first, she was like most women, a woman who liked to entangle. Every little clue could be inflated and extended by her into a tangled mess that he couldn't untie. Her entanglement made him annoyed, even desperate. He knew women liked to entangle, but he didn't know that their entanglement could be so crazy. At the beginning, he still maintained enough patience, explaining repeatedly and answering her sharp, meaningless questions. He remembered that before marriage, she was reading a book in his small house and accidentally found a photo of him and his ex-girlfriend from college. He originally wanted to burn the photo, but later thought it was just a memory and experience from his youth, so he kept it.

When she held up the photo, it felt like she was holding evidence. Before she could open her mouth, he spoke first, "Didn't I already tell you? I dated someone in college, had a girlfriend, and I didn't know you then." He preemptively blocked her words. But he completely underestimated her.

"I know you had a girlfriend. Since it's over, why do you still keep the photo?"

"Since it's an experience, there's nothing wrong with keeping it."

"Not keeping, cherishing. It's in a small box, isn't that cherishing?"

"The small box is just for preservation, it doesn't mean anything special."

"Did you secretly take it out and look at it often?"

"No deliberate looking."

"When you saw this photo, did you still have feelings for her? You know, those feelings?"

"Not strong feelings."

"So there are still feelings."

But their love was fresh and passionate, so when she entangled like this, although he was somewhat troubled, he still thought she was cute and pulled her over, stopping her endless entanglement with a kiss.

After marriage, she transformed from a girl who liked to entangle into a wife who liked to entangle.

Sometimes when he went out to socialize with friends, he would ask for leave on the phone first, and she would interrogate endlessly. A simple leave request would take ten minutes or more to get approved.

"Why are you going out to eat? What's the occasion for your gathering?"

"Who's going? Do I know any of them? I know a lot of your friends, how come you said some names I don't know? Tell me, who's going?"

"Where are you eating? About what time will you be back? Are there any girls?"

He answered all her questions one by one. After a round of questioning, he just felt exhausted but patiently answered until she was satisfied.

But this kind of entanglement was just the prelude. Since there was a prelude, there had to be an ending. Only after completing the entire set of actions could he finally settle down.

No matter how late he came back, she waited for him. When she saw him come in, her eyes lit up, and her line of questioning became crystal clear.

"Didn't you say you'd be back before twelve? Look at the time now!"

"Why didn't you answer my phone call? What do you mean you couldn't hear? Didn't you say you put it on vibrate? It was shaking loudly on the coffee table, but you didn't feel it on your waist or stomach?"

"You said Zhang Bo went too, but he called home tonight. Why did you lie? Who did you go with?"

He was very tired after spending the whole night outside and wanted to sleep quickly, but he had no choice but to endure and deal with her entanglement.
