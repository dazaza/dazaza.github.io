How to give gifts

by iu7dsf3wl on 2010-05-27 09:43:30

1. Understand others' tastes. Remember that giving gifts is not about making yourself happy, but rather bringing joy to others. Any item that doesn't align with the recipient's preferences should not be chosen as a gift.

2. Avoid ulterior motives. Gifts with hidden meanings or implications should be avoided as much as possible.

3. New is always better. No one likes receiving second-hand items as gifts.

4. Don’t buy "useful" gifts. This advice particularly applies to men who only know how to buy household items for the women they love. Practical gifts lack imagination and thoughtfulness. Remember, you're giving a gift to a person, not a household.

5. Regardless of whether your gift costs 3 yuan or 300 yuan, always remove the price tag first. Giving a gift with a price tag seems like reminding the recipient how much it cost. Are you expecting something in return? Or are you trying to make an equal exchange, getting value for money? Generally, attaching a price tag to a gift is considered impolite. For you, who wants to express heartfelt feelings, it's also unwise.

6. Carefully select the packaging. A gift differs from personal use; good content is important, but good presentation adds even more charm. The principle of giving gifts is to choose the prettiest packaging possible.

7. Relatively speaking, giving gifts to women is always simpler. Women have many small things in their lives that can all be good choices for gifts. The feminine nature of women, including their daughterly, wifely, and motherly traits, can all be catered to when giving gifts.

8. Even so, giving women clothing remains an unwise move. Not only are color and style preferences difficult to guess due to individual differences, but the key obstacle is size—too small is problematic, and too large can cause annoyance: "Am I really that fat?"

9. Grasp the right timing for giving gifts. There are many opportunities to give gifts. We can only give someone a birthday gift once a year, but nothing stops us from giving "non-birthday" gifts at other times. For a long time, non-birthday gifts have been responsible for strengthening relationships, comforting hurt hearts, and repairing broken friendships.

10. Like the gifts you send. If you don't even like a certain gift yourself, then you shouldn't let others receive such a gift.

11. Be creative. If you need to give a gift to someone who isn't interested in anything or lacks nothing, that’s truly the most troublesome situation. You might consider inviting him/her to a highly attractive performance.

12. Select more practical gifts based on the recipient's profession.

13. Assess a reasonable price. A gift should not be offensive because it's too cheap. In fact, the value of a gift should be measured by the relationship between you and the recipient to avoid any awkwardness.

14. Personalized gifts. Homemade gifts are one-of-a-kind in the world and will express your thoughts.

15. It is said that ties and belts should not be given to men unless you have an intimate relationship with them, as these items symbolize wanting to tie down the other person. Similarly, giving necklaces to women may not be appropriate, let alone rings.

16. Balance between practicality and impracticality. For refined individuals, a book might be better than anything else, but there are fewer and fewer such people nowadays. However, gifts are still gifts, and they shouldn't be overly practical.

17. Avoid fresh goods. Even if you're gifting a cooking enthusiast, it's best not to give poultry, fish, meat, or vegetables due to difficulties in preservation. Their characteristic of being used immediately for cooking and eaten straight away diminishes their meaning as gifts. By the way, when giving nutritional supplements, food, or cosmetics, pay attention to their expiration dates to avoid awkward situations.
