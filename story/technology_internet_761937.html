Nokia Reveals the Real Reason for Loving Microsoft

by geekzhang on 2013-05-16 17:19:01

An article by Duncan Madden was published on the online news site Mashable on Wednesday. The author revealed the true reason why Nokia is so fond of Microsoft's Windows Phone operating platform through an interview with Chris Weber, Nokia's Executive Vice President of Sales and Marketing.Given that Nokia's newly released flagship smartphone, the Lumia 925, has been well-received, we had a 15-minute chat with Chris Weber, Nokia's Executive Vice President of Sales and Marketing. We focused on the issue of Nokia's future direction.Weber naturally responded that the future lies with the Windows Phone camp. However, why would Nokia, in its time of life or death, bet everything on the mobile system that people mock the most?The reason seems simple: every employee at Nokia, from developers to executives, firmly believes that Windows will provide consumers with the most coherent and forward-looking services.Weber said, "The problem we face now isn't the product itself. We know that when people get their hands on a Lumia phone, they like the Windows interface and Live Tiles. These features are our distinctive marks compared to other phone manufacturers. The key lies in execution and attention to detail—how to make our collaboration with Microsoft more vibrant, making people realize that Windows phones offer another choice besides Android and iOS."Weber also explained why he is optimistic about Microsoft: "There are two reasons we have placed our bets on Microsoft. First, we feel we can stand out, so we engage in close cooperation, especially in imaging display. Second, Microsoft has the most extensive ecosystem, covering phones, tablets, PCs, and Xbox. Nokia calls this a digital lifestyle, which includes many aspects such as Microsoft cloud service SkyDrive, Bing search engine, Xbox gaming device, and Skype. All these products can integrate together, which is why we are very pleased to be in the same trench as Microsoft."In this regard, Weber's analysis makes sense. Microsoft's ecosystem is deeply established and well-developed. Nokia is responsible for creating the best Windows Phones. However, from previous news reports, it seems that Nokia's Windows Phones aren't favored. Consumers believe that Windows Phones are a tier below Android and Apple, and they don't think Nokia can stage a major comeback with Windows Phones.However, Weber claims that this situation is changing. Weber said, "We are making progress. In the fourth quarter of 2012, we sold 4.4 million Lumia phones globally, and in the first quarter of 2013, this type of product reached 5.6 million units. In the second quarter of this year, we are growing even faster. Compared to Apple selling 47.8 million iPhones in the first quarter of 2013 and Android capturing 75% of the market during the same period with 156 million units sold, you can imagine that Nokia still has a long way to go." Weber continued, "We do want to move faster, so we launched the Lumia 928 and Lumia 925. We have provided new models to all the major U.S. carriers—for example, the Lumia 920 exclusive to AT&T, the Lumia 928 exclusive to Verizon, and the Lumia 925 exclusive to T-Mobile. We are excited and will absolutely be committed to the development of Windows."Regarding the target audience, Weber's response seemed uncertain. However, Weber first extolled the imaging technology of Nokia phones extensively, indicating that Smart Camera is the core competitiveness of the Lumia 925, before returning to the main topic: "What we need to do is open up an entirely new market, giving consumers different choices. We see consumers switching to Lumia 920 because of the imaging software, and I believe the same will happen with the Lumia 925." However, the image processing software that Weber considers a selling point is not exclusive to Nokia; other users can download it as an app. Despite such insufficient evidence, Weber appeared calm.Weber said, "What we need to do is help people understand the significance of truly integrating phones, PCs, and XBOX. If we look at the Net Promoter Score (NPS), we find that Lumia phones rank at the top among Nokia products. However, we still have a lot of work to do—not just creating great products but making them significantly impact our lives."Although Nokia's path to recovery is still long, seeing the passion of their employees and some quality products like the Lumia 925 recently launched, I cannot yet overlook Nokia's existence.
