IBM's third quarter results were poor, and CEO Ginni Rometty is reshuffling the management team.

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 11:20:03

Due to the weak performance in the third quarter, IBM CEO Virginia Rometty has started making adjustments within the executive ranks.

According to reports, in an internal memo addressed to IBM employees, Rometty specifically criticized the growth markets department, which includes developing countries in Southeast Asia, Latin America, and the Middle East. In Q3 of this year, the revenue from this department fell by 9% year-over-year. In the same period, IBM's revenue from the BRIC countries (China, Brazil, India, and Russia) dropped by 15%, while its revenue from the Chinese market plummeted by 22% year-over-year.

Reports indicate that to boost fourth-quarter results, Bruno Di Leo, IBM's sales head, will take charge of the growth markets department; James Bramante, currently the senior vice president and head of the department, will be reassigned to another department. As of now, an IBM spokesperson has not commented on these reports.

For the fiscal quarter ended September 30, 2013, IBM's net profit was $4.041 billion, up 5.7% from $3.824 billion in the same period last year; diluted earnings per share rose 10.5% to $3.68 from $3.33 in the same period last year. IBM's revenue for the third quarter was $23.72 billion, down 4.1% from $24.747 billion in the same period last year. On a non-GAAP basis, IBM's adjusted earnings per share for the third quarter were $3.99, exceeding analysts' expectations, but its revenue fell short of forecasts. According to FactSet, analysts had on average expected IBM's third-quarter earnings per share to be $3.96 with revenue at $24.79 billion.

Jim Cramer, a well-known host on CNBC, stated during his Thursday show, "I consider IBM's performance as a disaster. The company seems unaware of how dire the situation is."

On Thursday, IBM's stock price fell $11.90 or 6.37% in regular trading on the New York Stock Exchange, closing at $174.83. Over the past 52 weeks, IBM's lowest stock price was $172.57 and its highest was $215.90. During intra-day trading on Thursday, IBM's stock price hit a new 52-week low of $172.57. Since the start of the year, IBM's stock has declined by 9%, significantly underperforming compared to the S&P 500 Index's gain of 21.51% over the same period.
