Extreme heat weather, there are good ways to cool down your car!

"Really hot! I can't breathe!" In the scorching sun, many car owners must have such feelings. Every time you open the car door, a wave of heat will rush towards you. The high temperature of forty to fifty degrees Celsius makes the inside of the car like an "oven", and you still have to crawl into this box like a "steamed bun". At that moment, you truly understand what it means to be unbearably hot. Therefore, in order to avoid such suffering, car owners try all kinds of "remedies". However, some of these methods seem reasonable at first glance, but they actually cause great harm to the car, so be sure to use them with caution.

Misconception One: Cold Water "Bath"

Case: As long as Mr. Wu, the car owner, has a chance, he will give his car a "cold water bath". He thinks this is the best way to cool down the car under the blazing sun. "When people feel too hot, they take a cold shower, and the heat dissipates immediately. So whenever the car temperature is very high, if conditions permit, I will find cold water to give my car a cold water bath." Mr. Wu told reporters.

Expert Suggestions: The method of cold water "bath" is not advisable. Just like people should not take a cold shower immediately after intense exercise. Metal parts in cars have the property of thermal expansion and contraction. After being exposed to the sun, the surface temperature of the car paint is very high. If you rinse it at this time, the paint surface is easily damaged, and there may be paint peeling.

In addition, do not splash cold water to cool down after high-speed driving. Because at this time, the engine and tires are overheated, which will directly affect the service life of the engine, and will inevitably cause changes in the internal structure of the tire due to sudden cooling, causing uneven deformation of each layer of cord fabric inside the tire body, leading to early tire damage, or even delamination of the cord fabric.

The correct method is to check whether the coolant is sufficient before driving, and check whether the thermostat is normal. Always pay attention to the readings on the water temperature gauge while driving, which should not exceed 95°C. When the temperature is too high, to strengthen the cooling airflow, you can lift the engine hood for better ventilation and heat dissipation.

Misconception Two: Alcohol Cooling

Case: Miss Liu learned a trick online to cool down using alcohol, which is mixing medicinal alcohol and water in a ratio of 1:10 and putting it into a bottle with a spray head. Whenever the car is exposed to the sun, she sprays it inside the car before driving, then turns on the air conditioning. "This method cools down quickly, it only takes one or two minutes for the temperature inside the car to drop. Alcohol evaporates easily, which can accelerate the evaporation of water. The evaporation of liquid is a heat-absorbing process, so it can quickly lower the temperature inside the car." Miss Liu told reporters with confidence.

Expert Suggestions: Although this cooling method can achieve a certain effect, it poses a significant safety hazard. Because alcohol vaporizes into flammable liquid, after cooling down, due to the small space inside the car, the heat exchanged out is still inside the car, and alcohol easily adheres to the seats. When it reaches a certain concentration and the temperature inside the car is too high, it is relatively easy to ignite spontaneously. Moreover, when spraying alcohol, you also need to consider the quality of the alcohol. If the alcohol is not up to standard, it can damage the interior. Also, after spraying, the smell of alcohol inside the car is quite strong. If you encounter a police spot check, it may lead to a misunderstanding of drunk driving. Therefore, it is recommended that car owners not frequently use this method. When the temperature inside the car is really too high, it is recommended to use car-specific spray cooling products.

Misconception Three: Dry Ice Coolant

Case: Due to the unbearable heat, Mr. Zhu heard about a high-tech product called car coolant on the market, so he bought a can. But unexpectedly, this thing suddenly "exploded". "Although no serious accident occurred, it just blew through the cushion on the back seat, it was still quite scary." Mr. Zhu told reporters with lingering fear.

Expert Suggestions: Many of the special coolants on the market now are not high-tech products. In fact, their main component is dry ice. Dry ice is solid carbon dioxide, commonly used as a refrigerant, which absorbs heat from the environment and lowers the surrounding temperature.

However, dry ice evaporates easily and also vaporizes when exposed to the sun and high temperatures, releasing gas that is 1000 times larger in volume than the original solid dry ice. Once the container cannot withstand this pressure, it is prone to "explosion". Therefore, when using this car coolant, avoid placing it in direct sunlight or places above 50°C. It is best placed in the trunk or below the car seats. Do not place it near the windshield to avoid shaking, vibration, or exposure to the sun, to prevent explosions. When spraying, note that it should not be sprayed directly onto glass or leather furniture to avoid water ingress or cracking caused by temperature differences.

Misconception Four: Removing the Air Intake Grille

Case: Recently, Mr. Zhou saw that many taxis had removed the front air intake grille. Later, he found out that removing the air intake grille helps with heat dissipation. "Removing the air intake grille and the engine bottom plate in front of the car helps with engine heat dissipation and can play a certain role in cooling."

Expert Suggestions: Theoretically speaking, removing the air intake grille and the engine bottom plate may play a certain role in engine heat dissipation, but it is not very practical. Because the normal operating temperature of the engine is around 100 degrees Celsius. Engine heat dissipation does not solely rely on forced heat dissipation of the cooling control system. Simultaneously, during driving, the airflow facing the radiator fins and the front engine compartment is also a pathway for heat dissipation.

Misconception Five: Removing the Thermostat

Case: Mr. Gong recently specially removed the car thermostat due to the hot weather, thinking that doing so could moderately reduce the water temperature and allow the car to cool down.

Expert Suggestions: Removing the thermostat can alleviate the phenomenon of excessively high water temperature to a certain extent, but this practice is not advisable and unscientific. Especially for electronically fuel-injected vehicles, because the thermostat can help warm up the engine quickly. If the water temperature is too low, it will lead to increased fuel consumption, and cold starts will exacerbate wear, affecting emissions and reducing engine power. The function of the thermostat is to automatically adjust the amount of water entering the radiator according to the temperature of the coolant, changing the range of water circulation, regulating the cooling capacity of the cooling system, ensuring that the engine operates within a suitable temperature range.

Trick One: Use External Circulation to Cool Down Inside the Car

Expert Suggestions: For high-temperature situations inside the car, the correct approach is to first turn on the external circulation, set the airflow to the maximum position, direct the airflow downward, and simultaneously open all windows and the sunroof. Since hot air has a smaller density, when cold air from outside enters, the hot air inside the cabin can escape through the windows and sunroof, achieving the purpose of rapid cooling. After continuing this for a few minutes, the temperature inside the car will quickly drop to a level tolerable by humans, allowing the driver to consider entering the car and driving for a while. During driving, the airflow outside the car circulates faster, and the air pressure is lower. The hot air inside the car will also dissipate quickly, achieving the purpose of rapid cooling. Then, you can close the windows and sunroof, adjust the appropriate airflow, or switch to internal circulation, entering the normal air conditioning usage state.

Trick Two: "Dress and Hat" Helps Cool Down Your Car

Expert Suggestions: Although the method of "dressing and wearing a hat" generally does not provide quick cooling effects, it is still effective for vehicle maintenance and reducing indoor temperatures. Actually, the best cooling method is sun protection. You can use car sun protection and shading tools, which can lower the temperature inside the car by 40°C, making passengers not feel stuffy or scorching. It can also prevent sun exposure and the accumulation of heat inside the car from damaging car instruments, meters, and decorations. You can also use heat-insulating sun-blocking car covers, place sunshades, install car curtains, etc., to protect against the sun. Additionally, tinting the car windows can prevent the air inside the car from being heated by sunlight. Good tinting can prevent more than 70% of light (including ultraviolet rays) from entering.
