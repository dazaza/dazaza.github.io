Do you know, beauty-loving you: pregnant women must use pregnancy-specific skincare brand products for skin care!

Beauty is the nature of women, and expectant mothers during the pregnancy period are no exception. For expectant mothers with low immunity during pregnancy, how should they choose skincare products? Recently, Miss Wang, an expectant mother, has been troubled by one question: what kind of skincare products can be both effective and safe for pregnant women to use? In fact, due to the accelerated metabolism of expectant mothers, coupled with special reactions during pregnancy, skin conditions can easily deteriorate at this time. For example, rough skin, excessive oil production or dryness, pigmentation, and the appearance of stretch marks may occur. However, many expectant mothers also worry that the chemical components in adult skincare products could harm their babies, so they choose not to use anything at all. Is this practice effective? What issues should expectant mothers pay attention to when it comes to skin care? They should try to choose gentle skincare products. "Since there are many categories of skincare products and their functions are not entirely the same, basic skincare products that simply provide basic maintenance can be used. For example, non-irritating, fragrance-free, and gentle moisturizing lotions, etc. However, some spot-removing skincare products need to be treated with caution. Although they can achieve spot-removal results in the short term, the mercury and lead in them, once absorbed through the skin into the body, will pose a threat to fetal development," said Dr. Cao Mei from the dermatology department of the hospital. "Moreover, prevention of hormones is also very important. Many beauty salons offer hormone-based beauty services. Although the results are good when used, such practices can easily lead to hormone-dependent dermatitis, which pregnant women should avoid."

So, can pregnant women use cosmeceutical skincare products? "In general, the formulas of cosmeceutical skincare products are fully disclosed, and all active ingredients and safety have been proven by medical literature and dermatological clinical tests. They do not contain allergens, pigments, fragrances, preservatives, and other harmful substances recognized by the public. Therefore, to a certain extent, they can be used by pregnant women. However, when purchasing, attention should be paid to choosing products certified by national quality standards," said Cao Mei. In addition, some dermatologists suggest that pregnant women should not use foundation, powder, eyeshadow, lipstick, and other cosmetics because they contain more pigments and heavy metals, which can easily harm the health of pregnant women. If there are special circumstances where cosmetics must be used, Cao Mei advises expectant mothers: "Choose cosmetics specifically designed for pregnant women, such as plant-based cosmetics with gentler mechanisms, and the duration of cosmetics on the face should be minimized. Additionally, when using lipstick, expectant mothers should wipe it off before meals to avoid unnecessary trouble."

For the sake of the little baby in their belly, pregnant women should try to choose relatively gentle skincare products so as not to harm the little baby.
