Spanish media expose Manchester United's plan to form an invincible trident, with the Red Devils also eyeing a prodigy who has made a name across Europe.

by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-09 09:29:10

Spanish media reports have exposed Manchester United's desire to form an unstoppable trident, with the Red Devils also targeting a prodigy renowned across Europe. According to Sina Sports, Ferguson and Wenger are set for another battle, this time in the transfer market rather than on the pitch. Both managers have shown interest in Belgian midfield genius Dede, but which is more appealing: the lure of the Red Devils' trophy cabinet or the Gunners' tradition of nurturing young talent?

In fact, Ferguson has long expressed interest in Standard Liège captain Dede. Back in October, the manager personally wrote a lengthy letter of condolence — sent to Standard Liège and then passed on to Dede — wishing him a speedy recovery and stating that "as long as you work hard to return to the field, I will continue to closely monitor your performance and maintain contact with Standard Liège."

At 21, Dede is a complete midfielder, excelling both offensively and defensively with excellent passing, vision, and technical ability. As Scholes grows older and contemplates retirement, finding his successor has become Ferguson's top priority. The personal letter to Dede shows the manager’s forward planning.

On the other hand, Wenger has also been linked with Dede, whose youth and potential fit the professor's standard recruitment model. However, Dede is still recovering from a fracture and is expected to return to action in February next year, making a winter transfer unlikely. Next summer will be the opportune moment for Wenger and Ferguson to compete for his signature.

A source told the Daily Star: "Steven (Dede) would be delighted to play in England. Several clubs are pursuing him, but it all depends on how well he performs after his comeback and whether he can regain his best form." Ferguson's personal letter is clearly an emotional appeal, but Wenger also has Dede's compatriot Vermaelen in his squad, whose success at the Emirates could sway Dede.

In addition to sparring with Wenger, Ferguson also faces competition from Chelsea boss Ancelotti, with their shared target being Atletico Madrid forward Agüero.

For the working-class team of Atletico Madrid, it is becoming increasingly difficult to hold onto the superstar Agüero. Rumors of Agüero considering a move away from La Liga have grown louder. The Daily Mail reported that according to Spanish media, Chelsea has made two bids for Agüero, both rejected by Atletico. Mourinho’s Inter Milan also has strong interest in Agüero, but the £55 million buyout clause has deterred Mourinho. Now Ferguson has joined the race for Maradona's quasi-son-in-law.

This summer, Manchester United sold Cristiano Ronaldo to Real Madrid for £80 million, but Ferguson barely touched the funds. Spanish media even speculated that the manager has the means and intention to build a super attacking trident of Villa, Agüero, and Rooney.

According to the Chronicle Evening Gazette, Danny Simpson, the Manchester United defender on loan at Newcastle, is likely to extend his loan deal. Since joining the first team in 2006, Simpson has only made three Premier League appearances for the Red Devils, leading to a series of loan spells at Antwerp, Sunderland, Ipswich Town, and Blackburn Rovers. Since August this year, the 22-year-old Simpson has been on loan at Newcastle. Originally, the loan was due to end in January, but now Simpson has become a key player for the Magpies and is adored by St James' Park fans. Newcastle has decided to extend his loan period. The paper predicts that Ferguson will also welcome this development.
