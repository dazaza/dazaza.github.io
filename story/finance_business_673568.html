Sixty-year-old man gets into dispute with villagers over tree and home foundation

by pstookia3 on 2012-02-23 17:10:43

Villagers turn against each other over broken promises

Recently, Gao was suspected of theft and desecration of a corpse and was approved for arrest by the Huairou District Procuratorate.

After regaining consciousness, Old Bao saw that his deceased wife's coffin was open, the lid leaning against the soil beside the grave. Her bones were exposed under the sun. Seeing his wife’s remains damaged, Old Bao felt immense sorrow. He found a mat at home to cover her body. When Bao’s son heard the news, he rushed to the scene and began picking up his mother's scattered bones from the dirt pile, but some parts of her skeleton were already missing.

Following the incident, the police attempted mediation between both parties. The Bao family requested that Gao wear mourning clothes and re-bury the unearthed remains, but Gao refused, stating that wearing mourning attire is a grand gesture reserved for one’s parents. He couldn’t bring himself to do it for someone else. Gao rejected the police's mediation, and the Bao family was enraged by his stance. Subsequently, the police criminally detained Gao on suspicion of theft and desecration of a corpse.

Dispute over Feng Shui location

However, when the chestnuts ripened again, another troubling event occurred involving the Bao family as Gao once again changed his mind.

According to Gao's confession, he did indeed dig up the grave because of a dispute with the Bao family over the ownership of six chestnut trees.

On August 8th this year, like any other day, Old Bao went to visit his wife's grave, only to find it had been disturbed. Recalling the moment, Old Bao said, “It felt like a bomb went off in my head.” Due to high blood pressure, he blacked out and collapsed under a chestnut tree near the grave, taking a while to recover.

In April 2009, Old Bao consulted a Feng Shui master who selected a specific plot of land. Part of this land included some of Gao's chestnut trees. Since the two families generally got along well, Old Bao approached Gao to negotiate an exchange of trees. Gao agreed to the swap, exchanging six of his trees on the chosen plot with six of Old Bao’s trees.

The Feng Shui master advised Old Bao that moving the grave should be done during Qingming Festival, which had already passed. The Bao family planned to wait until Qingming in 2010 to relocate the grave. However, in August 2009, Gao suddenly changed his mind. He believed that the chestnut trees provided by the Bao family produced smaller fruits, making less money compared to the six original trees he owned. Despite ignoring the three additional trees given by the Bao family later, Gao still felt he had lost out, saying, “I wouldn't feel good even if I lost a penny.”

Realizing the importance of the "Feng Shui treasure spot," Old Bao was reluctant to give it up. After much deliberation, he decided to sweeten the deal by offering three more trees. After receiving these additional trees, Gao promised not to raise the issue again.

This year, on September 20th, the case was transferred from the public security department to the Huairou District Procuratorate.

Currently, Gao has been approved for arrest by the Huairou Procuratorate on suspicion of theft and desecration of a corpse. According to Article 302 of China's Criminal Law, he could face up to three years in prison. Only after learning the severity of the consequences did Gao realize how foolish his actions were.

It is understood that Gao is 67 years old, childless, and economically disadvantaged. Besides selling chestnuts, he relies on a monthly low-income subsidy of 250 yuan to survive.

Police arrived at Gao's home to investigate and found part of Old Bao's wife's remains in the toilet of Gao's house, angering the Bao family immensely.

Huairou Procuratorate prosecutor Guo Xinghua stated that the suspect in this case is charged with theft and desecration of a corpse. Theft and desecration of a corpse are alternative charges, meaning either theft of a corpse or desecration of a corpse. Theft of a corpse refers to secretly stealing a corpse and placing it under one's control. Desecration of a corpse involves exposing, molesting, damaging, defacing, trampling, or otherwise violating the dignity of a corpse or harming the feelings of relevant individuals. The direct object violated by theft or desecration of a corpse is social morality, and the subjective intent is deliberate. Knowing it belongs to others yet intentionally causing harm can lead to various motives, none of which affect the determination of guilt.

Last year in April, Old Bao successfully moved his wife's grave, fulfilling a long-held wish.

If the charge is proven, the maximum sentence is three years.

It is understood that Old Bao's wife passed away early, and her remains were buried in the chestnut grove behind the village. A few years ago, after the production team disbanded, the chestnut trees were distributed among the villagers. Considering visiting graves would mean going onto someone else's land, Old Bao contemplated relocating his wife's grave.

Old Bao suspected Gao from the same village and reported the matter to the police. It was learned that Old Bao and Gao had previously disputed over the ownership of six chestnut trees near the grave.

Seeing another chestnut harvest season approaching, Gao issued an ultimatum to Old Bao, firmly refusing the tree exchange. In early August this year, Gao dug up the grave of Old Bao's deceased wife.

Gao's broken promise infuriated the Bao family. Old Bao questioned Gao, saying, “We've already exchanged the trees, and the grave has been moved. What do you want me to do now?” In the days that followed, Gao repeatedly proposed reclaiming the trees, all of which were strongly opposed by the Bao family. Each meeting ended in mutual dissatisfaction.

When the police arrived at Gao's home to investigate, they found part of Old Bao's wife's remains in the toilet of Gao's house, enraging the Bao family further.

■ Prosecutor's Commentary

On August 8th this year, in Bohai Village, Bohai Town, Huairou District, seventy-year-old Old Bao discovered that his deceased wife's grave had been dug up, with the bones scattered everywhere. Investigations revealed that the suspect was none other than Gao, a sixty-seven-year-old villager from the same village, who had thrown part of the remains into his own toilet. Gao took such action merely due to a dispute over a few chestnut trees.

If the charge is proven, the maximum sentence is three years.
