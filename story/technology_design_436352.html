Are there any regrets left from the kitchen renovation?

by qwy854627 on 2011-05-24 14:43:51

After the kitchen renovation is complete, how much regret do you have? More and more families are renovating, and if you encounter the same problems, I hope it can give you some reference.

Refrigerator and cabinets are not on the same horizontal line

When designing, only thought about reserving space for the refrigerator, but forgot to measure its width. So, except for changing the refrigerator, there's nothing else to do but endure it.

Too few shelves inside the cabinet

It’s correct to add a few more layers of shelves inside the kitchen cabinets.

Deviation in artificial stone countertop hole opening

From the hole position to the support plan, everything needs to be well-planned by yourself. Don't overly trust the wisdom of the workers.

Installing cabinets before the range hood causes trouble

The smaller things like this are easy to overlook. Homeowners should measure the size before installation and also refer to others' experiences.

Faucet gets dirty easily when turning it on or off

If you don't want to change the faucet, then you'll just have to clean more often.

Sink size is restricted

The spacing of the joists is relatively small, so later when buying a sink, it will be very frustrating as the size is restricted. Going to the building materials supermarket is too expensive, and going to the market results in poor quality. You need to consider the sink dimensions beforehand; the spacing of the joists determines the sink size.

Range hood looks good but isn't practical

The family chose an imported brand range hood, which has a beautiful appearance and full functions, but its smoke extraction ability is too weak. The flat design of the body is simple and fashionable. However, it is not suitable for Chinese cooking methods such as frying, stir-frying, steaming, and deep-frying because the smoke collector is too shallow or doesn't even exist, causing serious overflow and diffusion of unextracted smoke. To solve this problem, it's recommended to switch to a deep-hood range hood.

Unreasonable arrangement of the operation area

The water pipe and gas pipe are placed together against the wall, and for convenience, the stove and sink were installed tightly next to the pipes. The distance between the sink and the side wall is less than 10 centimeters, and it's right next to the stove, leaving almost no room for cutting boards or operations. To make a dish, you have to wash vegetables on the right side of the stove and then cut them on the left side, back and forth, making it particularly inconvenient. Renovating the kitchen must emphasize practicality. Not only should the operation area be reasonably arranged according to the operation process, but issues like countertop height, cabinet height and thickness, and light brightness should all be adjusted according to the specific needs of the homeowner.

Using inferior hardware

Within a year after the renovation was completed, the cabinet door fell off, and later it was found out that the quality of the hardware was too poor, but it was too late to regret. The hardware on the cabinets can be said to be the most important part of the overall kitchen, even more important than the cabinet panels or countertops. It directly affects the overall quality of the cabinets, so homeowners must be willing to spend money on high-quality hardware when customizing cabinets.

There are many other experiences worth learning from. For example, glazed bricks and high-gloss tiles in the kitchen are still relatively easy to clean, so never choose matte or rough surfaces.

Kitchen floor tiles must be anti-slip, and it's best to place a floor mat in the operation area.
