The Path of Wealth for Web2.0 Financial Visitors and Reviews on Accounting Websites

With the noisy boom of China's stock market this year, there has been a proliferation of stock-picking software and financial management concepts. In the Web 2.0 family, there is now a new kind of visitor — the "financial visitor" (or "caike"). As the name suggests, it refers to "online financial management" (of course, accounting is just one part of it).

A quick look at NewNew websites shows that there are quite a few financial visitors, such as Caike Online (www.coko365.com), China Accountant Network, Calculate and See, Mushroom Network, Share Network, Wallet Network, etc. Judging from their names, Calculate and See, Share Network, and Wallet Network have more of a Web 2.0 feel. On the other hand, Caike Online has the least appealing interface design among all the current accounting websites, but its accounting functions are currently the most powerful, distinctive, and best able to meet member needs.

Whether they are financial visitors, accountants, or sharers, these flashy names can only create temporary buzz. How to grow and strengthen the concept of financial visitors is a problem that all Web 2.0 financial management websites should consider:

1. User Issues: Regardless of what kind of financial visitor, they provide online accounting services. Is it anonymous bookkeeping (untraceable accounts) or real-name bookkeeping (clear accounts)? — Caike Online offers members the ability to freely set up either real-name or anonymous bookkeeping, allowing users to easily and conveniently record their finances according to their preferences.

2. Platform Issues: What core value services do they offer? Is it accounting or specific financial management? Is it a functional tool or interactive consulting? — Caike Online is primarily an online accounting software with a focus on functional tools, hence it boasts the most powerful features among all accounting websites, including many unique functions. On the other hand, websites like Wallet Network focus more on interactive consulting communities centered around accounting, so their accounting functionalities are relatively simpler, mainly focusing on community circles.

3. Business Model: Is it just for fun or long-term development? If it's for long-term development, there must be a corresponding business model. — Caike Online's main business model relies on advertising fees.

4. Team Issues: Do they have a core team to support technology and market needs? — Among all domestic accounting websites, 99% are operated by part-time teams. Caike Online, Wallet, Mushroom, etc., are all managed by part-time teams.

5. Funding Issues: Is the funding sufficient to sustain the project in a profitable state? — Caike Online and Wallet Network are preparing for early-stage financing.

The author briefly lists some advantages and disadvantages of current domestic accounting websites:

1. The most powerful accounting website: Caike Online (www.coko365.com)

2. The most distinctive feature accounting website: Caike Online was the first to introduce distinctive features such as multi-account functionality, transfer functionality, automatic accounting, instant query functionality, income and expenditure type sorting, project management, debt and creditor management, instant click-and-sort, unlimited level classification, etc.

3. The best-looking website in terms of interface design: Salt Sugar Record (www.ytji.com)

4. The worst-looking website in terms of interface design: Caike Online Accounting Website

5. The earliest accounting website in mainland China: Caike Online officially launched on February 10, 2006, and began market research in 2005.

6. The highest-ranked accounting website on Alexa: Caike Online, currently ranked around 220,000 on Alexa.

7. The accounting website with the highest member activity: Salt Sugar Record, Caike Online

8. The accounting website with the most effective market promotion: Caike Online, Wallet Network

9. The most creative accounting website: Caike Online (www.coko365.com)

10. The least creative accounting website: Calculate and See

11. The accounting website with the least creative functionality: Share Network
