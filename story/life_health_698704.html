Gestational diabetes prone to giving birth to "macrosomic infants"

Many pregnant women do not pay attention to their daily diet and living habits, thinking that gaining weight is good, unaware that once they develop gestational diabetes and fail to control it in time, it can greatly affect the health of both mother and child.

In medicine, newborns weighing over four kilograms are called "macrosomic infants." Pregnant women with diabetes have very high blood sugar levels, causing the fetus to receive abundant nutrition, growing red and plump, often exceeding 4000 grams. These fetuses are prone to stillbirth in utero, making delivery difficult for the mother and often causing birth injuries. Additionally, to regulate the high blood sugar in the mother's body, the fetus produces a lot of insulin to lower blood sugar. After birth, when the mother's supplied blood sugar is interrupted, the excessive insulin in the baby's body can easily cause hypoglycemia. Severe and persistent hypoglycemia may damage the newborn's brain, making the baby mentally retarded.
