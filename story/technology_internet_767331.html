Nokia's press conference on the 22nd: to launch phones, tablets, laptops

by anonymous on 2013-10-11 19:49:58

Prior to this, Nokia officially announced that it would host an event called "Nokia World" on October 22nd in the Emirate of Abu Dhabi. Today, Nokia has just released more details about this event.

According to reports, the event will begin at 11 a.m. local time in Abu Dhabi, and the global live streaming website address for the event was also announced: conversations.nokia.com.

In addition, in an official Nokia blog post, the company stated that after the live broadcast of this "Nokia World" event, Nokia will launch a second online video live stream on the same day, October 22nd, providing more in-depth details about the new products. This second online event will start at 4 p.m. Eastern Time and end at 6 p.m.

The original content of Nokia's official statement is as follows:

"The event will begin at 8 a.m. British time (11 a.m. Abu Dhabi time, 3 p.m. Beijing time), and the event will be streamed live worldwide. Those who cannot attend can choose to watch from home.

At the same time, after the conclusion of this event, we will additionally host a second live online event. This second event will feature more interactive elements and provide a deeper introduction to the products launched at the Abu Dhabi event. This online event also welcomes participation from media, bloggers, partners, and analysts around the world, and we are delighted to engage with everyone who cares about Nokia in real-time."

According to a recent report, Nokia is expected to unveil six new products at this "Nokia World" event, including the much-anticipated Lumia 1520 device with a 6-inch display. In addition, a Nokia tablet product with a screen size of 10.1 inches running the Windows RT operating system is also expected to be launched, and this product may be named Lumia 2520. Another report indicates that a new device codenamed Batman (Bat侠) will also make its debut at the event, and this coolly codenamed smartphone will be named Lumia 1320.
