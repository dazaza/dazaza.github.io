Here Are the Challenges for Apple in Seizing the Car's Center Stage

by anonymous on 2013-07-31 10:26:56

On July 30, according to foreign media reports, Steve Jobs once regretted that Apple didn't participate in the design of car products. Years later, Apple still hesitates about entering the automotive industry with its products. However, Microsoft, BlackBerry, and Pandora have become the three major competitors Apple must face when entering the in-car application market.

By the end of this year, consumers will be able to choose a few car models equipped with some features of the iPhone, including Siri voice navigation control, sending and receiving messages, emails, and music services. Compared to desktop computing and the smartphone market, Apple faces more challenges if it wants to surpass its competitors in the in-car applications and devices market. Apple has to deal with extreme temperatures, in-car noise, longer product usage cycles, and other issues.

Currently, car manufacturers are continuously catering to consumer demands and upgrading in-car devices within safe and controllable limits. As the world's highest-valued tech company, Apple undoubtedly hopes to take advantage of this trend and expand its product market. However, statistics from the market research firm IHS iSuppli show that BlackBerry QNX software systems and Microsoft are the main suppliers of in-car operating system software, while Pandora is the leading provider of in-car music streaming services.

Car manufacturers have found that many consumers use their iPhones as substitutes for in-car navigation systems. Therefore, they are striving to integrate Apple's technology into cars not only to reduce driver distraction but also to meet the growing consumer demand. Two small cars produced by General Motors, Chevrolet Spark and Sonic, have already incorporated Apple’s Siri voice technology.

Siri Needs Improvement

The two models mentioned above use Apple's iOS 6 operating system in the core audio area of the car's dashboard, eliminating the need for separate navigation devices.

Edmunds.com, a professional automobile data service website headquartered in Santa Monica, California, tested the Siri system installed in Chevrolet Spark. Ron Montoya, editor responsible for consumer advice at Edmunds.com, pointed out: "Apple's Siri does not perform satisfactorily. It performs well in some aspects, but personally, I think Siri's overall performance is unsatisfactory. It often fails to recognize my voice."

Montoya indicated that compared to the previous in-car systems used by car manufacturers, the operating system provided by Apple for General Motors' small cars was less satisfactory in navigation and other functions.

Research director Chris Schreiner from Strategy Analytics noted: "Siri is not an application specifically designed for in-car use because it is often noisy inside cars. Car manufacturers need to tune the audio system for each vehicle. Expecting Siri to achieve this is unrealistic." Schreiner had previously worked as an engineer for the OnStar wireless human-vehicle interaction system at General Motors.

Apple's Main Focus Area

Apple announced last month that the iOS 7 software would be fully integrated into vehicles produced by four major automakers: General Motors, Honda, Nissan, and Hyundai. Common icons on iPhones and iPads will also appear on the dashboards of these vehicles.

Apple CEO Tim Cook said during the earnings call on July 23: "Collaborating with the automotive industry is very important for Apple, proving what consumers actively desire. I believe Apple can uniquely achieve all this while overcoming other competitors. Therefore, this will be one of the company's key focus areas in the future."

Apple launched the Siri Eyes Free (hands-free) feature two years ago, and vehicles equipped with this function are just now hitting the market. Montoya pointed out that this means the concept of software integration with in-car devices proposed by Apple in June needs until 2015 to be realized.

Car manufacturers such as Honda, Hyundai, BMW, Daimler's Mercedes-Benz, Land Rover, Jaguar, Audi under Volkswagen, Toyota, and Chrysler will launch models equipped with the Siri Eyes Free feature this year.

Industry analyst Karl Brauer from the authoritative American car value evaluation media Kelly Blue Book stated: "For consumers, technologies like in-car infotainment and network access are becoming increasingly important. Apple knows that consumers want seamless product experiences."

However, in the early years of the 21st century, Apple wasn't very interested in directly collaborating with car manufacturers. Larry Dominique, executive vice president of the American car information website TrueCar Inc., said: "At the time, Apple focused on providing technical information to enable connectivity between iPods and iPhones so that consumers could play music on their iPhones, but Apple wasn't interested in anything else, let alone collaborating with car manufacturers." Dominique was formerly the product planning director for Nissan North America.

Microsoft and BlackBerry as Major Competitors

Jobs was passionate about cars. Mickey Drexler, Apple board member and CEO of American clothing retailer J.Crew Group, revealed last year that Jobs' dream before his death was to create a car product called iCar.

Cook, however, expressed that Apple's attitude towards the automotive industry has changed. He believes that in-car applications are part of Apple's ecosystem. He emphasized: "I attach great importance to embedding the iOS platform and Siri voice navigation service into car products. This is a part where the iOS operating system should play a role, just like how iOS plays a role in app stores, iTunes, all other content, and services."

In the in-car applications and devices market, Apple's competitors are not willing to give up the advantages they've established. Analyst Anna Buettner from IHS iSuppli, who studies in-car infotainment and human-vehicle interaction systems, pointed out: "BlackBerry's QNX and Microsoft are the two major competitors Apple must face. While Apple covets this big pie, Google Android has also prepared adequately to enter the in-car applications and devices market."

Pandora's Large Audience

Buettner indicated that Apple has a superior influence in music and entertainment but lacks capability in providing navigation and other core functions.

Among the new software products launched by Apple is iTunes Radio, which poses a significant threat to internet music streaming service providers like Pandora. Pandora announced last month that its music broadcasting service in the U.S. already has over 2.5 million driver listeners.

The annual revenue scale of the local broadcast advertising market in the U.S. reaches $15 billion, with approximately half of the radio music listening occurring in cars. Pandora's monthly active users have exceeded 70 million. Pandora stated that this year, one-third of all new cars sold in the U.S. market will be equipped with its music service.

However, due to automobile safety regulatory agencies and consumers viewing car manufacturers as responsible parties for vehicle defects, car manufacturers must exercise extreme caution when selecting new technologies.
