Scientific and technological innovation to achieve OLED

by ledlamp8 on 2012-03-03 23:45:26

OLED (organic light emitting display) is a technology where organic light-emitting semiconductor materials emit light under an electric field. It features an all-solid-state structure, self-luminescence, and does not require backlighting. Compared to LCD (liquid crystal), OLED has many advantages such as high brightness, high contrast, thin and lightweight design, short response time, wide viewing angles, low power consumption, good seismic performance, wide operating temperature range, and the ability to achieve flexible displays. The OLED industry is one of the most promising new display technologies and also a competitive hotspot in the international high-tech field. Tsinghua University started R&D on OLED technology in 1996 and established Visionox for production technology development and production line design and construction in 2001. Over the ten years of development, CIGNA Corporation and Tsinghua University have worked closely together through laboratory research, pilot technology development, and large-scale production across all processes. They have mastered key technologies in OLED materials, devices, processes, and driving mechanisms. In 2008, they independently designed and built China's first OLED mass production line, successfully transitioning OLED technology from the laboratory to large-scale production applications.

Through close cooperation with Tsinghua University and their own strong R&D teams, Cigna Company possesses powerful strength in OLED technology and continuous innovation capabilities. They successfully developed new electronic injection technology, breaking foreign patent restrictions. They were the first in the world to develop organic and inorganic compound technology, greatly improving device efficiency and lifespan. They successfully developed various monochrome, multicolor, and color OLED display products, reaching international advanced levels in performance. Transparent dot-matrix OLED products and double-sided displays are world-first OLED products that successfully resolved key technical issues such as high brightness, anti-electromagnetic interference, resistance to vibration, and resistance to mechanical shock. These products have been successfully applied in various fields.

The company built the first OLED mass production line, breaking the passive situation of relying on technology introduction in China's display industry. Their OLED products have been applied in consumer electronics, instrumentation, aerospace, serving both domestic well-known enterprises and being exported to international markets in Europe, South Korea, and Japan. In 2009, Tsinghua University and CIGNA Corporation jointly completed the organic light emitting display (OLED) materials, devices, and industrial projects, winning the highest award for scientific and technological achievements in China’s electronic information industry—the Chinese Institute of Electronics, Electronic Information Science and Technology Award.

As a new generation of flat panel display technology, OLED is in the early stages of industrialization but holds broad prospects for future applications. It can be used not only in the display industry but also as an ideal plane light source due to its large area, low power consumption, no flicker, softness, and flexibility. OLED also has a broad application prospect in energy-saving and environmentally friendly lighting and light sources. Depending on different driving methods, OLED is divided into two types: passive-driven OLED (PMOLED) and active-driven OLED (AMOLED). The development of small-size OLED represents the first stage of OLED technology industry development, which progresses in three directions: developing high-definition, ultra-thin, large-size AMOLED based on PMOLED; developing soft lights for the next generation of lighting; and developing low-cost printing preparation techniques and bending of flexible devices.

From the perspective of applications, current OLED products are mainly used in small and medium-sized mobile phones, MP3s, and other consumer electronics, industrial instrumentation, medical devices, automotive displays. As technology advances, the future application of OLED products will expand to computers, color TVs, large sizes, and lighting. Visionox simultaneously conducts R&D and industrialization of small-and medium-sized OLED technology and actively promotes the development of OLED cutting-edge technology. For flexible OLED displays, Tsinghua University and CIGNA are among the few units capable of preparing flexible OLED displays and flexible lighting devices. In AMOLED technology development and industrialization, CIGNA plans to achieve large-scale production of AMOLED within the next 2-3 years. In the lighting area, CIGNA has launched OLED decorative lighting products, lamps, and other lighting applications, planning to achieve mass production of OLED white lighting devices in 2-3 years.

To achieve the industrialization of scientific and technological achievements and encourage enterprise technological innovation, the comprehensive development of the company has always emphasized continuous innovation as the core competitiveness of Visionox's development. They believe that the focus of competition in the global OLED industry is still on technology, and the company must strengthen its awareness of independent innovation by mastering core technology to promote industrial development. During the development process, Visionox promotes enterprise technological innovation in several ways:

1) Continuously increasing efforts in OLED technology development and actively conducting research on the next generation of OLED technology. To deal with increasingly fierce market competition, Cigna strives to promote independent innovation and industrialization objectives while strengthening technology research and development. In the development of small-size OLED technology and industry, Visionox continuously advances innovative technology and product development in OLED flexible displays, transparent, two-sided, and large-size displays (product effects shown in Figure 2), striving to become the world's most competitive and important supplier of OLED products.

2) Creating an interdisciplinary R&D team. As a new display technology, OLED technology covers chemistry, materials, electronics, physics, and other disciplines, requiring multidisciplinary and technical backgrounds to solve facing issues. Tsinghua University and the promise of the OLED technology team focused on solving interdisciplinary problems from the beginning of the project, absorbing R&D personnel from different fields including chemistry, materials, and electronics, establishing an interdisciplinary R&D team and technical background.

3) Achieving technological breakthroughs through school-enterprise cooperation. The company and Tsinghua University established a long-term relationship, and the company also has its own R&D centers. By effectively integrating resources in colleges and enterprises, they effectively promote the industrialization of target technology research and accelerate the transformation of research results into industries.

4) Increasing efforts to protect independent innovation achievements. Since its establishment, Visionox has attached great importance to the protection of independent innovation achievements, setting up a special department for intellectual property rights, patents, and standards, including an intellectual property rights system. So far, Tsinghua University and Solutia Inc. have applied for more than 200 domestic and foreign patents (including more than 30 international patents), with 80 authorized. Tsinghua University and Solutia Inc. are important participants in OLED international standards and major makers of OLED national standards. As a new generation of flat panel following CRT (CRT) and LCD display technology, the OLED industry has great potential for future development.
