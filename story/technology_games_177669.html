A Guide to Grinding and Plot Progression in the New Area

by lk0gamee on 2009-07-16 13:44:22

Due to the change in the ring-running requirement to level ≥60, I will first discuss the storyline. Everyone rushing to the new area is busy leveling up, and forming a group of 5 people to pass together is really a waste of time. I recommend passing with 2-3 people being more appropriate.

Many new players have a misconception: the difficulty of storyline monsters is determined by the player's level. However, based on my experience helping friends through the storyline, the difficulty of storyline monsters should be fixed. Therefore, I suggest that players can handle the storyline before Jiǔ Yī (酒一) themselves. At level 40, I mixed with level 45 and brought a pet to solo all the way through Tiān Bīng (天兵), with all equipment being store-bought goods. As a main tank, you can save the money for hiring others to help through Tiān Bīng. Level 45 mixing can guarantee finishing Tiān Bīng.

Early players generally have low spirit power, so Jiǔ Èr (酒二) presents some difficulty. I recommend a team of one main tank with over level 50 + HS/PT/WZ. The Tang Dynasty only needs to mix, and let the pets concentrate their firepower to eliminate enemies one by one.

Yōu Míng Guǐ (幽明鬼) has a split skill, and his henchmen are all Di Fu (地府) monsters that can reduce maximum hit points. It's not recommended for players below level 50 to attempt this. I managed to pass with a WZ friend during the second week after the server opened, using two head-chopping Leo pets together, and mixing at level 55 was enough to hold them.

The next Xiā Shuāng (虾兵) and Shān Shén (山神) have difficulties comparable to Yōu Míng Guǐ. Mixing at level 55 can have an 80% success rate. As long as you can mix, I believe everyone can pass it no matter how they fight.

Xiè Jiāng Jūn (蟹将军) has significantly increased difficulty compared to before. At this point, your mixing ability should be leveled up to 60, and the main monster has a high-level counterattack skill. Tang players with not very high defense shouldn't rush to attack. Solve the small monsters first, then use a follow-up attack on the main monster later.

Liú Hóng Yī Lǐ Biāo (刘洪1李彪) has average difficulty, and mixing at level 60 can solve it. Just bring a pet that can deal AoE damage, pay attention not to get wiped out, and it should be quite safe. Any school plus Tang can pass without much issue.

Liú Èr (刘2) is the last BOSS of the Xuanzang storyline. Mixing should be leveled up to 65. Before mixing half of the monsters, it's recommended that your pet defends. Otherwise, if accidentally wiped out, you'll have to come back again. After finishing Liú Èr, you will have 13 storyline points, and those who make money by planting flags can take a break for now.

Next, many new players don't know how to choose between Xinmo and Sì Liú Lí (四琉璃) storylines. Personally, I prefer Sì Liú Lí because both storyline modes yield the same number of storyline points, but Sì Liú Lí only requires fighting female demons, Jin Liú Lí (金琉璃), Fān Tiān Guài (翻天怪), Yáo Tài Wèi (姚太尉), and Wáng Mǔ (王母), which are not too difficult. On the other hand, Xinmo involves more battles and also asks for Zui Sheng Meng Si (醉生梦死). If you just want to gain storyline points sooner, choose Sì Liú Lí. After finishing Wáng Mǔ, you will accumulate 20 points.

For Nǚ Yāo (女妖) and Jin Liú Lí (金琉璃), mixing should be leveled up to 70. There are many PT monsters among the henchmen. If you can't seal them in the shortest time possible, it won't be fun when they start attacking your upper limit. I believe that early on, everyone's body strengthening cultivation won't be very high, and low HP is a big problem. If it's really impossible, recruit a PT/HS to assist.

Fān Tiān Guài (翻天怪) is very easy to beat. They're all STL monsters. One mix is accurate, as long as your defense isn't too low, you can easily pass it.

It's time to fight Yáo Tài Wèi (姚太尉). Those heavenly soldiers are insane. It's recommended to level up mixing to 75. First, bring a wild pet to protect yourself, and call out the lions after mixing is ready. One Tang plus one sealing system or two Tangs can handle it without much issue. Afterward, you will have 18 storyline points and can buy and plant flags yourself.

Wáng Mǔ (王母) requires mixing at level 80 to subdue. The method is similar to Tài Wèi (太尉), except Wáng Mǔ uses Hellfire, so schools with low spirit like TG and WZ need to be careful.

I enjoy seeking excitement, so I single-handedly challenged Wáng Mǔ and the subsequent White Dragon at level 75 with full skills. At that time, I had a set of equipment crafted for level 70, with average attributes, equipped with 2-3 level gems, and only one special skill—Pò Xuè (破血)—on my helmet. My body strengthening was around 30, with no cultivation whatsoever, and I brought an 80-level lion. The difficulty wasn't great, but later on, I ran out of golden wound medicine and needed a friend to deliver some. I think as long as Tang players have average equipment, full skills and cultivations, and bring a pet capable of dealing AoE damage, they can single-handedly challenge most storylines. Why spend large sums of money inviting others to help or asking friends for assistance, wasting their time?
