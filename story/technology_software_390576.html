Mobile phone after-sales service staff splits 37 bricks in 52 seconds with bare hands (picture)

by wangniuniu on 2011-03-25 19:10:01

Deng Shaohua Performs Continuous Brick Splitting, Reporter Ren Jun Takes Photographs - The Thickness of His Right Palm, Long-Trained, is Almost Twice That of His Left

Crouching stance, gathering energy, and striking with his palm - one after another, bricks are split apart with just a single strike. Yesterday afternoon, amateur hard qigong practitioner Deng Shaohua demonstrated his signature skill to the Chongqing Evening News reporter.

Standing at only about 1.60 meters tall, kind-hearted, and speaking in a soft voice - if it weren't for his right hand thick as a bear's paw, no one would associate him with a martial arts master. He said that since the age of 15, he has been self-practicing the hard qigong technique "Iron Sand Palm." Now at the age of 42, he can effortlessly split bricks and crush hard peaches.

Live Performance

Splitting 37 Bricks Continuously in 52 Seconds

Deng Shaohua was born in Beiting Village, Luohuang Town, Jiangjin District. In his youth, performers frequently came to his hometown to showcase their skills, sparking his fascination with the hard qigong act of splitting bricks with a single hand.

Deng Shaohua mentioned that there were no local masters to learn from, and his family could not afford for him to travel elsewhere to study martial arts. So, he mimicked moves from television shows, enduring countless injuries before finally developing a set of training methods. Several years later, he was able to split bricks and break stones.

Yesterday afternoon, under the witness of the Chongqing Evening News reporter, Deng Shaohua successively demonstrated splitting sugarcane with one hand, breaking peaches with the back of his hand, and shattering bricks with a single hand. With one strike, an intact piece of sugarcane became fragments, sugar water splashing everywhere; a peach placed in his palm turned into powder with a single strike; a solid brick suspended halfway on a stool broke cleanly in half without any support or pressure.

Deng Shaohua also invited a colleague to pass him bricks and asked the Chongqing Evening News reporter to time him. The remaining 37 red bricks were all split into two pieces by his continuous strikes. It took 52 seconds to split all 37 bricks.

Deng Shaohua said that due to the slow speed of his colleague passing him the bricks, time was wasted. Under normal circumstances, he can split a brick in less than a second on average.

Deng Shaohua had a small wound on the base of his right palm that had just healed, so during the continuous brick-splitting demonstration, he wore gloves. He explained that he was concerned about the wound reopening from the continuous force.

Unusual Hands

Palm Thick as a Bear’s, Twice the Size of a Normal Person’s

Bricks split into two pieces were precious to Deng Shaohua because they still held value for practice and could be broken down further. Hard bricks, under Deng Shaohua's hands, were like tofu, easily cut any way he wished.

How did Deng Shaohua achieve such iron-fisted skills? He said persistence was the key. While practicing boxing at home, Deng Shaohua often warmed up with one-handed ground-strike push-ups. After moving to the city, worried about disturbing neighbors downstairs, he switched to doing regular push-ups with both hands off the ground. After completing the push-ups, he practiced finger strength by inserting two fingers into a steel bead-filled bucket 2000 times, then proceeded to practice palm strikes, striking bags filled with steel beads 1000 times each from three angles - front, back, and side.

Long-term practice left Deng Shaohua with some aftereffects. Now, his right palm and back muscles are extremely developed, his fingers, back of the hand, and palm are covered in calluses. Nearby colleagues jokingly referred to his hands as resembling "a bear's paw" from afar. The Chongqing Evening News roughly measured his right palm thickness at nearly 6 centimeters, while a normal person's is around 3 centimeters.

"Working in mobile phone after-sales service, I originally could have learned a technical skill, but my right hand isn't suitable for fine work, unable to repair phones," said Deng Shaohua. Normally, he wears gloves to cover his hands.

Common Events of the Extraordinary Man

Facing Provocation and Bullying, He Endures Without Retaliation

Currently, Deng Shaohua persists in practicing martial arts for about 2 hours daily. Colleagues Jin Bin and Han Wei, who live with him, said that every morning around 7 o'clock, the sound of Deng Shaohua practicing boxing serves as their wake-up alarm.

All of Deng Shaohua's colleagues are from the '80s and '90s generations. In their eyes, Deng Shaohua is a gentle, incredibly strong older brother. Jin Bin still remembers an incident where two company colleagues struggled to move a 250-pound safe for five or six meters. Passing by, Deng Shaohua lifted the safe with one hand, carried it on his shoulder, and walked away.

Deng Shaohua said that when he first arrived in the city, he endured provocation and bullying without retaliating. Firstly, fighting goes against martial virtue, and secondly, he feared causing serious injury with his powerful strikes.

Han Wei said that Deng Shaohua has never lost his temper with colleagues and treats customers with utmost respect.

The Iron Fist Skill Was Developed This Way

■ Every day starts with warm-ups, doing one-handed ground-strike push-ups.

■ After the push-ups, practice finger strength by inserting two fingers into a steel bead-filled bucket 2000 times.

■ Practice palm strikes by hitting bags filled with steel beads 1000 times each from three angles - front, back, and side.

Iron Sand Palm Brick Splitting

Can Easily Harm the Body

An unnamed person from the Chongqing Martial Arts community introduced that martial arts projects like "one-finger handstand", "two-finger handstand", and "Iron Sand Palm brick splitting" combine real skills with entertainment appeal, once sparking a wave of enthusiasm in many regions.

This individual mentioned that those who have mastered the "two-finger Zen" often have severely deformed thumbs; those practicing "Iron Sand Palm" often suffer from severely deformed palms, bone growth at the joints, full of calluses, and significantly swollen hands.

This individual reminded that skills like "Iron Sand Palm brick splitting" come at the cost of violating bodily norms, even damaging the body, making them unsuitable for promotion. If enthusiasts wish to practice, they should do so under professional guidance and complement the practice with medicinal adjustments.

Chongqing Once Hosted a Brick-Splitting Competition

Champion Split 1341 Bricks in One Hour

Last year, a 40-something water delivery worker in Xiamen achieved the record of splitting 60 bricks in one minute during a TV media interview.

However, this wasn't the most impressive feat. In 2002, five heroes representing Zhejiang, Chongqing, and Shandong teams broke the Guinness World Record for "Iron Sand Palm" brick splitting in Chongqing Chaotianmen. Among them, Lin Yi, representing the Zhejiang team, continuously split 1341 bricks in one hour with one hand. After the competition, all five competitors suffered from skin tears on their right palms, requiring hospital treatment.
