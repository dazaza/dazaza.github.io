Are there any side effects to dimpleplasty? Experts from BDL Plastic Surgery introduce that artificial dimples are formed through cosmetic surgery methods on the face, medically known as dimpleplasty. The position, shape, size, and number of dimples are mainly chosen based on the preferences of the beautification seeker, with the doctor only acting as an advisor to provide suggestions and opinions. Most people choose to have one dimple made on each cheek. Generally, the position of the dimple is determined at the intersection point of the parallel line passing through both corners of the mouth and the vertical line drawn from the outer corner of the eye.

Dimpleplasty is usually completed inside the oral cavity. First, methylene blue is used to mark the corresponding mucosa at the planned skin dimple point, followed by injecting local anesthesia and making a horizontal incision of about 0.5 cm in the mucosa. Then, using a blunt dissection method, the submucosal layer, muscle layer, and subcutaneous tissue are separated up to the deep dermis. Next, the needle enters from one side of the muscle layer, exits at the predetermined dimple point, then re-enters the dermis and travels a certain distance within the dermis before turning inward and exiting from the other side of the muscle layer in the incision, suturing the muscle and dermis together. The distance the suture travels within the dermis determines the size and shape of the formed dimple. Finally, the mucosal incision is sutured.

After dimpleplasty, no bandaging is required, but oral hygiene must be maintained by rinsing frequently. The intraoral sutures are removed after one week. The key point of the surgery lies in accurately and firmly suturing the dermis and muscle layer together so they adhere to each other; otherwise, the dimple will quickly disappear after the surgery. Initially, both dynamically and statically, the dimples can be seen, and generally, around one month later, they look very natural.

Dimples can be classified into three types: round, elliptical, and cleft-shaped. Most are distributed on the cheeks, with some also commonly found near the corners of the mouth, while relatively fewer are found on the zygomatic region or chin. Dimpleplasty should be performed at a regular plastic surgery institution, and postoperative care should be noted, generally without any side effects.
