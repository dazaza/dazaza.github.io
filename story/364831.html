◆Jiang Nanchen　　Hua Junwu, transformed into an artistic peak, stands tall and majestic with his permanent charm lasting forever. When I was in middle school, I liked Hua Junwu's cartoons. He was famous and well-known to everyone. After starting work, I subscribed to several newspapers which often published Hua Junwu's cartoons. I cut them out and bound them into a book. During the Cultural Revolution when Hua Junwu was criticized, I couldn't bear to discard my collection but was afraid of being discovered. At that time, I was also in a cowshed (a place for political prisoners). If those cartoons were found, it would add new "evidence" of crime. After much deliberation, I decided to use large articles criticizing Hua as a cover, pasting the cartoons on them. If they were discovered, I could argue that they were for criticism purposes, which would be enough to get by.  red suede pumas In the summer of 1992, Hua Junwu, who had already retired, came to Harbin to hold a personal cartoon exhibition. Upon invitation by cartoonist Zhang Bin, I went to visit the exhibition and had the opportunity to recount these old stories while meeting Hua Junwu and his wife Song Qi.  puma yo mtv raps But what is most unforgettable is after they left, Zhang Bin forwarded a cartoon gifted by Hua Lao titled "Du Fu's Self-Examination". On it was inscribed: "In 1962, a certain university suddenly criticized the poem 'The War Chariots', so I created this painting as satire. Soon after, during the Cultural Revolution, this painting was classified as a major poison weed. This is a record. Brother Fenghui, please correct me. Summer of 1992." I usually don't ask for paintings, including from familiar good friends, let alone Hua Lao's long-admired cartoons. Seeing this gift painting that made Premier Zhou laugh heartily, I was deeply moved, considering it a treasure, and would take it out to admire from time to time. My daughter then asked someone to specially make a picture frame, hanging it on the wall so it could be appreciated anytime.  

Unexpectedly, in June 1999, during the "Humorous Cartoon Competition and Exchange Seminar between Taiwan Straits Media Life", I met Hua Lao and his wife again. This time, there were also Ding Cong and his wife, Fang Cheng, Rong Fan, Guo Chang, Yi Ren, etc. I wrote "A Casual Record of Meeting Three Elders", roughly recording that unforgettable gathering in Chengde.

The three cartoon masters gathered in the Ice City, it was a relaxed, joyful, unforgettable gathering, a rare chance to witness their charm. In a private room on the third floor of Hua Mei, the three elders, with their wisdom, humor, and insights into life, benefited us greatly.

The conversation started with Fang Cheng's "Wu Dalang Opens a Shop", which brought him into thousands of households, almost universally known. Fang Cheng said, this was thanks to Hua Lao. Originally, the couplet was: "Blessings like the Eastern Sea flow endlessly, wealth flourishes and reaches the three rivers." Hua Lao suggested changing the couplet, thus we now have: "People don't need to be tall if they have power, the shop may be small but I am the only ruler," with the horizontal inscription "Legacy of Wang Lun." The art of language and visual language complement each other beautifully, fully revealing the depth of the theme. Hua Lao sitting opposite casually said, "There are always people telling me, you painted 'Wu Dalang Opens a Shop,' I've gained from your light." Experiencing the humility in the humor of these two masters, one can't help but smile knowingly.

Compared to the first time, Hua Lao hadn't changed much. He still kept saying "I'm old." Naturally, I asked Hua Lao if he had visited Hua Mei when he worked in Harbin? He said, back then, after making money, he would just go to Ba Zha Shi to buy a few steamed buns, dip them in soy sauce, and eat, already satisfied, not knowing what Hua Mei was.

Naturally, the topic turned to "Little Ding," who was actually an elderly man in his 80s. What was suspicious was his jet-black hair. Someone asked if it was dyed? Ding Lao said, if I dyed it, I'd dye it white. A photographer came in to take his photo, and Ding Lao actually found two white hairs in the photo, which made him quite happy. I was a loyal reader of "Reading" magazine, able to see his new works in every issue. Back then, everyone said "Little Ding doesn't age." Who would have thought, he passed away in 2009, aged 93.

Were the ceaseless labors of the three elders in their later years the secret to longevity? Fang Cheng slowly said, "It's simply about not being idle." I asked if it wasn't tiring? He said, "Doing things that make oneself happy isn't tiring." When he was drawing, he would even forget to smoke. Just retired from a news unit, yet more units wanted him to draw. Unable to refuse, he could only draw more, wanting to retire but having to return to work. Ding Lao said, "Others retire at 60, I started working after 60." He made up for the 20 lost years. Wasn't Hua Lao the same? With his unique humor, entering his eighties, excellent works continued to emerge, still retaining his wit and humor. Once, while walking in the courtyard, he accidentally fell into an uncovered sand well, injuring his leg. Friends called to comfort him, saying, "I heard you fell into a well?" Hua Junwu immediately replied, "It's fine as long as you don't throw stones at me in the well."  puma sky ii lo 

Cartoonists' gatherings can't lack jokes. Hua's son Duanduan said, Northeastern jokes are especially numerous, I learned many. Hua Lao joked, "When you return to Beijing, you'll forget them." Duanduan didn't agree, immediately telling a mosquito story on the spot, causing laughter throughout the room.

Hua Lao's closing remarks were particularly meaningful: "We are all salty (idle) people, you are sweet people," meaning it's not early, tomorrow we still have to work. He added, "We are salty (idle) people, but we're not bitter people." Humor is always closely linked with knowledge, cultivation, wisdom, and wit. Such humorous interests may be another reason for their longevity.

These past events flooded my mind on the day Hua Lao passed away, perhaps unforgettable for life.

I remember the day before Hua Lao passed away, the "Gao Mang Art Exhibition Symposium" was held at Volga Manor. I mentioned the pure friendship between Hua Junwu and Gao Mang, talking about the story of Hua Junwu drawing a tiger for Gao Mang. Unexpectedly, the next day I heard the news of Hua Lao's passing. Although he lived to be 95, enough to bring joy, the separation between heaven and earth makes it hard to remain calm. Writing some words of condolence to comfort Hua Lao's soul in heaven.
