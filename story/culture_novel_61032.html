The Murder at the Volcanic Crater

Author: Takaaki Okada

Dear readers, you may have pondered about the notion of intentional crime, which is nothing more than a dream for detective novelists. Now, I would like to tell you about an intentional murder case that took place at the crater of volcano A (the "Joushin border" or "Shinshu" was formerly one of the eastern provinces of Japan, now known as Nagano Prefecture. "Volcano A" could be a pseudonym for Mount Asama, which stands 2,542 meters high and spans across Nagano and Gunma Prefectures, being one of Japan's famous volcanoes) in front of eight eyes belonging to four onlookers, yet no one could fathom the truth. The secret of this crime has remained undisclosed until now, and even to my dearest wife... However, the burden of this once infamous, terrifying, and mysterious murder case, which only I know, has become unbearable. The pain of carrying this terrible secret until I leave this world is something I can no longer endure. In front of those who love detective novels, it is almost a relief to share this wall-fiction-like terror, which can exist as a "detective novel" just like the case from several years ago, bringing me immense happiness.

Perhaps some of you still remember the famous duel case involving young writer Kaoru Kagura at the crater of volcano A that was widely reported in newspapers. This happened on December 19th, Showa Year 13. At that time, I was still a studious student who had just graduated from I High School and entered the English Department of T University.

One morning, the maid tossed a snow-white envelope onto my futon in the dormitory room while I was lying face-up. I absentmindedly extended my right hand to pick up the letter. However, the vigorous handwriting on the recipient’s name immediately startled me, and all sleepiness vanished. When I flipped the envelope and read the sender's name, my eyes widened instantly. Unable to contain myself, I hastily sat up and nervously tore open the envelope.

It has been over ten months since we last met. I will never forget you all, but due to the matter you might already know, I deliberately distanced myself from you. There is no reason why we must part ambiguously despite our close relationship. I want to cut out the tumor and clear the poison. Let us reunite and shake hands in friendship again. Furthermore, I wish for all members of our quintet gang to gather together and have a heartfelt conversation. Here, I extend this invitation to you, and will subsequently send the same invitation to Kaoru, Arata, and Aramaki.

The university is on winter break now, and I hope you can come as soon as possible. Could you please invite the other three to join us? Especially Kaoru, please make sure to bring him along. We look forward to your arrival.
