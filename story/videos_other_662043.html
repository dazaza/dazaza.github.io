Wu Dong Qian Kun latest chapter list: A 28-year-old male PhD meticulously cares for and awakens his animalistic mother (photo)

by nbthwjehj8 on 2012-02-09 16:46:24

Huang Bi Hai held his mother's hand. "It is my fault, I have held you back..." On November 30th, in the gastrointestinal surgery ward of the Min Kang Building at Hubei Provincial People's Hospital, 54-year-old Lu Shengli looked at her son with tear-filled eyes. "Your health is my greatest comfort," replied 28-year-old Huang Bi Hai as he firmly held his mother's hand. What greatly consoled Huang Bi Hai was that now he could once again communicate normally with his mother, because not long ago, his mother was still a "vegetable person."

■ Father Runs Away, Mother and Son Struggle Alone

Lu Shengli and Huang Bi Hai, mother and son, live in Wuchang Vehicle Factory. Their suffering began in 1997.

That year, 14-year-old Huang Bi Hai was in the third year of junior high school, excelling academically; both parents worked at Wuchang Vehicle Factory, with no worries about food or clothing, and their family of three lived happily together. However, one summer day, his father took all the savings from home, abandoned his wife and children, and left without a trace. During that time, Lu Shengli lost her appetite completely, feeling unbearable pain. She suffered from intestinal disease, and with her husband gone, how could she, a single woman, support this family? Fortunately, her son was sensible. He consoled his mother: "I will study hard and not worry you!"

A sentence of promise from her son gave Lu Shengli immense relief. Due to worsening intestinal disease, she was frequently hospitalized and had to retire early after a few years, relying on her pension to support her son’s schooling and their daily expenses.

Lu Shengli recalled that to save money, she would walk through all the stalls in the market each time she bought vegetables, picking the cheapest ones; she never entered supermarkets to buy rice but instead went to the market hundreds of meters from home to select cheaper rice. "As long as we don't starve, it's happiness," she said. Although life was hard at that time, seeing her son's excellent grades made her happy. Her only wish was for her son to succeed in the future.

■ Good News Followed by Mother Becoming a Vegetable Person

In the year Huang Bi Hai took the high school entrance exam, his mother fell ill again. Because he had to take care of his mother, he only managed to enter an ordinary high school. Lu Shengli felt disappointed and hopeless, thinking her son "definitely wouldn't get into university" and cried secretly every day.

However, after entering school, Huang Bi Hai worked even harder and achieved excellent results in the college entrance exam. To take care of his sick mother, he applied to Hubei University, which was close to home, and entered the Chemistry and Biology Base Class.

In 2006, Huang Bi Hai successfully enrolled in the master's program in Chemistry and Molecular Science at Wuhan University, and then continued to pursue his doctoral studies. In June this year, after graduating from his doctoral program, due to his outstanding research capabilities, he was recommended by his tutor to work at a world-class institute in Japan and pursue postdoctoral studies, with an annual salary of 400,000 yuan.

Huang Bi Hai shared the good news with his mother, Lu Shengli, who was overjoyed. However, on August 21st, she was hospitalized due to a recurrence of her gastrointestinal disease, underwent surgery on September 16th, and two days later experienced a massive cerebral infarction, falling into a coma and becoming a "vegetable person."

■ Dilemma and Choosing to Stay with Family

After a consultation with hospital experts, it was determined that Lu Shengli faced life-threatening risks at any moment, and the possibility of regaining consciousness was extremely slim.

Huang Bi Hai was extremely anxious. On one side was his seriously ill mother, and on the other was a rare opportunity for further studies. He was caught between a rock and a hard place. After much deliberation, he finally decided to stay by his mother's side and take on the responsibility of caring for her, "I only have one mother in my life, she needs me, I should stay!"

"As long as I try hard, I believe everything can turn around," Huang Bi Hai deeply understood that it would be no easy task to awaken his "vegetable person" mother. But he didn't want to give up and hoped to create a miracle through action.

Since his mother couldn't eat, she could only rely on intravenous nutrition provided by the hospital. Huang Bi Hai worried about insufficient nutrition, so every day he used a syringe to inject eggs and milk through the gastric tube into his mother's stomach. To prevent bedsores from prolonged bed rest, he helped his mother turn over every two hours.

To awaken his mother's memory, whenever he had free time, Huang Bi Hai would lean over his mother's ear and tell her "stories"—about his university life, his future plans, and the difficult and joyful times they had spent together... over and over again.

According to Li Jin, a classmate of Huang Bi Hai, during that time, every time he visited the ward, he would see Huang Bi Hai sitting by his mother's bedside, incessantly telling her stories. Although his mother did not respond, he never got tired of it.

■ Believing in Miracles, Filial Piety Moved Heaven and Earth

The turning point finally came! A month later, Lu Shengli's lips began to move, emitting faint "iyiya" sounds.

Huang Bi Hai was overwhelmed with emotion—his efforts had finally paid off! "That was the happiest day of my life," said Huang Bi Hai. In the following days, he seemed filled with energy, calling out to his mother even more earnestly and telling her stories.

A few days later, Lu Shengli finally regained consciousness and could slowly speak, albeit in fragmented sentences, but for Huang Bi Hai, they were worth more than a thousand words.

Huang Bi Hai recalled that when his mother first woke up, she was still in a state of amnesia and repeatedly called him "mom." But for him, such misunderstandings were equally sweet.

After treatment, Lu Shengli spoke more and longer sentences and continuously stretched her hands and right leg. Therefore, besides continuing to tell his mother stories, Huang Bi Hai added another task—massaging his mother.

On November 12th, Huang Bi Hai discovered that his mother miraculously stood up while holding onto the bed rail. He immediately captured the moment on camera, uploaded it to Weibo, and sent group messages via his phone to share the good news with those who cared about them.

Yesterday afternoon, when the reporter returned to the hospital, Huang Bi Hai had just finished buying groceries. Without resting, he helped his mother exercise. Lu Shengli, like a toddler learning to walk, unsteadily moved her steps, step by step, circle by circle. After a round of exercise, Lu Shengli sat down to rest, and Huang Bi Hai's forehead was already drenched in sweat.

After regaining consciousness, Lu Shengli could eat on her own, so Huang Bi Hai bought chickens and ducks from the market every day to supplement her nutrition. As her hands were weak and she couldn't hold chopsticks well, he fed her bite by bite. However, her bowel movements still required the use of a colostomy bag. For this reason, Huang Bi Hai changed the bag daily, and if not careful, the bag could burst. This happened the day before yesterday at noon when the colostomy bag burst, splashing waste all over Lu Shengli's bed. Without hesitation, Huang Bi Hai quickly gave his mother a bath and changed her bedding.

Currently, Dong Shengli's memory has fully recovered. Her attending physician, Luo Jianfei, introduced that it was already fortunate for Lu Shengli, who became a "vegetable person," to survive, let alone recover her memory and partial motor functions, without developing common complications such as bedsores, permanent leg dysfunction, etc., "It's a miracle, truly a miracle!"
