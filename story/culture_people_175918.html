For the 60th anniversary of China's National Day, the five-starred red flag will fly in front of the American White House

by kaxiya on 2009-07-13 14:37:05

On the eve of the 60th anniversary of China's National Day, a five-star red flag will slowly rise on the lawn south of the White House in the United States.

The Chinese national flag will be raised at 10:00 a.m. on September 20 by Chinese compatriots in the Greater Washington area of the United States. This is also the first time that the Chinese national flag has been raised in front of the White House, where the US President resides, by a civilian organization.

Xiao Shuigen, Secretary-General of the Federation of American Chinese Professional Associations, said at a press conference on the 12th that the overall activities for this National Day flag-raising ceremony have been approved by the US authorities.

He Xiaohui, president of the Association of Compatriot Societies in the Greater Washington area, said that this first-ever National Day flag-raising ceremony in Washington, co-sponsored by the local overseas Chinese and academic circles and jointly participated by friendly individuals and organizations from all sectors, was held to celebrate the 60th anniversary of National Day, aiming to express the deep affection and unity of overseas Chinese and compatriots towards their homeland.

Director Zhao Luqun revealed that there will also be a large-scale cultural performance during the flag-raising ceremony. About 1,000 performers will participate in this flag-raising ceremony, and the cultural performance will showcase the friendly, inclusive, and kind spirit of modern Chinese people.

As the initiator of this National Day flag-raising ceremony and chairman of the Fujian Compatriot Association of the United States, Chen Ronghua choked up and shed tears several times during his interview with reporters. On the night of the Spring Festival Eve in 1989, Chen Ronghua, who was alone at the time, passed by the UN building in New York late at night, overwhelmed by homesickness. Among more than a hundred flagpoles, he found the Chinese national flag, hugged the pole, and burst into tears. This experience made him vow that no matter where he went, he would personally raise the national flag locally.

For twenty years, Chen Ronghua's home has always hung the five-starred red flag. While in New York, he actively organized and presided over many patriotic activities. In 1999, during the 50th anniversary of National Day, Chen Ronghua hoisted the national flag together with Qin Huasun, then the Chinese representative to the United Nations, in New York's Chinatown. After moving to Washington in 2000, he proposed for nine consecutive years to hold a flag-raising ceremony in the central district of the US capital, but due to various reasons, this wish was only realized this year.

Chen Ronghua told the reporter from the China News Service that the smooth approval to hold the Chinese national flag-raising ceremony in front of the White House in the United States was due, on one hand, to the currently good Sino-US relations, and on the other hand, it also shows that China, as a responsible major country, has become increasingly powerful, especially the last Olympic Games which impressed many Americans about China.

Someone asked Chen Ronghua, "Since you love China so much, which is better, China or the United States?" Chen Ronghua replied: "I make a living in the United States, the United States is my boss, but China nurtured me, she will always be my mother."
