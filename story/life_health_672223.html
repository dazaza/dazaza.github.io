Treatment and nursing methods for bedsores!

by sxykmgy on 2012-02-22 16:26:45

How to treat bedsores? What are the nursing methods for bedsores? Bedsores (also known as pressure sores or pressure ulcers) are caused by prolonged pressure on local tissues, resulting in sustained ischemia, hypoxia, and malnutrition leading to tissue necrosis and death. Skin bedsores are a common issue in rehabilitation treatment and nursing. According to relevant literature reports, approximately 60,000 people die each year from bedsores complications. The following is a detailed introduction by Director Zhao Jianlin of the Shanxi Taiyuan Traditional Chinese Medicine Bedsore Hospital on the treatment and care of bedsores.

Treatment methods for bedsores:

1. Traditional Chinese medicine therapy:

Treatment method: Invigorate Qi to unblock meridians, nourish blood to moisturize skin.

Formula: 15g Huangqi, 15g Dangshen, 10g Baishao, 15g Jixueteng, 10g Danggui, 5g Chuanxiong, 10g Honghua, 15g Danshen, 15g Shenjincao, 15g Yinhua, 15g Lianqiao, 10g Chenpi. If there is rotten flesh with pus, add Bai Zhi and Jiegeng to draw out toxins and pus; if the rotten flesh does not fall off, add stir-fried pangolin scales and stir-fried soap thorns.

Single formula prepared medicine: can take Bazhen Pills, Ren Shen Yangrong Pills.

2. Local treatment

(1) For local redness and swelling, apply Zi Cao Rong oil or a thin layer of purple anti-swelling ointment.

(2) In the early stage of ulceration with shallow wounds, use Huadu San ointment or purple anti-swelling ointment externally.

(3) For formed wounds, apply the traditional Chinese medicine method of resolving corruption and generating muscle:

Nursing for bedsores:

① When the wound has dead tissue (gangrenous flesh), inflammation is still spreading, and the boundary between dead tissue and healthy skin is unclear, it is advisable to mix equal amounts of purple carbuncle ointment and Huadu San ointment and apply externally.

② When the dead tissue (gangrenous flesh) has reached deep into the muscle or even deeper forming a sinus tract, it is advisable to use a red blood medicine thread dipped in purple carbuncle ointment inserted into the wound, covered externally with Huadu San ointment.

③ When the boundary between dead tissue and normal skin is clear and begins to fall off, it is advisable to mix equal amounts of purple carbuncle ointment, Huadu San ointment, and Ganru ointment and apply externally.

④ When the dead tissue has fallen off and new granulation tissue begins to grow, it is advisable to mix 80g of Ganru ointment with 20g of purple carbuncle ointment and apply externally.

⑤ When the new granulation tissue grows well and the edges of the wound have started epithelial growth, it is advisable to thinly sprinkle Zhu Dan powder over the wound, then cover with an oil gauze strip made from Ganru ointment.

⑥ During medication, if eczema-like changes appear around the wound, it can be treated with Qu Shi powder mixed with licorice oil.
