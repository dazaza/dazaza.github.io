Yahoo Shares Hit 5-Year High on Alibaba Profit Growth

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 15:45:32

On July 18, Beijing time, according to foreign media reports, driven by a sharp increase in the first quarter performance of Alibaba Group, Yahoo's stock price soared by 10.34% during regular trading on the Nasdaq Stock Exchange on Wednesday, closing at $29.66, setting a new high since May 2008.

Yahoo stated on Tuesday that Alibaba Group’s net profit in the first quarter had tripled year-on-year, reaching $669 million; its revenue surged by 71%, reaching $1.38 billion. After selling nearly half of its stake in Alibaba Group last year, Yahoo still holds about 24% of Alibaba Group's shares.

The strong performance of Alibaba Group has favorably supported Yahoo's stock price. One year after Marissa Mayer became Yahoo's CEO, the company is still in the early stages of transformation. By focusing on launching new products, Yahoo aims to attract advertisers and consumers from Google and Facebook. RBC Capital Markets analyst Mark Mahaney believes that most of Yahoo's stock price increase over the past year has been driven by the appreciation of its stakes in Alibaba Group and Yahoo Japan.

Riley & Co. analyst Sameet Sinha said, "Alibaba Group is showcasing a lot of operating leverage. This company has built a massive market in China and has started charging users." Yahoo's second-quarter earnings report released on Tuesday showed that due to a 12% year-on-year decline in display advertising revenue, the company's revenue excluding traffic acquisition costs (TAC) was $1.071 billion, down 1% year-on-year; net profit was $331 million, up 46% year-on-year. Yahoo earned $225 million in gains from its stakes in Alibaba Group and Yahoo Japan in the second quarter, higher than $180 million in the same period last year.

Performance Surge

Yahoo posted a message on its official website on Tuesday stating that Alibaba Group's net profit in the first quarter tripled, increasing from $220 million last year to $669 million. This Hangzhou-based company may conduct its initial public offering by the end of this year.

The Asian assets held by Yahoo also gave it an opportunity for hedging due to the depreciation of the yen exchange rate. Yahoo stated that it gained $243 million in cash income in the second quarter due to hedging transactions. In May, Yahoo stated that it gained $270 million in pre-tax income in the first quarter due to hedging its stake in Yahoo Japan, and $3 million in pre-tax income last year.

Yahoo stated on Tuesday that the company's revenue excluding traffic acquisition costs would be between $1.06 billion and $1.1 billion in the third quarter. Bloomberg data showed that market analysts expected Yahoo's third-quarter revenue to be $1.12 billion.

Efforts of the CEO

Bloomberg Industries analyst Paul Sweeney said that while Mayer is working hard to increase the number of Yahoo users, investors must be patient and wait for Yahoo's advertising revenue to grow. Sweeney said, "Mayer's investment in products to enhance user engagement and ultimately achieve revenue growth is a long-term endeavor. Based on Yahoo's current performance expectations, these investments are unlikely to drive short-term company performance improvements."

Market research company EMarketer estimated that the size of the display advertising market reached $17.5 billion last year, with Yahoo's share declining from 9.2% last year to 7.9% this year. Google's share will increase by three percentage points, reaching nearly 18%; Facebook's share is expected to increase by two percentage points, reaching 17%.

Yahoo's financial report showed that the company's display advertising business revenue declined by 12% year-on-year in the second quarter, dropping to $472 million. Colin Gillis, an analyst at U.S. investment company BGC Partners, said: "In fact, the difficulties of Yahoo's display advertising business have just begun. We haven't seen what measures Yahoo management can take yet to stop the downward trend."

Chain Reaction

Mayer likened her revival plan to a "chain reaction," saying it would eventually lead to revenue growth for the company. Mayer said in an analyst conference call broadcast live online on Tuesday, "Hiring and retaining a great team; they will develop inspiring products that attract users and increase traffic; the increase in traffic can capture advertisers' interest and ultimately convert into revenue. First people, then products, next traffic, and finally revenue."

Yahoo also lowered its revenue forecast for 2013 on Tuesday. Excluding traffic acquisition costs, Yahoo expects fiscal year revenue to reach a maximum of $4.55 billion, lower than the $4.6 billion forecast in April, and only slightly higher than last year's $4.47 billion. Analyst Sinha believed, "Yahoo will experience another year of no revenue growth."

Frenzied Acquisitions

Driven by the sale of its stake in Alibaba Group, Mayer began a frenzied acquisition spree after taking office. During her tenure as CEO of Yahoo, the company has acquired at least 18 companies, including Tumblr for $1.1 billion, as well as mobile application developers Stamped, Jybe, and Summly, etc. Earlier this month, Yahoo acquired Xobni, a contacts app and plug-in company, for about $70 million.

Yahoo announced on Wednesday that it had acquired Admovate, an innovative company providing technology for buying and selling mobile advertisements. Four technical personnel from Admovate will join Yahoo to help the company generate income through advertisements displayed on smartphones and tablets.

Yahoo spent $653 million to repurchase 25.3 million shares of the company in the second quarter, with an average repurchase price of $25.76 per share.

Yahoo's current stock price is nearing $31, which was also the offer price when Microsoft initially made a takeover bid in 2008. Although Microsoft later raised the acquisition price to $33 per share, Yahoo still rejected Microsoft's acquisition offer. Affected by the reduction in the number of outstanding shares, Yahoo's current market value is $31.8 billion, still far below the $47.5 billion acquisition price offered by Microsoft back then.
