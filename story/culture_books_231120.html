"Marriage Displacement between Metro Women and Phoenix Men"

by jqrxass on 2009-08-31 12:53:01

Lan Caihe and Tong Haibo are both children of the 80s. Haibo's family lives in a rural area; his father passed away early, leaving only his mother and younger sister behind. After three years of dating, Lan Cai defied her parents' pressure and married Haibo. They bought a house in Beijing, expecting to live a happy life. However, due to Haibo's poor family background, they were constantly burdened by their mortgage, leading to a strained life. Moreover, Lan Cai's wealthy ex-boyfriend sought revenge by getting engaged to her good friend Feng Yuanyuan, causing emotional imbalance in Lan Cai. The financial struggles, escalating mother-in-law and daughter-in-law conflicts, urban-rural differences, and other factors eventually pushed Haibo and Lan Cai's marriage to the brink.

(Editor's Note: So-called "Phoenix Men" refer to men who have worked hard for over a decade with the full support of their families, finally becoming successful and offering hope for their family's transformation. After moving to the city, these men marry "Peacock Women" (a term for urban women), starting a city life. However, due to their rural roots, various issues arise in their love, marriage, and family life.)

After Marriage - Part One

Lan Caihe and Tong Haibo dated for three years and should have gotten married earlier, but they never managed to buy a house. After graduating, Haibo worked as a salesperson at a medical device company, serving as a representative for two years before being promoted to manager. He didn't receive a basic salary, relying solely on commissions for income. During good times, he could earn ten or twenty thousand yuan a month, but during bad times, he would go months without earning anything, living off savings. Lan Cai graduated later than Haibo and had only just started working. Although her parents could offer some support, they were merely small employees at a state-owned enterprise with limited means. Faced with skyrocketing housing prices, Lan Cai felt increasingly anxious.

After Marriage - Part Two

Clearly, Haibo's mother Li Jinhua and sister Tong Hailian were extremely delighted by Lan Cai's arrival, even feeling somewhat honored. They bustled about, offering her water, washing apples, boiling peanuts, killing chickens and fish, and whenever there was a break, Li Jinhua would hold Lan Cai's hand and warmly inquire about her well-being. Haibo's home was very simple: three dilapidated tile-roofed rooms, a small courtyard with some accumulated snow where coal blocks were piled, and separate constructions for the toilet and kitchen. In the side room was a large heated kang bed, which made Lan Cai feel uncomfortable after sitting on it for a while. She wanted to stand up and walk around, but the three members of Haibo's family surrounded her, chatting idly about neighbors’ affairs, making her feel awkward until her bottom became damp from sitting too long.

After Marriage - Part Three

After eating and drinking their fill, driving back to the Moon Bay neighborhood in the red Polo, she couldn’t help but marvel at how this girl from Chengdu, with no family background, had come to Beijing to struggle and within less than two years after graduation, achieved a life with a house and a car. Though the house was merely a forty-square-meter one-bedroom apartment and the car was an eight-wan Polo, she still owned them. The transition from having nothing to possessing something was a significant change, giving her a foothold to survive in Beijing.

Of course, all that she owned was given to her by that person. To be precise, it was exchanged with that person. There was an exchange between the two: in spring, she gave part of her body and youth to that person, and in return, that person provided her with a house, a car, jewelry, and cosmetics that she could flaunt.

After Marriage - Part Four

Every time I hear that you've argued with or angered Tong Haibo, I feel upset and want to beat him up. Lan Cai, tell me honestly, is your marriage happy?" Fu Cheng’s voice was gentle, but anyone could hear the doubt in his heart.

Lan Cai didn't know how to respond, just sitting in the passenger seat and sighing deeply. Yes, was she happy? The four-thousand-yuan monthly mortgage payment annoyed her, the inability to buy high-end clothes annoyed her, Haibo's mother annoyed her, the backward little village annoyed her, and her parents' opposition annoyed her. Her marriage was like a small boat carefully navigating through a channel filled with hidden reefs.

After Marriage - Part Five

"Mom, we can't have a child too early. We haven't finished paying off our mortgage yet. With a child, we will endure so much suffering!" Lan Cai rejected the idea outright.

"That's not right! Poor people raise children simply, rich people raise them luxuriously. When Haibo was young, he drank porridge every day and grew up fine, didn't he?"

"That's your idea. I don't want my child to suffer like his father."
