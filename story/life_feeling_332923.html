Ten years passed in the blink of an eye.

by adbbs on 2010-03-13 16:04:30

Ten years passes in the blink of an eye. Most of the time in life is like this, uneventful but worth cherishing all the more. We can't have so many dramatic ups and downs, and we don't need breathtaking adventures. Let us linger at every moment of beauty, hurry through misfortune, stay strong in adversity, and above all, maintain a sense of calm and peace in our ordinary days. Let us bless this world, without giving up, without anger, without weakness, and move forward.

When I was in junior high school, my Chinese teacher once assigned an essay called "Ten Years Later." I vaguely remember setting a goal to earn 100,000 yuan. In those days over a decade ago, for a poor boy from the countryside, 100,000 yuan was absolutely an astronomical figure. And today, the house where I live is worth far more than that. Starting from today, for the next ten years, I really don't have any lofty ambitions. I just want to spend more time with my hardworking parents who have spent their whole lives working, and also let them spend more time with me.

As we were talking, my wife fell asleep. Our sleep quality ranks the same as our family's hierarchy: baby champion, wife runner-up, and I come in third. Every time, I listen to their breathing, even the sound of teeth grinding or some colleague’s snoring, then vaguely find my own dream, entering the most selfless realm, letting the snowflakes dance outside or the moon rest on the willow branch. But before falling into a deep sleep, I am always entangled by those messy old memories.

How could I not be restless? Ten years ago, we were still carefree single nobles, yearning for married life which has now passed seven years in the blink of an eye. The little baby we imagined a thousand times has now made her dazzling debut. She grows day by day. Ten years ago, she was still an imagination; now she is the "master" of her parents, controlling our happiness and sorrow. She once made us, first-time parents, at a loss, and even more so, made us feel endless joy in life.

In such a scenario, we began our dialogue. It was more like a casual conversation, interspersed with drowsiness, as sleep bugs had already sounded the rallying cry, so it can basically be defined as semi-dreamlike state. My wife said that when she was young and went to school, she felt that time passed very slowly, each week seemed so long. And now, as soon as the week starts, it's "whoosh" to Wednesday, then another "whoosh" to the weekend. Each year seems to go from May Day to National Day, and then back to May Day again.

My conversation with my wife was neither salty nor bland. There was nothing urgent, but such exchanges are important. A few days ago, my mother came to the city to see a dentist. She wasn't used to urban life and felt like a fish trapped in a jar, listless every day. Before I slept, I would always go over and talk about past events with my mother. My wife said that during that time, I rarely paid attention to her and the child, and I quickly "protested my innocence", but family matters are often right and wrong intertwined, making it really hard to explain clearly.
