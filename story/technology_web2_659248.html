Working couples trapped in small workshops, working more than 12 hours a day (photo gallery)

by anonymous on 2012-02-05 22:41:30

The "agreement" signed by Zhang and Lu when they started working at the small workshop. The guarantee letter signed by Zhang and Lu when they were redeemed by their parents.

By reporter Guo ZhiKun stationed in Baoding

On April 27 this year, two young people around 20 years old were introduced by a labor agency to a family-style bag-making workshop in Songxin Village, Sizhuang Town, Gaobeidian City. Nearly two and a half months later on July 10, when their families tried to take them home through the local police station, the workshop owner demanded a so-called "breach of contract fee" of 2500 yuan.

After leaving Gaobeidian with immense anger, the two young people and their families came to our newspaper and told the reporters about their inhumane experiences.

The couple working in the workshop was trapped

Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting are a young couple, each from Tang County and Quyang County respectively.

On April 27 this year, after losing their phones and running out of money in Shijiazhuang, these two inexperienced young people entered a nameless small labor agency near the train station. The agency staff said they could introduce jobs for them, working 6 to 10 hours a day with a monthly salary of 1000 to 1500 yuan. They thought the treatment was acceptable and agreed. That afternoon, a 30-year-old boss from Gaobeidian named Wang Hua drove to Shijiazhuang and took them to a family-style bag-making workshop inside Songxin Village, Sizhuang Town, Gaobeidian City. The boss said they would be processing schoolbags and informed them that they were not allowed to leave the gate unless accompanied by his family or someone he sent.

Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting said that they started work the next day. For more than two months, they did not have a single day off. They had to sew 200 to 300 bags per day, working from 8 am to 8 pm. Lu Tingting could just finish her quota, but due to his slower speed, Zhang Hao had to work until 9 pm, 10 pm, or even later. During this time, they only borrowed 268 yuan from the boss to buy clothes, toiletries, and medicine. They were not allowed to contact their families, and the door of the house was often locked even during the day, making it impossible for them to leave.

Family paid to "redeem" the two

On July 9, Lu Tingting bought a phone card with the help of a kind aunt and secretly charged an old mobile phone. She then called Zhang Hao's family at night, asking them to come to Gaobeidian quickly to rescue them.

On the afternoon of July 10, Zhang Hao's father, Zhang Hongyu, along with Zhang Hao's mother and brother, went through many difficulties to find the Sizhuang Police Station of the Gaobeidian Public Security Bureau. Two police officers heard the situation and drove them to Songxin Village, where they found the bag-making workshop where Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting worked. In this family workshop, the police let Zhang Hao's mother negotiate with the workshop owner, Wang Hua. Wang Hua proposed that Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting had agreed to work there for a year, but now had only worked for more than two months. According to the employment agreement, they had to pay a breach of contract fee of 6000 yuan. After much negotiation, Wang Hua finally agreed to let Zhang Hongyu take Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting away after Zhang Hao's mother paid a so-called breach of contract fee of 2500 yuan.

The handling result of the police station was "satisfactory"

In the morning of July 12, I accompanied Zhang Hao, Lu Tingting, and Zhang Hongyu to Gaobeidian to report the situation to relevant departments. Under the guidance of the staff of the Propaganda Department of the Gaobeidian Municipal Committee, Director Liu Huixiang of the Gaobeidian Human Resources and Social Security Bureau responsible for labor supervision and Captain Gao Jinhua of the Labor Supervision Brigade received us.

Liu Huixiang and Gao Jinhua said that the bureau had dispatched labor supervision law enforcement personnel to inspect the involved bag-making workshop. Subsequently, the two led us to the Sizhuang Police Station, where we met Deputy Chief Dong who handled the matter at that time.

Deputy Chief Dong introduced that he took Zhang Hongyu et al. to the bag-making workshop and let them negotiate with the workshop owner, Wang Hua. He also asked Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting to sign separate guarantees: "Introduced by the Shijiazhuang labor agency, worked at Wang Hua's home in Songxin Village, Sizhuang Town, Gaobeidian. Now voluntarily returning home with parents, any incidents from today onward will be unrelated to Wang Hua. Very satisfied with the handling result of the police station." The police did this because both parties had an agreement to work for a year, and it was voluntary for the two workers to pay the breach of contract fee to the boss.

Chief Ma of the Sizhuang Police Station said that if local workers wanted to leave the company and the company was unwilling to let them go, the police station should first ensure the personal safety of the workers and guarantee their safe departure. If there are other labor disputes, the two parties need to negotiate.

Regarding this, Zhang Hongyu expressed that they came to the Sizhuang Police Station for help, but the police station let them negotiate with the boss. As outsiders, they only hoped to leave this place as soon as possible. Moreover, during the negotiation process hosted by the police station, his son Zhang Hao was almost beaten. Later, when they arrived at the police station, the police took out pre-written "guarantee letters" and asked the two young people to sign. Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting also told the reporter that they did not agree with the content of the guarantee letter.

The boss and his family loudly scolded the working couple

While I was interviewing at the police station, the labor supervision law enforcement personnel who went to conduct a labor inspection at the bag-making workshop in Songxin Village also arrived at the Sizhuang Police Station. They claimed that the workshop's door was tightly closed, and after knocking for a long time, no one opened the door, so they left. I insisted on going to the workplace with the labor supervision law enforcement personnel and police officers to understand the situation on-site. When we arrived, we saw that the courtyard gate of the workshop was wide open, and several people inside were eating.

Boss Wang Hua and his father introduced to me and the law enforcement personnel that all their employment was legal, providing food and accommodation, implementing piece-rate wages, with worker salaries ranging from 800 to 1500 yuan, and working hours generally being 10 hours. If the work was slow, the working hours would be longer. They implemented an annual wage system, meaning the settlement would only occur after working for a full year, and within a year, only basic personal expenses would be given to the workers.

When I proposed to understand the situation from the workers, Wang Hua and his father said that there were only 10 workers in their home. Due to hearing that people from the town had come, seven of them were scared and ran away, leaving only three behind.

Just as I and the law enforcement personnel were understanding the situation from the remaining workers, suddenly loud quarrels were heard outside the courtyard. Fearing an accident, I quickly ran out of the yard with the law enforcement personnel. It turned out that Boss Wang Hua and his parents were loudly cursing Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting hiding in the car, scaring them pale and unable to move. To ensure the safety of the two, I proposed to immediately leave Wang Hua's house. On the car, I learned that Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting had learned at Wang Hua's doorstep that Wang Hua's family had taken the other seven workers away by car, and they were not "scared away by themselves." Wang Hua's family cursed them precisely because they learned about these matters.

I also contacted a recently departed Shanxi-born worker named Ye Ningning. Ye Ningning said on the phone that he had worked for Wang Hua's family for eight months. A few days ago, when he proposed not to continue working, Wang Hua grabbed his hair and slapped him a few times before sending him away. During the eight months, he only received 1800 yuan for personal living expenses, and apart from that, he did not receive a single penny of wages.

After feeling the arrogance of the boss's family at the bag-making workshop again and thinking about what his child had experienced, Zhang Hongyu angrily said, "I didn't expect that after liberation for more than 60 years, how 'indentured laborers' have appeared again?!"
