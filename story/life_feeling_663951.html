"How to spend Valentine's Day without a lover?" "What to do without a Valentine's Day gift?"

by xiaomyan2004 on 2012-02-12 23:21:34

"How to spend Valentine's Day without a lover?" "What to do without a Valentine's gift?"

In just a couple of days, it will be Valentine's Day. This year, I'll be spending Valentine's Day alone. Seeing couples exchanging gifts on Valentine's Day while I stay at home and dare not go out. I'm 28 years old this year. Looking back, in 2011 during Valentine's Day, my boyfriend and I broke up because our parents disapproved. He was the one who initiated the breakup, and I was heartbroken, crying all the time, unable to return to a normal state for several months. I tried hard to win him back, but in the end, we still parted ways. It's been almost a year now, and I still haven't completely forgotten him. Occasionally, I still think about him, remembering the happiness and romance we shared during our Valentine's Days together.

I met him online, and since we were in the same city, we quickly arranged to meet. After meeting, we felt a connection and soon started dating. During our relationship, we were very happy, respecting each other deeply. I depended heavily on him, spending at least two hours every day with him. I supported everything he did, and I was truly happy and enjoyed the joy of loving and being loved. So for this past year, I've been immersed in these memories and haven't looked for another boyfriend, naturally becoming a "leftover woman." Of course, these past few years, I've rarely gone out socially. Mostly, I just go to work, come home, and stay at home. My life has basically been a straight line between three points. Naturally, I've become a "homebody." Because of my painful past, I've never dared to love again.

I always believed that relationships are exhausting. Whether it's breaking up or staying together, whether it brings pain or hurt, it often doesn't give a person what they want, but instead forces upon them things they can't accept. Behind longing is pain, behind strength is loneliness, and behind hope, there's only despair. Often, longing may really be an unbearable pain. Lonely nights, memories flow like water, making everything seem so cold. In countless such nights, I began to understand that there is a kind of love that doesn't need tears to move it. This kind of love has seeped into the soul, even transcending the soul.

I once worked very hard and was very persistent, thinking that after a lifetime of effort, I would eventually gain the happiness I thought I deserved. Although the air before the rain is always oppressively terrifying, walking alone in the rain is a different kind of sorrow. To this day, many people still don't understand why I enjoy walking in the rain so much. My answer is, I want to cleanse my soul. But this reason seems clumsy and forced.

So many days have passed, and the crying from back then is still vividly imprinted in my mind, along with those indescribable pains. I cried, shedding tears over love, but ultimately couldn't even touch the pitiful happiness. Unable to forget the past pain, I also can't forget how humbled I was in love back then.

Now, I've learned to be strong, learned not to cry over love. But the person I once loved has already faded far away from my sight. These days, I've wanted to tell him that I've understood from loneliness how to love, and from tears, I've understood that our relationship is forever. Missing a moment might really mean missing a lifetime.

Recently, I heard that his family has introduced him to a girlfriend, and they're preparing to get married soon. I'm very sad; he's shattered my last hope. I can only bless him and tell him that I will be happy too.

Since I can't bring him back, I'll bless them. But I'm 28 years old now, and I see friends and colleagues around my age getting married. There aren't many friends left to play with at home during the holidays because they've all gotten married and have their own families' affairs. Thinking about this, I feel a lot of pressure. Colleagues ask, and my parents at home worry, fearing I'll miss my chance. Everyone says that if a girl is 28 and hasn't found a partner, she'll really become an old maid, making it hard to find someone later. I'm scared; I no longer have the ability to find happiness.

Maybe I'll never dare to fully love a man again. Will I ever meet a man who genuinely loves me? I'm on the verge of breaking down; what should I do? Watching my peers enter marriage one by one, I envy them so much! I'm a traditional good girl, kind, with decent conditions in every aspect, yet why am I left behind?

On the bustling streets, I can't find even a trace of familiarity. This world is truly frighteningly strange. I've been struggling, wandering, and trying to find my way in the world of emotions. The marks of youth are deep, but the memories of emotions remain shallow. Perhaps, it has no intention of lingering in a specific space. Witnessing the separations and reunions of life, some people consider it a helplessness of life. In fact, emotions are often just pale. Just like everyone who is still immersed in the pain brought by love, knowing full well that in the face of a dead-end situation, they cannot change anything, yet they still fantasize about trying to change it.

How can I resolve my emotional issues? How should I spend this year's Valentine's Day? How should I face my emotional problems in the future? I've been pondering this question...

Finally, I hope that friends like me who don't have lovers can have a happier Valentine's Day. And I wish all lovers a happy Valentine's Day!

I call on friends without lovers this year to share the 2012 Valentine's Day with me >>>>>>
