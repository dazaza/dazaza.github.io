ST Xiahua's Additional Issuance Rejected: Financial Pressure Hinders Industrial Upgrading

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 10:12:32

By Jia Li, reporter of this newspaper

Despite repeated interest-free loans provided by the first and second largest shareholders of the company to ST Xiamen Huatian, ST Xiamen Huatian has long been criticized for its high debt level as it relies heavily on large amounts of borrowing to sustain operations. If the rights issue financing is successful, it will undoubtedly be the only key move to save ST Xiamen Huatian from its dire situation. However, due to the rejection of the rights issue financing plan at the recent shareholders' meeting by small and medium shareholders, the "light of hope" was temporarily "cut off".

Rejection of the Rights Issue Plan

Small and Medium Shareholders Do Not Approve

For ST Xiamen Huatian, July 27th afternoon was undoubtedly a black Wednesday. The dream of ST Xiamen Huatian's financing blood transfusion ended at No. 22 Huli Avenue, Xiamen City. And the trigger was that this time small and medium investors had voting rights through the network.

It is understood that the main reason for the rejection of the rights issue plan was that the small and medium investors who voted online did not approve. Details involving the issuance target and subscription method, as well as the offering price and pricing method, were not passed with more than two-thirds of the shareholders' voting rights.

After several attempts to propose a financing plan failed. On July 8th this year, ST Xiamen Huatian issued a non-public offering plan, intending to issue 76.789 million shares to the top two shareholders, raising nearly 400 million yuan, which was seen as a "glimmer of hope" for ST Xiamen Huatian.

Regarding the rejection of the rights issue, Su Zaiquan, manager of the brand department of ST Xiamen Huatian, told reporters: "Each shareholder who votes online has their own considerations, and the company cannot know the thoughts of each shareholder. It is worth noting that the lock-up period for the shares subscribed by the major shareholders in the rights issue plan is three years, which is a sign of recognition and confidence in the fundamental situation of ST Xiamen Huatian."

Will the rights issue plan be revised or abandoned? Su Zaiquan responded: "There is no new conclusion yet."

Although the two major shareholders of ST Xiamen Huatian have repeatedly injected funds to barely maintain its livelihood, it is difficult to reverse the situation. However, the two major shareholders of ST Xiamen Huatian still expressed that they would continue to support the development of the company in various ways.

At the same time, ST Xiamen Huatian released its semi-annual report showing that the company achieved a net profit of 6.1448 million yuan in the first half of the year, down 84.96% year-on-year, with an actual loss of 3.33 million yuan in the main business. ST Xiamen Huatian stated that the main reasons for the decline in performance were factors such as limited liquidation of LCD panels, bank interest rate hikes, and RMB appreciation.

Industry insiders believe that the rejection of ST Xiamen Huatian's rights issue is not unrelated to its financial risks and the high risk of fundraising not being recognized by investors.

ST Xiamen Huatian Electronics was established in January 1995 and listed in February. Its main business is ODM exports. It fell into losses the following year and continued to lose money for three consecutive years without recovery. In May 2009, trading was suspended, and last May, it returned to profitability and re-entered the capital market.

High Financial Costs Leave No Room to Breathe

In order to successfully complete the rights issue, the two major shareholders initially injected tens of billions of yuan into ST Xiamen Huatian, using every measure to reduce the company's financial pressure. The "turnaround" was ineffective, but it made ST Xiamen Huatian deeply in debt.

The 2011 semi-annual report of ST Xiamen Huatian shows that total assets are approximately 1.6 billion yuan, total liabilities are 2.5 billion yuan, and the asset-liability ratio is as high as 156.3%. Due to the high asset-liability ratio, the company cannot operate freely.

Industry analysts pointed out that the development of ST Xiamen Huatian has always been hindered by a lack of funds. Compared to the annual average financial costs of over 20 million yuan for peers, ST Xiamen Huatian is far higher at over 100 million yuan, which is alarming. Excessively high financial costs have long been one of the main reasons suppressing the release of ST Xiamen Huatian's performance.

"Looking at the stock market, there are many listed companies that become cash machines for major shareholders, but ST Xiamen Huatian receiving support from major shareholders is very fortunate. After three consecutive years of losses, ST Xiamen Huatian thoroughly resolved historical legacy issues, and after that painful experience, ST Xiamen Huatian is now ready to move forward lightly." Su Zaiquan holds an optimistic view about ST Xiamen Huatian.

Strategic Mistake?

Under the dual pressures of rising interest rates and RMB appreciation, even though long-term debt forms a burden, ST Xiamen Huatian, unable to generate its own cash flow, has become addicted to large-scale borrowing to sustain its survival. Putting aside the huge debt, why has ST Xiamen Huatian's repeated adjustment of its main business not been effective?

Su Zaiquan told reporters: "This time the rights issue was not approved, but one point is clear: it will not substantially affect the company's production and operation."

From ST Xiamen Huatian's semi-annual report, overseas research and development contracting (ODM) remains the company's main business, with revenue reaching 1.24 billion yuan, accounting for 88% of the company's main business income. In recent years, ST Xiamen Huatian has actively contracted the domestic market and focused on foreign ODM. Its business covers major markets such as North America and continues to expand into new markets like Southeast Asia. Last year, ST Xiamen Huatian opened the doors to the Japanese and Indian markets.

However, there are many restraining factors such as the international macroeconomic environment and exchange rate fluctuations, making the already fragile export industry even worse. Industry insiders pointed out that if ST Xiamen Huatian wants qualitative change, it should take two steps: reduce debt and expand production capacity; and reclaim the domestic market.

However, Su Zaiquan believes: "ST Xiamen Huatian has never given up on the domestic market, just reduced the proportion. The company has abandoned OEM and only does ODM with independent intellectual property rights and high premium capabilities, choosing this model that fits ST Xiamen Huatian's current characteristics."

"The strategy of focusing on the international market is worth reflecting on. It can be seen that most domestic companies with strength, such as Haier and Hisense, focus on the domestic market while expanding internationally, gradually building their own brands. Without a base, it's like a water lily, unable to stand firm." Industry expert Liu Busheng said.

Financial Pressure Hinders Industrial Upgrading

Currently, the television industry is rapidly transitioning from traditional analog CRT TVs to digital high-definition flat-panel TVs, with new technologies and products such as LED TVs, triple-play TVs, and 3D TVs emerging one after another.

However, under financial pressure, it seems that ST Xiamen Huatian is struggling to catch up with this trend.

"Television is still a sunrise industry with a huge market. Competition is fierce but not fully saturated. Currently, ST Xiamen Huatian still chooses to stay in this industry. ST Xiamen Huatian has also tried diversification, but had to contract due to funding issues," Su Zaiquan told reporters.

In addition, the overall gross profit margin of domestic flat-screen TV companies is showing signs of decline, and ST Xiamen Huatian's expansion into other areas is also constrained by a lack of funds.

"Our debts consist of more than 60% bank loans and 20% support from the two major shareholders. The financial cost is indeed high, and improving the capital structure is necessary to further grow. This time it wasn't passed, but the two major shareholders are still trying hard to help ST Xiamen Huatian, hoping investors will support us in improving our situation," Su Zaiquan said helplessly.
