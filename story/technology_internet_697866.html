Dermatology experts at Shanxi Huimin Hospital point out that scabies mites belong to the mite family, hence they are also called scabies mites. There are many types of scabies mites. Animal scabies mites can parasitize on cattle, horses, sheep, pigs, dogs, cats, rabbits, and camels, and can also be transmitted to humans. Those that parasitize on humans are human scabies mites. Scabies spreads through skin contact; sharing beds or shaking hands can directly transmit the disease. Since sexual behavior or close contact similar to sexual behavior is one of the main ways scabies is transmitted, scabies is currently also considered a sexually transmitted disease.

Experts in dermatology at Shanxi Huimin Hospital introduce the main symptoms of scabies: severe itching, especially at night. Intense itching occurs in episodes during the night; tunnels dug by scabies mites can be seen between fingers, about 2-4 mm long, appearing as irregular gray-brown curves. These are the traces left by scabies mites digging. The rash tends to occur in thin-skinned areas, especially between fingers, the inner wrists, lower abdomen, breasts, armpits, lower abdomen, groin, and genitals. The head and palms/soles are less likely to be affected. The lesions mainly consist of papules or vesicles the size of millet grains.
