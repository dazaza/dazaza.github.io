Meats that help maintain body shape

Rabbit meat

Rabbit meat differs from the components of general livestock meat, its characteristics are: contains more protein, each 100 grams of rabbit meat contains 21.5 grams of protein; contains less fat, each 100 grams contains only 0.4 grams of fat; contains rich lecithin; contains less cholesterol, each 100 grams contains only 83 milligrams of cholesterol. The nutritional value of beef is second only to rabbit meat, and it is also the type of meat suitable for overweight people. Each 100 grams of beef contains over 20 grams of protein. Fish meat

The fat in general livestock meat is mostly saturated fatty acids, while fish fat contains multiple unsaturated fatty acids, which have excellent cholesterol-lowering effects. Therefore, overweight people eating fish meat is better, as it can avoid obesity and prevent the occurrence of arteriosclerosis and coronary heart disease.

Chicken meat

Each 100 grams of chicken meat contains up to 23.3 grams of protein and only 1.2 grams of fat, much lower than various livestock meats, the best-known weight loss product. Therefore, eating some chicken appropriately not only benefits human health but also does not cause obesity.

Lean pork

Lean pork contains a relatively high amount of protein, reaching up to 29 grams per 100 grams, with a fat content of 6 grams per 100 grams, but after stewing, the fat content decreases further, making it suitable for overweight people to consume.

Three types of meat: the lighter the color, the better

Meat can be divided into three categories based on whether it has color and its depth:

Bright red or dark red: such as pork, beef, lamb, etc., called dark-colored meat or red meat.

Tender white meat: such as chicken, duck, goose, rabbit, and fish meat, etc., called light-colored meat or white meat.

Almost colorless: mainly aquatic shellfish animals, such as clam meat, oysters, and crab meat, etc., called colorless meat.

Which of the three types of meat is more beneficial to human health? Nutritionists prefer the latter two. The secret lies in the fact that the saturated fat and cholesterol content in light-colored and colorless meat is significantly lower than in red meat. Particularly noteworthy is the almost colorless meat, which has the lowest saturated fat content among any other type of meat, being only half of cheese and eggs, thereby maximizing the avoidance of increased human cholesterol. If a ranking table were to be drawn up, it would undoubtedly be: colorless - light-colored - red.
