Ajax coach to join Chelsea, he once thrashed the 'Special One'

by adfho7fk on 2011-09-07 18:16:12

A few days ago, authoritative news agencies such as Reuters all reported one after another about Chelsea's contact with Ajax coach Ten Hag to serve as the chief assistant coach, and the Blues also confirmed that they were in contact with him. Currently, the situation has become clearer, and Ajax officially confirmed that it will not create obstacles for Ten Hag to join the Blues. As long as he and Chelsea reach an agreement on personal treatment, Ten Hag can join the Ruble Army at any time.

Ajax announced on its official website: "Last week, Ten Hag talked to the club management about leaving to join Chelsea. After urgent consultations, Ajax agreed to this request." According to reports by The Guardian of the UK, Ten Hag will terminate his contract with Ajax early, and Chelsea will not have to spend a penny to get this head coach of the star factory. In Abramovich's plan, Grant will still be the head coach of Chelsea, and the Israeli will mainly be responsible for match-related matters and dealing with stakeholders. Ten Hag will serve as the player supervisor and be responsible for the daily training of players.

Like Chelsea's former head coach Mourinho, Ten Hag also has a Barcelona background. Before taking over Ajax in 06, Ten Hag was once Rijkaard's chief assistant coach in the Barcelona coaching team. In the 05-06 season, Ten Hag helped the Black Swan win the double championship of La Liga and the Champions League. However, due to his explosive temper and falling out with the Catalan authorities, Ten Hag left quietly in the summer of 06.

In a sense, Ten Hag can be considered Mourinho's successor. The two Chelsea coaches with the same Barcelona bloodline also have a history of "becoming acquainted through conflict". In the 04-05 season UEFA Champions League, Barcelona played against Chelsea at home. Mourinho accused Rijkaard of having a secret deal with referee Frisk during halftime, which led to a clash between both coaching staffs in the player tunnel. Mourinho cursed at Rijkaard, pointing his finger at his face, and at that time, Ten Hag, serving as Barcelona's chief assistant coach, kicked Mourinho in the butt. Chelsea's goalkeeper coach Laro also joined the fray, resulting in a chaotic scene. Being able to kick the defiant Mourinho, Ten Hag can be considered "one of a kind."
