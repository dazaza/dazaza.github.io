Survey: Feng Shui Master Sells "Magic Charms" on Taobao, Earns Nearly Ten Million Yuan per Year

by anonymous on 2013-08-20 14:48:48

Self-proclaimed masters, using Weibo and WeChat to build their reputations, performing fake consecrations, selling talismans on Taobao... A recent investigation by reporters has revealed that some "feng shui practitioners," under the guise of "feng shui masters" or feng shui institutions, are using internet trading platforms like Taobao to sell so-called "spiritual talismans" and other feng shui lucky charms at high prices to earn substantial profits. Despite the scams being quite rudimentary, tens of thousands of people have fallen victim. Among them, a Crown-level Taobao online store selling consecrated "spiritual talismans" reportedly earns nearly 10 million yuan annually.

Buyers fear "retribution" and give positive reviews

A certain Crown-level Taobao online store sells 160 consecrated "spiritual talismans" and "feng shui items" claimed to be drawn by a master during rituals, promising protection for life. Prices range from 38 yuan to 5,888 yuan. The "Reconciliation Charm," priced at 300 yuan, sold over a thousand units in just one month. In less than ten months since opening, the store has sold 17,826 items, earning nearly 10 million yuan annually.

It was found that some buyers complained, saying, "It's not as miraculous as the master claimed, and things have even gotten worse; I feel deceived and request a refund." However, the seller would maliciously "warn" the buyer: This fee is a "merit fee," and refunds could negatively impact the effectiveness of the talisman. Out of fear, some buyers were forced to leave positive reviews.

An 80s-born man claims the title of "Global Mentor" to amass wealth

It was learned that this online store operates a Chinese Yi Jing Feng Shui website, with Weibo accounts including an officially verified China Yi Jing Feng Shui Official Website, China Yi Jing Feng Shui, and Feng Shui Master Luo Shun. They also have accounts on WeChat, Kaixin.com, Renren.com, and Mobile Weibo, pushing feng shui content to fans. The Yi Jing Feng Shui Official Website alone has 489,000 followers. The owner of this online store is merely an 80s-born man named Luo Shun, who boasts titles such as one of the Top Ten Global Yi Jing Celebrities, Outstanding Figure in Chinese Yi Learning, and one of the Top Ten Global Distinguished Yi Learning Mentors.

Reporters discovered that these titles were issued by the "China Yi Jing Vocational Management Center," which according to Hong Kong Special Administrative Region Companies Registry data, is a private company registered in Hong Kong mainly providing feng shui consultation and training services.

Additionally, Luo Shun serves as the Honorary President of the International Yi Jing Research Institute, which is also a private company registered in Hong Kong and not a legitimate academic research institution.

Under this meticulous packaging, "Master" Luo Shun expanded his influence and used these accounts and websites to direct consumers to his Taobao store to purchase feng shui items and spiritual talismans, achieving his goal of making money.

It was learned that registering in places like Hong Kong and the United States, conducting feng shui consultation and training businesses domestically, participating in or hosting global and Chinese-named feng shui forums and conferences to enhance their prestige, obtaining certificates with titles like "global," "Chinese," "China," and "international," and exaggerating their influence through social networks like Weibo, blogs, and WeChat, have become the standard path for many pseudo-feng shui masters.

Follow-up: Why has Taobao become the "headquarters" for "masters" to amass wealth?

It was learned that there are 1.012 million types of "consecrated" feng shui lucky charms available for sale on Taobao, and there are no shortage of those who use deceptive tactics to make money.

Regarding the situation of pseudo-"masters" scamming online, several interviewed experts pointed out that the key issues lie in loose management of online transactions, inadequate oversight by trading platforms, and consumers' lack of feng shui knowledge making it difficult for them to discern authenticity. Industry insiders pointed out that Taobao is an important circulation market for "feng shui lucky charms" and has become a "headquarters" for some "feng shui masters" to amass wealth.

Renowned Sinology master Lou Yuelie stated that reasons such as the lack of faith, the unpredictability of life, and policy changes have led people to seek the unknown through fortune-telling and attempt to change their fate through "spiritual talismans."

Vice President and Secretary-General of the Wutai Mountain Buddhist Association, Master Beiyue, said that currently, society is in a transitional period. In a restless social environment, some people feel spiritually empty and hope to find support, thus seeking psychological comfort through consecrated Buddhist artifacts. Some scams "exploit loopholes in people's lack of understanding."
