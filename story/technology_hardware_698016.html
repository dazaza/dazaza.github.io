Chelsea has officially announced that the prices for all seats at Stamford Bridge for the 2012/13 season will remain unchanged, including those in the VIP area. Instead, ticket prices for the Champions League group stage matches have been reduced.

The announcement on Chelsea's official website states that there will be no increase in next season's ticket prices. This applies to season tickets, match-day tickets for members, as well as general adult, youth, and senior citizen tickets. The price of all types of Premier League match tickets will remain the same as this season. Chelsea categorizes their Premier League matches into three levels: AA, A, and B.

Next season, Chelsea will continue with the discount policy for FA Cup and League Cup tickets, keeping the prices for these domestic cup matches unchanged. Additionally, Chelsea has brought some good news for fans - if they successfully qualify for next season's Champions League, they will reduce the price of group stage tickets. Adult ticket prices will drop from £40 to £35, while senior and youth tickets will decrease from £20 to £17.50 (for the East Stand Family Area, adult tickets will be £35 next season, with senior and youth tickets seeing an additional £2 reduction to £15.50).

Furthermore, ticket prices for all matches in the VIP area will remain unchanged. Chelsea CEO Ron Gourlay expressed that the decision not to raise ticket prices is a way to thank fans for their support, especially considering the current economic climate. He said, "The club has decided not to raise ticket prices next season because we deeply appreciate your loyal support at Stamford Bridge. We believe it is the right move under current economic conditions, as everyone's budget may be a bit tight."

Currently, Arsenal holds the title for the highest ticket prices in the Premier League, with London-based Premier League teams generally having high ticket prices. Chelsea experienced instability and poor performance this season, so announcing that next season's ticket prices will remain unchanged is indeed a clever strategy to win back fan support. In contrast to Chelsea, Manchester United's ticket prices have risen almost every season since the Glazer family took over.
