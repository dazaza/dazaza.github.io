After a man swallowed a blade and held a boy hostage, the security guard took decisive action to save the child.

by dr56581zf7 on 2012-03-02 08:28:25

At 10:20 AM yesterday morning, the ambulance of Huangjiang People's Hospital picked up a "special" patient. This Mr. Li, about 20 years old, claimed to have swallowed a razor blade. Lying on the stretcher bed in pain and moaning, his emotions were very unstable. Meanwhile, Mr. Zhang, the hospital security guard on duty, was directing parking at the scene.

**Incident: Man Swallows Razor Blade and Holds Boy Hostage at Hospital**

When interviewed, Mr. Zhang appeared very calm about his actions: "Regardless of whether he (Mr. Li) had a knife or not, I had to act, especially since he was unarmed. At that moment, I had only one thought: Absolutely, he must not harm that child."

**Text and Photos by Reporter Xie Yingjun**

Currently, Mr. Li has undergone surgery to remove the blade and is out of danger. The police are still investigating further.

A dramatic and dangerous scene akin to an action movie unfolded around 10 AM yesterday morning in the dispensing hall of Huangjiang People's Hospital. A man suspected of having suffered mental confusion due to drug use first mutilated himself by swallowing a blade, then held a boy hostage. Fortunately, the brave security guard acted quickly and subdued him within minutes.

It is reported that Mr. Zhang has worked in Huangjiang for over six years and usually loves to "stand up for justice." This wasn't the first time he had intervened to save someone. "In the past, I've caught drug users and thieves," said Mr. Zhang. Regarding whether this rescue would earn him an award for bravery or how the boy's parents might thank him, "I haven't even thought about it."

**Yesterday Morning Incident at Huangjiang People's Hospital**

**Man Suspected of Mental Confusion Due to Drug Use**

Security Guard: "This is an Ordinary Matter"

After being alerted, security guards Mr. Zhang and his colleagues blocked his path. Seeing this, Mr. Li retreated to a corner against the wall, choking the boy with his hands as he confronted the crowd. After about two minutes of standoff, the police had yet to arrive, and Mr. Li became increasingly uncontrollable. Suddenly, he fiercely scratched near the boy's eye socket and shouted, "If you come any closer, I'll gouge out his eyes!" In this critical moment, Security Guard Mr. Zhang leapt up, his hands like "eagle claws" tightly gripping Mr. Li's left hand. At this point, bystanders rushed forward to pin him down, finally freeing the boy.

Subsequently, the police arrived, controlled, and took away Mr. Li. The entire process lasted only about ten minutes, and the incident did not cause panic. Aside from the dispensing hall, other areas of the hospital continued to operate normally. When reporters arrived at the scene at 11 AM, the hospital had already restored order, and Security Guard Mr. Zhang, who decisively intervened to save the boy, went home for lunch as usual.

**Police: Suspected Loss of Control Due to Mental Illness**

Yesterday afternoon, the Huangjiang police informed us that Mr. Li was discovered by passersby at around 9:30 AM on Street 10 of Shebei Village, Shebei Fourth Street, lying on the ground in apparent agony. After questioning nearby residents, the police learned that Mr. Li had allegedly swallowed an object resembling a blade. Officers immediately called the 120 ambulance service for treatment. Unexpectedly, after arriving at the hospital, Mr. Li suddenly "attacked," and his intentions remain unknown. It is suspected that he may have a "mental disorder" leading to loss of control, though it has not been confirmed whether he had taken drugs beforehand. We also learned that Mr. Li has now undergone surgery to remove the blade. Other details are still under further investigation.

"Don't come any closer, or I'll gouge out his eyes!" Mr. Li, suspected of a mental breakdown, began applying force as he spoke, scratching blood marks near the eye socket of the 10-year-old boy. Just as he said this, Security Guard Mr. Zhang suddenly leaped up, his "eagle claw" firmly "grabbing" Mr. Li's left hand. Bystanders rushed forward and pinned Mr. Li to the ground.

After being lifted off the vehicle, an unexpected scene unfolded. Mr. Li screamed, jumped off the stretcher bed, and rushed into the nearest dispensing hall, colliding with a mother and son waiting in line. Without a word, Mr. Li grabbed the 10-year-old boy, holding him in front of his chest as he tried to rush outside.
