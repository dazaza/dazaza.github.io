I. Causes of Stretch Marks Formation

Medical research tells us:

The "main director" behind the changes in a pregnant woman's appearance during pregnancy is the hormones within her body. In the early stages of pregnancy, hormones are primarily produced by the ovarian corpus luteum. As the demand for hormones increases, the placenta takes over as the main hormone producer. At the same time, the workload of other glands such as the thyroid, pancreas, and adrenal glands also increases.

Among the many hormones, estrogen, progesterone, oxytocin, and prolactin play a decisive role in major metabolic activities during pregnancy. They effectively regulate the metabolic processes in the mother's body during pregnancy, promote the development of the uterus, mobilize the mother's reserves to meet the needs of fetal growth and development, and promote breast development.

However, an increase in hormone secretion can lead to pigmentation on the skin surface, with the "culprit" being the cortisone in the pregnant woman's body. The mechanism is that after pregnancy, the secretion function of the adrenal gland is enhanced, causing an increase in cortisone, which leads to stretch marks on the skin surface and dark brown spots on the face.

6. Persistent Itching in the Abdomen

Pregnant women often complain about itchy bellies, which is caused by skin irritation due to belly expansion. Applying some moisturizing lotion or massage cream can alleviate and relieve symptoms.

However, if a pregnant woman cannot sleep due to itching and notices the gradual formation of papules from the abdomen to the thighs or large patches, she must consult a doctor, as it could be prurigo. The exact cause of prurigo remains unclear, but according to statistics, prurigo usually occurs in the last three months of pregnancy. Although it does not harm the health of the pregnant woman or fetus, it causes suffering to the pregnant woman, with some even unable to sleep due to itching. For this, the doctor will prescribe treatments such as antipruritic drugs or oral medications.
