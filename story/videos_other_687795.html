We boarded the boat from Koh Samui to Koh Tao, finding a spot on the deck, mingling among the farangs, though in reality, we were the farangs. I don't know what day it is today. We only have the check-out time reminding us to head to the next destination. Koh Tao is located 45 kilometers north of Koh Samui and is called Turtle Island due to its resemblance to a turtle. This small island with an area of only 21 square kilometers rests on a coral reef. Most of the island is mountainous, with only 30% being flat land, yet it boasts Thailand's best waters. Here, there is excellent underwater visibility, lush and beautiful corals, abundant marine life, and provides an outstanding diving environment, making it a well-known diving and snorkeling destination in Thailand. On Koh Tao, you can choose from over 40 diving companies, and it is reputed to be the cheapest place to learn diving. We will be staying at Ban’s Diving Resort. We had already contacted them on our first day in Bangkok. Even if we hadn't made contact, it wouldn't matter. As soon as we boarded the boat to Koh Tao, many diving schools approached us for marketing. Going to a diving school doesn't necessarily mean you have to dive; they also provide relatively complete accommodations. Actually, Koh Tao and diving are inseparable. On Koh Tao, no hotel will fail to provide diving equipment. Relevant knowledge about diving will be introduced later. After more than 2 hours of travel, we arrived at the Koh Tao pier. We still enjoyed the reception service. Ban’s staff held up signs to pick us up, along with a British couple. We boarded Ban’s taxi. As mentioned earlier, Koh Tao is not very big. Later, we measured it, and it takes only about 10 minutes to walk from Ban’s to the pier. There is a very good BBQ by the pier, but let's leave that aside for now. Talking about taking the taxi, it was actually a truck modified into a vehicle, open-top, with people and luggage placed at the back. The ride from the pier to Ban’s, on a bumpy road, became our inevitable path for boat diving later. The high-voltage lines on both sides of the road were set quite low, buzzing. A remote and desolate feeling, without any signs of life, like traveling in the wilderness, truly reaching the rural areas of Thailand, unable to see the sea. Later, we learned that this road is merely the main traffic route of Koh Tao, while the concentrated areas are near the sea, with a continuous several-kilometer-long seaside road filled with various restaurants, convenience stores, ATMs, tattoo shops, massage places, and travel agencies.

Arriving at Ban’s felt like entering a paradise, with tropical plants densely covering the area, houses distributed wave-like on both sides of the plants, roaming farangs, and a soothing incense smell. Looking down from where we stayed, there was an unmatched ocean view. And for just 500B per night, it was really good. With air conditioning, hot water, television (able to receive MTV channel), it was very clean and comfortable. We spent 5 days on Koh Tao.

The check-in process was quick, and all expenses were settled at check-out without requiring any deposit. This was the only such experience during the entire trip. It was already sunset, and we had an appointment to meet our diving instructor Karen at 6 pm. We also met two couples from Kunming. One couple was learning intermediate courses, while the other couple and we were learning beginner courses, the Open Water course.

Here, let me briefly talk about diving. Why do you need to learn and get certified? Simply put, if you wear goggles, carry a gas cylinder, wear fins, and bite a breathing tube to go underwater, you need a diving license, just like how you need a license to drive a car. If you're just in shallow water, submerging your head to watch fish and using a tube to breathe outside air, that's called snorkeling, purely for entertainment. The Open Water course is the start of scuba diving, a 3-4 day course after which you can dive up to 18 meters deep. Based on the beginner level, another 2 days of study can reach the intermediate level, allowing you to dive up to 30 meters deep. Amateur enthusiasts and enthusiasts can dive at most up to 40 meters deep. If anyone is interested, you can search for relevant information. The price for learning an OW course on Koh Tao ranges between 8000-10000B, including basic accommodation for 4 days, reputedly the cheapest place in the world to learn diving. The issued diving certificate is universally recognized worldwide.

Returning to the topic, after meeting Karen, because the couple taking the intermediate course needed to dive at night, my and YY's lessons were scheduled to start tomorrow. Karen is a Singaporean Chinese woman who speaks Chinese, English, and Thai, very impressive. On this island, you hardly see Asians, let alone Chinese. We were lucky to meet a coach who speaks Chinese, teaching us in a one-to-two manner. The four compatriots from Kunming, we spoke our Sichuan dialect, they spoke their Yunnan dialect, it was very satisfying.

On the first night on Koh Tao, the evening glow was mesmerizing. This photo is of the famous tilted coconut tree. The ghost fires by the sea, initially, I thought it was just decoration, but later I found out that when the tide rises, these fires serve as boundaries.

The prices on Koh Tao have doubled. The same items at 7-11 cost twice as much on Koh Tao compared to Samui, so it is strongly recommended to prepare everything needed before going to the island. The cheapest drinking water is the white plastic bottle packaging, 30B for 6 bottles. The food prices are similar to Samui, with seafood slightly higher than Samui. To protect the ecological environment, seafood harvesting is prohibited on the islands, so the seafood here does not have a price advantage.

Life on Koh Tao begins, and you can sleep until you wake up naturally. We discovered that all life on the island becomes very casual and irregular, truly doing whatever you want, eating whenever you want without time restrictions. During the day, you sunbathe, and at night, this place becomes a huge bar.

The most famous SPA on Koh Tao is Jamahkir, enjoying SPA facing the sea. On the last day of our diving, as the nitrogen in our bodies slowly dissipated, we decided to thoroughly indulge ourselves at Jamahkir. If it weren't for a car to pick us up, we would never have been able to find this paradise. Moreover, if it weren't for their four-wheel-drive pickup trucks, we would never have been able to climb those crazy steep slopes. Traveling on mountain roads without road signs, if it weren't for driving, you wouldn't even realize you were on a road. The car drove up the mountain, feeling like another world, indeed very quiet, looking at the sea from the top of the mountain, more beautiful, and wider. After changing clothes, we first took a sauna, then lay on tatami mats, covered with cleaning and moisturizing lotion, wrapped like mummies, required to lie for 30 minutes. Listening to flowing clouds and yoga-like music, I quickly fell asleep. After waking up, I cleaned myself in the outdoor shower, then started facial SPA, followed by body MASSAGE, a series of kneading, rubbing, tapping, and finally a hot back treatment. The whole SPA lasted about 3 hours, with the cost for two people around 5000B, considered the biggest luxury of our trip.

Because of its resemblance to a turtle, it is also called Turtle Island. This small island with an area of only 21 square kilometers rests on a coral reef. Most of the island is mountainous, with only 30% being flat land, yet it has Thailand's best waters. Here, there is excellent underwater visibility, lush and beautiful corals, abundant marine life, and provides an outstanding diving environment, making it a well-known diving and snorkeling destination in Thailand. There are over 40 diving companies to choose from on Koh Samui and Koh Tao, with snorkeling prices approximately 800 THB per session, ten sessions around 5500 THB. A four-day professional diving certification course costs around 8000 THB.

The crystal-clear seawater and colorful tropical fish make you feel like walking into the world of "Finding Nemo."

Koh Tao offers comprehensive accommodation and dining services, detailed in the accommodation and dining introduction.

Transportation: Taking a boat from Koh Samui to Koh Tao takes 2 hours. Both Bophut Pier and Mae Nam Pier have ferries, with round-trip prices around 850 THB, including hotel pick-up and drop-off. The main transportation on Koh Tao is pick-up taxis, regardless of distance, 50 THB per person.
