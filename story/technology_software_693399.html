Sun Nan's trip to Kunming, Yunnan also drew much attention from many fans who followed his trail. Let's take a look at some places to visit in Kunming!

Sun Nan’s scenic spots in Yunnan Kunming

With its beautiful natural scenery, brilliant historical relics, and colorful ethnic customs, Kunming has become one of the top ten tourist hotspots in the country and was among the first batch of excellent tourist cities in China.

The city has more than 200 protected cultural relics at various levels, including the Shilin World Geopark, Dianchi Lake, Anning Hot Springs, Jiuxiang, Yangzonghai, and Jiazi Snow Mountain, which are famous national and provincial scenic spots. There are also over 100 key scenic spots such as the World Horticultural Exposition Garden and the Yunnan Ethnic Village. More than a dozen national tourism routes form a tourism system centered around Kunming, radiating throughout the province and connecting Southeast Asia, integrating tourism, sightseeing, vacationing, and entertainment. The tourism industry continues to show growth trends.

Sun Nan’s Shilin Scenic Spot in Yunnan Kunming

The Shilin Scenic Area is located in Lunan County, 86 kilometers away from Kunming City. The scenic area consists of seven scenic zones: Dashi Lin (Big Stone Forest), Xiaoshi Lin (Small Stone Forest), Naigu Stone Forest, Dadi Shui (Great Waterfall), Changhu Lake, Yuelu Lake, Zhiyun Cave, and Qifeng Cave. The entire county has a stone forest area of 400 square kilometers, making it a world-renowned scenic area dominated by karst landforms, hailed as the "First Marvel under Heaven."

Karst landforms, also known as limestone landforms, refer to phenomena where soluble rocks (mainly limestone) dissolve due to water, leading to erosion, sedimentation, collapse, sinkholes, and accumulation, forming unique landform features such as stone forests, stone peaks, stone buds, sinkholes, swallow holes, underground rivers, and peculiar dragon ponds, numerous lakes, etc. These phenomena collectively are named Karst (after the Karst Plateau in northwest Yugoslavia). Karst landforms are distributed across provinces such as Yunnan, Guizhou, Guangxi, Guangdong, Fujian, and Sichuan in China, among which the best-developed and most beautiful stone forests are found here. In 1966, the second Chinese Karst Academic Conference suggested renaming "Karst landforms" to "Limestone landforms," hence in China, they are also called Limestone landforms.

Sun Nan’s Jiuxiang Scenic Spot in Yunnan Kunming

Jiuxiang Caves in Kunming is a national-level scenic spot located in Yiliang County, Kunming City. It is about 40 kilometers away from Yiliang. People have different opinions on how Jiuxiang got its name. Jiuxiang is a national key scenic spot and an emerging comprehensive scenic area in Yunnan Province, featuring mainly cave landscapes, combined with natural scenery outside the caves, cultural landscapes, and ethnic customs.

Sun Nan’s visit to the Expo Garden in Yunnan Kunming

Kunming World Horticultural Exposition Garden (briefly called Expo Garden) is the site of the '99 Kunming World Horticultural Exposition. The overall planning of the park follows the natural terrain, creating a grand and harmonious atmosphere. It integrates local characteristics from all provinces, regions, and municipalities in China along with garden horticulture masterpieces from 95 countries worldwide, reflecting the theme of "Harmonious Development between Man and Nature." It is a world-class horticultural masterpiece garden with "Yunnan Characteristics, Chinese Grandeur, and World-Class Standards."

The park includes five main venues: International Pavilion, China Pavilion, Man and Nature Pavilion, Science and Technology Pavilion, and the Large Greenhouse; seven thematic exhibition gardens: Arboretum, Bamboo Garden, Penjing Garden, Medicinal Herb Garden, Tea Garden, Vegetable and Fruit Garden, and the newly built Famous Flower Art Stone Garden after the exposition; and three outdoor exhibition areas: International Outdoor Exhibition Area, China Outdoor Exhibition Area, and Corporate Outdoor Exhibition Area. A total of 2,551 species of plants, over 2 million plants (clusters) were cultivated and transplanted, including 112 rare and endangered plant species, setting the highest standards for A1-level World Horticultural Expositions ever held. Additionally, it set eight "World Guinness Records" in terms of land area, construction speed, plant species diversity, horticultural masterpieces, greenhouse size, bamboo species diversity, Cupressaceae transplantation, and artificial rockwork cliffs.

Sun Nan’s visit to Dianchi Lake in Yunnan Kunming

Located at the foot of Xishan Mountain south of Kunming City, its northern end adjoins the Daguan Park in Kunming City, while its southern end reaches into Jinning County, about 5 kilometers away from the urban area. Historically, this place has always been a resort for holidays and summer retreats. In Chen Yunhe's poem "Dianchi", "the mirror of Spring City dressed up, Dianchi reflects the lean history and the prosperous reality; both history and reality squat by the waterside of Hai Geng, repeatedly cleansing their thoughts." The ancient name of Dianchi Lake was Diannanzhai, also known as Kunming Lake, about 20 kilometers away from Kunming City. Rivers like Panlong River and more than twenty others converge into Dianchi Lake from the southeast and north, and the lake water flows out from the west through Haikou and enters the Jinsha River via Pudu River. Its shape resembles a crescent moon, stretching 39 kilometers from north to south, 13.5 kilometers wide from east to west, with an average width of about 8 kilometers. The shoreline is approximately 200 kilometers long; the lake covers an area of 300 square kilometers, ranking first in Yunnan Province. The maximum depth of the lake water is 8 meters, with an average depth of 5 meters, storing 1.57 billion cubic meters of water, commonly referred to as "Five Hundred Li Dianchi Lake." It is the sixth largest inland freshwater lake in China.

Sun Nan’s visit to Yunnan Nationalities Village in Kunming

Located in the national-level holiday resort area by the side of Dianchi Lake in the southwestern suburbs of Kunming City, Yunnan Province, covering an area of 89 hectares, 8 kilometers away from the city center, backed by Dianchi Lake, facing the Xishan Forest Park, Daguan Park, Zheng He Park, etc., across the water, it is a new emerging holiday resort and window showcasing the social, cultural, and folk customs of the 26 ethnic groups in Yunnan Province, a national AAAA-level tourist attraction, national ethnic culture base of the National Ethnic Affairs Commission, traditional folk culture base of the CIOFF China Committee, and one of the first national ethnic work contact points of the National Ethnic Affairs Commission.

Sun Nan’s visit to Daguan Tower in Kunming

Daguan Tower is located in the western part of Kunming City, about 6 kilometers away from the city center. It was originally built during the Kangxi period and named so because it faces Dianchi Lake and overlooks the Xishan Mountains, encompassing the beauty of the lake and mountains. After entering the park, visitors can tour pavilions and terraces such as Yongyue Pavilion, Ningbi Hall, Lansheng Pavilion, and Guanjia Hall. Among these, the Daguan Tower, built near the water, stands three stories high and contains many exquisite inscriptions and couplets. The 180-character couplet written by the Qing scholar Sun Ran翁 hangs on the door pillars facing the water, known as the "Longest Couplet in History."

Sun Nan’s visit to Xishan Mountain in Kunming

Kunming Xishan Mountain is a national forest park, a national AAAA-level scenic spot, and one of the core scenic spots of the National Dianchi Resort Area. It is located 15 kilometers west of Kunming City, consisting of Huating Mountain, Taihua Mountain, Luohan Mountain, etc. It stretches for more than 40 kilometers, with an altitude ranging from 1,900 to 2,350 meters. According to legend, ancient phoenixes used to rest here, but people did not recognize them and called them Biji, hence it is also known as Biji Mountain. Due to its shape resembling a reclining Buddha, it is also called Wofoshan.

Sun Nan’s visit to Cuilan Lake in Kunming

Cuilan Lake is located at the foot of Wuhua Mountain in Kunming City, covering an area of 352 mu. Northeast of the lake, "nine springs converge to form a pool," hence it is also called "Nine Dragon Pool." Originally known as "Cai Hai Zi," during the early Qing Dynasty, Prince Wu Sangui "filled half of Cai Hai Zi to build a new palace," renaming it "Honghua Palace," later changed to "Chenghua Pu." During the Kangxi period of the Qing Dynasty, Governor-General Fan Chengxun and Governor Wang Jiwen built the Biyi Pavilion (commonly known as Hai Xin Pavilion) in the middle of the lake, with shimmering water and lush green trees. During Tang Jiyao's time, embankments were built in the east-west and north-south directions, dividing the lake into four parts. There is a Hai Xin Pavilion in the middle of the lake, a Guanyu Hall on the west side, and a Shuiyue Pavilion on the southeast. The embankments are lined with weeping willows, their branches brushing against your face. Inside the lake, lotus flowers abound, exuding fragrance, earning the old saying "Ten acres of lotus flowers in a fish world, half the city shaded by willow trees caressing the pavilions," praising it as "a jade in the city." In recent years, additional pavilions, terraces, corridors, and curved bridges have been added, their architecture delicate and intricate. Each pavilion has a plaque or couplet singing the praises of the landscape. Before the Guanyu Hall, there is a couplet written by Huang Kuiguang: "A pavilion stands gracefully, occupying one-tenth of the green water; When will I have leisure time, to drink with the bright moon thrice."

Every winter, tens of thousands of red-beaked seagulls from the north fly to Cuilan Lake to spend the winter, playing in the water, competing for food, adding endless charm to the lake, becoming one of the major sights of the Spring City. With pavilions on the lake, pavilions on the island, famous trees and flowers, and famous people's inscriptions, Cuilan Lake is adorned even more beautifully, making it a great place for people to relax and tour, and one of the famous scenic spots in Kunming City.

Sun Nan’s visit to Jinma and Biji Arches in Kunming

The Jinma and Biji Arches in Kunming are located at the intersection of San Shi Street and Jinbi Road in the city center, standing 12 meters high and 18 meters wide, with exquisitely carved beams and painted rafters. The eastern arch is named Jinma Arch because it faces Jinma Mountain, while the western arch is named Biji Arch because it faces Biji Mountain. To the north, it complements the "Zhong'ai Arch" commemorating the crossing of Dianchi Lake, together forming the "Pinzi Three Arches," becoming a bustling scene in Kunming; to the south, it corresponds with the East-West Temple Pagodas built during the Nanzhao Dynasty, displaying Kunming's ancient civilization. The Jinma Arch was initially built during the Xuande period of the Ming Dynasty, nearly four hundred years ago.
