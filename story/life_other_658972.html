Who can understand the sorrow in one's heart?

by huiming on 2012-02-04 21:21:46

Who can understand the sorrow in my heart?

If we could hold on to those feelings that cannot be retained, there wouldn't be so many tragedies in the world. I like to look up at the sky; it's so blue and pure. I like to watch the sunset, which is red like blood. I don't know how many more times I will be able to see these scenes. Don't you know this is my sadness? You can never comfort me.

Everything is predestined, and I should let go of those things that shouldn't linger in my mind. Perhaps they will be forgotten in the midst of constant remembrance. If time could start over, I hope I could stay in the age of youthful recklessness, when we were still innocent and didn't understand harm or happiness. We just blindly pursued the vague impulses in our hearts.

I can never escape the loneliness of the past; the happiness I have now is merely wandering and searching through the memories of the past. I have always waited for your concern, tirelessly masking that sorrow. I've become accustomed to being sad, used to waiting, and used to your indifference towards me, but I haven't learned how to miss you. Time passes second by second, making us understand each other more. When we look up at the night sky, we always remember that lost beauty.

When I think of you, is it a form of comfort or am I stuck in deep regret? Maybe becoming strangers is a form of relief and a kind of destiny for us. The lies you told may have been out of concern; no one can decipher the laughter, and bitter smiles replace the tears in my heart. Those promises we once made seem so distant now. We never knew that our beginning was also the countdown to our end. Everything is so calm, without any ripples.

There are too many endings in life that we cannot change, and all we can do is try to forget some memories that are too heavy to bear. We are just two parallel lines on two different planes, walking in the same direction but never intersecting.

We are just young children, innocent angels who are kind enough to make people feel sorry for us. We learn to hold our heads high just so the tears in our eyes won't fall. Even though we're hurt, we'll pretend to be strong and tell you, "I'm fine."

I can't forget the recklessness of youth,

Time goes on, years continue, but who can understand the sorrow in my heart...
