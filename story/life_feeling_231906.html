The purest love in the world is the love of parents.

by lwf225 on 2009-09-02 00:24:18

Tags: Emotional Diary, QQ Zone Diary, Classic QQ Zone Diary, Touching QQ Zone Diary

Everyone hopes that their life is full of happiness and free from sorrow. I think that's impossible. In one's life, there will be many ups and downs and difficulties. Isn't it often said that "life" is made up of setbacks, hardships, and suffering? In our lives, I believe we will encounter these things. When we face them, we must not escape! Because escaping is just shirking responsibility and not a way to solve the problem. I think that in this world, many things can't be explained clearly with words alone; they must be demonstrated through "action."

I really like Zhang Shaohan's song "", because it brings new hope and motivation to those who are helpless. You should give it a listen too! The beautiful melody and moving lyrics will surely untie your heart knots. It will be your guiding light and courage refueling station. Each of us will have, as the song says, a pair of invisible wings. I believe that with this pair of wings that will never break, your life will be colorful. In life, "sourness, sweetness, bitterness, and spiciness" are inevitable; they are the "seasonings" in our lives. Actually, sometimes when you think about it, aren't we all lucky ones? In fact, we are the majority who don't appreciate our blessings and only know how to take without knowing how to be satisfied. I think this mainly comes from being "only children" and parents' "excessive love," which has influenced today's children.

Only through hardship do we grow up; only through being moved do we understand; only through happiness do we feel fulfilled. From ancient times to the present, there are many examples. For instance: 1. Sun Bin had a leg disability but was an outstanding military strategist in China; 2. Helen Keller was deaf and blind but had extraordinary introspective power; 3. Einstein once had learning difficulties but his scientific achievements are evident for all to see; 4. Beethoven was deaf but he was a giant in the music world, etc. Compared to us, these celebrities make us seem insignificant. Compared to them, we are happy and content. So, how do we look at them? Do we mock or admire them? I think we should admire their courage and attitude towards life. But what lies behind them? There is only one answer: "hardship." As the saying goes, "a minute on stage requires ten years of effort off stage." Their sweat and efforts have brought them the honor they enjoy today. However, "hardship" may be something that our post-90s generation "can't handle." Because "someone else has handled it," namely, our loving parents and relatives. If these people can succeed, why can't we? "Hardship" is an intangible wealth; it gives us the drive to improve and makes us face challenges without bowing down or resisting, striving upward instead. In today's society, I think enduring hardship is almost impossible. Having the spirit of endurance is enough. Sometimes, when you think about others' experiences and then consider your own good fortune, it becomes the best motivation and remedy. What others can achieve, we can achieve too.

The sky isn't always blue, clouds aren't always white; plants aren't always green, and flowers won't forever bloom. From babbling babies to sunset years, how many people truly have smooth sailing? Viewing life's hardships and pain with a different perspective, perhaps you'll discover that enduring hardship is also a form of wealth in life's journey.

Here, we must correctly approach the concept of "self." Only by understanding "self" can we comprehend life. The premise of self-awareness is accepting oneself. Accepting oneself means believing in oneself, liking oneself, accepting oneself, caring for oneself, trusting oneself, not deceiving oneself, not rejecting oneself, and certainly not hating oneself. You should feel that you are unique, valuable, and growing. Everyone in this world is one of a kind. Just like there are no two identical leaves in the world, we need to live out the value of life, earn respect from others, first by joyfully accepting ourselves, and continuously improving through various means. For those realities that have become unchangeable, rather than complaining and being troubled every day, it's better to accept them calmly. We must learn to perfect ourselves, and doing so involves three points:

1. Being good at mastering oneself and playing to one's strengths.

2. Paying attention to absorbing others' advantages to compensate for one’s shortcomings.

3. Courageously accepting challenges and constantly advancing.

This way, your life will be very exciting and splendid. You will also find living to be very joyful and happy.

In life, there are three major "touching moments," which are family affection, friendship, and love. They occupy a large place in our lives, teaching us how to be touched and how to be human. Family affection gives us care, friendship gives us help, and love gives us sweetness. From the moment we were born from our mother's womb, we already knew how to be moved. Moved by our parents giving us all their love. Parents' love is great and unselfish. Parents all "hope their sons become dragons and daughters become phoenixes." This is truly "the pitiable hearts of parents around the world!" Sometimes parents can be harsh and unreasonable because they love their children too much and are afraid they might get hurt. But parents around the world, have you thought about how children need to grow up and will eventually leave you? Like dandelions, they fly away from their mothers when the wind blows. Have you thought about how the dandelions feel? If I were it, I would be sad and heartbroken. But I would turn that longing into a sense of gratitude. Children at seventeen or eighteen years old are in the "rebellious phase," so I hope parents can be friends with us, consider things from our perspective, and find solutions. Children should learn to understand their parents, be tolerant of their parents, and be filial to their parents, not letting them worry about themselves. I believe that every child loves their parents. Why not give your parents the best gift on holidays? The best gift is four simple words — "I love you!" Though simple, how many can actually say it?

Children of the world, remember: "The purest love in the world is the love of parents."
