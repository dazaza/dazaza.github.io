Xiaozhao's "expression economics" as a waitress

by seo1751 on 2012-02-29 17:44:51

Who is Xiaozhao? She is one of the heroines in Jin Yong's wuxia novel "The Heaven Sword and Dragon Saber", the daughter of Zisun Longwang Dai Qisi and Han Qianye. Following her mother's orders, she infiltrated the Ming Cult's headquarters at Bright Peak to steal the manual for the "Kunlun Great Shift" martial arts technique. Zhang Wujie's constant protection made Xiaozhao grateful, leading her to fall in love with him. She became his maid without hesitation, always considering his needs. Later, Xiaozhao became the leader of the Persian branch of the Ming Cult, remaining pure and chaste for life.

After introducing Xiaozhao's life story, I feel a little disappointed. If she hadn't become the leader of any cult but instead traveled through time to work as a receptionist in a modern hotel, wouldn't that be great? It's a pity about her expressive face. Nowadays, we live in an era of attention economy where facial expressions can rise to the level of economics and receive significant attention, such as "smile service." If you have big, twinkling eyes, you could charm a crowd; if you have an unusually large and ugly mouth, you might shock people. A host with rich expressions can become a broadcaster; an actor with rich expressions can become a famous star. Expressions can attract audiences, create economic value, and improve economic efficiency. I once personally heard a certain manager tell employees: "Pay attention to your eyes!"

Now let's take a look at how Miss Xiaozhao uses expressions. When she first entered the Ming Cult's Bright Peak, she had a purpose. To avoid making Yang Buhui, another young beauty, jealous, Xiaozhao (pretended) to have a smaller right eye and a larger left eye, with her nose and mouth also twisted into a very frightening shape. Her left leg was lame, and her back was bowed like a bow. I think she must have undergone some professional training; otherwise, it would be hard for a fifteen-year-old girl to maintain such a high-difficulty expression and body disguise.

As the saying goes, when looking at someone, start by looking at their face. The face is the outward appearance of a person's worth and character. What is referred to as "face" is not only about looks but mainly about facial expressions. In facial expressions, there are two important parts worth noting: the eyes and the mouth. Yang Buhui was deceived, which is understandable since even Yang Xiao, who was astute, was fooled by Xiaozhao's disguised expression, which is indeed a miracle.

If Xiaozhao were a receptionist at a hotel and Yang Xiao were a customer, then Yang Xiao would not necessarily be considered a customer disliked by the store, nor would Xiaozhao deliberately pretend to be ugly. But Yang Buhui is different. Female customers usually show a kind of inexplicable superiority towards female waitresses, especially those with beautiful faces. Not being difficult would be considered having good breeding, but hostility cannot be avoided. The effect of Xiaozhao pretending to be ugly is to eliminate the hostility of female customers, making them stay happily, eat comfortably, and pay willingly, thus creating economic value.

After meeting Zhang Wujie, Xiaozhao no longer needed to deliberately pretend to be ugly because the economic benefits brought by small customers like Yang Buhui could be ignored, while Zhang Wujie was the potential big customer and the real main client.

Upon their first meeting, when Zhang Wujie sighed and said, "Originally you...you are so beautiful!" Xiaozhao smiled and said, "I was scared silly and forgot to put on my fake face!" Then she straightened her body. Originally, she was neither hunchbacked nor lame-footed. "Her eyes were bright and spirited, her eyebrows fine and her nose straight, with dimples on her cheeks, truly unmatched in beauty. Only because she was young, her figure was not fully grown, although her face was extraordinarily beautiful, it still couldn't hide the childishness." I'm unclear why Zhang Wujie sighed, perhaps lamenting that the beautiful-faced girl before him was unfortunately hunchbacked and lame-footed. However, the change in Xiaozhao's expression clearly carried the mark of interest: this handsome customer should not be missed.

Mastering expressions and skillfully utilizing them is Xiaozhao's professional skill as a hotel waitress, especially her eyes, which are particularly expressive. Many writers love to describe a person's inner feelings through their eyes, such as smiling pupils and greedy glances, all showing the close relationship between eyes and the soul. I remember a poem describing eyes: "Eyes are the windows of the soul, they will not conceal or lie. Anger splashes sparks, sorrow pours down tears, they add a bright shine to laughter." Clearly, the art of highlighting details has been deeply ingrained. Mr. Jin Yong is a master who excels at portraying characters' inner worlds through their eyes, and Xiaozhao is the most representative.

When leaving Bright Peak, Zhang Wujie did not plan to take Xiaozhao with him, but Xiaozhao chased after him herself, and deliberately let her face be illuminated by "the strong light reflected off the snow," probably with a pitiful look in her eyes. Zhang Wujie saw it and praised, "Xiaozhao, you look very nice." When Zhang Wujie said she was prettier than "Central Plains girls," Xiaozhao slightly frowned her eyebrows and said, "I'd rather be like your Central Plains girls."

Interestingly, Master Zhang did not blame her but was pleased, showing that Xiaozhao's study of emotional economics was indeed excellent. This is why Yang Buhui felt jealous: "Xiaozhao, you pretended so well. I knew you were strange, but I didn't expect such an ugly thing to be a charming little beauty."

Beautiful face, pitiful eyes sometimes with crystal tears, plus "slightly frowning eyebrows," these expressions of Xiaozhao were enough to captivate Zhang Wujie. Strangely, even when facing rivals, Xiaozhao's expressions often changed and leaned towards economic interests.

When Zhao Min gave a pearl flower to Zhang Wujie, he casually handed it over to Xiaozhao, and Zhao Min discovered it: just as she was about to speak, she turned her eyes and saw a pearl flower inserted on Xiaozhao's temple, exactly the one she had given to Zhang Wujie, making her extremely angry. Seeing Xiaozhao with bright eyes and a radiant smile, delicate and charming despite her youth, resembling morning dew on a lotus flower, Zhao Min felt even more hatred. Biting her teeth, she told Da Ajia, "Go chop off the arms of this Zhang boy!" Facing such a strong enemy, why did Xiaozhao still maintain such an expression? Of course, it was intentionally to provoke Zhao Min because Zhao Min was a competitor in the same business and also owned a large "hotel." Competitors are natural enemies, so who else should she provoke?

When Zhao Min started to consider not running her original "hotel" anymore and frequently went out alone with Zhang Wujie for drinks and chats, Xiaozhao's expression changed again. For example, in Dadu, when Zhang Wujie used Zhao Min's Tianyi Sword to cut Xiaozhao's chains, her expression was "her face blushed red, and her eyes shone with joy." Why did she blush, and why was she happy? The hope of marrying Zhang Wujie became more distant, thinking about it naturally made her blush. Since Zhao Min stopped running her hotel, Xiaozhao's hotel naturally gained a big customer, and Xiaozhao's joy was justified.

Because Xiaozhao successfully completed her duties as a waitress, bringing enormous economic benefits to the hotel, the board unanimously decided to elect Xiaozhao as chairman. Xiaozhao came in a small boat to invite Zhang Wujie and others aboard the Persian big ship. Xie Sun asked, "Xiaozhao, did you become the leader of the Persian Ming Cult?" Suddenly, two shiny tears hung from Xiaozhao's big eyes, flowing down her jade-like cheeks, followed by more tears streaming continuously. Did she really not want to leave her original position? Probably not. Even as chairman, she still had to consider the long-term interests of the hotel. Using such an expression could win the permanent consumption of big customer Zhang Wujie, making it worthwhile!

Facial expression economy is an important part of modern service economy and plays an increasingly huge role in economic life. This is most fully demonstrated in late-night television shopping programs. Just look at those program hosts, all with rich expressions and exaggerated postures, managing to sell some items that people don't really want at sky-high prices. I suggest hotel waitresses learn more about Xiaozhao's facial expression economics and use more "^_^" instead of "⊙︿⊙". If Xiaozhao worked as a hotel receptionist, I would definitely go. Would you?
