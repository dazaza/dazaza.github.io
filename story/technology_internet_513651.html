China Securities Regulatory Commission Branch Talks With Shi Yuzhu About His Weibo Post on Stocks, Weibo Claims Self-Castration and Close Blog

by edivista on 2011-10-22 10:07:52

Click to enter Shi Yuzhu's Weibo

At noon on October 21, 2011, Shi Yuzhu, chairman and CEO of Giant Network, announced on his Sina Weibo that he would self-castrate and close his blog. This move sparked speculation from many business tycoons and netizens.

The cause of the incident probably stems from a message disclosed by The Beijing News. The paper reported that at a training session for directors of listed companies held yesterday, Yu Hui, director of the First Division of Listed Company Supervision of the Beijing Securities Regulatory Bureau, revealed that Shi Yuzhu had been summoned by the Beijing Securities Regulatory Bureau due to information disclosed on Weibo about the China Life Insurance wanting to acquire Minsheng Bank. He also mentioned that this was the first time the Beijing Securities Regulatory Bureau had summoned a director because of Weibo. Additionally, Yu Hui stated that the regulatory authorities are closely monitoring the impact of new media and will take it into consideration when formulating future policies. Shi Yuzhu himself joked that he was invited for coffee; Weibo is not fun anymore, I'm done playing, self-castrating and closing my blog, but promised that the charity commitments made to his fans on Weibo would still be honored.

Trouble started with the most expensive Weibo post in history

On August 24, during the mid-year performance announcement of China Life Insurance, Vice President Liu Jiade expressed that China Life still looked favorably upon bank stocks and would provide support regardless if Minsheng Bank chose to raise capital through equity or debt in the future.

Subsequently, as a non-executive director of Minsheng Bank, Shi Yuzhu posted a Weibo message asking China Life not to eye controlling Minsheng Bank. This message was published at 9:39 AM on August 24 on his personal Sina Weibo account and was quickly deleted.

However, the deletion did not stop significant fluctuations in the capital market. The next day, Minsheng Bank's stock price surged by 6.47%, which also triggered a collective stir among bank stocks. According to financial media calculations, the gains of the day could result in an increase of 2.3 billion yuan in Shi Yuzhu's account. Speculations about Shi Yuzhu manipulating stock prices through Weibo were rampant, and there were even lawyers suggesting that the CSRC investigate the inside story of this "most expensive Weibo post in history."

However, in the following days, Shi Yuzhu continuously posted Weibo messages to clarify and promised not to sell Minsheng Bank shares within three years: I have repeatedly expressed my long-term investment plan, and I reiterate: our company will not sell any A-shares newly acquired in the secondary market or the original legal person shares of Minsheng Bank within three years. If we do, feel free to call me Black-hearted Capitalist, Villain, Faceless, Shi Meimei.

Weibo talk about stocks has been noticed by the securities regulatory system

Yesterday, Yu Hui also revealed that previously, the regulators only summoned people discussing stocks on Weibo without taking more severe measures mainly because the positioning of Weibo as a new media platform still needs discussion.

According to the Securities Law and previous regulations, Weibo cannot be considered as a public media platform. Yu Hui said, but we have seen the influence of Weibo.

Yu Hui further pointed out that the securities regulatory system has paid attention to the huge impact of new media, has monitored similar information, and will consider the influence of new media in future policy formulation.
