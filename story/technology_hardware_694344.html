8-Year-Old Girl Lives with Sick Grandfather after Relatives Passed Away (Photo)

by baimao55537682 on 2012-03-07 09:13:40

She is almost 9 years old and really wants to go to school. Xiao Caoli's eyes were filled with confusion as she muttered: "I want to study, I do." Xiao Caoli also misses her grandmother and hopes that her grandmother could come back from the prayer flags, even if it's just lying in bed. As long as she can hear her grandmother's call, it doesn't matter what she calls her. Her father passed away early, and his appearance has become blurred in her memory. Now, there's only her grandfather left. His dark hands sometimes pick up rotten vegetable leaves from the ground and put them directly into his mouth. When her grandfather has an episode, Xiao Caoli is very scared and can only run to her great-aunt's house and hide. Recently, she has been eating at her great-aunt's house every day, but her great-aunt is already old. Old Tian (her great-aunt's mother-in-law) is nearly 90 years old. Her great-aunt takes care of her own grandchildren, and Xiao Caoli is afraid that her great-aunt will send her away somewhere else, so she can only cry with her mouth open.

The neighbors said that in Xiao Caoli's family, even with the government's low-income assistance, they are unable to take care of themselves and survive. The future of this angel-like little girl is unimaginable. Cao Li cannot attend preschool due to lack of money, so she is unable to enter elementary school, and a person without normal life and learning is destined to be a modern "primitive person"!

Elderly Li Fujie said that although Cao Qibing has two half-brothers who often come to visit, they have moved down from Cao Family Village to be close to the land. Cao Laozu Gong and Cao Laozu Po are still alive, both nearly 80 years old and need their care. With their son and daughter-in-law working far away, they also have to take care of the left-behind grandchildren. They can barely take care of themselves, let alone help the grandfather and granddaughter.

In the village, when people hold banquets, leftovers are always sent over to the grandfather and granddaughter.

Xiao Caoli said, "I want to study."

When Xiao Caoli's grandmother passed away, the government gave 1500 yuan for the funeral expenses, plus the low-income assistance money, which was just enough to bury her grandmother.

Neighbor Elder Li Fujie said that for many years, Xiao Caoli and her grandparents relied on low-income assistance to get by. Originally, with her grandmother managing the household while mentally sound, now that her grandmother is gone and her grandfather is mentally unstable, he would chase after Xiao Caoli, beating and scolding her during his episodes, which is very frightening.

Neighbors told reporters that sometimes the neighbor's dog would also come to visit Xiao Caoli and her crazy grandfather. When her grandfather is calm, Xiao Caoli would quietly stay with the dog, watching the sun rise and set, watching the stars come and go, watching the village under the moonlight. When she gets tired, she returns to her small, cramped house to sleep. In the dim and messy room, Xiao Caoli dreams of carrying a new schoolbag like other children walking on sunlit roads. Sometimes, she even dreams of her mother's warm hand holding hers, celebrating the New Year and wearing new clothes.

Xiao Caoli and her crazy grandfather, Xiao Caoli's simple home

Five years ago, when Xiao Caoli was less than four years old, her mother left her and quietly departed, never to be heard from again. At home, there was only her severely ill grandmother and her crazy grandfather. Two months after her mother left, her father died unexpectedly. Two months ago, her grandmother, who had been paralyzed for six years, also passed away...

Elder Li Fujie said that when Xiao Caoli's father Cao Dexun was still alive, I used to grow some watermelons in the fields. But later, due to owing fertilizer money, the land was taken by the creditor for a year to offset the debt. When it was returned, Cao Dexun was no longer alive, and the land had lost all meaning for this family that had completely lost its labor force. Although Cao Qibing has another son, due to genetic reasons, this younger son is also disoriented, with no sense of work or direction, and no one knows where he has gone.

Villagers all know that these two depend on each other and cannot live apart. If the elder one is missing, the younger one will look everywhere, and if the younger one is missing, the elder one will search everywhere. When neighbors come to visit, only Xiao Caoli can understand her grandfather's words and act as a translator. Cao Qibing talks to himself, muttering things incomprehensible, laughing loudly sometimes, and crying uncontrollably at other times, tears streaming down his face.

From then on, the only clear-headed person in the family was the paralyzed grandmother. Lying in bed, she directed the young Caoli to do housework. Caoli is now more than eight years old and hasn't started school yet. When the children in the village went to school, this little girl wandered alone in the village, looking quite pitiful.

Elder Li Fujie said: "Xiao Caoli's grandfather often picks up the yellow vegetable leaves and rotten fruits that others don't want and brings them home to eat. The low-income savings book is now managed by a relative named Tian Hua, who withdraws the money every month and gives it to Cao Qibing. Cao Qibing's mind isn't functioning properly, so he buys random things and spends the money aimlessly."

Key Points

Zhang Feiqin is Xiao Caoli's neighbor. She told reporters: "When Xiao Caoli was four years old, her mother, seeing the impossible living conditions, left quietly, leaving behind her crazy grandfather and bedridden grandmother. Two months after her wife left, Xiao Caoli's father Cao Dexun fell off his bed one noon, hit his head, had a seizure, and then passed away."

Currently, Cao Qibing has about a hundred pounds of coal and half a bag of rice left. It won't be long before the fire goes out and they stop cooking.

Tian Xinghui is a caring person in the village who often visits this poor family. She said: "When Xiao Caoli's grandfather goes crazy, he smashes pots and pans, grabs knives, chases after Xiao Caoli, beating and scolding her. Since her grandmother passed away, this child has been running to her great-aunt's house to eat and play, returning home only to sleep at night."

Dark clouds and sorrow are collapsing this small family.
