Creative Potato Dishes - They Smell So Good!

French Fries

Ingredients: 2 potatoes / salt, pepper to taste / ketchup to taste.

Steps:

01. Preparation - Peel the potatoes and cut them into 1 cm wide strips. Soak in water for about 15 minutes, then drain. Pat dry thoroughly with paper towels if possible.

02. Fry - Heat oil in a pan, add the potato strips and fry until golden brown. Remove from heat.

03. Serve - Drain excess oil while hot, sprinkle with salt and pepper.

Tip: It's best not to use pre-frozen fries from the supermarket as fresh-cut fries are crispier and tastier. Add ketchup according to personal preference.

Sour Spicy Shredded Potatoes

Ingredients: 1 potato, small chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, garlic cloves.

Steps:

1. Peel and shred the potato as finely as possible. Cut green and red peppers into strips and garlic into pieces.

2. Rinse shredded potatoes under cold water to remove starch for a crispier texture.

3. Prepare salt and white vinegar; using white vinegar keeps the dish looking clean.

4. Heat wok, add oil.

5. When oil is warm, add Sichuan peppercorns until fragrant. Remove peppercorns. When oil is hot, add chili strips and garlic for fragrance.

6. Add potato shreds and stir-fry quickly.

7. Add white vinegar and salt quickly, stir again to evenly distribute seasoning.

8. Serve when cooked.

Cold Spicy Potato Shreds

Ingredients: Potato, minced garlic, chili powder.

Steps:

1. Shred the potato.

2. Blanch in boiling water briefly; do not overcook to keep it crispy.

3. Place blanched potato shreds in a bowl, add minced garlic and chili powder (freshly fried chili oil can be used for better aroma).

4. Heat some oil with Sichuan peppercorns (or use peppercorn oil) until smoking, pour over the potato shreds, cover and let sit for a while.

5. Add salt, monosodium glutamate, a little sugar (optional), white vinegar, cilantro, and a dash of sesame oil (optional).

6. Toss and taste, adjust seasonings as needed.

Braised Small Potatoes

Main ingredients: Small potatoes

Seasonings: Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean and chili paste), soy sauce, sugar, salt, scallions, ginger, monosodium glutamate.

Preparation:

1. Wash and peel small potatoes, cut into chunks.

2. Slice scallions and ginger.

3. Heat oil in a pan, sauté scallions and ginger until fragrant, add doubanjiang and stir until aromatic. Then add potato chunks, soy sauce, sugar, and salt, stir-fry for 2 minutes. Add an appropriate amount of water, bring to a boil on high heat, then simmer on low heat until the sauce thickens. Stir in monosodium glutamate before serving.

Xiangshan-Style Baked Potatoes

Main ingredients: 1 kg medium-sized potatoes (can also be 2 kg, 3 kg... depending on the size of your pot)

Secondary ingredients: Pickled shallots (also known as buckwheat heads; garlic can be substituted but will lack the same flavor) and their brine.

Seasonings: Cooking oil (optional), salt, monosodium glutamate.

Steps: Cook the potatoes with skin on, along with secondary ingredients and seasonings, adding water in a pressure cooker. Once cooked, cool down, then continue cooking until the skin wrinkles.

Note: Use plenty of salt or the skin won't wrinkle easily.

Dry-Fried Golden Potato Shreds

Main ingredients: 400g potato shreds

Secondary ingredients: Sichuan peppercorns, iodized salt, dried chili peppers, rice vinegar, garlic, ginger, sesame oil.

Method:

1. Shred the potatoes, wash to remove surface starch.

2. Heat 300g of oil to 50% heat, add potato shreds and deep-fry until golden brown. Drain and set aside.

3. Heat appropriate amount of oil to 70% heat, add Sichuan peppercorns, sliced ginger, garlic, and dried chili peppers, stir briefly, then add fried potato shreds. Season with salt and vinegar, stir briefly, then plate and drizzle with sesame oil.

Candy-Coated Potatoes

Main ingredients: Potatoes

Seasonings: Cooking oil, sugar.

Method:

1. Peel the potatoes, cut into large chunks, deep-fry until golden brown exterior forms, drain and set aside.

2. Heat a small amount of oil, add sugar, stir continuously until sugar melts into syrup, then add fried potatoes, stir until all potatoes are coated with syrup, then serve immediately.

Note: Best consumed while hot, otherwise they may harden.

Salt and Pepper Small Potatoes

Main ingredients: Small potatoes

Seasonings: Cooking oil, salt, sugar, green onions, salt and pepper seasoning.

Method:

1. Wash small potatoes, boil until cooked, cool down, then flatten with a knife. Set aside.

2. Heat a small amount of oil, add flattened small potatoes, season with a bit of salt, sugar, and green onions, stir-fry until done.

3. Add a touch of salt and pepper seasoning according to taste.

Russian-Style Potato Salad

Main ingredients: Potatoes

Secondary ingredients: Onions, purple basil, salad dressing, salt, black pepper.

Method:

1. Boil potatoes until fully cooked, peel, cut into small cubes, cool down and set aside.

2. Finely chop onions, heat oil in a pan, add diced bacon, onion, and purple basil (optional), stir-fry until fragrant, cool down and set aside.

3. Mix 1 and 2, add salad dressing, salt, and black pepper, toss well.

Old Lady's Mashed Potatoes

Explanation: "Old lady" refers to elderly women; "mashed potatoes" is another term for potatoes.

Main ingredients: Potatoes

Seasonings: Scallions, chili powder (Yunnan Kunming single mountain dipping fish seasoning is even better), salt, mushroom essence, scallions.

Method:

1. Regardless of size, cook potatoes until almost mashed (cook until soft enough that a chopstick can pierce through the entire potato).

2. After cooking, place small potatoes in cold water or cool down before peeling. Use a small knife to dice the potatoes and set aside.

3. Heat oil in a pan, add chili powder or dipping fish seasoning, stir briefly until fragrant, then add diced potatoes and stir-fry (do not overcook chili powder). Add salt, scallions, and mushroom essence just before removing from heat.

Characteristics: Fragrant, tender, smooth, melts in your mouth. A common home-style dish in Yunnan province, named "Old Lady's Mashed Potatoes" because it can be enjoyed by toothless elderly.

Potato and Pumpkin Soup

Main ingredients: Potatoes, pumpkin

Secondary ingredients: Apricots, red dates, goji berries, salt, mushroom essence.

Method:

1. Cut potatoes and pumpkins into small cubes, apricots and red dates into small pieces, soak goji berries.

2. Heat a small amount of oil in a pan, add diced potatoes and pumpkins, stir briefly, add water, a bit of salt, and mushroom essence.

3. Transfer to a pressure cooker, add diced apricots, red dates, and soaked goji berries, steam for 5-8 minutes (or slowly stew in a regular pot until potatoes and pumpkins are fully cooked).

This soup is sour and fresh, ideal for beauty and health maintenance for young ladies.

Curry Potatoes

Main ingredients: 3-4 potatoes, 1 onion, 1 carrot

Seasonings: Sugar, salt, curry powder or block, oil.

Method:

1. Cut potatoes into chunks, wash to remove starch. Dice carrots and chicken, slice onions.

2. Heat oil in a clean pan, add diced potatoes, carrots, and onions, stir-fry, add water, boil for ten minutes on high heat (add more water as it evaporates easily), remove from heat when onions melt. Add curry (pre-dissolved in cold water), salt, sugar, then simmer for five minutes on low heat.

Tomato Potatoes

Main ingredients: Potatoes

Secondary ingredients: Tomato sauce, salt, soy sauce, lemon juice (vinegar can also be used), sugar.

Method:

1. Wash and cut potatoes into strips (cutting into strips is preferable as thin shreds may disintegrate during prolonged cooking). Winter potatoes are recommended for their floury texture. Wash to remove starch.

2. Heat oil in a pan, lightly stir-fry potato strips, then deep-fry until three-tenths cooked. Add tomato sauce, salt, one or two drops of soy sauce.

3. Add water to cover half of the potato strips, cover and simmer for about five minutes. Uncover and boil on high heat to reduce the sauce until it thickens like gravy. Serve.

Characteristics: Bright red color, soft and smooth texture, excellent side dish.

Note: Remember to add a bit of sugar, but not too much. The flavor should be primarily salty with a hint of sweet and sour, as this is originally a Northeastern Chinese dish. Adjust flavors for variety.
