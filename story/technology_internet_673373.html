Please follow the crew's instructions.

by yiluxiangban on 2012-02-23 15:55:25

On February 1, a Chinese couple was [forced] off a flight by American crew members, and their 12-year-old daughter returned to Shanghai alone. On February 18, a passenger became emotionally agitated due to a flight delay and accidentally injured a ground service staff member of an airline… Recently, frequent conflicts between passengers and airlines have once again sparked heated discussions among netizens. Today, the teahouse will focus on the customer service relationship between passengers and airlines.

Abnormal situations witnessed in civil aviation

Nowadays, some individual passengers express their service needs through cursing or even escalating to physical altercations. In the media and online, it is not uncommon to see domestic airport passengers recklessly vandalizing airports, occupying runways, rushing through security checks, and chasing and beating airline and airport ground service personnel during flight delays or service disputes. Additionally, there are often passengers on domestic flights who curse and verbally abuse others due to delays or minor issues. Under public and media pressure, airlines generally adopt a policy of appeasement, regardless of right or wrong, likely apologizing to passengers and compensating for ticket losses. The remaining grievances are then shouldered by the service staff. A phrase like "passenger rights protection" almost absolves passengers from other legal responsibilities (at most resulting in a few days of administrative detention).

Harmonious flights require mutual respect as a prerequisite

For a long time, the service standard of "the passenger is God" has been upheld by many domestic airlines. However, in the minds of many European and American airlines abroad, employees come first. They believe in conveying the company's service quality to passengers while ensuring their own dignity and personality when serving passengers. Passengers have rights, but these rights are not unlimited. At the same time, passengers also bear responsibilities and should face the corresponding legal and economic consequences of their actions.

As service providers, airlines need to offer the best possible services. Similarly, passengers should make requests within reasonable limits. Making unrealistic demands or using inappropriate language and behavior upon encountering dissatisfaction is definitely not conducive to resolving issues and often leads to the opposite effect. In today’s world where etiquette and civility are increasingly becoming a new international language, communication should occur under mutual understanding. Mutual respect should be the premise rather than ignoring rules and regulations.

Please follow all instructions from the crew on board

According to relevant international civil aviation regulations, the captain of an aircraft holds the highest authority. If the captain feels that a passenger's behavior may affect flight safety, they have the power to remove the passenger from the plane. China's civil aviation regulations also stipulate that if a passenger violates relevant regulations on board and refuses to heed the advice of cabin crew, the captain can ask the passenger to disembark.

Flight crews prioritize safety above all else. For instance, the arrangement of luggage is generally handled by the crew in a way that benefits all passengers, avoiding random placement. Passengers might feel upset due to flight delays or other inconveniences, but at such times, mutual understanding between both parties becomes even more crucial. Beneficial communication should be encouraged, and one must avoid letting small matters escalate into major issues, which could disrupt the normal operation of the flight and ultimately result in unnecessary losses.
