1. Hugo Barra Resigns

AllThingsD reported that Hugo Barra, a senior Android executive, has left Google to join Xiaomi. Google subsequently confirmed Barra's departure but did not disclose further details in the usual official statement.

Hugo Barra also publicly announced his resignation on his personal Google+ page, expressing gratitude to Google and Android before confirming his decision to join Xiaomi.

Resignation Statement:

"After spending five and a half years at Google and three years as part of the Android team, which is the most amazing team I have ever encountered in my life, I have decided to open a new chapter in my career.

In a few weeks, I will join Xiaomi in China as the Global Vice President, helping them expand their product and business operations globally. I am very excited about this new challenge and it comforts me to continue working within the Android ecosystem."

Finally, Barra thanked all of his Android colleagues and specifically mentioned Andy Rubin (the father of Android), Hiroshi Lockheimer, Sundar Pichai, and Vic Gundotra.

Unlike many executives' resignation statements, Barra did not thank his highest leader, such as Sergey Brin. This can be understood for reasons you probably know.
