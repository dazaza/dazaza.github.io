4 Parent-Child Games to Learn How to Use Chopsticks_Children's Games_Sina Blog

by lovecathy on 2011-06-21 22:08:34

It's not an easy task to learn how to use chopsticks. To skillfully "manipulate" the two little sticks not only requires patience and time, but more importantly, interest. Learning with joy is the key for babies to master new skills.

One, Poke Holes

Mom pulls the newspaper tight at both ends and places it in mid-air, allowing the baby to quickly poke holes in the newspaper with one chopstick. This exercise enhances the baby's hand-eye coordination and accuracy.

Two, Pick up Paper Balls

Practice picking up paper balls with chopsticks and putting them into a small bowl. Mom can compete with the baby. This exercise strengthens the baby's hand muscles and patience.

Three, Pick up Beans

Prepare 2 plates, place some beans on one of the plates, and let the child use chopsticks to transfer the beans to the other empty plate. If the child is not yet proficient in using chopsticks, start by letting him pick up things that are easier to grab, such as cotton balls or popcorn.

Picking up beans can enhance the child's hand-eye coordination and improve fine motor skills.

Four, Move Ping Pong Balls

Give the child a pair of chopsticks and let him hold them flat. Place some light objects like ping pong balls on the chopsticks and have the child transport them from one side of the table to the other while holding the chopsticks flat. This can cultivate the child's attention and improve the regulation ability of arm muscles.

Additionally, when first learning to use chopsticks, let the baby pick up some larger, easier-to-grab food. Even if they drop halfway, parents should not scold. Necessary encouragement should be given. Moreover, since the baby's gastrointestinal tract is still fragile, mom should not forget to disinfect the tableware after meals! If new parents are unsure about temperature, baby-specific temperature-sensitive bowls, temperature-sensitive spoons, and other small tools are also good choices.
