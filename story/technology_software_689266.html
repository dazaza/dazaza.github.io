Eight Tricks to Win Customers

by qucqmysg on 2012-03-03 21:33:42

During this period, I often received calls from netizens who told me that the customers had been followed up for a year but still didn't place an order. What should they do? As a former front-line salesperson, I can deeply understand their situation. Should they give up or keep going? Here I still want to tell everyone six words: Persistence is victory! Here are eight strategies to teach you how to approach customers and then impress them.

The first strategy is that when we visit customers for the first time, salespeople should not introduce all products at once during technical exchanges with customers (reserve a few varieties without introducing). After introducing the product technology, don't send out all the product data books at once. Give them one or two varieties of data booklets each time. You can tell the customer that there were more customers today, so the materials have run out. Next time, I will bring them over for you, leaving room and opportunity for the next visit.

The second strategy is to revisit under the guise of delivering materials (for different salespeople, some salespeople can also tell customers that the company has recently developed a new variety, bringing the materials over to exchange ideas with you).

The third strategy is to pass by under the pretext of visiting XX company or XX department, finding a chance to chat casually with the customer.

The fourth strategy is home visits. When multiple visits fail to find a suitable reason to visit the customer, we can track and inquire about his home address after work, choose a weekend or appropriate time, encounter him in his residential area under the pretext of visiting friends or relatives. The feeling will be different from that in the factory, which can enhance the relationship. Find out the specific floor of his home clearly for the next visit, carrying gifts to visit and become friends with his family (especially his parents).

The fifth strategy is to bring colleagues or technical engineers, pretending to provide after-sales service for other customers, passing by their company, and checking if there is anything they need help with. Let the customer be moved by your service.

The sixth strategy is that after multiple visits and becoming familiar with the customer's face, naturally inquire about the birthdays of the customer and his family members clearly. If the customer or his family catches a cold, timely phone greetings or bringing fruits to visit are necessary. If you hear about the marriage or funeral of the customer's family, it naturally involves giving gifts and drinking.

The seventh strategy is to send New Year's greetings and blessings to customers in the form of letters on Spring Festival, Mid-Autumn Festival, and New Year's Day (never use meaningless greeting cards typed on the company computer).

The eighth strategy is to choose a good weather day on weekends, accompany the customer with two or three friends or colleagues, rent a car or drive yourself to go fishing in the suburbs, catch fish and eat them. Of course, before leaving, naturally give the customer three to four long and fresh big fish.

Friends, if you can achieve these eight strategies, I believe there will be no customer you cannot conquer!
