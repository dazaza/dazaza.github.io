Hefei throat slashing case second trial: the victim has no money for plastic surgery (photo)

by zsh0827303 on 2012-03-05 09:27:48

The person on the far left in the picture is Bao Fei (file photo) photographed by Ma Qibing. Xiao Wei's father was in a complex mood.

The highly anticipated "throat-slashing case" has made new progress. At 9:00 am today, the second trial of the "throat-slashing case" was held at the Second Courtroom of the Provincial Higher People's Court.

It is understood that on the morning of December 15, 2010, the first-instance verdict of the "throat-slashing case" was publicly announced at the Intermediate People's Court of Hefei City. Bao Fei was sentenced to death for robbery, rape, and theft, with his political rights deprived for life, all his properties confiscated, and compensation of nearly 160,000 yuan ordered to be paid to the victims. After the verdict was announced, Bao Fei entrusted his defense lawyer to submit an appeal to the court. The reason for the appeal was that the mitigating circumstances were not taken into account, resulting in excessive sentencing.

Bao Fei's relatives attended the hearing.

At 8:00 am, as soon as they entered the courtroom, a judicial police immediately informed them, "Today, the Bao Fei case will not be publicly tried, and the audience cannot enter the courtroom."

Before the trial began, the entrance for the public appeared very calm, with several judicial police maintaining normal order. Around 8:30 am, Bao Fei's defense lawyer Hu Guangbiao entered the courtroom. At 8:40 am, four young people walked into the entrance of the courtroom, and Lawyer Hu Guangbiao greeted them inside the entrance. Subsequently, the judicial police asked, "Are you part of the audience? Which case are you listening to?" A woman said, "Bao Fei's." The judicial police said, "You need to show your ID." "We are all his relatives." At 8:50 am, two prosecutors entered the courtroom.

At 9:00 am, the court conducted a closed-door trial. I waited outside the courtroom for nearly two hours, and Bao Fei's family also waited outside. By the time this article was published, the trial was still ongoing.

The lawyer stated that they would defend from a new angle.

This morning, I contacted Bao Fei's defense lawyer Hu Guangbiao. He told me that yesterday afternoon he met with Bao Fei before the trial and discussed some details of the case. He said, "Bao Fei's current state is okay. Regarding the first-instance verdict, Bao Fei believes the sentence is too heavy. There are also objections to some statements in the judgment, which need clarification."

The first-instance court believed that during the crime, Bao Fei acted "regardless of consequences, using cruel means, causing the victim's disability, posing extremely high social hazards, and thus no leniency should be granted." In response to this, Lawyer Hu mentioned during his visit with Bao Fei that Bao Fei had indicated that he was not "regardless of consequences." When encountering resistance from the victim, he could have easily caused the victim's death but instead chose to escape, avoiding any deaths among the victims.

For the second trial, will they present new evidence in their defense? Lawyer Hu Guangbiao revealed, "The defense opinion is largely the same, but we will present it from a new angle."

Bao Fei's mother did not want to talk about her son.

Before the first trial, I interviewed Bao Fei's mother. Talking about her son, she cried several times. She told me that during Mother's Day, Bao Fei bought towels and cups for her, hoping that as a cleaning worker, she would carry a cup to drink water and use the towel to wipe sweat while working outside during the day.

Yesterday, when I informed Bao Fei's mother about the news of Bao Fei's second trial, Aunt Fei expressed surprise and confirmed, "I don't know when the trial will take place." When I wanted to ask further if she was willing to attend the trial, Aunt Fei said, "I don't have the mood to deal with this matter now."

The victim Xiao Wei felt depressed.

At 9:50 am, I followed a narrow alleyway and arrived at Xiao Wei's home. Outside the dilapidated house, there was standing water. Inside and outside the house, clutter was piled up, and I heard the sound of a TV playing. No one answered when I knocked on the door. With the neighbor's guidance, I found Xiao Wei's father at her uncle's house.

He, who was having breakfast, told me that he had just finished his morning work and started eating. I asked him why he didn't attend the trial as an observer, and he said, "Since they won't pay compensation anyway, and I have no time to deal with this back-and-forth hassle, I still need to earn a living to support my family." Xiao Wei's father said that Xiao Wei currently works at a clothing store in Sipailou and goes home early after work every day. "Xiao Wei's physical recovery is going well so far, but our family doesn't have extra money for plastic surgery. She feels quite depressed every day, and we feel heartbroken seeing her like this."

Xiao Wei's mother angrily said, "It cost 39,000 yuan for hospitalization, and adding other expenses, it amounted to over 60,000 yuan. We only claimed compensation of over 50,000 yuan. But they even refused to pay medical fees and asked us to write a letter of understanding, which we absolutely won't do." She also told me, "If the second trial does not sentence Bao Fei to death, we will file a complaint. If Bao Fei neither compensates nor receives punishment, we feel it would be hard to give our daughter a proper explanation."

Progress Timeline of the Bao Fei Case

Date: March 19, 2010

Location: Wangwei Slaughterhouse

Event: At around 4:00 am, a 20-year-old girl named Xiao Wei was returning home from an internet cafe near Wangwei Slaughterhouse when she was tailed by a man who robbed her phone and bag and stabbed her neck and face. She was urgently sent to Anhui Medical University Affiliated Hospital and underwent rescue treatment for over six hours.

Date: March 22, 2010

Location: Anhui Medical University Affiliated Hospital

Event: Xiao Wei was transferred to the intensive care unit (ICU) on the sixth floor of the surgical ward for monitoring treatment. She couldn't move or speak. Our newspaper initiated the "Wu Na Love Fund," calling on the whole society to lend a helping hand.

Date: March 23, 2010

Location: Baohe Public Security Bureau

Event: Police locked onto Bao Fei as a suspect. They learned he had contact with a Ms. Luo in Tongling. At 11:40 pm on March 22, following Ms. Luo who took a taxi away from a foot massage parlor, they eventually arrested Bao Fei in a small hotel located in an old factory residential area near the Tongling bus station. He confessed to committing four knife-related robbery cases and was escorted back to Hefei from Tongling at noon on March 23.

Date: March 25, 2010

Location: Baohe Public Security Bureau

Event: The Baohe Branch of the Hefei Municipal Public Security Bureau disclosed other cases involving Bao Fei: In November 2009, he conspired with Zheng Moumou to impersonate auxiliary police officers and robbed a couple in a Shanghai hotel while injuring the male; On January 31, 2010, in Shuhong New Village, he robbed and raped a woman; On February 2, in Hengsheng Sunshine City, he robbed a young woman with a knife; On February 6, in Tongcheng Garden, he robbed a woman in the elevator and slashed her neck.

Date: May 2010

Location: Baohe District Procuratorate

Event: Since February, there have been multiple cases of single women being robbed on their way home at night in Baohe District. Among these, the victims of two cases - the Tongcheng Garden case and the Wangwei Slaughterhouse case - were brutally slashed on the neck. The suspect Bao Fei, who consecutively committed four knife-related robbery cases, was approved for arrest by the Baohe District Procuratorate on suspicion of robbery.

Date: November 23, 2010

Location: Zhuguang South Garden Community

Event: As the throat-slashing case was about to go to trial, Bao Fei wrote a letter home hoping his parents wouldn't be saddened. During an interview at the Zhuguang South Garden Community with Bao Fei's parents, they said their son was sentenced to a severe term in 2001 at the age of 16 for theft, robbery, and rape. After being released from prison, he faced job discrimination and returned to a life of crime, though they insisted he wasn't inherently bad. His girlfriend, Ms. Luo, claimed she stayed with him under threat.

Date: November 29, 2010

Location: Hefei Intermediate People's Court

Event: On the afternoon of November 29, Bao Fei stood on the defendant's stand at the Hefei Intermediate People's Court, alongside Zheng Moumou and Ms. Luo. Accused of robbery, rape, and theft, the prosecution proposed a sentence of life imprisonment or death penalty for Bao Fei. Xiao Wei's father said he showed some humanity by pleading for his girlfriend, while the defense lawyer claimed Bao Fei had mitigating or lenient circumstances.

Date: December 15, 2010

Location: Hefei Intermediate People's Court

Event: At 10:00 am, the Hefei Intermediate People's Court publicly announced the verdict. Bao Fei was sentenced to death, deprived of political rights for life, and ordered to compensate nearly 160,000 yuan to the two victims' families. Zheng Moumou was convicted of robbery and sentenced to fifteen years in prison with a fine of ten thousand yuan. Ms. Luo was convicted of harboring a criminal and sentenced to two years in prison. Bao Fei stated in court that he would not appeal. However, Ms. Luo and Zheng Moumou stated in court that they would appeal.

Date: December 28, 2010

Location: Hefei Intermediate People's Court

Event: Defense lawyer Hu Guangbiao stated that Bao Fei decided to appeal, and the appeal document had already been submitted to the Hefei Intermediate People's Court. Although he did not object to the crimes charged by the court in the first trial, he believed that his mitigating circumstances were not considered, resulting in an excessively harsh sentence. Despite verbally stating in court that she would appeal, Ms. Luo did not submit an appeal document.
