Shanghai Insurance Regulatory Bureau: In the second half of the year, the focus will be on improving the service quality of the insurance industry

by qiyouchax12 on 2011-05-21 08:30:18

According to the introduction, this event reports cases by adopting real policies, real vehicles and virtual accidents. It live broadcasts the processing status at the accident scene, and telephones to live broadcast the vehicle insurance case reporting situation in the meeting room. It evaluates the vehicle insurance claim service according to these three times: the time for the insurance company to arrive at the scene after reporting the case, the revisit time, and the text message reply time.

　　In this event, Pacific Vehicle Insurance became the first unit evaluated. The Pacific Vehicle Insurance replied to the accident 10 minutes after the report, and revisited the accident scene in about 20 minutes. Dai Guowen, General Manager of Pacific Property Insurance, said that the reaction speed of arriving at the accident scene within 20 minutes made him satisfied.

　　The data shows that more than 30 vehicle insurance companies participated in the on-site evaluation activities of vehicle insurance claims services. After receiving the accident report call, the fastest company arrived at the scene in 10 minutes, and the slowest company arrived at the scene in about 1 hour. Zhang Juncai, President of the Shanghai Insurance Peer Association, said that such an evaluation activity aims to standardize the vehicle insurance market in Shanghai, improve the vehicle insurance underwriting process, prevent and resolve risks in the vehicle insurance underwriting环节环节, and effectively protect the legitimate rights and interests of the insured. At the same time, it is also conducive to improving the service quality of the entire vehicle insurance industry, Tangshan 93 gasoline price.

　　This year's vehicle insurance evaluation activities were opened to the media for the first time. Compared with the evaluation results in 2008, there has been a significant improvement. Within about one hour after more than 30 insurance companies received the accident report, they all arrived at the scene, and some companies even provided towing vehicles to arrive at the scene. Director Ma Xueping pointed out that he was satisfied with such progress, but there are still shortcomings, such as some insurance companies proposing to let the traffic police solve the problem, and some insurance companies' service calls are difficult to get through. Director Ma further indicated that in the second half of the year, the CIRC will focus on standardizing the difficult claims issues in the insurance industry, greatly improving the service quality of the insurance industry, and re-establishing consumers' confidence in the insurance industry.
