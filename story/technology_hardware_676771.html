A third-year student drops out to start a business doing online game farming, using his tuition to buy six computers.

by h7837f291 on 2012-02-26 15:04:50

Zhang Hai simultaneously turned on six computers. Intern reporter Wei Shi took the photo. According to the report by intern reporter Wei Shi, "I'm not sure what to do next." Zhang Hai (a pseudonym), who was expecting to be expelled from school, heard that the school would clear out the academic records of students who hadn't registered by the end of the month and fell into a silence. At the age of 20, Zhang Hai is a student at the Continuing Education College of Hubei Second Normal University. He should have been attending class in the third-year classroom for his major in Engineering Management. However, he hasn't registered his academic record yet, instead using 8000 yuan of his tuition fees to rent a room and buy six computers to start a "business" project as an equipment proxy trainer for the game "Jian Xia Chuan Qi".

At 9:30 PM on the 19th, in a single room of more than ten square meters opposite the Continuing Education College of Hubei Second Normal University, the reporter met Zhang Hai who had just woken up. Inside the room was a single bed, with six computers placed around it, and two round stools placed between the computer desks.

"I slept too late this morning," said Zhang Hai from Shishou, explaining that to avoid the peak gaming hours, he chose to work during the night. "Starting from nine or ten in the evening, I stay busy until six or seven in the morning, sometimes sleeping all day. My main source of food is delivered meals; my girlfriend comes to deliver food when she has time." Due to pulling an all-nighter, Zhang Hai spoke slowly with a hoarse voice.

As he spoke, Zhang Hai successively turned on all the computers, entering the game interface, twisting his body on the stool in the middle, and alternately operating different characters on different computers. Zhang Hai said that he had only been doing this for over a month, and most of the game accounts were still in the early stages. "Each account needs to reach level 60 before it qualifies to cultivate equipment and start making a profit, which takes three to four days. Currently, the main task is to consume time, gradually increasing the number of accounts and their levels." He said that currently, he mainly earns money through 48 accounts that are already above level 60. "To increase 'productivity', I can only rely on the accumulation of time. I've barely left my place for over a month."

"A piece of equipment can sell for dozens of yuan at most, and only over ten yuan at least." In over a month, Zhang Hai earned 2000 yuan, after deducting 400 yuan for rent, utilities, food, water, cigarettes, etc., leaving him with only 500 yuan. "The entire night, I am alone facing the computer, so I continuously smoke to dispel loneliness." The 8000 yuan tuition fee and 2000 yuan debt used for Zhang Hai's "entrepreneurship" will "require at least another two to three months of pulling all-nighters to break even."

Zhang Hai's counselor said that since the second semester of his sophomore year, Zhang Hai almost never attended classes and didn't participate in exams. "Considering retaining students, the school didn't immediately handle his expulsion. I kept calling him to return to class, but with little effect. Later, he changed his phone number and couldn't be reached, nor did we have his parents' contact information." The counselor frankly stated that the quality of entrepreneurs plays a significant role in whether or not they succeed. "Skipping classes without reason, changing numbers to avoid people, not learning the knowledge one should, handling communication and interpersonal relationships this way, and choosing high-risk projects, this kind of entrepreneurship is very dangerous."
