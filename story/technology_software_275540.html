D - Absurd Actions Constituting Illegal Detention

In May this year, Tang inadvertently noticed a young woman at a dry cleaning shop by the roadside who looked acceptable. He pretended to be a customer asking about prices, struck up a conversation, and met 35-year-old Wang Qian (a pseudonym). After meeting a few times, he discovered that Wang Qian was running the dry cleaning shop alone. Upon inquiry, it turned out that Wang Qian’s husband had gone to work elsewhere and usually wasn't at home.

Because he had been imprisoned before, 37-year-old Tang became a well-known bachelor in the village. Constantly yearning for a wife, he seized an opportunity while working in the city and forcibly "brought" a wife home, detaining her for five days.

Society should not only focus on the living environment of migrant workers but also pay attention to their emotional education, sexual knowledge, and legal knowledge, teaching them to follow social norms.

Born in October 1972, Tang lived in Shanyang Village, Huangxi Town, Qianjiang District. He never attended school or understood the law. In November 2003, at the age of 31, he was sentenced to one year in prison for theft by the Qianjiang District Court and was released on September 10, 2004.

Recently, the Qianjiang District Court sentenced Tang to one year and ten months in prison for illegal detention.

To achieve the purpose of deceiving Wang Qian into becoming his wife, Tang claimed that he also did business. If Wang Qian partnered with him, it would be more profitable than running a dry cleaning shop. Listening to Tang's convincing arguments, Wang Qian began to consider the proposal.

Tang came up with a devious plan. He bought a discarded police uniform from a street stall, carrying it in a bag, pretending to wash clothes at Wang Qian's dry cleaning shop while casually striking up a conversation. He told her that he was the secretary of the Public Security Bureau.

Seeing the right moment, on June 3, Tang said that he had some goods coming into Qianjiang from out of town and invited Wang Qian to join him, saying, "If we sell them immediately, we can make tens of thousands." Believing him, Wang Qian followed him to Xishan Station in Qianjiang District, but by evening, there were no goods to be seen.

Wang Qian was constantly looking for opportunities to escape. Once, taking advantage of Tang's distraction, she sent a text message to her mother-in-law: "I can't come back, I've been deceived into the countryside..." Her family assumed Wang Qian had been kidnapped and became even more anxious, quickly informing the police. The police subsequently launched an investigation.

Tang visited Wang Qian's dry cleaning shop every few days, bringing his soiled "police uniform" for washing. To avoid any suspicion, he never picked up passengers near Wang Qian's dry cleaning shop again. As a result, Wang Qian had no suspicions about him whatsoever.
