Galaxy Securities: Qingdao Haier's washing machine assets injection into the listed company is unfolding as scheduled ＿ Stock Knowledge

by dx0966632 on 2009-12-03 22:48:42

1. Event: On November 30, 2009, Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) announced that the company was in negotiations with its actual controller, Haier Group, regarding the transfer of Haier Group's 31.93% stake in Haier Electronics Group Co., Ltd. (a listed company on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange, stock trading code "1169", hereinafter referred to as "Haier Electronics"). A preliminary consensus had been reached, but no letter of intent or agreement had been signed yet.

2. Our Analysis and Judgment: Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) is a leading white goods enterprise that we have consistently favored. Especially since the introduction of equity incentives policies in May this year and the commencement of asset restructuring, it has become one of our main investment recommendations to investors. Currently, Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) already owns refrigerator and air conditioner assets. With the control of Haier Electronics, the company will own washing machine and water heater assets, thereby possessing all of Haier's white goods assets, which are also the most profitable industrial assets under the Haier Group.

This announcement did not specify the method for the future acquisition of Haier Electronics' 31.93% stake. We believe there could be two possible methods:

(1) Cash acquisition or asset swap. As of November 30, Haier Electronics' closing price was HKD 4.31, with a market capitalization of HKD 8.735 billion. If acquiring 31.93% of the shares, it would require HKD 2.789 billion. From a financial perspective, Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) does not lack funds; at the end of the third quarter, the company had HKD 6.514 billion in cash on hand. However, from the perspective of control rights, using cash to acquire Haier Group's stake in Haier Electronics is not an optimal choice. Asset swaps carry more uncertainties, which we will continue to monitor.

(2)定向增发收购资产。If this option is chosen, Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) will issue shares to Haier Group and then purchase Haier Group's 31.93% stake in Haier Electronics. Through this plan, Haier Group can still obtain part of the rights in Haier Electronics by holding Qingdao Haier (600690.SH), and Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) does not need to pay out more funds. Therefore, we believe that this plan has a higher possibility of being adopted.

Haier Electronics’ revenue for 2007 - mid-2009 was HKD 8.549 billion, HKD 11.409 billion, and HKD 5.309 billion respectively, with post-tax net profits of HKD 226 million, HKD 175 million, and HKD 171 million respectively. The net profit margin ranged between 1.53% - 3.22%. If we do not consider the increase in share capital due to additional issuance, Haier Electronics will bring about HKD 0.13 per share benefit to the company in 2010.

Currently, Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) holds 19.38% of Haier Electronics' shares. Upon completion of this transaction, the company will hold 51.31% of Haier Electronics' shares. Both the company and Haier Group have sought advice from the Securities and Futures Commission of Hong Kong regarding whether a full tender offer needs to be made to Haier Electronics shareholders. They have received a principle-based response from the Hong Kong SFC agreeing to exempt the company from making a full tender offer to Haier Electronics shareholders. These matters still require approval from Qingdao Haier (600690.SH)'s board of directors, shareholders' meeting, relevant government departments, and Haier Group.

3. Investment Recommendation: We believe Qingdao Haier (600690.SH) is entering a period of rapid profit growth. As white goods assets are gradually injected into the listed company, the company's operational efficiency and profitability will rapidly improve through a strong supply chain management system and just-in-time production model under zero inventory conditions, providing continuous returns to investors. We expect the integration effects of Haier Electronics to become evident in 2010. We adjust the earnings per share forecasts for 2009, 2010, and 2011 to RMB 0.87, RMB 1.12, and RMB 1.27 respectively, corresponding PE valuations of 27X, 20.97X, and 18.50X. We maintain our "Recommend" rating for the company.

Buying and selling stocks involves specifying whether you're buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. Such a commission is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you wish to trade, the quantity, and the price at which you intend to buy or sell. The abbreviation is typically three to four Chinese characters, while the stock code consists of six digits. When placing a commission, the code and abbreviation must match. Additionally, there are specific rules regarding the quantity of stocks traded: the number of shares bought must be a whole multiple of 100, whereas the number sold does not have to be.

There are four ways to place a commission: counter submission, telephone automatic commission, computer automatic commission, and remote terminal commission.
