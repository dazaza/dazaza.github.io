The 17-year-old boy who drowned while saving a child was posthumously awarded as an individual of righteous deed

by ckdie72ds on 2011-08-24 15:18:37

Guo Qinfu was the one who saved us there.

Photo of Guo Qinfu taken before his death

On June 30, in order to save two drowning children, Guo Qinfu, a young man from Yudu County, heroically sacrificed his life at the age of only 17.

In the first half of 2010, Guo Qinfu dropped out of school due to illness and went to work in a mold factory. In this short period of time as he stepped into society, he added another ode of self-sacrificing rescue to Yudu with his heroic deed, evoking boundless admiration.

On July 12, Guo Qinfu, the rescuing hero, was posthumously awarded the title of "Advanced Individual for Bravery" in Yudu County. On July 27, the municipal government of Ganzhou City posthumously conferred Guo Qinfu with the title of "Advanced Individual for Bravery in Ganzhou City."

He jumped into the water upon hearing the cries for help.

On the afternoon of June 30, the weather cleared up after prolonged rains in Yudu County.

Guo Qinfu, who lived in Chaoxi Village, Chexi Township, was herding cattle by the Meijiang River with his junior high school classmate Guo Zongliang.

At around 15:00, 13-year-old Guo Yuanwen and his companions Guo Zhendong, Guo Zonghui, Guo Zongbin, and Guo Junhua decided to swim in the river less than 20 meters away from Guo Qinfu.

"The water near the riverbank is murky," Guo Yuanwen recalled. The first one to enter the water, Guo Zhendong, seeing that the water quality in the middle of the river was better, led the way swimming towards it. Unexpectedly, a sudden wave hit him, submerging his nose, and his limbs lost control.

"Come and pull me quickly!"

In panic, Guo Zhendong sought help from his companions.

Upon hearing the cries for help, Guo Zonghui and Guo Zongbin rushed over. Due to the strong current, Guo Zongbin was swept next to Guo Zhendong, and the two struggled together in the water.

Immediately following, Guo Zonghui and Guo Yuanwen also found themselves caught in the rapid current.

"Help! Help!" For a moment, the children screamed in fear as they were caught in the turbulent river.

Hearing the cries for help, Guo Qinfu didn't have time to think twice and ran in the direction of the cries.

Seeing children drowning, Guo Qinfu quickly took off his short-sleeved shirt, threw it on the ground, and jumped into the river to rescue them.

In the first instance, Guo Qinfu swam to Guo Yuanwen's side and forcefully pushed him towards the bank.

"Grab the weeds on the shore." Guo Yuanwen recalled, "Brother Qinfu pushed me from below, and Brother Zongliang pulled me from above. Soon, I reached the safe area."

Upon hearing Guo Zonghui shouting for help in the water, the exhausted Guo Qinfu immediately turned back and swam towards him.

Unexpectedly, a sudden wave hit him.

Before reaching the destination, Guo Qinfu lost his balance and started to sink. The surging river mercilessly swallowed him.

Fortunately, Guo Zonghui managed to grab a bamboo pole over three meters long in the water and escaped danger with the help of Guo Zongliang. Subsequently, Guo Zongbin, Guo Zhendong, and others were successively rescued onto the shore.

During his recuperation at home, he wanted to study independently and take the entrance exam for technical schools.

"The medicine prescribed by the hospital hasn't even been finished yet, but he..."

On August 15, when the reporter from Xin Fazhi Bao interviewed at Guo Qinfu's home, mother Xie Jiulian held a bag of Western medicine for treating pharyngitis. She couldn't finish a few sentences before large tears rolled down her cheeks.

On the day of the incident, there were still 48 days until Guo Qinfu's 17th birthday.

According to Guo Qinfu's family, in the summer of 2010, Guo Qinfu contracted chronic rhinitis. After being hospitalized for half a month and recuperating at home for another half a month, with the start of the third year of junior high school approaching and their poor economic conditions, Guo Qinfu made a decision that his parents were reluctant to accept: to drop out of school.

After the Spring Festival this year, Guo Qinfu went to Huamen in Dongguan with relatives and worked as a screw worker in a local mold factory.

"Just entering the factory, his monthly salary was only 1200 yuan. Over three months, he sent 2000 yuan back home, partly for his mother's medical treatment and partly for his 19-year-old sister Guo Xiaoying's education." Father Guo Chenstone spoke with tears welling up in his eyes.

Living frugally, Guo Qinfu hadn't stayed in the factory for several months when he felt discomfort in his throat. A hospital check revealed he had chronic pharyngitis. On the eve of this year's Dragon Boat Festival, Guo Qinfu submitted a leave application to the mold factory: to go home for recuperation.

The experience of working outside made Guo Qinfu realize that without education, it's hard to find a job. He began reviewing books read by his sister while helping with household chores.

"He originally planned to use the time recuperating at home to study independently and take the entrance exam for technical schools, learning a skill to have a place of his own in society in the future." Father Guo Chenstone cried, saying his son left before celebrating his 17th birthday.

Guo Qinfu's dream hadn't even set sail when he departed forever, leaving behind his beloved family.

In deep sorrow, grandfather Guo Ruoshou appeared dispirited. Through his halting narration, the reporter learned that Qinfu was his most beloved grandson. When he returned home for the Dragon Boat Festival this year, he bought two bottles of wine from Humen to show filial piety.

More than a month has passed since the incident, and an ID card photo with a simple expression has become the only memory for the family.

Father and son both performed acts of bravery in succession.

In the eyes of the locals, Guo Qinfu was a warm-hearted child since childhood.

At 17, Guo Qinfu had a slim build, but during every busy farming season, he helped neighbors harvest rice many times.

Whenever villager Guo Zongfu thought about what Qinfu had done for his family, he shed tears uncontrollably.

"At school, although Xiao Guo's grades weren't great, he was very warm-hearted. Not only did he often volunteer to clean up, but on rainy days, he always gave his umbrella to classmates and walked home in the rain himself." In the eyes of the 66-year-old retired teacher Guo Shichang, Guo Qinfu was a somewhat precocious child.

During the interview, the reporter also learned that his father, Guo Chenstone, had saved people multiple times.

"Little Qinfu's behavior is closely related to his family education." Guo Yuanqing, the secretary of the Chaoxi Village Branch Committee in Chexi Township of Yudu County, said that his father was also a rescuer hero, having saved women washing clothes by the river who were swept into the river and villagers struck by lightning. His figure could be seen every time there was flood rescue work.

Under the influence of his father, Guo Qinfu liked to lend a helping hand whenever others were in trouble from a young age.

In the autumn of 2009, Guo Bin, who lived in the same village as Guo Qinfu, was playing on the roadside and crossing the road when he almost got run over by a speeding truck. It was Guo Qinfu who rushed forward and pulled him to safety, saving his life. However, Guo Qinfu himself suffered severe abrasions on his leg after colliding with a tree stump.

Introverted and not too talkative, but very kind and warm-hearted, and would come to help at the call of anyone in trouble - this is how acquaintances of Guo Qinfu described him.

Nowadays, Guo Qinfu's advanced deeds of rescuing drowning children are widely praised locally, and people praise the Guo family for inheriting the "fine revolutionary tradition of the starting point of the Long March of the Central Red Army."

The Civil Affairs Bureau has already submitted materials for applying for the honorary title of Revolutionary Martyr.

Guo Qinfu's heroic deeds have sparked a learning craze in Yudu County.

On July 6, the Party Committee and Government of Chexi Township, Yudu County, issued a document requiring all cadres and masses in the township to regard learning from Guo Qinfu as an important measure to promote and implement the socialist sense of honor and disgrace, making it known to every household and deeply rooted in people's hearts.

The next morning, the main leaders of the Yudu County Party Committee and County Government visited and consoled Guo Qinfu's relatives and simultaneously organized departments such as political and legal affairs, civil affairs, and the Youth League County Committee to apply for relevant honors according to procedures.

Subsequently, the Yudu County Party Committee and County Government launched a campaign to learn from the heroic deeds of Guo Qinfu, requiring cadres and masses to use the touching deeds of Comrade Guo Qinfu as a mirror for self-examination, seriously conduct criticism and self-criticism, and wholeheartedly serve the people.

On July 12, the People's Government of Yudu County posthumously conferred Guo Qinfu with the title of "Advanced Individual for Bravery," and the Youth League Committee of Yudu County posthumously conferred him with the title of "Outstanding Member of the Communist Youth League of the Whole County."

On July 29, the People's Government of Ganzhou City posthumously conferred Guo Qinfu with the title of "Advanced Individual for Bravery."

"Guo Qinfu is the pride of the people of Yudu. His heroic deeds should be learned by the whole society," said Tang Qingmin, the Deputy Secretary of the Yudu County Party Committee and County Governor. Following the heroes Liu Jiurong and Lai Junhua who sacrificed themselves to save others, Yudu County has added another ode of self-sacrifice, evoking respect.

Currently, the Civil Affairs Bureau of Yudu County has submitted application materials to higher authorities for posthumously conferring Guo Qinfu with the title of Revolutionary Martyr.
