91 Wireless: Why did Baidu buy it? Why did NetDragon sell it?

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 11:30:11

Today, NetDragon announced on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange that Baidu plans to invest 1.9 billion US dollars to acquire all issued shares of NetDragon's subsidiary, 91 Wireless. Currently, both parties have signed a memorandum of understanding.

91 Wireless is an important channel for mobile app distribution in China, but the price of 1.9 billion US dollars has been questioned by many professionals in the tech industry. If the final deal is reached, this will become the most expensive acquisition in the history of China's internet industry.

If 91 Wireless is really worth so much money, why doesn't NetDragon continue to expand it instead of choosing to sell? And if 91 Wireless isn't worth this price, Baidu would definitely not make the acquisition. The existence of the 1.9 billion US dollar price is because Baidu and NetDragon see a different 91 Wireless.

For Baidu, it urgently needs a truly platform-level product in the mobile internet sector. More importantly, Baidu needs to form its own ecosystem in the mobile internet.

91 Wireless has download platforms for both iOS and Android apps and holds a relatively high market share. Additionally, 91 Wireless has already formed an ecosystem of "users-developers-platform", with 91 leading the development of this ecosystem. This is the biggest attraction of 91 Wireless for Baidu.

For NetDragon, although 91 Wireless still holds a high market share currently, it is already facing very serious challenges, with its biggest competitor being Qihoo 360. According to an insider, in terms of Android app distribution, 360 has already captured around 40% of the domestic market share, which is twice that of 91 Wireless.

360 is effectively transferring its channel advantages from the PC end to the mobile end. However, NetDragon lacks such a traffic-diverting channel. This means that if 91 Wireless remains independent, it will be at a disadvantage in competing with 360. At this point, the best strategy is undoubtedly to sell 91 Wireless while it still holds value.

For other companies, although 91 Wireless is tempting, it is generally impossible to purchase a company that may face asset impairment. However, Baidu is different. After incorporating 91 Wireless, Baidu can divert its own traffic to 91 Wireless just like 360 does from desktop to mobile, ensuring that 91 Wireless does not fall behind in competition with 360. In order to maintain its dominant position in the domestic app distribution field, Baidu might even continue to acquire similar app platforms.

From this perspective, 91 Wireless indeed has the potential to grow larger with Baidu's help.

Additionally, Baidu has one major advantage: it has already established its own ecosystem based on search, with the most typical example being its online alliance, which has attracted a large number of small and medium-sized website owners. In the era of mobile internet, these small and medium-sized website owners are various small and medium-sized app developers. With an app platform in place, Baidu can also attract developers to join.

In handling relationships with mobile app developers, Baidu can draw on many experiences from operating its online alliance. Meanwhile, Baidu can also assist websites in the alliance to transition into the mobile internet era, and after the transition, they will still remain within Baidu's own ecosystem. This point is crucial for Baidu. If website owners transition and then join 360, that would be the scenario Baidu least wants to see.
