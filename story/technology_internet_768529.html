Taobao Fake Bag Sellers Expose Themselves: Monthly Profit Exceeds 40,000 Yuan

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 19:14:29

Compared with the continuously increasing base number, Taobao is now facing the crisis of being flooded with counterfeit goods.

"I only dare to buy from the official flagship stores on Tmall. I don't have the courage to buy from other channels." During yesterday's "Double Eleven" promotional event, Ms. Shen from Hangzhou bought two pieces of clothing from the "Uniqlo" flagship store just after midnight, even though she found lower-priced online stores after comparing prices. "The probability of buying counterfeit goods in recent years has been too high."

Among Taobao buyers' levels, her level reached V5, which means that she spent at least 150,000 yuan in the past year. She is considered a very experienced buyer and still encountered a fake transaction recently. "I bought a cosmetic product because I had just finished using it. I found a Shanghai-based crown seller whose price was similar to what I paid in Hong Kong. The reviews were numerous, and many buyers commented that they compared it with genuine products and confirmed it was authentic."

When she received the item and opened the bottle, she immediately felt something was off. She then applied for a return, but the seller refused and asked why. Ms. Shen replied: "I've used more than 20 bottles of this cosmetic product. Yours is definitely counterfeit." The seller didn't say anything and gave an address in Keqiao, Shaoxing, agreeing to the return.

"These types of cosmetics aren't entirely counterfeit; they mostly contain unknown branded cosmetics inside," said Ms. Shen. At least, they won't cause issues on your face.

These sellers usually claim to be in places like Shanghai, Hangzhou, or Guangzhou, but actually ship from other wholesale markets. If you request a return, you will find that these sellers often provide addresses linked to wholesale markets. These sellers are usually very resistant to local transactions as they fear being reported to the industrial and commercial department if the buyer discovers the item is fake.

At a cosmetics market in Qianjiang Market in Hangzhou, reporters saw that many well-known cosmetics were casually placed on the ground. The wholesale boss told the reporter that SKⅡ Fairy Water, Lancôme's Little Black Bottle, and Estée Lauder's Red Pomegranate series sell quite well. The wholesale price for each bottle is around dozens of yuan.

These cosmetics typically retail for about a thousand yuan per bottle in malls. Some online store owners go to these wholesale markets to purchase items, but unlike traditional wholesale markets, they often don't stock up on goods and instead wait until their online store makes a sale before purchasing.

The most popular items are the "sample" series. Most consumers, even if they buy fake samples, often do not bother to pursue legal action due to the low total cost.

Previously, Zhejiang Provincial Administration for Industry and Commerce Director Zheng Yumin stated during a national experience exchange meeting on network market supervision that the current online shopping market has become one of the key battlegrounds for consumer rights protection.

For most sellers on Taobao who rely on counterfeit goods to survive, they have become increasingly discreet. A Taobao seller who sells counterfeit bags revealed to the reporter that her monthly sales are around 50,000 yuan, with profits of about 40,000 yuan.

In her Taobao store, there are hardly any photo displays. Inside, there are different priced "items" such as ten yuan, fifty yuan, one hundred yuan, and one thousand yuan. You just need to place an order for the agreed-upon total price, and she will ship the item. Her counterfeit bags are sold under the names of "A Goods" and "Super A Goods" within her circle of acquaintances.

However, more sellers lack risk awareness and act recklessly on Taobao. A search for "killer bag" reveals prices ranging from 98 yuan to over ten thousand yuan, while the actual mall price for these bags is generally over ten thousand yuan.

The reporter tried searching for the same keyword during "Tmall Double 11" and found several bags priced around three hundred yuan. Although they did not display the big brand logos, they still hinted at being imitation bags through references like "Mission Impossible 4" and images, with everything looking almost identical except for the logo.

On Taobao, this isn't just something that happens repeatedly—it's almost tacitly allowed.

This poses a constantly upgrading challenge for the local government. Despite attempts since 2010 to monitor the internet through various measures, such as requiring all online stores to register under real-name policies, the problem persists. According to data from Zhejiang's industry and commerce bureau, last year alone, Zhejiang conducted 32 special network operations, inspected 220,000 websites (times), physically checked 34,000 website operators, ordered 4,940 websites to rectify, shut down 452 websites, handled 970 illegal network cases, and transferred 28 cases to public security authorities.

Compared with the much larger base number, these efforts are merely a drop in the ocean.

Data shows that in 2012, China's online retail transaction volume reached 1.31 trillion yuan, growing by 67.5%, equivalent to 6.3% of the total social consumer goods retail. According to data released by the China Internet Network Information Center, in the first half of this year, China added 28.89 million new online shoppers, bringing the total to 271 million.
