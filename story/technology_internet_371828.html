Mr. Wang Dajun: How to judge the quality of a domain name and the relationship between domain name and SEO website optimization

by anonymous on 2010-11-20 18:18:04

Recently, I've been busy building a blog. After the content positioning is done, the first issue to address is the web hosting space and domain name. As everyone would consider, what kind of domain name is better? What kind of domain name is beneficial for the future development of the website? What kind of domain name helps with SEO?

Since I'm building a personal blog, I plan to use the full pinyin of my name "Wang Dajun" as the domain name, which will be convenient for establishing and promoting my personal brand in the future. Unfortunately, the .COM domain has already been registered by someone else, possibly because my name is quite popular (allow me to indulge in a bit of self-flattery). Fortunately, the wangdajun.net domain is still available, so I quickly registered it to avoid losing it again. Someone might ask why I prefer to register a .COM domain first, since wangdajun.net is also the full pinyin of my name. Actually, they are quite different, and we will discuss this point later.

In general, when choosing a domain name, we usually consider the following aspects:

1. Short

A good domain name doesn't have to be short, but a concise and powerful domain name is definitely good. Nowadays, forget about one-letter or two-letter domains, even three-letter and four-letter .COM domains are almost impossible to get. Currently, the best option is five letters or more, preferably less than ten letters. My case with wangdajun.net, which uses the full pinyin of my name, is a special need and should be considered separately. But remember not to exceed 20 letters; remembering a URL like that would feel like memorizing a book, and no one would want to do that. In this aspect, there's no impact on SEO optimization.

For domain names made up of word combinations, a single-word domain like game.com is top-notch; a two-word domain like newgame.com is excellent; a three-word domain like mynewgame.com is just ordinary; anything more can be considered a junk domain. The same principle applies to Chinese full pinyin domain names. Here, I remind everyone not to use obscure or rare English words.

2. Easy to Remember

How can a domain name be easy to remember? As mentioned above, it should be short and easy to understand, such as an SEO optimization site with the domain name seo.com, or a gaming site with the domain name game.com. For users who don't like using bookmarks, they can easily visit your site from memory.

Additionally, it's not advisable to include hyphens and numbers in the domain name. The reason is that users may easily overlook the hyphens when remembering the domain, for example, if the domain is china-map.com, users might only remember "china" and "map", and next time they enter the URL, they might directly type chinamap.com. It's also not advisable to include numbers in the domain name because they can easily confuse the spelling, for example, fil1 could be mistakenly seen as fill or fili. However, there are exceptions; if your domain has special meaning, it's another matter. For example, tui18.com means "push a little," with the full pinyin of "push" plus the homophones "1" and "8." Users can remember this domain through its meaning and are less likely to make mistakes.

3. Relevance

The relevance of the domain name adds value to your website, whether for search engines or users. Usually, the higher the relevance of the domain name, the higher the ranking in search engines. User experience will also be more friendly because seeing your domain name lets users know if the content is what they're looking for, making your site more attractive to users. I assume you're no exception.

4. Registration Time

Many new webmasters usually register new domains for only one year, leading to the domain being deactivated after one year of use. This is very detrimental to the growth of the domain. Imagine if you purchase it for ten years at once, indicating that you highly value this domain and website, thus increasing trustworthiness. Search engines will also give it higher weight. There's no need to elaborate further; everyone understands.

5. Consideration Based on Search Engine Weight

Domains inherently carry weight, which is their authority. The weight ranking of domains is generally as follows: gov > edu > org > com=net=cn...etc. The reason is simple: government websites have absolute authority, educational institutions and organizations don't need to be explained either, all having inherent trustworthiness. As a netizen said, a government website can achieve PR 5 within a week of establishment before the content is fully maintained, something personal sites cannot compare with. However, these domains cannot be registered by individuals, or the registration process is relatively complex. Now, individuals are not allowed to register cn domains either, leaving options like Com/net/co/me/cc... The inherent weight of a domain only plays a small role and does not significantly impact SEO; we should focus on user experience. Speaking of this, the reason I prefer to register a .com domain relative to wangdajun.net lies in user experience, as .com domains are the most widely accepted. Because .com domains have become deeply ingrained, many users visiting your site for content may remember your site name vividly but often overlook the domain suffix. When they want to revisit, they will instinctively type .com. Everyone should have experienced this. Therefore, I consider buying back the full pinyin .COM domain of my name when the opportunity arises (if it's not too expensive).

6. Domain History

Whether it's a newly registered domain or an expired domain grabbed, it's necessary to check the domain history beforehand. Especially for expired domains, it's crucial to confirm if there are any traces of being penalized by search engines. Below are some inspection methods summarized by netizens for reference:

Through http://whois.domaintools.com to check if the domain was previously registered. If the domain was registered before, further checks are needed to see if it has been penalized.

Check if the domain has been penalized:

1. Use the site:/link: search commands to check the indexing status of the domain. If the index count is zero but there are many external links, then the domain has been penalized. First, search site:***.com on Baidu. If there are no indexed pages, then search domain:***.com. If there are indexed pages, the site may have been penalized.

2. Directly input the website address into the search engine to check. If several results completely contain the domain but site: does not, then the domain is likely penalized. (One exception is that the search engine indexes but does not immediately publish in the search results.)

3. Check the website's historical pages and world rankings to determine if the domain is an old one.
