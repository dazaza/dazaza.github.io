Briefly Describe the Role of the People's Congress in Governing the Country According to Law

by mcvtsy on 2012-03-03 17:14:46

【Content Summary】

This article analyzes the relationship between rights and power, elaborating on the role of the People's Congress in governing the country according to law. It argues that China is a socialist country, and its state nature and political system determine that the people are the masters of the country, with all power belonging to the people. The way the people exercise their power is through the People's Congress, which plays a dominant role in governing the country according to law. The article also identifies the main factors constraining the People's Congress from playing its primary channel role in governing the country according to law and proposes improvement measures. It suggests enhancing civic awareness, equality awareness, and rights awareness; strengthening the construction of the People's Congress, improving personnel structure, and reinforcing the leadership of the Party to better transform the will of the Party into the will of the state, thereby enabling the People's Congress to play a more effective role in governing the country according to law.

【Keywords】

People's Congress, Governing the Country According to Law, Role

Governing the country according to law was proposed as the basic policy for national governance at the 15th National Congress of the Communist Party of China and is an important component of Deng Xiaoping Theory. The second session of the Ninth National People's Congress incorporated this strategy into the fundamental law of the state, making it a constitutional principle. This marks a new stage in the development of China’s socialist legal system. The People's Congress (hereinafter referred to as "Congress") bears an important historical mission in governing the country according to law. Fully recognizing the status and role of the Congress in this process is significant for improving the system of the People's Congress and advancing the governance of the country according to law.

I. Governing the Country According to Law: The Fundamental Path to Safeguarding Citizens' Rights

The protection of rights is achieved through legal means. Rights refer to the rights of citizens, while power refers to the authority of the state. How to use the law to protect rights and how to address the relationship between rights and power, i.e., the relationship between individual rights and state power, is central to understanding this issue. Under the framework of governing the country according to law, citizens' rights surpass and constrain state power, with power serving rights. Since state power originates from the granting of citizens' rights, it is subordinate to and serves the rights of citizens, thus ensuring and realizing citizens' rights as its reasonable basis. Governing the country according to law ensures that laws originate from the people, legally governing power and using power for the people, allowing citizens to control state power according to laws and regulations to serve their own rights and interests. On the other hand, ruling by power (where personal rights dominate over the law) elevates state power above individual rights, potentially infringing upon or even suppressing personal rights. Ruling by power leads to controlling the law through power and governing the people by power, making the law a tool for those in power to rule the people, infringe upon, and deprive them of their rights and the interests they represent. Therefore, governing the country according to law is the fundamental path to safeguarding citizens' rights. Governing the country according to law is characterized by citizens, as right-holders, protecting their legitimate interests by relying on the constitution and laws, using their rights to constrain power. Right-holders use one form of public power (state power) to constrain another form of public power (administrative and judicial power), achieving the goal of constraining power with rights. Governing the country according to law involves citizens, as right-holders, using the state power granted by the people to control state power, prevent its abuse, protect human rights, and safeguard citizens' rights and freedoms. This determines that the subject of governing the country according to law can only be all citizens as right-holders, while the object must necessarily be state power. Since state power is broken down into specific powers held by public officials at different levels, categories, and elements, the key focus of the legal system also lies with these public officials who hold certain powers.

The implementation of the legal system not only requires establishing a legal system as the standard for the rule of law, strictly defining the content of citizens' rights, the scope of state power, and the rules for their respective exercises, but also requires establishing a structural framework as the support for the operation of the rule of law. This forms channels and mechanisms for the constraint of citizens' rights over state power, social power over state power, and mutual constraints within the state power system, ensuring the realization of the rule of law.

II. China’s Political System Determines the Role of All Levels of the People's Congress in Governing the Country According to Law

The report of the 15th National Congress of the Communist Party of China pointed out: "[Governing the country according to law means that the broad masses of the people, under the leadership of the Party, manage state affairs, cultural undertakings, and social affairs through various channels and forms in accordance with the Constitution and laws, ensuring that all work of the state proceeds according to law, gradually realizing the institutionalization and legalization of socialist democracy]." China is a socialist country where the people are masters of their own destiny. The state nature of "people's democratic dictatorship" determines that the people are the masters of the country. The Constitution stipulates that "all power belongs to the people" and grants the people extensive rights. Here, the subjects of rights and power are unified in the people. This provision of the state nature determines that the subject of governing the country according to law must also be the people. Given that China is currently in the early stage of socialism, the current level of ideological and cultural literacy, productivity, and material conditions dictates that all citizens cannot directly participate in exercising state power. Instead, they must do so through the form of a "representative system" or "delegate system," electing representatives to exercise the power to manage state and societal affairs.
