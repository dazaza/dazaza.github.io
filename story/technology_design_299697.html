Leonardo Claims He Will Not Consider Real Madrid's Help for Qualification, Hails AC Milan's Young Wing Duo

Sina Sports - This Tuesday, AC Milan will face Zurich away in their final match of the Champions League group stage. On Monday, team coach Leonardo, accompanied by young player Luca Antonini, who is likely to make his debut in the Champions League on Tuesday evening, attended a pre-match press conference and answered some questions from reporters on site.

At the press conference, Leonardo said: "I am very happy for Antonini; his performance has been excellent. From the start of this season, he has earned more recognition for himself. The result against Zurich tomorrow depends solely on our own performance. We are currently in good form, but as for the other match between Real Madrid and Marseille (if Real Madrid does not lose to Marseille, AC Milan will qualify), that is not something we should be concerned about. We must maintain our competitive level at a high standard. Every match has its own uniqueness, including on the psychological level. Zurich knows us well; they found great motivation at San Siro, and they will do so again tomorrow. Regardless, this is still a Champions League match. Our game against Sampdoria over the weekend started off very well, and that's what we aim to replicate tomorrow. Our team is mature; in the past, our squad has experienced many crucial moments. Now we have a full understanding of the situation, and we have the necessary belief to seek victory. Our goal is to win. We need to assess Ronaldinho's condition further, but we are very optimistic about his situation, and it is highly possible that he will play. I haven't been coaching for long, so every match is important to me. Only at the end of the season can we evaluate how things went. With many players available, our style of play is not fixed; we have plenty of options. Both Abate and Antonini have performed well, and the role of full-backs isn't just limited to defense; they should also contribute to the team's attack. Now, the initial plan we had for the team has been fully confirmed."

Antonini also expressed that participating in the Champions League with the team made him very happy. He said: "I am very happy; this is the third time I've played here. I came here before with Empoli and during AC Milan's Europa League campaign. Tomorrow holds even more special excitement for me because if the coach decides to play me, it will be my first appearance in the Champions League. We are highly motivated and hope to play a great game and smoothly advance to the round of 16. We know exactly how to accomplish this task."

Attached: AC Milan's 23-man roster:

Goalkeepers: Dida, Storari, Roma

Defenders: Antonini, De Vito, Jankulovski, Kaladze, Nesta, Pasini, Thiago Silva

Midfielders: Abate, Ambrosini, Flamini, Novelic, Pirlo, Seedorf

Forwards: Borrello, Di Giovanni, Huntelaar, Inzaghi, Pato, Ronaldinho, Verdi
