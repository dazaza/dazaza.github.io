General manager was caught drunk driving and showed his CPC membership card to refuse getting out of the car (picture)

by mingdaolin on 2009-11-30 11:18:05

The traffic police had no choice but to call a tow truck to take the BMW car away. On the evening of the previous day, Lin, the driver of the BMW, was stopped by Jinshan traffic police on Tangyuan Road in Zhujin Town for suspected drunk driving. However, in the following hour, Lin staged a farce: first attempting to turn around and flee but failing due to the engine stalling; when the police asked him to show his documents, he actually pulled out a "Party Member Certificate"; because he refused to get out of the car, the police called a tow truck. During this time, he swapped seats with someone else in an attempt to find someone to take the blame for him; eventually, due to urgent need to use the restroom which he could no longer bear, he had to get out of the car.

In a phone call, Lin revealed his identity as the general manager of an enterprise in the Zhujin Industrial Zone in Jinshan. The on-site alcohol test showed that Lin's blood alcohol content reached 1.14 milligrams per milliliter, exceeding the standard for drunk driving, and he was finally administratively detained for 15 days. But because of this "wonderful performance," Lin was given the nickname "the most powerful boss" by the onlookers.

When caught for drunk driving, Lin tried to evade responsibility using his "Party Member Certificate." At around 7:25 PM on the previous evening, the Jinshan traffic police set up a checkpoint at the intersection of Tangyuan Road and Tingfeng Highway in Zhujin Town to inspect passing vehicles. At this time, a BMW sedan traveling north along Tangyuan Road suddenly stopped about ten meters from the checkpoint, preparing to turn around. During the turning process, the car suddenly stalled, its lights went out, and it remained stopped in the middle of the road. The duty traffic police immediately thought "this car must have a problem" and moved forward to check.

Even though it was nighttime, under the streetlights, one could still see through the car window glass that there were five people inside, among whom the driver had a red face, indicating suspicion of drunk driving. Because all the car doors and windows were tightly closed, after saluting, the traffic police signaled the driver to show his driver's license and vehicle registration certificate. Unexpectedly, not only did the driver not cooperate, but he also attempted to flee by starting the car. At this point, two traffic police blocked the BMW's way. After multiple attempts without success, the driver finally abandoned his escape.

When the traffic police again signaled him to show his documents, the driver actually opened a small gap in the car window and pushed out a red "Party Member Certificate." Subsequently, the car window was quickly closed, and no matter how the traffic police saluted or signaled, the five people in the car completely ignored them. This move by the BMW driver left the traffic police both laughing and crying, "You should show your driver's license and vehicle registration certificate, not other documents." After looking at the cover of the "Party Member Certificate," the duty traffic police returned the document to the BMW driver.

During the towing process, the driver swapped seats and sought help. Due to the busy location of the incident, this BMW with the license plate number沪FJ8852 soon attracted dozens of onlookers. "No matter how the police persuaded, that driver just wouldn't get out of the car and was even making phone calls inside the car," said Mr. Shi, a witness. He suspected that the "BMW" driver was trying to pull strings and seek help. "That driver is forty or fifty years old, quite authoritative, and looks like he has been drinking," Since the driver and others continued to refuse to get out of the car, the duty traffic police called a videographer to record the scene for evidence.

Regardless of the photographer filming outside the car window, the five people inside the BMW remained unmoved. Because the car had already affected the normal passage on Tangyuan Road, the traffic police had no choice but to call a tow truck. Even after the tow truck arrived on the scene, these five people still refused to get out of the car.

After more than ten minutes, the BMW was towed to the courtyard of the Fourth Detachment. At this point, the traffic police were surprised to find that the driver had actually swapped seats during the journey and sat in the back seat. Fortunately, the traffic police had recorded video evidence beforehand, otherwise they would have been taken advantage of. At this time, the reinforcements brought by the BMW driver also arrived at the Fourth Detachment.

It turned out that this BMW driver was the general manager of an enterprise in the Zhujin Industrial Zone in Jinshan, and the "reinforcement" was a staff member of the industrial zone. Faced with illegal facts, the "reinforcement" took a clear stance, demanding that he immediately get out of the car and accept handling. However, the BMW driver refused to cooperate.

Finally got out of the car due to urgency. Around 9 PM that night, nearly two hours after the incident occurred, the BMW driver finally opened the car door, the reason being that the long-held urge to urinate could no longer be endured. The police immediately conducted a breath alcohol test on Lin. The result showed that his blood alcohol content was 1.14 milligrams per milliliter, exceeding the standard of 0.8 milligrams per milliliter for drunk driving.

During the police investigation, the 43-year-old Lin claimed to be from Fujian Province. This BMW 7 Series car belonged to him personally; when asked about his occupation, he repeatedly emphasized that he was just an employee.

Traffic police: Handling is unrelated to identity

For Lin pulling out the "Party Member Certificate" to evade drunk driving charges, many onlookers believed this action was absurd.

Regarding this, a Jinshan traffic police officer who was on duty that day stated that regardless of any identity or other titles, the traffic police will handle cases according to law. According to relevant provisions of the "Road Traffic Safety Law," Lin was finally administratively detained for 15 days, and his motor vehicle driver's license was temporarily withheld.

According to Jinshan police, from 7 PM to 9:30 PM on the previous evening, a total of 36 cases of drunk driving violations were detected in Zhujin Town, including 10 cases of drunk driving.

Drunk driving checks cannot be downgraded in handling

In September this year, Shanghai police published the latest enforcement principles regarding drunk driving on their official website in the form of "government information disclosure." For individuals caught for drunk driving, they will be punished strictly and swiftly across the board, and no leniency can be shown for any reason.

Specifically, those caught driving under the influence will have their motor vehicle driver's licenses temporarily withheld for three months and fined 500 yuan. Those found to be severely intoxicated while driving will be detained for ten to fifteen days and have their motor vehicle driver's licenses temporarily withheld for six months, with a fine of 2000 yuan. If there are aggravating circumstances, they will be detained for fifteen days and have their motor vehicle driver's licenses temporarily withheld for six months, with a fine of 2000 yuan.
