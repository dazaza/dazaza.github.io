99 Health Network Guide: Sometimes men feel pain when they urinate. What is the reason for this? Male urethral pain refers to the sensation of pain in the urethra, bladder, and perineum during urination. The degree of pain varies, often feeling like a burn, and in severe cases, it can feel like being cut by a knife. What are the common symptoms of urethral pain? Since urethral pain is a symptom often associated with certain diseases, it's important to take preventative measures. Common symptoms of urethral pain include:

1. Sudden interruption of urination accompanied by pain or urinary retention: This may indicate bladder or urethral stones or foreign bodies in the urinary tract.

2. Difficulty in urination accompanied by a feeling of fullness and pain: In elderly males, this often suggests benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), but it can also be due to urethral stones.

3. Sharp or burning pain during urination: This is commonly seen in acute inflammatory conditions such as acute urethritis, cystitis, prostatitis, and pyelonephritis.

4. Prominent pain at the start of urination or difficulty in urination: This usually indicates a problem in the urethra, commonly seen in acute urethritis, which is one of the common symptoms of urethral pain.

5. Prominent pain at the end of urination, continued pain after urination, or a feeling of "emptiness" or pain even without urinating: This suggests issues in the urethra or nearby organs such as trigonal cystitis or prostatitis.

6. Pain at the end of urination combined with urgency: This usually indicates a problem in the bladder, commonly seen in acute cystitis.

99 Health Network Reminder: The above is an introduction to the common symptoms of urethral pain. Since there are many causes of urethral pain, and various symptoms can occur, it's essential to go to the hospital for proper examination before determining a treatment plan based on individual conditions. Urethral pain might be due to some kind of infection in the urinary system. If blood is significantly present in urine tests, it could indicate a severe urinary tract infection.
