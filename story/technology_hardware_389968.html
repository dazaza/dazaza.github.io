[1] Don't wait for me to tell you what to do before you act

After saying "The people are supporting you, so figure it out yourselves!", Premier Wen again made a statement at a government work report consultation that has been warmly received by netizens: Don't wait for me to tell you what to do before you act.

Because the urban registered unemployment rate reported in the government work report was 4.6%, but the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences Institute of Sociology published a social blue book that announced an urban surveyed unemployment rate of 9.4%. Why is there a 5.2% difference between the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security and the Academy of Social Sciences unemployment rate statistics? The public has serious doubts about this. In response to this kind of "fighting", Premier Wen stated:

You guys in the Ministry of Social Labor Security should quickly go online, or you should quickly hold a press conference, you explain what the difference is between the 4.6 and the Academy of Social Sciences' 9.4, don't wait for me to tell you what to do before you act.

Why are these two data points different? Li Peilin, director of the Institute of Sociology of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, explained it like this:

For the past several decades, there has always been a difference between the urban registered unemployment rate and the urban surveyed unemployment rate, usually the latter is higher than the former. When the job market is relatively stable, these two data points are close, when the employment situation is bad, there will be a big gap between the two data points. Because our unemployment insurance does not cover all urban workers, for example unemployed migrant workers who have not participated in unemployment insurance, after becoming unemployed they are difficult to be counted in the statistics.

However, the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security has another explanation:

The 9.4% surveyed unemployment rate data comes from a sample survey of 7,000 people, among whom 2,288 are economically active urban residents, among which there are more than 200 unemployed, this number is derived from comparing these two figures.

In fact, regarding these two sets of data, we ordinary people all have our own opinions:

Like me, graduates who have only worked in the city for a few years, our hukou (household registration) is still at school, where would we go to register as unemployed? After becoming unemployed, we can only quickly look for a new job ourselves, how dare we tarnish the country's unemployment rate.

For this method of calculation that does not include those who are neither employed nor registered, it has long been questioned by scholars both domestically and internationally. Premier Wen should make them explain not just the seriously underestimated urban registered unemployment rate, but give us a number that can withstand scrutiny.

[3] Every province can save money

The case of a Yunnan police officer shooting and killing someone has recently caused controversy due to a settlement agreement. The Mengzi County Public Security Bureau reached a reconciliation agreement with the deceased's family, paying the victim's family a huge compensation of 550,000 yuan.

Regarding the case of police officer Ji Zhongchun shooting and killing someone, the Mengzi County Public Security Bureau initially classified it as a personal act, having no relation to his identity or position as a police officer. If it was a personal act, why did the Public Security Bureau pay the compensation? Deputy Director Zhao Xiangrong of the Mengzi County Public Security Bureau explained it like this:

The reason the Public Security Bureau paid this amount is considering Ji Zhongchun's specific circumstances, Ji Zhongchun is an old police officer who has worked at the Public Security Bureau for over 20 years, with poor economic conditions at home, out of humanitarianism, the Public Security Bureau first gave a humanitarian compensation to the deceased's family. And this 550,000 yuan is office expenses saved by the Public Security Bureau.

It is well known that the incident occurred because Ji Zhongchun had a dispute while reversing his car, how can a police officer with a private car be classified as having poor economic conditions? This makes one involuntarily ask: what constitutes good economic conditions?

According to Article 14 of the "Regulations on the Use of Police Equipment and Weapons by the People's Police": If a police officer illegally uses weapons, causing unnecessary casualties and property damage, the agency to which the police officer belongs shall give compensation in accordance with the relevant provisions of the "State Compensation Law". Why doesn't the Mengzi Public Security Bureau admit that this compensation is state compensation? If it indeed isn't, why does the Public Security Bureau use taxpayers' money to "help privately"?

[6] Overblown internet entrepreneurship

Suddenly, as if overnight spring breeze came, millions of online stores sprouted up everywhere. Amidst the current economic crisis, the internet seems to have become an important way to solve employment problems. Not only do the media repeatedly promote online employment as a great way for students, even during the two sessions, representatives suggested that the state should subsidize college students starting businesses on Taobao.

Low cost, low investment, low risk, internet entrepreneurship became an instant favorite overnight, many domestic institutions have set up online store training classes to help students start businesses. Yes, if everyone goes along with the trend and opens online stores now, the employment problem can be solved immediately, but what about a year later, ten years later? How many people can truly succeed? Let alone issues like medical insurance and social security. Cybersecurity and legal standardization have always been the hardest parts of the information economy tide to ignore, are our experts and professors considering this point while promoting internet entrepreneurship for college students?
