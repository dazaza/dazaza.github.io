Let us see how the game of domestic LED chip survival (1)

by ledlamp8 on 2012-03-08 14:12:32

Enter the China market, where the number of China’s LED epitaxial chip companies is growing rapidly with capacity expansion. Domestic chip companies are faced with the challenge of how to develop core technology and establish a foothold in the market amidst this rise in testing LED technology. The rapid development of LED technology today has quickened the pace towards general illumination as the ultimate market goal. However, the increasing speed of the general lighting market is mainly driven by government initiatives, while the real market has yet to take off. Many lighting manufacturers are producing lighting fixtures integrated with low-power chips, which are inexpensive but have reliability issues. With the improvement in LED luminous efficiency demanding higher device reliability, high-power chips will become the mainstream of the market. As recently as 2009, under the initiative of the Ministry of Science and Technology, many successful enterprises opted conservatively for imported chips due to the demonstration project's requirement for ensuring quality, performance, and reliability. Domestic high-power chip companies benefited less compared to foreign chip makers who earned substantial profits in China with their hundredfold funds. Meanwhile, domestic packaging companies could only follow meekly behind. It is understood that among the dozens of domestic LED chip manufacturers, there are significant gaps in technology, equipment, and supporting capabilities. CSA statistics also indicate that China had installed 301 MOCVD units by 2011, with an expected addition of about 400 units, bringing the total to 700. With the continuous release of capacity, it is anticipated that white chip prices will drop overall by about 20% to 30%, impacting the domestic low-end chip market. Xiamen San'an focuses on the low-power LED backlight and display market; Hangzhou Shilan Ming and Wuhan Chang focus on the LED outdoor display market; Xiamen Dry specializes in red and yellow light chip manufacturing, while focusing on high-power chip production are Wuhan Die Yuan and Guangzhou Jingke. In the face of strong foreign capital entering the domestic chip market and releasing production capacity, domestic chip companies are under multiple pressures including price declines. From a technical perspective, the current domestic chip industry has reached R&D levels of 110lm/W ~ 120lm/W, but only a minority of enterprises are capable of mass production. Success or failure does not solely depend on key enterprise decisions; the most important factor is competitiveness in production yield, quality, and cost control. Currently, the issue facing LED chip enterprises in China is how to achieve industrialization and large-scale production to maximize benefits. Industry experts also stated that competition will be inevitable, but with potential for development, they can ultimately gain a firm foothold to compete with foreign companies. Insiders urged the government to increase support and guidance efforts.
