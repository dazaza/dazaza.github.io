Environment Chamber of Commerce urgently suggests establishing special funds for environmental facilities operation

by ditan360 on 2010-06-29 17:05:54

Exclusive to Low Carbon Network - On the afternoon of the 29th, the Chamber of Commerce for Environmental Services under the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce (hereinafter referred to as the "Environmental Chamber") urgently submitted a proposal titled "Suggestions on Establishing a Special Fund for Urban Environmental Infrastructure Operations" to the Ministry of Finance, National Development and Reform Commission, Ministry of Environmental Protection, Ministry of Housing and Urban-Rural Development, and the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce.

The reporter exclusively obtained this proposal document. According to Luo Jianhua, Secretary-General of the Environmental Chamber, in recent years, with the continuous acceleration of sewage and garbage treatment facility construction, the problem of insufficient operating funds for urban environmental facilities in our country has become increasingly prominent, directly affecting the effectiveness of environmental facilities and the manifestation of emission reduction results.

"The charging system for sewage and garbage treatment has yet to be improved and perfected, unable to meet the needs of normal operation of environmental infrastructure, especially in economically underdeveloped areas in central and western regions as well as small and medium-sized towns, where the shortage of operating funds is particularly prominent," said Luo Jianhua.

According to the latest survey by the Environmental Chamber, there is a significant funding gap for the operation of already built environmental infrastructure. By the end of 2009, a total of 1,993 sewage treatment plants had been built nationwide, with a total treatment capacity exceeding 1.06 billion tons/day; 839 domestic waste treatment facilities had been constructed, with a total treatment capacity of 379,000 tons/day. Based on these calculations, the annual demand for environmental infrastructure operation funds across the country reaches around 43 billion yuan.

However, due to an incomplete sewage and garbage charging mechanism and low collection rates, many environmental infrastructure operations struggle to obtain sufficient funds, leading to inability to operate normally and failure to play a role in pollution control and emission reduction. According to statistics from the Ministry of Housing and Urban-Rural Development in the fourth quarter of 2009, among the nearly 2,000 urban sewage treatment plants that have been built, 263 have a load rate of less than 60%, and there are also cases of sewage treatment plants not being put into operation for many years after construction.

The operational funding issues for under-construction environmental infrastructure will be even more prominent. Currently, 1,949 urban sewage treatment projects are under construction nationwide, with a total design capacity of about 55 million cubic meters/day. Most of these sewage treatment projects are located in central and western regions as well as small and medium-sized towns below the county level. These regions have underdeveloped economies, financial difficulties, and no established charging mechanisms. Once these environmental infrastructures are built, they may again face the situation of being built but not operated or operated unstably, making the issue of "being able to build but unable to maintain" even more prominent.

Luo Jianhua stated that the insufficiency of sewage and garbage treatment charges to meet operational demands has become a very prominent issue.

In 2008, China built 427 sewage treatment plants in counties, with a treatment capacity of 9.61 million cubic meters/day. The total treatment fee income for all counties throughout the year was only around 1.25 billion yuan, averaging a charge of only 0.36 yuan per ton of sewage, far from the current national standard of 0.8 yuan per ton.

Moreover, the situation of garbage disposal fees is even less optimistic. In 2008, 509 urban garbage harmless treatment plants were built nationwide, with a treatment capacity reaching 315,000 tons/day and a garbage harmless treatment volume of 100 million tons. However, the same year's urban garbage disposal fee was only 2.3 billion yuan, averaging a charge of only around 23 yuan per ton of garbage.

"Stricter environmental discharge standards and requirements will increase operational costs," explained Luo Jianhua, using sewage treatment as an example. The current "Emission Standards for Pollutants from Urban Sewage Treatment Plants" imposes stricter requirements on the discharge standards of sewage treatment plants, stipulating that discharges must reach Grade A or Grade B standards, requiring not only the removal of COD but also phosphorus removal, nitrogen removal, and sludge treatment in sewage treatment plants. It is estimated that if these standards are met, the cost of treating each ton of sewage will increase from the current 0.8 yuan to 1.2 yuan.

For this reason, the Environmental Chamber suggests establishing a "Special Fund for Urban Environmental Infrastructure Operations." Luo Jianhua told the reporter that the pressing task is for relevant national departments to quickly establish a special fund for urban environmental infrastructure operations to provide "end-of-pipe subsidies" for environmental infrastructure, ensuring the realization of our country's "Eleventh Five-Year Plan" energy-saving and emission-reduction goals and the implementation of sustainable development strategies.

The aforementioned suggestion report from the Environmental Chamber believes that "end-of-pipe subsidies" can improve the operational efficiency of environmental infrastructure, avoid the "crowding-out effect" caused by government investment, and generate a "leverage effect" in attracting social capital investment, improving the efficiency of government fund usage.

The reporter learned that according to the Environmental Chamber's design, the fund will be established through annual contributions from central, provincial, and municipal fiscal budgets at a certain ratio. The central fiscal budget will allocate a certain share from either treasury bond funds or reward-for-performance funds, while provincial and municipal fiscal budgets will contribute a certain share from sewage and garbage treatment fee revenues. After establishment, the fund will be managed uniformly by provincial construction departments.

The subsidy targets for the fund include already-built sewage treatment and garbage treatment environmental infrastructures in central and western regions, counties, towns, and other areas where sewage and garbage treatment fees are inadequately collected. Subsidies will be based on actual treatment volumes, providing a subsidy of 0.5 yuan for every ton of domestic sewage treated and 50 yuan for every ton of garbage treated.

"The fund can also provide loans for other environmental protection projects," explained Luo Jianhua. For environmental protection projects outside the scope of operational subsidies and in regions with better-developed market mechanisms, if the收费标准不足以维持其运营，基金还可以作为低息或无息贷款提供支持。所贷款项返还基金后将用于支持新的项目。
