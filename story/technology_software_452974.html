Strengthen the governance of petition work

by clockd63 on 2011-06-28 14:37:53

I. Strengthening Guidance and Seriously Implementing Responsibilities

Based on the requirements of higher-level authorities regarding petition work and the situation faced by our bureau's petition work, the Party Committee of the bureau attaches great importance to petition work. Overall, there is a significant increase in the awareness of responsibility among leaders for petition work. The petition work responsibility system has been implemented, with the party secretary and director of the bureau bearing overall responsibility for petition work, the deputy leaders being responsible for important aspects of the petition work, and the three deputy directors handling their respective areas of responsibility, forming a new situation where everyone takes responsibility and jointly manages the process. This year, the Party Committee of the bureau included petition work in its agenda, holding two special discussions on petition issues, effectively resolving some influential petition problems. Throughout the year, the party secretary and director of the bureau personally coordinated and handled major petition cases, received visiting members of the public and teachers, ensuring that issues were resolved satisfactorily. Meanwhile, the bureau established a system for receiving visits from the public. This year, leaders personally reviewed letters and received visitors, accounting for more than 70% of initial letters and visits.

II. Establishing Rules and Promoting Institutionalization and Standardization of Petition Work

On January 10th this year, after the State Council announced the new "Regulations on Petitions and Visits", our bureau promptly organized serious study sessions and, based on the "Provisional Measures for Gradual Appeals by Zhejiang Province Civil Servants and the Acceptance System for Party and Government Organs," established and improved the petition work system, strengthening management of petition work. We designated the 15th of each month as the "Reception Day" for public visits, with leadership team members responsible for receiving visitors. After acceptance, following the principle of "graded responsibility and categorized handling," cases are promptly signed off and handed over for timely resolution, ensuring that issues capable of being resolved on the same day are addressed immediately. Throughout the year, petition staff had clear responsibilities; correspondence and visits were accurately, promptly, and standardly registered, with clear results for handling and sponsoring. Approval, review, assignment, and trial procedures were relatively complete, and the petition archives were comprehensive and updated in a timely manner.

III. Increasing Efforts in Investigating and Resolving Petition Conflicts, Striving to Resolve Difficulties and Sensitive Issues in Petition Work

This year, while doing general petition work, we focused on key areas, prioritizing mass appeals, appeals beyond jurisdiction, repeated appeals, and irregular fees in primary and secondary schools as top priorities. This year, we handled 28 letters from the public, received six batches of visitors, investigated 10 cases, and dealt with 10 individuals. The reporting and conclusion rate for key petition cases assigned by the city and district reached 100%, with all other petition cases thoroughly investigated and every case responded to. Our bureau paid particular attention to major difficult and sensitive issues, organizing capable forces to conduct deep investigations. For reasonable requests made by teachers and parents, we ensured prompt resolution. For unreasonable requests not in line with policies, we conducted thorough and detailed persuasion and education, convincing through reason. In petition work, our bureau insisted on resolving conflicts at the nascent stage, stabilizing conflicts at the local level without escalation, promptly grasping the dynamics of petitions, and striving to control group appeals, appeals beyond jurisdiction, and repeated appeals.

Petition work is a major issue concerning social stability. With the reform of the education management system, petition work faces new problems, with strong policy implications and high expectations from petitioners. This requires cooperation from multiple parties. Compared to the requirements of district-level petition departments, there are still certain gaps in our bureau's petition work. We will increase our efforts, explore new paths for petition work under new circumstances, and safeguard the reform, development, and stability of the education sector in our region.
