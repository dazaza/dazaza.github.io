Gamers laugh at the tragic ties of life!

by sggotl38 on 2012-02-16 18:26:03

In recent days, a Beipiao (a person who leaves his hometown to seek fame and fortune in Beijing) named Stone has become very famous on the Internet. Because of a failed love affair, Stone decided to go on a solo bicycle trip, which moved millions of people online. On April 14th, Stone set off from Beijing at the predetermined time and place, beginning a nearly half-month-long bicycle journey. According to the planned route, Stone's bicycle ride will pass through dozens of cities of various sizes, including Tianjin, Dezhou, Linyi, Nanjing, and Fuzhou, with the final destination being Qingyun Mountain in Yongtai, Fujian. Stone said that this long-distance trip was both a commemoration of a recently ended love and a fulfillment of a wish for many fans of the novel "Journey to the Immortal". It was also a way to prove himself.

Stone is from Xinjiang and works alone in Beijing. Like other Beipiaos, loneliness often creeps into his heart during quiet nights. Thus, novels and games became the best ways for Stone to alleviate his loneliness. Stone said that his favorite novel to read was "Journey to the Immortal", because he yearned to meet a woman like Bi Yao, who could disregard everything for love. If he could achieve his wish within his lifetime, he would firmly grasp it. Stone also said that his favorite game to play was "Journey to the Immortal" because there was once a woman "S" (a pseudonym) with whom he had an unfulfilled relationship and an unsuccessful love story. It was precisely because of "Journey to the Immortal" and this relationship that Stone embarked on a solo thousand-mile bicycle trip.

The beginning of the story is familiar - a bond formed through "Journey to the Immortal".

The rise of online games brought a new form of communication to the younger generation, leading to more stories of lovers meeting through networks and online games. Stone and S formed a bond through the game "Journey to the Immortal". The story began at the beginning of this year when, for four yuan, Stone almost lost his life. In a game adapted from the online novel "Journey to the Immortal", Stone met S. Just like any other online romance, Stone spent each day taking S to fight monsters, level up, and admire the scenery. Over time, they developed feelings for each other, got married in the game, and then met in real life, becoming an enviable couple. Since both were living busy nine-to-five lives, they didn't have much time together, whether in the game or in reality. However, S would still find time on weekends to come to Stone's place to help wash clothes and clean up. S's thoughtfulness brought great comfort to Stone, who lived alone in Beijing. Their relationship was good, and they even thought about getting married someday.

However, the ending is sad - no happy ending in reality.

Although there are many couples who met and fell in love online and eventually became husband and wife in real life, many older generations still equate online relationships with promiscuity and one-night stands, believing that online feelings are unreliable. Stone and S were no exception. Their happiness was short-lived. When they were happily planning their future, their relationship faced strong opposition from S's parents due to various realistic reasons. Under pressure, S had to propose a breakup and disappeared. This was the biggest blow Stone had experienced since coming to Beijing. Stone was unwilling to talk much about the real reason for the breakup. However, through some of Stone's friends, it was learned that the main reason for S's parents opposing the relationship might be that Stone was a Beipiao, without a house or car, making their life together difficult. As parents, they probably didn't want their daughter to suffer, so they required S to leave Stone. Perhaps such things are not uncommon in this materialistic era, and this has always been what Stone finds hard to accept.

After the story, there was struggle - regret filled with helplessness.

Because of love, they couldn't be enemies.

Because of hate, they couldn't be friends.

So they could only be the most familiar strangers.
