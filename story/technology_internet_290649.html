The procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you want to buy or sell, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. Furthermore, this order is only valid on the day it is placed. The contents of the order include the abbreviation (code) of the stock you wish to trade, the quantity, and the price at which you want to buy or sell the stock. The abbreviation of a stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the code is six digits long. The code and abbreviation of the stock must match when placing an order. Additionally, there are certain rules regarding the quantity of stocks traded: the quantity of stocks to be purchased must be a whole multiple of 100, while the quantity of stocks to be sold does not have to be a whole multiple of 100. There are four ways to place an order: counter submission, telephone automatic order, computer automatic order, and remote terminal order.
