The Insurance Regulatory Commission's rules state that insurance companies must first compensate the owners of vehicles who are not at fault in an accident.

by abcearwj on 2012-03-09 10:05:46

Xinhua News Agency (Zhang Yixiao) - The China Insurance Regulatory Commission (CIRC) yesterday announced the "Notice on Strengthening the Management of Commercial Vehicle Insurance Terms and Rates." It clearly stipulates that insurance companies are not allowed to refuse compensation for motor vehicle damage by waiving their right of subrogation. This means that the so-called "no-fault no-compensation" clause, often referred to as a "tyrannical clause," in auto insurance claims will be halted.

"No-fault no-compensation" clauses have been stopped

When a vehicle is damaged in a traffic accident and the owner is not at fault, if the liable party delays compensation, most insurance companies would also refuse compensation to the owner citing the "no-fault no-compensation" reason. In response to this, the Notice explicitly states that in cases where a third party causes damage to the insured vehicle leading to an insurance event, the insurance company should exercise its right of subrogation, first compensating the vehicle owner, and then pursuing the insurance compensation from the responsible party or their insurance company. They cannot refuse to fulfill their insurance obligations by waiving their right of subrogation. This will maximize the timely compensation for the damaged vehicle owner and leave issues between owners to be resolved by the insurance companies.

Industry insiders stated that the right of subrogation is a clause clearly stipulated in the Insurance Law, introduced into auto insurance payouts as early as 2003, but it was not previously mandatory. This request from the CIRC's Notice means that the right of subrogation becomes an obligation that insurance companies must fulfill. In the future, insurance companies will face increased costs and cash flow pressures when handling auto insurance payouts, potentially intensifying industry competition.

Insurance amount will be determined based on actual value

The Notice also halts the common practice of "high premiums with low compensation" in auto insurance claims. In the past, when vehicles were insured under commercial insurance, they were insured based on the price of a new car, but during claims, compensation was calculated based on the actual value of the used car. In response to this, the Notice stipulates that when insuring for vehicle damage, the actual value of the insured vehicle will be used to determine the insurance amount, agreed upon by both the insurance company and the vehicle owner. This will make it relatively more affordable for vehicles with longer usage periods to be insured.

The Notice also establishes differentiated mechanisms for developing commercial auto insurance products among different insurance companies, while simultaneously establishing an exit mechanism for commercial auto insurance. If an insurance company's solvency adequacy ratio was below 150% in the previous year or had a combined cost ratio higher than 100% for two consecutive accounting years, the CIRC will prevent the company from drafting its own commercial terms.

■ Reaction

Vehicle owners worry about cost transfer

Regarding the CIRC's clarification of the right of subrogation, Mr. Yang, a vehicle owner, said that it indeed provides convenience to vehicle owners, ensuring that they no longer have to worry about being ignored after their vehicle is damaged. However, Mr. Yang is also concerned that the rising costs for insurance companies might lead to higher prices for insurance products, increasing the burden of insurance for vehicle owners.

According to statistics from the Guangzhou Insurance Supervision Bureau, since August 2011 when Guangzhou began piloting the "subrogation," the insurance industry has handled 75 subrogation cases, involving an estimated compensation amount of approximately 1.68 million yuan. (Zhang Yixiao)

■ Explanation

Subrogation refers to the situation where if the liable party refuses to compensate or cooperate, the insured can transfer the right to claim to their own insurance company. At this point, the insurance company of the blameless party can first compensate the insured and then pursue the insurance compensation from the responsible party or their insurance company. (Zhang Yixiao)
