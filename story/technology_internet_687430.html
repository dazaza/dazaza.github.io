Patent maintenance costs become a "bottleneck" for patent protection

by keyizhang008 on 2012-03-02 15:48:41

Enterprises, especially small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), are the main players in technological innovation. However, due to limitations imposed by the cost of patent maintenance, SMEs are unable to effectively protect their intellectual property, leading to insufficient motivation for innovation.

**Patent Annual Fee Costs:**

Currently, the situation regarding patent applications and annual fees in our country is as follows: The application fee for an invention patent is 950 yuan (including a printing fee of 50 yuan); the application fee for a utility model patent is 500 yuan; the application fee for a design patent is 500 yuan; and the examination fee for an invention application is 2500 yuan. To obtain and maintain a patent, applicants must also pay patent annual fees to the Patent Office after filing the application.

The current national standard for patent annual fees increases incrementally according to the patent year, which is intended to encourage patent holders to voluntarily abandon patents with no value. Invention patent annual fees are as follows: Years 1-3, 900 yuan; Years 4-6, 1200 yuan; Years 7-9, 2000 yuan; Years 10-12, 4000 yuan; Years 13-15, 6000 yuan; Years 16-20, 8000 yuan. Utility model patent fees are: Years 1-3, 600 yuan; Years 4-5, 900 yuan; Years 6-8, 1200 yuan; Years 9-10, 2000 yuan.

For companies with a large number of authorized patents, this cost is not a small figure. For example, Huawei pays tens of millions in patent annual fees each year. A medium-sized enterprise with hundreds of patents would need to pay over 100,000 yuan annually in patent fees. For some SMEs, patent annual fees have become a headache. Due to concerns about being unable to afford excessive patent costs, some SMEs will forego patent applications. Furthermore, for already acquired patents, if they fail to generate revenue through product sales or technology transfers, these patents may also be abandoned. Although the state or local governments offer preferential policies on patent fees, their impact is limited.

**Infringement Litigation Costs:**

Infringement behavior in the market continues despite repeated prohibitions. To safeguard their legitimate rights and interests, enterprises must seek protection under intellectual property law, thus incurring judicial-level patent maintenance costs.

To protect their patent rights, enterprises must invest corresponding human resources, material resources, and finances in investigating and gathering evidence. A single patent lawsuit can incur many expenses, including pre-paid litigation fees, lawyer agency fees, pre-litigation preservation fees, infringement evidence collection and notarization fees, judicial appraisal fees, travel expenses, etc. Moreover, because infringing acts often occur nationwide, this further increases the costs.

For instance, Apple's involvement in patent litigation has resulted in significant cost outlays. According to industry estimates, Apple spent over $100 million on just one case against HTC alone. In fact, Apple could have used this money for research and development or product development, actions that would likely have proven worthwhile for the company.

High litigation costs are common both domestically and internationally. The UK Small Business Group strongly urges the government to protect the patents of small businesses, arguing that high court litigation costs prevent illegal acts from being punished. They call on the government to provide more protection for small business innovations.

The cost of maintaining patents significantly impacts the innovative drive of China’s SMEs, especially emerging private science and technology enterprises at this stage. This issue requires urgent resolution.
