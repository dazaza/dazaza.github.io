Xiaomi 3 trial use: top-of-the-line configuration, mediocre appearance, slightly low-end leather case.

by anonymous on 2013-09-08 19:51:55

Tencent Digital News - At the launch event of Xiaomi 3, after a bout of enthusiastic praise from Mr. Lei and a deafening cheer from the crazy Mi fans, we got to try out this phone at the media experience zone on site.

First impressions are always superficial. First, in terms of appearance, despite high praise from Mr. Lei, holding the actual device feels somewhat mediocre. The phone is indeed very thin, just as mentioned in the presentation, both sides are very flat. The curved edges on the side make it feel more comfortable to hold, but overall, the design still lacks highlights; its shape truly feels a bit ordinary.

Besides, the body size of Mi 3 is actually on the larger side. From comparison pictures, we can see that among flagship products with 5-inch screens, Mi 3 is noticeably larger than Sony L36h. When adding a case, the bulk becomes quite substantial.

In terms of the screen, the environment on-site was too chaotic for me to focus and conduct detailed comparisons. My first impression is that the color reproduction on this screen is very accurate, with a slightly warm color temperature. As for the level of detail, I don't have obsessive-compulsive disorder, and since the popularity of 720P screens, I haven't paid much attention to such issues. A screen with a pixel density as high as 441PPI doesn't need further discussion about whether it's detailed enough or not.

The version available for on-site testing was the mobile custom edition equipped with an NVidia Tegra 4 processor. This is currently the only Tegra 4 phone available for experience. Although NVIDIA Tegra 4 has specs of only four cores at 1.8GHz, its graphics processing capability is absolutely reliable.

There were many game demonstrations on-site. Yes, they ran smoothly. However, for the purpose of testing the phone, this holds little significance. After all, the hardware's capabilities are there, along with optimizations and pre-event preparations, making it difficult for it to not run smoothly. What concerns us most is the power consumption level of the Tegra 4 chip, which cannot be measured at the moment due to time constraints. We will focus on testing this aspect in our future in-depth reviews.

Regarding photography functions, the hardware level of Mi 3 is average. It features a 13-megapixel Sony lens with an F2.2 aperture and a 28mm wide-angle lens. The front-facing camera is 2-megapixels. As Xiaomi's flagship product, this configuration can only be considered standard. In practical use, the focusing speed and shooting speed of Mi 3 are both extremely fast. The 10 shots per second burst mode may seem exaggerated, but it's not far off. As for the specific effects of manual mode and RAW format, we'll have to test them back in the office with a tripod.

For selfies, my face looks rather grotesque even after multiple skin-beautifying treatments by Mi 3, but judging the phone's beautification ability using my face might be a bit unfair to Xiaomi. However, after trying it out, the handsome journalists/editors on-site all said it was good. This... Selfies vary from person to person.

Finally, let's talk about Xiaomi's leather case. If Xiaomi 3's hardware configuration could score 9.5 points, then Mi 3's appearance would be around 7 points. If the appearance can only get 7 points, then Mi 3's leather case might just scrape by with a passing grade of 6 points.

Although the appearance isn't particularly outstanding, the Mi 3 actually feels pretty good in hand, reflecting a lot of effort from the engineers. But once you put on the case, the overall effect drops significantly. First, the phone feels clumsy when cased. The black leather case has average texture and fit, and the stand design on the back is slightly complicated, lacking elegance.

Secondly, this leather case is actually stuck onto the back of the phone. We asked the staff if it would remain secure after multiple removals and reattachments, and the staff on-site didn't seem confident...

In summary, as our title suggests, Mi 3 is a phone with excellent performance but an ordinary appearance. In terms of price, the 16GB version priced at 1999 yuan offers high cost-effectiveness. We believe that the sales of this phone should not pose any significant problems.
