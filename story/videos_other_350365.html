The silver-white metal palace, with exaggerated lines and constantly flashing colorful lights, all indicate that this place belongs to a world of high-tech civilization. The vast space can accommodate tens of thousands of people. All kinds of robots are busy at work. The forms of these robots vary greatly; some are mechanized, some human-like, and others in various bioforms, indicating one thing: the level of technology in this world has reached an unimaginable height. Despite such advanced development, it feels like something is missing—life! There are only all sorts of robots in the entire room, no humans!

After a long while, a golden round toy with small wings flew out, followed by a chubby little boy dressed in a silver robe. When the little boy appeared, the eyes of all the robots in the palace flashed with respectful light and they resumed their work after realizing that the boy paid them no attention.

"Little Gold, don't run around. Wait for me," the little boy clearly doted on the lively jumping toy, and his arrival also brought more vitality to the whole palace, driving away the rigidity and lifelessness. It must be said that although everything here looked splendid and advanced, it was too rigid, all bright metals that couldn’t tell the material quality. Some green plants as decoration would definitely make it look more comfortable.

"Master, come catch me. If you catch me, Little Gold will sing you a song," the little robot laughed, flapping its wings. Its big mouth split open as if its head were about to split into two halves, but it was indeed adorable, making people want... to give it a slap. But this time, the master of Little Gold didn’t go after him happily as usual. Instead, he paused for a moment, which made Little Gold a bit confused. Although it seemed insignificant, Little Gold was much more advanced than the robots working around him. He was the latest product of the Maya Empire, specially serving the great prince, which was the highest honor for a robot. After all, in this dimension, Maya technology had reached its peak, invincible across the stars.

In this dimension, the Maya Empire was as sublime as God. Countless star systems submitted to the Maya army. The space-time jump technology made spatial distance meaningless. Whether it was spiritual civilization, technological civilization, or other alien civilizations, none could withstand the Maya army. However, a thousand years ago, the Maya Empire stopped advancing because the king discovered an ultimate secret in the birthplace of Maya civilization, Earth—a secret about truly becoming a god.

Recently, it was heard that this plan had been realized. In fact, under the current level of Maya technology, as long as they thought of it, there was nothing they couldn't achieve. The only thing needed was time.

The cute little prince silently stared at Little Gold, then suddenly grabbed him. Little Gold didn't have time to escape because the force used by the master was something that no robot could resist. The little prince looked at Little Gold in his hand, his eyes flickering with hesitation, but he still firmly held onto his favorite toy and walked to a well-like object beside the palace.

The prince peered into the well, which looked like a mirror reflecting only the ordinary situation inside the palace. At this moment, clear footsteps were heard outside the palace. The little prince gritted his teeth and threw Little Gold into the mirror-like plane before he could shout. Splash... The mirror didn't break, but rippled like water waves. With a hiss, Little Gold disappeared without a trace.

At this moment, a dignified middle-aged man wearing a crown stood angrily in front of the little prince. Beside him was a woman whose beauty was beyond description. You couldn't find any flaws on her, and even if there were parts you didn't like, she would appear perfect in your consciousness.

"Kart, do you know what you're doing!" The angry voice of the middle-aged man echoed throughout the palace. The working robots were scared and fell prostrate on the ground. A few even exploded with sparks.

"Kart, what you did was wrong. The parallel mirror connects to other horizontal universes, worlds completely different from ours. If Little Gold escapes the judgment of the rules by chance, it will bring an unbalanced development factor to that universe!" The little prince didn't show any fear. He smiled slightly. "Father, Mother, you've already said that even we can't resist the laws of parallel universes. Little Gold merely has a theoretical chance of one in trillions of quarks."

The anger of the emperor didn't seem to diminish, but the empress beside him pulled him back. "Your Majesty, today is a great day. Let's not blame our son. My darling, today our Maya civilization will reach its final glory. Soon we will become true gods, the only ones in this universe!"

Speaking of becoming gods, the king's face finally improved. "We will become light. The universe is us, and we are the universe. There will be no separation anymore!"

"Let's go, my dear child. Let's become light!" The king and queen took the prince's hands and walked towards the exit of the palace. The huge doors of the palace opened, and dazzling light illuminated the entire world. In sight was a huge floating altar, from which burned milky white sky-reaching light. Below the altar was an endless mechanical army!

That light didn't come from the sun, but from countless mechs, each having the terrifying power to conquer a planet. And it was precisely this power that made the Maya Empire invincible!

The entire Earth was filled with densely packed robots... Not a single biological entity remained, only robots. The eyes of the robots unanimously gazed at the sole altar and their sole king!

Outside the Earth, the entire solar system was filled with densely packed giant starships. All soldiers on the ships stood solemnly, watching the altar on the screen and the appearance of the king, queen, and prince... The soldiers were all robots.

The starships extended from the solar system outward, still without an end in sight. This was the invincible fleet of the Maya civilization. No civilization could withstand their attacks; they could directly destroy an entire star system.

Today, all soldiers and vassal states of the Maya civilization would witness a glorious moment. Their great king was about to become a god, become light, and eternally illuminate this universe!

Gazing at the sacred altar, the king raised his hand, and all the soldiers raised their weapons in unison, shouting in perfect harmony—"Long live the king!" This sound quickly spread throughout the universe. In this dimension, the Maya civilization had become invincible!

But the Maya civilization now only had three people left. Now they were going to complete the ultimate goal of their predecessors—to become gods, to merge with the universe.

This was a solemn and great moment. Countless robots draped in pure white robes began to chant scriptures, the great history of the Maya civilization. And at this moment, all citizens would bear witness together.

The three slowly flew towards the altar, their bodies completely unaffected by gravity. Soon, the three arrived at the entrance of the altar. Before their eyes was the fiercely burning white flame, where the future leapt. This was the ultimate power of the Maya civilization. Only by merging with the universe was the ultimate destination—the ultimate goal of the Maya civilization.

The king, queen, and prince silently looked at each other, solemnly placed their hands on their heads, and gently removed their heads. "In this glorious moment, let us face the world with authenticity!" The king's head seriously said. After speaking, the hand slowly tore off the hair, the skin, revealing a semi-circular transparent vessel containing a red brain, Tang Jia San Shao.

After the king completed this return to reality, the queen and prince also accomplished this great feat. Their bodies respectfully lifted the brains and pushed them forward. Three brains carrying the final marks of the Maya civilization flew towards the center of the flames.

Peaceful, the entire world was silent. As if time and space had stopped, but the next moment, the towering flames violently compressed and instantly disappeared. Following was a beam of white light shooting up into the sky, reaching outer space. The white light expanded, fusing everything into light. Breaking through the limits of time and space, everything turned into light. Immediately after was a grand explosion. The solar system thus disappeared, and the existence of the Maya civilization would forever become history. There seemed to be a faint sadness drifting in the universe.

In the early days of the Maya civilization, there was a great king named Nietzsche who said he was the sun… The universe is not three-dimensional, nor seven-dimensional, but infinitely dimensional, originating from a single point, giving rise to parallel universes. They share the same origin but lead to different outcomes. For example, the same Earth, from the moment of its birth, possessed countless developmental possibilities. Each possibility formed a universe space. On one Earth, dinosaurs became extinct, and a mammalian species called humans ruled the world. However, in parallel universes, dinosaurs may not have gone extinct and continued to survive.

Each possibility could derive an existence. Parallel universes might be similar or completely different. Perhaps in some universe spaces, there was never life on Earth, while in others, Earth developed to the ultimate stage of civilization. Similarities also exist. For instance, humans rule Earth, but the first emperor who unified China wasn't Ying Zheng, Einstein didn't invent relativity, but rather his father did, and even more subtle similarities.

All of this constitutes parallel universes. Between parallel universes, there exist cosmic laws that cannot be crossed. Even at the height of Maya civilization, they couldn't violate these rules. Any object daring to challenge them would be destroyed during the crossing. When this conclusion was reached, the Maya civilization lost its purpose for survival.

However, as the prince said, when technological strength reached its peak, this law wasn't entirely unbreakable. The probability was so low it was almost zero. Even if by chance one succeeded, whether living or non-living entities would lose over eighty percent of their functions. According to the calculations of the main brain of the Maya civilization, this was utterly meaningless. The concept of becoming light was unclear, but like the previous emperor who wanted to become the sun, the Maya civilization perished, simultaneously causing a stellar explosion. The chain reaction of energy plunged the entire dimension to the brink of destruction, and the uncontrollable energy storm also affected the channels of parallel space.

A tiny golden dot disappeared at the edge of destruction. When it reappeared, before its eyes was a blue planet...
