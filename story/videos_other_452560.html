Cultivation of English for Accounting Majors in Colleges and Universities - Thesis

by hylw113k8l5r on 2011-06-27 16:56:45

Abstract: This article discusses the current vocational university English teaching for accounting issues, trying to reform the construction of materials and teachers, teaching and evaluation methods and means, so as to propose countermeasures to promote English teaching in these institutions for the accounting major.

Keywords: English teaching, accounting major, vocational

English for the accounting major is a practice-based exercise that closely integrates language application with accounting professional knowledge. It enables students to learn meaningful communication and business processes in English within an accounting professional setting. As accounting plays an increasingly prominent role in international trade, globalization, and international economic and political arenas, its development trend towards its own language becomes more evident. With China's full alignment with international accounting standards, the country needs international accounting professionals who are versatile talents. Their professional knowledge should not only understand accounting but also be able to communicate and handle accounting in English, thus enabling them to communicate effectively with others in general accounting language. Currently, whether in domestic foreign-funded enterprises, joint ventures, or foreign trade companies, English has become an indispensable tool for accounting practitioners and is also an important aspect for enhancing occupational competencies while in school. This article discusses the current vocational university English teaching for accounting issues, attempting to reform the construction of materials and teachers, teaching and evaluation methods and means, so as to propose countermeasures to promote English teaching in these institutions for the accounting major.

Problems faced by the accounting field in English: The existing materials are difficult to choose from among the domestic and foreign English textbooks for teaching courses. The content of simple written materials domestically structured is primarily aimed at improving students' reading comprehension, with insufficient accompanying exercises. Original materials, though truly authentic in language, have their shortcomings when used as vocational school textbooks: they lack clear focuses and teaching goals for vocational school students, and the teaching content and methods may not fully align with the characteristics of vocational education institutions. Although original materials, sample books, and accounts have strong participation in long-term standard processing cases, most do not reflect the basic principle of step-by-step language learning. Materials above the vocational level are difficult for students to comprehend, with reading being the main form, insufficient listening, spoken English training, and weak connections to fundamental English abilities. This makes it difficult for students to master basic English knowledge and skills, further complicating the practical application and use of accounting knowledge in practice. Additionally, translation and cultural differences in professional original textbooks can lead to teachers not being able to fully uncover the original meaning due to limitations in their foreign language translation capabilities. These all significantly impact English teaching practices (teaching applicability), affecting the acceptability and effectiveness of English teaching.

Outdated teaching concepts, methods, and foundational English emphasize the importance of knowledge and comprehensive language skills, accumulating English language knowledge while emphasizing practical application techniques in professional activities. In a highly specialized accounting practice, there must be a large amount of business reports and professional simulations to allow students to grasp basic theories and the practical operations of accounting methods. However, in practical accounting English teaching, teachers not only need a considerable amount of accounting professional knowledge but must also be proficient in English. Such teachers are relatively scarce, and due to students having uneven basic English levels, teachers generally limit themselves to reading and translating basic accounting knowledge. Teaching is mostly lecture-oriented, focusing on terminology and translation, and explanations of syntactic and grammatical content of textual materials. Teachers dominate, students participate little, and opportunities for students to apply their learned knowledge to handle foreign-related accounting business and case studies are lacking, leading students to become passive recipients of language knowledge.

The initiative and enthusiasm for learning have not been adequately mobilized in vocational schools. Some students lack clear learning objectives and awareness of career development in studying English, which is crucially related to future employment in English accounting. Coupled with weak original English foundations, many students find the English vocabulary references provided in the accounting field too small and rarely seen, with articles being overly theoretical and abstruse, leading to a loss of interest and confidence in learning English, resulting in a fear of difficulties in learning. Meanwhile, current English teaching or teacher-led instruction leads to student passivity, thereby increasing their laziness. The above situations indicate that the position of English teaching in the accounting major cannot meet the required situation, making educational reforms imperative. Reforming English teaching in the accounting major involves paying attention to material construction and selecting appropriate teaching content for English in the accounting major, focusing on cultivating students' abilities to collect and use English for accounting information, business processes, and external communication. Students should be able to read basic English accounting literature, make accurate business processes economically, compile accounts according to international standards for accounting and financial reporting and analysis, and possess good spoken communication skills in professional English, laying a solid foundation for their future study and work.

Therefore, using systematic materials allows students to understand the basic principles of international accounting and normal accounting processing methods comprehensively. Materials should set teaching content from a practical application perspective to enhance professional knowledge and expandability. Special after-class reviews should correctly outline the scope and key comments of accounting professional terms in English along with University English Examination essentials. For each teaching auxiliary textbook knowledge, the textbook should accompany relevant training points on themes to strengthen understanding and memory, focusing on comprehensive teaching through theory and practice. Simulating original accounting materials, documents, and real-life accounting cases, students should learn to handle accounts and prepare financial statements in English according to international accounting standards, analyze cases based on fundamental English abilities, and propose solutions, reinforcing chapter content during the learning process. Teaching auxiliary materials can refer to English original textbooks and training materials for modification to leverage their standardization advantages while appropriately reducing difficulty. Based on years of teaching experience in constructing accounting majors in British courts, teachers have started writing and explaining materials following the aforementioned suggestions.
