The Life-and-Death Love of Leftover Women

That year, I was 28 years old. All my female classmates and girlfriends were married, but I still hadn't found a partner, let alone fallen in love. I was extremely worried. Every night, I dreamed of getting married. Especially when my mother urged me, I wanted to snatch a man from the street and get married immediately. As one of my classmates put it: eager to get married but unable to find a partner. I often cursed men for being so superficial. In my younger days, when I was beautiful, I didn't think about finding a partner, yet they swarmed around me like flies. Now, though I'm not yet a faded beauty, with just a few wrinkles, they keep their distance. What's even worse is that during a class reunion, a male classmate asked if I was married. I said I was an old maid, and he actually replied, "Old you may be, but are you still a maiden? Back then, how impressive you were, always followed by a string of admirers." I was so angry I almost spat blood. At that moment, I thought: why are women like vegetables, losing value as they age? I also blamed myself for not realizing I'd end up this way during my youthful prime. Back then, I thought all men were worthless. With a snort, I dismissed them. Now, I look at each one as a treasure. Thinking about these things made me incredibly anxious. I felt the times had changed, and I had been left behind, abandoned by society.

In order to find a partner, I tried hard those years, showing quite a bit of sincerity. But unfortunately, despite going on blind dates with nearly a whole platoon of men, none caught my eye. Isn't that strange? Although I went on many blind dates, I didn't lose heart—not that I wasn't disheartened, but I couldn't afford to be discouraged because I hadn't found the right person yet. Until one time, I completely lost hope because when someone introduced me to another partner, who was also an older youth, our secret code turned out to be me holding a roasted chicken in my left hand while he carried a beer bottle in his right, meeting under the overpass of the Zijin Mountain Department Store. How could we discriminate against older youths in such a manner? From then on, I refused to go on any more blind dates.

At that time, the internet was gaining popularity. Encouraged by newspapers, television, magazines, and other media outlets, going online became synonymous with being trendy, avant-garde, and fashionable. Due to work reasons, I also had no choice but to learn how to use a computer. Unexpectedly, after learning, I mastered the internet and discovered the chat room—a perfect place to spend my leisure time. So, I enthusiastically dived into it. There, I could forget all my troubles in life, forget the arduous task of finding a partner, and breathe freely and easily. I could pretend to be a pure young girl, babble nonsense, make those gentlemen chase after me furiously on their keyboards, and then escape. I could also pretend to be a fake teacher, tangle with those so-called Miss Silk Stockings or Wacky Girls, occasionally encounter a sincere person, and confide my worries to him without hesitation. Though I bought a computer specifically for going online, I only regarded the internet as a tool for relaxation and entertainment, not expecting to gain anything from it. However, unexpectedly, soon after going online, I found a partner and began my emotional journey—my first emotional investment since turning 28. In this virtual world, perhaps the heart of an old maid is easier to move.

One day, I took the name "Apricot" and entered the "Boundless Sky" chat room at the Shangdu Information Port. The chat room was lively at that time. A user named "Sunshine Girl" was saying to another named "Scum": "Scum, Scum, I love you. I won't hit you or scold you; I'll carve you to death with my knife of love." Another named "Red Vine Entwining Tree" was telling "Mummy": "A wife is not as good as a concubine, a concubine not as good as a prostitute, a prostitute not as good as a courtesan, a courtesan not as good as stealing, stealing not as good as failing to steal, failing to steal not as good as imagining." A monk was saying: "Here we sell friendship, web affairs, dreams, reality, passion, melancholy, and wine. What do you want?" A fake moderator kept announcing: "The channel is preparing for a gathering. Please prepare cash, alcohol tolerance, cosmetics, come quickly, details inside." An old K was telling a user named "Flowing Hair" that he gently placed her on the bed, gently lifted her skirt, gently removed her underwear, gently kissed her face, and then gently told her, "Baby, it's time to change the diaper!" Flowing Hair spat on Old K and ignored him. Old K apologized: "I'm sorry, I'm wrong. I tried crashing my head into tofu and hanging myself with noodles, but both failed. What should I do?" Flowing Hair replied: "I am happy because you are happy, I am cheerful because you are cheerful, I am worried because you are thin, I laugh because you are fat, I have money because I sold you—that's my pig-raising experience." Old K replied: "There are six kinds of eggs in the world: chicken-laid ones called eggs, explosive ones called bombs, those reading this sentence are fools, those laughing are stupid, those angry are foolish, and those cursing are turtles." After seeing this sentence, Flowing Hair kicked Old K and cursed him with "NQS, 285 (You go die, two hundred and fifty)" and then ignored him. Despite Old K's repeated attempts to talk, she didn't respond and started chatting with the monk instead. Feeling uninteresting, Old K issued a false imperial edict: "By the grace of Heaven, the Emperor decrees: Because you ignore me, you are forbidden to defecate for three days, if you defecate, you must not bring paper, if you bring paper, it must not exceed three feet, until you die of constipation! This decree is final."

While I was laughing at this, a user named "Missile" entered and immediately started arguing with another named "Laugh Twisted." Missile asked Laugh Twisted why she didn't show up for their date. Laugh Twisted, apparently a woman, started cursing Missile for being a liar. It turned out that they met online, and Missile portrayed himself as charming and cultured big boss. However, when they met, she realized he was nothing like what he described, so she angrily left without a word. While I was listening to Laugh Twisted's story, Missile started talking again. Calmly, he told Laugh Twisted that he wasn't lying, he was just modest in introducing himself. Then he explained word by word:

"I am a mature man (58 years old), robust figure, everyone says I give off an elder brother vibe (size like Wu Song's elder brother), business is relatively successful, not a particularly large boss (self-operated cold drink stall), wherever I go, I carry a notebook (a paper notebook for recording phone numbers and daily income), the car I ride isn't too good, but it's wider than a Lincoln (takes public transportation every day). Years of熏陶at Henan University have cultivated my cultural taste (sold cold drinks within Henan University), I study Chinese ancient history in my spare time, especially Qing history (love watching 'The Story of Ming Dynasty'). I can also keep up with the times (know which cold drinks sell well this year), because I frequently interact with high society, I feel annoyed (the president of Henan University always tells me: No stalls allowed on campus!). Therefore, I often understand the lower levels (have to set up stalls in other small places), my requirements for an ideal partner aren't high (as long as the other person is taller than me), dear, come, when you come, I will treat you to a lot of ice cream (of course, the unsold ones that are about to melt). You will be very satisfied at first sight! (I guess you probably won't look at me a second time!)."

While I was thoroughly entertained by this, a user named "Ugly Man" started talking to me, asking me to guess a riddle: "Two drops of water on a pig's butt, guess a song title." I guessed for a long time but couldn't figure it out, so I asked him for the answer, only to find out it was "Your Face Crying Tears." I was so angry I almost spat blood and replied: "You look so creative, living so bravely, ugliness isn't your intention, it's God's little temper tantrum. Be brave, live on, without you, how could the world's beauty be highlighted?" After saying this, I ignored him. I thought he would have nothing else to say, but instead, he replied: "You're beautiful, you're beautiful, kangaroo arms, elephant legs, bear head, rabbit mouth, donkey ears, eight-shaped eyebrows, arms crossed, legs folded, crying and shouting to participate in a beauty pageant." I was speechless with anger and had no response. After giving him a "285," I ignored him too. He pestered me for a while, found it uninteresting, and left. I was then alone, watching others chat. It was in this situation that I saw a user named "Little Luo" enter. I noticed him because of his name. In this special place, all sorts of names exist, but there aren't any common names from everyday life. He was silent for a while before asking: "Who among those with happy families wants to chat?" Seeing this sentence, my heart couldn't help but tremble. Compared to "Old K," "Ugly Man," and phrases like "Not a beautiful girl, please don't talk to me," or "Are there ladies interested in becoming mistresses?" this sentence was far more significant. Why does one want to chat with someone who has a happy family? I replied, "To quench thirst by looking at plums, because I am a bachelor." He responded, "Plums hang on trees, looking is in vain." I said, "Looking is in vain, forgetting is better." He replied, "Looking, forgetting, networking...". Thus, we chatted aimlessly, and before we knew it, it was late. I realized we got along well, but I didn't expect to chat with him again later. However, the next evening, we met again and continued our random chatter.

Subsequently, on the third, fourth day..., we became regulars here. Additionally, users like Old K, Clear Beauty, Figaro, Water Bottle Whale, Green Caterpillar, etc., frequently visited this chat room. Sometimes, everyone would chat about a single topic together, but more often, people paired up freely to chat. I chatted more with Little Luo. Although my online experience wasn't deep and I wouldn't even call myself a networm, due to my fast typing speed, once online, I became exceptionally quick-witted, making many networms no match for me. They spent half a day coming up with a sentence, which often made me impatiently tap on the keyboard. But I found Little Luo different. Not only was his typing speed fast, but he was also quick-witted and had good literary talent. For example, when I asked him how old he was, he asked me how old I was. When I said I had one leg longer than the other, he said he had one eye bigger than the other. I introduced myself as follows: "A woman, currently 28 years old, wearing a white shirt on top, black pants on bottom, black leather shoes size 45 on my feet. If anyone falls in love at first sight, please write a love letter quickly." He responded: "A man, currently 28.5 years old, small eyes, big mouth, loses two teeth when smiling. Those interested, please contact quickly. Contact number: 1234567."

In this light-hearted laughter, we gradually came to know each other and become familiar. Gradually, I learned that he was half a year older than me and also single. Since we were both older youths, we had more resonance and topics. We even talked about our experiences with blind dates. He told me that during one of his blind dates, after meeting at the park entrance and finding each other acceptable, they decided to enter the park to get to know each other better. However, while walking, the girl's high heel broke off, leaving her extremely embarrassed. Unable to walk properly with one foot higher than the other, she was too shy to tell him. He, however, was so busy talking non-stop to showcase his knowledge that he didn't notice. Realizing he wasn't perceptive, the girl sat down on a nearby bench and refused to move. He continued walking and talking to himself until he finished, then asked a question and received no response. Turning around, he found the girl gone. Confused, he hurried back to look for her but couldn't find her anywhere. He returned home to seek help from the matchmaker and learned the truth upon reaching the matchmaker's house. When he tried to arrange another meeting, the girl declined, saying that encountering such an incident on the first meeting meant future encounters would surely be unlucky. He had no choice but to give up and return home, where his mother scolded him for several days, blaming his carelessness and saying no girl would want to marry such a clumsy man.

I, on the other hand, told him about my worst blind date ever—it was with a bald man. Not only did I see that he was wearing a wig, but his face was full of pimples, looking no better than Li Huozi. I was furious and confronted the matchmaker, who justified it by saying the man only lacked hair on his head but was already a section chief and about to be promoted to deputy director, accusing me of judging people by their appearance and lacking the ability to recognize true worth. I was left speechless with anger.

As time passed, we grew increasingly familiar with each other. I learned that his family consisted of four people, including a younger brother, so I jokingly referred to them as two monks. Knowing that I had two sisters, he didn't call us three nuns but said our family was like Zhu Bajie's in-laws, and he looked like Zhu Bajie, making me blush and feel flustered. Unconsciously, we had both entered our roles.

Once, he even said, "Adorable you stole my affection, stole my heart, I decide to sue you in court. The judge said he could sentence you to lifelong companionship with me." His words made my heart beat wildly.

After a brief silence, he said he would call me, and I silently agreed. Unexpectedly, when the phone rang and I picked it up with trembling hands, the standard Mandarin and beautiful, magnetic voice that came through was unlike any male voice I had ever heard. In my quiet little room, his voice seemed to come from heaven, accompanied by celestial music. I was so entranced that I almost felt weightless and transparent, unaware of the time, place, year, or month. Once, when I asked him about his profession, he said it was information dissemination. I asked if he was a journalist, he said no. I asked if he worked at the telecommunications bureau, he said no. I asked if he worked for a newspaper or magazine, he said no. Suddenly, I thought of TV stations and asked if he was a host, he said no. When I guessed broadcaster, he sent a few smiley face icons. At that moment, his excellent voice confirmed my suspicion without doubt, and subsequent facts proved I was indeed correct.

People are often like this. Before I heard his voice, although we chatted very harmoniously and had great chemistry, it still felt like there was some kind of barrier between us. But after hearing his voice and speaking on the phone, my feelings changed. He even sent me an email:

"Our relationship, under the correct guidance of a series of policies from the telecommunications bureau and the warm care of computer merchants, has developed healthily and vigorously in the past month. This is mainly reflected in the following:

"One, we've chatted many times, for not a short period, and our understanding is not shallow.

"Two, we live in the same city, providing a realistic basis.

"Three, I am a single man, you are a single woman, we belong to the same category.

"The above three points fully demonstrate that after a month of interaction, we have formed a consensus on our feelings. The main aspect of our relationship is mutual understanding and care. We are equal and mutually beneficial. Of course, any matter has two sides, and the existence of shortcomings is inevitable. There is still a certain gap in our enthusiasm, but this is a flaw in progress. I believe that in the new month, we will certainly build on our achievements, overcome our flaws, continue to strive, hold hands, and advance, creating a new situation for our relationship..."

Therefore, I propose three points for your reference: focus on the word 'love'; emphasize the word 'intimacy'; implement the word 'harmony'.

"Let us carry forward the spirit of unity, perseverance, and struggle, jointly revitalize our feelings, and strive to reach a new height and step in our relationship. Following the principle that our relationship is built by us and benefits us, let's create brilliance together."

"You are the book, I am the cover; you are the rat, I am the cat; you are the wood, I am the glue; you are the pork, I am the knife. Our relationship is so good, what else is unclear?"

Yours sincerely,

Little L
