Long-distance bus driver is caught cracking sunflower seeds with speed over 100 (photo)

by dipper3379 on 2012-02-12 05:25:50

The driver was so "calm" that he was cracking sunflower seeds with his speed over 100 km/h. The other day, a shocking scene appeared on a bus carrying more than 40 people traveling from Huaiji to Dongguan Bus Station: the driver was so "calm" that he cracked sunflower seeds with his speed over 100 km/h.

In Huabei Star Marketing, Dongguan Daily reported (by reporter Lu Zhan with photos): On a passenger-loaded large bus traveling from Huaiji to Dongguan Bus Station, the driver played "acrobatics" on the expressway. Instead of tightly gripping the steering wheel, both of his hands were busy cracking sunflower seeds, leaving shells all around the driver's seat. This scene was accidentally captured in a mobile phone video by a passenger.

Video Replay:

Bus Driver "Relaxedly" Cracks Sunflower Seeds

In the video, the reporter saw that the bus was speeding on the highway. A driver was sitting on the driver's seat, "relaxedly" cracking sunflower seeds: with his left hand gripping the steering wheel while holding a handful of sunflower seeds, and his right hand picking them one by one into his mouth.

The lonely gearshift was quietly placed aside, untouched by the driver. Shockingly, the left hand that was supposed to be on the steering wheel occasionally let go to make way for the sunflower seeds, sometimes leaving the steering wheel uncontrolled.

Passenger Ming provided this video and told the reporter that this trip started at 11:30 AM on November 26th from Huaiji, with the final destination being Dongguan Bus Station. After entering the highway, the driver began cracking sunflower seeds until they reached Sanshui, where he stopped because he noticed traffic police at the toll booth.

"Shocking" Journey:

Top Speed Reached 110 km/h?

Ming said that although the driver was cracking sunflower seeds, the speed was not slow. Sitting directly behind the driver, Ming could clearly see the numbers on the dashboard, showing the top speed reaching 110 km/h. The bus had a capacity of 49 people, and Ming said that the bus was basically full at the time.

According to the license plate number provided by Ming, the reporter confirmed that the bus belonged to the No. 807 fleet of Dongguan Automobile Transportation Co., Ltd. A manager surnamed Li from the inspection team of the No. 807 fleet stated that according to company regulations, buses are generally not allowed to exceed 100 km/h. The exact speed at that time would need to be checked through the onboard speed measurement device.
