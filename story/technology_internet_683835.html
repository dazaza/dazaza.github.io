Japanese media claims that Jiangsu prevents civil servants from visiting Nagoya

by loved7k9 on 2012-02-29 16:12:58

Multiple Japanese media outlets reported on the 28th that due to Nagoya Mayor Takashi Kawamura's denial of the [Nanjing Massacre] rhetoric, China’s Jiangsu Province recently issued an external document requesting government employees and state-owned enterprise staff to [not visit Aichi Prefecture for at least three months]. The Mainichi Shimbun reported that Jiangsu and Aichi are sister provinces/prefectures, and multiple exchange activities planned for March and April were rumored to have been suspended. The Tokyo Shimbun published an editorial on the 28th requesting that Mayor Kawamura retract his statements denying the Nanjing Massacre facts.

According to a report by the Chunichi Shimbun on the 28th, a document issued by Jiangsu Province stipulated that government officials and state-owned enterprise employees from key cities in the province such as Guangzhou, Suzhou, Wuxi, and Nantong should not travel to Aichi Prefecture in the near future, [with a duration of at least three months]. The report also mentioned that Jiangsu informed Aichi that it had canceled the originally scheduled inspection tour to Aichi on April 17. Citing a staff member of the Jiangsu government agency organizing the inspection team, the article stated that [about 10 senior executives of state-owned enterprises who were originally confirmed to participate requested to withdraw from the inspection team]. The report further noted that there are numerous Japanese companies in Jiangsu Province, and due to concerns about rising anti-Japanese sentiment locally, some Japanese enterprises have requested their employees to [avoid going out unless necessary].

Additionally, according to reports by the Chosun Ilbo, the Aichi Japan-China Friendship Association received a notice from Guangzhou canceling the originally planned calligraphy exchange event in April. The association stated that Guangzhou had originally planned to organize a 30-person delegation to visit Aichi and engage in calligraphy exchanges with locals, discussing techniques. On the 26th of this month, Guangzhou sent an email to the association stating, [Although we are very regretful, because of Mayor Kawamura's remarks, we have decided to terminate the exchange]. The report also mentioned that the Aichi Japan-China Friendship Association has submitted a protest letter to Kawamura, requesting him to retract his statements regarding the massacre. Moreover, the originally scheduled Judo exchange in Guangzhou on March 2 was also canceled due to Kawamura's remarks. Japan's Kyodo News Agency commented that following Guangzhou's announcement to suspend civilian exchanges with Nagoya, developments indicate that the repercussions of Kawamura's remarks [have expanded to the provincial level].

A Japanese Ministry of Internal Affairs official told the Global Times on the 28th that he is concerned that the [Japan Week] event scheduled for March 9 in Guangzhou may also be affected. He said that local Japanese officials lack the necessary diplomatic qualities, and the Ministry of Internal Affairs cannot send people to provide guidance to local governments. The official believes that Kawamura's remarks were merely to draw attention to himself within Japanese society. Due to Osaka Mayor Toru Hashimoto's recent promotion of reform policies under the "Restoration Policy," which has gained significant attention, Kawamura feels anxious. He believes that Kawamura's remarks still have a certain market in Japan because some Japanese do not fully recognize the benefits of Japan-China friendship for Japan. He believes that Kawamura's actions bring no benefit to Japan.
