App Store Jianghu

by ybcz0519 on 2010-10-13 15:47:49

If you say which is more like a江湖 (jianghu) in the mobile Internet circle now, it would be none other than the various types of App stores. Wukong divides domestic App stores into four categories: player communities, built-in stores, carrier stores, and download sites. Several trends will emerge in domestic software stores:

- Store appification: The experience of mobile applications is much better than downloading software on a web page, with convenient payment, search, review functions, etc.;

- Built-in: Currently, for app promotion, occupying terminals is the key. Carriers will occupy their own customized phones, OEMs will occupy their own branded phones, and player community stores will cooperate with OEM manufacturers to build in;

- Open: Except for OEM manufacturers like Moto whose own stores are unlikely to adopt an open strategy, other stores will all go with an open strategy; China Unicom's Wo Store will be open for Mobile users to download;

- Differentiation: If every store has the same application, it wouldn't be fun. Carriers with strong bargaining power will launch exclusive software (mainly games); Another factor to consider is what the needs of Chinese mobile phone users will be within a year;

- Channel: When the tripartite confrontation and competitive landscape initially appear, how to ensure developers quickly release multi-platform software;

- Developers: Don't look at developers as fools;

- Payment: As is the case of anti-piracy in the current online video circle, I believe that mobile software will also move towards the legitimate path;

- Advertising: Carrier stores will temporarily not consider entering the mobile advertising market, after all, they haven't even done well with their own store yet; but some smaller cross-OEM manufacturer stores will combine with advertisements, also because the proportion of free applications in this type of store is higher.

Classification:

- Player Community: These software stores were initially mostly supplementary products for Google Market on counterfeit phones, supported and maintained by several major player forums; due to the large amount of Chinese and pirated software on them, as well as the coverage of player communities, they became popular.

However, from the perspective of market development, app stores will gradually shift to official phones, cooperating with OEMs to embed in phones is their only way out, among which the possibility of cooperation with domestic second-tier phone brands is greater (such as OPPO).

- Built-in Stores: The success or failure of built-in stores is related to the market share occupied by the terminal, Samsung, Moto and other big brands will have a certain installation volume; but the possession of smart phones by second-line brands such as OPPO, and whether they really need to do their own store is worth discussing.

A big factory like Samsung will also face competition pressure from carriers, both China Unicom and China Mobile have their own markets, whether their customized phones allow OEMs to embed stores remains to be verified.

Since Application Hub is likely to be embedded, let's put it here for now,豌豆夹 (Wandoujia) does the best job of understanding the current mobile phone users, and is optimistic in the medium term.

- Carrier: Carriers' understanding of mobile Internet comes entirely from Apple's education, mobile Internet = smartphones + app stores. This type of store is the most promising one because of its huge user base (due to Mobile's poor network, still more optimistic about Unicom's store), but the state-owned enterprise style of carriers also awaits market testing (today Wukong went to the Communication Exhibition, the display personnel of mm were completely incomprehensible...).

- Download Sites: There are currently two types of download sites: PC downloads and mobile downloads; although the latter has a slightly better experience, the experience in terms of software classification, search, download, payment, etc., still can't match the form of app stores.
