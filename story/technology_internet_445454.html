The female protagonist in the autobiographical film "Coco Avant Chanel" by Coco, the French actress Audrey Tautou, is one of the photos she took for a special feature in British Vogue, photographed by Paolo Roversi.

For Audrey Tautou, my impression still lingers on the精灵who fell to earth in "Amelie". Unbeknownst to me, after several years, Tautou has blossomed into a woman with deep French elegance, no wonder Karl Lagerfeld also praised her performance in the movie highly. This biographical film about the legendary French fashion figure Gabrielle Chanel spans from her impoverished childhood, through her first passionate love, to her celebrated changes in the world of fashion; it can be said that it covers the most golden period of Coco's life. Speaking of this, the real images and classic statements of Madame Chanel during this period are surely what all fashion enthusiasts would enjoy seeing.

"A girl must have two things: elegance and beauty." Such wise and witty maxims were probably passed down through the generations under the earnest guidance of parents who understood the ways of style since childhood. But the items that Gabrielle Chanel left behind for posterity go far beyond this. In addition to boucle jackets, Breton tees, pajama styles, camellia brooches, there is also the world-famous No. 5 perfume.

But I believe that among the legacies of CHANEL, apart from No. 5 which can be applied to wherever a girl wants to be kissed, everything else is actually far removed from the average public; the mad pursuit of shiny logos has become an extreme measure of whether someone is fashionable or not in today’s society, but this is despised by true fashion connoisseurs. If Madame Chanel were alive today, she would surely feel deeply pained by how future generations have desecrated her fashion philosophy. However, all this, driven by commercial interests, means that ingenious creativity ultimately cannot compete with a so-called great name. And all this is even more exaggerated in China due to media lacking integrity, bombarding people with reverence for luxury culture and the elite class, distorting these big brands, which ultimately only serve as ugly symbols for flaunting wealth by "new rich" and "rich second generation".

If one truly wants to understand Madame Chanel's clever saying, "Fashion fades, only style remains the same," the black-and-white images recording her dreamlike life and her insightful thoughts on fashion can offer you a unique awakening amidst all the noise; and what can truly be passed down is not some houndstooth patterns or twill warmth, but rather this fashion spirit that permeates the blood and bones.
