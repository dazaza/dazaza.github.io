Abstract Research methods: The target is to study the preliminary average rate method and computer processing to stabilize traditional Chinese medicine. The initial average interest rate method conducts constant acceleration experiments at different temperatures, measuring the stability data of traditional Chinese medicine. The computer program design language PowerBuilder compiles and processes the data. The results establish a linear regression equation, calculate the correlation coefficient to predict drug stability. Conclusion: This computer data processing method is both precise and fast. This plan can be expanded to other applications to predict drug stability and high-speed data processing.

Keywords: Initial average rate method; Medicine; Stability; Computer methods

The initial average rate method [1] is a common method for studying drug stability. This method places the drug at several temperatures higher than room temperature for corresponding time constants, accelerating the decomposition of the drug (usually a reaction or false reaction), then samples are taken to determine the level. According to the Arrhenius equation, the reaction rate constant at room temperature is discovered, and then a kinetics equation predicts the drug's stability. However, preliminary processing of experimental data using the average rate method is not only very complex but also prone to arithmetic and rounding errors. To quickly and accurately predict drug stability, this principle of the initial average rate method and computer data processing application is used. Data processing 1.1 calculates a mathematical model at different temperatures, the average rate [1] V0, i = {C0 - Ci}/Ti where V0, i is the corresponding average growth rate; C0 is the original drug concentration (or corresponding absorbance value); Ci is the concentration of the tested drug (or corresponding absorbance value); Ti is the temperature and time (h) corresponding to that temperature.
