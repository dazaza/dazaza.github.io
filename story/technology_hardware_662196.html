Couple wins compensation from notary office for house secretly sold by son

by anonymous on 2012-02-09 23:38:42

In order to repay his huge gambling debts, a son stole his parents' property certificate, ID card and household registration book, hired a pair of fake parents to defraud the notary office of a power of attorney for selling the house, and then sold the house, leaving his real parents homeless.

Sixty-year-old Aunt Yang and her husband sued the notary office. The first-instance court ruled that the Fangyuan Notary Office had committed serious negligence and ordered it to compensate the elderly couple over 1.2 million yuan. Later, the Fangyuan Notary Office appealed.

This morning, the case was discussed pre-trial at the Second Intermediate People's Court.

Scene in the Morning: Their house was sold by their son; they sued the notary office and were awarded compensation in the first trial.

At 7 o'clock in the morning, Aunt Yang and her husband arrived at the Second Intermediate Court.

Both Aunt Yang and her husband are retired workers. Seven years ago, the couple acquired ownership of a more than 50-square-meter property on Hufang Road in Xuanwu District through inheritance.

According to the couple's lawsuit, at the end of December last year, a man surnamed Yin held the property certificate claiming to be the landlord and asked the couple to move out. Helpless, the couple had no choice but to sleep on the street.

They later learned that in August 2009, the Fangyuan Notary Office issued a notarial deed without their knowledge or presence. The notarized content stated that the couple entrusted their son Zhang to sell the house.

In September this year, the court ruled in the first instance that the Beijing Fangyuan Notary Office had committed serious negligence during the review and was ordered to compensate according to the market price of the house, amounting to more than 1.2 million yuan.

Currently Renting a Small House: Hoping for a swift second-instance judgment.

"We left home at 5:30 in the morning," Aunt Yang told reporters this morning. Now, the two old people rent a more than 10-square-meter flat near Lugou Bridge. The rent is 400 yuan, and fire is not allowed inside, nor is there any heating equipment.

"Winter is coming, and the hardest days are also here. But this is much better than before when we didn't have to live in tents," Aunt Yang said with red eyes. She added that what they care about most now is for the second-instance verdict to come out quickly.

"Without a house, how can we live the rest of our lives?" Aunt Yang said.

The Notary Office Claims Judgment Was Wrong: Maximum Responsibility Is 10%

At 10 o'clock in the morning, the appeal case was discussed in the No. 19 courtroom of the Second Intermediate Court.

Two agents from the Fangyuan Notary Office claimed that the first-instance court's judgment was wrong and put forward eight reasons for the appeal, believing that Aunt Yang and her son colluded maliciously to defraud the notary office. They believed that the notary office had fulfilled its duty of review, had no fault, and disagreed with compensation.

At the same time, the agent of the notary office believed that even if there were flaws, it should not bear full responsibility and should only bear no more than 10% of the responsibility.

In addition, the notary office suggested adding the buyer as a third party and recommended canceling the contract for the sale of the house by Aunt Yang's son.

No Malicious Collusion: Aunt Yang Disagrees with Adjustment

As the respondent, Aunt Yang and her husband did not agree with the appeal opinion of the Fangyuan Notary Office, insisting that the notary office failed to fulfill its review responsibilities, which led to them losing their only property due to the notary office's mistakes.

Aunt Yang and her husband believe that the first-instance judgment was correct and reiterated that they did not know about their son selling the house, so there was no malicious collusion. They also disagreed with adding the buyer as a third party.

The judge sought the opinions of both parties. The Fangyuan Notary Office agreed to mediate, but Aunt Yang firmly disagreed.

The judge announced that the trial would be postponed to another day.

Pre-Trial Dialogue:

Last night, the reporter contacted Aunt Yang who is still homeless by phone. While she condemned the notary office for dereliction of duty, she also angrily criticized her son's lack of virtue, saying "I wanted to strangle him."

Son Owed Gambling Debts, Sold Elderly Parents' House

FW (Legal Evening News): How did you find out your son had sold the house?

Yang: In November last year, my son's girlfriend suddenly came to me and said that my son owed gambling debts, stole the property certificate, and sold the house. My husband and I immediately searched frantically for the property certificate and found it was fake. We collapsed on the spot.

I still can't believe that my son could do such an extreme thing.

FW: Did you see your son afterward?

Yang: One night, my son returned with his girlfriend. It was obvious he had been beaten, his eyes were swollen. Sobbing, he told me he had gambled away more than 400,000 yuan, so he hired someone to impersonate his parents to get a power of attorney for selling the house from the notary office and sold the house cheaply.

FW: After knowing the truth, did you resent your son?

Yang: I wished to strangle him - how could there be such children in this world.

Chased Out on Snowy Day, Lived in Tent Downstairs

FW: I heard the buyer later kicked you and your husband out?

Yang: Yes. On December 25th last year, it was minus 19 degrees Celsius, and it was snowing heavily. My husband and I were forcibly evicted by a group of people, and all our pots, pans, and furniture were thrown out.

My husband and I hugged each other and sat on the ground crying downstairs. Some old neighbors felt sorry for us, helped us set up a tent, and brought us hot water bottles.

FW: Why do you remember the temperature of that day so clearly?

Yang: That was the most painful and difficult day of my life, and I will never forget it.

Covered with Three Quilts, Ate 'Hundred Families Rice'

FW: How did you two survive the cold weather?

Yang: At night, we used all the quilts, laid them thickly, covered ourselves with three quilts, and slept with hats on. Even so, we often caught colds. I never imagined I would one day be homeless.

FW: What did you do for food?

Yang: We relied on help from neighbors, eating a bite here and there, basically eating 'hundred families rice'.

If it weren't for the kind-hearted neighbors' help, my husband and I would have died in the tent long ago.

Unable to Contact Son, Sued Notary Office

FW: Have you been in contact with your son?

Yang: We haven't been able to contact him, even now.

FW: How did you think of suing the notary office?

Yang: After knowing my son had sold the house, I quickly found a lawyer and sued my son and the buyer.

But this case couldn't proceed - my son didn't show up in court, and neither did the buyer. The judge said my son didn't even have a sales agreement, so I had to withdraw the lawsuit. The judge suggested finding other ways, and my husband and I sued the notary office.

FW: Do you think the notary office has responsibility?

Yang: Yes. Neither my husband nor I went to notarize anything, so why issue a power of attorney for selling the house? This house belongs to us, not to our son.

Renting a Small Flat: Still Cold This Winter

FW: Are you satisfied with the first-instance judgment?

Yang: Very satisfied. My husband and I just hope to get the compensation money as soon as possible to buy a small house. We must have a home.

FW: Are you and your husband still living in a tent?

Yang: It was too hot to live in a tent in summer. We rented a small flat near Lugou Bridge. However, this small flat has no heating, so we will still have to endure this winter.

(The reporter proposed to visit the small flat to see the living conditions of the elderly couple, but Aunt Yang politely declined. She said her husband was very self-conscious, and after being exposed in the media while living in the tent, the dire situation of the couple was known by all the neighbors. Therefore, this time moving to a new place, they didn't want the new neighbors to know.)
