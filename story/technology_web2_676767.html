Weight gain method: two people tie magnets to the soles of their feet to steal coins wished by pilgrims

by tian_tian_2012 on 2012-02-26 15:02:02

It is said that no matter how cunning the criminal is, he cannot escape from the policemen's eyes. In the public security acceptance room, there was no hiding place for the two peoples' secrets under their feet. The two dug a hole about 5 centimeters long in the bottom of the flat shoes they wore, and then tied a magnet with a diameter of about 4.5 centimeters to their feet with transparent tape. Thus, they applied the magnet to absorb coins while walking. Eventually, the policemen searched out nearly 200 yuan of incense money from the two peoples' pockets.

At around 6 o'clock on the morning of the first day of the first lunar month, more and more citizens began to enter the Longhua Temple in succession, take incense, kneel down, worship, and pray for blessings. Besides, people also like to throw coins to the incense altar at the center of the temple to make a vow, and thus there were quite a lot of coins scattered around the incense altar.

It is a traditional custom of Chinese people to pray for blessing in the new year. However, two local women squeezed into the crowded temple at around 6 o'clock on the morning of the first day of the first lunar month not to burn incense, but to "absorb" the incense money on the ground. The strange actions of the two women aroused the attention of the policemen on the scene, who quickly caught them.

In order to maintain the public security on the scene that day, Xuhui police increased the police force to maintain the order on the scene. During the duty, the policemen suddenly found that there was a middle-aged woman in a red down jacket and another one in a beige down jacket among the throng. They carried empty bags and looked around in the crowd, which was very suspicious.

The two women were about forty years old, and were about 155 cm tall. They loitered in the temple for nearly half an hour, always circling around the donation boxes in front of the Buddha hall and the incense altar. Wherever the donation boxes touched by them were, the incense money left by the people on the boxes disappeared. More surprisingly, where they stepped around the incense altar, the scattered coins would disappear. The policemen noticed that every ten steps they took, they would squat down, pretending to adjust their shoes, and then clenched their fists and stretched them into their pockets...
