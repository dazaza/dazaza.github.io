Unified design, stripping IM, Google Plus evolves further

by geekzhang on 2013-05-16 17:22:41

Google+ has been a key focus of Google's efforts over the past two years, with some users complaining that Google seemed intent on integrating all its services with G+. Regardless of whether G+ is considered a "ghost town," one of the highlights of this year's Google I/O conference was the new interface and features of G+.Interface Changes:Google has always been torn about what the G+ interface should look like. After several major redesigns (the last change was heavily criticized), it seems Google has finally unified its design language. The new G+ interface looks very similar to the current mobile client, adopting a Google Now-style card layout.Google says the new interface focuses more on design and depth. This card-based interface not only adjusts the number of cards according to window size but also includes a small feature in the top-left corner of the interface to notify users of new updates, which is quite considerate. Overall, the style is now consistent, and the large blank spaces from the previous version have finally been filled. Currently, the new G+ interface has been fully implemented.Feature Additions:What does this so-called "depth" mean? Google appears to have integrated machine learning into its social network. First, user posts will be automatically tagged for easier search. The tags are automatically extracted by Google based on the content of the post. Not only text information, but even in a photo of the Eiffel Tower with no characters, Google can extract tags such as "Eiffel Tower."Of course, not all photos can be searched, but this function clearly demonstrates the integration of Google's search technology into its social network. During the demonstration of Google Search at the conference, the search could recognize sentences like "where did I take this photo" and find corresponding content from the user's photo album, which is undoubtedly very powerful.Additionally, the photo functionality of G+ has been significantly enhanced. Previously, G+ could identify and categorize faces in photos. Now, it can automatically select the best photos based on exposure, composition, duplication factors, upload them, and even beautify them automatically. Beautification includes simple adjustments to brightness and sharpness as well as further HDR and exposure adjustments.Social Features:In the previous version, the Hangouts group chat feature of G+ could not run independently and was part of G+. Meanwhile, the number of Google Talk users was dwindling due to its limited functionality. With this G+ update, Google Talk has disappeared, and its functions have been replaced by a standalone app called Hangouts, which also includes the original Hangouts functionality from G+.Right after the conference ended, we downloaded the iOS version from the App Store to test it. Like other Google apps, Hangouts' holo style is very prominent. Functionally speaking, it is essentially a cross-platform IM tool developed by Google. Now, users who only want to chat no longer need to open G+; they can send texts, images, or initiate voice chats with their G+ friends through this app alone.Unlike other IM tools, the new Hangouts makes it easy to start group text chats or voice chats. Simply select multiple contacts when starting a chat. Moreover, this service spans Android, iOS, and the Web, making it very similar to Line.The direction of Google+'s evolution undoubtedly aligns with Google's overarching strategy: machine learning, image search, facial recognition, etc. The rumored cross-platform IM tool from Google ultimately manifested in Hangouts. Compared to developing another separate application, this approach effectively re-engages Google Talk users, enhances the real-time attributes of Google+, and integrates a cross-platform IM tool all at once—a win-win solution.
