Classification and Function of Face Masks

Using face masks in skincare routines is one of the most effective methods to nourish the skin. It can make dry and rough skin become moisturized and smooth, oily and dark skin become fair and tender. Therefore, applying a mask is an important step.

I. Functions of Face Masks

Firstly, regular use of masks promotes the softening of keratin and dilation of pores, which facilitates the absorption of nutrients by the skin.

Secondly, as the mask gradually tightens, it enhances the surface tension of the skin, improving human blood circulation.

Thirdly, the adsorption effect of the mask helps to clear deep-seated dirt from the pores.

II. Classification of Face Masks

Face masks come in various types with different states.

Categorized by function: whitening, freckle-removal, wrinkle-removal, anti-inflammatory, acne treatment, etc.

Categorized by state: ointment-like, gel-like, powder-like, etc.

1. Soft Mask

A powdered semi-finished product that requires water for preparation when used. There are many varieties of soft masks, making them easy to operate and store. When using, first clean the skin, then take an appropriate amount of mask powder, mix with water into a paste, and apply it on the face with a brush or stick. After a few minutes, the paste will naturally solidify. Wait for 15-20 minutes, then peel off the solidified mask from bottom to top, and wash the skin.

Note: High-quality soft masks are non-irritating and do not require avoiding the eyelid area during use. However, ordinary commercial soft masks are best avoided around the eyes and lips.

2. Hard Mask

A powdered semi-finished product made from high-quality medical plaster and kaolin, mixed with other substances. Compared to soft masks, hard masks have more complex functions. The unique penetration power of hard masks and the easily absorbed active ingredients in the mask provide obvious effects whether for nutrition, whitening, or anti-inflammatory treatments.

Note: Clean the skin before use, apply water and base cream, cover eyebrows, eyes, mouth, male beards, and hair-heavy areas with paper. To remove the mask, let the customer move their mouth slightly to loosen the mask powder, then remove the mask from bottom to top.

3. Honey Wax Mask

Dissolves into a semi-fluid state, mainly composed of paraffin oil, beeswax, and mineral oil. The honey wax mask forms a closed layer on the skin, enhancing the penetration of active ingredients, promoting sebum and sweat secretion, replenishing skin moisture and nutrients, making the skin hydrated and elastic. Suitable for dry, normal, aging skin, and can also be used for full-body care. However, acne-inflamed and sensitive skin should avoid its use.

4. Drying Gel Mask

Appears as a translucent viscous substance, mainly consisting of film-forming agents, soluble agents, moisturizers, and ethanol. After drying, it forms a closed film closely adhered to the skin, effectively shrinking pores and cleaning the skin. Suitable for normal and oily skin cleansing and contraction.

5. Hydrating Gel Mask

Appears as a translucent viscous substance, mainly composed of moisturizers and nutritional additives. Gentle in nature with strong hydrating properties, suitable for dry and aging skin or eye care.

1. Mineral Clay Mask

Mainly composed of clay rich in minerals, appearing as fine powder. Mineral clay masks can improve and treat skin problems caused by lack of trace elements, with bleaching, anti-inflammatory, de-greasing, and spot-removal effects. Suitable for acne-prone, oily, and spotted skin.

2. Seaweed Collagen Mask

Made primarily from seaweed collagen, appearing as small particles that form a sticky mass when mixed with water. Contains multiple vitamins, protein collagen fiber, and minerals. Can supplement water, minerals, and vitamins, making the skin hydrated and white; also promotes cell vitality and enhances skin elasticity. Suitable for oily, dry, normal, and aging skin. Use cautiously on sensitive skin and avoid on inflamed acne skin.

3. Fruit and Vegetable Masks

Banana Mud Mask: Rich in vitamins and trace elements such as calcium and potassium, suitable for dry and sensitive skin.

Tomato Mud Mask: Contains Vitamin C with strong astringency, suitable for oily and spotted skin.

Lemon Juice Mask: Contains Vitamin C, lightens spots, shrinks pores, suitable for oily and spotted skin.

Potato Mask: Rich in starch, removes excess sebum from the skin, and effectively improves facial swelling and prominent eye bags, suitable for oily and swollen areas.

Watermelon Mud Mask: Clearly improves sun-darkened, overly oily, and large-pored skin, suitable for oily and whitening-required skin.
