Recently, there has been a piece of news on various media that has caused a great stir, saying that a female police officer Su from Lishan County in Shaanxi Province and her husband have created an "swinging" website online and personally participated in "swinging" activities. It is said that the number of registered members on this couple's social website has reached more than 60,000. The couple has made a fortune through this, even buying a house and a car. This incident has already caused a great uproar locally and nationwide.

Su's website is named "A Branch of Guiling", specializing in "couple socializing", with website columns such as "Member Grace", "Personality Decoding", and "Sex Event Feelings". Most of these are member stories about sexual experiences, and the vast majority are "swinging" stories between couples. Perhaps for the purpose of promoting their own website and business goals, Su accepted a video interview with the media, talking extensively about their couple's "swinging" experiences and insights. She also wrote online articles such as "Experience is Water Flowing Past the Skirt Edge" and "Difficult Narration", openly describing the process and inner struggles of their couple's "swinging" experiences. After being exposed multiple times, Su herself became an "outsider" in the eyes of friends and colleagues, facing a sea of criticism, and receiving countless "bricks" online. Due to the extremely negative impact of running the "Couple Bar", this couple had no choice but to submit their resignations and were both fired by their units.

During university, I once heard a foreign teacher say that the late 1960s to early 1970s in America was the peak period of the sexual liberation movement, during which a kind of "couple club" was popular among the middle class, with activities involving spouse swapping. At that time, Chinese people's sexual concepts were far less open than they are now. From the national conditions at that time, my limited knowledge, and the general perception of most people, this seemed avant-garde, mysterious, sensitive, and distant from real life. I just treated it as a strange tale or event from abroad and didn't think much of it. But now, it is happening right before our eyes on our own soil, even occurring in the Guanzhong region where traditional ideas are deeply entrenched. Moreover, the protagonist is a policewoman who not only participates personally but also profits greatly from organizing such activities, leaving many people astonished.

As the central figure of the entire incident, Su claims that the purpose of "couple socializing" is to improve the quality of family life and then promote feelings between couples, with sex being the natural progression of emotional development. The problem lies in the fact that this act itself goes against the ethics and morality that people have followed for thousands of years, and deviates significantly from the monogamy system we have always adhered to. Therefore, its impact on traditional moral, ethical values, legal systems, and marriage concepts can be described as unprecedented. Not only has it sparked heated discussions among ordinary people, but it has also become a focal point for scholars, who express their opinions from various angles.

In fact, regarding the current state of marriage in China, dissatisfaction exists in nine out of ten marriages. Otherwise, how would phrases like "seven-year itch", "fortress besieged", and "holding your wife's hand feels like holding your left hand with your right" come about? Many people say that the passion of romantic love and sexual love only exists before marriage and outside of marriage.
