AC Milan would be better off without one player who is holding back his teammates. What good is a defender who only knows how to shoot?

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-14 10:44:47

AC Milan: One player holding back his teammates; what good is a defender who only knows how to shoot?

Sina Sports reports: AC Milan's unbeaten run of 13 games in all competitions since October was ended by Palermo at home. In terms of attacking quality, Milan did not perform poorly in this game. They had a possession rate of 57.5%, took 13 shots, and Ronaldinho created several good chances, but the final shot lacked precision. Compared to previous matches, Milan lacked some goal-scoring luck this time, while their defensive issues remained unresolved, ultimately leading to their defeat.

Thiago Silva's injury saw Favalli enter the starting lineup. This 37-year-old veteran seemed to be the weakest link in the defense, but during the match, apart from being breached twice by Cavani, Favalli did not make any direct mistakes and was at least better than Kaladze. The responsibility for both goals conceded was clear: in the 49th minute, Bresciano passed the ball in front of the penalty area, and Miccoli cut in from the left wing. Zambrotta, wary of the opponent's speed, stayed two or three meters away and dared not press aggressively. After a simple feint, Miccoli easily deceived Zambrotta and comfortably aimed at the far post to score. Zambrotta’s only contribution in this goal was obstructing Dida's view.

In the 63rd minute, Zambrotta actively crossed from the right flank baseline, but Palermo cleared the ball and launched a counterattack. Miccoli raced through the vacated space left by Zambrotta. Left-back Antonini tried to cover across the field but was still a step too late as Miccoli elegantly bypassed him. By the time Pastore dribbled into the box and his shot was blocked, followed by Bresciano scoring on the rebound, Zambrotta had not even returned to the penalty area! Pirlo, Ambrosini, and Nesta were all covering for him, yet Zambrotta's lack of effort in tracking back only served to burden his teammates.

To be fair, Zambrotta had some bright moments offensively. In the 33rd minute, after receiving a pass from Seedorf, Zambrotta surged forward and unleashed a powerful shot that hit the side netting. In the 88th minute, Zambrotta struck from just outside the penalty area, forcing an impressive save from Palermo goalkeeper Sirigu. However, AC Milan does not need a winger-style Zambrotta reminiscent of his Bari days; they need a full-back primarily focused on defending. In the 28th minute, during Palermo's quick counterattack, Cavani broke past Favalli and sent a through ball to Miccoli on the left. It was Antonini who came over to cover, with no sign of Zambrotta. In the dying minutes, when Milan was countered again, Zambrotta showed no motivation to track back until the opponent began moving laterally in midfield, slowly returning to his own half after about 7-8 seconds! If Palermo hadn't substituted Miccoli early, Zambrotta's defensive zone would have been exploited many more times.

Leonardo actually had the chance to substitute Zambrotta, but when Pato came on, Leonardo chose to replace Abate instead. During the press conference, one journalist questioned: "Why was it Abate who was substituted, and not Zambrotta?" Indeed, having Zambrotta on the field made it exhausting for his teammates.

Zambrotta's active participation in offense to try and claw back the deficit demonstrated his desire to win and sense of responsibility. He attempted a total of seven crosses throughout the match, equaling the combined number from Abate and Pato. However, under fatigue, Zambrotta failed to balance his involvement in attack properly. Knowing his physical condition wouldn’t allow him to track back in time, he still often pushed too far forward. Clearly, Zambrotta struggles to handle the task of single-handedly dominating an entire flank in a three-forward system. Leonardo needs to adjust his usage of Zambrotta, leveraging his experience rather than exposing his lack of stamina.

Abate and Antonini are two crucial players in alleviating the rotation issue among full-backs. But with Pato's form declining, Abate has been repositioned by Leonardo as the backup right-winger. After the winter transfer window opens, the newly arrived Adja may not immediately serve as Pato's substitute. Therefore, Milan still needs to strengthen its bench depth by acquiring full-backs; otherwise, Zambrotta's tragedy will repeat itself.
