Invisible symptoms of diabetes to watch out for

by weixing123 on 2009-09-16 18:30:41

1. Erectile Dysfunction: The incidence of erectile dysfunction in male diabetic patients can be as high as 40-60%. Therefore, sexually functional males who develop erectile dysfunction may have diabetes.

2. Difficulty Urinating: Both male and female diabetic patients often experience difficulty urinating in the early stages. Besides being caused by an enlarged prostate in males, this symptom should also prompt consideration of diabetes.

3. Peripheral Neuropathy: Manifested as numbness in the shoulders, hands, or feet, a burning sensation or tingling sensation in the body, and weakened or absent Achilles tendon reflexes.

4. Intermittent Claudication: Experiencing severe pain in the lower limbs while walking, making it impossible to continue walking. Sometimes accompanied by hand spasms.

5. Rhomboid Glossitis: Atrophy of the papillae in the center of the tongue, manifested as a rhomboid-shaped defect area without tongue coating.

6. Hypoglycemia: Patients exhibit excessive sweating, especially localized sweating, hunger, dizziness, palpitations, irritability, and recurrent episodes.

7. Skin Conditions: Prone to skin itching all over the body, particularly more severe itching in the female genital area. Susceptible to boils and ulcers, with slow healing of wounds and skin infections.

8. Eye Diseases: Patients experience reduced vision, blurred vision, or the rapid development of cataracts and glaucoma.

9. Breath Odor: Patients exhale a smell similar to rotten apples; in severe cases, even sweat and tears have a similar odor.

10. Craving for Sugar: Patients often have a habit of craving sweets even when fasting.

11. Significant Fatigue: Patients feel lethargic and weak, feeling very tired even when doing nothing.

12. Genetic Factors: There are people with diabetes in the family.

The presence of one or two of the above conditions may indicate the possibility of latent diabetes.
