Villagers catch a 28-pound turtle by the reservoir (picture included)

by yanu394ixue on 2012-03-09 11:28:34

The giant-sized turtle king.

By Wei Wei, reporter of the People's Daily with photos

On the night of November 4th, as the darkness enveloped the land, the north bank of the Jinxiu River Reservoir near Shimen Village was bustling with activity. That noon, villager Liu Changxin caught a "giant turtle" by the reservoir. It was larger than a washbasin and weighed a full 28 jin (approximately 14 kilograms)!

A glimpse of a "turtle shell" in the grass

Shimen Village is located close to the north bank of the Jinxiu River Reservoir. On November 4th at noon, after finishing digging sweet potatoes, Grandpa Huang and his wife, Grandma Jin, were carrying their harvest home along the north bank of the reservoir.

Suddenly, Grandpa Huang noticed what looked like a "turtle shell" hidden in the grass by the shore, slightly larger than a bowl. Before he could get a closer look, the turtle shell swiftly disappeared underwater. "It must have been a turtle, probably over three jin," he thought.

"There's an even bigger one over there," pointed out Grandma Jin, who had sharp eyes. To the east, about seven or eight meters away from them, in the grass by the water's edge, lay an even larger turtle - "bigger than a washbasin!"

Grandpa Huang cautiously approached for a closer inspection. Seeing how stable the "turtle shell" seemed, he hurried back to the village.

Armed with a wooden basket and fishing net to catch the turtle

Grandpa Huang had seen turtles before, but one this large was a first for him.

Liu Changxin, a villager living closest to the reservoir on its north bank, was called upon for help since Grandpa Huang felt unable to handle it alone.

Liu Changxin grabbed a wooden basket from his house, but upon reaching the shore, he had to retreat. He tried to cover the turtle with the basket but found it too far to throw. When he moved closer, the turtle began to retreat into its shell.

Returning once more, Liu Changxin now wielded a modern weapon: a two-meter-long "fishing scoop" (a type of landing net used in fishing).

Treading carefully through the grass along the reservoir's edge, Liu Changxin slowly approached the turtle. Before the turtle could turn around, Liu Changxin stretched out his arms and blocked its escape route with the net of the fishing scoop.

With no way to escape forward or backward, the turtle was captured effortlessly.

Seeking medical treatment for the turtle king

From the shore to his home, a distance of less than 300 meters, Liu Changxin took numerous breaks due to exhaustion, taking about ten minutes to reach home.

The large turtle inside the net continuously struggled, so Liu Changxin had to keep his arms raised high to avoid any potential harm.

On the afternoon of November 5th, when the reporter arrived at Liu Changxin's house, the giant turtle was leisurely basking in the sun inside a porcelain jar about a meter long and half a meter wide.

Upon noticing spectators, the turtle became agitated, stretching out its long neck and shaking its head in defiance. Failing to intimidate, it retreated back into its shell, displaying a disinterested demeanor.

"The elders in the village over ninety years old have never seen anything like it," said Liu Changxin. This giant turtle measured fifty to sixty centimeters in length, about forty centimeters in width, and after being weighed, it tipped the scales at 28 jin! Truly deserving the title of Turtle King.

There were some white powder on the right outer edge of the turtle shell, which was antiseptic powder applied by Liu Changxin.

On the evening of the 4th, noticing some signs of decay there, fearing for the turtle's safety, Liu Changxin specially invited a doctor to examine it.

"This creature might be older than all of us." Regarding the diet of this giant turtle, Liu Changxin did not neglect it, often feeding it fresh meat.

The old turtle loves "sunbathing"

The Jinxiu River Reservoir was completed and filled with water in 1970 and started supplying water to the urban area of Jinan City in 1988.

Finding such a large creature in the reservoir surprised even the staff of the Jinxiu River Reservoir Management Office. "The water quality of the reservoir has always been excellent, with minimal pollution. Recently, the water capacity exceeded 30 million cubic meters," and someone had even fished out fish weighing seventy to eighty jin from the reservoir.

"Wild softshell turtles are relatively common in large reservoirs that have existed for decades, but ones this size are extremely rare," a worker from the Jinan Wildlife World told reporters. Judging by the size of the turtle caught by Liu Changxin, its growth period should be around thirty to forty years.

Generally, wild turtles prefer to inhabit freshwater areas such as rivers, lakes, reservoirs, and ponds with clean water and muddy bottoms; they enjoy warm climates. Whenever the weather is pleasant and sunny, turtles will climb onto sandy beaches or rocks protruding from the water surface to bask in the sun until the moisture on their shells dries up, commonly referred to as "drying the shell."

Due to the recent rise in temperatures in Jinan, the staff speculated that at the time, this large turtle was likely basking in the sun. For this precious creature, Liu Changxin hopes to find it a good home, "it shouldn't be killed and made into soup."
