The Macao economy grows at an average annual rate of more than 13%.

by dnfmeimei on 2009-12-06 01:10:53

Deng Xiaoping proposed the great concept of "one country, two systems," which has been successfully put into practice. Under the leadership of the central government, the compatriots of Macao have strived hard and united in cooperation, proving through their actions that "one country, two systems" has strong vitality, and the Basic Law is the cornerstone for ensuring Macao's prosperity and development.

I. High degree of autonomy and economic system

The Basic Law clearly stipulates that the Special Administrative Region (SAR) maintains fiscal and tax independence, implements a separate monetary and financial system, does not implement foreign exchange control, enforces free trade policies, retains its status as a free port. The central government does not levy taxes in Macao, and any surplus in the SAR’s finances is not remitted to the central government. The existing economic system remains unchanged for 50 years. On this highly autonomous economic system track, Macao's economy has grown at an average annual rate of over 13%. The local GDP increased from 47.3 billion Macao patacas in 1999 to 171.87 billion Macao patacas in 2008. Fiscal revenue increased from 16.943 billion Macao patacas in 1999 to 51.076 billion Macao patacas in 2008, nearly doubling. During the same period, the fiscal surplus rose from 2.45 billion Macao patacas to currently over 80 billion Macao patacas, increasing more than 30 times. The overall economic strength of Macao's small outward-oriented economy has greatly increased, completing the transformation from a remote ancient town to a modern tourist city.

II. Smooth operation of the political system characterized by administrative dominance

Based on Macao SAR's position as a highly autonomous regional administrative area under "one country, two systems," the Basic Law designed a political system dominated by the executive branch, where the executive and legislative branches check and cooperate with each other, and the judiciary is independent. Ten years of practice have proven that such a political system meets the needs of good governance in the Macao SAR, fits the characteristics of Macao as a regional administrative area, and achieves a balanced participation of all classes step by step, thus operating smoothly and achieving remarkable results.

III. Full protection of residents' rights and freedoms

The Basic Law specifically provides regulations to protect the basic rights and freedoms of Macao residents. The extensive rights of residents in various aspects such as personal, political, economic, and social are legally protected as never before.

In political life, Macao residents have truly achieved equality before the law and fully exercised their political rights in various elections. In the past ten years, Chinese citizens among Macao residents have legally elected national people's congress deputies three times, participating in the work of the highest state power organ; nearly 500 Macao residents were invited to serve as members of committees at all levels, engaging in political consultation and deliberation. The SAR conducted three Chief Executive elections and three Legislative Assembly elections internally.

In economic life, the free competitive economic system under the private ownership system in the Macao SAR continues, and residents' legal property is strictly protected by law. In social life, Macao residents' freedoms of speech, press, publication, association, assembly, procession, and demonstration are legally guaranteed.

IV. Expansion of external exchanges, maintaining the advantages of an international city

The Basic Law clearly authorizes the Macao SAR to handle relevant external affairs independently according to law, historically granting the Macao SAR extensive powers in external exchanges.

Currently, 78 countries and regions offer visa-free or landing visa treatment to Macao SAR passport holders; 77 countries have consular institutions in Macao performing consular functions; Macao participates in 11 intergovernmental international organizations in an appropriate capacity and attends official international conferences under the name of "Macao, China" as a separate delegation; 240 international conventions apply to the Macao SAR; it has signed 38 civil aviation agreements with other foreign countries and regions; the SAR government announces and implements UN Security Council resolutions according to instructions from the central government. The Macao SAR has established trade representative offices or liaison offices in Portugal, the EU, and the World Trade Organization, dispatched tourism representatives to some countries in Asia-Pacific and Europe, and successfully hosted large-scale international events such as the East Asian Games, Lusophony Games, and Asian Indoor Games, further establishing Macao's image as an international city.

The successful practice of the Macao Basic Law in the SAR convincingly proves that "one country, two systems" is entirely correct. Achieving the peaceful reunification of the motherland according to "one country, two systems" conforms to the fundamental interests of the Chinese nation and also aligns with the fundamental interests of all the Chinese people, including the compatriots of Macao.
