"Starcraft 2" cannot be played smoothly without a dx10 graphics card + 5G hard disk + 2G memory support.

by xiaopang on 2007-05-20 09:42:39

Starcraft 2 (SC2) is, to date, the most lavish real-time strategy game. Its planning began in 2002 — a full five years ago! It was originally supposed to be released last year, with all the plot characters completed by then. However, due to Microsoft's delay in releasing DirectX 10, the game engine couldn't be finalized. There was also uncertainty regarding whether the game would support physics cards. Currently, the general situation is as follows:

1. Fully based on DirectX 10 drivers, it features the latest physics engine and supports physics cards.

2. Still featuring three empires of different races, it supports online team battles with up to 12 players in one match. The standard competitive mode involves enabling all advanced modes within the game, where each team consists of six members operating one race each, engaging in warfare (this is not the traditional 6v6 format). Each member of the team has specific responsibilities: some are responsible for resource collection, others for troop production, command operations, or campaign management (such as controlling air forces and missile units), and there is also a chief strategist. Given the complexity of the game, everyone will have their hands full, emphasizing high coordination and individual capabilities, akin to forwards and defenders in football. Through continuous upgrades and discovering special rare minerals, extremely powerful weapons can be created! Of course, the game can still be played with fewer players, just without activating all advanced modes, which are automatically handled by the computer AI. This is one of the biggest distinctions between this game and other real-time strategy games.

3. The graphics in the game are extraordinarily stunning, reportedly so realistic that they look like they were recorded with a DV camera, making it difficult to tell they were generated by an engine. The scale of large-scale combat and command is immense, especially during formal team competitions, providing a highly entertaining spectacle. This is one of the main reasons why the game was chosen to launch in South Korea!

4. One of the most important points: if you don’t have a DirectX 10-supported graphics card + 5GB hard drive + 2GB memory, don't expect to play smoothly. Formal 6v6 matches require large gaming servers to support them.

5. Airspace will be re-divided into low-altitude, high-altitude, and space zones. Different vertical perspectives can be switched via shortcut keys. Low-altitude is defined as below the clouds, primarily the activity area for transport planes and low-altitude fighters; high-altitude is above the clouds, the zone for large aviation fighters; above the clouds is space, the domain for space warships and aerospace fighters. Some aircraft can operate across airspace layers, such as aerospace fighters. However, space warships can only operate in space and cannot enter below the clouds. The flight speeds of aircraft vary greatly. Coordination between fighters from different airspaces is often key to winning aerial battles, while units similar to radar warning aircraft are introduced. This makes air combat command exceptionally complex.
