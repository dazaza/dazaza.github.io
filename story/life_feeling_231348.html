QQ Zone melancholy diary: Worn memories midnight flight. If only.

by lwf225 on 2009-08-31 18:17:10

I don't know why I have to witness the sky above me change from day to night, and then from night to day, again and again. Just like I don't understand why insomnia finds me repeatedly. I am such a kind and innocent child.

If a normal night's sleep has to come at the cost of an entire night of wakefulness, then I'd rather not have it.

I want to cry. Can anyone tell me what to do? Who can save me?

The May Day holiday is coming. When time can be measured in hours, my silent joy is self-evident.

Thinking this way, I finally become immune to insomnia. Thus, I start to interpret life from another perspective. Reading books that I haven't touched for a long time, generously thinking about people I haven't seen for a while. Or just sitting there doing nothing. My mind is calm, without any ripples. Walking back and forth crazily in the large and slightly empty room. At night, I don't wear high heels, so there's no sound like Wang菲's footsteps in '2046'.

At some point, I also finally start to not know what I'm actually doing, everything seems pale and powerless.

Both batteries of my mobile phone are out of power. There's a faint sense of annoyance in my heart.

Gradually, I find that my words have nothing to do with the outside world. They are just my exclusive items. Perhaps they have already been isolated from the outside world.

Work starts to become frequent like rushing around.

Some familiar yet strange faces make me feel restless. Those people are all proficient in their own lives, while I stand out awkwardly like a fool.

All is quiet. I think this silence is like a disease.

Later. Later. When I'm exhausted from thinking, I smoothly fall into a dream.

Blurred images. A man looks at me with eyes full of pity, then he pats my head and gives me a lollipop. He mutters "Baby, be good."

Then, I wake up suddenly.

Someone said, don't be sad, it will pass, I'm here. Someone said, fill your mouth with Wantai mini sponge cakes, and smile happily.

Someone said I've lost weight, and I must have been having a hard time recently.

When I heard this, I felt so sad that I wanted to cry.

He was sitting on a rotating chair in the corner of the dim karaoke box, spinning around continuously. The room was filled with noise. They were singing loudly and telling vulgar dirty jokes. But I saw him being silent amidst all the commotion.

Next to me is a delicate music box. I keep winding it up, and thus the little Cupid in the center keeps spinning endlessly in the darkness, looking happy like a child.

Looking up at the mottled sky, the gray clouds gradually dissipate. The moon pushes through the clouds and appears surprisingly bright. The night breeze is cool. Unknown insects chirp intermittently. There are stars in the distance, but they're not bright. Like life, there's no passion, but I can't quite say what's wrong.

I start to try to reduce my activity range as much as possible, wanting to erase the traces of my existence in many places.

Everything seems like the end. Inside, the air conditioning is strong, I lie on the floor facing up. My skirt instantly slides down to my knees. Looking at myself, my skin is rough, my eye circles heavy. I look just like a clown. When I use my hands to comb my hair, I often pull out large clumps.

When typing, I'm used to having music in my ears. Some songs bring joy when listened to for the first time, but become boring after repeated listens. Just like those coarse loves that can't withstand scrutiny or chewing. So I realize that nothing is eternal, if there is, then it's loss. Monk Eight said this sentence is too desperate. But after seeing, hearing, and experiencing so much, we all have to view this thin life this way. Standing in the gaps of different lives, we only have bewildered faces, unable to find the direction of coming and going. Helplessly looking around, tears streaming down our faces.

The city at dusk suddenly experiences a fierce windstorm. Rain beats wildly against the windows, everything happens so suddenly. A grand and unprecedented rain falls. The only thing I'm sure of is that this city has gone crazy, completely crazy. The madness fills me with fear and panic. Partial areas experience power outages, and the crowd's noise makes me uneasy. Yet I'm grateful for this grand unexpected event, which finally gives me time to ponder certain things I thought had been forgotten. What I didn't know is that they had always been hidden in some corner, never leaving. In the warmest and most humid part of my heart, a bizarre and beautiful plant slowly grows and blooms. I immerse myself in the loneliness of my inner self, but time deliberately pushes me towards desolation.

Turning off the computer and all switches, I stand on the balcony gazing into the distant horizon. Once again, I clearly smell the earthy scent rising from the soil, which suddenly makes me miss that tin of tea-flavored Riche. Missing some far-off and blurred faces that I don't know where they are, remembering the crowds who have just left, worrying about the upcoming farewells. Bright streaks of lightning flash across the gray sky, illuminating the entire horizon, like accidentally blooming fireworks at the edge of the sky. Instinctively, I step back, but can't help but look up. Then, helplessly, I watch the sky outside the window turn dark so quickly that I can't see my own fingers. I'm waiting for the next sunny day.

Lying on a soft bed, my feet suddenly start to feel cold, so I tighten the thin blanket.

In the darkness, I punctually turn on the radio, hearing the voice I like. But I discover it's her last time hosting this program, feeling disheartened. Maybe a person never thinks about how their actions or inactions affect others. I only know that years later, at the same time and frequency, hearing the familiar voice still brings immense joy.

Getting used to people around me speaking sincerely, I assumed everyone was speaking sincerely. Perhaps I should thank those who have hurt us, because of being constantly hurt, we become more resilient, we start to think, and then we create writing. Then we discover that there aren't so many sincere people, and no one is worth waiting for a lifetime. Finally, when we realize that vows spoken easily actually require all our strength to uphold, we find it to be such a tiring thing.

In elementary school, I had my first favorite singer: Nicholas Tse. I repeatedly listened to 'Thank You For Your Love 1999'. Back then, I was still a child carefully copying books at the desk with a water blue fountain pen, making a rustling sound by rubbing my fingertips. The girl who did homework with me was very thin. Time flies. Many years have passed, and we haven't seen each other since.

And now, the boys I like have bright smiles and white teeth, looking radiant when they laugh. Seeing him, I think of a sunflower. However, he doesn't like writing. Too many people say that people involved with words tend to be unhappy easily. The girls I like have cute faces, warm laughter like the sun, and are very kind. They are all suitable for summer.

My mom likes to stare at me while talking non-stop, likes to read the texts I write down carefully, and enjoys watching sentimental or funny idol dramas with me. So in front of her, I always talk and smile endlessly, write texts frequently. Remembering to update the ongoing idol drama series on time. Remembering to wear her favorite clothes when we go out. As long as she's happy, I can give up everything.

The gentle wind blows my hair into my eyes, and I reach out to rub them. Leaning on a pillow reading a book for over an hour, my heart feels pressed and slightly painful. My eyes are sore and blurry. Sometimes, I turn off the light and light a sea-blue candle. It has colorful shells and small conches inside, and emits a faint fragrance when burning. By nearly ten o'clock at night, the sky is still bright. Somewhere in the distant sky, intermittent bright flashes of lightning illuminate a large area of the sky. Mom says she's scared, then soon falls deeply asleep. But I stay awake. My body doesn't move. Often, when the dawn is about to arrive, I gradually lose consciousness, then fall into a deep sleep. Occasionally plagued by nightmares, without luxurious dreams. Annie discovered long ago in 'Vain' that even as adults, we are still a group of women who keep dreaming. Lonely people mock loneliness, and innocent people mock innocence. The 'Paper Airplane' in my mp3 softly sang all night. Happy voices. Fragmented memories. Often waking up in the middle of the night. Getting up. Not turning on the light, fumbling in the dark. Drinking large gulps of water, walking barefoot in the dimly lit room, reaching out and finding that I can see my five fingers.

Washing my face in the middle of the night. The sound of water seems extremely abrupt.

Abrupt. Abrupt. Abrupt. The whole world is abrupt. Enough. Truly enough.

Looking at these fragmented pieces of text in front of me. Right now, that's all I can do.

I remain silent. I am speechless. I want to sleep. I gain and lose in great silence.
