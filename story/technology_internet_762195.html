Who says we're dull and lack sex appeal? Seven reasons to choose big companies

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 14:16:12

English Original: 7 Reasons Why You Need To Work For A Big Company

This article is written by Mark Stephen, the founder and CEO of IDR Solutions.

Large companies are often perceived as being slow and lacking in excitement, while startups are seen as flexible, efficient, and full of fun. I am currently running a startup, but to be honest, I'm glad I spent some memorable time working at a large company. Below are the reasons why I think working for a big company has its own merits:

1. Exposure to Excellence and Absurdity

In a big company, you'll encounter both brilliance and nonsense side by side. You will witness groundbreaking insights on source code control, as well as long-winded meetings that sap motivation. While large companies may not always be the most dynamic, they have their own ways of growing successfully. Even the most ordinary institutions are bound to have exceptional talent hidden within them. Uncovering the logic behind a large company's operations and identifying employees who excel both technically and politically can become an invaluable asset for you.

2. Working Alongside Talented Individuals

In certain departments of a large company, you'll find clusters of smart people. In my view, collaborating with talented individuals is a truly wonderful experience—it feels like the air around you is filled with intelligence. These intelligent people often choose to work for large companies because of family and responsibility considerations, preferring not to take the risks associated with startups. Experienced core employees can teach you many things, whereas colleagues in startups tend to be your peers, less seasoned in life experiences.

3. Networking Benefits

Twelve years ago, I worked for a large company. To this day, I still stay in touch with many of my former colleagues, and I frequently bump into them in various places. These connections are incredibly valuable. In today's era of social media, it remains crucial for people to know who you are and what you can do. Your employment history at a large company will be documented, and when you need to conduct business with such companies in the future, this experience can provide you with preferential treatment.

4. Enjoying Great Perks

I miss the cafeteria and gym facilities at the large company where I used to work, as well as that summer trip to Amsterdam... The Oracle training courses paid for by the company, the fully reimbursed MBA program—just thinking about it makes me feel good! In contrast, in a startup, getting reimbursement for book purchases would already be considered generous. Would you rather spend someone else's money to expand your knowledge at a large company, or work tirelessly 100 hours a week in a startup chasing some distant dream?

5. Mastering the Art of Politics

At a large company, you don't just learn technical skills; you also develop "diplomatic" and "political" skills. When one day you transition from being an employee of a large company to becoming a partner, these skills will come in handy. Large companies tend to buy products and services generously, and understanding their operational rules can help you better conduct business and tap into more potential clients through them.

6. Slower Pace, More Time for Growth

Startups operate at a fast-paced, meteor-like rhythm, while large companies often move more slowly. Although it might lack the excitement of a startup, it provides more downtime for learning, recharging, and self-reflection. Sometimes, this "slower pace" can even lead to unexpected positive outcomes. I won’t tell you how a major media company avoided spending millions on a ridiculous web project simply because their decision-making process was too slow for the dot-com bubble to burst first...

7. Defining Your Principles and Pursuing Your Dreams

When you first enter the workforce, you're bold and fearless. However, after experiencing the ups and downs of working in a large company, you will understand what is worth doing and what isn't, realizing which matters are truly important and which ones are trivial. You'll also gain clarity on what dreams you want to pursue throughout your life and what boundaries you must never cross. Defining your professional principles can help you perform better in your work.

These are my reasons for liking large companies. What are yours?
