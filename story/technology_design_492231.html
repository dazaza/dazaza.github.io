Tonifying kidney food therapy: Sparrow Millet Porridge for enhancing yang and enriching essence.

by lfiml496096 on 2011-09-20 18:14:27

According to Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) health preservation theory, human growth, development, aging are closely related to kidney qi. One could say that whether or not aging occurs, the speed of aging, and the length of life are largely determined by the strength of kidney qi. When kidney qi is abundant, a person does not easily age, the aging process is slower, and lifespan is longer; conversely, when kidney qi declines, aging begins earlier, the aging process is faster, and lifespan is shorter.

As the renowned Chinese medical expert Ye Tianshi once said, "When men grow old, their lower origin (xia yuan) weakens first." Here, "lower origin" refers to innate primordial qi, which resides in the kidneys. When primordial qi diminishes, it means kidney qi is deficient, thus the body ages. From this, we can see that the key to prolonging life lies in whether one can properly preserve kidney qi. So, what are some folk remedies and prescriptions that can help preserve kidney qi?

[Prescription 1]

One deer kidney, 30 grams of Cistanche Tubulosa, 100 grams of glutinous rice, an appropriate amount of green onion whites, black pepper powder, and salt. Remove the membranes from the deer kidney, rinse clean, chop finely and set aside. Chop the Cistanche Tubulosa finely and set aside. Wash the glutinous rice and place it in a pot, cook until half-cooked, then add the deer kidney, Cistanche Tubulosa, green onion whites, black pepper powder, and salt, and continue cooking until porridge consistency is reached.

This prescription has the effect of tonifying the kidneys, strengthening yang, benefiting essence, and replenishing the marrow. It is suitable for weakness and damage, kidney deficiency leading to impotence, tinnitus, deafness, uterine cold infertility.

This prescription originates from "Sheng Hui Fang," originally used for "five labors and seven injuries, declining yang qi, boosting energy and strength." It is a commonly used prescription for tonifying the kidneys. If long-term neglect or chronic illness leads to kidney deficiency, essence depletion, and weakening of yang qi, symptoms such as labor damage, impotence, uterine cold infertility may appear. The method should be to tonify the kidneys, strengthen yang, benefit essence, and replenish the marrow. The main ingredient in this prescription is deer kidney, which greatly tonifies the kidneys, benefits essence, warms the kidneys, and strengthens yang.

Cistanche Tubulosa and glutinous rice are auxiliary ingredients. Cistanche Tubulosa tonifies kidney yang and benefits essence and blood, while glutinous rice tonifies deficiencies and damages. When all these ingredients are combined, the kidney-tonifying effect becomes stronger. This prescription can both strengthen yang and benefit essence, with a strong kidney-tonifying effect, making it particularly suitable for those with impotence or uterine cold infertility. Other kidney-deficiency conditions can also consume this.

The book "Food Diet Essentials" records a deer kidney porridge recipe using deer kidney, glutinous rice, and fermented black beans for "kidney deficiency causing deafness." The book "Dietary Differentiation for Disease Adjustment" also records deer kidney porridge for "all kidney deficiency symptoms." Although pig kidney porridge and sheep kidney porridge can also tonify the kidneys, this recipe is the best, followed by sheep kidney porridge, and then pig kidney porridge.

[Prescription 2]

This prescription has the effect of tonifying the kidneys, supplementing deficiencies, clearing heat, and promoting urination. It is suitable for weakness and damage, spleen-stomach deficiency-heat, diarrhea, dysentery, and urinary tract infections.

This prescription originates from "Compendium of Materia Medica," "Medical Communications," "Dietary Differentiation for Disease Adjustment," etc., originally used for "benefiting the dan tian area, supplementing deficiencies, opening the intestines and stomach," "hot dysentery, hot urinary tract infection," and is a commonly used prescription for supplementing deficiencies and clearing heat. Long-term illness without treatment can lead to kidney deficiency and depletion. Spleen-stomach deficiency-heat disrupts normal digestion, leading to diarrhea and dysentery. Lower burner deficiency-heat and bladder dysfunction lead to urinary tract infections. The method should be to tonify the kidneys, supplement deficiencies, clear heat, and promote urination. This prescription uses only millet, which tonifies the kidneys, supplements deficiencies, clears heat, and promotes urination. This prescription excels in both tonification and clearing, making it suitable for various deficiency-heat conditions.

For clearing and tonifying to stop dysentery, aged millet should be used.

[Prescription 3]

One sparrow, 50 grams of millet, an appropriate amount of green onion whites and white wine. Pluck the sparrow, take its meat, chop finely, stir-fry until cooked, add an appropriate amount of white wine and simmer briefly, then add water, rice, and cook into porridge. Add green onions just before serving and boil for another 1-2 minutes. Consume twice daily.

The sparrow belongs to the family Passeridae, and its whole body or meat is used. Commonly known as house sparrow or old thief sparrow, it has a sweet taste and warm nature, excelling in strengthening yang and benefiting essence, warming the waist and knees, reducing urination, and is a common health food for enhancing male sexual function and treating impotence.

In this prescription, sparrow is the main ingredient, supplemented by millet to strengthen the spleen and nourish the stomach, moisturizing the tendons; green onions have a pungent and warm nature, invigorating yang qi; alcohol assists in strengthening the kidneys and enhancing yang, thereby enhancing the efficacy of the sparrow. These four ingredients combine to form a kidney-tonifying and yang-strengthening prescription. The original prescription was used for "visceral deficiency-damage, emaciation, yang qi deficiency." Regular consumption of this product can enhance physical constitution and improve sexual function. It is beneficial for male impotence, premature ejaculation, infertility, female vaginal coldness, and infertility. This formula is also recorded in "Book on Elder Care."

People with yin deficiency and internal heat should avoid consuming this product.

[Prescription 4]

1000 grams of dog meat, 1000 grams of glutinous rice, an appropriate amount of fermentation starter. Wash the dog meat, cook until tender, mash into a paste, cook the glutinous rice into a thousand-grain rice, mix evenly with the dog meat paste, let cool, add an appropriate amount of fermentation starter, ferment into rice wine. Drink 20-30 milliliters on an empty stomach each morning.

Dog meat comes from the Canidae family animal dog, also known as canine meat. In addition to protein and other nutritional components, it also contains purine compounds and muscle peptides, as well as potassium, sodium, and chloride compounds. Dog meat has a salty taste and warm nature, mainly tonifying kidney qi, strengthening yang, strengthening the waist and knees, warming the lower abdomen, and strengthening the spleen and stomach. It is most suitable for those who are physically weak. Combining glutinous rice with fermentation starter to make wine further strengthens primordial yang qi and supplements the middle energizer qi. The entire prescription greatly tonifies primordial qi, warms the kidneys, assists yang, strengthens the spleen, and nourishes the stomach. Those with weakened primordial yang experiencing impotence, premature ejaculation, and seminal emission can appropriately choose this.

Those with excessive yang, fire, or heat diseases should not use this.

[Prescription 5]

100 grams of chestnut meat, 25 grams of dried lotus root powder, 2 grams of osmanthus sugar, half a candied green plum, 2 rose petals, an appropriate amount of sugar. Wash the chestnut meat and cut into thin slices. Place a pot over high heat, add an appropriate amount of water, bring to a boil, add the chestnut meat and sugar, skim off the foam when it boils again. Then dissolve the dry lotus root powder in water and pour evenly into the pot, stirring until it forms a thick soup, remove from heat, serve in a bowl, slice the green plum thinly and place on top of the soup, sprinkle with osmanthus sugar and rose petals to complete.

This prescription tonifies the kidneys, benefits qi, strengthens the spleen, opens the appetite, and promotes blood circulation and removes blood stasis, suitable for those with spleen-stomach weakness during menopause.

[Prescription 6]

One white duck, gutted and washed, 20 grams each of black sesame seeds, peach kernels, mulberry fruits, soaked lotus seeds, Job's tears, red dates, Job's tears, and glutinous rice (enough to fill the duck cavity), an appropriate amount each of salt, yellow wine, and monosodium glutamate. Stuff the black sesame seeds, peach kernels, mulberry fruits, lotus seeds, Job's tears, red dates, and glutinous rice into the duck cavity, sew up the cavity opening with thread, place in a soup basin, add salt, yellow wine, MSG, and water, steam until cooked in a steamer. Before eating, remove the thread and it is ready to eat.

This prescription tonifies the kidneys, strengthens the spleen, and solidifies the teeth, especially suitable for those with long-term physical weakness, emaciation, and gum atrophy.

[Prescription 7]

100 grams each of chestnuts, glutinous rice, and rock sugar, 1000 grams of water. Place in a pot, add water, bring to a boil, add rock sugar and cook until porridge consistency is reached.

This prescription tonifies kidney qi, strengthens the intestines and stomach, and is effective for tooth looseness caused by kidney deficiency.

This prescription is called Tooth Solidifying Kidney Supplement Powder, suitable for patients with kidney deficiency causing loose teeth.

[Prescription 10]

1 rib of each of Acorus calamus, ginger, willow bark (brush off the green outer skin, take the white inner skin), Achyranthes bidentata (stem removed), garlic cloves, fresh dry Rehmannia, Cuscuta chinensis, Terminalia chebula, tung seed lacquer, and soap pod. This prescription tonifies kidney qi, regular use can make the teeth strong and not fall out, shiny and white.
