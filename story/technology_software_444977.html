Starry Change Cheats: The Dream of Rivers and Mountains (Part II)

by js585858 on 2011-06-13 10:05:18

Wild Mountain Tour in Mazhuang, Feixian

The car sped down a shaded avenue, the breeze from the river blowing through the windows. Just as I was enjoying this cool wind, my vision suddenly opened up, and the continuous mountains quickly came into view at the top of the road. I had found the great mountains where I could let my imagination soar, and take the lush greenery of these hills into my broad chest to bring back to the monotonous city, enriching my lonely inner world.

At the front of the mountain were terraced fields cultivated by local farmers, planted with wheat, sweet potatoes, and peanuts. The wheat was already eight-tenths full; normally, it should be completely ripe at this time of year, but due to the unseasonably cold weather this year, it has been delayed. In recent years, the weather has been unpredictable, which is surely related to environmental pollution caused by human activities. Picking a grain of wheat, I ate a few kernels that were fragrant and slightly sweet. Back when I lived in the countryside, every time the large wheat fields outside the village gave off their aromatic scent, we would go to our own fields and cut a big handful of wheat with a sickle. We'd then bring it home and roast it on a coal stove like modern-day barbecues. After roasting, we'd rub the wheat in a sieve, and the grains would "willingly" come out of their husks. Then we could stuff our mouths full of the grains, salivating over the delicious meal. That taste must be hundreds of times better than any feast in a restaurant.

We followed a small path carved out by the locals and walked freely up the mountain. There were many bitter herbs growing wild by the roadside, with delicate white flowers whose centers were a soft yellow, creating a harmonious beauty, like smiling country girls. These bitter herbs have many uses; they can be used in traditional Chinese medicine for detoxifying purposes, and are loved by many animals such as rabbits. On my family's dining table, they often appear, dipped in sweet soybean paste, tasting quite nice. As the terrain gradually ascended, there were many poplar trees swaying gracefully in the breeze, peaceful and serene, as if dreaming sweet dreams. This feeling was just like when I was a child, snuggled in my grandmother's arms, listening to her tell magical stories, warm and sweet, slowly drifting off to sleep in her embrace. Thinking back to those days, it was truly a wonderful and happy time.

On the southeast side of the mountain, there were several large apricot trees with lush foliage, covered in green young apricots. In a few more weeks, they will ripen, and when we come again, we'll see the tree full of golden apricots, how tempting that will be! The flavor of these apricots must be the purest and sweetest. Nowadays, many people focus solely on making money, so a lot of fruits and vegetables are heavily treated with pesticides to continuously increase yields. I estimate that these apricots cannot compare with the ones grown here, because they are tainted by humanity's insatiable greed for wealth. I gently touched these precious apricot trees, praying that they would forever live in the world, and when the season comes, I will come to savor their natural fragrance.

At the waist of the mountain, we stopped to rest. In the sparse pine forest, there were many white stones, layered upon each other as if hiding some mysterious creatures. I sat idly humming a mountain song:

Everyone says, Mount Yimeng is good,

Mount Yimeng has beautiful scenery.

Green mountains and clear waters, so beautiful,

Winds blow the grass low, revealing cattle and sheep...

As I was lost in my own singing, I accidentally saw a very large ant crawling confusedly on my hand. I was shocked, having never seen such a large ant before; it was nearly twenty times bigger than a common one, looking extremely strong. I was a little panicked, but seeing its demeanor, it didn't seem to have any intention of biting me, as if it were lost. I gently placed my hand on the ground, and it quickly crawled off and returned to its original path.

I thought that the reason there were such unique ants here was because the environment was well-protected, giving them the conditions to survive. And the fact that the ants did not harm me was because I did not make any aggressive moves against them. Actually, animals are inherently kind, and all their survival follows the laws of nature. However, since humans have intervened, it has completely disrupted their tranquil lives. Animals like tigers, lions, and wolves hate humans bitterly because of our rampant hunting and destruction of the environment. If humans are so brutal and merciless, then don't blame the animals for retaliating!

Looking afar near the summit, under the cover of the northeast mountains, there was a vast reservoir emitting bright streaks of light. The golden sunlight reflected on the rippling water surface, creating circle after circle of black halos, as if entering a splendid and colorful fairy tale world. There was always a feeling that there might be many beautiful mermaids hidden inside, and even a magnificent underwater palace. If I could enter, see those beautiful mermaids, and tour the underwater palace, how wonderful that would be! Unfortunately, my swimming skills are not good enough. Moreover, if I really entered, I would be afraid of disturbing their peaceful life. As long as they live happily, that's fine. Some things do not necessarily need to be seen or obtained personally; perhaps having a calm heart is the best.

Before we knew it, we had reached the summit, and the cool mountain wind blew in our faces. The rustling sound of the trees filled our ears, wrapping us in a soothing melody. It was like the sweet whispers of a lover, the lively voices of children, and the hearty laughter of mountain folk, nourishing my somewhat numb heart. At the top, we saw a broken little stone house and a stone column about three meters high made of piled stones. Could it be that someone once lived here? Were they guarding the mountain? Or something else? What does this stone column mean? Is it an artistic creation? A series of questions arose in my mind. I thought about these questions repeatedly, but ultimately couldn't find answers. Unable to figure it out, I had to give up and continue climbing to another peak. Perhaps one day, I would dream of that little stone house and that tall stone column.

Continuing west along the mountaintop, we saw the terraced fields on the opposite slope through the sparse pine forest. The terraced fields stretched long and curved around the east side of the slope, neatly built with countless small stones into walls as boundaries. I couldn't imagine how much patience and effort it took! From afar, these terraced fields looked like the water towns of southern China, but here they had a different charm: above was a vast reservoir, below were ancient pines covering the mountains. Thus, it seemed grander and more majestic than those in the north, reflecting the broad-heartedness of Shandong men.

When we descended to the foot of the mountain, we saw a solitary old locust tree standing proudly in the valley. Observing its rugged branches and lush leaves, it reminded me of a very resilient old Red Army soldier. I didn't know how many nights and days it had stood guard here, how much sweat and blood it had shed, contributing to the development of the old revolutionary base area and the prosperity of the country. I only knew that it was still fighting, still standing firm. I silently saluted it, expressing my respect.

Following the narrow valley, we climbed to the waist of another mountain. In the pine forest, we saw an old man herding his sheep, leisurely driving them while singing long mountain tunes, the sheep freely grazing on the green grass. What a vivid landscape painting! We went over and chatted with the old man, learning that he drove his sheep up the mountain every day, staying very healthy and leading a very fulfilling life. The sheep were all fat, bringing him a "prosperous" income every year. The old man was particularly enthusiastic, introducing us to the unique scenic spots of each mountain and the roads. Chatting with the old man made me feel close, without any sense of distance, something hard to experience in the city. More importantly, the old man's "contentment" mindset was worth savoring.

The pine forest on this peak was very dense, hiding many birds. On a pine tree, a pigeon suddenly flew out, startling us. Looking up, we saw a bird's nest not too high up. Standing on a nearby high rock, I could clearly see the baby birds inside, lying together, sleeping sweetly. The baby birds had yellow feathers and large crops, resembling cute little ducks. I wanted to touch them, but I knew I shouldn't, fearing that the mother bird hiding nearby would suspect me to be a "poacher." I took a picture of them with my camera, capturing that beautiful moment. I hope they and their mother live beautifully and safely, free from any "good person's" harassment.

Continuing westward along the mountaintop, we occasionally heard the cries of wild chickens until we reached the cliff. Looking down, the cliff was densely covered with pine trees and various other plants and grasses from top to bottom. You couldn't help but admire their incredible vitality. On this barren cliff, without a speck of yellow soil, they thrived, their vitality abundant. If it were me or anyone else living in such a harsh environment, could we be as resilient?

To the right of our vision was row after row of emerald pine trees, standing proudly in the wind like soldiers holding steel guns, shouting loud slogans, marching in unison towards the mountaintop. They looked spirited and resolute, as if the mountain was beneath their feet. I quickly recorded this beautiful scene with my camera. Back home, I could reflect deeply, hoping that in my future journey of life, I could be as determined as them: "The world is at my feet!"

After some time, we reached the foot of the mountain, and we were awed by a massive old locust tree not far from a large well. I had never seen such a robust locust tree; the thickest ones I had seen before were only as thick as a bowl's mouth. Unexpectedly, this tree was almost impossible for me to encircle with my arms. The gray bark seemed to depict the glorious history of Yimeng, gathering the sweat of the Yimeng people, concealing the great Yimeng spirit. The thick and bent branches with lush green leaves stretched powerfully towards the vast sky, symbolizing the infinite bright prospects of the Yimeng revolutionary old base area, and also indicating the Yimeng people's resilience, flexibility, and determination to stride confidently towards the future.

Beside a round large well, an old man was drawing water. We chatted with him, and he set down his carrying pole and told us about the secret caves in the south-facing mountains. He said the cave was located at the edge of a cliff, requiring one to cling to the rock wall and trees to enter. The entrance to the cave was extremely narrow, and one had to crawl inside for several meters before suddenly opening up. It was said that the space inside could accommodate three to four hundred people. The old man used to play in the cave often before he turned thirty, but now he was too old to enter.

I truly admired the old man's courage. Thinking about it, I am so young and yet lack the courage to enter this perilous and mysterious cave. The old man must have been a brave youth back then, and perhaps even participated in the liberation war. In our Yimeng region alone, many brave warriors emerged, unafraid of shedding blood or sacrificing themselves for the sake of the country. Even now, in many mountainous areas, one can still see the graves of many heroes. After bidding farewell to the old man, we followed a mountain path heading east.

Not far along, a magnificent mountain song flew into my ears. I quickly turned around to see where the sound was coming from, discovering it was the old man singing. The old man was already carrying two heavy buckets of water and had climbed to the high mountain waist. I was utterly amazed! For ordinary people like us, climbing to the mountain waist empty-handed is already exhausting, and many people can't even reach that point. The old man was over sixty, yet he was so strong and energetic. Clearly, the people of our Yimeng region are so sturdy and optimistic. This is our powerful Yimeng spirit!

What is painful now is that in many places in Linyi, some people have forgotten the Yimeng spirit. They have been corrupted by the pursuit of money, truly tragic! Nowadays, on the internet, some "boring" people often gain fame, which is disheartening! I think we should let the elderly of our Yimeng region "go viral" online, focusing attention on the Yimeng spirit, a living version, so that the whole country remembers and learns from the Yimeng spirit!

I don't know why I've said so much, maybe because I love every inch of land, every mountain and river in Yimeng so much!
