Cryotherapy: Generally uses liquid nitrogen to freeze the anal fissure wound, temperature usually at -160 degrees Celsius, freezing each time for 20-30 seconds, repeating 3-4 times. Include an oily gauze strip in the wound and fix it with dressings. Post-surgery, sit in a bath after defecation, include one hemorrhoid suppository in the anus.

Chemical Sphincterotomy: Modern research confirms that through non-adrenergic non-cholinergic pathways, nitric oxide can cause relaxation of the internal anal sphincter. A commonly used method is applying 0.2% nitroglycerin ointment locally for treatment, which has a high healing rate, is cost-effective, and has no aftermath of causing fecal incontinence, but has side effects such as headache and anal burning sensation.
