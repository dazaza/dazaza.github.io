Behind the scenes: Robert Zemeckis stated that when he initially created "Back to the Future," he didn't expect there would be sequels. However, due to the massive box office success, a sequel was inevitable. Apart from expanding the storyline, enhancing comedic elements, and creating Marty's hometown in 2015, the production team faced the challenge of bringing back the original cast. The creative team wanted Crispin Glover to reprise his role as Marty's father, George, but Glover insisted on receiving the same salary as lead actor Michael J. Fox, leading to the breakdown of negotiations. In a 1992 radio interview, Glover mentioned that he and Zemeckis had disagreements over George's character, and the $50,000 salary was unacceptable.

Despite certain key scenes in this movie and "Back to the Future III" requiring George, the creative team cleverly avoided direct shots of the character and hired Jeffrey Weissman to play George. The scenes featuring George either show his back, keep him in the background, or have him wearing sunglasses. Additionally, footage of George from the first "Back to the Future" was reused, prompting Glover to sue Universal Pictures for compensation as the original agreement did not include terms for sequels. On the eve of the trial, Universal Pictures agreed to settle, but refused to disclose the amount paid to Glover. Later, the American Federation of Television and Radio Artists revised the rules regarding the reuse of actors' footage in movies or TV shows, requiring appropriate compensation from producers and networks.

Claudia Wells, who played Marty's girlfriend Jennifer in "Back to the Future," intended to continue her role, but couldn't due to her mother's cancer diagnosis. Elizabeth Su replaced her, and the ending scene of the first "Back to the Future" was reshot accordingly.

Before filming began, the creative team spent two years completing the script and setting designs. Due to the large time span, the makeup department used the most advanced technology available at the time, spending four hours each time to complete Michael J. Fox's makeup to portray a middle-aged Marty in 2015.

Zemeckis joked in a TV interview that the flying skateboards in the movie were real, and the reason they weren't released was due to parental concerns about safety issues. Surprisingly, many fans believed it and rushed to toy stores to buy them. Fans also searched for Nike sneakers with automatic laces worn by Marty. In July 2008, Nike released a limited edition "Air McFly" to commemorate the sneakers featured in the movie.

Trivia:

- The US box office for this movie was $118 million, and the global box office was $332 million, impressive numbers but still short of the original "Back to the Future."

- This movie and "Back to the Future III" were shot simultaneously. Due to a five-year gap since the first movie, Michael J. Fox had forgotten how to ride a skateboard.

- Two police officers in the movie are named Rhys and Wylie. Robert Zemeckis and screenwriter Bob Gale often name police or agents Rhys and Wylie in their works.

- This movie marked Elijah Wood's film debut.

- The opening cloud sequence was inspired by Clint Eastwood's "Firefox."

- All main actors signed contracts before the script was written.

- Due to his involvement in "Who Framed Roger Rabbit," Robert Zemeckis postponed shooting this movie and "Back to the Future III" by three years.

- Grief's vehicle in 2015 is a modified BMW 6 Series.

- According to Robert Zemeckis, this movie utilized the most advanced technology of the time, though CGI was just beginning in the late '80s, and the CGI effects in this movie were minimal.

- Upon arriving in 2015, Doc Brown mentions the Reagan administration's slogan "Just say no," focusing on stress among youth in the 1980s.

Back to the Future Part III

Year: 1990

Rating: 6.6/10 (17,900 votes)

Link: http://imdb.com/title/tt0099088

Synopsis:

Based on a letter left by Doc seventy years earlier, Marty and the 1955 Doc find the time machine hidden in a mine. Marty accidentally discovers that Doc was killed shortly after sending the letter in 1885, so he travels back to 1885.

Marty arrives successfully but damages the fuel tank. He meets his ancestor William and receives care. Posing under an alias, he visits the town at its inception but encounters Buford "Mad Dog" Tannen, Biff's ancestor, and suffers persecution. Doc rescues Marty at a critical moment.

Doc learns of upcoming events and prepares to return to the future with Marty, but the damaged fuel tank prevents the time machine from reaching the necessary speed. Doc devises a plan using a train and saves schoolteacher Clara Clayton during site inspections, falling in love instantly.

At the festival, Marty rescues Doc from Buford Tannen but accepts a duel challenge. Doc's relationship with Clara, who loves science fiction, deepens, but his need to return to the future troubles him. On the night before returning, Doc bids farewell to Clara. Under Clara's questioning, Doc reveals the secret of time travel, which leads to a misunderstanding.

Doc feels despondent and goes to the saloon to drown his sorrows, while Clara, heartbroken, decides to leave the town.

Marty wakes up and finds Doc, but just as they are about to leave, Doc passes out from drunkenness, putting Marty in a crisis facing the duel with Buford. Marty wakes Doc and tries to escape, but Doc falls into Buford's hands. Marty cleverly uses a metal plate as protection and defeats Buford.

Marty and Doc begin their plan, and Clara, learning of Doc's true feelings for her on the train, comes to find him. Doc's plan proceeds smoothly, but Clara ends up in danger upon arrival. To save Clara, Doc misses the opportunity to return to the future.

The time machine successfully sends Marty back to 1985 but is unfortunately destroyed, preventing Marty from returning to rescue Doc. He finds his girlfriend Jennifer and goes on a trip together. Due to his experiences with time travel, Marty's impatience changes, helping him avoid future disasters. Marty and Jennifer visit the place where the time machine was destroyed and feel sad about Doc's inability to return. Suddenly, Doc appears in front of Marty and Jennifer in a new time machine. He has married Clara and had children. After telling Marty that "the future is whatever we make it," Doc continues his time travels.
