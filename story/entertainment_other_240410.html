Lantern Riddle History - The Origin of Mid-Autumn Festival Lantern Riddles

by lwf225 on 2009-09-20 02:02:50

Lantern riddles are a form of artistic expression in Chinese folk literature; they are the wisdom and crystallization of people's long-term labor practice, originating from and used in daily life. They are also an intellectual activity game that is popular among the masses. Lantern riddles attract enthusiasts from all walks of life across the country, allowing people to gain knowledge, stimulate their wisdom, and enjoy a wonderful experience at the same time.

The history of lantern riddles in China can be traced back more than three thousand years. During the Spring and Autumn and Warring States periods, "slim words" and "hidden language" appeared, which were the雏形of lantern riddles. By the Han Dynasty, "hidden language" began to differentiate into two directions: one was object riddles mainly characterized by descriptive features, and the other was word riddles mainly based on the form and meaning of characters. By the Wei Dynasty, they were called "riddles." During the Sui and Tang Dynasties, with the prosperity of poetry, a large number of poem riddles emerged and became mainstream. Starting from the Song Dynasty, some scholars often posted riddle strips on various lanterns during the Lantern Festival, attracting passers-by to guess them, hence the name "lantern riddles." After the mid-Qing Dynasty, the trend of riddles greatly increased, producing many riddle masters. After the Xinhai Revolution, lantern riddles formed two styles: the Southern School and the Northern School. In the old society, since most riddle experts belonged to the scholar-official class, some literati considered themselves superior, overemphasizing elegance and rejecting folk lantern riddles. After liberation, under the guidance of the Party's "let a hundred flowers bloom" cultural policy, lantern riddle activities developed even more vigorously, with increasingly perfect and rich riddle materials and works, making great contributions to building socialist spiritual civilization and enlivening public cultural life. Currently, there are lantern riddle activities and academic exchanges among overseas Chinese and Chinese communities around the world.
