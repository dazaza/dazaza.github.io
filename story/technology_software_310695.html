Two confirmed absentees for Arsenal against Liverpool as Wenger refuses Cruyff's call for Fabregas

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-16 13:11:16

Arsenal vs Liverpool: Two Confirmed Absences from the Starting Lineup, Wenger Again Rejects Cruyff's Call for Cesc Fàbregas

According to Sina Sports, Arsenal's away match against Burnley on Wednesday is not expected to be too challenging, with securing three points and reclaiming their position above Villa being the sole objective. However, Arsène Wenger's squad has seen some new injuries emerge. In Sunday's match against Liverpool, starting player Denilson suffered a back injury, while Traoré sustained a groin injury. Arsenal's official website reported that both players have been confirmed unavailable for the upcoming game against Burnley.

There is some good news though, as Eboué, who missed the Liverpool game due to a strained groin, has returned to the team and can bolster the right flank options. Wenger said, "There was too little recovery time after Sunday’s game, so we’ve picked up some more injuries. Denilson is definitely out of the question for this trip, he might be available for Hull City on Saturday, but we must check thoroughly to ensure his back is fully recovered before playing. However, Eboué has returned to the team."

After defeating Liverpool, Wenger was in high spirits as his team had completely shaken off the shadow of their heavy defeat by Chelsea. When discussing the championship prospects for this season, Wenger stated that the 2009-10 Premier League title race is the most open (in terms of the number of teams involved) since he arrived at Arsenal. Wenger told the official website, "No team has been able to take an early lead and hold it throughout the season. Instead, there are four teams tangled together, and I believe that in any given weekend, six, seven, or even eight teams could surprise everyone. Clearly, this is the most open battle for the top spot in the league during my 13 years here, and I feel that many teams have a chance to win the league title."

The respect from other teams does not equate to pessimism about his own. Wenger has always maintained particular confidence in Arsenal. He said, "From the start of the season, I have been deeply convinced that this will be our year, and I said the same after the Chelsea game. The reason I say this is because: when you look at us as a whole, how well we have improved, how strong our team spirit and quality are, and how thoroughly prepared we are for each match like a war, from September to next May, all of this will continue to improve."

For Arsenal to maintain its championship-contending momentum, Cesc Fàbregas' importance is undeniable. With Robin van Persie injured, Fàbregas has almost become Arsenal's only guiding light. Wenger naturally wants to ensure that Fàbregas can play as many key matches as possible, and he rejects certain external requests when he can. For example, the friendly match between Catalonia and Argentina scheduled for the 22nd of next month. Catalonia coach Johan Cruyf requested Fàbregas to participate in this game at Camp Nou, but Wenger firmly rejected it.

Fàbregas takes pride in his Catalan heritage and admires Cruyf greatly, but Wenger doesn't care about these factors. He has the right to reject such requests because Catalonia is not recognized as a FIFA national team. However, the 22-year-old Fàbregas feels that his physical condition is fine, and since there is no double-header next week, he believes he has time to visit Spain. He said, "I’ll play in the games against Burnley on Wednesday and Hull City three days later, but after that, there will be almost a week without Premier League matches, so I can return to Spain on the 22nd to play for Catalonia."

But Wenger can only think about his own team. There is a crucial match against Villa the following weekend, where three of the four major clubs have lost to Villa, raising questions about whether Arsenal can perform well. Van Persie was injured in international duty for Holland, and Liverpool explicitly refused to allow Mascherano to represent Argentina in the friendly against Catalonia. Wenger, who has always disliked the complications of international matches, would certainly not agree to Fàbregas playing in a game that isn’t classified as an A-level international match. Wenger said, "We must respect the concept of priority, and the priority is the club itself."

Besides this match, Spain also has another notable friendly match next week, which is the La Liga Stars versus the African All-Stars. This match is called the Second Africa Charity Match, jointly initiated by UNICEF and the Kanouté Foundation, and will be held at the Bernabéu Stadium. Originally, the African team list included five Arsenal players: Song, Nasri, Diaby, Sagna, and Eboué. Clearly, the African team composition includes not only native-born players but also those with African heritage. Wenger would not agree to such arrangements. Therefore, in the final list, there is not a single Arsenal player, and in fact, not a single active Premier League player either. The closest connection to the Premier League, apart from the founder of the Kanouté Foundation, Mali international and former West Ham and Tottenham forward now playing for Sevilla, Frederic Kanouté, would probably be former Manchester United striker Manucho, who moved to Valladolid this summer.
