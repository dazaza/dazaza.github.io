Can an abortion be performed at one month pregnant?

by dyb44553 on 2012-03-02 17:08:43

Can an abortion be performed at one month of pregnancy? Experts from the Gynecology Department of Shanxi Huimin Hospital introduce that unexpected pregnancies cause a lot of distress for many women. Most women, upon discovering their unplanned pregnancy, choose to have a painless abortion. However, some only realize this after one month, and so they are unclear whether it is still possible to have an abortion at this time. So, can an abortion be performed at one month of pregnancy?

Experts from the Gynecology Department of Shanxi Huimin Hospital state that a painless abortion is not recommended at one month of pregnancy because the ideal time for a painless abortion is generally between 35-50 days of pregnancy. If the pregnancy duration is too short, the embryo may be too small, increasing the risk of incomplete abortion. On the other hand, if the pregnancy lasts too long and the fetus becomes too large, the surgery time will extend, there will be more bleeding, and the trauma to the patient will significantly increase. The larger the gestational age, the more complications during surgery, so it's advised not to wait until the pregnancy is too advanced before performing an abortion or induced labor. Some people think the earlier the abortion, the better; however, this isn't necessarily true. Although theoretically, the earlier the abortion, the better, moving the painless abortion surgery up to within five weeks might make the fetus too small.
