A Study on Gender Differences in Online Shopping Leisure Behavior

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:23:25

Author: Hui Hong, Ding Gang, Jing Yang

Abstract: Just like shopping in stores, online shopping has become an important leisure activity for many people. However, there has been no research studying online shopping from a leisure perspective. This study investigates the online shopping habits of 800 college students and finds that in online shopping as a leisure activity, men are more rational while women are more emotional; men tend to be more extreme, while women tend to be more moderate; men and women also experience different pleasures in online shopping; women focus more on the products themselves, whereas men focus more on the process. This study proposes using a gender difference coefficient to measure the size of these differences, finding that there is a significant gender difference in online shopping decision-making behavior, but less so in brand preference. Based on this, the paper offers positive suggestions for improving e-commerce website construction based on gender differences.

Keywords: Online Shopping, Leisure, Gender

1 Introduction

This paper defines online shopping leisure behavior as including both the act of online shopping itself and browsing/searching for products online. Just like shopping in stores, online shopping has become an important leisure activity for many people. Online shopping is a major area of study in e-commerce, but so far, most studies have focused only on the quality of online shopping [1][2], security guarantees [3], customer integrity [4], online shopping behavior, and other economic and technical issues [5]. There has been little research studying online shopping from a leisure perspective. At the same time, studies on gender differences and barriers in participation in leisure activities [6][7][8] and gender differences in online behavior [9][10] have become hot topics in recent years. It is obvious that gender differences in store shopping are very clear, but are gender differences equally significant in online shopping leisure activities? If we can prove the above question, we can build e-commerce websites according to different gender needs, thereby improving the quality of online shopping services and providing customers with a better online shopping experience.

2 Research Methods

This study designed a questionnaire to investigate differences in online shopping leisure behavior habits, experiences, processes, and decision-making among respondents. The survey was conducted in April 2009 on 800 students at Chongqing University of Technology. A total of 789 questionnaires were collected, with 768 valid ones, of which 430 were female, accounting for 56% of the total number of interviewees.

As shown in Table 4, whether male or female, only a small part can feel that online shopping saves money, and similarly, only a small part believe it does not save money. 3.1.4 Pleasure in Online Shopping Leisure

As shown in Table 5, both men and women agree that online shopping leisure is an interesting leisure activity, with only a few people finding it uninteresting. This indicates that online shopping leisure has become a leisure activity that most people enjoy very much. Of course, similar to store shopping, women show significantly more interest in online shopping leisure behavior than men. Also, as shown in Table 6, only 1.1% of men and 6% of women believe that online shopping leisure is more fun than store shopping. 4 Conclusion

Overall, the differences in online shopping leisure behavior between men and women can be summarized into the following points:

(1) Men are more rational, women are more emotional.

Men like to browse product reviews, compare product prices, need more decision-making time, and like to bargain with sellers to obtain the best value-for-money products; however, women like to communicate with others, thus enjoying the pleasure brought by online shopping more, with shorter decision-making time, usually attracted by the products themselves.

(2) Men are more extreme, women are more moderate.

As previously stated, men either do not engage in online shopping activities or are loyal supporters of online shopping.

(3) Men and women obtain different leisure experiences.

As more women believe that online shopping leisure is happier, women enjoy the happiness of online shopping leisure more than men.

(4) Women pay more attention to the products themselves, men focus more on the leisure process.

The happiness women feel when receiving products proves that women pay more attention to the products themselves, while the happiness men feel when discovering high-value products proves that men focus more on the process.

5 Suggestions

Through discussing the differences in online shopping leisure behavior between men and women, we believe that e-commerce websites should be built according to gender differences, ensuring that users of different genders obtain the best shopping experience, greatly improving market share.

Website construction mainly aimed at female users should focus on the following aspects: (1) Significantly improve the speed of logistics. Currently, the obstacle of logistics transportation time limits the further development of many e-commerce websites, and saving logistics time will make the online shopping experience for women happier. (2) Build a platform for communication between buyers and sellers. Regardless of what kind of business model, a communication platform should be built for women, which is also a key factor in the rapid development of Taobao. (3) Increase advertisements and promotions for fashionable products. Since female users like to recognize new products online and do not reject advertisements, advertisements for new products can be increased on women's websites.

Website construction mainly aimed at male users needs to pay attention to the following issues: (1) Focus on building product review sections. Due to the grassroots nature of product reviews, rational male users feel their authenticity, so the mechanism of product reviews must be improved. (2) Since male users are more rational, the product type should ideally be positioned as scarce goods with high cost-effectiveness.
