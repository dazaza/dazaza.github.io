Strengthen the prevention and treatment of Raynaud's disease in winter!

by sxykmgy on 2012-02-16 11:27:08

Strengthen the prevention and treatment of Raynaud's disease in winter! In winter, when the weather gets colder, the skin on hands and feet turns white. After a few minutes, it changes from white to bluish-purple and finally becomes bright red. Sometimes, the fingers may also experience needle-like pain. The director of the Shanxi Yikang Peripheral Vascular Hospital reminds that these are the symptoms of Raynaud's disease. In medical terms, these three changes in conditions are called Raynaud's phenomenon. If these symptoms appear, do not ignore them. You should seek medical attention promptly because the symptoms of Raynaud's disease could be the first signs of other diseases such as systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE). Prevention and treatment during winter need to go hand-in-hand. While receiving treatment, one should avoid exposure to extreme cold as much as possible, pay attention to keeping hands, feet, limbs, and even the whole body warm, and control emotional fluctuations. Postmenopausal women should be particularly cautious.

Raynaud's disease is also known as digital artery spasm and is a common and frequent disease in cold regions. Raynaud's disease can actually be divided into two types: Raynaud's phenomenon and Raynaud's disease.

Raynaud's phenomenon refers to the spasm of finger (toe) arteries, while Raynaud's disease is different. It involves arterial involvement, which can even lead to complete occlusion. Many diseases can cause this condition, such as scleroderma, dermatomyositis, systemic lupus erythematosus, rheumatoid arthritis, peripheral vascular inflammation, etc. However, most patients with Raynaud's disease do not have any other underlying conditions. Overcoming emotional changes is the key to preventing recurrent symptoms.
