Preface

When mobile phones integrate the functions of radios, MP3 players, cameras, camcorders, TVs, PDAs, etc., they have long exceeded the initial simple definition of communication tools, transforming into an important part of daily life. The rapid advancement of communication technology has further accelerated the arrival of the 3G era. Compared to the previous 2G, 3G brings the internet to mobile phones, allowing users to handle images, music, browse web pages, conduct teleconferences, and engage in e-commerce on their phones. On the other hand, mobile phone manufacturers seize the huge business opportunities brought by 3G, continuously increasing research and development investment, leading to the release of large-screen, full-keyboard smartphones that support WIFI and WAPI. At the same time, the appearance of knock-off phones significantly reduces the average selling price of smartphones, greatly lowering the user threshold for high-performance phones, resulting in a surge in the number of smartphones, providing a complete hardware foundation for users to use mobile internet services.

According to data from the International Telecommunication Union, there were about 10 million minicomputers globally in 1980, over 200 million terminals connected to the internet in 2000, over 2 billion global internet users by the end of 2010, and over 5 billion mobile phone registrations; the rapid growth of users in the mobile internet era is an inevitable trend. The rapid increase in the number of users implies enormous opportunities and will create tremendous commercial value.

In the mobile internet era, terminals become one of the key focuses of mobile internet development. Around the demand for mobile internet development, the development of terminals in the mobile internet era shows a clear trend: closely surrounding user needs, providing users with comprehensive services and experiences, i.e., user experience is king. Today, as the mobile internet is just beginning, user experience has gradually become the ultimate pursuit of terminal development. Apple's introduction of the iPhone has elevated user experience to a new level, winning the favor of global mobile phone users, while the application experience based on Android (Google mobile operating system) has reached its optimal level.

Based on 3G networks and smartphone platforms, various demands of internet users based on PCs are slowly shifting to mobile terminals. The trend of 3G encompasses the integration of desktop internet and WAP content, and the development of 3G in China is making mobile phones constantly surpass PCs. According to DCCI's "2010-2011 China Internet Market Data Report," the growth rate of mobile internet in China is significantly faster than PC internet. It is expected that by 2013, the scale of mobile internet users in China will reach 721 million, exceeding the scale of 716 million PC internet users, marking China's true entry into the mobile internet era. Faced with a promising market, mobile internet quickly gathers a group of entrepreneurs to begin carving out territories.
