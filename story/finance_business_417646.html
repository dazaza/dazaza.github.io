How to maintain a laptop?

Reward Points: 0 | Solution Time: September 18, 2010, 2:02 PM | Questioner: ulmykd

Best Answer: How to properly maintain and care for a laptop is a topic of concern for every laptop user. In this article, we introduce maintenance tips for various components of the laptop, data backup essentials, and solutions to common faults, guiding users on how to use their laptops correctly and reasonably, extending the life of your laptop while ensuring the safety of your data.

I. Basic Laptop Maintenance

Liquid Crystal Display Screen (LCD Panel)

* When not using the computer for a long time, you can temporarily turn off the LCD screen power via the function keys on the keyboard. This not only saves power but also extends the lifespan of the screen.

* Do not forcefully close the LCD screen cover or place any foreign objects between the keyboard and the display screen, avoiding damage to internal components due to excessive pressure on the glass cover.

* Avoid touching the screen surface with fingernails or sharp objects to prevent scratches.

* The LCD screen surface may attract dust due to static electricity. It is recommended to purchase a dedicated LCD cleaning cloth to clean your screen. Please do not tap the screen with your fingers to avoid leaving fingerprints, and gently wipe it.

* Do not use chemical cleaning agents to clean the screen.

Battery (Battery)

* When there is no external power source available, if the current work situation does not require using the card in the PCMCIA slot, it is suggested to remove the card to extend battery usage time.

* Room temperature (20-30 degrees Celsius) is the most suitable operating temperature for batteries. Excessively high or low operating environments will reduce battery life.

* Using a laptop with a stable power supply and removing the battery to extend its life is incorrect. For general laptops, when the battery is fully charged, the charging circuit automatically shuts off, preventing overcharging.

* It is recommended to perform a battery calibration once every three months.

* Refer to international voltage instructions when using the power adapter (AC Adapter).

Keyboard (Keyboard)

* To clean accumulated dust, you can use a small brush to clean gaps or use a high-pressure spray can commonly used for cleaning camera lenses to blow out the dust, or use a handheld vacuum cleaner to remove dust and debris from the keyboard.

* To clean the surface, lightly dampen a soft cloth with a little cleaning agent and gently wipe the keyboard surface when the computer is turned off.

Hard Drive (Hard Disk)

* Use the laptop in a stable condition as much as possible, avoiding operation in places prone to shaking.

* The startup and shutdown process is the most vulnerable time for the hard drive. At this time, the bearing speed has not stabilized yet, and vibrations can easily cause bad tracks. Therefore, it is suggested to wait about ten seconds after shutting down before moving the laptop.

* Perform disk defragmentation and scanning once a month to improve disk access efficiency.

Floppy Drive (Floppy)

* Avoid using floppy disks that are in poor condition or moldy.

* Purchase a floppy drive cleaning disc and regularly clean the read/write heads.

Optical Drive (CD-ROM)

* Use an optical disc cleaning disc and regularly clean the laser reading head.

* When inserting the optical disc into the drive, use both hands—one hand supporting the CD tray and the other hand firmly holding the CD—this prevents deformation of the CD tray.

Touchpad (Touchpad)

* Keep your hands clean when using the touchpad to avoid cursor jumping.

* If the surface gets dirty accidentally, lightly dampen a dry cloth and gently wipe the touchpad surface. Do not use rough cloths or items to wipe the surface.

* The touchpad is a sensitive electronic component. Do not use sharp objects to write on the touchpad or press it too hard to avoid damage.

Thermal Dissipation (Heat Dissipation)

* Generally, laptop manufacturers use fans, heat pipes, large heatsinks, and ventilation holes to reduce high temperatures during use.

* To save power and avoid noise, the laptop's fan does not run continuously but starts when the CPU reaches a certain temperature.

* Placing the laptop on soft surfaces like beds or sofas might block the ventilation holes, affecting cooling efficiency and reducing performance, potentially leading to system crashes.

Maintenance of Other Components (Others)

Before cleaning and maintaining, please follow these steps to maintain your laptop and related peripheral devices:

* Step One: Turn off the power and disconnect the external power cord, remove the internal battery and all external device connection lines.

* Step Two: Use a small vacuum cleaner to remove dust from connection heads, keyboard gaps, etc.

* Step Three: Slightly dampen a dry cloth and gently wipe the case surface. Be sure not to drip any cleaning agent into the machine to avoid short circuits.

* Step Four: Wait until the laptop is completely dry before turning it back on.

II. Important Information - Data Backup

Purpose of Data Backup

Laptops, due to their convenient mobility, are the best companions for users. However, your laptop might lose data due to accidental deletion or overwriting, hard drive damage, computer virus attacks, natural disasters (floods, fires), or theft, causing interruptions to your work.

However, the importance of data is invaluable. Developing good and regular data backup habits can minimize losses and make your laptop use more free, convenient, and secure.

Useful Software Tools

1. Partition the hard drive appropriately.

Hard drive data problems can be divided into two main situations:

First type - Hard drive failure itself. Prevention involves avoiding shaking the computer during startup/shutdown and maintaining regular data backups.

Second type - Operating system corruption or virus infection causing inability to boot. In this case, if the hard drive was partitioned appropriately before installing the operating system, with important data backed up in a different partition than the one storing the operating system, it becomes easier to recover or reinstall the operating system without worrying about losing valuable data.

If the operating system is already installed across the entire hard drive (only one partition exists), a partitioning software compatible with Windows, such as Partition Magic, can be used to add another partition.

2. Systematically classify and store data on the hard drive.

To efficiently back up data, it is recommended to properly categorize data while storing it. Generally, data that needs backing up can be classified as follows:

A. Document-type data, such as Office files, image files, MP3s, etc.

B. Email backup data, such as sent mail backups, address books, etc.

C. Browser-used website link data, such as Favorites in Explorer, Bookmarks in Netscape.

It is advisable to store these three types of data in a specific folder on the D drive. This way, when performing backup operations, simply copying this folder to another storage medium reduces the likelihood of missing data and simplifies the complexity of backing up data.

The safest backup involves storing data on different storage media and placing the media in different buildings. This concept of offsite backup applies equally well to individuals. Although ASUS thoughtfully designed systems with dual or multiple drives, developing a regular backup habit remains the best way to safeguard data.

III. Other Recommendations

Confidentiality Features

Setting Methods:

1. Hard Drive Confidentiality: Users can set a hard drive password in the Security menu of the BIOS setup procedure. During the system boot process, it will first ask for your hard drive password. This way, if someone else tries to use your hard drive without the password, they cannot access it or boot the system, ensuring data security.

2. System Confidentiality: You can also set a user password in the Security menu of the BIOS setup procedure. Every time you boot or enter the BIOS setup, it will ask for the system administrator password. This prevents unauthorized users from operating your computer.

If both passwords are set, the system will first ask for the hard drive password during boot-up, followed by the system password (the two passwords can be set to different numbers). Please remember to keep your password information safe, as forgetting it will require contacting the original manufacturer for support.

Water Damage Handling

* Under no circumstances should you attempt to turn on the computer, as doing so could exacerbate the damage.

* Immediately disconnect the laptop's power cable and battery. Remove any external or removable modules (such as optical drives, floppy drives, expansion memory).

* Drain as much contaminated water as possible from the laptop chassis. Use a soft wet tissue or soft cloth to gently wipe away mud, avoiding surface wear.

* Use a fan to dry the chassis and components, and send it to a service station for professional engineers to handle promptly, minimizing damage.

Travel Usage

* Step One: Ensure all backup batteries are fully charged. You can check the battery level in the operating system.

* Step Two: Turn off the laptop's power switch.

* Step Three: Close the LCD screen cover and ensure it is securely locked in place.

* Step Four: Unplug the AC power cord.

* Step Five: Disconnect all cables.

* Step Six: Place the laptop in a dedicated carrying bag to avoid dust contamination and collisions. Make sure not to overload the bag to avoid damaging the LCD screen glass.

Develop Good Power-Saving Habits

When working on battery power, maintaining good power-saving habits can reduce energy consumption and extend battery life. Even if you have enabled the built-in power-saving features on your laptop, cultivating the following energy management habits is still important and should be kept in mind.

* Use the power adapter whenever possible.

The most obvious way to conserve battery power is to avoid using rechargeable batteries whenever an AC power source is available. Since transformers are small and lightweight, they are very convenient to carry around, so try to use the power adapter frequently so that you always have a fully charged battery when needed.

* Use the suspend key.

The suspend key is one of the most useful features of a laptop. If you need to step away temporarily, just press this key to put the computer system into the most power-saving mode. When you return, pressing any key (standby mode) or the power button (hibernation mode) will restore the system to where you left off.

* Screen Brightness

The brighter the liquid crystal display screen, the more power it consumes. Therefore, to extend battery life, avoid setting the screen brightness higher than necessary. Additionally, if you want to leave your laptop temporarily without shutting it down, you can turn off the LCD backlight to save the most power.

* Floppy Drives and Optical Drives

Both the floppy drive and optical drive consume significant battery power. Therefore, when your computer is running on battery power, try to minimize the use of these drives.

IV. Simple Troubleshooting

Q: What should I do if my computer freezes and fails to restart?

A:

1. Press and hold the power button continuously for 4 to 6 seconds to force a shutdown.

2. Insert a paperclip or sharp object into the reset hole on the machine.

Q: What basic checks should I perform if my computer won't start?

A:

1. If the computer starts when connected to the transformer, it indicates insufficient battery power.

2. Check if the LCD screen display switch or toggle key has been pressed.

3. Check if the issue is caused by additional memory.

4. Remove the battery and start the computer through external power only, then try pressing the Reset key to reboot.

Q: What are the possible reasons and troubleshooting steps if the system cannot resume after entering standby (or hibernation) mode?

A:

Standby (or Hibernation) Mode Definitions:

Standby (Stand By): When the system enters this mode, it allows the system and applications to store the execution state in memory (RAM). At this point, the computer uses only a small amount of power to maintain the memory state; you can resume normal operation by pressing a key.

Hibernation (Hibernate): When the system enters this mode, it allows the system and applications to store the execution state on the hard drive storage area. At this point, the computer has cut off the power; to resume normal operation, you need to press the power button.

Possible Reasons Why the System Cannot Resume:

1. Faulty external memory: If data is stored in the wrong memory address, it may fail to restore the correct execution state.

2. Incompatible external peripherals: Some external USB storage devices, memory cards (Compact Flash, MMC, Smart Media), etc., have special disk structures that can easily cause the inability to cut power or resume after entering standby (or hibernation) mode.

3. Improper drivers or applications: Inappropriate drivers can prevent .all, .sys, and .exe files from entering standby or hibernation modes. Additionally, applications suitable for Win98 installed on WinXP may fail to meet the power management characteristics of the operating system, making it easy for resumption to fail.

4. Operating system management failure: Currently, Microsoft's operating system power management mode is ACPI (Advanced Configuration and Power Interface), where all power configurations are managed by the operating system. If there is a problem with the operating system, it naturally affects the operation of power configuration.

Troubleshooting Steps for Systems That Cannot Resume:

1. Remove external memory.

2. Remove external peripherals and their drivers.

3. Update the BIOS.

4. Update appropriate drivers.

5. Remove inappropriate applications.

6. Reinstall the operating system.

7. Send the machine to the original factory for inspection.

Q: How can I extend battery life?

A:

1. Adjust the LCD screen brightness to an appropriate level.

2. Adjust the volume to an appropriate size.

3. Remove unnecessary PC card devices.

4. Set up power management configurations and share them with your friends via Renren, Sina Weibo, Kaixin, MSN, QQ Space.
