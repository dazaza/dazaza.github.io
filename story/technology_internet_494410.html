Time of US defunct satellite's fall to Earth may be postponed to afternoon of September 24th

by diglog on 2011-09-24 14:12:32

According to the latest news from NASA, an American decommissioned satellite weighing 6.5 tons may fall to Earth later than expected, possibly in the afternoon of September 24th Beijing Time. The debris could also potentially fall over North America. However, experts have stated that the probability of the satellite debris hitting a person is extremely low. Last week, an amateur astronomy enthusiast in France captured images of the satellite. In the footage, this out-of-control defunct satellite flickers and spins continuously. An amateur astronomy enthusiast near Dunkirk, northern France, captured the satellite's presence using a motorized lens on September 15th. After entering the atmosphere, the satellite is expected to break into more than a hundred pieces, most of which will burn up, but approximately 26 fragments might hit the ground. NASA previously predicted that the satellite would fall in the afternoon of September 23rd Eastern Time, but now states that it might be delayed until the early morning of September 24th, or the afternoon of September 24th Beijing Time. The estimated range for the satellite debris to fall is between 57 degrees north latitude and 57 degrees south latitude. Scientists had earlier predicted that the debris might fall into the Pacific Ocean or the Indian Ocean. However, NASA corrected on its website on September 23rd that the debris might now also fall over North America, though the probability remains low.
