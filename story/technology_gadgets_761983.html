Meitu Mobile: Cai's Platform Theory and the Positioning of the Mobile Phone Niche Market

by geekzhang on 2013-05-17 09:37:01

On the afternoon of May 16th, Meitu XiuXiu held a launch event at the central hall of Dpark in the 798 Art Zone for its new smartphone, the MeituKiss. The MeituKiss is manufactured by a domestic supplier and features Android 4.2, a 4.5-inch IPS high-definition retina screen with a resolution of 1280x720, and both front and rear cameras are equipped with 8 million pixels. Targeting female users, the first batch offers two models: white and pink, priced at 2199 yuan. Pre-orders started on the day of the launch, with the first official sales scheduled for June 6th, limited to 18,888 units.During the launch event, Chairman of Meitu XiuXiu, Cai Wensheng, and CEO Wu XinHong presented details about the Meitu phone. After the event, Wu XinHong also gave a joint interview to several media outlets. Skipping over complex procedures, this article only summarizes the valuable points from Cai Wensheng and Wu XinHong's speeches and interviews.Cai Wensheng: The best platform combines software, hardware, and cloud servicesAt the launch, Chairman of Meitu XiuXiu, Cai Wensheng, explained the reasons behind creating the Meitu phone and what he considers the best platform.Cai Wensheng believes that mobile phones will become the main carriers of mobile internet in the future, and the optimal platform model for mobile internet should be a combination of software, hardware, and cloud services. Based on this starting point and the current status of Meitu XiuXiu, they decided to produce their own smartphone.Software: Meitu series productsMeitu XiuXiu was founded in 2008 and began entering the mobile internet market in 2011, leveraging its advantages accumulated from PC-based applications to quickly dominate the mobile image market. According to data released at the event, the Meitu series has 445 million users, with 24.59 million daily active users processing 460 million photos daily. The success of the Meitu XiuXiu series of software provides a strong user base.Hardware: Meitu PhoneWith the robust user base provided by the Meitu XiuXiu series of software, Meitu XiuXiu decided to attempt something in the hardware space, leading to the creation of the MeituKiss. Combining the main functions of the Meitu XiuXiu software, it focuses on selfie-taking as its primary selling point, segmenting the smartphone market and laying out the hardware aspect mentioned by Cai Wensheng.Cloud: MeituAirAt the launch, Meitu XiuXiu also introduced MeituAir, a cross-terminal management software that embodies Cai Wensheng’s vision of the cloud. Users can manage their phones through this software. From the interface images shown at the event, it is somewhat similar to AirDroid. This platform also handles cloud storage and synchronization among other functions, allowing users' images and other data to sync via the Meitu account system to the cloud.Through the integration of these three levels, Cai Wensheng has achieved a layout of software + hardware + cloud. Although the software component has already been successful, Meitu still has a long way to go in terms of hardware and cloud services.Wu XinHong: Positioning within the segmented marketAt the launch, Wu XinHong discussed the positioning of the Meitu phone and his understanding of user needs. During the post-event interview, he also shared his views on questions raised by the media.The value point of vertical marketsIn Wu XinHong's view, the functions of smartphones are becoming increasingly homogenized, with their appearances and features becoming more uniform. Smartphone manufacturers are all striving to create a device suitable for a broader audience, but ironically, phones designed for everyone often fail to meet the specific needs of certain groups, such as those who prioritize selfies.Almost all phones have a camera function, but almost all phones prioritize the rear camera over the front one. In the past, the front camera was mainly used for video calls and didn't require high pixel quality. However, the current reality is that many people use the front camera for taking selfies, yet the mindset of smartphone manufacturers towards front-facing cameras remains stuck in the past. This unmet need for selfies presents the market value of the Meitu phone.No competition on specifications, focus on experienceDuring the interview, Wu XinHong repeatedly emphasized that the Meitu phone would not compete on specifications but instead prioritize the selfie-taking experience for users. Specifications are just a marketing gimmick for users and do not fully represent a phone's performance or characteristics.The technical optimizations made by the Meitu phone revolve around the demand for front-facing photography. For example, the front camera features 8 million pixels, an ISP (image signal processor) produced by Fujitsu, five specially designed lenses, AF autofocus, and an F/2.2 aperture.User needs we may never fully understandFor someone solely focused on developing software, Wu XinHong has already achieved success with Meitu XiuXiu. However, using a single software product as the basis for making phones is still a significant challenge. Despite Cai Wensheng's strategy being sound, Wu XinHong's execution being effective, and the user base of the Meitu XiuXiu series being intact, successfully integrating these positive factors into a new domain requires time.After the launch, I chatted with some colleagues about whether this phone would become popular. Generally, there was a pessimistic attitude, stating that the selling point was too singular, iPhone users wouldn't buy it, and Xiaomi users might find the functionality insufficient. Personally, I remain optimistic. The reason is simple: look at the success of OPPO and BBK series phones in second-, third-, and fourth-tier cities in China. A large portion of Chinese users don't care if the phone is smart or if its features are extraordinarily powerful. What they care about is whether the phone can make calls, send text messages, take pictures, play music, and chat on QQ — or now perhaps also use WeChat. They simply don't care about specifications or whether the phone can be rooted or flashed. Sometimes, user needs are very simple; we just complicate them unnecessarily. Additionally, women are peculiar creatures — never try to view them through so-called normal logic.
