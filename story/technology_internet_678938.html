(1) **Arbitrary Use of Medication Based on Experience**

Some women who have had fungal or trichomoniasis vaginitis use previously effective medications when experiencing recurrent itching. However, the type of vaginitis may have changed, and if the treatment is not targeted, relief will not be achieved. Therefore, women experiencing recurrent itching should first visit a hospital for examination to understand the type of vaginitis for accurate treatment.

Some women say they have taken many antibiotics but their vaginitis persists. In fact, proper treatment for vaginitis rarely involves oral medication.

(2) **Possible Presence of Other Complications**

Some patients with vaginitis experience repeated episodes despite proper treatment. For these patients, other conditions such as diabetes or sexually transmitted diseases should be considered, requiring further testing. Additionally, both partners should be tested and treated simultaneously to avoid mutual reinfection.

Long periods of sitting, prolonged use of panty liners, improper water usage, and blind use of vaginal washes make infections more likely or cause poor ventilation in the perineal area leading to infection. When using water, some women insert their fingers or towels into the vagina, which can easily introduce bacteria and cause or worsen infections. Nowadays, various vaginal washes are freely available in pharmacies, some containing mint components that create a cooling sensation after use. However, frequent use of vaginal washes may disrupt the vaginal environment, worsening vaginitis.
