January Rain

After being caressed by a night of drizzle, the ground was damp and not yet dry when I went out in the morning; the air was fresh but carried a hint of coldness, allowing one to see the puffs of white breath exhaled. Not long ago, the tall evergreen trees stood erect, but now they were left with only bare branches, sprawling towards the nearby houses, although they were over ten feet high, they certainly weren't beautiful at this moment, instead evoking a sense of desolation and decay.

The clouds here pass overhead numbering about five hundred thousand a day, most of them edged with a grayish hue that gradually deepens from the edge to the center until it becomes a heavy dark black, giving a feeling of oppression: always hiding the sun. The high-voltage electricity is transmitted here kilo-yard by kilo-yard, connecting all directions of this city, providing it with ample power day and night, etching grooves and circuits along every vein of its body, leading it into prosperity.

Here, the asphalt roads are laid mile by mile, often one can see several road rollers flattening the road, followed by a large construction team. Thus, layer upon layer of concrete is poured on, mixing with the asphalt and quickly hardening. Finally, its mission and the moment of glory arrive; no matter how many millions of footprints pile up on it, no matter how many times cars roll over it, no matter how time wears it down, it always remains upright. In these years, the lamplights have remained the same, faintly enveloping this city —— the city where I live.

In such a city, in such a prosperous city, I once naively had such thoughts, which now seem foolishly cute; I wanted to bring everything I saw and knew back to my hometown, to sit under the tall and lush camphor trees and tell those mischievous children stories, making them stare wide-eyed as I recount the city's tales, then quietly fall asleep amidst the countless light spots filtering through the gaps in the branches, perhaps curling their lips in dreams. These were my purest thoughts back then, I looked at this world with the most curious eyes like a child, and I couldn't understand it at all...

"Alas, looks like I need to lose weight!" (a girl who's already skinny said unhappily), "Another lump of flesh has appeared, so ugly!", "Oh my, you've lost quite a bit of weight again after just a few days, look at that waist... tsk tsk..."

Such a series of weight-loss talk frequently rings in the ears, with all the girls in the company, whether fat or thin, dissatisfied with their figures. But these things are actually trivial. I just silently lit a cigarette in the corner, letting the smoke swirl around, gazing out the window at the picturesque scenery with open eyes. At that moment, I saw the entire process of the world becoming clear then blurry again, thus, there was a moist warmth at the corners of my eyes; at that moment, I finally understood why your eyes often brimmed with tears.

But, is this a punishment?

I never believed that the person lying in the blood pool could be you, but when that familiar face clearly appeared before my eyes, I heard the sound of the flood rushing from my heart to my eyes, and in that instant, it splashed down my face. It was the first time I cried for a girl, I don't even know why I'm so sad, is it because of your kindness to me?

If there is a next life, I will surely understand your feelings, believe me, okay? I've never lied to you.

And now, do you, in heaven, hear my call?

Some say rain is the tears shed by the departed who still care about people and matters in this mundane world.

It rained outside the window again, stretching for dozens of miles, the drizzle falling gently, obscuring the view in a flurry.
