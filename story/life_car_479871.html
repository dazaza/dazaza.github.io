To Accelerate the Optimization of Dealer Network Talent, Volkswagen Establishes an Automotive Academy in China

Volkswagen, which has achieved tremendous success in China, is now pursuing more comprehensive development. On March 31st, Volkswagen established its first training academy in China, named "Volkswagen Automotive Academy." This academy focuses on training automotive sales and after-sales service trainers, primarily providing authoritative professional and management training from headquarters while emphasizing the application of new knowledge and skills. It is expected that within the next five years, 700 automotive trainers will graduate from this school.

1. Aimed at Enhancing Customer Service Levels

The Volkswagen Automotive Academy will focus on cultivating automotive sales and after-sales service trainers. Besides offering authoritative professional and management training from headquarters, it will also emphasize the application of new knowledge and skills. Trainers who complete the training courses will assist dealers in steadily developing core businesses while adapting to changes in customer purchasing behavior, enhancing customer service levels, and seeking new profit points.

According to reports, the Volkswagen Automotive Academy will be the first outside the group headquarters to issue globally recognized Volkswagen training certificates. The academy will not only bring Volkswagen's knowledge and training system but will also combine with the current development status of the Chinese automotive market, continuously seeking innovative technologies and concepts in talent cultivation, striving to become one of the leading automotive industry training institutions domestically and even in the Asia-Pacific region.

Christian Klingler, a member of the Group Board responsible for Sales and Marketing at Volkswagen Group, stated that China is the largest market for Volkswagen Group. In the coming years, they will further expand production capacity, introduce the latest models and advanced technologies. However, Volkswagen's strategic goals in China are not limited to sales performance; outstanding customer service is equally crucial. The establishment of the Volkswagen Automotive Academy signifies that Volkswagen will collaborate hand-in-hand with dealers to provide consumers with better products and services.

2. 700 Trainers Expected to Graduate Within Five Years

It is understood that from 2010 to 2014, the number of dealer networks in the Chinese market for Volkswagen will increase from 1,000 to 1,400, and in the next 6 to 8 years, the number of dealer networks will double. Volkswagen stated that this expansion mainly targets second-tier, third-tier cities, and even fourth-tier cities. Geographically, the growth rate of dealers in western regions will outpace that in eastern regions. Most will be in the form of 4S stores, but there will also be some new concepts and formats, such as the Huitem customization center or urban exhibition centers.

"This also means that the number of people requiring training every month will significantly increase, with tens of thousands needing training," Christian Klingler said. The training content includes sales skills, brand and customer service techniques, as well as technical aspects. Because Volkswagen will see more technological advancements in the future, it is necessary to prepare the existing team and employees. For example, future car maintenance will not just rely on manual labor but will also require computer operations, so it is essential to prepare them in advance to provide timely car maintenance and servicing for users.

Volkswagen anticipates that within the next five years, the Volkswagen Automotive Academy in China will train 700 trainers, and the academy will develop into a fairly large-scale school. "The first step of the Volkswagen Automotive Academy is targeting the Volkswagen brand. In the first year, 6 million euros were invested," said Mark Su, Executive Vice President of Volkswagen Group (China). In the future, Volkswagen will invest more, and the next step will be to serve the entire Volkswagen Group.
