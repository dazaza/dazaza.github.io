Family comes first, kinship is paramount —— "The Magic Remote Control" Click Blu-ray Brief Review

by diglog on 2010-10-07 05:15:06

Which is more important, life or career? Nowadays, many people are asked this question or face the choice. *The Magic Remote Control* presents an unbalanced choice in a comedic way. Although it's somewhat sci-fi and extreme, it can still make people reflect on this issue. The latter part of the movie shifts to a sad atmosphere. When everything is too late, no matter how magical the magic is, it’s useless; what has been missed can only be missed, there is no regret pill to take. The protagonist chases his son in the rain just to tell him to prioritize the family and not repeat his mistakes, pushing the movie to its climax. It's rare to see audiences crying in the cinema these days, but I saw it when this film was released, and I was among those who were moved to tears. Many contents of the public screening version were absurdly cut, and the crude editing made many lines and music abruptly jump halfway through, leaving people confused. The DVD had a mainland theater version and a "world theater version," which I tentatively called them. Now, the Blu-ray disc combines the two versions into one, providing me with the best audio-visual experience of this movie that I quite like. Although the picture has some graininess, this effect is partly related to the film's cinematographic style. Solid black color, sufficient black level depth, makes the night scene perform satisfactorily. Outdoor daytime scenes have rich colors, although I feel the film's colors are slightly saturated, but good picture quality ensures the optimal viewing state.

The Blu-ray disc provides English Dolby TrueHD surround sound, and Chinese Dolby TrueHD surround sound is only available in the mainland theater version, which I haven't listened to. First, because I don't like Changchun Film Studio's dubbing, even though it was publicly screened; second, I won't watch the nauseatingly edited version. The English soundtrack performs well, with clear surround sound and powerful bass. The first sound of firecrackers thrown by mischievous children during the outdoor gathering is indeed startling, a crisp "pop" sound that is both powerful and clean without any dragging. The sound of the protagonist freezing time and slapping his boss is very satisfying to hear, despite the slightly exaggerated sound design. In the final scene where the protagonist runs in the thunderstorm, the accompanying music plays a 50% role in stirring emotions, fully demonstrated through the enveloping surround sound, making it hard not to be moved (unless you really don't like watching, I know not everyone likes this movie).

The disc includes about 30 minutes of behind-the-scenes footage in 1080i HD, with the same content as the DVD version, which is a standard configuration for the video release of a film. Fans of this movie who own Blu-ray equipment should pick up this 104-yuan disc.
