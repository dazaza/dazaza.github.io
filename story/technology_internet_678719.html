Disputes arising from the realization of mortgage rights by banks

In construction projects, regarding the issue of whether the bank's realization of mortgage rights or consumer rights takes precedence, if consumers have completed property registration procedures and the ownership of the house has not been transferred, the principle that property rights take precedence over creditor's rights generally applies, allowing the bank to realize its mortgage rights. However, this principle is based on general circumstances and can change under special conditions.

Mr. Yan and his girlfriend had been in a relationship for many years and planned to get married after purchasing a house. In June 2004, Mr. Yan and his girlfriend pooled together enough money to buy a two-bedroom apartment from a development company in Kunshan. By July, they had fully paid the purchase price. Since the apartment was still under construction, Mr. Yan was unable to obtain the property certificate at that time. The development company promised Mr. Yan that they would process the property certificate after the project was completed. Although Mr. Yan had some doubts, he agreed.

In July 2004, due to an urgent need for funds, the development company mortgaged the apartment along with other properties to a commercial bank in Kunshan. The mortgage secured a loan of 280,000 yuan. Because the development company faced continuous difficulties in real estate development, it failed to repay the bank loan when it became due. As a result, the bank auctioned off the mortgaged properties, including the apartment purchased by Mr. Yan. After learning about this, Mr. Yan claimed that since he had paid for the apartment, he owned it and the bank had no right to auction it. The bank insisted that as the property was mortgaged, they had the right to realize the mortgage. Consequently, both parties took the matter to the Beiqu People's Court of Kunshan City.
