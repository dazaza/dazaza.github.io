**Pilz Detailed Introduction**

Pilz, headquartered in Germany, has a history of over 50 years since its establishment. It is a world-leading technical innovator and system supplier in the field of automation. In 1968, Pilz invented the world's first dual-hand operating relay, which was widely recognized as a major breakthrough in the field of safety control. In 1987, Pilz launched the world’s first emergency stop relay, PNOZ, making the Pilz brand synonymous with safety relays. Following the introduction of the programmable safety system PSS, in 1997 Pilz entered a new realm of safety technology with its safe, open-bus system SafetyBUS p. Today, Pilz products are applicable to any field of mechanical engineering, ranging from traditional automation industries such as automotive manufacturing, food processing, and wood processing, to modern airports, theaters, cable cars, and amusement parks. Pilz products not only apply to standard safety functions like emergency stop, safety doors, and dual-hand controls but also provide suitable solutions for various applications.

Pilz introduced the modular safety relay PNOZmulti, a multifunctional, freely configurable safety control product that can be used in many areas of mechanical engineering. With PNOZmulti, it is possible to safely monitor emergency stops, safety doors, light curtains/grids, dual-hand controls, and many other safety functions. Additionally, conventional control functions can be economically implemented.

**PNOZmulti Mini:** Despite being more compact and smaller in size, the PNOZmm0p retains all the common features of the PNOZmulti. Featuring direct information and status displays, the PNOZmm0p elevates communication with operators to a new level. More importantly, all required functions can be easily generated through simple configuration software on a PC.

The first product in the PNOZmm0p series has the following technical specifications:

- 20 inputs

- 4 safety semiconductor outputs

- 4 test pulse outputs

- LCD display for information and diagnostics

- USB configuration interface

- Configured via PNOZmulti Configurator

- Fully compatible with PNOZmulti

The PNOZmm0p has passed all relevant tests and complies with the following standards:

- EN ISO 13849-1

- EN/IEC 62061

- EN/IEC 61508-1 to -4

- EN 60204-1

- EN/IEC 61511 Part 1-3

- IEC 61131-2

- UL 508

- UL 1998

- NFPA 79

**PNOZmulti Mini**: Currently one of the most successful products in Pilz's product range, the PNOZmulti Mini inherits the main features of the PNOZmulti but with a more compact structure and lower cost. In certain specific applications, it offers excellent cost-performance ratios and can be applied across multiple industries such as metalworking, machine tools, automotive, and new energy. Particularly in the metalworking industry, its application prospects are very broad.
