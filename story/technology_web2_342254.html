Why did Zhang Ziyi win the Outstanding Contribution Award for Chinese-language films? [Original] Written by Fei Hua On the evening of June 14, 2008, the 11th Shanghai International Film Festival grandly opened. Zhang Ziyi was awarded the Outstanding Contribution Award for Chinese-language films by the organizing committee of the Shanghai International Film Festival, while Qin Yi and the late British director Anthony Minghella received the Lifetime Achievement Award. (According to Sohu Entertainment) Since the Shanghai Film Festival established the Outstanding Contribution Award for Chinese-language films in 2005, Zhang Ziyi is the fourth star to receive this honor, the other three being Zhang Yimou, Jackie Chan, and Maggie Cheung. Some people asked: Why did Zhang Ziyi win the "Outstanding Contribution Award for Chinese-language films"? I would like to adapt an old saying: Existence has its reason. A professor once claimed that "Zhang Ziyi's greatness has been underestimated a hundred times," since Zhang Ziyi is "great," naturally winning the "Outstanding Contribution Award" is as easy as water flowing into a channel. Try to imagine, how many people in the entertainment industry can be worthy of the two words "greatness"? Jokes aside, it's hard to bring up such topics seriously. In my opinion, Zhang Ziyi being awarded the "Outstanding Contribution Award" is probably the film industry's "keen eye for talented women." Zhang Ziyi is undoubtedly one of the most outstanding young actresses in the country. She has not been in the film industry for long, but her awards are already numerous. "The Road Home" transformed the relatively unknown Zhang Ziyi into the highly sought-after "Mistress of Zhang Yimou," earning her the Best Actress award at the Hundred Flowers Awards; "Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon" propelled Zhang Ziyi into the ranks of international stars, winning her the Best Supporting Actress award at the American Independent Spirit Awards; "House of Flying Daggers" continued Zhang Ziyi's martial arts romance, allowing her to win the Huabiao Award for Outstanding Actress; and "Jasmine Women" and "2046" achieved her Golden Rooster Award for Best Actress and the Hong Kong Film Award for Best Actress. It would be somewhat far-fetched not to acknowledge her excellence as an actress. Zhang Ziyi is also one of the most internationally renowned Chinese movie stars. She made her international debut wearing a red belly band, stunning the global film industry with her unique charm. She was invited to serve as a juror at the Cannes Film Festival, one of Europe's three major film festivals, which undoubtedly represents international recognition of her relatively short acting career. Remember when Zhang Ziyi attended the Venice Film Festival, the festival chairman Mark Muller knelt down to welcome her at the dock. The proud American Time magazine also named her one of the top 100 most influential people globally in 2005, fully demonstrating Zhang Ziyi's international influence. It's not excessive to say she brought glory to her country. Moreover, Zhang Ziyi is also the "Chinese Love Ambassador," and her charitable footprints have covered the length and breadth of China, from south to north and inside and outside the Great Wall. Especially after the Wenchuan earthquake, she not only set an example by donating generously herself but also worked tirelessly for disaster areas. When she didn't forget to raise funds overseas while shooting abroad, when she refused to walk the Cannes red carpet during the national mourning period, and when she scolded foreigners who were unaware of the Wenchuan earthquake as "idiots" at Cannes, she showed great love and a strong sense of responsibility, touching many people. Of course, if merely based on these miscellaneous reasons, Zhang Ziyi could win the "Outstanding Contribution Award for Chinese-language films," it would be laughable. The reason I use the word "laughable" is because Zhang Ziyi's achievements, contributions, influence, and kindness have long existed, just waiting for the "east wind." What is this "east wind?" Perhaps it's simply that only Zhang Ziyi's attendance at the Shanghai Film Festival could lead to her receiving the award. This is not a random guess by me. I remember that in the 2007 Shanghai Film Festival, Zhang Ziyi, rumored to be the first to walk the opening ceremony red carpet, suddenly announced her inability to attend due to reasons like "going to shoot in America and training for the Peking Opera 'Mei Lanfang'". Consequently, a Shanghai citizen, Ms. Wu, angrily called Xinmin Network, expressing her fury over Zhang Ziyi's absence, accusing her of "rejecting an opportunity out of arrogance" and lacking "integrity and patriotism." Zhang Ziyi's last-minute cancellation also upset the organizing committee, which clearly stated that the "individual" star's no-show had many stories behind it and they would "discuss it thoroughly" after the film festival. After a year, Zhang Ziyi became "trustworthy", "patriotic", and "attended", so naturally, this award belonged solely to her.
