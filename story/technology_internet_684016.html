In home decoration, the hidden project refers to the pipeline engineering and structural engineering concealed within the decorative surface. Pipeline engineering includes electrical circuits, water supply and drainage, gas pipelines, air conditioning systems, etc.; structural engineering refers to the internal structures used for fixing and supporting the load of the house. Currently, quality issues with hidden projects are quite common [www.9yuzs.com], and due to the inconvenience of repairs, many potential hazards are left behind. Therefore, it is necessary to understand its structure and materials. The hidden projects in home decoration mainly include six aspects:

1. Water Supply and Drainage Engineering

Since galvanized pipes are prone to rusting, scaling, lack of insulation, and can crack from freezing, they will be gradually phased out. At present, the most commonly used ones are aluminum-plastic composite pipes, plastic steel pipes, PPR pipes. These pipes have good plasticity and toughness, and they do not scale, do not crack, and are insulated. They use special copper joints or heat-molded joints, ensuring quality and reducing energy consumption.

2. Electrical Pipeline Engineering

Generally, power lines are divided into hard wires, flexible wires, sheathed wires, etc., and according to the thickness of the copper core, there are 1 square millimeter wires, 1.5 square millimeter wires, 2.5 square millimeter wires... For safety reasons, and more importantly for easy maintenance, power lines should be run through conduits. In addition, audio cables and signal cables are also commonly used. Outlets are divided into 10A, 15A, etc., and there are various brands, with many counterfeit ones as well. Poor-quality outlets can easily cause accidents. 3. Floor Base Layer

There are two methods for solid wood flooring base layers: one is brushing a layer of cold primer oil on the cement floor, laying wooden beams, and finally nailing down the floorboards; another method involves first laying a layer of plywood or rough flooring on the beams before nailing down the floorboards. Using this method, the floor is evenly stressed and the structure is sturdy. Composite floors are easy to install, and their base layers also have two methods: one involves first making a leveling layer, then laying a PVC cushion layer [Nanjing Meishi Decoration Engineering Co., Ltd.], and then laying the composite floor; another involves laying wooden beams and rough flooring on the cement leveling layer before laying the composite floor.

4. Wall Protection Base Layer

Wall panels come in flat and embossed styles. The method involves first brushing a layer of cold primer oil on the wall, then installing 30mm x 40mm wooden beams, followed by nailing multi-layer boards or plywood [http://b1328550.xici.net]. Finally, nail down the decorative veneer board. To prevent mold, a series of 6mm diameter vent holes are opened at the top of the wall panel.

5. Door and Window Frame Base Layer

First, set up the wooden beams, then nail plywood or density board, and finish by nailing decorative veneer boards and wooden strips. Note that the density board should be pre-soaked in water to avoid future expansion.

6. Ceiling Base Layer [http://www.9yuzs.com]

Ceiling materials include gypsum boards, gray boards, plywood, aluminum alloy panels, plastic panels, frosted glass, painted glass, etc., and the beams can be wooden beams or light steel beams. Cracks in ceilings often appear; if elastic putty is used to fill the seams and nylon binding tape is applied, this situation can be improved.

After the hidden project is completed, it needs to be inspected to ensure no potential hazards are left behind, and only after passing inspection can subsequent construction proceed. Otherwise, if problems are discovered later, rework will be necessary, which will affect quality and delay the schedule. Both the owner and the contractor will suffer losses.
