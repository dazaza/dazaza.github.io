"High-quality renovation" standards need to be improved, "system bathrooms" should be promoted more widely

by zhibizhe on 2009-01-06 11:48:23

Some people say that no two industries are as closely related as the real estate market and the home furnishings industry.

Indeed, the real estate market and the home furnishings industry are two industries that coexist in prosperity. Since the second half of last year, after experiencing five years of boom, the volume of real estate transactions began to cool down and continued to shrink, and the first industry affected was the home furnishings industry. From all kinds of specialized building materials markets to the early integrated kitchen and bathroom markets, this year they have without exception felt the "sluggish market." According to insiders, compared with the same period last year, overall sales volumes have generally fallen by 30%~40%, some companies even as high as 50%, and many well-known companies have been in a loss-making state for several consecutive months.

Under the economic background of RMB appreciation, decline in international markets especially the American market, sharp increase in raw material prices, in addition to increases in labor costs, transportation costs, export tax rebate adjustments, and soaring oil prices, it is clear that relying solely on national macro-control is no longer the fundamental way to "save the market". How to better achieve a "soft landing" that all parties are more willing to accept? This is a problem that every related company needs to seriously consider, rather than like many developers today who loudly shout about price reductions while using "luxury decoration" as a marketing gimmick, playing their own calculations, and eventually becoming the target of public criticism in China's real estate discourse.

Luxury decoration is not a marketing tool; being people-oriented is the way out

On July 29, 2008, the Ministry of Housing and Urban-Rural Development issued again the "Notice on Further Strengthening the Management of Residential Decoration and Renovation" (Construction Quality [2008] No. 133) (hereinafter referred to as the "Notice"), pointing out that it is necessary to improve supporting policies and promote fully decorated housing. Thus, it can be seen that fully decorated housing as a development trend for future new commercial housing market has become inevitable.

Fully decorated housing represents far more than just a simply decorated unit space concept; it is a new living standard that is perfectly integrated from inside to outside, from visual to function to details. However, realizing this beautiful wish is filled with many regrets. It is understood that Shanghai, as the pilot city earliest approved by the country for "fully decorated housing", only a few developers' properties have gained recognition from buyers, while most others face the embarrassing situation of "all effort but no reward".

In response to this, experts point out that it is necessary to set a high standard for "fully decorated housing", meaning that the current "fully decorated housing" model urgently needs an upgrade. Imagine if the market were completely regulated, the quality of luxury decorated housing was guaranteed, prices were cheap, and personalized requirements were not lost. I think no consumer would be foolish enough to not choose a worry-free, effortless, and money-saving luxury decorated housing and instead choose a bare unit. By then, would the springtime for luxury decorated housing still be far away?

You Cha Shi driving the overall industrial upgrade

The key reason why the pace of residential industrialization in China has been hindered lies in the lack of "standards" for kitchens and bathrooms. Due to unreasonable domestic housing area standards, unresolved issues of modulus coordination and interface specifications, complete sets of kitchen and bathroom equipment cannot be configured, and since problems of standardization, series production, and integration of kitchen and bathroom equipment have not been solved, the real estate industry cannot effectively drive the industrialization development of furniture, home appliances, and hardware industries, resulting in very low levels of industrialization in kitchens and bathrooms currently. With the transformation of Chinese residents' housing from living type to comfort type, the market demand for fully decorated housing and environmental-friendly, energy-saving, integrated kitchen and bathroom products is rapidly expanding.

For this reason, the author visited You Cha Shi enterprise, which was among the first to systematize and standardize Chinese sanitary ware domestically. Indeed, You Cha Shi system sanitary ware surpasses the past in both specifications and usage experience. Each product is a perfect combination of functionality, design, and humanization, interpreting a new industrialized sanitary ware concept and achieving true "ready-to-use". Compared to traditional sanitary ware installation involving massive construction, the design of system sanitary ware embodies more industrialized concepts, originating from customer and industry demands, i.e., "what is the need?" is primarily focused on, facing the strict requirements of standardized, scaled, industrialized, modularized, and universalized components for fully decorated buildings. System sanitary ware fits well with this point and ensures the controllability of each process node.

More importantly, You Cha Shi's system sanitary ware introduces the latest Japanese technology, the latest fourth-generation sanitary ware products from the world-famous Chofu, creating a healthier, more environmentally friendly, energy-saving, and safer bathroom environment, enhancing the standardization and technological level of our country's bathrooms, thereby changing the primitive and extensive state of the housing industry and driving the overall industrial upgrade.

Creating a new image for China's sanitary ware manufacturing

Some people say that in the sanitary ware industry in 2008, the low-end battle has faded, this is an era of innovative breakthroughs and advancing into the high-end market. With the improvement of people's consumption levels, there is a diversified demand for sanitary ware consumption. High-end brands fill the residential areas, public places, office facilities, hotels, leisure areas, etc., of major cities. The competition in modern sanitary ware products will become increasingly fierce, and the large-scale application of various high-tech means and art culture has become an inevitable trend and selling point for sanitary ware products.

In the past twenty years, the domestic sanitary ware market has experienced a process from nothing to something, from disorder to scale. The continuously booming real estate industry and architectural decoration industry have also provided a strong foundation and guarantee for the growth of the sanitary ware market. Currently, China has become the largest producer and consumer of sanitary ware facilities in the world. China's sanitary ware accounts for 30% of the world total, and sanitary ware accessories almost account for 35% of the world total.

However, despite having such a large consumer market, there is still the problem of difficulty entering high-end domestic building decoration projects. Five-star hotels, as the main consumers of the domestic high-end market, mainly choose international sanitary ware brands over local products when purchasing for their decoration projects. This is a harsh reality that the domestic sanitary ware industry must face and ponder. Facts prove that "brand-name manufacturing" is not the fundamental way for ethnic enterprises to develop, and brands relying solely on imitation and low-price shocks to the market will disappear within a few years; how to wash away the stigma of "Made in China," turning it into a designated brand; how enterprises can improve product quality, meet environmental standards, introduce the latest technology, meet public demand, making "Made in China" as deeply ingrained as "Made in Japan" and "Made in Germany," allowing Chinese brands to become world-renowned brands, is a path that enterprises must take for development.

Author's Notes:

Like many people, I am very optimistic about the Chinese market. We go to any town or city in China, and we can see skyscrapers being built everywhere, while in developed countries in Europe and America, the number of newly built houses lags far behind China. It is understood that currently, China has become the largest producer and consumer of sanitary ware facilities in the world. Facing such a promising market prospect, if domestic enterprises can bravely try to change from "manufacturing" to "intelligent manufacturing," then the pattern of the past twenty years where only international big brands dominate the domestic sanitary ware market will be completely overturned by domestic brands.
