CPPCC member Zeng Haisheng: Singing red songs is to create an atmosphere and should be instilled from a young age

by abcdfdhehe on 2012-03-09 11:39:49

Zeng Haisheng, a member of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC), former political commissar of the Management and Security Department of the General Staff Department of the People's Liberation Army, and daughter of Zeng Shan, the former Minister of Internal Affairs of the State Council.

This year, Zeng Haisheng submitted a proposal titled "Proposal on the Establishment of the Ministry of Women and Children’s Work under the State Council," in which she argued for enhancing the status of women, such as allowing more women to enter the decision-making level.

Singing red songs is about creating an atmosphere, and should be instilled from a young age.

Beijing News: We just saw your rehearsal. Will you sing "Love My China" for International Women's Day? What do you think about singing red songs?

Zeng Haisheng: Red songs are just one aspect of upholding our fine traditions. If everyone sings them, it creates a social atmosphere. But I think that singing red songs alone is far from enough.

Beijing News: Then what other aspects should we focus on?

Zeng Haisheng: Singing red songs is one method. What I want to talk about is early childhood education.

Parents now only focus on how their children can succeed in this competitive society and survive. They start learning piano at a young age, attend tutoring classes, and don't want their children to lose at the starting line. However, they neglect the education that children should receive from a young age, what they should and shouldn't do, and what constitutes the qualities of truth, goodness, and beauty.

Our children, when their thoughts are not yet stable, have already been influenced by societal pursuits of profit. This is a problem during the educational period. For example, if children start singing red songs from a young age, they will be subtly influenced and educated in this way.

Proposal for the establishment of the Ministry of Women and Children’s Work

Beijing News: What area does your proposal this year focus on?

Zeng Haisheng: In recent years, I have paid more attention to the legal system for protecting the rights and interests of women and children, and I have found significant problems. There are laws regarding the protection of the rights and interests of women and children, but it is very difficult to enforce them in practice.

Beijing News: What is your experience?

Zeng Haisheng: (Pointing to the document in her hand) There are many vague expressions here about relevant units. These relevant units themselves are abstract. How should relevant departments implement these policies? The responsible parties are particularly unclear. Without clear enforcement authorities, there will be no one to enforce the law.

Secondly, there should be national-level detailed rules for implementing the law, and this matter should not be pushed onto each province. The Women's Federation is a coordinating body. Even if its members run around tirelessly advocating for women, their efforts cannot match the power of an administrative department's official seal.

This year, I submitted the proposal titled "Proposal on the Establishment of the Ministry of Women and Children’s Work under the State Council."

Early retirement for women leads to lower economic status

Beijing News: Do you think women are still a disadvantaged group?

Zeng Haisheng: Relatively speaking, women are still a disadvantaged group. There are several issues. First is political participation. The proportion of female representatives and committee members in the National People's Congress and CPPCC is relatively low. In the high-level decision-making layer, there are very few female leaders. Most others hold deputy positions. Not only are they deputies, but the work they handle mostly falls under labor, youth, women, health, and sports, not the main officials who make decisions on economic matters.

Last year, I proposed that there should be a woman entering the leadership at the 18th National Congress.

Beijing News: How do you think the situation of women in China is overall?

Zeng Haisheng: Women's economic status is still weak, without standing in property rights and family dominance.

The work arranged for female comrades is at a low level. Why should female comrades retire five years earlier than male comrades? Retiring five years earlier means that women's income will definitely lag behind, and lagging income leads to lower economic status. Economic status determines family status, social status, and political status. Can these statuses improve?
