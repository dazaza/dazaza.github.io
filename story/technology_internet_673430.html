Note to protect the perineum when surrogate mothers in Shanghai deliver babies

by woai2dd on 2012-02-23 16:13:16

Childbirth is an event that all pregnant women will go through, yet the process is full of unpredictability. Pregnant women must take necessary measures under a doctor's guidance to protect themselves and their babies.

The perineum needs protection during childbirth. During delivery, when the mother lies on the birthing bed in the lithotomy position (legs apart and placed on fixed leg supports), medical staff usually stand on her right side. As labor progresses, the baby's head entering the vagina can exert significant pressure on the perineum. If the perineum isn't protected at this point, the sudden emergence of the baby’s head could cause a perineal tear, which in severe cases may result in rectal injury. In the past, most women gave birth at home, and some experienced fecal incontinence post-delivery due to this reason.

When the baby’s head is about to be delivered, obstetric personnel use one hand to support the perineum while the other hand applies downward pressure on the baby’s head, allowing it to pass through the vagina along its smallest diameter. This causes the baby’s head to descend slowly, gradually expanding the vaginal walls. This technique is called perineal protection.

At the same time as providing perineal protection, obstetric personnel also guide the mother in her pushing efforts. It's important for the mother to cooperate actively without moving her hips arbitrarily. Good coordination between the mother and the midwife can reduce the occurrence of perineal tears and facilitate the smooth delivery of the baby.

After the baby and placenta are delivered, the midwife carefully checks for any perineal tears. First-degree and second-degree perineal tears can be sutured, with wounds healing quickly and having no lasting impact on the body. Pregnant women should not worry about this.
