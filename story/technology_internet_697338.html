Technological innovation comes in many forms.

by keyizhang1 on 2012-03-08 14:57:33

Technological innovation is a hierarchical economic activity with the basic characteristic of progressing from creative technical conception to the successful market realization of new products; that is to say, technological innovation is a complete process from the generation of ideas for new products or new processes to their market application. It includes a series of activities such as the generation of new ideas, research, development, commercial production, and the market sales and transfer diffusion of products. The forms in which technology is applied to economic activities are diverse, which determines that the forms of technological innovation are also diverse. Different periods, different enterprises, different technical fields, and different innovation projects have different innovation management models.

Therefore, technological innovation activities can be summarized into five forms:

- (Customer demand [http://baike.baidu.com/view/3428739.htm] or opening up new markets.)

- Process innovation: Enterprises improve or transform product production technology and processes through research and application of new production technologies, operation procedures, methods, thereby enhancing the enterprise's production technology level, product quality, and production efficiency. The process of enterprise process innovation can roughly be divided into two stages: the process R&D stage and the stage where process innovation is transferred or introduced from the R&D phase to the manufacturing phase.

- (Customer [http://baike.baidu.com/view/260012.htm] communication and interaction methods, grasp customer needs, sell [http://baike.baidu.com/view/9250.htm] products, thereby opening up new markets.)

- (Obtaining a new supply source for raw materials or semi-finished products;)

- (Organizational environment [http://baike.baidu.com/view/1338670.htm] and systems, implementing new enterprise organizational methods or management approaches to make the enterprise’s various activities more effective.)

- Innovation in marketing models. Technological innovation should keep pace with the times in terms of steps and content, and the overall idea of marketing is to discover the market and establish brand and cost advantages with an innovative mindset. Specific pathways include: internally through marketing innovation, striving to open up the domestic market, externally by increasing the knowledge and technical content of products and marketing methods, catching up with the international knowledge economy and green economy wave, actively participating in international marketing competition.

Therefore, innovation can just be technological innovation and does not necessarily mean product innovation. Technological innovation may lead to but does not necessarily result in product innovation, and product innovation may require but does not necessarily need technological innovation. The same technology can be used to produce different products, and the same product can be produced using different technologies. Product innovation focuses on commercial and design behaviors and has the characteristics of results, thus having a more external manifestation; technological innovation has the characteristics of a process and often manifests more internally. Product innovation may contain components of technological innovation, and may also contain components of commercial innovation and design innovation. Technological innovation may not bring about changes in products but merely reduce costs and improve efficiency, such as improving production processes, optimizing operational processes to reduce resource consumption, energy consumption, labor costs, or increase operational speed. On the other hand, the birth of new technologies often brings about entirely new products, and technology development often corresponds to or aims at product innovation; while new product concepts often require new technologies to be realized.
