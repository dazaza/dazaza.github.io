The outdoor fan of the Shinco air conditioner does not rotate, while the indoor unit is normal. Analysis and maintenance: First, check the motor windings of the fan. The resistance value is normal, and there is no mechanical friction or jamming, indicating that the fan itself is in good condition. The initial diagnosis suggests a fault in the outdoor fan control circuit. This control circuit consists of a bidirectional thyristor TR13 and an optocoupler Q16. The control signal output from the motherboard microprocessor causes the 10th pin of the integrated circuit U16 to output a low level, causing the internal LED of Q16 to emit light. The internal photosensitive thyristor of Q16 conducts under illumination, and the conduction current triggers the bidirectional thyristor TR13, causing it to conduct. The 220V AC voltage passes through 3P23.TR13.fan, at which point the fan operates normally. Changing the luminous intensity of the LED inside Q16 can change the conduction angle of TR13, thereby changing the speed of the fan to achieve the purpose of changing the airflow.

Measure the voltage on pin 1 of Q16 as 12V, which is normal. The 220V AC is also normal. Measure the voltage on pin 10 of U16, which is normal, indicating that the fault may be due to a problem with TR13 and Q16 or their peripheral components. Short-circuiting pins 3 and 4 of Q16 still results in the fan not rotating, leading to the conclusion that TR13 is damaged. After replacement, the fan operates normally. After removing the short-circuit wire from pins 3 and 4 of Q16, the fan stops again, indicating that Q16 is also damaged. Contact the Beijing Shinco Air Conditioner after-sales service phone number for further assistance. After replacing Q16, testing the machine shows that the fan returns to normal operation, resolving the fault.
