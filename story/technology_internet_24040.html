Overseas investment analysts: Don't fixate on Google and Yahoo

by officefurniture on 2007-07-04 16:29:36

According to foreign media reports, recently some analysts reminded investors not to focus only on Google and Yahoo. In fact, the performance of Chinese Internet stocks such as Baidu and Sina may be even better this year.

As Google's stock price is approaching $600, and Yahoo has drawn much attention from Wall Street due to the replacement of its CEO Terry Semel, other Internet stocks are easily overlooked.

However, in fact, Chinese Internet portal, search, and gaming stocks listed on NASDAQ have also performed exceptionally well this year, even surpassing Google and Yahoo.

For example, each of the stocks of Baidu, Sina, Sohu, 9 Youcheng, Shanda, and Ctrip has increased by more than 20% this year. In comparison, the increases for Yahoo and Google were 8% and 14%, respectively.

W.R. Hambrecht analyst James Lee believes that the two most worthwhile Chinese stocks to invest in are Sina and Baidu. Coincidentally, Pacific Crest Securities analyst Steve Weinstein also holds a positive view of these two stocks and has upgraded their stock ratings.

In addition to Baidu and Sina, Susquehanna Financial analyst C. Ming Zhao believes that the prospects of Shanda, a game provider, are also optimistic.

At the same time, since Sohu has also performed well in the gaming market and has recently launched "Demigods and Semi-Devils," James Lee believes that Sohu will also perform exceptionally well in the next few quarters.

However, due to the impact of China Mobile's new policies, James Lee advises investors to avoid service providers that heavily rely on wireless value-added services, such as TOM Online, K-net, Lingtong Net, and Huayou Century.

Overall, Chinese Internet stocks will continue to maintain a strong upward trend this year and are expected to reach a new level by 2008, mainly driven by the hosting of the Beijing Olympics in 2008.
