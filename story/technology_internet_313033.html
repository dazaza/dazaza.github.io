The first leader of Manchester United's defense has seen his value skyrocket by four times, while a Barcelona mainstay was merely his substitute last year.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-17 22:50:16

The leader of Manchester United's defense has quadrupled in value, while a Barcelona pillar was merely his substitute last year. According to Sina Sports, on his 110th league start, Vidic finally scored his 9th league goal for Manchester United. Back in January this year, Vidic had continuously scored in the league. After such a long period without scoring in the league, he finally ended the season with a strong finish. This goal may be symbolic; the Serbian’s position in Manchester United's backfield is undoubtedly the first among equals, unmatched by anyone.

Including cup matches, Vidic's last goal was against Inter Milan in the UEFA Champions League round of 16, where he powerfully headed the ball past Vieira's defense. Today against Wolves, a similar move reappeared, a commanding header that symbolized conquest. Despite an average angle, due to its immense force, even though Hanemann touched the ball, it was to no avail. This was Vidic's fifth shot on goal this season, and he finally succeeded. This goal once again confirmed the absolute advantage Serbia holds in aerial duels. In fact, before Rooney scored from the penalty spot, it was precisely because of his powerful impact that forced the opposing defender into a handball foul. Thus, in today's match, Vidic created two goals. Ferguson praised Vidic after the game, stating that he was the most effective player during set-piece attacks.

Due to minor injuries this season, Vidic has only played 16 games for Manchester United, missing 10. Combined with other defenders' injuries, which could be described as "waxing and waning," Ferguson has been making significant adjustments to the central defensive line. After Rio Ferdinand's long absence due to a back injury, any issue with Vidic would understandably severely affect the confidence of Manchester United's defensive line. Therefore, whenever Vidic plays, the defensive confidence soars. In the game, we can see that because of Vidic's presence, young Darron Gibson dares to make bold breakthroughs on the right flank, knowing that the space behind him is protected. Another central defender, Carrick, also plays effortlessly. Of course, this was not a particularly difficult match, allowing Vidic's leadership abilities to shine more prominently. Remember, he was the captain in the starting lineup for this game, and Ferguson's trust in him is fully expressed through that small armband.

Recently, there have been rumors of some friction between Vidic and the club. His agent stated that the club intends to raise Vidic's salary to £100,000 per week, but with the contract still having two and a half years left, the club will not take action so early. Even if it remains two years by summer, the club might still not act. Since the upper management insists on starting contract renewal only when entering the final year, there exists the risk of being poached by other major clubs. Although Vidic shows no visible dissatisfaction, it is hard for him to control his agent's actions. If Barcelona offers a sky-high contract, it is uncertain whether Vidic would resist temptation. Pique, who became an absolute starting center-back for Barcelona, was Vidic's primary substitute until last summer. A deal involving top talent would certainly interest Barcelona.

However, it seems that external noise aside, Vidic indeed values the present moment more. Recently, he expressed that he only cares about Manchester United and does not think about the World Cup, which is still over half a year away. He said: "I don't want to think too far ahead. Sometimes the only thing you care about is your injury. So the best way is to focus on the next game and keep playing, just like that. There is plenty of time before the World Cup, so currently, I am fully focused on playing for Manchester United." Such statements are what any Manchester United supporter would love to hear.

Before the match against Wolves, Vidic urged his teammates to secure three points. In an interview with the official website, he said: "This is our best chance to play beautifully, score a few goals, and bring back our confidence. When you lose a game, it is important to win the next one immediately. So you need to play better and correct the mistakes made in the previous game."

Clearly, he and his teammates achieved their goals. A player like him, who maintains a powerful aerial presence at both ends of the penalty area, as long as he stays healthy and fully commits himself without distraction, can easily overpower opponents with the prowess of last season's Premier League MVP.

When Vidic joined Manchester United, his purchase price was £7 million, already quite high for a central defender. However, after these few years of refinement, according to Transfermarkt's valuation, Vidic's current worth is around €35 million, or £31.15 million, which is more than four times higher after four years. These figures speak volumes. It is foreseeable that Manchester United will eventually meet his demand for a pay raise, placing him among the highest earners in the Premier League. This is determined by Vidic's superior strength and dominance on the field.
