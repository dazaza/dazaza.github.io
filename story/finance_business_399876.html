Some Thoughts on Using PHPWIND and Discuz Programs for Forums

by zmcool654 on 2011-04-01 09:45:51

When I first encountered Discuz, it was back in 2009. At that time, we were working on a classified advertising platform integrated with search functionality. The team suggested building a community forum to cultivate popularity for later marketing efforts. We went ahead and used Discuz version 7.0, eventually upgrading all the way to X1.5. Due to unfamiliarity with the approach and unclear concepts of community operation, one could tell from the initial upgrade speed of the forum that our community wasn't very successful.

Our current project still incorporates a community, but now it's done separately. The community plus vertical e-commerce develops independently and then undergoes horizontal integration in the middle to late stages. Although the test version of X2 has been released, according to my current thinking, I'd rather stick with X1.5 and develop it myself.

Below, I'll share some personal thoughts on whether to choose PHPWIND or Discuz for community building.

PHPWIND is now developing in the direction of Hualong Lane, while Discuz has gradually moved away from its previous imitation of 19th Floor in X1.5. I haven’t tested X2.0 yet, so I can't say too much. When using X1.5, I spent day and night testing bugs, reporting bugs, and ultimately submitted a stack of bug reports, but the site wasn't successfully built. I imagine this is also the state of many diligent webmasters who spend their time on forums testing, reporting, and submitting feedback. Just considering the framework program and sites built from it, both Discuz and PHPWIND have commendable attitudes. After all, 19th Floor and Hualong Lane are still difficult to surpass in China at this moment, as they lead in community operation. However, many webmasters may overlook that Hualong Lane initially switched from Discuz to PHPWIND.

Now, Discuz has slowly moved away from imitating 19th Floor and returned to focusing on its own feature development, while PHPWIND 8.5 has just been born out of Hualong Lane today, seemingly a step behind.

With the release of PHPWIND 8.5, many webmasters have focused their attention on PHPWIND because its imitation of Hualong Lane is quite polished, with excellent features and very successful execution. It also integrates some image modules, cartoon wallpapers, which I personally think are superfluous and could be added in PHP 8.8 or even further down the line, as few communities starting now can do a good job with that image module.

The backend of PHPWIND imitates Hualong Lane very well, whereas Discuz’s imitation of 19th Floor only goes halfway. The main reason might be that 19th Floor is simply too large, and for those just starting out, such a big program isn't necessary. Meanwhile, Hualong Lane hasn't strayed far from the original shadow of the forum, making it easier for webmasters to accept. This might also explain why Discuz has shifted its focus from imitation back to feature development.

Both DZ (Discuz) and PHPWIND programs are powerful, each with its own distinct style. Having used 19th Floor, then using DZ feels very comfortable; having used Hualong Lane, then using PHPWIND feels identical and familiar.

For a novice webmaster, PHPWIND might be the first choice.

1. Graphic design doesn't need to be overly complicated; you can directly use a set of programs, add some images, and start right away. Analysts have lowered Apple's performance expectations: iPhone 5 might be delayed;

2. Backend program: authentication, team management, sock puppets, etc., already integrate most plugins, saving a lot of trouble with plugin installation and secondary development, which is also where many webmasters' technical limitations lie;

These two points might make PHPWIND more important in webmasters' minds than DZ.

However, Discuz's functionality is no weaker than PHPWIND, though its interface design is slightly inferior (DZ always speaks through its functions). The rough frontend gives plenty of room for secondary development, allowing DZ users to create outstanding sites across various fields. For webmasters, there isn't much left to improve in PHPWIND.

Currently, Guangzhou No. 40 is still using the DZ X1.5 program but imitating the overall style of Hualong Lane. Many people might wonder why not switch to PHPWIND, as it would be more direct. The reason I chose DZ as the framework program for No. 40 is mainly because I feel that DZ offers more space for secondary development. Our overall strategy now is to combine the strengths of Hualong Lane and 19th Floor, and then optimize and improve them later.

No matter whether it's PHPWIND, Discuz, or any other program, each comes with many advantages and some shortcomings. But now, who can still tell if 19th Floor and Hualong Lane were developed based on PHPWIND or Discuz? Moreover, the success of community operations relies more on ideas and the market than on the program itself.
