The Current Status and Development Trend of Non-destructive Testing Technology for Aviation Equipment

Non-destructive testing (NDT) technology has played a crucial role in ensuring the safe and reliable operation of aviation equipment. Whether it is during the manufacturing and production process of aviation equipment or during the in-service inspection and daily maintenance and checks of various aircraft types, NDT has played an extremely important role. To achieve sustainable development of NDT technology, it is necessary to promote some new NDT concepts and new detection ideas. Green NDT marked by health monitoring, integrated testing and data fusion, as well as digitization, imaging and informatization, may be the future-worthy detection technology.

Although traditional manufacturing industries have faced huge challenges from IT industries in the past one or two decades, especially since entering the 21st century, the level of equipment manufacturing, particularly large-scale equipment manufacturing, remains the most important indicator of a country's industrial level [1]. As NDT technology closely related to the safety, quality, and reliability throughout the life cycle of equipment, its development level is also an important measure of a country's equipment manufacturing industry level. The modernization of economic development must be based on reliable modernization and safe modernization, where the former must be rooted in manufacturing, which is the foundation of a nation and also an important guarantee for achieving sustainable social development. The latter's important guarantee condition is that the core and key manufacturing technologies of major equipment vital to national economy and people's livelihood are in our own hands. This is particularly prominent in the aviation industry. Aviation manufacturing technology has been identified as one of the top ten key development industries in China’s future, making its safety and reliability even more important. Although overall levels lag far behind developed countries like the United States and Europe, the launch of commercial large aircraft projects and the establishment of the Zhuhai Aviation Industry City mark the imminent arrival of a rapid advancement era for China's aviation manufacturing. Correspondingly, over a relatively long period in the future, aviation NDT technology will inevitably experience a phase of rapid, stable, and sustained development.

Extending the safe service life of aviation equipment, including aircraft, is a trend in modern aviation equipment development. NDT or non-destructive evaluation (NDE) technology plays an extremely important role throughout the entire life cycle of aviation equipment. It is very significant for ensuring flight safety and extending the life of aircraft. With the adoption of damage tolerance theory replacing the previous safe-life design theory in aviation technology, the application perspective of NDT technology in aviation equipment has fundamentally changed. That is, NDT technology should be responsible for the entire life cycle of equipment (from cradle to grave). Not only should it be able to detect existing defects or cracks (in the traditional sense), but it should also be able to predict crack development patterns to ensure the correct implementation of damage tolerance theory.

NDT technology is a typical engineering application technology with low input and high output characteristics. It is difficult to find any other application discipline branch that encompasses such extensive technical knowledge, covers so many basic research fields, and involves such wide-ranging application areas compared to NDT [2]. In October 1982, then U.S. President Reagan said in a congratulatory telegram to the American Society for Nondestructive Testing (ASNT) on its 20th anniversary, (NDT) can bring a higher degree of reliability to aircraft and spacecraft, power plants, ships, cars, and buildings, etc., and without NDT (the U.S.) would not enjoy the current leading position in many fields such as aircraft, ships, and cars and other areas [3]. As a highly practical technology, only by combining with large national projects and solving urgent safety assurance issues in large and key national projects can NDT technology find its place and broad development space.

The scope of NDT for aviation equipment is very broad, including the testing of aviation materials, the testing of the manufacturing process of aviation structural components, and the testing of in-service equipment (aircraft). Although all belong to NDT, there are significant differences in their testing priorities and covered fields. This article focuses on the testing of in-service equipment (or field testing), which may be the most challenging research and development area.

The main characteristics of NDT work for aviation equipment are [3]: (1) Complex structures, in most cases automatic equipment cannot play too much of a role outside of the production line. (2) Wide range of materials, including aluminum alloys, titanium alloys, high-strength steel, composite materials, etc., and different materials require different testing methods. For example, composite materials have special fault phenomena, the most threatening defects being delamination, matrix cracking, and fiber breakage. At the same time, due to acoustic anisotropy and a particularly large attenuation coefficient of sound waves, conventional ultrasonic testing faces difficulties. Titanium alloy is a non-magnetic material but belongs to a coarse-grained structure, presenting new issues for ultrasonic and eddy current testing. (3) Complex objects. In many cases, whether for old or new aircraft, the accessibility for testing is poor, especially for parts like blades, compressor discs, and turbine discs, which pose great difficulty for in-service testing. (4) Pre-research on specific locations, areas, and possible defect detection methods for leading flight aircraft is needed. Due to the lack of relevant data, this work often requires much pre-research based on existing testing experience, thus carrying greater risks.

The status quo of NDT work for aviation equipment in China: The aviation sector has always been the most common and active department in conducting theoretical and applied research on NDT in China. The principle that NDT work for aviation equipment should follow is to find suitable and effective methods rather than blindly pursuing high-end, sophisticated, and cutting-edge paths; to use "appropriate" methods rather than blindly pursue "expensive" ones! For example, in civil aviation, visual inspection (including instrument-assisted visual inspection) may account for more than 70% of NDT work, while visual plus conventional methods may account for over 90% [4]. Of course, due to the particularity of aviation equipment and the demand for high safety and reliability, the latest and most advanced testing methods are often first applied in the aviation sector (structural health monitoring, pulse eddy current, laser and infrared imaging technologies), but these are demands proposed by special locations or occasions in actual testing, not for the sake of applying new technologies.

Overall, China's NDT in the aviation sector ranks among the world's advanced levels, and its researchers and technicians can independently solve and overcome various complex technical problems facing the aviation sector. Friction welding is a new solid-phase welding technology with important applications in aviation manufacturing. Due to defects in the weld zone being tightly adjacent, fine, and the metallurgical structure of the weld zone being close to that of the base material, conventional X-ray or ultrasonic testing methods targeting fusion welds are difficult to apply to friction welding inspections. Liu Songping et al. used comprehensive information generated by incident sound waves in the weld zone to identify and recognize weld defects, achieving good inspection results [5]. Based on conventional TOFD technology, they proposed using L-TOFD and P-scan technologies to comprehensively inspect the quality of friction stir welding, which can replace traditional X-ray inspection methods and overcome the difficulty of detecting longitudinal cracks in welds, playing a role in ensuring the quality of friction stir welding. Liu Songping et al. also conducted innovative research on the detection of composite material defects, developing the CUS-6000 high-efficiency ultrasonic automated scanning imaging detection equipment for composite materials, which has reached an industrial application level and can directly serve composite material manufacturing. The ultrasonic microscopic imaging technology developed by the research group can accurately determine the defect conditions of each composite material layer, which will play an important role in improving the manufacturing quality of composite materials in the future.

Guo Guangping et al. have conducted in-depth research on laser misalignment speckle interferometry technology over a long period. Weak debonding defects in C-layer radar radomes during the manufacturing process are difficult to detect using conventional ultrasonic C-scan technology. They developed a vacuum-loaded laser speckle interferometry system that can implement on-site detection of tightly adhered weak debonding defects, which can be used for debonding detection between the outer skin and honeycomb structure of the radome, as well as between the middle skin and the upper side honeycomb structure, with detection capabilities reaching industrial application levels [6].

Geng Rongsheng et al. tracked the damage development situation of two types of third-generation aircraft during full-scale fatigue tests over eight years from 2004 to 2011. They established a comprehensive crack monitoring technology centered on acoustic emission (AE) technology for the first time, ensuring the smooth progress of the tests and increasing the lifespan of both aircraft models by more than 50% [7]. Their research group developed a complete set of AE signal processing and forecasting methods, adopting trend analysis techniques based on statistical analysis principles, filtering techniques based on time, space, amplitude, and energy, signal processing methods based on the distribution characteristics of AE signal amplitudes, and multi-parameter comprehensive recognition technology to obtain AE characteristics of fatigue crack initiation and propagation. They developed a unique method of decomposing monitored AE signals into different time series and then using the trend changes of correlation features of statistical average parameters to implement monitoring, achieving significant effects. Taking the monitoring of a certain model aircraft from 2004 to 2008 as an example, during the entire test process, they successfully forecasted and discovered 38 critical cracks and more than 100 general cracks with almost no false alarms, which was a miracle in the history of crack monitoring for full-scale aircraft fatigue tests. Due to the implementation of full-process monitoring, detection (and targeted durability repairs), the flight life increase of the aircraft fleet reached 50%. The above methods were also successfully applied to the full-scale fatigue test of another aircraft type after 2009. This research has enormous political, military, and social significance and created economic benefits measured in hundreds of billions. Future Directions and Areas to Emphasize in NDT Technology for Aviation Equipment:

The author has discussed multiple times about the future direction of China's NDT technology development, proposing to rebuild the Chinese NDT market at a higher platform, while vigorously developing intelligent, automated, and image-based testing devices, actively expanding new application areas. This article explores issues closely related to aviation equipment.

1. Vigorously Develop Structural Health Monitoring (SHM) Technology:

Structural health monitoring involves implementing a series of monitoring measures and evaluating the (health) state of structures. When early damage or fatigue crack initiation occurs, it allows for restorative measures on the structure, avoiding irreparable destruction. Just as the best way to "treat" is through prevention and early diagnosis to "prevent potential disasters," for aviation equipment, health monitoring is the best path to effectively improve equipment reliability and extend its service life. Besides the previously mentioned AE monitoring, which is somewhat a form of structural health monitoring, many "effective" methods have already been developed, such as smart sensor technology, smart material technology, fiber optic sensors, and strain monitoring technology. Since individual methods always have limitations, the combined use of multiple methods might be the most effective health monitoring approach. The essence of health monitoring is to make a comprehensive evaluation of the health state. On this level, currently, no single technology is truly meaningful health monitoring; most fall within the scope of damage detection (able to discover early damage or early cracks). Some "pre-embedded" or preset sensors can implement continuous detection. Regardless of the technology used (note: no technology is omnipotent), understanding the structure itself and engineering application experience are crucial.

2. Promote the Development of Green NDT Technology:

The development of NDT technology must align with the overall思路of China's industrial development. Green manufacturing, i.e., producing environmentally friendly mechanical equipment using energy-saving and emission reduction technologies, is undoubtedly the direction of development for the machinery manufacturing industry [6]. Future NDT equipment should also be green, i.e., environmentally friendly. Therefore, some traditional methods that could pollute the environment will gradually be eliminated or replaced by new methods and new detection media. For example, magnetic particle inspection might be replaced by flux leakage detection as the latter's sensitivity improves and it becomes easier to implement intelligent and visual detection in many scenarios. Another phenomenon that has already occurred and is accelerating is that digital radiographic inspection will eventually replace traditional film inspection methods. With the rapid development of new digital image plates and CR technology utilizing fluorescent storage in recent years, the imaging effect of digital radiography is now comparable to traditional films. Therefore, given its environmental friendliness and the ability to store, reuse, and transmit data over long distances, replacing traditional film radiographic inspection technology is just around the corner. Additionally, with advancements in ultrasonic inspection technology, such as the strengthening role of TOFD technology in weld inspection and the establishment of standards, (in welds) using ultrasonic TOFD to replace or partially replace radiographic inspection has become possible, aligning with the concept of green inspection. The aviation equipment NDT sector needs to emphasize the promotion and application of these new technologies, which is a daunting task.

3. Intelligent NDT and Informative NDT Devices:

A current hot topic in manufacturing is information manufacturing and intelligent manufacturing, where mechanical equipment systems can perform intelligent activities such as analysis, reasoning, judgment, conception, and decision-making. By integrating already developed chip sensor technology with microelectronics and high-performance computers, producing integrated (comprehensive), information system-functional, and intelligent inspection equipment should be feasible. A direct result of intelligent NDT and informative NDT is making flaw detection (NDT) "easier." In fact, as early as the 1990s, the Air Force made beneficial explorations in this area. Major flaw detection processes in the field were stored in digital ultrasonic and eddy current flaw detection equipment, greatly simplifying the operation steps for field personnel and enhancing the reliability of field flaw detection. The KK-30 intelligent ultrasonic flaw detector and SMART-97 intelligent eddy current flaw detector, which solidified the primary field flaw detection processes for major aircraft types, have prototypes of intelligent instruments, though their intelligence level remains low due to the lack of visualized detection results and automatic recognition functions.

Currently, image processing and automatic image recognition technology are rapidly developing, some of which are quite mature. Collaborating with researchers in this field could lead to the development of highly intelligent NDT equipment.

4. Integration and Data Fusion Technology:

Each NDT method has its applicable range, making it difficult to fully evaluate the inspected object. To enhance inspection reliability and efficiency, reduce costs, and achieve complete inspection, NDT integration technology containing multiple inspection methods can be adopted. However, results obtained from different inspection methods should not be isolated but organically linked and weighted comprehensively to evaluate the inspected object, meaning data (results) need to be fused. Thus, a basic requirement for integrated inspection instruments is to provide an effective data fusion platform rather than simply combining different inspection functions. At the Global Chinese Non-Destructive Testing Summit Forum in 2011, Lin Junming proposed the concept of cloud detection, attempting to integrate cloud computing into NDT technology [8]. This idea could disrupt traditional NDT design concepts, although it is still in the exploration stage. However, with the rapid development of so-called "cloud technology," its impact on the development of NDT deserves special attention.

Similar to integrated instruments is specialized inspection equipment tailored to specific objects, akin to "custom tailoring" and "demand customization" problem-solving methods, which is a promising development direction for aviation equipment inspection. This might be particularly needed in the field, where the Air Force has relatively rich experience. For instance, when inspecting the groove of the left blade on the first-stage disk of a certain engine's low-pressure compressor, a dual-crystal probe was used instead of a receive-transmit dual-probe in TOFD, effectively solving the crack inspection problem of the left blade groove on the first-stage disk. Additionally, in detecting cracks on the back of the second-stage turbine blades of a certain engine type, the Air Force's relevant research units developed dedicated eddy current probes and distributed them to the troops, significantly ensuring the safety of that engine type [9].

5. Application of Phased Array Technology in Crack Detection of Complex Components Such as Turbine Blades, Turbine Disks, and Compressor Disks:

Due to the convenient adjustment of focus position and beam direction (deflection angle) by circuits, phased array technology might first be applied in crack detection of complex components such as turbine blades, turbine disks, toothed disks, and compressor disks in aviation equipment. This requires some experimental research based on the geometric dimensions of specific inspection objects and the formulation of detailed inspection plans.

6. Application of New Technologies Such as Infrared and Laser Speckle Interferometry:

Composite materials are more widely used in new aircraft, especially fourth and fifth generation fighters (including improved third-generation fighters) and large commercial aircraft. Miniaturized infrared thermography and laser speckle interferometry inspection instruments can quickly and extensively detect major defects such as delamination and debonding in composite materials, so it can be foreseen that these instruments will have good application prospects in the field in the future.

Overview of the Chinese Society for Non-Destructive Testing (ChSNDT) and Domestic NDT Situation:

Founded in 1978, the ChSNDT is an important branch of the Chinese Mechanical Engineering Society (CMES) and accepts CMES leadership. However, in external exchanges, it is an independent society, known internationally as the Chinese Society for Non-Destructive Testing (ChSNDT). ChSNDT is one of the few oldest, most active, and largest NDT societies in the world in terms of membership.

Over 33 years, despite nine national conferences and personnel changes, ChSNDT has consistently aimed to unite NDT personnel nationwide, promote academic exchanges, enhance NDT technical levels, improve product quality, ensure safe and reliable equipment operation, encourage international cooperation and exchange, and contribute to the development and progress of China's NDT cause and international cooperation [4].

ChSNDT has seven professional committees (ultrasound, electromagnetic eddy current, radiation, acoustic emission, magnetic particle and penetrant, new technology, stress testing), five working committees (academic, education training and popular science, personnel qualification certification, in-service equipment detection, editing and publishing), and an NDT magazine editorial committee. The current ninth committee consists of 99 members [2].

In October 2008, ChSNDT held the 17th World Conference on Non-Destructive Testing (WCNDT) in Shanghai, which was unprecedented in scale, number of participants, and exhibitors. The conference aimed to bring (Chinese) NDT to the world and let the world understand China's (NDT). The successful hosting of this grand event marked the recognition of the progress and development of mainland China's NDT technology level by the world, as well as the high status and credibility of China's NDT community in the world.

Taking advantage of the opportunity provided by hosting the 17th WCNDT and the worldwide recognition of China's NDT technology level, ChSNDT expedited the mutual recognition of NDT personnel qualifications with the European Federation for Non-Destructive Testing (EFNDT). Through persistent efforts, this goal was ultimately achieved. In April 2009, the EU sent two evaluators to assess the qualification certification system of the NDT Society. In September 2009, at the first working committee meeting of the EU in Brussels, it was unanimously approved: the certification system of personnel from the Chinese NDT Society joined the mutual recognition system, officially approved and awarded certificates on September 15 of the same year. Mutual recognition with the EU represents an important development in the certification work of the Society's personnel, possibly a milestone event [2].

ChSNDT has hosted several large international NDT conferences. Besides the aforementioned 17th WCNDT, in August 2011, the first World Acoustic Emission Conference (WCAE2011, Beijing) was held in Beijing, and in November, the Global Chinese NDT Summit Forum was successfully organized.

The overall level of China's NDT technology aligns with the country's economic development level, and its position in the world NDT field is consistent with China's economic and industrial levels, especially the status of the equipment manufacturing industry in the world. Data shows that after 2008, China, as a world-leading machinery manufacturing power and the second-largest exporter of machinery, electrical, and transportation equipment, has firmly established itself among the world's machinery manufacturing powers [1]. Although NDT technology itself is not a direct productive force in some ways, its technical level reflects a country's industrial level and is closely related to the country's economic development trend. Corresponding to the world machinery manufacturing power, China is a major NDT country, with a vibrant and attractive NDT market, recognized by the global NDT community. Simply based on the number of NDT practitioners, data indicates that China currently has around 250,000 people directly engaged in or related to NDT-related technical work, involving approximately 5,000 organizations applying, researching, teaching, training, dealing, providing technical consulting services, and offering engineering services related to NDT technology, which is unmatched by any country. There are about 2,000 inspection and testing institutions in China engaged in NDT services, more than 20,000 mechanical manufacturing and installation enterprises with their own NDT teams, and possibly nearly 500 manufacturers of NDT equipment and materials. In aspects of ultrasound, eddy current, magnetic particle, and conventional radiographic inspection instruments, a number of scaled-up production enterprises or groups have emerged, and some frontier instruments (such as phased arrays, specialized ultrasonic TOFD instruments, array eddy current) have reached marketable production scales.

China's NDT technology in engineering applications is at an internationally advanced or even leading level. Benefiting from the rapid economic development and the widespread implementation of large-scale engineering construction projects across various sectors of national economy and people's livelihood, China has conducted many pioneering studies, applications, and technological developments in the field of NDT engineering applications. Significant achievements have been made in nuclear power, railways, especially high-speed railways, special equipment (pipelines, pressure vessels, amusement facilities, etc.), oil pipelines, natural gas pipelines, rock stability monitoring of mountains and dams, and fatigue damage monitoring of aircraft. Currently, China's engineering and technical personnel can basically independently solve various routine NDT and technical challenges encountered in various large-scale engineering projects. It can be said that there are almost no major industrial sectors where NDT technology remains a blank slate. Meanwhile, virtually all existing NDT methods have been applied or studied in China.
