Teaching you how to buy a virtual host without being deceived

by xwkj0983 on 2012-02-21 14:12:25

Virtualization has created tremendous flexibility for network architects in server deployment and saved costs. At the same time, this flexibility also creates opportunities to mask illegal activities and may obstruct or delay law enforcement (Lawful Interception refers to meeting the government's need to monitor router data) and prosecution because virtual hosts can move across geographic locations or legal jurisdictions.

By using appropriate tools and relevant knowledge, enterprises can address the challenges of lawful interception and compliance in a converged network environment caused by virtualization and take measures to prevent "jurisdictional overreach."

Protecting virtual infrastructure requires innovative strategies and solutions, ensuring that they support service providers and law enforcement agencies in fulfilling their respective duties.

Virtualization and cloud infrastructure bring monitoring challenges

Law enforcement agencies and service providers are under immense pressure to stop terrorists and other malicious intruders who often possess advanced technical skills and abundant resources. For example, in the past, it was relatively easy to identify point-to-point applications or dialog boxes using known port numbers, but things are not as simple now. Traffic patterns have changed, and most applications now use standard HTTP and SSL for communication, which is undoubtedly a huge burden on lawful interception systems. They need to identify more targets from massive amounts of data with very few filtering options.

Social networks undoubtedly add to the problem, allowing criminals to exploit various encrypted communication channels for criminal activities.

Effective legal interception technology must be able to collect traffic and process data at unprecedented speeds and with high precision.

Resisting multiple threats and protecting networks

According to legal requirements, service providers must provide necessary equipment to meet the unique needs of high-risk lawful interception tasks. Solutions should map to lawful interception standards and be able to isolate suspicious voice, video, or data streams based on interception criteria such as IP addresses, MAC addresses, or other parameters. To achieve this goal, virtual hosts and lawful interception require:

The ability to intercept all communications of a target, including discarded packets, as lost encrypted packets may lead to unreadable or incomplete information;

All network traffic of any endpoint in communication traffic must be visible - there can be no blind spots;

Sufficient processing speed matching the network bandwidth;

Undetectability, unobservability, and lack of performance degradation (red warning flags when intercepting criminals and terrorists);

Real-time monitoring capability, as time is a fundamental element in preventing crime or attacks and collecting evidence;

The ability to provide intercepted information to authorities as evidence;

Load sharing and load balancing techniques for lawful interception system traffic;

Moreover, network operators and service providers have their own needs, including cost-effectiveness, minimizing impact on the network, compatibility with prevailing technologies, and scalability.
