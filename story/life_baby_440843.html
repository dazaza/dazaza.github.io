Poker card puzzle game

by meier on 2011-06-01 22:55:14

Card puzzle games can enrich children's imagination, cultivate their creative thinking ability, exercise the flexibility of babies' fingers and hand muscles, develop hand-eye coordination, understand numbers and colors, and also allow them to enjoy the happiness brought by success.

Basic gameplay: Mom and baby work together to form various patterns with playing cards.

1. Enrich children's imagination

Arrange some patterns with cards, which can be neatly arranged into a long row, square, rectangle, triangle, or even simple cartoon patterns that babies like. Then, let the baby guess what the pattern you've created looks like. Is it a big snake! A table! A long bench! When the baby has guessed almost everything, you can then tell him which is the straight line, which is the rectangle, and which is the square.

During the game, there's no need to directly teach the baby the names of the patterns, as this may limit his imagination and hinder the cultivation of his independent thinking ability. You can let the child guess what it looks like?

2. Cultivate children's creative thinking ability

For older children, you can let her use her own creative thinking to create patterns herself, enjoying the joy of creation.

3. Sensory understanding of numbers and geometric shapes

This game not only enriches the baby's imagination, but also teaches the baby the names of several basic shapes, while at the same time stimulating his desire to create.

In the process of arranging the puzzles, the child may unconsciously notice the patterns and number names on the playing cards, such as four red hearts and the number 4. In fact, continuously receiving this information is also a sensory understanding of numerical relationships.

4. Development of hand-eye coordination and fine motor skills

Although arranging playing cards is a piece of cake for adults, for younger children, how to arrange them neatly and beautifully is quite a challenge because in this process, the child needs to complete the task with hand-eye coordination, which is beneficial for the development of fine motor skills.

5. Classification ability

You can extend the game further by letting the child arrange playing cards of the same color and pattern. To accomplish this task, the child also needs to first classify the playing cards, which is another process of classification by color and shape.

6. Enhance children's self-confidence

Enhance children's self-confidence: What mom can do, I can do too!

Playing games is the nature of children, and games have great attraction and educational significance for young children. During the process of playing card puzzle games, you can increase the fun atmosphere through comparing wins and losses, speed, and grabbing red flowers, making them feel endless fun, play easily, learn happily, satisfy their competitive spirit, and enjoy the joy of success.
