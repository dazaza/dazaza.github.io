The Irrevocable Path of Power and Lust: A History of Desires is Actually a Social History —— [French] Balzac The paths of the world, one follows you. Power lies in all the signs on the earth.

The summer of 1986, compared with the summers of previous years, in fact, did not have much difference. But for Deng Yiqun, a student in the Mechanical Power Department of Southern University, it had an extraordinary significance. It was the year that decided his fate, and also the year that determined the direction of his life from then on.

He was about to graduate, but he still didn't have a clear idea about the future. He was at another very important turning point in his life. During the four years he studied in this relatively famous university in the south, many changes were happening in the outside world. These changes might not seem obvious, especially as the ideological and theoretical circles were still continuously debating. However, it was precisely this lack of obviousness that determined its variability and unpredictability. The whole Chinese society was carefully exploring forward as the national door gradually opened. Everything was unpredictable, and no one knew what it would be like in the future. New and old, good and bad, conservative backwardness and advanced progressiveness, etc., all kinds of contradictions intertwined, conflicted, and collided. Various forces were confronting each other, openly or secretly struggling. And this huge society moved forward in the conflicts and confrontations of various contradictions (although somewhat slowly, but indeed moving forward). What Deng Yiqun felt was, on the one hand, the tranquility and peace in the campus, and on the other hand, the multiple changes outside (whether in social economy or political life). These were two aspects of a completely different society full of huge contradictions and disparities, just like the two sides of a coin. All these things made it quite difficult for young students like Deng Yiqun to step into and adapt to immediately. It was severe for him (them).

Four years ago, when Deng Yiqun entered this university, he already knew how significant a change had occurred in his destiny: he obtained the pass to another class (cadre class) in life. From then on, he embarked on a bright and smooth path. He had gained the power (right) to bid farewell to his past background. He was an outstanding individual from a poor and backward countryside. As the elders in the village said, he had become successful. He had leaped over the dragon gate. He had transformed from a farmer's child into a state cadre with higher education. However, he didn't expect that he had only obtained the first pass in life. There were still many things ahead of him. Now, as he was about to graduate, the road before him made him feel the sadness of his class origin again.

That summer completely changed Deng Yiqun's life trajectory, which he himself had never expected beforehand. In that summer, unlike other classmates who hurried back after getting their assignment certificates, he did not have the joy of stepping into society. On the contrary, his heart was filled with sorrow. He thought that the result waiting for him would only be one, so he preferred to linger longer in the campus. Four years of school life made him develop a lot of aversion, but at the moment of leaving, he developed a kind of attachment - the simple student life was about to end, and the dream of being a so-called elite was over. Although it was rigid, it was beautiful. This attachment should have appeared a few years after graduation, but he developed it earlier.

He believed that this was not a good attachment.

In that summer's campus, Deng Yiqun was full of worries, feeling very uncomfortable. He couldn't be happy. Compared to his wishes, he felt a strong sense of loss inside.

Deng Yiqun had always been a diligent and good student. During four years of university life, he served as the school's publicity cadre and a special correspondent for the school newspaper; in civilian terms, he was the deputy secretary-general of the alumni association. His performance was positively recognized by some teachers, especially his homeroom teacher, who saw his sincerity and actively recommended him. The school even considered letting him stay in the future. But as the graduation day approached, the dream was shattered - there were very few spots for staying, and they were already predetermined, and his name was not on the list.

He would face being assigned back to the county where his hometown was located. Of course, he didn't mind being sent back to his hometown, as long as he could get an ideal position, which would also be fine. He thought that based on his performance, entering the government agency should not be a problem. Perhaps because the school initially had the idea of letting him stay, his evaluation was written very well. However, the result turned out to be greatly different from his wishes, and he felt as if someone had suddenly poured a bucket of cold water over him.

In the meeting held by the county personnel bureau in April, he had been preliminarily determined to be assigned to the agricultural machinery factory in the county. There were more than sixty or seventy graduates from the county attending that meeting, most of whom were from the city and graduated from the county high school. Men and women, full of spirit. He was very unremarkable among that group of people. After four years of university life, he still looked like a student from a rural area, wearing blue student clothes, with a dark and thin face, and a pair of cheap old-fashioned black-rimmed glasses. His character was honest and obedient, even a bit timid - showing respect and awe for everything powerful and authoritative. This was inherited from his parents.

Deng Yiqun's major could be said to be absolutely professional - metal mechanical heat treatment. But under the current situation of rough social division of labor, such a major seemed insignificant. Graduates of veterinary medicine majors could also engage in secretarial work or archives management. Heat treatment majors could also become tax or procuratorial cadres. When he first took the college entrance exam, he didn't seriously consider the issue of majors. As long as he could get admitted, even if it was a secondary vocational school, he would still like it. Therefore, when filling out the application form, he just followed the teacher's advice. The teacher's words were always right. Once admitted to the school, he wouldn't have to be a farmer like his brothers and sisters anymore. He could register for urban household registration and eat grain supplied by the state.

At that meeting, many graduates were intended to enter the county party committee, government, and various directly affiliated bureaus, and very few would go to enterprises. In fact, some would be assigned to the city. College students in the mid-1980s were still quite scarce. Deng Yiqun also wanted to enter the government agency. There was an agricultural machinery bureau in the county, and Deng Yiqun thought that if he couldn't enter the county government or the party committee office, entering the government-affiliated machinery bureau would be great too. However, at the meeting, the fat director surnamed Zhu kindly told him that there was a machinery factory below that lacked talents like him (he could choose between the first or second machinery factory), and that young people like him should go down to exercise first. They would surely achieve great things in the future. The false warmth in those words made him feel particularly disheartened. He knew that the essence of the matter did not depend on whether he was a "talent", but rather on the fact that he had no backing or connections.

His family members did not have high expectations for his assignment. They were happy that he could be assigned to work in the county. The villagers looked at him with envious eyes. In that village, it had been decades since they produced a university student like him. The Dungs had been farmers for generations, and now finally had someone who ate the state-supplied grain. How could the family not feel proud? Deng Yiqun felt the estrangement between himself and his family. They would never understand. They would never understand his troubles because they were farmers and had not received good cultural education. "Ignorance starts with literacy," but they did not have this kind of confusion. This was the difference and distance between them, he thought.

On the night he returned to the village, the whole family gathered around the round table at his brother's house, facing an oil lamp, everyone discussed the issue of Deng Yiqun returning home (except for himself). They hoped he would return because he was the ace of the whole family. He was the honor and pride of the family. He was the strong spiritual pillar of the whole family.

Although the knowledge and experience of Deng Yiqun as a young student at the time were limited, he still strongly felt the huge contrast between his hometown (after four years of university life, he had called this place his hometown in his heart, rather than his hometown) and the outside world. This place was almost the poorest area in the northern part of Jiangsu Province, remote, backward, and the appearance remained unchanged for decades. The local people were confused and ignorant, unaware of the vastness of the sky and the thickness of the earth. Sometimes they were quite inferior, sometimes they were arrogant. Young Deng Yiqun did not want to come back to this place. If he could be assigned to the county government agency, he would be very happy. But being assigned to a small enterprise, he emotionally could not accept it. Since others could be assigned to the agency, why couldn't he? Although he had no backing, he believed that he was essentially the same as other classmates, and he couldn't help comparing.

The village was very quiet, not even a dog bark could be heard. A moon hung silently and coldly in the sky outside the house. Inside the house, however, it was a different atmosphere, lively and warm. His graduation coming back made the family feel a little prouder.

"I saw the village chief at noon. He drank wine, his face red like a smoked pig's butt. In the past, his arrogance was like heaven, but this time I don't know what nerve touched him. He nodded and smiled at me, but I ignored him." said elder sister Deng Yumei.

"After third brother came back, the big families in the village dare not bully us so arrogantly anymore," said the younger sister happily.

Sister-in-law Liu Zhengju said, "The eldest daughter of the village chief's family, Zhou Xiaohong, is now a primary school teacher in the village." She hoped deep in her heart that the only promising younger uncle in the family would be assigned back, even if assigned to the township, it would be better to marry a village cadre's daughter. That way, their family would have a good support. Elder brother Deng Yibin snorted and said, "What kind of primary school teacher, just a substitute teacher." Liu Zhengju was不服气 (unwilling to concede), and said, "What's wrong with being a substitute teacher? Substitute teaching can eventually lead to becoming a permanent teacher. My father-in-law's second cousin's son used to be a substitute teacher, now he has become a permanent teacher, earning hundreds of yuan a month." Elder brother said, "Can the village chief be a chief forever?"
