Unknown Camphor Land

by lk0swdd on 2009-07-11 19:07:25

I have finally started to worry about my future. I think that if I keep living such a muddled life, I will definitely end up as one of those women in cheongsams on the street, whether I want to or not. Although both janitors and presidents are laborers, I believe that if it wasn't for making a living, most people wouldn't choose to be janitors, and if given the choice, everyone would unanimously choose to be the president. This is as simple and certain as the fact that no matter whether 1+1 equals pears, apples, bananas, or oranges, the final answer is always 2.

All along, I've been praised by teachers for my good essays, but my writing is so similar to Xiao Si's. In other words, I blindly pursue imitating Si's style. I begin to feel like a failure. No matter what, I live under Si's shadow, and my brilliance can never shine on me. Thus, I start to believe that liberal arts students are destined to become beggars.

My long-held dream has always been to become a writer - those people who seem dignified but are hypocrites underneath. But I suddenly thought, what if no one reads my books? My idol is Si, so I began to be filled with countless sinking sorrows. But then, I realized I might starve. This thought startled me. So, I started to change my style. But look at the texts after I changed my style. What kind of text is that? Even I don't like it. Who would want it?

I constantly start to hesitate, start to feel sad, and start doing things that even scare myself. I'm a bad kid, I suddenly thought. This notion shocked me because I've always stubbornly believed I was a good child, regardless of whether I actually am or not. Hence, I continued to write, but my new style of writing is something even I don't want to see. I couldn't help but sneer several times. This is truly an infuriating matter. I start to endlessly ponder, what should I do three years from now? The answer, however, is laughably non-existent—no answer. So I started to sing ceaselessly until the end of time and the exhaustion of the seas.

I really like the sound when my fingertips touch the keyboard because that sound is akin to celestial music. My fingers and my keyboard work together harmoniously, producing a cacophony of sounds. But once I think about the day when my fingers wither, and I can no longer write any words, and all my sources of thought are cut off by the world, I start to feel deeply sorrowful. This sorrow extends endlessly, causing me to waver again. I wonder, could my science scores improve just a little bit more? Then I would need to study hard and strive upward every day, right? But I don't want to study hard and strive upward every day. My words slowly appear on the screen, making me feel happiness descending from the heavens.

I don't know why I've become so cold, why my writing style started to shift continuously after only reading a few of Si's extremely sorrowful books during a time without the internet or anything else. And since this change, nothing has been able to alter it anymore.
