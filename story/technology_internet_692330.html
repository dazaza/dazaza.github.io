3.5-month-old male infant was strangled by his own mother

by uelu6c6b on 2012-03-06 08:54:38

Infant Suffered Neck Injury at Mother's Hand, Life in Danger. Photographed by Wu Sheng.

At around 5 a.m. yesterday morning, near the Nanjing Qixia District Refinery, a mother injured her three-and-a-half-month-old baby boy’s neck with a kitchen knife in their home. After discovering the incident, the family immediately rushed the injured infant to the Nanjing Children's Hospital for emergency treatment. Upon examination, doctors found that the infant had a wound over 10 centimeters long on his right neck, and he was in critical condition requiring urgent care. Currently, the mother has turned herself in to the Qixia police station. It is understood that due to life and emotional issues, the parents of the infant are currently separated, and the infant has been temporarily taken care of by his biological mother and grandmother. On the night of the incident, the mother and grandmother had an argument over child-related matters. According to neighbors' speculation, it might be the pressure from various conflicts that led the mother to such an extreme act, resulting in this tragedy.

Scene: Three-and-a-half-month-old Boy Inflicted Neck Injury by His Own Mother

At around 5 a.m. yesterday morning, Ms. Chen, who lives near the Qixia District Refinery, was awakened by the cries of the baby. When she entered her daughter's room, she was shocked by what she saw. A shiny kitchen knife lay on the ground, her daughter's hands were covered in blood, and the baby beside the bed cried even louder. "Mom, I'm sorry, I don't want to live anymore," said Ms. Chen's daughter. Only then did Ms. Chen notice that her three-month-old grandson was soaked in blood. Upon closer inspection, she found a deep wound on the baby's neck, which was still bleeding. After realizing her grandson was injured, Ms. Chen quickly called the police and sent her grandson to the Nanjing Children's Hospital for emergency treatment. It turns out that the baby's own mother had inflicted the neck injury with a kitchen knife. Following the incident, the mother, possibly under significant psychological stress, appeared disoriented and subsequently surrendered to the Qixia police station.

At about 8 a.m. yesterday, I met the injured infant in the ward on the 12th floor of the Nanjing Children's Hospital. Due to the severity of the wound, the doctors were preparing for surgery. Beside the bed, the child's father had also arrived upon hearing the news. Seeing the scene, the father almost collapsed, crying out the baby's name and tightly holding onto his son's small hand, refusing to let go. After being persuaded by medical staff, he cooperated with the rescue efforts. The doctor told reporters that the wound on the infant's right neck was estimated to be about 10 to 15 centimeters long. Fortunately, the major arteries and trachea were not damaged; otherwise, it would have been impossible to save him. The infant had already gone into shock, with low hemoglobin levels in his body. Currently, his life status is still precarious, and the specific situation will need to be assessed after the surgery. At the time of reporting, the surgery was completed at 3 p.m., but the child was still in danger.

Investigation: Separation of Young Couple Leads to Family Tragedy

During the interview, the injured infant's grandmother and mother-in-law both arrived at the hospital. Due to heightened emotions regarding the child's injury, they began arguing but were soon calmed down by those present. The child's father informed reporters that he works in Nanjing and got married in 2009. His wife, being an older mother, was in her 30s when she conceived the baby. Originally, the entire family should have provided extra care. Since they lived in the Qixia District, in order to take good care of his wife and elders, he resigned from his job in the city center and found a new one near Xianlin University Town, which was closer to home and allowed him to balance both responsibilities. However, the good times didn’t last long as the families often argued over trivial matters, and his wife frequently lost her temper during pregnancy. His mother-in-law also tended to push things too far. In desperation, he eventually filed for divorce with the Xuanwu District Court, applying for custody of the child. Afterwards, her family prevented him from contacting the child, and despite multiple attempts to visit, he was repeatedly turned away. He never imagined that his wife would harm their own flesh and blood.

The infant's grandmother insisted during the interview that after the child was born, the son-in-law and his family completely neglected them, leaving her daughter and grandchild to fend entirely on their own, which she considered unjust. On the night of the incident, she merely reprimanded her daughter a few times, and unexpectedly, such a tragedy occurred. Given the conflicting accounts, it's difficult to determine who is right or wrong.

Lawyer: Mother's Surrender May Lead to Reduced Sentencing

Even tigers do not eat their cubs—why would a mother harm her three-and-a-half-month-old child? Currently, local police have intervened in the investigation. Regarding the details of the incident, the police stated that the investigation is ongoing and it's not yet convenient to disclose any information. Attorney Chen Weidong from Jiangsu Jin Da Law Firm stated: As the child's mother, her actions may constitute attempted murder or intentional harm. Regardless of how severe the family conflicts may be, inflicting such harm on a newborn baby, especially one's own child, is unacceptable. After the incident, the child's mother voluntarily turned herself in to the police, and according to legal provisions, this can result in lighter or reduced penalties.
