For me, those ten years were a period of accumulation.

by d924jjj9c4 on 2009-11-30 09:24:26

During those ten years, people all suspected each other and did not speak sincerely. For example, when the four of us who were undergoing labor reform were together, we worked from morning till night, always together, slept on the same kang (a traditional Chinese bed), and between each other, we only talked about the two most boring and safest topics at that time: sex and food. How to make braised pork, how the egg soup should be beaten... I remember in late September 1952, when the water went into the pot... I could cook various dishes, and the recipes were all heard during that time; as for the topic of sex, all my sexual enlightenment was completed during that period. Regarding all this, I already found it laughable and pitiful at the time. After the three difficult years of the Cultural Revolution, plus reading "Capital", I had realized that this was a very problematic period in China. As for those children of high-ranking officials, the 1960s were undoubtedly their golden years, and I can understand them. First, they didn't worry about material things, with ample economic conditions, no need to go to school, completely free without any restrictions. Students are most afraid of attending classes, so playing all day instead of going to school at that time wasn't a golden age? They were much happier than today's children. This was their kind of happiness. Behind their happiness, there was also a historical event - the large-scale movement of educated youth going to the countryside, China having no universities for ten years. No country has ever been without universities for ten years after its establishment, and during those ten years, China's secondary schools and primary schools were practically non-functional, which was a very serious damage in the history of Chinese culture. I would not envy the youth of those courtyard children, because at the time I didn't know about such a way of life. Now? I wouldn't envy their youth. Because I feel very fortunate that when all Chinese people began to thrive, I also started to prosper. In our era, I didn't waste a single day and quickly became famous. It was because of the accumulation of those ten years that I could accurately grasp the pulse of the times. Those ten years were for me a decade of accumulation, very beneficial. First, it helped me accumulate thoughts. During twenty years of labor reform, I read two books: "Capital" and "I Ching". Second, it honed my resilient character and strong survival skills. Later, I could transform a barren area into a large and well-known western film city both domestically and internationally, which originated from the resilience and strength of those ten years. This so-called success is based on the understanding gained from those ten years about migrant workers, the grassroots, and people's needs. Moreover, those ten years allowed me to accumulate humanistic care for others. I estimate that the children of the courtyards couldn't achieve this. Youth rebellion? I think I never went through adolescence, so I never indulged in rebellious emotions. On the contrary, during those ten years, I felt that if a person could wait patiently and have lofty goals, he would learn to endure. Waiting for our era to come, once it arrived, we suddenly became full of vitality. If you burst with youthful rebellion in a difficult situation, it would definitely lead to a dead end. Our era? Is to restore to a state of normal human life, where people and life are normal.

In the end, Kaka made the decision to stay in Milan, that's all. Live as you should, drink water, eat meals, no objections, that's all. In my youth, I possessed the stable restraint and composure of an adult. Perhaps this could only mean that I didn't go through adolescence. As I expressed in my novel "Youth" - by the early 80s, I was already over 50 and only then did I know the word 'youth'. I knew that it was a time in life when feelings for love and the opposite sex begin to stir. This sparked my interest in self-analysis, but the result of self-analysis was - I never knew when my youth started.

The prisoners undergoing labor reform with me said: "After three years in prison, even a sow looks like Diaochan." This vividly portrays how men who haven't seen women for a long time become indiscriminate, seeing pigs as beauties. However, I, who started labor reform at the age of 21 and had never interacted with women, didn't even think about women, so I didn't feel the torment of suppression at all. When I saw pigs, especially ones I could slaughter, I only thought about how to eat them. This was the benefit of having a youth without women or love, allowing me to avoid sexual torment in the toughest environment and fully utilize my survival skills. Without women or love, my youth became more resilient, because this kind of youth contained no水分(water). Men without the opposite sex to pursue express their youth as aggression towards the same sex, which is exactly the necessary condition for self-protection in a labor reform setting. In a wolf pack, you must be like a wolf...
