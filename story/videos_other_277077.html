1. Make a practical and feasible daily plan that must be completed, such as memorizing a certain number of words, phrases, and completing a certain amount of exercises each day. It is essential to make full use of these final thirty days; not a single day should be wasted. Time must be used reasonably. Therefore, having a daily plan is necessary. Of course, after making the plan, it must be diligently followed through to ensure progress every day.

2. Set a target score for the exam: at least 320 points for Level 1 and at least 280 points for other levels. The target score should exceed the passing score by at least 40 points to increase the chances of passing the exam. Additionally, each section should have its own target score. Aim for 90 points in vocabulary, 60 points in listening. For Levels 3 and 4, aim for at least 125-130 points out of 150 in grammar and at least 50 points in reading comprehension. For Levels 1 and 2, aim for at least 90 points out of 120 in reading comprehension and at least 70 points out of 80 in grammar. How to review each section effectively to improve is also crucial. Relatively speaking, improving by 50-60 points in vocabulary, listening, and grammar within these 30 days is possible, whereas improvement in reading comprehension may be smaller due to individual understanding abilities and cultural background.

3. Conduct extensive practice with question banks. Reviewing textbooks before the exam won't have a direct effect; doing practice questions yields the best results. It is recommended to first go over all the vocabulary, marking unfamiliar ones for focused memorization, then proceed to do a large number of simulation questions related to the proficiency test. The order of doing questions matters too; start with simulation questions, then move on to real exam questions from 1991 to 2009. In the last five days, review the questions you got wrong. For listening, in addition to the methods your teacher recommends, listen to all the listening sections from 1991 to 2009. Pay special attention to question types like weather forecasts, geographical locations, people, charts, etc. Ensure that you listen to the past fifteen years' listening sections at least five times, repeating until fully understood. For grammar, remember these ten words: conjunction, meaning, example sentences, distinction, and practice. To complete a grammar question within 20 seconds during the exam, extensive practice is required. For reading comprehension, the only thing that can be improved in the final stages is speed. If there’s time, relevant practice can also be done, focusing mainly on real exam questions.
