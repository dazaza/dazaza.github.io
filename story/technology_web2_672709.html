An old man, who delivered meals for a living, tied himself to an enforcement vehicle in order to appeal for justice due to a dispute with the city management department.

by mtonyhlg02 on 2012-02-23 09:59:44

The reporter contacted the netizen "NANANANA_" who posted the first picture. She told the reporter that the picture of "the old man tied to the car" was taken by herself when she happened to pass by the northwest corner of the intersection of Gaxin Technology Second Road and Unity Road where the incident occurred. Although she took pictures, she only knew that the incident was because the old man was an illegal roadside vendor. She did not know why he was tied up.

So how did the incident happen? The reporter saw a video from the scene on HuaShang website. It turned out that the old man had tied himself to the car because he was afraid that the city management's car would run away.

Subsequently, while the old man was already tied to the car, the city management staff still started the car and dragged the old man from the entrance of the company to the side of the road. The old man said that he was not operating illegally, but delivering food instead of selling it. He believed that such law enforcement was wrong. The old man also said that after the incident, some city management staff wanted to settle with him privately. However, after he mentioned the price of the food, he was accused of "extortion."

A police officer came to handle the situation. An enforcement officer in the uniform of the city management tried to persuade the old man to untie the rope and go to the police station to solve the problem. The old man sitting by the roadside appeared very distressed and aggrieved, refusing to untie the rope bound to the car and his hands. He insisted on seeing two rude enforcement officers who were not wearing uniforms, saying "it's all in the surveillance footage."

An enforcement officer from the High-tech Zone Branch Urban Management Enforcement Team who went to the scene to handle the matter stated that they received complaints from a company that this area had serious problems with boxed meal vendors and deliverers occupying the road at noon, causing the company entrance to be particularly messy. After receiving the complaint, the city management must take action. The enforcement officer also mentioned that the occupation of the road had been going on for 2 months, with the offenders playing hide-and-seek with the enforcement officers. If checked, they would move elsewhere. During this time, there have been continuous complaints. Now is the era of civilized law enforcement, starting mainly with education rather than confiscation. Later, it was found that there was a resurgence phenomenon, so for those who refused to cooperate, items would be legally confiscated.

When netizens were fiercely discussing the matter, an unnamed netizen revealed that the incident was due to "close contact" between the plainclothes city management and the old man. Since the plainclothes officers did not admit to being "city management," the old man angrily tied himself to the car, fearing that the plainclothes city management would run away.

■ New Express Reporter Hua Jingyan

According to the old man's description, he was helping a restaurant deliver meals to a nearby company. At 12:10 PM yesterday, he delivered the meals to the company entrance and had not yet started distributing them when two plainclothes city management officers arrived and dumped all the food. "I asked them for an explanation, but they didn't say anything. There were two officers in uniform on the (enforcement) vehicle, and two without uniforms dumped the food. They were about to leave, so I pulled them to stop them. Afraid they would leave, I tied the rope to the car."

After the police determine, handle what needs to be handled.

An elderly man in Xi'an was considered to be engaging in unauthorized street vending while delivering boxed meals. In defiance, he tied himself to a city management vehicle.

The elderly man was delivering boxed meals which was considered unauthorized street vending.

He tied himself to the car to resist the "plainclothes city management."

At 1:44 PM yesterday, the netizen "In Xi'an" posted a tip-off: "In the international metropolis of Xi'an, at the northwest corner of the intersection of Gaixin Technology Second Road and Unity Road, an old man delivering food to a vendor was tied to their war chariot by our heroic city management!!! My question to the city management of Xi'an is, which law gives you the right to restrict personal freedom and humiliate others? Is this what you call a rule-of-law country, civilized law enforcement?" In the accompanying pictures, an elderly man's left hand was tied with a rope to the "nose wing" of the front right side of a car. On the hood of the car, the four large characters "City Management Enforcement" were clearly printed, and the license plate number was "Shaanxi A.XG910."

The city management responded.

The reporter then called the office of the Gaxin Branch of the Xi'an City Management Enforcement Bureau to understand the matter. The staff member who answered the phone explained that the old man was selling boxed meals and engaging in unauthorized street vending. Despite multiple attempts by the enforcement officers to persuade him to leave, he refused to listen. This time, they legally seized his goods. Fearing that the city management would take his things away, the old man tied himself to the enforcement vehicle.

The enforcement officer stated that it has not been confirmed whether the two plainclothes individuals are city management enforcement officers. Once the police make a determination, if they are indeed city management enforcement officers and need to bear responsibility, appropriate handling will be done.
