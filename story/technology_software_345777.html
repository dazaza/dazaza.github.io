In the just concluded sixth game of the finals between the Mavericks and the Heat, the Mavericks unfortunately lost at home. Leading 2-0, they suffered four consecutive losses, handing over the championship to the Heat in an extremely regrettable manner!

The tragic ending of the Mavericks suddenly reminded me of the German national team competing in the World Cup now. Both are coached by young coaches and had good starts (the Mavericks eliminated the Grizzlies, Spurs, and Suns in the playoffs; Germany won all three group stage matches). Both have German cores (Nowitzki and巴拉克), and both enjoy home advantages (the Mavericks had four home games in the finals; Germany is the host of this World Cup). Indeed, the similarities between the two are striking, which can't help but make me worry about the current German team. Germany's World Cup journey may also end in tragedy.

Besides their similarities, my judgment is based on the following three points:

(1) The change in Germany's playing style: Under Klinsmann's careful coaching, Germany's offense has indeed greatly improved, or even undergone a qualitative leap. But this has come at the cost of defense. The steel backline that Germany once prided itself on no longer exists. The current string of victories is not necessarily a good thing and may further mask its own weaknesses.

(2) Internal conflicts within the German team: Although the team advanced early from the group stage, sparking celebration, internal struggles have not ceased. From the disagreement over tactics between Klinsmann and巴拉克to the mutual accusations between Klose and Podolski, there seem to be significant potential problems with the unity of the German team now. While many might say these are merely smokescreens or that Germans are highly disciplined on the field, are these contradictions really baseless? It’s hard to say for sure.

(3) Excessive desire for the championship: In the previous World Cup, Germany surprisingly won the runner-up position, shocking the entire country and fueling greater aspirations for this World Cup among the Germans. From the German president and football emperor Beckenbauer to German fans, everyone firmly believes that Germany will achieve something significant in this World Cup.

This "achievement" is just the Germans' understated way of saying it. With the impressive results in the group stage, Germany's ambitions for dominance will be made public, but this is not necessarily a good thing for their national team. Four years ago, they could create legends mainly because there was no pressure, whereas the situation is quite the opposite now.

I hope that the tragic ending of the Mavericks will serve as a warning so that the German team does not repeat the same mistake. Otherwise, tragedy may indeed happen again!
