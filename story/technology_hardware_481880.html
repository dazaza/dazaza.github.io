World Cup, 3D!

by koti47375 on 2011-08-26 13:23:46

By CBN reporter Ya Wang

Every fan dreams of an immersive experience before the World Cup. Thanks to "Avatar", in a month, fans might be able to fulfill this wish without going to South Africa.

At 11 PM Beijing time on June 11th, a group of fans will gather in the 3D movie theater. They can see Messi rushing towards them, feel the high-speed spinning football hit the net, and can cheer or drink beer. The cinema will consider allowing them to do so. If the cinema could provide different colored 3D glasses for fans of different teams, that would be very considerate.

Watching top-level football matches in 3D on a huge screen is such an exciting event for fans! With the attraction of 3D, fans have another reason to go to the cinema to watch sports events. Even if it's just out of curiosity, the 3D big-screen version of the World Cup will make them more eager to explore. From FIFA, technology providers, broadcasters to cinemas, all links of this commercial chain have reached such a consensus.

The cinema is the last link in the chain of transmitting the big-screen 3D World Cup to fans, and they have been welcoming this from the beginning. "The movie screening market is always slow during the World Cup period. Film distributors will also try to avoid this time slot, avoiding the impact on quality films. It seems that the audience of the World Cup and movie audiences are highly overlapping." Hongtao Qiu, General Manager of Meijia Happy Cinema under Beijing Xin Yinglian told "The First Financial Weekly". Since it is already the off-season, broadcasting the 3D World Cup will not cause any impact on the original market, and will add new business, so why not?

For the cinema, this will be a very good market supplement product. The additional cost required is not high: equipment cost - buying a signal receiving device costs only tens of thousands of RMB; and somewhat vague innovation service cost - for example, should alcoholic beverages be sold? What kind of rules should there be for viewing order? How much cleaning and maintenance cost will be added? Should there be some thoughtful small services? Although the ticket price is still unknown in terms of revenue, but according to time judgment, "a game is 90 minutes", Hongtao Qiu believes that the ticket price should not be more expensive than other movies.

Even though these factors have not been considered clearly, Meijia Happy Cinema has already shown some impatience, "without any hesitation."

Hongtao Qiu is waiting for CCTV to bring news of the cinema version of the 3D World Cup broadcast. Meijia Happy Cinema had previously reached an agreement with CCTV to broadcast the HD version of the World Cup in the cinema, www.gxzb.gov.cn/20111/, and the agreement at the time did not include the 3D version broadcast. But as the Chinese broadcaster of the 2010 South Africa World Cup, CCTV has now obtained the 3D broadcast rights for the World Cup in the cinema, but has not reached a 3D TV broadcast agreement with FIFA. The reason is easy to understand, there is no TV in the Chinese market that can receive 3D signals yet.

At the end of April, CCTV conducted a technical test for the 3D cinema broadcast of the World Cup - the first appearance of sports events on the global 3D big screen, and the technology provider is a very important link in the chain.

A company called Global Vision from Australia provided technical support for the 3D broadcast of the South Africa World Cup in Chinese cinemas. The main business of this company is to use satellite systems to remotely transmit live situations for customers, such as competitions, performances, meetings, activities, etc. The digital cinema project is a business that Global Vision Company has been developing in recent years. In China, they have talked about cooperation with more than 20 theater lines or cinemas, persuading cinemas to open up the business of broadcasting sports events, not just this World Cup.

With the popularity of 3D movies brought by "Avatar", the Global Vision Company, which has long coveted the market of broadcasting sports events in theaters, sees a new opportunity. Currently, there is only one company in the world called Sensio that can provide 3D decoding equipment that can be connected to movie projectors. As the Asian sales representative of Sensio, Global Vision smoothly obtained the cooperation authorization with FIFA in Asia. Besides providing technical support, Global Vision actively acts as a negotiator, becoming the "intermediary company" between cinemas and sports events. But they optimistically believe that in the future, the sports event organizers themselves will negotiate with the broadcasters, and they only need to play the role of a technical provider well.

At least FIFA currently appears to have such plans. In the entire chain of the 3D World Cup, they are the starting point. Of course, this cannot be done without the "behind-the-scenes instigator" Sony Corporation.

In the South Africa World Cup, 25 matches in five venues in Soccer City, Johannesburg Ellis Park, Durban, Cape Town, and Elizabeth Port will be filmed using 3D professional cameras, and all these cameras are provided free of charge by Sony. In fact, the filming equipment for "Avatar" was provided by Sony Corporation. The great success of "Avatar" around the world not only made 3D movies shine, but also allowed Sony Corporation to seize the opportunity and formulate a strategy to fully attack the 3D field.

For Sony Corporation, www.u0seo.com, 2010 is the year to promote 3D TVs. They hope to make 3D TVs enter ordinary families at a "quite popular" price, and make their consumer electronics products as compatible with 3D as possible, such as players and recorders, VAIO laptops, PS3 gaming consoles, etc., to achieve another transformation in home entertainment devices. Moreover, Sony is also a content provider, so it is currently the only consumer electronics giant that can provide a complete 3D experience, with products covering every link from shooting to playback.

And the World Cup is the perfect opportunity for Sony Corporation to show off. In addition to providing filming equipment, Sony will also produce an official 3D film (Official 3D Film) for the 2010 South Africa World Cup, which will be released through Blu-ray and other media formats, and the preview of this movie will be played in Sony product specialty stores around the world - of course, it will also be in 3D.

In addition, Sony will hold international fan festivals (International FIFA Fan Fest) in seven cities: Berlin, Germany; London, UK; Paris, France; Rome, Italy; Rio de Janeiro, Brazil; Sydney, Australia; and Mexico City, Mexico. The festival will broadcast 3D promotional videos for the audience, and Sony's commercial displays will be included.

Although these companies have already put all their efforts into the 3D World Cup, how the market situation will be is still up to the fans.

In Beijing, Xue Song Zhu from Far Ocean Property said he is willing to give it a try, "I haven't tried a 3D match, I want to taste something new. In terms of price, group stage matches should not exceed 50 yuan, and knockout stage matches should not exceed 100 yuan."

But not all fans share this attitude. Dalian fan Bo Wang, who works in the government department, clearly told "The First Financial Weekly" that he would not join this craze: "The most impressive is always at the scene. You see, if fans don't have tickets, where do they watch the game? On the big screens in the open-air squares. The key is to be outdoors, to have many fans, to have the atmosphere of the stadium, to be able to shout loudly. Unfortunately, the cinema doesn't have these. Will the cinema let me shout? Give me beer? So what, is it outdoors? Can it accommodate a thousand people? Watching the game, foreigners go to bars, we go to the living room. And the most important thing, watching at home is free." Ke Ruan from China Mobile Shenzhen branch has similar thoughts, "It depends on whether the price is reasonable. Then see if you can drink beer and smoke in the cinema, can you shout loudly, otherwise sitting there awkwardly watching, that would be too dull."
