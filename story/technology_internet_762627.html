Zhengzhou workers outside the production line

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 16:27:03

Labor costs are a major expense for enterprises. In order to cut labor costs, profit-driven companies either lay off employees or relocate their businesses to places with lower salary levels. Foxconn is one such example. From Shenzhen to Zhengzhou, Zhengzhou has gradually become the new manufacturing center. Around the clock, factories there produce international products like the iPhone. However, here, young Chinese workers refuse to become worker ants.

Near the factory, there is a bar where all kinds of people come in the evening to listen to loud disco music and shake their bodies. Lian Yulong (音), a 19-year-old inspector who works on the iPhone motherboard at Foxconn, is a regular visitor to the bar. "Dancing helps me vent my anger and stress. Whenever I am here, I forget everything else." He dances to forget the dull and suffocating reality of the day.

An increasing number of recreational spaces attract workers back to the assembly line. These workers manufacture goods that others desire but have to endure hardship themselves. Because opportunities are rare, their rest time is very precious. Now they endure the present for a better future. Every worker gets excited about the weekend, even though some of their plans are simple. For instance, Bai Si Hai (音), a 24-year-old worker, plans to indulge in online gaming and then call his girlfriend far away.

Recently, the overseers in the factory have begun to see the benefits of vacations. In recent years, "worker suicides" have been an ever-present shadow over companies, whether it be Foxconn or Samsung's factories. After several reports of "sweatshops," facing external pressure, Foxconn improved the workers' treatment and promised not to make them work overtime. However, after another suicide incident at the Zhengzhou factory, Foxconn initiated a "silent mode," prohibiting workers from discussing non-work topics during working hours. Although Foxconn claims to have canceled this rule under external scrutiny, the workers say that everything remains the same as before.

The environment in domestic factories cannot compare with those abroad. Workers overseas enjoy high subsidies and benefits such as haircuts, rock climbing walls, table football, and more luxurious offices. Domestically, workers wear uniform uniforms, listening to routine scolding and shouting from their supervisors. As for dormitories, a room contains 8 hard metal bed frames and a combined bathroom/toilet room, nothing more.

Perhaps because of this, there is a large market in front of the factory gate. When the sun sets, Mandarin pop music drifts out from the hair salons, DVDs are sold on the street, thin slices of watermelon and roses gleam under silver light. A flatbed truck full of toy bears in different colors attracts the attention of young girls. Some boyfriends, at the behest of their girlfriends, immediately take out 10 yuan Renminbi to buy one. In this market, there are vans with artistic tattoos, large video games. Young workers drink light beer while smoking continuously.

Occasionally, a singer dressed flamboyantly takes the nearby stage. As for the youth, they find sufficient entertainment in strong liquor. Luo Haojie (音) left the factory in May, quitting his job manufacturing iPhone 5 components, saying, "Our factory supervisor is malicious." The food in the factory cafeteria is hard to swallow, he told his friends drinking around him.

Eventually, he will look for another job, but for now, he enjoys his youth, drinking with friends and dating girls. Luo said, "I stayed because of my brothers. Without them, I would be miserable."

Summer is the low season for Chinese factories, allowing many workers to enjoy holidays on weekends, sometimes even double days off. The variety of entertainment near the factory satisfies their leisure needs. In the evening, mobile food stalls resembling KFC are set up. Acrobats perform, someone blows fireballs. Workers say that if you came a few months earlier, the acrobatic troupe had real lions and tigers.

On Saturday, another iPhone 5 motherboard tester, Zhou Peng Zheng (音), wearing roller skates worth thousands of yuan, carefully avoided plants and started spinning. These skates cost one-third of his monthly salary. He said, "It feels like I'm flying." Before the crowd got too big, he quickly skated another round.

Roller skating and inline skating are gradually forming a cultural atmosphere among those striving at Foxconn. They have formed several teams that, on weekends, wear their skates and glide through the city. Fang Ti Ma (音), 17 years old, joined the hundred-person skating team named "Shadows" shortly after starting work at Foxconn. However, due to her age, she could not work overtime and thus left the factory. She said, "I used to come to the skating rink twice a week, now I come every night."

At 11 PM, the performers have left, and the hourly hotels start to get busy. "We work every day, so why not go on dates?"
