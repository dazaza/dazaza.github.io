Police in Huizhou Explain Details of Gold Shop Robbery Case: The Robber Changed Clothes Three Times to Evade Investigation

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-03 12:18:59

The suspect Cai was successfully captured by the police in a rented house in Fishermen's New Village, Xiqu, Dayawan. The pistol used by the suspect during the crime had bullets loaded. However, the robber did not fire any shots throughout the robbery, and there were no casualties.

Text and images by reporter Huang Liang and Huang Liqi of the Yangcheng Evening News, with correspondents Yin Hanyang, Wu Jiang, and intern Tan Shiwei.

The highly anticipated robbery case of Ruiyi Jewelry Shop in Huizhou, Guangdong has been solved, with the suspect captured! It was merely a week after the incident occurred. On the 7th, the Huizhou police held a press conference to announce the details of the case resolution.

On January 30, the eighth day of the Chinese New Year (also known as Chubazi), the Ruiyi Jewelry Shop in Huizhou was robbed by an armed man who took jewelry worth over 420,000 yuan. The robber showed strong anti-detection abilities; to avoid surveillance cameras, he changed his clothes three times, appearing in white, yellow, and black attire respectively. The helmet and keys he discarded were both washed with clear water, leaving no fingerprints behind.

Facing such a cunning opponent, how did the Guangdong police manage to capture him in such a short time?

After tirelessly reviewing 4500 hours of video footage, they found him.

At 9:29 am on January 30, the first working day after the Spring Festival, employees at Huizhou's Ruiyi Jewelry Shop were preparing to welcome customers when a man about 1.8 meters tall suddenly burst into the shop—"Robbery! Don't move! Whoever moves, I'll shoot!" He was holding a handgun and wearing a helmet that obscured his face.

Startled by the sudden event, the shop assistants hid under the counter and dared not move. The robber smashed the glass with the butt of his gun, threw the gun on the counter, and greedily grabbed the golden jewelry, stuffing it into his bag. After 72 seconds, the robber fled with 1 kilogram of gold jewelry. Post-incident evaluations estimated the stolen jewelry to be worth 424,300 yuan.

Five minutes after the incident, the police received the report and rushed to the scene. An important clue was discovered: the robber escaped on a motorized scooter. Ten minutes later, the police found the scooter used in the crime about 3 kilometers away from the scene at the Huangtang Fenghu Lake. The jacket, helmet, and bag were discarded by the lake. In the discarded items, the helmet had been washed with clean water, and the key left in the scooter seemed deliberately placed to lure passersby, with all fingerprints wiped off. However, meticulous officers still found traces of blood on the handles of the scooter. Originally, after smashing the glass, the robber reached for the jewelry and was cut by shards without realizing it. This became the crucial clue for solving the case!

The police immediately conducted DNA tests on the blood sample and discovered basic information about the robber. Surprisingly, his blood sample matched that of a suspect in a gold robbery case in Henan in 2004.

After tireless efforts, the police identified the suspect through 4500 hours of video.

The Huizhou police raced to Henan, where, with local police cooperation, they learned the robber's identity as someone from Nanyang, Henan Province.

After confirming the household registration, the police urgently needed to confirm the suspect's physical characteristics. A 16-person team reviewed videos from five days before the incident. Based on past experience, the perpetrator usually scouts locations one or two days before committing the robbery. However, there were no clues in the five days leading up to the incident.

Could the holiday have disrupted the criminal's plan? The police extended their search to before the Spring Festival. The total video footage amounted to 3000GB and lasted 4500 hours, with the special task force working tirelessly day and night to find leads.

Four days later, an important clue surfaced, capturing the youth who reported the honor being denied but said he would step forward again next time.

In the video from January 18, the police spotted a suspicious man loitering in front of Ruiyi Jewelry Shop, seemingly aimless yet deep in thought. His walking gait displayed an outward-facing stance, habitually using his left hand to support his right while walking hands-free. These physical characteristics matched certain actions during the robbery, allowing the police to obtain a clear image of the culprit.

Deciphering the source of the scooter locked down the suspect's hideout.

After identifying the suspect's identity and physical features, the next question was: Where does he live?

The police deduced that since the robber used a motorized scooter during the robbery and scooters lack long-distance capabilities, his hideout should be near the crime scene.

The scooter used by the robber was a "Tai Gongzhu" brand, sold by 52 dealers across Huizhou city, including Boluo County, Huidong County, and Huizhou District. The police contacted the distributor, who mentioned that all sales records spanning years had been processed and could not reveal which store sold the scooter to the robber. Officers were dispatched to the manufacturer in Guangzhou to track information via the vehicle frame number. Upon investigation, the vehicle was sold at a dealership in Pingshi, purchased by someone from Jiangxi who left a phone number. When the police located the buyer, they claimed the scooter had been stolen long ago. Since the vehicle was stolen in Pingshi, the buyer should also be nearby. The police concluded that the suspect’s activities were centered around Huizhou's Pingshi area.

The search operation commenced. At 7 a.m. on February 6, members of the special task force successfully captured the suspect Cai in a rented house in Fishermen's New Village, Xiqu, Dayawan. When the police appeared before him, Cai offered no resistance but was instead surprised, not expecting the police to find him so quickly. “I didn’t even have time to think about how to deal with the jewelry or where to run,” he admitted.

Confession

He was once a border guard and worked as a security guard before the crime

To evade cameras, he changed clothes, shoes, and helmets

Standing at 1.8 meters tall with a high school education, Cai looked tall and robust. He had previously served as a border guard and moved to Huizhou five or six years ago to work as a loader, later becoming a security guard for a company. Cai appeared mild-mannered and honest, leaving a good impression on his neighbors.

However, he was unsatisfied with the security guard’s monthly salary of more than a thousand yuan. He wanted to do business and make big money but lacked capital. Thus, he decided to take a huge risk, planning to stop after pulling off one major heist.

Half a year ago, he conceived the idea of robbing a jewelry shop. Before carrying out the plan, he scouted the location around Ruiyi Jewelry Shop three or four times. Police surveillance footage revealed that he scoped out areas 20 meters away from the shop, spending as long as 52 minutes at one point, even standing in front of the shop and looking inside for three seconds—a meticulously planned operation.

On the eighth day of the Chinese New Year, Cai executed his robbery plan. He left home wearing a set of white clothes, stopping halfway to change into a set of yellow clothes. After succeeding in the robbery, he changed into a set of black clothes. Additionally, he swapped helmets twice and shoes three times. Such elaborate measures were taken to avoid cameras along the way and evade police detection—his skills resembling those of characters in films.

After succeeding in the robbery, he hid the gun and jewelry on a hill behind Huizhou University, walked for a period, then took a taxi for 11 kilometers, transferring to a bus to return home. His strong anti-detection abilities were akin to those seen in movies.

Dialogue

“All the bullets in the gun were loaded during the crime”

Why did you rob the jewelry shop? Robber Cai: Being a security guard has no future, and I don’t have enough capital to start a business. I wanted some starting money… I owe eight or nine ten-thousand-yuan debts due to medical expenses from a car accident, and my savings are gone.

“All the bullets in the gun were loaded during the crime”

On the 7th, a reporter from the Yangcheng Evening News interviewed the suspect Cai in the interrogation room of the Huizhou Criminal Investigation Team.

Yangcheng Evening News: Why did you change clothes, shoes, and helmets three times before and after the robbery?

Cai: I was afraid the police would recognize my clothes. There were so many surveillance cameras nearby, and I didn’t want to get caught. This wasn’t something I learned from movies; I just thought of it and did it.

Yangcheng Evening News: Why did you choose to rob on the eighth day of the Chinese New Year?

Cai: Many people had just finished celebrating the New Year, so their vigilance was lower.

Yangcheng Evening News: Why did you choose to rob a jewelry shop?

Cai: I wasn’t content with being just a security guard—it had no future. I wanted to start a business but lacked capital. So, I thought of getting some “starting money” and figured I’d stop after one big heist. Besides, I owed debts outside. Two years ago, I injured my neck in a car accident and sought treatment at a hospital in Guangzhou, borrowing eight or nine ten-thousand-yuan from relatives and friends. All my savings were spent.

Yangcheng Evening News: Did you know that there had already been two jewelry shop robberies in Huizhou, and both cases were solved quickly?

Cai: I heard about the previous two jewelry shop robberies, knowing that one case had been solved. I didn’t expect this time the public security bureau would solve the case so quickly—I was still hoping against hope.

Yangcheng Evening News: Were the places where you threw the scooter and helmet pre-examined? Why did you choose those locations?

Cai: Yes, including the places where I hid the jewelry and the gun—they were all examined beforehand, as I thought those places were less populated.

Yangcheng Evening News: How long does it take to ride from your home to there?

Cai: I left at six, arriving at the jewelry shop close to nine. I rested for a bit, taking a break opposite Huizhou University's West Campus. The gun was in the bag, placed on the footrest of the motorcycle. All the bullets were loaded.

The incident occurred at a jewelry store on Guangming North Road in Shiqiao Street, Panyu District. At around 7:10 p.m. on the 7th, a man drove a motorcycle to the jewelry store, parked it nearby, entered the store wearing a motorcycle helmet, wielding an axe and a dagger. He threatened the store staff with the dagger not to move while using the axe to smash the glass counter, stuffing large amounts of jewelry into a leather bag he carried.

Just as the man turned to leave after succeeding in his theft, a store employee stepped on the alarm pedal, immediately activating the alarm system. Alarms blared inside and outside the store, prompting the employee to call for help.

Hearing the loud alarms, the man panicked and ran towards the exit, attempting to jump onto his motorcycle when he was caught by patrolling plainclothes officers from the Fuhua Police Station in Panyu who arrived upon hearing the news, along with the pursuing store employees.

Upon interrogation, the suspect An (male, 29 years old, from Sinan County, Guizhou) confessed to the crime of robbing the store.
