Our childhood

by xue94fwsh on 2012-02-28 11:53:06

Alas, I really shouldn't have. Chatting is one thing, but why ask about someone's childhood? Look, now I've been asked back! Haha, it's all my fault for being too nostalgic! My childhood? Haha, many people shared it with me... To those who were once a part of my life, I wish you happiness, joy, and beauty for the rest of your lives!

First off, we used to play a game involving the seeds of a certain fruit; we called it "Liang He". However, only boys played this game. Back in my neighborhood, there were groups of boys shouting and playing in every alleyway. I was completely obsessed and very skilled at it. No kidding! I remember always asking my mom and grandma to buy the fruit just so I could get the seeds. I remember searching the house for an entire day just to find one seed, and the joy that brought me. I remember sitting on the back of my mom’s bike as we turned into an alley and seeing a group of kids playing Liang He. I remember so much more...

When I was in first or second grade, we played hopscotch. Both boys and girls loved it. Do you know what? I was very good at it. I remember the cement ground in front of the classrooms at Haoxi School covered with white chalk drawings of "houses." Groups of children would balance a stone on the back of their hand and hop on one foot past them. I remember stepping on the lines and following others' rules. I remember the rhythm of hopping... Haha.

At Haoxi School, there was another boy's exclusive game called "jump steps." Haha.

During holidays, a group of us would play games involving throwing soda cans and chasing each other, or hide-and-seek inside the house.

As the game of Liang He began to fade away, we started playing badminton at home or in the alleys. Ah, when talking about badminton, how could I forget my dad?! Although he only occasionally played with us, don’t forget that whenever all the shuttlecocks ended up on the roof of the house (called Xiashouhu), it was time for my beloved, handsome, adorable, and respected dad to step in! Even though my mom tried to stop him because it was a bit dangerous, Dad ignored her and climbed onto the roof anyway (something I admired from a young age – he’s so cool!). Then my brothers and I would pick up shuttlecocks from below. Haha, we could gather dozens at once... Strangely enough, despite enduring years of wind and rain on the roof, the shuttlecocks seemed to perform better, steadier, and less prone to damage... Haha. And soccer also required Dad’s help. I’m truly grateful to Dad.

Playing blind man's bluff with my younger brothers at home always made our stomachs hurt from laughing.

Next, we bought our first red-and-white video game console (buying a game console was strictly forbidden by Mom, but we got it anyway. Before that, we played handheld Xiaowangzhe consoles borrowed from others. I remember once fighting with my younger brother over the console, and in a fit of anger, I smashed it to pieces.

When I got angry as a child, it was earth-shattering and ghost-crying!

Speaking of which, my younger brother and I were natural enemies when I was small. We argued at least three times a day and fought five times. Our hands were often scratched, and Mom scolded us frequently. Eventually, she stopped caring when we argued. But even so, we still had fun playing together, haha!!

Why did Mom let us buy a game console? Oh~ because we borrowed them from others. At home, we played games like Super Mario, Contra, Snow Brothers, etc. We also borrowed game discs from some of my close friends. Haha.

Next, we often played football in the alleys. It was extremely fun! Hmm... Playing football in the alley requires more players to be interesting. That way, we could form teams of three or four and chase each other from one end of the alley to the other! My brother and I also often played football in the small courtyard at home. The result was quite disastrous – one football was burst by Mom (Mom was really tough, I admire her). But how could the passion for football be extinguished? We made a ball out of crumpled paper, wrapping layer upon layer until it became the size of a football. To reinforce and shape it, I tied it with flat wire (haha, translated from Chaoshan dialect, no explanation needed) and then covered it with a plastic bag. Hmph, the paper ball wasn't harmful anymore, so Mom didn't stop us from playing football at home. However, sometimes if we got carried away, Mom would glare at us sharply. I ignored it.

Then, at 6 PM or 5:30 PM, we would watch the animated shows on Sky Television. Haha (think about it, Sakura Card Captor, Doraemon, Naruto, One Piece, Dragon Ball, Inuyasha, Transformers... Damn, there are so many I can remember but can't recall their names).

Next... Me and my younger brothers were obsessed with imaginative sandcastle-building games.

Sneaking out to buy firecrackers and ice cream and eating them secretly outside was such a happy thing because of the firecrackers and ice cream. Haha (ah, Mom never let us play with firecrackers or eat ice cream when we were little, but I remember Mom herself buying us ice cream when we were very young, and during the New Year, Dad would buy fireworks and set them off himself).

Remember the feeling of kicking a football in the fields with a group of people? It left me breathless.

And then there was playing chess, although not very often, just for a few days.

Running around abandoned stone quarries and walls playing adventure games was one of my favorites. Haha.

Also, flying kites was super fun. Hmm... hmm... Also, riding bikes was a very pleasant and enjoyable experience – watching different faces, feeling the breeze, and admiring the beautiful blue sky of my hometown. Haha.

It seems there are still so many things I haven't written about. Hey, those who spent their childhood with me, tell me if you remember anything. Oh yeah, before I went to kindergarten, our family had a large piece of land used to grow rice. Ha! There was a tool on the attic floor used to remove the husk from coarse rice. I think it was a very traditional, completely manual job. The impression that stands out most is the big wooden barrel. Every time I came home after the autumn harvest, I liked climbing into the barrel and using the iron rings embedded in a straight line as stairs. Haha, thinking about this makes me remember that when I slept with Dad, I would pretend my two hands were two people and the bedding was mountains, and then I would stage an intense battle here, making martial arts sounds. Dad always looked at me in surprise, but I ignored it! Haha! Going out with Grandma to collect unhulled rice on the road was an unforgettable memory.

Let's talk about my kindergarten days. More than the cute little kids, it was the cartoons, human-powered merry-go-rounds, slides, etc., that accompanied me through kindergarten. Alas, these all got boring eventually. Let me tell you about a game that should be exclusively for boys, called "Da Ren Zi" (translated directly from Chaoshan dialect, although some might not understand). The card game had various ways to play, and even more ways to cheat (sticking two identical ones together ensures victory in combat... Too evil). Boys back then must have played this game, right? Haha, I was also very good at it. Not long ago, I saw my younger cousin playing this game again. I wanted to try and see if I was still sharp... Unfortunately, my hands have aged, even though my heart hasn't.

After saying so much, we finally come to the most important and exciting part – the self-boasting section! Hmm, let me think... Ah... Got it! When I was a child, there was one thing I remember very clearly: I helped Mom find her wedding ring (at that time, I probably hadn't fully mastered walking yet... Isn't that amazing?). Everyone knows that kids love looking everywhere, and that's how I am. Haha, I saw Mom's ring fall to the ground and sweep it into the dustpan. I thought Mom didn't want the ring anymore, until she said she couldn't find it and kept looking, then I pointed to the dustpan (Wow, I'm so cool!).

Now Grandma and Mom keep saying I was ugly as a child, but whenever I see the photos of myself wearing a yellow little cotton jacket leaning on the flower bed railing at Haoxi School, I know I was super cute back then.

This should be where my nightmare comes in! The most common monsters I encountered on the way to kindergarten were domestic and stray dogs (aren't they all dogs?). This was a dangerous thing for me going to school alone. I grew up battling with dogs every day. Many times, I was chased by dogs, but fortunately, I was a good runner. As the saying goes, "Daydreams reflect nighttime dreams," so I often dreamed of being chased by dogs, which was truly a nightmare.

Who am I? How could I be defeated by a puppy, even in a dream? That would be too tragic, wouldn't it? So, on a dark and windy night, before falling asleep, I gave myself strong psychological suggestions. Everything that happens next is fake, it's all a dream. As soon as I realize any impossibility, it means I'm dreaming (Truly cool, I still admire myself now). Then, I really dreamed of being chased by a dog. Although I was chased for a short distance, I truly realized I was dreaming (maybe because I've had similar dreams too often). Haha, I turned around, threw a punch, and delivered a kick. Laughing so hard, the wild dog was kicked away! Afterward, even though I still had similar nightmares, I always beat them up (Wow, so cool! This must be immunity, right?). Haha, so friends who have nightmares can try my pre-sleep suggestion method. Here's a secret: If you master it well, you can control your dreams. Truly, I've done it... Although it's a bit difficult.

Even though my connection with dogs was so terrifying, I don't hate dogs. I'm still a bit afraid of them, though.

Alright, boasting finished. Fellow villagers, go home and eat first, we'll continue later...

What else did I do in the four seasons of my childhood? Ah! How could I forget this! Haha, I worked as a duck herder for Grandma. The ducks we used to worship gods with were raised at home. When the ducklings grew up, they were taken to the pond behind Uncle Dingxin's house, and then driven back home at night. This was a tough job, as there were always one or two ducks that wouldn't listen. Sigh, I didn't want to do this job, but now I regret it! Speaking of Grandma, hahaha, when she asked me if I wanted to go worship Ber Gong with her, I was always very happy to go, and always the first to arrive! Worshiping Ber Gong was a pretense, playing was the truth. Because the temples in our hometown were generally park-like or peaceful and harmonious places. Moreover, we could enjoy the shimmering fishponds (alas, most of them have been filled up now). Strolling along the paths around the fishponds, playing cops and robbers with my younger brother on the inscriptions of the stone steles (at first, we thought the old guard would disapprove, but he didn't interfere). Wow, I once flew kites here with Xiao Kun, Xiao Jie, and my younger brother (Xiao Kun mentioned this once, but I had no recollection of it at all. Alas, I suffer from short-term memory loss, hey, but most of the time, it's permanent memory).

Besides being a duck herder, fishing (that "beautiful", "long" little stream, why did I only catch African tilapia? Could you give me a grass carp or a carp? Sigh, that's impossible! Speaking of fishing, sigh, I once secretly took shrimp as bait, and got scolded by Mom...

Because all the earthworms were caught by us, we had no choice... But when the earthworms were alive and wriggling, I couldn't touch them, because I was afraid of snakes! It was all my younger brother who caught them. But when one earthworm became several pieces, it was my domain!), catching tadpoles (brought home and raised for a few days, then discovered they had grown legs, so happy!), planting peanuts (why peanuts? Hmm... Think about it, oh, I know! It was in the natural science textbook in elementary school where there was an example of planting peanuts), raising silkworms, picking and collecting kapok flowers (boiling kapok flowers makes cooling water, which is something Mom and Grandma always did, I think it's right. Haha, although Grandma said we shouldn't be seen picking kapok flowers from the tree near Uncle Dingxin's door, I found Uncle Dingxin to be a good person, even though... Can't say bad things about elders), playing with kittens and puppies (small cats and dogs are so cute!), catching cicadas (there were so many cicadas on the kapok tree in front of Uncle Dingxin's house. Looking at such tall trees, I realized catching cicadas was so simple!! Haha, holding the cicada in my hand, feeling its vibrations, this sensation... Do you understand it?), having water fights with my two younger brothers through the gate of our home (Haha, even though we were scolded severely by Mom, it was worth it! And we didn't catch colds or fevers, so Mom worried too much)... These are all super precious memories...

Speaking of the four seasons, I remembered the Mid-Autumn Festival. Wow!! The Mid-Autumn Festival is too cool!! It must have been a long time ago, when those kind-hearted elderly people were still around, and when that dirt path hadn't yet become a concrete road. Haha, let's get to the point. The indispensable part of the Mid-Autumn Festival was burning towers near our house (why near our house? Because it was organized by us kids, even though it wasn't as grand as the ones built by adults near the school, which were one or two stories high, ours were still spectacular). Xuan's brother and classmates would go to construction sites or somewhere else to fetch bricks for building walls (this was mischievous behavior, kids don't imitate! Haha). The smaller ones among us would stay and guard the bricks.

In the evening, we would start throwing burnable items into the "tower" made of bricks. Haha, the flames burned fiercely, attracting many people. Under the full moon and in the autumn breeze, it felt particularly warm. Pouring salt into the tower produced crackling sounds, and the fire would burn even brighter - nice!! Grandpa seemed to have instructed us on how to stack the tower. Ah, the people of that year, the Mid-Autumn Festival of that year, you were truly beautiful...

I'm really sorry, how could I forget my incredibly brilliant and shining comic career when talking about childhood! (Moved) Why did I start drawing comics? Well, this story has deep roots! It's inseparable from my love for watching cartoons (why do I like watching cartoons? It's all the kindergarten teacher's fault for showing us cartoons for no reason). Of course, this isn't the main reason! I remember the first time I picked up a pen to draw comics was on the back of Dad's lottery newspaper... Almost every sheet of newspaper had my bold handwriting on its blank back. Later, people always seek progress. I felt that the characters I drew were just a circle plus some irregular ovals, with no storyline, drawn on newspapers weren't satisfying and were used as fire starters... Haha, the moment of innovation arrived! I forgot how old I was, but I started drawing comics in lined notebooks. I mimicked famous anime plots and characters (forgot whether the first imitation was "Naruto" or "Dragon Ball", but I definitely mimicked both! "Inuyasha" was definitely included too). Later, I realized that mimicking others wasn't meaningful (the key reason was that Star TV didn't continue airing "Naruto"...). Therefore, I decided to come up with my own plot (Hehe, basically revolving around saving the world). As for how my comics turned out, purely amateurish, just a kid's scribbles. It's time to explain why I have such a deep bond with comics. Actually, this involves fate and character. Before drawing comics, I would look around the house or search textbooks for pretty pictures, then copy them down and stick them on the wall in the living room (this wasn't to show off to others, and at that time, I didn't have this awareness. I did it entirely to make the living room walls, which were ravaged by pencils and pens, look nicer, to make up for our sins). I don't know if it was because of this that I developed patience and a sense of ease when drawing comics, not getting frustrated when the characters didn't turn out well (though my drawings weren't great either, I don't know why I felt good about myself back then, leading to better and better results later). There was one more important reason: I felt like I had found a way to make my dreams slightly real - drawing them out.

(Haha, didn't I mention earlier that I liked fantasizing about battles between justice and darkness before sleeping? Hehe, because I went to bed too early and had nothing to do, so I fantasized, which surprisingly helped me fall asleep faster! Isn't it true that some people write down their dreams and become novelists? But I didn't want to express my dreams in words, possibly due to the ridiculous homework assignments in elementary school where we had to write a single word multiple pages... This aversion to writing continued until I improved somewhat in junior high).

One last reason: Um... Um... Around that time, I was almost always at home, extremely bored. Drawing comics allowed me to visualize my stories and pass the boring holiday time, achieving two goals at once. Haha, I remember Mom and Grandma telling my younger brothers to look up to me because I was always studying at the table, but they rebutted that I was just drawing. I, on the other hand, mostly stayed silent and continued my journey to another world (There's one thing that made me angry because of comics, but I won't mention that super mischievous and annoying cousin here! I wasn't that naughty as a child! I was actually quite shy...)

After several additions and modifications, I've roughly outlined my childhood, but I still feel like I've forgotten something... It seems the story isn't over yet... You think so too, right? Yes! How could we forget the person I would see whenever I had a fever? I only knew he was a doctor, Dr. Shi Renzi.
