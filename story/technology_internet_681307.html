Three departments request that the rent of taxis be subject to individual negotiation

by abcfofas on 2012-02-28 12:51:56

Fare and oil price linkage: enterprises and drivers share the burden

Taxi "quota fee" can be individually negotiated

Exemption of labor day contract fees to ensure drivers work at least one day per week

By Lu Yanan, Our Newspaper

On February 27th, the Ministry of Transport, the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security, and the All-China Federation of Trade Unions held a video conference, announcing the launch of a two-year campaign starting from March last year called the "Establishment of Harmonious Labor Relations in the Taxi Industry." Nationwide, there will be a gradual promotion of employee-based operating models for taxi companies, replacing the current contracting and affiliation system. At the same time, extensive individual negotiations will take place regarding key issues such as vehicle contract fees (i.e., "quota fee"), social insurance, labor expenses, and labor leave.

"This campaign is determined not to engage in 'show projects' or 'face-saving projects'. Instead, it aims to solve fundamental and long-term problems in the development of the taxi industry and establish a long-term mechanism for equal rights and responsibilities, shared risks, and harmonious development between enterprises and drivers," said Feng Zhenglin, Vice Minister of Transport.

In recent years, the management of the taxi industry plagued by "quota fees," fuel costs, and wages has finally awaited institutional breakthroughs.

Drivers' income grows in tandem with corporate benefits

Currently, China has over 8,700 taxi companies, more than 1 million taxis, over 2 million taxi drivers, and an annual passenger volume reaching tens of billions, making significant contributions to employment expansion and socioeconomic development.

However, the taxi industry remains one of the sectors where labor relations conflicts are relatively prominent. Currently, the signing rate of labor contracts in taxi companies is low, drivers' income levels are relatively low, some companies have non-standard labor employment management, drivers often work overtime, and their labor rights are not effectively protected. As a result, during peak hours and periods of rising oil prices, many taxis simply stop operating, causing urban travel difficulties known as "cab shortages." Moreover, in some areas, property rights of taxi companies are unclear, labor relationships are ambiguous, and given the highly mobile nature of taxis with broad reach and significant influence, they are prone to triggering mass incidents.

"More than half of the instability incidents in the taxi industry in recent years are related to non-standard labor relations, unreasonable operating contract fees, and inadequate protection of drivers' rights," said Feng Zhenglin. This establishment campaign aims to address these issues at the institutional level, allowing drivers to work with dignity and enabling companies to develop healthily.

Promoting the labor contract system to protect drivers' legal rights from the source. Starting from March, various regions will gradually advance reforms in the taxi industry's operating system, promoting employee-based operating models for taxi companies. Enterprises and drivers should sign labor contracts and legally pay social insurance.

"We will soon formulate and promote model texts of labor contracts in the taxi industry to facilitate the signing of labor contracts between enterprises and drivers," said Yang Zhiming, Vice Minister of Human Resources and Social Security.

Establishing individual negotiation systems to achieve synchronized increases in drivers' income and corporate benefits. The three departments stated that through this establishment campaign, active individual negotiations will be conducted on key issues such as vehicle contract fees, social insurance, labor income, labor leave, and labor protection to genuinely safeguard the legal rights of taxi drivers and allow them to share in the economic and social development outcomes.

"We will strengthen the cost accounting of taxi operations, urging taxi companies to reasonably determine operating contract fees based on the principles of equal rights and responsibilities and shared risks," said Feng Zhenglin.

Improving pricing adjustment mechanisms

Ensuring taxi drivers' labor and vacation rights has also become a focus of industry management over the next two years.

"The quality of taxi services hinges on the drivers. As ordinary workers, if drivers' legitimate rights are not protected, they will harbor grievances and dissatisfaction, making it impossible for them to provide satisfactory service to society. Therefore, establishing harmonious labor relations requires focusing on implementing the labor compensation rights, labor vacation rights, and social security rights of taxi drivers," explained Feng Zhenglin. This will involve exploring the implementation of shift systems for drivers, exempting labor day operating contract fees, and establishing labor vacation systems to ensure that taxi drivers work at least one day per week.

Yang Zhiming revealed that the labor department will guide eligible taxi companies to legally implement special working hour systems, adopting concentrated work and rest schedules to protect employees' labor rights while enhancing competitiveness in the market and meeting public travel needs.

To gradually address the issues of excessively long working hours and high labor intensity for taxi drivers, leading to urban "cab shortages," various regions will also establish and improve pricing adjustment mechanisms for taxis. "Regions should scientifically define the functional positioning of taxis relative to urban buses, maintain reasonable price ratios between taxis and buses, establish a linkage mechanism between taxi fares and refined oil prices, and adjust taxi fares in a timely manner to ensure that drivers receive reasonable operating incomes within reasonable working hours," said Feng Zhenglin.

Industry development environment will significantly improve over the next two years to stabilize the driver workforce. Various regions will improve supporting facilities for taxi operations, setting up free dedicated parking spaces for taxis in passenger-dense areas such as transportation hubs, commercial districts, hotels, tourist attractions, schools, and hospitals, and planning the construction of taxi refueling stations and service points to address drivers' difficulties in parking, eating, refueling, and using the restroom.

The union establishment rate for enterprises must reach 80%

The taxi industry is highly mobile, has significant social impact, and involves multiple touchpoints for labor relations with low thresholds for conflict, making it prone to group labor disputes. Expanding channels for drivers to voice their concerns has thus become particularly urgent.

"Trade unions are representatives and protectors of workers' interests. Establishing trade unions widely in the taxi industry can smooth out channels for workers' interest appeals and resolve issues at the bud stage," said Wang Ruisheng, head of the Discipline Inspection Group of the All-China Federation of Trade Unions and member of the Secretariat. By the end of 2011, the union establishment rate for taxi industry enterprises nationwide was 77.39%, and the membership rate for employees was 69.32%, with individual contracts only implemented in general cities. By the end of 2012, the national union establishment rate for taxi companies must reach 80%, with provincial capitals achieving full coverage. "For taxi drivers who meet the conditions for joining the union, we must ensure maximum inclusion and organize as many drivers as possible into the party-guided trade unions."

To listen to the opinions and suggestions of frontline practitioners, China will also establish a liaison meeting system for taxi drivers, regularly holding driver liaison meetings. Through liaisons, both the demands of frontline drivers can be heard, and industry policies can be communicated and explained to eliminate misunderstandings and resolve conflicts.

Additionally, China will establish and improve mechanisms for preventing and handling sudden labor relation incidents to genuinely maintain labor relation stability. "In cases where drivers request wage increases or improved working conditions, leading to collective strikes or other mass incidents, emergency response mechanisms should be immediately activated to swiftly and appropriately handle the situation and prevent escalation," said Yang Zhiming.
