Outside, at most, only exists in the early stages of marriage. As the length of marriage increases, romantic feelings will inevitably decrease. However, if the quality of this union is indeed good, another kind of feeling will gradually grow. This new feeling stems from the original romance and seems less passionate and obsessed than romance, but it has many factors that romance does not possess. The most important one is the mutual trust formed in long-term common life, tacit understanding in behavior patterns, deep longing, and the sense of sharing the same destiny in this life. We might as well regard this feeling as a kind of affection. But it is different from the affection of blood relationship. It is indeed the affection produced on the basis of sexual love. I think it is fully qualified to be recognized as a form of love, and it is a mature form. In order to distinguish it from romantic love, I call it affectionate love. The love in marriage exists in such a form. According to this idea, marriage is not only not the grave of love, but also the soil for the growth of love - affectionate love.

Ask a married man a question: who is the woman that makes your heart flutter most in this world? You said it's not your wife? It's normal, you don't have to feel ashamed, this doesn't mean you don't love your wife. Ask another question: who do you think is your closest relative in this world? You said it's your wife? Very good, that's enough, this shows that you still love your wife very much.

Love is the process of two souls constantly pursuing and attracting each other, and this process should not end because of marriage. Regarding marriage as the completion of love is a harmful concept. Under this concept, those who get married think they have achieved great success, have already obtained the other party, and no longer need to continue pursuing. However, courtship, courtship, love is contained in the pursuit. Once the pursuit stops, love must disappear. A good marriage should keep love in an uncompleted state, that is, both parties always maintain necessary distance and tension, and each party regards the other as an independent individual, so it is an object that needs to be pursued again forever, and can never be possessed once and for all. In this situation, both sides can continuously rediscover and appreciate each other rather than bind and weary each other, and love can gain the space to continue growing.

There are all kinds of marriages in the world, and truly unions based on love are not too many, thus making them especially precious. However, precisely the more the unions based on love, compared with those marriages based on traditional ethics and practical interests, the more fragile they are. So-called good couples are hard to last long, and the perfect match in people's eyes often cannot live together till old age, which is almost an ancient and ever-new story. Investigating the reasons, perhaps it is because human inner feelings are more difficult to fathom and grasp than external norms and interests, and love is more changeable than secular marriage ties. Taking love as the sole basis for marriage logically means that love is higher than marriage, therefore, on the one hand, if there are flaws in the existing love, the marriage becomes problematic; on the other hand, once a new love arises, the marriage should give way. Truly marriages based on love will never achieve great success and be labor-saving, even the best marital connection cannot obtain lifelong insurance.

Sexual love is essentially a very uncertain emotion. On the one hand, it has a romantic tendency, so-called "people tend to value difficulty and easily despise ease, like new things and dislike old ones", this psychology is particularly prominent in sexual love. People often beautify and idealize unknown things and things that are difficult to obtain, so the freshness of encounters and the freedom of breaking taboos become the main sources of sexual love pleasure. For this reason, the most unforgettable love experiences are either unmarried loves before first falling in love or extramarital affairs. This situation cannot be simply attributed to moral defects, but there are psychological reasons. On the other hand, sexual love is a purely personal experience without objective standards. Whether one has fallen in love, whether two hearts truly please each other, and where the boundary between liking and love lies, not only others find it difficult to judge, sometimes even the parties involved cannot grasp it. If such an unstable and unclear emotion is taken as the sole bond of marriage, the precariousness of any marriage can be imagined.

In order to find a way out in the paradox of marriage, we should change our thinking and distinguish the love as the foundation of marriage from romantic love. Romantic love may lead to the conclusion of marriage, but it cannot serve as the lasting basis of marriage. The basis should be a kind of affection developed from love, which is different from romantic love, and I call it affectionate love. In this kind of love, romantic elements may still exist, but they have been reduced to a secondary position. The basic components are the mutual trust and the feelings of knowing and cherishing each other formed in long-term common life. This trust is not only based on good wishes, but also the ability of mutual respect and approval cultivated over time in important behavioral patterns. It increases with the length of marriage, giving people a sense of security, and will make the marriage emit a solemn and peaceful atmosphere.

In fact, the reason why many families have not broken up is not because they have never encountered the temptation of romantic love, but precisely because the parties concerned value this hard-won affectionate love containing trust, thus consciously avoiding such temptation, or still making rational choices after falling into temptation, and the aggrieved party is willing to forgive. In my view, any marriage established on the basis of such affectionate love is not only stable, but also of high quality.

I do not deny the possibility of a new romantic love bringing a better marriage, but first, this is ultimately unknown, so it is a risk, second, even if it is really so, after getting married, the new romantic love will sooner or later turn into affectionate love. I believe that realizing the inevitability and necessity of taking affectionate love as the basis of marriage, people will be more objective in evaluating their current marital status, and once facing the choice of staying or leaving, they will be more cautious.

One of the important reasons why people see marriage and love as conflicting is the overly narrow understanding of love, limited only to the romantic feelings between men and women. Such romantic feelings depend on some kind of encounter and freshness, and their manifestation is love at first sight, soul-stirring, drunk with passion, inseparable. Such feelings are indeed beautiful, but they definitely cannot last, and this has nothing to do with marriage. Even if you don't get married, it will last just as long. Because once it lasts, any encounter will return to the ordinary, and any strangeness will become familiar. Trying to use the form of marriage to continue this romantic feeling, the result will certainly fail, but the fault is not in marriage.
