Yesterday, four Jiangxi natives - Yi Changhong, Yi Ou, Yi Haibo, and Yi Shuisha - who are suspected of theft, stood at the defendant's bench in the Intermediate People's Court of Shaoxing City. Their wives were also charged with concealing and covering up the proceeds of crime.

On the afternoon of February 16th, the four stole more than 1.4 million yuan in cash, 2000 US dollars, laptops, and other items from a resident's home in the Dongchi Garden community in Shaoxing. This was the largest case of housebreaking theft that occurred in Shaoxing since the founding of the People's Republic of China. More than ten days later, the four were captured by the Shaoxing police. It was found that they were a wandering theft gang. The details of this case were disclosed yesterday.

"Doing business" is the synonym for "theft"

These four people are all from Yuanzhou District, Yichun City, Jiangxi Province, without fixed jobs, basically living off stealing.

"Some people work outside our hometown, saying they are 'doing business', but it's actually theft. We didn't know what they were doing back in our hometown," the defendants' families sitting on the audience seats quietly told reporters. In the local area, "doing business" is actually the synonym for "theft".

Since last year, the four have been roaming around committing crimes. On the morning of February 16th at around 10 o'clock, they drove a blue Chevrolet car to a neighborhood in Wucheng District, Jinhua, to steal, but did not get anything. That afternoon, the four came to Shaoxing, where they randomly picked a neighborhood and unexpectedly hit a big "business".

They discussed using walkie-talkies and stole over 1.4 million yuan in cash

At that time, the car stopped in front of Building 3 of the Dongchi Garden community. One person got out of the car, found the electronic security door open, went straight up to the 6th floor, and saw no one. He went downstairs and informed the three brothers downstairs, after which the three went upstairs while he stayed downstairs to keep watch.

After prying open the door, the three rummaged through the house. When they opened the wardrobe, they saw a cardboard box. Inside the box were bundles of cash. The three hesitated, unsure whether to take so much money or not. They then used the walkie-talkie to ask the brother keeping watch downstairs. The four ultimately decided to take it.

Half an hour later, they left the community and immediately drove back to their hometown to divide the loot, each receiving 350,000 yuan.

The homeowner who was stolen from is a businessman. He said that this money was intended for buying a house.

A blue Chevrolet became the breakthrough in the case

This case caused a sensation in Shaoxing. The breakthrough in solving the case was the blue Chevrolet car they drove. This car was parked not far from the scene, leaving the community within half an hour before and after the incident.

Based on this clue, the officers quickly utilized the network to search for similar cases. The search results soon came out. In the morning of February 16th, a case with extremely similar modus operandi also occurred in the urban area of Jinhua.

Soon, the Jinhua police passed on the message: in the case that happened in Jinhua, a blue Chevrolet car also appeared, but the license plate number was different from the one that appeared in Shaoxing. The police investigated and found that both were fake license plates.

Focusing on the blue Chevrolet, the police retrieved all surveillance video footage in the city area and carefully searched. After several days of identification, finally, a suspicious blue Chevrolet car appeared on the screen. This time, the car was hanging another Guangdong license plate. The license plate was real, and the car came from a car rental company in Guangdong. Following the trail, the special case team caught 2 men and 2 women in the car on the afternoon of February 28th. Subsequently, the police also successively captured other suspects, solving this largest housebreaking theft case in Shaoxing.
