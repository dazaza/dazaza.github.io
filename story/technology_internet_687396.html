Daughter Flaunts Wealth, Gets Father in Trouble

Is Weibo flaunting wealth the epitaph of [getting one's own father in trouble][http://www.hanyue-sh.com]?

Like most people from the post-80s generation, 27-year-old Li Ying loves to post on Weibo and take selfies. On her Weibo profile [Xiaoxiao Smiled], she describes herself as [lacking ambition, fond of enjoying life, and surprisingly already a mother, a happily married young woman].

On Weibo, Li Ying makes no secret of her love for shopping, frequently posting pictures of luxury items she has purchased, including LV handbags, Ferragamo shoes, and Dior and Chanel cosmetics. A single black suede Prada bag is worth nearly 20,000 yuan.

According to the whistleblower, Li Ying, who currently works at the Xiqing District Land Resources Bureau of Tianjin, [only earns about two or three thousand yuan per month]. Her father, Li Zhiyang, 55 years old, is currently the deputy district chief of Xiqing, overseeing urban and rural planning, construction management, real estate management, and development-related work.

All four current bureau leaders of the Xiqing District Land Resources Bureau were without exception previously subordinates of Li Zhiyang.

Investigation

1. Easy Transfer, Bridging the Admission Score Gap

When interviewed by a journalist, a person in charge of the admissions office at Tianjin Polytechnic University checked relevant materials and said, [At that time, Li Ying was admitted to Xi'an Engineering University and later transferred to our school.[http://www.hanyue-sh.com]]

Regarding this, after checking, a staff member of the Academic Affairs Office at Xi'an Engineering University stated that Li Ying processed her transfer procedures after her first year, with the related procedures being handled in September 2005.

Strangely, Li Ying spent all four years of her university education at the School of Humanities and Law at Tianjin Polytechnic University, majoring in law, which was confirmed by her class teacher, Ni Juan.

Li Zhiyang also clearly stated: [Li Ying spent all four years of university at Tianjin Polytechnic University, never leaving there.]

In 2004, the minimum score for admission to Tianjin Polytechnic University for local liberal arts students was 490 points, while the lowest score for admission to the law program at Xi'an Engineering University in Tianjin was only 462 points, a difference of 28 points.

This means that through transferring schools, Li Ying easily bridged the gap between the admission scores of the two schools. Regarding this, Li Zhiyang said, [I am not quite clear about the specific procedures (for transferring). This is a matter between the schools.]

2. Was There a Deal Behind the Admission Notice?

The whistleblower claimed that in 2004, Li Zhiyang exempted a commercial residential project named Xushui Lanxuan in Xiqing District from 20 million yuan in supporting fees. As a return favor, the Xushui Lanxuan side helped Li Ying enter Tianjin Polytechnic University through [mediation].

Li Zhiyang denied the above claim, [In 2009, the Tianjin Procuratorate once investigated it. The large supporting fee (municipal public infrastructure engineering fee) is not collected by the district but directly by the Tianjin Supporting Office. I do not have the authority to grant exemptions.] However, Li Zhiyang admitted that at the time, the Xiqing Supporting Office granted an exemption of approximately 900,000 yuan in small supporting fees (residential construction non-commercial public supporting fees), which was recovered during the investigation by the procuratorate in 2009.

Regarding the details of his daughter's admission and transfer, Li Zhiyang said he was not very clear, just mentioning that in 2003 and 2004, the new campuses of Tianjin Polytechnic University, Tianjin Normal University, and Tianjin University of Technology were located in Xiqing District and under construction. As the then director of the Construction Committee, he was responsible for a large amount of land requisition and demolition work.

[At that time, the Tianjin leadership said that those who contributed to the construction of the universities would be taken care of.] Li Zhiyang admitted to having close ties with relevant leaders of Tianjin Polytechnic University. Journalists were unsuccessful in interviewing Tianjin Polytechnic University.

3. Special Recruitment for Just One Person

In 2008, the year she graduated, Li Ying took the Tianjin civil service examination, applying for the Xiqing District Justice Bureau, but failed to achieve her goal due to insufficient scores.

[I didn't get involved in this matter at the time (referring to the civil service exam), but later when she started working, I had to manage it.] Li Zhiyang did not hesitate to admit this.

In 2007, the land resources department underwent management adjustments, splitting the Xiqing District Planning and Land Resources Bureau into the Xiqing District Land Resources Branch and the Xiqing District Planning Bureau. Before the adjustment, Li Zhiyang served as the director for seven years.

[After establishing the Land Resources Branch, we still needed to recruit people. I'm very familiar with the city bureau. Kids go to school, they also need a class to attend, right?] Thus, Li Zhiyang approached the leadership of Xiqing District regarding this matter. [Then I communicated with the municipal land resources bureau, and after research, the leadership of the city bureau also approved it.]

A person in charge of the Xiqing District Land Resources Branch said: [That recruitment only hired Li Ying, it was considered special recruitment[http://www.hanyue-sh.com].]
