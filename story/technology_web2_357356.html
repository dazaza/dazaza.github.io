Costs for round-trip business class flights.   Day 1: Beijing/Seoul/Saipan   Depart from Beijing on Korean Air flight OZ 336 (1620/1920) to Seoul Incheon, then transfer to Korean Air flight OZ 603 from Incheon to Saipan (20:10/01:40+1).   Day 2: Saipan   The plane arrives at Saipan International Airport around midnight (local time is two hours ahead of China and Hong Kong). After completing immigration procedures, you will be met by a representative who will escort you to your hotel. Guests may rest briefly before enjoying breakfast at the hotel. After breakfast, enjoy free time until the afternoon when you head to Coral Ocean Point Resort, Saipan's most challenging golf course. This might be because Coral Ocean Point Resort is currently the only course over 7000 yards and also the only one that has hosted professional tournaments. The biggest challenge at Coral Ocean comes from the sea breeze. There are almost no mature trees on the course as all trees over three meters tall have been blown away by typhoons. Low-flying wind-resistant balls are a common tactic here, but insufficient distance means you won't reach the green easily due to the long distances and the hard Zoysia grass that won't let your ball roll easily. Dinner can be enjoyed at a local restaurant at your own expense. After dinner, visit the largest duty-free shop or take a stroll through Garapan's night market, where there are restaurants, izakayas, shops, bars, and karaoke places offering different styles from various countries.   Day 3: Saipan   Breakfast is served at the hotel restaurant, followed by a morning round at LaoLao Bay Golf Course (East Course) (please book tee times with the travel agency before departure). Afterward, enjoy free time. Dinner is a self-paid traditional Saipan folk performance dinner show at the hotel.   Day 4: Saipan   Breakfast is served at the hotel restaurant, followed by a morning round at LaoLao Bay Golf Course (West Course) (please book tee times with the travel agency before departure). Afterward, you may choose to swim at the pristine "Managaha Island" at your own expense. This turquoise-blue enchanting island offers coconut groves and clear waters where you can swim, snorkel, or sunbathe on the beach. You'll marvel at the incredible seven colors of the sea visible from the soft white sandy beaches. Alternatively, relax on the beach and bask in the clear waters. Dinner is a self-paid traditional Saipan folk performance dinner show at the hotel.   Day 5: Saipan   After breakfast, enjoy free time or choose an optional city sightseeing and island tour. Learn about the history of the Northern Mariana Islands along the way. Visit attractions such as Banzai Cliff, the Japanese Last Command Post, Bird Island, and the Diving Grotto (the second most famous diving site in the world). Lunch and dinner can be enjoyed at a local restaurant at your own expense.   Day 6: Saipan/Seoul/Beijing   Transfer to the airport early in the morning to board Korean Air flight OZ 604 returning from Saipan to Seoul Incheon (0250/0630), then transfer to Korean Air flight OZ 331 (0835/0940) back to Beijing, concluding a pleasant journey.   LaoLao Bay Golf Resort Designed by Greg Norman, LaoLao Bay Golf Resort features Saipan's newest championship courses, both located on the Kagman Peninsula. A type of water bird called "LaoLao" circles above the tees, emitting a sound akin to its name. Against the backdrop of blue skies and seas, many golfers pause here, wishing this moment on the tee could last forever.   West Course Entirely land-based, offering panoramic views of Mt. Tapotchao and the Kagman Valley from most locations on the course. Playing here often involves dealing with winds, which not only increase difficulty but also completely alter the characteristics of the course.   East Course This course serves as an excellent tourist attraction. Short at only 5900 yards, it boasts breathtaking scenery, especially the sixth hole along the cliff coastline overlooking LaoLao Bay. From the top of the cliffs, golfers will discover a magnificent view comparable to the world-renowned Pebble Beach.   Coral Ocean Point Resort Saipan's most challenging course. This might be because Coral Ocean Point Resort is currently the only course over 7000 yards and also the only one that has hosted professional tournaments. For 15 years, no one has completed 18 holes under par. This record has stood for 15 years!   The biggest challenge at Coral Ocean comes from the sea breeze. There are almost no mature trees on the course as all trees over three meters tall have been blown away by typhoons. Low-flying wind-resistant balls are a common tactic here, but insufficient distance means you won't reach the green easily due to the long distances and the hard Zoysia grass that won't let your ball roll easily.   Marianas Country Club The 6,500-yard course at Marianas Country Club features undulating hills and sloping greens, presenting a rare challenge for most golfers. Of course, compared to other championship courses on the island, this course is more open, which is why many golfers struggle to perform at a high level.   Kingfisher Golf Links At Kingfisher Golf Resort, no matter which patch of grass you look out from, you'll see the endless Pacific Ocean. This 18-hole course is named after the kingfisher bird as it symbolizes good luck. If you're playing on this special seaside course, luck is what the golfers need most.   Saipan Optional Tourist Activities:   Windsurfing Gentle breezes make the ocean surface ideal for windsurfing. In Saipan, windsurfing is possible year-round, especially from November to April when the southeast Pacific brings gentle breezes, making it the best time for surfing.   Parasailing Similar to a parachute, parasailing is tethered to a speedboat by a rope, taking you up to 30 meters into the sky for a panoramic view of the ocean.   Sea Walker Walk slowly underwater while appreciating the colorful underwater world; at a depth of three meters, various tropical fish surround you. Sea Walker is a safe, interesting activity allowing tourists to experience the sensation of professional scuba divers underwater.   Banana Boat Recently added to water sports, banana boats themselves lack power and are towed by motorboats, traveling at speeds of 50-60 km/h. For families or friends, sitting on a banana boat with 5-6 people is very entertaining.   Tandem Skydiving With an instructor carrying the parachute pack, connected front and back, you jump together into the vast blue sky, requiring no technical skills as the instructor operates everything. Just follow the instructor's instructions to enjoy an experience of flying like a bird! Participants must be over 18 years old and weigh less than 95 kg. A skydiving certificate is awarded upon completion as a permanent souvenir.   Cycling Tour Amidst the warm southern charm, embarking on a cycling tour is the most relaxing choice! Along the way, enjoy beautiful scenery, visit scenic spots, beaches, and jungles. Professional lifeguards and guides accompany the group, and vehicles are available if you get tired, so there's no need to worry about physical strength.   Submarine Tour Embark on an underwater adventure inside a real submarine! During the 45-minute underwater journey, visitors will explore the colorful underwater world at a depth of 30 meters; coral reefs, tropical fish...
