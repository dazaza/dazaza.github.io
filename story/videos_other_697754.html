The children's furniture market is promising, and the design should gradually meet diversified needs

by 1751seo on 2012-03-08 17:27:00

With the home life concepts of many current consumers becoming increasingly progressive, many consumers are no longer limited to traditional home design concepts. Breaking conventions and pursuing individuality has become a consensus for many people. This is especially true when it comes to children's bedroom home design. Nowadays, parents, in order to promote the multi-faceted development of their children, will not restrict their children's personality preferences. Therefore, this directly results in many children's furniture products needing to focus on meeting the diversified needs of the children's market during the design and R&D process.

There is high demand but plenty of gaps. Ms. Zhang from Baohe District in Hefei has twin sons, and having them share a room has troubled the parents. "Even though they look alike, their preferences are completely different. The older one is active and likes toy guns, while the younger one loves writing and drawing. Neither allows the other to place toys in their own space. Do we have to buy two storage cabinets?" Similar confusions like Ms. Zhang's are not only present in families with twins but also in single-child households' children's rooms. Some children's rooms are irregularly shaped, and the furniture doesn't fit well. The lack of "considerate edition" children's furniture leaves many parents at a loss.

The market demand is huge, but it's difficult to find satisfactory furniture in the market. The head of Tianjin Jiuran Jiayi explained that children's bedroom furniture is mostly mass-produced on assembly lines, all made according to preset designs, and customization is not possible. A salesperson from Red Star Macalline also mentioned that there are quite a few customers who bring their own design drawings requesting customizations, but most brands cannot accommodate such requests. They can only choose furniture styles from the showroom or catalog; even some Danish imported children's furniture brands explicitly state that they do not take on custom orders.

Design needs to gradually cater to diversified demands. "This is a wardrobe with acrylic panels where you can place your baby's photos; this writing desk can be freely combined, you decide whether the bookshelf goes on the left or right," introduced a salesperson from a furniture brand at Jiuran Jiayi. However, if you want to design and customize children's bedroom furniture according to your own ideas, the salesperson outright rejects it. Changes in size and color, free combinations of various cabinets and boxes, and changes in patterns on wardrobes—these "minor adjustments" are often labeled as "customization." However, for children and parents with genuine personalized needs, these "minor adjustments" are already unable to meet market demands.

A home square manager frankly stated that children's preferences are hard to predict, sometimes coming and going quickly. These practical issues are one of the main reasons why some parents abandon customizing furniture for their children and instead purchase mass-produced products. However, he still believes that customized children's furniture will become a future trend.

Children's furniture lacks standards. Although children's furniture emphasizes personalized design, insiders in the Hefei furniture industry revealed that most children's furniture manufacturers in the market do not have the original design capability, and there are many copycats. "Chinese children differ greatly from foreign children in height, weight, preferences, and living habits. Therefore, using foreign children's furniture as a model often causes inconvenience and unpleasantness in their use. In fact, children need professional and considerate exclusive furniture design more urgently than adults. In this regard, we hope that children's furniture manufacturers improve promptly," urged a woman selecting children's furniture.

Emphasizing ergonomics for the benefit of children's physical health is the promotional phrase for most children's furniture brands. However, since the country currently does not have special standards for testing the functionality, quality, and environmental protection of children's furniture, everything is benchmarked against the national standard for adult furniture. This means that there are no standards to speak of regarding whether many children's furniture products on the Hefei furniture market conform to ergonomics.
