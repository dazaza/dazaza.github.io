Xingma Automobile (600375) (Enter the stock forum, stock knowledge, new version market trend) has once again hit the upper limit today, with five consecutive trading days of hitting the upper limit, making it the A-share with the most consecutive trading days hitting the upper limit in the recent market excluding ST stocks. The company is the largest domestic producer of tank-type special-purpose vehicles, holding over 50% of the national market share for bulk cement transporters, and its production and sales volume of bulk cement trucks have ranked first nationwide for many years consecutively. Its concrete mixer truck production and sales volume also rank first nationwide, and the company will significantly benefit from the country's infrastructure investment. The company's comprehensive earnings per share forecast for 2009-2011 are 0.58 yuan, 0.66 yuan, and 0.84 yuan respectively, corresponding to dynamic price-to-earnings ratios of 25, 22, and 17 times; currently, there are 6 analysts tracking the company, with 4 giving a "strong buy" rating and 2 giving a "buy" rating, resulting in a composite rating coefficient of 1.33.

China has entered a period of about 10 years of accelerated urbanization. With the strengthening of policy enforcement and the expansion of implementation scope, the mixer truck and bulk cement truck industry will see an annual growth rate of more than 15% in the next 3-5 years. From January to September 2009, the company achieved a total operating revenue of 1.89 billion yuan, an increase of 17.7% year-on-year; operating profit was 65.88 million yuan, an increase of 57% year-on-year; net profit attributable to the parent company was 53.06 million yuan, an increase of 64.8% year-on-year; basic earnings per share were 0.28 yuan. This year, the low operation of raw material prices has led to a decrease in product costs, significantly improving the company's gross margin.

According to the quarterly report disclosure, apart from continuing to focus on the main business of concrete mixer trucks, the company is also expanding into high-profitability areas such as concrete pump trucks, crane trucks, and aluminum alloy transporters. Currently, the company's technology for modifying 52m-class concrete pump trucks, 12-ton class folding-arm crane transport trucks, and lightweight aluminum alloy transport trucks has been developed maturely, which will hopefully steadily improve Xingma Automobile's future gross margin and profitability.

The company recently announced that it plans to acquire 100% equity of Huali Automobile. After the asset purchase is completed, Huali Automobile will become the company's wholly-owned subsidiary. Guoyuan Securities believes that through the reorganization of Huali Automobile, the company can share whole vehicle production resources with Huali Automobile, while at the same time, it can expand the scope of production and operations of listed companies, allowing heavy-duty truck production and special-purpose vehicle production to be organically combined, fully playing the complementary advantages of the company and Huali Automobile in the industry, thereby having stronger competitiveness in the domestic heavy-duty automobile industry and special-purpose vehicle industry. This asset integration not only enriches the variety of products, improves the product structure, reduces procurement costs, and increases profits, but also solves the long-standing problem of huge related-party transactions within the company, clearing the way for future refinancing, and opening up broader development space for the company.

First Entrepreneur Securities believes that China is currently in a period of accelerating urbanization. The acceleration of urbanization will inevitably be accompanied by a large amount of engineering and infrastructure construction, thus providing long-term support for the demand for concrete machinery. At the same time, the country is stepping up efforts to implement policies that forcibly prohibit on-site mixing of concrete, eliminate road vehicles with non-compliant exhaust emissions, and govern "overloading" on highways, all of which will further accelerate the demand growth for concrete mixer trucks and bulk cement trucks and other cement delivery vehicles in the coming years, looking favorably upon the company's long-term growth prospects for its cement delivery vehicle business.

Risk Factors: (1) Risks of macroeconomic fluctuations; (2) Risks of raw material price fluctuations.
