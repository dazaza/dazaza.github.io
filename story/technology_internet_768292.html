Facebook's successful experience: In the battle for free mobile IM traffic, carrier relationships are more important.

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 20:08:53

Recently, Alibaba's "Laiwang" and NetEase Telecom's "Yixin" have successively announced that they will launch their own free data plans. They will cover the costs for users using Laiwang, mobile Taobao, Yixin, and other products.

The reason why they are willing to pay the operators on behalf of users is simple: it's about "grabbing people," attracting more mobile phone users to use their services. In the current competition landscape of mobile internet, products like "Laiwang" and "Yixin" are not yet in the same league as "WeChat."

In past competitions in the internet industry, "free" has often been the most basic competitive strategy used by newcomers facing strong competitors. However, against WeChat which doesn't charge fees, simply being free isn't enough. Paying for users became a new trick thought up by Alibaba and NetEase Telecom.

In fact, globally speaking, "free data" wasn't pioneered by players in the Chinese market. Global social giant Facebook also employed this tactic during its transition to mobile internet.

Taro Kodama, Facebook's Japan national development manager, stated that the zero-rate project promoted by Facebook and operators successfully boosted the number of mobile end users significantly. Currently, Facebook already generates 49% of its revenue from mobile ends, whereas last year this figure was still zero.

Data-Free War

In terms of mobile data traffic, Tencent could be considered one of the first manufacturers to experiment with this idea, though not entirely thoroughly. As early as July this year, Tencent WeChat and Guangdong Unicom jointly released a WeChat-specific SIM card named "WeChat Wo." The WeChat Wo card provided users with some exclusive functions, including more data traffic.

However, from today's perspective, the data plan of the WeChat Wo card can only be considered as a "discount," meaning that users still need to pay certain fees, but it is much cheaper compared to directly purchasing data traffic from the operator.

Subsequently, in August, the Yixin launched by NetEase and Telecom took the data war a step further. All telecom Tianyi users would enjoy completely free data traffic when using Yixin. Additionally, users who registered before the end of 2013 were gifted 300M of nationwide data traffic from China Telecom, which could be used on other applications.

Nevertheless, Yixin's "free data traffic" was still not thorough enough, as only telecom mobile phone users could benefit. Since Yixin is a cooperative product between NetEase and Telecom, this could only be regarded as an internal resource exchange. Whether it's the data cooperation between Yixin and Telecom or the previous data discount between WeChat and Guangdong Unicom, the application providers do not need to pay the operators.

It wasn't until recently that Alibaba announced its cooperation with Mobile and Unicom in some provinces to provide users with free data packages, marking the entry of the mobile internet "data-free" era into a serious phase.

According to Alibaba's plan, starting from November, users who use Alibaba Group-related clients such as Laiwang, mobile Taobao, Juhuasuan, Tmall, Alipay on their phones will receive a monthly 2G free data package gifted by Alibaba. The costs will be covered by Alibaba.

An official from Alibaba stated that the free data plan initially covers areas including Guangdong Mobile, Jiangsu Unicom, Zhejiang Unicom, Hunan Unicom, etc., and will expand the service scope and operation time limit based on user experience and operator support capabilities. Stimulated by Alibaba, Yixin began to quickly follow suit. Yixin stated that starting from November 8th, a new free data plan will be implemented. Not just telecom users, all three operators' users chatting via Yixin will no longer be constrained by mobile data traffic.

"The cooperation with the other two operators was facilitated by Telecom, and Wingtech Company will cover the costs." On November 4th, a relevant person from Wingtech Company confirmed to reporters that the data traffic costs for Mobile and Unicom users using Yixin will be paid by them.

Operator Relations Become More Important

As more and more companies follow the "free data" trend, pressure naturally falls on Tencent.

Whether it's "Yixin" or "Laiwang," currently, the number of users is relatively small, thus reducing the generated data traffic costs correspondingly. However, WeChat, which already owns a massive user base, if it were to follow, might have to bear enormous data traffic costs. Moreover, due to the strained relationship between Tencent and China Mobile in the past, negotiations may face more obstacles.

However, judging from Facebook's experience, it's not unbearable for dominant large companies to cover the users' data traffic costs. Taro Kodama, Facebook's Japan national development manager, detailed Facebook's mobile-end development data at the "Ericsson Innovation Forum" held in Tokyo recently.

Kodama believed that Facebook's collaboration with operators on the free data plan helped significantly boost the activity level of mobile-end users within a short period. After achieving the desired results, Facebook gradually ceased this policy.

He cited an example from an Indian operator, stating that after Facebook ended its free data policy, user activity indeed decreased compared to when it was free. "But compared to before the free period, people's data consumption volume is still much higher than the original trend," Kodama said. Additionally, according to their statistics, 75% of new users remained.

Kodama believed that in collaborations like these, global operator relationships are crucial. "We need to provide them with many incentive factors and new business models, encouraging them to participate more in these collaborations."

Specifically regarding the Chinese market, with the imminent issuance of 4G licenses, how to quickly shift the focus of income from voice to data is also the most pressing issue for operators in the 4G era. All three operators have the motivation to explore new data revenue models.

From this perspective, similar collaborations between mobile internet companies and operators will increase in the future. For mobile internet players, if they can handle their relations with operators well and obtain more preferential support in data collaborations, they will undoubtedly gain greater advantages.
