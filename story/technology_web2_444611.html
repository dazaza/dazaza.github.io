Mount Hua Free Entry Guide

This guide is written for those who love Mount Hua but are economically challenged yet experienced in traveling. For reference only. I should say that my strategy has not been personally tested. However, based on the information I have observed, if I were to practice this strategy alone, the success rate would be close to 100%. That day when Leewill attempted a night raid on Mount Hua, I had intended to inform him. But considering the position and preparation at that time were not appropriate, I did not interfere with his plan.

There is a classic old gate for climbing Mount Hua located about 1 kilometer east of the Huashan West Train Station (formerly Huashan Station) south of the railway tracks. The road inside this gate is the ancient path to Mount Hua.

Mount Hua also has a newly built East Gate, exclusively for vehicle access to the cable car lower station. It is located about 10 kilometers west of the Huashan Train Station (formerly Mengyuan Station) south of the railway tracks. This road inside the gate is the route where Mount Hua was cleverly taken. At night, fewer vehicles pass through this gate and no pedestrians walk here.

If you do not take the train but instead take a bus, you usually stop at the entrance of Yuchuan Temple. That place is located north of the railway tracks. If you have no interest in it or intend to climb at night, you can bypass Yuchuan Temple from the west side, go under the bridge through the culvert, and head directly to the old gate of Mount Hua.

You don't need to pass through Yuchuan Temple, and buying tickets there is unnecessary as it is almost a bundled scam.

In summary, the most economical way to visit Mount Hua from Xi'an should be: Take a train from Xi'an to arrive at Huashan West Station or Huashan Station in the evening, costing 10 yuan. Then walk to the East Gate of Mount Hua, find a place nearby to rest until it's completely dark. Then hide in the bushes near the East Gate of Xiyue, observe the terrain and the situation at the gate. When the opportunity arises, take advantage of the darkness to quickly move along one side of the road. From then on, there will be no major obstacles.

Then follow the mountain road around (about 10 kilometers), or save time by climbing directly. Lighting must be good.

When you reach the end of the mountain road, it is the lower station of the cable car. You will also find a small path, which is a stone staircase leading up along the cliff. Although the steps are relatively new, they are not fully maintained. Extra caution is needed for safety. Finally, you will reach the upper station of the cable car, North Peak.

Be mindful to avoid any possible staff members, then quickly leave the cable car station and return to the main path. There may occasionally be someone checking tickets at the entrance of a small house on North Peak, but many times he is sleeping inside. So, if you are patient, you can pass through without any further issues.

Sichuan - Daocheng Yading Free Entry Guide

Daocheng Yading has become incredibly popular over the past few years. After visiting personally, I truly feel that the beauty there is almost indescribable. Even now, I still feel like it was a dream, unreal, so much so that I often seem to forget it in my daily life. Isn’t that a strange feeling?

Here, I will briefly introduce a method for free entry, which each friend can consider accordingly.

Whether coming from Zhongdian (now Shangri-La County) in Yunnan or from Chengdu in Sichuan, generally speaking, arrival time is usually in the evening. You can first check out the famous but average Red Grassland on the way before arriving. Now it’s surrounded by stones, and anyone wanting to take pictures inside must pay 5 yuan (it’s too commercialized, so you don’t have to give money; just stand on the road and use a telephoto lens to take a photo, unless you're an enthusiast trying to capture artistic shots).

Mainly, let’s talk about the most important scenic area of Daocheng—Yading. Personally, I suggest that everyone try to book a car to stay overnight in Yading (although conditions there are tough, but if you save 128 yuan per person on the ticket, maintenance of the car, and save on sightseeing time, it’s worth it). Booking a car from Daocheng to Yading takes about 3 hours, and the later it gets, the easier it is to sneak in (of course, the roads are dangerous at night, so please judge for yourself, hoho). Generally, these are small minivans like Changan or Songhuajiang, seating 6 passengers, costing 50 yuan per person, 300 yuan per car. If you can lie down at the back seats when approaching the entrance, hiding smaller individuals beneath others' feet can save 128 yuan per person (this is why it's easier to sneak in at night—one reason being that the guards outside can't see clearly inside the car, and secondly, the later it gets, the more likely the guards are half-asleep and simply want to handle things quickly to go back to sleep). Also, it's best to get some student IDs beforehand when going to Daocheng, allowing you to buy half-price tickets for 68 yuan per person. In other words, if everything goes well, the normal price for six people is 128 * 6 = 768 yuan. If one person hides while five others buy half-price tickets with student IDs, that's 5 * 68 = 340 yuan. Thus, you save 768 - 340 = 428 yuan!! So, it's quite a bargain.

Sichuan - Southern Bamboo Sea Free Entry Guide

Sichuan's scenic area—the Southern Bamboo Sea—currently has a ticket price of 65 yuan. The method is simple: On the bus heading to Wandi (Changning County) or Wanling (Jiang’an County), find a local restaurant owner and promise to stay with them (I spent 20 yuan for a standard room during the off-season, though there was no hot water). That’s all. Just remember: Go to the Forget-Worries Valley after 6 PM because tickets are checked there. Other places like Tianbao Fort, Seven-Colored Waterfall, Moxi... are open freely. One additional note—if you get friendly with the boss (i.e., sweet-talking), their tour guide friends can provide used tickets, making re-entry into the Forget-Worries Valley not difficult.

Another Huangshan Free Entry Route (Part 1)

Written by an old travel companion from Nanjing, very detailed. His name is unknown. This time we followed this line, possibly avoiding the entrance fee at the main gate. A relatively classic route up the back mountain:

1. Tunxi Train Station: After exiting the station, there are many breakfast shops. Turn left about 100 meters away on the opposite side of the road. Their pan-fried buns taste pretty good. After dining, take a minibus to Zhaixi Tollgate to Tangkou Town or the Huangshan Entrance, costing 13 yuan per person, or rent a 6-seat agricultural minivan for 40-50 yuan (during non-holiday periods). During May Day and National Day holidays, prices rise to 80-100 yuan.

2. Tangkou Town: Located on the east side of the South Gate of Huangshan, it is the hub and transfer station for tourists and vehicles entering and exiting the mountain. The town is filled with buildings and hotels. There is a unique small supermarket at the entrance of the town. There are minibuses or Santana cars to Ciguang Pavilion and Yungu Temple, minibuses to the Huangshan Railway Station, and long-distance buses directly to Nanjing, Hefei, etc.

3. Huangshan Entrance: The South Gate of Huangshan, a grand colorful archway with "Huangshan" written on it by Chen Yi, then Foreign Minister, in October 1963, when he accompanied various ambassadors to China on a tour of Huangshan. Looking north from here, Tian Du and Lotus Peaks pierce the clouds, quite spectacular. At this point, you can take a minibus to the parking lot of Ciguang Pavilion or the Water Plant, costing 10 yuan, negotiable down to 5 yuan. Alternatively, you can rent a Santana for 30 yuan.

4. Human-Character Falls: Originally named "Flying Rain Spring," also known as "Double Dragon Falls." The waterfall splits into two streams from one source, flowing down a hundred-foot perilous rock with protruding stones in the middle, creating a 26-meter cascade resembling the character "ren" (human). When heavy rain pours, the splashing water forms a white curtain, roaring like thunder, majestic like two white dragons flying down, quite spectacular. You can view the falls from the Observation Pavilion on the waist of Peach Blossom Peak, the Viewing Platform above Listening Wave Cottage, or the Back Dragon Bridge.

5. Tiger Head Rock: A giant rock shaped like a tiger's head, marvelously adorned with dragon whisker grass around its eyes and mouth, fluttering in the wind, extremely similar to a tiger's eyebrows and whiskers. The rock is inscribed with "Tiger Head Rock," written by Luo Yuan of She County during the Southern Song Dynasty. Above Tiger Head Rock, you can reach the Water Plant in a minute or two.

Special Note: Here, you can find an ancient trail up Huangshan. Look behind the rock for a stone slab bridge called Tiger Head Bridge. Following the Lotus Gully, climbing vines and touching moss, you can reach Lotus Peak. This was the common path ancient climbers took, now rarely known and deserted.

6. Water Plant: Going up the large road to the right leads to the ticket booth at Ciguang Pavilion. The stone stairway to the left leads to the Wuli Bridge Reservoir, Diaqiao Nunnery, Baiyun Residence, and Jiaocun.

7. Wuli Bridge Reservoir: Starting from the door of the Water Plant, it takes about ten minutes to reach the Wuli Bridge Reservoir. Construction began in 1999 and entered the second phase in early 2000. Passing through the migrant workers' dormitories, cross the bridge into the mountain trails.

8. Mingxian Spring: One of the famous "Seventeen Springs" of Huangshan. On the right side of the road at the foot of the mountain lies a huge rock. The rock cliff is hollowed out and thick on the left, resembling an ancient zither. Above it is Mingxian Brook, and above that is Luoxing Spring. On the side of the rock wall are three characters in running script, "Mingxian Spring," said to be written by Li Bai. Nearby, the three characters "Xibeiquan" are also Li Bai's handwriting, referring to the place where Li Bai drank wine. On the western stone wall, four characters "High Mountain Flowing Water" are carved, with the inscription below: "Xu Zihanqing and his wife Xu Sunqiqing, together with their social friends Zheng Songxian, Cai Chuang, and Qian Yiru, visited Huangshan. Inscription by Xu Hanqing on the 17th day of May in the 25th year of the Republic of China. Guide Wang Chongzhi, engraved by Wanli Mountain."

9. Drunken Stone: Located under the Mingxian Bridge, next to the Yuetian Pavilion, a tilted massive stone. The stone is inscribed with the two large characters "Drunken Stone," traditionally attributed to Li Bai but actually inscribed by Luo Yuanchang of the Ming Dynasty.

10. Guanyin Sitting on the Lotus: This scenery is across the river from the Drunken Stone. Its shape resembles the Bodhisattva Guanyin, hence the name. In front of the scene stands a pine tree, resembling the willow in Guanyin's bottle.

Another Huangshan Free Entry Route (Part 2)

11. Yuetian Pavilion: Located under the Mingxian Bridge, next to the Drunken Stone. The pavilion has two levels, the upper level aligns with the road surface; the lower level is relatively hidden and should not be overlooked when touring. Below the pavilion to the left is a small path, with a stone cave on the side of the path, and a stone blocking the path ahead. Just when you doubt the path ends, a glimmer of light between the massive stone and the cave acts as your "guide," revealing another gap. Exiting the cave, you arrive at a small stream. On the stream's rocks are carved holes for climbing. There are numerous unnamed small pools here, with crystal-clear water shimmering. This pavilion was built in 1986, named after Li Bai's poem "Raise a Cup to Invite the Moon," and the three characters "Yuetian Pavilion" were inscribed by Deng Lijun.

12. Tangling Pass: After resting for a moment at Mingxian Spring, set off again. After walking quite a long distance, finally look up to see a magnificent mountain pass, this is Tangling Pass. According to historical records, it was built during the Qing Dynasty's Xianfeng period to defend against the Taiping Army's attacks. Upon reaching the top of Tangling Pass and looking across to the opposite side, immediately exclaim "Cool!" At this point, you can rest for a moment on the city wall, overlooking the distant Jiaocun and scenery. However, the wind here is strong, so beware of catching a chill.

13. Sweet Spring: It is said that on the northern wall of Tangling Pass, there is a sweet spring that never dries up, refreshing and quenching thirst for travelers. However, when we went to see it, it had already become a dry well.

14. Diaqiao Nunnery: Departing from Tangling Pass, the scenery along the way is pleasant, unknowingly walking downhill and forward. After approximately 7-8 miles, finally arrived at Diaqiao Nunnery. This place is about 20 miles from Jiaocun and Tangkou Hot Springs, serving as the halfway station up the mountain via the western route. Despite multiple renovations, it is now abandoned. Currently, the entrance to the building is sealed off. Turning left here leads to the Diaqiao Nunnery Ticket Office to the Diaqiao Nunnery Bus Station, then connects to Jiaocun and the northern town of Huangshan, Gantang (originally Taiping County). Turning right leads through the abandoned WC, starting towards the long "Xihai New Path."

Special Note: Walking straight from here for 20 miles (approximately a 2-hour journey) reaches Jiaocun. Along the way, you can visit the Kuimen Gorge where local villagers have laid ancient trees and old cypresses to form the "Kuimen Plank Road," thrilling and exciting. Reaching Jiaocun, you can find another ancient mountain path, the stone road still exists, passing through the Large and Small Yang Lakes leading to Songgu Nunnery, about 15 miles long, with water and mountains reflecting, the scenery enchanting, but rarely visited by tourists, occasionally visited by photographers and artists.

15. Xuanpu Bridge: Not long after, passing through a section of green tree-lined stone steps, arrives at the Xuanpu Bridge with clear water flowing beside it. The bridge is arched, spanning over Baiyun Stream. Standing on the bridge and looking down, you can see a deep ravine and a green pool, adding charm, instantly refreshing the mind and spirit, feeling as if entering a pure realm. This is also the starting point of the "Xihai New Path." From here, the "Xihai New Path" leads to Baiyun Hotel and Haixin Pavilion, with a total of 5527 steps, 9 bridges, and a total length of about 16 miles. Climbing from the intermediate station Sanxi Bridge and Baiyun Residence increases the altitude by nearly 1000 meters. Subsequently, passing through Songshi Bridge, Xiuyu Bridge, and Tian'e Bridge, finally reaching Baiyun Residence at the side of Sanxi Bridge for a rest.

16. Baiyun Residence: Resting for a moment at Xuanpu Bridge and setting off again, it is a long journey full of anticipation, hoping to pass through the three valley-crossing bridges, because after these three bridges, you can reach Baiyun Residence on the fourth bridge. Perhaps after walking for 8 miles, finally reaching the destination. This is a small dwelling built in 1989 at the side of Sanxi Bridge, with granite walls, red glazed tiles, ancient and elegant, surrounded by green peaks and white clouds. It also has toilets and kitchens. Due to the rarity of visitors and poor management, it has been abandoned. Since Zhang Renbao, Ye Rui, and their team from the Nanjing Outdoor Activity Club frequently come here, this place has become a popular "ghost house." Additionally, a reminder: the toilet is female left and male right, so don't get it wrong.

17. Baiyun Mountain Path: Finally, it's time to test the willpower and strength of the climbers, continuously climbing step by step. Fortunately, there are multiple viewing platforms along the way to rest. Initially, resting every 200~500 steps, then constantly resting, even some companions lean their big bags against the steps and lightly nap on the backpacks. Water starts becoming increasingly precious.

Starting from Sanxi Bridge, after climbing 667 steps, you reach the second viewing platform. Looking to the right from here, you can see "Immortal Exploring Wonders," also known as "Immortal Reciting Poetry," while observing it from the Paiyun Pavilion in the Xihai area reveals "Immortal Walking on Stilts." After climbing 1145 more steps, you can see "Peach Stone," which looks very similar to "Hershey Chocolate," whereas the well-known Peach Stone is located between the Tian Du Peak and the Fishback Ridge.
