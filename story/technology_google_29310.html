Google Reader Upgrade: Added Search Functionality and Detailed Improvements

1. Added search functionality

The search function in Google Reader has been a long-awaited feature for users, and it is now finally available, as shown in the image below:

Google Reader Search Interface

By default, it searches all items in your subscriptions, but you can also limit the search to starred items, shared items, or a specific subscribed feed. For example, searching for "Google" across all articles yields the following results:

Google Reader Search Results

2. Loading status icon has changed

Previously, the loading status was displayed in the middle of the page with a chemistry flask icon. In this update, the loading status has been moved to the upper-middle part of the page, and the icon has been changed, as shown below:

New Loading Status Icon in Google Reader

3. Increased unread item count limit

When there were more than 100 unread items in Google Reader, it would display the count as "100+". It was easy to exceed this limit when subscribing to frequently updated feeds like forums. This update has increased the unread item count display limit to "1000+":

Unread Item Count Limit Set to 1000+ in Google Reader

4. Ability to hide the subscription list

Previously, you could use the shortcut key "U" to hide the subscription list. Now, you can also hide it by clicking with the mouse, as shown below:

Google Reader Subscription List Hidden

A small clickable icon has been added that allows you to enter full-screen reading mode upon clicking. Clicking again restores the default view. In full-screen reading mode, you can click the dropdown button shown below to display the subscription list in a floating form:

Interface After Hiding the Subscription List in Google Reader

5. Two new Reading Trends viewing options added

The new options are "Emailed" and "Read on mobile," which allow you to see rankings of feeds that are frequently emailed or read on mobile devices, as shown below:

Two New Reading Trends Viewing Options Added in Google Reader

6. Quick filtering of the subscription list (this feature may have been available in earlier versions)

In the subscription management settings interface of Google Reader, you can quickly search through your subscription list, as shown below:

Quickly Search Subscription List in Google Reader
