Pentagon Plans to Open Pentagon Networks to Apple Devices

by geekzhang on 2013-05-11 12:43:09

May 11th, Beijing time, according to foreign media reports, the US Department of Defense announced on Friday that they plan to allow Apple iOS devices to connect to the Pentagon's secure military network early next week. This means that the US Department of Defense will open up to Apple what has been a lucrative military procurement contract monopolized by BlackBerry.Alana Johnson, a spokesperson for the Defense Information Systems Agency at the Pentagon, said in an interview with Bloomberg that if approved next week, Apple iOS6 devices will be able to access the US military's secure network.In the previous week, Samsung’s flagship product, the Galaxy S4, passed the security certification of the US Department of Defense due to its Knox good security performance, becoming the only Android device that can connect to the US Department of Defense network. This also means that the US Department of Defense can include the Galaxy S4 in their procurement plan. According to reports, devices equipped with the latest BlackBerry 10 operating system have also obtained the security certification of the US Department of Defense.Recently, Apple has made significant progress in entering the American government sector. On May 8th, Apple's iOS Core Crypto Kernel Module v3.0 officially received FIPS 140-2 standard certification authorized by the Computer Security Division of the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST). It is reported that this department conducted relevant encryption module tests on the iPhone 4, iPhone 4S, and iPads running iOS 6.0.Alana Johnson said that for Apple, it was another step in the right direction. However, in order to allow iPhones and iPads into the military secure network, the US Department of Defense must first establish a mobile management system. There is currently no timeline for this project. (kirk)
