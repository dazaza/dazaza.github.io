Be humble when posting on forums.

by hartyqekb on 2009-11-25 17:48:07

Mere mental preparation without actual action is not enough. Let's take the first step by gaining knowledge about home decoration and arming ourselves with it. This will be a powerful weapon for you when facing potential pitfalls from renovation companies, building material merchants, and workers in the future. Preparation of home decoration knowledge mainly involves the following aspects:

1. Accumulating knowledge online: The Internet currently holds a vast amount of knowledge, making it easy to search for a large amount of home decoration information. However, how to extract the essence from this massive amount of knowledge has become a problem for everyone. This requires diligent organization and accumulation, as well as good classification of knowledge. After understanding the general knowledge of home decoration, browsing home decoration forums is a good choice. You can consult netizens whenever you encounter questions that you don't understand and exchange experiences online. Similarly, you can also share your own lessons learned with everyone, accepting help while helping others in return. At the same time, you can pay attention to collective procurement and home decoration lectures organized by netizens online. While preparing knowledge, you can also purchase some furniture and building materials simultaneously.

★ First Step: You can visit the home decoration section of some professional home decoration websites to gain a comprehensive understanding of home decoration. During this period, make sure to jot down any questions you don’t understand.

★ Second Step: For questions you don't understand in the first step, use a search engine to eliminate these issues initially. If you still cannot solve them, there is a third step.

★ Third Step: Hang out on forums. Find a good home decoration experience exchange forum and seek help from experienced users on the forum. There are many enthusiastic people here, especially those who have gone through renovations themselves, as they can offer practical experience. Of course, one should not blindly believe everything others say; it's important to stick to your basic principles. Finally, when participating in forums, remain humble and don't forget to say "thank you" when someone answers your questions.

★ Best tip: A shortcut to obtaining systematic home decoration knowledge is reading netizens' home decoration diaries. These are summaries of valuable experiences!

2. Consulting those who have undergone renovations: Such as your colleagues, relatives, or classmates, if they have done home decoration, do not miss the opportunity to ask for advice. From them, you can obtain direct home decoration knowledge. Often, they will showcase their achievements during the renovation process, and at the same time, vent their frustrations, which is a good learning opportunity. Their painful lessons can serve as direct references. In the process of understanding home decoration knowledge, you will also accumulate some information about renovation companies and construction teams, achieving two goals at once. Why not?

[Netizen Experience]

★ Listen more to your colleagues' and friends' renovation experiences, but treat their words differently. The general rule is: if someone says something is good under a clear package deal, it may or may not be reliable; if they say something is bad, it should be believed because people rarely easily negate themselves. For semi-package deals, if they say something is good, it should be believed; if they say something is not good, it may or may not be reliable, as people tend to be picky about others.

★ One of my teachers was my colleague who was renovating or had just finished renovating. They were mainly negative examples. People's behavior changes somewhat during the renovation period, one of which is the tendency to talk about the progress of their home renovations. What I mostly heard from them were negative opinions, including tricks used by renovation companies to deceive, lazy techniques used by migrant workers, brands that do not live up to their names, and their own failed choices. Generally speaking, those doing clear packages talk more about experience and creativity, while those doing semi-packages talk more about problems and regrets. No one among my colleagues opted for full packages.

3. Visiting building material markets and consulting renovation companies: Visiting building material markets focuses on acquiring knowledge about building materials and furniture. After visiting multiple times, you can roughly understand the categories, brands, craftsmanship of building materials and furniture, as well as possible price traps. Consulting renovation companies mainly involves their designers and foremen of the construction teams (though in some companies, you might not meet the foreman). Through them, you can acquire home decoration design knowledge, refer to some design plans, and learn about matters to note during construction.

[Netizen Experience]

★ Visit building material markets near your home first, then those close to your new house, followed by supermarket-type building material markets, and finally mid-to-high-end ones.

★ Record every visit. Note down where you went today, what you liked, the approximate price, the stall number of the salesperson in the building material market, and if possible, buy a camera to take pictures. When starting, don't hesitate to ask questions. Don't worry about being laughed at by the salespeople, but don't trust their compliments about their products. Remember what they say negatively about other products.

★ Actually, the initial source of my renovation knowledge was the salespeople. At that time, I knew nothing. My wife and I often asked the salespeople some very basic questions when we shopped. Most salespeople were quite helpful. Even when I clearly told them I wasn't buying anything but just looking around, they would enthusiastically introduce their products and give me some promotional materials, sometimes even belittling their competitors. Of course, the levels of salespeople vary. Some are very professional, and I enjoy chatting with these professionals. When interested, I would sit down, offer them a good cigarette, and humbly ask for advice.
