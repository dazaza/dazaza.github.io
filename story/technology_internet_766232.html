It is reported that China Unicom and China Telecom will sell the new iPhone immediately after its release.

by anonymous on 2013-09-08 20:03:05

Within days after Apple releases its new generation of iPhones, China Telecom and China Unicom will begin selling the device. Apple hopes to reclaim the market share it has lost here by accelerating its launch in China.

This week, Apple announced that it will hold a new product launch event on September 10th. The industry speculates that the company may introduce a new generation of iPhones at that time. Simultaneously, the company announced that it will set up a sub-venue in Beijing on September 11th. This marks Apple's first step in synchronizing China's product release with the U.S., highlighting the importance of the world's largest smartphone market.

An informed source said: "Previously, Apple products would take several months to be available in China after their announcement, but now, due to the significance of the Chinese market, it will become one of the first markets to sell the new generation of both low-end and high-end iPhones next week."

According to current information, the high-end iPhone will be named iPhone 5S, while the low-end model will be named iPhone 5C.

As the second and third largest mobile operators in China, China Unicom and China Telecom collectively have 266 million users, but the total number of smartphone users in China exceeds 1 billion. The gap exists because China Mobile, which has the most users, has not yet signed a contract with Apple, but rumors suggest that an agreement will soon be reached.

The high-end iPhone 5S is likely to support TD-LTE networks, paving the way for Apple to sign a deal with China Mobile, whose user base is twice that of China Unicom and China Telecom combined.

Spokespersons from all three carriers declined to comment.

Within days after the new generation of iPhones is launched in Beijing, both China Unicom and China Mobile will start accepting pre-orders for the phone. An insider stated that it will probably take about a week to deliver the new iPhones to customers.

News of the iPhone launch was leaked on Sina Weibo when a verified China Telecom Weibo account posted a pre-order advertisement for the product. Although the post was quickly deleted, the news had already spread, and some people took screenshots of the post.

"Are you ready with your money for the iPhone 5S and 5C? I'm looking forward to it," said one Sina Weibo user.

Fierce competition from Samsung Electronics and local companies has significantly reduced Apple's market share in China over the past few quarters. According to data from market research firm Canalys, Apple's market share in China dropped to 5% in the second quarter, ranking seventh, behind Samsung, Lenovo, Yulong, ZTE, Huawei, and Xiaomi. In the first quarter, the iPhone's market share in China was 8%, ranking fifth.

"Apple's decision to launch a new model as soon as possible in China amidst declining brand appeal is a correct decision," said Nicole Peng, an analyst at Canalys, "This shows they value Chinese consumers."

Another indication of Apple's shrinking sales in China is that in the fiscal quarter ending June, Apple's Greater China revenue contracted by 43% quarter-over-quarter and decreased by 14% year-over-year, reaching only $5 billion.

The iPhone event will be held on September 10th.
