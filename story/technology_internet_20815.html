The end of the Eastern Han Dynasty was divided into three kingdoms: the Romance of the Three Kingdoms in the stationmaster circle.

by 360300 on 2007-05-30 20:00:41

The quote comes from the lyrics of a JJ Lin song, which I think fits well with what I want to write. Everyone, please bear with me.

In 2007, it was already May in the blink of an eye. This year is the grassroots year and also the year of the Internet winter; the collapse of numerous advertising alliances, the country strengthening its monitoring of online information, search engines' crackdown on junk content, and hackers running rampant have all made this year difficult for individual webmasters. And the once numerous webmaster websites and forums are showing signs of gradual extinction and integration starting this year. Currently, the webmaster community has formed a tripartite situation dominated by Luowu, Admin5, and Chinaz. The vast number of individual webmasters slowly gather and exchange ideas in these three bases within the webmaster domain.

Wei Kingdom: Luowu Im286 - Mop of the Webmaster Community

Popularity Index: ★★★★★

Resource Index: ★★★

Web Earning Index: ★★★

Cao Cao: Fish Refusing to Swim

Saying that Fish is Cao Cao isn't because I want to imply he's a villain, but because during the Three Kingdoms period, the Wei Kingdom had elite soldiers and generals, unmatched in the world, somewhat similar to today's Luowu. Those who just came into contact with Luowu would wonder why it's called im286 instead of luowuzhe? There are still many speculations so far, and I don't know why either, but I just feel that this forum is very excellent.

Luowu has high popularity, so high that if you post in the "Resource Exchange" channel, after five minutes of refreshing, it might not be on the first page anymore. Luowu also has a lot of original content, ranging across various types: there are cries, laughs, joy, and tears. Good people in Luowu are as famous as bargains. In Luowu, you can see someone cursing scammers on the same page while others praise good people.

Luowu is very similar to Mop, not only in terms of popularity but also in cultural atmosphere. The names of the managers are also quite special: Fish Refusing to Swim has some humorous undertones in its name itself. And the culture inside Luowu is more of a webmaster's absurd culture. Things like reading signatures rather than articles, the YY culture of Luowu, make everyone who enters Luowu feel very relaxed and at ease. The technology and resources accumulated over several years are also worth savoring for many people.

Shu Kingdom: Webmaster Network Admin5 - Making Money and Information Go Hand in Hand

Popularity Index: ★★★★

Resource Index: ★★★

Web Earning Index: ★★★★★

Liu Bei: Tuwang

What characteristics does Liu Bei have? Respecting talented people and being approachable. What about Tuwang? He helps anyone, and if newbies get the right opportunity, they can even chat with Tuwang casually.

Tuwang is just a nice guy who does good deeds. Recently, he's been busy setting up a trading forum. As the moderator of Luowu's web earning section, Tuwang indeed has his own insights into making money online. Not long ago, I saw someone on Luowu say that Tuwang's GGAD earns at least $6000 a month, and I think that's entirely possible.

Admin5, in my personal opinion, is actually a pretty strange place. Compared to Luowu, the content is relatively serious and rigorous. Fortunately, I got to know EndTo, the chief editor of Admin5, who gave me a very formal impression. When reading content on Admin5, you almost never see very satirical things, almost devoid of human sentiment, but the article content is indeed very precise, especially in the web earning part where Tuwang has put in a lot of effort. It can be said that entering Admin5 can indeed teach you a lot about making money.

Another feature of Admin5 is its forum. Its forum is not just a forum but more like a trading platform, and Tuwang plays the role of a human intermediary, using his years of integrity to build the entire website buying and selling transactions. I don't have money, and my site isn't great, but I still like to visit his trading forum every few days to see roughly what kind of sites are currently traded at what level. (PS: Recently, it seems someone is willing to spend 500,000 yuan to buy sites on Admin5, hoho, big deal!)

Wu Kingdom: China Webmaster Station Chinaz - Resource King

Popularity Index: ★★★★

Resource Index: ★★★★★

Web Earning Index: ★★★

Sun Quan: Fei

Wu Kingdom dominates the south of the Yangtze River, rich in resources, with a suitable climate. Using it to metaphorically represent resource-rich sites like Chinaz is extremely appropriate.

Looking at the domain of Chinaz makes one feel official, and looking at the pages of Chinaz makes one feel grand. Compared to Luowu's forum, Admin5's middle ground, Chinaz visually gives people a sense of being official and stable. And its content is exactly as its name suggests, covering almost all webmaster-related content.

Resources! Yes, Chinaz's feature is resources, article resources, source code resources, materials, everything is there, and each one is done pretty well. Moreover, something interesting that Chinaz does is the webmaster tools. Although many websites on the Internet provide webmaster tools, Chinaz's webmaster tools, integrated and designed, make me feel very comfortable.

Chinaz is suitable for building sites, building sites while looking at materials. Admin5 is suitable for web earning and grassroots information. Once your site is built, take a look and find ways to earn some money. Luowu is suitable for making friends and relaxing. Spend some time writing some good articles, fall behind a bit, then chat with three or five friends, YY a bit, and life is pretty pleasant.

These three websites each have their own advantages. I hope everyone can use them to bring themselves the hoped-for and imagined online life. Reprinted from www.kuziyuan.com
