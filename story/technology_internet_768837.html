WeChat discloses its open path: Official Accounts to connect with WeChat Pay

by anonymous on 2013-11-25 11:32:36

**November 18 News**: At today's WeChat Partner Communication Conference, Zhang Ying, Vice President of WeChat Product Department, provided a detailed interpretation of WeChat's open path and ecosystem blueprint. The public account open permissions will be further expanded and gradually integrated with the WeChat Pay process.

The core theme of this communication conference still revolves around openness: what is the overall open capability of WeChat, and what value it can bring to enterprises and users. These open capabilities include: the openness of the public platform, the openness of WeChat Pay, and the openness of pattern recognition capabilities, involving the openness methods of the WeChat Public Platform and WeChat Pay.

Since its launch in November 2010, WeChat has been online for three years, with 270 million active users. Since Tencent announced its open strategy in 2011, WeChat’s openness has also been on the agenda. In today's release session, WeChat introduced its open roadmap in detail. The open interfaces obtained by public accounts are gradually expanding, including integration with the WeChat Pay process.

### 1. Mobile Application Openness:

- Currently, nearly 20,000 apps are hosted within WeChat. The developer access process includes registration, filing, and invocation, with a very low threshold.

- Content shared within WeChat displays an information tail showing which application it comes from, facilitating circulation between social networks.

- Accumulated sharing among WeChat friends exceeds tens of billions of times.

### 2. Public Platform Openness:

- Currently, WeChat has over 2 million public accounts, with 8,000 new accounts added daily and more than 1 billion daily information interactions.

- The gap between subscription accounts and service accounts is widening: subscription accounts allow one daily group message, with messages displayed in the subscription account folder under Contacts - Subscription Account Folder; service accounts allow one monthly group message, with messages displayed in the chat list under Contacts - Service Account Folder.

- Each public account is like an app. WeChat is working hard to develop the connection capabilities of the public platform, where WeChat provides the stage and partners perform.

- High-level open capabilities include voice interface, customer service interface, web authorization interface, parameterized QR code generation interface, user geographical location acquisition interface, etc.

- Several types of public accounts were specifically mentioned: self-media in subscription accounts, finance, government affairs, carriers, and media in service accounts.

- Future plans for new technology openness include:

1. **WeChat Image Recognition Technology**: Merchants upload flat pictures and set intelligent AR display methods. In the future, the ability for public accounts to upload images will be opened up and seamlessly connected with the "Scan" function.

2. **Semantic Analysis Technology**: Provides public accounts with semantic parsing and intelligent customer service technology platforms, deeply customized for vertical fields.

3. **LBS Location Services**: Collaborates with Tencent Maps to provide LBS open services. Open interfaces include positioning, storage, search, WeiR Map, street view map, etc.

### 3. WeChat Pay Openness:

- WeChat Pay is not just about payment; it aims to achieve a closed-loop payment experience from online to offline: such as purchasing goods, following accounts, offline logistics, online information push, and repurchase.

- WeChat Pay currently includes three payment channels: public account payment, in-app payment, and scan code payment (online and offline).

- Comprehensive offline shopping experience: Taking the Pacific Coffee WeChat official account as an example, it realizes membership card binding, combining public account and scan code payment methods, and supports offline quick redemption via QR codes.

This event also featured a dedicated cooperation case display area, showcasing WeChat's potential from different dimensions such as payment and public accounts. This included offline retail company Youbao (one-yuan beverage purchase to experience WeChat Pay), Pacific Coffee (one-yuan coffee purchase via WeChat Pay); Meitu app (on-site lomo photo printing through WeChat); Tencent's e-commerce website YiXun (scan code payment experience), CaiFutong QQ Lottery (one-yuan lottery purchase via WeChat Pay); TV and video service provider FutureTV (controlling the TV with WeChat, purchasing paid programs with WeChat Pay), Haier Air Conditioning (controlling air conditioning with WeChat).

### Attached: WeChat Open Timeline

- January 26, 2011: WeChat 1.0 version released

- February 3, 2012: WeChat Official Accounts Platform project initiated

- August 17, 2012: WeChat Official Accounts Platform launched

- November 9, 2012: WeChat Official Accounts Platform message interface released

- November 21, 2012: WeChat Official Accounts Platform certification opened

- February 6, 2013: WeChat Official Accounts Platform review registration launched

- August 5, 2013: WeChat Official Accounts Platform divided into subscription accounts and service accounts, WeChat Pay launched

- October 29, 2013: New test version of WeChat Official Accounts Platform launched, nine advanced interfaces offered for free, along with a safer and stricter new certification system
