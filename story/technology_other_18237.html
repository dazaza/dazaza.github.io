Love offering or business炒作?

by ofree on 2007-04-30 17:19:13

Today, I received the latest Feedsky topic ad invitation. This time, it's a public service advertisement about charitable donations for Fang Yinyin. The main content is as follows:

"I am a fan of Deng Ning, last year's champion of 'Absolute Singing,' and I also love singing. I hope that after I get better, I can stand on the stage of 'Absolute Singing.'" This is the heartfelt voice of a special girl who signed up during the current wave of entries in Youku's online "network zone" for the new round of 'Absolute Singing.' Her name is Fang Yinyin. Although she shares the same youthful vitality as her peers and is also an enthusiastic lover of music and singing, she is a leukemia patient.

In previous talent shows, we have seen all kinds of contestants, but compared to them, Fang Yinyin's unwavering courage in pursuing her dreams moves and inspires us. To allow Yinyin to better receive treatment and participate in the competition, Youku, which has always upheld love and social responsibility, promises that if Fang Yinyin's video clip in the online zone of 'Absolute Singing' reaches a certain number of clicks, Youku will donate a corresponding amount based on the number of plays. For every view of Fang Yinyin's video by netizens, Youku will donate one cent to Fang Yinyin, encouraging and affirming her strength and bravery, and supporting her to walk further on the road to achieving her dream.

Fang Yinyin's participation, from one perspective, proves that 'Absolute Singing' is gaining increasing resonance in society with its sunny, energetic, and healthy theme. At the same time, the opening of the network zone also provides special contestants like her with opportunities to participate in the competition and many conveniences in terms of time and space. 'Absolute Singing' is like a big family; let's send more blessings and care to Fang Yinyin, wishing her a speedy recovery and hoping that she can shine brightly in 'Absolute Singing.'

To be honest, after reading the above introduction, the webmaster feels quite disgusted. Donating for leukemia patients is beyond reproach, and helping others is a traditional virtue of the Chinese nation. However, using acts of kindness as a means of commercial hype is despicable. Perhaps, in the eyes of 'Absolute Singing' and Youku, this is a "win-win-win" situation, but the webmaster doesn't think so...
