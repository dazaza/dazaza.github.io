Many of my friends think it's troublesome to make their own brine at home and prefer to use ready-made brine from supermarkets, like the Lee Kum Kee brand which I've tried and found to taste really good. However, making your own brine can be more suited to your taste and is super cost-effective. So, how do you make a pot of fragrant brine?

**Materials for producing brine: (based on 10 bowls of water)**

- Star anise (aniseed) - 3 pieces

- Cumin - 1 teaspoon (5ml)

- Pepper - 1 teaspoon (5ml)

- Licorice - 3 pieces

- Cinnamon - 1 piece

- Strawberry - 1 piece

- Dried tangerine peel - 1 piece

- Soy sauce - 1 tablespoon (15ml)

- Dark soy sauce - 1 tablespoon (15ml)

- Salt and crystal sugar - appropriate amount

- Fat pork skin - a small block

**Methods for producing brine:**

1. Place all spices in gauze or a one-time charge, or box them up.

2. Pour water into the pot, add all ingredients, and bring to a boil.

3. Simmer over low heat for about 40 minutes. Leaving it overnight is better for a fuller taste.

4. Remove the spice residue and the brine is ready to use. Brine can be reused and gets better with use. Add flavoring according to the situation. When not in use for a long time, filter it and store it in the refrigerator or freezer.

**Tips for producing brine:**

1. Adjust the brine spices according to your preferences, but star anise (aniseed) and cinnamon are indispensable. If you like Ma spicy, you can add chili, pepper, etc., referring to specific methods for making spicy duck necks.

2. Adding a small piece of pork fat makes the brine more fragrant. If stewing, there's no need to put meat in advance. For items like halogen peanuts, there's no need to put meat either, or peanut oil would kick in. If it's spiced corned egg, then putting a piece of meat can make the eggs more fragrant.

3. Brine can be reused and gets better with use. Add flavoring according to the situation. When not in use for a long time, filter it and store it in the refrigerator or freezer. It can be stored in the fridge for seven days and in the freezer for three months.

**Steps for making spiced corned egg:**

1. Boil water in a pot and gently place the eggs into the water. Cover and cook for 15 minutes.

2. Immerse the cooked eggs in ice water for easier peeling. After cooling, remove the egg shells.

3. Place the boiled eggs into the brine, heat and simmer for 30 minutes. The longer they stay in the brine, the tastier they become.
