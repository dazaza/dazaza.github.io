Mother holds her 1-year-old daughter to do double eyelids, saying that she doesn't want her child to lose at the starting line

by umanhua1 on 2012-03-07 10:29:30

Nowadays, in various plastic surgery institutions, it is commonplace to see mothers bringing their children for cosmetic consultations. But have you ever seen anything as shocking as this? Yesterday, at the Plastic Surgery Department of the City Emergency Center, a beauty-conscious mother, fearing her daughter would lose in the appearance race from the start, actually brought her one-year-old daughter to the hospital for double eyelid surgery. The mother's behavior was met with firm rejection and criticism from the doctors. Experts stated that plastic surgery must comply with the objective laws of human physiology, generally only being performed once the body has reached full development. Furthermore, plastic surgery reflects the aesthetic cognition and wishes of the individual; even parents do not have the right to impose their own aesthetic views on their children.

Chongqing Evening News reporter Li Kui and intern Li Yingting reported on this shocking case where a mother wanted to perform plastic surgery on her one-year-old baby. At around 10 a.m. yesterday, Director Wu Yi of the Plastic Surgery Department at the City Emergency Center had just finished a surgery when Xiao Mou and her nanny sat in the consultation hall with her just over one-year-old baby. Wu Yi thought Xiao Mou was recommending an acquaintance for surgery and greeted them to go to his office.

“Director Wu, look, Niu Niu (a pseudonym) has single eyelids, so ugly. Could you please help her with a double eyelid surgery?” As soon as they entered the office, before any pleasantries, Xiao Mou took her daughter from the nanny, pointed to Niu Niu’s eyes, and hoped Wu would personally perform the double eyelid surgery for her daughter.

"Is the child so young to undergo surgery?" Wu Yi said he initially thought Xiao Mou was joking. "Really, I'm serious," Xiao Mou earnestly explained that because her daughter had single eyelids, it not only made her eyes appear smaller but also became a significant flaw in her mind regarding her daughter's appearance. Therefore, she wanted to give Niu Niu a double eyelid surgery now, so when her daughter grew up and developed her own aesthetic concepts, she wouldn't worry about her single eyelids and would be more beautiful and confident among her peers.

"Your idea is outrageous; whether from ethical or medical professional aspects, we will not perform this surgery." After listening to Xiao Mou's explanation and thoughts about her daughter's plastic surgery, Wu Yi immediately rejected Xiao Mou's request.

To dissuade Xiao Mou from her thoughts, Wu Yi called Nurse Supervisor Fan Shimai to join him in educating Xiao Mou through a mix of humor and seriousness. He explained to Xiao Mou that from a medical perspective, double eyelid surgery is a process of mutual cooperation between the customer and the doctor. Niu Niu was too young to follow the doctor's instructions to blink variously; additionally, plastic surgery is generally performed once the body reaches full development. Niu Niu was only one year old, violating medical principles, and even if the surgery were performed, it would not achieve perfect results during future growth and development.

As a fellow woman, Nurse Supervisor Fan provided psychological counseling to Xiao Mou, criticizing her for holding such a concept and advising her that in future child education, she should focus more on cultivating her child's abilities and inner cultivation, which would benefit her child's physical and mental health.

After nearly half an hour of communication, Xiao Mou abandoned the idea of giving her daughter surgery and somewhat sheepishly admitted she had oversimplified the matter.

The Reason: This Mother is Obsessed with Beauty and Plastic Surgery

It was learned that Xiao Mou is 30 years old and lives in a high-end neighborhood in Yubei District. Due to her being an absolute beauty, she participated in multiple beauty pageants during her university days and after work and also modeled for some fashion magazines. Two years ago, after getting married, she became a housewife, taking care of her husband and teaching her child at home.

"Such an idea comes from her own love for beauty," according to Director Wu Yi, Xiao Mou underwent rhinoplasty at the department when she was still in university seven or eight years ago. After working, she underwent five or six large and small plastic surgeries, including malar reduction and liposuction. It was precisely because Xiao Mou herself was obsessed with plastic surgery that she conceived the idea of performing double eyelid surgery on her baby still in swaddling clothes.

"Hehe, it's all my fault for thinking things were too simple. I sincerely accept the criticism and education from the doctors and nurses." In the afternoon, Chongqing Evening News reporter contacted Xiao Mou by phone. She frankly admitted that her daughter was actually very cute, and the single eyelids were just a minor flaw in her mind. She simply wanted her daughter to become more beautiful and perfect from a young age.

Xiao Mou also indicated that through this incident, in future child education, she would pay more attention to methods and correctly guide her child.

Doctors Say: Parents Have No Right to Impose Aesthetic Views

Director Wu Yi reminded parents that clinically, except for some scar surgeries, minors are not recommended to undergo plastic surgery, especially major surgeries like malar reduction, breast augmentation, and jawline shaping.

Director Wu explained that from the perspective of physiological development, minors are in the peak developmental stage, and all organs have not yet stabilized, making plastic surgery inappropriate; from a medical ethics standpoint, plastic surgery pertains to personal aesthetic cognition. As parents, one cannot impose one's own aesthetic views on their children. If medical institutions inadvertently follow parental wishes and perform plastic surgery on children, it may cause greater psychological harm to the child.

Professor Mei Qixia of Chongqing Medical University's Psychology Department also believed that parents' desire for beauty is understandable, but if excessive, it can adversely affect the child's physical and mental health. For example, Xiao Mou's "appearance-first" mentality, if unaltered, could subconsciously influence her daughter in future education, leading her to believe that beauty is the most important thing and pursue superficial things excessively.

Plastic surgery and cosmetic procedures satisfy many people's desires for beauty, but this does not mean one can manipulate their bodies at will. Cosmetic surgery experts stated that cosmetic surgery must conform to the objective laws of human physiology, and different parts require appropriate timing and principles.

Optimal Ages for Various Types of Plastic Surgery:

● Eyelid Surgery

Double Eyelid Surgery: Requires local anesthesia, following the doctor's instructions to repeatedly open and close the eyes. This should be done after the child has sufficient recognition ability and psychological maturity. Additionally, as age increases and face shape changes, it can affect the shape of double eyelid surgery. It is recommended to wait until the age of 16 or older (after puberty).

Lower Eyelid Blepharoplasty: Common in men and women over 40, it is suggested to wait until after the age of 45, which can be combined with an eye lift procedure. Performing it too early can prematurely age the skin around the eyes, making it sag instead.

● Rhinoplasty

In childhood, the nose root is flat, the bridge is collapsed, and the small nose tip faces upward, commonly known as a saddle nose. Generally, as the child grows older, the nasal bone fully develops and lifts, and this deformity disappears accordingly. The appropriate time for saddle nose or low nose rhinoplasty is after the body has basically matured, usually recommended at the age of 18 or older.

● Ear Reconstruction Surgery

To avoid affecting normal psychological development in children, surgery is generally performed around the ages of 5-6 when the ear size is almost similar to that of adults, and the impact on development is minimal.

● Cleft Lip and Palate Repair

Patients should ideally complete cleft lip repair within 2-3 months after birth and palate repair within two years.

● Facial Wrinkle Removal

Suitable age is between 40-60 years. If considering injectable wrinkle removal, it is best not to start earlier than 37 years of age, as people begin to lose collagen that maintains facial fullness at this age.

● Breast Augmentation

Candidates for breast augmentation typically experience issues post-development, such as poor breast development, breast shrinkage after breastfeeding, or gender reassignment surgery. These candidates meet surgical age requirements. However, severe macromastia in underdeveloped teenage girls can also consider surgery.

● Liposuction

Surgical age is generally between 20-50 years. Minors in the growth and development phase are not suitable for this surgery. Additionally, those suffering from severe coronary heart disease, hypertension, pulmonary dysfunction, diabetes, or coagulation mechanism disorders are contraindicated for liposuction.

Sixty Percent of Children's Motivation for Plastic Surgery Comes from Parents

From the plastic surgery departments of several hospitals, including the City Emergency Center and Chongqing Medical University Affiliated Second Hospital, the reporter learned that Xiao Mou wanting to perform double eyelid surgery on her one-year-old daughter was merely an extreme case, but cases where parents hope their children become more perfect and lead them to plastic surgery are not uncommon.

It was understood that adolescent plastic surgery is becoming increasingly popular, especially during winter and summer vacations, when many plastic surgery institutions are exceptionally busy. Many high school students are accompanied by their parents to consult the hospital for various plastic surgeries such as double eyelid, rhinoplasty, and chin enhancement.

Plastic surgeons mentioned that in daily consultations, over sixty percent of children come to the hospital for plastic surgery due to encouragement from their parents. Especially for students preparing for college entrance exams, most parents hope to enhance their children's image and temperament through plastic surgery.
