Yahoo Shares Hit 5-Year High on Alibaba Profit Growth

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 15:55:22

On July 18, Beijing time, according to foreign media reports, boosted by a sharp rise in Alibaba Group's first-quarter performance, Yahoo!'s stock price surged 10.34% during regular trading on the Nasdaq Stock Exchange, closing at $29.66, marking its highest stock price since May 2008.

Yahoo! stated on Tuesday that Alibaba Group's net profit for the first quarter had tripled year-on-year, reaching $669 million; its revenue for the first quarter increased dramatically by 71%, reaching $1.38 billion. After selling nearly half of its stake in Alibaba Group last year, Yahoo! still holds approximately 24% of Alibaba Group's shares.

The robust performance of Alibaba Group favorably supported Yahoo!'s stock price. One year after Marissa Mayer became Yahoo!'s CEO, this company remains in the early stages of transformation. By focusing on launching new products, Yahoo! aims to attract advertisers and consumers from Google and Facebook. Analyst Mark Mahaney of RBC Capital Markets believes that the majority of Yahoo!'s stock price increase over the past year has been driven by the appreciation of its stakes in Alibaba Group and Yahoo! Japan.

Analyst Sameet Sinha of Riley & Co. said, "Alibaba Group is showcasing a significant amount of operating leverage. This company has built a massive market in China and is beginning to charge users." Yahoo!'s second-quarter earnings report released on Tuesday showed that due to a 12% year-on-year decline in display advertising revenue, the company's revenue excluding traffic acquisition costs (TAC) was $1.071 billion, down 1% year-on-year; net profit was $331 million, up 46% year-on-year. Yahoo! gained $225 million in profits from its stakes in Alibaba Group and Yahoo! Japan in the second quarter, higher than $180 million in the same period last year.

Skyrocketing Performance

Yahoo! announced on its official website on Tuesday that Alibaba Group's net profit for the first quarter tripled, increasing from $220 million in the same period last year to $669 million. This Hangzhou-based company may conduct its initial public offering by the end of this year.

The Asian assets held by Yahoo! also provided an opportunity for hedging against the depreciation of the yen exchange rate. Yahoo! stated that it generated $243 million in cash income in the second quarter due to hedging transactions. Yahoo! stated in May that it generated $270 million in pre-tax income in the first quarter through hedging its stake in Yahoo! Japan and $3 million in pre-tax income last year.

Yahoo! stated on Tuesday that its revenue excluding traffic acquisition costs would be between $1.06 billion and $1.1 billion in the third quarter. Bloomberg data showed that market analysts expected Yahoo!'s third-quarter revenue to be $1.12 billion.

CEO's Efforts

Bloomberg Industries analyst Paul Sweeney said that while Mayer strives to increase the number of Yahoo! users, investors must remain patient in expecting growth in Yahoo!'s advertising revenue. Sweeney said, "Mayer's investment in products to enhance user engagement and ultimately achieve revenue growth is a long-term endeavor. Judging from Yahoo!'s current performance expectations, these investments are unlikely to drive short-term gains in company performance."

Market research firm EMarketer estimates that the scale of the display advertising market reached $17.5 billion last year, with Yahoo!'s share declining from 9.2% last year to 7.9% this year. Google's share will increase by 3 percentage points to nearly 18%; Facebook's share is expected to grow by 2 percentage points to reach 17%.

Yahoo!'s earnings report showed that the company's display advertising business revenue declined by 12% year-on-year in the second quarter, decreasing to $472 million. Colin Gillis, an analyst at U.S. investment company BGC Partners, said: "In fact, Yahoo!'s difficulties with display advertising have just begun. We haven't yet seen what measures Yahoo! management can take to stop their declining trend."

Chain Reaction

Mayer likened her revival plan to a "chain reaction," stating that it would eventually lead to revenue growth for the company. During a live-streamed analyst conference call on Tuesday, Mayer said, "Hiring and retaining a great team; they will develop inspiring products that attract users and increase traffic; the increase in traffic can capture advertiser interest and ultimately translate into revenue. People come first, then products, followed by traffic, and finally revenue."

Yahoo! also lowered its revenue forecast for 2013 on Tuesday. Excluding traffic acquisition costs, Yahoo! expects fiscal year revenue to be up to $4.55 billion, lower than the $4.6 billion forecast in April and only slightly higher than last year's $4.47 billion. Analyst Sinha believed, "Yahoo! will experience another year without revenue growth."

Crazy Acquisitions

Driven by the sale of its stake in Alibaba Group, Mayer began a spree of acquisitions after taking office. During her tenure as Yahoo!'s CEO, Yahoo! has acquired at least 18 companies, including Tumblr for $1.1 billion, as well as mobile application developers Stamped, Jybe, and Summly, etc. Earlier this month, Yahoo! acquired Xobni, an address book application and plugin company, for about $70 million.

Yahoo! announced on Wednesday that it had acquired Admovate, an innovative company providing technology for buying and selling mobile ads. Admovate's four technical personnel will join Yahoo! to help the company generate revenue through advertisements displayed on smartphones and tablets.

In the second quarter, Yahoo! spent $653 million to repurchase 25.3 million shares of the company's stock, with an average repurchase price of $25.76 per share.

Yahoo!'s current stock price is close to $31, which was also the offer price when Microsoft initially made a takeover bid in 2008. Although Microsoft later raised the acquisition price per share to $33, Yahoo! still rejected Microsoft's acquisition offer. Affected by the reduction in the number of outstanding shares, Yahoo!'s current market capitalization is $31.8 billion, still far below the $47.5 billion acquisition price offered by Microsoft at the time.
