Quick and Easy Tricks for Buying and Selling on Paitao

Starting a business on Paipai is indeed a very good entrepreneurial project because it has a low starting point, simple operation, small start-up capital, and with the development of the network and the perfection of the platform, the number of online shopping people is gradually increasing, and the future development prospect is bright. Precisely for this reason, many people have chosen to go through this narrow bridge. Just this year, the number of sellers on Paipai has doubled compared to last year. This无形ly increases the pressure on each seller. Moreover, the goods on Paipai can be described as dazzling, almost everything. Similar products in the same industry are everywhere. And due to the special environment of the network, compared with traditional industries, the price of products is very transparent, and who is high and who is low can be seen immediately with just one click. In such a big environment, under such fierce competition, many people come to Paipai with great enthusiasm, but eventually "come with great expectations, leave with disappointment". So I want to say to the remaining sellers: "You are great!" Let's work together, believe that our tomorrow will be better. The competition among many heroes has not yet been decided, right! Of course, if you want to make a difference on Paipai and carve out a career with your own abilities, it is not that easy. First of all, you must have good psychological quality and have certain business concepts.

Many new sellers on Paipai come to Paipai with full confidence, wanting to manage their small shops well. However, after a month, the business is still cold and quiet, where did the customers go? I think they all queued up at the diamond and crown sellers' shops, hehe~ Although some new sellers also made a lot of efforts, often looking at some predecessors' excellent posts, learning from the experience of the predecessors, busy reading posts and replying posts all day, and many sellers are working hard to promote their shops and treasures. Frequently studying the excellent posts of the predecessors and avoiding detours is very worthy of affirmation and encouragement, and promotion is also indispensable. But if these two aspects of effort are still cold and quiet, you may need to think carefully whether there are still many things you haven't realized or haven't done.

As the saying goes: "To repel external enemies, first stabilize internally." If your own shop is not ready, you rush to promote it, no matter how much you promote it, although the popularity in the store is good and the browsing volume of the shop has increased, but ultimately only more people look, and few actually make purchases. It may excite you for a while, but it seems like a waste of effort. What is the reason? Like a vivid example that happened to me before, I asked my friend on Paipai to take a look at my shop and find out the reasons why my business was still cold after a month. After she looked at it, she sincerely and directly told me, "To be honest, from the perspective of a consumer, I didn't find any attractive places in your shop. I believe I wouldn't stay in your shop for more than a minute." This is the truth. No matter how much you promote, if the customers feel this way when they enter, all the efforts are wasted. Therefore, everyone must take care of their own shops before promoting.

1. Have your own characteristics

If you are from Shaoxing, you can sell pickled vegetables; if you are from Fuzhou, you can sell the three treasures of Fuzhou; if you are from Xinjiang, you can sell jade; if you are from Northeast China, you can sell chestnuts and ginseng, etc. Of course, this is just an example, mainly to tell everyone to make the products you operate distinctive, advantageous, talkative, and become a prominent landscape on Paipai. This way, others don't have what you have, how good is that, hehe~ If you can't achieve distinctive operations in terms of products, you can also start from the above points, Guangzhou VIP Information Technology Co., Ltd., work hard to do it well, and I believe it will be very helpful to everyone.

2. Products should be fashionable and trendy

Aim at the market hotspots, grab the products that general consumers like, and let the products speak for themselves, saving money and effort.

3. Pictures should be real and clear

Using the official website's advertising pictures is certainly attractive and effective, but after the buyer receives the goods, the actual result differs greatly from the imagination, which inevitably leads to a large psychological gap. I think bad reviews might accidentally visit your home. This is the reason why many new sellers complain that their things are so good, how could they still be unsatisfied, and the treasures of those diamond-level sellers are not necessarily better than mine. One aspect might be the reason! Clearer pictures require sellers to be more patient and spend more time thinking and researching. My photos taken by little brother are also embarrassing, shared with all sellers for mutual encouragement, hehe.

4. Higher quality, lower price

High-quality products are your best promotional tool and the most effective and fundamental guarantee to retain old customers. Imagine which buyer would remember buying a pair of shoes from your store that turned into crocodile skin after a week! And he definitely won't recommend to his friends that he bought a pair of shoes for ten dollars from a certain store, which is quite cheap, not bad, and can still be worn for a week. On one hand, people don't want others to know they are greedy for small bargains. Although your price may already be very low, even to the point of no profit, they won't care about these things. So the things you sell should rather be better, even if the price is higher, it's fine. Purchasing is also very important! Lower prices: Not making money, losing money for promotion, I think it's not impossible, depending on whether you consider it a long-term career. If you only want to make a little money or get short-term returns, I don't recommend it. As Ma Yun said, "Making money is actually very easy, as long as you do it big, the places and projects where you can make money now, you may not even know or understand them." Everything comes slowly. As long as your store has enough popularity and loyal customers, are you worried about not making money? At that time, just signing a contract with the courier company and getting some discounts on courier fees; just negotiating with the factory to get a lower price per piece than before, I think it will be enough. You can also apply for some big brands' agency... Isn't that great, hehe, personal opinion, it depends on how you view your Paipai. Is it to make a little money first or to expand your business? It's up to you.

5. More publicity, broader mindset

Publicity is an indispensable and crucial part of online stores. Regarding methods of publicity, the posts by predecessors have explained them in great detail, so I won't elaborate here. However, I found that many new sellers seem to be a bit impatient, which is understandable, but who doesn't hope that their hard publicity will show results quickly! But I want to say that publicity should be considered a long-term investment, whether in terms of manpower or financial resources. Perhaps the short-term benefits are not obvious, but in the long term, they will certainly be very significant. Think about the most familiar and funny advertisement around us: "This year, don't accept gifts during the festival, only accept Brain White Gold." When I first saw the advertisement, I thought in my heart that such a grammatically incorrect advertisement could be thought up and even dare to be broadcast on TV, hehe, but don't underestimate it, this is the genius of Shi Yuzhu, chairman of the Giant Group. With such publicity, people can easily remember it. Because of such advertising influence, when I go to large supermarkets to buy supplements for my parents, I choose Brain White Gold over Brain Gold because it sounds more familiar, and the advertisement is broadcast every day, indicating class, hehe...

6. Be more serious, have a better attitude

Here we are talking about service. Service is something everyone knows about, but implementing it can be said to vary from person to person. Since I used to work as a customer service representative in a listed company, I feel that service is really important. Maybe your product is not the best or the most cost-effective, but if you provide good service, I believe customers will return. When I first joined this company, it was just an unknown small company, but it employed 400-500 customer service personnel, and even more staff to handle problems in the back office. Eventually, it went public in Hong Kong in less than a year. I think what attracts customers is not just the product itself, but more importantly, the subsequent service. Why is Haier famous? Because its after-sales service is famous.

On Paipai, many new sellers seem to pay insufficient attention to it and do not do enough. In our competition with diamond and crown sellers, our greatest advantage and the one where we can fully leverage our strengths is service. We have more time and energy than diamond and crown sellers. In this era and social environment, I personally feel that service is our core competitiveness. Because in this era of rapid information network development, no product can be exclusively enjoyed by one person. Stockpiling has become outdated, akin to suicide. Your product and business model may be discovered and replicated by smart opponents, but your thoughtful service and the deep feelings established between you and the buyers cannot be replicated. As the great Chinese motivational master Chen Anzhi said, "Selling products is not as good as selling yourself," which means exactly this. Often, what buyers buy is not your product but the satisfactory service that pleases them. Such emotional offensives are everywhere nowadays. China Mobile also sends blessing messages to its customers during festivals. Even government agencies send caring words like "With the Spring Festival approaching, please take precautions against theft and fire, wishing everyone a happy Spring Festival." etc. Even these large groups and state organs understand the importance of serving with heart. Shouldn't we learn from them? Anyway, following the concept of "Customer First" is correct. Whether you want to or not, you must believe one thing: customers are your bread and butter. There is a very famous 20/80 rule in economics, which I believe everyone knows. That is, 80% of your total income comes from the 20% of major customers and old customers in your customer base. How to maintain these 20% of old customers and let them continuously bring you new customers depends on everyone's efforts!
