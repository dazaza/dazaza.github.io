Top Ten "Deadly" Habits of White-Collar Diets

by impark on 2007-11-03 12:18:31

Today, when I opened my personal email, I found a post sent by the former president of the Dalian Tongmen Association. I thought it was very good and would like to share it with everyone. Frequently, I hear people around me say: "What should I do? My blood lipid is abnormal again," "Someone's blood pressure is too high," "I've gained weight again, what should I do? The last physical examination showed that I already have fatty liver!" These problems may have also occurred to you. In fact, the root cause of these problems is "eating" —— Below, the editor lists for you the top ten health killers related to "eating": Deadly Habit 1: Eating too fast Deadly Reason: Increases gastrointestinal burden, leading to obesity. Many office workers eat their lunch in a very hurried state. Eating too fast means food is not chewed sufficiently, which is not conducive to the preliminary digestion of food and salivary amylase in the mouth, increasing the gastrointestinal burden; chewing time is too short, the vagus nerve remains excessively excited, over time, it is easy to become obese due to increased appetite. Countermeasure: Try to let your teeth and tongue move more slowly. If you really don't have much time, then sacrifice a little time from playing cards or taking a nap. Everyone knows that unhealthy eating habits can lead to terrible diseases, but often neglect some small habits after meals. Unbeknownst to us, these seemingly insignificant lifestyle flaws can very likely become fatal "killers" for your health... Deadly Habit 2: Skipping breakfast Deadly Reason: Severely damages the stomach, making you unable to work energetically, and also prone to looking older. Researchers at the University of Erlangen in Germany, after long-term tracking of 7,000 male and female subjects, found that 40% of them had the habit of skipping breakfast, and their lifespan was on average 2.5 years shorter than the remaining 60%. Another university, in a study of elderly people aged 80-90, found one of their common points of longevity was: eating a hearty breakfast every day. Countermeasure: Breakfast foods should be as tasty and appetizing as possible; there should be enough quantity and better quality; they should be small in volume but high in energy; preparation should be time-saving and labor-saving. We know that different foods stay in the stomach for varying lengths of time, and their blood sugar responses are also different. Under normal circumstances, mixed solid and liquid foods can stay in the stomach for 4-5 hours, while liquid foods, due to their large volume, feel very full just after eating, but stay in the stomach for a short time. The nutrients do not have enough time to be fully digested before being expelled, and the rising blood sugar levels soon drop, unable to last long. Therefore, when choosing food, it is necessary to pay attention to combining dry and wet foods and having a balanced diet of meat and vegetables. Deadly Habit 3: Too rich dinners Deadly Reason: In the evening, the insulin content in the blood reaches its peak for the day. Insulin can convert blood glucose into fat, which is condensed on the walls of blood vessels and the abdominal wall. Eating too rich dinners over a long period will make people obese. Moreover, such lengthy and rich dinners disrupt the body's normal biological clock, making it easy for people to suffer from insomnia. For working people, dinner almost becomes the main meal of the day. Breakfast depends on the "clock," lunch depends on the "work," only in the evening can they truly relax, sitting steadily at the dining table, enjoying a hearty meal. Countermeasure: First, dinner should be eaten early. Eating dinner early can greatly reduce the incidence of urinary tract stones. Second, dinner should be simple. Dinner should definitely be vegetarian-oriented, mainly consisting of carbohydrate-rich foods.
