Home maintenance_How to maintain a carpet

by fowqn43533 on 2012-02-07 21:44:38

Among home flooring decoration materials, carpets are becoming increasingly popular due to their many advantages such as luxurious appearance and comfortable foot feel. However, during use, people often encounter headaches caused by some disadvantages of carpets, such as easy dirtiness, difficulty in cleaning, fading, lack of pressure resistance, and easy marking. To maintain the long-lasting beauty and cleanliness of carpets, we can adopt a series of professional carpet maintenance methods.

Firstly, prevention is the most important. Appropriate preventive protection can prevent carpets from getting dirty, avoid fiber erosion and wear, and keep carpets clean and durable. We can lay combined anti-pollution mats at entry and exit points to isolate outdoor mud and dirt outside the door, keeping indoor carpets clean and free from wear. At the same time, using carpet stain repellents sprayed on carpets can prevent water, oil stains, or dust from penetrating into the fibers, making carpet cleaning easier.

Accidents happen, spilling onto the carpet is common. In such cases, it is best to "strike while the iron is hot" and immediately use carpet stain removers to remove the stains before they accumulate and penetrate deeper into the fibers, causing future cleaning difficulties. There are various specific cleaning methods: A certain proportion of flour, refined salt, and gypsum powder can be mixed with water into a paste, cooled, shaped into small pieces, sprinkled on the dirty parts of the carpet, and then scrubbed with a hard brush until it turns into powder, which can then be vacuumed clean. Grease on the carpet can be wiped off with cotton dipped in high-purity gasoline. Coffee, cocoa, and tea stains can be removed with one spoon of water mixed with one spoon of glycerin. Fruit juice stains can be removed with cold water mixed with a small amount of ammonia water. Ink stains can be wiped with lemon acid or lemon juice, then washed with water, and finally dried with a towel.

We must also pay attention to the fact that carpets should not get damp. Therefore, ensure the floor is completely dry before laying the carpet, and avoid excessive moisture during cleaning.

If the carpet has been used for a long time and its color is no longer bright, you can restore its vividness by spreading salt on the carpet the night before and removing it with a clean damp cloth the next morning, which will restore the carpet's vibrant colors.

After implementing these maintenance measures, our carpets will surely remain fresh over time. Believe me, give it a try!

How to Maintain Tiles

For a long time, consumers have had a misconception about tile products, believing they are easy to clean and maintain. This idea is entirely wrong, as tile products, especially polished tiles, are the easiest to get contaminated and require careful maintenance.

Polished tiles have very few pores before polishing. During the polishing process, the surface is affected by diamond planers and various coarse and fine grinding heads, severely damaging the original structure of the tile and forming numerous microscopic capillaries invisible to the naked eye. Once pollutants seep in, cleaning becomes a very difficult task.

So, how do you maintain tiles?

(1) After laying polished tiles during home renovation but before use, to avoid damage to the tile surface from other construction projects, cover them with non-fading items like textile bags for protection.

(2) If there are slight scratches on the tiles, toothpaste can be used for treatment. Apply toothpaste around the scratch and wipe repeatedly with a clean cloth.

(3) When cleaning daily, use a dry mop as much as possible, minimize the use of wet mops, and clean dirtier or stained areas with household cleaners like dish soap or laundry detergent. Depending on usage, apply floor wax regularly or irregularly, then after drying, polish with a dry cloth to keep the tile surface shiny and new.

How to Maintain Wooden Floors

In home flooring renovations, wooden floors are becoming increasingly popular. Selecting high-quality wood is crucial. However, as the saying goes, "three parts material, seven parts maintenance." After solving the problem of selecting wooden floors, the next step is learning maintenance techniques. When visiting friends and family, you may notice that some wooden floors used for six or seven years still look brand new, while others used for only two or three years already show numerous issues. The secret lies in the word "maintenance."

To enjoy the comfort and warmth unique to wooden floors for longer, here are several key points to focus on during use:

(1) For houses with installed wooden floors that remain unoccupied for extended periods, avoid covering them with plastic sheets or newspapers. Over time, the lacquer film may become sticky and lose its shine;

(2) Avoid direct sunlight exposure on the floors to prevent localized drying, contraction, cracking, deformation, or premature aging, discoloration of the paint. Paint exposed to ultraviolet rays may also crack and age prematurely;

(3) Keep the floor dry and clean, avoiding contact with large amounts of water. If water accidentally spills on the floor, wipe it clean immediately with a soft dry cloth. Do not clean with alkaline water, soapy water, or other corrosive liquids to avoid damaging the paint film. Use a slightly damp pure cotton mop for wiping; if there are stains, use steel wool for scrubbing, and avoid using wet mops, gasoline, flammable items, or high-temperature liquids for cleaning.

(4) Sweep the floor regularly and vacuum to prevent sand or abrasive dust accumulation that can scratch the floor surface. Place a shoe mat at the entrance to avoid bringing sand or abrasive dust indoors.

(5) Wax the floor every so often, depending on the gloss of the painted surface. First, dust off the floor, wipe it clean with a slightly damp cloth, and apply wax. Then, evenly apply the floor wax on the surface and let it dry slightly. Finally, rub the floor back and forth with a dry cotton cloth until it is smooth and shiny.

(6) Solid wood floors fear "wear" the most. Frequent walking inevitably wears down the wooden floor. Choose soft-soled slippers. Also, avoid scratching the floor with heavy metal objects, glass shards, shoe nails, etc., and avoid dragging furniture across the floor. Avoid stepping on cigarette butts, placing acidic or alkaline objects, or directly placing high-temperature objects like kettles or stoves on the floor.

(7) Try to prevent humid bathroom gases or various kitchen gases from lingering in rooms with wooden floors for long periods.

(8) Frequently open windows for ventilation to refresh the indoor air and disperse humidity.

How to Maintain Stone Materials

The unpredictable "composition" and unique "strength" of stone deeply captivates people's attention. From house construction to interior and exterior decoration, stone has always been loved by people. However, ordinary consumers know very little about its maintenance.

There are two types of stone: marble and granite. Laying stone in living rooms, dining rooms, and corridors creates a beautiful and luxurious atmosphere. Cleaning stone floors is relatively simple. Simply wipe with a damp cloth wrung out, but never wash directly with cleaning agents or soapy water, as this can damage the surface luster of the stone. Although the surface of the stone is smooth and hard, it should be regularly coated with a protective film, such as applying floor wax evenly and wiping clean with a dry cloth. As stone is a natural material, some stones may appear yellowish after long-term use. If obvious yellow spots appear, place a cloth or paper towel soaked with industrial-grade hydrogen peroxide over the area, and the yellow spots will gradually fade. Finally, wipe clean with a cloth.

How to Maintain Wooden Furniture

Experienced consumers all know that wooden furniture has a lacquered surface, and maintaining wooden furniture accurately means maintaining and preserving the lacquer finish. Regardless of the type of lacquer finish on the wooden furniture, maintenance and preservation generally involve the following aspects:

(1) The placement of furniture is very important. It should not be placed under direct sunlight or close to heat sources like furnaces or air conditioners, nor near sinks, and definitely not in uncovered ventilated areas. These conditions can easily cause local cracking, deformation, and changes in the quality of the lacquer finish.

(2) Maintain a relatively stable humidity level in the indoor air. Excessive absorption of moisture can lead to furniture swelling and deformation, while excessive dryness can cause furniture to crack. Humidity adjustment can be done through air conditioning dehumidification, window opening for humidity exchange, mopping floors, and wiping furniture to increase humidity, or through planting flowers and grass to adjust dryness and humidity levels.

(3) Do not let hard metal objects or other sharp tools collide with wooden furniture, which could leave marks. Once the lacquer finish is damaged, it is almost impossible to repair it. If scratches or dents appear, they can be covered by applying shoe polish of a similar color using a cotton ball or brush.

(4) Do not soak furniture surfaces with acidic, alkaline, or salt solutions, and do not let hot teacups, cigarettes, or irons touch the furniture surfaces.

(5) Regularly use soft cotton cloths to wipe away dust from the furniture surface along the grain direction. Before dusting, dip the soft cloth in a cleaning agent. Note that dry cloths should not be used to avoid scratching the surface. Occasionally, use a damp cotton thread wrung out to carefully wipe the accumulated dust in corners of the wooden furniture, then thoroughly wipe the entire piece of furniture, and finally dry it with a clean, soft, fine cotton cloth. You can also apply a thin layer of high-quality furniture wax after drying to enhance the furniture's luster and preserve it.

Although different lacquer finishes vary, the maintenance methods are largely the same. Take good care of your furniture, allowing it to stand firmly and beautifully in your home!

How to Remove Small Scratches on Furniture

When the lacquer surface of furniture develops various "scratches," they should be promptly eliminated to avoid leaving permanent marks. Here are a few common methods to remove "scratches" from furniture.

(1) Scratches

If furniture is accidentally scratched but the underlying wood beneath the lacquer is not touched, a small amount of melted wax liquid can be applied with a cotton cloth to cover the scratch. Once the wax hardens, another layer can be applied, repeating this process several times until the scratch is less noticeable.

(2) Burn Marks

If the lacquer on furniture is scorched by cigarette butts or unextinguished matches, but the underlying wood is not burned, use a small piece of fine-textured hard cloth wrapped around a chopstick head to gently scrub the burn mark. Then apply a thin layer of wax liquid, and the scorch mark will disappear.

(3) Heat Marks

Placing hot containers directly on wooden furniture can leave white circular scars. Generally, these can be wiped off immediately. However, if the heat mark is deep, lightly apply ordinary iodine alcohol or petroleum jelly on the spot, and after two days, gently wipe it with a soft cloth to remove the heat mark. Alternatively, use a soft cloth dipped in alcohol, perfume, or kerosene, and wipe forcefully. Afterward, clean the white heat mark with a soft cloth dipped in water, and then apply a layer of furniture wax.

How to Maintain Leather Goods

Leather has strong absorbency, so it is important to prevent contamination, especially for high-end suede leather.

(1) Taboos

① Avoid collisions with sharp objects.

② Prevent animals from scratching the leather.

③ Avoid exposing leather to direct sunlight or near heating devices. Keep it away from heat sources and avoid direct sunlight exposure.

(2) Cleaning

① For daily cleaning, use a clean cloth dipped in a small amount of detergent solution, wipe to remove dust and dirt, then rinse with clean water one or two times. When the water is eight-tenths dry, evenly apply leather cleaning and shining agent, which can make the leather look like new and prevent it from becoming dry and brittle over time, or even cracking and being damaged.

② If beverages are spilled on the leather, immediately use a clean cloth or sponge to absorb it, and then wipe with a damp cloth, allowing it to air dry naturally. Never use a hairdryer to dry it.

③ If grease adheres, use a dry cloth to wipe it clean. Let the remaining grease dissipate naturally or clean it with a cleaning agent, but do not use water to wipe it.

④ Do not use strong cleaning products (such as gasoline, turpentine, etc.) to clean sofas.

How to Clean Indoor Decorations

(1) Ceramic Statues

Hold the ceramic statue vertically to clear dust. Use a makeup brush with long soft bristles, dipped in warm dishwashing detergent or soapy water solution. Start brushing from the top down. Then rinse with warm water in the same way and dry on a paper towel.

(2) Glass and Porcelain Decorations

Place them in a washing basin, spray window liquid cleaner, rinse and dry on a towel.

(3) Dried Flowers and Artificial Flowers

Dust on dried flowers and artificial flowers can be blown away using a hairdryer set to the lowest setting.

(4) Photos and Paintings

To clean the glass on photo frames and paintings, spray window liquid cleaner on a cloth and wipe it, not directly on the glass. This prevents the cleaner from seeping into the frame. Alcohol can also be used for cleaning. Gently wipe the glass, and if the picture is not securely mounted, vigorous rubbing may blur the image. Most modern metal or wooden frames can be wiped with a damp cloth, but extra care should be taken with finely gilded frames, as real gold leaf is very thin and can easily rub off. Use an extremely soft dry brush to dust.

If the frame is not too heavy, take it down to clean. It's easier to clean on a flat surface and less likely to damage the wall.

How to Clean Walls and Ceilings

Cleaning methods for walls and ceilings can be summarized into two categories: water-based cleaning and non-water-based cleaning. For water-based cleaning, including wooden panel walls, colored tile walls, and wooden ceilings, cleaning can be done with a damp cloth dipped in diluted soapy water, without excessive force to avoid damaging the surface. For non-water-based cleaning, including plaster walls and wallpapered walls, simply use a feather duster to remove dust. Regardless of the type of wall, when dirt accumulates, avoid vigorously scrubbing to prevent wall damage. The best method is to mix 1 cup of alcohol with 1 teaspoon of cleaner, spray it on the dirtied area with a sprayer, then cover it with a hot towel, and the dirt will come off easily.

Which cleaning supplies should households prepare?

Living at home is a long-term endeavor, and we need a warm, clean home to wash off the dust from the outside and rest our weary bodies. If you love your family, have you prepared the necessary cleaning supplies for your home?

Firstly, you should prepare basic cleaning supplies. Old cotton shirts or old velvet fabrics can be repurposed into general-purpose rags. Additionally, prepare a batch of rags for wiping and polishing different surfaces. The fabric should be sturdy, soft, absorbent, and non-fading. Prepare sturdy brushes and brooms. For quick everyday mopping, you should also have a sturdy mop. Nowadays, stores sell extendable metal handles that can adjust length, which work well for cleaning kitchen and bathroom grease and water stains. Consider buying one as a backup. Also, buy a pair of plastic gloves to protect your hands from dirt or cleaning agents.

Cleaning agents are also essential household items, such as dishwashing detergent, kitchenware cleaner, furniture, floor, and glass cleaner, toilet cleaner, leather, and fur clothing cleaner, etc.

Many kitchen items can also serve as cleaning agents, effectively cleaning various furniture surfaces.

(1) Baking Soda

Mix baking soda powder (on a damp cloth) with water to form a paste or solution, which can clean ceramics, stainless steel, refrigerators, freezers, ovens, iron plates, and plastic furniture.

(2) Lemon Juice

Pure or diluted lemon juice can remove tarnished copper surfaces, mineral deposits, rust spots, and stains on marble and plastic countertops.

(3) Methanol Alcohol

Used to clean window glass, mirror surfaces, bookshelf glass, and to remove adhesive residue left after peeling off labels. However, methanol alcohol is flammable and highly toxic, so it should be stored out of reach of children.

(4) White Vinegar

Pure vinegar or a vinegar-water solution can clean windows, glass surfaces, tiles, wooden furniture, remove mineral deposits from faucets, toilets, and sinks, and clean kettle stains.

(5) Salt

Salt works well for cleaning cups and kettle stains, leaving them shiny and new.

What regulations should construction units follow during construction?

According to Article 11 of the Ministry of Construction's "Management Measures for Residential Interior Decoration and Renovation," decoration and renovation enterprises engaged in residential activities must comply with construction safety operating procedures and take necessary safety precautions as required. They must not arbitrarily use fire or perform welding operations, ensuring the safety of workers and surrounding housing and property. Additionally, Article 15 of the "National Family Management Measures for the Interior Decoration Industry" stipulates that family decoration design units and construction enterprises must adhere to the following regulations: strictly implement national or industry-prescribed indoor decoration quality standards; strictly enforce national or relevant departmental regulations concerning building safety and fire prevention, prohibiting destruction of load-bearing structures or building appearances or significantly increasing loads, not using flammable or harmful materials to human health, and not arbitrarily dismantling gas, heating, electrical, and other house equipment without approval; strictly enforce national or relevant departmental regulations concerning environmental protection, taking measures to control dust, exhaust fumes, and noise, not hindering the normal lives of surrounding residents, and ensuring personal safety.

Therefore, construction units must consciously abide by the aforementioned regulations during construction.

How should neighbors be handled during the renovation process?

A new home is so desirable, but unfortunately, there's the renovation! This is the lament of most people. Sometimes, even newly acquainted neighbors turn into enemies due to renovation, making encounters awkward! To avoid such embarrassing situations, there are ways to handle matters during the construction phase.

Construction noise is unavoidable. Avoid construction during the evening when neighbors are resting. Some construction teams rush the schedule, renovating day and night, which will undoubtedly upset the neighbors.

Do not litter garbage in the hallway. If it falls during transportation, clean it up promptly; do not pile garbage outside for convenience, blocking the hallway.

Do not destroy the existing structure during construction. Some construction teams inadvertently drill through the floor while installing wooden floors, turning the ceiling below into a honeycomb-like mess; some create wall backgrounds by drilling through walls into neighboring homes... All these are undesirable. Therefore, consider carefully during design and construction, anticipate potential problems, and avoid unpleasant situations beforehand.

Be polite during construction. When there are many people and the elevator is busy, temporarily refrain from using the elevator to transport materials. If any unpleasant situations occur, apologize to the neighbors proactively, fostering a harmonious and pleasant atmosphere.

Most importantly, ensure the quality of the construction site, especially the water and electrical systems. Do not allow the bathroom to leak onto the floor below shortly after renovation, ruining the newly installed ceiling. Repeated repairs can be very troublesome. Or, during the renovation process, constant seepage can greatly inconvenience and frustrate the neighbors.

In summary, besides considering the owner, the construction team should also think of others, making them realize that renovation isn't as troublesome as imagined.

Who should handle renovation waste?

According to Article 16 of the Ministry of Construction's "Management Measures for Residential Interior Decoration and Renovation," implemented on May 1, 2002: The renovator, or the renovator and the decoration company, should sign a residential interior decoration management service agreement with the property management unit regarding issues such as the disposal of waste.

Based on the above provisions, we believe that who handles the renovation waste can be negotiated between the renovator (home user) and the decoration company. If there is no negotiation or if negotiations fail, the renovator should handle the waste according to relevant regulations.

What to do if the construction unit delays the project without reason?

According to Article 24 of the Ministry of Construction's "Management Measures for Residential Interior Decoration and Renovation," the renovator and the decoration company should sign a residential interior decoration contract. This contract clearly specifies the start and completion times of the decoration project, liability for breach of contract, and dispute resolution channels. Therefore, once the contract is signed, both parties should fully fulfill the rights and obligations stipulated in the contract. If the construction unit does not execute according to the contract and delays the project without reason, the home user (renovator) can, based on the contract, require the construction unit to bear liability for breach of contract.

How to prevent the contractor from substituting inferior materials and cutting corners?

When doing renovations, nothing is more feared than the construction unit substituting inferior materials and cutting corners, and such incidents are quite common in the renovation industry, with the majority of victims being consumers. So, how can one prevent the construction unit from substituting inferior materials and cutting corners? As the homeowner, whether during the pre-renovation inspection or during the renovation process, one must be meticulous.

First, when choosing a decoration company, be very cautious. Do not choose companies where the decoration company and the construction team have a subcontracting relationship. If it is a subcontracting relationship, by the time it reaches the last subcontractor, your renovation budget has been reduced significantly after multiple layers of deductions. To make a profit, the final contractor can only resort to deceiving customers, substituting inferior materials, and cutting corners.
