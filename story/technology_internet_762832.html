Zhengzhou workers outside the production line

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 17:35:52

Labor costs are a major part of business expenses. To cut labor costs, companies can either lay off employees or relocate to areas with lower salary levels. Foxconn has done just that, moving from Shenzhen to Zhengzhou, where Zhengzhou has gradually become the new manufacturing hub. In this area, factories operate 24 hours a day producing international goods like the iPhone. Here, young Chinese workers refuse to be mere worker ants.

Near the factory, there is a bar where people of all kinds come at night to listen to lively disco music and dance their bodies away. Lian Yulong (音), a 19-year-old inspector working on the iPhone motherboard at Foxconn, is a regular visitor to the bar. "Dancing helps me vent my anger and stress. Whenever I'm here, I forget about everything else." He uses dancing to forget the monotonous and dull reality of his days.

An increasing number of recreational spaces attract workers back to the assembly line. These workers manufacture products that others crave but must endure hardships themselves. Since opportunities are rare, their rest time is precious, and now they endure the present for a better future. Every worker looks forward to the weekend, even if some have simple plans. For instance, Bai Si Hai (音), a 24-year-old worker, plans to indulge in gaming online and then call his girlfriend who lives far away.

Now, supervisors in the factory are beginning to see the benefits of vacations. In recent years, "worker suicides" have been an ongoing shadow over enterprises, whether it's Foxconn or Samsung's factories. After several reports of "sweatshops," facing external pressure, Foxconn improved worker benefits and promised not to make them work overtime. However, after another suicide incident at the Zhengzhou factory, Foxconn initiated a "silent mode," prohibiting workers from discussing non-work-related topics during work hours. Despite Foxconn's public claim that such regulations were canceled under external scrutiny, workers say things remain the same as before.

The domestic factory environment cannot compare with foreign ones. Overseas workers enjoy high subsidies and various welfare and entertainment options like haircuts, climbing walls, and table football. As for offices, they are luxurious. Domestic workers wear uniform clothing and listen to routine scolding and yelling from their supervisors. Dormitories contain rooms with eight hard metal bed frames and a combined shower and toilet room, nothing more.

Perhaps because of this, there is a large market in front of the factory gate. As the sun sets, Mandarin pop music drifts out from hair salons, DVDs are sold on the street along with sliced watermelons and roses covered in glitter. A flatbed truck full of colorful teddy bears catches the attention of young girls. Some boyfriends, at the request of their girlfriends, immediately take out 10 yuan RMB to buy one. At this market, there are vans with artistic tattoos, large arcade games. Young workers drink light beer and continuously smoke cigarettes.

Sometimes, elaborately dressed singers will perform on nearby stages. As for the youth, they find sufficient entertainment in strong liquor. Luo Haojie (音) left the factory in May, quitting his job making iPhone 5 components. He said, "Our factory supervisor is malicious." Speaking to a circle of friends drinking with him, he complained about the unbearable food in the factory cafeteria.

Ultimately, he will look for another job, but for now, he enjoys his youth, drinking with friends and dating girls. Luo says, "I stayed because of my buddies. Without them, I would be miserable."

Summer is the off-season for Chinese factories, allowing many workers to enjoy holidays on weekends, sometimes even double days off. The variety of entertainment near the factory satisfies their needs. At night, mobile dining stalls set up with names resembling KFC. Acrobats perform, and someone blows fireballs. Workers say it would be better if you came a few months earlier when the acrobat troupe had real lions and tigers.

On Saturday, Zhou Peng Zheng (音), another iPhone 5 motherboard tester, wearing roller skates costing thousands of yuan, carefully avoided plants and began spinning. This pair of skates equals one-third of his monthly salary. He said, "It feels like I'm flying." Before the crowd grew, he quickly skated another lap.

Roller skating and inline skating have gradually formed a cultural atmosphere among those striving at Foxconn. Several teams have been formed, and on weekends, they put on their skates and glide through the city. Fang Tiema (音), 17, joined a hundred-person skating team named "Shadows" soon after starting work at Foxconn. However, due to her age, she couldn't work overtime and left the factory. She said, "I used to come to the skating rink twice a week, now I come every night."

At 11 PM, the performers have gone, and clock hotels start getting busy. "We work every day, why not go on dates?"
