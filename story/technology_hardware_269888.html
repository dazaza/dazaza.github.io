Skills for Grasping Short-term Selling Points

I. Deciding selling points based on the position of K-line charts and moving average lines:

1. The moving average line shows a large upward trend.

2. After a period of increase, the moving average line starts to flatten out.

3. When the stock price falls below the flattened moving average line from above, it is a sell signal.

4. To verify the reliability of the signal shown by one moving average line, multiple moving average lines can be selected for cross-referencing. Meanwhile, under normal circumstances, trading volume will also decrease correspondingly, making the sell signal clearer.

II. Deciding selling points based on the position of K-line charts and moving average lines:

This signal has the following characteristics:

1. First, confirm that the moving average line is in a declining process.

2. Then, confirm that the stock price breaks through the moving average line from below.

3. After breaking through the moving average line, the stock price immediately retreats and falls back below the moving average line, which is a sell signal.

4. This sell signal should be examined during the rebound after the stock price falls, i.e., when the stock price rises to about 1/3 of the previous fall during the decline and then shows a downward trend again, breaking below the moving average line should prompt a sell action.

5. If this sell signal appears multiple times during the market downturn, it's better to offload as early as possible or make short-term hedging.

III. Deciding selling points based on the position of K-line charts and moving average lines:

This signal has the following characteristics:

1. The moving average line is gradually declining.

2. After fluctuating below the moving average line for a period, the stock price begins to rise more obviously towards the moving average line.

3. When approaching the moving average line, the stock price immediately retreats and slides down; this is when the sell signal appears.

4. If this signal appears after the stock price has fallen to a considerable extent, it should be used as a reference rather than a reason for immediate selling. If this signal appears when the stock price hasn't fallen much, it can be considered as a sign that the time to sell has come. This situation should be treated differently.

IV. Deciding selling points based on the position of K-line charts and moving average lines:

This signal has the following characteristics:

1. Confirm that the moving average line is in an upward process.

2. The stock price rises sharply above the moving average line (Sina Stock), and the deviation from the moving average line increases.

3. Calculate the lower deviation rate. If the obtained lower deviation rate has reached 30%-50%, and the stock price begins to reverse sharply and fall while the distance from the moving line decreases, this is a sell signal.
