Rulin Life changes its president, and Lu Weiding takes the helm in a low-key manner

by zjsr2010 on 2010-07-01 13:30:38

There was no press conference, no inauguration speech, not even a notice on the official website about the change of the company chairman. However, a recent announcement by the China Insurance Regulatory Commission (CIRC) revealed the identity of the new chairman of Minsheng Life.

On June 29th, the CIRC published on its official website an "Announcement Regarding the Approval of Qualifications for Lu Weiding and Others." The announcement stated that Lu Weiding's qualifications to serve as the chairman of Minsheng Life were approved, with the approval date being June 21st.

Since the beginning of this year when Fosun exited, the Wanxiang Group, which actually holds nearly 36% of shares, has finally stepped forward officially.

The settlement of control disputes: Wanxiang steps forward

In February this year, the CIRC announced that it agreed to Zhejiang Hangmin Industrial Group Co., Ltd. and Tonglian Capital Management Co., Ltd. taking over the 458 million shares of Minsheng Life held by China Oceanwide Holdings Group Co., Ltd.

The above transferees are all related companies of the Wanxiang Group. Tonglian Capital was originally named Shenzhen Wanshang Investment Management Co., Ltd., which is the capital operation flagship under the Wanxiang Group and a sister company to Wanshang Financial Co., Ltd.; while the Wanxiang Group is the second largest shareholder of Hangmin Share, a listed company under Zhejiang Hangmin.

Since then, after years of equity disputes, Oceanwide Holdings ultimately chose to exit, allowing Wanxiang to maintain its position as the largest shareholder.

This equity dispute began in 2007 when Wanxiang and Oceanwide were once both ranked as the first shareholders of Minsheng Life. However, from the second half of 2007 to early 2008, Minsheng Life underwent two rounds of capital increase, raising its registered capital to RMB 2.7 billion. After this capital increase, Wanshang Finance held 461 million shares, accounting for 17.09%, becoming the largest shareholder; Oceanwide Holdings held 458 million shares, accounting for 16.96%, ranking second. It was speculated that the multiple changes in equity were one of the important reasons for Oceanwide's intention to withdraw.

After Oceanwide's formal withdrawal, currently Zhejiang Hangmin holds 243 million shares of Minsheng Life, accounting for 8.98%; Tonglian Capital holds 268 million shares, with a holding ratio of 9.91%. Adding the 17.09% of shares held by Wanshang Finance, the Wanxiang Group controls nearly 36% of Minsheng Life's equity, firmly maintaining its position as the largest shareholder. Shanxi Haijin Steel Group and China Nonferrous Metal Construction Co., Ltd. hold 14% and 13.71% respectively, making them the second and third largest shareholders.

In fact, there had been indications earlier that last August, after the original president of Minsheng Life, Duan Jingquan, resigned and joined Fortis Life, Lu Weiding, as the vice chairman of Minsheng Life, had already taken over the actual operational authority and became the de facto helmsman.

According to data from the CIRC, Minsheng Life achieved a total premium income of RMB 4.857 billion last year, a year-on-year decrease of 22.63%. However, in the first four months of this year, the company's cumulative premium income reached RMB 2.815 billion, a year-on-year increase of 58.68%.

Holding 11 financial licenses: Wanxiang outlines its financial blueprint

The appointment of Lu Weiding as chairman of Minsheng Life and his appearance on stage is merely one piece of the puzzle in Wanxiang Group's construction of its financial blueprint. The "Wanxiang System" controlled by Lu Guanqiu has been involved in the financial industry for a long time.

As early as the beginning of 2001, the Wanxiang Group established China Wanxiang Holding Company Limited as a platform to build the Wanxiang financial empire. Previously established specialized entities engaged in investment banking business - Shenzhen Tonglian (1995), Wanxiang Leasing (1996), Wanxiang Futures (1999), and Tonglian Venture Capital (originally Wanxiang Venture Capital, renamed in 2004) - were gradually integrated under Wanxiang Holding.

In May of the same year, the Wanxiang Group, through its wholly-owned overseas subsidiary - Wanxiang America Corporation, formally invested in and participated in the U.S.-based Horton Insurance Group, marking Wanxiang's first foray into the insurance industry. Both parties also jointly established the Wanxiang-Horton Insurance Brokerage Company in Chicago, USA, with Wanxiang obtaining 51% controlling interest.

Subsequently, Wanxiang's moves into the financial sector became more frequent. In 2002, they established Zhejiang's first finance company, Wanxiang Finance. That same year, they invested RMB 1.2 billion in Minsheng Life, holding 14.45% of the equity, ranking alongside the Oceanwide Group as the largest shareholders.

In 2003, the Wanxiang Group acquired 24.85% of the shares of Zhejiang Provincial Industry Trust Investment Co., Ltd., becoming the second largest shareholder. In 2004, Wanxiang Holding participated in Zhejiang Merchant Bank.

"The financial insurance industry, as an emerging industry awaiting development with broad market prospects, has always been within Wanxiang's expansion vision," Lu Guanqiu previously told the media. According to currently available public information, they have invested in six financial institutions, directly or indirectly controlling ten listed companies, holding a total of eleven financial licenses, clearly revealing their financial ambitions.
