It's not the fault of your teaching that my sister hasn't learned properly.

by yilu8866 on 2012-02-08 21:00:18

My sister has always been my role model. Why? Because she is a very competitive person who strives to be the best in everything—academics, life, love, and even weight loss. When she was younger, she loved eating meat and rarely touched vegetables. Our grandfather tried many times to change her habits but failed every time. As a result, by high school, she had become quite overweight.

As I mentioned earlier, my sister is a very determined person, and as a girl, she naturally cares about her appearance. Seeing her body getting bigger and bigger, she started to feel both hatred and helplessness toward it. At that time, countless thoughts came into her mind, but the strongest one was the desire to lose weight!

Unlike me, my sister approached weight loss with incredible persistence. Once she decided to do something, nothing could stop her—not even nine oxen pulling together! From that point on, this "meat-eating animal" completely bid farewell to her old habits and switched entirely to a vegetarian diet. In the beginning, when we saw how hard she was struggling, some of us almost gave up hope and felt sorry for her, seeing how much she craved the food she used to eat. But ultimately, her rational side overpowered her emotions—a feat that was truly impressive for her.

What impressed me most was her unwavering spirit. Proper weight loss requires not only dietary control but also a combination of diet and exercise—the perfect pairing. So, my sister joined the ranks of those exercising to lose weight, and her chosen method was skipping rope! At first, she jumped 200 skips per day, then increased it to 300, 500... and now she jumps 1500 skips every day, regardless of wind or rain. For her, there are no excuses. Even during the cold winter nights, when the air outside is freezing, she refused to give up. Her aunt once advised her to stay indoors during the cold weather, but my sister believed that if she didn’t persist with her weight loss, all her efforts would have been wasted. She made a solemn vow to herself and continued practicing upstairs with her skipping rope.

Through her persistence, my sister went from 150 pounds to 120 pounds, then from 120 pounds to 100 pounds, and now she weighs less than 90 pounds. This is the result of her perseverance!

Actually, I realized that the key to successful weight loss lies in persistence! Even though my sister is already slim now, she still jumps rope every day, no matter how late it gets. She says, "Even if I've succeeded, how can I not keep going?" Therefore, maintaining a healthy weight through persistent effort is the most important thing!

In conclusion, persistence and perseverance are the keys to success!
