Man loses tens of thousands of yuan after giving away free radishes and having his sweet potatoes stolen, says losing is a blessing

by fg45r4ryop on 2012-03-03 09:46:21

On November 25th and 26th, our newspaper consecutively reported on Han Honggang's (microblog) free radishes that were grown on the Yellow River banks. Among those who came to collect radishes, some took advantage of the situation and also took away sweet potatoes and peanuts.

Facing significant losses, Han Honggang did not complain. With the help of his friends, he opened a microblog yesterday afternoon. In his microblog, he also asked citizens who had not received radishes for their understanding. From his words, the reporter felt the broad-mindedness, simplicity, and honesty of this Yellow River vegetable farmer. By 10 PM last night, his microblog followers had exceeded 1,100.

With the help of friends, he opened a microblog. At 7 PM yesterday, I accidentally saw a new person pop up in my microblog feed. Upon closer inspection, it was Han Honggang, who was reported on by our newspaper.

He said two enthusiastic friends helped him open the microblog. "I don't usually play with this kind of thing. I mainly wanted to say a few words to the citizens who have come to my place through the microblog."

Han Honggang did not expect his radishes to be so sought-after, nor did he expect his microblog to attract so many followers once it went online.

"The free radish incident has taught me many lessons."

His first sentence on the microblog was a greeting at 4:48 PM: "Hello, I'm Han Honggang." The second sentence was "My typing speed is slow." The third sentence was "Please understand." The fourth sentence was "The free radish incident has taught me many lessons."

I called him to ask what lessons he had learned.

Han Honggang said: "Firstly, I didn't organize the free radish distribution well; I didn't expect so many people to come. Secondly, my method was wrong; I should have set up a sign at the intersection to tell everyone where to put the radish leaves, and not to pull them while selecting. Thirdly, there were no restrictions; one car took away more than ten thousand pounds at once. Some people wasted gasoline without getting any radishes. I feel very sorry for those who couldn't get radishes."

Netizens left over 1,000 comments supporting Radish Brother.

Netizen Shuangyangshui Weibo'er: These people who steal things don't know what 'gratitude' means. Friends living nearby can buy vegetables from Radish Brother instead of taking them for free. His enthusiasm deserves support.

Netizen Sihaigongcheng ZHUANGSHI Hanfeng: Good deeds will be rewarded with good results. When will you come to the city to sell vegetables? Here's a big umbrella for you to shield you from the wind and rain, wishing you good luck.

Netizen Tangtangyu: Blessings to you, Radish Brother. In the future, whatever you grow, just post it on your microblog, and we'll come to buy.

Liu Yupu of Nian Nian Culture: Friends, if each of us donates 10 yuan to @Han Honggang, with 5,000 people participating, we can raise 50,000 yuan. This can compensate for some of his losses, and we can also restore the face of Chinese people. What do you think?

Radish Brother says "losing is a blessing"

At 9 PM last night, I called Han Honggang. Han Honggang said that his hometown is in Zhao Ying Township, Hua County, Anyang. He attended a secondary vocational school but had poor academic performance.

"I didn't do well in school, so I thought of renting land on the Yellow River bank to grow vegetables," Han said. This radish-growing incident made him realize some truths, such as learning market-related knowledge.

"Tomorrow morning, CCTV reporters will fly to my vegetable field. They said that leaders involved in the central government's rural 'vegetable basket' project are paying close attention to this matter. In the future, how farmers sell their produce and deliver it promptly to the public is an issue to consider," Han Honggang said frankly.

When talking about his losses, Han Honggang laughed.

"Losing is a blessing; no one loses forever. It's fine. Even though I lost tens of thousands of yuan, my life is still manageable. As long as everyone understands, that's all that matters." (Reported by Xu Fuying with text and photos)
