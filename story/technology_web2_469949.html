Daisy (Touch of the Heart)

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-03 12:48:31

It has been a while since the rendering of this "mark", perhaps because of work, or perhaps because of other reasons, I can't find the right mood to start typing on the keyboard.

Tonight, listening to the song "Daisy", scenes from the movie flashed through my mind like slides.

"But I wish I could meet her earlier, sooner..."

The movie "Daisy" is like an oil painting, with light colors, gentle brushwork, and meticulous conception, giving people a fresh and elegant feeling, making them unknowingly drunk in it, experiencing the comfort of the mind and the shock of the soul.

I especially like the narration style and character portrayal techniques in the movie. From three different perspectives, the whole event was narrated repeatedly. Although there wasn't much dialogue in the entire movie, each person's inner feelings were portrayed so thoroughly:

Jung-woo - an international police officer, who picked up a pot of daisies by chance during the investigation process, causing Hye-young to mistakenly think he was the person she had been looking for. But due to his love for Hye-young, he didn't confess to her. Later, because of a misunderstanding, he selflessly left Hye-young and unfortunately died in the line of duty.

Hye-young - a free-spirited painter who loves painting daisies (her calm and gentle personality contrasts sharply with her portrayal in "My Sassy Girl"). She has been searching for her dream love. Initially, she mistakenly thought Jung-woo was the person she was looking for. After going through many events, she realized that the person she was looking for was actually right beside her, but it was too late. She used her body to shield her lover from a fatal bullet and fell into the arms of the person she had been searching for, slowly closing her eyes.

Bok-yi - a hitman surviving on the edge of the blade, who likes using the .357 Magnum, silently loves Hye-young, but feels unworthy, so he never confessed to Hye-young. He regrets not meeting Hye-young before his first kill. Despite doing everything in his power, he couldn't protect Hye-young's happiness or her life. His selfless love only brought him inner struggle and pain, eventually leading him down the path of revenge...

There are two scenes in the movie that left a deep impression on me.

One is when Jung-woo comes back from Korea to look for Hye-young and meets Bok-yi at Hye-young's house, where the misunderstanding begins. But because Hye-young couldn't speak, she couldn't explain anything. The director skillfully cuts the expressions of the three characters into the same frame, contrastingly portraying the inner fluctuations of the three protagonists. After Jung-woo leaves, Hye-young painfully knocks on the door outside, and at this moment, we can more deeply feel the inner thoughts of Bok-yi inside the door: conflict, complexity, pain... These are all vividly expressed by the main characters.

The second scene is the moment Bok-yi is about to shoot. Hye-young appears holding the painting of the daisy. Even though she couldn't speak, her excited lip movements deeply moved Bok-yi. Unfortunately, their first embrace became their last.

After watching this movie, I always wanted to buy some daisies. After searching through several flower shops, I finally found daisies in a very inconspicuous corner of a flower shop. When I put them in the vase, I realized how truly beautiful daisies are.

I found the Chinese translation of the lyrics of "Daisy". This song delicately portrays Hye-young's inner thoughts when she was shot.
