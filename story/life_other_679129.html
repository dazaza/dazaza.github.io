Accompanying leaders to drink--the knack of drinking with leaders

by gege on 2012-02-27 19:59:51

Drinking with your boss - the tricks of drinking with your boss

Trick 1: Although at the dinner table, "if you have deep feelings, drink it all in one gulp; if you have shallow feelings, just lick it", but you must not say this phrase while drinking.

Trick 2: Keep a low profile and accumulate strength, never act big as soon as you sit down at the dinner table.

Trick 3: Wait until the leaders have finished drinking to each other before it's your turn to toast.

Trick 4: It's okay for multiple people to toast one person, but never one person toasting multiple people unless you are the leader.

Trick 5: If you're toasting someone else and not clinking glasses, how much you drink can depend on the situation, such as the other person's alcohol tolerance or their attitude towards drinking. You must not drink less than the other person since you're the one offering the toast.

Trick 6: If you're toasting someone else and do clink glasses, say "I'll finish mine, you drink as you please" to show generosity.

Trick 7: If your position is humble, remember to frequently refill your leader's glass. Don't blindly offer to take their drinks, but if you must, ensure the leader really wants someone to take over, and pretend that you're drinking because you want to, not because you're taking the leader's drink. For example, if Leader A can't hold their liquor, subtly stop those who intend to toast Leader A.

Trick 8: When picking up your glass (beer glass), hold it with your right hand and support it from underneath with your left hand. Remember, your glass should always be lower than others'. If you're the leader, know your place and don't lower your glass too much, otherwise, how will those below you conduct themselves?

Trick 9: Unless there's a special person present, when clinking glasses, follow the clockwise order and treat everyone equally.

Trick 10: When clinking glasses and toasting, have something to say.

Trick 11: Don't talk business at the dinner table. Once everyone has had enough to drink, the business will naturally be resolved, and everyone understands this. Otherwise, why would they drink openly with you?

Trick 12: Don't act strange, say wrong things, or make mistakes. Don't argue, just voluntarily punish yourself by drinking more.

Trick 13: Suppose, purely hypothetically, there's not enough alcohol. Place the bottle in the middle of the table and let people help themselves. Don't foolishly go around refilling everyone's glass one by one, otherwise, what will those coming later do without any alcohol?

Trick 14: There will definitely be one last silent toast, so don't let your glass be empty. It's inevitable~

Trick 15: Pay attention to not losing control after drinking. Don't boast, lose composure, drool, throw your chopsticks around, point fingers randomly, slurp loudly while drinking soup, or fart and belch. If you can't hold it, go to the bathroom. No one will stop you.

Trick 16: Don't keep saying "I can't drink" (if you actually can). Otherwise, others might call you a hypocrite. Whether you believe it or not, people can usually tell if someone can drink or not.

Trick 17: If your leader drinks with you, it's giving you face. Regardless of how much the leader asks you to drink, finish yours first as a sign of respect. Remember, use both hands, and keep your glass lower.

Trick 18: Peanuts are a good thing for drinkers. Keeping a clear mind is important. After drinking, showing concern is essential. Offering a cup of yogurt, a cup of hot water, or a warm towel shows thorough care.
