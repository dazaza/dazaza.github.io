A woman over 70 has been cleaning the community for more than 10 years and spent her own money on repairing the community road

by au63fvecy11 on 2012-03-02 23:16:09

"I feel good all over if I don't work for a day." Fan Yinghua, 76 years old from Tonghua City, is responsible for cleaning the sanitation in her jurisdiction every morning, helping the elderly, the weak, the sick and the disabled, mediating neighborhood disputes. In addition, she pays the water and electricity bills for residents without ever asking for money.

She has gotten used to running errands for the residents. "The busier I am every day, the happier I am," said Fan Yinghua who lives in Zu'an Community, Laozhan Street, Dongchang District, Tonghua City. For more than ten years, she gets up early every day. The first thing she does is clean the community's sanitation, which includes nearly 300 steps, and then cleans the toilet. After breakfast, she continues to visit households. If anyone is in trouble, she never refuses to help.

On February 29, the East Asia reporter met her at her home. She spoke with confidence and had a smile on her face, making her look like a person in her 60s. She feels that her optimistic attitude, daily work as exercise, and attention to nutritional diet are the reasons why she looks young.

The East Asia reporter saw that she was holding three household registration books and IDs, preparing to go downtown to help apply for low-income assistance. These three households are all elderly with mobility issues.

She personally funded the construction of roads for the residents.

In 1995, after Fan Yinghua retired, she voluntarily took on the role of group leader for Group 6 of Zu'an Community. The 70-plus households here are all located on a hillside, with no road accessible by car. The north-south side paths up the mountain were also very steep, muddy in the rain, and icy in the snow, causing frequent falls and injuries.

She reported the situation to her former workplace's leadership and contacted relevant departments to obtain cement and sand. She personally funded a large amount of money and led everyone in building a 300-meter-long road, solving the problem of no vehicle access for Group 6. To improve the difficult walking conditions of the path, she also built two 300-meter-long north-south path stairways and installed steel pipe handrails, completely resolving the issue of difficult passage for the residents of Group 6.

For more than 20 households, she collects wages on their behalf.

To ensure residents have a better living environment, she often organizes them to clean up the environment. Before the public toilets were taken over in 2002, she organized manpower to clean the public toilets at least four times a year, which was undoubtedly dirty, smelly, and tiring work, but she always took the lead. Under Fan Yinghua's leadership and inspiration, some residents voluntarily joined the team to maintain street and alley cleanliness, actively sweeping streets and the paths up the mountain.

For more than ten years, Fan Yinghua has collected wages on behalf of the elderly with mobility issues, starting from just a few households to now more than 20 households, without making any mistakes. Some retired elders trust her more than their own children, handing over their bank cards to let her collect their wages on their behalf.

Fan Yinghua pays the phone bills, electricity bills, and cable TV fees for the residents on the hill every month, cumulatively advancing various expenses of about 3,000 yuan per month. For families in difficulty, she simply doesn't ask for repayment.

East Asia Correspondent in Tonghua: Li Hongli
