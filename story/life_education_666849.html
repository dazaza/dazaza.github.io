Don't let star signs decide children's destiny

by hunanshida on 2012-02-16 10:49:47

Teachers of Hunan Normal University's self-study program have discovered the biggest root of superstition in astrology. For today's children, it mainly lies in a lack of confidence; this lack of confidence, once linked with their deep yearning for the future, makes them more likely to believe in seemingly plausible but false claims. Astrology and traditional fortune-telling methods cater to this psychology, spreading from one to ten, and from ten to a hundred, gradually leading to superstition. Regarding this point, parents should maintain a very clear mind.

However, teachers at Hunan Normal University's self-study program found it interesting that many parents today are just as superstitious about astrology as their children. Some even study the influence of astrology on themselves together with their children, firmly believing in astrological predictions. In this way, what appears to be playful behavior can subtly have a negative impact on children, causing them to often subconsciously use certain astrological claims to guide their actions, forgetting that they themselves are the ones who control their own fate, and only they can seize the throat of destiny.

Therefore, teachers at the Hunan Normal University Self-Study Examination Network suggest that to eliminate the influence of astrology on children, parents must first set an example. Whether it is astrology or other superstitious things, they should discern right from wrong; at the same time, explain the pros and cons clearly to their children so they understand the principles involved. More importantly, children's self-confidence should be built up, and their ability to withstand difficulties and setbacks should be cultivated, along with the spirit, resilience, and endurance to rise up and fight. It can be said that this will play a decisive role in breaking the superstition of astrology.

In fact, people are most likely to believe in astrology and similar fortune-telling when they are in the most difficult, confused, worst mood, and most disordered thinking times. At such moments, if one lacks sufficient self-confidence, resilience, determination to fight, and enough perseverance and endurance, they will easily believe in seemingly plausible things. To put it simply, when a person's body and mind are most vulnerable, it is precisely the best time for viruses to invade.

Thus, not only do parents need to educate their children to stay away from astrology, but they also need to stay away from astrology themselves. Then, through scientific means, such as cultivating a spirit of hardship through hard work, training scientifically through reading popular science knowledge, etc., they should strive to strengthen their physical and mental health, building a watertight firewall against the invasion of astrological viruses.
