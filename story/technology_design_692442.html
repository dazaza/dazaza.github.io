By reporter Sun Lin, edited by Liang Wei -- According to relevant sources, the regulatory authorities recently convened senior executives from some futures companies and fund management companies to solicit industry opinions on lifting restrictions for futures companies to obtain qualifications for distributing public mutual funds. Relevant individuals stated that the regulators are quite positive about this move and have required qualified futures companies to prepare for conducting mutual fund distribution business.

Previously, futures companies were not included in the list of specified mutual fund sales institutions as per the "Measures for the Administration of Securities Investment Fund Sales."

The mystery of the "4+X" model for mutual fund distribution qualification is about to be solved.

In June of last year, the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC) released the revised edition of the "Measures for the Administration of Securities Investment Fund Sales," stipulating that fund managers may handle the sales of their own raised fund products, meaning self-distribution by fund companies. In addition, commercial banks (including foreign-funded legal entity banks in China), securities companies, securities investment advisory firms, independent fund sales institutions, and other institutions specified by the CSRC can apply to the CSRC for mutual fund sales qualifications.

Besides self-distribution by fund companies, the terms "commercial banks", "securities companies", "securities investment advisory firms", "independent fund sales institutions", and "other institutions specified by the CSRC" are collectively referred to as "4+X" within the industry, where "X" represents "other institutions specified by the CSRC." It was widely believed at the time that the regulations related to "X" left room for other financial institutions to distribute mutual funds.

Not long ago, the CSRC just announced a batch of independent fund sales institution qualifications. Now, the vague outline of "X" is gradually becoming clear, and futures companies are expected to gain a share in the mutual fund distribution market.

Regarding the qualification management of mutual fund distribution institutions, the "Management Measures" set different key evaluation criteria for different types of distribution agencies: for commercial banks, emphasis is placed on examining their capital adequacy ratio; for securities companies, mainly focusing on net capital and other financial risk indicators; for securities investment advisory firms and independent fund sales institutions, they are primarily required to reach a certain scale of registered capital.

Relevant insiders revealed that relevant departments are studying the qualification requirements for futures companies to distribute mutual funds. It is expected that the evaluation direction for futures companies will be similar to that of securities companies, with an emphasis on evaluating financial risk indicators such as the company's net capital.

Whether it can bring real profits to futures companies remains questionable

Some analysts believe that obtaining the qualification to distribute mutual funds enriches the profit-making methods of futures companies, while also expanding the sales channels for public mutual funds, which is beneficial for both parties' development. Especially for futures companies, gaining the qualification to distribute mutual funds means that the company begins to have the ability to provide more comprehensive asset allocation services to customers, which will have a certain impact on the transformation of the futures company's business model and development direction.

However, some market participants also pointed out that in the short term, obtaining the qualification to distribute mutual funds does not necessarily bring real profits to futures companies.

This person pointed out that currently, mainstream institutions distributing mutual funds all have their own natural strengths in profit models. Commercial banks mainly rely on their extensive marketing networks and good reputation to earn distribution income; while securities companies leverage their research capabilities in the stock market and the investment needs of their original customer base, giving them a natural advantage in distributing mutual funds.

In comparison, futures companies do face certain challenges in distributing mutual funds. Analysts pointed out that currently, China's futures market and stock market are relatively independent, and there is a certain difference between the original business scope of futures companies and the construction needs of mutual fund distribution business. If futures companies want to provide high-quality mutual fund distribution services to customers, they need to make significant new investments in institutional setup, management models, talent reserves, hardware facilities, etc. To truly achieve substantial profits in the mutual fund distribution business, futures companies still need to further explore and innovate.
