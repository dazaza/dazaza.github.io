Facebook Home's Miserable Failure: Continued Drop in App Store Rankings

by geekzhang on 2013-05-13 10:16:29

Mobile desktop software Facebook HomeMay 13, Beijing time - Although Facebook had high expectations for its mobile desktop software Facebook Home, after the initial promotional push subsided, the product's ranking in the Google Play app store has gradually declined.Despite Facebook having a massive user base of up to 1 billion, the company announced last Thursday that the total downloads of the Facebook Home app were barely close to 1 million. Additionally, AT&T also announced a price cut for the HTC First phone, the first device preloaded with Facebook Home, reducing it from $99 to $0.99 last week.All data indicates that since its launch, Facebook Home has performed very poorly in terms of user numbers. The lack of support for desktop widgets, docked sidebars, and app folders has been the key factor limiting the growth of the software's download volume, active user count, and recommendations.The app officially launched on Google Play on April 12 and initially only supported a few phones, including the HTC One X, HTC One X+, Samsung Galaxy S3, and Galaxy Note 2. Furthermore, the software was pre-installed on the HTC First phone, which went on sale on the same day. Last week, Facebook Home added support for the HTC One and Galaxy S4.Fairly speaking, the limited number of compatible phone models has somewhat restricted the app's rankings. On April 24, Facebook Home achieved its best post-launch ranking in many countries where the app could be used, but since then, the rankings in several key markets have started to decline. This trend suggests that many Android users downloaded the app out of curiosity when it was first released, but as negative news spread, downloads began to decrease.Data from U.S. app analytics company App Annie corroborates this trend of initial rise followed by decline. Not long after its public release, Facebook Home ranked 72nd in terms of downloads on Google Play in the United States on April 16. By April 23, it ranked in the top 100 in eight countries/regions, including Norway, Singapore, Canada, Denmark, Australia, Hong Kong (China), Hungary, and the UK. It was in the top 500 in 38 countries/regions. By the end of April, the rankings began to drop, and subsequently, it only remained in the top 500 in 29 countries/regions, completely disappearing from the top 100 rankings in all countries/regions.The app has not re-entered the top 100 rankings in any country or region since then.An analysis of the top 500 apps by U.S.-based market research firm Distimo also confirmed the same trend. The company found that Facebook Home reached its highest ranking in Luxembourg on April 29 at position 83 and its lowest ranking in Portugal at position 477, but the rankings subsequently began to decline.Data from App Annie also confirms this decline. On May 10, the company's data showed that Facebook Home was only in the top 500 in 19 countries/regions and did not make it into the top 100 in any country or region, with its highest ranking being 191 in Norway.In most countries/regions, the rankings are between 300 and 400, such as the 338th position in the United States. Such a poor performance is clearly not that of a groundbreaking product. Facebook also mentioned when discussing the data for Facebook Home: the download volume is close to 1 million times. This is just the download volume, not the active user volume. And the above data shows that this application has already taken a downward turn.However, for those who are already using Facebook Home, engagement is increasing. Facebook claims that users using this app spend 25% more time on Facebook, comments and likes increased by 25%, chat volume rose by 7%, and message sending increased by 10%.But their current goal is to attract more people to download and use this app. Facebook admits that disabling user-customized widgets and app folders was a mistake. Previous media reports suggested that these errors were partly due to Facebook's iPhone culture: some developers and testers of the software were iPhone users and did not realize the importance of these features on the Android platform.Facebook has disclosed plans to boost Facebook Home. The company says that soon new users will have a better experience, an icon docking bar will be added, and the DashBar function will simplify the initiation of chat functions. They will also shift the positioning to a layer on the home screen rather than completely replacing it, avoiding disrupting various customizations users have made to their phones.As for whether Facebook Home can succeed, further observation is needed, but negative news about the app has already spread widely, so action should be taken as soon as possible.
