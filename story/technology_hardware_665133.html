(4) Several forms of humidification

1. Humidification amount: the amount of gaseous water added to the air in a specific space within a unit of time, denoted as W.

2. Isothermal humidification process: a humidification process where the temperature of the air remains unchanged before and after humidification.

3. Isenthalpic humidification process: a humidification process where the enthalpy value of the air remains unchanged before and after humidification, such as certain spraying, mechanical atomization, wet membrane evaporation processes which can be approximately regarded as isenthalpic humidification.

4. Moisture content d: the amount of water vapor contained in one kilogram of moist air, kg/kg dry air.

5. Wet bulb temperature: theoretically, the wet bulb temperature is the adiabatic saturation temperature reached when air and water come into direct contact under constant pressure conditions until stable heat and moisture equilibrium is achieved, also known as thermodynamic wet bulb temperature.

6. Dry bulb temperature: the temperature of moist air measured by a thermometer.

7. Relative humidity: the ratio of the absolute humidity of unsaturated moist air to that of saturated moist air at a given temperature is called relative humidity.

8. Absolute humidity: the mass of water vapor contained in each cubic meter of moist air is referred to as the absolute humidity of the moist air.
