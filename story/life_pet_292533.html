The incredibly bold thief killed a hundred-pound Tibetan Mastiff and boiled it for hot pot.

by flying0663 on 2009-12-04 15:33:23

Zangaoxinxi.com (http://www.zangaoxinxi.com/) reported an interesting story. Yesterday, Mr. Zhu from Yuhuan called: Normally, when people see ferocious Tibetan Mastiffs, they try to avoid them at all costs. However, four men from Yuhuan were incredibly bold and actually stole a Tibetan Mastiff to make hot pot.

At around 7 p.m. on November 26, Ye from Yuhuan realized that his Tibetan Mastiff was missing. "This Tibetan Mastiff is very obedient. Every day, it goes out at a fixed time to relieve itself and then comes back by itself. This time, it has been gone for so long," Ye thought something was not right, so he went out of the factory gate to look for his Tibetan Mastiff.

Near the factory by a small ditch, Ye noticed four men behaving suspiciously, as if they were trying to put a burlap bag into the bed of a Wuling vehicle. Ye approached to investigate. When the four men saw someone coming, they got scared and ran away due to their guilty conscience. Realizing something was wrong, Ye caught one of them and found his missing Tibetan Mastiff inside the burlap bag, which had already been killed.

In anger, Ye reported the incident to the Yuhuan Public Security Bureau. After receiving the report, the police arrived at the scene and arrested another suspect. The two suspects, surnamed Zhang and Luo, confessed that because of the cold weather, they drove around stealing dogs to take home and cook in a hot pot.

"This dog is completely black. According to some experts, this Tibetan Mastiff has a tiger head and is quite large. When we discovered it at the scene, the Tibetan Mastiff almost filled up the entire bed of the Wuling vehicle, weighing over 100 pounds. It's really hard to imagine how they managed to do it," said Detective Jiang Keqiang. According to Ye, he had owned this Tibetan Mastiff for some time, originally purchasing it for more than 20,000 yuan. It was usually very obedient and wouldn't eat random things. It's unimaginable how these four men managed to kill such a large Tibetan Mastiff.

Currently, the two suspects have been criminally detained by the police. The case is still under further investigation.
