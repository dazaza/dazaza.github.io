Giants: Speeding up China's green process

by ditan360 on 2010-07-01 10:26:34

Low Carbon Network exclusive - The 2010 Hillary International Leadership Academy Honorary Award will be presented in Beijing to Ms. Ruby Liu, Chair of JUCCCE. This award is specifically given to young and mid-career talents who have made efforts to bring about change in addressing some of the world's most challenging problems. Ms. Liu leads JUCCCE, a pioneering non-profit organization dedicated to accelerating China's green process.

In the past 25 years, China’s energy demand has tripled. JUCCCE believes that, according to the climate change timetable and China's development timetable, the next 5-8 years will be a very critical window period during which we must strive to bring about significant and systemic changes in behavior patterns to achieve sustainable development balance between society and nature.

The 2010 Hillary Honorary Award will be presented at the 2010 JUCCCE China Energy Forum. This forum is jointly organized by JUCCCE and the Chinese Mayor Training Institute, gathering more than 400 high-end participants from around the world. At the forum, speeches by mayors from all over China and leaders of state-owned enterprises will become the focus. Then, Mark Prain, Executive Director of the Hillary Institute of New Zealand, and Rajendra Pachauri, Chairman of the United Nations Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change, Nobel laureate, and board member of the Hillary Institute, will jointly present the award to Ms. Ruby Liu, Chair of JUCCCE.

"We are delighted to present the 2010 Hillary Honorary Award to Ms. Ruby Liu, Chair of JUCCCE," said Mark Prain, Executive Director of the Hillary Institute. "China is crucial to solving today's global energy issues, and JUCCCE has made unique contributions in this area."

"I feel greatly honored. This award represents the hard work of a large team, including volunteers and JUCCCE partners who are committed to making an impact on the entire world," said Ms. Ruby Liu, recipient of the Hillary Honorary Award. "Every day, I feel very fortunate because JUCCCE's work inspires people to make changes."

The 2009 Hillary Honorary Award was presented to social entrepreneur Jeremy Leggett, Founder and Executive Director of Solarcentury. Every four years, the institute also awards the "Hillary Crossing Award" to one of the recipients of the Hillary Honorary Award. The "Hillary Crossing Award" is named after the nearly vertical final 40-foot cliff that Hillary and Tenzing Norgay climbed to reach the summit of Mount Everest. The "Hillary Crossing" is a metaphor for the great challenges faced by innovative leaders.

About the Hillary Institute

Headquartered in New Zealand and named after the legendary climber Edmund Hillary, the Hillary Institute annually presents an award to an emerging leader. The institute's main work is to search globally each year for a mid-career leader and present them with the Hillary Honorary Award. In addition to the award program, the institute also holds conferences annually in New Zealand and around the world, developing new partnerships based on its assessment of the latest developments in the selected focus areas over at least four years, exploring new achievements, and creating new relationships to promote integration and cooperation among people and institutions.

About JUCCCE

JUCCCE is a non-profit organization dedicated to changing the way China creates and uses energy. By collaborating with outstanding individuals across various industries worldwide, JUCCCE aims to significantly accelerate China's green process within ten years. JUCCCE's main projects include: training Chinese mayors to build energy-smart cities; helping students become green advocates by providing free energy-saving light bulbs and educational outreach; and collaborating to establish a smart grid. JUCCCE believes that the real challenge of changing China's energy usage lies in management transformation, not just technical or policy issues. JUCCCE focuses on these questions: How can we connect with key energy decision-makers through various channels? How can we promote dialogue and communication to enhance mutual understanding?
