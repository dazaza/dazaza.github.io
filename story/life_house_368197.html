India's richest man moved into the world's largest mansion with 630 million pounds (Group photos)

by geekzhang on 2010-10-16 12:54:40

The design of "Antilia" is staggered, with different levels of garden greenery dividing the different functions of the building, and a large garden in the middle separating the conference facilities and parking from the residences above. 27 floors 173 meters; Bigger than Versailles; The market price is 630 million pounds. Ambani is also the fourth richest person in the world.

Imagine what it would be like to live in a mansion like this: 27 stories high, overlooking the city and the sea, with more than 600 management staff, nine elevators, a helipad on the roof, and an interior decorated with Picasso paintings and gold chandeliers. India's richest man, Mukesh. Ambani will soon welcome his housewarming. Priced at £630 million, the 173-metre-high 27-storey mansion, which is bigger than the Palace of Versailles in France, is by far the largest private home in the world. According to the British "Guardian" reported yesterday, the mansion is called "Antilia" after a mythical island. The total area of the house is 37,000 square meters. The interior of the building is fully equipped with facilities, including a health club, ballroom, guest rooms, swimming pool and cinema for 50 people. It is reported that Ambani, his wife and three children are not the only members of the dream mansion, in order to keep it orderly, Ambani employs about 600 managers. The family lived on the top floors of the building, which overlooks the Arabian Sea and the city of Mumbai. An Indian businessman who visited the building described the beauty of Antilia to The Times of India, saying it was "breathtaking" with paintings by Picasso and a gold chandelier at the top. Ambani is holding a big housewarming party at the end of the month and has already sent out invitations to some people. At that time, guests can choose various modes of transportation. If you want to avoid Mumbai's heavy traffic, you can go there by helicopter, because the roof of the building has a landing pad that can accommodate three helicopters at the same time; Guests who want to drive, don't worry, the building's underground garage can accommodate 160 cars at a time. Once inside, guests have the option of taking one of nine elevators from the lobby to the floors. (Source: Oriental Morning Post)(Managing Editor: Chen Ruobing)
