Young man lied that he placed a bomb in the supermarket and asked for 1 million yuan

by yanb403rxue on 2012-03-09 10:02:24

A 22-year-old young man, after watching a plot in Jackie Chan's movie where bombs were used to threaten people, wanted to imitate it. He sent threatening text messages twice to a supermarket, falsely claiming that he had placed bombs and demanded 1 million yuan.

In the dead of night, Ms. Rao, the supermarket manager, was playing mahjong with her friends when suddenly her mobile phone received a text message. It read: "I have installed three remote control time bombs in your supermarket. Don't be nervous, I'm just testing the power of the bomb. Do not report it to the police, otherwise I will immediately detonate the bombs remotely. Tell your boss to prepare 1 million yuan in cash and wait for further notice, or you know the consequences." All the friends at the table thought it was definitely a prank, so Ms. Rao ignored it.

But the next afternoon, when she received a similar text message again, she became flustered. Was someone seeking revenge or was it a prank?

After watching Jackie Chan's movie, he wanted to use an alarm clock to impersonate a "bomb".

In August this year, 22-year-old Wang Dong (a pseudonym) stayed in a certain hotel in Xin Qiao. While wandering around, he discovered a Yonghui Supermarket and decided to buy some toiletries and an alarm clock. On the morning of August 27th, Wang Dong was surfing the internet in an internet cafe and watched Jackie Chan's movie "New Police Story". The scenes involving bomb threats excited him greatly.

Thus, Wang Dong conceived the idea of imitation and wanted to try it out. Subsequently, he saw a group-buying information at the entrance of Yonghui Supermarket, which included a mobile phone number (actually the telephone number of Ms. Rao, the manager of the food and supplies department of the supermarket). Wang Dong has a good memory and memorized the number directly.

After returning to the hotel, Wang Dong began to plan his scheme. Making a fake bomb might make it more realistic and scare the other party more effectively. He suddenly remembered that the alarm clock he bought a few days ago could also be wrapped in cardboard to look more like a "time bomb".

Sent two text messages, threatening the supermarket for 1 million yuan

However, Wang Dong later thought that making a fake bomb was too blatant and troublesome, so he decided against it. At 00:06 on August 29th, Wang Dong became increasingly excited: "Just send a text message to scare the supermarket, it's not a big problem right?"

Thus, he composed the first text message. Ms. Rao was playing mahjong at the time and, upon seeing it, assumed it was a prank and didn't pay attention. At 3 PM the same day, while Manager Rao was analyzing data with two other colleagues, she received another text message, which made her break out in a cold sweat.

The content of the text message was: "You have two and a half hours left to prepare what we want. Remember, the first bomb will explode at 5:10 PM. By then, it won't just be about one million yuan..." Ms. Rao immediately called the two numbers from which the messages were sent, but both were turned off.

Seeing hundreds of people evacuated, he got scared and dared not go out

Why did he send two text messages? After Wang Dong was caught, he confessed to the police that after the first text message, seeing that the supermarket was still open, he thought they really weren't afraid and felt he hadn't scared them enough, so he sent a second one to see if it would "work". Facing the prosecutor's questioning, Wang Dong quickly admitted. He said that at the time, he wanted to send another text message to threaten the supermarket. "Originally, I thought it would be nice if I could actually extort some money, but I hadn't yet figured out how to collect the money, so I didn't write how to deliver the money."

After sending the second text message, Wang Dong's mind was filled with anxiety and fear. He didn't dare to wait for a reply, so he took out the SIM card from his phone. That evening, he went to a snack shop near Yonghui Supermarket to eat and check the effect of the text messages. He saw many people rushing out of Yonghui Supermarket in panic, with police cars and police dogs arriving. The police set up a cordon outside Yonghui Supermarket and evacuated hundreds of people, causing traffic congestion on the road. "This is serious now," Wang Dong didn't dare to go out anymore and quickly returned to the hotel. Soon, the police found Wang Dong.

On the 21st of this month, the Shapingba District Procuratorate will transfer this case to the Shapingba District Court. The suspect, Wang Dong, fabricated explosive threats, disrupting social order,涉嫌 fabricating false terrorist information, and will be prosecuted.
