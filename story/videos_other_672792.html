14-Year-Old Boy Learns to Transform and Steals Gasoline for 5 Years

by anonymous on 2012-02-23 10:40:16

The doctor is treating Xiaofang. Currently, the teenager's intelligence has declined and he is hospitalized for treatment. The doctor stated that addiction to drinking gasoline is a mental disorder.

Text and pictures by reporters Tian Fuyou and Luo Xuan. Yesterday, our hotline received a report from Mr. He in Yibin. He said that a 14-year-old student in Xingwen County of the city, after watching the Transformers cartoon where "Optimus Prime" and "Bumblebee" gain energy by drinking large amounts of gasoline and transform into invincible warriors, became very envious and started imitating them by drinking gasoline. Now, he can't stop and has developed a "petrol addiction." Helpless, his family sent him to the Fourth Hospital of Yibin for treatment.

The Teenager Mimics 'Transformers' by Sneaking Gasoline

When the reporter arrived at the hospital, he met the boy and his father. According to the boy's father, Chen Dahai, his son Xiaofang (a pseudonym) is 14 years old. In the past, Xiaofang had good grades and was willing to think. Even when the TV or DVD player at home broke, he would fix them himself. But after watching the Transformers cartoon, Xiaofang began imitating "Optimus Prime" and "Bumblebee" by sneaking gasoline, hoping to become a warrior.

"Xiaofang's addiction to drinking gasoline started five years ago with sniffing lighter gas," said Chen Dahai. His wife, He Kaiqin, runs a small grocery stall outside their home. Between February and March 2004, He Kaiqin always found that every box of 50 lighters she bought were gone within two or three days. Knowing her store couldn't sell that many lighters so quickly, she asked everyone at home, but they all said they didn't know. Later, He Kaiqin discovered discarded lighters in some corners around the house and paid attention. One afternoon, she finally found Xiaofang in a small grove behind the house, holding three or four lighters and inhaling the gas towards his nose forcefully. After finishing one lighter, he would discard it and move on to the next one. That evening, He Kaiqin told Chen Dahai about this, and the family educated Xiaofang. In October 2004, Chen Dahai noticed that the gasoline in the motorcycle parked at home often disappeared. One day, he finally found Xiaofang holding a mineral water bottle containing half a bottle of gasoline, sniffing and sipping it.

After Drinking Gasoline for Five Years, Intellectual Decline Leads to Hospitalization

Upon discovering his son sneaking gasoline, Chen Dahai locked the motorcycle in the bedroom every night to cut off Xiaofang's "oil supply." The lighter gas and motorcycle gasoline sold at home no longer disappeared, but Xiaofang's enthusiasm for watching cartoons only increased. Chen Dahai said he never expected that since February this year, neighbors reported that Xiaofang was stealing gasoline from other people's motorcycles, sometimes using mineral water bottles to carry two or three bottles of gasoline to nearby woods for inhalation. When someone asked him why he inhaled gasoline, he replied that it was to gain energy.

Drinking gasoline for five years, the once smart child has now become intellectually impaired, struggling even with simple arithmetic problems like addition and subtraction within 100. Seeing Xiaofang progress from sniffing lighter gas to now being able to drink two or three bottles of gasoline each time, the family had no choice but to send Xiaofang to the Fourth Hospital of Yibin for treatment.

At the hospital, during an intelligence test conducted by the doctors, Xiaofang indeed gave incorrect answers. The doctor asked him: "What is 100 minus 7?" Xiaofang answered: "92"; The doctor asked: "What is 17 plus 7?" Xiaofang answered: "20"; The doctor asked: "How many seasons are there in a year?" Xiaofang answered: "30."

Hospital Diagnosis: Addiction to Drinking Gasoline is a Mental Disorder

According to Dr. Peng Houquan from the Fourth Hospital of Yibin, after preliminary diagnosis by the hospital, Xiaofang's addiction to drinking gasoline is a "mental disorder caused by volatile organic compounds." His dependence on gasoline has made him severely addicted and ill. The hospital had previously treated cases of "alcohol dependence," "drug dependence," and "soil dependence," but this is the first time they've encountered "gasoline dependence."

Dr. Peng said that due to the lead content in gasoline, it causes significant brain damage. Moreover, as Xiaofang is still in the physical development stage, it will cause severe intellectual damage. From the hospital's examination of Xiaofang's arithmetic skills within 100 and some basic knowledge, his memory and comprehension are currently very poor. Therefore, the hospital will organize experts to observe and treat Xiaofang for a period, allowing for targeted treatment to improve his "gasoline dependence" condition.
