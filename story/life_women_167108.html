Precautions for buying lingerie

by mixian on 2009-06-27 15:20:41

The main function of underwear is to adjust body shape. Neither too loose nor too tight underwear can fully perform its function and effect, and cannot perfectly sculpt the ideal body shape and beauty. Therefore, correctly choosing lingerie is very important.

1. Measuring your body size

When selecting underwear that fits your body, you must first accurately understand your body's dimensions because they change over time. Your body changes with diet, exercise, and seasons, especially from "winter to spring" and "summer to autumn." Women should remeasure their body sizes when purchasing underwear.

Measurement positions should include:

(1) Upper bust

(2) Underbust

(3) Waist

(4) Hips

(5) Height

Correct bra sizing method:

1. Stand with feet together, breathe naturally, and let your hands hang by your sides. Use a soft tape measure to measure around your ribcage parallel to the breast root line to get the underbust measurement.

2. Gently lift your breasts to simulate wearing a bra, then use a soft tape measure around the fullest part of your breasts to get the bust measurement.

Correct panties sizing method:

1. First, measure your waist. Use a soft tape measure around the narrowest part of your waist.

2. Then measure your hips. Use a soft tape measure around the fullest part of your hips and over your waist.

2. Choosing the right size

(1) Underbust measurement (allowable deviation 2.5 cm).

(3) Choose underwear suitable for your chest shape

Flat and diffused chest

Characteristics: Large base area, low height, not obvious curves.

Choose a 1/2 or 3/4 cup bra with steel underwires. It helps gather chest muscles inward from both sides and has an upward lifting effect. This enhances the force pushing towards the center, deepens the cleavage, and accommodates excess fat spread to the ribs and upper abdomen. Avoid bras with detachable cups.

Small chest

Characteristics: Smaller base area, low height, small chest.

Choose a seamless molded cup bra to compensate for insufficient height, making the curve natural and uniform. Alternatively, choose a 1/2 or 3/4 cup bra with steel underwires or inserts.

Moderately drooping chest

Characteristics: Moderate base area, higher but loose and sagging breast muscles, typically seen in women who have given birth. Choose a larger cup size than usual, with a noticeable upward angle and deeper cups that can completely accommodate the full breasts. Select a bra with a stable bottom edge. The shoulder straps should be slightly wider and shorter to increase the upward support force and raise the breast position. Bras with steel circles and butterfly wings provide additional support from below, raising the breast position. Ensure the breasts are fully inserted into the cups when wearing.

High and full chest

Characteristics: This body type appears more round and full on the upper body.

If a full figure wears a bra improperly, it can easily distort the breast shape and affect body shape. Therefore, it’s best to choose a waterdrop-shaped cup and ensure the breasts are fully inserted into the cups to make the chest contour more distinct and maintain the breast's aesthetic shape.

Moderate and diffused chest

Characteristics: Large base area, moderate height.

Choose a 3/4 or full cup bra with steel underwires, with soft strips and elastic fabric on the side seams to enhance the gathering effect. Both 3/4 cup thin lining and full cup with or without lining are acceptable, but ensure to choose the correct size and cup capacity.

Regardless of your body type, as long as you choose correctly, the bra can become a part of your skin, cover flaws, create more attractive curves, and boost female confidence.

2. Shapewear – indicated by waist size (allowable deviation 3 cm).

Example: Size 64 shapewear suits people with a waist size of 61-67 cm.

Selection for different body types:

a. Protruding abdomen – caused by long-term wear of low-rise underwear and shapewear. Switch to high-waist underwear and shapewear, allowing some looseness in the skirt and pant bands. If the abdomen protrudes significantly, choose firm shapewear that adjusts the buttocks' shape, reaching up to the waist, with excellent stretchability in the abdomen.

b. Triple-layered abdomen – first switch to soft underwear, using all-cotton knitted flat-cut underwear to eliminate layers of fat. After eliminating the triple-layered abdomen defect, quickly adjust the buttock shape by wearing shapewear that helps improve the buttock curve. The shapewear can be high-waisted, improving the waist and thighs simultaneously.

c. Drooping buttocks – select shapewear with a flatter bottom edge, good elasticity, and manually lift the buttock muscles inside the shapewear. The flatter bottom edge provides supportive fixation for the buttocks. The shapewear waistline at the normal position has a better upward pulling force, which helps elevate the buttock position.

d. Large thighs with drooping buttocks – wear thigh-high shapewear. When wearing, manually lift the thigh muscles from the bottom up and use the shapewear to encompass and fix them. Only by elevating the buttocks can the defect of thick thighs be improved.

e. Undefined buttock curves – do not wear bikini-style underwear. Choose inclusive shapewear, using the bottom edge to define the boundary between the buttocks and legs. Select underwear with sufficient elasticity but no tension.

3. Full-body shapewear – indicated by bra size and hip size (allowable deviation 4 cm).

3. Selection of underwear material

Bra materials can be divided into natural fibers and chemical fibers.

1. Natural fibers, further divided into silk and cotton. Silk feels soft and fine, dyes well, and has skincare and moisturizing effects; Cotton absorbs sweat, breathes well, has good thermal insulation, and does not irritate or cause allergies.

2. Chemical fibers, such as nylon and polyester fabrics, have good elasticity and strength, and offer advantages like durability, easy washing, and quick drying.

Underwear materials are generally divided into natural fibers and chemical fibers.

1. Natural fibers mainly include all-cotton and silk. All-cotton is soft, absorbent, and breathable, while silk moisturizes and nourishes the skin.

2. Chemical fibers mainly include nylon and polyester, which are easy to wash and dry quickly. Most are used in the form of lace on underwear.
