Microsoft, Watch Out: Apple Is Making Big Moves in Office Suites

by anonymous on 2013-11-21 14:01:30

Apple vs. Microsoft: The battle for the office heats up

Microsoft and Dell, take note—Apple is coming to steal your market share in the office equipment sector! Apple's upcoming expansion into the office equipment market has put traditional office hardware and software manufacturers under immense pressure. Analyst Ted Schadler from Forrester stated, "Apple will enter the office equipment market, and their trump cards are the iPhone and iPad. Although they haven't yet dominated this market, they are undoubtedly accelerating their growth within it."

Apple is now working hard to reduce production costs while offering free software to consumers and providing higher security for business customers. These efforts have paid off. In fact, in recent years, Apple's market share in the office equipment sector has increased. Taking the North American market as an example, just a few years ago, their share of the commercial equipment market was only 3%, but now that number has risen to 18%.

The biggest driver behind Apple's rapid increase in market share in the office equipment sector is people's desire to use Apple products at work.

Trip Chowdhry, an analyst at Global Equities Research, said, "It is due to the demand from consumers or employees that Apple products have started entering offices. Whether mobile devices or laptops, Apple’s products are well-designed with user-centric interfaces that make them easy to use. Meanwhile, Microsoft's operating system lags behind Apple in this aspect. Many people report that their productivity increases when using Apple devices. Therefore, IT managers respond to employee requests by purchasing Apple products for the company."

Meanwhile, Apple has also begun recruiting talent to gain more corporate clients. They posted several job listings on LinkedIn, one of which was for an "Apple Enterprise Sales Representative." From the job description, we can see that Apple is looking for a representative who can "sell products to Fortune 1000 companies" and requires deep understanding of "device management, security, and device integration."

An analyst pointed out that while Apple has an excellent sales team for the education market, the quality of their enterprise client sales team is relatively low. It seems Apple has become aware of this issue and is taking steps to change the situation.

Chowdhry stated, "So far, even with a subpar sales team, Mac computers have remained successful products. If Apple adds an excellent sales team to support the Mac line, it could bring substantial returns for the company. This would certainly be bad news for Dell, Microsoft, and HP."

Besides hiring sales personnel, other recent moves by Apple have also put pressure on Microsoft. Particularly allowing users to download the iWork suite for free and offering free upgrades to the Mavericks OS. Regarding Apple's move, Schadler remarked, "Apple's sudden decision to let users freely download so many software products has significantly increased the pressure on Microsoft. While Apple's free software offerings won't affect Microsoft's business model, it forces Microsoft to prepare for potential price adjustments."

However, in Apple's view, the real growth point for future office equipment lies in mobile devices.

Apple equals mobile devices

Although BlackBerry and Microsoft have also launched mobile devices, when it comes to tablets and smartphones, most people still think first of Apple's iPad and iPhone. Apple firmly believes that in the future, more people will start using these mobile devices at work.

Data from Forrester Research shows that by 2017, tablet shipments will account for 18% of all office equipment shipments. This figure is a significant boon for Apple, as the iPad holds the highest market share in the mobile office equipment sector.

Gartner analyst Carolina Milanesi said, "With the iPad, Apple has carved out a niche in the office equipment market. Companies have finally realized the value of tablets and are increasingly incorporating them into their workflows."

During Apple's fourth-quarter earnings call last month, Apple's CFO Peter Oppenheimer specifically highlighted how many companies are developing internal apps for iPads to enhance employee productivity. Oppenheimer noted during the meeting that nearly 35,000 businesses worldwide are currently developing internal apps for iPads and iPhones, with millions of employees using these apps.

Apple has fundamentally changed how enterprises view mobile devices. Just a few years ago, many companies were rejecting iPads, citing high prices and difficulty in control. However, due to the rapid adoption of iPhones and iPads over the past few years, companies have gradually started accepting Apple devices in the workplace and even consider Apple a key device provider.

Compared to Windows devices, Apple's greatest advantage isn't necessarily its higher adoption rate. In fact, more people around the world still use PCs running on Windows. Apple's greatest advantage is its security. For enterprises, what they value most is the security of critical data, making this the most attractive feature of Apple devices for business users.

Timothy Lesko from Granite Investment Advisors stated, "Apple's iOS system is much more secure than the open-source Android system. Most companies choose Apple products when selecting mobile devices for this reason. iOS is safer compared to Android, and Microsoft's mobile operating system arrived too late."

If you're an executive, how would you choose between Apple devices and Dell or HP Windows devices?
