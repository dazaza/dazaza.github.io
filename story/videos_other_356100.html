The new policy ushers in five major changes in the real estate market: home buyers' wait-and-see attitude becomes increasingly intense_SH Y8

by xl0917111 on 2010-05-26 21:00:34

Under regulation, new changes have appeared in the real estate market. First of all, there has been a significant drop in the trading volume of the second-hand housing market. Once the new property market regulation policies were announced, cities such as Beijing, Shanghai, and Shenzhen saw a wave of selling off second-hand homes. The second-hand housing market, known as the weathervane of the market, experienced a shift from a sharp increase to a decrease in trading volume. Taking Beijing as an example, during the first week after the announcement of the new policy, homebuyers eager to avoid policy restrictions sparked a rush for signing contracts. Data from the Beijing Real Estate Trading Network showed that on April 19th, a total of 2,369 second-hand homes were traded across Beijing, including 2,208 residential units, breaking the daily trading record for second-hand homes in Beijing. After the peak of concentrated transactions, trading volumes began to decline, dropping to 1,654 units on April 30th, and only 240 and 186 units on May 1st and 2nd respectively.

"Originally, we could receive over ten groups of customers in a day," said Li Fang, an intermediary broker in Beijing's Guanzhuang area, "but now we can only receive two or three groups per day." In March, they would sit in their stores waiting for customers, but now they have to go out with advertising boards to look for customers again.

Secondly, there has been a contraction in the trading volume of new properties. As a traditional peak sales season, the May Day holiday saw some city real estate markets clearly cooling down. During the holiday, the entire city of Guangzhou sold only 178 residential units, which was half of the normal trading volume, and the trading volume at the "May Day Holiday Property Market" exhibition in Shanghai also significantly shrank. According to the latest data published by the National Bureau of Statistics, in April alone, the sales area of commercial housing reached 80.51 million square meters, a reduction of 1.55 million square meters compared to March, among which residential sales amounted to 72.49 million square meters, decreasing by 1.8 million square meters.

Due to the relatively low base number of data from the same period in 2009 and the time-lag effect of policies, house prices in 70 large and medium-sized cities nationwide still showed an upward trend in April. However, Sheng Laiyun, spokesperson for the National Bureau of Statistics, stated that with the passage of time, the effects of the policies will continue to become apparent, and both real estate prices and the expectations of home buyers will undergo significant changes.
