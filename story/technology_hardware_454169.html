"The Biography of Li Ka-shing 1"

Young Li Ka-shing 1: Skilled Salesmanship

Li Ka-shing, the chairman of Cheung Kong Holdings, is a billionaire known to all Chinese people around the world. He is not only a superman in the business world but also a popular figure that both the Beijing and Hong Kong governments vie to court. The industries he operates include real estate, electricity, telecommunications, retail, and port transportation, all of which are closely related to the lives of Hong Kong citizens. His companies employ over 31,000 workers, making him the fourth largest employer in the entire city.

He is immensely wealthy, and the public can calculate his wealth as holding 34% of Cheung Kong's shares, worth more than HK$52 billion, not counting his personal overseas and other assets. The multinational commercial empire he controls, including the exchange of British Orange shares for German Mannesmann shares, has a listed market value exceeding HK$1.2 trillion.

In fact, he was once a pioneer in breaking the monopoly of British capital groups on the Hong Kong market, with epoch-making significance; and his achievements in the local business community, as well as his influence on the political arena, have made him one of the outstanding figures of the era. Born in the Year of the Dragon, the dragon in the "I Ching," which explains the changes in the way of heaven and the rise and fall of human affairs, represents great and capable individuals. The first hexagram of the "I Ching," the Qian Hexagram, appropriately describes the five stages of Li Ka-shing's half-century of business operations.

"Hidden Dragon, Do Not Use": A Young Man from Chaozhou Relies on His Uncle

Li Ka-shing, whose ancestral home is in Chaozhou, had a father named Li Yunjing who earned a living as a teacher. They had three sons and one daughter, with Li Ka-shing being the eldest son. In 1937, due to financial hardship, his mother Zhuang Biqin and younger sister stayed in Chaozhou City, while the father and son walked for half a day to settle in the nearby Jitang Town of Hong'an Village. The father served as the principal of Chongsheng Primary School, where he taught sixty students and lived in the school.

Li Ka-shing, as the principal's son, left a deep impression on his senior Xu Youkun: Li Ka-shing was originally called Li Yulin. We Chaozhou people use different names when we are young and when we grow up, but people in the school called him Li Doutu because his head was particularly noticeable. He was very polite and respectful to classmates and teachers, never taking advantage of being the principal's son to bully others, and he never got into fights with classmates.

Besides attending school, Li Ka-shing also took care of his father's daily life. The two lived in a house behind Chongsheng Primary School, with a plaque engraved at the entrance called "Duyue Shuzhai." Xu Xifeng, a former student of Principal Li, said: Principal Li taught me arithmetic. When he napped in the afternoon, his son would cook rice for him on a small stove, eating just one bowl of rice per meal, which was never enough. He still remembers Principal Li wearing an old blue robe and round glasses. Striving upward despite depending on others

After staying at Chongsheng Primary School for two years, Li Ka-shing and his father moved to Hong Kong to escape the Japanese war, when Li Ka-shing was only twelve years old. Later, his mother and siblings came to reunite with them, relying on his uncle Zhuang Jing'an. At that time, Zhuang was already a respected elder in the Hong Kong watch industry, owning the renowned Zhongnan Watch Company, which was famous for distributing the Roamer watches. The company had considerable scale, with its office located on the 11th to top floors of the Zhongnan Building on Des Voeux Road Central. Three years after moving to Hong Kong, his father passed away due to tuberculosis, forcing Li Ka-shing to drop out of school. He then worked as an apprentice in his uncle's shop, serving tea and sweeping the floor, becoming very familiar with his cousin Zhuang Yueming. Despite living with his uncle at No. 73 Pokfulam Road, across from the University of Hong Kong, his dream of continuing his education seemed unreachable.

Not wanting to depend on others indefinitely, two years later Li Ka-shing left the watch shop and became a street salesman (Cantonese dialect term for salesperson) at a hardware factory. His method of selling iron buckets was to avoid competitive grocery stores and instead target middle and lower-class housewives. The leisure-loving aunties thus spread the word about him, turning into his unpaid salespeople and making the hardware factory prosperous.

Later, he switched to selling plastic water buckets. The tall and slim Li Ka-shing carried product samples from the Kennedy Town factory every day, taking buses and ferries to go around Central and Kowloon. His sales technique was to let the products speak for themselves. Once, while promoting a new type of plastic sprinkler, he proactively helped the cleaning staff of a wholesale company sprinkle water, coincidentally catching the attention of the manager responsible for the daily goods department, who readily agreed to distribute his plastic sprinklers.

At the age of eighteen, Li Ka-shing was promoted to department manager of the factory, and two years later became the general manager, fully responsible for the factory's daily operations. All the money he earned was handed over to his mother Zhuang Biqin for safekeeping, waiting for the right opportunity.
