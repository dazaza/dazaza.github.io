1. Purchasing from wholesale markets

Whether it's a physical store or an online store, most sellers purchase their goods from wholesale markets. Although manufacturers have first-hand goods with larger profits in terms of price, generally, manufacturers have certain big clients and they usually do not cooperate with small sellers. So when you haven't decided what to sell yet, go to the wholesale market and take a look, you might be pleasantly surprised.

1) The variety of goods in the wholesale market is abundant, giving you a wide range of choices. It's also easy to compare prices among different suppliers.

2) The wholesale market is very suitable for part-time sellers, as the time and quantity of purchasing goods are relatively flexible.

3) The prices in the wholesale market are relatively low, making it easier for online stores to achieve thin-profit but high-turnover sales, which also helps accumulate transaction credibility for the online store.

In comparison, the wholesale market is indeed a good choice for new sellers. If you happen to live in a big city with large wholesale markets, why not go there and take a look? You won't be disappointed. If you live in a smaller city without a wholesale market, don't worry. There are many ways to Rome, and there will be a way suitable for you as long as you are patient.

2. Wholesale on Alibaba website

The largest wholesale markets in the country are mainly concentrated in a few cities, and many sellers do not have the conditions to travel thousands of miles to these wholesale markets. Therefore, at this time, Alibaba as a platform for online wholesale fully demonstrates its superiority. It provides a large selection space for many sellers in small places. It is not only convenient to search for information but also specifically provides services for small sellers, with a very small starting bid quantity.

1) Alibaba not only offers wholesale purchases but also small-scale auction purchases, which are popular ways of purchasing goods among Taobao sellers.

2) When purchasing goods on the website, it is best to choose products that support Alipay or are members of TrustPass. Alibaba launched 'TrustPass' four years ago. Generally, if a seller is a TrustPass member for two or three years or more, and the TrustPass index reaches nearly 100 or more, they are relatively trustworthy. However, this can only serve as a reference, and specific communication is still necessary.

3) Alibaba has a powerful search function, allowing maximum comparison of goods during purchases.

4) When negotiating with merchants, try to use TradeManager. If any disputes arise, it can serve as evidence.

5) For the first purchase, you can also choose local manufacturers or companies, which makes it convenient to pick up goods in person.

Online purchasing is not like a wholesale market, as it involves a certain degree of virtuality. Therefore, you must be extremely cautious when choosing merchants. Make sure to transact with reliable companies.

3. Online Consignment

Online consignment (http://www.haodp.com) involves displaying products provided by merchants online, collecting order funds from buyers, transferring a certain amount of money to the merchant, letting them ship the goods, and then earning the difference as a consignor. Two types of newcomers are suitable for choosing consignment. One is those who do not have much capital, and the other is those who are afraid of risks and just want to try it out.

1) Online consignment requires almost no capital investment, making it very suitable for new and small sellers.

2) Online consignment does not require warehouse preparation or personal responsibility for logistics. Merchants will ship directly to buyers after receiving the deposit and data, saving the trouble of mailing.

3) Online consignment saves the hassle of taking product photos and writing product descriptions. Product pictures obtained from merchants are usually of good quality and more likely to attract buyers.

4) Since online consignment cannot directly contact the goods, it is difficult to have a good grasp of product quality, inventory, and after-sales service. Therefore, when selecting, you should find some relatively formal companies and choose the most suitable one according to your own requirements.

Due to the many advantages of online consignment, I am now mainly doing online consignment. I currently act as the agent for the brand clothing mall of 123 Business Alliance, managing dozens of brands, including men's and women's clothing, shoes, bags, children's clothing, etc., which can be handled simultaneously, making it very convenient. This is my reason for choosing it.

4. Finding Merchant Surplus Goods

Here, the term "merchant" refers to the first one or two levels of sellers of goods, such as foreign trade clothing processing plants or wholesalers.

What we usually refer to as "A goods" are generally surplus goods produced by foreign trade companies, order returns, or orders temporarily canceled, resulting in inventory.

Also, larger wholesalers generally have a certain amount of inventory accumulation, sometimes even including branded goods. Of course, the styles may not be the latest, but their brand effect is still present. Therefore, finding good merchant surplus goods can actually be quite a good source of supply.

1) Merchant surplus goods generally have a large market demand, with a certain guarantee of product quality, belonging to mid-to-high-end items, making it easy to earn positive reviews in online transactions.

2) The sources of merchant surplus goods are relatively limited, so the competition is small but highly competitive. Moreover, you can utilize the regional differences of online stores to enhance the quality of accumulated products and increase prices. That is, these goods may not sell well in one market, causing inventory accumulation, but they could become best-sellers in another market.

In comparison, merchant surplus goods are among the hardest to find among several sources of supply. Although many manufacturers may have inventory accumulation, the difficulty of finding them is also significant. However, if the city where you live has many manufacturers, it's a different story.

7. Techniques for Purchasing Goods on Alibaba

1) Always use Alipay for purchasing goods. I've helped my classmates purchase goods on Alibaba over 20 times, and every time there were no problems because I always insisted on using Alipay. Don't let cheap goods sway your decision to use Alipay; it is our basic protection as small sellers.

2) Be extra careful when making payments. Many merchants on Alibaba do not conduct direct transactions on the platform. They will show you their company album, and after you place an order, they will provide you with a link to their Taobao store for you to purchase. At this point, you need to be extra careful. First, check if the Taobao shop owner is the same person you negotiated with on Alibaba. Second, carefully examine the shop's review content. Check if there are reviews similar to yours, indicating that others have also used Alipay through links. Also, check how others evaluate the goods. Third, pay attention to the price changes made by the seller when you make the payment.

3) Find fixed sellers. If you don't have good wholesale markets nearby, Alibaba is indeed your best choice for small-scale purchases. If you have decided to purchase goods from Alibaba, I suggest you first buy a small amount to test the quality. If the quality is satisfactory, you can continue purchasing from this company. This way, you get more discounts, and the risk is significantly reduced.

4) Compare and research more. I once spent two days comparing and researching for a 300-yuan purchase on Taobao and Alibaba. Not rushing into purchases is something we all should pay attention to. There are many factors to consider. For example, is the merchant trustworthy? How do their prices compare to their peers? Remember, cheaper doesn't necessarily mean better. This requires insight from each of us.

5) A special reminder: Some goods may even be cheaper on Taobao than on Alibaba. Some Alibaba merchants also have stores on Taobao. When you encounter goods you want to purchase in bulk, it's best to check the prices on Taobao.

6) Do not easily engage in agency work. Many people choose agency work, which is a risk-free good thing. However, I do not recommend doing agency work. You cannot control the goods or shipping. You are just a puppet for them. If there are issues with the goods, you will suffer. Either way, you end up being the one at fault. Therefore, I insist on seeing the goods before releasing them for sale. (This point may be somewhat subjective, so take it as a reference.)

7) Find Alibaba merchants who offer consignment sales. This method eliminates financial pressure and is worth trying. (This is also an important point.)

8) To find the lowest price for a single product on Alibaba, click on the price option in the upper right corner when searching for each product, and it will sort from low to high, making it very convenient.

8. Techniques for Purchasing Goods at the Yiwu International Trade Mart

1) Follow a principle: prioritize the most important products and take them first. Place less important goods or shops located farther away at the last moment to purchase. If there's no time, just skip them (the trade mart closes at 5 PM). This ensures the quality of your goods. I suggest you record the distribution map of the shops you need during your first and second visits to the goods source (this will save time and reduce detours later), and note down whether there are passages between different areas to save time and energy.

2) After each purchase, obtain business cards from the most important shops and determine based on the market feedback which shops you frequently visit for restocking.

3) Don't forget to buy a luggage handcart when purchasing goods, preferably a stainless steel one, as it's durable and won't break easily. Also, bring packaging bags (woven bags), available at the entrance of Phase II, first floor. The cost is 45-50 yuan per cart, and woven bags are 3-5 yuan each in different sizes (don't save on this; cheap ones are not of good quality).

4) The CDE zones of Phase I of the trade mart are suitable for small-batch purchases, mostly concentrated in zones C and D. When you go to purchase goods, first check if the shop has a large stock of goods on display. If yes, you can make small-batch purchases. However, many shops specialize in foreign trade. If you see attractive samples but only one piece per style, they are generally intended for large orders (occasionally they may also sell in small quantities). Shops with ready-made goods tend to focus on headwear. Customers looking for small batches can directly check some headwear and jewelry parts, such as small pendant alloy diamond jewelry and silver jewelry. Some large shops are better, and you don't have to take whole packages or pieces; you can take individual items, and they generally won't restrict the amount or quantity. Here, it's necessary to supplement a bit about professional terminology related to purchasing and bargaining. For example, "whole piece," which they refer to as a box of goods shipped, abbreviated as "piece." Don't let the seller know you're a novice (otherwise, they might raise the price or refuse to sell in bulk).

5) If you have established fixed cooperation with a merchant, you can let them send express delivery or freight, so you don't have to go there in person, which is very convenient!

6) The first principle when going to the trade mart to purchase goods is to save wherever possible. Therefore, you must budget in advance for food, accommodation, and transportation costs, and then roughly determine the purchase amount based on the types/quantity of goods needed. Of course, don't be too hard on yourself; don't save where you shouldn't.

7) Loading principle: Heavy, large-volume goods that are not easily damaged should be placed at the bottom of the packaging bag. Fragile and valuable goods like jewelry should be placed on top or carried with you.

8) Layout of the trade mart area: There is a corridor connecting the north side of the second floor of zone E in phase I and phase II, so you don't need to exit the north gate of phase I, cross the motor vehicle lane, climb the stairs, and then enter phase II, which is both troublesome and dangerous! The second floors of zones G and H in phase II are also connected, so you don't need to cross the motor vehicle lane from the first floor to enter zone H.

9) Regarding banks: There are construction banks and postal savings on the first and second floors of zone E in phase I of the trade mart, Chouzhou Commercial Bank and Credit Union in zone D, Bank of China and Industrial and Commercial Bank in zone B, and Agricultural Bank in zone A. There is Zhejiang Merchant Bank next to the south gate of phase II, and banks at the entrances of zones F, G, and H!
