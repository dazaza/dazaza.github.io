Riding Diary: The Yellow River waves surge, galloping horses roar in the west wind.

by ditan360 on 2010-06-22 09:53:17

The exclusive manuscript of the Low Carbon Network. In the morning, we were awakened by a hubbub. The person next to me said, look! That's the Yellow River. I looked over and saw a winding stream of blue water. My companion said, never would have thought that the Yellow River isn't yellow, but is so clear in color. I thought of the ancient literati Wang Xizhi and Xie An gathering by the clear stream at Orchid Pavilion, composing poetry couplets. So, I poured a cup of light tea for each of my companions and clinked cups with them. I told them that there was an ancient custom called "qu shui liu shang" (floating cups along a winding stream), and now we are at the source of the Yellow River, substituting tea for wine, performing purification rites by the water, and seeking good luck for our journey. They also jumped up joyfully and drank the tea in one gulp.

As the car approached Xining, all the scenery seemed clearer and more transparent. There was very little suspended dust in the air, and it seemed as if the moisture had been ground away. Breathing felt dry. The basin didn't have strong sunlight, and heavy fog arose in the distance. The occasional residential houses on the side of the road were too ordinary, red bricks and white tiles, like the flat-roofed houses in Guanzhong. More disappointingly, the golden rape flowers weren't in full bloom at this time, and looking out at the slopes brought less joy.

At 11:20, we arrived in Xining City. The altitude of Xining is 2195 meters, and we stopped here, which is already 2000 meters higher than Beijing located in the North China Plain. What a wonderful feeling this is. After retrieving our bikes from the station, we quickly assembled them. The station was bustling with people of various appearances. I noticed carefully, and half of them were tourists, their tour agency-issued hats giving them all away.

Upon arriving in Xining, we couldn't help but try the authentic beef noodles. The three of us pushed our bikes to find a restaurant. We ordered medium-thick noodles. Perhaps due to overly high expectations, we felt average after eating. The diners at the next table, seeing our cycling attire, initiated a conversation with us. When he learned that the purpose of our trip was to promote low-carbon environmental protection, his enthusiasm for us increased even more. He kept giving us thumbs up and praising us. I asked him about some issues regarding going to Xihai, such as the distance from Xining to Xihai Town and the road conditions. He said it was about 130 kilometers, and biking would only take three or four hours.

After bidding him farewell, we decided to head to Xihai immediately. After noon, the sun was no longer gentle, and the ultraviolet rays were abnormally strong. We applied sunscreen to the exposed parts of our skin. Heading west, we sped through the streets of Xining. Suddenly red lights appeared, then crossroads, then several cars blocking the way emerged. Our speed couldn't pick up. It took a long time before we finally broke through the encirclement and left the city.

The broken national highway was difficult to travel, but it was extremely suitable for mountain bike riding. At this moment, a few other cyclists called us over, telling us to follow them back onto the expressway. We asked, won't anyone manage it? They said that people coming by bike all go this way. We couldn't help but marvel at the inclusive spirit of Xining Expressway.

The first stop, Huangyuan. Distance 49 kilometers. The road conditions were good, mostly flat terrain. We followed the fellow cyclists, parched and desperately chasing the fast-moving buses ahead under the scorching sun. I felt shoulder pain, butt pain, and my body felt like it was about to be scorched. Three hours later, I stood dazedly in the county of Huangyuan.

The noodles were quickly digested, and we had lunch at a restaurant named "Dangge'er" in front of Zanpu Lin Ka Temple. Here, the altitude reached 2600 meters, the air was clear, clouds drifted across the blue sky, and the plateau scenery was fully visible. We already dared not expose ourselves to the sun, always avoiding ultraviolet rays. The boss of the restaurant himself was a cycling enthusiast. Over the years, he has hosted countless cyclists from the south and north. Meeting us cyclists naturally made him generous and hospitable. He advised us to change our inner tubes and inflate them to 70 atmospheres of pressure, which would minimize the contact area between the outer tire and the ground, making the friction coefficient closer to that of a road bike.

All the fatigue and discomfort from the train ride suddenly surfaced, so we decided to spend the night here.
