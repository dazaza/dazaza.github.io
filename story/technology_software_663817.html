Extend the evening's lines (Part II)

by chandadada on 2012-02-12 17:05:33

Fashion Show - The next team-building game we will conduct is called "The Coolest Fashion Show." First, each odd-numbered table must select a male participant (note: the most handsome and suave), and each even-numbered table selects a female participant (the most beautiful and elegant). Let’s give a round of applause to welcome our selected male and female participants on stage. Music starts playing (lively and fast-paced).

Everyone, do you think they are handsome? Do you think they are beautiful?

Now don’t be in a hurry, because we’re not going to have them walk the runway just yet. From each table, we need to select one fashion designer. These designers will create fashionable outfits for our models. Odd-numbered tables are required to design women's clothing for the selected male participants, which will then be modeled by the men. Conversely, the even-numbered tables' designers will create men's clothing for the female models, which will be modeled by the women.

Once the designs are completed, the male and female models will wear the designed outfits and walk down the stage runway to music, showcasing their outfits with their charming model steps. Meanwhile, the designers will explain their design concepts to the audience...

Let’s invite the designers up on stage now, and please have the logistics staff prepare the props for them. Design time is five minutes. Everyone, get ready! Timing begins. After the designs are completed, please have the models get ready. DJ, play the music (dynamic and fashionable)!

Prize Drawing - Third Prize

Now it's time for the prize drawing. We’ll start with the third prize, and this method of drawing is quite special. How is it special? It involves all our friends participating in the draw themselves.

Ah, drawing themselves? How does that work exactly? You see, using your sharp eyesight, carefully observe your utensils and see if there is anything different about them compared to others.

Huh? They all look the same, where’s the difference? Music plays (lively and fast-paced).

Please everyone take a closer look at your chopsticks. Yes, chopsticks, chopsticks, chopsticks – quickly! A big prize awaits you all.

Second Prize

It's time again for the prize drawing. This time, our friends will once again draw the prizes themselves. But this time, the requirements are a bit higher. Each friend needs to find a slip of paper nearby, which will say “Congratulations, you’ve won the second prize.” Alright, everyone, let’s begin... Music plays (lively and fast-paced).

After half a minute, you can give a hint: under the chair.

First Prize

Let’s warmly welcome the General Manager or President to draw the first prize for us. Please welcome *** with applause. Music plays (Radetzky March). After the draw, we bid farewell to the winner.

The room number drawn for the first prize will have the lucky partner come on stage for the final PK to win the grand prize. Please have the stage crew bring out the balloons. As the balloons burst, golden flowers scatter everywhere.

Who will ultimately win...? Music plays (gradually strengthening drum music).

Birthday Celebration Segment

In the next segment, we will invite two special friends. Let’s warmly welcome *** and ***. Music plays ().

The host interviews the two in a Q&A format, asking if they know why they were invited… Logistics staff pushes out the cake, and someone with a microphone leads everyone in singing the birthday song while the host improvises. Help light the candles, let the two make a wish… blow out the candles. Music plays for the magic segment.

The two hosts on stage use passionate language to introduce magician Liu Qian, but Liu Qian doesn’t appear. The male host says: I am Liu Qian~ sent here to perform magic for everyone. The male and female hosts start teasing and guide the audience into performing Liu Qian's coin magic. The male host invites an audience member to perform with coins, then invites a lady to find the coin and step on stage to assist in the second close-up magic trick with cards, also involving the audience. For the third large-scale human levitation act, we involve a male participant from the audience as part of the team-building exercise.
