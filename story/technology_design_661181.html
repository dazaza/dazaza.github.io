The statement "sketches have no market in the Spring Festival Gala" seems like a trick played by Hawen.

by caip6924 on 2012-02-08 17:15:26

What is the market? It is demand. Specifically speaking of the Spring Festival Gala, it is of course the audience's demand. Therefore, as the director of the Spring Festival Gala talking about the Gala market, one certainly cannot ignore the audience. Furthermore, regarding Ha Wen's statement that "sketches have no market," I, as one of the demanders, am quite confused. Is it that there are no good sketches or that sketches have no market? This is a question worth pondering. At least, I think Ha Wen's statement reflects her personal preferences, and to discuss the market based on personal likes and dislikes is not a responsible attitude in itself. On January 29, Ha Wen, the director of the Dragon Year Spring Festival Gala, stated in an interview with a media outlet that "sketches on the stage of the Spring Festival Gala no longer have a market." This comment immediately sparked heated discussions among netizens. Many netizens expressed that "it's not that sketches don't have a market, but rather there are no good sketches for the audience to appreciate." One netizen even passionately stated: "I feel that Ha Wen seems a bit arrogant and self-righteous. Whether sketches have a market isn't something you can decide. If you failed as a director, you should sincerely reflect instead of making excuses or even fabricating lies about sketches having no market. Such a person with no depth of thought being qualified as the chief director of the Spring Festival Gala is truly absurd." What concerns me is not whether Ha Wen has depth of thought, but where this conclusion comes from. The conclusion that "sketches have no market" is definitely not derived from any investigation, at most it is a personal preference of Comrade Ha Wen. In such a case, can we then assume that because Ha Wen herself doesn't particularly like sketches, they therefore have no market during the Spring Festival Gala. It seems like Ha Wen has pulled the wool over everyone's eyes regarding the lack of sketch market. Every year after the New Year, people who gather together naturally and unconsciously talk about the popular phrases from the Spring Festival Gala sketches, but not this year. People don't even have the desire to mention the Spring Festival Gala again. Frankly speaking, the language programs directed by Ha Wen were not successful. The failure is related to Zhao Benshan's withdrawal, but more so to Ha Wen's attitude towards sketches. The Spring Festival Gala is a party, a party to liven up the common people's New Year celebration. An important premise for livening up is to make the common people happy. Crossover comedy shows can no longer make the common people laugh, so naturally, the audience will look forward to sketches as an important program type. Regrettably, just as the Spring Festival Gala entered its crucial rehearsal phase, Zhao Benshan mysteriously withdrew, repeatedly claiming it was due to "health reasons." But at such a time, these health reasons seem like excuses, possibly hinting at some hidden circumstances. I can imagine that Ha Wen and Zhao Benshan had inconsistent views on sketches, leading to irreconcilable conflicts, and finally, Zhao Benshan had to withdraw. Ha Wen's first move upon taking charge of the Spring Festival Gala was to cut ties with Zhao Benshan, clearly showing her disregard for sketches.
