Short "Trap" v4

by ww0910844 on 2010-04-18 14:05:07

The market always follows the principle that the greater the hope, the bigger the disappointment. When most people are disheartened and pessimistic, it may be a good time for the overall market. Before the two sessions, the media hyped up so-called "two sessions market rally" and introduced hot sectors during the two sessions, which greatly disappointed many investors. After the two sessions, they again promoted the "second bottoming", but no one knows how low this "second bottom" would be. Is it 2800 points or 1700 points? If it's 2800 points, is there any need for all this折腾 (trouble)? If it's 1700 points, then that would be too terrifying.

In recent days, institutions have been suppressing and accumulating "the petrochemical twins" in preparation for the stock index futures. Friends don't need to be overly panicked. For stocks that have already skyrocketed, resolutely sell them. For blue-chip stocks that haven't taken off yet and stocks with performance expectations, hold on to them firmly. Patiently wait and enjoy the fun of trading. Don't think you're smart enough to be able to sell high and buy low. That's impossible. Because when you sell high, or buy low, are the buyers and sellers fools? I doubt it.

In the near future, focus closely on stocks with performance expectations in the first quarter because only performance is an eternal theme. Moreover, stock trading is about trading expectations; 2009 has already become history.

Additionally, extreme weather has seriously affected the growth of crops. Water and food issues are global problems. People regard food as their heaven. Everything else is trivial. The water utility sector and the agriculture, forestry, animal husbandry, sideline production, and fishery sectors lagged behind other sectors in terms of gains in 2009. They are worth looking forward to. I will continue to firmly hold onto 600323 Nanhai Development, 000833 Guitang Co., Ltd., and 600371 Wanxiang Denuo until the very end, even if it feels like riding a roller coaster, it doesn't matter.
