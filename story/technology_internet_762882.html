**Boss Must-Read: Points to Consider When Selecting Work Phones for Employees**

When using the "Master Enterprise Communication" app, some bosses may choose to let employees use their personal phones, and provide a small subsidy for this. Some companies prefer to issue uniform phones to employees. Both methods are good. If you choose the first method, please be sure to pay attention to the following issues:

1. The differences between smartphones on the market are significant. Although they are all smart devices, due to variations in smartphone operating system versions and manufacturers, the performance of "Master Enterprise Communication" may differ across different phones. Please remind employees to avoid using low-quality, non-brand-name phones as much as possible. These phones may have quality issues, and if problems arise when using "Master Enterprise Communication," it will increase the difficulty of determining whether the problem is with the phone or the software system we provided.

2. Avoid using phones produced many years ago, such as smartphones with a resolution of 240x320. These phones may experience poor display effects and slow operation speeds when using "Master Enterprise Communication."

If you choose the second method, where the company provides uniform phones to employees, sometimes the company may work with carriers to handle phone services collectively. This approach has several advantages:

1. The company doesn't need to pay for the phones directly; instead, by agreeing to a consumption plan, the carrier can gift a phone.

2. The phone numbers belong to the company, so when an employee leaves, the number can be reassigned to someone else.

3. Calls between employees can be free, and some carriers even allow free calls between mobile phones and the company's landline.

However, please also note the following:

1. There are differences in signal quality among carriers in different regions. In some areas, China Telecom may have better signals, while in others, China Unicom or China Mobile may perform better. Therefore, try to consult your employees' opinions.

2. China Mobile phones often have slower internet speeds, and in many areas, only 2G signals are available without 3G. If you need to use "Master Enterprise Communication," especially for features like uploading photos taken on-site, make sure to test the speed beforehand. China Unicom phones sometimes have poor signals in remote areas, so if your employees frequently work in rural areas, please consult their opinions.

3. Preferably choose a 2-year or 1-year contract package from the carrier. Some carriers may offer promotions where the company agrees to a 3-year consumption plan in exchange for a free phone. It’s widely acknowledged that the average lifespan of a smartphone is around 2 years. Choosing a 3-year agreement could result in a large number of phones being obsolete or damaged after 2 years.

Regardless of whether you procure phones through a carrier's unified consumption contract or not, consider the following issues when purchasing phones:

1. What happens if the phone breaks or gets stolen? This could directly affect business operations. For example, if no orders can be placed or sales cannot be reported for a day, it would be difficult for the company to accept. Therefore, it's essential to prepare for such scenarios. We recommend that companies either keep spare phones in stock or encourage employees to use their personal phones temporarily if the company-issued phone malfunctions. Alternatively, the logistics department should be able to quickly purchase replacements.

2. Smartphones consume a lot of power, so when selecting phones, try to pick ones with longer standby times. Generally, phone batteries have specifications like 1500 mAh or 2000 mAh, with higher numbers indicating longer standby times. If the phone can come with two batteries, that would be even better.

3. Finally, after providing phones to your employees, it's recommended to equip them with silicone protective cases, which can significantly extend the lifespan of the phones!

After mobile phones became inseparable from our daily lives, the influence of mobile internet began to penetrate enterprises. It's time to incorporate location information into enterprise management considerations. "Master Enterprise Communication," developed by Zhongtai Group, is a software application that uses location data provided by mobile devices to enhance the efficiency of employee management within a company.

In traditional models, company managers find it difficult to monitor the real-time locations of employees working outside the office. Data collected by employees outside must be transferred via phone calls or recorded upon returning to the office. With the development of smartphones and mobile internet, more convenient location-based management allows companies to manage field employees just as they do office employees.

Through a mobile app, employees can sign in more promptly and accurately, and transfer data collected during fieldwork to the company's internal network in real time. Such needs are concentrated in industries like fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) and logistics express delivery, where there are numerous tasks requiring fieldwork. Statistics show that over 30 million company employees work outside the office, and with the increasing penetration of mobile internet into daily life, the popularization of mobile office software is an inevitable trend.

In addition to basic attendance management, "Master Enterprise Communication" offers comprehensive mobile office services, such as managing/updating customer information via a phone, recording new business opportunities, uploading materials to the company intranet, checking company notifications and receiving new tasks through a phone, writing summaries of work, and applying for expenses or registering travel plans via a phone.
