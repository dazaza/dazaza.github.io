It's been reported that Microsoft has confirmed the name of the next-generation Xbox as "Infinity".

by geekzhang on 2013-05-08 12:39:15

According to a report by the International Business Times website on the morning of May 8 Beijing time, informed sources revealed on Tuesday that Microsoft's next-generation Xbox console will be unveiled on May 21 and has been officially named "Infinity".Recently, the American social news site Reddit released leaked images of the next-generation Xbox console, calling it "Xbox Infinity". Previously, the industry referred to it as either "Xbox 720" or "Durango", with the former being named after its processor similar to the Xbox 360, while the latter was rumored to be the development codename for the next-generation Xbox. Now, insiders have revealed that the next-generation Xbox will indeed be named "Infinity". However, Microsoft stated that the company would not comment on market rumors and speculations.A different leaked image from the NeoGaf forum showed that the next-generation Xbox might simply be named "Xbox" without any numbers or suffixes, much like Apple's naming convention for the latest iPad model. However, an examination of this photo by a user from the famous leak site VGLeaks found that the logo's letter alignment was no longer in a straight line, suggesting that the logo was not created by Microsoft professionals.Recent rumors surrounding the Xbox also include the possibility that Vince Zampella and Jason West's joint production, "Respawn Games", may be launched on the next-generation Xbox. Additionally, the latest installment in the "Call of Duty" series, "Call of Duty: Ghosts", is expected to launch on this new platform, with the game anticipated to be released alongside the next-generation Xbox on May 21.Although Microsoft has not disclosed any official information, the Xbox Infinity is expected to be launched in November 2013, aligning with the release schedule of Sony's PlayStation 4 in the U.S. and Asian markets.
