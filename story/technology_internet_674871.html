Middle-aged man easily believes surrogacy advertisements and gets defrauded of nearly 10,000 yuan in a month.

by dfasd98fh on 2012-02-24 16:15:38

Mr. Li from Cicheng, Ningbo, is in his early fifties.

On the morning of January 21st, while returning from morning exercise at a park, Mr. Li saw a surrogacy advertisement on a utility pole with the title [Searching for Destiny]. The ad featured a picture of a young and beautiful middle-aged woman who was married to a wealthy man. Due to her husband's infertility, she wished to find a healthy man to help her fulfill her dream of motherhood. In return, the man would receive a compensation of 500,000 yuan upon successful completion. Mr. Li was very interested and called the number listed on the ad.

The person on the other end of the line claimed to be from a [brokerage company], stating that they could arrange a meeting between Mr. Li and the woman but required an intermediary fee of over 400 yuan first. Thinking about the substantial reward, Mr. Li transferred the money to the designated account.

When Mr. Li called again to urge them, the [brokerage company] said that in order to ensure the safety of the woman, the man needed to pay a deposit which would be refunded after the meeting. Mr. Li sent more money to the account.

After paying twice, Mr. Li still had not met the desired woman. When he requested another meeting, the [brokerage company] said that a notary fee needed to be paid. Mr. Li, blinded by greed, sent more money.

For the last time, the [brokerage company] charged Mr. Li a commission based on a percentage (3% to 5%) of the large sum promised in the advertisement.

However, despite transferring the money, Mr. Li never met the young and beautiful woman. He only communicated multiple times over the phone with the [brokerage company].

By the time Mr. Li realized he had been scammed and demanded his money back from the [brokerage company], the phone line was no longer answered. Mr. Li quickly reported the incident to the police. Over the course of a month, Mr. Li had sent nearly ten thousand yuan.

Officer Wu Xiaoxiang from the Cicheng Police Station reminded the public: Scams like [Searching for Destiny] or [Surrogacy] have existed for a long time. In reality, the criminals' methods are not sophisticated; they simply exploit the greed of some people who wish to gain wealth and pleasure without effort. Remember this phrase: "There’s no such thing as a free lunch," and you won’t be so easily deceived.
