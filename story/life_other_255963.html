Green tea. Nutritional highlights: The caffeine in tea leaves not only helps to refresh, but the Vitamin C and Vitamin E they contain, especially tea polyphenols, have anti-radiation effects. For white-collar workers who spend long hours in front of computers, radiation is a worrying "byproduct." Eating principle: When you're too irritated to drink plain water or feel like drinking cola, why not replace it with a cup of clear tea?

Coffee, milk. Nutritional highlights: Some people cannot drink milk or simply do not like its taste. However, it's hard to give up on the nutritional value of milk, which is rich in lactocalcium and has a high absorption rate; some people like coffee but choose three-in-one instant coffee packets. The three-in-one combination consists of vegetable fat, white sugar, and coffee powder. Vegetable fat brings harmful trans fats that affect cardiovascular health, and you end up consuming excessive sugar as well. Eating principle: Prepare a cup of low-fat milk (reduces 2/3 of the fat compared to whole milk), add a small spoonful of coffee powder, and use Taigu sugar or Bar sugar (safe sweeteners) for flavoring.

Peanuts. Nutritional highlights: The abundant linoleic acid in nuts can help improve blood circulation in the brain; they also contain rich protein and plant oil, which can satisfy hunger when your stomach growls. White-collar workers who sit for long periods should prepare peanuts, walnuts, almonds, and other nuts. Sitting at a desk for long periods increases the risk of cardiovascular diseases, but regularly eating walnuts and other nut-based foods can significantly reduce the likelihood of developing such conditions. Eating principle: However, peanuts are also high in fat, so intake should be controlled to avoid obesity.

Dried tofu. Nutritional highlights: The standout nutritional benefit of dried tofu is its high calcium content. The greatest feature of dried tofu is its balanced nutrition, containing proteins, fats, and carbohydrates, none of which are lacking or excessive. According to measurements, every 100 grams of dried tofu contains 336 kcal of heat energy, 731 mg of calcium, and less than 16 grams of fat, so eating more won't lead to weight gain. Eating principle: If you miss a meal, eating some dried tofu can both satisfy your hunger and curb cravings. A single vacuum-sealed five-spice dried tofu piece weighs about 20 grams; eating two or three pieces as a snack can supplement 40% of the daily required calcium intake.

Ready-to-eat fruit and vegetable chips. Nutritional highlights: Once-popular pineapple and banana chips are now considered the least healthy among various ready-to-eat fruit and vegetable chips due to their higher energy and sugar content. Nowadays, apples, carrots, even shiitake mushrooms and ginger can be processed into tasty snacks using low-temperature vacuum drying technology, minimizing nutrient loss in fruits and vegetables. Eating principle: Excessive consumption isn’t beneficial since fresh fruits and vegetables remain the best option.

Plum candy and olives. Nutritional highlights: Plum candy and olives can help stop coughs, remove phlegm, and quench thirst. Rich in organic acids, they promote the secretion of digestive fluids and aid digestion. Therefore, plum candies and olives are suitable to eat between meals or after meals rather than on an empty stomach. They also freshen breath, making them indispensable snacks in any office! Eating principle: Consume 2-3 pieces daily after meals.

Dark chocolate. Nutritional highlights: Among all chocolates, dark chocolate contains the lowest amount of sugar and fat. After turning into glucose and entering the bloodstream, it slowly releases energy over 2-3 hours before returning to fasting levels. Hence, eating chocolate when hungry is far more effective than eating biscuits or cakes. Eating principle: For those whose meals are spaced 5-6 hours apart, eating two pieces of dark chocolate (2 cm x 4 cm size) about 3 hours after the first meal can quickly relieve hunger.

Beef jerky, marinated beef. Nutritional highlights: Marinated beef or beef jerky is rich in protein, contains some heme iron, and also has relatively high amounts of zinc, selenium, and B vitamins, which nourish the spleen and stomach, strengthen tendons and bones, and replenish qi and blood. Compared to small packages of marinated beef, beef jerky contains less water and salt. Additionally, the nutritional value of marinated beef is roughly equivalent to half the weight of beef jerky, but its sodium content is twice as high. Eating principle: These snacks are suitable to eat when feeling very hungry.

Seaweed. Nutritional highlights: Nori (seaweed) contains multiple vitamins and minerals, especially iodine. Regular consumption can prevent skin dullness, dry hair, and slow growth caused by iodine deficiency, reduce fat accumulation in the body, and prevent hypertension and coronary heart disease. Eating principle: This thin seaweed won't make you gain weight because it almost contains no fat and has minimal calories!

Oatmeal. Nutritional highlights: Oatmeal provides energy from complex carbohydrates and offers both soluble and insoluble dietary fibers, balancing daily nutritional structure. When work causes mood swings, consider soaking a packet of oatmeal. Its high fiber content slows down digestion, continuously supplying carbohydrates to the bloodstream, providing the body with continuous energy while maintaining blood sugar levels at a consistently high level, preventing dizziness, memory decline, and reduced work efficiency. Additionally, supplementing vitamins related to brain and nerve metabolism, such as B vitamins, which are richer in brown rice and whole wheat, is beneficial. Raw oats requiring long cooking aren't suitable for offices, but instant pure oatmeal, pre-treated at high temperatures, can be enjoyed after microwaving for 2-3 minutes. Some find pure oatmeal too bland and difficult to consume, so pairing it with milk or honey is recommended. From a nutritional perspective, minimally processed whole grains retain the highest nutritional value. Eating principle: If you miss a main meal, having a bowl of oatmeal porridge is a good alternative.
