Facts and Reasons:

I am the plaintiff Zhao Yunhai, and the defendants are the Dao County People's Government and the Dao County Land and Resources Bureau.

In July 2001, the plaintiff signed a land use rights transfer agreement with the Dao County Fugang Fish Farm. The agreement stipulated that the Fugang Fish Farm would transfer its ownership of 250 square meters of land to the plaintiff for use. The Fugang Fish Farm filled in the plaintiff's name on the planning map drawn by the Dao County Planning Bureau and stamped it with the official seal, submitting it to the Planning Bureau. On January 12, 2007, the Dao County Planning and Construction Bureau legally issued a (0701120018) number construction land planning permit to the plaintiff. The plaintiff applied to the Dao County Land and Resources Bureau for a land use certificate with relevant procedures, but the Dao County Land and Resources Bureau has neither informed the reasons nor fulfilled the relevant obligations according to law. In November 2010, the plaintiff learned that the Dao County Land and Resources Bureau had confirmed the plaintiff's land to Xushanlin for use under the Dao Collective Construction Land Use Certificate (2001) No. 0814164. The plaintiff believes that the issuance of the above-mentioned land use certificate by the Dao County Land and Resources Bureau violated legal procedures, contained factual errors, lacked sufficient evidence, and violated legal provisions, causing damage to the plaintiff's legitimate rights and interests. Therefore, the Dao County Land and Resources Bureau's issuance of the Dao Collective Construction Land Use Certificate (2001) No. 0814164 should be revoked according to law.

The first-instance and second-instance courts held that according to Article 63 of the "Land Management Law of the People's Republic of China": The right to use collectively-owned land shall not be transferred, transferred or leased for non-agricultural construction. The Fugang Fish Farm is an agricultural collective economic organization under the jurisdiction of the Fugang Township People's Government of Dao County, and its land nature belongs to collective ownership. The agreement signed between the Fugang Fish Farm and the plaintiff was invalid, therefore not protected by law. The court dismissed the lawsuit on the grounds that the defendant's issuance of the certificate did not have a legal interest relationship with the plaintiff.

The Truth of the Matter:

1. See the evidence file submitted by the Dao County government: Document (1978)037 of the Dao County Revolutionary Committee

In 1978, the Dao County government organized the dispersed independent fishermen into a unit, which was a collective unit, but its land source was government-granted land, clearly stated in the certification document. The base issue: After organizing the dispersed independent fishermen, they must establish production and living bases. For this purpose, the Fugang Commune allocated Dagufutang (more than 200 mu) to the fish farm for fish pond construction...

How can granted land be called collective land? Proving the nature of the land is simple; just provide the original land certification. Why doesn't the government provide it, but instead provides a 1978 document? Is it because the 1978 document is easy to find while the land certification cannot be found, or dare not be provided? Does Document (1978)037 clearly state that it is land granted by the Fugang Commune, trying to fool us as if we don't understand the difference between state-owned land, collective land, and granted land?

According to Article 2 of the "Land Management Law of the People's Republic of China," the state implements a paid-use system for state-owned land according to law. However, the granting of state-owned land use rights within the scope prescribed by law is exempted. According to the "Interim Measures for the Administration of Granted Land Use Rights," after approval by the land management department of the municipal or county people's government and payment of the land use right transfer fee, the land user may transfer, lease, or mortgage the land use rights.

2. See the land transfer fee receipts and issued state-owned land use certificates of the Dao County government

The Dao County Fugang Fish Farm not only signed a transfer agreement with me but also with multiple others. Please see the names on the planning map. Others who signed transfer agreements with the Dao County Fugang Fish Farm in the same way were issued state-owned land use certificates and receipts by the Dao County Land and Resources Bureau.

Evidence One: Internal settlement receipt of Hunan administrative and public institutions 湘财统字 (2002)3339768, payee unit: Fugang Fish Farm, date: December 8, 2002, item: prepayment for land transfer fees, etc., amount: 30,000 yuan, stamped with the official seal of the Dao County Land and Resources Bureau.

Evidence Two: Internal settlement receipt of Hunan administrative and public institutions 湘财统字 (2002)0140677, payee unit: Fugang Fish Farm, date: September 25, 2002, item: prepayment for handling land use procedures, amount: 60,000 yuan, stamped with the official seal of the Dao County Land and Resources Bureau.

Evidence Three: State-owned land use certificate 道国用 (2007)第01031421号 issued by the Dao County Land and Resources Bureau on March 22, 2007. The holder of this certificate is my right neighbor, consistent with the names on the planning map. Besides, many others have also obtained state-owned land use certificates, for which I have evidence.

The above evidence fully proves that the land transfer by the Dao County Fugang Fish Farm was approved by the land management department of the county people's government and the land use right transfer fee was paid. Please ask why it is legal for others to obtain state-owned land use certificates, but when I apply for a state-owned land use certificate, it becomes an illegal collective land transfer?
