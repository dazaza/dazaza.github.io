Cold Rain Honors the Qingming Festival

Suddenly it got cold, and it began to rain.

The Qingming Festival requires some atmosphere. Just before daybreak, fireworks were set off in the distance.

Perhaps, on such days one should reflect for a while. Mostly filled with sorrow, those who have passed are remembered in deep thought. Those left behind recount the events of the past year.

The graves are overgrown with wild grass, and the mountain flowers are in full bloom. Red gravestones are covered with green moss, the inscriptions clear yet ancient, and the bamboo in the woods rustles in the wind...

In my memory, our family graves look like this. It has been a long time since we last paid respects - perhaps ten years. This morning, grandma said that she has already prepared everything for the Qingming Festival memorial service.

Actually, I really want to write something about our ancestors from my great-grandfather down to me, maybe as a kind of family tradition. There are many things I'm not sure about, so I don't dare to write rashly. Moreover, sometimes it is taboo to write such things.

Every time we paid respect to our ancestors in the past, we were very cautious and devout. Despite the fact that they never saw me, after starting school, I knew that my name was on that gravestone, at the end. Perhaps because they passed away too early, the name was added later. Every time, I would earnestly kowtow to them, and then grandpa would say some words beside me. We would place offerings, set off fireworks, and piles of yellow paper would flutter in the wind...

Perhaps, those who have left are watching me grow up. Every year at this time, when I am home, I would go see them. To me, perhaps these ancestors I have never met have no more ties. But to the whole family, they are indispensable. There are no frames of them in the main room of our house, and there is absolutely no memory or recollection about them. However, every year during the Qingming Festival and their memorial days, I hear some reminiscences and sighs.

I have always been afraid of those who have passed. In the past, when someone from a family passed away and a spirit hall was set up, it was especially frightening at night, especially at dusk when the noisy instruments and firecrackers mixed together. I felt particularly terrified throughout the entire night. The departure that left a particularly deep impression on me was my mother's uncle's passing, many years ago. I was still a child then, and only felt particularly cold, but not very afraid, probably because I was a child, or because he was a relative. This was the first real death I witnessed. Later, during elementary school, I saw a person's skull on the mountain, which scared me for many days.

In the mysterious world, there may be another one where the deceased exist, watching their loved ones come during the Qingming Festival, and then leave...
