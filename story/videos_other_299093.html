>> How to Do a Good DM Design

Although advertising delivery has many advantages, your DM may still end up being ignored or even hated. The best things, like a rare gem, if its brilliance is not known to the public at the right time, will ultimately just be a piece of ordinary stone. A good DM is not created blindly. When designing a DM, if you consider more aspects around its strengths beforehand, it will help improve the effectiveness of the DM's advertising.

1. Designers must thoroughly understand the product and be well-versed in consumer psychological habits and rules. Knowing oneself and the enemy ensures victory in every battle.

2. People generally dislike unpleasantness. New designs should be fresh and creative, with exquisite and beautiful printing to attract more attention.

3. DM delivery and design formats have rules that can be flexibly adapted according to specific situations, allowing for free expression without creating negative effects.

4. Fully consider its folding method, size, actual weight, and mailing convenience.

5. You can add some small tricks in the folding method, such as drawing inspiration from traditional foreign paper-folding art to create something visually striking, but ensure it remains easy for recipients to unfold and read.

6. When selecting images, choose patterns that have a strong correlation with the message being conveyed to stimulate memory.

7. Consider the charm of colors.

8. A good DM design should avoid being too dense or scattered, forming a series to accumulate advertising capital. In the eyes of ordinary consumers, DMs are often no different from the large flyers handed out on the street—roughly printed, poorly designed, and considered a type of advertisement to avoid. However, to impress and engage independent-minded consumers, putting in effort on your DM is essential. In DM design, the difference between high-quality and trash is often just a step away. To make your DM a premium piece rather than waste, you need to employ some effective advertising techniques to enhance its impact. Effective DM advertising techniques can make your DM look better, more appealing, and serve as a bridge to establish good interactive relationships between businesses and consumers. These include:

1. Selecting appropriate delivery targets.

2. Designing an attractive envelope to create a positive impression.

3. Writing a summary of important content on the back of the envelope to increase the open rate.

4. Ensuring addresses and recipient names are neatly written.

5. Including a letter to consumers in the DM.

6. Addressing the recipient by name in the letter’s opening to create a sense of intimacy and encourage reading interest.

7. Using sincere, down-to-earth, enthusiastic, and polite language to make the recipient feel close.

8. Keeping the content concise, with clear indications of purchase locations and methods.

9. Including a feedback form or order slip.

10. Using a regular letter format so that the recipient thinks it’s a letter from a friend, increasing the open rate.

11. Designing it in a three-dimensional or series format to catch attention.

12. Finding ways to guide consumers to read it repeatedly, even making it an art piece worth collecting.

13. Timely handling of consumer feedback.

14. Repeated mailing can deepen impressions.

15. Depending on the situation, using single, phased, or repeated distribution formats.

16. Frequently using question-based delivery methods in Shenyang, as they usually encourage consumers to answer questions through rewards, achieving two-way communication, which is more engaging than introduction-style DMs.

DM design is also one of the print products. When designing, the following issues need to be considered:

Bleed: For the dimensions you design, there should be an additional 3mm on all sides (top, bottom, left, and right). If it's an irregular shape design, extend the inner image by 5mm across the entire DM (the same applies to irregular shapes and standard rectangular DMs, as there may be significant deviations during die-cutting, so the bleed size is generally larger). This includes special processes like fill-in-the-blank sections, commercial immigration services.

Special Processes: For processes like gold/silver printing, gold/silver foil stamping, UV coating, embossing, etc., you need to create a separate plate for the areas requiring these special processes, using single-color fills.
