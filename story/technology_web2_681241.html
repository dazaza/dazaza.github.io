Five young people pretending to be police officers robbed drivers ten times in two months and were caught

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-02-28 12:06:54

By Li Jiefu, Today's Reporter; Correspondents Wang Yongqing and Zhang Ya

Five young men, without much capital and unwilling to seek help from their parents, wanted to independently start a business. In order to quickly earn their "first pot of gold" for their careers, they came up with an idea — pooling money to buy fake police uniforms, fake batons, and other items, then robbing passing large trucks on the national highway. Over the span of two months, they committed dozens of疯狂 robberies. The main suspect came from a wealthy family, making him part of the "rich second generation."

Case Details:

Frequent Robberies on Eight-Wheeled Trucks

At 3:50 AM on February 3rd, the Shenze County Public Security Bureau received an alarm from an eight-wheeled truck driver who reported being stopped and robbed in Shenze County. Several suspects were driving a black car, wielding pickaxe handles and machetes, and stole 200 yuan from him.

Subsequently, the Shenze County Public Security Bureau received several similar alarms.

The Second Detachment of the Criminal Investigation Team of the Shenze County Public Security Bureau discovered through investigation that similar cases had occurred multiple times in Anguo and Anping.

After extensive interviews and investigations, as well as reviewing surveillance footage before and after the incidents, the investigators identified a black HaiMa car. Through meticulous investigation and descriptions provided by the victims, the suspects gradually came to light.

The number of robberies was so numerous that the suspects themselves couldn't count them all.

On the afternoon of February 24th, with the strong cooperation of the Shijiazhuang Municipal Public Security Bureau and the Anguo Municipal Public Security Bureau, the vehicle of the suspects was successfully located.

"At the time, there were four people (Huo Mou, Jiao Mou, Zang Mou, Ji Mou) inside the car, plotting their next crime," said the investigating officers. The police swiftly arrested the four suspects. After intensive questioning, another suspect, Diao Mou, was captured in his rented apartment. As a result, all members of the criminal gang were apprehended.

Upon interrogation, the suspects confessed to the crime of robbing passing large trucks.

Investigations revealed that from January to late February this year, the group frequently committed crimes, targeting passing large trucks in the areas of Anguo and Shenze along the Anxin Line, and in the areas of Shenze and Anping along the Zhengrao Line. They used violent coercion with weapons such as machetes, pickaxe handles, and electric batons to rob drivers of their belongings. Due to the high frequency of robberies, they could not recall exactly how many times they had committed these crimes.

According to confessions, the five individuals committed numerous crimes between January and the end of February this year. They would come out every few days to rob, targeting three or four vehicles each time, demanding between 200 to 2000 yuan per vehicle. If the money was paid, they would let the vehicles go; if not, they would smash items. They also used tactics like smashing windows and headlights to force the truck drivers out of their vehicles, searching the cars and the drivers themselves, and stealing their belongings. They committed dozens of crimes in total, involving amounts totaling tens of thousands of yuan.

Currently, the five suspects have been criminally detained, and the case is still under further investigation.

Call for Victims to Report Crimes

Although the case has been solved, the investigating officers stated that capturing the suspects was just one-fourth of the investigative work. A lot more follow-up work remains to be done. "Because the victims are passing drivers, confirming the details of the cases is a huge challenge."

Here, we remind our readers: Truck drivers who encountered robberies along the Anxin Line and Zhengrao Line in Shenze, Anguo, and Anping counties in January and February 2012, please report your cases to the Second Detachment of the Criminal Investigation Team of the Shenze County Public Security Bureau as soon as possible to expedite the case resolution. Contact number: 0311-83522691.

Investigation:

Walking the Path of Crime for "Independence"

The investigating officers introduced that the five suspects were all from Anguo City, Baoding. Only one was 23 years old, born in 1989, while the other four were all post-90s.

So, how did they end up on the path of crime?

The main suspect, Huo Mou, first worked in Beijing, then returned to Anguo in 2011 to run a fruit stall. Huo Mou's parents had been in business for many years, and the family was well-off, making him a typical example of a "rich second generation."

It is understood that just before the Spring Festival this year, Huo Mou was preparing for his wedding. Prior to this, his parents had bought him a property in a bustling area of Anguo City and a minivan, which was supposed to be used to fetch his bride.

Reasonably speaking, having both a house and a car, what else could Huo Mou possibly lack?

Yet, Huo Mou was the earliest initiator of this gang. "Because I wanted to be independent and start my own business, but what should I do? Considering I didn't have enough capital and didn't want to rely on my parents, I had to use the resources at hand. —— With a house and a car, I could find a few like-minded people and start a business that doesn't require capital."

The five met in an internet cafe, spending time together playing online games, drinking, and eating meals. They lived together in Huo Mou's house. Later, under Huo Mou's suggestion, they pooled their money to buy pickaxe handles, machetes, electric batons, and fake police uniforms. Starting from the beginning of the year, they began robbing large trucks passing through Anguo along the Anxin Line, mainly targeting "eight-wheelers." For trucks with license plates bearing the character "Ji," they demanded less than 500 yuan, while for out-of-province trucks, they demanded at least 2000 yuan. If the money wasn't given, they would smash the headlights; if necessary, they would break the windows, forcing the drivers out of their vehicles, searching the cars and the drivers themselves, and robbing them of their belongings, succeeding repeatedly.

After each successful robbery, they would first refuel their car, divide the money, then go eat, play online games, and spend the money. Once it was spent, they would rob again.

In this way, within less than two months, the gang committed dozens of crimes.

Trapped in the Robbery Cycle

Huo Mou admitted that after participating in a few robberies, he wanted to stop. However, the other members were addicted to the process —— earning money so quickly! Whenever they ran out of money, they would go rob, squander the proceeds, and repeat the cycle, trapping themselves in a vicious loop.

According to the suspects' confession, during the robberies, they did not physically harm the drivers, but they did encounter resistance. Once, a failed robbery attempt resulted in their rear tail light being smashed by a large truck driver.

On February 23rd, Jiao Mou dressed in a fake police uniform impersonated a police officer checking for violations, stopping passing vehicles. Once stopped, they demanded money, and if refused, they would resort to robbery.

When questioned by the investigating officers why they chose to commit crimes wearing fake police uniforms, they replied, "This makes stopping cars easier. As long as the car stops, it's easier to carry out the robbery."

When asked, "Aren't you worried about being recognized since you're using your own car?" They responded, "We cover the license plate with a white towel or camouflage during the crimes, so we don't have to worry about being recognized."
