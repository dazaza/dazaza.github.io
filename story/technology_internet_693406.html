The starting point of the statute of limitations for joint and several liability guarantees

by ai9ewud2q on 2012-03-06 15:57:35

The statute of limitations for guarantee lawsuits involving suing borrowers first and then general guarantors

For cases involving general guarantees on loans, the lender can file a lawsuit against both the borrower and the general guarantor as co-defendants or can sue the borrower alone first. After suing the borrower alone, the lender can decide whether to sue the guarantor based on whether the borrower fulfills their loan repayment obligations. Then, how does the statute of limitations for general guarantees start when you sue the borrower first and then the general guarantor? According to Article 34, Paragraph 1 of the Interpretation of Issues Concerning the Guarantee Law: [If a creditor of a general guarantee files a lawsuit or arbitration against the debtor before the end of the guarantee period, the statute of limitations for the guarantee contract begins from the date the judgment or arbitration award takes effect.] Therefore, the statute of limitations for suing the general guarantor later starts from the date the court's judgment on the loan contract becomes effective. However, if this time is used as the starting point for the statute of limitations for suing the general guarantor later, three issues should be noted: First, the lender must sue the borrower within the guarantee period. If the lender sues the borrower after the guarantee period has expired, since the guarantee responsibility of the general guarantor has already been waived, it is no longer meaningful to determine the starting point of the statute of limitations for suing the general guarantor later. Second, the lender did not include the general guarantor as a co-defendant when suing the borrower. If the general guarantor and the borrower were sued as co-defendants, there would be no need to further determine the starting point of the statute of limitations. Third, only "judgment" taking effect is specified here. So, if the lender and the borrower resolve the issue through mediation during litigation, can the effective date of the mediation order also serve as the starting point for the statute of limitations for suing the general guarantor later? The author believes that the right of action and the statute of limitations for the general guarantor should not be denied due to different methods of resolving the loan contract case; therefore, the effective date of the mediation order can also be considered the starting point for the statute of limitations for suing the general guarantor later.

The starting point for the statute of limitations for joint liability guarantees

For joint liability guarantees, as long as the lender legally asserts the guarantee rights against the guarantor within the guarantee period, the role of the guarantee period ends, and the statute of limitations for the guarantee contract begins to calculate. Unlike general guarantees where the statute of limitations for the guarantee contract is determined by filing a lawsuit against the borrower, for joint liability guarantees, the statute of limitations begins from the day the written notice requiring the guarantor to assume the guarantee responsibility reaches the guarantor. According to Article 34, Paragraph 2 of the Interpretation of Issues Concerning the Guarantee Law: [If the creditor of a joint liability guarantee requires the guarantor to assume the guarantee responsibility before the end of the guarantee period, the statute of limitations for the guarantee contract begins from the day the creditor requires the guarantor to assume the guarantee responsibility.]
