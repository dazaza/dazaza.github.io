By reporter Luo Shanshan, reporting from Guangzhou — "People may think that low price and relatively lower quality are characteristics of small cars, but Ford wants to inform consumers that the Fiesta is not only fuel-efficient and easy to park, but also of high quality with the best soundproofing effect in its class," said Mark Ovenden, President of Changan Ford Mazda Automobile Co., Ltd. He was concerned that Chinese consumers' general perception that small cars have low technological content might weaken the efforts made by the new Fiesta in this aspect. From soundproofing effects to safety ratings and body structure, Ford has been striving to convince Fiesta car owners and potential customers that they are buying a global model with the quality of mid-to-high-end cars, not just a makeshift small car.

As the only American auto giant that did not go bankrupt or seek government assistance during the financial crisis, Ford hopes to expand its 2% market share in this largest automobile producing country by introducing global models tailored for Chinese consumers and developing markets in second- and third-tier cities. Even if the Chinese automobile market faces an impending downturn, Ford still has confidence that its business in this region can exceed the industry's average growth rate.

Targeting young consumers

At 10 a.m. on May 20, more than 200 journalists across the country sat in front of their computers, waiting for the launch of the 2011 Fiesta. For this "fashionable" launch method, Jeff Guyton, General Manager of Changan Ford Mazda Sales Company, believed that it was closer to the target customers of the 2011 Fiesta - those who spend 4-5 hours on the Internet every day and do not often watch TV, "we choose their most commonly used channel to communicate information."

In addition to the online launch, Ford has opened up a "Carnival Type World" for car owners and potential customers. Users can upload videos, experience games, obtain the latest fashion entertainment information, and share their driving feelings and exchange information with friends in the virtual community of the Fiesta.

But for these economically weaker target groups, the price of the 2011 Fiesta at 79,900 yuan - 112,900 yuan did not bring them much surprise, and was even 1,000 yuan more expensive than the old model, unlike many competitors who add features without increasing prices.

Clearly, Changan Ford did not view price as the biggest weapon for the Fiesta. In Jeff Guyton's opinion, what attracts young consumers most is the dynamic design of the Fiesta's appearance, "This is one of the most beautiful cars on the road in China." In addition, the excellent soundproofing effect and audio system of the 2011 Fiesta in its class cater to the tastes of young people. Moreover, "The Fiesta is the first small car in the world to receive the highest safety rating in all major automobile markets, using boron steel stronger than ordinary steel and equipped with six airbags, which are usually configurations found in mid-to-high-end cars," said Xing Gang, Executive Vice President of the sales company to reporters.

The biggest adjustment for the 2011 Fiesta lies in the fact that the three-box model has its own independent name - "Fengchao". The company believes that this not only facilitates better communication between the three-box Fiesta model and its target consumers but also further enhances the market recognition of the three-box model. In terms of price, the four models of Fengchao are the same as the two-box car.

Pushing into second- and third-tier cities

As Ford's first global model, the Ford Fiesta has sold 1.35 million units globally since its launch in October 2008. Data from JATO Dynamics, an automotive data and intelligence statistics agency, shows that in the first quarter of this year, the Fiesta sold 102,859 units in Europe, becoming the best-selling small car in Europe in March and the first quarter of this year, as well as the second best-selling car in Europe.

In the joint venture small car market in China, the Fiesta also has a relatively clear advantage.

Since Ford introduced the Fiesta to the Chinese market in March 2009, cumulative sales have reached 140,000 units. Last year, Changan Ford sold 75,199 Fiestas, an increase of nearly 60% year-on-year.

During the same period, the sales of competitors Fit, Yaris, and Polo were 33,575 units, 27,000 units, and 115,609 units respectively. What made Changan Ford dissatisfied was that the performance of the three-box Fiesta, which should be more in line with Chinese tastes, was not prominent, accounting for only 30% of the overall sales of the Fiesta. On the contrary, the previously criticized "head but no tail" two-box car became increasingly popular. For example, the monthly sales of the two-box model Qida from Dongfeng Nissan often exceeded 10,000 units.

To break this situation, Changan Ford not only gave the three-box Fiesta a new name but also aligned its price with the two-box model, which undoubtedly had great appeal to the second- and third-tier city markets emphasized by the enterprise. "The current sales ratio of the two-box and three-box Fiesta is 70% and 30%, respectively. With our sales push into second- and third-tier city markets, the sales ratio will trend towards 60% two-box and 40% three-box," Jeff Guyton said at the press conference.

It is understood that Changan Ford will continue to add 100 dealerships this year based on the 100 authorized dealerships added last year, with 70% distributed in second- and third-tier markets in China. By 2015, the number of Ford dealerships in China will at least double.

"The second- and third-level markets of today will become first-level markets tomorrow, and we need to ensure that we enter this market today! Therefore, an important step in our market share plan is to ensure that consumers in second- and third-level markets can access Changan Ford dealers starting now. We have also seriously considered our product matrix to ensure that we can provide the products needed by consumers in second- and third-level markets, which is precisely why we made the price of Fengchao more competitive."

Investing 2.3 billion yuan in a transmission factory

The intensification of competition in the small car market and second- and third-tier cities is also an unavoidable challenge for Changan Ford. Data shows that from 2005 to 2008, the share of the A0 and A00 level markets in China continuously decreased to 38.1%, 36.2%, 32.4%, and 29.7%. Stimulated by relevant government subsidy policies, the market share of small cars in 2009 was only 22.8%. Entering 2010, supporting policies were gradually withdrawn, leading to continuous declines in the sales of A0-level cars, especially 1.3L models. By the first half of last year, the market share of small cars had dropped another 1.2 percentage points. Clearly, relying solely on small cars cannot secure the long-term development of the enterprise.

Last year, Changan Ford ranked ninth among passenger car manufacturers with annual sales of 406,400 units, surpassing Toyota Motor Corporation's tenth place by 20,000 units. Among them, the Focus sold 172,270 units, while the flagship model Mondeo-CD391's sales were even less than the annual sales of the two-box Fiesta, at only 51,600 units, holding a 3.06% share of the mid-size car market.

To reverse the decline of the flagship model in the Chinese market, Changan Ford launched the 2011 Mondeo-CD391 in March, equipped with Ford's latest global power technology system - EcoBoost GTDi direct-injection turbocharged engine and PowerShift dual-clutch transmission. In its first month of release, the model sold nearly 7,000 units, surpassing the past six months' average monthly sales of the Mondeo by nearly 2,400 units.

Regrettably, less than two months after its launch, some of the new Mondeo-CD391 models were recalled by Changan Ford due to "possible interference between some air conditioning auxiliary heater power lines and the air filter bracket, causing wear of the insulation material of the power line and short circuit," raising doubts about the results of Ford's "engine swap."

Introducing new technologies is only part of Ford's increased investment in China.

The day before the new Fiesta was launched, Changan Ford signed a memorandum of understanding with the Chongqing municipal government, announcing plans to invest approximately $3.5 billion (about RMB 23 billion) in building a new transmission plant in Chongqing. Construction is expected to begin by the end of July 2011, with production commencing in the fourth quarter of 2013. The new plant will have advanced transmission production lines and an initial annual capacity of 400,000 units. These transmissions will be installed in multiple Ford-branded products produced in China before 2015.

"The first is a new whole vehicle plant that will produce the Ford Focus, the second is a new engine plant, and now we have committed to building a new transmission plant. This move will make Changan Ford and Chongqing one of the largest manufacturing technology centers in China, integrating car assembly, engine, and transmission production," said Mark Ovenden. The new plant is the third major strategic investment announced by Changan Ford in recent months. After the plant starts production, Chongqing, a key municipality in China, will become Ford's largest manufacturing base outside of Detroit.
