How to Turn Your Phone Into a Lifesaving Device

by geekzhang on 2013-05-16 16:59:11

Imagine this scenario: You're enjoying a rare holiday, hiking alone in the mountains. At this point, you decide to take an alternate route and explore where another trail leads. Before you know it, you realize there's no path ahead, just trees and shrubs. After wandering around for a bit, you have no idea which way to go. As night falls, you realize you're lost.

Getting lost is not a fun experience. I have some experience in this area and can share with you what can help in such situations. The first element: fire. We all know clichés like how a single spark of light in the dark can dispel all darkness, but do you know that it’s true? In the terrifying silence of a dark forest, a single flame can provide immense psychological comfort and help you find flammable wood to keep the fire burning.

If you can grasp some other crucial information, it will give you hope and prevent the suffering that comes after getting lost, such as knowing where you are, the direction you’re heading, and how to get out of the situation.

In this article, I will tell you how important it is to carry a mobile phone in a wild environment. The importance of carrying a phone is not about being able to call 911 for help — after all, many deep forests have no signal. All the little tricks introduced below can be used on your phone and even help you survive in areas without signal.

5 Tips to Increase Your Chances of Survival

My whole family was once obsessed with watching documentaries on Netflix about missing people who were rescued just before death. What amazed me is that each scenario was highly similar. Each emergency happened because the parties involved went ahead without any research on their destination. Actually, simply knowing a little information could completely avoid such situations: knowing where you are, knowing the direction you're going, knowing when it will get dark, knowing basic survival rules, and knowing how to correct things when they start going wrong.

What bad things can happen in the wild? Most people are completely unprepared for this. Here are a few simple examples: There are many poisonous plants and dangerous animals in the wild, there is a risk of falling during travel, and hypothermia, among others.

Avoid Blind Movement

The most common mistake is that when people get lost, they often blindly head in the direction they think is right based on their feelings. More often than not, they wander around trying every direction, making them even more lost than before. For this, a smartphone app called 4DCompass has emerged to solve this problem.

When holding a phone with 4DCompass installed, the app will display the horizon ahead and show the compass-determined degree orientation. As you can see from the screenshot, it will display East, South, West, and North.

If you activate the Towns function, the compass will show the distance to the nearest famous places in the world. Of course, I wouldn't walk 370 kilometers to Quebec just to get out of these Maine woods, but at least I can produce a sense of direction through familiar geographical locations.

I had seen the relationship between Maine and Quebec on a map, so I have a basic concept of direction in my mind, although everything around me looks completely indeterminate.

Of course, if you have a GPS device, you can completely get rid of this terrible situation. But who carries a TomTom or Garmin navigation device in their pocket regularly? However, smartphones are different; you definitely have one with you, right?

The problem is that you can't use apps like Google Maps in areas without signal, because these apps rely on signals to obtain data. Don't worry, your Android device can connect to an excellent offline GPS navigation app called MapsWithMe.

This app allows you to download the complete map of your destination before you go there. When I first downloaded this app, I downloaded the map of Maine first, so no matter where I go in Maine, I can use the offline map anytime. If you're interested, you can download the paid version of the software, which provides more detailed maps.

Finally, to avoid blind exploration, you can also use a flashlight app tool installed on an Android device. My favorite is an app called Flashlight. When it comes to choosing flashlight apps, there's no "best" because it's too simple to implement. Just turn on the flash of your phone's camera, and you can illuminate the front.

You might wonder how you can get out of trouble before the rapidly depleting battery runs out while running GPS and keeping the camera flash on. Yes, my solution is to buy a AA battery charger, so you can use these apps as long as possible while charging. Believe it or not, according to the iGopowerXtender Amazon ranking, the AA battery charger can provide enough power to play music for 28 hours, play games for 20 hours, or talk for 10 hours.

I personally have seen charging devices in use and confirmed that they work perfectly. So if you're going into the wild, at least bring one of these and a few AA batteries, just in case.

Wilderness Survival Rules

Now you know which direction to go to get out of these woods, perhaps you realize it will take at least one to two days to walk out, so you need to pitch a tent and think about what you can eat.

No matter where you are, you should be able to find edible food. Of course, many wilderness survival experts suggest not using this option unless absolutely necessary, as too much food may be dangerous if consumed. But if you can't find edible insects or ants, you can try vegetation. This is where the EdibleVegetation app comes in handy.

Select your location, and the app will search for edible foods in your area.

Each item will provide detailed pictures of the plant and many useful tips, informing whether the plant or parts of it are edible and how to consume them.

Of course, besides food, you also need to understand other survival rules. You need to know how to make a covering, how to maintain body temperature to prevent hypothermia, and how to find water sources, etc.

If I were to recommend a simple and practical app to those who are about to go on a wilderness adventure, I would choose U.S.ArmySurvivalGuide. If your country offers a similar survival guide, try to download one. This app provides very detailed information on how wilderness survivors handle the psychological stress of getting lost and how to prepare life-saving equipment, etc.

This app actually consists of many chapters, like an e-book. You can search for keywords or chapters based on what you immediately want to know.

All the articles are very detailed, containing a lot of descriptive details and images to help readers accomplish tasks.

There are also unexpected situations, such as getting injured by falling from a tree while looking for food, or making some other tragic mistake. This situation could affect life-and-death matters, so knowing how to treat wounds will greatly increase your chances of survival. ARFirstAid can be considered your lifeguard (literally).

This app can quickly search for corresponding rescue methods based on the type of injury suffered, providing step-by-step detailed guidance to help the injured person.

Unlike the American Red Cross app, it won’t tell you to call 911 for help in emergencies. Instead, even if you're seriously injured, it will do its best to guide you on how to handle it. Of course, any first aid app can achieve the same effect as long as it's written by experts and contains detailed content.

As you can see, these apps essentially provide information. Therefore, if you're lost, having enough information is the most important thing. If you're at home, you can certainly Google how to do something, but if you're lost in the wilderness, you have no way of knowing how to make a waterproof shelter before a storm arrives. At that moment, you'll thank yourself for having your phone with you and being able to look up the information on it to get out of danger.
