Experts claim that the conflict between the US and Iran is unlikely to escalate into a world war.

On February 6, 2012, the US Marine Corps AAV7 amphibious assault vehicles participated in large-scale amphibious combat exercises codenamed "Fierce Alligator 2012", with some media outlets suggesting the exercise was aimed at Iran.

According to reports by Global Times' Li Liang, as the new year began, the long-standing Iranian nuclear issue did not see any relief but instead became more volatile. The US and Europe joined forces with allies to impose a ban on Iranian oil, while Iran threatened to block the Strait of Hormuz to choke off the world's oil supply. With both sides drawing red lines, the shadow of war loomed over the Gulf once again. The Global Times forum invited renowned international political and military observer Liu Linchuan and Xiao Yun (online name pupu) to engage in an online exchange with netizens, providing detailed analysis on the military strength comparison between the US and Iran, their war red lines, the possibility of war, and elaborating on China's role and interests in the US-Iran standoff.

The US and Iran have drawn their respective war red lines, and if a war were to break out, it would not spread globally.

A netizen asked about the current tense situation between the US and Iran and the likelihood of a war breaking out. Liu Linchuan stated that whether a war would break out depends on four elements: strategic interests, power base, risk cost, and environmental background for both conflicting parties. Currently, the US has announced two red lines for launching a military strike against Iran: Iran cannot develop nuclear weapons and cannot block the Strait of Hormuz. From the first perspective, if Iran develops nuclear weapons, it will have a significant impact on neighboring countries, severely threatening the US's special ally Israel, and greatly weakening the US's ability to militarily interfere in the Gulf region. Therefore, for the US, Iran developing nuclear weapons poses a serious challenge to the global hegemony status of the US and the world order led by the US, directly affecting the strategic interests of the US. Thus, Liu Linchuan believes that the US's threat of military action is real.

The second red line is even clearer. A large amount of oil and liquefied natural gas produced in the Gulf region needs to pass through the Strait of Hormuz for export. If Iran blocks the Strait of Hormuz, it would be equivalent to cutting off half of the world's oil lifeline. This would deal a heavy blow to the US and Western economies, exacerbating the recession in some Western countries already caught in a financial crisis storm, which is absolutely intolerable for the US and the West.

"If Iran becomes unable to restrain itself due to the tense situation and blocks the Strait of Hormuz first, the US will definitely lead its Western allies to launch an immediate and fierce attack on Iran, with a 100% probability of military action," said Liu Linchuan. "Therefore, comprehensively speaking, the possibility of war breaking out in Iran is very high. But the process is dynamically changing all the time, whether it can be avoided or when it will break out depends on the game between the US and Iran. As long as there is an opportunity that the US considers good, the US has a great possibility of starting a war. If the US and Iran go to war and fight a regular war face-to-face, the US has a high probability of achieving quick victory and reaching its strategic goals."

Regarding the concerns of the US in initiating a war, Liu Linchuan pointed out that the biggest concern for the US is Iran's geographical location and its retaliatory capability. Iran is located on the narrow northern shore of the Strait of Hormuz. If it uses anti-ship missiles to fire at passing oil tankers, it will have a significant impact on the world's oil supply. This would have severe consequences for the US and Western economies, exacerbating the recession in some Western countries already caught in a financial crisis storm, which is a risk that the US and the West must consider. If the US does not have confidence in delivering a fatal blow to Iran and directly destroying Iran's ability to block the Strait of Hormuz, the US must be extremely cautious about this risk.

A netizen asked what impact the US-Iran war would have on the world situation. Liu Linchuan analyzed that from an economic perspective, the world oil market would experience significant fluctuations, and every oil-importing country would be affected, just to varying degrees. For those countries with significant domestic oil production potential, the outbreak of a Gulf oil crisis would provide them with an extraordinary opportunity to gain ample benefits from the crisis and hit the national economy of potential rivals. A typical example is the US.

From a political point of view, if the US-Iran war breaks out easily, the international community cannot help but worry about the unilateralism and militaristic approach of the US. To a certain extent, it would lead to the re-alignment of different countries in the world, forming new visible or hidden camps. The world will not become peaceful and united because of the US-Iran war; on the contrary, it will sow more complex seeds, fostering new oppositions and alignments. However, since external countries do not have direct intervention interests, the possibility of the US-Iran war escalating into a large-scale war covering the entire world is not very high.

The US military has obvious four major advantages over the Iranian military and will quickly win the frontal battle.

A netizen questioned whether Iran's military force could possibly counter the US military offensive. Liu Linchuan believed that compared to Iran, the US military had several significant advantages: one, in terms of using space for comprehensive reconnaissance, surveillance, communication, and navigation. The US can conduct comprehensive reconnaissance of Iran's strategic depth through its space reconnaissance and surveillance capabilities, while Iran's space countermeasure capabilities are seriously insufficient, leaving it with few options. In this way, the US military may have an "informational unidirectional transparency" advantage over Iran.

Two, the US has a clear advantage in electronic warfare. Liu Linchuan said that many people like to list the range and hit rate of various air defense missiles, but if they are overwhelmed in electronic warfare, these air defense missiles cannot function effectively. At that point, the range and hit rate of air defense missiles are only on paper, and the hit rate cannot guarantee even 10%, making air defense operations severely ineffective.

Three, the US aviation forces have a clear advantage, with strong air strike capabilities. Combined with the US's obvious advantage in information reconnaissance capabilities, it is possible to conduct an asymmetric war of precision strikes against Iran through air strikes without entering ground battles too early, rendering Iran's ground forces ineffective. This is the asymmetric war controlled by air that the US has been practicing since the 1991 Gulf War. This threat is very serious for Iran.

Four, cyber warfare. The US is the birthplace of cyber attacks, the home of network root servers, and the manufacturer of a large number of software and hardware, giving it a high advantage and practical experience in this area. The recent瘫paralysis of Iran's Bushehr nuclear power plant by computer viruses launched by the US and Israel is a clear proof. Modern electronic computers are deeply integrated into all aspects of the national economy, so networks can also attack all aspects of the opponent's national economy. As a new type of military means, cyber attacks can cause great chaos to the opponent, causing economic losses, and intentionally spreading various rumors on the network, severely undermining the morale of the opponent's people, disintegrating the opponent from within.

"In short, if the US and Iran really choose to fight a regular war face-to-face, the possibility of the US achieving a swift victory is very high," said Liu Linchuan.

"China will only appear as a mediator and peacemaker"

Regarding China's attitude towards the Iranian issue, Liu Linchuan stated that China's position on the Iranian issue has always been clear: First, Iran cannot develop nuclear weapons. Apart from the five major countries - the US, Russia, China, the UK, and France - that have the right to legally possess nuclear weapons, China does not support any other country in developing or possessing nuclear weapons. Second, in the Middle East, not only should Iran not develop nuclear weapons, but Israel should not possess nuclear weapons either. Third, the US must provide reliable intelligence about Iran's nuclear program and concrete evidence to give China sufficient basis for judgment. Fourth, China consistently advocates resolving issues primarily through negotiation and mediation. Fifth, unless the international community unanimously confirms that Iran is developing nuclear weapons and the UN passes new sanctions measures, no other country should interfere with normal trade between China and Iran. Whether the US and Iran are verbally attacking each other or truly engaging in armed conflict, China will only appear as a mediator and peacemaker.
