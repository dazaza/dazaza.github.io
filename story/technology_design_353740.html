Dragon's Valley Cheats Reading "The Vegetarian Root Camp" Random Thoughts 11 [Original]

by sdhyxcu7m on 2010-04-30 21:35:52

38. To cultivate virtue, one must forget fame and fortune; to read books, one must have deep devotion. If one truly wants to cultivate the mind and improve moral integrity, they cannot start with the pursuit of fame and wealth, otherwise their virtue will have flaws and they won't genuinely reach a noble peak. Studying should involve full commitment, carefully savoring the principles in books and absorbing their nourishment, not merely for pretentiousness or to satisfy utilitarian desires, otherwise it's hard to achieve profound learning. In real life, we can't demand that all cultivated individuals possess such virtue, nor can we require all scholars to forget fame and fortune and wholeheartedly create new knowledge and serve society, because such demands are completely unrealistic. Seeking reputation through proper means is not entirely wrong; what's important is not to take "seeking reputation" as the goal and use improper methods to obtain it. Reading and studying, fully devoted, even for so-called "beauty" or "gold house," is not entirely wrong either; what's crucial is not using improper means to seek "beauty" or "gold house." The author believes that even if someone seeks reputation, if they truly help others and serve society, we should commend them and not criticize recklessly. If someone studies for personal development and gaining fame and fortune, as long as they contribute to society, it is also acceptable. Today's higher education institutions operate under three mechanisms: officialdom, academia, and commerce. This operational mechanism has many drawbacks. Academia should be prioritized; one shouldn't abandon academic pursuits for official positions, turning mentors into "contractors," placing transactions first and treating academia casually. This seriously violates the spirit of universities and is an issue that current higher education institutions must pay attention to.

39. Officials should love the people; establishing a career involves cultivating virtue. Reading and studying should grasp the philosophy left by ancient sages, not just observing form without observing essence, focusing on traces rather than spirit. Reading books rigidly without understanding the truths within makes those truths mere empty words, and no matter how much knowledge one learns, if it cannot be applied practically, one remains "knowledgeable but uncultured." Officials are supported by the people and should serve them; enjoying support without serving the people, or even engaging in corruption and oppressing them, makes such officials no better than beasts in human clothing. To succeed in a career, one must be honest and widely practice benevolence, not solely profit-driven, otherwise, even if successful temporarily, the business will soon decline. In reality, under the heavy pressure of college entrance exams, many people study only for exams and material gain, not deeply understanding knowledge content; there are too few conscientious officials who enjoy tax revenues from the people yet do not advocate for them, instead tyrannizing the countryside and exploiting the common folk, like Wen Qiang of Chongqing, whose lives are corrupt, dirty, and worse than animals; there are also many people who only focus on developing their careers, neglecting nurturing successors and utilizing excellent management talents, thus fulfilling the saying "riches do not last beyond three generations." Many students, after years of diligent study entering university, should have clear goals and lofty ideals. Living aimlessly and idly not only fails to live up to their parents' sacrifices but also to themselves and the hardships endured over twelve years of schooling. Nowadays, many students ignore politics, indulge in celebrity worship, and forget the responsibilities entrusted by their parents and society. Such "talents," if they become officials, how could they understand loving the people? If they establish a career, how could they develop and expand it?

40. A bright mind leads to perceptive insights. Writing and reading should not be done in isolation but should immerse oneself in nature and society. Learn to listen to "bird songs and insect sounds," which convey the voice of nature, and appreciate "flower blossoms and grass colors," which display the diversity and harmony of nature. All this requires being "clear in heavenly insight" and "bright-hearted" to truly comprehend and express articles that blend scenery and emotion, creating beautiful literature with superior artistic conception. Whether reading, studying, researching, or striving for ideals and achieving success, it's often similar. The principle is simple: doing things requires single-mindedness, full devotion, aiming to achieve "clear heavenly insight" and "a bright heart." This way, studying yields results, and pursuing a career brings prosperity. For example, studying, playing ball, or shopping, if while studying you think about playing ball, while playing ball you think about shopping, and while shopping you think about studying, the result will be that studying isn't solid, playing ball loses its standard, and shopping isn't enjoyable. Conversely, if you can study earnestly, play to your heart's content, and shop thoroughly, in the same amount of time, you not only acquire knowledge but also enjoy yourself and shop well, and that's all.
