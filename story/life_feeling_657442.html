Indifferent回顾, the years slip away

by lili1 on 2012-01-08 22:28:10

Indifferently looking back, the years gradually fade away.

Sing a song to commemorate my youth that will eventually pass away, love a person, naively thinking it would be a lifetime.

Sing a song to remember my love that will eventually fade, there is a person who will always accompany you and never leave again.

In the dimly lit place, time stretches on endlessly. You forgot me, time also forgot me.

When I was still a child, I once daydreamed of standing at the edge of time, grasping my own simple happiness. But time has already thrown me into the sea of people, losing myself in the bustling streets. Standing in the surging crowd, I realized how helpless I am. All the scenes that appeared in my imagination flickered before my eyes like shadows. Reality became increasingly clear.

I'm in my second year of high school now. This is a statement without any emotional color. But because of this sentence, my heart was filled with tumultuous waves, fluctuating endlessly.

I will be in my second year of high school. I wrote such an essay for my teacher, hoping someone could understand the turmoil in my heart. But the teacher only spent two days grading more than twenty essays. Facing my lament, she gave me a score as usual without even looking at it. Finally, I understood that entering the second year of high school is a very normal thing. The whole world is celebrating, but I am alone, silently grieving. It's like attending a party where everyone is drunk except for me who remains sober. This kind of loneliness, being abandoned by the whole world, is an indescribable sorrow.

After the final exam of the first year of high school, everyone rushed home like tired birds returning to their nests. On the first day of summer vacation, I went to the amusement park alone, bought a ticket to the haunted house, and entered that dark and thrilling place by myself. When I was scared out of my wits, I clearly felt my heartache, which indicated that I was still alive, so I was quite relieved. While seeking thrills, I also felt that I was still alive. I once thought that there could be someone accompanying me into that eerie and terrifying cave. After searching my mind for a long time, there was no one who could go with me. I realized that I was lonely. After a year of high school, I still walk alone. After coming out of the haunted house, I found a place without people and wiped my tears hard. I didn't want to be fragile. Even if no one saw. Their encouraging gazes, their playful laughter, all replayed in my mind, but none of these had anything to do with me. I think I have grown old, already old at eighteen, unable to join them. The young ones are still playing childish games, falling in love, smoking, fighting, full of lies, or grand ambitions, completely ignoring others' strange looks. Perhaps, after a period of ups and downs, they will realize how naive they were back then.

In the twilight of my years, my youth, my tears, my memories. June is like a grand fireworks show, first thunderous, then splendid, and finally fading away, all in one breath. It seems that this is inevitable. But I feel it more like a flood waiting to happen. Either it doesn't come, or when it does, it sweeps over the entire earth. In the last few days of June, barely clinging to life, I struggled to study hard, repeatedly telling myself: Look, look, the flood is about to burst, if you're not prepared, you will surely be drowned in this overwhelming and unavoidable disaster. But no matter how hard I tried, the days flowed like water in my heart, creating a hole in my chest, with the wind rushing through. I heard the triumphant sneer of the wind after its victory. Memories turned into a ray of sunlight, drifting alone in the sky above the city full of ruins after the storm.

My lonely youth. All along, I refused to let anyone enter my inner world. As a child, I always did my own things, spoke my own words, and walked my own path alone. Later, as I grew up, a friend was exceptionally kind to me. I told her my little secrets, and she promised not to tell anyone else. Ironically, the next day, everyone knew what I was thinking. This made me feel like I was naked in front of everyone, pointing at me and laughing mockingly, showing utter disdain. Since then, the door to my heart has been tightly closed. The world is beautiful. This is a comforting phrase. The real world is a muddy swamp, whoever steps in will get dirty. I became increasingly resistant and fearful of this world. Loneliness is a personal matter; loneliness among a group of people is not called loneliness. Many times I stood in front of a large window, watching the last glow of the sunset disappear, watching students hurry into the teaching building with books in hand, watching the bustling streets, watching the night quietly fall, watching the city lights decorate the city extraordinarily enchanting. If I belonged to this world, I wouldn't stand at the edge of the city so aimlessly, thinking I had wings to soar through the skies, far from the mundane world. My lonely youth grew up in the empty sky, boundless fields, and the turbidity of right and wrong.

Everyone writes "cherish," everyone says "cherish," but how many truly cherish? Time rolls on like this, can you see the wrinkles on your parents' foreheads? Can you understand their helplessness towards such a life? Can you hear their low sighs at night? Can you remember your elementary school deskmate? Can you remember that you were once a member of the Young Pioneers? Can you remember the headaches you had over exams? Can you remember that you once held a great and beautiful dream? When I open the box full of memories, the cycle of time won't stop for it, only I remember everything from the beginning. However, the music hasn't ended, but people have scattered. I danced passionately on stage, unaware that the audience seats below were already empty. Everyone is still immersed in so-called youthful years, while I am like a scavenger, bending down to pick up the loneliness they lost, not indulging with them in this rapidly played plot. It's like the banquet has ended, everyone is drunk, and when they wake up, they find themselves with gray hair. And I, have already turned white-haired.

Time carries its unique stubbornness, changing much. My parents stopped arguing. When I was very young, I had a happy family, my parents treated each other politely, very sweet both in public and private. Later, around three or four years old, they started arguing, and after arguments became common, they started fighting. Witnessing those bloody scenes, I was left with many psychological scars. But now, my parents, due to our growth, have stopped arguing, become harmonious. They once argued to the point of divorce, now the whole family lives peacefully. Why? They also have their moments of old age, do they really only realize the importance of cherishing each other when they grow old, and understand the bitterness of living alone? A friend once gave me such a topic to write about: If one day, I have nothing to rely on in old age. I never put pen to paper, but if I really wrote, I would say, if I really end up with nothing to rely on in old age, there are two reasons: one, I was wrong, two, this world was wrong. If my parents end up with nothing to rely on in old age, what went wrong? Time washes away our thoughts day by day. Unconsciously, we have been changed beyond recognition by it.

On many nights, I listen to the sound of the night wind hitting the windows, watching the bright moonlight slide from here to there, and then it becomes light, a new day begins. Or sometimes I hide in a dark corner, watching the weary birds fly across the sky, watching the years rush past with the rustling leaves in the wind. Life is a journey, and in this journey, we have to give up too much.

If you think I am still a child and shouldn't possess so much sadness. Then, are you willing to take some time out of your busy schedule, bend down, and talk to this child, telling her that this world is still beautiful? If you think I don't have the ability to bear these small sorrows, then, are you willing to face the world's rights and wrongs, grudges and hatreds every day with a smile?

Indifferently looking back, I didn't realize the years had faded away. Countless painful days have been buried, flowers wither, west wind decays, geese pass without leaving a trace. The future continues, dreams haven't begun, hearts are already missing, the setting sun flows through the years in confusion.
