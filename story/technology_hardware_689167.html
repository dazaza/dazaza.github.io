Dear Future President of the Movie Club,

Hello, first of all, congratulations on your successful election as the second president of the Movie Club of the International Teaching Institute at Shandong University of Finance and Economics. On this meaningful day of National Day, I write this letter to you, hoping that what I write here will give you more confidence and help you better complete your work in the future.

To be honest, when I founded this movie club, I didn't think too much. I just wanted to enrich my college life. Plus, there was a void in this area at the school, so I decided to do it. I still remember when Sister Qing (that is, your current head of the association department) asked me about my specific plans for this club, I talked nonsense without any real direction, making both of us feel quite unreliable at the time. But after careful consideration and decision-making by the Association Department, the Movie Club was still retained. When this club was officially established, a sense of responsibility and mission naturally arose on my shoulders because this wasn't just for fun; we truly had to do some things to make many students willing to participate and enjoy themselves. The Movie Club has weathered a year of storms and achieved some accomplishments, which are inseparable from the hard assistance of the board members, the free and relaxed management style of your senior sister Kang Jiao, and more importantly, the support of the wide student body for the Movie Club. And now, what you need to do is to continue developing and strengthening the Movie Club. Below, I will briefly describe several important matters regarding club activities, hoping these will help you carry out your work more smoothly in the future.

Firstly, the recruitment of new club members. On the 29th, you have successfully completed the task of recruiting new members. It's a pity that I couldn't arrive in time on that day, but I heard from your senior sister that you did an excellent job, even better than what we did during our freshman year. I hope this good start can herald a smooth year ahead. Secondly, the recruitment of new board members and allocation of club tasks. The board members of the Association Department will assist you in gradually carrying out this work. Next, applying for classrooms to host events. There is a relatively complex process involved in applying for classrooms and multimedia equipment, including filling out forms at the office in Building No. 2, applying for approval from the Teaching Department in Building No. 4, getting signatures from the Property Center in Building No. 3, and finally getting signatures from the Security Department in Building No. 1. The entire process takes about 15 minutes. I hope you can build good relationships with the various teachers, which will be more beneficial for classroom and multimedia applications. Of course, merely hosting viewing activities is not enough. The most crucial aspect of club activities is an idea. As long as your ideas are feasible, the Association Department will actively cooperate with you. Even if financial support is needed, as long as the planning and application are in place, this won't be a problem. In clubs, creativity and execution are both indispensable. Sometimes, inter-club exchanges are also necessary. Last year, representatives from the Movie Club participated in the "I Love Watching Movies" competition organized by the Financial and Economic Institute’s Student Union, ultimately achieving second place and bringing honor before the entire school. At the end of the year, the school will conduct a club evaluation activity. The Movie Club ranked highly last year, and I hope you can continue this good momentum and push this club to its peak.

Of course, your potential is limitless, and I believe that under your leadership, the Movie Club will be more outstanding than its first year. Duan Haoran, Xu Congge, although I cannot yet confirm who will be the president of the Movie Club, since you both intended to run for the position, you must have made full preparations. I wasn’t present during your campaign defense, but through my understanding from your seniors and private interactions with you, I find each of you very excellent. Regardless of who gets this baton, I hope you will put your heart into it, cooperate with the staff of the Association Department, and let the Movie Club shine infinitely under your leadership. Keep going!

Sincerely,

First President of the Movie Club

Shao Chunhui

October 1, 2011
