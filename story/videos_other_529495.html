Promising to spend a huge amount of money to sponsor poor students to go to school

by yy516221509y on 2011-11-01 11:24:16

On August 15, the Yaodian Police Station of the Weicheng Public Security Bureau received a report from Sun in Cangzhang Village. Sun claimed that a middle-aged man who called himself an "entrepreneur" had deceived food and drink at his home under the pretext of sponsoring impoverished students admitted to universities this year, and even demanded money. The police of the Yaodian station responded urgently and caught the suspect who was about to escape at Sun's home.

By the time of the incident, Bai had visited seven families in Xingping City, Qindu District, and Weicheng District. According to Bai's statement, no one had doubted his identity. However, according to the police handling the case, Bai was wearing shorts and slippers, taking the bus to students' homes. Despite claiming to be a big boss, he didn't even have a decent car. Once, he even asked one of the parents to take him on a motorcycle to a nearby station. In fact, with these clues, it would have been easy to expose his scam. Bai claimed to work on a construction site earning 100 yuan per day, but after half a year, he had only received a few hundred yuan for living expenses. Due to lack of money, he devised this fraud. Currently, the Weicheng Public Security Bureau has imposed an administrative detention of 10 days on him.

Upon investigation, the suspect Bai, male, 30 years old, lives in Xining, Qinghai Province, and has worked in Shaanxi for three years. Recently, he worked on a construction site in Xunyi County, Xianyang. In August, after reading news reports about sponsoring poor students and donation hotlines, Bai conceived the idea of fraud. On August 9, Bai took a bus to Xianyang and called the Youth League Committee, claiming he wanted to donate to impoverished college students and needed to conduct on-site investigations, thus obtaining the phone numbers of nearly ten students. From August 11 to 15, Bai continuously contacted these candidates, visiting their homes and lying that he was either the boss of a real estate company or was commissioned by the boss to conduct on-site investigations. He promised to sponsor each student annually between 2500 to 5000 yuan, plus 500 yuan monthly living allowance and a laptop for each. After receiving hospitality from these families, he further deceived them by saying his company would hold a press conference soon, requiring each family to contribute half of the venue fee, thereby defrauding 460 yuan in cash.

Our newspaper reported from Xianyang (reporter Yu Zhonghu): A man from another place pretended to be a big entrepreneur, promising huge donations to help poor students attend school, then proceeded to deceive food, lodging, and money. Recently, the man was arrested by the Yaodian Police Station of the Weicheng Branch of the Xianyang Public Security Bureau.
