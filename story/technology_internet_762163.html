Who says we're dull and lack sex appeal? Seven reasons for choosing big companies

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 14:06:15

　　English Title: 7 Reasons Why You Need To Work For A Big Company

　　This article is written by Mark Stephen, the founder and CEO of IDR Solutions.

　　Large companies are often perceived as sluggish and lacking in excitement, while startups are seen as agile, efficient, and fun. I currently run a startup, but to be honest, I am grateful for the memorable time I spent working at a large company. Below are the reasons why I believe working for a big company can be rewarding:

　　1. Exposure to Excellence and Absurdity

　　In a big company, you will encounter both brilliance and absurdity under one roof. You will witness groundbreaking ideas about source code control and also experience long-winded discussions that lead to disengagement. While large companies may not be the most dynamic, they have their own ways of growing and succeeding. Even the most ordinary institutions have hidden talents. Understanding the logic behind a large company and identifying employees who possess both technical skills and political acumen can be a great asset to your career.

　　2. Surrounded by Talented Individuals

　　In certain departments of a large company, you will find clusters of intelligent people. Working alongside these talented individuals is an incredible experience; it feels like wisdom is permeating the air around you. These smart people often choose large companies due to family and responsibility considerations, avoiding the risks associated with startups. Experienced core employees can teach you a lot, whereas colleagues in startups tend to be around your age and less experienced.

　　3. Networking Benefits

　　Twelve years ago, I worked for a large company. To this day, I still keep in touch with my former colleagues and frequently bump into them in various places. This connection is invaluable. In today's era of social media, it remains crucial for people to know who you are and what you can do. Your employment history at a large company is documented, and when you need to conduct business with such companies, this background can provide you with preferential treatment.

　　4. Enjoy Premium Benefits

　　I miss the cafeteria and gym at the large company where I used to work, as well as that summer trip to Amsterdam... Oracle training courses paid for by the company, fully reimbursed MBA programs, all sound amazing! In contrast, startups might only reimburse the cost of books. Would you rather spend someone else's money to gain knowledge at a large company or work 100 hours a week at a startup chasing a distant dream?

　　5. Mastering Political Skills

　　At a large company, you learn not only technical skills but also "diplomatic" and "political" skills. When you transition from being an employee to a partner of a large company, these skills will come in handy. Large companies like to buy things and spend generously. Understanding their operational rules helps you conduct business more effectively and tap into more potential clients through them.

　　6. Slower Pace, Better Learning

　　Startups operate at a fast pace like meteors racing across the sky, while large companies often move more slowly. Although this slower pace may lack the excitement and adrenaline rush, it provides more time for learning, self-reflection, and recharging. Sometimes, this "slower pace" can have unexpected benefits. For instance, I won't tell you that a major media company avoided spending millions on a brainless web project simply because their decision-making process was too slow, allowing the dot-com bubble to burst first...

　　7. Establishing Boundaries and Pursuing Dreams

　　When you first enter the workforce, you may be full of confidence and fearlessness. However, after experiencing the ups and downs of working at a large company, you will understand what is worth doing and what is not, realizing which issues are critical and which are trivial. You will also become clearer about your lifelong dreams and your unbreachable boundaries. Defining your professional principles can help you perform better in your work.

　　These are the reasons why I enjoy working for large companies. What are your reasons?
