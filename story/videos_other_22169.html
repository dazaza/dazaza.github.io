Common sense you need to know before moving!

by webban on 2007-06-10 16:36:07

1. How to prepare before movingRelocation is a big deal for a family, and no one wants to be in a rush. It's best to prepare in advance. First, arrange the time. A move requires a certain amount of time; if the distance is far, there's traffic, or there are many items to move, it might take even longer. Secondly, book the moving company's vehicles in advance so they arrive on time and start the move accordingly. If you have a lot of items and one vehicle isn't enough, it's best to book multiple vehicles simultaneously to move everything at once, saving time. Additionally, in case of rainy weather, choose a covered truck to avoid wetting your belongings. Thirdly, pack up your things in advance. Items inside large boxes or cabinets should be taken out beforehand to make them easier to carry. Shoes and other small items should be packed in cartons to prevent loss. Cash, jewelry, and other valuable small items should be carried with you. Dishes, tea sets, and other fragile items should be packaged separately and moved individually to avoid breakage. As long as you prepare sufficiently in advance, your move will go smoothly.

3. Principles for choosing a moving companyReputable companies will provide quotes. If a moving company refuses to quote and lists numerous reasons for not doing so, you need to be extra cautious. Without a clear quote, your move might cost more than expected. Several factors could increase moving costs, including: adverse weather, incomplete packing, lack of parking spaces at the origin or destination. Moving items exceeding the vehicle's capacity, moving too early or too late, additional fees for traffic delays, requiring moving companies to provide cartons or packing services, all these would increase expenses.
