Is it a shortage of workers or a panic among enterprises?

by mumu321 on 2012-02-27 16:01:28

Every year after the lunar new year, the media in the Pearl River Delta region will focus on the "labor shortage" issue, especially in traditional industries like rosewood furniture, which is a typical example.

Rosewood furniture workers looking for jobs should prioritize production bases. In theory, rosewood enterprises in these bases shouldn't have too much trouble recruiting workers. However, during investigations into several major production bases such as Zhongshan in Guangdong, Dongyang in Zhejiang, and Xianyou in Fujian, reporters found that many small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) were reluctant to discuss this issue in depth, either glossing over it or avoiding the question altogether. Meanwhile, interviews with larger enterprises revealed that most of them currently face certain labor issues. This has significantly hindered the smooth post-festival resumption of operations for many SME factories. Some orders were placed before the festival, but due to delays in resuming work after the holiday, some orders may not be delivered on time, forcing the enterprise to bear the cost of delays. Moreover, SMEs are generally inferior to larger enterprises in terms of benefits and treatment. A common practice is to attract talent by offering higher salaries, and over time, annual salary increases for workers in rosewood enterprises have become the norm. However, this "norm" is gradually becoming unbearable for some companies.

Many companies expressed that "workers who have been in the rosewood industry for a long time tend to be more savvy." As a result, this year's recruitment methods for rosewood enterprises have become more diversified. Besides the traditional methods of posting job advertisements at storefronts and factory gates and asking veteran workers to introduce acquaintances from their hometowns to work at the factory, some business owners personally traveled to major labor-sending provinces such as Zhejiang, Jiangxi, and Sichuan to recruit workers. Additionally, enterprises have started using the internet to post job advertisements, a method that greatly expands the recruitment scope and is more suitable for the younger generation of workers, namely those born in the 80s and 90s.

As companies are anxious about project schedules and urgently seeking workers through various means, there is a stark contrast with the calm demeanor of the rosewood workers. It's rare to see workers on the streets of the production bases under normal circumstances, but during the post-lunar new year "labor shortage" period, many workers can be seen on the streets. These workers either walk or ride electric scooters, wandering around the production bases. Some enter stores to ask a few questions, while others glance at job postings and leave without hesitation, appearing as relaxed as if they were browsing in a bookstore. According to reports, workers typically only inquire about wages, gaining a rough understanding before rarely making an immediate decision. Instead, they compare offers from multiple employers. Even if a company offers a wage that meets their expectations, they still won't immediately agree to start work but continue to look for enterprises offering higher wages.

This situation usually lasts for one or two months. In fact, during the first month after the lunar new year, it is the hardest time for factories to recruit workers. Many workers come to inquire every day, but very few agree to start work. Even if they do start, they continue to observe the situation and aren't fully committed to working for the enterprise, leading to frequent job-hopping. By around the second month of the lunar calendar, more than half of the factories manage to hire over 80% of the required workforce, meeting the basic labor needs of the factory, and at this point, the workers' states are relatively stable. Of course, some enterprises still experience labor shortages, primarily due to operational issues and inadequate treatment within the company.

From this perspective, although traditional industries are indeed facing a certain degree of "labor shortage," based on observations from several major production bases of rosewood enterprises, the severity of the "shortage" is not too serious, at least enough to maintain the normal operations of most enterprises. However, the观望 attitude of workers after the lunar new year creates a false impression of a severe labor shortage, causing panic among many enterprises. (Source: China Classical Furniture Network, reporter Xiao H)
