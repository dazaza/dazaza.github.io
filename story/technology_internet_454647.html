The choice of fabric in clothing directly determines the style and pattern in fashion design. With the development of textile technology, the rapid development of new combinations of modern fashion design fabrics brings infinite imagination space. However, the unique style of traditional fabrics remains irreplaceable charm. Silk, brocade, satin, hemp, and blue prints play an important role in modern fashion design with strong Chinese characteristics. Silk, with its softness, delicacy, elegance, and splendor, better embodies the grace and gentleness of women. In recent years, many international fashion designers have used silk fabrics in their designs, dominating the stage and showcasing the luster charm of silk. In addition, the use of brocade, satin with high design levels, strong colors, and distinct features creates modern clothing forms with strong decorative effects. Printing on linen and cotton fabrics is our old tradition, bringing traditional Chinese clothing design onto the international stage, evoking thoughts of Red China, especially in the use of red and green. The application of traditional Chinese clothing in modern fashion is the most widely used. As for gold-colored yellow, it was a color exclusively used by the royal family. In traditional Chinese clothing culture, it gives a sense of majesty, grandeur, and sanctity. It should be said that with its unique bright yellow, it is widely applied in various clothing designs, often giving off a luxurious and elegant aura and feeling.

The design pattern of traditional Chinese clothing is often the essence of modern Chinese representative floral clothing patterns, such as peonies, winding floral patterns, dragons, phoenixes, and Chinese characters. In Chinese feudal society, the dragon was a graphic pattern exclusively used by the royal family, with strong totemic traces, but in folk culture, it was cleverly used in clothing decoration. As for flowered fabrics in ancient clothing design, these traditional patterns have been widely used in Chinese-characteristic fashion to enhance performance. Designers apply various techniques such as embroidery, dyeing, hand-painting, appliqué, weaving, etc., to decorate clothing and accessories with these patterns. In recent years, with the resurgence of fashion trends, inlaying, embedding, rolling, plateau, and embroidery processes have been widely used in modern fashion design, becoming fashionable embellishments.
