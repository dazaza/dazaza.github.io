Living beings are the root.

by haiitaonlg on 2011-09-25 10:36:05

Without the sentient beings, there would be no Bodhi. Bodhi belongs to the sentient beings. It is the sentient beings who bury themselves in the mire, arduously absorb in the dark, and grow the flowers and fruits of Bodhi.

Because of the cycle of karma through the six realms, we are always unable to repay our karmic debts, whether willingly or unwillingly.

Homage to the Sixth Patriarch! Namo Avalokitesvara Bodhisattva! Namo Samantabhadra Bodhisattva! Namo Amitabha Buddha!

We are deceived because our greed is intense; we are harmed because we are clumsy and unwise. In the process of deceiving and being deceived, harming and being harmed, we have formed endless karmic connections with sentient beings, entangled and bound without end. Because we refuse to let go, because we still want to reclaim what we lost, we continue to play out one drama after another filled with sourness, sweetness, bitterness, and spiciness, getting stuck in one role after another without end.

Loyal use the Buddha's name to cleanse our hearts, seeking the blessings of the Bodhisattvas. May the filth peel off, may compassion be revealed, may we remain calm and clear in all conditions without anger or ignorance, without sadness or joy.

The Sixth Patriarch said: The mind is just a moment away. Confused, it is the sentient being; enlightened, it is the Bodhisattva, the Buddha.

When we see others committing evil deeds, when we see others suffering, do we feel the urge to save them? Perhaps we think that they will naturally receive their karmic retribution for their evil deeds, that their suffering has its own causes and conditions, and that it has nothing to do with us. We only need to do our own part.

Forgiving someone is not difficult, forgiving someone who has deeply hurt us is also not difficult; because we have also sought the understanding of others, because we have also deeply hurt sentient beings.

When sentient beings are deluded, I am also deluded. On the path of samsara, we are even more burdened by sin. In the entanglements between people and events, it is hard to say who is right or wrong, hard to distinguish who is at fault. Everything is the cycle of cause and effect, everything is dependent origination and emptiness.

Why do we only discover our own fragility in adverse situations? Why are we powerless when we see others on the brink of hell? Because our hearts are not broad enough, because our hearts are not compassionate enough. Because we cannot give without expecting anything in return, we always want to get something back, we always think about ourselves. When things don't concern us, we stay aloof, often finding countless excuses to escape, finding countless reasons to regress, not seeking progress, not keeping precepts, forgetting the teachings of the Buddhas and Bodhisattvas completely.

Until we create the karma leading to the evil realms, we wake up one day and regret it.

Only by listening to the teachings of the Bodhisattvas, having deep faith and vows, taking the Ten Great Vows of Samantabhadra as guidance, unafraid of difficulties, step by step, striving to cultivate and protect compassion, using compassion to continuously benefit sentient beings, can we reach the shore and break free from the cycle of rebirth.

A scripture like "The Practice and Vows of Samantabhadra", makes me realize that if we leave behind those we love, those we hate, those who teach us, those who entangle us, making us wander, be grateful, be moved, and feel lonely in this bustling world, then we would be like a tree without roots, a branch detached from the trunk, ultimately unable to bear the wisdom fruits of Bodhi.

But have we ever thought that we are also those who keep committing evil and suffering endlessly? If it weren't for the great compassion of the Bodhisattvas pulling us out of the sea of suffering, our epitaphs would already be written in hell.

Sentient beings are the root, and Bodhi is the flower and fruit.
