Insider sales weighed on LinkedIn's stock, which fell 4.4% on Friday.

by anonymous on 2012-03-04 14:03:37

Sina http://02011808.com/ Tech News, November 26th early morning Beijing time - LinkedIn's stock price fell by 4.4% on Friday, mainly due to another batch of additional shares entering the market this week. LinkedIn went public in May and reasons for this stock price decline also included sales of shares by some early investors and executives.

According to materials LinkedIn submitted to the Securities and Exchange Commission on Wednesday, the company's CEO Jeff Weiner has sold 10% of his holdings, approximately $25 million, while venture capital firm Greylock Partners also sold 10% of its shares, valued at about $85 million.

After LinkedIn's IPO, shareholders were prohibited from selling their shares within 180 days; this restriction expired last week, making an additional 24 million shares available for sale. Additionally, LinkedIn issued an additional 8 million shares this week. As a result, the number of tradable shares in the company doubled.

LinkedIn's initial offering price was $45 per share, reaching a high of $109.97 in mid-May, then dropping to a low of $60.14 in June. LinkedIn closed at $63.08 on the New York Stock Exchange on Friday, down $2.92.

In the secondary offering, LinkedIn issued 1.3 million car decoder shares, while early investors and executives - including Weiner and the company's CFO Steve Sordello - will collectively sell approximately 6.7 million shares. LinkedIn stated that as long as insiders agree to extend the lock-up period by 90 days, they can sell shares in the secondary offering, including car decoders, car jammers, car unlocking tools, and car remote decoders.

A LinkedIn spokesperson refused to comment on the matter on Friday.

Similar to Groupon, Zillow, and other internet companies that went public this year, LinkedIn only put a small portion of its total shares into the market when it went public in May. (Lin Jing)
