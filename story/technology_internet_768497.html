The Internet's first academician expert workstation lands at Baidu: Ni Guangnan, Huai Jinpeng and others join

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 18:56:20

China's Internet industry's first academician expert workstation was unveiled at Baidu. Attending the unveiling ceremony were Academician of the Chinese Academy of Engineering and computer structure and algorithm expert, Dr. Guangnan Ni, Academician of the Chinese Academy of Sciences and President of Beihang University, Dr. Jinpeng Hua, Academician of the Chinese Academy of Engineering, Professor at Peking University and Vice Chairman of the National Natural Science Foundation Committee, Dr. Wen Gao, Secretary General of Beijing Association for Science and Technology, Mr. Qiang Xia, Founder, Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of Baidu, Mr. Robin Li. Mr. Robin Li personally presented appointment letters to Academicians Guangnan Ni, Jinpeng Hua, and Wen Gao.

According to reports, the Baidu Academician Expert Workstation will focus on key areas in the mobile Internet era such as big data, cloud computing, multimedia, etc. Based on Baidu's technology and the research results of academicians, the workstation will jointly promote industry progress through industry-university-research cooperation. The first batch of academicians stationed at the Baidu Academician Expert Workstation includes: Chinese Academy of Engineering members Guangnan Ni, Wen Gao, Deyi Li, Changxiang Shen, and Chinese Academy of Sciences member Jinpeng Hua. It is reported that the "Baidu Academician Expert Workstation" will continue to invite well-known domestic academicians and experts in the future.

Vice Chairman of the Beijing Association for Science and Technology, Ms. Lijun Zhou, said in her speech at the unveiling ceremony: "The collaboration between Baidu and the team of academicians and experts is a strong alliance in the field of scientific and technological innovation, a milestone for the Internet industry to carry out industry-university-research cooperation innovation. We hope that the Baidu Academician Expert Workstation seizes the strategic opportunity of global Internet industry development, continuously explores and improves the station construction model with an international perspective, and plays a leading role in the construction and development of Beijing's Academician Expert Workstations."

Wen Wang, Vice President of Baidu Technology, said in his speech that we are currently in a new historical period transitioning from traditional Internet to mobile Internet. The era of reading images has arrived, big data has become the hottest topic of discussion, new application models brought by cloud computing continue to emerge, and due to the large user base and China-specific application models, China's Internet companies are the first to encounter new technical challenges.

Hai Feng Wang, Vice President of Baidu, also shared Baidu's technical innovation report at the scene, introducing the guests present to Baidu's innovations in basic technology, especially in deep learning.

Under the leadership of its founder, Mr. Robin Li, Baidu has made great strides in frontier technologies such as NLP (natural language processing), Deep Learning (deep learning), OCR (optical character recognition), Speech (speech), Image Recognition (image recognition), Big Data (big data), Computing Power (computing power) since pioneering modern search technology with "Hyperlink Analysis Technology" and creating the unique concept of "Box Computing". Previously, in 2006, Baidu was approved to establish the first post-doctoral scientific research workstation in China's Internet industry, and began officially recruiting young technology newcomers in 2009.

In the past few years, Baidu has successively established joint laboratories with famous domestic universities and research institutes such as Peking University and Beihang University. Baidu has accumulated deeply in many technical fields. Relying on Baidu's platform advantages, it provides platforms and resources for innovation in technologies such as big data, cloud computing, multimedia, etc. Aggregating forces from academia and industry can also create greater value for China's Internet industry.
