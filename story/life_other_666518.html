A Eulogy to Youth

It's been a long time since I've come here to write something. At the end of the semester, there are always a lot of small papers to write, and I always have to memorize large chunks of literary and historical theories for those mechanical exams.

Today's Tai Chi class is also over. The teacher generously gave us a fairly good grade with a stroke of his pen. Although it was slightly lower than expected, considering that I didn't practice much after class, we were all quite happy.

Then, assuming I won't pursue graduate studies anymore, and in fact, I've already earned enough credits for my physical education courses, this might very well be my last PE class in my student life, marking its conclusion.

As the winter sun has become incredibly gentle and the trees on the campus paths remain lush and green, I can't help but feel an overwhelming sense of sadness about this last PE class. As Yi Shan said: will there ever be someone who patiently teaches you Tai Chi again? Will there be someone who takes attendance so responsibly, just to make sure we learn? Will there be someone who incessantly tells you to correct this or that mistake? Will there be someone who strictly requires you to perform during exams but then gives lenient scores? No, there won't be. Even if you go to the gym in the future, it will be a different feeling.

So I think, being called out by name is also a kind of happiness, after all, it's a very strong proof of your existence. At least, you will feel that there are still people who care whether you're present or not, whether you pass the exam or not. In a more poetic sense, at a certain place and time, your presence or absence is cared about by someone, what a blissful thing that is!

We always live in great insecurity. In a place where there are only too many people and nothing else, it's easy to be overwhelmed by many things. Therefore, we desperately try to prove our existence in various ways, proving that we still occupy even a tiny bit of space in this world. However, the powerlessness of reality makes life pale, so we can only constantly vent and seek proof of our own existence in the virtual world, and microblogs and forums play such a role: we are all lonely people, we all need to prove our existence here.

In fact, a year ago, how could I have thought that in a very long time in the future, taking PE classes would also be a luxury wish? Being randomly called out by name would also be a kind of happiness that proves one's existence?

Time passes like a knife, making people age with every cut.

Suddenly, I am overwhelmed with nostalgia for our youth, while she hasn't even ended yet.
