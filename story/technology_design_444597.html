A Case Study of Parents Successfully Educating Their Children

by fkflr067 on 2011-06-11 18:04:24

"Encouragement is more effective than criticism in making children readily accept - Written by Liu Feilu, November 18, 2008.

My daughter has already started the second grade this year. Because almost every family in this generation only has one child, they tend to be somewhat dominant and lonely in their temperaments or personalities. However, I have unintentionally become her confidant because she loves hearing the encouragement I give her.

Since my daughter started kindergarten, I realized how crucial it is for a child to have an optimistic attitude and healthy psychology when interacting with the outside world. Because only a child who is psychologically healthy and happy can develop good habits and understand the changes and developments of things. So, I am accustomed to discussing with my daughter the happiest and least happy events. When she's happy, I laugh and share her joy; when she's unhappy, I strive to help her eliminate sorrow, correctly recognize right from wrong, and give her more encouragement so that she can optimistically face all things, whether she thinks they should or shouldn't happen.

When she was three years old, from the day I sent her to kindergarten, I tried every method possible to make her happily spend each day there, confidently face peers her age, accept the care and help from teachers, including the criticism when she made mistakes. When she cried and didn't want to go to kindergarten, I would do crafts with her, encourage her, tell her to let the teacher and classmates admire the toys made by her little hands, and gain praise from the teacher. This would give her confidence to willingly interact with classmates and willingly share her toys and happiness. Every time I picked her up from kindergarten and heard her excitedly calling me: "Mommy, Mommy... today the teacher praised me again, the classmates all like to play with me, I'm very happy, Mommy, I will still go to kindergarten tomorrow." At that moment, I felt relieved because as long as the child is happy, it shows that her growth is healthy. I clearly understood that giving children more encouragement is more effective than too much criticism in making them easily accept and understand how to face situations because encouragement can build up the child's self-confidence. When I see some parents forcibly sending their unwilling children to kindergarten, the helplessness and tears in the child's eyes make me feel worried.

Unconsciously, my daughter grew up. In her three years and six months of kindergarten life, she gained the happiness and sense of achievement that childhood should have, learned that caring for and helping others is a joy, and developed a competitive character and self-esteem.

When some parents complain that their children didn't score double hundreds on exams, I am satisfied with my daughter scoring ninety-eight points; when my daughter sadly tells me that she didn't perform well on the exam, I tell her it's fine, just a mistake, as long as she recognizes her shortcomings, strives to learn, and strives to do better next time, she is still a good child because scores tell us the key issues. Only by discovering problems can we solve them, improve, and let her understand reflection and recognizing mistakes; when she is criticized by the teacher and pouts unhappily, I help her analyze the teacher's mood, look at her own mistakes from the teacher's perspective, and encourage her to have the courage to admit mistakes after doing something wrong; when she quarrels with classmates and refuses to play with them, I tell her to learn tolerance and understanding because only by giving others more tolerance can she have more friends; when she doesn't know how to save and always wants to buy things, I often take her to the Sun Village to understand the living conditions of the children there, cultivating her love. After returning home, she tells me, "Mommy, I won't waste anymore. I will keep my books and notebooks, and when I don't need them, I will donate them to the children in the Children's Village. They are too pitiful!"

In terms of learning, although my daughter isn't a top student, considering her overall qualities, I feel satisfied and proud of her. Every day, I tell her one sentence: "Sweetheart, you must be happy today!" After returning home in the evening, I always ask her one question: "Sweetheart, were you happy today?" Then, my daughter always affectionately clings to me and tells me the stories that happened to her during the day.

In my daughter's growth record, there is such a diary entry titled "Today I Was Criticized by the Teacher":

After lunch, Yan Rui took out her Chinese textbook and workbook. I was surprised because there wasn't usually such homework at noon. My daughter didn't wait for me to ask her why but voluntarily told me what happened at school today.

There are four grades in the teaching building. The first grade is on the first floor for convenience during breaks, while the second grade is on the second floor. During breaks, they have to walk down the stairs to the playground. To ensure students' safety, the school rules state that going up and down the stairs must be done on the right side, without crowding, playing around, or running. Today during the morning break, Yan Rui was pushed to the left side of the stairs by classmates, which was recorded by the duty student and reported to the homeroom teacher. The homeroom teacher was angry and criticized the children, not allowing them to go outside during breaks and punishing them by making them copy the words from the ninth Chinese lesson five times. My daughter was upset, with tears in her eyes, seeking some comfort and solutions from me.

To let my daughter know that she was punished because she made a mistake, when I heard her still defending herself and refusing to admit defeat, I told her a story about how I was punished by copying something a hundred times when I was young. I told her that everyone makes mistakes, whether children or adults, because we grow by making and correcting mistakes. Making mistakes isn't a big deal; what matters is whether you realize the mistake and see the consequences of the mistake.

My daughter said, "It was my mistake, but the teacher shouldn't not let me go out to play either, right? It doesn't affect studying."

This sentence let me see the worry in my daughter's heart. Playing is the nature of children, especially during breaks. Ten minutes is the time when children should relax and experience happiness. I told my daughter, "You can talk to the teacher and tell her your real thoughts. The teacher will understand you."

My daughter said, "I'm afraid to because the teacher hasn't talked to us. I'm afraid the teacher will punish me even more and still not let me play."

For my daughter's mental health, I decided to communicate with the teacher. The teacher should realize that a child's mind is actually very simple and shouldn't make the child feel anxious and negative after one mistake.

My daughter said, "If the teacher still doesn't agree to let me go out to play, what should I do? Mommy, I want to transfer schools. I don't like being scolded by the teacher. I really miss Ms. Zhang from first grade because she always praised me."

I told her, "Actually, teachers and mothers are the same. We all love you and hope you study well and live well, which is why we are strict with you. You need to learn to communicate with the teacher, let the teacher understand your thoughts, and also understand the teacher's thoughts. That way, you will better understand the teacher, and the teacher will understand you. Each teacher's teaching method is different, so don't expect the teacher to act a certain way. You need to learn to adapt to the teacher's teaching methods."

After my daughter copied the words five times, she started to go to bed. I stayed by her side, encouraging and comforting her, telling her to actively admit her mistake to the teacher in the afternoon and get the teacher's permission before going out to play during breaks. My daughter fell asleep, but I couldn't sleep. I picked up my phone and sent two text messages to her homeroom teacher.

"Teacher Li, hello, sorry to bother you. Today, Yan Rui told me she made a mistake at school and got punished. She caused you trouble. Punishment is necessary, and persuasive education is also important. I had a short conversation with her, and she understands her mistake. I hope you can find a bit of time amidst your busy schedule to communicate with the child. She is very worried about losing the opportunity to play during breaks. A child's psychology cannot be ignored. We are the most trusted friends in a child's heart, and children grow by making and continuously correcting mistakes. Thank you for your protection and education. I believe she will remember the teacher's kindness and painstaking efforts. Sorry to bother you. The whole family sincerely thanks you and wishes you all the best. Yan Rui's mom."

The teacher replied, "Thank you for timely feedback. I will find time to communicate with the child."

After my daughter woke up, she hurried to go to school. I felt like she was much better. I reminded her to definitely admit her mistake to the teacher in the afternoon and have a good talk with the teacher, telling the teacher her real thoughts. I told my daughter that she must be happy every day because happiness is irreplaceable. My daughter happily said, "I know! Today went by so fast! Mommy, goodbye!"

Suddenly, the worries on my face disappeared. I still had half an hour before work, so I relaxed and rested for a while. I believe my daughter will handle her affairs well. Sweetheart, cheer up!

After school, I was still concerned about my daughter's situation, so I asked if she had talked to the teacher. She said the teacher found her and talked to her, asking her what she thought.

Rui Rui: "Teacher Li, I'm sorry. My mommy asked me to apologize to you."

Teacher: "Why should you apologize?"

Rui Rui: "Because I made a mistake."

Teacher: "Do you know where you went wrong?"

Rui Rui: "I shouldn't have intentionally walked on the left side of the stairs."

Teacher: "Knowing you're wrong makes you a good child. Be more careful next time."

Hearing my daughter's recounting, I felt her happiness. She said, "Great! Admitting mistakes makes people feel comfortable. I start to like Teacher Li. Teacher Li doesn't blame me anymore and agrees to let me go outside during breaks."

I said, "Isn't talking to the teacher great? Next time you have any thoughts, you can talk to the teacher. Actually, the teacher really likes you. If you don't say anything, how can the teacher possibly know what you're thinking?"

My daughter nodded in agreement and responded to me, then happily went to do her homework.

October 14, 2008, Tuesday, Sunny

Mom's notes: Liu Feilu

I vividly remember a sentence said by the head of the national touring lecture group 'Sister Xin Xin' during a parent report meeting: Affirmation, freedom, tolerance, emotion, and idealism are the five most indispensable nutrients for the mental growth of children. Giving children the greatest affirmation can make them more confident in facing everything unfamiliar to them.

In 2010, I was awarded as a "Good Parent" by the district women's federation."
