Neck pain is very common.

Many office workers have such experiences: after sitting in front of the desk for a long time to handle affairs, they suddenly realize that their neck muscles are tense and stiff, with indescribable discomfort.

A doctor from a local Traditional Chinese Medicine treatment center pointed out that the most common cause of neck pain is poor posture.

The human head is very heavy, weighing at least 7-8 kilograms or even 10 kilograms. Modern people often maintain the same posture for a long time, which over time causes wear and tear on the neck tissues. Therefore, desk-bound workers, those who use computers for long periods, and drivers are all high-risk groups for cervical spine pain.

In response to this cause, some measures can be taken to adjust and improve working postures. When necessary, the height and position of workstations and chairs can be adjusted. If conditions permit, regular breaks and exercises during work hours can be maintained, and timely neck exercises can be performed.

The doctor mentioned that there was once a patient who worked in the cafeteria. While serving dishes, her head constantly turned from left to right repeatedly, causing severe cervical degeneration, leading to dizziness and nausea. After analyzing the cause and adjusting her work, the condition was quickly controlled and never recurred.

Some patients feel discomfort upon waking up in the morning, with stiff necks. In such cases, one should consider whether the pillow used while sleeping is appropriate. The doctor explained that people are accustomed to placing pillows under the back of their heads to elevate it. Since the normal cervical spine has a physiological curvature that is convex anteriorly and concave posteriorly, if a pillow elevates the head, it forces the cervical spine into a forward-bent, reverse physiological curvature posture. Over time, this leads to spasms in the neck muscles and the disappearance of the cervical physiological curve. Therefore, placing the pillow behind the neck not only maintains the physiological curve but also allows the neck muscles to rest well. As for the requirements for pillows, the doctor believes there is no fixed standard because individual differences are significant. As long as it's suitable, it's fine.

The doctor pointed out that prolonged neck muscle fatigue keeps the muscles in a state of tension without rest, leading to stiffness due to lack of oxygen and blood supply.

If adjustments and improvements are not made, the muscle length will gradually shorten, making it harder to stretch, and structural changes may occur, such as degenerative arthritis and intervertebral disc protrusion. A vicious cycle may ensue, compressing nerves and causing weakness and numbness in the upper limbs.

The doctor said that actively treating cervical spondylosis not only alleviates pain but also prevents and delays the progression of the disease, preventing secondary lesions.

The symptoms of cervical spondylosis are easy to grasp, but sometimes the onset is subtle, which can lead to misdiagnosis. Therefore, diagnosing cervical spondylosis is very important. Only with an accurate diagnosis can the disease be better controlled and treated to prevent recurrence.
