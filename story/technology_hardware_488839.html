August 24, 1998

by sqrfeifeic on 2011-09-13 19:29:54

On August 24, 1998, a special memorial service was held in Houzhongzhuang Village, Jiaxiang County, Shandong Province.

The deceased, Shen Chunling, was a young girl of only 16 years old, yet she received the village's highest funeral honors. Her three brothers wore mourning clothes typically reserved for the passing of parents. They knelt for long periods before her coffin, unable to rise. The entire village, young and old, spontaneously wore black armbands and wept as they paid their respects — but who would have known that this young girl had no blood relation to this family at all? She was a stepdaughter without even a household registration. After her stepfather became paralyzed and her birth mother left home, she bravely stayed behind, using her fragile shoulders to support her four university-student brothers!

In June 1994, after losing her husband, Chunling's mother brought her and her siblings from Fanzelongzhouji in Shandong to Houshengzhuang Village in Jiaxiang County. Chunling’s stepfather, Shen Shuping, was a carpenter known for his loyalty and honesty. He had elderly parents in their seventies above him and four sons below him who were all studying. The eldest son, Shen Jianguo, attended Xi'an Jiaotong University, while the other three sons were in high school in the county. Despite the heavy financial burden on the family, the stepfather possessed excellent carpentry skills, and with the family's frugality, life was manageable if not entirely comfortable. The arrival of Chunling and her mother was met with great enthusiasm by the stepfather’s family. Perhaps because there were no girls in the house, the grandparents and stepfather showed immense affection toward little Chunling, and her brothers affectionately called her "Little Lingdang." When Little Chunling came to live with her stepfather, she had already passed the age for schooling due to her father's death, which forced her to drop out. However, without hesitation, the stepfather paid for her education. With five children now attending school, the stepfather's burdens increased significantly. Fortunately, he was diligent, working part-time with the town construction team during the agricultural off-season to earn extra income, just enough to cover the family's expenses.

Little Chunling cherished this hard-won opportunity to study and ranked third in her grade in her first semester. Besides studying, she also took on part of the household chores, washing her brothers' dirty clothes whenever she had free time, helping her stepfather carry wood and saw logs. Her stepfather would proudly tell anyone: "I'm so lucky; it's like the heavens sent me a wonderful daughter!"

However, happiness was fleeting, and disaster struck suddenly.

In the early summer of 1995, the stepfather fell from the third floor during a construction job and became paralyzed. A major pillar of the family's economy collapsed, and the family was left with heavy debts from medical treatment. Watching their father lying helplessly in bed, the second brother, Shen Jianjun, proposed dropping out of school first, but their father firmly refused, as he and the third son were about to take the college entrance exam, and his grades were top-notch in the school. The third and fourth sons also volunteered to drop out to shoulder the family's burdens.

Amidst the brothers' mutual yielding and the stepfather's dilemma, Little Chunling stepped forward and proposed dropping out herself to help her mother support the family. Tears streamed down the stepfather's face, and the grandparents wiped their eyes repeatedly. The stepfather painfully said, "Ling'er, I've wronged you. Your brothers have studied for so many years; it's a pity to give up now. It can only be you who sacrifices..." The three brothers tightly held their younger sister's hand and solemnly promised in front of their father's bed: "Whoever gets into university in the future will repay your kindness doubly."

But the mother of Chunling, who had just emerged from hardship, could not withstand another blow. Learning from the doctor that her husband might be permanently paralyzed, she lost all hope in the family and feared taking on such a heavy responsibility. She decided to leave with her youngest child. No matter how much Chunling begged or tried to dissuade her, her mother left the troubled home three months after the stepfather's injury. With her departure, another pillar of the family was gone. The grandparents cried every day, the stepfather spoke bravely, but the brothers' hearts were filled with fear and anxiety. Once again, tears filled the house. The villagers consoled Chunling kindly:

"There are no relatives left here for you. Why don't you go back to Fanze to your grandmother's place? Otherwise, you'll suffer for the rest of your life!" Little Chunling firmly shook her head: "No, I can't leave. If my mom leaves, I can't abandon this family." She gathered her brothers around the stepfather's bedside and solemnly promised, "Dad, mom leaving shows her lack of conscience. I won't leave. I'll stay and help you through this difficulty. From today onward, I am your real daughter." That year, Shen Chunling was only 12 years old.

[Only when the brothers make progress does the little sister succeed.]

Little Chunling kept her word. She took on all the farming and household chores, living like a true housewife, rising with the sun and resting after sunset, meticulously budgeting for the entire family. Little Chunling knew that for the family to improve, her stepfather must get better first. So, amidst her busy farming duties, she never stopped seeking treatment for her stepfather.

In the sweltering summer of 1996, due to the heat, the stepfather's condition worsened. Little Chunling decided to take him to the hospital in Jining City. After arranging everything at home, she set off with a cart. The 80-kilometer journey took her two days and one night. Upon arrival, her feet were blistered, and her shoulders were swollen. To save on accommodation fees at the hospital, Chunling slept in the bicycle shed. The elderly security guard thought she was a beggar and tried to drive her away several times.

Little Chunling had to tell the truth, deeply moving the old man. Not only did he place her cart in the safest spot inside, but he also specially found a mosquito net for her.

Under Chunling's meticulous care, the stepfather's condition stabilized, and she then pulled him back home. As soon as they arrived, it was time for wheat harvesting. The brothers were all in school, and the grandparents could only help with cooking or bundling wheat. The seven mu of wheat fields relied solely on Chunling. To ensure a timely harvest, she slept in the field for several days, collapsing on the wheat stack whenever she couldn’t hold on any longer, waking up to continue cutting.

Due to urgency and overwork, blisters formed on her lips, and her limbs bled. She was almost unable to bear it, but what about the remaining two mu of wheat? This was the family's food supply! She couldn't help but burst into tears in the wheat field. Her cries drew the attention of the villagers, who sympathized deeply and helped her finish the harvest. This difficult wheat harvest provided food for the whole family, and her second brother achieved a great success in the college entrance exam, being admitted to Tongji University in Shanghai with excellent results. Holding her second brother's admission notice, Little Chunling seemed to forget her own fatigue, jumping and shouting happily. Looking at her darkened and emaciated younger sister, the third brother, Shen Jianwen, who failed the exam, couldn't help but shed bitter tears, blaming himself: "I’m sorry to Little Sister. She has suffered so much for us, but I—"

Hearing this, Little Chunling panicked, holding onto her third brother's hand and consoling him: "Brother, if you don't pass this year, try again next year. Don't lose heart!" Her words made Shen Jianwen feel even more guilty, and he expressed that he wouldn't retake the exam but would stay home to help his sister. Little Chunling firmly refused, crying and asking her third brother: "Why do you not understand? My suffering is for you all to study well. If all of you succeed, that means I succeed too. How can you not understand?" Finally, the third brother followed his sister's advice and decided to retake the exam. The second brother's departure date for Shanghai was approaching. The tuition fee of 3000 yuan weighed heavily on the entire family.

At a loss, Little Chunling thought of selling blood. The first time she went to the blood bank, the doctor refused to draw blood because she was too young. The second time, she falsely reported her age and was allowed to donate 200CC of blood. When she received 400 yuan as "nutrition fees," her frown didn't disappear. She knew that this amount was insignificant compared to the 3000 yuan tuition fee.

Thus, she returned to the blood bank for the third time. This time, the doctor refused to draw any more blood.

Desperate, Little Chunling knelt before the doctor and explained why she needed to sell blood. The doctor remained silent for a long time, then sighed and said, "Alright, just this once. Don't come back anymore; you're still young and need to grow strong." This kind-hearted doctor symbolically drew a small amount of blood and took money from his own pocket, giving Little Chunling a total of 700 yuan. Little Chunling was moved to tears.
