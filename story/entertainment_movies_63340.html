Synopsis of "The Forbidden Kingdom"

by diglog2008 on 2008-04-16 15:54:22

The film "The Kung Fu Master" starring Jackie Chan, Jet Li, Bingbing Li, and Yifei Liu was recently launched in Dunhuang with a low-key ceremony. However, director Rob Minkoff did not give the actors much time to "warm up," as the opening scene filmed in the desert involved a chase and confrontation. According to insiders, both Bingbing Li and Yifei Liu have broken away from their previous on-screen personas in terms of appearance.

Jackie Chan and Jet Li fight all the way

The first collaboration between Jackie Chan and Jet Li in "The Kung Fu Master" naturally has fans most excited about their combat scenes. However, it was revealed that the two only engage in some martial arts sparring when they initially meet. The main fighting scenes throughout the movie revolve around accompanying an American boy on his mission, battling various demonic forces. Among these, Bingbing Li's portrayal of the "White-Haired Witch" and the "Jade Emperor" are the toughest adversaries.

After the filming commenced, one of the opening scenes shot was where Jackie Chan's character "Lu Yan," Jet Li's "Momo," Yifei Liu's "Golden Swallow," and the American boy were chased into the desert by the "White-Haired Witch."

It is reported that in this scene, Bingbing Li led nearly a hundred assassins, staging a massacre in the desert. She also had extensive fight scenes with both Jackie Chan and Jet Li.

Bingbing Li debuts with two contrasting hairstyles: black and white

Since the group of four is always on the run, their costumes and appearances are mostly tattered and disheveled. The opening scene shot in Dunhuang featured them dressed in beggar attire. In contrast, the most stunning and elaborate costume in the entire film belongs to Bingbing Li's "White-Haired Witch."

According to insiders, besides using vibrant clothing colors and striking eye makeup to highlight her allure, the "White-Haired Witch's" signature white hair emphasizes this characteristic even more. In the film, Bingbing Li's long hair extends past her knees, and the classic scene where the "White-Haired Witch" turns her black hair white overnight due to love will also be depicted in the movie. Additionally, over 95% of Bingbing Li's scenes involve action, and among the seven weapons of the "White-Haired Witch," her long hair proves to be a highly effective tool.

Yifei Liu no longer plays the innocent role

In the film, Yifei Liu plays the girlfriend of the American boy, delivering a significant amount of English dialogue. Her biggest breakthrough in the movie is through her transformation in appearance. It is reported that she completely overturns the pure and ethereal image of "Little Dragon Maiden" in the audience's mind, instead donning beggar attire with a face smeared with dirt. However, her martial arts moves are very "cool," differing from others who use fists and feet. Instead, she uses a pipa (Chinese lute) to release hidden weapons to subdue enemies, for which Yifei Liu spent considerable time learning how to play the pipa. Furthermore, the film features numerous scenes of her riding a horse while wielding twin swords.
