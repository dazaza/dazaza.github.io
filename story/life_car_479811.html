Small wiper, big car maintenance secrets: regular inspection and cleaning required

"Why can't my wiper clean properly?" "I've only been using it for six months, but when the wiper moves across the glass, it makes a screeching sound, which is so annoying." During the transition from summer to autumn in Changsha, we often encounter sudden showers, making the wiper indispensable. So, how can we keep the wipers in good working condition?

Master Wang from Jiutong Chevrolet said that regular inspections are essential. During the inspection, set the wiper switch to various speed positions to check if the wipers maintain a constant speed at different speeds and observe whether there is any uneven movement or missed wiping of the wiper blades. Either of these issues indicates damage to the wiper blade. Then, activate the wiper and pay attention to its motion smoothness and whether there is a loud noise. If the noise is significant, it means the wiper is pressing too hard on the glass, requiring calibration. If during high-speed driving or heavy rain, you find that the wiper cannot make close contact with the glass to remove rainwater, it suggests that the spring tension of the wiper arm is insufficient, necessitating the replacement of the entire wiper arm. If there are no foreign objects on the windshield or the wiper blade, but the wiper becomes less effective or leaves water spots after wiping, it indicates that the rubber of the wiper blade has aged. In this case, simply replace the wiper blade.

Some drivers try to clean dust and debris off the glass before driving by using the wipers without spraying water, leading to dry scraping. Many owners of two-box cars with rear wipers don’t pay attention and often turn them on when it's not raining, causing dry scraping, which damages the car’s glass significantly. If you want to clean the dust on the glass, it's better to spray some cleaning solution first. If you notice oil stains on the glass, use a glass cleaner to wipe both the wiper blade and the glass. Additionally, be mindful of avoiding high temperatures. When a car is exposed to intense sunlight, the wipers tightly adhere to the scorching front windshield, easily "burning" the rubber of the wipers. Therefore, it's best to park the car in the shade under the sun. If there is no shade, lift the wipers away from the windshield.
