How Small and Medium Sellers Can Deal with the Off-Season of Online Store Marketing

The biggest fear for online store operations is encountering an off-season. For many online store operators, the period from August to November is a sales off-season. Adding to this, the three reforms in Taobao's platform system in July this year have affected the sales of many online stores to varying degrees! According to information obtained from interviews with online store owners in Beijing by the author, most online stores saw a decline of about 50% in profits throughout July, with even steeper drops in page views! Many small and medium-sized online stores openly admitted that there were hardly any customers during this time, feeling like they were just passing the days. For some Golden Crown stores that did not opt for paid promotions, returning customers became the only lifeline during this marketing off-season. In such circumstances, how should small and medium sellers deal with the off-season of online store marketing?

Think Outside the Box: Utilize Other Media Resources for Store Promotion

Many online stores are complaining that Taobao’s new policies have made it feel like throwing money down the drain, even with the Direct Express service enabled. They don’t know what good methods can be used for product marketing! In fact, whether it's forum marketing or using other media resources for promotion, there are many third-party online store media platforms available now. For example, Yidababa Online Store Circle has been dedicated to helping promote small and medium-sized online stores! Additionally, these third-party online store media platforms have a high chance of being indexed by search engines like Baidu, which can yield good promotional results for online stores!

Do not lose confidence due to poor sales; learn to survive in adversity; increase your promotional efforts, post more quality threads, and reply to posts actively. As long as you put in the effort and work hard, success will come knocking at your door. Think of every possible way to increase the exposure of your store and let your products catch the customer's eye.

Master the Right Sales Techniques and Quickly Clear Outdated Products

The less business you have, the more you need to promote sales. You can bundle popular items with less popular ones for sale. For example, if customers buy a certain amount worth of items, you could give them one of your less popular items for free, or allow them to purchase your discounted items. This is a good way to promote and recommend less popular items.

You can also include a price list of your promotional items or discount goods in the packages you send out. Who knows? Your less popular items might become hot sellers too.

Off-Season: A Good Time to Find New Sources

Everything has two sides, and the off-season is not entirely bad. The off-season is actually a great time for restocking, especially for large sellers. Stocking up on high-quality, low-cost items during the off-season and selling them below market price during the peak season gives you a competitive edge, and you can do it too.

Cherish Valuable Time and Remember the Principle of Price Victory

Perhaps during a certain period, the traffic to your items may be low or your business may not be doing well. Do not lower your sails or give up. Instead, use this valuable time to summarize experiences, broaden your thinking, and develop strategies to prepare for the "boom" that may come in the future.

In fact, during the off-season, the purchase prices of many items may also be relatively low. You should pass on this price difference to your customers, allowing them to get more value when purchasing during the off-season. This can spark the purchasing desire of many customers.

Be Creative and Lead Consumption

With the improvement in people's living standards, consumption habits are quietly changing. Start from the general consumption habits, think more from the customer's perspective, and unconventional ideas may make your store a hit!
