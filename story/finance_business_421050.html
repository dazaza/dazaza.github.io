2011 College Entrance Exam Guidance: How to Choose Between Schools and Majors

According to the Shandong College Entrance Examination Admissions News, when filling out college applications, should one prioritize choosing a school or a major? This is the hardest decision for students and parents. Especially for mid-to-low scoring candidates in each batch, this problem is most evident. Choosing a good school may mean giving up a good major; wanting to fill in a favorite major means having to be cautious about the school, because popular majors at prestigious schools are highly competitive. For this reason, admissions officers from some universities in Shanghai suggest that one can compromise on the choice of school but should strive for a better major. Interest is the best teacher; each student should discover their own interests so that they can study hard and consciously after entering university, thus laying a solid foundation for their future career path.

Major vs School

One step back for school, one step forward for major, interest comes first

According to statistics, over 70% of university dropouts occur in the first year, mainly due to lack of interest in the chosen major, inability to adapt to the academic requirements of the school, and lack of motivation to study.

Zhuang Lianxiong, Deputy Director of the Admissions Office at Shanghai Jiao Tong University, stated that there are currently some students in universities who only considered the prestige of the school when applying, neglecting the importance of the major. After entering the university, they realize they don't like their major, leading to academic neglect. Some parents, when helping their children fill out applications, disregard the child's interests and insist on applying to famous universities. The child gets admitted but ends up being placed in an undesirable major, making their studies painful. Zhuang Lianxiong emphasized that interest is very important when filling out applications, and both students and parents should try to grasp its significance.

Zhou Hong, Director of the Admissions Office at East China Normal University, with years of admissions experience, also reminded applicants that interest should be the primary factor when choosing a major, while the choice of school should also be carefully considered. Prestigious universities generally have strong faculty and a concentrated learning atmosphere, which gives them significant advantages over general institutions. Although professional divisions are becoming increasingly detailed and what one studies in university does not necessarily determine one's future career, universities with stronger teaching capabilities not only focus on professional education but also emphasize the cultivation of students' overall quality and innovative abilities. He pointed out that key universities provide more opportunities for students to study abroad than ordinary universities and also have higher proportions of direct graduate admissions, which may benefit students' future development.

Lu Zhongping, Director of the Admissions Office at Donghua University, believed that choosing a school and a major are equally important. When selecting a school, one should not only look at visible indicators such as doctoral programs and key laboratories but also consider the academic atmosphere and educational environment of the school. Lu Zhongping said that parents should not only help their children choose a good school and a good major but also assist them in identifying their interests and planning their future careers. His suggestion is: it is better to compromise on the choice of school and strive for a better major.

Some parents think that getting into a university first and then transferring to a different major is a viable option. However, admissions officers from various universities indicated that most Chinese universities still have many restrictions on major transfers for current students. If candidates rely on the hope of transferring majors after enrollment, they should at least find out whether the university allows major transfers and what conditions are required.

Popular vs Unpopular Majors

Industries develop and change, popularity is not absolute

Majors with higher employment rates usually become popular choices in university admissions, such as economics management, finance, computer science, civil engineering, architecture, machinery, and foreign languages, which have always been hot. Zhou Hong reminded that popular majors are not necessarily always popular, and currently unpopular majors may not always remain so. He explained that the computer science major, which began to be less favored a few years ago, has now become a popular employment choice again. For example, the software engineering major at East China Normal University used to have a low admission score, but in recent years, its employment situation has been very hot, with an average starting salary of 5300 yuan for graduates.

Some popular majors at certain universities are not necessarily those with high demand for talent. Some urgently needed talents, such as petroleum engineering, marine engineering, nuclear energy, etc., due to their specialized nature, have relatively narrow employment prospects and fewer admissions. However, because these fields are in urgent need of talent, employment is generally not a problem, and salaries and benefits are also good. Candidates should consider not only the current popularity of a major but also its future prospects when filling out applications.

New vs Traditional Majors

Key consideration for new majors is the school’s characteristics

Compared to traditional majors like economics, law, and literature, newly added majors in local universities this year are almost all concentrated in the field of new energy technology. East China University of Science and Technology added two new majors: Resource Recycling Science and Engineering, and New Energy Materials and Devices; Donghua University added the Functional Materials major; and Shanghai University of Engineering Science added the New Energy Science and Engineering major. These new majors started recruiting from 2011 and can also accept transfers from other majors within the same university or from second-year undergraduates. The new energy technology field will become a new growth point for future economic development, with a large demand for professional talent, which is the purpose of adding these majors by various universities.

For new majors, since there are no previous admission scores as references, it is often difficult for candidates and parents to make accurate judgments. Generally speaking, a university adds a new major based on a certain level of educational foundation. Sometimes, a newly added major quickly becomes a popular one with high admission scores; while at other times, the admission scores for newly added majors may be relatively low.

What is a popular major?

So-called popular majors are relative. Popular ones have broad employment prospects but also attract a large number of job seekers, with many schools offering these majors, such as finance accounting, computer science, international economy and trade, etc. So-called unpopular majors have narrower employment prospects but fewer job seekers, meaning less competition if there are job opportunities, such as minor languages, water conservancy and hydropower (small hydroelectric power stations in southern mountainous areas have a promising future and profit), horticulture, etc. Some majors may currently face employment difficulties, but in a few years, they might become very popular. For example, e-commerce might currently face employment difficulties, but with the continuous popularization of the Internet, online transactions will surpass traditional commerce. Bill Gates once said, "In the future, it will either be e-commerce or no commerce." Therefore, there is no absolute concept of a "good" major; the key is whether it suits your child. For example, girls generally prefer language-related, finance-related, financial-related, and economic trade-related majors, while boys tend towards computer science, architecture, and engineering-related majors.

Currently popular majors include international economy and trade, accounting, computer science, civil engineering (civil construction), architecture, urban planning, etc.

Therefore, for new majors, one can judge them based on the school's educational characteristics and the social demand for the major. If the newly added major aligns with the school's educational characteristics, its professional strength is relatively stronger and has certain characteristics and advantages. If the newly added major is a new attempt by the school to broaden its academic scope, then due to the lack of historical accumulation in professional development, one should pay more attention to the school's measures to develop this major and the changing trends in social demand corresponding to this major.
