How should a woman handle an unplanned pregnancy?

Psychological aspects of unplanned pregnancy:

First, stay calm. Don't be afraid. There is no obstacle that cannot be overcome. An unplanned pregnancy is just something that happens at the wrong time. Don't blame yourself too much. Instead, you should first seek help from someone you trust. Ideally, you should tell your mother. Even if she gets upset or scolds you, it's all out of concern for you. If this is not possible, you should at least seek help from a close friend and quickly move past the shadow of the unplanned pregnancy. Let your family and friends provide emotional support and guidance on how to handle the situation.

Secondly, be brave. Since the fact has already occurred, you need to face it bravely. Dealing with the unplanned pregnancy as soon as possible should be your immediate priority. Consider this experience as a lesson and make sure it doesn't happen again. So, how should you handle an unplanned pregnancy?

Preparations before abortion:

To handle an unplanned pregnancy, first, prepare for the abortion. Abortion methods include medical abortion and surgical abortion. However, whether it's surgical or medical abortion, pre-abortion checks are very necessary, mainly to confirm if it's a normal pregnancy. If it's an ectopic pregnancy, an abortion cannot be performed, which is very important.

The timing for surgical and medical abortions differs. Medical abortion is only suitable for normal pregnancies within 49 days, as confirmed by ultrasound. Surgical abortion can be performed up to 12 weeks of pregnancy. However, the earlier within the specified timeframe, the better, as it causes less harm to the body. Therefore, if you decide to have an abortion due to an unplanned pregnancy, you should act quickly and go to the hospital for a check-up. Then, based on your specific situation, proceed with the abortion.

Doctors generally recommend surgical abortion because painless surgical abortion has a high success rate. The best current surgical abortion method can avoid complications and has strong uterine preservation capabilities, making it very suitable for young women who plan to have children in the future. On the other hand, medical abortion has significant side effects and carries over a 10% chance of abortion failure or incomplete abortion, causing more harm.

Nutritional supplementation and rest after abortion: Regardless of the type of abortion, you need to observe a small postpartum period. Ideally, you should rest for 15 days, but if that's not possible, at least 3-7 days. Besides resting, pay attention to your diet. Try to eat protein-rich foods and consume fewer high-fat foods. Avoid cold and spicy foods. It's best to clean the vagina daily and abstain from sexual activity for a month to prevent infection.

What are the symptoms of an unplanned pregnancy? Many women don't realize they're pregnant and aren't prepared, so when they discover their pregnancy, they become flustered. This is mainly because they are unaware of the symptoms of unplanned pregnancy. Below, experts from Hospital 411 provide detailed answers regarding the symptoms of unplanned pregnancy.

● Symptom one of unplanned pregnancy: Menstrual cessation

Women who have had sexual intercourse and whose periods are usually regular, if their period does not come on time and exceeds 10 days, should consider the possibility of pregnancy. This is the earliest sign of pregnancy. The longer the overdue period, the greater the likelihood of pregnancy.

● Symptom two of unplanned pregnancy: Morning sickness

After missing a period, pregnant women may gradually experience some abnormal phenomena known as early pregnancy reactions. The first reaction might be feeling cold, followed by fatigue, sleepiness, dizziness, loss of appetite, food aversions, cravings for sour foods, nausea towards greasy smells, morning sickness, and even vomiting.

● Symptom three of unplanned pregnancy: Frequent urination

Due to the enlarging uterus pressing on the bladder during pregnancy, there is an increase in urination frequency. However, there are no symptoms of urinary tract infections like urgency or pain.

● Symptom four of unplanned pregnancy: Breast changes

Breast development may occur, with enlargement of the nipples, darkening of the nipples and areolas, formation of small nodules around the nipple, breast tenderness, swelling, and occasionally secretion of small amounts of milk.

● Symptom five of unplanned pregnancy: Fatigue

In the early stages of pregnancy, many women feel tired, weak, and sleepy. However, this phase won't last long and will soon pass. Generally speaking, women with regular sexual activity who do not get their period a week after their cycle should go to the hospital for a urine test to confirm pregnancy.

● Symptom six of unplanned pregnancy: Elevated temperature

After an unplanned pregnancy, women can measure their basal body temperature daily, and those who are pregnant often show signs of elevated temperature.

● Symptom seven of unplanned pregnancy: Pigmentation

Some women may develop brownish pigmentation on their face and along the midline of the abdomen during pregnancy.
