Yesterday, villagers from Zhanggou Village in Xizhen Town, Gongyi City, called our reporter to say that they had uncovered a cave while drilling a well on the mountain. The naturally formed lava inside was crystal clear, colorful, and extraordinarily magnificent, just like a crystal palace, with a very large area.

A natural cave was discovered during well-drilling.

Yesterday morning, I arrived at Zhanggou Village. Villagers Zhang Dazhou and Zhang Sanhong stood by a dry well on a mountain peak over 800 meters above sea level, excitedly telling me: "From this well opening, down below, there's a whole other world. All sorts of rocks are crystal clear, colorful, and extraordinarily magnificent, just like the legendary Crystal Palace."

According to Zhang Dazhou, he contracted three major peaks of the Songshan Mountain Range - Taibai Peak, Diwu Peak, and Yinxing Peak - for reforestation and greening of barren mountains. Not long ago, they planted 200,000 young cypress trees. To solve irrigation issues, they decided to drill a well on the mountain. During reconnaissance, they found that the soil on the north slope of Yinxing Peak was relatively moist, so they chose to drill here. Unexpectedly, when the well was dug to 20 meters deep, they found it was hollow underneath, revealing a large cave, pitch black and seemingly bottomless.

To investigate further, they descended into the cave using ropes. They were astonished to see that the naturally formed lava inside had bizarre shapes, crystal clear, colorful, and exquisitely crafted, as if entering another world.

The cave area is not small, but its depth is unknown.

I tied a triangular belt around my body and descended into the cave. Following the weak light of my flashlight, I saw that the lava in the cave took various forms, some resembling small mountains, others grain warehouses, with shapes like cones, lions, Maitreya Buddhas, dragons emerging from the sea, eagles spreading their wings, roosters crowing, rock walls with surging rock waterfalls, sharp swords radiating cold light, upright jade pillars, fish scales made of pearls and agates, colorful flower clusters, condensed jade forming snowflakes, vast rivers, or emerald green, or snow white, or dark brown, or purple, or blood red, or yellowish green, splendid colors making one unable to take it all in.

The entire cave runs from northeast to southwest, with many collapse traces inside. Judging from the appearance, no one has entered before. Apart from the dust that fell in when the villagers were digging the well, the entire cave remains in its original natural state.

Currently, the cave length that can be explored on foot is about 30 meters. Since I did not carry any safety equipment, I did not venture deeper, and the cave's depth is still unknown. The temperature inside the cave is slightly lower than outside. The air inside is more humid, with dewdrops on the rock walls, mud sticking to the body that cannot be brushed off. I could hear dripping water sounds from time to time.

The air feels somewhat suffocating. Despite the great tranquility inside, my ears still feel ringing, and hearing feels more strenuous than under normal conditions.

The cave is 3 kilometers away from the Shaolin Temple scenic area.

Zhang Dazhou said: "Our location is over 20 kilometers away from the urban area of Gongyi City, 3 kilometers east of the Songyin Scenic Area, and 3 kilometers west of the Shaolin Temple Scenic Area. Developing tourism projects here has a unique geographical advantage. This natural cave will play a positive role in further studying and verifying the formation of Mount Song and its geological structure. We must protect this Crystal Palace intact and wait for relevant departments to conduct scientific research."

It is reported that this natural cave has been discovered for 15 days, but few local people know about it.

Currently, Zhang Dazhou and his partners take turns on duty, guarding the wellhead day and night, waiting for relevant experts to further investigate and verify.

Cave and Stalactites

The exposure area of limestone and dolomite in China is approximately 1.3 million square kilometers, accounting for about 1/7 of the national total area, with hundreds of thousands of underground caves, making it the Cave Kingdom of the world.

Rainwater falling on the surface absorbs acidic substances such as carbon dioxide from the air, becoming acidic water flows, slowly seeping into the ground along the cracks in the rocks, dissolving the rock walls along the way, forming various types of caves.

Due to reduced pressure, loss of carbon dioxide, and evaporation of water, oversaturated water containing carbonate substances precipitates and crystallizes again inside the cave, attaching to the ceiling, walls, and floor of the cave. Those generated by dripping water from the cave ceiling are called "stalactites", those growing upwards from the cave floor are called "stalagmites", and when the two connect, they are called "stone columns". Those hanging from the cave ceiling due to flowing water are called "stone belts" or "stone flags", those flowing down the cave walls are called "stone curtains", "stone waterfalls", or "stone shields", those generated on the cave floor are called "edge stone dams" or "stone terraces". Those generated by splashing water are called "grape stones", "stone mushrooms", "stone lingzhi", or "stone corals". Those deposited by pool water are called "stone flowers", "moon milk stones", "cloud basins", or "cave pearls", etc. Therefore, the morphology of cave deposits varies greatly, extraordinary and strange, and since groundwater also contains different mineral components, the deposits appear colorful.
