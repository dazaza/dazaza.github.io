On weekends or holidays, have you ever thought about having a fragrant SPA? Even if you stay at home, you can turn the bathroom into a home SPA room.        Hydrotherapy generally refers to SPA, which comes from the Greek acronym Solus Par Aqua, meaning health is in the water. Therefore, people combine natural water resources with bathing, massage, and aromatherapy to promote metabolism, satisfying six sensory needs: hearing (music), smell (natural floral aromas), sight (natural landscapes), taste (healthy diet), touch (meticulous massage), and thought (relaxation of the mind) for a refreshing experience.

       SPA not only focuses on cleaning the skin and massaging the body but also emphasizes the integration and interaction of humans with the environment, pursuing spiritual cleansing to attain peace. SPA encompasses nutrition, exercise, liberation of the mind, and facial and full-body care and conditioning. If you think SPA salons are too expensive and you can't go on vacation, doing your own SPA at home is actually very simple as long as you have a bathroom. The prerequisite is to create a relaxed and comfortable environment with ample time, forgetting the mundane matters that bother you. Play your favorite music and create a comfortable, natural setting. Once everything is ready, you can start your Home SPA.

       Preparing for Home Spa

       1. Fill the bathtub with water at about 36-42 degrees Celsius. You can prepare a thermometer to conveniently control the water temperature.

       2. Prepare exfoliating products to exfoliate the whole body, slim down, detoxify, and promote lymphatic circulation.

       3. Choose different scented essential oils to promote blood circulation, relieve fatigue, and enhance metabolism during your bath.

       4. Equip yourself with massage tools such as natural loofah gloves or sponges.

       5. Additionally, you can prepare music, herbal tea, candles, etc., according to your personal preference to enhance the ambiance.

       6. Besides buying various SPA products, you can also mix your own custom SPA concoctions. Here are some recipes for you now.

       Home Spa Procedure

       Part 1: Cleansing

       The first step of Home SPA is cleansing. Pour your favorite shower gel over your body for washing, then use exfoliating products with your fingertips or a sponge to gently scrub away dead skin cells, allowing your skin to renew itself. Even on regular days, it's advisable to exfoliate about once every two weeks. If certain parts of your skin are more sensitive or dry, you can avoid those areas when exfoliating.

       DIY SPA-grade Shower Gel

       3 teaspoons of honey

       2 liters of fresh milk

       10 drops of essential oil

       A handful of bath salts

       A few rose petals (adjustable based on personal preference)

       Method: Mix honey, fresh milk, essential oil, and bath salts together, pour it into the hot water in the bathtub, then sprinkle rose petals into the water, and you're ready for your bath.
