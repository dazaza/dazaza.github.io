The Last Time We Hold Hands

by huiming on 2012-02-06 23:48:38

The Last Hand-holding

This time, it was his hand being held in hers.

It was a pair of hands gnawed thin by the teeth of time: grayish-yellow skin, like old yellow paper, full of spots like stains; restless tendons exposed, slightly creating a little space between the skin and finger bones. The muscles that once made this hand appear strong and powerful were gone. This was a piece of work carved out by years of illness. To put it disrespectfully, you could almost use the word "claw" to describe that hand.

However, she still tightly held onto this hand. For an hour, then another hour, she sat by the bed where he lay, looking at his emaciated face, listening to oxygen come out from the steel cylinder like a cannon barrel, bubbling through water filtration, flowing slowly through fine, blue tubes into the two lungs that had been almost entirely consumed by cancer cells, her expression somewhat vacant.

For a long time, they were both silent. Suddenly, she felt the hand move in hers, so she loosened her grip on it. The hand immediately turned lightly like a bird yearning for freedom, gripping her hand in return.

"Do you want some water?" She asked softly, leaning close to his face.

He didn't answer. He just pulled her hand weakly. She knew he simply had no strength left; from that hand, she already sensed the speed at which life was preparing to leave this body. But based on her understanding of their fifty-plus years of married life, she followed the will of that hand, chasing its subtle direction, gently moving closer to him.

When she reached his chest, she felt his fingers still moving. When she moved to his neck, those fingers seemed to still command: forward! Don't stop!

Everything became clear. She gripped that withered hand with all her might, along with her own hand, placing them together on his lips. That withered hand stopped moving, only his lips quivered faintly. A drop of turbid tear rolled down from his grayish-yellow, wrinkled cheek.

Many memories surged up in her heart.

From the first time these two hands were joined together, he would boldly and recklessly pull her delicate hand to his lips. Back then, his hands were strong, rosy, and full of power. She tried to break free from his grasp, but like a bird trapped in a cage, she couldn't escape the door formed by his fingers until she willingly let her hand stay on his lips.

Habits are formed from the first time. These two hands have been holding each other, year after year, until their children grew up one by one and flew away from them. In times of poverty, they would sit by the bed, he pulling her hand to his lips; in times of hardship, he would raise her hand to his lips. Fingers were like some independent lives with spirit and speech, as long as they were held together and kissed lightly, it was like a magician blowing a magical breath, and everything was there. Confidence, courage, wealth, everything was there.

They sometimes curiously asked each other what love is, could it be just these two hands holding each other, plus a light kiss? Perhaps this was just their unique way. Whether it was a short separation or a sudden reunion, even resolving any quarrel for life that every family must inevitably have, it was always this ritualized action.

Yet, they understood each other's hand language: concern, longing, resentment, apology, encouragement, comfort...

Now, life was about to reach its end first through his hands. The youth and love they once had, the shared happy memories, will all depart from these...

Her hand stayed on his lips for only a brief moment before she felt that withered hand stop moving, losing its temperature. The room suddenly fell silent, as the gurgling sound of the oxygen filter bottle ceased. It was time!

She did not shed tears, standing up, looking at that face which was once so familiar yet suddenly became strange, slowly grabbing his hand, lightly pressing it against her own lips. She felt that the life of that person ran over the bridge of her arm, merging into herself.

She believed she would not be alone, tomorrow, it would still be two lives, two souls facing this same world.
