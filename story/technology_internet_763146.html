Living in pain for the sake of face: 3 reasons why Apple doesn't launch a large-screen iPhone

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 16:19:18

Today, there are media reports claiming that Apple is testing iPhone and iPad models with larger display sizes. Since Apple continuously tests new products, this report does not necessarily mean that Apple will soon release an iPhone with a larger screen.

However, the question remains: why has Apple been slow to introduce phones with larger displays, especially when competitors like Samsung have already sold tens of millions of large-screen phones?

Here are the reasons that Apple CEO Tim Cook has repeatedly given over the past few months:

— The iPhone is designed for single-handed use by users. The iPhone 5 features a 4-inch display, which most users can still comfortably use with one hand.

— A larger iPhone screen would compromise image quality. Cook said that a larger display means making sacrifices in terms of color and resolution. This argument seems a bit strange, considering the stunning 9.7-inch Retina display on the iPad.

— It would create difficulties for developers. If Apple releases an iPhone with a larger screen, developers would need to modify their apps to adapt to the new screen size; otherwise, black borders would appear around the apps. This would cause frustration for developers and prevent them from delivering the ideal user experience.

Of course, Apple's usual strategy is to claim "we won't make a certain product," only to release such a product years later, as was the case with the iPhone, iPad, video-playing iPods, and the iPad Mini.
