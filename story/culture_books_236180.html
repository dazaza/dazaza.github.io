"The Ex-Wife's Leisurely Days" - A Woman Striving Among the Mistresses

by jqrxass on 2009-09-11 10:39:28

Song Huaying is the young lady of Hanlin House, but her father is a flirtatious man who constantly replaces his concubines. After her birth mother became desperate and left home to become a nun, she had no choice but to transform into a "mother tiger" to protect her young brother from the various abuses of the concubines. Until Song Huaying met a nobleman by chance and started secretly operating the richest business in the country - Luo Huaying. Then the "old fox" who lusted after her finally appeared...

Equipped with a treaty with the "old fox", Song Huaying, who appears tough and aggressive on the outside, married into Ning王府 in great pomp as the principal wife. However, at the same time, there was also the side wife Yuan Jixiang, whose beauty surpassed all others. In order to gain freedom out of the cage and due to the unequal treaty, she already had no hope for love or marriage, so she deliberately drove away Prince Ning on their wedding night. In an instant, the joyous bride turned into a pitiful divorced wife...

Her intricate connections with Prince Ning fully explained the truth that fate is unpredictable. Despite the multiple identities between them, the scheming concubines, and the "old foxes" eyeing their wealth, Song Huaying, who believed in "one lifetime, one spouse", and Yang Yuling, who followed the belief of "wishing for a devoted partner, never parting until white-haired", eventually encountered their ideal love in their own way.

The Leisurely Days of the Divorced Wife Part One

Song Huaying joked while flipping through the papers: "Jun'er isn't young anymore. How about we take this opportunity to pick a decent sister-in-law? The second miss of Minister Yuan's family isn't bad, but after being described by you, even if we give it a fifty percent discount, it's not worth looking at; This one is the third miss of Censor Zhang's family, quite refined and elegant, like a modest jewel, with unique charm; Isn't this one the youngest granddaughter of Prime Minister Zhang? She seems lively and carefree, very innocent and romantic..."

The Leisurely Days of the Divorced Wife Part Two

"Although your father made mistakes back then, men having three wives and four concubines is very common. Your mother just couldn't handle it and thought too much, which is why she became a nun," sighed Master Song Hanlin. He had been pondering for over a decade but still couldn't understand why his seemingly gentle original wife would be so resolute. He then seriously educated Song Huaying: "You mustn't follow your mother's example on this matter. This time, the Emperor arranged the marriage, and more than one person will enter Ning王府, but since you are the principal wife, you ultimately have the upper hand..."

The Leisurely Days of the Divorced Wife Part Three

"Miss, you don't know, ever since that Lady Yuan took charge, our life at Qiushuzhai has been difficult. Originally, as the Princess Consort, the quality of your food should at least match hers, right? But yesterday, Xiaowu heard that Lady Yuan's meals were much better, and everything in the kitchen was prepared for her first before it came to us at Qiushuzhai. Even the laundry service does the same – they wash her clothes first and only slowly get around to ours at Qiushuzhai. Miss, you don't know, we've been bullied by everyone."

The Leisurely Days of the Divorced Wife Part Four

Song Huaying also realized her inappropriate behavior. Taking a deep breath, she calmed herself down and then lied to Master Xingkong: "Master, you may not know, but I have long-standing grievances with Prince Ning. I've always avoided him. If he discovers my whereabouts now, I fear both my brother and I will lose our lives. Master, saving a life is equivalent to building a seven-tiered pagoda, let alone saving three lives now." In a critical moment, Song Huaying included Xiaowu's life in the count.
