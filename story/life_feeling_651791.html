Luxurious melancholy, lonely release

The wind stirs up the sky full of sand, willow catkins fill the air, a streak of sunset reddens the clouds. Trees that have grown for ten years bow their heads, century-old iron trees bloom, the scorching summer takes away the flowers in full bloom, and also takes away that haunting thought. The fiery month of July flashes with passion, but we meet in this season, part ways, and carry the same longing.

The waves of life are stirred up into ripples that spread circle by circle. The laughter of the past appears before my eyes, and I, like a passerby, numbly watch in the hustle and bustle of the world. Walking through those once familiar street corners, they now feel hollow and lonely. Faint whispers reach my ears; the bustling streets have now fallen into decline, with a few scraps of paper fluttering in the wind! A pair of embracing lovers walks by, and I realize it was my own encounter!

The fireworks have faded, but the sky remains. Moonlight spills over a thousand miles, silver light, bamboo shadows, small windows, swaying boats quietly narrate. The vows made then were so sincere, so innocent, but now seem so powerless. Fleeting youth, the ground still bears faint footprints, striking the shackles of time, flowing into the river of time, silently, distantly, never to resurface again. That splendor flies towards the horizon, departing from me. To touch it leaves only a sigh, a faint memory.

Wisteria blooms beautifully, spiraling upwards circle by circle, carrying my heart far away. For whom does this rainy year cry? Strumming the zither, concealing feelings of loneliness, tears slide down slowly. Picking up a petal of a fallen flower, can she smell it? Holding the pen here, starting on plain paper, depicting with a calm heart, gazing at the plain face on the wall, my heart breaks.

In dreams, tears flow without words, drizzling rain spreads, whose courtyard is it? Fallen flowers are not unlike infatuated people. Whose window sill sees shadows swaying, forgetting extinction, lampshades following, leaving me wandering alone. How unbearable solitude is, pouring a cup of wine, raising it to invite the moon, drinking empty dreams, unable to sleep, leaves cover the pavilion steps, as if from another world, who will entangle with me! In the dimly lit place, breaking off a piece unspeakable, I leave alone, emotions drift away with the wind.

Birds chirp in the cold forest, the cold moon reflects through the curtain, the curtain rolls up clear shadows dancing, disrupting the dance. Half a pond of river water, half a river of tears, half the sky, half a lifetime's heart. The fleeting years gone by, who waits for me in that dimly lit place? Suddenly looking back, only leaving behind an encounter!

When flowers bloom, butterflies dance, when flowers fade, rain falls in profusion. Playing a tune, who dances for me? Blowing a flute, who accompanies me? After the song ends, I dissipate like wisps of smoke, misty and hazy. Enjoying the leisure of a pale moon, observing the serenity of pink lotuses, a beauty rests on the bridge, under which the river flows vast and mighty, green lotus waving silver, viewing the long river from the platform. Yet unknown to all, you look out from under the moon, just for the fragrance of willows by the bridge, clouds roll and disperse, two unforgettable, a thread of hidden emotion breaks the intestines!

Longing afar, leaving trails of sentiment, spreading pools of meaning, only knowing the moonlight, lingering in gentle breezes, following shimmering ripples, dispatching a solitary boat, recalling tunes of sorrowful departures? Adrift, heart follows the wind, yet cannot find those memories, bewildered by yesterday's encounter, yet unable to express the parting sorrow!

Rain meets the sky, knowing each other but parting ways. People walk on the bridge, as if treading on lotus fluff! Drop by drop, disturbing the mind, borrowing a tune slightly drunk, dispersing a lifetime's intentions, walking in the rain filled with sorrow, feeling the beauty of what once belonged to us!
