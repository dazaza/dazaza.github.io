Weibo real-name system is a disadvantage for Tencent Weibo but a boon for Sina Weibo.

by edivista on 2011-12-21 11:16:22

Background: The Beijing Municipal People's Government News Office, the Beijing Municipal Public Security Bureau, the Beijing Municipal Communications Administration, and the Beijing Internet Information Office today released the "Several Provisions on the Development and Management of Weibo in Beijing", stipulating that any organization or individual registering a Weibo account must use real identity information (backend information rather than frontend disclosure), otherwise they can only browse but not post. Relevant websites need to standardize users within three months. I may not be an expert in other areas, but I do know something about Weibo, so let me share my thoughts.

Firstly, the four main functions of Weibo:

(1) Social function (interaction between you and your friends)

(2) Media function (such as New Weekly, Southern Metropolis Daily... also including self-media celebrities with large fan bases like Pan Shiyi)

(3) Marketing function (brand Weibos of various companies, such as Durex, Xiaomi mobile phones...)

(4) Open platform (such as game open platforms...)

I. The impact of real-name registration on Sina Weibo:

The impact of real-name registration on Sina Weibo is minimal. Simply put, no matter how it changes, Weibo remains the freest place for speech. You can't access more free platforms like Facebook or Twitter since you're in China. As long as you are in China, Weibo is the freest place, and there is no short-term alternative. Detailed analysis is as follows:

Real-name registration has limited impact on (1) and (2), and no impact on (3) and (4).

On (1), the impact is limited because:

Before the real-name system, as long as you didn't have many followers, even if you said controversial things or posted pornographic images, Sina would turn a blind eye and wouldn't bother managing it - because your words were only spreading in a very small circle, just like chatting with a few friends, which doesn't affect the overall social situation. The only impact on (1) might be that some people find certification troublesome or have other concerns, so they don't certify and thus don't post, but generally, such users actually contribute little to Sina Weibo as a whole. Those who have a need to speak will naturally undergo real-name certification.

On (2), the impact is very limited because:

To make a big thing happen on Weibo, it must be either

A. Initiated by media/celebrities with large fan bases: For example, Yao Chen's previous statement about her relative's unfair treatment during demolition. Or

B. Participated in forwarding by media/celebrities with large fan bases.

In fact, most situations are A + several Bs, and these media/celebrities with large fan bases are usually V-certified users. Some non-V users are also well-known, such as @veggie being Wang Fei, @blowgod being Eason Chan. Even people like @zuoyeben, it's basically known that he's an advertising copywriter from Qingdao, and people have seen his photos... My point is: even for the media function, the most important participants have already been certified under real names. The impact of real-name registration is small.

II. Now let's talk about the benefits of real-name registration for Sina Weibo:

1. Basically eliminates the problem of zombie followers

This is extremely good. Ordinary users may not feel it deeply, but if you are an operator of a large Weibo or a professional in Weibo marketing, you will know that zombie followers pose a huge threat to Weibo. Simply put, it turns the natural process of survival of the fittest into survival of the unfittest.

Firstly, Weibo itself has a screening process of survival of the fittest:

The more famous you are, the more people like your Weibo posts, the better you write, the more forwards you get, and the more fans you have. But zombie followers (including zombie forwards and zombie comments) destroy this screening process. The biggest impact of zombie followers is on (2) media function and (3) marketing function.

Impact on (2) media function:

People cannot choose who to follow based on the most convincing indicator - the number of followers. This media Weibo might be doing poorly, but due to buying zombie followers, it appears to have many followers. Most people will think that this media-type Weibo is doing well. Weibo can acquire these deceived followers through the same method, so the Weibo owner doesn't need to focus on content creation, while truly good Weibos without bought followers fall behind in terms of follower count. Therefore: survival of the unfittest.

Impact on (3) marketing function:

This directly leads to various harsh and rigid Weibo marketing activities - originally, Weibo marketing should be interesting and encourage active sharing and forwarding.

If your Weibo is poorly done, no one will forward your content, and your clients won't hire you next time.

But with zombie forwards, why should Weibo marketers bother to come up with interesting content? Just create a rigid Weibo, buy followers and forwards, and often clients can't tell (or subordinates notice but deceive their bosses together with the marketing company for performance reasons). Ultimately, excellent Weibo marketing is eliminated by poor-quality Weibo marketing. That's why what was supposed to be cool and great Weibo marketing has turned out this way. After these issues are exposed, more brands hesitate to engage in Weibo marketing (because it's hard to evaluate promotional effects), leading the Weibo marketing industry to become increasingly low-end and worse. Still, those four words: survival of the unfittest.

Without zombie followers, Weibo would be much more fun, marketing would be much more fun, companies would trust Weibo more, and be willing to invest money in Weibo - or, in other words, Sina.

2. Compared to Sina Weibo, Tencent Weibo is affected more, indirectly striking at Sina Weibo's strongest competitor.

The reason is simple: most Tencent Weibos are linked to QQ accounts (what? You don't have QQ but you use Tencent Weibo? Incredible!). Certifying Tencent Weibo means certifying QQ together, adding another psychological barrier for Tencent Weibo users. Who wants their QQ to be certified under real names? So, many people on Tencent Weibo might really not certify.

Therefore, I believe that real-name registration has a slight impact on (1) and (2), but promotes (2) and (3) well. In the long term, it is absolutely beneficial to Sina. (Source: Sina)
