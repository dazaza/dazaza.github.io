Conditions for Registering a Hong Kong Company:

1. Provide a new company name → Name checking → Results out in 15 minutes, informing whether the name is available for registration in Hong Kong.

2. Provide copies of shareholders' ID cards or passports (at least one shareholder, aged 18 or above, any nationality or corporate relationship is acceptable).

3. Registered capital (no capital verification required; if registered capital exceeds HKD 10,000, a 0.1% stamp duty will be charged by the government, equivalent to domestic stamp tax).

Example: For HKD 100,000, pay HKD 100 in stamp duty; for HKD 1,000,000, pay HKD 1,000 in stamp duty (Note: calculated accordingly).

4. Confirm the share distribution ratio of the company's chairman and shareholders.

5. Fill in the usual contact address, telephone, mobile phone, and email of the shareholders and directors in mainland China.

Note: If the company has more than two shareholders, each additional shareholder starting from the third will incur a document handling fee of HKD 200.

Options for Choosing a Hong Kong Company Name:

According to the Hong Kong Companies Ordinance, regardless of the size of the registered capital, companies registered in Hong Kong are allowed to include names such as Federation, Promotion Association, Foundation, Association, Society, Chamber of Commerce, Research Institute, Center, Travel Agency, Publisher, Business Association, Group, Enterprise, Holding, Investment, Stock, Trade, Import & Export, Culture, Film & Television, Design Institute, College, University, etc., with no restrictions on their business operations (except for industries regulated by the Hong Kong Government).

Explanation: The registration of chambers of commerce, associations, foundations, or other social organizations can be divided into two types: private and partnership. The former requires only one person, while the latter requires at least two people. Social organizations are non-profit institutions but bear unlimited liability. Only residents of Hong Kong or Hong Kong Limited Companies can establish chambers of commerce, associations, and foundations. The registration process and regulations for these social organizations are based on Chapter 310 of the Hong Kong Government's Social Organizations Registration Ordinance. Registration Process for a Hong Kong Company:

1. Sign the Power of Attorney and Agreement for the registration of the Hong Kong company.

2. Prepay 70% of the total fees for registering the Hong Kong company.

3. RuiFeng Accountant will collect statutory forms from the Companies Registry and the Inland Revenue Department, and draft the company’s articles of association.

4. Shareholders sign the statutory documents for company registration and return them to the RuiFeng organization.

5. RuiFeng submits the statutory documents for the company application to the Hong Kong Government Companies Registry and the Inland Revenue Department for registration.

6. Upon providing the above materials, we will complete the new company within 10 working days and provide the following materials to the client:

a. Certificate of Incorporation: Issued by the Hong Kong Companies Registry.

b. Business Registration Certificate: Issued by the Hong Kong Inland Revenue Department.

c. Articles of Association: Includes organizational framework and detailed rules.

d. Company Seals: Steel seal, atomic round seal, director signature seal (for bank check endorsements).

e. Company Shares: An internally issued stockbook.

f. Major Records: A record book containing information about the company's shareholders, directors, and related data.

g. Green Hard Case: For storing company documents.

7. Collect the full set of registration documents for the Hong Kong company and settle the remaining 30% balance.
