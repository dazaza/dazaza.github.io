Zhengzhou No. 29 Middle School is located in Nancun Township, Guancheng District. Yesterday being Monday, according to the school's tradition, there should have been a flag-raising ceremony for all teachers and students, but the campus was empty without any students. All classrooms were locked, without students attending class or teachers teaching.

At the gate of No. 29 Middle School, many people gathered. A bystander said, "I am the neighbor of the deceased. The boy's mother has been widowed for many years, and there is also a 78-year-old grandfather at home. All the family's hopes were on this child."

"We know about the difficulties of this student's family. Referring to the compensation standards of similar incidents at other schools, we agreed to compensate Wei Yongbin's family with 100,000 RMB, but they demanded 150,000 RMB. We refused, and as a result, the family blocked the school gate with three minibuses," said Secretary Liu. On November 20th, after the family used three minibuses to block the school gate, the school had no choice but to organize the students to climb over the wall and use ladders to divert some students home. Now, all teachers and students are forced to stop classes and wait at home for the notice of resuming classes.

In the afternoon around 5 PM, when the reporter called Secretary Liu for the negotiation results, he said that an agreement had been reached with Wei Yongbin's family in the afternoon regarding this matter, and the issue had been satisfactorily resolved for both the school and the family. "Our school currently has about 1,240 students and more than 90 teachers. We have already arranged for teachers to notify students one by one to return to school for classes in the afternoon of the 24th. If all 1,240 students are notified, they will all return to school for classes no later than the evening of the 24th."

Regarding this, Liu Zhiyu, the secretary responsible for coordinating this matter at Zhengzhou No. 29 Middle School, said that Wei Yongbin and several classmates were playing football on the playground. Without any physical collision or handling the ball, Wei Yongbin suddenly collapsed due to chest tightness and later died despite rescue efforts. After the incident, the insurance company intervened in the investigation. Since last Friday night, the school leadership has been in contact with the deceased's family, but both parties have not yet reached an agreement due to differences in compensation amounts.

According to Liu Songqiang, his cousin Wei Yongbin was a student in Class 2, Grade 10 at No. 29 Middle School. Around 12 PM on the 20th, Wei Yongbin suddenly fainted while playing football on the school playground. Upon hearing the news, the school's PE teacher, Mr. Lu, rushed to provide assistance. Due to the urgency of the situation, the school subsequently called 120 emergency services. The medical staff from the First People's Hospital of Zhengzhou arrived at the scene and performed on-site rescue. After 50 minutes, the medical staff announced that Wei Yongbin had no vital signs.

"My cousin's name is Wei Yongbin. He is 17 years old, a student here, and he died at school last Friday," said Liu Songqiang, Wei Yongbin's cousin. "The school has not solved our problems until now. Knowing that death is a big deal and that burial is peace, we really have no other options. Our family blocked the gate to seek justice."

In the morning, Wei Yongbin's homeroom teacher, Zhai Zhiyong, said at the Nancun Township government office that the doctor's explanation at the time was: Yongbin's heart could not withstand intense exercise leading to his death.

Ms. Wei, Wei Yongbin's aunt, said she was the first relative to arrive at the scene after the incident. "I held Yongbin in my arms and kept shouting, hoping to wake him up," Ms. Wei said. Wei Yongbin's father passed away unexpectedly when he was four years old, and his mother raised him and his sister under very difficult circumstances. Wei Yongbin became the spiritual pillar of the family. His mother, Ms. Liu, almost collapsed mentally upon learning of her son's death and has not eaten or drunk water for several days, leaving her physically very weak.

"We don't want to neglect the family members either." As for when the school would resume teaching, Secretary Liu said he wished he could notify the students to return to class immediately.

Regarding the negotiation results, Secretary Liu indicated that it would be inappropriate to disclose the details. At around 6 PM yesterday, Liu Songqiang said they had preliminarily reached an agreement with the school, with the school compensating 150,000 RMB. "Currently, we are further negotiating with the school regarding the posthumous affairs of Yongbin."

In response to this statement, Liu Songqiang expressed confusion: "My cousin was in excellent health. He won first place in the 3000-meter race at the spring sports meeting, and he underwent a physical examination at the Third People's Hospital just recently, with all results showing normal."
