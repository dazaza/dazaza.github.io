Seen through the window

by sujie5z60 on 2010-04-10 12:09:40

In the student dormitory, the collapsed bed frame remained as it was during the incident. The mother, Qu Yaofen, was heartbroken over the loss of her son. Chief reporter Chen Yuzhou took the photo.

"You son has encountered an issue at school, please come to the school immediately." On the morning of March 30th at 8 o'clock, two guests arrived at Qu Yaofen's home in Aniluo Village, Wandhe Village Committee, Luquan Zehai Township. They were teachers from the Wandhe Primary School where her son Xiao Su studied. After hearing their message, Qu Yaofen had a premonition of something ominous.

More than an hour later, when she arrived at the school, her premonition turned into a cruel reality that left her devastated - her seven-year-old first-grade son Xiao Su had died in the school dormitory, the cause being the collapse of the dormitory bed frame...

A classmate’s neck was trapped by the bed frame.

The children did not call for help and continued sleeping.

Around 1:00 AM on March 30th, in a boys' dormitory of Wandhe Primary School in Zehai Township, Luquan County, 12 students were sound asleep. Suddenly, a steel-framed bunk bed with four elementary school students collapsed, and in his sleep, Xiao Su was tightly pressed on the neck by the fallen bed frame.

Other students in the same dormitory woke up and found Xiao Su trapped. They tried to lift the bed frame but it was too heavy, and they couldn't lift it. They called out to Xiao Su a few times, but there was no response. Several students, unaware of the severity of the situation, climbed onto other beds and continued sleeping without notifying the teacher in time. When the school authorities discovered the incident at 6:45 AM, Xiao Su had already passed away...

At 6 PM yesterday, the reporter arrived at Wandhe Primary School.

The school is located on a flat area surrounded by mountains. There are 230 students in the whole school, 42 of whom are boarding students. Among them, 29 boys are divided into two dormitories, and 13 girls live in one dormitory. These students all come from several relatively remote villages. Due to limited school buildings and beds, boarding students could only share one bed between two people, with four students squeezed onto a single bunk bed.

The boy's dormitory where the incident occurred was a simple tiled house. The dormitory windows and doors were locked tightly. Through the window, it could be seen that one side of an iron-frame bunk bed had collapsed and was almost broken. The body still lay on the bed, covered with a quilt. It was introduced that there were three iron-frame bunk beds and one wooden single bed in the room, accommodating a total of 13 students. On the night of the incident, only 12 students stayed in the dormitory, including two 10-year-old higher grade students, while the rest were first graders, all only seven years old.

The principal realized something happened in the student dormitory only after he got up in the morning.

Principal Su Yanming of Wandhe Primary School introduced that on the evening of the 29th, seven teachers were on campus, but none of them heard any unusual sounds. From 8:00 PM to 10:00 PM on March 29th, the duty teacher checked the dormitory three times and found no abnormalities. At 6:45 AM on March 30th, Principal Su just got up and heard some strange noises coming from the boys' dormitory. He hurried over to check and found that the door couldn't be pushed open.

"I heard the students inside say the bed frame fell down, blocking the door so it couldn't be opened, and Xiao Su was still pressed under it," recalled Principal Su. "I quickly forced the door open slightly and squeezed in. I saw one end of a bunk bed near the window lying on the ground, and Xiao Su's neck was pressed. I quickly pushed the bed frame aside and picked him up, but I found his body had already stiffened, so I had to put him back on the bed..."

Before he could ask more, Principal Su urgently called the superior authority, the Zehai Central School, to report the situation, and arranged for two teachers to notify Xiao Su's parents. Later, he learned from the students that the bed frame had collapsed during the night. "The students found Xiao Su was pressed, but they couldn't lift the bed frame. They thought Xiao Su was sleeping..."

When asked about this, the other students who were in the dormitory at the time all seemed very scared and remained silent.

On the morning of March 30th, staff members from the Luquan County Party Committee, County Government, Education Bureau, Public Security Bureau, Work Safety Supervision Bureau, as well as the Zehai Township Government and Central School, rushed to the scene to investigate the matter.

According to Deputy Director Liu of the Luquan County Public Security Bureau, after the technical department of the Public Security Bureau conducted an on-site inspection, it was determined that the incident was caused by the loosening of the bed frame joint and the detachment of the support point, resulting in the collapse of the bed frame, which trapped Xiao Su's neck causing him to suffocate to death. The forensic examination showed that Xiao Su's face and neck had blood stasis marks, and there were no obvious injuries on the rest of his body, indicating death by suffocation.

Deputy Director Liu introduced that the police questioned the other students in the same dormitory. At the time of the incident, two students were sound asleep and didn't wake up, while the rest claimed they noticed Xiao Su was trapped, but unable to move the bed frame, they went back to sleep and did not inform the school teachers.

The school explained the cause of the accident,

and the family demanded 500,000 yuan in compensation.

With the loss of their son, Xiao Su's parents were overwhelmed with grief.

Xiao Su's mother, Qu Yaofen, recalled that March 28th was Sunday, originally the day her son would return home for a break. But by 7:00 PM, her son hadn't returned, making her a bit anxious. After finding out that the school was "making up classes" and thus didn't have regular breaks, she relaxed. Who would have thought that early on March 30th, the bad news came.

Xiao Su's father, Su Zaiyou, worked in Kunming for a long time and only returned home a few times a year. At 9:00 AM on March 30th, he received a call in Kunming and rushed home immediately. After viewing the scene and hearing the investigation results from the public security bureau, the family had no doubts about the cause of their son's death. However, they believed that since their son died unexpectedly in the school dormitory, the school should bear full responsibility and provide compensation.

Why was there talk of "make-up classes"? Principal Wu of the Zehai Central School explained that recently, Wandhe Primary School was undergoing "school safety engineering" construction, requiring the demolition of the existing toilet. For convenience in construction, temporary arrangements were made for normal classes on Monday and Tuesday (March 29th and 30th), with Wednesday and Thursday off as rest days.

Principal Wu introduced that Wandhe Primary School originally did not have boarding students. In September 2009, after the removal and merging of several village schools under the Wandhe Village Committee, some students moved to Wandhe Primary School for boarding education. "The school's accommodation conditions are indeed very rudimentary, but it is just a transitional period," said Principal Wu. A primary school in the Zehai Township Center, with an investment of over 10 million yuan, is under construction and will be put into use in September this year. At that time, all fourth, fifth, and sixth-grade students in the township will move to the new school, freeing up many spaces in the village primary schools. Some old buildings will then be demolished. The dormitories of Wandhe Primary School are outdated and are among the objects to be demolished, hence they haven't been repaired in recent years. Principal Su Yanming of Wandhe Primary School also mentioned that some of the beds in the student dormitories were indeed very old. During the start of this school term, some parents raised objections, and the school had repaired some of the beds, but they didn't expect an accident to happen.

Principal Wu stated that Xiao Su's unexpected death is something no one wanted to see, and the school should take full responsibility. "However, according to relevant laws, the deceased's family can only receive more than 70,000 yuan in compensation. We, adhering to the principle of putting people first and considering the difficulties of the deceased's family, offered a compensation of 150,000 yuan."

As of yesterday afternoon, the family was still negotiating with the school and relevant departments regarding follow-up matters and compensation. However, due to the large gap between the 150,000 yuan offered and the 500,000 yuan requested by the family, an agreement was not reached.

(Reporter Xu Zhaoquan)
