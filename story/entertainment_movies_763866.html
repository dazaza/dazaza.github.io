Plot:

In the extremely chaotic late Unified Silla period, Lee Kwa (played by Jung Woo-Sung), who possesses the ability to see ghosts and spirits, joins a royal exorcist team after his fiancée, Yeon-hwa (played by Kim Tae-Hee), is taken away by an evil spirit. Due to Lee Kwa's supernatural abilities, he becomes the top warrior in his unit. However, his teammates plot a rebellion under the influence of evil spirits, but their plan fails, leading to their execution. Having lost not only his beloved lover, more important than life itself, but also his comrades-in-arms who were like brothers to him, Lee Kwa vows eternal enmity with the demon world and becomes a true exorcist.

One day, he unexpectedly travels through a temple to a strange world called "Jungcheon," a place that only the dead can reach. Jungcheon is the third world between heaven and earth, where souls prepare for rebirth after forty-nine days. In this world, Lee Kwa encounters a woman who looks very much like Yeon-hwa. However, this woman insists she is a celestial being named So-hwa and does not recognize the heartbroken and grieving Lee Kwa. Unfortunately, the same evil spirits that incited the rebellion are also present here and begin hunting him down. Despite knowing the dangers he faces in this alternate dimension, Lee Kwa decides he will never leave Yeon-hwa again.

Determined to protect Yeon-hwa at the cost of his own life, Lee Kwa unexpectedly meets his comrades who were unjustly killed not long ago. By this time, they have transformed into real demons. Lee Kwa is resolute about bringing Yeon-hwa back to the mortal world, but facing his comrades, who once were like family and even saved him from execution, makes wielding his sword feel unbearably heavy...

Behind the Scenes:

The preview trailer released in advance immediately caught the attention of numerous audiences across South Korea. Its innovative imagination and cinematography create stunning visuals, even capturing the interest of picky fantasy film viewers. "Jungcheon" tells the story of a person whose soul consciousness remains after death; this place where souls reside is called Jungcheon. The protagonist, Lee Kwa, steadfastly guards his love who has lost her memory, crafting a new ghost-human romance. Especially poignant is the theme of love shining brighter amidst life-and-death separations.

This film had a pure production cost of 11 billion Korean won. The crew includes art directors who worked on "Hero" and "A World Without Thieves," as well as costume designer Emi Wada, who frequently collaborated with director Akira Kurosawa. This film undoubtedly gathers the strongest lineup of talent from both in front of and behind the camera in Asia, ensuring high-quality work.

Fun Facts:

The two main actors of Korea's first fantasy movie "Jungcheon," scheduled for release at the end of 2006, Kim Tae-Hee and Jung Woo-Sung, received invitations to attend the opening ceremony of the 11th Busan International Film Festival. Although there was a scheduling conflict between the festival opening on the 12th and the original welcome party for "Jungcheon" planned for the 14th, both actors managed to attend the festival despite their busy schedules. Prior to this, although Jung Woo-Sung had participated in many films, he had never attended the Busan International Film Festival. Known for rarely appearing at domestic or international film festivals, he eagerly accepted the invitation from one of Asia's leading film festivals without hesitation. Similarly, Kim Tae-Hee, making her film debut, gladly attended upon receiving the invitation.
