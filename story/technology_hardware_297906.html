The Current American Mindset as Seen from the Movie "2012"

by nomyliu996 on 2009-12-07 15:43:55

The American disaster blockbuster "2012" has been released in China for a while, and it's said to have received good feedback. Even I, who haven't been to the cinema for N years, couldn't help but visit the Oscar Cinema with my friends to witness the legendary end of the world with my own eyes.

Of course, Americans never forget to showcase their "human rights slogan". Near the end of the film, just as the ark is about to close its doors, facing countless ordinary people without power or money who have come to escape, the Americans advocate opening the doors of the ark, saying that everyone is equal in the face of disasters. Then, leaders from various countries all represent their people in advocating opening the doors to welcome those refugees on board. This is one of the great advantages of Americans liking to show off themselves, and we might as well learn from them.

The Americans' transformation is very positive. They are fully aware that only by uniting can they achieve victory in the face of disasters. The ideology of "American supremacy" has begun to weaken in their hearts.

It can be said that any work of art reveals its ideological connotations, and its ideological connotations reflect its ideological background, which inevitably relates to the social environment at the time. In the creation of "2012", the Americans abandoned the previous mindset of relying on their technological strength to save the Earth and added elements of cooperation between countries. Of course, courting audiences from other countries is one aspect of commercial films. However, from the actual connotations implied in this movie, the current American mentality has changed. Their subtle changes deep inside can be fully expressed through this film. The reason for their change may be related to the American economic crisis. In this major economic recession in the United States, they strongly desire to strengthen cooperation with various countries and hope that other countries will lend a helping hand. Especially at the end of the film, everyone escapes the calamity on the "arks made in China", clearly showing how much hope they place on China in resolving this economic crisis.

To put the storyline simply, the Mayans had long predicted that 2012 would be the end of the world. Although it was a prophecy, as 2012 approached, it gradually gained attention. Some American scientists confirmed through investigations that the Mayan prediction would become reality. At that time, the entire Earth would experience catastrophic destruction - strong earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, tsunamis, and other severe disasters happening simultaneously. All life and civilizations on Earth would be completely destroyed. To preserve human civilization and continue the human species, the United States joined forces with multiple national leaders to secretly research an "ark" capable of withstanding all impacts. When the disaster occurred, some national leaders and world elites would hide inside the ark, emerging after the disaster to rebuild their homes. The task of building this great ark fell on the shoulders of the Chinese.

Such fiery scenes naturally became the highlight to capture the audience's attention. However, there are some very noteworthy plots and dialogues in this film, especially the depiction of other countries during the disaster: The Italian Prime Minister preferred to die praying with his people rather than escape on the ark, undoubtedly singing high praises for the Italian leader; The male protagonist fleeing America did not pilot a Boeing 777 from the US West Coast straight across the ocean to Tibet, China, nor was it Air Force One, but rather a Russian An-225 aircraft, which was a tremendous commendation of Russian aircraft performance; After American officials arrived in China and saw the enormous Noah's Ark and the vast launch site, they couldn't help but exclaim in amazement, "Such a massive project could not be completed by any country except China." This was an infinite affirmation of China's current level of technology and manufacturing capabilities.

American disaster blockbusters rank among the world's best, with grand and realistic scenes. This "2012" not only carries forward the previous style but also perfectly combines movies with advanced technology, making the evolution of the end of the world heart-stopping. Especially when the earth splits and skyscrapers collapse, the Vatican Basilica crumbles, the Christ the Redeemer statue in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, is destroyed, elevated bridges break apart section by section, the USS Kennedy aircraft carrier is flipped over by a giant tsunami, and lava bursts during volcanic eruptions - all these scenes leave people in awe. Finally, Mount Everest sinks into the sea, the Earth's surface completely shifts, the North and South Poles thoroughly reverse, and a new Earth is born.
