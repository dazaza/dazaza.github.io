Domestic demand for clothing is promising, but exports are not optimistic.

by janydtfa04 on 2009-12-08 01:19:43

We firmly believe in the prospects of domestic clothing consumption and give the sub-industries of branded clothing and home textiles a "buy" rating. We recommend Seven Wolves (23.45, 0.27, 1.16%), Baoxin Bird (22.68, 0.08, 0.35%), Winsun Co., Ltd. (21.50, -0.01, -0.05%), and Huafu Colored Yarn.

Investment Highlights:

As of November 20th, the A-share textile and apparel sector has cumulatively increased by 110.28% in 2009, outperforming the Shanghai Composite Index by 29.10 percentage points but underperforming the Shenzhen Component Index by 7.13 percentage points. The price-earnings ratio of the textile sector is 43.78, significantly higher than the average level of the Shanghai market, and close to the overall valuation level of the Shenzhen market.

From January to October 2009, the production of chemical fiber, yarn, and clothing increased year-on-year by 11.30%, 9.63%, and 14.45%, respectively; fabric production slightly decreased by 0.3% year-on-year. Compared with the beginning of the year, the growth rates of chemical fiber, yarn, and fabric production have increased by 14.63, 7.47, and 7.98 percentage points, respectively, while the growth rate of clothing production has slightly decreased by 3.08 percentage points.

From January to October, the cumulative fixed asset investment in the textile industry increased year-on-year by 10.05%, and in the clothing manufacturing industry by 13.29%, which are respectively 30 and 9.38 percentage points higher than at the beginning of the year. However, compared with the average fixed asset investment growth rate of 33.35% for the national manufacturing industry during the same period, it is still significantly lower.

From January to October, the industry's cumulative export value reached $131.203 billion, down 12.08% year-on-year; the growth rate declined by 0.14 percentage points compared with January to September. Among them, textile exports were $49.729 billion, down 12.53% year-on-year; clothing exports were $81.474 billion, down 11.8% year-on-year.

Compared with exports, the decline in domestic clothing consumption was shorter in time, faster in recovery, and stronger in rebound. From January to October 2009, the retail sales of clothing and textiles of enterprises above the designated size amounted to 360.78 billion yuan, up 20.60% year-on-year, basically returning to the level of previous years.

In November, the domestic spot price of 328 cotton reached 14,281 yuan/ton, approaching the high point since 2006, up 30.58% from the beginning of the year, and up 9.65% since September. Viscose short fiber and polyester short fiber have risen by 53.08% and 35.83%, respectively, from the beginning of the year. Compared with fibers, the price changes of yarn and gray cloth often lag behind, and the amplitude is also smaller. This means that the rise in fiber prices is difficult to fully pass on to downstream industries, and the profit margin of enterprises will be compressed.

Regarding exports, we expect the growth rate of global textile and apparel trade in the future to decline compared with the previous few years. In the future, China's share of global trade will continue to increase, but the speed will be relatively slow. We estimate that the total export value for the whole year of 2009 will decrease by 9.27% year-on-year, and the export will increase by 5.90% year-on-year in 2010, basically returning to the 2008 level. With the recovery of the global economy, the industry's exports should maintain a 5%-8% growth over the next 3-5 years.

We believe that there is still huge growth potential in domestic clothing consumption, and the rapid urbanization process and the high-speed development of the central and western regions will become an important driving force for the growth of future clothing consumption. In the next ten years, China's clothing consumption will welcome a golden growth period.

We maintain a "neutral" rating for the textile and clothing manufacturing industries, suggest increasing holdings of listed companies in branded clothing and home textiles, and focus on recommending Baoxin Bird, Seven Wolves, Winsun Co., Ltd., and Huafu Colored Yarn.
