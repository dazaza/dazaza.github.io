Love Cupid

A small bridge over running water, the sun setting in the west, a hazy feeling about to engulf the entire world. I dragged my weary body and tailed her from behind. This was the twenty-fifth day, night and day.

I am an assassin, named Jie, known as Ruthless. One who has no feelings and does other people's dirty work for money. Twenty-five days ago, I received a job. Due to a conflict in the marketplace, I was tasked with assassinating the daughter of a business tycoon. For me, a professional hitman in the "Hidden Shadows" organization, this kind of thing should be effortless. But I forgot the greatest enemy of an assassin - emotion!

That day, after meticulous planning, I waited for my prey to arrive. Killing, taking the money, and drinking alcohol had become my habit. Sitting in the tavern waiting for my prey also became my habit.

The informant reported that the prey had appeared. After giving him his reward, I took my sword and left.

Closer, closer, step by step, the killing intent spread out. Just as I was about to draw my sword, she turned around and smiled, defusing all my murderous intentions. Staring blankly at her, I let her walk away until she disappeared. Only then did I realize that I had failed... For a good assassin, this was absolutely unacceptable.

Thus, I became a laughingstock in the assassin world. The client also hired another assassin of equal fame to complete the task I had failed.

I found him and hoped he would abandon this mission. He looked at me like I was a monster and said: With your skills, there is no way you couldn't complete such a mission. You've triggered our greatest enemy.

I turned my back on him and said: That feeling, you wouldn't understand it. Considering we were once brothers, grant me this unreasonable request!

"Impossible!" He rejected without hesitation, "This goes against the spirit of being an assassin!"

"Brother, I'm begging you," I turned around and drank a cup of strong liquor.

"Jie, you're making this difficult for me," he said hesitantly, drinking a cup and said: Give us one month. If I can kill her, I win. If I can't kill her, I'll quit.

"Thank you," raising the cup, I finished it in one gulp: Brother, I will never forget your kindness!

...

Since that day, I have been secretly guarding her,

His archery skills are superb, able to shoot through a hundred paces, and his stealth techniques are among the best of assassins. It has been twenty-five days, and I haven't dared to slack off even a bit. Releasing my killing intent, I pay attention to every breath around me. Fortunately, these twenty-five days have passed safely. But I can already feel my energy being completely drained, but I must persevere...

Finally, the twenty-ninth day has come, tomorrow is the agreed upon date. As long as I endure this one more day, she will be safe. As usual, I release all of my remaining internal strength without reservation. My killing intent fills all corners that could threaten her. She happily walks ahead, like a little doll, very beautiful and sweet...

Perhaps it was fate, or perhaps my energy wasn't enough, she turned around just like the first time I saw her, revealing her charming smile, completely stirring the fragile string in my heart. My originally almost exhausted spirit unconsciously relaxed.

For a top-level assassin, the time I relaxed would be more than enough to kill a woman with no means of defense. His killing intent surged instantly, in the dark corner, readying his arrow, drawing his bow, aiming, shooting - everything done in one smooth motion, without any delay.

I instantly understood the tragedy about to happen. There was absolutely no time to stop it. Without hesitation, I lunged forward in front of her, hoping to use my body to ensure her safety.

But I failed. I underestimated his archery skills. The arrow shot from the bow pierced my heart with perfect accuracy, its force undiminished, still penetrating her body. In the darkness, he revealed a smug smile.

The sunset was blood red. I felt life slipping away quickly, gradually losing consciousness. Turning my head, I said to her, "I'm sorry..."

She smiled, her smile was sweet.

At that moment, I also smiled. I have never laughed so happily in my entire life. The name engraved on the arrow that pierced both our bodies was clearly visible - Cupid!
