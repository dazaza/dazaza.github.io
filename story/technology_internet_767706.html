Surface 2 Review: A Second Chance for Windows RT

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 12:40:05

"Surface 2 is not a minor update, but a complete transformation," said Panos Panay, Microsoft's Surface director, during the Surface 2 launch event when discussing the redesign of the new product. "This isn't just a simple change that everyone wants to see, but it is a change that people need."

Panay mentioned changes including a lighter and thinner body, a sharper 1080p display, a faster Tegra 4 processor, and memory capacity tripled compared to its predecessor. The main and secondary camera pixels on Surface 2 have also significantly improved, allowing for better low-light performance. Other hardware improvements include a two-position adjustable kickstand and a silver-white body.

The latest Windows RT 8.1 system and Office 2013 suite also add value to Surface 2. How does the new device perform? The Verge, AnandTech, Engadget, and Wired got their hands on the machine one after another—let’s take a look at their review feedback.

Hardware and Design

The surface of the Surface 2 remains a matte magnesium alloy, effectively preventing scratches and fingerprints. The beveled edges, rounded corners, and blunt edges remain consistent with the previous generation.

Adjusting the kickstand requires a bit of force, but not too much. The hinge on the kickstand has no annoying stiffness or difficulty, and the overall structure doesn’t feel fragile. Even if you adjust the kickstand beyond its set angles, you don’t have to worry about it breaking. You can see the painstaking efforts Microsoft put into adjustments here, but Surface 2 still has a long way to go before reaching higher-end tablets.

The overall body of Surface 2 is silver-white, more appealing than the black Surface RT. The silver-white appearance also makes Surface 2 easier to distinguish from the first-generation device, avoiding any confusion.

External Keyboard

The price of Touch Cover 2 remains at $120, slightly thinner at 2.75mm compared to the previous generation. The second-generation Touch Cover adds backlighting, greatly improving text input accuracy. Microsoft says that Touch Cover 2 has 1092 sensors, while the first-generation Touch Cover only had 80 sensors.

With the cooperation of the Windows RT 8.1 system, the Touch Cover can perform some gesture operations, such as swiping the space bar to get input suggestions and using two fingers to swipe through the numeric area to highlight text.

The starting price of the physical keyboard Type Cover 2 also remains the same as the previous generation at $130. Like the Touch Cover 2, the Type Cover 2 also has backlighting, making text input convenient in the dark.

However, the touchpad on both new keyboards is still small, making file movement and dragging inconvenient. However, single-finger movement, clicking, and two-finger dragging and zooming operations are exceptionally convenient.

Unfortunately, the Power Cover keyboard introduced at the launch event couldn't be released simultaneously. Microsoft will release this battery-powered external keyboard early next year, so we’ll have to wait a bit longer for the Power Cover experience report.

Screen and Speakers

The screen is a significant update for Surface 2, with the resolution upgraded from 1366x768 to 1920x1080. Colors are brighter, contrast is higher, and the viewing angle is wider, especially providing an excellent reading experience when the kickstand is open.

The deeper black color gamut makes watching movies a pleasure, and the vivid colors almost make pictures pop out of the screen. With dual stereo speakers, Surface 2 is an excellent tool for experiencing Hulu and Netflix.

Unlike Surface Pro 2, the speakers on Surface 2 are not hidden under the kickstand but placed directly outside the machine. Two speaker grilles are located on the left and right sides of Surface 2, producing strong sound when playing videos and eliminating annoying background noise during Skype calls.

Camera

The main camera on Surface 2 has been upgraded to 5 million pixels, capable of shooting 1080p video. Image quality is average, not particularly impressive, but not terrible either.

Mashable said that the main and secondary cameras on Surface 2 perform consistently well in low light as described in advertisements, with even stronger color reproduction capabilities. The camera app can be accessed directly from the lock screen, supporting simultaneous video recording and photo taking.

System and Applications

Windows 8.1 brings back the Start button and introduces native Metro-style alarm clock, voice recorder, and calendar applications. Multi-tasking windows support proportional displays of different windows.

The number of Windows RT applications has exceeded 100,000, with commonly used apps like Amazon, Twitter, and Facebook available in the Windows 8.1 Store. Compared to last year, there is a noticeable increase in the number of Windows 8.1 applications, but it still cannot compete with App Store and Google Play. Application numbers accumulate over time, and Microsoft needs to put in more effort than its competitors to move forward faster.

Summary

The upgrades on Surface 2 are quite evident, with improvements in the screen, camera, backlight keyboard, etc., all representing leaps compared to last year. However, Surface 2 still cannot be considered a revolutionary product; the system and application quantity limit the further potential of Surface 2. Before true innovation arrives, Surface 2 may still be far from the mass market.
