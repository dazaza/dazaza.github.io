This Week, A-share Market Rebounds Sharply, and the SSE Reclaims 3300 Points＿Stock Forum

by xl0958870 on 2009-12-05 02:16:29

The much-anticipated 2009 Central Economic Work Conference is about to be convened, and it is expected to take place in Beijing this weekend. From an industry perspective, some sectors are likely to achieve significant excess returns within 1-3 months after the Central Economic Work Conference with a relatively high probability of price appreciation. Therefore, the Central Economic Work Conference may act as a catalyst for related themes. On one hand, from a historical perspective, industries mentioned most frequently at the Central Economic Work Conferences—agriculture, consumption, and energy conservation & environmental protection—have had a much higher probability of price increases and excess returns compared to other industries after the conference. On the other hand, senior central government officials and various ministries have conducted on-site investigations in regions such as Xinjiang, Chengdu-Chongqing economic zone, Ningxia, and Gansu. There is a possibility that these regions' economic revitalization will be clearly addressed during the economic work conference. Unexpected policies might serve as catalysts for the rise of related themes.

This week, large-cap blue-chip stocks surged across the board on Friday. Sinopec rose 6.78% over the week, while PetroChina increased by 5.59%. The financial sector performed relatively well this week. Brokerage stocks were the best performers, influenced by news regarding margin trading and stock index futures. Haitong Securities rose 24.32% over the week. Insurance stocks also showed strong momentum, with China Pacific Insurance increasing by 13.76%. The banking sector was relatively weaker, but Industrial and Commercial Bank of China still managed a 6.68% increase. The ChiNext market became the highlight of the first half of the week but experienced a broad correction on Friday. Dayu Water Conservation rose 29.74% over the week. Xima Automobile achieved five consecutive daily limits, driving the automobile sector upward, with Jinlong Automobile rising 25.08% over the week. Industries with significant potential for product price increases, including steel, cement, and some chemical products, also showed strong performance recently. Baosteel shares rose 17.31%, while Jidong Cement increased by 8.10%.

Institutions noted that the key focus of the market this week was the style switch on Friday. The strong rally of large-cap blue-chip stocks on Friday was mainly due to factors such as stock index futures, margin trading, and valuation. The reason why large-cap blue-chip stocks are favored by funds is due to the upcoming introduction of stock index futures and margin trading. Institutions need to gain "voice" through weighted stocks, which usually leads to a pre-emptive rush for weighted stocks before their launch. As a result, institutions increase their allocation to weighted stocks in the benchmark index, leading to the rise of these stocks. Historically, style switches often accompany short-term adjustments in the broader market. The transition of mainstream funds from one sector to another requires time and can lead to market volatility. Therefore, completing the style switch amidst fluctuations and adjustments has its rationality.

Mastering basic stock knowledge, stock forums, and further research can help understand buying and selling procedures. When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether it's a buy or sell order, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. These orders are only valid on the day they are placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you wish to trade, the quantity, and the price for buying or selling. The abbreviation typically consists of three to four Chinese characters, while the code is six digits long. The code and abbreviation must match when placing orders. Additionally, there are specific rules regarding the quantity of stocks traded: the quantity for buying stocks must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity for selling does not need to be.

There are four ways to place orders: counter submission, telephone automatic orders, computer automatic orders, and remote terminal orders.
