Story Outline:

The Birth of the Black Warrior

McTi Shuang has always been full of business acumen since childhood, deeply understanding the truth that doing business leads to prosperity. Thus, he worked hard and completed his MBA at Stanford University in the United States through sheer effort, seeking opportunities there.

After graduation, Shuang saw the potential of Ho Wentai's company and proactively approached Wentai, frankly stating that he didn't want to work as an employee but only as a partner, even if it meant owning just 0.01% of the shares. Wentai, seeing that Shuang was different from others, allowed him to join as a partner. The two became both teacher and friend, working seamlessly together, specializing in acquiring companies and selling them off in parts, becoming famous snipers in the American business world.

Later, Shuang became known as the ruthless Black Warrior in the business world, merciless and unrelenting, gradually gaining power. Similarly, the differences of opinion between him and Wentai increased. For the benefit and development of the company, Shuang decided to fully acquire Wentai's company. Unable to bear the blow, Wentai drove drunk. Shuang learned that Wentai's car lost control and fell into the sea. After the incident, Wentai's body could not be found, but Shuang firmly believed that Wentai was dead, feeling extremely guilty. Is the value of a business deal really greater than friendship, life? Shuang reflected on everything in the past and decided to no longer be the Black Warrior, retiring from the business world. From the newspaper, Shuang learned that Wentai's wife and child were living in difficult conditions in Hong Kong. Shuang felt responsible and decided to return to Hong Kong to take care of Wentai's family.
