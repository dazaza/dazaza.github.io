When the object to be weighed or the loaded vehicle is placed on the load platform, under the action of gravity, the load is transmitted through the load cell to the weighing sensor, causing deformation of the elastic body of the sensor. The strain gauge bridge attached to the elastic body loses balance and outputs an electrical signal proportional to the weight value, which is amplified by a linear amplifier. Then, the signal is converted into a digital signal through A/D conversion, and processed by the microprocessor (CPU) of the instrument to directly display the weight data. If configured with a printer, it can print records of the weighing data; if configured with a computer, the measurement data can be input into the computer management system for comprehensive management. Whether it’s a scale or a weighing machine, the basic principle is to use sensors (force sensors) to convert the weight of objects into electrical signals, send them to conditioning, A/D, and computational circuits (MCU), and then convert them into digital displays.
