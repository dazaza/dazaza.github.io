China should have at least 1 million fashion stylists.

by zhenwu5566n on 2012-02-10 10:49:08

Recently, the fast fashion brand Vancl launched a popular recruitment drive for clothing stylists: "1. Responsible for designing and formulating combinations of outfits and accessories for models; 2. Responsible for executing the overall styling and effect presentation of product displays." Around the same period, large department stores such as Cuimao Building and Ganjiakou Building also began large-scale recruitment drives for clothing stylists, offering salaries around 5000 yuan per month. At the "Finals of the Fifth Haidian District Commercial Services Vocational Skills Style Competition - Clothing Styling," Ms. Kang Lanxin, the General Manager and Director of Beijing Poshang Culture Communication Co., Ltd., stated: "Styling is no longer exclusive to runway models; clothing stylists can create looks for ordinary people using regular clothes and accessories. China lacks not image consultants but clothing stylists." Against this backdrop, Kang Lanxin founded Poshang Company. Within less than a year of opening, the company had already provided styling services to high-end clients such as bank presidents, female private enterprise bosses, and university professors.

China Should Have at Least 1 Million Clothing Stylists

Coincidentally, the Haidian Commercial Association in Beijing began searching for and cultivating a group of clothing stylists for major malls. This move aligns perfectly with Kang Lanxin's vision. Thus, under their cooperation, the "First Fashion Styling Competition" was born. In July last year, the National Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security also incorporated clothing stylists into the vocational training system. As the first company to spread the culture of clothing styling, Poshang obtained national certification for clothing stylist training and initiated the training of the first batch of clothing stylists.

The Predecessor of the Stylist: Color Stylist

The predecessor of the clothing stylist is the color stylist, a fashionable profession that emerged in countries like Japan in the 1990s. Color stylists are active across various industries. Experts have predicted that Japan has 400,000 professional stylists. If calculated based on Japan's population ratio, China should have at least 1 million professional stylists. The prospects for this profession domestically are very broad. "In China, there is a talent market gap of nearly 200,000 for this profession," said an internationally renowned fashion styling designer.

Stylists Enable 'Zero Inventory' for Enterprises

"Good styling can promote clothing sales. If clothing companies have a group of good stylists, their grasp on manufacturers and fashion trends can completely guide purchasing, achieving zero inventory. This might be the purpose of many brands hiring stylists," Kang Lanxin expressed.

"In the impact of fast fashion brands, the consumption impulse and purchasing power of the Chinese public cannot be underestimated. However, such a large consumer market is mostly consuming blindly, lacking professional guidance. With the participation of professional clothing stylists, this market can develop better, more reasonably, and healthier," industry insiders commented.

Styling Is No Longer Exclusive to Runway Models

The rapid development of China's fashion industry urgently needs clothing stylists. People in the fashion industry, fashion editors from fashion media, costume personnel from film and television production teams, enthusiasts of clothing styling, and students in art-related majors all need to learn about clothing styling. In the future, identities such as "clothing stylist," "senior shopping companion," and "wardrobe organizer" will become trendy freelance professions. In Japan, regular store clerks in clothing stores are all certified professionals in clothing styling.
