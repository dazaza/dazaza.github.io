The theory of Chinese health preservation emphasizes the coordination of Yin and Yang.

by oecsvrcg55 on 2012-03-09 11:09:17

Yin and Yang, originally referred to the orientation towards or away from the sun, with the side facing the sun being Yang and the side away from the sun being Yin. Later, it gradually developed into one of the important categories in ancient Chinese philosophy. It is an abstract generalization by the ancients of the opposing sides of interrelated natural phenomena and things, as well as the two opposing factors within the same thing. In the "Yi Zhuan" that appeared at the end of the Warring States period to the beginning of the Western Han dynasty, the theory of Yin and Yang was first comprehensively and systematically developed in the field of philosophy. The book "Yi Zhuan" contains rich dialectical thought. While fully affirming the world's eternal attribute of motion, the "Yi Zhuan" also focuses on exploring the reasons for the movement and change of things, believing that all movements originate from the interaction of two opposing forces: Yin and Yang. The "Yi Zhuan・Xi Ci" states: "The rigid and the flexible mutually push each other and generate changes"; "The sun and the moon mutually push each other and brightness is born"; "Cold and heat mutually push each other and years are formed." This means that whether it is the cyclic shift of spring, summer, autumn, and winter, or the alternation of day and night brightness and darkness, without exception, they all arise from the mutual interaction of Yin (soft, moon, cold) and Yang (hard, sun, heat). As the saying goes, "Heaven and Earth harmonize, all things transform purely; men and women combine essence, all things transform and are born." (from "Xi Ci") Therefore, everything in the world grows and changes under the mutual influence of Yin and Yang; if Yin and Yang were to be destroyed, then the movement and change of things would no longer exist. On this basis, the author of the "Yi Zhuan" clearly proposed that "the way is called the balance of one Yin and one Yang," believing that the opposition and unity of Yin and Yang is the general law of the world's movement and change. Most philosophers and natural scientists in later generations followed the line of thought of the "Yi Zhuan," using the theory of Yin and Yang as their own worldview and methodology. The famous Ming Dynasty physician and health preservationist Zhang Jiebin pointed out in the "Lei Jing・Yin Yang Le" that: "The Way is the principle of Yin and Yang. Yin and Yang are the two parts of one whole. Taiji moves and generates Yang, and stillness generates Yin; Heaven is generated from movement, and Earth is generated from stillness. Therefore, Yin and Yang are the Way of Heaven and Earth." "The Way of Heaven and Earth uses Yin and Yang to create and transform all things; the principles of people and things use the two energies of Yin and Yang to nourish and grow the hundred bones." (from "Lei Jing Tu Yi・Yi Yi") Because of this, the rules of Yin and Yang naturally became the basic guideline for the ancients to grasp and analyze the material structure, physiological function, pathological changes, dialectical treatment, and health preservation and disease prevention of the human body. In fact, traditional Chinese health preservation theory directly explains life activity phenomena under the guidance of the theory of Yin and Yang, constructing theories and practical methods for preventing illness and extending life. First, traditional Chinese health preservation theory believes that Yin and Yang are the fundamental attributes of human life activities. The "Su Wen・Sheng Qi Tong Tian Lun" states: "The root of life, fundamentally lies in Yin and Yang." What is meant by "fundamentally lies in Yin and Yang" refers, on the one hand, to the fact that human life activities can essentially be attributed to the contradictory motion of "essence" and "Yang energy." On the other hand, it refers to the fact that the human body, as an organic whole, all its organizational structures can be divided into two parts: Yin and Yang, which are both interconnected and opposed. Secondly, the theory of health preservation in our country believes that the balance of Yin and Yang is the basic sign of human health. The "Huangdi Neijing" proposes corresponding signs of human health: "Yin and Yang are evenly balanced, filling the form, the nine pulses are the same, called a normal person." It also says: "A normal person does not get sick, a person who does not get sick has the Cunkou and Renying respond to the four seasons. They correspond up and down and move together, the six meridians' pulses do not knot or stir. They correspond up and down and move together, the six meridians' pulses do not knot or stir. The origin and the warmth and coldness guarding each other, the shape, flesh, blood, and energy must correspond appropriately, this is called a normal person." (from "Ling Shu・Zhong Shi") Since the balance of Yin and Yang in the body signifies health, then the destruction of balance naturally implies the onset of disease. Zhang Jingyue's "Lei Jing" from the Ming Dynasty believed that "a person's illness, whether on the surface or inside, whether cold or hot, whether affected by the five phases and six qi, or injured in the internal organs and meridians, are all due to the loss of relative balance between the two energies of Yin and Yang." Thirdly, the theory of health preservation in our country takes coordinating Yin and Yang as the most basic guiding principle. The "Su Wen・Sheng Qi Tong Tian Lun" mentions: "If Yin and Yang are not in harmony, then harmonize them, this is called the holy rule." What is meant by "holy rule" is essentially taking the coordination of Yin and Yang as the highest standard for health preservation and longevity. Guided by this principle, the practice of health preservation in our country has gradually formed a complete set of specific methods for coordinating Yin and Yang. Here are a few examples: Balanced Yin and Yang, nurturing spirit. This is what the "Huangdi Neijing" says: "Balanced Yin and secret Yang, the spirit will be governed." (from "Su Wen・Sheng Qi Tong Tian Lun"). Carefully harmonizing the five flavors, balancing Yin and Yang. In the eyes of ancient health preservationists, various foods, like Chinese medicine, have differences in the four natures of cold, heat, warm, and cool, and the five flavors of sour, bitter, sweet, spicy, and salty. If the nature and flavor of food are properly combined, it helps maintain the balance of Yin and Yang in the human body, thus benefiting health; conversely, if the combination of nature and flavor is inappropriate, it will disrupt the balance of the body, thereby damaging health. Adapting to nature, protecting and nurturing Yin and Yang. The "Huangdi Neijing" mentions: "Yin and Yang with the four seasons are the beginning and end of all things, the root of life and death. Going against them causes disasters, following them prevents diseases... Following Yin and Yang leads to life, going against them leads to death." This affirms that going with or against Yin and Yang will have a direct impact on the strength, weakness, longevity, and short life of the human body. Especially valuable is that the understanding of balance in Chinese health preservation theory is not an absolute stillness, but a special form of motion filled with contradictions. The "Su Wen・Liu Wei Zhi" proposed the view that "ascending, descending, entering, and exiting, there is no object without it," meaning that the balance of all things in the world, including healthy "normal people," is a relatively static state in the process of motion.
