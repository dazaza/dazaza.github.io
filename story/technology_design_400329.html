by anonymous on 2011-04-01 12:18:48

At the end of November 2010, a new men's clothing brand named "2AM" quietly appeared on the e-commerce market. However, its conspicuous "Cabbeen" logo on the online shopping platform still caught the attention of the industry, sparking discussions about the relationship between Cabbeen and "2AM."

As a leading brand in Chinese fashion menswear, every move by Cabbeen has been enough to attract industry attention. But compared to the previously rumored acquisition of Cabbeen by Anta, the launch of "2AM" could only be considered a small ripple. Early last year, rumors began circulating in the industry that Ding Shizhong, chairman of Anta's board, had acquired shares in Cabbeen for two hundred million yuan. The recruitment of Cabbeen specialty store managers by Anta in the southern region made the industry more certain of the authenticity of the acquisition.

In the eye of the storm, Yang Ziming, Chairman and Design Director of Cabbeen Clothing Development Co., Ltd., remained silent for a long time, making the relationship between Cabbeen and Anta even more mysterious.

**Partnering with Anta Distributors**

For years, rumors about Cabbeen seeking investors have been circulating in the industry. Last year, these rumors continued to intensify, and many insiders confidently told reporters that Ding Shizhong of Anta had already invested in Cabbeen. However, this information was ultimately denied by Yang Ziming. According to Yang Ziming, there is indeed a cooperative relationship between Anta's southern distributors and Cabbeen, but it is not the acquisition model widely rumored outside.

Reporter: In recent months, the industry has been abuzz with the rumor that Anta has taken a stake in Cabbeen, with specific details claiming that Anta spent two hundred million yuan. Is this true?

Yang Ziming: I know there are many rumors outside, but I've never thought about using the media to clarify this matter. Now I can clearly tell you that all the equity of Cabbeen belongs to me, and no one else is involved. However, I am very open to discussions with investors. In fact, there have been many investors who approached me before, including international giants like the LV Group. But I feel now is not the right time. As a fashion designer, I hope this brand can develop according to my vision. If other institutions can help promote the development of this brand through investment, I would be very willing to accept it.

Reporter: The industry is so confident that Anta has taken a stake in Cabbeen because Anta's southern distributors put out recruitment ads for Cabbeen specialty store managers. Can you explain this?

Yang Ziming: This is indeed true. The southern distributors of Anta also happen to be distributors for Cabbeen. This arrangement was finalized around Labor Day last year. Currently, the southern distributors of Anta are responsible for expanding the markets in Guangdong and Jiangsu provinces for Cabbeen. However, this does not mean that Anta has taken a stake in Cabbeen.

Reporter: How did you come to partner with Anta's southern distributors? Many menswear companies are currently experiencing intensified conflicts with their distributors. How do you effectively balance against such strong distributors without losing control?

Yang Ziming: Conflicts between distributors and headquarters arise when you cannot ensure their profitability. As long as you guarantee the interests of the distributors, they will not appear overly aggressive in front of you because it's a win-win situation. Strained relations benefit no one. Cabbeen is very strict when selecting distributors. First and foremost, they must share common values. Partnering with Anta's southern distributors is part of our strategy to further optimize distributor selection. Ding Shizhong and I are sworn brothers, and we maintain a very good relationship. Communication between us is smooth and effective.

Reporter: You mentioned channel optimization earlier. What does Cabbeen's current channel structure look like, and how will you optimize it?

Yang Ziming: Cabbeen's main operations are run by distributors, including provincial and regional distributors. We will continue with this structure, and the so-called optimization mainly focuses on improving the quality of stores. Cabbeen has become a leading brand in domestic fashion menswear, laying a solid foundation for entering core commercial areas in major cities. Currently, Cabbeen has over 800 retail outlets, and this number will definitely exceed 1000 this year, with more stores located in core commercial areas of key cities.

Additionally, Cabbeen currently operates three brands: casual, urban, and trendy. These brands have different positioning. The casual brand is the most commonly seen, the urban brand is slightly higher-end and suitable for business and negotiation scenarios, while the trendy brand is the highest-end and appropriate for banquets and fashionable occasions. It is also the main force behind our internationalization efforts. In the future, these three sub-brands will each have their own dedicated stores.

**Entering the International Market via the Buyer Model**

Through partnerships with Anta's southern distributors, Cabbeen has gained more confidence in its domestic expansion, allowing Yang Ziming to focus more energy on his internationalization strategy. Over the past seven years, Cabbeen has opened five specialty stores in places like New York and Paris. After seven years of testing the waters, Cabbeen's internationalization strategy is about to accelerate, with the buyer store model becoming the primary channel for entering the international market.

Reporter: You mentioned internationalization earlier, which is a dream for many menswear companies. Where does Cabbeen stand in this regard?

Yang Ziming: Seven years ago, Cabbeen opened a store in New York. Currently, we have flagship stores in New York, Chicago, and Paris, but we are still in the trial stage, with only four or five stores in total. However, Cabbeen's internationalization strategy will soon accelerate, with preliminary plans set for next year.

Reporter: You frequently appear at international fashion weeks. Is this partly to pave the way for internationalization?

Yang Ziming: Yes, the first fashion week I participated in was the New York Fashion Week. This fashion week differs from other international fashion weeks as it considers whether your work has commercial value and whether participating in the fashion week can generate business. This is the sole standard for evaluation.

When we went, we provided them with a lot of content about media coverage in China and global attention towards me. They didn't care about any of that; they were solely interested in the works I presented at the New York Fashion Week. They believe that showcasing your works here not only announces your brand but also holds buyers accountable. The primary audience at the New York Fashion Week consists of buyers, followed by the media. Therefore, the entire evaluation and assessment process is very strict, focusing on whether you have a market in New York, if you have a store in New York, if your works suit the American market, if you have a company in the U.S., and if you're prepared for orders. If you don't have a market in New York, they might reject your participation in the fashion week.

Reporter: Talking about internationalization is easy, but implementing it is difficult, facing challenges such as channel construction and brand building. How does Cabbeen overcome these difficulties?

Yang Ziming: Of course, internationalization isn't that simple. Even basic adjustments like fit modifications are necessary, so it's important to first clarify the positioning before proceeding. While we may attempt to open branded specialty stores internationally, we won't rush into expansion. Instead, I've chosen a faster route—mainly through international buyer stores. The reason I participate in the New York Fashion Week is due to its commercial nature. There are many international buyers at the fashion week, and if they like your works, they will place orders with you and sell your products in their stores. This model reduces costs and risks.

**Re-launching with "2AM"**

The expansion of domestic and international physical stores is just one point in Yang Ziming's strategic layout. Besides physical stores, he has also set his sights on the borderless online shopping market. The introduction of "2AM" marks the beginning of Cabbeen's full-scale entry into the online shopping market, and through "2AM," Cabbeen has completed its high-low strategic layout both online and offline.

Reporter: Besides the expansion of physical stores, e-commerce channels are becoming the next focal point of competition among menswear brands. Does Cabbeen have any plans in this area?

Yang Ziming: E-commerce is indeed a trend. Two years ago, our online sales ranked over 1200th in the menswear industry, but during this year's Singles' Day, we ranked ninth. However, the Cabbeen brand will not deliberately expand into the online shopping market. Online prices are often lower than in physical stores, which can hurt consumers' feelings and impact physical stores.

This does not mean Cabbeen will abandon the e-commerce market. Since last year, we have been considering the layout of the e-commerce market, from product design and image to logistics operations, with an initial investment exceeding 100 million yuan. However, we will not use the Cabbeen brand for online shopping. Instead, we have launched a completely new brand, "2AM," specifically targeting the online shopping market. This was done last month, and the name of this brand comes from the name of the show I held when I first received the title of Top Ten Chinese Designers in 2004.

Reporter: Even though the Cabbeen brand does not actively develop online shopping, there are still a large number of low-priced "Cabbeen" menswear being sold online. Are you not concerned that by not taking control, allowing a flood of low-priced "Cabbeen" menswear to exist, it will have an even greater impact on physical stores?

Yang Ziming: Genuine products will always remain genuine, and counterfeit ones will never be real. Consumers who truly like this brand can distinguish between what is real and what is fake. We make it clear that any Cabbeen menswear purchased outside the official mall is considered counterfeit. I believe that consumers who genuinely like the Cabbeen brand will still choose to purchase from our official mall, as evidenced by the sales figures during Singles' Day.

Of course, some dealers may sell Cabbeen menswear online. To prevent this, we have implemented technical arrangements. For example, each piece of Cabbeen menswear has a unique code, which the dealers themselves do not know. Once a garment appears online, we can scan it with a computer to determine which dealer it came from, and then we take action against that dealer. Not long ago, we removed one such dealer. We make it clear that any Cabbeen menswear purchased outside the official mall is considered counterfeit. I believe that consumers who genuinely like the Cabbeen brand will still choose to purchase from our official mall, as evidenced by the sales figures during Singles' Day.

Reporter: "2AM" is the online brand of Cabbeen's main brand. What are the differences in positioning between the two?

Yang Ziming: After the launch of "2AM," it formed a high-low combination with Cabbeen, both online and offline. Compared to Cabbeen, "2AM" is more casual, youthful, and lower in price. The focus of the Cabbeen brand is not on online shopping. Besides maintaining the pricing system, the more important reason is that the consumer preferences in the online and offline markets differ. As a designer, I understand that finding the market is actually about understanding human nature. To enter a market, you must know who your target consumers are, their educational background, and their lifestyle and work status. Only by deeply understanding the inner thoughts of your target consumers can you develop products they like. Launching a separate brand for online shopping is precisely to allow this online brand to focus more on studying the characteristics of online shoppers.
