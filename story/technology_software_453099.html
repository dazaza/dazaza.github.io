Hospital robots can sweat and shed tears, and may also die from taking wrong medicine(Photos)

by jyq2011668 on 2011-06-28 16:41:35

The simulated man can talk, sweat, and shed tears just like a real person. (Photo by Zhao Bin, our staff reporter)

According to our staff reporter Li Lin: "The patient has suffered cardiac arrest, his lips have turned blue, his eyes are closed, immediate rescue is required." Several doctors quickly approached and performed external chest compressions and artificial respiration on the "patient" as part of emergency measures. After about ten minutes, the "patient's" heartbeat gradually returned to normal.

This is no ordinary patient; his name is "simMan3G" - the third-generation simulated man. He recently made an appearance in Xi'an, primarily to assist with teaching at Xijing Hospital and clinical doctor training.

He is highly capable, able to sweat, shed tears, and even bleed.

Yesterday afternoon, at the Clinical Skills Training Center of Xijing Hospital's Medical Education Department, there was a foreign handsome man in his 30s, standing at 1.8 meters tall - "simMan3G," wearing a blue-and-white checkered hospital gown. Lying quietly on the bed, his eyes blinked occasionally. His heartbeat was weak, his breathing steady. The monitoring equipment beside him showed his vital signs: pulse 80, temperature 37°C, heart rate 80... Suddenly, his breathing became rapid, his heart stopped, and after "resuscitation," he gradually returned to normal. "The imported simMan3G is worth millions and is the most expensive and intelligent simulated man we have here. He can sweat, shed tears, produce nasal mucus, and even bleed. He can also speak Shaanxi dialect," said Yang Guitao, director of the Training Center. Speaking in the "Shaanxi dialect" and sweating are techniques controlled by doctors through a computer. According to the introduction, the "simMan3G" represents the highest level of international simulation medical education. Not only does he look like a real person, but he can also simulate various physiological and pathological characteristics of patients, generating corresponding physiological responses to the treatment operations performed by "doctors," displaying various physiological data on the monitors connected to him.

If given the wrong medicine, he will feel pain and may even "die."

"Any clinical case can be input into the computer, regardless of age. After entering the data, he can instantly develop heart disease or hypertension. If the doctor administers the wrong medication or makes mistakes in the rescue procedure, he will feel pain and may 'die.' Conversely, if treated appropriately, he will recover, just like a real patient," Yang Guitao explained. There is a chip-based smart recognition device under the robot's right arm that automatically identifies drug components, concentration, and dosage during medication administration. It then provides feedback based on the medication situation.

It is understood that there are over 700 simulated men "living" in the Clinical Skills Training Center, covering all possible diseases and surgeries you can imagine. Just for heart sounds alone, one simulated man can mimic up to 130 different types.
