The editor will first introduce to you what is collagen! There are 27 kinds of human collagens, and the types of collagen contained in different parts are also different. Skin collagen is mostly Type I and III, which is made up of three left-handed peptide chains twisted into a right-handed helix. Collagen is the main component of the dermis layer of human skin, accounting for more than 80%. Collagen forms a fine elastic net in the skin, locking in moisture and supporting the skin like a scaffold.

In the skin, collagen is the "spring" that determines the elasticity and firmness of the skin; it is also the "reservoir" that determines the water content and storage capacity of the skin. Collagen directly determines the hydration, smoothness, tightness, and "skin age" of the skin.

Collagen is an extracellular protein, consisting of three peptide chains twisted into a spiral fiber protein. Collagen is the most abundant protein in the human body, accounting for more than 30% of total body proteins. Collagen is rich in amino acids such as glycine, proline, and hydroxyproline required by the human body. Collagen is the most important component of the extracellular matrix. An adult's body contains about 3 kilograms of collagen, mainly found in the skin, bones, eyes, teeth, tendons, internal organs (including heart, stomach, intestines, blood vessels), etc. Its function is to maintain the shape and structure of the skin and tissues and organs, and it is also an important raw material for repairing damaged tissues. In the components of human skin, 70% are composed of collagen.
