Tencent and Guangdong Unicom Launch "WeChat Plan" on August 8th

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 15:57:04

The rumored collaboration between WeChat and Guangdong Unicom, dubbed as "good buddies," which surfaced yesterday, has been verified today. The timing was carefully chosen - at 8:08 AM, Tencent announced on its e-commerce platform YiXun the launch of the "WeChat Wo" package in collaboration with China Unicom. According to YiXun, the official launch date for this collaboration is August 8, 2013.

In the official introduction, it is stated that "WeChat Wo is more than just a SIM card." Users who choose the "WeChat Wo" package will be gifted additional data, enjoy exclusive WeChat privileges, and can even select Unicom premium numbers ending with "88."

Globally, there are many internet companies (referred to as "OTT enterprises" in China) that collaborate with telecom operators, such as Line's partnership with Docomo in Japan, Google Voice with Sprint in the US, and Spotify with Deutsche Telekom in Germany. However, "WeChat Wo" sets a precedent: allowing users to choose the form of service integration between the internet company and the telecom operator.

Tencent did not announce the specific details of the collaboration with the operator but instead allowed users to choose their desired "WeChat privileges." Among the available privileges are:

Group chat limit increased from 40 to 60 people

Additional data provided

Discounts on WeChat Pay

Free games

Free emoticons

Free music downloads

Free HD movie viewing

Seamless sharing of HD images

Easier visibility in the "People Nearby" feature (if desired)

Approximately 23,000 people have already participated in "customizing" WeChat privileges. Based on this model, the "WeChat Wo" package that Tencent and Guangdong Unicom will jointly launch will offer WeChat privileges based on popularity. It is expected that Tencent may not provide just one privilege but potentially multiple ones. Of course, users could also request all privileges.

The "WeChat Wo" package will be launched 22 days later on August 8. From today until August 4, users can "like" and choose their desired privileges on the YiXun page; from August 5 to 7, pre-orders will be accepted; and sales will begin on August 8.

According to previous reports from Phoenix New Media, WeChat might offer WeChat privileges on top of Guangdong Unicom's existing 36 yuan "Wo Pai" package targeted at students. However, it remains unclear whether the "WeChat package" is a small-scale trial or if it will be extended to more users after enthusiastic participation.

Last Monday, we exclusively reported that WeChat would collaborate with mainland operators to launch customized services. At that time, based on overseas examples, we speculated that the collaboration between WeChat and operators might involve data packages, exclusive services, or call discounts, though we were unsure about the depth of the cooperation. Currently, we still need to wait some time to learn what type of package they will base their collaboration on and whether they will introduce a standalone "WeChat package."

Given that the "WeChat Wo package" announcement was made through Tencent's e-commerce channel, YiXun, it indicates that there will be collaboration in specific business sales. The official statement mentions that "WeChat Wo" will issue physical SIM cards, leading us to believe that future buyers won't be limited to student users but will also include more loyal WeChat users. After all, WeChat data usage may already constitute a significant portion of their mobile data expenses.

To reiterate, as a user, I am pleased to see WeChat collaborating with telecom operators. While the privileged features may not be something I urgently need, bundling WeChat data into a single package is certainly something I require.

Finally, there is one detail worth discussing: among the selectable "WeChat privileges," there is an option for "free emoticons." Although the free emoticon screenshots from YiXun's official Weibo remain traditional, the mention of "free" implies the existence of paid emoticons. This suggests that the WeChat 5.0 version, which will feature paid emoticons, will not be released later than August 8. For further updates, everyone can continue digging for information.
