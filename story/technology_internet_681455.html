Insights into life

by songsdp82pm on 2012-02-28 13:42:54

Life Insights People often say, "Time is ruthless, life is like a dream." Yet I have always stubbornly and naively believed that time would wait for me... This Spring Festival, I returned to my hometown for the New Year celebrations, revisiting a place I had been away from for so long. With a heart brimming with curiosity and enthusiasm, I went to visit old relatives, close friends, and former classmates. The harsh reality struck me repeatedly, forcing me to awaken rationally and remind myself: reality is reality, not a beautiful dream. Reality truthfully told me, "Time is indeed cruel and merciless, and life truly resembles a dream..."

(I) Revisiting Hometown Sentiments

Returning home for the Spring Festival, my mother said to all of us siblings, "I miss the old courtyard in our hometown, and I also miss the old neighbors..." In fact, our feelings were the same as mother's; we always cherish emotions and yearn for the past.

Thus, we all unanimously responded, "Before the Spring Festival, let's go back to the old courtyard and visit those neighbors."

So, my younger brother drove us, carrying the entire family back to our long-lost hometown.

As soon as the car entered the old courtyard and hadn't even stopped properly, the old neighbors, hearing our arrival, enthusiastically gathered around to open the car doors for us and warmly welcomed us.

Each heartfelt embrace, every sincere and warm greeting, every pair of hands shaking warmly and closely, and each loud, cheerful laugh filled the air with deep homesickness! It was wonderful to be reunited with the familiar hometown, its genuine warmth, the laughter that had been absent for so long, and the familiar dialect.

The hometown I had been away from for many years has already transformed dramatically, with high-rise buildings everywhere, modern and fashionable. Although there are many unfamiliar yet somehow recognizable faces, you can still hear children asking who we are, evoking the sentiment: "Departing young and returning old, the accent unchanged but hair grayed. Children meeting me do not recognize, laughing ask where the guest comes from."

That long-absent dialect sounds so intimate! That deep homesickness feels so warm, so warm!

Tightly holding each other's hands, silently gazing at each other's faces, though words cannot fully express the depth of emotion, tears flow freely. At this moment, a heartfelt embrace dissolves us into the deep nostalgia, all unspoken, yet more meaningful than words...

Time may pass, appearances may change, but what remains constant is that deep homesickness. A song writes well: "These years, I've been to many places, but none feels closer than here, my hometown... Closest are the villagers, sweetest is the water of my hometown..." Yes, today I truly feel and understand this most genuine and deep emotion.

(II) Flower-like Smiles, Dream-like Memories

With excitement, I visited old classmates. Upon meeting, our eyes locked, filled with tears, and words were unnecessary. We embraced tightly again and again... Then, we attentively stared at each other, trying to find traces of the youthful, elegant figures we once knew. Our tear-filled eyes laughed until our faces were streaked with tears. Introducing our now taller children to each other, we shared a knowing smile and joked in unison, "We're old, we've all grown old..." Yet our tears remained crystal clear!

Female classmates' figures became fuller and more graceful, while male classmates' waists thickened but stood tall. Despite their genuine smiles, the marks of time couldn't be concealed.

We laughed freely, reminiscing about the innocent and amusing stories from our school days. As we talked, the children listened intently on the side. This feeling of being transported back in time was truly like a dream...

(III) Turning Back Brings Longing

During the days spent celebrating the Spring Festival in my hometown, I stayed by my mother's side every day, feeling how quickly time flew by. My eyes were constantly focused on the movements of my mother and all the family members. I wanted to eternally freeze these precious moments of reunion, reluctant to leave even for a second... In my heart, this intense happiness of family reunion felt full and content.

No matter how old I grow, no matter how aged my mother becomes, in her eyes, I will always be her beloved child.

Whenever I return home, I will always sleep with my mother, reliving the happy times of childhood. Listening to her tell old stories, I fall sound asleep with great satisfaction.

My mother's bed is the warmest, her stories the most fascinating, her laughter the sweetest, her snoring the most endearing, and her presence the most comforting!

Every day was filled with the joyous atmosphere of family reunions. Watching my mother, my younger brother and sister, and the children healthy, happy, and content, my heart was perpetually at peace.

Time flies, and before I knew it, the holiday was over, and I had to set off again...

Once again, I reluctantly packed my belongings, filled with the blessings and hopes of my family, and set sail again, carrying thoughts of my mother and loved ones!

As I descended the stairs, I dared not look back at my mother's eyes, filled with reluctance and tears, nor at her slightly raised hand waving goodbye.

Because, I knew that in that wave, our hearts were once again filled with the deepest sincerity, reluctance, and longing for each other!

(IV) A Dream Unwilling to Wake From

I once naively thought the world was like a glass of turbid water, clear, perfect, and sweet.

I once naively thought love and romance were all beautiful, dazzling, romantic, and warm.

Who quietly stole our youthful prime?

Who gently kissed and teased our shy, naive dreams?

Who made time so ruthlessly fleeting, letting storms shatter one pure and beautiful dream after another?

And who guided us through the endless cycles of the seasons, blooming and fading?

Unknowingly, upon sudden reflection, flowers are not flowers, mist is not mist, and this world has already changed beyond recognition, like a sea turned to fields. All the warmth and beauty of the past are gone...

Ah, only when I wake up do I realize that time is truly cruel and merciless. Life is like wine, intoxicating me in the bustling world, making me lose direction of my original pure aspirations...

Deep within, I question myself: Am I confused by this fashionable and prosperous world, or has my heart become numb, losing the desire to pursue beauty?

The changes in life make my heart feel helpless and lost. What exactly have we lost? Only by calming down and reflecting carefully can we find the answer: Time, like wine, has intoxicated both you and me...

Perhaps silence is the most resonant answer between hearts!

Because time is ruthless, life is like a dream. No matter how unwilling we are to face reality, none of us can hold back the steps of time, because none of us can escape it!
