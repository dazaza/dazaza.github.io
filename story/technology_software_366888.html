[Recommend] 5 Must-Have Firefox Extensions for Sync Control

by xdash on 2010-10-04 05:05:47

Weibo user @YY小楠 requested recommendations for some commonly-used Firefox services, preferably those that support cross-browser synchronization. Here, I will briefly introduce five excellent online services centered around Firefox (though supporting other browsers where possible), covering the main aspects of common online activities. Hopefully, this will be helpful to everyone.

Last Pass Password Manager (Supports Firefox, IE, Safari, Chrome)

Last Pass can encrypt and save passwords for your frequently-used online services. You no longer need to worry about remembering and managing numerous passwords; just remember Last Pass's password, and let it handle the rest. Last Pass also automatically generates and synchronizes a set of highly secure passwords across your different computers, preventing others from easily accessing important private data between your multiple devices. The basic version is free, while the premium version costs $1 per month and offers phone support.

Read It Later (Supports Firefox, IE, Chrome, Safari, iPhone)

Since discovering this tool, I have been using it consistently as a bookmark management solution. When you come across an interesting article but cannot read it immediately due to work or your surroundings, simply click "Read It Later," and the article will be automatically added to your unread list. Simple and lightweight, it reduces information anxiety and allows you to focus more on your current tasks. It supports cross-platform use and even offline reading.

Xmarks (Supports Firefox, IE, Chrome, Safari)

Widely regarded as the most powerful cross-browser synchronization tool, Xmarks can sync bookmarks, favorites, and user profile information. It was once the top choice, but unfortunately, Xmarks recently announced that it will discontinue its service (due to financial issues, facing closure). Enthusiastic users have organized an online vote to try to retain this service. If you're interested in it, feel free to cast your vote in support (deadline is the 15th of this month).

Evernote Web Clipper (Supports Firefox, IE, Chrome, Safari)

An Evernote plugin that allows you to sync and store great content (text, images, videos, etc.) discovered during browsing into Evernote. It automatically indexes, adds tags, and makes the content searchable for future reference. A must-have for Firefox + Evernote users.

Adblock Plus (Supports Firefox)

Filters out annoying ads, allowing you to "subscribe" only to clean pages after filtering. This can significantly enhance your internet browsing experience.
