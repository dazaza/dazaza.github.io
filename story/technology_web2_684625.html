Youth walks 340 km to save mother: Ms. Luo to undergo tumor removal surgery today

by lpoihjkmkk on 2012-03-01 09:25:10

Xiaoluo has liked to fix things since he was a child. When the alarm clock or mobile phone at home broke down, his family would give them to him for repair, but the results became worse and worse. "My father once told me that repairing cars can earn more money," Xiaoluo said sensibly. "I will graduate from junior high school in one semester," said Xiaoluo's sister Luo Qianping. "I also want to continue my studies." More than a month after dropping out of school automatically, Xiaoluo finally gave a definite answer. ● Bank: Bank of China Guangzhou Avenue Branch (the purpose must be noted: Luo Weike) Grandma gave Xiaoluo a bag, inside which there was a can of adult milk powder and a baby bottle, a telecom broadband prepared for Luo's mother, and an envelope with money for Xiaoluo's schooling. The elder encouraged Xiaoluo to be strong, "I also had cancer before, underwent chemotherapy, and now I am slowly getting better." Besides reporting on Xiaoluo's filial piety, the Express Newspaper also specially opened a love hotline for him. Yesterday, the hotline telephone did not stop ringing, and the reporter received hundreds of text messages and calls. Some readers called to inquire as early as 8 o'clock in the morning yesterday, and the farthest donation came from Heilongjiang, Inner Mongolia, and Xinjiang. As of 8:30 PM last night, the public welfare account announced by the hospital had received 160,176.66 yuan in loving donations for Luo Rujiao. Sister wants to read nursery teacher, Xiaoluo wants to go to technical school, he said: "I want to learn car repair!" New Express News: A 13-year-old boy, Luo Weike, walked 340 kilometers and came to Guangzhou to shine shoes, raising money to treat his seriously ill mother. His deeds have attracted the attention of enthusiastic people, and after our detailed report yesterday (see A8 edition on June 10), his filial piety moved more enthusiastic people. At around 2 PM yesterday afternoon, a gray-haired grandma walked into the ward where Xiaoluo's mother was staying, carrying a bag. Seeing Xiaoluo, the old woman's voice choked up, and she said to Xiaoluo: "No matter what, you must be strong! It's too hard for such a small person!"
