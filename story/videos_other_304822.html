Xu Hongtao's reputation ruined by match-fixing: calmly optimistic about the future of Chinese football

The anti-gambling and anti-match-fixing storm in Chinese football has uncovered another case of promotion-related match-fixing. Yesterday, public security authorities confirmed through a CCTV program that the 2007 away game between Chengdu Sheffield United and Qingdao Hailifeng was a fixed match. Former Hailifeng team leader Liu Hongwei confessed to police that in order to ensure Chengdu Sheffield United's promotion to the Chinese Super League (CSL), Xu Hongtao, chairman of the board at Chengdu Sheffield United, and vice president You Kewei bribed the Hailifeng team with 500,000 yuan.

To secure their promotion, Chengdu Sheffield United spent 500,000 yuan to fix the match. This game between Chengdu Sheffield United and Hailifeng was confirmed as the second domestic league match-fixing case after the Guangyao vs Shanxi Lutiger game. Unlike the gambling-related match-fixing orchestrated by Wang Pei and others in the previous case, this one involved Xu Hongtao and You Kewei taking risks to bribe the Hailifeng team to ensure Chengdu's promotion into the CSL.

According to police descriptions, on September 22, 2007, when Chengdu Sheffield United faced off against Qingdao Hailifeng, it was a critical moment for Chengdu's promotion efforts. To win the match, You Kewei and Xu Hongtao conspired to buy the game. After arriving in Qingdao, You Kewei met secretly with then Hailifeng team leader Liu Hongwei to plot the match-fixing. In the end, Chengdu offered 300,000 yuan in cash and free winter training at their facility for one month (both parties agreed this condition was "worth" 200,000 yuan) in exchange for Hailifeng to "ease up" during the game. Ultimately, Chengdu Sheffield United won the match 2-0.

Police worked tirelessly for six days and nights to capture Liu Hongwei. Police disclosed that those involved in this case had deep ties and the case dated back many years, making the arrest and evidence collection very difficult. One of the suspects, Liu Hongwei, fled upon hearing rumors but was eventually captured after six days and nights of police surveillance.

After detaining You Kewei, Xu Hongtao, and Liu Hongwei, the police stated that their manipulation of match results through commercial bribery violated Articles 163 and 164 of the Criminal Law and were suspected of criminal activity. They would continue to interrogate them.

In yesterday's CCTV Focus Talk program, the three main individuals involved in the fake match between Chengdu Sheffield United and Qingdao Hailifeng showed three different expressions. They all expressed regret for their roles in the match-fixing, but held differing views on the future of Chinese football.

A Sigh

Liu Hongwei: Low level, couldn't even let goals in.

"The level of Chinese football is too low; we couldn't even let goals in," said former Hailifeng team leader Liu Hongwei in yesterday's program, revealing details of the match-fixing. Although Hailifeng fielded several young players in this game, Chengdu Sheffield United still couldn't score in the first half. During halftime, Chengdu Sheffield United vice president You Kewei had to call Liu Hongwei, who reassured him, "Don't worry, we'll let you win."

Regarding this fixed match, Liu Hongwei sighed deeply, believing that the level of Chinese football was so low that they couldn't even properly fix a game. When asked by the TV interviewer if insiders could not tell, Liu Hongwei responded nonchalantly, "Insiders might be able to see it, but such things happen too often in Chinese football."

A Cry

You Kewei: Fixing matches was not my intention.

As the first exposed protagonist in this match-fixing case, former Chengdu Sheffield United vice president You Kewei tearfully expressed his remorse on TV, "(Fixing matches) was not my intention, I'm sorry!"

Although known for his involvement in gambling and match-fixing in the Chinese football circle, You Kewei appeared heartbroken before the camera, saying, "I myself am an athlete, but the club requested..." In You Kewei's view, he felt somewhat compelled to participate in match-fixing, "It really wasn't my intention!"

Calm Composure

Xu Hongtao: Optimistic about the future of Chinese football.

Xu Hongtao is undoubtedly the most significant figure in this match-fixing case and also the heaviest-weight individual since the anti-gambling storm began in Chinese football. When interviewed, Xu Hongtao reflected on his journey from initially "upholding principles" to later "going along with the corruption." Facing the camera, Xu Hongtao remained calm. Regarding the current anti-gambling storm in Chinese football, Xu Hongtao shared his thoughts: "Chinese football is like a critically ill patient who needs nourishing medicine. This anti-gambling campaign is like undergoing surgery to make the patient better!" Therefore, Xu Hongtao stated that, at least currently, he is optimistic about the future of Chinese football.

"During that 2007 game, it was a critical moment for our promotion. For safety's sake, we entertained the idea of buying the match," Xu Hongtao said. He admitted that he bears undeniable responsibility for the "match-buying" incident, regretting being overly eager for quick success.

From Legendary Agent to Chairman of Chengdu Football Club

Xu Hongtao's Reputation Ruined by Match-Fixing

In the latest match-fixing case announced by the public security authorities, Xu Hongtao, chairman of Chengdu Sheffield United, is not only a key figure in this case but also the most significant person since the anti-gambling storm began in Chinese football.

Previously, Xu Hongtao was considered the most legendary agent in Chinese football, orchestrating the overseas careers of numerous Chinese players. Notably, Hao Haidong, Li Tie, and Sun Jihai's moves to England were masterminded by him. At the end of 2005, Xu Hongtao arrived in Chengdu as a representative of the English Championship team Sheffield United to negotiate the acquisition of Chengdu Wuniu. In 2006, Xu Hongtao officially took over the Chengdu club and participated in that year's China League Two (CL2). In 2007, the team competed under the name "Chengdu Sheffield United" and successfully promoted to the CSL.

Even while hiding in Shenzhen, Xu Hongtao was ultimately apprehended by the police.

The detention of chairman Xu Hongtao has cast a shadow over Chengdu Sheffield United's prospects. As the only club in the CSL with shares owned by a local football association, the Chengdu Football Association formulated corresponding strategies yesterday. It is reported that Chengdu Football Association Chairman Gu Jianming will temporarily assume the role of chairman of Chengdu Sheffield United and will travel to Hong Kong and meet representatives of the English Sheffield United to discuss further actions.

Speaking about the attitude of their joint venture partner, the English Premier League Sheffield United Club towards Xu Hongtao's involvement, Gu Jianming said, "They are actively supportive of the anti-gambling campaign, and our cooperation will certainly continue."

Following the Guangzhou Pharmaceutical Club, Chengdu Sheffield United became the second team embroiled in the "match-fixing scandal." According to relevant regulations of the Football Association, both clubs, which successfully promoted to the CSL in 2007, are likely to face the harshest punishment of demotion ordered by the Chinese Football Association. Based on the Guangyao Club incident, Chengdu Sheffield United is more likely to be penalized points.

Nanyong: It's these people who corrupted the kids.

Yesterday, while CCTV News Channel broadcasted the latest news on the anti-gambling campaign, Vice President of the Chinese Football Association Nanyong watched the entire report in the CFA office building. According to CCTV reporters, Nanyong attentively followed the latest developments in the anti-gambling campaign. In the TV news, a suspect incited players to gamble, saying, "Don't listen to the club." Nanyong was furious and rebuked, "It's these people who have corrupted the kids."

Chengdu Fans: Chinese football has ruined Xu Hongtao.

After the exposure of the match-fixing case between Chengdu Sheffield United and Qingdao Hailifeng, fans from both cities were shocked. Chengdu fans were particularly astonished by the arrest of club chairman Xu Hongtao. The head of the Chengdu fan organization "Red Knives," Chen Tao, angrily stated, "It's Chinese football that has ruined Xu Hongtao!" According to Chengdu media, Chengdu fans generally have a good impression of Xu Hongtao, especially his previous promise not to engage in match-fixing, which made Chengdu fans admire him greatly.
