Spending 40 minutes on a stationary bike is most suitable for losing weight.

by anonymous on 2012-03-06 13:57:40

In recent years, the power bikes originally used for sports scientific research have been transformed into more and more effective and stylish exercise bikes, entering the homes of ordinary people.

The exercise bike looks simple, but to truly achieve the exercise effect, some methods need to be mastered:

Firstly, adjust the appropriate seat height. The method is: when stepping down to the lowest point, the knee joint should not be fully straightened; when stepping up to the highest point, the knee joint should not bend less than 90 degrees.

For different groups of people, according to different exercise goals, there are different cycling exercise methods:

For those aiming at weight loss, they can do aerobic exercise cycling. That is, adjust the resistance load to feel comfortable, ride for more than 30 minutes, about 40 minutes is most suitable, and the heart rate reaches 100-110 beats per minute. This exercise intensity is moderate, and only after 30 minutes can fat decompose to provide energy for exercise. This kind of exercise is also a good way to improve cardiorespiratory function.

For those who aim to strengthen muscles, they can choose high-intensity, short-time cycling. Choose a resistance that feels easy, first warm up with slow cycling for 5 minutes, then try to cycle at high speed for about 5 minutes, with the heart rate reaching 170-180 beats per minute, then gradually decelerate for 5-10 minutes, restoring the heart rate to within 100 beats per minute. This is one set, you can do several sets, and arrange 3-5 minutes of rest between sets. Or first increase the resistance load, then gradually reduce the load to restore the heart rate. This method strongly stimulates the lower limb muscles and is suitable for young people.

For the elderly or those with knee injuries, it is important to be realistic. The elderly can choose lower resistance, and when riding, they should feel relaxed and effortless, breathe naturally, and avoid holding their breath to prevent increasing the burden on the heart. However, a certain intensity must also be reached. We often use the method of "170-age" to control exercise intensity. For a 60-year-old person, the heart rate during exercise must reach 110 beats per minute to show results. When riding, you can alternate between fast and slow speeds, and there must be a slow-speed warm-up and finishing process before stopping. The length of exercise time can be self-controlled.
