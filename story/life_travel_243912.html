Learn Silence

Sometimes, when you are misunderstood by others and you don't want to argue, so you choose silence. Not everyone needs to understand you, so you think there's no need to shout to the whole world. But sometimes, when you're misunderstood by the one you love most, you're too sad to argue and can only choose silence. The whole world may not understand you, but he should. If he can't even understand, what is there left to say? Life often has moments where even Schubert would be speechless. After all, not every right or wrong can be listed clearly, and there may not even be a true right or wrong. So, if you don't want to talk, then don't. In times when talking more won't help, perhaps silence is the best explanation.

At Least Stay Calm

When you fall into the lowest point in life, everyone around you tells you: be strong, and be happy. Being strong is absolutely necessary, but happy? In such a situation, it might be too hard for you. After all, who can feel happy when they've fallen and hurt themselves? But at least you can stay calm. Calmly look at this matter, calmly handle other things that need to be dealt with. Calmness doesn't mean happiness, nor does it mean unhappiness.

Learn to Bend Down, This Will Be My Unexpected Gain

When you have disagreements with others, or even verbal conflicts, so you're sulking because you think it's all due to their ill intentions. Don't dwell on it anymore, go home and mop the floor instead. Grab a cloth, bend down, kneel on both knees, and thoroughly clean every corner of the floor in front of you. Then rethink every word you said during that conflict. Now, do you find that you were also wrong in some ways? Are you gradually becoming more calm and composed? Sometimes you must learn to bend down, because this action can make you humble. While laboring your body, you also brighten your own mood. And, you also end up with a spotless floor. That's your second gain.

Don't Think "What If"

You say, life is a long road with countless forks, constantly making choices. If the choice is just between eating noodles or fried rice, the impact seems small, but choosing a major, a job, whether to marry or not, whether to have children or not, each choice has profound impacts, and different choices will inevitably lead to completely different lives. You also say, the unbearable part of life is that there's no chance to start over. If only...then...this kind of wistful muttering, it's better not to say anymore. Each choice at the fork in the road actually has no real good or bad. As long as you see life as your unique creation, you won't keep looking back on what would have happened if you had made different choices.

Work Hard, Regardless of Success or Failure, At Least It Was Once Beautiful

Strolling through the forest, you see a vine clinging to a tree trunk, softness intertwined with strength. You're moved by this serene beauty. Let happiness and belonging linger here, you think. What storms might come in the future? Perhaps the vine will break, the tree will fall, perhaps the sky will become barren, the earth will grow old. You think again. Then, please let time pause at this moment. A pause is eternal. If eternity contains this serene moment, then any calamities that may come in the future have already been consoled and rewarded.

Stay Simple

Because you overthink, you often complicate your life. Clearly living in the present, yet you always reminisce about the past and worry about the future; insisting on carrying the past, future, and present together, your life naturally becomes messy. Simplicity is a state of grace. Simply feeling the weather changes with your skin, simply tasting the fresh grass scent after rain with your nose, simply seeing distant and nearby mountains like a painting with your eyes. Simply live in the moment. In the moment, there is no need to worry about right or wrong, truth or falsehood. Since there is no right or wrong, truth or falsehood, isn't it like dreaming? Yes, just simply treat your life as a dream and carry it out.

Occasionally Be “Vulgar”...

After consuming too much healthy food, occasionally you also want to nibble on duck tongues and salted chicken. After watching too many masterpieces, occasionally you also want to glance at endless slapstick and tearjerker TV series. After listening to too much classical music, occasionally you also want to sing songs about loving someone for a hundred years and hating them for ten thousand. You know healthy food is good for digestion, masterpieces help cultivate temperament, and classical music elevates the soul, but occasionally you actually don't want to live so purposefully all the time. Life doesn't need to be bound so tightly. Occasionally indulging a little is moral. Being full of spirit may make you closer to sages, but being occasionally vulgar makes you more approachable.

Control Your Emotions, Don't Waste Them~

Today, you're unhappy because someone hurt you with words. You don't like arguing, so you leave; but you just left the place, not the situation where you were hurt by that person, so the more you think about it, the angrier you get. The angrier you get, the less energy you have to deal with other important matters, many events that require more attention and thought are neglected, ignored, and skipped in your overwhelming distress. Because you're solely focused on being angry. Writing off emotions is a waste of yourself, and it's a very bad waste. After all, anger takes effort, and anger definitely harms your vital energy. So, smart as you are, don't let emotions control you. When you're about to get angry, gently remind yourself: "Don't waste it."

Seize the Best Opportunity, Never Miss It

You once bought a piece of clothing you liked but didn't dare to wear, solemnly enshrined in your wardrobe; after a while, when you saw it again, you found it was already out of fashion. So, you missed it. You also once bought a beautiful cake but didn't dare to eat it, solemnly enshrined in the refrigerator; after a while, when you saw it again, you found it had already expired. So, you also missed it. Clothes not worn when they were most loved, cakes not tasted when they were most delicious, just like things not done when you most wanted to do them, are all regrets. Life also has an expiration date, things you want to do should be done as soon as possible. If you just solemnly enshrine your wishes in your heart without ever implementing them, the only result will be missing them, just like that out-of-fashion clothing, just like that expired cake.

Occasional Departure from the Track

Once you took a train intending to go to destination A, but midway you suddenly decided to get off at B. Maybe it was the charming name that attracted you, or maybe it was a fleeting view that touched you, anyway, you changed your original plan and experienced an unexpected trip full of surprises. A was your original goal, but B gave you a little adventure. Looking back, you say, that was an unforgettable experience of going off-track. Many times in life are like this. Focusing solely on one goal is just fulfilling the original journey; leaving the predetermined track gives you the chance to discover other scenery.

Quietly, Quietly Return to Peace..

There was a time when you were feeling low, even too lazy to pull open the curtains and look at the sunshine outside. Therefore, you naturally forgot to take care of the Marguerite flower on the windowsill that needed water every day. This went on for an unknown period until one day, you got over your low mood and remembered your Marguerite. Goodness me, poor flower, is she still alive? Trembling, you pulled the curtain open, only to see her swaying in the wind, her flowers blooming cheerfully. It turns out that during this period, although you forgot to water her, heaven didn't forget to nourish her with rain. Many things quietly happen beyond your sight and arrange themselves accordingly. Heaven creates all things and nurtures all things; everything is actually unnecessary to worry about... All you need to do is to be the best version of yourself and leave no regrets... That's enough.
