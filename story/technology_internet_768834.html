WeChat Pay vs Alipay - Where is the core of the war?

by anonymous on 2013-11-25 11:24:08

WeChat Pay has emerged, and the media is fiercely reporting and hyping its threat to Alipay. Jack Ma also feels that Pony Ma is kicking his butt; a payment war is quietly and intensely happening between Alibaba and Tencent.

Tencent's Losses and Wins

Pony Ma’s Tencent adopted the usual strategy of following first, then striking in the online shopping market. Unfortunately, Paipai failed to copy Taobao well. Tenpay was only able to survive its toughest years thanks to the strong demand for Tencent Coins. The allure of e-commerce has been something that Pony Ma has never been able to resist. After Paipai and QQ Mall, Tencent acquired YiXun.com, which started with 3C products, and fully promoted YiXun's development. Currently, YiXun has become an e-commerce site with enough strength to compete long-term with Tmall, JD.com, and Suning.

On the PC end, Tencent lags far behind Alipay in the e-commerce payment sector. However, Tencent has Tencent Coins and a large online gaming platform, so the scale of virtual currency payments is very large. Therefore, Tencent has not completely lost in the online payment sector. The number of users within the Tencent ecosystem who have payment habits should exceed hundreds of millions. Although it has lost in e-commerce payments, Tencent lacks not ability but a new scene where payment experience is convenient and user scale is large. Thus, after Zhang Xiaolong led WeChat to carve out a mobile empire, Tencent's strategic opportunity for flipping the e-commerce consumer payment sector arrived.

WeChat Pay is sufficiently convenient in terms of experience. After testing simple mobile games and some e-commerce businesses, WeChat probably has a promising plan for payment data. Guiding user consumption in immersive mini-game scenarios is what Tencent excels at. In my view, there are parts of WeChat Pay that are exaggerated. If applied to the e-commerce field, WeChat Pay might ultimately only open interfaces to its own siblings like YiXun. E-commerce payment is just part of shopping; subsequent services and after-sales processes still exist. While these can be connected via APIs to various e-commerce websites, this easily allows strong marketing harassment into the WeChat ecosystem. Non-Tencent e-commerce platforms can realize many service conveniences through public accounts on the WeChat platform, but the door to WeChat Pay may be closed to them.

Alipay's Dangers and Opportunities

In the past year, Alipay has been almost crazily promoting its Alipay Wallet. The Alibaba Group launched a series of mobile-end tools such as Weitao, QianNiu, and Laiwang, seizing the market position of mobile payments being an important defensive battle for Alibaba. Alibaba understands the position of Alipay in its strategy. Once Alipay falls back, Alibaba will retreat step by step.

WeChat Pay is one of the few products in recent years that makes Alibaba nervous. With hundreds of millions of users and extremely high activity levels, WeChat has replaced SMS functions. This level of popularity even surpasses the degree of Alipay's popularity on the PC end. Once WeChat finds the best strategy for the large-scale application of WeChat Pay, Alipay will easily be marginalized, and this is not an exaggeration.

At the same time, Alipay also has its own world - wealth management. The field of wealth management requires professional depth and accumulation, and WeChat will find it difficult to form direct competitiveness in this area in a short time. Alipay Wallet has already strengthened credit card bill management functions. Among young people who overconsume, paying credit cards on time is a strong demand. Although Alipay's current wealth management is relatively primitive and cannot compare with foreign counterparts like Mint, as banking technology progresses and opens up, various APIs may be released, offering limitless prospects for Alipay.

WeChat Pay vs Alipay - What is the Core of the War?

Many people know there is a threat, but where does the threat lie? Is it really just that one payment? I don't think so. The most terrifying aspect of WeChat Pay lies in its scenes. WeChat itself is a high-frequency application, while Alipay is relatively low-frequency. The use habit of mobile payment is the core battle of the payment war. Whoever captures and cultivates the habit of mobile payment first will be the victor.

Careful netizens may have noticed that Alipay is using its PC-end advantages to induce users to use Alipay Wallet, while WeChat tests the experience and effect of WeChat Pay through small embedded games and YiXun promotions during Double Eleven. Another focus of the war is Zhang Xiaolong's thoughts on WeChat's monetization methods: is it mainly through game value-added earnings, which Tencent excels at, or is it heavier backend e-commerce earnings, or O2O-style merchant cooperation models? Of course, these several models may coexist for a long time. Whichever becomes the focus in the next year will determine the direction and user habits of WeChat Pay.

The competition mode in the mobile payment sector is similar to other application sectors and also has a window period. My prediction is one year. After one year, users will make their own judgments and become set. Whether it is Alipay Wallet or WeChat Pay, how they open interfaces to the ecosystem and form a closed-loop ecosystem like on the PC end will be another highlight of the war.
