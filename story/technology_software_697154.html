Other exhibitors attending from Iran mainly came from Croatia, Italy, UAE, India, Poland, Vietnam, etc. Market introduction The UAE is a country rich in oil production, with its national income mainly coming from oil extraction. Due to the lack of natural resources, apart from oil and natural gas, other products ranging from industrial raw materials, equipment to civilian living items all rely on imports! The UAE ranks first among Arab countries in terms of imports. As the re-export trade center of the Middle East region with an import value of 41.3 billion US dollars, international credit rating agencies give the UAE an A2 grade in national loan strength. The long-term economic diversification policy of the country has yielded abundant fruits. Re-export trade has made significant contributions to the gross national product of the entire country, bringing in nearly 1.5 billion US dollars in revenue. Around 40% of the imported products in the UAE are re-exported to other regions in the Middle East, Africa, and Eastern European countries. The market radiates nearly 30 countries with a total population of 1.5 billion, making it the largest commodity distribution center for various products entering these countries and regions. In 2003, the UAE's GDP reached 273 billion dirhams (approximately 74.6 billion US dollars), growing by 4.7% compared to 27.06 billion dirhams in 2002, with per capita national income approaching 20,000 US dollars, making it one of the richest countries in the world. Sino-UAE trade is developing rapidly. In 1985, the trade volume between China and the UAE was 63 million US dollars. In 2003, the total import and export trade volume between the UAE and China reached 5.8 billion US dollars. In 2004, it even reached a record-breaking 8.146 billion US dollars, increasing by 40.2% year-on-year; with exports reaching 6.842 billion US dollars, increasing by 35.8% year-on-year.
