What is B2B? What is B2C?

B2B is a model of e-commerce, which is the abbreviation of Business-to-Business in English. It refers to business-to-business or inter-enterprise e-commerce, meaning the exchange of products, services, and information between enterprises over the Internet.

In B2B, both Bs stand for Business, while "2" is a homophone of the English word "two," representing "to." Therefore, we generally pronounce B2B as "B-to-B" following the English pronunciation instead of pronouncing "2" in Chinese. However, many people are now accustomed to pronouncing "B2B" using the Chinese pronunciation when discussing e-commerce without causing misunderstandings. Hence, no matter how it's pronounced, it is usually understandable.

B2C (Business To Customer) is one type of e-commerce classified by transaction objects, indicating the e-commerce conducted between commercial institutions and consumers. This form of e-commerce is mainly dominated by online retailing, conducting online sales activities via the Internet. B2C is the earliest form of e-commerce generated in our country, marked by the official operation of the 8848 online mall. In B2C, enterprises provide consumers with a new shopping environment - an online store through the Internet, where consumers can shop and pay online. This model saves time and space for both customers and enterprises, greatly improving transaction efficiency, especially beneficial for busy office workers who can save precious time with this mode. However, the characteristics of goods sold online are also very evident, being limited to some special items such as books, audio-visual products, digital products, flowers, toys, etc. These goods have lower requirements for visual, auditory, tactile, and olfactory experiences of buyers, whereas goods like clothing, audio equipment, and perfumes that require specific sensory experiences from consumers are not suitable for online sales. Of course, there are exceptions where some consumers decide to purchase a certain brand or model without needing to experience it on-site, but such consumers are few, especially in our country where the product quality management system is not yet perfect, and people prefer to rely on their own experiences to decide whether to make a purchase. Therefore, so far, successful enterprises in the B2C market, such as dangdang and Joyo, sell special goods. Currently, the payment method for B2C e-commerce combines cash-on-delivery with online payments, and most enterprises choose logistics outsourcing to reduce operating costs. With the change in consumer habits and the promotion effect of excellent enterprises, the number of online shoppers has rapidly increased, reaching 5.4% of all Internet users in 2004. The scale of China's B2C e-commerce market reached 4.5 billion yuan by the end of 2004, indicating that this business model has basically matured in our country.

The B2B e-commerce model includes two basic types: one is direct e-commerce between enterprises (such as manufacturers' online procurement and online supply), and the other is business activities conducted through third-party e-commerce websites. For example, Alibaba (china.alibaba.com), a well-known domestic e-commerce website, is a B2B e-commerce platform where various enterprises can conduct B2B transactions through Alibaba, such as publishing and querying supply and demand information, engaging in online communication and business negotiations with potential customers/suppliers, etc.

Other abbreviations related to e-commerce include B2C (business-to-consumer), C2C (consumer-to-consumer), G2B (government-to-business), etc.

Traditional inter-enterprise transactions often consume a large amount of enterprise resources and time, whether in sales and distribution or procurement, occupying product costs. Through B2B transaction methods, both buyers and sellers can complete the entire business process online, from forming the initial impression, comparing multiple options, negotiating prices, signing contracts, and delivering goods, to providing customer service.

B2B enables enterprises to reduce many procedural workflows and management expenses in inter-enterprise transactions, lowering operating costs. The convenience and extensibility of the network expand the scope of enterprise activities, making it easier and more cost-effective for enterprises to develop across regions and internationally.
