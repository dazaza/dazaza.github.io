Helpless thoughts...

The flowers fall silently but people have feelings, sitting alone in the twilight of loneliness.

These days, I've always been swayed by my thoughts, shuttling back and forth between the worlds of exuberance and tranquility. Many times, I'm at a loss and even become somewhat distant from myself. This has been going on for quite a while. The days of silent sorrow seem to have become ordinary, and in the absence of emotional ripples, I feel as though something is missing. I find problems to think about when there are none, leaving my heart restless for a long time.

I sought out a friend to tune my strings, only to be told by my friend that this might be the last time they'd help me tune them. Upon inquiry, I learned that my friend was about to stop studying. I felt it was sudden, yet such unexpected events occur most frequently in my life. This time, he tuned very seriously.

I am reluctant to part with my friend.

I can't forget the days we spent together, with tears accompanied by smiles and indelible wounds. I always remember those years, when we hustled for life, quarreled over trivial matters, and became indifferent to each other's interests. Now, when we meet again, we smile and pass by each other. I really don't know why I care so much about joy and sorrow, reunion and separation. For me, if there were no complicated thoughts, it would be the strangest thing.

I always think, if I could hold onto all of life, how good that would be. In that case, there would be no joy and sorrow, no reunions and separations, and thus no shadow of sadness. Such thoughts are truly laughable. Imagine if there were no joy and sorrow, no reunions and separations, then there would be no final moments of touching emotion; without shadows, there would be no fleeting moments of longing. Fate always plays tricks on us, letting dawn slip away early. I wanted to embrace the warmth of early spring, but ended up with the changing seasons. Thus, I am truly helpless.

It started raining again tonight, with lightning and thunder outside.

Many days have passed in the rain. I don't particularly love rainy days, and just like now, I can't sleep. I wanted to stay quietly through the night, but the relentless thunder makes it impossible. The atmosphere on rainy days feels somewhat deadened, and rainy nights even more so. I try hard to recall everything from the past, hoping to find some solace. But instead, my mind becomes a jumble, leaving me utterly helpless. Fortunately, there are still some memories lying quietly in my heart, even as heavy rain pours outside. There was once sincere love, which I failed to grasp properly. Isn't it a heart-wrenching pain for me to miss out on love despite its extraordinary nature?

What exactly allows me to understand life? Love and hate? Joy and sorrow?

They are the ones who have left, the things that have faded. They are the rainy days, cold nights, and lonely hearts. Have I grown up again? These changes feel more like a trade-off: gaining the fruits that belong to me, but losing the beautiful flowers of the past. I dare not make a final reckoning of them, fearing that I won't be able to accept the gains and losses, and not knowing where to go in the hurried years ahead. The rain continues to pour heavily. I feel that each raindrop is a thought in my heart, suddenly cut off and difficult to gather. I try hard to understand the meaning of the rainy days, but end up with questions that cannot be foreseen. I start to fear rainy days more than sleepless nights. Helplessly, I must watch the rain continue to fall and listen to the incessant thunder. I must also sort through these inherently complex and disordered thoughts. However, it seems I may never be able to fully sort them out.
