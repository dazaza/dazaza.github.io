Nail Decoration

&；nbsp；

Firstly, remove the old nail polish. Dip cotton in nail polish remover and wipe from the base of the nail to the tip several times to thoroughly remove it. Then use nail tools to shape the nails according to Wuhu Nail Training [http://www.whainisi.org]. Afterward, soak your hands in soapy water to soften the cuticles. Use a towel to carefully clean the fingers and gaps between them. If necessary, use a cuticle clipper and small tweezers to remove the inner spines and uneven dead skin at the base of the nails.

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；

When applying nail polish, start with a base coat. Apply oil or cream around and at the base of the nails, and then apply a protective layer on the nails. Shake the nail polish before application to ensure even color mixing inside the bottle.

&；nbsp；

Nail polish colors include rose red, bright red, flesh tone, bean paste color, silver, etc. The most popular nail polish nowadays is semi-transparent pink and beige, with light-colored nail polishes being the current trend. Nail polish can be freely mixed, so you don't need to buy a large number of different colors to match your clothing. Just buy a few main colors and mix them using overlapping or zonal coloring methods to achieve unexpected effects.

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；

For overlapping coloring, you can first apply a high-intensity glossy color, then overlay it with a pearl-like space color to create another color effect.

&；nbsp；

For zonal coloring, you can apply contrasting colors at the tips of the nails or divide the ten nails into two sections, using two or more colors to alternate contrast or coordinated colors.

&；nbsp；

French manicures involve painting a different color on the moon-shaped area at the top of the nail. For example, if the base color is white, you can paint the moon-shaped area with a bright contrasting color such as red or black; or you can paint soft orange, baby yellow, powder blue, or powder pink on a white base. Nail dyeing forms can be divided into full-style and crescent-style.

Careful Application

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； When applying nail polish, avoid letting the brush bristles carry too much polish. Gently wipe the brush on the bottle opening and adjust the bristles smoothly. Pay attention to the following points when applying nail polish:

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； Usually, start by painting from the middle of the nail root forward, then along the sides of the nail. That is, brush three times evenly in order of central, left, and right to prevent the nail polish from peeling off quickly.

&；nbsp；

To increase the durability of the nail polish, you can first apply a layer at the front end of the nail, then apply it over the entire nail. Alternatively, apply the polish in two batches: the first batch should be less to dry easily, and the second batch should be more. After applying, use a cotton swab to clean the oil stains around the nails.

Nail Care

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； Regardless of what kind of nail polish, if used for a long time, the nails cannot breathe and are prone to turn yellow. Therefore, it's best to apply a layer of moisturizing oil before and after removing the nail polish. This product, similar to transparent nail polish, is applied in the same way and has the function of preventing pigment deposition.
