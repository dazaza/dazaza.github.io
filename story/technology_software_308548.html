Villa Finally Breaks the 'Nightmare Theatre' Embarrassing Record, Temporary Worker of Red Devils Becomes the Most Unlucky Person

By Reporter Ke Han - Chelsea's draw against Everton gave Manchester United hope to catch up in points before Christmas; however, an unexpected home loss to Villa widened the gap between Red Devils and Blue Army to 3 points. This was also Manchester United's first league loss at home since March this year.

For Villa, the significance of this victory cannot be overstated: This 'Theatre of Dreams' has been Villa's real 'Nightmare Theatre'. For 26 years, they have never taken away 3 points from here; but this time, they celebrated their 100th away win in the Premier League in the best way possible. What does 26 years mean? Back then, not only had the Premier League not started, even Ferguson was still struggling in Aberdeen! Villa boss O'Neill hadn't retired from Notts County yet, and Abou Diaby (born in 1986), who scored the only goal in this match, wasn't even a fertilized egg 26 years ago!

The Unlucky Temporary Worker

Previously, Villa had been unbeaten in 27 league matches against Manchester United (20 losses and 7 draws), which was the longest suppressed record in the Premier League. Historically, it was second only to Tottenham's 32-game league unbeaten streak against Chelsea from 1990-2006. But all nightmares disappeared this night.

Ashdown, the reporter for The World News, titled his post-match article with a bit of schadenfreude - "Ferguson's wings broken by Angel Gabriel (referring to Abou Diaby)." This gentleman wrote a critical report about Manchester United last week and was therefore considered as an 'unwelcome person' and expelled from the Red Devils' base. However, this title did indeed point out the crux of Manchester United's loss: insufficient attention to the opponent's two-wing crosses.

Villa is most adept at crossing from both flanks (8 goals from corner kick combinations, highest in the entire Premier League), but Manchester United used a temporary worker, Fletcher, as the right-back. Despite a more battered Manchester United defense holding up well in the midweek Champions League game, Villa's strength was certainly not inferior to Wolfsburg's, and their tactics were targeted. Moreover, Villa deserved some luck: Last season, they led Manchester United until the final moments but unfortunately got overturned by Ronaldo and Macheda's consecutive goals.

At the 21st minute, Fletcher's defense clearly had issues: Abou Diaby passed to Ashley Young on the left flank. Fletcher obviously lacked positional sense, initially turning around to help Brown double-team Abou Diaby, then realizing after a second that he was the right-back and hurriedly turned again to block Ashley Young's cross... Ashley Young, the PFA Young Player of the Season last season, easily confused Fletcher with a simple feint. His precise pass couldn't be headed away by Brown, who isn't good at heading anyway, while Vidic, blocked by Heskey, rushed over to cover but was too late to stop Abou Diaby, the 'Archangel', who calmly headed into the empty net, 1-0!

Villa Perfectly Counters the Top Four

Villa has already achieved three consecutive victories against the top four this season, previously Liverpool and Chelsea were both defeated. Their specialty is scoring and then running away. In this match, Villa started defending hard after scoring, leaving only the speedily fast Abou Diaby and Ashley Young upfront for counterattacks, resulting in Manchester United having a possession rate as high as 68%.

But Villa's united defensive efforts, especially outstanding performances from Friedel, Dunne (who found a second youth after transferring to Villa), Cuellar, and Petrov, rendered all Manchester United's attacks futile. After the match, the man-of-the-match Abou Diaby said: "In previous seasons, we always got overtaken after scoring here at Old Trafford. But this time, our 11 players collectively defended, and we finally got a beautiful result."

Ferguson could only vent his anger on the referee: Why didn't they give the Red Devils a 'Ferguson Time' like in the Manchester Derby this time? The fourth official indicated only 3 minutes of added time, and the dissatisfied Sir Alex argued with him on the sideline. Post-match, he further commented: "How much stoppage time should be added shouldn't be decided by the referee. There were two injury stoppages in the second half, I timed it, totaling 2 minutes and 20 seconds, plus numerous substitutions, this added time was indeed disappointing."

Before Christmas, Manchester United has already lost 4 league matches, which was the number of matches they lost last season (both previous seasons they only lost 5 matches). But Ferguson believed the problem wasn't significant, "As long as we're not far behind the leader by January, there's still great hope."
