Three people were prosecuted for selling rare wild ducks after anesthetizing them with pesticides (Picture)

by zzf000zxye0 on 2011-06-27 17:25:21

Wild ducks that were anesthetized and died at the hands of illegal poachers. Gansu Daily picture.

According to a report by reporter Guo Yuhong: At the end of 2009, a large number of precious wild migratory birds such as ruddy shelducks that inhabit the Jing River Basin in Pingliang City of our province were hunted down by unscrupulous individuals using pesticides made into "Meng medicine". On October 29 this year, I learned from the Provincial Procuratorate that the People's Procuratorate of Kongtong District has recently filed a public prosecution against three people including Dou from Shaanxi Province for suspected illegal hunting and concealing and covering up criminal proceeds.

In November 2009, the Jing River Basin in Kongtong District welcomed a group of precious guests - ruddy shelducks, northern pintails, and egrets. These migratory birds are included in the national list of protected wild animals and are considered "beneficial", "of important economic value", and "of research value" under the "three have" category of protected wild animals. However, local residents often found someone poaching and selling these "wild ducks" in a certain wild flavor shop in the urban area. On the morning of February 21 this year, several photography enthusiasts from the locality went to the Jing River Basin in the twenty-mile Pu area of Kongtong District for artistic inspiration when they were shocked to discover a large number of ruddy shelducks dead within about a one-kilometer radius around the riverbank, sand dunes, and nearby farmland. The photographers immediately reported the situation to relevant departments and handed over the 27 dead ducks collected on that day to the city's river management station. The city immediately formed a special investigation team to conduct an investigation. On the morning of March 7 of the same year, three suspects from Shaanxi Province, including Dou, were arrested in Dianzhang Town, Xingping City, Xianyang City.

It was found that on February 18 and February 23 this year, the three people, including Dou, drove twice to Pingliang City, mixed highly toxic pesticide "Furadan" with lard, and sprinkled it along the riverbanks mixed with corn and rice grains, causing a large number of wild ruddy shelducks and northern pintails to be anesthetized and "unconscious". Dou and others sold 21 surviving wild ruddy shelducks and 2 wild northern pintails to Tang at a price of 180 yuan per bird. Recently, after reviewing the case, the public prosecution department of Kongtong District in Pingliang City believed that the facts of Dou and others' illegal hunting and concealing and covering up criminal proceeds were clear and the evidence was solid and sufficient, and has separately prosecuted the three people for suspected illegal hunting and concealing and covering up criminal proceeds.
