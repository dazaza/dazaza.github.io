Our past is over, understand?

by lk0gamef on 2009-07-18 00:02:52

--- "The bright moon shines as the clouds part and people come into view," hinting that the author hails from Yun Kai and is a female player.

I: Originally from Yun Kai in Zone 60, I was a female warrior who reached level 49 with a baby at level 37 and an enhanced lightning set. I also had another beloved beautiful female mage ID, which remained at level 45 with a baby at level 14, wearing protective gear.

Now, calling myself Yun Kai again holds no meaning except for nostalgia. Just like the lost players after the server merge, curiosity brings us together, but curiosity cannot retain the steps of those who have lost faith. Those who left were all heartbroken. Those who returned may not have healed their wounds.

Mo Ling once said: "Being hurt by someone you truly love leaves a wound that cannot heal."

Half is seawater, half is fire; the natural revelation of strength amidst charm has made me what I am in this legend. It wasn't bloated heroism that shaped me, but the extreme duality of my character in life. My seemingly casual exterior hides the essence of my emotions, which others see as indifferent to everything, but only I know the truth. Like the indecision between being a warrior or a mage, they both accompanied me throughout.

Therefore, when betrayed emotionally, I chose perfection, swallowing my pain, and in giving up, broke the wings that lacked the resilience and patience of femininity. Falling, failing, losing him, and losing myself.

I haven't played Legend for a long time now.

Recently, I heard about the merging of Zone 60. I wonder what former comrades, colleagues, or enemies think about it. The game can start anew, but what about our youth and love? Five or six years ago, the fashionable Legend created a myth of a happy kingdom. Now buried among countless online games, it isn't particularly remarkable. What's more worth remembering are those young days of madness when we lived beyond reality in the virtual world, untouched by worldly concerns. We wandered through a land of equal beings, ideal in appearance, doing good deeds and roaming through the Fa Ma continent, leaving our marks in the illusory dangerous lands, living under the stimulation of spiritual opium until we were utterly exhausted...
