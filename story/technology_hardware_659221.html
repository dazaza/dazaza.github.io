Sina Sports news, February 4th, Los Angeles Lakers (Weibo) defeated the Nuggets away from home, but there was different news off the court. The door for Dwight Howard to join the Lakers has basically been closed. Today, Howard personally confirmed that the Lakers had no chance. More surprisingly, Howard said, "Kobe messed this up," according to Jilin News and Sohu Rolling News.

American media earlier today revealed that even if Howard were traded to the Lakers, he would not choose to renew his contract and would instead opt out to enter the free agent market. So why is he not inclined to join the Lakers? An insider close to Howard gave the answer - "Kobe messed this up, that's all I can say now," according to Xingtai News.

Kobe's competitive spirit is unparalleled in the league, and he also seeks good teammates to regain the championship. So how could he have ruined the deal for Howard to join the Lakers? There could be three reasons. The first is that Kobe demanded the Lakers keep Andrew Bynum (Weibo). To get Howard, the Lakers would definitely have to send Bynum away. However, this young center has shown significant improvement this season, gradually revealing the potential to become a dominant inside player. Moreover, he is younger than "the beast" and has a lower salary. His performance has won Kobe's trust, so Kobe is unwilling to see Bynum leave.

The second reason is that Kobe's playing style and dominance make Howard unwilling to come to Los Angeles. Even when facing Shaquille O'Neal at his peak, Kobe was unyielding, constantly challenging Shaq's status as the team leader. Now, Kobe is undoubtedly the number one player on the Lakers. In Mike Brown's system, he has absolute offensive rights. During games, it is often seen that he ignores the double towers inside and insists on personal attacks. This behavior would certainly make Howard cautious. Going to the Lakers to be Kobe's "little brother" is not what Howard wants, and because of this, he does not wish to play for the Lakers again.

The third reason is the recent decline in performance by Kobe and the entire Lakers team. In the last game, Kobe only made 2 out of 11 three-point attempts, and today in Denver, he only made 2 out of 10 shots in the first half. Although Bryant's performance this season has been strong, his condition is indeed not as good as during his prime years. The decline in skill level at this age is just a matter of time. Howard naturally leaves the Magic hoping to join a team to challenge for the championship. If Kobe's condition declines, it will be difficult for the Lakers, who rely solely on him, to compete with teams like the Heat (Weibo) and Thunder. Therefore, he does not want to play for the Lakers.

Howard only left a sentence without giving more detailed explanations. Perhaps only time will reveal the answer to this "riddle." People will then understand exactly how Kobe messed this up.
