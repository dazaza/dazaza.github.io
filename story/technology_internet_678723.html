It attracts thousands of readers with an irresistible beauty, influences people's spiritual world, and for some people, it even affects their whole life. "Jane Eyre" is a long novel with autobiographical color, which expounds such a theme: human value = dignity + love. Charlotte Bronte, the author of "Jane Eyre", and Emily, the author of "Wuthering Heights", are sisters. Although they lived in the same society and family environment, their personalities were very different. Charlotte Bronte seemed gentler, purer, and more fond of pursuing some beautiful things. Download, although her family was poor, she lost maternal love when she was young, and received little paternal love. Moreover, she was short in stature and not beautiful in appearance. But perhaps it was this deep inferiority from the depths of her soul that reflected in her personality as a very sensitive self-esteem, using self-esteem as compensation for her inner inferiority. The Jane Eyre she described was also an unattractive and short woman, but she had an extremely strong sense of self-esteem. She pursued a bright, holy, and beautiful life with unwavering determination.

Jane Eyre grew up in an environment where both parents had died and she was dependent on others. From a young age, she endured treatment unlike other children of her age - disdain from her aunt, contempt from her cousin sister, humiliation and beatings from her cousin brother... This was a ruthless trampling on the dignity of a child. But perhaps because of all this, Jane Eyre gained infinite confidence and an indomitable spirit, a kind of intrinsic personal strength that could be overcome.

In front of Rochester, she never felt inferior because she was a lowly governess. Instead, she believed they were equal. She should not be disrespected just because she was a servant. It was precisely because of her integrity, nobility, purity, and her heart being untainted by secular society that made Rochester feel shocked and regard her as someone he could have equal spiritual conversations with. He gradually fell deeply in love with her. His sincerity moved her, and she accepted him. However, on their wedding day, when Jane Eyre learned that Rochester already had a wife, she felt she had to leave. She said, "I will follow the laws issued by God and recognized by mankind. I will adhere to the principles I accepted when I was sober, not crazy like now." "I will firmly uphold this position." This was Jane Eyre's reason for telling Rochester why she must leave. But from the heart, a deeper reason was that Jane Eyre realized she had been deceived, and her self-esteem had been mocked because she deeply loved Rochester. Ask any woman if she can bear being deceived by the person she trusts most and is closest to? Jane Eyre bore it and made a very rational decision. Surrounded by such a powerful force of love, tempted by a beautiful, wealthy life, she still insisted on maintaining her personal dignity. This is what makes Jane Eyre the most spiritually charming.

The novel designed a very bright ending - although Rochester's estate was destroyed and he himself became disabled, we see that it was precisely under these conditions that Jane Eyre no longer had a conflict between dignity and love, and could obtain satisfaction simultaneously - she had dignity when she married Rochester, and she also had love.

The novel tells us that the best life for humans is human dignity plus love. The ending arranged for the female protagonist is such a life. Although I think this ending is too perfect, and this perfection itself marks shallowness, I still respect the author's ideal of this kind of good life - dignity plus love. After all, in today's society, to realize the formula of human value = dignity + love often requires the help of money. People seem crazy for money and status, drowning love. Choosing wealth over poverty, choosing not to love over love. Few people would abandon everything for love and personality like Jane, and do so without looking back. What "Jane Eyre" shows us is a simplification, a return to simplicity, a feeling of full-hearted devotion, a simplified emotion regardless of gains and losses. It is like a glass of icy water, purifying every reader's heart, and resonating with readers, especially female readers.
