360 Apps Pulled from Apple's App Store for Over 100 Days, No Official Explanation Yet

by geekzhang on 2013-05-16 17:26:17

As the most well-known app store, Apple's App Store has now exceeded 50 billion downloads. However, this app store which was established for the purpose of openness and win-win cooperation, has not been tolerant towards Chinese app developers since entering 2013. Due to issues such as chart manipulation, infringement of user privacy, and copyright problems, several well-known domestic mobile apps have suffered from being taken off the shelves, with 360's related products being the most severely affected.As of this week, 360's related products have been off the shelves of Apple's App Store for over a hundred days. Even worse, due to allegations of impersonating MIUI official notifications to mislead users, 360 Mobile Guardian was recently completely removed from Xiaomi's app store.Regarding the reasons for being off the shelves of Apple's App Store for such a long time, 360 has yet to provide an official response.Industry analysts believe that the removal may be related to 360's security and privacy issues. There are reports that 360 accessed personal sensitive information and system-level operations by calling APIs that Apple does not allow, posing a risk of privacy leakage.In fact, controversies regarding network security applications infringing on personal privacy have never ceased. It is not just 360; many mobile security products currently on the market have faced similar doubts. The "2012 Annual Report on Personal Network Security" jointly released by iResearch and Tencent PC Manager pointed out that in 2012, most Internet users held pessimistic attitudes towards network security: 70.0% of Internet users were concerned about network security, with 33.3% expressing great concern; compared to 2011, Internet users' concerns about network dangers focused more on financial losses and privacy leaks.With the enhancement of crisis awareness, actions to protect network security and privacy rights have increased in recent years. Taking 360 as an example, last October, well-known online anti-counterfeiter Fang Zhouzi publicly accused 360 of collecting user privacy under the guise of security, questioning the safety of 360 software. In late March this year, Nanjing consumer Huang Jia discovered that 360 Browser had been spying on his privacy for a long time, and in anger, filed a lawsuit against Qihoo 360 Company, which has now been officially accepted.Regarding the current situation, veteran IT observer Hong Bo stated that security software is an armed force on the Internet. It has higher system privileges on computers and can obtain a lot of underlying information from the operating system. If the security software itself is not independent, but uses it as a stepping stone to do other businesses, this is somewhat similar to organized crime. Hong Bo pointed out that due to insufficient market regulation in China and relatively lenient law enforcement on these issues, many Internet companies are exploiting legal loopholes.Regarding this, internet legal expert Zhao Zhanling expressed that the continuous outbreak of network privacy scandals has already had negative impacts on users and the industry. On this key issue concerning industry development and competitive order, apart from enterprises seeking judicial channels, there is also a need for government authorities to strengthen supervision and further improve laws and regulations, increasing supervision and crackdown efforts.
