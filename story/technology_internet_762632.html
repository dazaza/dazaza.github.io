"The Inevitable 'Death of Yougou': Belle's E-commerce Has Many Fatal Flaws"

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 16:27:53

Recently, Yougou.com, which has been severely affected by the departure of senior executives, suffered another blow. On July 17th, it was confirmed by an insider at Yougou.com that COO Zhang Xiaojun will be leaving and is currently in the process of handling his resignation procedures. As one of the founding members of Yougou.com, Zhang Xiaojun's departure is more indicative of a serious crisis faced by Yougou.com.

Let’s first review the e-commerce journey of Belle International, to which Yougou.com belongs:

Belle cautiously experimented with e-commerce several years ago, developing steadily through three channels: Taobao distribution, Tmall, and B2C distribution (B2C distribution was completed via Taixiu.com). Led by veteran group member Xie Yunli, a not-so-luxurious e-commerce team initially sold products from Belle's own brands, followed by exclusive online versions, then new network brands. This development path was considered a successful case of traditional brand enterprises entering e-commerce more solidly than Li-Ning before 2011.

With the rapid development of Letao.com and Haolemai after they received over $100 million in financing in 2010, Belle Group became restless. On one hand, there was concern about being constrained by distribution channels again; on the other hand, an evaluation revealed significant development advantages in the e-commerce field, including:

1. Numerous brands: Besides Belle's own major women's shoe brands, the agency also sells international well-known sports brands like Adidas and Nike, whose online sales have grown astonishingly fast;

2. Easier category expansion: For Belle Group, which owns its own women's shoe brands and agents for famous sports brands, how to achieve absolute market advantage and status through multiple brands and categories has been the most thought-provoking issue these years. The way of expanding offline stores is obviously too slow, while the ongoing e-commerce business can easily expand brands but cannot quickly expand categories;

3. Financial advantage: In the process of cooperating with e-commerce companies, Belle discovered that e-commerce is a capital-intensive business. However, Belle Group's financial strength allows it to develop without relying on capital, thus inevitably defeating more vertical e-commerce companies that must rely on capital, becoming the "survivor as king" type;

4. Talent advantage: After several years of e-commerce practice, a professional e-commerce team with strong execution capabilities has already been formed. The cultivation of talent can meet the scale development or even higher requirements of the e-commerce business;

5. Supply chain advantage: This excites Belle internally, and it is also the core reason for attracting many high-level executives from brand e-commerce or platform e-commerce. After all, for e-commerce, supply chain issues always fascinate because this seems to be the part that decides life and death apart from funds. The supply chain includes product research and development, production, warehouse management, delivery after-sales, etc., a series of "business" contents. In traditional enterprises that value "commerce" more than "e-commerce", this is considered the best competitive advantage compared to e-commerce enterprises.

Thus, based on these advantages, Belle e-commerce decided to go all out, first purchasing the double-character domain name "yougou.com".

The slightly copycat "topshoes.cn" Taixiu website was directly abandoned and redirected, stopping supplies to all distribution websites. Although direct third-party online shops such as Taobao and Tmall were retained, their investment and attention decreased. High salaries were offered to attract JD.com VP and Vancl VP to serve as CMO and COO respectively, and a veteran executive was sent from the group to serve as CEO, forming a golden triangle. They attempted to create a vertical B2C mall covering all categories, implementing a multi-brand strategy on one hand, and expanding into clothing and other categories on the other, realizing the dream of a department store empire impossible to achieve offline!

Everything sounded very beautiful, but no matter whose dream needs to face the reality of the backbone. The actual situation is:

1. Higher technical requirements for independent B2C: Being located in Shenzhen headquarters, Belle couldn't recruit more excellent technical talents, and technical outsourcing service companies are even scarcer in Shenzhen. But technology determines user experience, backend management efficiency, and more reliable data. Independent B2C belongs to dynamic technology, different from the relatively static technology like ERP. Continuous changes remain a hurdle that many traditional enterprises cannot overcome;

2. Increased management difficulty due to category expansion: Someone once said that doing well in women's shoes does not mean doing well in men's shoes, something Belle probably felt deeply when expanding into men's shoes. Expanding into women's clothing and accessories within a short year can imagine what kind of difficulties the back end would encounter. For traditional brand enterprises accustomed to meticulous calculations, this rapid approach worked for Vancl during special times, and could be understood within the systems of Vancl and JD.com. However, although Belle now has Vancl's COO and JD.com's CMO, whether they can be understood and executed with "exaggerated costs" is a big question;

3. System: Many people don’t understand this unless they cross between e-commerce and traditional enterprises. Simply put, the content such as department settings (organizational structure), approvals, finance and budget, reporting methods, corporate culture, communication methods, execution methods, risk control, processes, and systems form what is called the "mechanism" of an enterprise. The result of this conflict is that e-commerce tries to persuade and accept the "system" of e-commerce, while the traditional team tries to understand but can never fully understand the "system" of e-commerce, leading to low efficiency and instability of the execution team. Eventually, the chairman or board of directors might say, "E-commerce and traditional enterprises each have their own strengths and weaknesses, the future will certainly be a fusion, so you must also fuse in these aspects..." If this so-called "fusion without harmony" appears, it will be called management innovation, and the "CEO rotation system" actually appeared in a company like Belle!

It was originally believed that Yougou.com of Belle e-commerce had already taken the lead, and the few senior executives were waiting for the spin-off listing to enjoy the glory of countless traditional enterprises' success in the e-commerce era, rather than being entangled and sorrowful in front of these hard injuries.

After all, whether it is the veterans who grew up with Belle or the e-commerce professionals recruited, it can be seen that they did not leave because of lack of ability. When the VPs from JD.com returned to JD.com, they could still perform outstandingly, and when the VPs from Vancl had the courage to start their own businesses. Are the hard injuries merely these three points? Or are these not only Belle's hard injuries but also the hard injuries of many traditional enterprises trying to enter e-commerce?
