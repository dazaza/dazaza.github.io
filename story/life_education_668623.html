Childhood obesity has become one of the problems troubling parents.

by hudazikao on 2012-02-18 10:53:02

Teachers from the self-study examination office of Hunan University have found that in reality, some parents fail to realize the harm caused by obesity and even think that it is not a big deal. In fact, obese children generally have slower and less coordinated movements, with their walking, jumping, and running development being hindered. Since the movements of obese children cannot develop properly, this will directly affect their intellectual development. Generally speaking, movement and activity training can directly promote the intellectual development of children, while the level of intelligence inevitably reflects in the flexibility of movements, etc.

Teachers from the Hunan University Self-Study Examination Network believe that obesity also affects the personality development of children. Because an obese figure is not aesthetically pleasing and an unwieldy body is inconvenient. When obese children participate in activities with their peers, they are often ridiculed due to their clumsiness, thereby generating negative emotions, which may lead to inferiority complex and reclusive tendencies.

Parents have the responsibility to ensure the health rights of their children and let them live a healthy life. As parents, one should reassess the health of children, eliminate factors that are detrimental to the health of children from aspects such as diet and exercise, let children form good eating habits, form good exercise habits, and become truly healthy children, which is the goal we aim to achieve in children's education.

Let children enjoy the nutrition and fun brought by food and exercise, lay a foundation for lifelong health. The educational suggestions from Hunan University self-study teachers are:

1. Spend money on exercise instead of medicine.

2. Parents exercising with their children

3. Encourage children to exercise with their peers.

4. Do not set overly high exercise goals.

5. Pay attention to the diet.
