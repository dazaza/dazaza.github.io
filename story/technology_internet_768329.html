The Nexus 5 is the first Google Phone.

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 20:38:23

There have been articles that revealed the Android team was a "small kingdom" within Google, with little cooperation and constant friction with other departments. This situation was also reflected in the products; it's hard to believe that the poor search experience on Google Play comes from a search company. Moreover, the browser previously built into Android was not even Google's own Chrome.

However, after Andy Rubin left the Android team, this began to change. We saw Google separate the upgrades of the mobile system from the built-in apps. As a result, before the Android 4.4 upgrade package was released, the built-in apps could already be upgraded through Google Play, allowing users to enjoy some new features of the new system in advance. This also means that Google has reduced its reliance on Android in the mobile ecosystem.

This change within Google is also reflected in the Nexus 5. The Verge even said, "Past Nexus devices were Android phones, but the Nexus 5 is the first true Google phone."

Matias Duarte, Android design director, said, "On this hardware product, we had more time than on any other product. We started seeking intrinsic value and design stories that would unite the entire Nexus family." For example, he really likes the ceramic button design because the material is "natural" and "just right," and this small detail and design style are very compatible.

-- This reflects that Google is paying more attention to design and investing more effort into it, and also reflects that Google is starting to customize Android like other Android device manufacturers to reflect the feel of the hardware and internal consistency. Duarte said, "In the end, we had to design both software and hardware simultaneously. We see the product as a united, single-leader team. Therefore, both naturally convey the same values."

In the software part, we can see that the Nexus 5 has an exclusive Launcher. Regardless of whether Google will change its mind in the future, it is exclusive for now. Duarte calls this new Launcher "the most exciting thing on the Nexus 5."

Another reflection of Google's influence includes other built-in applications - it uses the new version of Hangouts to replace the SMS in the phone, even though SMS still exists on other phones. For users, they can download another SMS application, but Hiroshi Lockheimer, Senior Vice President of Android Engineering, said, "Consistency... leads to a better user experience. We combined three buttons into one, making it easier for users to understand."

The new dialer also integrates Google's local search feature, allowing you to find business opportunities without opening other applications. And this function is a core part of Android, accessed via private APIs. In addition, the new file manager integrates cloud storage services. Although it can interact with other cloud storage services, Google's own Google Drive is the most integrated.

To put it simply, the Neuxs 5 and Android Kitkat will make people feel more of Google's presence. Someone once commented that Android is the "least open open-source operating system," which has some truth to it. At least in Android Kitkat, we can see a Google that cares more about control. Ars Technica has a detailed report pointing out how Google distinguishes between AOSP members and its own products by controlling the built-in applications, such as being more stingy with open sourcing and cutting functions.

It’s no surprise that the Nexus 5 has undergone such changes. Recall when Google launched Google+ despite all opposition and used it to integrate its services, which actually aligns with Larry Page's usual business practices. His goal is to make different parts of Google move forward together, with aligned interests. Recalling the rumors that Larry Page threatened other executives for lack of unity, **integration has become a keyword under Page's leadership at Google.
