Snapchat Acquisition Battle: Who Will Win, Google or Facebook?

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 20:55:30

In recent days, the news that Facebook and Google's bids to acquire Snapchat were rejected has been everywhere. Because of this, Snapchat has once again become the "shining belle of the ball."

Snapchat is a "disappearing message" mobile service that allows users to share images and videos, which vanish just seconds after being viewed. Snapchat has now attracted acquisition offers from multiple companies, including Facebook. It is reported that Snapchat has long been an app coveted by Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg. However, on Thursday of this week, there were also reports that in addition to Facebook, Google was also interested in acquiring Snapchat, with a bid comparable to Facebook’s offer of $3 billion to $3.5 billion. Both Google and Facebook have not commented on these rumors so far. There are also reports suggesting that Tencent, an internet giant from the Chinese market, has shown interest in the company.

What will be Snapchat's final outcome? This remains unknown at present. According to insiders, even Snapchat CEO Evan Spiegel himself does not know. But it's worth pondering: Are Tencent, Google, and Facebook still interested in acquiring Snapchat? If so, which company is most likely to secure the rapidly growing Snapchat?

Please take a look at the following relevant analysis.

1. Facebook

In fact, Zuckerberg wanted Snapchat so much that Facebook once completely cloned Snapchat, but this clone version ended up failing. According to insiders, Zuckerberg is deeply infatuated with Snapchat. Insiders revealed that Zuckerberg has made multiple offers for Snapchat, some of which greatly exceeded the $1 billion Facebook spent to acquire Instagram last year.

Likelihood of Facebook successfully acquiring Snapchat: Very high.

2. Google

Google already owns Google+, but the company is fully aware that in terms of social mobile applications, it cannot compare with Facebook, nor even with Twitter. Therefore, acquiring Snapchat could potentially bring about an immediate qualitative change in Google’s mobile social application capabilities, while also delivering a heavy blow to its old rival Facebook. Of course, for a highly profitable company like Google, an acquisition cost of $3 billion would be a small matter.

Likelihood of Google successfully acquiring Snapchat: High.

3. Tencent

This might be a very suitable pair. Spiegel once described Tencent as a "model" for Snapchat's revenue generation, which may also hint at the possibility that Snapchat will introduce in-app purchases. Certainly, Tencent is indeed interested in acquiring Snapchat — even if not a full acquisition, then at least making a significant strategic investment in Snapchat.

Likelihood of Tencent successfully acquiring Snapchat: Very high.

4. Yahoo

Yahoo could be a dark horse contender, but there are currently no reports confirming Yahoo's definite intention to acquire Snapchat. In fact, Yahoo CEO Marissa Mayer has a substantial amount of cash on hand, enough to complete the acquisition of Snapchat, and Mayer is no novice when it comes to mergers and acquisitions. Additionally, acquiring Snapchat could help enhance Yahoo's mobile business (which is currently still relatively weak) and improve Yahoo's image. However, there is currently no solid evidence indicating that Yahoo has contacted Spiegel or Snapchat to discuss possible acquisition matters.

Likelihood of Yahoo successfully acquiring Snapchat: Unlikely.

5. Twitter

After considering for a long time whether to completely eliminate its direct messaging function, Twitter finally realized last year that users actually enjoy sending private messages quite a lot. Perhaps, acquiring Snapchat could play a significant role. However, the issue is that Snapchat's current value might already exceed what Twitter can afford.

Likelihood of Twitter successfully acquiring Snapchat: Impossible.

In the past week, rumors about various companies wanting to acquire Snapchat have been abundant, and news about increasing acquisition prices has also been frequent. However, it should be noted: Spiegel also plans to conduct a new round of financing at a higher valuation. If this new round of financing is implemented, one possible scenario is that Spiegel and co-founder Bobby Murphy may sell off part of their own shares to cash out. This would mean that the two might continue to work wholeheartedly on Snapchat rather than selling it at a high price. However, many sources also claim that Spiegel and Murphy are very keen on developing Snapchat into a mature company.

Overall, if Snapchat continues to grow and strengthen, Spiegel will face immense pressure in deciding Snapchat's future. If everything goes smoothly, Spiegel's acquisition conditions and the high price they demand may not disappear.
