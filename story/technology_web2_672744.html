The registration of Jeremy Lin's Chinese pinyin domain name, which cost 100 yuan, is now priced at 3 million yuan, while the registration of Whitney Houston's name domain is priced at 21 million US dollars on eBay... This string of crazy numbers has once again brought the long-forgotten investment channel of domain name registration and speculation back into the spotlight.

Registering or preemptively registering domain names at low prices and then reselling them at high prices seems as easy as Jeremy Lin's "one-shot fame" legend, enough to make everyone excited. However, when people see the halo of high returns, they often overlook the fact that beneath a high-priced domain transaction, there are tens of thousands of domain names waiting for buyers, with not a single inquiry.

The valuation of the domain name shenyang.com is 10 million yuan. To most people, huoche, 55, tianmao... are just ordinary combinations of letters or numbers. But in the eyes of domain name enthusiasts, these simple combinations are often true gold that can bring huge profits.

Registering place names as domain names has always been highly pursued by players. Domain names composed of place name abbreviations and double pinyin are considered one of the top-level domain bloodline signs. In real life, "Shenyang" may not be as famous as "Beijing", but in the virtual online world, domain names related to "Shenyang" have long been owned by players.

Yesterday, through domain registration service websites such as Wanwang and Meicheng Interconnect, I found that most of the English and Chinese domain names related to "Shenyang" have already been registered. Among them, "shenyang.net" was registered as early as 1998, with a usage period of up to 20 years.

For the value of domain names, Cai Wensheng, one of China's most successful domain investors, believes that good domain names should be short and memorable, making them more valuable; secondly, they should ideally overlap with words or trademarks, increasing their value. Clearly, domain names like "Shenyang" fully meet the high-value standards.

Take "024shenyang.com" as an example. The asking price set by the registered user "chejiacheng" on "Meicheng Interconnect" is 200,000 RMB, while the domain name for "山寨版小沈阳" (a parody version of Xiao Shenyang) is priced at 500 yuan.

In the world of domain names, ".com" is the king. Players who preemptively register similar domain names can simply wait for the right time to sell them at a high price. Currently, the ".com" domain names related to Shenyang are not in trading status, but this does not prevent us from imagining how much space there is for the future price of "shenyang.com".

According to the current international standard widely accepted, the price calculation formula for a regional domain name (referring to ".com domain") is: the number of non-agricultural population in the urban area multiplied by 2. That is, if there are 50,000 people in an urban area, then the .com domain price is 100,000 yuan. Therefore, according to the urban population of Shenyang being 5 million, the price of "shenyang.com" climbing to over ten million yuan is not a problem.

Domain Name · Maintenance

Self-protection of luxury companies

The phenomenon of "Jeremy Lin domain name" is not the first or last case of domain name pre-registration. Due to Facebook's IPO news, the recent transaction case of the similar domain name ****book.net also caught attention. This domain name was sold for 100,000 US dollars, approximately 630,000 RMB, with the buyer's identity mysterious.

Mr. Wang, who once served in several multinational electronic industry companies, told me that the main purpose of domain name registration websites is to build websites and maintain domain names. Nowadays, all the domain name companies found online are agents. Applying for domain names and building websites on such websites only belongs to a semi-maintenance state.

Some large companies, such as HP, Acer, and ASUS, have dedicated personnel at headquarters responsible for website and system-related maintenance work, and domain names are entrusted to one company for maintenance. Network space all occupies the company's own server, ensuring confidentiality. Taking ASUS as an example, the company has registered all suffixes of ASUS as domain names, and the registration work is completed by ASUS's agency company. All trademarked domain names worldwide have been pre-registered, preventing anyone from impersonating the official website of the company. Most international large companies operate this way.

Some companies with less substantial funds may not register all domain names at once, leading to situations where all suffixes of domain names have been registered domestically, but in other countries, suffixes using their trademarks as domain names have already been registered. For instance, .cn is China's suffix, but Japan's suffix is .jp. Some products perform well in China, but when expanding to the Japanese market, they need to consider whether the domain name has been registered.

Most small companies do not have dedicated personnel to maintain their websites, so they entrust the maintenance work to domain name companies. Domain name companies are responsible for providing hardware and network space and creating a fixed template. Afterwards, the client's staff can only maintain the website through the template, just like posting on a forum, reducing website maintenance costs.

In the 1990s, when the internet was just emerging in China and many international companies were entering the Chinese market, there were many cases of domain names being pre-registered. Some companies had to spend a lot of money to buy back their domain names.

Products selling well in foreign markets prompted some people to see business opportunities, and they pre-registered related domain names in China. This cannot be stopped legally. As long as the person who registers the domain name continues to pay, they can continue to use it unless the holder is willing to transfer it.

Domain Name · Analysis

Domain Names Not Within Intellectual Property Rights

Apple's IPAD trademark infringement dispute led to the removal of IPAD from stores, and Apple's lawsuit against Shenzhen Weiguan is still ongoing. What are the differences between domain names and registered trademarks?

In the internet world, domain names are a form of trademark. With the gradual "partitioning" of this limited scarce resource, many companies face the awkward situation of their brand domain names being preemptively registered. How to protect the value of domain names in the internet world, and whether domain name registration and protection are associated with intellectual property issues?

Mr. Zhu, who has been engaged in intellectual property-related work for many years, introduced that intellectual property refers to the "exclusive rights enjoyed by the creator of intellectual labor results," which are generally valid for a limited period. Various intellectual creations such as inventions, literary and artistic works, as well as marks, names, images, and designs used in commerce, can all be considered intellectual property owned by an individual or organization. Intellectual property law is the general term for legal norms that regulate social relationships arising from the creation and use of intellectual achievements, as well as during the confirmation, protection, and exercise of intellectual property rights by the owner of intellectual achievements. It usually includes copyright (copyright), patent rights, trademark rights, trade name rights, place of origin marking rights, trade secret rights, etc.

Domain names are not within the scope of intellectual property protection, and no one applies for intellectual property protection for domain names. "The registration of a domain name is definitely unique, and building a website is to let more people understand and log in. It's impossible for only the applicant to log in while others cannot. Some websites require payment to view certain content, but this is only related to the website and cannot be achieved through intellectual property protection," Mr. Zhu said.

Domain Name · Gossip

tianmao.com Sold for 120,000 Yuan

In the Valentine's Day "schedule" of 2012, Zhuang Liangji from Xiamen Bookman revealed that the double-pinyin domain name tianmao.com of "Tmall" was sold for only 120,000 yuan last year. President Zhuang jokingly claimed he was deceived by Alibaba and frankly admitted, "Who would have thought Taobao Mall would be renamed Tmall? Otherwise, I wouldn't have sold it for less than 8 million yuan."

From mall.taobao.com to tmall.com, and then renaming to "Tmall," in terms of domain marketing, Taobao Mall has always been one of the most talked-about topics on the internet.

Recently, Taobao Mall was renamed to "Tmall" and acquired the three Tmall domain names tianmao.com/.cn/.com.cn, indicating that the website domain remains tmall.com, and currently, these three domain names have not yet been applied.

According to Zhang Yong, president of Taobao Mall, Taobao Mall was renamed to Tmall mainly because the pronunciation of Tmall is close to "Tianmao." This renaming move is rich in meaning. Taobao's Taobao Mall needs to develop further under the banner of Alibaba, shaping a new brand, and "Tmall" was born out of necessity. A new brand, a new domain name, creates a new news focal point, serving as free marketing for Taobao Mall.

On February 20th, there was a rumor that Taobao Mall plans to activate the double-pinyin domain name tianmao.com within a month to strengthen the brand.

Zhang Yong mentioned on the day Taobao Mall changed its logo that Tmall's purchase of tianmao.com was a defensive measure and denied that this domain name would replace tmall.com in the future. However, it is undeniable that the double-pinyin domain name tianmao.com clearly aligns better with the habits of Chinese internet users and can reinforce the Tmall brand externally.
