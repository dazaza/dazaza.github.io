New iPhone Meets Cold Response in China; Apple Loses Over $10 Billion in Market Value

by anonymous on 2013-09-18 21:58:39

On Sunday evening, China Unicom announced that the pre-orders for its iPhone 5S/5C had surpassed 100,000 units, which was less impressive than last year's iPhone 5, whose pre-sales broke 200,000 units in two days. Additionally, analysts predicted that China Telecom's subsidies for the new Apple iPhone 5S/5C would be approximately 15% lower than those for the iPhone 5. Influenced by these factors, Apple's stock price opened low and gradually declined on Monday in the NASDAQ Stock Market. By the close of trading, Apple's stock price fell by $14.78, a decrease of 3.18%, closing at $450.12, resulting in a loss of nearly $14 billion in market value.

Apple's stock price trend on Monday

According to the plan published on China Telecom's website, regardless of the package chosen, the upfront payments for the 16GB version of the iPhone 5c and iPhone 5s were RMB 4488 and RMB 5288 respectively. If users sign a two-year contract, paying monthly minimum package fees of RMB 329 for the iPhone 5c and RMB 389 for the iPhone 5s, they can enjoy a zero-cost purchase. Market analysts stated that the subsidy received from the 289 yuan monthly package for the iPhone 5s is RMB 2890, which is 15% lower than the RMB 3400 subsidy for an equivalent package for the iPhone 5. Industry insiders pointed out that because carriers hope to control subsidy costs and promote cheaper models from domestic manufacturers such as Lenovo, ZTE Communications, and Xiaomi, support from carriers for the Apple iPhone has also decreased.

Unlike markets such as the U.S., consumers in China need to spend more money initially if they want to buy an iPhone, meaning that the high price in the Chinese market makes it difficult for the iPhone 5S/5C to compete. The subsidies the iPhone receives from American carriers allow this product to be sold at the same price as other high-end smartphones like the Galaxy S4 in the U.S. In the Chinese market, the Galaxy S4 is also priced higher than other products.

Although market analysts believe that China Mobile may offer higher subsidies for the new iPhone, enabling lower-income users to purchase this product, China Telecom's actions indicate that this carrier no longer considers it necessary to attract consumers with the iPhone. Marvin L, an analyst at Mizuho Securities Japan, said that reducing subsidies for the iPhone is due to China Telecom's attempt to strictly control subsidies to improve profit margins. He stated, "Compared to the iPhone 4S, China Telecom has already reduced subsidies for the iPhone 5. The subsidy for the iPhone 4S was 47% of the total agreement price, dropped to 39% for the iPhone 5, and further down to 31% for the iPhone 5S."

China Unicom announced on Sunday that shortly after the conclusion of Apple's China launch event on September 11th, it became the first to open online reservations for the iPhone 5s/5c contract phones. Less than an hour after opening reservations via the mobile app, the number had already exceeded ten thousand. As of Sunday, the number of online reservations made through China Unicom had broken 100,000.

Since Apple released the iPhone 5S/5C last week, the company's stock price has fallen by about 10%. The price of the iPhone 5C in the Chinese market is higher than many market analysts expected, causing concerns among analysts about the growth prospects of iPhone products in the Chinese market. Starting from the 20th of this month, the new iPhones will begin selling in the U.S., China, and some other markets.

On Monday, Apple's stock price fell by $14.78, a decrease of 3.18%, closing at $450.12. In subsequent after-hours trading, Apple's stock price fell another $1.63, a decline of 0.36%, closing at $448.49. Over the past 52 weeks, Apple's lowest stock price was $385.10, and its highest was $705.07.
