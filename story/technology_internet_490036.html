Liberate thinking, abolish the mental obstacles to the growth of the brand "Tie Buya Yin"_8452

by bhhz0410 on 2011-09-16 09:18:35

After the boom and subsequent decline of Pu'er tea in 2005, China was left with a burgeoning large tea market. Following Pu'er, green tea, scented tea, black tea, and Tieguanyin tea all showed signs of movement, hoping to become the new dominant force in the tea market. Ultimately, luck favored Tieguanyin tea, which, with its unique taste and remarkable compatibility, successfully carried forward the domestic tea market fire ignited by Pu'er tea, further expanding it. Tieguanyin became the hottest tea of the new era, visible from the central government in Beijing down to the countryside towns. Even in northern border regions like Inner Mongolia and Xinjiang, Tieguanyin was popular.

If there was any regret amidst this fortune, it was that while the Tieguanyin tea industry flourished, brand building for Tieguanyin tea made no significant progress. To date, only a few companies such as Rinchun, Bama, Anxi Tieguanyin Group, Huaxiangyuan, and Huarong have annual outputs exceeding hundreds of millions. Only a handful of companies like Rinchun, Huaxiangyuan, Bama, Ideal, and Changhe have more than 100 retail outlets. The billions of yuan in annual output for Tieguanyin tea is not concentrated in the hands of powerful brands but divided among tens of thousands of tea companies and individual businesses.

Although during the rise of Tieguanyin tea, some tea companies attempted to build their brands to capture a larger market share, various inherent or acquired factors led to widespread failure. This not only wasted social capital but also dampened the ambitions of Tieguanyin tea companies to build strong brands. The most fatal issue was that almost everyone believed that the slow and lagging brand construction of Tieguanyin tea was mainly due to the low allocation of social capital in the Tieguanyin tea industry. As a sideline industry, over 99% of Tieguanyin tea companies lack substantial funds, advanced technology, and outstanding teams, making them a typical weak industry and weak enterprises. Under these circumstances, even with lofty ambitions and worldly plans, they were unable to realize them.

Less known is that beyond the obvious issue of unfavorable social capital allocation, there is a deeper problem: a significant obstacle in the mindset of Tieguanyin tea companies. If this mental obstacle is not removed, the hope for Tieguanyin tea companies to build strong brands cannot be realized. Regrettably, this mental obstacle is seen by everyone as the most successful model and experience, leading to widespread imitation, which poses a deep隐患for the brand-building efforts of Tieguanyin tea companies.

This mental obstacle is the "Tianfu Model." Tianfu Tea, with an annual output value close to 2 billion yuan, ranks at the forefront of the industry. Tianfu's success has been defined by tea companies nationwide as a benchmark for success, resulting in complete imitation. Naturally, Tieguanyin tea companies are no exception. However, this simple imitation has significant flaws, such as ignoring the attribute differences between tea, companies, and brands. Although Tianfu Tea is successful, not all of its successful experiences are applicable to all tea companies. For instance, in business operations, this model is clearly unsuitable for Tieguanyin tea companies in their early growth stages. Tianfu Tea places its retail stores in the first-tier commercial districts of each city, a typical retail-oriented business setup. Combined with Tianfu Tea's product structure (mainly green tea, with various types of tea) and brand positioning (common people's tea store, citizens' tea store), this retail model has great profit-creating power. However, Tieguanyin tea is entirely different. Although the Anxi County government hopes that Tieguanyin tea will become a tea that everyone can afford, the market often does not follow human will. Due to rising costs at various stages, Tieguanyin tea gradually evolved into the "noble" of the tea industry, becoming a luxury (high quality, high price, non-essential product). The consumer group for luxury goods is undoubtedly narrow and small. Selling Tieguanyin tea in stores aimed at the general public is akin to a significant waste. The high unit cost has far exceeded what Tieguanyin tea companies can bear. Compared to the meager retail profit, the high unit cost makes most Tieguanyin tea stores in commercial areas unsustainable. In the long run, most attempts at brand-building for Tieguanyin tea companies naturally fail.

Selling Tieguanyin tea with a retail mindset is a fatal mental obstacle. Tieguanyin tea has classic characteristics of gift consumption, personal consumption, and group consumption, with high unit value and long-term value. What Tieguanyin tea needs to pursue is not the quantity but the quality of consumers. A seasoned tea connoisseur or an enterprise could potentially generate sales worth hundreds of thousands or even millions. This feature is clearly different from retail daily necessities. In choosing retail locations, Tieguanyin tea should prioritize communication quality over foot traffic, focusing on point-to-point communication. Delivering products to customers or providing customized tea services should be the focus of Tieguanyin tea sales. Therefore, store locations should be suitable for areas where enterprises or high-end groups gather, such as CBD areas, high-end chain hotels, or around government offices. These areas have low rent but high single-store output, maximizing the reduction of store operation pressure and creating store operation profits.

Only by boldly liberating thoughts and removing mental obstacles can Tieguanyin tea companies survive in market competition and have the opportunity for sustainable brand accumulation, ultimately welcoming the spring of their brand!
