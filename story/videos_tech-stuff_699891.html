Last year, 90% of Chinese stock investors lost an average of 40,000 yuan each.

by aswee25 on 2012-03-09 16:52:33

Last year, 90% of Chinese stock investors lost an average of 40,000 yuan each, while institutional investment losses exceeded 40 billion yuan. This morning, the first plenary speech of the Fifth Session of the 11th National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference was held. Feng Peien, standing committee member of the CPPCC National Committee and former vice chairman of the Central Committee of the Jiusan Society, said that huge losses can easily provoke public resentment. He suggested linking the scale of financing and refinancing of listed companies, restructuring plans, operator compensation, and related preferential policies closely with cash dividend regulations, and forcing listed companies to fulfill their dividend obligations. In the morning, a total of 16 members of the CPPCC spoke.

The income gap between urban and rural residents is widening.

Suggestion: Reduce the proportion of indirect taxes and increase the proportion of direct taxes.

In 2000, the ratio of per capita income between urban and rural residents in our country was 2.85:1, reaching 3.45:1 in 2010. The widening income gap among the national population has become an increasingly prominent structural imbalance issue in the distribution field. For this, Liu Kugu, member of the CPPCC National Committee, former vice president of the China Development Bank, deputy director of the Academic Committee of the China International Economic Exchange Center, and chairman of the China Microfinance Institutions Liaison Association, suggested increasing the proportion of direct taxes to narrow the income gap among residents.

He suggested first reducing the proportion of indirect taxes and increasing the share of the household sector. It would be better to take the opportunity of the reform from business tax to value-added tax to reduce the proportion of indirect taxes and alleviate the tax burden on ordinary people.

Additionally, increasing the proportion of direct taxes can help narrow the income gap among residents. On the basis of quickly realizing deductions for basic living expenses for families, the coverage of individual income tax should be expanded. The rate difference should be widened, and the tax rate for the middle-income group should be reduced. The stock stamp duty rate should be appropriately reduced at the right time, and income tax should be restored for short-term stock transfers with certain deductions allowed. The role of property tax in adjusting the gap in residents' assets and property income should be played.

Information sharing should also be implemented. Information sharing systems with conditions, constraints, and control among tax, public security, industry and commerce, social security, housing, finance, and other departments should be realized as soon as possible, laying a technical foundation for fiscal and tax reforms and improving government efficiency.

To compete for GDP, there is serious duplication of construction in some places.

Suggestion: Do not use local GDP, investment, and other indicators as performance evaluation criteria for local governments.

A notable feature of economic development in China in the previous stage was administrative dominance by the government. This operational method could concentrate various resources to boost GDP, but in the new development stage, this operational method will result in the relationship between the government and the market being "inseparable," and the relationship between the central and local governments being "confused." For this, Song Xiaowu, member of the CPPCC National Committee and former deputy director of the State Council Northeast Revitalization Office, pointed out that the boundaries between the government and the market should be defined as soon as possible, and the fundamental role of the market in allocating resources should be played.

He said that the competition among local governments has achieved remarkable results, but it has also led to problems such as duplicate construction, overcapacity, resource waste, and environmental pollution. More seriously, local governments have become the main entities in resource allocation, blurring the boundaries between the government and the market, and providing a broad platform for money-power transactions.

Taking the GDP growth target in the "Twelfth Five-Year Plan" as an example, the central government expected 7%, while the weighted average of the targets of all provinces, cities, and regions was 10.5%, which was 50% higher than the central government's target. Such situations are widespread, and the responsibility does not lie with the localities, but rather with the relevant mechanisms and systems not meeting the requirements of transforming the development model. This indicates that reforming the mechanisms and systems is inevitable.

He said that defining the boundaries between the government, including the central and local governments, and the market involves many fields such as administrative systems, fiscal and taxation systems, foreign trade systems, land systems, distribution systems, price mechanisms, and social management. It is suggested to use the equalization of basic public services and the basic completion of public facilities as the main indicators to measure whether provinces, cities, and regions are developing in a coordinated manner. The GDP, investment, and other planning indicators of localities should be abolished as soon as possible, and these economic indicators should no longer be used as performance evaluation criteria for local governments, which will help ensure the uniformity of national policies and markets.

It is suggested to gradually concentrate the authority of basic public services appropriately and timely to the central government, where the central government will coordinate and balance the standards of basic public services in various places and establish a unified national guarantee mechanism for basic public service funds.

Being the first in manufacturing does not mean being an industrial power.

Suggestion: Accelerate the transformation and upgrading of industries, and the key is to scientifically and reasonably determine the development speed.

In 2010, our country became the world's largest manufacturing country. However, it is "big but not strong." Li Yizhong, member of the CPPCC National Committee, deputy director of the Economic Committee of the Eleventh CPPCC National Committee, and former minister of the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, said that currently, the R&D investment in our country's industry is insufficient, with R&D funds for large-scale industrial enterprises accounting for only 0.69% of operating revenue, while foreign large companies average more than 2% to 3%. Independent innovation is insufficient, and many technologies, materials, components, and complete sets of equipment still rely on imports. The concentration of some industries is not high, backward production capacity accounts for about 20%, and there is a lack of well-known brands. Generally speaking, we are still in the mid-to-low end of the global value chain.

Due to the lack of good transformation and upgrading, resources and the environment are difficult to meet. Li Yizhong said that in 2011, our country's crude oil import dependency exceeded 56%, iron ore imports reached 680 million tons, accounting for more than 60% of the world trade volume. Primary energy consumption reached approximately 3.5 billion tons of standard coal, with a growth rate of 7% still being high. Per unit GDP energy consumption is three to four times that of developed countries; sulfur dioxide, carbon dioxide emissions, and chemical oxygen demand rank among the highest in the world.

He said that to accelerate the transformation and upgrading of industries, the key is to scientifically and reasonably determine the development speed. A speed that is too low is not conducive to improving people's livelihoods and employment, affecting social stability; a speed that is too high cannot be supported by resources and the environment, and is not conducive to changing the development model. Moderately slowing down the growth rate allows more energy to be focused on eliminating backwardness, saving energy and reducing emissions, and developing strategic emerging industries.

It is necessary to do a good job in industrial layout, control overcapacity, and strictly control "two high" projects. Strategic emerging industries and productive service industries should be developed orderly, cultivating new growth points.

More human, material, and financial resources should be tilted towards technological renovation and the real economy.

Insufficient financial innovation may lead to risks.

Suggestion: Actively broaden the direct financing channels for enterprises.

Yang Kaisheng, member of the CPPCC National Committee, president, vice chairman, and deputy secretary of the Party Committee of the Industrial and Commercial Bank of China, said that slowing down the pace of financial innovation due to the recent international financial crisis is like giving up eating because of choking. Because insufficient financial innovation may lead to risks.

In 2011, the proportion of loans from various types of financial institutions in the scale of social financing in our country was as high as about 75%, while the proportion of corporate bonds and stock financing was only 14%. The imbalance in the structure of direct and indirect financing is very obvious.

"This situation reflects that our financial innovation is still insufficient from one side, the development level of the capital market is not high, and there are too few enterprise financing tools." Yang Kaisheng said that if we do not speed up innovation and fail to introduce more financing and investment tools, it will cause a large amount of credit risk to continuously accumulate in the banking system, which is not conducive to the sound operation of banks and will affect the sustainable development of the entire economy.

He suggested actively broadening the direct financing channels for enterprises. Decisively solve the problem of multi-departmental approval and supervision for enterprise bond issuance, expand the scale of corporate bond issuance. Allow bank loans to be securitized, through securitization allow bank loans to be transferred and traded, so that the total amount of bank assets no longer grows infinitely and thus have sustainable loan issuance capabilities. As for the risks that may exist, they can be prevented and controlled through technical design.

More than 100 Wenzhou bosses "ran away"

Suggestion: Open up channels for private capital to enter the financial sector

"A Wenzhou enterprise with a thousand or so workers, after hard work and careful calculation, made a profit of just over a million yuan in a year, while the wife of the enterprise boss bought ten houses in Shanghai and made a profit of 30 million yuan effortlessly after eight years." The above example given by Hu Chengzhong, member of the CPPCC National Committee and standing committee member of the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce, during his speech at the conference was heart-wrenching. He said that the development crisis faced by small and medium-sized enterprises has reached a critical stage.

He said that since April last year, more than 100 Wenzhou bosses have "run away" due to broken capital chains. According to data published by the Zhejiang Provincial Bureau of Statistics, in the first half of last year, 14,447 small and medium-sized enterprises in Zhejiang Province went bankrupt and were deregistered. He suggested that financial institutions "release water to nurture fish" and coexist with enterprises. The interest rate spread of our country's banks exceeds 3%, far higher than the average level in Western countries, and the per capita net profit is more than 12 times that of industrial enterprises. Banks need to lower loan interest rates, control the floating space, not forcibly bundle wealth management products and retain deposits compulsorily, optimize the loan structure, increase the proportion of medium- and long-term loans, and support the technological transformation and upgrading of manufacturing.

He suggested breaking industry monopolies and opening private banks. Private manufacturing enterprises have narrow financing channels and narrow investment channels, resulting in narrow survival and development space. Government departments need to formulate detailed implementation rules as soon as possible to broaden the industrial development space for manufacturing enterprises and open up channels for private capital to enter the financial sector.

"Currently, it is estimated that several trillion yuan of private capital in the country is not well used for the development of the real economy, but is instead being used for real estate speculation, coal speculation, gold and silver speculation, and art and antiques speculation." He said that it might be better to develop private banks and specialized lending institutions, "recruit" private capital, solve the financing difficulties of small and medium-sized private enterprises, and lower loan interest rates through competition, forming a positive interaction and common development between the development of the real economy and private capital.

Last year, the Shanghai and Shenzhen stock markets led the decline in global stock markets.

Suggestion: Compel listed companies to fulfill their dividend obligations.

Last year, the Shanghai and Shenzhen stock markets fell by 21% and 28% respectively, leading the decline in global stock markets. Among 50 million Chinese stock investors, 87% suffered losses, with an average loss of 40,000 yuan per person, and institutional investment losses exceeded 40 billion yuan. Feng Peien, standing committee member of the CPPCC National Committee and former vice chairman of the Central Committee of the Jiusan Society, said that huge losses can easily provoke public resentment, and measures to rescue the market should be completed as soon as possible through institutional reforms.

In 20 years, the total amount of cash dividends given to stock investors in the A-share market only accounted for 17% of the total financing, lower than the transaction costs of stock investors, so stock investors can only profit from price fluctuations in the secondary market stocks.

Feng Peien suggested revising relevant laws and regulations, requiring listed companies to clearly state return and cash dividend plans in their prospectuses, linking the scale of financing and refinancing, restructuring plans, operator compensation, and related preferential policies of listed companies closely with cash dividend regulations, and compelling listed companies to fulfill their dividend obligations.

At the same time, implement a strict delisting system to optimize the allocation of stock market resources. The NASDAQ stock market reduced the number of listed companies by 13.08% over three years, while in our country, only 1.82% of listed companies have been delisted in 21 years.

In 2011, 1,264 executives resigned or left their positions in the A-share market, an increase of 68% compared to the same period last year. The enrichment of executives through resignation and cashing out hinders the steady development of the stock market. Feng Peien suggested formulating relevant laws and regulations stipulating that company directors, supervisors, and executives shall not transfer more than 10% of the total shares they hold in the company each year during their term of office; executives who sell shares must disclose relevant information two months in advance; prohibit directors, supervisors, presidents, and chairmen from working in listed companies within three years after resigning and cashing out, and from working in non-listed companies within one year; impose a high capital gains tax on executives selling shares to cash out; directors, supervisors, and executives shall not transfer the shares they hold in the company within one year after leaving their positions.

Land sale revenues need to be properly managed.

Suggestion: Conduct audits on the use of land sale revenues over the past five or ten years.

In 2010, the total proceeds from land sales nationwide amounted to 2.7 trillion yuan, with Beijing earning 163.85 billion yuan and Shanghai 15.27 billion yuan. Over the five years from 2006 to 2010, the total land sale revenue nationwide reached more than 7 trillion yuan. Is it time to properly manage the "feast" of the domestic land market? This morning, Lu Budong, member of the CPPCC National Committee, member of the Central Committee of the China Democratic建国Party, and deputy head of the Zhejiang Provincial Committee, directly addressed how to better use the enormous "land sale" revenue in his speech.

Lu Budong pointed out that due to the lack of effective supervision over the use of land sale revenue, its use has been arbitrary and lacks necessary principles. For example, a municipal government agreed to prioritize the increased income from the development of two plots of land over the next ten years for repaying the capital of a certain state-owned enterprise debt.

Secondly, corruption cases are frequently seen. An audit by the Audit Office showed that among the 84.826 billion yuan of non-tax revenue that should have been included in budget management but was not, 62.642 billion yuan came from land sale revenue in ten provincial-level regions, 23 city-level regions, and 41 counties.

More importantly, this can be detrimental to the sustainable development of cities, causing a "temporary prosperity at the expense of the future" in cities. Land is generally sold for 40 to 70 years, and this future income is used for current various constructions. However, land will eventually run out, and when that day comes, what else can we sell once houses, factories, and land are sold?

He suggested conducting audits on the use of land sale revenue over the past five or ten years. After more than twenty years of land sales, how much have these land sale revenues accumulated? Where have they been used? Are the uses reasonable? How much has been used for the benefit of ordinary people? What is the proportion according to the notice issued by the General Office of the State Council? These figures should not be a mess, but should be clearly communicated to society and the public.
