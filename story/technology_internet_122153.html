The Business of Selling Traffic

1. Nielsen, the world's most reliable third-party consulting company, partnered with China's least reliable pig to create CR-Nielsen. The foreign monk became a playboy monk in China and recently released some unreliable data: "Summary of the 2008 Annual Chinese Internet Advertising Market."

The data points out (see the figure above): In the internet industry, 9 companies entered the TOP50 advertiser rankings, namely Jiayuan.com, ChinaHR.com, Zhaopin.com, Perfect World, VANCL, Alibaba, Giant Interactive, eBay, and Zhuangdian.com. Their advertising expenditures were respectively 480 million yuan, 300 million yuan, 270 million yuan, 1.1 billion yuan, 1 billion yuan, 910 million yuan, 820 million yuan, 730 million yuan, and 640 million yuan.

2. Thus, Mai Tian noticed an interesting point in the data: Zhuangdian.com's advertising expenditure in 2008 was 640 million yuan, and he reprinted Chu Mingli's article "PPG of the Cosmetics Industry - Zhuangdian.com." Huang Zhiguang, who had specifically visited Zhuangdian.com in Nanchang, also wrote a summary guide titled "Talking about Zhuangdian.com." In fact, more than half a year ago on Paidai, someone complained that one of Zhuangdian.com's sub-sites was a scam, and He Tian replied saying Zhuangdian.com was an "aircraft carrier + plane."

3. In my view, the business models of U88.cn, Xiangguo, PPG, Zhuangdian, and even my friend Keso who bought hundreds of thousands of keywords on Google, are all essentially businesses of selling traffic, just differing in how they sell the traffic or handle it once it arrives. Let us examine these businesses:

4. U88-type:

At the end of 2003 and beginning of 2004, this model of U88 had already started. Our idea at the time was very simple: portal site advertisements have great effects but are very expensive, and many chain franchise projects were scared off by the cost when they approached advertising agencies. Thus, a business opportunity arose: "buy one ad link and divide it among several chain projects."

Then, numerous ad companies began using this model: create a webpage similar to a million grids listing various franchise projects, buy a homepage ad link from the portals to drive traffic (similar to "Top Ten Enrichment Projects of 2009," etc., during their peak, 80% of the text ad links on the homepage were these), then start selling the grids. At the time, those purchased for 100,000 yuan could be resold for 300,000 yuan.

Gradually, as the effect of portal ads diminished, buying traffic expanded from portals to search engines, and even to television. Finally, it evolved into today's U88.cn, 3158.cn, 288.com, 78.cn, 8588.com, and so on (the funniest part being U88.cn hiring Andy Lau as their spokesperson, while 3158.cn hired Emperor Qianlong, playing around quite joyfully, and then got hit by CCTV).

5. Oak International: His fate was directly determined by the people who sold him the traffic, and there were others surpassing him with lower costs or deeper operations (selling directly themselves).

PPG: PPG relied on telephone ordering. Early on, after laying down a large number of ads, significant results emerged. But once the ads stopped, there was no business, continuously burning money in a vicious cycle... Too reliant on telephone orders without truly utilizing the website's potential. Eventually, before reaching the stage where ads were unnecessary for self-sufficiency, major internal issues arose.

My friend Keso's business: Initially, a large amount of Google keywords were purchased. As business came in, customer resources were retained on the website. The more repeat customers, the less need for ads. Gradually, even without keyword purchases, the business was sufficient. If there was no business, continue buying keywords, repeating the cycle and slowly accumulating resources... Until one day, basically no advertising is needed, the brand and customers have emerged, and it can even become an export platform or a larger scale B2C export business.

6. Whether all businesses of this type can ultimately succeed depends on how they handle the "selling traffic" model.

If this model is regarded as the core forever, buying traffic and distributing it only to make money, this business can be determined to be like a rabbit's tail, not lasting long; if this model is treated as a necessary means during the early stages of the business, attracting people and business in the early stages, gradually accumulating people and necessary resources during the process of selling traffic, then there's hope.
