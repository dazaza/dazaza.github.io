Gallery: The Best Astronomical Photographs of 2013

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 21:02:43

In recent years, the pace of humanity's exploration of outer space has been accelerating. Mars rovers have given us our first real look at the "Red Planet," and the Voyager 1 spacecraft flying beyond the solar system has provided humanity with its first opportunity to gather information from a broader expanse of the cosmos, thus making communication between cosmic civilizations possible. For most people, however, they are more accustomed to standing on the ground and gazing up at the beautiful and mysterious starry sky.

Recently, thousands of professional astrophotographers from around the world have entered their best works in the competition for the title of Astronomical Photographer of the Year. Below are the top 13 most outstanding works, capturing various wondrous celestial phenomena.

The Milky Way in the southern night sky

The photograph of the Milky Way in the southern night sky taken by Australian astrophotographer Mark Gee won the top prize. This photo was taken at Cape Palliser in New Zealand.

The image shows the Milky Way, a celestial scene that many astrophotographers love to capture. The cluster of stars near the center of the Milky Way is approximately 26,000 light-years away from us. The night sky, the Milky Way, the sea, the coast, and the cliffs together form this beautiful picture.

Auroras in Norway

The second place in this year’s contest went to an aurora photographed in Norway, captured by Fredrik Broms. The collision of charged particles from the sun with the Earth's upper atmosphere forms the stunning auroras resembling colorful ribbons in the night sky, contrasting beautifully with the snowy landscape below.

Comet over the snow-capped mountain

This photograph was also taken by Fredrik Broms, showing Comet C/2011 L4 PANSTARRS as it passed over a snow-covered mountain in Norway.

Perseid meteor shower

In mid-August, the Perseid meteor shower visited Earth, and it didn't escape the lenses of astrophotographers. British photographer David Kingham captured them. The night sky, mountains, cliffs, and the long trails of meteors correspond, displaying a "cold" tone.

Snowy scenery under a moon halo

The Mid-Autumn Festival in China has just passed, but abroad, people also love the tranquility brought by moonlight. A Spanish photographer combined moonlight with snow, pushing this feeling to its extreme.

Galaxy 12 million light-years away

Our solar system is not alone in the universe. American photographer Ivan Eder captured a stunning image of the M81 and M82 galaxies, which are about 12 million light-years away from Earth.

"Dark" gas from the universe

There are many mysteries in the universe, including the enigmatic dark matter. Photographer Tom O'Donoghue captured an image combining Antares and the Heart nebula, containing much "dark" gas material.

Omega Centauri Cluster

Below is a photograph taken by Ignacio Diaz Bobillo in Argentina, showing the Omega Centauri globular cluster composed of millions of stars.

Breathtaking total solar eclipse

A total solar eclipse is a rare and awe-inspiring celestial phenomenon. Many dream of capturing a perfect total solar eclipse photo. Now, an Australian photographer has achieved it, with this image originating from a total solar eclipse in 2012.

Transit of Venus

The transit of Venus is an extremely spectacular celestial event. We can admire this phenomenon through the high-end equipment and excellent shooting skills of photographer Sam Cornwell.

People working under the moon

The winning photographer Mark Gee brings us another work where people work under the moonlight, creating silhouettes that convey a unique meaning.

Annular solar eclipse process

This is a remarkable piece where photographer Jia Hao records the entire process of an annular solar eclipse in one photograph, looking both stunning and beautiful.

Milky Way and snow

Photographer Ben Canales cleverly combines the Milky Way with the snow. The figure in the photo stands amidst the "infinite" land and sky, expressing a primitive natural beauty.
