The fading traces of time

Snow fell continuously for several days, drifting down in abundance and without end.

I opened the door, and I, who hadn't been outside for a long time, was stunned by the sight before me. In front of my eyes, the mountains and rivers were all covered with thick blankets of snow, a white expanse stretching endlessly, creating a crystal-like fairy tale world. The mountaintops, tree branches, rooftops, roads, even the steps of my home had become loyal fans of the snow, completely enveloped in its whiteness, losing their original appearance and fully indulging in this pure world.

The snow continued to fall, but now it was snow grains, falling one by one, handfuls at a time, from the overcast sky. Hitting the snowy ground, they made a rustling sound. This clear rustling sound I had listened to by the window, I had appreciated quietly in my dreams; it seemed to come from a distant heaven, traveling far to resonate around us, drifting through our dreams, settling into that most pristine beauty.

I gazed at the snow on the steps, each piece crystal clear, like lively and adorable children, now sleeping in the embrace of Mother Earth, enjoying a rare peace. A ripple of joy passed through my heart, rippling with happiness that hadn't surfaced in a long time. Suddenly, I felt an impulse and quickly stepped onto the snowy ground, jumping happily on the pristine white snow.

I was like a dancer, showcasing my dance moves on this white stage, again and again. My body, which hadn't moved much for a long time, was now radiating youthful vitality, fully displaying the energy of youth. On the snow-covered ground, like a blanket, there sounded a series of thuds and squeaks, leaving behind gray-black footprints, one after another, like leaping notes harmonizing with the dancing snowflakes, flying freely in the beautiful heaven.

The snow grains were playful, hitting my head, sliding into my neck, covering us like enthusiastic friends vying to embrace me. Instantly, a refreshing chill stirred on the surface of my skin, then disappeared instantly, leaving only boundless comfort. The rustling sound of the snow falling, the squeaking sound of my footsteps on the snow, intertwined together, forming a moving melody, echoing in this tranquil world, also resonating in my calm heart. Perhaps only at this moment, in this scenery, could I have the heart to appreciate such a scene. Suppressing the joy that was about to overflow, I turned back, and the beautiful footprints came into view. In the deep snow, they were so disordered yet so prominent, like a naive painting drawn by some ignorant child, entering the gaze of the world, also leaping into my heart.

I know, I haven't done this for a long time. Maybe only those who possess innocence and joy would add their clumsy brushstrokes to nature's masterpiece.

I remember particularly liking winter when I was younger, eagerly anticipating the snowflakes of winter. As soon as the snow stopped, the children in our village would spontaneously burst out of their homes, gathering in the yard to build snowmen, have snowball fights, pick icicles, busily enjoying ourselves. Amid the snowfall, along with the wind and the sound of snow hitting the ground, echoed our carefree laughter. Despite the cold, with our hands frozen red, we paid no heed; despite our parents forbidding us from going out, we acted secretly: in our childhood, we would go all out for whatever we wanted to do, regardless of consequences or others' opinions. As long as we were happy, that was enough. Thus, the originally beautiful snowfield was messed up by us, and the originally quiet environment became lively because of us. We were the most active spirits between heaven and earth, blooming with the joy and innocence of childhood in the white world.

But as time passed, with the passage of years, I feel myself getting further and further away from innocence, further and further away from nature. When it snows, I can only stand by the window, admiring it from afar, isolating myself while watching. That joy only shows on my face; what my parents say is always right, and I follow the path they have laid out for me without complaint, hoping to achieve the person they and I imagine in our hearts; old companions gradually lose touch, each person on their own path of growth, lonely and arduously trekking in one direction, placing past joys deep within their memories. Our footprints disappear from nature, our lively figures fade away, our joyful laughter is locked within our own worlds, firmly bound by aspirations for a certain place.

I don't know what exactly we've lost in the tide of time. I don't know if it's merely the footprints of growth? Those indelible marks? The innocence of childhood, the purity of childish fun, the beauty of childhood has gradually faded away. When our hearts are blank and we strive to search in the river of memory, fortunately, we can still find the traces left behind, together with them, crossing the chasm of time, advancing towards a future that doesn't belong to tomorrow.

Perhaps, my choice today is right. After not stepping out for days, I have appreciated the charm of nature and possessed the wondrous grace of winter. More importantly, I am dancing in the embrace of nature. I am letting my spirit fly in the snowy world, bringing back those once-forgotten traces, propelling myself towards places full of childish fun, and etching unique marks on the long road of life ahead.
