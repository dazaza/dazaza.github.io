Human-Centric Design, Achieving Ultimate Comfort for Villas

As the quality of living improves, villa owners have become more specific in their requirements for living: through functional design that aligns with the lifestyle of the residents, personalized solutions created by understanding the residents, and the design of psychological space and atmosphere to meet the deeper emotional and spiritual needs of the residents; it aims at living as its purpose, still adhering to the "human-centric" design concept, prioritizing comfort everywhere.

How to achieve human-centric design, Ti Ma He Villa experts suggest:

1. Ordinary apartments often integrate multiple functions into one space, such as combining the audio-visual room, reception hall, and even the dining room into the living room. However, villa spaces are clearly divided, each function being independent. The exclusivity of each area should be utilized to the fullest extent, only then can the villa become a place to gain freedom and relax the mind.

2. Although villa renovation styles are mainly European classical and country-themed, when renovating, the quality of life must always come first, considering whether it aligns with living habits and preferences.

3. For the elderly and children living in villas, pay attention to the following issues: avoid ground height differences, ensure corridor width, focus on shape design, make door and window opening convenient, provide adequate and uniform lighting, reduce temperature differences between rooms, keep the distance between the bedroom and bathroom close, install handrails, pay attention to contact surface materials, ensure equipment safety and ease of operation, set up emergency call devices, and make the height of storage spaces suitable.

Livable Craftsmanship, Perfect Living Quality

From a certain perspective, "withstanding the test of time" is the way of life for villa living. Even the most meticulous designs and the most luxurious styles will become empty talk without construction craftsmanship and attention to detail quality. For villas, whose construction scale is several times larger than that of apartments, the quality of hidden engineering becomes even more important. Whether it's the water supply and drainage system, electrical wiring projects, or the base treatment of ceilings, floors, and walls, villa renovations differ due to their unique architectural properties and usage patterns.

The suspended floor treatment makes villa life private and quiet, free from external disturbances; the internal wall insulation system keeps the indoor warm and comfortable even after the heating is turned off overnight; the soundproofing process for bathroom pipes eliminates the annoying sound of the toilet flushing... Each craft enhances the quality of the villa brilliantly.
