Precautions for the treatment of anorectal disease patients_Zhengzhou Anorectal Disease Hospital

by anonymous on 2011-04-17 11:20:18

1. Before the operation, please have the patient describe in detail their medical history, allergy history, and whether they have a scar-prone constitution for the doctor's reference.

2. On the day of the surgery, the patient is required to stay in the hospital for about 2 hours of observation. They can leave only after the doctor permits it.

3. Post-surgery daily life is generally unaffected. Pay attention to rest and avoid strenuous activities; diet should avoid spicy and stimulating foods, and it is advisable to consume more vegetables, bananas, yogurt, and other foods that aid digestion.

4. Maintain smooth bowel movements. Bowel movement time should be as short as possible, ideally controlled within 1-3 minutes. Avoid prolonged squatting and excessive force, which may cause anal swelling. After defecation, immediately rinse with running water and apply medication.

5. Post-surgery, difficulty in urination may occur. Warm compresses on the lower abdomen or the sound of running water may help induce urination. If difficulties persist, contact the doctor for appropriate treatment.

6. Post-surgery, there may be slight bleeding during bowel movements due to the wound, which will gradually disappear after a week. There’s no need to worry.

7. After treatment, patients may experience a feeling of heaviness in the lower abdomen and discomfort in the anus. Some patients may experience urinary retention, which usually disappears within a few hours. If necessary, one-time catheterization may be given.

8. Some patients may notice blue urine, which is a normal phenomenon caused by the excretion of anesthetic pain-relief drugs through the bladder. There’s no need to worry.

9. Some patients may experience re-bleeding during defecation, which is due to slight oozing from the wound when the hemorrhoid scab falls off. Standing up afterward stops the bleeding, which is a normal phenomenon. If the bleeding is significant, seek immediate follow-up at the hospital.

10. Remove corruption, promote muscle growth, stop bleeding, and reduce inflammation: The area around the anus is prone to bacterial growth, which may lead to infection post-treatment, affecting recovery speed and causing unnecessary complications. Follow the doctor's advice and use medication appropriately to prevent infection.

11. Red light therapy: Effectively improves local microcirculation, accelerates tissue repair, quickly clears local inflammation such as redness, swelling, heat, and pain, promotes the absorption of inflammation, and inhibits and kills pathogens and microorganisms.

12. Regular dressing changes: After treatment, follow the doctor's instructions to come to the hospital regularly for dressing changes and follow-ups. Keep dressings dry and clean to prevent infection.

13. The healing period for post-surgical wounds is approximately three weeks. During this time, activity should be minimized as much as possible. Generally, within 7-25 days after surgery, bleeding due to scab detachment is not directly related to the surgery but rather to individual differences (hypertension, arteriosclerosis, coagulation disorders, etc.). Minor bleeding will not cause serious consequences. In case of heavy bleeding, seek immediate treatment at our hospital or a nearby hospital. Failure to handle promptly may lead to hemorrhagic shock, endangering life.

14. After PPH surgery, patients often feel heaviness and frequent urges to defecate. It is important to regulate the diet, avoiding spicy, stimulating foods and alcohol.

15. Sitz bath: A simple and effective method to clean the anus, promote wound healing, and reduce inflammation. A sitz bath must be taken after each bowel movement and before sleeping, lasting about 15 minutes. Initially, use Chinese herbal detoxification soup, and later use warm salt water or diluted potassium permanganate powder for sitz baths while strengthening anal sphincter muscle function exercises.

16. Depending on individual differences (such as chronic diseases, diabetes history, etc.), healing may be delayed, leading to scar contraction, and even anal stenosis or incontinence. Timely visit the hospital for treatments like anal dilation.

17. If the patient develops other conditions unrelated to anal or intestinal diseases post-surgery, these are not associated with the surgery itself. Please consult the relevant department promptly to avoid delaying treatment.

18. All information provided is for educational purposes only and should not replace professional medical advice. Always consult your healthcare provider for personalized care.
