• Measuring the "safety factor" of car floor mats

An increasing number of cases involving car floor mats causing trouble has led people to also care about the safety factor of these seemingly insignificant car floor mats. Industry experts point out that car floor mats affect normal driving safety mainly in several aspects. When selecting them, emphasis should be placed on evaluating the quality of the car floor mats from these aspects.

1. Whether the car floor mats are prone to slipping

If the size of the car floor mats is either too large or too small, it may cause the mats to shift during braking. If your pedals get caught or hooked by the car floor mats, it might trigger a traffic accident. It's not hard to imagine that in a high-speed moving vehicle, if an emergency event occurs, the driver can avoid an accident by stepping on the brakes. However, at this critical moment, if the car floor mats shift or slip, resulting in the driver being unable to brake in time, the probability of an accident happening would undoubtedly be very high. Generally, the reasons for car floor mats slipping are two-fold: one is that the size of the mats is unsuitable, easily causing misalignment; another reason is that ordinary car floor mats usually have flat bottoms, making them prone to slipping when stepped on. Some car floor mats add "anti-slip nails" at the bottom, but due to manufacturing processes, these so-called "anti-slip nails" are hard objects that do not possess good grip, thus they may still slip during use. Therefore, when selecting, it is recommended to choose car floor mats with specially designed soft anti-slip nails (with good grip) at the bottom, which will not slip even under heavy pressure. Car floor mats with buckle anti-slip features undoubtedly have dual anti-slip effects. You can observe the adhesive backing of the car floor mats; ordinary products mostly have foam backing glue, which is prone to tearing, cracking, and slipping. In such cases, apart from easily damaging the product, it also poses significant safety hazards while driving. On the other hand, the adhesive backing of high-quality car floor mats is made of specially designed eco-friendly rubber anti-slip soft nails, making them safer and more durable.

2. Whether the car floor mats contain toxic substances

Among the various types of car floor mats, some use cheap materials and add chemical substances like chlorinated paraffin. Therefore, as soon as you open the product, it emits a strong, pungent rubber smell, especially in high-temperature environments where the odor is particularly intense. Long-term inhalation of these harmful odors has a great impact on human health.
