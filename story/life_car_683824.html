Generally, cars are rented and returned in the same place. This is called the round way, and renting a car this way is cheaper. If you rent in one place and return it in another, there will be an additional fee for returning the car in a different location. I don't know how this fee is calculated either. In California, many places just add $10 in total. For example, last time we rented a car in San Diego and returned it at the Los Angeles airport, it only cost an extra $10. However, this time, we rented a car in Washington DC and returned it in Boston, and they charged us over $200.

We waited for a long time and the price remained the same. I don't know if I was scammed, but I gritted my teeth and accepted it. I personally feel that the car rental prices on the East Coast are more expensive than in California. Also, picking up a car at the airport is generally more expensive than picking it up elsewhere - about $10 more per day, but it's more convenient. I have a friend who picked up a car at the airport (news.wodingche.com), drove it to the hotel, and returned it at a nearby car rental place after about a day; then he rented another car from that place and switched. He said this would save a lot of money if you're out for a long time (around 20 days). Yeah, as long as you're not afraid of trouble and have the time to sort it out.
