Sing a love song, to myself

Recently, I have been indulged in Annie's novels and can't extricate myself. I follow her words into despair, filth, decadence, and shame, dying over and over again. Holding the P5 that contains her novels, I curl up in a corner, reading, feeling sad, and then face the solitary clouds outside the window, letting the light pierce my eyes as I fall sound asleep peacefully. After waking up, I frantically flip through the pages of the book, searching for a blank sheet of paper, intending to write down the stillness that lingers in my heart, but in vain. In the end, there is only a pen, suspended in mid-air, unable to find a foothold. That is the posture of disappointment. Because of the lack of a piece of paper to scribble on. I always sit dazedly by the window, contemplating the thrill of jumping off. But I am not one of the female protagonists in Annie's novels who hold the dagger of love in the dark and stab themselves.

I am not lonely enough, disappointed enough, dark enough, or fallen enough. Behind the madness of my words, there is still a warm face. I think I am still a woman with my palm half-upward, occasionally looking up and letting the sky pour into my eyes, but I am not lonely when I look up at the sky and see birds flying by. I am just thinking, thinking about youth gradually shedding its innocence, I am just thinking, thinking about life gradually losing its passion. And we, walking, passing by, laughing, hurting, in the end, only the loneliness in the corners of our eyes remain. How much longer will time last, for us to reminisce or recall? Suddenly, looking at those once radiant faces that are now aging, I start to constantly fear death, fear passing away. Afraid that after a moment of warmth "entwined" with you, all I can touch in the end is your cold skin. Can you love me forever?

Recently, my emotions have been flooding like a breached dam. Watching a touching scene in a TV drama or movie, my heart involuntarily clenches and feels sad, and finally, my eyes are filled with hot tears. Every time, I mock my own ignorance and foolishness. I want to tell everyone around me, in this season where autumn lingers, that I have grown up. Growing up in words. Learning to neurotically lay out my emotions in one white area after another. Those red fonts. The color of blood. Blinding. That is the vitality of life. Those who do not understand the colors of my words always make me sad. But I realize that in the twisted curves of my tone, there are too many emotions in my heart that cannot be easily 'desecrated' by others. Growing up, emotions overflowing, but I never think that simplicity after growing up is a happy thing. I love myself wholeheartedly, loving my heart that has been refined by fire - depressed yet powerful. Yet loyal to the enduring flow of emotions.

Regarding the future, we are all striving forward. We begin to continuously restrain our surging madness, determinedly splashing it around. I am afraid how long my career can depend on, so I keep 'repenting'. Everyone seems to be preparing to leave, sitting together and discussing, always unintentionally mentioning gatherings, eating, karaoke, taking pictures. Everyone is lingering and reluctant to part. How long will it take before we can pick up the craziness of the past and attend the banquet of youth together? Perhaps, after passing through this phase of reluctance, those once 'sharp' faces and piercing laughter will be stored in the freezer and become spoiled or rotten. But, those passersby, will not be immortal. Because memories are too full. What will permanently remain is the deepest pain of love in the bottom of our hearts. Loved, hurt, hated, trying to forget what cannot be forgotten, that is eternity.

Now, I am still sitting in the warm, cozy dormitory, wearing loose pajamas, repeatedly listening to every emotional love song, inhaling the fresh scent emitted by my hanging hair, speaking bit by bit on the only bright screen in the darkness. I think, I will miss my current self. Comfortable and content, holding onto my own happiness. Occasionally, on some morning, a roommate might say lazily: "Hey, not sleeping in the middle of the night, still indulging." I would proudly tell them, turning my lazy body: "I like it." In the future, I don't know if this "I like" can withstand the trials of life, love, career, and family, leaving only the shell behind.

Have liked, have possessed, can it be called eternal?

The remaining warmth in my palm, the residual heat in my heart. Repeatedly mortgaged to memories. Flipping back and forth, it's no more than life and death. After death, the corpse becomes a memory, burned to ashes.

So, there is no eternity in life, only the past and possession. Possessing is eternity.

Therefore, treat each day as the last day to love, support, and cherish each other. At least, I will try. Strive to build more crystalline love on the foundation of possession. Because, I fear losing before possessing enough. To those I love, I always overflow my emotions with you, and then use the magic of words to give you love. I just want the silent words to brush across the most stirring initial intentions in my heart to let you know, in memories, you are warm. This is my favorite way. Then what about me, how do you remember me, with a smile or in silence?

We have all been unique in someone else's world. Thank you to those who once considered us unique. Thank you for saying uniqueness cannot be replaced.

Every step in the future leaves a footprint. Whether it's 'pleasant' or 'bloody'.

Please remember, we have possessed, been unique, irreplaceable, and immortal.

That is eternity.

The bleak autumn posture, refreshing at the end of October, flows gently.

Accompanied by drizzling rain and gentle breeze.

We scribble in the thick mist of autumn.
