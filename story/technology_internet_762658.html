Zhengzhou workers outside the production line

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 16:36:53

Labor costs are a major part of corporate expenditure. To cut labor costs, profit-driven enterprises either lay off employees or relocate to places with lower salary levels. Foxconn is doing just that - moving from Shenzhen to Zhengzhou, which has gradually become a new manufacturing hub. In this place, Chinese young workers refuse to be worker ants. Nearby the factory, there’s a bar where people of all sorts come at night to listen to explosive disco music and sway their bodies. Lian Yulong (音), a 19-year-old inspector who works on iPhone motherboards at Foxconn, is a regular customer at the bar. "Dancing helps me vent my anger and stress. Whenever I'm here, I forget everything else." He uses dancing to forget the monotonous and dull reality of his daytime life.

An increasing number of recreational spaces attract workers back to the assembly line. These workers manufacture goods that others desire while enduring hardships themselves. Due to the rare opportunities, their rest time is extremely precious. Now, they endure the present for a better future. Every worker gets excited about the weekend, even though some of their plans are simple. For example, Bai Si Hai (音), a 24-year-old worker, plans to indulge in online gaming and then call his girlfriend far away.

Recently, supervisors in the factory have started to see the benefits of vacations. In recent years, "worker suicides" have been an inescapable shadow over companies like Foxconn and Samsung's factories. After several reports of "sweatshops," facing external pressure, Foxconn improved the workers' treatment and promised not to make them work overtime. However, after another suicide incident at the Zhengzhou factory, Foxconn initiated a "silent mode," disallowing workers from discussing non-work topics during working hours. Although Foxconn claims to have canceled this rule under external scrutiny, workers say that everything remains the same as before.

The domestic factory environment cannot compare with those abroad. Foreign workers enjoy high allowances and various benefits and entertainment such as haircuts, rock climbing walls, table football, etc., and their offices are luxurious. Domestic workers wear uniform uniforms and listen to routine scolding and shouting from their supervisors. As for dormitories, a room contains eight hard metal bed frames and a combined bathroom and toilet room with nothing more.

Perhaps because of this, there is a large-scale market in front of the factory gate. When the sun sets, Mandarin pop music drifts out from the hair salon, and street vendors sell DVDs, sliced watermelons, and roses whose shine is dimmed by silver light. A flatbed truck carries different colored teddy bears, drawing the attention of young girls. Some boyfriends, at the request of their girlfriends, immediately take out 10 yuan Renminbi to buy one. At this market, there are vans with artistic tattoos and large video games. Young workers drink light beer while continuously smoking cigarettes.

Sometimes, elaborately dressed singers will perform on nearby stages. As for the youth, they find sufficient entertainment in liquor. Luo Haojie (音) left the factory in May, quitting his job making iPhone 5 components. He said, "Our factory supervisor is vicious." Speaking to a circle of drinking friends, he complained about the unbearable food in the factory cafeteria.

Ultimately, he will look for another job. But for now, he enjoys his youth, drinking with friends and dating girls. Luo said, "I stayed because of my brothers. Without them, I would be miserable."

Summer is the low season for Chinese factories, allowing many workers to enjoy holidays on weekends, sometimes even double days off. The variety of entertainment near the factory satisfies their needs. At night, mobile food stalls resembling KFC are set up. Acrobats perform, someone blows fireballs. Workers say it would be better if you came a few months earlier when the acrobatic troupe had real lions and tigers.

On Saturday, Zhou Pengzheng (音), another iPhone 5 motherboard tester, wearing roller skates worth thousands of Renminbi, carefully avoided plants and began spinning. These skates cost one-third of his monthly salary. He said, "It feels like I'm flying." Before the crowd gathered, he quickly skated another round.

Quad skates and roller skates have gradually formed a cultural atmosphere among those striving at Foxconn. Several teams have been formed, and on weekends, they skate through the city. Fang Tim Ma (音), 17, joined a hundred-person skating team named "Shadow" shortly after starting work at Foxconn. However, due to her age, she couldn't work overtime and left the factory. She said, "I used to come to the skating rink twice a week, now I come every night."

At 11 PM, the performers have left, and clock hotels begin to get busy. "We work every day, why not go on dates?"
