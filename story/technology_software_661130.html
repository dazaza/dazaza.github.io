Flower of Italy Sweet Sparkling Wine The Flower of Italy sparkling wine presents an alluring light wheat-yellow hue, offering a visually pleasing experience. The bubbles are not only elegant but also long-lasting. Its aroma is fresh and rich with fruity scents. Upon tasting, one can feel waves of fruity fragrance and sweetness, with subtle hints of honey. This fully showcases the characteristics of the Malvasia grape variety: rich and open aromas, low acidity, and high sweetness. It pairs easily with meals and is suitable for various seafood dishes, or can be enjoyed on its own. It's particularly ideal for entry-level wine enthusiasts and female wine lovers.

The Flower of Italy Sparkling Wine (Dry) is produced in Emilia-Romagna, a region located in central Italy. To the north lies the Po River, to the southwest the Apennine mountain ridge line, and to the east the Adriatic Sea. It includes the provinces of Bologna, Ferrara, Forlì, Modena, Parma, Piacenza, Ravenna, and Reggio nell'Emilia. Covering an area of 22,100 square kilometers, the terrain is higher in the southwest and lower in the northeast, featuring large areas of hills and also wide plains. There are several coastal lagoons, among which the Val di Comacchio is relatively famous. With numerous rivers and abundant water sources, the Po River is the main river. Other tributaries such as Trebbia, Taro, Secchia, and Panaro all originate from the northeastern slope of the Apennine Mountains, where vineyards and fruit orchards are located at the foot of the mountains. The wines produced here are very famous.

The Cavicchioli winery in Italy is located between the Secchia and Panaro rivers. The Cavicchioli winery owns many vineyards near the town of Modena province and has established their own winery in the Lambrusco vineyards. A few kilometers away from the winery, the highest quality Lambrusco Sorbara and Lambrusco Grasparossa grape varieties are planted. After decades of development and innovation, the winery has researched and produced some of the best Lambrusco wines in Italy, with products sold worldwide.

After the first sip, there is an endless stream of aroma and sweetness, refreshing and pleasant, with a subtle hint of smooth honey that seems to melt the tongue, making it impossible to stop taking another sip.
