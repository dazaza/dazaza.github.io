The person stands alone amidst the fallen flowers, while swallows fly in pairs in the drizzle.

by zhouzhou56 on 2012-02-02 22:35:44

"Standing alone amidst the fallen flowers, swallows flying in pairs in the drizzle"

After several days of scorching 30-degree weather, Guiyang finally received some rain. This undoubtedly brought a refreshing drop in temperature to the stifling heat, with the cool air invigorating the spirit. Before dawn, birds were chirping incessantly outside my window. When I opened it, a fresh scent of soil mixed with fallen blossoms greeted me. Beneath the gardenia tree, petals scattered with the rain, covering the ground. Suddenly, a line from Yan Xiaoshan's "Lín Jiāng Xiān" came to mind: "Standing alone amidst the fallen flowers, swallows flying in pairs in the drizzle." I am quite fond of this line. Xiaoshan was the seventh son of Yan Shu, a renowned Song Dynasty poet who held the position of Chancellor, and is often referred to as Little Yan. Both he and his father, Yan Shu, were staunch proponents of short lyrical poems known as "xiaoling." After Xiaoshan, the era of these short poems gradually faded away.

An YiRu has already provided a clear portrayal of Yan Xiaoshan. For such a talented individual, his ability to masterfully craft these short poems is truly enviable. Xiaoshan experienced significant ups and downs in his life, growing up surrounded by luxury and wealth. His father, Yan Shu, served as the Grand Academician in the imperial court and passed away at the age of 65 during Emperor Renzong's reign. Yan Shu was also a famous poet. Following his father's death, Xiaoshan's family fortune began to decline. As the saying goes, "When one leaves, tea cools," leaving behind only faint remnants of past glory. Xiaoshan fell from a life of comfort and privilege into the harsh realities of human warmth and coldness.

However, Xiaoshan was a man of deep emotions. His refusal to rely on powerful figures was one of his defining traits. His writings avoided adopting the tone of newly successful scholars, and he showed little interest in managing finances or property, spending extravagantly while neglecting his family's needs. Despite being betrayed repeatedly, he harbored no resentment, always trusting others and never doubting their sincerity. This paints a vivid picture of Xiaoshan's character. Although his life was tumultuous, he maintained an aristocratic demeanor, characterized by his stubborn pride and deep affection. In an era obsessed with fame and materialism, Xiaoshan valued integrity and dignity above all else, seeing them as the last defenses worth guarding with his life.

After waking from a dream, the tower gates are locked high, the curtains hang low after sobriety returns. The springtime regrets of last year return again. Standing alone amidst the fallen flowers, swallows fly in pairs in the drizzle.

I remember first meeting Xiao Ping, her silk robe adorned with double heart characters. She played the lute, expressing yearning. The bright moon of that time still shines today, once illuminating her graceful figure returning like a cloud.

The imagery of "fallen flowers" complemented by "drizzle" creates a scene of beauty tinged with sorrow. While the poem appears to describe the scenery, it subtly conveys feelings of separation and reluctance. Throughout the entire poem, not a single word explicitly mentions "emotion" or "sorrow," yet it perfectly captures the melancholy, helplessness, pain, and farewell. This essence forms the soul of the poem. One can imagine the situation: the destitute Xiaoshan, after drinking, stands on the balcony as if just awakening from a dream, the brothel owner mercilessly urging him to leave. What a poignant farewell it must have been! He steps out of the pavilion, standing alone on the flower-lined path, gazing far off at Xiao Ping, who looks back with reluctance. The unkind swallows, flying in pairs through the fine rain, pass over his head. Swallows can fly together, but what about people?

On the strings of the pipa, she speaks of longing; the bright moon of that time still shines today, once illuminating her graceful figure returning like a cloud. This vividly portrays Xiaoshan's lonely and desolate state. In his memory, their first meeting marked a mutual understanding of hearts, a connection unforgettable for life. This was Xiaoshan, immersing himself in poetry and wine, harboring an extremely sincere and passionate love consciousness. It was his way of resisting the allure of fame and worldly affairs. Those tender and mournful short poems became the sole solace for his soul...
