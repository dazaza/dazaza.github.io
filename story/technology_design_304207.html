Real Madrid Exposed: Eight Players on the Winter Sale List - A Mixture of Truth and Falsehood, Only Two Are Seriously Committed to Leaving

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-12 18:40:45

Real Madrid Exposed: Winter Sale of Eight Players - A Mixture of Truth and Falsehood; Only Two Are Truly Determined to Leave

According to a recent report by a Catalan media outlet, Real Madrid is preparing for a major overhaul during the winter break. Some players who have failed to make it into Manuel Pellegrini's plans have become redundant members of the squad—simply collecting their salaries without contributing. As a businessman, Florentino Pérez must consider costs and profits, and as such, these players are set to be offloaded.

The publication listed a total of eight names: reserve goalkeeper Dudek, backup left-back Drenthe, out-of-favor defensive midfielder Gago, frequently omitted striker Van Nistelrooy, substitute holding midfielder Mohamed Diarra, reserve center-back Garay, Real Madrid's vice-captain Guti, and backup attacking midfielder Van der Vaart. The article also stated that Real Madrid hopes to generate 40 million euros from the sale of these eight players. However, from the prices listed in the report, it is clear that this news should be taken with a grain of salt, as some of the valuations do not align with market trends or the actual worth of the players. In fact, these prices can be described as rather "amateur."

For instance, Garay is valued at just 5 million euros. Where in today's European transfer market could you find a promising central defender for such a low price? Furthermore, Van der Vaart is priced at merely 1.5 million euros—a fee more suited for a loan deal. Any club hoping to sign Van der Vaart for such an amount would surely be met with laughter from Real Madrid’s board unless they had all lost their minds.

Looking at the list, it is true that most of these players struggle to secure playing time, with some often excluded from matchday squads. Regardless of whether Florentino places commercial value above all else, basic team-building principles must still be respected. A football club needs both first-teamers and substitutes. If Real Madrid were to sell all eight of its substitutes, how could the team maintain balance? With the challenging second-half fixtures ahead, how could Real Madrid ensure sufficient squad depth? To chase after a supposed 40 million euros, would Real Madrid really transform itself into a team reliant on only one starting lineup for the entire season? Just a moment of thought would reveal that such a headline about Real Madrid cleaning out eight players during the winter break is implausible, especially since this is the winter break, not the summer transfer window.

Real Madrid executives Baldano and Pardeza have repeatedly emphasized that the club's principle for this winter break is neither to sell nor buy players. Of course, if a player insists on leaving and the offer from another club is reasonable, then Real Madrid might consider it. However, Real Madrid will not approve all transfer requests, and so far, no satisfactory offers have been received.

Dudek has known since joining Real Madrid that he would be a backup. The Polish goalkeeper has never complained about his lack of playing time. This summer, Real Madrid decided to keep Dudek, and he accepted a new contract. Why would he suddenly want to leave during the winter break, and why would Real Madrid release a reserve goalkeeper for free in the middle of the season? As for Drenthe, his chances of leaving are also slim. He does get some playing opportunities at Real Madrid, and the club's left flank requires backup strength. Currently, there are no buyers offering a reasonable price for Drenthe, and he himself has expressed his desire to succeed at Real Madrid.

Mohamed Diarra, after recovering from injury, has indeed become a substitute, but he harbors no complaints. He now gets some playing time off the bench and believes that with effort, his playing time will increase. Recently, his agent stated, “Mohamed will participate in the Africa Cup of Nations in January. At least during the winter break, he will not consider a transfer.”

Garay is arguably the least likely to leave Real Madrid. On one hand, Real Madrid views him as a future asset, and on the other, the defense needs to ensure backup strength. After all, Pepe and Albiol cannot play every single game. Real Madrid absolutely will not sell Garay for a mere 5 million euros. Metzelder, however, has a slightly higher chance of leaving. The German player is dissatisfied with his lack of playing time, as he worries it may affect his prospects of participating in the World Cup. It is reported that Zenit Saint Petersburg in Russia wants to sign Metzelder, but Real Madrid's approval of the transfer is unlikely for the same reasons mentioned earlier. Unless Real Madrid finds a replacement for Metzelder before the winter break, they are unlikely to accept Zenit's offer.

Van der Vaart never considered leaving during the toughest times, and now that his situation has improved, he has even less reason to leave. Moreover, his wife, Silvie, wishes to remain in Madrid for her breast cancer treatment. After Kaká's injury, Van der Vaart became a key player in the team. In a recent interview, Van der Vaart explicitly stated that he would not leave Real Madrid: “At the beginning of the season, things were very difficult, but now I feel happy and satisfied. My playing time has increased, and I hope to become an important member of the team. In the past few games, I've played in a crucial position, and I'm feeling good. Gradually, I'll adapt completely. I hope to stay at Real Madrid next season and become an important member of the team. Additionally, I hope the team wins both La Liga and the Champions League this season.”

Guti has no motivation to leave Madrid. We’ve already mentioned that he doesn’t want to leave the environment of Madrid, and his career is nearing its end. He doesn’t wish to wander elsewhere. Currently, the ones most likely to leave Real Madrid are Gago and Van Nistelrooy. Both players want to participate in the World Cup but currently aren't getting enough playing time. Their agents are actively helping them find potential buyers. However, suitable buyers have yet to be found. No one is willing to pay the 15 million euro transfer fee for Gago, and Van Nistelrooy’s request for a free transfer hasn't been approved by Real Madrid. They continue their search, and Real Madrid, seeing their eagerness to leave and having sufficient depth in those positions, reluctantly agrees to let them go, provided that the other clubs submit reasonable offers.

One of Real Madrid's top figures, Pardeza, also revealed that Real Madrid will take no action during the winter break: “The winter transfer window is short and carries risks. We have no plans to buy or sell.” (Sergio)
