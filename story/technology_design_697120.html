Zhao Wei's Pure Film Directorial Debut, "So Young," Begins Filming in Miyun on March 8, 2012.

Zhao Wei's directorial debut, the pure film "So Young," has officially begun filming! Upon hearing the good news that Zhao Wei's "So Young" has started filming in Miyun, we, her fans, can hardly wait with anticipation.

Zhao Wei mentioned that although "So Young" features new actors, the behind-the-scenes team is very strong. Besides inviting her old classmate Huang Xiaoming and Chen Kun to join, her good friends Wang菲 and Na Ying also play important cameo roles in the film. This work is adapted from Xin Yiwu's namesake novel of the post-80s female writer. The original novel "So Young" tells the story of Zheng Wei, a college girl who falls in love with two boys during her campus life, but both leave without a word. After entering society, Zheng Wei reunites with the two boys, who rekindle their pursuit of her, leaving her torn between her feelings. It is reported that the story in the movie version of "So Young" will undergo significant changes.

Currently, Zhao Wei's "So Young" is being filmed in Miyun, Beijing. It is a story about young people chasing their dreams. The protagonist participates in multiple art school exams and various competitions but fails to realize his dream. In the darkest and most helpless moments, he is discovered by a director, leading him to embark on an acting career. "So Young" also incorporates some personal experiences of Zhao Wei herself. The renowned director Guan Jinpeng serves as the producer, and the well-known screenwriter Li Qiang is responsible for the script.

Four Key Highlights of "So Young":

Highlight One: Newcomers take center stage, worth looking forward to.

Highlight Two: Big names support, including Zhao Wei's old classmates Huang Xiaoming and Chen Kun, and her close friends Wang菲 and Na Ying joining the project.

Highlight Three: Stars dominate, with the theme song and end credits performed by the mainland's new generation of kings and queens of pop, Zhang Liangying and Zhang Jie.

Highlight Four: Story-driven, the development of the plot is highly anticipated.

While we eagerly await Zhao Wei's "So Young," let's explore the scenic spots in Miyun where filming takes place...

Natural Landscapes of Miyun, the Shooting Location of "So Young":

Yunmeng Mountain, with a mid-altitude of 1414 meters, and Wuling Mountain, the main peak of the Yan Mountains at an altitude of 2118 meters, are located on the east and west sides of Miyun County respectively. Among them are distributed a large number of famous suburban attractions such as Heilongtan, the First Waterfall of Jingdu, Yunmengshan National Forest Park, Longtan Scenic Area, Yunxiu Valley, etc. These attractions feature dense clusters of streams, ponds, and waterfalls, lush vegetation, towering rocks, and picturesque scenery, making them ideal places for citizens to relax and travel.

Cultural and Historical Sites in Miyun, the Shooting Location of "So Young":

Miyun has a long history with numerous ancient sites and cultural landscapes. The Ming Great Wall stretches over 200 kilometers through the county, the longest among all districts and counties. Simatai Great Wall was praised by China's Great Wall expert Luo Zhenwen as the "best of China's Great Walls." In Xitian Gezhuang Town's Baidaoyu Village, there is also a section of "misaligned Great Wall." Gubeikou was a historical stronghold and a battleground for generations of military strategists. Not only does it have the Ming Great Wall, but it also preserves the ancient Northern Qi Great Wall. There is also the Imperial Road built in 1680. The temple complex of Shugu Mountain, which was constructed 1200 years ago, the ancient Buddhist site on Yunfeng Mountain, the Temple of General Yang Linggong built in 1025 AD, as well as Bai Longtan and Yexian Pagoda, add profound cultural depth to Miyun tourism.

Miyun Reservoir, the Shooting Location of "So Young":

The Miyun Reservoir is the largest reservoir in North China. Located in the middle and lower reaches of the Chao and Bai Rivers, it was formed by damming the Bai River and Chao River, spanning the two rivers. As Asia's largest man-made lake and known as the "Pearl of the Yan Mountains," it is a summer resort. The Miyun Reservoir attracts tourists with its enchanting mountains and waters, becoming one of the famous tourist attractions in the eastern part of Beijing. When you arrive at the reservoir, the first sight is the majestic dam. Climbing to the top of the dam, the view suddenly opens up to reveal vast waves of misty water stretching endlessly, dotted with fishing boats. Various buildings around the reservoir are hidden among the green mountains and clear waters, resembling celestial palaces and jade pavilions.
