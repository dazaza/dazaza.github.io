Sina Portal Laid-off 10% of Employees: Sales Tasks Assigned to Channel Staff

by geekzhang on 2013-05-16 17:21:10

After the replacement of senior executives, Sina is accelerating a series of personnel tightening and sales improvement plans.Recently, an informed person told the "First Financial Daily" that Sina overall laid off 10% of its workforce, some marginalized small channels will be eliminated; In addition, according to the different advertising value of the content of each channel, Sina also assigned the portal's advertising sales tasks to every content employee of Sina Portal.So far, Sina has not commented on the above rumors.An insider at Sina revealed to the reporter that Sina has currently divided the internal channels into three categories: commercial channels such as automobiles, real estate, sports, etc., comprehensive channels such as finance, technology, etc., and media channels such as news centers. After the division, non-sales staff such as content editors will also share sales targets.An insider at Sina analyzed that the sales tasks borne by each channel may just be the beginning. The underlying grand plan might involve the future spin-off and independent operation of each channel. Independent vertical media is becoming increasingly popular now, and Sina cannot but consider this trend. The sales targets of each channel are only the groundwork for the future independent spin-off of the channels.Sales OrientationFor the above three types of channels, Sina has different sales requirements for the content editors of each type of channel: commercial channels must bear larger sales tasks, comprehensive channels come second, and media channels do not bear sales tasks. The sales target is a new indicator for evaluating content editors in addition to traffic indicators, and there is no replacement of indicators.In addition to assigning advertising tasks, Sina has also adjusted some marginal channels. In the adjustment plan, the percentage of personnel reduction is around 10%.A Sina content editor told this reporter that the aforementioned adjustments were made after the change of portal executives and the revamp of Sina Portal.However, he said that although Sina has set sales targets for most channels, each channel has different response methods, not every front-line editor in each channel bears sales targets. For example, some channels assign sales targets to one or two dedicated people, while some channels adopt a more extreme approach, requiring every editor to bear the burden.Financial reports show that in the fourth quarter of 2012, Sina's revenue was $139.1 million, an increase of 4%; among which, advertising revenue was $110 million, an increase of 7%. Tencent's advertising revenue was $150.8 million, with a year-on-year increase of 58.3%. Sohu and NetEase's advertising growth rates are also higher than Sina's.Due to the impact of Weibo expansion, in the fourth quarter of 2012, Sina's net profit was only $2.4 million, a decrease of 74%. Some industry insiders believe that Sina's revenue growth will become the primary evaluation point for its new executives.An insider at Sina revealed that with the appointment of new executives, Chen Tong, the chief editor of Sina, also had his influence on Sina Portal affected. Last year, Charles Chao (Chairman of Sina) set high indicators for Chen Tong. Once they were not met, Chen Tong's benefits and authority within Sina would be further marginalized, and the channelization of sales tasks would also have an impact on the content of some channels directly supervised by Chen Tong.Sina has a strong sales imprint, which can be seen from the talks with Focus Media before the MBO, and currently introducing Alibaba to help commercialize Weibo. In the past ten years, capital acquisition rumors related to Sina have all been associated with companies with strong sales cultures.Gene Loss?Sina played a card, but lost another one. The above-mentioned person indicated that Sina invested three years in Weibo, among which it spent about a year and a half exploring the commercialization of Weibo. During this one and a half years, Sohu, NetEase, and Tencent heavily invested in mobile news clients, while Sina was too busy with Weibo to pay attention.In today's era where mobile fields are increasingly capturing user attention, will Sina lose the crown of being the top internet media?A company's strategy has a strong gene inheritance effect. Sina started doing Weibo and began leading with strong media operations, but as the user base of Sina Weibo expanded, Sina had to switch its thinking direction from media to social orientation, but social is not Sina's main gene.Sohu does not have a product gene either. However, while Sina heavily invested resources in Weibo, Sohu entered the mobile field through its news client. Yue Jianxiong of Sohu News Client told the reporter that news is not the ultimate form of Sohu News Client. Sohu News Client is considering introducing user relationships, and every traditional media outlet that joins Sohu News Client will exist as a unidirectional relationship account similar to Weibo. This is actually a reverse product strategy.It is still too early to judge at present.
