When the Gates of Memory Open

Deep within everyone's heart lie secrets, known to few or deliberately concealed, be they bitter, sour, or self-indulgent. These secrets are carefully buried in some corner, rarely touched upon for fear of the pain they bring. But sometimes, when one is moved by a scene or finds themselves alone, the gates of memory open like floodgates, spilling over with memories. Scenes from the past replay before our eyes, a mix of sweetness, bitterness, joy, and sorrow welling up in our hearts, emotions intertwining as tears blur our vision.

Memories take me back to that distant era, to that bitter childhood, to those moments of disappointment and helplessness, to that cry for meaning in life, to that solitary confusion, to an age filled with tears. They say childhood is beautiful, carefree, full of laughter and joy. But my childhood was painful, filled with tears. The changes in my family circumstances meant I faced abandonment from the moment I was born (or even before). I quietly entered this world without blessings or the company of loved ones. My birth brought not joy to my family but sighs.

Thus began my unstable life, without a fixed place to live, without regular meals, growing up silently amidst cries ignored by others, hunger, and incomplete love. When I saw other children thriving under the protection of their parents' wings, I cast countless envious glances. How many times, under the moonlight on the treetops, did I run to the village entrance alone, hoping to see my mother's familiar figure appear? How many times did I shout at the top of my lungs, my voice echoing through the valley, accompanied only by the dark night and hunger? How many times did I yearn, worry, endure... This special living environment made me experience the myriad aspects of human nature, making me understand the hardships of life too early. It shaped my independent and resilient character, burying my pain deep inside as I strive to be the best version of myself.

Perhaps it was fate, or perhaps just coincidence, but this life has destined me to walk alone, wandering far from home. Alone in unfamiliar lands, I struggle without the help of friends or relatives, without the support of acquaintances, without anyone to shield me from the storms, without anyone to wipe away my tears. I have always been self-reliant, striving for the ideals and dreams in my heart, shouldering responsibilities, hiding all pain, letting my tears flow inward. Setbacks, disappointments, and blows accompany me along the way... Effort, tolerance, and endurance become my chosen path in life.

The confusion during times of failure, the helplessness during loneliness, the powerlessness during times of doubt, and the uncertainty of the journey lead me to choose silence. I do not blame heaven or earth, nor lament the misfortunes of fate, nor complain about being born at the wrong time. Facing reality, the road lies beneath my feet. "When Heaven is about to confer a great responsibility on any man, it first disciplines his mind, strains his sinews and bones, starves his body, and confounds his undertakings, thereby stimulating his mind and hardening his nature, increasing his capabilities." (I know I am very small and ordinary, this is merely a form of self-consolation.)

Everything has a dual nature, and God is fair to everyone. While closing one door, He opens another window. Hardships are treasures, and setbacks make me more mature. Through storms and hardships, after the rain comes the rainbow, sunshine always follows the storm. Even if there are thousands of mountains and rivers ahead, I will face them calmly.
