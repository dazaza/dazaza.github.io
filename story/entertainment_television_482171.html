Introduction of the first episode of "Twelve Workshops of Jiǔjiāng"

When people in the Jiǔjiāng liquor industry were busy holding the dragon boat sacrifice ceremony, Song Zhengyao (played by Lam Pou Tik), as the leader of the Liquor Guild Institute, failed to appear on time, which caused dissatisfaction from his uncle Song Tao (played by Guo Feng) and cousin Zhu Guoxiong (played by Chen Rongjun). In fact, the reason Zhengyao was late was because he was trying to persuade Magistrate Zhou Shixun (played by Mak Cheung Ching) to help him fight against the corrupt county magistrate Zeng Jianliang (played by Liu Jiang). On the other hand, Liang Zhikang (played by Chen Jinhong), Liang Xiuyan (played by Yue Tong), and Liang Guanhui Lan (played by Snowie Sha) from Liang's River Fresh Shop were busy cooking; Hui Lan told Zhengkang that she had encountered a problem, and Zhengkang came up with a solution. Poly Friendship Home and Liang's River Fresh Shop held an anonymous taste test competition for the right to host the dragon boat meal. Both sides ended in a tie, only waiting for Zhengyao's vote, and finally Zhengyao voted for Liang's. Zhengyao dined with his sister Song Zicheng (played by Bellamy Chan), stepmother Song Ding Jiabi (played by Karen Mok), and aunt Song Meiqin (played by Xue Ni). At the table, Zhengyao expressed his intention to arrange a marriage for his sister; a wine shop owner told Zhengkang that he wanted to retire and proposed to transfer the wine shop to Zhengkang. Zhengkang applied for a liquor license, and Jianliang praised Zhengkang's private wine, originally intending to create conflicts between the Liang and Song families; Mr. Liu, the secretary, reminded Jianliang that actually Zhengkang and Zhengyao were biological brothers. On the opening day of Liang's, they were immediately accused by government officials of not having a valid license. Zhengkang was arrested and imprisoned, leaving Hui Lan no choice but to seek help from Zhengyao, who was the head of the Liquor Guild Institute.
