Anhui "AIDS woman" posts online to expose the mobile phone numbers of 89 people who had contact with her

Changsha, Hunan, April 12 (Rolling News Reporter Hong Ren) - Yan Deli, the instigator of the "AIDS woman" incident in Rongcheng, Hebei, and her ex-boyfriend Yang Yongmeng have just been sentenced to three years in prison by the local court. Who would have thought that there are still people willing to break the law, causing regret. Recently, Anhui exposed an incident suspected to be a replica of the Yan Deli event, once again shocking netizens.

The "AIDS woman" posted online exposing the mobile phone numbers of 89 people who had contact with her.

On the afternoon of April 11, netizen "Worry Virus" posted a thread titled "They sell their bodies in Hefei and got AIDS" on the Hefei Forum, claiming that Li Jingxia, from 2006 to present, has had sexual relations with many people and now has AIDS.

The poster published the mobile phone numbers of 89 people who had dealings with her from last November to February this year, along with photos of the "parties involved," hoping those involved would go to the hospital for a check-up after seeing it.

Xiangxiang Morning Post reporter noticed that the registration information of the poster "Worry Virus" showed personal photos provided in the post, name: Li Jingxia, gender: female, birthday: July 29, 1974, residence: Hefei, Anhui, hometown: Bozhou, Anhui.

In response, a moderator of the Hefei Forum added a note reminding: If what the poster said is true, thank the poster for the friendly reminder; if it's false, all legal risks will be borne by the poster himself, and used the case of Yang Yongmeng, the main perpetrator of the "AIDS woman" incident in Rongcheng, Hebei, being sentenced to three years in prison as a warning.

Reporter investigation: Among the disclosed numbers, both men and women denied it.

On the evening of April 11, reporters from Xiangxiang Morning Post called more than 20 of the disclosed 89 telephone numbers. Except for unanswered calls or turned-off phones, all denied the matter and said it was fabricated. One lady, upon hearing about this, expressed great anger, saying it was completely defamatory.

Subsequently, the reporter contacted Hefei 110, and a staff member said they had not yet received any reports regarding this matter, stating that it should be handled by the city's Public Security Bureau's Internet Monitoring Brigade.

Following this, Xiangxiang Morning Post reporter contacted the Hefei Public Security Bureau's Internet Monitoring Brigade, where police officers introduced that they were aware of the matter, and some citizens had reported this issue to them. They assured that they would handle it according to regulations and procedures.

Finally, the reporter noticed that the phone numbers attached in the post on the Hefei Forum had been deleted. Attempts to contact the poster were unsuccessful.

Lawyer opinion: If the post is not true, the poster may be suspected of defamation.

Regarding this matter, on the evening of April 11, Xiangxiang Morning Post also consulted with lawyer Wang Qing from Changsha. She introduced that if the post was not made by the person herself or authorized by her, and after the party involved reported the matter to the public security organ, it was found that the content of the post was not true, then the poster may be suspected of defamation.

Lawyer Wang Qing also cited relevant legal articles, stating that according to Article 246 of the Criminal Law of the People's Republic of China, "Publicly insulting others by violence or other methods, or fabricating facts to defame others, if the circumstances are serious, shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of not more than three years, criminal detention, public surveillance, or deprivation of political rights."

At the same time, Lawyer Wang Qing emphasized that defamation is only prosecuted upon complaint. If the victim in the post does not report to the public security organ themselves, requesting legal responsibility for the poster, generally speaking, the public security organ will not initiate an investigation on its own.

Commentary analysis: A heavy questioning of the "Yan Deli Incident" replica!

On the evening of April 11, senior media commentator Wang Shichuan expressed his views: If this matter is fabricated, the black hand behind the creation of the Hefei AIDS woman incident will certainly be punished. Regardless of the reason, publicly leaking others' privacy and deliberately fabricating false facts are dangerous acts against the law. While destroying others, it also burns oneself, leading to severe legal punishment.

This is an era where everyone has a microphone. The Internet provides an unprecedented platform for public discourse, but the Internet has also become a tool for some lawbreakers to satisfy their selfish desires. Because the Internet has instant transmission and immense amplification effects, even trivial matters can snowball when they attract attention and focus.

Incidents like the Yan Deli event and the Hefei AIDS woman event, due to elements such as sex, lies, pornographic photos, prostitution, and AIDS that catch the eye, easily attract netizens' attention and spread rapidly across major forums nationwide within a short period, causing immeasurable damage and harm to the parties involved.

Then, how can we reduce the occurrence of similar incidents and lower the degree of harm caused by these events? Wang Shichuan said that this requires network forum managers to strictly take responsibility. For posts suspected of infringing on others' rights, they should conduct more verification rather than blindly approve them for clicks.

And the netizens watching should ask more questions and not blindly believe and spread posts that seem lively at first glance. In fact, some people maliciously exploit this psychology of netizens to achieve their own purposes.

Wang Shichuan also emphasized that the key is to uphold the dignity of the law and adhere to moral bottom lines. When the law truly takes effect and severely punishes those daring to break the law, these people will not dare to make fake posts and infringe on others' rights.

At the same time, netizens must strengthen their self-discipline awareness. A netizen with a sense of reverence and responsibility will neither create false posts nor spread false posts.
