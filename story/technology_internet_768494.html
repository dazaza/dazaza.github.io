Happy 12th Birthday, Apple iPod! 'Retirement' begins this year

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 18:55:49

Twelve years ago today, Apple began selling the iPad. At the time, Apple viewed this product as a disruptor to MP3 music players. It could hold up to 1000 high-quality songs and its 6.5-ounce weight made it fit perfectly into a user's pocket.

The Rise of iPod

Steve Jobs commented on this product by saying — "Apple's iPod redefined digital music players, allowing users to carry entire music albums in their pockets wherever they go." Jobs predicted that the iPod would completely change the way humans listened to music.

In the fourth quarter of 2001, Apple sold 125,000 iPods. In 2002, Apple released the second generation of iPods priced at $399 with 5GB of memory and featuring a high-definition display.

In 2013, Apple introduced the third generation of all-touchscreen iPods. By September 2013, Apple’s iPod shipments broke through the one million mark for the first time. In 2004, Apple sold 4.4 million iPods, far surpassing Mac computer sales, leading to the establishment of a new independent iPod operations department. In the early part of the fourth quarter of 2005, Apple’s iPod sales reached 22.5 million units, increasing to 39.4 million units in 2006.

Paving the Way for the iPhone Smartphone

The success of the iPod helped Apple establish the foundational framework for the iTunes Store and the iOS App Store, laying a solid foundation for the emergence and rise of the iPhone.

In 2007, Apple began selling the smartphone iPhone. At that time, iPod sales had exceeded 100 million units, and iTunes had become the world's largest electronic music market, offering over 5 million songs, 3.5 million TV episodes, and more than 4 million movies. By then, Apple had sold over 2.5 billion songs, 50 million TV episodes, and more than 130 million movies. iTunes became the most popular "store" globally for music, TV shows, and movies.

The “Retirement” of the iPad

In 2007, Apple launched the iPod touch, a portable mobile product considered a branch of the iPod series and can be seen as a simplified version of the iPhone. Compared to the iPhone, except for not being able to make calls, all other functions were the same. The iPod Touch helped Apple attract BlackBerry and Symbian users to the iOS platform.

Currently, Apple's iPod Touch largely depends on software, specifically applications. However, iPod Touch sales have been surpassed by Apple's Mac computers. This year, Apple did not host an annual iPod media event or upgrade the iPad hardware, marking the first time in the past 12 years. Instead, Apple has focused most of its efforts on the iPhone 5S/5C smartphones and the new iPad mini and iPad Air tablets.
