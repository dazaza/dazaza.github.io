Unveiling the Suspense of *The Quest for Cheng Long*

by kaxiya on 2009-07-04 10:37:26

The youth inspiring film "Looking for Jackie" is directed by Jiang Ping, vice general manager of China Film Group Corporation and stars child actor Zhang Yishan. It also features nearly a hundred other actors in supporting roles including Jackie Chan, Yu Lan, Tian Hua, Yuan Hua, Yuan Qiu, Wang Xuebing, Yu Nan and Jiang Hongbo. The film will be nationally released on July 3rd, 2009.

English Title: Looking for Jackie

Genre: Youth/Inspiring/Comedy/Action

Director: Jiang Ping, Fang Gangliang

Starring: Zhang Yishan, Jackie Chan

Featuring: Yu Lan, Yuan Hua, Yuan Qiu, Yuan Wu, Yuan Bin, Tian Hua, Bai Bing, Li Xiang, Yu Nan, Yao Di etc.

Release Date: July 3rd, 2009

Producer: Children's Film Studio of China Film Group Corporation, CCTV Film Channel Program Center etc.

Plot Summary of "Looking for Jackie":

At the China Film Base, a martial artist dressed in Republic of China attire (played by Jackie Chan) scans his increasingly encircling enemies with a sharp gaze. In the ensuing fierce battle, Jackie Chan displays his incredible kung fu skills and defeats all the enemies. Jackie Chan will take on a young hero, Zhang Yishan (played by Zhang Yishan, the lead actor of the TV series "I Am Not A Star") as his disciple. At a critical moment, however, Zhang Yishan is woken up by his grandmother (played by Zhu Xijuan). It turns out that this was just a wonderful dream.

Zhang Yishan grew up in Indonesia but has absolutely no interest in his mother tongue, Chinese, repeatedly failing exams and becoming the laughing stock of his classmates. To improve Zhang Yishan's Chinese proficiency, his grandmother decides to send him to Beijing to study Chinese at his maternal grandmother's (played by Tian Hua) and grandfather's (played by Zhang Yongshou) house.

Zhang Yishan firmly believes that once he arrives in China, he will find his hero, Jackie Chan, become his disciple, learn kung fu, and then he won't be bullied by his classmates anymore. Thus, Zhang Yishan secretly makes an exciting decision - to find Jackie Chan.

He sneaks off without telling his grandmother and heads straight to Tansuo Temple as soon as he gets off the plane because he knows Jackie Chan is shooting a kung fu blockbuster there.

However, due to his extremely poor Chinese skills, Zhang Yishan mistakes Qing Shi Guan for Tansuo Temple and gets into conflict with the nun there (played by Yuan Qiu, winner of the Hundred Flowers Award for Best Actress). After a bout of fighting, Zhang Yishan successfully enters the Taoist temple but doesn't find Jackie Chan. Instead, he meets Jackie Chan's younger brother, Yuan Hua. But Zhang Yishan does not give up and resolutely embarks on his journey to Tansuo Temple.

On the way, Zhang Yishan encounters a cold and beautiful female thief (played by Yan Bingyan, winner of the Golden Rooster Award for Best Actress). The female thief wants to keep Zhang Yishan as a substitute for her lost younger brother. Zhang Yishan cleverly escapes from the female thief's hideout but is captured again by the bandit leader (played by Wu Jun). Fortunately, the police arrive in time and Zhang Yishan is finally rescued.

It is precisely because of this strange rescue that Zhang Yishan meets a beautiful and kind policewoman (played by Jiang Hongbo), but misses Jackie Chan while saving the policewoman during a capture operation.

Unwilling to give up on his goal, Zhang Yishan decides to wait for Jackie Chan at the entrance of the China Film Base. He must not miss this opportunity to become Jackie Chan's disciple again. As night falls, Jackie Chan's caravan slowly approaches...

Director: Jiang Ping

Jiang Ping, Vice General Manager of China Film Group Corporation, former Deputy Director of the Department of Film under the State Administration of Radio, Film, and Television. Representative works include "Smoke and Rain Red Beauty" etc.

Starring: Zhang Yishan

Zhang Yishan, a new generation actor born in 1992, became well-known for his role in "I Am Not A Star". He started practicing martial arts from a young age and loves playing basketball. A classmate once commented on him: "More like Liu Xing than Liu Xing himself."
