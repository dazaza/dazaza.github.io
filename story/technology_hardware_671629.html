Subsequently, Dean Yang prepared to inform the man who came along about the condition of the mother and child. However, the man, Mr. Li, said that he was not the wife's husband but merely a neighbor. Dean Yang was puzzled, "In such a major event, if the husband isn't here but the neighbor is, doesn't that defy common sense?" Later, under repeated questioning by Dean Yang, Mr. Li finally admitted hesitantly that he was actually the wife's husband.

On December 18 at around 3 PM, shortly after Mr. Li admitted to lying, he voluntarily came to the office and said he wanted to immediately process discharge procedures to take the mother and child away.

At 4 PM on December 19, the reporter also specifically went to the hospital to try to dissuade Mr. Li.

On December 20 in the afternoon, considering that the mother had moderate anemia, the nurse was preparing to transfuse 400 milliliters of blood into the mother. At this moment, Mr. Li again indicated that they still chose to leave.

Originally, two months ago, the young couple had a conflict with the elders and in anger moved from their hometown of Bijie to Guiyang. Mr. Li said his wife was only seven months pregnant, and they always thought the baby would be born next month, so they planned to buy train tickets on December 21 to return to Jiangxi to celebrate the New Year with his mother-in-law. But unexpectedly, on the morning of December 18, his wife showed signs of labor. Since they were not mentally prepared, Mr. Li took his wife to a small clinic intending for her to give birth there but was refused. In a panic, Mr. Li hailed a taxi, and the taxi driver, seeing the urgent situation of the pregnant woman, directly ran red lights to deliver them to the door of the Retired Physicians Hospital without even charging a fare.

After much persuasion from everyone, Mr. Li still rushed to the dean's office to process discharge procedures. Soon, after a brief tidying up, Mr. Li forcibly pulled his wife out of the hospital, tears streaming down Peng's face. However, watching the family's retreating figure, Dean Yang couldn't help but sigh: Is saving face more important than the lives of two living beings?

Upon seeing this situation, Dean Yang Chang immediately helped the mother receive a blood transfusion and arranged for a doctor to specially observe the baby for timely rescue.

He insisted on taking the mother and child away

Just because he couldn't let go of "a man's face", ignoring the health of his wife and child -

"Actually, I wasn't intentionally lying, but I couldn't help myself," Mr. Li candidly admitted when questioned by everyone, as the family did not approve of their relationship, so in the past four years, they had never been to his mother-in-law's house.

But surprisingly, an hour later, Mr. Li still insisted on taking the mother and child away from the hospital. Seeing this, the head nurse personally took out one hundred yuan and gave it to Mr. Li to buy formula milk, and later another kind-hearted person gave fifty yuan. However, all that everyone did seemed to have little effect on Mr. Li's determination to leave.

After learning the situation, the hospital immediately studied and decided that economic issues would be temporarily set aside, prioritizing the treatment of the mother and child first. Subsequently, Dean Yang told Mr. Li to take the 300 yuan first to buy formula milk for the baby. If there were real economic difficulties, the hospital would reduce or exempt fees as appropriate, hoping that Mr. Li's family could stay until both the mother and child were fine before leaving. Following this, the hospital immediately provided the mother with a free transfusion of 400 milliliters of blood.

Just because he couldn't let go of "a man's dignity"

Mr. Li said that as a man, not having money for his wife to be hospitalized during childbirth made him feel very ashamed. Receiving help from good Samaritans made him feel both happy and sad. He was happy because along the way, he encountered many kind people, but he was sad because accepting others' kindness made him feel like he owed them forever, making it hard for his heart to find peace.

At this point, Mr. Li lowered his head, silent for a long time. But soon, he said, "I can't answer your request; I need to see if my wife agrees." Subsequently, when everyone approached the bedside to ask the mother, Ms. Peng, she replied, "I don't want to leave the hospital." After hearing Ms. Peng's statement, Mr. Li then said, "Then we'll stay for a few more days."

Because he didn't have money, he falsely claimed to be a neighbor

At 11 AM on December 18, a taxi hurriedly stopped at the gate of Guizhou Retired Physicians Hospital. The car door opened, and a young couple rushed into the hospital. Upon seeing the doctors, the man anxiously said, "Doctor, please help her. Her water has broken, and she's about to give birth."

Dr. Chen from the Obstetrics Department immediately examined the patient and found that the pregnant woman's cervix was fully dilated and needed to be urgently sent to the operating room for rescue. In about five minutes, a boy was born, and both mother and child were safe. However, the doctors discovered that the baby was premature, weighing only three pounds four ounces. After simple treatment, the baby was immediately placed in an incubator for observation. Meanwhile, the mother had mild anemia before giving birth, with only 6 grams (6g-L) of blood at the time, and after losing some blood postpartum, she was left with only 4.3 grams of blood.

Rumor behind

The hospital is willing to support

After hearing the news, Director Sun Ronggang from the Obstetrics Department once again came to the ward to persuade Mr. Li to stay for further treatment. Director Sun informed that since the baby was premature, possibly the organs inside were not yet fully developed, and resistance was extremely weak. Once leaving the incubator and catching a cold, it might lead to lung infection, gastrointestinal, and respiratory infections, which would be very detrimental to the baby's growth. Additionally, the mother was continuously in an anemic state, and if blood was not replenished in time, late postpartum massive bleeding might occur, posing significant safety hazards.
