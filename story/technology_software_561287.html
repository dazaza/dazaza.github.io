90% of workplace newcomers don't understand the standard etiquette for oral interviews_844

by xgoibj34 on 2011-11-16 10:07:47

Currently, it is the peak season for job hunting, especially for those who are about to step out of the school gate and into society as new entrants in the workplace. For them, job interviews and resume writing may still be somewhat unfamiliar and strange. A resume is the stepping stone to open the door to the workplace, while an interview is a comprehensive test. Besides having a professional resume, background experience, and other hard skills, etiquette as a soft skill also plays a crucial role in interview success.

According to a survey jointly launched by英才网联 (Yingcai Network), Sohu Education, Rui Li Workplace, and others on job hunting for new entrants in the workplace - "Job Hunting Survey for New Entrants in the Workplace - Etiquette Edition," the internet has become the preferred channel for job seekers to obtain knowledge about etiquette. More than 90% of new entrants in the workplace are unclear about the criteria interviewers use to judge interview etiquette.

In most cases, an interview involves direct face-to-face interaction with the interviewer, where every word and action is fully visible to the interviewer. Those who understand etiquette are more likely to gain an advantage and easily secure the highest score, leading to smooth employment. Etiquette is an external manifestation of personal quality and is a key to succeeding in interviews.

The survey conducted by英才网联 shows that 65% of participants choose to learn about workplace-related etiquette through online channels. Attending lectures and using professional books are also effective choices.

Employment guidance experts from英才网联 suggest that job interview etiquette requires shaping one's image from every detail, which is closely related to each person's growth background and experience, requiring long-term cultivation. Therefore, it is essential for new entrants in the workplace to familiarize themselves with basic workplace rules and etiquette, which is fundamental homework they need to do well.

(Job hunting interviews have "etiquette" everywhere)

The survey shows that "speech and behavior," "dress code," and "resume preparation" are several aspects of interview etiquette that new entrants in the workplace value. Employment guidance experts from英才网联 indicate that interview etiquette does not simply refer to speech and behavior or dress code, which are the easiest details to think of. In fact, starting from the moment the resume is submitted, the entire process of job hunting for new entrants is already under the standard of "etiquette," being used by interviewers to compare candidates against each other. For example, writing a resume, applying for a position, answering interview calls, and subsequent interviews and re-interviews—any oversight in a small link can affect the overall job hunting situation.

More than 90% of new entrants in the workplace are unclear about the judgment criteria of interviewers. The survey shows that 78% of people are not very clear about the criteria interviewers use to judge interview etiquette, 16% are unclear but hope to understand it, and only 6% are very clear. Employment guidance experts from英才网联 suggest that although the current economic and cultural background provides a two-way choice scenario for job application, for most new entrants in the workplace, it is more of a one-way selection. Therefore, to improve their interview success rate, it is necessary to understand the criteria interviewers use to select candidates, thereby knowing how to target improvements and meet the requirements.

Resume content isn't necessarily better when longer. In the option "What does your resume look like?" 66% chose "1-2 pages," 28% included "cover, back cover, and cover letter," and 29% added "various certificates, transcripts, and works," making these the "three major components" of most job seekers' resumes. Choosing "color binding, distinctive photos" and "illustrated, detailed and comprehensive" accounted for 7% and 4%, respectively.

Employment guidance experts indicate that as a showcase for personal image, ability, and quality, many people do not know how to write a resume properly. Most people choose to include everything comprehensively without prioritizing. In reality, the least practical parts of a resume are "cover, back cover, and cover letter," which waste time and paper. Similarly, attaching various certificates and transcripts to the resume is unnecessary. An excellent resume should be concise, with 1-2 pages sufficient unless specifically requested; otherwise, additional attachments are not necessary.

Professional attire is more likely to gain favor. According to the results of the workplace etiquette survey, 72% of respondents indicated they would prepare appropriately for interviews, wearing clean and tidy clothing with a professional feel. Seventeen percent said they "don't make special preparations, just like everyday wear," and 11% stated they would definitely choose "professional suits, appearing professional." Employment guidance experts from英才网联 suggest that while "judging a book by its cover" has some truth, appropriate attire matching the hiring company and position not only easily gains the "first impression" favor of the interviewer but also demonstrates the individual's serious attitude towards the company, thus gaining interview points.

Employment guidance experts emphasize the importance of non-verbal communication, such as clothing, gestures, facial expressions, etc., which leave a deep first impression on interviewers. Studies show that the impression a person leaves on others depends 7% on words, 38% on voice tone, and 55% on non-verbal communication. Therefore, appropriately utilizing non-verbal communication skills during interviews can yield twice the result with half the effort.
