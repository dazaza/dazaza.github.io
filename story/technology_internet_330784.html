Li Yanhong says that Baidu's current development in China is due to Google's lack of attention at that time - Nanjing SEO Bar

by maomaochaoren on 2010-03-01 10:25:45

Baidu's current development in China is entirely due to Google's lack of attention back then, said Robin Li. It's like the situation between Yahoo! and Google in the past. Back then, Yahoo! was the world's largest search engine, but when Google rose, Yahoo! did not take it seriously. As a result, Yahoo! lost its dominant position in the search engine market to Google, and since then, various problems have emerged for Yahoo!.

Sina Technology News - In the early hours of March 1, CCTV-2's "Dialogue" aired its first program of the Tiger Year last night, with Baidu CEO Robin Li as the guest, discussing Baidu's ten-year history and future development goals. During the interview, Robin Li stated that if Google had paid attention to the Chinese market earlier, Baidu would not exist; furthermore, Baidu's revenue growth goal ten years later will be far more than 40 times.

This program was recorded on January 19, with singer Haiquan Hu, CBS China President Lu Wang, GGV Capital Partner Jiuxun Fu, and other internet industry insiders such as Bowen Lu, Benfu Lu, Yanping Hu, and Jingwen Ruan also participating in the dialogue.

If Google had paid attention earlier, Baidu would not exist

When talking about Baidu's ten-year entrepreneurial journey, Robin Li recalled how they even rented room number 1414 that others dared not rent just to reduce rent. He said he has always believed that entrepreneurship is tough, and success that comes from such trials and tribulations, emerging from such an environment, "is what makes one feel solid."

In the summer of 2001, Baidu faced a choice of transformation. Robin Li insisted on directly entering the search service sector, but the board of directors did not fully agree with this opinion, leading to rumors that Robin Li had become extremely angry over this. "Actually, it wasn't as intense as throwing a phone," though Robin Li also revealed that the board members were indeed moved by his attitude at the time.

Although Robin Li said, "We have never had the concept of opponents, only peers," in Baidu's development history, Google has always been the biggest competitor.

Google's Chinese version of search even appeared a year earlier than Baidu. Before Baidu's IPO in 2004, Google CEO Eric Schmidt even directly proposed an acquisition during a two-person dialogue with Robin Li, willing to continue investing, but also requiring Baidu to abandon its IPO. This proposal was clearly rejected by Robin Li.

Just like IBM failing to pay early attention to the operating system led to Microsoft, Microsoft failing to pay early attention to portal sites led to Yahoo!, and Yahoo! failing to pay early attention to search engines led to Google, Robin Li frankly admitted that if Google could have discovered the importance of the Chinese market earlier, Baidu would not exist.

Revenue growth goal after ten years far exceeds 40 times

In Robin Li's concept, success = technology × market demand. In Robin Li's view, no matter how advanced the technology is, without the market, it cannot succeed.

After confirming the direction of the bidding ranking model, one day Robin Li received an email where the product manager responsible for the business model mentioned that Baidu's daily click income had exceeded 100 yuan. This 100 yuan made the management team of Baidu at that time very excited, feeling unimaginable. And now Baidu's daily income is close to 20 million yuan.

However, this model gradually exposed its shortcomings during its development, and Baidu officially launched Phoenix Nest last year to replace bidding ranking, aiming to correct this. Robin Li expressed in the program that Baidu is neither a consumer association nor a business administration bureau, and does not have the authority to evaluate information on the Internet, but has clearly marked the paid links.

Previously, there were reports that Baidu set a revenue growth goal of 40 times for the next ten years. Regarding this, Robin Li did not elaborate much, but indicated that the internal goal is much larger than this number. On the other hand, Robin Li also revealed that he thinks about Baidu's weaknesses every day, and if he can't sleep at night, it's because he's thinking about this.

Regarding the criticism that there are too many people with science backgrounds in Baidu's senior management team, Robin Li said that Baidu's development over the past ten years has relied on precise grasp of product direction, and in the next ten years, Baidu indeed needs excellent management, and the senior management team is improving in this regard, though "fortunately, our competitors have even more people studying science."
