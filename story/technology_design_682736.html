Dark破坏神's Destruction of Destruction 27-Year-Old Woman from Guangzhou Scammed by Two African Men

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-02-29 08:13:45

Marrying a handsome foreigner and having a mixed-race baby with big eyes and curly hair is the dream of some Chinese girls. Lily (a pseudonym) never expected that waking up from this dream would be so painful. Recently, the Guangzhou girl Lily has repeatedly called the Yangcheng Evening News to vent her feelings. Last December, she married Ugo, a Nigerian man she had known for several years. However, after obtaining the marriage certificate and visa-free residency in China, Ugo "turned his back on her."

At this point, she discovered she was pregnant with the child of another African boyfriend, Eric, who disappeared without a trace upon learning about her pregnancy.

"I want to find them and make them take responsibility for what they've done. At the same time, I hope other Chinese girls like me wake up sooner," Lily said.

Just after getting married, Ugo "turned his back"?

27-year-old Lily comes from a well-off family and has been in the mobile phone accessory business for many years. She said, "Many black people come to China to buy mobile phone accessories to sell in Africa. I couldn't avoid dealing with them every day. I had heard bad rumors about them before, but seeing them every day made me get used to it, and I started treating my business partners as friends."

Lily told the reporter that one of these clients from Nigeria was named Ugo. Since 2007, Lily and Ugo had been doing business together, transitioning from business partners to close friends.

"Last September, he suggested taking me to Africa for a trip, and I thought it was a good idea," she said. Upon arriving at a small city in Nigeria, Ugo proposed to her with a "high-quality imitation diamond ring," and in a moment of confusion, she accepted.

"We got two 'marry papers' (marriage certificates) locally, and he insisted that both should be kept by him. Then he asked me to write an application letter to the local embassy. With this letter and the marriage certificate, he claimed he could obtain a free one-year residence permit," Lily explained. "Ugo said that after our marriage, he would open a company in Guangzhou, start a couple-run business, buy a house, and live with me. He would even drive me to and from work... But after I finished writing the application letter, I never saw him again."

After returning to China, Lily reflected on the entire whirlwind marriage process. "I felt like I had been cheated. After coming back, we were still in contact, and I requested to dissolve our marriage. He agreed over the phone but refused to show up."

Pregnancy leading to "disappearance"?

Lily emphasized to the reporter that her marriage to Ugo was indeed a "moment of confusion," and they had not engaged in any sexual relationship. What troubled her more was that she was already two months pregnant, and the father was a Nigerian man named Eric.

"Lily and Eric genuinely loved each other, and they had been dating for more than half a year," Lily described with a hint of nostalgia. "He's 1.93 meters tall, very handsome — you'd fall in love with him too if you saw him. But he's very fickle, truly a heartbreaker."

About a year ago, Lily met Eric through a friend, and they began their relationship. "I found out that he had many girlfriends, mostly met online. There were wealthy women from Shanghai and young girls under 20 from Foshan who even helped pay his rent," she said. "In Nigeria, agreeing to marry Ugo was partly out of frustration."

In December 2011, shortly after returning from her whirlwind marriage, Lily discovered she was pregnant and confessed everything to Eric, the child's father. However, she didn't expect Eric to also "disappear." Her voice carried a sob as she said, "Eric's friends claim they know where he is, but they demand 3,000 yuan first to provide his new phone number and address. I can't tell if they're telling the truth or lying."

When the reporter dialed the phone number provided by Lily for Eric, the voice prompt indicated that the number had expired. The reporter also contacted Eric's roommate via the number provided by Lily. This roommate confirmed that he had recently seen Eric. He speculated that Eric avoided Lily because she "constantly blamed him." According to the roommate, Eric and Lily had only been dating for two months, not "more than half a year" as Lily claimed. Additionally, the reporter was unable to reach Ugo in Africa.

Lily has already reported the case to the Huangqi Police Station where Eric originally rented his apartment.
