1. "Jiang Cheng Zi" by Su Shi: For ten years, life and death have been vast and desolate, not to be thought of, yet unforgettable. A lonely grave a thousand miles away, with no place to speak of sorrow. Even if we met, you probably wouldn't recognize me, my face covered in dust, my temples as white as frost. Last night, in a dream, I suddenly returned home, to the small window where you were combing your hair. We looked at each other in silence, only tears flowed in streams. I can predict the place where I break down every year, under the bright moon, on the short pine hill.

Review: This was a poem written by Su Dongpo after he dreamed of his deceased concubine Wang Zhaojun. The sincere and simple emotion, the painful parting and reunion, moved me every time I read it. Separated by life and death, reunion could only be expected in dreams. What Su Dongpo couldn't let go of for ten years was the affectionate relationship. He couldn't bear the loneliness after losing his companion, rather than the lack of grand love. "Even if we met, you probably wouldn't recognize me, my face covered in dust, my temples as white as frost." In dreams, all that might be seen are fragments of the daily life of the departed loved ones. Because in those trivialities, there is an unbreakable bond of affection. What is the highest realm of love in this world? Holding your hand is one realm, living together through thick and thin is another, and pledging life and death is yet another. There is a most solemn, most simple kind of love called depending on each other for survival. That is the long-term infiltration, a warmth that has been integrated into each other's lives.

2. "Bu Suan Zi" by Li Zhiyi: I live at the head of the Yangtze River, you live at its tail. Every day I think of you but cannot see you, yet we drink the same river water. How long will this water flow, when will this regret end. I only wish your heart is like mine, surely not betraying the meaning of longing.

Review: This short poem by Li Zhiyi consists of only forty-five words, yet it is concise but full of emotion. The whole poem revolves around the Yangtze River water, expressing the yearning and separation of lovers. It begins by describing the two people separated by a great distance, symbolizing the difficulty of meeting, showing deep longing. The last sentence describes "sharing", using water to connect both places and hearts; integrating feelings into water, using water to metaphorically represent feelings, which are equally endless, expressing the female protagonist's unwavering pursuit and fervent hope for love. The entire poem is filled with emotion, layer upon layer, yet also repetitive, a few short sentences with emotional fluctuations.

3. "The Book of Songs - Bei Feng Ji Gu": The drums beat loudly, soldiers are ready to fight. The earth is fortified, I alone travel south. Following Sunzi Zhong, pacifying Chen and Song. Not allowing me to return, my heart is full of worries. Where do I stay? Where did I lose my horse? Where should I look for it? Under the forest. Life and death, parting and reunion, we have agreed. Holding your hand, growing old together. Alas, how far apart we are, unable to live together. Alas, how distant we are, unable to trust each other.

Review: This poem expresses the feelings of soldiers who have been away from home for a long time, missing their hometowns and wives. Among them, the line "Life and death, parting and reunion, we have agreed. Holding your hand, growing old together," has captivated countless people who are in love and desire perfect love, becoming the best representative of eternal vows. Through life and death, joy and sorrow, I once told you that I would hold your hand and grow old with you. This is the highest realm of love, and the highest realm of love is enduring the ordinariness of time.

4. "Shang Ye": Oh heavens! I want to know you, may our lives never fade. Mountains without peaks, rivers drying up, winter thunder rumbling, summer snowfall, heaven and earth merging, only then dare I part with you!

Review: From the Han dynasty folk songs. This is a love song, the protagonist's oath: even if the sea dries up and the rocks crumble, love remains steadfast, a passionate declaration by a devoted woman to her lover. After the protagonist calls to heaven and frankly expresses the desire "to know you, may our lives never fade," she turns to the perspective of "parting with you," using five impossible events to express her undying love, deeply strange and unimaginable. This emphasizes the protagonist's undying love even more intensely.

5. "Xing Xing Chong Xing Xing": Walking further and further, separating from you. We are more than ten thousand miles apart, each at the edge of the sky. The road is blocked and long, when will we meet again? Horses rely on the winter wind, birds nest in southern branches. As days pass, my belt grows looser. Clouds obscure the sun, travelers don't return. Thinking of you makes me old, time passes quickly. Discard these thoughts, take care and eat well.

Review: This is a song of yearning and separation during the turbulent times of the late Eastern Han Dynasty. Although the author's name was lost during its transmission, reading it evokes boundless sadness, repeated reflection, and is touched by the sincerity and pain of the female protagonist's love. The poem has a rich and fresh folk song style, with an internal rhythm of overlapping repetition, expressing the psychological characteristics of Oriental women in love and longing.

6. "Que Qiao Xian" by Qin Guan: Delicate clouds play tricks, flying stars transmit hate, silently crossing the vast Milky Way. Meeting on a golden autumn breeze and jade dew, surpasses countless worldly encounters. Tender emotions flow like water, the date is like a dream, how can one bear to look back at the magpie bridge's return path! If two hearts are truly lasting, why should they be together morning and evening?

Review: "Que Qiao Xian" was originally created to sing about the love story of Niulang and Zhinv. Borrowing the story of Niulang and Zhinv, it uses a supernatural way to express the joys and sorrows of human life. The last sentence offers heartfelt comfort to Niulang and Zhinv: as long as the two hearts remain true until death, why should they seek the pleasures of morning and evening? This shocking and awakening stroke elevates the entire poem to new ideological heights. The author denies the vulgar life of morning pleasures and evening delights, singing instead of the loyal and enduring love across mountains and rivers.

7. "Yan Qiu Ci" by Yuan Haowen: Ask the world, what is love, that it makes life and death promises? Flying together from the south to the north, old wings have endured several winters and summers. Joyful reunions, painful farewells, among them are more foolish sons and daughters. You should have something to say, amidst ten thousand miles of layered clouds, thousands of mountain ranges shrouded in evening snow, whose shadow will you follow?

Review: Once, Yuan Haowen went to Bingzhou for exams. On the way, he met a goose catcher. The goose catcher told Yuan Haowen about a strange event he encountered today: he set a net to catch geese and caught one, but one escaped the net. Surprisingly, the escaped goose didn't fly away, but circled overhead and then dove to its death. Yuan Haowen looked at the two geese in the hands of the goose catcher, feeling emotionally unsettled. He bought the two geese, buried them by the Fen River, piled stones as a marker, named it "Yan Qiu," and wrote this "Yan Qiu Ci." Imagining the pair of geese, "traversing the ends of the earth," flying south in winter and returning north in spring, "enduring several winters and summers," blooming lotus flowers side by side, supporting each other, deeply in love. There were joyful reunions, and there were painful farewells, but nothing could separate them. And after "searching disrupts their shared dreams," the beloved partner has passed away, how can one survive alone! Thus, the "escaped one" made a firm decision to follow to the netherworld, "died by self-inflicted impact." The ancients believed that love reaches its extreme, "the living can die, the dead can live." "Promising life and death" is such an ultimate depth of passion!

I was born before you, you were born after me.

I am far away from you, you are far away from me.

I was born before you, you were born after me.

Transformed into butterflies to seek flowers, nightly resting on fragrant grass.

Review: Just looking at this poem, the language is simple and unadorned, expressing the author's regret and longing for a lover much older than himself. However, due to a tragic online article titled "You Were Born Before Me, I Was Born After You," this poem has been elevated. Readers can search for it, and this article is the best commentary.

9. "Li Si" by Yuan Zhen:

Once having seen the vast ocean, it's hard to admire other waters,

Apart from the clouds over Mount Wu, none can compare.

Among the flowers, I wander freely and look back often,

Half because of practicing Taoism, half because of you.

Review: This is a poem written by the Tang Dynasty poet Yuan Zhen for his lover Cui Yingying. "Once having seen the vast ocean, it's hard to admire other waters, apart from the clouds over Mount Wu, none can compare." This means that someone who has experienced the vastness of the ocean finds it difficult to be attracted by other waters; except for the clouds over Mount Wu, other clouds pale in comparison. Using the ocean water and the clouds over Mount Wu as metaphors for deep and steadfast love, having seen the ocean and Mount Wu, other waters and clouds become unappealing, except for the woman the poet cherishes and loves, no other woman can move him.

Leaning on a high tower against the gentle wind, gazing far into the spring melancholy, darkly rising at the horizon. In the fading light, the grass color blends with the mist, silent, who understands the intention of leaning on the railing? Planning to indulge in wild abandon to get drunk, drinking wine and singing, forced merriment still lacks flavor. My belt grows gradually looser, yet I do not regret, wasting away for her until I am emaciated.

Review: This poem expresses yearning and admiration for the beloved. The entire poem tightly focuses on "spring melancholy" or "longing," yet hesitates to reveal it until the last sentence, when the truth finally emerges. When the longing reaches its peak, the poem abruptly ends, the emotions echoing, with greater感染力. The final sentence expresses a persistent determination and steadfast attitude, hence its widespread popularity.
