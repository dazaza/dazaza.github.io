Analysis of Future Market Predictions for 3G Mobile Phones

by man8737 on 2009-09-16 23:41:59

On August 28, Chang Xiaobing, chairman of China Unicom, announced that China Unicom had reached an agreement with Apple Inc. to cooperate in selling iPhone mobile phones in China for the next three years, and that the iPhone would be launched in China in the fourth quarter of this year.

On August 31, China Mobile also released OPhone, the world's first mobile intelligent software platform developed under the leadership of a carrier, and launched OPhone mobile phones with rich application functions.

As the three major carriers accelerate their investment steps and engage in competition in the construction of 3G base stations, the battle for 3G mobile phone terminals is imminent. Whichever of the three carriers offers better quality and more affordable mobile phones will gain an important competitive edge in the market.

The 3G Apple is not easy to bite

Chang Xiaobing, chairman of China Unicom, announced at the half-year performance press conference that Apple Inc.'s iPhone had obtained network access permits for sales in mainland China and would exclusively cooperate with China Unicom.

Thus, the iPhone, the globally renowned "3G Apple," after being in limbo for nearly two years, has finally landed in China. The negotiation process prior to this was full of twists and turns, and was not at all easy.
