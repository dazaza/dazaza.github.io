Product Interpretation: Your Supply Chain Code

Selling goods, cutting through the complexity, product performance speaks everything! For a long time, e-commerce companies have been competing on the advantage of product integration. So how this batch of goods is realized reveals the current state of your supply chain. After helping e-commerce companies realize products for many years, I've summarized that there are basically three ways to play: spot procurement - production orders - inventory products.

Spot procurement has no cost advantage, styles are hard to unify, but product resources are abundant;

Production orders have high risks and thresholds, but strong product realization capabilities;

Inventory products have large limitations and low sustainability, but high cost-performance ratio and strong risk resistance.

Even if you specialize in one particular way, no matter how well you master it, it will be difficult to break through in the existing e-commerce environment. Our e-commerce product supply chain usually combines two or all three of these methods.

1. Spot Procurement: Market selection and procurement of spot goods are for testing market reactions and risk control.

Often product managers tell me: "Mr. Li, I plan to launch a special session, help me prepare a batch of spot goods." Playing with spot goods, especially those with high cost-performance ratios, branded sources, unified styles, but first-line brands don't give you goods, second and third-line brands willing to give you private label goods, but branders don't stockpile goods, factories won't either. That kind of wholesale market resource has no advantage in the e-commerce market. So spot procurement products can only serve as project preparation or product supplementation, like Uniqlo's Taobao flagship store.

2. Production Orders

A. Many merchants love copying new styles from official websites, despising Taobao styles. It's an undeniable fact that official website styles are at least 1-2 quarters ahead of Taobao styles. However, daring to sell Taobao-selected styles shows confidence in their own operations, especially in the supply chain. Moreover, these are data tested by others, why not make good use of it? Choose styles suitable for high volume and homogenization competition. Don't underestimate this ant vs elephant strategy, it can cause headaches for many big e-commerce platforms. Conversion rates around 5% for shops that aren't overly personalized, copying one style works every time. Beware of copying styles that rely on traffic accumulation, those don't make money. Carefully analyze Taobao's market dynamics. A previous client found a wooden edge cardigan, tested well, he smartly changed it to a mid-length version, tested with traffic, followed up with large traffic activities, Paipai, Taobao, the entire network together, sold ten thousand pieces. Product-wise, changing cotton yarn materials to higher-end 37%, selling cheaper by three to five yuan, becoming a market follower, already making them headache enough.

B. Styles that work well offline will also work well online. If they don't, it's your operation problem. So many people can't understand why many hot-selling styles online are considered outdated by physical stores. Actually, the audience within a few kilometers is completely different from my audience of tens of millions of users. Seasonal clothing changes once a year, a cycle of one or two years is normal. We often take some well-performing regular styles from physical stores and evolve them for e-commerce companies, such as embroidering flowers on the front chest of basic t-shirts, printing patterns on plain cardigans according to our own styles, and they sell well too.

C. Designer brand styles, independently designed, factory produced. D2C is a purely aesthetic unilateral desire, the favorite product realization method fantasized by Beijing-style e-commerce. Especially those new Taobao Mall teams, registering a brand, confidently designing and producing a batch of goods, just watching makes me feel uneasy. Even if you're an amazing designer, you need to give customers a buffer period, a recognition period. Selling goods is what builds a brand, who gives you traffic support? They cultivated for two years, this year just started to show some results. Ask yourself how long you can endure loneliness. So don't start with this, it's too painful.

This time I won't go into depth about how the supply chain factory production specifically deeply cooperates with e-commerce, just roughly mention it.

For the first trial production, don't place too many orders. To reduce risks, distribute among various channels like Taobao, JD.com, Dangdang platform types, or group buying, generally three to five hundred pieces. The first round of orders shouldn't have too many colors and sizes, generally two colors and two sizes. With a sales foundation, we then consider building a pyramid structure, which requires your factory to be flexible and able to withstand pressure when necessary. We cannot continuously use group-buying tools like Juhuasuan to expand the market, but these group-buyings are the best supply chain pressure testing tools. If you're purely preparing goods for Juhuasuan, humanity can no longer stop you from entering a harmonious society. Take a customer's previous wool sweater that sold over ten thousand pieces on Juhuasuan as an example, the conservative play is to prepare up to 30% before the final review, produce after the final review, the first batch of goods out by the 7th day, all out by the 10th day. If using a previous foreign trade order production line, even the workers would struggle to finish in two weeks. This is due to integrating seven or eight external knitting factories, mainly focusing on sewing, post-processing, and final inspection, with quality inspection and follow-up at each step, greatly accelerating production progress. Change your factory's business philosophy, because e-commerce sales fluctuate too much, a single factory can't satisfy you, multiple factories you can't afford to maintain, let the factory integrate peer resources, subcontract procedures, similar to KD structure. After the group buy, it's time to sell overflow value, subsequent production replenishment depends on specific activity needs.

3. Inventory Product Play. Here, inventory products refer to those caused by poor operations or channels, not inferior quality products. Nowadays, most physical brands going online sell mostly inventory goods, which is already a public secret. Products go online, hang for a few weeks, then sell with 5-7 discount activities to clear inventory, user experience. Another way to play with inventory is to change labels. Those with channel restrictions or operational deficiencies may waste excellent products, we change our own labels to sell them. Helping e-commerce find goods and change labels daily, I'm almost becoming a professional label changer now.

Product Style Trends

Uniqlo's market strategy is to grasp the development trend of M-shaped society, being able to grasp the trend of consumer groups is what every e-commerce marketer wants to pursue. Undoubtedly, the first wave of e-commerce products that sell the most are the Japanese-Korean styles for 18-22-year-old girls, including those still dominating e-commerce market sales. But when the online shopping Xia Yuhe from years ago has turned into Grandma Rong, we must think about whether our products are changing too. On the other hand, if you're sensitive enough, you'll notice the recent extremely successful marketing campaign of "Ordinary Youth, Artistic Youth, 2B Youth." Everyone isn't ordinary, nor wants to be 2B, so we all choose artistic. Although I don't have specific market data, judging from the growth of my own customer orders and the current market positioning battle, Chuke, Chuyu Mianhua, Suoran... These are all e-commerce brands playing the artistic style card. We can easily see some signs. If artistic style is currently a niche, I think it's a niche with explosive potential. The evolved simple fresh style from European and American styles.

Finally, emphasize the importance of product PDCA

The most important content for a product manager is planning, purchasing, and inventory model construction. But what struck me most is that only a very few e-commerce apparel companies do product planning. It's not about needing a certain sales scale to do product planning, nor is it about planning what single items to sell in the next one or two months being called product planning. Combine your new product marketing needs with supply chain planning, and also inform your factory, making sure your factory understands your marketing needs and rhythm plans. Otherwise, your product will lose its essence and increasingly deviate from the market. Earlier, I mentioned how others copy your designs, now I'll explain how we help clients prevent design copying.
