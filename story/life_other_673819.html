Reflections on Autumn

Note: Recently, because I have been preparing for a class presentation, I've been reading a lot of romantic novels by female writers. Moreover, the age at which these writers composed their works is similar to mine today, and this has inadvertently led to many resonances. Perhaps some things, despite transcending time, still carry the same essence.

I wonder if everyone is prone to confusion and bewilderment. I often feel my own powerlessness and inefficacy. My heart is constantly pierced by something. Each day seems busy, yet in reality lacks much meaning or gain. Many seemingly subtle trivialities in life can evoke deep reflections within me. I am someone who rarely gains confidence easily. Even though I grew up under my father's extraordinary care and encouragement, I could not escape the inferiority complex rooted deep within me. Especially when it comes to things that I consider important and fear failing, I am so easily negated, plunging me into deep self-doubt. Can I really do it well? How I yearn for even a tiny bit of success to boost my confidence, but even that little bit is shattered.

Because I have long felt the pain of being hurt, I also constantly fear hurting others, as this would undoubtedly be like slaughtering them. I often tell myself to learn to be more steady and rational, preferably keeping emotions from showing on my face. But frequently, I lack endurance and often lose composure amidst the noise. I know that if one is ignorant or lacks sufficient confidence, the best way to avoid trouble is silence. When my heart, which fears hurting others, encounters someone overly sensitive to trivial matters, all I get in return are reproaches. In life, we must learn to earn the respect, affection, and approval of others. But where does this charm and goodwill come from? Perhaps people need to cultivate themselves gradually before they can achieve enlightenment?

Soon, it will be the first anniversary of my father's passing. On these special days, more sadness and memories are inevitably evoked. I often feel a sense of aimless loss. Over the past year, I have frequently dreamed of my father returning to my side, his sweet smile still lingering around me. It was unimaginable that someone in the prime of youth could become so lonely and desolate after losing both parents. I often search every room and corner of our home for my father's presence, and often feel in the depths of my heart that he might have just gone far away. He will definitely return. Only now do I truly understand how much mental anguish my father endured during those twenty-two years of solitude, relying solely on his own efforts.

How much in life is truly worth trusting and believing in! The innocence and romance I once had in my youth have been cruelly severed by harsh realities. All that remains is the responsibility I bear towards family and reality. As for the true love that female writers so ardently pursue, can it really be achieved? Can spirit and body truly achieve unity? In my view, in this era, sex and love must be seen as entirely separate. Spiritual connection may lead to love and marriage, while mere physical experiences only lead to emptiness and nihilism. But can marriages built on the foundation of love ensure happiness? Lovers can change, but can lost youth and chastity be recovered? In this era, after supposedly liberating human nature and removing repressive shackles, can people lose all moral boundaries, using sexual frustration as an excuse to create events of chaos and destruction? Must our spiritual solace depend on sexual satisfaction to resolve? And after that, can we truly enjoy inner joy and fulfillment? With material abundance, desires expand infinitely, yet our spirit and values become increasingly lost. The marital dilemmas that troubled humanity were not merely focal points of the May Fourth era; in new times, reflecting on proper views of marriage has become even more meaningful.

Like many of my peers, I face various challenges in life and love, seeking myself amidst the hustle and bustle. There is pain, there is sorrow, just like Sha菲 in Ding Ling's writing. Like Sha菲, I am searching for the exit in life and existence. I don't know what the future holds, but at least for now, I must strive hard for the future, moving forward amidst contradictions and pain. Sometimes I'm very tired, sometimes very annoyed, but the road must go on.
