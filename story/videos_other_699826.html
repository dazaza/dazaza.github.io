A morning's work, and a few people didn't do a bit of it, but they sure ate a lot for lunch. After the afternoon break, Peng Wei and his crew sat at their respective workstations resting again, and they all fell asleep. The foreman was getting anxious; the schedule was already tight, and these workers were not only eating and staying for free, but also affecting the enthusiasm of other workers. It was really infuriating.

On the first day of work, the foreman saw this and got very angry, constantly urging them, but these seven people acted as if they hadn't heard anything.

Waiting for that sentence

This group of fake migrant workers consisted of seven people, six from Henan and one from Sichuan. Peng Wei from Henan was the leader; he was responsible for contacting projects, and wherever there was a need for workers, he would immediately agree and gather the workers. On September 8, 2010, he led six others from Henan to a construction site in Xinghua, Jiangsu, bringing bedding and settling down properly.

Facing dismissal, besides cursing and causing trouble, Peng Wei and his crew had other ways to cheat money.

It turns out that the seven had already planned before setting off that they wouldn't work well wherever they went, forcing the foreman to dismiss them, and then demand compensation. Back and forth, not only could they earn enough for the travel expenses, but they could also make a little more without doing any actual work. This method of making money was Peng Wei's usual trick. As his accomplice said, "We all know Peng Wei makes a living this way."

Collectively not working

On September 11, the foreman decided not to use these seven people anymore. Upon hearing about being dismissed, the seven suddenly became energetic, "We came such a long way, and you're saying we can't work? That's unacceptable. You must compensate us for travel expenses and lost wages." Peng Wei justified confidently.

"Not paying compensation? We're not leaving," Peng Wei's group sat in the foreman's office, cursing and making a fuss. Both sides argued fiercely and even started fighting. With Peng Wei's advantage in numbers, he refused to compromise at all. The foreman was cursed and beaten, and this group still didn't stop, going to the construction site to cause trouble, preventing other workers from working normally.

Scammers come to make money, usually pretending to be someone, either transformed into some bureau chief or pretending to be section chiefs. But now, even the identity of migrant workers is being faked. Not long ago, the police caught seven fake migrant workers in Jiangning. They traveled through various construction sites in Nanjing, Lishui, Taizhou, Danyang, etc. Their way of making money wasn't by working hard, but by not working hard...

The next day, the foreman assigned tasks, handed out tools, and the seven people started working. However, this group took their time, walking around with tools in hand, or squatting on the side smoking, playing cards, chatting idly. A job that should take an hour took four hours to finish.

They scam six times

On September 10, the foreman watched them all day, but this group became even more scattered. The seven people were assigned to different positions to work, but soon gathered together to play. As Peng Wei put it, "faking work", while the surrounding workers were busy knocking and hammering, only these seven were having fun.

Yesterday morning, Qixia Court heard this case. The seven admitted to the illegal facts without reservation, and the case is still under further review.
