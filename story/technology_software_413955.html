A Superficial Understanding of Why Ningbo Companies Don't Take Outsourcing Projects

Now, having settled in Hangzhou for nearly 3 years, time really flies by. When I used to work in Ningbo, the company had a principle: generally, we didn’t take on outsourcing projects. At the time, I didn’t understand why we didn’t accept outsourcing projects.

Today, several years later, I fully understand why we didn’t take on outsourcing projects.

1: Nowadays, most merchants lack integrity and generally don't abide by contracts. During the process of outsourcing, they often adopt strategies like poaching employees. As the saying goes, "No trickery, no business." Some merchants even start poaching before the contract is properly executed.

It's impossible now to restrict an employee’s personal freedom. If the other side offers more salary and a better working environment, employees are willing to switch jobs. In terms of the entire contract, paying an extra 1000-2000 RMB in salary is often more cost-effective than a commercial contract worth hundreds of thousands or even millions.

If all employees defect, the contract cannot be continued. Not only do you lose your employees, but the contract also fails. There aren't many benefits for IT software companies doing outsourcing.

2: If you encounter shameless, powerful clients, they may directly ask you for your employees. A long time ago, when we did outsourcing business for BenQ in Suzhou, their outsourcing department clearly pointed out that they hoped certain individuals would join their company. If we didn't allow it, there would be no further cooperation. Additionally, the employees themselves were also willing to switch to this company. Everyone hopes to climb higher and flow lower (metaphorically meaning people seek better opportunities). If you don't agree, you get nothing. Even if you agree, it's not just losing some flesh—it results in significant losses and reduced business.

Outsourcing requires a relatively honest and credit-worthy business environment where everyone follows commercial rules.

3: During the outsourcing process, situations often arise where employees leave halfway through. At this point, you need to recruit new people, which is very troublesome. The instability of personnel brings a lot of trouble to the project.

4: During the outsourcing process, many technologies and methods will be learned by the client. Once the client masters everything, there is rarely any sustained cooperation. Or, during the next collaboration, they will push the price down to an extremely low level. Often, outsourcing requires providing complete source code and documentation for the project.

5: Although outsourcing can yield small profits, it cannot ensure long-term profitability. The copyrights of the finished products belong to the client, not the development company. You create one project at a time, and it's impossible to guarantee long-term benefits. Making a profit of 100,000 RMB from a single deal seems like a lot for an individual, but for a formal company, 100,000 RMB is nothing. The risks involved in earning 100,000 RMB are too great. For capable companies, outsourcing is just a burden. Therefore, companies without economic pressure or operational pressure simply don't engage in outsourcing business, not even discussing it.

Focusing on permission management and workflow management to the best of my ability, a person can only excel in a few things.
