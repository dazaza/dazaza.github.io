Guangzhou Walmart's approaching expiration date products are at least 20% off, most supermarkets do not have a famous brand leather goods network

by kecnlv88qx on 2012-03-07 16:29:05

Recently, the State Administration for Industry and Commerce issued a notice requesting food operators to make prominent reminders to consumers about food that is nearing its expiration date, also known as approaching-expiry food, at their business premises.

When reporters visited several stores yesterday, they found that except for a few cases, the vast majority of supermarkets have not established "approaching-expiry food sections" nor made prominent reminders about such food. Some supermarkets even promote these foods nearing their expiry dates by bundling them with fresh products as gifts.

At Walmart on Zhongshan Road, reporters saw a special "approaching-expiry goods section" set up in the third-floor food sales area, where shelves were stocked with various foods nearing their expiration dates, along with labels stating "Please consume before the effective expiration date." A supermarket staff member told reporters that this section mainly features food items, which are usually sold at discounted prices due to their approaching expiration dates.

"These borderline foods are generally sold at half price, with deeper discounts closer to the expiration date, sometimes even reaching 20% of the original price. We almost change the tags every day. These goods are previously agreed upon with manufacturers not to be returned. To reduce the loss of goods being taken off the shelves on time, we do this to respond to national regulations," the staff member said.

Compared to Walmart, the "Clearance Sale Section for Approaching-Expiry Goods" set up by CR Vanguard contains relatively fewer items, mainly focusing on daily necessities like shampoo and shower gel. Due to their longer shelf life, many of these items do not have significant discounts.

Since the notice issued by the State Administration for Industry and Commerce was not a mandatory regulation, during interviews, reporters found that apart from a few exceptions, the vast majority of supermarkets have not established "approaching-expiry food sections" nor made prominent reminders about such food. Many supermarkets handle food nearing its expiration date through "special promotions" or "bundled sales."

In the dairy product area of a supermarket in the East District, reporters saw bundled packages of yogurt where the production dates of two bottles differed by six days compared to another bottle. The packaging of the bundled yogurt only had a promotional label and no mention of it being an approaching-expiry item.

In the bread section of another large supermarket, reporters observed that loaves of bread with different production dates were all placed together for sale. Notably, the fresher bread was placed inside the shelf, while the older ones were placed at the front. A supermarket staff member informed reporters that there are dedicated food inspectors responsible for checking if any goods are nearing their expiration date.

"For food nearing its expiration date, we usually conduct discount sales or 'buy one get one free' promotions. If the promotion doesn't clear the stock, we will return the goods to the manufacturer where possible, ensuring that no expired food remains on the shelves," the staff member explained.

Regarding the supermarket's practice of bundling approaching-expiry items, many citizens interviewed expressed that consumers should have the right to know about the condition of the goods. Large supermarkets should quickly establish sections for selling food nearing its expiration date and provide clear reminders to customers.

(Source: Southern Daily, You Yu Hua)
