Pregnant women need to be alert to 3 types of abnormal fetal movements

by childrenstory126 on 2012-02-23 15:26:56

Pregnant women can distinctly feel the movements of their fetus starting from the 5th month of pregnancy. Normal fetal movement indicates good uterine placental function, sufficient oxygen supply to the fetus, and healthy growth and development within the uterus, with the fetus moving happily. However, when abnormal fetal movements occur, pregnant women should take note. Below are three possible types of abnormal fetal movements.

1. A sudden decrease in fetal movement.

Diagnosis: Fever in the pregnant woman.

Expert Analysis: Generally speaking, even if the pregnant woman has a mild fever, the fetus is protected by the amniotic fluid and will not be greatly affected.

What needs attention is the cause of the fever in the pregnant woman. If it's due to a common cold, it won't significantly affect the fetus. However, if it's an infectious disease or influenza, especially for pregnant women nearing their due date, the impact on the fetus could be more significant.

If the pregnant woman's body temperature remains excessively high, exceeding 38 degrees Celsius, it will reduce the blood flow to the placenta and uterus, causing the fetus to become much quieter. Therefore, for the baby's health, the pregnant woman should go to the hospital as soon as possible and seek help from a doctor.

Expert Suggestions:

- During pregnancy, the pregnant woman should rest well and avoid catching a cold.

- When there are outbreaks of diseases, the pregnant woman should avoid crowded places.

- Keep the indoor air circulating and fresh every day.

- Drink plenty of water, eat more fresh vegetables and fruits.

2. A sudden increase in fetal movement.

Diagnosis: The pregnant woman suffers a severe external injury.

Expert Analysis: Generally, the fetus inside the mother’s womb is protected by the amniotic fluid, which reduces the impact of external forces. In case of minor accidental collisions, the fetus may not be harmed. However, if the pregnant woman suffers from serious external force impacts, it will cause intense fetal movement and may even lead to miscarriage or premature birth. Additionally, if the pregnant woman experiences head injuries, fractures, or heavy bleeding, abnormal fetal movements may also occur.

Expert Suggestions:

- Pregnant women should try to avoid crowded places to prevent being bumped into.

- Pregnant women should reduce activities that require large physical exertion.

3. Fetal movement suddenly intensifies and then quickly stops.

Diagnosis: Premature placental abruption.

Expert Analysis: This condition usually occurs after the middle of pregnancy and is more common in pregnant women with hypertension, severe trauma, or rapid reduction of intrauterine pressure. Symptoms include vaginal bleeding, abdominal pain, uterine contractions, and severe shock. Once such a situation occurs, the fetus will also react, showing brief intense fetal movements due to sudden oxygen deficiency, followed by a quick cessation.

Expert Suggestions:

- Pregnant women with hypertension should regularly visit the hospital for check-ups and follow the doctor’s advice for daily life arrangements.

- Pregnant women should avoid unnecessary external collisions and stimuli.

- Pregnant women should maintain a good mindset and relax.
