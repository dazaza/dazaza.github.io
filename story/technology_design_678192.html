Essential Methods for Men to Protect Their Liver while Drinking

by yangliu435sh on 2012-02-27 12:21:09

Men like to drink alcohol, or for some reasons, they may consume some alcohol regularly. Their wives can't help but worry about their husbands' health. Health experts have proposed seven tips regarding drinking that can help drinkers reduce the chances of getting drunk and minimize the harm caused by alcohol.

Tip 1: Drink at the ideal speed

The ideal speed means a drinking pace that does not exceed the liver's processing capacity. The liver breaks down alcohol at a rate of about 10 milliliters per hour. The amount of pure alcohol (ethanol) in the drink can be calculated from the alcohol content indicated on the label of the bottle. For example, a 250 milliliter drink with an alcohol content of 16%, would contain 40 milliliters of alcohol (250ml x 0.16 = 40ml). If one drinks it over four hours, the average alcohol intake per hour is 10 milliliters, which aligns with the liver's processing speed. Drinking at this pace can significantly reduce the burden on the liver.

Tip 2: Drink water to restore the body's water balance

Alcohol can alter the water balance inside and outside the body's cells. Normally, two-thirds of the body's water is inside the cells. However, after alcohol consumption, the water inside the cells moves into the blood vessels. Even though the total body water remains unchanged, due to the reduction of water inside the cells, one might feel thirsty. Regardless of the reason, it is always beneficial to take in sufficient water. "Sobering water" has been a method used since ancient times to relieve discomfort after drinking. Mixing three small pinches of salt into a full glass of water and drinking it down will stimulate the stomach and make it easier to vomit out food.

Tip 3: Consuming sports drinks and fruit juice is also effective

On the morning after excessive drinking, one often feels a dry throat upon waking up. At this time, there is residual alcohol and harmful substance acetaldehyde in the body, and these should be expelled as soon as possible. Drinks containing inorganic salts and sugar not only replenish water but also eliminate alcohol in the body. Sports drinks and fruit juices are very effective. Especially sports drinks, whose composition closely resembles human body fluid, making them easy for the body to absorb. They are not only effective for hangovers but also prevent severe drunkenness if consumed while drinking. Moreover, tea rich in tea polyphenols and vitamin C, or honey lemonade made with lemon and honey, are also effective for hangovers.

Note: No matter what kind of beverage, if it is too cold, the active ingredients will not be easily absorbed, so it should be consumed at room temperature or warm.

Tip 4: Eating persimmons helps decompose alcohol

Persimmons are fruits rich in fructose and vitamin C, and have been used since ancient times as an effective food to prevent drunkenness and alleviate hangovers. The astringent components in sweet persimmons can break down alcohol, and the potassium content promotes diuresis. Persimmon leaves also contain dozens of times more vitamin C than citrus fruits, and their tender young buds can be fried or dried to make persimmon leaf tea.

Tip 5: Eat more shellfish to enhance the liver's detoxification function

Take clams as an example, their nutritional composition contains protein comparable to eggs. Moreover, because they contain balanced essential amino acids, they do not impose a burden on the liver and can promote its recovery. Shellfish usually contain abundant vitamin B12, taurine, and glycogen; vitamin B12 and glycogen also play important roles in promoting liver function. Taurine, an amino acid, combines with bile acids to activate the liver's detoxification function.

Tip 6: Drink aloe vera juice to lower blood acetaldehyde concentration

Aloe vera's spiky green parts and its internal gel contain polysaccharides and glycoproteins, which can reduce the concentration of harmful acetaldehyde produced after alcohol decomposition in the blood. Therefore, drinking aloe vera juice before drinking alcohol can effectively prevent headaches, nausea, red face, and other symptoms. Additionally, aloin, the bitter component in aloe vera, has a stomach-strengthening effect and can treat nausea and vomiting caused by hangovers.

Tip 7: Eat protein-rich foods to reduce the liver's burden

Protein and fat stay in the stomach the longest, making them the most suitable as side dishes with alcohol. To avoid gaining weight from consuming too much high-protein food, it is best to choose fish, shellfish, lean meat, chicken, tofu, eggs, cheese, etc. Dairy products such as milk and cheese, eggs, tofu, scallops, and soups made from these ingredients benefit liver function and do not burden the stomach. Some people like to eat salty foods after drinking, such as oily noodles, which can burden the gastrointestinal tract and prolong the feeling of drunkenness. Therefore, it is better to choose fruits, honeyed milk, yogurt, eggs, and other easily digestible foods that improve liver function.

As men enter their thirties, their health often declines, such as gaining weight, reducing physical strength, energy, and sexual function. At this point, what should you do for your health? Some people first think of tonifying the kidneys, but in fact, this is akin to "picking up sesame seeds and losing watermelons."

In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), the kidney is considered the foundation of innate constitution. It is natural for elderly men with kidney deficiency to appropriately tonify the kidney, but middle-aged men don't need to follow this trend. The liver, however, is different. It governs free flow and prefers smoothness, functioning smoothly when unobstructed. If liver qi stagnates, the body’s circulation of qi and blood becomes disordered, leading to digestive dysfunction, hypertension, and other diseases. Middle-aged men are at the forefront of career and family life, facing great psychological pressure and mental suppression, easily causing liver qi stagnation, irritability, anger, anxiety, loss of appetite, and other symptoms. Additionally, men often attend social gatherings, smoke excessively, and drink heavily, coupled with the rampant hepatitis virus, often affecting the liver. Diseases such as "fatty liver" and "viral hepatitis" quietly target you. Modern medicine places even greater emphasis on the relationship between the liver and health or even life. Medical experts refer to the liver as the "chemical factory" within the human body. All nutrients consumed in three meals must go through its metabolic processing, converting them into biologically active proteins, lipids, and glycogen to supply the needs of all organs in the body. If the liver malfunctions, it will affect the entire body and even threaten life.

So, how exactly do we protect it? Health experts give you six "golden ideas":

"Golden Idea" 1: Diet to protect the liver

There are two key points in dietary liver protection: First, select the right foods to provide adequate nutrients to meet the various physiological needs of the liver; second, pay attention to food hygiene to prevent bacteria and viruses from invading the liver.

Nutritionists tell us that the five major nutrients the human body needs—proteins, fats, carbohydrates, vitamins, and mineral elements—are also what the liver requires. However, the liver needs more proteins, carbohydrates, and vitamins, while excessive fat can lead to fatty liver, so it must be appropriately restricted. Therefore, it is suggested to incorporate the following details into your daily diet:

Include milk, eggs, fish, lean meat, soy products, etc., in your daily diet rotation to provide the liver with sufficient high-quality protein.

Consume appropriate amounts of easily digestible monosaccharides and disaccharides such as glucose, sucrose, honey, and fruit juice to increase liver glycogen reserves.

Do not neglect yeast, which is rich in B vitamins.

Hawthorn contains ursolic acid, which can reduce the deposition of animal fat in the vascular wall and has a certain effect in preventing or alleviating arteriosclerosis. Eating fresh hawthorns regularly, drinking hawthorn tea, or adding hawthorn when stewing meat can both flavor the dish and aid digestion.

Green tea clears heat, detoxifies, eliminates food, and relieves greasiness; chrysanthemum soothes the liver and brightens the eyes; rose flowers soothe the liver and relieve depression. Drinking these teas regularly benefits liver protection.

Wolfberry nourishes the liver and kidneys, benefiting the eyes, and can be brewed as tea, added to soup, or cooked into porridge.

Eating nuts like walnuts and pistachios can regulate liver qi and relieve anxiety.

People with signs of liver qi deficiency, such as yellow complexion and poor sleep, can eat livestock or poultry liver once a week to achieve the effect of "nourishing the liver with liver."

Avoid alcohol and all spicy and stimulating foods. Avoid fried and hard foods.

Prevent food contamination, including pesticide residues in vegetables and melons; certain food additives, such as flour whitening agents and preservatives; smoked and roasted foods, and spoiled foods, such as rotten ginger, red-colored yuanxiao (rice balls), and sprouted potatoes. Measures: try to buy vegetables with light or no pesticide pollution and rinse them multiple times with clean water. Peel melons and fruits. Try to eat fewer or no additive-containing foods, such as canned goods. Stay away from smoked and spoiled foods.

Drink more plain boiled water. Keep the water fresh, drink 3-4 times a day, each time a small bowl. Plain boiled water increases circulating blood volume, enhances liver cell activity, and helps eliminate metabolic waste, thereby achieving liver protection.

"Golden Idea" 2: Sleep to protect the liver

During sleep, the body is in a lying position, allowing the liver to enjoy more blood irrigation. With the body in a resting state, the liver bears the least burden, making high-quality sleep highly effective for liver protection. Conversely, poor sleep quality, especially sleep disorders, can easily affect liver function. Medical experts have noticed that a sleep disorder called sleep apnea syndrome can cause liver damage.

Sleep apnea syndrome refers to brief cessation of breathing during sleep due to narrowing of the tongue-pharynx area and blockage of the airway by the uvula. In severe cases, the breathing pause can last more than one and a half minutes. Medical data shows that approximately 32% of people with severe sleep apnea exhibit abnormal liver function, with the degree of liver damage being proportional to the severity of the respiratory pause. Further studies have found that this liver damage is related to hypoxia and insulin resistance caused by sleep apnea.

It is clear that improving sleep quality primarily involves actively treating sleep disorders such as insomnia and sleep apnea syndrome. Secondly, do not engage in overly mentally taxing work in the evening, and do not stay up late. Some people's poor liver health is often due to staying up late and sleeping late. According to TCM, among the day's sleep, the two most important times are noon (from 11 am to 1 pm) and midnight (from 11 pm to 1 am). These four hours are also the time for bone marrow hematopoiesis, with the most blood flowing through the liver, which is beneficial for liver function repair. In other words, you need to take advantage of naps and nighttime sleep, especially nighttime sleep. It is best to go to bed before 10 pm and fall asleep around 11 pm to lay the groundwork for liver function repair.

"Golden Idea" 3: Exercise to protect the liver

Actively engaging in physical exercise is another effective way to protect the liver, as exercise can reduce excess weight, prevent obesity, and eliminate the harm excessive fat poses to the liver. It also promotes gas exchange and accelerates blood circulation, ensuring the liver receives more oxygen and nutrients.

From the perspective of liver protection, first, choose a good exercise venue, preferably a place with wide space, open views, and fresh air; second, choose appropriate exercise programs. Exercises aimed at building endurance and physical strength, such as low-intensity dynamic exercises, are preferable, such as jogging, brisk walking (approximately 110~120 steps per minute), cycling, climbing stairs, hiking, playing badminton, kicking shuttlecock, bouncing a ball, dancing, jumping rope, swimming, practicing Tai Chi, etc. Do these exercises once a day, each lasting 20~30 minutes, with fatigue disappearing within 10~20 minutes after exercise being ideal.

"Golden Idea" 4: Emotional regulation to protect the liver

Regulating emotions and resolving negative feelings in the heart ensures one always maintains a good mood, which is beneficial for liver health preservation. If emotions fluctuate, it can cause hormonal secretion imbalance, leading to circulatory disorders and affecting the liver's blood supply, causing liver cells to die from lack of blood.
