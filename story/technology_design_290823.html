The report released exclusively by the National Information Center in China Securities on the 3rd believed that next year, there is likely to be a GDP growth rate of around 8.5% in our country, roughly equal with 2009. There is a hope for steady economic growth and prices, meaning that within the short term, there will not be obvious inflation. However, in the future it will not be suitable to further increase the strength of expansion policies. Analysts believe that the entire report does not have too many new ideas; basically it is within everyone’s expectations. The tendency of the policy will not change; there will be minor adjustments made regarding anti-inflation measures. The main focus will be whether or not the M2 growth rate can be controlled at around 18%. This year, M2 growth is expected to reach 30%. On such a high basis, continuing with so much monetary supply will still make liquidity loose within society, which would be beneficial to the stock market and real estate market. Due to the factor of the suspension of new share issuance during the first half of this year, it is expected that the scale of enterprise financing from the securities market next year will be greater than this year. Judging from the recent rapidly increasing issuance speed, the new share financing speed tomorrow will also not be slow. The capital pressure on the market will be greater than this year. With these two factors offsetting each other, the difficulty for the market in 2010 to achieve such high increases like this year will increase.

Large-cap blue chips are weaker than small- and medium-sized stocks. Sinopec fell 1.14%, PetroChina fell 0.37%, and Ping An fell 1.51%. The shareholders on the Chinese side of Shanghai Disneyland have initially formed a picture: Lujiazui Group holds 60% of shares, reactivating the Disney concept, causing Lujiazui to rise 6.57%. The General Manager of Shenzhen Stock Exchange stated that margin trading has been prepared, resulting in an excellent performance of broker stocks early in the day, with Haitong Securities rising 3.36%. The ChiNext continued to extend yesterday's large increase, among which Dayu Water Conservation hit the daily limit. Agricultural stocks continued their recent strong momentum, reversing upward today, with Denghai Seeds hitting the daily limit. Automobile stocks with new energy concepts continued to rise, with Sinoma Automobile hitting the daily limit again. In addition, shipbuilding, medical devices, and instruments and meters sectors ranked at the top in terms of gains, while aircraft manufacturing, real estate, and nonferrous metals sectors were relatively weak.

Institutions believe that the market has recently welcomed a true small-cap stock rally. The main reason may be the high bonus issue theme. This is because the high bonus issue theme is often one of the main themes at the end of the year and the beginning of the next. Since companies in the SME board with higher capital reserves per share, higher undistributed profits per share, and smaller share capital often have higher expectations for high dividends. Furthermore, those newly listed secondary stocks this year, due to high-priced issuance and high capital reserves, have laid the groundwork for next year's high bonus issues. Therefore, finding some individual stocks with potential for bonus issues is a direction for investment in the SME board in December.

Mastering basic stock knowledge, stock knowledge, and stock forum information before conducting research. The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows: when buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. Moreover, this order is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock you want to buy or sell. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the code of the stock is six digits. When placing an order, the code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent. Additionally, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks being bought or sold: the quantity of stocks being purchased must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks being sold does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four methods of placing orders: counter submission orders, telephone automatic orders, computer automatic orders, and remote terminal orders.
