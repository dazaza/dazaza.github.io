At graduation, the girl said to the boy: "I'm going to Beijing. Zhongguancun in Beijing is known as China's Silicon Valley, where there are more opportunities and a better chance for success." The boy replied, "Then I'll go back to my hometown in Sichuan, which is called the 'Heavenly Country' with many beautiful women. If you succeed and no longer want me, it will be easy for me to find someone else."

The girl playfully hit the boy's chest and pouted, saying, "You're always thinking about beautiful women. Hmph, even if I don't want you anymore, you can only think of me and love me. You're not allowed to find other girls."

The boy held the girl's hand, kissed her forehead tenderly, and said, "Silly girl, our parents are in Sichuan. Since you're going to Beijing, I, your good future son-in-law, should go back to take care of my future father-in-law and mother-in-law."

Tears welled up in the girl's eyes as she threw herself into the boy's arms and never wanted to get up. Though they were separated by distance, their feelings remained strong. Their deep longing could only be conveyed through radio waves—sending text messages and making phone calls. They contributed wholeheartedly to the turnover of the country's telecommunications industry.

One day, the girl read a story online about a couple. Every time they made a call, the boy would wait for the girl to hang up first. After the girl had experienced much of life, she realized that the man who loved her most was the boy who always waited for her to hang up first.

The girl remembered this touching and tearful story. One evening after finishing a call, she told the boy, "You hang up first."

The boy was surprised and said, "Silly girl, does it matter who hangs up first when we're on the phone?"

The girl teased him, saying, "No, no, you have to hang up first. If you don't, I won't talk to you anymore, and you'll never find a wife."

The boy paused for a few seconds, chuckled lightly, and said, "Alright, silly girl. To make sure I don't end up alone and become a scammer, I'll hang up first. You're just wasting phone credits."

After hearing the boy hang up and the first busy tone, the girl smiled happily, whispering in her heart to the boy, "Dear, I love you, more than you love me."

From then on, every time they said goodbye during a call, she would quietly listen until the boy hung up first. The boy would always laugh and affectionately call her "silly girl" before hanging up.

As time passed, the girl began to feel a faint sense of injustice: "Do you know? Hmph, every time I wait for you to hang up first, I silently love you so much, but you don't even realize it." She wanted the boy to read that article so he could understand how much she loved him. Perhaps one day, he would let her hang up first, allowing her to truly feel what it means to be loved.

The girl endured, finding happiness in both sweetness and bitterness. She thought, "Being able to deeply love a man in such a unique way for a lifetime is also a kind of happiness."

Like all those who drift to Beijing, the girl's life wasn't comfortable. However, living in a dormitory building was better than those who lived in basements. Her initial ambitions had been worn down to a small tail, but the strong girl never complained to the boy. Instead, she grew more accustomed to telling him, "I love you."

The community where the dormitory building was located had poor security, and there was even a pervert who stole women's underwear. Previously, with a roommate around, the girl hadn't felt too worried, but now the roommate had taken leave due to family matters, leaving the girl alone in a two-bedroom apartment with a kitchen and bathroom. Naturally, she felt lonely and afraid.

That night, the girl was awakened from her sleep by strange sounds. Listening carefully, she realized it was coming from the door. She clutched the blanket tightly, trembling all over, not daring to breathe. Helpless tears silently streamed down her face.

Suddenly, her hand touched the mobile phone under the pillow, like grabbing a lifeline. She immediately sent a text message to the boy: "Dear, I'm scared."

Of course, the boyfriend was far away in Sichuan. What could a text message do? Moreover, it was late at night; the boy might have already turned off his phone and gone to sleep. The girl forgot to call the police first; in her most fearful moment, she could only think of the boy.

To the girl's surprise, a few seconds after sending the message, the boy's call came in. She answered quietly, hearing the boy's concerned voice: "Silly girl, are you missing me?"

The girl tried to lower her voice as much as possible, telling the boy that she was alone in the house and suspected there was a thief outside the door. She was very scared.

The boy consoled the girl not to be afraid. After thinking for a moment, he told her: "Turn on the external speaker of your phone, set the volume to the maximum, and slowly go to the door. Don't be afraid, darling, trust me, don't be afraid."

The girl was smart and immediately understood the boy's intention: The boy would shout loudly, letting the person outside know that there was a man inside. Anyone trying to steal or with other intentions would quickly leave.

The girl carefully approached the door, turned on the external speaker, set the volume to the maximum, and softly said into the microphone, "Okay, I'm at the door, and the external speaker is on."

At this point, the girl confirmed that there were people outside, and not just one. She could hear their quiet conversations.

Just as the girl's body was shaking to the point of almost collapsing, the phone suddenly shouted loudly: "What the hell? Who's messing with my door? All the brothers inside are up, we've got visitors!"

The boy's voice was loud and rough, startling the girl even in the quiet night. However, the people outside must have been even more frightened. The girl only heard a series of thumping footsteps moving further away—it seemed they had been scared off.

The girl exhaled a breath of relief, her legs giving out as she slumped to the ground.

After waiting a while, the boy gently asked, "Are the people outside gone, baby?"

The girl finally cried, speaking into the phone, "Dear, I miss you."

Still shaken, the girl was comforted by the boy. That night, the two talked on the phone until dawn. The girl said, "We should hang up soon; the long-distance call has lasted so long, it must cost a lot."

The boy laughed and said, "You're really a silly girl." The girl responded, "Yes, I'm silly, silly enough to fall for you. Hang up, dear. Be careful not to fall asleep at work today, boss."

After hanging up, the girl felt a surge of sweetness in her heart. She enjoyed the sense of security the boy gave her. However, there was a slight regret: The boy seemed to have formed the habit of hanging up first, and this time was no exception. The girl thought, "Although he is great, he doesn't seem to love me as deeply as the boy in that story loves the girl. He has never let me hang up first."

As the weather started to get warmer, the girl's many single-layer clothes didn't have pockets, so she often forgot her phone. For example, she left it on her desk during lunch breaks or at home when she went out with her roommate. Each time she returned, she would find several missed calls and messages from the boy. Only at these moments did she feel a bit more balanced: "Hmph, you always hang up first. Not being able to answer your calls in time is a small punishment. Don't feel sorry, you big dummy."

On May 12th, an ordinary day, the girl was diligently working at her small company, striving tirelessly for her tiny dream.

As the workday was nearing its end, news spread through the office: A massive earthquake had occurred, with the epicenter in Wenchuan, Sichuan. It was said that the magnitude was similar to the Tangshan earthquake.

The girl's heart skipped a beat, instinctively reaching for her phone in her pocket—she had forgotten it! She immediately picked up the office landline to call the boy, but the signal cut off as soon as she dialed. She then tried calling his home number, but it was also unreachable. It appeared that the earthquake had severely damaged the communication infrastructure in Sichuan.

A foreboding feeling rose within her. Unable to wait for the workday to end, the girl rushed out of the office building, hailed a taxi, and headed toward the dormitory building where she rented a room.

Turning on her phone, she found more than fifty missed calls, all from the boy. She checked and found an unread text message:

"Darling, sweetheart, silly girl, I call you with all the love I have in this life—I love you, more than you love me. Since that time you insisted I hang up first, I knew you must have read that story too. But, darling, I want to tell you—I love you, more than you love me.

Actually, I've read that story too, a long time ago. It’s a beautiful story, beautiful because of its imperfection. But it doesn’t belong to us. I don’t want that kind of beauty, that kind of imperfect beauty—I don’t want it! Heart-wrenching pain will accompany unforgettable love. I’d rather we live peacefully together for a lifetime than experience that unforgettable pain. I only want to spend my life with you, holding your hand until we grow old together.

I don’t want that sorrowful beauty; I only want real happiness. I’ve never dared to forget my phone. I’m afraid that one day you’ll miss me, and if I don’t have my phone, I’ll be as disappointed as you. Many times, even when I go to the restroom, I still keep my phone in my pocket. I never turn off my phone at night. Before sleeping, I always change the battery and increase the ringtone volume, fearing that one night you might be scared and want to talk to me. If I turned off my phone, you'd be lonelier in another place.

Each time you let me hang up first, I know it's because you love me. I’m happy about it, and every time I think about it, my eyes well up. My phone is never turned off, but you don’t know why—that’s because I love you even more. Don’t blame me for not telling you, my treasure. I wanted to wait until we’re both old and gray to say it.

Silly girl, it seems I don’t have the fortune to accompany you for a lifetime. The precast slab on my back has been pressing on me for over two hours. My entire chest and back are tearing with pain, and I can smell the metallic scent of my own blood. Darling, I may not be able to continue walking alongside you.

Dear, I want to hear your voice. I’ve called you repeatedly, but why don’t you answer? Dear, can you hear me calling you? Dear, it’s so dark here, I’m so cold. I want you to hold me.

Dear, my beloved, my treasure, I love you. Right now, I am so cowardly, so afraid of death, because it means I can no longer kiss you or cherish you. I’m more worried that you will grieve endlessly over me. Please don’t, dear. I’m leaving, and you should find someone else in Beijing to take care of you. There are many successful people there with plenty of opportunities. You’re the purest angel in heaven. Without anyone to protect you, I fear you might get hurt.

Allow me, dear, if another man loves you as much as I do, never ignore his calls. I know how much it would hurt him then.

I can't breathe anymore, darling. Goodbye. In the next life, I will definitely be your husband!

Darling, my beloved, silly girl, once again, I call you with all the love I have in this life—I love you, more than you love me."

The girl's tears flowed like a broken dam, crying so hard she couldn’t catch her breath. She looked up to the sky, eyes tightly closed, and screamed with all her heart:

"Dear, in the next life, I will still be your wife. I will never turn off my phone again..."
