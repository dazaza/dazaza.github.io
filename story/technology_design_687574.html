On the 25th at 14:00, the Super Cup: Guangzhou vs Tianjin; at 16:00, the National Women's Volleyball League: Guangzhou vs Tianjin. This was an ordinary Saturday, but also a Saturday destined to be awaited by Tianjin fans.

Guangzhou, this metropolis that people usually associate with vibrant colors, has never before attracted so much attention from Tianjin fans as it does today.

After successfully winning the FA Cup, Tianjin Teda finally had the chance to compete for the coveted China Football Super Cup in Guangzhou. Meanwhile, after being reversed by Guangzhou's volleyball team at home, Tianjin's volleyball team must once again demonstrate their dominant spirit and carve out a path in the home court of the Lang Ping-led team to regain their championship momentum.

This was an ordinary Saturday, but also a decisive battle Saturday.

Unexpected Event:

Pre-match Training - Zhang Xiaoting Injures Her Right Eye

With the life-and-death battle approaching, an unexpected event occurred during Tianjin Volleyball Team’s preparation in Yangcheng. Main blocker Zhang Xiaoting was hit in her right eye by the ball during training, causing inflammation and eyelid congestion. Due to the team's critical need for personnel, Zhang Xiaoting resolutely expressed her determination to participate despite the injury.

Yesterday afternoon, the Tianjin team conducted its first venue training session at the Guangzhou Sport University Gymnasium. With a sudden rise in temperature recently, the highest temperature yesterday even reached 26 degrees Celsius, making the players feel stiflingly hot inside the venue. The plastic floor became slippery due to increased humidity in the air. Soon after the start of the training, head coach Liu Xiaoming noticed this issue and immediately reminded the players to pay attention to their footwork to avoid accidental injuries.

Despite the entire team's heightened vigilance to prevent accidents before the major competition, what everyone feared still happened. During defense training, Zhang Xiaoting's right eye was struck diagonally by the ball. At the time, her contact lens was directly hit and disappeared, causing her intense pain that made her burst into tears instantly. Concerned about possible damage to the retina, the coaching staff quickly decided to send team doctor Wang Xueyi along with Zhang Xiaoting to the hospital for examination. Fortunately, detailed checks confirmed no damage to the retina. There was only some eyelid congestion and slight abrasion on the eyeball surface caused by the contact lens falling off. The doctor prescribed some anti-inflammatory medication for Zhang Xiaoting. On the way back to the hotel, Zhang Xiaoting re-fitted another contact lens.

Back at the hotel, it was already past the team's mealtime, yet team leader Chang Liangcai and coach Li Shan were still waiting in the dining room. After understanding Zhang Xiaoting's situation, Li Shan joked, "Getting a pair of contact lenses is no longer useful; you should quickly get a large-framed glasses like the former Dutch footballer Davids wore, which would look cool on the field!"

According to the plan, the Tianjin team will conduct the second venue adaptation training session at the Guangzhou Sport University Gymnasium in the morning today. □

Logistics:

All Mobilized, No Relaxation in Food, Clothing, Housing, and Transportation

The second leg of the semi-final between Evergrande Volleyball Team and Tianjin Team is about to begin. To ensure players have sufficient rest time, Tianjin specially put great effort into logistics support during this trip to Yangcheng. However, the importance Evergrande Group placed on this semi-final clearly cannot compare with the football Super Cup, as until yesterday, the ticketing plan for this match had not been announced externally.

During the regular season when the Tianjin team visited Yangcheng, they were arranged by Evergrande to stay at a budget hotel located at the boundary between Guangzhou and Foshan. It took a half-hour drive to reach the competition venue located in the city center, and if caught in traffic, the time was unpredictable. To allow players to have ample rest time, this time Tianjin specially arranged for staff to come to Guangzhou earlier to investigate hotels, ultimately choosing a four-star hotel opposite Guangzhou Sport University. A walk to the competition venue takes no more than ten minutes, greatly saving daily training travel time. Additionally, the Sports Working Team dispatched several more logistics support personnel to Guangzhou, meticulously arranging catering and transportation issues, allowing the coaching staff to focus more energy on preparing for the competition. □

Dual City Battle:

Super Cup

Giving Way to Women's Volleyball

In February in Guangzhou, a scene of early spring, because of the two focal events in domestic football and volleyball taking place, Guangzhou naturally became the focus of media and fans. Coincidentally, the Super Cup final between Evergrande and Teda and the peak confrontation between Guangzhou Evergrande Women's Volleyball Team and Tianjin Women's Volleyball Team in the semi-finals of the Women's Volleyball League are both scheduled for the afternoon of the 25th, adding more highlights to these two events. Notably, considering CCTV Sports Channel's live broadcast of these two events, the host has already moved up the start time of the Super Cup to allow fans to continuously enjoy this rare dual-city showdown.

In fact, it is quite coincidental that two key events are held in the same city on the same day. Although the Super Cup game time and location were determined before the Spring Festival, the Women's Volleyball League did not finalize the semi-final matchups until the last round of the top 8 league matches. The top-ranked Evergrande Women's Volleyball Team naturally secured the opportunity to play the second leg at home, leading to this rare "dual-city battle."

Perhaps the schedule was not initially considered when setting it, so the FA Association set the Super Cup start time at 3:30 PM, while the Volleyball Association set the start time for the Evergrande vs Tianjin Women's Volleyball match at 4:00 PM, resulting in a scheduling conflict. Since CCTV Sports Channel planned to broadcast both events live, the times had to be readjusted. Fortunately, after negotiations, the Super Cup gave way to women's volleyball, moving the start time to 2:00 PM, allowing the two events to be consecutively presented, resolving the difficulties of television broadcasts and giving fans a feast for the eyes, enjoying two outstanding domestic sports events in one day.

However, although the two game times were staggered, it still posed some dilemmas for Guangzhou fans. On one hand, they eagerly anticipated the Super Cup showdown between Evergrande and Teda, as this was the team's first appearance of the new season, filled with many highlights, especially the suspense of the league champion versus the FA Cup champion, which excited local fans. But the clash between Evergrande and Tianjin's volleyball teams was equally spectacular. However, since the distance between the Guangzhou University Town Stadium for the Super Cup and the Guangzhou Sport University Gymnasium for the volleyball game is at least an hour's drive, "you can't have both fish and bear's paw," so Evergrande executives had to make a choice. □

Claudio Returning

Evergrande Winning Relies on Foreigners

Camacho recruited eight Evergrande players for the national team training camp, completely disrupting Evergrande's plans for preparing for the Super Cup. What frustrated Lee Jang-soo was that many main players, including Zheng Zhi and Jiang Ning, were injured and unable to participate in the Super Cup on the 25th, thus Kaka, Muriqui, and Claudio, who had been sidelined for over seven months, naturally became the backbone for Evergrande's cup victory.

After returning from the Spain training camp, Evergrande continued preparations at the Qingyuan base. Due to the excessive number of players called up for the national team, the few friendly matches conducted by the team left Lee Jang-soo having to arrange for a few foreign players to lead the substitutes in drills, prompting the South Korean to repeatedly complain to Camacho that the national team training disrupted Evergrande's preparations. On Monday this week, Lee Jang-soo relocated the team to the Evergrande residence on the outskirts of Guangzhou for the final pre-game intensive practice. Although the eight national team players won't return until the evening of the 23rd, the foreign players remained in good condition, boosting Evergrande's confidence in winning the Super Cup. “The collision between the league champion and the cup champion is an important match in any country, and the Super Cup carries significant weight, there is no reason not to want to win this championship.” National team player Gao Lin stated that this was Evergrande's first official match of 2012, and if they could win this championship, it would greatly encourage the entire season.

Coach Lee Jang-soo similarly hoped the team could kick off the Asian Champions League journey with a victory, especially since the club had already dangled an 8 million yuan bonus, and playing at home, the desire for victory was naturally more urgent than Teda. From the current situation, Evergrande indeed faced challenges with injuries, as Zheng Zhi and Jiang Ning were certain to miss this match, while Zhang Linpeng and Muriqui also had minor injuries. Therefore, avoiding unnecessary losses before the Asian Champions League became Evergrande's greatest wish.

However, what comforted Lee Jang-soo was that the key forward Claudio, who had been sidelined for over seven months, could finally return from injury. This Super Cup was also the first joint appearance of the three attacking foreign players Kaka, Muriqui, and Claudio, thus placing the responsibility of Evergrande's cup victory naturally on them. □

High Ticket Prices, Fear of Cold Audience for the Super Cup

The Super Cup match between Evergrande and Teda has many highlights, but the ticket prices for this match are somewhat high, and combined with the stadium's distance from the city center, ticket sales have not been ideal.

Interestingly, while external ticket sales have not been ideal, tickets for internal use within Evergrande and among association staff have been very tight. "There are many requests for tickets, but they are hard to obtain, even the association's tickets are particularly scarce." Local media frankly admitted that this cold-hot contrast indicates that the Super Cup remains a marginal event. □
