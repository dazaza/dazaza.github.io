The boss of the bullfrog restaurant employed thugs to use tear gas to attack their competitors

by lovei0c1 on 2012-03-06 14:01:05

Tang Yongguang, who used to run a bullfrog business at the Peace Village Seafood Market in Kunming, sought to dominate the market by first negotiating with his competitors to uniformly raise the price of bullfrogs, ensuring that no one could sell them for less than he did. If others agreed, there would be peace. But if they refused and insisted on selling bullfrogs at lower prices, Tang would hire thugs to smash their shops. Yesterday, Tang Yongguang and 16 other defendants were put on trial at the Guandu District People's Court, where the prosecution charged Tang Yongguang and others with suspected hooliganism.

The vandals used tear gas

On the afternoon of September 9 last year, four well-built men armed with weapons appeared in front of a shop at the Peace Village Seafood Market and violently attacked Mr. Chen, who was selling bullfrogs, causing him minor injuries. Afterwards, the four men quickly fled the scene.

Mr. Chen guessed that perhaps his cheaper bullfrog prices had aroused resentment.

And as it turned out, Mr. Chen's suspicions were correct. On September 20, just 11 days after the first attack, his bullfrog shop was once again mysteriously attacked, and his helper was injured. What infuriated Mr. Chen was that this time the number of attackers had increased by three compared to the first time. Not only were they armed with weapons, but they also used tear gas (commonly known as tear bombs), scaring the people in the shop into fleeing the scene.

The mastermind behind the attacks was a competitor

After the incident, the police identified the involved vehicle through surveillance and ultimately uncovered the details of the assault gang: most of them were from other provinces, aged between 20 and 40 years old. Most were unemployed individuals, while a few worked part-time in video game rooms and restaurants, gathering after work to do "someone pays, someone eliminates trouble" jobs. The mastermind behind the vandalism incidents was Tang Yongguang, who ran a bullfrog business at the same market. The police found out that Tang Yongguang raised the price of bullfrogs in Kunming's seafood market to achieve a monopoly. To this end, he hired a gang of hooligans to threaten his fellow bullfrog sellers not to sell at low prices.

Two rounds of vandalism cost 10,000 yuan

Yesterday, Tang Yongguang and the other 16 defendants, wearing yellow vests, were led into the courtroom under the escort of court police.

The prosecution accused: On September 9 last year, Tang Yongguang met with co-defendant Li Xinjie and others, agreeing that Li Xinjie would be responsible for finding people to teach a lesson to the merchants selling bullfrogs in Peace Village. For this, Tang Yongguang paid Li Xinjie 2,000 yuan as a reward, which Li then distributed among the thugs. After the first vandalism of Mr. Chen's shop, Tang Yongguang did not stop there. He thought the punishment wasn't severe enough, so he spent another 8,000 yuan to hire thugs to vandalize Mr. Chen's shop for a second time.

In addition, the prosecution also found that Wen Wenshan, who had shares in a noodle factory, in order to retaliate against a competitor, also spent over 30,000 yuan to hire people to vandalize his competitor and injure their employees.

For this reason, the prosecution believed that Tang Yongguang and the other 17 defendants should be held accountable for the crime of hooliganism.

In court, Tang Yongguang did not acknowledge the facts charged by the prosecution and denied paying people to vandalize his competitors. The other defendants detailed the ins and outs of the vandalism incidents and the distribution of rewards.
