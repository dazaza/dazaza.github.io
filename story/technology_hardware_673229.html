What to Do If You Feel Stomach Discomfort After Drinking

Drink some milk to protect the gastric mucosa. Eat some plain porridge and light dishes. Every time I drink alcohol, my stomach feels uncomfortable, but sometimes it's unavoidable. I suggest that if you can avoid drinking, it's better not to. Drinking too much alcohol harms your health. After drinking, you should eat yogurt appropriately, which helps restore gastrointestinal function.

If your stomach feels very uncomfortable after drinking, it's best to drink wheat porridge. If there’s no wheat porridge, other types of porridge will also work. Just make sure they are easily digestible liquids. Other porridges shouldn't be too thick, and you shouldn’t drink too much.

Celery juice treats gastrointestinal discomfort and facial redness after drinking. This is because celery contains abundant B vitamins, which can break down alcohol.

If you feel nausea or vomiting after drinking, you can eat some fresh grapes. Fresh grapes contain rich tartaric acid, which can react with ethanol in alcohol to form ester substances, reducing the concentration of ethanol in the body and achieving the purpose of sobering up. At the same time, its sour taste can effectively alleviate post-drinking nausea and vomiting symptoms. If you eat grapes before drinking, it can also effectively prevent drunkenness.

If you experience stomach pain, you can take other appropriate stomach medications according to the situation, but you must not take painkillers because they have minimal effect on stomach pain.

Precautions

Issues to pay attention to when drinking alcohol to prevent stomach discomfort:

1. Drink as little alcohol as possible, preferably not at all.

2. Try to avoid drinking on an empty stomach.

3. Within 1-3 days after drinking, consume easily digestible foods and avoid overexertion to ensure the damaged gastric mucosa repairs as quickly as possible.

4. Do not eat greasy, spicy, or stimulating foods!
