Shoulder pain, back easily sore

Health experts at Harvard University in the U.S. have found that wearing 4-6 cm high heels is most effective for weight loss. This height of shoe effectively increases the metabolic rate of abdominal fat, making your stomach flatter and sexier! However, regularly wearing 4-6 cm high heels mainly causes increased pressure on your back, leading to aches.

Reduce back pressure, avoid back getting cold

It’s recommended to switch to a softer mattress while sleeping to reduce back pressure. Additionally, when back muscles stiffen, cold air more easily invades the bladder meridian running along the back, causing cold hands and feet and reduced immunity. Experts advise against wearing backless clothing to prevent the back from getting cold!

Cervical spine pressure, neck fatigue

When the height of high heels rises to 6-8 cm, the body's center of gravity naturally shifts upward while walking. A study found that if you walk for 2 hours in 7 cm high heels, the stiffness in your neck will increase by 22%. Health experts usually do not recommend that office workers who face computers long-term wear 6-8 cm high heels, as this will only make your neck more tired!

Ensure foot rest and strengthen foot massage

It's suggested that after every 2 hours of wearing high heels, you take off your shoes and let your feet rest for 15 minutes, doing some moderate foot massages. Focus on massaging the Yongquan acupoint located at the front one-third of the sole, which helps relieve muscle tension. Also, if your heel height is between 6-8 cm, avoid wearing heavy necklaces to prevent excessive pressure on your neck.

Prone to nerve pain

With heels over 8 cm, your body's center of gravity continues to shift upward while walking. Medical experts at Temple University in the U.S. have found that women who frequently wear high heels over 8 cm often experience neurological headaches and eye pain, with retinal pressure being 25% higher than average levels.

Avoid wearing contact lenses when wearing ultra-high heels

If you really like this elevated feeling, it's recommended to eat more green vegetables rich in vitamin A to provide sufficient nutrients for the retina, avoiding sudden vision decline after intense work. Additionally, never wear contact lenses while wearing ultra-high heels to avoid neurological eye pain.

Prone to leg swelling and obesity

Square heels and wedge heels have relatively less impact on health. They help maintain a certain balance in the body, preventing dizziness caused by intense work. Square heels and wedge heels are heavier and exert more pressure on the foot surface. Wearing them frequently can cause leg swelling and weight gain!

Loss of balance, prone to dizziness

A pair of slender stiletto heels indeed adds a lot of femininity, but health experts at the University of New England in Australia point out that frequently wearing stiletto heels makes women lose their sense of balance, greatly increasing the likelihood of motion sickness or airsickness.

Do finger exercises to strengthen balance
