There is a kind of beauty called nostalgia

I am really leaving, really saying goodbye to my teachers, classmates, and the familiar campus, embarking on the road back home. I can only savor my student days in dreams, where they linger.

I finally understand that possession also means loss. I want to find a place, sit quietly alone, let the sunset's afterglow fall upon me, let the gentle evening breeze brush against my forehead, and in the blend of heaven and earth, I will have a long talk with the past.

Thinking about every day at school, busy studying, eating, sleeping, dreaming, falling in love, and breaking up. I have finally come to know what it feels like to live a tiring life, and what kind of loss truly hurts.

But I still strive hard to fill each day with dreamlike colors. On every moonlit night, sitting in the tranquil red moonlight outside the window, I hold up a flute and let the sorrowful melody flow out from between my ten fingertips. In that nearly desolate tranquility and remoteness, I give my heart a gentle caress, letting it rest in the soft and ethereal ancient music. My heart temporarily escapes its prison, as if I were an elegant hermit in the deep forest, melting into the boundless poetry and philosophy.

I wonder how the delicate clouds circle around the moon, how the clear wind plays with shadows, I wish to possess the joy of Boya meeting Ziqi, to experience Tao Yuanming's leisure of picking chrysanthemums under the southern mountain, to feel the vastness of the Jiangnan moonlit nights with my heart, and the poignant and ethereal beauty of fishermen's songs returning through the misty waves under the fading sunlight.

I yearn for my heart to be bathed in the tender words of love, to sing freely during the days of gathering wild herbs, exposing my heart to lush greenery, and in the tearful rain of half-scattered friendships, I realize how sentimental I truly am.

Now, graduation is near, and I can only turn my nostalgia into a beautiful butterfly to set free. Yet, when I watch the sunrise and bid farewell to the setting clouds alone, I still like to hang my dripping sadness on both cheeks.

Time flies, and by the stream of fleeting years, I have picked up countless endless thoughts! Scooping up a handful of fallen green youth, things remain the same but people change, and the mood no longer echoes with the sound of waves. When I shoulder my baggage and step onto the return journey, glancing back suddenly, I finally understand that all I can take away is a kind of beauty called —— nostalgia.
