Cause 1: Skin Type

Oily skin is more prone to papular acne. This is an undeniable fact. Oily skin naturally produces more oil, with larger pores compared to dry skin, and has a thicker layer of keratin. In winter, oily skin often remains in good condition, but once summer arrives, the face becomes excessively oily, leading to pore blockage and the appearance of numerous pimples.

Solution: It is recommended to use a complete set of oil-control products for maintenance, while also taking care of the skin during the summer.

Cause 2: Endocrine System

Some women, despite regular beauty treatments and rarely having oily skin, still develop clusters of small pimples. This may require a medical check-up to determine if there are any endocrine imbalances, as hormonal disorders are an important cause of acne.

Solution: Continue with your beauty regimen and consult a Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) practitioner to balance your hormones. Through internal medication and external skincare, you can achieve both symptomatic and root-cause treatment, resolving the acne issue effectively.

Cause 3: Menstrual Cycle

Some women tend to develop acne on their chins about a week before their menstrual cycle due to hormonal changes during this period.

Solution: Acne associated with menstruation will disappear automatically after the period ends, so using some anti-inflammatory and calming skincare products to prevent worsening is sufficient.

Cause 4: Constipation

Constipation often leads to facial acne because toxins accumulated in the body are expelled through the skin.

Solution: Adjust your diet habits by consuming more high-fiber vegetables and fruits to improve constipation. Once constipation improves, the acne will naturally subside.

Cause 5: Stress

Being under prolonged stress makes one more prone to acne. For example, students before exams or those frequently under tension and insomnia are particularly susceptible to acne.

Solution: If your stress and life pressures are temporary, there's no need to worry excessively, as such acne usually doesn't last long. During this period, just enhance skin cleaning and ensure adequate sleep, and the acne will quickly disappear. If there's a tendency toward nervous disorders or psychological suppression that leads to persistent acne issues, it's recommended to consult a psychologist first. Once the psychological adjustment is made, the acne will resolve itself.

Cause 6: Environmental Adaptation

Environmental changes can make the skin less adaptable or temporarily alter its nature, especially in areas with high temperature or humidity, where sebum secretion increases, making acne more likely.
