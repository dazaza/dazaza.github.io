The Dilemma and Way Out for Points Websites!

I have been following points websites for over a year now. I have always been sensitive to this market and familiar with domestic points websites, but I have never dared to rashly enter this field. After all, it is a very complex market. After more than a year of reflection and observation, I have some insights that I would like to share with everyone.

Common dilemmas faced by domestic points websites?

1. Insufficient stickiness

I previously believed that the stickiness of points websites would be high, but after going through many detours, I realized that the stickiness of points websites is not actually very strong. Why? If I were a member of a points website, would I go there every day? Would I spend a lot of energy on just a few points or to exchange for a small gift? Many people definitely wouldn't. As a points website, the primary issue to solve is how to allow members to quickly complete all operations on the site, rather than trying hard to make members come every day and spend more time on the site. Therefore, most points websites focus heavily on how to get members to come every day and increase stickiness, which often backfires. As a result, currently in China, points websites generally have few members, low activity, and insufficient stickiness.

2. Difficulty in finding partners

Although points websites operate in a model where they help other websites, many websites are still unwilling to cooperate with points websites. Why? For example, Alibaba has many members who have accumulated many points. A points website could certainly help Alibaba's members use their points to exchange for gifts, etc., but as a large website, they definitely wouldn't want to easily contribute their members because members are the core vitality of the website. Who would willingly do that? Moreover, currently most domestic points websites have few members, making it even harder to negotiate cooperation with other websites. Large websites are definitely unwilling to cooperate, while small websites' cooperation doesn't mean much because they have few members, few points, and most importantly, small websites lack sufficient funds to exchange members' points.

3. Domestic websites' points are priced but lack substance

Almost any domestic website has points, but most domestic websites' attitudes towards these points are unclear—they are mostly nominal and lack real value; they are just numbers. It is basically impossible for members to realize exchanges or transactions with them. Therefore, for points websites to achieve greater development is almost very difficult unless most domestic websites commit to redeeming their points.

4. Points websites are still an ancillary service model

When points websites collaborate with others, it is usually to assist other enterprises and websites in exchanging prizes. These tasks can be done by companies themselves, and there is no need to find third-party companies for services. Furthermore, issues involving core membership data make merchants even less willing to cooperate. Therefore, the development of points websites has remained in an ancillary nature without fundamentally changing its own nature. Although using a third-party platform can make points websites larger, from the current perspective, the model of points websites is not good or mutually beneficial. Therefore, current points websites need to change their thinking and innovate their models. Otherwise, if they continue down the old path, the road will only get narrower. From 2006 to 2007, many points websites emerged, and at the same time, many points websites either went bankrupt or transformed, showing the risks and difficulties involved.

In fact, there are many more dilemmas facing China's points websites, and I won't list them all here. Next, let's talk about the way out for points websites. What exactly is the way out for China's points websites?

1. The positioning and model must be clear and innovative

I have been paying attention to points websites, and I also developed one myself (www.fenqu.cn). However, due to limited funds and energy, I have not easily entered the points market. This is because I have not yet properly positioned the website, nor have I had a bold and innovative model to allow the points website to achieve greater breakthroughs and developments. Therefore, for a points website to achieve great success and breakthroughs, it must first have a unique positioning and innovative model. Although the points website model has been developing for several years, it has not grown significantly. Why? This shows that the previous model and positioning were unreasonable. So what kind of positioning and model can win the future market? I believe that a points website should be a platform for distribution. Members accumulate points on other websites, and their purpose in coming to the points website is to consume or share those points.

2. Specialization is key to quickly capturing the market

Currently, most Chinese points websites accept anyone as clients—any website or any member. In my opinion, such an approach makes it difficult to build influence. Therefore, I believe that specialization is important, specifically collaborating with websites or merchants in a certain industry or field. For example, specializing in cooperating with B2C websites for points accumulation. Many friends discussing points websites with me think that accumulating many partners is a great idea. Theoretically, cooperating with various merchants and websites seems like a quick solution, but considering energy, ability, financial resources, and material resources, it is very difficult to achieve this all at once.

Regarding the way out for points websites, I don't have particularly creative ideas either. If I did, I would have already started my own points website!
