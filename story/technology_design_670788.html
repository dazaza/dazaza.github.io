Guilin plans to build 351 kilometers of expressway this year, with a planned investment of more than 3.4 billion yuan

by citscits5 on 2012-02-21 11:20:58

This year, the city's expressway investment plan totals 3.405 billion yuan to construct 351 kilometers (including link lines), according to information obtained yesterday from the Municipal Transportation Bureau by our reporter through Guilin International Travel Service. According to relevant responsible persons of the bureau, with the accelerated progress of construction projects, the Guilin to Xing'an Expressway will be completed and opened to traffic within the year. Meanwhile, the city will advance the construction of the Yangshuo to Luzhai and Guanyang Yonganguan to Quanzhou Phoenix Expressways, commence construction on the Guilin to Sanjiang Expressway (Guilin section), expedite preliminary work on the Ziyuan to Xing'an, Guanyang through Gongcheng to Pingle, Licheng to Yulin Expressways (Guilin section), Guihai Expressway (Guilin section) renovation and expansion projects, and strive to commence construction.

"This year, we will focus on constructing highways and trunk roads, strongly advancing the development of Guilin as a regional comprehensive transportation hub city," said the responsible person. This year, highway and trunk road construction investment in our city will reach over 4 billion yuan, accounting for 67.17% of the annual transportation infrastructure construction target tasks.

In addition to highway construction, the Ziyuan to Longsheng secondary road will also be completed and opened to traffic this year. The Lingui to Suqiao, Licheng bypass roads, and the Longsheng to Piaoli through Pingdeng to Chuansu roads are planned for completion within the year. The National Road 322 Lingchuan bypass line, National Road 322 Yongfu to Luzhai, and National Road 321 rerouting projects and other road network projects will successively commence.

This year is the year of transformational and leapfrog development for our city’s transportation. While increasing transportation infrastructure construction, the Municipal Transportation Bureau will also carefully plan the seamless connection of the integrated transportation system. It is understood that intercity, urban, rural-urban, and rural four-level passenger transport networks will receive coordinated planning. By optimizing existing transportation infrastructure networks and transportation service structures, the overall advantages and comprehensive efficiency of various modes of transportation will be leveraged, gradually achieving the goals of "zero transfer" for passengers and "seamless connection" for freight.

The continuous updating and improvement of hardware facilities contribute to the good development of tourism. Guilin International Travel Service CITS will make even greater efforts to provide higher quality tourism services for a wide range of tourists.
