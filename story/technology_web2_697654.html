Training Method One: Seriously answer your child's questions. During this period, the baby already has a lot of vocabulary and can connect two sentences together, sometimes even being talkative. Their curiosity often leads them to ask "why." Parents should be patient, answer every question, and respond in simple language that the child can understand. If you encounter a question you don't know, look it up with the child or ask others; never make something up just to get by.

Special Suggestion: If the child asks some strange questions or does mischievous things, parents should handle it patiently and flexibly.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Training Method Two: Identify size, shape, and color. Children at this stage already have the concept of distinguishing various colors and sizes. In daily conversations, you can intentionally emphasize adjectives describing the size, shape, and color of objects, which will unconsciously create some concepts in the baby’s mind. Then use painting tools, cards, building blocks, etc., to teach the child to recognize colors, basic geometric shapes such as squares and circles, and have a certain understanding of object sizes. This way, the child's brain will become more developed, and their intelligence will rapidly improve.

Special Reminder: A child's learning ability always surpasses what parents imagine.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Training Method Three: Let the baby do more exercise. Encourage physical activity for the child, not only outdoors but also indoors if conditions allow. You can let the child do indoor gymnastics or bed exercises like forward and backward rolling. This helps develop the baby's motor skills alongside their intelligence. In short, physical exercise can be adapted to local conditions without too many restrictions, but attention must be paid to the baby's safety.

Special Reminder: Do not make promises lightly or deceive the child.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Training Method Four: Finger exercises promote brain development. At this age, children can learn to do some simple small crafts. For example, you can teach him how to tie knots and learn different knot-tying methods. You can also teach the baby to hold scissors and cut paper. Proper use of scissors can strengthen the baby's finger muscles, but safety must be emphasized, and it should be done under parental supervision.

Special Reminder: As long as the child is energetic, there is no need to worry because they eat less.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Training Method Five: The child's thirst for knowledge is strong. Parents should take advantage of every opportunity to let the baby learn new things. Reading stories before bedtime is a good practice. Besides stories, you can recite nursery rhymes, Tang poems, riddles, lullabies, etc. Gradually, the child's language and thinking abilities will improve.

Special Reminder: Ages two to three are a turning point in the psychological development of children, known as the "first opposition period," which is the psychological response after the child develops self-awareness.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Training Method Six: Learn to use chopsticks correctly. By this time, you can teach the child to eat with chopsticks. You can also find a writing brush and teach him how to hold it, as the method of holding a writing brush and chopsticks is similar. If you don't teach the correct way to hold chopsticks now, it will be hard to change later.

Special Suggestion: For children who like to resist, parents should first adjust their educational methods and attitudes, fully satisfying the child's reasonable requests.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Training Method Seven: From this period onward, try to let the child play with friends rather than just playing with parents, to avoid the child developing a shy personality. During playtime, conflicts may arise between children, and parents should provide proper guidance, teaching the child to yield to others. You can encourage them to play games like "house" so they can experience the joy of cooperating with others.

Special Reminder: Learn through play and grow happily.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Training Method Eight: Praise the child more. At this stage, children generally can handle basic daily life tasks independently. So, let the child do it themselves as much as possible, with adults helping them complete tasks when necessary. When the child doesn’t know how or performs poorly, patiently explain to them. Don’t scold them easily. When they perform well, praise them promptly. Tasks that seem simple to adults can be quite complex for children. In general, avoid scolding and focus on giving praise.

Special Reminder: Don't excessively praise the child. Growing up in an environment of excessive praise makes it difficult for children to distinguish between truth and falsehood, right and wrong, success and failure.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Training Method Nine: Develop good habits. Let the baby help with some housework, assisting mom with simple chores, which can help develop the child's intelligence and cultivate their character. For example, the child can tidy up their own toys, learn to fold quilts, wash handkerchiefs, etc.

Special Suggestion: The child's desire to do everything themselves is very strong, so let them do it as much as possible. However, you must tell them what dangerous things are.
