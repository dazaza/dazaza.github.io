Anchor Li Siyu Slaps Her Boyfriend Dong Nan Who Suffers from Cancer, and Scolds His Mother

by mlwang on 2011-09-23 09:27:47

Recently, a Weibo user disclosed that Li Siyu, one of the main hosts of "Beautiful Glamour" and "The Happier Shopper", had abandoned and slapped her boyfriend of 7 years who was suffering from cancer. She also insulted her boyfriend's mother.

It was reported that Dong Nan passed away on September 15th. In response to such serious negative rumors, the involved party Li Siyu only indirectly responded on Weibo: "Let the lies go with the wind; he understands, I understand, that's enough." However, she herself completely avoided the matter. Li Siyu's staff denied this news, claiming that during Dong Nan's lifetime, their relationship was good, and Li Siyu is still struggling to come out of the pain of losing her boyfriend.

Compared to the limited word count on Weibo, an article in a certain forum titled "Questioning Li Siyu of 'Beautiful Glamour,' Is Slapping Your Terminally Ill Boyfriend and Insulting His Mother the Embodiment of Your Wisdom and Fashion?" described Li Siyu's "slapping" incident in much more vivid detail. The post wrote that Dong Nan, a fashion planner who had been in a relationship with Li Siyu for seven years, was diagnosed with liver cancer in May this year and thus proactively proposed breaking up with Li Siyu. Afterwards, Li Siyu visited Dong Nan three times, but each visit resulted in conflicts, leading to verbal abuse whenever they disagreed. The most severe incident occurred when Li Siyu got into a verbal altercation with Dong Nan's mother. Enraged, Dong Nan shouted at Li Siyu a few times, only to be met with two slaps from her. The poster indicated that the situation was currently being documented through video collection, and this matter had become known throughout Beijing's modeling industry.

While the truth of the facts presented in the post remains temporarily indiscernible, what has led to Li Siyu becoming the subject of gossip is the information on her Weibo account. This netizen made public her Weibo records online to demonstrate that during Dong Nan's illness, Li Siyu failed to show the concern expected of a girlfriend. Especially in the last few days before Dong Nan's passing, Li Siyu was still advertising for sponsors and rushing to appointments, leading netizens to conclude that she was "not sufficiently sorrowful."

Faced with the relentless online attacks in recent days, Li Siyu seems overwhelmed. On her personal Weibo, she only posted two cryptic texts as a response: "Let the lies go with the wind; he understands, I understand, that's enough" and "If there really are people who believe that defaming others can divert attention, making oneself feel less sad, less sorrowful, then let them slander me..." Yet, she did not clarify the matter properly. Despite multiple calls to both her and her agent's phones, all were turned off, resulting in complete silence.
