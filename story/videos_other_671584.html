There are several surgical methods for facial wrinkle removal. These surgeries aim to tighten the facial skin tissue in order to flatten, eliminate, or reduce wrinkles while enhancing skin elasticity and improving its luster.

1. Facelift Wrinkle Removal: Generally speaking, a full facelift should be the most reliable method and is the first choice for those with prominent wrinkles and severe skin laxity. For older patients, we recommend the full facelift surgery which addresses significant skin laxity and prominent wrinkles through deep-layered separation and targeted treatment of local muscle tissues. The results are clear and effective, capable of completely removing frown lines and forehead wrinkles, as well as eliminating most crow's feet. The effects can last up to 10 years or even longer. Typically, a woman aged 40-50 who undergoes one wrinkle removal surgery will not need a second procedure.

2. Brow Lift Wrinkle Removal: As people age, their skin begins to age and lose elasticity, causing both the upper and lower eyelid skin to become loose. In milder cases, the drooping upper eyelid makes the eye slit smaller and deformed, changing the once "almond eyes" into "triangular eyes" and "double eyelids" into "inner double". In more severe cases, the highly drooping upper eyelid may cover the outer corner of the eye, significantly affecting vision. Performing a brow lift wrinkle removal surgery is a contemporary popular cosmetic technique. After the surgery, eyebrow embroidery can not only remove poorly shaped eyebrows but also create new personalized designed eyebrows. It corrects the "triangular eyes" caused by the sagging upper eyelid skin, restoring beautiful double eyelids and reducing or eliminating forehead and crow's feet wrinkles, achieving multiple benefits at once. Before the surgery, based on the degree of skin laxity, the amount of crow's feet wrinkles, and the width of the post-surgery double eyelid line, along with the shape and height of the eyebrows, a detailed design and precise calculation of the amount of skin to be removed is made. During the surgery, meticulous operation and periosteal suspension fixation are performed. Post-surgery, careful nursing care is provided, avoiding raw water contact, stitches are removed on the third day, and eyebrows can be drawn on the fourth day. This surgery is safe and effective, meeting patient requirements well, and the results are long-lasting.

Fascia Suspension Wrinkle Removal: In the U.S., fascia suspension wrinkle removal is known as lunchtime wrinkle removal, meaning that undergoing surgery during a lunch break won't affect afternoon work. This technique challenges traditional facelift wrinkle removal and represents a comprehensive facial youth restoration project. Using patented instruments, there is no redness or swelling on the face after surgery, and normal people can hardly notice any wrinkle removal traces. It can significantly lift large areas of the facial fascia and skin, notably improving the wrinkles around the drooping corners of the eyes and facial wrinkles. Nasolabial folds can become visibly shallower, making one appear 5-10 years younger. The entire procedure takes only thirty minutes. Tightening the facial fascia can also slim down a chubby face, providing obvious slimming effects. Sunken cheeks are improved, and the skin appears finer and smoother. There is no need to cut hair, and it does not affect hair growth. The operation is painless. After suspension, there may be varying degrees of pain on the same night, which can be alleviated by taking painkillers. Afterwards, oral painkillers are taken continuously for several days, allowing one to return to work normally. Showering is possible 48 hours after suspension.
