I've been secretly following you, just in case you don't get to eat the carrots.

by wang751xr211 on 2012-03-08 22:10:14

The little white rabbit grew up and began to hope for more than just carrots; she started yearning for love... The gray rabbit was good, always giving me his carrots to eat. I tilted my head back, looking at the sky, just so my tears wouldn't fall... But is the gray rabbit really my lover?

The little white rabbit carried all the carrots the gray rabbit had given her, bid him farewell, and walked into the forest.

The first thing the little white rabbit encountered were the wild geese. She thought they had fallen in love, but gradually, the little white rabbit realized she could never keep up with the geese's pace. When the geese flew, all she could do was run while looking up. Her neck ached, and she was exhausted from running. The little white rabbit secretly considered giving up, but she didn’t say it out loud. One day, the goose told the little white rabbit—

"I have to leave you because you cannot fly with me."

This was the little white rabbit's first love. She cried until her eyes were red and continued walking forward with the remaining carrots.

"The wild geese are not my lover. I can't walk alongside him."

The little white rabbit met a big bear. She felt that she didn't even like the bear, let alone love him. However, the bear said that the forest was actually dangerous, and he would accompany her until she met her true love.

The big bear was very kind to the little white rabbit. On cold nights, he would put her inside a tree hollow and guard the entrance himself. When food was scarce, he would save his dinner for the little white rabbit to have as breakfast the next morning.

"The big bear is also good, but how do I tell him that what I need is someone to cuddle with when it's cold? Also, I don't like eating the same things as him."

Just then, the big bear met a fox. The fox was beautiful and said she liked the bear and wanted to be with him. The big bear told the little white rabbit—

"I only want to be with you. I don't love the fox."

But eventually, one morning when the little white rabbit woke up, she found no trace of the big bear at the tree hollow. He had left without saying goodbye. The little white rabbit knew the big bear had followed the fox away.

The little white rabbit tidied up her backpack, wanting to continue moving forward. Suddenly, she discovered there were many more carrots in her bag.

"Finally, the big bear understood what I needed, but he still left. He won't come back, will he? Maybe this way is better for both of us."

On a rainy day, the little white rabbit met a wolf. Although she clearly knew that being with the wolf would only end up hurting herself, the little white rabbit fell helplessly in love with him anyway, living each day on edge while being with him.

In the end, the wolf waved his hand at the already battered little white rabbit during another rainy day and said—

"I'm tired of you. Leave quickly."

The little white rabbit packed her bag. There weren't many carrots left inside. Should she continue moving forward? Would she truly find love? She placed her bag under a tree and watched the storm outside.

"Should I not have fallen in love with the wolf? Or maybe I shouldn't have entered this forest in search of something I wasn't even sure about?"

The little white rabbit began to miss her old life with the gray rabbit. But after traveling so far, could she still go back? Suddenly, she heard rustling sounds. Turning around, she saw a gray figure placing carrots into her small backpack—

"I've been secretly following you, just in case you ran out of carrots..."

The little white rabbit finally understood what love was. Love might just be two people sharing carrots together.
