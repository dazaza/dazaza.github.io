On December 5th, Beijing time, FIFA and the French Football magazine held a press conference for the FIFA Ballon d'Or award in Paris, announcing the three candidates for the 2011 FIFA Ballon d'Or: Barcelona players Messi and Xavi, along with Cristiano Ronaldo from Real Madrid made it into the top three. The final result will be announced at the FIFA annual meeting on January 9, 2012. After the nominations were revealed, Ronaldo updated his Tencent Weibo in the first instance. (Click the image to enter Ronaldo's Weibo.)

Ronaldo wrote on Weibo: "It’s great to be nominated again as one of the FIFA Ballon d'Or final three contenders." Indeed, for Ronaldo who stands at the peak of international football, making it into the final three candidates is something natural and not worth boasting about. Being nominated again as one of the final three contenders for the FIFA Ballon d'Or is truly fantastic. Perhaps for Ronaldo, failing to make it into the final selection for the inaugural FIFA Ballon d'Or last season was his biggest regret. Now he has the chance to win the FIFA Ballon d'Or jointly awarded by FIFA and French Football for the first time, even the proud Ronaldo started accumulating good karma in a low-key manner.

Previously, although he had long been considered a favorite, Ronaldo never discussed the topic of the Ballon d'Or on Tencent Weibo. When accepting the European Golden Boot Award this summer, Ronaldo subtly expressed his preference for awards directly measured by data because they are fairer and more impartial, and he showed no interest in awards selected through voting.

Last year, due to his unremarkable performance in the South Africa World Cup, coupled with being affected by injuries during his first season at Real Madrid, Ronaldo did not make it into the final three nominees for the inaugural FIFA Ballon d'Or. For Ronaldo, who is considered at least one of the top two players in the world today, this was quite a disgrace. In 2011, the Portuguese player saw a full-scale explosion in form. He scored an astonishing 41 goals in last season's La Liga, setting a new record and winning the European Golden Boot Award. With his goals, Real Madrid defeated their arch-rivals Barcelona in the Copa del Rey final, securing their first title in several seasons. In the 2011-12 season, Ronaldo continued his excellent form, leading the scoring charts in La Liga with 17 goals and also scoring 4 goals in the UEFA Champions League. Currently, Real Madrid leads Barcelona by 3 points despite playing one fewer game in La Liga, and Ronaldo's role is undeniable. On the national team front, Ronaldo successfully led Portugal out of the UEFA Euro qualifying playoff round. With such all-round performances, Ronaldo garnered a large number of votes from national team coaches and captains.

Back in 2008, at just 23 years old, Ronaldo won the Ballon d'Or, which was then voted on by professional football journalists from across Europe. In 2009, Ronaldo lost out to Messi, who helped Barcelona achieve the treble and stole the limelight, finishing second with the silver ball. In 2010, the Ballon d'Or merged with FIFA for the first time, and the voting method changed to being decided by national team coaches and captains from various countries. As a result, Ronaldo fell short and didn't even make it into the final three selections. This year, Ronaldo made a comeback, aiming to lift the Ballon d'Or for the second time in his career and equal the achievements of his lifelong rival Messi.

In his Weibo post, Ronaldo also attached the web address of the official FIFA website announcing this news. In the accompanying picture of the news article, Ronaldo wearing a Real Madrid jersey is positioned in the middle, while Xavi and Messi are on either side. One wonders if this arrangement hints that Ronaldo will surpass the Barcelona duo to become the winner of the FIFA Ballon d'Or?
