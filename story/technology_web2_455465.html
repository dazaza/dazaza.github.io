Chongqing Public Security Bureau's original captain of the drug enforcement team gets 2 years in prison for bending the law for personal gain

by zzf000zxys6 on 2011-07-01 17:38:26

Chongqing - According to a report by journalist Shen Yi, the recent first-instance verdict has been handed down by the Shapingba District Court on the case of Tang Nong, former captain of the second detachment of Chongqing Public Security Bureau's anti-drug general team, who was accused of bending the law for personal gain, prosecuted by the Shapingba District People's Procuratorate. Tang Nong was sentenced to two years in prison for the crime of bending the law for personal gain and stated in court that he would not appeal.

The court ascertained that Tang Nong took office as the captain of the second detachment of Chongqing Public Security Bureau's anti-drug general team starting from April 2008. On May 11, 2010, the Shapingba District Public Security Branch uncovered an ice drug processing plant at Xiyong Mine Slope, apprehending the drug manufacturer Li Shunping (separately handled), and seizing a large quantity of ice drugs. Subsequently, the anti-drug general team of the Municipal Public Security Bureau formed an investigation team with Luo Li, the original deputy captain of the general team (separately handled), as the group leader, and Tang Nong as the deputy group leader. On May 15, the investigation team determined that the factory was run by Tan Liren, and on July 1 decided to issue a warrant for his arrest online. On July 9, after Tan Liren was captured and brought to the Narcotics Control Detachment of the Shapingba District Public Security Branch, Luo Li, Tang Nong, and police officer Zhou from the Narcotics Control Detachment interrogated Tan Liren in the interrogation room of the Narcotics Control Detachment. After Zhou left the interrogation room, Luo Li hinted for Tang Nong to stand guard at the door while he gave advice to Tan Liren not to confess to the crimes. When leaving the Narcotics Control Detachment, Luo Li told Tang Nong not to spread the word about their conversation, to which Tang immediately agreed and did not report it to the organization.

The court ruled that defendant Tang Nong, knowing that Luo Li used his official position to shield a criminal suspect but failed to report it, and even helped create conditions for him, thus committing the crime of bending the law for personal gain. Considering his auxiliary role in the joint crime as an accomplice, the court made the above judgment with leniency according to the law.
