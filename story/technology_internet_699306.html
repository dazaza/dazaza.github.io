A brief stay at the sweet potato pavilion let us understand how sweet potatoes crossed the sea to come to China. It turns out that during the Ming Wanli period, Fuzhou suffered a great drought, and the common people were plunged into famine. Chen Zhenlong, a Changle overseas Chinese who was doing business in the Philippines at the time, saw that the local sweet potatoes were easy to plant and could be used as food, so he冒险 brought back sweet potato seedlings hidden in bamboo tubes and baskets to Fuzhou. After a successful trial planting, he reported it to Jin Xuezeng, the Fujian patrol officer, who then guided the common people in planting them, thus saving the famine-stricken victims. Sweet potatoes have since spread across the country.
