Is the major of mold design and manufacturing in 2012 good?

The quality of any profession is actually closely related to the macro environment. Therefore, it is necessary to start with the big picture. If it's an industry in decline, then there’s not much point discussing whether it's good or bad.

In recent years, the global manufacturing industry has been shifting to the Asia-Pacific region in a vertically integrated model. China is becoming an important base for world manufacturing. According to authoritative reports, China has become the largest manufacturing country. As an important process equipment for product manufacturing and one of the basic industries of the national economy, the mold industry will face the front line of competition. In addition to requiring [highly skilled] workers, the mold industry also needs more [high technology] to ensure its competitiveness.

As pointed out in the 15th Five-Year Plan, molds are the basic process equipment for industrial production. The five pillar industries of the national economy - machinery, electronics, automobiles, petrochemicals, and construction - all require the development of the mold industry to keep up with their own growth.

Molds have gained wide application due to their high production efficiency, good product quality, low material consumption, and low production costs, which other processing and manufacturing industries cannot match. From the perspective of the industrial product production sector, molds are indispensable process equipment for modern industry, especially for the automobile, motorcycle, aviation, instrument, instrument, key equipment, electronic communication, weapons, home appliances, hardware tools, and daily necessities industries. According to statistics, the number of parts manufactured using molds accounts for more than 80% in products such as aircraft, automobiles, motorcycles, tractors, motors, electrical appliances, instruments, and meters; more than 85% in electronic products such as computers, televisions, cameras, and video recorders; more than 90% in light industrial products such as refrigerators, washing machines, air conditioners, electric fans, bicycles, and watches; and more than 95% in weapon products such as bullets and guns.

Currently, after China joined the WTO, with the rapid development of contemporary industry, the variety and quantity of products in various industries continue to increase, and the frequency of type changes is accelerating. New requirements are constantly being made for product quality, style, and appearance, leading to an increase in demand for molds and higher requirements for mold quality. Mold technology directly affects the development of manufacturing, product updates, and product competitiveness. Therefore, rapidly improving the technical level of molds has become an urgent task. For example, one of the important reasons why Japanese products such as cars, computers, televisions, and mobile phones have an advantageous position in terms of variety, quantity, and quality in the international market is that Japan's mold technology is at the world-leading level.

Under the great importance and care of the government, corresponding preferential policies have been proposed for mold technology development. Advanced technologies and equipment are widely used in the mold industry. Efforts are made to improve the level of mold design and manufacturing, achieving significant economic benefits. In addition, according to available data, currently, the total output value of molds in developed countries such as the United States, Japan, and Germany has exceeded the total output value of machine tools. The progress of mold technology has greatly promoted the production development of industrial products. Molds are "efficiency amplifiers," and the value of the final products produced by molds can exceed their own price by dozens, hundreds, or even thousands of times.

According to reports from various countries, the mold industry in Europe and America and other industrially developed countries is called the "magnetic industry" that "turns iron into gold." Nowadays, the development speed of the world mold industry has exceeded that of the emerging electronics industry, and it has achieved specialization, standardization, and commercialization, thus receiving widespread praise. The American industrial community believes that "the mold industry is the cornerstone of American industry," Japan calls the mold industry the "original driving force for entering a prosperous society," in Germany, it is crowned with the title of "King of Metal Processing," while some EU countries call "molds are gold." The Singaporean government considers the mold industry as the "magnetic industry," and Chinese mold experts call it the "money printer." It can be seen that the mold industry holds an important position in the economic development of various countries around the world. Mold technology has become one of the important indicators to measure the manufacturing level of a country.
