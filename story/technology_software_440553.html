Three house demolition personnel were injured by a farmer in Nantong, Jiangsu who wielded a cleaver

by zzfhhxy22 on 2011-06-01 13:40:07

By Fan Chuangui, Legal Daily

The bloodstains have turned black and are covered with dust.

From the kitchen to the doorway corridor, some places have large patches of blood, while others show a trail of drops marking the route taken by the injured as they left. On May 22, a timely downpour washed clean the cement patio and paths that had been left with large bloodstains, making this household, at the center of the storm, appear unusually calm.

Neighbors of this household recounted to the Legal Daily reporter an unsettling scene that took place here:

"Bang!" At around 10:25 AM on May 21, in Group 9, Brigade 19, TONGLingqiao Village, Jinsha Town, Tongzhou District, Nantong City, Jiangsu Province, the kitchen door of Zhu Zhilin's house was slammed shut. In the following short span of 3 minutes, sounds of furniture being knocked over, a woman's cries, and a man's angry shouts were heard successively. When the door opened, the scene shocked and terrified everyone present - "This is terrible! Someone has been killed, quick, save them!" The cries of Xing Xiaohui, the village women's committee director, startled the neighbors.

Subsequent investigations confirmed that this was a bloody conflict triggered by demolition. According to local official statements, during negotiations, the Zhu family "suddenly demanded additional compensation," which was refused by the staff. Thereafter, Wu Guoquan, Zhu Zhilin's son-in-law, suddenly attacked three staff members with a kitchen knife, causing minor injuries to all three and also accidentally injuring his mother-in-law, Wu Qimei.

However, according to eyewitnesses and villagers, the demolition personnel were rude, overturned the table, broke the tableware, and both verbally insulted and physically assaulted them, leading Wu Guoquan to lose control and defend himself with a knife.

The exact reality of the scene awaits police investigation. However, in this village that is about to be collectively demolished, almost all interviewees expressed to the Legal Daily reporter two common viewpoints: "the attitude of the demolition personnel is arrogant" and "we don't want to be forced into moving into apartment buildings." Was Wu Guoquan's behavior an extreme expression of these two viewpoints? The Legal Daily reporter conducted an investigation.

Eyewitnesses claim demolition personnel were rude

Traveling from the city center of Tongzhou towards TONGLingqiao Village, after passing TONGLing Bridge for about 700 meters, turning left onto a small road leads you to Brigade 19, Group 9. A row of two-story bungalows extends along the small road, each home marked with a red "demolish" character spray-painted on their walls.

Pan Jun's house is the fourth one at the entrance, separated by a few households from Zhu Zhilin's, less than 200 meters away. Regarding the incident at Zhu's house on May 21, Pan Jun's first reaction was, "If it didn't happen at his house, it might have happened at mine."

At around 8 AM on May 21, upon hearing that demolition personnel would come to negotiate compensation, Pan Jun and his wife felt like they were facing a major threat and called back their eldest son-in-law, Shan WeiFeng. Before 9 AM, three demolition workers arrived, proposing a total compensation of 300,000 yuan based on the assessed value plus subsidies for their two-story building.

Pan Jun thought it was insufficient and requested 400,000 yuan. "Negotiations kept failing, and at around 10 o'clock, they called two bald men who cursed us and got into arguments with me," Pan Jun's wife emotionally described the scene to the Legal Daily reporter.

"While we were arguing, around 10:30, suddenly there was commotion outside. Women's Committee Director Xing Xiaohui shouted at the door: 'This is terrible! Someone has been killed, quick, save them!' Several workers then hurriedly left." Pan Jun's family quickly followed out. Not long after, some workers came to borrow Shan WeiFeng's Chery car parked at the door to rescue people. "Arguing is one thing, saving lives is another, so we lent it to them," said Pan Jun's family.

Regarding what happened at Zhu Zhilin's house, Pan Jun could only learn bits and pieces through neighborhood rumors. He was certain of one fact: the injured people lost a lot of blood, "there was blood on the road, on my son-in-law's car, it couldn't be washed off."

What exactly happened in Zhu Zhilin's house just before Director Xing Xiaohui's shout of "killing" at around 10 AM that day? Neighbors' rumors varied widely. Party involved Wu Guoquan has already been criminally detained, and Zhu Zhilin's mother Wu Qimei was nowhere to be found at Zhu's house by the Legal Daily reporter. Only Zhu Zhilin's wife Ma Cuiping, who experienced the scene but cannot speak Mandarin, could reconstruct the event. With the help of local villagers translating her dialect, the reporter obtained a version of the scene reconstruction.

Ma Cuiping, over 60 years old, appeared somewhat dazed, showing the reporter the bloodstains left in various places, then narrated the events in the kitchen:

"At the time, my son-in-law was sitting near the door peeling cucumber skins, preparing lunch. My mother-in-law and I were also in the kitchen. Four of them came in, closed the door, and stood there. After a while, they got angry, overturned the entire table, and everything on it fell to the ground." Ma Cuiping doesn't understand Mandarin, she doesn't know what they were arguing about, but she used both hands and feet to try to make the scene more vividly real, "then they started arguing, they began to grab the knife. In the process, my son-in-law injured three of them, and my mother-in-law was also injured. Then they opened the door and ran out."

Wu Guoquan subsequently turned himself in.

Discrepancies between local residents and official accounts

In the evening of May 23, when the reporter returned to Zhu's house, Wu Guoquan's wife and parents had already come back, but all remained silent about the matter. Villagers trying to persuade them to tell the truth even made Wu Guoquan's wife, Zhu Chunhong, angry at one point. Villagers revealed that they had already been afraid to speak up, and at 2 PM on May 23, Zhu's family had signed the agreement to accept the compensation plan — a fact confirmed by Zhu Chunhong.

The reporter went to the Chengnan Police Station where Wu Guoquan had turned himself in to inquire about the case. Instructor Zhang Jian said, "There was a report of an argument, but we don't know the specific situation; you need to ask the district public security bureau." The reporter called the office of the Tongzhou Economic Development Zone Management Committee, and the head of the office indicated that it was just a minor accident caused by the emotional excitement of the parties involved, with three people sustaining minor injuries.

As of the reporter's deadline, local authorities had not issued any formal public announcement regarding the incident. On a website named "HaoBin Forum" in Nantong, the reporter discovered a post signed by the "TONGLingqiao Village Demolition Mass Work Team," providing another account of the scene: "On the morning of May 21, a member of the TONGLingqiao Village Demolition Mass Work Team, along with two demolition officers, went to Zhu's household in Group 9, as per an appointment made on the afternoon of May 20, to discuss specific issues related to demolition compensation and resettlement. Around 10 o'clock, during further discussions of the agreement terms, Wu, the son-in-law residing with the Zhu family, suddenly proposed an additional compensation requirement. The staff explained according to policy that the compensation for the household was already in place. During further policy explanations, Wu suddenly used a kitchen knife to cause minor injuries to three staff members and a slight injury to his mother."

This statement was refuted by multiple neighbors and relatives of Zhu Zhilin. According to Pan Jun, Zhu Zhilin's family was known locally as the most honest. Zhu himself is both deaf and mute, never assertive, and has only two daughters, both married. His elder daughter Yang Chunhong married Wu Guoquan from Group 7 of the same village. Last year, after Wu Guoquan's house was demolished, Yang Chunhong moved back to her parents' house with her husband and in-laws.

In the impression of Pan Jun's family, welder Wu Guoquan appeared refined and had a very good temper. Moreover, Wu Guoquan was living in the house temporarily and wasn't the owner. Many villagers said he resorted to using a knife because the demolition personnel were rude, possibly even forcing him to sign documents. According to a relative surnamed Gu from Zhu Zhilin's family, "Because the Zhu family is honest, their compensation was particularly low, with only 140,000 yuan for a single-story house, and disputes with the demolition department have been ongoing," rather than a sudden demand for extra compensation.

Regarding the injuries sustained by the three individuals, locals have a different account from the official version, claiming "one dead, two injured." On the evening of May 23, the Legal Daily reporter rushed to the People's Hospital where the injured were located. From the hospital's admission office, it was learned that one of the injured named "Xie Ping" was hospitalized in bed 44 of the sixth-floor neurology ward, but when the reporter arrived, the bed was empty. A neighboring patient informed that last night, three people took him to the observation room, saying "he needed to stay with the other two."

The reporter then separately found the other two injured individuals in the ENT ward and the emergency observation room, where there were multiple guards at the doors. According to the nurses, the patient in the emergency observation room was still unstable, and it was uncertain if there would be any sudden problems, such as intracranial bleeding.

Villagers express unwillingness to "be relocated to high-rise apartments"

During the Legal Daily reporter's interviews in Group 9, Brigade 19 of TONGLingqiao Village, villagers from multiple surrounding villages also gathered upon hearing the news. They reported that although in different villages, they belong to the same batch of demolitions.

According to relevant data from the Tongzhou Economic Development Zone Planning and Construction Bureau, in 2006, "The Tongzhou Municipal Party Committee and Municipal Government, aiming to strengthen the main urban area and accelerate the development of open-type economy, expanded new platforms for construction projects, handing over seven villages south of the Tonglv Canal under the jurisdiction of Jinsa Town to the Development Zone for management and organized development." In the first half of 2007, the regional planning was completed, and demolition commenced.

Simultaneously, "The Tongzhou Development Zone planned and constructed two relocation housing complexes, Xinling Garden and Jinle Jiayuan, capable of accommodating 2,800 and 1,500 residents respectively."

This demolition and relocation equates to "being forced into high-rise apartments" in the eyes of the villagers. "We previously had houses and land, lived comfortably, now we're all required to move into apartment buildings, living upstairs, how will we eat in the future?"

Multiple villagers from TONGLingqiao Village and Yuan Shuai Temple Village told reporters they were dissatisfied with the compensation standards. Lu Jianguo and his wife from Yuan Shuai Temple Village informed reporters that they haven't been able to return home for several months. Their three-story house built in 1999 cost nearly 500,000 yuan for construction and decoration, but now, facing demolition, they can only receive a compensation of over 300,000 yuan.
