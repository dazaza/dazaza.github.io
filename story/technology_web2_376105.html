"Interview" BXB Bookshelf: Helping People Share Idle Books

by ybcz0519 on 2010-12-27 21:06:31

This article is simultaneously published on Mobinode. Business media please do not reprint! We have previously introduced Babei Bookshelf, a social library service. All the books in Babei Bookshelf come from member donations and are stored at member locations, and all members can borrow them anytime. The bookshelf is equivalent to a platform where users can share their idle books and borrow others' shared books for free.

Introduction to Babei Bookshelf functions: After user registration, there is a limit on the number of books you can borrow. You can choose your favorite books from the current Babei Bookshelf to borrow, and the book owner will send it to you for free. After reading, you can mark the book status as "read," allowing others to borrow it from you. You need to pay to send the book to the person borrowing it from you.

After registration, users only have a one-book borrowing limit. When you send out one borrowed book, you get another borrowing limit, which you can acquire by donating your own idle books.

When borrowing books, users do not need to pay fees. However, when someone borrows your book, you need to cover the shipping costs. The more books you send out, the more borrowing limits you gain, promoting equal sharing and acquisition of books.

Link: http://bookfor.us/

Despite being online for a short time, Babei Bookshelf has been nominated by many users and was selected as one of the most watched start-ups in ChinaMode 2010. For this reason, we conducted a special interview with its founder, Liu Baiguang (Light Liu), who was also the founder of the well-known domestic blog network Weborn.org (www.weborn.org). Despite his young age, one can still feel his passion for the internet and his pursuit of dreams!

The following interview was completed via email, hoping that everyone can understand a more genuine side of Babei Bookshelf:

1. Please briefly introduce Babei Bookshelf (launch time, website introduction, target audience, etc.)

Babei Bookshelf has an impressive concept — a social library. All books come from member donations and are stored at member locations, and all members can borrow them anytime. Simply put, it's a website that helps people share idle books, mainly targeting college students and white-collar workers who love reading and possess a sharing spirit.

Babei Bookshelf is still very young, launched on November 11th this year, a very special day.

2. Please introduce your team

Besides me, there are three other people in the team.

Plidezus, an excellent designer, all the beautiful interfaces of Babei Bookshelf owe to his efforts. Additionally, he always comes up with many exciting new ideas.

Orchid, wrote the first version of the product specification requirements document, currently working on operational aspects.

Piptu, working on mobile development.

3. What was the initial idea behind creating Babei Bookshelf? Was it triggered by an actual need in daily life?

I personally like giving away the books I've read. After once again gifting over 20 books as usual, I thought there must be many people with similar ideas, so I conducted a survey on Twitter.

It was January 14th this year, I posted a tweet: "Let's do a small survey, would you be willing to take out your idle books to exchange with others or even give them away directly? Welcome RT." I quickly received many positive responses and even made it onto @rtmeme.

I immediately decided to create a platform for sharing books, benefiting both others and myself.

4. Currently, Babei Bookshelf should be a purely user-focused platform for sharing books they've read. Have you considered any commercialization aspects?

Babei Bookshelf is actually just our side project, and we haven't yet considered commercialization. However, if there is a suitable profit model that doesn't deviate from our original intentions, we would be willing to try.

5. Can you talk about the future direction of Babei Bookshelf?

Although Babei Bookshelf has many possibilities, in the foreseeable future, we will remain focused, making the sharing of idle books better, further promoting the circulation of books.

In the future, I hope that through the platform of Babei Bookshelf and related systems, we can output values externally, influencing more people.

6. You spent a long time focusing on your Weborn site before. How did that blogging experience help you in product development?

It had a significant impact on my personal positioning. Without managing Weborn, I would have positioned myself as a Developer, but now I prefer to be a product manager — it's more interesting and challenging.

7. Personally, what expectations and ideas do you have for the future?

In the next five years, I have three progressively difficult wishes:

1) Create two or three excellent internet products.

2) Open a profitable coffee shop.

3) Write a book that helps readers.
