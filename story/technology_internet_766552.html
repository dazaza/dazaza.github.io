Qvod launches a box product: priced at 399 yuan, capable of running Windows

　　This afternoon, Qvod Technology released its multi-screen interactive hardware product named "Little Cube" - Qvod Living Room Computer. This product focuses on the home entertainment market, priced at 399 yuan, and will be exclusively sold by JD.com starting from October.

　　In terms of hardware, Qvod's "Little Cube" has a compact design, measuring 70 mm * 70 mm * 70 mm, with a base made of pure aluminum or pure copper material, giving the entire product a strong metallic feel.

　　In terms of internal components, Qvod's "Little Cube" uses a system-on-a-chip provided by Rockchip, featuring a quad-core CPU + quad-core GPU (graphics processor). It scores high on some benchmarking platforms, surpassing some powerful smartphones.

　　Equipped with WiFi and Intel WiDi wireless connectivity modules, Qvod's "Little Cube" can also enable content sharing between smartphones, tablets, and TVs.

　　Qvod's "Little Cube" runs on the Android 4.2 operating system. In terms of video content, Qvod Living Room Computer is integrated with the video broadcasting platform of Future Television ICNTV, allowing users to watch movies, TV series, and other video content on this platform. Additionally, Qvod's "Little Cube" is connected to Qvod's gaming platform, enabling users to enjoy gaming entertainment through it.

　　Qvod CEO Wang Xin stated that Qvod's "Little Cube" can also run the Windows operating system and support Windows applications. For this reason, Qvod prefers to refer to "Little Cube" as a living room computer rather than a mere "box" product available in the market.

　　In the future, Qvod's "Little Cube" will receive weekly software updates. With a specially customized remote control, users can easily operate Qvod's "Little Cube".

　　Qvod's "Little Cube" focuses on the home entertainment market, priced at 399 yuan, and will be exclusively launched on JD.com starting from October. Wang Xin claimed that this product is being sold at zero profit.

　　When Qvod Company was founded in 2007, it initially developed a video player. By accumulating a large user base, Qvod subsequently launched a gaming platform called Kuaikan. In recent years, Qvod has also introduced multi-screen interactive products such as set-top boxes and TV sticks.
