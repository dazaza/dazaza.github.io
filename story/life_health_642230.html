What fruits are the most beneficial to eat when you're sick?

by zhouzhou56 on 2011-12-23 12:12:23

Diarrhea:

It is advisable to eat fruits with astringent properties such as grapes, pomegranates, apples, and bayberries; it is not recommended to eat bananas, pears, or watermelons, which are cold in nature and promote bowel movements, as they may cause loose stools and worsen the condition.

Obesity:

Those who are dieting should be selective when eating fruits. It's best to avoid eating apples and grapes, as the former is high in calories and the latter is high in sugar. There are many types of fruits available on the market, each with different nutritional compositions, so one should be mindful of what they consume.

Constipation, Hemorrhoids:

It is advisable to eat bananas, pears, peaches, and mandarins, which help to lubricate the intestines and promote bowel movements; it is not recommended to eat persimmons, hawthorns, apples, or lotus seeds, as these fruits contain more tannins, which have an astringent effect and can easily cause constipation after consumption.

Ulcers, Excessive Stomach Acid:

It is not advisable to eat sour pears, lemons, bayberries, plums, or apricots, which are high in acid, as they may damage ulcer healing or exacerbate symptoms due to an increase in stomach acid.

Diabetes:

It is advisable to eat fruits rich in pectin, such as pineapples, bayberries, and cherries, which can alter insulin secretion and help lower blood sugar levels. It is not advisable to eat fruits high in sugar such as jujubes, grapes, bananas, apples, pears, figs, lychees, or lemons, as they may raise blood sugar levels and increase the burden on the pancreas, hindering treatment.

Hepatitis:

It is advisable to eat fruits rich in vitamin C, such as bananas, pears, jujubes, mandarins, apples, and watermelons, which protect the liver and promote the regeneration of liver cells.

Heart Failure, Severe Edema:

It is not advisable to eat fruits high in water content such as watermelons, pears, and pineapples, as excessive water intake can worsen heart failure and edema.

Acute Nephritis:

If there is poor kidney function or swelling requiring salt restriction, it is not advisable to eat bananas, as they contain higher levels of sodium salts, which can exacerbate swelling and increase the burden on the heart and kidneys.

Hypertension, Arteriosclerosis:

It is advisable to eat fruits rich in vitamin C, such as hawthorns, jujubes, and mandarins, which have antihypertensive and vascular hardening relief effects.

Coronary Heart Disease, Hyperlipidemia:

It is advisable to eat fruits like mandarins, pomelos, hawthorns, peaches, and strawberries, as these fruits are rich in vitamin C and niacin, which help reduce blood lipids and cholesterol.

Myocardial Infarction, Stroke:

It is advisable to eat digestion-promoting fruits such as watermelons, bananas, mandarins, and peaches; it is not advisable to eat persimmons, apples, or lotus seeds, as they contain tannins with astringent properties that can easily cause constipation and worsen the condition.

Respiratory Infections:

Especially for patients with sore throat, cough, and excessive phlegm, it is advisable to eat fruits like pears, loquats, oranges, apricots, and monk fruit, which can dissolve phlegm, moisturize the lungs, and relieve coughs.

Hot Constitution:

It is advisable to eat fruits with a cooling nature such as pears, bananas, and watermelons; it is not advisable to eat warm-natured fruits such as grapes, mandarins, jujubes, and cherries.

Fever:

It is advisable to eat fruits with functions of promoting body fluids, quenching thirst, reducing heat, and detoxifying, such as pears and mandarins. Since feverish patients sweat a lot, pears and mandarins, which contain abundant water and potassium, are more beneficial for them.
