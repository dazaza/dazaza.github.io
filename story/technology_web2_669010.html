Wang Lijun: Severe Punishments to Solve Food and Drug Safety Issues

by m123456789 on 2012-02-18 19:00:35

This kind of harm has already emerged, it's just that it hasn't yet drawn attention. Wang Lijun said that when he saw those disabled children in kindergartens and orphanages, the shock was indescribable.

"I've faced so many murder scenes, been to so many large-scale natural disaster sites. Though heart-wrenching, I've endured them all. But seeing these children, seeing how they live such tragic lives, leaves me shaken in ways unimaginable," Wang Lijun said. Many people only see that these children have physical disabilities but rarely consider why this happens. He took blood samples from these children and found through research that toxic food is the main culprit.

Wang Lijun suggested that food and drug safety issues should be addressed as an important institutional issue, implementing "severe punishment without pardon, leaving offenders bankrupt."

"We see meat left out in the countryside where flies don't even land on it; blooming rapeseed flowers that bees won't go near," he noted. With today’s food, you can achieve any shape, any color, preserve it for a certain time, or create any form you want. Wang Lijun worries that the altered lifespans of plants and animals will transfer through food to human lifespans, causing DNA mutations and affecting several generations.

"Severe punishment without pardon, leaving offenders bankrupt."

"The report is imbued with law throughout," Wang Lijun counted and found that in the work report of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, there were 375 mentions of the word "law," including references to the Constitution, laws, judiciary, law enforcement, legislation, rule of law, compliance, legality, legal system, and various enacted laws, setting a historical precedent.

"Food and drug safety issues have become related to national development and ethnic security." On March 10, Wang Lijun, a national people's representative and Director of the Municipal Public Security Bureau, stated during the review of the work report of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress that the legal system of socialism with Chinese characteristics has been formed, marking China's entry into a rule-of-law society. We should adopt severe punishments to resolve livelihood safety issues.

Wang Lijun believes this is an important sign that China has entered a rule-of-law society, indicating that the mature framework of China's legal society has been established. "Since we are a rule-of-law society, I believe using the law and enforcing it, including adopting severe punishments, to resolve livelihood safety issues is the most effective means."

Why does he say this? Wang Lijun explained that now food and drug safety issues have become related to national development and ethnic security. "If social security in a region is poor, it can be stabilized quickly through various measures. If food and drug safety reaches this current level, everyone remains silent and bears it silently, affecting several generations."

Mushroom cultivation requires gas masks

"Our fire department's gas masks, after being decommissioned, were actually snapped up. Who's using them? Those who cultivate mushrooms use them," Wang Lijun said. The spray used during mushroom cultivation is so strong that gas masks are needed, forming a harmful three-step process: taking out loans of over 100,000 yuan to start cultivating mushrooms, the first step is getting rich, building a small house, buying a diesel car; the second step is finally sending their children to school while accumulating some savings; the third step is lying in a hospital bed with cancer! Wang Lijun said that although not every case follows this pattern, the probability is quite high.

Wang Lijun's words are not exaggerated. Since February last year, Wang Lijun and relevant experts and scholars, while studying social stability topics, visited countless factories, small workshops, plastic greenhouses, farms, etc., where they witnessed a terrifying phenomenon.

Food and drug safety issues affect several generations

Therefore, Wang Lijun earnestly appealed, "How much responsibility we can bear depends on our sense of duty and conscience." He believes that paying attention to livelihood safety is the best form of livelihood. If we do not adopt severe punishments to decisively resolve food and drug safety issues, the public can only continue to silently endure.
