Ge said that at first he did not intend to overdraw so much, but later the credit limit kept increasing, and the amount to be repaid also grew larger, which is why he started using other credit cards to cover the gaps. "Most of the overdraft was used to pay interest, and the remaining money I withdrew and transferred it to a bank card in my own name," Ge said he had a dream - to start his own company.

Ge shed tears of regret as the police seized the involved credit cards from him ■ Photos by reporter Chen Si On November 11 last year, when he planned to return home from Shenyang, he was seen by Officer Zhang Tian on the way to Taoxian Airport.

Yesterday in the detention center, he cried bitterly and said that he wanted to use this money to start a company and achieve his dream.

He deeply regrets, "I hate myself..."

Currently, Ge has been approved for arrest by the procuratorate for maliciously overdrawing credit cards.

The police had to take him back to the police station, "It was obvious that something was wrong with him because after he got into the police car, he became more nervous, like 'once I enter the police station today, I won't be able to leave'."

The ID cards were not Ge's, and the bank cards involved more than ten banks. Ge admitted that although these cards were all his, the identities of the cardholders were not him, but rather the names on those more than ten ID cards. Previously, he also threw away more than twenty credit cards after malicious overdraft.

Most of this money he didn't spend, but instead withdrew it as cash and transferred it to his own account. A man suspected of killing a family of three due to emotional and financial disputes.

Ge is from Zhengzhou, Henan Province. After graduating from university, he worked as a technician in a company, often traveling on business.

"My father sold the house to help me repay the debt, I feel sorry for my parents, wife, and child." Ge said that actually his job was good, with a monthly salary of nearly ten thousand yuan, and the travel allowance was enough for daily expenses, there was no burden at home.

The Economic Investigation Brigade of Dongling (Hunnan New District) Branch reminded citizens to protect personal information well, report lost ID cards promptly and apply for replacement, regularly check personal credit reports at the Bank of China Credit Department, if any illegal misuse or other situations are found, immediately report to the police. If ID card copies are used to handle business, clearly state the purpose and time on the copy to avoid being exploited by others in the future.

"At that time, I just thought he was suspicious. Normally, people going to the airport take taxis, drive, or take airport shuttles, but he was walking to the airport, carrying two bags." Zhang Tian said, it was these two bags that made him stand out, obviously someone who was flying, "the computer bag was bulging. When I asked him what his name was, he hesitated."

In three years, he maliciously overdrawn more than 200,000 yuan using credit cards under others' names. The IDs were all picked up in internet cafes.

Yesterday in the detention center, I met Ge, he seemed quite depressed, speaking softly, always looking down.

From 2009 until now, he has maliciously overdrawn more than 200,000 yuan using credit cards under others' names.

"These ID cards were all picked up in internet cafes over the past few years. Since picking up the first ID card in an internet cafe and successfully obtaining a credit card, I began malicious overdrafts." Ge said, although banks require proof of income and other information, this was too easy for him who works in software development, just using software to create a fake income statement, even the official seal can be done online himself, January 5, 2012.

At the police station, under police insistence, Ge opened his computer bag, inside besides a laptop, there were actually more than forty various bank credit cards, more than ten ID cards, and a SIM card for a mobile phone.

Morning Post News (reporter Yang Wei) With a monthly salary of nearly ten thousand yuan, and travel allowances, he still fraudulently obtained dozens of credit cards using others' ID cards.

When questioned, the suspicious man refused to open the bag.

Until he was caught by Shenyang police, despite having a university education, he still didn't understand why he had to go to the detention center, he always thought that once all the money was paid back, he could go home and celebrate the New Year with his family.
