Those Past Hurts

The bustling city of yesteryears slept for a few days under the dim shadows, and today finally, we can see bright sunshine, so clear, so pure, slanting in through the broken glass windows. My mood is no longer as heavy as before; when one becomes cheerful, they will find that if you look around, every person in this world is so lovable.

I've grown accustomed to such days, flipping through my old diaries under the sunlight, though they are all filled with memories that make me worry. But each one of them is a story from my growth. On such a morning, in loose pajamas, with a cigarette in hand, I let the pungent smoke swirl around me. Life is sometimes dull, yet at other times abundant. The perfumed lilies on the dressing table emit an enticing and alluring fragrance; the sunlight makes me feel suffocated. Yes, all the painful memories have been deeply etched into my heart. I've tried leaving that sorrowful city behind, living another kind of life without her shadow, without her scolding, and perhaps never seeing again the man I detest.

Emotions, truly, are strange things. The more you want to forget, the more they entangle you. I originally wanted to live quietly and peacefully by myself. Every midnight, listening alone to the sound of typing on the keyboard, though monotonous, it allows me to completely release my inner melancholy. Only in such a state do I feel like I am a man living with dignity.

But after being lonely for too long, I always feel like something is missing beside me.

Shuttling back and forth between the dormitory and the office building, what’s missing when I sleep at night is a pair of warm, delicate little hands. In fact, every midnight is spent in agony, plagued by frequent nightmares that wake me up in the middle of the night. I think, my life should be able to accommodate another woman.

As for my heart, it has already become somewhat broken, even showing cracks.

Closing my eyes, I really cannot forget her in my heart, nor can I forget that unforgettable, heart-wrenching midnight.

At three o’clock in the morning that day, the telephone on the dressing table rang. I was still drowsy, wondering who would call at such a late hour.

Ah Juan lay in my arms, hearing the ringing, her face showed a slightly panicked expression.

"Dear, I'll answer the phone, go back to sleep, okay?"

Regretfully, I pulled away from the warm blanket, ready to reach out and answer the phone.

"Don't bother with it, let's go back to sleep!" Ah Juan's expression seemed to calm down a bit. The phone kept ringing, but neither of us paid attention. Our bodies were tightly intertwined. It had been over a year since we'd seen each other, and today I finally returned to my own city. Though physically and mentally exhausted, I knew what I needed most at this moment. After a passionate session, Ah Juan lay comfortably next to me.

"—Ring—"

Damn it, the cursed phone rang again. This time she panicked, pulling the blanket aside and jumping out of bed in one swift motion. She reached out and ripped the phone cord out.

"Midnight calls, insane!" She cursed angrily.

We lay down again. After a while, noisy voices came from the hallway – several men's scolding and shouting voices. The door was violently smashed open. The scene that followed...

Three hooligans, and a sharp knife glinting coldly. The wounds left on my body.

Among them, the silhouette of a stranger looked unusually familiar to her. I understood, I finally understood everything.

"Juan, I didn't expect that after returning to you after a year, I would end up with nothing, not even anything left inside. I hurt, I'm disappointed..." This was the last time I shed tears for her; I’m tired. My tears have dried. From now on, I won't be so obsessed. I hate my infatuation, I hate my foolishness. I hate how our love crumbled so easily against time. Apart from leaving, I can't think of any other good choice.

Perhaps, people start fleeing from the moment they are born.

Perhaps, our feelings were a game from the very beginning.

Now, everything is over.

Today, it's a sunny day, and my mood shouldn't stay gloomy anymore.
