Collect sticky rice, promote group buying! What on earth is Baidu up to?

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 13:08:33

Six months ago, Baidu's group-buying navigation, which had separated from Hao123, rebranded itself and quietly launched a group-buying feature based on Baidu's LBS division. The acquisition of Nuomi by Baidu also marked the completion of BAT giants' encirclement of the group-buying market. Since then, group-buying has become a game for giants. Each company has its own expectations, so what is the logic behind Baidu's group-buying that relies on Baidu Maps?

Starting from October last year, Baidu began to aggressively layout its mobile O2O business in the form of a division. Prior to this, Baidu had already achieved some success in mobile advertising and gaming due to its traffic advantage, but it still faced challenges in mobile monetization internally. According to insiders, Baidu's mobile advertising business was still in the transition phase, with some mobile ad orders mainly sold through bundling with PC ads, not yet completely independent from the previous system. However, e-commerce and group-buying businesses, as powerful tools for monetization, had not seen significant breakthroughs.

Meanwhile, Baidu's previous advantage of "traffic diversion" in the e-commerce sector was gradually being replaced by vertical giants. Meituan and Dianping's vertical advantages were attempting to break away from Baidu and go solo.

Although the mobile advertising landscape was opening up, Baidu could not afford to lose the future "cash cow" of O2O! The battle of O2O had just begun, and Baidu naturally wanted a piece of the pie. How would they play it? — Start with group-buying!

In recent conversations with insiders at Baidu Maps, Ba Ren obtained the following information:

1. From navigation to group-buying, Baidu wants to go self-operated

Previously, Baidu's group-buying relied on Hao123 to operate a group-buying navigation service, earning a little money through its traffic advantage. However, as the group-buying market consolidated, with Dianping and Meituan becoming leaders, Baidu realized that simply buying traffic was outdated. If they continued to stand idly by the door of group-buying, they might never have a mature model to reach grassroots merchants in the future. Therefore, Baidu internally determined the basic strategy of shifting from a guide to self-operation in group-buying.

2. Accumulating data and experience for Baidu's O2O strategy

Internally at Baidu, it was believed that the only reliable O2O handle in the map industry was the group-buying model. For Baidu Maps and even the entire Baidu, O2O monetization was an inevitable choice in the future. Currently, group-buying could ensure a relatively stable cash flow for Baidu Maps, while also allowing them to establish good interactions with merchants and accumulate data, laying the foundation for building a long-term profit model!

An insider from Baidu Maps emphasized that in the long run, Baidu's group-buying was merely a transitional product for Baidu's O2O monetization. Although the group-buying model was highly replicable and easy to start, its integration with LBS was still relatively rigid, and it was not the best LBS monetization model for Baidu Maps. Reconstructing the group-buying model in the future was not impossible.

Therefore, Baidu's current group-buying operation has two tasks: short-term self-operation monetization and long-term accumulation of data and offline experience, using this as a basis to derive new O2O models based on maps.

3. Group-buying data mainly relies on cooperation + agency

Baidu's group-buying online data mainly comes from two aspects: one is the group-buying data provided by major group-buying websites such as Dianping and Lahou, and the other is the group-buying data collected by Baidu's authorized local agents and its own offline team. As an insider from Baidu Maps put it, Baidu was not adept at doing online work. Currently, more than half of the LBS division consisted of technical teams, and the entire department lacked strong business genes. Thus, outsourcing BD business was an inevitable choice. The agency model was also a quick channel to seek self-operation shortcuts.

4. The product logic of Baidu's group-buying mainly addresses immediate needs

Unlike traditional group-buying logic, Baidu's group-buying places greater emphasis on mobile ends and positions users' needs as "immediate group-buying needs," meaning users use their group-buying immediately after purchasing. A group-buying industry analyst believed that Baidu's logic was mainly based on the fact that although its group-buying product had an independent app, it still primarily depended on Baidu Maps. The location-based consumption demands generated within Baidu Maps were often immediate. Therefore, its group-buying logic was similar. In the future, Baidu's group-buying would likely further weaken PC services and eventually build a completely mobile-based group-buying platform centered around Baidu Maps.

5. Nuomi did not actually gain priority in Baidu's O2O strategy

Although Nuomi was an important piece for Baidu, insiders revealed that there was not much interaction between Nuomi and Baidu. Perhaps they were still in the process of integration, or perhaps both parties had not yet finalized the way of cooperation. In any case, at this stage, Nuomi did not play a major role in Baidu's group-buying and currently existed in the cooperation list of Baidu's group-buying only as a data provider, like Dianping and Lahou.

In the eyes of most analysts, Nuomi's role for Baidu mainly focused on layout and the future. On one hand, Baidu's internal teams could learn offline experiences from Nuomi, and on the other hand, Nuomi practically became a tool for Baidu to defend against Meituan and Dianping.

An insider from Dianping's group-buying told Ba Ren that after Baidu took control of Nuomi, Dianping's internal team no longer looked favorably on data cooperation with Baidu and was studying relevant countermeasures. Internally, they believed that Dianping's professionalism and data advantages were something Baidu Maps did not possess, but Baidu's platform, brand, and traffic advantages would lay the groundwork for future direct competition with Dianping and Meituan.

6. Platform ambition highlights weakening of cooperative brands

An insider from a group-buying website told Ba Ren that when handling cooperation with group-buying partners, Baidu mainly followed the logic of "Baidu solving traffic and online issues, other group-buying brands solving offline and data issues." Due to its traffic advantage, Baidu's group-buying exerted significant pressure on other group-buying brands, including Nuomi. The data they provided could not showcase their own brands within Baidu Maps' group-buying; Baidu only retained the unified name of Baidu Group-buying.

Finally, regarding Baidu's future O2O model, analyst Huang Yuanpu predicted that Baidu might be seeking to convert users attracted by high discounts into an O2O logic based on "convenience."

In summary, Baidu's group-buying logic becomes much clearer: first, access multiple group-buying data sources through Baidu's traffic advantage to establish a group-buying platform and accumulate data resources; second, achieve group-buying self-operation based on Nuomi and agency BD and accumulate offline experience; finally, reconstruct the group-buying model on the basis of previous accumulation and build an O2O monetization ecosystem centered on Baidu Maps. This logic of first "catching up" and then focusing on O2O monetization is indeed quite clever!
