(1) The Shocking Story of Co-ed Roommates

Not long after graduation, my roommate had to leave due to work reasons. As he was leaving, he half-jokingly suggested that this would be a good opportunity for me to find someone else to co-rent with. His words were casual, but they struck a chord in me. In a moment of impulsiveness, I actually started looking.

I carefully stated my requirements on the rental listing: co-ed roommates only. Although I received many calls from persistent men asking if other men could apply or if it absolutely had to be a co-ed arrangement, I rejected them all. Some cautious women asked why I specifically wanted a co-ed roommate. I could confidently fabricate a reason and say that having a girl around would keep things tidier than an all-male household. My intentions were clear to everyone, but I was waiting for someone who either didn't know or knowingly chose to take the risk. Of course, I left myself an out by saying that a classmate might also move in, but the applicant could check out the place first. If she liked it, I'd tell her the classmate decided not to move in and it would be hers. If she didn't like it, I'd tell her to go back home and I'd call if the classmate backed out. Everything was perfectly prepared, just waiting for the right person to fall into the trap.

A famous CCTV commentator once said, "Be as lustful as a man." Perhaps this perfectly guided our male comrades' way of life. But often, when intentions are too obvious, opportunities become scarce. After posting the ad, most of the harassment came from men, occasionally even women with their families. It was truly frustrating. Eventually, I had no choice but to modify the requirements to: gender preference unlimited, ladies preferred.

Some men began coming to check out the place one after another. But I couldn't seem to find the right fit. One Sunday, just as I was getting anxious, a student from a prestigious university contacted me, saying he urgently needed a place to live and would take it if it was suitable. He was on his way over, and I told him there was no problem. About 20 minutes later, I suddenly received a text message that read: "I am a girl. I think my outgoing and generous personality should meet your requirements. I can check out the place around 3 PM. Would that be convenient?" This unexpected hope made me ecstatic. I immediately called the boy and said, "You can come around 3:15. I have something to do today and may not be back until then." Meanwhile, I had already given them both the address.

Surprisingly, at around 3:15, I received calls from both saying they were almost here. I panicked, thinking, "What if they arrive at the same time? I can't obviously reject the man and choose the woman if the man gets here first." But surprisingly, the girl arrived first.

When I met the girl who introduced herself as Xue'er, I was so shocked that my mouth nearly dropped open. Such a reaction revealed how excited I was inside. Although she wasn't exactly a beauty queen, Xue'er was undeniably sexy. She dressed well, fashionably, and provocatively. Her figure was curvaceous, and her short top exposed her navel, which was particularly enticing. At that moment, I almost screamed inside, "Oh God, living with such a girl, how can I avoid committing a crime?" But I managed to control my excitement. I showed her the apartment, and she examined me carefully first. Later, she asked me if she could stay in the larger room. Originally, I only intended to rent out the smaller one. The larger room had better conditions and air conditioning, while the smaller one had a TV but a broken lock. I hesitated without agreeing, and at that moment, another boy who wanted to see the place also arrived. Clearly, he hadn't expected me to let someone else see it first. Surprised, he still looked around thoroughly. This guy kept praising it, saying he found no issues and could live in the smaller room.

Because competition had been introduced, Xue'er became somewhat anxious. She said she could also live in the smaller room since she was the first to see the place. After some thought, I generously told them, "You two can decide among yourselves. You're from the same school anyway." Naturally, out of gentlemanly manners or perhaps just to save face, I assumed the man would step aside. Sure enough, after a while, he said, "Since the girl came first, let her stay." He could look for another place and left, reminding me before he went that if the girl decided not to stay, he was still interested. Once the man was far away, Xue'er again negotiated with me to let her stay in the larger room. After some consideration, I thought it wouldn't be right to be so petty and agreed. She happily paid the deposit, got a key copy, and left.

Watching her charming back gradually disappear from my sight, I suddenly realized I hadn't asked her if she had a boyfriend. What if she moved in with her boyfriend? I'd be miserable, listening to live broadcasts through the walls every night. But it was too late to change my mind, so I could only leave it to fate.
