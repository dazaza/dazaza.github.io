Unpaid water bills amounting to 2.6 million yuan over 10 years

by shengshuay on 2009-11-27 20:17:37

Xinhua News Agency, Wuhan (reported by Zhu Sufang and Chen Lihong) - Yesterday, the meter readers from the Hankou Water Supply Department of Wuhan Water Utility Group visited Changsi Community in Hanying Street, Jianghan District for the first time to check water meters for 631 residents of Changqing No.5 Compound. This is the first time in the 15-year history of this neighborhood that these households have been included in the normal meter reading management system.

"Resolving the 'hard nut' of Changqing No.5 Compound serves as a model case, worthy of promotion," said Lei Ming, head of the arrears clearance office of the Hankou Water Supply Department of Wuhan Water Utility Group. Currently, there are more than 300 similar legacy issues just in the Hankou area alone, with an initial plan to resolve them within three years.

Zhang Zhenghua, resident of Room 304, Unit 2, Building 9 of Compound No.5, spoke fairly: "Water is a commodity, and buying commodities requires payment – it's only natural. Now, we feel reassured when using water!" Changqing No.5 Compound in Changsi Community was completed in 1994, mainly housing relocated residents from the Liuduqiao area, totaling 631 households. Due to inadequate property infrastructure at the time of relocation, 31 water tanks and pump houses were not properly handed over to the Water Utility Group for management, and the water fees had been paid on behalf by the district housing bureau. By 1999, the district housing bureau could no longer bear the burden and stopped paying the fees, causing the water supply situation in the neighborhood to deteriorate.

"Every day, a dedicated person would go to the pump house to draw water three times, each time for one hour; otherwise, water supply above the fourth floor would be cut off," leading to continuous complaints from residents: broken pipes went unrepaired, water tanks unwashed, and water quality substandard. Over ten years, more than 600 households in Changqing No.5 Compound accumulated water fees amounting to 2.6 million yuan. With no way to pay, residents became accustomed to using free water.

In June this year, Wuhan Water Utility Group issued a "final ultimatum" to cut off water supply while simultaneously seeking support from district and street offices. Relevant departments of Jianghan District held four coordination meetings consecutively, dividing the renovation work into "three contributions": the government contributes a portion, investing 800,000 yuan in funds to renovate and upgrade the pump house and replace water tanks; the water company contributes a portion, laying new pipelines for each unit and installing individual meters for each household; residents contribute a portion, with the streets and communities taking charge, collecting historical arrears and meter installation fees at the lowest price.

This initiative received support from 60% of the community residents. By the beginning of this month, water meters were installed for all 631 households. After inspection, the water quality after renovation met municipal standards, allowing the 631 households to access tap water again.
