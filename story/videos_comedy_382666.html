Fat, who I offend? I'm willing to!

by djs365 on 2011-02-28 10:47:09

I'm very fat, and people always say I'm fat. But I think, how does being fat bother you? Whenever people see me, they would say: "You've gained weight again." I don't know what's wrong with me being fat, but in other people's eyes, I have to lose weight. If I don't, it's considered abnormal, and there are often many embarrassing moments.

When I go shopping, I can only enjoy it with my eyes because there is no clothes for me to buy. I believe those service ladies who say: "You can't fit into this piece of clothing." Thus, wearing clothes forces me to lose weight.

In the past, when I had meals with colleagues, if their plates were full to the brim, no one would say anything. However, if I just had a bowl of congee and some pickles on my plate, someone would immediately shout loudly: "How come you're so fat and still eating?"

Others leave their large water cups on my table, and immediately someone comes over with a smirk: "You need a big cup to drink water too." They won't even let me drink water, isn't that annoying?

During summer swimming, while doing warm-up exercises on the shore, someone shouts: "Everyone move up, the tide is rising." Just as I was puzzled, I heard whispers from the side: "The fat guy is going to get in the water." Isn't that infuriating?

In the morning when taking the bus, after finally squeezing onto the bus, someone gives me a dirty look: "No wonder the bus is crowded, one person takes up two people's space." Where can I go to complain about this?

On a freezing winter day, while walking on the road, a colleague comes over and puts his cold hand under my armpit: "Thick fat, warm me up." It's as if I'm a polar bear, and I really want to eat him.

When climbing stairs in a hurry and panting slightly, immediately someone shakes their head: "It's not easy for fat people." Showing sympathy on their face.

In the past, when colleagues invited us for a meal with some fruits, after everyone ate them all, the latecomers immediately stared at me: "It must be you who ate a lot." Leaving me speechless.

For overtime work, immediately someone would "give advice": "Comrades with good health should not refuse."

For voluntary labor: "The fatter ones should do more work."

Sometimes, some of the above experiences would repeat several times in a day.

I am a fat person, I have a broad mind, and I don't care about all kinds of inhuman "attacks".
