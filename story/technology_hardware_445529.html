More than a hundred people searched but got no result about the missing 60-year-old lawyer in Shanghai

by xiaoyou234l on 2011-06-14 08:41:03

Oriental Net, May 24 News: According to the "Oriental Morning Post" report, on May 20th, a group of 24 middle-aged and elderly hiking enthusiasts from Shanghai spontaneously organized a trip to the Taihu Source Scenic Area agritainment. Unexpectedly, during the hike on the afternoon of May 21st, 60-year-old hiker Zhang Quancai got lost and did not return from the mountain. As of last night, he was still missing.

On the day of the incident, the Lin'an City government had already dispatched more than 100 police officers, armed police, special police, and firefighters to search the mountain in groups, but the next day, Mr. Zhang's mobile phone was turned off. By 7 p.m. yesterday evening, there was still no news.

Currently, the local search and rescue operation is still ongoing.

Hiking friends were largely unfamiliar with each other

On May 20th, Ye Jinguo and his group of 24 people set out from People's Square in Shanghai to their destination - Bai Sha Village, Taihu Source Town, Lin'an, Zhejiang Province, at the Jinxintianli Resort. They stayed overnight at the inn.

On the morning of May 21st, 60-year-old lawyer Zhang Quancai, along with his friends and the Ye couple, finished touring the Taihu Source scenic area. During lunch, someone recommended another primitive scenic spot called "Yin-Yang Gou".

Ye Jinguo planned to go with his wife, and coincidentally, Mr. Zhang also wanted to go. The three of them then headed towards "Yin-Yang Gou". At around 1 p.m., they walked along the ditch towards an elevation of about 500 meters.

"All of us are people who love traveling, so we just gathered together. We don't know each other very well. I didn't even know the missing Mr. Zhang before," said Ye Jinguo.

At first, the three were not far apart, but as their physical conditions varied, the distance gradually widened. Ye Jinguo and his wife stopped and ate some food, deciding not to continue climbing and went back along the same route.

"Before going back, I shouted 'Mr. Zhang, we're going back!' but didn't hear any response."

During dinner time, Mr. Zhang's friend mentioned that he hadn't returned yet. Hearing this, Ye Jinguo realized something was wrong and quickly asked someone to call Mr. Zhang. In the phone call, Mr. Zhang said he was lost on the mountain! Everyone was shocked and told him to stay where he was and wait for rescue.

More than a hundred people are still searching

After receiving the alarm, the Taihu Source town police immediately organized dozens of people, led by local villagers, to search the mountain, but by 3 a.m. the next morning, they found nothing and had to temporarily retreat. More worrying was that from 8 a.m. on the 22nd, Zhang Quancai's mobile phone also turned off.

According to Jiang Tao, deputy mayor of Taihu Source Town, Zhang Quancai's last phone call saying he was lost was in the afternoon, still one or two hours before sunset. He worried that Zhang might have fallen from the rocks after wandering aimlessly.

The mountainous area where Zhang Quancai disappeared has an altitude of over 1,000 meters. The road only reaches halfway up the mountain, but there is no path beyond that point. If he walked in a straight line direction, it would take about five hours to descend from the Anji side. But yesterday, the rescue personnel also searched the halfway point on the Anji side, where there are many dried bamboo shoots fields, but the workers there all said they hadn't seen anyone.

Moreover, it started raining in Lin'an from the evening of the 21st and rained all day on the 22nd, with low temperatures. Zhang Quancai did not carry any food, and the chances of survival have become slim.

Currently, the search and rescue operation is still ongoing. According to the Taihu Source Town staff, they have divided the 100-plus rescuers into 10 search teams for dispersed searches.

Agritainment is privately operated

From the Changqingfang Community Committee where Mr. Zhang lived, we learned that Zhang Quancai was unmarried, and his parents, younger brother, and sister all live elsewhere. He had lived alone in the neighborhood for over ten years, but was warm-hearted and often provided free legal consultations to residents.

At around 10 a.m. yesterday, Mr. Zhang's workplace, Shanghai Zhenxing Law Firm, stated that they had arranged for two colleagues to go to Lin'an where Mr. Zhang disappeared, but there was no new information yet.

In the afternoon, when the reporter contacted Pan Guifa, the person in charge of the agritainment where the 24 people including Mr. Zhang participated, he indicated that the Jinxintianli Resort belongs to his private operation. If guests want to travel, they just need to call them, and once a certain number of people is reached, they will arrange a bus to pick them up and drop them off at designated points.

Yesterday, relevant persons from the Shanghai Municipal Tourism Administration said that the administration had not yet received any distress calls. The tourism administration reminds tourists that when traveling, they should participate in regular and qualified travel agencies. If traveling alone or freely, they should ensure whether the other party has the necessary tourism qualifications. In case of unexpected situations, they can also call the tourism administration hotline for help.
