JAC Motors: The Most Profitable Automotive Stock＿Stocks

by fj0953210 on 2009-12-05 21:38:01

JAC Motors (600418) (Enter the stock forum, new version of market conditions) has been rising for four consecutive trading days since the rebound began. Today it rose against the trend by 2.89%, with a turnover rate of 6.08%. In 2010, the company's vehicle sales growth is certain, and there will be definite growth in light trucks, Rui Feng MPVs, and passenger cars. At the same time, cost increase space is small, and the gross margin will remain stable. Analysts say that the company’s performance in 2010 has great potential for improvement. The comprehensive earnings per share forecast values for 2009-2011 are respectively 0.31, 0.53, and 0.67 yuan, corresponding to dynamic price-to-earnings ratios of 39, 23, and 18 times; currently, there are a total of 19 analysts tracking the company, with 4 analysts recommending "strong buy" and 15 analysts recommending "buy". The overall rating coefficient is 1.79.

The light truck and MPV businesses have performed exceptionally well, continuously providing support for the company. In terms of sales, affected by national economic stimulus policies, in the first ten months of 2009, the company sold 133,300 light trucks, up 29.32% year-on-year, and 38,500 MPVs, up 24.03% year-on-year. In terms of profitability, due to direct steel procurement costs accounting for about 15%-20% of the production cost of light trucks, as a result of falling steel prices, the company's gross margin on light trucks improved by over 3 percentage points year-on-year. It is estimated that the current gross margin on the company's light trucks is between 15%-18%. The gross margin on Rui Feng MPVs also recovered to over 30% due to reduced depreciation and amortization expenses and lower steel costs, approaching historical best levels. Due to the superior performance of the light truck and MPV businesses, the company's operating conditions have greatly improved. By the end of the third quarter of 2009, the company's long-term and short-term borrowings had decreased by 343 million yuan compared to the end of 2008.

In 2009, the passenger car business still incurred huge losses. The company invested approximately 2 billion yuan in its passenger car production line, which was put into use in 2008. This brought annual depreciation expenses of about 200 million yuan for the company. Additionally, due to the low pricing of the existing Tong Yue model, the company found itself in the predicament of producing more but losing even more. We estimate that the company's passenger car business loss in 2009 will be between 500 million yuan to 600 million yuan.

He Yue is the biggest variable for the company in the future. On October 18th, the highly anticipated He Yue model was launched, priced at 65,800 yuan to 88,800 yuan. Compared to the Tong Yue, the average selling price of the He Yue increased by 16,000 yuan. Since the He Yue and Tong Yue are produced on the same line, the launch of the He Yue significantly improved the profitability of the passenger car products. Besides the launch of new products, the company's independently developed small-displacement engine production line also started operation, with a current monthly output of around 1,000 units. The company stated that since the self-developed engine just started mass production, its product reliability still needs time to verify, so the company intentionally controlled the supply volume of self-developed engines. As the company's processing technology improves, it is expected that the self-developed engines might be mass-produced in the second half of next year. With the increase in engine self-sufficiency, the gross margin of the passenger car business will also improve. It is estimated that when the annual sales volume of He Yue reaches 80,000 units, the gross margin level of this model will reach around 15%.

A flexible sales strategy helps the company achieve its sales target of 400,000 units in 2010. The company's sales target for 2010 is to ensure 400,000 units and strive for 500,000 units. To achieve this goal, the company adopted a sales strategy of "one policy per area, one policy per product, channel revolution, network penetration", and we believe the core of this strategy is network penetration, fully doing a good job in county and township-level market sales. The company targeted the characteristics of rural areas in China and attempted to integrate the sales platforms of light trucks and passenger cars, constructing a new supermarket-like sales model. From the current experimental results of the company, the automobile supermarket sales model in rural areas performs much better than the 4S store model. This new sales model will help promote the company's products.

Looking forward to 2010, Haitong Securities believes that the company's light truck and MPV businesses will continue to provide financial support for the company's operational development, but profitability will decrease somewhat due to the impact of rising steel prices. It is expected that He Yue will start scaling up in the second quarter of next year, and the growth in He Yue sales will make it possible for the company's passenger car business to reduce losses or even turn a profit. The "overweight" rating for the company is maintained.

Risk factors: Economic recovery below expectations.
