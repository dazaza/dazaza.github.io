1 The Inevitable Path of Manufacturing Industry Informatization - ERP

1.1 Why the Implementation and Utilization of ERP Have Not Fully Met Expectations

ERP has gone through more than 20 years of ups and downs in China, once being regarded as a miraculous cure-all, and also receiving fierce criticism. Now, informatization has become a rational pursuit for Chinese manufacturing enterprises. Therefore, we must re-examine the fact that the utilization status of ERP in China over the past 20 years has not fully met expectations. Many experts have investigated companies that failed to successfully implement ERP and discovered that the following phenomena are almost universal:

- Inaccurate basic data.

- Lack of proactive and feasible planning.

- Employees are unwilling to abandon their habitual work methods to use the ERP system.

- Insufficient education and training.

- Lack of emphasis from enterprise leadership. This last item is often the most serious problem.

It is clear that all these issues are essentially human problems, not problems with the principles or processing logic of ERP.

1.2 Informatization and the Basic Equation of Manufacturing

The fundamental characteristics of manufacturing can be expressed through its basic equation. A manufacturing enterprise, as long as it continues to exist, must repeatedly answer and solve the following four questions:

- What products should be manufactured?

- What raw materials or components should be used to manufacture these products?

- What raw materials or components are currently available?

- What additional raw materials or components should be prepared?

These four questions form the basic equation of manufacturing. If represented by A, B, C, and D respectively, this equation can be expressed conceptually as: A × B – C = D.

Relevant literature points out: The basic equation of manufacturing is like gravity; we can only face it and cannot change it.

As is well known, the core of ERP is MRP (Material Requirements Planning). MRP simulates and solves the basic equation of manufacturing. The development process of ERP is one where the scope of resource planning and control integration continues to expand, but its core remains MRP.

Looking back on the development process of ERP in China, why hasn't ERP disappeared due to fierce criticism and instead regained rational attention with the changing situation? There are two reasons.

Firstly, under the new competitive landscape, informatization has become a rational pursuit for Chinese manufacturing enterprises. Using computer technology to improve enterprise management levels has become a consensus.

Secondly, the basic equation of manufacturing exists universally within the industry. The core issue of informatizing manufacturing management is solving the basic equation of manufacturing.

Then, what is the result of combining informatization with the basic equation of manufacturing? Only ERP!

2 Correct Concepts and Methods Must Be Adopted When Implementing and Utilizing ERP

2.1 Carry Out Necessary Education and Training to Deeply Understand the Basic Principles of ERP

In the development process of any discipline, some fundamental concepts, theories, and methods will accumulate. It is precisely these basic concepts, theories, and methods that support the existence of a discipline and its development. ERP is no different. Software systems are merely carriers of concepts. Only by combining these fundamental concepts, theories, and methods with software functions can one truly grasp an ERP software system. Therefore, enterprises must carry out necessary education and training so that their employees deeply understand the principles of ERP in order to effectively use it and make it a tool for comprehensively improving management levels and winning competition.

2.2 Adhere to the Correct Implementation Methodology

Regarding implementation, domestic and international enterprises have gained rich experience over nearly 50 years of implementing MRP, MRP II, and ERP systems, forming a "reliable route (proven path)." Following this reliable route involves much work and effort (which are unavoidable), but eliminates the risk of failure. The concepts and implementation methodologies of ERP have become "commonplace" within the industry. The characteristic of "commonplace" is that they seem "clear" at first hearing, but after encountering difficulties, one realizes the lack of clarity. However, life doesn't allow us to do everything ourselves. We don't need to encounter the same obstacles others have faced; we can avoid the mistakes others have made. Standing on the shoulders of our predecessors, we should see further than they did. But if we fail to learn from past mistakes, we will repeat the same errors.

3 ERP Should Be Fully and Deeply Utilized

ERP is an effective tool for enterprises to improve management levels and transform operational mechanisms. It should be fully and deeply utilized rather than superficially applied.
