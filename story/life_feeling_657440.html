See again the poem "Butterflies in Love with Flowers"

by lili1 on 2012-01-08 22:26:28

Once again, I see the Ci poem "Butterflies in Love with Flowers"

Flowers fade and red turns to green as small apricots appear. Swallows fly while clear water flows around homes. Willow fluff on the branches is blown away bit by bit, yet there's no place under the sky where fragrant grass doesn't grow. Inside the wall is a swing, outside the wall is a path. Outside the wall walks a passerby, inside the wall a beauty laughs. Her laughter gradually fades away, quietly. Passionate feelings are always troubled by indifference. - "Butterflies in Love with Flowers", Su Shi

The second time I saw this timeless masterpiece "Butterflies in Love with Flowers", I was struck with awe. What left me awestruck and marveling wasn't Su Lao's style of poetry or his exquisite diction. A month ago, I saw this "Butterflies in Love with Flowers" in a phone text message. I like Song Ci poems, such as Liu Yong's "Sweet Eight Provinces" and "Rain Bells", and also Li Qingzhao's "Slow Sounds". The slow Ci poetry of the Song Dynasty is no less than today's rock and jazz music. Perhaps due to personal character factors, I am extremely fond of that faint, leisurely culture of slow Ci poetry. Many times, a book like "I Wish to Live in the Song Dynasty" makes me forget many sorrows and helplessness.

This time, seeing "Butterflies in Love with Flowers", what I want to express is not my obsession and preference for slow Ci poetry. My thoughts immediately turn to that girl whom I've only met once.

Under the incandescent light, my thoughts wander endlessly. My eyes fix blankly on the flashing cursor. I recall a line from "Rain Bells": "Speechless, choked with emotion." It feels as if there's an olive weighing thousands of catties falling down my throat, as if it wants to suffocate and engulf me. I try to suppress these feelings but find no way to do so.

Standing on the third floor of the dormitory building, looking out at the twilight and this beautiful city, the noisy clamor slowly becomes peaceful. The spots of neon in the twilight embellish the night about to deepen. Occasionally, a few cuckoo calls pass by my ears, flowing gently into my heart. I enjoy being enveloped by this tranquil and serene twilight, unconsciously coloring and melting my inner self. It's not loneliness, not loss, but rather a resonance between my heart and the twilight, strange and yearning. At such moments, I like to pour myself a glass of red wine by the window. The deep red, sweet wine emits an alluring aroma, its crystalline brilliance shining in the tall glass, accompanied by a song by Wu Bai, transporting my heart back to those years and months as if they were yesterday. I like Wu Bai's music just as I like slow Ci poetry. In Wu Bai's music, there's progress, excitement, desolation, rebellion, and madness. Every time I listen to his songs, my heart often surges uncontrollably.

Tonight, a single "Butterflies in Love with Flowers" has made me feel this way. How I wish I could sincerely ask her: Are you doing well?
