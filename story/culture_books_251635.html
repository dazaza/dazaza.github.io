Si Xing was unfulfilled in her previous life, originally thinking that traveling back to ancient times would allow her to reshape her life. However, she was still a commoner, later even selling herself into servitude. She successively met Xiao, a talented young man whose abilities were unrecognized, Jun, a mysterious young master, and the unruly son of a high-ranking judge. Si Xing had ideas, could compile book catalogs, make carts, and led the servants in playing polo, thus easily attracting them.

But could she, with her humble status, break free from the fate of becoming a concubine? And where will her love ultimately lie?

Flowers Fade and Green Apricots Grow Small Part One

My previous life wasn't one of fortune; one might even say it was somewhat bizarre. I was an avid reader, which made me mature for my age. Elementary school studies were too easy, leading to laziness, and by middle school, these bad habits hadn't changed. In an era where everything revolved around college admission rates, I inevitably received various forms of "disciplinary" education, some of which caused lifelong harm. The final result was —— in order not to affect the evaluation of college admission rates directly tied to teachers' bonuses, I accepted my teacher's advice, dropped out early, and returned home.

Flowers Fade and Green Apricots Grow Small Part Two

Thus, summer passed unnoticed, as I climbed up and down looking for books, wiping here and there, constantly receiving orders and training from Master Jun Wen. The world shrank to just him, me, and the books. Every day, I dragged my heavy legs back to my small room and fell asleep immediately. Whether anything else happened in the mansion, no one told me, and I had no time to care.

Gradually, as the number of trainings increased, I began to think. After all, I am also a reader, having once won the annual borrowing champion at my school's library. I consider myself to have some affection and passion for books. To be scolded for the sake of books was something my past self could never have imagined in her dreams. What is the reversal of worldly affairs? This is it.

Flowers Fade and Green Apricots Grow Small Part Three

I swayed as I sat, trying to keep my head low while calculating in my mind: I've already left the Jun family. Right or wrong, I've come this far, and going back would surely bring no good. Not many people in the Jun household know me, and they're mostly womenfolk. Even if Jun Wen discovers I've fled, he must first report to Lady Jun, who then sends someone to find me. The only person who knows me among those who can leave is Instructor Sun. If portraits need to be drawn, it will take some effort. Calculating this way, if I can escape the city, my chances of success will be greater. But, how do I get out of the city? As a single woman, and looking disheveled, though the guards don't check everyone, wouldn't they suspect me upon seeing me?
