Changsha bookkeeping agent - favored by small and medium-sized enterprises

by liweilwb on 2009-08-15 00:12:50

For ordinary people, the profession of "bookkeeping agency" is still relatively strange. However, when it comes to "bookkeeping agency", Mr. Chen, the boss of a certain auto parts company on East Road, can't help but praise it.

Three years ago, Mr. Chen began to venture into the auto parts field. As his business expanded, inventory management, accounts receivable and payable, cost accounting, etc., became increasingly complicated. Relying solely on his wife's "ledger" was no longer suitable for their needs. The dense numbers and texts in the ledger left him utterly confused. Especially after establishing the company, monthly tax payments were no longer determined by the tax authorities but based on actual monthly sales. Busy with business during the day and calculations at night, this troubled both Mr. Chen and his wife, affecting their focus and mood while doing business. Thus, the idea of hiring an accountant emerged.

When hiring the first accountant, Mr. Chen was quite confident. He spent money to purchase a computer and office desk and even made space in the small shop as her office, thinking that this would unload the burden. However, the hired accountant had mediocre skills. She failed to produce qualified ledgers and reports as required and could not handle tax declarations according to the requirements of the tax department. Once, they were fined by the tax department. What annoyed the boss even more was that when he inquired about inventory data and other relevant information, she gave vague answers. Spending nearly 1000 yuan on an incompetent accountant left Mr. Chen furious, so he fired her.

Next, Mr. Chen hired a part-time accountant whose skills were passable. However, he rarely came to the company and always rushed in and out like a passerby. Mr. Chen wanted to discuss management and accounting matters with him, but he was always busy and didn't put his heart into it. When Mr. Chen found out that he was simultaneously handling five accounting jobs, he decisively fired him again. How could any boss tolerate an employee who receives a salary but doesn't focus on their work?

Then, Mr. Chen hired a young girl who had just graduated as an accountant. Unexpectedly, after working for several months, this young woman left for a higher-paying job, causing Mr. Chen to miss the timely declaration and payment of taxes for that month.
