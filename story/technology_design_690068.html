Movie "Admission Notice" -- a terrifying movie by Qian Xiaomian

by hoouk686 on 2012-03-04 22:23:28

How many people have suffered from the烦恼of the college entrance examination? How many people have grown up but still find in their nightmares that there are always so many unfinished exercises, and always a blank test paper when the bell rings. How many people are about to face the confusion of the college entrance examination, bearing the enormous torture and worries in their hearts? Have you ever thought about the future? Is life without experiencing university really incomplete? The attraction of this movie for me does not lie in the "admission notice", but in the fact that this school was created by them. From the initial depression, fear, loneliness, escape, and venting, to later calmness, acceptance, and facing it head-on. This school is literally created out of nothing. At first, they just wanted to solve their own confusion and deal with their parents' "attention". Who would have known that there are many children like them who have been rejected by many universities, and they gather together, excitedly appearing in this school that admitted them.

What to do? For this school, its existence is a huge lie. But when their shining dreams are instantly lit up, who would have the heart to cruelly extinguish this dream? Thus, naturally, there are courses and teachers in the school. What class do you want to take? It's not about passively choosing some necessary dry and boring options, but about choosing those shining options in your dreams. Maybe, horror movies, quietly doing nothing, is the lifestyle you desire most, then choose quiet contemplation. Maybe, what you're best at is rock music, filming, skateboarding... or even watching beautiful women. All these can be openly realized here. Even when facing challenges from other schools, everything becomes possible because dreams are ignited.

For me, I greatly admire their thinking. That burst of inspiration, that action of practicing an idea once it comes to mind. That daring to think what others cannot, daring to do what does not exist. Breaking conventions, overturning the ordinary. Returning to one's original intention, the meaning of university is not absolute dullness, but should be a lively process of enhancing one's skills.

The "waste" students from other schools are all "geniuses" here. They are the happiest group of people, living and experiencing life in ways they like, rather than being taught in traditional formulas or other things.

Looking back, how many people have confronted their own dreams? How many people, because of liking something, have turned their liking into work and used this work to bring themselves a good life? Have you deviated from your original path? After studying in university for several years, have you learned to use anything? After leaving university, do you continue to learn in a persistent and resilient way?

An admission notice is a university, and life is also a university. If others cannot give you the dreams you desire, why not step out first and give yourself the dreams you desire?
