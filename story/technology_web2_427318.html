23 Injured in Rear-End Collision of Two Buses; Vehicles Suffer Serious Damage

by dehua900 on 2011-05-08 09:40:08

At 8 o'clock last night, the Bao'an Transport Bureau sent a draft to this newspaper saying that after examination and treatment, 10 injured people had left the hospital on their own. The remaining injured continued to stay in the hospital for observation, http://www.vdisk.cn/down/index/7694972A6170, and the current situation was stable.

After the accident, relevant departments of the Municipal Transport Commission and Bao'an District required relevant units to do a good job in treating, comforting, and compensating the injured, and required an investigation into the accident responsibility. Western Public Bus Company organized a large safety production inspection, www.dnfaa.com. Starting from November 25, the Bao'an Transport Bureau decided to carry out a thorough investigation of potential safety hazards in the bus industry throughout the district until the Spring Festival in 2011, ensuring the safety of passengers.

Master Zheng, the driver of the No. 714 bus, said that he stopped at the bus stop and was about to leave when the No. 728 minibus collided. Master Chen, the driver of the No. 728 minibus, claimed that the No. 714 bus suddenly stopped, and he couldn't react in time, failing to brake. The reporter found that there was no obvious bus stop here. Injured passengers were sent to Xianghang Hengsheng Hospital for treatment, with a total of 23 people injured. After examination, one person was confirmed to have a ruptured liver, but vital signs were stable. Another person had a fracture, while others mostly suffered from soft tissue contusions, which were not serious according to DNF dual-opening tool download.

The buses involved in the accident were the No. 714 bus and the No. 728 minibus, both belonging to Western Public Bus.

Shenzhen Special Zone Daily News (reporter Ma Peigui, intern Chen Zhi) At 1:50 PM yesterday, two buses collided in a rear-end collision while traveling on Hangcheng Avenue in Xiangxiang. The vehicles were severely damaged, and 23 passengers were injured.
