Preface by Fu Bergeng

It is a great honor to write the preface for "Be the Charming Woman You Want to Be." This is a topic of great contemporary significance and one that captures the attention of all women around the world.

Someone once said that half of the Analects of Confucius is enough to govern the world, simply emphasizing its comprehensiveness and profundity. Regrettably, this encyclopedic work only mentions women in one sentence: "Only women and villains are hard to deal with." However, Confucius also said another sentence, "Knowing what you know and knowing what you don't know, that is wisdom." Clearly, this sage chose to retreat from a difficult challenge.

Compared to Confucianism, Taoism seems much more profound. In the Tao Te Ching, it says, "Yin and Yang constitute the Way." It seems to answer the nature of women. Women belong to Yin, invisible and intangible, known but hard to fully understand.

In Chinese philosophy, there is also Buddhism, which does not discuss women but regards the attraction between sexes as a typical phenomenon named "color" (form). Then it explains this form, saying, "Form does not differ from emptiness, emptiness does not differ from form; form itself is emptiness, emptiness itself is form." It says both yes and no, yet neither completely.

It's unclear whether the Chinese people revere or depend on their sages. Anyway, for nearly two thousand years, the topic of women was no longer explored until the early 20th century when China saw the emergence of women's schools. Mr. Sun Yat-sen, inspired by poetic sentiment, left his only poem behind. A poem that highly praises working women: Women are ordinary. When the moon shines and stars are scarce, it's women who ignite a new day with morning meals. Piece by piece, it's women who patch together fragments into beauty. Women are extraordinary. In stormy weather, it's women who open the door for us. Through ups and downs, it's women who give us care and warmth. Yet, women are also great. Humanity often compares mothers to embodiments of beauty and vastness. As humanity gives birth to women, women also give birth to all of humanity. The world cannot do without women. Without women, the world would lose fifty percent of its truth, seventy percent of its goodness, and one hundred percent of its beauty. Without women, there would be no humanity.

Throughout history, human society has been unable to escape its dependence on women, constantly drawing from what it needs while measuring women with the same yardstick used for men, ignoring the differences between men and women and the unique qualities of women. Thus, the sacred and great roles of women have been underestimated, their kind and noble personalities distorted, their beautiful and graceful demeanor criticized, and worse still, women have been treated as wastebaskets and trash bins, leading to the psychological torment and physical abuse of women, even causing women to turn against each other, erasing their own beauty.

Sanyaa and Jiang Hui are two young writers I've watched grow up. From a young age, they had countless thoughts about excellent female characters in Chinese and foreign literary works: If Adam and Eve hadn't eaten forbidden fruit until now, what would the world be like? Why did Nüwa mend the sky? Why did Wu Zetian insist on becoming an empress? Why wasn't Lin Daiyu happy? Why did Nora leave home? Despite being separated by vastly different cultural backgrounds, why did Du Shiniang and Camille end up with similar fates? Why were Li Qingzhao's poems melancholic and sorrowful? Why did Xiang Linshao chatter endlessly...

They have always thought of women as an important part of humanity. They have refuted and interpreted various prejudices and misunderstandings that society and women themselves have about women. Focusing on topics such as women's concepts, mentality, charm, and happiness, they delve deeply and expound profoundly, elevating their personal life experiences to philosophical reflections shared with countless women.

Sanyaa and Jiang Hui take helping women's spiritual growth as their mission. They have given nearly a hundred public lectures across various fields of society. They voluntarily run a hotline for women's mental support, allowing countless strangers to regain their self-awareness, rediscover happiness, and perfect their life journeys. They have also done much work for the development of women's education.

This book is a product of the times, an essence formed through life and intelligence, a beautiful poem written in blood and tears, and a spoken book. The entire book uses dialogues, aphoristic writing, numerous examples, simple and understandable principles, and elegant language appreciated by both the refined and the common, answering the common doubts about life that contemporary women share. It interprets and supplements the proposition of "women being hard to nurture."

"Be the Charming Woman You Want to Be" — this is a book dedicated to humanity and the future.
