Small enterprises play a major role in the civic economy of our country. Statistical data shows that among all the enterprises registered with the Administration for Industry and Commerce nationwide, small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) account for 99%. Their output and profits represent approximately 60% and 40%, respectively. Moreover, SMEs provide 75% of urban employment opportunities; out of last year's total export value of 150 billion US dollars, SMEs accounted for about 60% of the share. In addition, SMEs also play an important role in satisfying people's diverse needs, nurturing entrepreneurs, conducting technological innovation, and participating in specialized cooperation. To a certain extent, SMEs influence the prosperity of regions and even the entire national economy, as well as social stability.

The development of SMEs is an inevitable trend, and creating a good environment for SMEs is an important direction of economic system reform. Since 1998, various policies related to investment and financing, foreign trade policies, and relevant measures to reform the socialized service system to promote the growth of SMEs have been successively introduced by the government. In April 1999, the drafting work for the "Promotion Law of Small and Medium Enterprises" also began, and society has become increasingly concerned with SMEs.

How SMEs can seize this excellent opportunity to obtain the motivation and ability for long-term survival and continuous growth is a serious issue facing SMEs in competition. Of course, the success of SMEs depends on multiple factors within the enterprise, but the talent strategy is at the core of the entire enterprise growth strategy.

### I. Difficulties Faced by SMEs in Attracting Talent

Due to the characteristics of SMEs themselves and various reasons, SMEs face numerous difficulties in attracting talent, and most of these difficulties are determined by the inherent characteristics of SMEs.

1. **Small Scale**: Whether in terms of production scale, number of personnel, asset ownership, or influence, SMEs are smaller than large enterprises. This makes it difficult for most SMEs to offer high salaries and benefits to attract talent. Furthermore, generally speaking, SMEs are less stable than large enterprises. Changes in both internal and external environments significantly impact SMEs more than large enterprises. Therefore, for talent, the risk of developing within an SME is higher than in a large enterprise.

2. **Wide Industry Distribution but Strong Regional Characteristics**: SMEs are distributed across various industries, from handicraft-style processing industries to high-tech industries, including some areas unsuitable for large-scale capital operations. Thus, SMEs have more diverse and complex needs for talent. SMEs often operate within limited scopes, with strong regional characteristics, especially in terms of personnel composition, which can sometimes create an exclusive corporate atmosphere, making it difficult for enterprises to introduce new talent. Some enterprises are located in medium and small cities, towns, or even remote areas, making it hard to attract talent.

3. **High Dependence on Individuals with Significant Impact**: Whether it is the operators or each employee, they play a crucial role in ensuring the stable operation of the enterprise. The continuous normal operation of large enterprises relies on perfect systems, whereas SMEs often depend more on individual strength. In other words, the growth of the enterprise relies more on the initiative of each person. Often, there is no systematic or complete management system, nor a continuous and comprehensive human resource management system, which is not conducive to SMEs introducing talent in a targeted and planned manner.

4. **Lack of Excellent Corporate Culture**: Most SMEs do not pay attention to building corporate culture, and employees lack unified value concepts, resulting in weak identification with the enterprise. This often leads to a misalignment between personal values and the enterprise's philosophy, which is also one of the main reasons why SMEs find it difficult to attract and retain talent.

In other words, SMEs also have their own advantages in attracting talent.
