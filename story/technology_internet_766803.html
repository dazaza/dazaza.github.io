Lighter, Stronger, More Considerate: Summary of Foreign Media Hands-on with Surface 2

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 21:50:19

Microsoft unveiled the new Surface 2 series today in New York. Like the previous generation, the lineup includes the lightweight Surface 2 (powered by an nVidia Tegra 4 chip) and the PC-replacement level Surface Pro 2 (powered by an Intel Haswell chip). Major foreign media outlets (The Verge, CNET, Engadget) have already conducted hands-on trials, so let's take a look at what has changed.

We'll start with the Surface 2.

Design

The front of the Surface 2 still features the black border design, but the sides and back differ from the all-black body of the previous generation. The magnesium alloy body now sports a lighter silver-gray color. Thanks to the use of new materials, the thickness has been reduced from 9.3mm to 8.9mm, and the weight has dropped from 680g to 676g.

This time, the Surface 2 introduces a new two-stage stand design. Microsoft not only adjusted the angle of the stand but also added a second foldable angle, making it more convenient for use on your lap.

The Microsoft logo that used to be on the stand is now replaced with a simple "Surface".

Specifications

The Surface 2 features a 1.7 GHz Tegra 4 quad-core processor, and the screen has been upgraded from 1366 x 768 to full HD 1080P, which is a significant improvement over the previous generation. According to initial impressions from foreign media, the color representation on the Surface 2 screen appears more natural than the previous model.

In addition, the battery life of the Surface 2 has been improved, offering 25% more power than the previous generation, and charging is reportedly 3-4 times faster. During the hands-on tests, multitasking performance was very smooth.

The front and rear cameras are 3.5 MP and 5 MP respectively. According to official introductions, this time the Surface uses a large sensor, resulting in excellent performance during actual testing.

Additionally, it’s reported that the speakers on the Surface 2 have been enhanced, but due to the noisy environment in the hands-on area, further insights are yet to be determined.

In terms of ports, the Surface 2 offers USB 3.0, a microSD card reader, and a video output port.

Type & Touch Cover

The star accessory, Type Cover 2, has also been updated. It is now one-third thinner (only 2.5mm thick) compared to the previous generation. The second-generation Type Cover adds keyboard backlighting, which automatically turns on in low-light environments. Additionally, the 1092 touch sensors built into the keyboard not only enhance typing sensitivity but also support touch gestures. The price is set at $130. Due to the short hands-on time, apart from increased sensitivity, it is difficult to discern the essential changes brought about by these over a thousand sensors.

On the other hand, the Touch Cover 2, which lacks a physical keyboard structure, is even lighter and thinner. It also supports keyboard backlighting, a feature that received consistent praise from journalists. However, some foreign media outlets noted that the backlighting on this keyboard is quite small, making it hard to notice in certain situations. Nevertheless, the sensitivity of the new Touch Cover has been improved, priced at $120.

Other Features

In terms of the system, the Surface 2 will run Windows RT 8.1 specially designed by Microsoft for ARM chips. More importantly, Microsoft is providing the Surface 2 with a free Office 2013 RT suite (including Outlook), greatly enhancing its practicality. Additionally, users purchasing the Surface 2 will receive one year of free international long-distance calls from Skype and 200 GB of SkyDrive cloud storage space for two years.

Pricing starts at $449 for the 32GB model, available for purchase starting October 22nd.
