Opening a library was his childhood dream. "Idle books can be exchanged and shared here; books you don't want can be placed here, and you can also find books you like," said Mr. Xu, who graduated from the Chinese department and has been fond of reading since he was young. He was deeply moved when he happened to see the concept of the "Deserted Island Library" online.

However, at around 4 PM yesterday afternoon, in this shabby library of more than 30 square meters, there were still consumers from Zhangzhou who came for expired journals and magazines. At the same time, an old professor from Xiamen University found an old book here.

Southeast Network - Strait Herald reported on December 28th (reporter Luo Yumin, Chen Yangqin, text/pictures). We all have had dreams, we've all "boasted". When we were young, some planned to open big companies, others wanted to become stars, and some aspired to be politicians...

"These two are regulars at the library," said the owner, Mr. Xu, born in the 80s. According to him, the library has nearly 2000 books available for free borrowing, and over 2000 books for sale.

"In fact, I haven't given up my job," said Mr. Xu, who is still involved in website maintenance work, with income sufficient enough to support his family, so his family didn't oppose him opening this library.

Mr. Xu dreamed of opening a library when he was young. For this reason, he quit his teaching job and moved from Quanzhou to Xiamen, where he opened a "Deserted Island Library" that combines a public library and bookstore, providing free idle books for those who love reading.

Thus, he conceived the idea of opening a public library. Meanwhile, he regularly organizes themed reading activities, attracting many book-loving citizens to exchange reading experiences together.

Mr. Xu was born in 1980, is married, and has a child. He said he used to be an elementary school teacher, teaching Chinese and mathematics. To pursue his dream, he quit his teaching job in his hometown of Quanzhou and came to Xiamen.

Such a "deserted island" being able to survive in Lugu Island itself can uplift spirits and give people hope.

We look forward to more public welfare forces and inclusiveness in the beautiful Lugu Island, allowing more "deserted islands" to thrive.

But what is the reason that made him choose to open a public library while also having to support his family?

Mr. Xu said that all the books in the library were donated by netizens. Their motto is: sharing and exchange. However, they are not offering free borrowing to everyone; only residents near Minzu Road can borrow books for free. Additionally, other readers who wish to borrow books for free must either donate an equivalent number of books or purchase a certain amount of books in-store to obtain the right to borrow books.

Nevertheless, to sustain the continuous operation of the library, Mr. Xu said he also sells second-hand books, postcards, etc., in the library, generating enough income to cover the rent of the store, "the monthly rent here is 800 yuan, too expensive to bear."

This is an unremarkable bookstore hidden at No. 145 Minzu Road. There aren't as many crowded people as on Zhongshan Road, and there aren't many shops on the street. Outside the store, a simple sign hangs: "Deserted Island Library @ Xiamen Xiaoyu Island."

Why name the bookstore "Deserted Island"? "Nowadays, people are too busy, fewer people are reading, and people are reading less and less. People in this city are too busy to have time to read books. Sometimes, books are like abandoned islands, ignored by everyone."

Nearly two thousand books are available for free borrowing.

Discussion・Position

How long can this library stay open? Mr. Xu hasn't thought about it, "As long as it can be kept open, it will continue." Mr. Xu hopes that more people will pay attention to this small bookstore, and more public welfare forces will join in, only then can the small library grow stronger.

For Mr. Xu, this seemingly shabby library is the revival of his ideal, just as described on a poster posted at the entrance of the library: a city, a group of youth, a kind of persistence. In the quiet corner of this city, he and the supporters of the library continue to uphold their love for books.

In fact, we can also try to expand Mr. Xu's ideal. If such pocket-sized public libraries could flourish in communities across Xiamen, families with idle books could bring them to these libraries for exchange and sharing, making Xiamen smell even more of books.

"For life-long struggle for the boasts of youth." When asked to describe his idea of opening a public library in one sentence, Mr. Xu pointed to this sentence most frequently posted in the store. He said that the library was his childhood dream.
