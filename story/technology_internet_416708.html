Overview English name: paintingroom Spray booth is also known as Definition: The spray booth is a device that provides a dedicated environment for painting operations. The artificial environment created in the spray booth can meet the requirements of temperature, humidity, illumination, and cleanliness needed for painting operations; it can protect the safety and health of operators; it can manage waste emissions from painting operations to protect the environment from pollution. Broadly speaking, it ensures that paint particles (mist particles) generated during spraying can be promptly removed from the spraying area to ensure painting quality, protect the environment, and safeguard the physical health of the operators. Painting operations are typically conducted in small rooms with partial drainage facilities, which are called spray booths. Spray booths are generally divided into dry-type, wet-type spray booths, water curtain spray booths, water-wash spray booths, oil-wash spray booths, pumpless spray booths, vortex spray booths, etc. For dry-type processing of paint mist particles, it usually involves steps such as exhaust, filtration of paint mist particles by filters, adsorption of mist particles by baffles, and forced ventilation. Afterward, the baffles need to be cleaned, and the filters need regular replacement, making it unsuitable for large-scale production. Wet-type uses water curtains to absorb or adsorb paint mist particles, allowing the spray booth to be used continuously for long periods. It has high efficiency in purifying paint mist, simple operation and maintenance, flexible and convenient installation, no wastewater discharge, clean and safe operation, and higher advantages. Water curtain spray booths utilize positive and negative atmospheric pressure, installing air intakes above the operator's head. A water curtain wall is installed in front of the spray bench. Behind the water curtain wall, powerful fans extract air, forming an airflow from the top of the head to behind the water curtain wall. Air carries oil and gas ions through the water curtain and filters them into the water.

Design Points:

1. Determine a reasonable configuration form.

2. Determine a reasonable average horizontal air velocity in the spray booth.

3. Match the supply and exhaust systems reasonably to ensure a slight positive pressure inside the spray booth.

4. Ensure uniform water curtain thickness and pay attention to the structure of the upper water tank and water supply pipe distribution.

5. The distance from the bottom of the water curtain board to the water tank liquid surface should be reasonable.

6. The water wash atomization nozzles and pipelines should be easy to dismantle, replace, and clean.

7. The air-water separation and sedimentation should be sufficient and easy to clean.
