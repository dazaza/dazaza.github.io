Ms. Li, who sustained minor injuries, was eventually contacted by the reporter through her family. She told the reporter: "It's a trivial matter, we don't want to delve into it further. The traffic police said Mr. Feng has no responsibility, so that's that."

After receiving the report, the Shunde traffic police first attempted mediation between both parties. However, Mr. Feng hoped for an authoritative investigative decision from the traffic police. Subsequent investigations revealed no collision marks on either vehicle. Based on the scene investigation, the accident was given the judgment code "016". After checking the traffic violation codes, "016" falls under "other actions." According to regulations, if there are specific violations, they must be written out in detail. In Mr. Feng's traffic behavior appraisal report, the traffic police did not specify his exact actions. "In reality, in this small accident, Mr. Feng had no responsibility," said a traffic police officer.

Mr. Feng immediately stopped and helped Ms. Li up, asking if she was okay. Ms. Li managed to call her husband, and ten minutes later, her husband and another man arrived at the scene. "As soon as those two men arrived, the woman claimed I knocked her down!" Mr. Feng recalled incredulously. "They wouldn't let me leave, so I had no choice but to call the police." Subsequently, the traffic police arrived, followed by an ambulance. The traffic police conducted on-site measurements and records, and both vehicles were towed away.

News Report (by reporter Zeng Yi): Last Friday's incident in Lunjiao, Shunde, where a helper was falsely accused, has finally come to light. The Shunde traffic police issued an accident appraisal report yesterday, determining that the helper, Mr. Feng Shuangwen, had no responsibility. With the clarification of responsibility, Mr. Feng's electronic factory decided to issue a commendation notice throughout the entire factory this morning and publicly awarded him 1000 yuan in cash.

Due to the remoteness of the location, Mr. Feng recalled that there were a few motorcycles and bicycles passing by, none of which stopped to help, leaving no eyewitnesses. Ms. Li was sent to a nearby hospital and was found to have only sustained a chin abrasion.

Around 5:15 PM last Friday, just after work, Mr. Feng was riding an electric bike toward Jizhou when he passed the Fei Xi Nongzhuang restaurant. Suddenly, he heard a motorcycle fall behind him. Looking back, he saw a woman (later identified as Ms. Li) lying on the ground after sliding for about 10 meters.
