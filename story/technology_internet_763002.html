The story of price reduction

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 12:07:26

On July 14th, Microsoft adjusted the prices of various 32GB and 64GB versions of the Surface RT on its official website. The price of the 32GB version without a keyboard dropped from $499 when it was launched at the end of October last year to $349, while the price of the 64GB version without a keyboard fell from $599 to $449, representing a price reduction of 30%.

A Microsoft spokesperson stated: Over the past few months, the Surface RT has seen great success in the U.S. and other markets through promotions on price and Touch Cover sales. People who have purchased the Surface RT really love it, and we are excited to share in the joy of our new customers with others.

However, according to IDC data, the shipment volume of Surface devices in the first quarter was approximately 900,000 units, while the total shipment volume of tablets was about 492 million units. It seems that Microsoft's promotional strategy did not significantly boost the sales of the Surface as claimed.

When the news of this price cut spread online, readers' feedback was not very positive either. Many people had numerous questions about the product itself, and the price reduction didn't seem to impress them unless it was lowered to an irresistible price.

Around this time two years ago, HP's TouchPad sales were consistently poor. When the product was discontinued and the price was slashed to $99, the TouchPad became extremely popular. In less than a day before and after the price drop, almost all HP TouchPad tablet computers in stores across the U.S. were sold out. Especially at Best Buy stores, their inventory of TouchPad tablet computers was sold out within just 15 minutes.

In the view of Brian, a reporter for NYT Bits, when a new electronic product undergoes a significant price reduction, it might be good news for consumers, but for manufacturers, it often signals poor sales. For example, Nokia's Lumia series and HTC First, despite manufacturers generally being reluctant to publicly admit it.

At around the same time as the Surface RT, BlackBerry also reduced the price of its Z10, which was officially launched in February this year. A BlackBerry spokesperson explained this by saying: Like many other smartphone manufacturers, we adjust based on newly launched products. We recently introduced our new flagship Q10, so now is the right time to adjust the price of the Z10.

For this secret that no one wants to talk about, once it's revealed, it seems to have absolutely no humor at all.
