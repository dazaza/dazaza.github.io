Lovers separated by distance

by rocksky88 on 2008-07-14 08:50:57

Every morning, her boyfriend would appear punctually downstairs, giving her a steaming hot egg pancake; on every sunny weekend, they could hold hands and go shopping together, carefully picking out clothes or gifts for each other; every evening, they would bid each other a reluctant farewell in front of the dormitory building, with the boyfriend straightening her collar and wrapping her scarf properly for her; they would have meals together, study together, and kiss on secluded little paths where no one else was around; they could feel each other's hearts and breaths, and without any words, just express their love through eye contact. They could cry, make a fuss, act spoiled without restraint, and when the other party was upset and not answering the phone, wait downstairs holding roses and gifts. There were really so many things they could do, so many that they hadn't even discovered themselves. And some couples couldn't...

There are such people whose love is separated by thousands of miles, enduring pain that others have never tasted, because they know that in that distant city, there is the happiness they desire.

They can't accompany each other while shopping, so they can only send gifts repeatedly;

When the other party is sad, they can only offer comfort through pale text messages;

When the other party is helpless, they can only hold the phone tightly and try to listen to their breathing and crying voices.

When they need warmth, maybe all they can do is flip through past text messages, curl up at night, hug themselves, and tell themselves, we are together;

When sick, they can only lie alone in the dormitory or go to the hospital alone to buy medicine and get an IV drip;

When they argue, both parties always get hurt, unable to explain, unable to let go. They are always alone, or in groups.

Seeing other lovers paired up, they feel a wave of sadness, and at most can only send a text message, telling the other party, I miss you...

But this damned love! There are still so many people who rush towards it like moths to a flame! They firmly believe that time and distance are not issues, they can be loyal! Wang Fei sang, "Loving to the point of being like a moth drawn to fire, is a kind of enjoyment." Though it's not necessarily enjoyable, they are willing!

They will constantly worry if the other party has eaten enough, if their health is good, if they are happy.

Every evening, they check the weather of that distant city, and early in the morning, they inform the other party that the temperature will drop, reminding them to wear more clothes.

Upon seeing something interesting, they share it with the other party immediately.

When they are upset, they only vent on forums.

The most romantic thing for them is meeting once a month, or even longer!

The joy of meeting and the pain of parting, the stations in both places witness their feelings, witnessing their joys and sorrows.

Each meeting elevates their feelings, increasing their love for each other. Those short two days are beautiful, allowing them to savor the experience until the next meeting!

"Distance is a test paper, measuring the vows of love, whether they will finally come true. We are still learning the language of communication for love, learning to understand and not cry. When we learn to fly, flying over the nights and tests, our days will graduate from loneliness, and we exchange a little more hardship for a little more happiness."
