The personal blog is dead.

by jedor on 2008-10-22 09:28:08

With the addition of fresh services like Flickr, Facebook, and Twitter, blogging has long ceased to be a very creative idea, and it no longer represents progress. Compared with services that advocate sharing such as Facebook, Twitter, and Flickr, personal blogs are indeed on the brink of extinction.

In terms of article depth, personal blogs can no longer match the NEW YORK TIMES which is involved in entertainment-oriented writing. In themes that clearly and thoroughly express emotions, personal blogs can no longer compete with platforms like Twitter and Facebook. As for video and audio content, there's no need to mention that they can't rival YouTube... Those who are still writing personal blogs are more like "living fossils" from 2004 compared to people using Facebook, YouTube, and Twitter.
