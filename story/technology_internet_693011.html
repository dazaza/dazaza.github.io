Shenzhen Special Zone Daily (reporter Yu Qin) -- Shenzhen has recently uncovered the most notorious "blue-plated" car with an astonishing 254 traffic violations! According to information obtained from the Port Detachment of the Traffic Police Bureau under the Municipal Public Security Bureau, during the recent "Thunderbolt No. 5" special rectification campaign, a vehicle with 254 traffic violation records engaged in illegal operations was apprehended, making it currently the vehicle with the most violations, and thus being called the most "notorious" blue-plated car.

According to Deputy Squadron Leader Wang Le of the Port Detachment, around 7:30 PM on March 1st, while the joint law enforcement team was enforcing the law on the first floor of the Transportation Building, they noticed a suspicious white Xiaoli car with the license plate number粤BM3800. "There are often water guests and illegally operating vehicles here. For empty cars entering and fully loaded ones leaving, we will conduct investigations," Wang Le stated. Based on the information at hand, they tracked this vehicle and subsequently separated the driver and passengers for investigation and evidence collection.

Upon investigation, it was found that the Xiaoli car had four people onboard, and the passengers admitted to riding in an illegally operating blue-plated car. The driver also confessed to engaging in illegal operations. When the police checked the vehicle's information via their mobile police system, they discovered that the car had 254 unprocessed traffic violations, with the earliest violation dating back to April 29, 2008, after which the car had not undergone its annual inspection.

According to the officers of the Port Detachment, after verification, the blue-plated car with 254 violations must pay a total fine and penalty fee of 95,500 yuan, and the car has already been impounded by the detachment.

From February 1st to the present, the Port Detachment has confiscated a total of 27 suspected illegally operating blue-plated cars, 50 passenger-carrying electric bikes, and controlled 8 disabled persons' tricycles. More than 60 vehicles suspected of traffic violations have been added to the "blacklist database."
