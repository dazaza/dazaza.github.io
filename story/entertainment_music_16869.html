Wynners 33 Good Times Concert 2007

by pndongdong on 2007-04-19 16:34:28

The "Wynners 33 Good Times Concert" consisting of 13 consecutive shows was held at the Hong Kong Coliseum in Kowloon from February 18 to March 2, 2007, during the Lunar New Year period. The Wynner Five burst onto the stage with a high-energy 20-minute performance of popular songs and electrified the entire audience. Their new album includes 17 love songs, making it a must-have for fans.

In the 1970s, the Hong Kong male band, the Wynner Five, which comprises Roman Tam, Kenny Bee, Tony Ho, Bill Chan, and Yau Ching-Wai, rose to fame across half of Asia. At their concert, they reprised the nostalgic theme by bringing back the most popular Wynner outfits from the 1970s and 80s for the audience to enjoy.

The double CD of "Wynners 33 Good Times Concert 2007" features love songs such as "Accompany Her," "Feelings in the Melody," "This Song," "Can't Avoid It," and "Every Day is a Good Day." The Wynners gave an excellent performance of these songs at the concert. During the concert, the Wynners re-arranged their past songs. In the "Thousand Years Unchanged - Everyone's Happiness" segment, they used a musical theater format to transport the audience back to the 1970s, sharing the story of how their friendship was built. In the segment showcasing individual talents, Yau Ching-Wai performed solo on the drums, while Tony Ho dressed in Hip-Hop style sang a rap song, delivering a spectacular performance. Kenny Bee played his solo saxophone, then sang "Today I Am Very Lonely," bringing the concert to another climax. The Wynners specially created a new main song, "33 Revolutions" (lyrics by Chris Shum, arrangement by Tsui Chun-Tung), to express each member's feelings towards the Wynners' 33-year journey. www.laiting.net
