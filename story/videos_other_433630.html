Centipedes, also known as Tianlong or hundred-foot worms, are warm in nature, pungent in taste, and toxic. They have the functions of calming wind and relieving pain, attacking toxins and dispersing nodules, stopping spasms and relieving pain, and fighting cancer. They are mainly used to treat hemiplegia, tetanus, bone tuberculosis, scrofula, snake bites, infantile convulsions, apoplexy, etc. Modern research shows that centipedes mainly contain histamine, hemolytic protein, various amino acids, fatty amines, and cholesterol. In addition, they also contain orange pigments and pale blue-green pigments, etc. Centipede toxins have a primary hemolytic effect on the central nervous system of rats, with nicotine-like blocking acetylcholine properties, which can cause necrosis and disappearance of spermatogonial cells in mice, indicating an inhibitory effect on tumor cells. In recent years, it has shown good efficacy in treating digestive system tumors and cancers. Centipedes are also the main ingredients in traditional Chinese medicine preparations such as snake venom tablets, pus-removing ointment, and wind-dispelling and blood-circulating pills. It can be seen that centipedes are indispensable among animal-based medicines, and their medicinal value is highly regarded by medical experts at home and abroad.

Adult centipedes bred by this project are about 12 cm long and 0.5-1.1 cm wide, slightly flattened dorsally and ventrally. Their head plates are nearly round, with the front end protruding. The head plate and the first dorsal plate are golden yellow, hence they are also called golden-headed centipedes. They are distributed throughout southern and northern China, with wild populations mainly found in the middle and lower reaches of the Yangtze River region.

Centipedes are omnivorous animals, feeding on various insects, animal viscera, vegetables, fruits, and kitchen waste.
