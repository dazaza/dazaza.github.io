Xunlei CEO Zou Shenglong: The network video market has great benefits.

by mnqyua924 on 2009-12-08 22:05:16

Related thematic articles: Xunlei CEO Zou Shenglong and the host, Xunlei CEO Zou Shenglong. Sina Technology News reported on the afternoon of November 3 that the 2009 Internet Conference was held in Beijing from November 2 to 3. As the official designated chief partner, Sina.com provided full live coverage of the conference. In an exclusive interview with Sina Technology, Zou Shenglong, CEO of Xunlei, said that the Internet video market has great benefits, and Xunlei does a good job in this area, which has aroused jealousy among some people.

Zou Shenglong said that as a party to the verbal spat, I definitely think this is a very negative thing, because the instigator is a well-known Chinese Internet media company. It made a big fuss and caused trouble at Xunlei's headquarters in Shenzhen, which is not only inappropriate for its identity but also makes us very angry. This is our reaction as a party involved.

Zou Shenglong stated that Xunlei was dragged into this verbal war. Xunlei subjectively has no intention of creating more verbal disputes and has no such intention. Of course, if the instigator continues to criticize, we will still respond.

The following is the transcript of the exclusive interview:

Sina Technology: Hello, netizens of Sina. We are now at the site of the 2009 China Internet Conference. We are very happy to have Mr. Zou Shenglong, CEO of Xunlei Company, here to talk about the recent situation of Xunlei. Mr. Zou, please say hello to the netizens!

Zou Shenglong: Good afternoon, netizens of Sina.

Sina Technology: Xunlei has taken many actions in product strategy this year. The first question is, could you please introduce us to some new products that have been launched recently?

Zou Shenglong: There have been some changes in the products recently. Mainly on the Xunlei client, Xunlei has started to try out value-added services around downloading. We have created a plug-in platform.

Sina Technology: Xunlei KanKan is a video playback platform, and it has received particularly high attention recently. In the video field, it is a force that cannot be ignored. We would like to hear your views on the current state of the Chinese Internet video industry.

Zou Shenglong: Internet video may be the next wave of growth hotspots in the Internet, and it should be a strategic high point that every Internet company pays close attention to. Of course, we are different because doing downloads and doing videos actually do similar things. We are moving data. The difference is that the downloaded data is unordered, while stream data is ordered. Video data is stream data, which follows order. However, when facing users, it becomes a download tool, changing its face to become the face of a video-on-demand portal. The business model hasn't changed much either; when we do downloads, we do advertising, and when we do videos, we still do advertising.

Sina Technology: The development of the Internet in China so far has seen many verbal wars in the industry. We believe that many people in this industry have firsthand experience, whether in the antivirus field, SNS field, or video field. How does Mr. Zou view this characteristic of the Internet? Do you think verbal wars are a driving force or an obstacle to the industry?

Zou Shenglong: Verbal wars are definitely due to some underlying reasons. The old saying goes, "There is no profit without early rising," meaning there must be some underlying reasons that give both parties reasons to engage in verbal disputes. Verbal wars are a necessary phase when contradictions arise in any industry, and they are a phenomenon rather than a cause-effect relationship with the results. We should try to look at this phenomenon from a positive angle. Objectively speaking, verbal wars indicate the entire society's attention to it. Under the more focused attention of society, it is definitely not a bad thing for the industry. Many opportunities and problems in the industry will be more objectively understood under this magnifying glass. Therefore, this is definitely a good thing. Of course, for the two parties involved in the verbal war, it may not necessarily be a good thing because it consumes a lot of energy and affects the mood of others while working, which is definitely not a good thing.

Sina Technology: I know that Xunlei has recently been involved in a verbal war in the video field. How do you view this matter? Is it a driving force or not a very scientific factor for the development of the video industry in China?

Zou Shenglong: As a party involved, my perspective may not be neutral. As a party involved, I definitely think this is a very negative thing because, after all, the instigator is an influential Internet media company in China. It made such a big fuss and caused trouble at Xunlei's headquarters in Shenzhen, which is not only inappropriate for its identity but also makes us very angry. This is our reaction as a party involved. Of course, when stepping back from the role of the party involved and looking at the industry, we objectively see a fact: why did such an event happen? Because indeed, the Internet video market has great benefits, and the pie is very large. Also, because Xunlei KanKan does a good job in this area, it has aroused jealousy among some people, leading to such reactions.

When we look at it this way, our mindset becomes more peaceful, indicating that our efforts in the right direction have been recognized by the industry.

Sina Technology: You just mentioned the advantages and disadvantages of verbal wars. Since Xunlei is a platform-based Internet company and has ventured into many fields, it is often involved or dragged into verbal wars. Does the verbal war affect Xunlei's advertising strategy and advertising revenue?

Zou Shenglong: Verbal wars always have positive and negative aspects, and there will definitely be some impact. However, it is difficult to determine the overall effect at present. It might make more advertisers realize that Xunlei is doing a great job, or it might cause some advertisers to worry during this process. Both possibilities exist. However, when we step back from these two companies and look at the industry, we see the development of the industry. Verbal wars themselves are milestones in the development of the industry, representing the existence of huge commercial interests in the industry. The reason it is chaotic is because there are profits in the industry, and the competition for industry profits leads to verbal wars. Specifically, when looking at the industry, we say that the industry is doing a lot of positive and negative public relations around the issue of copyright. But what we actually see happening in the industry is that in the past two years, the top three companies in the industry have done two specific things. First, they spent hundreds of millions of RMB to purchase a lot of content. Taking Xunlei KanKan as an example, movies like "If You Are the One" at the beginning of this year and "The Message" and "The Founding of a Republic" now are all legally purchased films, which is a contribution to the industry. Second, these companies, especially since entering 2009, whether it is Youku or Xunlei, have achieved annual revenues in the hundreds of millions scale. That is, real GDP has appeared in this细分market. Only by earning money here can you take out part of the money earned and distribute it to the upstream content providers, allowing them to produce better content and enter a virtuous cycle. These are two facts that cannot be ignored, and they deserve our very positive view.

Sina Technology: When will this verbal war end?

Zou Shenglong: It's hard to say, because it depends on whether both sides have achieved their respective goals. I said that Xunlei was dragged into this verbal war, and Xunlei subjectively has no intention of creating more verbal disputes and has no such intention. Of course, if the instigator continues to criticize, we will still respond.

Sina Technology: Mr. Zou, you just mentioned that Xunlei KanKan's revenue in the video field has reached hundreds of millions this year. How is Xunlei's overall profitability this year?

Zou Shenglong: Specific figures cannot be disclosed, but Xunlei's profit scale is in the hundreds of millions, and Xunlei's main income still comes from advertising.

Sina Technology: When did Xunlei start making profits?

Zou Shenglong: Xunlei started making profits in 2006.

Sina Technology: Can you share with us your plans for next year?

Zou Shenglong: Next year's plan is to make the video better and the download better.

Sina Technology: Due to time constraints, we will stop here today. Thank you very much, Mr. Zou, for coming to our interview room, and thank you to the netizens for participating. Thank you, Mr. Zou.

Zou Shenglong: Thank you, Sina, and thank you to the netizens of Sina. Thank you!

(Interview ends)
