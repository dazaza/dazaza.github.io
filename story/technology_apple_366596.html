Will Tim Cook join HP?

by diglog on 2010-09-29 21:01:27

On Tuesday morning this week, rumors that "Apple's Chief Operating Officer Tim Cook might resign and become the CEO of HP" caused Apple's stock price to instantly drop by 20 dollars. It recovered a bit afterward, but Apple's stock still lost about 1.48%. If calculated based on Apple's current market value, Tim Cook's worth in Apple could be close to 3.8 billion US dollars.

The rumors seem not entirely baseless. Ever since Mark Hurd suddenly left his position as HP's CEO and joined Oracle, HP has been actively searching for a suitable new CEO. And from all aspects, Tim Cook appears to be an ideal candidate.

If you don't know much about Tim Cook, you can refer to Mu Yao’s very detailed translation: "Who is Tim Cook?"

Tim Cook was born in Alabama in 1960, making him exactly 50 years old this year. In college, he majored in industrial engineering and later obtained his MBA from Duke University. He previously served as the Chief Operating Officer at a computer retailer called Intelligent Electronics, then joined IBM as the General Manager of North American computer sales, where he worked for 12 years. In 1997, Cook joined Compaq as the Senior Vice President of Corporate Materials, responsible for inventory management of all products produced by Compaq. However, it didn’t last long—only six months.

In 1997, when Jobs returned to Apple, he recruited Tim Cook from Compaq early in 1998 to reorganize Apple's then chaotic operations. Prior to 2007, Tim Cook was in charge of global sales management of Apple products and the Macintosh department. After 2007, Cook began serving as Apple's Chief Operating Officer (COO), responsible for the daily operations of all of Apple's businesses.

Cook has always been very low-profile within Apple and is renowned for his long hours; he remains single to this day. Most of his private time is spent on physical exercise. He likes football teams, cycling, and running, and he is also a member of Nike's board.

From 1998 until now, Cook has worked at Apple for 12 years, which are also the 12 years during which Apple transformed from the brink of bankruptcy into the world's largest technology company. One could say that in this most dramatic and brilliant growth in tech history, Cook's contributions have been indispensable.
