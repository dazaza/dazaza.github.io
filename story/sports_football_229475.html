Introduction to the plot of "Two Wives":

"Two Wives" tells the story of a man who loses his memory in a traffic accident and the series of events that unfold between his ex-wife and current fiancée. Sun Tae-young plays Han Ji-sook, an unmarried mother who independently runs a high-end wine shop and flower shop. Ji-sook, whose personality lies between calmness and passion, fell in love with her boyfriend while studying in the U.S. and had a child. However, she returned to Korea alone with her child without marrying her boyfriend. Despite introductions from her family to many men, all of them demanded a large divorce settlement if they were to divorce in the future. Already disillusioned with men and deciding to live alone, Ji-sook meets Kang Cheol-soo (Kim Ho-jun) and falls in love with this romantic writer who is indifferent to money. Cheol-soo, a full-time writer, is a poor writer without even a representative work, earning money only by working part-time as a lecturer at a university and writing essays. The financial power naturally rests with his wife, Yoon Young-hee. After being married for ten years and feeling that his wife only cares about money, Cheol-soo gradually distances himself from her. Participating in an online "borrow a husband" activity for novel research, Cheol-soo meets unmarried mother Ji-sook and develops feelings for her. After divorcing his wife, Cheol-soo begins a new romance with Ji-sook and plans to marry her. Unfortunately, Cheol-soo encounters a traffic accident before the wedding, suffers serious injuries, and after being comatose for a month, wakes up with amnesia. The amnesiac Cheol-soo only remembers his ex-wife Yoon Young-hee (Kim Ji-young), but forgets his current girlfriend Han Ji-sook. The sudden car accident puts the three in an awkward and complex situation. Yoon Young-hee's immature younger brother Yoon Nam-jun (Andy) is an unemployed loafer who looks good on the outside but is just a troublemaker. He will have a romance with Zhao Meimei played by Lee Yu-jung.
