Sohu Sports CSL Round 25 Best XI: Chen Tao Overcomes Injury, Mao Jianqing Breaks Free Article by Tears of the Canopy Email: cangqiongzhilei@gmail.com QQ:445910068 After China's national team was held to a 0-0 draw by Singapore, Chinese football plunged into an unprecedented low, with criticism and ridicule dominating public discourse. The resumption of CSL action was like a child whose father had made a mistake, too afraid to step out of the house, as it quietly and uneventfully concluded Round 25. Although there were not few goals in this round, they failed to excite. Perhaps it was due to the lack of high-intensity matches that these goals did not leave a strong impression on fans. Notably, Liaoning Football Team finally secured sponsorship from Huludao Port but lost in the Liaoning Derby; after Lu Bofei's violent attack incident, Shenzhen Football Team triumphed in a goal-fest, showing that fortune is unpredictable indeed. Below is Sohu Sports' selected best XI for CSL Round 25:

Best XI: 3-4-3

Goalkeeper: Chen Dong (Dalian Shide)

Defenders: Sun Feng (Wuhan Guanggu), Li Weifeng (Shanghai Shenhua), Caballero (Changchun Yatai)

Midfielders: Mao Jianqing (Shanghai Shenhua), Zheng Bin (Wuhan Guanggu), Chen Tao (Shenyang Jinde), Shen Longyuan (Shanghai Shenhua)

Forwards: Wensheng (Xian International), Cao Tianbao (Changchun Yatai), Joachim (Xiamen Blue Lion)

Best Coach: Xie Feng (Shenzhen Jinwei)

Goalkeeper: Chen Dong - The former glory of "Eight Stars Dalian" has long faded, and this away match against Tianjin Teda even risked defeat. Facing Tianjin's overwhelming offensive pressure, Chen Dong fearlessly made numerous saves. His intentional time-wasting and verbal sparring with opponents highlighted the experience of a seasoned goalkeeper. There are rumors that senior management at Shide does not value Chen Dong much, and there may be some rifts and misunderstandings. Through his experienced and brave performance in this game, one wonders if Chen Dong can regain the trust and respect he once enjoyed.

Defender: Sun Feng - Who would have thought that trailing by two goals, the Wuhan team would stage a stunning comeback? Sun Feng fired the first shot in the counterattack. Despite being assigned many defensive tasks, particularly to prevent Changchun Yatai midfielders from breaking through, Sun Feng did not strictly adhere to pre-game tactical arrangements. He advanced multiple times after regaining possession, threatening the opponent's goal. At 42 minutes, Zheng Bin crossed from the baseline, and Sun Feng immediately scored a crucial goal with a powerful volley.

Li Weifeng - Li Weifeng's impetuous "character flaw" seems unchangeable, as he appeared overly aggressive and fierce in this game. However, domestic experts often misinterpret the evaluation criteria for defenders. A typical example is Zhang Lu's opinion that Italian captain Cannavaro was too reckless, unaware that man-marking center-backs are not the last line of defense sweepers; they have the capital to be more alert and courageous. In fact, if they were too steady, it would indicate problems. Therefore, Li Weifeng, who repeatedly used his physical advantage to confront Beijing Guoan's attackers, should still be commendable from a man-marking perspective.

Caballero - The Honduran foreigner is hailed by local media as a stabilizing force. His judgment of ball trajectories and precise clearances truly helped his teammates overcome psychological panic. It's rumored that scouts from Japan's J-League have taken notice of Caballero's superior defensive abilities, leaving doubts about whether the Black Pearl will remain in Changchun's lineup next season. In the match against Wuhan, Caballero demonstrated great composure, repeatedly clearing dangerous 45-degree crosses out of the penalty area and scoring a header from a corner kick.

Midfielders: Mao Jianqing - After the BMW car-smashing incident, the main suspect Mao Jianqing was internally cold-treated by Shanghai Shenhua and hadn't undergone systematic training for months. The image of the brave young player gradually faded from people's memories. In the Beijing-Shanghai clash, Wu Jinguai naturally sought unconventional strategies to win. Knowing each other's strengths and weaknesses well, neither side could make any surprising moves. Thus, Shenhua surprisingly started Mao Jianqing as a key player, which greatly surprised Beijing Guoan. In actual play, Mao Jianqing's repeated breakthroughs almost collapsed Beijing's flank defense. If not for Yang Zhi's Banks-like saves, Mao Jianqing might have scored a goal.

Zheng Bin - After the Singapore game where Zhu Guanghu heavily relied on Zheng Bin, the media widely questioned his soft style and lack of distinctive features, placing significant psychological pressure on him. As the leader of Wuhan, Zheng Bin suddenly erupted when trailing by two goals, not only scoring himself but also orchestrating multiple attacks, assisting Sun Feng and Zhang Xinxin to score. His performance was impeccable. When fully utilizing his "small, fast, and agile" characteristics, the technically skilled Zheng Bin becomes a terrifying "midfield schemer." Now, what he needs to improve is psychological control in critical matches.

Chen Tao - A country cannot go a day without its ruler. As a symbolic figure of Shenyang Jinde, every move of Chen Tao captures the hearts of fans. Facing the Liaoning derby and an unclear relegation situation, despite his knee injury not fully healed, Chen Tao voluntarily requested to play under coach Kuffmann, greatly boosting the morale of Jinde players. It was his "world-class" strike from 30 meters out that directly hit the bottom corner of Liaoning's goal, ending Jinde's six-year embarrassment of never beating Liaoning in league matches. Judging from his versatile performance on the field, Chen Tao wisely avoided exerting force on his knees, preventing further injury. Regrettably, due to poor physical condition, he missed the opportunity to trial with Wang Daye at Inter Milan.

Shen Longyuan - The Beijing-Shanghai showdown, crucial for determining the CSL runner-up, captured everyone's attention. Both teams stationed heavy forces in the midfield, unwilling to let their guard down for a moment. Time passed second by second as both sides consumed each other. In such situations, the performance of wingers becomes extremely important. Young Shenhua player Shen Longyuan continuously initiated attacks by crossing balls from the flanks and half-spaces, creating chaos in Beijing's goalmouth. At 53 minutes, he capitalized on a series of exquisite combinations in front of the goal, finding space to score.

Forwards: Wensheng - The news of International moving from Xi'an next season has been wildly hyped, with various false rumors negatively affecting the team's communication with some fans. Under such adverse conditions, the International team urgently needed a convincing victory to reward their home fans and demonstrate their sincerity. In fact, International played very hard, but unfortunately, the deadlock lasted until the 80th minute, causing all players to lose patience. Right at his 27th birthday, Wensheng stepped up with a powerful shot, celebrating his special day with all fans.

Cao Tianbao - Recently, the most prominent player in Yatai has undoubtedly been Du Zhenyu. After the national team was held to a draw by minnow Singapore, Du Zhenyu, who came off the bench, might be the only player not criticized. As two famous attackers from Changchun, Cao Tianbao has been overshadowed by Du Zhenyu's brilliance. To be fair, Cao Tianbao's overall strength is not much inferior to his teammate, especially in terms of goal-scoring instincts, reminiscent of "Inzaghi's legacy." After Wuhan's intense home-field assault, Cao Tianbao's counter-attack goal in the 28th minute brought hope for victory.

Joachim - Highly regarded by Gao Hongbo, this "Black Peak Killer" showcased exceptional explosive power and dribbling skills right from the start of his transfer. Official technical statistics show he always receives the most fouls whenever he plays. The only downside is that Joachim's chemistry with the whole team needs improvement, and he tends to rush shots with heavy strikes. In this game, the Black Pearl learned from past mistakes of playing solo, actively seeking cooperation with teammates. Although Blue Lion lost, Joachim's two goals and their vibrant attacking performance still count as significant achievements.

Best Coach: Xie Feng - Having gone through events like Lu Bofei's attack and Wang Baoshan's dismissal, Shenzhen FC was in turmoil. Xie Feng, appointed as the emergency manager, took over the struggling team. On his first day, he emphasized team discipline, leading to noticeable improvements in team spirit after a serious rectification campaign. Nowadays, young coaches taking charge has become a trend in world football, with examples like Basten, Dunga, and Donadoni. Xie Feng, once a renowned fast striker in domestic football, exudes youthful confidence. In the game against Blue Lion, he aggressively pursued an attacking style, with three goals erasing Shenzhen's recent "goal drought."
