Facebook to Restrict Ad Targeting Amid Teen User Exodus

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 19:11:25

On November 2, Beijing time, according to foreign media reports, given the loss of teenage users, social networking giant Facebook will limit the number of ads that appear in the information feed.

However, some analysts issued reports saying that Facebook's profit in the third quarter would improve, and they might increase ad prices. Plus, due to the favorable factor that Facebook's mobile advertising revenue exceeded analysts' expectations in the third quarter, Facebook's stock price rose by 2.44% on Thursday. Since the beginning of the year, the stock of this social network has cumulatively risen by 89%.

Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg has been committed to developing new features so that advertisers can more easily reach their consumers. But Zuckerberg has also been looking for a balance between user experience and advertising marketing.

Analyst Brian Wieser of investment company Pivotal Research Group said: "This is a warning line. Facebook wants to manage the user experience, but they don't want users to be overwhelmed by ads when using Facebook." Wieser gave Facebook's stock a "hold" rating.

Facebook said that the revenue of its latest financial quarter soared by 60%, reaching $2.02 billion, exceeding the previous analysts' average expectation of $1.91 billion. Mobile advertising revenue contributed 49% of Facebook's total advertising revenue in the third quarter, higher than 41% in the previous quarter, also exceeding the general expectation of analysts at 45.6%.

Loss of teenage users

In yesterday's conference call, Facebook CFO David Ebersman said that the company would not significantly increase the number of ads appearing in the information feed.

Ebersman said in the conference call: "Compared to the second quarter, the overall teenage users in the U.S. remained stable in the third quarter. But we did find a decline in the number of daily visits by teenage users."

Analyst Ben Schachter of Macquarie Securities USA said: "The precision of ads on Facebook is stronger. At such a stage, it is indeed surprising to see a company still accelerating its revenue growth."

Facebook said that excluding equity reward expenses, Facebook's earnings per share in the third quarter was $0.25, higher than the market analysts' average expectation of $0.19.

Mobile strategy

Analyst Heather Bellini of investment bank Goldman Sachs said in her report that Facebook management expects to continue raising ad prices.

Facebook's net profit in the third quarter reached $425 million, or $0.17 per share. The company continued to increase investment in equipment, employees, and services in this quarter. Facebook had a net loss of $59 million, or $0.02 per share, in the same period last year.

In the conference call, Ebersman said: "The number of Facebook information feed ads (including desktop and mobile) in the third quarter was slightly higher than in the second quarter, and this is just one of the three main ways Facebook increases revenue, but not the least important way." Ebersman said that the most important way to increase revenue is to expand the user base, followed by demand growth, which can increase ad prices.

Reaping rewards

Facebook's total number of users worldwide reached 1.19 billion in the third quarter, up from 1.15 billion in the second quarter. Facebook's mobile user count was 874 million in the third quarter, up 6.7% year-on-year.

Ebersman said: "Facebook's investment in the mobile field is paying off. The mobile business is very important to us because users are spending more and more time on mobile internet."

In fact, Facebook only started to focus on mobile services at the beginning of 2012, and since then the company has continuously improved the mobile user experience. In early October, Facebook announced that it would expand mobile advertising services, allowing advertisers to do precise ad marketing based on users' online activities outside of Facebook. Additionally, Facebook's photo-sharing website Instagram is also planning to launch advertising services.

Bloomberg Industries analyst Paul Sweeney said: "For Facebook, the good news is that more than 70% of this social site's users are mobile users, but only 49% of revenue comes from mobile, meaning Facebook's mobile advertising business holds growth potential." (kirk)
