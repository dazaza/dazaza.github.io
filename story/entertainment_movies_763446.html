Synopsis:

The New Year film "Happiness Forever" produced by Jack Neo has engaged local newcomer director Tan Chui Mui, who had previously directed "S11", to take the helm. Tan Chui Mui co-directed with Zhang Jia Ren. The attention is on whether Tan Chui Mui can smoothly achieve a box office of 3 million SGD under the guidance of producer Jack Neo during the festive season.

The script for "Happiness Forever" took one and a half years to create, written in English and then translated into Chinese by the production team. Tan Chui Mui's previous collaboration with Zhang Jia Ren on "S11" saw a rushed release and withdrawal, grossing only 2229 SGD. Besides appreciating Director Tan's rich experience in advertising, Jack Neo also sees his potential in "commercial" prospects, as S11 equates to S+11=$.

Additionally, the cost of "Happiness Forever" was 2 million Malaysian Ringgit (approximately 840,000 SGD). In recent years, Jack Neo's films have all had budgets exceeding 1 million SGD. This time, "Happiness Forever" broke the norm, indicating an ambition to achieve great returns with minimal investment.

"Happiness Forever" is a romantic comedy about searching for love. Its story is heartwarming and mainly explores relationships across three generations: a married couple in their 40s for many years, another couple in their 30s, and a young pair.

Chen Mucheng (played by Chris Ng), 52 years old, is stern and conservative, a typical middle-aged Chinese man. Due to his wife Jiayi (played by Yeo Yann Yann) being preoccupied with household chores, there is a lack of communication between them, leading to frequent quarrels over trivial daily matters, causing Mucheng much distress. A chance encounter leads Mucheng to develop the intention to "stray"... But he soon discovers that Xuelian is not the dream lover for men, but rather a "nightmare"...

Jeremy Jieqi (played by Lin Derong), Mucheng's "adopted younger brother," 36 years old, considers himself a ladies' man, like a bird without feet, fluttering among different women every day. Once, he impersonated Mucheng to attend the "Parents' Day" for Benny, meeting the elegant teacher Huang Meishi (played by Li Xinyi), and starting a pursuit action. Just as he was about to win her over, trouble ensues!

Benny Chen Benny (played by Liang Ming Hao), 17 years old, is a high school student, seemingly at the age where curiosity and fantasy about love between men and women are budding. Benny becomes a target of teasing by classmates when a video of him dancing sensually with his crush Jennifer (played by Ai Wen) goes viral online, making him the male lead of the video.

The story uses Chen Mucheng, Jeremy Jieqi, and Mucheng's son Benny Chen Benny to represent males of three different age groups. Through their individual explorations of romance, it digs up some thought-provoking correct notions while naturally leading to a series of jokes. Anyone living in this Eastern society, which is both conservative and repressive, will certainly understand, empathize, and resonate with the laughter and tears depicted in the story!
