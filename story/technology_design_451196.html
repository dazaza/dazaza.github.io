Video of Japanese Student Stabbing Her Mother is Exposed, Bystanders Watch as a Foreigner Steps in to Help

by zzfdhdb on 2011-06-24 09:30:23

A foreigner passing by came to rescue. Image source: Xinmin Network

Oriental Net, April 12 News: According to the report of "Shanghai Morning Post", the case of a Japanese student stabbing his mother has caused a great stir in society after being disclosed by the media. Yesterday, a netizen provided the video taken at that time to the Morning Post. In the video, the perpetrator had left, leaving only the mother lying on the ground and a foreigner who was trying to save her.

Meanwhile, Shanghai police also confirmed yesterday that the judicial appraisal procedure for the mental status of Wang, a Japanese student who stabbed his mother nine times, has been officially launched.

This video, filmed around 9 pm on March 31, is more than 40 seconds long. In it, you can see a middle-aged woman lying on the ground with her legs curled up, and there is a faint pool of blood on the ground. A foreigner squats on the ground, pressing his left hand against her abdomen while holding a white towel-like cloth in his right hand, supporting her head. The rescuing foreigner wears a red casual jacket and carries a backpack. Around the 43rd second of the video, the woman's left leg, which was curled up, suddenly straightens, and her right arm, which was originally protecting her body, becomes limp and hangs on the ground.

According to the netizen who filmed this video, he shot it with his mobile phone in the passage between Terminal 1 and Terminal 2 of Pudong Airport on the evening of March 31 when he just happened to pass by. He heard from the onlookers that the woman lying on the ground was stabbed by a young man who ran away after the crime: "No one thought the perpetrator would be her son at the time."

In this video, passengers come and go from time to time, and quite a few people gather around, but no one steps forward to help.

The unnamed netizen said that he heard the foreigner roaring loudly to the people around him at the scene. Later, another young foreigner wearing glasses came to help, lifting the injured woman's legs and placing them on a suitcase.

Why did so many people stand by watching, and only the foreigner lent a helping hand? This netizen said that he didn't know the mentality of others, himself not knowing how to help: "I was afraid that the wrong rescue method would worsen the injury, so I could only wait for the 120 emergency services, but that foreigner seemed to have received first aid training." Soon after, the doctors arrived and took the injured woman away.

Victim: Wants to Find Benefactor to Express Thanks

On April 8, after eight days of rescue, Ms. Gu was transferred from the intensive care unit to a regular ward, but still needed oxygen.

Besides wanting to know why her son did this, Ms. Gu, who still cannot eat, is also thinking about someone: the foreigner who saved her at the airport.

Ms. Gu recalled that after being stabbed by her son, she might have lost too much blood, and her head became heavier and heavier. In a blur, she only saw her son's figure fading away, "He was wearing yellow clothes and walking further and further away."

She only remembered that a foreigner ran over and lifted her head: "I was wearing a scarf around my neck, and he took it off and used it to cover my wound." Due to the language barrier, Ms. Gu couldn't understand what he was shouting.

When Ms. Gu closed her eyes, the foreigner used his fingers to pry open her eyelids and breathed heavily in front of her, "He was afraid that I wouldn't make it, and wanted me to take deep breaths..." Ms. Gu tried to open her eyes and breathe heavily. The foreigner waited until the ambulance came, helped her onto the ambulance, and then left.

"The doctor said that if it weren't for this foreigner, I might have 'passed away'." Ms. Gu was very grateful and wanted to find this life-saving benefactor through the Morning Post to thank him in person, zxy_Sina Blog A.

[Perpetrator's Status]

Judicial Appraisal of Perpetrator's Mental Status Has Begun

Yesterday, Shanghai police confirmed that the judicial appraisal of the mental status of Wang, a Japanese student who stabbed his mother nine times, has officially begun.

Regarding this, lawyer Zhang Peihong from Shanghai Zhaijian Law Firm said that as the family of the suspect, if they think there is a problem with the suspect's mental health, they can report it to the police, and the police will decide whether it is necessary to conduct a judicial psychiatric appraisal after reviewing it. Normally, a judicial psychiatric appraisal takes about a month.

Senior psychological counselor Lin Zi believed that to accurately analyze a person's psychological status, it is necessary to grasp accurate information from all aspects, such as personal growth history, family situation, past disease history, family disease history, individual cognitive, emotional, behavioral status, whether there is intact self-awareness, whether they have experienced major acute trauma events, etc.
