The treatment of newborns with hyaline membrane disease using ventilators (etc.) has been checked and the results indicate: in some aspects, it is satisfactory; however, in some areas, such as nursing records, they are incomplete and inaccurate, needing improvement. There are defects in the nursing records.

Copying the standard nursing plan for care measures: records must reflect measures that nurses have actually implemented, not just standard care plan measures. Some nurses develop a standard care plan to mechanically complete medical records, without writing down the nursing records based on actual nurse performance or execution.

Two. Lack of records or record continuity: there is no understanding of specialized knowledge and overall care plans, not knowing what to record, or even failing to record at all. There is no awareness of special medication, special treatment, or special inspection records for patients. No follow-up records in nursing records fail to reflect the dynamic process of care. Some nurses only record events and changes in condition for this class, a group of disease changes, medication reactions, phases, and nursing activities.

3. Lack of authenticity in records: head nurses overly rely on accompaniment and do not personally observe. Nurses sit in offices without observing patients and white rows. For example, after peritoneal drainage following resection of N-sac, patient care was recorded as having dry wound dressings and fixed drainage tube patency rounds. However, upon finding that the patient's dressing had already spread and there was no drain within the drainage tube, these discrepancies were noted. Another example: a patient who just underwent surgery had their nursing records described by nurses as smooth techniques and stable disease conditions upon returning to the ward, which should not be recorded by the ward nurse since the ward nurse was not involved in the surgery.

4. Inaccurate use of language in records: records should objectively reflect the patient’s condition and avoid conclusive language. The actual patient vital signs may vary greatly sometimes, and the patient’s condition can change unpredictably. If unexpected proof arises regarding the patient, we will face tremendous difficulties.

5. Care records not completed in a timely manner: there are many retrospective nursing records lacking guidance and timeliness. If legal disputes arise, and medical records are immediately sealed, then children published should not make up their minds that even if clinical care lacks records, there is no legal effect.

Response measures and ways to improve:

a. Enhance legal awareness: for the protection of healthcare safety and the legitimate rights and interests of nurses and hospitals. First, hospital nurses need to pay attention ideologically, strengthen the learning of Medical Malpractice and supporting documents, emphasizing the importance of writing nursing records. Enable nurses to fully recognize the important role of documented care in medical disputes concerning proof burden. Establish a focus on prevention in medical disputes. Strengthen the education of nurses' responsibility, encouraging nurses to communicate more with patients, fostering mutual trust and care relationships, ensuring patients feel at ease during hospitalization, trusting nurses with treatment and care, obtaining first-hand clinical data for the successful completion of nursing records rather than relying on family members' relays.

Two. Key is to strengthen theoretical knowledge learning, improving the standard of nursing theory: solve the problem of nursing records to enhance the overall quality of nursing staff. Hospital requirements for nurses' nursing programs should combine undergraduate characteristics, understanding the learning requirements of nursing programs, recording meaningful practical information concisely. High-quality nursing records require nurses to possess certain knowledge of medicine, nursing, health education, and psychology to complete. Therefore, strengthening the training of nurses' professional quality is essential. Select care backbone personnel to participate in holistic nursing classes or advanced studies organized by national and provincial institutes. After returning to the hospital, organize learning sessions to guide departmental work. Encourage nurses to participate in higher-level self-retaining programs, regularly organizing holistic nursing training courses, lectures by qualified nursing specialists and experienced nurses in practice, constantly summarizing and exploring experience, thereby enhancing the level of holistic nursing records.

3. Improve software while strengthening hardening construction: during the learning process in nursing theory, the nursing department and head nurse should actively seek the attention of hospital leaders, ensuring human, financial, and material support, especially trying to protect nurses in post-staffing so that nurses have more time with patients to understand diseases. Only through such measures can nursing records be real, timely, and reliable.

4. Specification management, strengthening inspection and guidance: departments specify charge nurses responsible for further studies to ensure regular spot checks on undergraduate medical writing and implementation measures. Study sections of an organization’s business, ask questions during morning meetings, conduct comprehensive examinations of undergraduate medical records during nursing rounds, choose better and more prominent records to comment on during early meetings, continuously improving the integrity and science of written records.
