Working relationships should not be reduced to a form of personal relationship. The impression you give to your colleagues and leaders, according to the Inner Mongolia Xilingol Recruitment Network, is highly relevant to your future.

1. Treat others sincerely. Your emotions should be genuine. You should strictly avoid manipulating others or touching on their sensitive nerves.

2. Be confident and unwavering. Defend your own ideas rather than follow the crowd. It's impossible for everyone to agree because each person has their own way of thinking.

3. Be a good listener. Listening means understanding what others are saying to you. Being a good listener to your leader's opinions will save you thousands of troubles.

4. Be humble and respectful. Words like "please" and "thank you" should often be in our vocabulary.

5. Understand each other. Tolerance is necessary. To understand others' attitudes towards specific issues, you should empathize and think from their perspective.

6. Express yourself directly. Frankly expressing yourself without beating around the bush is a good way to avoid misunderstandings.

7. Care about others. Pay attention to others' personalities and respect their space for excellent results. Show concern for your colleagues by asking questions like, "How is your mother's health?" or "How did your son do on his recent exam?"

8. Maintain eye contact. Look into the eyes of the person talking to show interest in what they are saying.

9. Moderate your tone. Whether loudly noisy or whispering secretly while building working relationships can both be disadvantageous to you.

10. Behave elegantly. Excessive gesturing during conversations looks bad. Hands in your pockets might make people think you're hiding something.

11. Speak cautiously. Talking without understanding the situation is blind. Fully understand the reasons before expressing your opinion.

12. Dress appropriately. Boastful words, untidiness, forgetting deodorant, and oil stains all over your clothes create a frightening appearance that few people want to approach. Personal hygiene is the minimum standard of civility, unrelated to expensive or beautiful clothing.

13. Pay attention to details. When introduced to others, remember their names and preferences; these are the most important details.

14. Be open-minded. Don't be artificial; making others say you are such a good person only leads to the opposite result.

15. Adapt to the environment. For doing a good job, being smart and talented is the best weapon. Remember the proverb "When in Rome, do as the Romans do."

16. Give timely compliments. Kind words always make people happy. "You look beautiful in this shirt." "Since you've been dieting, you look even more attractive." These are easy things to say, but at the right time.

17. Smile. This is the best way to defuse anger. Few people oppose an open smile and conversing with a friendly, warm tone.

18. Be self-confident and self-loving. Self-love is very important for approaching and helping others. Confidence helps break down barriers between oneself and others.

19. Respect each other. Wanting others to like you does not mean allowing them to be disrespectful. Allowing others to be rude or harsh towards you is a very bad thing.

20. Care about your leaders. Regardless of who your superior is, it doesn't hurt to ask, "Is your mother feeling well? Does her tooth still hurt?" They will appreciate your concern, and you will gain goodwill.

How to build good interpersonal relationships with colleagues:

People play different social roles based on their age, gender, occupation, position, and circumstances. Different roles have different behavioral standards when interacting with others, so there are different requirements and skills when dealing with different people. Here we will only address the issue you raised—how to get along with colleagues.

First, always consider others and avoid being self-centered. To maintain good relationships with colleagues, you need to learn to consider problems from others' perspectives and be adept at making appropriate sacrifices.

To complete a task well, cooperation with others is often required. After achieving success, share the credit together. Avoid showing off or taking all the credit for the team's work. Providing opportunities and helping others achieve their life goals is crucial for maintaining good interpersonal relationships.
