The Oath Before Two-Sixths

The oath made before two-sixths (one third) of the journey: I cut my hair, ready to dedicate myself wholeheartedly to studying. It feels like I've returned to the month before the start of freshman year in high school. My hair is shorter now. I always feel a bit empty and cold; perhaps I'll need to wear a hat in this chilly weather. The days of long flowing hair don't belong to my youth anymore.

Suddenly, the word "romance" came to mind - it's too heavy, an unaffordable term in life. I think I don't need love right now; family affection and friendship are enough. There's no time for learning, and if I have nothing better to do after eating, then I'm just being silly. I don't think I'll regret it since I decided to distance myself from matters of love once I started high school. I'm not some kind of genius; once a relationship is established, both parties get tied down. Trivial matters can affect studies. Sometimes, even if you meet the right person, you still have to say no! Because meeting the right person at the wrong time is ultimately still a mistake. Someone said that falling in love in high school is a kind of experience. It sounds like working, and there's also the fear of being deceived.

I prepared everything for the upcoming semester, made a schedule, and planned to follow it strictly once school starts. No one can stop my forward progress. Let those rumors drift away with the wind. I feel like I'm a prisoner who has just been released from jail, feeling the first ray of bright sunshine, starting anew. Pfft, what a metaphor! Too ominous. This reminded me of a metaphor from Guo Jingming's "Sadness Flows Upstream Like a River." When I first read it, it made me feel sick. The female protagonist described the voice of a woman on the morning exercise broadcast as sounding like she had phlegm in her throat - a very disgusting sentence, quite absurd. I remember crying uncontrollably after reading that book, not even knowing what I was sad about. Recently, I saw an annual list of the worst books, and "Sadness Flows Upstream Like a River" was prominently listed. Sigh, this digression went a bit far.

I've been following a TV series called "Richi no Challengeshin Real Version." After watching the animation where Konno's foot was fractured but she endured the pain while kneeling for so long, it felt like I received a shot of adrenaline. Suddenly, all my previous troubles seemed trivial. Later, when I found out they made a live-action version, I quickly checked it out. Yihan's acting is exaggerated but cute and beautiful. I didn't really like Ren played by Dong Hai. Watching the live-action version completely lost the genuine feeling of the anime; it's more for entertainment. Still, I kept watching episode by episode, unfortunately, it only updates once a week. I've been following it since last year until February. I remember the day sister left for school; I discovered a new episode had just been updated. I was secretly pleased but also felt sorry for her. Later, I mentioned this over the phone, and she was so annoyed, feeling utterly helpless. Sometimes I really want to read comics, but academics must come first. We can deal with other things in college.

Phew! Finally added all my classmates on QQ. The people on QQ are a mess, like scattered sand, including those I met online, classmates, and friends from Baidu. Since I didn't add notes, sometimes chatting feels like nonsense. Seeing so many unique QQ names gives me a headache, and I have to add notes one by one.

Strong people have the ability to fight. People shouldn't always dwell on their weaknesses; every storm will eventually pass. If I keep dwelling on these things, the bright sunshine will leave me. Two quotes by Tagore that I really like are: "Clouds themselves hide the sun but complain about the lack of gloom." "If you cry because you've lost the moon, you will lose the stars too."

Recently, I listened to Professor Yu Dan's lectures on Confucian Analects and Zhuangzi's philosophy. I reflected on many things and realized how many mistakes I made in the past, which I deeply regret. I began to learn patience and tolerance, becoming calmer inside. A true gentleman's calmness can resolve many regrets in life. Because of the beauty within, one can face the outside world with composure. I believe I am someone born with regrets, and facing regrets, I will accept them. Now that I know about this regret, the next step is to use all my strength to make up for it. A true gentleman is calm without arrogance (having great aspirations), whereas a small man is arrogant without calmness (showy and confrontational everywhere). I started learning to treat others like a true gentleman should, respectful and courteous. That's a sincere expression of respect.

As for why it's called "two-sixths," it's because high school only has six semesters. Thinking three years seems long, but divided into six semesters, it feels short. Time is a strange thing, just like youth. When you have it, you waste it recklessly, and when you lose it, it makes you regret endlessly. Today, I wrote this article to commemorate my 16th year and to begin a new life, walking towards the bright sunshine. This means, once again, I am growing through the pains of youth.
