Top Ten Online Game Novels

Online game novels have become an important branch of novels. The origin of online game novels can be traced back to the start of the legendary online game. Among many online game players, their enthusiasm for online games naturally gave birth to online game novels. It can be said that the legendary online game played a decisive role in the creation of online game novels. Subsequently, the emergence of new online games such as "Rising" extended the framework of online game novels and established a new genre of novels with simulated real games as the background. Later, with the gradual development of online games, various game elements were integrated into online game novels, and many creative ideas from online game novels also promoted the better development of online games. For example, the online game "Magic Domain" adopted many creative ideas from online game novels. Until now, online game novels and online games have become two closely related entities.

Categories of online game novels: [Virtual Online Games] [Game Careers] [Boys' Love Online Games]

Additionally, [Chess Careers] [Basketball Sports] [Sports Competitions] [Football Sports] [Electronic Sports], these types of novels are often considered as online game novels. Among them, virtual online games are the most common model in current online game novels. They generally involve wearing a virtual helmet and controlling the game through brain waves.

After years of accumulation, Juyitang (www.juyit.cn) and its predecessor Xihuanxiaoshuo website (www.yqwei.cn) have selected the top ten online game novels. Ninth Place: Online Game Three Kingdoms - City Strategy

War is about burning military salaries, burning food, burning soldiers, burning supplies. How can you do without a strong rear base?

The protagonist, Zhan Xiaobai, aims to create a wave of construction in the Three Kingdoms era. Building villages, exploring scholars, advancing technology, developing water conservancy, cultivating farmland, prospecting for minerals, promoting trade, gathering equipment, taming horses, rectifying morale, conquering territories, all aiming to dominate the Three Kingdoms.

In this virtual world, there are no villages or small towns, only system-controlled large and medium-sized cities (such as Chang'an, Luoyang). Idle places allow players to build their own villages, towns, even ascending to imperial cities. Population, land, labor force, technological productivity, resources, taxation, military sources determine how powerful your force is.

Recommendation reason: No one knows when it started, but everyone is increasingly paying attention to that star-studded era, a time of the strong ruling. This book vividly portrays the Three Kingdoms era. After reading this book, whether it's historical novels about the Three Kingdoms or strategy games, you will feel confident.

Seventh Place: Online Game - Dragon Slaughter Wizard

This tells the story of a rookie who was forcibly sent into an online game and grew into an "indecent and shameless" evil wizard.

Recommendation reason: Dragon Slaughter is an online game novel, but it can be said to be the best online game novel I've read. Personally, even compared to the classic "On the Run", Dragon Slaughter is no less impressive. There are three reasons.

First, the plot is full of twists and turns, truly remarkable. Although it initially seemed like pure YY literature, from the accidental encounter with a dragon and Medusa, it became evident. Frankly speaking, if the plot had continued in this direction, I wouldn't have kept reading for long. However, the author quickly began changing, introducing characters like Chu Shiming, angels, and Yezi during the battle with the Skeleton King, creating a main storyline of conflict, opposition, and conflict; meanwhile, the legends of the system, legendary NPCs, and equipment formed the second storyline; additionally, the protagonist's emotional experiences and real-life grudges and loves formed the third storyline. With other plot threads, the content of the entire novel is incredibly rich, rarely seen in online works.

Then, regarding the characters, they initially carried some stereotypical colors. The most prominent representatives were Apple Pig purely as a bad guy and Zi Yi's capriciousness. However, as the novel progressed, the author seemed unwilling for his characters to be simply represented by stereotypes. Therefore, the villainous Apple Pig made fewer appearances, replaced by the somewhat unpredictable Chu Shiming as the protagonist's biggest rival. This character's appearance brought all readers into a non-stop fantastical journey. Zi Yi, beneath her capriciousness, had a sensitive heart, typical of being sharp-tongued but soft-hearted, portrayed very well by the author. Of course, characters like Angel, Yezi, and Sad Philosopher need not be mentioned.

Finally, let's talk about Xin Yi: I can say this, everyone's joy is Xin Yi's joy. This is what I admire most about him. Writers are human, especially professional writers whose income from writing is their life in reality. Xin Yi seems to be such a writer, so he entered VIP status. But as an author, he could even become the moderator of Baidu's "Dragon Slaughter Wizard" bar where his articles were pirated, and he didn't despise those who read pirated versions. When I first heard this, I didn't believe it, but upon verification, it was indeed true. I think this is the author's magnanimity, hoping more readers find happiness, and then he finds happiness too.

Fifth Place: We Are the Champions

This book tells the story of Zhang Jun, a representative figure in the golden generation of Chinese football. Although the title is "We Are the Champions," the main character is still Zhang Jun, who is the protagonist. The novel follows his career from entering professional football until his retirement. Through his eyes and experiences, it sketches the development process of Chinese football. There is joy, sadness, hope, and disappointment. Chinese football moves forward with the ups and downs of Zhang Jun's career. There is progress, regression, darkness, and light. The climax of the book is when the Chinese team wins the World Cup, but this is not the final message of the book. The true meaning of "We Are the Champions" does not lie in winning a historic world championship, but in the novel's ending: What kind of champion does Chinese football really need? Therefore, please all readers patiently finish the entire text. To be honest, the author hopes to write everything at once and upload it all, allowing readers to finish in one breath. That way, the book "Champion" would have more flavor. However, due to the characteristics of online serialization and writing, readers have to wait together. In the waiting process, there is joy, sadness, anger, and disappointment, but please don't give up. Because although "Champion" is a reality-based YY, overall, it is a positive, inspiring, and hopeful story. Perhaps after enduring the darkness, you'll inadvertently discover a glimmer of light waiting quietly for you. Walk towards it, and you'll see a Chinese football that shines brilliantly after absolute darkness... P.S.: The book "We Are the Champions" has been officially published in simplified Chinese by Beijing Correction Library and Northern Arts Publishing House, available in major city Xinhua Bookstores, or through online ordering. Currently published is the first part of the novel: Twin Stars. The remaining parts will be published subsequently. Thank you for supporting the official version!

Recommendation reason: Chinese football is the eternal pain in the hearts of Chinese fans. Reading this book brings comfort to the imagination.

Third Place: Rogue Expert

Reading this book leads to consequences: among ten people, three fantasize about having an unmentionable relationship with one of the two female protagonists. Six fantasize about having an unmentionable relationship with both. The remaining one is a girl. Among ten people, five will happily laugh from beginning to end. Three will comment in the review section that Old Wu is lewd. Two will laugh until they cramp and ask Old Wu for compensation. Among ten people, two will find Old Wu's daily update of ten thousand words exhilarating, seven will lewdly request Old Wu to update a hundred times a day. One remains neutral. Among ten people, eight are lewd, one is about to become lewd, and another might be pure, liked by everyone, as cute as a white flower. In summary, this is a book as stylish as a hand-guided tractor on a highway.

Recommendation reason: Personally, I think one of the greatest highlights of this book is the use of language, very humorous and witty, very close to reality. I played online games in college, especially in internet cafes. In that atmosphere, sometimes the emotions one wants to express truly require "cursing" to describe, and sometimes this "curse" is actually a great way for brothers and teammates to express friendship. Throughout the book, there are many expressions that make people laugh out loud, and the descriptions of the gaming process feel as if you're right there in the game. Therefore, I am currently studying the StarCraft game hard, hehe... It can be said that if the author of this book hadn't played StarCraft, it would be impossible for him to write such a novel; if the author wasn't so humorous and witty with language, I think this book wouldn't have been successful. The use of language, combined with the author's deep understanding of games, is the key to this book. Regarding some language or sentences in this book, sometimes they can be confusing, such as "MLGBD", if you've never said it or don't have previous experience, I think many people wouldn't be able to guess what these five letters represent; also, the commonly used phrase in the book "topping your lungs", hehe, cursing without using dirty words doesn't get better than this, but this phrase isn't really cursing, it's just something said when encountering extremely frustrating situations in the game, like a soldier going to the toilet when the army suddenly sounds the emergency assembly call, this feeling, isn't it frustrating and helpless? And throughout the book, the protagonist's StarCraft level or tactics are described as "lewd", hehe, I think the naming of this book "Rogue Expert", the word "rogue" should be another affirmation of the protagonist's gaming skills—"lewd", and the word "expert" is a straightforward description, just like Fang Shaoyun's strategies and tactics in StarCraft—"virtual and real", wow hahaha...
