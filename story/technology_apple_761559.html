Apple rumored to be integrating maps, Siri and car systems

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 19:01:43

It is reported that Apple Inc. is currently cooperating with automobile manufacturers to deeply integrate the Maps and Siri services of iOS into cars.May 1, Beijing time -- According to a report by American tech blog 9to5Mac, sources revealed that Apple plans to accelerate its entry into the field of in-car integration later this year. The company is currently working with carmakers to deeply integrate iOS's Maps and Siri services into vehicles.Although there are already many third-party accessories available that can integrate iPhones and other iOS devices into the dashboard of a car, making it convenient for users to use Apple Maps' turn-by-turn navigation function, Apple is now preparing to offer an official solution.Sources said that Apple is collaborating with automakers to upgrade the software of car dashboards so that they can be integrated with iOS devices. Specifically, you can plug your iPhone into the car, and the newly optimized version of Apple Maps will appear on the in-car display, eliminating the need for dedicated GPS navigation devices.Sources stated that this feature is akin to projecting the Maps app from an iPhone onto the car display. Previously, Volkswagen's iBettle could also achieve this functionality through third-party apps.In addition, after connecting the iPhone to the car, users can directly control Maps and other iOS functions using Siri.Apple launched a new hands-free Siri service last year, allowing users to connect their iPhones with cars and use Siri with the iPhone screen turned off. At the Worldwide Developers Conference (WWDC), Apple announced that it was developing hands-free functionality in collaboration with carmakers such as BMW, Toyota, Audi, Honda, and Land Rover. Based on current information, the company seems likely to continue collaborating with these manufacturers to leverage the new Maps and Siri in-car solutions.While this new car function is based on iOS 7 technology, sources warned that it may take a long time before it is ready for public release. Before officially launching, Apple needs to overcome many obstacles, including more comprehensive in-car testing, upgrades to Apple Maps and Siri infrastructure, and agreements with carmakers. It is unclear whether Apple will announce this plan at this year's WWDC. Considering the processing power required for this function, it might only be compatible with the latest iOS hardware.Although Apple's current plan appears to be limited to the two most important functions for drivers—Maps and Siri—industry insiders believe that Apple may eventually focus on the entire car dashboard operating interface.Apple's ambitions in the automotive market are not limited to integrating with iOS. The company's senior vice president of Internet Software and Services, Eddy Cue, has long been very interested in cars. At the 2011 WWDC, he specifically took pictures of toy cars from Pixar movies when demonstrating the image streaming feature of iCloud. Additionally, Cue serves as a director of Ferrari. Earlier this year, Ferrari began equipping one of its models with an iPad mini.Apple co-founder Steve Jobs had also considered designing cars. Recent patents suggest that Apple indeed intends to develop some software to help users find and start their cars.Earlier this year, Apple listed a series of job postings on its website seeking to integrate iOS devices with in-car audio systems. The company is also recruiting software testers to test the compatibility of iOS products with in-car audio equipment. These job postings require candidates to have experience testing Bluetooth products. This could mean that the relevant functions will be achieved through Bluetooth.Although Apple's voice assistant and map services lag behind Google in terms of reliability and functionality, entering the automotive market allows iOS to play a more important role in users' lives, achieving a more unified user experience. Meanwhile, due to the fragmentation issues of hardware and software experiences, Android struggles to compete with Apple in this aspect. (Shuyu)
