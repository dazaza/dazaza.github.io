Living in accordance with the sun... snow disasters... Tibetan Mastiffs...

by fkflr938 on 2011-05-27 01:30:25

Gloomy weather always makes people sleepy and listless. It's inevitable to struggle in the quilt for a long time after opening eyes in the morning.

My mother always calls me a "night owl". The main reason is because I like to stay in bed. However, it's not exactly because I sleep late, but because the morning sun is not yet brilliant. Carefully thinking about it, my biological clock has always been following the movement of the sun, purely "working at sunrise". The most convincing evidence is: compared to other seasons, the winter alarm clock seems more annoying to me. In summer, I often stare at the alarm clock and decisively press the button as soon as it starts ringing, "choking" it back. After the beginning of spring, I wake up earlier day by day, even around five or six o'clock in the morning. How primitive!

Of course, I can't deny the attachment to the comfortable quilt, because that feeling can't be enjoyed while sleeping.

Due to all these factors, I woke up late again today. I had to take a car instead of walking just to avoid our assistant nagging about the attendance sheet next month.

The driver was quite interesting. He started to comment on the snow disaster of the past two days without any introduction or conclusion. He expressed regret for the people in the disaster area while celebrating that his compatriots in the south could enjoy the snow and ice in their lifetime. How interesting! However, my logic was wandering between natural disasters and man-made disasters: human infection of the environment causes climate chaos, which leads to snow disasters or winter rain and other natural disasters, causing harm to humans. (It seems a bit like worrying unnecessarily and too serious. Let's start over!)

Snow disaster mm Tibet snow disaster mm the snow disaster that causes famine and wolf troubles hh Yeah, this line of thought is pretty good!

I started to listen to the novel broadcast "Tibetan Mastiff" every day at 11:00 pm a few months ago.

The novel describes the endless wolf troubles in Tibetan pastoral areas after a big snow disaster. Thus, humans and dogs fight together for survival. The humanity and mastiff nature are perfectly blended. As "father" said, "I was a mastiff in my previous life." Compared with the wolf spirit in "Wolf Totem", it emphasizes the loyalty, responsibility, sacrifice, rules, etc., of Tibetan mastiffs, thereby calling for humanity through mastiff nature.

I remember when I read "Wolf Totem", it once overturned the image of wolves in my mind as cunning and vulgar. I even placed the "spirit of the wolf" at a high position, gaining a lot of inspiration. But as I became more obsessed with "Tibetan Mastiff", in the fierce battles between the territory dog pack and the wolf pack, I obviously favor the human friends - mastiff dogs. Whether out of sympathy or admiration, they have an absolute advantage over my former appreciation for the wolf spirit.

Wang Kai's narration, with a deep and simple voice, vividly brings the brave and admirable mastiff dogs to life, no less impressive than Zhou Tao's "Wolf Totem" in those days. Super recommended! Female mastiff Zhuoga, Grey Mastiff, King Mastiff Gangrisenge hh

The driver and I were lost in thought about the same topic, completely parallel lines, non-intersecting and non-affecting. Of course, neither of us expects the other to understand, at least I don't.
