Today, I would like to introduce an online mobile phone synchronization tool to everyone. Before using it, you can check on this page if your mobile phone model is supported: https://www.vufone.com/devices. However, there are also supported models that are not listed, such as the 6700 (right image) used by Jarry.

Quotation

This service named vufone(R) allows users to achieve synchronized telephone cloud computing, telephone network management, and connect data to various network services and applications. Vufone will be released globally through mobile devices or the web (http://www.vufone.com). The currently supported language versions include English, Chinese (simplified and traditional), French, Spanish, German, Russian, Dutch, and Portuguese. This service targets mass-market phones using J2ME, Symbian, and Windows Mobile platforms. Future versions will consider professional users using iPhone, BlackBerry, and Google Android. Compared to other services, this service offers the following advantages: complete virtual phone function, two-way synchronization, access and management of user data (such as contacts, calendar, pictures, videos, music, and files); uploading pictures and videos to personal zones and social websites (such as Facebook); uploading mobile media files to services like Flickr, Picasa, and YouTube, as well as synchronization between personal computers and mobile phones; adding Outlook functions for phones using the Outlook plug-in; convenient, wireless transfer of user data between devices, ensuring data integrity and applicability to new devices when upgrading a phone; directly transmitting any website's images to the phone via vufone's MSIE or FireFox plug-in. (This introduction text is quoted from the Internet)

When registering, you must fill in your real email and phone number because email verification is required during registration, and your phone number is needed for phone synchronization verification. After successful registration, you will have 1GB of space to store your mobile phone backup data. To use the vufone online synchronization tool, you need to install a synchronization server program on your mobile phone so that vufone can read the information on your phone. The installation method is as follows: click the "Download mobile phone installation program" button. Then, a short message will be sent to your phone. Click the link on your phone to download the program directly onto your phone for installation. However, the short message received by Jarry displayed garbled characters. Jarry has already written an email, attached with screenshots, to report this bug to the official site. Hopefully, they can solve this problem in the near term.

However, to use vufone, the software must be installed on your phone. Later, in the vufone official forum, a post provided an HTTP download address. If the phone download fails, you can use your computer to download and then install it on your phone. With this issue resolved, let's continue.

Run the vufone software on your phone. It's located in Start - Programs. The first time you run it, you need to input your phone number and password. Then set up the content and method of synchronization you need, and you can start synchronizing.

After completing the synchronization process, log in to vufone on your computer, and you will see the last synchronization time:

Expand the project box in the main workspace to see the detailed contents of the synchronization (to protect privacy, Jarry has collapsed them).

From the above figure, it can be seen that vufone can synchronize our phone's contacts, schedule, short messages, tasks, photos, videos, music, and documents. The purpose of backup is, of course, just in case. When your phone is damaged, stolen, submerged, or other accidents occur, your data is already saved on vufone.

Moreover, you can set the association between vufone and your Google account. This way, you can synchronize your contacts and schedule to the corresponding Google applications.

There is no need to install any programs on your computer. Using your phone network, you can synchronize your data anytime, anywhere. vufone is indeed a very convenient helper.
