From the first year of the Internet in 1994 to the present, China's Internet has gone through more than ten years, after burning money, bubbles, diving, hibernation, hard support, until it rises again, thousands of Wanlu, blowing up the crazy sand.

Looking back on the past 12 years, the development of China's Internet has staged a scene from scratch, from small to large, ups and downs, and breathtaking tragicomedy. Overnight, it was mythically pushed to the top, making all those involved with the Internet stand tall and proud, as if wearing a sacred garment; Overnight, its bubble burst and fell into the trough, making the former high-minded network elites like rats crossing the street.

Four years after the first wave of Internet bubble, venture capitalists seem to have forgotten the pain and flocked to China in 2005, carrying huge amounts of money to frequently start, vertical search, narrow notice, Web2.0, Blog... Some Internet companies that were widely questioned even yesterday are suddenly thriving. Foreign media have used the words "crazy", "blowout", "violent wake up the Internet" to describe these performances. At the same time, they also lamented that this seems to be back in 1999, the era of the Internet bubble. And now, SP, which has been hyped by the media, has slashed staff, venture capital has turned to withdraw, and concept stocks have collectively fallen... These, too, seem strikingly similar to the signs of the bubble bursting in 2000.

If the current phenomenon is called the "second wave of the Internet bubble", then this time the bubble will make those websites disappear with it?
