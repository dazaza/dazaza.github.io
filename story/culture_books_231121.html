"The snow hidden in the previous season" conceals love beneath the surface of still water.

by jqrxass on 2009-08-31 12:53:44

After the death of his first girlfriend in a car accident, Liu Gonglin (that is "I") went to Shanghai alone for development and cohabited with Su Yan, a classmate from junior high school. They were constantly facing internal and external crises. She had a liking for another boy, and Liu Gonglin also developed feelings for Chengru, the girlfriend of his classmate Tang Song.

Amidst emotional conflicts, Liu Gonglin experienced several ups and downs in his career and realized that a significant part of his inner self seemed to have remained stuck in childhood, unable to keep up with this increasingly materialistic world. The only thing that sustained his belief in life was love. In this belief, he unconsciously idealized Chengru.

Liu Gonglin ended up not possessing either of the two girls, but he simultaneously took on the responsibility of taking care of both. At the entrance of the bar where Chengru went, Su Yan stabbed her with a knife, and Liu took the stab for her. Su Yan fled, and Liu stood by the roadside, waiting for a taxi to chase after Su Yan while contemplating what to do next...

In the posture of questioning love, seeking love. Different people will have completely different interpretations of this book.

Part One: Hiding in the Snow of the Last Season

Finally, both Su Yan and I moved out of Meiling Branch Road, each finding new housing, and thus lost contact.

I temporarily had no plans to buy a house, having gotten used to moving around. It's less about life being in motion, more about impulse and blind movement. My new residence was a garden villa on Huaihai Road, not far from the Soong Ching Ling Former Residence.

Part Two: Hiding in the Snow of the Last Season

Gui Chengru laughed while fanning herself with her hand, casting sidelong glances at me. She whispered softly into my ear, her soft voice stimulating me even more. My face flushed as I said, "I'm not Tang Song; he can carry you, but I can't."

"What's so impossible? You're a coward, afraid of everything." She then lightly tapped on my back with her hand.

"Stop tapping, put your hand down."

"I insist on tapping, coward."

I grabbed her hand but didn't let go, clutching it tightly. The green shaded stripes on her chest rose and fell with her breathing, and I couldn't help but want to sip the sweet spring there. The sky suddenly turned dim and heavy, pushing all sounds far away, and everything would be swallowed by the scorching heat. Finally, I came to my senses, released her hand, and walked forward.

Part Three: Hiding in the Snow of the Last Season

Su Yan doubted yet believed, her crying gradually weakening as she leaned against my chest. This was our first intimate contact, and I never expected it to happen under such an unpoetic background. I lacked much passion but couldn't refuse her initiative; the steps and rhythm were all within her control.

That night, she didn't return to East China University of Science and Technology. Her body was plump and full of vitality, knowing how to act coy beneath me. My perception of her needed updating; she was no longer the little girl from back in the day. The sense of conquest satisfied me greatly, and we made love four times. Her final words made me laugh; she stroked my head and said, "You've worked hard, thank you very much."
