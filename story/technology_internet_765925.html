Twitter Acquires Social TV Tracking Service Trendr

by anonymous on 2013-08-29 16:40:23

Twitter announced on Wednesday that the company has acquired social TV tracking service Trendrr.

"For years we've been at the intersection of TV and social, analyzing data from many platforms," Trendrr wrote in a blog post today. "What makes Twitter unique among these platforms is its ability to connect users to live moments. When something is happening, people can share it with the rest of the world." Trendrr also stated: "We believe we can help further this connection between users and live moments from within Twitter."

Trendrr tracks social activity across social networks like Twitter and Facebook, helping users discover relevant tweets around specific events. For Twitter, it's a very appropriate deal as the company continues to push forward with its strategy to combine its 'social soundtrack' second screen content with TV.

Trendrr's main product is Curatorr, a Twitter-certified product that allows media companies and brands to organize tweet timelines around an event. Industry insiders pointed out that this kind of product is very useful if brands are trying to reach users who are watching live content.

After being acquired by Twitter, Trendrr will no longer provide end-to-end analytics services to other partners, such as Facebook. Ironically, Facebook had just benefited from a paid Trendrr research report, which indicated that compared to Twitter, Facebook users post five times more comments related to TV shows.

This acquisition by Twitter means that the three major players in the social TV commentary field no longer exist. Earlier this year, Twitter acquired Bluefin Labs, while Nielsen acquired SocialGuide; now, Twitter has again acquired Trendrr. After all existing contracts with Trendrr expire, Twitter will shut down the company's analytics tools.

For Twitter, this deal not only provides it with a useful content management tool, but also eliminates some competitive pressure, making it a two-for-one move.
