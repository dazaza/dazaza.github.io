### How to Prevent the Formation of Melasma

Precautions for preventing spots:

1. Avoid prolonged sun exposure and apply sunscreen to exposed areas when going out.

2. If you suspect that melasma is caused by certain medications or cosmetics, discontinue their use.

3. Avoid taking photosensitive drugs such as contraceptives, antihypertensive drugs for treating high blood pressure and coronary heart disease, non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs, diuretics, and some antibiotics.

4. Eat more fresh fruits and vegetables like celery, daylily flowers, black fungus, lotus root, apples, pears, and watermelons.

5. Reduce intake of spicy and other stimulating foods like coffee, cocoa, leeks, garlic, cinnamon, chili peppers, and Sichuan peppercorns.

### Sunlight-induced Dermatitis

Commonly known as "sunspots," this condition manifests as edematous erythema on exposed skin areas within hours after intense sun exposure. Blisters may form, often accompanied by burning, itching, or pricking sensations. Mild cases resolve in a few days, while severe cases may present with flu-like symptoms such as fever, fatigue, and general discomfort, usually recovering in about a week. Lighter-skinned individuals are more prone to this condition, and it is more common among women.
