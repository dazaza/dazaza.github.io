Key points for answering questions

Question Six: What predictable difficulties do you have for this job? Question Seven: Talk about your mistakes. Question Eight: Tell us about one of your failure experiences. Question Nine: If we hire you, how will you carry out your work?

During the interview process, the interviewee's response to the questions raised by the interviewer is often the key factor in deciding whether to accept or reject the candidate. If you really answer the interviewer's questions "honestly" and ignore the rules of the interview and the way of thinking when answering questions, it may directly affect the impression you leave on the interviewer. Therefore, learning to analyze the true purpose of the interviewer's questions and providing reasonable and comprehensive answers can indeed become an important weight for passing the interview.

Question One: Please recommend yourself.

This question is the most common "opening statement" in the interview process. Many people have the habit of "reciting their resume" when introducing themselves. Although this is not a wrong approach, if you are attending a group interview, a bland self-recommendation will make you disappear among the many interviewees. By reorganizing the content of your personal introduction or putting some effort into highlighting the highlights in your narration, you can leave the interviewer with an "unusual" impression.

Key points for answering the question:

1. When it comes to self-recommendation involving resume content, the content must be consistent with your personal resume;

2. The expression method should be as colloquial as possible;

3. Focus on the key points, don't talk about irrelevant or useless content;

4. The structure should be clear and the hierarchy distinct;

5. It's best to write it down in text form and memorize it well at the time.

Question Two: Why did you choose our company?

Some interviewees, when answering this question, often start from a personal perspective, such as: "Hope for better growth space", "Hope to get opportunities for exercise", "Hope to get better treatment". However, in fact, the interviewer wants to know whether your motivation for the interview aligns with the goals of the position being recruited by the company. In other words, the interviewer is more concerned with what you can bring to the company after joining, rather than what the company can bring to you.

Key points for answering the question:

1. The interviewer is trying to understand your motivation for applying, your expectations, and your attitude towards this job;

2. It is recommended to answer from three angles: industry, company, and position.

Question Three: What can you do for us?

This question can be categorized under the same type as the above question, just asked more directly. As an interviewee, it is essential to prepare beforehand, such as understanding the company's profile, products, services, and the requirements of the position you are applying for, and then fully combining these elements to discuss the topic according to the needs of the company you are applying to.

Key points for answering the question:

1. The basic principle is "to cater to their preferences";

2. The hiring unit generally hires candidates who meet the following criteria: basically qualified, interested in this job, and confident enough;

3. Before answering this question, the interviewee should ideally "take the initiative", understanding what the hiring unit expects from this position;

4. The interviewee can answer this question based on their own understanding, combined with their advantages in the professional field.

Question Four: You are a fresh graduate, lacking experience, how can you be competent for this job?

This is one of the most challenging questions for fresh graduates.
