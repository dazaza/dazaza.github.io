They spent hours watching the 21-year-old girl sitting at a high place, gradually draining her of her last attachment to life. Only the 93-year-old man, waving his aged arms, managed to emit a raspy and weak voice: "Don't jump, don't jump!"

Currently, people do not know if the final fall after four hours of hesitation was related to the jeering voices below. A staff member of the residents' committee told reporters that the police and the residents' committee were "broadcasting through two loudspeakers simultaneously", creating a noisy environment. "Even if there were individuals saying such things below, they definitely wouldn't be heard above." This staff member also claimed that the girl's eventual tragic fall was due to "exhaustion causing her to fall."

In Shen Jian's one year and two months of breaking news reporting, August 23rd was already his sixth encounter with a jump scene. "Almost every scene had hecklers, but this time there were an unusually large number."

The old man's worried eyes were tightly fixed on the fifth floor. A witness recalled that at the time, many people at the scene were shouting loudly from below, trying to comfort the "distraught girl."

A middle-aged man stood bare-chested, wearing slippers, smiling as he stood aside. Another woman with gray hair recalled muttering at home, "What are you looking at? If you're going to jump, just do it. We won't even look." But soon after, she became one of the onlookers under Building No. 152. Some came to watch briefly in the morning, seeing no movement, then went to the market to buy groceries before continuing to watch.

Most of the people present pieced together the girl's information from the next day's news. She was 21 years old, grew up in Anhui, and was currently a third-year student at a university in Shanghai. Since May, the girl had rented a small room in Building No. 152. Emotional disputes led her to the window ledge.

She sat on the window ledge for over four hours. Most of the time, she tightly gripped the handle embedded in the window frame with her right hand, while holding a phone in the other, "calling, crying, pausing." The girl's face bore an expression of extreme exhaustion. Occasionally, she would lean her head against the window, blankly looking at the ground.

Then, unexpectedly, a rescue scene unfolded.

People suspected that the girl was pushed to jump by the heckling.

A boy wearing a white short-sleeved T-shirt kept shouting, "Sister, don't jump!" But when the neighborhood committee staff handed him a gray megaphone, he shyly hid his face in his mother's arms.

Shen Jian's video captured not only the crowd's laughter and shouts of "jump," but also the trembling arms of the elderly man Zhou Zehua. This video was retweeted over 20,000 times on Sina Weibo and received over a million views on Youku.

"Come down! Either come down or jump, both are fine." Someone laughed.

And then, in a moment unnoticed by those watching and heckling with their arms crossed, the girl fell from the fifth floor.

As her body hit the ground heavily, applause erupted among the onlookers.

Apart from a broken bone and facial abrasions, everything seemed unchanged. She was discharged from the hospital soon after and returned to the small rented room to recuperate. She was still the same girl who loved beauty, but due to her facial injuries, she anxiously questioned the emergency room doctor, "Will there be scars?"

The deafening heckling at the jump site has never ceased. It occurred in Haikou and Chengdu in 2007, Chengdu and Shangrao in 2009, Anshan in 2010, and Shanghai in 2011.

An elderly man named Zhou Zehua.

Someone at the scene mockingly made a "hook hand" gesture towards the wooden girl. In Shen Jian's video shot that day, the onlookers burst into laughter, and the video faintly caught a sound similar to "jump."

A woman buying vegetables on the street said, "Around noon, a crazy person was shouting, it was an elderly garbage collector. The police asked her to stop, and later she did." Nearby, several people echoed, "Yes, yes, it was that garbage collector. We all told her not to jump."

In Wan Junren's view, "Many people think others' misfortunes have nothing to do with them, or even consider witnessing someone else's misfortune as their own luck. But what if you were in that misfortune?"

An 80-year-old woman named Jin discovered the girl's intention to jump and spent the entire afternoon trying to persuade her without giving up.

Ultimately, the girl's heavy body crashed onto the rescue air cushion, then bounced towards the drainage ditch, and was taken to the ambulance. "Her face was covered in blood."

"A person is going to jump" news spread quickly, and more and more people gathered to watch. By around 3 PM, nearly two hundred people had gathered at the scene. Some were residents of the area, others had rushed over from nearby neighborhoods or shops after receiving phone calls.

As early as May 9, 2003, in Xiangtan, Hunan Province, a 41-year-old man named Jiang Jianmin climbed onto the roof of a five-story building intending to commit suicide by jumping. Several firemen ascended the roof to attempt a rescue.

Jiang Jianmin was enraged by the shouting and picked up bricks nearby, throwing them down.

Zhou Zehua anxiously shouted upwards, "Don't jump, don't jump, whatever issue you have, go home and talk to your mom, it'll be resolved."

"Bang," a loud noise.

At a temperature nearing 34 degrees Celsius, those who had been standing for hours below began to "lose patience."

The distance between the shouting people and the girl was merely a few dozen meters.

After receiving a tip-off call from a citizen, journalist Shen Jian from a local Shanghai media outlet arrived at the scene around 2 PM. At that time, he noticed that some people nearby were already "waiting impatiently." Most had never seen this girl in the neighborhood before, and they joked about the stranger who was on the brink of life and death, discussing her profession and appearance: "She looks pretty good, maybe she chose the wrong path."

Right beside this journalist, two middle-aged men even set up a "bet": "She definitely won't jump, I bet you she won't. If she does, I'll treat you tonight." These people made Shen Jian feel as if he wasn't at a jump scene, but rather in a "lively film set."

No one knows exactly when the girl climbed out onto the fifth-floor windowsill.

Suddenly realizing that the girl upstairs was planning to jump, the elderly man urgently shouted in Shanghai dialect, "Good neighbor, please don't jump!"

On August 23rd, in this quiet old residential area in Shanghai, someone passing by Building No. 152 glanced up once and muttered, "Cleaning the windows, huh?" Then walked away. Until around 12 noon, when Grandma Jin living on the first floor went out to throw away the garbage, she saw the girl with a ponytail sitting outside the fifth-floor window, her feet dangling in the air.

Almost everyone admitted that there were hecklers at the scene, but no one claimed to have participated. Everyone pointed fingers at others.

A journalist named Wu Jiawen from a Shanghai media outlet arrived at the scene minutes before the girl fell. He remembered hearing a relaxed male voice from the crowd: "This height, jumping won't kill you."

However, others said, "A nearby shopkeeper was shouting, and some other people too. We don't know them. Anyway, not us."

Every time he spoke a few sentences, he would experience a severe bout of coughing. His hearing wasn't sharp either; sometimes, people wanting to talk to him had to loudly repeat their questions near his ear. But at 4 PM on August 23rd, he still heard the news of someone wanting to jump from a neighbor upstairs.

As soon as the girl fell, a few people discussed the incident, and like ants attracted to honey, quickly gathered together. Within a minute or two, more than a dozen people had assembled. The discussion grew louder. Among the crowd, a short-haired girl worriedly placed her index finger in front of her mouth: "Shhh, the girl has returned, don't upset her further."

Shakily raising his thin and aged arms like bamboo poles, he continuously waved them back and forth, shouting "Don't jump, don't jump."

Some saw this 93-year-old man "with nothing but laughing faces behind him, waiting for someone else's blood to exchange for a momentary thrill." A Beijing girl recalled a jump scene she witnessed at her company building: "(People) laughed below as they waited for her to jump, some even heckled urging her to jump faster. After the girl was saved, the dispersing crowd was extremely disappointed."

"Society is now very dangerous, people have lost their sense of shame and moral judgment." This professor sighed deeply, "The whole society is facing the problem of jumping off the 'morality building.' Will we choose to jump or save ourselves?"

A female police officer peeked out of the sixth-floor window, continuously talking to the girl. They also tied a bottle of mineral water to a rope, extending it to the girl from outside the window. However, the girl "never responded."

Indeed, many people had tried their best to rescue the girl. Zhou Zehua might be the most special rescuer among them all. This tall and thin elder lived not far from Building No. 152. He was 93 years old, with completely white hair and eyebrows. This frail elder almost replicated the same life every day—sitting in a wheelchair pushed by a nanny to chat with old neighbors in a small park or reading the newspaper at home with a magnifying glass.

Time passed second by second, and the girl in the white T-shirt and black pants remained seated on the windowsill, occasionally making a phone call or changing her leg position, with almost no other movements.

Seated in a rusty steel-framed wheelchair, the elder arrived downstairs at Building No. 152. There were too many people, so he had to stand up and shuffle forward step by step until reaching the caution line. He couldn't clearly see the girl's face, only noticing that she "wore a pair of sneakers, her feet swinging below the windowsill."

More than three hours later, Jiang Jianmin bowed to the rescuers amid a chorus of shouts, expressing gratitude with clasped hands. He then turned around, took a swig of liquor, and leaped off, ultimately succumbing to his injuries.
