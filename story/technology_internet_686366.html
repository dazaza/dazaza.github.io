Eight Key Points and Emergency Measures to Prevent Summer Car Spontaneous Combustion: 18671079388

by hbjndfwang on 2012-03-02 09:13:27

In view of the frequent spontaneous combustion of private cars in summer, an expert put forward eight key points for preventing the spontaneous combustion of vehicles in summer:

1. In summer, the oil circuit should be tested 1-2 times conventionally. If there is an oil leakage problem, it must be repaired in time. The rubber pipes at both ends of the oil circuit are the most likely to age and crack. If they have been used for too long, you should follow the advice of the repair service station and replace them in time.

2. Be cautious and try to avoid making changes to the circuit. If you need to modify the circuit, go to a regular car repair service station. Under the operation of professionals, the modified circuit will be safe.

3. Check the car chassis after parking to make sure there are no flammable materials under the car. Do not place flammable items such as gas lighters, air fresheners, perfumes, and mousse in areas of the car that can be easily exposed to sunlight, like the dashboard; and never keep gasoline, diesel or other dangerous oil products in the car.

4. When driving, note that when the engine is running, do not pour gasoline into the carburetor mouth; when maintaining the gasoline filter, do not burn the filter element with gasoline; do not frequently use the spark plug ignition method; avoid dripping leaks in the oil system; avoid keeping the ignition switch on for a long time after the car has stopped.

5. Do not throw unextinguished cigarette butts around in the car, and it's best not to smoke inside the car to prevent [self-immolation].

6. In summer, when the car runs for a long time under high temperature, it should rest more in the middle of the journey, and should not be exposed to the sun for a long distance.

7. Equip the car with a fire extinguisher according to regulations, and remember to replace it regularly. A fire extinguisher should not become an optional decoration. You must be familiar with how to use the fire extinguisher so that you won't be at a loss in case of an emergency.

8. The car interior materials should preferably have fire-resistant properties. Once a fire occurs, the fire will not spread easily.

When a car catches fire, whether to save it or escape depends on the situation. Generally speaking, fires in private cars within three minutes may be extinguished by oneself. If the fire burns for more than three minutes, the danger is too great, and it would be wise to abandon the car and escape. During driving, once the driver smells a burnt smell or sees smoke, he/she should stop in a safe place immediately and turn off the power. This is very important because it cuts off the car's ignition and fuel spraying, reducing the chances of fire or minimizing damage. Then pull the handbrake, leave the vehicle, and find out the cause. After discovering the fire, take the next step based on the situation.

If the fire is small, act quickly. Car fires usually start from one part and then spread. If detected early, the fire is limited to a small area with only slight smoke. At this point, the fire extinguisher in your own car can generally resolve the crisis. If there is no fire extinguisher, you can seek help from others and cover the fire source with a blanket or sand, which might also extinguish the fire.

For moderate fires, methods matter. If the engine compartment begins to smoke and flames shoot out from the gaps, the fire has developed to a relatively serious degree. Do not open the hood to prevent the airflow from intensifying the fire. Pull the lock-stop lever to expose a gap in the hood, and then spray the fire extinguishing agent inside until there is no more smoke. At this point, you can open the hood and clean up.

For large fires, run away immediately. Don't expect a small car-mounted fire extinguisher to put out a roaring fire. If effective firefighting has not been achieved within three minutes, the entire car is likely already engulfed in flames. It is difficult to reverse the situation alone. Leave immediately, evacuate passengers and onlookers or well-meaning non-professionals who want to help, to avoid injury.
