"The Woven Bag Army" becomes the "Luggage Army" - Travel Luggage Becomes a Festival Favorite

Passengers pulling luggage walk out of Fuzhou Train Station.

The 20th was the first day back to work after the Spring Festival holiday, and on the 19th, Fuzhou saw a new wave of migrant workers returning. At the exit of Fuzhou Train Station, I noticed that the hallmark of the Spring Festival travel rush — woven bags — were gradually disappearing, replaced by various stylish luggage.

Every year during the Spring Festival travel rush, what people remember most is the migrant workers returning home for the New Year, carrying large and small packages on their backs, shoulders, or dragging them along. In old newspapers, reports about the Spring Festival travel rush would always feature these big red and blue plastic-woven bags, very noticeable in the tide of returning migrant workers. These bags cost only a few dozen yuan each, cheap, durable, and with large capacity, making them the top choice for many migrant workers traveling, thus becoming a symbol of the Spring Festival travel rush.

The 19th was the day with the highest number of returning migrant workers this Spring Festival. At the exit of Fuzhou Train Station, I found that the former "woven bag army" had gradually disappeared, replaced by the "luggage army." They were neatly dressed, clean, and many even fashionable, carrying or holding neat and stylish luggage, some even branded.

A five-year-old child from Wuyishan, Lu Leqi, returned with his grandparents. They dragged three large suitcases, and the little boy earnestly helped his grandmother pull the suitcase.

Mr. Li, who has been working in Fuzhou from Chongqing for eight years, has moved up from being a delivery worker to a department manager. He said that his family lives in the countryside, where several generations have been farmers. Going out to work there is a kind of "honor," and even children call those who work outside as "money-makers." In the past, working away meant being able to buy new clothes and snacks for children, allowing the family to live better. Now it's different. After saving money from working for a few years, they can not only maintain their living but also buy motorcycles and spend three or four ten thousand yuan to build two-story brick or cement houses in their hometowns, allowing the whole family to live an enviable "well-off" life.

Mr. Li said that besides material wealth, the spiritual culture back home is also changing. Migrant workers bring back urban customs, which many villagers have learned and started adopting fashionably. Usually, they watch TV programs where city people rushing to catch flights carry stylish luggage and dress modernly. If they still use woven bags now, it doesn't match their identity, so they need to carry luggage to "adorn" themselves.
