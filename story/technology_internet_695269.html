The latest statistics show that as of January this year, the number of group-buying websites still operating normally in China was 3,790. Compared to the peak of over 5,000 such websites in 2011, nearly 2,000 have closed down. The once rapidly developing domestic group-buying industry has collapsed in just one winter, closely related to its approach of merely imitating foreign group-buying websites. Only by aligning with China's national conditions, changing development strategies, and focusing on consumer demand can the domestic group-buying industry survive the current harsh winter and welcome a second brilliant spring. Five Major Leaders of Group-Buying Websites Discuss; Expect Profitable Sites to Emerge by Q3

Five CEOs of group-buying websites interviewed by the Securities Daily all unanimously stated that 2011 was extremely brutal, but there is hope for 2012, and ultimately, no more than five group-buying websites will survive.

■ By Reporter He Jun

Although thousands of small and medium-sized group-buying websites had quietly exited the "group-buying arena" beforehand, it wasn't until the news broke before the 2012 Spring Festival that the well-known group-buying website Tuanbao was facing closure due to a broken capital chain that people truly realized that the winter for group-buying websites was not just "talk," but something that could "freeze people out."

The latest data from group-buying navigation site Tuan800 shows that as of January 2011, there were 3,790 group-buying websites nationwide, a reduction of over 1,000 compared to the peak of over 5,000 three months prior. Against the backdrop of numerous group-buying websites shutting down, some opinions suggest that group-buying and even e-commerce as a whole have entered a winter. Is it really a winter? The answer to this question exactly verifies the saying "where you sit determines what you see." For those group-buying websites at the forefront of the industry, they are pleased to see the "scattered forces" unable to continue causing trouble. In fact, during interviews with the Securities Daily, the losses of leading group-buying websites in the industry are narrowing, and some have even announced that they will turn a profit by the end of this year. Of course, from another perspective, for the vast majority of small and medium-sized group-buying websites, this winter is bone-chillingly cold, easy to start but hard to finish.

Tuan800 data shows that in January, the group-buying websites with sales exceeding 100 million yuan were Lashou (225.71 million yuan), Wowo (208.96 million yuan), Meituan (200.71 million yuan), Nuomi (102.75 million yuan), F团 (95.20 million yuan), and Mansuo (82.06 million yuan). The top ten group-buying websites accounted for nearly 92% of total sales. Although these figures may not be entirely accurate, they are sufficient to indicate trends— the dividing line between group-buying websites is already clear. Unexpectedly, the future "survivors" will likely emerge from these websites.

Thus, the understanding and grasp of the leaders of the top-ranked group-buying websites basically represent the development trend of the group-buying industry. For this reason, the Securities Daily specially interviewed the CEOs of five major group-buying websites to discuss issues of concern in the industry. Although the CEOs' views on many issues differ, there is one point that is roughly the same — 2011 was brutal, but 2012 offers hope.

How to View the Winter Theory of Group-Buying Websites

Securities Daily: Since the end of last year, a large number of group-buying websites have gone bankrupt, including well-known ones like Tuanbao which also faced a broken capital chain, attracting widespread social attention. Some believe that group-buying websites have entered a winter. What do you think about this view?

Wu Bo (CEO of Lashou): Some say that group-buying has met its winter, but in my view, group-buying is always in spring. Today's Chinese internet needs persistence! Group-buying is a long-distance race, and endurance is very important.

Xu Maodong (CEO of Wowo): From the perspective of the entire industry, we do not want to see this kind of thing happen because it has quite a significant impact on the industry, especially for merchants and consumers. Now, when the salespeople of group-buying websites go to merchants to negotiate cooperation, many merchants begin to inquire about the company qualifications and guarantee measures of group-buying websites, which did not happen before. Because merchants are worried about another "Tuanbao". This situation also prompts group-buying websites to strengthen their own management and standardization, and merchants and consumers will choose group-buying websites with qualifications and credibility for cooperation and consumption, which is something the entire industry must go through to develop healthily.

Wang Xing (CEO of Meituan): In the development of the internet, there is a "721" rule: in the end, each industry may be left with 3 companies, with the scale ratio being 7:2:1. The group-buying industry will likely not escape this pattern either. After a period of development and elimination, there may be around 3 left. Looking at the industry landscape, after the groundwork laid in the previous two years, differentiation has appeared, making the situation clearer. 2012 will be a turning point for the group-buying industry, further integrating and eliminating companies with poor service capabilities and cost control.

Shen Boyang (CEO of Nuomi): In 2011, the entire group-buying industry experienced a phase of savage growth, filled with high salaries poaching employees, losing money to compete for merchants, and other forms of unfair competition. The only benefit was educating the market in a short time, allowing more consumers to come into contact with group-buying. Now, the reshuffling of group-buying has begun, and the phase of savage growth has stopped. In 2012, group-buying websites should return to the essence of business operations. In this year, the number of times consumers choose group-buying may decrease, but the number of consumers accepting group-buying may continue to increase. At present, the group-buying winter has spread throughout the e-commerce industry, and the ones facing closure are not just small and medium-sized group-buying websites. In the future, apart from regional and specialized group-buying websites that can still survive, most national comprehensive websites will shut down, and the number of national comprehensive group-buying websites will not exceed 5.

Lin Ning (CEO of F团): Currently, for group-buying, it is the winter of capital. It is difficult for group-buying websites to secure investment, but it is absolutely not the winter of the group-buying industry. Group-buying is still in its youth. The local life service market is worth trillions, while the entire group-buying industry currently produces only about 20 billion yuan, leaving enormous room for development.

How to View the Large-Scale Closure of Group-Buying Websites

Securities Daily: Including large group-buying websites, many group-buying websites have started layoffs and closures, sparking much controversy. How do you understand this behavior of rapid expansion followed by rapid contraction in the group-buying industry?

Wu Bo: In all internet industries, none have developed like group-buying, gathering such tremendous strength in a short time, attracting so much attention and participation. And group-buying continues to innovate new models and create new value in the process of exploration and integration. The development path of group-buying ascends amidst twists and turns, just like all细分 fields of China's internet industry, following the early SNS, video paths, only with competition reaching an extreme.

Xu Maodong: Looking at China’s leading internet companies today, initially they all engaged in imitation, but eventually succeeded through innovation and surpassing others. The group-buying industry is no different—it must continuously innovate to succeed. Another factor is that the enterprise must possess a distinct corporate culture with true cohesion. Because after group-buying websites grow large, they face hundreds of city branches and thousands or even tens of thousands of employees, so the enterprise must have a unique corporate culture to unite so many post-80s young people. Currently, the vast majority of group-buying websites lack these characteristics, coupled with excessive early investments, leading to contraction, which is a normal industry adjustment.

Wang Xing: Because we expanded at a steady pace and developed with quality, we don’t need to resort to drastic layoffs now. Instead, we are still hiring against the trend. Many group-buying websites were overly eager, spending exorbitantly on promotion, with high station operation costs and human resource costs, resulting in tight capital chains, forcing them to contract and lay off employees, showing extreme irresponsibility to employees.

Shen Boyang: Group-buying is a marathon, only it burst too quickly within a short period, and the number of companies doing similar things was slightly excessive. Now, looking at it, the competition is far less intense than imagined. For the rapid expansion followed by quick contraction, those group-buying websites that got used to "big spending" in the early stages find it difficult to "tighten their belts" and survive, naturally leading to their demise.

Lin Ning: In the long term, the group-buying industry faces a talent shortage. Currently, the partial website layoffs are individual phenomena. The rapid expansion and contraction of the group-buying industry are actually normal market phenomena, similar to the video websites five years ago, starting with around 200, and after market elimination, only the largest few remain. This year, the group-buying industry should enter a further consolidation phase, ultimately leaving 3-5 of the largest enterprises in the market.
