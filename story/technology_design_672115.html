Understanding the role of collagen, and easily becoming a beautiful woman

Currently, the most popular skincare product is none other than collagen. As a woman who loves beauty and collagen, the more you understand about collagen, the closer you are to health and beauty. Below, I will take you through some relevant knowledge about collagen:

1. Collagen and Skin: Collagen is the main component of skin collagen, accounting for 70-80% of total skin protein. The collagen molecule has a triple helix structure that other proteins do not possess, and it is linked with a large number of anionic side chains. The triple helix structure and anionic side chains can firmly lock water molecules, thereby giving the skin high elasticity and tension. It can stretch outward and quickly retract. A decrease in collagen content and quality leads to a decline in elasticity. The loose skin on an elderly person's neck cannot quickly retract after being stretched.

2. Functions of Collagen:

1) Locks water molecules to maintain skin moisture and elasticity, blocks the formation of brown pigments, and promotes healthy skin (preventing spots, peeling, red blood vessels, etc.)

2) Nourishes hair making it black and shiny

3) Nourishes capillary walls: Capillary walls are also composed of collagen. A decrease in collagen content in the capillary wall makes the vessel thinner and transparent, resulting in visible red blood vessels on the face. Supplementing enough collagen can prevent the formation of red blood vessels.

4) Cell proliferation, growth, metabolism, etc.: Collagen is the nutrition and environment for cell growth, promoting cell proliferation, growth, and metabolism, which enhances human immunity and energy.

5) Promotes bone formation and prevents osteoporosis.

3. Collagen is the young molecule that keeps the skin beautiful:

With age, the content and quality of collagen in the skin gradually decrease. Collagen fibers become dry, coarse, and stiff, leading to water loss in the skin. The face shows signs of aging. The process of collagen content decreasing and collagen fiber aging begins at the age of 20.

4. Taking collagen has a significant tightening effect on the skin, making loose skin firm and powerful:

This tightening effect includes correcting facial skin sagging, leg muscle relaxation, and abdominal swelling.

5. Oral collagen can prevent and treat UV radiation damage:

UV radiation severely damages collagen fibers, causing them to age and reduce in content. Only by orally taking collagen and promoting the synthesis of new collagen in the body can UV damage to the skin be prevented.

6. Collagen fibers are the functional molecules that keep breasts firm and attractive:

1) Collagen has a unique triple helix structure, and the ligaments composed of collagen fibers act like "steel cables," firmly fixing the breasts on the skin and chest wall.

2) Collagen fibers are widely distributed within the breast. Young collagen fibers have a dense structure, like an elastic net, pulling and supporting breast tissue.

3) The collagen fibers in the breast surface skin must be youthful and strong to tighten the breast skin and make the breasts stand upright, restoring youthful charm.

7. Weight loss and collagen:

Weight loss involves expelling or sucking out excess fat accumulated under the skin through different methods. The relationship between fat and skin collagen is similar to that of "grain" and "bag." Removing some "grain" causes the "bag" to shrink. After weight loss, wrinkles appear on the face and abdominal skin sags due to the removal of fat. Oral collagen can promptly deliver highly elastic triple helix collagen fibers to the skin, tightening the skin, increasing its tension, and preventing sagging and wrinkle formation after weight loss.

8. Taking collagen has a regulatory effect on many gynecological and obstetric diseases:

Because the endometrium is composed of collagen fibers. In a normal menstrual cycle, the endometrium matures (rich in collagen fibers) and sheds (collagen fibers lost). After the endometrium falls off, the uterus needs to quickly synthesize new collagen to repair the endometrium. However, due to the lack of hydroxyproline, a raw material for synthesizing collagen in food, or excessive stress from study or work, the endometrium cannot reach sufficient thickness, leading to irregular bleeding and menstrual disorders. Many girls and women with menstrual irregularities who take collagen for 1-2 months find their menstruation becomes regular. Similarly, collagen plays a nourishing role in postpartum and post-abortion uterine recovery. Taking collagen can quickly repair the endometrium damaged during childbirth and abortion, allowing women to recover promptly.
