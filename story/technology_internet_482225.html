The braid used for communication

2010 began with "Avatar".

Cameron gave us a visually stunning feast supported by cutting-edge technology, and through it, he completed the recognition of human imagination. But what's truly impressive is that he could use unnatural elements to restore the essence of nature.

The ongoing Dakar Rally is considered a game chasing death. As usual, someone has died in this year's Dakar Rally (kc.xaiu.edu.cn/news.html). That 28-year-old Argentine female spectator was the 55th victim since the event started. It's hard for ordinary people to understand the charm of this deadly game. Men Guangyuan, a Chinese driver who has participated in this race multiple times, said, "The Dakar Rally has three key factors: people, cars, and nature. If ranked by importance, nature always comes first. This rally, from start to finish, is there to tell you that humans can never surpass nature; they can only conquer themselves."

What he said is actually the same as Cameron's point. The essence of human civilization is anti-natural. People always think about conquering nature, but eventually realize that nature cannot be surpassed. Such understanding can only be truly completed after self-conquest, which is probably the charm of the Dakar Rally.

A few days ago, a mysterious place called Cabinda, like Pandora from "Avatar", saw disaster strike without warning. Fragile lives were instantly obliterated, and the mysterious sounds of heaven turned into dirges. Before the Africa Cup of Nations began, the unfortunate Togo team suffered a terrorist attack resulting in 2 deaths and 9 injuries.

The question arises: Is a country that cannot absolutely guarantee the personal safety of participating players suitable to host an international sporting event like the Africa Cup of Nations? Should a country where 40% of its population still live below the poverty line spend $1 billion to host an Africa Cup?

Such questions are difficult for us to answer.

One thing is certain: if not for the government's strong order, Adebayor and his teammates would not have participated in the Africa Cup (mba.gdufs.edu.cn/site/default.asp). They expressed in various occasions that they need to use victories on the football field to mourn the dead and defend the dignity of their team and even their country.

A few days later (www.fi228.com), the Africa Cup commenced as scheduled, with over 50,000 spectators attending the opening match. They observed a moment of silence for the deceased and went wild for football.

In fact, on the barren African continent, football is the ticket out of poverty for people like Adebayor, a weapon many use to find dignity, and even a faith for survival for some.

In reality, whether it's football under the guns or racing in the valley of death, both are like the long braids of the Na'vi in Cameron's mirror image, the braids used to communicate with nature. This is one of the charms of competitive sports.
