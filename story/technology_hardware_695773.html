3 Hydration Tips Suggested by Experts

by zhaoboshit46 on 2012-03-07 17:14:32

1. Toner

Toner does not belong to moisturizing care. It is used to remove residues from the face after washing, and is a necessity for cleaning the skin. Therefore, skin that has already been cleaned does not need toner again. If you want to check whether your face is clean enough after washing, you can use a purer toner.

However, in the toners currently on the market with skin-cleaning functions, many other functions are added. Skin-soothing agents are added to reduce irritation to the skin, moisturizing agents improve dryness, antibacterial components disinfect acne, and there are also toners that can remove sebum. But it should be noted that the additional functions of toners may instead harm the skin. For example, alcohol contained in the added antibacterial components cannot only fail to disinfect acne but may also irritate the skin and induce other inflammations. Moreover, overly strong added moisturizing functions can also produce side effects for all oily skins.

Based on the above situation, when we choose toners, we first need to understand the nature of the product, i.e., consider what kind of side effects it may have. If you choose a product with certain moisturizing functions due to dry skin, then adding a little skincare fluid is enough. If you choose a product containing moisturizers because of oily skin, there is no need to use others.

In addition, there are quite a few products similar to toners, some of which claim to have pore-blocking effects, but in fact, because they contain alcohol components, they only cause temporary heat and tightness. If you have one kind of toner, there is no need to prepare or use other products.

2. Nutritious Milk Emulsion

Nutritious milk emulsions belong to skin moisturizers. Although their product names vary, their compositions are roughly the same. These products differ from ordinary skincare products or milk emulsions in that they are highly concentrated with nutritional components. Therefore, this type of product is mostly very expensive.

In fact, if we carefully examine the composition of these moisturizers, they are not much different from skincare fluids and milk emulsions. If we were to deliberately find differences, it would be that they contain slightly more advanced anti-oxidation and moisturizing components. That is to say, the price is high, but the composition is not much different from general products. The choice and use of such moisturizers depend on the consumer's personal financial ability. If you prefer to use high-grade moisturizing ingredients and anti-oxidation products, then you can purchase them; otherwise, ordinary cosmetics are sufficient to meet the needs of the skin. Sometimes, the moisturizing effect of ordinary milk emulsions may even exceed that of expensive nutritious milk emulsions. Therefore, if you want to complete skin care at a low cost, you can completely ignore the existence of expensive moisturizers.
