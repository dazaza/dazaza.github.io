Full-text reading network: A Japanese traveler's bicycle was stolen after arriving in Wuhan during a global cycling trip.

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-01 08:14:23

Yesterday, at the Chu River Han Street incident site, Kawahara Keiichiro showed a picture of his bicycle to passersby.

According to reports, early this morning, the bicycle that was lost by Japanese global cyclist Kawahara Keiichro in Wuhan was returned after being purchased from the black market. On the 17th, while touring Wuhan, Japanese global cyclist Kawahara Keiichiro had his bike worth 16,000 yuan stolen, which drew significant attention.

Last night, the Wuhan police announced on their official Weibo account "Safe Wuhan" that they had initiated an investigation into the theft of the Japanese cyclist's bike in Wuhan. Simultaneously, the Wuhan police will provide him with a bicycle so he can "continue his Wuhan journey."

Relevant parties in Jiangcheng and netizens moved Kawahara deeply with their efforts to help find his bike. On his blog, Kawahara previously wrote: "Wuhan, a metropolis that can rival Tokyo, is now searching for my bicycle across the entire city. Thank you! I will definitely find it; I won't give up!"

【Lost】

While shopping, he forgot the time, and his bicycle was stolen.

Yesterday, according to a friend of Kawahara Keiichiro, Kawahara is a global cyclist who has already ridden through more than 10 countries and regions alone. After passing through Shanghai, Jiangsu, and other places, he arrived in Wuhan three weeks ago.

On the afternoon of the 17th, Kawahara and his friends went to the nearby Han Street in Wuchang's Sha Lake area for sightseeing. Since Han Street is a pedestrian street and bicycles are not allowed inside, and because his bike did not have a stand, he could only park it leaning against a wall nearby. According to his traveling companion, an elderly man watching the bikes asked them to come back before 8 PM when he would finish his shift. However, Kawahara and his friends lost track of time and when they came back at 8:30 PM, the bicycle that had accompanied him through over 10 countries was nowhere to be found.

【Search】

Weibo discussions heat up as the whole city looks for the bike.

A friend took Kawahara to report the loss to the police. The next day, officers from the police station found the elderly man who was watching the bikes but did not obtain any clues regarding the missing bike.

Kawahara’s friend mentioned that because the bike wasn’t parked within the designated parking lines and they exceeded the agreed-upon time to watch the bike, the elderly man would not assume responsibility.

By 10 PM last night, news of the lost bike had spread all over the internet. A poll initiated on Weibo alone garnered participation from over 8,000 people. The Wuhan police's official Weibo account "Safe Wuhan" posted updates, stating that at 9 PM on the 17th, the Dongting Police Station of the Wuchang Public Security Bureau received a report from Kawahara Keiichiro visiting Wuhan, who claimed that his bicycle parked at the entrance of Han Street in the Dongting area had been stolen. The Wuchang police stated that they would provide Kawahara Keiichiro with a bicycle to facilitate the continuation of his Wuhan trip.
