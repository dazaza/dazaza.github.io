Search engine landscape changes again: Baidu, 360 and Tencent compete for Sogou

by geekzhang on 2013-05-09 09:29:20

According to sources in the investment community, Sohu's Sogou is currently seeking a buyer. Baidu, 360, and Tencent are all participating in the bidding process, with 360 being the most active bidder. However, Baidu has made a higher offer, and negotiations are still ongoing.

Sogou has encountered development bottlenecks, struggling to increase its market share under pressure from Baidu and 360. About half a year ago, there were indications of an intention to sell. The source mentioned that 360 offered approximately $1.4 billion for Sogou, proposing a cash plus stock swap deal. Meanwhile, Baidu has more financial resources and can offer more cash. Tencent's involvement stems from their desire to prevent Sogou from being acquired by 360.

Within Sohu, there are differing opinions on the sale of Sogou. Led by CEO Wang Xiaochuan, the Sogou team hopes to sell to 360, while Sohu Chairman Charles Zhang prefers selling to Baidu. Due to his current mental health condition, Zhang mainly relies on Wang Xiaochuan to drive the transaction but retains the final decision-making power. Based on these factors, the source judges that 360 has a higher probability of success.

If 360 successfully acquires Sogou, it could drastically alter the market landscape.

According to CNZZ data, in April 2013, Baidu held a 67.21% market share in search engines; 360 Search ranked second with 14.94%; and Sogou was third with 9.15%. If 360 successfully bids for Sogou, their combined market share would exceed one-quarter, and they would control two major search promotion channels: 360 Security Browser and Sogou Browser.

In addition, Sogou owns the market-leading Sogou Pinyin Input Method, which has 195 million active users, covering over 80% of China's internet population. It ranks alongside 360 Safe Guard and Tencent QQ as one of the three major desktop client software programs in China. A successful acquisition would allow 360 to own two out of the three major desktop clients, significantly strengthening its position in the PC internet sector.

Another source revealed that Sogou plans to go public independently within this year. The company might sell 15-20% of its shares either before or during its IPO, meaning it may introduce strategic investors rather than undergo a full acquisition. Whether through acquisition or investment, Sogou needs to find a strong partner to survive in the highly competitive search engine market.

The source disclosed that 360's chairman, Zhou Hongyi, has been very active in various capital operations, including the bid for UC, offering relatively high prices to enhance 360's search advantages. In contrast, Baidu tends to be cautious in pricing during capital operations, leading to greater optimism about the potential union between 360 and Sogou. If these two companies merge, it will disrupt the existing search engine market structure, creating a true duopoly.

When contacted, relevant responsible persons from Sogou, 360, and Baidu all stated that they would not comment at this time. Wang Xiaochuan wrote on Weibo: "Not reliable." He humorously added that with the news circulating about Baidu, the three BAT giants (Baidu, Alibaba, Tencent) could form a complete Mahjong table.
