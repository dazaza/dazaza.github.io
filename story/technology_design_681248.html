Is the suspect going to undergo a psychiatric evaluation? The reporter sought confirmation from his family and the police, but did not receive a direct response. Yesterday, on a local forum in Hefei, one netizen revealed that the parents of the suspect Tao Rukun were applying for a judicial psychiatric evaluation for their son. However, this claim has yet to be verified by relevant parties.

At 9:58 AM yesterday, a netizen named "-CICI" posted on a local forum section in Hefei, stating, "According to inside information, Tao Rukun's parents are arranging for him to undergo a psychiatric evaluation," claiming that this was certain and "believe it or not." The poster also commented that if Tao Rukun is successfully diagnosed with a mental illness, he will not bear any criminal responsibility.

Whether "-CICI's" "inside information" is true or not, from morning to evening yesterday, the reporter tried repeatedly to contact Tao Rukun's mother Xu via mobile phone, but the call went unanswered.

In the afternoon, the reporter sought confirmation from the jurisdictional police handling the case, but also did not receive a direct response. Therefore, the online claim about "Tao Rukun undergoing a psychiatric evaluation" has not been confirmed. The reporter will continue to follow up on the progress of this matter.

Tao’s father apologizes, netizens call it "showboating"

Recently, Zhou Yan's aunt posted Zhou Yan's situation online, drawing national media attention. Major portals such as Tencent, Sina, Sohu, Phoenix News, Xinhua News Agency, and People's Daily all provided related reports in prominent positions.

At 4:41 AM on February 25, 2012, Tao Wen (verified real name), Tao Rukun's father, posted an apology on Weibo: "I am Tao Wen, an employee of the Hefei Audit Bureau. Due to my failure in educating my son, I deeply regret the irreparable harm and pain caused to Zhou Yan and her family, and I sincerely apologize to the vast number of internet users. I will spare no effort in treating Zhou Yan. Tao Rukun has been detained, and the case is proceeding according to legal procedures. I will accept the legal judgment and will not evade the legal responsibilities I should bear."

As of 20:20 yesterday, this Weibo post had been forwarded 73,157 times and received 151,019 comments. Among them, celebrities like Yao Chen, Li Kaifu, Zhang Ziyi, and Chen Kun also forwarded and commented on it. In numerous comments, many netizens questioned whether Tao Wen was "showboating," using Weibo to earn "sympathy points" for Tao Rukun.

The perpetrator's statement

We have never owed a single treatment fee

The reporter contacted Tao Rukun's mother Xu, and had a ten-minute conversation with her regarding issues such as whether they had applied for bail for Tao Rukun and payments made after the incident.

Reporter: The victim's side claims that before the Spring Festival, after Tao Rukun was criminally detained, you applied for his bail and asked the victim's side to sign. Is this true?

Xu: Now everything about the case is following legal procedures. You can ask the judicial department.

Reporter: The victim's side claims that because they refused to sign the bail application, you stopped paying Zhou Yan's medical fees, leading to Zhou being discharged from the hospital last December 20 due to owing more than 100,000 yuan. What is the actual situation?

Xu: We have never owed a single treatment fee, including the initial intensive care unit and later general ward fees, all of which have been settled. Later, the other party asked us for a lump-sum compensation, the amount was too large, we didn't agree. They then said we were delaying the medical fees.

Reporter: How much did they ask for?

Xu: This is not convenient to disclose (later disclosed that they initially asked for 10 million yuan, then reduced to 6 million yuan, and finally requested 2.8 million yuan plus a residential property).

Three major doubts

Zhou Yan's disfigurement has sparked heated discussions. While everyone sympathizes with Zhou Yan's plight and condemns Tao Rukun's violent behavior, there are also discussions about the many doubts surrounding the case.

One, Why did it take five months to expose the case?

"We didn't want to make a big deal out of it, but now we're really at our wit's end," said Zhou Yan's mother Li Cong. "She is still a child, and at the time, I was worried that making a big deal out of it would cause her even greater harm." The reason they sought help from society five months after the incident was because they couldn't afford Zhou Yan's continued treatment. "We hope to attract social attention so that this case can be fairly and reasonably resolved."

Two, Were Tao and Zhou in a romantic relationship?

Tao Wen, Tao Rukun's father, posted online on February 26: "In early 2010, Tao Rukun started an early romance with Zhou Yan. Although we strongly opposed it, their feelings remained good." Tao Wen also claimed that the reason Tao Rukun did such an extreme act was because "Zhou Yan had another boyfriend, and Tao Rukun could not handle it properly."

Zhou Yan's family responded online. "This is completely fabricating excuses for Tao Rukun and trying to tarnish Zhou Yan's reputation." Zhou Yan's father said, "We firmly oppose early romance. Besides, in the year before the incident, Zhou Yan was studying at home, always with us. How could she possibly have another boyfriend?"

Three, Did they demand exorbitant compensation?

Tao Wen posted online that after the incident, the Zhou family demanded 10 million yuan in compensation from him, "which was reduced to 6 million yuan last December and further reduced to 2.8 million yuan plus a house on February 12 this year."

"Our family could never propose such astronomical figures like 10 million or 6 million yuan," said Zhou Yan's father. The Zhou family did not proactively propose compensation, but rather Tao's family kept asking them for a specific compensation figure, "out of necessity for continuing our daughter's treatment, we reluctantly proposed a compensation of 2.8 million yuan and a house."
