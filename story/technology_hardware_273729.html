Beijing will conduct its first centralized rectification of the electronic air ticket market. It was reported yesterday (May 28) that from today until October 31, Beijing will for the first time conduct a centralized rectification of the electronic air ticket market, strictly investigating unlicensed operations, unauthorized establishment of sales branches and other illegal acts. The Civil Aviation Building in Xidan and other areas have become key targets for rectification.

The scope of this rectification includes airline self-operated institutions, agency institutions, part-time companies, network service companies selling aviation e-tickets, as well as individuals and entities illegally selling aviation e-tickets. The areas around Xidan Civil Aviation Building, Capital Airport, and Nanyuan Airport are listed as key areas for rectification. The industrial and commercial department will severely investigate and punish those who arbitrarily set up sales branches using the virtual nature of aviation e-tickets to sell them. Those who do not clearly inform consumers of the conditions for signing, changing, or returning tickets, overstate the amount of the ticket in the itinerary, or deceive consumers with forged itineraries will be strictly investigated according to the 'Consumer Protection Law'. Those with serious circumstances will be handed over to the public security organs.
