Qu Limin's disrespect for her predecessors is a sign of lacking cultural background.

by zxyasdu40 on 2011-07-01 14:28:53

Qu Limin's disrespect for the sages is a sign of lack of cultural heritage, NetEase Blog 2.

Recently, I read an article on Qu Limin's new book online. The article quoted a passage from her book: "Why doesn't the medical world promote the Ba Wei Shen Qi Pill, which is such a good medicine, but only promotes the Liu Wei Di Huang Pill? It's because for three or four hundred years, China's medical community has believed that everyone in the world is deficient. Since Zhu Danxi's time, the nourishing yin school has been prevalent. A doctor prescribes certain medicines based on a fixed group of patients they serve. Zhu Danxi himself was not from a prestigious family, and he failed his 'college entrance exam'. Later, he studied Neo-Confucianism, and after learning Song-Ming Neo-Confucianism, he befriended many officials. These officials indulged in alcohol, sex, wealth, and power, and Zhu Danxi particularly liked to use nourishing yin methods to replenish them."

The above passage is full of flaws, here we will only make a brief analysis. First, for the past three or four hundred years, China's medical community has never thought that everyone in the world is deficient. This is merely her subjective assumption, indicating that she has not studied the history of medicine well. Secondly, anyone who has studied Traditional Chinese Medicine knows that Zhu Danxi was one of the Four Great Masters of the Jin-Yuan period, the founder of the Nourishing Yin School, and made great contributions to the development of Traditional Chinese Medicine. However, Qu Limin mocks Zhu Danxi as someone who "failed the college entrance exam"! As descendants, we should respect our predecessors, which is a traditional virtue of China. One should not become arrogant and conceited just because they have gained some fame. Qu Limin's statement calling Zhu Danxi "someone who failed the college entrance exam" shows extreme disrespect for the great Traditional Chinese Medicine master Zhu Danxi! Disrespect for the sages also indicates a lack of proper cultivation in herself. Moreover, Qu Limin's concept of talent development is also problematic. Passing the college entrance exam simply means obtaining the opportunity for higher education, right? Does failing the exam mean one cannot become talented? Graduating from university does not necessarily guarantee becoming talented either. Those who succeed in the college entrance exam are not necessarily useful members of society, and there are many successful people who did not attend university. This single statement exposes Qu Limin's outdated concept of talent development and her lack of traditional Chinese cultural heritage.
