Warning: Don't be a book hoarder!

It seems like we are always searching, looking for the secret to getting rich overnight, looking for the ultimate SEO techniques that can get you to the first page in just a few days.

We keep downloading e-books on how to find niches and how to create profitable websites, constantly reading RSS feeds from various sites, storing over 2000 English emails in our Gmail accounts, all under the pretense of saving them to read later.

I want to read books, so I bought "The Absolute Close," "Practical Online Marketing Codes," and "Meeting the Unknown Self," but most of the time, they just end up being collected and not read.

A lot of time is wasted in searching and downloading, while very little time is left for thinking and taking action.

The essence of guerrilla warfare is: concentrate superior forces, annihilate the enemy one by one. Cutting off ten fingers is not as effective as cutting off one finger. Remember this well, those who deeply understand its meaning will benefit endlessly.

Let me tell a story about Lord Thousand Swords:

Once upon a time, there was a young lord who wanted to become the best martial artist in the world, but he lacked confidence because there was no standard for being the best. As time went by, a belief formed in his mind: if he could collect a thousand famous swords from around the world, he could declare himself successful and the best martial artist. So he kept collecting without end. To obtain these famous swords, he didn’t hesitate to use the most despicable means. Finally, he had collected nine hundred ninety-nine swords. He was excited but also a bit scared. To get the last sword, he gave up everything – his humanity, his wife, everything. In the end, he got what he wanted. Just as he was celebrating his success with great joy, a very cheap sword ended his brief glory. A sword is not meant to be used for killing people; it’s not meant to be collected. Even a very cheap sword can kill.

Simple actions practiced to perfection become masterstrokes.

No matter how many weapons or tools you have, if you don’t know how to use them properly or only know the surface, it’s worse than having nothing at all. It's merely a waste of time and energy.

Information entering the brain becomes wisdom, but carrying it around will only weigh you down.

Be a wise person, don't become a donkey carrying books.
