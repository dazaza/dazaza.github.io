Why are environmental protection barriers for enterprises so vulnerable in the face of serious natural disasters?

by ditan360 on 2010-08-03 20:37:36

Yitan News - At present, floods are rampant, natural disasters occur frequently. Severe natural disasters not only test the government's ability to deal with disasters, but also test whether the environmental protection barriers of enterprises are solid. The environmental protection system built over many years has proven its worth at this time. However, in the face of severe natural disasters, the environmental protection barriers of many enterprises in our country are fragile and pollution incidents occur frequently.

On July 3, a copper acid water seepage accident occurred at the Zijin Mountain Copper Mine hydrometallurgical plant of Fujian Zijin Mining Group. The accident caused serious pollution in some areas of the Ting River. Relevant personnel said that this pollution accident was largely related to the heavy rain.

At around 18:50 on the evening of July 16, an explosion occurred in Dalian's oil pipeline, causing crude oil leakage.

In the morning of July 28, an explosion occurred at Nanjing Plastic Factory No.4 in Qixia District of Nanjing, injuring more than 300 people.

On the evening of July 28, material drums containing trimethylchlorosilane from a factory in Jilin Province were washed into the Songhua River by floods, causing local environmental pollution.

It is not difficult to find from the above accidents that natural environmental disasters have become the fuse for man-made environmental disasters. The occurrence of natural environmental disasters highlights the environmental problems of enterprises and ultimately leads to disasters. If the efforts of numerous enterprises across the country in environmental protection do not meet the standards, then such disasters may trigger a chain reaction in the face of irresistible natural disasters, spreading nationwide. This will lead to terrible consequences.

Faced with such frequent environmental accidents, we can't help but ask: why are the environmental protection barriers of enterprises so fragile in the face of natural disasters! The state repeatedly requires enterprises to pay attention to environmental protection and orders enterprises that do not meet environmental protection standards to rectify. But why is the effect so poor? A single heavy rain shower can destroy the environmental protection barrier of an enterprise!

There is no doubt that in recent years, China's attention to environmental protection has reached an unprecedented height, and environmental protection policy documents have been issued frequently. However, these documents often cannot be effectively implemented at the local level, becoming empty words. Local enterprises deal with their own environmental issues perfunctorily. When small problems occur, the local government adopts a lenient and indulgent approach. Meanwhile, there is a lack of effective public opinion supervision at the local level, neither government supervision nor public supervision can play a role, and enterprises lack necessary responsibility, leading to a laissez-faire attitude.

When natural disasters do not strike, enterprises underestimate the hidden dangers of safety accidents and take them lightly, assuming everything will be fine. When small disasters come and accidents happen, they shift the blame onto natural disasters, finding hundreds of reasons to negotiate with relevant departments and shirk responsibility. They never question themselves about whether they have a high sense of responsibility or whether they have done enough in environmental protection. This is a common practice of some enterprises today.

Natural disasters are unavoidable, but the environmental protection barriers of enterprises can absolutely be made unbreakable. Only in this way can the safety of people's production and life be guaranteed.
