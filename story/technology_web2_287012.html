Fingerprint attendance machine draws controversy

by tuoqing05 on 2009-12-01 01:50:30

Pro: Improve management efficiency

Con: Invade personal privacy

How to reconcile the contradiction?

It is understood that companies using such attendance machines almost never signed relevant confidentiality agreements with employees. Since there was no confidentiality agreement, individuals had the right to refuse fingerprint attendance. The law protects personal privacy and also protects an individual's control over their own fingerprint information.

In other words, whether or not to disclose one's personal privacy, whether or not to disclose one's fingerprints, is an individual's right, and others have no right to force it.

So, can it be considered that there is an irreconcilable contradiction between fingerprint attendance and individual rights? Is fingerprint attendance a "dead end"?

Legal scholars believe that fingerprint attendance is actually a game between management authority and personal privacy rights. For companies, the purpose of fingerprint attendance is to improve management efficiency, and the resulting infringement on personal privacy rights is essentially caused by the expansion of management authority. In fact, personal privacy rights are not unadjustable. Based on individual voluntariness, privacy rights can also be relaxed. However, the premise is that the company needs to make some adjustments in terms of management authority, which means signing relevant management agreements equally on the basis of respecting the personal rights of employees.

Just pressing your finger on the machine makes information about being late or leaving early immediately clear. Nowadays, an advanced device called a fingerprint attendance machine is becoming popular in some companies in Shanghai. However, with the appearance of the fingerprint attendance machine, debates have continued: supporters claim that this attendance method is an inevitable result of technological development and is beneficial for company management; opponents accuse the attendance machine of collecting employee fingerprints, infringing on employee privacy.

Company: Not related to invasion of privacy

Yesterday morning, at the Yibai Lotus Shipyard store, I saw a fingerprint attendance machine near the entrance, about the size of a telephone, with the employee ID number, name, and arrival time displayed on the screen after accepting attendance.

According to Ms. Xiang Jun from the Brand Department of Yibai Lotus Shanghai Company, currently most of the 16 stores of Yibai Lotus use fingerprint attendance machines. In the past, when using punch cards for attendance, sometimes there would be cases of someone punching attendance for another person. Later, from the first day new employees started working at Yibai Lotus, "the company informed them that fingerprint attendance would be used. After investigation, 95% of employees can accept the use of fingerprint attendance by the company."

The MeiMei Department Store on Huaihai Road has also just switched to this product, but the head of the personnel department of the company is unwilling to comment on the effect of using this attendance machine.

So what is the role of fingerprint attendance? A manager of a company using fingerprint attendance frankly said: Traditional attendance methods give some dishonest employees many opportunities to escape attendance or falsify records. From this perspective, fingerprint attendance is a feasible but unavoidable method adopted by enterprises. For those being managed, it is "roll call," and it has nothing to do with "infringement of employee privacy."

Employee: The company's approach seems inappropriate

However, an employee of Yibai Lotus believed that this kind of management is a form of unequal compliance. If the company leaks the employee's fingerprint, it could cause trouble. He thought that the company had no right to collect the employee's fingerprint, and at the same time, the company's use of fingerprint attendance machines constitutes intentional infringement of the employee's personal freedom and privacy rights.

Regarding this, Ms. Xiang believed that it cannot be said to infringe on the employee's right to privacy, "Only when the employee's fingerprint is used for commercial activities does it constitute an invasion of privacy. Yibai Lotus uses the employee's fingerprint only for attendance; when the employee leaves the company, the fingerprint will be deleted, and it will not be used for any other purpose."

Lawyer Qiu Bin said that whether or not it constitutes an infringement on the employee's personal freedom and privacy mainly depends on the company's motivation and consequences of using the fingerprint attendance machine. According to the principle of "freedom without prohibition," if the company uses the fingerprint attendance machine only for employee attendance evaluation, without using the provided fingerprint information for illegal activities or deliberately disclosing it externally, and without causing other harm to the employee, it does not constitute an infringement on the employee's personal freedom under the law.

The question is, what if the employee's fingerprint information is accidentally leaked? Lawyer Jiang Xian of Shanghai United Law Firm emphasized that the storage and protection of employee fingerprints is also an issue. If there is a leak in the future, the company should bear the corresponding legal responsibility.
