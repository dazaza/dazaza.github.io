Callaway ERC Hyper Driver: While out on the course, there are several ways to move between holes and transport your golf bag. First, there's always the golf cart, which is a quick way to get around the course. However, this method has drawbacks as it can be expensive to purchase or you may have to rent one every time you play a round. Additionally, using a cart means you don't fully enjoy the scenery, especially if you're new to the course. Walking with your bag over your shoulder is another option, but it risks injuring your shoulder or at least making it sore. Carrying your golf bag for nine or eighteen holes will surely dampen your evening. This is why using golf trolleys is an excellent alternative.

Electric golf trolleys are compact devices designed to carry your golf bag. With motorized assistance, you can gently push the bag with one hand as it rolls across the green and up to the path between holes. The base of the trolley is slightly larger than your golf bag, featuring a metal backstop that slopes upward at a slight angle. The golf bag rests comfortably against the backstop, and the design ensures it stays in place whether pushing it or keeping it stationary. The backstop transitions into a single handle, allowing you to push your bag around with one hand if you prefer. As you start pushing the trolley, the motorized assist engages to help turn the wheels, reducing the effort needed to transport the bag from one hole to another. Steering is simple; just twist the handle left or right to change direction. When pushing the trolley, the front wheel is usually slightly off the ground, giving you a better angle for pushing, though it’s still possible to push the bag with all three wheels on the ground. This is more about comfort than necessity. When the trolley is stationary, all three wheels rest on the ground while the bag leans slightly against the back of the trolley.

For anyone seeking an easy and comfortable way to move between holes without spending too much, golf trolleys are the ideal choice.
