What is online shopping?

Since the rapid development of the Internet in our country, it has provided a good platform for online shopping. Now, online shopping has quickly become a fashion, an irresistible trend, and even a fixed lifestyle for a considerable number of people. So, what exactly is online shopping? For non-professionals, this is still a rather vague concept.

So-called "online shopping", as its name suggests, refers to shopping on the internet. That is, browsing, selecting, purchasing, bargaining, and payment are all realized online, ultimately completing a buying and selling transaction. The convenience of online shopping allows you to enjoy the fun of shopping without leaving your home. All you need is a computer and an internet connection to browse through countless items. A simple click of the mouse takes you from "browsing" to paying for your purchases effortlessly. Then you just wait for your items to be delivered. This is the product of the E-era - online shopping. To describe this fashionable and trendy lifestyle with one Chinese character, "easy" would be the most fitting.

Currently, there are two basic types of online shopping related to ordinary consumers: B2C mode and C2C mode.

B2C mode is the earliest form of e-commerce in our country, which stands for Business-to-Consumer in English, meaning business to consumer. In other words, it's the usual commercial retail where companies directly sell products and services to consumers. Currently, the payment method for B2C e-commerce combines cash-on-delivery with online payments. Most enterprises choose to outsource logistics to save on operating costs.

Representative websites: Dangdang, Joyo.

C2C refers to Consumer-to-Consumer e-commerce. For example, if a consumer has an old computer and sells it to another consumer via an online auction, this type of transaction is called C2C e-commerce.

C2C e-commerce mainly refers to online auctions. Its characteristic is mass-market transactions, as it involves personal-to-personal transactions.

Representative websites: Taobao, eBay.

Whether it's B2C or C2C, as forms of e-commerce, their indispensable condition is a network environment. Without access to the internet, naturally, there can be no talk of online shopping. Additionally, the other two important conditions - logistics and electronic currency - are what all consumers need to learn how to use and care about during the online shopping process. We will elaborate on their functions and usage methods in later chapters.

Why choose online shopping?

In comparison, the biggest feature and advantage of online shopping can be summed up in six words: convenient, fast, economical.

1. Convenience. The convenience of online shopping manifests in two aspects: more choices and the ability to shop anytime from home.

The amount of information available on the internet is immeasurable, and search technology fully showcases its advantages. If you see a product in a physical store and want to compare its price and variety, you would have to personally visit many stores, which is time-consuming and labor-intensive, and the information obtained may not be comprehensive. However, online shopping allows you to compare prices across hundreds of stores, breaking the limitation of having to physically go to malls. You can check out products that interest you from the comfort of your own home, compare prices and quality, and have more options. Shopping websites, especially one-stop shopping sites, list hundreds of thousands of products, similar to a department store. You can virtually browse through the website, and each time you click on a product, you can see detailed information about it, akin to receiving an explanation from a salesperson on-site.

Online shopping also breaks geographical restrictions. Regardless of where you are located, you can enjoy the same range of products offered by the internet, giving you the opportunity to buy items that might be hard to find in your local market. For instance, if you like IKEA products but there isn't an IKEA specialty store in your city, in the past, this might have been a source of frustration. However, with online shopping, you can simply choose an online proxy service, and everything is easily taken care of.

Friends who enjoy shopping know that shopping is not only a pleasure but also a physical activity, especially in larger cities where traveling to any place often requires multiple bus transfers, which is both time-consuming and labor-intensive. Online shopping completely overturns traditional shopping methods; you don't even need to leave your house, so you don't have to worry about the weather or wait until the weekend, nor do you have to concern yourself with when the mall closes. As long as you have a computer and an internet connection, you can shop whenever you want, day or night. Even at 2 AM, you can switch from one store to another in just a few seconds, satisfying your shopping desires and needs at any time, freely and without having to deal with salespeople's pitches.

2. Speed. Modern people value efficiency; time is everything.

Anyone who has tried postal shopping knows how slow it can be; back and forth, it takes half a month to a month. If there are issues, returning or exchanging goods takes another month. This speed in today's information age is simply unbearable. Imagine ordering the latest smartphone model to lead a trend among friends, but after a month, when you receive the phone, several new models have already hit the market. How would you feel then?

The greatest advantage of online shopping, besides saving effort, is saving time. It eliminates the time spent running around between stores. You can enter the item you want to buy at any time online and instantly obtain all the detailed information about the product. After comparing and choosing, you can make a payment through a convenient electronic bank transfer in just a minute. Within a few days, express delivery will bring the item to your door. Such a fashionable way of shopping, why wouldn't you embrace it?

3. Economy. Besides being convenient and fast, the most attractive aspect of online shopping for consumers is its affordability. It satisfies the majority of people's desire for good quality at low prices. Online stores don't have to pay rent, water, electricity, etc., allowing sellers to effectively reduce costs. The internet also provides many sellers with a platform to showcase their advantages. Online stores generally have special procurement channels, controlling first-hand sources, or even direct sales from manufacturers, eliminating the profit margins of intermediaries in the wholesale and retail process. Even with shipping fees added, the price of goods remains low. Additionally, group buying, bidding, and other online shopping methods allow you to get desired items at very low prices.

Precisely because online shopping has such charm, fewer and fewer people can resist its allure. Let's look at some data: in the third quarter of 2006, the average amount spent per person on online shopping was 572 yuan; while in the same period in 2005, the average annual amount spent per person on online shopping was 273 yuan, increasing by 109%. This is a trend!
