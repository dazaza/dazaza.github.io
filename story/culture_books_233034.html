"Tiger Ben Wan Sui" A War Memory Across Time and Space

by jqrxass on 2009-09-04 10:36:40

Zhang Henshui meticulously narrates the personnel, attack and defense, equipment, and casualties in each sporadic battle. Through the elaboration of numerous details, he constructs the image of the valiant and tragic 57th Army. Many grand yet plain scenes of tragedy cannot fail to move the reader.

"Ten Thousand Ages for the Tiger Ben," Part One

The people in this city, those who left have gone, and those who stayed are busy packing their valuables at home and nailing their doors shut. Tomorrow, November 15th, is the last day of evacuation. The division headquarters and the county government have repeatedly posted announcements that not a single citizen will be left in the city. Therefore, this is the second-to-last day of the mass evacuation, and the citizens are preparing to have their last dinner in the city. Smoke from several chimneys on rooftops has risen, and above the smoke, there are groups of crows flying quietly back to their nests.

"Ten Thousand Ages for the Tiger Ben," Part Two

After everyone had eaten their fill, this road was quickly traversed. From Changde to Gaoqiao is a highway, but after leaving Gaoqiao Town, they walked on stone-paved small roads. Occasionally, they encountered one or two groups of ragged refugees, but it was rather quiet. After walking for seven or eight more miles, two reconnaissance aircraft appeared, flying towards them. Since all three were wearing military uniforms, they couldn't avoid hiding, so they immediately hid in the pine forest on the hill.

"Ten Thousand Ages for the Tiger Ben," Part Three

Cheng Jianren said to Deputy Battalion Commander Wang: "The division commander often says that the people of Changde are good, and the people of He Fu are even better, which is absolutely true. However, I can clarify one thing: the Chinese people are all good; it's just that the army doesn't know how to utilize them. I am from the north, and I know that northerners can cooperate with the army in combat. In recent years, Shandong has been behind enemy lines, and the stories of the common people fighting against the enemy and conducting guerrilla warfare sound like tales from the 'Investiture of the Gods'."

"Ten Thousand Ages for the Tiger Ben," Part Four

Huang Jiumei saw Wang Biao looking so pitifully dumbfounded, nodded to him, and immediately turned around and walked away. Wang Biao, seeing her walk far away, wanted to call out "Nine Sister," but dared not shout it out loud. His lips moved slightly, but she naturally didn't hear and returned to the warehouse on her own. Wang Biao muttered to himself with a smile: "Time reveals the true nature of people; she treats me quite well." Standing there staring blankly at the empty alley for a while, he then remembered that Staff Officer Cheng called him out to find cigarettes, and now his hands were still empty.

"Ten Thousand Ages for the Tiger Ben," Part Five

Madam Huang sighed: "Not to mention the bombardment and aerial bombing, just burning twice a day would reduce Changde to ashes." Jiumei said: "I truly wish I could hold a gun and fight a battle, to vent this anger. The way Japanese devils treat the Chinese is simply too overbearing." As she spoke, Wang Biao suddenly rushed in from the alley entrance, running up to them, saluting, and saying "Report," before abruptly stopping. He realized that this wasn't an occasion to report to any superior officer, so he laughed awkwardly, stuttering, and called out "Dry Mother."
