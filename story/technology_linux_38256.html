Six Questions You Must Know Before Installing Linux

Before installing the Linux operating system, users should first understand the following six questions:

1. How many versions of the Linux operating system are currently available?

According to DistroWatch.com, there are currently at least 350 versions of Linux. Due to support from Sun CEO Jonathan Schwartz, Ubuntu has recently gained popularity in the Linux market.

Next is SUSE, provided by Novell, which comes in both free and commercial editions. The commercial edition requires payment but offers technical support from Novell. Additionally, major Linux distributors include Red Hat, Mandriva, Debian, Fedora, Slackware, Knoppix, Gentoo, and MEPIS, among others.

2. What companion applications come with the Linux system?

In the office software category, the most well-known is Sun's StarOffice. Similar to Microsoft Office, it includes word processing, spreadsheets, and presentation applications. Typically, OpenOffice is bundled with various Linux systems such as Red Hat and Novell.

Besides OpenOffice, other office suites that support Linux include GNOME Office and KOffice. In the browser market, options supporting Linux include Firefox and Konqueror browsers.

3. How can I listen to music on a Linux system?

For home users, aside from word processing and other office applications, one of the most commonly used applications on Linux is the music player. Currently, music players that support Linux include RealPlayer and amaroK from KDE.

For Linux users, purchasing music tracks can be challenging. Apple’s iTunes, Yahoo Music, and Urge do not support Linux. As for Rhapsody, users can only stream music online but cannot purchase it. In comparison, MP3tunes.com can partially meet the needs of Linux users who wish to buy music tracks, with each song costing 88 cents.

4. What desktop environments are available for Linux?

Here, the term "desktop" refers to the "user interface." Currently, Linux primarily offers two desktop environments: GNOME and KDE. The differences between these two environments are more about form than technology.

Compared to GNOME, KDE is closer to Windows in terms of user experience, while GNOME is characterized by its faster running speed. Many vendors, including Red Hat and Novell, offer Linux systems that support both GNOME and KDE desktop environments.

5. Is Linux more suitable for servers or end-users?

Frankly speaking, Linux is better suited as a server system. Currently, Linux has not gained sufficient recognition in the desktop market. Analyst Rob Enderle believes that Linux may never rise in the desktop market.

According to authoritative data, the usage ratio of Linux in the desktop market is less than 3%. However, this does not deter Linux vendors from pursuing their determination and confidence to enter the desktop market. Many Linux advocates believe that the rise of Linux in the desktop market is just around the corner.

6. How do you install the Linux system?

First, download the ISO image of the Linux operating system, then burn it directly into a bootable CD. It is worth noting that you should not install the Linux system on the same hard drive as Windows. It is best to have two hard drives installed; when using the Linux system, disconnect the data cable of the hard drive with Windows. When using the Windows system, disconnect the hard drive with Linux. For detailed information, users can refer to related books.
