How does the years-old "poison milk" reappear?

by 60888888 on 2010-02-05 10:55:01

Today, my headline is "How the resurgence of tainted milk powder happened". Recently, there has been a rampant outbreak of toxic milk powder, and several provinces have been affected. The three Northeast provinces may have become the epicenter of this "poisoned milk powder" spread. Most of the problematic milk candies have flowed into the three Northeast provinces and are currently being recalled. The tainted milk candies in the Guangdong region have all been recalled.

Hundreds of cases of "problematic milk candies" are still in circulation. On February 3rd, the Shaanxi Provincial Public Security Department announced that the Zhangzhou Southern Food Company from Fujian's Changzhou Xiangcheng district and the Zhenmei Company from Chao'an county in Guangdong used leftover melamine-tainted milk powder from 2008 to produce milk candies. Out of the 1,148 cases of milk candies produced by the Zhangzhou Southern Food Company, only 164 cases were sealed off, while the rest are still being recalled. "More than 50% of our milk candies were sold in the three Northeast provinces (Jilin, Liaoning, Heilongjiang), with a small portion sold in Guangdong and other regions. Currently, the company has halted milk candy production and is fully recalling the problematic milk candies," said Mr. Ke, the head of the Zhangzhou Southern Food Company's crisis response team for the tainted milk candy incident, in an interview with the *Everyday Economic News* on February 4th. As of now, out of the total 1,148 cases of problematic milk candies, 164 cases have been sealed, 109 cases have been recalled, and another five to six hundred cases are still in the process of being recalled. All the tainted milk candies in the Guangdong region have been fully recalled.

The website of the Zhangzhou Southern Food Company shows that it mainly produces candies, jelly, and saqiama, with its milk candy brand named "Little White Mouse." Its sales network covers most provinces in the country.

According to the numbers provided by Mr. Ke, there are still 300 to 400 cases of problematic milk candies in the market or already consumed by consumers.

For those who have already consumed the tainted milk candies, how will the company compensate them? Why is the milk candy named "Little White Mouse"? Is the company treating consumers as guinea pigs for experiments? Besides the three Northeast provinces and Guangdong, which other provinces have the tainted milk candies circulated?

In response to multiple questions from reporters, Mr. Ke refused to answer, only saying to keep an eye on the news updates from the Zhangzhou City Food Safety Office, Zhangzhou City Quality and Technology Supervision Bureau, and Zhangzhou City Health Bureau websites. However, checking these websites revealed no relevant information.

Ningxia Tian Tian Dairy continues normal production

According to previous announcements from the Ministry of Health, apart from two products produced by Weinan LeKang Dairy in Shaanxi having不合格melamine levels, the full-fat powdered milk produced by Wuzhong Tian Tian Dairy Co., Ltd. in Ningxia (hereinafter referred to as Ningxia Tian Tian Dairy) was also found to be substandard. On February 3rd, this reporter called Chairman Guo Jianxin, who claimed that the product was qualified.

If the product is indeed qualified, will Guo Jianxin seek clarification from the Ministry of Health?

On the afternoon of yesterday (February 4th), when the reporter tried calling Guo Jianxin again, his phone was constantly off. However, on the morning of February 4th, Guo Jianxin told another media outlet that the company was applying for a re-inspection, and the enterprise was continuing normal production. Meanwhile, there were reports on March 3rd indicating that the company was still conducting normal sales.

Deputy Director Yang of the Consumer Protection Division of the Ningxia Hui Autonomous Region Administration for Industry and Commerce introduced to local media that from February 1st to February 7th, the Ningxia industrial and commercial departments were carrying out a grid-based inspection of a batch of dairy products containing melamine published in 2008. From the situation from February 1st to February 3rd, no "toxic milk powder" has yet been found in the current distribution field.

However, according to messages from the Guangzhou Municipal Health Bureau, a bakery in Conghua District of Guangzhou purchased melamine-tainted milk powder from Ningxia, but did not disclose whether the problem milk powder was purchased from Ningxia Tian Tian Dairy. It was reported that this bakery made and sold bread daily, but the products had not flowed into the urban area of Guangzhou. Currently, the store is under suspension for rectification. Nevertheless, relevant officials from Guangzhou’s health supervision department stated that it is still unclear who should manage the issue of the problem milk powder. Officials from the Guangzhou Health Bureau Legal Supervision Division also expressed "unclearness", saying they do not know if the local industry and commerce departments or quality supervision departments are clear. If none of them are clear, then who should be the clearest? Could it be the consumers? Or could it be the unscrupulous merchants who have lost all sense of morality? I'm too tired to criticize the "each managing their own segment" food safety regulatory system anymore, and too tired to doubt the grandiose campaign-style law enforcement—please! Even if it's just a temporary crackdown, this wind must blow fiercely!
