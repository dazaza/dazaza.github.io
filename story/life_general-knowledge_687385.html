Two Stories, A Lifetime

We often hear people sigh: a lifetime is not long, but also not short! Most of us spend our whole lives yearning, pursuing, taking, and giving. We do this for love, happiness, sincerity, and joy, not for pain, tears, doubt, and suspicion. In our lives, as long as we receive the appreciation, attention, tenderness, and true feelings from one person, along with their tears, it’s enough!

Regardless of whether it's sweetness or sorrow, love or heartbreak, be it enduring together through hardship or holding each other's hands to grow old together, there are two stories that interpret our lifelong emotions and love. It's about clearly understanding each other and genuinely communicating so that we can feel each other's love and bring joy to each other.

At a newlywed couple's wedding, the master of ceremonies held up a hundred-dollar bill and asked everyone present if anyone wanted it; they should raise their hand. Everyone thought it might be a prank by the MC, so no one spoke. The MC said: "I'm serious. If you want it, please raise your hand."

Finally, someone raised their hand, followed by more and more people raising theirs. The MC looked at everyone and swapped the bill for an older-looking hundred-dollar note. Fewer people raised their hands this time. The MC smiled again and then exchanged it for a crumpled, slightly damaged old hundred-dollar bill. But now, only a few people in the audience raised their hands. The MC invited a little boy onto the stage and placed the old bill in his hand. He said: "Because he kept his hand raised," which made the crowd burst into laughter. The little boy's face turned a bit red, but the MC gestured for everyone to quiet down and took out the new hundred-dollar bill, saying: "I'll exchange this new one for your old one, okay?" The little boy replied: "No need, thank you, Uncle. New or old, they're both worth a hundred dollars."

The MC nodded, allowed the boy to take the money and go back to his seat, and then had the bride and groom hold hands and walk onto the stage. He said: "Even the most beautiful faces will age one day. Even the most romantic love will change over time with life's changes. Just like the bills in my hand, they will gradually become wrinkled and old over time. But just as the little boy said, whether new or old, they're still worth a hundred dollars. Their value doesn't change because of the wrinkles on them, right? I hope the newlyweds can understand the true value and meaning of love. Don't forget the vows you just made to love each other forever when you look old or when passion turns into calmness. Please cherish each other for a lifetime."

The newlyweds glanced at each other and deeply nodded. Immediately, thunderous applause erupted from the audience.

Another story goes like this:

It's a small world after all, just as birds migrate south in winter and north in spring. A southern girl married a northern man. The girl liked light flavors, while the man couldn’t live without spicy food.

The girl often went to her parents' house for meals. One day, her father made the dishes a bit salty. Her mother quietly took a glass of water, dipped her chopsticks into a dish, swirled it in the clear water before eating it. Suddenly, the girl understood something profound from her mother's subtle actions.

The next day, the girl made her husband's favorite dishes at home. Of course, every dish had chili peppers. However, in front of her, there was an extra glass of water. The man watched her enjoy the dishes soaked in water with satisfaction, and his eyes became slightly moist.

Afterward, the man also competed to cook meals. But there were no longer any chilies in the dishes. Instead, in front of him, there was an additional plate of chili sauce. He dipped the dishes into the chili sauce before eating them, and with every bite, he felt completely satisfied.

For love, and for themselves, one clung to a plate of chili sauce, and the other clung to a glass of water. They better understood how to maintain a lasting and steady love.

Deep love needs no words. Love isn’t about formality or needing to boast. Love is the flower of happiness that fills the hearts of those who understand it with its fragrance; love is the oar that ferries true feelings, allowing each person with emotion to fearlessly face the length of life. When a simple dish is seasoned with love, how could the heart fail to taste the flavor of happiness!

These two stories are actually our entire lifetime.

Love certainly requires understanding and comfort! When we grow old, perhaps we can still watch the sunset together—wouldn’t that be a different kind of romance?
