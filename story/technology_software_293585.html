Coal Sector: The King Returns ＿ Stock Market Basics

by ww0982993 on 2009-12-05 03:39:22

Based on this week's observation of the market, we believe that the style of the market is transforming as we predicted last week when the market just started to plummet. The appeal of small and medium-sized cap stocks to investors will decrease in the future, while the large-cap blue-chip stocks will gradually be favored by investors. The Shenzhen Stock Exchange's move against the bubbles in the Growth Enterprise Market (GEM) on Friday has a very strong warning significance for the current bubbles in small and medium-sized cap stocks, which may push these high-valued small and medium-sized cap stocks into a consolidation period; and the several intra-day rallies of the large-cap blue-chip stocks this week have sent out a signal to the market that they are about to start, especially the roller-coaster trend on Friday. We judge that the main force institutions are taking advantage of the external force of the management's move to prick the GEM bubble to suppress the small and medium-sized cap stocks and test the willingness of the small and medium-sized cap stocks to continue their upward trend. Institutions must attract funds from small and medium-sized cap stocks to large-cap stocks if they want to launch a blue-chip rally. Combining with the intra-day trends of large-cap stocks and small and medium-sized cap stocks this week, we judge that the strategy of the main force institutions is gradually taking effect. There are three important events next week that will affect the market trend: First, the annual Central Economic Work Conference will be held. The tone of this conference is basically in line with the market's expectations. What needs special attention is the top leadership's positioning of the real estate market policy for next year. Although the conference may send out some signals of partial structural fine-tuning, it does not really constitute a bearish factor under the current environment. Instead, the end of the conference will be interpreted by the market as the clarification of uncertainties, thus being bullish for the market; Second, the economic data for November will be announced at the end of next week. It can be anticipated that these data will further confirm the consolidation of the positive trend of the macro-economy, so it is also bullish for the market; Third, the much-anticipated global climate conference will be held next week. Since before the opening of the conference, China, the United States, India and other major carbon-emitting countries have made commitments to reduce emissions, specific results can be expected from this conference, which will increase the market's bullish hotspots. In summary, we believe that after the index makes a moderate oscillation consolidation around 3300 points next week, it will resume its oscillating ascending trend and launch an attack on the high point in August. The conditions for setting a new high in the next stage have basically matured. Strategically, we suggest that investors maintain an optimistic view, pay close attention to the changes in the market style, and choose the right investment field and individual stocks. Although we are optimistic about the performance of blue-chip stocks in the future, we do not think that the market will repeat the clear-cut blue-chip rally like in June and July. In the future, some small and medium-sized cap stocks and related theme stocks may remain active and strengthen. First, we certainly suggest that investors focus on the blue-chip sector. You can take advantage of the oscillation period of the market to absorb these blue-chip stocks at low prices and increase your position proportion in these areas; Secondly, with the possible rise of large-cap blue-chip stocks, the index will maintain a good upward trend, but if you select the wrong stocks, it may be difficult to make profits. Therefore, we suggest that investors avoid small and medium-sized cap stocks and related theme stocks with large gains in the previous period, and do not participate in the rebound and rising trend of these stocks; Thirdly, for some small and medium-sized cap stocks and related theme stocks that have not yet broken through the high points in July and August, the market will still leave opportunities for this type of stocks to perform, such as the new energy sector and other low-carbon economy sectors that have been quiet for more than half a year, there are still profit-making opportunities in the future. In terms of sectors, we suggest that investors pay attention to the coal sector. The coal sector has multiple investment values: Firstly, the continuous depreciation of the US dollar has led to the high operation of international oil prices, which indirectly supports the firmness of coal prices. As one of the main resources, coal is naturally one of the main beneficiaries of inflation expectations; Secondly, under the background of economic recovery, the downstream production of the coal industry enters the accelerated recovery channel, which will fundamentally pull the demand for coal consumption. As one of the most sensitive basic departments to the prosperity degree of the macro-economy, the coal sector is already ahead of the curve entering the recovery stage, which will inevitably be reflected in the capital market; Thirdly, with the arrival of winter, the country enters the peak electricity consumption period, and the demand for coal has entered the seasonal peak. The coal inventory in various places has decreased, and there is a requirement for coal price increases, which forms a seasonal stimulus for the coal sector; Fourthly, the performance of relevant listed companies in the coal sector has a stable growth prospect. It is understood that at this stage, funds are busy preparing for the market situation next year, and the market situation next year will mainly revolve around the 2009 annual report and the 2010 Q1 report. It is expected that industries and individual stocks with performance pre-increases or exceeding expectations will be the focus of market funds chasing. From the reports of each quarter this year, the performance of listed companies in the coal sector has maintained a relatively high growth rate. Therefore, optimistic expectations can be held for the 2009 performance and the Q1 performance next year, so the attraction of the coal sector to institutions is increasing. With the strategic reduction of institutions' positions in sectors and individual stocks with large gains in the previous period approaching the end, increasing holdings in the coal sector is an inevitable choice, and even aggressive buying behavior may appear. In the secondary market, after a long period of consolidation, the enthusiasm for many individual stocks in the coal sector has become difficult to contain this week, and they have risen one after another, performing significantly stronger than the market. Many individual stocks have even sounded the charge horn first, breaking through the high point in August. With the gradual rise of blue-chip stocks, more individual stocks in the coal sector will break through the previous high point in the future, making the entire sector enter a strong upward channel, and the sector effect will appear, regaining prominence in the market in a high-profile manner.

The procedure for buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. Moreover, this order is only valid for the day it is placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price at which you want to buy or sell the stock. The abbreviation of the stock is usually three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is six digits. The code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent when placing an order. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks bought or sold: the quantity of stocks ordered for purchase must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks ordered for sale does not have to be a whole multiple of 100. There are four ways to place an order: counter submission, telephone automatic order, computer automatic order, and remote terminal order.
