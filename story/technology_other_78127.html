Bruce Lee was defeated by an American with one hand???

by lxlcq on 2008-07-17 17:53:46

To commemorate Bruce Lee, a grand TV production with an investment as high as 50 million yuan, titled "The Legend of Bruce Lee," is CCTV's grand tribute to the Olympics this year. It is also considered one of the most anticipated TV works of the year. However, due to the relatively low-key approach of the production team, very little information about the series has been made public. Perhaps because the main filming locations for the drama were set in Shunde, Guangdong by CCTV, when the reporter interviewed an official from the Publicity Department of the Guangdong Provincial Committee yesterday, the official revealed that he had just received the full set of DVDs for "The Legend of Bruce Lee" and disclosed that "the last American defeated Bruce Lee with just one hand."

Defeating Japanese and Korean masters and the US champion

"It's very exciting, thrilling!" When mentioning "The Legend of Bruce Lee," the interviewee’s first impression was excitement. He told the reporter that the fight scenes were incredibly spectacular, and Bruce Lee exuded immense confidence. He successively defeated martial arts masters from Japan and Korea, as well as the all-American champion. "First, a Korean practitioner of karate challenged him but was defeated. Then the Korean followed him around hoping to learn martial arts from him. Afterward, a judo master from Japan came to challenge him and was also defeated by Bruce Lee. Like the Korean, the Japanese man kept following Bruce Lee, hoping to become his disciple, but Bruce Lee proudly told these followers, 'You are not worthy of learning the profound Chinese martial arts; you can only learn simple karate and judo.' Later, Bruce Lee participated in the American professional tournament and defeated the all-American combat champion, becoming famous overnight!"

"Being defeated by one hand" carries a deeper meaning

In reality, Bruce Lee, at the young age of 32, died suddenly at Elizabeth Hospital under mysterious circumstances, and his cause of death remains an unsolved mystery to this day. However, the ending of this new drama does not mention this event, abruptly stopping at "Bruce Lee suddenly left the martial arts world and no longer accepted any challenges." When the reporter pursued the reason, the other party revealed a shocking plot twist - "Because after he became the all-American champion, an American privately challenged him and defeated Bruce Lee with just one hand." The reporter found this ending extremely unbelievable, but the other party explained: "In fact, the director had already laid the groundwork early on in the drama. When Bruce Lee defeated the all-American champion, an American told him, 'We Americans are not without experts; it's just that the experts don't want to show themselves.' I think the director's arrangement of such a dramatic ending was not intended to denigrate Bruce Lee's martial arts skills but rather transcended the story itself, symbolizing that there are always higher mountains and better people out there."

Missed the Olympic broadcast schedule due to potential negative impact

According to further reports, "The Legend of Bruce Lee" was actually completed at the beginning of this year and was rumored to be airing soon on CCTV. CCTV Channel 8 even heavily promoted the show's trailers in April, but its official premiere was delayed. According to informed sources: "Whether it will air on CCTV-1 or CCTV-8 has not yet been decided, and the timing is uncertain. It was originally prepared as a tribute to the Olympics and scheduled to be broadcast during the Olympics, but concerns arose that the portrayal of Bruce Lee beating foreigners to bloody noses might create a negative impression."
