Sunbathing Can Prevent Lymph Node Cancer

The latest research by Australian scientists has found that exposure to sunlight has a significant preventive effect on lymph node cancer. Dr. Click of the University of Sydney, while researching the causes of lymph node cancer, suspected that excessive exposure to sunlight might lead to this type of cancer. However, they unexpectedly reached a completely opposite conclusion. The survey subjects were adults aged 20 to 74, with 704 lymph node cancer patients surveyed about their past exposure to sunlight, with some data spanning up to 60 years. The results showed that the higher the frequency of sun exposure, the lower the risk of developing lymph node cancer. Those with the most sun exposure had a 35% lower chance of getting lymph node cancer compared to those with the least exposure.

Researchers attributed this to the fact that extensive sun exposure helps the body produce large amounts of vitamin D, which effectively inhibits the formation of cancer cells. This research result has been published in the December 10th issue of the International Journal of Cancer Research.
