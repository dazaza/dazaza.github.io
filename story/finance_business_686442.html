Girl falls into gap between platform and train, emerges unscathed after subway passes by

by aiipii12987 on 2012-03-02 09:40:33

Staff at Metro Line 1 Wenshui Road Station recounted the incident yesterday. Early Post intern Wang Haoran contributed to this report with illustrations by Zhao Jiafeng.

Dangerous Situation! Girl falls onto subway tracks as train roars past. Lucky Outcome! Trapped in the track gap, she emerges unscathed.

A shocking scene unfolded on the platform of Metro Line 1 Wenshui Road Station during yesterday's morning rush hour.

Due to inertia, by the time the train had come to a complete stop after emergency braking, two carriages had already rolled over the section of track where the girl had fallen.

The girl fell directly between the two rails of the subway station platform and landed in the space between them. There was a 50-centimeter gap between the bottom of the train and the tracks, and her small frame fit perfectly into this space without moving. It would have been very dangerous if she had lifted her head or turned over at that moment.

Early Post reporter Shi Yin contributed to this report with an intern Yan Qianyun.

At the morning peak hour of 8:21 am yesterday, a terrifying scene occurred on the platform of Metro Line 1 Wenshui Road Station - a young female passenger in her twenties suddenly fell from the platform onto the tracks 2 meters below just as a roaring train approached. Although the driver applied emergency brakes, the train still passed over the spot where the girl had fallen. Fortunately, due to being trapped in the gap between the tracks and under the carriage, the girl escaped completely unharmed.

Two carriages passed over the girl

According to staff at the Wenshui Road metro station, the incident happened at 8:21 am yesterday. At the time, a petite female passenger was standing outside the safety line on the platform waiting for a train bound for Fulin Road when she suddenly pitched forward and fell onto the tracks.

Station staff who noticed the situation immediately pressed the emergency stop button on the platform. According to the subway operator, the driver also spotted someone on the tracks and immediately took emergency braking measures. However, since the girl had fallen near the fifth carriage closer to the direction of the incoming train, by the time the train had overcome its inertial slide and finally stopped, two carriages had already passed over the tracks where the girl had fallen.

Perfectly positioned between the two tracks

After all the trains were brought to a halt, subway staff descended onto the tracks and approached the location where the girl had fallen via the space between the up and down lines. "Although we've been trained for such situations, seeing the person before us was still unsettling," admitted the staff. Previously, there had been incidents of passenger fatalities caused by falling onto the tracks, so they feared the worst for this female passenger who had fallen and then been run over by the train.

"When we saw that she was actually uninjured, we were both surprised and relieved," said the staff. Through the gap between the train and the tracks, they observed that the female passenger was not only uninjured but also conscious, "She fell precisely between the two railway tracks and remained motionless. According to the construction data, there is a 50-centimeter space between the bottom of the train and the tracks, and she was inside this gap. If she had lifted her head or turned over at that moment, it could have been very dangerous."

After the staff helped the girl back onto the platform, they found that despite falling from a height and experiencing the perilous situation of having a train pass over her body, she was not injured, though she appeared somewhat dazed.

It was learned that the female passenger from Nanjing claimed she had fallen onto the tracks due to sudden discomfort caused by high work pressure while unconscious. After declining invitations from relevant departments to undergo a physical examination, the female passenger left accompanied by family members.

The subway operator stated that the incident briefly affected the operation of Line 1, but the train that applied emergency brakes did not evacuate passengers and resumed normal operations after the situation was handled. By 8:29 am that day, normal operational order was restored.
