Kaiyue Interconnection teaches you how to make Baidu fall in love with your keywords. Recently, I've been unsure about what articles to write because I'm just a beginner. My research on online marketing methods hasn't reached the level of an expert yet, so I have been paying attention to some large forum Q&A sections. In order to find my writing inspiration and discover some questions in the minds of the students who communicate with me! In many Q&A sections, the most asked questions are about SEO. Many newcomers are very concerned about this area. Although SEO optimization is only one of the vast promotion methods, it is the most welcomed by everyone. So today, let's briefly discuss some ways for newcomers to choose keywords when building their websites.

First, determine the theme of your website.

Nowadays, many beginners will ask similar questions like "Which keyword should I use for my site?" or "How to select keywords?" Such questions indicate that the asker doesn't have a clear understanding of their site's positioning. We might as well first ask ourselves why we want to do SEO? It's because it can bring users and benefits to our site. For example, after graduation, we look for jobs, and schools recommend us to employers. The employers are like customers who come here to find the employees they need, and the school is like Baidu recommending you or someone else to the employers. But not everyone will be recommended; there are always rankings. The students recommended by the school are those they think fit the requirements of the employer or satisfy the needs of the employer, which is like the keywords we are looking for. What can we offer to users? Or what do users need?

Find user-preferred keywords

By positioning our own website, we understand who our users are? Then start analyzing what words users like to use to search for their favorite websites! Some may ask what kind of keywords are liked by users, which is also the question that many newcomers want to know most.

Here, we webmasters need to learn how to analyze. There are many keywords, popular ones, long-tail words, etc. How do we find the right keywords for our website? First, we need to know which keyword is the main one for our website. For example, the author once did Taobao affiliate marketing, with the theme being weight loss products! Everyone knows that weight loss pills are highly profitable products, so the competition is intense. This is well reflected in the search engine optimization keywords. Popular words like "weight loss" are very competitive. Can we do this word? We wouldn't choose such words because those doing these words are big sites and large companies at the forefront of the industry. Smaller companies won't do them, let alone us webmasters. But we need to understand that similar "weight loss" industry popular keywords are concepts or abstract statements. These words may not necessarily be the ones users like the most, although they have a lot of searches. We would find that these words are just a way for users to find more precise content. We can understand this as finding the road first, then the door! By searching for such words, we simulate the user's search habits. What will users discover when they search for keywords on Baidu? They will find long-tail keywords in the search box, which is what users need because long-tail words are more specific! The more specific the word, the more it shows specific needs - whether it's to find industry information, understand products, or find a good site to conduct transactions!

Choose the keywords you think are suitable

Through simulating user search habits, we roughly understand the keywords users like. How to choose then? First, combine it with the specific content of your own website. For example, a friend's site is an information site, so he should focus more on keywords related to questions and knowledge-based content. Here, I won't give examples one by one. Beginners can consider carefully on their own! Webmasters all know about Baidu Index. Personally, I think it can still be used as a reference because users won't use Baidu Index as a search tool. It can be used as a tool for reference. You can observe the hot content that users often view. Specifically, how to do it, the author thinks that newbies can analyze out some suitable keywords through two points:

1. Through the long-tail words in Baidu's built-in search box (and some long-tail words at the bottom of the content page), a small tip: use the Baidu Advanced Search tool more often. If you want detailed analysis, this tool is quite good.

2. Through the search volume investigation at the bottom, check the popularity of the keywords. You can use advanced search to see Baidu's inclusion status for the day. This popularity determines whether we are suitable to compete for ranking in certain keywords.

There are some other things that I won't recommend individually. A small reminder: you can analyze your competitors' websites, that is, by searching for the keywords you want to do, check the data of some websites on the front page and analyze the reasons for high rankings. Then analyze several to find their commonalities - whether they have more external links or whether their user experience is done well, etc.

Summary: No matter how well you choose your keywords, don't go against the users because the site you build is for the users, not for the search engines. Baidu continuously adjusts its algorithms to meet user needs. What users like is definitely what Baidu favors. Don't do SEO for the sake of SEO!
