The procedure for buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying and selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code), quantity, and price of the stock. And this entrustment is only valid on the day the entrustment is placed. The content of the entrustment includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of buying or selling the stock. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is six digits. When entrusting to buy or sell, the stock code and abbreviation must be consistent. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks bought and sold: That is, the quantity of stocks entrusted for purchase must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks entrusted for sale does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four ways to entrust: Counter submission entrustment, telephone automatic entrustment, computer automatic entrustment, and remote terminal entrustment.
