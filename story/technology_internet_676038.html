The domain name 360.com.cn is listed for sale at a price of one million yuan. Will Qihoo 360 buy it or not?

Source: Webmaster News 365   February 25, 2012

Recently, a well-known domestic current affairs commentator [Ran Xiang] posted on Weibo to sell the premium three-digit domain name 360.com.cn on behalf of someone else. Since this domain shares the same prefix as the official website of Qihoo 360 Security Guard, the news has attracted widespread attention from industry insiders. Subsequently, Aiming Network found based on the sales address Ran Xiang posted on Weibo that the seller owns multiple 360 domains in addition to 360.com.cn, including am360.com, msn360.com, and my360.cn.

According to the Whois query of the domain, 360.com.cn was registered on June 4, 2003, making it nearly nine years old. The domain owner is Mr. Wang, the head of a domestic design company. Among these, 360.cn has already been acquired by Qihoo 360 to serve as the official domain for its security software. 360.com was acquired in 2010 by an overseas company called DaFeng and is currently in use. 360.net was once used as the domain for a domestic enterprise information platform but was closed due to poor management, and the domain changed hands several times before eventually ending up abroad.

Regarding the seller's asking price of 1.2 million yuan for 360.com.cn, some domain investors frankly stated: At this price, unless Qihoo 360 doesn't want it, there will be no market. Relevant domain experts estimate the value of 360.com.cn to be around six figures, indicating that selling it at the seller's listed price of 1.2 million yuan might be challenging. After all, .com.cn suffix domains have a certain value gap compared to mainstream suffixes like .com and .cn. Additionally, there are very few cases in China where such type of suffix domains have sold for over a million yuan.

In recent years, the installation base of Qihoo 360's security guard software on PCs has steadily increased. Virtually overnight, 360 became one of the top internet companies in China, with the name 360 becoming a widely recognized security brand. Last year, 360 made frequent moves in the domain acquisition space, acquiring woxihuan.com, kouxin.com... As the saying goes, when one person succeeds, everyone benefits. With the rising value of the 360 brand, related 360 domains have seen their prices increase several-fold since Qihoo 360 gained fame.

Numeric domains, due to their simplicity and memorability, have always been highly regarded by domain investors and internet companies alike. Whether in domestic or international domain trading markets, they are considered hard currency. Good numeric domains are often priced beyond what buyers are willing to pay, and there are countless examples of numeric domains being put into practical use. As the first security company in the field to adopt a numeric domain, 360 achieved tremendous success, becoming a learning material for many entrepreneurs - in internet entrepreneurship, securing a good domain name is paramount.

Since Qihoo 360 is the largest potential buyer of 360.com.cn, and among numerous internet companies, 360 places relatively high importance on protecting its domain rights. Having missed out on 360.com and 360.net previously, will Qihoo 360 take action this time? Who will ultimately acquire this domain? Will it attract Zhou Hongyi’s attention and result in a protective acquisition, or will a dark horse emerge to quietly purchase it? These questions still require time to answer, so let's wait and see.
