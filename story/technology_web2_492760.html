When the wind blows

by gvzhaihaik on 2011-09-21 17:06:28

When the wind starts to blow, the points of those knives of wind shine even more dazzlingly. The knives of wind are intangible yet sharp, sweeping through every corner of summer. The fiery wind goes mad, reaching everywhere with an atmosphere; they passionately sing and dance like a group of unruly men, wildly celebrating with their knives in hand. I often stand by the door when the wind blows, watching the wind pass by and the clouds drift away. Even if it's about to rain, I welcome it with a positive attitude. In my view, the wind is the legend of time. Youth is just like the wind, fleeting and ephemeral. The most poetic wind I can imagine is a gentle breeze, but I also love storms. They best understand how to fight and how to walk out of adversity strongly. As long as it's wind, it deserves my respect. Just like life, wind has a soul.

When the wind blows, trees sway and shadows move. The birds in the forest are startled and fly wildly in the sky, like streaks of black lightning, crossing the farmers' complete gaze. And my old mother is still on the construction site. Does the concrete made of cement, sand, and steel bars protect my mother when the wind starts to blow? When the wind blows, people naturally think of rain, the impending heavy rain, umbrellas, and rainbows after the rain. When all these become reality, the wind has already disappeared. Then you realize that it's just like a dream. The wind is just a dream from another world, and so are memories.

When the sun shines infinitely well, a sudden gust of wind makes people feel incredibly refreshed. When the wind blows, kites will fly. The wind will bring out a series of teenage memories in those green years.
