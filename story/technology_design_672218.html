The most beautiful corners of sunrise and sunset, Yangshuo

by yrhqmxxb on 2012-02-22 16:25:03

Departing from Guilin, take the bus to Yangshuo. Experience different sunrises and sunsets in different places. After arriving in Yangshuo, have a simple dinner, then walk to the Lijiang Riverbank to enjoy the sunset over the Lijiang River scenery. There are many domestic and international tourists by the river, and bamboo raft boat owners are constantly enticing tourists to take bamboo raft tours of the Lijiang River for 10 yuan per person. As night falls, begin to experience the night scene of Xijie Street. All kinds of shops are brightly lit with people coming and going. There are numerous bars here, each with its own unique decor, and waiters enthusiastically inviting customers. You can wander aimlessly on the bustling Xijie Street, listening, looking, and contemplating. The inns, shops, teahouses, and bars, or others, sometimes you may stand for a while in a shop with pleasant music, without speaking, quietly observing the myriad items, your heart gaining an instant sense of wordless satisfaction. Xijie Street seems like a paradise beyond the mundane world, where Chinese and foreign tourists of various skin tones leisurely stroll through the streets, entering and exiting various shops to select their favorite specialty goods. Bars generally don't get lively until after 9 PM, so you can casually choose a bar to lazily pass the time and daydream.

Meanwhile, we experienced the essence of the Lijiang River's scenic section between Yangdi and Xingping via bamboo raft drift. Our bamboo raft master was Mr. Li, whom we had already contacted before even leaving home. He is very honest and recommended by friends. In fact, from the moment we landed in Guilin and yesterday’s car ride from Guilin to Yangshuo, we began to feel the wondrous beauty of Guilin's mountains and waters. But when you sit on the bamboo raft, when you drift on the Lijiang River, when your body and mind merge with the mountains and waters of the Lijiang River, the most beautiful corners at sunrise and sunset - Yangshuo, your perception of the Lijiang River's mountains and waters is an inner shock, as if we were immersed in a typical Chinese landscape painting, traveling within the picture, with the scenery passing by. The mountain peaks on both sides of the Lijiang River are majestic and towering, with countless shapes. Many stone peaks are covered with lush shrubs and small flowers, resembling the clothing of a beautiful woman from afar. On the riverbank embankments, the emerald green phoenix tail bamboo all year round, like the skirts of young women, sway gracefully with the wind, elegant and charming. The most adorable feature is the mountain peak reflections, partly misty, partly clear. Reflections are one of the great wonders of the Lijiang River. The river water endows the heavy mountains with dynamics, spirituality, and life, while also bringing us into a mythical world. Touring the Lijiang River requires tourists to appreciate the essence spots discovered by our predecessors, but more importantly, it requires us to use our imagination to discover our own inner scenic spots. Perhaps a new scenic spot will emerge from your photos and travel notes. During the drift, Master Li introduced us to attractions such as "Carp Hanging on the Wall", "Child Worshipping Guanyin", "Magic Brush", "Old Man Guarding Apple", "Eight Immortals Crossing the River", "Turtle Climbing the Mountain", "Frog Jumping into the River", etc., and the most famous is the "Nine Horses Painting Mountain" - (When the boat reaches here, a sheer cliff greets you, seemingly flying up. The steep cliff faces the river, white predominating over black. The black parts are mostly rock quality dark patterns and lush small shrubs and fern moss plants, forming nine galloping horses outlined by the heavens' brush. After the boat docks, under Master Li's guidance, you can clearly identify a horse rearing its hooves and leaping into the air, vividly lifelike, especially divine. At the bottom of the cliff, two foal horses bow their heads to drink water. And the other horses? They seem real but not quite, hidden and untraceable, elusive. Strong explained: "Seeing three horses makes you an ordinary person, seeing five makes you a scholar, seeing seven makes you a third-place laureate, seeing nine makes you a top laureate." Then he remained silent, leaving suspense and mystery... the other horses are left for you to find). There are many local women doing small businesses on the shore, little fish, shrimp, and crabs strung together in twos and threes, fried in oil pans, sold for one yuan per skewer, with a very fresh taste. During the Lijiang River drift, you can indulge in playing with water, having close contact with the Lijiang River! Especially with a good water gun, you can have water fights with other bamboo rafts drifting by. The most popular saying during Lijiang River drifts is that men must make beauties "lose themselves" when they see them, and over the years, many beauties have "lost themselves" under water guns. To have a successful water fight, you need to find a good advantageous position, which is the upwind side, so you need to cooperate well with the raft workers. Master Li often stops the raft workers to bring us ashore to a small island by the river to admire the wondrous scenery, where you can see the beautiful reflection in a tranquil pool of water, a group of water buffaloes playing in the Lijiang River, wildflowers and grasses by the riverbank. Walking along the riverbank for a while, if you sing a song at this moment, your voice will drift with the wind following our bamboo raft, allowing us to forget the hustle and bustle of the city, calming our restless hearts, creating an atmosphere filled with tranquility and gentle beauty, weaving through the ink-painting-like Lijiang River, with lush forests on both banks, missing your loved ones or family members while on the rippling emerald waves—how romantic is that?

The next morning, watching the sunrise in Yangshuo: According to the arrangement after yesterday's dinner, we planned to climb the mountain in Yangshuo today to watch the sunrise over the Lijiang River. I woke everyone up at 5 AM, and our group of six followed the instructions given by Strong last night to reach the park. Initially, the gatekeeper refused to open the gate, only letting us in after giving him 10 yuan, while holding a flashlight claiming to guide us to the path up the mountain. It was still very dark, and on the way, we saw a path leading uphill (which we later learned was indeed the path), but the gatekeeper insisted we continue forward. Upon reaching an iron gate, he opened it and let us pass, saying to follow the road to the town government area where we could ascend the mountain. We initially thanked the gatekeeper for his kindness, but soon realized he had misled us out of the park again (he feared the danger of climbing in the dark and tricked us out of the park). With no choice, we had to ask for directions to find the path up the mountain, eventually spending another 10 yuan to hire a local person to lead us up. Since it wasn’t yet bright and the path was steep and slippery, the climb was arduous, especially challenging for the female graduate student. Everyone helped each other and reached the summit at 5:38 AM. Upon reaching the mountain, we found suitable positions to start taking pictures. Due to the overcast sky, the clouds partially obscured the sun, making the shots less than ideal. However, viewing the Lijiang River's mountains and waters, along with the city of Yangshuo from the mountain, still thrilled us, showcasing the most beautiful corners at sunrise and sunset in Yangshuo.
