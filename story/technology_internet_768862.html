As If a Sci-Fi World: A Glimpse of Apple's Solar Farm in the US

by anonymous on 2013-11-28 11:07:56

On the morning of November 19th, news emerged that the foreign website Gigaom had released several photos of Apple's "solar farm" located in North Carolina, USA — an array of solar fuel cells.

The vast fields of solar fuel cell arrays standing tall across the expansive landscape of North Carolina give a sense of being in a science fiction world.

As early as February last year, Apple announced through its environmental report plans to accelerate construction in renewable energy, aiming to make its data center in North Carolina more environmentally friendly. In the initial engineering phase, the installation of solar panels next to the data center was already completed successfully, with the potential to generate 20 megawatts of power capacity within this 100-acre area.

Now, Gigaom has provided a detailed report on the current status of this project. It mentions that the solar farm near the data center spans over 100 acres and includes approximately 50,000 solar panels. Each solar panel has a controller on its back connected to a turning device. The solar panels use single-axis trackers, which are less precise than dual-axis ones but are cheaper and simpler in construction. During the day, a computer automatically calculates the tilt angle to ensure the solar panels face the sun at the optimal angle (similar to how sunflowers track the sun).

The three solar energy facilities combined can provide 50 megawatts of electricity, which is about 10 megawatts more than Apple’s data center requires (and also seems to be more than the 20 megawatts reported last year). Due to North Carolina law, this energy will be fed into the grid, from which Apple then draws the energy it needs. This method means Apple doesn’t need to prepare batteries to store energy and can still have sufficient storage even when the sun isn't shining.

Apple appears to have adopted its habitual "closed" mindset in constructing the solar farm, preferring to keep everything under its own control, including energy. For this purpose, Apple spent over a year building this location. Currently, renewable energy has enabled self-sufficiency. The solar farm now produces more energy than the data center requires for its operations.
