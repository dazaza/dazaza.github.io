&nbsp; After a day's work, for anyone, the best rest is sleep.

It has been passed down in our folk culture that one should develop the habit of washing their feet with hot water before sleeping every day. Soaking your feet in hot water before sleeping can help you fall asleep quickly. There are many ancient medical books that discuss how warm foot baths can improve sleep, such as "feet are the bottom of a person, wash once a night." And "soak your feet and lie down, no cold disease in the limbs." The longevity secret of Lu You, a great poet from the Southern Song Dynasty, included washing his feet with hot water before sleeping. In his poem, Lu You wrote: "The old man does not do farming anymore, but he still remembers to count chickens and dogs, washing his feet and getting into bed feels so refreshing." Persisting in long-term warm foot baths can improve sleep and eliminate insomnia.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Insomnia is a common condition and also a symptom of many diseases. The main symptoms of insomnia include difficulty falling asleep at night, or sleeping but not deeply, waking up easily, having many dreams, and having trouble falling back asleep after waking up, feeling exhausted upon waking. There are many causes of insomnia, with common ones being excessive emotional tension, high nervous excitement, and cerebral hyperemia. People with insomnia have disordered autonomic nerves, and at night, their sympathetic nerves are overly excited, putting them in an excited state where they cannot fall asleep. Physical fatigue, mental fatigue, nerve sensation fatigue, and coldness in the limbs can all lead to insomnia.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Warm foot baths can promote blood circulation and metabolism, eliminating accumulated fatigue substances in the feet and throughout the body, keeping the autonomic nerve function in a normal state. Therefore, warm foot baths can improve sleep and eliminate insomnia. The feet are rich in nerve endings and capillaries, and soaking them in hot water has a gentle stimulating effect on the nerves and capillaries. This warm stimulation reflects onto the cerebral cortex, inhibiting it and allowing the excited sympathetic nerves to smoothly transition to the parasympathetic nerves. When the parasympathetic nerves are excited, the person is in a quiet resting state, thereby improving sleep and eliminating insomnia. Warm foot baths promote blood circulation in the feet and throughout the body, accelerating blood flow, expanding foot vessels, increasing blood volume, thus speeding up head blood flow and resolving brain congestion, eliminating insomnia. After a warm foot bath, foot blood circulation improves, making the cycle smooth, dispersing deposited substances at the foot sole and clearing fatigue substances within the body, eliminating fatigue, putting the person in a resting state, improving sleep, and eliminating insomnia. After a foot bath, foot blood circulation improves, increasing foot temperature, improving coldness in the limbs, and also helping people fall asleep quickly. According to Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), "the head should be cool, and the feet should be warm," so using hot water to wash your feet before sleeping can prevent insomnia for this reason.
