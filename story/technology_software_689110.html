Couple buried in privately-dug basement in villa

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-03 17:48:49

□ By reporter Dunyuan Bao, text and photo A nine-member construction team was excavating a basement for one of the villas in Yangming Valley Villa Area. They usually worked from 7 am to 5 pm, having a simple boxed lunch on the construction site at noon. They earned only about a hundred yuan per day. Despite this, they had been working tirelessly for over ten days. By 4:30 pm the day before yesterday, a passage approximately 20 meters long and 2.5 meters wide had been dug to a depth of three meters underground. Just as it was almost time to knock off work, two workers had already finished their tasks and climbed up to the ground. At that moment, the soil on the outside of the corner suddenly collapsed, burying Mr. Zheng and his wife, who were still working in the underground passage, in a deep pit. By the time everyone managed to dig them out, they had already stopped breathing. The buried couple were locals from Zhuan塘. Members of this construction team mainly consisted of villagers from Longwangsha Village in Zhuan塘 Town. Although the road leading to Longwangsha Village was quite wide, there was a lot of dust due to many construction sites along both sides, making the road appear dim and gloomy under the yellow streetlights. Longwangsha Village is quite large, divided into several groups, spanning an area of about two kilometers in circumference. The group closest to the city center is Group One. Two aunts were chatting with hand gestures by the roadside when the reporter approached and asked, "Aunties, have you heard that a couple in the village got buried while working?" The two aunts immediately responded, "Alas, how pitiful, the couple passed away together. We went to see them this morning, and we couldn't help but shed tears." The aunts pointed towards the traffic light ahead, instructing the reporter to turn right at the intersection and then ask for further directions. There were hardly any people around on the way. After turning right, the reporter didn't know how to proceed and sought help from an uncle at a small restaurant by the roadside. The uncle's surname was Zhao, nearly 60 years old, and he had lived in Longwangsha Village for almost 30 years. Upon hearing the reporter's purpose, Uncle Zhao didn't hesitate and led the reporter to Mr. Zheng's house. As soon as he sat in the reporter's car, Uncle Zhao sighed deeply, pausing for a moment before saying, "What a good couple! I can't say for sure about all the villagers, but anyone who knew them would feel sorry for their loss. This morning, I went to their home to pay my respects; what a sin!" Mr. Zheng was well-liked in the village for his warmth. On the way to Mr. Zheng's house, Uncle Zhao became increasingly agitated, gesturing vividly as he spoke. He described Mr. Zheng as very honest, not talkative, but always greeting familiar people first with great courtesy. Moreover, whenever there was a wedding or funeral in the village, Mr. Zheng would take the initiative to help without being asked. In short, no one could find fault with Mr. Zheng's character. And as for the relationship between the couple, it goes without saying. The day before the incident, which was Saturday, their son and daughter-in-law were resting at home. Mr. Zhao felt sorry for his wife and advised her to rest for a couple of days and spend more time with the children. But she wouldn't hear of it, simply because she didn't want her husband to suffer alone; she insisted on working alongside him. Before Uncle Zhao finished speaking, the car had already turned several corners and finally stopped in front of Mr. Zheng's house. It was two interconnected courtyards, where the small villa in the outer courtyard looked very grand, with marble exterior walls. In contrast, the two-story building inside the inner courtyard seemed worlds apart, its cement-painted exterior gray and unadorned. Upon entering, it was clear that there weren't many valuable possessions inside. Mr. Zheng and his wife lay quietly in this small building. Compared to the quiet inside the building, the courtyard was bustling with people gathered in small groups discussing the accident, some even deliberating on the arrangements for the aftermath. "The homeowner pays, we just do the work" In the courtyard were Mr. Zheng's younger brother, brother-in-law, and other relatives and friends who witnessed the accident on-site. They explained that not long ago, they had taken on a job in the Yangming Valley Villa Area, digging a passage for the basement. In fact, the basement of this villa was also dug by these people for the homeowner. "We all know that digging basements is illegal. This issue exists in some nearby villa areas, but for us, we're just doing the work," said Mr. Zheng's younger brother. Could the property management of the villa area really be unaware of the excavation of basements and sunlit passages? The reporter visited the Yangming Valley Villa Area, but relevant officials from the Guodu Property Yangming Valley Service Center refused to show up. Director Guo stated, "Relevant departments are currently holding meetings to negotiate solutions, so it's not convenient to disclose details yet. Once the meeting concludes, I'll call you immediately." The reporter left a phone number, but by 10 pm last night, there was no call from Director Guo. When the reporter tried calling again, the line was unanswered. Relevant departments suggest the accident may be related to improper construction. The reporter learned from relevant departments that at 4:41 pm the day before yesterday, after receiving the alarm, the leadership of Zhuan塘 Police Station, along with fire services and the 120 emergency medical team, immediately rushed to the scene for rescue operations. The accident site was a two-story villa, owned by someone from Ningbo. The homeowner required the construction personnel to dig a passage approximately 20 meters long and 2.5 meters wide along the villa's corner. The current excavation depth was three meters, resulting in the death of a married couple aged 56. Currently, the homeowner is rushing to Hangzhou to handle the matter. Prior to this, the homeowner had sent representatives to communicate with the deceased's family. After the accident, the Xihu District Government immediately established an accident cause investigation team, specifically hiring lawyers to assist in the investigation. It was found that this villa had previously illegally excavated a basement, which was subsequently filled back in after being investigated by urban management authorities. During the villa's renovation, a water pipe within the neighborhood was damaged, prompting the property management to require its repair. The repair needed to be conducted at a depth of 1.5 to 2 meters underground, but the construction team dug as deep as three meters. Investigations revealed that this construction project was ostensibly for repairing the water pipe but actually aimed to create a light well. The contractor lacked any qualifications, and the workers called in by the contractor also had no professional qualifications. Furthermore, no safety measures were taken during the construction process. In response, relevant departments believe that this construction has likely involved unauthorized construction and constitutes an illegal structure. The cause of the accident may be related to improper construction practices, but the final determination will require further investigation by authoritative departments.
