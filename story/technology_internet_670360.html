SEO optimization simplifies the optimization and promotion process.

First, it's essential to categorize and store some important websites properly. I recommend using the 360 bookmark bar because you can access it anytime, anywhere. It makes it easy to save good websites or articles, and most importantly, they won’t get lost. For instance, we can collect forums for optimization, site clusters, article publishing platforms, and commonly used webmaster tools, organizing them into categories. This method is very convenient. Don't underestimate this—it can save a lot of time and is a must-have tool for us webmasters.

Second: Now comes the practical operation. If you want to save time, the steps are crucial. I suggest that no matter how tight your schedule is, you should write soft articles (soft posts) and then publish them. When publishing, it’s important to focus on key platforms; timing and location are very important. Afterward, there’s reposting. Here’s a detailed breakdown:

Writing soft articles—We may spend a lot of time at work, leaving little time each day to summarize experiences or study. But without these, it’s hard to write good soft articles. Therefore, we must first take a few minutes to read. There’s no need to go elsewhere; A5 is the best place. Find the soft articles recommended on the homepage, which are generally the best. This saves a lot of time. Then analyze whether you have any new insights. If not, learn to compare by taking a few well-optimized sites and comparing them. You’re sure to find good entry points. If still stuck, reflect on today’s observations and analyses, even if it's just a small point, thoroughly think about it. You'll surely be able to write good soft articles. Publish them on A5 and Webmaster Network; other places aren't necessary as their weight isn't high and they rarely get reprinted. This place will do. I suggest finishing by 9 PM. If unsuccessful, check other places like Chongchong Software. If I fail in these two places, I'll post on those platforms with lower weight and fewer reprints, though it's more effort, but sometimes it can't be helped.

Third: After A5 includes your content, start reprinting. I suggest forums and blogs. Baidu Tieba is still worth visiting, but other places can be skipped to save time. When reprinting, there are methods involved. You can't directly reprint; you must change the title. If links aren't allowed, ensure there's a link in the signature. This helps improve rankings. By now, roughly 15 minutes have passed, and the remaining time can be used to optimize the site.

When optimizing the site, I suggest checking the friendly links first to see if there are any broken links. Once checked, you can start optimizing. I usually analyze why the ranking has dropped despite continuously building external links. The method is simple—find the sites ranked ahead of me and analyze them, especially those that suddenly rise. Look at their methods. Recently, one of my competitors was on the first page for a day and then disappeared. I suspect something, but I'm not sure why. In fact, it's not just this one; several sites behave similarly. They use site brushing software, but I don't use such methods since they don't seem to have good outcomes, and the rankings are only temporary. Back to the analysis—when analyzing, focus on their homepage, looking at keyword density, anchor text, and changes in external links. Adjustments can basically be made based on these factors. Then, optimize the internal pages reasonably, such as adding random articles or reprints, automatically adding descriptions, etc. Analyze the strengths of other sites and make adjustments where possible. Currently, I mainly analyze the homepage.
