Be the Best Version of Yourself

At home, my son's closest companion has become the computer. I have had to suppress my own deepest affection to its lowest level, restraining the strong emotions in my heart, and silently waiting for him to be otherwise occupied: when he doesn't get up at dawn, when he watches NBA basketball matches on TV, when he finds a partner to play basketball on the playground, or when he goes to bed late at night—during these times, the computer becomes mine, my beloved treasure that I can't help but hold close. At such moments, I want nothing more than to pour all my feelings into it, embrace it deeply, and kiss it passionately. Then, I eagerly confide in it, sharing my longing, my infatuation, and the love and passion that are hard to put into words, the restless sentiments in my heart that feel like being constantly scratched by little bugs, so unbearable, so frustrating, so helplessly beyond control. Over time, I've grown accustomed to her companionship, her listening, her understanding, her endless patience in accepting me, her broad-minded tolerance, as if she has become my support in life, my spiritual companion, my mental pillar, the blood in my veins—she is a uniquely brilliant landscape in my journey through life. In front of her, I find inspiration and am filled with joy, as if entering another world without noise, without烦恼, without any selfish desires, only purity and comfort. My imagination wanders freely in clouds and mists, my thoughts soar boundlessly across the sky, how refreshing, how delightful, never tiring—I have always loved writing.

The deeper the night, the calmer my heart, and the more relaxed and effortless my hands become as they tap on the keyboard. Knowing that I am not a genius, lacking that natural talent, I rely only on my sincerity, my love, and my interest, most importantly, my unwavering pursuit and yearning for the dream in my heart. Knowing that I have already fallen deep, too deep, unable to extricate myself. Once, I made great efforts to convince myself to give up, to pull myself out of this difficult quagmire, to free myself from this hardship and toil, which was also an invisible self-inflicted wound. Coming out of it would be a kind of spiritual liberation, wouldn't it? But in the end, I just couldn't do it. It's because I've poured in too much blind love, feeling too deeply, making it impossible to break free. It is fated that she will accompany me for life, bringing true happiness and joy deep within my heart. I often console myself: this is enough. Just like two people in love in life, love and liking need no reason, just as my love and fondness for writing, unconditional, wholehearted, without expecting anything in return.

Often alone, whether lonely, bored, or restless, as long as I sit in front of the screen and touch the words, everything else ceases to exist. There are only those joyful words in front of me, in my heart, leaping and dancing happily, accompanying my emotions, guiding my thoughts, wandering in the kingdom of words, enjoying myself fully. Without weariness, without fatigue, I force myself to stop, giving myself a chance to rest. For writing, I have poured out all my feelings, whether waking early, staying up late, or losing much more, I am unrepentant—my heart remains unchanged.

Dreams are hopes, love is happiness, giving is joy. Life's hardships, the trials of living, reality's helplessness, have taught me many truths about life, the meaning of existence, and the real value of being alive. Status and wealth are external things; only spiritual wealth is eternal, truly belonging to oneself. That happiness, that satisfaction, that feeling of happiness, is the most genuine.

There is a harvest for every effort. In just a short month, despite pouring all my feelings into it, I received unexpected rewards. "Good Sentiments" and "Mountains and Rivers" have published more than ten of my essays in print. I am truly happy, excited, and grateful to the editors for their encouragement and kindness. Thank you very much! They have unknowingly given me immense motivation and confidence, giving me the courage and hope to continue moving forward.

As long as I strive, don't lose heart, don't give up, don't fantasize about success or fame, don't covet wealth or honor, and enjoy my own happiness, being the best version of myself is my greatest wish!
