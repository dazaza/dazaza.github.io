Suddenly, two people appeared in front of them. Their hearts were filled with anticipation as they looked up. Si Han's eyes widened - it was Jing and the friend she had met last time.

"How late is it? Who hasn't eaten yet? If you want to treat us to a meal, we'd rather go online." Zi Xuan's suggestion made Si Han's eyes light up. Thus, the three of them entered the nearest internet cafe on the third floor of the square. They booked a private room and started chatting on QQ. Si Han and Jing reminisced about the past and talked about the present. Jing asked Si Han, "Is your girlfriend doing well?" This question startled Si Han. It turned out that the person he had met on the street that time was Jing.

"You guys are talking, right? Why have you all gone mute?" Zi Xuan asked bluntly. They both shook their heads instinctively. Jing was waiting for Si Han to speak, but after waiting for a long time, all he said was, "Have you eaten?"

He finally said it, telling Jing everything he wanted to say through the computer. In the end, Si Han finally uttered the long-hidden apology in his heart. The two of them listened simultaneously to Eason Chan's "Long Time No See" - "I came to your city, walked along the path you once took. I imagined how lonely you must have been without me. Holding the photo you gave me, I walked down the familiar street, only now there's no picture of you. We can't return to that day. Will you suddenly appear at the coffee shop on the corner? I'll smile and reminisce, sitting and chatting with you. I so much want to see you again, to see how you've changed. I won't talk about the past anymore, just casual conversation. I'll say one sentence, just one sentence: Long time no see..."

Seven o'clock finally arrived. Si Han appeared at the Civilized Square as he had promised. He kept paying attention to his surroundings, his rapid gaze like a hunter searching for his prey, though lacking a certain ferocity. More and more people strolled on the evening square, yet the person he was waiting for never appeared. Around him was the狂欢 of groups, while before his eyes was his own loneliness.

Si Han continuously glanced at the watch on his wrist, always feeling that the hands moved slower than usual. After several taps, it still moved slowly. He picked up his favorite magazine, but still couldn't concentrate, always distracted.

Jing spoke first before Si Han could, "My mom doesn't let me come out alone. I called Zi Xuan this time." Si Han merely responded with an "Oh," saying nothing else. He had many things to say, but somehow now he couldn't utter a single word. Again and again, he hesitated halfway, until Zi Xuan pulled them together. This was their first physical contact since their last parting, so familiar yet strange.

Si Han began to feel restless, the long-hidden secret suddenly revealed. Such a revelation made Si Han's eyes moisten. He tried hard not to cry, but the tears flowed uncontrollably. Under the dim lighting, although it wasn't clear enough to see, the glow from the computer screen had already exposed Si Han, despite his efforts to hide it. Originally, Si Han didn't want to explain. In his mind, he held this belief: if someone understands, no explanation is needed to know the truth; if they don't understand, any explanation would be futile. But deep down, he yearned for Jing's understanding.

Looking at his watch, the hour hand had already pointed to the number 9. Jing still hadn't appeared. Strangers kept passing by, none creating the spark he hoped for. His nose gradually became stuffy. He looked up at his feet, recalling the journey so far, leaving behind only deep footprints on the muddy road.

The song continued. Si Han stood up, deciding to leave, but was stopped by Jing. With tears in her eyes, she told him, "I still want to be your treasure angel." Si Han froze for a moment, expecting to burst into happy laughter, but instead, he cried. They agreed that from then on, neither of them would leave without saying goodbye. That day, the two happily left the internet cafe.
