Guilin Seven Stars Park

by xiaocui on 2010-10-11 15:36:25

Located on the east bank of the Lijiang River and covering more than 120 hectares, it is the largest park in Guilin with the most concentrated scenery. Looking eastward from Liberation Bridge, one can see seven peaks composed of four peaks of Putuo Mountain and three peaks of Moon Hill. Their layout resembles the Big Dipper constellation, collectively known as Seven Stars Mountain, from which the park gets its name. The park features beautiful mountains, clear streams, strange stone forests, deep caves, and unique charm, gathering the essences of mountains, water, caves, rocks, courtyards, flora and fauna, and cultural relics. The main attractions include Flower Bridge, Putuo Mountain, Seven Stars Cave, Camel Peak, Moon Hill, Longyin Cave, Guihai Stele Forest, "Glory of China" Square, etc., making it a must-visit for tourists.

Flower Bridge is the oldest bridge in Guilin, originally built during the Jiayi period of the Song Dynasty. It is located at the main entrance of the park, spanning over the confluence of Xiao Dongjiang River and Lingjian Stream, with a total length of 135 meters. The bridge has a wind and rain corridor, pavilions covered with green glazed tiles, and a body made of solid stone, showcasing a strong ethnic style. Every spring and summer, flowers bloom vibrantly, forming a sea of blossoms. On both sides of the bridge are peach trees and bamboo, hiding the small bridge within the scene of "half the stream filled with flowers, half with flowing water," hence the name Flower Bridge. The current bridge was rebuilt in 1965 according to the original design, with four arches made of blue stones. Under the bridge, four semi-circular arches form four jade plates together with their reflections in the water, resembling full moons, just like the "flower bridge rainbow shadow" beauty described in the poem "Four bright moons accompany lotus flowers, half reflected in the clear river, half transformed into rainbows."

Putuo Mountain is the main part of Seven Stars Park, consisting of four peaks: Tianchu, Tianxuan, Tianji, and Tianquan from the northwest of Seven Stars Mountain. With an altitude of 265 meters, it is named after Guanyin Bodhisattva worshipped on the mountain. Putuo Mountain is rich in scenic spots, with many caves and pavilions. Among them, Seven Stars Cave is the most famous, while Xiqia Pavilion and Bi Xu Pavilion stand out for their unique architecture. To the west of the mountain lies Yuanfeng Cave, where cool breezes blow even in the hottest summer days. Walking north along the osmanthus shaded path leads to Putuo Gate, through which one ascends the mountain. Attractions on the mountain include Putuo Stone Forest, Putuo Tower, Guanyin Cave, Xuanwu Pavilion, and finally Zhaixing Pavilion atop Tianji Peak, offering panoramic views of the magnificent scenery described by the great Song poet Liu Kezhuang: "Thousand peaks encircle the wilderness, one river embraces the city."

Putuo Mountain is abundant in historical relics, with over 200 stone carvings alone. Among the most precious ones are Yan Zhenqing's "Xiaoyao Building" from the Tang Dynasty, Fan Chengda's "Bixu Ming" from the Song Dynasty, and the Ming Dynasty's "Tortoise and Snake Combined" bas-relief.

Seven Stars Cave, also known as Qixia Cave or Bixu Cave, is located on the west side of the waist of Putuo Mountain. Over a long period, rainwater continuously seeped in, dissolved limestone, and crystallized inside the cave, forming various stalactites, stone pillars, stone shoots, stone curtains, and stone flowers, creating many splendid and fascinating landscapes, earning it the title of "Immortal Cave." Seven Stars Cave is majestic and deep, with impressive scenery. The cave is divided into three layers, with a total tour distance of 814 meters. The middle layer is currently open for tours, divided into six cave heavens. The widest point is 43 meters, and the highest point is 27 meters, maintaining a constant temperature of around 20°C throughout the year. Since the Sui and Tang Dynasties, Seven Stars Cave has been a tourist attraction, leaving behind numerous poems and inscriptions. Ming Dynasty painter Zhang Wenxi called it the "First Heavenly Cave" and engraved it on the stone wall, becoming the first landscape visitors see upon entering the cave.

Moon Hill is located south of Putuo Mountain, consisting of seven peaks from the south of Seven Stars Mountain. It is named so because there is a crescent-shaped rock in the waist of the mountain, resembling a new moon when viewed from afar. Moon Hill has the Moon Hill Tower, where vegetarian food has a history of over a hundred years, and the Buddhist nuns' noodles are particularly famous. Walking southwest along the mountain foot path leads to Ban Yue Pavilion, offering poetic views of Flower Bridge mirrored in the clear Xiao Dongjiang River. A little further ahead are Moon Rock and Jinjiang Pavilion. Continuing down south along the mountain trail leads to Longyin Cave. Above the cave ceiling is a naturally winding dragon-shaped stone groove, and at the cave entrance are four large characters "Breaking Through the Wall and Flying Away," said to be the "Divine Dragon Relic" left behind when the dragon soared into the sky, inspiring ancient poets to write "Flying away for thousands of years, until now revealing the scales of the dragon."

Guihai Stele Forest includes the stele carvings inside Longyin Cave and nearby Longyin Rock, so dense that there is no intact stone left on the wall, hence the name Guihai Stele Forest. The stele forest contains over 220 stone carvings, involving economic, military, cultural, and folkloric content, with diverse forms such as poetry, prose, songs, fu (rhapsodies), couplets, and images. The calligraphy styles include regular script, cursive script, clerical script, and seal script, possessing extremely high historical value and artistic appreciation value.

Camel Mountain (Tuofeng Mountain) is located behind Putuo Mountain, resembling a camel squatting on the ground. Also known as Hu Mountain due to its resemblance to an ancient wine pot. At the end of the Ming Dynasty, renowned scholars from Jiangnan settled here and planted peach trees all over. Every spring, the peach blossoms flourish, blooming like red clouds, as if draping Camel Mountain in a red glow, creating an extremely beautiful scene called "Red Clouds of Tuofeng," one of the ten major attractions of Guilin. In July 1998, U.S. President Bill Clinton held an environmental seminar with Chinese civilians in the bonsai art garden beside the mountain and then delivered an environmental speech on the lawn in front of Camel Mountain, humorously referring to a hand-held Guilin folding fan as an "energy-saving air conditioner."

"Glory of China" Square is located between Putuo Mountain and Moon Hill, covering a total area of 13,520 square meters. It features two large artworks: the "Glory of China" stone carving mural, stretching 106 meters long and 5 meters high, composed of more than 100 stone carvings, integrating the advanced achievements of ancient China's four great inventions and scientific technologies into one image, reflecting the essence of five thousand years of Chinese civilization; and the Millennium Treasure Tripod, standing 4.6 meters tall, supported by four legs, placed on a circular granite pedestal, symbolizing national peace and prosperity. Weighing 24 tons, the tripod is carved from a single block of curved stone. These two artworks form the new landscape of Seven Stars Park.
