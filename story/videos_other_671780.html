Young men and women are very concerned about how to take good care of their skin and prevent acne (pimples). They must pay attention to their behavior in the following aspects and develop good living habits, fundamentally preventing potential problems:

1. Develop good living habits.

- First, avoid close contact with people who have pimples, acne, rosacea, blackheads, or oily skin (such as cheek-to-cheek contact or sharing towels), because pimples, acne, rosacea, and blackheads are caused by mite infections, which spread through contact. Preventing mite infections can prevent pimples, acne, and rosacea.

- Pay attention to personal hygiene, bathe frequently, change clothes often, keep your face and hands clean to ensure normal sebum excretion.

- Do not squeeze, pinch, or press acne with your hands, as this may cause inflammation and bacteria to spread deeper, resulting in scarring.

- When suffering from acne, it is best to avoid cosmetics, as they contain substances that may worsen acne and prolong the condition. Acne sufferers should especially avoid oil-based cosmetics and opt for oil-free products like gels.

2. Diet is important in preventing acne and rosacea.

For diet, we recommend focusing on two key points: what to eat and what not to eat. Avoid high-fat, high-sugar foods, seafood, and spicy foods; instead, consume zinc, vitamins, and fiber-rich foods.

Why should certain foods be avoided? High-fat, high-sugar, and seafood contain hormones that may overstimulate certain bodily functions or disrupt metabolism. Additionally, some foods contain foreign proteins that may trigger allergic reactions and exacerbate conditions such as eczema or psoriasis.

- Avoid high-fat foods

Increased sebaceous gland secretion is a major cause of acne, and high-fat foods increase this secretion. Therefore, try to limit or avoid lard, cream, butter, fatty meats, etc.

- Avoid seafood

Seafood can cause allergies and worsen acne, making chronic sebaceous gland inflammation harder to cure.

- Avoid high-sugar foods

High-sugar intake accelerates metabolism, increasing sebaceous gland secretion, thus worsening acne. Limit sugary foods.

- Avoid spicy foods

Traditional Chinese medicine suggests that acne results from lung heat or accumulated stomach heat manifesting on the skin. Spicy foods aggravate acne and may lead to recurrence. Smoking, alcohol, strong tea, coffee, chili, garlic, leeks should be avoided.

- Consume zinc

Zinc and Vitamin A help control sebaceous gland secretion, reduce shedding and keratinization of epidermal cells. Foods rich in zinc and Vitamin A include beef liver, soybeans, eggplant, green beans, cabbage, radishes, spinach, chives, carrots, pumpkins, tomatoes, apricots, cream, milk powder, etc. Zinc-rich foods also enhance immunity and promote wound healing, such as corn, green beans, radishes, mushrooms, nuts, animal livers, scallops, etc.

- Consume vitamins

Similarly, Vitamin B2 prevents seborrheic dermatitis, Vitamin B6 plays an essential role in fat metabolism, and Vitamin B1 aids digestion. Consuming foods rich in B vitamins, especially B2 and B6, helps prevent and reduce acne occurrence, such as animal livers, eggs, seaweed, dried mushrooms, soybeans, peas, yeast, chicken, beef, pork (lean), carrots, bananas, grapes, etc.

- Consume fiber

Fiber promotes intestinal movement, absorbs excess water, and speeds up metabolism, reducing skin edema and preventing acne caused by metabolic issues. Fiber-rich foods include whole grains like corn, millet, sorghum, buckwheat, oats, cassava, sweet potatoes, bamboo shoots, beans, vegetables like celery, leeks, garlic sprouts, daylilies, chives, peppers, soybeans, water chestnuts, bamboo shoots, asparagus, onions, mustard greens, Swiss chard, apples, pears, grapes, apricots, persimmons, hawthorns, strawberries, dried fruits, olives, red dates, chestnuts, walnuts, peanuts, fungus, mushrooms, shiitake mushrooms, cornflakes, eggplants, kelp, seaweed, agar, etc.

Spring dietary therapy should focus on cooling and blood cleansing, combined with lubricating intestines and promoting bowel movements. Regularly consume cooling and heat-clearing foods such as lean pork, pig lungs, rabbit meat, duck meat, crucian carp, mushrooms, silver ear fungus, black fungus, celery, spinach, amaranth, lettuce, bitter melon, loofah, cucumber, winter melon, tomatoes, water chestnuts, bean sprouts, mung beans, soybean sprouts, tofu, lotus roots, watermelon, pears, mulberries, grapefruits, hawthorn, apples, etc.
