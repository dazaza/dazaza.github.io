When I miss you,

Today's mood can be said to have fallen into the deepest valley. It cannot be expressed, and I dare not say it. The torture in it can only be experienced by myself. Your once spoken words have become my air again and again, allowing me to still stand normally among people without being blown down. When I cannot see you, I greedily suck on your past tenderness, supporting myself to slowly endure, counting every second with difficulty.

The music that has been echoing for a long time, in this lonely and cold night, is like a sharp blade, cutting through the feigned calmness. You've said that you might change everything, but your love for Lianzi will never change throughout your life. But tonight, hearing this deep and lingering music, I feel as if my soul is about to break, filled with endless worries, thousands of words, with nowhere to vent them. Do not be too emotional when awake, and do not wake up if you are emotional, but alas, this life. There is the same sorrow both in heaven and on earth.

You've said it. You've said, you've said that your life cannot go without me. But tonight, I want to ask you, are you okay without Lianzi's care?

You've said it. You've said, you've said that you only love me for life. But tonight, I want to look at you, watching the busy passersby, is there still room for Lianzi in your heart?

You've said it. You've said, you've said that in dreams and reality, there is always me. But in the quiet of the night, how can I disturb your peace? How can I disturb your dreams? How could I bear to see your pain?

I don't know how to bring it up to you, I don't know how to start writing the message I want to send you, I don't know how to vent the accumulated grievances in my heart. The aimless longing, back and forth, coming and going, has nowhere to return. The night gets deeper, the wind gets colder, looking down at the stretched shadow under the lamplight gradually becoming thinner, listening to the wind outside the window scattering the dried leaves in the corner. What are you doing? Will I still appear in your dreams? Are you still deeply thinking of me? My eyes are full of thoughts for you, blending with the murmurs in my dreams, falling on the pillowside, becoming a wisp of smoke, stopping with a gentle call to you, sadness enveloping the whole night.

I don't want to fall down myself, I don't want to be infatuated with you. But walking alone on the dark street, letting the cold wind caress me, between the furrowed brows, concentrating the unbreakable sorrow, swaying, it's still you, full of warmth. I want to see your eyes clearly, reflect myself in them, see if I'm getting more beautiful. I want to kiss your face, turn around and wait, your figure disappears in the dim lights, the noisy alley becomes empty. Surrounding me is the unrestrained sadness, tightly wrapping me up. The burning flame at the crossroads of life, several times ambitiously circling the horizon, wanting to enact a 'legend', didn't expect to meet you again deeply, one glance from you in the corridor, rich and profound, makes my mind unsettled, emotions fluctuating. The hurried passersby see this, leaving behind a bit of sympathy, turning around, sending a sigh. Whether someone is present or not, the past is like a romantic movie, making people convulse. Don't ask, don't ask, where are you now. Because I have already lost the ability to pretend, can only hide in the nights unseen by others, tears flowing before words.

Once upon a time, sleeping in your warmth, the thin sweetness became extraordinarily full, quietly dropping a tear, wetting your collar. The tender Lianzi's yearning for you has cooled her scattered thoughts, turned into rain, floating outside your dreams. Thinking about it, when you wake up from your dream, you won't understand the hidden meaning in her faint smile. Pulling open the pearl curtain, every night she paces by the willow bank, composing exquisite poems, revealing words and sentences that you cannot read, melancholy partings, spreading like red clouds flying in the sky.

We promised to never separate in our lifetime, we promised to be loving each other until our hair turns gray, we promised that none of us would change. But how could you imagine that the beauty of this encounter in this life has intoxicated the Lianzi floating between heaven and earth, gentle and poetic in the vast years, soft and affectionate like water. Without asking about the result, without seeking the next life, don't talk about parting, looking at each other seems like the old times. I just wish to be able to snuggle in your arms. Countless reunions, every time I approach your broad chest, it always stirs some heartache, rippling in each other's eyes, hard to dissipate.

I am used to talking to you every day, even if it's just a simple greeting. I am used to looking at you every day, even if it's just a busy back. Who would have thought that just as I was feeling worried, I remembered you again. Once I cannot see your familiar name, hear your intimate call, an inexplicable anxiety comes with panic, impossible to resist. Missing you without hesitation, sweeping everything away, loneliness also becomes a kind of sorrow. Embracing a green quilt alone in drunkenness, drunkly embracing a lonely quilt, even the night feels tired. Heart palpitations and confusion, a sour pain invades every complete cell, devouring the tranquility of body and mind, groaning bitterly in the depths. Unspoken worries and cold fingers piercing into the keyboard are like millions of ants crawling into the heart, warm greetings instantly withering, not knowing how to initiate them.

Dear you, dear you, the red candlelight by the small window shines on a desolate night, who knows this emotion. In a world without you, who can I share the loneliness and warmth of a solitary pillow with? Do you know, tonight people are scarce, thoughts lingering, boundless affection, deep as the sea! How much I want to gently tell you in your ear to give me a good hug. How much I want to stick to your body, listen to the sound of your heartbeat. How much I want to hear your hot breath in my ear, muttering a bunch of crazy love words, warming my wounded soul. Can you, please?
