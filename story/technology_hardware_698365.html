It is true that it rains on the way home.

by anonymous on 2012-03-09 08:59:45

Hiking is a great way to exercise, but I find it too tiring, so I prefer walking. My residence is about ten minutes' walk from the office, and I walk four times back and forth every day for work, which is a good opportunity to exercise. Normally, if I can walk, I will avoid taking a vehicle. Besides regular walking, I also enjoy walking to scenic spots as a form of exercise.

On weekdays, I walk from Jingning Road southward, turn left at Qingyang Road, walk past the Lanzhou Museum, and then continue forward to reach my workplace. The office is on the fifth floor without an elevator, so climbing the stairs is another chance to exercise.

The first time I walked extensively was during the first weekend I arrived in Lanzhou. At that time, I was staying in a hotel at Square East. I walked north along Jinchang Road until I reached Nan Binhe Road. Then, I continued westward along the riverside path. In winter, the water level in the river was relatively low, and I enjoyed the sunlight on the riverbed in the afternoon. As I watched the river, I could feel the gentle sway of the willows by the shore and hear the melodious Qin opera echoing across the water. It felt wonderful. I kept walking forward, passing over Zhongshan Bridge and continuing until I reached the Mother of the Yellow River statue. Along this route, I enjoyed the scenery of Lanzhou while walking slowly for perhaps over an hour. From the Mother of the Yellow River statue, I took a bus to Xiguan and then walked back to my accommodation.

I increasingly enjoy walking. In the winter of 2010 after a snowfall, I walked from my residence to Wenta Mountain. Starting from Jingning Road to Zhangye Road, I crossed Zhongshan Bridge and followed the northern riverside road until I reached the back gate of White Pagoda Mountain. Strictly speaking, climbing Red Pagoda Mountain cannot be called hiking since it is too low. After descending from White Pagoda Mountain, I returned along the same route. That day, I left at around nine in the morning and didn't return home until two in the afternoon, skipping lunch altogether. The feeling after the snow was excellent.

On one day in early winter 2011, I started from the General Hospital of the Army in Qilihe District, walking along the southern riverside road beside the Yellow River all the way back to my place. It was noon with a beautiful sunny day. When I reached the island in the middle of the Yellow River, it was full of reeds. Walking through the reeds felt like entering a paradise away from worldly concerns. This time, I walked all the way to Xiguan and continued walking until I reached the Jingning Road and Gan'nan area. Especially near the water of the Yellow River, I tried to immerse myself in the harmony between heaven and earth. Under the warm sunlight of a winter afternoon, walking slowly by the river felt incredibly pleasant.

On weekends, I usually hike one day and focus on walking the other. I have visited Baiyun Temple several times. I would walk from my residence to Xiguan, then head south and north again to reach Baiyun Temple. Baiyun Temple is a famous Taoist temple in Lanzhou, known for its sacredness and the supposed accuracy of the deity's fortune-telling signs. Inside the temple, there are places like the Wenchang Palace. The temple is surrounded by ancient trees, making it a revered Taoist site.

Walking in the rain offers a different kind of reflection. Once when I went to Jiuzhou Platform, it started drizzling lightly as I left without an umbrella. By the time I reached the foot of the mountain, the rain had become heavier, and I hesitated whether to continue. After climbing a few steps, I decided not to proceed further to avoid getting stuck in heavy rain halfway up the mountain. Fortunately, I descended in time as the rain intensified afterward. On my way back, despite the rain, I still walked slowly. Starting from LanYa Group on the northern riverside road, I continued walking until I passed Zhongshan Bridge, reaching the pedestrian street and finally Feisheng Government. The rain never stopped, and by the time I reached the government building, the rain became even heavier. A vacant taxi happened to pass by, so I took it home.

The greatest advantage of walking is that it is not tiring. Of course, the exercise effect cannot compare with hiking. But for someone lazy like me, it is quite appealing.
