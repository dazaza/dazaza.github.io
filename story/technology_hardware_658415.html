Name: Tian Kai

Nationality: China

Specialty: Cinematographer, Director

Started in the film and television industry in 2000. Previously worked as an actor, agent, program editor, film and television director, evening party director, producer, stage costume designer.

In 2005, co-founded "Beijing Flying Dreams in the Morning Film and Television Culture Co., Ltd." The train stopped urgently due to a pregnant woman giving birth prematurely. In the same year, he joined CCTV's "China Sentiment" program group, shooting advertisement films and serving as producer.

In 2009, he started specializing in photography work, laying a solid foundation for his future career in Beijing wedding photography studio.

Advertising works:

Media reports: In 2003, he had the idea of creating his own video website. At that time, there was no professional video website in the Chinese market, and the market was unclear, so friends advised him to wait and see because of financial and other factors, which prevented him from realizing this great dream. The following year, "Cool Eight" appeared, considered the first relatively professional video website. Tian Kai quickly became a senior member of the site and even contributed his tribute film on the first anniversary of the site establishment. Since then, the title of the eighth generation director has been established.

Digital movie "Dad Don't Cry" as planner and producer.

The eighth generation directors are not as orthodox as the first, second or even the fifth generation directors represented by Zhang Yimou and Chen Kaige. They are a passionate generation of young people with their unique ideas, breaking out of the social era dogma, having more independent thoughts without being swayed by others. Therefore, "truth" is their label, and "interaction" is how they gain reputation. Representative figures include Pan Guan, Tian Kai, etc. Of course, there are more "underground workers". Their works often surpass their fame.

Cinematography Director: Tian Kai

Director Tian Kai calls himself the eighth generation director, initially as a self-deprecating joke and a self-positioning. His style does not hope to be like the sixth generation mainly dominated by art films; instead, he hopes to combine commercial, comedy, use new media platforms like the internet and mobile phones, and conquer the audience with niche communication methods and concepts, focusing on Beijing evening party cinematography.
