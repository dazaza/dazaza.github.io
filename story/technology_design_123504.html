Introduction:

Plot:

An enigmatic pyramid is discovered beneath the ice layers of Antarctica. Industrial magnate Charles Bishop Weyland (Lance Henriksen) leads a team of archaeologists, scientists, and security experts, headed by adventurer Alexia Woods (Sanaa Lathan), nicknamed "Law," to explore this frozen continent at the southern end of the Earth. After descending over 2,000 meters into the ice, they discover that the pyramid combines elements of Aztec, Egyptian, and Cambodian cultures. The advanced technology within the pyramid indicates that an extraterrestrial species has been visiting this place for thousands of years. As they are astonished by their findings, the pyramid doors suddenly close without warning, trapping everyone inside the labyrinthine structure. A terrifying and horrifying truth is revealed: the alien species known as "Predators" have imprisoned another extraterrestrial being, the "Alien Queen," here. Every 100 years, the queen lays embryonic eggs, and young Predators prove themselves in a "rite of passage" by battling the Alien offspring. An ultimate battle between these two extraterrestrial species ensues, where whoever wins, we lose.

Behind the Scenes:

[Alien + Predator]

In 1979, 20th Century Fox released Ridley Scott's "Alien," which not only received critical acclaim but also ushered in a new era of science fiction cinema, becoming an undeniable classic. Subsequently, sequels directed by James Cameron, David Fincher, and Jean-Pierre Jeunet—“Alien 2,” “Alien 3,” and “Alien 4”—were also released. In 1987, Fox created the extraterrestrial Predator, whose unconventional and violent sci-fi plot once again captivated audiences, leading to inevitable sequels. A quarter-century later, Fox combined the Alien and Predator franchises into this film, "Alien vs. Predator," which became one of the most anticipated transformations in the history of science fiction cinema.

The idea of bringing "Alien vs. Predator" from concept to script and then to the big screen took nearly a decade. Fox was undecided among various storylines until director and writer Paul W.S. Anderson presented his vision. Producer John Davis said, "Over the years, I've heard stories from more than forty writers, but when Paul came with his story, placing humans in the middle of the conflict between Aliens and Predators, we were completely captivated."

Anderson recalls that he had this idea even when he was just starting out in the film industry. Anderson said, "About nine years ago, just for fun, I thought about making a movie where the Alien and Predator would face off. At the time, I was attending the Sundance Film Festival with my debut film 'Congo,' and I never imagined I'd get a chance to make a movie like AVP."

"Eight years later," Anderson continued, "Fox started planning to make such a movie, and they invited me for discussions. Our ideas coincided, and this time I knew I wouldn't miss the opportunity to realize my dream." According to producer Davis, finding a director who was very familiar with both the Alien and Predator franchises was particularly important. As a super fan of both franchises, Anderson had watched these movies hundreds of times and could recite any scene from memory. His passion made him the ideal choice for the director.

To ensure the storyline aligns logically with previous "Alien" and "Predator" films, Anderson meticulously searched through every detail in his memory. For example, in "Predator 2," the Predator's trophy collection includes an Alien skull, suggesting that the two species had previously encountered each other. Anderson designed the Predators as having visited Earth thousands of years ago, revered as gods, and profoundly influencing certain ancient civilizations, much like the Aztec and Maya civilizations, aligning perfectly with the interior design of the alien spaceship in "Predator 2."

Meanwhile, Anderson also introduced characters closely related to the "Alien" series, namely industrial tycoon Charles Bishop Weyland (played by Lance Henriksen, who starred in "Alien 2" and "Alien 3"). Alien fans will surely recognize Bishop Weyland; Bishop was the android priest in "Alien 2" and "Alien 3," while Weyland comes from the unlucky company that sent people to search for aliens in the original "Alien." Anderson explained, "Weyland made a fortune investing in high-tech enterprises and is considered the father of modern robotics technology. Therefore, the android priest created 150 years later retains his likeness."

The life cycle of the Alien begins with eggs laid by the Queen, containing eight-footed suckers. When the time is right, the sucker attaches itself to a suitable host, implants an embryo inside it, and then emerges as a snake-like infant, shedding its skin to grow into a ferocious and terrifying adult.

Sharp Tail: The pointed tail is also a highly lethal weapon, causing death or injury wherever it strikes.

Burst Teeth: Bright and sharp, under normal circumstances, you rarely see an Alien with its mouth closed, so its teeth are quite intimidating.

Inner Nest Teeth: Stronger than the outer burst teeth, with a hardness equivalent to diamonds, capable of biting through metal.

Wall-Climbing: This is actually a skill of the Alien, allowing it to use its claws to crawl on walls and ceilings, surprising opponents with unexpected attacks.

Acidic Blood: This yellow acid is highly corrosive, equivalent to the Alien's blood, which it does not sacrifice unless absolutely necessary.

Predators usually operate alone, hunting being their sole pleasure. Each Predator selects its preferred weapons and style to capture prey. However, they typically consider killing humans to be the most challenging, and close combat with intelligent humans is worthwhile. The two most famous encounters between humans and Predators occurred in the jungles of Vietnam and Los Angeles, both resulting in heavy human casualties.
