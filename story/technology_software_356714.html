Do you know the difference in stone colors?

by yaha2628 on 2010-05-28 20:59:09

The reason why stones are used for the decoration of high-end buildings is largely due to their excellent physical properties, especially their rich colors, which are unparalleled by other materials. However, since the colors of stones are naturally formed, the quality of the color is determined by the pigment ions contained in the stone and its internal structure. At the same time, the different formation sequence of different minerals that make up the stone also affects the change of color, which is what we call color difference. Therefore, we often encounter the problem of color difference when using decorative stones. According to the different structures and color characteristics of stones, we roughly classify the decorative stones with color difference phenomena into two categories and Beijing cleaning company provides different solutions:

First, stones of the same color system. Stones of the same color system refer to a category of stones with the same structure and characteristics, and the same or similar color components. For example, the large flower and medium flower, medium flower and small flower, smooth surface and rough surface, new surface and old surface of Shandong white marble, as well as the upper and lower mine layers all belong to the stones of the same color system. For the color difference between these types of stones, we recommend using color-enhancing stone maintenance agents (also known as wetting agents or fresh-keeping agents) for treatment. Color-enhancing stone maintenance agents are suitable for adjusting minor color differences between stones of the same color system and for enhancing the color of dark-colored stones such as marble, granite, sandstone, and slate that have breathing micro-pores and exhibit wear, fading, and dullness. The color enhancement effect on light-colored stones is not very obvious. Since the color enhancement lasts long and does not fade easily, we suggest conducting a small-scale experiment first to confirm the effect before proceeding with large-scale construction.

Second, stones of different color systems. Stones of different color systems refer to a category of stones with completely different structures and characteristics, and completely different or significantly varying color components. For example, Jin Xian Mi Huang and Xin Xi Mi Huang, Spanish Mi Huang and Bai Sha Mi Huang, Shandong White Marble and American Gray Marble all belong to stones of different color systems. For their color differences, we recommend using coloring methods (dyeing) for treatment. Currently, there are about twenty methods for stone coloring domestically and internationally, most of which require specific equipment or venues. This is not practical for the stone maintenance industry. After summarizing our years of experience, we believe that using stone dyeing agents for coloring treatment is a simple, convenient, and effective method. However, since it is not easy to remove the color once the stone has been dyed, we suggest that professional cleaning companies carry out the construction to avoid unnecessary losses.
