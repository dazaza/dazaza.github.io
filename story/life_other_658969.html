Rhyming Sorrow

The clouds in the distance, silent and still, entwined with the chill of winter. I stood quietly on the shore where the tide was receding, my bangs disheveled by the wind. Upright and immobile, I helplessly gazed into the vast darkness without bounds. The coastline's pale streaks, devoid of stars, connected only water to sky. All around stretched desolation. I watched as the mountain peaks blurred in the misty haze. I felt time silently slipping through my fingertips, unable to grasp the innocent yearnings. Along this long journey of the heart, melancholy lay before me, tranquil yet winding like a spring. Recalling it all remains vivid; for when we said goodbye, it meant ceasing to miss each other.

Days gone by cannot be revisited, events have passed, circumstances have changed. The past has condensed into a roll of black film, its sorrow suppressed and dismissed. Grievances of yore left to time's amusement, silence used to evade. Under the guise of habit, we mourn. Promises made without certainty, vows too late to redeem. Allow me three seconds to pay my respects, retreating into the left ventricle of my heart, stranded in place. Sketching out the accolades of silence, unable to pen a magnificent transformation. Past disdain, present indifference, dissipated like smoke.

In this exhausted space, carrying the remnants of decay, face-to-face with solitude, tears fall into my palm, muddying the lines of life. I and the sunlight are two parallel lines, forever unable to embrace the intersection of light and heat. Lamenting the infatuation of youthful arrogance, bidding farewell to those once shallow, carefree, ignorant, pure years that drifted by.

So-called eternities, so-called ages till the end of time, cannot escape time's grasp, destined to erode into fragmented voids. Recollections lightly sketched from chapters long past cannot fill the regrets of time. The pen struggles to conclude the tale, silence habitually tangled, thoughts practicing amidst busyness to steal moments of leisure.

Fantasizing about that street lined with towering trees, loneliness forcefully frozen in that summer. This prolonged, monotonous path of life, collecting weariness in my gaze, sees smiles slowly form at the corners of my lips. I stand on the edge of darkness, awaiting the dawn, pondering the duration of happiness. Unintentionally touching the side profile of my soul, so near, yet so far. My attachment to the past sinks within the nearly perfect illusions.

The air carries damp, warm tears, memories resting on sweet, intangible murmurs, deeply asleep.
