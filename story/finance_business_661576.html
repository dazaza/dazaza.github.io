4. There were two monks living in temples on adjacent mountains. Between these two mountains was a stream, and every day both monks went down the mountain to the stream to fetch water. Over time, they became good friends. This went on for five years unknowingly. Suddenly one day, the monk from the left mountain didn't come down to fetch water. The monk from the right mountain thought, "He probably overslept." He didn't worry much about it. But the next day, the monk from the left mountain still didn't come down, nor the third day, or even after a week, and still the same after a month. Finally, the monk from the right mountain couldn't bear it anymore, thinking, "My friend might be sick, I should visit him and see if I can help." So he climbed up the left mountain to visit his old friend... When he arrived at the temple on the left mountain, he was shocked to see his friend practicing Tai Chi in front of the temple, not looking like someone who hadn't had water for a month. Curiously, he asked, "You haven't come down the mountain to fetch water for a month, can you survive without drinking water?" The monk from the left mountain said, "Come, let me show you." He led the monk from the right mountain to the backyard of the temple, pointing to a well, saying, "In these five years, I would dig this well whenever I finished my daily tasks, even if I was busy, I dug as much as I could. Now that I've finally dug out well water, I no longer need to come down the mountain to fetch water, and I have more time to practice my favorite Tai Chi."

Moral: No matter how much salary we earn in the company, it's all fetching water.
