Street fight

Mutual insults and curses as "brain-dead garbage"

On March 30th, netizen "Crazy Blood" uploaded a video on a video website to counterattack the "post-90s" non-mainstream girl. In the video, she refuted kululu's views one by one like a machine gun. After this video was uploaded, it was strongly supported by "post-80s" netizens and heavily criticized by "post-90s", escalating the battle again, with related comments reaching 45,000. On April 5th, someone posted on the NetEase forum - "Protest: Saying We 'Post-90s' Are Non-Mainstream? Brain-Dead Style? 'Post-80s' Are Garbage." Within a day, the click rate exceeded 10,000 and was listed as the top post on the forum, escalating the argument again.

This "battle" had a wide impact, easily triggering mutual insults, cursing, and endless arguments between two generations of young people. Someone sighed in relief: "'Post-70s' are finally free, now it's the turn of 'post-80s' and 'post-90s' to fight." The criticism of non-mainstream has escalated to the debate between "post-80s" and "post-90s", rising to the discussion about each side's attitude towards life, ideals, and pursuits, such as which generation lacks taste, which generation is more absurd.

■ Expert opinion

Hu Guangwei of the Provincial Academy of Social Sciences said, whether it's "post-80s", "post-90s", or us "post-50s", they are just terms and cannot be absolutely divided. A decade is an interval, each generation has many differences, different ideals, advantages, and disadvantages. Appropriate discussions on the Internet are fine, I think this form is still a good thing, but it should not escalate to insults and attacks, but rather calm and rational communication, tolerant treatment, and mutual understanding.
