Calculation of vehicle loss insurance claims

by lengliu9838 on 2012-02-07 16:38:02

Calculation of vehicle loss insurance claims compensation When an insured vehicle is involved in an insurance accident, the insurance company will compensate for the insured vehicle. Once the insurance company confirms that the accident falls within its scope of responsibility, it will compensate the policyholder (insured).

(1) Provisions on compensation stipulated by the unified terms formulated by the China Insurance Regulatory Commission

(1) Vehicle Loss Insurance compensates according to the following provisions:

① Total Loss: If the insured amount exceeds the actual value, compensation is calculated based on the actual value at the time of the accident; if the insured amount equals or is less than the actual value, compensation is calculated based on the insured amount.

② Partial Loss: For vehicles with insured amounts determined by the price of a new car, compensation is calculated based on actual repair costs and necessary, reasonable rescue expenses. For vehicles with insured amounts lower than the price of a new car, compensation for repair and rescue costs is calculated proportionally based on the ratio of the insured amount to the price of a new car.

Compensation for damage to the insured vehicle and rescue costs are limited separately to not exceed the insured amount. If the sum of one-time compensation for partial loss and the deductible equals the insured amount, the insurance liability for vehicle loss terminates.

(2) In the property being salvaged, if there is property not insured under the insurance contract, the salvage expenses should be apportioned according to the ratio of the actual value of the insured property under the insurance contract to the total actual value of all salvaged property.

(3) The remaining part of the insured vehicle after suffering a loss should be negotiated and valued and returned to the insured, with the amount deducted from the compensation. The validity period of aviation insurance applies.

(4) Based on the responsibility of the driver of the insured vehicle in the accident, an absolute deductible rate is applied to the amount of vehicle loss insurance compensation that complies with the compensation regulations: 20% deductible for full responsibility, 15% for major responsibility, 10% for equal responsibility, 5% for minor responsibility, and a 20% absolute deductible for single-party accidents. A single-party accident refers to an accident not involving third-party damages, excluding accidents caused by natural disasters.

(5) If the loss of the insured vehicle within the scope of insurance liability should be compensated by a third party, the insured should claim compensation from the third party. If the third party refuses to pay, the insured should file a lawsuit. After the court files the case, upon receipt of the insured's written compensation request, the insurer will partially or fully compensate according to the insurance contract. However, the insured must transfer part or all of their right to claim compensation from the third party to the insurer and assist the insurer in pursuing compensation from the third party. If the insured waives their right to claim compensation from the third party or due to fault causes the insurer unable to exercise subrogation rights, the insurer will not bear the compensation responsibility or correspondingly deduct the insurance compensation amount.

If the loss of the insured vehicle within the scope of insurance liability should be compensated by a third party but the third party cannot indeed be found, the insurer will compensate, but within the range of compensation regulations, an absolute deductible rate of 5% will apply.
