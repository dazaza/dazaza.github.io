Dry cleaning store franchise technology for general stains

When people wear clothes, they get stained with substances that are difficult to remove, leaving traces on the fabric surface. These types of stains are called general stains. There are roughly several kinds:

(One) Dry cleaning store franchise oil-based stains

1. Stains caused by animal oil, vegetable oil, wax oil, and mineral oil     Once these stains adhere, they become quite firm. They cannot be removed with detergents and must be dissolved using chemical agents before washing can clear them away.

2. Stains caused by pigmented oils     Including paint, ink, colored oil, stamp pad oil, ballpoint pen, etc. Removing these stains is much more difficult than removing colorless fat-based stains. Especially if not handled promptly after staining, over time the pigment molecules penetrate into the fiber interior and combine with the fibers, making removal even more challenging.

(Two) Dry cleaning store franchise pigment acid-based stains

Some stains are caused by various fruit juices, all of which contain pigment acids that stain clothes firmly. It's very hard to remove them by water washing alone. Only by using certain chemical agents to neutralize the organic acid esters in the fruit juice can the stains be removed.

(Three) Dry cleaning store franchise protein-based stains

Stains caused by substances containing proteins such as blood and milk. These stains generally dissolve in water but are sensitive to high temperatures. Once exposed to high temperatures, the proteins turn into modified proteins and firmly bind with the fabric fibers, making them very difficult to remove.

(Four) Dry cleaning store franchise pigment stains

Pure pigment stains are caused by various pigments and inorganic substances with pigments. Such stains are generally very hard to remove once they adhere, especially on white clothing where it becomes even more difficult to remove the pigment. Appropriate chemical agents must be used, and removal can only be achieved through chemical or physical treatment.

(Five) Dry cleaning store franchise other types of stains

For example, stains caused by asphalt, iodine tincture, rust, ointments, etc. Since there are many kinds of such stains with different properties, the agents and methods used for handling also vary accordingly.
