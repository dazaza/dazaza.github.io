How Pregnant Women Can Relax Prenatally

by ciaifuke on 2012-03-07 16:54:37

As the due date approaches, it is natural to feel fear about childbirth because it is a painful, difficult, and exhausting process. Since every pregnant woman experiences labor pain differently, you need to consider how to express this pain. Therefore, relaxation, preparation, and understanding are very important for your childbirth experience. Below, we will introduce some relaxation techniques from multiple perspectives.

**Vision**

People often say: "The eyes are the windows to the soul." What we see can affect our mood and behavior, so we should try to create a relaxing environment for ourselves: soft lighting, lamps, and candlelight can bring tranquility, safety, and warmth, while also reducing distractions. You can also place landscape paintings in your room to help you relax at any time, and putting up pictures of your baby can make you feel more cozy.

**Sound**

Music can make you want to dance or help you sleep better. Music therapy can help you relax and improve your focus. You might find that sounds like ocean waves help you notice the frequency of your contractions, or bird songs can take your mind to another world. Many CD stores allow you to listen to music before purchasing, so you can find music that suits you. Once you find music that makes you feel relaxed, you can play it often during your pregnancy, which will help you naturally relax when you hear it during labor.

**Smell**

Certain scents have relaxing and calming effects. If you decide to give birth in a hospital, you can bring familiar scents with you, such as your favorite pillow, blanket, or T-shirt. You can also bring essential oils like lavender and rose to keep the air fresh and create a quiet, relaxing environment. Jasmine and sage scents are often used in delivery rooms, reportedly helping with contractions and relieving muscle pain.

**Taste**

Many experts believe that eating foods rich in carbohydrates and Vitamin B is very helpful during the first stage of labor. Even though most pregnant women may not be interested in food at this time, they may still enjoy nutritious and energy-providing snacks. Fruits, functional drinks, mints, or chewing gum can freshen your breath, moisten your mouth, and help relax your body.

**Touch**

Every woman has different feelings about touch. Some pregnant women may feel uncomfortable with light pressure, while others may find it comforting. So you need to spend some time trying different types of massages, acupressure, or hydrotherapy to find the right pressure and method for you.
