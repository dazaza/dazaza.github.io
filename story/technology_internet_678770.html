Shanghai surrogacy needs to understand the care of newborn babies

by wo1ai2sjm on 2012-02-27 16:31:02

Accompanied by the cries, a new life begins. Taking good care of newborns is the sacred duty of maternity and gynecology medical staff. What treatments are needed after the fetus is delivered? Expectant mothers should have a general understanding.

Firstly, after the fetus is delivered, it is necessary to clear the respiratory tract, which means promptly using a suction tube to remove amniotic fluid and mucus from the newborn's mouth and nose to prevent aspiration pneumonia. Excessive amniotic fluid entering the digestive tract can lead to swallowing syndrome, i.e., the newborn will not eat milk or vomit milk. In such cases, gastric lavage may be required, which is detrimental to the newborn. Therefore, clearing the respiratory tract must be given due importance. During the process of clearing the respiratory tract, it is important to observe the newborn's skin color, heart rate, breathing, laryngeal reflex, muscle tension, and perform APGAR scoring.

Handling the umbilical cord is also an important step. Since newborns have poor resistance, sterile operation is essential. Cutting the umbilical cord requires a separate pair of scissors and must not be mixed with episiotomy scissors. About 1 centimeter of the umbilical cord is retained, and iodine and alcohol are used for disinfection when cutting. The cut end can be ligated with a ligature line or a valve core, and advanced methods use specialized umbilical clamps. In summary, the purpose is to close the cut end. The cut surface is disinfected with 2.5% iodine and 75% alcohol or 20% potassium permanganate solution. Note that the medicine must not contact the newborn's skin to avoid burns. Finally, cover it with gauze and then wrap it with an umbilical cloth. The ligated umbilical cord root will fall off on its own in about a week.

If there is a lot of vernix caseosa on the newborn's body, peanut oil can be used to remove it to avoid infection.

The physical examination of the newborn includes: weight, height, paying attention to whether the newborn is mature and whether it conforms to the gestational age. When examining the head, note whether there is a birth tumor or skull hematoma. It is also necessary to examine the heart and lungs, liver and spleen, limb movement, and whether there is anal atresia or other malformations.

After the examination, skin-to-skin contact, early sucking, and early breastfeeding should be performed. Then bathe the newborn, dress them, and the baby can then be with the mother.

During the treatment of the newborn, thermal insulation should be particularly emphasized because the biggest discomfort after the fetus is delivered is temperature. Where conditions permit, infrared radiation tables are best used. If thermal insulation is improper, a series of problems may occur in the newborn.
