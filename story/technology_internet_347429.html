Construction Company Drills Through Natural Gas Main Pipe, Causing 5000 Residents in Huangpi, Wuhan to Lose Gas Supply

by mjzd6713 on 2010-04-17 23:11:43

On a flower bed less than 2 meters away from the drilling rig, there was a warning post that read "Protected by law, construction strictly prohibited. Legal action will be taken for any damage to the pipeline."

The natural gas company issued a black alert. Some residents of Gushu Tree area were evacuated and users in Huangpi region experienced gas shutdowns. The natural gas company activated the highest level emergency response plan. The construction party ignored warnings and drilled through a high-pressure gas pipe. The process of eliminating danger was cautious. The time for restoring gas supply is currently difficult to determine.

An unapproved drilling operation resulted in a 711mm diameter high-pressure gas transmission pipe being penetrated, cutting off the main artery of the entire Wuhan city's outer loop natural gas delivery system. Staff members of the natural gas company immediately initiated the highest-level emergency response plan and categorized it as a black alert. A few natural gas users in Hankou and Hanyang were affected, while more than 30 industrial and commercial users and 5000 residential users in the Huangpi region experienced gas shutdowns. It wasn't until 4 PM that the black alert was lifted, but the time when the damaged pipeline could restore gas supply remains uncertain.

The incident occurred on the motor vehicle lane about 100 meters near the intersection of Gushu Tree Road and Third Ring Road in Hankou. When journalists arrived at the scene around 1 PM yesterday afternoon, a strong smell of gas permeated within a radius of hundreds of meters. Below a simple drilling rig by the roadside, white gas kept emerging, accompanied by a "hissing" sound. Around the drilling rig, repair personnel from Wuhan Natural Gas Co., Ltd. had set up a cordon line. Two fire trucks stood ready not far away.

According to the repair personnel, the incident happened at around 12:55 PM yesterday noon. Safety patrol officers from the natural gas company discovered a construction team conducting soil exploration here, which resulted in the penetration of the natural gas pipeline and caused a gas leak. "We immediately stopped their construction and reported it to the police, then proceeded with pressure reduction treatment on the pipeline." Subsequently, the natural gas company activated the highest-level emergency response plan for natural gas, assembling four repair teams to rush to the scene for repairs and contacting the fire department and public security departments.

To prevent open flames, the police persuaded residents within 500 to 1000 meters of nearby Gushu Tree Village to evacuate partially, "requiring them to refrain from using electricity or fire." Due to timely control, no safety accidents occurred at the scene.

The construction party ignored warnings and drilled through the high-pressure gas pipe

The "culprit" of this accident was the Municipal Geotechnical Design Institute, with the contact person being "Lu Bin". It was understood that an overpass would be built here, and the institute's drilling was to investigate geological conditions. The construction period was from April 14 to April 30.

Journalists saw that on the "Road (Pipeline Construction Contact Form)", Wuhan Natural Gas Co., Ltd. had clearly informed the other party, "Within the safety distance of the gas pipeline (6.5 meters on both sides of the pipeline), it is strictly forbidden to use mechanical excavation or heavy vehicles to roll over or drive piles." If construction must take place within the safety distance, they need to promptly contact the natural gas company for safety supervision.

A staff member of the municipal natural gas company told reporters that yesterday morning, the Municipal Geotechnical Design Institute was already working at another intersection and was stopped by patrol personnel, who explicitly stated that there were high-pressure pipelines underground and drilling would trigger danger. "We required them to check the pipeline location before proceeding with construction," but they refused to locate the pipeline and resumed work elsewhere after the patrol personnel left, eventually leading to the accident. Journalists observed that less than 2 meters away from the drilling rig on the flower bed, there was a warning post that read "Protected by law, construction strictly prohibited. Legal action will be taken for any damage to the pipeline."

At 8 PM last night, journalist contacted Lu Bin, the relevant responsible person of the Wuhan Rock and Soil Exploration and Design Branch. He said that the construction here was known to Wuhan Natural Gas Company; the natural gas pipeline did not match the drawings. The drawings indicated that the pipeline was inside the roadside flower bed, so the construction workers moved the drill hole outward several meters, yet still hit the pipeline.

The process of eliminating danger was extremely cautious

Repair personnel told reporters that because the current condition of the damaged pipeline was unclear, the repair process was very complicated: first, the gas release and pressure reduction treatment had to be carried out, and only after the natural gas in the pipeline was basically emptied could large machinery be brought in to dig up the road surface to inspect the condition of the damaged pipeline. "If digging begins before the gas is fully released, even a small spark could cause an explosion," and the pressure reduction process lasted more than 2 hours.

By 4:15 PM, repair personnel began operating the drilling rig, pulling the drill head out of the ground. To prevent sparks, several firefighters continuously sprayed water to cool down the area beside it. Gas continued to emerge from the drilling pit, forming numerous bubbles. Throughout the process, repair personnel were extremely cautious, trying to avoid sparks caused by collisions. Around 4:40 PM, the drilling rig was successfully removed.

It wasn't until this point that the continuous flashing black alert, which had lasted for 3 hours, was lifted.

The time for restoring gas supply is currently difficult to determine

It was understood that this was the most serious case of gas pipeline destruction in recent years, with incalculable economic losses. The damaged pipeline was the largest underground natural gas high-pressure network in Wuhan City. Since the city's natural gas outer ring network has not yet been formed, other networks cannot take over the gas supply, resulting in a gas shutdown for 5000 households in the Huangpi region and discontinuation of natural gas use for more than 30 industrial and commercial users.

Yu Guohua, spokesperson for Wuhan Natural Gas Co., Ltd., stated that due to changes in the pressure within the main pipeline, areas affected were not limited to the Huangpi region, as the gas pressure for some users in Hanyang and Hankou regions would also decrease. However, since civil gas does not have high requirements for gas pressure, urban residents' gas usage was unaffected.

At 8 PM last night, Yu Guohua, who was still at the scene, told reporters that the repair personnel had already dug up the pipeline but had not yet entered the repair phase. First, secondary disasters needed to be controlled to prevent explosions caused by leaked natural gas encountering open flames. Due to the pipe rupture and leakage, the gas pressure in an 8-kilometer-long natural gas pipeline was released, abandoning tens of thousands of cubic meters of natural gas. The time for restoring gas supply is currently difficult to determine.
