**Main Symptoms of Facial Port-Wine Stains:**

Experts explain that facial port-wine stains are usually unilateral, occasionally bilateral, and sometimes involve mucous membranes. Initially, they appear as irregular patches of varying sizes with pale red, dark red, or purplish-red colors. The borders are clear, and they do not rise above the skin surface. Capillary dilation may be visible, and pressing on them may cause partial or complete fading. The surface remains smooth.
