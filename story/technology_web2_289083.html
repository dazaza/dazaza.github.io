Google optimization, community forum promotion techniques

by qiaoyao934 on 2009-12-02 15:11:23

1. Submit to search engine indexing (Everyone knows this - only with indexing is there a possibility of converting to traffic.)

2. Join local QQ groups (Join all QQ groups related to local topics. Prepare 3-10 QQ accounts for this. Note: Don't directly post advertisements, as it's easy to get banned. The promotional methods in QQ groups can vary. For example, if you're promoting in a local food and entertainment QQ group, you could say: "New discoveries of local specialty foods, did you know?" Then attach the link. Optimize for Google, engage more with group members, and don't be too direct with advertisements.)

3. Local popular sites (You can search for hot websites in your area for promotion. Everyone can promote according to their local situation. In short, change tactics and stir up hype accordingly.)

4. Baidu Zhidao (This should be clear to everyone. Answer more local questions and attach links. This method works well. A single account should not exceed 3 links per day, exceeding this may result in being blocked. Go slowly, accumulate little by little.)

5. Baidu Tieba (Be very careful when posting ads here. A careless move might get your account banned, or even worse, the entire site blocked. So, be cautious.)

6. Tianya Forum Local Channel (Most Tianya community users are over 22 years old and enjoy forum discussions. Website optimization here can be effective in bringing in members, which helps create an active atmosphere on the forum. You can find your own methods, I think it's pretty good.)

7. Soso Wenwen, Sina Knowledge, NetEase Community Local Channels, etc. You can try these places, there will always be some effect. In short, persistence is key!

8. You can communicate with the administrators of some local QQ groups you've joined, place the website in the group announcements. This method works, depending on how everyone communicates with the administrator!

9. New forums generally have very few people. I'm not good at creating an active atmosphere, so my method is poaching – luring moderators from other forums over.  ；
