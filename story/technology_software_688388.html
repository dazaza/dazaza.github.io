Will lip augmentation with autologous fat leave any marks?

Experts from Bidalu Plastic Surgery and Aesthetic Hospital explain that lip augmentation with autologous fat involves selecting an injection point on each side of the upper and lower corners of the mouth, injecting the autologous fat into the red part of the lips, and gently massaging it to evenly distribute the fat tissue within the red part of the lips to achieve a fuller effect. Since the process of lip augmentation with autologous fat is completed through injection, there will be no scars or wounds after the surgery.

In most cases, extracting autologous fat does not require incisions, as the amount of fat needed for lip filling is minimal, requiring only a small amount of fat to be extracted. We highly recommend that friends considering lip augmentation with autologous fat can also consider simultaneous lip augmentation if they want their lips to appear thicker.

Since the fat will be absorbed, the final result may be slightly discounted after absorption. Therefore, doctors will inject a little more fat based on individual needs each time so that the remaining fat after absorption still maintains a good effect. Thus, although lip augmentation with autologous fat will not leave any wounds, the lips may appear slightly swollen during the recovery period compared to normal.
