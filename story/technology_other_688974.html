The main purpose and classification of mineral processing flow test

by hx515317 on 2012-03-03 16:45:48

The main purposes of the process examination are: ① to understand the production status and existing problems of each operation, each procedure, and each unit in the mineral processing technology flow, and to conduct a comprehensive analysis and evaluation on the quality and quantity aspects of the technological production flow; ② to provide basis for formulating and revising technical operating procedures; ③ to provide information for summarizing the design and production technology experience of each procedure; ④ to identify the causes of abnormal situations in production and propose improvement measures and solutions; ⑤ the process examination data of some mineral processing plants can serve as a basis for design.

The general classification of process examinations is as follows:

① Unit examination, measuring a certain operation of the mineral processing technology, such as crushing screening flow examination, grinding flotation flow examination, etc.; ② Unit examination, measuring two or more interconnected operations, such as screening and jigger unit measurement, hydraulic grading and shaking table unit measurement, etc.; ③ Quantity-quality flow examination, this measurement scale is relatively large with many sampling points. Depending on the size of the workload, it can be further divided into full-plant flow examination and partial (main sections) flow examination.

Due to the relatively complex process, full-plant flow examinations are less common in heavy media plants, while partial flow examinations are more frequent.

The content of the process examination varies according to the purpose of the examination. However, a plant-wide flow examination generally needs to provide the following information:

(1) Nature of the raw ore, including chemical composition, mineral composition, mud content, moisture, true and false density of the raw ore (including valuable minerals, gangue, and wall rock), and the embedding characteristics of the valuable minerals in the raw ore.

(2) Quantity-quality flow chart, which calculates the quantity-quality indicators of the flow based on the data obtained from the examination. The quantity indicators include output, yield, and recovery rate, while the quality indicators include enrichment ratio and grade. By incorporating these indicators into the flow chart, we obtain the quantity-quality flow chart.

(3) Sorting efficiency and operating conditions of each major equipment.

(4) Efficiency and operating conditions of each major auxiliary equipment.

(5) Total recovery rate of the entire plant, sectional recovery rate, metal recovery rate of each particle size of the final product, and the quality situation of the factory-delivered products.

(6) Specifications and technical operating conditions of each equipment.

(7) Slurry flow chart, which calculates the slurry mass fraction and water consumption based on the data obtained from the examination, and incorporates these data into the flow chart to create the slurry flow chart.

(8) Metal loss situation and its causes.

(9) Other various mineral processing technical and economic indicators.
