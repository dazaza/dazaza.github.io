Six ways to make women's complexion rosy and radiant

by wswen156 on 2012-02-20 14:04:42

Some people say that natural beauty in women is the most beautiful. What kind of natural beauty in women is the most beautiful? Of course, it's not about being sallow and emaciated with a dull complexion. A naturally beautiful woman, apart from exuding confidence and grace, should also have a healthy physique and a rosy, fair complexion.

Cultivating good dietary habits: Whitening is a lifelong pursuit for women, an endless topic of conversation when they sit down together. However, cultivating whiteness is not something achieved overnight. In fact, the skin reflects a person's inner state. If the body is well-adjusted, the skin will naturally be smooth and elastic, radiating a healthy red glow. But if one has irregular living habits, often staying up late, smoking, drinking coffee without water, then melanin is likely to deposit, making it impossible to achieve fairness. Therefore, to whiten, one must first cultivate good dietary and sleeping habits. Every day, one needs to consume enough nutrients and vitamins, drink sufficient water, and avoid staying up late at night because between 11 PM and 1 AM, the skin enters a maintenance state. Without proper rest during this time, the skin will lack luster and elasticity. Additionally, don't forget to use sunscreen products against UV rays when going out.

Internal Nourishment: No matter how many or how good cosmetics are used, they can't match the effects of internal nourishment. The saying "a woman is nurtured through nourishment" is not wrong. If a woman's internal organs are dysfunctional, her blood circulation is poor, her essence is insufficient, and there's an imbalance of yin and yang, her complexion will easily appear dull, prone to spots, and loose, sagging skin.

Food supplementation is the best method of nourishment. So, how can one eat to achieve a healthy and fair complexion? One must learn to identify what foods are beneficial for a woman's body. To supplement antioxidants and metabolize melanin from within, patience and daily usage habits are necessary. Don't think that consuming whitening herbs will instantly make you fair because everyone's condition of dark complexion varies, so the methods of adjustment and the herbs used also differ. Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) believes that different skin colorations result from different causes, thus adjustments must target different root causes to truly solve the issue of skin unable to whiten. Daily consumption of vegetables and fruits, along with supplements of vitamins and other trace substances, drinking more water, eating less fried oily foods, avoiding smoking and limiting alcohol intake is recommended.

Blood Supplementation: Many women present with pale white or sallow faces. If the complexion is pale white or sallow, it is mostly due to blood deficiency. Women with blood deficiency usually feel easily fatigued, dizzy, and sometimes experience palpitations. Moreover, blood deficiency manifests as a thinner, whiter tongue coating, and menstrual blood tends to be lighter in color and consistency.

Women can supplement their blood by consuming some healthful natural foods such as black beans, hair moss, carrots, golden needle mushrooms, and longan flesh. Drinking Siwu Decoction (made from angelica, ligusticum, peony, and rehmannia) can also achieve nourishing purposes. Combining it with black chicken soup would be even better. Also, eating more jujubes and goji berries helps.

Kidney Nourishment: If your complexion often appears dull, it is usually caused by insufficient kidney qi. Due to kidney deficiency leading to yin fluid loss, the skin cannot be properly nourished, appearing dim. Thus, supplementing kidney qi helps metabolize melanin, making the skin more tender and pink. Kidney qi deficiency is mostly due to excessive fatigue. If you suddenly feel tired and have a poor appetite, you should check if you have kidney deficiency.

In TCM, replenishing kidney qi requires strengthening the spleen. You can regularly consume kidney-nourishing foods like black sesame paste, longan, or use polygonum multiflorum or mountain yam to stew chicken soup. If there's spleen qi deficiency, you can add ginseng, astragalus, and tangerine peel.

Uterine Adjustment: If your complexion is fair but unhealthy, often appearing pale and lacking blood color, it might indicate a lack of vital energy, making the skin look unappealing. This type of problem usually occurs due to consuming too many cold foods, especially in summer while staying in air-conditioned rooms, leading to a cold uterus constitution. Due to insufficient fire energy, one easily feels cold and experiences dysmenorrhea.

To adjust the uterus, one must first warm the meridians and dispel cold, improving the cold constitution, eventually achieving a rosy complexion that naturally highlights the skin's fairness. Food supplementation for this includes cinnamon, cloves, aconite, and fine cinnamon, which can be boiled into a decoction. If busy, you can directly drink hot ginger brown sugar water, helping disperse internal cold and adjust the uterus, improving complexion.

Qi Supplementation: Women should pay special attention to qi supplementation in spring. TCM believes that the three treasures of life are essence, qi, and spirit. Qi is the fundamental and driving force of life activities, filling the entire body and continuously circulating, relating to human health and longevity. Women with qi deficiency generally show symptoms of fatigue, a grayish-white complexion, poor appetite, a pale tongue with white coating, and weak pulse. They always feel breathless, even when sitting still without any physical activity.

Red ginseng has the effects of tonifying qi, nourishing yin, enriching blood, generating body fluids, strengthening the heart, invigorating the stomach, and calming the mind. Therefore, women seeking qi supplementation should primarily choose red ginseng. Select an appropriate amount of red ginseng, cook it with black chicken to make a soup, and consume both the soup and meat. Regularly eating shiitake mushrooms, sweet potatoes, jujubes, yams, and chestnuts is also beneficial. These foods have excellent qi-tonifying and blood-nourishing effects.
