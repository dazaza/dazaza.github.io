The Effectiveness of Lemon Juice - A Natural and Mild Bleach

Lemon juice is a natural and mild bleach with quite effective stain-removal capabilities. Whether for washable or non-washable clothes, after conducting a colorfastness test, apply the lemon juice directly onto the stained area and let it air dry naturally. If it can dry in the sun, the stain-removal effect will be even better. This method can also remove old red wine and fruit stains.

If the stain is on white fabric or baby clothing, you can use lemon juice to bleach it, then dry it in the sun, and the clothes will become very white.

Spray hair gel on ink stains and then rub under running water; the alcohol in the hair gel can effectively remove ink stains. Before use, conduct a colorfastness test and use non-aerosol hair gel, such as hair conditioner or hair moisturizing spray.

Papain is a powder primarily composed of papaya enzyme or pineapple enzyme. It can break down proteins and has excellent results in removing fresh blood stains from clothes. Simply sprinkle the papain on the blood-stained area, rub gently, leave it for 15 minutes, and then rinse with clean water.

Borax can be used for home disinfection, as a detergent, water softener, and for cleaning greasy stains. For example, to remove jam, jelly, or fruit stains from clothes, apply borax paste to the stained area for 15 minutes before rinsing it off. To remove grease stains from wool clothing, simply add one tablespoon of borax into a bowl of warm water, soak the wool clothing in the borax solution, and it will remove the grease.

In addition to clothing, borax solution can also be used to wipe tiles, sinks, drains, floors, windows, mirrors, and painted surfaces. Simply spray the borax solution onto a wet cloth and wipe. With borax solution, you can phase out your existing chemical cleaners because borax solution can almost clean all the items mentioned above.

If borax accidentally gets into your eyes, it can cause irritation, so when using borax, always wear plastic gloves and avoid touching your face with your hands.
