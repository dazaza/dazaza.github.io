Sales of economy cars in the first 11 months hit 2.01 million units, up 4.6% year on year www.eevig.net

by jin00d on 2012-02-10 18:26:27

Tencent Exclusive by Zhou Lei

In November, the sales volume of economy cars reached 205,000 units, achieving a year-on-year growth of 10.8%, higher than the 2.4% growth rate of the narrow definition passenger car market, and also higher than the overall industry growth rate of -2.4%. The market share of economy cars in the narrow definition passenger car market was 17.9%. From January to November this year, the cumulative sales volume of economy cars reached 2.01 million units, with a year-on-year growth of 4.6%, lower than the 7.0% growth rate of the narrow definition passenger car market, but higher than the overall industry growth rate of 2.6%. The market share in the narrow definition passenger car market was 18.0%.

The trend throughout the year is contrary to the traditional off-peak and peak seasons.

From the monthly trends of economy cars in the first eleven months, the performance of economy cars could be said to be contrary to the traditional off-peak and peak seasons. In the traditional off-season of June and July, economy cars achieved the highest monthly growth rates of the whole year. However, during the traditional "Golden September and Silver October," the growth rate of economy cars was not significant; there was even negative growth in October. Even as the Spring Festival approached, the economy car market continued to decline.

It should be noted that the sluggish performance of the economy car market in the first five months of this year was mainly due to the full exit of purchase tax preferential policies. The increase in growth rate in the middle of the year could be attributed to the weakening impact of the exit of purchase tax preferential policies. However, the low growth and negative growth from August for three consecutive months were caused by the tightening economic situation, including smuggled cars.

The monthly sales of the economy car market from January to November 2011 compared with the overall industry and narrow definition passenger car markets show that the monthly growth rate of the economy car market is generally lower than that of the narrow definition passenger car market, fully demonstrating that the economy car market is not the mainstream of China's narrow definition passenger car market. It can be said that after the full exit of the purchase tax policy and before the maturity of China's automobile market, the marginalization of China's economy car market will be a development trend.

The upward consumption has led to the decline in the market share of economy cars, and the pressure on self-own brands continues to increase.

Compared with the rapid growth and quick rise in market share of the luxury car market this year, the economy car market has seen an overall decline in growth rate and market share. The core reason for this situation is, on the one hand, the upward consumption trend in China's automobile market, and on the other hand, it is a manifestation of the immaturity of China's automobile market. This immaturity is reflected in the fact that cars are still associated with "face" for Chinese people.

In 2009 and 2010, under the influence of the favorable purchase tax preferential policies, the economy car market performed well, and the self-own brands also performed well, with their market share increasing over the two years. However, since the beginning of 2011, despite the launch of several new self-own brand products, the market share of self-own brand economy cars has shrunk due to the full participation of joint venture brands in competition. This situation shows, on the one hand, that the overall product competitiveness of self-own brands has not improved compared to joint venture brands, and on the other hand, it also shows that joint venture brands are increasingly valuing the economy car market. Under these two overlapping factors, the competitive pressure that self-own brands will face in the economy car market in the future will become even greater.

The product competition pattern has basically remained unchanged, with a 6:4 split between joint ventures and self-own brands.

Looking at the top ten models in terms of sales volume, the ratio of joint venture brands to self-own brands in the economy car market from January to November 2011 is 6:4, which is similar to the market share ratio of joint venture and self-own brands in the economy car market. From the ranking of the top ten models, a clear feature is that joint venture brand products are generally newer, while self-own brand products are generally older. This product status is not a good sign for self-own brands. If self-own brands do not have new products to replace the old ones before the end of their product life cycle, the existing market share of self-own brands in the economy car market will be difficult to maintain.

High-quality economy cars may be the future direction of development, and competition in the economy car market will become more intense.

Although the overall consumption level of China's automobile market is shifting upwards, this does not mean that there will be no market for economy cars in the future. Judging from the increasing number of joint venture brands launching economy car products, automobile manufacturers still have relatively high expectations for the future of the economy car market. With the improvement in consumption levels, the potential consumer group for economy cars will inevitably have higher requirements for car quality. In such a situation, high-quality economy cars may become a new direction for development in the future. This can be seen from the fact that high-end economy cars occupied the top five spots in the sales rankings for the first eleven months of this year.

At the same time, looking at the number of products in the economy car market—50 in 2009, 53 in 2010, and 55 in the first eleven months of this year—the competition in this market is quite fierce, with low product concentration. It is believed that under the circumstances where absolute sales volume in China's automobile market is unlikely to increase significantly, the competition pressure brought by automakers focusing on each细分market will be unprecedented.
