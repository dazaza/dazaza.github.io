**Q: How to clean a sofa?**

**A:** After vacuuming the sofa, it's time to clean it. If the sofa is made of leather, extra care should be taken during cleaning because it can easily be scratched or stained. Normally, you just need to wipe off the dust on the surface with a damp cloth. When there are stubborn stains on the sofa, a special sofa spray can be used. Simply spray it about ten inches away from the stain, then gently wipe it with a damp cloth. This will make your sofa look as good as new. This kind of housekeeping routine only needs to be done once a month. To make your sofa shine even more, you can also use a polish, which is as easy to use as cleaning the sofa.

**Cleaning fabric surfaces:** For light-colored fabric surfaces, dry-cleaning powder should be used for dry cleaning. If glue is attached to the fabric, a small amount of emulsifier should be added. For severe stains on the fabric, start from the outer edge of the stain and apply medicine in a clockwise or counterclockwise direction toward the center. After cleaning, use a dry cloth or paper to absorb the moisture on the surface of the cleaned object to avoid yellow marks after drying.

**Q: How to clean the floor?**

**A:** The floor should be mopped once a day. First, sweep the floor with a broom to ensure it is free of dust. Then mop it with warm water mixed with detergent. The mop should not be too wet. Rinse the floor with clean water afterward. Then wipe it with a polishing agent, but do not use too much, as excessive polish can make the floor slippery and greasy. Generally, cleaning the floor once a month is sufficient.

**Floor waxing:** Purpose - Long-term use of floors without maintenance can cause them to lose their luster and reduce their lifespan, affecting the overall indoor effect. Therefore, floors must be maintained regularly, using different wax agents based on the material of the floor.

**Q: How to clean a carpet?**

**A:** If there are stains on the carpet, such as coffee, cola, or juice, first use a dry cloth to absorb the liquid, then gently wipe it with a damp cloth. If the stain remains, spray a carpet cleaner directly onto the stain, let it turn into powder, and then vacuum it as usual. Additionally, carpets should be steam-cleaned once a year. A steam cleaner sprays hot steam while sucking up dirt at the same time.

**Cleaning principles:** When cleaning a carpet, attention should be paid to the direction of cleaning. The correct direction ensures that the carpet is thoroughly cleaned and all hidden dust in the fibers is removed. The right way is to identify the direction of the carpet’s weave and clean perpendicular to it, ensuring the cleaning machine makes full contact with the carpet for effective cleaning.

**Cleaning steps:**

1) **Vacuuming:** Before cleaning, thoroughly vacuum the carpet to prepare for the next steps.

2) **Cleaning:** Use a floor scrubber with an appropriate amount of cleaning agent for proper scrubbing.

3) **Water extraction:** Use a professional water extractor to reduce the amount of cleaning agent left in the carpet after scrubbing.

4) **Rinsing:** Rinse with clean water and add deodorizer to remove any unpleasant smells from the carpet.

5) **Water extraction:** Continue the water extraction process mentioned above.

6) **Grooming:** Use a grooming tool to straighten the carpet fibers and remove any residual hair.

7) **Drying:** To quickly dry the carpet, use a professional dryer to blow it dry, ensuring fast and efficient drying.
