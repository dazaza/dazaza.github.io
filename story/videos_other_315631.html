Manchester United crushes Inter Milan and Juventus can't defeat AC Milan; Sir Alex Ferguson aims to fulfill Matt Busby's lifelong dream.

According to a fan poll on Manchester United's official website regarding the Champions League draw, AC Milan is the top choice among Red Devils fans for their preferred elite opponent in the tournament. This is likely due to the fact that in previous Champions League knockout stages, Manchester United faced AC Milan four times and was eliminated every single time. After the draw results were announced, those within Manchester United expressed satisfaction with the outcome.

Let us first review Manchester United's four eliminations by AC Milan in the Champions League. In the 1957-58 season, Sir Matt Busby and his "Busby Babes" made all of Europe tremble, but the Munich air disaster claimed the lives of eight Manchester United players, including the universally recognized most talented absolute genius, Duncan Edwards. Under these circumstances, Manchester United faced AC Milan in the semifinals, with the schedule being home first and then away. Although the Red Devils defeated their opponents 2-1 at home, AC Milan crushed Manchester United 4-0 at San Siro in front of 80,000 cheering fans, eliminating "Busby's Babes," who had lost eight players, from the final with a total score of 5-2. However, disappointingly, AC Milan also lost 2-3 to Real Madrid in extra time in the final.

After ten years of hard work, Manchester United finally lifted the big-eared trophy for the first time in the 1967-68 season. In the semifinal, they met Real Madrid, winning 1-0 at home in the first leg. In the second leg at Bernabeu, Manchester United conceded three goals in just twelve minutes of the first half, but the resilient "Busby Babes" equalized 3-3 before the end of the game, eliminating their opponents with a total score of 4-3. In the final, after 120 minutes of extra time, Manchester United triumphed 4-1 over Benfica thanks to two goals from Bobby Charlton, one each from George Best and Kidd, achieving their first-ever Champions League title.

In the 1968-69 season, defending champions Manchester United met AC Milan again in the Champions League semifinal. This time, the schedule was reversed, with Manchester United playing away first and then at home. However, Manchester United lost 0-2 at San Siro, and despite returning home to give their opponents a 1-0 defeat, they were still eliminated with a total score of 1-2. Once again, Busby and his Babes fell under the guns of the Rossoneri. At the end of this season, Sir Matt Busby chose to retire, and AC Milan remained an insurmountable obstacle throughout his career.

In the 2004-05 season, during the Champions League round of 16, Sir Alex Ferguson's Manchester United played home first and then away, but AC Milan, with two goals from Hernan Crespo, defeated Manchester United 1-0 both home and away. However, AC Milan, who reached the final, experienced a once-in-a-century nightmare in Istanbul. Leading 3-0, they allowed Liverpool to stage an incredible comeback, losing the almost certain championship.

In the 2006-07 season, during the Champions League semifinal, Sir Alex Ferguson's Manchester United met AC Milan again. Despite Wayne Rooney scoring twice and Cristiano Ronaldo's header helping the Red Devils win the first leg 3-2, AC Milan, with goals from Kaka, Clarence Seedorf, and Alberto Gilardino, won the second leg at San Siro, eliminating Manchester United with a total score of 5-3. In the final, AC Milan defeated Liverpool 2-1 to claim the title.

It is worth noting that in the second leg where Manchester United suffered a 0-3 defeat against AC Milan, the Red Devils' starting left-back Patrice Evra was suspended, captain central defender Rio Ferdinand and then-starting right-back Gary Neville were injured, leaving Manchester United to rely on Vidic, just recovered from injury, leading Heinze, Brown, and O'Shea, who were backups at the time. As a result, they were thoroughly outplayed by the rested AC Milan. However, in that season, AC Milan finished fourth in Serie A, trailing the league champion Inter Milan by a staggering 36 points. This indicates that Manchester United was constrained by the immense pressure of domestic league competition; whereas AC Milan, having abandoned the league, had the advantage of rest in the Champions League.

Since its founding in 1878, Manchester United has a history spanning 131 years, during which it has seen 19 managers. The 24-year tenure of Matt Busby and the equally long 24-year reign of Sir Alex Ferguson are recognized as the two greatest legends in the club's history. However, both have the unfortunate record of being eliminated twice by AC Milan in the Champions League. It can be said that never being able to prevail against the Rossoneri in the Champions League knockout stages is the greatest regret of these two legendary coaches.

Nevertheless, against other Italian giants, Sir Alex Ferguson and his Manchester United have consistently delivered excellent performances. Against the current Serie A leader Inter Milan, the Sir and his team have had very favorable results over the past decade. In the 1998-99 season, Manchester United eliminated Inter Milan in the Champions League quarterfinals with a 2-0 home win and a 1-1 away draw, advancing with a total score of 3-1. Last season, Manchester United again eliminated Inter Milan in the Champions League round of 16 with a 0-0 away draw and a 2-0 home victory, advancing with a total score of 2-0.

Against Juventus, Sir Alex also holds significant advantages. In the 1998-99 season Champions League semifinal, Manchester United advanced with a total score of 4-3 over Juventus, playing home first and then away. In the 2002-03 season, Manchester United swept Juventus in the Champions League group stage with a 2-1 home win and a 3-0 away victory. For another Italian giant, Roma, Sir Alex and his Manchester United are particularly adept at handling them. In the 2006-07 season, in the Champions League quarterfinals, Manchester United defeated Roma 1-2 away and crushed them 7-1 at home, advancing with a total score of 8-3. In the 2007-08 season, Manchester United achieved an impressive 3 wins and 1 draw in 4 matches against Roma in both the Champions League group stage and knockout rounds.

Sir Alex Ferguson leading Manchester United against Inter Milan, Juventus, and Roma has a high chance of success, but he and the legendary Sir Matt Busby could not overcome AC Milan throughout their coaching careers, which is undoubtedly a regret. If Manchester United can eliminate AC Milan in this Champions League campaign, Sir Alex Ferguson would have fulfilled a lifelong wish before his retirement.
