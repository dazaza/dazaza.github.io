300 fines issued to 8 bus routes

by zsh0827840 on 2012-02-21 11:19:24

Our reporter (Mei Guosheng) - "During the period from May 5 to May 12, the No. 572 and seven other bus routes heading towards the Yanzi Road station on Minzu Road received 300 penalty tickets from the Wuchang Traffic Brigade, all for 'crossing the line' while parking." On the 7th, Mr. Tu, the route manager of Bus Route 572 at Public Transport Company No. 5, complained to our newspaper hotline in frustration.

Mr. Tu explained: At the end of April, renovations were taking place along Yanzi Road on Minzu Road, with building materials piled up on the pedestrian walkways at the bus stops, forcing waiting passengers onto the motor vehicle lanes. This resulted in incoming buses being unable to park as per regulations and had to cross the parking lines instead. At the previous station, the Armed Police Hospital station, the width of the entry and stop line from the manhole cover to the road is approximately 2 meters, while the bus body itself is 2.5 meters wide. Additionally, there is a large tree leaning towards the road beside the station sign. How could the driver enter the station without crossing the line?

From May 5 to 12, within just 8 days, the buses stopping at these two stations on Routes 572, 529, 521, 573, 515, 530, 14, and 19 received a total of 300 penalty tickets, each costing 100 yuan.

On the 7th, I observed at the Yanzi Road bus station on Minzu Road that the pedestrian walkway beside the station sign was filled with old tiles, wood, construction barriers, and other materials. Waiting passengers had mostly moved to the motor vehicle lane, and three large blue trash bins squeezed alongside took up considerable space, requiring passengers to squeeze tightly against the bins to board the bus. The entry and stop line width from the manhole cover to the road at this station is 2.7 meters, only allowing 2.5-meter-wide buses to carefully enter the station. From 4:20 PM to 4:50 PM, I observed at this station that out of 19 buses entering the station, not a single one could enter without crossing the line.

At the next station, the Armed Police Hospital station, it was seen that for buses entering the station, their wheels closer to the manhole cover had to press onto the sidewalk to barely ensure the outer wheels did not cross the line; otherwise, if they didn't cross the line, the slanted tree above the station sign would block the bus's path.

Yesterday, when the Wuchang Traffic Brigade was interviewed by reporters, they introduced that the basis for the 300 penalty tickets was that the drivers of these eight bus routes violated relevant provisions of the "Road Traffic Safety Law": "Wuhan public buses must not pick up or drop off passengers outside designated stops." In recent times, the public transport sector had also appealed to them multiple times. They have already stopped video surveillance at these two bus stops since May 13 and conducted relevant investigations. They have also assisted the gardening department in dealing with the slanted tree at the Armed Police Hospital station. Yesterday afternoon, the traffic brigade specially dispatched two officers to accompany the reporter to the Armed Police Hospital station, where on-site measurements showed that the entry and stop line width from the manhole cover to the road was 2.23 meters, making it indeed difficult for 2.5-meter-wide buses to "comply" with the regulations when parking.

The Wuchang Traffic Brigade stated that they will handle the penalty tickets involving "justifiable reasons" among the 300 tickets appropriately.
