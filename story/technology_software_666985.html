How Personal Websites Evolve

by hebei5602i on 2012-02-16 12:22:37

If you want to create a personal website, the positioning aspect needs to be determined first. 1. Clear objectives. Most clear objectives are thought of during the planning and conception phase of building a website, and often these objectives are quite "grand." However, whether these goals are reasonable and truly clear requires careful investigation, inference, and planning. 2. The personality of the website. If website culture is similar to corporate culture, it may even be easier to understand. Due to the saying "there is no second network," when creating a website, it's best to find a good niche entry point, giving it its own unique qualities and individuality. This way, rapid development will be easier to achieve. Even if there's no available niche and you still want to improve an industry, you must have your own concept to differentiate yourself from others. This individuality can be reflected in many aspects such as content, graphic design, ideas, operation methods, models, etc. 3. A clear development strategy. Many websites start by positioning themselves as large-scale but end up stuck in the mud due to insufficient capabilities, much like someone with shallow skills trying to practice the "Heaven and Earth Great Shift," which easily leads to disastrous consequences. Therefore, for a website to develop rapidly, it should not initially crave a large quantity or completeness of content. Instead, it should focus on its own characteristics and let its individuality attract the target audience. This way, the initial cost is low, and operations become relatively easier. Then, as it develops, content can gradually be added to make it truly grow larger. 4. Clear profit points. Building a website is mostly for making money; therefore, before starting, while considering how to quickly develop, one should also consider how the website will generate profits and whether these profit models can be effectively implemented. In the past few years, many websites had high traffic but no profits, ultimately leading to their closure.
