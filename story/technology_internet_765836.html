Li Kaifu: Conscience-driven Weibo posts, doesn't know online promoters

by anonymous on 2013-08-26 14:08:19

A TV news program aired two nights ago featured a segment lasting only four or five seconds, which has made Li Kaifu, chairman of Innovation Works, the focus of netizens' attention over the past two days. The segment referenced Li Kaifu's Weibo homepage, highlighting text discussing the relationship between influential figures (so-called "Da V") forwarding unverified information and the spread of internet rumors. Li Kaifu has not yet directly responded to the aforementioned program. However, he subsequently posted on Weibo stating that he "posts and forwards Weibo messages in good conscience," that he "does not know any online promoters or planners," and suggested that relevant departments "use big data technology to catch those who create and spread rumors."

The TV footage sparked speculation among netizens.

This TV news segment extended from the detention of online promoters such as Qin Huohuo and Li Erchai Si to an examination of how internet rumors are generated. Previously, according to reports by Beijing police based on public tips, they "successfully dismantled an internet promotion company —— Beijing E’erma Interactive Marketing Planning Co., Ltd. —— that deliberately manufactured and spread rumors on the internet, maliciously damaged others' reputations, and illegally sought economic benefits." The main members of this company were Qin Huohuo, Li Erchai Si, et al.

The program introduced some brief cases of Qin Huohuo and others manipulating online events, then discussed the relationship between online promoters creating online events and influential figures (Da V), mentioning that "in the process of spreading online rumors, a large number of real-name verified Da Vs with numerous fans also disseminate and forward these rumors. Their forwarding without verifying the information exacerbates the spread of rumors, amplifying the negative effects of the rumors several times over," accompanied by a screenshot of a certain Da V's homepage.

Although the footage was not entirely clear, based on some of the photos and text within it, many netizens immediately recognized it as Li Kaifu's Weibo homepage. For example, netizen @Dong Lu once said on Weibo, "Although only half of the face was shown, I think it's @Li Kaifu, do you agree?" Perhaps coincidentally, in yesterday's Beijing Evening News report on online promoters, one promoter also mentioned Li Kaifu, saying, "For different projects, we need to find suitable people... I can't possibly ask Xue Manzi to post about cosmetics, or Li Kaifu to post about car-related projects, right?"

Claiming his Weibo "would not have problems"

As of 23:00 yesterday, Li Kaifu's Weibo had more than 51.39 million followers, with a cumulative total of 13,413 Weibo posts. Li Kaifu has not yet directly responded to the aforementioned TV news program, nor has he replied to interview requests from journalists.

However, shortly after the aforementioned TV news aired the previous evening, Li Kaifu posted what seemed to be a response on Weibo, stating, "I firmly believe in the importance of spreading positive energy. I think criticism brings progress. I insist on addressing issues factually without taking sides. I agree that the greater the influence, the greater the responsibility. I oppose the fabrication and arbitrary labeling of rumors."

Subsequently, Li Kaifu suggested "using big data technology to catch those who create and spread rumors." He wrote on Weibo, "I suggest relevant units restore all deleted Weibo posts and forwards, using past reported and confirmed rumors and the most severe rumors identified by relevant units as seeds to build a big data rumor database. Then analyze the forwarding routes and zombie accounts to fairly assess the biggest rumor creators, the biggest promoters and planners, and the biggest rumor spreaders." Li Kaifu also expressed, "I am willing to participate in such a technical task."

In addition, Li Kaifu posted two related Weibo messages yesterday morning, stating, "Some netizens asked about my account evaluation results? My answer: I post and forward Weibo messages in good conscience, I'm not afraid of data restoration, I don't know these (online) promoters or planners, so of course there won't be any problems."
