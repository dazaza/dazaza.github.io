Gutter oil is popular, what can be eaten to remove toxins from the body

by yangliu435sh on 2012-02-29 16:52:37

A "edible oil Co., Ltd." in Hebei province has many regular customers from various places such as Hebei, Shanxi and Henan provinces who often come to buy their oil. Some well-known food processing enterprises also purchase oil from this company. This factory has complete production certification, and as the saying goes: "as long as you find the right person, any certificate can be processed."

These gutter oil processing plants usually don't sell their products in small quantities; instead, they mainly sell to food factories or construction sites. The grain and oil wholesale market is another important destination for "gutter oil". An informed source told reporters that after the oil was transported to the wholesale market by tanker trucks, the merchants at the market would repackage the oil into white gasoline drums weighing dozens of kilograms each. These bulk oil drums generally have no labels.

At the Shuitun wholesale market in Beijing, a blue advertising board stood prominently stating it was a "designated wholesale market" by a national department. When reporters went undercover there, apart from seeing various small-packaged branded edible oils, white bulk oil drums were everywhere, making it impossible to identify their contents.

One merchant boss said that the Shuitun market indeed sold "that kind of oil" made from swill oil or waste chicken, duck and pig fat, but "recently, with tightened controls, everyone has become more restrained, and fewer people are buying." In front of the reporter, the boss called one of his previous suppliers, who replied that he had no stock at the moment. The boss then told the reporter: "If you really want it, go to Tianjin yourself; they have stock there."

Of great concern is that higher-grade oil enters the market under a more formal guise. During an undercover investigation of a "gutter oil" processing den, reporters saw many neatly stacked, properly packaged "soybean blended oil" containers.

As an illegal operation, every individual in the "gutter oil" profit chain has a natural sense of caution, and over time, these operations have developed organizational structures and violent tendencies, making the discovery and evidence collection against illegal "gutter oil" production extremely difficult.

Without a "trusted person" referral, it's hard to get involved in the industry. Pig farms are one of the main sources of raw materials for "gutter oil". The reporter decided to break through at the pig farm first. "Without a trusted person, even if you find a pig farm and see the swill oil, they won't pay attention to you, let alone sell it to you," said an informed source, indicating that without a trusted person's recommendation, it's very hard to enter the industry.

Even with a trusted person's introduction, the other party remains highly vigilant during the first meeting. During the reporter's first meeting with a contact person from a black den, they were highly alert and thoroughly checked both inside and outside the reporter's car.

Some dens and swill oil transfer stations are almost always guarded by fierce dogs, making them hard to approach. They have strong violent tendencies. During a visit to a so-called "wildfire" gutter oil refining workshop, due to insufficient knowledge of the industry rules, we rashly entered the yard and asked if they had any oil for sale. Suddenly, several bare-armed men with tattoos all over their arms and chests rushed out of the inner room, shouting at us, "There's no oil here! Hurry up and leave!" But we clearly saw that behind their house, large areas of land were scorched black and soaked with oil stains. According to informants, these places are controlled by criminal organizations, and the surrounding villagers dare not interfere, allowing them to "light fires" every night, and the land where "wildfires" have been lit won't grow grass.

During a conversation with a contact person from a "gutter oil" processing den, the other party threatened: "I have connections in both legal and illegal circles. If you dare to play tricks on me, just wait for the consequences!" Such threats and tests were encountered multiple times.

It has a certain degree of organization. An informed source said that this illegal business lacks legal protection. To survive and expand, besides competing on price, the most important thing is to have people to strengthen its own strength. These people gradually form "power", and have a "boss".

In Beijing, the person in charge of an underground filling plant is known as the "boss" within the circle. As long as his name is mentioned, the other party will immediately show respect. Industry insiders told reporters that his factory can produce bucket-packed edible oil of any brand, using genuine buckets from manufacturers with anti-counterfeiting features. Every day, the products are delivered to some supermarkets and stores in Beijing, with a daily output of about 10 tons.

The Tianjin area also has such a well-known figure who holds significant influence within the "circle".

An informed source who has been involved in the "gutter oil" business for many years said that thousands of people in Beijing are engaged in this line of work, each having their own division of labor, closely cooperating, with an annual output value of hundreds of millions.

When humans consume gutter oil, they ingest large amounts of aflatoxin and other harmful substances, increasing the detoxification burden on the liver and kidneys, negatively impacting the entire body's absorption and immune system.
