During the professional subject interview process, the angles used to examine candidates mainly include professional knowledge, research direction, graduation thesis, published papers, etc. Therefore, guidance for candidates is also provided according to these aspects. First, professional knowledge. In the postgraduate entrance examination interview, the assessment of professional knowledge is more flexible. For example, teachers may ask about some hot news in the industry and require you to talk about your understanding of these hot events based on your own knowledge.

Overall, many candidates become incoherent due to nervousness during the interview. Therefore, before answering questions, it is essential to organize your thoughts, quickly sort out the questions to be answered into several points, and then answer them briefly according to the order. The answering process should not be too lengthy; simplicity is better.

Scores from various institutions are being announced one after another. Following the usual practice of previous years, the复试 (interview) generally takes place between late March and early May. Previously, it was a normal interview, but now it has mostly been changed to a differential interview, with the differential ratio usually controlled around 120%. For admission units with sufficient student resources, the differential interview ratio can be moderately expanded. Every year, some candidates fail in the interview, making the competition extremely fierce. Professional Subject Interview: Theory Combined with Practice

Interview Communication: Play to Strengths and Avoid Weaknesses

It is also necessary to pay attention to some interview communication skills on how to play to strengths and avoid weaknesses during the interview process: first, response is very important. Second, do not be nervous. Speak more about familiar topics and try to avoid unfamiliar ones. Do not pretend to know what you don't know, as teachers generally delve deeper into a question. If you realize at the beginning that your grasp is not strong, do not follow the teacher's line of thought; instead, guide the teacher to a field you are familiar with. Third, pay attention to honesty. During the interview, showcase your good side and talk about your excellent qualities without exaggeration. It is best to express your interest in the major, your strong learning ability, and your research capability in professional knowledge. Your answers to questions should be reasonable and evidence-based, using dialectical methods to comprehensively address issues. Do not casually exaggerate or criticize a certain school of thought or viewpoint. For questions you do not understand, you must not pretend to know; you can say "I do not know" or "I am unclear" and humbly seek advice from the teacher. In short, pay attention to honesty.

For English review, it is necessary to strengthen your English listening and speaking abilities. English listening and oral tests are important components of the interview. On one hand, through speaking English, allowing tutors to understand some basic information about you is very important; on the other hand, through listening to English, you can understand some basic information, which is also very important. During the preparation for the English interview: strengthen listening practice, prepare an English self-introduction, and make sure to memorize it thoroughly; additionally, prepare some discussable topics. These topics can roughly be divided into two categories: the first category includes those commonly encountered in daily life; the second category includes those related to your major. English Interview: Prepare in Advance and Strengthen Practice

The interview mainly includes two parts: English interview and professional interview.

First, during the interview process, apart from fully preparing for the content of the interview (professional subjects and English), it is also necessary to pay attention to other details such as etiquette and dress code. This is because the impression the tutor has of the candidate is very important and will directly affect the final score and admission situation. There are mainly the following aspects: Dress code, candidates should follow the principle of "simple, generous, clean." On the day of the interview, male candidates should wear suits, comb their hair neatly, and keep their beards clean. This is both a sign of respect for the tutor and will make the teacher feel very comfortable. Female candidates should dress solemnly and generously, not too flashy; if they have long hair, it should be tied up to appear spirited and efficient; they can apply light makeup appropriately, avoiding heavy makeup. Secondly, in terms of etiquette, candidates should be polite and modest. Before entering the examination room, whether the door is closed or not, they should gently knock and enter only after receiving permission from the teacher; when seeing the teacher, they must bow and greet the teacher actively; sit down gently after receiving permission from the teacher, which can first improve their impression score. During the process of answering questions, they should be polite and confident, not wandering with their eyes, sitting or standing upright, speaking loudly and not too fast so that each examiner can hear clearly, which can further improve their impression score. When leaving the examination room after the written test, they should bow again and bid farewell to the teacher, expressing once again the strong desire to study at the school. This can once again improve the impression score. In short, paying attention to the above details during the interview process will greatly improve the teacher's impression of you. You can believe that your interview score will not be low, which will greatly improve your total score and thus greatly increase your chances of being admitted.

Countermeasures: Since graduate education avoids silent individuals, candidates must avoid discussing matters superficially, merely focusing on the event itself, nor should they talk about theories in vain. Instead, they should pay attention to combining theory with practice, using theory to guide practice, or summarizing and elevating practice to theory. Second, research direction. Many majors clearly define research directions during the graduate stage, and the content examined varies greatly. The best reference for preparation regarding research direction is the tutor's research direction. The tutor's research direction reflects the focus he pays attention to during this period, and his interests during the interview may also be related to his research direction. Countermeasure: For this, candidates should regularly read authoritative journals related to their majors and have a deep understanding of the academic views, papers, and monographs of the tutors they are applying to. Special reminder: If your view is consistent with the tutor's or can innovate on his basis, he will definitely leave a good impression on you. Third, graduation thesis. If you are applying for a master's degree in the same undergraduate major, many teachers will ask about your undergraduate graduation thesis. Because it is a summary of four years of professional study in university, it shows to a certain extent the candidate's research ability. For candidates switching majors, the choice of direction for the undergraduate graduation thesis reflects the candidate's sensitivity to thesis writing and preference for the major, which is also a key point of attention for the tutor. Countermeasure: Before the interview, you must fully grasp the content of the graduation thesis so that you can explain it clearly if the teacher asks. Also, you can prepare the combination points between the undergraduate thesis and the graduate major, so that you have something to say when the time comes. Fourth, published papers and writings. Some tutors may ask during the interview whether the candidate has any published papers, especially for candidates with equivalent qualifications, which may be mandatory. Therefore, preparation in this area is also necessary. Countermeasure: If you have published papers and writings, it is best to photocopy them in advance and give them to the teacher face-to-face. If not, you can elaborate on your vision for scientific research. Fifth, read writings. Please introduce a professional book you have read, which is almost a required question during interviews for liberal arts majors. This is a window for the tutor to understand the candidate. Countermeasure: Candidates can completely solve this problem during regular review. Reading related professional works can bring you joy in reading amidst the tedious review process, and the wisdom of scholars can provide inspiration.
