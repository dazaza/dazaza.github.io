Infant Feeding - 9 Major Mistakes in Feeding Your Child

by gege on 2012-02-27 20:03:06

Taking care of children seriously every day and feeding them the most nutritious food, but is it necessarily correct? If you are feeding your child using any of the following methods, be careful!

Misconception 1: Adding eggs to breakfast

A high-quality nutritious breakfast must include four parts: grains, animal-based foods, dairy products, and vegetables or fruits. A breakfast containing three of these parts is considered average in quality, while one that only contains one or two parts is considered poor in quality. Grain products, such as steamed buns, noodles, porridge, etc., play an important role in children's height development. Vegetables and fruits provide vitamins for the morning.

Misconception 2: Feeding children calcium tablets excessively

If infants and toddlers consume enough dairy products, their bodies do not lack calcium, but rather lack vitamin D, which promotes calcium absorption. Instead of giving children calcium tablets, it would be better to supplement them with cod liver oil or increase outdoor activities and sun exposure.

Misconception 3: Bean products are vegetables

In fact, bean products can be considered meat substitutes. Their main component is plant protein and they do not contain the vitamins and dietary fiber that vegetables are rich in. Therefore, bean products should not be regarded as vegetables. Even after eating bean products, some vegetables still need to be consumed.

Misconception 4: Children with dryness need to eat bananas or sesame oil

Eating these does not work. The most important thing is to develop good bowel habits in children, with fixed defecation times. When food is digested and reaches the large intestine, the main function of the large intestine is to absorb water and produce blood; peristalsis - expelling food residue from the body. If a daily bowel movement habit can be formed, food residue can be expelled from the body in time, thereby relieving constipation. The longer the food residue stays in the large intestine, the more thoroughly the water is absorbed.

Misconception 5: Children cannot be exposed to sunlight

Exposure to sunlight can promote human blood circulation, enhance metabolism and immune function, especially in preventing osteoporosis in children or adults, showing special therapeutic effects.

However, some parents cover their children with gauze, wear hats, or let them bask in the sun through glass. Experts point out that this has no effect. Sunbathing should allow the skin to directly contact sunlight. Expose the back of the head, wrists, ankles, and buttocks to prevent illness. If concerned about strong sunlight stimulating the skin, parents can use early morning or evening sunlight, or stay under trees or eaves, or open windows to use reflected light, benefiting the baby.

Misconception 6: Offal equals liver

Animal offal is nutrient-rich and is a high-protein food that greatly benefits children's development. Many parents like to feed their children pig liver soup or stir-fried pig liver, thinking this is enough nutrition. In fact, animal offal also includes heart, lungs, and kidneys. The liver often contains harmful components and is not suitable for excessive consumption. Therefore, parents should feed their children other types of animal offal.

Misconception 7: Snacks harm health

Snacks are not equivalent to junk food. High-calorie, low-nutrient foods are junk food. For some nutritious snacks, experts suggest that they can be consumed in moderation without affecting regular meals, to supplement nutrients in the body. Especially for slim children, it’s best to give them snacks right after dinner until just before sleeping. Elementary school children can also use this method to enhance development.

Misconception 8: Peeling vegetables and fruits makes them cleaner

Many parents believe that the peels of vegetables and fruits are unhygienic and always want to remove them before eating. However, when you remove the peel, you also remove many nutrients. In fact, boiling vegetables with their peels on is very nutritious. Leaves are more nutritious than stems, and apple peels contain a lot of iron. The correct approach is to clean the surfaces of vegetables and fruits thoroughly and then eat them with the peels on.

Misconception 9: Toddlers dislike eating meat

Actually, children dislike eating tough meats like chicken, beef, or mutton. However, they still love tender meats like bullfrog, shrimp, and eel.
