More than 10,000 parents jointly call for the launch of the college entrance examination away from registered residence

by dianxin8 on 2011-05-04 12:06:48

The calls for the state to resolve the异地gaokao (college entrance exam in a place other than where one is registered) issue for children of migrant workers are growing increasingly louder. The ks5u Gaokao Resource Network mentioned earlier that our country is expected to launch a system for gaokao in places other than where candidates are registered, see this article: "Hope that the gaokao in places other than where candidates are registered for children of migrant workers can be realized as soon as possible." Since July this year, a petition initiated by nine parents of migrant workers living in Beijing titled "Petition for the Cancellation of Hukou Restrictions on the College Entrance Examination" and "Urgent Suggestions for the 2011 Beijing Gaokao Registration" has garnered over ten thousand signatures and has been repeatedly submitted to multiple departments numerous times. For more details, please refer to the following report from the ks5u Gaokao Resource Network.

Relevant responsible persons from the Growth Planning Department of the Municipal Education Commission carefully listened to the suggestions put forward by the parents, which were bound into a thick stack of documents titled "Petition." The main content of the "Petition" allows non-Beijing hukou students who have completed three years of high school in Beijing to register for the college entrance examination, including students studying in Beijing and people working in Beijing from other places.

Three parents of non-Beijing hukou senior high school students among the representatives said that they should participate in enrollment in the place where they take the exam. More than ten parent representatives of students from other places again submitted their suggestions to the Ministry of Education and the Beijing Municipal Education Commission, stating that they would quickly forward them to the relevant departments, indicating that they would respond to the relevant departments. They suggested that Beijing implement temporary measures. As for which region's test papers will be used subsequently, parent representative Mr. Zhang said that they would continue to collect signatures until reaching 20,000.

Some parent representatives of students from other places in Beijing began submitting suggestion books to the Ministry of Education and the Beijing Municipal Education Commission, which will trial the practice of allowing children of migrant workers to take the college entrance examination in places other than where they are registered. Both organizations accepted the suggestion materials. These signatures were collected over several months by dozens of parents on the streets. Yesterday, relevant responsible persons from the Student Affairs Department of the education department met with the parents and pointed out these issues. According to the monthly schedule, the "Petition" was submitted to the Ministry of Education and the Beijing Municipal Education Commission five times so far.

After listening to the suggestions, the petition service staff of the Ministry of Education did not promise any response. Next Wednesday, but the representative group will continue for a year. The Ministry of Education is expected to start research work this year on formulating measures for children of migrant workers to take local college entrance exams after completing compulsory education. According to the ks5u Gaokao Resource Network, Beijing's 2011 college entrance exam registration will begin on December 1st. Their actions are not just aimed at calling for change.

It is reported that this suggestion has gained support from an increasing number of people from all sectors of society. Ninety percent of the signatories are parents of migrant workers coming to Beijing. They hope that Beijing will allow the offspring of a large number of migrant populations to take the college entrance exam in Beijing. With a blue cover and prominently marked in large font, the citizen signature section stands out significantly. The "Petition" signatures exceeded ten thousand for the first time. So far, the relevant departments have accepted the "Petition" from the parents.

Since July this year, it has been successively submitted to the Municipal Education Commission and the Ministry of Education, calling for the resolution of the college entrance examination qualification issue for students borrowing their education in other places. It remains fundamentally conditioned on having a Beijing hukou.

According to the ks5u Gaokao Resource Network, they indeed proposed reform suggestions for the college entrance examination policy. Yesterday, when the opportunity matures, this suggestion has received more and more support from all sectors of society. Among the signatories, there are 90% of parents of migrant workers coming to Beijing, hoping that Beijing can allow the offspring of a large number of migrant populations to take the college entrance exam in Beijing. Prominently marked in large font, the citizen signature section stands out significantly. The "Petition" signatures exceeded ten thousand for the first time. Until now, from July this year, based on pilot research. Not opening up to any candidates with non-local hukou, it is not something that the Ministry of Education alone can decide. Hoping to provide reference for the research on gaokao in different places, Beijing will start gaokao registration, participating in the 2011 gaokao, although there is still no hope so far. Policies need to be jointly formulated by Beijing and multiple government departments, the parents expressed.

We hope that this joint action by the parents can effectively promote the implementation of the gaokao system in places other than where candidates are registered. For more related information on gaokao, please continue to follow other reports from the ks5u Gaokao Resource Network.
