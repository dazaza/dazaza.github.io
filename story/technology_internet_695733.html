2012 Hurun Global Rich List released, real estate tops the list of Chinese tycoons' wealth

by abcj5hehe on 2012-03-07 17:03:31

On February 6, Hurun Research Institute released the "2012 Hurun Global Rich List". Mexican billionaire Carlos Slim topped the list with $55 billion, Bill Gates followed closely with $50 billion ranked second, and Warren Buffett was third with $48 billion. Zong Qinghou became mainland China's richest man with $10.5 billion, also being the only mainland Chinese billionaire on this list.

This year, five Chinese billionaires made it onto the list, four from Hong Kong and one from mainland China. At the age of 84, Li Ka-shing has become the [2012 Richest Man in China] with a wealth of $24 billion. Zhou Yutong, the 87-year-old founder of Chow Tai Fook, had a very fruitful year, ranking 49th globally with a fortune of $14 billion. The IPO of Chow Tai Fook last year brought Zhou Yutong a personal wealth of $10 billion.

Zong Qinghou, at the age of 67, ranked 78th with a fortune of $10.5 billion, becoming the [2012 Richest Man in Mainland China], and also the only mainland Chinese billionaire on this list.

The list shows that the most global billionaires come from the finance and investment industries, followed by IT, with retail ranking third. However, for Chinese billionaires, the top three industries are real estate, manufacturing, and finance & investment; IT ranks fifth, and retail ranks tenth.
