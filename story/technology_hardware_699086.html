by bhelki913 on 2012-03-09 13:08:18

Nanjing Real Estate Lawyers Protecting Rights: Both the Gross Floor Area and the Usable Floor Area Should Be Specified in the Contract. According to relevant judicial interpretations, when the usable floor area or gross floor area of a house does not match the agreed-upon area, if there is an agreement in the contract, it shall be followed; if there is no agreement, the error ratio within 3% should be settled based on actual measurements. If it exceeds 3%, the buyer can request a refund and claim the return of the paid purchase price and interest. Experts remind that attention should be paid to the details of the agreement when signing the contract. Therefore, it is suggested that the buyer agrees on both the gross floor area and the usable floor area when signing the contract.

To prevent planning shrinkage, detailed supplementary clauses should be added to avoid changes in planning. Some developers may depict the later phases of their plans in brochures and include exemption clauses or very light breach of contract responsibilities in the contracts to gain smaller breach costs and differences in benefits from neighborhood planning changes. Experts remind that the developer's plan should be included as a supplementary clause in the contract. It is suggested that buyers first check whether the developer has obtained the development rights for subsequent plots and then check if the community plan has been approved by the planning department. They may also require the developer to include this plan as a supplementary clause in the contract and agree on higher penalties in the supplementary clauses.

For reduced supporting facilities, related details should be clearly stated in the contract. The clubhouse is not a mandatory facility in real estate development, and buyers generally cannot claim to terminate the contract based on this. Many buyers often only focus on the house conditions when signing the contract and forget to agree on related terms of supporting facilities, leading to constant disputes. Experts remind that agreements on supporting facilities should clearly specify responsibility. It is suggested that owners clearly specify related terms such as delivery time, ownership, service functions, and service objects of the clubhouse in the pre-sale contract and clarify responsibility.

For reduced floor height, clear breach compensation amounts should be agreed upon. Legally speaking, the contract is based on the written content in the contract, so a typographical error cannot affect the breach fact. If the floor height of commercial housing does not match the agreed-upon height in the contract, it is considered a breach of contract, and the developer must bear the responsibility. If the owner does not agree on the compensation amount from the developer, the court can only determine the compensation amount reasonably, which is generally lower than the owner's expectations. Experts remind that clear breach compensation amounts should be agreed upon regarding reduced floor heights. It is suggested that buyers clearly specify breach responsibilities and compensation amounts in the contract.

For reduced usage periods, the land nature should be clarified before signing. Many buyers are unclear about the specific differences between land use periods and building use periods. Nowadays, most developers directly acquire land from government departments or transfer it from other developers. After buying a house, the usage period enjoyed by the buyer should be: the land use period specified in the original land transfer contract minus the period already used by the developer. Experts remind that it is best for buyers to understand the land nature and the starting period of the house land use rights at relevant departments before signing.
