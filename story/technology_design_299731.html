Inter Milan could lose 20 million euros if they lose in the Champions League, and a club legend has revealed that Mourinho's contract stipulates he will be fired if they don't advance from the group stage.

Inter Milan's Loss in Champions League Could Cost 20 Million Euros: Legend Reveals Clause in Mourinho's Contract for Elimination

According to Sina Sports, this coming Wednesday, Inter Milan faces not only a crucial match that will define the direction of their season but also one that will significantly impact the club's financial gains. If Inter Milan loses to Kazan Rubin or draws by more than two goals (2-2, 3-3, or 4-4), they won't just be saying goodbye to the round of 16 in the UEFA Champions League but also forfeiting nearly 20 million euros in match bonuses from this stage up to the final. Moreover, if Dynamo Kyiv defeats Barcelona under such circumstances, Inter Milan could lose even the qualification for the Europa League. Such an unfortunate outcome would deliver a significant blow to the team built by Mourinho, creating a tense atmosphere within the club as they prepare for the upcoming match against Kazan Rubin.

In Saturday's game against Juventus, Inter Milan coach Jose Mourinho received his fourth red card in Serie A. Despite his notorious reputation from his time in Portugal and England, Mourinho has already surpassed the record set by his predecessor, Roberto Mancini, in just 15 months in Serie A. In four years with Inter Milan, Mancini accumulated only three red cards, while Mourinho reached four in just 15 months. Remarkably, all four of Mourinho's red cards were issued within a single year (January 25 vs. Sampdoria, March 15 vs. Fiorentina, September 20 vs. Cagliari, and last Saturday vs. Juventus). Coincidentally, the referee who sent off Mourinho last Saturday, Sacchi, had previously expelled Mancini during Inter Milan's Italian Cup match against Lazio on May 7, 2008.

The Turin-based newspaper "Tuttosport" reported that Mourinho did not attend the post-match press conference due to "physical condition reasons," though it doesn't imply he left Turin in a rage. Certain incidents witnessed at the Stadio Olimpico di Torino left Mourinho extremely dissatisfied. While the primary cause was disagreement with referee Sacchi's decisions, some players' attitudes and behaviors also upset Mourinho. Similar to what happened in Barcelona, Mourinho felt betrayed by his players, particularly Mario Balotelli, who not only participated in the scuffle following Marchisio's goal but also failed to make any significant impact on the game, as has happened multiple times before.

After returning to Milan on Sunday, Mourinho addressed the team, avoiding overly harsh criticism of the defeat at the Stadio Olimpico di Torino. Mourinho clearly wanted to avoid further exacerbating the tension within the team, which had already been heightened during the Juventus match ahead of the critical UEFA Champions League clash. However, this match is vital for him. Yesterday, legendary Inter Milan player Sandro Mazzola revealed in an interview: "I believe if Inter Milan fails to advance from the group stage, Mourinho will be dismissed. As far as I know, there should be such a clause in his contract. After all, being eliminated by Kazan Rubin in the group stage is different from losing to Manchester United or Barcelona in the final."

Currently, Inter Milan must focus solely on how to win this crucial match for the team. For a club that has only won one out of their last ten UEFA Champions League matches (a November 4 away game against Dynamo Kyiv), this appears almost impossible. To boost morale, Inter Milan plans to organize fans at San Siro to cheer for the team, believing that "this way, the cheers won't be scattered, and the atmosphere will be lively." From another perspective, this plan might also increase the pressure on visiting Kazan Rubin and the referees appointed by UEFA.

"Tuttosport" also disclosed that over the weekend, Massimo Moratti watched the team's match against Juventus while on a short holiday in the mountains with his family. The second-half goal scored by Marchisio weighed heavily on everyone at Inter Milan. With the decisive match against Kazan Rubin approaching, the heavy defeat in Turin added more pressure to Mourinho's squad. Before the home game against Kazan Rubin, Moratti, currently on vacation, will return to the club to support and motivate the team. Disappointed by the loss in Turin, which didn't meet his expectations, Moratti was even more frustrated by the fact that Inter Milan almost always lost when playing away against strong teams (previously in Manchester, Barcelona, and now in Turin) and only woke up after receiving a hard blow (as in Kazan and Kyiv). Moratti was disappointed by the lack of identity in the team, and even more so because the failure in Turin meant that, aside from one Milan Derby, Inter Milan had almost suffered defeats in all major competitions this season—from the Italian Super Cup held in Beijing during the summer to the match against Barcelona at Camp Nou. With high hopes, Moratti made his first trip to an away game this season, only to witness his team get crushed alongside his friend Joan Laporta. Now, facing a critical moment in European competition, Moratti knows that this isn't the time for condemnation; a significant victory—such as defeating Kazan Rubin—would be enough to restore the team's normal mindset. The previous match against Fiorentina proved that Inter Milan knows how to survive adversity, so the most important thing now is to relieve the team's pressure. However, come Wednesday, the ball beneath the feet of Inter Milan's players may feel as heavy as a thousand pounds...
