I thought I couldn't live without you

by gege on 2012-01-03 00:33:22

I once thought that I couldn't live without you.

All along, the hardest thing to do was to make a choice, and the hardest thing to affirm was my love for you.

Because I loved you, you always acted without fear of consequence, repeatedly talking about breaking up. You thought I wouldn't take it seriously, and in the end, I would still forgive you. Maybe it was like this many times before. It was you who made me see my weakness, my humility, and my repeated retreats. I should have realized long ago that two unsuitable people shouldn't force things. Forcing things will only lead to one result: no result.

This time, I just took it seriously. No compromise. The breakup you mentioned finally moved me with such an ordinary reason - we were unsuitable. I just told myself that I didn't love you anymore. Thus, I decided to let go, completely let go. I am still the same person as I was at the beginning. After the breakup, we can no longer be friends. I deleted your QQ, your social network accounts, your phone number. Ironically, that string of numbers which I used to complain was hard to remember now refuses to fade away. But what does it matter? It's just a string of numbers I will never dial again.

It's not that I'm cruel. Almost 5 years have passed, and all sorts of emotions come to mind right now. My eyes are sore. What I've done for you, what I haven't done for you, are all past events, and I don't want to remember them anymore. Looking through my blog posts from the past few years, I only see heartbreak. There were happy times, but I can't ignore the unhappy ones either. They're all in the past now, all history.

I know I'm not a kid anymore. I clearly know what I want, and I also understand what you want. I can't give you what you need, nor can I get what I need. Continuing to hold on is just a mockery. This relationship, I can no longer continue. Am I deceiving myself or deceiving you? Constant arguments and cold wars have worn me out completely. I can only give up. It was your choice to give up, and all I could do was agree, and agree.

But in the dead of night, I often dream of that girl who has lost all her sunshine. She is still me. Always refusing to face the sun, I mistakenly thought I was just in the corner, just sad. I've always known that I've already lost my true self, just unwilling to face reality, unwilling to change, unwilling to save myself. Now, I can no longer indulge myself. I know it's time to choose to do something, to make some changes. The first thing that needs to change is actually you.

Cutting off contact means cutting off thoughts. What I was used to, liked, depended on, relied on, are all gone now. I still have myself, I only have myself now. It's not sorrowful, nor heartbreaking, just a faint estrangement. It's not that I can't love, not that I don't want to love, it's that I dare not love, I don't want to love, I refuse to love, I no longer love. That's finality.

For such an ending, it was expected. There's no regret, no curse, no expectation, no despair, just so calmly ended. This is the best ending, we both know it.

No more recounting the happiness or misfortunes of the past, no more assuming whether we should have been different at the start. What has happened cannot be changed. From now on, you just existed. Please occupy your own corner quietly, without words. Fortunately, I am still myself, and you remain yourself.

Five years, I wish to describe lightly. After all, time passes, and what grows old is just oneself.

I once thought that I couldn't live without you.

But now, without you, I still raise my calm smile under the brilliant sunlight.

Starting journey.

Beginning.

I just slowly walk on my little path, hoping for peace, composure, undisturbed.

This is the last time I write about our past. The last time.

Because I will gradually walk further and further on the road that belongs only to me.

Goodbye, my past.
