Handkerchiefs Deep in the Memory of Time

One morning, I opened a drawer that hadn't been used for a long time. In a daze, my eyes were caught by a pale pink little cardboard box. I opened the box and there were those neatly folded handkerchiefs. Though they had been stored for years, they still emitted a faint fragrance. The pure white cotton base with light purple and pale yellow tiny flowers was as new as ever, still elegant and delicate.

I remember that when I was a child, my companions didn't use handkerchiefs; they used their sleeves. When they stretched out their arms, their sleeves were all greasy and black. Our family was more particular because my mother was a doctor who loved cleanliness. She often kept a handkerchief in her pocket. When we used it, we would pretend to dab our foreheads or gently wipe our mouths, drawing envious looks from our companions. I would hold my head high and proud. At that time, girls shopping in stores loved nothing more than gathering around the counter to look at those colorful handkerchiefs, taking a long time to pick one. The first household chore I learned was washing my own handkerchief. After washing it, I would stand on a stool and stretch hard to hang it on the rope. In the evening, when I took it back inside, it smelled of fragrant soap and dried sun. When I grew older and attended boarding school, some of the girls in the class already understood charm, but the school had many strict rules. So they would wash their hair in the evening, and when it was half-dry, they would loosely tie a handkerchief around their heads as decoration. During evening study sessions, the boys' eyes would often glance at their fluffy hair.

When I was in college, I met a man who was handsome and tall, with graceful manners. Once during an outing, seeing the sweat on my face, he handed me a handkerchief - white with fine dark blue grids, soft and clean. Holding it in my hand, I could feel his body temperature transmitted through the air. Later, I never returned the handkerchief to him. I washed it and stored it in the drawer until one day during a move, it got lost amidst the chaos, leaving me secretly melancholy for a long time. There aren't many men who use handkerchiefs, but if there are, they must be careful and warm-hearted people.

I have collected many handkerchiefs - cotton, silk, linen, gingham, floral patterns, solid colors, with lace edges... carefully folded and placed in the drawer. I disliked the strong smell of camphor, so I specially bought lavender-scented soap and placed it inside. The light fragrance lingered for a long time. Nowadays, the shelves in stores are filled with various paper towels pressed into elegant shapes. They are not without beauty, but looking at them, I always feel that paper towels are like instant noodles - used and discarded immediately, quick and convenient. However, many lingering nuances have disappeared unnoticed.

The memories deep in time are the most precious. Those innocent expressions will never return. Preserving these memories is all we can do.
