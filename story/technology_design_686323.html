The boss of Wuxing Sword Truth Barber Shop has been charging only 1 yuan for elderly customers for 11 years

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-02 08:35:02

In A Bing Hair Salon, A Bing is giving a haircut to Grandma Wang who is 78 years old. The other day, Grandma Wang from the Yuetan area came again to A Bing Ronghui Barber Shop. She has been a loyal customer here for over ten years because elderly people only pay 1 yuan for a haircut here.

As Grandma Wang was about to step onto the stairs, a waiter greeted her with a smile, "Take it slow, Grandma, let me help you." Then he supported her into the shop. Just after 9:30, all six hairdressing chairs were occupied, and there were eight more seniors waiting in line.

"I walked by the door and saw the 1 yuan sign so I came in. Have you had your hair cut here before? How is it?" The aunt sitting next to Grandma Wang was a first-timer and still a bit hesitant. "I've been getting my hair cut here since the store opened. It's been more than ten years," Grandma Wang pointed at the people around her, "You see, every morning there are so many people."

In less than ten minutes, it was Grandma Wang's turn. The barber helped her sit on the hairdressing chair and put an apron around her. "Oh, today A Bing is cutting my hair." The A Bing mentioned by Grandma Wang is the owner of A Bing Ronghui, Ye Weibing.

A Bing is 40 years old. When he first arrived in Beijing in 1992, he noticed many elders getting their hair cut at roadside stalls. In summer, they were exposed to the sun, and in winter, they were battered by cold winds. At that time, he thought if one day he opened his own shop, he would offer free haircuts to the elderly.

In June 1998, A Bing opened his first hair salon in Xijiaominxiang. He posted a notice outside the door offering free haircuts to the elderly and printed 3000 free coupons for the elderly, which he distributed to the neighborhood committees nearby and also went door-to-door to hand them out. Soon, more and more seniors started coming.

Back then, many seniors insisted on paying after their haircuts, but A Bing always refused the money. As a result, people often brought him drinks, fruits, pancakes... Some even left them at the door saying, "A Bing, this is for you," before rushing off. Over time, some of the elders said to him, "Just charge a little money, we feel better this way." Thus, starting from 2001, seniors at A Bing Ronghui Hair Salon began to symbolically pay 1 yuan for a haircut.

In no time, Grandma Wang's hair was done. "Do you like it?" A Bing held up a mirror behind her. "Yes, I do," Grandma Wang walked to the front desk and dropped the 1 yuan she had clutched in her hand into the donation box.

The 1 yuan paid by the seniors was called the "1 Yuan Fund" by A Bing. Every year, A Bing supports three lonely elders in the community, sponsors seven drop-out children, donates to projects like "Mother Water Cellar" and the Yushu earthquake relief. Although the "1 Yuan Fund" alone isn't enough for these charitable acts, it all turns into donations.

"When I first opened the store, I had one apprentice; now there are five or six people in the store dedicated to cutting hair for the elderly, and one master responsible for home visits." Nowadays, A Bing Ronghui has three chain stores, providing haircuts for over 100 seniors daily and offering home services for those with mobility issues. In the past 13 years, he has issued 180,000 free coupons.

"Individual charity efforts are limited, but the government fully supports us." Outside A Bing Ronghui Yuetan Store, there are six or seven steps, making it inconvenient for wheelchair-bound seniors. In June this year, A Bing applied to the Civil Affairs Bureau of Xicheng District for the installation of barrier-free facilities, which was approved as a special case. The barrier-free facility renovation was completed within the month.

"Treating the elderly well is treating our future selves well." There is such a small sign hanging in A Bing Ronghui Hair Salon. Employees say it is the "store motto", and doing good deeds and showing filial piety cannot wait.

Seniors aged 60 and above can get a haircut for 1 yuan at 100 stores.

Starting this month, the first batch of 100 "1 yuan haircut" stores have been officially recognized, including A Bing Ronghui, Montain Beauty Salon, Simei International, Oriental Beauty Research, Oriental Famous Cut, etc., with 100 outlets. Seniors aged 60 and above with senior citizen certificates or ID cards can get a haircut for 1 yuan at these recognized salons every morning.

"Helping the elderly is a tradition in the hairdressing industry. Since the 1980s, state-owned barbershops have set up special seats for the elderly and children. In recent years, state-owned barbershops have gradually been replaced by private ones, but this tradition has been passed down generation by generation. Among these 100 stores, at least 40 have been offering 1 yuan haircuts for seniors for more than 10 years," Wu Zhijun, market director of the Hairdressing and Beauty Association, introduced.

A Bing Ronghui is currently the longest-standing in the industry, having persisted for 13 years. Three years ago, the Municipal Hairdressing and Beauty Association learned about this situation and began promoting it within the industry. In the future, the number of 1 yuan haircut outlets will gradually increase to 500.
