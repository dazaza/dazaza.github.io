Employment index is out, education becomes the second most popular job hunting industry

by hophi on 2008-10-15 22:14:56

According to information obtained from Berle Professional Guest Network on October 13th, the latest Employment Index for 2008 has been recently released. Through surveys of over a thousand graduating students, nearly 2000 company employees, and HR managers, it was found that some new trends and changes have appeared in this year's job market.

Employment Rate: Construction Industry is the Highest

In this survey, through comparisons of different industries, it was discovered that the employment rate of the construction industry is the highest among all industries, more than double the average level. This also indicates that the current brief slump in the real estate industry has not affected the overall demand for talent within the industry. The next industries with higher employment rates are chemicals, IT, and pharmaceuticals, accounting for 25%, 23.4%, and 20% respectively. According to the survey, the employment rate of the media industry is 7.5%, while the employment rate of the machinery industry is only 3.4%.

Job Hunting Hotspot: Education Ranks Second

The survey shows that currently, popular job hunting industries remain concentrated in traditional IT and finance sectors. Notably, the education industry has become the second most popular job hunting industry, reflecting the relatively stable working environment and increasingly better treatment of teachers under new circumstances, which gradually increases their appeal to graduating students. Interestingly, the construction industry, which ranks first in employment rate, seems to be less favored by job seekers, dropping directly to seventh place. Industry insiders stated that during the job hunting process, if one can calmly analyze market conditions and choose non-hot but potential-rich industries, they could achieve unexpected results.

Graduating Students: Salary Expectations Decrease

Facing increasingly intense employment pressure, the general salary expectations of the surveyed group tend to be realistic.

The survey shows that over 80% of graduating students expect a salary below 2000 yuan, and only 3.6% of respondents expect a salary above 3500 yuan. This relatively low psychological expectation reflects, to a certain extent, the maturing psychology of graduating students. For employment prospects, more than half of the respondents no longer focus on the nature of the enterprise; instead, personal development space within the enterprise becomes their primary concern. Whether foreign-funded, state-owned, or private enterprises, as long as there is development space, 36.3% of people would be willing to give it a try. In interviews, it was found that previously very popular public institutions are now chosen as the first option by only 5.5% of respondents, indicating to a certain degree that graduating students' job hunting has become more pragmatic.

Graduating Students: Expect Employment Guidance

The survey shows that 80% of graduating students express the need for various forms of employment guidance to achieve twice the result with half the effort in future job hunting processes. As for the other 20% who do not wish to receive employment guidance, the reasons mostly concentrate on "wasting time" and "employment guidance does not play a big role."

Experts from Berle Professional Guest Network stated that the graduating student employment market calls for professional and effective employment guidance, and this strong market demand will become the next opportunity for human resources professional institutions.
