Without a house, where does love land?

by xiaoxiao67 on 2012-01-03 00:01:26

Without a house, where can love land?

Recently, a photo has been circulating on Weibo. The photo is a reprint of a real estate advertisement in Shanghai, with the slogan: "Without a house, how can you have a wife?" This phrase has sparked heated discussions and forwards online.

As early as two months ago on Qixi Festival, I received a multimedia message from a friend in Xiamen - an advertisement for a property development in Xiamen, with the slogan: "Without a house, where can love land?" It's a pointed comment, and my friend exclaimed that it was brilliant.

At the time, it was the hundredth day of the new policy, and its deterrent power was still strong. However, for young lovers, this did not diminish the impact of the entire advertisement.

I don't know when it started, but houses and love, or rather marriage, have become so closely linked. Since the "mother-in-law demand" has been infinitely magnified, houses have almost become a necessary condition for men to win a woman's heart.

Without a house, where can love land?

It seems like a gentle love message, but it sharply reveals the cruelty and helplessness of reality. It truthfully makes us admit that feelings and houses are openly tied together, which has become a pathological social phenomenon.

Tragically, another Weibo post has also been circulating recently: "Marriage is precious, but houses are more expensive." This directly criticizes the "fake divorce wave" triggered by the purchase restriction order.

Since love has already landed, why let love hover again?

Under the purchase restriction order, China's real estate market seems to have returned to the planned economy era, reminding people of the "food coupons" created to deal with material shortages. Limiting each family to one house purchase is equivalent to an invisible "house coupon." The underlying meaning is: houses are scarce, so there must be restrictions on purchases.

However, urban men and women who are accustomed to the commodity economy mindset always have the instinct to stock up on scarce items. Without strong supply support, rapid transactions increase volume, followed by supply falling short of demand. So, what rationale supports a drop in housing prices?

In the end, whether you have a house or not, your love seems to only hover in mid-air, unable to land. What a tragedy!
