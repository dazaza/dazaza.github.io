**Title: The thermostat of the LG refrigerator fails, requiring recharging of the temperature-sensing medium**

**Analysis and Repair:** If a household refrigerator experiences a failure in its temperature control system, it is usually due to an abnormality in the temperature-sensing system of the thermostat. A common root cause of this issue is poor sealing of the temperature-sensing system, which leads to the gradual leakage of the temperature-sensing medium. This article introduces a simple method for recharging the temperature-sensing medium into the temperature-sensing system, which may be useful for readers.

**(1) Pressure testing of the thermostat's temperature-sensing system:** Open the original welding point (or cut) of the capillary copper tube of the thermostat's temperature-sensing system. After removing burrs, attach a nylon tube to the capillary copper tube. Connect the other end of the nylon tube to a medical needle. Submerge the temperature-sensing system assembly and copper tubes in water, then use a medical syringe to apply pressure while observing whether any bubbles escape from the temperature-sensing system. If there is leakage, repair it with soldering.

**(2) Vacuum extraction:** Maintain the above connection and use a syringe to extract air while simultaneously using a multimeter on the resistance setting to measure the resistance between the two ends of the temperature-sensing system assembly. When the two ends are disconnected, it can be considered that the vacuum level inside the capillary copper tube meets the requirements.

**(3) Reconnecting the capillary copper tube:** Flatten and seal one capillary blind tube located on the base of the temperature sensor spring tube. Cut off the welded and sealed point, and remove any solder burrs. Then, connect the capillary copper tube of the thermostat's temperature-sensing system, and seal it with solder.

**(4) Recharging the temperature-sensing medium:** First, use pliers (or gently tap with a hammer) to restore the flattened and sealed portion of the temperature sensor's capillary copper tube to its original state, allowing the temperature-sensing medium to reach the welded area. If no leakage occurs, perform the same treatment on the flattened section of the thermostat's temperature-sensing capillary copper tube to ensure it is unblocked. Once both sides reach equal pressure, flatten and reseal both capillary copper tubes, disconnect the welded points, and seal the disconnected areas with solder. Finally, reinstall the repaired thermostat temperature-sensing system back into the controller and place it inside the refrigerator for normal use.
