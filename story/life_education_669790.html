Over-protection and over-replacement: the source of children's inferiority complex

by hudazikao on 2012-02-20 11:09:37

The teachers of the self-study examination office of Hunan University believe that a child is an independent individual with their own will and personality from the moment they are born. Parents do not have the right to支配 or limit their behavior. Especially in most cases during a child's growth, parents cannot make choices for them regarding objective matters; instead, they must let the child feel that they are the master of themselves. When parents take care of everything and handle every matter for the child, it gives the child very few opportunities to deal with problems and exercise their abilities, thus leaving a shadow of inferiority on the child's psychology.

Teachers from Hunan University's self-study examination program have found that if parents replace everything for the child, the child lives under such meticulous care and appears happy, but there are hidden concerns behind this: getting used to a life where clothes are handed to them and food is served at the table can lead to strong dependence over time. Even when they grow up, they will still be unable to live independently without others' care.

The teachers from Hunan University's self-study examination website believe that although children are relatively young and lack skills and experience, making mistakes is inevitable. However, as long as the parents patiently teach them methods and encourage them to practice more, they will gradually learn to do things well. If their opportunities to exercise are deprived merely out of fear that they might make mistakes, the child's labor skills will never improve, and their confidence will also be affected.

Perhaps in the eyes of parents, labor is a responsibility and a burden, but in the eyes of children, labor is a pleasure and a way to relax. Labor can increase a child's knowledge, strengthen their willpower, enhance their sense of responsibility, and cultivate good qualities such as seeing tasks through to completion and respecting others' work. Therefore, participating in an appropriate amount of labor during their free time will not only not affect their studies, but also allow them to balance their activities better and thus focus better on their studies.

Parents need to teach their children to develop the good habit of doing their own things from a young age: when attending kindergarten, they should learn to brush their teeth, wash their face, eat, dress, tie their shoelaces, and clean up toys by themselves; after starting school, they should learn to wash their small clothes, pack their bags, and tidy their rooms, using these tasks to cultivate a child's concept of labor and love for labor from a young age.

If parents do everything for their children, then when the child grows up, they may feel useless and lacking in confidence in group settings, and fundamentally lack the ability to get things done well. If you truly love your child and consider their best interests, please let go of the hand that handles everything for them, and do not sacrifice your child's lifelong happiness under the guise of love.
