First Place: The CEO Teaches His Secretary How to Use a Fax Machine

The CEO teaches his secretary how to use a fax machine.

A newly hired secretary looked confused in front of a fax machine when another colleague came by to send a fax. He patiently helped the secretary send the fax and taught her how to use this complicated device. The secretary was very grateful to him and asked for his name, he said his name was Schmidt.

Schmidt is the CEO of Google. At Google, everyone does everything themselves, including the CEO.

Second Place: Google's Colorful Soda

Google's colorful soda.

Every April 1st on April Fool's Day, the geniuses at Google come up with some strange tricks to amuse everyone. One of them was Google's colorful soda, and it was rumored that someone even tried to auction these non-existent items on eBay!

Setting up a research center on the moon was another April Fool's joke, and some people actually fell for it, applying to join Google's future lunar research center.

Third Place: Aftermath of April Fool's Day Affects Gmail

Aftermath of April Fool's Day affects Gmail.

Too many jokes on April Fool's Day made the real things seem fake. Gmail was launched on April 1st, and many people thought it was just another clever prank by Google, especially since no company had ever offered such large capacity (1GB, now over 2GB) email for free at that time.

Initially, even some internal employees thought this news was false, showing just how far-reaching the "poison" of the aftermath of April Fool's Day jokes could be.

Fourth Place: Spend a Month Indoors Comfortably

Spend a month indoors comfortably.

A new employee at Google, who hadn't yet rented a house, lived in the company for a month. His life was much more comfortable than Tom Hanks' life in the airport (a movie plot, I forgot the name). Naturally, he didn't have to worry about three meals a day, there were comfortable sofas everywhere to sleep on, he could take showers in high-end restrooms, exercise on treadmills and swimming machines, and occasionally play the piano, making his life more leisurely than at home. It's said he managed to stay indoors for an entire month!

Fifth Place: Food Within 100 Feet

Food within 100 feet.

There must be food within 100 feet! This is a rule inside Google. An engineer felt this wasn't being followed, so he measured the distance between himself and the nearest food, and indeed found it exceeded 100 feet, measuring 120 feet. So he humorously requested the logistics department to improve their service, and their response was classic: the dining area is right above your head, the distance absolutely does not exceed 100 feet!

Sixth Place: Unimaginable Recruitment Techniques

Unimaginable recruitment techniques.

If you're a celebrity or tech expert on the internet, then you've likely already been spotted by Google's HR team. They have a little trick: every time you search for your own name using Google, a small prompt might appear on the results page - "Would you like to join Google?" (Do you want to join Google).

Google once put up a huge billboard on the highway leading to Silicon Valley, with only a simple question. Many passing engineers out of curiosity would try to solve the problem. Once solved, entering the answer into the browser address bar would lead them to Google's recruitment page.

Seventh Place: The CEO Shares an Office with Engineers

The CEO shares an office with engineers.

When CEO Schmidt first joined Google, all employees did not have their own individual offices, but the employees still felt it necessary to provide him with a relatively quiet workspace, so they arranged a very small private office for him.

Schmidt soon realized his "special treatment," and insisted on "being one with the masses." Under his insistence, he shared a small office with another engineer. To this day, no employee at Google has their own private office.

Eighth Place: Cows and Madonna on Halloween

Cows and Madonna on Halloween.

The founders of Google, like all young people, love Halloween and actively participate in the company-organized Halloween events by dressing up in all sorts of bizarre costumes. Two of the most classic costumes were as cows and Madonna.

Both costumes were very exaggerated, with prominent maternal symbols, causing many colleagues to curiously tug at these symbols, creating quite a fun scene.

Ninth Place: Space-Saving Swimming Machines

Space-saving swimming machines.

Google has many swimming enthusiasts, but due to the lack of space to build a swimming pool, a Google employee conceived a swimming machine based on the principle of a treadmill: water continuously flows into one side of the machine, and the swimmer swims against the current. By controlling the speed of the water flow, you will never reach the end. Surprisingly, a company was able to provide a similar product, so Google custom-made a set.

Tenth Place: "Errand" Companies Flourish

"Errand" companies flourish.

Wang Shuo described in "Rebel Without a Cause" the "Three Replacement Company" (apparently replacing worries, solving problems, handling troubles? haha), which would definitely find plenty of business at Google. Google spends money hiring many "errand-running" companies and individuals to help employees handle personal matters, such as car repairs, license plate registration, etc., and of course, these services are free for employees.
