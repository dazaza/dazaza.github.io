1. Not knowing her husband's birthday

There are some women whose husbands give them their entire salary every month. These women don't work and spend their days taking care of their children and then going out shopping with friends. When it's time to eat, they go to their mother-in-law's house for meals. One evening, she went to her mother-in-law's house and saw a birthday cake on the table. She asked, "Is anyone's birthday today?" This sentence shocked everyone present. That day happened to be her husband's birthday, and their son had already turned five, yet she didn't know her husband's birthday. On the contrary, she knew her own birthday and wedding anniversary well, and her husband remembered to give her presents on those occasions. Recently, she found out that her husband often texted another woman. In such situations, shouldn't the woman reflect on her own behavior before blaming her husband?

2. Not knowing what brand of shoes her husband wears

A 26-year-old girl complained to me that after eight years of dating, her boyfriend recently kept saying he was tired of being with her and no longer cared about her. I asked her, "How do you usually eat? Do you know what brand of shoes he wears?" She replied, "I always eat out. He seems to like wearing leather shoes or maybe sneakers." People need to care for each other and understand each other. Dating for eight years and always eating out—aren't you just wasting time? Not even knowing what kind of shoes your boyfriend likes, do you really care about him? "Do not impose on others what you do not desire." I have to admit that this gentleman must have great patience if he only started feeling tired after eight years.

3. Not knowing what size clothes her husband wears

A 30-year-old woman complained to me that she had been married for seven years. She stayed home to take care of the children while her husband worked outside. One day, while shopping, she wanted to buy her husband a shirt but didn't know his size, so she called him. Unexpectedly, her husband hung up after asking her, "Do you even know what size I wear?" From then on, her feelings towards him became colder. This lady felt wronged and didn't know where she went wrong. The real reason was simple: the man wasn't upset because she didn't know his clothing size; he was upset because after so many years, his wife had never truly cared about him. If she really cared, she could have just checked one of his shirts at home to find out the size.

4. Pushing all household chores onto her boyfriend

A 25-year-old girl told me that recently, her boyfriend, who had been doting on her for three years, suddenly started ignoring her, behaving completely differently from before. I asked her to describe a typical day in her life. She said that her boyfriend came home after work every day, cooked, washed clothes, cleaned the floor, and then consoled her if she was unhappy. I asked, "What did you do?" She replied, "I don't work and watch TV at home. He cooks breakfast in the morning and then goes to work. When I wake up, I eat by myself." Some boys might have a high tolerance, but remember, even the strongest spring will snap if stretched too far.

5. Not knowing what dishes her husband likes to eat

A 31-year-old woman complained to me that after six years of marriage, her husband used to love her very much, but recently he kept finding excuses to eat out. I suggested, "To catch a man's heart, first catch his stomach. Just cook him a table full of your signature dishes, and he will naturally come back home to eat." She replied with a sentence that shocked me. She said, "We don't even have bowls at home, how can I cook?" It turned out that for six years of marriage, the couple and their child had always eaten at her mother-in-law's house and then left without helping. Although her mother-in-law loved her son and did more work, she never complained. But for a man, his heart starts to drift away when his wife doesn't care enough about him. After six years of marriage, the wife still didn't know what dishes her husband liked. Making breakfast and cooking dinner aren't difficult things, and they can create a warm atmosphere in the family, making the husband want to come home.

6. Not knowing what warmth and coldness mean

A 27-year-old girl complained to me that she had been dating her boyfriend for five years. Her boyfriend took great care of her and pampered her immensely. Since she had been taken care of as a child, she didn't know how to give back or take care of others. Could this relationship continue? I replied, "It seems like everything depends on your boyfriend."

7. Not cherishing an ordinary life

A 47-year-old woman wrote to Non-mainstream Fanciful Font Diary that her daughter had already gone to college. Her husband was a very diligent factory worker who went to work, came home, ate, and rested every day. Life was very plain, but they had lived peacefully for many years. As for her husband, she was completely satisfied. However, she couldn't bear the mundane life and felt that her life lacked passion. So one day, she found her passion online and became deeply entangled in it. Actually, there was something she hadn't realized. Everyone knows that soda is better than plain water, especially when you first drink it and feel that refreshing sensation. But can that sensation last a lifetime? What can last a lifetime is plain water. Only an ordinary life can be the most enduring. Don't fail to appreciate the blessings you have.

8. Holding onto a doomed relationship

A 28-year-old girl complained to me that she had a boyfriend who was older than her. Many "butterflies" surrounded his window. She had proposed breaking up with him many times, but each time she missed him terribly. Whenever he contacted her again, she would immediately dance around. In her heart, she knew that what she was doing was wrong, that it wasn't good for herself, and that it wouldn't lead to anything good. But she refused to face reality. Besides telling her that she was holding onto a doomed relationship, there was nothing else I could say.

9. Playing with people's feelings

A girl told me, "Rat brother, I'm so troubled lately. There's a boy who keeps chasing me, sending me gifts and flowers. But I don't like him, and I don't know what to do. Can you help me?" I replied, "Little sister, sorry, my abilities are limited. I think it's better to let that boy go. I believe he is suffering more than you."

10. Comparing your husband to your first love

A 33-year-old woman told me that although her husband was a good person, he had many flaws. She often thought about the happy times she spent with her first love. Back then, her first love seemed perfect, and if it weren't for his infidelity, they would have definitely been very happy together. I believe that many women have similar thoughts. But we need to realize that objectively speaking, everyone has flaws. No matter how many flaws your husband has, as long as he loves you, what more do you want? And no matter how perfect your first love seemed, if he didn't love you, how could you be happy with him? Moreover, what you see now might not be the truth because you didn't live together. He didn't have the chance to take off his mask and show you the real him before you ended things.
