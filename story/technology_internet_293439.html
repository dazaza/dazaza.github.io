The procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows: when buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. This order is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of buying or selling the stock. The abbreviation of the stock is usually three to four Chinese characters, and the code of the stock is six digits. When entrusting a buy or sell, the code and abbreviation of the stock must match. At the same time, there are certain rules regarding the quantity of stocks bought or sold: the quantity of stocks commissioned for purchase must be a multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks commissioned for sale does not have to be a multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place an order: counter submission, telephone automatic order, computer automatic order, and remote terminal order.
