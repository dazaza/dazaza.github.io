Guilin encounters the biggest fog of the year, and the Meteorological Bureau issues an orange warning.

by citscits5 on 2012-02-22 10:28:33

Standing by the Lijiang River, you can't see the scenery on the other bank at all, right? When looking far away, the buildings and mountains that were originally visible have all disappeared into a vast expanse of white. Yesterday, the entire city of Guilin floated in mist like a "fairyland." Some netizens joked that while there's a穿越 drama called *The Palace*, our Guilin yesterday was "Fog Locked Guilin."

"This is the largest fog that Guilin has encountered so far this year," an expert from the city meteorological station told reporters. "Because the warm moist air mass met with relatively colder ground air, large amounts of water vapor condensed in the air, causing the visibility in the urban area to be only 1,000 meters during the daytime yesterday, and the visibility on the river and lake surfaces was even lower."

As night fell, the fog became even thicker, and the visibility in the urban area continued to decrease. At 6:30 PM, the Meteorological Bureau issued an orange warning signal for dense fog: it was expected that within the next 6 hours, the urban area would experience dense fog with visibility less than 200 meters. This was also the first orange warning signal for dense fog in our city this year.

The Meteorological Station forecasted that due to the lingering of a strong warm moist air mass, Guilin, including the 12 counties, will continue to experience foggy weather for the next two days. Around the 25th, a new cold air mass will arrive in Guilin, dispersing the thick fog at that time.

It should be noted that in some mountainous areas such as Longsheng and Ziyuan over the next few days, the fog will be much denser than in the towns. Drivers should pay attention to traffic safety, stay focused, reduce speed, and drive cautiously.

Low visibility made it difficult for vehicles on the road to move smoothly. By around 5:00 PM yesterday, it was already dark in the urban area, and the streetlights on both sides of the roads had lit up early. The urban area of Guilin and some roads leading in and out of the city were enveloped in thick fog. In some areas, visibility sometimes dropped to only 150 meters, and traffic accidents, especially rear-end collisions, increased compared to normal days.

At 6:00 PM, I drove south along Zhongshan Road, and the traffic volume was very high. Many vehicles turned on their front and rear fog lights, and the speed of the vehicles was very slow.

When the vehicle approached Nanmen Bridge, due to its proximity to the Lijiang River, the fog was very thick. The bridge surface to the south was almost entirely shrouded in white, and only the red tail lights of the cars could be seen. Near Liberation Bridge, the fog on the road surface was even thicker, with visibility estimated to be less than 100 meters. Additionally, there were many motorcycles and cars driving against traffic, so many drivers honked frequently to warn nearby vehicles for safety.

Around 6:30 PM, I saw at Liberation Bridge that the bridge was basically covered by thick fog, and the lights of the vehicles passing through the bridge appeared blurry.

On the airport road, I witnessed two minor traffic accidents caused by slippery road conditions - one rear-end collision and one scraping incident. Fortunately, no one was injured, and the losses were minimal.

As the northern gateway to Guilin, National Highway 322 in Lingchuan County sees heavy traffic flow, especially on the section from Lingchuan to Xing'an, which is an asphalt road that becomes more slippery during rainy and foggy weather. According to traffic police, a major truck rollover accident occurred in the Gantang Bridge section of National Highway 322 in Lingchuan County early yesterday morning due to slippery road conditions.

In the morning, on the Yongfu Tangbao section of National Highway 322, a minivan suddenly collided with a medium-sized bus. Firefighters rescued a couple trapped inside the minivan. The woman who was rescued said that due to the heavy fog in the morning and the slippery road, the car skidded when turning, resulting in the collision with another vehicle.

**Precautions for driving in foggy weather:**

- Vehicles should turn on anti-fog lights and width indicator lights. Maintain a safe distance between vehicles and pedestrians, strictly control the speed of the vehicle, appropriately honk the horn to attract the attention of pedestrians and vehicles. At the same time, closely monitor the road environment, especially when passing through villages, intersections, stations, and mountain road bends, carefully observe the situation, and prepare to stop or yield.

- When driving on highways, if visibility is too low, temporarily leave the highway and park in a nearby service area. If unable to leave the highway, choose an emergency parking lane or shoulder to park, and according to regulations, turn on the hazard warning flashers and place parking warning devices. Transfer the passengers in the vehicle to a safe area and resume driving when visibility improves.

- Do not turn on the high beams, as the light emitted may be reflected by the fog, causing the area in front of the car to appear completely white, further affecting visibility.

- When driving on asphalt roads, drivers need to be extra cautious. Increase the distance between vehicles to more than twice the distance on dry roads, avoid sudden braking or sharp turns, and reduce the frequency of lane changes.

Guilin CITS reminds tourists visiting Guilin or self-driving friends recently to drive slowly and carefully.
