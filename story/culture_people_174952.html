Let go of love, let go of love.

by lk0swcc on 2009-07-11 14:39:29

I became a person who wants to love but doesn't know how, after that incident.

Every time I trace back my memory, I always encounter her. Her every smile and movement is as clear as a painting. At that moment, space and time bend into a distant background, while her image nearby flashes like a series of slides. In the midst of dizziness and bewilderment, I, immersed in the past, become her prisoner once again.

It snowed, we once walked out of the city where dirty snow turned into mud, we went on an outing to the suburbs, hand in hand strolling in the snow untouched by human footprints. Looking back at the two long and winding rows of footprints, we let loose our inhibitions, shouting wildly and laughing at each other. At that moment, we thought we could walk together to the end of life.

Later, when all these beautiful things could not be retained by human effort, I began to believe in fate and destiny. Her departure was as unstoppable as melting snow. She went to the south of the Yangtze River, where the water is beautiful, the mountains are clear, and the willow catkins are in full bloom, leaving me standing alone in the snow, not knowing where I was. The snow accumulated thickly on my body, and I was reluctant to shake it off. I realized that snow is also deeply lonely...

Afterwards, her letters came from the south. The tone of her words was bright and dark, elusive, making me gradually understand her distress. With high mountains and wide waters separating us, we were only able to communicate through letters carried by wild geese, but we were distressed by the inability to meet. The days of worrying about each other were exhausting and heart-wrenching. When would they end? Therefore, it's better to have short-term pain than long-term pain. After saying some trivial words in the letter, we stopped communicating.
