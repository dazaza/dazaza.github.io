What I want to say is, none of these points are wrong. Talent packaging may not be a bad way to compensate for or establish a new investment research team: Firstly, such fierce competition might help demonstrate the "catfish effect," giving a sense of urgency to the fund companies being poached, prompting them to reform their incentive systems or structures, realize their shortcomings, and mend their ways to reduce the loss of talent in "packages."

Who says the 21st century lacks talent? Southern Song dynasty philosopher Wei Liaoweng would tell you that the Southern Song dynasty also lacked talent! There is evidence in his poetry: "Upon meeting high officials, who doesn't say, 'talent is hard to come by.'" This is his lamentation on the difficulty of finding talent and the rarity of talented individuals.

In fact, every century lacks talent. As long as there is an industry where personnel supply cannot meet demand, experienced relevant practitioners are considered talent. The fund industry is no exception. How dire is the lack of talent in the fund industry? Talents can be "packaged" and poached. This applies to old fund companies like China Merchants Fund and Tianhong Fund; it is especially true for new fund companies like Chang'an Fund Management Co., Ltd., which is a microcosm of the current shortage of investment research talent.

So, how do we select talent? Wei Liaoweng said in the Southern Song dynasty: "Admonish the high officials, look at the world, search for people."

Of course, we cannot rule out the possibility that this five-person team works well together, combining seamlessly, and even without having managed a fund before, they have great innate talent for investment practice. However, based on the currently available information, this is still a low-probability event.

The five-person investment decision-making committee, four of whom have previously worked at Guojin General Fund Management Co., Ltd. during its preparation period. After four years of preparation, Guojin General might have left its talents feeling "useless," forcing them to leave "in groups" and "in packages." Perhaps it's the ideal of "a man's ambition lies beyond the horizon," regardless of whether it's the "Imperial Capital" Beijing or the "Magic Capital" Shanghai, a platform suitable for personal development is worth following. To put it nobly, they simply hope to realize their self-worth in this industry; personally speaking, they also hope for increasingly higher income, even aiming for a "moderately prosperous life" and seeking "wealth and honor."
