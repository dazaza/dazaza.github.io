From a "street stall" portrait artist to the cultural station chief of a town - interview with folk artist Tang Jialiang

by xyxandy8 on 2012-03-06 09:25:47

From Street Portrait Artist to Cultural Station Chief - Interview with Folk Artist Tang Jialiang

Tang Jialiang is painting. Photo by Huang Dingning.

I have heard several times that there is a folk artist named Tang Jialiang in Longgang, skilled in calligraphy, painting, photography, playing musical instruments, and performing. As an old cultural station chief of the area, for more than 20 years, he has been deeply rooted in the vast rural stage, continuously exploring and innovating various traditional folk arts, passing on his skills, teaching others, while inheriting and promoting these traditions, leading the local people to make their leisure cultural life colorful and vibrant. Recently, I specially visited this folk artist, Tang Jialiang.

Tang Jialiang's house was newly built last year, and its interior structure is very modern. As soon as you enter the living room, the whole wall behind the TV is covered with a vivid lotus flower painting, giving a strong sense of folk flavor. This was painted by Tang Jialiang himself. His wife said it's too vulgar. Tang Jialiang disagreed with his wife’s opinion, insisting that the lively and visually appealing painting fits well with a rural family. Then, he pointed to an eastern wall where hung one of his own creations, a Chinese painting called "Valley Clouds," and said to the reporter, "Look, isn't there also something elegant? This is called catering to both refined and popular tastes."

10 Years of Artistic Life Selling Skills

Born in 1963, Tang Jialiang has always liked painting since he was young. However, in the environment where even pigments were unaffordable, he never thought about pursuing art as a career path. After graduating from high school, his father saw his interest in painting and let him learn lacquer craftsmanship because at that time, when people made furniture, they were popularly decorated with paintings of flowers, birds, insects, and fish. The lacquer craftsman didn’t come, so Tang Jialiang went to Lin'an, occasionally seeing someone painting portraits on the street in Lin'an, and suddenly had an epiphany. He returned immediately, apprenticed under a portrait artist from Houying Village in Changhua, and learned how to paint portraits. In just over ten days, the master said he was good enough and could start working independently.

Thus, starting in 1980, Tang Jialiang began setting up stalls on the streets of Changhua to paint portraits, charging one yuan per piece, painting two to three pieces a day, which was quite a good income at that time.

Around 1986, village-run enterprises and community-run enterprises emerged in Longgang. Some craft factories specialized in manufacturing and processing tourist souvenir canes, requiring paintings and inscriptions on the canes. He worked in the factory, writing and painting West Lake scenery on the canes every day. Later, he moved to another art factory to paint screens, during which time the "branding technique" (using a heated iron to burn patterns onto objects) became popular. From 6 AM to 6 PM, he ran around painting for six or seven different places, earning 10 yuan a day, which was a considerable income back then. His wife said, "At that time, I could carry more than 10 yuan in pocket money, and everyone envied me."

Despite making relatively good money, Tang Jialiang's creative passion remained undiminished. Besides painting, he also wrote his own sketches. In 1987, the advertising industry began to flourish, and Tang Jialiang opened a store in Changhua, painting wall advertisements. At that time, individual clothing vendors were also emerging in large numbers. Inspired by real-life situations, Tang Jialiang self-wrote and performed a sketch titled "Boss and Customer" at the Changhua Industrial and Commercial Bureau's cultural evening party. It was recommended to participate in Hangzhou City's Green Leaf Sketch Competition for individual business owners and won third prize. Since then, he often joined the artistic troupe organized by the Changhua Culture Museum to perform in rural areas, singing, dancing, and acting in sketches. He would close his shop to perform for each session lasting 20 days, then return to continue running his shop and painting advertisements.

In 1990, he took his apprentices to Hangzhou's Arts and Crafts Factory to paint, where he came into contact with various painting techniques and contents, such as landscapes, flowers and birds, and figures. In the evenings, he studied orthodox sketching on his own, significantly enhancing his traditional painting skills.

10 Years Leading Folk Art Culture

In 1991, Tang Jialiang was recruited to work at the town's cultural station, fully devoting himself to rural mass cultural work, utilizing his strengths to organize and guide cultural and entertainment activities in various villages. Together with senior folk artists, he explored and compiled traditional folk art programs that were gradually disappearing locally, innovating, improving, and enriching them based on the original content, allowing folk art heritage to be passed down and promoted.

"The Colorful Cool Boat" is a traditional art program in Longgang. The earliest performances only featured one boat, which was bulky and cumbersome, with a single performance form. Starting in 1992, Tang Jialiang improved this program, changing it from one boat to two boats, and then to three boats the following year, adding characters like "shrimp soldiers and crab generals," "turtles fighting clams," greatly enriching the content of the program, giving the audience a fresh experience.

He personally led the lantern-making, choreographed dances, and composed music. Under Tang Jialiang's meticulous production and organization, a series of folk lantern dances such as "Lion Lanterns," "Cloud Head Dance," "Nine-Headed Divine Dragon," and "Grass Seed Bodhisattva" were created and participated multiple times in city-wide Lantern Festival activities and grand displays of folk lantern arts, winning numerous honors including "Performance Gold Award," "Innovation Award," and "Outstanding Performance Award."

Whenever the town or village needed to arrange some cultural programs, the villagers would first think of Tang Jialiang. There were even many villagers from other towns who came to invite him for guidance. During his free time at home, besides practicing calligraphy and painting, he would ponder about programs, learning waist drumming and line dancing by watching TV first, then creating and practicing a set of movements before riding his motorcycle to teach in the villages.

His wife said, "He usually doesn't have time, a phone call comes in, asking him to teach this or that. Sometimes when sleeping, his mouth still mumbles 'dong dong qiang, dong dong qiang,' if you ask him what he's doing, he says he's designing rhythms."

Dong Yonggao, the cultural station chief of Taiyang Town, has said more than once that among so many cultural station chiefs, he admires Tang Jialiang the most because he is proficient in everything and fully devoted to culture.

Cultivating More Cultural "Successors"

The rural work conference has just concluded. In the new year, our city will focus on advancing the coordinated development of urban and rural areas, and the implementation of rural cultural work will undoubtedly welcome more opportunities and gain better support. Tang Jialiang is full of confidence. He told the reporter that he plans to recruit and cultivate a group of excellent cultural volunteers to jointly innovate and create folk art performance programs, making the mass cultural activities even richer and more diverse.

The reporter also followed Tang Jialiang to visit his elementary school teacher Zhang Mingde's home, listening to their discussion on how to revive the "Colorful Cool Boat" program that had not been performed for several years. Tang Jialiang said that the audience has developed aesthetic fatigue, and later the "Colorful Cool Boat" performances moved to Heqiao. He plans to restore it this year, enriching it with more "Longgang characteristics." Besides shrimp, crabs, and turtles, he can also include loaches, yellow catfish, and stone perch, and further innovate the performance forms. He also discussed with Mr. Zhang how to bring out, learn, and teach the nearly lost "Tenfold Gongs and Drums."
