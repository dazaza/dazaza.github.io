China Unicom specifies the fee standard for the 3G trial commercial use for the first time, with the lowest fee being 93 yuan

by diglog2008 on 2009-05-31 08:26:22

May 30th news: China Unicom has today clearly announced the fee standard of its 3G services during the trial commercialization period. After implementing a 50% discount on the fees, the lowest package will be actually charged at 93 yuan.

According to the "WCDMA-3G mobile phone package" fee plan previously announced by China Unicom, it includes three categories: 3G basic packages, wireless Internet cards and optional packages. The basic package ranges from 186 yuan to 1686 yuan with seven tiers, and the voice call rate within the package is 0.24-0.36 yuan per minute. After implementing the 50% discount, the lowest tier of the 186 yuan basic package will only be charged at 93 yuan in reality. Among this, the voice part adopts a unified national call fee method, without distinguishing between long-distance fees and roaming fees.

On May 17th, China Unicom announced that it would conduct friendly experience of WCDMA services in the trial commercialization phase in the first batch of 55 cities where the network was built nationwide. It also recruited 250,000 friendly users nationwide for experience and testing, while announcing a 50% discount on communication fees for friendly users.

Previously, China Unicom determined seven tiers of "3G basic packages" ranging from 186 yuan to 1686 yuan, categorized according to content as voice, video and other streaming media, or pure text charges. Among these, the 186 yuan package includes 510 minutes of domestic call duration, 20 minutes of domestic video call duration, 20M (the pricing unit for multimedia content), 40T (the pricing unit for text content), 60MB data traffic, caller ID, and morning and evening news updates. For any excess domestic video call duration, M/T will be charged at the standard rate.
