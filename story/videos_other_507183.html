Talent is the first capital of a business and the source of its growth energy. Zhao Sheng, the Director of Human Capital at XX Company, revealed to the reporter from Oriental Enterprise Civilization how XX Company activates the talent "energy source."

Helping new employees cross the threshold

XX Company recently launched its 2010 "Campus Recruitment Grand Campaign" in four major cities with concentrated universities - Chengdu, Changsha, Xi'an, and Wuhan, drawing attention from many universities and media outlets.

Why was the recruitment process for 2010 started half a year earlier?

Zhao Sheng smiled and said that although the current talent market seems very abundant, the bipolarization of talent has made the competition for high-end talent between companies increasingly fierce. For Chinese enterprises, the main battleground for talent sources is the various universities.

"XX Company's strategy to win this battle involves three forward shifts." Talent is the most important capital for XX Company, and its human capital work has also seen numerous innovative strategies.

XX Company begins its campus recruitment during the third year of students' studies and establishes campus recruitment stations at target universities to select some outstanding third-year talents. Then, it moves the training program into the last semester of university, where instructors from the training department enter the university to train pre-selected talents, allowing students to basically complete the company's foundational training courses before joining the company. During the training, there will be simulated project practical training content, with senior company personnel guiding each project. Before students join the company, they already understand the company's work model, project processes, and entire research and development scope through the company's practical training projects. Thus, the three steps of advanced recruitment, advanced training, and advanced work are elegantly completed.

In university recruitment presentations, many companies heavily promote their products. Zhao Sheng believes this is wrong. He thinks that campus recruitment should first clearly identify what students are most concerned about regarding the recruiting company, which means understanding the needs of the students.

"Compared to the company's product situation, students are more concerned about their growth opportunities within the company." Confirming this point, XX Company clearly informs students during its university campus recruitment presentation about the carefully designed "Full Sky" training system and each employee's career growth channel at XX Company.

"After being hired, an employee's concerns shift; they worry about whether they can quickly get up to speed with their work," Zhao Sheng said. In human capital work, it is crucial to understand the needs of employees at every stage.

He believes new employees worry about two things: whether they can quickly integrate into the team and how to interact with older employees.

To address this, XX Company stipulates that when new employees report, their department manager must personally go to the human resources department to pick them up. Regardless of what they are doing, they must stop to hold a short introduction meeting for the new employee. Additionally, the company provides care for the new employee's food, clothing, housing, and transportation, making them feel valued by the company.

XX Company assigns a mentor to each new employee who will guide and inspect their work in detail. Mentors conduct weekly seminars and have clear evaluation requirements and reward/punishment measures, ensuring that new employees can quickly integrate into the team.

Do not get trapped in tools

The human resources department should be the most important strategic partner to the company's boss. However, in many Chinese enterprises today, the role of human resource management has not been fully utilized. Many HR departments' work remains simply at the level of personnel operations. Why is this?

"Falling into the trap of tools," Zhao Sheng expressed some regret, "They use a large number of HR tools, such as 360-degree evaluations, balanced scorecards, KPI performance tools, etc. They apply these Western tools very well but neglect the 'human' aspect. The application of professional tools is merely the foundation. If human capital work stays at the level of tools and data, it only reaches the realm of 'technique,' not the realm of 'principle.'"

"As a human capital worker, I believe one must always focus on the company's business growth." For example, if a business department experiences a decline in performance, as the HR department, one must first analyze the reasons. Is it due to the loss of old employees? Employee skill issues? Or internal management problems within the department? Or is the department manager incompetent, leading to a drop in team morale?

The HR department must actively participate, communicate, and coordinate with relevant personnel. Then, after holding meetings and discussions, propose a preliminary solution plan for the decision-makers to reference.

"Human resource management work must rise to the level of overall enterprise growth," Zhao Sheng emphasized. "HR managers should pay more attention to optimizing organizational human structures, updating and growing talent to drive organizational growth. Excellent human capital workers must broaden their horizons to keep up with the thoughts and growth speed of company decision-makers."

"If each department is a pearl, the HR department is the thread that strings these pearls together." Zhao Sheng's other concept is that HR work cannot be done in isolation. To achieve good results, the entire organization must play a role. He believes that the responsibility of HR workers is to guide and facilitate connections. To do HR work well, it must be the result of the combined efforts of all employees in the company.

XX Company's human capital policy planning is not solely formulated by the HR department.

At XX Company, before the HR core makes a plan, they conduct surveys and gather opinions from various department heads, analyzing the plan's feasibility and estimating potential obstacles to implementation. "Actually, we talk about reform, which always comes with pressure. If you want to make changes in human capital, you must fully consider the pressures faced by those changes. Not everyone will support your changes. How do you make these people recognize your ideas? This requires skills," Zhao Sheng said.

In the process of formulating plans, conflicts are normal. Zhao Sheng always tells his team at XX Company that the HR core is the broad employees' home. His office door is always open to employees, welcoming them anytime. He believes that communication resolves conflicts, and the views and suggestions raised by employees from their perspective always have some rationale. These views may not have been considered in the plan, but communicating with employees instead promotes his work.

When asked if there would be disagreements with top decision-makers, he calmly replied: "Disagreements in work perspectives are normal. At XX Company, major decisions must be collectively discussed and passed through high-level meetings. But if there really is a disagreement, we must acknowledge the foresight of the top decision-makers. Their perspective considers the entire company, even the company's growth five or ten years from now. The key issue is whether we can analyze whether this plan aligns with the company's current resources. If it must be done, as human capital workers, we must persistently execute it, but while doing so, we must prepare risk mitigation plans."

"Emphasize promotion rather than just notification. We need to ensure that information is accurately conveyed to every employee."

Currently, many human resources workers do not place enough importance on conveying decisions thoroughly to each employee. Often, they only provide simple notifications without necessary publicity and guidance, resulting in many errors during execution, leading them to believe employees are uncooperative.

Zhao Sheng said that the same clause or plan can be understood differently by each person, naturally causing deviations during execution. He believes that many times it is not that employees do not want to implement it, but rather differing understandings of the plan hinder their accurate execution.

1+1＞2 complementary configuration

This year, XX Company completed its first round of strategic financing, becoming China's first consumer general software export company to receive venture capital. It also set a clear goal — to become China's largest listed company exporting consumer general software.

"For this goal, I need to start considering the talent demands the company will have after going public and begin to reserve talent proficient in company listing operations and capital market planning." Many Chinese companies fail in overseas acquisitions partly because they lack talent for overseas acquisition operations and the alignment of human capital with the overall strategy.

When the journalist pressed for details on the expected personnel increase and optimization over the next three years, Zhao Sheng smiled and said, "Specific numbers are meaningless. The personnel increase ratio will definitely be high. In a situation where the company grows rapidly, it is essential to maintain the stability of the talent pool. Since the company grows very fast, the proportion of new employees will be very high. If human capital does not keep up in areas like training, employee relations, and corporate culture construction, there will be certain risks."

Zhao Sheng said that to suit the company's future goals, detailed analysis and optimization of the human structure are needed, along with various measures for training adjustments and corporate culture shaping.

"When configuring the personnel structure of a department, we need to consider the complementarity of talent advantages. For example, if a department manager is relatively strong-willed, I would pair them with a softer or more psychologically sensitive assistant. In software industry companies, most businesses are individual projects requiring developers and testers. Therefore, we need to focus more on the types of talent the organization needs and how to configure teams based on talent complementarity to improve team operability."

Considering the optimization of talent configuration in the organizational structure, Zhao Sheng introduces each position's work content in detail during recruitment and evaluates each newcomer's suitability for their position during practical training. Adjustments are made in real-time during work to build the most efficient team through layer-by-layer adjustments.

"The quality of development work across the entire organization during operation is, in reverse, a measure of the progressiveness of human resource management."

Additionally, there are two other indicators for measuring the progress of human resource management work: quantifiable performance metrics in work and overall employee work conditions and satisfaction indicators.

Zhao Sheng said that in recruiting new employees, professional skills are the starting point, but the most important thing is that the recruit identifies with XX Company's culture. XX Company is a young person's company and hopes that the team consists of passionate, like-minded young people who genuinely wish to strive together for common goals.

The responsibility of the best employer

In the 2009 China Best Employer Annual Awards, XX Company won the title of "2009 China Excellent Employer." Regarding this, Zhao Sheng was pleasantly surprised: "We aim to be a long-lasting enterprise and a world-class brand. This goal determines that we must continuously improve employee welfare benefits, create larger platforms for employees, and continuously create greater value for society."

Zhao Sheng believes that to become the best employer, it is not only necessary to improve employee welfare benefits but also to constantly understand the needs of employees at different stages. Then, according to the unique needs of both the enterprise and employees, various human capital improvement measures should be formulated to achieve a win-win situation for the enterprise and employees.

Accordingly, XX Company launched the "Full Sky" training system, which includes a series of training programs at different levels: "New Star," "Silver Star," "Golden Star," and "Morning Star." In 2009, it also introduced on-the-job rotation training, where every Tuesday to Friday, at least two courses are available for training. Employees can freely choose training courses based on their actual needs. At XX Company, whether they are new employees, long-term employees, or mid-to-upper-level managers, all can participate in corresponding training to enhance their professional abilities.

Most positions in the company adopt an internal competitive hiring system. The company sets up two career growth channels for employees: one is the management channel, and the other is the professional channel. "Regardless of an employee's specialty, they can find their own growth channel and see their path clearly, thus avoiding the occurrence of career fatigue. Because some older employees, staying too long, don't know their growth path and easily develop a sense of career fatigue."

"Hai Na Bai Chuan You Rong Nai Da, Bi Li Qian Ren Wu Yu Ze Gang" (The sea accepts hundreds of rivers because it has the capacity to do so; the cliff stands thousands of feet tall because it has no desires) has always been Zhao Sheng's motto. He says that human capital workers must be outwardly flexible but inwardly principled, having both principles and a broad mind, possessing the spirit of a gardener, tirelessly pursuing the realm of "Shang Shan Ru Shui" (the highest goodness is like water).

Epilogue:

Zhao Sheng believes that the HR director must break free from heavy daily affairs, raising the focus of work to a strategic height, understanding more about organizational growth and employee growth situations, communicating more with the highest decision-making level, and understanding the company's future growth objectives. Only by achieving a certain level in these areas can corresponding human capital strategies be formulated. The highest realm of human resources is to participate in operations and guide operations.
