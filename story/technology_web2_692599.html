Opportunities in the Internet of Things are accompanied by challenges

by gdshstyle on 2012-03-06 10:35:26

The Opportunities of Internet of Things (IoT): Challenges Follow Closely Behind

On February 14th, the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) released the "12th Five-Year Plan for the Development of the Internet of Things" on its website. This has pointed out a direction for the emerging IoT industry that has been gaining popularity in recent years within China, and is believed to potentially give birth to a trillion-level market.

A report titled "2011 China Internet of Things Industry Prosperity and Entrepreneurs Confidence Index Report" published by Beijing Guopulse Interconnection Information Consulting Co., Ltd. had previously pointed out that the number of IoT projects in China significantly increased in 2011. Most entrepreneurs in the industry were highly enthusiastic about investing, and all saw promising development prospects for IoT.

Supporting Viewpoint:

Analyst Xiao Jia Yan from Analysys International believes that as the pioneer in the development of IoT, vehicle networking will strengthen the links between industrial chain segments such as dynamic traffic information automobile onboard terminals. Surrounding the intelligent development of onboard systems and road infrastructure construction, it will attract more non-traditional enterprises to enter this field.

National Committee Member Jian Zhu Wang mentioned in his proposal this year that he focuses heavily on IoT construction. He sees its promising future and suggests accelerating the commercialization process of IoT.

At the same time, industry experts have also reminded that China's IoT industry is still in its infancy and faces many challenges. For example, there are significant gaps between China's core IoT technologies, high-end products, and those of foreign countries. The ability for high-end comprehensive integrated service provision is weak, lacking backbone leading enterprises, with low application levels. Moreover, there is a lack of standards, unclear business models, and potential security risks. Therefore, investment needs to be rational, and blind concept speculation and underlying market risks should be carefully guarded against.

Supporting Viewpoint:

Shanghai analyst Yi Fei Xu from TrendForce indicated that "IoT is an emerging industry, and its current main driving force comes from policy promotion rather than market demand traction. The market acceptance is still unknown, and investors should be mindful of policy risks. Additionally, the establishment and perfection of standards require a relatively long period of time. Before the standards are basically perfected, there may be situations of disorderly competition."

Vice President of the Chinese Academy of Engineering, He Quan Wu, believes that IoT will generate more indirect benefits in the near term, and only in the mid-to-long term can it produce substantial direct benefits. In addition, the industry threshold for IoT applications increases the difficulty of cross-industry applications. Furthermore, the business model of IoT itself is unclear, and what drives its development is a major issue! The government needs to have overall planning, avoid duplicate construction, and break through institutional and systemic barriers.

Neutral Perspective:

Some experts have stated that "everywhere is promoting smart cities; whether they are too many or too few depends on economic effectiveness. Before economic effectiveness is achieved, it’s hard to say how well it's going."

Experts have expressed that since IoT truly entering the residential market of thousands of households may still take a considerable amount of time, its applications will inevitably start from industries or enterprises.
