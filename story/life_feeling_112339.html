He slept with me just to prove I was a virgin.

by xiaoshiyiluokuang on 2008-12-18 08:39:42

To be honest, I still can't get over this. What is wrong with this person?

Why can he on one hand be passionately in love with me, and on the other hand fantasize about marrying the purest woman in the world in the future?

Why is this? Is it because I loved someone else before him that I am not worthy to be his wife?

If I had known from the start that he always saw me as a lover in our relationship, maybe I wouldn't have fallen so deeply. I loved him as my partner, wholeheartedly wanting to marry him. Everything was so perfect: the two of us were inseparable, talking endlessly every day. Until this year's "National Day," when I mentioned marriage to him, he told me his true thoughts. He said, Su Jing, please forgive me, but I cannot marry you.

At first, I thought he was joking, so I replied, "Marry or don't marry." But then he continued the conversation very seriously, saying, "Since we've brought up this topic today, I think it's best to lay everything out clearly. I can't deceive you." Hearing this, my head spun, and I asked him, "Are you in love with someone else?" Quickly, I demanded an answer. He said, "Where did that come from? With you by my side, how could I fall for anyone else? Others may not know how much I am captivated by you, but surely you must know..." After hearing his words, I became even more confused. If he loves me so much, then why won't he marry me?

Then, he mentioned those four words, and I understood everything. He said, "I have a 'virgin complex.'"

To be honest, Alei, this wasn't the first time I'd heard these four words. There have been many online debates about this issue, but I don't know why, when these words came out of his mouth, they sounded so strange to me.

"Since you have a virgin complex, why did you still... with me?" I couldn't continue. Except for the lack of a marriage certificate, what difference was there between us and a formal couple?

"Jingjing, if I hadn't done that with you, how would I have known whether you were or weren't?" He actually had the nerve to say.

"You're sick!" I was furious at the time. Yuan said, "I knew you'd get angry, but think about it. If I didn't love you, I wouldn't have taken even this step. I had already decided on you, which is why I got so involved. Who would have thought... you disappointed me. I can only say, we weren't meant to be. Even so, I still can't let go of you, except for the fact that I can't marry you."

"If being with you was my first time, would you marry me?"

"In that case, I would definitely take you home without hesitation. Do you think I'm not suffering too? I like you so much, but I can't be your first man..."

After hearing his words, I was both crying and laughing.

What kind of absurd theory is this?

I also know that many men have the same requirements for their future wives as Yuan does. The question is, while demanding absolute purity from others, are you offering absolute fidelity yourself?

No one can decide the future, no one knows what will happen next.

Before Yuan, I had two previous relationships, experiencing first love and heartbreak. But those were all real love, not games. In each relationship, I invested genuine feelings and effort. They ended without result due to fate and helplessness. But this doesn't mean I've been sentenced to death, losing the right to love openly and be loved openly. I've never felt inferior about this aspect, though everyone wishes to succeed in love the first time. Love or happiness should be unrelated to when it arrives; early arrival means early blooming, late arrival means late blooming. Who can say that only the first love is the truest and most suited for marriage?

If it were money, qualifications, or appearance, I could find ways to improve, but this thing—a girl's so-called first time—I can't change. His pursuit of perfection in this regard leaves me speechless.

I've also considered that there are only two paths before us: either he changes his mind, or we break up.

But Yuan said neither path works.

He said he would never change his mind in his lifetime, and at the same time, he doesn't agree with breaking up.

I said, "Then what can we do? Do you want me to stay tangled up with you indefinitely?"

Yuan said, "What do you mean 'tangled'? Haven't you been happy with me all along? If you hadn't brought up marriage, I wouldn't even have told you any of this. Besides, it's not that I don't want to marry you—it's that you don't meet the conditions. How can you blame me?"

Alei, please forgive me for telling you so much about our conversations, because in the past month, my mind has been completely empty except for constantly thinking about these things.

To be honest, Yuan really is a partner who makes me feel quite satisfied.
