Programmer Opens a Taobao Store - Search Optimization Chapter

by geekzhang on 2013-05-05 22:08:24

Everyone knows that whether you run a physical store or an online shop, foot traffic (or web traffic) is the first and foremost factor. For Taobao stores, the largest source of traffic comes from Taobao search. How can you make your products rank higher in Taobao search? How can you ensure your products rank higher than similar products? How can others find your products? In response to these questions, Xiaoqiang will uncover the secrets behind this from the perspective of a programmer.Taobao Search RulesSince we aim to optimize search, we must first understand the rules of Taobao search. By default, Taobao item searches are sorted comprehensively, and all discussions below revolve around this comprehensive search. There are numerous resources online about Taobao's search rules, which essentially boil down to the following points: title relevance, product delisting time, window recommendation, sales volume, number of favorites, seller reputation, positive review rate, page views, and Wangwang response time. Since the store is newly opened, data for sales volume, number of favorites, seller reputation, positive review rate, page views, and Wangwang response time are unavailable—these require time to accumulate and thus fall outside the scope of discussion in this article. Window recommendations only have 30 slots; for my new store, all items will be recommended, so this is also not within the scope of this discussion. Today, Xiaoqiang will focus on discussing two indicators: title relevance and product delisting time.Title RelevanceFor me, as someone who has just started a store, the keywords in the title aren't unfamiliar. I've already had deep experiences with website operations, both for my own site and for my company's site. Taobao identifies keywords primarily through the following two aspects:1. Titles of competing products in the same category, especially those with high sales volumes. These keywords can even be directly copied over.After identifying the keywords, the next step is to check how many times these keywords are searched daily on Taobao, known as keyword popularity. Website keywords can be checked on Baidu Index, while Taobao has a similar tool called Taobao Index, which is an essential tool for optimizing Taobao titles. When selecting keywords, I did not blindly choose the most popular ones, as they face intense competition. Instead, I chose some keywords with moderate popularity but less competition, such as "Hong Kong version Wyeth stage 1" and "Hong Kong version Cow & Gate stage 1." The keyword search heat is shown in the figure below:After selecting the keywords, it’s important to add distinctive information about the store and the product into the title. Since I sell baby formula, people buying Hong Kong versions prioritize quality. Therefore, I included the two most important features of my store in the title: Hong Kong Watsons and Mannings purchases, and SF Express free shipping. I tried to fully utilize all 60 characters allowed for the Taobao title. Here’s an example of a complete title after combining everything: "Hong Kong Watsons Mannings purchase Hong Kong version Wyeth stage 1 baby formula Gold Care stage 1 SF Express free shipping."Product Delisting TimeTaobao's search rule is that the closer a product is to its delisting time, the higher it ranks. Based on this rule, everyone would think that if I set my product to delist during peak traffic hours, it would be more likely to be found in searches. This idea is correct, but the key factor is determining when the peak traffic occurs. Many related resources online suggest that generally, the peak traffic period is from 9:00 AM to 10:00 PM. However, this span is quite long. Which specific time segment sees the most active transactions for Hong Kong version baby formulas?The most accurate way to determine this is by analyzing Taobao's transaction data to judge the transaction times. Having figured out a method, it was time to implement it. Manually tallying each piece of data would be inefficient, so since I am a programmer, I decided to write a program to scrape the transaction data of competitors on Taobao and then analyze these data.After two nights of hard work, I finally completed the program and gathered some transaction data.
