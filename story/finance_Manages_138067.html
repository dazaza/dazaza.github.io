10 Things An Excellent Manager Must Do Every Day

by wgmking on 2009-04-07 17:08:31

1. Select the Best Person

As a manager, you and other members of your team are equally outstanding. You need to give yourself a better chance from the start by selecting the best member for a particular job.

When choosing the best candidate for a role, you can read "Interview Questions" to better select the most suitable person for the job.

2. Become a Motivator

The reason people do things is because we want to. Sometimes we want to do things because the consequences of not doing them would be unpleasant. However, most of the time, we do things because we hope to free ourselves from them.

Work is no exception. People work well for wages, or reputation, or recognition. They perform poorly at work because they hope to get away with minimal effort while still getting paid. They work very hard because they want their performance to leave a deep impression on someone. To better motivate your team, you need to figure out what your team members need and what you can offer them in exchange for their efforts.

3. Build Your Team

Simply motivating employees to successfully complete tasks is far from enough. They need to work as a team to achieve group goals. After all, if we just wanted them to "do their own thing," we wouldn't need you as a manager to bring them together as a team, right?

4. Be a Leader, Not Just a Manager

You've assembled the best employees into a team. You’ve motivated them to peak performance. Is there anything missing? Unless you provide a clear direction, your motivation will lose its meaning; if you don’t inspire and lead the team toward a goal, your motivation will have no value. Leading the rest of the team is indeed the ability that sets a manager apart from his peers. Remember, leadership is found at all levels of an organization, so you need to strive to become such a leader.

5. Enhance Communication Skills

Communication may be the single most important skill for a manager. After all, all other skills depend on it. If you cannot communicate your vision to your team members, you cannot be a leader. If your members don't understand what you expect from them, you cannot motivate them. Communication skills can be improved through practice. Here are two exercises that can help you effectively enhance your communication skills.

6. Better Manage Money

To sustain business, a company must make money. This means you must bring in more money than you spend. Depending on your role in the organization, you may have greater influence over one area or another, but you must understand them all. You can help your company, your colleagues, and yourself manage money better.

Don't let numbers tie you down, or be affected by the fact that "it's math." You can learn more about financial management by reading the following articles:

10. Relax

If you’re under too much stress as a manager, your efficiency will decrease. Your tolerance will drop, and you'll be more prone to losing your temper. No one wants to approach you. You need to relax. Give yourself the opportunity to unwind and recharge. When you return to work after taking a break, you'll find your efficiency high enough to make up for the lost time. Have a good laugh or lie down by the beach somewhere.

Summary: Management is a skill that can be acquired through learning. As a manager, you can improve yourself every day at work to become better. Bookmark this page and come back every day for the next two weeks to review. If you practice one tip each day, you can progress in your field of work and become a better manager compared to when you first learned these concepts. Others will also notice your improvement.
