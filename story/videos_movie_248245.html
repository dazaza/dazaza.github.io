Plot introduction of "Cold Arrow": Liu Qianjin, who has been through countless battles, is straightforward, smart, and somewhat cunning. He looks forward to the day when his old superior, Director Cheng, will send him back to the battlefield to lead troops into battle again. When an order appointing him as the captain of the vanguard team of the reformed public security forces was placed before Liu Qianjin, he reluctantly accepted the task of escorting prisoners westward. One reason why Liu Qianjin was unwilling to accept the mission was that Director Cheng had assigned Peng Hao, a fellow townsman whom he looked down upon as an "intellectual," as the political commissar of the detachment.

Before the troops set out, Miss Zhou Yuan, who was cheerful, talkative, and loved to laugh, was transferred to join the army, and Liu Qianjin did not hide his affection for her, appointing her as the confidential secretary of the detachment.

Tang Jingyin, the "First Guerilla Army Commander of Western Sichuan" of the Nationalist Party, followed the westward-moving convoy because her husband, the "Southwest Regional Guerrilla Zone Commander-in-Chief" Ning Jiahe, was in custody. She almost succeeded in rescuing her husband by scheming at Wofosi Temple. In order to stop the westward progress of the convoy and rescue Ning Jiahe, Tang Jingyin blew up the plank bridge and burned the grain station, causing heavy losses to the westward-moving convoy. With the cooperation of the lurking spies, Tang Jingyin finally abducted Ning Jiahe.

Liu Qianjin began investigating internal traitors, and various signs indicated that Peng Hao was a major suspect, which made Liu Qianjin very distressed. However, he firmly believed that his good comrade would not be a traitor. Director Cheng came to the front line to understand the situation of the troops, and Liu Qianjin told Director Cheng about his troubles. Director Cheng decided to investigate Peng Hao's history.
