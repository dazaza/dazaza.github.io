The environmental situation in our country is worrisome, and the situation of pollution control remains severe.

by ditan360 on 2010-07-28 18:30:47

http://www.ditan360.com/ 2010-07-28 12:27 Pingping

After the Copenhagen Conference, China began to vigorously address environmental pollution issues. By the first half of 2010, the overall quality of China's environment was steadily improving, but some environmental indicators showed a rebound trend. Environmental pollution in many cities remained severe, and the situation regarding pollution control was still grim.

Waste surrounding cities is everywhere visible

Many cities have become genuinely besieged by waste.

Compared with developed countries like France, Germany, and Japan, China's waste treatment methods are extremely primitive. Developed countries have a very high rate of waste recycling, usually employing high-tech processing plants for incineration treatment. In China, most garbage is simply landfilled. The "China Environmental Performance Assessment Report" states that the biggest problem with China's current waste treatment is the excessive concentration of waste treatment sites, with the main method being simple landfilling. 44% of China's urban waste requires landfilling, while incineration and reuse only account for 3% and 5% of urban waste treatment respectively. This treatment method has made many Chinese cities truly become "cities besieged by garbage."

Take Beijing alone as an example; to the east there is Ga'antun garbage landfill in Chaoyang District, to the south there is Xizhongxin Village South Camp West Team garbage site in Changxin Store Town, Fengtai District, to the west there is the garbage landfill site on the west side of Yongding River from Lugou Bridge northward to the southern foot of Yan Mountain, and to the north there is Asuwei Garbage Comprehensive Treatment Center in Xiaotangshan Town, Changping District. These four major waste treatment sites form an encircling posture, completely surrounding Beijing. Not only is the entire large city surrounded by garbage, but many urban villages within Beijing have also formed small-scale garbage sieges. For instance, around the well-known urban village near Zhongguancun, garbage can be seen everywhere.

Environmental pollution accidents occur repeatedly

In recent years, serious environmental pollution accidents have occurred frequently in China. From 1998 to 2009, over an 11-year period, nearly 500 major and supermajor environmental pollution accidents have occurred, causing significant impact on China's environment. The recent Zijin Mining pollution and Dalian oil pollution incidents have been particularly heart-wrenching. It is reported that the environmental impact caused solely by these two incidents has already far exceeded the total for all of 2009.

The final handling results for those responsible for the Zijin Mining pollution incident and the Dalian oil pollution incident have yet to emerge. Recently, two more oil explosion accidents have occurred, directly causing environmental pollution in residential areas. Among them, the explosion at the Sinopec gas station in Nanjing not only affected the environment but also resulted in over a hundred casualties.

Environmental pollution accidents occur repeatedly, leaving environmental safety without any guarantee. Under the current circumstances, this absolutely poses a threat to the long-term stability and security of the country.

Local governments do not place enough emphasis on environmental protection

Looking at all the environmental accidents that have occurred, none of them were not due to insufficient attention to environmental protection and neglect of relevant safety hazards. Even under the current situation where the central government repeatedly emphasizes the importance of environmental protection and safety hazards, such accidents still occur, clearly indicating that local governments do not pay sufficient attention to environmental protection.

I also believe that the root cause of various environmental problems lies in the insufficient attention given by grassroots levels to environmental safety and protection. If there were sufficient attention, it would certainly lead to comprehensive consideration of all possible events that could cause environmental disasters or problems, and preventive measures would be taken beforehand. Thus, environmental accidents would surely be greatly reduced.

Besides insufficient attention, local governments, for local interests, adopt perfunctory attitudes towards enterprises with potential environmental safety hazards, tolerating and conniving at them, forming tacit collusion between officials and businessmen. When building environmental protection barriers or dealing with environmental accidents, their attitude is not based on responsibility and obligation but on money, interests, and personal connections. Without problems, they just get by and take chances; when problems arise, they cover up and deceive. In short, everyone is tied to the same rope like grasshoppers, and if one is good, everyone is good. People dealing with accidents with such mentality result in countermeasures against policies from above; people treating environmental safety with such mentality will definitely end up having problems sooner or later.

This is an important reason for the frequent occurrence of environmental accidents in China today.

Although phased achievements have been made, the effects are minimal, and the situation regarding pollution control remains severe.

There is no doubt that in recent years, China's investment in environmental protection has been substantial. However, recent statistics from relevant departments show that China's input into environmental protection does not correspond proportionally to its returns. Although phased achievements have been made, the effects are minimal.

Take water management as an example. Recent environmental monitoring data released by relevant departments shows that due to China's backward garbage treatment methods, garbage buried deep underground affects the surface, along with the disorderly discharge of industrial garbage water, resulting in nearly a quarter of surface water still being polluted and even unsuitable as industrial water, while less than half of the surface water can be used for drinking.

Due to the special national conditions of China, in future development, China will face significant difficulties in pollution control in aspects such as changes in water systems, garbage treatment, air quality, acoustic environment quality, and acid rain prevention and control. The situation regarding pollution control in China remains severe.
