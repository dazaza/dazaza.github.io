5 Mitsubishi 5P floor-standing air conditioners, can only cool but not heat. Repair tip 80.

by hhw33zs34 on 2011-11-06 13:44:34

Analysis and Repair: This is a typical case of a malfunctioning four-way valve. The main causes of such malfunctions are as follows:

(1) There is a leak in the refrigeration system, which reduces the pressure difference between high and low pressures within the system, making it difficult for the four-way valve to reverse direction.

(2) Refrigerant leakage leads to insufficient return gas cooling for the compressor, causing it to operate at higher temperatures for extended periods. This results in overheating and deformation of internal components, reduced exhaust volume, shaft seizure, aging of coil insulation, and decreased work efficiency.

(3) Insufficient supply voltage to the four-way valve results in inadequate magnetic force, leading to operational failure.

(4) Prolonged operation at high temperatures causes the refrigerating oil inside the compressor to change color (yellowing, darkening), becoming turbid and deteriorating. The degraded oil loses its lubrication properties, causing severe wear on parts and having a corrosive effect on the motor windings.

(5) Carbonized substances produced by the deterioration of refrigerating oil cause dirt blockage in the capillary tube, resulting in difficulties and delays in the reversing valve's switching actions.

(6) Damage to the four-way valve itself.

Upon checking the above factors, it was found that the carbonization of the refrigerating oil led to damage of the four-way valve.

Repair Method: After replacing the four-way valve with one of the same model, the fault was eliminated.

Repair Tip: When replacing the four-way valve, it’s important to follow certain steps to avoid rework and potential damage to the valve. The steps are as follows:

(1) After confirming the damage to the four-way valve, select a replacement valve of the same specifications and model, and first remove the electromagnetic coil.

(2) Weld off the old four-way valve from the air conditioner completely and replace it with a new one.

(3) Ensure that the four copper pipe connections on the four-way valve are properly aligned, maintaining their original direction and angle. The reversing valve must remain in a horizontal state.

(4) During welding, start with the single high-pressure pipe, then weld the middle pipe out of the three (low-pressure pipe), and finally weld the left and right pipes.

(5) Use an appropriately sized welding torch with the flame adjusted to a level where it can immediately perform welding. Aim to weld about 2/3 of the copper pipe, and after welding, immediately reheat once to ensure the joint is secure. Wet towels can be used to cool down the four-way valve and copper pipes during welding.

(6) During welding, accuracy and speed are crucial. Weld each pipe in sequence, ensuring each is cooled before moving on to the next. Keep the welding time short and fast to complete the process before the four-way valve heats up excessively, thus preventing damage to the valve.
