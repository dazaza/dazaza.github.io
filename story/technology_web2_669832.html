87-year-old veteran guards the tomb of a hero for 32 years, earns 400 yuan per month (picture)

by ming1980k on 2012-02-20 11:44:25

Resting in front of the Martyrs' Shrine. Wiping the memorial tablets for the martyrs. Taking care of his wife. Showing his military medals. On the morning of December 19th, at the White Pagoda Martyrs' Shrine in Longchi Town, Weifang City, Shandong Province, 87-year-old Chen Guangju, dragging his injured left leg, carefully took down the memorial tablets from the altar, wiped them clean, and then placed them back in order.

Chen Guangju joined the army at the age of 24 and participated in both the Liberation War and the Korean War. In 1952, his left leg was injured on the battlefield. After the war, he twice politely declined job offers from his superiors and returned home to farm.

In 1979, Chen Guangju began guarding the White Pagoda Martyrs' Shrine, keeping vigil for the 527 martyrs who sacrificed their lives during the Anti-Japanese War and the Liberation War. He has been doing this for 32 years. Every day, he meticulously cleans the shrine. Among the martyrs honored in the shrine is his third younger brother, Chen Guangjin. Chen Guangju recalled that when he left home, his younger brother was only 8 years old, and they never saw each other again after that.

The elderly man has no children. Now, he and his 91-year-old wife Qi Xiusheng live in the shrine. Relatives and friends have advised him to move into a nursing home, but he said he could not bear to leave these "old comrades-in-arms." When they suggested he apply for better benefits, he replied: "With 400 yuan a month, it's enough for both of us."
