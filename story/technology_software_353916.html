My Seasons at Dawei

It feels like just the blink of an eye, but it's been a year since I entered the world of Dawei. Of course, compared to many old players, a year isn't very long. However, the joys and sorrows experienced in this virtual game world have allowed me to experience a different life that cannot be felt in reality. Although I don't have much writing skill, I want to use simple language to record the journey I've taken over the past year. Thank you to everyone who has accompanied me, whether you've already left or are still continuing to journey with me. I am grateful for all these memories...

Spring 2009: While recuperating at home from an accidental car accident, I saw an advertisement for Dawei on a webpage. Due to the familiar characters from "Dahua 3," I decided to use this game to pass the time. After graduating from university, I hadn't touched online games. When I first started playing, I could only describe myself with one word: naive. Plus, due to real-life changes, my personality became more reserved. Thus, I became a silent passerby in the outskirts of Tang Dynasty, quietly burning mountains.

One night, while doing a quest alone at Yangguan, a beautiful woman with a flying sword stopped in front of me and sent a group invitation, saying, "Let me help you." It was through meeting her that my gaming life changed (don't jump to conclusions!). Alright, let me formally introduce the first character: this beautiful woman is—Su'er. She's now the only sister I have in the game, and coincidentally, she looks a lot like my real-life younger sister (seems like I've gone off-topic).

After getting to know Su'er, through her, I found a master (haha, the irresponsible Cang Mang JJ) and joined my first guild. I no longer worried about not being able to complete quests. With her help, I got through my naive beginner days in Dawei...

Summer 2009: As I gradually became familiar with the game, I started to get the hang of it. After joining a guild, I also met many friends (though many of the early ones in Xianren Village are no longer around, I still miss our days in Xianren. Piaomiao, Dada, Guxing...). This was also when I experienced my first server merge. As an immigrant, my friends and I moved from Chaohua Xishi to Canghai Yixiao, starting my growth phase. After the merge, there were many more people. My master approached me, suggesting we form a fixed team, needing a DF. That's how my fixed-team life began. As a "top-tier" beginner, I passed Cang Mang's "test" and became acquainted with Diaoluo, Yueyue, and Fatzi, forming my first fixed team. (I remember our first group quest was ZY. After finishing, I even publicly messaged them asking if I was too weak, fearing they wouldn't want me anymore.)

This period was also when I grew the fastest. Being with them taught me about trust and responsibility among teammates, that gaming isn't just entertainment—it requires serious thought and strategy. I learned how to combine skills and cooperate with teammates. I really thank all of you for teaching me so much, especially Cang Mang, whose many PvP skills continue to benefit me to this day.

Autumn 2009: Once again, I went through another server merge, moving from Canghai Yixiao to Yueguang Baohe as an immigrant. Due to work trips, I hadn't played for nearly two months. During this time, my fixed team disbanded because of my absence and other reasons. After the merge, many guilds began merging as well. I followed Diaoluo to my current guild, Xiyue Xuan (it's still a pleasure to join Xiyue, despite having had many complaints about the guild in the past, I truly thank all the warm-hearted brothers and sisters here). Under Diaoluo's cunning planning, along with the full cooperation of Baozi (who is extremely not CJ) and the endlessly gossipy Wen Yi V, I proudly laid the foundation for my popularity as a handsome fox, though I ended up with a regrettable nickname—JJ.

In Xiyue, I formed my second fixed team, which underwent the most changes. Diaoluo, Yueyue, Ge Shou, Niu Niu, Ren Sheng. This fixed team didn't last very long, with frequent personnel changes, but it brought the most joy and conflicts. However, it gave me my biggest insight: cherish what you have, cherish everything around you. All virtual successes and rankings aren't as important as having a close friend by your side.

Winter 2009: There's no such thing as a banquet that never ends. The fixed team disbanded again. Four men decided to search for a handsome man to join our group. We captured a low-key man with both strength and idol charm (the idol charm comes from his own name). He joined our team, known for its "back-to-back" style in Xiyue. Together, we took our first steps from 2009 into 2010. Diaoluo, Ren Sheng, Idol, Ge Shou, and of course, the most handsome me, formed my third fixed team, which continues to this day...

Alright, the records may be plain and repetitive, but if I were to say it, the most important words would be four: cherish and gratitude. Now let's start my anniversary message:

Thank you, NetEase. Thank you, Dawei. Thank you, CCTV, MTV, Wen Yi V (this seems to come out too smoothly). Su Su, good little sister. Even though we don't often do quests or level up together now, you will always be my sister, and my guide in this virtual world. Cang Mang, even though you don't play anymore, I still miss you. You may have been an irresponsible master, but you were a great friend. Thank you and the group of friends like Diaoluo for helping me regain confidence and become cheerful again during the lowest point in my life. Fatzi, you're not fat, but I still consider you my fat younger brother. Due to various reasons, we contact less now and aren't as close as before, but I believe friendship lies deep in our hearts. Yueyue, for various reasons, you chose to leave and find a new fixed team. I have many misunderstandings and can't accept it, but since you made your choice, I only hope you're happy. As a friend, I wish you blessings. Niu Niu, we're not in the same guild anymore, but every time I see your pink wings flapping, it reminds me of our shared memories. Your transformation into a butterfly has taught me the joy of playing the game lightly. Baozi, a red scarf who isn't very CJ. I really hope to have a younger brother like you in real life. Thank you for your milk and your selfless help every time. Wen Yi V, I got to know you through Diaoluo, but I really admire your boldness as a girl, something many men lack. But please, don't flirt with me—I'm someone who can only be admired from afar. You're the most puzzling friend I have. We've had many frictions and contradictions, but happily, we can slowly change within our friendship. Keep it up, and let us see a more mature Baba Wu. Idol, two words: thank you. Thank you for joining us and moving forward together. Also, thank you for trusting us. I believe we will try our best to catch up with you. Let's keep pushing forward, good brother. Ren Sheng, your baby face holds a unique calmness. I really enjoy the feeling of exchanging thoughts and ideas with you. Seeing your efforts and contributions for the team makes me glad to have a friend like you. We'll witness each other's growth together. Diaoluo, with your carefree and scheming ways, I understand your many pressures and difficulties. Regardless of how others see you, remember: persistence and effort, whether in the game or in real life, can overcome any obstacle. I believe you'll find your sky.

There are still many friends who haven't appeared yet: Jiang Xue, Tie Qiu, Xiao Shan, and so on... Thank you all for your help and the happiness you've brought me. Perhaps we'll continue fighting together in Dawei, or perhaps one day we'll part ways in this game world. But I am truly grateful to all my friends. The memories you've given me will be a very beautiful and profound recollection.
