Only because the encounter is too beautiful

by mimx22w33 on 2012-02-23 22:27:23

Missing a person means silently blessing him/her in the bottom of your heart. Recalling your humorous jokes when you're worried, recalling your hearty laughter, recalling the lingering depth and sadness within you, it's like a blooming flower, its fragrance permeating a dimly lit room, quietly seeping into my heart, intoxicating my soul...

Missing is an inexplicable obsession, paying attention to your joys and sorrows, caring about your every frown and smile, valuing your every move, worrying endlessly for not receiving your messages. Restless with deep longing, melancholic due to the threads of separation; the dinner candle still on the table, as if your shadow were opposite me, discarding all desires and distractions, savoring your meticulousness; the moon follows people away, only because the encounter was too beautiful despite the endless yearning; who else can I tell my dreams to? For whom have I broken them? The flowers remain silent first of all, but I must apologize... I've started thinking about you again. Missing you, being concerned about you has become incurable, my thoughts always unexpectedly gently knocking on the door of my heart, leaving me unprepared.

Tonight, there are no stars or moon, the drizzle continues more tenderly. Raindrops tapping on the window frame, soaking my thoughts until they are blurred and drenched. A fleeting glance, watching time page by page torn away, a year just passes in this instant... Looking back, I don't know what kind of mood to use to decipher the confusion of this moment... In this year, so many things have happened, knowing I am too emotional, thinking too much, always too tired, looking up and down, always feeling physically and mentally exhausted, hesitating between dreams and reality, looking back, yet ultimately piecing together fragmented memories in the quiet rain pavilion, in the flying catkins sky, retaining a faint sense of loneliness.

The tea's fragrant mist fills the room, floating incessantly. I daydream in front of the screen, holding my breath waiting for you, awaiting the beginning of our story. Left to me is still a string of memory symbols. I try to get drunk in the place where you are, listening to you recount the traces of chasing your dreams.

In fact, regarding you, I am really quite conflicted. I know that my excessive display of affection towards you causes you great distress. Actually, you don't have to be like that, I don't require you to love me back, whether or not you love me is your personal matter, just like loving you is my personal matter. I really enjoy the feeling of loving someone, without needing to take, without needing return, just fully enjoying this spiritual landscape-like realm. Just like standing on the beach edge, quietly appreciating the sea, my heart rising and falling with her tides, suddenly sad and happy, yet never having thought of owning her.

Eternal love, that is too distant a dream, no one can really guarantee it. Moreover, from the start, I clearly knew the distance between us, so I had long prepared myself to accept passing each other by. I deeply admire your talent, for your sacredness, willingly filling your blank space, allowing you to step on my feelings to find your brush; willingly becoming your safe harbor, calm and peaceful, far away from there watching you, blessing you, waiting for you occasionally to seek refuge from the wind and rain, then continuing your journey.

For one person to meet another, the possibility is only one in ten million, the possibility of becoming friends is approximately one in two hundred million, and the possibility of becoming lovers is only one in fifty billion. Fate is a very mysterious thing, the world is so vast, there are so many people, yet I met you. Just such an accident, just such a coincidence, opened the curtain of another sky.

Buddhism often mentions the two words "fate" and "law", carefully pondering these two words are truly very profound. Fate and law can be encountered but not sought, I also know that forced love, merely outwardly united while inwardly estranged, how long could it last! Not forcing is actually also a philosophy of life.

Your sky is so beautiful, I can only appreciate it, unable to possess it; your world is so vast, I can only look at it, unable to enter it. After all, we are forever people from two different worlds. My world can accommodate you, but your world cannot encompass my sky. Reality is just so cruel, secular conventions are just so helpless, so when I can still possess it, I will love with all my might, burdening myself heavily, yet willingly enduring the bitterness, and when one day I must withdraw, I will also tell myself, I have no regrets towards my own heart, nor towards the person I have loved.

Love or not to love, often it's just a thought away. Sometimes, I'd rather believe that you've never been moved, only having a sense of being touched, this way, that day I can calmly face myself, not tasting the pain gnawing night after night, the hurt of being forgotten minute after minute. Rather be a landscape in your heart, the most beautiful, the most unique landscape, regardless of the future road, she can always give you a lasting comfort, clear beauty, romance, warmth... So, please allow me to love you in my own way, letting your every day carry my thoughts, my blessings, until forever.
