Yahoo Shares Hit 5-Year High on Alibaba Profit Growth

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 14:26:53

On July 18 Beijing time, according to foreign media reports, driven by a sharp increase in the first-quarter results of Alibaba Group, Yahoo's stock price soared by 10.34% during regular trading on the NASDAQ Stock Exchange, closing at $29.66 and reaching its highest level since May 2008.

Yahoo said on Tuesday that Alibaba Group's net profit in the first quarter increased threefold year-on-year to $669 million; revenue surged by 71% year-on-year to $1.38 billion. After selling nearly half of its shares in Alibaba Group last year, Yahoo still holds about 24% of Alibaba Group's equity.

The strong performance of Alibaba Group has favorably supported Yahoo's stock price. One year after Marissa Mayer became Yahoo's CEO, this company is still in the early stages of transformation. By focusing on launching new products, Yahoo aims to attract advertisers and consumers from Google and Facebook. Analyst Mark Mahaney of RBC Capital Markets believes that the majority of Yahoo's stock price increase over the past year has been driven by the appreciation of its holdings in Alibaba Group and Yahoo Japan.

Analyst Sameet Sinha of Riley & Co. said, "Alibaba Group is showing a lot of operating leverage. This company has built a large market in China and started charging users." Yahoo's second-quarter financial report released on Tuesday showed that due to a 12% year-on-year decline in display advertising revenue, the company's revenue excluding traffic acquisition costs (TAC) was $1.071 billion, down 1% year-on-year; net profit was $331 million, up 46% year-on-year. Yahoo gained $225 million in the second quarter through its holdings in Alibaba Group and Yahoo Japan, higher than $180 million in the same period last year.

Skyrocketing Results

Yahoo posted a message on its official website on Tuesday saying that Alibaba Group's net profit in the first quarter tripled from $220 million in the same period last year to $669 million. This Hangzhou-based company may conduct its initial public offering by the end of this year.

The Asian assets held by Yahoo also gave it an opportunity for hedging due to the depreciation of the yen exchange rate. Yahoo said it gained $243 million in cash income in the second quarter due to hedging transactions. In May, Yahoo had stated that due to hedging on its shares in Yahoo Japan, the company gained $270 million in pre-tax income in the first quarter and $3 million in pre-tax income last year.

Yahoo said on Tuesday that its revenue excluding traffic acquisition costs would be between $1.06 billion and $1.1 billion in the third quarter. Bloomberg data showed that market analysts expected Yahoo's third-quarter revenue to be $1.12 billion.

CEO's Efforts

Bloomberg Industries analyst Paul Sweeney said that while Mayer strives to increase the number of Yahoo users, investors must be patient and wait for Yahoo's advertising revenue to grow. Sweeney said, "Mayer's investment in products to enhance user engagement and ultimately achieve revenue growth is a long-term endeavor. Judging from Yahoo's current performance expectations, these investments by Mayer are unlikely to significantly boost the company's performance in the short term."

Market research company EMarketer estimated that the scale of the display advertising market reached $17.5 billion last year, with Yahoo's share declining from 9.2% last year to 7.9% this year. Google's share will increase by 3 percentage points to nearly 18%; Facebook's share is expected to increase by 2 percentage points to reach 17%.

Yahoo's financial report showed that the company's display advertising business revenue declined by 12% year-on-year in the second quarter, dropping to $472 million. Colin Gillis, an analyst at U.S. investment company BGC Partners, said: "In fact, the difficulties in Yahoo's display advertising business have just begun. We haven't yet seen what measures the Yahoo management can take to stop their declining trend."

Chain Reaction

Mayer compared her revival plan to a "chain reaction," saying it will eventually lead to revenue growth for the company. During the analyst call broadcast live online on Tuesday, Mayer said, "Hiring and retaining a great team; they will develop inspiring products that attract users and increase traffic; the increase in traffic can attract advertisers' interest and eventually convert into revenue. First comes the people, then the products, followed by traffic, and finally revenue."

Yahoo also lowered its revenue forecast for 2013 on Tuesday. Excluding traffic acquisition costs, Yahoo expects fiscal year revenue to be at most $4.55 billion, lower than the $4.6 billion projected in April, and only slightly higher than last year's $4.47 billion. Analyst Sinha believed, "Yahoo will experience another year with no revenue growth."

Crazy Acquisitions

Driven by the sale of its shares in Alibaba Group, Mayer began a spree of acquisitions after taking office. During her tenure as Yahoo's CEO, Yahoo has acquired at least 18 companies, including the $1.1 billion acquisition of Tumblr, as well as mobile application developers Stamped, Jybe, and Summly. Earlier this month, Yahoo acquired address book application and plugin company Xobni for approximately $70 million.

Yahoo announced on Wednesday that it has acquired Admovate, an innovative company providing technology for buying and selling mobile advertisements. The four technical personnel from Admovate will join Yahoo to help the company generate income through ads displayed on smartphones and tablets.

In the second quarter, Yahoo spent $653 million to repurchase 25.3 million shares of the company's stock at an average repurchase price of $2.576 billion.

Yahoo's current stock price is close to $31, which was also the offer price when Microsoft initially made its acquisition offer in 2008. Although Microsoft later raised the acquisition price per share to $33, Yahoo still rejected Microsoft's acquisition offer. Due to the reduction in the number of outstanding shares, Yahoo's current market value is $31.8 billion, still far less than the $47.5 billion acquisition price offered by Microsoft back then.
