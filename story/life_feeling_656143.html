Be used to waiting, be used to staying away

by huiming on 2012-01-06 22:19:25

Habitual waiting, habitual distancing

People always recall certain things and people in specific environments. Those we deem insignificant, like a tiny raindrop casually falling into the river of life, or a flower drifting with the current. Unintentionally, they converge into a glittering river, flowing day and night, running through our entire lives. Standing at the crossroads of time, looking back at those past landscapes, the light is so faint that it's hard to find any bright sunshine.

Walking on the streets at night, the sky is covered with dark clouds, no moonlight or starlight. A cool drizzle falls, seemingly present yet absent, deepening my feelings of melancholy. In search of inner peace, I unconsciously arrive at an abandoned small station. In the darkness, distant mountains appear faintly. A brightly lit train passes by, like a meteor streaking across the sky, breaking the night's tranquility.

I think I am a traveler under the train, docked at my heart's station. Unaware of the journey ahead, not questioning the path behind. Like the wind, like the rain, I drift aimlessly without direction. Just as I don't know how I ended up here, I also don't know how long I will stay. I feel like a kite without a string, wandering aimlessly between heaven and earth.

The small station has long been deserted, its weathered characters hinting at yesterday's hustle and bustle. Walking around a few rows of houses, I see the neatly trimmed flowers in the yard, clearly just recently pruned. The scattered flowers are eagerly receiving the nourishment of the rain. Curiously, I wonder who would be so dedicated to tending these flowers in this desolate station... As the fine rain becomes denser in the night, I still don't want to stop, because I want to see who will come here? For whom does the fragrance linger? For whom do the flowers wait? Is it like me years ago, waiting for my own happiness? I don't know if the person waiting has already achieved their wish.

Thinking while walking, walking while thinking, when I feel completely soaked, the rain suddenly stops above my head. An umbrella opens up a clear sky. "Don't walk anymore, the rain is getting heavier." Turning back, I see a woman standing in the rain. Her age is indistinguishable in the night, her face calm and elegant with an indescribable nobility. When she walks beside me, I see her hands - they aren't the hands of a countrywoman, delicate and tender, I imagine they belong to an artist.

That night, in the rain, I heard a heartbreaking story -

A girl from an art school deeply loved a boy. When they planned to get married, his family returned from overseas to take him away. So, like all movie scripts, the boy and the girl decided to run away together. They came to this small station, three months later, the boy left. The girl didn't know where the boy went, only remembering the boy's promise to send her flowers on her birthday...

After a moment of silence, I know this woman has been waiting, at this already abandoned station. She says: "I've become accustomed to the sound of the train whistle, accustomed to waiting, accustomed to parting..." The rain continues, when I turn around, she hands me the umbrella, without saying thank you, there's a mysterious feeling of emotion spreading in my heart. I really want to tell her: Behind every love story are tears, how could two people who love each other so deeply disappear without a trace in such a technologically advanced era, leaving no sign? In fact, love comes in many forms, letting go is one of them, just as the song goes: "Please be happier than me, it won't be in vain for my lifetime of waiting." Perhaps that woman already knows the outcome, as long as he feels happy, that's enough.

Maybe women are born with higher emotional intelligence than IQ. Waiting a lifetime for a man who has long forgotten her seems like something that only happens in scripts, but there are indeed women in the world willing to spend their entire lives waiting. Just so that when they meet again in old age, they can say to him: "Please remember to be with me in the next life."

I don't know when the rain stopped, the moon appears, its silk-like light shining brightly as if washed clean. The night is a bit chilly, the raindrops on the tree leaves crystal-clearly reflect the gentle affection of the moon. In this silent distance, I hear confessions from heaven... Recalling past events, recalling you, recalling our joys and sorrows together, memories sealed away come alive in this instant. Those past events flood my heart like waves. Thus, I am immersed in endless thoughts. Remembering that classic line: "What makes people unable to extricate themselves, besides teeth, is love."

I don't know if you, who once promised to be with me for life, still remember me, still remember our solemn promises. How much I wish we could walk together in this gentle night like before, weaving a net with our purest hearts, involuntarily immersing ourselves in it. Like a lost bird, happily captured. At this moment, there's a soft, tender, joyful pain. After wandering for so long, I still don't know where my final destination is. Are all dreams mirages, the mythical Shangri-La, unseen by anyone? Maybe it doesn't even exist, only in the dreams of fantasizers. And I am just a person, habitually waiting for you, habitually accepting your departure.

My scenery is in my heart, while your scenery is on the road... Life's fleeting comings and goings, some accompany for a while, others miss each other; regardless of the outcome, fate cannot be avoided. I just want you to know, my life has had you, and I have been in yours. That's enough...
