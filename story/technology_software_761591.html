Hands-on Preview of the Firefox OS Geeksphone Peak

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 21:00:15

The first batch of Firefox phones, the Geeksphone Peak/Keon, sold out on their release day. According to foreign media reports, the actual sales volume was only 1,000 units. Now, the first batch has been shipped out, and we have conducted a simple hands-on test after receiving it.Hardware AppearanceLet's review the configuration of the Peak first:The size of the Peak is close to that of the Nexus 4, with a very light body. The screen bezel ratio and home button position at the front resemble the iPhone 5 at first glance. It is wrapped around by cheap engineering plastic. On the right side from top to bottom are the power button and volume buttons; at the top are the 3.5mm headphone jack and microUSB interface. Near the camera on the back, there is a protrusion similar to the moto xt910. The overall build quality is quite average. For example, as shown in the picture below, the details of the buttons easily remind people of cheap plastic toys.The MSM8225 processor originally supports a maximum resolution of only WVGA, with relatively weak performance, being the lowest-end product in the Snapdragon S4 series. Although it is paired with an IPS screen, the colors appear grayish and whitish. Moreover, the awkward qHD resolution significantly affects usability, which will be mentioned later.Firefox OSConsidering that the Peak is merely a developer preview version, we can ignore all the hardware flaws. After Geeksphone, many manufacturers such as Huawei, ZTE, Sony, LG, etc., will subsequently launch products with more complete hardware. The significance of the Peak lies more in showcasing Firefox OS and demonstrating the determination to bet on web developers.Back to the system itself. After sliding up on the lock screen, there are quick start buttons for the camera and unlock buttons, requiring users to click again to enter the desktop. This design seems cumbersome, and it's unclear what the intention behind it is. In the pre-release version released at the beginning of the year, Firefox OS used a design similar to Android 2.3 where swiping left starts the camera and swiping right unlocks. Was this change made to avoid patent issues?The operation logic of Firefox OS is basically similar to iOS, with only one layer of menus and paged app icons. The far-left column is global search, and the status bar can be pulled down for quick settings. A distinctive feature is that under the global search box, there are categorized web app recommendations. Actually, these seemingly rich applications are just mobile versions of web links, but they can help alleviate the initial lack of apps for Firefox OS to some extent.Firefox OS apps are divided into two types: hosted apps, which are common web apps that direct users to a web address via a manifest file and require loading each time they are opened; and packaged apps, which are downloaded directly onto the phone after packaging and have more permissions and system APIs, similar to local apps. It's easy to notice that both Ubuntu Touch, the desktop version of Chrome OS, and Firefox OS have this strange dual-app ecosystem. This is contradictory because, on one hand, packaged apps offer better experiences, and all operating systems hope that more developers will create custom applications tailored to this platform; on the other hand, they highlight HTML5 as a key feature, hoping that HTML5 and web apps can capture a share of the mobile OS market.Under current circumstances without much anticipation for the future, the experience of current web applications remains quite poor. Even packaged apps only include some rudimentary works, lacking any attractive major titles, making these apps reminiscent of the early applications for S60v5. The HTML5 version of Cut the Rope demonstrated at various exhibitions previously is not available in the app store.Additionally, since Firefox OS targets the low-end market, most applications are HVGA resolution, directly leading to many applications maintaining HVGA DPI levels. Due to the Peak adopting a qHD resolution, situations like displaying only half a screen or having overly small icons occur. Not only third-party apps, but also the official app store and built-in NOKIA Here map have this problem (as shown below). In the next update, the resolution issue will be resolved as soon as possible, hopefully improving this situation when a large number of consumer versions of Firefox phones hit the market in the middle of the year.Due to hardware limitations and the inherent imperfections of the system, the browser experience is unsatisfactory. Most websites fail to recognize it as a mobile device, and videos cannot be played on YouTube, greatly limiting its usability. Some benchmark scores are as follows:All native apps of Firefox OS follow their own development standards and UI design guidelines. Whether it's wallpapers or UI, they have a strong sense of style unique to themselves. Of course, there are drawbacks, such as inheriting the anti-human design of the back button in the upper left corner from iOS, which now appears as a vertical rectangle with an extremely low hit rate. Apart from the built-in phone, SMS, and browser, the player design stands out, with Cover Tile reminiscent of Microsoft's Modern UI.SummaryIf used as a daily machine, the Geeksphone Peak is almost unusable. Christian Heilmann, a participant in Firefox OS, also pointed out that Geeksphone is not intended for everyday consumers. As a mobile operating system, Firefox OS is also not yet fully developed, still lacking some very simple and basic functions, such as the inability to lock the screen, a feature that has long been criticized on Windows Phone, which is currently missing in Firefox. After all, it is only a Preview phone.If you want to try out Firefox OS, there is another very simple method: add the Firefox OS simulator add-on in the Firefox browser, which can give you a rough impression of Firefox OS.The only thing worth celebrating is that compared to the current Ubuntu Touch, Firefox OS is clearly a more complete operating system. More manufacturers (ZTE, TCL, Sony, LG, etc.) will also launch their own Firefox phones in the middle of the year. Time is also an important factor in the race for the third place in the market.
