Only Wei Xiaobao is the most suitable to be a product manager.

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 12:09:17

Written by Kuaidao Qingyi

If someone were to ask me point-blank, "Who do you think is the most suitable product manager among Jin Yong's wuxia characters?" I would answer without hesitation, "Wei Xiaobao!"

Why?

For such a brilliantly shameless, cunning, lewd, smooth-talking, and uneducated guy, he always knows what he wants and strives for it.

Unfortunately, I've encountered too many Guo Jings, Yang Gous, Chen Jialuos, and Zhang Wujis...

1. Over-Consumed Product Managers

In this day and age, how many ignorant and naive young men and women clutch their copies of "Everyone Is a Product Manager," their eyes gleaming with wolf-like hunger. In recent interviews, I have indeed come across some laughable situations. Here are a couple for your amusement, but please don't take them personally.

1. IT Information Maniac

"Teacher, every day I read tons of tech news—from Huxiu, TMT Post, 36Kr, Lei Feng Net, Internet Matters, to Sohu, Tencent, NetEase, Sina's IT channels—and I also follow many product circle teachers' Weibo blogs, like Kentzhu, Chunya Yin, etc. Every night, I stay up until 3 AM. Do you think I'm suited to be a product manager?" His face was full of anticipation, his eyes brimming with deep yearning and dreams.

"Whether or not you're suited to be a product manager, I'm not sure, but I think you'd make a great night security guard!" In that instant, I felt like Yin Zhiping, cruelly crushing Longnü's dreams under my weight, shattering many men's aspirations without a trace.

There are plenty of people who consume vast amounts of information and know Silicon Valley's major tech companies better than they know how many kebabs are in their neighborhood. They can talk at length about Yahoo!'s shopping addiction, Amazon's ambition, and Apple's future, but when asked about the product they want to create the most, they stammer and fumble. When asked to draw a simple flowchart of modules, it's riddled with errors.

2. Dreamers Who Want to Change Human Life

"I really want to create a product. I believe once this product is out, the appeal and users of the major portals will decrease by at least one-third, changing many people's internet habits."

The above is the exact words of an interviewee, and in that moment, I had high hopes for him.

"Tell me more!"

He glanced at me, his eyes full of caution, perhaps just like Wei Xiaobao when he first met A Ke with Zheng Kesha nearby, fearing that this小白face would steal his beloved.

"Swear you won't leak this, and you won't use it to start a business!"

For any self-respecting interviewer, this should be the moment to slam the table and storm out.

Unfortunately, I lack self-respect and only have a heart full of gossip. I really did swear...

But the product idea he told me could roughly cost around 150 million yuan for its first version.

Yes, in RMB, not ghost money.

3. Over-Anxious Premature PMs

I used to think staying at a company for ten years meant loyalty.

After reviewing countless resumes, my worldview has changed. Being able to stay at a company for a year already makes a PM quite reliable.

One particular PM was scheduled for a 10 AM interview. After half an hour with no sign of him, I called. On the other end of the phone, he questioned me, "Why is your company so congested? Is it even a place for humans?" In that instant, my little heart was utterly crushed by his domineering question, and I silently hung up.

I spent ten minutes gathering my emotions, telling myself to be brave. I prepared to point outside the door as soon as he reached the company building and say, "Get lost, we don't want people without manners here!" As I pondered whether to use my index finger or middle finger to point at him, he called and said, "I'm not coming there anymore!" Then he hung up.

That feeling is like spending tremendous effort collecting all seven Dragon Balls, summoning the dragon to lay your dream lover on the bed, and just as you're about to pounce, the alarm clock rings.

4. Job-Hopping God

No need for more words, who has seen someone job-hop six times within a year?

I have.

Hearing the truth in the morning means dying contentedly in the evening!

Two. Why Wei Xiaobao is the Most Suitable Product Manager

After reading through Jin Yong's novels, I still believe Wei Xiaobao is the most suitable product manager. His traits cannot be simply summed up as "little thug" while overshadowing his brilliant qualities. There are at least five traits worth learning from.

1. Strong Communication Skills

Tailoring his speech to the audience, with extraordinary adaptability. Just by adding the three words "with madam" to the phrase "Hong Sect Master enjoys eternal blessings and longevity," he showed his keen insight. Instantly transforming a tense atmosphere into warmth, Su Quan greeted him with a smile, and Hong Sect Master, seeing his young mistress happy, naturally became delighted as well.

Towards Mao Shiba, Wei Xiaobao emphasized chivalrous spirit. When Mao Shiba doubted he would inform the authorities for a reward, Wei Xiaobao angrily cursed, "Your grandmother! How can you betray friends and still talk about chivalry?" Chivalry was Mao Shiba's soft spot.

Towards Kangxi, Wei Xiaobao emphasized friendship. For a lonely emperor, nothing compares to a true friend who treats him as an equal. Thus, when exposing the fake Empress Dowager hiding a man in the palace, Wei Xiaobao said, "There is a confidential matter to tell my good friend Xiao Xuanzi, but absolutely not my master, the Emperor. If the Emperor hears, he'll cut off my head. Xiao Xuanzi, being my friend, might not care." This statement wouldn't anger the young emperor but instead move him to consider Wei Xiaobao a confidant.

Towards Chen Jinnan, Wu Sangui, Shi Lang, and even Sophia, Princess of Russia, Wei Xiaobao managed to resolve issues through language. This ability to switch roles is exactly what a product manager needs.

Being able to cry poverty to the boss, sell cuteness to users, paint grand visions to engineers, and flatter designers requires constant practice to quickly find everyone's sweet spot.

2. Grounded

Compared to other protagonists in Jin Yong's novels, Wei Xiaobao is undoubtedly the most grounded. Born in Yangzhou's Lichun Brothel, his mother was a prostitute, and his father unknown. From Chen Jialuo and Duan Yu to prestigious disciples Linghu Chong, Xuzhu, and Zhang Wujie, none of them have backgrounds worse than his. However, precisely because of Wei Xiaobao's humble beginnings, he knows what lower-class individuals like himself need.

Looking at Wei Xiaobao's successes, many are built upon help from seemingly insignificant figures. For example, small eunuchs in the palace, young maidservants near the Empress Dowager, guards in the royal city, and even Yang Yizhi, Wu Yingxiong's confidant. Among Jin Yong's numerous characters, Guo Jing may be a great hero for the country and its people, but he often lacks eloquence; Yang Guo is infatuated with Xiaolongnü, leaving the tomb to flirt with every girl he meets; Linghu Chong is stuck in personal emotional whirlpools, trying to be his younger sister's backup; Zhang Wujie keeps having strokes of luck yet remains weak; and Chen Jialuo is just a pretty face, making Xiang Xiang's death worthwhile. Only Wei Xiaobao completely separates the "hero" from the "martial arts" restriction. The level of martial arts is unimportant to Wei Xiaobao. The essence of being a hero permeates the imperial court, the grassroots, the martial world, and romantic relationships, achieving the realm of "like a gazelle hanging its horns, leaving no trace."

For many product managers, grounding oneself is essential. Understanding Silicon Valley is important, but capturing the demands of migrant workers in the Pearl River Delta ensures greater success. Years ago, countless ambitious overseas returnees started businesses in China. Those unwilling to localize or change, merely copying Silicon Valley's advanced experiences, mostly failed miserably.

3. Results-Oriented and Perseverance

Wei Xiaobao always sets clear goals before making any decision and then strives to achieve them. For instance, his ultimate life goal was to return to Yangzhou and open several brothels with his mother as the madam. To reach this goal, he went to great lengths to amass wealth.

This is more evident in his pursuit of women. He once vowed to A Ke, "By heaven above and earth below, in this lifetime, even if I climb a mountain of knives, dive into a sea of oil, am dismembered by a thousand cuts, my entire family is executed, commit high treason, incur ten thousand unforgivable sins, become a thief and a harlot, leave no descendants, and am struck by lightning, causing a thousand and one boils on my body, I will still insist on marrying you as my wife." For Wei Xiaobao, who always cheats when swearing brotherhood with Mao Shiba, this shows his determination.

However, when Zheng Kesha was rescued and A Ke embraced the handsome youth, Wei Xiaobao felt as if his chest was shattered by a boulder. In such a situation, most people would lose all hope, sorrowfully retreat, but only someone like Wei Xiaobao, the forefather of backups, would defiantly declare, "In short, I will spend my entire life chasing after you, haunting you relentlessly, refusing to let go, even if you marry eighteen times, the nineteenth time must still be with me."

Currently, 95% of product managers in the industry are essentially cannon fodder. Success doesn't come easily to anyone. Only after killing countless projects and escaping countless pursuits by engineers and artists can one carefully avoid all hidden dangers in the next product process.

To be a product manager, one must possess Wei Xiaobao's tenacity, "Even if I kill eighteen products, the nineteenth will succeed!"

4. Core Competence

A product manager is a position requiring broad expertise—ideally understanding technology, design, prototyping, and operations. While knowing a bit of everything is beneficial, excelling in one area makes one an outstanding product manager. Whether it's deep technical knowledge, operational expertise, persuasion skills, or even PowerPoint proficiency, these are all significant strengths.

Just like Wei Xiaobao, lacking both internal and external kung fu skills, his core competence lies in his mastery of the escape art, Shenxing Baibian.

5. Great Achievers Don’t Sweat the Small Stuff

Just thinking about Wei Xiaobao's rise to fame brings pain to one’s testicles—"He then crawled under the salt merchant's crotch, grabbed the person's scrotum, and squeezed hard." Additionally, when Mao Shiba criticized him for using lime powder to blind opponents and chopping feet under tables during rescues, violating martial codes, Wei Xiaobao retorted, "Killing with a knife is killing; killing with lime is also killing. What's the difference between upper class and lower class? If it weren't for this little devil using low tactics to save you, you old devil would have become an upper-class ghost long ago."

Many young product managers overly focus on whether their PRD documents are thorough, unwilling to communicate face-to-face with engineers for just five minutes; striving to perfect prototypes and sending out emails believing their work is done. For product managers, if the product doesn't launch smoothly, no matter how much work was done beforehand, no one will care. Focus more time and energy on delivering results rather than obsessing over interaction effects in prototypes.

Learn to adjust your methods based on the team's style. There's no set template.

Conclusion:

From ranting about recruitment to discussing product traits, looking back at this piece, it can be summarized by Wei Jueye's words, "Many flaws, few merits!"

I just hope many fellow product managers can have fewer pretentious airs like Guo Jing and less hypocrisy like Yang Guo, and more down-to-earth charm like Wei Xiaobao. Stop dreaming about creating a product that changes the world and strengthens China; first, strengthen yourself. Only then can you handle a scenario where six women from Lichun share a bed, allowing Su Quan and A Ke to hit two birds with one stone.

Alright, mission accomplished, give me a kiss!
