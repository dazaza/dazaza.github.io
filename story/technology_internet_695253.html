[Exclusive] Qingdao Evening News interviews the downloader of Apple's 25 billionth app

by qssqwe215 on 2012-03-07 15:03:40

Abstract: Starting from February 17, Apple's App Store launched a countdown event for the [25 billionth] download. The content was simple: anyone in the world who happened to be the person downloading the 25 billionth Apple app would receive a $10,000 Apple App Store gift card. Fu Chunli from Qingdao became the lucky [Apple girl], unintentionally becoming the 25 billionth downloader of an Apple app, with odds lower than winning a 5 million lottery jackpot. Yesterday, after much effort, reporters from the Qingdao Evening News and Qingdao All Search learned about this lucky girl who had a connection with Apple.

Qingdao Evening News reporter tracked down the winner, Fu Chunli

Her chances of winning were even lower than winning a 5 million jackpot

From February 17, Apple's App Store started a [25 billionth] download countdown event. It was straightforward: whoever downloaded the 25 billionth Apple software globally would win a $10,000 App Store gift card. Fu Chunli from Qingdao became this lucky person, with her chances of winning being lower than hitting a 5 million lottery jackpot. Yesterday, after many twists and turns, the reporter contacted Fu Chunli by phone. Surprisingly, she had only bought an iPhone a month ago and had just learned how to download games from the Apple store from a friend. Before learning she had won, Fu Chunli was unaware that Apple had held such a prize event. On Saturday, Fu Chunli received a call from Apple’s Chinese staff informing her that she had won. "At first, I didn't take it seriously and thought it was a scam, but the staff patiently explained the situation to me very seriously," Fu Chunli said. Later, Apple communicated through email and its official website to confirm the news.

Doubtful upon receiving the call

Yesterday, after much effort, the reporter finally managed to contact Fu Chunli by phone. Talking about her win, this young woman from Qingdao frankly admitted it was too lucky, "I accidentally won." Fu Chunli is not an Apple fanatic; she had only owned an iPhone for a month and had just learned how to download games from the Apple store from a friend. Before knowing she had won, Fu Chunli was unaware that Apple had held such a prize event. Last Saturday, Fu Chunli received a call from Apple's Chinese staff, telling her she had won. "At the time, I didn't pay attention, thinking it was a scam, but the staff patiently explained the situation to me very seriously and carefully clarified the situation." Fu Chunli said that afterwards, Apple communicated via email and their official website to confirm the news.

Happy beyond words - harder to win than a 5 million jackpot

After learning about Fu Chunli's win, many friends called to congratulate her. "They all said I was too lucky, not because of what I won, but because the chances of winning were so small, smaller than winning a 5 million lottery jackpot. So I thank Apple for giving me this opportunity, and I also thank the patient explanation from Apple's Chinese staff. If it weren't for their service attitude, I might have missed the chance to claim the prize."

According to Apple, Fu Chunli will receive a $10,000 iTunes gift card, thanking her for bringing the Apple mobile app store to this historic moment.

In daily life, Fu Chunli is an outgoing and lively girl who likes fresh things and is a loyal fan of "Palm Qingdao". "I think Palm Qingdao is very convenient. As soon as I heard about it, I downloaded and used it," Fu Chunli said. She plans to recommend Palm Qingdao to more friends. Upon learning about Fu Chunli's win, the Unicom company immediately expressed their congratulations and promised to provide additional free trial services.
