May I ask 12306: What about the promised mobile phone ticket purchasing?

by anonymous on 2013-09-12 12:14:54

This year's National Day holiday is from October 1st to October 7th. According to the 20-day advance sale period (including the day itself) for online ticket purchasing and telephone reservations, starting today, passengers can book train tickets for October 1st through the internet and telephone. However, the mobile phone ticket purchasing service that was expected to be launched last September has still not been officially introduced.

Starting today, you can purchase tickets for October 1st

Currently, the advance sale period for online (12306 website) and telephone bookings (95105105) is 20 days (including the day itself); the advance sale period for station ticket windows and authorized ticket outlets is 18 days (including the day itself). According to the advance sale schedule, starting today, passengers can purchase tickets for the first day of National Day via the internet and telephone. Tickets for October 1st purchased at the station window or authorized outlets will be available starting September 14th.

Online and telephone ticket booking now allows nationwide ticket collection. Passengers can use the valid ID of the passenger used during booking to collect their reserved tickets at any station window or authorized outlet nationwide. Online tickets must be collected before departure. For telephone bookings, the specific collection times are: tickets booked before 12 PM on a given day must be collected before 12 PM the next day; tickets booked after 12 PM must be collected before midnight the next day.

In addition, starting September 1st, train tickets can be refunded or modified nationwide. According to regulations, if the ticket is returned more than 48 hours before the departure time listed on the ticket, a 5% cancellation fee will be charged; if the ticket is returned between 24 and 48 hours before departure, a 10% cancellation fee will be charged; if the ticket is returned less than 24 hours before departure, a 20% cancellation fee will be charged.

No word yet on mobile phone train ticket purchases after one year

On September 13th of last year, the former Ministry of Railways announced the launch of a mobile ticketing application called "Palm 12306" by the end of September last year. It was introduced that this software supports both Apple and Android platforms, and after its launch, passengers could download and use it for free. The Apple platform app could be downloaded from App Store, while the Android platform app could be directly downloaded from the www.12306.cn website. Through this software, passengers could inquire about train schedules across the country, reserve tickets, and complete online payments via a mobile payment system.

However, up until now, almost a year has passed, and the highly anticipated official mobile ticketing application "Palm 12306" has yet to be officially launched. Although there have been multiple rumors of its impending release, each time nothing materialized.

While the "official version" of the mobile ticketing software failed to appear, various unauthorized versions flourished. When China News Service journalists searched "12306" in the Apple App Store, they found as many as 63 different versions of mobile ticketing clients, some free, others paid, with prices ranging from 6 yuan, 12 yuan to 30 yuan.

To confirm, China News Service journalists contacted the 12306 customer service hotline. A staff member clearly stated that the railway department indeed has not yet launched the "Palm 12306" mobile ticketing service. To ensure safe ticket purchases, passengers are advised to book tickets on the official China Railway Customer Service Center 12306 website. Regarding when the "Palm 12306" mobile ticketing service will be launched, the staff member said "no notification has been received so far."
