Author: Cao Jianwei. Only by persistently protecting one's mind can one transcend the commercial world. Following the foundational work of Chinese business philosophy literature, "Gray Business," Cao Jianwei has released his new business philosophy novel, "The Merchant's Curse." This is a classic and pioneering work reflecting on the life and fate of Chinese merchants, using eerie writing to carve out the essence of the business world and religious sentiment to abstract the helplessness of the soul. In the illusory commercial world, three extremely restless small merchants, harboring ambitions of becoming tycoons, embark on different paths of doing business. Inevitably, they encounter different turning points in the destiny of merchants and conclude their lives with different fates of death: one loses the material world of a merchant—physical death; one loses the spiritual world of a merchant—losing direction; one loses the soul world of a merchant—unable to distinguish truth from falsehood. The business paths of these three merchants are strange, changeable, and even absurd, yet the underlying commercial logic is reasonable. Behind the explicit structure of the three merchants' stories, there lies a hidden thread composed of various merchants of all sizes and three women, collectively portraying a commercial world filled with truths and falsehoods, illusions and realities. Ultimately, all merchants' destinies transform into fleeting dreams. This book continues the author's consistent style—sharp, humorous, and wise, with dialogues among characters brimming with profound philosophy. Moreover, "The Merchant's Curse" not only depicts the myriad phenomena of society and human conditions within the business world but also writes about the inevitability of the merchants' illusory world. If "Gray Business" profoundly reflects on the "gray philosophy" of the commercial world that is neither black nor white, then "The Merchant's Curse" reflects on the "illusory philosophy" of the commercial world that is neither true nor false.
