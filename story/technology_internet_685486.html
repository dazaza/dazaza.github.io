**China's First Copyright Infringement Case in the Education Industry:**

**New Thinking Sues New Starting Point, and New Starting Point Loses!**

The Civil Judgment of the Haidian District People's Court of Beijing Municipality

Case No. (2011) Hai Min Chu Zi Di 24619 Hao

The plaintiff, Beijing Haidian New Thinking Training School (hereinafter referred to as "New Thinking School"), filed a lawsuit against the defendant, Future New Starting Point Education Consulting (Beijing) Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as "New Starting Point Company") for copyright infringement and unfair competition. After accepting the case, this court conducted an open trial presided over by Judge Li Dongtao. The entrusted agents of the plaintiff, Xu Jingwu and Chen Jichun, and the entrusted agent of the defendant, Feng Xiaoyu, attended the hearing. This case has now been concluded.

**Plaintiff's Claim:**

The plaintiff stated that it was founded in 1999 and had received multiple honors, including being named a "Model Institution of Online Education by the Ministry of Education." It is a modern, standardized comprehensive private educational institution. In May 2011, to better promote its services, the plaintiff arranged for its online marketing department, computer science teachers, and students to jointly create the homepage of its website (www.xsledu.com). The website took more than two months to complete, involving the efforts of over ten staff members, including planners, programmers, and graphic designers. It included 221 basic images, 221 design drafts, and over 100,000 edited words, with a total design capacity of over 1GB, at a production cost of approximately RMB 240,000.

In September 2011, the plaintiff discovered that the defendant's website homepage (http://bj.xqdedu.net) contained over thirty instances of plagiarism, copying images, text, and design content from the plaintiff's website, seriously infringing on the plaintiff's copyright. The defendant's website also falsely used the plaintiff's website filing registration number. Furthermore, the defendant misrepresented itself as the "Designated Educational Assistance Institution of the Examination Center of the Ministry of Education," which is exclusive to the plaintiff, clearly constituting fraudulent behavior and forming unfair competition against the plaintiff.

Thus, the plaintiff requested the court to order the defendant to:

1. Immediately cease infringement and delete the infringing content on the webpage bj.xqdedu.net;

2. Publicly apologize to the plaintiff in the *Beijing Times* and on the webpage bj.xqdedu.net;

3. Compensate the plaintiff for economic losses of RMB 1 million;

4. Cover the reasonable expenses incurred by the plaintiff in this case;

5. Bear all litigation costs.

**Defendant's Defense:**

The defendant argued that the plaintiff did not provide evidence proving that the pages on its website existed earlier than the notarization date of September 2, 2011. Therefore, even if there were identical contents on both the plaintiff's and defendant's websites, it could not prove that the defendant plagiarized from the plaintiff's website. The website www.xqdedu.net [http://www.xqdedu.net/] was registered under Liu Wenjing's personal credentials, making her the owner and responsible person of the website. Thus, the website in question was unrelated to the defendant. The defendant was neither the owner nor the operator of the website in question, so whether the website infringed or not was irrelevant to the defendant. Additionally, the plaintiff's claim for economic losses lacked a basis for calculation. The defendant requested the court to dismiss all of the plaintiff's claims.

**After Investigation, the Court Found:**

- The domain name of "New Thinking School" is xsledu.com, with the hosting entity being New Thinking School and the filing number being Jing ICP Bei 07029102. The website administrator is Li Dongmei.

- The domain name of "New Starting Point" is xqdedu.net, with the hosting entity and website administrator both being Liu Wenjing, and the filing number being Jing ICP Bei 09044722.

On September 15, 2011, the Beijing Wenzheng Notary Office issued Notarial Certificate No. (2011) Jing Fangzheng Nei Jing Zheng Zi Di 08699 Hao. The certificate showed that the agent of New Thinking School used a notarized computer to access the website bj.xqdedu.net. The website displayed the filing number Jing ICP Bei 07029102 and "Copyright @2010-2011 XQDedu.net." Although the website indeed used New Thinking School's ICP filing number, it did not acknowledge ownership of the website.

During the trial, New Thinking School submitted a CD containing the source code files of its website to prove that the images and content on the disputed website originated from its own website. Upon comparison, it was found that the filing numbers, certificates, images, and design content on the websites of New Thinking School and New Starting Point Company had many similarities. However, New Starting Point Company refused to recognize this due to the inability to confirm that the source code of New Thinking School's website was generated earlier than the notarization date.

On December 2, 2011, Li Dongmei issued a statement confirming that although she was the website administrator of www.xsledu.com [http://www.xsledu.com/], the operator and owner of the website were actually New Thinking School. New Starting Point Company acknowledged that www.xsledu.com [http://www.xsledu.com/] belonged to New Thinking School but questioned the authenticity of Li Dongmei's signature.

New Thinking School submitted invoices for RMB 2,500 in notary fees and RMB 15,000 in legal fees.

The above facts are supported by the filing information, notarial certificates, statements, receipts, and other documents submitted by New Thinking School, as well as the filing information submitted by New Starting Point Company and the court's trial record.

**This Court Believes:**

As the owner of the New Thinking School website, New Thinking School legally enjoys copyright over the website's content. The layout, design style, background color, animations, images, artwork, and text of the website bj.xqdedu.net are similar to those of New Thinking School's website, with much of the content being identical. Although the ICP registration lists Liu Wenjing as the responsible party, the content of the website pertains entirely to the business of New Starting Point Company. In the absence of contrary evidence from the company, it should be considered the actual user of the website. Without permission from the rights holder, the company used the content of New Thinking School's website, including its certificates, constituting copyright infringement and unfair competition. It must bear legal responsibility for the infringement. The company's defense denying the infringement lacks supporting evidence and is therefore not accepted by this court.

New Thinking School demands that New Starting Point Company apologize and compensate for RMB 1 million in economic losses, but the evidence provided is insufficient. This court will determine the manner of responsibility based on factors such as the extent of the infringement and will not fully support the plaintiff's claims.

**Accordingly, in accordance with Article 48(1), Article 49(2) of the Copyright Law of the People's Republic of China, Article 2(1), and Article 14 of the Anti-Unfair Competition Law of the People's Republic of China, the judgment is as follows:**

1. From the date this judgment takes effect, the defendant, Future New Starting Point Education Consulting (Beijing) Co., Ltd., shall cease infringement.

2. Within 30 days of the judgment taking effect, the defendant, Future New Starting Point Education Consulting (Beijing) Co., Ltd., shall publish a statement in the *Beijing Times* to eliminate the influence on the plaintiff, Beijing Haidian New Thinking Training School (the content of the statement will be reviewed by this court; if the defendant fails to comply within the deadline, the main content of the judgment will be publicly disclosed in the *Beijing Times*, with the costs borne by the defendant).

3. Within 10 days of the judgment taking effect, the defendant, Future New Starting Point Education Consulting (Beijing) Co., Ltd., shall compensate the plaintiff, Beijing Haidian New Thinking Training School, for economic losses and reasonable expenses totaling RMB 150,000.

4. The other claims of the plaintiff, Beijing Haidian New Thinking Training School, are dismissed.

If the defendant, Future New Starting Point Education Consulting (Beijing) Co., Ltd., fails to fulfill the monetary obligations specified in this judgment within the designated period, it shall pay double interest on the delayed performance debt in accordance with Article 229 of the Civil Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China.

The case acceptance fee of RMB 6,900 (prepaid by the plaintiff) shall be borne by the defendant, Future New Starting Point Education Consulting (Beijing) Co., Ltd., to be paid within seven days after the judgment takes effect.

If either party disagrees with this judgment, they may file an appeal to this court within 15 days of receiving the judgment document, submitting copies according to the number of opposing parties, paying the appeal case acceptance fee, and appealing to the Intermediate People's Court of Beijing Municipality. If the appeal fee is not paid within seven days after the appeal period expires, the appeal will be automatically withdrawn.
