Yixiang's store promotes a new sales promotion: Kiss in public on Valentine's Day for a discount

by yixan2012 on 2012-02-23 16:23:03

By Shi Yin of the Early Times, interns Zhao Wenxin and Cai Liu - Every February 14th on Valentine's Day, it is always a peak season for businesses to "suck gold." However, under the current circumstances where physical stores and e-commerce often offer discounts and promotions under various pretexts, Valentine's Day has also become a new excuse for discounts and promotions. Yesterday, multiple catering shops claimed that they would offer discounts during the "Valentine's Day" period, and to enjoy these discounts, diners would need to kiss to prove their affection.

The staff can also cooperate, which some find absurd

Yesterday afternoon, a reporter from the Early Times visited a roasted fish restaurant located on Jinling East Road. Previously, this roasted fish restaurant announced that to celebrate the opening of its branch store on Nanjing West Road, the new store would offer discount promotions from February 12th to 14th. Different from the usual discount methods of the restaurant, "as long as you hold hands with anyone in the store, you get an 80% discount, a hug gets you a 70% discount, and a kiss gets you a 53% discount."

Ms. Li, the manager of the roasted fish restaurant, told the Early Times reporter that customers regardless of gender or intimacy could participate in this activity. Couples, friends, young and old, even strangers could all obtain discounts by holding hands, hugging, or kissing. This manager even stated: "If necessary, the waiters in the store can also cooperate with the customers to complete hand-holding or hugging."

However, some customers in the store expressed that such unconventional promotional tactics were "a bit absurd."

Ms. Li said that the reason for organizing such a promotional activity was mainly to liven up the atmosphere through interaction. However, the rule of "strangers kissing to get discounts" seemed to have the possibility of customers forcibly requiring others to hold hands, hug, or even kiss. In response to this, Ms. Li replied that unreasonable customers would not be considered for discounts. Ms. Li admitted frankly, "The newly opened store does not consider profitability, but mainly aims for publicity purposes."

Kissing for 60 seconds exempts the entire bill at the coffee shop

Compared to this roasted fish restaurant, a coffee shop located on Yu Garden Road had put forward even clearer promotional slogans. A poster had already been posted on the inner wall of the coffee shop, stating that on the evening of February 14th, any couple who kissed for a full 60 seconds inside the coffee shop would be able to freely enjoy any food in the store within two hours.

The staff of the coffee shop told the Early Times reporter that all three branches of this coffee shop would participate in this activity. The staff emphasized that only couples could participate.

According to the rules set by the coffee shop learned from the server, as long as the couple kisses for a full 60 seconds, they can freely consume all the food in the store for two hours. The server mentioned that there were currently no plans to limit the number of participants, "We haven't thought too much about it, after all, we only organize this event once a year, and the event lasts only two hours, so it shouldn't result in significant losses."

The competitiveness of "kissing promotion" is limited

In fact, using "kissing for discounts" as a promotional tactic is not uncommon. At the end of December last year, a real estate development in Nanjing even promoted with the slogan "kiss to reduce by ten thousand yuan," meaning that if buyers kissed on the spot at the sales office, they could deduct 10,000 yuan when purchasing a house.

However, all promotional tactics ultimately come down to balancing price and quality. Local media reported that the "kissing price reduction" real estate development in Nanjing eventually couldn't compete with a nearby more affordable real estate development.
