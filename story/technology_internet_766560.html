Big names in the tech circle congratulate Sogou on its acquisition by Tencent via Weibo, Zhou Hongyi sends text message to share his feelings

by anonymous on 2013-09-18 21:57:49

After going through rumors with 360 and Baidu, Sogou today announced the introduction of Tencent as a strategic investor and officially formed an alliance with Tencent. This has sparked heated discussions among tech industry leaders on Weibo. Especially, Zhou Hongyi, chairman of Qihoo, did not express his feelings on Weibo in the first instance but shared his thoughts via a text message.

Zhou Hongyi, chairman of Qihoo, talked about the following points in a text message sent to Tencent Technology:

1. Congratulating Charles Zhang and Sogou, this price is still quite good;

2. Hopes that Old Zhang takes this opportunity to develop Sohu better;

3. Tencent's entry into the search war is a good thing for the entire search market. Currently, the search market is in a monopolistic pattern, and netizens' rights have not been fully respected. We are willing to provide better search services to compete with Tencent and Baidu, the two giants, in this market;

4. The competition in the search market will intensify, but netizens will ultimately benefit. Those search promotion behaviors that harm the interests of netizens will be eliminated in the competition.

Broadcast to Tencent Weibo

Li Kaifu, who is currently undergoing cancer treatment, congratulated on Weibo: "Congratulations to Sogou, congratulations to Xiao Chuan!"

Liu Chun, vice president of Sohu, forwarded Li Kaifu's Weibo post and revealed details: "This morning, I called Xiao Chuan, he sounded so happy, yeah!"

Keso, a veteran internet observer, said: "360 and Sogou had a marathon romance, but in the end, Sogou and Tencent got married."

Xie Wen, another seasoned internet observer, analyzed the intentions behind this alliance on Weibo. "Defensive moves: 1) Dispose of unsuccessful search assets; 2) Support Sogou to suppress 360. No impact on the industry landscape, it belongs to non-innovative industrial adjustment." Xie Wen wrote on Weibo.

Hu Yanping, general manager of DCCI, analyzed on Weibo: "Who will win between Baidu, 360, and Sogou in the future? Answering media questions: 1. Who can become the first natural interface for users, voice recognition being just one aspect; 2. Who can build a need-based computing system centered around an open location data platform; 3. Who can become the most widely distributed and connected entity on the internet; 4. Which company owns the largest data cloud on the internet... The last point is who can do search well, search is the result and no longer the cause, non-search determines search, and search triumphs non-search."

Yu Yang, president of Analysys International, expressed on Weibo: "The hint I gave at the Internet Conference came true, very similar to the capital operation of 3721 ten years ago, both cases saw a third party intervene."

Fu Sheng, CEO of Kingsoft Network, posted on Weibo: "Congratulating Xiao Chuan! It seems like the number input method is ready to go."

Fang Xingdong, founder of Blogchina, discussed on Weibo: "Tencent's investment in Sogou is a brilliant move, activating multiple levels. First, the Chinese search market has truly moved away from monopoly and returned to a competitive state. Second, the competition among industry giants has become more diversified and balanced. Third, Tencent has once again become one of the main players in the search market, filling an important gap. This move is a heavy blow to Baidu and an incentive for 360, making China's internet more exciting overall!"

It is worth noting that this morning, Tencent's early trading session rose by 1.51%, reaching HKD 417.60, equivalent to USD 100.12 billion according to the current exchange rate, becoming China's first internet company with a market value exceeding USD 100 billion. In the afternoon, Tencent announced its strategic investment in Sogou and formed an alliance with them.

After Tencent and Sogou formed an alliance, it significantly impacted 360 and Sohu. As of 6:35 AM US Eastern Time (18:35 Beijing Time) on September 16, Qihoo 360's stock price fell by USD 9.86 in pre-market trading, reaching USD 78 per share, with a decline of 11.22%. Sohu's stock price increased by USD 6.47, reaching USD 71.22 per share, with a rise of 10%.
