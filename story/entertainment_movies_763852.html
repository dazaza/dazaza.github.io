Synopsis:

Main Cast: Kwang Soo Lee / Lee Bo-young / Lee Beom-soo / Choi Song-hyun / Nam Gyu-ri / Jung Joon-ho

"This person must be placed by someone's side..."

Radio producer Kai (Kwang Soo Lee), who was abandoned by his parents as a child, and lyricist Lorraine (Lee Bo-young), who lost both her parents in a car accident, filled the voids in each other's lives like family, friends, and lovers. Although their lives had regrets, they were simple and happy. However, an unexpected illness left Kai with only 200 days to live. If he were to leave this world, Lorraine would truly be alone. Within the limited time, he wanted to find a man who could take care of Lorraine for the rest of her life.

Joo-Hwan (Lee Beom-soo) appears.

A dentist, wise and reasonable, with a naturally gentle temperament, in Kai's eyes, he is a man more capable than anyone else of giving Lorraine happiness for the rest of her life. And even more coincidentally, on a snowy day, Joo-Hwan fell in love at first sight with Lorraine and became infatuated. Kai decided to choose Joo-Hwan as the ideal candidate for his plan.

"If you knew my painstaking intentions beforehand... In the end, isn't your love with him blossoming smoothly?"

Kai deliberately created opportunities for Lorraine and Joo-Hwan to meet, and their feelings deepened within the boundaries he controlled. Despite the inevitable heartache, Kai personally selected Lorraine's wedding dress and led her step by step to Joo-Hwan in the church.

Even though love becomes a sorrowful matter, though very sad, because of the happy things, their story that is sadder than sadness begins.

Behind-the-scenes Production:

This film is a literary romance aimed at young audiences, directed by renowned young Korean poet Won Tae-yeon, with producer Kim Gwang-soo from Mnet Media, known for successfully launching "Blood Midterm Exam" in 2008. Won Tae-yeon published his first poetry collection in 1992 and gained popularity among the younger generation with his profound thoughts and delicate emotions. He has continued to release best-selling poems. Starting in 2008, he participated in the lyrics creation for the vocal group Paran and the scriptwriting for the TV series "Cain and Abel," entering the realm of popular culture.

The Popular Poet Turns Director

Director Won Tae-yeon is a famous young Korean poet who previously contributed to the scriptwriting for the TV series "Cain and Abel" and the lyrics for music songs. This is his first time directing a movie, and it's natural that the screenplay is entirely his own work. The signing event for the novel with the same name, attended by the main creators including Won Tae-yeon, Kwang Soo Lee, and Lee Bo-young, received an exceptionally enthusiastic response. Originally expecting 200 attendees, nearly 5000 fans showed up, prompting the publisher Kyobo Library to request police assistance to maintain order. It's expected that when the novel officially releases on the day of the movie premiere on March 14th, it will attract even more readers.

The identity of a poet entering the directorial field is already quite topical, plus the films from Chungmuro are renowned for their "beautiful romance." Won Tae-yeon's presentation of "sadness" distinct from past classics is highly anticipated.

The film started shooting in mid-December 2008, completing 70% of the filming in less than a month, which is astonishing. According to reporters, Won Tae-yeon on set exhibited a mature calmness unlike that of a newcomer director. Perhaps due to being a poet and having written the screenplay himself, he can touch the nuances of human emotions and convey his feelings to the actors through exquisite language, giving him an unparalleled advantage over other directors.

Additionally, like many artists willing to bear responsibility for the future of Chungmuro, the film adopts a system where the lead actors' salaries are used for investment before profits are shared based on box office returns. However, even before filming began, the film attracted many distribution companies bidding. The production company estimates that as long as the box office exceeds 500,000 viewers, the investment cost can be recovered, and given the current situation, achieving this goal should not be difficult. Since Kwang Soo Lee and Lee Beom-soo have considerable fame in Japan, the Japanese side has offered about $3 million to purchase the overseas distribution rights for the film.

Kwang Soo Lee: A Happier-than-Happy Story

Despite criticism for his lightning-fast marriage to Sun Tae-young, as time passed, the initial doubts slowly turned into blessings. With the birth of his son in February 2009, Kwang Soo Lee is overjoyed. Thus, the real-life Kwang Soo Lee is completely opposite to his character in the film. The newly revealed poster shows Kwang Soo Lee lying amidst yellow chrysanthemums with tears in his eyes, seemingly returning to the "Prince of Tears" persona from Korean dramas like "Stairway to Heaven" — revealing a strongly infectious sense of sadness. Another image of him and Lee Bo-young missing each other's gazes in the snow suggests that viewers should bring tissues to the theater. The promotional slogans personally created by Won Tae-yeon, such as "Did you know back then? Love isn't touched by hands but by hearts," "If love needs words, how do the mute fall in love?" add poetic beauty and subtlety.

When asked if his real-life happiness would prevent him from immersing himself in the sad emotions of his character, Kwang Soo Lee openly admitted he is satisfied with his new marital status: "On the contrary, because I have a harbor to dock in my heart, acting in sad stories has become more effortless — since I want to cherish the happiness I now possess, imagining the extreme pain of losing it makes it all the more poignant." During the final scene shot in early January 2009 at a church in Seoul, some media were invited to observe. That day's scene depicted Kwang Soo Lee playing Kai, whose life was counting down, personally sending the woman he loved, Lee Bo-young, into the church to marry Lee Beom-soo. What should have been a joyous wedding turned into heartbreak for all three. One close-up showed Kwang Soo Lee walking and weeping on the street after turning around, leaving the journalists deeply impressed by his realistic acting. Interestingly, "remarried" Kwang Soo Lee's steps and expressions while leading Lee Bo-young into the church were practiced and confident, whereas the female lead, due to nervousness, frequently messed up her lines, prompting admiration for Kwang Soo Lee's genuine status as a bridegroom.

Jung Joon-ho: The Truth Behind the Cameo Appearance

Mnet Media, originating from a music company, achieved good box office results with its low-budget "Blood Midterm Exam" in 2008. "More Than Blue" is its trial stone for fully entering the film industry. Producer Kim Gwang-soo had a pleasant collaboration with Jung Joon-ho, plus a more convincing reason: Jung Joon-ho is a fervent reader of Won Tae-yeon's works, "I diligently read his works during my middle school years and even quoted his poems in love letters to my girlfriend." Therefore, upon learning that he would direct a film, he particularly wished to collaborate. Upon receiving Kim Gwang-soo's invitation, he readily agreed without hesitation.

Lee Bo-young: The Movie Queen Supported by Male Stars

From "My Brother" to "Vile Street", "Game Queen", to recent works like "I Am Happy" and this "More Than Blue", Lee Bo-young's status in Chungmuro gradually rose through collaborations with top male stars such as Won Bin, Jo In-sung, Ju Jin-mo, Hyun Bin, etc. This time, being able to star in Won Tae-yeon's work, acclaimed as "the most beautiful depiction of love and parting unmatched by others," Lee Bo-young did not hide her excitement: "If I can receive comments from the audience like 'Oh, so Lee Bo-young can also be like this,' I would be content." Despite the tragic theme, she revealed that just thinking about being able to star made her smile happily at home before filming. She had high hopes for this film.

Theme Song Takes the Lead, Kwang Soo Lee Participates in Editing

The first theme song of the movie, "More Than Blue," sung by Kim Bum-soo, was publicly released at the end of 2008. The MV was directed by the talented director Cha Eun-tak. The MV lasted 7 minutes and was separately filmed by Kwang Soo Lee, Lee Bo-young, and Lee Beom-soo — not taken from original scenes. To achieve perfection, Kwang Soo Lee worked until midnight in the editing room with Cha Eun-tak, participating in the editing of the MV for the first time. The second theme song "There Has Never Been Such a Person in My Life" was exposed in early February 2009 and featured the renowned ballad singer Lee Seung-chul. Unlike the first wave of theme songs, this music video re-edited and included the deleted scenes from the movie, satisfying the audience's curiosity about the entire filming process.

Kwang Soo Lee plays Key, a radio program producer who was abandoned by his parents as a child. Lee Bo-young plays Cream, a program writer who lost both her parents in a traffic accident. Key and Cream, sharing similar fates, deeply rely on each other. Key is seriously ill and has little time left. He hides this news and silently stays by Cream's side. Lee Beom-soo plays Joo-Hwan, a warm and charming doctor who is Key's attending physician and falls in love with Cream at first sight. Key also believes that Joo-Hwan is the person who can protect Cream for life, so he endures the heartache and actively creates opportunities for Joo-Hwan and Cream to be together, eventually sending them to the wedding altar.

[Poet Won Tae-yeon's Debut Work]

"More Than Blue" is poet Won Tae-yeon's debut work as a director, attracting attention from the start of filming. The eponymous novel written by Won Tae-yeon will also be released on Valentine's Day.

[Main Cast Reinvests Salaries, Expected Profit]

Kwang Soo Lee, Lee Bo-young, and Lee Beom-soo reinvested their salaries into the film's production out of a desire to save the Korean film industry and trust in the production company and script. The warmth and sensibility of the romance drama, the popularity of the lead actors, and the best production team will ensure the box office success of this movie. Excluding marketing costs, the salaries of the main cast were reinvested as production costs, significantly reducing overall production expenses compared to other similar films. Even before filming began, many distribution companies bid for the rights. As long as the movie attracts over 500,000 viewers, it will make a profit, and given the lineup, surpassing 500,000 viewers should be easy. In the overseas market, just with the participation of Kwang Soo Lee and Lee Beom-soo, Japan has offered $3 million (approximately 4.2 billion won) to purchase the film's copyright.

[Chung Joon-ho Special Cameo]

Renowned Korean actor Chung Joon-ho will make a special appearance in the movie "More Than Blue". Chung Joon-ho specially appeared in this new film due to his previous collaboration with the film's producer Kim Gwang-soo from Mnet Midia. Chung Joon-ho expressed that he had carefully read poet Won Tae-yeon's works. During his past relationships, he even copied his poems to write love letters. After learning that Won Tae-yeon would transform into a director and shoot his debut film, he particularly wanted to meet him once. Therefore, upon receiving the invitation to act, he immediately agreed.

[Choi Song-hyun Drops Out Midway, Jeong Ae-yeon Replaces Her]

KBS TV announcer Choi Song-hyun decided to terminate the filming of "More Than Blue" midway due to scheduling issues. Choi Song-hyun simultaneously accepted roles in two movies, "Insadong Scandal" and "More Than Blue," after resigning from her TV announcing job and transitioning to become a freelance actress. However, her agency stated that as a newcomer, Choi Song-hyun should focus all her efforts on one work. After careful consideration, Choi Song-hyun decided that concentrating on one movie would be respectful to both film crews, thus ultimately deciding to withdraw from the filming of "More Than Blue." Another reason was the significant difference in the characters' images in the two films, requiring different hairstyles. In "Insadong Scandal," she played a member of a fraud ring, needing to display a strong personality by dyeing her long hair yellow. In "More Than Blue," she was cast as an intellectual photographer with short black hair. Previously, Choi Song-hyun had proposed wearing a wig, but the crew advised her to cut her long hair. To maintain her image in the earlier-started "Insadong Scandal," she had to decline the crew's suggestion and ultimately withdrew from "More Than Blue." The role of Jenna originally planned for Choi Song-hyun was replaced by model-turned-actress Jeong Ae-yeon, showcasing the polished image of urban women. Recently gaining recognition through various advertisements, TV dramas, and hosting activities, Jeong Ae-yeon expressed her honor to co-star with excellent seniors like Kwang Soo Lee and Lee Beom-soo, vowing to live up to everyone's expectations.

[Mournful Poster Draws Attention]

The personally photographed pictures of Kwang Soo Lee and Lee Bo-young underwent softening, compositing, and other special effects processing, resulting in a feeling akin to art pieces. To directly express the sadness of the movie, both actors' eyes conveyed a sense of sorrow, especially the tear-filled gaze of Kwang Soo Lee, capturing the audience's hearts. The text printed on the poster reads: "Do you know? Love isn't something you touch with your hands but with your eyes." "If love needs language, how do the mute still fall in love?" These are selected poems from Director Won Tae-yeon's previous works, adding a touch of sentimentality. Some netizens suspected that Kwang Soo Lee's eyes underwent post-production effects, but the film production company stated that Kwang Soo Lee acted out the emotionally charged sad scenes, and there was absolutely no modification to his eyes.

[7-Minute Special MTV, Kwang Soo Lee Personally Participated in Editing]

The theme song MTV was publicly released on December 27, 2008. Viewers can watch it through the official homepages of the main actors Kwang Soo Lee, Lee Bo-young, and Lee Beom-soo. Different from the usual method of first releasing through television music channels or online music websites, it drew the audience's attention. On Kwang Soo Lee's fan club website, the MV garnered 170,000 clicks. The clips in this MV were specially filmed and not extracted from the movie. The movie's namesake theme song "More Than Blue," sung by Kim Bum-soo, produced a roughly 7-minute MV. Directed by the talented MV director Cha Eun-tak, the MV presented beautiful visuals and a touching love story. To correct unsatisfactory scenes in the MV shooting, Kwang Soo Lee specifically worked overtime in the editing room with MV director Cha Eun-tak at 2 a.m., re-editing scenes of Kwang Soo Lee and Lee Bo-young's farewell and Lee Beom-soo and Lee Bo-young's wedding.

[Ultra-High-Speed Shooting Progress]

The movie "More Than Blue" became a topic in the film industry due to its ultra-high-speed shooting progress. Since starting filming in mid-December 2008, it completed 70% of the film in less than a month. Director Won Tae-yeon's directing style displayed a maturity unlike that of a newcomer, and the cinematographer and lighting director fully cooperated, making the movie's progress very smooth. As a poet, Won Tae-yeon has a deep understanding and grasp of subtle human emotions, combined with his personally written script, providing a deeper understanding of the characters, allowing him to effectively guide the actors and specify the required performances. This is the main reason why the movie was quickly completed under Director Won Tae-yeon's leadership.

About the Film:

Director Won Tae-yeon, born as a poet, showcases solid screenwriting and meticulous directing skills.

In the early 1990s, Won Tae-yeon, in his twenties, published sensitive poetry collections such as "Do You Occasionally Think of Me When Walking, While I Occasionally Think of Other Things When Walking," earning him the title of "Love Language Expert." This time, he will stimulate our senses by replacing words with images. With his sharp insight into the inner depths of humanity, Won Tae-yeon does not miss any subtle expressions or actions of the actors, presenting a unique love story through meticulous direction.

The Most Outstanding Love Story Actors Deliver Emotionally Charged Performances

Korean's most touching love story actor Kwang Soo Lee, and Lee Beom-soo, who firmly occupies the leading role through love dramas, along with Lee Bo-young, who has received praise in numerous love dramas. The film "More Than Blue" gathers Korea's most outstanding love story actors. They will deliver emotionally charged performances and "melancholic tearful performances."

The Best Team from "Good, the Bad and the Weird" Brings Sensual Images and Delicate Scenes

To create sensual and aesthetically pleasing visuals, this team gathers the best talents in Korea today. "Good, the Bad and the Weird" won the Best Cinematography Award at the 2008 Blue Dragon Film Awards, "Rose Flower, Red Lotus" won the Best Lighting Award in 2003, and "Le Grand Chef" won various film festival art awards. All these award-winning elites have come together in "More Than Blue" to present the most sensual and delicate visual imagery.

After "Our Happy Time," Another Heartrending Love Story

Three years after the release of "Our Happy Time," Korea's orthodox love films return to the audience. "More Than Blue" will premiere during the White Day period in March. The sad story of a man who cannot keep his love and entrusts his beloved to another man allows the audience to see the long-awaited orthodox Korean love story, and this film also declares the glorious return of Korea's orthodox love films.
