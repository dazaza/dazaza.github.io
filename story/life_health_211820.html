The best time for psoriasis treatment

by mengmengyu on 2009-07-21 10:02:23

From the perspective of the onset season, summer is the best time to treat psoriasis.

The onset or recurrence of psoriasis is closely related to the seasons. The general pattern is that it tends to flare up in winter and improve during the summer and autumn seasons. The treatment effectiveness of psoriasis also has a significant relationship with the seasons.

Generally, for psoriasis patients, after the climate warms up in summer, the treatment of skin lesions shows quick results, shorter courses of treatment, and the best effects. For patients with mild psoriasis, the skin lesions may even spontaneously resolve in summer. Therefore, treating psoriasis is best done during the summer when the "susceptibility genes" are relatively less active, allowing for prompt and effective treatment. Before the peak psoriasis season of winter and spring arrives, effectively controlling the condition and enhancing the body's immunity can reduce the chances of recurrence and worsening, which helps in the early recovery of psoriasis patients.

From the timing of onset, the initial stage of the disease is the best period to treat psoriasis.

Psoriasis at its onset is relatively easier to treat. If appropriate treatment methods are used, many patients can remain free of recurrence for a long time. Generally, the treatment course for newly diagnosed patients is shorter, and the condition can be better controlled. On the other hand, for patients who have had the disease for a longer time or experienced multiple recurrences, the resolution of skin lesions will be slower, and the difficulty of treatment will be greater.

Many psoriasis patients, when they first develop the condition, choose inappropriate treatment methods, failing to effectively control the activity of the "susceptibility genes." They only treat from one aspect, such as internal environment or immune capacity, leading to abnormal expression of susceptibility genes that cannot be normally interrupted, thus leaving potential problems. Additionally, some psoriasis patients develop drug resistance due to improper medication use, adding difficulty to the treatment process. Misusing topical steroid-containing drugs or immunosuppressants not only fails to effectively control the condition but can also make psoriasis more severe. Therefore, for the control of psoriasis, choosing targeted treatment against "susceptibility genes" at the onset of the disease can yield very significant results, achieving the goal of complete cure.

From the population affected, middle-aged and young adults are the optimal group for treating psoriasis. Compared to other populations, middle-aged and young adult psoriasis patients have a high cure rate, mainly because this group has certain advantages.
