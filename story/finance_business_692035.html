Long-distance bus driver is caught cracking sunflower seeds with speed over 100 (Photo)

by zuowenhl3 on 2012-03-05 21:23:41

The driver was extremely "calm" while driving over 100 km/h and still managed to crack sunflower seeds.

Recently, a shocking scene occurred on a bus carrying more than 40 passengers traveling from Huaiji to the Dongguan main station: the driver was extremely "calm," driving at over 100 km/h and still cracking sunflower seeds.

Dongguan Daily (report by Lü Zhan with photography) - On a passenger-filled large bus traveling from Huaiji to the Dongguan main station, the driver performed a "stunt" on the highway. Instead of tightly gripping the steering wheel, both hands were occupied with cracking sunflower seeds, leaving a trail of shells around the driver's seat. This scene was captured by a passenger using a mobile phone video.

Video Replay:

Bus Driver Cracks Sunflower Seeds Leisurely

In the video, it can be seen that the bus is rapidly moving along the highway. The driver sits leisurely in the driver's seat, cracking sunflower seeds: his left hand holds the steering wheel while simultaneously grabbing a handful of seeds, and his right hand transfers them one by one into his mouth.

The lonely gearshift lies quietly nearby, untouched by the driver. Shockingly, the left hand, which should have been holding the steering wheel, occasionally releases its grip to make way for the seeds. At times, the steering wheel is left uncontrolled.

Passenger Ming provided this video clip and informed that this trip departed from Huaiji at 11:30 AM on November 26th, with the final destination being the Dongguan Bus Terminal. After entering the highway, the driver began cracking sunflower seeds, continuing until reaching Sanshui, where he stopped due to the presence of traffic police at the toll booth.

Dangerous Journey:

Speed Reached Up to 110 km/h?

Ming stated that despite cracking sunflower seeds, the vehicle speed was not slow. Sitting directly behind the driver, Ming could clearly see the speedometer, which reached up to 110 km/h. The bus had a capacity of 49 people, and Ming said that the bus was almost fully occupied at the time.

According to the license plate number provided by Ming, investigations confirmed that the bus belonged to the No. 807 fleet of Dongguan Automobile Transportation Co., Ltd. A manager surnamed Li from the No. 807 fleet inspection team stated that according to company regulations, buses are generally not allowed to exceed 100 km/h. The exact speed at the time would need to be verified through the onboard speed detection device.

Supervisory Loopholes:

No Cameras Installed on In-province Passenger Buses

Upon hearing about the bus driver cracking sunflower seeds while driving, the management personnel of the No. 807 fleet inspection team also expressed surprise. He said, "Such a leisurely behavior as cracking sunflower seeds is something I've never heard of before."

The management personnel stated that the fleet director is currently unavailable, but with the video and license plate number, the matter can be handled easily. Drivers who disregard passenger safety will certainly be dealt with seriously.

Mr. Li mentioned, "Currently, inter-provincial buses in Dongguan have all been equipped with cameras, but for intra-provincial passenger vehicles, there are no such regulations yet within the company, so none of them have cameras installed." He admitted that the lack of cameras on the buses indeed creates a management loophole. (Editor: SN029)
