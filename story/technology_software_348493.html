Exclusive interview with UC's Yu Yongfu: Break-even has been achieved, future acquisitions not ruled out

by tfseo8888 on 2010-04-19 20:36:06

Yongfu Yu 　　Sina Technology News, March 30th afternoon message, mobile Internet company UC Browser (YouShi Technology) today announced that it has received investment from Nokia Growth Fund. In an exclusive interview with Sina Technology, YouShi Technology CEO Yongfu Yu revealed that UC Browser had achieved break-even at the beginning of 2009 and indicated that they would not rule out expanding development scale through acquisitions. Exclusive Interview with Yongfu Yu of UC: Break-even Achieved, Future Acquisitions Not Ruled Out Media Source: Sina Technology

Yongfu Yu stated that there is no secret to Internet profit-making methods, which basically include charging enterprises, charging users, and collecting fees from e-commerce transactions. These three methods will all be attempted by UC Browser in the future. Yongfu Yu disclosed that UC Browser made attempts in value-added services at the beginning of 2009 and had already achieved financial break-even.

After completing the fourth round of financing, Yongfu Yu mentioned that the immediate task at hand would be to expand recruitment of product research and development personnel. Given his experience in venture capital work, he has more mature thoughts on investment and acquisition. He believes that investment and acquisition will certainly be a method used in UC's growth process, and future possibilities for investment and acquisition cannot be ruled out.

Regarding whether to consider going public after four rounds of financing, Yongfu Yu stated that going public is merely a process, and it can bring value to the brand and incentivize employees. Only under the influence of these two roles will UC consider going public, but currently, there is no detailed timetable.

Yongfu Yu emphasized that despite undergoing four rounds of financing, UC Browser remains independently developed, and the entrepreneurial team still holds absolute controlling equity. He also revealed that the company would continue to adopt a system where all employees hold shares until the number of employees reaches 800 people. "We already have 500 employees, meaning there are still 300 spots left. As long as an employee has been regularized for over a year, they will receive stock options."

Below is the full transcript of the exclusive interview:

Sina Technology: Congratulations, Yongfu. YouShi Technology has completed another round of financing. First, could you share with our netizens the basic situation of this round of financing?

Yongfu Yu: We've been communicating about this round of financing for some time. Since 2007, UC hasn't done any pure financial or enterprise-only financing, mainly considering what kind of help we need in the Internet industry to facilitate UC's rapid growth. For example, last year we accepted strategic investment from Alibaba, and this year we've also accepted investment from Nokia Growth Partners. Introducing investments into UC's growth process involves thinking about various aspects of the industrial chain. After some communication, we ultimately reached an agreement with Nokia Growth Partners.

This event itself marks an important step towards achieving UC's significant dream. This dream concerns how UC will develop rapidly over the next decade in the mobile Internet industry. Starting today, one very important thing we will do is to further enhance UC's accumulation in product technology over the past five years. Currently, 70% of our staff are product R&D personnel. We plan to recruit 500-1000 software engineering technicians to expand UC's innovation and accumulation in technology.

Sina Technology: We noticed that very few Internet companies receive investment from telecommunications companies. Could you share with us the scale of this investment?

Yongfu Yu: I'm not at liberty to disclose the exact amount of the investment, but I can tell you that UC and Nokia's deal is the most expensive transaction in the mobile Internet industry thus far. UC's valuation is several hundred million US dollars.

Sina Technology: What resources can be utilized after receiving Nokia's investment?

Yongfu Yu: I believe that for any two companies to form a long-term cooperation in business, it must be a win-win situation, whether it's Nokia or any other company. We must consider what we can offer to our partners and what support our partners can provide us. It must be a win-win situation. Looking at the development of China's mobile phone terminals, mobile phones will no longer be simple communication devices; in the future, mobile phones will definitely be Internet phones. So, over the past two years, UC has cooperated with more than 30 mobile phone brands. Combining mobile phones with UC's browser equals an Internet phone, which is a division and cooperation of the entire hardware and software industrial chain. Therefore, I believe that such cooperation will bring mutual support in each other's businesses, creating a win-win situation, unaffected by other factors.

Sina Technology: I recall there was a debate about mobile Internet at the IT Summit in Shenzhen a few days ago. You've been involved in the mobile Internet sector for quite some time. How do you view this industry?

Yongfu Yu: On this issue, I have two thoughts: First, over the past 30 years, the IT industry has experienced a huge innovation and upgrade every ten years, and during each decade, many excellent companies and leading-level companies have emerged. We can summarize the IT industry as having gone through three dynasties. Before the 1990s, IBM created this dynasty, and the keyword for this dynasty was hardware. Before the 1990s, computers were not a specialized field and were not prepared for individual users. The second dynasty was in the 1990s, the golden decade of personal computers, with Microsoft as the king. The keyword for this decade was software, so software surpassed hardware. In the first decade of the 21st century, the past decade, it was the third dynasty, with the keyword being the Internet. The Internet surpassed software because the original profit model was selling software on CDs to users. However, after the birth of the Internet, no one considered packaging their software on CDs to sell to users anymore. Instead, software could be provided for free use, and profit models could be added during the process of using the software or services.

Looking at the three dynasties, China seized two opportunities and gave birth to two dynasties. In the 1990s, during the golden decade of personal computers, Lenovo seized the opportunity and became China's dynasty. In the past decade, Tencent seized such an opportunity in China. Tencent is a software company, but it is also a massive profit machine.

Today, standing at an important point in 2010, we will jointly experience and witness the fourth dynasty over the next decade. The keyword for this dynasty is wireless. From the trend of the industry, the wireless Internet or mobile Internet will undoubtedly be the golden development decade in the future. This is the natural law of the industry and will not be affected.

The second point is about the development of the Internet over more than a decade. We have seen the current development environment of the entire Internet industry. Some things are developing in a positive direction, while others are not conducive to the development of the industry. But let's think about it: the Internet has two very important internal beliefs, which we call faith. The two keywords of this faith are innovation and openness. Factors that affect these or suppress these two things are just like weather conditions in the overall industry environment. Sometimes it's sunny, and sometimes it's stormy, but they won't affect the overall progress of the industry.

Looking at the Internet industry over more than a decade, so many excellent companies and leading companies have emerged. Every company has made a huge breakthrough in innovation to achieve its current success. This tells us that if UC wants to develop, it must continuously excel in innovation.

Sina Technology: What is the current profit situation of UC Browser? What specific aspects will this round of financing be used for?

Yongfu Yu: Your first question is how to make money, and the second is how to spend it.

Actually, making profits on the Internet is the least formulaic. First, charge enterprises; second, charge users, which in China is value-added services; the third is to make profits through e-commerce transactions.

Looking at the entire development process of the Internet over more than a decade, in terms of business models, generally speaking, once an industry reaches a certain stage of commercial maturity, these Internet companies will adopt common or similar profit models. These companies are also considering how to make profits besides portal advertising, how to make profits in value-added services, and how to make profits in e-commerce.

From UC's own perspective, we will try all three income models in the future, but the most important point here is timing, and we must grasp the progress of Internet commerce well. At this point in time, from the user's perspective, it is a stage of rapid development, as the user base grows by 100 million each year. However, from the perspective of commercialization, the mobile Internet is still in its early stages. We made some preliminary attempts in 2009, mainly in the first two months involving value-added services. I cannot specifically share how much money UC has earned, but I can share one thing: since January 2009, UC has been financially balanced and has achieved break-even. After obtaining a new round of investment, the immediate task is to expand the recruitment of R&D and product personnel. In Internet companies, human resources are always the most important asset for an Internet company.

Sina Technology: Are there any considerations for acquisitions?

Yongfu Yu: We should regard investment and acquisition as tools, means, and ways of growth. This is similar to recruiting like-minded partners to the company. Actually, Internet companies have only gradually started to try using this method in recent years. In foreign Internet industries, we see that mergers, acquisitions, and investments by major companies have become routine development methods. Perhaps due to my past experiences, I am more mature in terms of investment. Therefore, investment and acquisition will certainly be methods adopted in UC's own growth process.

Sina Technology: I see that some companies start planning for IPOs after three or four rounds of financing. Have you started considering this aspect?

Yongfu Yu: Actually, this topic is a very good question. First, we need to return to the essence. Today, we are entrepreneurs, and as entrepreneurs, we need to think about why we want to go public. Going public is absolutely a process, not an endpoint. Looking at any company after it goes public, the founding team values their shares the most. We provide our investment shareholders with a means to liquidate and realize their investments, and we give internal employees stock option incentives, bringing market mechanisms. But ultimately, going public is a process, and the two most important values of this process are: one is the value for the brand; you transform from a private company into a public company, and people perceive a significant increase in your standardization and transparency. Most importantly, it's for the incentive mechanism of employee stock options, allowing them to operate effectively.

In the past few years, UC has consistently adopted a system where all employees hold shares. Every colleague who joins UC, we call them classmates, will receive UC stock options as long as they have been regularized for a year. At the beginning of this year, we announced a matter: before reaching 800 people, we will continue to adopt a system where all employees hold shares. We have now reached 500 people, meaning there are still 300 spots left. As long as an employee has been regularized for a year, they will receive stock options. I believe these two roles are the reasons we consider going public. Specifically, the timing of going public depends on each company's own business and circumstances, as well as the maturity of the industry itself. I currently do not have a specific timeline.

Sina Technology: What is the current status of the management team's shares?

Yongfu Yu: Currently, UC is still an independently developing company, and this company is still absolutely controlled by our founding team. (Cui Xi)
