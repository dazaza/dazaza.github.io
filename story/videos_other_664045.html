"Ten Cities, Ten Thousand Lamps" Witnesses the Growth of China's LED Industry Amid Controversy [Lianchengfa]

by szlcf339 on 2012-02-13 09:20:32

In 2009, to expand domestic demand and promote the implementation of energy conservation and emissions reduction, China launched its first batch of "Ten Cities, Ten Thousand Lights" pilot projects. Twenty-one cities, including Tianjin, were selected to implement a semiconductor lighting application project, which was then expanded in May 2011 to include another sixteen cities such as Beijing. This promoted the use of LED lights for road illumination and accelerated the upgrading of the LED lighting industry.

The "Ten Cities, Ten Thousand Lights" project, as a national pilot project for promoting semiconductor lighting, has had a positive and significant impact on the development of the semiconductor industry. According to authoritative institutions, under the guidance of the pilot demonstration work of "Ten Cities, Ten Thousand Lights", it is estimated that by 2015, LED lighting will account for 30% of general lighting, saving 140 billion kilowatt-hours annually. By then, the scale of the semiconductor lighting industry will reach 500 billion yuan, with exports reaching 30 billion US dollars, creating employment opportunities for more than one million people. There is no denying that by then China will become one of the pioneers in the semiconductor lighting industry.

At the same time, controversies have not ceased throughout the course of "Ten Cities, Ten Thousand Lights".

The competitive advantage of outdoor LED lamps has become apparent. Currently, in the application of outdoor LED lamps represented by "Ten Cities, Ten Thousand Lights", their competitive advantages are evident. In outdoor lighting fixtures, the cost of LED streetlights and tunnel lights used to replace 250W high-pressure sodium lamps is lower than that of traditional lighting, with a cost difference of about 30%. The competitive advantage of LED streetlights and tunnels has already emerged. Based on the continuous reduction in the price of LEDs and lighting fixtures and the continuous improvement in performance, it is expected that before 2013, the comprehensive cost of the entire life cycle of most LED lamps will be significantly lower than that of traditional lamps, becoming the mainstream lighting source.

China's LED lighting application market has begun to form. It is expected that with the introduction and implementation of subsidy policies for domestic LED lighting products, the domestic LED lighting application market will experience rapid growth in the next two years. With the continuous improvement in the performance of semiconductor lighting products and the continuous reduction in manufacturing costs, along with the improvement of related application environments such as standards, product certification and testing, engineering application design and demonstration, relevant policies, etc., the LED lighting application will maintain a high growth rate from 2011 to 2015. Calculated based on lamp data, it is expected that by 2015, its share in the lighting market will increase to around 30%, becoming one of the most competitive mainstream lighting sources.

"Ten Cities, Ten Thousand Lights" is a touchstone, reflecting the current state of China's LED industry development. However, currently, the quality of China's LED products varies greatly. From chips to packaging, although there are enterprises involved, there are more than 4,000 companies engaged in packaging applications alone, but each company has different testing standards, leading to a chaotic market. The national standard draft should be officially released as soon as possible to regulate the market and prepare for the application of "Ten Cities, Ten Thousand Lights".

LED lamps are a system engineering project. It's not just about choosing good LED light-emitting chips; their heat dissipation systems, drive systems, secondary optical design, and structural design are all indispensable factors in making a good lamp, none of which can be missing. Any arbitrary combination will only yield good results if every aspect is good.

China's LED industry started relatively late, and the related core intellectual property rights are basically held by foreign manufacturers. From the current global development of GaN-based LED industries, the top five companies - America's CREE, Germany's Osram, Holland's Philips, Japan's Nichia, and Toyoda Gosei - own 80% to 90% of the original inventive patents. Philips' international patents exceed ten thousand. Additionally, international companies place greater emphasis on future patent layouts. Taking CREE as an example, it currently has 423 patents with validity extending until 2026. One could say that continuous technological innovation is one of the reasons why foreign companies' brands remain strong.

In contrast, although the scale of domestic application enterprises has reached over 3,000, and those producing LED displays exceeding 100 million yuan have surpassed 25, regrettably, few possess their own brands of high quality. This fundamentally relates to Chinese enterprises' haste for success and insufficient emphasis on technological innovation. And undoubtedly, before the emergence of the 337 bill related to LED, enterprises almost did not pay attention to patents.

"Ten Cities, Ten Thousand Lights" can be considered both an opportunity and a challenge for domestic enterprises to establish their brands. If a company’s product is reliable, the brand effect will naturally be visible. However, if they fill the ranks with substandard products, it will not only ruin their brand but also affect the world's confidence in the entire LED industry. Technological innovation and self-quality enhancement present a significant challenge for Chinese enterprises.

Moreover, with the support of the government and the efforts of the enterprises themselves, chip, packaging, and application manufacturers have begun to take shape. Unfortunately, after many companies achieve some decent performance, they are no longer satisfied with the status quo and invest in the front and back end production of the industry. For instance, some chip manufacturers develop towards front-end LED equipment; some application companies move towards epitaxial chip development; some develop towards the back end, moving from chips to packaging, and from packaging to application. This can be seen as a good thing, indicating that everyone sees the very promising development prospects of the LED industry. However, while developing, we also hope that enterprises focus on the "quality" of their products while paying attention to the "quantity".

On the other hand, some international companies abroad strictly adhere to quality management systems and reliability tests when producing products. Taking CREE Company as an example, every time a new product is launched, it strictly follows JEDEC test standards, and its LED color grading levels are strictly produced according to the standards of the American National Standards Institute. Very few domestic LED manufacturers can achieve this.

Therefore, the development of the "Ten Cities, Ten Thousand Lights" project requires unified guidance. It requires policy guidance from the government, with policies issued by the state and local governments, such as those from Guangdong and Shanghai; association-led guidance actively organizing exchanges, selecting good lamps, making good lamps, and establishing industry standards; enterprises need long-term planning, carefully making lamps, standardizing inspections, and making qualified lamps that withstand the test of time!

"Ten Cities, Ten Thousand Lights" will have unified guidance

The implementation effect of "Ten Cities, Ten Thousand Lights" directly concerns the future and fate of the LED industry.

Although "Ten Cities, Ten Thousand Lights" has been piloted for more than two years, there has never been a unified set of guidelines issued. This is also because the timing for the initial implementation of "Ten Cities, Ten Thousand Lights" was not ripe, and issues such as product quality were unclear. It was hoped that problems would be discovered and summarized through pilot demonstrations, and application would drive technological innovation. Overall, there have been both experiences and lessons learned.

Achievements: In the first batch of 21 cities, more than 1.6 million LED lamps have been demonstrated, saving over 164 million kWh of electricity. Among them, there are approximately 220,000 outdoor applications, including about 200,000 streetlights and 16,000 tunnel lights; about 660,000 indoor applications; and about 720,000 landscape lighting applications. The 21 cities participating in the pilot project have more than 2,000 semiconductor lighting enterprises, with a related output value of about 75.9 billion yuan, preliminarily forming industrial agglomeration.

From the latest survey situation of the alliance, some application demonstration projects have achieved positive results in terms of scale and product quality; the application fields and scope of LED are continuously expanding, and the profitability and innovation capabilities of product manufacturing enterprises have also significantly improved; some places have established a "enterprise + user + finance" development model during the pilot process, gaining valuable experience in EMC mode operations; especially in the controversial streetlight field, a batch of new streetlight demonstration projects surveyed have received positive evaluations from professionals in terms of luminous efficacy, lighting uniformity, and color rendering, though they still need time to verify, but progress is evident.

Existing problems: Relevant promotion policies and guiding opinions have yet to be introduced; some local governments blindly adopt the "market-for-investment" method to attract LED enterprises, resulting in narrow product selection range, low product cost-performance ratio, and repeated investment; coordination between science and technology, construction, and NDRC departments still faces certain obstacles, and future efforts must be intensified to form synergy; some places' EMC projects lack financing platforms, and business models still need exploration; additionally, the lack of standards for LED product specifications and interfaces is an urgent issue that needs solving.

How to face challenges: Regarding the challenges faced by the development of "Ten Cities, Ten Thousand Lights", Minister Wan Gang proposed countermeasures, including 1) Further improvement of relevant policy measures and coordination mechanisms; 2) Strengthening of product testing and project management; 3) Further improvement of business models and their promotional environment, meaning that the development of an industry under market economy conditions will certainly be competitive, breaking regional restrictions, and truly achieving a nationwide coordinated approach; 4) Relying on alliances to build an information release platform, where relevant information such as the industrial status, pilot work progress, recommended product catalogs, product suppliers, and energy-saving service companies of each pilot city is uniformly released on the "information release platform".

Deputy Secretary General of the National Semiconductor Lighting Project Research and Development and Industry Alliance, Mr. Gong Bo, also stated that in response to the most pressing specification and interface issues facing the industry at present, the alliance has listed specification and interface standardization as an important research direction. In the future, the alliance will further improve the relevant information platform, release product catalogs, and better implement specification and interface standardization. He particularly pointed out that with the acceleration of the "reshuffling" process accompanying structural overcapacity in future industry development, the "reshuffling" itself may be painful, but some enterprises with technological innovation capabilities and financial strength will stand out. Enterprises should especially seize opportunities in a timely manner to gain better development space.
