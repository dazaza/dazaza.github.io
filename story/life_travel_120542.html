Common Knowledge for Hosting International Conferences - FAQ for Conferences

by longersing on 2009-01-22 07:46:15

I. Overview of International Conferences

An international conference mainly refers to a gathering of representatives from more than a few countries who come together to address mutual concerns regarding international issues, coordinate their respective interests, and seek or take action based on joint discussions (such as passing resolutions, reaching agreements, signing treaties, etc.). International conferences come in various forms with numerous titles and can be categorized into many types: bilateral or multilateral; intergovernmental or non-governmental; single-issue or multi-issue; organized by international organizations or non-international organizations; global or regional; for developing countries or developed countries. From the perspective of the issues discussed and resolved at international conferences themselves, they can also be divided into: peace conferences; military conferences; diplomatic conferences; economic conferences; political conferences, etc.

As international conferences become more frequent and cover a wider range of topics, they are increasingly dedicated to addressing common issues of universal concern in human society, such as economic development, social progress, ecological protection, etc. At the same time, there has been a significant increase in non-governmental, civilian international conferences.

Since the implementation of China's reform and opening-up policy, China's influence in the world has grown, and its political status on the international stage has continuously improved. The number of international conferences organized by the Chinese government or relevant departments in China, or entrusted by international organizations to China, is increasing day by day. Through reform and opening up, external exchanges among provinces and cities in China have expanded significantly, and the software and hardware environments for hosting conferences have become relatively complete. As a result, the organization of international conferences commissioned by national departments or international organizations has gradually increased. Some units in our city have also successively held some regional or bilateral small-scale international conferences. This not only enhances China's international standing and the external influence of local areas but also promotes the development of local foreign economic and trade cooperation, expands exchanges in science and technology, education, culture, and other aspects, deepens mutual understanding and friendship between professionals and foreign counterparts, and facilitates technical information and economic exchanges.

Examples of international conferences held across the country include the ASEAN Expo, various foreign trade meetings, world cultural cooperation exchange meetings, and various business institution meetings, etc. Among these, Guilin has always been an excellent choice for hosting conferences because the combination of conferences and tourism makes everything more relaxed and natural.

II. Preparatory Work for International Conferences

To ensure the success of a conference, adequate preparation must be done beforehand. The success of a conference largely depends on careful planning before it takes place. General preparatory work includes initiating the conference, selecting the time and location for its holding, sending out invitations, drafting the agenda, preparing conference documents, and addressing relevant technical issues (such as seating arrangements for delegations from participating countries, usage of the conference name, etc.). Thorough preparations lay a solid foundation for the successful conduct of the conference.

In a sense, initiating a conference is the process of its conception. The relevant initiating country often uses appropriate diplomatic channels to exchange opinions in advance with other countries, especially the main relevant countries, regarding the proposed conference, listens to suggestions, and reaches consensus or understanding on the convening of the conference. The agenda, venue, time, and participating countries of the conference are often the main issues discussed and negotiated by various countries.

1. Preparation of Request Reports

If relevant units in our city wish to jointly organize international conferences, seminars, or workshops with foreign institutions, they should generally apply for approval procedures more than six months in advance after preliminary consultations on the purpose, content, time, and location of the conference with foreign units and reach a preliminary agreement, but before making any verbal or written commitments (for example, if organizing international academic seminars related to ideology, the content of the meeting should be carefully considered). The organizing unit should submit a request report to its superior authority. The request report should include the following contents:

It should explain the cause, purpose, content, time, and location of the conference; the source of relevant expenses; what verbal agreements have been preliminarily reached with domestic and foreign relevant units; which countries or domestic and foreign representatives or scholars are planned to be invited to participate in the conference; the proposed Chinese and English names of the conference as well as the language used for the conference; when the attending representatives need to provide what kind of papers; the preliminary agenda of the conference; the protocol arrangements for the conference; potential sensitive diplomatic or political issues that may arise during the conference, such as some attendees coming from countries without diplomatic relations with China, etc. Generally speaking, when hosting international conferences in our city, non-diplomatic countries are not invited. For scholars from non-diplomatic countries applying to attend international conferences initiated by our city, the organizing unit should politely decline. If it is indeed necessary for them to attend, the organizing unit must report to the Municipal Foreign Affairs Office, which will then report through the Provincial Foreign Affairs Office to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs for approval. The organizing unit should attach the unit's communication address, telephone, and fax numbers, as well as contact person information to the request report. The request report must be reported level by level to the competent authorities and reviewed by the Municipal Foreign Affairs Office and the Provincial Foreign Affairs Office before being submitted to the provincial government for approval. No unit shall bypass the hierarchical reporting procedure. If approval from central or State Council departments is required, it must be directly submitted to the corresponding central department for approval after obtaining the consent of the provincial party committee or provincial government. Only after the relevant higher-level departments agree can invitations be sent to domestic and foreign representatives and preparations for the conference commence.

If this international conference is hosted by central or provincial relevant departments, and the venue is selected in our city, requiring relevant units in our city to undertake the international conference, then the preparatory work should be carried out according to the requirements of the higher-level relevant departments. If the funding for organizing this conference needs to be allocated from the municipal finance or requires participation or speeches by municipal leaders at the conference, or requires municipal leaders to hold a certain position in the conference preparatory committee, the undertaking unit must report or request in writing to the municipal government in advance.

2. Establishing a Working Team for Conference Preparation

Establish an Organizing Committee for the conference preparation, select relevant responsible persons to serve as chairman or members of the committee, and set up several working groups under the committee according to actual work needs.

3. Sending Out Invitations

Invitations are usually issued by the host country of the conference, sometimes by a non-host country. When sending out invitation letters, a draft of the proposed conference rules of procedure, agenda and schedule, registration form, simple traffic map should be attached, and the requirements for attending the conference should be explained (such as whether registration fees are required, when to pay, when to send papers to the host unit, which hotel the conference representative plans to stay at, when to check-in, when to reply to the host unit whether to attend the conference, etc.). The invitation letter should clarify the purpose of the conference and the expected results. Invitation letters are generally sent out four months before the conference. The scope of sending out invitations should follow the range originally agreed upon by the conference organizers and approved by relevant departments in China. Any additional invitations must be approved by relevant higher-level departments.

4. Drafting Reception Plans

After all preparatory work is basically ready, the organizing unit or the unit entrusted by central or provincial relevant departments to undertake the conference in our city should draft reception plans and report them to relevant departments for approval.
