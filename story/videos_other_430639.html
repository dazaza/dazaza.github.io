The Land of the Disabled

One day, I had a dream where I found myself on an island. It was a land of the disabled. Allegedly, it was one of God's mistakes that made most people here disabled, and healthy individuals were extremely rare. In this place, social status wasn't determined by occupation or wealth but by physical health and the degree of bodily integrity.

When guests first arrived here, a person with one leg longer than the other would serve a cup of clear tea. Due to the significant difference in leg length, he walked as if he were hopping in a pit, bouncing quite severely. Therefore, balancing a cup of tea without spilling it, a task easily done by a healthy person, required great effort from him. Thus, when he placed a fragrant cup of tea with floating petals steadily in front of us, I was deeply moved and sincerely thanked him for his work, even though he merely served me a cup of tea.

He had just left when a girl with only one leg danced for us. Then, a man with only one hand came to peel apples for us. Although his arm abruptly ended at the wrist like a stick, it seemed to have a magnetic force, coordinating with another hand to peel the apple and pass it over. One of my male companions wanted to smoke, and a blind person came over to light his cigarette. After the host and guest exchanged pleasantries, we began to speak. However, the elder sitting in the main seat, who had been smiling all along and nodding to encourage us to drink tea and eat fruit, turned out to be mute. A blind person beside him spoke on his behalf.

Our hosts told us that there was no quarreling or crime here. People were gentle and wouldn't fight. The disputes, brawls, and arguments we often see on the street didn't exist here at all. Quarreling and horseplay were considered shameful here because some could speak while others couldn't—how could they argue? Some had two legs, some had one, and some were in wheelchairs—how could they fight? More importantly, they understood better than us that God gave us a mouth to communicate, not to argue; hands to labor and help each other, not to steal or point fingers; legs to walk, not to chase and do evil; a spine to stand tall and be human, not to bend for fame and profit; intelligence to live better, not to scheme against each other, fight heaven and earth, and fight among ourselves; and health to live well, not to overtake others after eating our fill.

In short, everything that God or nature created for us has its purpose, yet we humans have misused it all. God created humans so the Earth wouldn't be lonely, but instead, humans have caused unbearable chaos on Earth—digging into its skin (cutting down trees, building houses on farmland), cutting its bones (developing various minerals, including coal and ore mining), draining its blood (oil extraction, excessive groundwater pumping), suffocating its breath (natural gas and coal gas exploitation), blinding its sight (air pollution), and killing its ability to breathe (destroying vegetation, forests, and grasslands). As long as the Earth still has life, humans continue to wreak havoc until its destruction.

Waking up from the dream, looking around at the world around me, I broke out in a cold sweat.
