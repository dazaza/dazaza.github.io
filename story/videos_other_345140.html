Facial cleansing is the first step in skin care. You might not think that something as simple as washing your face could have such a significant impact, but many skin problems like acne and blackheads are often caused by improper facial cleansing. If the first step in your skincare routine is wrong, everything else becomes futile. Here are ten common mistakes people make when washing their face.

**Mistake 1: Foam Cleanser Leaves Skin Tight After Washing, Losing Nutrients**

Correction: The feeling of tightness after washing is due to the detergent removing sebum, natural moisturizing agents, and the stratum corneum. Moisture retention is crucial for every face. Gentle foam can penetrate pores to clean stains. If the foam cleanser contains rich nourishing ingredients, it can also smoothly enter the pores. Using only a foamless cleanser will not thoroughly cleanse, leading to fat accumulation and causing skin problems. Selecting the best facial cleanser involves choosing one with fewer bubbles, as more bubbles do not necessarily mean better cleaning effects—it’s quite the opposite.

**Misunderstanding 2: More Expensive Facial Cleansers Yield Better Cosmetic Results**

Correction: A face wash is meant to clean the skin. If it contains too many functional ingredients, it may not clean the skin thoroughly. Too many functional components entering the pores without protection can burden the skin. Therefore, a face cream needs to meet two basic conditions: Clean thoroughly and moisturize the skin. Choosing the best option now includes very popular coastal beauty cosmetics DIY-self-made ones, which can be tailored according to your skin type.

**Misunderstanding 3: Using a Towel to Wash Your Face is Cleaner**

Correction: Towels have loops ten times larger than pores, making it difficult to remove deep-seated grease and dirt. Old wet towels can breed various bacteria, causing skin allergies and friction. So, using gentle cleansing foam is best, and you should frequently dry and sterilize your towel.

**Mistake 4: Using Water to Wash Your Face Thoroughly Cleanses the Skin**

Correction: Choosing the right water temperature is very important. Hot water completely clears the protective film on the skin, easily loosening the skin and increasing pore size, resulting in rough skin and wrinkles. Additionally, if too much oil is washed off, it will accelerate skin aging. Commonly used low-temperature water washes will close the skin pores, unable to wash away accumulated sebum, dust, and dirt residue, not only failing to achieve beauty effects but also prone to skin diseases like acne, impacting beauty. The correct method is to use 35°C warm water for cleansing, then rinse with cold water.

**Misunderstanding 5: Using Cleanser Alone Can Wash Your Face Very Clean**

Correction: For women who regularly wear makeup, facial cleansing must be divided into two steps: First, use liquid remover, then wash with cleanser. Cleanser can only wash away sweat, sebum, aging stratum corneum cells, and hydrophilic moisturizers. Makeup remover, which contains vegetable oil components, oil emulsifiers, and surfactants, can more thoroughly wash away makeup and rid the skin of dirt left deep within.

**Misunderstanding 6: After Washing Your Face, Let It Dry Naturally, Which Plays the Role of Water Supplement**

Correction: Natural evaporation can cool the skin, potentially leading to dryness, peeling, and even wrinkles. So after washing your face, you should immediately use toner, followed by a moisturizing face cream.

**Misunderstanding 7: When It's Hot and Sweaty, Washing Several Times a Day Will Make Your Face Cleaner**

Correction: In normal circumstances, washing once at night is sufficient. If you've been sweating all day, dust has accumulated, and your skin feels uncomfortable, you can do a deep cleansing. In hot weather, you can increase the frequency.

**Mistake 8: After Makeup, Wiping First With Tissue Makes It Easier to Cleanse**

Correction: Wiping makeup with a tissue first is the most harmful to the skin, as rough paper towels without oil or water will roughly rub cosmetic particles into skin pores, increasing the difficulty of cleaning afterward and making the skin rough. Cleansing products should be used properly, and cleansing techniques must be gentle.

**Misunderstanding 9: Using Powerful Cleansers Will Wash Everything Clean**

Correction: Choose cleaning products that are clean and moderate. Excessive washing can lead to dryness, sensitivity, redness, and peeling. Ordinary skincare products may cause allergies. We should examine whether our face is being exposed to overly stimulating supplies or cleansers with too strong power. The best time to switch to more moderate face products is when excessive cleaning harms skin health, making it too dry and prone to fine lines, which may not be worth it!

**Misunderstanding 10: Every Corner of the Face Should Be Washed Equally**

Correction: The T-zone is the most susceptible to dirt, so it should be rubbed a few extra times. Cleaning should be done from bottom to top, gently kneading and washing the cheeks. Since the stratum corneum cells on the face are distributed in layers from top to bottom, cleaning in the opposite direction will certainly be less effective. Hair and temple areas are prone to styling agent residue, so they require particular attention during cleansing, especially for sensitive skin. Eyes should be cleaned last to prevent irritation from cleansing products.

In conclusion, proper facial cleansing is essential for maintaining healthy skin.
