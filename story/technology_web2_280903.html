Promoting articles is actually very simple, it's about finding the right news source. When I wrote articles before, I didn't specifically analyze which websites were news sources. I just knew that after writing the article, I tried to publish it on the most well-known information portals in the industry, such as DONEWS, Webmaster China, etc. I found that as long as the article appeared on the homepage of DONEWS or Webmaster China, it would be reprinted by a large number of websites. In the early stage, mainly rely on these well-known platforms in the industry to promote your blog. In the later stage, you might not need to forward it elsewhere, just posting it on your own blog or in promotion groups would lead to some websites actively reprinting it. Here, I'm just using IT as an example, but the principles are the same for other types of soft article publishing channels.

I think when writing soft articles, you must first clearly identify which type of articles you are best at writing. Only by writing what you're good at can you give readers something valuable, and it won't easily be considered as a "soft article". Additionally, you need to clearly understand the type of user group affected by the article content. For example, I mainly write articles about network promotion experiences, and the main audience is new webmasters and new promotion personnel in network companies who have a demand for network promotion. Therefore, all the new websites I have created since starting my independent blog are webmaster-related websites. For instance, zzdh.net (Webmaster Navigation), zzbk.org (Webmaster Encyclopedia), go9go.cn (Link Exchange Platform). Based on my understanding of webmaster user needs, I create websites that cater to the needs of the webmaster user group. This way, when casually promoting my own websites in blog posts, others won't feel it's too subtle. I always try to naturally and appropriately include what I want to promote in the article.
