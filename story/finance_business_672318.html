[1] Overview of Areola Reduction Surgery

An overly large areola is more common in women after pregnancy and breastfeeding, often accompanied by overly large or sagging breasts. In men, it is more commonly seen in gynecomastia. An aesthetically pleasing areola has a diameter of 3-5 cm, and exceeding this standard can make the breast appear loose and shapeless, often with color changes. This is usually the result of breast enlargement during breastfeeding, followed by the breast shrinking again. Therefore, it often accompanies breast ptosis. To restore a beautiful areola, an areola reduction surgery is needed. Through surgery, the excessively large parts around the areola are removed to restore its normal size. If ptosis is present, it can be corrected simultaneously. The surgery is performed under local anesthesia, leaves no scars, and the recovery period is about a week.

[2] Indications for Areola Reduction Surgery

Males and females with overly large areolas.

[3] Surgical Methods for Areola Reduction

With the nipple as the center, draw a circle with an appropriate radius (usually 1.5-2.5 cm). Remove the ring-shaped areola skin outside this range while preserving the dermis layer and subcutaneous vascular network to prevent insufficient blood supply to the nipple and areola. Cut out a triangular piece of skin from the inner and outer sides along the transverse diameter of the outer circle to reduce the radius, aligning it with the new areola's radius. Finally, slightly mobilize the subcutaneous tissue of the outer circle and suture it in position.

[4] Characteristics After Areola Reduction Surgery

The areola returns to its normal size with no scars.
