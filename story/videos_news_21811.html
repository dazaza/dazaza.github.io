The raise of export threshold and the intensification of elimination: the toy industry is experiencing a process of survival of the fittest.

by chengshangdao on 2007-06-07 10:27:41

The world's toys look to China, and any ripple in China’s toy industry draws immense attention within the sector. Due to various international trade barriers and increasing costs, in recent years, 10% of Shenzhen enterprises have exited the toy industry annually. This has led to the question: "Is the toy industry beginning to enter a period of decline?" Industry insiders refute this claim: "The bankruptcy of individual enterprises is due to their own operational issues. For the entire toy industry, it is akin to a few trees falling in a forest, which is a normal phenomenon." "This is something that toy enterprises in the transformation and adjustment phase must go through. The elimination of some small-scale, less competitive toy enterprises benefits the development of the industry."

From January to February this year, China's toy exports reached $1.06 billion, an increase of 45.6% year-on-year, which is 42.3 percentage points higher than the same period last year (3.3%). In February alone, China's toy exports amounted to $500 million, a year-on-year increase of 83.3%, which is 92.6 percentage points higher than the same period last year (-9.3%). Among these, exports to Canada were $14.63 million, and to Russia $13.25 million, with respective increases of 1.2 times and 1.8 times year-on-year. However, despite the high-speed growth of China's toy exports, due to various international trade barriers and increased costs, every year in Shenzhen, 10% of enterprises exit the toy industry. This cannot help but raise the question: Is the domestic toy industry entering a period of decline or adjustment?

In response to this, Wang Tie, Secretary-General of the Guangzhou Toy and Giftware Association, stated during an interview with reporters: "It is not surprising that the current phenomenon of toy enterprises being eliminated stands out. This is something that toy enterprises in the transformation and adjustment phase must go through. The elimination of some small-scale, less competitive toy enterprises benefits the development of the industry." Relevant officials from the Guangdong Toy Association also expressed regarding this phenomenon: "The bankruptcy of individual enterprises is due to their own operational issues. For the entire toy industry, it is akin to a few trees falling in a forest, which is a normal phenomenon."

Industry insiders pointed out that the export growth data for the first two months of this year strongly confirms that the domestic toy industry has not entered a period of decline.

Wang Tie told reporters that various international trade barriers and rising production costs are the main reasons for some enterprises exiting the toy industry.

As a labor-intensive industry, the production costs of toys mainly focus on raw material procurement and labor wages. Since last year, the appreciation of the RMB has exceeded 6%, and the sharp rise in raw materials and labor costs has made the profit margin for toys increasingly narrow. The increase in toy export costs has put pressure on domestic enterprises. Meanwhile, Vietnam and India are also vying for the international traditional toy market.

Additionally, Shenzhen's toy export markets are mainly concentrated in the United States and the European Union. The continuous improvement of environmental protection and safety standards in Europe and America has significantly raised the "threshold" for toy exports, with exorbitant testing fees. Since 2003, U.S. toy sales have been declining for three consecutive years. At the beginning of 2006, the U.S. banned the import of artificial Christmas trees containing wood, worsening Shenzhen's toy exports. The second largest export market, the EU, is also continuously raising market access thresholds. On August 13, 2005, the EU began levying recycling fees based on the "Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment Directive," thereby increasing the export costs of electronic and electric toys by about 15%. On January 16, 2007, the EU's new regulations restricting the use of phthalates in toys officially came into effect, causing the cost of China's toys to increase more than fourfold if substitutes are used. Moreover, Russia has enacted a series of measures to restrict the import of Chinese toys, and Mexico has announced the continuation of anti-dumping taxes on Chinese toys.

Therefore, some small-scale toy enterprises with weak product development capabilities and limited ability to overcome foreign technical barriers can only be excluded from the international market due to failing to meet technical standards and having too small profit margins.

The increase in international barriers and rising production costs prompt domestic toy enterprises to urgently need adjustments. Toy enterprises should accelerate innovation when facing pressures, actively adopt advanced technologies and management methods at home and abroad, pay more attention to quality-benefit oriented development paths, continuously enhance their independent innovation capabilities, improve production processes, develop safe and environmentally friendly high-tech alternative products, and rely on industrial upgrading to cross over new thresholds.

At the same time, toy enterprises should establish credibility, seek new market footholds, and strive to open up domestic and international markets. According to Wang Tie, in recent years, the market prospects for derivative products of cartoon animation images have been broad, forming a certain impact on the toy industry. Toy enterprises should take a long-term view, seize the great opportunity for the development of the animation industry, fully utilize this advantageous resource to promote the development of new products, and increase industrial added value; explore how to combine the animation industry with the toy industry, build an exchange platform between animation production enterprises and toy enterprises, and seek profitable models for the common development of animation and toy enterprises. Journalists learned that some enterprises have already ventured into this area, but the products mainly target the domestic market, and the international market awaits further development.

According to Wang Tie, the Guangzhou Toy and Giftware Association is currently initiating the establishment of an animation industry association. Relevant materials have already been submitted and are awaiting approval from relevant departments, with the possibility of realization as soon as within this year.

Some viewpoints suggest that the increase in market thresholds affects traditional toys the most, and enterprises should focus on developing safe and environmentally friendly high-tech products. Regarding this, Wang Tie believes that although the increase in market thresholds affects the export of traditional toys, it does not mean that traditional toys have no market. Enterprises should still base their developments on market demands and appropriately develop products according to local conditions, which is the correct direction for enterprise development. Wang Tie expressed confidence in the future of China's toy industry. As an evergreen industry, the toy industry is steadily advancing in the adjustment process. The key lies in how it will develop in the future.

Industry Focus

The Implementation of Toy 3C Certification Enters the Countdown

Starting June 1st, six categories of toys including children's vehicles, electric toys, projectile toys, metal toys, doll toys, and plastic toys will comprehensively implement China's mandatory product certification ("CCC" certification). At that time, uncertified products will not be allowed to leave the factory, be sold, imported, or used in other business activities. The inspection and quarantine department reminds toy manufacturers that companies without certification should promptly apply for certification.

According to reports, since March 1, 2006, designated certification institutions in our country started accepting certification applications from enterprises. Currently, most enterprises' toy products have obtained certification or are actively applying for certification. To support and facilitate enterprises, certified toy manufacturers can directly exchange certificates or obtain other preferential treatments when applying for export goods quality permits (registration).

Industry insiders pointed out that with the promotion of the 3C certification system, for those enterprises with strong strength and high-quality products, it will play a protective role and promote enterprise development. However, for some enterprises lacking sufficient strength, infringing on intellectual property rights, and profiting through cutting corners and counterfeit inferior products, it will bring the issue of survival.
