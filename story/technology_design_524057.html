Continuation of the Weibo post by the peasant woman sending her son away to be raised alone: A businessman is willing to donate 10 years of child support fees.

by iface011 on 2011-10-28 13:58:50

□ Reporter Yu Yang welcomes your comments. Yesterday, Mr. Han from Zhengzhou Lianri Household Goods Co., Ltd. said that after learning about the misfortune of Shan Xujiong's family, the company felt great sympathy from its leaders to its employees. The company leaders decided to provide long-term assistance to Shan Xujiong's family. They were willing to pay donations regularly to Shan Xujiong through relevant charitable organizations to help this family get through the difficulties.

A car dealer in Anhui Province expressed willingness to raise Shan Xujiong's two children for 10 years or provide support costs. The employees of Jin Hua Hardware and Electronics Factory in Qingxi Town, Dongguan, where Shan Xujiong used to work, also donated 1480 yuan and sent it to Dancheng. After a General Manager surnamed Ye from a Hong Kong-based group saw the report online, he was deeply moved and had already remitted 5100 yuan to Shan Xujiong a few days ago, indicating his intention to continue helping this suffering family.

Netizen "Huanan Tiger Xu Wei Yu" remitted 10,000 yuan from Guangdong and promised to raise the two children for 10 years. Mr. Xu sincerely told Shan Xujiong: "It is very tragic for children to be separated from their parents, which is extremely detrimental to their growth. I am willing to take care of them for 10 years on the condition that the children stay by your side while I provide the expenses."

After undergoing a period of chemotherapy, Shan Xujiong's husband Ba Ruifeng returned home temporarily yesterday for recuperation. "With so many kind-hearted people helping us, our family will definitely not let everyone down. I will make my utmost effort to take care of my brother and sister-in-law's orphans, as well as my husband and children," Shan Xujiong said emotionally.

Zhoukou News: Farmer Shan Xujiong from Changzhuang Administrative Village, Qiandian Town, Dancheng County posted on Weibo (click to view Weibo) expressing her wish to give up her own child for adoption in order to raise the orphan left behind by her brother and sister-in-law. After this matter was reported by our newspaper (see A07 edition of May 14th),热心 readers helped this unfortunate family in different ways. Yesterday, several benevolent individuals expressed that they did not want to see Shan Xujiong's family separated, and were willing to provide long-term support fees so that the two children could remain with their parents.
