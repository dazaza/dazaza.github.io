Media exposes that Li Weifeng's national football team career has come to an end, and Camacho says goodbye to the old captain.

by hlz2012h on 2012-02-29 14:13:25

It is reported that Li Weifeng's road to the national football team has come to an end, and Camacho said goodbye to the old captain. The Chinese team will have the last battle of the 2014 World Cup qualifiers in the Asian zone. The result of the game will not affect the advancement of the two teams from the group, so there are not many suspense in this match. One of the highlights is whether the head of the Chinese Football Association, Wei Di, will come to Guangzhou, which may be a "weathervane" for the popularity of the national team in the near future.

Camacho's team: People haven't left but tea has cooled down. One thing is certain: Wei Di does not approve of replacing Gao Hongbo with Camacho. He plans to let the latter finish the 20 qualifiers. Even if it doesn't go well, changing coaches won't make much difference. So after the failure in the Asian Cup last year, Gao Hongbo was not immediately dismissed. What caused a huge change in the coaching staff of the Chinese team was the draw for the 20 qualifiers group. China's luck was not bad, as they were grouped with Jordan and Iraq, two teams that are not too strong. Regarding the issue of Gao Hongbo's stay or departure, one opinion prevailed: whoever leads the team can advance from the group. This opinion was something Wei Di could not refute.

The Mayans called 2012 the "end of the world" in their calendar, but such baseless rumors are not worth worrying about. However, 2012 is indeed the "end of the world" for this national team in the Brazilian World Cup. The renowned coach Camacho came with a "good omen," but unlike in movies, there will be no Noah's Ark to save Camacho and the Chinese team from the "World Cup end-of-the-world" predicament. With no major competitions for the national team this year, next year, and the year after, Wei Di's attitude towards the national team has become a mystery. After all, showing up or not represents an attitude, and attitude determines the level of support. Of course, Vice Chairman Wei probably still needs to say the right things publicly, but his true thoughts remain unknown.

Wei Di last watched a Chinese team game on October 11th of last year. It has been four months since he last visited the "eldest son" of Chinese football. As of the time the journalist went to press yesterday, the organizing committee was still uncertain whether Vice Chairman Wei Di of the Chinese Football Association would attend the "Cardajo Team 2012" game. This might not be a good sign.

There was also an interesting episode yesterday. At the pre-match press conference, only a dozen or so media outlets showed up. The questions were perfunctory, and the answers were absent-minded, lasting only about five minutes before concluding. In contrast, the friendly football match between the Guangdong journalists' team and the national journalists' team in the afternoon attracted three or forty media outlets. The local TV station conducted live interviews, and everyone enjoyed themselves for two hours. This stark contrast reflects the "public sentiment" that can only be understood implicitly.

Compared to always failing at big tournaments, another fine tradition maintained by the national team is their ability to always close with a beautiful ending. Hopefully, today will be the day of the national team's "rebirth". Efforts must be made!

Camacho: Saying Goodbye to Li Weifeng

A shortage of center-backs has hit the national team, causing Camacho distress while firmly stating that the Chinese team must undergo generational changes. This means that Li Weifeng's career as a national player will end under his leadership.

At the press conference, Camacho said he was unaware of the doubts raised by Evergrande's head coach Lee Jang-soo. In fact, Lee Jang-soo did have some criticisms towards Camacho. During this period of national team training and matches, it coincided with the final preparation period for the AFC Champions League for the top three teams in the Chinese Super League (CSL). Camacho summoned eight players from Evergrande, disrupting Lee Jang-soo's preparation plan. The Korean coach was very upset and openly questioned, "Does the match against Jordan still hold significance for the Chinese team?" Starting center-back Feng Xiaoting suffered a minor injury during the match against Kuwait and subsequently participated in the Super Cup, but his injury worsened, making him unavailable for the national team's match against Jordan. Adding insult to injury, Zhao Xuri missed the morning training session when the national team arrived in Guangzhou due to an ankle injury sustained during training. Zhao Xuri also missed yesterday's training and is likely to sit out today's match. Upon hearing this news, Lee Jang-soo became furious. According to the plan, immediately after the national team's match, Evergrande will depart for Korea. Lee Jang-soo is very concerned that Feng and Zhao, two absolute key players, may miss the AFC Champions League match against Jeonbuk Hyundai Motors on March 7.

In response, Camacho's attitude was relatively tough: "In our hearts, all matches are equally important, whether they are friendlies or official matches." Currently, the only available center-backs in the national team lineup are Zhao Peng and Zhang Linpeng. Liu Jian has been practicing as a guest center-back. Camacho said, "We can only rely on young players now. Although Li Weifeng is a player I admire greatly, the team is in a transformation phase, and we must say goodbye."

If the translation is accurate, Camacho has designated this year as the "year of discovery" for the national team. He said, "I hope the players understand the gap with stronger teams. Through overseas training, we realize that our players still have significant capability gaps."

The national team's "decentralization": Dabao remains a substitute.

During yesterday morning's field practice, the national team conducted group sparring sessions, making today's starting lineup clearer. Newly transferred Yu Dabao will still be a substitute, and Camacho did not treat him differently just because he set a record for the highest transfer fee for a domestic player.

From the first day he was called into the national team, Yu Dabao was destined to be a versatile "wild card" and not part of the Chinese team's starting lineup. He has already gotten used to not being assigned a yellow vest at the start. When the groups began, he didn't even think twice and stood on the side without the vests. During the 30-minute sparring session, teammates sometimes seemed unaccustomed to Yu Dabao's quick passes, leading to some promising combinations being abandoned due to differences in playing habits. Yu Dabao calmly dealt with the lack of harmony during the training. Yu Hai scored first with a header, and Yu Hanchao equalized for the starting lineup. After the training ended, Yu Dabao calmly returned to the locker room and declined all media interviews.

Feng Xiaoting's withdrawal due to injury forced the reorganization of the national team's defense. Zhao Peng and Zhang Linpeng partnering as center-backs has become the last option. In the left-back position, team captain Sun Xiang will start, while the right-back position will still be occupied by the reliable Liu Jianye. Adjustments in the midfield come from the defensive midfielder position. Lu Peng's performance has already been recognized by Camacho, and Qin Sheng's performance has always been evident. If it weren't for missing the start of the match against Kuwait due to diarrhea, Qin Sheng should have entered the public eye earlier. The attacking midfielder position will still be held by Chen Tao, while Yu Hanchao and Hao Junmin, two players with distinct characteristics, will continue to play on both wings of the national team. The forward line will still feature Gao Lin.

In Camacho's training sessions, set-piece and corner-kick tactical drills are often interspersed. During the morning adaptation training, Yu Hai made a late run and directly headed the ball into the goal. During the Qingyuan training period, Liu Jian from the substitute lineup frequently scored headers like this. Such frequent lapses in marking are obviously not accidental. The "decentralization" of the national team's main squad in set-piece defense is the biggest hidden danger.
