Mob boss hires a hitman but doesn't pay; the hitman is bribed by the other side and gets killed (picture included)

by lovei0c1 on 2012-03-06 14:06:21

The picture shows the guns and bullets seized by the police during anti-gang operations.

In 2006, the Criminal Investigation Bureau of the Municipal Public Security Bureau established an anti-gang team dedicated to combating various kinds of criminal gangs. By December last year, the city had dismantled 17 mafia-like organizations, eradicated 45 criminal gangs, and arrested 305 criminals. Yesterday, I visited the anti-gang team of the Criminal Investigation Bureau, uncovering little-known stories behind a series of major anti-gang cases in our city. Although these anti-gang heroes cannot be publicly identified, they have been silently safeguarding the safety of all the people in the city.

[Case of Zhang Chengyi]

Captain's head was put up for bounty

Between 1998 and 2001, there were multiple serious gun-related murder cases in the three towns of Wuhan. The stormy city was filled with fear. Through investigation, the anti-gang team discovered that Zhang Chengyi, the head of the mafia, was a major suspect. Due to a conflict with the head of a criminal gang, the Pan brothers, Zhang Chengyi was shot and became disabled. Afterwards, he carried out a frenzied retaliation against the Pan brothers, planning several murders.

After arresting Zhang Chengyi, the police eventually released him due to insufficient evidence. Because Zhang Chengyi had strong counter-surveillance awareness, his wealth came from lending at high interest rates in foreign casinos, while domestically, he had two teams of hitmen. These two teams rarely interacted but supervised each other, making it difficult for the police to lock down evidence. Moreover, Zhang Chengyi was extremely cruel, and anyone who went against him was retaliated against. After a driver who had followed Zhang Chengyi for many years was captured and handed over some evidence to the police, he was stabbed upon being released from prison.

On September 9, 2007, Zhang Chengyi was unexpectedly gunned down. The police investigation revealed that this was because Zhang Chengyi failed to pay the hitmen their promised compensation, leading them to be bribed by the other side and turn their guns on Zhang Chengyi. The anti-gang team then decisively acted, capturing the Zhang Chengyi crime syndicate that had been entrenched in Wuhan for many years. In this case, a total of 27 firearms and more than 600 bullets were seized, which the Ministry of Public Security recognized as the "Number One Anti-Gang Case" of the year.

During the investigation of this case, the captain of the anti-gang team and other police officers faced death threats from the criminal elements. Their work vehicles had their tires slashed. There were also rumors circulating in society that someone was offering a high price for his head. One evening, the glass in his bathroom at home was smashed. This clearly indicated that the other side already knew his residential address.

However, the captain remained calm, strengthened security precautions, and ultimately brought the Zhang Chengyi mafia group to justice.

[Case of Zeng Ailing]

Hitman had a hand grenade

At around 9 p.m. on June 8, 2004, a shooting incident occurred in Dawai Chen Village, Jiangxia District. The culprit was Li Yinqun, one of the main offenders of the Zeng Ailing mafia gang in Jiangxia District. Zeng Ailing is from Puan Village, Jiangxia District, Wuhan City. Since 2002, he has gathered many people in the Puan area of Jiangxia District, opening gambling companies and monopolizing construction projects to make huge profits. He also committed multiple intentional murders and disturbances.

On the evening of June 21, 2005, dozens of armed police officers surrounded the room where Li Yinqun was located. As soon as the police broke through the door, Li Yinqun immediately fired back and fled onto the roof. Li Yinqun, positioned higher up, fiercely shot at the pursuing police officers. When he jumped to another roof, he was hit in the calf by the police and captured.

Subsequently, the police found a hand grenade in Li Yinqun's residence. It turned out that he had prepared to detonate the hand grenade in the final moment to die together with his pursuers.

[Case of Zou Chenghong]

Fighting off a fierce bodyguard to seize the gun

At around 1:00 p.m. on September 4, 2009, on the 13th floor of a certain hotel on Minzhu Road in Wuchang, the mafia boss Zou Chenghong led his bodyguards Zhou and Pi among others to cause trouble. A department manager of the company tried to leave and was choked by Zou Chenghong's henchman Zou. Several others then proceeded to punch and kick him.

In the chaos, five men rushed out and controlled Zou Chenghong. One of them jumped onto a table, pulled out a handgun, and identified himself as a policeman. Unexpectedly, the bodyguard Zhou, relying on his large build, charged forward to try to seize the plainclothes policeman's gun. He also declared, "I only listen to the boss."

To stabilize the situation, the policemen temporarily loosened their grip on Zou Chenghong. In this encounter, a policeman's right thumb was injured. At around 2:00 p.m. that afternoon, a large number of police forces arrived and surrounded the hotel room where Zou Chenghong and his gang were staying.

Bodyguard Zhou resisted to the death, attempting to seize the gun from the policeman in the struggle. With a bang, a bullet made a big hole in the wall. The policeman used his fingers to firmly press the trigger, preventing it from being pulled. Nearby, three or four policemen also rushed forward, tightly controlling Zhou. After the event, the policeman suffered a fractured finger, and his forehead was bloodied.

After a thrilling battle, the main members of the Zou Chenghong mafia gang were captured on the spot, and some escapees were subsequently apprehended.
