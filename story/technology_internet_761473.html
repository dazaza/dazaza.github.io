Cheng Lingfeng: A Case of Internet Companies Leaving Beijing

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 14:18:51

Zhou Pin was previously the director of Baidu Union. In 08, he founded the creative goods e-commerce website Quwan, which received investments from Zhenfund and Matrix Partners. In 2013, Quwan left Beijing, where it was founded, and set up its headquarters in Chengdu. As someone who has also left Beijing to settle in a southern town and start a self-media business, I asked him a few questions with great interest.Cheng Lingfeng: Why did you move?Zhou Pin: Ask yourself, what will be the driving force for development in the next few years? The financing environment for e-commerce is not good, and we have already raised funds. So the driving force is not money.In fact, it's people-oriented. All the motivation comes from people. None of our team members in Beijing had the possibility of living better lives. For example, they couldn't afford houses. I want them to live better so that they have the motivation to do great things.Cheng Lingfeng: Why Chengdu?I am from Sichuan. The high-tech zone in Chengdu is developing very well. Of course, this is not the reason. I've examined all aspects of Tianjin, Guangzhou, Xiamen, etc. But the housing prices in Chengdu are the most undervalued, having barely risen in the past two years. The high-tech zone in Chengdu is between the third and fourth ring roads and is becoming a new center. The municipal government has moved to the high-tech zone. Chengdu will have two centers, like Puxi and Pudong in Shanghai.I want our team to be able to focus on entrepreneurship for 3-5 years and avoid restless thoughts.Cheng Lingfeng: Are your people united with you?Zhou Pin: Some people didn't want to move and therefore resigned. I started having this idea last June and began communicating with the executives, then gradually communicated downwards. Most of the middle-level managers agreed, and all the executives agreed. This result was contrary to my previous conjecture. At the time, I thought older people with families wouldn't want to move, while younger people would be willing to drift around. But I was wrong.The real situation is that young people think Beijing is full of opportunities and believe that ten years of struggle in Beijing equals thirty years of struggle elsewhere. But in reality, only a few companies and a few people can achieve this, such as Baidu and Tencent. Most companies and most people cannot. Young people often overestimate their own opportunities and underestimate the difficulty of becoming one in ten thousand.On the contrary, people born in the 70s are more willing to move to Chengdu. They have experience, are more pragmatic, have a clearer understanding of the company's development, and are more optimistic. It's easier to reach a consensus on vision.Cheng Lingfeng: Are there enough talents in Chengdu?Zhou Pin: I started understanding this when I had the idea of moving. There are quite a few friends' companies in Chengdu. Although the overall environment is not as good as in Beijing, the accounts are clear when doing business. It suits companies that have reached a certain stage of entrepreneurship.The high-tech zone in Chengdu was once in name only a few years ago. But now it has 40,000 employees and hundreds of enterprises. Many people eat lunch in the collective canteen. This group has沉淀ed excellent people. The Sichuan government is also smart; they first recruited famous enterprises, then held job fairs nationwide, bringing back enlightened Sichuan people, and prompting more enterprises to return.In January, I recruited more than 20 people in Chengdu, all of whom were good. Their characteristics are being down-to-earth and pragmatic.Cheng Lingfeng: How about the rent?Zhou Pin: Cheap, 4 yuan per square meter per day, 120 yuan per month. It's 1/3 to 1/6 of Beijing's price. In Beijing, either it's too expensive or too shabby. Therefore, many companies in Beijing rent villas in remote areas to start businesses, such as beyond the fifth and sixth ring roads. This is different from garage startups abroad. The difficulty of villa startups is the long commute time. In Beijing, the fast pace is an illusion. Time is spent in the subway without a chance to catch your breath.Everyone can think about where you spend your time working in Beijing.In the high-tech zone of Chengdu, everyone is within a 3-kilometer radius. The housing price is 1/3 of Beijing's. The living space becomes larger, and time becomes more abundant. The speed of serving dishes in restaurants is three times faster than in Beijing, and you just eat holding your bowl. Time is more effectively released into work.Cheng Lingfeng: People say Chengdu is too leisurely and doesn't work hard.Zhou Pin: That's hearsay without basic investigation. You don't hear mahjong sounds here. Everyone is at home online. Especially the construction of the new urban area does not resemble the tile houses, small streets, and dampness of the old urban area in Chengdu. The high-tech zone is full of tall buildings. We took more than 50 people out for a countryside outing. I originally wanted to play mahjong to build rapport. But most people didn't play. They liked basketball, badminton, drinking tea, and chatting.The employees here are not lazy. There has never been any complaint about overtime. We just want to try ourselves, not believing those rumors. Not superstitious.Cheng Lingfeng: What is the most painful thing?Zhou Pin: Smoothly transitioning between evacuation and the new home is a big challenge. We decided to move in December. On one hand, we understood the employees' thoughts, on the other hand, we understood the government's attitude, and at the same time looked for houses, calculating how many people to recruit. Layer by layer communication, step by step advancement.The agreement with the government was finally signed at the end of March. Before that, it was all verbal promises. But we still advanced, we couldn't wait. Any entrepreneur cannot take the government's promise as the original driving force for moving. If you move just because of tax and land incentives, you will die miserably.Whether the government gives support or not, we have to move. But the government keeps its promises and is pragmatic. In the end, half of the house + relocation costs are paid, free for three years.Cheng Lingfeng: How do investors see it?Zhou Pin: Investors hope you do well. He will raise his own considerations for reference, but in the end, you make the decision yourself.Cheng Lingfeng: What kind of company is suitable to leave Beijing?Zhou Pin: In the early stages of entrepreneurship, you need to build a backbone team and raise funds. Beijing is the most suitable place. There are more mid-to-high-end talents in Beijing, and they are more willing to communicate. You can meet 20 investors in a week, naturally increasing your chances. There are more internet partners. Face-to-face meetings are different from phone calls; trust is higher.But after the entrepreneurial phase, if you have your backbone team and funding secured, and the next round of funding will come after profitability, when the game is halfway through, you can consider retreating. Liu Bei wasn't from Sichuan, but he chose Sichuan. Zhuge Liang wasn't from Sichuan either. Each stage has corresponding suitable regions. You need to ask yourself, where should you go to develop the next stage?We find it relatively easy to recruit people in Chengdu. For large companies, Chengdu is always a branch, a cost center, and employees lack a sense of belonging. However, our headquarters is in Chengdu, and we aim for a focused journey of entrepreneurship for the next 3-5 years. In Chengdu, striving for five years allows you to buy a house, which is a goal for many people. Our company needs such people.However, departments like marketing and business development are still placed in Beijing to capture primary information and conduct BD.Cheng Lingfeng: How do wages in Chengdu compare to Beijing?Zhou Pin: High-level talent wages are about 80% of Beijing's. Because the good ones will all run away. Good people use their abilities to wander, and they don't feel their wages are high. Enlightened companies are willing to offer good salaries. Entry-level talents aren't cheap either. Because Beijing has already reached its limit.Cheng Lingfeng: Are there any tricks to the entire relocation process?Zhou Pin: How to solve the problem of overlapping and交接between employees in two places. Policy is very important. Employees are practical. Timely appropriate policies must be given. There are incentives for those who are moving, those staying behind, and those leaving. Some came to Chengdu first to交接and then returned to Beijing. We didn't have anyone leave prematurely; everyone was willing to stay until the end.Promise less, deliver more. Employees have fear about relocation. You need to manage everyone's different expectations.Cheng Lingfeng: Would you move again?Zhou Pin: Not likely. Perhaps setting up a new branch. For example, moving the warehouse to Guangzhou. Look at the housing prices; other cities have been rising continuously.Cheng Lingfeng: Will the business be affected?Zhou Pin: Moving will definitely affect the business. It's just a matter of degree. I originally estimated a 20% decline in March, but it actually reached 30%.
