JD Driving School is located at the eastern end of the Bai Li Chang Jie in Beijing, and it is the largest driving training school in Tongzhou District. It has convenient transportation, exquisite surroundings, complete facilities, a full range of vehicle types, and standardized management. It is also the first designated unit in the city to implement the driver training hour-based reform. At JD Driving School, you can choose your coach, vehicle, and time segment at your convenience, ensuring that you can start learning whenever you arrive. Regulations training classes are held weekly, with shuttle bus services available.

JD Driving School offers five types of vehicles: Santana, Zongshen pickup, Fukan automatic transmission, two-wheeled motorcycles, and three-wheeled motorcycles.

According to reports (by reporter Wang Shu), the new regulations for learning to drive and obtaining a license that were originally scheduled to be implemented yesterday did not start on time. However, the wave of price increases caused by this has indeed occurred as expected. Yesterday, Beijing's large and small driving schools raised their prices simultaneously, with most exceeding 4000 yuan. The VIP class, which provides door-to-door service and free lunch, has reached up to 12,000 yuan. "Enroll quickly, there will be further adjustments in a few days," said a staff member at a large driving school's enrollment office.

The old rules for obtaining a license are still in effect.

The price hike yesterday was the third "collective action" by Beijing's driving schools in over a month. From just over 3000 yuan to more than 3500 yuan and now breaking through 4000 yuan, since early March, many Beijing driving schools' tuition fees have experienced a "three-step jump," with an average increase of 300-500 yuan per round, including Eastern Fashion Driving School enrollment.

Each driving school explained that the reason for the price increase was due to "officials introducing new regulations, increasing the cost of driving schools, forcing them to raise prices." The new regulations mainly include two items: increasing practical road test training time by 6 hours; limiting the recruitment scope to 40 people per vehicle. A week ago, all driving schools interviewed by this newspaper stated that with the implementation of the new regulations, another price increase would occur yesterday. Although the fee standards have risen, the original rules for learning to drive and obtaining a license are still being enforced across the city.
