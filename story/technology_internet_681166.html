Revealing 10 Strong Signs of a Smart Baby

by lmxbbwudi23 on 2012-02-28 11:47:37

Smart babies often show extremely strong adaptability in aspects such as life and cognition. How can mothers know the potential of smart babies in the first place?

Performance 1: Early talking

If your baby learns to talk very early, and progresses especially fast, mastering a large amount of vocabulary before the age of two, with clear pronunciation, likes to ask questions, and can say complex sentences that are not commensurate with their age, then congratulations, you have an extremely smart baby!

Performance 2: Curiosity

Babies who are good at observing and discovering problems always have particularly agile thinking. When your baby shows a strong curiosity about things around them and always loves to ask "why", mothers should patiently answer the baby's questions, do you know? The baby has already started the process of solving various confusions in life.

Performance 3: Love for reading

Smart babies always have a strong reading ability, recognizing pictures and words relatively early, and each time they see a book, they have the impulse to pick it up and flip through it.

Performance 4: Love for numbers

Smart babies are particularly sensitive to various numbers, good at remembering various numbers, such as telephone numbers, doorplate numbers, and start recognizing numbers in books at an early age.

Performance 5: Good memory

When mothers find that babies can quickly remember things they've seen each time, such as remembering the plot of animated cartoons after watching them, the names of characters; every poem or nursery rhyme taught by mothers can be remembered, these all indicate that the baby has a good memory, which is the strongest advantage of being smart.

Performance 6: Ability to concentrate

Don't think that babies always love to move and cannot quietly do any one thing well. Smart babies often focus on interesting things for a long time and do their best, such as being very focused and patient when completing jigsaw puzzles.

Performance 7: Strong comprehension

Smart babies can understand complex concepts and perceive relationships between things. When they know what cars, airplanes, and ships are, they will understand the meaning of [transportation].

Performance 8: Broad interests

Smart babies will simultaneously be interested in writing, drawing, music, dancing, etc., and be enthusiastic about participating in these activities.

Performance 9: Strong judgment

Smart babies can make objective analyses of surrounding things, such as distinguishing good from bad characters on TV, and distinguishing right from wrong regarding things.

Performance 10: Strong perception

Smart babies often have prominent perceptual abilities, which can be shown even at a very young age.

Cultivate [smartness] with care

Reading can broaden horizons and increase wisdom. Provide various reading materials to babies more often, such as picture recognition, children's picture books, fairy tales, nursery rhymes, etc. When babies can understand what adults say, tell them stories, recite nursery rhymes, and teach them to read, repeatedly practicing. Smart babies will be able to recite stories and match the story with the content of the pictures. When babies discover that they can read books, they will become interested in reading, greatly enriching their thinking and cognition. Mothers should set an example for babies and develop a habit of loving to read. For the baby's habit of asking questions, timely praise should be given, and never adopt an impatient attitude.

Cultivate interest in science

How to cultivate a baby's interest in science? You can often ask him questions, such as "[What are the stars in the sky?]" "[How does an egg turn into a chick?]" When the baby makes a mistake, patiently help him explain.

Prepare some toys for the baby, such as small wooden blocks, building blocks, paper pieces, small stones, etc., let him play with them frequently. Continuously create opportunities for the baby to come into contact with new things, enhance his curiosity, and cultivate his ability to explore and solve things.

Special cultivation of hobbies

Each baby has its own special hobby, such as playing football, painting, music, dancing, chess, etc., and mothers should be good at discovering his special hobbies and create conditions for him. Only by satisfying the baby's special hobbies can his talent be stimulated.

Three precautions against over-promotion

Although smart babies have natural talent, learning is a gradually increasing process. It is definitely inappropriate to let a just three-year-old baby learn knowledge that ten-year-old children can master. Never let the baby "rushed" to learn this and that, fully occupying the learning time, which will instead make the baby develop aversion to learning.

Grasp the degree of praise

It is appropriate to appropriately praise the baby's progress. Some mothers overly praise the baby's every bit of progress, even saying: "[The baby is amazing, surpassing everyone]," such words will make the baby become self-centered and unable to listen to others' opinions.

Correctly deal with the baby's temper

Talented babies in music, painting, and language often lose their temper more easily than ordinary babies when facing setbacks. In such cases, you don't need to pay too much attention, give cold treatment, let the baby vent negative emotions, and then guide him to control his emotions.
