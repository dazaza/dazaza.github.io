Couple trapped under cement mixer's wheels, husband shouts to save wife first

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-07 13:34:23

It was only a few meters away, yet it was a matter of life and death. Rescue personnel managed to lift the wheels to rescue the man who was trapped.

Accident scene, photo source: Quanzhou Evening News

Southeast Network - Strait Metropolis Daily (Weibo) September 16th report (reporter Tu Chuanzhi, Xie Mingfei, correspondent Yan Jie with text and pictures). Time: around 2 PM yesterday.

Location: The roundabout at the head of Houzhu Bridge in the Taiwan Merchant Investment Zone.

Event Record: He, trapped under the car wheel, furrowed his brows, endured the excruciating pain, his body unable to move, but his eyes kept staring at the woman about three meters ahead, crushed under the rear wheel of the car. That was his wife. With an increasingly weak voice, he called out to the traffic police and firefighters on the scene: "Save my wife first," "Save my wife first"... However, until he was lifted onto the stretcher, he still didn't know that his wife had already passed away. He didn't know that despite the short distance between them, they were now worlds apart.

[Rescue Scene] From start to finish, he was constantly worried about his wife.

At the accident site, a cement mixer truck with license plate number Min C71565 was parked in the middle of the road. Its left front wheel was pressing down on a man and a motorcycle, while its left rear wheel was pressing down on a woman. The man's head was facing the rear of the car, and he was holding a woven bag in his arms. From his perspective, he could clearly see the woman about three meters away. That was his wife; half of her body had been caught under the wheel.

At around 2:30 PM, Huian Fire Department arrived at the scene and quickly began the rescue operation. After multiple methods of rescue, including using jacks, proved ineffective, the fire department called in a large crane to prepare to lift the entire cement mixer from the front end to save the two people trapped. During the fire department's rescue efforts, the man repeatedly said in a weak voice, "Save my wife first," "Save my wife first"... Every three or four seconds, he would repeat this plea.

At 2:50 PM, the front of the cement mixer was lifted, and the man was quickly pulled out. His right leg bone was exposed and bent in the opposite direction, indicating serious injuries. As he was lifted onto the stretcher and taken into the ambulance, the man still shouted, "Save my wife first." A few minutes later, the woman was rescued, but after examination by the on-site medical staff, she was declared dead.

[Behind the Accident] The 21-year-old son pounded his chest wildly in grief.

After the traffic police conducted an on-site investigation, they speculated that the motorcycle may have been traveling towards the coastal expressway at the bridgehead roundabout when it was hit by the cement mixer coming from behind, causing both people to be dragged under the vehicle.

During the rescue process, a young man rushed to the scene in an agitated state. Upon seeing the woman trapped under the car wheel, he suddenly started pounding his chest wildly. After the trapped man was rescued, this young man also followed the ambulance and left. Afterwards, relatives and neighbors of the deceased and injured gradually arrived at the scene.

According to the sister of the injured man, the couple involved in the accident were her second brother and sister-in-law. Her second brother's surname is Lin, and her sister-in-law's surname is Zhuang. Both are 47 years old and are from Luoyang, Huian. She said that the couple usually worked in Quanzhou. Yesterday, after work, they rode their motorcycle home. The emotionally charged young man earlier was their son, 21 years old, working locally in Huian. They also have a daughter working in Fuzhou. Due to the sudden nature of the incident, the family had not yet managed to inform her.

Currently, the Traffic Police Brigade of the Taiwan Merchant Investment Zone has intervened in the investigation of this case, and the driver responsible for the accident has been controlled by the police.

The ruthless wheels crushed the couple's way home.

The accident occurred at the roundabout of Houzhu Bridge in the Taiwan Merchant Investment Zone of Quanzhou, resulting in the immediate death of the wife.

By reporter Wu Dandan and Pan Deng with text and pictures.

At around 2 PM yesterday, at the roundabout of Houzhu Bridge in the Taiwan Merchant Investment Zone of Quanzhou, a collision occurred between a cement mixer truck and a two-wheeled motorcycle. A couple riding the two-wheeled motorcycle was thrown under the cement mixer truck. After an emergency brake, the cement mixer truck still ran over the male and female riders, leading to the immediate death of the woman and severe injuries to the man, who was rushed to the hospital.

The motorcycle got stuck under the wheel, saving his life.

"At the time, I heard the sound of braking and collision, so I quickly ran out to check, and the people were already crushed under the wheels," said auxiliary police officer Xiao Lin, who was on duty at the Houzhu Bridge security booth at the time. After seeing the accident, he quickly dialed 110 and 120.

At the accident site, cups, slippers, and other items were scattered all over the ground. There was a long braking mark on the road surface. Not far away, a cement mixer truck with obvious damage to its front was parked.

Under the left front wheel and rear wheel of the cement mixer truck, there was one person each. The left front wheel pressed down on a motorcycle and the legs of a man, while the woman under the rear wheel faced the ground and showed no signs of life.

To rescue the man who still had signs of life, the on-site traffic police brought in a crane to lift the front of the cement mixer truck, allowing the man with bloody legs to be pulled out. Subsequently, the woman was also pulled out.

Son too grieved to believe mother had passed.

As the on-site medical staff were temporarily bandaging the injured man, the families of the victims arrived at the accident scene. The son of the deceased could not accept the reality and rushed to the bottom of the wheel, trying desperately to pull out his mother. He was held back by several police officers on the scene, his grief unbearable.

The younger aunt of the deceased, who arrived later, told reporters that the injured person surnamed Lin, and the deceased surnamed Zhuang, were a married couple from Luoyang, Huian. Both were 47 years old and had one son and one daughter. Because taking a bus was inconvenient, the husband usually rode a motorcycle to take his wife from Huian to a construction site in Donghai, Quanzhou. Today, they finished work early and were heading home when this misfortune occurred. The husband suffered severe injuries, and the wife would never return home.

According to the hospital, the injured Mr. Lin was in critical condition upon arrival at the hospital.

Traffic congestion lasted nearly two hours at the accident site.

After the accident, due to the cement mixer truck occupying the main lane to the Taiwan Merchant Investment Zone, the section remained congested for a long time, with vehicles passing slowly. The congestion persisted for nearly two hours from the time of the accident until it was relieved.

Currently, the Traffic Police Brigade of the Taiwan Merchant Investment Zone has intervened in the investigation of this accident.
