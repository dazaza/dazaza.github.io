SEO998756: The Pros and Cons of Baidu's Face Swap Technology for SEO

by seo2012seo on 2012-02-16 14:16:21

As usual, I opened Baidu to search for some popular keywords and found that the search results have increased significantly compared to before. Upon closer inspection, it turns out that Baidu has changed its search result ranking system. It now not only prioritizes bidding users but also considers naturally ranked users. I am wondering whether this move by Baidu is beneficial or harmful.

Let's first look at the advantages:

1. Bridging the gap with SEO

In the past, as long as a keyword could be boosted through bidding, SEO experts were not confident in claiming they could get it on the first page because the first page was mostly dominated by paid rankings. Now, SEO experts can boldly say they can achieve the first page! (Of course, Baidu is still a company, and whether it will change its stance again needs to be carefully observed.)

2. Exposing false information

Recently, too many scammers have infiltrated Baidu's bidding system. They exploit the tendency of users who find information on the first page and do not bother to go to the second page, using Baidu's bidding system for fraud. However, now there are more non-bidding results, giving users more choices. I won't enumerate them all here, but I believe everyone has noticed.

Now let's look at the disadvantages:

1. When Baidu covers up its first face with its left hand, Google's first face becomes increasingly favored by SEOs. While Google continues to improve its algorithms while accepting SEO practices, Baidu currently lacks search engine optimization. Baidu still relies on backlinks and content filtering systems. This will inevitably lead to another wave of backlink craze on the Internet. Why do I say this? Currently, if a website has 50,000 backlinks and slightly disguises its content, it can obtain good rankings on Baidu. However, under SEM practices, this might not be true for Google.

2. Since rankings can be achieved through SEO methods, more and more companies may opt for SEO-based website promotion instead of bidding. There are two reasons: firstly, direct bidding costs are high; secondly, bidding does not conflict with natural rankings. Websites only need to participate in bidding when they don't have good rankings.

3. Everyone knows that Baidu's "Hot Zone" has always had mediocre sales performance due to user habits. The "Hot Zone" has never had significant effects. Often, Baidu has to incorporate some natural rankings into the "Hot Zone" search results. When both natural and bidding rankings appear on the first page simultaneously, it inadvertently raises the bar for the "Hot Zone," which already had low attention rates.

Some personal speculation: commercial search engines are quietly transitioning from relying solely on advertising revenue. I remember in 2004, we could solve Baidu ad accounts for just 400 RMB including server fees. From 2005 to 2007, the cost kept doubling. In some special industries, such as real estate, the opening account fee plus server costs could reach tens of thousands of RMB. This has become a bottleneck for grassroots users. With the introduction of Phoenix Nest, Baidu is also quietly launching its data research center. Data services provided by the data research start at 250,000 RMB. Baidu is finding new ways to compete.

In summary, for foreign trade SEO, this transformation by Baidu gives clear direction to the SEO industry and to itself. Baidu is no longer the commercial search engine that relied solely on bidding rankings to survive. While SEO practitioners applaud this change, they must also rethink their own positioning.
