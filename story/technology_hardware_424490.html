The hatching process of the egg mass is relatively slow. After 15-16 days, the egg grains form a kidney shape and start to crack in the middle, which is the first molting stage; after 20 days, the second molting forms a crescent shape, initially showing the larval morphology; at 35-40 days, the juvenile enters the third molting stage and can crawl up and down within the mother's embrace; after another 5-7 days, the juvenile's outer skin chitin gradually thickens, the body color turns yellowish-brown, living under the protection of the mother, after a month it can live independently away from the mother and forage on its own.

Breeding centipedes should pay attention to possible viral infections that centipedes themselves may encounter. On the other hand, breeders should prevent being stung and poisoned by centipedes. Each female centipede usually lays 20-60 eggs, and the smooth laying process takes about 2-3 hours. The egg grains stick together like peanuts, oval-shaped. The mother centipede guards the eggs for about 43 days. During this period, if disturbed or stimulated by external factors, it can easily become agitated and devour all the hatching egg masses, leading to hatching failure. Therefore, the quietness of the hatching pool environment must be absolutely maintained. Especially note that centipedes are oviparous animals. They usually mate after rain in March to June, and each spring and early summer, the egg grains gradually mature. Egg-laying starts in mid-June, with the peak egg-laying period occurring in early to mid-July [Huimin], continuing until early August. Before laying eggs, the mother's body becomes bloated, her abdomen close to the ground, moving slowly. For mothers showing signs of egg-laying, about 3-4 days before, use a bottomless canister with a diameter of about 10 cm to cover them one by one. Cover the canister opening with fiberboard or glass, effectively utilizing the breeding area, avoiding mutual interference and predation, facilitating smooth egg-laying in a quiet environment, and also making it easier to separate young centipedes from the mother (or into different pools).

Preventing centipedes from suffering from diseases: Due to poultry, rats, skinks, etc., eating centipedes, especially ants being the main natural enemy of centipedes, it is necessary to frequently observe whether there are any harmful insects inside and outside the centipede field, preventing these natural enemies from entering the pool and attacking the centipedes. To prevent ants from entering the pool, you can draw several circles around the pool with cockroach and ant powder pens, or mix one part camphor balls, one part vegetable oil, and five parts sawdust evenly, then often sprinkle a circle of this medicine powder around the exterior wall of the breeding pool. Centipedes that are molting or laying eggs, as well as those in the hatching phase, have very weak resistance and are easily bitten to death by ants. Once ants enter the pool, quickly transfer the centipedes and scald the ants with boiling water.
