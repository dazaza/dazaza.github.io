The person I love the most has betrayed me, and the person closest to me has hurt me.

by lwf225 on 2009-10-10 11:41:36

The person I loved the most betrayed me, and the person closest to me hurt me. The world was plunged into darkness, and life on earth seemed no different from hell. You can have your paradise on earth; as for me, I will never drink the forgetfulness soup at the Naihe Bridge. I believe in reincarnation. In my next life, I must reclaim everything that is rightfully mine.

They say two people in love are sometimes like twins. I say that two people who are twins share the same tastes, the same discernment, and sometimes even fall in love with the same man, sharing the same affection.

I was born in a rural area. Since the moment I came into this world with my first cry, I was destined to be an ugly duckling, unloved and unwanted. Just because my sister was born three minutes before me, just because of one "good" word, my mother said that a "good" word means a girl comes before a boy. My mother hoped that her first child would be a girl. As she wished, she gave birth to twins, and my mother still regarded my sister as a sign of "good". But I am also a girl. Why does my mother believe that the first female child to arrive is a lifelong symbol of goodness? Therefore, my sister always receives more favor than me, and I can only secretly hide under the covers to listen to their laughter. Mother, do you know that while the character "good" has the female radical in front, not everything with the female radical in front is good. The character "slave" also has the female radical in front. Don't you, Mother, fear that my sister might become a slave all her life?

I hate, I hate all of you. Ever since I could understand, I knew I was the extra one in this family. You don't even consider me. I look exactly like my sister. If it weren't for the mole on my arm that my sister doesn't have, I think even you would find it hard to tell who's the sister and who's the younger sister. Even our voices are indistinguishable. So why do you treat me differently just because of those three minutes?

I see other twins dressed alike, eating the same, using the same things, but for us, from a young age, the clothes and toys Mom bought for my sister and me were different. You always give the best to my sister. At mealtime, you keep piling food onto her plate, filling it up, and while doing so, you call out to me: "Younger sister, eat more." It makes me want to throw up.

Whenever you leave me alone at home to watch the house, whenever I see Dad and Mom holding my sister's hand as they walk out the door, whenever I hear your laughter fading away at the corner of the stairs, I can't help but pick up a pen and stab an apple with all my might. I turn the apple into my sister, making it a prop to vent my inner dissatisfaction.

It is you who gave me life, shaping my solitary personality, turning an originally innocent life into a devil filled with darkness, cultivating a criminal.

I am lonely, I am cold. I know that my entire life can only depend on myself, and I yearn to escape this sea of suffering and distance myself from this family.

Fortunately, my academic performance has always been on par with my sister's, which is the only thing that gives me solace. After graduation, I was assigned to work at a rural bank, while my sister was assigned to teach in the city. When I saw my sister pack her luggage and board the special bus to school, I almost couldn't contain my excitement. I thought, finally, the pestilence is gone, leaving me as the only princess in this house. It's about time I get some attention, right?

My sister left, returning home at most once or twice a month, staying only for two days each time. And me, working in the countryside, I could go home every day after work. I thought my parents would focus their attention on me, but contrary to expectations, my parents would rather sit by the phone waiting for my sister's call than pay more attention to my life. I've completely given up hope. My parents will never share any of their love for my sister with me, and I will never replace my sister in their hearts.

At this time, there was a new male colleague at the bank. His appearance painted a rosy glow in my gray sky. Tall stature, kind eyes, and habitually two shallow dimples at the corner of his mouth. A single caring word, a single affectionate glance from him was enough to make me lose my head. Since he came to the bank, my face had gained a new expression, my life had gained a new vitality, and my heart had gained a new attachment.

They say when a man chases a woman, there's a wall between them; when a woman chases a man, there's only a sheet of paper separating them. That saying is absolutely true. I like him, so I will chase him. I crave love, so I must obtain his love.

I started my offensive. Knowing he likes dancing, yet being too shy to ask him directly because I'm a girl, I eventually came up with a great plan. After work, in the changing room, I sent him a text message with my phone: "Tao, are you free tonight? Teach me how to dance."

In no time, I received his reply: "Sure, if you're not afraid of me stepping on your feet, come on then, haha."

Hehe, I went crazy jumping around with my phone in my hands. He’s always so humorous. I invited him to the Jinma Dance Hall. For the first time, he held my waist, and I hooked my neck around his. At that moment, I had a thought - I wanted to hook onto him forever, making him belong solely to me.

"Tao, do you have a girlfriend?" I whispered, bringing my lips close to his ear.

"I do, what's wrong? Are you conducting a background check?" He laughed.

I cried, realizing he already had a girlfriend. I didn't want to steal someone else's love, nor did I want to be the third party: "No, I'm just curious. Why haven't I heard you mention her?"

"You never asked, I can't possibly shout all day that someone is already taken, or that someone's heart belongs to another, can I?" He gripped my hand tighter, excitedly.

"That's true, someone as excellent as you, how could you not have a girlfriend yet?" I felt very disappointed and wanted to push him away and run out of the dance floor.

Truly, I was disappointed, feeling utterly foolish for having made such a misstep on my first attempt to invite a guy. I tried hard, not to pursue, not to compete with others for him, but to stop thinking about him, to avoid speaking to him. However, the more I wanted to escape, the more trapped I felt. I could control my words and actions, but not my heart.

Summer vacation arrived, and my sister came back. Dad went fishing, and Mom slaughtered chickens to welcome my sister. At the dinner table, they kept calling out: "Ice, eat more. During the summer vacation, you need to nourish your body. Look at how you've tortured yourself this semester?"

The rice already in my mouth, I found myself unable to open my mouth. Staring straight at them, they remained oblivious to my expression, ignoring my existence. How could I possibly continue eating this meal? I excused myself, claiming a stomachache, and ran off without anyone pretending to care about me or asking if I was alright. Yes, it was an endless nightmare. I have been waiting, hoping for someone to take me away from this place of strife. But why is the world so vast, yet offers me no refuge? Dazedly, I stared at the sea and wept. At this moment, I wondered whether the seawater was saltier or my tears?

I called Tao, and within fifteen minutes, he rushed to the seaside. Seeing me, he anxiously asked: "Xia, what's wrong? Why are you alone here by the sea during dinner time?"

"Tao, don't ask why. I just want to lean on your shoulder for a bit. I'm so tired," I replied. Wordlessly, Tao sat down on the rock. I rested my head on his shoulder, closed my eyes, and let the sea breeze dry the tears on my face. Besides the sea breeze and the sound of the waves crashing against the rocks, Tao was silent. I didn't want to open my eyes, wishing time would stay frozen at this moment forever.

"Xia, can you tell me what happened? I want to share the burden with you."

Hearing Tao's voice, my tears involuntarily flowed again: "You already belong to someone else. Even if I told you, it wouldn't change this fact."

"Xia, I don't belong to anyone. My heart already belongs to you. Haven't you noticed? Last time, it was just a joke I played on you."

Wiping away my tears, I raised my head, looking at him in confusion. Memories of past moments floated in the air above the sea. Indeed, he had always paid attention to me. Every time I had something on my mind, he was always the first to notice. Every time I shed tears quietly, he always handed me tissues discreetly. Could these signs not indicate his concern for me?

Love, it turns out, is so simple. He silently gazed at me, and I foolishly looked back at him. We simply stared at each other.

"Xia, stand up, let's go," Tao pulled me to my feet. On the beach, Tao picked me up and spun me around continuously. We kept spinning until we both fell onto the sand, lying on our backs looking up at the night sky. Love erases all worries, and at this moment, the sweetness covered all the sorrow I brought with me.

On the beach, I told Tao everything about my background. Tao tightly embraced me and said, "Xia, it's okay, it will all pass. I will protect you and cherish you for the rest of my life, ensuring you never feel wronged again."

"Tao, is this true? If you truly love me, take me away from here, away from this family."

Without hesitation, Tao pulled me into the car and took me to where he lived. A two-bedroom apartment, simple inside but neatly arranged.

Tao spoke, "Xia, do you know why I've stayed single all this time? I didn't dare to love or accept love because love means giving happiness to the other person. In my current state, how could I possibly give happiness to anyone?"

Both of us are wanderers lost in the world. I tightly hugged Tao, listening to his story. Tao was born in the countryside, an only child. His father fell ill early on, and to treat him, Tao sold the house allocated by his unit. A few years later, his father passed away, leaving Tao to live with his elderly mother in the countryside. His mother now stays in the village, while Tao rents this place himself. Tao's greatest wish is to own his own house soon and bring his mother to live with him.

"Tao, as long as there is love, warmth can be found everywhere in the world. Don't lose heart, let's work together. I believe happiness is just around the corner."

Soon, I moved in with Tao, and we began living together. Every day, Tao cooked for me, told me fairy tales from his childhood, bathed me, and gave me countless surprises I had never experienced before, making me feel like a child again, a cherished child. I was no longer the unloved ugly duckling.

This period was the happiest time of my life, but why does happiness always slip away quietly from beside me?

After meeting Tao's mother once, she liked me very much and urged us to get married sooner. She even took off the only ring she wore on her hand and gave it to me, saying she had decided that I would be her only daughter-in-law for the rest of her life. Of course, I also wanted to get married as soon as possible. Tao desired it even more, after all, he was already in his thirties. But the house became a stumbling block in our marriage.

Tao said he wanted to try his luck and buy stocks. Indeed, most of the colleagues at the bank were trading stocks, and that year, the stock market was doing well. Tao and I pooled all our savings, which amounted to over 20,000 yuan, and immediately invested it all in Shenzhen stocks. It was our first time buying stocks, and we were very nervous. The stock we bought rose quickly, and we hastily sold it, earning several thousand yuan.

Having tasted the thrill of a successful challenge, we quickly took the second step. We started researching online and consulting old experts at the bank. Haste makes waste, and indeed, we were both too eager, wanting to seize the opportunity. The second stock became the weapon that sent me to prison. To buy a house sooner, I secretly embezzled public funds from the current account. I thought it wouldn't take long to replenish the funds.

Who could have known that it would be a total loss, amounting to over 100,000 yuan. All this I did secretly, Tao had no idea. But at the moment I was arrested at the bank, he could only stare blankly at me...

Prison was hell. In that dark place, I endured all kinds of humiliation. Fellow inmates bullied me, the drill sergeants beat me, everyone was like a ferocious demoness. I preferred death to life, gazing through the iron window at the autumn water.

After months of interrogation, Tao came to see me. Through the glass, I could clearly see Tao's tears.

"Xia, it's my fault. If it weren't for me, you wouldn't have committed this crime."

"Go back! Don't be stupid, forget about me!" Although I loved him, I was fully aware that it was impossible for us to be together.

As he left, Tao said one sentence: "Xia, reform well, I'll wait for you. No matter how long, I'll wait for you to be my beautiful bride."

I was sentenced to six years. Six years, could a 33-year-old single man really wait for a criminal for six years? Possible? I kept telling myself: Xia, don't be silly, it's impossible. Although I clearly knew it was impossible, I still remembered what Tao said before he left: reform well, wait for me.

A single sentence from Tao gave me motivation, a goal, and hope. For Tao, I had to strive. Whenever my family brought me good food, I couldn't bear to eat it and shared it with the sisters in the same room. Over time, they slowly accepted me as a friend. Prison wasn't much different from the outside world; to survive, one had to rely on oneself, on one's wits. Many things one didn't want to do still had to be done, had to be accepted. This is life. The kindness of fellow inmates gave me space to express myself. I taught lessons in the prison, being the highest educated among them. I diligently and sincerely completed every task assigned by the warden, encouraging the inmates.

Though the days dragged on endlessly, I still didn't give up, knowing that someone was waiting for me outside.

Everything ended, and everything began anew—this was rebirth. Due to my excellent behavior, I was released two years early.

At the prison gate, only my parents came to pick me up, and Tao was nowhere to be seen.

I didn't want to talk to my parents. If it weren't for them, I wouldn't be like this today.

Mom helped carry my luggage: "Xia, let's go home! Mom rented a house for you outside the village so you won't have to endure the villagers' gossip. Xia, find a job, live well, and start anew!"

Suddenly, I looked at Mom again. This was the first time in my life I heard a warm word from her. I gripped Mom's hand and followed them.

They rented me a two-bedroom apartment, neatly arranged, with everything ready.

I called Tao, but his phone was already disconnected. I asked Mom, "Mom, where's Tao? Why isn't he here to pick me up?"

"We didn't tell him you were getting out today. We'll inform him when we see him. Xia, what you need to focus on now isn't Tao, but finding a job for yourself," Dad said to me.

That's all I could do. I rested at home for a few days, still hearing nothing from Tao.

I started looking for a job. No matter what, one must continue surviving. I wouldn't return to the old house in the countryside. Fortunately, in the city, few people knew me, and no one would know my past. Mom had thought it through quite thoroughly; it seems she loves me, but I just didn't feel it.

After searching for many days, I still couldn't find a suitable job, wandering aimlessly on the streets. Tao, where are you? I miss you so much. You said you'd wait for me, but now that I'm out, I can't find you.

I couldn't suppress myself anymore. On the street, I hailed a taxi and headed straight for the rural bank.

At the entrance of the bank, I witnessed a romantic scene. My sister and Tao walked hand in hand out of the bank, then drove off together.

All this was too sudden. I couldn't believe my eyes, I refused to believe. There was no need for me to accept reality; Tao was mine, Tao had been waiting for me somewhere. As for what I saw, there was no need to verify it because, despite my sister falling for Tao, Tao was mine and could never belong to my sister.

Pushing open the door of the old house in the countryside, everyone was shocked speechless by my unexpected arrival—not just them, even I doubted if I had entered the wrong house. Under the old tree, my parents were teaching a child to walk, Tao was cooking in the kitchen, and my sister was folding clothes. Upon hearing the door open, everyone stopped what they were doing and stared at me dumbfounded, presenting a perfect picture of a happy family.

I told myself to stay calm, told myself not to act rashly, told myself this was all an illusion, trying to rationalize the lies with various excuses, but to no avail. In a frenzy, I ran around wildly, grabbing each of them by the collar and demanding why? Why? No one answered me. They all stared at me like dead people until Mom hugged me: "Xia, calm down and listen to me. It was my idea. Tao didn't want to. I wanted Tao to come home and be my son. You should understand, I didn't have a son, just you two sisters..."

"Enough! Not only do you not have a son, you only have one daughter, and I am not your daughter...," accompanied by a wail, I interrupted Mom. No, she is no longer my mom. There is no mother in this world who would allow such a thing.

Approaching my sister, I sneered at her and said: "This life, you only win me once, and that is because you won those three minutes, the three minutes you landed first. This life, you are only fit to be a slave, a tool for our parents, a servant to take care of my husband. Tao loves your body because you look exactly like me. You are merely a substitute, just my shadow. And my soul will forever live in Tao's heart, and Tao's soul will forever accompany me. Tao will live his whole life in guilt and remorse, and so will you. If you base your happiness on someone else's pain, exchanging it for blood and tears, can that still be called happiness?"

Laughing triumphantly, I looked up at the sky and laughed long and loud. This is my home, my family, my lover, the demons who pushed me into the abyss, causing me eternal damnation.

Everything is over. Standing on a high rock by the sea, listening to the sound of the waves crashing against the rocks, it is so beautiful. Let me turn into a seagull, freely flying above the vast sea, letting this lonely soul forever accompany the sea.
