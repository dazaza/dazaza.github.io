 【Daily Focus: Guo Tai-ming Quietly Enters Real Estate】

The world's largest contract manufacturer Foxconn could be transitioning from the thin-margin contract manufacturing business to the lucrative real estate sector. According to media reports, Guo Tai-ming has long intended to establish a development company, with Hong Kong Xuan Sheng Investment Co., Ltd., headquartered in Shenzhen, serving as Foxconn's real estate platform, registered capital being 3 billion HKD. From existing projects, most of Foxconn's developments rely on the location advantages of science parks, such as Chongqing, where on August 4, 2009, Foxconn Technology Group signed a project with the Chongqing municipal government for a notebook factory producing 20 million units, and also planned to invest 10 billion yuan in developing commercial real estate in Chongqing.

《Foxconn's Investment in Real Estate Goes Beyond "Minor Ventures"》

Analysts say that Foxconn's investment in real estate goes far beyond "minor ventures", establishing a real estate company and piloting in central and western regions. The only astute aspect of Guo Tai-ming's strategy is that all his land acquisitions rarely go through the "bidding" process. From Xuan Sheng Investment's targets, Chengdu and Langfang could be their next goals. What remains puzzling is that during the shareholders' meeting on June 8 this year, when shareholders suggested Foxconn invest in real estate and criticized Guo Tai-ming for losing out on billions annually by not investing in real estate, Guo still clearly stated that he would not invest in real estate but remain rooted in manufacturing.

《This is Essentially Doing 2.5 Generation Industry》

China Property Information Group analyst Jianxiong said that wherever Foxconn goes, it can not only boost local secondary industry development and solve employment issues but also promote tertiary industry development. Therefore, local governments are willing to offer land at low premiums. Using its industrial advantage to acquire land for real estate development is both easy and relatively cost-effective. "Foxconn headquarters building, doing commercial projects is more realistic, partly self-used, partly rented out, can rent to suppliers who are willing to work alongside them, making business easier. This model is what many companies are doing in the 2.5 generation industry."

【Commentary】

《Why Are Zhejiang Entrepreneurs the Main Force Behind China's Third Wave of Emigration?》

Although official statistics have not provided accurate numbers, according to some industry estimates, at least 1,500 people from Zhejiang successfully emigrate each year, increasing at an annual rate of 10% to 20%. Among these individuals, most fill in their occupation as businessmen. Behind this conservative figure, many more are eager to try.

Zhuo Yongliang, Director of the Zhejiang Reform and Development Research Institute: Objectively viewing the phenomenon of emigration is also a sign of social progress. If emigration is merely due to business activities or educational reasons, it is not enough to cause concern within society. What we need to pay attention to is the large number of investment emigrations caused by anxiety over the domestic investment environment or regulatory policies. For investment-related emigrations, we need to guide and optimize them.

Ma Jinlong, a well-known economist from Wenzhou: Many people still have vivid memories of the planned economy era, inevitably causing them some worries. Current anti-rich sentiments online and in society also lead these entrepreneurs to consider emigrating.

Jia Xijin, Associate Professor at Tsinghua University's School of Public Policy and Management: The flow of population is a very normal natural selection phenomenon, but it certainly reflects the social development environment. Regardless of the type of emigration, everyone essentially has only one goal: a better life. The appeal of emigrating overseas mainly lies in the openness, fairness, and stability of institutions. For knowledge elites, developed societies provide more fair opportunities; for wealth elites, they better satisfy their expectations for stability.

《Institutions Predict RMB Appreciation Could Prompt Finished Oil Price Decline》

Some institutions predict that if the RMB appreciates 3% within the year, China could save at least 18.7 billion yuan in oil import funds. A senior executive at Zhenhai Refining & Chemical stated that after the currency reform, if the RMB appreciates, the imported crude oil price would decrease under unchanged crude oil prices, reducing domestic refineries' import costs. Costs and pricing for gasoline, diesel, petrochemicals, etc., would also fall. Liang Kaishun, researcher at Enno Research, calculated theoretically that for every 0.1 yuan appreciation of RMB against USD, domestic gasoline and diesel prices would drop by 77 yuan.

《Zhejiang Foreign Trade Accelerates Transformation; Imports and Exports Increased by 41.3% in the First Five Months》

According to the Department of Commerce, in the first five months of this year, Zhejiang’s foreign trade has shown a sustained restorative growth trend, achieving a total import and export value of $94.75 billion, growing by 41.3% year-on-year. Of this, exports amounted to $65.54 billion, increasing by 37.1%; imports were $29.21 billion, increasing by 51.8%.
