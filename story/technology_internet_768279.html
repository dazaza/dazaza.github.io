The report claims that Microsoft's WP system has surpassed iOS in the Italian market.

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 19:59:34

For Microsoft's plan to develop its mobile business, a latest report released recently brought a piece of good news.

According to this report issued by market research agency Kantar Worldpanel ComTech, in the three months ended September 2013, Windows Phone had surpassed Apple's iOS operating system in the Italian market. The report showed that currently, the share of Windows Phones in the smartphone sales markets in the five major European countries - the UK, Germany, France, Italy, and Spain - had exceeded 10%.

The report stated that the driving force behind this progress made by Windows Phone in the European market was "almost entirely" from Nokia phone sales, which continued to grow rapidly in Europe. In the three months ended August this year, the market share of smartphones based on the Windows Phone operating system in these five countries was 9.2%, meaning that sales volume had increased by about one percentage point over the past month.

In addition, Nokia's Windows Phone phones were also showing signs of growth in emerging markets, especially in Latin America. Kantar pointed out that for many years, Nokia had held a dominant position in the Latin American market, where most consumers still owned Nokia feature phones today. Therefore, it was a reasonable guess that these consumers' next move would be to upgrade to entry-level Lumia smartphones.

In the UK market, smartphones based on the Windows Phone operating system accounted for 11.4% of the market, but Android still dominated with a staggering 58.4% share; meanwhile, the share of iOS had dropped by 1 percentage point to 27%. However, Kantar pointed out that due to Apple's launch of new iPhone models, it was expected that iPhone's share in the UK market would see a "strong" rebound during the holiday shopping season.

However, in the Chinese market, local brands still maintained an advantage over overseas brands. The report showed that in the three months ended September, the share of Android smartphones in the Chinese market reached an astonishing 81.1%, increasing by 14.6 percentage points compared to the same period last year; during the same period, the combined share of domestic manufacturers in China's smartphone sales market reached 44%, up by 14 percentage points compared to the same period last year.

Kantar pointed out that in Chinese cities other than first-tier cities, smartphones produced by local manufacturers such as Huawei, Xiaomi, Lenovo, and Coolpad were particularly popular because these phones generally offered better cost-performance ratios compared to global brands.

Dominic Sunnebo, head of strategic observation at Kantar, pointed out: "Chinese consumers are ready to invest heavily in smartphones, and they are willing to spend up to 70% of their monthly salary to buy new phones. Due to such a large investment, Chinese consumers want the best cost-performance ratio, so they are increasingly inclined to choose high-specification local brands rather than low-specification global brands."
