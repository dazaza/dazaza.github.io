The Slight Pain in the Sky

When all the places in my heart are occupied, it is just like the whole forest losing its little birds and becoming less colorful instantly.

When I looked up to the sky, I felt dizzy, as if the Earth was playing tricks on me and threw me heavily onto the ground.

A person must get up even by crawling. In this world, no one will care about anyone else more. They will only step on you hard when you're down.

Don't worry about who's right or wrong. Keep your heart calm as still water, and then you'll find your own madness. I'm not an ant, and I don't want to be an ant. I want to be strong, I must be strong, and no one can stop me. Then, I couldn't control my steps. The slight pain in the sky, with its salty taste, made me feel disgusted.

The stories of missing someone will always become memories, so don't hurt yourself foolishly anymore. It has passed, so don't think about it again. Tomorrow, the sun will rise as usual.

It just hurts to look at it too directly. There's a slight pain buried in the brown pupils, unknown to anyone, unknown to anyone.

I have been used to the days accompanied by sounds, and I cannot adapt to the soundless world. That's a barren land for a single person, a world without human traces, where colors lose their original authenticity during this time.

I don't want to go back to the past, but I still recall the past.

I am really a contradictory animal. Different thoughts always lead to different endings. I never want to let myself go, and I keep living in confinement, unable to free myself. All my thoughts are drowned under countless persuasions. I don't want to live without meaning. I just want to put my dream into a message bottle and let it drift far away. Then, I can continue my dream for that humble promise, letting my heart bear the pain it should endure and all the memories of myself in this summer.

On that day, when I looked at a dog we raised at home, I realized that he was also depressed and sad, with eyes full of characters that common people couldn't read. Suddenly, I felt as if I had carried very heavy stones on my back, unable to let go or continue. Melancholy lingered halfway up the mountain, and I groaned helplessly, having to arch my back and persevere once again. The road was so long that I didn't want to continue walking. When I looked back, I saw a narrow and quiet path that couldn't be narrower. At that moment, I wished so much that the dog would follow me, giving me a sense of security. In that way, maybe I wouldn't have lost too much, and maybe I would have gained different harvests.

I don't want to breathe the air of the slightly painful sky again. I want to return to that brilliant night, even if it's just a night. I would still be happy and gain small satisfaction because, during that time, I could forget all worldly disturbances, not wanting to face them, and it would be fine.

After turning around and standing on the grassland where the breeze blew, the smell I would inhale would no longer be the slight pain in the sky.
