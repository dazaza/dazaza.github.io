The travelogue of Huangshan Mountain has joy in its bitterness.

by jj0505 on 2012-03-07 14:49:02

I have long heard that Mount Huangshan is the culmination of all the famous mountains in China. It has the grandeur of Mount Tai, the precipitousness of Mount Hua, the elegance of Mount Emei, and the misty clouds of Mount Heng. It has always been known as "the greatest wonder mountain in the world" and "a fairyland on earth." Thus, my fascination with Mount Huangshan grew stronger day by day. Finally, last summer vacation, six of my friends and I decided to visit Mount Huangshan.

With simple luggage packed, we set off. After a night's train ride, we arrived at Huangshan City. Without resting from our journey's fatigue, we joined a tour group, boarded a bus, and eagerly looked forward to unveiling the mysterious veil of Mount Huangshan.

The car sped along the winding road, and we swayed left and right, forward and backward inside. Looking out the window, there were sheer cliffs below the road, making our hearts race. Fortunately, despite the scare, we arrived at our destination safely.

Since we had taken a night train, everyone was tired, so we chose to take the cable car up the mountain from Yun Gu Temple. After waiting for more than an hour, we finally boarded the cable car. Suspended high in the air, the higher we went, the more dangerous the terrain became, with strange rocks and peculiar pines surrounded by layers of peaks. It was truly beautiful, and it seemed no words could adequately describe such beauty. In front of this magnificent scenery, any praise would seem pale. After getting off the cable car, standing on Bai'e Ridge, looking far into the distance, the view was enchanting, making one feel as if they had reached paradise. I couldn't help but marvel at the wondrous power of nature. Here, every peak, every strange rock, every peculiar pine, every sea of clouds, all gave boundless imagination, making one fall in love forever. Beautiful Mount Huangshan, with her vast bosom, opened her passionate arms to welcome friends from all over the world. All the previous fatigue was blown away with the wind.

It was nearing noon when the guide asked us to rest at Baiyun Hotel. After lunch, after a short rest, everyone was spirited and followed the guide to the Beihai Scenic Area. Climbing step by step, what came into view were pine trees of various postures. The Huangshan pines are adaptable and grow anywhere—on rocks, between steep cliffs, and atop precipices. Everywhere, one could see tall pines standing straight, freely stretching their robust bodies amidst heaven and earth. The needles of Huangshan pines are short, dense, and dark green, with branches curving naturally and flat crowns, showing a kind of simplicity, resilience, and majesty. And as altitude and light change, each pine tree differs in posture and charm. Some stand tall like brave soldiers guarding Mount Huangshan, while others are graceful like young maidens after a bath gently stretching their waists. Who knew that pines could be so charming! Gazing afar, the forest resembles an ocean, and it seems only these ubiquitous pines are the true spirits of Mount Huangshan, the soul of Mount Huangshan.

Mount Huangshan has ten famous pines, each with its own style. Since ancient times, countless literati have written poems and songs about them. Among them, the two pines that left the deepest impression on me were Lianli Pine and Yingke Pine.

Lianli Pine, with one root and two trunks connected side by side until the top, reminded one of the ancient love quatrain: "In the sky, may we be birds flying wing to wing; on the ground, may we be branches intertwined." Legend has it that this pine is the reincarnation of Emperor Xuanzong of Tang and Consort Yang, witnessing worldly love. Couples vie to take pictures here as a keepsake, and I stood quietly under the tree, silently making a wish for love, hoping all lovers can find eternal love!

Visiting Mount Huangshan, one must not miss Yingke Pine. Yingke Pine grows leaning against a cliff, with a straight trunk, and the branch extending outward displays incredible grace, as if opening its warm embrace to welcome guests from all directions. Tourists took out their cameras and snapped photos from different angles, trying to capture the demeanor of the most famous pine in the world and make it last forever. We also managed to take a photo amidst the throngs of people under Yingke Pine.

After touring the Beihai Forest Area, it was almost dusk. With sore legs, we returned to the hotel to rest. Originally, we planned to get up early the next morning to watch the sunrise, but due to weather reasons, we missed the spectacular sight of the sunrise, which was quite regrettable.

On the second morning, after breakfast, we set off towards the highest peak of Mount Huangshan - Lotus Peak. Lotus Peak is renowned for its steepness, standing at an altitude of 1873 meters. From afar, it looks like a blooming lotus flower, hence its name. Along the way, the breathtaking scenery was endless. After climbing the Hundred Steps Cloud Ladder, we reached the path to Lotus Peak. This path was so narrow that most parts could only accommodate one person. The higher we climbed, the steeper the path became, with deep valleys or towering cliffs beside us. After nearly an hour of climbing, we were just a step away from the summit. The remaining five-meter path had steps at an angle close to ninety degrees. I simply held onto the ladder with both hands, using all four limbs, fully embodying the essence of "mountain climbing." Upon reaching the top of Lotus Peak, there stood a stone tablet inscribed with "Lotus Apex." Holding onto the railing, I rushed to the peak of Mount Huangshan, feeling as if I were among the clouds. Looking far into the distance, the peaks gathered together, the cliffs stretched thousands of feet, rising from the ground to support the sky, majestic and towering. The pines competed for beauty on the cliffs, the strange rocks vied for charm on the peaks, the clouds spread through the valleys, and the colorful lights flowed on the rock walls. The beauty of nature converged here and ascended here. In front of Mount Huangshan, time and space seemed small, and history seemed ordinary. Mount Huangshan deserves to be the pride of nature, leading the trend of the world's most wondrous mountains.

As the saying goes, "It's easy to climb up but hard to climb down." Coming down from Lotus Peak did require some courage. Looking down, the deep valleys on both sides seemed ready to pull you in, making one shiver with fear. In front of us descending the mountain was a mother and son, the boy around eleven or twelve years old. Perhaps suffering from acrophobia, the boy tightly gripped the railing, his legs trembling, his face pale with fright. No amount of encouragement from his mother helped. Seeing this, our group began to encourage the boy. Leveraging our teaching skills, our encouraging words were very infectious, and the boy gradually calmed down. He held onto the railing, facing inward, buttocks outward, and slowly descended the steepest section step by step. His mother repeatedly thanked us. Actually, meeting someone is a kind of fate. In this natural beauty, it feels like everyone around is a long-time friend. A smile, a glance, and understanding is mutual. Communication can be this simple.

The strange rocks of Mount Huangshan are one of the four wonders of the mountain. The ingeniously formed rocks rise competitively, like countless pearls scattered by heaven, vividly resembling myriad forms. Monkeys Watching the Sea, Child Worshiping the Bodhisattva Guanyin, Immortal Drying Boots, Dream Pen Producing Flowers, Flying Stone, etc., all make one marvel at the miraculous craftsmanship of nature.

Flying Stone is the first strange stone of Mount Huangshan. At first sight, it is truly amazing—a several-meter-high boulder stands on a cliff, completely unconnected to the mountain body, as if it flew in from outer space. Therefore, people have given it many bizarre legends, the most divine being that it fell from Nuwa's patching of the sky. Perhaps because of the legend, the Flying Stone seems endowed with spirit. After passing three or four peaks, looking back at the Flying Stone from Qishi Peak, it resembles a peach bitten by a monkey, round and translucent. If you have watched the TV series "Dream of Red Mansions," you will surely remember the otherworldly stone in the opening sequence—it was filmed based on the "Flying Stone" of Mount Huangshan. It is said that touching the Flying Stone three times brings good fortune, and four times brings romance, so many tourists flock to touch it devoutly and silently make wishes.

It has been a year since returning from Mount Huangshan. I sincerely hope that in the future, I will have another chance to climb Mount Huangshan again and appreciate its unique beauty.
