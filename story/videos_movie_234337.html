Introduction to the plot of "Forever and Ever Continued":

The 4th part of the long-running series "Forever and Ever" will start airing on CCTV-8 at 5 PM every day starting from April 6, 2009, with two episodes broadcast consecutively in the Youth Drama Garden. CCTV's "Forever and Ever Continued" follows the storyline from Part 3, consisting of 168 episodes, continuing from the original version of 280 episodes aired by Formosa Television.

The first 168 episodes broadcast on CCTV mainly describe the story of the protagonist Wang Shengtian striving to build his fortune from scratch, intertwined with his emotional entanglements with three women. Director Wang Wei mentioned that the focus of the second season’s 160 episodes shifts to the second generation of the main characters. As time progresses, Wang Shengtian and his younger brother Shengzhi shift their businesses towards electronics and engage in trade with Japanese businessmen, once again becoming commercial rivals. Meanwhile, Tianzhu, the son of Shengtian and Lizhu, has grown up but ends up helping outsiders scheme against his own father. Xuelian, the wife of Lin Dazhong, died after falling off a cliff in the first season, which surprised many viewers. Wang Wei explained that this was because Han Yu, who played Xuelian, had to shoot another drama, so this plot twist was designed. However, due to the audience's fondness for Han Yu, she was requested to be "brought back to life." Therefore, after she finished shooting the other drama, a character named Shanni, who looks exactly like Xuelian, appeared in the drama. Wang Shengtian still harbors feelings for Xuelian, and Shanni's appearance stirs up ripples in Shengtian's heart. In summary, the second season not only features conspiracies and calculations in the business world but also explores brotherly affection, father-son relationships, and mother-in-law-daughter-in-law dynamics, making it even more captivating than the first season.

CCTV Film and Television Department Director Wang Guohui stated that prior to this, CCTV had never introduced such a long-running TV series as "Forever and Ever," and initially, there wasn't much confidence in its ratings. Unexpectedly, a drama that started at 5 PM reached a peak viewership rate of 2.23%, surpassing some prime-time dramas. Moreover, the audience's demand for urgent introduction was very high, with the editorial office and film department receiving numerous calls daily. After fully considering the audience's opinions, they quickly introduced another 160 episodes after the first season ended, bringing the total number of episodes of "Forever and Ever" broadcasted to 328. Depending on the viewing ratings, the remaining episodes have a significant possibility of being aired subsequently. Viewers will be able to watch the complete "Forever and Ever," while CCTV also sets a new record as the 526-episode "Forever and Ever" surpasses the Korean drama "Mermaid Lady" to become the longest-running drama. Additionally, "Forever and Ever" enriches CCTV's variety of dramas during the Spring Festival period. Zhao Benshan and Fan Wei starred in "Rural Love 2," which aired on February 9 in the "One Yellow" slot, a rural comedy featuring China's most popular comedians, perfectly fitting the festive atmosphere. "Eight Yellow" aired "Women Flowers" starring Qin Hailu and Liu Tao, an ancient costume drama, while "Forever and Ever," with its tumultuous character fates, is gripping and pairs well with "Rural Love 2," providing audiences with different choices.
