Who is responsible for Picasso's digital oil painting?

I saw a photo online, it was the ceremony of central government organs granting plaques to "special supply" units. I have long heard about the existence of "special supply". In the past, it was exclusively for national leaders, but now it has expanded to all state organs. This photo clearly confirms this matter. It's hard to say whether this situation should make people feel sad or happy.

"Special supply" is a remnant of feudal imperial power. During the feudal dynasty era, in order to ensure the absolute safety of the emperor's life, food ingredients supplied to the palace had clear origins and were strictly monitored through military control measures, forming the "special supply" system. At the same time, local officials, in order to show loyalty to the emperor, would offer the best local products to the emperor, such as the finest rice, fruits, wine, rare birds, and exotic animals. Thus, these items became "tributes," specially provided for the emperor. After the founding of New China, this tradition continued, with party and state leaders enjoying "special supply" like emperors and high-ranking ministers. Today, this "special supply" has further expanded, covering all government agencies.

In today's context, "special supply" has another meaning: in the current environment where counterfeit and substandard—even toxic—food products are rampant, government agencies use their power as a form of "self-protection." Nowadays, not only government agencies, but even departments with a bit of authority have adopted various measures. It’s rumored that tax departments in various districts of Beijing rent hundreds of acres of land in the suburbs, hiring dedicated personnel to produce "green vegetables" specifically for their own units. In reality, provincial and local governments with power follow suit, implementing different forms of "special supply."

All levels of government departments with power know that food safety issues exist and that food is unsafe. Therefore, they use their power to "self-protect," using tax revenue paid by citizens to establish "special supply." But what about ordinary citizens? Who ensures the food safety of common people?

Why do people establish governments? One of the purposes of establishing governments is to protect the lives of every member of society, maintain social order, and combat various criminal activities. Counterfeit and substandard toxic foods are crimes that the government must crack down on—it is the government's responsibility because they directly endanger the health and lives of members of society. The fact that such counterfeit and substandard toxic foods not only appear but also spread widely is the result of negligence by governments at all levels. In such circumstances, instead of conscientiously fulfilling their duties and actively taking measures to eliminate problems, governments at all levels exploit their power to create "special supply," protecting themselves first while ignoring the health of the general public. So, why do the people pay taxes to support such a government?

Even worse, some local governments not only fail to hold themselves accountable for their negligence or rectify their mistakes, but also reduce punishment for companies and individuals who produce fake goods, refusing compensation to victims and even retaliating against those who expose the problems or seek justice. For example, in the Shanxi fake vaccine case, neither the fake vaccine manufacturers nor the Shaanxi epidemic prevention department were held accountable, but instead, the families of the victims who demanded accountability were arrested, forcing Bao Yueyang, the editor who exposed the case, to resign. In the Hebei Sanlu milk powder "melamine" incident, many affected families received no compensation, and Zhao Lianhai, an advocate for justice, was sentenced to prison on charges of "picking quarrels and provoking trouble." This is not just dereliction of duty or abuse of power, but also颠倒黑白 (turning black into white) and aiding wrongdoers, pushing the evil of power to its extreme. Don't talk about "serving the people"—there isn't even a shred of human conscience or moral integrity left.

Due to the repeated appearance of melamine, people have lost confidence in the safety of domestically produced milk powder. Many have no choice but to buy foreign-made milk powder. However, the most despicable act is that the General Administration of Customs, one of the state organs, is dissatisfied and wants to impose higher tariffs on imported milk powder, citing the need to protect domestic enterprises. What a bizarre way to protect domestic enterprises! The best way to protect domestic enterprises is to crack down on counterfeiting, improve product quality, and turn domestic food into "special supply" products so that everyone can use them with peace of mind. Instead, these government agencies fail to fulfill their responsibilities, choosing to block the people rather than address the real issues. What kind of logic is this? You know the food is unsafe, which is why you eat "special supply." What do the common people eat? Who provides "special supply" to the common people? Are the common people destined to eat "counterfeit, substandard, and toxic" food? Are your lives valuable, but the lives of the common people worthless? If domestic products were reliable, who would willingly spend more money on foreign products? Is it because the common people have too much money to spend?

They don't fulfill their required duties, only focusing on making money and exploiting the people. It's said that China's current fiscal revenue has already exceeded 7 trillion yuan, and exceeding 8 trillion within the year will definitely not be a problem. Moreover, the country is still studying new taxes like property tax and vehicle tax, and prices for water, electricity, and natural gas keep rising. They only know how to take money from the pockets of the people without doing anything for them, even harming the people. What good is such a government? Isn’t this like "paying money to hire a master"?

If we let the people vote, would they elect these officials? If we let the people vote, would they dare act this way?
