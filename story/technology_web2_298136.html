Essential preparations for aspiring small business owners

by ki582ganxi on 2009-12-07 18:11:06

Mr. Yang from Harmony Road called to consult:

"I used to work in some organizations, and over the years I've changed jobs many times. Now I want to be a 'small boss'. Currently, I have prepared some start-up capital, and the project is also roughly selected. But before officially 'starting', I always feel uneasy inside. So, Mr. Wu, what other preparations do I need to make?"

Working or starting one's own business has already been an old issue.

Many people want to fulfill their "boss dream", but indeed not everyone can be a good "boss". Some people only see the successful money-making "bosses", but don't see the failed, bankrupt bosses. I once heard Jack Ma say to the students who had just graduated from university that out of 100 entrepreneurs, 94 fall off the cliff without even having time to shout "ah". Five more struggle by grabbing the edge of the cliff, but eventually fall down too. Only one can survive. This is cruel, but it also includes the hardships of entrepreneurship within itself.

I consulted with successful or less successful entrepreneurs of all sizes on issues such as self-employment, and summarized that apart from start-up capital, you must also prepare the following "elements" if you want to be a "boss".

Stick to principles when doing business

A famous female singer once opened her own hotel. When guests and friends came, after chatting with her, this big sister immediately waved her hand and exempted the bill. This hotel didn't last long before it closed.

Therefore, if you want to start a business and become a boss, you must know how to invest and manage finances, understand accounts, and at least have some financial knowledge. You should be able to understand balance sheets and income statements.

People without principles, who are too nice, shouldn't be bosses. These people often have soft hearts, can't bear to refuse others, are full of human emotions, can't say "no", and this is a fatal weakness for doing business and managing employees.

Bosses need to grasp the degree of "human touch", stick to principles, talk business when doing business, and handle public affairs formally. You need a set of effective management systems, performance evaluations, and avoid hiring lazy or idle people. Strictly require your employees, guide them correctly, and fully tap into their potential. Be moderate and mature in character, so that whether in the market or managing subordinates, you can handle things with ease and confidence.

Some small bosses are busy all year round, but they don't even know whether they made a profit or a loss, and they certainly don't know how to do financial analysis to find problems in operations, where they lost money and where they earned it. They also don't understand other financial chains or cash flows. One day, when they can't turn over funds, their entrepreneurship fails.

Don't do something unfamiliar, and don't follow trends blindly

Mr. Liang originally did teaching software agency work and accumulated some money over the years. Later, he heard that online game agency could make a lot of money. Since Chen Tianqiao's "Legend" was not available for him to agent, he impulsively decided to agent an online game from another province that hadn't started running yet. Without knowing much about the game, he pre-paid 1 million yuan to become the provincial general agent. However, due to various reasons like content and technology, the game was delayed in being officially launched on the market. Mr. Liang wanted to withdraw, but it involved legal disputes, and this 1 million yuan became his "tuition fee".

"In different fields, there are different mountains." If you want to invest in a new industry as a small entrepreneur, you must understand the field, "know the depth of the water", have experience and talent in this area, be familiar with its operational procedures, and understand the development trends and risks in the operation process of this industry. Know your competitors, and know your target market share.

For example, the online gaming industry has a particularly short product cycle and high profits come with great risks.

For entrepreneurs, you need to know the life cycle of the industry and products in your target market. Don't follow the trend of currently popular industries and products because by the time you thoroughly research them, these industries and products may already be in the decline phase.

Make friends widely, prioritize integrity

Mr. Xue worked in a research institute in Hangzhou for more than ten years. After resigning and starting his own business, his original customers quickly gathered around him. In less than two years, Mr. Xue's company's annual total revenue from technology, industry, and trade exceeded 10 million yuan.

If you want to start your own business, you must have your own resources. This includes several aspects: in this industry, you are familiar with professional talents, whether in technology, business, or management, you deeply understand the operations; you have a good network of personal relationships, and many friends help you expand your business relations, solve problems, and deal with crises during the entrepreneurial period. Good interpersonal relationships are a huge wealth during the entrepreneurial period.

In today's increasingly competitive market environment, integrity must be prioritized in business operations. If you have the mentality of "making a quick buck and then leaving", you definitely won't achieve great success. You should treat your employees, customers, and banks lending money to you with sincerity... A good "credit" will play a multiplier effect on your career.
