Specific methods for grabbing rebounds

by gpgp6486 on 2009-11-22 13:34:33

Specific methods to grab a rebound: First is the timing of purchase. If you grab the rebound too early, it can easily cause being stuck; if you grab the rebound too late, you will often miss the fleeting buying price and lose the opportunity. When grabbing a rebound, one should adhere to the principle of not chasing high prices, because grabbing a rebound has certain uncertain risk factors. Blindly chasing high prices can easily put oneself in a passive situation. Buying some oversold stocks after a sharp fall at low prices can give oneself the initiative to enter and exit freely.

Secondly, the selling technique is important. Many investors are often deceived by the rapid rise of the rebound, thinking that a new round has started. Although there have been historical examples of rebounds eventually evolving into reversals, the probability of this happening is very small, often requiring the cooperation of many market environment factors. The vast majority of retaliatory rebounds will encounter resistance and retreat at a certain important position. When the rebound approaches the resistance level, one must be vigilant. Investors who have missed out should not recklessly chase the rise, and those who have made profits should liquidate their positions in time.

In addition, selecting stocks for grabbing a rebound is also very important. One should not choose blue-chip stocks or low-priced large-cap index stocks with investment value but sluggish stock characteristics. Instead, one should pay attention to choosing speculative stocks with smaller circulation and active stock characteristics. At the same time, one should not choose cold stocks with excessively sparse trading volumes, to avoid operational errors due to inconvenience of entering and exiting. Once an individual stock shows short-term speculative opportunities, one should pay attention to the trading volume. If the trading volume increases, it indicates that the main force has already moved, and following at this time has a higher chance of success.

At the same time, remembering the following principles is greatly beneficial for grabbing a rebound.

1. Estimate the risk-reward ratio. Before participating in a rebound, one should estimate the risk-reward ratio. When the risk of an individual stock's rebound far exceeds the reward, one should not easily grab the rebound. Only when the expected reward far exceeds the risk is it suitable to grab the rebound.

2. Do not participate in a rebound when the trend is unclear. When the downward trend of the stock market has already formed or is running in a standard downward trend channel, investors should not grab the rebound. Grabbing a rebound at this time is no different from picking chestnuts out of the fire, resulting in losses outweighing gains.

3. Set a specific stop-loss price and be prepared mentally for stop-loss. A rebound does not mean the market has completely turned strong. When participating in a rebound, one should adhere to the principle of safety first and profit second, and must set a stop-loss position. When the stock price reaches the predetermined price, it should be immediately and decisively sold.

4. Avoid full-position operation. In a weak market, grabbing a rebound should choose an appropriate capital investment ratio based on market environmental factors. It is inappropriate to rashly participate heavily or fully in a rebound. If misjudged, the loss will be enormous.

5. Do not set profit expectations. Grabbing a rebound should adapt flexibly according to market conditions. When the trend is favorable, even if the profit is substantial, one can continue to wait. When the upward trend of the rebound is blocked, even if the profit is meager or slightly trapped, one should resolutely clear the warehouse and sell, without letting profit expectations bind oneself.
