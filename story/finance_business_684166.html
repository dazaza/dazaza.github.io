A middle school student in Wuhan shows off his wealth on Weibo; most of the rich second generation are real estate investors.

by sspj8031 on 2012-02-29 18:26:12

The exposure of a student named "Daodao" from Wuhan No.4 Middle School sparked heated discussions online. In his Weibo posts, he showed off his luxurious lifestyle, including an Hermes handbag gifted by his pet dog and mocking the poor. Netizens commented that his flaunting of wealth makes Guo Meimei seem ashamed. (Yangcheng Evening News, February 8)

The topic of second-generation rich has always been sensitive in this era. According to surveys by authoritative institutions: among the post-80s second-hand home buyers, the average area of homes purchased fully-paid in Beijing's market is 124 square meters, nearly 50 square meters more than their peers who buy with loans, indicating that the wealth gap in young homebuyers is widening day by day. "In fact, it's a wealth competition between the parents behind the young people and their peers who are selling houses today."

According to statistics from Lianjia Real Estate Market Research Center, from 2007 to 2010, the average area of homes bought by post-80s showed a slow annual increase. In 2007, the average area of homes bought by young people was about 80 square meters, while in 2010, the average area for post-80s homebuyers was approximately 92 square meters, an increase of 15%, with an average annual growth rate of about 5.4%.

The real estate market and stock market have been able to create so many young millionaires at such a fast pace. Apart from their ability to seize opportunities, are there other issues worth our reflection? In recent years, both the real estate market and the stock market have been in unhealthy development states. For the real estate market, not only has the rapid rise in housing prices given speculators opportunities, but also some local governments' short-sightedness and mismanagement in developing the real estate industry have contributed to the wealth myth of speculators. Many speculators appear to be trading houses, but in essence, they use various improper means to speculate on plans and local government policies. Many speculators have seen their wealth multiply after colluding with local officials. As for the stock market, the big ups and downs before and after the share reform, as well as loopholes in listing, supervision, and information disclosure, have provided quite a few opportunities for speculators. The wealth myth of the stock market owes more to its disorder and weak supervision than to the shrewdness of stock traders.

Analyst Yue Zhang believes that from the data alone, the trend of increasing area conforms to normal market rules; "As the overall age of the post-80s group grows, their overall purchasing power and income will also increase. Thus, the purchasing power of the post-80s is gradually strengthening."

However, further investigation shows that this group of homebuyers is beginning to diverge, with the difference between the "second-generation rich" who pay in full and the "second-generation poor" who pay with loans reaching a historical high. This is mainly reflected in two aspects: on one hand, the average area of homes bought with loans has been decreasing year by year, shrinking from 83 square meters in 2007 to 72 square meters in 2010, a reduction of about 8%, with the current average total price being 1.3 million yuan. On the other hand, the average area of homes bought in full payment has been increasing year by year, rising sharply from 77 square meters in 2007 to 124 square meters in 2010, an increase of 61%, with the current average total price being 1.73 million yuan.

Zhang Yue analyzed that although the economic level of the "second-generation poor" has improved, their purchasing power has instead decreased due to the rapid rise in housing prices, leading to a reduction in the size of the homes they purchase. Meanwhile, the average area and total price of homes bought with one-time payments have significantly increased. "For example, a young person with five years of work experience earning 10,000 yuan per month would need to live frugally for more than 15 years to afford a 124-square-meter house priced at 15,000 yuan per square meter. Even if a couple buys together, they would need to live frugally for 7.5 years. Therefore, it is evident that most of these homebuyers rely heavily on their parents' support."
