Twitter made its debut on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday. Its IPO was valued at US$14.1 billion

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 18:06:00

Since last May, when the largest IPO of an American Internet company - Facebook - was launched, the market has been paying close attention to its competitor Twitter's IPO. However, unlike last year, currently the U.S. Internet companies are caught in an IPO frenzy, with the specter of a tech bubble reappearing. Will this cause Twitter's stock price to replay the scenario of breaking below its IPO price on the first day?

Yesterday (November 7), reporters from The Economic Information Daily learned that Bloomberg reported that the famous American social networking site Twitter finally set its IPO price at $26 per share on Wednesday (the 6th) and will officially be listed for trading on the New York Stock Exchange on Thursday (the 7th) Eastern Time, with the trading code TWTR. Calculated at $26 per share, Twitter's market value will reach $14.1 billion.

Many analysts believe that during the IPO process, Twitter carefully balanced the demands of investors, striving to avoid a runaway valuation, so its stock issuance price is already very close to its final closing price in non-public trading. To avoid the situation where Facebook broke below its IPO price on the first day of listing, Twitter chose to list on the New York Stock Exchange rather than the NASDAQ exchange, which caused technical chaos for Facebook.

Funding scale will reach $2.1 billion

It is understood that this time, Twitter's IPO will issue 70 million shares, raising $1.82 billion. If the over-allotment option of 10.5 million shares is included, the funding scale of this IPO will reach $2.1 billion, making it the second-largest IPO in the history of American Internet companies after Facebook.

Calculated at $26 per share, Twitter's market value will reach $14.1 billion, higher than recently-listed group-buying website Groupon and review website YELP, but lower than LinkedIn's $26.8 billion and Facebook's $120.2 billion market values.

In comparison to Facebook's tortuous pricing process during its IPO, Twitter's pricing kept rising, from the initial range of $17-$20 two weeks ago, to $23-$25 a week ago, and finally issued at a price higher than expected, indicating a high level of investor enthusiasm for technology enterprises.

Notably, although Twitter's financing scale is less than Facebook's, with the precedent of Facebook breaking below its IPO price, many investors are concerned. Currently, from the ratings given by Wall Street analysts, the general expectation is still relatively optimistic, believing that after listing, Twitter's price could reach $29-$54 per share.

American Redpoint Ventures principal and former Google product manager Tom Tunguz pointed out in his report that for a technology enterprise, the main factor determining its value and affecting its valuation is revenue, not profit. Therefore, Twitter's revenue situation is still somewhat attractive to investors.

From Twitter's revenue perspective, despite a loss of $133.9 million for the first nine months of this year, higher than last year's $70.7 million, the company's revenue growth has been very strong. In 2012, the company's total revenue tripled year-on-year to $318.9 million, and the revenue for the first nine months of this year has already exceeded this level, reaching $422.2 million, far higher than last year's $204.7 million.

From the perspective of Twitter's user numbers and sources, although the user growth rate dropped from 65% in the same period last year to 39% between September last year and September this year, showing some slowdown, its mobile terminal users have increased rapidly. In the third quarter of 2013, about three-quarters of active users accessed Twitter through mobile terminals, compared to 69% in the same period last year. Moreover, 70% of Twitter's advertising revenue comes from mobile clients, a higher proportion than Facebook, indicating that Twitter's position in the digital advertising market is becoming increasingly strong.

Five billionaires will emerge

Of course, every successful IPO produces a batch of new millionaires, and Twitter is no exception. With the determination of Twitter's IPO price, five billionaires who are founders and initial investors of Twitter will also emerge.

According to MarketWatch statistics, calculated at $26 per share, Jack Dorsey, Twitter Chairman and co-founder, aged 36, holds approximately 23.453 million shares, with a market value reaching $610 million.

Twitter's largest shareholder is another co-founder, Evan Williams, aged 41, holding approximately 56.9098 million shares, with a market value reaching $1.48 billion.

In addition, Dick Costolo, Twitter's current CEO, aged 49, who previously served as an executive at Google, holds approximately 7.675 million shares, with a market value reaching $200 million.

Peter Fenton, a general partner at Twitter, aged 41, is not only a shareholder of Twitter but also an early investor in eBay. Since 2009, he has invested in Twitter and currently holds approximately 31.5687 million shares of Twitter's stock. Calculated at $26 per share, Fenton's market value reaches $821 million.

As an investment-type shareholder, Fred Wilson, co-founder of Union Square Ventures, holds approximately 27.83899 million shares of stock, with a market value reaching $720 million. Other shareholders, including subsidiaries of Risvi Traverse holding 85.2 million shares, have a total market value reaching $2.21 billion.
