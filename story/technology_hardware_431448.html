Can polycystic ovary syndrome cause dysfunctional uterine bleeding?

by hdudkjsk on 2011-05-15 13:51:35

Menstrual disorders are problems commonly encountered by many women, including continuous shortening or extension of the cycle, increased bleeding, etc., all of which fall under dysfunctional uterine bleeding (DUB). Some people have asked whether polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) can cause DUB. Gynecological experts have introduced that the primary cause of DUB is PCOS. The main reason for DUB is PCOS. In gynecological endocrinology outpatient clinics, PCOS accounts for approximately 1/4 to 1/3 of the total number of outpatient visits.

This condition is mainly characterized by an increase in androgen levels in women, leading to issues such as excessive hair growth, acne, and obesity. Hirsutism is a typical sign of elevated androgen levels, with increased hair growth on the limbs. For Asians, a more specific characteristic is the presence of longer hairs around the areola or below the navel, even if there's only one, it has diagnostic significance. Acne is also caused by enhanced androgen activity, which stimulates sebum secretion from the sebaceous glands, leading to bacterial infections.

PCOS may also trigger infertility and endometrial cancer, while causing a series of metabolic diseases. The treatment of PCOS mainly involves drug therapy, but medication must be used under the guidance of a gynecological endocrinologist to prevent adverse reactions. Women who experience irregular menstruation or changes in physiological status are advised to get checked at the hospital as soon as possible, and not to mistakenly think it's just occasional menstrual disorder or physiological change, which could delay diagnosis and treatment.
