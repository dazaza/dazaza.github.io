"Silver Empire" comes to Shanghai, Zhang Tielin claims to "wrestle" with Hao Lei

by zhlbblgv80 on 2012-02-08 16:05:24

Zhang Tielin, Hao Lei: Director Conservative About Box Office

On August 9, director Yao Shuhua, along with actors Zhang Tielin and Hao Lei, arrived in Shanghai to promote their new film *Silver Empire*. In the movie, Zhang Tielin shares an intense romantic scene with Hao Lei. Zhang humorously referred to the intimate scene as "fighting on the bed." Additionally, when asked about box office expectations, Yao Shuhua candidly stated, "Amateurs look for excitement, while experts look for substance. We've already created a lot of buzz through all this promotion. As for the box office, I naturally hope as many people as possible will watch it." Zhang Tielin described the bed scene as "combat on the bed," adding that he was more concerned about her safety than any romance.

**Zhang Tielin calls the bed scene 'bed combat'**

In the film, Zhang Tielin and Hao Lei share a passionate "bed scene." However, Zhang Tielin doesn't consider it simply a "bed scene," explaining, "Rather than calling it a bed scene, it's more like a fight on the bed. I had a sharp weapon in my hand and was very worried about injuring her." When asked about his experience filming the scene with Hao Lei, he joked, "She was lying in bed, wrapped up like a mummy." Regarding whether he felt awkward shooting such scenes with younger actors, Zhang Tielin responded with a bemused expression, "I'm already an older actor, so why would I be embarrassed? What I really feared was accidentally hurting her with the weapon."

**Yao Shuhua conservative about box office**

As the summer blockbuster season draws to a close, many domestic films are rushing to release in order to grab the last piece of the "lucrative pie." When asked about how much box office revenue the beautifully packaged artistic work *Silver Empire* might generate, director Yao Shuhua was rather cautious: "Amateurs look for excitement, while experts look for substance. With all this promotion, we've already generated a lot of attention for the film. As for the box office, I naturally hope as many people as possible will watch it." On the other hand, Zhang Tielin expressed confidence, saying, "A good wine does not fear a narrow alley. I don't see many art films in the summer lineup, so ours should stand out and perform well."

**Jeremy Thomas optimistic about domestic box office**

On the same day, Jeremy Thomas, the producer of the Oscar-winning film *The Last Emperor* and also a producer of *Silver Empire*, attended the press conference. When asked about the film's overseas distribution prospects, he was very optimistic but emphasized that the key still lies in how *Silver Empire* performs in China: "If the film does well in China, foreign audiences will become interested in what kind of movie it is. After all, even Hollywood's bad movies wouldn't be popular in China, right?"
