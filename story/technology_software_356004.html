I don't want the allowance

There are people poorer than me

Liu Botao is the only student from Taipingba Township in Fengdu County who has been admitted to a key university. Classmates thought he was very frugal: he wore the same red jacket with sneakers all year round, and in winter, he wore the camouflage uniform issued during military training. He didn't buy a desk lamp, didn't use a schoolbag, and always went to class holding books...

But he always appeared confident and optimistic, making people overlook that he also needed help. During his first year, there was one financial aid spot for impoverished students in the class, which came with an 800 yuan subsidy. Everyone thought Liu Botao needed it the most, so they decided to give the spot to him. However, when he heard the counselor say another classmate had no money to live on, he refused the counselor's suggestion to split the prize money with that classmate and gave up his share as well. In fact, his family had already incurred a debt of 30,000 yuan for his education. Liu's mother, Wang Mingxiang, once asked her son, "Isn't there financial aid at school? Why haven't you received any?" Liu Botao would always say, "Mom, there are families poorer than ours in the class."

At his memorial service, class president Kou Sha visited Liu Botao's home for the first time. As a child who also grew up in the countryside, this home still shocked her: his father was bedridden and unable to walk, his brother had intellectual disabilities, and the entire household relied on his mother. The house was shared with others and contained only one table, one bed, and one pot. "Even his memorial altar was partly in someone else's home." The portrait in the altar was a black-and-white registration photo taken when he entered university, the only photo Wang Mingxiang could find after searching every corner of the house. Kou Sha and counselor Xue Wei Yi cried uncontrollably, feeling particularly sorry for him, "I've never seen a family in more difficult circumstances than his."

The hometown is beautiful

Walking on mountain roads is fun

Roommate Liu Kai remembered Liu Botao telling him, "Lying down in the forest of my hometown and sleeping, with sunlight filtering through the branches onto my body, is the most wonderful thing." Kou Sha said Liu Botao once casually chatted with her about how he saved 30 yuan in bus fare by walking four hours on mountain roads to go home, "and it was very enjoyable along the way."

Liu Botao once invited classmates to visit his home, but Kou Sha never had the chance until after Liu Botao's death. Liu's home was deep in the mountains, taking over five hours to drive from the main city, "some of the small roads were muddy and narrow, almost impossible to walk on."

In Liu Mom's eyes, Liu Botao was very considerate. It took a day of travel to return home from university, and each time he would bring a one-yuan bread and a bottle of water. But even this small bread, he would save half for his parents. He said that it was hard to eat such delicious bread in the mountains, and he wanted his parents to taste it too. And when he returned home, he always took the initiative to do the work in the fields, allowing his parents to rest for a while.

He always tried to contribute to the household expenses. Kou Sha said that after the holidays, Liu Botao always worked on construction sites to supplement the family income, mixing cement and carrying cables. Liu Botao once joked, saying that such a large bundle of cables made his legs feel weak when he carried it. But then he would casually add, "Working on construction sites earns more money, the food is good, and you can eat your fill."

Kou Sha said that not long ago, Liu's mother came to the school to collect awards for her son and kept muttering, "There's nothing special at home, I don't know how to thank everyone," so she brought a bag of fresh young corn from far away to give to the school leaders and those who helped them. "But with the mainstay of the family gone, what will this kind-hearted mother do?"
