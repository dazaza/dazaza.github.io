1. Person A went to buy cigarettes, which cost 29 yuan, but he didn't have any matches, so he said to the shopkeeper: "Could you give me a box of matches for free?" But the shopkeeper refused. Then person B went to buy cigarettes, also costing 29 yuan, and he didn't have matches either, so he said to the shopkeeper: "Can you give me a discount of one cent?" In the end, he used that one cent to buy a box of matches. This is the simplest form of psychological marginal effect. The first situation: the shop owner thinks he made money on one item but not on the other, making his profit satisfaction index 1. The second situation: the shop owner thinks he made money on both items, making his profit satisfaction index 2. Naturally, the psychological tendency leans towards the second option. Similarly, this psychology is also reflected in the trick of "buy one get one free," where customers think they don't have to pay for one item, thus feeling like they're making a profit, which is actually due to the psychological marginal effect. Insight: Changing an approach often yields unexpected results. Often, doing things differently leads to different outcomes. On the path of life, improving mental models and ways of thinking is crucial.

2. There was a little boy who went with his mother to a grocery store one day. The owner saw this cute child and opened a can of candy, inviting the boy to grab some candies himself. However, the boy made no move. After several invitations, the owner personally grabbed a big handful of candies and put them into the boy's pocket. When they got home, the mother curiously asked the boy why he didn't grab the candies himself and let the owner do it instead? The boy cleverly answered: "Because my hand is smaller! And the owner's hand is bigger, so he must be able to grab much more than I can!" Insight: This is a smart child. He knows his own limitations, and more importantly, he knows others are stronger than him. Relying on others appropriately when necessary is a kind of humility, as well as a kind of intelligence.

3. One day, American famous host Art Linkletter interviewed a little boy and asked him: "What do you want to be when you grow up?" The boy innocently replied: "Hmm...I want to be an airplane pilot!" Art Linkletter then asked: "If one day, your plane flies over the Pacific Ocean and all engines shut down, what will you do?" The boy thought for a moment: "I will first tell everyone on the plane to fasten their seat belts, then I'll put on my parachute and jump out." As the audience laughed uncontrollably, Art Linkletter continued to look at the boy, wondering if he was being clever. Unexpectedly, tears streamed down the boy's face, making Art Linkletter realize the boy's compassion could not be described in words. So Art Linkletter asked him: "Why do you want to do that?" The boy's answer revealed his sincere thoughts: "I'm going to get fuel, and I'll be back!!!" Insight: This is the art of listening. First, don't listen halfway through. Second, don't project your own meaning onto what others say. Learn to listen attentively, listen with heart, and humbly.

4. There were two monks who lived in temples on adjacent mountains. Between these two mountains was a stream, so every day these two monks would go down the mountain at the same time to fetch water from the stream. Over time, they became good friends. Time passed unnoticed in the daily act of fetching water for five years. Suddenly one day, the monk from the left mountain did not come down to fetch water. The monk from the right mountain thought: "He probably overslept." So he didn't mind. But the next day, the monk from the left mountain still did not come down to fetch water, nor did he the third day. A week passed, and still the same. Until after a month, the monk from the right mountain finally couldn't stand it anymore. He thought: "My friend might be sick, I should visit him and see if I can help." So he climbed up the left mountain to visit his old friend. When he arrived at the temple on the left mountain and saw his old friend, he was greatly surprised because his old friend was practicing Tai Chi in front of the temple, looking nothing like someone who hadn't drunk water for a month. Curious, he asked: "You haven't come down the mountain to fetch water for a month, can you really survive without drinking water?" The monk from the left mountain said: "Come, let me show you." So he led the monk from the right mountain to the backyard of the temple and pointed to a well: "For these five years, I've been digging this well whenever I finished my daily tasks. Even if sometimes I was busy, I dug as much as I could. Now finally I've dug out well water, so I don't need to go down the mountain to fetch water anymore. I can spend more time practicing Tai Chi that I like." Insight: No matter how much salary we earn in our company, that's just fetching water. But by using our spare time after work to dig our own well, cultivating another strength, in the future when we are old and cannot compete with the young in physical strength, we will still have water to drink, and we can drink leisurely.

5. Two people agreed to go up the mountain to find exquisite stones. Person A filled his basket full, while Person B had only one stone in his basket which he considered the most exquisite. Person A laughed at Person B: "Why do you pick only one?" Person B said: "There are many pretty stones, but I only need one exquisite one." Person A smiled silently. On the way down the mountain, Person A felt the burden getting heavier and heavier. Eventually, he had no choice but to continuously throw away the worst stones from his basket. By the time he reached the bottom of the mountain, his basket ended up having only one stone! Insight: There are many things in life worth holding on to, sometimes you need to learn to let go.

6. There was a toothpaste factory whose products were excellent and beautifully packaged, loved by customers. For ten consecutive years, its turnover increased annually by 10% to 20%. But in the eleventh year, the performance stagnated, and the same happened for the next two years. The manager convened a high-level meeting to discuss countermeasures. During the meeting, the president promised: whoever could come up with a solution to increase the company's performance would be rewarded 100,000 yuan. A young manager stood up and handed the president a note. After reading it, the president immediately signed a 100,000 yuan check to the young manager. The note read: Enlarge the current toothpaste opening by 1 millimeter. Consumers squeeze out the same length of toothpaste every morning; enlarging the opening by 1 millimeter means each consumer uses 1 millimeter more toothpaste, how much more consumption will there be daily! The company immediately changed the packaging. In the fourteenth year, the company's turnover increased by 32%. Insight: Facing changes in life, we often stick to past ways of thinking. Actually, if you expand your mindset by just 1 millimeter, you will see that all changes in life have a positive side, full of opportunities and challenges.

7. A turkey and a cow were chatting. The turkey said: "I hope to fly to the top of the tree, but I don't have the courage." The cow said: "Why don't you eat some of my dung? They are very nutritious." The turkey ate some dung and found it indeed gave him enough strength to fly to the first branch. The next day, the turkey ate even more dung and flew to the second branch. Two weeks later, the turkey proudly flew to the top of the tree, but soon, a farmer saw it and quickly shot it down from the tree. Insight: Cow dung luck may take you to the peak, but it won't keep you there.

8. A crow sat on a tree, doing nothing all day. A rabbit saw the crow and asked: "Can I be like you, doing nothing all day?" The crow said: "Of course, why not?" So the rabbit started resting on an open space under the tree. Suddenly, a fox appeared, jumped up, caught the rabbit, and swallowed it. Insight: If you want to do nothing, you must stand very high, extremely high.

9. A little bird flew south for winter. It was very cold, and the bird was almost frozen stiff. So it flew to a large open area, and a cow passed by there, dropping a pile of dung on the bird. The frozen bird lay in the pile of dung, feeling warm, gradually coming back to life. Lying comfortably warm, it began singing soon. A passing wild cat heard the sound, walked over to investigate, and quickly discovered the bird lying in the pile of dung. The cat pulled the bird out and ate it. Insight: Not everyone who poops on you is your enemy. Not everyone who pulls you out of the dung heap is your friend. Also, when you lie in the dung heap, it's best to keep your mouth shut.

10. Once upon a time, two hungry men received a gift from an elder: a fishing rod and a basket of fresh, large fish. One man chose the basket of fish, the other chose the fishing rod, and they parted ways. The man who got the fish built a fire right there and boiled the fish. He wolfed it down, not even tasting the flavor, and within moments, he had eaten all the fish and soup. Soon, he starved to death beside the empty fish basket. The other man carried the fishing rod and endured hunger, struggling step by step toward the sea. But when he finally saw the blue ocean not far away, his last bit of strength was exhausted, and he could only die with regret. Another two hungry men received the same gift from the elder: a fishing rod and a basket of fish. But they didn't part ways; instead, they agreed to find the sea together. Each time they cooked only one fish. After a long journey, they reached the sea. From then on, the two men began living by fishing. A few years later, they built houses, had families and children, and owned their own fishing boats, leading happy and healthy lives. Insight: A person who only looks at short-term gains gets only fleeting joy; a person with lofty goals must also face real-life situations. Only by combining ideals and reality organically can one become a successful person. Sometimes, a simple truth can offer profound life lessons.

11. One of Confucius' students was cooking porridge when he noticed something dirty fall into the pot. He quickly scooped it out with a ladle. Just as he was about to throw it away, he suddenly thought, "A bowl of porridge is hard-earned." So he ate it. Coincidentally, Confucius walked into the kitchen, mistaking what he saw as the student stealing food, and thus reprimanded the student responsible for cooking. After an explanation, everyone realized the truth. Confucius sighed deeply: "Even what I saw with my own eyes wasn't necessarily true, let alone hearsay." Insight: Selling business is an organized business, because there are many people, there are many personnel issues. We often hear confusing words, such as one company attacking another, which often confuses people and affects confidence. Therefore, finding out the truth, rather than easily believing rumors, is crucial to ensure the painstakingly built career does not collapse instantly.

12. A scholar was taking the imperial examination for the third time in the capital and stayed in a regular inn. Two days before the exam, he had three dreams. The first dream was planting cabbage on a wall, the second dream was raining while wearing a hat and holding an umbrella, and the third dream was lying with his beloved cousin, but back-to-back. These three dreams seemed to have some deep meaning, so the scholar hurried to find a fortune-teller to interpret them. The fortune-teller listened and slapped his thigh saying: "You'd better go home. Think about it, planting cabbage on a high wall isn't a waste of effort? Wearing a hat and holding an umbrella isn't redundant? Lying on the same bed with your cousin but back-to-back, isn't that hopeless?" The scholar, hearing this, lost heart and returned to the inn to pack his bags to go home. The innkeeper was very curious and asked: "Isn't the exam tomorrow? Why are you going home today?" The scholar explained, and the innkeeper laughed: "Oh, I can interpret dreams too. I think you should stay. Think about it, planting cabbage on a wall isn't high planting? Wearing a hat and holding an umbrella doesn't mean you're prepared for everything? Lying back-to-back with your cousin doesn't mean it's time for you to turn over?" The scholar, hearing this, felt more motivated and took the exam with renewed spirit, and surprisingly, he ranked third in the exam. Insight: Positive people are like the sun, wherever they shine, it becomes bright. Negative people are like the moon, waxing and waning. Thoughts determine our lives. Whatever thoughts we have, that's the kind of future we will have.

13. One day, the zookeepers found that the kangaroo had escaped from its cage. So they held a meeting and unanimously believed the cage was too low. So they decided to raise the height of the cage from the original 10 meters to 20 meters. The next day, they found the kangaroo was still outside. So they decided to raise the height again to 30 meters. Unbelievably, the next day, they saw the kangaroos were all still outside. So the keepers were highly alarmed and decided to raise the height of the cage to 100 meters. One day, the giraffe and a few kangaroos were chatting: "Do you think these people will continue raising the height of your cage?" The giraffe asked. "Hard to say," the kangaroo said: "If they keep forgetting to close the door!"

14. One night, an elderly couple entered a hotel very late. They wanted a room. The front desk clerk responded: "Sorry, our hotel is fully booked, there isn't a single room left." Seeing the tired expression of the elderly couple, the clerk didn't feel right sending them out in the middle of the night to find another place. And in such a small town, other hotels were probably also full and closed, leaving the tired couple stranded on the street? So the kind clerk led the elderly couple to a room and said: "It might not be the best, but this is the best I can do now." The elderly couple, seeing the clean and tidy room, happily stayed. The next day, when they came to the front desk to check out, the clerk told them: "No charge, because I just lent you my room for one night—wish you a pleasant journey!" It turned out the clerk spent the whole night working at the front desk. The elderly couple was deeply moved. The old man said: "Young man, you are the best hotel manager I've ever seen. You will be rewarded." The clerk smiled and said it was nothing. He sent the elderly couple out, then forgot about the whole incident. Unexpectedly, one day, the clerk received a letter. Inside was a one-way ticket to New York with a brief note, offering him another job. He flew to New York, followed the route indicated in the letter, and looked up to see a magnificent hotel standing there. Originally, the elderly couple he hosted that night months ago was a billionaire and his wife. The billionaire bought the hotel for the clerk, firmly believing he could manage it well. This is the legendary story of the first manager of the globally renowned Hilton Hotel.

15. Before a performance master went on stage, his disciple told him his shoelaces were loose. The master nodded gratefully, squatted down, and carefully tied them. When the disciple turned around, the master squatted again and untied his shoelaces. A bystander saw everything and asked in confusion: "Master, why do you untie your shoelaces again?" The master replied: "Because I am portraying a weary traveler, whose long journey has loosened his shoelaces. This detail can express his fatigue and exhaustion." "Then why didn't you directly tell your disciple?" "He could carefully notice my shoelaces were loose and kindly informed me, I must protect his enthusiasm and encourage him timely. As for why I untied the shoelaces, there will be more opportunities to teach him acting, it can be discussed next time." Insight: A person can only do one thing at a time, knowing how to prioritize is truly talented.

16. A man found a baby eagle in a high nest on a mountain peak. He brought it home and raised it in a chicken coop. This baby eagle pecked, played, and rested with chickens, thinking it was a chicken. As the eagle grew older, its feathers became fuller. The owner wanted to train it into a hunting eagle, but since it had always mixed with chickens, it no longer had the desire to fly. The owner tried various methods, all without effect. Finally, he took it to the top of the mountain and threw it out forcefully. The eagle fell like a stone, flailing wildly, until it finally learned to fly! Insight: Hardship calls for success.

17. After the rain, a spider struggled to climb up the broken web on the wall. Due to the wet wall, when it climbed to a certain height, it would fall down. It kept climbing up and falling down repeatedly... The first person saw it, sighed, and muttered to himself: "Isn't my life just like this spider? Busy all the time but gaining nothing." Thus, he became increasingly dispirited. The second person saw it and said: "This spider is so stupid, why doesn't it crawl around from the dry side nearby? I shouldn't be as foolish as it in the future." Thus, he became smarter. The third person saw it and was immediately inspired by the spider's persistent spirit despite repeated failures. Thus, he became stronger. Insight: Those with a successful mindset can discover the power of success everywhere.

18. An old man on a high-speed train accidentally dropped a newly bought shoe out of the window. People around him felt sorry for him. Unexpectedly, the old man immediately threw the other shoe out of the window as well. This action shocked everyone even more. The old man explained: "One shoe, no matter how expensive, is useless to me now. If someone can pick up a pair of shoes, maybe they can wear them!" Insight: Successful people know how to let go.

19. A large company was ready to hire a driver at a high salary. After multiple rounds of screening and exams, only three competitors with the best skills remained. The examiner asked them: "On the edge of a cliff, there's a piece of gold. How close do you think you can drive to the cliff without falling?" "Two meters," the first one said. "Half a meter," the second one confidently said. "I will keep as far away from the cliff as possible," the third one said. As a result, the company hired the third one. Insight: Don't wrestle with temptation, but stay as far away as possible.

20. An old monk traveled with a young monk. On the way, they encountered a river. They saw a woman wanting to cross but dared not. The old monk actively helped her cross by carrying her. After putting her down, the old monk continued traveling with the young monk. The young monk couldn't stop muttering: "How could the master carry a woman? Is it not against the rules?" Thinking about it all the way, he finally couldn't help asking: "Master, you broke the rules, how could you carry a woman?" The old monk sighed: "I have already put her down, but you haven't!" Insight: A gentleman is broad-minded, while a petty person is often worried. To maintain a healthy mindset, one must be able to let go of things.

21. A psychology professor visited a mental hospital to observe the lives of the patients. By the end of the day, he felt that these people were crazy and unpredictable, quite enlightening. Unexpectedly, when preparing to return, he found that someone had removed the tire of his car. "It must be one of those crazy people!" the professor angrily thought and started to replace the spare tire. Things got worse. The person who removed the tire had also taken off the screws. Without screws, the spare tire couldn't be mounted either! The professor was at a loss. While he was anxiously pacing around, a patient came bouncing along, singing an unknown happy song. He noticed the professor in trouble, stopped, and asked what happened. The professor reluctantly ignored him but politely told him anyway. The patient burst out laughing and said: "I have a solution!" He unscrewed one screw from each tire, thus obtaining three screws to mount the spare tire. The professor was amazed and grateful, asking curiously: "How did you come up with this idea?" The patient giggled and replied: "I'm crazy, but I'm not stupid!"
