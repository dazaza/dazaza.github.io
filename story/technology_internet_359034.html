 [Daily Focus: The World's Largest E-commerce Community]

"Ma Yun Teams Up with Masayoshi Son: Plotting the Asian E-commerce Community"

Recently, Ma Yun and Masayoshi Son, who have cooperated for ten years, have once again joined forces, announcing a strategic cooperation between Taobao under Alibaba Group and Yahoo Japan - Yahoo Japan will open a "China Mall" channel, while Taobao will open a "Tao Japan" channel. This means that Japanese merchants can sell their products to the Chinese market through "Tao Japan" without leaving the country, and Chinese merchants can also sell their products to Japan through the "China Mall". Masayoshi Son told reporters that after the cooperation between Taobao and Yahoo Japan, they will create the world's largest e-commerce community.

"China's E-commerce Will Surpass the U.S. in Five Years"

Masayoshi Son: Since the investment in Alibaba in 1999, our cooperation has exceeded ten years. Seven or eight years ago, we reached a consensus: Asia's internet will definitely surpass the U.S., and even China's internet industry alone will surpass the U.S.; it's just a matter of time. Today, the e-commerce markets of China and Japan have already surpassed the U.S., and we predict that within five years, China's e-commerce will surpass the U.S.. In China, e-commerce is the core internet application. To win the Chinese internet, you first need to win China's e-commerce.

"The Two Significances of This Cooperation"

Some people believe that this cooperation between Yahoo Japan and Taobao has bigger ambitions. The Asian region has replaced North America and the EU as the largest regional internet user community. This cooperation has two significances: one is to resist competition from global competitors like eBay, Amazon, Google, etc., and the other is to suppress competitors within the region. Competitors within the region include Tencent, Baidu, etc. Three months ago, Baidu announced its cooperation with Rakuten to enter the Japanese e-commerce market, and Baidu's search business had already entered the Japanese market two years ago, which is equivalent to setting fire in Masayoshi Son's backyard. Tencent has not yet directly competed with SoftBank, but in China, Tencent competes with Alibaba, which has capital ties with SoftBank, in multiple fields.

[Hot Topics]

"The Ministry of Commerce Will Reduce Export Rebates for Some Products This Year"

At the regular press conference on the morning of June 12, Yao Jian, spokesperson for the Ministry of Commerce, stated that export rebate policies are expected to be adjusted this year. Yao Jian mentioned that "to achieve the goal of reducing energy consumption per unit GDP by 20% during the 'Eleventh Five-Year Plan', export rebates for some products will be reduced this year." However, he also emphasized that reducing export rebates does not mean tightening of our foreign trade policies; stability in trade policies remains our top priority.

"Seven Ministries Push Public Rental Housing, Each Unit No Larger Than 60 Square Meters"

The "Guidance Opinions on Accelerating the Development of Public Rental Housing" jointly formulated by the Ministry of Housing and Urban-Rural Development and six other departments was officially released yesterday. The "Guidance Opinions" emphasize that newly built public rental housing should primarily meet basic living needs, and the floor area of complete units should be strictly controlled below 60 square meters. The "Guidance Opinions" require that for tenants who owe rent and other fees, their employer may be notified, and deductions can be made directly from their wages.

"Global Banking Giants Build Vaults to Store Gold Coins and Bars"

According to the Financial Times, to fully utilize the current demand for gold recovery and record-high prices, some banking giants and security companies are busy building vaults to store gold bars and coins worth hundreds of billions of dollars. Driven by concerns about the global economy and the European sovereign debt crisis, investors' interest in gold is rising, leading to a shortage of long-term storage space, prompting banks to build new vaults for the first time in many years.

[Commentary]

"Will China’s Real Estate Control Policy Reverse?"

Wang Juelin, Deputy Director of the Policy Research Center of the Ministry of Housing and Urban-Rural Development: Various parties, including some local governments, have doubts about the ability of this round of property market regulation. Some even think that previous rounds of property market regulation ended with 'relaxation' or 'rescue', and speculate that the central government might rescue the market in the second half of the year or early next year. The central government has reserved a large margin for regulation, and this round of regulation differs from previous ones; it will not end halfway.

Wang Tao, Chief Economist for Greater China at UBS: Although we expect that China's real estate industry will not collapse and economic growth will not plummet sharply, we believe that by the end of this year, real estate starts and construction activities will significantly slow down, although they will remain relatively strong in the short term. We expect the government will not introduce any new major regulatory policies for real estate, but existing policies will not reverse quickly either. We believe that construction activities will gradually slow down, and demand for building materials will fall year-on-year in the fourth quarter of 2010.

Chen Xingdong, Chief Economist for China at BNP Paribas: The purpose of this round of government regulation is not to bring prices down, but to slow down the excessively rapid rise in prices, and the result of achieving this will inevitably lead to a price drop. It is estimated that this round of regulation will cause a national average decline in house prices of around 10-20%. Prices in first-tier cities will fall more than in second and third-tier cities. If prices return to the level of November 2009, consumers may accept it, transaction volumes will recover, and prices will then move towards steady growth.

"Dragon Boat Festival, Beware of the 'Rejuvenated Green Dumplings'

The Dragon Boat Festival is coming, and supermarkets, snack shops, and stalls have begun supplying various kinds of dumplings. But regardless of the filling or packaging, there is one kind that consumers must beware of, which is the 'rejuvenated green dumplings.' So-called 'rejuvenated green dumplings' refer to dumplings wrapped in 'rejuvenated green leaves.' These 'rejuvenated green leaves' are chemically dyed by some unscrupulous vendors, specifically by adding industrial copper sulfate when soaking the leaves, making them appear fresh and green. However, eating dumplings wrapped in these leaves can lead to copper poisoning.
