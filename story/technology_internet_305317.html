Real Madrid's midfield maestro interprets the definition of soul, the Galaxy Fleet can't afford to be without him.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-13 20:53:01

Real Madrid's midfield maestro interprets the definition of soul: the Galacticos can't afford to be without him. Real chose the most hostile venue, the least friendly territory, to showcase their best version of the season. This is a real team, with flesh and blood, orderly on the field, knowing how to cover up its still-existing flaws while showcasing all its advantages. Without Cristiano Ronaldo or Kaka, Gonzalo Higuain took on the responsibility of scoring, and Karim Benzema reached the heights people expected. And facing such a fierce opponent as Valencia, under Xabi Alonso's leadership, and with the assistance of Lass Diarra and Marcelo, Real displayed their most united and brilliant tactical side. It could be said that these few players carried Real, allowing them to complete an excellent match.

Under Alonso's leadership, Real intercepted the opponent in midfield, pressuring them, organizing attacks, and providing opportunities for the forwards. Despite receiving an early yellow card for deliberate handball, Alonso constantly demanded the ball and controlled the pace of the game. Whenever possible, Lass Diarra made efforts to move forward confidently with the ball. In the first half, he disrupted a great chance from David Mata. Marcelo once again showcased good form in his left-wing position. Though he wasn’t a "finisher," he was adept at creating chances for teammates, delivering several good balls to the frontline. Higuain’s second goal came directly from his assist. In comparison, Rafael van der Vaart’s performance was slightly inferior, despite participating in the offense but showing low efficiency.

Alonso's performance in the second half was particularly outstanding. After the start of the second half, both teams maintained a fast rhythm, making for an exciting back-and-forth contest with almost no pauses. However, amidst the chaos, Real remained steady, better controlling the game under Alonso's direction, discerning the path forward, and thus making Real's midfield appear more intelligent than Valencia's. Despite twice leading and being equalized twice, in the 83rd minute, Alonso personally shone by delivering a free-kick cross, assisting Gabriel Heinze in scoring the winning goal. In the first half, his free-kick had helped Benzema score, although the latter's goal was disallowed.

Clearly, Alonso is indispensable to Real, which is why he has firmly held the starting position. Moreover, as time goes by, Alonso has begun to integrate into Real, a trend that has become very apparent in recent games. The Basque midfielder is absolutely representative of Real's "starting lineup ideology." Apart from goalkeeper Iker Casillas, the player with the most appearances in Real is none other than Alonso and Marcelo. Initially, Real brought in Alonso because they felt their midfield lacked a leader. Pellegrini and Valdano even persuaded Florentino Perez to agree with the signing, stating that whether Alonso joined or not could likely determine the success or failure of Real's next season. Despite having Kaka and Ronaldo, Real still needed a midfield conductor, and Alonso could provide the best qualities—control, passing, and tempo—that would allow Kaka and Ronaldo's roles to be maximized.

However, during this half-season, Alonso's performances have been only satisfactory. Although he has held the starting position without making significant mistakes, he also hasn't shown anything extraordinary. It wasn't until recently that he started to slowly find his form. In this game, with Kaka and Ronaldo absent, Alonso became the team's compass, providing not only passes to teammates (he is currently Real's top assister with four assists) but also stability to the team. In this match against Valencia, apart from three key passes, he also made nine tackles, proving proficient in both attack and defense.

Even Valencia's 'Provincial Newspaper' stated that this Basque man is a midfield master whose displays both with and without the ball are textbook-worthy. Although Real's possession rate was lower than Valencia's, they had more shots and higher passing accuracy, clearly thanks to Alonso's organization and planning in midfield. Before the game, Alonso mentioned that Real could win without Kaka and Ronaldo, but no one expected that he himself would be the hero of victory. At this point, recalling the words of Liverpool goalkeeper Pepe Reina, it becomes clear that Alonso's departure and arrival indeed affect the rise and fall of two major clubs.

The Liverpool goalkeeper remarked, “Alonso possesses qualities that make him the most important player in any team in the world. His understanding of Liverpool's football system and style of play makes him our key player. I wish him all the best at Real Madrid; as a person, he deserves good fortune, and as a player, he will demonstrate his worth. But at Anfield, having or not having Alonso makes all the difference. We are now experiencing difficulties due to his absence.”

For Real, the only regret is that Alonso will miss the last game of the year against Zaragoza due to accumulating five yellow cards. Alonso received his fourth yellow card in the 11th round match against Santander, and in the following few games, he was very cautious, avoiding malicious fouls to finish the year's games intact. However, he eventually received his fifth yellow card. Of course, Real doesn't need to worry too much because Ronaldo will be able to return by then, and the game will be played at home against a less formidable Zaragoza.
