102-Year-Old Attends a 23-Minute Class in Elementary School, Fulfilling a Lifelong Dream of Studying

by pah201seg on 2010-04-13 11:27:55

On March 31, in Class One, Grade One of Weisan Road Primary School in Jinan, 102-year-old Ma Xiuxian was attentively listening to the class. Reporter Zuo Qing and intern Liu Tianlin took photos. The elder's school supplies were complete, including a magnifying glass. Reporter Zuo Qing and intern Liu Tianlin took photos.

Jinan, March 31 (Reporter Xu Jie) – "Young Second Brother, carrying his schoolbag to school." On the morning of March 31, sitting in the classroom of Weisan Road Primary School in Jinan, 102-year-old Ma Xiuxian finally realized her decades-long dream of attending school. Although her first class lasted only 23 minutes, it was the first time in her 102-year life that she entered a classroom… Video: A 102-Year-Old Elder Attends 23 Minutes of Elementary School to Fulfill Her Dream of Education Source: Qilu TV's *Laguo*

At 8:20 AM on March 31, the 36 students of Class One, Grade One at Weisan Road Primary School sat quietly in the classroom. They were waiting for their 37th classmate — 102-year-old Ma Xiuxian. This was a Chinese language class, and Chinese teacher Xu Yan wrote "Welcome Grandma Ma" on the side of the blackboard.

Following the agreed-upon class time with Weisan Road Primary School, at 8:30 AM, white-haired Ma Xiuxian, carrying her schoolbag and supported by her youngest son Yin Fengxin, walked into the classroom. The children welcomed this oldest classmate with整齐的 applause. Ma Xiuxian was seated in the third row next to seven-year-old Zhang Ruizhe.

"Hello classmates! Hello teacher!" Ma Xiuxian responded to the students' welcome with this opening line, her voice clear and loud, earning another round of applause.

The class began, and Ma Xiuxian placed her hands flat on her knees, eyes wide open. Wearing a hearing aid, she smiled as she listened attentively and devoutly. On her desk in front of her were new books, exercise books, and sharpened pencils issued by the school. Unlike other elementary school students, her "equipment" also included a magnifying glass.

Due to her advanced age, Ma Xiuxian seemed unable to hear clearly what the teacher was saying or what the classmates were reading. She wrote her name, "Ma Xiuxian," on the exercise book, with the character "马" written in traditional characters. Then, she laboriously wrote "Class One, Grade One."

Two minutes before the end of the class, considering safety, Ma Xiuxian had to leave the classroom early. At the students' request, she spoke a few words: "Thank you, teacher! Thank you all! Study hard and become successors to the nation!" Her voice trembled slightly but remained very clear.

After the class, the Peking Opera Society of Weisan Road Primary School sang a segment of *Farewell My Concubine* for her. Ma Xiuxian replied with *The Sky Over the Liberated Areas is Bright and Clear*.

"In the old society, there was no such happiness! I may have slightly impaired hearing, but my eyesight is still good, my mind isn't confused, and I don't forget things." Ma Xiuxian said that today, being able to fulfill her lifelong dream of attending school made her very happy.

Scene Details: The Elder Smiled While Entering and Leaving the Classroom

"Afterwards, I will read textbooks. I really envy today's children..." On March 31, after returning home from Weisan Road Primary School, Ma Xiuxian happily recounted her experience of the class to visiting neighbors, unable to stop smiling. Yin Fengxin said that after returning home from school, this was the sentence his mother repeated most often.

Facing classmates nearly a century younger, Ma Xiuxian kept smiling when entering the classroom, showing her excitement. Walking out of the classroom, she smiled and said, "I am very happy today! In the past, we had many children and couldn't even eat enough food, so there was no such condition. Today's children are so well-off."

During Ma Xiuxian's class, there were many reporters present, and cameras clicked continuously. Several video cameras also filmed in every available space. Due to the noisy environment, Ma Xiuxian often couldn't see the blackboard or hear the teacher clearly, which also affected the children's listening. Therefore, some parents of the students questioned whether allowing a centenarian to enter the classroom was just for show. Relevant personnel from Daguan Garden Subdistrict Office said, "The elder was indeed happy and content today. Our purpose has been achieved."

Raised Nine Children, Seven of Whom Were University Students, the Elder Most Envied "Cultured People"

Why did a 102-year-old elder want to carry a schoolbag into the classroom, and how did she manage to enter?

Selling Dowry to Fund Children’s Education

Ma Xiuxian was born in 1908 in Hancang in eastern Jinan. At the age of 13, she became a child worker in a textile factory. After marrying at 18, she stayed home managing household chores. Throughout her life, she raised nine children, seven of whom attended university, while the other two graduated from high school. Now, her eldest daughter, aged 82, attended the famous Qi Lu University Medical College in the 1940s and became a doctor at Beijing Tiantan Hospital; her second son graduated from Shanghai University of Finance and Economics and worked in central government agencies; her third daughter, after graduating from university, worked long-term in the northwest on railway construction design...

Yin Fengxin, 58, is the elder's youngest son. He said that his mother always took pride in her children's success. Their father worked at Jinan Boiler Factory, and with only one income in the family, they weren't wealthy. His third sister planned to work after high school graduation but didn't get her mother's approval. Under her mother's insistence, she went to university instead. Yin Fengxin said that his mother always admired "cultured people." During the hardest times, she sold the jewelry she received as a dowry to fund her children's education. As long as her children wanted to study seriously, no matter how tough life got, she would fully support them.

Subdistrict Office Helped Realize the Elder's Dream

Yin Fengxin told reporters that his mother worked tirelessly throughout her life. After his brothers and sisters married and started families, she helped raise grandchildren. She only attended a literacy class for a few months, learning to write a few characters. At home, his mother was absolutely "diligent and eager to learn," often "seeking advice" from her children about the pronunciation and meaning of certain characters. Not long ago, during an interview with media covering centenarians, the elder expressed her wish to attend school. The Jing'er Road Community Committee and Daguan Garden Subdistrict Office quickly learned about her wish.

Li Jun from Daguan Garden Subdistrict Office told reporters that Ma Xiuxian was a well-known figure in the community. Upon learning about her wish, everyone was deeply moved. They, along with the Jing'er Road Community, contacted Weisan Road Primary School, the closest school to her home. The primary school was very supportive and agreed to allow the elder to attend classes. On March 31, Ma Xiuxian became the oldest student in the school's 51-year history. □ By reporter Xu Jie
