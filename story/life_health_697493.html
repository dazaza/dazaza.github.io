Experts introduce the late-stage symptoms of thyroid cancer:

1. Local compression symptoms: As the thyroid tumor continues to grow, it can compress the recurrent laryngeal nerve, causing hoarseness, invade the esophagus leading to difficulty swallowing and weight loss, or invade or press on the trachea, resulting in breathing difficulties, coughing up blood, and chest discomfort.

2. Local metastasis symptoms: In the late stages of thyroid cancer, local metastasis often results in hard and fixed lymph nodes appearing in the neck. Medullary cancer and follicular cancer are more likely to cause enlarged lymph node metastases in the neck.

3. Accompanying symptoms: Patients with medullary cancer may also have pheochromocytomas and parathyroid hyperplasia or tumors. Clinically, they may experience diarrhea, palpitations, facial flushing, and decreased blood calcium levels.

4. Distant metastasis symptoms are also late-stage symptoms of thyroid cancer: The metastasis rate of thyroid cancer can reach 50% to 90.5%, with a cervical lymph node metastasis rate of 64.56%. Distant metastases of thyroid cancer commonly occur in the lungs, skull, vertebrae, and pelvis. Brain metastasis can cause headaches and vomiting; lung or mediastinum metastasis causes coughing, hemoptysis, and chest discomfort; bone metastasis can cause pathological fractures leading to pain, and spinal metastasis can cause numbness or weakness in hands and feet.
