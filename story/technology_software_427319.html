Drunk elder lying on the road vomiting blood and refusing medical treatment

by dehua900 on 2011-05-08 09:40:30

The police officer helped the drunken elderly man up.

"Do you know him?" the police officer hurriedly asked the sanitation worker, who replied that the old man was over 70 years old, originally from Anhui province, and currently living in a shanty area near Fuxin Hospital.

"When I got closer, I saw it was an elderly man lying on the ground, vomiting some things," Ms. Bai said. What made her feel scared was that the old man had a strong smell of alcohol, there was some blood around him, and a dog was licking the blood on the ground.

"My three sons..." The old man, with his eyes closed, answered while spitting out blood onto the ground but did not mention where he lived.

The reporter noticed that the old man had sparse hair, wore thin and worn-out clothes, and there was a strong smell of alcohol at the scene. That stray dog completely ignored the people around and continued to linger. "When I arrived, this stray dog was already licking the blood the old man had vomited," Ms. Bai said, adding that the stray dog couldn't be driven away.

On the evening of November 21, after drinking heavily at home, a seventy-year-old man came to Liaoyang West Road, later lying unconscious on the road, vomiting blood. His body emitted a strong smell of alcohol which attracted a stray dog that lingered around him. After seeing this horrifying scene, Ms. Bai immediately called the police. When the police officers and emergency medical personnel arrived, the old man refused to go to the hospital for treatment. Thus, two police officers began searching for his home in the cold wind.

Soon after, the ambulance also arrived. Seeing that the old man was still vomiting blood, the emergency medical staff hoped he would go to the hospital for treatment. However, when they tried to lift him into the ambulance, the old man struggled and refused to go to the hospital, claiming that he "had just drunk some alcohol at home, nothing serious."

"When we arrived at the scene, the old man was lying in the middle of the motor vehicle lane with cars passing by closely," a police officer told reporters. Another police officer then dialed the 120 emergency number.

"Where is your hometown?" the police officer asked.

Five minutes later, two police officers from the Fushan New District Police Station arrived.

The sanitation worker revealed the old man's residence.

After about ten minutes of deadlock with the emergency medical staff, the old man still refused to get into the ambulance. Since the emergency medical team had other tasks, they left first. "Since the emergency medical team has left, we can't just leave the old man here," one police officer said at 7:50 PM.

"My home is in Anhui, but now I live in Qingdao," the old man said.

"The sight of the dog licking the blood and vomit on the ground really made me feel terrified," Ms. Bai said. She tried to ask the old man about his physical condition, but he didn't respond while lying on the road. Worried that the old man might be hit by a car, she quickly called the police.

The old man was lying on the road, vomiting blood.

At around 7:30 PM on the evening of November 21, Ms. Bai, who lived in the Fourth Community of Fushanhou, was taking a walk along Liaoyang West Road. When she reached the卜Feng Lotus Supermarket area, she suddenly noticed someone lying on the road.

Thus, the police officers put the old man and the sanitation worker into the police car and drove him home. Upon arriving at the shanty area, they found the old man's house locked. A neighbor said that the old man's wife had not returned yet. Therefore, the police officers helped the old man rest temporarily in a neighbor's house. Text and pictures by reporter Wang Yongduan.

The old man struggled and refused to go to the hospital.

A few minutes later, a sanitation worker arrived at the scene and asked the old man, "Did you drink again? Why did you drink and end up here?"

"Where are your children? Where do you live?" the police officer asked.
