Can marketing through group buying turn group-buying customers into loyal customers?

by beetgk2958 on 2011-11-14 10:18:26

"We previously conducted two surveys, one for users and one for merchants," Meituan.com Vice President Wang Huiwen told the "Successful Marketing" reporter about group buying. "The survey on merchants shows that local service merchants have a strong demand for the group-buying model for two reasons: first, local service merchants lack marketing means and channels, most rely on traditional word-of-mouth promotion; group buying provides them with a great opportunity and platform for display. Second, the overall life cycle of local service merchants is relatively short, they need a faster way than word-of-mouth to increase their visibility and gain profit."

It can be said that when group-buying websites were established, they used "low price" as a selling point, presenting the originally highly differentiated, low transparency local services through an e-commerce platform in a clearly priced, visually rich manner to consumers, thereby completing the e-commerce process for local merchants, meeting people's increasingly growing demand for life service consumption, and thus achieving rapid growth themselves. So what about now? "We still focus on services," confirmed the heads of group-buying websites such as Meituan, Mansuo, and Nuomi to the reporter. Lashou, which is building a light logistics system, also stated that the situation where "local service merchants are the mainstream" will not change in the short term. An exception is the group-buying websites built on B2C platforms, such as Taobao Juhaosuan and JD Group. Taobao Juhaosuan leverages the B2C strength of its platform, positioning itself closer to a new project in the service resource package provided by Taobao to merchants, mainly serving various physical goods merchants on the platform.

At this point, we must mention those group-buying websites that rely on platforms, such as Juhaosuan and Nuomiwang. When Groupon started cooperating with traditional brand advertisers like Gap, these domestic group-buying websites, due to the brand advertising resources possessed by the platform itself and the user groups they attract who have a desire to consume, have already assumed a role within the overall brand advertising service system of the platform, thus becoming the first group-buying websites to come into contact with traditional brand advertisers. For example, the recent 0 yuan lottery activity for Chery cars launched by Nuomiwang is one item in the cooperation package between Renren Network and Chery cars. As for whether this contact will lead to more direct advertising cooperation between brand advertisers and group-buying websites, it is currently very difficult to judge.

Just as everyone was celebrating, thinking they had discovered another low-cost marketing model and sales channel, DQ (Dairy Queen) and the industry image-heavy Meituan openly broke ties. After the DQ cash coupon went live on Meituan and sold 12,000 copies, DQ issued a "serious statement," stating that "all information about group-buying DQ cash coupons and product coupons released by third parties has nothing to do with our company." After nearly a year of crazy growth, facing the high exposure generated by participating in group buying and the potential risk of low-priced group buying impacting brand image, brand enterprises cannot help but calmly consider: at the product level, apart from the most common small-amount service and product group buying, are expensive items equally popular? What is the quality of group-buying users? Can merchants convert group-buying customers into loyal customers by taking profits and using group buying for marketing? At the merchant cooperation level, can group buying serve brand advertisers outside of local merchants due to its high participation rate and high dissemination rate? Group buying + SNS, group buying + LBS, group buying + data mining... after infinite combinations, from a marketing perspective, how much value does group buying actually have?

In the past year, what was the hottest word on the Internet? Probably, aside from Weibo, it was group buying. Just months after China's first Groupon imitator went online, the number of group-buying websites increased to 1,726, and all heavyweight players had entered the market. Besides third-party group-buying websites, the three major portals, B2C e-commerce platforms, SNS communities, and others have all launched their own group-buying platforms. Such a fiery scene has drawn the attention of local merchants and brand advertisers: On September 9, 2010, the Mercedes-Benz smart car group-buying event on Taobao Juhaosuan went live as scheduled, selling 200 units in just 3 hours and 28 minutes, generating a total sales amount of 27 million yuan, with a per-second sales amount of 2,100 yuan. In just one hour, 116 cars were sold via group buying, matching the sales volume of national 4S stores over 10 days. Calculated at this ratio, group buying sold one-thirtieth of Mercedes-Benz's annual smart car sales in just one hour.
