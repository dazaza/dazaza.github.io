The process of introduction

by lnov11iket on 2009-12-03 16:07:59

Under the two major factors of China's economic adjustment and the world financial crisis, our national economy has seen a relatively large economic downturn since the fourth quarter of last year. Through these few months, especially since the implementation of an expansionary fiscal policy in October last year, it can be said that the economy has stabilized, and we are the first to recover in the world. From the perspective of the global backdrop, it can be said that the situation has basically stabilized. This time we had a bigger financial crisis than in 1929, the largest financial crisis in history. Due to the joint efforts of governments around the world to adopt expansionary policies, we successfully avoided a great depression. After 1929, there was a decade-long great recession, historically known as the Great Depression, which lasted for ten years with deep recessions, GDP dropped by 50%, another world war broke out, resulting in 20 million deaths, and it wasn't until 1954, 25 years later, that the level of 1929 was restored. This time we have prevented a major depression. In this sense, it is a progress for humanity, learning from the lessons of 1929. We say policies come at a cost, but because the opportunity cost is too high, if the opportunity cost is a great depression, then the cost we bear, including the cost of excessive liquidity, is worth it.

In such a broad context, some industries have begun to recover. In the past few years, during the period when the economy was overheated, some excess production capacity formed, especially after the big crisis, the excess production capacity that emerged is now being digested. Some industries digest faster, while others, due to their larger volume, take longer to digest. For example, steel and cement, the 4 trillion government investment plan has played an important role in utilizing their production capacity, producing direct positive results. The past overcapacity in steel was too large. Now with the 4 trillion, the demand for products is over 400 million tons but less than 500 million tons, while our capacity is around 700 million tons, so the pressure on capacity remains high. The machinery and electronics sector is doing better, with some overcapacity, but not too much. Through the government's investment plan, after a period of time, the overcapacity is being digested, inventories have been largely digested, and now we are using capacity, hoping to utilize it to a certain extent this year. Some industries stand out, and the most prominent one is where the government's expansionary policy has led to new growth. For instance, the automobile industry, with government subsidies, Chinese consumers' purchasing power has reached this stage, the rural market policy, has entered a new peak, unlike the automobile industries in other countries which are declining by twenty or thirty percent, ours is growing. It can be said that some industries are developing further, some are gradually digesting excess capacity, and some may recover more slowly. But overall, we are leading the recovery process.

The development of domestic brand automobile enterprises still requires time. From the perspective of the automobile industry, the accurate guidance of national policies plays an important role in the recovery of the automobile industry. Some of these policies are short-term, while others have become long-term, such as the tax reduction for small displacement vehicles, which may in the future become clean energy, hybrid power, new energy technology vehicle tax reductions, becoming a long-term policy.

In China's automobile industry, domestic brand automobile enterprises form a special group. As everyone knows, developing domestic brands is difficult. It can be said that domestic brands are still some distance from success, and still have some way to go.

Firstly, the fundamental reason is that others have been doing it for a hundred years, while we have only been doing it for fifty-sixty years, but the first thirty years were mostly stagnant, and many areas progressed slowly. The last thirty years were just the beginning, this is what economics calls the disadvantage of lagging behind, not only in the automobile industry, even the once severely polluted Thames River has now become clear and calm. What does a hundred years mean? Not only has technology advanced, capital has expanded, but it also means the market has been divided, the market has been occupied. To enter, to produce a car that can compete with theirs, this task is very arduous, very difficult. Everyone should not criticize without understanding the difficulties. How did we not create a car? This requires a process. With an objective view, it is already quite fast to get to where we are today.

Secondly, since there is a gap, economics discusses how to catch up, how to shorten the gap. What was mentioned earlier is the disadvantage of lagging behind, and the second point is to utilize the advantage of lagging behind. What is the advantage of lagging behind? It means not repeating the mistakes others have made, not making the same errors others have tried, learning from their technology, learning from their business models, innovating on the basis of learning. Since we are far from the frontier, innovation is still something others have chewed on, we must first learn well to lay a good foundation before we can innovate. Just as Mr. Wu mentioned several stages earlier, the first and second stages cannot be ignored, which are the introduction stages, the process of introduction. Apart from directly introducing capital and technology, many believe that technology hasn't come in, and the exchange of technology for market hasn't happened, the market has circled back to technology, core technology still lies in others' hands. Core technology, the latest things, naturally remain in others' hands, if you invent something, you will surely hold onto it tightly. However, during this process, we have learned a lot. An important concept in economics over the recent decades is spillover effect, which is the free acquisition of a large amount of knowledge, because they produce cars here, you see how cars are produced, how they are managed, how assembly lines are designed, you learn how cars are made. Now some people say making cars isn't impressive, it's just assembling parts together. We didn't have a parts industry before, and even if we did, we couldn't assemble them into cars. How did the parts industry develop? Joint ventures were an important factor. Joint ventures plus an 80% localization requirement led to the development of automotive parts, laying a foundation for many innovations and our own creations. Otherwise, they wouldn't be possible.

This process has cultivated a large number of talents, not just skilled workers, many general managers of domestic brands come from joint ventures, trained for ten to twenty years, all of which are called spillover effects, diffusion of knowledge, and learning processes. When we talk about independent innovation, it is correct, it is our ultimate goal, and what we pursue, but to do a good job of independent innovation, we must first do a good job of learning. Because we are lagging behind, without the learning process, the gap cannot be shortened. Domestic brands are like water without a source. This is the second point.

The third point is how to do a good job of independent innovation, how to carve out a bloody path in a market already occupied by others, squeeze in, this is indeed a very difficult thing, and indeed various paths need to be explored. Various forms need to be explored, and this exploration process may gradually form from different sources and historical backgrounds, not breaking away from history. Generally speaking, there are three models now: Geely model, Chery model, Huachen model. I hope to see more models, perhaps more explorations from everyone, theoretically it is hard to imagine, only practitioners can gradually create them. I would say all are worthy of our observation, learning, and analysis. Some can be replicated, some cannot, combining them will give us some new insights. From the perspective of society or the government, a cherishing attitude should be taken. Imagine how hard it is for them to reach today's level. Just heard that in the international ranking recognized by China, Chery ranks around 22-23rd, Huachen 26th, which is very hard-won. How do we cherish these developments, encourage these developments, make all kinds of policy environments, from market environment, more conducive to the development of domestic brands, and how to make domestic brands enter the international market more favorably, these are the issues we need to think about most now, all are worth cherishing, all are worth valuing, all are worth developing.

From a long-term perspective, first of all, fair market competition is needed. It can be said that the automobile market, in this sense, is still better than some other markets, some other markets cannot be entered, various private capitals cannot enter. The development of the automobile market at this stage is largely due to the involvement of private capital, and in various aspects of access, obstacles are gradually being eliminated, which is very important. To develop an industry, it must be developed in a competitive environment, everyone must start from the same point, not only facing domestic competition, but also international competition with standards, consumers choosing various products in the market with neutral standards, providing detailed information on granulation machines, that is truly forming a brand, otherwise it's just bean sprouts in a bottle, never growing big. As mentioned earlier, the lessons learned from sixty years ago, we worked hard for sixty years to develop the automobile industry, at that time because we were closed off from the outside world, on one hand there was no learning effect, on the other hand, there was no market standard, even if we made something, it was only remembered domestically for a while, a few brands developed then, but did they really form brands? Were they particularly distinctive brands? They could not form brands with world competitiveness. Now we talk about developing domestic brands, not self-entertaining brands behind closed doors, but brands with world competitiveness, brands that will participate in international competition, competing with existing major brands, those are meaningful brands. There must be a fair environment, we are not saying to discriminate against joint ventures, nor to give them more favorable conditions, everyone in the same market, ethnic brands don't need special preferences either, government support is support, but in the market, everyone must compete equally to finally stand firm. In this sense, the policy environment is still very important.

Secondly, from the perspective of the country and the government, there should be some strategic thinking. Any country treasures its production capacity, any government values its enterprise brands, the ones that create jobs for themselves first, create profits and taxes for their nationals second, and third, truly contribute to the country from brands and culture, any government values them, in a sense they are public goods, wealth that enhances a country's production capacity and competitiveness. On this issue, the government indeed needs some strategy. Under the condition of equal competition in the market, for our domestic brands, at least those that are visible, emerging brands, a certain degree of support should be given, especially in terms of policy, including expanding domestic demand, responding to the financial crisis, revitalizing the industry plan, these brands should be supported with a certain intensity.

Therefore, various models of domestic brands like the Huachen model are all worth cherishing, all worth developing. One thing to remember is that not all brands will succeed in the end, not all models will succeed in the end, the market will choose in market competition, the US ends up with three companies, Japan two or three, France ends up with two, Italy almost one, we cannot hope to keep all, which is impossible, still need to let the market choose through market competition, survival of the fittest to produce world-competitive Chinese domestic brands.
