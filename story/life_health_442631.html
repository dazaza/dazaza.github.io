Why doesn't vaginitis get cured easily?

by zzzz7557 on 2011-06-07 16:28:07

Why does vaginitis not heal after a long treatment? Some women with vaginitis do not know why it cannot be cured for a long time during the treatment, so how should it be treated correctly? Gynecological experts said that first of all, don't be anxious, go to the hospital for timely treatment, formulate corresponding treatment plans according to different conditions, and at the same time find out the reasons for the long-term treatment without cure. Next, let's take a look at the introduction of the experts from Beijing Jington Hospital on why vaginitis is not healed after a long treatment. The main reasons for the long-term treatment of vaginitis are as follows:

1. Irregular medication, insufficient course of treatment, insufficient dosage, failure to have regular follow-up visits, stopping the medicine without confirming the recovery can not completely kill the pathogen, residual pathogens can continue to reproduce locally, causing symptoms unable to relieve and repeated attacks, leading to inability to cure.

2. Inflammation caused by mixed infections, using drugs targeting only one pathogen cannot kill the pathogens of mixed infections, which may lead to serious imbalance of vaginal flora and the emergence of drug-resistant strains, bringing difficulties to the treatment.

3. Some types of vaginitis can be infected through sexual intercourse or direct/indirect contact. For example, trichomonas vaginitis and candida vaginitis, if the male partner has already been infected, he can spread the infection to the female through sexual intercourse. Therefore, during treatment, the spouse or sexual partner also needs synchronous treatment.

4. Patients who have been taking broad-spectrum antibiotics or hormone therapy for a long time are prone to decreased body resistance and microbial imbalance, making it difficult to cure vaginitis.

5. During the treatment, it is necessary to keep the vagina clean, wash the vulva frequently, change underwear regularly, and avoid sexual intercourse. Underwear and towels should be boiled for disinfection and placed in the sun to dry.

6. Existence of other diseases. For chronic fungal vaginitis that is difficult to cure, diabetes should be suspected, because high blood sugar levels increase the sugar level in the vagina. After the action of lactic acid bacteria, the acidity in the vagina increases, which is suitable for fungal reproduction and growth. If diabetes is not controlled, fungal vaginitis will be difficult to control.
