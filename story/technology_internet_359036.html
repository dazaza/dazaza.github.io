[Daily Focus: Cross-border trade RMB settlement expanded to all countries]

[Zhejiang listed as a pilot province]

After Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Zhuhai, and Dongguan became cross-border trade RMB settlement pilot areas last April, Zhejiang has recently been approved as a cross-border trade RMB settlement pilot area. Hangzhou, Ningbo, and Wenzhou within the province have become newly added pilot cities. Yesterday, Bank of China Zhejiang branch successfully handled the first cross-border trade RMB settlement business in the province. Besides Bank of China, banks such as Bank of Communications and Industrial and Commercial Bank of China also opened relevant businesses.

[Effectively avoiding exchange rate risks]

For the export-oriented large province of Zhejiang, as well as many Zhejiang private export enterprises, the significance of using RMB for cross-border trade settlements lies in avoiding exchange rate risks and locking in profits. Currently, import and export enterprises usually use dollars for pricing and settlement, and the exchange rate risks brought about are mainly borne by domestic enterprises. If export goods lock in foreign currency prices, depreciation of foreign currencies will lead to reduced corporate gains or even losses. Using RMB trade settlements allows enterprises to avoid hedging through forward sales and swap transactions, thereby avoiding exchange rate risks.

[Export companies start to taste benefits]

According to Bank of China personnel, the expansion of the cross-border trade RMB settlement pilot program will further promote investment and trade facilitation. For both foreign trade enterprises and domestic banks, it is an opportunity for development. For foreign trade enterprises, the most direct benefit is simplifying cross-border trade procedures, avoiding exchange rate risks, locking in transaction costs, and enhancing the enterprise's risk resistance and profitability. With the continuous expansion of cross-border trade RMB settlement pilot areas, pilot enterprises, and pilot businesses, more enterprises will benefit from this policy, helping them expand their trade fields, open overseas markets, and promote the continuous development of their trade businesses.

[Hot Topics]

[China cancels export tax rebates on steel and other selected commodities]

The Ministry of Finance and the State Administration jointly issued the "Notice on Canceling Export Tax Rebates on Certain Commodities", clearly stating that starting July 15, export tax rebates will be canceled for products under 406 tariff codes, including some steel and non-ferrous metal building materials. Experts pointed out that the fiscal and taxation departments chose this time point to cancel part of the export tax rebate to better complete the "Eleventh Five-Year Plan" energy-saving and emission-reduction tasks. Relevant persons pointed out that the strength and scope of this policy of canceling part of the product export tax rebates were unprecedented. This time, the fiscal and taxation departments not only fully eliminated the export tax rebate rates for "two-high-one-resource" products but also expanded the scope to agricultural products, pharmaceuticals, and chemical products.

[Zhejiang standardizes non-listed company share transfers]

In the future, changes in shareholders and shares of non-listed joint-stock companies will be fully disclosed and can be queried through the industrial and commercial department. Yesterday, I learned from the industrial and commercial department that the Provincial Administration for Industry and Commerce jointly issued the "Operational Opinions on Share Changes and Articles of Association Filing for Non-Listed Joint-Stock Companies in Zhejiang Province" with the Provincial Financial Office. Starting from the issuance date, the industry and commerce department will further standardize the filing of articles of association changes for shareholders outside the initiators of non-listed joint-stock companies. A relevant person in charge of the Enterprise Registration Division of the Zhejiang Provincial Administration for Industry and Commerce stated that conducting share change filings for non-listed joint-stock companies is a powerful measure to serve enterprises and reduce their burdens.

The "General Plan for the Comprehensive Tourism Reform Pilot Zone in Guilin" and the "Outline of the Comprehensive Tourism Reform Pilot Zone in Guilin" have passed expert review. According to the outline, Guilin will make breakthroughs in key areas and critical links, breaking institutional obstacles to tourism development, exploring a new path with tourism as the leader, modern services as the mainstay, and low-carbon economy as the feature. [Commentary]

[Zhejiang private enterprises call for policy fairness, vice governor praises sharp comments as useful]

On the afternoon of June 22, the Zhejiang Provincial Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference held a special collective democratic supervision hearing in Hangzhou on the theme of "Private Economic Development Environment and Transformation and Upgrading". Representatives from private enterprises proposed that Zhejiang should fairly treat private enterprises in formulating local policies. Thirty-eight entrepreneurs, experts, and public representatives gathered together to speak up. Vice Governor Jin Deshui said the representative's speech was sharp but very useful.

Vice Chairman of the Zhejiang Federation of Industry and Commerce, Chairman of the Board of Directors of Guangsha Group Lou Zhongfu: Private enterprises are increasingly burdened, not physically but mentally. It is hoped that local governments will formulate and implement national policies closer to Zhejiang's realities, especially targeting private enterprises, hoping to align policies with state-owned enterprises and foreign-funded enterprises. Create a good social public opinion environment for private enterprises; refine the "New 36 Measures" related policies and truly implement them, changing the "glass door" and "spring door" phenomena; further improve relevant laws and regulations, play the role of industry associations and intermediary organizations, creating a fair competition environment and financing environment.

Chairman of Huzhou Silk Road Group Ling Lanfang: The government plays a guiding, encouraging, and supporting role in the transformation and upgrading of private enterprises. It is particularly important to create a fair, just, survival-of-the-fittest environment. Enterprises leading in entrepreneurship, innovation, hard work, and transformation and upgrading should be enthusiastically supported, while those with no hope of transformation and upgrading should be resolutely eliminated, freeing up resources. Marketing platforms and R&D platforms should be built for a wide range of small and medium-sized private enterprises, stimulating enterprise vitality and encouraging enterprise innovation.

Chairman of the Zhejiang Provincial Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference Zhou Guofu: Currently, Zhejiang's private economic development is at a critical stage of climbing over obstacles. It is necessary to further emancipate the mind, update concepts, continue to deepen the understanding of the transformation and upgrading of the private economy, with a broader vision and greater courage, deepening reforms, focusing on optimizing the development environment, broadening development paths, and jointly promoting the realization of new leaps for the private economy, seizing new opportunities.

[Industry]

[Shanghai housing prices fell 7% last week; national policy effects evident]

Last week, the average price of residential properties in Shanghai was 17,998 yuan per square meter, falling 7% compared to the previous week, hitting the lowest level since the introduction of the real estate market regulation policy in early April; the volume of residential property transactions also continued to decline, with a trading area of 65,000 square meters, falling 23% compared to the previous week, which was the seventh consecutive week that Shanghai's trading volume remained below 100,000 square meters. Housing prices in first-tier cities like Beijing, Shanghai, and Shenzhen have all declined to varying degrees, indicating that after more than two months of the introduction of real estate market regulation policies, policy effects have begun to show in various places.

[Ningbo will build six marine ranches in the next ten years to develop aquaculture and fisheries]

I learned from the Ningbo Ocean and Fisheries Bureau yesterday that in the next ten years starting from this year, Ningbo will build six distinctive marine ranches in surrounding sea areas to alleviate the decline in fishery resources and the desertification of fishing grounds. It is understood that Ningbo currently preliminarily plans to select Xiangshan Port, Yushan Islands, Jiumashan Islands, and the waters near Panchi Mountain Island, Tantou Mountain Island, and Nantian Island, preparing to construct various styles of marine ranches according to their respective location conditions and natural foundations. Among them, Xiangshan Port will conduct trials and constructions first due to its resource conditions and location advantages, with relatively complete basic work in the construction of marine ranches.
