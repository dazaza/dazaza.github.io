Two female elementary school students committed suicide in Fujian – what does it have to do with time-travel dramas?

by ai9ewud2q on 2012-03-06 15:59:23

Yesterday, I was immersed in the sadness of a high school classmate who died from gas poisoning. I saw this news about my hometown on Weibo and couldn't bring myself to read it carefully. Today, when I opened it and read it thoroughly, it made me extremely angry.

Children don’t understand the value of life

When we were kids, we often asked our parents how we came into this world. Our parents would always find ways to dodge the question; urban parents would say [you were picked up by the trash can], while rural parents would say [you were found in the field]. As a result, none of the kids in kindergarten were actually their biological children.

For children, the meaning of life is too heavy. But should the meaning of life be conveyed to children in such a heavy way? There's no need to educate children on how to endure hardships or how impoverished children in remote mountainous areas are struggling to survive. As for how to educate them, I am not an education professional nor do I have any teaching experience. However, I believe that teaching children courage and honesty is more important than anything else.

Nowadays, most families only have one child. The two young girls who committed suicide had a sister, so their parents still have some hope. For most single-child families, they only have one child, and they are treasured immensely! Fathers and grandfathers aren't allowed to smoke at home, mothers and grandmothers must wash clothes separately from the child's, and various children's products make a lot of money. By the time they grow up and are ready for college entrance exams, these days many parents accompany the candidates. Some grandparents even come along! In ancient times, children going to take the imperial examinations would set off on their own at the age of fifteen or sixteen! Growing up in such a secure environment, will children develop a sense of crisis or a spirit of adventure? When they encounter problems, they will just cry and run back home to their parents and grandparents instead of facing them bravely.

I haven't watched "Pleasant Goat and Big Big Wolf", so I won't comment. But I remember watching "Tom and Jerry" as a child. The weak mouse and the strong cat outwit each other. At the time, I didn't understand, but now thinking about it, it was actually teaching children strategy and wit.

The letter left behind mentioned wanting to travel back to the Qing Dynasty. Is it all the fault of time-travel dramas?

Time-travel dramas are very popular, and the high ratings have led to investment fever. This is understandable for the TV drama industry. However, the prevalence of similar types of TV dramas indeed reflects a lack of creativity and failure of regulation. But when stocks soar, ordinary people still invest heavily, even borrowing money to do so! Now, two young girls have committed suicide, leaving a note saying their wish in this life was to travel back in time to make movies. But the reasons were: one was afraid of taking responsibility for losing something, and the other was due to loyalty. Many onlookers took the opportunity to vent their dissatisfaction with time-travel dramas, calling for a ban. Carefully considering, do time-travel dramas cause death?

In the past, when "Meteor Garden" came out, it was banned because people feared children would learn to flaunt wealth. Hunan TV's "Together Watching Meteor Shower" wasn't banned, perhaps because its imitation production, cheap school uniforms, and domestic sports cars seemed too rustic to constitute flaunting wealth. To resist the next generation being corrupted, Japanese animations and Western animations were banned. As a result, we ended up producing things like "High-Speed Train Hero", which can only lament the glorious works like "Havoc in Heaven". Now that something has happened, should we ban time-travel dramas? Why aren't spy dramas, which promote deceit and betrayal, banned?!

The lack of creative topics and blind market orientation have caused us to turn on the TV now and see uniformity across channels. All dating shows in winter, all bikini adventure shows in summer, these are really not noble!

All these factors lead to our children lacking imagination and perception. They are bound by weekly small tests and monthly big tests, held hostage by piano keys and violin strings, and confined to balconies and boring TVs. How could they feel that life is full of fun and childhood is full of sunshine!

As the last generation of the 80s, I once thought we were miserable, but it turns out today's children are even worse off. Now the National People's Congress is in session. Representatives, please pay attention to the healthy growth of children and propose some reliable motions. Thank you!
