Is the era of online earnings for newcomers over?

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 11:50:10

Let's rewind a few years: it could be said that as long as you set up an online store, you would make a lot of money. However, with the diversification and increasing demands of consumers, if you open a small store on Taobao now, it will only provide basic subsistence. And even this is only possible after painstaking promotion.

Although realistically speaking, opening a store on Taobao may offer a higher starting point than other platforms, do you have enough stars? What makes you think picky customers will choose you, a newcomer? From my personal experience shopping on Taobao, I generally first select stores with ratings. Therefore, if you want to make money online nowadays, it's best not to go to Taobao because in the early stages, you need to invest a large amount of time and money into promoting yourself. Otherwise, you can only survive half-dead. It's like being a civil servant, where you gain seniority by waiting until you're old. But the question is, do you have the time?

In today’s world where new things are constantly emerging, many people pessimistically say that despite mobile phones and other terminals being able to make payments, making money online has basically no potential left because of monopolies like Taobao. Just like in instant messaging, no one can break Tencent's monopoly.

If that were the case, it would be too pessimistic. Tencent may be unbeatable, but e-commerce platforms aren't solely dictated by Taobao. If that were true, group buying wouldn't have appeared. If you don't believe me, look up group buying websites online and see how many are similar to Taobao.

The issue with making money online isn't about who monopolizes what, but rather who discovers new ways to make money. Group buying sites seized the opportunity of discounts and savings through group purchases to snatch a piece of the market. One might say that these billionaires are much like lawyers - lawyers exploit legal loopholes, while they exploit market loopholes.

Taobao introduced online shopping, and group buying made online shopping cheaper.

Now, there's a new type of e-commerce model where everyone involved can share in the profits. This is the ABC theory proposed by Taofula. What is ABC? It's the combination of the initials of Agents (AGENTS), Businesses (BUSINESS), and Consumers (CONSUMERS) to form a new concept - Consumer-Agent-Business. The Consumer-Agent-Business integrates all three identities. In other words, the Consumer-Agent-Business can switch between these three roles.

Essentially, the newly emerged Taofula and Taobao both belong to members sharing in the pie of the e-commerce platform. However, this website has a very distinct feature: have you ever seen consumers earning money while shopping online? Taofula can do that. Consumers earn points with every purchase, and these points can be exchanged for bonuses and upgrades. Sounds fantastical, doesn't it?

Some might say, "Is it real? If that were the case, wouldn't they go bankrupt?" No, because if you receive a rebate when shopping on Taobao, and the quality of the goods is guaranteed, and the price is reasonable, wouldn't you come back? Of course, you would. This is actually a snowballing process. The more consumers there are, the more the site earns, and the more merchants earn. It's a win-win situation where everyone makes money and is happy. Consumers repeatedly shop for bonuses, which leads to thin-profit high-turnover for merchants, which increases the site's earnings, which raises the consumer's bonus. Although fundamentally, it's still the consumer's money supporting all three parties, other platforms only use the consumer's money to support merchants and the site. In comparison, the ABC theory is quite excellent. Of course, the ABC theory isn't as simple as that; I've just roughly explained the specific relationships.

There may be many ways for newcomers to make money online, but similarly, if there's a smooth and clean road, many people would choose it. The ABC theory model was established last October and can be considered a very new field. Friends who want to make money online, if you seize this opportunity, it's equivalent to opening a store at the beginning of Taobao's rise.

A single e-commerce model clearly can no longer meet the growing needs of consumers. Regarding online shopping, if a friend from Heilongjiang sees a product they like online, but the merchant's shipping address is in Hainan, and if this friend is in a hurry, this merchant is likely to be passed over. Taking Taofula as an example, it promptly proposed a new model - community service stores and KFC-like chains. Due to the serial connection in the ABC theory, meaning if consumer A finds a desired product in store B, but store B's shipping location is far from A, then B applies to the website to send the goods to the website side, and simultaneously, the website sends the same-priced goods to A from the nearest location. This achieves a win-win situation for all three parties.
