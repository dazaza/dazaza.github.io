The reality in drama, the drama in reality.

by lili1 on 2012-01-06 21:26:45

Reality in drama, drama in reality

Life is like a drama, full of unpredictable changes, full of joys and sorrows, full of contradictions and misunderstandings. Drama reflects reality, and reality highlights drama. But ironically, drama has more luck than life, with an additional happy ending. Once I naively believed that reality was like drama, with a guaranteed happy reunion at the end. However, reality cruelly told me that if you make a mistake, it's a mistake; if you miss an opportunity, it's missed; if you lose something, it's lost. It's hard to make up for past mistakes, it's hard to regain previous feelings, and it's hard to reclaim lost territory...

Ha, ha, ha, when heaven causes calamity, it can be forgiven; but when one causes calamity oneself, there is no escape from death. Let's mock ourselves. Thinking alcohol could numb our sensory nerves, thinking memory fragments would just fade away from the big screen in our minds, glass after glass. Fate always likes to play jokes on you. Drinking to forget sorrow only brings more sorrow, cutting water with a knife only makes it flow more. After waking up, you find that those beautiful moments haven't disappeared. Instead, as time passes, they've directed a movie of memories that keeps playing on the screen in your mind. Uncontrollable tears crystallize at the corners of your eyes. You try to look up at the sky, hoping the tears will return to your eyes. But there are too many tears, and your tear ducts are too small to hold them all. No matter how much you look up, large drops of tears still slide down your cheeks, splashing onto the ground, sounding resounding, a kind of echo that only the depths of your heart can hear.

Is it because I once poured too much emotion into you that it overflowed? The "cup" lost its balance because of this. I hurriedly reached out to catch it, but it was too late. The "cup" fell onto the "floor" in my heart with a bang, shattering. The emotions spread out, and I wanted to collect them back, but alas, there was no container by my side. Could you give me another "cup"?

People prefer perfect endings. In dramas, no matter how many obstacles, twists, conflicts, or misunderstandings, everything can be resolved in the end, leading to heartfelt reconciliation, haha. When you're immersed in this dream, reality mercilessly knocks on your head, "Boy, it's time to come back. You've been dreaming for too long." "I won't go back. Why is real life always like a drama, yet the drama always ends perfectly while real life is tough?" "Haha, because drama is designed by people who like happy endings, while outside the drama, fate arranges things. Fate doesn't like happy endings, so when you have even a tiny flaw, it mercilessly strikes you down, not letting you have a chance to recover. Only if you do your best from the start, cherish what you have, and avoid making mistakes that fate can exploit, can the ending possibly be good."

People always ask themselves, did I really make a mistake? An explanation is seen as a cover-up. Think about it: if you didn't care, who would bother to explain? The most painful thing in the world isn't dying normally, but dying inexplicably.

People always hope that the beautiful scenes in dramas will come true in reality, challenging fate, carefully planning and arranging. Fate, after all, is a great director, and every step we take is within his expectations. He gets ahead of us, and ultimately our drama plays out according to his plans, while we remain immersed in the beautiful blueprint. When we wake up, we no longer have the strength to call for a redo, only endless regret and despair.

If the shooting isn't good, the drama can be redone, but in reality, taking one wrong step makes it hard to start over. When surface appearances blind the other person's eyes, all your efforts become useless. You may have done work, but in others' eyes, your force is always perpendicular to the direction of progress, resulting in zero work.

Inside and outside the drama, a person always plays different roles, but they know the script inside the drama has already been written, everything is set in stone. What they truly care about is outside the drama, where the script is always changing unpredictably. How the next second will play out, what the next moment will bring, and what the next step will lead to are all unknowns. When we want to participate in the script design, we realize that fate has already arranged everything. Our life paths don't go as smoothly as expected, instead filled with difficulties. Many people are scared and lost by these unexpected arrangements, unsure of what to do next.

In both drama and reality, one thing is the same: people always focus on appearances. Why do people always focus on appearances? To better understand you, chatting with a few close friends, and you accuse them of disloyalty; to get a promotion opportunity, having dinner with the boss, and you accuse them of infidelity; sometimes even asking for directions, and you wonder why they ask strangers... There are too many examples. Is jealousy really that satisfying? Can the relationship between two people be less durable than a single moment? People need trust. Without investigating the root cause, accusations are meaningless. Should we let a single moment destroy all the days we've spent together?

Watching flowers bloom and fall in the courtyard without being disturbed by honor or disgrace, looking at clouds gather and disperse in the sky without intention of staying or leaving. In this complicated world, learning to treat everything around us with a calm heart is also a kind of realm! Sometimes, we might be blinded by appearances, our hearts becoming confused. We don't know how to move forward, whether to believe in what's in front of us or the underlying truth. Believing in what's in front of us is easy, as long as you agree to believe in the surface, everything falls into fate's trap. Don't forget, fate loves tragic endings. Are you willing to let your life script be completely determined by fate? On the contrary, uncovering the underlying truth is difficult. It requires effort and trust. When the truth emerges, it's like the fog clearing from your eyes, allowing you to participate in your own script. Give the other person a chance, give yourself a chance, don't let each other's efforts go to waste. We should also participate in writing our own scripts.

A prodigal son returning is worth more than gold. Can you not give me a harbor, a chance for my ship, which has taken the wrong route, to dock again? Would you let me drift aimlessly in the ocean until I'm swallowed by the sea?

Reality in drama, drama in reality.
