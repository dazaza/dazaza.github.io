It was long after that I finally regarded you as ordinary.

by zhouzhou56 on 2012-02-20 21:20:27

Much later, I finally regarded you as ordinary.

Allow me to arrogantly understand: Love is being with someone all day and constantly tolerating their flaws until you get used to them and accept them, even indulgently laughing at them; Love is showing your ugliest side, then shamelessly retaliating when mocked: "If you have the guts, change person!"; Love is no longer deliberately paying attention to one's figure and appearance, letting the belly fat grow out; Love is shopping for an afternoon and buying not a single piece of clothing for oneself. Despite always complaining, every time you unconsciously do this.

Many times I thought I didn't love anymore because life was so dull without any ripple. Even if we argue or fight, it has all become normal. Every action, every word, every strand of hair, even smell, body temperature, skin touch, are all too familiar and seem completely ordinary, there is no more heartbeat.

It's strange, I know everything about this man, and everything has changed. This person is completely different from what I originally imagined. Not handsome at all, average taste, picky eater, no particularly strong sports skills, narcissistic, stingy, talkative, messy, ungraceful in high-altitude maintenance, with a pile of flaws that can't be finished listing. But in my heart, no other man can compare to him. Those who are smarter than him aren't as handsome, those who are handsomer aren't as cute, those with better personalities don't have his physique. Each time I make such awkward comparisons in my mind, and cheat countless times in imagination, but in reality, I still only accept this man in front of me. No matter how outrageous the jokes are with other guys, accidentally touching their skin will strangely induce nausea. Only this man in front of me can give an indescribable sense of fulfillment. When he buys a piece of clothing, attends a formal occasion and asks me for advice on his outfit, when he likes the dessert I recommend, when he depends on my taste, when he doesn't see me for three hours and starts looking for me everywhere... This is a satisfaction others cannot give, when a person needs you everywhere.

Thus, I slowly became a vulgar woman, no longer writing flowery articles, no longer pretending to be elegant and refined, no longer spending an hour trying on clothes in front of the mirror, no longer taking half an hour to put on makeup for a dinner, starting to nag, complain about everything, calculate how to spend the living expenses, start to be calculating, become less lovable. When you also endure these one by one, I think, this is love.

Much later, I finally regarded you as ordinary, love.

I remember the youthful days, those once hesitant infatuations. Hiding under the quilt late at night waiting for a call that may come; insomnia due to seeing you playing around with a girl during the day; extremely excited after watching a movie together by coincidence; at a loss after a hasty kiss. Perhaps there were gifts carefully treasured and heartbeats carefully preserved; perhaps there were unforgettable moments breaking barriers to be together bravely; perhaps there were text messages kept in the phone for a long time unwilling to delete; perhaps there were deep despairs leaving lifelong scars on the wrist. All of these, undoubtedly profound, profound enough to last a lifetime. But sometimes, one would rather not recall them. Is it like this?

Much later, I sat in a small eatery by the school gate eating a bowl of wontons. In the misty hot steam, I saw the patient and inclusive eyes of the man opposite me, and finally understood that blandness is love. Just like this plain bowl of clear soup wontons, which could comfort my stomach perfectly on such a rainy and chilly evening.

Much later, I finally regarded you as ordinary, never avoiding, never escaping, always calm, composed and content.
