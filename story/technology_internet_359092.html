Change the way you light your world. Change the whole planet.

by ditan360 on 2010-06-25 09:56:48

Seminar Venue

Domestic and international policy makers and experts in the fields of sustainable development and green lighting gathered in Beijing on the 24th to discuss how seemingly small efforts, such as replacing light bulbs, can help effectively mitigate the challenges brought about by climate change.

"Sustainable energy is a fundamental prerequisite for China to achieve the United Nations Millennium Development Goals, and green energy is also crucial to reaching the goal of a moderately prosperous society," said Akie Morimoto, Deputy Country Director of the United Nations Development Programme at the seminar. "Phasing out incandescent bulbs is not just a possibility, but an activity that is taking place globally, and is being carried out on a large scale in China."

As part of the "China's Gradual Phase-out of Incandescent Lamps and Acceleration of Energy-saving Lamp Promotion Project", this seminar was co-hosted by the United Nations Development Programme and the National Development and Reform Commission, with the aim of allowing participants to understand and exchange experiences from the first year of project implementation in China.

At the seminar, relevant personnel introduced the main achievements since the start of the project, including the formulation of a roadmap for phasing out incandescent lamps and plans for promoting energy-saving lamps, as well as activities to distribute energy-saving lamps in remote areas. For example, with the support of this project, Dingxi in Gansu received a donation of 100,000 energy-saving lamps in December 2009.

Lighting accounts for 19% of energy consumption, and the use of energy-saving lamps can reduce this figure by one-third or more. As UN Secretary-General Ban Ki-moon stated at the signing ceremony of the green lighting project last July, "By changing a light bulb, and at the same time changing our way of thinking, we can change the entire world."

About the Green Lighting Project

"China's Gradual Phase-out of Incandescent Lamps and Acceleration of Energy-saving Lamp Promotion Project" is a three-year project. Of the $84 million in project funds, $14 million comes from the Global Environment Facility, $18.9 million from the government, $32 million from the private sector, $600,000 from other funding, and the remaining $18.5 million from in-kind contributions. This large-scale national project is jointly implemented by the National Development and Reform Commission and the United Nations Development Programme, aiming to reduce carbon emissions caused by the use of incandescent lamps by approximately 4.4 million tonnes annually. The project strategy includes promoting energy-saving lamps and driving changes across the entire Chinese lighting market towards high-efficiency energy lighting products, while also promoting related technologies and practices.

In addition to transforming the lighting market and improving the quality of light bulbs, this project also aims to educate the public about the necessity of using high-efficiency light bulbs, as well as addressing the issue of proper disposal of waste.

"China's Gradual Phase-out of Incandescent Lamps and Acceleration of Energy-saving Lamp Promotion Project" is being carried out under the context of promoting a sustainable development society. In 2009, China invested $36 billion in the renewable energy sector, and by the end of 2009, energy consumption per unit of GDP had decreased by 14% compared to 2005. The support provided by the United Nations Development Programme for China's low-carbon transition is not only reflected in the green lighting project, but also through other environment and energy-related projects, with a total amount reaching $683 million.
