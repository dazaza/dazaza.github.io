Bright and charming eyes can attract people's attention and praise. However, for the auricles located on both sides of the skull, except for nicknaming deformed auricles, people rarely appreciate normal auricles.

Some animals' auricles can collect sound waves to help survival. Human beings have evolved to the present day, and the function of the auricle seems to be limited to wearing glasses and ear accessories. However, anthropologists do use the shape of the auricle as a method to identify races, psychologists see it as a symbol of personality and mental illness, criminologists use it like fingerprints to identify criminals, and traditional Chinese medicine regards the auricle as a site for visual diagnosis and acupuncture treatment.

The aesthetic view of the auricle changes with time and region. In the artworks of early cultures, the shape of the auricle is often distorted or stylized. Buddha's portraits always have big ears. In Oriental ethnic groups, protruding ears, which are taboo for Westerners, are still considered by some people as symbols of success, happiness, and wealth. The presence of auricular tubercles is common among most Chinese, but few people pay attention to its existence. In the West, however, overly prominent auricular tubercles were once considered a sign of a born criminal.

What is "protruding ear" plastic surgery?

Ears that stick out too much to the side are commonly known as "protruding ears." Protruding ear plastic surgery involves bringing the protruding ears closer to the skull. By the age of four, a child’s ears have developed close to adult size, so the surgery is usually performed between 4-14 years old. The earlier the surgery, the less teasing and psychological damage the child will experience; however, unless the child themselves requests a change in the shape of their ears, there is no need to force them to undergo surgery. Adults with protruding ears can also correct them through surgery.

Protruding ears are a relatively common congenital auricle deformity, often seen bilaterally, characterized by slightly larger auricles and a flat upper half. The antihelix is underdeveloped, losing its normal contour. Normally, the angle between the concha and the scapha of the auricle is 90 degrees, but in protruding ears, the concha is overdeveloped, causing this angle to exceed 150 degrees. In severe cases, the entire auricle lacks any curvature structure, resembling a butterfly.

Who is suitable for protruding ear plastic surgery?

1. Protruding ear plastic surgery involves minimal trauma, and generally, anyone seeking improvement in the shape of the auricle can undergo the surgery.

2. Those suffering from acute or chronic otitis media should first receive treatment for the middle ear.

3. To not affect the normal psychological development of children, surgery can generally be performed around the age of 5-6 years, at which point the auricle is only a few millimeters smaller than an adult's, and the surgery has little impact on its development.

4. Bilateral auricle reshaping should be completed in one surgery.

Methods of protruding ear plastic surgery:

There are many methods for protruding ear plastic surgery. One method that changes the wall of the concha is relatively simple and mainly includes two approaches:

1. A wedge-shaped piece of skin and cartilage is removed from behind the ear, and then the conchal cartilage is sutured to the mastoid process;

2. An elliptical piece of conchal cartilage is directly removed below the antihelix.

In addition, there are more methods that form folds and elevations in the antihelix. The principle mainly involves altering the anterior or lateral surface of the auricular cartilage or changing the posterior or medial surface of the auricular cartilage to fold it into shape.

Characteristics after protruding ear plastic surgery:

Within the short term after surgery, avoid pressing or hitting the ear area to prevent suture line rupture. Generally, antibiotics are routinely used 3-5 days post-surgery, and the bandages can be removed and sutures taken out about 10 days later.

Contraindications for protruding ear plastic surgery:

1. Those with hypertrophic scars should not undergo surgery.

2. Those suffering from acute or chronic otitis media should not undergo surgery until the middle ear is treated, and the inflammation is controlled or cured before proceeding with surgery.

3. Bilateral auricle reshaping should be completed in one surgery.
