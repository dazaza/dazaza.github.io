Projector rental in Guangzhou

by jcauc88512 on 2012-02-27 10:15:50

Daheng hopes to bind some good partners through capital complementarity. Therefore, in choosing partners, Daheng is still very cautious. After all, Daheng is positioned as a distribution platform. It's impossible for Daheng to set up a branch company and hire dozens of people to do projects. It must rely on dealers to develop the market and protect their interests. In today's fierce environment, there needs to be a reasonable return. This requires Daheng to provide various compound and combined products to dealers. In the aspect of ViewSonic, it's also such a strategy. Effective product binding can indeed increase overall sales. I believe that Daheng will cultivate a relatively good system in the next one or two years, which is also Daheng's unique property.

In April 2011, when Daheng just started cooperating with ViewSonic, it mentioned transitioning to value-added products and clearly reduced the proportion of low-end SVGA products to 35%. At that time, Daheng did mention this concept, meaning that the direction of ViewSonic products would not be limited to mid-to-low-end products. Daheng was expanding its product line towards mid-to-high-end products. Actually, the reason for reducing the proportion of mid-to-low-end products wasn't because they wanted to decrease sales. Logically speaking, the more mid-to-low-end products sold, the larger the market share would be. However, in practice, Daheng also needed to consider value since Daheng is a brand company that needs to take into account brand enhancement.

In the first quarter, statistics showed that the proportion of SVGA products from Daheng was close to 60%. At that time, Daheng was still in a purely distribution model, selling only mid-to-low-end products. Last month, after another statistical review, mid-to-low-end products now accounted for about 36%. This shows that Daheng hopes for the elimination of a product line to come with growth in two areas: first, an overall increase in the number of Daheng's products sold in 2011, but not achieved by cutting prices on low-end machines; secondly, Daheng aims to refine its operations and increase channels across different regions to expand coverage and thereby increase sales volume. On the other hand, it involves changes in the structure of Daheng’s products. Daheng does not simply rely on price cuts for mid-to-low-end products to capture the market and boost sales. In 2011, Daheng promoted the concept of 3D, launched projectors with handwriting functions, high-end machines, and home-use devices, significantly expanding its product line towards higher-end directions. Thus, the proportion of low-end price-cutting products has decreased.

For the channel, whether they are willing to deal with more valuable products, this involves cooperation and communication issues with the channel. For Daheng, previously it was professional product sales. Today, this team at Daheng is transforming. The future goal of Daheng is to become a comprehensive team capable of selling both professional products and distribution products.
