With the rapid development of the world economy and the continuous growth in demand for oil, conventional oil reserves are gradually decreasing. Heavy oil, with great exploration potential and broad development prospects, has become one of the important energy resources in the 21st century.

So-called heavy oil refers to unconventional petroleum in general, including heavy crude oil, high-viscosity oil, oil sands, natural asphalt, and oil shale. Relevant statistical data indicates that the geological reserves of heavy oil in the world amount to approximately 10 billion tons. The proportions of heavy oil, conventional crude oil, and natural gas geological reserves are 53%, 25%, and 22% respectively. The geological reserves of heavy oil are equivalent to the sum of conventional oil and gas reserves.

China is rich in heavy oil resources. Onshore heavy oil and asphalt resources account for more than 20% of total oil resources, with a predicted resource volume of 19.8 billion tons. After over 50 years of exploration, China has discovered more than 70 heavy oil fields in 12 basins nationwide. In recent years, deep heavy oil resources have been found in the Tuha Basin and Tarim Basin. By the end of 2005, China had explored 2.06 billion tons of heavy oil geological reserves. Heavy oil has become an important component of China’s crude oil production. The power generation equipment industry is a key fixed asset investment industry supported by the state, shouldering the responsibility of providing complete sets of power generation equipment for the national economy and people's daily lives. In recent years, it has developed rapidly.

In 2008, the production of power generation equipment in the country continued to grow. From January to October, the cumulative output of power generation equipment reached 107,458.3 million kilowatts, an increase of 1.9% year-on-year. China's electric power industry industrial policies will develop towards optimizing the power structure, prioritizing the development of renewable energy sources such as hydropower, nuclear power, wind power, solar power, and biomass power. For coal-fired power plants, the policy emphasizes "focusing on structural optimization, conserving resources, emphasizing environmental protection, and improving technical and economic levels." In the near future, renewable and new energy power generation equipment will face good development opportunities, with its proportion in China's total installed capacity gradually increasing.

Currently, the international petroleum sector still faces some unresolved challenges in terms of heavy oil exploration, development, refining, comprehensive utilization, and environmental protection. Among these, the most critical issue is how to innovate and develop based on existing technology to significantly reduce costs. Additionally, with the continuous increase in oil demand, how to enhance the importance of exploration and development in various heavy oil producing countries worldwide and ensure reasonable use of heavy oil by consumer countries has become the key to the sustainable development of the heavy oil industry and even the global petroleum industry.

Heavy oil power generation units are mostly medium- and low-speed generators, characterized by low operating costs, long-term continuous operation, reliability, long service life, fast return on investment, and low noise. The drawback is relatively higher initial investment. They are the best choice for factories, large-scale mining enterprises, and regional electricity usage that require uninterrupted power supply.
