Introduction to the Development of Injection Breast Augmentation:

Injectable breast augmentation materials mimicking human soft tissues were widely used in clinical practice in the 1960s. These materials are stable in shape, complete, and elastic, mainly used for soft tissue filling and correction of certain organ shapes. In the 1980s, they began to be extensively used for breast reshaping.

In September 1995, the European Bureau of the International Organization for Standardization and the International Technical Appraisal Committee also validated the clinical application results of these materials. The 1996 International Symposium on Plastic Surgery pointed out that the method of using these materials for soft tissue filling is the direction of future development in cosmetic surgery. In September 1997, its safety was officially confirmed, after which it started to be widely promoted and applied in plastic surgery in countries like Europe and America. After the two fillers, "Injetolfar" from Ukraine and "Omeida" produced by Jilin Aodong Pharmaceutical Company, were approved by the National Medical Products Administration of China for import and clinical use in 1998, injection breast augmentation began to be promoted domestically.

In 1999, the national health department stipulated that injection breast augmentation must be performed by formal plastic surgery institutions and doctors who have undergone related training. Diaoyutai Hospital's Department of Plastic Surgery was one of the first recognized "Designated Units for Injection Breast Augmentation." The following year, due to outstanding surgical quality, it was certified as the "Training Center for Injection Breast Augmentation Physicians" and the "Complication Management Center."

How do complications from injection breast augmentation occur?

Between 1999 and 2003, due to lax actual control by health departments, untrained doctors and even numerous non-medical institutions, driven by profit, also carried out this surgical procedure, inevitably causing many adverse consequences. Based on our hospital's clinical experience of over 500 cases of injection breast augmentation repair surgeries over the years, the largest and most common issue leading to the failure of injection breast augmentation lies in the operator's insufficient or blind grasp of the injection layer position. This material must be directly injected into the space between the mammary tissue and the pectoralis major muscle. Only then will the resulting breasts be overall soft, with a full shape, and if needed, easy to remove. If the injection position is too low, the material will be injected into the pectoralis major muscle, forming hematomas, hard lumps that are immobile, poor shape often accompanied by persistent pain; if the injection position is too low, the material will be injected into the mammary glands, forming one or more smaller free-floating hard lumps, thereby affecting the normal function of the breast. Therefore, our hospital proposed the "single-layer injection" theory as early as 1999. Practical evidence shows that only in this way can the aforementioned complications be avoided, ensuring the overall softness and firmness of the breast post-surgery.

The Best Surgical Method for Handling Injection Breast Augmentation Complications:

Most hospitals and doctors, due to a lack of basic understanding of injection breast augmentation, often use the "incision stripping removal method" for handling post-injection breast augmentation sequelae — that is, making an incision at the areola or other parts of the breast to remove the material. This method not only leaves obvious wounds and severely damages the mammary tissue (resulting in scars, slow recovery), but in severe cases, it can affect the normal function of the breast, even leading to the serious consequence of breast removal at some renowned hospitals.

Pay Attention to Your Health Regarding Injection Breast Augmentation:

The success or failure of a breast augmentation surgery involves many aspects and stages: First, the quality of the breast augmentation filler material is crucial; never choose materials harmful to the human body or counterfeit inferior products. Secondly, the conditions of the hospital performing the surgery, the technical level of the physician, and their clinical experience are also very important. Thirdly, ensure the surgery is performed under sterile conditions. Lastly, consider the individual's own physical immune (anti-infection) status. Some beauty institutions do not possess medical aesthetic plastic qualifications yet operate beyond their scope; some have incomplete medical hardware and lax sterile operations; others have doctors with immature personal technical skills who rashly perform surgeries, all of which could potentially lead to disputes.
