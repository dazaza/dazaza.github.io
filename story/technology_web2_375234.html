**This Issue's Theme: Shopping Search Engines**

E-commerce and search engines have a natural connection, especially in terms of product guidance, price comparison, and shop presentation. To a certain extent, search engines directly determine the sales performance of online stores. Currently, we observe that the cost of e-commerce is continuously increasing, particularly in promotion and marketing. Due to the monopoly of giants, merchants' bargaining power has weakened, forcing them to accept high-cost promotions. At this point, we especially hope for a new, more direct, and lower-cost promotion method, which we refer to as "shopping search engines."

As early as the initial stages of e-commerce and search engines, shopping search engines already existed. Especially with the success of well-known foreign shopping search engines like Shopping.com and BizRate.com, China saw a batch of imitators emerge in 2004, mainly focusing on price comparison and cashback services. By 2009, with the launch of price comparison and cashback services by Youdao and Google, it reached its peak. Looking back now, although no major players emerged, sites like YaYa Search, Clever Point, and Cashback Network are gradually maturing... In early December this year, with Amazon's acquisition of local shopping search engine Milo.com, the popularity and emergence of Taobao cashback websites like Wuliuzhe and Wuchizhe, along with the start of visual search engines like Taotao Sou and Bishi Gu, the topic of shopping search engines has once again come to our attention. It will also become the discussion theme for the second session of NetEase Technology's "Wudaokou Salon" event in December.

**About NetEase Technology's "Wudaokou Salon"**

As the internet continues to deeply integrate into people's work and lives, more people choose to start their entrepreneurial journey with the internet. With China's internet users surpassing 420 million and internet companies creating capital myths, the world believes that China's internet industry has the largest and most promising market and business opportunities.

In this gold rush, replication and innovation have always gone hand in hand. Looking at the current large and small enterprises in China's internet sector, too many can find prototypes abroad, but they are different. This is what we call "innovation through replication, replicating while innovating." If we all recognize this approach, then starting from small ideas and small website models, new ideas and new websites fused with various Chinese elements will emerge wave after wave, advancing the development of the internet industry.

When these ideas and concepts become the starting point for entrepreneurship, NetEase Technology, as a media and observer, collects and organizes new models and ideas from domestic and foreign internet sectors through the "One Site Per Day" column. Our aim is to provide new clues and ideas for internet entrepreneurs, generate thought collisions, and inspire more creativity, leading to Chinese-style entrepreneurship and ultimately fostering the rise of a group of emerging websites and internet companies.
