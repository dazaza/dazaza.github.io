Central Park Parent-Child Paradise will add a barbecue area; indoor badminton gym to start construction within the year

For urbanites living amidst steel and concrete, kicking a ball, planting some vegetables, or basking in the sun near their homes over the weekend is a necessity for leisure and relaxation. For the residents of Jiaxing, the increasingly well-equipped Central Park is the top choice for a relaxed lifestyle. Yesterday, we learned from the Sports Industry Development Investment Co., Ltd., a subsidiary of Jiafu Group, that the second phase of the Central Park Family Fun Land will be launched this year, and the sports facilities section will also undergo reorganization to provide sports enthusiasts with more comprehensive facilities.

　　Enjoying the fruits of one's labor

　　Since the opening of the Family Fun Land at Central Park last December, it has been favored by many citizens. According to information from the Sports Industry Development Investment Co., Ltd., the first batch of over 200 plots of vegetable land has already been rented out. In just two short months, online farming has been realistically transplanted into real life, allowing some citizens to truly realize their "farmer dreams." Many "farmers" have already started eating their own homegrown green and environmentally friendly vegetables, enjoying the fruits of their labor.

　　It is understood that the second phase of the Family Fun Land is located in the central area of the park, offering nearly 100 new plots of vegetable land, planned to be launched between the end of February and early March. Unlike the first phase, all plots in the second phase will adopt a self-managed rental model, allowing citizens to fully enjoy the fun of farming. Due to varying plot sizes, the rental will be carried out on a one-plot-one-price basis.

　　To further enhance the overall environment of the Family Fun Land, enrich its activities, and increase its appeal, the management of the Family Fun Land has already lined up a series of follow-up plans. For instance, to create a better vegetable-growing environment, rest benches, trash bins, swings, and other facilities will be added within the park, along with trellises for climbing plants like cucumbers and cowpeas. This not only enhances the beauty of the park but also allows "farmers" to participate in picking events during harvest season, exchanging planting experiences.

　　In addition, the Family Fun Land has disclosed plans to hold two lectures in spring and autumn, inviting experts to guide on-site planting techniques and share knowledge. Each quarter, there will be a competition to select the best seasonal plots, where participants can freely take photos and upload them for voting.

　　Soccer field grass has been relaid

　　Besides the launch of the second phase of the Family Fun Land, other projects in Central Park are progressing smoothly. Currently, Central Park has one soccer field, four tennis courts, three basketball courts, and three gateball courts. The head of the Central Park Management Department told reporters that the soccer field underwent a grass relaying process starting from the end of last year. "We replaced the original Bermuda grass with rye grass, ensuring the field remains green even in winter."

　　Many sports enthusiasts have offered improvement suggestions for the original soccer field, leading the park to level the field again. "If the previous field was more like a lawn, the current field meets amateur competition standards. We've installed nets around the field and are preparing to install high-pole lights to meet competition needs." To the north of the soccer field, an indoor badminton hall with 18 courts is set to begin construction within the year.

　　Every time spring brings warm weather, many citizens come to Central Park for the weekend. Some have expressed the desire to barbecue within the park. "We originally planned to place the barbecue area in the kite lawn area, but considering environmental protection, we changed the location and prepared a dedicated space within the Family Fun Land for citizens to barbecue and picnic."
