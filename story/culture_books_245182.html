"Flowers Bloom at Midsummer" - A Tragic Romance Between Brother and Sister That Moved Millions of Readers

by jqrxass on 2009-10-04 20:55:11

Pure love, original sin, punishment, redemption - challenging the limits of emotion, law, and reason, touching millions of readers over 3 years of anticipation, focusing on juvenile crime.

The story unfolds from a major case involving organized crime. Detective Ye Xiangrong sends an undercover agent, code-named 1149, to investigate Cheng Hao, the largest hidden smuggler in Haiping City. In the dangerous confrontation with Cheng Hao, Ye Xiangrong unexpectedly uncovers a tale of pure love and redemption buried amidst criminal deeds. Orphan Wei Rufeng is adopted by Grandma Xia and grows up alongside his sister Xia Ruhua as childhood sweethearts. Fate's twists unintentionally lead Wei Rufeng to become one of Cheng Hao's "gangsters." Their destinies, manipulated by Cheng Hao, take a dark turn despite their unwavering love. Yet, during that summer, they truly experienced happiness...

*Hua Kai Ban Xia* is adapted from a true event witnessed by Jiuye Huan in 1999. Through cold yet vivid prose akin to cinematic shots, it narrates a poignant story of lost youth. It features intricate case investigations, a passionate sibling romance, a deadly battle between justice and evil, and the gray areas of law and society. There are pure and sincere emotions as well as regretful betrayals of conscience. With dramatic character fates and captivating plots, Jiuye Huan pushes the boundaries of human nature in terms of emotion, law, and morality, crafting a deeply moving story based on real events. *Hua Kai Ban Xia* has been hailed by readers as a new brutal youth literature—"more touching than Infernal Affairs, more poetic than Young and Dangerous."

*Hua Kai Ban Xia*: Twenty-Two Years Old - Farewell to Beauty

Wei Rufeng and Xia Ruhua decided to escape Haiping, the city where they met and suffered. They started planning a real life, a life solely for the two of them. After calculating their savings, there were tens of thousands in their account. However, Xia Ruhua refused to use the money. Based on Wei Rufeng's monthly salary of 1500 yuan, she deducted living expenses and kept 10,000 yuan, locking the rest untouched in the drawer.

Xia Ruhua's condition annoyed Cheng Hao greatly, so at the end of the year, he gave her LSD. The hallucinogenic drug finally brought some vitality back to Xia Ruhua's lifeless spirit. She developed a strong dependence on LSD and began experiencing frequent hallucinations, speaking incoherently to Cheng Hao. In her confused consciousness, she resisted the passage of time, alternating between spring and autumn, memories and reality intertwining like a dream. She firmly believed Wei Rufeng would come back for her, sinking deeper into this illusion and refusing to awaken.
