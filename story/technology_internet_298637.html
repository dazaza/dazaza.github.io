If the humidity in the air is below 30%, it will be too dry and uncomfortable, so it can be controlled by a reliable humidity controller to maintain a range of 50%～60%. When using, doors and windows should be closed as much as possible to achieve the desired dehumidification effect indoors. The machine body should be placed flat, not tilted or laid horizontally, to avoid machine failure or abnormal noise. If the water storage inside the dehumidifier is too full, to prevent overflow and wetting the floor, please unplug the power cord first, then empty the water in the storage bucket before moving the machine while it is running. After moving the dehumidifier, it is best to let it stand still for four to six hours before turning it on again, because the refrigerant in the compressor pipelines needs time to return to its original position after being moved. Clean the filter net regularly (about once every two weeks) to maintain the machine's dehumidification efficiency, dust-removal efficiency, and lifespan. The filter net can be washed with clear water at a temperature below 40℃. Using cleaning agents for washing is even better. After washing away the dust, rinse it with water, dry it in the shade, and reinstall it back into the machine body. When cleaning the machine body, gently wipe it with a damp cloth. Avoid direct splashing of water to prevent damage to electrical insulation. If there are adhesive substances on the machine body surface, they can be cleaned with soapy water. Avoid using gasoline, kerosene, solvents, or spray insecticides, which may cause paint peeling or discoloration. Do not use thin rods or wires to poke inside the machine to avoid malfunctions or dangers. Unplug the power cord during power outages, long-term disuse, or travel to extend the life of the household dehumidifier (household dehumidifiers costing 100-400 yuan use semiconductor electronic components for refrigeration and dehumidification, suitable for closets, archive cabinets, electrical cabinets, children's rooms, storage rooms, bathrooms, and other smaller spaces, but not suitable for family rooms).
