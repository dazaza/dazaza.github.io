Wine.com: Great Wall Huaxia 95 Dry Red Wine

by glsd5678264 on 2012-02-21 17:51:30

Product Introduction: Great Wall Wine is a famous brand under COFCO Group, one of the top 500 enterprises in the world. It is the first brand of Chinese wine, the "China Famous Brand Product" and the "Industry Landmark Brand". For many consecutive years, its production and sales have ranked first nationwide. In 2004, the "Great Wall" trademark was recognized as a China Well-known Trademark by the State Administration for Industry and Commerce. In 2006, Great Wall Wine became the official wine product for the Beijing 2008 Olympic Games. In the same year, it topped the "2006 China Top 500 Brands" list with a brand value of 12.587 billion yuan. In 2009, Great Wall Wine became the exclusive designated wine for the 2010 Shanghai World Expo.

Product Features: The Great Wall Huaxia 95 Dry Red Wine is made from Cabernet Sauvignon, an internationally renowned noble wine grape variety cultivated in China's famous grape-growing region. It is brewed according to the international O, I, V standard, with a deep ruby red color, a mellow, round, and balanced taste, and emits a delicate vanilla fragrance. After being aged in French oak barrels and stored in bottles for many years, it is a dry red wine masterpiece worth collecting.

Wine Review: Deep ruby red in color, with a mellow, round, and balanced taste, emitting a delicate vanilla fragrance.

Winery Introduction: Great Wall Wine owns three major wineries in China's best grape-growing regions: Hebei Shacheng, Hebei Changli, and Shandong Yantai. Its famous products such as the Great Wall Sanggan Winery series, Huaxia Vineyard Small Region series, Star Dry Red series, and Coast Wine series have won the highest awards at multiple international professional wine tasting events in Paris, Brussels, London, etc., and are exported to more than 20 countries and regions including France. With a unique style and taste, it brings consumers a colorful wine experience. Great Wall Wine is also the only Chinese wine to be included on the "21st Century Luxury Brand List - Top Brand List" by the authoritative global commercial research institution, Gallup USA.

Great Wall Sanggan Winery, established in 1979 along the Sanggan River in Hebei Shacheng, has a unique 75-hectare ancient fossil mud river soil vineyard located on the world's golden wine grape growing belt at the same latitude as Bordeaux, France (40 degrees north). The unique microclimate environment, superior wine grapes nurtured in a 30-year-old prime vineyard, and adherence to traditional winery wine craftsmanship have created the typical robust river valley style of Great Wall Sanggan Winery wine: rich layers, multi-variations, clear structure, and concentrated taste. The "Gesha City Vineyard" recorded in "The Travels of Marco Polo" is today's location of the Great Wall Sanggan Winery. The 2 million-year-old ancient fossil mud river soil, unique microclimate, 30-year-old prime grape vines... countless advantageous conditions determine that this place is born for top wines. After 30 years of refinement, Great Wall Sanggan Winery has become the Chinese coordinate in the global winery map, standing shoulder to shoulder with world-renowned wineries like Lafite and Margaux.
