Some doctors have no moral bottom line

People's Congress Representative Zhong Nanshan criticizes medical chaos

Some doctors have no moral bottom line

Shenzhen Evening News reported that according to Qilu Evening News, since the SARS outbreak in 2003, people have remembered the name Zhong Nanshan. From being a three-time member of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference to being a representative of the Eleventh National People's Congress, his willingness to speak frankly and "high-profile political questioning" style has earned him titles such as "cannon king" and "conscience of the medical community", making him a focus of media attention for many consecutive years at the two sessions.

Zhong Nanshan said that to promote China's medical reform and eradicate the various chaos currently existing in the medical field, medical elites represented by tertiary Class A hospitals need to be more proactive. He also believes that medical humanities should also be included in medical reform.

Throwing live babies away as dead ones, giving people abortifacient drugs instead of pregnancy-preserving drugs... Zhong Nanshan recounted the various chaos he encountered in his work, "This shows that some medical personnel have lost their basic medical ethics." Zhong Nanshan said that if medical personnel could practice medicine with a clear conscience, many doctor-patient conflicts could be resolved.

When talking about the monitoring of PM2.5, Zhong Nanshan said that comprehensive monitoring nationwide should begin now. Just monitoring is not enough, specific measures must be taken to solve air pollution problems. "If the environment is not good, even if you have a good lifestyle, it's useless, those diseases will still occur."

For many years, Zhong Nanshan has been known for speaking the truth, earning him the title of "cannon hand" at the two sessions. When asked "Why dare to speak the truth, why different from other representatives and committee members", Zhong Nanshan smiled and counter-asked: "What does it mean to ask why tell the truth? Is telling lies advocated? One should tell the truth."

Suggestive voices

Spring Festival holiday

Should be extended to the Lantern Festival

Committee Member Gu Yili

Spring Festival holiday

Should be extended to the Lantern Festival

Shenzhen Evening News reported that according to New Express, "Our citizens have too few holidays." National Committee Member Gu Yili, Vice President of Guangdong University of Foreign Studies, told New Express in an interview that he plans to submit a proposal suggesting the restoration of the May Day long holiday and extending the Spring Festival long holiday to the Lantern Festival, "Holidays can make ordinary people experience real happiness, isn't it very happy to sleep until you naturally wake up?"

Gu Yili said that when the May Day long holiday was canceled, the consideration was the huge pressure on transportation and tourist attractions at the time, but now citizens have become more rational about holidays, unlike before when everyone rushed out, and they can plan their holiday travel well. Moreover, tourism site management is now more mature, so there is no problem with overcrowding at sites.

Finally, Gu Yili pointed out that increasing holidays can improve work efficiency. Gu Yili believed that being immersed in work every day doesn't necessarily lead to high efficiency, "Frankly speaking, a week's work can actually be done in three days."

The crime of illegal absorption of public deposits should be abolished

People's Congress Representative Guo Guangchang suggests

The crime of illegal absorption of public deposits should be abolished

Shenzhen Evening News reported that according to Oriental Morning Post, Guo Guangchang, a national people's congress representative and chairman of Fosun Group, said that private enterprises have played an important role in improving the income level of middle- and low-income groups, especially in expanding the middle-income group. Guo Guangchang said that in order to promote the development of the private economy, it is suggested that the areas where private capital enters finance should be liberalized and the crime of illegal absorption of public deposits should be abolished; more private banks like Minsheng Bank should be cultivated to reduce the financing burden on small and medium-sized enterprises; the scale of loans from small loan companies should be expanded and trustworthy small loan companies should be supported to take the road of banking; direct financing should be relaxed and equity investment type enterprises should be encouraged actively.

Guo Guangchang also suggested reducing corporate taxes, such as abolishing the cultural undertakings construction fee; reducing personal tax burdens, studying individual income tax collection methods based on family units, stimulating consumption, making economic development more dynamic.

Online shopping needs legislation

It is understood that currently, more than 30 countries and regions around the world have enacted comprehensive e-commerce laws. Our country should accelerate the legislative progress of online shopping, putting online shopping on a legal track, making the supervision of online shopping and consumer rights protection have basis, fundamentally ensuring the long-term development of the online shopping industry.

An Internet operation feature-adapted regulatory method should be established, using legal means to promote the establishment of an integrity mechanism, linking the integrity of enterprises with enterprise qualification licenses, policy preferences, etc., focusing on supervising and checking behaviors such as false promotions on group-buying websites, intellectual property protection, prohibited goods sales, product quality, price fraud, etc.

National People's Congress Representative Liao Renbin, General Manager of China Telecom Hunan Company

Tax increase faster than Liu Xiang

Tax cut slower than snail

When we increase taxes, the speed is like Liu Xiang; when we reduce taxes, the speed is like a snail. China's tax revenue has been growing at a rate far exceeding GDP growth for many years. Tax revenue last year increased by 24.8% compared to the previous year, while the GDP growth rate was only about 9.2%. This has far exceeded the normal range.

So far, the number of local governments or departments that have disclosed detailed government budget information remains extremely small, and ordinary taxpayers have no way of knowing how tax funds are allocated and used, let alone tax supervision. Meanwhile, people often see that the annual cost of official vehicles alone across the country is in the hundreds of billions, official dining, overseas trips, training, officials' private villas, illegal construction of office buildings, face-saving projects, etc., which continue despite repeated bans. This greatly damages people's understanding and support for public budgets and the tax system.

National Committee Member Zhang Guojun, Audit Partner of PricewaterhouseCoopers Zhongtian Certified Public Accountants Limited Company
