The main dosage forms of TCM beauty external use prescriptions are powder, solution, ointment, paste, face mask, etc.

(I) Powder

Powder is a medicine made by grinding various different drugs into powder. During preparation, impurities and soil in the drugs should be removed, dried, and then ground into extremely fine powder. The oil and water will separate within the shelf life, screened and then stored in a dry place after being sealed. Normally it can be used as a washing medicine applied to the face and hands, then gently massaged, or used as foundation. Except for a few that are not suitable for dry skin, ordinary people of all skin types and different constitutions can use it. The scope of application is relatively wide.

The powder mainly aims at treating diseases affecting appearance, with functions of protection, absorption, evaporation, drying, relieving itching, reducing friction of the skin against the outside world. It is suitable for acute or subacute dermatitis without exudation, such as heat rash and facial sores. Common formulations include Qing Dai Powder, Liu Yi Powder, and itch-relieving powder.

There are also washes, cosmetic skincare, also known as suspensions, hanging agents, water-powder agents, shaking agents, which are mixtures of water and powder (containing 30%-50% powder). Their effects and indications are basically the same as powders. Such as Qing Dai Wash Agent, Inverted Powder Wash Agent, etc.

(II) Solution

The liquid obtained from decocting herbs, or using boiling water to dissolve powdered herbs and cool it down is called a solution. Additionally, preparations made by pounding fresh animal herbal medicines or using alcohol, vinegar, etc., as solvents can also be categorized under this type. Except for wine agents and vinegar agents, most are prepared and used immediately, and should not be stored for long periods. Solutions have cleaning, itch-relieving, swelling-reducing, astringent, heat-clearing and detoxifying effects. There are two methods of using solutions to treat diseases affecting appearance: external washing and wet dressing. The main purpose of external washing is to clean the affected area, while wet dressing has anti-inflammatory, swelling-reducing, and astringent effects, suitable for contact dermatitis and eczema with more exudation. Wet dressing can be applied either openly or closed. Moreover, solutions can also be used for bathing.

(III) Ointment

It is made by incorporating herbs into a suitable base material, forming a uniform, delicate, easily spreadable semi-solid external preparation for the skin, mucous membranes, or affected areas. It does not dry out easily, adheres well to the body surface, has prolonged and deep effects, protects the skin, and prevents physical and chemical factors from affecting it. According to preparation methods, ointments can be divided into six categories: (1) Adjusted Ointment: Mix animal fat or plant oil (anciently mineral oil like petroleum jelly could also be used) with herbs until they form a paste-like consistency. (2) Decocted Ointment: Use water or alcohol as a solvent, heat and extract soluble components from raw herbs, filter out the residue, and further concentrate by heating. Raw herb juice can also be heated and concentrated directly. (3) Oil-wax Ointment: Use plant or animal oil to boil herbs and extract soluble components, filter, then add beeswax or insect wax to melt into an ointment. (4) Pound and Grind Ointment: Made by pounding and grinding oil-rich raw herbs. (5) Vinegar Ointment: Prepared using vinegar as a solvent following the method for decocted ointments. (6) Honey Ointment: Prepared by mixing honey with finely powdered herbs.

Oily ointments are more greasy, hard to wash off, and unsuitable for oily skin. Ointments made with oils and honey should be prepared in small quantities and stored in a cool place to avoid rancidity. Ointments containing heavy metals may oxidize and reduce over time, decreasing efficacy and potentially increasing toxicity, so they should be prepared temporarily and stored sealed. If the drug separates from the base or water precipitates after storage, stir evenly before use.

Ointments are commonly used external Chinese medicine beauty preparations, often used for health and medical beauty. Used for health beauty, they have effects of moisturizing the skin, pleasing the complexion, whitening, and removing wrinkles. For treating diseases affecting appearance, they have protective, anti-inflammatory, itch-relieving, scab-removing, wart-eliminating effects.

(IV) Paste

Process the herbs by pounding and grinding them into fine powder, then use non-oily liquids as shaping agents to make a semi-solid mud-like agent.

Beauty pastes often use water, alcohol, vinegar, or bodily fluids like milk, butter, saliva, bile, or raw herb juices, mixing one or several to adjust the powder into a mud-like consistency. Except for alcohol and vinegar-based pastes, others should not be prepared in large quantities at once, and should be stored in a cool place. Generally, they can first be made into powder, then adjusted into a paste when needed, especially those made with milk.
