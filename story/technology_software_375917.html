How to use the sync tool on holidays?

by xdash on 2010-12-27 09:25:05

As the end of the year approaches, holidays such as Christmas, New Year's Day, and even Spring Festival that everyone has been looking forward to all year are about to happen one by one. At this time, what can synchronization tools do for us? Here are a few tips on how to use synchronization tools during the holidays.

1. Portable shopping list.

We all have plans to buy new things and reward ourselves, but it's not easy to decide how to spend the budget. So why not use a synchronization tool to record what you want to buy at any time? Before your wallet takes a hit, check this shopping list to see if the items you want to buy are necessary. This can reduce the loss caused by impulsive buying and make spending more planned and purposeful.

2. Plan a group trip.

Use synchronization tools like SugarSync that support shared folders to create a "travel plan" folder. Share various materials for the trip (destination guides, flight information, exchange rate information, hotel reservation status, etc.) with friends who are going together, so that the entire travel team has smooth communication and consistent steps.

At the same time, SugarSync also provides an Email upload function. You can automatically send received emails to the SugarSync shared folder, such as airline ticket booking emails.

3. Organize a party.

Create a shared folder for the upcoming party and let guests discuss and display the preparations through it, such as event preparation status, venue decoration photos, who brings popcorn and who brings board games ——

4. Prepare gifts.

Want to create lasting beautiful memories for elderly relatives? Why not collaborate on making a digital photo album. Through a shared folder, collaborate with everyone to collect and organize the album materials, then consolidate and generate them for sending to the elders. I believe that when the elders receive such thoughtful gifts during the holidays, they will be deeply moved.

5. Synchronize a wish list.

Are you worried that the holiday gifts for kids might overlap with those bought by other relatives? Then consider creating and maintaining a wish list (Wish List), listing the gifts you plan to buy. If there are duplicates, you can conveniently communicate and negotiate in advance, allowing everyone to give the most suitable and preferred gifts. (via SugarSync Blog)
