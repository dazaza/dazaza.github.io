The State Department: Beijing, Tianjin, Hebei and other provincial capitals began to monitor PM2.5 in previous years.

by abcurtbt on 2012-03-01 15:55:54

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing (Jinyu) - According to the website of the Chinese government, Premier Wen Jiabao presided over a State Council executive meeting yesterday which approved the release of the newly revised "Ambient Air Quality Standard." The new standard includes monitoring indicators for fine particulate matter (PM2.5) and ozone (O3) 8-hour concentration limit values. This is the first time China has included PM2.5 in its air quality standards and also the first time that an ambient air quality standard has been passed by the State Council.

It is understood that the Ministry of Environmental Protection will hold a press conference this week to announce the new "Ambient Air Quality Standard."

Highlight 1:

PM2.5 Monitoring Included in the New Standard

To make the results of ambient air quality assessments closer to the public's personal feelings, the meeting approved the release of the newly revised "Ambient Air Quality Standard." The new standard includes monitoring indicators for fine particulate matter (PM2.5) and ozone (O3) 8-hour concentration limit values.

Thus, the controversy over the inability of the air standard to reflect actual pollution conditions, which has been widely discussed since last autumn and winter, has finally been resolved at the policy level. It is reported that there is not much change compared to the content of the new version of the "Ambient Air Quality Standard" announced in November last year. This is the first time China has included fine particulate matter and ozone in its ambient air quality standards, aligning with international practices.

Understanding: The Ministry of Environmental Protection will hold a press conference this week to announce the new "Ambient Air Quality Standard."

Highlight 2:

Clear Timeline for PM2.5 Monitoring

The meeting requested that in 2012, PM2.5 and ozone project monitoring be carried out in key areas such as the Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei region, Yangtze River Delta, Pearl River Delta, municipalities directly under the Central Government, and provincial capitals. In 2013, monitoring will be extended to 113 environmental protection key cities and national model environmental protection cities, and by 2015, it will cover all cities at or above the prefecture level.

This timeline aligns with what Zhou Shengxian, Minister of Environmental Protection, publicly disclosed at the end of last year during an executive meeting of the Ministry of Environmental Protection.

Environmental experts described this requirement as advanced and practical. Du Shaoyong, inspector of the Guangzhou Environmental Protection Bureau (Weibo), said that implementing PM2.5 monitoring nationwide all at once would not be realistic. Starting from municipalities and other places can send a strong signal and align with reality.

■ Related Actions

Yesterday, the State Council executive meeting also arranged key tasks to strengthen comprehensive air pollution prevention and control and announced five aspects of action.

1. Three Emission Reduction Measures Involve Energy Industry

[Action] Accelerate the elimination of outdated production capacity in industries such as power, steel, building materials, non-ferrous metals, petrochemicals, and chemicals. Implement relocation and energy-saving and environmental protection technology upgrades for heavily polluting enterprises in urban areas. Implement stricter special emission limits for air pollutants in key regions, prohibiting the construction and expansion of coal-fired power plants, steel mills, and cement plants except for cogeneration. Strictly control access for new projects and enforce environmental laws. Promote sulfur dioxide emission reduction management in the power industry and non-electricity industries such as steel and petrochemicals. Strengthen nitrogen oxide management in the cement industry.

[Interpretation] Yesterday, at the [Energy Consumption Total Control] seminar, researcher Jiang Kejuan from the National Development and Reform Commission's Energy Research Institute said that China's high-energy-consuming industries are still developing rapidly, and the coal industry is not only related to high consumption issues but also an important source of PM2.5 pollution.

Han Xiaoping, CEO of China Energy Network, said that after years of efforts to adjust the energy structure with little success due to lack of consensus, now PM2.5 has become a national consensus, which is conducive to promoting the transformation of [burning less coal and more gas].

2. Phase Out Old High-Emission Vehicles Within Four Years

[Action] Improve the quality of vehicle fuel and motor vehicle emission standards. By 2015, basically phase out operating vehicles registered before 2005 that do not meet national I standards.

[Interpretation] "Yellow-label vehicles" refer to high-emission vehicles that do not even meet national I standards. Since 2009, China has incentivized the phasing out of "yellow-label vehicles" through programs like "old-for-new" to encourage owners to eliminate these vehicles. Taking Guangzhou as an example, vehicle exhaust pollution is listed as the second largest source of PM2.5, producing 58% and 40% of the city's total nitrogen oxides and volatile organic compound emissions, respectively. These two substances react in the atmosphere to form PM2.5 particles.

However, experts pointed out that the key to controlling vehicle pollution lies in improving fuel quality, but progress has been slow. The automobile industry has been more active in reducing emissions, but fuel quality has not kept up. The two major oil companies, CNPC and Sinopec, need to accelerate the improvement of fuel quality.

3. Implement Joint Prevention and Control and Early Warning Systems

[Action] Implement joint prevention and control of air pollution in key areas such as the Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei, Yangtze River Delta, and Pearl River Delta regions. Establish an early warning system for air pollution under extreme weather conditions.

[Interpretation] Compared to larger particles, fine particles with diameters of 2.5 micrometers or less have strong transmission capabilities and undergo long-range transport near the ground. For example, according to analysis by Guangzhou's environmental protection department, one quarter of Guangzhou's PM2.5 pollution comes from external dust, mainly originating from heavy-pollution industries such as cement, steel, refining, and thermal power in the Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei region.

Last year, the increase in foggy days drew social attention. Guangzhou took the lead in announcing the establishment of a severe pollution day early warning system, adopting measures such as suspending earthwork operations and slag transportation at construction sites, partial shutdowns or reductions in production by some enterprises.

Ma Jun, director of the Institute of Public and Environmental Affairs, indicated that when real-time data on PM2.5 monitoring shows severe pollution, the public should be promptly informed through the early warning system. For example, people should be advised to stay indoors, children should stop outdoor strenuous activities, and health risks should be reduced.

Key Events in PM2.5 Monitoring

October 2011 Haze Draws Attention

Some large and medium-sized cities frequently experience prolonged haze weather, raising concerns about air quality. An unfamiliar string of characters—PM2.5—began to enter the public eye and quickly became a social hotspot.

Mid-November

Standard Solicits Opinions

The Ministry of Environmental Protection announced the draft of the "Ambient Air Quality Standard" for public comment for the second time. The public generally supports including PM2.5 and ozone (8-hour concentration) in routine air quality assessments.

December 21

Announcement of Monitoring Timeline

Minister of Environmental Protection Zhou Shengxian announced the timeline for implementing PM2.5 monitoring at the environmental protection conference.

December 30

Addition of PM2.5

The Standing Committee of the Ministry of Environmental Protection reviewed and in principle approved the revised "Ambient Air Quality Standard." The standard added average concentration limit values for PM2.5 and 8-hour average concentration limit values for ozone.

January 21, 2012

Guangzhou First Releases Data

The Guangzhou Environmental Monitoring Center website was the first to publicly release hourly concentration data for PM2.5 research monitoring. The Chegongzhuang automatic monitoring station data updates every hour. Subsequently, several provinces announced their monitoring timelines.

February 29

State Council Approval

At a State Council executive meeting, the newly revised "Ambient Air Quality Standard" was approved for release, and arrangements were made to strengthen key tasks in comprehensive air pollution prevention and control.

More Than Ten Provinces Announce Monitoring Timelines

Since January 21 this year, the Guangzhou Environmental Protection Monitoring Center website was the first to begin publicly releasing hourly concentration data for PM2.5 research monitoring. It is reported that besides Guangzhou, currently more than ten provinces including Guangdong, Shanxi, Shaanxi, Zhejiang, Hubei, Jiangsu, Guangdong, Guangxi, Yunnan, Sichuan, and Xinjiang have announced plans to monitor PM2.5, and some provinces have already included PM2.5 monitoring in their government work reports.

Multiple Measures for Guangzhou's Air Pollution Control

■ Guangzhou Situation

Yesterday, the State Council executive meeting also heard a report on Guangzhou's air pollution control measures for 2012-2020.

Currently, before the new national standard is issued, Guangzhou has already taken strong actions and formulated air pollution control measures. According to the "Guangzhou 2010-2020 Air Pollution Control Work Plan," by 2020, the proportion of coal use will be controlled below 10%, and the total coal consumption will be controlled within 10 million tons, reducing by 62% within five years.

Guangzhou will prohibit the construction and expansion of highly polluting and energy-intensive industries such as oil refining, petrochemicals, cement, steel, casting, and building materials. By 2020, all commercial cement factories in the city will be shut down.

In addition, by 2013, Guangzhou will phase out 400,000 old vehicles in advance, and by 2015, another 300,000 old vehicles purchased before 2001 that meet national I standards will be forcibly phased out; by 2020, 900,000 old vehicles below national II standards will be phased out. At the same time, priority will be given to developing the public transportation system.

Previously, Guangzhou had prioritized implementing a "squeeze" policy for high-emission yellow-label vehicles (those not meeting national I standards). Since 2009, yellow-label vehicles have been prohibited from entering the fifth and sixth rings. Last year, Guangzhou began encouraging the phasing out of old vehicles meeting national I and II standards and will be the first to adopt national V standards this year. Nationwide, according to the requirements of the Ministry of Environmental Protection, the vehicle emission standards in many cities have reached national IV standards.

Ma Jun stated, "For monitoring PM2.5, each center should have a sense of responsibility for its territory."

Twenty Percent of Guangzhou's PM2.5 Comes from Vehicle Exhaust

■ Background

On February 24 this year, at the 2012 Capital Energy and Economic Operation Adjustment Work Conference, Vice Mayor Hong Feng of Guangzhou introduced that in the formation of PM2.5 in Guangzhou, apart from dust and emissions from industrial processing, the main contributor is energy emissions. Among them, vehicle emissions account for 22.2%, and coal combustion emissions account for 16.7%. Additionally, 24.5% comes from surrounding areas, most of which is caused by burning coal. Therefore, coal combustion emissions account for about 40% of Guangzhou's PM2.5.

According to a report by the Xinmin Evening News, on February 29, the Guangzhou Environmental Protection Bureau announced monitoring data. Preliminary research using various source analysis methods showed that among the sources of PM2.5 in Guangzhou's ambient air, vehicle emissions accounted for the highest proportion, at 25%; petrochemicals, chemicals, industrial spraying, steel, and building materials industrial processes accounted for 15%; industrial boilers and kilns accounted for 11%; power plant boilers accounted for 10%; construction sites, roads, and stockpile dust accounted for 10%; regional impacts accounted for 20%.
