Complete home decoration premise, how to decorate your home

by longersing on 2009-09-30 18:23:53

Completing the premise of home decoration can effectively improve the efficiency of house decoration, and also allow one's own decoration to proceed steadily and achieve the best results. How to decorate your home? The living room, entrance, dining room, bedroom, and even the children's room that some families create separately all have a lot of knowledge in terms of decorative design.

Here are the decoration secrets:

The entrance is located right at the door. The entrance serves as a transitional space for entering and leaving the house, playing a role in transition. The function of the entrance is to block the outdoor view from seeing the indoor area completely, ensuring the privacy of indoor life; secondly, in terms of scientific layout, it prevents the indoor energy from leaking out. A house should not be open like a city gate, and the entrance can solve this problem. Now, the entrance of a house is like the shadow wall in old Chinese houses. Before entering the courtyard, there is a prelude space that guides people's gaze, then enters the hall.

Living Room: The living room is generally the largest open space in the household and is the main place for daily activities. It gathers various functions such as entertainment, receiving guests, and relaxation, and connects with the communication between the dining room, kitchen, bathroom, bedroom, study, and balcony. In the planar layout of this area, attention should be paid to ensure that each functional area and passage do not interfere with each other, and the movement lines should be smooth. The living room is the place that most reflects personal characteristics, decoration style, class, cultural taste, cultivation, and lifestyle interests.

Kitchen: The kitchen holds an important position next to the household door in the home, because food is essential for survival. One day without eating can make you very hungry. Although the kitchen occupies a small area, the investment is quite substantial. A decent set of cabinets and cookware, plus stoves, may cost tens of thousands of yuan. Nowadays, the concept of the kitchen has new open options, or what we call Chinese and Western styles. Generally speaking, frying, stir-frying, cooking, and deep-frying belong to the Chinese style, while microwave and electric heating belong to the Western style. Many house designs only separate the kitchen and dining room into two areas without further distinguishing between Chinese and Western styles, while some high-end residences clearly separate them. Of course, whether it is Chinese or Western, it must be convenient for you to use.

Dining Room: The dining room can be set up separately, but most current home designs do not have a dedicated dining room area. Many are natural extensions outside the kitchen door or outside the living room, completely open. Some wine cabinets and bars are also designed here. In general, homes do not have a specially designed closed room for the dining room, blending it into the common area is very convenient. The plane design and layout of the dining room should fully consider the spatial dimensions of human movement, coming and going, serving activities, and the distance dimensions of placing sideboards, small carts, side cabinets, wine cabinets, refrigerators, tables, and chairs. Everything should be arranged conveniently and reasonably, while paying attention to the natural and smooth transition space from the dining room to other areas, not interfering with the activities and passage of other areas. Also, note that the dining table should not face the bathroom door, which should face other directions. If the original division inside the room has the bathroom facing the dining table, it should be renovated during decoration.

At the same time, if the family mainly eats Chinese food, the lighting near the dining table should be brighter; if it mainly eats Western food, the brightness can be dimmer, known as "bright for Chinese, dim for Western." It's best to hang a still-life painting on the wall next to the dining table, such as apples, bananas, teapots, etc., to create a good dining environment.

Bathroom: The bathroom is just as important as the kitchen, although their functions are exactly opposite. In some homes, the bathroom is separate and distinctly divided from the bathing function, with a separate shower room; there are also second bathrooms (also called double bathrooms) inside that include the bathing function, naturally serving both bathing and toilet purposes. Establishing double bathrooms in a home is relatively scientifically reasonable, achieving distinction between internal and external use and convenience for everyone’s daily life. A typical home bathroom usually includes three major fixtures: pedestal sink, toilet, and showerhead. More luxurious ones can be designed with dual sinks, water-saving intelligent electric heated toilets, shower rooms, and even equipped with massage bathtubs, depending on the size of the bathroom and the amount of investment.

Master Bedroom: The master bedroom is the largest room among several bedrooms and is where the homeowner rests daily. The functions of the master bedroom mainly emphasize sleep, storage, makeup, reading, etc. The plan layout should center around the bed, with the sleeping area being relatively quiet. Emphasizing decoration over renovation, the warm feeling is mainly created through later-stage curtains, decorations, plants, etc.

Children's Room: The children's room is a relatively smaller bedroom subordinate to the master bedroom. In specific renovations, some people also refer to it as a nanny's room, guest room, or elder's room, all under the same concept, specifically determined by usage needs, holding a secondary position in the home. Study: Not all homes have the conditions to set up a study, and a general study often doubles as an office. The study usually centers around a desk or office table. Besides bookshelves, the study might also include bookcases or filing cabinets. Decorations reflect the owner's style, determined according to needs. For example, if one has many books, a large bookcase might be set up; if one enjoys tea drinking or playing chess, specific areas can also be designated.

Balcony: The initial design intention of the balcony should be to open a window towards the outside in a relatively enclosed indoor space, borrowing the external landscape into the indoor area, while also allowing people indoors to exchange with the outside both physically and mentally, wandering momentarily. The function of the balcony will vary in meaning according to different owners, such as leisure, fitness, planting flowers, raising birds, drying clothes, etc.
