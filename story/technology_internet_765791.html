Alibaba Offers 1 Million Cash to Encourage Employees to Promote Its Mobile IM

by anonymous on 2013-08-20 16:17:00

On the noon of August 20, a strong market attention was drawn by YiXin, the IM tool jointly launched by China Telecom and NetEase. In this area, Ali has also started aggressive marketing for its long-planned but less effective product: Alibaba CEO Jonathan Lu sent an internal email to encourage employees to use and promote their mobile IM "LaiWang", and the first place winner will receive a cash reward of 100,000 yuan.

According to an internal email from Alibaba, the group initiated an activity called "Let's see who has more LaiWang friends" within the company, encouraging employees to invite family and friends (mainly those outside of Ali colleagues) to download and register the mobile APP LaiWang.

This activity will conclude on December 31, 2013. At that time, whoever has the most active non-colleague friends will win. The first prize winner will receive a post-tax cash reward of 100,000 yuan, the three second prize winners will each receive a post-tax cash reward of 50,000 yuan, and the ten third prize winners will each receive a post-tax cash reward of 10,000 yuan.

The team with the highest average number of friends (team unit based on manager level, senior manager if no manager, director if no senior manager, and so on), will receive a special reward from either Jack Ma or Jonathan Lu. The details of the reward are currently "kept as a surprise." There are unconfirmed rumors that the reward is Alibaba Group stock options.

It is worth noting that yesterday, China Telecom and NetEase jointly released the mobile IM product "YiXin" which challenges WeChat, and it has drawn high attention from the industry. Analysts believe that this high-profile entry by traditional operators means that competition in the mobile IM sector will become even more intense. Alibaba, which has already made arrangements in this field, clearly does not want to miss out on this round of market competition.

Data shows that as part of Alibaba's social platform layout, the APP LaiWang officially went online on the iOS client in July 2012. According to the introduction, "LaiWang" is a social product close to people's lives, with the keywords being: "mobile", "lifestyle", "acquaintance". Acquaintance-based social networking can help users expand their online social networks through offline acquaintance relationships.

A relatively distinctive feature is the "grouping" function, which is a form of social interaction between "topics" and "circles". People are attracted to interesting topics discussed by their friends, then join the discussion, and the number of participants grows like a snowball. More and more friends see the topic, and if lucky, they can even meet friends of friends inside.

Alibaba's Chief Strategy Officer Zeng Ming previously admitted that in the past two years, Ali's progress in SNS social networking has been poor, but in the overall development thinking of the entire group, SNS is inevitably an important organic component. Alibaba's launch of LaiWang is also aimed at starting new attempts.

However, in the past year, WeChat has grown rapidly, while Ali has not invested too many resources in promoting LaiWang. Analysts believe that this time, Ali's adoption of the familiar recommendation model used by Kaixin.com when it became popular is also aimed at quickly gaining market recognition and market share. However, whether it will be effective under the current highly competitive market environment remains to be observed.
