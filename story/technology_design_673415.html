Unlicensed doctor sentenced for brokering the sale of male infants

by yesebaby5s5j on 2012-02-23 16:11:30

Around 11 p.m. on July 2, 2009, Cui Jiantang and his wife Li Ruiqin, in the unlicensed individual clinic they operated in Xianghuangqi, Haidian District, assisted Xu (handled separately) in giving birth to a baby boy. "At the time, this couple said that they were poor and couldn't afford to raise the child, so they asked us if we could find someone to sell the child to," Cui Jiantang testified. "My wife then said she could help them make contact, but would need a 4000 yuan commission, which they agreed to." On July 3, Cui Jiantang and his wife introduced Ma and her husband, who ran a hair salon, to Xu. In the end, both parties agreed to sell the baby boy for 26,000 yuan. Cui Jiantang and his wife profited 4,000 yuan from the deal and forged one medical birth certificate. On July 15, after an anonymous tip-off from the public, the police arrested Li Ruiqin. On December 19, 2010, Cui Jiantang was apprehended by the police in Zhengzhou, Henan Province. After reviewing the case, the court ruled that defendant Cui Jiantang, together with others, acted as intermediaries in selling another person's biological child, illegally profiting and committing acts of extreme malice. His actions constituted the crime of abandonment. Given that Cui Jiantang showed better remorse and confession upon being captured, and considering that he assisted Li Ruiqin in facilitating the transaction and counting the money, playing a relatively smaller role, and also taking into account that the male infant has been returned to his biological mother without causing serious consequences, the court decided to lighten his sentence accordingly.
