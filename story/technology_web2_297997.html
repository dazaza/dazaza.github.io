Wenwen's abnormal behavior made Ms. Wang vigilant. Therefore, she decided to take Wenwen to see a doctor. Since that day, Wenwen often woke up screaming from her dreams.

Currently, the Education Bureau of Yuzhong County has intervened in the investigation.

On September 26th, Ms. Wang from Gongjing Town in Yuzhong County waited for her daughter who studies at the Fourth Middle School of Yuzhong County to come home for a long time. Although anxious, since Wenwen is usually quite obedient, Ms. Wang did not worry too much. That evening, Wenwen returned home and went straight to her room. During dinner, Ms. Wang noticed her daughter sitting down with a look of pain on her face. After persistent questioning, Wenwen hesitantly told her mother that during the English class in the morning, the teacher announced the results of the previous day's English test. Due to her low score, she was severely reprimanded by the teacher. When Ms. Wang took off her daughter's pants, she was shocked to discover that her daughter's calves and thighs were covered with bruises, especially the bruised marks on her lower legs which were filled with blood spots. Although she felt extremely heartbroken, the teacher was ultimately concerned about her daughter's studies, so Ms. Wang did not blame the teacher. However, the significant changes in her daughter over the next two months were something Ms. Wang had not anticipated.

After two months of treatment, Wenwen was admitted to the psychiatric department of Lanzhou University Second Hospital. On November 30th, 14-year-old Wenwen from Gongjing Town in Yuzhong County was taken by her family to the psychiatric department of Lanzhou University Second Hospital for treatment. The reason was that over the past two months, the originally introverted Wenwen suddenly became irritable and easily angered. All this began two months ago with an English exam when 14-year-old Wenwen was publicly beaten dozens of times by her teacher with a board due to her poor exam results.

On September 29th, Ms. Wang took Wenwen to Gongjing Town Health Center. After listening to Ms. Wang's introduction, Director Wang of the health center said that Wenwen might have been frightened and needed psychological counseling. He then took Wenwen into an independent examination room at the health center and provided her with individual psychological treatment. After returning home from the health center, Ms. Wang noticed a noticeable change in Wenwen's mood; she became more cheerful and willingly went to school the next day. However, good times didn't last long. A few days later, Wenwen refused to go to school again because she felt that after the incident a few days ago, both teachers and classmates treated her coldly and strangely. Recalling Director Wang's previous diagnosis of Wenwen at the health center, Ms. Wang believed that Wenwen's illness was not completely cured and needed further treatment. Thus, over the following two months, Wenwen, accompanied by her parents, sought treatment at various hospitals including the People's Hospital of Yuzhong County, the Provincial People's Hospital, Lanzhou University First Hospital, and Lanzhou University Second Hospital. According to the requirements of the doctors at Lanzhou University Second Hospital, Wenwen was admitted to the psychiatric department of Lanzhou University Second Hospital on November 30th.

Due to poor exam results, Wenwen was beaten by her teacher with a board.

By Yao Zhi, Kang Yuan, and intern reporter Wang Boya

On December 4th, more than two months after Wenwen was beaten, when the reporter visited the Fourth Middle School of Yuzhong County, Vice Principal Jin Xingrong expressed deep regret regarding the incident where Sun Teacher injured a student. Jin Xingrong said that overall, the English level of students in Class (2) of Grade 2 was relatively poor. This semester, the school transferred Sun Teacher, who excels in English, to Class (2) of Grade 2, hoping to significantly improve their English level. On September 27th, after learning that Wenwen was injured by the English teacher, the school apologized multiple times to Wenwen's family and accompanied Wenwen to check her condition at the People's Hospital of Yuzhong County and the Provincial People's Hospital. After the examination, the doctor informed them that Wenwen was physically normal, but later, Wenwen experienced physical discomfort again. When they visited Wenwen's home, they were rejected by the family. Principal Jin said that after the incident involving Wenwen being beaten occurred, it drew high attention from relevant leaders of the Education Bureau of Yuzhong County, who came to the school to investigate and handle the matter. At the same time, the school also held an emergency meeting to prevent similar incidents of excessive corporal punishment against students. The school also actively cooperated with the parents in treating Wenwen, hoping that Wenwen would recover soon and return to school.

On December 3rd, when the reporter met Ms. Wang outside the psychiatric ward of Lanzhou University Second Hospital accompanying her hospitalized daughter, she appeared worried. According to Ms. Wang, on the morning of September 27th, her daughter claimed she had a headache and hoped her mother could call the school to ask for leave. Considering that her daughter was unwilling to go to school the next day after being disciplined by the teacher, it was reasonable. So, Ms. Wang did not refuse. Subsequently, Ms. Wang talked with her daughter about the process of being disciplined by the teacher the previous day. During the conversation, Ms. Wang learned that Wenwen scored only 30-something points in the previous English test. On the morning of September 26th, due to dissatisfaction with Wenwen's abysmally low score, Mr. Sun, the English teacher, harshly beat Wenwen 68 times with a wooden board. Hearing this, Ms. Wang, feeling heartbroken for her daughter, hurriedly let her rest. Unexpectedly, while observing her daughter, she found her swallowing "Analgesic" and "Pain Relief Tablets." Just as Ms. Wang moved to stop her, her normally obedient daughter suddenly shouted at her.

School apology, county education bureau intervenes in the investigation

On the morning of September 28th, when Ms. Wang called her daughter to go to school, Wenwen once again refused to go, claiming she had a headache and was uncomfortable. That morning, Ms. Wang informed Wenwen's homeroom teacher, Ms. Yue, about the situation. Upon hearing the news, Ms. Yue brought the English teacher, Mr. Sun, to Wenwen's house that noon. When the teachers arrived, Wenwen's parents invited them into Wenwen's room, hoping the teachers could guide Wenwen. Unexpectedly, while the three were talking in Wenwen's room, Ms. Wang suddenly heard Wenwen's frantic screams, “I'm not going to school! I'm not... Get out, get out quickly.” Wenwen receives treatment at Lanzhou University Second Hospital. Photo by our reporter Fang Yi.

Introverted girl's personality suddenly becomes irritable

According to Mr. Sun, the English teacher of Class (2) of Grade 2, Wenwen is relatively introverted, has average relationships with classmates, and performs poorly in English. He urges Wenwen to study English every day. On September 25th, it was the fifth week of the semester's small test at school. After the English test, he found that Wenwen couldn't even write the most basic English words, scoring only more than ten points. In anger, he used a wooden board over a foot long to discipline Wenwen, hoping she would study hard in the future. Mr. Sun stated that he did not hit Wenwen as many as 68 times, but he couldn't recall the exact number. Wenwen's homeroom teacher, Ms. Yue, said that after learning about Wenwen's illness, she and Mr. Sun went to visit Wenwen at her home. On September 28th, Wenwen claimed her headache was severe. While discussing the cause of Wenwen's headache with her family, Wenwen suddenly burst into loud cries and said she wouldn't go to school, becoming quite agitated.
