"Those Badass People in History" First Chapter: A Laugh Echoes Over the Sea

After Chiang Kai-shek became the president of the "Republic of China," he wanted to demolish and expand his old house located in Wuling Town, Fenghua, Zhejiang. He planned to have the neighbors relocate to make room for the Chiang family. Upon learning about the Chiang family's expansion plans, the neighbors all willingly gave up their properties. However, the owner of the adjacent Zhou Shunfang, who sold layered cakes, refused to give up his land.

Originally, the owner of Zhou Shunfang was a childhood playmate of Chiang Kai-shek, born on the same year, month, and day, so he did not regard him as an untouchable great figure. After receiving the demolition notice, the owner sarcastically remarked, "Ruiyuan (Chiang Kai-shek's childhood name) has become emperor; if he asks me to move, I have no choice but to move..." and insisted that Chiang Kai-shek come personally to speak. After hearing this, Chiang sighed, "Let him stay if he wants." Thus, this Zhou Shunfang was jokingly referred to as the biggest钉子户(nail household) against demolition in the Republic of China era.

"Those Badass People in History" Second Chapter: A Roar Accompanies the Sword's Draw

Who was the most powerful imperial mentor in history? According to Mao Zedong, who was well-versed in history, it wasn't Zhang Liang, who "planned strategies within the tent and won battles thousands of miles away," because although Zhang Liang helped Liu Bang conquer the empire, he never proposed any overarching national policies. Neither was it Zhuge Liang, whose divine calculations were renowned, because despite having a comprehensive plan like the "Longzhong Dialogue," Zhuge Liang did not enable Liu Bei to unify the empire but only secured one-third of it, making Shu the weakest of the Three Kingdoms, leading to his own death before achieving success in battle. Even more so, it wasn't Liu Bowen, who was highly deified by the common people, because historically, the real Liu Bowen accomplished little. His name didn't even appear in Zhu Yuanzhang's list of founding heroes in 1370 until twenty days after the rewards were distributed, when he was granted the lowest rank of baron.

In Mao Zedong's view, the most powerful imperial mentor was Zhu Sheng, Zhu Yuanzhang's strategist, praised for his "nine-word national strategy that secured the empire."

The psychological shadow cast by his mother accompanied Qin Shi Huang throughout his life. His resentment towards his mother evolved into generalized hostility towards all women, causing a bias in his marital relationships later on. Despite his harem being filled with beauties from the six states, he merely treated them as objects for venting his anger and hatred, or tools for satisfying his physiological needs. Qin Shi Huang despised the numerous beauties brought into his harem after unifying the six states, hating how they abandoned their sense of shame over their fallen nations and instead pleased their new master. However, he greatly admired women who remained chaste and loyal. It is said that there was a young widow named Qing, who had adhered to her widowhood vows for years. Qin Shi Huang once honored her by allowing her to sit beside him, something even the prime minister of the time rarely received. Qin Shi Huang also built a "Huaiqing Terrace" in her honor to commemorate her deeds. To this day, there is a mountain in Sichuan called "Zhen Nv Shan" (Maiden Mountain), which was where this widow once resided.
