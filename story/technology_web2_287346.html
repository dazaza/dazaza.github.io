Post-80s girl plays online games and overdraws 70,000 yuan on 8 cards, then surrenders to the police

by 9rha186b3h on 2009-12-01 10:49:36

A post-80s girl applied for 8 credit cards consecutively. Addicted to online games, she went on a swiping spree to purchase all sorts of gaming equipment until she overdrawn by six or seven hundred thousand yuan and was unable to pay it back, finally having no choice but to surrender herself to the authorities.

Having maxed out eight cards just to play online games, the post-80s girl overdrawn seventy thousand yuan and was unable to repay it, facing at least five years in prison. The girl applied for 8 credit cards consecutively. Addicted to online games, she went on a swiping spree to purchase all sorts of gaming equipment until she overdrawn by six or seven hundred thousand yuan and was unable to pay it back, finally having no choice but to surrender herself to the authorities. On July 15, 2009, she was arrested by the procuratorate on suspicion of credit card fraud. Yesterday, the Haidian Court heard the case. She is facing at least five years in prison.

At 1:30 PM yesterday, Li was brought into the courtroom. She did not hire a lawyer but chose to defend herself. The prosecution charged that between December 2007 and May 2008, Li used the CITIC Bank, Guangfa Bank, Minsheng Bank, and Everbright Bank credit cards she had obtained while working at the Hualpu Supermarket in Wanshou Road, Haidian District, to make large purchases, overdrawn by a total of 68,442.85 yuan beyond the specified period, and still did not repay after being prompted by the issuing bank. On June 8, 2009, Li voluntarily surrendered to the public security authorities. The prosecution believes that Li's actions are suspected of credit card fraud.

Li was born in March 1987 and is only 22 years old this year. In 2007, Li worked as a cashier at the Wanshou Road branch of Hualpu Supermarket. Various banks came to the supermarket to set up booths to "promote" their own credit cards. Faced with the temptation of advertisements about how credit cards can be used for overdraft consumption, Li, who earns less than two thousand yuan a month, was tempted. Li first applied for two credit cards from China Merchants Bank. The joy of being able to shop without money made Li lose control, and she then applied for six more credit cards from CITIC Bank, Guangfa Bank, Everbright Bank, and Minsheng Bank.

At the same time, Li became addicted to online games. She spent her days immersed in "The Legend of Tian Long Ba Bu", constantly upgrading herself by purchasing large amounts of gaming equipment online, "spending more than ten thousand yuan in one day at its peak". Level by level, she advanced, forgetting the problems in reality - the huge overdrafts on her credit cards.

Until the banks' collection notices were posted on her door, and calls were made to her home, she suddenly realized that she had incurred a huge debt. Prior to this, she had already been fired from her job due to her obsession with online games. To pay off her debts, she found another job, but the income was low, making it difficult to cover the large gap. She even "painfully" sold several online game accounts, "the most expensive one sold for 1600 yuan", which was her highest-level account, but for which she had invested tens of thousands of yuan.

In June 2009, Li had no choice but to surrender to the public security authorities because she was truly unable to repay the amount overdrawn on her credit cards. In response to the judge's inquiry whether she could borrow money to repay, she replied: "If I could borrow money to repay, I wouldn't have surrendered myself." The verdict will be announced on another day.
