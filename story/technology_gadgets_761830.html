Nokia 1050 Hands-on: How did this thing become a godsend?

by geekzhang on 2013-05-12 11:22:12

Recently, while browsing Weibo or forum posts online, three mobile phones have been very popular. One is the HTC One, which many people praise but don't buy; another is the Samsung GALAXY S4, which has been criticized a lot for its plastic shell; and the third one is Nokia 1050, which everyone seems to be worshiping as the ultimate miracle of the mobile phone world in 2013 so far.I've never understood what makes the Nokia 1050 a dream phone in so many people's hearts. Is it just because of the price? Fortunately, I got my hands on a retail version of this phone before it was officially launched. Let me share my experience with this so-called dream phone.First, let's talk about the strengths of this phone:Compact and lightweightThe Nokia 1050 is incredibly compact, measuring only 107 x 44.8 x 14.3 mm. If you're not familiar with these numbers, compare the size of this phone with the Lumia 920 and the S4. For those who are used to using large-screen smartphones, holding the Nokia 1050 feels like playing with building blocks from your childhood because it's really that light—only 70 grams, 60% lighter than the Lumia 920.Although the Nokia 1050 isn't thin, we can't expect a phone costing over 100 yuan to be as thin as a blade. It's not realistic. However, there are advantages to being thick. Despite the lightness of this phone, the thickness ensures at least a decent feel. Imagine if it were 7 mm thick—I believe the feel would be significantly compromised.As for whether this phone can crack walnuts, I didn't dare to try because the plastic shell of the Nokia 1050 looks quite fragile, especially the back cover, which is as thin as the S4's. You can easily bend the back cover by holding the top and bottom with one hand without much effort. The entire phone has a matte finish, providing a very good feel.Another point is that the SIM card slot on this phone is a standard SIM card, while the photo uses a Micro-SIM card. Placing the card directly into the slot aligned with the contact points allows it to be used directly. Additionally, this phone cannot enter the system without inserting a SIM card, which I find disappointing. In other words, without a SIM card inserted, you cannot access the address book or listen to FM radio.Nice button feelPerhaps having a keyboard is one of the key factors why the Nokia 1050 is called a miracle. After getting used to touch screen phones, suddenly returning to the most primitive 9-key keypad brings an inexplicable sense of intimacy. Although the 9-key keypad isn't as direct or fast as the QWERTY keyboard on a touch screen, compared to touch screens, the improvement in feel is decisive. After getting used to it, the keyboard of the 1050 can also quickly input Chinese characters, reminding me of the Nokia 3100 I used during school days. Back then, comparing who could type faster became a fun activity among classmates.The keys on the Nokia 1050 are integrated, but each key has a raised surface, making finger positioning very convenient. Each key provides excellent tactile feedback. Although the keyboard protected by a thin layer of rubber membrane may seem fragile, according to the durability of previous Nokia feature phones, this keyboard should last 2-3 years without any issues.Battery is indeed powerfulIn the official promotion, Nokia prominently advertises the biggest feature of the 1050—35 days of ultra-long standby! Is it really that amazing?Let's first talk about the battery equipped in the 1050. The battery model is BL-5CB, with a capacity of only 800 mAh, and it's removable. At first glance, the capacity doesn't seem large. When I first got this phone, I thought Nokia might give it a 1020 mAh 5C battery since they advertised 35 days of standby time. However, when I got the actual phone, I was a bit disappointed.However, in practical use, the battery of the Nokia 1050 is indeed very powerful. Under normal circumstances, with a SIM card inserted and daily call time around 2 hours, a full charge can last for 4 days. And if you remove the SIM card and leave the phone on standby, after 5 days, the phone only lost 2 bars of power (out of 5). If Nokia's last bar theory still holds true for this phone, then it's estimated that the pure standby power consumption of the 1050 in 5 days is around 30%.Now that we've talked about the strengths, let's discuss the weaknesses of this phone. Some people might think I'm nitpicking, but nothing in this world is perfect, everything has its flaws:Few contacts and difficult to importThe Nokia 1050 belongs to the S30 series, which is a functional phone, so its functionality is very limited. In terms of basic functions, this phone can only make calls and send text messages, and lacks basic expansion features, which posed a problem for me. First, this phone can only store 500 phone numbers, while I have 743 contacts, so obviously they can't all fit on my phone.Secondly, when I got the phone, I was confused for a long time because I didn't know how to import the contact numbers into the 1050. The commonly used network backup and restore function on smart phones has no effect on the 1050. I could only import them in batches via the most primitive SIM card method. My SIM card can import 200 contacts at a time, but the prerequisite is that I must copy these 200 people to the SIM card and delete them from the smartphone, otherwise, I would go crazy trying to find the right person to copy the remaining contacts.Therefore, the Nokia 1050 can only be used as a backup phone. If you want to replace your current phone with it, it seems impossible.It can't connect to the internetYou read that right, the 1050 cannot connect to the internet. The WAP function has been removed, and the phone does not even have a built-in browser. This might be hard to accept for users with basic internet needs. On the bright side, you no longer have to worry about unexpected data usage.Average headphone compatibilityThe Nokia 1050 supports FM radio functionality, which should be quite useful for those who purchase this product. However, the standard package does not include headphones, so you need to purchase them separately to use the FM radio.In use, I found that Nokia's poor headphone compatibility issue also applies to the 1050. Out of four headphones I tried, including Apple's EarPods, BW's C5, and the default headphones for the S4, none were recognized by the phone. Only an unnamed three-segment contact point headphone worked properly. If you buy the 1050 and find that your headphones don't work, I suggest spending 5 yuan on a pair from a street vendor—it will definitely work.I've been using this phone for about 10 days. A phone costing over 100 yuan doesn't deserve too much fuss. The cost of skipping a meal out can buy such a phone. Nevertheless, I still want to offer some personal suggestions, which can be seen as complaints.Not suitable for elderly useAt first, I thought this phone could be used as an elderly phone for my parents because it's simple to operate and doesn't consume data unnecessarily. Generally, the requirements for seniors regarding phones are low—they just need to make calls. However, upon receiving this product, I dismissed that idea. First, the screen of this phone is too small, only 1.4 inches, and the font is also small. For most seniors, this is definitely the biggest hurdle. For seniors, being able to clearly see the content displayed on the screen is a basic requirement, and in this aspect, the 1050 does not qualify as an elderly phone.Secondly, this phone has no camera. From my observations, I've noticed that seniors actually use cameras quite frequently—they like taking pictures in parks, buying flowers, or taking photos of their pets. In today's context, taking pictures has transcended age barriers, and the low cost of the 1050 means that it lacks some basic additional features.This is not a rugged phoneThis isn't a joke—I've indeed seen many people online asking if the Nokia 1050 is a rugged phone. I can definitively say it is not. In Nokia's official promotional materials, they mention a very misleading feature: a dustproof keyboard, leading many people to mistakenly believe it's a rugged phone.In reality, the Nokia 1050's integrated keyboard design eliminates gaps between keys, preventing dust from settling, hence the "dustproof" claim. As for water resistance, just look at the exposed interface ports and obvious gaps on the back cover—the claim of waterproofing is clearly baseless. Regarding the final "rugged" aspect of drop resistance, well, that's where Nokia shines.This is not the cheapest phoneJingdong MallYixunSuning YigouThe main reason I believe the Nokia 1050 has been called a "miracle" has a lot to do with its price. Officially priced at 162 yuan, it started pre-sales on major e-commerce sites on May 7th. As of the time of writing, Yixun's price matches the official one at 162 yuan, while Jingdong and Suning are both at 168 yuan. However, this is not actually the cheapest Nokia phone—model 1280 with a black-and-white screen is only 137 yuan, color-screen model 1030 is only 148 yuan, and there's even a contract version of the 2730C on Jingdong for only 159 yuan. Comparatively speaking, if you're looking for a cheap phone that can make calls, the Nokia 1050 doesn't even make the top three. And this is just considering Nokia—if you factor in international brands like Samsung and domestic ones like Haier and Daxian, the Nokia 1050 would rank beyond the top 50.Thus, purely in terms of price, the Nokia 1050 hasn't really gained any advantage in the ultra-low-price phone market.SummaryA 162-yuan phone that has garnered significant online attention is indeed remarkable. The Nokia 1050 has its own advantages, as well as shortcomings. However, spending the equivalent of a meal to buy a phone that can make calls and has long standby time shouldn't be overly scrutinized. I recommend this phone for users with idle SIM cards who only need to make calls. As a supplementary device to a smartphone, the 1050 can serve well as a backup phone.
