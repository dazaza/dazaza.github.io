Baidu sues 360, second trial begins; 360 submits 5 new pieces of evidence

by anonymous on 2013-08-20 15:46:50

Reporters learned that the second trial of Baidu's lawsuit against 360 for trademark infringement and unfair competition was heard in Beijing Higher People's Court.

Previously, the first trial was held by the First Intermediate People’s Court. The court ruled that 360 must stop its unfair competitive behavior and apologize on the home page for 15 days; Baidu was awarded a compensation of 450,000 yuan and reasonable expenses of 50,000 yuan. After the verdict, 360 appealed against the judgment.

360 claims there is no competitive relationship with Baidu

In March last year, Baidu filed a lawsuit claiming that 360 tampered with Baidu search results pages, causing confusion. In addition, 360 also used its browser to bundle its URL navigation site, intentionally imitating and confusing search results, infringing on Baidu's trademark rights and engaging in unfair competition.

After hearing, the First Intermediate People’s Court believed that 360 Security Guard violated the Anti-Unfair Competition Law, which constituted an act of unfair competition. Regarding Baidu's claim that 360 infringed its trademark rights, the court did not support it.

For the first trial verdict, 360 expressed dissatisfaction, citing errors in factual determination and legal application in the first trial, and appealed to the Beijing Higher People's Court to overturn the first trial verdict.

"Network security services and URL navigation services are not homogeneous services compared to search engine services. There is no competitive relationship between 360 and Baidu." In the second trial hearing, the lawyer representing 360 said that inserting risk warning signs on the search results page is also an internationally recognized practice today, and major security software at home and abroad have widely adopted this method. “Even Baidu itself inserts risk warnings next to risky websites in its search results through the 'Baidu Security Alliance'. It is very obvious that Baidu's single lawsuit against 360 establishes double standards, so the finding of the first trial goes against basic industry common sense and international practices.”

Baidu's agent unaware of new evidence submitted by 360

Baidu's agent believed that the first trial verdict was correct. "If we allow 360 to arbitrarily modify others' web page codes for a series of improper behaviors such as inserting marks, and arbitrarily grab traffic, then the normal order of the Internet will be greatly disrupted, and the legitimate rights and interests of network operators will not be protected," said Baidu's agent.

In addition, both Baidu and 360 made statements on several detailed issues, including whether 360 security software tampered with source code and whether the drop-down prompt words set up on the 360 URL navigation station affected Baidu.

During the trial, 360 submitted five new pieces of evidence to the court and claimed that they had already been submitted within the extended evidentiary period, but Baidu's agent said "I am not aware", and needed time to "digest" them after returning. The judge then announced a recess and scheduled another trial for a later date.
