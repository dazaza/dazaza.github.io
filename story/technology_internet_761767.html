Microsoft wants to spend $1 billion to acquire the Nook e-books unit

by geekzhang on 2013-05-09 13:29:43

According to a report on May 9, citing information from the American tech blog TechCrunch, internal documents obtained by the website indicate that Microsoft is currently considering acquiring the e-book related business unit of Barnes & Noble's subsidiary Nook Media for $1 billion. This includes the college textbook department.The documents also reveal that Nook Media plans to discontinue its Android tablet business by the end of the 2014 fiscal year. The company is now shifting towards distributing content via applications on third-party partner devices. The documents do not specify whether the tablets from third-party partners refer to Microsoft's Windows 8 devices or those from other manufacturers (including competing platforms). However, the documents show that Nook will introduce third-party tablet products in 2014.The Nook e-reader has not been included in the discontinuation plan. As users gradually shift towards more versatile tablets, e-readers are bound to gradually fade into history.Currently, spokespeople for both Microsoft and Barnes & Noble have declined to comment on this news.In April 2012, Microsoft invested $300 million in Barnes & Noble to help develop Nook devices and content. Additionally, Microsoft provided an extra $180 million to support the development of content for Windows 8 devices. Therefore, for Microsoft, acquiring Nook Media's e-book business is entirely the next expected step.To date, Nook has sold 10 million devices, including tablets and e-readers, and currently has 7 million active subscribers. Although Microsoft previously announced the sale of 100 million Windows 8 licenses, it is not interested in Nook's Windows 8 devices. Currently, the Nook app supports mainstream platforms such as Android, iOS, and Windows.It is understood that Nook Media officially separated from Barnes & Noble last October. Microsoft acquired a 16.8% stake in the company for $300 million. However, the purpose of this cooperation was to bring Barnes & Noble's content to Windows 8 tablets as soon as possible. Jamie Iannone, president of digital products at Nook Media, stated at the time that the company would maintain a long-term business relationship with Barnes & Noble. The decline of Nook-related businesses has had some impact on Barnes & Noble's development strategy. The company's founder, Leonard Riggio, once proposed repurchasing the company's retail business.The documents show that Barnes & Noble's current valuation is $1.66 billion. When Nook Media was established, just this subsidiary's valuation reached $1.7 billion. When Pearson Group invested $85 million for a 5% stake earlier this year in January, Nook's valuation once reached $1.8 billion. Therefore, if Microsoft can complete the transaction for $1 billion, the offer will be significantly lower than the purchase price when the joint venture was formed.The documents predict that Nook's total revenue for the 2012 fiscal year will be $1.215 billion, with a loss before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization of $262 million. In the 2013 fiscal year, Nook's revenue is expected to drop to $1.091 billion, with a loss of $360 million. By the 2017 fiscal year, its revenue is expected to gradually increase to $1.976 billion, with a profit before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization of $362 million.Meanwhile, Nook has significantly reduced product prices during this year's shopping season. The price of the company's flagship model, the Nook HD+, was cut from $269 to $179, aligning with Amazon's Kindle Fire HD. Despite having superior hardware configuration, Nook remains at a disadvantage in competition with Amazon. If Microsoft can get involved, it is believed that Nook's market position will improve.
