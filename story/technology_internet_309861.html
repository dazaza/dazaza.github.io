Manchester United's fierce attack failed to break through Villa's iron桶defense, and at this moment, Red Devils fans began to miss Cristiano Ronaldo.

Villa's last victory over the Red Devils was on the opening day of the 1995-96 season. It was that game that prompted Alan Hansen's famous statement: "You'll never win anything with kids." Ferguson later silenced Hansen with results. This defeat against Villa again calls for the boss to silence the media with results.

So far this season, in the three top-four battles, the Red Devils have scored no goals in two matches (against Liverpool and Chelsea), and their match against Arsenal saw a penalty from Rooney and an own goal from Diaby. Manchester United has yet to score in open play against the top four this season.

The aftermath of Ronaldo's departure is beginning to show. The Red Devils thrashed Wigan 5-0 this season, beat Manchester City 4-3, crushed West Ham 4-0 last round, and defeated the Bundesliga champions 3-1 in the Champions League midweek. However, it cannot be denied that these opponents often lacked tight defensive organization and played open formations. When faced with teams like Villa who set up a solid defense after scoring, the Red Devils start missing Ronaldo - a killer who can turn non-opportunities into goals.

In this match, Manchester United had 19 shots, but were thwarted by the opposing defenders' disciplined formation and repeated self-sacrificing blocks, leaving the Red Devils with only three shots on target. The best performer for United was Wayne Rooney, who has scored five times against Villa: In the 27th minute, he received a pass from Evra and cleverly cushioned it to Valencia at the far post, but the Ecuadorian didn't arrive in time; Four minutes later, he executed a sublime first-touch control of Evra's cross, shaking off Cuellar in the process, before smashing the ball onto the crossbar!

This was the closest the Red Devils came to scoring all game. As the lone striker, Rooney dropped too deep, taking several long-range shots, but his aim was off this night. It was clear how anxious the little fatty was, as he even tracked back to dispossess Heskey in the second half.

But anxiety is exactly the fatal flaw when breaking down an iron bucket defense. In his haste, the little fatty also committed an unsportsmanlike act: In the 25th minute, he broke into the box trying to draw a penalty kick, but was caught out by the referee and booked. Relying on his national treasure status, the little fatty won't be pursued by the media like Eduardo of Arsenal for diving, but his reputation is now tarnished.

Giggs, despite his age, might have been preoccupied with thoughts of the BBC Sports Personality of the Year award to be presented on the evening of December 13, contributing little beyond hitting the side netting just before halftime. Owen, who came on as a substitute, was almost invisible, and Berbatov, who underwent minor knee surgery just last Monday, was clearly not in form. In the 73rd minute, Carrick delivered a brilliant pass to Berbatov, but he could only stab weakly from the penalty spot!

In the 80th minute, Rooney took a corner, Vidic headed it towards goal, only for Downing to block it on the edge of the area, and thus United lost their last chance. What about Macheda, who scored here last season? The Italian youngster didn't even make the bench for this game! Faced with the fans shouting for "attack," the boss had to admit after the game: "Their defenders were running themselves into the ground, they always blocked players, used their bodies to stop shots, doing everything possible. Villa's defense was really hardworking!"

Ferguson said helplessly after the game: "We didn't have many chances in the first half. But in the second half, we bombarded them relentlessly, practically camping in their penalty area. Unfortunately, we had plenty of good chances but couldn't find the net. If we had taken one, the situation would have changed. But today, the ball just wouldn't go in."

To protect his disciple, Ferguson fell into the quagmire of mysticism. Actually, the problem lies in whether a team aiming for an unprecedented fourth consecutive title should use the opponent's tight defensive organization as an excuse for not scoring?
