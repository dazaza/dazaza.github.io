Comparison of various insulator pollution measurement methods

by zhihao2033 on 2012-02-08 16:35:16

Comparison of Various Insulator Contamination Measurement Methods, 2466 http://www.2466.cc The methods for measuring the degree of insulator contamination include equivalent salt density, contamination layer conductivity, surface conductivity, leakage current, contamination flashover voltage, and contamination flashover gradient. This article selects the measurement of the equivalent salt density of insulators to evaluate the contamination level due to the following reasons:

a. Contamination layer conductivity is defined as the conductance value of the contamination layer per unit surface area of the insulator. In practice, it is obtained by multiplying the conductance derived from the ratio of current to voltage applied across the contamination layer with the shape factor of the insulator. To measure the surface conductivity of the contamination layer, a sufficiently high power frequency voltage must be applied to the insulator under saturated moisture conditions, and the leakage current is measured to obtain the conductance G = I / U. However, this measurement has a large degree of scatter and is significantly affected by uneven distribution of contamination. Additionally, a power source with a large capacity is required for the measurement, making it cumbersome.

b. From a physical perspective, the local surface conductivity of the contamination layer and the overall surface conductivity are the same parameter with identical physical significance; the difference lies only in the measurement method. Although the measurement method is similar to that of equivalent salt density, conductivity is more significantly affected by temperature changes.

c. Leakage current tests involve multiple parameters indicating contamination levels: the maximum pulse amplitude of leakage current under operating voltage, the number of leakage current pulses exceeding a certain amplitude, and the maximum leakage current value just before flashover. Measuring these parameters requires applying a specific voltage to the insulator, which can be inconvenient for on-site testing.

d. Contamination flashover voltage and flashover gradient are the most direct and ideal parameters for characterizing insulator performance. On-site contamination tests can accurately measure the flashover performance of insulators. However, the critical state of natural contamination and the meteorological conditions causing flashovers do not always occur simultaneously. Often, the contamination has reached a critical level, but sufficient humidity conditions have not appeared, making it impossible to measure the critical flashover voltage. Therefore, flashover voltage measurements should be combined with other contamination parameter measurements. The test equipment has a large capacity, the test is inconvenient, and the site does not have the necessary conditions.

e. The measurement method for the equivalent salt density of insulators (the equivalent salt amount per unit surface area of external insulation) involves using a certain amount of distilled water to completely wash off the contaminants on a specific porcelain surface area, then using an equivalent salt density tester to measure the salt density value ESDD of the contaminant solution. The YM3200 direct-reading salt density meter can directly measure and read the ESDD value. Equivalent salt density can intuitively assess the contamination level without being affected by temperature, voltage, test equipment capacity, or test site limitations.

Given the current technology, equipment, and operational feasibility, on-site evaluations generally use the measurement of the equivalent salt density of insulators to assess the contamination level. Equivalent salt density objectively reflects insulator flashover due to contamination and serves as one criterion for judging insulation performance. It can intuitively measure the contamination level without being constrained by temperature, voltage, test equipment capacity, or test site, and it is convenient for on-site operation.
