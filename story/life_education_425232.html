Wisdom Education - SAT Reading Methods and Common Question Types

by thatseed123 on 2011-05-05 22:55:40

In response to the common difficulties faced by Chinese test-takers, a great deal of reading article practice and method explanation is integrated into the practical teaching of SAT classes. Simply put, there are several methods for reading:

1. When encountering an article for the first time, skim through it roughly to find the core idea behind the author's writing and gain an overall sense of the article's structure.

2. Read the questions after the article. If some questions seem difficult to answer on the spot, you can skip them temporarily and return to them after answering other questions.

3. For more complex questions, go back to the original text to find the initial description of the queried item.

4. Try to describe what you believe to be the correct answer in your own words.

5. Finally, review the five options under the question stem and select the correct one.

Of course, while reading articles, test-takers should also follow some basic principles:

☆ Pay attention to the brief introduction about the author and the article at the beginning. Since the SAT requires test-takers to understand the author's thought process and writing style during the exam, test-takers should focus on the provided information about the author and the article from the very start. By reading this content, grasp the basis for the article’s layout and the author’s purpose.

☆ Do not overly dwell on specific details within the article. The details in each article are only meant to illustrate the author's thoughts and descriptions. Therefore, test-takers should adopt a quick-reading method when dealing with these details. Many candidates often hope to precisely understand all the content of the article within the short time frame of the exam, which is impractical both theoretically and realistically, and also unnecessary.

☆ Read the first third of the article more carefully, as important elements are often presented here. These "important elements" refer to the theme of the article. SAT reading passages usually reveal their themes within the first third of the article. Under normal circumstances, the author's writing intent and attitude towards the subject matter become apparent.

☆ Pay attention to the main theme of each paragraph and describe the article's structural pattern in your mind. Paragraphs are essentially the fundamental components of an article. Therefore, test-takers should focus on capturing the main theme of each paragraph and outline the article's general framework. You can ask yourself questions like "What is the main point of this paragraph?" "How does this paragraph connect with the previous and next paragraphs?" "What role does this paragraph play in the article?" The better you understand these questions, the faster your problem-solving speed will be, and the higher your accuracy will improve.

☆ Summarize the content of the article promptly. After finishing the entire article, take a moment to think about its structural pattern and overall layout, and abstractly summarize the central idea of the article in your own words.

Based on past SAT exam questions, the reading section generally includes the following types of questions:

(1) Theme-related questions. This type relates to the overall article, and the question stem might inquire about the main idea or the basic purpose of the article. In the answers for this type of question, the correct answer is the option that clearly summarizes the main content of the article, while incorrect distracting options often come in the following forms:

- Irrelevant to the main idea of the article;

- Clearly opposite to what the article states;

- Replacing the theme of the article with details;

- Exceeding the scope of the article, being too broad;

- Too extreme, exceeding the narrative scope of the article.

(2) Detail-related questions. This type often asks about specific content within a certain part of the article. When solving these types of questions, candidates generally must return to the relevant part of the original text to find the source of the correct answer. Interfering options for detail questions usually come in the following forms:

- Confusing with narratives from other parts of the article;

- Using vocabulary similar to the original text but distorting its meaning;

- Contradicting the original text;

- Content not expressed in the original text and cannot be logically inferred;

- Using extreme narratives.

(3) Inference-related questions. This type can examine content at both the thematic and detailed levels of the article. It mainly requires candidates to deduce options that are not explicitly stated in the article but are logically correct based on the provided information.

(4) Vocabulary-related questions. Vocabulary questions in the reading section primarily test the specific meaning of a word within the context of the article. Candidates need to deduce the exact meaning of the tested word from the context. It is worth noting that even if the candidate knows the word, the final correct answer in the reading passage is often not the familiar meaning commonly known, but rather a more obscure one. This situation is generally referred to as "common words with rare meanings."

A thorough understanding of the various characteristics and specific question types of the SAT reading section provides candidates with a solid foundation for achieving high scores on the SAT. Truly implementing the above points enables candidates to actively and positively engage with SAT articles instead of passively and ineffectively reading them. Through further detailed explanations in class and extensive practice with numerous articles, candidates will certainly conquer the SAT reading section, successfully achieve high scores, and realize their ideal of further education at top American universities.
