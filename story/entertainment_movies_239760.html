"The Founding of a Republic" vs TV Series "Liberation"

"Watch the movie 'The Founding of a Republic', watch the TV series 'Liberation'", this is the slogan proposed by netizens. The key tribute drama for the 60th anniversary of the founding of New China, "Liberation", aired on CCTV-1 on September 7. This 50-episode TV series cost 50 million RMB, had a cast of 2400 actors, and gathered numerous special actors such as Tang Guoqiang, Liu Jin, Wu Fu Wang, and XiaoWei Ma, considered a major bombshell tribute to the 60th anniversary of National Day in the TV series category.

But compared to past tribute dramas, "Liberation" is vastly different.

Difference 1

War scenes cleverly borrowed from movies

The first episode of "Liberation" aired with the Siping Defense Battle, showing a cruel and shocking scene. Besides the three major campaigns - Liaoshen, Huaihai, and Pingjin - there are also famous battles such as the Yan'an Defense Battle and Menglianggu Campaign. Moreover, "Liberation" does not just talk about the victory of the PLA; the first episode frankly discusses the initial defeats in the civil war, especially in the Northeast battlefield, which is a less chosen topic in past TV series.

For the display of large-scale war scenes, "Liberation" differs from previous works with its grand scale; the director and lead actor Tang Guoqiang revealed that many war scenes are cleverly borrowed from the movie "The Great Decisive Battle": "The large-scale scenes are basically similar, but they only have three campaigns, while we have much more here, including scenes and details they don't have."

Difference 2

Leaders no longer high above

In past tribute dramas, leaders either sit and command wars or hold meetings to discuss strategies and tactics, seemingly without private lives. "Liberation" is different; it strives to add ordinary people's lives, making leaders no longer high above.

For example, at that time, Chairman Mao was waiting for frontline news in his cave dwelling in Yan'an when insomnia hit him. So Zhou Enlai invited people to cure him, and the method was a familiar activity to Sichuan people - playing Mahjong. Thus, in the cave dwellings of Yan'an, the founders of the republic played Mahjong. To highlight Chairman Mao's emotional life, there are frequent exchanges with Jiang Qing and warm and harmonious scenes playing with his children.

And meeting scenes are not only in smoke-filled meeting rooms but also in bathhouses and on horsebacks, appearing very vivid.

The image of Premier Zhou Enlai on past screens has always been gentle and elegant, but in "Liberation," it reveals the fiery temper of Zhou Enlai. At that time, Zhou Enlai rarely burst out in anger criticizing the compromise of democratic figures, revealing another side of Zhou Enlai's character.

Difference 3

Leader's appearance different from before

Tang Guoqiang plays Mao Zedong, and Liu Jin plays Zhou Enlai, which seems to have become a rule, but "Liberation" is different from before, breaking through in the leader's appearance.

"'Liberation' is one of the best performances of Mao Zedong I've portrayed," said Tang Guoqiang, who has played Mao Zedong more than 10 times. His feelings about portraying Mao Zedong again in "Liberation" are, "To portray Chairman as more intimate and amiable, he should first be a person, then a great man."

And Zhou Enlai rarely sports a beard. Liu Jin has played Zhou Enlai over 40 times, putting in a lot of effort in "Liberation": "In the period when Zhou Enlai commanded the three major campaigns as Vice Chairman of the Military Commission and Chief of General Staff, his work was very busy. It is well known that Zhou Enlai is a handsome man, but to show the沧桑感 (sense of vicissitudes) of the leaders during that period, the costume designer let me go a few days without shaving, leaving a full face of stubble. After entering Beiping, the costume designer then created a radiant image of the leader."
