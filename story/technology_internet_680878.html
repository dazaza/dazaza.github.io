Supported by buy orders above 1760, spot gold (1769.40, -5.50, -0.31%) regained its upward trend during the European afternoon session on Monday (February 27), but faced pressure and retreated near 1780 during the early New York session.

At 02:22 Beijing time, spot gold was quoted at $1775.00 per ounce; COMEX April gold futures were quoted at $1776.20 per ounce.

Gold has risen over 13% so far this year. Based on expectations that major economies will continue to maintain loose monetary policies, analysts remain bullish on gold in the long term.

Ole Hansen, vice president of Saxo Bank, pointed out, “Buyers who failed to enter the market in time last week went long as the price fell towards 1760 because this entry point provided a good return/risk ratio. The stop-loss for long positions is placed below 1750, with a target of 1800.”

He also stated that if the gold price breaks below 1750, it could lead to a rapid decline due to stop-loss sell orders being triggered, as many investors have established long positions above 1750.

Technical analysts at Standard Chartered Bank noted in their report that once the gold price breaks above the November high of 1,802, it would open an upward channel for a return to historical highs. However, short-term momentum indicators remain bearish on gold strategies [http://www.octopus63.com/]. Support for gold is at $1,705. Although the rebound in gold continues, the resistance at 1802 in the near term will remain effective.

The G20 finance ministers postponed increasing capital for the IMF and requested eurozone leaders to establish a stronger firewall to contain the debt crisis. This pressured the euro and dragged down most commodities, but the positive correlation between gold and the euro has become increasingly weaker recently, indicating more investment buying entering the gold market.

UBS Group pointed out on Monday that gold is moving forward at its own pace rather than simply following other markets. The correlation between gold and risk assets continues to decrease, with its 20-day rolling correlation with the S&P 500 index remaining negatively correlated for three consecutive weeks. The correlation with the euro has also dropped to a one-month low.

Gold's detachment from the euro should help it withstand the impact of negative news from the eurozone, although this also implies that the better-than-expected Long-Term Refinancing Operation (LTRO) might put pressure on gold prices.

CFTC data showed that speculative long positions in gold held by hedge funds and other large speculators rose to a five-month high in the week ending February 21.

Precious metals ETF investments are booming. The holdings of the world's largest gold exchange-traded fund, SPDR Gold Trust, increased by a total of 3 tons on Thursday and Friday last week. As of last week, the total holdings of all gold ETFs reached a record high of nearly 2,397 tons.

As of February 24, the gold holdings of SPDR Gold Trust increased by 1.81 tons to 1,284.61 tons.

The world's largest silver ETF, iShares Silver Trust, saw its silver holdings increase by 60.44 tons as of February 24, reaching 9,692.96 tons.

Spot silver was quoted at $35.55 per ounce; spot platinum at $1707.99 per ounce; spot palladium at $702.50 per ounce.
