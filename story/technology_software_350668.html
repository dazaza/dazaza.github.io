His foundation was very poor, so poor that he wasn't even as good as an ordinary low-level card maker. If he spent time on this card now, it wouldn't be very helpful to his own development. A shaky foundation becomes more dangerous and narrower as you go higher. Although he didn't understand big principles, the simple truths from his childhood wandering life were still clear to him.

These days, he had been immersed in the rapid progress of sensations. Years of accumulation, he never stopped thinking, although some key issues that he didn't know how to solve blocked his progress. And when these plugs were removed, the sensation of the gushing was simply incomparable.

He believed that he could already start trying to make one-star illusion cards.

Illusion cards are a traditional strength of the Tian You Federation. The founder of the card theory, Rosenberg, was most skilled at making illusion cards. The initial stage of illusion cards is shadows, while the advanced stage is object simulation. The actual use of basic illusion cards is not significant; they can only emit some illusions. These illusions, though realistic, are merely images and cannot harm people. Experienced individuals can easily identify them.

The power of advanced illusion cards is quite formidable. They can stimulate the energy of energy cards, simulating objects through special arrangements and combinations, representing high-end weapons and being specialized equipment for card practitioners. Such illusion cards are called Illusion Skill Cards. Generally speaking, what ordinary people refer to as illusion cards mostly refers to low-level illusion cards.

The production of illusion cards differs significantly from energy cards. Energy cards can be produced if the card maker understands their structure and possesses sufficient drawing skills. However, illusion cards are different; they require the card maker to have a certain perception. Perception is the communication ability between card makers and energy.

Energy, material properties, is the most core skill that card makers need to master.

Perception can be enhanced through special methods, which is a required course for every card maker. In today's card maker evaluations, perception is one of the most important and basic abilities, with extremely strict level classifications.

Of course, perception isn't something only card makers need. Some professions, such as card practitioners (card users), also have considerable requirements for perception.

Actually, during the early stages of the card system's development, the era of Rosenberg, there was no such profession as card practitioners. At that time, card makers, as the creators of cards, were also the most familiar with cards. They were adept at using and manipulating cards. During this era, the most common were card masters, who simultaneously studied card making and card techniques.

Until the Hena Van Sant era 300 years ago, after over two hundred years of development, the quantity and variety of card makers had reached a quite impressive number. This allowed more cards to circulate into the hands of non-card makers. As the card system became increasingly complex, card makers needed to learn far more knowledge compared to the Rosenberg era, leading them to gradually focus on research and learning.

It was also from that time that a new profession - card practitioner - emerged. They were professional card users, and because they devoted all their energy to maximizing the effectiveness of cards, they were far better at using cards than those card makers. Human energy is limited, and card makers who both learned card making and used cards often failed to achieve good results in both areas. Over time, the distinction between the two professions became increasingly clear.

However, there were exceptions among them, like Hena Van Sant. He was a genius card maker and also one of the top card practitioners of that era. He became the last recorded card master.

In modern times, the training of perception for card makers and card practitioners has different focuses. Card makers mostly focus on the perception of energy, while card practitioners focus on controlling energy.

There are many methods to train perception, but they all share a common feature, which is the need for a medium. Different media will directly affect the characteristics of perception. The characteristics of perception will then directly influence the cards made by card makers. The nature of the cards will then directly affect the card practitioners who use them. Therefore, in many places, within some schools, card makers and card practitioners often coexist. These card practitioners use the cards of their school, and since they cultivate the same perception, they can often unleash the maximum power of these cards. For example, the famous Bitter Silence Temple produces various mind cards, which almost no one outside of the temple can use effectively.

Compared to card makers, there are more card practitioners. The nobility of card makers goes without saying, but the prerequisite is that you must at least reach mid to high levels. Compared to card makers, card practitioners have much better adaptability. Private bodyguards, explorers, mercenaries... Additionally, card practitioners skilled in controlling water梭cards can move freely underwater, making them the best candidates for underwater exploration. Card practitioners skilled in controlling detection cards are essential personnel for local security and indispensable members of each expedition team.

The types of cards are varied, and the card practitioners, as users, are equally diverse.
