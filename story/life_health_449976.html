What is the best way to terminate an ectopic pregnancy? Treating ectopic pregnancy through hysteroscopic surgery is the best method, favored by many women as it maximally ensures women's health and fertility. Laparoscopic surgery for ectopic pregnancy only requires making a few small incisions near the navel and lower abdomen to complete the surgery. The laparoscopic lens (with a diameter of 3-10mm) is inserted into the abdominal cavity, and the operation method is similar to open surgery, allowing for one-time cure. Its biggest advantage is minimal trauma, quick recovery, less likelihood of pelvic organ adhesion, and less impact on future fertility.

Currently, our hospital generally adopts laparoscopic surgery, where patients can usually be discharged 3-5 days after surgery. This minimally invasive technique has almost replaced open surgery for ectopic pregnancies. It involves less disturbance to the pelvis, no contact with tissues by gauze or hands, and thorough rinsing of the pelvic cavity during surgery. Therefore, post-surgery adhesions in the pelvic cavity are much less than in open surgery. In most cases, the affected fallopian tube can be retained, requiring little or no suturing. Additionally, the scar is small, and the surgery is clear and reliable.
