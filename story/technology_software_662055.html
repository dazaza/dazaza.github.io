Maintenance of White Light Glasses:

1. Follow the instructions on the solution for maintenance. Each bottle of maintenance solution has different emphases, but there isn't necessarily a difference in quality. "In fact, accurate maintenance can achieve the same cleaning results." Also, pay attention to the production date and shelf life of the maintenance solution, and do not use expired solutions. Once opened, the maintenance solution should be discarded within three months, even if it hasn't been fully used.

2. Regularly replace your white light glasses. Even long-wear types, including hard white light glasses, should be updated every 1-2 years. Don't extend the usage period by using daily disposable glasses as bi-weekly disposables or bi-weekly disposables as monthly disposables, as this can harm the cornea.

3. Regularly remove proteins. If using hydrogen peroxide solution, remove proteins first before soaking in the solution. For those using multi-functional maintenance solutions, it is recommended to remove proteins weekly to reduce infection opportunities.

4. Do not store lenses in saline solution. Saline solution does not have a sterilizing effect, and storing lenses in it can easily grow bacteria.

5. Clean the storage box at least once a week and replace it every three months to avoid becoming a breeding ground for bacteria. The cleaning method involves wiping the inside of the small box with a cotton ball soaked in cleaning solution, then disinfecting it in hot water above 79℃, and finally drying it. Try not to keep it in the bathroom, as moisture can easily cause mold.

6. Do not soak the lenses in the solution for a long time, as the solution will gradually lose its disinfection ability and instead become a breeding ground for bacteria. For those who don't wear white light glasses often, it's best to change the soaking solution every two days, and remember to re-disinfect and wash before wearing.

7. Never wear white light glasses while swimming. Chlorine, bacteria, impurities in the swimming pool can attach to the contact lenses, damaging the corneal tissue or causing infections, even leading to corneal ulcers.

8. If you want to switch solutions due to new products or friend recommendations, you must pay special attention to the status after using the new solution. If symptoms such as redness, swelling, itching, foreign body sensation, dryness, tearing, or secretions occur, stop wearing immediately and confirm whether the problem is with the eyes or the solution. If the issue is caused by the solution, it is recommended to stop using it and return to the original solution.

Glasses Maintenance:

1. Single-handed removal or wearing will damage the balance of the frame, leading to deformation. It is suggested to use both hands to hold the temples along the parallel direction of the cheeks for removal and wearing.

2. When removing or wearing, fold the left temple first to prevent frame deformation.

3. It is recommended to rinse the glasses with clean water and dry with a paper towel, then use a dedicated glasses cloth to wipe. Hold the edge of the lens frame on the side being wiped gently and wipe the lens lightly to avoid excessive force that may damage the frame or lens.

4. When not wearing glasses, wrap them with a glasses cloth and put them in the case. If temporarily placing them down, make sure the convex side faces up to avoid scratching the lenses. Also, glasses should avoid contact with insect repellents, toilet cleaners, cosmetics, hair gels, medicines, etc., to prevent long-term sunlight exposure and high temperatures (above 60℃), which can cause deterioration, degradation, or discoloration of the lenses and frames.

5. Regularly visit professional stores for reshaping adjustments. Deformed frames can burden the nose and ears, and the lenses may easily loosen.

6. Do not use glasses during intense activities to avoid strong impacts that could shatter the lenses, causing eye and facial injuries. Do not use worn lenses to avoid light scattering that causes vision decline. Avoid looking directly at the sun or strong blinding light to prevent eye damage.
