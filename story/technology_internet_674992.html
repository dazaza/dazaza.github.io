Case: OEM Private Labeling

1. In 2001, through Ruifeng Accounting Firm, she registered [XX International Apparel Co., Ltd.] in Hong Kong [http://www.rf.hk/company/hongkong/] and simultaneously registered trademarks in both Hong Kong and mainland China. Under the name of the Hong Kong company [http://www.rf.hk/company/hongkong/], she commissioned relevant factories to perform private label processing. By marking the international apparel company’s name on the clothing packaging and labels, her clothing gained its own brand and developed towards a higher-end direction. Now, Miss Lin's family has invested in Hangzhou, introducing advanced equipment to establish a large-scale garment production line factory.

Therefore, they suggested registering an Italian company [http://www.rf.hk/company/italiana/] locally in Hong Kong and simultaneously registering trademarks in both Italy and mainland China [http://www.rf.hk/trademark/]. Case: International Trade

1. [Tax Optimization] Mr. Wang from Fujian: Mainly engaged in project engineering investment bidding, due to business expansion needs, he wanted to establish a reputable group company in mainland China. However, tens of millions of registered capital were unavailable, and the desired company name was already taken by someone else. Later, through a friend's introduction, he established the group company in the Cayman Islands [http://www.rf.hk/company/cayman/introduction.html], and also founded holding subsidiaries in Hong Kong, Samoa [http://www.rf.hk/company/samoa/13235.html], Bahamas, and mainland China, becoming a true multinational group. After meticulous planning by accountants, the company's tax arrangements were optimized.

2. [Import & Export] Ms. Du from Hefei: Long-term engagement in international import trade. Once, a French chemical raw material supplier refused to sign a contract with her and insisted that she commission an overseas intermediary company to sign the contract and issue a letter of credit [http://www.rf.hk/exp/xinyongzheng/], citing the high credibility and reliable payment of overseas intermediaries. Since it was a long-term business, Ms. Du had no choice but to establish a company in Delaware, USA [http://www.rf.hk/company/usa/], and open an offshore account for the Delaware company at a foreign bank in Shanghai (the account possessed all settlement functions in international trade). This way, she established a long-term good cooperative relationship with the client.

3. [Trade Settlement] Mr. Jin from Tianjin: Engaged in international textile product export trade, he had always exported through middle agents but was concerned about commercial secrets and settlement security. To ensure long-term stability and avoid exchange rate differential losses, he wanted to register an overseas offshore company [http://www.rf.hk/company/offshore/], conduct independent exports, and directly settle payments. The prerequisite was that the offshore company did not require complicated financial reporting procedures. With the help of Ruifeng professionals, Mr. Jin registered a BVI offshore company [http://www.rf.hk/company/bvi/bvijr.html] in the British Virgin Islands (this company does not need to provide an annual financial report to the government) and opened an offshore account at a foreign bank in Beijing. This fully met Mr. Jin's offshore trade planning requirements and allowed for greater international development.

5. [Payment Settlement] Mr. Chen from Shenzhen: Has been supplying electronic components to Hong Kong manufacturers.
