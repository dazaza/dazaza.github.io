Real Madrid Exposed: Eight Players Marked for Winter Sale - Separating Fact from Fiction

According to a recent report by a Catalan media outlet, Real Madrid is preparing for a major squad overhaul during the winter break. Several players who are no longer part of Manuel Pellegrini's plans have become redundant members of the squad, simply collecting salaries without contributing on the field. As a businessman, Florentino Pérez must consider costs and profits, leading to the decision to offload these players.

The publication lists eight names: reserve goalkeeper Duduć, backup left-back De Laet, out-of-favor defensive midfielder Gago, frequently excluded forward Van Nistelrooy, reserve holding midfielder Mohamed Diarra, substitute center-back Garay, deputy captain Guti, and backup attacking midfielder Van der Vaart. The report claims that Real Madrid hopes to generate 40 million euros from selling these eight players. However, the prices listed in the article seem arbitrary and do not reflect market values or the actual worth of the players, making this report appear rather amateurish.

For example, Garay is valued at only 5 million euros, which seems implausible given the current state of the European transfer market. Additionally, Van der Vaart is priced at an astonishingly low 1.5 million euros, a fee more suitable for a loan deal than a permanent transfer. Unless the Real Madrid board has lost its mind, such an offer would be dismissed outright.

From the list of names, it is evident that these players do not get much playing time, with some often excluded from matchday squads. However, regardless of whether Florentino prioritizes commercial value, the fundamental principles of team building must be respected. A football team requires both first-choice and reserve players. If Real Madrid were to sell all eight substitutes, how could the team maintain balance? How could they ensure sufficient depth for the challenging second half of the season? To chase 40 million euros, Real Madrid would risk becoming a one-squad team, something that doesn't make sense even with a bit of thought. After all, this is the winter break, not the summer transfer window.

Real Madrid executives Baldazo and Pardeza have repeatedly emphasized that the club's principle for this winter break is neither to buy nor sell players. However, if a player insists on leaving and the interested club offers a reasonable bid, Real Madrid might consider it. That said, the club will not approve all transfer requests, and so far, no satisfactory offers have been received.

Dudkać knew from the day he joined Real Madrid that his role would be as a substitute, and the Polish goalkeeper has never complained about his limited playing time. This summer, Real Madrid decided to keep Dudkać, and he accepted a new contract. It’s unlikely that he would leave during the winter break, and Real Madrid wouldn’t release a backup goalkeeper for free mid-season.

De Laet also has limited chances but still plays occasionally. Real Madrid needs a backup for their left flank, and there are no buyers offering a reasonable price for him. Moreover, De Laet himself has expressed his desire to succeed at Real Madrid.

Mohamed Diarra, after recovering from injury, has become a substitute but has no complaints. He gets some playing time off the bench and believes that with effort, his playing time will increase. His agent recently stated that Diarra would participate in the Africa Cup in January and wouldn't consider a transfer during the winter break.

Garay is the least likely to leave Real Madrid. The club views him as a future asset, and the defense needs to ensure backup strength. With Pepe and Albiol unable to play every game, Real Madrid would never sell Garay for a mere 5 million euros.

Van der Vaart didn't consider leaving during the toughest times and now that his situation has improved, he has no intention of departing. His wife Silvia also wishes to stay in Madrid for her breast cancer treatment. After Kaká's injury, Van der Vaart became a key player for the team. In a recent interview, he explicitly stated his intention to remain at Real Madrid.

Guti has no motivation to leave Madrid. We've already mentioned that he doesn't want to leave the Madrid environment, especially with few years left in his career. He doesn't wish to wander elsewhere.

Currently, the most likely departures are Gago and Van Nistelrooy. Both want to play in the World Cup but find themselves sidelined. Their agents are actively seeking buyers, though none have emerged yet. No one wants to pay 15 million euros for Gago, and Van Nistelrooy's request for a free transfer hasn't been approved. They continue their search, and Real Madrid, seeing their eagerness to leave and sufficient depth in their positions, has reluctantly agreed to let them go if reasonable offers come in.

Italian agent Bronzetti noted that Van Nistelrooy joining a Serie A team is unlikely. In fact, according to the information he has, Real Madrid does not intend to let any players leave during the winter break. One of Real Madrid's top executives, Pardeza, revealed that the club has no plans for transfers in the winter market, stating, "The winter transfer window is short and risky. We have no plans to buy or sell."
