They are passionate about starting their own business but at the same time worry about the tragic fate after failure! Therefore, many friends think for a long time and finally decide to wait and see. One month passes after another, one year passes after another. Time flows by, and they are deeply immersed in the life of being an employee. After getting married and buying a house, the economic burdens on them become increasingly heavy, leaving them powerless to fight anymore. The people of Wenzhou in China are known as the wealthiest group in China. I know several young friends from Wenzhou. When they were still in college, their concepts were already extraordinary. Their favorite phrases were: "Rather sleep on the floor than be an employee" or "Rather do business and earn 1000 yuan a month than work as an employee and earn 3000 yuan a month."

These principles are just some concepts. This concept is very different from the thoughts of many other places. Even if there are similar choices now, I estimate that many friends would make the opposite choice. However, this concept is quite advanced. It is precisely this concept that makes the people of Wenzhou extraordinary and become the wealthiest group in China.

"Thinking determines the future"!!!

An economist from Zhejiang Province once summarized the working philosophy of the people of Zhejiang: "Rather be a start-up wolf than a working dog." Of course, this statement is extreme, and many employees don't want to listen. But if we think carefully, what this guy said really makes sense.

Wolves pursue freedom. They prefer independent personalities and free thinking. Every day, they run on the grasslands, hunt cattle and sheep at will, and enjoy all the delicacies provided by nature. After eating, they lie on the grass, think of nothing, and enjoy the sunshine and free air. They are the masters of the grasslands, and they have dignity.

However, when the cold comes, they must learn to resist the severe wind and snow, learn to find prey under the thick snow, often endure the pain of hunger, and always worry about freezing or starving to death. The life of wolves can be described as half water and half fire.

The life of dogs is just the opposite. Dogs usually eat the leftovers from their owners, are ordered around everywhere, have no freedom, and no dignity. They can only beg for mercy. The life of dogs is guaranteed. Although they never eat anything delicious, they don't worry about hunger or cold in winter because they are protected by their owners. Dogs hate it deeply and swear to remain loyal to their owners in the next life. Even if the owners scold and beat them, most dogs dare not say a word because obedience and submission are the marks of dogs.

Enduring humiliation is the label of dogs. For a stable job and a fixed ration of food when they get old, they accept everything!

If we compare the lives of people in real life, I guess most of us live like dogs, stable and comfortable, but never fully satisfied. We want to leave the doghouse of our employers, but lack the courage to fight to the bitter end!

I feel that the career choices of young people in reality are mostly to be working dogs rather than start-up wolves. Many friends may say, how can fresh graduates have the capital and experience to start a business? The best choice is to work in a company for a few years, accumulate some experience and funds, and then start their own business if there is an opportunity in a few years.

In fact, such an idea proves to be wrong. After working for a few years, a university graduate not only can't earn the money needed for entrepreneurship but also can't learn the experience and skills of entrepreneurship. The things learned from working life are basically useless for entrepreneurship because the perspectives are different, the ways of thinking are different, and the experiences gained are also different. To put it simply, after working for a few years, the only thing you gain improvement in is your working skills, and what entrepreneurship needs least is technical skills.

More terrifyingly, after working for a few years, young people generally lose the passion for entrepreneurship and the courage of the inexperienced. They become increasingly immersed in the unit and cannot extricate themselves. Later, the motivation for entrepreneurship can only stay in their hearts forever, becoming a permanent regret.

When they are around 40 years old and are laid off by the boss, they regret why they didn't start their own business 20 years ago!

So, is the requirement to be a start-up wolf really high?

My answer is: As long as you have the concept of being a start-up wolf, even if you only have 100 yuan in your pocket, you can still succeed in entrepreneurship!

How much money did the older generation of Wenzhou people have when they started doing business? Wasn't it by exchanging eggs and iron pots that they earned their first pot of gold? If you only have 100 yuan, you can at least set up a stall on the street, sell socks and gloves in winter, pineapples in summer, and then barbecue when you have more funds. Anyway, no matter how little money you have, you can still start the path of entrepreneurship. The key is whether you want to be a start-up wolf?

Working life and entrepreneurial life must be different.

Over time, the personalities of workers and entrepreneurs will have increasingly large differences.

We should ask ourselves honestly: Are friends who have been working for a long time more prone to fear loss, afraid of the unfamiliar world outside, and afraid of the risk of unemployment? Their hearts become increasingly sensitive and fragile.

Not only does their mindset gradually become tired and lazy, but the whole person also loses their sharpness and energy. They can only comfort themselves, satisfy themselves with happiness, and be indifferent to fame and wealth.

But life becomes increasingly monotonous, family economic burdens become increasingly heavy, housing and children's education gradually become economic ropes around their necks, tightening more and more, making it hard to breathe. They can only adjust their mindsets and gradually adapt to the positioning of small urban insects, thinking they are originally mediocre people. Why should mediocre people disturb themselves? Getting rich is someone else's business. They don't have that fate.

The most fearful thing is undoubtedly hearing the news that the company is not doing well and plans to lay off employees. The most important thing is to maintain good relations with the leaders, keep learning flattery and sycophancy, and adapt to local customs.

Their minds become numb, and they can only follow the crowd. After all, the bowl of rice is in the hands of the leader or the boss. If he wants to kick you out, you have to go.

The second most fearful thing is seeing oneself getting older day by day, but without any improvement in work skills. Although the salary increases with seniority, looking at the energetic young university graduates coming in, they work hard for so little salary. The boss's criticism echoes in their ears: Look at those young people who eat less and work more, while you old revolutionaries are getting worse with age.

No wonder, the result of working life is depreciation with age, especially in the 40-50 age group, which is almost the saddest stage of one's career. At any moment, it feels like falling into an abyss, like stepping into a minefield, easily leading to layoffs and unemployment.

Some people say that the past ten years were the peak period for blue-collar workers' layoffs, and the next 10-20 years will be the peak period for white-collar workers' layoffs. Do you think that having a university degree makes you an elite?

Society is constantly progressing, but your knowledge structure, physical fitness, and professional concepts are no longer comparable to the newly graduated university students. The boss is practical and will definitely lay off the white-collar workers in the 40-50 age group first when encountering a crisis.

So, having a stable job now and feeling safe is actually equivalent to having a time bomb planted next to you. After more than ten years, it will explode, and the pain and cost of unemployment at that time will probably be 100 times crueler than now.

So what are the personality characteristics of entrepreneurs?

I have a relative with whom I have a close relationship. He is already a billionaire now, and he is 39 years old. Originally, he was a discharged soldier and later entered a state-owned unit where he always felt oppressed. With his strong personality, he immediately resigned and chose to venture into the business world.

Ten years later, he has accumulated assets worth hundreds of millions. During the Spring Festival when our relatives gathered, he told me: "XX, you are the one with the highest academic degree in our family, and you teach in a university. Tell me how much your annual income is."

I honestly answered: "My annual income is less than 50,000 yuan."

He continued: "I have observed a phenomenon. Those who work for me in the company all performed better than me during school days. So, the lowest academic qualification among them is secondary vocational school. I worked hard for three years in high school, but I couldn't even pass the entrance exam for secondary vocational school, so I had to join the army. Therefore, I have always respected educated people, thinking they are intelligent and capable. But now I am their boss, providing them with jobs, which is unexpected even to me."

Why is this?

He continued to analyze: "I think the success of a person's employment depends on three factors, namely IQ, EQ, and courage quotient (dare quotient). But for a true talent, the importance of these three should be ranked as follows: courage quotient first, EQ second, IQ third. Currently, the so-called talents cultivated by Chinese education are very one-sided, only superior in IQ, but EQ and courage quotient are not included at all."

After listening to my relative's words, I was greatly shocked. His words seem to make a lot of sense!

I carefully thought about it, and it seems that in survival competition, people with great courage often have advantages over smart people.

Smart people often lack courage, while courageous people are often not too smart.

There is another interesting phenomenon: the more books people read, the smarter they are, but the more books they read, the smaller their courage tends to be. Smart people like to think carefully before acting, while those with less education tend to act first and think later. These people with strong action ability often achieve success first.

From the perspective of IQ, the group with the highest intelligence level in China currently is often the so-called master's and doctoral students, and the professor group. Most of them work and survive in universities, but the overall income level of this supposedly smartest group is not high. A doctorate's normal annual income in a university is 50,000 yuan, a master's degree holder earns 40,000 yuan, and professors mostly don't exceed 80,000 yuan (this is the higher education institution in Jiangsu Province where I am located).

This is a phenomenon worth studying: the smartest group does not earn the highest income, not even as much as the high-level white-collar workers in companies.

Finally, I came to a conclusion: Doing well in studies is not useful!

The risk of entrepreneurship is losing the expected income from working for a few years, and even after bankruptcy, you may have to pay back some borrowed money. But after all, you are young, and you can fight again and again. You can absorb nutrients and experience from failures, and your business acumen and ability will increase each time. After gradually integrating into the business community, your vision and experience will increase day by day, eventually achieving a qualitative leap from quantitative change.

As long as you truly enter the business world, your future career will basically be smooth sailing. You will earn more and more money, accumulate more and more wealth, and your own value will be maximized.

Concepts determine how you choose, choices determine which path you take, and which path you take determines your future life process and results.

The ancients also said: "Men fear choosing the wrong industry, women fear marrying the wrong man." This saying is actually profound. There are 365 industries in the world, and all of them can be done. But for the same person, choosing to do business and choosing to work can lead to very different futures. The difference is really huge!

If you choose a life of entrepreneurship, you may be relatively lucky, and in the future, you can become a billionaire in China, donate several Hope Primary Schools at will, and occasionally enjoy golf and private yachts.
