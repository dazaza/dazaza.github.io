Team building is the lifeline of the company_2677www.zp-nmg.com

by xeybmlqv on 2011-10-26 16:05:19

Reflecting on the process, under Mr. Yang's direct guidance, I was very fortunate to participate in the establishment of Tiandi Company in Sui Ning and Pei County, as well as the team building and other works. Coming to an unfamiliar environment, starting a job that I have never touched before, there were moments of success and joy, but also hardships and trials along the way. As I journeyed through this experience, I met many people and gained much personally.

In interacting with local distributors, I saw their shortcomings and discovered the characteristics that contributed to their success. Boss Tian's hard work, Boss Yuan's meticulous cost control, and Boss Wei's warm and approachable nature stood out. From various misunderstandings about the company's establishment to fully cooperating with all aspects of the company's development, we had numerous confrontations. At times when I felt unable to continue progressing, Mr. Yang provided me with excellent advice and trust, which helped transform the county distributors from misunderstanding to understanding, from obstruction to cooperation, thus enabling the company to rise to new heights in the county. Although perhaps the first step wasn't taken firmly enough, with the continuous learning and growth of the company and its employees, the future of the county company will surely align with the expectations of the leadership. The biggest insight I gained from working in the county is: for business partners, only through sufficient trust and delegation can we sincerely cooperate, striving wholeheartedly for mutual benefits. Only then can the cooperative company grow and strengthen normally and rapidly. Currently, it's gratifying to see that each company is growing in a healthy and steady manner. For issues encountered in work, they should be reported promptly to the leadership and solutions discussed together.

Now, I've been back working in Baoxue County for nearly a month. While maintaining contact with various counties, I have become more deeply immersed in my current work. From May 31st to June 25th, I visited 62 town markets, organized terminal data for 668 households. This data included customer locations, store names, contacts, phone numbers, product items and specifications sold by them. While registering customer information, I worked alongside sales staff to exchange materials, organize shelves, communicate with customers, recommend new products, and set visit routes, discussing ways to improve deficiencies in our work. I noticed significant improvements in Li Ning’s work methods and attitude. Other colleagues also strengthened their focus on terminal packaging and customer relations.

The rural market truly has infinite prospects. Shi Yong's small grain store sells a considerable amount of rice. Li Ning's small dry goods wholesale department does well in sesame oil sales. Zhong Yihua's street wholesale department's Yuanbao oil is also very popular. The most memorable case was a small village called Hou Xiang in Wang Shihai's area. Despite its narrow streets, it has six customers who can consume over 30 boxes of 5-liter soybean oil in a week. Now, weddings and funerals in rural areas require over 60 pounds of cooking oil at once, equivalent to 6 bottles of 5-liter oil. Many terminals rely on such occasions to sell their oil. Our younger generation living in rural areas has deeply embraced the health consumption concept of packaged oil and no longer actively purchases loose oil. Of course, currently speaking, the rural market has not yet reached the urban consumption level. Most of our products still remain in the warehouses of our clients. In developing work in rural areas, I believe the three most important points are: one, comprehensive image packaging; two, good customer relations; three, selling the inventory in the terminals as quickly as possible. Simply transferring stock isn’t fundamental; helping terminal customers sell the oil and bringing tangible profits to the customers is the most practical work. During visits with colleagues, we discussed many relevant issues: treating the client's store as your own, considering the client as your best friend, standing in the other party's shoes, listening more, observing more, thinking more, talking less, doing more, and going beyond the call of duty to help clients, thereby winning their goodwill and trust. Cultivate clients into our most loyal and professional frontline promoters, and form the best friendships with the relevant stockers in every supermarket. Ask yourself, in such a sales environment, how could competitors ever enter the hearts of these clients?

"Helping others is helping oneself." A small gift or a warm greeting, while genuinely helping them, indirectly and wisely helps ourselves. In today's market economy system, whoever wins the hearts of the customers wins the market. In specific market operations, under the guidance of the company's leadership, I believe the following four points can effectively strengthen our team and service philosophy:

1. "Details Determine Success or Failure"

A harsh word in summer chills the heart, a kind word in winter warms it. There are no trivial matters in work; one detail can win the favor of a customer, and one sentence might lose a terminal. Therefore, while the company focuses on professional skill training for sales personnel, it should also train them in interpersonal etiquette and skills. Pay attention to personal appearance and conduct, often reflect on one's own mistakes, and avoid gossiping about others.

2. "Winning Through Execution"

An excellent brand and a great strategy without an excellent team to promote and execute it is worthless. How to build a highly execution-capable team? First, the company leadership must lead by example, consistently follow through on sales plans without frequent changes, avoid making promises lightly, and if made, fulfill them promptly. Care for employees' lives and health, explain the reasons and prospects of marketing plans comprehensively to sales personnel, establish reasonable reward and punishment systems, and avoid leaning towards the notion that fines alone can manage teams. If execution is insufficient, fines are acceptable, but are rewards for successful execution implemented effectively? One breach of trust might lose the hearts of employees. The company is responsible for both employees and customers, and employees, in turn, are responsible for the company and customers. I also discovered in the market that previous clients who had a good impression of Fulinmen and Shengzhou soybean oil lost their affection due to a lack of a company responsible for clients and a team capable of executing business standards, leading to non-cooperation with their staff and resulting in no bottles of their products in many terminals.

3. "Exerting Effort Only Makes You Competent, Applying Heart Makes You Excellent"

In the same work and environment, some people thrive while others struggle. I often tell my colleagues, we are not laborers, our work is not merely moving inventory. We have many details to pay attention to and many relationships to maintain. When you stand in front of a terminal customer, you must consider multiple identities. Exerting effort makes you a qualified salesperson, but applying heart reveals unexpected opportunities. Saying the same sentence with a different tone or handling the same task differently can yield unexpected results. For instance, when promoting new products at the same terminal, one salesperson may sweat profusely explaining with professional knowledge, while another builds a friendship first and casually recommends the product during friendly chats. Which approach do you think would be more easily accepted?

4. "Every Rule Has Its Exceptions"

Company leadership should not only see employees' faults but also discover their strengths. No one is perfect, just as gold cannot be pure. In real market performance, while pointing out employees' inadequacies, leadership should also affirm what they do well. A word of praise, an approving attitude, can make sales personnel naturally self-reflect, correct flaws, and develop strengths.

Finally, I want to say that although brands are important, a team that can unite with the company, integrate with clients, and has strong execution capabilities is the company's greatest asset. "The road ahead is long and arduous, I shall search high and low."

I myself still have much to learn, but I also believe that under the wise leadership of the company, Tiandi Company in Xuzhou will surely have a brighter future!
