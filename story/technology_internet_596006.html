Nankai University is criticized for inaction over the damage to the statue of Zhou Enlai on its Weibo account

by edivista on 2011-11-30 13:55:42

Click to enter Nankai University's official Weibo account. A netizen posted a message on Sina Weibo stating that the statue of Zhou Enlai in Nankai University's campus was damaged. Subsequently, Nankai University's official Weibo commented on and forwarded this post, but its passive comment was heavily criticized by netizens.

Yesterday afternoon, Sina netizen Sa Benye posted a short first-person message on Weibo, which only had 16 characters: "I love Nankai. Why can't my eyes see clearly?" It was accompanied by three pictures. The pictures showed the statue of Zhou Enlai in Nankai University's campus. In the close-up picture, it could be clearly seen that something had blocked or damaged the eye area of the Zhou Enlai statue. The reporter subsequently contacted netizen Sa Benye and confirmed that all these photos were taken by him on that day.

This Weibo post was then forwarded by many netizens who were concerned about this matter, including the ID "Nankai University" Weibo account, which has a blue V certification on Sina Weibo, verifying it as Nankai University's official Weibo account. The Weibo account commented: "In recent days, many teachers, students, and alumni have reflected that the eye of the Premier's statue is damaged, and it is heart-wrenching to look at. I hope relevant departments can handle it in time." However, such a tone immediately raised doubts among many netizens. The first netizen to reply, He Ma Lao Da, commented: "Isn't this ID representing the relevant department?"

Subsequently, many netizens expressed similar doubts. Netizen Xue De Jia Tu Suo commented: "Is getting a V certification just for looking good? Even if you are not the relevant department, shouldn't you be pushing for a solution?" Later, Nankai University's official Weibo apologized to netizens: "There was indeed some problem with my expression, and I apologize to you. You can understand it this way: Alumni and teachers reflect that the eye of the Premier's statue is damaged, and it is heart-wrenching to look at. I hope relevant departments can handle it in time. I am making a repost, which already indicates my attitude. What do you think?" However, netizen Xue De Jia Tu Suo seemed not to accept such an apology and replied: "You don't need to apologize to me. I was wrong, not knowing that your responsibility is just to repost and make a statement. I wonder whether the guy who initially reported this to you on Weibo also misunderstood. Here's a suggestion: If you can't meet the expectations, change your name."
