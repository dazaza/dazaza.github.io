Don't Rush to Judge the Phone of the Year - Nexus 5 Hasn't Made Its Appearance Yet

by anonymous on 2013-09-18 21:34:08

The September launch extravaganza is gradually coming to an end, with the Meizu MX3, Galaxy Note 3, Xperia Z1, Xiaomi 3, and the two new iPhones all having made their appearances. Faced with a deluge of flagship devices, many people surely have their favorite model in mind.

Don't rush to pick this year's phone of the year just yet, because Google’s budget-busting Nexus 4 hasn’t been updated.

The Nexus 4 was unveiled on October 30th last year. Due to Hurricane Sandy, the device’s debut was rather “underwhelming.” However, this didn’t stop the $299 starting price Nexus 4 from sweeping across tech media headlines.

Subsequently, the Nexus 4’s explosive market performance demonstrated its high cost-effectiveness. The device even experienced stockouts at one point. LG executive Cathy Robin stated that demand for the Nexus 4 was ten times what Google had anticipated.

Let’s take a look back at the Nexus 4’s specs:

Runs Android 4.2, 1.5GHz Qualcomm Snapdragon S4 Pro quad-core processor, 2GB RAM, 4.7-inch display with a resolution of 1280×768, rear 8MP camera, front 1.3MP camera, 2100mAh battery.

The Nexus 4 was priced at $299 for the 8GB version and $349 for the 16GB version. Recently, both versions were reduced by $100. Currently, the 8GB version is sold out, and Google has indicated that they won't restock it. This seems to hint at the upcoming arrival of the Nexus 4 successor, the Nexus 5.

PhoneArena compiled possible specs for the Nexus 5 based on rumors:

4.96-inch display with a resolution of 1920 x 1080, Qualcomm Snapdragon 800 quad-core processor (MSM8974), 2300mAh battery, running Android 4.4 KitKat, multiple LTE bands supporting Verizon, AT&T, Sprint, and T-Mobile, design similar to the Nexus 7 tablet.

In addition to these specs, the camera on the Nexus 5 is expected to see significant upgrades. Google executive Vic Gundotra hinted at this early this year, and the Android 4.4 promotional video released on September 4th shows what appears to be a Nexus 5 device with a large camera lens.

Notably, September 4th was also the day Samsung and Sony launched their flagship phones. The seemingly casual leak of the Nexus 5 could very well be part of a marketing strategy orchestrated by Google.

Subsequently, someone unearthed a large number of images suspected to be related to the Nexus 5 on the FCC website.

The model in the image does not have a device name labeled, but its appearance closely resembles the suspected Nexus 5 exposed in the Android 4.4 promotional video, especially the large camera lens. It can be seen that the device does not use the glass back cover of the Nexus 4, instead opting for a matte plastic similar to the new Nexus 7.

Based on current market information, October is likely to be the release time for the Nexus 5, when Android 4.4 will also make its debut. The official release date should be around November. Given Google’s current positioning of Nexus phones (considering the existence of Moto X), the Nexus 5 will still follow a high cost-effectiveness route, starting at around $299.

There is another intriguing suspense regarding the Nexus 5: whether this model will come in both LG and Motorola Mobility versions (already rumored).

Motorola Mobility spent six months renovating a factory originally used by Nokia to produce highly customized Moto X devices. Currently employing 2,500 workers, although weekly shipments are only 100,000 units, Motorola Mobility says expanding capacity is no problem.

To maximize the efficiency of factories and personnel, it is not impossible for Motorola Mobility to secure the "manufacturing rights" for the Nexus 5. Of course, production costs in U.S.-based factories are higher than in Asia, which might impact device profits.
