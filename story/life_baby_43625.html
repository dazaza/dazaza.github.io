Xiao San's Weight Loss Diary (Part II)

by xiaoyige on 2008-01-18 10:29:29

Under the nourishment of her mother's milk, Little Third soon broke through 12 jin (approximately 13 pounds). After some reflection, Third Mother identified two reasons: first, she herself was eating too much, which made her milk overly nutritious; second, Little Third had an insatiable appetite and would always open her little mouth to ask for more food without any restraint. With the support of the doctor, Third Mother decided to immediately start a weight loss plan for Little Third.

Third Mother formulated the following strategy:

1. Reduced feeding from six times to four times.

2. Limited meat-based soups to only once a day, replacing the rest with vegetable-based soups.

3. Maintained regular breastfeeding intervals, feeding every three hours.

The first two points were easy to implement. Not only did they help Little Third lose weight, but they also helped Third Mother herself shed some pounds. This win-win situation allowed Third Mother to control her own eating habits tightly (actually, she didn't want to eat that much anyway), and she would rather pour out the soup she couldn't finish than eat another bite.

As for the third point, it wasn't so easy to implement. Little Third had a big appetite, and each feeding session usually lasted over an hour. During the first month, whenever she cried loudly due to discomfort from a wet diaper, Third Mother would quickly offer her the breast to let her eat to her heart's content. Now, strictly following the book, Third Mother fed Little Third every three hours during the second month. If it hadn't been three hours yet, no matter how loudly she cried, Third Mother remained unmoved and refused to feed her. Sometimes, seeing her cry with tears streaming down her face (some say babies under three months don't cry tears, but our Little Third started crying tears on the third day after birth and has been doing so more frequently since then), Third Mother felt truly sorry. However, for Little Third's health and future slim figure, Third Mother decided to stick to her guns.

One day, at 4 PM, Little Third had just finished her afternoon snack. By 6 PM, she was loudly protesting to Third Mother, demanding dinner. Since the time hadn't come yet, Third Mother naturally ignored her. Little Third already knew how to make demands and continued crying persistently. The first one to give in was Third Mother's own mother. She ordered her daughter, "Quickly, feed your daughter!" Hmph, even if she's my mom, it doesn't matter. Third Mother didn't listen and seriously told her mom, "It's not time yet, she can't eat. You need to cooperate with me." On this principle issue, Third Mother's father finally realized the severity of the problem and firmly supported her. He said with foresight, "To prevent our Little Third from hating her mom later for feeding her too much and becoming overweight, we must scientifically feed her and not overfeed her!" Third Mother gratefully glanced at her father, plugged her ears, and stopped paying attention to her daughter's cries. With the support of Third Mother's father, Third Mother seemed to see hope for Little Third's successful weight loss. As mentioned earlier, Third Mother's father was the primary culprit for Little Third's current weight. Since he had such awareness now, why worry about Little Third not losing weight?

Thus, Third Mother followed her perfect plan until now, recording Little Third's daily diet and routines. Until today, Little Third has basically developed the habit of eating every three hours. However, before and after her bath in the afternoon, Third Mother would sometimes add an extra meal as a small reward for good behavior.

Alas, our chubby girl, the cotton clothes bought by the beautiful auntie in size 6M are too tight for you, making you cry loudly. Even the pretty little coat your mom bought in sizes 6-9M seems a bit small for you. When will you slim down a little?

For health and beauty, mom will definitely stick to the end with you on this weight loss journey!
