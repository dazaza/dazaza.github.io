by wzyei596 on 2012-02-26 11:11:24

Professional social networking sites are currently engaged in fierce competition centered around online recruitment. China's largest professional social networking site, Tianji.com, has launched a recruitment channel. Compared to traditional online recruitment, the positioning of professional social networking sites entering the recruitment market creates differences and segments this market. Just as Tianji.com’s recruitment channel is named “Elite Recruitment,” which precisely reflects its positioning. This market is bound to experience significant growth in the near future, challenging the traditional recruitment market.

Professional social networking sites have accurately identified their market positioning. In the past, although the online recruitment market appeared competitive on the surface, it still provided opportunities for newcomers like professional social networking sites. Currently, there are mainly two types of recruitment websites: the first type covers middle and lower-level groups through traditional recruitment sites such as 51job and Zhaopin.com; the second type includes classified information websites covering blue-collar workers, chefs, housekeeping service providers, etc., such as 58同城 and Ganji.com. In contrast, there are no specialized websites targeting high-end groups, which are mostly covered by headhunters.

Now, numerous professional social networking sites are entering the online recruitment field, primarily targeting high-end users. Although it is a niche market, it has enormous market potential and is essentially a "vacuum" domestically. A recent report from the overseas social recruitment website JobVite shows that approximately 89% of U.S. companies are using social media for recruitment. Social networks can present more comprehensive information about job seekers, allowing interaction with employers, making recruitment more personalized and facilitating talent discovery.

Domestically, this field is gradually gaining recognition and importance, with more and more professional social networking sites entering this market. Who will gain an advantage in this market? It likely depends on several key aspects:

Firstly, the scale of high-end users. To attract corporate attention, a substantial base of mid-to-high-end talent users is necessary. Mid-to-high-end talents are mostly passive job seekers, rarely submitting resumes actively, and thus are not easily found on traditional recruitment websites. Conversely, these individuals are concentrated on professional social networking sites. Currently, in China's professional social networking market, Tianji.com is growing rapidly, with 250,000 new users per month. In the second quarter of this year, the number of new users increased by 300% compared to the previous quarter, and the total membership is expected to reach 10 million by the end of the year. More than 65% of these users are mid-to-high-end professionals, establishing Tianji.com's leading position in the professional social networking market and laying a solid foundation for capturing a share of the online recruitment market.

Secondly, user experience. Understanding user needs, improving user experience, and enhancing user stickiness are critical to the development of recruitment services on professional social networking sites. These platforms can offer users a completely new experience in online recruitment, serving both as a communication platform and a comprehensive showcase where users can display their career backgrounds, views, experiences, styles, networks, and interests comprehensively. This allows for two-way interaction between enterprises and job seekers, breaking the previous limitation of resumes only being able to display basic flat information. Tianji.com continues to revamp its platform focusing on user experience, providing more display opportunities for users, such as being the first to add an English version of personal profiles. As a result, users not only gain more social connections but also help enterprises understand job seekers better.

Lastly, professional services. The recruitment business of professional social networking sites has significant growth potential but requires professional and considerate services. In comparison, Tianji.com leads significantly in this aspect. Its recruitment channel collaborates with CR International, a well-known global provider of comprehensive recruitment solutions, conducting deep cooperation in multiple areas such as recruitment position management, employer brand communities, and online/offline marketing activities. This helps better grasp the future development trends of the online recruitment market and provides users with superior service experiences.

In conclusion, a large user base, better user experience, and professional services enable Tianji.com to take the lead in the online recruitment battle among professional social networking sites.
