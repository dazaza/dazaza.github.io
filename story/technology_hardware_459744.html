Abstract: In the discussion of the objectives and effectiveness of computer-aided project management, this paper highlights the analysis and comparison of several widely used modern construction project management methods. It provides an overview of the development and necessity of computer software applications in project management and explores future development trends.

Keywords: Modern construction, Project Management, Computer Applications; Network Planning; Integrated Control Document Classification

Project management developed as a new management technology at the end of the 1950s. As an efficient organizational, planning, coordination, and control technique for construction projects, it has played an important role in construction. The application of computer technology in project management began in the late 1950s with network planning technology. In 1957, DuPont Corporation collaborated with the RAND Corporation to integrate computer technology with the Critical Path Method (CPM), which is a method for using network diagrams in planning.

In 1958, the U.S. Navy's Special Projects Bureau proposed the Program Evaluation and Review Technique (PERT) during the Polaris missile submarine process, aimed at handling large and complex projects. This technique combined mathematical statistics and network analysis methods, fully utilizing computer technology while considering various factors, representing a significant improvement over CPM's critical path method. Both CPM and PERT demonstrated their advantages in initial computer applications. At the end of the 1950s to the early 1960s, project management developed step by step as a science, and computer technology began to be widely used. The U.S. Apollo moon landing program, which lasted 11 years, achieved ultimate success due to the successful integration of computer technology in project management. Since the 1960s, with more and more large-scale projects worldwide, both project management and computer technology have experienced significant development globally.

In China, starting from the 1960s, technologies similar to CPM and PERT gradually promoted the use of these methods in projects. However, there remains a significant gap between the level of project management and computer application in China compared to international advanced levels. Currently, many enterprises in China only apply computer technology at the local system or text-processing report level. Therefore, there is an urgent need for research and development of project management and computer applications in our country. From a modern perspective, applying computer technology in modern construction project management is necessary given the characteristics of modern construction projects, such as large scale, long duration, uncertain factors, economic and technical risks, and profound impacts on society and the environment. Thus, advanced, scientific project management that integrates systems theory, control theory, information theory, and other scientific theories should be applied, utilizing scientific methods like probability statistics, earned value, numerical simulation, etc., for quantitative evaluation. Many of these theories and methods are built on the foundation of computer applications. From the perspective of project management:

Modern construction project management involves comprehensive management and control of the entire project, including not only traditional quality, schedule, and cost control but also contract management, information management, organization, and coordination. It encompasses design, procurement, finance, risk management, and other aspects. The content of management cannot be separated from large database systems and powerful data processing capabilities. As projects become increasingly complex and resources grow, manual calculations become impractical for accurate and effective handling, making the use of computers essential.

(3) Concept of project management processes. Information collection, tracking, and processing play a crucial role in project management. In modern construction projects, information interaction is frequent, and the completeness, timeliness, and accuracy of information collection, storage, and processing are very high. This requires the assistance of advanced computer technology to effectively achieve these tasks.

(4) From the perspective of project management solutions. To achieve project management objectives, multiple solution choices are involved, essentially making it a multi-objective decision-making process for optimal selection. Whether it’s PERT, linear programming, numerical simulation, or the use of advanced decision systems like expert systems, the application of advanced project management decision models cannot be separated from computer technology.
