After successfully solving a series of major cases in Youzhou, Bingzhou, Sheling, Jiangzhou, and others, Di Gong was finally granted permission by Empress Wu to return to his hometown of Bingzhou for rest after repeated requests. He planned to take a detour on his way home to visit Zeng Tai, the governor of Liangzhou, and bid him farewell. Thinking of his old friend, their poetic gatherings and discussions over wine, returning home to escape political disputes, and focusing on farming and fishing in the Fen River, Di Gong found some solace. However, Li Yuanfang bluntly pointed out to him that he did not have the destiny to enjoy such peace.

Zeng Tai, a disciple of Di Gong, received an urgent report from the court stating that a massive earthquake was about to occur in the northwest, and he was instructed to prepare for it. Zeng Tai immediately organized disaster prevention measures and timely evacuated the city's residents. When the earthquake struck, there was still significant destruction with buildings collapsing. Zeng Tai was deeply worried about the 3000 armored soldiers escorting the military payroll in the desert. If anything happened to them, it would be catastrophic. When the rescue team found the convoy, all the soldiers were possessed, forming circles and dancing wildly without stopping. Anyone who stopped dancing would immediately die. The 30 million taels of silver they were escorting had vanished without a trace, as if swept away by a sandstorm. This shocking event left Zeng Tai speechless and trembling. In response to the situation, Zeng Tai took emergency measures: sealing off the borders, checking all merchants and travelers, and reporting to the court for further instructions. Meanwhile, rumors spread throughout the city, army, and government offices that the Black Robe Sect's great deity was responsible. To prevent unrest among the superstitious populace, Zeng Tai urged officials to control public opinion and maintain stability.

Di Gong arrived just in time. He criticized Zeng Tai for his lack of knowledge about the Black Robe Sect and quickly made accurate deductions about the case. A thrilling battle of investigation versus counter-investigation, control versus counter-control unfolded, leading to a climactic showdown in uncovering the "God of Death" and the "Hell Operation." Ultimately, the true Black Robe Great Deity revealed herself — she was none other than bandit leader Xiao Tao and Wei'er. Xiao Tao proudly explained the entire concept behind the "Hell Operation" and revealed the truth about the "God of Death" and the "Hell Operation," which was indeed chilling and ominous. However, Di Gong confidently smiled and narrated a "true story" of the Black Robe Great Deity, captivating even the bandit leaders.

Di Gong began recounting the origins of the Black Robe Sect, detailing every aspect of their plan and analyzing it meticulously. At this moment, a thunderous shout of battle echoed outside. Di Gong informed the bandits that it was General Feng Yang of Liangzhou leading his cavalry into the city to capture the Black Robe Sect rebels. Shocked, the bandits listened as Di Gong recounted his brilliant process of unraveling everything. When he revealed that Xiao Tao and Wei'er’s belief in the sect's success was merely part of his deception, Xiao Tao fainted in despair. But Wei'er held her steady because Di Gong's story was not yet finished. Di Gong then described the final battle, the deployment of forces, and the dramatic changes in the outcome, making it vivid and heart-stirring. As Xiao Tao and Wei'er realized their impending failure, Xiao Tao attempted to resist by threatening Di Gong with a sword. Her attempt was easily thwarted by Li Yuanfang. Zhang Huan and Li Lang then surrounded the yard, capturing Xiao Tao and Wei'er. Di Gong emphasized the value of life, explaining that only by respecting others' lives could one claim the right to live. A deity is compassionate and does not kill indiscriminately; the Black Robe Great Deity should understand this. Xiao Tao retorted that there was no fairness or compassion in this world, and only the victors had the right to judge right and wrong. Di Gong responded that this was precisely why she would never become a victor.

At Xiao Tao's final request, she and Wei'er, along with two other wealthy lawbreakers, committed suicide. Even in death, they clung to their determination to seize Ganliang in their next life. Di Gong sighed helplessly. Zeng Tai asked Di Gong why the secret passage in Cuilv Courtyard led directly to Lord Guiyi's residence but not to the underground network.
