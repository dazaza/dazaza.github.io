The collection and appreciation of antiques is an elegant hobby.

by fbfshtwk0423 on 2012-02-07 09:42:09

Some people believe that the antique circle is all about estimation and play, testing one's ability to identify authenticity. If you buy the wrong item, you just have to accept your misfortune. Some even cite historical examples of famous individuals who faked and sold counterfeit antiques, trying to justify the existence of forgery and fake selling in antique collection, making many people turn a blind eye to such behavior in the antique trade. However, this is not the case. The antique trade does not condone counterfeiting. Buying the wrong item is painful for anyone. This pain not only means financial loss for those pursuing unique collections, but it has also caused well-known collectors to lose their reputation, face humiliation, develop heartaches, and eventually succumb to depression. This pain has also extinguished the enthusiasm of countless eager enthusiasts as soon as they enter the field. As victims of counterfeit and fake selling, the antique collecting community detests any act of forgery and deception. If so, why haven't these acts of forgery and fake selling been effectively stopped in the antique industry? Besides the drive for profit that perpetuates such practices, another reason is consumer tolerance and indulgence. In the past, experts were ashamed when they made mistakes in purchasing goods. They blamed themselves for lacking expertise, kept quiet, and did not publicize the matter. They automatically gave up their right to pursue compensation. In today's society, China's 'Consumer Rights Protection Law' clearly stipulates that during the buying and selling process, if the operator provides false or dishonest statements, promises, or uses deceptive means causing economic losses to the buyer, the buyer has the right to claim compensation, and the antique trade is no exception. Therefore, for malicious deception, one should actively take action to claim compensation. On one hand, it protects one's legitimate rights from being infringed, and on the other hand, it contributes to purifying market behavior, making those who intentionally deceive others with dishonesty feel restrained and unwelcome. Imperial Workshop reminds buyers to obtain transaction receipts noting relevant commitment content after completing transactions. State-owned cultural relic stores and other operating units usually provide customers with unified invoices for ancient book and cultural relic sales. For private transactions, if the seller has made a commitment, it is prudent to obtain corresponding commitment evidence or transaction records for unforeseen needs. Collecting and appreciating antiques is an elegant form of entertainment, a tasteful way to play, a cultural way to play, a way to carry history and seek the old and cherish the past. Antique investment falls under the category of art investment. Zhuo Yi Xuan Hetian Jade indicates that the scope of antiques is very extensive, with numerous categories. From the perspective of material, there are wooden antiques, ceramic antiques, jade antiques, jewelry antiques, metal antiques, etc.; from the perspective of use value, there are classical furniture, writing rooms, utensils, grooming accessories, ancient coins, books and newspapers, cigarette labels and posters, etc. Antiques are like a vast market encompassing everything. There are many items, broad knowledge, and various ways to play. "Prosperous times for cultural relics, chaotic times for famine-stricken civilians." When the country prospers and progresses, and the common people live abundantly, there is a market for antiques. When the country is in decline and chaos, and the common people lack food and clothing, no one has the leisure to deal with antiques. As a commodity, the essence of antiques is concentrated in two points: one is its antiquity, giving antiques a sense of historical沧桑(cang1sang1), often making these things the best witnesses to the political, economic, cultural, and military aspects of a societal development period; the other point is that they possess culture and taste, having artistic qualities. It is precisely because of these two most distinct features of antiques as commodities that determine that specialized talents in a certain field of antiques can possibly win big in the complex and mixed antique market.
