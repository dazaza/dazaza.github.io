In the UK Immigration Law regarding student visas, you must be a genuine student and have sufficient funds to support your studies. Visa officers wield a mighty sword, determining the dreams and fates of countless international students. Any subjective judgment they make could potentially ruin a student's future.

Below are some possible reasons for visa rejection, reminding applicants to avoid these situations to ensure a smooth visa approval:

1. The UK embassy often rejects students on the grounds of "serious doubts about the authenticity of visa materials" due to:

a. Incomplete information leading to one-sided judgments by the visa officer.

b. Exaggerated information making the visa officer suspect deliberate embellishment for the visa application.

c. Untrue information lowering the credibility of the entire visa application.

d. Extremely high income without supporting documents such as personal tax statements, salary books, or salary cards, failing to demonstrate a reliable source of income.

2. Large amounts of money deposited in a short period without historical records like interest statements, leading the visa officer to believe the funds do not belong to the sponsor, resulting in rejection.

3. Subjective errors made by visa officers.
