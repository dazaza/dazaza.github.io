Many netizens, without hesitation: Since the OP requests a name, they'll help come up with one, but mostly as a joke. Many netizens think it should be named "Zhang Ziyi," and there are also many suggestions for "Octopus," along with others like "Zhang Xixi," "Zhang Naowan," "Zhang Lang," etc.

Netizens suggest naming it "Zhang Ziyi"

The exact identity of the person behind this "name solicitation show" and their motive remain unclear. The reporter attempted to contact the poster "Zhangya Wuzhua" yesterday, but no response was received by the time of publication.

"I surname X, just started a business in XX"

According to local media reports, my sales brothers in South China 1D, an office building with a giant canvas hanging on it is located at the intersection of Dong Street and Second Ring Road in Chengdu. This office building was developed by Guojia Real Estate. Relevant officials said they were also unaware of the origin of the canvas. Property management is currently discussing the matter with relevant law enforcement departments. The official also stated that the office building, named Guojia New Vision, has been almost completely sold out, and the developer would not engage in such hype at this stage.

The poster "Zhangya Wuzhua" delayed appearing, which sparked much curiosity among netizens. Netizen "Hulala Xiaopangpang" exclaimed: "Brother Entrepreneur, why don't you come out!"

Under the call of netizens, at 8:18 PM on May 24th, "Zhangya Wuzhua" reappeared, uploading another picture. Compared to the previous two, the distance and angle were slightly different.

When the post was initially made, there was no specific location mentioned, and even its authenticity was unknown, but it still sparked heated discussions and frenzied reposts among netizens.

Naming faction:

At 9:39 AM on May 25th, "Zhangya Wuzhua" reappeared again, posting two more pictures. The pictures showed that another giant canvas had been hung on the office building, responding to the first canvas. Above the canvas was the content posted by "Board Stool Chili Sister" at "2010-5-25 9:15": "Why did you choose to start a business in Chengdu?" The timestamp on the canvas was almost synchronized with the posting time.

Property Management: The building has been sold out

Some netizens believe that this post is another hype. Netizen "Wind Blows Leaves Cry" said: "OP, are you a real estate developer? Doing publicity for your building? You can think of any kind of cat trick!" Netizen "Lemeizi" also said: "This advertisement is huge~!!"

Curious faction:

This post also triggered a Photoshop storm similar to the "Little Fat Event." Many netizens posted photos of famous landmarks around the world, using Photoshop to hang giant canvases on them. Beijing's Bird's Nest, Shanghai's Oriental Pearl, Sydney's Opera House, Egypt's Pyramids, and even Mars were all affected. On the "canvases," the sentences were uniformly styled: "I surname X, just started a business in XX," causing much amusement.

The post only included two pictures, one long shot and one close-up, both showing a skyscraper with a giant canvas hanging from its top. In the center were several large red characters: "Everyone, help me pick a name for my daughter." Below, in slightly smaller black characters, it read: "I surname Zhang, just started a business in Chengdu." The canvas was designed to look like a forum post, with blue small letters above: "OP Zhangya Wuzhua posted on 2010-5-24, after drinking 7 cans of Wang Lao Ji, at 9:30." The reporter noticed that this netizen's posting time on Tianya Community was exactly "2010-05-24 09:29:35."

Our report (by reporter Li Liang): "Everyone, help me pick a name for my daughter." On May 24th, this post appeared on major online forums. The giant canvas hanging on the skyscraper only wrote this sentence, yet quickly attracted a frenzy of support from netizens, becoming one of the hottest posts on the internet recently.
