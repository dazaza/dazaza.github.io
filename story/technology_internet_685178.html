Stairs play a crucial role in villa (duplex house) design. After all, they connect different levels (perhaps better described as linking upper and lower sections).

There are mainly several types of stairs found in homes:

1. Straight Stairs: These are now rarely seen because they take up too much space. However, this design suits only large duplex houses. Such stairs should have smooth, rotating lines.

2. Curved Stairs: Some duplex houses still use this design. The stairs must not have sharp angles or be square-shaped, as that is a major feng shui taboo.

3. Folded Stairs: Commonly used in most duplex house designs. They must not bend excessively.

4. Spiral Stairs: A variation of folded stairs. Similar considerations apply as above.

Of course, there are many forms of stairs, but when it comes to materials, they are easier to count. For example, solid wood, stone slabs, tiles, steel plates, carpets, etc., can all be used for stair decoration.

The key lies in the design of the handrails. The quality of the handrail design is crucial in evaluating the overall design of the staircase. Based on my experience, indoor railing designs should avoid using mirror-polished stainless steel or other shiny metal surfaces. If you do wish to use stainless steel for your staircase design [http://www.china-kewen.com], the ideal material for the handrails would be forged steel. The next best option is cast iron, followed by wood, then porcelain. Stone ranks last. The most ideal handrail material is wood.

Many existing architectural designs already have set shapes, but many homeowners opt to remove and reinstall new ones. If you want to redesign, the ideal material is cement concrete, followed by steel structures, and finally wood. From this perspective, it's important to ensure that the stairs make as little noise as possible when walked upon. Another issue worth noting is that I've seen many temporary renovations where people must tread carefully while climbing the stairs, which detracts from the elegance expected in a duplex home.

Other issues to consider in stair decoration:

1. The slope of the stairs should not be too steep. This is not just for the consideration of the elderly and children, but also for ordinary adults.

2. Stairs are high-wear areas, so durable materials should be used.

3. The width of the stair railings should either be too small for a child's head to fit through or large enough for it to pass freely, avoiding potential hazards.

4. For stairs with gaps between steps, attention should be paid to how women in short skirts appear to prevent embarrassing situations.

5. The edges of the stair treads should be rounded to avoid foot injuries.

6. The number of steps should consider the height of each level and the total height. It's also important to consider the auspiciousness of the step count. Generally, [Life Prosperity Death Extinction] is the key phrase. The final step before reaching the landing should ideally correspond to [Life] or [Prosperity], avoiding [Death] and [Extinction]. Based on years of feng shui design experience, this has an 80% validation rate.

Because stairs themselves are segmented, if the [Qi] enters the house and encounters the stairs first, the horizontal cut lines of the stairs could either completely sever the [Qi] or disturb its flow, disrupting the energy field. When the energy field is disturbed, the overall environment of the house suffers. Therefore, having a staircase immediately visible upon entering the door may not be convenient, and unknowingly affects the homeowner's health. There are three main methods to address this:

1. Redirect the staircase so that it does not face the door directly. For example, designing the staircase in an arc shape to reverse its direction, facing away from the door.

2. Hide the staircase, preferably behind a wall, enclosed by two walls. This not only avoids the cutting of the energy field but also enhances the safety of the homeowner while using the stairs. The space under the stairs can be designed into a storage room or toilet.

3. Place a screen between the door and the staircase to allow the [Qi] to flow smoothly into the house.
