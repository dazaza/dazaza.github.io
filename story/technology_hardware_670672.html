Man Publicly Mourns Deceased Wife

Guangxi News - According to Nanguo Morning Post, on the bustling wharf nearby, placing portraits and "billboards," playing music, from August 25-26, Liao Daxiong, a citizen of Baise in Guangxi, consecutively mourned his deceased wife in this manner. This "high-profile mourning" behavior moved many netizens, while others expressed doubts.

Public Mourning by the River

Liao Daxiong, residing in Yicang Alley, Gonghe Street in Baise City, is 55 years old. On the afternoons of August 25th and 26th, for two consecutive days at 5 PM, he stood solemnly dressed near the large wharf on Jiangbin Road in Baise City with a serious and heavy expression.

To his right were boards titled "The Pain of Losing My Beloved Wife Also an Advertisement" and "One Year Anniversary of Losing My Beloved Wife," featuring two portraits of his deceased wife Li Pingfeng, as well as a board inscribed with the song title "I Love You, My Wife (Mom, I Love You)." These were arranged in a line. In front of these boards were several flower pots. On the ground, there were also two boxes containing flyers with the same content as that on the boards.

Liao Daxiong said he deeply loved his wife and daughter but regretfully, his wife passed away last year due to cancer, leaving him unable to shake off the shadow. The song was co-created by him and his daughter, Liao Yuanting. The two photos were taken when his deceased wife was 30 and 40 years old. Liao Daxiong explained that his actions aimed to express his deep怀念for his deceased wife and warn all couples worldwide: "Regardless of the storms, cherish each other and walk hand in hand."

Netizens Both Praise and Criticize

Regarding Liao Daxiong's actions, many netizens were moved and admired, saying "He is truly a role model for men," while others had different opinions.

"Among 100 people, 100 would think he's gone mad," some netizens commented, finding Liao's behavior "too unconventional." They believed Liao should keep his love for his wife buried in his heart or transform it into love for his daughter and friends, without needing such ostentation. Some netizens thought Liao did this merely to gain fame or "to solicit donations."

In an interview with reporters, Liao Daxiong admitted that relatives and neighbors didn't support his actions, saying, "My family thinks I'm too showy and not giving them face." His elderly mother-in-law also advised him, "Don't wave your wife's photo around everywhere."

According to Liao, a few months after his wife's death, he held a similar memorial event. He invited over 20 relatives and friends, but only two showed up. However, to his relief, his daughter understood and supported him. Later, in "One Year Anniversary of Losing My Beloved Wife," he added this passage: "I don't care what others say; I tightly embrace my wife, who has made me worry since our first love 36 years ago, in my heart, and I will never be lonely or desolate again."
