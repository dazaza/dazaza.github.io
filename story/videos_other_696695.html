Qian Weiren, a police officer in Shanghai - a first-class hero of cybersecurity technology on his sickbed

by xingfu127 on 2012-03-08 09:58:49

Xinhua News Agency, Shanghai, March 1 (reported by Luo Zhengguang) - Shanghai police officer Qian Weiren, currently bedridden, was recently awarded a first-class merit by the Municipal Public Security Bureau. From an ordinary policeman to a recipient of the second prize for national scientific and technological progress, Qian Weiren has been "obsessed" with studying the core technology of network security for many years, eventually falling ill due to overwork.

Qian Weiren, aged 55, is a police officer of the Cybersecurity Detachment of the Shanghai Municipal Public Security Bureau and a senior engineer. Graduating from a normal university with a degree in mathematics, he became "fascinated" when he first came into contact with cybersecurity technology. In the 1990s, when network technology research was still in its infancy, the police's network security work mainly involved killing viruses and managing internet cafes. One year, there was a case in Shanghai where a pager computer information system was destroyed. The suspect had planted a "logic bomb" in the program, causing the entire system to crash. Such a novel form of high-tech crime left frontline investigating officers at a loss, and the suspect was rather arrogant during the interrogation: "Even if I explain, you won't understand."

Refusing to be intimidated, Qian Weiren volunteered to take on the task. He took back the computer used in the crime and printed out a thick stack of source code that was even thicker than a book. He studied it day and night, ultimately finding the malicious code hidden within, which left the suspect amazed and humbled.

In 2001, the Shanghai Municipal Public Security Bureau established the Supervision Office for Public Information Network Security. To study the core technology for combating high-tech crimes, Qian Weiren took on the heavy responsibility and became "immersed" in his work. He would even jot down sudden ideas on napkins while eating in the cafeteria. Later, the technology he led in developing solved a large number of cases in actual operations and was promoted nationwide among public security organs, earning him the second prize for national scientific and technological progress.

Qian Weiren's "obsession" with work gradually deteriorated his health. A few years ago, during a physical examination, a shadow was found in his colon area, but burdened with important tasks, he never considered a follow-up check. In April 2009, during the preparation stage for the Shanghai World Expo, Qian Weiren was borrowed to the Expo Security Department to be responsible for internal network security work. Due to the shortage of personnel, as a senior engineer, Qian Weiren not only handled technical and managerial tasks but also sometimes patrolled the front lines. In the hot season, he often walked dozens of kilometers on foot, inspecting all network terminals throughout the park. To transplant a sonar monitoring system originally used in the ocean for use along the Huangpu River, he led a team through repeated tests. For three consecutive weeks, he worked until around 3 a.m. every day and got up early the next morning to continue working.

After successfully completing the Expo security task, Qian Weiren refused the suggestion to "rest more" and started new research. In May 2011, he again led a team to initially overcome a key core technology in network security. Considering the need to promote this technology nationwide as soon as possible, but the system was not yet fully stable, he spent the next five days continuously debugging, finally resolving the stability issues of the system. This technology was later demonstrated at a national meeting of public security bureau directors and received high praise.

However, the day after this meeting ended, Qian Weiren suddenly collapsed while preparing to go to work and was rushed to the hospital for emergency treatment, thus becoming bedridden ever since. In February this year, despite suffering immense pain from cancer, he insisted on being helped by his wife to rise from his sickbed and accepted the certificate of personal first-class merit awarded to him by the Shanghai Municipal Public Security Bureau.
