Teaching Insights for Children's English Education

by kayaedu on 2009-08-28 16:59:22

(Kaya Education www.kakayaya.com)

"Teaching has methods, but no fixed method." Since no single teaching method can suit all foreign language teaching, each teacher needs to adapt their actual teaching situation and draw on the essence of various foreign language teaching schools to creatively use strong, practical teaching methods. Through teaching practice and summarization of teaching experience, I will introduce several commonly used methods in foreign language teaching as follows:

1. Imagery Intuitive Method

Using vivid language or with the help of physical objects, pictures, models, specimens, slides and other visual aids, fully mobilize students' multiple senses to participate, so that they learn knowledge, develop thinking, and cultivate abilities in a visible, audible, and tangible teaching process. For example, by using charts, simple drawings, or slides to create a language situation to teach 3A Let's go to the park, we can organically link the four pictures in part B into an interesting story. The teacher can first organize the students to practice vocabulary and sentence patterns based on each picture's situation, conduct question-and-answer and retelling exercises, then string together the four situations in English for comprehensive practice. Students can understand the content of the text fully without opening the textbook, only in the context provided by the pictures, under the teacher's guidance and leadership. They can master vocabulary and sentence patterns, narrate the entire story fluently, and engage in oral communication, making the students feel close, leave a deep impression, and achieve good results.

2. Joyful Game Method

Games are children's paradise. Playing games can satisfy their love for movement and play, making attention not only last longer and more stable, but also at a higher level of intensity. Games are the most beloved activity by children and also a good form of English classroom teaching. In the game, teachers combine the textbook content to play English games in class, use game methods to teach and consolidate knowledge, embed knowledge in games, such as practicing grammar structures and vocabulary in games, applying sentence patterns, etc. Students use English to explain the learned words and phrases; guide students to turn the learned key sentence patterns and interesting dialogues into chants or poems. Music and art can also be integrated into English teaching in the form of games. Generally speaking, there are many types of games. In primary school English classrooms alone, there can be listening, speaking, reading, writing, phonetics, intonation, vocabulary, grammar, and other major types of game training. As long as the teacher uses them appropriately, it can create a healthy, light-hearted atmosphere suitable for primary school students' physical and mental well-being, effectively prevent fatigue and students from developing aversion to learning, fully mobilize students' enthusiasm, and improve teaching effectiveness.

3. Situational Performance Method

Based on the content of the textbook, create a lively and interesting situation through role-playing performances, allowing students to be visually infected, which can deepen students' understanding of the textbook and also aligns with children's playful and active psychological characteristics. Performances can either be role-based dialogue performances or involve creating simple props for stage performances. For example, after studying Shopping and Asking the way in Book 4 of the Yilin Edition, students can arrange store models, collect various physical items, police flags, and hats as simple props, and perform on stage in groups. This method allows both actors, listeners, and viewers to enter their roles fully, consolidating the learned knowledge during performances. Only when language is closely combined with its context can students directly associate it with the expressed situation.

Students learn, use, understand, and communicate in situational performances. Additionally, in our English teaching, we can also adopt methods such as joyful teaching, interest-based teaching, activity-based teaching, question-and-answer method, picture method, etc. All these methods have their own strengths and varied forms, but the overall approach and principles are consistent: targeting the age, psychological and physiological characteristics of primary school students, intentionally introducing or creating certain emotional colors in the teaching process, focusing on imagery, fun, joy, and operability, paying attention to emotional effects, fully mobilizing students' enthusiasm, stimulating students' interest in learning English, enabling students to master English in a relaxed and pleasant environment, and learn how to use English.
