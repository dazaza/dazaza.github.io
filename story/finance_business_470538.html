TCL Group's 2.1 billion shares were released from restrictions yesterday, and the shareholders who participated in the fixed increase cashed out through block trades.

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 10:12:59

⊙Reporters Xiaoli ○Editors Quan Zeyuan

Yesterday was the first trading day for the release of 2.125 billion shares out of TCL Group's total of 2.284 billion newly issued shares in the market. Unexpectedly, despite the early morning dip in the overall market trend, TCL Group only opened 1.5% lower and its stock price fluctuated only between 2.63 yuan and 2.68 yuan per share throughout the day, with a trading volume of 155 million shares and a turnover rate of 2.77%. The trading volume increased compared to the previous day.

According to the定向increased issuance price one year ago at 3.46 yuan, after a 10-for-10 bonus issue, the cost of the increased issuance shares was only 1.73 yuan, still leaving considerable profits; this "calm" is indeed surprising.

However, as expected, TCL Group appeared in the block trades yesterday. According to Shenzhen Stock Exchange block trade data, yesterday, CITIC Securities Beijing Majiabao West Road branch sold two lots of 65 million shares and 15 million shares respectively, which were likely from one shareholder, while the buyer was Shenwan Hongyuan Wuhan Zhongshan Road branch and Harbin Zhongshan Road branch. The transaction price was 2.52 yuan per share, only 4.5% lower than the closing price yesterday.

Since among TCL Group's top 10 shareholders, only one fund and the company's major shareholder hold more than 80 million shares, it is clear that these two transactions must have come from shareholders who participated in the directional increased issuance.

According to the announcement of the listing of the increased issuance shares of TCL Group, the registered addresses of Xi Bu Jian Yuan Holding Co., Ltd. and Mengke Investment Holding Co., Ltd., each holding 320 million shares (after bonus issue, same below), are in Chaoyang District and Huairou District of Beijing, so logically, these two shareholders are the most likely to sell.

In addition to geographical considerations, the reporter found that although other shareholders holding the released shares all had the possibility to reduce their holdings, they seemed to have "reasons not to rush to reduce."

Data shows that Shenzhen Ping An Innovation Capital Investment Co., Ltd., holding 483 million increased issuance shares of TCL Group, is a company fully owned by Ping An Trust Investment Corporation Limited, and is the capital investment company under the Ping An Group. It has participated in the increased issuance of many listed companies and has significant influence and capital operation capabilities in the industry.

The controlling shareholder of Shenzhen Yuanzhi Investment Co., Ltd., holding 289 million increased issuance shares of TCL Group, is Shenzhen Investment Holdings Co., Ltd., and the actual controller is the State-owned Assets Supervision and Administration Commission of the Shenzhen Municipal People's Government; Guangdong Hengjian Investment Holding Co., Ltd., also holding 289 million increased issuance shares of TCL Group, was approved by the Guangdong Provincial Government, and the SASAC of Guangdong Province fulfills the duties of the contributor. It is a provincial financing platform in Guangdong, a provincial state-owned capital operation platform, an investment development platform, and an asset disposal platform, and serves as the center for implementing property rights diversification reform and business transformation of Guangdong Provincial Enterprises.

It is worth noting that Shanghai Rongsheng Real Estate Investment Advisory Co., Ltd., which obtained 173 million increased issuance shares (after bonus issue) of TCL Group, appears to be facing tight financial conditions. After spending 300 million yuan to subscribe to the increased issuance shares of TCL Group, the company pledged the relevant shares to a trust company as early as August 2010, indirectly cashing out through the "Zhongrong - TCL Phase III Equity Income Rights Investment Collective Fund Trust Plan." Data shows that the fundraising period was from August 9, 2010, to August 30, 2010, with a trust plan term of 1 year and an expected annualized return of 8.5%. Clearly, it completed a "leveraging" of its investment through the trust pledge.

Meanwhile, on August 2, 2011, TCL Group announced that the progress of the Houxing Photoelectric 8.5th generation LCD panel project in this increased issuance project was proceeding smoothly; on July 8, it forecasted a net profit growth of about 190% to 240% for the first half of 2011; earlier, it also announced winning the bid for the promotion project of high-efficiency lighting products in 2011 and being selected as one of the national technology innovation demonstration enterprises in 2011.

"All these have played a certain role in stabilizing TCL Group's stock price. However, calmness on the first day does not necessarily mean it will always be this calm. With such a low turnover rate currently, if a large number of restricted shares are sold, it would be detrimental to all shareholders," said a private equity yesterday, "Further observation is still needed."
