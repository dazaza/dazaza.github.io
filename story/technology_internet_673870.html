Mr. Literature bowed to Du Jingxuan and Qing Li'ruo, then had each disciple handed a piece of paper with a line of poetry on it. "Everyone continues the poem from the given line." This was what they called a baseline test! The line that fell into Qing Li'ruo's hands was: "Since ancient times, who can escape death?" Qing Li'ruo was quite troubled. She recognized the characters, but writing poems or couplets were obviously not her forte. After racking her brains and coming up with nothing, she saw everyone else writing furiously, including Du Jingxuan who wrote confidently. With gritted teeth, she managed to come up with a line. Halfway through writing, she couldn't think of the next line and could only put down her pen and daydream.

She noticed a disciple sitting there gazing out the window, seemingly daydreaming as well. He looked to be around eleven or twelve years old, handsome like jade, already exuding an air of charm beyond his years, truly a passionate young man with a face like peach blossoms. Qing Li'ruo chuckled, thinking this must be another child struggling like herself. Taking advantage of Mr. Literature's inattention, she sneaked over to bond with the fellow struggler.

However, upon reaching him, she saw that the young man had already filled a page with his writing. His handwriting was as elegant as his person, flowing with charm yet subtly sharp, full of grandeur.

Qing Li'ruo couldn't help but exclaim, "Good calligraphy!"

This drew everyone's attention, all eyes on Qing Li'ruo. The teacher frowned, while Du Jingxuan smirked, "Sister Qing says good poetry. Why don't you recite it so we can all appreciate it?"

"Evil senior brother," Qing Li'ruo muttered. She had said good handwriting, he deliberately twisted it to good poetry. She grinned slyly, "Or... maybe not..."

Du Jingxuan had already seen Qing Li'ruo's eyes light up when she read the poem, surely it was interesting. So he continued to smile, "Sister Qing, don't be modest. Teacher, isn't that right?"

Since Du Jingxuan had spoken, the teacher could only say, "What Senior Du says is true..."

Qing Li'ruo looked at the young man, who smiled at her with his charming eyes. Seeing that he didn't mind, she gritted her teeth. If you insist, here goes:

"Midnight songs fill the clear moonlit tower, ten thousand miles of war have left men behind. Clouds and rains are unforgettable, I and my master take off our battle robes. A moment of spring night is worth a thousand pieces of gold, knowing this matter requires personal action."

It was completely the deep affection between a passionate emperor and a glorious general!

The teacher was so angry that his face changed colors, very vividly. Steam rose from his head, "You... you... you! Don't think just because you're a prince you can do as you please!"

The young man innocently said, "I have a pseudonym as a realistic poet. Is being realistic wrong? Isn't adhering to realism our Tian Heng school motto?"

The disciples exchanged glances, considering the young man's identity, feeling even more awkward. However, some bold disciples began to exchange looks, gossip flying everywhere.

One look, "Rumor has it that the emperor has a penchant for same-sex relationships. It seems it's true!"

Another look, "Oh! Even a homosexual can have sons. I wonder if it's because someone wore a green hat?"

Another look, "No, no! The emperor can both give and receive, truly a remarkable talent!"

One look, "I wonder if that general is our powerful General. I've seen his figure before, indeed impressive!"

"Cough cough!" The teacher was speechless, furious, but could only suffer in silence. Pointing at the young man, "You, copy the 'Tao Te Ching' three hundred times. Everyone must submit an essay on their reflections after reading the 'Tao Te Ching'. That's today's homework." There were complaints all around. Constant reflection essays or study summaries really made one want to end it all!

To shift everyone's attention, the teacher suggested, "Senior Du, why don't you recite your masterpiece?"

Du Jingxuan did not hesitate, "Fulfilling the king's ambitions, earning a bad reputation in the brothels. Shockingly sitting up in mortal illness, climbing the wall under the cover of night."

It was completely about a wolfish man who abandoned his beloved courtesan for the king, then regretfully returned to his old self.

The teacher spat blood, barely holding on to his last breath, looking at Qing Li'ruo with hope. He thought that since Qing was a woman and a disciple of the Immortal Lord, she would surely set an example for the students, "Let's see Sister Qing's masterpiece."

Qing Li'ruo quickly declined, "Teacher, you flatter me. My humble work doesn't deserve such high praise, let's not."

The teacher saw that indeed Sister Qing was modest and polite, feeling quite pleased. He bowed, "Sister Qing, don't be too modest. As a disciple of the Immortal Lord, you must be exceptionally talented. Let us open our eyes to your brilliance."
