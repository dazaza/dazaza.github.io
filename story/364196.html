Chuan Dong is now a famous poor mountainous area.

by yangli on 2010-09-10 15:09:23

Chuan Dong is now a well-known impoverished mountainous area. Back then, this poor mountainous region was indeed even poorer than it is today. Most of the people wore patched-up clothes, lived in thatched-roof houses, and children ran around naked without access to schooling. Farmers worked the land with their faces to the yellow earth and their backs to the sky, and many young women didn't even have pants to wear, walking around rather casually. However, when it came to having enough food to eat, things weren't as dire as some TV dramas and novels portray. At that time, although the people in this impoverished mountainous area didn't eat well and could only enjoy meat during the Spring Festival, making eating meat a rare treat referred to as "da ya ji" (feast), it was actually quite easy to have enough to eat because the land here produced crops all year round. Corn, barley, wheat, sorghum, various beans, sweet potatoes, potatoes, and vegetables – in short, the land here was always producing edible items that could fill one's stomach. Even if there was a disaster that caused a loss in one season, other seasons would still yield crops without fail. Thus, there is a local saying: "A drought can starve lazy people," meaning that as long as one works hard, they won't starve even during a drought. Of course, if powerful political forces were to cause trouble, that would be a different matter.

In the beginning, everything was difficult. But after completing a few business deals, my master had capital, confidence, and became more daring. Soon, he took over a shop, called back his assistants, and announced the establishment of Jian Fu Yong in the city. During this time, my father was sent by his teacher on several trips to Hunan, following the addresses left behind to find the guest from Jiangnan. He went to thank him for his kindness but was actually establishing a commercial alliance. They started by trading familiar grains and soon moved on to oils. During its peak period, they controlled the export of tung oil and rapeseed oil from Youyang, Xiushan, Qianjiang, Wulong, and Pengshui counties (referring to transporting goods out of the Three Gorges). My father became one of the top two richest capitalists in Fuling. He continued to buy shops, build mansions, and take on many disciples. In no time, he had over ten apprentices. Among all his disciples, he trusted and favored two the most: his eldest disciple Pan Zuoqing, who earned his trust through blood ties and loyalty, and my father. To show his trust and affection, in the large business house of Jian Fu Yong, my father's salary was very high, reaching thirty silver dollars per month, the same amount as the master’s own nephew. The saying "Misfortune may be an actual blessing" was fully realized at this point. Later, I had the good fortune to read several copies of "Misfortune may be an actual blessing," gaining a deeper understanding of it.
