Keep loving

by llvf9397 on 2009-12-01 10:50:10

Chinese ethnic music, nowadays probably few people listen to it, and there are even fewer who love to listen. Following this trend, in the future, fans of Chinese ethnic music will be hard to find in China and even rarer in the world.

Beloved China, the hateful Chinese nation, composed of fifty-six or more ethnic groups, with a vast territory and a long history, has forged the characteristics of Chinese ethnic music being extensive, simple, and good. However, the actual situation is not as ideal. Or we can say that in today's reform and opening up, Chinese ethnic music is quietly changing.

At the beginning of the school sports meeting in November, I went to Nanjing. Standing on the bus, when night fell, the radio played music that was sometimes slow and sometimes uplifting. I was somewhat surprised and then delighted because I recognized the sound of the hulusi (a traditional Chinese instrument). Its beautiful melody took me to Yunnan, a beautiful and mysterious place. In the past, I heard "Butterfly Lovers" at Xinhua Bookstore, and now only during each year's Spring Festival Gala or in elective courses can one hear this beautiful, rare, and sacred ethnic music. No matter when you walk on the streets of Lianyungang, you hear popular, rock, and jazz music all around, stimulating your eardrums. Of course, this situation is not just in Lianyungang; many small towns abroad are like this too. Some even play a few tracks of Eastern sounds, add English lyrics, and think they are sophisticated and elegant, unaware that truly elegant and refined music is right here in China, right by their side.

After the reform and opening up, social development has accelerated, and the speed of materialization of spirit is unstoppable. However, despite the simplicity and authenticity, people still yearn for the nourishment of official music.

Ethnic music is the cultural heritage of a nation. Chinese ethnic music has a long history, and many classic pieces have been passed down through thousands of years without decline. Folk songs and ethnic instruments from various regions, famous or not, all kinds of forms of ethnic music have flowed through the long river of history, becoming the pride of the Chinese nation. However, some people say that folk music is too rustic. This is because they lack understanding of China's history or are simply ignorant. Popular music and songs are like a strong wind, once blown away, they disappear like a river flowing far away never to return, while folk music remains vibrant for decades or even hundreds of years.

Why can't our classic folk music attract modern young people? If Song Zuying and Jolin Tsai held concerts at the same time, regardless of who has more fans, among the young and very young fans, there would definitely be more fans of Jolin Tsai. Why?

Folk music lacks the hysteria and decadent colors of popular music. The essence contained in folk music cannot be clearly understood by simple life experiences. Popular music flows so fast that its newer speed can almost compete with the development speed of computers, adapting to the trendy style of modern youth. There is also some media hype. Of course, this is just a large part of the reasons, and it is quite difficult to change this current situation.

Finally, I want to mention a special person.

That evening, while riding my bike past the repair stand near the water room, I unexpectedly heard this melodious and familiar folk music. Although I still don't know the exact name of the piece, I can confirm that it is a piece of ethnic music. This sound made me feel both close and delighted, making me admire the bike repairman immensely. It seems that our Huaihai Agricultural College does indeed produce capable seniors, even those who repair bikes are accomplished individuals!

A three-foot layer of charcoal freezes due to one night's cold. The current state of ethnic music wasn't formed overnight, nor can it be completely changed overnight. It requires the joint efforts of our entire nation. All I can do is continue to love, keep loving, and try to lead the people around me to love our ethnic music. Hopefully, in the near future, across the vast land of China, the songs flying high will belong to the Chinese nation itself.

People will forever support and love Chinese ethnic music!
