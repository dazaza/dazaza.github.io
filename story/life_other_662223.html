What can I do to stop being so vulnerable

by mimi456 on 2012-02-10 00:09:06

How can I stop being so fragile?

Today, after an exam ended, I didn't feel like studying, so I watched a movie called "My Life Without Me". Since it was a comedy, I found it very heartwarming. But when Alex and Wind argued, and Alex sat alone in the cold corner of her house hugging her shoulders, I cried. Watching her desolate scene, my tears naturally fell. I don't know if it was because of Charlene Ng's good acting or because I was too sentimental. Was I sympathizing with the loneliness and sadness of the heroine, or was I sympathizing with myself... I don't know. I always watch my favorite movies again, and when I returned to this scene, I still allowed myself to cry again. There were indescribable feelings, too many, both good and bad... Of course, it was completely because of the plot that I shed tears.

The movie ended, and I logged onto the campus network, where I saw a status update - it was good news, and I should have congratulated the owner on their success... Then I immediately checked my email inbox. The first thing I saw was 2 unread emails. I was a bit excited, but then I realized they both belonged to the "spam" category. I knew they were junk emails sent by advertisers... But at that moment, I cried again, not because of the plot, but for myself this time. I wanted to cry my heart out, but I couldn't. My mood plummeted again. This time, I was really hurt. I finally accepted my own fragility. My heart was so painful, so painful that I couldn't breathe...

I finally didn't know what to do. I wanted to take out my anger on something, throw things, or curse people, but I didn't do any of those. At this moment, my heart was still suffocating, unable to breathe. I've become truly weak. Every time I tell myself to be strong in the face of setbacks, after the first failure, I didn't care and continued moving forward; after the second one, my life was too chaotic, so I missed many opportunities to experience failure, thus I still didn't care; this time, I couldn't escape it anymore. I don't want to comfort myself with empty words anymore. I failed, and that's it. No one will sympathize with me, and I don't want that kind of sympathy either. Because my heart is bleeding, and other people's sympathy is like salt sprinkled on my wound. Although it makes me feel my own existence, it hurts more, much more...

Am I growing up? I finally feel the price of growing up. This time, I began to doubt myself. I really thought I was excellent, and I really thought I wouldn't fail. But what stands before me, engraved in my heart, are only ten words: "You failed, you are nothing." I want to cry out loud, but roommates have come back from the study room, so I can't.

I deleted the only two Weibo friends I had, and my mood was very complicated. Actually, I hope someone would sympathize with me, but I couldn't let them... So I decided to change my profile picture, delete familiar friends, and immerse myself completely in a strange online world, venting freely. Let my tears turn into words to mourn my sorrow.
