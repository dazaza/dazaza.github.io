WeChat's luxury purchasing agent behind the friends circle: selling "counterfeit goods" in broad daylight

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 20:09:34

WeChat's luxury goods proxy purchase: Circle marketing openly selling "fake goods"

Zhang Hua, 30 years old, starts her day like this: waking up, grabbing the phone by her bedside, and browsing WeChat or Weibo. The update speed of her own WeChat "friend circle" has increased much more than usual, mostly with pictures of famous brand handbags and clothes. Reporters randomly interviewed several mobile phone users and found that most people's WeChat "friend circles" had someone doing such WeChat business, mainly focusing on international brands like LV, Chanel, Cartier, etc., they call themselves "luxury goods proxy purchasers".

Is this a new e-commerce "friend sales model"? Or a hidden "fake goods A goods circle"? What is the process behind "luxury goods" proxy purchases? This reporter conducted an investigation.

The reporter inputted the two words "proxy purchase" in the WeChat search bar, and a series of public marketing accounts appeared, from red wine, glasses to clothing, bags, rich in variety. The reporter saw that these WeChat stores were mainly focused on luxury goods proxy purchases, filled with various international branded clothing, handbags, watches.

A "Acquaintance Business" monthly sales exceed ten thousand yuan

A month ago, Zhang Hua's understanding of "e-commerce" was only those shopping websites she often visited. Now, in front of the reporter, Zhang Hua said proudly, "After working for a while, once I have a stable customer base, I will strive to open a physical store," while skillfully opening her phone and sending some product pictures to her WeChat "friend circle".

Zhang Hua is an employee of a public institution in Taiyuan. During the National Day holiday, she learned about the new business of "luxury goods proxy purchase" at a gathering with friends. A friend recommended her to do it too, but she refused, "At first, I didn't understand it at all. I thought I didn't know much about luxury goods, and I didn't have enough capital." But her friend dispelled her doubts in just a few minutes, "You don't need a store, you don't need inventory, you don't even need startup funds." It turns out that this is not the traditional way of opening a store, buying goods, and then selling them, but rather "opening a store" in WeChat.

Zhang Hua introduced that she once thought about opening an online store, but she didn't know what to sell, and secondly, opening an online store required Alipay real-name certification, publishing more than 10 items, passing the store-opening exam, building and beautifying the store page, purchasing traffic to upload pictures, answering purchase inquiries online every day, which made her hesitant. Her friend introduced that opening a WeChat store only required registering an account, and the recommended business was "luxury goods proxy purchase": joining a few QQ groups and WeChats of "manufacturers", and then forwarding the pictures of "luxury goods" sent by the "manufacturer" to her own friend circle every day.

"They are all big-brand luxury goods, I don't know the price or details," Zhang Hua said. Whenever a WeChat friend sees her posted pictures and asks about the goods, she would say "send picture for inquiry", and then forward the picture to the "manufacturer", who would quote the price and explain the issues. She would add some money based on the quoted price and then report it to the consulting friend, "Actually, I'm just doing a forwarding platform. After the buyer pays me, I let the manufacturer ship the goods. I don't need warehousing costs, logistics costs, or even procurement costs. The difference between the selling price and the factory price is pure profit."

Zhang Hua told the reporter that in WeChat business, payment is received before delivery, and it's all among acquaintances. Generally, third-party payment platforms like Alipay are not needed; payments are directly transferred to one's bank account. In Zhang Hua's eyes, WeChat proxy purchase marketing is a new marketing method. Compared to traditional marketing and Taobao-style marketing, both parties in WeChat proxy purchases are friends on WeChat, and possibly also friends in real life. There is a sense of connection in the products of WeChat proxy purchases, relying on friendship. She told the reporter that her sales in the first month had already exceeded ten thousand yuan.

B Circle Marketing, Openly Selling "Fake Goods"

The reporter inputted the two words "proxy purchase" in the WeChat search bar, and a series of public marketing accounts appeared, from red wine, glasses to clothing, bags, rich in variety. The reporter saw that these WeChat stores were mainly focused on luxury goods proxy purchases, filled with various international branded clothing, handbags, watches. The reporter found one of these in Taiyuan city, "famous watch proxy purchase". After applying to be added as his friend as a buyer, the reporter saw his WeChat "friend circle", updating at a speed of about 10 posts per day, each post being pictures of big-brand products, with famous-brand watches being the majority: Breguet, Rolex, Vacheron Constantin, IWC...

The reporter asked about the price of one of the Rolex watches via WeChat, and the "famous watch proxy purchase" replied immediately: "There are three color options, priced at 2800 yuan." The reporter asked about the source of the watch, and the "famous watch proxy purchase" replied: "The watches are brought back from Hong Kong and Macau, and come with invoices and complete packaging." When the reporter took the picture of this watch to the "Rolex boutique" in a high-end mall in Taiyuan city, he saw that the same watch was priced at 54,000 yuan. With such a huge price difference, what was the secret?

The reporter proposed to the "famous watch proxy purchase" whether he could see the goods in person and select a watch style. After repeated communication, the "famous watch proxy purchase" finally agreed to let the reporter see the goods, but did not disclose the location, only letting the reporter contact him via WeChat. One day in late October, under the guidance of the "famous watch proxy purchase's" WeChat voice message, the reporter found a residential building near the Taiyuan train station. After multiple confirmations of the reporter's buyer identity via WeChat, the seller "famous brand proxy purchase" directed the reporter to a room.

The room was not large, filled with various packaging boxes. The seller was a man around 30 years old, who skillfully took out a box. Upon opening it, the reporter saw the previously viewed "Rolex watch", packaged in a full wood box, with invoices, warranty cards, and manuals all present. The reporter asked why the same watch had such a huge price difference compared to the market price, "This is A goods," the seller frankly told the reporter. "So-called A goods are fake goods, high-quality imitations among fake goods."

He told the reporter that WeChat's "luxury goods proxy purchase" was almost 100% "A goods", with the only difference being the high-end and low-end within "A goods." Unlike Zhang Hua's "luxury goods proxy purchase" which only forwarded pictures, he had inventory, "Friends can come to pick out goods, making the shipping speed faster." He said the reason was because he had been doing it for a long time and already had a stable sales circle, generally handling over ten orders a week without any problems.

The "famous watch proxy purchase" had many insights into doing the business of fake brand-name watches via WeChat, "Doing A goods in physical stores carries too much risk, if caught, one would be fined to bankruptcy; on Taobao, a common method is to blur the LOGO in the pictures, calling Chanel 'Little Xiang' and Prada 'P Family', but there is supervision on Taobao too, making it hard to do business; moreover, prices on Taobao are hard to raise, and using Alipay poses risks like returns and bad reviews," he told the reporter, on WeChat, since the content is only visible to the friend circle, adding friends can be assessed slightly, for example, flipping through their albums, the possibility of being investigated by the industrial and commercial department is very small.

"How can others believe you when a watch that sells for tens of thousands or even hundreds of thousands outside is sold here for two or three thousand yuan?" the reporter asked him. The "famous watch proxy purchase" answered, "Most of those who buy know this is A goods, it's impossible to buy genuine watches at such a low price, everyone knows the truth but buys anyway, but as long as the service is good, there have hardly been any disputes with customers." He indicated that the watches he sold came with a two-year warranty, and customers could even place custom orders, sending pictures from magazines or websites to him, which he would forward to the "manufacturer." Due to good management, introductions within the WeChat friend circle led to many new customers being brought in by friends introducing other friends.

C Providing "original factory packaging, Hong Kong invoice"

Through Zhang Hua, the reporter obtained the contact information of the so-called "manufacturer" she mentioned, joining a WeChat ID named "Luxury Goods Manufacturer Wholesale." Under the guise of being new to the business and wanting to do luxury goods proxy purchases, the reporter obtained the phone number of the head of this manufacturer, Mr. Dai, which showed as a Guangzhou area code.

After several phone communications, Mr. Dai requested the reporter to call again after 11 PM for discussion. At 11 PM, the reporter dialed Mr. Dai's phone. Regarding the reporter's inquiries, Mr. Dai was very cautious, only telling the reporter that one could forward pictures daily like Zhang Hua, and anyone inquiring about prices could be redirected to him. The reporter asked if a complete goods list could be provided so that the reporter could post items according to the preferences of their friend circle. Mr. Dai said that initially, they wouldn't provide a quotation list, but if one could reach over 50 orders a month, it could be considered.

When the reporter asked Mr. Dai about any tricks for doing "luxury goods proxy purchases," he told the reporter that there were two approaches: either having a large circle oneself, with extensive connections. For example, being a TV station editor, stylist, or staff member of a well-known mall, where such backgrounds gain great trust, and posting some commodities would make people believe they are genuine, "able to command higher prices. He mentioned a stylist in Beijing who used his identity to post luxury handbags on WeChat, attracting many buyers who believed they were real, earning a substantial amount."

In addition to personal connections, Mr. Dai revealed that one could take advantage of WeChat's feature of not requiring identity verification for "promotion marketing": "Find photos of fashionable, trendy boys and girls, claiming to be overseas students or flight attendants, then post commodities on WeChat. Besides the product catalog, one can also regularly post pictures of express delivery slips, customs, and airports to create the impression that one is genuinely engaged in proxy purchases." This model suits adding strangers, such as going to large malls or other public places, opening WeChat and searching for nearby people. "If you're attractive, definitely a crowd will add you, imagine adding 10 people in one place, within a week, you'll easily add 1000 people. When posting, attach an invoice, write 'genuine product from the boutique, supports inspection,' making people trust more."

Mr. Dai told the reporter that although the sources they provided were all counterfeit, they were divided into several levels: "1:1, original version, genuine product," the highest level almost indistinguishable from the real thing. Subsequently, he sent over several pictures, indeed showing invoices and exquisite outer packaging. The reporter asked if customers taking these invoices and packaging to the boutique for inspection could really "pass the test?" Mr. Dai said, "This is a promotional strategy." The invoice provided by Mr. Dai was a machine-printed invoice, bearing the name of a "Hong Kong jewelry merchant," with the invoice amount close to the market price of the goods. Mr. Dai told the reporter, "Such invoices cannot be used for reimbursement, mainly for convenience in gift-giving or making customers more convinced." The reporter called the service phone number left on the invoice, finding it unable to connect.

In addition to promotion strategies, Mr. Dai "emphasized" reminding the reporter, "Whatever happens, ensure advance payment, and no returns or exchanges." Mr. Dai said that among those doing well in his network, at most there might be one or two "return slots" per month, generally absolutely no returns, "requiring advance payment before shipping is for this purpose."

D Zero Complaints Behind the "Regulatory Dilemma"

Tang Hong, the responsible person of a certain international brand bag at Taiyuan's high-end commercial brand "Tianmei New World," talked about how indeed there were customers bringing "proxy purchased" branded bags to their store for inspection, but they generally did not provide authentication services. "Our insiders can tell the difference between genuine and fake, details are crucial means of identification, but we won't explicitly state the results," Tang Hong expressed, stating that this was the legal requirement of the brand, to avoid unnecessary disputes. He estimated that more than half were obviously counterfeit replicas.

Luo Yi, deputy director of the Taiyuan City Administration for Industry and Commerce 12315 Consumer Complaint Reporting Center, pointed out in an interview with the reporter regarding this matter, that they had been paying attention to the "luxury goods proxy purchase" on WeChat, but as of now, the Taiyuan City 12315 Consumer Complaint Reporting Center had not received a single complaint about "WeChat proxy purchase." Luo Yi analyzed that on one hand, it was because WeChat is a social networking tool based on friendships, both sellers and buyers are friends, even if there are some disputes, they are mostly resolved privately and not publicly. On the other hand, this kind of "proxy purchase" follows more of an unwritten rule of "knowing it's fake yet buying it," even if the product has flaws, consumers would not go through the complaint channels. When discussing how to manage such "proxy purchases," Luo Yi indicated that the sellers involved in proxy purchases were suspected of infringing on the intellectual property rights of brands and illegal operations. If there were warehouses for the goods, the administration had the obligation to investigate, but if it was merely forwarding pictures on WeChat, it would be difficult to define this behavior. More effective regulation should start from the production source.
