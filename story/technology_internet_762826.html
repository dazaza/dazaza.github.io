This is an era of great change, an interesting era.

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 17:29:54

These are the notes taken by Brother Feng while attending a class at an international top business school. This is an era of great change, an interesting era.

One

The PRADA flagship store in New York. Each piece of clothing has an RFID code. Whenever a customer picks up a PRADA item and takes it into the fitting room, the RFID is automatically recognized, and the screen in the fitting room will automatically play a video of the model walking down the runway wearing that piece of clothing. Once people see the model, they subconsciously beautify themselves.

At the same time, the data is transmitted to PRADA headquarters. The data for each piece of clothing - which city, which flagship store, what time it was taken into the fitting room, and how long it stayed there - is all stored and analyzed. If a piece of clothing has very low sales, the usual practice would be to eliminate it directly. However, if the RFID data shows that this piece of clothing, although having low sales, is frequently taken into the fitting room, then it can explain some issues. Perhaps the fate of this piece of clothing would be completely different.

Is it reasonable and unexpected that luxury brands use cutting-edge technology?

Two

There is a restaurant in Thailand that can accommodate 5,000 people dining simultaneously. It's quite large. Its menu is very similar to Beijing's Yellow Pages, quite thick, with just three categories under Sichuan cuisine in Chinese dishes. If you were in a small restaurant and ordered Mapo Tofu and Twice-Cooked Pork, these two dishes would almost certainly be served at the same time, and you wouldn't be surprised. But in this Thai restaurant, if you order Japanese cuisine and Mapo Tofu, they are also almost simultaneously served, and the flavors are quite authentic. You couldn't help but be amazed.

How does it achieve this?

In the kitchen. There are many chefs from different countries. In front of each chef is a display screen that tells the chef which dish to prepare now. The Japanese cuisine and Mapo Tofu you eat are made by two chefs who may be far apart and perhaps don't even know each other.

To ensure both dishes are served simultaneously, the system automatically calculates: if the Japanese dish requires 1 hour and Mapo Tofu requires 10 minutes, the system will notify the Japanese chef to start immediately and notify the Chinese chef to start after 50 minutes so that both dishes are almost ready at the same time.

Three

There is a bank's call center. In front of the operator is a computer screen. On it, there is a line. Points appearing above this line indicate that the user can bring sufficient profit to the bank. Points appearing below this line indicate that the user cannot bring enough profit to the bank.

When a phone call comes in, the user says, another bank is offering me a 50% discount to become its user. The operator will see where the point representing this user is located on the screen. If it's below, the operator will say: Alright, then cancel your account, wishing you smooth sailing. If it's above, the operator will say: I've consulted with the client manager, we will offer you a greater discount, please stay.

The call center is the profit center.

Four

UPS entered the financial industry from the express delivery industry.

Traditionally, when a package is sent from the manufacturer's warehouse until it is signed for by the user, it requires the guarantee of a bank. Only after the user signs to confirm receipt, will the money be paid to the manufacturer. And usually, this process takes two weeks.

UPS can tell the manufacturer, instead of waiting two weeks, I only need two days to pay you. The manufacturer will be happy. UPS can achieve this because it is the first to know when the goods are sent out and when they are signed for by the user. Usually, the first action UPS takes when picking up the goods is scanning the barcode. The last action UPS takes when delivering the goods is also scanning the barcode.

With financial services, UPS' advantage in the express delivery industry will be further deepened. Moreover, UPS has gained additional income and profits.

Five

Traditional business models must choose one of two options: either satisfy breadth or depth. For example, pharmaceutical manufacturers satisfy breadth; a cold medicine can cure everyone's colds. Family doctors satisfy depth; one doctor can only provide family consultation services to a few people. Breadth and depth cannot be achieved simultaneously.

But technology will change this. Gene drugs will allow large pharmaceutical manufacturers to satisfy both depth and breadth. Because they can provide gene drugs customized for each patient, containing their own genetic codes.

Media is also like this. Weibo satisfies both breadth by providing information to everyone, and depth as everyone's information is different, personalized. Moreover, whether in terms of breadth or depth, it surpasses all previous media forms.

Six

The Spanish clothing brand ZARA shortened the design and production cycle to two weeks. And each design is produced in very small quantities. This makes piracy unsustainable.

Previously, the cycle for design and mass production was several months. When the model walks down the runway, piracy begins. By the time the clothes are mass-produced and hit the market, there are already people on the streets wearing pirated versions. No one is interested in these clothes anymore. This is a blow to the fashion industry.

But ZARA, relying on a powerful IT system, solved this problem. Now, the boss of ZARA is Spain's richest man.

Seven

The impact of social issues on business is greater than technological innovation.

Aging population and uneven distribution. By 2050, Russia's population will shrink by 33%, Italy by 40%. The Middle East's population will double. The number of elderly people will exceed young people for the first time.

By 2025, water will become the most limited resource. In every gallon of water on Earth, only one drop is drinkable. China accounts for 22% of the global population but only has 8% of the water resources. India accounts for 19% of the population but only has 4% of the water resources. Thousands of people die every hour from diseases related to water pollution.

Children born after 2000 have an average life expectancy of 120 years.

Many European countries will go bankrupt. Because they didn't anticipate the significant increase in life expectancy. They previously formulated insurance and welfare plans based on an average life expectancy of 80 years. But when these people live 20 more years, everything changes.
