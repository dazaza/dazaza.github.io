Home remedies for children's coughs through diet

Coughs can be divided into external pathogenic coughs and internal injury coughs. External pathogenic coughs can further be categorized into wind-cold coughs and wind-heat coughs. The medications used for different types of coughs are completely different, and so are the dietary therapy methods. Here is a simple way to differentiate: observe the child's tongue coating. If the tongue coating is white, then it is a wind-cold cough, indicating that the child has excessive coldness. The phlegm from this type of cough is also relatively thin, white, sticky, accompanied by nasal congestion and runny nose. At this time, the child should eat some warm, expectorant, and cough-relieving foods. If the child's tongue coating is yellow and red, then it is a wind-heat cough, indicating that the child has excessive internal heat. The phlegm from this type of cough is yellow, thick, difficult to expel, accompanied by sore throat. At this time, the child should eat some lung-clearing, expectorant, and cough-relieving foods. Internal injury coughs are often chronic and recurring. At this time, parents should pay attention to feeding the child some spleen-regulating, kidney-supplementing, and lung qi-supplementing foods.

Wind-Cold Cough:

1. Ginger + Brown Sugar + Garlic

If the child catches a wind-cold感冒, drinking warm ginger brown sugar water can have a good therapeutic effect. If the child also has a cough, you can add 2~3 cloves of garlic in the ginger brown sugar water and cook it on low heat for 10 minutes to remove the spiciness of the garlic, making it easier for the child to drink.

2. Steamed Garlic Water

Take 2~3 cloves of garlic, smash them, place them in a bowl, add half a bowl of water, one piece of rock sugar, cover the bowl, and steam it in a pot. After boiling on high heat, switch to low heat and steam for 15 minutes. When the garlic water in the bowl is lukewarm, feed it to the child. The garlic does not have to be eaten. Generally, the child can drink 2~3 times a day, each time half a bowl. Garlic is warm in nature, enters the spleen, stomach, and lungs meridians, and is very effective in treating cold-induced coughs and kidney-deficiency coughs. It is also convenient and simple, and children like to drink it.

3. Roasted Tangerines

Place the tangerine directly on a low flame and keep turning it until the peel turns black and hot steam comes out from the tangerine. Let the tangerine cool slightly, peel off the skin, and let the child eat the warm tangerine segments. If it’s a large tangerine, the child can eat 2~3 segments at a time; if it’s a small mandarin orange, the child can eat one at a time. It’s best to combine this with garlic water, 2~3 times a day. Tangerines are warm in nature and have the effect of transforming phlegm and relieving cough. After eating roasted tangerines, the amount of phlegm will significantly decrease, and the cough-relieving effect is remarkable, and children like to eat them.

4. Scallion Oil Stir-fried Eggs

Put a small spoonful of scallion oil into a wok. After the oil heats up, add minced ginger, stir briefly, then crack an egg and stir-fry evenly. For children with wind-cold cough or weak constitution, let the child eat once before bed while it’s still warm. Continue doing this for several days, and you will see noticeable results.

5. Pear + Sichuan Pepper + Rock Sugar

Take one pear, wash it, cut it horizontally in half, scoop out the core, place 20 grains of Sichuan pepper and 2 pieces of rock sugar inside, put the pear back together in a bowl, and steam it in a pot for about half an hour. One pear can be eaten in two portions. This method is very effective for treating wind-cold coughs, but some children may not like the taste of Sichuan pepper, so parents can choose accordingly.

These are dietary therapy recipes for treating wind-cold coughs. While using these methods, parents should also note that children should avoid eating foods that are cold in nature, such as mung beans, crabs, clam meat, snails, slugs, persimmons, pomelos, bananas, kiwis, sugarcane, watermelons, cantaloupes, bitter melons, water chestnuts, cattail tubers, seaweed, kelp, raw radishes, eggplants, leek flowers, lotus roots, winter melons, loofahs, sweet potatoes, etc.

Wind-Heat Cough:

1. Pear + Rock Sugar + Sichuan Fritillary Bulb

Cut the pear horizontally near the handle, scoop out the core, place 2~3 pieces of rock sugar and 5~6 pieces of crushed Sichuan fritillary bulb inside, reassemble the pear, place it in a bowl, and steam it for about 30 minutes. Divide it into two portions for the baby to eat. This recipe has the effects of moistening the lungs, stopping coughs, and resolving phlegm. Since most babies nowadays tend to consume cold items excessively, leading to frequent use of air conditioning when they feel hot and year-round consumption of cold fruits, cases of wind-heat coughs among babies have significantly decreased.

2. Boiled Radish Water

Wash the white radish, slice it into 4~5 thin slices, place it in a small pot, add more than half a bowl of water, bring it to a boil, then simmer for another 5 minutes. Once the water cools slightly, give it to the baby to drink. This remedy is quite effective for wind-heat coughs, dry noses, sore throats, and dry coughs with little phlegm. Babies under 2 years old experience better results.

3. Other Foods:

① Persimmon: Very cold in nature, can clear heat, resolve phlegm, and relieve coughs. However, the baby can only eat one at a time, eating too many may cause discomfort.

② Watermelon: Cold in nature, can treat all kinds of heat syndromes. If the baby suffers from wind-heat cough during summer, let him eat more watermelon.

③ Loquat: Cool in nature, can moisten the lungs, resolve phlegm, and relieve coughs. Suitable for babies with hot coughs who produce yellow pus-like phlegm.

④ Water Chestnut: Cold in nature, water chestnut water can resolve phlegm and clear heat. Take 2~3 water chestnuts, peel them, slice them thinly, place them in a pot, add a bowl of water, and boil for 5 minutes. This remedy works well for babies with hot coughs producing pus-like phlegm.

Other foods like boiled winter melon soup, stir-fried loofah, stir-fried lotus root slices, stir-fried bitter melon, etc., also help reduce internal heat, eliminate fire, and stop coughs. Avoid spicy and easily heat-inducing foods such as lamb, dog meat, black-bone chicken, fish, shrimp, dates, longan flesh, lychee, walnut kernels, chili peppers, cherries, silkworm pupae.

Internal Injury Cough:

Internal injury cough refers to chronic coughs that occur over a long period or repeatedly. Or, it could be a cough triggered by a common cold or fever. Even though the symptoms of the cold or fever have disappeared, the cough persists. Babies with repeated coughs have used a lot of antibiotics and cough suppressants, leading to poor appetite and a white tongue coating. Therefore, parents should first regulate their baby's spleen and stomach functions and improve their physical constitution. Specific dietary therapy methods are as follows:

1. All dietary therapy recipes for wind-cold coughs are suitable for babies with internal injury coughs.

2. Yam Porridge

Peel the yam, cut it into small pieces, and place it in a food processor. Add half a bowl of water, process the yam into a thin paste. Then pour it into a pot, heat it on the stove while continuously stirring, and bring it to a boil. It’s best for the baby to eat it on an empty stomach. A bowl of cooked yam porridge can be fed to the baby in 2~3 portions. Yam strengthens the spleen and stomach, replenishes lung qi, and nourishes kidney essence. This recipe is especially suitable for infants and young children. Not only does it stop coughs and treat asthma, but it also effectively treats conditions such as loss of appetite, excessive sweating, drooling, qi deficiency, and timidity in children. Note that the cooking time for yam should not be too long, otherwise, the amylase it contains will decompose, losing its nourishing function.

3. Red Dates + White Nuts

This recipe is suitable for babies over 2 years old. Take 3 red dates and 3 white nuts, place them in a small pot, add more than half a bowl of water, and boil on medium heat for 10 minutes. Give it to the baby before bedtime every night. Red dates are warm in nature, tonify qi and energy, strengthen the spleen and stomach; white nuts are neutral in nature, consolidate lung qi, stop coughing and wheezing, and also have a kidney-tonifying effect. Therefore, it is very effective for children with prolonged coughs, repeated colds, coughs, and fevers. Additionally, it can also treat enuresis. Note that the quantity of red dates and white nuts must be controlled, limited to 3 each. Excessive amounts can lead to heatiness and stagnation of qi in babies.

4. Walnut + Sesame + Red Dates + Honey

Take half a pound of walnut kernels, 2 ounces of black sesame seeds, and half a pound of red dates, grind them into powder, mix them in a large bowl, then add one tablespoon of honey and three tablespoons of water (since honey is hard to mix evenly, it can be heated with water). Cover the large bowl, place it in a large pot, and steam it. After boiling on high heat, switch to low heat and steam for 40 minutes. Give the baby one spoonful in the morning and evening. This recipe is most suitable for children to consume, not only treating prolonged coughs, bronchitis, and asthma in children, but also having a very good effect on constipation in children. If consumed regularly, it can enhance the baby's physique.

Massage Therapy for Children's Coughs:

1. Back Patting

Infants cannot spit out phlegm, even if the phlegm has been coughed out, they will swallow it down into their stomachs. When the baby is coughing, hold the baby and gently pat the baby's back with an open palm, covering all areas up and down, left and right. If patting at a certain area makes the baby cough, it indicates that the phlegm is accumulated there, so focus on patting that area. Most often, it is the area below the shoulder blades, where phlegm tends to accumulate in the lower part of the lungs. As long as there is phlegm stimulation, the baby will cough, and once the phlegm is expelled, the cough will temporarily subside. Therefore, back patting helps widen the chest, regulate qi flow, and promote phlegm expulsion. It is best to do back patting when the baby has just woken up or is about to go to sleep.

2. Foot Massage for Babies with Wind-Heat Cough Accompanied by Sore Throat and Tonsillitis

First, rub the baby's feet back and forth, rubbing each foot 30 times. Then massage each toe up and down 20~40 times. Focus on massaging the sides of the base of the big toe on each foot. When tonsillitis occurs, this area will be painful. Massage each foot for 5 minutes. After the massage, the baby's throat swelling and pain symptoms will significantly improve. After the massage, promptly give the baby plenty of warm water to drink, or diluted salt water. Persist in giving the baby a massage twice a day, combined with dietary therapy, and the baby's condition will recover quickly.

Attention to "Nourishment" for Children's Coughs:

As the saying goes, "three parts treatment, seven parts nourishment," curing children's coughs requires attention to "nourishment."

(1) Maintain Fresh Air in the Room

Polluted air can cause adverse stimulation to the respiratory mucosa, causing the respiratory mucosa to become congested, swollen, secrete abnormally, or worsen the cough, and serious cases can lead to wheezing symptoms. Therefore, it is important to maintain fresh indoor air, ensure kitchen smoke is vented, and parents should not smoke at home. Regularly open windows for ventilation.

(2) Adjust Clothing Appropriately

Many parents believe that children are definitely more afraid of the cold than adults. They often disregard the season, whether indoors or outdoors, and dress the child too warmly, wrap them too tightly, and prevent them from catching any cold air. The result is reduced body regulation ability and weakened resistance.

(3) Regulate Room Temperature

Children with coughs often have a fever, and high room temperatures are not conducive to body heat dissipation. Cooler and fresher air can compress the respiratory mucosa, reducing congestion and swelling, maintaining unobstructed airways. However, excessively low temperatures can cause the body to use more nutrients for oxidation to generate heat and maintain body temperature, weakening disease resistance and affecting growth and development. An appropriate temperature is 25-28°C, known as neutral temperature. Under normal circumstances, it is difficult to maintain this temperature, but it is possible to ensure that the room temperature is not too high or too low. Appropriately opening and closing windows, avoiding overcrowding indoors, using fans, heaters, or air conditioners are all simple and practical methods.

(4) Keep Indoor Humidity Appropriate

An overly dry environment reduces air humidity, causing the mucosa to dry out and become brittle. Small blood vessels may rupture and bleed, cilia movement is restricted, and phlegm is not easily expelled. When the respiratory organs are inflamed, the impact is more significant. Maintaining a certain level of humidity indoors is not difficult. During dry weather, a wet mop can be used to clean the floor, or water can be sprinkled on the ground.

(5) Pay Attention to Dietary Adjustment

As the saying goes, "fish generates heat, meat generates phlegm, green vegetables and tofu ensure safety." According to Traditional Chinese Medicine, fish, crabs, shrimp, and fatty meats are greasy and can assist in generating dampness and phlegm. Some may even cause allergic reactions, worsening the condition. Spicy foods like chili, pepper, and ginger stimulate the respiratory tract, exacerbating the cough. It is necessary to avoid them. Fresh vegetables like green vegetables, carrots, and tomatoes provide various vitamins and minerals, benefiting the recovery of bodily metabolic functions.

(6) Ensure Adequate Sleep

During sleep, all muscles relax, the response to external stimuli decreases, heart rate, breathing, and excretion activities decrease, which helps restore the functions of various organs and aids in disease recovery. Try to let the child rest more in bed, ensuring adequate sleep, which benefits the child's recovery.
