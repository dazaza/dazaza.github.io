Introduction: In real life, our bodies may encounter some issues from time to time, and medication is often the most reliable way to restore normal bodily functions. But what should you do if you feel unwell and don't have any medicine on hand? Don't worry; below, let's take a look at some simple emergency measures for eight common symptoms provided by doctors.

Symptom 1: Elevated Body Temperature

Place a bottle of cold water under your knees and wipe your neck and shoulders with vinegar. Alternatively, eat 2-3 oranges or one bitter grapefruit, as an adequate amount of vitamin C can also reduce fever.

Symptom 2: Sore Throat

If your tonsils are inflamed, boil an egg (without peeling it to maintain heat), wrap it in a towel, and apply it to the affected area for 10 minutes. Or use a simple yet effective mouthwash: a cup of hot water mixed with a teaspoon of salt and 10 drops of iodine tincture. If you don't have iodine, you can substitute it with white liquor.

Symptom 3: Severe Cold

Mix equal parts onion juice and cool boiled water, and use it to drip into your nose every hour. Aloe vera juice is also effective; you can directly drip the juice from freshly broken aloe leaves into your nose.

Symptom 4: Indigestion

If you don’t have activated charcoal at home, drink a raw egg—it’s a good absorbent. Mint is a natural antispasmodic; chewing mint gum or eating mint-flavored candy will help alleviate discomfort.

Symptom 5: Sudden Onset of Kidney or Liver Issues

To cleanse your kidneys, drink two glasses of lukewarm water and wrap a towel around your waist. Nephrologists suggest placing a hot water bottle between your feet and sitting for half an hour to an hour when experiencing lower back pain. If your liver feels uncomfortable, drink some fennel soup or place a hot water bag on your liver area for relief.

Symptom 6: Intense Headache

If you have lemons nearby, that’s great! Apply a few drops of lemon juice to your temples. Citric acid can dilate blood vessels, alleviating spasms. Then gently massage the cervical vertebrae at the base of your neck.

Symptom 7: Drop in Blood Pressure, Dizziness

Do not lie down or close your eyes; instead, sit and massage your temples. Drink moderately concentrated hot coffee or strong sweetened black tea, which is even better with mint added.

Symptom 8: Toothache

Rinse your mouth with warm water mixed with baking soda (one teaspoon per cup of water), or place a cut clove of garlic on the painful tooth. In an emergency, you can also place cotton soaked in a vasodilator on the affected tooth.

Conclusion: When you experience any of the above symptoms without access to medication, you can refer to these emergency measures suggested by doctors. However, I would like to remind everyone that these methods are only simple emergency solutions. For persistent discomfort, it’s essential to visit a hospital for examination to find the root cause. Regularly taking care of your body is crucial, as good health is the fundamental guarantee of life.
