Receptionist secretly opens online store, earns 4000 yuan per month

by anonymous on 2011-03-28 09:56:24

(Reprinted from Xinhua News: http://news.xinhuanet.com/internet/2010-05/13/content_13482281.htm)

According to a statistical report, 80% of white-collar workers have the idea of opening an online store, and 50% have actually opened one. However, this report does not reveal a harsh reality: among those who open online stores, 95% give up halfway.

The network economy is booming, with e-commerce leading the charge. More and more people are joining the ranks of online merchants, but have they all succeeded? What difficulties have they encountered?

Behind the success stories of giant sellers like "Lemon Green Tea," many aspiring entrepreneurs follow in their footsteps. However, while the media focuses on the brilliance of the successful, it often overlooks the pain and helplessness of those who fail.

I. Mr. Li's Online Store Story

Mr. Li was a programmer at an IT company - or rather, he used to be, because he quit his job at the end of 2008 to start a "clothing online store" with his girlfriend.

Mr. Li's entrepreneurial impulse came from an encounter in the elevator. He would often see courier company staff squeezing into the elevator carrying large numbers of small packages. His intuition told him that there was a "big seller" in the same building. When he asked the courier staff how much the seller paid for shipping each month, they told him that the seller was a clothing merchant who paid them 20,000 yuan per month for shipping.

"Just the shipping costs amount to 20,000 yuan, and clothing is a high-profit product, so how much profit do they make each month?" Mr. Li thought happily, and in excitement, he quit his job to become a "full-fledged" online merchant.

However, Mr. Li, who didn't understand business, soon hit a roadblock. He went to the clothing market and purchased dozens of styles of clothes in different colors and sizes, quickly depleting over ten thousand yuan of his savings. Then came the photography, uploading pictures, installing customer service software, and waiting for customers to come. To his frustration, hardly anyone visited his small store to inquire.

"Is it because I don't have enough 'diamonds' (a credit rating system)?" Mr. Li attributed his failure to insufficient credit, and thus took the risk of paying someone to "brush diamonds." Brushing diamonds is a prohibited behavior for inflating false credit, and if caught, the online store would have to be closed.

Of course, more people slip through the cracks, and Mr. Li thought that as long as he didn't get caught, it would be fine. Mr. Li eventually successfully brushed up to "diamond" status, making his online store look bustling with good reviews galore. However, after a few days, he realized that the "diamond" had little value; while inquiries increased somewhat, he still couldn't sell any clothes.

As the season began to cool down, fewer people were interested in summer clothes. With a large inventory backlog, Mr. Li anxiously sought advice from some "big sellers" on how to run an online store. After being advised by the big sellers, Mr. Li finally understood that registering an online store only takes a few minutes, but doing well requires "deep waters."

According to the big sellers, just like opening a physical store, an online store needs returning customers to keep buying. Without customer accumulation, relying solely on brushing diamonds will not result in business, even if given a "crown" (higher credit rating). To accumulate sufficient old customers, one must "at least lose money for half a year," and generally, after patiently holding out for six months, one can earn about several thousand yuan.

There are many people like Mr. Li who don't understand traditional businesses but impulsively quit their jobs to open online stores. Often, these individuals struggle helplessly on the brink of survival, needing guidance and attention.

II. The Success Story of a Small Clothing Factory Owner

Indeed, there are quite a few profitable online stores on some large shopping websites. However, these "successful online stores" often have their reasons for success, which are closely related to traditional commerce.

For example, some stores started early, such as those who began making money from online stores in 2006. By persisting until 2009, they had accumulated a large number of customers. This is akin to "getting the first bite" in traditional commerce. Of course, not everyone can "get the first bite," and there are also many who started making money from online stores in 2009. There is a story about a clothing factory owner that is truly remarkable.

This boss had a deep understanding of consumer psychology and his own clothing factory. Therefore, he first observed which Taobao sellers' clothes were "best-sellers," then had the factory produce them, and sold them below cost to saturate the market, causing smaller sellers to complain bitterly.

At the same time, he offered numerous discounts, such as free shipping, gifts, etc., and spent tens of thousands on advertising for these "loss-making deals," resulting in an initial investment loss of hundreds of thousands of yuan. But with investment comes return, and the returns were astonishing. The big seller's credibility reached "crown" level, accumulating a large number of loyal customers.

The small boss then hired female models to shoot clothing model photos in overseas tourist destinations like Paris and London. This elevated the quality of his clothing. Girls are naturally vain, seeing Paris and London-branded clothes, plus the fact that these clothes were not expensive—only costing dozens of yuan—they began placing orders enthusiastically.

At this point, the small boss was no longer losing money. The profit margin of his clothes jumped from the original 20% wholesale price to a 200% retail price. Within just a few months, he recovered the initial losses and gained a "super-popular large online store."

In fact, the level of business acumen demonstrated by this small clothing factory owner far surpasses that of well-known big sellers like "Lemon Green Tea" frequently mentioned in the media. Those who make money are often low-key individuals and therefore are reluctant to be publicly interviewed by the media.

If you carefully analyze the "big sellers" on Taobao and Paipai, you'll find a pattern: these people are often experts in traditional commerce. Those who succeed in online sales are usually experienced and insightful within the industry.

The network world cannot escape the issue of "income inequality." Some large sellers rent an entire floor for their warehouses, with inventories worth millions, employing dozens of people, while some small sellers struggle to survive, encountering obstacles everywhere. These constitute the various states of e-commerce.

III. How Should Small Sellers Survive and Move Forward?

In fact, small sellers face many problems, and online stores have not resolved the various issues of traditional businesses.

For instance, "inventory" is a huge problem. Take clothing as an example: women are picky, so let's say women need about 100 styles to choose from. Each style has at least two colors and three different sizes. Even if each style has only one piece in stock, that's already 1000 pieces of inventory.

The inventory problem in traditional clothing sectors also exists in online store sales. I once visited the studio of a "big seller." It wasn't really a studio, but an abandoned factory that had been renovated into a business park. This big seller occupied an entire floor. He didn't have many employees, only 30 people, occupying two small offices. Aside from his photography studio, the majority of the floor was his warehouse.

That warehouse contained nearly 300 styles of clothes. The big seller smiled and said, "This is equivalent to several Mercedes-Benz cars," but his smile carried a hint of helplessness. As the weather gradually cooled, a large amount of summer clothing began to be heavily discounted online. Advertising, free shipping, and giving away gifts were all methods used to clear inventory at a loss.

If big sellers are like this, what about small sellers? Small sellers like Mr. Li mentioned earlier lack both strength and financial resources. When seasons change, they can only hold onto their goods. They might want to sell at a loss, but it's uncertain whether they can sell them.

If challenged by "traditional commercial elites," it's merely normal competition. For small sellers like Mr. Li, a more lethal threat comes from the rampant "picture party."

The "picture party" is also operated by some experts. Although these experts may not be masters of traditional business, they are adept at manipulating pictures. For example, the same piece of clothing photographed by them looks extremely desirable to purchase. The same 70-yuan piece of clothing, after being processed by the "picture party," appears in photos that could sell for 170 yuan. And don't argue; your 70-yuan item doesn't sell, while theirs at 170 yuan does.

Of course, becoming part of the "picture party" isn't easy. Mr. Li mentioned earlier once wanted to join the picture party and hired a photography company to take pictures. The enormous cost startled him: 1000 yuan for a model and outdoor fees, 80 yuan for shooting cost per style. Mr. Li hadn't even started shooting when he was scared off. If anyone could casually become part of the "picture party," then the "picture party" itself wouldn't survive.

Many people think opening an online store is easy and jump into the industry without hesitation, even quitting their jobs to open online stores. Is opening an online store really that easy? Perhaps registration is indeed simple; Guangzhou VIPshop Information Technology Co., Ltd. allows you to have an online store ready in ten minutes.

But is simply registering considered opening an online store? I think not.

Registering is merely the "first step" in the long journey of running an online store. Ahead lie many challenges, treacherous mountains, and stubborn enemies. Initial investment, advertising, inventory issues, seasonal risks, image problems, customer accumulation—each is a bottleneck and difficulty. Failure to solve any one of these problems means the online store won't succeed.

Unfortunately, many people often imagine the future too beautifully, seeing others spend 20,000 yuan on shipping fees and daydreaming, completely forgetting the old saying: "You only see the thief eating meat, but not the thief getting beaten."

Faced with the crisis of survival, where should small shop owners go?

IV. A Receptionist Quietly Opens an Online Store, Earning Four Thousand Yuan Monthly

Ms. Fang runs her online store very successfully, earning more than four thousand yuan monthly. She hasn't quit her job and continues to receive her salary. Ms. Fang repeatedly reminded me not to disclose her name because she still works at a company as a receptionist, earning 2,500 yuan monthly, and she doesn't want to resign due to her online store.

Ms. Fang works as a receptionist at a company. Having just graduated, she lacks work experience but has a pretty face, so she became a receptionist. Being a receptionist is mainly about maintaining the company's appearance; as long as she looks nice, she's basically just a decoration. Normally, there isn't much work, and her salary is the lowest, only around 2,500 yuan.

Ms. Fang starts her day handling trivial matters, like calling couriers to send contracts, ordering water when the water dispenser runs low, and similar tasks. These things don't take up much time.

If we consider her real work, it's playing online games like tank battles and card games. Another task is shopping on online stores. From university, Ms. Fang has been a fervent online shopper, spending hundreds of yuan monthly on cosmetics and clothes, all bought online.

Of course, Ms. Fang is a woman, and women have to marry, so they don't need grand ambitions; striving is a man's job. So she didn't have Mr. Li's kind of "start an online store, expand sales, then open a factory, and finally list on the stock market" kind of grand vision. Opening an online store was entirely accidental.

Ms. Fang is a fervent online shopper and a veteran buyer. Once, while buying clothes, she noticed that many sellers were selling the same clothes, even with identical photos. Upon investigation, she learned that these clothing online stores didn't handle inventory themselves but used a service called "online store drop shipping." This means that sellers only display the clothing photos on their online store, and once a customer places an order, they place an order on the drop shipping institution's website, with the clothes being shipped directly from the drop shipping institution.

"If I pretend to be an online store owner and buy a piece of clothing in bulk, wouldn't I save more money?" Ms. Fang approached a well-known online store drop shipping website, "Best Seller Network," with a bargain-hunting mindset.

Upon entering the website, Ms. Fang was shocked to discover that many clothes on large shopping websites were actually "drop shipped" from here, and the price difference was significant. So, were the clothes here of good quality? Ms. Fang had doubts, but they were quickly dispelled. Best Seller Network had a section called "Free Shipping Products," where expensive wool sweaters only cost 29 yuan including shipping. Since it was only that much money, why not try a piece to check the quality?

Without hesitation, Ms. Fang placed an order. A few days later, she received a very nice piece of clothing, and from then on, she became a regular customer of Best Seller Network, frequently "pretending to be an online store owner" to buy clothes.

Having saved so much money, Ms. Fang suddenly thought, "All the money I've worked hard to earn goes towards cosmetics and clothes. Why don't I open an online store to make some pocket money?" Thus, she transformed from "pretending to be an online store owner" buying clothes for herself into a genuine "online store owner."

Since she was doing "online store drop shipping," she didn't have to worry about sourcing products, taking photos, or even after-sales services, as Best Seller Network provided "unconditional exchange service." Moreover, Best Seller Network initially had hundreds or even thousands of styles available for Ms. Fang to list. Because Best Seller Network is a platform, many well-known online brands sell their products here, such as 1400KM, Fashion Garden, Urban Space, etc.

Due to its multi-brand platform characteristics, Best Seller Network has more advantages than ordinary drop shippers. The more styles available, the easier it is to run an online store. This way, Ms. Fang's business slowly started, and apart from calling couriers, ordering pure water, and placing lunch orders for colleagues, she mostly played games and ran her online store. Like all new online store owners, Ms. Fang's store initially had no business. Of course, she didn't put in much effort; all she needed to do was upload Best Seller Network's "online store data package" to her own online store.

Ms. Fang had a great attitude. In the first month, she made 500 yuan and was very happy: "This month's pocket money is earned."

In the second month, she made 900 yuan and felt: "This month's rent is earned."

In the third month, she made 2000 yuan and felt even happier: "If my husband wants to sell the car, the car loan doesn't have to come out of my salary."

In the fourth month, she made 4000 yuan and thought: "Wow! If this continues, I can use this to pay off the mortgage, and a house won't cost anything."

Because of her positive attitude and Best Seller Network's continuous updates of quality sources, Ms. Fang's business kept coming. All she had to do was sell the goods, and shipping was completed by clicking a mouse online. After-sales services were handled, unconditional exchanges, no questions asked. Now, the only difference is that she no longer plays "tanks" during work hours but spends her time chatting with customers using the customer service software.

Ms. Fang is still working as a receptionist, and her colleagues know she casually opens an online store during her free time. However, they don't know that her monthly income from the online store has surpassed her receptionist salary. Ms. Fang doesn't think there's a conflict between working and running an online store; she believes in earning money from both.

V. Small Online Store Owners Should "Do Business Standing on the Shoulders of Giants"

Elderly businessmen often say: humans have two legs, money has four. Humans chasing money can never catch up, but money chasing humans will always catch up.

If we analyze Ms. Fang and Mr. Li, both running clothing online stores, one finds it very strenuous while the other runs smoothly. Apart from differences in mindset, we discover that men starting businesses often have an issue of "too ambitious," which is often the root cause of failure.

Chinese men starting businesses often exhibit a "poor scholar, sour scholar" temperament. Despite having minimal ability and limited financial resources, they insist on appearing overconfident and overly ambitious, portraying themselves as invincible, which makes them the most unsuccessful and foolish.

Ambition becomes particularly foolish when it extends to "eating the entire industrial chain." For instance, some online store owners strongly oppose "online store drop shipping," feeling that they are losing profits and wanting to manage their own inventory to "eat the entire profit chain," which is very foolish.

Managing your own inventory can indeed "eat the entire profit chain," but do you have enough money to handle hundreds of styles? Even if you invest over a million, do you have more money to update the inventory since clothing styles change constantly? What about seasonal changes? How do you liquidate the inventory, and how do you control the risk? Furthermore, even if you have tens of millions to handle inventory, you're only "eating a tiny portion of the profit." The profit margin of online wholesale is usually less than 10%, while the profit margin of online retail is typically 50%-100%. Spending such a large cost for such a small profit is irrational.

The biggest feature of industrial society is social division of labor and industrial chains. While an industrial chain is a "profit link," it enhances efficiency and reduces costs, thereby lowering overall costs.

For example:

Farmers grow wheat and sell it to flour merchants, earning a layer of "wheat profit." Flour merchants process wheat into flour, earning a layer of "flour profit." Bakers turn flour into bread, earning a layer of "bread profit."

However, combining these "three layers of profit" is still cheaper than growing your own wheat and making your own bread. This is the value generated by the social "industrial chain" division: it makes goods cheaper.

Taking Best Seller Network as an example, it uses centralized procurement to reduce clothing costs. Even with added profit margins, it's still cheaper than traditional purchasing channels. Its large-scale centralized procurement ensures stable quality due to mass production. With such large procurement volumes, manufacturers must comply with its requirements, allowing for "unconditional exchange" after-sales services. Even with an added profit margin, it's still cheaper than regular channels. This is the value generated by the social division of the industrial chain.

DuPont, a powerful Fortune 500 company specializing in chemical raw materials, sells its raw materials to clients who turn them into products with higher profit margins. DuPont, such a large company, doesn't "consume the entire industrial chain profit" by making products itself. Clearly, society has a division of labor within the industrial chain, and small online store owners must learn to harness the industrial chain and learn to do business standing on the shoulders of giants.

Newton once said: "I see further because I stand on the shoulders of giants." Conversely, if you only stand on a stool, how far can you see?
