Car Beauty Knowledge: Car Washing

Several Misconceptions about Car Washing

A: Using car wax colors randomly

Black cars have black dedicated car wax, red cars have red dedicated car wax, and white cars have white dedicated car wax. These should not be used indiscriminately. The main difference between these waxes lies in their cleaning components, with some also containing brightening formulas. Especially for metallic paint, the correct car wax must be used.

B: Adding interior equipment arbitrarily

Private cars are like the mobile homes of car owners, and pursuing comfort and luxury is understandable. However, some car owners install various devices inside the car themselves. Such modifications, if handled improperly, can adversely affect the car's performance and the convenience and reliability of its operation, potentially leaving behind problems or affecting driving safety.

C: Washing the car under the scorching sun to dry quickly

Many private car owners like to wash their cars under the scorching sun, thinking that this way the water on the car body will dry quickly after washing. In fact, this is wrong. Washing the car under the scorching sun causes the water droplets to form a convex lens effect, creating localized high temperatures on the top layer of the car paint. Over time, the car paint will lose its luster. If waxing is done at this time, it can also cause uneven coloration on the car body. Therefore, washing and waxing the car should ideally be done in a shaded area. If this cannot be guaranteed, it is best to choose an overcast day or early morning/evening on a sunny day.

D: Circular waxing is the most scientific

Many people habitually use circular motions when waxing, which is an incorrect method. The correct way to wax is in straight lines, alternating horizontal and vertical lines, and finally applying the last coat in the direction of water flow. This achieves the effect of reducing the appearance of concentric circles on the car paint surface.

E: Wrong order

Roof, front and rear cover plates, side of the body, lights, bumpers, skirts, wheels.

F: Washing the car with stagnant water

It is essential to use running water. Stagnant water is one of the main causes of scratches on the car. At least two buckets of water should be prepared because the lower third of the car body is always the dirtiest. It should be wiped separately from the upper two-thirds.

G: Washing and waxing the car every day

Washing and waxing the car once a month is sufficient. Excessive washing and waxing can actually make the car's brightness gradually fade. If you must wash your car weekly, choose a cleaner with a neutral cleaning power and car wax without abrasive components.

H: Excessive wiping of the hood

The hood is the face of a car, and many car owners pay special attention to this area. When washing and waxing the car, they put extra effort into this part and may even wipe it casually while waiting. Additionally, the hood panel is lower than the roof and trunk panels, making it easy to apply full force. The result is: under sunlight, countless fine scratches appear, and there is a slight loss of luster.

Car Beauty Knowledge: New Car Decoration and Maintenance

How to Wash Your Car More Effectively

1. Body Paint Surface Maintenance

The most important aspect of maintaining the car's paint surface is washing the car. Many car owners have washed their own cars, but how exactly should it be done correctly and effectively?

Rinse the car body with clean water to remove dirt. Mix the car shampoo according to the dilution ratio specified in the product manual (the dilution ratio for Hote car shampoo is 1:100) with clean water.

Use a sponge dipped in the mixed car wash solution to wipe the entire car body. After wiping the entire car, rinse the car body clean with clean water. Be sure to rinse in a top-down order to avoid unnecessary effort.

Finally, use a clean towel or chamois leather to wipe the car body clean. After washing the car, you might notice that the four tires do not match the clean car body. At this point, it's time to beautify the car's tires.

2. Interior Comprehensive Cleaning

After finishing the exterior work, let's focus on the cabin. If cleaning the car's exterior is for others to see, then cleaning the car's interior is truly for your own health.

Spray dashboard wax onto the car's dashboard and wipe clean with a clean cotton cloth. Use a multi-functional foam cleaner to spray onto the stains on various plastic parts inside the car and wipe clean with a clean cotton cloth.

Spray velvet cleaner or leather cream onto the stains on velvet seats or leather seats and wipe clean with a clean cotton cloth.

Clean the air conditioning ducts. First, insert the fine tube of the air conditioning cleaner into the air conditioning duct (it's best to remove the air conditioning outlet), spray an appropriate amount of air conditioning cleaner. After completing, close the air conditioning outlet, turn on the air conditioning internal circulation, start the engine, and idle for about 10 minutes.

Turn off the vehicle, open the engine hood, remove the air conditioning filter (refer to the user manual of each model for the location of the air conditioning filter), blow clean the air conditioning filter (use a high-pressure blowing gun if possible), and wipe the air conditioning filter opening with a clean cotton cloth.

Finally, spray air freshener into the air inside the car cabin.

3. Car Body Waxing

The correct steps to wax your car are as follows:

Follow the car washing procedure described above to thoroughly clean the car body. Before waxing, ensure the car body is completely dry with no residual water. You can use an absorbent sponge or chamois leather to manually wipe the entire car body, paying attention to edges and corners and other detailed parts.

After the water marks on the car body are completely dry, pour a strip of wax onto the sponge dedicated for waxing.

Wax the car using either linear or circular methods. Regardless of which method you choose, use a sponge to evenly apply wax on the painted surfaces of the car body.

Try to avoid applying wax to unpainted parts (such as some plastic components on the car body). If accidentally applied, wipe it off immediately with a clean towel.

After 5-10 minutes (usually quick wax requires 5 minutes, diamond wax requires 10 minutes), confirm that the wax has dried (turned white), and use a soft, dry cotton cloth to wipe off the car wax.

Waxing should be done in a cooler weather condition. Avoid waxing under direct sunlight or on a hot car. If the car has just stopped, wait until the engine hood is completely cooled before waxing.

If there are small scratches on the car body, you can repair them yourself with a touch-up pen.

4. Replace Wiper Blades

In this season, the rainy season in southern China is approaching. Many car owners' wipers used throughout the winter may have cracked and are unable to clean properly. At this point, you need to replace the wipers yourself.
