Weather forecast: only one precipitation and cooling weather in Beijing during the Two Sessions, News Center, Sina.com

by psyzems1 on 2012-03-02 23:29:41

South China is currently in the midst of spring planting. Over the next 10 days, there will be more overcast and rainy weather in South China, with temperatures rising compared to earlier periods. The daily average temperature in the central and northern parts of South China will generally be between 10-13°C, while in the southern regions, it will be above 15°C.

He pointed out that over the next 10 days, the cold air affecting the Beijing area will not be strong. Apart from a rainfall (snow) and cooling weather process around the 5th day, the weather for most other times will be good, with the daily average temperature ranging between 2-5°C (the long-term average is 4°C). The daily minimum temperature will generally not fall below -2°C, and the daily maximum temperature will usually not exceed 10°C.

Most areas in the south will have more overcast and rainy weather. The total precipitation will generally be between 30-60 millimeters, with some areas in Jiangnan and northern South China receiving 80-180 millimeters. The main precipitation periods will occur between the 3rd and 7th days, during which some areas in Jiangnan and northern South China may experience heavy rainstorms. In addition, the precipitation in northern Xinjiang, eastern Northwest China, North China, Huaihai region, and Northeast China will generally be between 1-5 millimeters, with some areas receiving 10-15 millimeters.

Chen Zhenlin stated that over the next 10 days, the cold air activity affecting China will be relatively frequent but not strong. In terms of temperature, the temperatures in most parts of the country will basically be on par with the long-term averages for the same period, with areas such as Huai River, Jiangnan, and South China being slightly lower by 1-2°C compared to the long-term averages due to insufficient sunshine.

On March 2nd, the China Meteorological Administration held a press conference where Chen Zhenlin, Director of the Emergency Disaster Mitigation and Public Service Department of the China Meteorological Administration, introduced the weather conditions during the "two sessions" and the first ten days of March. He expressed that over the next 10 days, the cold air affecting the Beijing area will not be strong, with only one rainfall (snow) and cooling weather event occurring around the 5th day.

There will be weak precipitation in the Northern Winter Wheat Region. From the 2nd to the 5th, there will be weak precipitation in most parts of the Northern Winter Wheat Region, with a precipitation amount of 1-5 millimeters. There will be no significant precipitation during other times.

Precipitation will continue to be scarce in meteorological drought areas such as Yunnan. From the 2nd to the 4th, there will be a weak precipitation process in the meteorological drought areas of Yunnan and southern Sichuan, with precipitation generally less than 8 millimeters, and some areas receiving 10-15 millimeters. From the 5th to the 11th, these meteorological drought areas will mainly see clear or partly cloudy weather, with higher temperatures. This is a significant province-wide precipitation process in Yunnan since the beginning of this year, especially since winter began.
