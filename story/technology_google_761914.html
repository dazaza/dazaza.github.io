Chinese Developers Focus on Google Conference: Most Concerned about Android Trends

by geekzhang on 2013-05-15 10:55:57

Chinese Developers Focus on Google Conference: Most Concerned About Android TrendsWe just arrived in San Francisco today, and many developers from home have come over. On May 14th, Beijing time, Chen Qing Shan, the technical director of Shanghai Hanvon Intellisoft Technologies (the developer of Business Card Reader), told reporters that everyone had specially come to attend the annual Google I/O (Google Global Developer Conference).The Google I/O conference has already been held for five sessions, attracting a large number of developers each year. The 2013 Google I/O will officially open at midnight on May 15th, Beijing time. Google opened the registration for Google I/O 2013 two months ago, but within less than 50 minutes, the tickets for the conference were sold out.According to my understanding, more than 5,500 developers from all over the world will gather at the conference site this year. In addition, Google will live-stream the conference via the internet, allowing anyone to watch online. Google's offices in various regions will also organize some developers to watch the live broadcast.Why are so many developers eager to attend the Google I/O conference?Because there are too many things that attract developers that might be announced at the conference. Seizing any trend could give developers a first-mover advantage, which in turn could bring real income.More and more Chinese developers are no longer satisfied with watching the live stream on their computers; they have chosen to go directly to the scene. As one developer put it, you can't experience the product experience of those geeks if you're not there.Moreover, being able to communicate directly with Google's team and exchange experiences with developers around the world is also the direct reason why more and more Chinese developers are willing to personally attend the Google I/O conference.We come to attend the meeting every year; Google is our recommender. A person from TouchPal Technology told reporters that apart from exchanging ideas with Google's Android team, they would also meet many partners. Because TouchPal mainly targets the overseas market, all its partners are also overseas.I contacted several domestic developers who went to the scene, and everyone generally paid the most attention to the trends of Android because it involves the survival issue of developers. Secondly, what everyone was most curious about was Google Glass (Google Glasses). Everyone wanted to know when this product would reach consumers' hands and what kind of applications they could develop for it.In fact, besides Android and glasses, this year's conference also includes discussions on various platforms such as Chrome, Google+, cloud platforms, maps, YouTube, Google Wallet, and advertising. According to informed sources, the Chrome platform may be another focus that Google wants to draw developers' attention to this year.After the father of Android, Andy Rubin, stepped down, Sundar Pichai, the senior vice president of Chrome and Google Apps business, took over the position of Android head.The Trends of Android Affect DevelopersIn the highlights of the Google I/O released by various tech websites currently, the speculation about whether the Android 5.0 system will be released has been placed in the most prominent position.Since the launch of the first phone equipped with the Android 1.0 system in 2008, Android has grown into the world's most widely used mobile operating system. Google CEO Larry Page once said, we have more than 60 hardware manufacturer partners worldwide; the activation volume of Android devices has reached more than 750 million units; the download volume of Google Play Store apps has reached 25 billion times.The reason for achieving such a status is the open ecosystem and continuous updates of Android. Through rapid version updates, the current Android system phones have an operation experience that is no longer inferior to Apple's. Therefore, the industry previously speculated that Google would release the new generation of Android system called Key Lime Pie this year, different from the previous Jelly Bean.However, there are also reports now that Google will only make a minor upgrade based on the existing Android 4.2 system this time.The reason why developers are so concerned about the new version of Android is still to understand Google's attitude towards the Android ecosystem. Will the new Android boss Sundar Pichai impose more control over Android after taking office?In fact, due to excessive openness, the rapidly growing Android has not brought Google income matching its growth. Moreover, it has brought challenges of losing control and ecosystem fragmentation. Especially in the Chinese market, since Google has not yet formally entered, various small and large Android markets have made it harder to identify and manage applications.Sundar Pichai recently admitted in an interview with foreign media that the biggest challenge for Android at present lies in how we assist in improving the terminal user experience worldwide without changing the open essence of Android?Does this mean that Google hopes to strengthen the control over Android? Sundar Pichai denied this. For example, Facebook's recently released Facebook Home phone and Amazon's Kindle Fire have completely changed the user experience of Android.Generally speaking, Google hopes that everyone works under a unified Android version because it will benefit everyone. But we will not prohibit different approaches. Sundar Pichai stated, our focus is not on Facebook Home or Kindle Fire. Computing technology is experiencing a once-in-a-lifetime explosive development. Our opportunity lies in ensuring that computing technology suits everyone and solves important problems for them.Chen Qing Shan told reporters that he expects Google to introduce more new technologies in Android development for developers to use, allowing everyone to achieve better user experiences on Android.The Overlooked Chrome?In my exchanges with developers, I found that apart from the Android they rely on for survival, the next thing they care about the most is the news about Google Glasses. The emergence of Google Glasses has made people see more possibilities for wearable devices in the future.Since Google only released the test version of the glasses to developers not long ago, the final product is likely to not be launched until next year. However, everyone still expects to get more information about the new functions of the glasses and how developers can participate at this year's I/O conference.However, after checking the agenda of the conference, I found that the much-anticipated Google Glasses only have four conference sessions on the second day of the conference. In comparison, Android has 35 discussion meetings. But it still isn't the most discussed topic; Chrome has a total of 48 discussion meetings.According to informed sources, such an arrangement may indicate that Google will pay more attention to the construction of the Chrome ecosystem this year. This is a market overlooked by developers because compared to Android, the global influence of the Chrome browser and operating system is not as great.After Sundar Pichai took over the management of Android, people have always speculated how he would integrate the Chrome and Android systems. However, Google stated that these two systems would continue to develop independently and would not interfere with each other.Users care about the applications and services they use, not the operating system. Sundar Pichai said in an interview with the media, users rarely ask why MacBook uses MacOSX while iPhone and iPad use iOS? People use iTunes, iCloud, etc. Developers are human beings too; they hope to develop applications once and want more choices.So, following this logic, Google hopes to create a unified experience on PCs and mobile terminals through Chrome and Android, whether for users or developers. Does this mean that after the development platforms are connected, developers will have more opportunities on Chrome?
