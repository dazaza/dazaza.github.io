Offshore company registration, Ruifeng successful cases of registering offshore companies [import and export trade and foreign trade companies]

Case: OEM Private Labeling

1. [Apparel Industry] Miss Lin from Hangzhou: Engaged in the wholesale clothing business at Hangzhou's Sijiqing market and Beijing's Dahongmen, she has been purchasing goods from other factories for wholesale due to the lack of her own brand. In 2001, through Ruifeng Accounting Firm, she registered [XX International Apparel Co., Ltd.] in Hong Kong and simultaneously registered trademarks in both Hong Kong and mainland China. She then commissioned related factories under the name of the Hong Kong company for private label processing, marking the international apparel company's name on the clothing packaging and labels, giving the clothing its own brand and developing towards higher-end products. Now, Miss Lin's family has invested in advanced equipment in Hangzhou, establishing a large-scale assembly line clothing factory.

2. [Furniture Industry] Mr. Wang from Guangdong: Founded a furniture production factory in Shunde, Guangdong Province, investing considerable funds in advanced production equipment. Although his product craftsmanship was good, in the fierce competition of the home furnishings market, current home consumers favored international foreign brands. After careful consideration, he learned that Ruifeng Accounting Firm could help clients register an Italian company. He specially came to Ruifeng's Hong Kong headquarters to consult and entrust them with handling relevant matters. Our commercial advisor explained the situation to Mr. Wang; registering a company in Italy would be costly and involve complicated maintenance procedures in the future. We suggested registering an Italian company in Hong Kong instead, while simultaneously registering trademarks in both Italy and mainland China. The furniture’s instruction manual and labels were marked with the name of the Italian company and the trademark registration TM. Now, Mr. Wang's home products have truly become popular in the Chinese market.

3. [Kitchen Appliances] Mr. Zhang from Guangdong: Engaged in the kitchen cabinet equipment manufacturing industry in Zhongshan City, Guangdong Province, his products had passed national safety quality standards certification, but the market was difficult to expand. At an industry product exhibition, he noticed that some competitors at the exhibition had inferior product quality, safety standards, and production processes compared to his own, yet received more orders. During an accidental business encounter, the client revealed the secret: to register a German or Japanese company for OEM private label processing. Realizing this, Mr. Zhang immediately searched online and found Ruifeng Accounting Firm in Hong Kong, gaining a comprehensive understanding of the company registration process and corporate brand image planning methods. Subsequently, he registered [German XX Electrical (Hong Kong) Co., Ltd.] and [Japanese XX Kitchen Electrical Corporation]. Mr. Zhang said: With these two companies, the brand image improved, the market share increased, the product price rose, and he remarked that he didn't realize how much the domestic population favored foreign brands.

Case: International Trade

1. [Tax Optimization] Mr. Wang from Fujian: Mainly engaged in project engineering investment bidding, due to business expansion needs, he wanted to establish a reputable group company in mainland China. However, due to the inability to secure tens of millions of registered capital and the desired company name already being occupied by others, later through a friend's introduction, he established the group company in the Cayman Islands, as well as subsidiaries in Hong Kong, Samoa, the Bahamas, and mainland China, becoming a true multinational group. After meticulous planning by accountants, the company's taxes were reasonably arranged.

2. [Import/Export] Miss Du from Hefei: Long-term engagement in international import trade. Once, a French chemical raw material supplier refused to sign a contract with her unless she entrusted an overseas intermediary company to sign the contract and issue a letter of credit, citing high credibility and timely reliable payments from overseas intermediaries. Since this was a long-term business relationship, Miss Du had no choice but to establish a company in Delaware, USA. She opened an offshore account for the Delaware company at a foreign bank in Shanghai (the account possessed all settlement functions required in international trade), thus establishing a long-term good cooperative relationship with the client.

3. [Trade Settlement] Mr. Jin from Tianjin: Engaged in international textile product export trade, he always had to export through middle agents, but was concerned about business secrets and settlement security. To ensure long-term stability and avoid exchange rate difference losses, he wanted to register an overseas offshore company for autonomous exports and direct settlements, provided that the offshore company did not require complicated financial reporting procedures. With the help of Ruifeng professionals, Mr. Jin successfully registered a BVI offshore company in the British Virgin Islands (this company does not need to provide annual financial reports to the government) and simultaneously opened an offshore account at a foreign bank in Beijing, fully complying with Mr. Jin's offshore trade strategy and requirements, allowing for greater international development.

4. [Export Rebate] Mr. Lin from Xiamen: Long-term engagement in stone import-export trade. Due to the cancellation of stone export rebates in Xiamen, trade profits plummeted. Through online research, he successfully registered [XXXX Stone Import-Export Co., Ltd.] through Ruifeng Accounting Firm, not only eliminating the export rebate issue but also maximizing profit retention. Moreover, the speed of receiving overseas payments significantly improved compared to before.

5. [Payment Settlement] Mr. Chen from Shenzhen: Long-term supply of electronic components to Hong Kong manufacturers, most of whom have factories in Shenzhen, Dongguan, Zhuhai, etc. Sometimes, Hong Kong manufacturers paid him via Hong Kong bank checks. Mr. Chen consistently struggled with safe and direct collection issues. Later, after registering a company in Hong Kong and opening a bank account there, he not only solved the long-standing payment problem but also gained the trust of clients for long-term cooperation.

Case: Return Investment

1. [Policy Benefits] Mr. Pang from Shandong: Engaged in plastic machinery manufacturing in Jinan City, due to development needs, he wanted to relocate the enterprise to a development zone. However, as a domestic-funded enterprise, the government did not offer many land requisition and tax policy benefits. Later, with Ruifeng's assistance, he registered [XX International Machinery Stock Company] in Hong Kong, using the Hong Kong company to form a joint venture with the original enterprise in the development zone, enjoying all preferential policies offered by the government for attracting investment.

Case: Exhibition Meetings

1. [Exhibition Meetings] Mr. Dong from Beijing: Operated an exhibition company specializing in organizing meetings, exhibitions, and public relations activities. Due to intense market competition and lack of official institutional partnerships, the company remained in loss. When Mr. Dong was struggling with the development and operation of the company, he learned that it was convenient to apply for association or society registration in Hong Kong. Immediately, he applied to register three associations in Hong Kong (the Hong Kong government registry allows names like 'China XX Industry Association'). Subsequently, he invited and organized enterprises to participate in exhibition meetings under the association's name, gaining the trust of participating enterprises.

Case: Cultural Education

1. [Publishing Distribution] Miss Qu from Beijing: Engaged in cultural publishing, the procedure for registering a publishing house in mainland China had always been a headache for her. Introduced by a friend, Miss Qu successfully established a publishing house in Hong Kong within less than 10 working days. She also applied for an international standard ISSN number from the French ISSN agency under the name of the Hong Kong publishing house, thereby developing her own cultural publishing business. If applying for publication distribution-related publications in mainland China, the approval time and procedures would be very complex.

Case: Business Secretary

1. [Business Secretary] Mr. Ma from Guangzhou: Had many businesses in Hong Kong but couldn’t frequently visit Hong Kong to handle them, encountering many difficulties. After registering a Hong Kong company through Ruifeng, Ruifeng's business secretary resolved all his difficulties, such as providing the company address, telephone, fax, dedicated personnel answering calls, and collecting checks on his behalf. They also designed quotation forms, invoices, delivery notes, picking-up notes, etc., enabling him to do business in mainland China under the name of the Hong Kong company without any worries.

2. [Phone Transfer] Mr. Ren from Hangzhou: After registering a Hong Kong company, many customers directly called Hong Kong to inquire about business issues, but Mr. Ren himself couldn’t personally go to Hong Kong to handle the business. To ensure every deal went smoothly, Mr. Ren devised a perfect solution: transferring the Hong Kong phone line to be answered in mainland China. This not only resolved the company image issue but also ensured no business call opportunities were missed.
