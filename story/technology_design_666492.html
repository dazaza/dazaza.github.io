What is dead skin and why do we need to remove it? Dead skin, also known as keratin, tends to accumulate as we age because our metabolism slows down. This is especially true for facial skin. In order to better absorb the skincare products we use, removing dead skin is a necessary process. Below, I will introduce three DIY face masks specifically targeting dead skin.

Oatmeal Mask for Dead Skin: Go to the supermarket and buy oatmeal (there are generally two types: loose and packaged. It is recommended to buy the packaged one because it's finer and easier to dissolve. Ideally, buy the type that doesn't require cooking and can be soaked in hot water directly). Take a small bowl, put about two porcelain tablespoons of oatmeal, and add a small amount of hot water (note: just a little!). Now go wash your face. By the time you come back with a clean face, the oatmeal will have softened. Use a spoon to mash the oatmeal, then gently and evenly apply it to your face. Be gentle; if you're too rough, you might remove skin that shouldn't be removed... After about 15-20 minutes, rinse it off with clear water. Then feel your face; it will be very smooth.

To make the mask, soak a piece of mask paper until it's completely saturated. Squeeze out excess liquid with your hands, unfold it, and lay it flat on your face. Use a cotton ball, tissue, or fingers dipped in milk to touch areas where you feel there isn't enough milk. Lie down on the sofa for 15-20 minutes, then remove the mask and wash your face with clear water.

Mint Protein Mask for Exfoliation: If you have oily skin, you can use skimmed milk, protein, and mint for a face mask. Skimmed milk nourishes the skin, protein enhances skin elasticity, and mint contains essential oils that can cool the skin and reduce heat, minimizing external irritants to the skin.

Materials: 1 egg white, 1 teaspoon of mint, 1 tablespoon of skimmed milk powder.

Method: Mix all the ingredients into a paste, apply it to your face for 20 minutes, then wash it off with water.
