China's Richest Man Wang Chuanfu's 4-Year Journey of Laughter and Tears

by slid1334b on 2010-04-11 13:09:08

College life witnesses brotherly affection: the elder brother travels a thousand miles to Changsha for him, and he helps his brother with business during spare time. After going into business, he develops from researching batteries to developing cars. In 14 years, 2.5 million yuan turns into 35 billion yuan.

Recently, Hurun Report released the "2009 Hainan Clearwater Bay Hurun Rich List" in Beijing. Wang Chuanfu became China's richest man in 2009 with a wealth of 35 billion yuan, increasing by 29 billion yuan compared to last year, rising from the 103rd position last year to the first position this year.

This "richest man in China" who achieved success in just 14 years has a great connection with Hunan - In 1983, Wang Chuanfu entered the Metallurgical Physical Chemistry Department of Central South Mining and Metallurgy Institute in Changsha (the predecessor of Central South University of Science and Technology which merged into Central South University).

How did Wang Chuanfu spend his four years of college life? Was there anything extraordinary about him? Did he show his business wisdom during his university days? On October 13th, I interviewed his counselor and classmates at Central South University.

Wang Chuanfu's Profile:

Anhui native, born on February 15, 1966, graduated from the Metallurgical Physical Chemistry major of Central South University in 1987, then entered the Beijing Nonferrous Metals Research Institute to pursue a master's degree, stayed to work after graduation in 1990. Resigned in 1995 and founded BYD Company. In a few short years, it developed into China's largest and the world's second-largest rechargeable battery manufacturer. Entered the automotive industry in 2003, currently serves as Chairman and President of BYD Co., Ltd., Chairman of BYD Electronics (International) Co., Ltd., and Fidelity Red Hurricane. Ranked 559th on the Forbes Global Billionaires List in 2009 with a personal wealth of $1.3 billion. Ranked as China's richest man on the 2009 Hurun Rich List with a net worth of 35 billion yuan.

"Wang Chuanfu becoming China's richest man this year depends on two factors: the 30% growth rate of China's automobile industry, and the huge influence of Buffett's investment." - Hurun

1. Endured hardship, smart, studied hard

"He lived a very hard life during his university days," said Liu Di, Wang Chuanfu's counselor and current Deputy Secretary of the Party Committee of the School of Chemistry and Chemical Engineering at Central South University. According to him, Wang Chuanfu's parents passed away early, and he was raised by relatives, especially his older brother Wang Chuanfang. After Wang Chuanfu was admitted to university, his brother moved from their hometown in Anhui to Changsha to do some small businesses to pay for Wang Chuanfu's tuition and daily expenses.

When Wang Chuanfu graduated from Central South University and entered the Beijing Nonferrous Metals Research Institute to pursue a master's degree, Wang Chuanfang also followed him to Beijing, continuing to do small businesses to support Wang Chuanfu.

"During his spare time, Wang Chuanfu would go back to help his brother with the business," said Liu Di. "He has a very good relationship with his brother, and currently, his brother manages the logistics at BYD."

According to Liu Di, Wang Chuanfu was not an outstanding student in all aspects during his university days. "But he was very smart and ambitious, joining the Communist Party in his sophomore year, and was the first among the students of the class of '83 to enter the department's Youth League General Branch."

In 1987, Wang Chuanfu entered the Beijing Nonferrous Metals Research Institute to pursue a master's degree and worked there after graduation. He progressed methodically to become director, senior engineer, associate professor, and even mentored a group of graduate students.

After working in the institute for five years, he went into business and registered BYD Industry in Shenzhen in February 1995.

2. Likes dancing, but not an expert

Previously, media reported that Wang Chuanfu was a "dance master" during his university days. However, Liu Yi, Wang Chuanfu's classmate and close friend during university, said, "Wang Chuanfu danced well, but he wasn't a 'dance master'."

According to Liu Yi, when they were in university, the school held lectures on dance knowledge, and they took these opportunities to learn. "They taught social dances like ballroom dancing."

"Wang Chuanfu often danced in the school canteen," Liu Yi said. "As long as we moved the tables and chairs aside and turned on the recorder, we could dance and drink with the music, having a blast!"

In university, Wang Chuanfu was considered an active member, "He even helped introduce matches for the counselors," Liu Di said.

When asked if Wang Chuanfu had a romance during university, Liu Di said, "He didn't have one. At that time, romances in universities weren't as common as they are now, and the school rules didn't encourage them."

3. From batteries to cars, achieving riches in 14 years

During his university days, Wang Chuanfu wasn't very enthusiastic about entertainment activities. "He studied very hard," said counselor Liu Di. Perhaps it was this seriousness and professionalism that allowed him to always foresee the correct direction in his future path.

During his undergraduate and graduate studies, Wang Chuanfu researched batteries, and after entering the business world, he remained in this field.

In February 1995, Wang Chuanfu registered BYD Industry in Shenzhen, starting his entrepreneurial journey with 2.5 million yuan borrowed from his cousin. Today, BYD has become the world's second-largest rechargeable battery manufacturer, holding nearly 15% of the global market.

In July 2002, BYD successfully listed on the Hong Kong main board. In seven years, Wang Chuanfu expanded BYD from an initial team of 20 people to a scale of 15,000 employees.

On January 23, 2003, BYD announced its acquisition agreement with Xi'an Qinchuan Automobile Co., Ltd., investing 269.5 million yuan (approximately 254 million Hong Kong dollars) to acquire 77% of Qinchuan Automobile's equity, entering the automotive industry. The 2.5 million yuan used to start the business 14 years ago has now transformed into a market value of over 30 billion yuan.
