by anonymous on 2012-03-03 23:13:43

Fuzhou, News Network - A man from Jiangsu Province, Mr. Zhang, defrauded 60,000 yuan at Fuzhou Train Station four years ago and then went into hiding in Eastern Tibet. His father, now in his eighties, in order to atone for his son's sins, has gone out to work in order to prepare money to repay the debt.

After more than two months of effort by the police officers of the Fuzhou Railway Station Police Station, Mr. Zhang was successfully captured. On the 22nd, he was escorted back to Fuzhou.

In late 2007, the 44-year-old Mr. Zhang defrauded 60,000 yuan in cash from a victim at Fuzhou Train Station under the pretext of helping him arrange for overseas travel, and then went into hiding. The Fuzhou police had tried multiple times to arrest him in Jiangsu but failed. Therefore, they sought out his father, hoping that he could persuade his son to turn himself in. After committing the crime, Mr. Zhang cut off all contact with his family. The elderly father also did not know the whereabouts of his son. Upon learning that his son had defrauded the victim of 60,000 yuan, this impoverished old man made an astonishing decision: to work outside and repay the money to the victim, thereby atoning for his son's sins.

This year, the old man is already 80 years old and still working, while Mr. Zhang remains nowhere to be found. In early September, the police returned to Jiangsu again to investigate Mr. Zhang's whereabouts. After more than two months of effort, the police discovered that Mr. Zhang was hiding in a civilian house in Xuzhou, Jiangsu. She rarely went out, only choosing to go out at night to buy necessities every dozen days or so. One evening, the police officers stormed into the house and captured Mr. Zhang. Upon learning that her father was still working at the age of 80 in order to help her atone for her sins and prepare money to repay the debt, Mr. Zhang burst into tears.

On the 22nd, Mr. Zhang was escorted back to Fuzhou. (Fuzhou Evening News reporter, Mr. Yanxin, correspondent from the Front Station)
