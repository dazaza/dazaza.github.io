Mourinho's bizarre changes confuse Italian media; the master's touch may never be reached by Lai or Ferrara

According to Sina Sports, before the Italian Cup match against Livorno, Mourinho's list of 19 players made the Italian media think hard. There were only four defenders (Maicon, Donati, Lucio, and Chivu). What kind of formation was he going to deploy?

The Sports Post predicted: "Cambiaso will partner with Lucio, with Chivu and Maicon on the left and right." All Sports had a slightly different idea: "Donati will start, Cambiaso will partner with the sole central defender Lucio, and Chivu will be on the left." If the media could guess his thoughts, Mourinho might no longer be Mourinho. Lucio as the center-back was correct, but partnering with him was Chivu. In Chivu's usual position stood Montari...

"The boss told me to play as a left-back, so I did as he asked," Montari seemed quite calm, moving from the center to the wing, from midfielder to defender, and he didn't seem uncomfortable because "I played left-back when I was very young."

After the game, in the fan ratings on Inter Milan's official website, Montari scored 7 points, which was an average score for the defense, indicating that in the eyes of most fans, the Ghanaian's performance was competent, and the game process was roughly so. At the 14th minute, Pieri crossed the ball looking for Lucarelli, Montari moved early and headed the ball out of bounds ahead of the latter. However, his usual impatience didn't diminish due to the positional change. At the 22nd minute, Montari committed a foul on Dani Leviychus in front of the penalty area, giving Livorno a direct free-kick opportunity. Montari wasn't lacking in defensive confrontation ability, just a bit less calm. Just like in the Champions League against Barcelona, he was repeatedly hit by Alves' left-wing attacks in the first 45 minutes, lacking adaptability and ideas for improvement, often leading to the same mistakes happening repeatedly. But as mentioned earlier, Montari has the fighting spirit and tenacity that Mourinho wants to see. In the away Champions League game against Kiev, it was his long-range shot from the left wing that led to subsequent shots by Milito and Sneijder.

Mourinho loves to think about changing player positions. When Sneijder was injured and absent, he even considered letting Montari play as an attacking midfielder. As for other players, Cambiaso had been used as a center-back, Balotelli had been placed on the wing, and Santon had moved from the right to the left. As for changes in Zanetti's position, there is no need to elaborate further.

During the game, Rai's camera captured details of Mourinho's tactical board, where the names of four players appeared, with a horizontal line between the first and second players in the first row. In almost every game, Mourinho could be seen writing and drawing on a small notebook. Such actions were once considered a way for Mourinho to relieve stress, similar to Ferguson chewing gum or Ancelotti smoking. But this might not be the case. The constantly changing tactics of Inter Milan undoubtedly most intuitively explain that Mourinho is always thinking, and meaningfully so. From trying out four formations in 18 months at Inter Milan, to continuously adjusting player positions, Mourinho is actively and proactively building his own team.

In fact, this is Inter Milan's advantage over AC Milan and Juventus, an absolute advantage of Mourinho over Leonardo and Ferrara. Without the long-term training in Spain, Portugal, England, Italy, and on the Champions League stage, you might never be able to understand such thinking in your lifetime.

Juventus lost 2-3 at home to Napoli, and Mazza's substitution of D'Alessandro in the second half proved decisive. After this substitution, Ferrara had no reaction at all, watching helplessly as the 2-0 situation was squandered. Ferrara also made changes; once behind, he substituted De Ceglie and pushed Grosso forward, that's all. Leonardo's situation was slightly better due to AC Milan's performance, but the hesitation in substitutions during the Milan Derby, which delayed Gattuso being taken off, should be etched in his memory.
