A reasonable diet must be appropriate. Balanced nutrition is an important principle of a reasonable diet. Its reasonableness lies in its scientific nature. It is the conclusion drawn from countless examples and observations. This conclusion applies to everyone, has widespread universality, which is also the commonality. However, as individuals, people are slightly different while being largely the same, with their own demand characteristics, which is individuality. Ignoring this individuality and not paying attention to individual differences will make it difficult to achieve the purpose of balance. Therefore, for each specific person, it is necessary to pay attention to suitability. This suitability means that it should be suitable for individual nutritional needs. Paying attention to suitability means formulating a balanced diet according to personal nutritional needs. This requires paying attention to individual age, physical condition, health status, and labor intensity, as their nutritional needs vary accordingly. Only by noticing these differences can nutritional balance be achieved.

In terms of physical constitution, according to the perspective of traditional Chinese medicine, human constitutions are classified into: yin deficiency constitution, yang deficiency constitution, phlegm-dampness constitution, yin deficiency with hyperactivity of fire constitution, etc. Different constitutions have different nutritional needs. For example, people with yang deficiency and hyperactivity of fire constitution should eat less or avoid hot-natured foods, such as spicy foods, smoked and grilled foods, tobacco, and alcohol. They should eat more cooling and yin-nourishing foods, such as duck meat, scallops, and silver ear fungus. Although fruits can be eaten, they should not consume too much "hot" fruits like lychees and longans. People with phlegm-dampness constitution, especially those who are obese, tend to produce phlegm easily and should avoid eating foods that assist dampness, such as glutinous rice cakes, sweets, fried foods, fatty meat, cream, etc.

Regarding health status, it refers to the comprehensive evaluation of a person's mental state, physical strength, thinking ability, and stress response capability. There are different states, including normal times (when there is no illness), illness periods, and recovery periods, each having corresponding nutritional needs. When formulating a diet, this suitability must be considered. For instance, patients with diabetes should strictly control their carbohydrate intake during abnormal blood sugar periods. To supplement sufficient calories, they can appropriately increase protein intake from fish, meat, beans, and vegetables. However, the amount of intake should also be controlled. Excessive intake may lead to abnormal protein metabolism, resulting in increased uric acid levels. This illustrates that balance and suitability need to be combined to achieve reasonable and scientific nutritional needs. Specific operational content can be referred to in relevant materials, which will not be detailed here.

In terms of age, during the process of individual growth and development, different periods have different nutritional needs. The teenage years are a critical period for physical growth and development. Both energy and nutritional needs should be appropriately increased. Increase the intake of proteins, carbohydrates, and fats to meet the increasing energy demands. At the same time, sufficient vitamin A, vitamin D, vitamin C, calcium, zinc, and other minerals should be supplemented to meet the needs of growth and development. In old age, physical strength gradually declines, activities relatively decrease, and energy needs also relatively decrease. Therefore, in terms of nutritional supply, garlic oil effects should also be relatively reduced. In food selection, attention should be paid to easy digestion and rich nutrition. Relevant features and needs of nutrition in old age can be found in Chapter Four.

In terms of labor intensity, different labor methods have different nutritional needs. Generally speaking, physical labor requires increased energy needs, and the proportion of proteins, carbohydrates, and fats should be correspondingly increased. Physical labor is divided into heavy physical labor and light physical labor, and their nutritional requirements differ accordingly. Mental laborers, due to less physical activity, consume less energy but expend more mental energy. In terms of nutrition, some proteins, especially fish and meat proteins, should be appropriately increased. Due to less activity, fat intake should not be increased to avoid fat accumulation and obesity. In labor methods, there are high-temperature operations, such as metallurgy and glass industries; flight operations, such as pilots and astronauts, all of whom have corresponding nutritional needs, which will not be elaborated here regarding garlic oil effects.

Therefore, it can be seen that a reasonable and scientific diet not only needs to be balanced but also needs to be suitable. Only by paying attention to the suitability of individuality can the goal of general balance be achieved.
