In 2008, competition in Internet services intensified; analysis of service provider agency strategies.

by now on 2008-01-18 10:48:02

The "China Internet Market Channel Agent Investigation Report," released by the China Internet Association, states that the concept of a channel market within China's internet industry is still in its infancy. From the distribution of internet product agents, the more popular businesses mainly consist of three areas: one is hosting and domain names, another is search engines, and the last is advertising. For the hottest hosting and domain name agency market, industry insiders analyze that from aspects such as scale and profitability, the development potential of the internet basic product agency market is enormous, making it the most fiercely contested area for service providers in 2008.

From the perspective of China’s informatization phase, traditional enterprises have surpassed the initial stage of enterprise informatization and entered the online marketing stage, beginning to experiment with network business applications. In the future, not only will the internet industry, but other traditional industries also enter the internet domain, starting to conduct marketing online, etc. There is still a vast market to be explored in the collaboration between internet basic product service providers and agents. Internet service providers are continuously strengthening communication with agents and adjusting agency policies in a timely manner to further penetrate the agency market and jointly create better market prospects with agents.

In this fiercely competitive market, how should internet basic product service providers use channels to rapidly expand new businesses? How can they attract agencies and develop channel resources? How can they effectively collaborate with agents? The channel manager of Times Interconnect claims that service providers must align themselves with agents and consider the interests of agents to achieve win-win outcomes. Agents generally consider the following when choosing a partner: 1. Agents should be able to find products that match their capabilities and have high market recognition; 2. The agency price should be reasonable, bringing higher benefits and profits; 3. The brand and market reputation of the service provider; 4. After-sales service provided by the service provider. After-sales service is something more consumers care about and what agents pay attention to. 5. The flexibility of the service provider's agency policy and regular provision of dynamic agency activities.

After attracting agents, how to retain them is an important issue facing service providers. The key to building a quality agency channel lies in the service provider offering flexible agency policies and regularly providing dynamic agency promotional activities. This includes product strategy, product updates, discounts, rebates, after-sales service, technical support, and market advertising support. Times Interconnect (www.now.cn), which has seen rapid growth in agent channels within the IT service industry, performs relatively well in this aspect. Its agency promotional activities have generated significant responses within the industry. For example, in January 2008, Times Interconnect launched a special offer of 100M space for 56 yuan, which was the lowest price in the industry since the shocking HK$79 privilege price in December of the previous year. The person in charge of Times Interconnect's agency channel stated, " 'Privilege' is a new service we provide for high-end customers. As a privilege user, you can enjoy considerate and supreme services. 'Privilege' comprehensively solves past cumbersome issues such as domain modification and transfer, greatly saving valuable customer time. We will offer each period's lowest price in the industry as a privilege to reward our high-end customers."

Although the number of hosting and domain name agents continues to increase and the agent market expands rapidly, competition among service providers in this agency market is also intensifying. Service providers should continuously innovate in terms of agency support, strengthen communication with agents, assist in the development of agency teams, and jointly create a better future for internet services.
