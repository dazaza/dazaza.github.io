The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you want to buy or sell, the name (or code), quantity, and price of the stock being traded. And this order is only valid for the day it is placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you wish to trade, the quantity, and the price at which you intend to buy or sell the stock. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is six digits long. The code and abbreviation of the stock must match when placing an order. At the same time, there are certain rules regarding the quantity of stocks traded: the quantity of stocks ordered for purchase must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks sold does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place an order: counter submission, automatic telephone order, automatic computer order, and remote terminal order.
