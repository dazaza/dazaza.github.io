Nitric oxide enters the smooth muscle cells of the corpus cavernosum and activates guanylate cyclase, converting guanosine triphosphate into the second messenger cyclic guanosine monophosphate (cGMP). The increase in intracellular cGMP concentration leads to relaxation of the corpus cavernosum smooth muscle, causing the sinusoids of the corpus cavernosum to swell, increasing arterial blood flow, and expanding the volume of the corpus cavernosum. The tunica albuginea surrounding the corpus cavernosum passively stretches, compressing and narrowing the sub-tunical venules that drain blood from the corpus cavernosum, increasing venous outflow resistance, leading to penile enlargement and increased rigidity, resulting in an erection. As cGMP is degraded by phosphodiesterase type 5 (PDE5) within smooth muscle cells and after ejaculation when the sympathetic nervous system recovers, the penis transitions from a taut state to flaccidity.

Furthermore, under the mediation of adrenergic neurotransmitters, contraction of the corpus cavernosum smooth muscle cells reduces penile blood flow and opens venous return, leading to penile flaccidity.
