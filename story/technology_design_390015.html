A pep talk for friends who want to open an online store (written for those who haven't opened an online store yet or have just started) Author: Tao Tao's Little House

Recently, I've had a lot of... a lot of friends who, upon hearing that I run an online store, have all shown interest in opening one themselves and want to ask me about the specifics. Some friends have even asked me to help them set up their stores, throwing question after question at me, leaving me overwhelmed.

With the widespread use of the internet and the rising standard of living, the once mysterious e-commerce has gradually entered our lives. Online shopping has become increasingly common. More and more people are seeing potential in e-commerce. Among the people I know, many bosses also want to join the ranks of online sellers, hoho. Under my influence and guidance, some friends have already opened their shops.

I am a bit lazy by nature, so to avoid repeating the same work in the future, I've made this simple compilation for everyone! This blog is aimed at newbies; veterans can skip it, hehe. However, perhaps you often encounter friends asking you how to open an online store. You might find it helpful to share this blog with them, saving you some effort. Everyone's opinions are greatly valued, and I welcome your supplements and feedback.

Below, I will address several common questions that friends who want to open an online store often ask, expanding on what I want to say to aspiring sellers.

1. Question: I want to open an online store. Can I make money?

Answer: Many people definitely make money from opening online stores. Nowadays, more and more people are dedicated solely to running online stores, which serve as their means of livelihood. Additionally, there are many online stores with good sales volumes that have developed into online sales companies, hiring dozens or even hundreds of people to manage their stores. Some are professional sellers in their respective industries, transitioning from physical stores to online stores, allowing them to tap into broader markets. However, there are also many people who don't make much money, only earning a little pocket change. Just like physical stores, some make money while others suffer losses. However, because the overall investment is relatively small, the risk is less than opening a physical store. Many friends who plan to open physical stores but have no business experience choose to start with an online store first. Personally, I think this is a good way to practice, and it’s worth a try.

2. Question: What do I need to prepare to open an online store?

Answer: First, you must have basic material conditions: a computer connected to the internet. If you're selling physical products, you generally need a digital camera, as buyers cannot see the actual items, and product images are an important way to understand the product.

If you have good photography skills and know how to use some image editing software, that would be even better. Product images are the buyer's first impression of the product. Whether they look good and are clear has a significant impact on making a sale. If you don't know now, it's okay. You can learn and explore slowly after opening the store. Many Taobao sellers learn gradually after opening their stores. There are plenty of related tutorials on Taobao that you can refer to. If you don't want to learn, you can spend a bit of money and ask shop owners on Taobao who specialize in store decoration to help with image processing and store decoration. Having a beautifully decorated store is certainly beneficial for sales, just like decorating a physical store.

The software you must download for opening a store: Wangwang and Taobao Assistant. Wangwang allows you to communicate promptly with buyers and resolve their doubts. Using Taobao Assistant makes it easier to list the items you want to sell compared to online editing, and it is essential to use.

You also need a heart willing to serve others. As a seller, you not only provide products to buyers but also services. Good service will earn you a big positive review from buyers, and credit ratings on Taobao are very important.

Additionally, you need firm beliefs and determination not to give up easily. Many sellers on Taobao don't get any business after opening their stores for a while, and then many sellers give up. The beginning is the hardest time, the most testing time for sellers. This trial period can last a month or several months (of course, there are also sellers whose businesses are good right from the start, but they are few). If you don't have enough perseverance and give up halfway, it's better not to waste time and energy opening a store in the first place.

3. Question: What kind of products should I sell? Which products are the most profitable?

Answer: Choosing the right product is extremely important. It directly affects how well the product sells, which is crucial.

Many friends have asked me this question, but I don't know specifically which product is the most profitable. The most profitable ones often attract many people, and when everyone rushes in, it becomes harder to make money. Moreover, I think the most profitable product isn't necessarily the best fit for you. Not everyone who sells the most profitable items can make a lot of money. Instead, you should ask yourself what interests you the most, what you know best, what is most convenient for you to do, and what sources of goods you have. Ideally, you should treat opening such a store as a hobby. Because we can only operate well and enjoy ourselves with products we love! We can only persevere!

If you can get goods directly from a company or factory, that would be ideal. A friend of mine is a beauty mentor, an expert in the field of beauty, and can get products from the company. This friend also has a lot of time online, so opening a cosmetics online store undoubtedly has significant advantages.

4. Question: Where can I find suppliers? How much do I need to invest roughly?

Answer: You can check out wholesale markets or look on Alibaba for online wholesale purchases. There are merchants there who support small-batch purchases for Taobao buyers. I personally think that buying from physical wholesale markets is better. I suggest not buying too much stock the first time. Try to have more varieties but fewer quantities of each item. After purchasing, understand the market response and visit other similar stores for advice before making further decisions. If you can get goods directly from the manufacturer, that would be even better, as prices would likely be more advantageous. There are also other channels available.

If you feel uncertain, you can try drop shipping initially when starting your store. Some well-performing stores on Taobao recruit online store agents and can ship goods on your behalf. Some wholesale malls also offer drop shipping services for online stores. This method carries low risk, requiring a membership fee of one hundred to several hundred yuan, or even no membership fee at all. You only pay when you make a sale, allowing the other party to ship the goods, thus avoiding inventory risks (please choose carefully, as there are many scammers or poor-quality products). However, there are many drawbacks. Profits will be small, and it won't be conducive to your own development since you won't see the actual products. When buyers inquire, it will be more troublesome for you, as many aspects will be constrained by the other party. Additionally, if there are issues with shipping or quality problems, you may have to endure silently, apologize, and compensate for the buyer's losses.

I recommend not doing drop shipping long-term. To develop, you should purchase your own stock.

5. Question: Can we meet in person so you can teach me how to open a store?

Answer: I would love to teach you everything I know all at once, but there are simply too many things related to opening a store to cover in just one or two days. The basic registration and store-opening process is professionally demonstrated through Taobao's FLASH display (see the link at the end of the article). Friends can follow along and it's easy to understand.

If you are familiar with the network, you might not even need to read the tutorial to set up your store. Each step of the Taobao store-opening process has relevant prompts. A friend who didn't initially plan to open a store suddenly managed to open one effortlessly.

For other related knowledge, friends can first visit Taobao University (see the link at the end of the article), browse a few posts, and learn from the collective wisdom there. Many experts spend a lot of time writing and sharing their experiences. I'm still a one-diamond seller, so I don't have much authority to speak. It's better to learn directly from these super-sellers' posts.

You can also join some nice Wangwang groups where members help each other. You can ask questions in the group, and with up to 200 people in a group, someone else may know what you don't, helping you solve your doubts promptly.

Explore and think for yourself as much as possible. If you still can't find answers, seek help afterward. This will be more beneficial for your growth.

Additionally, some good friends have asked me to help them set up their online stores. After careful consideration, I declined because the experience of building something from scratch is far more rewarding when done entirely by oneself. What do you think, friends?

When I was preparing to open my store, I consulted a friend I'd known for a long time. She simply said, "Go check the forum." Then I stumbled around like a headless fly on the Taobao forum, gradually becoming familiar with various sections, searching for useful information, slowly exploring everything myself, learning from posts when I had questions, and occasionally asking others. Hehe, you're much luckier than I was back then...

6. Question: After setting up the store, can I just wait for others to buy stuff and be done with it?

Answer: If you think that listing the products completes the task, you're mistaken. It's best to keep Wangwang online for a long time, just like guarding a physical store. Although buyers can place orders even when the online store owner is offline, communication with buyers can be improved if the seller is online, and timely handling of buyers' concerns significantly increases the chances of making a sale. Many buyers prefer to pass over stores where the seller is offline when selecting products. Sometimes, I search for items and directly search for those where the seller's Wangwang is online.

Promotion is also a crucial part. With so many Taobao stores, how will buyers find you? Doing some promotion and participating in promotional activities organized by Taobao will greatly help your business. For related promotional methods, you can refer to the introductions and posts on Taobao University.

Another point I'd like to mention is that since buyers and sellers rate each other after transactions and leave reviews, this serves as an important reference for future buyers. Therefore, treating your buyers thoughtfully will make reviews a promotional tool for you. If you perform poorly, negative reviews could negatively affect your business.

7. Question: I want to quit my job and focus solely on running an online store. My family opposes it, thinking I won't make money. What do you think?

Answer: Recently, I've heard many friends say they want to run an online store full-time and stop working for others, believing they can earn money freely. However, they hesitate due to fears of not making money. Personally, I think this question should be answered by yourselves. Do you believe in yourself? If you don't believe in yourself, how can your family and friends believe in you? Of course, sometimes things turn out unexpectedly, hoho. Some matters become clearer as you proceed. Trying once might not be a bad idea, but you should consider how you plan to earn enough money on Taobao to sustain your life and assess your confidence level.

I suggest starting by running an online store part-time. Once the store reaches a certain scale, accumulates a certain level of credit, and you've gained sufficient knowledge about Taobao, then consider quitting your job to focus fully on the online store. This is a more stable approach.

However, I personally think running a part-time Taobao store offers more fun. Earning a little pocket money reduces pressure. Competition on Taobao is fierce nowadays, and standing out isn't easy.

If you've read all the text above and decide not to open a store, I still recommend becoming a Taobao buyer. My stance is simple: there are truly countless wonderful items on Taobao. Only what you can't imagine, not what you can't buy, and they're usually quite affordable. I really enjoy online shopping...
