How to prevent antral gastritis? Experts from the Gastrointestinal Department of Zhengzhou Xiehe Hospital introduced: Antral gastritis is a chronic inflammation that occurs in the antrum of the stomach, and it can generally be divided into two categories: superficial and atrophic. Through years of observation and research, it has been found that there is a close relationship between gastric cancer and atrophic antral gastritis.

So, how to prevent antral gastritis? Let's listen to the experts' explanation:

1. Hard, spicy, salty, hot, overly rough, and strongly stimulating foods should be avoided. Eating should be done in moderation with regularity, at fixed times and in fixed amounts, with small meals more frequently, chewing slowly to allow food to mix fully with saliva, avoiding binge eating. Foods should primarily be nutritious and easily digestible soft foods, with more plant protein and vitamin-rich foods.

2. Smoking and alcohol should be avoided. After smoking, nicotine can stimulate the gastric mucosa, increasing gastric acid secretion, affecting blood supply to the gastric mucosa, and the repair and regeneration of gastric mucosal cells. Alcohol can directly destroy the gastric mucosal barrier.

3. Drugs such as aspirin, acetaminophen, butazolidin, indole drugs, tetracycline, erythromycin, and prednisone should be avoided, especially during the active phase of chronic gastritis.

4. Those with excessive gastric acid should avoid eating foods that can stimulate gastric acid secretion, such as strong spices, alcohol, and acidic agents.

5. Those with insufficient gastric acid should not dilute the gastric juice. Vinegar, lemon juice, and acidic seasonings should be added to the diet, while difficult-to-digest and gas-producing foods should be consumed less. Meals and after-meal drinks should be kept to a minimum.

6. Mental tension is a promoting factor for chronic gastritis and should be avoided. Anxiety and irritability can easily cause gastric mucosal disorders and gastric dysfunction. Therefore, emotional stress reactions should be avoided as much as possible, and a relaxed mood should be maintained.

7. Irregular lifestyles and excessive fatigue should be avoided. Appropriate rest and exercise are necessary. Physical exercise promotes gastrointestinal peristalsis and emptying, enhancing gastrointestinal secretory function, improving digestion, and aiding in the recovery of gastritis.

Superficial gastritis is a chronic superficial inflammation of the gastric mucosa and is the most common type of chronic gastritis. Patients should seek timely treatment at regular hospitals. How does Zhengzhou Hospital treat superficial gastritis best? Let's take a detailed look at the experts' introduction.

The onset of superficial gastritis is often related to dietary factors, such as long-term consumption of strong liquor, strong tea, coffee, excessive chili seasoning, excessively salty, sour, or rough foods repeatedly stimulating the gastric mucosa. More importantly, unreasonable eating habits, irregular eating patterns, binge eating, or due to long-term lack of protein and B vitamins leading to gastric mucosal degeneration.

Experts from Zhengzhou Xiehe Hospital point out that dietary therapy is the best for treating superficial gastritis. The general principle of dietary adjustment: non-irritating, low-fiber content, easy to digest, and sufficiently nutritious diet; eat small meals more frequently; relax during meals and maintain a pleasant mood. The treatment of superficial gastritis should pay attention to the following points:

1. Avoid eating various irritating foods such as strong liquor, concentrated coffee, raw garlic mustard, etc., which damage the gastric mucosa, and also avoid eating hard, acidic, spicy, cold, hot, and overly rough foods. Easily digestible foods can be selected, paying attention to avoiding fried or grilled cooking methods. Food should be light and soft.

2. Increase nutrition, paying attention to choosing high-protein foods and vitamin-rich soft foods, such as milk, tofu, carrots, and some fermented foods. Food should be chewed thoroughly and eaten slowly.

3. Eating should be regular, at fixed times and in fixed amounts, without binge eating. Good eating habits should be cultivated to reduce the burden on the stomach. If the three main meals have smaller portions, snacks can be added between meals. Pay attention to food pairing, preferably having both dry and liquid foods, including protein foods and a small amount of staple food.
