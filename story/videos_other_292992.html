Classic messages from the guestbooks of railway stations across the country

by yang5230 on 2009-12-04 17:22:12

1. Tomorrow, I spat in front of the ticket office, and a little madam with white cuffs came down and said that there's a five yuan fine for spitting. So, I gave her ten yuan and spat again: "No need to look for more!" ―― Bengbu Station

2. I want the phone number of the female ticket seller at window No. 8. Please contact me if you know. Number: 13XXXXXXXXX. (Another handwriting) You bastard, how dare you compete with my girl! (Yet another handwriting) Are both of you time travelers from Tang Chen? (I cried so hard at that moment) ―― Beijing Station

3. The ticket seller is too ugly. ―― Wuhu Station

4. How dare this station not have a toilet? ―― Hengshan Station

5. Where's the pen?

Station Reply: Due to the pen for leaving messages being stolen and damaged, resulting in less collection, the original station cannot bear it, thus we decided that those who leave messages should bring their own pens. We apologize for any inconvenience. ―― Yanchang Station

6. I am a scalper, currently selling long-distance tickets to Shanghai, Nanjing, Nanchang, Hangzhou, etc. Interested parties please call 13XXXXXXXXX.

Classic reply one: Young man, it's an invalid number.

Reply two: I thought I could make some money!

Station response: We hope you are not playing around, otherwise, please report yourself to the relevant department. Contact number: 110 ―― Kaifeng Station

7. At 15:00 PM, Nanjing time, I will place a bomb on the 17th seat of the third row on the right side of the entrance to the fourth waiting hall. There are a red and blue wire attached. The safety code is in the XX trash can, please exchange it with the ticket placed at XX (forgot the exact location). ―― Nanjing Central Station

8. Could you please tell me whether your train 2239 will be on time?

Station reply: Please report to the railway department, our station does not have the authority to explain. ―― Liangguang Station

9. Your service attitude is too good.

(The classic part of this sentence lies in its brevity and broad coverage. The writer was extremely angry, tearing dozens of pages of the complaint book. Some even displayed artistic talent by drawing vivid pictures of turtles, snakes, cats chasing mice, etc.)

Station Classic Reply: We haven't started improving yet, and we criticize employees who don't perform well regularly. We apologize for the inconvenience caused by the oversight of our station staff. Regarding foreign railways, I have already made comments, silently observing and waiting. Hopefully, one day, foreign railways will truly improve. Just opened a space, come and take a look! At this point, I might as well just take a plane, like Bi Fugan naming his child -- too strong, or burying myself underground. ―― All major and minor stations nationwide
