Post-90s少年 was disfigured by burns after failing to win the love of a 17-year-old girl

by uelu6c6b on 2012-02-27 09:10:40

Comparison of Zhou Yan before and after being burned (picture from family's Weibo)

China Daily News message (Ge Qiwei, Li Hongyang) On September 17, 2011, this day was destined to become the biggest nightmare in the life of 17-year-old Zhou Yan. Before this, she was just like an ordinary teenager, studying at Shouchun Middle School in Hefei, Anhui Province, as an ordinary student. Due to rejecting the advances of a male classmate surnamed Tao, she was doused with lighter fuel and set on fire by him, resulting in disfigurement. Zhou Yan suffered severe burns on her face, neck, and chest; one of her ears was also burned off, with the overall burn area exceeding 30%.

The young girl refused his love and was severely burned and disfigured. With no money for treatment, her family posted a plea for help online.

On February 24, when the post "Flower-seasoned Girl Refuses Love and is Burned and Disfigured, Please Save My Child" was broadcast on Weibo, it instantly caught widespread attention from netizens, and within a short time, it was forwarded tens of thousands of times.

According to Ms. Li, Zhou Yan's mother [Weibo], around 6 p.m. on September 17, 2011, Tao, who studied at the same middle school as Zhou Yan, carried lighter fuel to Zhou's home due to unrequited love. Taking advantage of Zhou Yan's unawareness, he poured the prepared oil onto her head and ignited it. After being burned, Zhou Yan was rushed to the intensive care unit of Anhui Medical University Affiliated Hospital where she underwent seven days and nights of emergency treatment to escape life-threatening danger. However, her injuries were extremely serious, with severe burns on her head, face, neck, and chest. One of her ears was also burned off, with the burn area exceeding 30%, reaching second and third-degree burns. Her entire appearance was completely disfigured.

Ms. Li also mentioned that while setting her on fire, Tao was continuously shouting "Die." Zhou Yan's aunt discovered her and extinguished the fire before calling the police and seeking medical help. During this period, Tao remained indifferent.

Ms. Li stated that Tao's parents initially paid for Zhou Yan's hospitalization and treatment fees. Later, Tao's parents presented a document acknowledging Tao's active rescue efforts and self-surrender on the day of the incident, requesting the Li family's signature for bail. Since it did not match the objective facts, the Li family refused to sign. Subsequently, Tao's parents stopped paying the high medical expenses. Helpless, Zhou Yan's family had to discharge her from the hospital, still owing nearly ten thousand yuan in medical expenses. Watching their teenage daughter with little hope of recovery and possibly lifelong disability, facing unaffordable high medical costs, the Li family fell into extreme grief. With Ms. Li's consent, her contact number is now published: 15156877191. Netizens, let us offer more care and attention to give this family a hope.

It is reported that the Hefei Public Security Department has intervened in handling this matter, and Tao is currently still detained in a detention center in Hefei.
