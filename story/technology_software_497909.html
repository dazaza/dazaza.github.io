The Method and Steps of Vacuuming the Cooling/Heating System of Gree Split Air Conditioners

The process of vacuuming the cooling/heating system of a whole air conditioner is the same as the operation method and steps for vacuuming the refrigeration system of a refrigerator. Here, we will focus on explaining the method and steps for vacuuming the cooling/heating system of a split air conditioner. Split-type air conditioners are equipped with shut-off valves on the outdoor unit, so when vacuuming the system, there is no need to use gas welding for pipe connections, making the operation relatively simpler.

There are many methods for vacuuming the refrigeration and heating systems of air conditioners, but the simplest and most practical method still involves using a three-way repair valve in conjunction with a vacuum pump.

Step 1: Turn on the vacuum pump and open the three-way repair valve. The air inside the system is extracted, and the needle on the pressure/vacuum gauge of the three-way repair valve gradually deflects toward the negative pressure direction, indicating that the pressure within the system is lower than atmospheric pressure.

Step 2: After the vacuum pump has been running for more than 30 minutes and the reading on the pressure/vacuum gauge reaches -0.1MPa, first close the repair valve, then turn off the vacuum pump. To ensure all residual gases are removed and the vacuum level meets the requirements, wait 20 minutes after closing and stopping the vacuum pump until the system pressure balances, then perform another vacuum cycle. When performing the second vacuum, start the vacuum pump first, then open the three-way repair valve. After the vacuum pump runs for another 30 minutes, again close the repair valve first, then turn off the vacuum pump.

Step 3: Remove the vacuum pump from the three-way repair valve and connect it to a refrigerant cylinder.

Step 4: Loosen the nut connecting the three-way valve to the connection pipe (the one connected to the refrigerant cylinder).

Step 5: Slightly open the refrigerant cylinder to purge the air from the connection pipe, then tighten the nut.

Step 6: Open the three-way repair valve and fully open the refrigerant cylinder to charge the refrigerant gas into the refrigeration system. When the pressure gauge reads around 0.1MPa, close both the refrigerant cylinder and the three-way repair valve, and reconnect the vacuum pump.

Step 7: Wait about 20 minutes to allow the system pressure to balance, then restart the vacuum pump and open the three-way repair valve to vacuum for another 30 minutes. Once completed, the vacuuming process is finished.
