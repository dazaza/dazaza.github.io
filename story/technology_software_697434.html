How to inspect the condition of a used car?

by anonymous on 2012-03-08 15:33:44

Buying imported second-hand cars in Huangjiang, Dongguan: Identifying a used car is a highly technical task. It's best to bring along an experienced driver for advice when checking out a car, or go to a second-hand car appraisal center with equipment for evaluation. Additionally, be particularly cautious of accident cars that have been involved in major traffic accidents, as the body strength after repairs does not meet the original safety design standards set by the manufacturer, failing to effectively protect the safety of the passengers. Moreover, it significantly affects the price of second-hand cars.

Exterior Inspection:

Check if the car paint color is consistent, if the surface curvature on both sides is smooth. Look at the reflection on the paint surface from a 30-45 degree angle to see if it looks reasonable. If there are differences, the car must have been repaired, and the repair quality is poor. Place the car on level ground, stand 3-5 meters in front of the car, and observe if the shoulders of the car are equally high. If they are not, it indicates the car frame has been repaired or the suspension and shock absorbers are not properly fixed. Check if the seam between the engine hood and the fenders is even, if the gaps around the door edges are consistent, if the headlights and tail lights' seams with metal parts are the same, and if their newness levels match. Check if all glass brands are identical. Manually open and close all doors, opening them to 45-60 degrees, then close them with normal force to check if the doors close tightly, if the sound and effort are consistent.

Interior Inspection:

Check if the seats and interior are clean, if they have been replaced or disassembled. Check if all headrests and decorations are complete, if all switches operate smoothly without issues. Be extra cautious if the broker has refurbished the seats and interior, as this car might have serious problems. Before ignition, first open the key door and observe if the instrument panel lights display normally, and if any displays are missing. Most fuel-injected cars have fault warning light prompts. When the key door is opened, all indicator lights should turn on. If any indicator light doesn't light up, the owner may have intentionally removed the bulb due to unresolved faults to confuse buyers.

Test Drive:

Check if the car starts easily. If the start sounds heavy, it indicates issues with the starter motor, battery, or related mechanisms. After starting, check if the steering wheel (with power assist) has consistent force when turning left and right, if the turning angle is reasonable, and if the front wheels grind against the tires when fully turned. This could indicate the car has been in an accident, and tire rubbing can pose a threat to driving safety. For manual transmission cars, judge the clutch condition and whether replacement is needed based on mileage and clutch height. To evaluate automatic transmissions, feel if gear shifts are smooth. Also, observe if the engine RPM and speed are within the manufacturer's specified range during gear shifts. Pay attention to the noise source and volume while driving to assess the car's sealing and soundproofing effects. Additionally, check if the engine, transmission, differential, and suspension systems make abnormal noises. If the engine makes "thud" or "clang" sounds, or the transmission and differential make "clatter" sounds, these components need major repairs. For the braking system, focus on the braking distance and if the brakes pull to one side. Also, check if the ABS drags. Parking brake tests should be conducted on a slope, testing both uphill and downhill.

Even senior second-hand car appraisers need professional tools and equipment to more reasonably evaluate the true value of inspected vehicles. However, for ordinary consumers with relatively less automotive knowledge, buying a second-hand car without being misled is quite challenging. Imported second-hand cars in Huangjiang, Dongguan suggest consumers bring a few knowledgeable friends for inspection.
