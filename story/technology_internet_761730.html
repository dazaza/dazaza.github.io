Pony Ma: The mobile Internet trend is fierce, and it's an opportunity for reshuffling.

by geekzhang on 2013-05-08 11:49:30

At today's GMIC, CCTV's "Dialogue" program moved its studio onto the stage, and Pony Ma changed from his usual plain speaking style of last year, delivering a textbook-style interview with a well-prepared host.

WeChat was the hottest topic, and is also one of the most popular applications. Naturally, the topic of WeChat charges led the discussion.

Operators are not a big problem

In the month when the WeChat charge issue was at its peak, there was a rumor that Pony Ma was questioned by a police officer about the authenticity of the WeChat charge rumors while submitting materials at an airport. It can be seen that the gossip of an application has become a social hot news item, demonstrating its influence.

During that month, various rumors were rampant, and the closest to a conspiracy theory was the guess: could this be Tencent's marketing strategy? Pony Ma's response was direct: we are not a company skilled in hype. In his view, this is a relationship issue between an operator and an Internet service provider, and these two should be interdependent and mutually beneficial.

Facing the conflict with operators, Pony Ma answered in his usual way: cooperation is possible, there's been some misunderstanding.

The debate over WeChat charges stems from the occupation of communication resources, and the emergence of this situation needs to be attributed to cost issues. Pony Ma believes that these problems will ultimately fall on cost-sharing, which cannot be solved by any one company alone but requires the entire industry chain to overcome together. In fact, in today's rapidly developing mobile Internet era, the issue of network resource occupation has appeared in several countries, and some solutions worth learning from have emerged.

He said that Tencent has already found a preliminary solution, and it's not a big problem.

WeChat Business Model? Mobile Social Games Are the Biggest Selling Point

WeChat's user base is close to four hundred million, and merchants and third-party platforms have flocked to WeChat's embrace. People have also begun to focus on the ways WeChat commercializes.

The audience's question was straightforward: how to make money with WeChat? The discussion on WeChat's profit model has been very popular recently, and Pony Ma finally gave the standard answer: from the perspective of the open platform and industry chain, mobile social games are the biggest selling point.

The technical principle of WeChat is simple, and its basic functions are very similar to QQ's corporate account. Why does only WeChat have such charm? Upon careful consideration, it's not hard to find that what distinguishes WeChat from QQ's corporate account is photo sharing and voice recording; this method of information transmission is different from PCs.

For Pony Ma, all they're doing now is building rules; we don't know what kind of creativity users and partners will come up with.

Talking about making games and building platforms, someone began to worry: how does Tencent ensure that it won't favor its own products, and how does it guarantee the fairness of platform operations?

We also have things we can't do, shouldn't do, and aren't capable of doing. For example, content industries and game development must rely on partners.

Pony Ma emphasized that he pays more attention to building economic systems and platforms, allowing partners to develop freely on them, perhaps leading to many unexpected new methods. Perhaps, opportunities still need to be given to partners, with the focus on O2O, offline merchants, and grassroots, but eventually everything will be connected to WeChat.

Mobile QQ is a treasure, just a couple of years late

Initially, Tencent had two teams simultaneously developing an application called WeChat. The team that finished first and did better officially got the name WeChat, while the other was renamed Qxin. The criterion for choice was still user experience: could it retain people within a few seconds? This is the most direct interpretation of product experience.

Talking about product strategies inevitably brings up the predicament faced by Mobile QQ. In Pony Ma's eyes, this is a very sensitive issue, and this issue boils down to organizational structure.

Surprisingly, the number of active accounts on Mobile QQ is nearly twice that of WeChat, which is a great treasure, but we haven't done well in the past, being a couple of years late. Due to this issue, Tencent recently made a large-scale organizational restructuring, the largest in seven years, showing the urgency of the problem. Currently, the QQ product line is also under adjustment to fully tap into this wealth.

This is an opportunity for reshuffling

Our greatest opportunity comes from cross-border integration.

Tencent is a company with cross-border genes. Initially, QQ's Chinese name was "Network Pager," a product of the fusion between the telecommunications industry and the Internet. History always repeats itself - now WeChat is also a cross-border product of mobile communications and mobile Internet.

The discussion ultimately shifted to predictions about the future and grasping trends. In Pony Ma's eyes, although the trends are fierce, the business models of most companies are still stuck in the PC Internet era.

It must be said that Tencent's ability to seize opportunities is unmatched. Now, out of 800 million active QQ users, 550 million are mobile QQ users, which is the big wave of mobile Internet, and this wave is sweeping the entire industry with overwhelming momentum.

This is a great business opportunity, and also an opportunity for reshuffling.
