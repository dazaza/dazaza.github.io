Introduction:

Main Cast: Aaron Kwok / Ruby Lin / Simon Yam / Charmaine Sheh / Kent Cheng / Gordon Lam

A murder case occurred at Peninsula Mansion, where retired police officer Tai was killed. When the police arrived at the scene, they found Inspector Ling Yun (Aaron Kwok) unconscious and injured on the seventh floor. Various signs indicated that this case was related to recent serial killer cases. However, Ling Yun woke up from his coma with no memory of what had happened.

Ling Yun lived with his wife (Ruby Lin) and adopted son in a high-end residence in the picturesque Sa Tsoi Uk area. Their originally peaceful life changed due to the serial killings. In order to quickly recover his lost short-term memory and understand why he was at the crime scene, Ling Yun led his colleagues in earnestly investigating the truth behind the serial killer cases.

However, every time Ling Yun got closer to the murderer, he would discover some eerie clues in his own home. These unexpected events made him start doubting the relationship between the killer and himself. Meanwhile, the police had also gathered a large amount of evidence against the murderer, all of which were related to Ling Yun.

Facing doubts from his colleagues and family, as well as mounting pressures, Ling Yun teetered on the brink of mental collapse. How will Ling Yun recover his memory? How will he prove his innocence? Why is he so entangled with the serial case? Where is the cruel serial killer? Ling Yun is deeply confused, and the fog of the case unfolds layer by layer.

Ling Guang (Aaron Kwok) is a young and promising Chief Inspector of the Serious Crimes Unit. He is a model figure in the police force, having solved many bizarre cases over the years. He is about to be promoted to Superintendent, making him one of the most promising candidates for the top position in the police force. Ling Guang has a happy family: his beautiful and gentle wife Cheng Xi Ai (Ruby Lin), and their lively and adorable son. They live an elegant lifestyle. However, this picture-perfect life was completely rewritten due to a series of murders.

During the investigation of a serial murder case, Ling Guang suddenly collapsed. Upon waking up, he discovered not only had he lost his short-term memory, but his good partner had also died at the same scene, becoming the third victim of the recent bloody serial killings. The murderer used the most brutal and cold-blooded methods each time, causing the victims to bleed to death. After each killing, the murderer vanished without a trace, leaving the police completely helpless.

Encountering this historically brutal murder case just before his promotion, Ling Guang naturally spared no effort in capturing the murderer and seeking justice for his deceased partner. However, during the investigation, he found that every clue and piece of evidence pointed towards him being the murderer. The deeper he investigated, the more suspicion fell on him. Ling Guang's short-term memory did not recover, and gradually, even the only trustworthy evidence - himself - could not prove his innocence.

Step by step, Ling Guang was forced towards the edge of madness... Is he the murderer?

Behind-the-Scenes Production:

"Crime and Punishment" is directed by newcomer Ray Chan, who apprenticed under Ang Lee. Popular screenwriter Du Zhilang, known for works like "Gangster", "Fearless", and "Secret", created the most thrilling and mysterious detective story in the past decade for the film. Two-time Golden Horse Award-winning actor Aaron Kwok was deeply attracted by the script and joined wholeheartedly, challenging new performance heights.

The Golden Horse Award-winning cinematographer Mark Li Ping-bin ("In the Mood for Love", "A Woman, Age Forty", "A City of Sadness"), art director Man-kit Wan ("Maybe Someday", "Infernal Affairs"), editor Ka-Fai Cheung ("Kill Zone", "Flash Point", "Ip Man"), and renowned Asian composer Ryuichi Sakamoto ("2046") formed a solid behind-the-scenes team. They created action-packed scenes, realistic visual effects, and mysterious film music, creating a tense, suspenseful, and strange atmosphere for the film. It should be the most anticipated police thriller of 2009.

The poster for "Murderer" was shot by renowned photographer Wing Shya. The look in Aaron Kwok's eyes in the poster is chilling. To capture Kwok's fierce and crazed gaze, Wing Shya waited patiently on set. Near the end of filming, Aaron Kwok hadn't slept for three days straight, looking entirely brutal, perfectly fitting the character's personality. On the last day of shooting, Kwok cultivated his emotions by mostly sitting quietly aside, speaking very little, with a very fierce expression. Even the crew members exclaimed, "Don't provoke Aaron Kwok."
