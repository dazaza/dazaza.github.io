G5 Review: Competing for the Jingzhou territory, a major gray market phone to be released in July.

by anonymous on 2012-02-29 14:43:33

During the Three Kingdoms period, Jingzhou was known as the "belly of the land," with its four-way transportation network and extremely fertile land. Its strategic position was extremely important, hence the saying "to rule the world, one must first take Jingzhou." Turning back to the present day, in July, the middle of the year, it is the most crucial time for the digital product market, and also the best time for grey-market smartphone manufacturers to offer discounts and launch new products. The hot-selling products from the first half of the year would boost sales in July during the "summer promotion," while major brands' key new models would be released one after another to capture the second-half market. Therefore, July is undoubtedly the "Jingzhou" in the eyes of various grey-market smartphone manufacturers. Although obtaining dominance in July does not necessarily mean ruling the world in the grey-market smartphone industry, the importance of July can significantly influence the final results of grey-market smartphone manufacturers this year and their future development trends. During this July, multiple brands successively announced dozens of new models, among which there were quite a few popular flagship models. These models are expected to occupy a considerable share of the market in the competitive landscape of the second half of the year. Below, let's review with the author which powerful new models came out to vie for the "Jingzhou."

Striving for Jingzhou: Reviewing the Powerful New Models Released in July

Nokia C6

This month, Nokia launched several new models, among which the hottest one currently on the market is undoubtedly the Nokia C6. It not only continues the best-selling style of the 5230 but also incorporates many fashionable and personalized elements. The side-sliding keyboard design has won the favor of numerous consumers, drawing significant attention immediately upon its release.

The Nokia C6 adopts a side-sliding full keyboard design, making the body appear individualistic and fashionable. It features a 3.2-inch 16 million color TFT resistive touch screen with a resolution of 360x640, providing fine display effects and good touch control. The system runs Symbian 9.4 S60 5.0 intelligent operating system, benefiting from a vast amount of software resources, offering excellent expandability. The device is equipped with an ARM11 processor with a frequency of 434 MHz, with a body memory of 512MB ROM + 128MB RAM, though the overall operation speed still needs improvement. It also features a 5-megapixel camera with good imaging quality. Bluetooth, WIFI, GPS functions are all fully available, showcasing comprehensive performance.

Editor's Comment: The Nokia C6 can be called the "upgraded slide version" of the Nokia 5230. After this upgrade, the Nokia C6 directly competes head-to-head with the Nokia N97mini. Whether in terms of price or overall performance, both are very similar. In the second half of the year, the market is likely to see a significant market share for the Nokia C6, which will leave many fans scratching their heads trying to decide which one to buy. Since its release, the price of the Nokia C6 has quickly dropped, with a significant decrease. Currently, the cost-performance ratio is not yet prominent, making it worth waiting for a better deal.

Apple iPhone 3GS 8GB

At the end of June, Apple released the iPhone 4, with its completely new system and functional upgrades capturing global attention. Meanwhile, Apple also launched a new version of the iPhone 3GS 8GB. What differences exist between this model and other versions of the iPhone 3GS besides capacity? Let's understand it together now.

The iPhone 3GS 8GB uses a 3.5-inch 16 million color TFT screen like other versions, with a resolution of 320x480, supporting multi-touch, offering different gaming experiences. The camera also uses a 3-megapixel CMOS camera, supporting auto-focus, digital zoom, and photo effects, with commendable photography results. The biggest difference of this version lies in its operating system. It runs the latest iOS 4 intelligent operating system, the same as the iPhone 4. With its extensive software resources and newly added features, all these can be realized on this version. Paired with an ARM Coretex A8 processor with a frequency of 660 MHz, the experience becomes easy and smooth.

Editor's Comment: At first glance, the iPhone 3GS 8GB looks no different from other versions of the iPhone 3GS, just differing in capacity. However, the new iOS 4 system it carries is indeed exciting. Currently, the iPhone 4 has just been released, with high prices and continuous negative news. On the other hand, the iPhone 3GS 8GB, running the same system as the iPhone 4, wins consumer recognition due to its more stable performance. With the sale of the Hong Kong version of the iPhone 4, the price of the iPhone 3GS 8GB is expected to drop further.

Motorola ME511

Motorola's design has always been outstanding in the industry. Personally, I think its appearance style is much more comfortable than other brands. They can design body appearances that perfectly fit different target consumer groups. The cheerful Ming series and the fashionable RAZR series all demonstrate Motorola's profound design and craftsmanship. Now, the new Motorola ME511 rotates onto the stage.

The Motorola ME511 adopts a 45-key full keyboard and rotatable touchscreen design. It is equipped with a 2.8-inch 260K color TFT capacitive touchscreen, supporting multi-touch, providing a very comfortable touch experience, though the screen quality remains at a mid-level. It features a 3-megapixel camera, with average image quality but sufficient for daily casual shooting. The system runs Android OS v2.1 intelligent operating system, with user interface MOTO BLUR, both software extendability and application entertainment being commendable. It is configured with a Qualcomm MSM7200 processor with a frequency of 528 MHz, with notable overall operation speed. Additionally, functions such as Bluetooth, WIFI, GPS, etc., are all included.

Editor's Comment: The appearance of the Motorola ME511 makes young consumer groups who love to express themselves and have vitality shout excitedly. It further verifies that Motorola's design style can well match its target consumer groups. The unique rotating touchscreen, paired with various colored shells, plus the smart operating system, and other complete functions, truly make it irresistible.

Nokia 5235

In recent years, Nokia's marketing strategies have truly troubled some consumers. Repeated revisions and transformations of a best-selling model, then pushing the derived new models back into the market, and even achieving a good market response. Here we go again; Nokia 5235, the younger brother of the Nokia 5230, has "transformed" in July.

The Nokia 5235 adopts a straight-board full-touchscreen design, looking identical to the Nokia 5230. It is equipped with a 3.2-inch 260K color TFT resistive touchscreen, with a resolution of 360x640, showing average display effects, belonging to the current mid-low configuration. The system runs Symbian 9.4 S60 5.0 intelligent operating system, with relatively rich application software resources. It features a 2-megapixel camera, supporting 3x digital zoom, with imaging quality needing improvement. The available body space is 70MB, with a maximum expansion capacity of 16GB.

Editor's Comment: The Nokia 5235 is derived from the Nokia 5230, and I have lost count of how many deformed brothers of the Nokia 5230 there are. They are almost identical, except that the Nokia 5235 adds Ovi music store services, with hardware being entirely the same. The current price of 1499 yuan tests the psychology of consumers.

BlackBerry 8910 News:

On July 28, 2010, BlackBerry (BlackBerry), the only brand globally specializing in intelligent grey-market smartphones, grandly opened its first BlackBerry product experience retail store in China at Joy City Mall located in Xidan Commercial Center, Beijing. This marks BlackBerry's official entry into China's domestic intelligent grey-market smartphone market, with the main promotion of BlackBerry 8910.

The BlackBerry 8910 maintains the traditional straight-board full keyboard design, with a slightly thick and atmospheric body. It is equipped with a 2.44-inch TFT screen, with a resolution of 480x360, showing average display effects. The system runs BlackBerry OS 5.0 intelligent operating system, with a body memory of 256MB ROM + 256MB RAM, satisfying overall operation speed, and good software extendability. It features a 3.2-megapixel CMOS camera, supporting auto-focus, with average imaging quality sufficient for daily life shooting. Additionally, GPS, Bluetooth, etc., are all included, with the regret being that the domestic version cancels the WIFI function.

Editor's Comment: The release of the domestic BlackBerry 8910 signifies BlackBerry's official entry into China's domestic intelligent grey-market smartphone market, delighting numerous fans. BlackBerry grey-market smartphones enjoy a good reputation and numerous consumers in China, combined with BlackBerry's mature intelligent system, which will inevitably spark another wave of competition in China's domestic intelligent grey-market smartphone market. With the dense release of domestic BlackBerry grey-market smartphones, what will be the future direction of grey-market BlackBerry grey-market smartphones?

Nokia C3

After seeing the picture of the Nokia C3, I believe many netizens will carefully check the model number of the phone again. Yes, it is the Nokia C3, not a new product from Nokia's E series. Compared to the numerous intelligent models in the E series, the Nokia C3 wins the recognition of numerous consumers through its mature, stable, and extremely smooth S40 system.

The Nokia C3 adopts the currently popular straight-board full keyboard design, although belonging to the C series, it has a style very similar to Nokia's E series. It is equipped with a 2.4-inch 260K color TFT screen, with a resolution of 240x320, currently considered mid-range equipment. The system runs Nokia's Symbian S40 system, which is not an intelligent system, but the software resources of the S40 system are still relatively abundant. The body memory is 128MB ROM + 64MB RAM, with very smooth overall operation speed, worthy of being called a grey-market smartphone running the S40 system. It features a 2-megapixel camera on the back, with average imaging quality sufficient for daily life shooting.

Editor's Comment: The appearance of the Nokia C3 brings the first word that flashes in my mind "cross-series". Yes, its style is very similar to Nokia's E series. Although it is a non-intelligent S40 system grey-market smartphone, the quantity of application software resources is still relatively large, and it also features WIFI functionality. Please note that this phone only supports GSM networks and does not support 3G networks.

Samsung I7680

Although there is still a long time before the Oscar Award Ceremony begins, "Oscar" has indeed arrived. It is Samsung I7680. As the latest representative of Samsung's business models, it is endowed with advanced hardware configurations in multiple aspects. Moreover, as a customized model by China Mobile, it seems that "Oscar" is determined to secure a place in the July market.

The Samsung I7680 adopts a straight-board touch design, with a stylish and steady body. It features a 3.5-inch 16 million color AMOLED capacitive touchscreen, with a resolution as high as 480x800, supporting multi-touch, offering excellent display effects. Just from the screen perspective, this is currently one of the top-tier configurations. The system first runs the OMS 2.0 intelligent operating system, with the richness of software resources still to be evaluated. It is equipped with a processor with a frequency of 720MHz, supporting 3D acceleration, with smooth overall operation speed, providing an extremely operational feeling. It features a 5-megapixel camera, supporting auto-focus and 4x digital zoom, with good imaging quality. There is also a 300K secondary camera on the screen for video calls. Additionally, Bluetooth, WIFI, GPS, etc., are all included.

Editor's Comment: The Chinese name of Samsung I7680 is Oscar, from which we can see the market positioning and hardware configuration status of the phone. It is a TD-SCDMA customized grey-market smartphone by China Mobile, not only featuring very rich mobile value-added services but may also introduce a "zero-yuan purchase" campaign for this phone in the future. Therefore, Oscar is worth the anticipation of many business people.

Summary:

Ancient wisdom had Cao Cao say, "If I were to have a son, I would want him to be like Sun Zhongmou." Today, we say, "If I were to make a phone, I would want it to be like the iPhone guy." The release of the Apple iPhone 4 was like a heavy bomb from Apple, not only stunning numerous consumers but also greatly shaking up the various grey-market smartphone manufacturers in the industry. Subsequently, various grey-market smartphone manufacturers launched their own key new models this year to counter Apple's sweeping global trend. The Nokia C6, Motorola ME511, Sharp SH-07B, etc., once released, gained good market heat, and it is believed that they will soon occupy a considerable market share. Now, the grey-market smartphone brands divide the world into three parts, and so do the intelligent systems. The contention for Jingzhou will continue, allowing consumers to purchase grey-market smartphones that are both affordable and practical.
