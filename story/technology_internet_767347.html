Rumored Summary of Apple's iPad 5: Large Screen and Wireless Charging Are Unreliable

by anonymous on 2013-10-12 19:21:18

If there is one big event left in the digital product domain for 2013, it must be the release of the new Apple iPad. This year, Apple is expected to launch two new iPads — the iPad 5 and the iPad mini 2. Both will receive a certain degree of upgrade. Recently, rumors and real photos of the iPad 5 have frequently surfaced. If everything goes as planned, we will see its appearance on October 22. Below is the "final version" of rumors about the Apple iPad 5. After reading this article, all that's left to do is patiently wait.

High-resolution iPad 5 photos leaked, confirming the basic design

The latest news comes from October 9, when high-definition images of the iPad 5 were first leaked online. From the details of the pictures, it can essentially be called an "enlarged version of the iPad mini." The device features a narrow bezel screen, with a significantly reduced width, thickness, and weight, making it more portable. It seems that Apple has overcome the design challenges posed by Retina display and large-capacity batteries. Additionally, the color options for the body shell are likely to follow those of the iPhone 5s, including the new deep space gray and silver/white, though whether a "gold" option exists remains unknown for now.

Release date: Locked on October 22

According to the renowned IT blog AllThingsD, Apple will hold a press conference in the Bay Area of San Francisco on October 22. There, they are highly likely to release the iPad 5, iPad mini 2, a new MacBook Pro, and officially introduce OS X Mavericks. Based on past experiences, AllThingsD’s reports on Apple-related news tend to be quite reliable, so the date of October 22 is definitely worth looking forward to.

New features: Touch ID?

A new video was exposed on the internet, showing some details of the front panel of the iPad 5, hinting that the iPad 5 may feature the Touch ID function. Furthermore, according to iOS 7 code leaks from the well-known Apple blog 9to5Mac, the new iPad will also have this feature. Considering that this function is one of the key features of this year's iPhone 5s, Apple has ample reason to integrate it into the new iPad, forming a comprehensive ecosystem and laying the foundation for the future widespread application of fingerprint recognition technology.

Hardware: 64-bit A7 processor

This year, the iPhone 5s has already been equipped with the latest 64-bit A7 processor; following the usual practice, the next generation iPad should adopt the most recent processor with enhanced graphics capabilities, namely the A7X. Therefore, at the very least, the iPad 5 should come with the A7 processor. There are also reports that the iPad mini 2 will have a high-end version that also includes the latest A7 processor. For Apple, the 64-bit iOS 7 represents a new phase in the development of iOS, with various apps beginning to adapt to 64-bit processors. Thus, popularizing this in new models to achieve a unified user experience will be a key focus moving forward.

Screen: 13 inches not likely

Whether it's the leaked high-definition images or related parameters, they indicate that the iPad 5 will still use a 9.7-inch, 2048*1536 resolution Retina screen. However, rumors about larger-sized iPads have become increasingly intense recently. At least for the iPad 5, we won't see a 13-inch screen. Perhaps next year will be the year when Apple increases the screen sizes of their iOS devices and adds more models, with both a 13-inch iPad and a 5-inch iPhone appearing.

Wireless charging: Not likely in the short term

The well-known Apple product blog appleinsider once pointed out that Apple would equip its iPad products with wireless charging functionality, achieved through a "protective cover integrated with inductive charging." Similar to how you use the current Smart Cover or Case, closing the lid would turn off the iPad screen and begin charging. Previously leaked patents related to Apple seem to confirm this idea. However, we believe that Apple will not launch such products in the near term.

Price: Likely to continue the tradition

Based on past experiences, the iPad 5 will continue with the pricing of the current iPad 4, starting at $499 (approximately RMB 3054) for the 16GB WIFI version before tax, with domestic prices starting at RMB 3688. Additionally, a 128GB capacity version might be launched simultaneously, priced at $800 (approximately RMB 4895). If the price exceeds previous levels, then perhaps this could be the biggest "surprise" of the iPad 5.
