The Economic and Technical Cooperation Bureau of Cangxi County was established in 1987 and mainly takes charge of the county’s internal and external openness, investment promotion, regional economic cooperation, organization, and participation in county-wide economic and technical cooperation and investment promotion projects, bottleneck issues, tracking services, etc.; responsible for formulating the overall plan and annual goals for the county's economic and technical cooperation and coordinating work; optimizing the investment environment; participating in research and investigation, establishing and improving the information network, providing consulting services, compiling statistical data.

Investment Environment and Preferential Policies

Introduction to Cangxi County

Cangxi County is located on the northern edge of the Sichuan Basin, at the southern foot of the Qinba Mountains, in the middle reaches of the Jialing River. To the east is Bazhong, to the west is Mianyang, to the south is Nanchong, and to the north is Guangyuan. The population is 780,000, covering 39 towns and 554 villages, with an area of 2,330 square kilometers. Cangxi is rich in natural resources, with large yields and high-quality agricultural products. Notable products include Cangxi Snow Pears, red heart kiwis, crisp sweet pomelos, Sichuan bright ginseng, silkworm cocoons, mushrooms, asparagus, green beans, etc. Annually, the county produces 400,000 tons of high-quality grain, 45,000 tons of oilseeds, 145,000 tons of fruit, 640 tons of silkworm cocoons, keeps 4 million rabbits, and raises 1.2 million pigs and 12 million small poultry.

The Nine-Dragon Mountain Natural Gas Reserve contains 3 billion cubic meters, while the Yuanba No. 1 Well Natural Gas Reserve is estimated to hold 8 trillion cubic meters. The Jialing River and Songjiang River run through the entire territory, with a hydropower reserve of 1.1 million kilowatts. The Tingzi Kou Hydropower Hub Project and the Cangxi Navigation Power Project, with a total installed capacity of 900,000 kilowatts, have already started preliminary construction. Famous scenic spots such as the Red Army Crossing, Linjiang Temple, Yancong Temple, Dahuo Lotus Pond, Chongxia Pagoda, and Xunle Book Rock leave unforgettable impressions.

In recent years, Cangxi has vigorously implemented a private economy-led strategy, with four pillar industries—food and beverage processing, hydroelectric energy, pharmaceuticals, and silk textiles—developing rapidly, forming five major industrial chains: canned raw materials, livestock meat products, high-quality grains and oils, sericulture and silk, and Chinese medicinal herbs.

Cangxi has convenient transportation, with National Highway 212 and the Northern Sichuan Tourism Ring Road directly connecting the entire region. The Lanzhou-Chongqing Railway started construction in 2007 and connects with the Baocheng Railway. The urban and rural living environment continues to improve, with the county's mountain-water garden city construction initially taking shape, and the provincial-level health city creation achieving standard compliance. The construction of beautiful ecological demonstration towns has made remarkable achievements, and the results of urban spiritual civilization construction rank among the best in the province. Commerce is prosperous and active, with the largest county-level commerce and logistics center in northern Sichuan built, and the annual total social consumption goods retail amount close to 1 billion yuan.

In encouraging investment and attracting foreign investment, apart from the national preferential policies for the development of the western region, Cangxi County has also formulated flexible, open, and preferential economic policies, warmly welcoming domestic and foreign investors to come and invest, develop, and negotiate cooperation.

Cangxi County's Encouragement Policy for Investment and Industry Development (Summary)

1. Tax and Financial Preferences

(a) For enterprises engaged in national encouraged industries located in our county, if their main business revenue accounts for more than 70% of the enterprise's total income, during the period from 2005 to 2010, upon approval, the enterprise income tax will be levied at a rate of 15%.

(b) For new traffic, power, water conservancy, postal, and radio and television enterprises in our county, if the business income of these projects accounts for more than 70% of the enterprise's total income, they can enjoy the following corporate income tax preferential policies: domestic-funded enterprises will be exempt from corporate income tax for the first two years from the start of production and operation, and will be subject to half the corporate income tax for the third to fifth years; foreign-invested enterprises with an operating period of more than 10 years will be exempt from corporate income tax for the first two years from the year of profit, and will be subject to half the corporate income tax for the third to fifth years.

(c) New or reinvestment enterprises with an operating period of more than 10 years, fixed asset investments exceeding 5 million yuan for production and processing type enterprises, and fixed asset investments exceeding 30 million yuan for large-scale commercial and logistics enterprises, in the third year, the same-level finance will arrange special expenditures for supporting enterprise development according to the local portion of the paid corporate income tax. If the annual sales reach over 30 million yuan, in the fourth to seventh years, the same-level finance will arrange special expenditures for supporting enterprise development according to 50% of the local portion of the paid corporate income tax. Enterprises starting up long-term shutdown, long-term loss-making, or severely insolvent enterprises within our county by means of investment, purchase, merger, and other methods, and employing more than 30% of the original enterprise employees, can also enjoy the above-mentioned policies.

(d) New high-tech enterprises and agricultural industrialization leading enterprises with an operating period of more than 10 years, from the beginning of production and operation, in the first to fourth years, the same-level finance will arrange special expenditures for supporting enterprise development according to the local portion of the paid corporate income tax. In the fifth to eighth years, the same-level finance will arrange 70% of the local portion of the paid corporate income tax as special expenditures for supporting enterprise development.

(e) New high-tech, industrial production, agricultural industrialization, and large-scale commercial and logistics enterprises with an operating period of more than 10 years, and annual sales reaching over 30 million yuan, from the start of realizing sales revenue, within three years, upon approval by the same-level government, the same-level finance will arrange special expenditures for supporting enterprise development according to 50% of the local portion of the annually paid value-added tax. For enterprises with annual sales reaching over 50 million yuan, from the fourth to sixth year after realizing sales revenue, the same-level finance will annually arrange special expenditures for supporting enterprise development according to 50% of the local portion of the paid value-added tax.

(f) Production and processing enterprises, agricultural industrialization leading enterprises, large-scale commercial and logistics projects, and important urban infrastructure projects using more than 400 (including 400) laborers from our county, upon government approval, will receive a working capital interest subsidy support of 2 million yuan for three years starting from the date of production.

2. Land Use Preferences

(a) All land prices for production and processing enterprises and large-scale commercial and logistics enterprises can be charged at no less than 70% of the benchmark land price of the used land.

(b) For production and processing enterprises, agricultural industrialization leading enterprises, and large-scale commercial and logistics enterprises with large investment amounts, long industrial chains, and strong driving effects, according to the situation proposed in the project feasibility study report, upon government approval, the land can be transferred at the cost price according to the principle of separate management of revenue and expenditure. The part lower than the legally prescribed preferential benchmark land price will be used as subsidies for enterprise supporting facilities, technological transformation, employment subsidies, or technical renovation interest subsidies, arranged by the same-level finance as special expenditures. However, if the enterprise fails to reach the agreed investment amount, number of employees, etc., it will not enjoy this preferential policy.

(c) For enterprises obtaining state-owned land use rights through administrative allocation, after paying the allocated price and various fees according to national regulations, they can enter the site for construction and obtain the "State-owned Land Use Certificate." When building or expanding productive enterprises, if they use the originally allocated state-owned land and do not change the land use purpose, they can continue to use the land through allocation.

3. Other Preferences

(a) Administrative and institutional charges involving enterprises will strictly follow the administrative and institutional charge directory published by the county finance, pricing, and other departments. Without the approval of the state and the provincial government, no department or unit shall add new charge items or raise the charge standards. For any charges outside the directory, enterprises have the right to refuse payment; for administrative charges within the directory belonging to local income, only exemption or collection of cost fees is allowed; for institutional charges, they will be uniformly charged at 50% of the low limit standard.

(b) For high-tech, industrial production, agricultural industrialization major projects with long-term impacts on economic construction and social development, and other projects with larger investments and longer investment payback periods, a one-enterprise-one-policy, special-case-specific-handling approach can be adopted, approved by the county government, to provide greater preferences and support to investors in terms of taxation, land, charging services, etc.
