The procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you want to buy or sell, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. And this commission is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of buying or selling the stock. The abbreviation of the stock is usually three to four Chinese characters, and the code of the stock is six digits. The code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent when placing a commission. At the same time, there are certain regulations on the quantity of stocks traded: That is, the quantity of stocks commissioned to buy must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks commissioned to sell does not have to be a whole multiple of 100. There are four ways to place a commission: counter submission, telephone automatic commission, computer automatic commission, and remote terminal commission.
