I am a supporter of Shanghai Shenhua.

by hartyqekb on 2009-11-25 17:48:21

In 2009, it was a very busy year for Shenhua. Searching for foreign players was hectic. Shenhua's search for foreign players has long relied on Zhang Defa. The chief advisor, Zhang Defa, was initially cursed by fans but later vindicated by them: "Defa is also human." Unfortunately, once he became seen as human, he disappeared without a trace. This year, Shenhua had to rely on themselves to find players. They found a whole bunch, all of whom were said to be rare treasures. But then they were identified as water goods (subpar talents) by the official website of Shenhua itself. These subpar talents couldn't adapt to Shanghai’s environment, yet when sent elsewhere, they turned into treasures again and even claimed this year's Golden Boot. After all that hustle, nothing came of it—they were merely busy for others.

In external competitions, they were busy too. In the AFC Champions League, Shenhua flew all over, hastily formulating a strategic guideline: "Pick off three stars, break the antlers, annihilate the corps." This was more slogan than strategy. Instead of picking, breaking, and annihilating, they ended up being completely wiped out by their opponents. Originally aiming to uphold the image of Chinese football, after all that bustle, they achieved nothing, only disgracing themselves.

Investigating internal traitors was also a busy affair. In the league, Shenhua charged around everywhere, unable to win away games and consistently giving points to the last-placed teams. There must have been a mole—a case of double-crossing. I mentioned espionage, and later Zhu Jun did too. I criticized Shenhua fiercely, never imagining they would actually start investigating like Yu Zecheng (a famous spy character). This caused panic, but in the end, they only caught the "old traitor" Du Wei—no innovation at all. After much ado, they accomplished nothing, with no results to show for it.

Competing for the championship was also busy work. Shenhua aimed to win this year's championship, constantly calculating accounts, scores, and rankings along the way. In the end, they calculated themselves into fourth place. But the Football Association didn’t give them face, settling instead on fifth place. Without even a chance to participate in next year's AFC Champions League, their own hopes for the title dashed, they busied themselves puncturing other teams' tires. They tried to block Beijing Guoan but still watched them lift the championship trophy. After all that hustle, they achieved nothing, ending the year empty-handed.

Polishing the facade was also busy work. How many "gates" did Shenhua create this year? I can't even count them clearly. These various "XX-gates" left Shenhua looking disheveled and unappealing. So Shenhua had to keep cleaning up their public image with a bucket of whitewash every day. Busy, so busy, exhausting themselves. By year-end, they still had to clarify issues regarding the "shareholding gate." After all that hustle, they achieved nothing, only to have the health inspectors slap a "not clean household" sticker on their door.

However, Shenhua's busyness ultimately belonged to Zhu Jun alone. Searching for foreign players, competing externally, investigating internal traitors, polishing the facade... Zhu Jun took care of it all. If we were to select a labor model for Shenhua this year, none other than Zhu Jun would deserve the honor. In the Genbao era, there was a labor model named Vasily, a foreign player who hustled tirelessly on the field. Now Zhu Jun hustles off the field, up and down, working himself to the bone. Even though it was all for naught, medals still need to be awarded. As for bonuses, well, just like the rest of the players, they’ll have to go unpaid. Haha, interesting.
