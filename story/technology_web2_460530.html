Otis elevators involved in accidents to be taken out of service nationwide

Editor's note: Last night, the Beijing Municipal Government held a press conference on the accident of the No. 4 subway line escalator. Deputy Director Zhang Jumin of the city's Quality Supervision Bureau said that after preliminary judgment, the Otis 513MPE model escalators that experienced faults have design and manufacturing defects. Currently, the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine has specially issued an urgent notice requiring the immediate suspension of use of Otis 513MPE model automatic escalators nationwide. Some media believe that special equipment related to public safety, such as elevators, must have special safety measures; ordinary people cannot see what is underneath elevators that go up and down or roll, but the safety assurance of elevators cannot become an invisible black box.

Nationwide Suspension of Otis Elevators - How Can Safety Assurance Be Placed in a Black Box?

The General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine Urgently Suspended Use of Otis 513MPE Model Escalators

Zhang Jumin stated that on the day of the incident, the city's Quality Supervision Bureau issued an emergency notice requiring all similar types of Otis brand escalators currently in operation in the city to be immediately suspended for potential hazard inspections.

He introduced that two days after the incident, the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine specially issued an urgent notice halting the use of Otis 513MPE type escalators. The notice required immediate suspension of use of Otis brand 513MPE type escalators across the country, with users contacting manufacturers and maintenance units to conduct comprehensive inspections and eliminate safety hazards. At the same time, the notice required quality and technical supervision departments nationwide to urge subway, airport, station, mall and other public places escalator users to immediately conduct comprehensive inspections and take effective measures to eliminate safety hazards.

Recognition of Escalator Design Defects

Deputy Director and Spokesperson Zhang Jumin of the Beijing Quality Supervision Bureau stated that the direct cause of the accident was damage to fixed parts, causing displacement of the drive host and detachment of the drive chain; specific causes were entrusted to the National Elevator Quality Supervision and Inspection Center for examination. He emphasized that it can be preliminarily determined that this accident was due to design defects in this model of Otis escalators, along with inadequate maintenance, making the company irrefutably responsible for the accident.

Chief Engineer Zhao Boshui of the Beijing Special Equipment Testing Center said that the model of the accident escalator was the same as the one involved in last year's Shenzhen accident, both being Otis 513MPE model escalators. He said that from current observations, the two pedestals of the accident escalator were broken, indicating defects in manufacture, which should not have broken but did, so we preliminarily determined that there were issues with manufacturing and design defects.

Escalators Have Two Braking Systems: First is the working brake, second is the additional brake. For this escalator accident, the main reason was due to displacement of the fixing device and drive device, leading to detachment of the chain, and the working brake failed to function.

The additional brake would activate if any one of three conditions occurred: chain breakage, power failure, or elevator reverse travel. As for why the additional brake did not function, investigations are still ongoing, with the final conclusion to be made by the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine.

Shenzhen Accident Escalator Was the Same Model

Zhang Jumin introduced that the faulty escalator involved in this incident and the escalator involved in the December 14, 2010, Shenzhen accident were identical in brand and model. The General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine specially issued an urgent notice requiring immediate suspension of use of Otis 513MPE model automatic escalators across the country, with users contacting elevator manufacturers and maintenance units to conduct comprehensive safety inspections and eliminate safety hazards.

At the same time, the notice required quality supervision departments nationwide to urge subway, airport, mall, station and other public places escalator users to immediately conduct comprehensive inspections.

Zhang Jumin said that there were a total of 14 Otis 513MPE model elevators in operation on Subway Line 4, including 6 at Xizhimen Station, 2 at Zoo Station, 2 at Xuanwumen Station, 2 at Caishikou Station, and 2 at Haidianhuangzhuang Station. These have already been investigated according to the requirements of the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine, conducting accident investigations and safety hazard inspections of the elevators.

Zhang Jumin indicated that penalties for the responsible parties will mainly depend on the severity of the accident and the results of the investigation, with legal penalties imposed on the responsible parties based on relevant national laws and regulations.

Otis Elevator Trouble in Shanghai: A Five-Story Free Fall Traps More Than Ten People

One trouble hasn't settled, another arises. Yesterday, an Otis passenger elevator in a business building in Shanghai caused trouble again.

At around 12 noon yesterday, at No. 2025 Zhongshanxi Road in Xuhui District, while employees were taking the elevator downstairs for lunch, unexpectedly, the No. 1 elevator suddenly dropped as it approached M floor (between the third and fourth floors), followed by a loud bang before stopping. The frightened passengers then realized they had just experienced an unexpected elevator accident.

"It's something I've only seen on TV, never thought it would happen to me. Scared me to death," described Mr. Zhang who worked on the 24th floor. Before the incident, he and several female colleagues took the No. 1 elevator down from the 24th floor for lunch. There were already many passengers in the elevator. Subsequently, when the elevator descended to the 18th floor, a few more passengers entered. According to Mr. Zhang's description, the number of passengers in the elevator exceeded the allowable limit of ten, but no alarm sounded.

"At the time, there were definitely more than 17 passengers in the elevator because everyone was squeezed together and couldn't even turn around." After reaching M floor, one person exited the elevator. When the door closed, the elevator suddenly plummeted, failing to stop at the first floor. Then came a "boom" sound, followed by a huge collision, finally bringing the elevator to a halt.

The lighting and ceiling panels on the elevator's ceiling were smashed due to the impact and hung loosely in mid-air. Meanwhile, the digital buttons controlling the floors popped out, and the communication facilities for calls were also damaged.

At this moment inside the elevator, dust filled the air, and it became increasingly hot and stuffy. Due to fear, many female passengers began screaming, and an elderly man showed signs of discomfort.

At this point, some passengers started using their mobile phones to call for help, while two men standing close to the door tried hard to pry open the inner side of the elevator door, but the outer layer of the door remained tightly shut.

After a long ten minutes, security personnel used tools to pry open the outer door. The passengers discovered they were trapped between the first floor and the basement level of the building. Afterwards, more than ten passengers climbed out of the elevator and were rescued.

Ningxia Urgently Halts Otis Escalators, Identifies 228 Safety Hazards

Yesterday, when visiting some malls and hotels in Yinchuan, problems were found, such as the escalators at Jianfa Modern City on Liberation West Street in Xingqing District running intermittently fast and slow, emitting sharp noises, and the escalator at XinHua East Street Ximing Electrical Mall suddenly slowing down when descending, nearly causing passengers to fall.

A responsible person from the Special Equipment Department of the Autonomous Region Quality Supervision Bureau told reporters that according to national regulations, elevators must operate at a set speed. If they run erratically or emit abnormal sounds, these are considered safety hazards and must be promptly eliminated. It was reported that the 12365 Quality Complaint Reporting Center of the Autonomous Region Quality Supervision Bureau and the quality supervision department receive multiple complaints about the quality and safety of automatic escalators every year.

It was understood that in April this year, Ningxia launched a two-and-a-half-month special inspection campaign targeting the use units and maintenance units of automatic escalators and moving walkways. Among the 824 automatic escalators and moving walkways in use in Ningxia, a total of 57 'Special Equipment Safety Supervision Instruction Letters' were issued, identifying 228 safety hazards, rectifying 204 items, achieving a hazard rectification rate of 89.5%.

Elevator Safety Cannot Be Hidden in a Black Box

Elevators and other special equipment concerning public safety must have specific safety measures. What lies beneath elevators that ascend, descend, and roll may be invisible to ordinary people, but the safety assurance of elevators cannot become an invisible black box. Moreover, we do have ways to identify potential safety hazards early on, freeing people from fear of their living environment.

To safeguard public safety, responsibility is most needed. Elevator companies and user units should strictly adhere to standards with a sense of responsibility towards public safety and not cut costs in matters concerning human lives. Quality supervision departments must take responsibility in supervising the implementation of regulations and standards. For production and user units that repeatedly cause problems, punishment measures must be taken. Specifically for each individual at every link, responsibilities should be clearly defined, and everyone should perform their respective duties. In some well-managed areas, we can see that each elevator indicates its safety responsible person. After each inspection and maintenance, labels are affixed and signed off. Only in this way can past invisible safety efforts become visible and supervised.
