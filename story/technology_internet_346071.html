Four Tricks of Online Marketing Solve Problems

by saitseo2012 on 2010-04-15 15:08:41

With the continuous development of Internet technology, the wave of networking has swept across the globe. Online marketing is increasingly becoming mainstream in modern marketing markets due to its unique features such as low cost, fast deployment, and obvious effectiveness. The emergence of online marketing has also opened up an entirely new marketing approach for a vast number of small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs). The application of online marketing among domestic enterprises is gradually deepening; however, compared to internationally excellent enterprises, domestic online marketing applications are still in their infancy. Many SMEs cannot effectively leverage online marketing models to benefit their businesses, primarily because they have not mastered the methods of online marketing. Here, I will introduce four basic methods of online marketing to help SMEs solve their online marketing problems.

### First Method: Accumulate Small Streams into a Mighty River

As we all know, the first step in online marketing is to build a website. Currently, website construction technology is already quite mature, making it relatively easy to create a site. The critical issue lies in promotion—getting as many people as possible to visit your website, which is commonly referred to as "traffic." This forms the foundation of online marketing. SMEs can acquire traffic through methods such as link exchanges, online advertising placements, and natural search engine rankings. Among these, the traffic obtained from search engines can be quite substantial, but ranking at the top of search engines is not easy. This requires professional optimization of the website, and generally, this part is handled by specialized companies. SMEs can simultaneously try multiple approaches mentioned above, accumulating "small streams" into a "mighty river."

### Second Method: Know Yourself, But Know Your Competitors Better

After solving the traffic problem, the next step is to consider how to utilize this hard-earned traffic. First, companies need to classify and analyze visitors, establishing customer tracking profiles. It's essential to know where customers come from and how they arrived at the website, which helps companies understand the return on their network investments. Then, record which web pages customers browse and which products they spend the most time on. Companies should collect and analyze these firsthand materials to form data. There are already software solutions available on the market that can achieve this functionality.

### Third Method: Targeted Efforts to Convert Traffic into Sales

Now that the company has some information about the visitors, it can make judgments about them. For example, companies can judge visitor needs based on the products they browse and assess their purchasing intentions based on the time spent on a specific product page. After understanding these aspects, companies should act decisively by contacting visitors promptly through online chat tools, further understanding their needs, guiding them towards purchases, addressing any issues they encounter, and alleviating their purchase concerns. Ultimately, this leads to successful transactions, achieving sign-offs and converting website traffic into sales.

### Fourth Method: Identify Gaps and Strive for Complete Success

Many SMEs believe that after successfully closing a deal through online marketing, the sales process is over. However, this is not the case. After signing up, visitors become the company’s customers but not necessarily loyal ones. To pursue long-term benefits, companies must strive to gain more loyal customers. To achieve this, companies should establish tracking profiles for customers who have signed contracts and enable customer online reminders. In this way, companies can provide targeted "one-on-one" services to customers based on the information provided by the software, continuously strengthening their relationship with customers until they ultimately become loyal ones. Additionally, to realize the feature of 24/7, round-the-clock sales on the website, companies can activate SMS message functions, enabling real-time grasp of the latest business opportunities and staying ahead of competitors.

Currently, online marketing has become an indispensable component of SMEs' development strategies and represents the future direction of marketing methods. It will have profound implications for the development of SMEs. Therefore, mastering the basic methods of online marketing has become an urgent task for SMEs engaging in online marketing activities and must receive sufficient attention.
