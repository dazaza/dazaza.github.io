Bali      Sohu Sports News - Bali is a famous tourist area in Indonesia, known as the "Golf Mall." It is an island located among the small Sunda Islands to the east of Java, with an area of over 5,560 square kilometers and a population of about 2.8 million. The island has a tropical climate influenced by the ocean, characterized by a warm and rainy weather, fertile soil, lush green mountains and rivers all year round, colorful flowers, and towering trees. Balinese people love flowers and decorate everywhere with them, thus giving the island its nickname as the "Island of Flowers" and enjoying the reputation of "Paradise in the South Sea" and "God's Island." Most of the island is mountainous, with crisscrossing mountains and an east-high-west-low terrain. There are four or five cone-shaped intact volcanic peaks, among which Mount Agung (Bali Peak) stands at an altitude of 3,142 meters, making it the highest point on the island. Nearby is the active Mount Batu that erupted in 1963. The beaches at Sanur, Nusa Dua, and Kuta are the most scenic bathing spots on the island, featuring fine sand, wide beaches, and clear blue sea water. The dry season from April to October is relatively cool, making it the best time for climbing mountains or visiting nature reserves. Every year, tourists from all over the world come here in a steady stream.

Special Introduction to Golf Courses: New Kuta Golf

New Kuta Golf

The latest completed golf course on Bali, located southeast of the Kuta district near Bali's most famous Dreamland Beach, offering panoramic views of the Indian Ocean and beautiful scenery. Only 15 minutes' drive from the airport and Kuta downtown. Established in 2007, Par: 72, Course Length: 6812 yards, Number of Holes: 18, Designer: Ronald Fream, David Dale & Kevin Ramsey, USA. Successfully hosted the Indonesian Golf Open in March, attracting many golfers to participate.

Bali Golf and Country Club Course

Designed by Robin Nelson and Rodney Wright, this is an 18-hole international-level competition course and was rated by Fortune magazine as one of the top 5 courses in Asia. It is located next to the sea in the Nusa Dua luxury hotel resort area of Bali. If you stay in this lush mini peninsula, it will be very convenient. The fairways here are quite flat, mostly using trees as obstacles. You don't have to worry too much about how to get the ball onto the green, just enjoy the tropical rainforest atmosphere and the breathtaking sea view of the Indian Ocean!
