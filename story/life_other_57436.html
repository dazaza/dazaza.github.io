"Efficiency is too high to be complained" has nothing to do with efficiency.

by mokey53 on 2008-03-26 15:33:35

"The divorce registration was too fast, and the staff were all too irresponsible. They registered our divorce in a flash, and I want to complain," said Miss Zhang from Shenzhen, complaining about the high efficiency of the marriage registration office which didn't dissuade her, leading to her impulsive divorce with her husband, leaving her regretting it too late. In response, the Marriage Management Office of Shenzhen Civil Affairs Bureau stated that this was not within their scope of responsibility. (Southern Metropolis Daily, March 25)

When citizens are dissatisfied with low work efficiency and angrily file complaints to higher authorities, such situations are very common. However, citizens complaining about "staff working too efficiently" can make the parties involved feel wronged, and from an outsider's perspective, it indeed seems somewhat unreasonable. But if we put ourselves in their shoes, it is not difficult to find that the complaint about the high efficiency of handling divorce procedures actually has nothing to do with "efficiency."

In the traditional habits of the Chinese, there has always been a belief in "persuading reconciliation rather than separation." Although modern people's thoughts and concepts have changed, and if a couple cannot live together anymore, divorce may be a form of relief. Nevertheless, in reality, couples living together inevitably encounter some bumps along the way. Many people, when angry, might say extreme things or even threaten to "divorce" out of spite, which may not reflect their true intentions. If someone could mediate at this moment, it might possibly calm down a dispute.

As the legal institution certifying marital status, the civil affairs department deals with both marriages and divorces. As long as the procedures are proper, handling relevant formalities according to regulations seems beyond reproach. However, it should also be noted that indeed some people get divorced because they are "heated-headed." In such cases, if the staff handling the matter would ask one more question as to why, and kindly remind them, it might save a family on the brink of breaking apart. Following the prescribed procedures for divorce, behind this "high efficiency," reflects the lack of human-centered awareness among the staff.

The family, as the "cell" of society, its stability is also one of the factors of social harmony. The separation of a well-functioning family casts a psychological shadow on children and causes harm to relatives, increasing the disharmonious elements in society. And some who impulsively get divorced soon come back to remarry, unnecessarily increasing societal costs. Staff members of the civil affairs department, without asking any questions, efficiently handle a divorce in five minutes. Behind this "official business-like" approach lies the absence of social responsibility.

Therefore, it appears that being complained about due to excessively high work efficiency has nothing to do with "efficiency" but everything to do with "responsibility." One wonders whether the managers of the civil affairs department understand the "unspoken meaning" behind the complaints?
