Sohu Video announces Charles Zhang as interim CEO and Liu Chun as President

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 12:15:05

As the industry's attention was focused on the Sogou sale transaction, a major change in Sohu Video, a subsidiary of Sohu, was announced first. Sohu Video sent out an internal email today, announcing that Charles Zhang would serve as the acting Chief Executive Officer, taking full responsibility for Sohu Video operations; Liu Chun would be the President of Sohu Video, and Deng Ye would take the position of Chief Operating Officer.

Regarding this structural adjustment, Charles Zhang stated: "Video is one of the main battlegrounds in the current internet industry competition, and I will focus my primary energy on video-related work." Charles Zhang also affirmed Deng Ye's work, saying that since June 2006, Deng Ye had built the Sohu Entertainment Division and served as CEO of Sohu Video, making significant contributions to the healthy growth of Sohu Video.

"Deng Ye has ranked Sohu Video among the top three in the industry. In her new role, she will leverage her deep understanding of the video industry and team cohesion to continue driving the growth of Sohu Video," Charles Zhang added simultaneously, "Liu Chun will use his years of experience in visual media to develop Sohu Video to new heights with us. This is a new dream team that will enhance Sohu Video's competitiveness, allowing Sohu Video to achieve a new breakout."

This adjustment also implies that Deng Ye has been 'demoted,' while Liu Chun has regained some authority. Liu Chun once worked at CCTV and Phoenix Satellite TV, serving as the executive producer of "News Investigation" during his time at CCTV. In 2000, he joined Phoenix Satellite TV, holding various positions such as assistant chief planner, assistant director, deputy director, and executive director. Many people believe that he is fully capable of assuming the position of CEO of Sohu Video.

Prior to this, a person close to Sohu Video revealed to Tencent Technology that after Liu Chun joined Sohu Video, he initially wanted to make a significant impact and reform Sohu Video according to his ideas. However, he soon realized that he had little decision-making power within Sohu Video, eventually shifting to handle Sohu portal affairs.

In May this year, Tencent Technology published an exclusive report titled "Sohu Video Trapped in Prisoner's Dilemma: Severe Internal Consumption, Mergers and Acquisitions to Break the Deadlock." The article pointed out that Sohu Video had experienced relatively frequent changes in senior management and personnel turmoil this year, which has affected Sohu Video's development. This report profoundly influenced the personnel structure changes in Sohu Video.

An informed source disclosed that this report had a tremendous impact on Deng Ye. At one point, Deng Ye expressed to Charles Zhang her desire to resign from the position of CEO of Sohu Video. However, Charles Zhang had initially tried to retain her. Currently, Charles Zhang is only temporarily serving as CEO, and after the transition period, Liu Chun is likely to officially assume the CEO position.

Below is the internal email from Sohu Video:

Dear colleagues of Sohu,

To promote the continuous development of Sohu Video, approved by the management, the following adjustments have been made to the Sohu Video management structure:

1. Mr. Charles Zhang will serve as Acting Chief Executive Officer (Acting CEO), taking overall responsibility for Sohu Video;

2. Mr. Liu Chun will serve as President (President) of Sohu Video, responsible for the overall content strategy and execution, reporting directly to Mr. Charles Zhang;

3. Ms. Deng Ye will serve as Chief Operating Officer (COO) of Sohu Video, responsible for sales and product technology operations, reporting directly to Mr. Charles Zhang;

4. Mobile video will be incorporated into the mobile product center of the Sohu Group.

The above adjustments will take effect immediately. We wish Sohu Video stronger development momentum under the new management structure and entry into a new phase of development.

Human Resources Department

July 19, 2013
