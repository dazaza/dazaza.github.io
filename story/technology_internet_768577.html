Li Mingyuan: Baidu Light App to be Opened Next Month

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 19:54:29

On November 13th, it was reported that the MDCC2013 Mobile Developer Conference opened in Beijing today. Baidu Vice President Mingyuan Li introduced Baidu's Light Application Platform at the conference and officially announced that Baidu's Light Application Platform will be open next month. Li Mingyuan mentioned the following points:

1. The installation volume of Native APPs is increasing, but the average daily number of launches is decreasing.

2. The bonus for urban users has become very scarce, and the incremental market comes from third- and fourth-tier cities. We need to study how they use their phones and applications.

3. High-frequency mobile applications are becoming monopolized, forcing developers to develop both Native and Web versions. The solution is Baidu Light Applications.

4. The core advantage of Baidu Light Applications is mobile search, with large distribution volumes, high-quality users, good service experiences, and strong application functions.

5. The ranking strategy for Baidu Light Applications includes: content relevance, user feedback, content quality, and interaction quality.

6. Baidu’s openness strategy for light applications involves providing template-based creation tools, an integrated cloud-end Web App development framework, rapid PC site mobilization tools, as well as cloud capabilities and end capability invocation.

7. The access process involves developers submitting basic application information, search channel information, and operational management. Baidu will then store the application, guide it, and launch it.

The following is the full speech transcript:

Li Mingyuan: I am very glad to be here at the Mobile Developer Conference today to exchange ideas with everyone. The deep-water zone in the mobile field is indeed a blue ocean of opportunities, and what we all need to do is survive.

This industry is clear to everyone. In the Internet domain, over the past 30 years of development, there has been a fundamental disruptive innovation every 10 years or so, which leads to the disintegration of existing technologies. This brings about a peak in entrepreneurship.

In my view, the movement into mobile is accompanied by a technological revolution, a disruptive change. For those entering the mobile sector during this period, the next five to ten years will bring significant rewards, surpassing the achievements of those who started businesses in the PC internet era. Choosing mobile today is the right choice.

How to Survive in the Deep-Water Zone

In such a complex deep-water zone, with big waves and various underwater conditions, last year I saw many people jump in but couldn't make money.

In such a situation, can we help entrepreneurs by addressing some technical foundations?

We talk about mobile being a disruptive innovation in technology, a breakdown of old systems. The popularization of smartphones has greatly changed our lives. Recently, when I went to get glasses, he asked me what I do, and I said IT. He said that business has been good this year because your IT folks have dropped their vision by an average of 100 degrees. Screens are getting smaller, and eyesight is deteriorating.

A while ago, I met a foreigner who said coming to China made him feel that Chinese people are truly hardworking. They used to say Chinese people were noisy, but I don’t find them noisy at all. On the subway, I see everyone looking at their phones. So, I feel that mobile internet has brought many changes to our lives.

Even companies like Baidu and Tencent, so-called big players, have undergone many changes. Baidu now has 11 mobile applications with over 100 million users, yet still feels unsafe. The same goes for Tencent. Our sense of crisis is very strong as user demands are exploding.

In the PC era, doing internet seemed simple. I could create a website, drive traffic to it, and the infrastructure was complete. All users knew how to use keyboards and mice, and their demand paths were clear. In the mobile internet era, whether the input method is good, whether voice recognition is good, whether images are good, all affect the services behind them. Platforms not only consider what services to provide but also consider whether users have a good experience. This creates many opportunities. Platforms must analyze and perform more foundational, lower-level work, studying how mobile internet develops. As a result, niche markets present enormous opportunities. Smartphones have transformed our behavior from going online to being always online. Mobile says we are constantly connected to the network, creating a surge in demand. The combination of these two factors is indeed the integration of developers.

During the development process, what changes do we see? Through data analysis, we find that people think Native APPs are what should be done on mobile. Over the past year, each user's Native APP installation count has indeed increased by 5.6%, from the third quarter of 2012 to the first quarter of 2013. However, per capita usage has decreased.

Many new users are in environments we don't understand. Their lifestyles are different from ours. What needs do they have after using smartphones? We don't know. Our new users, even farmers, have never touched smartphones. They are different from the existing 400 million users.

Additionally, data shows that high-frequency APPs are becoming increasingly centralized. From 2012 to 2013, the trend toward monopolization has intensified. App stores primarily distribute Native APPs, while searches in the past distributed Web apps. Developers need to handle both. You may find you're dealing with a common need, but your daily usage frequency isn't high.

The Long-Tail Dilemma: Distribution and Usage

With our large population, we quickly surpassed other markets, and the monthly growth rate is fast. Only six out of ten users continue to use an app after one week, and over 30% uninstall it.

There are also significant changes in overall demand coverage. We see that for life information services, the installation coverage decreases as the number of smartphones increases. Users increasingly express their lifestyle service needs through their phones. How we acquire these growing users and meet their expanding needs is crucial.

From the perspective of search share, service-related demands have grown from 26% to 34%, maintaining a high growth rate. In this process, we communicate with both developers and users. When asking users how they would fulfill certain needs, they say they would search or download recommended apps from friends. With frequent updates, they might not know what's different in the latest version. After updating, if they no longer need the app, they uninstall it. Therefore, finding a good app becomes increasingly difficult for users.

For app developers, the inability for users to find their apps is their biggest problem. Baidu Light Applications is a mechanism that helps developers achieve both lightweight app forms and placement in app stores through a single development effort.

After considering the combined characteristics of Native apps and Web, light users first need cross-platform functionality, meaning one development can run on multiple devices regardless of platform.

Secondly, the development cost is relatively low, reducing costs. User experience must be excellent; we want users to feel this is the app they’re looking for. Now, distribution occurs through mobile search platforms. Recently, for light users like Avatar TaoTao (a general need but with low frequency), we didn't know which avatar product was good. After searching, we integrated it with Baidu Search, leading to a significant increase in media downloads.

Baidu's Tools

Many mobile users operate their phones with their thumbs. Last year, we early-on made our entire search interface fully accessible, allowing users to load the search engine with just a tap. We’ve made many bold attempts in mobile search, including foreign mobile search providers following suit.

In terms of search structure mobility, we collaborate more with developers. During the National Day holiday in October, since August, we explained to many tourism site developers that initially building large applications with huge packages wasn’t feasible, as putting all Chinese tourist sites into one app was unrealistic. In the first phase of development, during the National Day holiday, growth exceeded 100%. By breaking down each scenic spot, when users searched for specific attractions, they directly accessed the light application, making the path very simple.

We offer a large quantity of products with high quality, excellent experience, and robust functionality. Currently, based on mobile search, we are the only platform that truly helps reduce costs while effectively distributing applications.

Everyone is ultimately concerned about openness. In December this year, we will fully open up the entire Baidu Light Application Platform. The main channels in Baidu Search are through mobile search and the Baidu client.

We mainly focus on two things: first is relevance, which affects user satisfaction. Second is quality—whether you provide the best information services and interactive products. Some do it well, others less so. In the era of light applications, the mechanisms are the same, hoping everyone can improve their own light applications. In interactions, we hope for some innovations. We provide numerous interactive components to help quickly realize your creative ideas.

Technically, Baidu has its own set of core systems. If you wish to generate both media and light applications, you can use Clouda. If you want to create a simpler application, you can use SiteApp, which is templated. Another category is webmasters, which is quite easy. Since last year, we've already opened our SiteApp, allowing you to use Clouda to make the mobile site experience approach media standards.

Behind the scenes, we provide Baidu Cloud and direct access to hardware within mobile devices, enabling us to support various sensors.

The access process is straightforward—developers only need three steps: submit basic application information, submit search channel information, and manage operations. We will officially open the platform in December, and you can visit it then.

We will hold an event from December 7th to January 18th, engaging face-to-face with developers. Afterward, we will provide technical support. If you have any questions, please pay attention to our activities in December. We sincerely hope to work together to create useful things for our consumers. Thank you all.
