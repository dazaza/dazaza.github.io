So, how can you know if you're pregnant at the first moment? What are the symptoms of early pregnancy?

Experts from Xi'an Abortion Hospital point out that women can check if they are pregnant by observing changes in their body. The physical changes during early pregnancy include:

1. Missed period: The earliest and most important sign of pregnancy for women is a missed period. If your menstrual cycle is usually regular and you have a history of sexual activity, a delay in menstruation of more than 10 days may indicate pregnancy. If the missed period lasts for more than two weeks, you should go to a professional hospital for an examination.

2. Early pregnancy reaction: Most women experience symptoms such as fatigue, dizziness, sleepiness, increased saliva secretion, loss of appetite, and nausea/vomiting around 5-6 weeks after becoming pregnant.

3. Increased urination: In early pregnancy, women may experience an increase in the frequency of urination due to the growing uterus pressing on and stimulating the bladder. After 12 weeks of pregnancy, the uterus moves out of the pelvis, relieving the pressure on the bladder, and the frequency of urination decreases.

4. Changes in breasts: After becoming pregnant, under the influence of estrogen and progesterone, the breasts gradually grow larger, the nipples and areolas become darker, and dark brown nodules may appear around the nipples. After 12 weeks of pregnancy, some women may secrete a small amount of clear milk-like fluid.

5. Changes in basal body temperature: Another early sign of pregnancy is a change in basal body temperature. Women can check if they are pregnant by measuring their basal body temperature. Normally, a woman's basal body temperature follows a biphasic curve—lower before ovulation and higher afterward. If menstruation does not come on time and the temperature remains elevated for more than 18 days, then there is a possibility of pregnancy.
