Young man lied that he placed a bomb in the supermarket and asked for 1 million yuan

by yanb403rxue on 2012-03-09 10:03:05

A 22-year-old man, after watching a plot in a Jackie Chan movie where bombs were used to threaten people, wanted to mimic it. He sent threatening text messages twice to a supermarket, falsely claiming he had placed bombs and demanded 1 million yuan.

In the early hours of the morning, Ms. Rao, the supermarket manager, was playing mahjong with friends when her phone received a text message that read: "I have installed three remote-control time bombs in your supermarket. Don't panic, I'm just testing the power of the bombs. Do not report this to the police or I will immediately detonate the bombs remotely. Tell your boss to prepare 1 million yuan in cash and wait for further instructions, otherwise you know the consequences." Everyone at the mahjong table thought it was definitely a prank, so Ms. Rao ignored it.

However, the next afternoon, when she received another similar text message, she began to panic. Was someone seeking revenge or was it just a prank?

Inspired by a Jackie Chan movie, he wanted to use an alarm clock to impersonate a "bomb"

In August, the 22-year-old Wang Dong (a pseudonym) checked into a hotel in Xin Qiao and while wandering around, he discovered a Yonghui Supermarket, deciding to buy some toiletries and an alarm clock. On the morning of August 27th, Wang Dong was surfing the internet at an internet cafe when he watched Jackie Chan's movie "New Police Story". The bomb threat scenes in the movie excited him greatly.

Thus, Wang Dong conceived the idea to mimic what he saw in the movie and give it a try. Subsequently, he noticed that there was group-buying information at the entrance of the Yonghui Supermarket, which included a mobile phone number (actually the phone number of Ms. Rao, the manager of the supermarket's food and supplies department). Wang Dong has a good memory and memorized the number directly.

After returning to the hotel, Wang Dong began to ponder his plan. Making a fake bomb might make it more realistic and scare them more effectively. He suddenly remembered that the alarm clock he bought a few days ago could be wrapped in cardboard to resemble a "time bomb" even more closely.

Sent two text messages, threatening the supermarket for 1 million yuan

However, Wang Dong later thought that making a fake bomb would be too blatant and troublesome, so he decided not to proceed with it. At 00:06 on August 29th, Wang Dong became increasingly excited: "Just send a text message to scare the supermarket, it's not a big problem right?"

Thus, he composed the first text message. At the time, Ms. Rao was playing mahjong and upon seeing the message, assumed it was a prank and didn't pay attention. At 3 PM that day, Ms. Rao, along with two other colleagues analyzing data, received another text message, which made her break out in a cold sweat.

The content of the message was: "You have two and a half hours left to prepare what we want. Remember, the first bomb will explode at 5:10 PM, and then it won't just be about one million yuan..." Ms. Rao immediately called the two numbers from which the messages were sent, but both were turned off.

Seeing hundreds of people being evacuated, he was scared and dared not leave his room

Why did he send two text messages? After Wang Dong was caught, he confessed to the police that after sending the first message and seeing that the supermarket was still open, he thought they weren't afraid and he hadn't scared them enough. So, he sent a second one just to see if it would "work." When questioned by the prosecutor, Wang Dong quickly admitted everything. He said that at the time, he wanted to send another text message to threaten the supermarket, "I originally thought, if I could really extort some money, that would be fine too, but I hadn't yet figured out how to collect the money, so I didn't specify how the money should be delivered."

After sending the second text message, Wang Dong felt uneasy, scared, and anxious. Not daring to wait for a reply, he removed the SIM card from his phone. That evening, while eating at a snack shop near the Yonghui Supermarket and checking the effect of his text messages, he saw many people rushing out of the Yonghui Supermarket in panic. There were police cars and police dogs. The police had set up a cordon outside the Yonghui Supermarket, and hundreds of evacuated people had already caused traffic congestion on the road. "This thing has gotten out of hand," Wang Dong didn't dare to go out anymore and quickly returned to the hotel. Soon, the police found Wang Dong.

On the 21st of this month, the Sha District Procuratorate will transfer the case to the Sha District Court. The suspect, Wang Dong, fabricated explosive threats and disrupted public order,涉嫌 fabricating false terrorist information, and will be prosecuted.
