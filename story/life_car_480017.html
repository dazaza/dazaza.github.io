Car Beauty: New Car Decoration and Maintenance

Matters to be noted for car decoration:

A. The color of the decoration materials must coordinate with the color of the car, and one should not blindly pursue high quality or high price to avoid counterproductive results. For example, a black car body should be paired with light tea-colored sun film.

B. Depending on the size of the interior space, try to select some small, beautiful, practical decorations that can fully reflect the owner's personality, such as cups, perfume bottles, and timers.

C. Cleanliness requires all decorative items inside the car to be clean, hygienic, and neatly arranged. A tidy car with fresh air and no dirt will give people a sense of relaxation and comfort.

D. Safety mainly refers to the fact that in-car decorations must not hinder the driver's safe driving or the safety of passengers.

E. Comfort means that the color and texture of the in-car decorations should conform to the owner's aesthetic view. Only a comfortable environment can bring the owner and passengers a pleasant mood and relaxed feeling, ensuring safe driving.

Common Decorations for New Cars

Basic decorations for a new car include floor mats, foot pads, seat covers, handle covers, perfumes, alarms (gear locks), reversing radars, and anti-explosion films; high-end decorations also include leather seats, sound systems, wood trims, panoramic roofs, satellite cruise positioning systems, airbags. More practical ones also include various cushions, backrests, cup holders, anti-static labels, debris trays, mudguards, car covers, roof racks, car fridges, air pumps, brake lights, position lights, rearview mirrors, parking signs, fire extinguishers, chamois leather, dusters, oxygen bars, electric windows, automatic antennas, car curtains, mechanical locks... and large bumpers, spoilers, fender flares, rain guards, decorative lights, stickers, steps... Below are introductions to several commonly used decoration items:

1. Alarm (Gear Lock): The safety of the car is what people care about most, so alarm products are essential. Cars equipped with electronic alarms generally have remote control door functions, which are very convenient and practical. However, if thieves figure out how to crack them, it’s easy for them to steal the car. Therefore, many cars now also install gear locks. Gear lock is a mechanical lock that locks the car's gearshift in neutral or reverse, making it impossible for thieves to drive away even if they start the car. The interior space is small, and the lock rod is hard and difficult to saw through. Due to usage or product issues, electronic alarms sometimes encounter problems, and once installed, gear locks are not easily removable. When choosing an alarm or gear lock, consider product quality, installation technology, and after-sales service.

2. Floor Mat: Generally, there is a carpet-like item under the car seat that is pre-installed in the original car. Once dirt or stains are left on it, it is hard to clean. If you lay a waterproof and easy-to-clean item on top, cleaning becomes much easier. This protective layer is called a floor mat. It comes in two types: handmade and molded. A well-made floor mat can effectively prevent dust and dirt from seeping into the carpet, but its water resistance is slightly worse. Molded floor mats are pressed into shape at once, with no seams in the middle, good leak prevention, but when encountering uneven ground in the car, their aesthetics suffer. Generally, floor mats are made of 3mm thick rubber products, available in gray, beige, and black colors. Additionally, the level of floor mat laying is also very important. Poor laying can cause edges to curl and the center to be uneven, leading to an uncomfortable overall appearance. Since floor mats are rubber products, they may have some odors and fewer color options, making some high-end cars feel lower-class after installation. Now some cars use home carpets, which look great, showing class without being too expensive.

3. Reversing Radar: As more cars hit the road, usable space becomes smaller. With more new drivers and female drivers, accidents increase, and disputes caused by these accidents also rise. What was once a non-issue - reversing - has gradually become a problem. We face reversing multiple times a day. A reversing radar allows the vehicle to get as close as possible to obstacles while reversing, unaffected by darkness or fog, greatly enhancing reversing safety.

4. Anti-explosion Film: In the scorching summer, driving on roads without shade, even with the air conditioning on, you cannot escape the sun's and UV rays' harm, which can make you restless. If you apply a good anti-explosion film, it will block 99.99% of UV rays, preventing direct sunlight from entering the car and giving you a cooling sensation. This is due to the anti-explosion film's heat insulation and UV protection functions. Additionally, if the glass breaks, shards won't fly around, reducing harm to people.

Issues Regarding New Car Modifications

1. Front Bumper Guard: Absolutely do not buy the common bumper guard found in the market, which is installed on the original front bumper. These guards cannot provide collision protection and are only for decoration.

2. Rear Bumper Guard: Generally, don't install them because the departure angle of the rear part of the car is already small, and installing a rear bumper would further reduce this angle, making it unsuitable for off-road use.

3. Roof Rack: Ordinary roof racks often fail due to substandard materials or excessive fixing parts, causing noise easily. Also, their load-bearing capacity is usually low.

4. Off-road Lights: There are two types. One is mounted lower, such as on the front bumper or front bumper guard, serving as auxiliary lighting to widen the illumination angle and assist the main headlights.

5. Suspension Lift: Professional lift kits are very expensive, like ARB's full set of lift components, plus four professional shock absorbers costing 10,000 yuan, but with excellent performance. Alternatively, amateur lift kits can be used, such as heavy-duty suspension components for six-cylinder engines, including front springs, rear steel plates, and six-cylinder shock absorbers.

6. Larger Tires: Generally, 235/75R15 tires are installed, divided into highway and highway/muddy road dual-purpose types. They are not cheap, each costing over 400 yuan. Changing tires is a significant modification point to improve the entire vehicle's driving performance, worth considering if funds allow.

7. Negative Value Wheel Drums: Due to the width of 235 tires, they might touch the lower arm (a component of the front axle suspension) when the steering wheel is turned fully. A better solution is to replace with negative value wheel drums.

8. Others: Modifications also involve other aspects. For off-road vehicles, there are high-position intake pipes, winches, and more advanced differential locks.

Summer Driving Tips: Monitor Water Temperature and Air Conditioning System

1. High Water Temperature

High water temperature is one of the major problems of summer engines. Many factors can cause high vehicle water temperatures, such as internal and external clogging of the radiator leading to poor heat dissipation, malfunctioning electronic fans, damaged fan couplers, and leaks in the cooling system.

2. Maintenance

In summer, regular maintenance of the vehicle becomes more important. Due to high summer temperatures and humidity, engine oil absorbs moisture from the air, making it prone to degradation. Most users' vehicles are primarily driven within the city, covering short distances and remaining parked for extended periods.

Brake fluid itself has hydrophilic properties, so as usage time and mileage increase, some water molecules are absorbed into the braking system. External temperatures and frequent use of the braking system evaporate this moisture. Therefore, manufacturers specify that brake fluid should be replaced every two years or 40,000 kilometers. Without timely replacement, the boiling point of the brake fluid decreases, directly affecting braking performance and damaging the braking system's components due to degraded brake fluid.

The condition of tire usage is also crucial in summer. Tire pressure must be checked and adjusted after the tire temperature has cooled down because high tire temperatures can affect the accuracy of tire pressure readings.

3. Air Conditioning System Maintenance

Summer is the high-frequency season for using air conditioning, and many problems arise, such as malfunctioning air conditioning and unpleasant odors. Various reasons lead to these issues: poor heat dissipation, dirt and blockage of the radiator, which can be cleaned by washing the condenser. Non-functioning electronic fans require checking corresponding circuits and motors; additionally, system pressure issues, either too high or too low, can prevent the air conditioner from cooling properly. For unpleasant odors in the air conditioning, it could be due to a blocked air filter or foreign matter on the evaporator surface, requiring cleaning or replacement.
