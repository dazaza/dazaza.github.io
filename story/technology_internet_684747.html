Zhejiang Online News - March 1st: In the early hours of yesterday morning, many residents near the Zhonghe elevated road in Hangzhou were woken up by the urgent sound of sirens. Numerous red fire trucks, lights flashing, rushed northward. The Tesco supermarket on Desheng Road was on fire!

This supermarket was one of the earliest to open in Hangzhou. At the end of 1999, Tesco opened a hypermarket on Desheng Road. In an era when supermarkets were still rare, the Desheng Tesco store once enjoyed great popularity. People from the northern part of the city would frequently visit due to its proximity, while those from more distant areas would also come for a visit on weekends.

This large supermarket, which had existed for over 10 years in the northern part of Hangzhou, suddenly turned into a sea of fire around 1:30 AM yesterday!

Eyewitness: The entire building where the supermarket is located caught fire, with flames reaching halfway into the sky.

Ms. Wang, who lives directly across from the Tesco supermarket, was the first to report the incident to our 96068 hotline after she woke up hearing the sound of fire trucks.

"I pulled back the curtain and saw that the entire building opposite was on fire, with flames reaching halfway into the sky and black smoke billowing. I saw firefighters rushing inside, it was terrifying."

Subsequently, Mr. Zhou also reported live over the phone: "A massive fire at Tesco supermarket, so many fire trucks have arrived!"

After receiving the tip-off, reporters immediately rushed to the scene. Under the elevated bridge, police cars, fire trucks, and crowds of onlookers surrounded the Tesco supermarket.

Pushing through the crowd to get closer to the supermarket, we found that the western facade of the supermarket was on fire, with large flames and occasional bursts of thick smoke.

From the appearance of the fire site, it could be seen that the western side of the ground floor of the supermarket consisted of a row of small shops, whose roller shutters had already been deformed and twisted by the heat of the fire, leaving everything inside reduced to ashes except for some iron machinery. Moving upwards along the shops, the advertising signs on the west wall of the supermarket were also burned down to their skeletons, with all the external plastic advertisements completely destroyed.

Zhang Jun (a pseudonym as requested), a network manager at a nearby internet cafe, was one of the first people to call 119 to report the fire. He said that it was his shift last night when he accidentally discovered the fire at the supermarket.

"At that moment, I was standing in the internet cafe looking outside when I suddenly saw flames appear on the first floor of the supermarket. The fire spread very quickly, reaching the upper floors in no time, turning the entire supermarket into a sea of fire." Zhang Jun quickly called the emergency services.

The mobile phone call record showed the alarm was made at 1:27 AM.

Nineteen fire trucks rushed to extinguish the fire, with logistics support fuel trucks and fire scene gas supply vehicles also arriving.

After receiving the alarm, the Hangzhou Fire Department's 119 command center immediately dispatched rescue forces, with seven fire stations including Chaohui, Daguan, and Banshan sending out 19 fire trucks urgently for rescue operations.

The first to arrive was the Hangzhou Fire Department Chaohui team. Firefighter Zhan Bin said, "When we arrived, the fire was in a fierce burning stage and spreading rapidly into the interior of the supermarket. The scene was a sea of fire with thick smoke everywhere."

After confirming there were no trapped people inside the fire scene, the rescue personnel began firefighting. Two groups of firefighters separately used water guns from the northwest and southwest sides of the supermarket to suppress the visible flames.

Considering the large amount of flammable materials inside the supermarket, in order to minimize losses, the rescue personnel formed two firefighting teams, smashing through the glass exterior walls to forcibly enter the interior of the supermarket for internal attack firefighting, controlling the fire from spreading further into the interior.

"The indoor smoke was very thick, making it hard to see, with visibility less than half a meter. Moreover, the shopping carts on the second floor were haphazardly piled up, obstructing the passage and causing much trouble for firefighting."

Nineteen fire trucks were fully engaged in battle. Logistics support fuel trucks and fire scene gas supply vehicles acted as "nannies," rushing to the scene yesterday to provide logistical supplies to the participating fire trucks.

Around 2:00 AM, the visible flames were basically under control, and the fire was completely extinguished at 2:25 AM.

Yesterday afternoon, after on-site investigation by the fire department, the area affected by this fire was approximately 300 square meters: "A row of small shops on the ground floor of the western side of Tesco supermarket and the western exterior wall of the supermarket were heavily damaged. The area of the exterior wall was about 200 square meters, and the area inside the supermarket affected by the fire was about 100 square meters."

Food market affected, fortunately, the shops were insured

Tesco supermarket Desheng branch is connected to the Hangzhou Desheng Food Market.

Yesterday's big fire also affected the food market.

Yesterday morning, we saw at several shops on the second floor of the food market that although the fire did not spread to the market, several shops were flooded, including candy shops, walnut shops, tea shops, etc., with water dripping from the roofs of many shops.

Three food shops close to the outer side of the market were severely affected, with staff busy clearing items off the shelves. One shop specializing in walnut and tea wholesale suffered heavy losses, with water seeping through the floor and water still dripping from the ceiling, forcing the boss to relocate some dry goods.

Mr. Chen, the relevant person in charge of Hangzhou Desheng Food Market, stated that they had already contacted the insurance company for compensation. "We have insurance for all the shops, and in such cases, some compensation should be obtained to minimize the losses for the merchants."

Supermarket claims "losses are not significant," short-term resumption of business unlikely

After the incident, the Hangzhou Desheng Food Market continued normal operations, but the Tesco supermarket remained tightly closed, with warning lines also set up around the perimeter.

This has caused quite an impact on the surrounding residents.

Yesterday morning, many housewives gathered at the entrance of Tesco supermarket, most of whom were regular customers. They only learned about the fire upon reaching the entrance.

"Normally, I buy vegetables here every day. How did it suddenly catch fire?" Like Aunt Zhang, the housewives had no choice but to choose the nearby farmers' market. Fortunately, there is another supermarket not far away on Dongxin Road, and some residents joked that walking a few extra steps would count as exercise.

In the evening of yesterday, Fu Tao, Senior General Manager of the East China Regional Affairs Department of Tesco Supermarkets, accepted an interview with the reporter and stated that this fire did not cause too much loss to Tesco Supermarket.

Fu Tao said that the visible flames were mainly on the exterior wall of the supermarket, and did not spread inside. Only places like the mobile phone counter on the first floor and the cash register on the second floor were scorched. "According to our statistics, the loss situation is not significant, with some items being soaked by water." However, because cooperation with the fire department is needed to investigate the cause of the fire, the supermarket will be difficult to resume operations in the short term.
