Introduction: Marvell's brand new 8800 LED driver chip family will significantly reduce the cost of high-quality professional and consumer lighting LED technology, accelerating the pace of mass-market application of sustainable LED solutions. It can be used to develop high-end lighting products that are still more expensive for the general consumer market. On the 24th, Marvell Technology Group Ltd., a global leader in integrated chip solutions, announced the launch of the world’s first dual-channel intelligent LED driver chip 88EM8801. The chip leverages Marvell's unique power capabilities and the company's profound strength in highly integrated and advanced mixed-signal technology to integrate functions originally performed by multiple driver chips, micro-controllers, and numerous discrete components into a single chip. Marvell's new small form factor 88EM8801 drive chip, with on-chip intelligent digital control functions, can help customers easily apply high-end lighting and control features to mainstream professional and consumer lighting products such as downlights, Zhaga-compatible LED modules, PAR spotlights, AR lamps, energy-saving lamps, A19 bulbs, and MR bulbs. The 88EM8801 is the first product in Marvell's new 8800 Series of LED lighting driver chips. This product portfolio offers intelligent digital control functions, significantly reducing system design complexity and the cost required for high-end lighting projects. Through current adjustment, the new 8800 series can use lower-cost LEDs at the sub-file level to reduce system costs while maintaining constant luminous flux and color temperature throughout the LED lighting manufacturing process and long life cycle. Additionally, the 8800 series includes intelligent pulse width modulation (PWM) dimming control circuits and other digital dimming control mechanisms to achieve a dimming depth of as low as 0.1%. Besides reducing the size of the LED driver circuit, it also reduces design complexity and cost. The intelligent 88EM8801 brings high-quality light output while reducing the overhead of LED components, which typically account for 60% of the total cost of LED bulbs. With Marvell's dual-channel drive technology, the 88EM8801 provides an excellent solution that can mix and control LEDs of different colors to achieve a significantly high Color Rendering Index (CRI) and lumens per watt. Moreover, the 8801 solution provides up to 95% efficiency while also providing an on-chip wireless lighting control communication interface. Marvell has started providing samples and evaluation boards of the 88EM8801 to major OEMs.

Product Overview: Marvell's 88EM8801 incorporates four power MOSFETs, an I2C interface, and a one-time programmable (OTP) memory block, enabling accurate and effective current control of each LED string. Pre-calibration and compensation data written into the OTP memory tunes optical control signals via PWM and the I2C interface to reach target specifications. Due to its high level of integration, the 88EM8801 eliminates the need for most external components such as MOSFETs and rectifier diodes, significantly saving on bill of materials (BOM) cost and board space. The 88EM8801 configures a dual-channel synchronous-rectified buck topology, capable of providing up to 95% efficiency. Product details include:

- Independent control of two LED strings, allowing red LEDs to be mixed with cold white or blue and green LEDs for warm mild high-color rendering index.

- Up to 40V DC input.

- An LED channel current up to 1000mA, another LED channel built-in MOSFET up to 500mA.

- Dual-channel DC/DC synchronous buck with a high switching frequency of 800kHz, reducing component size.

- On-chip OTP memory block pre-stores compensation and calibration data.

- PWM control resolution up to 10 bits can achieve 0.1% dimming depth.

- I2C interface supports OTP memory programming and communication protocols such as ZigBee, WiFi, and powerline communications (PLC).

- PWM dimming input control eliminates input ripple.

- Soft-start.

- Overcurrent and temperature protection.

- Undervoltage lockout.
