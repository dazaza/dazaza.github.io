1. Therapeutic Diet

Food Name: "Western-style Scrambled Eggs." This dish has preventive and curative functions for insomnia and is highly regarded in Japan.

Specific Method: Place one tomato in boiling water, quickly retrieve it, then place it in cold water. Peel and slice the tomato, then sauté it in vegetable oil and set aside. Beat six eggs and mix them with 250-300 milliliters of milk. Add a little salt and pepper, then mix evenly. Cut 150 grams of cheese into small pieces and set aside. Heat 500 milliliters of salad oil. First, add the egg mixture, gently mixing it, then add the tomatoes, stir-frying until cooked.

Analysis: The key to this dish's sleep-inducing effect lies in the combination of rich protein and fats, while the tomatoes supplement vitamins and reduce greasiness. This complementary effect is worth promoting. When eating, note the following: it must be consumed during dinner, and tea should not be used to cut the grease. It’s unnecessary for one person to consume the entire portion at once.

2. Key Points of Rehabilitative Diets

Proteins entering the body are broken down into various amino acids, among which tryptophan serves as the raw material for synthesizing 5-hydroxytryptamine (serotonin), which is related to sleep. Therefore, consuming sufficient tryptophan can induce sleep. Foods rich in tryptophan include fish, meat, and eggs, but the most abundant sources are dairy products like milk, yogurt, and cheese. Since these dairy products contain a large amount of calcium that calms the nerves, they are particularly effective for insomnia. Drinking a glass of milk or yogurt before bed will have a good sedative effect.

For dinner or nighttime meals, you can consume more fatty foods. Since fats digest slowly, blood from the head concentrates in the gastrointestinal area, making people feel drowsy and aiding in falling asleep. Among fats, the best choice is plant oils rich in linoleic acid. You can use this oil to fry chicken legs, pork chops, oysters, and meat patties.

Before sleeping, you can drink alcoholic beverages in moderation, such as wine, beer, whiskey, or sake. These drinks have a certain degree of麻痹 (numbing) effect on the central nervous system, helping to calm the nerves and relieve tension, thus inducing sleep. Conversely, caffeinated beverages like tea and coffee stimulate brain neurons, so insomniacs should avoid them before sleeping.
