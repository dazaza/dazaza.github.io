Outsider talks about SEO chain wheel strategy First, let's explain what a SEO chain wheel (SEO link wheel) is: It refers to establishing a large number of independent websites/independent blogs or opening free blogs on major portal sites. These independent websites or blog groups unilaterally, strategically, and plannedly link tightly to a target main website (or main keyword) to be optimized. At the same time, each site or each blog also interlink sequentially, forming a tight spider web of links surrounding the main target website (or main keyword), thereby quickly enhancing the weight of the target main website (or main keyword) in search engine results and achieving the SEO goal of gaining keyword rankings for the main website. So, how to create a SEO chain wheel? SEO chain wheels and site cluster strategies were two of the most popular terms in the industry in 2010. In fact, a SEO chain wheel strategy can be implemented using a site cluster. Currently, many SEO chain wheels rely on blogs from major portals (Baidu Space, Sina Blog, Blog Bus, Hexun, etc.), because they have lower costs. If all were independent domain name websites, the cost would be relatively higher, but one advantage is that they won't be easily banned and the links remain stable. A disadvantage of portal blogs is that if you have too many anchor texts, they might get banned. Many people understand the chain wheel strategy, but very few actually implement it. This is because maintaining blogs/websites for a SEO chain wheel requires a significant amount of time and effort. Ideally, you should update articles daily and continuously add backlinks. Closely tie together normal anchor text imports/exports among various sites/blogs in the chain wheel, which is something many people implementing chain wheel strategies find hard to achieve. Most webmasters who create chain wheels: 1. Build a few free portal blogs, then add friendly links to the main site within the constructed free blogs. Blogs exchange friendly links with each other, and next, blog articles link to the main site without increasing mutual links between the blogs. This cannot enhance the entire chain wheel's weight, leading to a significant reduction in the chain wheel's effectiveness. The SEO chain wheel strategy is quite mature abroad, but it has only recently emerged domestically. If used properly, it can significantly improve your website's keyword rankings. However, improper application could waste time at best and affect the main site's keyword rankings at worst. Now, many webmasters apply multiple SEO chain wheels, linking the main sites of multiple chain wheels to the target site. This SEO chain wheel strategy should be the most effective. How to determine if your site has been penalized Many webmasters judge that their site has been penalized based on the phenomenon of keyword rankings dropping or disappearing entirely. In fact, there are many signs that a site has been penalized, especially as search engine algorithms develop, more and more phenomena indicate site penalties. 1. Site K'd, site:www.595seo.com returns no results. 2. Homepage K'd, site results lack the homepage. 3. Baidu snapshot overnight inclusion is normal, but keyword rankings are pulled down, indicating a drop in keyword rankings. Keyword ranking drops do not necessarily mean your site has been penalized, as it could be due to competition rising and you falling. If keyword rankings disappear or differ significantly from before, it suggests your site has been penalized. 4. Baidu snapshots suddenly stop updating, which is a precursor to site penalties. 5. Sudden sharp decrease in site inclusion, poor originality, or collected articles mostly deleted after three months. You can refer to: How to pseudo-original. 6. Internal pages K'd clean, site only left with the homepage. 7. Snapshot rollback, this is a clear sign of site penalties. 8. Articles published on the same day are included the same day, and searching for the article title shows rankings! Rankings and inclusions disappear the next day. This phenomenon could be due to Baidu's internal issues, as many sites experienced this during a certain period.
