Guangxi Luchuan becomes the Iron Pot Capital of China - Xingxing Iron Pot brand is the first

by longersing on 2009-07-09 16:16:39

In Luchuan County, located in the southeastern corner of Guangxi, unremarkable iron pots have gradually gained prominence, and are now known as the "Iron Pot Capital of China." However, the outdated model that relies on traditional manual labor and extensive production to achieve a numerical advantage has become a hidden pain for the "Iron Pot Capital." Yet, Guangxi Xingxing Iron Pots has relied on advanced technology reforms, combining mechanized production with handcrafted processing to perfection, becoming a nationally renowned iron pot brand.

"The 'Iron Pot Capital' Handcrafted"

Recently, reporters visited a factory located in the suburbs of Luchuan County, which appears to be abandoned at first glance. The deafening sound of machine polishing continues throughout the day without pause, while workers busily work alongside the production line. Newly delivered rough iron pot blanks are repeatedly polished by machines, sparks flying everywhere, gradually revealing the original form of the iron pot. Before final departure from the factory, the inside and outside of the iron pot must also be coated with special materials and placed into a high-temperature furnace for calcination.

In December 2006, Luchuan County was awarded the title of "China's Iron Pot Capital" by the National Productivity Promotion Center for Daily Hardware Industries. However, reporters observed that the entire production process still primarily relies on manual labor.

According to historical records, as early as the Wanli period of the Ming Dynasty, Luchuan began manufacturing iron pots, with a history spanning over 400 years. This industry started from traditional handicraft workshops and began to rapidly develop from the late 1960s to the early 1980s. During its peak, there were more than 70 iron pot production enterprises in Luchuan County, where the output of Luchuan iron pots once accounted for more than two-thirds of the national iron pot production.

Nowadays, Luchuan iron pots have become highly sought-after goods in the market, offering nine major series with nearly 300 specifications and over 1000 varieties, including civilian pots, enameled iron pots, and stainless steel edged pots.

A 400-Year-Old Industry Without a Famous Brand

Entering the late 1990s, due to small production scale, low technological content, and product uniformity, the Luchuan iron pot industry suffered significant losses. The profit margin for each ordinary iron pot was only a few cents or a few dollars, leading to the elimination of 50 iron pot production enterprises within the county.

Mr. Luo Shinan, Chairman of Luchuan Nanfa Kitchenware Co., Ltd., has witnessed the development journey of the Luchuan iron pot industry. When the domestic kitchenware industry transitioned towards home appliances, new types of kitchenware such as electric rice cookers, electric frying pans, and induction cookers gradually consumed the space of traditional kitchenware, yet the Luchuan iron pot industry remained complacent, missing a crucial opportunity.

Despite its 400-year history, Luchuan iron pots have yet to establish a true famous brand, leaving Mr. Luo Shunan perplexed. He particularly laments the lack of innovation awareness among local enterprises, resulting in a single product line and low technological content. More seriously, there is a lack of brand consciousness, failing to register trademarks effectively, thus remaining stuck in the production of low-end products with low selling prices, ultimately becoming a contract manufacturing base for major brands.

According to statistics from the Economic and Trade Bureau of Luchuan County, currently, a large number of Luchuan iron pots are directly supplied to well-known brands such as Midea, Supor, and Joyoung for their dedicated induction cooker pots, accounting for over 90% of the national market share for induction cooker-specific pots.

The Breakthrough Path of the "Iron Pot Capital"

Having long served as subcontractors, the people of Luchuan feel dissatisfied. In recent years, Luchuan County has spared no effort in promoting and building the "Luchuan Iron Pot" brand. They have successively convened an "Iron Pot Forum" attended by renowned national industry experts and carefully planned an "Iron Pot Culture Festival." While working for famous brands, the Luchuan iron pot industry quietly learns and borrows ideas.

"We initially made very large iron pots specifically for rural sales, getting larger and larger; now, the new generation of iron pots is getting smaller, smaller yet more refined, more refined yet more beautiful. A small wok can sell for nearly 200 yuan, with products sold nationwide and exported to the United States, Europe, and other places," said Luo Shunan, who remains confident about the industry's development.

County Magistrate Mo Xinrong of Luchuan believes that the Luchuan iron pot industry must make significant efforts in technological innovation, corporate restructuring, operational innovation, and brand packaging strategies to change the current situation of minor skirmishes and being satisfied with modest wealth. It is necessary to expand iron pot production beyond mere iron pots into the broader kitchenware field, and into related industries involving iron pot production, such as pig iron smelting, mechanical processing, mold manufacturing, etc., allowing the "golden phoenix" of iron pots to lay more "golden eggs."

It is reported that Luchuan is considering establishing the Luchuan Iron Pot Group Company and an industry association to unify and strengthen the "Luchuan Iron Pot" brand.
