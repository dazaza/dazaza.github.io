Chapter Fifty-Eight: On July 13, 2001, the whole country celebrated Beijing's successful Olympic bid. One word: "Awesome"! That day, Yezi and I drove to Chang'an Street like two happy children. We rolled down the windows, and Yezi waved a fiery red silk scarf she had specially retrieved from her cabinet.

Chang'an Street was brilliantly lit, crowded with jubilant people. We were infected by this atmosphere until we returned home late at night. Just after returning home, Yezi received a call from Xiaoyun. She woke me up as I was about to fall asleep and said, "Honey, get up, something happened at Xiaoyun's place!" I forced myself to get up, dressed, and drove with Yezi towards Xiaoyun's house.

What a sight! Xiaoyun's house was in utter chaos, everything that could be smashed was smashed, even the TV was lying on the ground. It looked like a battlefield, completely unrecognizable. I thought, what a unique way for this couple to celebrate the successful Olympic bid.

Chen Yang sat on a large box, his gold-rimmed glasses nowhere to be found. His eyes were swollen and red. Xiaoyun was pulling him by the hand, saying, "Get up!" Chen Yang, regardless of our presence, pleaded softly, "Don't go!"

Ah, it turned out this couple had been fighting. As the saying goes: "It's better to destroy ten temples than to break up one marriage." Although Yezi and I didn't know the cause of the quarrel, there was one thing we were sure of - it absolutely wasn't because Chen Yang had been fooling around outside. Even if Chen Yang wanted to fool around, he would have to actually do so, right? Therefore, whatever issues they had weren't fundamental problems. Non-fundamental problems were worth discussing and forgiving.

Just as we were about to step forward to mediate, we heard someone ringing the doorbell. Xiaoyun left Chen Yang to open the door, and two policemen walked in.

"Who called 110?" one asked.

"It was me," Xiaoyun replied.

"What's the problem? Is there an issue?"

"He won't let me leave," Xiaoyun pointed at Chen Yang. Poor Chen Yang stammered, "No, Officer, she..."

"What's your relationship with her?"

"We're not related," Xiaoyun's response was classic, almost laughable.

"If you're not related, why are you here?" the policeman stared at Xiaoyun.

"She's my girlfriend, she wants to break up with me, but I don't want her to leave. Officers, please don't let her go," Chen Yang looked pitiful, his face tear-streaked.

"You called 110 for this? You," one policeman pointed at Chen Yang, "get off the box and let her go!"

Chen Yang started crying, tears streaming down his face, "You...you heartless woman!"

That night, Yezi and I drove Xiaoyun back to Tianjin. This long-distance night drive exhausted me, and the next day, I almost fell asleep while negotiating business with clients. To say Xiaoyun's actions were extreme - I've never heard of anyone inviting the police to maintain order when breaking up with their boyfriend, and she even destroyed all of his belongings.

According to Xiaoyun, she never really loved Chen Yang in the first place. Initially, it wasn't too bad since Chen Yang was in Hong Kong and they only saw each other every two or three months. However, once Chen Yang moved to Beijing, Xiaoyun found it increasingly hard to tolerate him. In the end, it all came down to trivial matters, but fundamentally, Xiaoyun didn't love him, so many small things became problematic. They say couples who fight can always reconcile, but unfortunately, Chen Yang was inadequate in certain aspects, leading to frequent conflicts.

After numerous arguments, their feelings were damaged. Before midnight, Xiaoyun finally made up her mind. To ensure Chen Yang would hate her and never think of her again, she smashed everything in the house.

"Yezi, Haitao," Xiaoyun said, "don't laugh at me, I've realized that money and comfort mean nothing compared to love. From now on, I will live well, date well, and find a man who loves me and whom I love. Whether rich or poor doesn't matter as long as we love each other."

I glanced at Yezi in the rearview mirror nodding approvingly at Xiaoyun. She hugged Xiaoyun's shoulder and said, "Good for you, we support you. But, alas, you're all leaving. Xue'er is in America and won't be back until next year. Yiting has returned to Singapore. Now you're going back to Tianjin. If Haitao mistreats me in the future, you'll all be far away. Who will help me?"

"He dare? Li Haitao, if you dare mistreat Yezi, I'll pull out all your teeth!" Xiaoyun poked me with her finger and laughed. "Hey, by the way, you can ask Qingqing. She's coming back from Shanghai soon."

...

The film academy's advanced course lasted a year. Not long after Xiaoyun returned to Tianjin, Yezi officially graduated.

That day, I managed to squeeze time out of my busy schedule to buy a bouquet of red roses from the flower shop. The diamond ring in my pocket had been prepared for quite some time. As soon as I entered the house, I knelt down on one leg, startling Yezi who was completely unprepared. In my left hand, I held the flowers, and in my right hand, I raised the jewelry box high: "Dear Miss Ye Linglong, please marry me. I promise to love you forever."

Yezi took a while to recover. She buried her face in the bouquet of roses, her beautiful features blushing coyly. She said, "Baby, oh, baby, you've truly spoiled me!"

My kiss landed on Yezi's ear, then easily found her lips...

Rose petals scattered all over the floor, like rain...

Leaning against my chest, Yezi said, "Husband, I feel...a bit awkward getting married in this house. Let's sell or rent this apartment and buy another one. Anyway, it's sooner or later. We can't make it for National Day this year, so how about next May Day? What do you think?"

I had no objections. Yezi was right; this used to be the wedding house for me and Xiaolin. After so much wind and rain, I firmly believe that neither of us will accept anyone else besides each other. But currently, I'm extremely busy. Mr. Wang has entrusted me with all the company's affairs, and the title on my business card has changed from department manager to vice president of Yunhai Company. Thus, the house matters can only be handled by Yezi. However, Yezi has seen many houses but hasn't been very satisfied with any of them. Some she liked were priced at over ten thousand yuan per square meter, which we can't afford yet.

Finally, I suggested waiting for our company's housing project to launch. Since the project has already started, I believe it will officially launch next year.

Thus, the wedding date was set for next National Day.

Chapter Fifty-Nine: I clearly remember encountering Xiaoyu on September 11th because on that evening, the world watched the collapse of the American skyscrapers.

So, in the days that followed, whenever the US 9/11 incident was mentioned, I couldn't help but think of Xiaoyu, even though it had nothing to do with her.

That evening, I was holding Yezi's hand shopping. On our way to the parking lot, Yezi suddenly poked my palm and stopped.

Yezi was staring at a woman squatting on the ground selling pirated DVDs.

At that moment, the woman also looked up at Yezi. Then, she slowly stood up. As dusk fell, I recognized her face, and my heart sank.

Heaven! Xiaoyu, Qi Yuqiong!

Xiaoyu's hair was pulled back loosely behind her ears. Her face was thin, making her cheekbones more prominent. She wore a simple, loose sweater, a stark contrast to her glamorous image from a few years ago. Except for a faint trace of her former allure, she looked no different from an ordinary street vendor.

The two women stared at each other without blinking until Xiaoyu forced a sneer from the corner of her mouth and arrogantly said, "If you want to hit me, do it now. I won't fight back anyway. We've settled accounts long ago."

Yezi bit her lip. Honestly, I didn't know what she was thinking. She squatted down and picked up a stack of CDs from the black cloth bag on the ground, saying, "I'll take these."

At that moment, Xiaoyu glanced at me. She snorted through her nose and grabbed the CDs from Yezi's hand, "Sorry, I'm not selling anymore."

Yezi didn't show any strange expression. She turned to look at me. I furrowed my brows and shook my head, signaling for Yezi to leave.

Suddenly, commotion arose around us. Someone whispered, "Run, run, the industrial and commercial inspectors are coming!" The vendors scattered quickly. Xiaoyu hurriedly picked up her bag to run but was pulled back by Yezi. "Get in our car," Yezi said.

I was almost infuriated but couldn't stop her.

"When did you come out?" Yezi asked from the front seat without looking back.

"About a week ago," I looked at Xiaoyu through the rearview mirror, catching her gaze, still that bone-chilling look. I quickly shifted my eyes.

Not out of fear, but I felt an ominous aura around her, like a corpse crawling out of a grave.

"How...did you start selling CDs?" Yezi continued asking.

"Hmph, nonsense. Do you think I'd return to Diamond World in my current state? Isn't it fine to earn a living through my own labor? Why should only you live luxuriously? Can't I do a little business to support my family?"

"That's not what I meant. Why didn't you go back home?"

"My mom passed away last year. Why go back? I like Beijing. Here..." Xiaoyu snickered again, "Here holds everything I can't forget."

"Where to?" Afraid they would talk further, I interrupted and asked Xiaoyu. I feared most that Yezi might suggest letting Xiaoyu stay at our place, which would surely cause trouble.

Thank goodness, Xiaoyu spoke, and I dropped her off in front of a bungalow.

When she got out of the car, Yezi handed her a phone number through the car window, "If you need my help with anything, I'm willing to assist you."

Xiaoyu looked up at the moon just emerging in the sky and asked, "Do you really not hate me?"

"Past events...hating you, what's the point? Am I not fine now? Go home quickly." Yezi smiled and rolled up the window.

"How can you still invite her?" I asked Yezi.

"Poor Xiaoyu, look at her just now..."

"What just now?" I interrupted her impatiently, "Did she pity you when you cried yourself awake at night? Wounds healed, pain forgotten... Besides, do you not worry about what tricks she might play?"

"People loved you back then, didn't they? Everyone knows that. If I don't trust you now, who else in this world can I trust?"

"In that case, we should hurry up and get married. Night brings forth dreams, let's get married first."

"You, seriously, we haven't sorted out the house yet. We've gone through so much together. Xiaoyu has been reformed for over two years. Are people really that bad? Look, she's trying to lead a decent life now, doing legitimate business..."

"Legitimate? She's selling pirated discs!"

"Oh, I didn't mean that. I mean it's progress that she's no longer a prostitute... You're still holding a grudge, aren't you? Endless retaliation... Back then, she was ruthless, but, women, when they encounter love, lose their minds, right?"

"Ah, I can't argue with you." Since Yezi has a heart big enough to forgive, I, as a man, shouldn't be petty. "But, you mustn't get too close to her. Appropriate help is understandable, but remember, beware of others. Understand?"

"Hehe, understood, husband..." Yezi pinched my face lightly, "She might not even have the courage to call me. Rest assured, I won't be stupid enough to create trouble for myself. I know my limits."

"She'd better not call..."

In those days, I ran to the construction site every day, dealing with a pile of work at the real estate development. Every day, I was exhausted like hell, wishing a day could be split into two.

One night, Yezi lay beside me and asked, "Husband, Xiaoyu called me. Would it be okay if I bring her along when I see the production team again?"

"Are you crazy? Why bring her?"

"Selling pirated discs isn't a good job, it's kind of pitiful. If I could bring Xiaoyu to shoot an advertisement or something, that would be good. If she tidies up her appearance, she wouldn't be bad. It would be helping her."

"Do you think others are fools? She's never studied acting, and besides, she just got out. If this gets out, who would dare hire you for filming? Why bother with unnecessary kindness?"

"Eh, shooting an ad doesn't require acting skills, does it? Even I am just a minor role, who would care about her? Do you think this is Hong Kong? Gossip spreads in the entertainment industry even over trivial matters. I just want her to earn a living."

"Earning a living? Be careful, she might bite you."

Yezi giggled and left two rows of little teeth marks on my shoulder.

It was rumored that Xiaoyu later indeed accepted an advertisement for a brand of detergent, and this was just the beginning. Due to Xiaoyu's smoothness, versatility, and willingness to go all out in certain areas, she quickly grasped some of the entertainment industry's ins and outs. Soon after, she dated a boyfriend, reportedly a screenwriter.

Xiaoyu concealed her past as a hostess at Diamond World and her two and a half years in prison, claiming she graduated from an art school in Jilin and came to Beijing to realize her childhood dream of becoming a star.

She didn't cause any trouble for Yezi. Sometimes, I thought, people change. Perhaps she had genuinely reformed.

At this point, Yezi, through her natural beauty and relentless effort, was gradually gaining recognition in her acting career, showing signs of emerging talent.

Sometimes when Yezi went out of town to shoot, she called every day to tell me sweet words of longing. Each time, the fatigue from work disappeared, and my heart overflowed with tenderness.

I often thought at night, when should I find an opportunity to talk to Yezi? I'm not opposed to her starting a new life or becoming famous through acting. I just have a little concern. After all, this circle is too complicated and unpredictable. What if my beloved gets hurt?
