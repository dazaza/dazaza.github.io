"守候" can be translated into English as "wait" or "guard", depending on the context. If it refers to waiting for someone or something, "wait" would be more appropriate. If it implies protecting or keeping watch over something, "guard" would be a better choice.

He was successful in his career, and I imagined that he should live in a garden villa. Unexpectedly, when I went to visit him, I found out that he lived in an old-style barrack building in the old district, a very shabby house.

After we settled our business, he invited my friend and me to have dinner with him. We agreed to eat at a hotel outside, but just before the meal, his wife, children, and mother were there giving way to each other, because they wanted to leave someone at home to watch the house. The three people who were giving way all insisted on letting others go to the hotel for dinner, indicating their willingness to stay behind. After much arguing, everyone still couldn't persuade his elderly mother, so she was left at home.

This made me feel very apologetic and confused. How could we leave the old lady alone at home when we went to eat? Was it really necessary to leave someone at home to watch the house?

During the meal, I apologized to him repeatedly about this matter. He smiled and said, "It's nothing, it's been a habit for over twenty years. There must always be someone staying at home."

Why? Are you afraid of thieves? At this question, his 8-year-old son loudly interrupted, "No way! We're waiting for someone! My grandma is waiting for my aunt!"

I still didn't quite understand. Nowadays, phones are convenient, you can just call and tell the person. Why does someone have to wait at home? So he explained the reason to me.

He has a sister. When the sister was 9 years old, one day she went to school and never came back. The family went to the school to look for her, but the teachers and classmates all said she didn't come to school that day. They had to report it to the police and then searched everywhere, but they never got any news of the sister. Everyone suspected that the sister had been abducted by human traffickers.

From the day his sister disappeared, the family began a long process of searching. His father almost ran through all places in the two provinces near their home. Even on his deathbed, his father was still muttering his sister's name. No matter how the family went out to search, there was always someone staying at home. Because the sister knew the phone number of the house and where the house was located. They thought, if the sister was really abducted by human traffickers, maybe she would find a chance to call home or escape when the traffickers weren't paying attention. Someone had to stay at home to answer the phone and wait for the sister to return.

This wait lasted for more than twenty years. For over twenty years, their phone number has never changed. No matter how busy they were, there was always someone staying at home. Later, the local phone numbers were upgraded from six digits to seven digits. For this matter, his mother cried anxiously, fearing that if the sister called the original number, she wouldn't be able to get through to the house. She argued, caused a scene, and begged at the telecommunications department to retain her six-digit number, but the phone number upgrade was city-wide, and the telecommunications department was powerless. He could only comfort his mother, telling her that if the sister dialed the original six-digit number, there would be a voice prompt guiding her to dial the upgraded number. His mother tried dialing the six-digit number, and indeed there was a prompt in the phone, so she calmed down. But he knew that such a voice prompt couldn't last long, and after a period of time, it would no longer prompt.

Gradually, as his career became successful, he started his own company and bought a car. It was hard to find parking spaces in the old district where the barrack building was, so he always had to park far away from home. This was inconvenient, so he thought about moving. But his mother refused to leave the barrack building, worrying that her daughter might come back and not be able to find her. She told them to move, saying she would continue living alone in the old house. How could he let his mother live alone here, while he had an urgent desire to move? He knew that after such a long time, it was impossible for his sister to come back, so he asked a female employee from his company to pretend to be his sister and deceive his mother. He thought that after such a long time, his mother must have forgotten what his sister looked like. The female employee called home and met his mother, calling her "Mom," and his mother treated her as a daughter, but still refused to move. He then realized that no matter how long the time passed, the memory of her children in a mother's heart could never fade. How could a mother not recognize her own daughter?

However, in the end, the female employee became his wife, and they got married and had children. No one mentioned moving again, and their family continued to live in the barrack building. No one complained about the shabbiness of the house. His sister has been missing for over twenty years, and if she were still alive, she should be over thirty years old. Over these twenty years, there has always been someone at home. No matter how busy or how important the event, there was always someone staying at home, waiting for his sister's call, waiting for his sister to suddenly come home.

Listening to his story, I felt both heartbroken and moved. An unfortunate incident completely changed the family's living habits. Someone was always at home, waiting for a person who might never return.
