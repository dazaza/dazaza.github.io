Hidden food safety hazards in bakery shops

by hdghu19056 on 2012-02-20 14:44:20

On July 28, Beijing announced its "12th Five-Year" food safety action plan, increasing the requirements for the transparency of food-related information. Consumers can report any food safety hazards at any time. However, a recent investigation by reporters into several well-known bread and pastry shops in the city found that issues such as insufficient weight of bread, failure to clearly indicate food additives, incomplete or incorrect price tag information were quite common.

Experts Suggest Strengthening Supervision

Regarding the reasons why bread and pastry shops don't take "price tag information" seriously, Associate Professor Liang Jianfen from the College of Food Science and Nutritional Engineering at China Agricultural University analyzed that on one hand, consumers who shop at branded bread and pastry shops generally have stronger purchasing power and are more concerned about the taste and style of the bread rather than its weight; on the other hand, relevant regulations are not very strict regarding the weight requirements of ready-to-eat foods, and there is relatively more leniency in restrictions on whether restaurant dishes, snacks, breads, and pastries are sufficient in quantity. Therefore, some bread and pastry shops don't care much about this. It is hoped that relevant departments can establish more detailed enforcement criteria for such special industries.

Bread Goes Flat Overnight

Mr. Liu bought a few loaves of bread at a bread and pastry shop located southeast of the Sihong Bridge yesterday noon. Due to his busy work schedule, he quickly chewed down two pieces and placed the remaining meat floss bread in a bag to bring home.

The next morning, Mr. Liu found that the two fist-sized meat floss breads had collapsed into a thin strip shape. "I didn't press it, I didn't squeeze it, how did it turn out like this?" Mr. Liu was puzzled.

Ms. He, on the other hand, encountered an issue with the reduced weight of her bread. She went to a bread and pastry shop near the Cuimai Department Store at Princess Tomb and has been buying bread regularly for breakfast. Recently, she noticed that the price of bread has increased significantly but it doesn't last long when eating, as three or four bites would finish one loaf. It's hard to feel full without spending over 20 yuan on two or three loaves. Thus, she decided to check how much the bread actually weighed.

A price tag stood in front of the meat floss bread tray, indicating a weight of 120 grams. Initially, Ms. He thought nothing of it, but she suddenly noticed that the hot dog bread lying next to the meat floss bread also weighed 120 grams. The hot dog bread was significantly larger than the meat floss bread and contained a sausage as "hard goods" to support it, yet both breads were indicated to weigh the same.

Ms. He purchased this meat floss bread and asked the staff to weigh it, but the staff replied, "Our store does not provide this service."

Fifteen minutes later, Ms. He arrived at her workplace canteen and asked the chefs to help her weigh the meat floss bread. The electronic scale clearly showed "60 grams," which was half the weight indicated on the price tag.

Ms. He was furious. She called the complaint hotline of the bakery and demanded an explanation for "how the bread was weighed." Initially, the staff stated, "The bread's weight was uniformly measured upon leaving the factory," then they returned the call to inform: "The dough of the meat floss bread weighs 50 grams, the meat floss weighs 12 grams, adding up to around 60 grams after accounting for loss. The 120 grams indicated on the price tag was due to a printing error, and they expressed willingness to compensate the consumer."

Labels Do Not Clearly Indicate Additives

Are Mr. Liu and Ms. He encountering blacklisted stores? Over the past two days, reporters visited a total of seven different brands of well-known bread and pastry shops located at Princess Tomb Cuimai Department Store, Shuangjing Bridge R&F Plaza, Oriental Square, and Chongwenmen New World Mall. Hardly any of these shops followed the relevant regulations to clearly indicate product information.

At a bread and pastry shop located southwest of Shuangjing Bridge, people queued up continuously to buy bread, making long queues a common sight. However, apart from the name and price information, no other details including weight were provided on the price tags. When the reporter asked the staff, they appeared confused and asked the reporter, "What information needs to be labeled?"

In a bread and pastry shop on the west side of the road near Cuimai Department Store, seventy percent of the price tags included weight information, and most of these tags indicated a weight of 120 grams.

Among the above-visited bread shops, none of them used various signs to clearly inform customers about the food additive components in the bread. Experts told reporters that during the bread-making process, "it is inevitable to add food additives."

Citizen Ms. Zhao believes that when bread is sold, consumers can only visually see the ingredients placed on top of the bread such as bacon, ham, meat floss, and fruits. Whether food additives are added to the bread cannot be seen. Only through clear notification can consumers purchase with peace of mind.
