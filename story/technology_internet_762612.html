Taobao draws a national "insomnia map": Shanghai is the city where people are most likely to be sleepless.

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 16:19:55

"Every day, I go to bed around 11 o'clock, then I watch videos and browse online stores on my phone, and it's not until around 1 o'clock that I start feeling sleepy." Mr. Ding, a 30-year-old white-collar worker in a commercial building on Moganshan Road in Hangzhou, complained. In fact, more and more people are starting to have trouble sleeping. "People who sleep are all similar, but those who can't sleep have their own reasons," "Dear, why can't you sleep?" Taobao store owners take advantage of this trend to sell "sleep aid" products.

Yesterday, a set of data published by Taobao was also quite interesting. People who can't sleep have very obvious regional characteristics. In the top 5 cities with the highest per capita purchase volume of eye masks and earplugs, these two sleep aids, in the past 5 months, all four first-tier cities - Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen - made the list collectively. Hangzhou is the only non-first-tier city among the top five, ranking third.

The most active "insomniacs":

23 million people shop online at midnight

"At this time, is there anyone still awake?" Nowadays, such late-night insomnia tweets have become ubiquitous. The "insomnia tribe" instantly transforms into the "night shopping tribe", using mobile Taobao to shop while lying in bed. The passion for shopping does not fade with the deepening night; instead, late at night becomes one of the times with a high mobile online shopping order rate.

"In such hot weather, I definitely won't go out shopping during the day, so when I can't sleep at night, I use the time to shop." Miss Li from Hangzhou is a typical night owl. She said she often has no sleepiness after 12 o'clock, so she opens her mobile Taobao and browses. "Yesterday, in just an hour, I bought 2 pairs of sandals and 1 dress." Finally, with the satisfaction of shopping, she finally fell asleep. However, the money flowed out every day.

There are not a few such "night shopping tribes". According to the data disclosed by Taobao, in 2012, a total of 22.832 million people browsed Taobao between 11 pm and 5 am. Among them, many men stayed up late browsing Taobao, accounting for 48% of the total, while women accounted for 52%.

The most insomnia-prone provinces:

Jiangsu, Guangdong, Zhejiang

What to do if you really can't sleep? More and more people are beginning to rely on external tools like earplugs and eye masks to fall asleep. Clever online store owners have obviously already spotted the business opportunity. A search for earplugs on Taobao revealed over 200,000 items for sale, and nearly 90,000 eye masks.

The "sleep aid market" is quite promising. Just the product of eye masks comes in various types such as 3D, cartoon patterns, summer-appropriate ice packs, silk eye masks, bamboo charcoal eye masks, etc. Since the start of summer, ice pack eye masks have become very popular. On one Taobao store, the monthly transaction volume of a single item exceeded ten thousand pieces.

Many sellers also make great efforts on pillows, launching lavender pillows and buckwheat pillows for calming the mind, and even foods like millet and dates are labeled as promoting sleep.

The "insomnia map" shows that according to Taobao's transaction data over the past 5 months, looking at the proportion of people buying earplugs and eye masks among the total city population, all four first-tier cities - Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen - made the list. Hangzhou, which ranks third, is the only non-first-tier city among them.

Top 10 cities for purchasing earplugs: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chongqing, Ningbo.

Top 10 cities for purchasing eye masks: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, Guangzhou, Chongqing, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chengdu.

Shanghai is the city with the most severe insomnia. Jiangsu, Guangdong, and Zhejiang are the three provinces with the worst sleep.

"Can't sleep at night, can't wake up in the morning" forms a vicious cycle. The cities where people have more trouble sleeping at night are also the cities where people have the hardest time waking up in the morning. In the past 5 months, the top five cities in terms of the total population purchasing alarm clocks are the same: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, and Guangzhou.

"I need to wear earplugs every night to fall asleep, but this way, I can't hear my phone's alarm in the morning. After setting my phone alarm to vibrate, I'm afraid it won't be powerful enough to wake me up, so I bought a vibrating alarm clock." Xiao Mao's "sleeping" and "waking up" equipment has become a necessity for many insomniacs.

The most helpless reason for buying earplugs:

Husband snores

So, who are the people suffering from insomnia? Online store owners know best. "The number of people who need earplugs to sleep increases every year, more customers consult us, and the age range is getting wider. Basically, students and urban white-collar workers account for 90% of the total sales," said the owner of an online store selling earplugs.

Look, the store's signature product - earplugs priced at 19 yuan each - sold 19,000 pieces in just one month.

Why can't everyone sleep? There are actually many reasons. It is understood that students buy eye masks and earplugs possibly because they live in school dormitories. Mobile devices are now widespread among students, and after bedtime, some classmates may still be playing games. To avoid being disturbed, they simply "shut themselves off."

White-collar workers have relatively higher work pressure during the day, leading to sleep problems at night. Some amusing buyers cited "my husband's snoring is too loud" as the reason for buying earplugs.

According to Taobao data, the majority of buyers of earplugs and eye masks are "post-90s" and "post-80s." Taking the eye mask sales in the past 5 months as an example, the "post-90s" are the largest demand group, with 36% of the eye masks purchased by people aged 18-24. Additionally, people aged 25-29 account for more than 30% of the purchases.

And from the perspective of the purchasing ratio of various products, men are more likely to have insomnia than women, with about 20% more male insomniacs than female.
