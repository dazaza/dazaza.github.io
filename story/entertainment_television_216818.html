In the year of a heavy summer storm that caused widespread flooding, Chen Zhong could not bear to see the farmers of Liangcai County suffer from natural disasters. He secretly opened the government granary to provide relief to the farmers. Consequently, he faced criticism from the court and was eventually exiled to the frontier, where he fell ill and died, leaving behind his only daughter, Chen Ximei.

Fortunately, Mr. Zhu Qingting treated Ximei as his own daughter, raising her like part of the family. The farmers of Liangcai County also regarded Ximei as their own child. Older women became her godmothers, and older men became her godfathers. Ximei grew up happily, inheriting her late father's chivalrous spirit, becoming a passionate protector of the farmers. She solved many bizarre cases.

Xie Huangshang was known for his literary talent, intelligence, and ambition to be an upright official. However, his father, Xie Cangtian, was a profit-driven rice merchant. Despite this, Cangtian deeply understood his son’s nature and aspirations. To fulfill his son's wish, he donated a large sum of money to secure an official position for him as an agricultural officer. He also hired Dai Congwen, a clever strategist nicknamed "Great Lord," as his son's advisor to assist and resolve issues.

Liang Feiyuan pursued revenge on behalf of his sister Xiaodie against Huangshang. Xiaodie, a famous courtesan in Hangzhou, had fallen in love with a man claiming to be Xie Huangshang, the young master of the Dali Rice Store in Liangcai County. However, Xiaodie was deceived and left destitute by Huangshang, leading her to drink poisoned wine and commit suicide. Feiyuan found evidence in Xiaodie's diary implicating Huangshang, which placed Huangshang in grave danger. Meanwhile, Su Zhen Dong took over as the new magistrate of Liangcai County and personally investigated the case. With deep resentment towards Congwen and having received bribes from the real culprit, Zhen Dong intended to pin the crime on Huangshang. Fortunately, Congwen meticulously uncovered the truth, proving that someone else was responsible for Xiaodie's death, thus saving Huangshang.

Later, in the perfume murder case, Huangshang and Congwen used their intelligence to discover that the victim was not the person they initially thought. They sympathized with each other, strengthening their relationship.

Soon after, Tang Hai's father, Tang Dahai, was murdered. The case involved Zhen Dong's younger brother, Su Zhen Nan, but it turned out that Hai was accidentally killed by Zhu Qingting. To conceal the truth, Ximei took the blame for her brother. When Ximei was charged, Huangshang, desperate to save her from prison, devised a plan to marry her falsely. Once Ximei became part of the Xie family, she was protected by the imperial mandate granting Huangshang his official position. This act deeply moved Ximei.

On another note, Congwen left Liangcai County to search for his twin brother, Congwu. Unfortunately, Congwu had already passed away. Grieving but determined, Congwen decided to impersonate Congwu, a corrupt official, and returned to Liangcai County as the newly appointed magistrate.
