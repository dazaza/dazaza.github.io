Sina's Premier League Round Best XI: No Manchester United players, Korean winger selected.

In the challenging away match against high-powered Tottenham Hotspur, Wolves goalkeeper Rui Patricio made an impressive seven saves, securing a clean sheet against his opponents. Notably, this was only the third league appearance for the American goalkeeper this season. However, in these three games, Wolves conceded only two goals, helping the team achieve an excellent record of two wins and one loss. Who would have thought that Patricio is already 37 years old!

Defenders: Branislav Ivanovic (Chelsea), Sol Campbell (Portsmouth), Jody Craddock (Wolves), Emilio Nsue (Wigan)

Despite being held by Everton at home, Chelsea’s manager wasn't without his gains, notably right-back Branislav Ivanovic. Ivanovic's two assists helped Drogba and Anelka each score a goal. Two assists, five key passes, and three successful tackles - after first-choice right-back Bosinwa's injury, Ivanovic has been performing satisfactorily both offensively and defensively. At the 93rd minute, it seemed Portsmouth would suffer another defeat away from home, but Campbell came to the rescue with a crucial follow-up shot, earning Pompey a vital point for their relegation battle. Besides, Campbell contributed ten clearances as a center-back and was awarded a match-high 9 points by Sky Sports. Regrettably, he received a second yellow card just before the final whistle for celebrating by removing his shirt. For newly-promoted Wolves to secure three points at White Hart Lane, captain Jody Craddock was absolutely instrumental. In both the experts' and fans' ratings on Sky Sports, Craddock received the highest score of the match. This season, Craddock has scored four goals in the league, showing his prowess both offensively and defensively. A stunning 60-meter free-kick strike by Figueroa resulted in a world-class goal, securing a point for Wigan at Stoke City's notorious home ground. Additionally, the 26-year-old Honduran left-back provided one key pass during the game, earning him a match-high 9 points from Sky Sports.

Midfielders: Lee Chung-yong (Bolton), Kevin Davies (Birmingham), Ashley Young (Aston Villa)

One assist and several brilliant passes saw Lee Chung-yong make his first appearance in Sky Sports' weekly best XI since joining the Premier League. Sky Sports' experts rated him 8 out of 10, while fans gave him an even higher 9.1. Bolton's chances of staying up could hinge significantly on Lee's performances. Another last-minute winner from Bob Paisley ensured Birmingham City extended their unbeaten run in the Premier League to four consecutive victories. During this period, Bob Paisley delivered three decisive strikes. Under the ownership of Chinese businessman Kenny Yeung, Birmingham has achieved an impressive record of five wins and two draws in their last seven matches, with the two draws coming against Liverpool and Manchester City. Now, they sit proudly in eighth place in the Premier League standings, level with Liverpool. Aston Villa's historic first away win over Manchester United in 26 years owed much to Ashley Young. His assist for Gabriel Agbonlahor was voted the best pass of the week by Sky Sports. With Steven Gerrard occupying England's starting left-midfield spot, Ashley Young can compete for the backup role.

Forwards: Carlos Tevez (Manchester City), Didier Drogba (Chelsea), Andrey Arshavin (Arsenal)

After scoring a stunning free-kick to beat Chelsea last round, Carlos Tevez once again shone brightly in the away game against Bolton. His brace helped Manchester City twice draw level when they were down. These were Tevez's fourth goals in his last three matches across all competitions. Besides the two goals, Tevez also provided two key passes, two successful tackles, and won two free-kicks. The Argentine's form is getting better and better. Although Chelsea didn't walk away with all three points, Didier Drogba still managed to score twice, showcasing a performance befitting a superstar that even the harshest critics couldn't fault. This season, Drogba has scored 13 goals in the Premier League and 18 across all competitions. We must now accept that Chelsea with or without Drogba are two entirely different teams. Despite not being overly active throughout the game, stars are stars. With one precise shot, Andrey Arshavin secured a place in our weekly best XI for his decisive strike against Liverpool. At Anfield, the Russian Tsar always delivers spectacular performances. Remember the epic 4-4 clash in April where Arshavin scored all four goals for Arsenal? That shut up all his doubters.
