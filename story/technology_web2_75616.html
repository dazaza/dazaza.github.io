Web 2.0 Mini Review (1) - RSS Subscription Services (Domestic Edition) | Net Walker's Journal

by cpxxpc on 2008-06-26 08:34:49

Catch Shrimp [Brief Review]: Currently the best and most influential RSS subscription website domestically. [Features]: Convenient and quick personal submission and collection, supports OPML list import/export, detailed and comprehensive channel lists, the hot articles section cleverly combines RSS subscriptions with Digg mining, excellent group function.

Fresh Fruit [Brief Review]: A latecomer, its basic functions bear a deep imprint of Catch Shrimp, but there is still a gap. [Features]: A relatively rich set of affiliated widgets, such as recommended articles can be easily displayed on blogs, and provides a short domain personal recommendation homepage.

Sofa Reader [Brief Review]: An RSS reader launched by Souyou Blog Search Engine, quite different from the previously mentioned sites. [Features]: Numerous unique features that are worth further research.

BoLing [Brief Review]: A specialized RSS submission and subscription website. [Features]: Automatically adds voting and pagination buttons to the original webpage links, possesses a Digg style.

Bangkan [Brief Review]: The most special feature of this site is that it can submit webpages even without RSS functionality and track them in real-time for us. [Features]: Using the Bangkan toolbar allows convenient tracking and collection of any webpage; its collection page can be self-edited from the original webpage, possessing network note-taking characteristics (like 360doc).

Nezha [Brief Review]: A professional blog/news/entertainment/RSS personalized information subscription and delivery service website, slogan--information arrives. [Features]: Can independently choose or add RSS feeds for subscription, supports OPML import/export, through integration with MSN, Gtalk, Skype (add Nezha bot as a friend), it realizes the unique function of IM software delivering information, subscription methods and related widgets are relatively abundant (specifics can be referenced here), provides a personal subscription sharing page with a short domain.

Fat Gourd [Brief Review]: A distinctive website combining RSS subscriptions with mobile reading. [Features]: Through a special QR code, the RSS subscription function can be extended to mobile phones.
