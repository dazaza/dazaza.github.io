The school requires students to be fined 1 yuan for each mark below the full score and rewards those who get high scores.

by anonymous on 2012-03-07 17:41:31

Weibo recommendation | Today's Weibo hot topics: The county education bureau director, Liu Yuhong, stated that such phenomena are absolutely not allowed, and he will arrange relevant personnel to participate in the investigation.

Before the article was published, the reporter informed the Huoqiu County Education Bureau of this situation.

Response: Teachers have good intentions but inappropriate measures

Subsequently, the reporter went to the classroom of Class 9(3), where Wang Mingnan (a pseudonym) told the reporter that his English score was poor, and he was fined more than ten yuan, but then received a five-yuan reward for his math exam. "I haven't even recovered my 'capital' yet."

Progress: The education authorities will be involved in the investigation

If a student's test score is below standard, they are fined one yuan for each point below the standard, which is used to reward high-scoring students. On December 20, a parent named Li Xiangdong (a pseudonym) reported to our newspaper that his child is in the ninth grade, and studying is already very hard. Instead of being cared for after doing poorly on exams, the teacher requires them to take money out to "reward" classmates who did well. The parent said that the teacher’s good intentions can be understood, but as a parent, it cannot be accepted. The head of the junior high school department at Huoqiu County Agricultural Machinery School admitted that such incidents did occur, and the teacher’s methods were inappropriate and have been stopped. Currently, the local education bureau has expressed that they will participate in the investigation.

He introduced that this method improved the average score of the whole class significantly. "Later, three students from Class 9(3) wrote to the 'Principal's Mailbox' about this matter, so I had the whole class vote on whether to continue. At that time, students with mid-level or lower scores were quite worried, and only a minority agreed to continue. Finally, after the first monthly exam, I stopped it and had the class president return the money to the penalized students."

According to Li Xiangdong's introduction, his child is in the ninth grade at Huoqiu County Agricultural Machinery School Junior High School Department. More than a month ago, the homeroom teacher, Mr. Chen, required students who performed poorly on exams to contribute money to reward those who did well.

The school principal, Li Lintao, explained: "The incident reflected by the parents and students is true. The teacher's intention was certainly to encourage the students' learning enthusiasm. The collected fines were held by the class president, rewarding those who scored well, while temporarily holding onto the money of those who scored poorly, returning it if they perform better next time."

Li Xiangdong said that studying in the ninth grade is already very hard, and the pressure is also very great. Some students who performed poorly were even fined dozens of yuan. Originally, children who performed poorly wouldn't dare tell their parents, let alone about the fine. They would either borrow from others, or worse, steal money from home. "Such fines started in the seventh grade and have continued for three years now."

Student Cheng also introduced that when he was in the seventh grade, he was fined eight yuan. In the eighth grade, the teacher calculated based on rankings; if a student dropped five places compared to the previous exam, they would be fined five yuan. "Up until now, I've been fined nearly twenty yuan."

This student's parent also told the reporter that when the child was fined eight yuan in the seventh grade, he cried all the way home.

Investigation: One student was fined up to 24 yuan

Reflection: Children must pay fines for performing poorly on exams

On December 20, the reporter went to the county agricultural machinery school junior high school department for an investigation. A student from Class 9(3), Cheng Xiaoguo (a pseudonym), told the reporter: "For single-subject tests worth 150 points, if a student's score is below 105 points (i.e., 70% of the total score), the homeroom teacher, Mr. Chen, will impose a fine of one yuan for every point below the standard. The collected fines will be used to reward students who performed well. During the last monthly exam, I was also fined a few yuan. One student in the class was fined up to 24 yuan."
