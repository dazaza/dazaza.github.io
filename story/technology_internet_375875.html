Bai Gengxiao: Looking at website planning from Mu Changqing's overnight fame with 28 Tuī

by anglebarie on 2010-12-26 19:32:40

Among the recent big events in the webmaster circle, 28Tui.com’s grand appearance is definitely worth mentioning. Led by webmaster Mu Changqing and his 28 apprentices, 28Tui.com was officially launched on Singles' Day. In just a short half day, the number of registered users approached 2000, the number of posts reached as high as 4000, and Baidu quickly indexed the site within 3 hours of its launch, making all industry insiders take notice.

Why has 28Tui been so successful? Many experts have made comments and analyses, but they often focus on the success of its promotional methods, neglecting the success of its pre-launch website planning.

For a website to be successful, the first step is website planning. Without good planning, even the strongest promotional methods will find it difficult to achieve breakthroughs and success. Taking 28Tui as an example, let's look at some small tricks in website planning.

1. Website Positioning

Website positioning is a key point that all webmasters must carefully consider before building a site. Take 28Tui for example. Mu Changqing can be considered a highly renowned veteran in the industry, with extensive involvement in various fields and rich resources and connections in different industries. If he had launched a portal site, a group-buying site, or a movie or novel site, it might not have failed. However, he ultimately chose his most proficient and focused field - online marketing promotion sharing and interaction. From this, we can see that when webmasters build a site, they must not act impulsively and rashly enter industries they are not skilled in or dislike.

Another point about website positioning that cannot be ignored is that before building a site, we must define our target user (customer) group and which percentage of the market we hope to capture according to the Pareto principle (80-20 rule). By analyzing the consumption psychology and audience psychology of different groups, we can better plan our website (I will analyze this point in more detail in future articles).

2. Pre-launch Promotion (Online Marketing)

Many webmasters fall into the same trap: build the site first, then promote it. This is actually a very foolish approach and can easily put the website into a stagnant situation early on, losing confidence. As the ancient military strategists often said: "Before the troops move, the provisions go ahead." This is equally applicable to today's webmasters. Let's look at Mu Changqing's 28Tui. Before its official launch, large-scale promotion had already been carried out for quite some time.

• On Mu Changqing's blog, there was constant promotion. Since Old Mu's blog has always been highly recognized and has extraordinary traffic, naturally, 28Tui was well-known before its official launch, laying a popular foundation for its successful debut.

• Similarly, on Old Mu's microblog, there was constant promotion of the soon-to-be-born 28Tui. With the support of his fans, 28Tui became famous even before its birth, making many newcomers in the industry eagerly await its arrival.

• Most importantly, we must mention Old Mu's apprentice recruitment campaign. Using the event of recruiting apprentices as a way to promote the website, Old Mu brought the essence of event marketing to its peak, which was also facilitated by his own celebrity effect. Almost no one in the online promotion world does not know Old Mu. Such a veteran recruiting apprentices naturally attracted countless new and experienced people to rush in. And Old Mu's method of gathering apprentices through tasks was surprisingly effective. Only 28 apprentices were recruited, but the number of candidates participating in the tasks was N times 28, and their promotion efforts for 28Tui were absolutely powerful.

From these three points, we can see that the promotion before the website's launch often brings better results than promoting after the website is built. Webmasters may want to consider this. For companies hoping to achieve breakthroughs through online marketing, this is a valuable experience worth learning from.

The above two points are just some experience and small tricks in website planning. Due to limited space, I won't elaborate too much.
