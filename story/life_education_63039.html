Let's talk about the names of the buildings in my school.

by zntzls on 2008-04-15 13:12:28

The names of the buildings in our school, except for Zhongnan Building (the main building of Shouyi Campus) and Zhongyuan Building (the main building of Nanhu Campus), are all called "Wen * Building", and the second character is always from the water department. Compared with some schools' "* number" buildings, they naturally have more culture and much deeper meaning.

Nanhu Campus

Wenlan Building: The teaching building of Nanhu Campus, the first modern teaching building of our school, named after the first president Mr. Wenlan Fan. Since then, the names of our school's buildings have followed the characters "wen" and "water".

Wenbo Building: Opposite to Wenlan Building, one south and one north, taking the meaning of "calm waves".

Wentai Building: Teaching building behind Xixian Ridge. The naming intention is unknown. I speculate that because it is behind Zhongyuan Building, it probably takes the meaning of being calm and as solid as Taishan Mountain. It can be seen that the "mountain" of our school is stable. But if the character is interpreted as "spring water", I don't know what it means.

The above three buildings are modern teaching buildings.

Wenhong Building: Originally the teaching building of Central South University of Politics and Law, now the North Building is the Intellectual Property School, and the South Building is still a teaching building.

Wenqing Building: The location of the Forensic Appraisal Center, symbolizing restoring people's innocence.

Wenzhi Building: The location of the Law School and Public Security College, symbolizing rule of law.

Wensu Building: The old library, now the site of the School History Museum and Legal Culture Research Institute, related to history, hence called "su".

Wentan Building: Originally the university student activity center, now the location of the Student Affairs Department, Career Guidance Center, etc. I don't know the symbolic meaning. "Tan" means deep water, does it mean this place is unfathomable? I don't understand. Some students once jokingly called it "Wentan, the mire of employment."

Wenpei Building: The location of the School of Humanities, I don't know whether it takes the meaning of "rich in literary thoughts" or something else. However, "Pei" means "water, market". Is it because it is close to the Nanyuan Market?

Wenyong Building: The location of the Safety Management College. Since it is the Safety Management College, the safety forever is self-evident.

Wenquan Building: In my opinion, this name has a very beautiful implication! And there is much to say. "Quan" means "money". Those stationed here are all from the original Central South University of Finance and Economics majors. How could they not have money? Furthermore, look at the two white marble bridges between Wenquan Building and Zhongyuan Building. The one leading to Zhongyuan Building is called "Qingyun", meaning "soaring straight up". The one leading to Wenquan Building is called "Baishui", which splits the character "Quan". There is a stone beside the bridge engraved with "one clear and two whites". Think about our Central South University, backed by Taishan Mountain (Wentai Building), facing financial resources (Wenquan Building), "Qingyun" soars straight up to "Zhongyuan" and sits steadily, naturally becoming a high official; leaving "Zhongyuan" and heading towards "Wenquan", how many people still remember the warning words on the stone - "one clear and two whites"? Moreover, the poems and famous sayings on the railings of the two bridges, perhaps only the stonemasons of those years still remember.

Shouyi Campus

Wenjin Building: The largest teaching building, unfortunately recently showing signs of wear and tear, showing the poor quality. "Jin" means "ferry", the place of teaching is indeed where teachers guide students through difficulties.

Wenji Building, Wenze Building: Located on both sides of Zhongnan Building, like the blue dragon and white tiger, combining strength and gentleness. Governing the world and benefiting all beings, this is indeed the responsibility of us Central South students.

Wenhui Building: Ha ha, originally the key place for the national certified public accountant examination grading organized by the Ministry of Finance. Thinking about the word "hui", it actually implies that exam papers from all over the country are gathered here. Moreover, "hui" also means "money".

Look down from the new map six buildings, what shape is it? —— An ancient silver ingot!

Looking at all the buildings, even though "Wenlan" started the tradition, the later ones following the "water" character also carry profound implications. "Water" means "wealth". Looking at all the majors in our school, is there one that doesn't involve wealth? Observing the prevailing风气of our school, is there one that doesn't lean towards wealth? Some leaders, receiving bribes and embezzling school assets, end up in jail; some teachers, working multiple jobs and making money elsewhere, neglect their teaching duties; some students, measuring themselves by wealth, aim for "practical" goals and do not strive for progress; high tuition fees and expensive vegetable prices, many students but few classrooms. Worse yet, the gymnasium is rented out to merchants, the school profits while students have to pay to enter and exercise. I ask, according to this trend, can the school playground be rented out to farmers for farming and collecting rent? Can the office building be rented out to businesses for operation? Can the dormitories be converted into hotels to welcome guests? (The building upstairs from the Beiyuan Supermarket was originally a classroom, now it's the Beiyuan Hotel.) Can the canteen be converted into a hotel to entertain guests? (Qiáo Building and New Sunshine Hotel occupy the former student cafeteria.) Classrooms are already scarce, and when office space is insufficient, classrooms can be occupied again (universities where office buildings and teaching buildings are equal in quantity are rare outside our school). In the end, will the entire school be sold, with teachers becoming bosses and students becoming employees?

If this continues, how can academia flourish? How can Central South revive?

Only someone who loves the school would say these words.
