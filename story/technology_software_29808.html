**Product Description:**

Hai Zhi Hospital Management System includes functional modules such as outpatient pricing and billing system, pharmaceutical warehouse management system, inpatient management system, president's decision support system, outpatient electronic medical records, maintenance management system, patient-doctor relationship management, membership management, logistics material management, medical order management, financial management, and more.

**Outpatient Pricing and Billing System - Functional Overview:**

This module mainly includes outpatient pricing, outpatient billing, billing audit, refund, prescription return, re-printing receipts, clearing arrears, prescription query, billing query, etc.

1. Categorizing prescriptions by Western medicine, Chinese patent medicine, herbal medicine, and health materials; calculating the prescription amount and generating a prescription number for billing purposes.

2. Outpatient billing, including prescription billing and other item billing, printing invoices, calculating responsible income, self-paid amounts, etc.

3. Cash input to record the cash handed over by billing staff, enabling verification of their actual income versus the financial department's receipt.

4. Auditing billing staff by comparing the cash they submit with their actual cash income to review their actual income and submission status.

5. Daily revenue report for billing staff, summarizing daily income, printing reports for submission to finance.

6. Prescription return based on prescription numbers; if the prescription has already been dispensed, update the corresponding pharmacy inventory.

7. Refund based on patient invoices; if the fee involves a prescription, also perform the prescription return operation.

8. Reprinting receipts based on receipt numbers.

9. Clearing overdue payments for patients.

10. Outpatient pharmacy dispensing (drug collection) based on uncollected prescription orders, enabling bulk or individual prescription dispensing.

11. Querying prescriptions by doctor or pharmacy, checking collected and uncollected prescriptions based on payment status, and statistically analyzing drug consumption within any time period.

12. Billing query, allowing queries of outpatient income by billing staff, department, doctor, and various billing items within a specific time frame, including detailed and summary statistics.

13. Medical account fee management, recording prepayments from patients with medical accounts, querying patient expenses, and summarizing unit-based expense statistics.

14. Refund query, allowing refund details to be queried by billing staff.

15. Combined pricing, billing, and drug dispensing functionality, designed for hospitals that require simultaneous processing of these tasks.

**Pharmaceutical Warehouse Management System - Functional Overview:**

1. Normal drug warehousing and distribution operations, categorized by different warehousing/distribution types.

2. Querying warehouse inbound details, either for a specific warehouse or all warehouses, and also for specific drugs.

3. Querying warehouse outbound details, either for a specific warehouse or all warehouses, and also for specific drugs.

4. Statistical analysis of drug warehousing/distribution within any time period, tracking transactions with specific suppliers or drug categories.

5. Querying drug inventory balances, either for specific suppliers or classification categories, such as all externally purchased drugs.

6. Single-drug inventory balance query, allowing checks of current stock levels or historical balances, with graphical representations of inventory trends.

7. Reprinting inbound and outbound documents.

8. Special operations such as returning drugs to suppliers or transferring drugs between warehouses.

9. Confirmation of distribution to sub-warehouses.

10. Financial management functions to track payment statuses of specific inbound orders.

11. Drug price adjustment functions, calculating profit/loss impacts after price changes.

**Inpatient Management System - Functional Overview:**

1. Patient admission registration, prepayment operations, charge entry, prescription returns, medication returns, changing departments (transfers), interim settlement, discharge procedures, etc.

2. Querying prescriptions and patient medication statistics by pharmacy, doctor, or patient.

3. Querying daily billing reports of billing staff, detailed billing information within a certain period, and cash daily reports.

4. Querying patient prepayment details, detailed expenses incurred during a specified period, and an overview of inpatient expenses.

5. Inpatient revenue queries (including departmental, doctor-specific, and overall charge statistics), as well as lists of inpatients per department.

6. Interface with social security systems, printing specialized reports, generating data in multiple formats, and uploading via file, email, or FTP.

7. Recall discharged patients to re-settle their accounts.

**President’s Decision Support System - Functional Overview:**

The President’s Decision Support System provides real-time dynamic queries on income and inventory of various hospital departments and pharmacies, offering intuitive charts and graphs. This ensures close communication between the president and departments, enabling timely issue identification and resolution to maintain smooth hospital operations. The system primarily includes four modules: Inventory & Cost, Outpatient Billing, Inpatient Billing.

**Outpatient Electronic Medical Records - Functional Overview:**

The Outpatient Electronic Medical Record system includes the creation of outpatient patient records and electronic prescriptions, solving issues related to lost prescriptions and laying a solid foundation for advanced hospital management. Traditional manual prescriptions have the following shortcomings:

1. Irregular writing often leads to errors in pricing and dispensing, causing disputes between the hospital and patients.

2. Many prescriptions go unpaid, resulting in significant financial losses for the hospital.

3. Poor confidentiality and many non-standard management practices.

Electronic medical records (electronic prescriptions) solve these problems, performing the following functions:

1. Establishing patient medical records.

2. Recording each visit's prescriptions, tests, treatments, and handling information, facilitating doctors' tracking of patient histories.

3. Implementing electronic prescription functionality, printing prescription slips for billing use.

**Maintenance Management System - Functional Overview:**

1. Basic Information Maintenance: Includes operator information, department information, doctor information, drug information, supplier information, warehouse/pharmacy/outlet information, outpatient billing items, inpatient billing items, procurement personnel information, and maintenance of self-payment ratios for social security personnel. These are basic pieces of information required for the hospital system to run correctly.

2. Medical Account Maintenance: Includes maintaining patient units and medical accounts. Some hospitals need to track patient treatment records and summarize costs incurred during a specific period, requiring an account to be established for each patient.

3. Data Maintenance: Includes data backup, data recovery, system optimization, dictionary upgrades, etc.

4. Initialization: Used to configure the hospital's business processes according to its own needs. When the hospital's business processes change, initialization can be used to reconfigure them. It also performs operations such as clearing data tables, setting print line numbers, and setting the hospital name.
