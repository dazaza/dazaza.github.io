At around 15:00, the captor, wearing a gray shirt and long pants, with his face down and hands and feet tied, was carried out of the steel shop where the incident occurred by four policemen. Before the onlookers could see the suspect's figure and appearance clearly, he had already been escorted onto a police car.

Report (written by reporters Wang Guangyong, Yang Bo, correspondents Wen Miaosen, Ning Shuangquan, photos by reporter Long Chengtong): At 1:20 PM yesterday, a hostage-taking incident occurred at a steel shop on the east side of the Lingnan Avenue Deep Village section. A man in his 20s held a pair of scissors and took a young woman hostage inside the steel shop. During the incident, the woman who was taken hostage was slashed multiple times on her face, neck, and wrists.

At around 14:20, the police dispatched more SWAT officers into the scene. A sniper was seen loading bullets outside the steel shop, ready to act at any moment. At around 14:50, the police opened the anti-theft door on the side of the steel shop and broke through from both the front and the side. Immediately after, the police rushed in and led a frail female hostage away from the scene, putting her onto an ambulance.

The Foshan police sent negotiation experts to negotiate with the captor, eventually breaking through from both the main door and side door of the steel shop, subduing the man and successfully rescuing the hostage. The hostage was then quickly sent to the First People's Hospital of the city. According to the police report, the hostage suffered minor cuts. According to preliminary investigations, both the suspect and the hostage were from other places and did not know each other beforehand. The suspect, Zheng, has been feeling depressed due to high work pressure recently. Currently, the case is still under further investigation.

Rescue: Negotiation experts stabilized the man's emotions first.

At around 2:00 PM yesterday, when the reporter arrived at the incident location on the east side of Lingnan Avenue, the police had already set up a cordon at the intersection outside a certain ancestral hall beside the Deep Village bus stop. A man was holding a pair of scissors and had taken a young woman hostage inside the steel shop next to the ancestral hall. About three to forty onlookers gathered at the cordon beside another intersection on the side of the steel shop.

Across from the incident steel shop, Zhang (a pseudonym) anxiously told the reporter: "The incident should have happened around one o'clock in the afternoon. Someone called the police, and I realized someone was being held hostage." Above the convenience store was a rental apartment where a female tenant on the third floor witnessed the entire hostage-taking process. "That man chased this woman on the road, and as the woman had nowhere to hide, she ran into the steel shop," Chen said. The woman tried to break free and escape, but the man grabbed her right arm, sat down in a dark corner of the steel shop, and cut her two wrists with scissors. The woman collapsed onto the ground, covered in blood, looking like a 'blood person.'

At the First People's Hospital, the reporter met the just-rescued woman Wang Xing and her family. After the doctor gave Wang Xing an emergency bandage treatment, they prepared to perform a suturing surgery. Wang Xing's left cheek and both hands were already bandaged. Although the wounds had been cleaned, there were still visible knife marks and bloodstains on her face, neck, and ears.

Wang Xing's eyes were filled with tears. After going through a terrifying experience, she looked exhausted and couldn't speak. Her father, who worked as a decorator, had just come from the construction site. "We are from Hengyang, Hunan. Wang Xing is only 22 years old this year," Wang Xing's brother said. Both he and his sister worked as security guards. At 3:00 PM that day, his sister was about to go to work when an accident happened at the rental house where they lived in Deep Village. "None of our family knows that kidnapper," Wang Xing's brother said.

Mr. Wu (a pseudonym) among the onlookers said that there were three men in the steel shop, including the boss, who tried to stop the man from committing violence, but the man slashed the boss's arm and clothes with scissors. "There was a big tear in the belly area of the clothes," Mr. Wu said: "The man also said to the steel shop boss, why do you look at me like that?!"

Last night, the Chancheng police issued a statement regarding the hostage incident, stating that at 1:20 PM yesterday, the Chancheng police received an alarm that a woman was held hostage by a stranger in a shop in Deep Village, Lan Shi. After receiving the report, the municipal and district public security organs mobilized criminal investigation, SWAT, police station, and other police forces to rush to the incident location, sealing off the scene while arranging negotiators to talk with the suspect to stabilize his emotions and prevent him from harming the hostages. During the negotiations between the negotiators and the armed man, the on-site commander formulated a response plan and made all preparations for the surprise attack on the suspect to rescue the hostages. In the afternoon at 3:00 PM, after multiple unsuccessful negotiations, the police acted and captured the suspect.

Subsequently, the reporter learned from the Foshan police that the abductor was in his 20s and had been working in Foshan for a short time. Last night, the Foshan police announced that the hostage had suffered minor cuts and had been sent to the hospital for treatment. According to preliminary investigations, both the suspect and the hostage were from other places and did not know each other before. The suspect, Zheng, has been feeling depressed due to high work pressure recently. Currently, the case is still under further investigation.

One of the first 120 emergency vehicles to arrive at the scene was on standby. A nurse surnamed Wang (a pseudonym) said that he saw the police send negotiation experts to negotiate with the abductor. However, he heard loud shouts from inside the shop: "I'm not doing it for money!"
