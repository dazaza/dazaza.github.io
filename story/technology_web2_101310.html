The economic crisis has prompted an explosive growth in the number of people who keep accounts.

by fenqu on 2008-11-05 13:47:30

The economic crisis has prompted explosive growth in the number of people who keep detailed financial records, or "Zhangke."

An increasing number of individuals are joining the ranks of "Zhangke" and beginning to track their expenses online to understand where their money is going and gain control over their spending.

From China's largest online accounting platform, Caakee Online (www.caakee.com), it can be seen that there was an explosive increase in the number of accountants in the second half of 2008. As China's earliest online bookkeeping website, Caakee Online was established on February 10, 2006. In 2006, the number of registered members was less than a hundred, and in 2007, the number of registered members was only around tens of thousands. Entering 2008, the number of registered members grew explosively, reaching over 25 million registered users. In just over half a year, the membership increased tenfold compared to the previous few years. Moreover, the number of new members continues to grow explosively by the thousands daily and sometimes even by the tens of thousands. Recently, many large venture capitalists have proactively approached us at Caakee Online to discuss investments, demonstrating the development potential of the online bookkeeping market.

Due to the economic crisis, rising prices, stagnant wages, and various other reasons, more and more people are starting to keep accounts. At the same time, more and more people are becoming increasingly aware of financial management, and keeping accounts is the foundation of financial management.

Although traditional methods such as notebook accounting, Excel-based accounting, and standalone software-based accounting exist, these methods all have certain defects. For example, using a notebook for accounting makes statistical analysis inconvenient; using Excel for accounting not only makes statistical analysis inconvenient but also allows for only simple record-keeping, and if the computer gets infected with a virus, reinstalling the system would result in the loss of all accounting data. Although standalone accounting software is powerful and convenient for statistics, it can only be used on the installed computer, making it impossible to use on another computer. Additionally, if the computer gets infected with a virus and the system is reinstalled, the data will also be lost. Caakee Online specifically addresses these inherent flaws of existing accounting methods. Because online accounting is not only convenient and feature-rich but also allows for anytime, anywhere accounting as long as there is internet access, and the data is both safe and stable. Naturally, online accounting has become favored by everyone.

Since I took charge of operating Caakee Online, many friends and members often ask me questions about online accounting. Below, I will address some common questions:

1. Is online accounting secure?

Answer: Caakee Online regards the security of member data as its first priority. As a responsible and mission-driven legitimate company, we absolutely do not sell any member data or information. Simultaneously, regarding security measures, we have implemented several steps: First, encryption technology ensures the security of member passwords and data. Second, dual hard drive backups and twice-daily backups ensure data integrity. Third, we offer a data export function, allowing members to export their recorded accounts and save them on their computers or USB drives after completing their accounts. Fourth, when registering on Caakee Online, the registration information provided by members is virtual, so even if the accounts are leaked, they cannot be matched to real individuals. Moreover, since Caakee Online was founded over two years ago, there hasn't been a single case of member data loss or leakage.

2. What are the benefits of keeping accounts?

Answer: The purpose of keeping accounts is to clearly understand where your money is being spent. By doing so, you can plan your income and life more reasonably, cultivating good habits in account-keeping and financial management. Currently, 90% of people live with unclear finances, unaware of how their hard-earned money is being spent. Who wants to live with unclear finances? Who would want their hard-earned money to be wasted? Developing the habit of account-keeping and financial management can benefit you for life, so why not persist in daily account-keeping?

3. Can keeping accounts help you save money?

Answer: Keeping accounts does not directly save money for you, but it can tell you where you need to cut back.

4. Do you not need to keep accounts if you don't have money?

Answer: Keeping accounts is not about having more or less money; rather, it helps you scientifically and reasonably manage your accounts and plan your life, ensuring clarity. If you don't have much money, you should spend every penny wisely and avoid wasting your hard-earned cash. If you have money...

5. How big is the market for account-keeping?

Answer: We at Caakee Online conducted a market survey and found that among ten people, at least one person keeps accounts. With China's population of 1.3 billion, at least 100 million people have the habit of or are capable of keeping accounts. This market is vast, and our actions at Caakee Online have proven its potential.

6. What are the characteristics of people who keep accounts?

Answer: In over two years of market operations, Caakee Online has discovered that most account-keepers are urban white-collar workers aged 25 to 35, with a male-to-female ratio of 1.5:1. Moreover, the current registered members tend to be younger, with more and more college students joining the "Zhangke" group, as well as a large number of middle school and high school students. Not long ago, schools in Shanghai began offering financial management courses to middle school students.

7. How can you persist in daily account-keeping?

Answer: I used to have no habit of keeping accounts, but now I persist in daily account-keeping. Every time I come home from outside, the first thing I do is turn on my computer and record my accounts. To persist in daily account-keeping, you can set a specific time for yourself, such as before sleeping at night, during lunchtime, or right after starting work in the morning.

8. What changes have occurred in me since I started keeping accounts?

Answer: Since I started keeping accounts, I first understood how my money was being spent. Therefore, each time afterward, I would remind myself to save in certain areas. Also, since I started keeping accounts, when I go shopping at the supermarket, I compare products to find those with the highest cost-performance ratio. For instance, previously, whenever I visited clients, I would buy drinks. Since I started keeping accounts, I always bring a bottle of water with me, which is both clean and economical. Previously, I would often dine out with friends, but since I started keeping accounts, I try to invite friends to my home, where I buy groceries and cook meals, which is both hygienic and economical. Previously, whenever I went shopping, I would buy anything I liked, but since I started keeping accounts, I think about whether something is worth buying before purchasing it, and for items that are optional, I try not to buy them. Therefore, since I started keeping accounts, many of my consumption habits and concepts have gradually changed. I feel that I have become more economically minded.
