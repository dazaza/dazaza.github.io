Microsoft announces the first batch of 19 apps for Xbox One

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 20:17:22

　　The Verge

　　After Sony just announced the first batch of entertainment apps that support PS4, Microsoft, as Sony's competitor, recently also announced the list of entertainment apps that will be available on the Xbox One launch day. Moreover, these apps will be available for download simultaneously in the 13 initial markets where the Xbox One will be launched.

　　In the U.S., there will be 19 applications available, and Microsoft stated that this is only the first batch, with more apps to come for the Xbox One platform in the future. However, it is worth noting that YouTube, a popular video application currently supporting both Xbox 360 and PS4, is missing from the initial list. Although Microsoft mentioned that users can access the web version of YouTube through the built-in IE browser on Xbox One, considering Microsoft's efforts to encourage YouTube to launch a Windows Phone app and the fact that YouTube was always among the first applications to support each generation of Xbox consoles, its absence is somewhat disappointing. If players want a better app experience on the Xbox One, they should consider registering for an Xbox Live Gold subscription.

　　The first batch of applications for the U.S. region includes: Amazon Instant Video, Crackle, CWTV, ESPN, Fox Now, FX Now, HBO Go (coming soon), Hulu Plus, Machinima, MUZU TV, Netflix, Redbox Instant by Verizon, Target Ticket, TED, The NFL on Xbox One, Twitch, Univision Deportes, Verizon FiOS TV, Vudu.

　　According to the information released by Microsoft so far, the price of the Xbox One will be $499 (approximately RMB 3039), which is one week later than Sony's PS4 priced at $399 (approximately RMB 2430). Considering Microsoft's aim to provide users with a complete gaming and entertainment experience through the Xbox One, supporting most mainstream entertainment applications makes sense. However, some applications may vary depending on the market where the console is launched.
