Starry Legend: A Nonagenarian Crawled Out of Her Coffin 4 Days After Being Laid to Rest, Doctor Claims She Was Clinically Dead (Picture)

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-02-29 08:19:04

On the morning of the 26th, grandmother Li Xiufen, who had "returned from the dead," sat silently by her front door.

"Grandma Li Xiufen was dead for six days, lying in the coffin and then came back to life!" In recent days, this astonishing news spread like wildfire in Liu Tower Village, Liu Ma Town, Beiliu City, becoming a hot topic among villagers during their leisure time. On February 17th, Grandma Li Xiufen, a senior villager under the five-guarantees welfare program in Liu Tower Village, was thought to have "passed away." The villagers placed her "corpse" in a coffin awaiting an auspicious day for burial. Unexpectedly, six days later, they found her cooking porridge in the kitchen.

The Elder's Sudden "Death"

"The Corpse" Placed in the Coffin

On the morning of February 26th, I found Grandma Li Xiufen, who had "come back from the dead," at Shi Kou Group in Liu Tower Village. She sat quietly by the door, looking no different from any other normal elder.

According to the villagers, the 95-year-old Grandma Li Xiufen was a senior under the five-guarantees welfare program. Her neighbor, the 60-something Chen Qingwang's family often took care of her daily life, occasionally sending her some vegetables or buying her meat. Grandma Li Xiufen usually liked to get up early and take walks. On the morning of February 1st, she fell on a downhill path and sat on the ground shouting about her headache. After seeing her condition, the villagers helped her up, brought her home, and called a doctor. The doctor diagnosed her with a mild concussion and prescribed some medicine, advising her to rest well. Afterwards, Chen Qingwang began taking care of her three meals a day.

At 7:30 AM on February 17th, Chen Qingwang went to Grandma Li's place as usual to bring her breakfast, but she hadn't gotten up yet, which made him curious. He went to wake her up, but no matter how hard he pushed her or loudly called her name, she didn't respond. Feeling something was wrong, he reached out to check her breathing and found that it had stopped. Then he checked her body temperature, but she didn't feel as cold as a corpse should be.

Based on his common sense, Chen Qingwang deduced that Grandma Li Xiufen might have passed away. The reason for her still having body warmth could be due to the thick blankets covering her. Subsequently, the kind-hearted Chen Qingwang and his son, Chen Lisheng, prepared for her funeral. Following local rural customs, they chose a date, setting the burial for the morning of February 24th. On February 19th, they placed her "corpse" into the coffin, covered it with a lid without nailing it shut, and placed a rice-filled incense bowl in front of the coffin, burning a large bundle of incense every morning.

Climbed Out of the Coffin

Ran to the Kitchen to Cook Porridge

On the morning of February 23rd, when Chen Qingwang and his son entered the room where the "deceased" was placed, they suddenly discovered that the coffin lid had been opened, and the "corpse" inside had vanished.

"Such an incident of a missing corpse has never happened before, it scared us father and son badly, not knowing what to do," said Chen Qingwang. He called over several nearby villagers for consultation. Everyone believed that the "corpse" had been stolen. But who would steal the "corpse" of a five-guarantees welfare program elder? And what use would there be for stealing it? This left everyone utterly baffled. However, since it had disappeared, they decided to try and find it first.

Just as they were about to set out to search for the "corpse," someone shouted: "She's in the kitchen!" Everyone quickly rushed into the kitchen and saw Grandma Li Xiufen sitting on the floor, with the stove fire burning and a pot on the stove. This sight shocked everyone greatly. After observing her for a while, they decided to test if she was a "normal person." Someone noticed she was wearing a watch and asked her, "What time is it?" After checking her watch, she told everyone, "It's 11:15." They compared it with their phones, and indeed, it was correct.

Relieved, they asked her how she got out of the coffin. She replied, "Why did you put me in the coffin? I woke up feeling hungry, so I thought of getting up to eat something. I pushed (the coffin lid) for a long time before managing to crawl out." According to her, she woke up around midnight on the 23rd, climbed out of the coffin after dawn, found the room empty, and used the rice from the incense bowl in front of the coffin to cook porridge.

The Elder Was Originally "Falsely Dead"

"Revival" Was Truly Lucky

I learned that according to local rural customs, all personal items such as bedding and clothing used by the deceased must be burned after death. Therefore, during the process of helping arrange Grandma Li Xiufen's "funeral," Chen Qingwang and his son burned all of her belongings. Currently, her only possession is the set of "mourning clothes" she wore when placed in the coffin.

Since Grandma Li Xiufen is a beneficiary of the five-guarantees welfare program, on the morning of February 23rd, accompanied by the head of Liu Tower Village, Chen Qingwang went to the Liu Ma Town government office to explain the entire incident and seek assistance. After the Liu Ma Town government sent personnel to verify the facts, they immediately allocated two quilts, 25 kilograms of rice, and several sets of clothing, delivering them directly to Grandma Li Xiufen. Additionally, neighboring villagers also gave her quite a few daily necessities, and Chen Qingwang continued to take care of her daily life.

A doctor from a hospital in Yulin analyzed that Grandma Li Xiufen might have been in a coma or light shock state caused by brain damage, which in medical terms is called "false death." Although her breathing couldn't be felt, she still had body warmth, indicating that signs of life still existed. Luckily, the villagers followed the custom of choosing an auspicious day for burial, giving her the opportunity to "revive."
