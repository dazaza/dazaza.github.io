[ Qingdao Decoration ] Decoration Design Knowledge: Top 10 Decoration Details Most Likely to Be Overlooked

by qssqwe215 on 2012-02-28 14:58:56

Problem One: The indoor door size has no reserved seam.

Old-style houses generally have two doors installed, one for anti-theft and the other for entering the household. During renovation, it is easy to cause insufficient space between the floor and the door, making it impossible to place a thick and beautiful doormat.

Solution: Before renovation, when measuring the door's size, fully consider the thickness of the floor, tiles, doormat, etc., and reserve the seam in advance. If similar problems occur afterward, you can replace with a thinner doormat; or dig out a piece of the floor according to the doormat's size, seal the edges, and then place the doormat.

Problem Two: Insufficient reserved outlets.

Nowadays, the variety of home appliances is increasing. Initially, during the renovation, a few more outlets were installed according to needs, but now they are not enough. The concealed wiring done during the renovation cannot be altered either. Moreover, when designing the TV background wall, power outlets for wall-mounted TVs and speakers were not reserved, forcing the family to use exposed wires, which not only affects aesthetics but also makes cleaning inconvenient.

Solution: During renovation, consider the number of electrical appliances that will be added in the future and install as many outlets as possible to prevent them from falling behind the development of appliances. If your home uses an LCD TV, it’s best to reserve a position for the wall-mounted power outlet; if not installed, you can wrap some fake flowers around the exposed wires for decoration.

Problem Three: No proper space left for the refrigerator.

When renovating the kitchen, the size of the refrigerator was not considered, and a random spot was left. Later, when purchasing the refrigerator, either the size did not fit or the appearance was unsatisfactory.

Solution: When the cabinet designer comes to measure the dimensions, provide him with the exact sizes of all kitchen appliances and fix their positions based on your living habits before designing the cabinet dimensions. If the kitchen is not far from the dining room, the refrigerator can be placed in the dining room; if it's far away, only a suitable-sized refrigerator can be purchased.

Problem Four: Ceiling light lacks dual control.

The switch for the bedroom ceiling light is only set at the entrance, without a double-linked double-control switch near the bedhead. At night, after watching TV or reading in bed, you still have to get up to turn off the light and return to bed in the dark, which is especially troublesome in winter when you have to get up shivering to turn off the light.

Solution: If you have this special need, you can communicate with the workers during the electrician stage of renovation to lay a dual-linked dual-control circuit. For those who didn't set up a dual control circuit, purchasing remotely controlled lighting fixtures from a lighting market can also achieve the desired effect.

Problem Five: Ceiling beams cannot support chandeliers.

When installing the ceiling beams, the installation of heavier lighting fixtures like crystal chandeliers was not considered, leading to a very simple design. Later, when installing the lights, it was discovered that the ceiling could not bear the weight of the lights.

Solution: Either replace with a lighting fixture that the ceiling beam can support, or install a resin lamp similar to a crystal chandelier. If a crystal chandelier must be installed, cut a small part of the ceiling, install expansion bolts on the original ceiling, then add hooks, and finally hang the lighting fixtures.

Problem Six: Insufficient storage space.

It is difficult to arrange sufficient storage space in a small kitchen. However, the kitchen has the most miscellaneous items, and the pots, pans, ladles, and bowls have nowhere to hide. Placing them haphazardly on the stove is neither aesthetically pleasing nor convenient for use.

Solution: The kitchen must prioritize convenience and practicality, and small kitchens should plan the space well. If both the floor cabinets and hanging cabinets are insufficient, you can buy some appropriate metal kitchen shelves or brackets and install them on the wall to store microwaves, rice cookers, etc., on the wall. Shelves with hooks can also hang various soup spoons, knives, and other miscellaneous items.

Problem Seven: Wardrobe space focuses only on the present.

For a two-bedroom apartment, most people only make large wardrobes in the two bedrooms and do not try to squeeze out separate spaces for a storeroom. In the future, as the number of clothes increases, these two cabinets will definitely not be enough, especially in summer when you have to forcefully cram the cabinets.

Solution: Small apartments must fully consider the storage function at the design stage. Nowadays, it is very popular to design a whole wardrobe or storage room in limited space to meet future needs.

Problem Eight: Drainage of the floor drain is insufficient.

This problem usually has two situations: one is that the floor drain outlet is not the lowest point, and the second is that the number of floor drains is insufficient. This way, not only does bathing easily cause the trouble of [water flooding], but the washing machine may not even be usable.

Solution: Regarding the lowest point issue, remember to test the drainage during the acceptance of bathroom renovation; otherwise, it will not only affect the bathing experience but serious cases may lead to leaks, affecting the neighbors downstairs. For the frustration caused by insufficient floor drains that prevent the use of a washing machine, you can install a floor drain that serves both as a laundry water supply and drainage. Typically, floor drains are installed under washbasins, in shower areas, next to washing machines, and beside toilets.

Problem Nine: Exposed water heaters.

Water heaters are bulky, and if left exposed during renovation, they not only occupy space but also create a messy appearance with complex pipelines, making them difficult to clean.

Solution: A cabinet with louvered doors can be custom-made to enclose the water heater, making it both aesthetically pleasing and dustproof while allowing heat dissipation. The louvered door cabinet can be made to match the style of the kitchen cabinets for better overall results.

Problem Ten: Unreasonable height of the waterproof layer in the shower area.

Some families, during renovation, in order to prevent water leakage, hope to extend the waterproof layer in the shower area all the way to the top. Other families neglect the waterproofing issue and make it too low, leaving a seepage hazard.

Solution: Waterproofing in the bathroom cannot be ignored and must be done properly, but generally, 30 cm high is sufficient. Making it too high can cause problems such as wall hollowing and poor adhesion of tiles. Moreover, tiles themselves have a certain waterproof effect, so there's no need to overdo it.
