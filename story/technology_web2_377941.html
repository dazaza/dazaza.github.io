1Q84.fm: A Social Music Radio (with Invitation)

by ybcz0519 on 2011-01-13 14:25:27

1Q84.fm is a service that provides social music radio, where everyone can create their own music station. Additionally, you can also listen to music stations created by your friends, collect music shared by them, and post comments, etc. It also offers a series of fun features such as a radio ranking list and mini-radios that can be placed on personal blogs. Introduction to 1Q84.fm functions: After user registration, you have your own radio station which you can manage; Users can add music in two ways: one is by searching for music already uploaded by other users, the other is by uploading your own music; Users can listen to their friends' radios and collect their favorite radios; You can listen to all public radios and discover your favorite radios and music; You can check out the popular radios and music of the week and see how popular your own radio is; A WordPress plugin is provided so you can place your radio on your blog; Why create 1Q84.fm? We found the original intention behind the creation of 1Q84.fm from the 1Q84.fm team! When we were kids, we enjoyed picking out favorite songs from many different tapes, then dubbing them onto a blank tape, and proudly naming it "my collection." When giving it to classmates, we would talk endlessly about how good this song was, who sang it, and which album it was dubbed from, and so on. As we grew up with the internet and MP3s, free downloads, trial listening, and recommendations are everywhere, making it much easier to listen to and share music than before, readily available at our fingertips. However, it's hard to find the joy of sitting at home carefully selecting and dubbing music, and introducing it to classmates and friends. Why is this? Carefully considering, the reasons can roughly be summarized into two points. First, although there are various recommendations online, these come from strangers or even computers and have little relevance to oneself. Music sometimes isn't just the music itself; behind a song is often a story or a person nearby. When a friend recommends a familiar melody, a story beginning with "once" will appear before our eyes, a kind of pleasure that cannot be obtained from strangers or computers. Second, there are too many choices now and not enough time, people become lazier. If you tell a friend on MSN that a certain song is nice, the response is usually "uh-huh," and rarely does anyone search, download, listen, and tell you how they feel based on the information you provide. Sending MP3 files is too slow, ideally, you should find and send the listening URL to the other party. As music enthusiasts who like to share and recommend, we've encountered these problems many times. Why can't there be a way to let everyone relive the simple joy of childhood? Thus, after a few months, 1Q84.fm was born. Our goal is: to let people find joy and a sense of accomplishment in the process of sharing music! Watsonxu said: From the official introduction of 1Q84, we can feel that this is a product made by a team that loves music and enjoys sharing music, and its goal "to let people find joy and a sense of accomplishment in the process of sharing music" also lets me feel their passion for creating this website and their love for music. At the mention of radio, people might easily think of Douban Radio, which recommends music to users based on their listening preferences. I agree with 1Q84's point of view that recommendations based on user relationships are necessary. Music recommendations need to be socialized, and socialized recommendations will definitely give new life to music. However, for music websites, copyright issues are always something to consider, and I hope 1Q84 can further strengthen the social recommendation function while handling music copyrights well.
