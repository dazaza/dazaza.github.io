Dell Chairman: We'll Focus on Enterprises, Not Mobile Phones

by anonymous on 2013-09-15 08:02:20

Dell CEO Michael Dell

After the proposed privatization plan was approved by shareholders, Michael Dell, Chairman and CEO of Dell Inc., gave an interview to the US financial media CNBC on Friday, talking about the company's future plans. Dell said: "We will focus on our consumers and increase end-to-end solutions."

Dell also listed five strategic areas for the company's future progress, including "enhancing enterprise solution capabilities, increasing sales volume, doubling efforts in emerging markets, achieving cloud client computing on tablets and PCs, and improving user experience through investment." "We will adjust our quarterly focus cycle to a longer period, such as five years or even ten years. We will double our investment to better serve corporate users."

Dell estimates that the global market size of the business sectors involved is $3 trillion, and Dell accounts for about 2% of the share. Dell said: "No one can still hold a 4% or 5% market share. There are too many companies now, and the market share is scattered." Dell also predicted that the size of the global computing industry would reach $6 trillion in the medium term.

Dell pointed out that the most obvious trend in the market now is to develop private cloud systems for customers. In addition, emerging technology companies need servers, infrastructure, and storage equipment, which are exactly what Dell is developing.

Dell indicated that his company would not enter the mobile phone market but would maintain its position as a reseller of other mobile phone products. In addition, Dell said that the company would join the development of tablets and various client devices. "Our main business is to help our customers achieve information security, protect their data, and meet the need to extract information through any device."

When asked about the declining PC market, Dell said that the company's core products, scale, and leadership position would help it succeed in such a tough environment. Dell said: "We are the only manufacturer that is still growing at scale, at least for now. We are no longer the Dell of five years ago."

Two major groups led by Dell and activist investor Carl Icahn had fiercely competed for control of Dell. Dell said that their struggle inevitably distracted the company's attention. However, during this period, some businesses of Dell, including enterprise solution services, have grown.

On Thursday, Dell shareholders voted on the privatization proposal at the company headquarters in Round Rock, Texas. Based on the preliminary voting results, the approximately $24.9 billion privatization offer initiated by Dell and private equity firm Silver Lake was approved. The transaction is expected to be completed before the end of Dell's third fiscal quarter of the 2014 fiscal year.
