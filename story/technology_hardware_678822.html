Air conditioner market remains stable this year, price war may not break out

by imtoms373 on 2012-02-27 16:45:52

Last week, Suning released the 2012 white paper on the air conditioning industry, and Gree announced a two-year free replacement warranty for frequency conversion air conditioners; earlier this month, Zhigao launched new frequency conversion cloud air conditioner products... The preheating before the peak season of the air conditioning industry has already begun.

Due to high inventory levels and weak demand, some experts predicted: the opening of this year's air conditioning peak season may see fierce price competition. A person from a mainstream channel also privately said that the air conditioning industry is overcapacity, and it is difficult to avoid a price war this year.

Will the air conditioning industry engage in a price war this year?

In the 2011 refrigeration year (from September 2010 to August 2011), domestic air conditioning sales increased by nearly 40% year-on-year, reaching 53 million units, setting a historical record. However, with the end of the energy-saving惠民policy, the sales situation of air conditioners took a sharp turn downward in the second half of last year. The "trade-in-for-new" policy also expired at the end of December last year, plus an unfavorable macroeconomic environment and sluggish consumer demand, a negative year-on-year growth in domestic air conditioning sales in the first quarter of 2012 has become inevitable.

Zhang Yanbin, Dean of the AVC Academy and Assistant General Manager of Avic Consulting, believes that the air conditioning industry follows a pattern of rapid growth for two years, followed by a correction or slowdown for one year. After two years of rapid growth in 2010 and 2011, there is a relatively higher probability of adjustment in 2012. Moreover, real estate remains a key focus of regulation.

A relevant person in charge of an air conditioner compressor factory revealed that after several months of digestion, the current domestic air conditioner inventory has decreased from more than 20 million units at the end of last year to over 10 million units. But it is expected to be completely digested only by March or April this year.

Of course, there are also positive factors. The demand potential for air conditioners in China's third- and fourth-tier markets remains huge. New rural construction and increased farmers' income will gradually make air conditioners a standard configuration for rural households, and not just one unit per family. The subsidy policies for rural electrification will continue to be implemented this year; the delivery of affordable housing will also bring new demand —— this year, 5 million units of affordable housing will be basically completed nationwide, accounting for one-third of the total number of residential completions in 2012. In addition, the country is also planning new energy-saving and environmental protection subsidy policies.

From the perspective of supply and demand relationship, the air conditioning industry faces certain risks of oversupply, implying pressure for price reductions. However, other aspects contain factors for price increases.

Firstly, cost increases. On raw materials, plastics have risen in price due to oil price hikes, compressors have become more expensive due to rare earth price increases compared to last year, and labor costs are also rising. "There hasn't been much change in the cost of air conditioners this year, at most differing by dozens of yuan. Adjusting the price of thousands-of-yuan air conditioners by dozens of yuan doesn't offer much attraction."

Secondly, the strategy of many listed companies this year has shifted from pursuing scale to ensuring profitability. In the third quarter of last year, the net profit growth of Gree Electric and Midea Electric both slowed down. These two companies, along with Haier, account for about 70% of the domestic air conditioning market share. This year, they all hope to ensure continuous performance growth and do not have the desire to initiate or participate in a price war. Wang Jinliang, Vice President of the China Marketing Headquarters of Midea Cooling Appliances Group, believed that the focus of competition this year will be full DC frequency conversion air conditioners, not prices.

Thirdly, consumption trends are making frequency conversion air conditioners increasingly become the mainstream of home air conditioners. Due to the higher cost of frequency conversion air conditioners compared to fixed-frequency ones, the smoke of price wars seems unlikely to rise again.

Therefore, demand upgrades make consumers more rational. At the end of last year, some enterprises launched split-type air conditioners for 1999 yuan and cabinet air conditioners for 2999 yuan, but few responded, large gaming. A dealer said that now one-horsepower frequency conversion air conditioners sell better than one-horsepower single-cooling special-price machines.

Thus, facing many uncertainties, some insiders believe that air conditioning prices will remain stable this year. Even if there is a price war, it will only be local and regional promotions. The kind of "naked" price wars seen ten years ago should not happen again. If small brands ignite the war and mainstream brands do not participate, the market will not experience significant fluctuations.
