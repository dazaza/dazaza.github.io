Analysis: Apple's Mac OS X and iOS to Merge Next Year

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 10:01:45

Sina Technology News, evening of August 3 in Beijing Time - Analyst Peter Misek from the U.S. securities research agency Jefferies predicted on Wednesday that starting from next year, Apple will integrate its computer operating system Mac OS X and mobile operating system iOS into a single platform.

Misek wrote in a research note to clients that merging the two systems could create "synergistic" effects, such as improving gross margins and simplifying content licensing. Misek said: "Users want seamless content connectivity between their iPhone, iPad, and Mac. This is hard to achieve if Mac OS X and iOS remain independent."

Misek continued: "Therefore, we believe that starting from 2012 or 2013, Apple will consider integrating Mac OS X and iOS onto a single platform." Misek believes that Apple's A6 processor will be the first processor to support the new integrated platform.

Misek predicts that the A6 processor will first be used in the iPad 3, which is expected to launch in the first quarter of 2012. Then, the iPhone 5 will use the A6 processor in the summer of next year (Misek believes that the upcoming next-generation iPhone this year is not the iPhone 5 but the iPhone 4S). The MacBook Air will adopt the A6 in the second half of 2012 or 2013.

Misek said that the MacBook Pro and Mac desktops will continue to use the current system and Intel processors in the near term, mainly to better support 64-bit computing, but they will transition to the new integrated platform by 2016.

Misek believes that the new integrated platform will drive growth in Apple's hardware sales and profit margins. Misek maintains a "buy" rating for Apple stock and sets a target price of $500. (By Ming Li)
