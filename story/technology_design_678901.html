How should arthritis be treated? We must find the root cause!

Arthritis refers to the general term for diseases characterized by inflammation of connective tissues and degeneration of joint cartilage. Arthritis includes common osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis. Osteoarthritis is also called degenerative arthritis, with symptoms of joint pain, stiffness, and swelling. Rheumatoid arthritis, on the other hand, is caused by immune system disorders, leading to wear and deformation of joint cartilage. In other words, the pain is in the joints, but the problem lies in the cartilage!

Currently, arthritis treatment mostly involves conservative therapies such as pain relief and traction, as well as physical massage. The widespread practice of calcium supplementation for joint soreness is a significant misconception! Because calcium mainly acts on hard bones (bone mass) and has no direct relationship with joint cartilage. Why is that? Because the main component of joint cartilage is protein, along with glucosamine, hyaluronic acid, chondroitin sulfate, etc. Therefore, calcium supplementation for treating arthritis is a misconception. Can taking a single nutrient required by cartilage be effective?

Indeed, substances like collagen, glucosamine, hyaluronic acid, and chondroitin sulfate can provide certain nutrients to the joints. Unfortunately, these components cannot address the fundamental cause of joint pain: cartilage wear and degeneration. In other words, these substances cannot cure arthritis but can only temporarily suppress the pain, making it difficult to achieve cartilage regeneration!
