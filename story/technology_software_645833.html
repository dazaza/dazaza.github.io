First, let me introduce what collagen is! There are 27 types of collagen in the human body, and the type of collagen varies depending on the part of the body. The collagen in the skin is mostly Type I and Type III, formed by three left-handed peptide chains twisted into a right-handed helix. Collagen is the main component of the dermis layer of human skin, accounting for more than 80%. In the skin, collagen forms a dense elastic net that can lock in moisture and support the skin like a scaffold.

In the skin, collagen acts as a "spring," determining the elasticity and firmness of the skin; it is also a "reservoir," determining the water content and water storage capacity of the skin. Collagen directly determines the hydration, smoothness, tightness, and "skin age" of the skin. Collagen is an extracellular protein, consisting of three peptide chains twisted into a spiral-shaped fibrous protein. Collagen is the most abundant protein in the human body, accounting for over 30% of total proteins. It is rich in amino acids such as glycine, proline, and hydroxyproline required by the human body. Collagen is the most important component of the extracellular matrix. An adult's body contains about 3 kilograms of collagen, mainly found in the skin, bones, eyes, teeth, tendons, and internal organs (including the heart, stomach, intestines, and blood vessels). Its function is to maintain the shape and structure of the skin and tissues and is also an important raw material for repairing damaged tissues. In the components of human skin, 70% are composed of collagen.
