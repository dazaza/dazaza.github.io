Regional B2C, ground promotion is king

by wosohu on 2009-05-11 13:57:33

B2C is now a dime a dozen - sites like Joyo, Dangdang, Taobao, Newegg, JD.com, Redbaby... and so on. When the economic crisis hit, many traditional companies turned to e-commerce, becoming latecomers in the B2C market. They launched nationwide electronic malls hoping for a miracle cure, but reality has been brutal - fierce competition turning into a bloodbath.

But is there still room for success in B2C? If so, how?

B2C is a major application direction of the internet. Future B2C will focus more on regional specialization and product expertise. Expertise means focusing on specific products, narrowing down rather than trying to cover everything. Focusing on fewer, better products can lead to annual sales of hundreds of millions of yuan - no longer a pipe dream. Specialized B2C players may soon surpass general ones.

Regional focus means concentrating efforts in specific high-profit areas instead of spreading thinly across the whole country.

New directions harbor new e-commerce giants. Marketing methods will be innovative, even learning from traditional marketing techniques like ground promotions and sales. Why can some products sell hundreds of millions offline but only tens of millions online? The main reason is offline markets are well cultivated - visible and accessible. Changing the game rules will birth new B2C giants. These new giants will excel at cultivating regional channels and cyberspace, possibly adopting gaming industry's ground promotion strategies and mindset. Or they might use a light B2C model combined with health supplement sales: online sales with offline promotion, or even outsourcing all logistics and warehousing while focusing on marketing and customer service.

Let me share a personal case study to illustrate how to cultivate regional B2C markets.

I ran a site selling child car safety seats and strollers. I chose this niche because: high price points (500-6000 RMB), small but defined market (car owners with children), not attractive to large players, difficult for individuals to enter, edge/crossover market but essential for parents - creating product differentiation and market barriers.

The site operated for 1 year and 5 months before closing when my partners went abroad for studies.

Here are some operating data:

First month sold 50 units

Second month 140 units

Third month 300 units

Peaks were before National Day and Spring Festival

Before National Day, daily sales reached 150 units

Before Spring Festival, daily sales reached 240 units

At our peak, we almost exclusively focused on local customers as express shipping was too costly for bulky items weighing around 10kg. Shipping one unit to Beijing cost us 150 RMB, making national distribution unviable. We repositioned to focus on local/regional markets.

Our initial investment:

Website program - free download with minor modifications, almost zero cost

Hosting + domain - about 680 RMB/year

Staff - started with 3 customer service + me, total 6 people, later hired 3 business development staff

Office/warehouse - 280 sqm, total hardware investment around 100,000 RMB

Though we sold 50 units in the first month, we barely broke even. Shipping costs killed us when we tried national distribution. After realizing this, we refocused on local markets.

Operating an e-commerce business is tough, especially without your own delivery system. Customer special requests can't always be met by express services. But getting orders is always exciting - our first order came at 6:10 AM!

Great customer service is key in e-commerce. One memorable experience involved a Beijing customer ordering at 8:30 AM and requesting delivery to Pudong by 10:00 AM. With Shanghai's notorious traffic, especially over the Xupu Bridge, it seemed impossible. I tied the seat onto a luggage cart and took the subway to deliver it in time. Packaging for a child safety seat is about 1 cubic meter - too big for taxis. This kind of hustle is part of entrepreneurship. Formality comes after you survive the early stages - your primary goal is profitability.

Now let's discuss our marketing strategy:

Positioned the website as a product showcase, purchase platform, and community forum.

Represented 18 brands, categorized by brand, import/domestic, price, age, function, configuration.

Created an information section covering child seat knowledge, selection, usage, maintenance, linking to relevant products.

Set up a gift/promotion area with complementary items and gifts for children.

Our main focus was ground promotions - very effective if done well:

1. Community experiences and displays

Since child safety seats aren't widely used in China, education is key. We targeted high-end residential areas, doing door-to-door promotions. Unlike car manufacturers who also do community displays, we emphasized child safety during car rides.

2. Online-offline interaction

Ideally having a physical store helps. Without one, we opened our warehouse to customers for selection, experience, installation assistance.

We encouraged customers to bring their children when visiting, using several psychological techniques:

- Children often influence purchase decisions

- Letting children choose colors/styles reduces returns

- Shows genuine concern for child safety

- Parents are accustomed to involving children in purchases

Incentives included discounts, toys, and emphasizing comfort/safety for children.

3. DM (Direct Mail) promotions

DM involves sending printed advertisements directly to consumers via postal services. Key points:

- Focus on one main product per DM for best results

- Avoid excessive discounting which creates perceptions of inflated prices

- Gifts work better than discounts - should be related to the product

- Include useful tips/information to make the DM valuable beyond just advertising

- Set expiration dates for offers

- Use best-selling products on the cover

- Use transparent plastic packaging with eye-catching designs

- Send monthly, not too frequent

- Target neighborhoods matching your product demographic

4. Kindergarten recommendations and group buying

Kindergartens are excellent channels for reaching target customers. Strategies include:

- Focus on high-end kindergartens where families have cars

- Partner with schools to recommend products to parents

- Provide discounted units to schools for their buses

5. Car club promotions

Car clubs can generate volume sales though sometimes with lower profit margins.

Finally, regarding Baidu promotion:

We advertised on both Google and Baidu with good results. Key was controlling the geographic targeting to focus on relevant areas.
