For many years, Sharon Chan (played by Yang Gongru) has been the manager of a high-end Western restaurant in Hong Kong. Her dream is to open a Hong Kong-style tea house in Shanghai with her boyfriend, Joe Chiu Lik-hang (played by Cheung Chi-lam). Thus, Chiu Lik-hang became the vanguard helping Sharon realize her dream. The two came to Shanghai with their hard-earned savings of two million dollars and sought help from their friend, Bao Jialong, who often shuttles between Shanghai and Hong Kong. Jialong is the heir of a prestigious Hong Kong family and also a "Shanghai expert," capable of serving as an all-around consultant. However, this person often disappears without a trace, leaving his girlfriend Cheng Lin (played by Fan Bingbing) to warmly welcome Chiu Lik-hang and Sharon. After Chiu Lik-hang settled down, Sharon entrusted him entirely to Cheng Lin's care and returned to Hong Kong alone.

Chiu Lik-hang was full of confidence to start a new catering business in Shanghai, but unfortunately, something terrible happened - his two million funds were all embezzled by Jialong overnight! And Jialong disappeared as if he had vanished into thin air! Chiu Lik-hang and Cheng Lin searched all over the streets of Shanghai but found nothing. To make matters worse, Jialong had accumulated months of unpaid rent, and they were evicted by the landlord. Chiu Lik-hang was almost left with nothing, being expelled from the hotel, so Cheng Lin kindly took him in. They moved into an old villa under Jialong's name and decided to wait for Jialong's appearance like sitting ducks. However, living under the same roof as a single man and woman, their relationship inevitably became somewhat delicate...

Chiu Lik-hang was afraid that Sharon would worry about him, so he kept the matter of the money hidden and lied, saying that the "tea house plan" was proceeding smoothly! Sharon firmly believed it - but the truth can't be hidden forever, and the straightforward Cheng Lin finally couldn't help but reveal the truth. Sharon descended from heaven once again to come to Shanghai, intending to take Chiu Lik-hang back to Hong Kong. Chiu Lik-hang didn't want to return to Hong Kong facing his family with nothing accomplished, so he still decided to stay here seeking new development opportunities.

By chance, Sharon met the overseas returnee man, Man Hao. Man Hao was captivated by her demeanor and was just about to launch a pursuit when he received news that he was dispatched to work in Shanghai. The company he worked for was precisely Cheng Lin's "Dingli Real Estate." Through Cheng Lin's clever arrangement, Chiu Lik-hang managed to apply for a job at Dingli. Due to his outstanding performance, he was appreciated by his new supervisor, Man Hao. From then on, Cheng Lin and Chiu Lik-hang developed another layer of a colleague relationship. Just as their relationship seemed to enter an ambiguous stage, Jialong appeared! The hearts of the three began to ripple...

Chiu Lik-hang became Man Hao's capable assistant, and they respected each other. However, they were unfortunately caught up in an office political storm, and Cheng Lin also became a victim of the struggle. The three were "exiled" to the suburbs to handle the company's worst-performing real estate project, but they ultimately created a sales miracle for the company through their collective wisdom and cohesion. Chiu Lik-hang accidentally learned that Man Hao had feelings for Sharon, which made him feel quite uneasy. After enduring hardships together with Cheng Lin, his feelings were gradually catalyzed, but due to Jialong's reasons, the two could only rationally suppress their true feelings. However, they accidentally sparked the fire of love. Coincidentally, the next day, Sharon returned to Shanghai, which gave her a significant shock, and she found solace in Man Hao's kindness. But Man Hao is inherently kind and upright; he knows that Sharon is Chiu Lik-hang's girlfriend, so he will never use his position as a superior to pursue his subordinate's girlfriend - however, the more he interacts with Sharon, the more he likes her! In fact, deep down, Sharon also feels very compatible with Man Hao! In the world of emotions, three people are already crowded, let alone five...

Cheng Lin did not intend to destroy the long-standing relationship between Chiu Lik-hang and Sharon, so she actively encouraged them to reconcile and decided to marry far away, painfully letting go of her feelings and leaving this battlefield. Later, she was moved by Jialong's sincerity and accepted his proposal. In fact, Sharon had already persuaded Chiu Lik-hang to return to Hong Kong for marriage, just wanting to leave this place filled with emotional disputes. The relationships of these four people seemed to have settled, but would everything really be calm afterward?

The most anticipated partner of Dingli Company turned out to be Mr. Ma, an elderly acquaintance Chiu Lik-hang met by chance. His appreciation and encouragement reignited Chiu Lik-hang's career ambition, and he decided to continue working in the company to seize the opportunity and make a big move, making Sharon begin to doubt whether Chiu Lik-hang valued their relationship. A breakup was an inevitable outcome. The understanding Man Hao approached her again, comforting her, making Sharon momentarily confused if Man Hao was truly her destined soulmate. However, how could seven years of feelings be easily abandoned? Sharon lost her sense of direction in love, but her lonely heart couldn't withstand Man Hao's fiery passion...

Cheng Lin was busy preparing for her wedding. Jialong promised to hold an ultra-luxurious wedding, but he received the news of his business partner's death, freezing all the funds. Poor Jialong became a penniless man overnight. To keep his promise, he was forced to accept the financial support of Dalu, Chiu Lik-hang's rival in the company. Dalu threatened him to steal commercial information from Chiu Lik-hang and Man Hao. Unwilling to betray his friends, Jialong teamed up with Chiu Lik-hang to turn the tables and successfully thwarted Dalu's commercial conspiracy.
