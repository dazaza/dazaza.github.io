Mutation Surveyor is a Mutation/SNP detection software jointly developed by Softgenetics in the USA and Huasheng Hengye Technology Co., Ltd. in China. It is used to achieve high-throughput, high-sensitivity, and highly accurate DNA mutation/SNP detection. Currently, it has been adopted by hundreds of renowned institutions worldwide (evaluations and opinions from foreign experts on Mutation Surveyor). Within less than a year, five articles have appeared in **Science** and **Nature**, reporting research achievements using MutationSurveyor (or MutationExplorer) for studying DNA base mutations.

In the field of mutation detection, Mutation Surveyor employs two world-first technologies:

a) **Anti-correlation curve technology**: This technology frees the software from the dependency on base calls that traditional mutation detection software relies on. Mutation Surveyor directly compares point-to-point the DNA sequencing curves of the sample sequences and reference sequences, generating anti-correlation curves. During the comparison of the test DNA sequence with the standard reference sequence, five different parameters are used as criteria for mutation judgment. The anti-correlation curve technology effectively eliminates background noise interference and accurately analyzes DNA sequencing curves produced by different DNA sequencers.

b) **Accurate identification of heterozygous indels**: The analysis and judgment of heterozygous indels pose a challenge for all mutation detection software. Mutation Surveyor successfully addresses this global issue.

Perhaps you've encountered a situation where, in the sample sequences for mutation detection, the quality of the sequencing at the 5' end is good, but starting from a certain position, the sequencing peak graph suddenly becomes disordered, and the base call results contain a large amount of erroneous information or "N". If analyzed using ordinary mutation detection software, the sequence would produce multiple false-positive mutations. You might think this result is due to poor sequencing quality and discard it. However, hold on—this could be a heterozygous indel mutation, i.e., one or more bases are inserted or deleted in one strand of the double-stranded DNA, causing the subsequent sequence peaks to shift and become unreadable. Mutation Surveyor will help uncover the truth; after processing the messy peaks with anti-correlation, their true forms will be revealed. The wild-type sequence and the insertion/deletion mutant sequence will be presented sequentially. You can see how the mutant sequence shifts due to the insertion or deletion, and the number of shifted positions indicates the number of inserted bases. By comparing the two types of sequences, we can clearly observe the peak shapes of the mutated bases.

**High Sensitivity**: Mutation Surveyor provides extremely precise analysis of bidirectional sequencing results, with sensitivity greater than 99.9%. Even for single-directional sequencing results, the sensitivity reaches over 96%. The software has unique capabilities in analyzing heterozygous mutations, detecting all heterozygous mutations where the heterozygous peak height exceeds 5% of the main peak. Users can easily obtain the mutation location and various parameters of the mutation from the peak shapes of the anti-correlation curves.
