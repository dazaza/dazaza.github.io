From this week's market performance, on one hand we see the activity of mid-small cap stocks with market themes has been quite hot. They not only brought vitality to the related sectors but also warmed up the market sentiment after last week's sharp decline. They even contributed significantly to the stabilization of the index and the repair of short-term technical shapes. Examples include Sinoma Automobile, United Chemical, Feida Environmental Protection, and Yongan Forestry, etc. Meanwhile, the adjustment needs brought by the continuous rise of mid-small cap stocks in the past quarter and the accumulation of profit-making chips further manifested during this week's market rebound. This bubble-like risk will exacerbate the differentiation and volatility of individual stocks and the future market trend. The performance of individual stocks on Friday is a good example. On the other hand, we also observe that the heavyweight stocks have shown a gradual upward trend this week. For instance, the strengthening of the steel sector led by Baosteel and the sustained force of the coal sector led by Jinniu Energy have given the market the momentum for gradual increase and strong oscillation. Overall, this week's market should be in a painful period where the previous mid-small cap theme style gradually shifts to the direction of heavyweight blue-chip sectors. Before this style transformation is completely successful, the market will experience an oscillating rise, and the differentiation of individual stocks will continue to rise in the future market trend. Through the analysis of the heavyweights' market movement on Friday, despite the possibility of the initiation of banks and brokers being influenced by news, looking at the situation as we enter the last trading month of the year, these blue-chip stocks with relatively small gains themselves have the demand for catch-up rise to embellish their annual reports. Plus, the arrival of the year-end performance wave and the market's expectation of future policy directions, the attention from capital towards such themes will further increase. That is to say, the pace of the market style transformation will continue to play out. Technically, after breaking through the 120-week line this week, the market sentiment next week will become more optimistic. With the golden cross formation of the rising 60-week moving average and the 250-week moving average, their supporting effect on the index's rise in the future will further manifest. Finally, the red column of the MACD indicator on the daily K-line chart has reappeared. Although the length is currently relatively small, we have reason to believe that a spark can start a prairie fire. With the trend of the 5-day moving average forming a golden cross with the 10-day and 20-day moving averages, and the breakout of the 3300-point resistance level with increased volume on Friday, the upward space for the index in the future has opened up. In the current relatively optimistic market fundamentals and technical aspects, investors need to guard against the risks brought by the future differentiation of themes. Under the tone of the overall mid-term upward trend remaining unchanged, firmly grasp the varieties with continuously excellent performance in the market, seize the market rhythm and trend situation, then steadily following the index upward to gain profits in the future are very much worth expecting.

The procedure for buying and selling stocks is as follows: You must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. And this order is only valid for the day it was placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock. The abbreviation of the stock is usually three to four Chinese characters, and the code is six digits. The code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent when placing an order. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks bought and sold: the quantity of stocks ordered to buy must be a multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks ordered to sell does not have to be a multiple of 100. There are four ways to place an order: counter submission order, telephone automatic order, computer automatic order, and remote terminal order.
