Agriculture sector strengthened in the afternoon, GEM stocks surged in the late session＿Stock Forum

by xl0958870 on 2009-12-05 02:23:53

The Shanghai Composite Index opened low this morning at 3268.00 points. After several waves of low-position oscillation, the steel and broker sectors simultaneously supported the market, causing the index to continue its oscillating rise. It touched the highest point of the day at 3270.37 during trading hours. However, under the pressure of yesterday's closing price, the index once again oscillated and fell back. Subsequently, the index conducted several bottom-touches under the drag of PetroChina and Sinopec as well as the banking sector, touching the lowest point of the day at 3227.77 points during afternoon trading hours. The index then surged again significantly but ultimately failed to close in the red. In terms of support and resistance levels, the index took the 10-day and 20-day moving averages as potential supports and yesterday's closing price as potential resistance. From the perspective of gap conditions on the board, there are still two short-term gaps at 2635.31 points and 2803.86 points below the market that need to be filled. Moreover, the lowest gap at 1762 points below the index has yet to be replenished, implying a long-term technical adjustment demand for the Shanghai Composite Index. The next long-term target above the index should be the gap left by the January 22nd last year’s low open at 4818.00 - 4891.28 points. Therefore, the confrontation between bulls and bears will determine the directional choice of the index. By the end of today's trading, the Shanghai Composite Index was reported at 3264.63 points, falling 5.12 points from the previous trading day's closing price, with a decline of -0.16%, and a trading volume of 191.355 billion yuan.

Similarly, the Shenzhen Component Index also opened low this morning at 13789.08 points. After continuous oscillation and washing out, the steel and broker sectors strengthened significantly, driving the Shenzhen Component Index to turn red during trading hours and touch the highest point of the day at 13822.91 points. Subsequently, the weakening of the real estate, brewing, and electrical appliance sectors dragged the Shenzhen Component Index to narrow its fall, soon touching the lowest point of the day at 13604.15 points. The index then rallied again but failed to close in the red. From the perspective of support and resistance levels, the area around yesterday's closing price constituted significant pressure on the Shenzhen Component Index, while the 10-day moving average provided potential support. From the perspective of gap conditions on the board, there are gaps at 11874.90 points and 11300.03 points below the Shenzhen Component Index that need to be filled. The previous gap at 7266.41 points still suggests the necessity of a long-term adjustment for the index. By the end of today's trading, the Shenzhen Component Index was reported at 13759.83 points, falling 40.67 points from the previous trading day's closing price, with a decline of -0.29%, and a trading volume of 127.828 billion yuan.

In terms of sectors, the initial determination of Chinese shareholders for Shanghai Disneyland includes Lujiazui holding 60% of shares. Lujiazui opened with a limit up, stimulating Disney concept stocks to perform relatively strongly after opening. Stocks such as Jielong Industrial,斯米克(Smic), and Pudong Jinqiao currently lead in gains. Song Liping, General Manager of the Shenzhen Stock Exchange, stated that margin trading is now ready, and funds will become financing targets for margin trading and financial targets for hedging risks of stock index futures. Affected by this news, the stock index futures concept made a comeback, with surging individual stock trends. Margin trading rumors stimulated strong overall trends in broker stocks during the morning session. Additionally, the transport logistics sector index also performed well. With the global economy warming up, the recent increase in raw material demand has been noticeable. The recent coal demand peak season has also led to slight price increases for some freight companies.

The Ministry of Finance issued a notice on December 1st stating that after the new corn crop from Northeast China hits the market in 2009, the central government will provide a one-time fixed-amount subsidy to southern feed consumption provinces that purchase the new corn crop from Northeast China and transport it locally. This news stimulated agricultural stocks to surge significantly near the end of trading. Xinong Development, Denghai Seed, and Fengle Seed all hit their daily limits. However, gold stocks experienced a minor sell-off in the afternoon. In summary, agriculture, stock index futures, Disney, brokers, transportation, and coal sectors were among the top gainers today, while banking, aerospace military, insurance, nonferrous metals, and real estate sectors performed relatively poorly.

From the news aspect, key points include: 1) 68 central enterprises involved in financial derivatives businesses have floating losses of 11.4 billion yuan; 2) High-level central authorities mention curbing real estate speculation again; 3) The State Council will strengthen incentive and constraint mechanisms for capacity elimination; 4) Shanghai Disneyland's Chinese company is established, with Lujiazui holding 60% of shares; 5) WTO ministerial meetings make no significant progress on Doha Round negotiations; 6) Excess inventory causes New York oil prices to fall by 2.3%; 7) New York gold prices close above 1200 dollars, setting another record high; 8) Financial and energy stocks decline, leading to mixed US stock market closes: Dow Jones down 18 points, Nasdaq up 9 points.

By the end of today's trading, a total of 952 stocks rose in the Shanghai and Shenzhen markets, with 34 hitting their daily limits, including 10 S, ST, and *ST class stocks. The three newly listed stocks—Santai Electronics, Rihai Communications, and Yazzi Shares—all opened with gains exceeding 80%. By the end of today's trading, the gains for these three stocks were: Santai Electronics up 130.77%, Rihai Communications up 114.92%, and Yazzi Shares up 90.07%. A total of 634 stocks fell across both markets, with Seg Samsung and Shen Seg hitting their daily limits. Among the top 50 weighted stocks in the Shanghai market today, excluding Shanghai Automotive which was suspended from trading, 15 blue-chip stocks rose and 34 weighted stocks fell. Clearly, the short side dominated the weighted stocks in the morning session, suggesting a strategy of laying out rebounds at lower levels, switching positions, and enjoying individual stock rally situations ahead.

After the earlier massive declines and recent "revenge" rebounds, profit-taking pressures have significantly decreased. The subsequent rise will be more independent and localized, with large-cap blue-chip stocks possibly becoming the most suitable varieties for widespread participation. Under the premise that the stock index has filled previous gaps and adjusted sufficiently, the recent sharp fluctuations indicate that the current market possesses the prominent feature of rapid falls followed by gradual rises within an upward trend. Additionally, since capital that fled earlier has thoroughly gone short on a batch of stocks and turned to enter a new batch, it is unlikely for individual stocks to experience significant rises. It is expected that the market will return to a rhythm of oscillatory upward movement.

1. Gold Price Rise May Help A-shares Reach New Highs

At 3 am Beijing time on December 3rd, the nearest-term gold futures contract on the New York market closed above 1200 dollars per ounce, peaking at nearly 1220 dollars during the session. Since entering November, gold prices have set historical highs on 19 trading days. Since the beginning of the year, gold prices have risen approximately 37%, with increases in 17 of the past 20 weeks. Throughout November, the New York gold market fell only twice, and the first two trading days of December both saw strong gains. The London gold market's midday fixing price was 1212.5 dollars per ounce, also setting a historical high. This price serves as one of the benchmark prices for the global gold market, having risen nearly 40% since the start of the year.

Statistics from the Comex department of the New York Mercantile Exchange show that speculative buying positions in gold remain at historical highs. Additionally, the total amount of gold held by gold ETFs has again reached a new high. Previously, investors mainly attributed the reasons for the rise in gold prices to the depreciation of the dollar, but the recent rise in international gold prices differs from the earlier situation. Before November, the depreciation of the dollar was indeed the main driver behind the sharp rise in gold prices, but explaining the current surge in gold prices solely through dollar depreciation seems to lack sufficient persuasiveness. Currently, the rise in gold prices appears unstoppable, with fluctuations in the stock and commodity markets having little impact on gold prices. Moreover, the movements of the dollar seem to only amplify rather than suppress gold prices: when the dollar rebounds, the drop in gold prices is limited; but when the dollar falls, gold prices soar sharply.

We believe that apart from being influenced by dollar depreciation, the current strength in gold prices is mainly due to the following reasons: First, supply and demand, the peak purchasing season for major gold-consuming countries like India and China is the most direct cause of the strengthening gold prices; Second, hedging demand, when gold prices were hovering near low levels, gold producers sold part of their expected output and committed to delivering goods on a future date, but as gold prices began to rise, these producers incurred losses, so they bought back hedging positions; Third, central bank gold reserves, central banks hold gold as part of their reserves, and central bank buying and selling of gold can affect gold prices, such as the agreement recently reached by a group of 19 European central banks to extend the term of the agreement limiting gold sales, which has had an important impact on the strengthening of gold prices; Fourth, political tensions, gold is widely regarded as a "safe-haven asset," and there will be safe-haven buying during times of uncertainty; Fifth, crude oil prices, historically gold prices have had a strong correlation with crude oil prices because gold can be used to hedge against inflation caused by rising oil prices, and the strengthening of crude oil prices also enhances interest in commodities as an asset class; Sixth, investor factors, investment funds have increased their interest in commodities including gold in recent years, which is one of the main underlying drivers of gold prices reaching historical highs. Additionally, investors' demand to use gold to hedge against the risk of dollar depreciation remains strong, with the dollar index currently hovering near its lowest point in 15 months.

We believe that under the circumstances of continuously rising gold prices and continued weakness in the dollar index, resource prices will continue to rise, enhancing expectations for inflation and renminbi appreciation, which is beneficial for the rise in the A-share market. Therefore, from this perspective, the continuous rise in gold prices provides necessary conditions for A-shares to reach new highs in the cross-year rally.

2. Some Technical Analysis of the Current Market

Firstly, the current status of the moving averages reflects that oscillatory consolidation will still be the main way the index operates in the future. This is because the distance and direction of the various moving averages currently lack a unified pattern: In terms of direction, although the 5-day moving average completed the upward shape today, the 10-day moving average still points downward; In terms of distance, the 10-day and 20-day moving averages are currently tangled, but the 60-day moving average is far away from the dense moving average area. Therefore, the current state of the moving averages reflects that the direction of the index's operation in the short term is not obvious, and oscillatory consolidation will still be the main tone.

Secondly, in terms of form characteristics, the Shanghai Composite Index currently exhibits a standard "pennant" pattern. Observing the three rebounds starting from September 1st, the connection line of their bottoms and the connection line of their peaks are almost parallel, forming a relatively clear ascending channel. According to technical form theory, the "pennant" is a standard form of oscillatory consolidation, and after the completion of this form, the index will continue to break through along the original direction. Based on this judgment, the index will still be in a pattern of oscillatory rise in the future.

Thirdly, regarding the gaps, there are two short-term gaps at 2635.31 points and 2803.86 points below the Shanghai Composite Index that need to be filled, and the lowest gap at 1762 points below the index has yet to be replenished, hinting at a long-term technical adjustment demand for the Shanghai Composite Index. Below the Shenzhen Component Index, there are gaps at 11874.90 points and 11300.03 points that need to be filled, and the previous gap at 7266.41 points still implies the necessity of a long-term adjustment for the index. Notably, the previous adjustment of the index came very close to filling the gap at 3077 points but unfortunately did not actually fill it, leaving the possibility of another short-term adjustment in the market.

In conclusion, under the backdrop of the US stock market constantly rewriting new highs for the year and the domestic economy recovering well, the occurrence of sharp falls reflects that investors' tolerance for the market still needs improvement. Although China's banking industry may face the challenge of raising billions in capital to replenish capital adequacy ratios, the Huijin Company has already hinted at continuing capital injections, so the situation may not be as bad as the market imagines. We believe that the divergence between the CSI 50 and CSI 180 representing large-cap stocks and the Shenzhen Composite Index and SME Board Index representing small and medium-sized stocks will not persist for a long time, and the recovery of blue-chip stocks is the largest potential for the index to return to an upward channel.

Stock trading procedures are as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. Furthermore, this order is only valid on the day it is placed. The abbreviation of a stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is six digits long. When placing a buy or sell order, the code and abbreviation of the stock must match. Meanwhile, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks traded: the quantity of stocks commissioned for purchase must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks commissioned for sale does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.
