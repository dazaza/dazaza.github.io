Smart Grid - Making Life Exciting

Smart electricity use, making life exciting. If you are an office worker, before you finish work in the evening, you can press a few specific numbers on your phone to start the electronic shoe cabinet, rice cooker, air conditioner, humidifier, or even the electric curtains at home. Half an hour later, when you return home, the temperature inside will be pleasant, the air fresh, and a pot of fragrant rice already cooked... Isn't such a lifestyle exciting? Nowadays, 418 households living in the Sino-Singapore Tianjin Eco-City enjoy such an exciting life.

In this neighborhood, solar photovoltaic panels are installed on residents' balconies, with 20% of household electricity coming from this clean energy source. As China's first comprehensive smart grid demonstration project, it integrates power fiber optics and information technology, enabling residents to achieve intelligent home living. The magic of the smart grid is not only reflected in remote control of home appliances for laundry and cooking, but also through the smart grid integrated management platform, residents can use their home's main remote control to understand the electricity consumption ratio of various appliances, conduct power consumption analysis, reasonably choose electricity usage methods, and reduce household electricity expenses. Meanwhile, the smart grid also promotes the popularization and application of electric vehicles, with three charging stations built in this neighborhood using solar power to charge electric vehicles.

"By the end of this year, 25 smart communities and buildings across the country will realize two-way interaction of intelligent electricity use, and people's daily lives will become more low-carbon and environmentally friendly due to the smart grid." Lin Hongyu, Director of the Smart Department of the Science and Technology Department of State Grid Corporation told reporters that the smart grid involves six major links: power generation, transmission, transformation, distribution, electricity use, and dispatching. In the past two years, the State Grid Corporation has comprehensively promoted the pilot construction of smart grids, fully launching 228 pilot projects in 21 categories, achieving important breakthroughs in six areas: smart substations, distribution automation, electricity information collection systems, electric vehicle charging and swapping facilities, smart grid dispatching technical support systems, and power fiber to the home. To date, China has built eight smart substations including Shaanxi's 750 kV and Yan'an, Jiangsu's 220 kV, becoming a leading force in guiding the development of world substation technology. There have been continuous efforts to fill domestic gaps in key equipment research and development and technical standard system construction for smart substations, forming the world's first series of technical standards for smart substations, applying for 126 patents, with overall technological level internationally leading.

Ultra-high voltage technology, China's creation. On December 9th, the "1000 kV Jindongnan-Nanyang-Jingmen Ultra-high Voltage AC Test Demonstration Project", the world's first successfully commercialized ultra-high voltage line, brought good news again. As the starting project for the State Grid Corporation's "Twelfth Five-Year Plan" ultra-high voltage grid construction, on that day, during the high-load test phase of the expansion project, the expected goal of continuously transmitting 5 million kilowatts of power was achieved, setting a new world record, further verifying the ability of ultra-high voltage AC lines to transmit large volumes of power over long distances and its economic superiority over conventional grade transmission lines.

Ultra-high voltage transmission key technology is a major project under the national "Eleventh Five-Year Plan" scientific and technological support plan and a significant frontier technology in the global energy field. According to international standards, ultra-high voltage (UHV) refers to voltages of 1000 kV and above. In China, the UHV grid refers to a transmission network at voltage levels of 1000 kV AC and ±800 kV DC. Shen Jiang, Deputy Director of the Science and Technology Department of the State Grid Corporation, introduced that China's energy and productivity layout is inversely distributed, resulting in long-term tension in energy transportation. UHV transmission has the advantages of long distance, large capacity, low loss, and high efficiency. Building a strong smart grid with UHV grids as the backbone network can promote the intensive development and utilization of large coal-fired power plants, large hydropower plants, large nuclear power plants, and large renewable energy bases. At the same time, UHV grids can enhance the grid's ability to resist sudden events and severe faults, further improving the reliability and stability of power system operation, providing a solid network foundation for the construction of a strong smart grid.

During the "Eleventh Five-Year Plan" period, the State Grid Corporation successively completed the UHV AC Test Demonstration Project from Jindongnan to Nanyang to Jingmen and the ±800 kV UHV DC Transmission Demonstration Project from Xiangjiaba to Shanghai through major innovation engineering projects in UHV, comprehensively mastering core UHV technologies, possessing the capability to manufacture complete sets of UHV equipment, achieving a domestication rate of equipment of 90%, and formulating a complete set of UHV technical standards. UHV AC voltage officially became an international standard due to China's breakthroughs in the UHV technology field. The International Electrotechnical Commission specifically established a High Voltage DC Transmission Technology Committee, with the Secretariat located at the State Grid Corporation.

In 2009, US Energy Secretary Steven Chu visited the State Grid Corporation of China. He was reluctant to leave while standing in front of the UHV project sandbox. The originally planned five-minute introduction was extended to fifteen minutes. "China's UHV project is impressive, reaching a very high technical level. America's wind power is mainly concentrated in the north, and solar power is mostly in the southeast, also facing the problem of long-distance, large-capacity transmission like China does, requiring such UHV technology," Mr. Chu commented, "China has set an example for the United States and many countries around the world. We also have some research in this area and hope to have more cooperation with the State Grid Corporation of China."

Wind-solar-storage-transmission project, solving the problem of new energy power generation. In the depths of winter, Zhangbei Dam Grassland. Looking out, the towering white wind turbines and shining solar photovoltaic panels blend beautifully with the blue sky and earth, stretching endlessly, presenting a magnificent sight! More than half a year ago, this place was still a hilly slope. Now, a world-class new energy demonstration base stands tall.

This is the site of the National Wind-Solar-Storage-Transmission Demonstration Project. Despite the bone-chilling cold, construction personnel are urgently carrying out the final debugging of the joint power generation panoramic intelligent monitoring system, with the project entering the final stage. The goal of putting into operation by the end of the year is within reach.

"Implementing the wind-solar-storage-transmission demonstration project aims to solve the problems of new energy power generation. Only by achieving new breakthroughs in management and technology can we ensure stable new energy power generation, effective transmission, proper reception, and good utilization," Wang Min, Vice President of the State Grid Corporation, introduced. The National Wind-Solar-Storage-Transmission Demonstration Project is the first key project launched jointly by the Ministry of Finance, the Ministry of Science and Technology, the National Energy Administration, and the State Grid Corporation under the "Golden Sun Project". It is also one of the first key projects of the State Grid Corporation for building a strong smart grid and currently the largest integrated renewable energy project in the world combining wind power, photovoltaic power, energy storage, and transmission engineering.

Wind power depends on the wind, and solar power generation depends on sunlight. However, unpredictable weather conditions make most new energy power generation intermittent and fluctuating. These characteristics disrupt the balance between the original grid's power supply and load demand, affecting the grid's frequency regulation, peak regulation, and safe and stable operation! This project focuses on wind-solar power generation control and energy storage system integration technology, aiming to achieve smooth output, plan tracking, peak shaving and valley filling, and frequency modulation control goals for new energy power generation, solving the technical problems of large-scale grid connection of new energy. With the project's commissioning, it will achieve five major technological breakthroughs, complete the R&D of 10 autonomous devices, create 20 technological innovations, obtain 70 intellectual property rights, cultivate 100 high-level talents in the new energy field, and achieve the organic integration of scientific research and industry!

Last October, the "State Grid Corporation Green Development White Paper" was released. This is the first green development white paper issued by Chinese enterprises, conveying the rational voice of the State Grid Corporation to the government and society: building a strong smart grid, promoting the green transformation of China's development model. According to the plan, by 2015, China's UHV will form a "three verticals, three horizontals, and one loop network", basically completing a strong smart grid with the characteristics of informatization, automation, and interactivity. A three synchronous grid will be formed with North China, Central China, and East China as receiving ends and Northwest and Northeast grids as sending ends, preliminarily building a world-class grid. By 2020, after the completion of the strong smart grid, carbon dioxide emissions can be reduced by about 1.65 billion tons, reducing the carbon dioxide emission intensity of that year by 8.8% compared to 2005, contributing 20%-23% to achieving China's carbon reduction target.
