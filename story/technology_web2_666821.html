Analysis of the Causes of Schizophrenia

From ancient times to modern days, schizophrenia is not a rare disease. It is a mental illness that we are all familiar with. It not only severely damages the physical and mental health of patients and makes them lose their normal life and work, but also causes great harm to society. Therefore, preventing schizophrenia is particularly important. So what are the causes of schizophrenia?

Traditional medical models emphasize biological causes. According to this view, schizophrenia belongs to diseases with unknown causes. Because since the discovery of pathogenic microorganisms, people have been accustomed to viewing the causes of various diseases as single factors. If a single cause cannot be found, it is considered "cause unknown." Many common diseases can be regarded as having unknown causes, such as hypertension and gastric ulcers. Schizophrenia, of course, also falls into this category.

However, this traditional concept has changed, reflected in the changes in disease models since the 1970s, shifting from the original biomedical model to the bio-psychosocial medical model. This means that for most diseases, their occurrence does not absolutely depend on a single factor. Whether one gets sick also depends on their mental and physical condition at the time, which is closely related to their environment. The incidence of physical illnesses in the elderly is twice that of young people.

What might be the causes of schizophrenia? Some people believe it is caused by the combined effects of many factors. Some even think that even in the future, it will be impossible to find a single causative factor that explains all cases of schizophrenia. Therefore, the etiological research introduced in this article involves multiple fields, including clinical genetics, molecular genetics, brain imaging, neurodevelopment, and psychosocial factors.

The human brain, as a complex organ with a strict protective structure, has very complex functions. In research, it is generally difficult to obtain a large number of live brain samples. Moreover, schizophrenia patients either have impaired cognitive abilities or are unable to cooperate with researchers as required. Long-term use of antipsychotic drugs changes the biochemical conditions in the brain, affecting its structure, function, or other aspects. Stopping medication leads to recurring mental symptoms. The diversity of symptoms is also a challenge in research, as the same patient may experience fluctuations in symptoms at different times. Additionally, the diversity and fluctuation of symptoms make them harder to identify due to the use of antipsychotic drugs, among other situations. These various circumstances together constitute the complexity of schizophrenia research. The above introduction covers four aspects of the causes of schizophrenia. Due to these complexities in research, there are numerous difficulties in studying the etiology of schizophrenia. However, up to now, the exact cause of the disease has not been fully elucidated. These causes should not be ignored in daily life and need more attention.

The above introduces several causes of schizophrenia. Understanding and mastering these common knowledge points will help us prevent the occurrence of schizophrenia in a timely manner. Since schizophrenia is the most severe and harmful type of mental illness, active prevention is the best strategy, which can reduce the harm of schizophrenia to humanity.
