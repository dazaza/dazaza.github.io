China will become a new global financial center: two factors boost China's financial rise. Wu Xiaoyu proposed that the reason why this financial crisis did not lead to a great depression like in 1929 was mainly due to China's economic stimulus to the world. The fundamental reasons for China's leading financial recovery are twofold: one is having a good financial system domestically, and the other is having a large domestic demand market. Additionally, China has become the world's second-largest economy. Such a large economy recovers first, coupled with very strong external demand, which enables the global economy to quickly get out of the rut. Whether it is Europe or the United States, their most difficult economic times have passed.

According to reports by the Securities Times, yesterday at the first alumni finance forum of Jiangxi University of Finance and Economics, Wu Xiaoyu, director of the Institute of Finance and Securities of Renmin University of China, stated that China's financial market will become a new global financial center. By 2020, the combined market value of the Shenzhen and Shanghai stock markets will exceed 100 trillion yuan; the RMB will become the most important international currency after the US dollar within the next 10-20 years; China will transform into an emerging wealth management center.

For China's finance to rise, the RMB must be internationalized first, achieving this goal within at least 20 years. Before the financial crisis, the US dollar accounted for 95% of the share in bulk commodity transactions and pricing. By 2020, China should strive for the RMB to account for 20% of the global trade share.

Wu Xiaoyu pointed out that for China to become a global financial center, it must possess multiple rules. In terms of hardware, it includes strong economic competitiveness, large trading scale, obvious industrial advantages, and the internationalization of the RMB; in terms of software, it includes the perfection of democracy and rule of law, high social integrity, etc. Currently, China already possesses some of these rules, and others are in the process of being met.

If China's capital market reaches 100 trillion RMB by 2020, it will become the world's second-largest market at that time—not a trade market but an asset trading market. Now, the RMB is transitioning from settlement and payment functions to wealth management functions, but only by prioritizing supply can the dynamic balance between supply and demand be achieved.

Wu Xiaoyu believes that for China's finance to advance, the RMB must be internationalized first, achieving this goal within at least 20 years. Before the financial crisis, the US dollar accounted for 95% of the share in bulk commodity transactions and pricing. By 2020, China should strive for the RMB to account for 20% of the global trade share. Moreover, for China to become a financial power, it must have a very strong manufacturing sector and modern industry, and this industrial advantage cannot be replicated.

Wu Xiaoyu summarized the causes of this financial crisis as "one center and two basic points." "One center" refers to the long-term imbalance in the economic structure, where the financial system and virtual economy pattern do not match the underlying economic structure, inevitably causing significant fluctuations in the virtual economy to reach a match with the economic structure. "Two basic points" include the single-polar international monetary system dominated by the US dollar, under which the United States arbitrarily transfers global financial risks; the other basic point is the increasingly severe trade and investment protectionism, which exacerbates the arrival of the financial crisis.

Regarding the widespread concern about the tightening of monetary policy next year, Wu Xiaoyu believed there was no need to worry. Currently, the foundation for recovery needs to be consolidated, and it is expected that the overall monetary policy next year will not significantly tighten, and the scale of credit issuance will still reach a level that promotes financial development accordingly.
