V. Common Issues

1. GHOST installation interrupted, showing the following prompt:

output error file to the following location: A:\ghosterr.txt

The cause is generally due to read or write data errors, which may be caused by poor quality of the CD or poor reading ability of the CD drive, or errors when writing to the hard drive.

2. Realteck HD Audio sound card front microphone and headphone interfaces cannot be used.

This is due to a new feature in the latest official Realteck sound card driver, not a system issue. If this happens, go to "Control Panel" -> "Sound, Voice and Audio Devices" -> find "Realtek HD Audio Manager" -> "Audio I/O" -> "Connector Settings" -> check "Disable Front Panel Jack Detection."

3. After installation, a notification appears indicating that the system did not pass the genuine validation, with a star icon appearing in the lower right corner.

This situation is mostly due to incorrect system time. Correct the computer time and then restart the computer. Do not use so-called crack tools, as they do not solve the problem.

4. What should you do if the system crashes or continuously restarts during installation?

Such situations are generally due to Windows misidentifying hardware during driver installation. You can re-GHOST restore the system, and when the driver selection screen appears, press the [ESC] key to cancel all integrated drivers to resolve the issue.

5. After GHOST, the entire hard drive becomes one partition.

This is usually due to manual GHOST operation errors, restoring the partition image to the entire hard drive. Immediately turn off the computer and avoid writing data to the hard drive.

It is recommended to connect the hard drive to another computer as a secondary drive and use relevant software to restore the partition table. If the data is important, seek help from a professional data recovery company.
