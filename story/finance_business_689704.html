Chinese Concept Stocks Rose and Fell on Friday, with 12 Stocks Up by Over 3%

by anonymous on 2012-03-04 14:14:15

The Nasdaq Composite Index fell by 0.75%. Sina Tech reported on the morning of November 26, Beijing time, that US stocks slightly declined on Friday, with the Nasdaq Composite Index dropping by 0.75%. Chinese concept stocks showed mixed performances, with 12 stocks rising by more than 3%, while another 5 stocks fell by over 3%. Among them, VIMicro surged by 9.37%, Phoenix New Media rose by 8.58%, Linktone increased by 8.26%, Youku and China Distance Education climbed by 7.16% and 7.38% respectively, and Perfect World went up by 7.05%. Huas视 Media and Ku6 Media dropped by 8.33% and 8% respectively.

On Friday, Spreadtrum Communication increased by 6.82%, along with E-car lock decoder, car jammer, car unlocking tool, car remote decoder, etc. Dangdang (Weibo) and Century互联 each rose by 4.29% and 4%, while SMIC, AutoNavi Software, and NetQin had gains between 3% to 4%. In addition, Qiaoxing Mobile fell by 4.17%, Jins Finance and Dexin Wireless dropped by 3.28% and 3.26% respectively.

US markets were closed on Thursday due to Thanksgiving and closed early on Friday. The reason for the decline on Friday was that S&P downgraded Belgium's sovereign credit rating by one notch from AA+ to AA; Italian bond yields reached a new high in the Euro era, putting pressure on the market.

At 1:00 PM EST on November 25 (2:00 AM Beijing time on November 26), the Dow Jones Industrial Average fell by 25.61 points, closing at 11,231.94, a drop of 0.23%; the Nasdaq Composite Index fell by 18.50 points, closing at 2,441.58, a drop of 0.75%; and the S&P 500 Index fell by 3.12 points, closing at 1,158.67, a drop of 0.27%.
