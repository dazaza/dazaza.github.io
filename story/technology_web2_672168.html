The first station of wealth creation, dare to start big businesses

by 91chuangye on 2012-02-22 16:03:15

―――The Fifth Essay on Deepening the New Round of Ideological Emancipation Activities

Encouraging entrepreneurship and developing private economy is the way to strengthen a city, the source of enriching people, the foundation of promoting industries, and the strategy of consolidating the base. Standing at a new starting point and achieving new leaps, we must implement the "People's Entrepreneurship" project, fully mobilizing the enthusiasm for entrepreneurship among the masses, allowing more people to increase their property income through self-employment, hard work in entrepreneurship, and lawful business.

Encouraging entrepreneurship is an issue universally valued both domestically and internationally. In the 1980s, the UK launched the "Youth Entrepreneurship Program," encouraging unemployed youth to transition towards self-employment. Now, one out of every eight workers in the UK is a self-employed person. According to statistics, in developed countries, there is on average one small enterprise for every 20 people; in Qingdao and Wenzhou, there is one for every 27 people; in Shanghai, one for every 18 people; in Beijing, one for every 13 people. Currently, in our city, there is one individual or private enterprise for every 35 people. Compared with advanced regions, whether in terms of the number of enterprises, the workforce, the scale of enterprises, or tax contributions, there is a clear gap in our city’s private economy. Facing fierce competition where hundreds of boats race forward and thousands of horses gallop ahead nationwide, we must further promote an entrepreneurial culture, optimize the entrepreneurial environment, release the potential for pursuing wealth and courage to create that lies within the masses, effectively utilize accumulated social capital and various resources, and build Weihai into fertile ground for entrepreneurship where all elements gather and all vitality bursts forth.

Exploring the reasons for the gaps between us and advanced regions in the development of the private economy, policies and measures are an important aspect. Wenzhou has been open and flexible in promoting the development of the private economy, encouraging entrepreneurship and allowing exploration, providing entrepreneurs with broad space for development. The first "Individual Business License," the first non-regional restriction private financial group, the first crowd-funded airport, the first shareholding railway, and the first farmers' city all originated from Wenzhou. At all levels and in all departments, we should seriously reflect on advanced regional experiences: Are we truly valuing the private economy? Have supportive policies played their intended roles? What preferential policies do others have that we lack, and can we quickly improve them? How well are policies supporting and encouraging public entrepreneurship implemented, and does the phenomenon of "middle-level obstruction" exist? We need to scientifically grasp policies: anything not prohibited by national laws and policies can be done; any industry or field allowed by the state for the private economy to enter can be entered; anyone meeting market access conditions or operational standards should be promptly granted permission. We need to further enrich and improve existing policies, freeing up what needs to be freed, and giving fully what needs to be given, accelerating the transformation from cadre-led economy to mass-based economy, truly making the masses the main creators of social wealth.

Currently, backward thinking remains the biggest obstacle to public entrepreneurship. Influenced by traditional culture, some people are still content with the status quo, satisfied with modest wealth, complacent with minor progress, unwilling to take risks, and uninterested in arduous entrepreneurship; some want to start businesses and get rich, but due to insufficient confidence, they observe much and act little, think much and do little; others aim too high, unable to earn large sums yet unwilling to earn small ones, even idling away their time doing nothing. To promote universal entrepreneurship, we must change this stagnant situation where people don't want to become bosses, dare not venture into markets, and are unwilling to pursue great undertakings. We need to learn from the pioneering spirit of Wenzhou people who "traverse thousands of mountains and rivers, endure countless hardships, try every possible means, and fear no difficulties or dangers," and their innovative spirit of "taking risks others dare not take, enduring hardships others cannot bear, and earning money others look down upon." We need to strive to create a social atmosphere that encourages thinking about entrepreneurship, daring to engage in entrepreneurship, and being capable of succeeding in entrepreneurship.

Practice has proven that leading by example is an effective way to promote public entrepreneurship. In recent years, our city has seen the emergence of many distinctive star enterprises and models of wealth creation such as Huaxia. All levels need to carefully summarize and promote these advanced examples of entrepreneurship and wealth creation, guiding farmers out of their fields to become brokers, engage in trade, run factories, and explore markets; encouraging officials to step out of government compounds to start businesses, create careers, and become bosses; helping laid-off workers escape unemployment dilemmas by opening shops, starting real enterprises, and focusing on earning money; driving small and large owners out of the misconception of being satisfied with modest wealth and minor progress, daring to establish large enterprises, undertake great endeavors, and become major bosses, enabling everyone with capability to become active economic cells, forming a lively situation across the city where everyone engages in grand entrepreneurship and enterprises pursue great achievements. (Editorialist of Our Newspaper)
