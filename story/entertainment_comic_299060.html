★ Wig Care Instructions:

• Try not to expose the wig to high temperatures, as the material is not heat resistant.

• Do not dye the wig. If trimming is needed, please ask a professional stylist to trim and style the wig.

• Generally, wash the wig every 1-2 months, depending on the frequency of use.

• Wash with cold or warm water. Use regular shampoo, and conditioner can be used if desired.

• After washing, try not to dry the wig with a blow dryer or other high-temperature devices. Instead, use a dry towel to gently absorb excess water and place it in a ventilated area, avoiding direct sunlight which may damage the wig.

• Do not comb the wig immediately after washing. Wait until it is dry before combing.

• Use a wig-specific comb, not a plastic comb.

• For curly wigs, generally do not use a comb. Simply tidy the curls by hand after each use.

• If the wig gets tangled after long use, do not pull hard. Instead, spray a non-oily wig-specific care solution and carefully untangle it.

• Note: Do not spray gel water, hair wax, or other styling agents meant for real hair on the wig, as this will make the wig sticky.

• Using a non-oily wig-specific care solution can make the wig soft and shiny, prevent static electricity, and keep the wig moisturized, just like when it was newly purchased!

• Wigs can be tied up, but not too high, otherwise your real hair may show.

• When combing longer wigs, divide the wig into sections and comb from bottom to top. Be gentle and patient.

• A small amount of shedding during arrangement and wearing is normal.

• When not in use, store the wig in its original packaging. Shake it before wearing, and it will return to its original shape.
