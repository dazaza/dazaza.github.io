Abstract: This article introduces the background and necessity of research on computer education courses oriented towards application, analyzes the characteristics and curriculum of applied education, outlines the factors to consider, and on this basis, establishes a computer education course oriented towards application. It also highlights the distinctive features of each specific component of the course and finally explores the potential issues that may arise during the detailed implementation of the course.

Keywords: Computer science undergraduate innovation ability cultivation, since 1995,

Higher education has achieved the goal of building world-class universities and high-level research-oriented universities and teaching universities as part of national construction [1]. These universities focus on disseminating knowledge, production, and application, with the aim of conducting high-level scientific research and cultivating high-level talents. Among China's more than 1000 colleges and universities, some can be categorized as either research or teaching institutions. The elite education for applied talents lacks a clear training program aimed at research, and the technical worker training programs cannot meet the needs of skill-based talent development. Therefore, it is significant to explore application-oriented basic course education to improve quality.

University computer science is one of the largest professional knowledge fields. By 2005, more than 771 universities and colleges offered computer science programs [2]. Besides the education oriented towards applications, there are also distinct features in undergraduate education focused on computer applications and the inherent characteristics of computer science education itself. Thus, studying the curriculum of application-oriented computer undergraduate education requires first exploring the positioning of applied education. Research training aims for elite education, while application-oriented education focuses on improving the quality of talent cultivation. The goal of applied education is to support local or regional economic and social development, adhere to the integration of industry, academia, and research, and achieve professional interaction with the industry. Graduates should be able to adapt to enterprises, institutions, management, and research and teaching units, engaging in operations, management, services, advanced technical research work, or applied engineering technology talents [3-6].

Education and training plans oriented towards application adopt a market-driven approach, focusing on application based on market demand. They fully consider local economic development, industrial structure, technological level, and sustainable development concepts and methods, ensuring that personnel training standards, knowledge, and capabilities align with the quality requirements of economic development to meet local market demands [2-6]. As an undergraduate level education, applied education curricula should not only have a more generous foundation of knowledge but also consider meeting societal practical needs by developing knowledge to analyze and solve problems [5]. Therefore, applied undergraduate education cannot follow normal educational models to achieve academic and research university standards. Neither can it omit excellent application and teaching content's foundational theories but should moderately deepen, balance points and surfaces, comprehensively consider students' knowledge and abilities, improve overall quality, enhance the application aspect of basic course content throughout the teaching process, and strengthen students' capabilities and application abilities [Computer Core Journal Publication].
