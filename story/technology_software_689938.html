"Starry Sky Change: A tourist wins a big prize in Lijiang's scenic spot ticket lottery, but without a ticket stub, it's difficult to claim the 200,000 yuan bonus."

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-04 17:52:13

"Mr. Hui, your real-name ticket for the trip to Lijiang Ancient City in November last year has won a grand prize of 200,000 yuan! Please bring your ticket stub to Lijiang before March 6th to claim your prize!" On February 16th, Mr. Hui Jinbie, who works in Chen仓 District of Baoji City, received such a phone call. After multiple verifications, it was confirmed that this event was true. However, "the guide never gave me the ticket," as the deadline for claiming the prize approaches, Mr. Hui is extremely anxious.

Tourist: Learned About the Prize Only After Receiving the Call

"I didn't believe it at first, but after contacting the Lijiang Ancient City Protection Administration Bureau by phone, I found out that it was indeed true! But they told me that I must have the ancient city maintenance ticket stub to claim the prize. I was immediately stunned: I had no idea about the maintenance fee tickets or the lottery, and the guide didn't give us the ticket stubs."

According to Mr. Hui's introduction, on November 3rd of last year, he joined a tour group from a travel agency in Baoji to visit Kunming, Dali, Lijiang, and other places. The contract clearly stated: the tour fee includes the entrance fees for scenic spots, ancient city maintenance fees, etc. On November 6th, after arriving in Lijiang, the local "Sunshine Holiday Travel Agency" took over and was fully responsible for the Lijiang itinerary.

"I participated in a tour group, so I didn't pay much attention to the ticket matters, which were all organized by the guide. Since we didn't know about the lottery, we didn't ask the guide for the tickets." Mr. Hui Jinbie said. When the reporter contacted several other tourists in the tour group, they also said they hadn't seen the ticket stubs.

Voucher: Both Tickets and ID Are Necessary

On February 27th, Hui Jinbie set off again for Lijiang. Guide Liu Zheng'an, who was responsible for receiving the tour group in Lijiang, claimed that all tickets had been handed over to the tourists. His travel agency also stated that they would never withhold tourists' tickets. Hui Jinbie went to the Lijiang Ancient City Protection Administration Bureau and found his winning ticket stub. However, the staff informed him that both the purchase ticket and personal ID are required to claim the prize; neither can be missing.

The person in charge of the travel agency in Baoji said, "After the tour group arrived in Lijiang, it was handed over to the local travel agency and is no longer related to us. Since Mr. Hui registered with our group, considering this, I personally accompanied him to Lijiang to verify the matter, hoping to resolve it as soon as possible."

Hui Jinbie said, "Winning the prize should be a happy thing, but now I am extremely disheartened. I don't understand, since the tickets are under a real-name system and the Lijiang Ancient City Protection Administration Bureau called to notify me of the prize, I hope relevant departments can understand my actual situation."

Claiming the Prize: Only 3 Out of 10 People Have Claimed Their Prizes

On March 2nd, the reporter contacted guide Liu Zheng'an, who also had a belly full of grievances, "The tickets are under a real-name system. After verifying, I let the tourists take their tickets back. Keeping those tickets myself would be useless anyway."

Director He Shiyong of the Lijiang Ancient City Protection Administration Bureau said, "The opportunity to win a big prize by paying the Lijiang Ancient City maintenance fee has been reported on the official website and major media outlets in Yunnan Province. The rules for claiming prizes are very clear. Since Mr. Hui does not meet the redemption conditions, we cannot process the prize collection for him. Currently, both the travel agency and the tourists insist that they did not take the winning ticket, which is a matter between the travel agency and the tourists, and we cannot intervene."

Media reports indicate that the regular lottery for ancient city maintenance fee tickets is a precedent set by Lijiang domestically, aimed at minimizing the possibility of tourists evading the ancient city maintenance fee. It is understood that 10 lucky tourists were drawn nationwide, and currently only 3 people have claimed their prizes, while others have also said they cannot find their ticket stubs.

Lawyer: Legal Procedures Can Be Pursued

Attorney Fang Ligang from Shaanxi Ligang Law Firm said, first, after tourists pay the corresponding travel fees, they should receive corresponding tourism services, including the right to obtain tickets. The travel company should also provide evidence of delivering the tickets; secondly, since the tickets are under a real-name system and the staff have called to notify the winners to claim their prizes, this proves that Mr. Hui's winning information is genuine. "Within the effective redemption period, if Mr. Hui pursues legal action, there is still a good chance of obtaining the prize."

Note for Lijiang Tours: Starting January 2012, Lijiang City decided that lottery events for ancient city maintenance fee tickets will be held annually on January 6th and July 6th. On January 6th, the organizer of the Lijiang Ancient City maintenance fee ticket lottery drew 10 winners from 1,991,584 ticket numbers, each winner receiving 200,000 yuan in cash, with the deadline for claiming the prize being March 6th.
