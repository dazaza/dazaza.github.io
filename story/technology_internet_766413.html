A shocking translation error appears on Apple's Chinese official website.

by anonymous on 2013-09-12 12:03:14

Just like art, IT has also transcended national boundaries and races, but obviously, language remains a difficult gap to bridge. In recent years, many English-language products have encountered translation issues when being localized, some of which have even led to embarrassing mistakes.

Today, Apple released the iPhone 5s and iPhone 5c. After enduring widespread criticism regarding price and creativity, Apple was once again caught by eagle-eyed netizens for a small issue—this time, it was a translation problem on its official website.

On the "Buy iPhone" page of Apple's online store, a line of large text made people think they were seeing double: "The new iPhone is now available. The new iPhone is also now available." While we all know that these refer to the iPhone 5s and iPhone 5c respectively, the phrasing still leaves an awkward feeling, prompting one to ask, "Was your Chinese teacher actually your math teacher?"

According to past conventions, the content on Apple's Chinese official website is translated from the English version. So, I found the English page, which reads, "Introducing the new iPhone. And the new iPhone."

By now, you probably understand where the problem lies—it’s in the translation. The phrasing on Apple's English official website conforms to the linguistic habits of native speakers, with no grammatical errors and nothing inappropriate about it. It even has a bit of a literary flair, much like what Lu Xun wrote in "Autumn Night": "There are two trees outside the wall; one is a jujube tree, and the other is also a jujube tree."

Looking at Apple's Chinese official website, "The new iPhone is now available. The new iPhone is also now available." Such a bizarre translation could either be due to irresponsible machine translation or an attempt to mimic the original style without success.

On Weibo, there are already numerous netizens mocking this issue. I originally wanted to ridicule it as well, but upon further thought, if one really needs to strictly adhere to "Introducing the new iPhone. And the new iPhone." while translating it creatively, it indeed isn't an easy task. Dear readers, you are always quick-witted. Do you have any good suggestions for Apple China this time?
