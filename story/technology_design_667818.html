Historical Introduction: In 1758, councilman Johann Bernhard Berentzen founded the I.B. Berentzen distillery in the small German town of Haseluenne. At that time, in this town with less than a thousand people, there were already 25 similar small schnapps workshops.

More than 250 years have passed, and Hundred People City has now developed into one of the largest distilleries in Germany and Europe, producing and selling numerous famous brands. Its main products include the Hundred People City fruit liqueur series, Hundred People City milk liqueur series, Pushkin Vodka series, German spirits series, and more than 30 other well-known brands with over 150 varieties. However, the company headquarters remains in that ancient and charming small town.

In 1976, the release of Hundred People City Apple Liqueur (Berentzen Apfel) brought a historic breakthrough to the company, making the Berentzen brand renowned not only in Germany but also worldwide. Today, this apple liqueur is still one of the most popular wines in the Berentzen series.

In 1993, the grand launch of Hundred People City Sour Apple Liqueur (Berentzen SaurerApfel) marked another milestone in the development of the company's brand.

In 1995, the company produced Germany's first seasonal spirit, Winter Apple Liqueur (Berentzen WinterApfel). For the first time, people could enjoy winter apple liqueur with spices heated like traditional German mulled wine (Gluehwein) during the cold yet beautiful winter.

From 1995 to 2005, some popular products such as plum liqueur, wild cherry liqueur, and forest berry liqueur were also successively added to the Berentzen series. Meanwhile, the Berentzen mini-series was launched for the first time, becoming the first strong alcohol packaged in miniature bottles.

In 2007, the release of Hundred People City Red Apple Liqueur (Berentzen RoterApfel) continued the legend of the Berentzen apple liqueur series.

Also in 2007, the Hundred People City milk liqueur series, including Berentzen Milk-Coffee and Berentzen Caramel, quickly became popular across the European continent upon their release.

Looking back at history, Hundred People City products have always been synonymous with nature, high quality, and the joy of life.

Today, Hundred People City employees continue to work hard to develop new products and research new production processes. A sip of Berentzen brings a sweet and refreshing pleasure, akin to taking a crisp bite out of a summer fruit, aromatic and rich, leaving an endless aftertaste.
