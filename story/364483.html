My sister and my husband have a spark, what should I do

by rfwerferwf on 2010-09-14 17:39:25

For more than ten years, I've been giving in to my younger sister, and in the end, even my husband is going to be "given" to her? Originally, this was something hard to talk about. A week has passed, I haven't gone to work, staying at home all day without saying a word. Now, I'm not as heartbroken as before.

I just can't understand why my own sister would do such a thing and destroy my family? Why did the man who once vowed to love me and take care of me for life have a new lover after three years of marriage, and it turned out to be my sister?

That day I called mom, I really wanted to know if I am her biological child or if my sister isn't her biological child? Otherwise, I can't figure out why it would be my own sister?

I am the eldest in the family, my sister is five years younger than me. Since childhood, mom has taught me that as the eldest, I should give in to my sister and take good care of her. From childhood to now, she has never worn my old clothes, and any snacks or fruits at home were always given to her first.

Of course, I didn't like eating snacks anyway. In high school, my scores exceeded the key adjustment line, but I still went to an ordinary high school, while my sister spent over 30,000 yuan to get into a key middle school. Later, when I went to university, I only got into a second-tier school, whereas my sister barely made it into a top one. After graduating from university, I started working, my sister was still in university, I thought our family's financial situation wasn't good, so I decided to help my parents by supporting her through university.

Because I was poor when I studied, I still felt a bit inferior, so when my sister entered university, I tried my best to satisfy her materially. The phone I use until now is the oldest model of Nokia, with a blue screen. But the phones I bought for her cost over two thousand yuan each. In university, I bought her a computer, a camera, and clothes. Every time I saw her dressed beautifully, I also felt happy.

I think at least she doesn't have to suffer like I did when I was studying, nor feel as inferior. Although my sister received more attention and affection than me, there was one thing she often complained about: why are we from the same mother, yet I am so pretty while she looks plain? I admit, I am much prettier than my sister.

I look very much like my father, inheriting his thick eyebrows, big eyes, and oval face, and I am taller than her. My sister inherited from mom, single eyelids, round face, and is only 157 cm tall. Actually, we always thought she was very cute. Because the family conditions improved after she was born, she grew up fatter than me, but these past few years, after losing weight herself, her figure became slim too. It was this innate advantage that made her feel inferior to me for over twenty years.

Sometimes life is really so dramatic. I never imagined that my own sister would treat me like this. But now everything has happened. Looking at the album on the table, it was the one my husband gave me when he proposed. There are many photos inside taken when we went out together, along with heartfelt words he wrote to me, and of course, the proposal itself.

At that moment, moved by his sincerity, I accepted him. My sister was present then, and my husband even said that giving me the album was her idea. Why did the cute sister who once helped plan my husband's proposal now personally destroy my marriage? I really can't accept it.

Recently, mom came with my sister to see me, bringing some food. I ignored them and closed the door. They knocked on the door for a long time, but I didn't respond, so they left. After they left, I cried alone in the room until now. I feel so painful and helpless. I remembered the unpleasant scene again! When I saw my own husband being intimate with my sister on my bed, I couldn't believe it was true, it felt like my heart was being hollowed out.

My husband and I met after I started working. My husband works in bridge design, earns a high salary, looks very handsome, and most importantly, is very humorous, which is what I like the most. Of course, my sister also liked this brother-in-law back then. Now I understand, she used to frequently say to me: Sister, your husband is so good, why don't you give him to me? I always believed she was joking around as a kid. Joking between sisters like this should be normal, right?

A few months ago, it was our third wedding anniversary, and he bought me a platinum necklace. As soon as my sister saw it, she said she liked it and asked me to give it to her as a birthday gift, saying that many of her classmates had real gold jewelry. Actually, she knew that it was bought by that despicable man for me, and she deliberately asked me for it.

At the time, I didn't suspect anything, thinking it was just the vanity of a young girl. So I said I would buy her one when she turns 24 next year. The next day, she wore a necklace identical to mine around her neck, claiming she bought it with her saved pocket money. I even laughed at her for saving so much pocket money just to imitate her sister. I work as a nurse in the hospital, often working night shifts. My husband is busy during the day and frequently goes on business trips to other places, so we rarely spend time together.

My sister is in her senior year of college, basically having no classes, so whether or not she goes to school doesn't matter. Initially, I had suspicions after seeing a text message on his phone by accident. The next day, my husband was going to Hangzhou on a business trip. That text message read: "Make sure to bring me tomorrow." At that moment, I began to doubt.

I set his phone to save sent messages and quietly memorized that phone number. The next morning, when my husband went to wash his face, I saw that he had sent a message to that number late at night: "It's still not quite right, it could easily be discovered." At that moment, I felt blocked in my chest, knowing that my husband was having an affair. If I had known the outcome would be this, I would rather have remained ignorant forever, deceived by them forever. But at that time, I didn't know, that number was unfamiliar to me.

I can't tolerate sand in my eyes. More than a week after the man returned from Hangzhou, one evening I was going to work the night shift. Before leaving, my husband said his phone was out of power and asked me to charge it for him. I went into the room and, using the last bit of battery, sent a text message to that number: "Come tonight, I miss you." "The phone is out of power, come directly, she is on night duty." Then I turned off the phone and charged it, and then I went to the hospital as usual.

As a result, I witnessed such an embarrassing scene. It turned out to be my own sister, the one I have always given in to, spoiled, and supported. I was furious and immediately rushed to the kitchen to grab a knife, crazily running to the room, wanting to kill both of them! Seeing my demeanor, the despicable man quickly rushed forward to grab the knife from me. I desperately slashed at him with the knife, and eventually, he managed to take the knife away from me, but his arm was cut by me, blood gushing out. Then I ran to my sister and slapped her repeatedly, she didn't resist or make a sound. With such a ruthless and heavy blow, who should I blame?
