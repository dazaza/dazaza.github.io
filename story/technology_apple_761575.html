US Department of Defense to Issue Security Clearance to Apple and Samsung Devices

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 19:05:13

May 2, Beijing Time -- According to sources on Wednesday, the US Department of Defense will grant two separate security approvals in the coming weeks for Samsung Electronics' Galaxy series smartphones and Apple's iPhones and iPads equipped with the latest iOS operating system. This move is expected to increase the number of US government agencies authorized to use these devices.Apple and Samsung Electronics have been striving to secure orders from US defense agencies, which are also among the most security-conscious clients from the government sector. The size of this market is relatively small and still dominated by BlackBerry, so even after obtaining security approval from the US Department of Defense, it would be difficult for Apple and Samsung Electronics to quickly convert it into large orders or new customers.The security clearance from the US Department of Defense is regarded as the gold standard in the communications security industry. Passing the review by the US Department of Defense allows other security-conscious consumers, such as bankers and lawyers, to feel more comfortable using these devices.For Samsung Electronics, entering Washington represents the company's latest attempt to gain more business and government clients. Samsung has already hired several former RIM (BlackBerry's predecessor) executives and experts from security firms. Recently, Samsung established an internal department focused on enterprise business specifically to deal with Western governments.Samsung hosted the first meeting of a new government advisory board last month in London. The advisory board consists of Samsung executives and technical security experts from Western government agencies, including experts from the US National Security Council. The US National Security Council occasionally participates in industry organizations dedicated to developing products specifically for government agencies.During the two-day conference, BC Cho, head of Samsung’s enterprise division, stated that considering the shipment volume of their smartphones, it was somewhat embarrassing that their smartphones were not being used by government agencies. Representatives from the UK National Technical Information Security Assurance Bureau, as well as government officials from Canada, Australia, France, and Germany also attended the meeting. Samsung informed the attendees that the company plans to launch enhanced versions of the Galaxy S4 smartphone. As previously reported by The Wall Street Journal, a device temporarily named Galaxy S4 Active will be waterproof and dustproof. Such devices will attract consumers who take their phones climbing mountains or participating in kayaking activities, but they will also meet the needs of government personnel in special positions.Although the main purpose of Samsung holding this meeting was to give the company an opportunity to secure government contracts, the company also openly admitted its greatest ambition. Young Lee, Executive Vice President and head of Samsung's European operations, said that while Samsung understands that compared to the consumer market, this meeting may not bring significant government procurement orders, orders from other institutions will follow. Meeting notes show that Young Lee had mentioned at the time, "Once we obtain government certification, we will be able to convince other users more easily, such as banks, large enterprises, etc."A senior Samsung executive joked near the end of the meeting that once the company's new device receives government approval, the US National Security Agency might allow President Obama to use Samsung's equipment. Obama is also a famous BlackBerry phone and iPad tablet user.According to sources, the US Defense Information Systems Agency will determine that Samsung's Galaxy series smartphones and Apple's latest operating system, iOS 6, meet different security requirements. This will allow the military to use iPhones and iPads for non-classified communications, such as sending emails and browsing the web. Samsung and Apple must pass through different approval processes because Samsung's devices run on Google's Android operating system, which is open-source and thus more susceptible to security attacks. In contrast, Apple's operating system is considered to be more secure.In the meantime, BlackBerry is also awaiting the security inspection of its latest devices, the Z10 and Q10, by the US Department of Defense, as both products use BlackBerry's latest BlackBerry 10 operating system. A BlackBerry spokesperson said that the company is closely cooperating with the US Department of Defense and the US Defense Information Systems Agency to ensure swift passage of the security check.
