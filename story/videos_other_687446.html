Principle of electronic dog

by ckqskj007 on 2012-03-02 16:02:13

Industry Technician Exposes the Inside Story of "Electronic Dog Principle"! Electronic Dogs: There are two types of electronic dogs - broad-sense and narrow-sense electronic dogs.

1. Narrow-sense electronic dog refers to early anti-speed detection products, which are still sold in a certain quantity on the market today. They can only play a limited role in some areas. The electronic dog is essentially just a wireless signal receiver, simpler in principle and cost than a radio. On the market, electronic dog navigation devices sell for about 200 RMB, yet they cannot warn against mobile photography at all. In fact, the ground-laid S-coil used by many countries does not emit signals. Usually, manufacturers will pre-bury transmitters 300 to 1000 meters before fixed cameras. If there is a voice warning device installed on the vehicle, it can receive warning signals. Therefore, some merchants must specify where these electronic dog products are applicable when selling them.

The drawbacks of this product are quite apparent. First, whether or not the camera is actually working, the signal transmitter will send out a signal, leading to false alarms for drivers. Second, due to changes in roads and fierce commercial competition, manufacturers often sabotage each other's transmitters, or the transmitter batteries run out, causing the warning device to fail completely, resulting in significant losses for consumers. Therefore, one should be particularly cautious about such products. Additionally, in our country, the use of radio frequencies also requires approval from the Radio Committee. However, the P frequency has not been approved, so it can be detected by the Radio Committee's detection vehicle and can be shut down or interfered with at any time.

2. Broad-sense electronic dog refers to what many people habitually call all anti-speed detection products as electronic dogs. These products are now generally called anti-speed radars, with product packaging labeled as safe driving instruments or radar warning devices. After studying the current anti-speed radars, Zhongguanbao categorizes them into four types based on their functions:

(1) Full-frequency anti-speed radar

Full-frequency anti-speed radar is a type of car equipment that emerged with the widespread use of radar speed detectors. Radar speed detectors calculate the movement speed of an object based on the frequency shift of received reflected waves. Currently, radar speed detectors are widely used in urban traffic, mainly to measure whether cars are speeding, either fixed (installed on bridges or intersections) or mobile (generally installed on patrol vehicles).

Full-frequency anti-speed radar is a device that detects radar speed detectors. Installed inside a car, it can detect radar speed detectors within a certain distance while driving. When the car approaches a radar speed detector, the anti-speed radar will issue a warning, giving the driver 2-3 seconds to slow down within a range of 100-400 meters. Full-frequency can receive all radar signals across all bands (earlier products no longer need classification, and manufacturers no longer produce them). Better products include Conqueror 206, Antarctic Star 5500, Antarctic Star X3 (6200), etc. These products are effective against all radars, responding sensitively on highways, usually forecasting 500 meters to 2 kilometers ahead. This product cannot know its own speed, so it cannot remain silent when not speeding, and it cannot distinguish direction, sounding wherever there is a radar, thus being overly disturbing to residents. Moreover, currently, most cities use underground speed sensing coils to detect speeding, rendering radar detectors completely ineffective (only relying on manufacturers installing radar transmitters near speed check points to alert, but this practice is illegal and difficult to maintain), hence the emergence of the second and third types of products we will discuss.

(2) GPS anti-speed radar

This type of anti-speed radar does not rely on receiving radar waves to warn of speeding. The working principle of GPS function is as follows: it stores the latitude and longitude data of fixed speed check points or red-light cameras (which can be updated and users can also create their own coordinates). Through GPS positioning, when approaching a target point at a predetermined distance, it triggers an alarm. Due to the availability of latitude and longitude data for underground speed sensing coils, it becomes more reliable for fixed speed checks. Combined with an electronic compass, it can calculate and display the vehicle's speed and direction of travel, eliminating the problem of inability to determine direction. It can automatically remain silent when not speeding. These products also contain a large amount of traffic information and life information, with voice prompt functions, making them good assistants for driving. More famous ones include Conqueror 101, Prophet X-611, Pathfinder 303, Antarctic Star MINI, etc. However, these products do not work for mobile speed checks.

(3) GPS full-frequency anti-speed radar

GPS full-frequency anti-speed radar combines the functions of the first two types of anti-speed radars, accurately forecasting fixed radar speed detectors and also forecasting mobile speed checks, comprehensively addressing the shortcomings of the first two types of products. This type of anti-speed radar can be divided into two kinds: one is integrated style, which is relatively less common now. Representative products include Prophet X-605, Conqueror 968, Antarctic Star M3. The other is more commonly seen as separate style. With the mentality of discovering earlier and preparing more, it enhances the radar detection external machine (for example, the enhanced external machine of Antarctic Star improves measurement distance by over 600% compared to traditional radars). The advantage of this product is increasing the warning distance, preventing friends who cannot operate the anti-speed radar well from braking too abruptly and causing rear-end collisions. However, installation is slightly more complicated, but it is not a big problem. Reading the manual should clarify things, or if you don't want to do it yourself, finding a repairman won't cost much. Representative products include Conqueror 210, Antarctic Star 388, Conqueror 528/588, Antarctic Star Series 9, etc.

(4) Bluetooth GPS full-frequency anti-speed radar

This type of product mainly appears in the Conqueror and Antarctic Star series and can be upgraded into a satellite navigation system: connecting to a Bluetooth-enabled phone, PDA, laptop, and navigation electronic maps can upgrade it into a GPS satellite navigation system. Zhongguanbao believes that this product can only be considered a direction; it cannot yet be regarded as a mature product because this GPS navigation connection is relatively professional. Of course, some friends with Bluetooth phones, PDAs, laptops can find free navigation software to use, which should still reflect the value of this product.
