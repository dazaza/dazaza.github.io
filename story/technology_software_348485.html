Exclusive Interview with ZTE President Shi Lirong: Unite the Team to Pursue New Goals

by tfseo8888 on 2010-04-19 20:07:43

New President of ZTE Corporation, Shi Lirong: In a recent interview with Sina Tech, the newly appointed president of ZTE Corporation, Shi Lirong, expressed that the corporate governance structure of ZTE is transparent, and the management team is very united. Facing many opportunities in the market, ZTE has set a new goal to become an excellent global enterprise during his term, and he will spare no effort to achieve this.

Shi Lirong made his first appearance in his new role. As one of China's two major telecom equipment suppliers capable of competing with global telecom giants, the recent appointment of Shi Lirong as the new president of ZTE has drawn significant attention within the industry, raising questions about where he will lead ZTE.

On the afternoon of April 9, Shi Lirong appeared in front of the media in a dark suit at ZTE's 2009 performance announcement, sharing some insights on the global communications market and ZTE's future development with Sina Tech.

"2009 was a year of breakthrough against the trend for ZTE. During the reporting period, ZTE firmly seized rare market opportunities, achieving synchronized growth in scale and profitability," Shi Lirong reviewed ZTE's 2009 performance, considering it quite satisfactory. He indicated that the large-scale launch of domestic 3G construction and breakthroughs in the European market significantly contributed to the growth of revenue from operator networks. The increase in terminal business revenue was mainly due to successes in the European, Latin American, and domestic terminal markets. Additionally, he believed that in international markets, ZTE overcame the impact of the financial crisis, achieving continuous growth in international market performance despite the ongoing weakness in the global telecommunications market last year, thanks to its advantages in cost, technology, and comprehensive capital.

International and domestic markets growing simultaneously

The industry is more concerned about ZTE's direction and measures under Shi Lirong's leadership in 2010. On this, Shi Lirong stated, "In the new year, ZTE will increase efforts to expand overseas markets while continuing to consolidate the domestic market, achieving simultaneous growth domestically and internationally in 2010."

He particularly elaborated on the international market. In 2009, ZTE's international market achieved operating revenues of 29.869 billion yuan RMB, increasing by 11.34%, accounting for 49.56% of overall operating revenue. However, Shi Lirong said, "ZTE still has great opportunities in overseas markets. We have calculated that our share is still relatively small, leaving room for growth, so I am confident in the high-speed development of the company's overseas markets."

Regarding ZTE's advantages in overseas markets, Shi Lirong believed that research and development and technological advantages are crucial.

He said, "Due to continuous investment, we have formed significant advantages in research and development and technology. For example, in customized solutions for operators, some overseas equipment providers are unwilling to do it, but ZTE is willing to help operators. Additionally, in terms of cost, ZTE has always adhered to cost control; in service, ZTE can actively respond to operator needs, helping them reduce operation and maintenance costs, such as energy consumption. ZTE has been working hard on developing energy-saving equipment, making telecommunications equipment more integrated, which helps operators save land use significantly. This precisely meets the demand in developed countries for smaller, energy-efficient telecommunication equipment due to their limited space and expensive housing rents."

For a long time, ZTE has emphasized not solely pursuing maximum scale overseas but rather controlling the balance between cash recovery risks and market expansion first. This is considered one of the reasons for ZTE's relatively stable growth in overseas markets. Regarding this, Shi Lirong said that in the future, while strengthening expansion in overseas markets, they will still balance the relationship between market scale, profit margin, and market aspects in terms of cash flow.

Continuing to consolidate domestic market advantages

For the domestic market, ZTE grew rapidly in 2009. In 2009, ZTE's domestic market realized operating revenues of 30.404 billion yuan RMB, increasing by as high as 74.07%. Since the three domestic operators recently mentioned in their annual reports that capital expenditures might decrease in 2010, the industry worries that ZTE's domestic market performance may decline accordingly, but Shi Lirong believes there is still space in the domestic market.

He indicated that the domestic operator market mainly sees a reduction in equipment investment by China Unicom and China Telecom, while China Mobile saw little reduction. However, in terminal procurement, both China Unicom and China Telecom have increased.

He told Sina Tech, "In 2010, we will continue our past advantages, maintain market share, and seize the mobile phone market opportunity domestically. Meanwhile, transmission equipment accompanying 3G will also increase significantly. Additionally, there is a large demand for broadband fixed network equipment. Therefore, we believe we will continue to grow in the domestic market."

He also revealed that ZTE has prepared for the bidding of China Mobile's TD fourth-phase network construction equipment and is confident in achieving good results in the fourth phase of TD.

Discussing the goals of the new team

When talking about his feelings after taking office as president, Shi Lirong said, "ZTE's governance structure is transparent, and the management team has worked together for many years. Everyone is very united. My feeling is that every member of the team works hard in their respective areas."

"There are many opportunities in the market, and shareholders, stockholders, and employees have high expectations. The responsibility is heavy, and I will adhere to my duties, spare no effort, lead the management team well, and we have proposed the next strategic development of the company, aiming to become an excellent global enterprise by 2015. For this, we will go all out," Shi Lirong concluded. (Kang Zhao)
