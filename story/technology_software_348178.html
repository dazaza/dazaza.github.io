Enter the market! This sector has a "money" unlimited future.

by huaxun880 on 2010-04-19 14:57:35

Results speak for themselves! Ping An of China's net profit soared by 786%, significantly exceeding market expectations. Data shows that, under the complex circumstances in 2009, Ping An of China's three major businesses—insurance, banking, and investment—all achieved continuous, rapid, and healthy development. The construction of a comprehensive financial platform showed good momentum, and the synergistic effects of cross-selling became increasingly apparent. Reports indicate that in 2009, Ping An of China realized a net profit of 14.5 billion RMB, surging by 786% compared to the previous year, clearly surpassing market expectations. Judging from today's trading floor situation, Ping An of China might lead the insurance sector to become the vanguard of the next wave of market trends.

From a fundamental perspective, the insurance industry is about to enter a phase of rapid growth. In 2010, the insurance industry will transition from "adjusting structure" to "promoting growth." It will move away from a crude model characterized by one-sided pursuit of scale and speed, as well as low-technology content expansion through setting up institutions and employing large numbers of salespeople. Instead, it will gradually shift towards a more intensive model focused on quality and efficiency, and an internal development approach centered on modern information technology, capital density, and the aggregation of human capital. In 2010, China's insurance industry will re-enter a period of rapid growth. The structural adjustment of the life insurance industry will conclude, and premiums will resume rapid growth. Benefiting from national policies promoting automobile consumption, property insurance premiums will continue to grow at a high rate. It is expected that the overall growth rate of the insurance industry in 2010 will reach 15%. Bond yields will gradually rise, and equity investment returns will remain high. Over the next ten years, the annual average growth rate of premium income is expected to reach 20%.

At the same time, there is room for profit improvement in the insurance industry. The primary profit model of the insurance industry involves achieving stable growth in long-term investment assets through premium revenue and using the obtained investment returns to offset funding costs, ultimately generating profits. First, the investment environment in 2010 is still favorable, with bond yields gradually increasing due to rising interest rate expectations. The domestic macroeconomic trend remains positive; under a monetary policy that is neutral with a slight bias towards looseness, corporate profits have further room for improvement, and the upward momentum of the A-share market is relatively strong. Additionally, with channel expansion and improved investment environment, insurance companies' investment return rates will further increase. Meanwhile, in the stage of rising interest rates, the pressure on rising funding costs is small, often lagging behind the pace of rising investment return rates. Therefore, insurance companies are expected to maintain better profitability over the next period. It is estimated that the equity return rate of Ping An of China in 2010 will reach 19%, the equity return rate of Pacific Insurance will reach 24%, and the equity return rate of China Life Insurance will reach 23%.

Furthermore, policy benefits will help the insurance industry achieve continued success. First, the implementation of the new reserve extraction method will result in a comprehensive increase in profits for life insurance companies. The new accounting standards will begin to be implemented on January 1, 2010. Although the new standards do not alter the company's fundamentals, they will change the timing distribution of profits, allowing profits to be realized earlier. This will influence company management to focus more on products with higher policy value through premium risk decomposition. Overall, although the new standards are not fully aligned with international accounting standards, the implementation of premium risk decomposition and optimal estimation of reserves will be more beneficial than detrimental to industry development. Second, the Shanghai deferred tax pilot program for pensions is likely to be implemented in 2010. The originally planned tax incentive pilot program for pensions in Shanghai this year may be delayed until 2010. Although its impact on the life insurance market in terms of premium scale is not significant, the positive signals conveyed by this policy remain a boon for the insurance industry. Third, the effects of the new Insurance Law will gradually take hold. The new Insurance Law was officially implemented on October 1, 2009. Two potential benefits for insurance companies could be gradually realized in 2010: firstly, detailed rules for real estate investments by insurance companies may be released; secondly, the abolition of the domestic priority reinsurance regulations means insurance companies may benefit from reinsurance pricing in the coming year.
