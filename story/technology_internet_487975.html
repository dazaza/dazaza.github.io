Qin Jijie thought, if he could open a school and gather these children together, take good care of them, provide them with good education, they will definitely make progress. "My original idea was to start a business in Beijing, earn some money, then go back to my hometown in Henan to open a school." Qin Jijie has worked as a photographer and distributed advertisements. In 2001, after passing the examination, he opened his first migrant workers' children school in Wali Township. Gradually, the number of students increased from the initial 23 people to two or three hundred, until five or six hundred people, and the school moved to Changping District in the suburbs. A few years later, it moved to Dong Sanqi Village north of Tian Tong Bei Yuan. "Now there are two campuses, one in Dongxiaokou Village and one in Beiqi Family. The campus in Beiqi Family is rented land, and I built the school myself."

Rent costs 200,000 yuan, office supplies cost 30,000 yuan, electricity costs 20,000 yuan. "The oil and maintenance costs of the two vehicles per semester are about 30,000 yuan, which is 60,000 yuan a year, plus about 20,000 yuan for insurance and vehicle inspection," Hu Ming said. The annual heating cost is about 40,000 yuan, and the school's daily maintenance and protection cost about 10,000 yuan annually.

As the new semester approaches, students from the closed migrant workers' children schools have gradually found new campuses.

Zhang Gezhen's school receives donations from society. He pointed at the desks and books and said: "These are all donated by society. They will provide equipment sponsorship. If there were no support, the school would have collapsed long ago. Many principals will use the surplus to improve teacher benefits and improve the school conditions." Zhang Gezhen took out the rental agreement and teachers' salary slips, "We don't mind being looked at, anyone can come and see."

Migrant workers' children schools have once again become a topic of discussion. Have migrant workers' children schools really become places for profiteering? What is the operating situation of these schools? Reporters visited three such schools and tried to restore the economic situation of the many migrant workers' children schools scattered across Beijing's urban and rural areas through examining their accounts.

Twelve years ago, Hu Ming opened the Mingxin School in Beijing. After several twists and turns, it finally settled in Dongxiaokou Village. "Our school is medium-sized, with about 700 people." Regarding the school's income and expenditure status, Hu Ming did not hesitate - among the expenditures, faculty and staff salaries, house rent, water and electricity are the three fixed major items.

Two schools can earn over ten thousand yuan, and the surplus is used to build school buildings.

Twelve years ago, Hu Ming opened the Mingxin School in Beijing. After several twists and turns, it finally settled in Dongxiaokou Village. "Our school is medium-sized, with about 700 people." Regarding the school's income and expenditure status, Hu Ming did not hesitate - among the expenses, faculty and staff salaries, house rent, water and electricity are the three fixed major items.

Two campuses have a total of 1,100 students, and this year the tuition fee increased from 570 yuan per semester to 720 yuan. Regarding the reason for the tuition increase, Qin Jijie explained: "Now prices, rents, and labor costs are all rising. If we don't raise the tuition, the school will be unable to survive." Qin Jijie told reporters that the parents of the students here first ask about the tuition when consulting about enrollment, not the teaching quality or the school curriculum, but rather how much it costs and whether they can pay less.

Before 1995, Zhang Gezhen came to Beijing as a carpenter with a simple goal of learning traditional Chinese medicine. In the situation where his own child couldn't attend school, he had the idea of establishing a school for the children of migrant workers, aiming to solve the problem of schooling difficulties for the children of migrant workers.

Qin Jijie said that the two schools have a total of 46 teachers and 10 staff members, with an average salary of 1,250 yuan, and they receive 11 months of salary annually, approximately 770,000 yuan. The annual heating cost is 70,000 yuan, water and electricity are 90,000 yuan, office expenses are 40,000 yuan, and maintenance is 20,000 yuan. "The surplus of the two schools is around 100,000 yuan. When there are more students, it will exceed 100,000 yuan, and when there are fewer students, it is around 80,000 yuan."

Previously, the tuition was 700 yuan per semester. After the Education Commission subsidy, the school directly transferred the subsidy to the tuition, charging 600 yuan per semester. Hu Ming wrote on paper, the total annual income is one million yuan, and the expenditure is 880,000 yuan. "The remaining 120,000 yuan is under ideal conditions where no unexpected situations occur. Occasionally, when a student gets injured, the school still needs to cover medical expenses. The annual inspections and reviews of the school are not included in the total account. In our school, any expense exceeding 1,000 yuan is considered a large expense."

Mingyuan School Scale 300 people

Mingyuan School currently has nearly 300 students, two years ago, the number of students here was over 500. "The mobility of students is very high, some have moved due to demolition, and others have gone to other schools." In the past 16 years, Zhang Gezhen has founded four schools for the children of migrant workers. Zhang Gezhen described the situation of these schools as "half is seawater, half is fire" — difficult to manage, among the four schools, some are losing money, while others have slight surpluses.

Qin Jijie combined the accounts of the two schools: the annual tuition income (excluding those who pay less or cannot pay) is approximately 1.5 million yuan. The rent for the two schools is 400,000 yuan.

Hu Ming said that the annual surplus of the school is about 70,000 yuan. A few days ago, Hu Ming received a call from a former principal of a school for the children of migrant workers. The caller told Hu Ming that he had found a job with a monthly salary of more than 6,000 yuan, and Hu Ming envied him greatly, "This amount of money is not less than running a school, and the key is that you don't have to worry or bear risks." Hu Ming said that more than 80% of the principals of schools for the children of migrant workers do not earn much money, "There are also some who earn more, and a few principals make money through book fees, etc. A few years ago, some principals also received a considerable compensation due to demolition."

Zhiquan School located in Dong Sanqi Village, Beiqi Town, Changping District, Beijing, is near several large recycled goods acquisition stations. It is a row of blue and white school buildings.

Zhang Gezhen calculated the accounts for the Mingyuan School in Haidian District. The school has 300 students, each paying 900 yuan per semester in tuition. "The annual tuition is 540,000 yuan, but in reality, we don't collect that much money." Mingyuan School has 20 faculty and staff members, with an average monthly salary of 1,500 yuan, and salaries are paid for 10 months a year, with an annual rent of 280,000 yuan. "The rent plus salaries add up to 580,000 yuan, already exceeding the amount of tuition collected. Plus water and electricity, repairs, and office expenses, these also amount to about 50,000 yuan, so it costs 350,000 to 400,000 yuan per semester to run a school for the children of migrant workers."

At Mingxin School, there are 28 teachers, a principal, an educational director, and two drivers. "This totals 32 people, with an average monthly salary of 1,500 yuan. No salaries are paid during winter and summer vacations, so there are only 10 months of salaries, totaling 480,000 yuan. Some teachers also receive subsidies, such as those accompanying the school bus to pick up and drop off children, making the total salary reach 500,000 yuan."

Hu Ming casually picked up a pen and a piece of blank paper, "The school's income is exceptionally single-source, just the students' tuition, 600 yuan per semester per person. Mingxin School has about 700 children, let's say 700, so the school's annual income is 840,000 yuan." Hu Ming subtracted 40,000 yuan from the 840,000 yuan income, "Some children cannot afford the tuition or cannot pay the full tuition, this roughly accounts for about 10%."

In Dongxiaokou Village, Changping District, beside the low civilian houses on the roadside, colorful plastic bags containing garbage emit foul odors intermittently. Nearby streets, Mingxin School hung a banner reading "Welcome Teachers and Students from Hongxing School to Work and Study at Mingxin." Hongxing School, a school for the children of migrant workers located in Xisanqi, Haidian District, was demolished earlier this month, and part of its students were diverted to Mingxin School. Principal Hu Ming stood alone smoking in the campus formed by two two-story simple buildings, "We have already accepted more than 200 students from Hongxing."

As for why he continues despite the losses, Zhang Gezhen said: "Someone has to do this, and ensure that these children have a place to study. Fortunately, not all four schools are losing money, and I can use the surplus from profitable schools to cover the losses of the unprofitable ones." (Reporter Zhao Xibin)

Schools running for over ten years with million-yuan assets? Principals of schools for the children of migrant workers reveal their accounts

Last year, Hu Ming started receiving an annual subsidy of about 200,000 yuan from the Education Commission for the students, and he also lowered the tuition. "A few years ago, the tuition was 350 yuan per semester, which was not enough to cover the school's daily expenses. There were also many people coming to the school to impose fines, either because something was not up to standard or another issue." Hu Ming often works odd jobs at construction sites with some fellow villagers from Henan Province, buying wood and scrap iron from the site and reselling them, "Each time, I earn a few thousand yuan, and a large portion goes towards the school. As far as I know, many principals of schools for the children of migrant workers have to engage in side businesses, otherwise, it would be impossible to ensure the normal operation of the school." Some additional expenses of the school, Hu Ming dare not fully record on the school's accounts, "Because we have a permit to operate the school, regulations require us to have a surplus. If we are in deficit, we will lose the permit for private schools, and the school will be closed."

Principal Qin Jijie of Zhiquan School was previously a middle school teacher in his hometown of Henan Province. Among his students were many "left-behind teenagers." Without their parents nearby, these children became extremely sensitive, and some went astray due to lack of care and management.

If fewer than 400 students enroll, the school will incur losses.

Last semester, the number of students at Mingyuan School in Haidian fell below 300. The school does not have a permit to operate and is not within the scope of subsidies from the Education Commission. "The reduction in numbers puts the school in a continuous state of deficit operations. Schools for the children of migrant workers will incur losses if there are fewer than 400 students, and will only have a surplus if there are more than 500 students."

Heading northeast from Anhe Bridge North Station on Subway Line 4, the traffic flow on the road is not heavy, and distant mountains connect. Moving eastward continuously, Mingyuan School for the children of migrant workers is located in a bungalow area. The school is surrounded by a cluster of low bungalows including breakfast stalls, bicycle repair shops, small bathhouses, and roadside pool tables. This year marks the 16th year since Principal Zhang Gezhen founded Mingyuan School.
