Taboos among friends, how much do you know?

by loveq7s0 on 2012-03-07 10:54:19

In your life, you will meet a lot of different people. Some of them will become your good friends or enemies, even foes. But the point is: have you ever thought about the reasons? Why do some relationships evolve into enmity, hostility or friendship? In fact, there are some small tips for you to consider, hoping they will be helpful for your friendship.

【Taboos of Friends】:

1. Don't think that you and your friend's belongings are interchangeable!

Tell you: wrong! Things must be clearly divided, otherwise, in the end, if something is broken, like a birthday gift [http://www.lppf.cn], you will feel awkward asking for compensation and end up accepting the loss. However, this will naturally form a kind of rejection in your heart.

2. Don't think that you and your friend are so close that someone will always drive or treat you wherever you go.

Tell you: wrong! Occasionally once or twice might be tolerable, but over time, anyone would find it unbearable. So before going out to play, it's best to agree beforehand that everyone splits the gas money and pays their own expenses, which not only makes everyone happy but also enhances the feeling between friends.

3. Don't think that you and your friend are so familiar that you can freely enter their kitchen, room, etc., even for birthday gifts [http://www.lppf.cn].

Tell you: wrong! The better the friends, the more mutual respect is needed because, after all, it's not your home. What gives you the right to freely enter someone else's territory? That kind of behavior will only make others feel that you don't respect them. If possible, avoid it. Don't think it's nothing; the other party may already be cursing you inside!

4. Don't think that since you and your friend have such a good relationship, everything can be casual, even when visiting their home, you don't need to care about etiquette.

Tell you: wrong! The better the friends, the less you can neglect etiquette. When visiting his house today, absolutely do not bring just two bananas! You must bring some gifts, even if it's just a bag of fruit. As the saying goes, "A light gift carries heavy feelings," and "The more polite one is, the fewer complaints." So never bring just "two bananas," remember!

5. Don't think that you and your friend can be so close that even going to the bathroom can be inseparable.

Tell you: wrong! Occasionally give the other person some space to let each other see the people, events, and things around them. When you come back, your vision will be broader! It's like living together every day. To put it bluntly, it's no different from being in jail. No matter how you look at it, it's just me looking at you, you looking at me, glaring at each other. Giving yourself and the other person more space will allow both of you to grow faster!

6. Don't think that you and your friend can imitate each other's favorite things.

Tell you: wrong! Everyone's aesthetic view is different. Imitating for too long will only make the other person lose interest! Because friends appreciate the things you like yourself, rather than imitating or learning what you like. It's precisely because you are different from yourself that he finds it fresh. Otherwise, if what you like is the same as what he likes, then why not just be friends with yourself? Why bother finding someone to match? Therefore, learn to appreciate the other person's preferences instead of imitating!

7. Don't think that you and your friend are so close that you can run away from home and hide at his place when in trouble.

Tell you: wrong! Perhaps he can help you for a while, but correspondingly, he also has to take responsibility for having you around. Over time, I think anyone would start thinking, "Why should I bother?" Having a friend brings trouble, and it will also create a sense of aversion in the heart! So, the better the friends, the more you need to learn to empathize with his feelings and difficulties! Bear your own troubles yourself, and don't bother others for a long time! As the saying goes, "Long illness has no filial children," which can also be changed to "Long annoyance has no true friends" —— mutual encouragement.
