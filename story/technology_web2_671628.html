Driver mistakenly stepped on the gas instead of the brake, crashing into a utility pole (Picture)

by aiipii12987 on 2012-02-22 11:13:06

The Broken Telegraph Pole

At around 9:30 this morning, at the intersection of Anqing Road and Huancheng West Road, after a Ford car hit an electric bike in the middle, the driver momentarily panicked and mistakenly stepped on the accelerator instead of the brake, pushing the electric bike forward for four or five meters. It finally stopped after breaking a telegraph pole by the roadside.

The reporter saw at the scene that the silver Ford car was parked diagonally on the sidewalk, with its airbag fully deployed. The steam from the leaking radiator enveloped the front of the car. A five or six-meter-long telegraph pole was broken in half, and large pieces of branches fell off, narrowly missing the car before landing by the side of the sidewalk.

About four or five meters away from the front of the Ford car, a yellow electric bike was crushed under the fallen telegraph pole, unable to move at all. All over the ground nearby were plastic fragments from the electric bike.

The two parties involved, still not fully recovered from the shock of the accident, stood by the roadside continuously negotiating something. According to the electric bike rider, at the time of the incident, he was riding north along Huancheng West Road, while the Ford car was turning from Anqing Road onto Huancheng West Road. "The speed was a bit fast, I was just about to change lanes when suddenly it collided," said the electric bike rider. At the moment of impact, he was shaken off his vehicle and rolled to the side, which is how he avoided injury. "After the collision, I clearly felt the speed increase, pushing my electric bike forward for four or five meters, then hitting the telegraph pole before stopping," the electric bike rider looked at his unrecognizable beloved vehicle with a sense of having survived a disaster.

The driver of the Ford car was a middle-aged woman who suffered a wound about twenty to thirty centimeters long on her right arm due to the strong inertia during the collision. "When the collision happened, I got nervous and didn't know where to step. I probably pressed the accelerator instead of the brake," because of the great shock, the driver could hardly recall the moment of the incident.
