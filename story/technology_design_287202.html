How to Choose the Right CRM Software: Shangyi CRM

by bwcrm864 on 2009-12-01 09:40:57

CRM (Customer Relationship Management) software is both an innovative management method and a more advanced management tool. For many enterprises, whether it's a new management method or an advanced management tool, as long as it can bring efficiency, they are willing to try it. In China, when we mention "CRM," it mostly refers to "an IT software tool that contains its own management methods and can help businesses practice more management methods." Although domestic CRM software only started at the end of 1999, it rapidly developed in 2001, 2002, and 2003. Today, many industries and companies have already taken action and are gaining increasing value from the implementation and application of CRM. Even so, we still regretfully see that a considerable number of companies have not gained more benefits from the actual application of CRM software. If we only look at CRM software products, there are actually quite a few practical, user-friendly, and mature CRM software products domestically. Some may ask, why do different companies have such drastically different application results? As the leading brand in China's CRM market, MyCRM has drawn the following conclusions based on its five years of CRM industry experience and in-depth analysis of the domestic CRM market and application status. We would like to share these with you.

China has a famous saying: “A good beginning is half the battle,” and “Choosing the right CRM product is the core and key to successful CRM application.” So, how can one choose the right CRM product?

1. First, understand your own enterprise.

CRM software is a type of management software, essentially serving to help various levels of roles within an enterprise manage their business more effectively. Doctors treating patients emphasize "prescribing the right medicine for the illness," meaning they must first understand the patient's cause and condition of the disease before proposing and implementing a treatment plan. To effectively implement CRM and improve management efficiency, the principle is the same; companies need to clearly understand their own "causes" or "conditions." And based on this, choose and evaluate whether the corresponding CRM product is suitable.

Of course, not all companies and people can truly clearly understand the "cause" and "condition." In this sense, some management consulting companies have a reason to exist. They will use their rich knowledge and experience, professional teams, and methods to help companies gain clarity. However, Chinese companies, especially the vast majority of small and medium-sized enterprises, from their business model, management characteristics, and current management situation, are not very complex business models. Many management awareness and needs are still at a relatively basic stage. In this larger context, if companies want to choose more suitable CRM products, understanding themselves is not difficult. We can start from the following very practical aspects:

What is the company’s basic motivation for choosing and implementing CRM software? Is it to please investors and shareholders? Is it to increase sales performance and capability? Is it to train employees to establish customer-first awareness and habits? Or is it to improve corporate management level and efficiency? Etc.

What is the company’s "CRMization" phase? Is it in the unconscious stage? The primary stage? The intermediate stage? Or the advanced stage? Etc.

How much cost (including: CRM software price, implementation cycle, implementation scope and resources, etc.) can the company bear?

How strong is the company’s post-implementation promotion of CRM? Is it being promoted "top-down" by the absolute management high-levels? Is it a suggestion "bottom-up" from specific business departments? Or is there not much promotion effort, just work guidance for relevant staff?

There should be other factors and links, but the above four points are extremely important. Only when companies truly understand these series of issues and examine corresponding CRM vendors and products accordingly can they maximize the guarantee of ultimate application success.

2. Second, understand domestic CRM vendors and products.

The domestic CRM software market has been developing for five years. For CRM software products that heavily rely on knowledge accumulation, this period is still too short. Common problems with non-mainstream, non-professional domestic CRM vendors and products include insufficient application depth and breadth. Moreover, the competitive atmosphere in the current domestic CRM market is not too intense - because there are still many differences between vendors' target customer groups, product application features, and pricing strategies. Therefore, relative to the not-so-"hot" domestic CRM market, each vendor is quietly and intensively doing their work. On one hand, they continue to develop new products steadily; on the other hand, they effectively seize emerging sales opportunities and convert them into current sales orders.

Companies rationally purchasing CRM software, if they can understand the status of mainstream domestic CRM vendors and their products, can achieve twice the result with half the effort. We recommend evaluating vendors and products based on the following elements: R&D investment costs, product development cycle, product maturity, completeness of product functions and applications, technical architecture of the product, product stability, implementation experience and professionalism, after-sales service capabilities, future development potential of the company, and the company's current industry position.

3. Third, go through the correct selection process.

What kind of process is the correct selection process?

Business Wing CRM software has more than 300 enterprise users. Regarding how to effectively select CRM software, here are a few suggestions:

• Determine whether to purchase commercial software or custom-developed software;

• Based on the company's own characteristics and actual requirements, preliminarily determine multiple suitable CRM vendors;

• Prepare corresponding demand documents and materials, and communicate fully with representatives of each vendor;

• Evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of the products, solutions, quotations or quotation schemes, implementation capabilities, and implementation cycles provided by each CRM vendor according to the demands;

• Determine further shortlisted vendors;

• Evaluate the further plans and commitments proposed by each shortlisted vendor regarding "how to effectively avoid implementation risks?" and "how to effectively ensure application efficiency?";

• Finally determine the cooperative vendor and product.
