Kindergarten teacher sends wedding invitations to the whole class, parents claim they can't afford to offend her

by bill214rii on 2012-03-07 10:36:09

(Xiamen Evening News) Share to: Welcome to post comments.

Psychological counseling teacher Liu Pingchao from Hubin Middle School said that this matter should be viewed from two sides. "If the teacher invites little friends to attend the wedding and specifies that no gifts are to be received, it can be seen as a good interaction between teachers and students; if it is an opportunity to collect gifts from parents under the guise of marriage, then the teacher's actions are very inappropriate." Liu Pingchao said that children in kindergarten are generally under 6 years old, they do not yet understand the behavior of teachers, nor do they understand the meaning of gifts, so the teacher's actions will not have too much impact on the child's psychology.

Citizen Ms. Lin said that once the teacher comes for a home visit, the parents all make full preparations. "After the home visit, I prepared some cake coupons and shopping vouchers, which I handed over to the teacher, and they accepted them." Ms. Lin said that giving these things is not something the parents willingly do, but if they don't give them, they worry that the teacher might have an opinion, affecting the child.

Mr. Liu lives on Lianqian Road, his child is currently in the middle class of kindergarten, and both he and his wife work during the day. "At this age, children cannot take care of themselves, and what parents worry about most is whether their child eats well, sleeps well, and is taken care of at kindergarten," Mr. Liu said. "If we offend the teacher, the one who suffers will be the child."

A kindergarten teacher got married and actually sent out invitations to all the children in the class. Whether to go or not, the parents were embarrassed and dissatisfied, but most dared not say anything. Once the post was released, netizens unanimously condemned the teacher, saying she was "crazy for money."

Reporters randomly interviewed several parents, and the answers were consistent: kindergarten teachers are hard to offend.

Opinion: Collecting gifts under the guise of an event is absolutely unacceptable

The reporter interviewed Mr. Huang, the director of the Jiaoyu Affairs Service Center of Huli District, Xiamen. Mr. Huang said that this kind of incident is the first time he has heard of it. If the teacher and the parent have a private relationship, asking the child to bring the invitation home is fine, but if the teacher uses the teacher-student relationship to send invitations to parents, the nature changes, and it is absolutely unacceptable. The education department must investigate, order the teacher to return the gifts.

"Vine Little Winter Melon" said that her colleague's child is 5 years old and in the senior class of that kindergarten. All 30 plus classmates received such invitations. "Vine Little Winter Melon" told the reporter that her colleague is now most worried that this trend will spread throughout the kindergarten, and in the future, every teacher who gets married will send invitations to all the children. She said that her colleague is now very conflicted, not knowing whether to go or not.

Post: All classmates received the invitation

On the morning of February 2, netizen "Vine Little Winter Melon" posted that her colleague received an invitation, which was sent by the child's kindergarten. The invitation was written with the child's name, and the teacher also gave the children a pack of candy, instructing them to tell their parents.
