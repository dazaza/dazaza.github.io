How a gambling-bankrupt man can be more deceitful than a fraudster

by piaoc5641 on 2012-02-08 21:30:29

I am 23 years old, almost 24. I am of acceptable height and average looks (though quite a few people have complimented me on my beauty). Last March, I met a boy at my workplace. Since I was in sales at the time, he came to buy something from me. I didn't think much of it and greeted him with my usual enthusiasm. Perhaps he thought I was particularly warm and easy to talk to because in the end, he asked for my phone number. Normally, I wouldn't give out my number to strangers, but after hesitating for a moment, I ended up giving it to him. After he left, he started calling and texting me. I didn't pay much attention at first and ignored him because I considered it all rather boring, especially since we weren't familiar with each other. However, he kept sending messages and even asked why I hadn't replied. His "perseverance" moved me a bit. It seemed a bit pretentious to ignore someone who had gone so far, so I suggested we add each other on QQ and chat there (at the time, my job involved using a computer, so I could access the internet).

On QQ, he introduced himself: He was 28 at the time, had served in the military, and was quite accomplished. In 2005, he started his own business, which went well for a few years. In just two short years, he made several million yuan, handling everything with ease, owning both a house and a car. People from all walks of life flattered him. But in 2008, his friends took him to Macau for fun, where he gambled and lost tens of thousands of yuan in just a few hours. Feeling不服气 (unwilling to accept defeat), he continued gambling on his own and ended up losing everything overnight. There were also issues at his factory, and it felt like he fell from heaven to hell in that one incident. Later, he handed over his factory to a fellow soldier to manage while he "reflected in solitude" for nearly a year (apparently, he stayed in a hotel room, never leaving except when meals were delivered).
