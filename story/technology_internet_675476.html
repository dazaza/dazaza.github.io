The styling life cycle of general machine tool products is usually 5 to 10 years, while for electronic and daily-use mechanical products it is less than 2 to 3 years. Moreover, the speed at which new models replace old ones is accelerating. Especially in home appliances, often there are no significant changes made to the internal core components, but the products are re-launched with new designs. With the rapid development of technology and the continuous improvement of human cultural standards, the styling life cycle of modern industrial products is getting shorter and shorter. In such a high-speed product renewal competition, as one of the main links inseparable from modern product design - styling design, structural design, and mold design - traditionally follows a routine where design personnel from these three specialties and departments work together to complete the task of industrial product design. In product styling, color design is an important task that cannot be overlooked, and the choice of its tone is crucial. For a single machine, its main color will primarily consist of [two-tone] or [three-tone], breaking away from the past monotonous gray or "kaleidoscope" style. Conversely, if the product's color treatment is inappropriate, it not only affects the performance of the product, destroys the overall beauty of the product styling, but also easily disrupts people's working mood, causing feelings of boredom, depression, indifference, or even despair, distracting the operator and reducing work efficiency. Generally speaking, proportions can present various combinations as long as they do not violate the product function and material technical conditions, showcasing the quantitative change relationship between the whole and parts or parts and parts, such as size, thickness, length, etc. For example: the purpose and function of an airplane is to carry passengers and cargo flying high and fast in the air, so its main color is generally treated as highly bright silver white, making it easy for people to feel the lightness and precision of the plane. This is the consistency of form and color, and color assists the form.

Conversely, if the plane were painted in a dark gray tone, it would easily make people doubt whether it is too heavy to fly. This is color destroying the form, and the wrong choice of tone. A well-proportioned object not only looks beautiful but is also practical, giving people a comfortable and intimate feeling. For instance, the railings of bridges, the protective covers of machines, the carriages of cars, the seats of bicycles, and the barrels of fountain pens all first consider the scale of the shaped object, i.e., the adaptation of human body dimensions in terms of length, width, height, diameter, etc., followed by the proportion and detail adjustment of the shaped object. If the product color is handled well, it can coordinate or compensate for certain deficiencies in the styling, making it more splendid and perfect, easily winning the favor of consumers and achieving twice the result with half the effort. These factors basically include functionality, material technical conditions, and aesthetic trends. The proportion and scale of the shaped object should be reasonably arranged and coordinated based on various influencing factors. Correct proportions and scales are the foundation and framework of a perfect shape.

In the foreseeable future, a longer period will see simpler, more concise, more lively, stricter structures, more ingenious craftsmanship, and a pursuit of rational beauty as the main mark of mechanical product styling. The selection of tones should be particularly cautious and generally determined artistically based on the product's purpose, function, structure, timeliness, and user preferences. The standard for determination is the consistency of color and shape, using color to assist the form, and creating brilliance through the combination of form and color. Color is the first element to affect visual sensation in the entire product image, which could be described as "stunning". Tone refers to the overall color impression of an industrial product at a glance, which can express liveliness, activity, delicacy, solemnity, coldness, dullness, intimacy, or briskness, among others. Scale, on the other hand, is relatively fixed, specifically referring to the degree and range of adaptation between the dimensions of the shaped object and human body dimensions or some standard. In line type, straight lines or large radius circular arc surfaces with small rounded corners will be used more frequently. If the shape has good proportions but lacks correct fixed scales, the design will definitely fail. Therefore, the correct order of shaping design should first determine the scale, then adjust the proportion of the shaped object accordingly. In terms of color, light, bright high-brightness low-saturation cold and warm tones will be dominant. However, such a slow design process does not suit today's rapidly developing situation.
