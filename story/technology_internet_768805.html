Ma Yun Switches to the "Charles Zhang Model": Three Cards to Challenge WeChat

by anonymous on 2013-11-22 12:02:51

By Zhao Nan

Ma Yun is about to switch to the "Charles Zhang mode".

What does that mean?

Yesterday, Alibaba held a "one-month anniversary" event for Laiwang in Hangzhou. They invited three stars popularized by Hunan TV, an always controversial 80s crossover figure, and a hardworking headline-grabbing male singer embroiled in scandals.

Who are they? Kuai Male, Bai Jugang, Zhou Bichang, Du Haitao, Guo Jingming, and Wang Feng. And Ma Yun was there with them on stage.

With entertainment celebrities present, fans were naturally not absent. "Follow your idol on 'Laiwang'." The host Du Haitao got up N times, and the fans at the scene shouted N+1 times.

The discerning understood. This is Laiwang's entertainment marketing strategy. In today's world, the strongest Follow relationship is first the relationship between believers and their leader, and secondly, the relationship between fans and stars.

This is the first time Ma Yun has personally stood up for entertainment marketing. To attract young users to Laiwang, Ma Yun had to change tactics and adopt the "Charles Zhang mode," working tirelessly to entertain small stars, widely adding entertainment circle figures as Laiwang friends, all physical labor, not easy at all.

Ma Yun said, "Laiwang will not only have this 'one-month anniversary,' but also a 'hundred-day celebration,' a 'first birthday party'..." The unsaid part of this statement is that in the future, more stars will be invited to stand up for Laiwang. To find stars and create influence among young fans, Ma Yun will have to go out and meet them. At least for a considerable period of time, it must be like this.

The "Charles Zhang mode" seems to be the competitive strategy of the "number two" trying to catch up with the "number one," personally standing up to greet guests to gain C-end users. When Sohu competed with Sina in the portal era, Sina firmly held the advantage in sports information, so Charles Zhang frequently appeared in front of the camera to grab entertainment content. In an interview back then, Charles Zhang also said, my appearance was a decision by the board, which could reduce the company's publicity costs and increase Sohu's exposure. Later, when Charles Zhang wanted to emulate Sina's Weibo, he still used the same old trick of going out to meet guests and pull in stars.

In recent years, Jumei Youpin CEO Chen Ou has also played this model. Cosmetic e-commerce inherently has a semi-media attribute, and Letube founder Li Jing is a cross-border person in the entertainment and media circles. For Chen Ou to make his company memorable to young users, the lowest-cost way is to turn himself into someone in the entertainment industry. In an interview, Chen Ou also said, my idea of shooting advertisements came from my investor Xu Xiaoping, who told me to learn from Charles Zhang.

Whether it's Charles Zhang or Chen Ou learning from Charles Zhang, from their cases, we can see one point: a significant portion of today's user perception of a company or product's brand and culture comes from the influence of its founder. And the lighter and more agile the company or product, the younger the users, the more the founder needs to have an entertaining hue.

But Ma Yun is Ma Yun after all. Who did Ma Yun use to greet guests in the past? At the West Lake Summit, Ma Yun invited Clinton, Schwarzenegger, Jin Yong, Zhang Jizhong. If it wasn't high-end, if it wasn't bone-level, if it didn't have international influence. And now, it's changed to small stars catering to young tastes. Judging from this, Ma Yun is really in a hurry for "Laiwang."

Why is Ma Yun in such a hurry?

WeChat, with its massive public accounts (service accounts), is akin to Tmall or Taobao loaded with user relationships. Only one is on mobile devices, and the other originated on PCs. Fundamentally, both are about circling users, building platforms, and connecting merchants. Tmall or Taobao is where merchants directly open stores; WeChat is where each public account (service account) corresponds to a merchant.

Would Ma Yun let Tencent build another Tmall or Taobao on mobile phones? Now, WeChat has 500 million registered users, 270 million active users, 2 million public accounts, and 8,000 new ones added daily.

Even scarier is that although the battle for domestic e-commerce on PCs has ended with Tmall's victory (this year's "Double 11," Tmall's transaction volume reached 35 billion yuan, JD.com 3.5 billion yuan, YiXun 370 million yuan), in the entire retail sector, Tmall and even the entire Alibaba only account for 3.5% of transactions. This means that the battle for domestic e-commerce has not only not ended but has just begun, with mobile and offline being the larger decisive battlegrounds.

An extreme imagination is that in the future, users might express what they want through the public account of a brick-and-mortar store and pay via WeChat Pay, after which the mall would deliver directly to their door. In this entire transaction chain, Tmall and Taobao would be outside the closed-loop transaction structure built by WeChat.

Therefore, no matter what, Ma Yun will put forth the greatest effort to counter WeChat; no matter what, Ma Yun must continue PC-based advantages in the mobile field.

Ma Yun's tough words were already spoken at Laiwang's "one-month anniversary": "If I don't do it, no one else might; if we don't do it well, others will certainly not do it as well as us." This sentence sufficiently reflects Ma Yun's attitude and determination in this fight.

But Ma Yun doesn't just have the one card of "Laiwang." He has three cards.

The first card, "Laiwang," is just a direct attack on WeChat. Apart from subtle differences in product functions, the strategic significance Ali has endowed "Laiwang" with is to confront WeChat head-on, hard against hard, with fierce artillery, having stronger restraining power.

In market strategy, Laiwang's entertainment marketing investment will increase, hard advertising placements will include more Laiwang content, e-commerce resources will flood in, and phone bill subsidies will be even more aggressive. In terms of entertainment resources, Ma Yun is a director of Huayi Brothers; Alibaba and Hunan TV have a joint venture, a close cooperative relationship. Besides simple star endorsements, movie, TV series, and variety show integrations won't be lacking. Moreover, the results of next year's CCTV bidding are already known, with Tmall's winning bid being 4.3 times that of Tencent. A large portion of this money will be used for "Laiwang." This move is called "whoever is tougher wins."

In products, Laiwang's official accounts are also under development. One includes brand merchants with offline stores on the Tmall platform, two includes Taobao sellers, three includes digital media content, four includes offline merchants not yet covered by Tmall and Taobao. For offline merchants not covered by Tmall and Taobao, Alibaba can compensate through its O2O projects it has invested in, such as Meituan and JuHuaSuan. This move is called "resource diversion."

Secondly, it's the open platform. Currently, WeChat only connects Tencent's own mobile games. However, "Laiwang" can conduct joint operations with more mobile game companies to enhance platform stickiness. In the overall platform opening strategy, if WeChat opens an inch, Laiwang opens a yard, uniting ecological forces to isolate WeChat. This move is called "dimension reduction."

In addition, it's the integration with Sina Weibo IDs. Currently, the ID integration work between Taobao and Sina Weibo is ongoing. Then what about Laiwang? Laiwang's most urgent task right now is to quickly weave users together. On one hand, it’s about leveraging phone contact lists; on the other hand, directly importing Sina Weibo follow relationships through account interconnectivity can allow Laiwang to develop faster. Moreover, Laiwang can collaborate with Renren Network to enable ID interoperability. Renren users themselves are users of Tmall and Taobao, and they also watch Hunan TV. With Alibaba as a strategic partner, Chen Yizhou's situation will improve. This move is called "alliances and stratagems."

The second card is Alipay Wallet. "Laiwang" is the frontal assault, while Alipay Wallet is the flanking maneuver. Unlike "Laiwang," which focuses on user relationships, Alipay Wallet enters the mobile space from the perspective of mobile payments. Currently, Alipay Wallet is gradually downplaying its payment tool attributes, continuously expanding its open platform ecosystem, integrating local merchant services, and opening digital media public accounts, and it is also highly likely to integrate mobile games. The purpose is to enhance product stickiness and make it more lifestyle-oriented through platform measures. Its advantage lies in the fact that the user characteristics of payment products determine that its paid conversion rate is higher than other products. This is also the main reason attracting merchants to join.

The third card is investment layout. Currently, good projects not acquired by BAT include Dianping, Ganji.com, and Wandoujia. Dianping and Ganji.com have abundant offline merchant resources, and Wandoujia's application distribution capabilities are on par with 91. These are all acquisition cards that Alibaba can play. Wandoujia can give Alibaba's mobile products an advantage in download channels. Don't forget, SoftBank is currently Wandoujia's largest shareholder. If Alibaba successfully completes strategic investments in Dianping and Ganji, they will be able to deeply synergize with Laiwang, Alipay Wallet, and Gaode Map, which Alibaba holds a controlling stake in. Of course, it still depends on integration.
