Academic qualifications are no longer the competitiveness for job seekers.

by whdxzcb110 on 2012-02-23 09:25:46

In today's society, who exactly qualifies as talent? Perhaps different places and periods have different requirements. However, for businesses, those who can create wealth for the company are considered talents. Wealth creation is not directly proportional to one's educational level; sometimes experience is more valuable than education. Therefore, most business owners often don't take degrees seriously; they only care about your ability to get things done.

If today’s college graduates still flaunt their red diplomas in front of companies, it will only invite ridicule. Because there are too many college graduates now, 80% of job seekers in the talent market are college students. So a college graduation certificate is not something worth boasting about. Nowadays, recruiters won’t care too much about your educational background. If they find you capable and experienced, even without a college degree, they might hire you just the same. Conversely, if you have a degree but no ability, they wouldn’t hire you either.

Why do both job seekers and recruiters feel disappointed in today's talent market? It's because job seekers emphasize their educational qualifications while recruiters need your abilities, resulting from inconsistent supply and demand. This leads to serious waste of resources in the talent market. Job seekers look for jobs every day, and recruiters search for talents every day. Moreover, some companies explicitly state that they do not want college graduates, which is heartbreaking for them! Have we studied hard for three or four years, attended executive classes [http://www.whuzcb.org.cn], and yet still not be better than high school or junior high school graduates? Yes, from the perspective of businesses, they have their own concerns! Because most of the work in enterprises is not as good as job seekers imagine: browsing the internet, chatting, making phone calls, and so on. Especially in manufacturing companies, employees face cold machines every day! If employees lack patience and are unwilling to endure hardship, how can they adapt to such positions or environments? Therefore, instead of hiring someone who leaves after a few days of work, it would be less troublesome for recruiters to not hire at all [http://www.whuzcb.org.cn].

However, if every college graduate only wants to sit in a spacious and beautiful office, typing on computers, enjoying air conditioning, and feeling satisfied, is that possible? Where does the income of the enterprise come from? What does the boss rely on to pay salaries? Why do many so-called blue-collar workers or migrant workers earn higher wages than the so-called white-collar workers? Because they indeed create more value for the company! Therefore, for enterprises, bosses don't care what "collar" you are; as long as you can make more money for the company, you're a good fit. However, compared with these factory workers, college graduates lack experience, physical strength, and diligence. No wonder it's hard for college graduates to find jobs.

Because what bosses focus on is an individual's ability, not their educational background. Ability creates wealth, while education only proves your diploma. Particularly in sales-oriented roles, lower-educated individuals may perform better! The author has found in multiple industries and enterprises that the top salespeople are often not highly educated individuals but passionate, ambitious, determined young people who love learning and taking action, and whose educational levels are generally low. Therefore, current college graduates need to learn to forget their educational backgrounds. Because bosses will not give you more salary or bonuses simply because you are a college graduate; they only focus on results, and these results are determined by performance.

Because there are more and more college graduates, but office positions cannot meet the needs of large numbers of college graduates, naturally the value of college graduates decreases.

Perhaps asking a college graduate to become a worker and go down to the workshop makes many of them feel confused. They feel reluctant, thinking that if they had known they would end up working in workshops and doing manual labor after studying for three or four years, they might as well not have gone to college. However, this statement isn't entirely correct. Why? Because some migrant workers work all their lives and remain migrant workers, whereas if college graduates work as migrant workers, within three to five years, if they work with dedication, they could become team leaders of migrant workers, or even contractors or big bosses—this is the role of education. Therefore, the role of education should not be reflected in job hunting but should be more applied in actual work. If college graduates merely aim to gain more competitive opportunities without acquiring real skills, then even if they succeed in competition, their success won't last long.

The competition among enterprises is not a sprint but a marathon. Talented individuals must withstand tests, endure loneliness, and tolerate grievances. Taking a step back is for moving forward further. Today's lowering of the head is for tomorrow's raising of it!
