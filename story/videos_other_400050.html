Shentong Express equals to thief

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 11:22:35

Today was supposed to be an exciting day, but now it's turned into a really frustrating one.

I was expecting my swimsuit that I ordered online to arrive today. After finishing my work, I rushed to the reception room to wait for the courier. When I got there, the auntie who guards the place told me that my package had just arrived, and since she was about to finish her shift, and considering that Guangzhou Vipshop Information Technology Co., Ltd doesn't usually call people, she didn't notify me. This really caused a big problem.

My package was sitting on the table. The sealed part was still intact, but the other end had been wrapped with tape for a few rounds. Could it have been torn? I took a picture with my phone and when I pulled the taped area with my hand, a large opening appeared. When I took out the contents, I was stunned. It's over, it's been stolen by Shentong again...

This is the second time this has happened. They stole an Anna doll from me before the New Year, and now they've done it again. This time, I lost ten swimming goggles and eleven nose clips and earplugs, worth over two hundred yuan. If I gave these things to someone else, they would thank me, but now they're gone to thieves.

They don't notify me before delivery and deliberately let the gatekeeper sign for the package, making it easier for them to steal items without the recipient knowing. There are many people in our office who shop online, and none of them ask the gatekeeper to check the goods personally. If I were to make such a request, it would be... But the thing is, our gatekeeper is very trustworthy, so it's hard to make such a request.

The express delivery person called me to ask if there was anything wrong with the package. I said yes, and he asked if the outer packaging was damaged. I asked what "damaged" meant, and she replied that it means exposing the contents inside. I asked her if fixing a broken bag with tape would still be considered as "damaged". She calmly told me that as long as the contents inside are not exposed, it is not considered "damaged". I said that if they open the bag, take out the contents, and then reseal it with tape, and the contents inside are not exposed, can it still be called "not damaged"? She said that as long as the contents inside are not exposed, it is not considered "damaged".

To the person at Shentong Express who stole my stuff, if I can't get my things back, consider this money I spent as buying paper to burn for you. I hope you rest in peace on your way to the Western Paradise!
