Extreme weather appears frequently, and low-carbon strategy cannot wait

by ditan360 on 2010-07-07 11:40:55

Low-carbon Network News: Since the beginning of this summer, most areas in China have experienced high temperature weather with a daily maximum temperature greater than or equal to 35℃. Some areas even saw temperatures above 38℃. Yesterday in Beijing, a historical record was broken when the ground temperature reached 68℃. Such extreme weather has brought great inconvenience to people's lives and work, and also raised suspicions among many: Is global warming the chief culprit for abnormal weather? Has the climate disaster already arrived?

According to experts, "heat waves" frequently attacking us are somewhat related to global warming. Because of global warming, some rare weather phenomena that were uncommon before climate change now occur more frequently. "If it wasn't for global warming, such 'heat waves' would not appear. Global warming increases the probability of 'heat wave' formation," said relevant experts.

The frequent appearance of abnormal climatic phenomena in our country is an indisputable fact. In fact, not only our country, but many countries around the world have seen abnormal climates, and the frequency is accelerating. It is reported that New York in the United States recently reached a temperature of over 36℃, and some areas even saw extreme highs above 38℃. Apart from the United States, France, Germany, Spain and other countries have also experienced high temperatures, with some parts of Italy reaching 40℃. The temperatures in some Gulf countries have even soared to 50℃. Meteorologists predict that in the foreseeable future, the world will suffer from more severe 'heat waves', and abnormal weather will become increasingly frequent. Cities located further north will be more affected by extreme weather. This will seriously impact the livelihoods and economic development of various countries.

How to avoid extreme weather under the current harsh natural environmental conditions and make the natural climate stabilize has become the top priority for all countries. Relevant experts say that finding the root cause of global warming and taking steps to solve it may be an important way to avoid these issues.

Studies have found that the main reason for global warming is excessive greenhouse gas emissions, or rather, excessive carbon dioxide emissions. Therefore, promoting low-carbon strategies and reducing carbon emissions will be an effective means to address abnormal climate changes. Especially under the current situation where extreme climates have already significantly impacted people's production and life, the low-carbon strategy appears even more urgent.

On the issue of global warming, there is a consensus worldwide: promote low-carbon strategies, jointly reduce emissions, and jointly respond to crises. For our country, regarding the issue of carbon reduction, we certainly cannot stand aloof. Therefore, we have recently committed to the goal of reducing carbon emissions per unit of GDP by 40-45% in 2020 compared to 2005.

Addressing climate change and promoting low-carbon strategies are not only effective means to solve global warming, but also major measures to boost national economic development. Promoting energy conservation and emission reduction and sustainable development through technological innovation can drive institutional and systemic innovation in the country. The renewal of production models will provide an industrial foundation for the country in the next round of global competition in the future.

Promoting low-carbon strategies is not only a major strategy related to national macro-control, but also an important event involving the daily lives of ordinary people.

For national macro-control, promoting low-carbon strategies requires vigorously advocating energy conservation and emission reduction, and launching renewable energy projects such as wind and solar power.

And for the general public, facing extreme climates that have a huge impact on our lives, we should start from ourselves and take immediate action to reduce carbon emissions, and contribute to solving the problem of global warming with our actual actions, so that our climate can return to normal.
