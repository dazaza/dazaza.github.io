46-Year-Old Man Spends 12 Hours Swimming Across the Qiongzhou Channel (Picture)

by anonymous on 2012-02-24 11:04:14

The Qiongzhou Strait is located between the Leizhou Peninsula of Guangdong Province and Hainan Island, connecting to the Beibu Gulf in the west and the northern South China Sea in the east. It extends from east to west, approximately 80 kilometers long, with a width ranging from 20 to 40 kilometers. The narrowest point is only 18 kilometers wide, covering an area of 0.24 million square kilometers. The average water depth is 44 meters, with the maximum depth reaching up to 114 meters.

During his crossing, he encountered circular currents, hidden currents, and was stung by jellyfish multiple times, even contemplating giving up. However, encouraged by his companions, he finally successfully reached the opposite shore beach.

Morning Post Fushun News (reported by Li Zhanzhou) - In 12 hours and 8 minutes, 46-year-old Li Kaijun from Fushun successfully crossed the Qiongzhou Strait on July 14. This crossing covered a straight-line distance of 26 kilometers, but the actual distance swum was 32 kilometers.

During the crossing, he faced circular and hidden currents, was stung by jellyfish multiple times, and even considered giving up. However, under the encouragement of his companions, he finally succeeded in collapsing onto the beach.

Blindly Entering the Water

Injury from Jellyfish Stings During a Previous Attempt 3 Years Ago

Yesterday afternoon, the 46-year-old Li Kaijun stood before the reporter. Five days ago, after being submerged in seawater for 12 consecutive hours, his skin turned dark and shiny. The intense sun in Hainan caused peeling on his face, and abrasions on his cheekbones and back neck due to prolonged friction with swim goggles and a one-piece swimsuit had scabbed over.

"Yearning for the sea, yet still fearing the seawater," Li Kaijun frankly expressed his feelings post-crossing.

Li Kaijun is an employee at the Fushun Petrochemical Company's Ethylene Chemical Plant who has always loved swimming. On July 24, 2005, he and five partners crossed the Dashofang Reservoir in 10 hours, actually swimming nearly 20 kilometers, becoming the first "swimmers" to cross the reservoir since its construction 46 years ago. For this crossing, Li Kaijun prepared for three years.

However, that crossing was merely a warm-up. As early as 2000, he was captivated by the Qiongzhou Strait and resolved to cross it.

In July 2006, he arrived at Haikou Holiday Beach, planning to cross the Qiongzhou Strait from there. Due to the lack of navigators and protective measures, combined with unfamiliarity with sea conditions, he was forced to abandon his attempt after swimming just over 1,000 meters and was stung by jellyfish.

Three Years of Hard Training

Swimming 1000-2000 Meters Daily

"Failure was due to blindly attacking." Li Kaijun summarized the lessons learned; at the time, he didn't even know that wearing a one-piece swimsuit was necessary for the crossing.

After returning to Fushun from Haikou in July 2006, he connected with the "Hai Niu Team Crossing Club" in Haikou. From then on, he first encountered concepts such as "circular current", "wave height", and "current shift".

"I underestimated the complexity of the sea. To cross it, one must understand the sea," Li Kaijun admitted his naivety. Besides learning, he intensified physical training, swimming 1000-2000 meters daily and running 5000 meters to 10,000 meters.

Li Kaijun informed the reporter that wearing a swimsuit was primarily for safety. There are jellyfish near several entry points of the Qiongzhou Strait; the purple ones are highly toxic, and being stung by them could be life-threatening. Being stung by yellow ones causes pain and itching. Additionally, a wet swimsuit weighs about 5 kilograms, so he increased his training intensity to handle this burden.

Swimming for 12 Hours and 8 Minutes

Supplemented with Eight Treasures Porridge and Glucose Over Ten Times

On July 11, 2023, Li Kaijun returned to Haikou, where the Special Police Brigade of the Haikou Public Security Bureau had already planned his crossing route. This route included three entry points and one landing point.

He hired a small boat responsible for supplies, protection, and navigation. After two trial crossings, at 4:56 AM on July 14, he entered the water in Paishui Village, Xuwen County, Guangdong Province, beginning his crossing.

Just upon entering the water, his feet were quickly cut by shellfish in the nearby aquaculture zone. To conserve energy, he used breaststroke. Within the nearshore area, his feet were frequently "kissed" by jellyfish, feeling like electric shocks.

After exiting the 3000-meter nearshore aquaculture zone, guided by the navigator, he bypassed five or six circular currents. A few times, while resting by treading water, he encountered hidden currents and quickly moved away.

During the crossing, he received supplies over ten times, each consisting of crushed eight treasures porridge and glucose. According to international conventions, he could not touch the boat when receiving food, otherwise, his achievement would be invalidated. During supply sessions, seawater continuously poured into his throat.

At around 2 PM that day, he was about 800 meters away from the designated landing point, but due to the impact of the sea current, he couldn't reach the shore. After changing the landing location three times, exhausted, he decided to give up the crossing. One companion jumped into the water to encourage him to persevere and closely protected him.

At around 5 PM, he finally landed at Xinhai Village in Haikou. Upon reaching the shore, he wanted to kneel down, but his legs couldn't bend. He collapsed directly onto the sand, tears streaming down his face.

Dialogue

Crossing and Life Are the Same Thing

Reporter: What is the purpose of the crossing?

Li Kaijun: Just intending to fulfill my long-held wish. It might not be important to others, but it is very important to me.

Reporter: What is the biggest gain from this crossing?

Li Kaijun: Crossing and life are the same thing. There are people encouraging you, and you will encounter hidden reefs. You may feel disheartened, but you can also revive yourself. The path you take depends on yourself.

Reporter: If you had given up during the final sprint phase, what impact would it have on you?

Li Kaijun: If I had given up, the worst result would be being swept away and never seeing my family again; less severely, it would damage my self-confidence. I might think I'm incapable, and I might not have the face to return to Fushun.

Reporter: What sensation left the deepest impression during the crossing?

Li Kaijun: One is the sense of fear, especially when encountering hidden and circular currents; the other is gratitude, as local villagers saw me off before entering the water, companions encouraged me during the crossing, and passing ships protected us.

Reporter: Do you have any larger crossing plans?

Li Kaijun: Not for now. The fear of the sea hasn't disappeared yet, but challenging myself is definitely certain. It's just uncertain in what form. (Reported by Li Zhanzhou)

Extension

It's Not Easy to Cross the Qiongzhou Strait

The Qiongzhou Strait is one of the three major straits in the country, located between the Leizhou Peninsula in Guangdong and Hainan Island, about 80 kilometers long, with a width ranging from 20 to 40 kilometers. The average water depth is approximately 44 meters, with the maximum depth reaching about 120 meters.

The straight-line distance between the shores of the Qiongzhou Strait is 25 kilometers, suitable for people to challenge themselves by crossing. The water temperature is around 30℃ in June to August, and no sharks have ever been spotted. Based on these three conditions, the Qiongzhou Strait is considered the most suitable strait for humans to cross.

The difficulties in crossing the Qiongzhou Strait lie in the complex sea conditions, large tidal changes, and numerous aggressive jellyfish. (Huashang Morning Post)
