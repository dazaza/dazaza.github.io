Regardless of whether you rent or own a house, the initial shallow treatment of drinking water at the end of the yard is quite unnecessary, and it is the most basic health requirement for living. When building a house, how do you select and purchase home water treatment equipment? In other words, what basic knowledge of water points do you need to help you make reasonable budget arrangements? Home water treatment equipment refers to devices used in homes to treat water. The reason water needs to be treated is because it has problems. What issues are present with the water supply to your home? Generally, there are four common issues with tap water.

1. The first issue that needs to be addressed is the problem of impurities. What consequences can these particulate impurities cause when they enter the pipes? Pipes and water-related equipment installed on the pipes (such as washing machines, dishwashers, soft water machines, clean water machines, and cold water dispensers) may be damaged or even completely broken due to these impurities. Valves, connecting parts, and filters in the water system will need to be replaced because of this. The best solution is to install a protective impurity filter, thereby preventing unnecessary maintenance and expenses. Even the German government issued a decree in 1988, DIN1988, mandating that all new buildings after 1988 must be equipped with protective impurity filters in their water systems. Germany's Cillit Water Company has been researching and producing self-cleaning filters for over 40 years. Their self-cleaning filters have high flow rates, low pressure loss, and a filtration pore size of 90-110 microns, effectively blocking rust, mud, and other suspended impurities from tap water, providing excellent protection for the concealed pipes, faucets, and water-related appliances in your home. The self-cleaning function of Cillit Water avoids secondary water contamination and frequent daily maintenance.

Municipal return water pollution mainly occurs in two stages: First, the pipeline network. Currently, various diameter tap water pipes laid under the streets of Shanghai total 50 kilometers in length. Due to historical reasons, some of these tap water pipes were laid before 1949, and some "ancient" pipes over 100 years old are still in use today. These pipes suffer from severe rusting, which contaminates the water quality. Data shows that about 300 kilometers of tap water pipes in the city have been in use for more than 50 years, about 800 kilometers of pipes are prone to bursting and leaking, and about 400 kilometers of pipes lack internal lining. Second, the water tank. The cleaning of water tanks at pumping stations is another source of contamination. According to relevant regulations in Shanghai, water tanks should be cleaned and disinfected once per quarter; later, based on practical circumstances, it was stipulated that water tanks must be cleaned twice a year. However, since water tanks are managed by property management companies while the water inside is managed by the tap water company, it is difficult to ensure standardized cleaning of the water tanks.

2. The second issue that needs to be resolved is the problem of non-potable water. The World Health Organization (WHO) states that high-quality drinking water should not contain any substances that are toxic or harmful to the human body, and at the same time, it should contain minerals and trace elements beneficial to the human body.

Solution: Install an end-of-line water purifier.

Cillit Water's direct drinking installation uses three-stage filtration, representing advanced technology in the current market. The first stage uses activated carbon filtration to remove colors, odors, and harmful substances such as residual chlorine; the second stage uses silver-coated granular material to destroy bacterial DNA and effectively inhibit bacterial growth; the third stage uses ultrafiltration with a pore size of 0.02 microns, effectively filtering out 100% of bacteria and viruses known in medical science. Special reminder: currently unknown bacteria in medical science are larger than 0.02 microns, and viruses that can survive in water are smaller than 0.02 microns.

3. The third issue that needs to be addressed is the problem of agricultural water use, specifically anti-scaling and anti-corrosion. Improving the quality of irrigation water hinges on solving these two problems.

To solve the scaling problem, a water softener is generally used. Using a water softener not only solves the problem of calcium and magnesium ions in the water forming scale on metal surfaces in pipes and water-related equipment, ensuring the effectiveness and extending the lifespan of the equipment while reducing costs, but more importantly, solving the scaling problem also prevents serious and often unnoticed contamination caused by scale (which harbors and breeds bacteria).

In households, water softeners are generally used to solve scaling problems in pipes and equipment. However, users cannot avoid the following facts: 1) Water softeners remove a small portion of calcium and magnesium ions beneficial to the human body; 2) Hard water significantly increases sodium ions beneficial to the human body (according to the principle of ion exchange, the increase in sodium ions in softened water is equivalent to the removal of calcium and magnesium ions); 3) Softened water, due to increased sodium ions, becomes much more corrosive to pipes and water-related equipment. These are the three major costs that users of traditional water softeners using ion exchange technology must pay.

Supplementary measures include: First, the water pipes for domestic use and drinking water must be separated to ensure that drinking water does not lose calcium and magnesium ions, nor gain sodium ions; Second, install a corrosion inhibitor upstream of the water softener to prevent damage to metal pipes and the metal surfaces of water-related equipment caused by increased corrosiveness of the softened water.

What pleasantly surprised me is that Germany's Cillit Water Company, based on traditional water softeners, adhering to the principles of safety, hygiene, and health in water use, developed the revolutionary "Nanoflow" anti-scaling filtration system, also known as the "salt-free water softener." This new technology does not remove calcium and magnesium ions (beneficial to the human body) or add sodium ions (harmful to the human body), but instead transforms calcium and magnesium ions into nanocrystals that can only float freely in the water, thus achieving the goal of no scaling.

Nanoflow simultaneously addresses the scaling problem without removing calcium and magnesium ions, adding sodium ions, or increasing water corrosiveness.

"Nanoflow," compared to traditional water softeners (ion exchange technology), requires no salt and no wastewater discharge, making it healthy and environmentally friendly. In recent years, it has become the leading product for household water treatment in Europe.

To solve the corrosion problem, corrosion inhibitors are generally used. They form a protective film on the contact surfaces of pipes, water heaters, and bathroom faucets, preventing corrosion.

Water itself has a certain degree of corrosiveness, especially traditional water softeners, which increase water corrosiveness due to added sodium ions. Therefore, corrosion treatment for pipes, especially hot water pipelines, is particularly necessary.

Although water softeners solve the scaling problem, they do not solve the corrosion problem and, to some extent, increase water corrosiveness (because removing calcium and magnesium ions increases sodium ions equivalently). For this reason, corrosion inhibitors are essential to help solve pipe and equipment corrosion problems. The food-grade materials added by corrosion inhibitors are themselves food. It is reported that the material added is polyphosphate, and an average adult has about 2.1 kilograms of this substance in their body, requiring a daily intake of 6 grams. Other reference data include 9.5 grams, 9 grams, and 4.5 grams of this component found in one kilogram of chocolate, bread, and milk respectively. High-quality drinking water should also contain a considerable amount. Germany's Cillit dosing device sets the dosage at 4 grams per ton for life, being micro, precise, and stable. Not only does it form a protective coating on the inner walls of the pipes, effectively preventing pipe corrosion, but it also improves the taste of the water and adds beneficial minerals and trace elements to drinking water for human health.
