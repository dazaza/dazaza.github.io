Former CSRC Chairman Zhou Daojiong: Stock index futures will come out soon

PECC China Finance Committee Chairman and former CSRC Chairman Zhou Daojiong stated on Thursday that China should focus on risk control and management, actively and steadily develop the domestic financial derivatives market, and launch stock index futures as soon as possible. Zhou Daojiong said at the "Fifth China (Shenzhen) International Futures Conference" on Thursday that because the domestic derivatives market is not well-developed, some large enterprises, especially central enterprises, have to rely more on overseas financial derivatives markets for hedging. However, in OTC transactions with foreign investment banks, transparency is low and liquidity is poor, thus implying significant risks. He believes that a transparent market environment has a considerable advantage in reducing risks and can better prevent default risks. Therefore, China's financial derivatives market should particularly develop exchange-traded derivatives markets, starting with futures products related to the real economy. Zhou Daojiong called for the accelerated introduction of the Futures Law and said, "Stock index futures will come out very soon."

In the secondary market, futures-related stocks have recently been quite active. Their trends and today's relevant speeches from regulators indicate the significance of these movements. GEM (Growth Enterprise Market) Experiences Another Collective Frenzy

Statistics show that since the opening of the GEM on October 30, until yesterday, among all the buying and selling seats disclosed by SZSE, institutional investors have appeared less than 10 times, with the majority being speculative trading departments. Among the more active brokerage firms, Guosen Shenzhen Taoranjiu Road branch, Haitong Securities (600837) Shangyu Bai Guan Town branch, Guosen Securities Yiwu Chouzhou North Road branch, Yingda Securities Shenzhen Yuanling Sanjie branch, etc., appear most frequently, with transaction amounts generally ranging in the six-digit range. "I didn't expect speculative funds to be so interested in the GEM. Although the call for value return on the GEM is still strong, there are no signs yet of a full retreat, and the market will remain active," said an analyst from a securities company. Moreover, he believes that the high P/E ratio of the GEM needs to be viewed dialectically because the high P/E ratios seen now are calculated based on 2008 performance. However, the Q1-Q3 performance growth of the 28 GEM companies was significant. Looking at this year's dynamic P/E ratio, it is not excessively high and is relatively similar to the P/E ratio of SME boards. This indicates that all GEM markets around the world have excess returns at the start, lasting between 3 months to 2 years. In other words, the GEM will have excess returns at least within the first 3 months of its establishment. Before the compilation of the GEM index, they will further manifest individual stock opportunities. While paying attention to the risks of GEM stocks, we should also recognize their superlative market opportunities and end-of-year rotational speculation.

The procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. And this commission is only valid for the day it is placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price at which you buy or sell the stock. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of four to three Chinese characters, and the code of the stock is six digits. When entrusting to buy or sell, the code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent. At the same time, there are certain regulations on the quantity of stocks bought and sold: the quantity of stocks commissioned for purchase must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks commissioned for sale does not have to be a whole multiple of 100. There are four ways to entrust: counter submission, telephone automatic entrustment, computer automatic entrustment, and remote terminal entrustment.
