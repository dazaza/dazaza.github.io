In the training courses of the training company, the coach often quotes some very classic and healthy educational stories that have been practiced by the ancients, summarizing inspiring truths. As a staff member of a Beijing expansion company, I have the opportunity to frequently come into contact with such courses when team members are undergoing training. Listening to these inspiring stories and phrases has proven to be highly beneficial. It feels as if I suddenly understand many principles of life, elevating my thinking. I feel that life should be more thoroughly dedicated to effort, clarifying my career path and increasing my motivation at work. It's like an injection of adrenaline into my consciousness, making me eager to start each day's tasks.

I often take out these classic sentences to read, constantly urging myself and adding infinite motivation to my work. This makes me even more hopeful about life and work, giving me courage to move forward. These become the lubricants for my progress. Practical knowledge I hope to share with everyone, hoping that you, like me, will read them often, taking your abilities to the next level.

The following is some motivational content I've collected from the training courses:

Today, I must pay extra attention to my own value. Mulberry leaves become silk in the hands of a genius. Clay becomes a fortress in the hands of a genius. Cypress wood becomes a hall in the hands of a genius. Wool becomes a cassock in the hands of a genius. If mulberry leaves, clay, cypress, and wool can increase their value hundreds or thousands of times through human creativity, why can't I increase my own worth manyfold? Today, I must pay extra attention to my own value. My destiny is like a grain of wheat, having three different paths. A grain of wheat may be put into a sack, stacked on a shelf, waiting to feed pigs; it may be ground into flour and made into bread; or it may be sown in the soil, allowing it to grow until golden ears produce hundreds of grains. The only difference between me and a grain of wheat is that the wheat cannot choose whether to rot, be made into bread, or be planted and grow. But I have the freedom to choose. I will not let my life rot, nor will I let it be crushed under rocks of failure and despair, leaving me at the mercy of others. Today, I must pay extra attention to my own value.

To make the wheat grow and bear fruit, it must be planted in dark soil. My failures, disappointments, ignorance, and incompetence are that dark soil. I must deeply root myself in the soil, waiting for maturity. Under the nourishment of sunlight and rain, the wheat will eventually sprout, bloom, and bear fruit. Likewise, I must strengthen both my body and mind to achieve my dreams. Wheat needs to wait for nature's opportunities to mature, but I don't need to wait because I have the ability to choose my own fate. Today, I must pay extra attention to my own value.

After reading, at the Beijing expansion base, I learned not to be a dwarf in action anymore. I’ve developed the good habit of doing things right away. I also learned how to set daily, weekly, and annual goals. The larger direction was already established long ago, but it felt like a distant dream. Now, it doesn’t seem so far away. Because every day is fulfilling, each goal achieved gives a sense of accomplishment. When short-term goals are continuously realized, isn't that getting closer to our dreams?

Perhaps after reading this, everyone might gain even greater inspiration than I did. We all have desires for life and expectations for work. While striving to realize our dreams, looking at these classic motivational phrases may give us even more motivation to push forward.
