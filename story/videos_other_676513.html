Emotional infidelity and physical infidelity

by ybnalal7688 on 2012-02-26 09:23:26

As for the topic of infidelity nowadays, whether in real life or in media and film and television, it seems that without the topic of infidelity, it's hard to achieve a good story. Especially in some emotional dramas, similar cases of infidelity, extramarital affairs, due to career success and changes in status, make the once beloved person slowly distance themselves. Either they have a lover outside or they are sleeping in the same bed but dreaming separately. The two people who were once most familiar with each other become strangers every day. What's even worse is that the other person's emotional infidelity causes both parties to suffer great stimulation and harm. Although they keep promising and confessing that they won't do anything to hurt the family, if their heart is no longer there, what's the use even if their body is still present?

One doesn't understand why her husband has been ignoring and neglecting her existence all along. Although he is also doing his best to maintain this home and is very responsible for this home, she clearly feels that her husband can be gentle and talkative with friends and colleagues outside, but when he comes home, he has a gloomy face, as if she did something shameful that makes him avoid being with her. But because of appearances, he has to勉强(marginally) interact. She has also tried her best to make efforts, hoping to win back her husband's heart through her contributions. But the closer she gets, the farther her husband seems to move away. Sometimes she really hates her own way. In order to make her husband happy, she tries her best to smile and do things that make him comfortable, trying to become his ideal person. But she never expected that the man she once deeply loved would become so strange, just like someone said: there is nothing more helpless and defeated than the betrayal of the person next to you on the pillow. Especially, the betrayal of the heart is actually more painful than the betrayal around you. If the other person's heart is no longer there, what's the point of holding onto an empty shell? She has repeatedly asked her husband what happened to make him treat her this way, and the other party actually threw out a question: where was I in his heart these years? She really didn't know what to say. Perhaps in life, with her busy schedule and the child's needs, she indeed neglected her husband, but her husband's position in her heart has never changed because he gives her security, because her husband is worth entrusting, because she knows her husband will not leave her behind. So she acts capriciously and throws tantrums, but she never thought that one day her husband would seriously hurt her.

Her husband chats online every day, sometimes goes out to meet friends, but always remembers to come home. Although she feels that there is a rift between them, she doesn't know how to win back her husband's heart, nor does she know what happened between them to make him so cold. Just like her husband said, he will be responsible for this family and will not do anything to betray her. He is just passing time with friends because going home makes him feel oppressed and suffocated. Outside, he can feel very relaxed and happy, free and easy. He enjoys and cherishes the pleasure of being with friends. He doesn't want to be like this, but he really doesn't want to go home, doesn't want to see her face and the oppressive atmosphere at home. He wants to change, but he feels very powerless ----

Emotional infidelity and physical infidelity, many people think that as long as there is no physical infidelity, it should not be a big problem. They will use a lot of so-called big truths to persuade each other to accept, and even boast about taking responsibility for both parties. But the issue of feelings itself is a very selfish thing. When truly in love with someone, one develops "obsessive-compulsive disorder", which cannot tolerate any impurity in the other person's feelings, whether physically or emotionally. Therefore, don't take emotional infidelity lightly. This kind of ambiguity is like a bomb that may explode at any time, bringing fatal harm and shadow to each other unintentionally.

For him whose heart has already strayed, don't easily give up. Problems need to be found in the interaction and communication between each other. Like the husband in the article asks the other party a question: what position is the husband in your eyes? Yes, when various problems arise between two people who once loved each other, do we give ourselves time to calm down and reflect, what position is the other person in your eyes, and what position are you in the other person's eyes? Don't blindly torture each other. Find your appropriate position, learn to enjoy and appreciate each other, and believe that you will surely regain your own happiness and joy. The question is, can you find a suitable position for yourself?
