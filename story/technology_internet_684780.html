Mini school buses are more suitable for China's national conditions

by adcocklz on 2012-03-01 10:31:32

In China's first school bus exhibition, SAIC Commercial Vehicle Company brought the MAXUS V80 light school bus product. Although it is smaller than other school buses on display, being less than 6 meters in length, it is [small but complete]. According to the new national standard for school buses, the MAXUS school bus is currently the only light-duty passenger vehicle with a national school bus qualification announcement.

Facing various large and medium-sized school bus products in the market, how is this MAXUS V80 small school bus [http://www.zyqc.cc/Article/Search/%e5%b0%8f%e5%9e%8b%e6%a0%a1%e8%bd%a6] positioned? What is the market size of small school buses [http://www.zyqc.cc/Article/Search/%e5%b0%8f%e5%9e%8b%e6%a0%a1%e8%bd%a6] domestically? With these questions, First Commercial Vehicle Network interviewed Gao Mingnuo, Senior Deputy Manager of Brand and Market Management Department at MAXUS.

SAIC MAXUS primary school bus

Small school buses [http://www.zyqc.cc/Article/Search/%e5%b0%8f%e5%9e%8b%e6%a0%a1%e8%bd%a6] are more suitable for China's national conditions

Gao Mingnuo believes that considering the geographical locations of most middle and primary schools in domestic cities, smaller school buses are more popular.

According to his introduction, in many cities in China, most middle and primary schools are not built along convenient major roads, but are "hidden" in narrow streets and alleys with dense populations. Such geographical locations make many large vehicles difficult to maneuver. Because they are too long and wide, they are extremely inconvenient to drive in densely populated areas. If they encounter traffic congestion and lack flexibility to weave through, it could very likely delay students' arrival or departure time. [Some schools are located deep within narrow alleys; in such cases, the role of large school buses is greatly diminished because the alleys are too narrow, so large school buses can only park at the alley entrance, and students have to walk a long distance from the school gate to board the bus. However, if it were a small school bus [http://www.zyqc.cc/Article/Search/%e5%b0%8f%e5%9e%8b%e6%a0%a1%e8%bd%a6], this problem would be easily solved by driving directly to the school gate, making travel more convenient for students.]
