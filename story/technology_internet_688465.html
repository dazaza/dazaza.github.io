On Ecological Politics and the Politicization of Ecology

by pascxvty on 2012-03-03 10:51:16

【Abstract】Modern industrial civilization has brought enormous progress to humanity. However, due to humanity's incorrect understanding of nature and itself, as well as blind exploitation, a global ecological crisis emerged in the 1960s and 70s. The emergence and development of the ecological crisis became the driving force and foundation for the rise of ecological political movements. This, in turn, promoted the ecological transformation of national politics and international politics. The development of ecological politics is the premise and foundation for the formation of political ecology and ecological politicization. Political ecological transformation is the inevitable trend and result of the development of ecological politics, an indispensable fundamental measure to address the current global ecological crisis, and a necessary choice for the coordinated, sustainable, healthy development of the global economy, society, culture, and ecological environment. It is also one of the trends in the future development of politics.

I. Introduction

Since the 1960s, with the appearance of global environmental issues, the ecological crisis has increasingly drawn the attention of countries worldwide. From a certain perspective, this concern has far surpassed the contradictions and conflicts between nations, regions, and ethnic groups. Humanity is increasingly paying attention to its shared ecological environment and home—the Earth. The focus on the ecological crisis forces humanity to re-examine its relationship with nature, as well as its original ways of thinking, production and consumption patterns, development models, ideologies, ethical views, and developmental perspectives. It also prompts reconsideration of the future prospects and destinies of economic, cultural, and political development in various countries. The emergence of global environmental problems means that any country—whether developed or developing, socialist or capitalist—has once again come together.

Based on the above global environmental issues and the appearance of the ecological crisis, at the end of the 1960s, a vigorous and widespread ecological political movement began in many countries around the world, especially in some developed Western countries. Since the 1970s, every year countries around the world have used occasions such as "Earth Day" and "World Environment Day" to continuously strengthen these movements, which remain vibrant to this day. In the 1980s and 1990s, under the impetus of the ecological political movement, numerous ecological organizations and green parties (commonly referred to as ecological parties or green parties) were established in both developed and developing countries. Ecology and political science thus merged, forming new interdisciplinary fringe disciplines such as ecological political science and political ecology, prompting a shift from traditional political development perspectives to ecological political development perspectives.

II. The Rise, Development, and Significance of Ecological Politics

(A) Ecological Crisis - The Driving Force and Foundation of the Rise of Ecological Politics

An ecological crisis refers to the degradation of the natural environment or ecosystem structure and function due to unreasonable human exploitation and utilization, leading to a severe imbalance in ecosystems.

Since the Industrial Revolution, especially in the last 50 years of the 20th century, the global environment has suffered unprecedented severe destruction and pollution, described by some ecologists and politicians as one of the three major foolish acts committed by humanity in the 20th century and the "Third World War." The founder of "Earth Day," Gaylord Nelson, aptly stated: Threats from nature (ecological crisis) pose a more dangerous challenge than war. We know from Germany and Japan that a country can recover from the trauma of war, but no country can quickly rise from a destroyed natural environment. After the 1950s, the world faced ten major global environmental issues, including the "greenhouse effect," destruction of the atmospheric ozone layer, worsening acid rain pollution, the spread of toxic chemicals, population explosion, soil erosion, rapid deforestation, expansion of land desertification, water resource pollution and shortages, and sharp declines in biodiversity. The severe destruction of the global ecological environment is cruelly shattering every beautiful wish and dream humanity has for the future. This impact will not only affect one or two generations but will influence several, even dozens of generations, in terms of survival and reproduction.
