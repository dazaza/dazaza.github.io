Arsenal Wins the Declining Powerhouse Match, Reviving Championship Hopes

By Xiang Tong

If the top four clubs are compared to The Beatles, then the Liverpool vs Arsenal match could be seen as a contest between George and Ringo (the less famous members of The Beatles). However, the battle between declining powerhouses was equally significant. Liverpool needed to reclaim their top-four position, while Arsenal naturally hoped to take advantage of Chelsea and Manchester United's struggles in this round to narrow the gap with the league leaders. Arsenal had previously lost every time they faced strong opponents this season, but last Sunday at Anfield, it was Arsenal who came out on top.

Arsenal’s 2-1 comeback victory marked their first league win at Anfield since 2003. Now, with one fewer game played, Arsenal are only three points behind Manchester United and six points behind league leaders Chelsea. Everything is still possible. It can be said that both teams played well for half the match each. Arsenal were suppressed by the home team in the first half, but their explosion after halftime was due to Wenger's fiery halftime speech.

Steven Gerrard’s Penalty Claim Fails

Liverpool now has no excuse regarding injuries; apart from Babel, there are no other first-team casualties, and Torres made his first league start in six weeks after overcoming a hernia issue. On the contrary, Arsenal had more than half a dozen key players injured. After using youth players in the midweek Champions League match, Wenger fielded his strongest available lineup, with Walcott making his first Premier League start in two months.

After kickoff, Liverpool took control of the game, not only producing brilliant moments like Aurelio's fake move that fooled Nasri, but also creating scoring opportunities quickly. In the 12th minute, Liverpool launched an impressive counterattack through their front double stars. Torres acted as a wall in midfield to release Gerrard, who raced down the right flank into the penalty area and sent a through ball. Torres received the pass and attempted a shot, but it lacked both angle and power, allowing Almunia to make the save. Just one minute later, Gerrard broke into the box from the center, attempting to go past Gallar, who collided with him and knocked him down. Despite Gerrard's plea for a penalty, referee Howard Webb ignored it.

In the 21st minute, Liverpool produced smooth combination play on the right flank. Kuyt delivered a beautiful pass, and Glen Johnson dribbled past Denilson and Nasri inside the box before crossing. However, the final cross was disappointing. Johnson, fired up, soon unleashed two powerful shots from distance, and Liverpool's set-piece crosses frequently caused chaos in the opponent's penalty area. In the 34th minute, Torres was fouled by Gallar just outside the box, but again Webb showed no interest.

Arsenal paid the price for their set-piece defensive issues in the 41st minute. From 40 yards out, Aurelio's free-kick was floated into the box. Almunia came out to punch it clear but fumbled, allowing Kuyt to chest the ball down and poke it home for a 1-0 lead. This extended Liverpool's club-record streak of scoring in 19 consecutive home Premier League matches. Wenger was very upset about this goal because Lucas appeared to be offside when he jumped to challenge Almunia for the ball.

Two Key Transfers, One Villain

Among the spectators at this match was a familiar face: former Liverpool player Xabi Alonso, who joined Real Madrid this summer, returned to Anfield to watch the game. Some British media outlets published pictures alongside sarcastic comments: "We won't make any jokes about this photo." However, the real black humor lay in the fact that Alonso's replacement, Alberto Aquilani, was once again limited to substitute appearances, while another important summer signing, Glen Johnson, became the villain by contributing to two goals conceded within eight minutes in the second half.

In the 50th minute, after a series of slick passes from Arsenal's attack, Nasri received a through ball from Fabregas, entered the box from the right side, and delivered a low cross. The ball deflected off Carragher slightly, and Johnson, in panic, inadvertently deflected it into his own net with his left foot, leveling the score at 1-1. In the 58th minute, Fabregas broke through on the right flank and crossed the ball to Arshavin at the far post. Arshavin easily maneuvered past the disoriented Johnson and hammered a fierce shot into the near post, which hit the inside of the post and rolled into the net, giving Arsenal a 2-1 lead.

Just after the equalizer, Torres had a chance. He cut inside from the left flank, dancing along the baseline into the box, but neither shooting nor finding a way to pass back. His attempt ended fruitlessly. Seeing the opposition score twice in quick succession, Gerrard charged angrily down the right flank, kicking Nasri and knocking Kuyt over before delivering a cross via Johnson. Unfortunately, Torres' header glanced wide.

Neither side could create many more genuine chances thereafter. Arshavin, receiving a pass from Nasri, tried a shot from the right side of the box, but it posed no threat to Reina. A frustrated Liverpool made multiple substitutions, with Aquilani replacing Mascherano amid applause from Alonso, but to little effect. Arsenal ended their run of six games without a win against the top four.

Liverpool, meanwhile, have only won three of their last 15 matches across all competitions. Since beating Manchester United at the end of October, they have gone two draws and two losses at home across both competition lines, exacerbating their precarious situation. The recurring problem of being overturned in the Champions League has now spilled over into the league, causing further concern. Benitez admitted post-match that the team lacked confidence: "Our performance in the first half was very good, and we were satisfied. The foul on Gerrard by Gallar should have been a penalty. But the first goal changed everything. Our confidence disappeared, and we started making mistakes. As we conceded the second goal, our opponents grew in confidence while we did the opposite. Now, finishing in the top four remains our target. The road ahead is long, but we must keep moving forward. Even though Aston Villa is already five points ahead of us, there's still plenty of time left in the season, and we will catch up."
