The Mystery of Life

Gentle silk-like rain quietly seeps into the dispersed void, hastily losing its own will, and finally stops; the sun sleeps in its sweet dream, unwilling to wake up yet; mischievous clouds weave into silver-white waves, roaring and surging together like a formidable fortress with great momentum; a rainbow shimmering with seven-color splendor pierces through the vastness and ruggedness of the sky, transforming into an arch bridge resembling a crescent moon spanning the horizon. Its remaining power tears through the lofty blue expanse and falls onto the city covered with misty green light. I smell the life's breath mixed in the wind, carrying a romantic soil taste, surging and swallowing like waves, flowing with a penetrating serenity. The weather becomes ambiguously pleasant. I stand at the highest point of the city center, gazing at the hazy horizon ahead where the green light confuses my vision. Suddenly everything becomes quiet, and I wait on the pinnacle of the dome for a magnificent dream to arrive...

From the horizon, turmoil begins to spread. The shining green light, like an enraged beast, starts to devour the entire gray world. The city, swept by the green tide, is renewed. One piece, two pieces, three pieces... each crystal-clear and emerald leaf floats like down in the air, falling into every corner of the city, like a heavy snowfall. The soft snowflakes gently cover the earth, singing sacred music with their lines of desire, slowly adorning the weary land. At that moment, all the world is enveloped in cheerful green. I stand in the direction of the warm breeze, looking up at the mysterious sky, sincerely praising and praying for the strange change of life. I decide to start my dream journey and recklessly flap my holy dream wings into the radiant green light...

My steps lightly land on the leaves as I push aside the mist and begin walking. Gentle and pleasant warm breezes greet me face-on. What a brand new and wonderful dream world it truly is! Clean streets are lined with flowers and grass of various colors, decorating this side of the city and stretching endlessly far away. The flowers dance and sing amidst the lush trees, colorful and competing for beauty, indeed a grand reunion in the year of prosperity! Red roses, like a woman with pink lips and a smoky allure, wrap her fiery passion shyly within patches of fresh green, pretending to be mysterious; pink peonies, like a queen beautiful beyond compare, display her innate majesty freely spreading across her brows; purple lilacs, they are the embodiment of dreams and melancholy, carrying countless people's vague love dreams; blue tulips, they have a serene love, their beauty becoming gentle and grand in quiet confession. This city has become beautiful due to the flowers' careful decoration, colorful and splendid. I see mist swirling above the flowers, jumping and dripping water droplets between petals, nurturing growth for life. I am already drunk in this dreamlike world woven by the sea of flowers, gradually sinking into oblivion.

Ancient evergreen vines guide my gaze. What kind of symbol is this? Is it the resilience of life, or the perfect mutation after thousands of years of natural selection? They climb along houses, igniting the vitality of the world with their verdant lives. The city turns into a green continent, interwoven with veils of mist. I especially like this city's attire; everywhere there is the green of life, everywhere there is the green of hope. Just like the lonely lampposts entwined with bright green branches, they will tell the green fairies at night about the strange things they've seen; originally cars driving on the road have turned into small rooms with four wooden wheels. People joyfully sing and exchange their strange stories inside these houses, enjoying grapes and wine while heading to different destinations. Fences and trash cans along the way are also wrapped with green lives; short shrubs are singing songs, flower beds echo with laughter, broad beans sleep on billboards, and flyovers immerse themselves in the ivy's embrace dreaming of being stars. You can hear the trumpet calls of morning glories, read the news delivered by hyacinths, and even smell the precious perfume made by jasmine in workshops.

People are bustling around now, gathering under the rainbow to sing happily. Who was the first to immerse themselves in the beautiful nature, using their silhouettes to adorn the tranquil and warm picture? I see children stepping out of the dream castle, passing through the magical Ferris wheel and square avenues, running competitively under the rainbow, becoming another charming sight in the green world. They happily circle around mushroom-shaped houses, cheering for the rainbow in the sky. How they wish they could go up and take a look! What is on the rainbow? Dreams sprout in their young hearts. I can fully read my own childish innocence from them. They love to dream, love to ask curiously under the sunlight. They come up with a plan together! I hear an older child say that long, long ago, there lived a kind old man in the Green Light Forest. He liked people to exchange seeds of love with him through a kind act filled with love. Watered with love's nourishment, those seeds would take root and grow taller and taller, eventually forming a bridge connecting to the rainbow. People could cross it to uncover the secrets of the rainbow and realize their dreams. The children are moved to action. I follow their figures towards the Green Light Forest to find the legendary old man who owns the mysterious seeds.

The children wake up a few dandelions by the roadside, which happily float up and lead the way ahead. The children tightly follow behind the dandelions, heading in the direction they indicate. Along the way, they laugh freely and play with butterflies and dragonflies. They step over a lush meadow, how beautiful it is! They eat wild fruits they've picked and drink dew collected on flowers. They cross a clear stream and wash their hands and faces there. They use the lake as a mirror and wave to distant flying birds. Finally, the meadow disappears, the stream vanishes, and the lake retreats until towering ancient trees appear on both sides. Only then do the children and I snap back to reality. We're almost at the Green Light Forest. The children's courage is truly remarkable. In this world, I genuinely admire them. They don't need to fear tigers and lions in the mountains, spiders and scorpions, crocodiles and great white sharks. They have always been the best friends, coexisting harmoniously in this nature. Since childhood, they have built friendly and harmonious relationships. They deeply understand that this world belongs to all of them, their roots are closely connected, none can live without the other, and none will betray the original covenant!

The children stop, and I see ahead magnificent green light entwined and spilling out from a cluster of robust and winding towering ancient trees. Layer upon layer of light and shadow is like incense smoke inside a room, soft and delicate, dreamlike and enchanting. I enter the forest, as if entering another magical dream. Breathing in its scent, I am finally moved by the transcendent charm of life it emits. Bathed in the baptism of falling leaves, just as the ancient and compassionate voice suddenly echoes from the distant void. Yes, it is a kind and aged voice. Instantly, the soul is coated with a chaotic blankness. I see everything the past world faced, still feeling heart-pounding. My world, what is it like! Suddenly, there is a sound of wings flapping behind me, cutting through the horizon. Is it time to leave? Yes, it's not early anymore. I dimly feel an invisible force pulling me away from this world, slowly drifting away from the Green Light Forest, leaving this world. A nameless sorrow spreads in my heart, a kind of loss I cannot find or cry about. I know here does not belong to me.

Many times in my dreams, I tried hard to look back, trying to see more of that world. I am comforted to see that the children finally found the legendary old man. Each of them received a rare seed of love, planting it in the courtyard and protecting it with love as it grows. One night, those seeds quietly took root and sprouted, growing taller and taller until they reached the sky and connected with the seven-colored rainbow. The children finally agreed to climb the rainbow together to explore its secrets. During the day, they run and draw there, and at night, they play the piano and pick stars there. Before sleeping, they bid farewell to the moon and clouds on the horizon and begin to tell the hardworking adults about their day's experiences. They forever live in a happy and complete world, where dreams take flight. I am so delighted to have once visited such a world. I imagine they will plant more flowers and grass to embellish their world, and more fairies will join their harmonious rhythm. And they will forever uphold the eternal truth of that world, the revelation passed down from the benevolent and aged voice in the Green Light Forest!

Beautiful love ah, it is the mystery of beautiful life!
