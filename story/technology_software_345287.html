On March 29, 2009, the "Third Private Equity Fund Forum" hosted by the Shenzhen Financial Advisors Association and Private Placement Network was held at the Wuzhou Hotel in Shenzhen. Wei Jianping, General Manager of Chengdu Xinxing Venture Capital Co., Ltd., said at the forum that the Growth Enterprise Market (GEM) had been approved by the State Council.

On March 31, 2009, the China Securities Regulatory Commission issued the "Interim Measures for the Administration of Initial Public Offering of Stocks and Listing on the Growth Enterprise Market," which would take effect on May 1, 2009. This meant that the GEM, which had been under preparation for more than a decade, could officially open from May 1, 2009.

The announcement of the GEM IPO rules also indicated that the new stock IPO would soon resume. To prevent the GEM from becoming a "mini-main board," the issuance of new stocks would inevitably divert some funds, which is a negative factor for the A-share market index and blue-chip stocks. Regarding private enterprises, this might psychologically affect market confidence. However, it would be beneficial to venture capital concepts and broker sectors. In summary, with business opportunities online in 2010, the introduction of GEM IPOs would exert pressure on the A-share market and bring adjustment pressure to short-term stock indices.

It should be noted that since the GEM is launched as an independent sector, why economic crises are also considered?
