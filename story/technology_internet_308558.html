Arsenal Wins the Clash of the Declining Powers, Reigniting Championship Hopes

By Xiang Tong

If we compare the top four clubs to The Beatles, then the Liverpool vs Arsenal match could be seen as a contest between George and Ringo (the less famous two members of The Beatles). However, the clash between these declining powers was just as significant. Liverpool needed to reclaim a top-four spot, while Arsenal naturally hoped to take advantage of Chelsea and Manchester United's unfavorable situations in this round, narrowing the gap with the league leaders. Arsenal had previously lost all their matches against strong opponents this season, but last Sunday at Anfield, it was they who emerged victorious.

Arsenal's 2-1 comeback win marked their first league victory at Anfield since 2003. Now, with one fewer game played, Arsenal are only three points behind Manchester United and six points behind league leaders Chelsea, making anything possible. It could be said that both teams played well for half the game each. Arsenal were dominated by the home team in the first half, but their second-half explosion was thanks to Wenger’s halftime motivational speech.

Steven Gerrard's Penalty Claim Unsuccessful

Liverpool now has no more injury excuses to make; apart from Babel, there are no other first-team injuries, and Torres made his first league start in six weeks after overcoming his hernia issues. On the contrary, Arsenal have over half a dozen key players injured, and after using young players in the midweek Champions League match, Wenger fielded his available starting lineup for this game, with Walcott making his first Premier League start in two months.

Liverpool took control early on, not only producing impressive moments like Aurelio's beautiful feint that tricked Nasri, but also creating goal-scoring opportunities quickly. In the 12th minute, Liverpool launched an excellent counterattack through their front two stars. Torres acted as a wall in midfield to send Gerrard flying down the right flank, where he drove into the penalty area and delivered a through ball. Torres received it and attempted a shot, but it lacked sufficient angle and power, allowing Almunia to save it. Just one minute later, Gerrard broke into the box centrally and tried to nutmeg Gael Clichy but was tackled down by him. Despite Gerrard's demand for a penalty, referee Howard Webb ignored it.

In the 21st minute, Liverpool executed smooth play on the right flank, with Kuyt delivering a perfect pass. Glen Johnson entered the box, skillfully dribbling past Denilson and Nasri, but his final cross was disappointing. Excited by the action, Johnson soon unleashed two consecutive long-range shots, and Liverpool's set-piece crosses continuously troubled the opposing penalty area. In the 34th minute, Torres was brought down by Clichy just outside the box, but Webb again chose not to act.

Arsenal paid the price for their set-piece defense problems in the 41st minute. Aurelio's free-kick from 40 yards out reached the box, where Almunia fumbled the catch, allowing Kuyt to chest the ball down and poke it into the net, making it 1-0. This extended Liverpool's club-record streak of scoring in 19 consecutive Premier League home games. Wenger was very dissatisfied with this goal, as Lucas appeared to be offside when jumping to challenge Almunia for the ball.

Two Major Transfers, One Sinner

Among the spectators for this match was a familiar yet unexpected visitor: former Liverpool player Xabi Alonso, who transferred to Real Madrid this summer, returned to Anfield to watch the game. After some British media outlets published pictures, they cynically wrote: "We won't make any jokes about this photo." However, the existing black humor is that Alonso's "replacement," Aquilani, still only has a substitute role, while another significant summer transfer, Glen Johnson, became the sinner for Liverpool's loss by contributing to two goals within eight minutes in the second half.

In the 50th minute, after a series of precise passes by Arsenal, Nasri received a through ball from Fabregas, entering the right side of the box and sending a low cross. The ball deflected slightly off Carragher, and Johnson, in panic, inadvertently blocked the ball into his own net with his left foot, equalizing the score at 1-1. In the 58th minute, Fabregas broke through on the right wing and crossed the ball. Arshavin received it and easily dribbled past the disoriented Johnson before unleashing a powerful shot into the near post, which ricocheted off the inside of the post and into the net, putting Arsenal up 2-1.

Immediately after being leveled, Torres had a chance. Cutting inside from the left, he danced along the baseline into the box, but neither a shooting angle nor a passing route was available, so his attempt was futile. Witnessing the opponent's quick double strike, Gerrard angrily charged down the right flank, kicking Nasri and knocking down Kuyt before Johnson delivered a cross. Unfortunately, Torres' header glanced wide.

Neither side managed to create many genuine chances thereafter. Arshavin's shot from the right side of the box after receiving a pass from Nasri was no trouble for Reina. A frustrated Liverpool made consecutive substitutions, with Aquilani replacing Mascherano amidst applause from Alonso, but it yielded little effect. Arsenal ended their six-match winless streak against the top four.

Liverpool, meanwhile, have only won three of their last 15 matches across all competitions. Since beating Manchester United at the end of October, they've drawn twice and lost twice at home in both competitions, raising concerns. The recurring problem of being overturned in the Champions League has now spread to the league, equally worrying. Benitez admitted post-match that the team's confidence was fragile: "Our performance in the first half was very good, and we were satisfied. The foul by Clichy on Gerrard should have been a penalty. But the first goal changed everything; our confidence waned, and we began making mistakes. As the second goal went in, the opponent's confidence grew while ours diminished. Now, finishing in the top four remains our goal, though it's a long road ahead, we must keep moving forward. Even though Aston Villa already leads us by five points, there's still plenty of time until the end of the league, and we will catch up."
