The way home is bound to have rain.

by anonymous on 2012-03-09 08:57:49

Climbing is a good way to exercise, but I don't like climbing because it's too tiring. Instead, I prefer walking. It takes about ten minutes on foot from where I live to my workplace. I go to and from work four times a day, which is a great opportunity for exercise. During normal days, if I can walk, I will definitely not take a vehicle. Besides walking regularly, I also like to walk to beautiful places to exercise my body. On weekdays, I walk from Jingning Road to the south, turn left at Qingyang Road, walk forward a bit, pass the Lanzhou Museum, and then continue forward a little more to reach my office. The office is on the fifth floor without an elevator, so climbing the stairs is another chance for exercise.

The first time I went for a long walk was during the first weekend after arriving in Lanzhou. At that time, I was staying at a hotel in the east square. I walked north along Jinchang Road until I reached Nan Binhe Road. Then I continued west along Binhe Road. In winter, the water level in the river was relatively low, and the sun shone brightly on the riverbed. It felt great. While enjoying the view of the river, I could feel the gentle swaying of the willows by the riverbank, accompanied by the melodious sounds of Qinqiang Opera echoing across the water. This experience was very pleasant. I kept walking forward, passing Zhongshan Bridge, and continued until I reached the statue of Mother Yellow River. Along this journey, I enjoyed the scenery of Lanzhou while walking slowly, which took me about an hour. From the statue of Mother Yellow River, I took a bus to Xiguan, and then walked back to where I was staying.

I increasingly enjoy walking. In the winter of 2010, after a snowfall, I walked from where I lived to Wentai Mountain. I walked from Jingning Road to Zhangye Road, passed through Zhongshan Bridge, continued along Bei Binhe Road until I reached the back gate of White Pagoda Mountain. Coming down from White Pagoda Mountain, I returned along the same route. Strictly speaking, climbing Red Pagoda Mountain doesn't really count as climbing because it's too low. That day, I left home at around nine and didn't return until two in the afternoon, skipping lunch altogether. Walking after a snowfall feels especially nice.

On a winter day in 2011, I started from the General Hospital of Lanzhou Army in Qilihe District, walking along the South Binhe Road beside the Yellow River all the way back to where I stayed. At that time, it was the afternoon with excellent sunshine. When I reached the island in the middle of the Yellow River, it was full of reeds. Walking through the reeds felt like entering a utopia. That time, I walked all the way to Xiguan, and then continued walking until I reached the side of Jingning Road and Gan'nan Road. Especially near the water of the Yellow River, I tried my best to feel the harmony between heaven and earth. Under the warm sunlight of the winter afternoon, walking slowly by the river felt particularly good.

During weekends, I usually climb a mountain one day and walk the other. I've been to Baiyun Temple a few times. I walked from where I stayed to Xiguan, then turned south and north again to reach Baiyun Temple. Baiyun Temple is a famous Taoist temple in Lanzhou, well-known for its divine fortune-telling sticks. Inside the temple, there are also places like the Wenchang Palace. The ancient trees inside make it a sacred place for Taoism.

Walking in the rain has a unique charm. One time when I went to Jiuzhou Terrace, it started drizzling when I left home, and I didn't bring an umbrella. By the time I reached the foot of the mountain, the rain became heavier, and I hesitated whether to continue. After taking a few steps up, I decided not to proceed further and returned quickly. Fortunately, I came down in time, as the rain indeed became heavier later. Although it was raining on my way back, I still walked slowly. Starting from Lan Ya Group on North Binhe Road, I continued walking forward, crossed Zhongshan Bridge, and reached Buzou Street, then to the provincial government. The rain never stopped, and by the time I reached the provincial government, the rain had become even heavier. Just then, an empty taxi passed by, so I took it back home.

The greatest advantage of walking is that it's not exhausting. Of course, the exercise effect cannot compare with climbing mountains. But for someone lazy like me, it’s still quite appealing.
