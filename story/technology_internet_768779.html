Who's Next to Replace Google?

by anonymous on 2013-11-22 11:51:00

"After 20 years, my predictions today will seem trivial." This was a statement made by Kevin Kelly, the founding editor of Wired magazine and author of Out of Control, during a speech at Sogou today.

Kelly has visited China many times and has repeatedly emphasized in public that he learns a lot every time he comes to China because he believes that China is creating the future.

Prediction Error 20 Years Ago: Ignored the Value Created by Users

Kelly is often hailed as a prophet, but he began his speech by telling everyone about a major prediction error he made regarding the internet 20 years ago. At that time, Kelly predicted in Wired that the internet would be similar to 5000 channels, each occupied by various content providers, allowing users to use it like a television from their computers.

Kelly pointed out that his mistake was ignoring the value created by users, which was self-established by the internet itself. Therefore, today, computers are not televisions.

Who Will Be the Next to Replace Google?

Kelly reviewed the changes in technological development at the scene, from ships to steamships, printers to computers... He summarized: "Disruptive products were initially low-quality, high-risk, low-profit, small-market, and unproven."

Microsoft used software to defeat the hardware company IBM, and Google used search and services to overcome Microsoft. So, who will surpass Google next?

Kelly said that large companies are busy pursuing profits while neglecting innovation. Thus, innovation always originates from small companies. When a company does not focus on profit margins and lowers efficiency, its growth curve declines. However, in reality, before achieving greater growth, the company must have experienced a trough and ultimately gained new competitiveness through innovation.

Perhaps the next company capable of replacing Google is currently a small technology on the fringes, maybe cloud storage application DropBox.

What Will the Future Become?

Kelly has long been regarded as a spokesperson and observer of "network culture," and his predictions about IT and the internet are highly favored by various internet leaders, such as Pony Ma.

In Out of Control, he documented his exploration of the cutting-edge technology, society, and economy of the time in 1994, as well as the glimpse of the future he obtained. This time, coming to China, Kelly brought more of his views on the future.

He pointed out that the changes of the past 20 years will be much smaller compared to the changes of the next 20 years, with rapid development exceeding current imagination. Kelly listed several technologies that will soon become trends:

1. Bodily tracking data will become increasingly comprehensive and intelligent, forming a big data set for body tracking;

2. Holographic images will become smarter in serving people, generating content based on where a person's eyes are focused;

3. Various flexible screens may even allow people to wear screens like clothing;

4. He emphasized that 3D printing technology is very important for China. Combining 3D printing with robotics will produce disruptive effects;

5. Humanity will deeply rely on AI (artificial intelligence), enabling autonomous driving for cars and planes, making them safer and more efficient;

6. Crowdfunding will commonly support innovative product financing;

7. Privacy will become more secure. Passwords will disappear within 10 years, and biometric traits will be used for identity verification.

Finally, Kelly concluded by saying that all these will be realized within 20 years, but the biggest disruption 20 years from now will not be any of the things Kelly predicted today.
