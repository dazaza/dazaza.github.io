Lonely City

I have been living in this city for a very, very long time.

Ever since I can remember, I've never left here. I don't know how many stations there are in the neighboring city, whether there are small shops like the fruit store my aunt runs downstairs, or if the stray cats there attack people. But I understand this city better than anyone else. I know that every morning at exactly six forty-five, all the streetlights in the city dim, no matter how bright the summer dawn is or how dark the winter mornings are; I know which back alleys belong to the stray cats and which dead ends house a lame old wild dog; I know which building has the best quality exterior tiles...

No one knows this city as well as I do, even though it's getting old.

I have memories and stories about this city that no one else has, so I can tell you everything about this city, only I can.

But no one ever asks me about the stories of this city, no one asks me what this city looked like ten, twenty, or even thirty years ago. They don't trust me.

Just like I've never trusted this city.

I'm old, and I don't want to take these memories with me into the grave. These memories are too vivid, too bright, and I'm afraid they'll blind me in the dark underworld. So, child, let me tell you everything about this city. Everything.

This is a city that is incredibly cold yet incredibly passionate. Every year, month, day, hour, minute, she accepts any kind of life: newborn babies, migrant workers moving from far away, travelers constantly changing locations, and all forms of life from nature. She is so small, yet she keeps accepting people endlessly. People think she is the most lively city in the world, but once they step onto her soil, they realize she is the coldest city in the world. When she extends her hand, her face is filled with disgust and hatred. She embraces other creatures while cursing nature's creation as the root cause of the end of the world. In reality, she has never accepted anyone.

Child, even I, even as her priest, have never been accepted by her. She is a skilled liar, deceiving everyone and then creating a sinful situation.

But, she is lonely. Did you hear the cry of the leaves falling yesterday morning? We usually don't hear such faint sounds, but yesterday morning, that cry was especially loud, waking me up from my sleep. I broke out in a sweat. I thought the city was going to collapse suddenly, although I had imagined her collapse countless times before - I was still startled. It turned out she was pinching her own skin; the cobblestone road at the end of the street suddenly turning purplish-black was her bruise.

She cried. She said she felt sad. Autumn will tell people that this city has already declined.

But, child, I won't be deceived. I won't believe her anymore. Every time, she deceives me, taking parts of my body, then mocking me, laughing at my foolishness...

Child, did you hear? Did you hear my story?
