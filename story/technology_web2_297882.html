10-Year-Old Donates 700,000 Yuan Worth of Charitable Contributions to Sichuan Disaster Area for Birthday Banquet

by 6itt959o8d on 2009-12-07 15:20:52

On November 28, a 10-year-old elementary school student from Nanjing announced at her birthday banquet that she would donate all the more than 200,000 yuan in cash gifts received that evening to the disaster areas of Sichuan. She also donated ten thousand sets of customized cotton clothes and quilts that her parents had spent nearly 500,000 yuan on. It was reported that this elementary school student had once donated 398,000 yuan of her lucky money to build a kindergarten in Sichuan and had visited Sichuan five times to show her love. The "little birthday girl," Ai'ai, made her birthday wish. Guests enjoyed themselves at the birthday banquet.

According to the report, the grand prize at the birthday banquet held on the evening of November 28 at Daji Resort Village in Nanjing was three cars, along with a series of other prizes such as jade jewelry and laptops. This 10-year-old elementary school student's birthday banquet shocked the attending guests and her 37 classmates. However, what truly astonished the guests was not just these prizes but also the decision made by the "little birthday girl" at the banquet: to donate all the more than 200,000 yuan in cash gifts received that evening to the disaster areas of Sichuan. Simultaneously, she also donated ten thousand sets of cotton clothes and quilts that her parents had spent nearly 500,000 yuan on, which were to be sent to the earthquake-stricken areas of Sichuan.

The protagonist of the birthday banquet was Ai'ai, a second-grade student from a primary school in Baidia District of Nanjing. Thirty-seven classmates attended the banquet, each accompanied by their parents. Ms. Lu, the homeroom teacher, said that due to health reasons, Ai'ai had been absent from school for some time, and her classmates missed her very much. Knowing it was her birthday, many of them prepared small gifts for her. "There were magic shows, performances by young singers..." Yesterday, the classmates were still excited when talking about Ai'ai's birthday banquet.

"What was surprising was the lottery draw segment, where three cars were given away as the grand prize!" A guest revealed: "The banquet was estimated to cost over a million yuan, with numerous awards. The first and second prizes were jade jewelry, the third prize was ten laptops, and the winning rate was quite high." Ai'ai then made a touching speech that left the students and their parents even more astonished: "My parents have always taught me to learn how to share with others. I also hope that I can bring happiness and joy to others. People often say 'to give is to receive.' If I have to choose one character between 'give' and 'receive,' I would choose 'give,' because only by giving can we gain."

Ai'ai donated all the more than 200,000 yuan in cash gifts received at the birthday banquet and encouraged her parents to donate nearly 500,000 yuan worth of cotton quilts and clothes to be sent to the earthquake-stricken areas of Sichuan.

This lavish birthday banquet, costing a fortune, also sparked many people's speculation and discussion. A student told the reporter that Ai'ai's family must be very wealthy, and the birthday banquet was too luxurious! Another parent said that Ai'ai's family runs a business, they are well-off, and their emphasis on educating their child about love and charity shows that the parents are far-sighted. Ai'ai's mother said in an interview yesterday that this was a very private gathering, and all expenses were borne by the family. "We turned it into a charity-themed parent-child activity and presented it as a birthday gift to the child. We don't want our child to 'lie' on wealth; we've educated her from a young age to understand dedication. She once donated 398,000 yuan of her lucky money to build a kindergarten in Sichuan and has visited Sichuan five times to show her love."

Professor Xu Xiang, a sociology doctor from Nanjing Normal University, believes that now there are more and more "rich second-generation" individuals, and how to educate children who grow up in "sugar water" is a big problem. The educational concept of Ai'ai's parents is commendable. Giving material wealth to children is not important; what is important is teaching them to understand love and dedication from a young age. Of course, holding such a lavish birthday banquet under the guise of charity and showing love is debatable, as it may lead to competitive mentality among classmates. Moreover, showing love does not necessarily require such a grand occasion. However, Professor Xu also believes that we should view this matter with tolerance. Nowadays, social concepts are diversified, and wealth is stratified, making it difficult to measure an event with a single standard. Acting within one's means and focusing on social effects should be the starting point for doing things for children.

Relevant Views: The Focus of "Rich Second-Generation" Education is "Being Human"

Educating the "rich second-generation" is a fresh topic for both their parents and educators. Associate Professor Bu Lin from the Department of Sociology at Nanjing University of Technology believes that their education should primarily focus on the education of "humanity." Therefore, it is necessary to start from the details and gradually guide them to establish a peaceful mindset through daily educational carriers, dispelling the notion of being superior, and learning to be "human" to interact equally with others and society.

The most significant challenge in educating the "rich second-generation" is the family challenge. In many wealthy families, parents are extremely busy and rarely have time to communicate with their children. Another challenge is the societal challenge. The "rich second-generation" often have excessive pocket money and over-supplied clothing, food, and transportation, making it hard for them to find close friends among their peers.

Bu Lin said that educating the "rich second-generation" is not an easy task. In the West, the rich never hesitate to indulge their children materially, but while providing a superior material life, they emphasize shaping the children's civic awareness, independent spirit, and social responsibility at all times. This is something China can learn from. Not all "rich second-generation" individuals have problems like flaunting wealth, being capricious, or impulsive. Jiangsu is a province with a large number of private enterprises, and many companies are now at the stage of cultivating successors. Bu Lin mentioned that only by enabling the "rich second-generation" to establish a positive and healthy civic consciousness from a young age, understanding love and responsibility, will they possibly integrate into society in the future and master the ability to "create wealth."

Journalist Connection: "Let the Child Understand Giving and Sharing"

Dialogue with Ai'ai's Mother

Why did the parents decide to hold such a luxurious and special birthday banquet for the child? Yesterday afternoon, the reporter had a conversation with Ai'ai's mother.

Passing on Love Through Activities

Q: Why did you organize such a birthday banquet?

A: We hoped to use this event to pass on the message of love. I often teach my child to understand giving and sharing, to take social responsibility seriously, and to help those in need. But now I find that many parents are used to giving the best things to their children first, and the children think that is normal. Even the elderly in the family are not respected, which I believe is a problem in education.

Child is Not Unaware of Money

Q: Ai'ai comes from a family business, lives comfortably, and has reportedly donated 398,000 yuan of her lucky money to build a kindergarten in the disaster area. Is it because she doesn't fully understand the concept of money?

A: Actually, Ai'ai has a good understanding of money. When calculating the cash gifts received at the birthday banquet, amounting to more than 200,000 yuan, she immediately started planning how to use the money to buy things to help others. This money will be used to purchase medicines, nutritional supplements, books, cotton shoes, etc., to be delivered to villages near Mianyang, close to Yingxiu in Sichuan. Additionally, we originally ordered 10,000 sets of cotton quilts and clothes, but due to supply issues, we currently have 2,500 sets of quilts and bedding and 7,200 sets of cotton clothes, which are already being transported to Sichuan by company staff. The child's actions actually stem from her nature. For example, on the fifth day after the Sichuan earthquake, Ai'ai went to Sichuan with me. The child fully understands what she is doing, so she solemnly took out all her lucky money from childhood and donated it to the disaster area, building a kindergarten on the Yingxiu dam. This kindergarten has now started enrollment, and the child is very happy. She told me, "I am a small molecule, and I will play my role as a molecule." I was deeply moved when I heard this.

Enrolling the Child in an Ordinary School Deliberately

Q: I heard that the child's health is not good. Are you particularly indulgent towards her?

A: When Ai'ai was three and a half years old, she went to the hospital due to persistent high fever, but unexpectedly, she was diagnosed with leukemia. Our family only has this girl, and this news was like a bolt from the blue for us; I could hardly believe it at the time. However, Ai'ai is strong and gave us faith. During the bone marrow puncture, the little Ai'ai curled up in my arms, sweating profusely from the pain, but she refused to cry. Later, she told the doctor that if she cried, her mother would cry. This child is very sensible and hasn't cried loudly since she got sick. Sometimes when the pain becomes unbearable, she cries quietly in her blanket. After enduring a long and painful treatment, Ai'ai's disease is now basically cured, but due to medication, there are still some aftereffects. When the weather gets cold, her cerebral blood vessels tend to spasm, so she cannot go to school temporarily. However, Ai'ai is not idle; she frequently goes to nursing homes and children's welfare institutes to volunteer. After the Sichuan earthquake, Ai'ai has already visited Sichuan five times. Every time she goes, she helps with work, not afraid of hardship or fatigue. Most importantly, she finds joy in giving. We hope that the child can receive equal and loving education. Therefore, we abandoned the idea of sending her to an international school and chose this ordinary but well-atmosphered school instead.

Meaning Behind the Birthday Banquet Prizes

Q: I heard that the prizes included cars, jade jewelry, and laptops. Why set such expensive prizes?

A: For many people, such prizes might seem expensive, but what we value is not their monetary worth but their symbolic meaning. For instance, the grand prize is three cars, symbolizing progress. It represents that life's road will encounter many obstacles, but when faced with barriers, cars do not stop moving forward and give up; instead, they refuel, repair, and continue moving forward bravely. Our first and second prizes are jade jewelry. Why set two levels of awards? This also has its meaning. Jade is not ordinary stone; it undergoes countless trials, but the final quality varies. This symbolizes that life will also experience hardships. Sometimes, despite efforts, one may not achieve the desired results, and one must face life with a calm heart. The third prize is laptops, and the fourth prize is Parker gold pens, symbolizing the importance of studying diligently. The fifth prize is Yixing purple clay, as we do not encourage children to drink coffee or cola but hope they drink tea, which is fragrant and symbolizes tranquility and detachment. The sixth prize is MP4 players, reminding us that life contains beautiful songs, and we should love life.

Q: For ordinary families, this birthday banquet might seem extravagant and even invite criticism. How do you consider this?

A: We turned it into a charity-themed parent-child activity as a birthday gift for the child. On one hand, the child could not attend school due to health reasons but yearned to go to school and meet her classmates. On the other hand, we have always been low-key in our actions and do not wish to publicize them excessively.
