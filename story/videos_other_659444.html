The period drama *Ruyi*, starring Yang Mi, Liu Kaiwei, Liu Xuehua, Yu Xiaofan, Zhang ChenGuang, and others, is currently airing on Hunan TV's Golden Mango Exclusive Broadcast Theater. Since its premiere, the show has achieved a prime-time viewership rating of 1.21 and has maintained high ratings. The complex and tumultuous fates of the characters in the drama have left audiences emotionally invested, alternately happy and sad, with many commenting that it’s heart-wrenching.

In the series, Yang Mi, known for her sweet and clever screen persona, takes on the role of a tragic heroine for the first time. Her frequent tearful performances in poignant scenes have earned praise from many viewers who find her character touching and commend her acting skills, saying, "I didn’t expect her to be able to handle such a role."

Set against the backdrop of the tea-making rivalry between two prominent families in southern Jiangnan, *Ruyi* tells the story of a tea-picking girl named Ruyi (played by Yang Mi) and her enduring but fraught love with Tan Mingkai (played by Liu Kaiwei), the eldest son of the Tan family.

As the character Ruyi evolves from an innocent tea picker to becoming the daughter-in-law of a wealthy household, she undergoes numerous hardships, including being disfigured and becoming a mother through tragedy. This makes her a deeply heavy and sorrowful role. Speaking about her character, Yang Mi humorously notes that this role was absolutely a new challenge for her because her character’s state and temperament span a wide range, and there are many crying scenes. "Actually, I’m quite good at crying scenes, but while playing Ruyi, I often cried from morning to night. Sometimes, by the last scene, I really couldn’t cry anymore, so I had to think of sad things. Moreover, Ruyi has gone through a lot, and the feeling of crying differs depending on her situation. For example, when she was a teenager and felt wronged, it might have been a simple release, but later, after experiencing so much, her crying took on a different meaning because her mindset had changed. I worked hard to understand these differences, and sometimes I was exhausted from crying," said Yang Mi.

Yang Mi has previously appeared as lively and cheerful on screen, so why did she choose to challenge herself with such a heavy role? Yang Mi laughed and said she wanted to push herself: "I hope that through my efforts, everyone can see that I’m not just capable of playing those giggly roles; I can also handle heavier ones. Little girls grow up too."

Yang Mi hopes to win over the audience with her tears, and the audience has responded positively to her performance. Many viewers praised Yang Mi, saying her tears make her seem pitiable and endearing. One online commenter wrote: "Every time I see Ruyi’s loneliness, disappointment, and tears, I feel heartbroken." Another netizen praised Yang Mi, saying her breakthrough in this role was significant: "I didn’t expect her to be able to handle such a role. Her acting skills have improved a lot!"

Despite their public denials, the chemistry between Yang Mi and Liu Kaiwei in the drama has sparked rumors of a real-life romance. During an appearance on *Happy Camp*, Liu Kaiwei once joked about the rumors, saying, "I’m single, and I’ve heard she (Yang Mi) doesn’t have a boyfriend either, so if rumors spread, it’s not bad." When contacted by phone during the show, Yang Mi admitted under persistent questioning from the host that Liu Kaiwei was very considerate and even hinted that among her male co-stars, there might be her "true love." Unlike her previous responses to rumors about her relationship with Feng Shaofeng, Yang Mi’s attitude towards the "Liu-Kaiwei-Yang-Mi" romance remained ambiguous, fueling further speculation.

Through her portrayal of Ruyi, Yang Mi aims to demonstrate her versatility as an actress, showing that she can handle both lighthearted and serious roles.
