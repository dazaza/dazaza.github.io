A layer of red mist over the pastries

by anonymous on 2012-03-09 08:59:02

My body, trembling with fear, retreated a few steps involuntarily. I turned around and closed the door. Outside came the sound of screams. Everyone has gone mad, that was not Miao Wei at all, but more like a walking corpse. His expression, his eyes were like a starving wolf for a long time. Huang Hua Dong who was there clipping his toenails also heard the sound outside and looked at me suspiciously. I didn't want to explain anything, went back to my seat, looked at the dark sky outside, and felt that the world had become chaotic. The voices outside mixed and soon quieted down. Huang Hua Dong looked at me as if he was seeing a monster. Then out of curiosity, he tried to open the dormitory door. I wanted to stop him but found myself drenched in cold sweat on my back, and my body began to ache again.

The door opened, and I saw many people gathered outside. The bloodstains on the ground spoke volumes. I struggled to move step by step towards the door. I saw several people running out covering their wounds, and lying on the ground between the crowd was someone covered in blood, completely dyeing his clothes red. That person was clearly Miao Wei. Not far away, Yang Jian Xiang stood there motionless, holding a blood-stained chair in his hand, and his ankle was bleeding profusely. However, he seemed to feel no pain at all, his face full of horror, his eyes fixed on Miao Wei.

Huang Hua Dong pulled Tang Fei from the crowd and asked what happened. Tang Fei, half-stunned, replied, "Miao Wei... I don't know why he started biting people. Several people have been bitten by him. In a panic, Yang Jian Xiang grabbed the chair to try to knock him out, but he misjudged the force, and it ended up like this."

"Move aside," said the dorm supervisor and several security guards as they separated the onlookers. None of them could believe what they were seeing. Within such a short time, two bloody incidents occurred.

"Hey, Hey, there's been a bloodshed at Linjiang Garden, come over and help."

"De Xing Garden has a few students who went crazy, come over."

The security guard’s radio kept buzzing. "This..." the security guard stood there helplessly, and the dorm supervisor, in an emergency, dialed 110, but the line was busy!!! With 110 so occupied, has the whole world gone mad? We students stood there dumbfounded, vaguely sensing that something big had happened.

The dorm supervisor dialed 110 a few more times and finally managed to get through. She reported the situation at school to the police station, and they should be coming to support us soon. For now, we could only treat the wounded and send them to the hospital.

The phone in my pocket kept vibrating, and it took me a while to realize it. It seems my spirit has been devoured by bugs until it's unbearable, even my nerves have weakened significantly. But why haven't I seen any blood seeping through my clothes? Could these black bugs also suck blood? I really don't want to take off my clothes to see my battered self; let it be, live day by day.

"Mom, what's the matter?" I answered the phone.

"Child, are you alright? Your father and I watched the news. There are people biting others everywhere in the country now. An incident also happened at your father's school yesterday. Is everything fine where you are?"

"Mom, nothing is wrong. I'm in a very remote place, and nothing has happened here yet. I feel like something bad is about to happen. You and dad should go downtown." I replied. I've always felt unfilial, never calling home, always waiting for my parents to call. And now, with my semi-disabled body, I probably can't fulfill my filial duty anymore. The only thing I can do is not let my parents worry.

"Child, come back. Buy a ticket and fly back now. We'll feel safer with you by our side."

"Mom, really, nothing is wrong. You've seen the news. Tickets are hard to get. Besides, I've been an adult for years, I'll be fine. If anything happens, how could I hide it from you? That's all. I have something to do, I'll hang up now." As soon as I finished speaking, I quickly pressed the end button, my eyes moist. I was afraid that if I continued to answer the phone, I would reveal my own situation. Rather than letting them worry endlessly, I'd rather face my fate alone.

In the distance, police cars raced toward us. Many people poked their heads out to watch. This wasn't the first time we'd seen police cars. I thought a large group of policemen would come to handle the scene, but only two showed up. One was taking notes, and the other explained to the dorm supervisor and security guards, "Crime is happening everywhere now. All the police have been dispatched. The police station has already detained a large number of people, and they cannot arrest suspects anymore. We hope the school can set aside a place to separately detain student suspects." The dorm supervisor understood and immediately contacted the school authorities. The students dispersed, many contacting their families, though more packed their luggage, hoping to return home as soon as possible.

I sat alone on the balcony, gazing at the night sky. The sky was still pitch black, and I had already gotten used to living without sunlight. The frequency of body pain gradually decreased, but I still lacked the courage to face my own body. Now, the only pain was in my abdomen, occasionally excruciating, as if it was splitting apart. The number of people attending class today was significantly reduced, even teachers took leave and left the school. Everyone was enveloped by fear, none knew when this darkness would end.

I swallowed a mouthful of saliva, realizing everyone's mouths were cracked, and stomachs were growling incessantly. I had just eaten dinner half an hour ago, and surprisingly, I ate all meat. Yet, I was hungry again. I had a strong urge to bite someone, but I knew I couldn't do it. Miao Wei's face kept appearing in my mind; he resembled a thousand-year-old zombie, feeding on humans. I didn't want to turn into him because... I felt it wouldn't be cool.

I walked aimlessly out of the dormitory. Looking back, I saw Cheng Wei and Huang Hua Dong talking on the phone. They didn't notice the abnormality in my eyes at that moment. This was the best time for me to act, just one bite, to taste the flavor of human flesh. Just one bite was enough, and saliva was uncontrollably flowing out.

I slapped myself across the face, hating what I was thinking, then turned and left. On the way out, I met Tang Fei, the simpleton. He thought I was going out to buy food and kept encouraging me to invite guests. I ignored him and kept walking silently. I passed Jin Di Yuan, the supermarket, missed the canteen, passed the fourth teaching building, and finally stopped at the sixth dormitory. All along, I kept thinking about my future, feeling only a bleak path ahead.

When I reached the sixth dormitory, I suddenly felt a chill. I saw the gate closed, locked with chains, and through the glass, I could see people wandering inside. But for some reason, I felt everything was abnormal.

Tang Fei followed my gaze and explained, "The sixth dormitory is now used to detain those who bite people. However, it seems like those who are delirious have also been thrown out."

"What about the original residents of the sixth dormitory?" I asked.

"They've been relocated to the school's hotel."

I said before, my curiosity has always been heavy, and this time was no exception. I approached the glass door of the sixth dormitory to see the situation inside clearly. However, due to the heat, a layer of red mist covered the inside, and I could only vaguely see a few shadows moving around. I had to press against the glass to look inside.

Suddenly, a face appeared right in front of me, scaring me to fall to the ground. My heart rate increased significantly. The face was so flushed, eyes protruding, and the mouth opening and closing, drool streaming down the corner of his mouth onto the glass door.

I took a deep breath, calming myself down. I thought it was a ghost, which scared me greatly, but this guy's appearance was almost indistinguishable from a ghost. I wanted to turn and leave, but his face remained stuck to the glass door, his eyes staring at me without moving, continuously pounding on the glass.

At this point, my heartbeat returned to normal. I leaned against the glass door and said to the guy inside, "I know you want to come out, but what you did before was wrong, why bite people? If you hadn't bitten people, you wouldn't be confined here. If you weren't confined here, you wouldn't have the idea of wanting to come out. If you didn't have the idea of coming out, you wouldn't want to break the glass. Wukong, I know you miss your master dearly, but doing this is wrong."

I intended to continue lecturing him, but suddenly the situation changed. More figures moved towards the door, and more faces appeared on the glass door. Their appearances were basically the same, eyes staring straight at me, continuously pounding on the glass. I felt uneasy under their gaze.

Everyone inside was insane, and it was best for me to leave quickly. Tang Fei, who had been chattering nonstop, was clearly also frightened and followed me away from the sixth dormitory.

I stopped after walking a few steps and asked Tang Fei, "Are you sure all those who are delirious and have the tendency to bite people are detained inside?"

Tang Fei wasn't quite sure, saying, "It should be."

"Then look at the people passing by us."

Before, I was contemplating my future and didn't notice any abnormalities. But now, I realized something was off with the people around me. Among those who passed by me, there were always one or two who looked lifeless, like drunkards stumbling forward. Moreover, there were always a few whose exaggerated twisting movements caught my attention. The people around seemed numb, and no one noticed those abnormalities.

Tang Fei also noticed this, and his uneasy mood was evident on his face. I told Tang Fei, "I think we'd better go back to the dormitory for safety." Tang Fei immediately agreed, and the two of us ran back to the dormitory like wild horses let loose.

The dormitory was like a utopia, unaffected by the chaos outside. Cheng Wei and Huang Hua Dong were still on the phone. I was panting heavily, adjusted myself, and fell asleep immediately. As I drifted off, I recalled the scenes I saw at the sixth dormitory. Their eyes seemed increasingly strange, lacking the characteristics that human eyes should possess. Moreover, when they opened and closed their mouths, no warm breath came out, otherwise, how could I not see the glass becoming foggy? Could it be... No, it must be me overthinking. They were clearly still moving, so they must still be alive. Anyway, I might as well sleep first.

A scream tore through the silent night sky, accompanied by a beast-like roar. I woke up startled by these unexpected sounds. By then, the dormitory lights were already on, and Huang Hua Dong and Cheng Wei were getting dressed, apparently ready to go check out what had happened.

Judging by the time shown, it should be close to midnight in the morning. It was still dark outside, and we had completely lost track of time. The three of us rushed to the bottom of the dormitory building. At the bottom, there was chaos. More specifically, three pale-faced people were arrogantly chasing a large group of people. At the entrance, the dorm supervisor lay in a pool of blood, being torn apart by two individuals. Large chunks of flesh were bitten off, blood splashing from the wounds, instantly covering the glass door with fresh red blood.

Seeing this scene, I was utterly shocked. Were they cannibals? So bloody! Or perhaps, were they the zombies described in previous movies? Tang Fei and others were also caught up in the chaos. A zombie chased after Tang Fei relentlessly, cornering him. In a moment of desperation, Tang Fei smashed the nearby fire window, pulled out a fire extinguisher, and swung it hard at the zombie from behind. The zombie's head was deformed and fell to the ground. Even more confirming my thoughts, the zombie didn't bleed a single drop of blood.

Not far away, two other zombies tackled two students, and the outcome was predictable. Those two zombies bit down mercilessly like beasts. Surrounding classmates simply avoided them from afar, completely unwilling to attempt rescuing the victims.

A thought flashed through my mind. If zombies attacked me, I would likely become their prey. I needed to find a tough person nearby, at least someone who could deal with these zombies. Of the people I knew, Xiao Qin was undoubtedly the best choice. She should be at Linjiang Garden, a bit far from here. Given the situation here, it was likely similar elsewhere. It would be difficult for me to reach her alone. Additionally, two zombies were eating the deceased dorm supervisor at the entrance, blocking the way completely. I needed to find a way to break out.

I called out to Tang Fei and Wan Fa Xian nearby, "You guys quickly take out the fire hydrant and use its powerful water pressure to destroy these zombies. Otherwise, none of us will survive."

Fang Xiaochun also came to help. The two held the fire hydrant while another spun the switch, and a powerful stream of water shot out. However, the recoil of the water pressure was considerable, and the two lacked the strength to control it properly. The hose twisted uncontrollably, making it impossible to aim accurately.

Eventually, the originally hiding classmates also came to help. With a large group of people, they managed to control the hose. The powerful water stream sprayed towards the surrounding zombies like a dragon. Zombies that came into contact with the water were squeezed out of the door, clearing the obstruction at the entrance. However, shortly afterward, the crowd fell into chaos again, everyone fleeing for their lives. With no one controlling the hose, the powerful recoil threw Wan Fa Xian and Fang Xiaochun to the side. Tang Fei had no choice but to shut off the switch, ending the water flow.

There were originally five zombies at the entrance. Two were pushed out by the water pressure, one was knocked down, and two entered the crowd just moments ago. Two more people became victims. Wan Fa Xian and Fang Xiaochun seemed injured badly. Huang Hua Dong and Cheng Wei fled far away. Between me and them were those two zombies. Only Tang Fei was relatively near me. I grabbed Tang Fei to run outside, but he pulled me back, panicking and asking, "It doesn't look safe outside. Are we just going out to die?"

I firmly responded to him, "Staying here is suicide. Follow me, it's the right decision." Without further argument, I pulled him and ran. Initially reluctant, he eventually followed me.

Outside, the lighting was dim. We could see two zombies just pushed out by the water pressure leaning against the opposite dormitory building wall. The roads were already in chaos, with zombies chasing and tearing apart people everywhere. However, among them, there were still a few zombies wandering on the road without actively attacking the humans fleeing past them. Following a few loud bangs, several vehicles exploded nearby. Thick black smoke rose slowly, and the huge flames illuminated everything around. Blood was everywhere, and bodies littered the ground. We tried our best to avoid crowded areas, or roads with many fleeing people, and didn't see zombies chasing us. We passed through the fourth teaching building, with only one destination in mind: Linjiang Garden.

Just as we exited the fourth teaching building, we felt a gust of wind beside us. Something fell from the sky, colliding strongly with the floor. Turning my head, I saw a sight I would never forget— the corpse had turned into a pile of meat mush, limbs completely twisted, and blood flowed continuously outward.

Looking up, I saw a classroom window wide open. A student had fallen beside the window, and another, seemingly a teacher-turned-zombie, was tearing the corpse with large bites. Glass shards kept falling. I felt something sticky on my face, warm and wet. Wiping it with my hand, I realized this dark red liquid could only be blood.

I knew my time was running out. This area would soon become a land of death. Linjiang Garden was roughly a street away from here, and we had to pass by the sixth dormitory, possibly facing a group of zombies. Regardless, I decided to go there first since it was the nearest route.

What has happened to this world? How could such a dramatic change occur so suddenly? This doesn't seem like a biochemical infection. What exactly is the cause? Humanity's days seem numbered, and everything is inexplicable. I urgently need someone to resolve all my doubts, but does anyone even know what's going on?

Sure enough, the street leading to the sixth dormitory was already occupied by zombies. At the other end of this street was Linjiang Garden. I had a strong premonition that once I found Xiao Qin, I wouldn't have to fear these zombies anymore. Thinking about it, it sounded funny—a grown man needing protection from a little girl. Well, this world has already turned upside down, survival is the key.

Just as I was at a loss, an unexpected situation occurred. A sound resembling a wolf's howl came from somewhere around the fifth or sixth teaching buildings. Surprisingly, upon hearing this sound, the zombies began moving in that direction. Only a few zombies remained, wandering aimlessly like headless flies. The difficulty had significantly decreased.

Tang Fei tapped my shoulder, and following his pointed direction, I saw a tricycle. There was nothing else to rely on now—it was our only chance to break through this street. No words needed, let's go!
