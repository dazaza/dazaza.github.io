Who would want their child to live the same life as us, watching our parents save every penny so that we can finish university in the US [http://www.yzteb.com/], only to face unemployment after graduation, then having no choice but to leave home for a meager salary, working as laborers in the world's factory, renting houses, living a life without quality, letting them consume toxic milk powder, paying sponsorship fees to attend school...

A couple doing business in Beijing doesn't have a Beijing hukou (household registration), worrying about the inconvenience for their child’s schooling. Now they can directly send their child to international primary schools in Beijing [http://www.yzteb.com/], where conditions are better. They don't care about the 400,000 RMB cost; comparatively speaking, sending their child to a local primary school would require networking and paying sponsorship fees which could exceed this amount. This is actually a very common practice, generally seen among the middle class, something unimaginable for ordinary people because an average family simply cannot afford 400,000 RMB to have a child in the US [http://www.yzteb.com]. A family's income is almost entirely spent on the child's education, medical treatment for parents, and themselves, leaving little savings.

The most ideal scenario is investment immigration, obtaining citizenship or permanent residency in another country before returning to China to manage one's business or continue one's career. The benefits of obtaining foreign nationality go without saying. The reason for returning to China to work and live is that when Chinese people come to Western societies, due to numerous differences in ethnicity, culture, and history, it is difficult for us to truly emotionally integrate into local societies. Moreover, there are far fewer opportunities in Western countries compared to rapidly developing China. There are friends who have immigrated to Canada, where spending just over two million RMB can buy a very nice villa in Toronto, whereas the same amount in Beijing can only buy a sixty-square-meter apartment. Therefore, using a foreign nationality, purchasing property abroad, working in China, and flying back for vacations during leisure time is the best choice.

If you think about the next generation, it is a wise choice if your financial resources allow. This has nothing to do with patriotism; those who oppose either lack money or knowledge. Giving birth in China means even strangers might end up with a cesarean section; doctors will always persuade you to have a cesarean section, and if not, they'll opt for an episiotomy, which involves cutting the most private part of a woman horizontally, all for the sake of earning a bit more from surgery fees! Those who have made money and have high academic qualifications have already left. Factors such as poor public security, unstable policies, and low national credibility lead many wealthy and capable individuals without political power to go abroad.

Having a child in the US [http://www.yzteb.com] gains more than just an American citizenship certificate. As an American, one does not have to carry a schoolbag as heavy as oneself, does not have to struggle fiercely to enter university, does not have to compete with migrant workers for jobs in the talent market after graduation, and does not have to bow down for housing after starting work. As an American, one can enjoy visa-free entry to more than 180 allied countries, 13 years of free compulsory education in the US, save millions of RMB in tuition fees for entering American universities, enjoy various social welfare measures, live in low-cost, high-quality elderly apartments, and even have priority boarding rights when evacuating during political turmoil in other countries... These privileges are fundamentally unattainable in China.
