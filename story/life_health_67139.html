Medical expert: Keeping your children away from hand-foot-mouth disease, ensuring the quality of water resources is the key

by fashioninchina on 2008-05-06 15:36:57

Since the outbreak of the EV71 enterovirus in Fuyang, Anhui, there have been reports of hand-foot-mouth disease (HFMD) infections across the country. According to medical experts, viruses like enteric infectious diseases generally spread more easily in hotter climates and less so in colder ones. However, in China, both the south and north experience cases. During outbreaks, certain regions may see an explosive spread of the disease, with rapid transmission, especially when water sources are contaminated, leading to more severe consequences.

HFMD is highly contagious, with pathogens primarily entering the intestines through the mouth. Contaminants can include food, water, saliva, air, dirty hands, toys, etc. Therefore, prevention should start with stopping germs from entering the body via the mouth. Without attention to environmental and food hygiene, anyone could become infected. So why are most domestic patients young children? This is mainly because children have weaker resistance compared to adults, and they haven't yet developed good hygiene habits or the ability to distinguish cleanliness. Adults must work harder to cut off infection sources to keep children safe from HFMD.

Currently, there is no vaccine for HFMD. The medical community suggests that the key to prevention is cultivating good hygiene habits, with particular emphasis on ensuring water source quality. Even if we develop good habits, contamination from water sources can still lead to illness. Many people are busy working and earning money, leaving little time to supervise every aspect of their children's lives or constantly disinfecting utensils and toys. What can be done? With daily news reports showing a sharp rise in infections, citizens are becoming increasingly anxious, unsure how to protect themselves. They worry about their children's health and the surrounding environment, feeling the pressure mounting.

Spring and summer bring continuous rain, and recently the weather has been gloomy with occasional heavy rains or showers. This not only creates a damp environment but also accelerates the breeding of viruses and bacteria. Although our daily tap water is treated at plants and becomes safe with heating, if it gets contaminated during transportation due to pipe damage or mixing with rainwater, it could still infect us once it comes out of home faucets. How can we thoroughly prevent water pollution and ensure our families use safe water? More and more people are starting to use water purifiers, water filters, and direct drinking machines to make water cleaner before using it. Using these devices is the best way to directly prevent water pollution. Since human survival depends on water resources, whether it's washing food, cleaning the body, or housekeeping, tap water is essential. If this water is filtered by a water purifier or filter before use, we won’t need to worry about family members or children getting infected by viruses, nor will we fear that the living environment might cause HFMD.
