Eyeshape correction techniques

I. Upward-slanting eyes

Upward-slanting eyes are also called slant-up eyes. The inner corner of the eye droops, but the outer corner rises up, giving people a very sharp, clear, and arrogant impression.

Correction: Eyeshadow - When modifying, you can apply a bright color on the upper eyelid at the inner corner of the eye, and do not emphasize the outer corner of the eye, just gently brush over with a soft tone. You can use a light bright color to brighten the lower side of the inner corner of the eye, and similarly, you can use a dotted color to emphasize the lower side of the outer corner of the eye.

Eyeliner - When drawing the eyeliner, widen the inner corner of the upper eyeliner and the outer corner of the lower eyelid shadow to achieve a sense of visual balance.

Eyebrows - Pairing with slightly curved eyebrows can effectively correct the upward-slanting eyes.

II. Drooping eyes

Drooping eyes usually have a high inner corner of the eye and a low outer corner, giving people an impression of melancholy, indifference, and weakness. If the downward-sloping eyes are not obvious, it gives people an impression of innocence, cuteness, and naivety.

Correction: Eyeshadow - Before applying makeup to the downward-sloping eyes, you can stick beauty stickers on the outer corner of the upper eyelid to lift the shape of the eyes. When choosing eyeshadow, it is exactly the opposite of the upward-slanting eyes, focusing on the outer corner of the eyeshadow method, which can be slightly lifted upwards (oblique blending). The area of the eyeshadow above the inner corner of the eye should not be too large, and cool colors can be selected. The lower eyeshadow should not emphasize the outer corner of the eye, and brown can be added below the inner corner of the eye.

Eyeliner - When drawing the upper lash line, adjust the position appropriately according to the degree of the downward-sloping outer corner of the eye, thicken and lift it at the end, and extend inward without drawing all the way to the inner corner of the eye, fading out in the middle position.

Eyebrows - Should make appropriate upward or parallel modifications according to the situation of the downward-sloping eyes.

I. Swollen eyes (thick eyelids)

Characteristics: The fat in the upper eyelid is thicker, making the thickness of the upper eyelid prominent, causing the sign of swollen eyelids.

Correction - Swollen eyes are very common in the Eastern population, so when choosing eyeshadow colors, you must be very careful. Try to avoid warm colors such as pink and red, as these colors can create an impression of more prominent eyes. You can choose cool colors such as coffee tones, gray, and blue. In addition, you can use bright colors to brighten the height of the brow bone, and choosing longer false lashes can also weaken the thickness of the swollen eyelids.

II. Fine eyes

Small eye fissures but too wide, making the eyes appear lifeless.

Correction: Eyeshadow - 1. Draw fake double eyelids that are darker on top and lighter below.

2. Use the technique of shading darker on top and lighter below, try not to select too glaring and unconventional colors, and fade out the inner and outer corners of the eyes.

Eyebrows - Draw following the eye shape and appropriately shorten the length of the eyebrows.

V. Round eyes

Gives a feeling of being smart and lively.

Correction: Eyeshadow - Should be shaded horizontally, the inner corner can be shaded towards the nasal root, and the outer corner can be shaded outward and upward. The shading area in the middle of the eye should not be too wide, and use a bright color on the orbital edge.

Lash line - The middle part of the upper eyeliner should be straight, and the tail should be slightly raised upward. The lower eyeliner should be straight, and the tail should be slightly extended outward.

Eyebrows - Can be modified to be straighter and longer.

VI. Narrow space between eyebrows and eyes

Gives a feeling of being serious and melancholic.

Correction:

Eyebrows - Mainly adjust the height of the eyebrows, remove some of the lower half of the eyebrows, then draw the eyebrow shape based on this, slightly raising the position. Choose slightly lifted eyebrows or arched eyebrows to expand the space between the eyebrows and the eyes.

Eyeshadow - Select bright or gentle colors, and shade upward from the root of the upper eyelid (flat coating or gradient). Apply pearl powder to brighten the orbital edge.

Lash line - Both upper and lower eyeliner should remain thin, and the upper eyeliner should be slightly darker than the lower eyeliner.

VII. Eyes spaced too narrowly

The distance between the two eyes is less than the length of one eye, giving an impression of introversion and restraint.

Correction:

Eyeshadow - Focus on the outer corner of the eye, and the shading area can be appropriately extended outward.

Eyeliner - The upper eyeliner can be appropriately extended outward, and false lashes can be attached at the outer corner of the eye.

Eyebrows - The start of the eyebrows should be slightly moved backward, and the tail of the eyebrows should be appropriately extended to try to make the distance between the two eyebrows equal to the length of one eye.

VIII. Wide spacing between eyebrows and eyes

Makes people look lackluster and empty.

Correction:

Eyebrows - Adjust the height of the eyebrows, remove the upper part of the eyebrows, try to lower the position of the eyebrows, and draw the eyebrow shape based on this, slightly thickening downwards. Choose flat and straight eyebrows.

Lash line - Upper lashes can be appropriately thickened and darkened, and the lower eyeliner can be omitted.

Eyeshadow - Choose cooler colors like blue and green (flat coating), and the tail can be slightly raised.

IX. Eyes spaced too widely

The distance between the two eyes is greater than the length of one eye, giving a feeling of childishness, youthfulness, and openness.

Correction:

Eyeshadow - Emphasize the inner corner of the eye with cool colors to reduce the distance between the two eyes.

Eyeliner - Move the upper eyeliner forward, and do not extend the outer corner of the eye.

Eyebrows - Slightly move the start of the eyebrows forward, and try to make the distance between the two eyebrows equal to the length of one eye.

X. Puffy under-eye bags

Thick and puffy under-eye bags make the eyes look tired and listless.

Correction: Apply deep skin tone foundation on the protruding part of the under-eye bags, and apply light skin tone foundation on the lower edge of the protruding part of the under-eye bags. The transition should be soft and natural.

Eyeshadow - It is advisable to choose gentle colors, and should not be overly emphasized.
