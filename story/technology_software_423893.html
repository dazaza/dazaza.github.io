As the season with the highest incidence of sensitive skin, the fluctuating spring temperatures cause sebum secretion to vary, while the warm spring breeze brings stimulants such as sand, dust, and pollen. The intensification of ultraviolet rays can also become a source of sensitivity. Even if you don't have sensitive skin, it's easy to experience symptoms like pricking sensations or swelling at this time. How do you evaluate sensitive skin? You need to add an extra layer of protection for your skin.

Active Pre-Defense

If in previous years you've already experienced spring skin sensitivity, then at the beginning of this year's seasonal change, start preparing your skincare routine to prevent sensitivity. Prevention beforehand is usually easier than repair after the fact. Providing enough hydration to the skin is the primary key to strengthening its tolerance. In addition, some low-stimulus essences that can strengthen the skin quality are good choices too. They can enhance the skin's tolerance, thereby reducing the occurrence of sensitivity.

Tips

1. Sunlight, dirty air, and dust can all potentially become sources of sensitivity. Therefore, when your skin shows signs of sensitivity, pay more attention to sun protection and isolation care during the day. Of course, choose a low-allergy isolation cream.

2. If you have sensitive skin, avoid abruptly switching your entire skincare regimen during seasonal changes. Because when the skin's resistance is weak, it may be very sensitive to new ingredients.

Soothing Intensive Calming

Whether your skin is on the dry side or oily, improper care can make the skin increasingly fragile, leading to skin sensitivity issues. At this point, the skin will desperately want repair, but due to decreased function of skin cells, sometimes even essence products might become irritants, causing discomforts like redness, swelling, and itching to escalate. What the skin needs most at this moment is a light, water-based repair essence that not only soothes and calms the skin promptly but also repairs the barrier function, reactivates cell vitality, and enhances the skin's defense against external stimuli.

Tips

1. When your skin is sensitive, hydrating essence can be used appropriately, but whitening and anti-wrinkle essences with stronger effects must be used cautiously. Some components in these essences can have certain stimulating effects on sensitive skin.

2. Do not exfoliate randomly. No matter what, exfoliating products will have a certain degree of irritation on the skin. Use them cautiously during sensitive periods.

Isolation and Modification

If your skin is sensitive, makeup isn't recommended. However, in certain special occasions where you absolutely need to wear makeup, besides suggesting the use of hypoallergenic cosmetics, choosing the right isolation product is very important. They can keep your skin in a relatively safe state. Additionally, if you're troubled by redness caused by sensitivity and feel self-conscious about showing your bare face, hypoallergenic isolation creams with color-correcting functions will be your savior. They can even out your complexion, allowing you to have bright skin without makeup while moderately reducing skin imperfections.

Tips

1. If the main purpose is to correct redness, green isolation cream is recommended as it can effectively neutralize red tones, giving the skin a healthy glow. Of course, before using any isolation cream, a patch test for sensitivity is advisable.

2. This season, choosing an isolation cream with an SPF below 30 is sufficient. After all, the higher the SPF index, the greater the burden on the skin.
