The sniper Long Shaoqin, who had received special training at a German military academy, returned to China and joined the Kuomintang army to fight against the Japanese. Standing out like a crane among chickens, he was constantly at odds with those around him. In the bloody battles against the Japanese, he fought alongside the Eighth Route Army sharpshooters Dachun and Ji'er, cooperating seamlessly and gradually growing into an infallible anti-Japanese hero.

To counter Long Shaoqin, the Japanese headquarters sent over ace sniper Ketsugawa, who also trained at the German military academy. The Military Intelligence agents discovered that Ketsugawa and Long Shaoqin were from the same school, suspecting that he secretly passed intelligence to the Japanese, and wanted to execute him on the spot. With his incredible marksmanship, Long Shaoqin killed the Japanese "Flower of Famous Generals," establishing his reputation.

Horse hooves stirred up yellow sand and dust everywhere. Amidst the swirling dust, a camouflaged sniper rifle resembling a dead branch slowly raised its barrel. Behind the scope were those piercing eyes, devoid of the cynicism from four years ago, no longer naive, now filled with anger and hatred.

This professional soldier in camouflage hadn't changed much in appearance, but his eyes revealed maturity beyond his years. That face clearly hadn't smiled in a long time, the muscles slightly stiff.
