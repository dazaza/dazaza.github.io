Breast Augmentation with Implants - Let Beauty and Safety Go Hand in Hand

Breast augmentation with implants is the earliest form of breast enlargement surgery. It involves surgically placing implant materials suitable for the patient's thoracic cage type in the chest area. There are various incision options, which can be selected based on the patient's specific basic conditions. The surgery is simple to perform but requires precision. The recovery period after the surgery is around one week, and massage is required afterward. Currently, there are a wide variety of implant materials available, differing greatly in quality and price. Internationally, breast implants commonly used include products from Mentor and McGhan companies, both of which have a long history and excellent quality. These implants contain substances such as silicone gel and saline solution. Common models include teardrop shape, full shape, and round shape.

Who is Suitable for Breast Augmentation with Implants?

Adult women with flat chest development who are slim and not suitable for autologous fat breast augmentation.

Surgical Methods for Breast Augmentation with Implants:

Traditional breast augmentation with implants requires making a small incision in the armpit, under the breast fold, or around the areola, then inserting the pre-selected breast implant, adjusting its position and shape, and finally suturing the wound. After the wound heals, the stitches are removed. Generally, about 5-7 days of rest time is needed.

Common Incisions for Breast Augmentation:

Submammary Fold Incision:

This incision is close to the inner lower origin of the pectoralis major muscle. It allows for adequate dissection of the lower-inner part of the cavity while avoiding excessive dissection of the outer-upper part. Post-surgery, the implant is less likely to shift upward, and the cleavage is quite evident. However, the downside of this incision is that it leaves a scar on the underside of the breast, which many unmarried individuals may be unwilling to accept.

Axillary Incision:

This incision is higher up, leaving a more concealed postoperative scar. It is relatively easier to place the breast implant behind the pectoralis major muscle. However, it is difficult to adequately dissect the inner-lower origin of the pectoralis major muscle through this incision, resulting in a cavity that is larger at the top and smaller at the bottom. Post-surgery, the implant tends to be positioned higher, the cleavage is not prominent, and the breast shape may not be satisfactory.

Areolar Incision:

This incision is designed within the areola, i.e., within the pigmented skin. Since the areola has a darker color and uneven surface, the postoperative incision scar is almost invisible, achieving truly scarless breast augmentation with satisfactory shape results.
