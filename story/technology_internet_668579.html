**Coronary Artery Atherosclerosis and Hypertension Study in Nanyang City, Henan Province People’s Hospital Department of Cardiology (473010) Chen Xiaorui**

**Abstract**

Objective: To investigate the occurrence and extent of coronary atherosclerosis in patients with high blood pressure using coronary angiography.

Methods: Among 125 cases who sought medical attention due to chest tightness, chest pain, or myocardial infarction for various reasons, contraindications for coronary angiography were checked. The standard Judkins method was used for selective coronary angiography, where stenosis ≥ 50% in ≥ 1 branch vessel diameter had clinical significance. Patients were then divided into four groups based on the degree of coronary artery lesions: Group A included left main coronary artery stenosis ≥ 50% and/or left anterior descending artery, circumflex artery, or right coronary artery stenosis ≥ 75%; Group B included any one coronary main branch stenosis between 50% and 75%; Group C included stenosis < 50%; and Group D included no stenosis. Finally, statistical analysis was performed.

Results: The findings showed that hypertension was significantly correlated with coronary artery stenosis. The higher the degree of coronary stenosis, the higher the proportion of hypertension.

Conclusion: Hypertension is an important risk factor involved in the development of coronary heart disease events. The more severe the coronary artery stenosis, the stronger the correlation. Systolic blood pressure has a closer relationship with coronary heart disease than diastolic blood pressure.

**Keywords**: Coronary angiography, hardening, hypertension, coronary angiography, correlation

With the improvement of people's living standards, changes in work pace, and environmental pollution factors, the prevalence of hypertension continues to increase. According to a unified sample survey organized by the Ministry of Health in 1991, the average prevalence rate of hypertension in China was 11.44%. Therefore, in the population, there are high morbidity, high mortality, low awareness, low treatment rates, and low control rates. Hypertension, as one of the risk factors for coronary atherosclerosis, how the two are correlated was analyzed through coronary angiography.

**1 Materials and Methods**

**1.1 General Information**: From June 2003 to July 2001 at our hospital, 125 patients were selected who experienced chest tightness, chest pain, or busy hospitalization due to various reasons, including myocardial infarction. Coronary angiography was performed on these patients. There were 79 males and 46 females, aged 35-76 years old, with an average age of (59.48 ± 13.21). A total of 86 cases were diagnosed with coronary heart disease, including 7 cases of AMI (acute myocardial infarction), 18 cases of OMI (old myocardial infarction), and 61 cases of angina pectoris. The other 29 cases had normal coronary angiography. The minimum onset time was 10 days, up to 20 years, with an average disease duration of (6.8 ± 5.86) years. Among the 86 cases, 72 had high blood lipids, and 17 had diabetes.

**1.2 Methods**: Routine examinations such as ECG, liver and kidney function, blood lipids, and blood clotting were conducted after admission to exclude hemorrhagic diseases and prepare for coronary angiography. No contraindications for coronary angiography were found... Coronary angiography was then carried out using the standard Judkins method, where stenosis ≥ 50% in ≥ 1 vessel diameter had clinical significance. Patients were then divided into four groups based on the degree of coronary artery lesions: Group A included left main coronary artery stenosis ≥ 50% and/or left anterior descending artery, circumflex artery, or right coronary artery stenosis ≥ 75%; Group B included any one coronary main branch stenosis between 50% and 75%; Group C included stenosis < 50%; and Group D included no stenosis.

**1.3 Statistical Analysis**: Measurement data were expressed as mean ± standard deviation. Paired t-tests were used to analyze differences between the two groups. Measurement data were expressed as percentages, and x-tests were used. P < 0.05 was considered statistically significant.
