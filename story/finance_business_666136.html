The pain of group buying lies in the problem of following the trend.

by beetgk2958 on 2012-02-15 14:29:28

The Pain of Group Buying: Whether it was the former five thousand companies or the remaining three to five companies as the survivors, they all represent a gray landscape in the memories of participants. They are not saints, and I do not want to be an armchair critic. Even if we appear to be following trends on the surface, it is likely out of necessity. After all, the big environment in China is there, the slogan for scientific and technological innovation is shouted loudly but not implemented. There are not many people who are truly willing to work hard, nor are there many opportunities given to those who are willing to work hard.

As an entrepreneur, I feel sorry for the numerous group-buying industry entrepreneurs; similarly, I also feel regretful for the numerous investors in the group-buying industry. Because you chose to follow trends, natural selection will inevitably lead to elimination, and even those with vision may fall by the wayside due to lack of support. The only comfort is that we have chosen our own direction in life, a journey that others have not experienced. Even if we don't succeed, it is enough to console the heart moving forward, and there is still hope!

The problem of following trends is difficult to eradicate, and Chinese Internet innovation is unlikely to make great progress. Not to mention the network giants that are already in a monopolistic position in the industry, they have stifled the opportunity for industry innovation, making it difficult for small and medium-sized enterprises to innovate on the Internet. This is the widely criticized issue of knock-offs, which is no longer just a problem in the network industry. Looking at all kinds of industries in China, similar problems caused by following trends are everywhere, and it is difficult to count how many entrepreneurs and families have been harmed.

Let's talk about how many capable people or institutions have been trapped in the group-buying industry? Indeed, there are some public data available, including those who were once successful entrepreneurs, innovators, or businesspeople, as well as those famous investors or investment funds. From a well-known group-buying website failing to go public overseas to another high-profile announcement of massive funding and preparation for listing, which also failed... whether they can exit cleanly remains unknown.

The trend of group-buying over the past few years has almost turned the domestic Internet industry into chaos. Yesterday one company closed down, today another fled, debt disputes continue, weak development prospects, and a bleak future rather than a bright one, this is Beijing group-buying. The pain of group-buying lies in the problem of following trends, which needs further discussion.

What I am talking about is opportunism, also known as speculation. Entrepreneurs and investors are not really interested in good innovation or doing something beneficial for the industry or audience, but rather seeking what they think are visible opportunities for profit. The result is very likely losing money and reputation. Perhaps investors need to be responsible for investment returns and are under pressure; while entrepreneurs often have a mindset of grabbing quick profits and leaving without caring about innovation or profitability - this is unacceptable!

To eradicate the problem, we must eliminate following trends, but unfortunately, there is a lack of discerning eyes and talent. Back in the day, QQ was not favored by domestic investors, and instead, African media gave it the capital to grow. We always focus on what is right in front of us, yet complain that China does not have Apple or Facebook. These investors do not reflect on why they rarely support future innovations, but only want to harvest visible benefits. In fact, such benefits are often bubbles, just like the illusion of beauty in group-buying websites.

So, how were the problems in the group-buying industry created? At this point, we must discuss the Chinese concept of following trends. Or say, it is part of Chinese traditional culture, where uniqueness is not pursued because it easily leads to failure. As a result, everyone tends to chase hotspots, thinking that this reduces risk and captures the fruits of opportunism. In reality, no one is a fool, and in an environment where following trends is prevalent, it is easy to develop blindly and lose development opportunities, ultimately leading to failure.
