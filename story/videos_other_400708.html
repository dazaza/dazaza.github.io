The rapid development of online home appliance shopping may lead to channel diversification

Since the 2008 economic crisis, the online shopping market has experienced a period of high-speed development, and the online home appliance shopping market has shown an unexpected growth. In cities where online shopping is more developed, such as Beijing and Shanghai, the growth of online home appliance sales is even faster. According to the investigation data from the Consumer Electronics Product Investigation Office of the China Electronic Commerce Association, the national online home appliance sales in 2008 was about 20 billion yuan, and this year it is expected to exceed 40 billion yuan. For a long time, home appliance manufacturers' sales channels have been mostly controlled by strong home appliance chain stores, lacking terminal discourse power. In the future, the flattening, diversification, and penetration from traditional channels to new channels of home appliance sales channels will be an inevitable trend, and the resulting home appliance online shopping method will have a significant impact on the future home appliance channel pattern. The scale of China's online home appliance shopping will exceed 100 billion yuan in the next three years, which may shake the status of "Mei Su" and other chain giants in the entire home appliance sales channel.

The surge in internet users and changes in consumption habits have heated up online shopping

The rapid increase in the number of internet users has accelerated the change in consumer shopping habits, which is one of the important factors that have heated up the online home appliance shopping market. As of July 30, 2009, the scale of internet users in our country has reached 342 million. Online shopping has gradually become a habit for many internet users, and a considerable part of them are the backbone of the current and future consumer market.

The main force of the online home appliance shopping market is mainly young people aged 25 to 35. New consumption trends generally start with the trial and error of young people. It can be said that without the continuous efforts of young people, it is difficult to form the prosperity and long-term development of a brand. It is this group of consumers who have changed the consumption habits of home appliances and guided the trend of online home appliance shopping. The development and improvement of the credit system and logistics system in our country's e-commerce market have also further promoted the development of the online home appliance shopping market. In the past, people who intended to shop for home appliances online would first go to the store for an on-site inspection, compare, and then buy online. But according to authoritative investigations, last year, 30% of people did not go to physical stores to gather information before shopping online, and this year the proportion has increased to 45%.

Price as a weapon, service as support

Price advantage is currently the survival base of many home appliance B2C websites. Low prices are the most important weight and the only weight for home appliance B2C websites to compete with chain giants for the market. The selling price of six categories of electrical appliances sold online by the home appliance B2C website giant "JD.com", including televisions, audio-visual equipment, air conditioners, refrigerators, washing machines, and mobile phones, is on average 10% to 15% lower than that of home appliance chains. The price difference for some large-size flat-screen TV products can even reach around 2000 yuan.

Currently, many B2C websites have established comprehensive direct supply agreements with mainstream home appliance manufacturers. By centralized bulk procurement with manufacturers and eliminating store entry fees, home appliance B2C websites ensure cost reduction from both the source and channel, thereby gaining stronger price advantages than home appliance chain enterprises. Home appliance manufacturers hope that the retail prices of B2C websites are similar to those of physical stores like Gome and Suning when cooperating with home appliance B2C websites, making the purchase price of B2C websites at least 5% higher than that of Gome and Suning and other chain giants. However, B2C websites save on the rent costs of physical chain stores, and relatively lower human resources, promotional, and logistics costs. Therefore, the final product retail price is still 10% to 15% lower than that of Gome and Suning. When the sales volume of B2C websites reaches a certain scale, its procurement scale will give it greater bargaining power, and its price advantage will be further enhanced.

Improvement in service is also an important reason for the rapid development of online home appliance shopping. Many B2C websites spare no expense to improve service levels to establish brand credibility. JD.com invested 20 million yuan to establish "Shanghai Yuanmai Express Company" to significantly increase delivery speed. Liu Qiangdong, Chairman of the Board and CEO of JD.com, stated that 70% of the 21 million US dollars financing received in 2008 will be used to improve the logistics and distribution links. Behind the "money-burning" behavior is the enhancement of online shopping creditworthiness. I believe that with the improvement of after-sales service in B2C and the establishment and popularization of cash-on-delivery and other guarantee payment systems, more and more consumers will choose to buy home appliances online.

Home appliance chain layout B2C plans for the future online shopping market

The development trend of online home appliance sales has already attracted the attention of home appliance chain giants. Gome and Suning have already opened their own online malls, and most products have achieved online ordering. At the beginning of 2008, Gome even upgraded e-commerce to a first-level department at headquarters, placing it in equally important status as the 16 centers. In late July, Suning launched its online shopping website "Suning Buy," investing tens of millions of yuan to redesign the page and update products, claiming that within the next two to three years, the company's B2C sales scale could reach 5 billion yuan. Zhang Jindong, the helmsman of Suning Electric Appliances, even stated: "Suning can switch to a B2C website at any time."

Gome, Suning, and other home appliance chain giants entering the online home appliance sales market and opening new business growth areas is understandable, but how to properly handle supplier relationships in online business and balance the relationship between online and offline consumers is "not an easy thing" for traditional home appliance chain stores. I believe that the price issue between online and offline channels is the first problem that chain giants need to handle. If handled improperly, it could even harm the interests of physical stores, which would be "cutting off one's nose to spite one's face." After all, among the currently physically-oriented home appliance retail chains, none have yet explored a successful B2C operational model. For example, a Philips (PHILIPS) 47-inch full HD LCD TV model 47PFL3609 being sold online by JD.com is priced at 6999 yuan, while the concurrent market price in chain stores is 8999 yuan, making JD.com 2000 yuan cheaper than the physical store. The Gome Electrical Online Mall (Beijing) lists this product at 7499 yuan, still 500 yuan higher than JD.com, while another chain giant Suning's (Nanjing) online mall "Suning Buy" lists it at 7548 yuan. This phenomenon occurs because physical stores have operating costs, and "Mei Su" and other home appliance chains implement two different pricing systems for online and offline products, which to a certain extent affects the appeal of the home appliance chain store brands to consumers. In this aspect, Century Electrical Network, JD.com, Xin Qi Tian, and other websites primarily focused on online retail have more advantages. If "Mei Su" and other home appliance chain stores want their online retail business to truly reach the same level as Century Electrical Network, JD.com, Xin Qi Tian, and other home appliance B2C websites, they still have a lot of work to do.
