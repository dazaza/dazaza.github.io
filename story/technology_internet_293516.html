The procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows: When trading stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. Furthermore, this order is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to trade, the quantity, and the price for buying or selling. Stock abbreviations usually consist of three to four Chinese characters, and stock codes are six digits long. The stock code and abbreviation must match when placing an order. Additionally, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks traded: the quantity for buying stocks must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity for selling does not have to be.

There are four methods of commission: counter submission, telephone automatic commission, computer automatic commission, and remote terminal commission.
