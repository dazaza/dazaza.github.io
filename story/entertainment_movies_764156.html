Synopsis:

Indiana Jones and the Raiders of the Lost Ark (1981)

Indiana Jones is a professor of archaeology at a university. One day, the U.S. military finds him after deciphering a German telegram about the "Ark of the Covenant," which was used to hold the stone tablets inscribed with the original Ten Commandments given to the Jews by God. The U.S. military sends Jones to Cairo to get the Ark first. Using his only measuring tool—a copper hat with red crystals—and with the help of Marion, they find the "Ark." One night, with the assistance of a local Egyptian friend, Jones excavates the "Ark." Unfortunately, they are discovered by the French archaeologist Belloq, and the Germans seize the "Ark." On a small island, the "Ark" is finally opened, and these Germans see what they should not have seen. Thus, God's wrath is unleashed! The "Angel of Death" emerges from the "Ark" and kills all the Germans present and Belloq. Fortunately, Jones and Marion keep their eyes tightly shut. Later, Jones hands over the "Ark" to the U.S. military, and it is properly preserved.

Indiana Jones and the Temple of Doom (1984)

In China, Dr. Indiana Jones (Harrison Ford) trades with a Shanghai gangster boss. After receiving Nurhaci's ashes, the gangster breaks his promise and attempts to silence Jones. At a critical moment, the clever young Short Round (Jonathan Ke Quan) saves Jones and an innocent songstress, Willie (Kate Capshaw), who gets dragged into the mess.

Due to a plane crash, after parachuting, they drift in a rubber boat to an Indian village. Here, the river has dried up, crops are destroyed, famine is rampant, and the people are suffering. The locals blame the evil spirits of the Pankot Palace and regard the "fallen from the sky" Jones group as incarnations of deities. They ask Jones to help retrieve the stolen holy stone and all the children.

The palace is indeed full of peculiarities; the ruler is a little child, the nobles eat leeches, pythons, beetles, and monkey brains, and the palace is filled with traps. By chance, they witness a bloody ritual sacrifice of a devilish cult... This battle is destined to be extraordinarily arduous!

Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade (1989)

Set in 1938, Indiana Jones and his father search for the legendary Holy Grail used by Jesus during the Last Supper. Due to the legend of the Holy Grail having miraculous healing powers, it attracts the greed of the German Nazis, who specially dispatch the beautiful female spy Dr. Elsa Schneider. She uses her charm and deception to force Jones to hand over his notes. After discovering Elsa's intentions, Jones and his father manage to retrieve the notes and head to the temple to retrieve the Holy Grail, but the Nazis follow closely behind and injure Jones' father. After a fierce battle, they escape the Nazis. Thinking everything is fine, Jones hurriedly uses the water from the Holy Grail to heal his father, only to find Elsa appearing before him wanting to seize the Holy Grail...

Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull (2008)

It has been exactly 19 years since Indiana Jones' last adventure. Time moves forward both in the fictional world on the big screen and in reality—Indiana has spent 19 years teaching at Marshall University, but he now faces the threat of being fired under government pressure... In 1957, at the height of the Cold War, Indiana's enemies shift from German Nazis to Soviet spies.

After experiencing a series of inexplicable gunfire and chases, Indiana meets a young man named Mutt Williams, a standard greaser with a leather jacket and a motorbike. Upon learning Indiana's identity, he asks Indiana to accompany him to the ancient tombs in the Peruvian jungle to search for the artifact known as the "Crystal Skull," which is said to be left by aliens and possesses the powerful ability to control human minds.

Another group is also desperately trying to obtain the "Crystal Skull"—a elite team led by Soviet female agent Irina Spalko. Despite being a woman, Irina is extremely cold-blooded, as evidenced by her perfectly styled hair, indicating her meticulousness and strictness, qualities that make her the head of the KGB. Others involved in this chaotic competition include the evil archaeologist Mac and Indiana's old love, Marion Ravenwood... Whoever ultimately obtains the "Crystal Skull" may very well influence the overall situation of the Cold War and the fate of two nations.
