The Sword of Excellence: The Spirit of the Wizard - Everything about Wizards

by yysky1314 on 2008-07-14 08:46:38

In GE, how should the role of a wizard be described? Perhaps the most fitting method is to compare it with other professions.

Firstly, the wizard is a magic-based profession without the health and defense of a warrior, which means they are not meant to tank monsters; wizards do not have high single-target damage output skills, so they are not intended for boss fights or instant kills.

Although wizards have many area-of-effect damage skills, their damage values are not high, their mana consumption is significant, and their cooldowns are long, which means they cannot unleash flashy skills like elemental casters can.

So what kind of profession is the wizard? Compared to other professions, where does its advantage lie?

It could be said that in GE, the wizard is a control-oriented profession. Whether in Player vs Player (PvP), boss fights, or leveling up, they do not excel in any particular area.

The true brilliance of the wizard shines in large-scale conflicts such as guild wars, where they play the role of a controller in the war.

2. Posture Analysis:

I. Posture Name: Mental Power

Attack Type: Magic Attack Weapon Used: Staff or Wizard Staff

Posture Description: The posture for casting mental magic, characterized by having both attack magic and curse magic.

This is the most commonly used and only offensive posture for wizards before level 60, with a total of 4 skills. Overall, all 4 skills are quite practical. It's normal to train this posture up to level 25, and you should definitely learn all the skills.

Magic Missile (Against Air, Ground)

This is a single-target skill with the important added effect of knocking down. Whether fighting NPCs, in 1v1 PvP, or even farming bosses, for a wizard lacking high single-target damage output, this is the most practical skill. It has no excessively long casting time, locks on during casting, has moderate range, and at first glance may seem underwhelming, but once you realize its significance, you will surely love it.

Net Casting (Against Air, Ground)

How should this skill be evaluated now? It has almost no practical value for leveling. In 1v1 PvP, instead of wasting MP and time controlling, using a skill with negative effects would be more practical. The only possible use might be in large-scale battles like guild stronghold wars, when the wizard doesn't need to deal too much damage but rather focuses on control. This skill, like binding, has the greatest advantage of preventing the opponent from drinking potions during the skill's duration.

Earthquake (Against Ground)

There's not much to say about this skill. It deals the highest group damage, has a knockdown effect, a decent range, fast casting time, can hit 8 enemies, and has a short cooldown, making it very practical. You can use it from around level 15 until just before reaching level 60, and there's hardly anything to criticize about it.

Necromantic Silence (Against Air, Ground)

In other words, it's an updated version of Earthquake. What's the difference? The range has expanded, becoming somewhat terrifying, and the number of targets hit has increased to 10. However, the casting time has been extended, the damage has decreased, and most importantly, the knockdown effect is gone. Is this skill useless then? Actually, no. In leveling, it's even more practical than Earthquake, mainly because the range has increased, with the diameter being almost one-third larger than Earthquake's. Combined with the increase in the number of targets hit, clearing mobs goes quickly. Additionally, starting at level 11, this skill reduces the target's defense by 1 level, which while not extremely noticeable, can still be felt. The added effect of preventing the opponent from using skills is effective in PvP or large-scale battles.

II. Posture Name: Prayer

Attack Type: Magic Attack Weapon Used: Staff

Posture Description: The posture for casting mental magic, primarily focused on defensive blessing magic.

This is the wizard's beneficial BUFF posture. Proper usage will yield good results whether in monster hunting or leveling. Personally, I recommend fully learning the first two BUFFS, while the last two can be chosen based on personal preference.

Mental Shield, Elemental Shield.

These are resistance BUFFS. A 20% increase at level 10 might not seem like much, but the difference becomes apparent. The most obvious example is brushing dolls in the estate. Which monsters make you die the easiest? Besides bosses, it's the witch dolls (monsters that cause damage with lightning-based spells). Four witch dolls attacking your character at level 50+ makes it hard to keep up with healing items, but with BUFFS, you can basically ignore these damages. Only one type of BUFF can exist at a time.

Defense Shield.

This is a highly debated skill. At level 12, it increases defense by 86 points and defense grade by 1 for 51 seconds. This attribute seems too overpowered, as 86 defense points are nearly the basic defense value of a level 60 equipment robe. However, when people ask me if they should focus on this skill, I generally respond that they don't need to. The reasons are: firstly, the effect duration is too short! Secondly, this BUFF must be applied individually to each character. If you calculate: the time interval from the start of casting to the next casting is approximately 2 seconds, meaning adding to 3 characters takes 6 seconds, which means almost 1/8 of the time is wasted on casting! While it has value, its practicality is low.

Magic Dispel.

This is another highly debated skill. I almost dare not use this skill at level 16. The reason isn't its magic dispel effect but the absurd negative side effect of zero mana for 8 seconds. What does 8 seconds of zero mana mean? It's not just that you can't cast spells; your mana stays at zero! What can a wizard without mana do? Compare defenses with warriors? Too ridiculous!

III. Posture Name: Illusion

Attack Type: Magic Attack Weapon Used: Wizard Staff

Posture Description: The posture for casting mental magic, primarily focused on indirect curse magic.

This belongs to the curse-type BUFFS. Overall, the effects are pretty good. Personally, I recommend learning the skills Hypnosis and Slow.

Hypnosis. This is a very useful control skill. Although the range feels small, the success rate of hypnosis is really high, whether against players or monsters. When hitting boxes, cast a hypnosis, then slowly use area-of-effect skills. In PVP, hypnotizing several opponents gives you enough time to deliver fatal blows.

Elemental Curse. I haven't specifically tested this skill, but given the current number of elementals on the battlefield, its PVP value is low. More often, it's used in boss fights, though the effect isn't great and requires cooperation from elementals. Consider that the cooperation between wizards and elemental equipment isn't suitable for killing bosses, so it lacks practical value.

Slow. Another very useful skill, mainly used in PVP. In stronghold battles, it's often used to escape from a group of pursuers.

Invisibility. This is a bit of a joke skill. Its practicality, in my opinion, is low. Why? On one hand, when you're invisible and trying to ambush someone, you can't use skills. The first hit will be a regular attack, then a skill, giving anyone with slightly better awareness enough time to run away before you can use your skills. On the other hand, if you're seen while invisible, your defense is 0, and a single area-of-effect skill can hit you while your movement speed is reduced. If you can't escape, the consequences... well, you know. However, while invisible, monsters cannot detect you, which occasionally proves useful.

IV. Posture Name: Levitation

Attack Type: Magic Attack Weapon Used: Staff and Wizard Staff

Posture Description: The posture for casting mental magic, primarily focused on indirect curse magic.

Levitation. You're not a wizard if you don't learn this. This posture feels like an enhanced version of Mental Power. Fly into the air, bring enough mana, and unleash firepower freely. No one can stop you anymore. Definitely train this to level 25 and learn all the skills...
