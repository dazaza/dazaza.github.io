※ What is the customer thinking during the face-to-face sales process?

Answer: The six constant questions in the customer's mind during the face-to-face sales process?

One, who are you?

Two, what do you want to talk to me about?

Three, what benefits will your discussion bring to me?

Four, how do you prove what you say is true?

Five, why should I buy from you?

Six, why should I buy now?

These six questions may not be asked explicitly by the customer, but they will think this way subconsciously. For example, when the customer sees you instantly, their feeling is: I haven't seen this person, why is he smiling and coming towards me? Their subconscious is thinking, who is this person? When you walk up to him and start talking, he is thinking what you want to talk to him about? When you speak, he is thinking, what benefits are there for me? If it doesn't benefit him, he won't want to listen further because everyone's time is limited, and he will choose to do things that benefit him. When he feels that your product really benefits him, he will also think, are you deceiving me? How do you prove what you say is true? When you can prove that the benefits are indeed true, he will definitely think, this product is indeed good, are there better ones elsewhere, or are others selling it cheaper? When you can give him enough information to let him know that buying from you is the most cost-effective, he will definitely think, can I buy quietly again, buy next month? Can I buy next year? Therefore, you must give him enough reasons to know the benefits of buying now and the loss of not buying now.

Thus, before visiting your customers, ask these questions yourself and answer them, design good answers, and provide sufficient reasons. Customers will buy what they think is best and most suitable for them.

※ How to compare with competitors when introducing products after-sales?

One, Do not belittle the opponent

1, You go to belittle the opponent, possibly the customer has some connection with the opponent, such as using the opponent's product now, his friend is using it, or he thinks the opponent's product is good, belittling equals saying he has no vision, making mistakes, he will immediately dislike it.

2, Absolutely do not casually belittle your competitors, especially when the competitor's market share or sales are good, because if the other side really does poorly, how can they become your competitor? Unreasonably belittling competitors will only make customers think you are not trustworthy.

3, Once you start talking about the opponent, saying others are bad, the customer will think you lack confidence or have quality issues.

Two, objectively compare your three major advantages with the opponent's three weaknesses

As the saying goes, compare goods among three fields. Any kind of goods has its own strengths and weaknesses. When introducing products, you should highlight your three major strengths and compare them with the opponent's three major weaknesses. Even if the products are of the same level, once compared subjectively, the superiority and inferiority will immediately become apparent.

Three, USP Unique Selling Point

Unique selling point is the unique advantage that only we have and the competition does not possess. Just like each person has a unique personality, any product will also have its own unique selling point. Highlighting and emphasizing the importance of these unique selling points during product introduction can increase the chances of successful sales.
