Daily maintenance of suits

by longyears on 2010-10-20 11:28:10

1. Cotton Fabric

Cotton fabric is highly alkali-resistant but not acid-resistant and has good high-temperature resistance, so it can be washed with various soaps or detergents. Before washing, soak the fabric in water for a few minutes, but not too long to avoid color damage. Intimate undergarments should not be soaked in hot water to prevent protein in sweat from coagulating and attaching to clothing, which may result in yellow sweat stains.

When washing with detergent, the optimal water temperature is 40-50°C. When rinsing, you can adopt the method of less water but multiple rinses, meaning each rinse doesn't require a lot of water, but more rinses are needed. After each rinse, wring out the water before proceeding to the next rinse to improve washing efficiency.

Clothes should be dried in a ventilated, cool place, avoiding direct sunlight, which could cause colored fabrics to fade.

2. Linen Fiber Fabric

Linen fibers are stiff and have poor cohesion. When washing, handle them more gently than cotton fabrics. Avoid using hard brushes or vigorous rubbing to prevent pilling on the surface. Do not wring out linen fabrics forcefully after washing, and do not use hot water for colored fabrics. They should not be exposed to direct sunlight to avoid fading.

3. Silk Fabric

Before washing, soak the silk fabric in water for about 10 minutes, but don’t soak for too long. Avoid washing with alkaline water; instead, use neutral soap or detergent. The bath water should ideally be lukewarm or room temperature. After washing, gently squeeze out the water without twisting.

Dry the fabric in a cool, well-ventilated area. Avoid direct sunlight and do not dry with heat to preserve its luster and elasticity.

4. Wool Fabric

Wool does not tolerate alkalis, so neutral detergents or soap flakes should be used for washing. Wool fabrics will shrink and deform in water solutions above 30°C, so the washing temperature should not exceed 40°C. Typically, room temperature (25°C) water is used to prepare the detergent solution. Avoid scrubbing with a washboard, even if using a washing machine, choose gentle wash cycles and limit washing time to prevent felting.

After washing, squeeze out the water by hand rather than wringing. Use a spin cycle briefly (about half a minute). Dry the fabric in a cool, well-ventilated place, avoiding strong sunlight to prevent loss of sheen, elasticity, and strength.

5. Viscose Fiber Fabric

Viscose fiber has a large shrinkage rate and low wet strength. It should be washed and soaked immediately, avoiding prolonged soaking. Viscose fiber becomes hard when wet, so wash gently to avoid pilling or tearing. Use neutral or mild alkaline detergents. The washing solution temperature should not exceed 45°C. After washing, fold the clothes and press out the water instead of wringing. Avoid drying in direct sunlight; instead, dry in a cool or well-ventilated area.

6. Polyester Fabric

Soak the polyester fabric in cold water for 15 minutes, then wash with general synthetic detergent. The washing solution temperature should not exceed 45°C. Collars and cuffs can be scrubbed with a soft brush if they are dirtier. After washing, rinse thoroughly, gently wring out the water, and dry in a cool, well-ventilated place. Avoid direct sunlight or drying machines to prevent wrinkles caused by heat.

7. Acrylic Fabric

The washing process is similar to that of polyester fabrics. Soak the acrylic fabric in warm water for 15 minutes, then wash with a mild alkaline detergent, gently rubbing and kneading. For thick fabrics, use a soft-bristled brush. Finally, either spin dry or gently squeeze out the water. Pure acrylic fabrics can be air-dried in the sun, but blended fabrics should be dried in a shaded or well-ventilated area.

8. Nylon Fabric

Soak the nylon fabric in cold water for 15 minutes, then wash with a regular detergent (regardless of its alkalinity level). The washing solution temperature should not exceed 45°C. After washing, dry in a cool, well-ventilated area and avoid direct sunlight.

9. Vinylon Fabric

First, soak the vinylon fabric in room-temperature water, then wash at room temperature using regular laundry powder. Avoid using hot water as it may cause vinylon fibers to expand, harden, and even deform.

After washing, dry in the shade, avoiding direct sunlight.
