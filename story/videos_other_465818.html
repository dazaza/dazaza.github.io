The sound effects of the movie "Transformer 3" are also unforgettable.

by tvbing21 on 2011-07-25 18:57:52

"Transformers: Dark of the Moon" gave me the scariest cinema experience: I fell asleep several times and woke up to find that the movie was still not over. I thought this movie would never end. The sound effect of "Transformers 3" is also unforgettable: the volume was equally loud from beginning to end, as if someone was hitting your head with a pile driver, each hit accompanied by various screams. As monotonous as the volume were the story and plot, this is one of the most clumsy Hollywood genre films. A two-and-a-half-hour movie should be cut down by two hours. For this 3D movie, I bought four tickets and four pairs of glasses, which cost me nearly five hundred yuan, making it the least valuable money I've ever spent on watching a movie.

The first "Transformers" was amazing, with a touching story and innovative action, spectacularly and splendidly presenting the process of Transformers transforming. The sequel did not make another breakthrough in imagination, but instead followed the Chinese way: using a sea of people, with a lot of things on the screen, when half of them fall, it indicates that justice has been done, and the movie finally ends. The male protagonist is fat like a man in his thirties, yet he is still called a "youngster" in the movie. Compared to the brilliant performance in the first film, his acting in the sequel is reduced to just shouting. The director often lets him shout: when he is angry, depressed, upset, or scared, shouting becomes the way to express most emotions. The American flag appears multiple times in the movie, coinciding with the American National Day, it turns out to be a patriotic movie. In July, China aired "The Founding of a Party", and Americans also came up with a great founding work, timely promoting Americanism. The screens on both sides of the Pacific Ocean were occupied by the Five-Star Red Flag and the Star-Spangled Banner respectively. This July, is very partisan. (By QDFilm)
