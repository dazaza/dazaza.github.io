by tfbgfnbv on 2009-11-25 11:34:22

Ilford, a company based in Basildon, Essex, UK, is a supplier of high-precision components for the automotive industry. Due to the need for more complex parts by companies like Lotus and McLaren, as well as for electronic whiteboards, the company installed a Prima Rapido five-axis laser cutter. Previously, this type of work was subcontracted out. The laser software CAD/CAM system chosen by the company is PEPSSolidCut. This software offers many benefits, one of which is the improvement in simulation functionality for LED screens. Chief programmer Rob Blackwell pointed out that this version can even display the feed pipe during application. The use of the PEPSSolidCut program with the Rapido laser cutter has significantly increased the types of parts Ilford can manufacture in-house.

On the other side of the world, in Melbourne, Australia, Laser3D, a 15-year-old subcontracting center with 40 employees, operates 13 laser cutting systems provided by Cincinnati's LaserLab and TRUMPF. The company supplies formed metal and plastic components, mainly to automotive clients such as Ford, Toyota, and General Motors Holden. To meet the increasing demand for metal cutting, Laser3D expanded its laser cutting team, thus needing to handle programming issues more efficiently.
