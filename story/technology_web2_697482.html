Kunming police capture drug dealers by firing gun at toll station

by yann946rxue on 2012-03-08 15:53:13

Police capture scene, reported by都市时报journalist Mingfei Qu, rephotographed. At 1 PM on November 27th, 2011, at the Western Toll Station of Kunming, a car waiting in line to pay suddenly surrounded by several men, one of them holding a handgun, using the gun handle to shatter the car window. However, the car still "fled through" amidst the astonished gazes of the crowd. "Bang, bang", gunshots were heard from behind the car... This movie-like scene was actually the Kunming police capturing drug dealers.

Recently, the Eighth Detachment of Kunming Public Security Bureau's Anti-Drug Brigade successfully solved a major case of armed transportation of drugs, capturing 7 suspects, seizing 34.5 kilograms (gross weight) of methamphetamine tablets (ice), one imitation Browning 7.65mm caliber pistol, one magazine, 8 bullets, and over 1.1 million yuan in drug funds. They dismantled a drug trafficking group mainly composed of people from Songming who had been transporting drugs from the China-Myanmar border to Kunming for distribution, destroying a drug transportation channel from the China-Myanmar border to Kunming.

Yesterday, the police informed the media about the details of the case.

The drug dealers planned to stop once they earned 10 million yuan.

In early November 2011, the Eighth Detachment of the Anti-Drug Brigade received information that a drug trafficking group led by a person surnamed Yue from Songming had been frequently smuggling drugs from abroad into Kunming for distribution. After receiving the lead, the detachment leader paid high attention and immediately instructed the Eighth Detachment to conduct investigations. After a large amount of detailed investigation, they initially grasped the basic situation of the drug trafficking group.

Yue Mou, 34 years old, is from Songming County, Kunming. He had previously been involved in earthwork projects in Kunming but went bankrupt due to a broken capital chain and high-interest loans. Gradually unable to keep up with his financial obligations, after selling his excavator at a low price, Yue Mou, under the instigation of a person he knew from abroad, invited Yao Mou, Cheng Mou, and others, who had fallen on hard times due to gambling, to join him in the drug trafficking business.

Starting small since July-August this year, Yue Mou and others reinvested their profits back into purchasing more drugs instead of dividing them immediately. After months of accumulating like rolling snowballs, their scale had reached the point where they could buy 2.7 million yuan worth of drugs from abroad in one go. "If this batch of drugs was transported to Kunming, the profit after sales could reach ten million yuan. Yue Mou and others' initial goal was to stop once they made ten million yuan," said Captain Yuan Bin of the Eighth Detachment of the Anti-Drug Brigade.

Crossing the border on foot to purchase drugs

On November 25th, the special task force learned that the group was planning to drive from Kunming to Myanmar to purchase drugs.

Yuan Bin, leading three police officers, drove overnight to Zhenkang. Early the next morning, Yuan Bin and his team arrived in Zhenkang and secretly conducted surveillance work with the cooperation of local police.

Yue Mou and others drove towards the border and stopped about 20 kilometers away from it. Two members of the group got out of the car and crossed the border on foot via a small path, making contact with the waiting personnel from abroad.

At around 2:30 AM on the 27th, the surveilling police noticed that the two cars driven by Yue Mou and others left Zhenkang heading towards Baoshan. "Based on many years of investigative experience, we judged that the group had already purchased drugs from abroad and hidden them in the vehicles they were driving, preparing to return to Kunming," Yuan Bin revealed. During the preliminary investigation, the police also learned that the drug trafficking group might be carrying firearms.

Considering various factors including safety, after research, the special task force decided that while investigators in Zhenkang would track the suspect vehicles, police forces would be organized to wait at three points—"Western Toll Station of Kunming", "Anfengying Toll Station", and "Caopu Intersection"—ready to seize the returning group members when the opportunity arose.

Probe car observing front and back

Generally speaking, if drug traffickers use two cars to transport drugs, the front car is used for reconnaissance. Yue Mou and others were no exception, but this reconnaissance car wasn't just probing whether there were any police blockades ahead; it also often circled around to observe whether there were any vehicles tracking them from behind.

Around 1 PM on November 27th, when the special task force police attempted to intercept the Volkswagen Lavida car transporting drugs at the Western Toll Station of Kunming, the suspects disregarded the police's warning shots and forcibly broke through the checkpoint, colliding with a microcar parked ahead before fleeing.

The police immediately gave chase and successfully intercepted the car at the entrance of the Provincial Party School, capturing the suspect Li Mou and discovering 34.5 kilograms of methamphetamine tablets (ice) in the trunk of the car.

Simultaneously, another group of police discovered another suspect-driven SUV at the entrance of the Western Toll Station of Kunming. The police quickly surrounded the vehicle, but all four doors were locked. Detective Lin Su from the Eighth Detachment went around to the back of the car, opened the rear door, jumped onto the car, and firmly held down a man attempting to resist in the passenger seat. By then, Lin Su's colleagues had shattered the car window and subdued the SUV driver, the main criminal of the group, Yue Mou.

Subsequently, Yue Mou's wife Zhao Mou, responsible for the group's finances, was also captured. Police found 30 grams of methamphetamine and some drug paraphernalia in her rented residence.

After Yue Mou's arrest, 3 other suspects were still at large. Through investigation, the police separately captured co-defendants Li Mou, Feng Mou, and Cheng Mou on December 2nd, 3rd, and 5th at the Walmart Supermarket entrance in the North District of Kunming City, Nanhua County in Chuxiong Prefecture, and Room 1706 of a residential complex on Chuangjin Road in Kunming.

Currently, the case is under further review. (Reported by journalist Wenjie Sun)
