Mobile QQ update: I don't do what WeChat does

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 17:44:49

The latest update of Mobile QQ has added many new features, and the main ones share a common characteristic: We don't do what WeChat does.

The iPhone version of Mobile QQ was updated to version 4.5 yesterday, while the Android platform was updated to version 4.5 a few days ago. The latest version of Mobile QQ includes several important new features:

— Added a Reading Center that is integrated with user accounts

— Snapshots, allowing users to send photos that can only be viewed for a limited time

— Linking QQ numbers, enabling users to monitor messages from other QQ numbers at any time

Tencent's last major update received widespread criticism, so they have become more cautious with updates since then. In this update, the features are not original to Mobile QQ, but through these updates, we can see the attempts being made by Mobile QQ to focus on its mobile attributes and become more "handQ" rather than more like WeChat.

Reading Center: Tencent Reading's Mobile Expansion

Two months ago, Tencent established Tencent Literature, integrating multiple channels including PC portals, wireless portals, mobile clients (QQ Reading), and QQ Reading Center, and announced that Tencent Literature would be integrated into Mobile QQ. Using Tencent's strongest mobile channel to promote Tencent Literature represents their determination in this field.

In the era of mobile internet, mobile reading has become one of the primary needs for users when using mobile devices. However, in the past, Tencent did not dominate the mobile reading market as it did in the IM sector; instead, its market share was divided among different types of products. Although Tencent had various product lines in mobile reading, none of them achieved absolute dominance. Besides content issues, another reason was the lack of unified channels and content operations across different platforms. After establishing Tencent Literature, Tencent unified the content center and gained the ability to distribute content uniformly. As a result, Mobile QQ, as Tencent's largest mobile channel and resource, will inevitably be utilized.

From another perspective, the success of self-media and subscription accounts on WeChat also helped Tencent validate the demand for mobile reading. However, WeChat does not integrate any content from Tencent Literature or even official content from Tencent Reading. Thus, what WeChat didn't do, Mobile QQ did.

Snapshots: Tencent's Version of Snapchat

This version of Mobile QQ introduced the Snapshots feature, which allows users to send photos that can only be viewed for a limited time. This feature is identical to the once-popular Snapchat, except that Snapchat does not incorporate real social relationships, and all interactions are anonymous. By introducing the 'view-and-delete' function within real social relationships, the fun factor might decrease from a product standpoint, but it provides users with another way to communicate. In today's era, simple voice and image communication no longer satisfies user expression needs. Users require more diverse and three-dimensional ways to express themselves. Despite this being a micro-innovation by Tencent, this feature is currently not implemented in WeChat.

Linking QQ Numbers: More Google? Or More Tencent?

When I was prompted to bind other accounts to receive related messages after updating Mobile QQ, the first product that came to mind was Google. Google’s account system supports multi-account binding for message reception and management, but this is unprecedented for Tencent.

Traditionally, all of Tencent's openness has been concentrated on account association and binding between QQ and other services, but it has never involved Tencent's own social relationship chains. This has always been one of Tencent's open prohibitions. In other words, you can access external services via your QQ account, but you cannot obtain Tencent's friend relationship chain. This account binding in Mobile QQ can, to some extent, be seen as a small trial of opening up the relationship chain, albeit just within Tencent's own products.

Additionally, this feature highlights an issue: many users own multiple QQ numbers, and managing messages across multiple accounts is a headache for users. For example, the iPhone version of QQ can only log in with one QQ number, which is inconvenient for people who need multiple accounts. Although this problem can be solved by carrying multiple devices, owning multiple devices is still rare. Mobile QQ resolves the issue of only being able to log in with one QQ number on mobile devices quite well through this method. This feature is also not yet implemented in WeChat.

Besides message management, I believe that Tencent will have more attempts regarding its own account system in the future. This binding and proxy message receipt is just a small trial. After all, even Google hasn’t fully unified multi-account management, and there are still problems between account systems. These are issues that Tencent will eventually face.

From the perspective of Mobile QQ's functional updates, whether intentional or unintentional, it indeed reflects the principle: what WeChat does, I don't. Geeks, what are your thoughts?
