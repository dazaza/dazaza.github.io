Traveling to Dubai, read the Dubai travel guide. Based on my personal experience of traveling to Dubai, I have recorded the following text, which can be called a travel guide. This is not only for my own reminiscence but also to provide convenience for many travelers who wish to visit. First, let me introduce my preparation work.

Dubai's abundant sunshine, picturesque long sandy beaches and warm seas, as well as its enchanting deserts, are ideal places for adventure-loving tourists. The personal safety factor in Dubai is very high (the crime rate in the UAE is very low), and the number of world-class hotels and leisure centers in Dubai continues to increase. Stores have ample inventory and are open 24 hours a day, creating a unique leisure spot.

Dubai is an exotic and inclusive city, and it is absolutely the best choice. Dubai has excellent sports and leisure facilities, from golf to water activities; from desert wilderness adventures to cruising on traditional dhows along the river, everything is available. Or you can bask in Dubai's warm sunlight, enjoying the tranquility of nature on its clear water and clean sand beaches.

Dubai is also a shopping paradise. Gold, jewelry, designer clothing, cameras, electronics, and famous Middle Eastern carpets, etc., have a wide variety of goods that are dazzling. Whether in the traditional markets full of Arabic charm or luxurious department stores, you can select your favorite items in Dubai.

Parks and playgrounds of various sizes are scattered throughout the city, providing plenty of opportunities for children to play. Restaurants and leisure venues with different flavors gather in the city. After a day of fun, the whole family can enjoy delicious food together and share the joy of family time.

Dubai Climate and Clothing: Most of the year in Dubai allows for wearing summer casual wear, but during the winter in Dubai, sweaters and coats are still useful, especially at night. Compared to some Middle Eastern countries, Dubai's dress code is relatively relaxed, but travelers should still avoid wearing overly revealing or inappropriate clothing. You can wear swimsuits and bikinis by the pool or beach, but when leaving the pool or beach, you must at least wear a shirt and shorts. Due to the strong sunlight in Dubai, high-quality sunglasses are essential for tourists, and those who wear glasses should consider colored lenses. Hats or other head protection measures are also necessary under direct sunlight.

For summer trips to Dubai, hats, sunglasses, sunscreen, heatstroke medicine, cold medicine, diarrhea medicine, and stomachache medicine are all indispensable travel supplies. In the Red Sea area of Dubai, guests will have the opportunity to swim, so please bring your swimsuits, swim trunks, high-strength sun protection products, and bathing supplies.

In business settings in Dubai, suits and ties are usually worn. In other situations, clothing is casual, but ladies should avoid wearing low-cut dresses or short skirts in public places in Dubai. Traditional swimwear can be worn at hotel pools and beaches in Dubai.

Language: Arabic is the official language of Dubai, and English is widely used.

Time Difference: The time in Dubai, UAE, is 4 hours behind Beijing Time. If it is 16:00 on May 1 in Beijing Time, then it is 12:00 on May 1 in Dubai Time. It is recommended that visitors adjust their watches to be 4 hours slower upon arriving in Dubai.

UAE Dubai Tourist Visa: Dubai visas are divided into Dubai visit visas, Dubai tourist visas, Dubai work visas, and Dubai transit visas. Dubai visit visas are further divided into one-month and three-month visas, which can be extended twice and once respectively, with extension periods of one month and ten days respectively. Tourist visas last for one month and cannot be extended. Expired visas must be left, otherwise penalties will be imposed. Work visas last for three years, but must be processed by a guarantor company or person. Travelers transiting briefly in the UAE to a third country can apply for a local 96-hour transit visa (must have tickets, visas, and valid documents for the third country, costs around $30).

UAE Dubai Tourism Entry and Exit Requirements: Before entering Dubai, you need to fill out an entry card and submit it to the entry inspection office. After verifying the passport and entry visa without errors, the entry inspection office stamps the passport and visa with an entry stamp, allowing entry. After entering, the visa must be kept properly for use when exiting.

When entering Dubai, passengers are strictly prohibited from carrying drugs, weapons, pornographic books, VCD discs, video tapes, painkillers, and other banned or specially approved items. According to customs regulations, each passenger entering Dubai may carry duty-free 2 bottles of spirits or two cases of beer, 2000 cigarettes, 400 cigars, and 2 kilograms of tobacco.

When handling customs import and export procedures in Dubai, the required documents must be provided: delivery note, packing list, commercial invoice, import and export license, certificate of origin, copy of business license, letter of authorization, tax receipt or tax exemption certificate.

Currency: The currency in Dubai, UAE, is the Dirham, and the exchange rate is approximately 1 USD equals 3.65 Dirhams. (The exchange rate may fluctuate, the above is for reference only.) The subsidiary currency is Fils, 100 Fils = 1 Dirham. Traveler's checks and internationally recognized credit cards can be used in the UAE, and using US dollars as cash is also very convenient. Of course, local currency can also be freely exchanged at banks, airports, and hotels. Please keep the exchange receipts properly for use when exchanging back foreign currency.

The UAE Dirham (AED) is divided into 100 Fils. Banknotes are issued in denominations of 5, 10, 20, 50, 100, 200, and 500 Dirhams; coins are issued in denominations of 5, 10, 25, 50 Fils, and 1 Dirham.

Dubai Hotels: Hotels generally do not provide toothbrushes, toothpaste, slippers, wooden combs, electric hot water cups, etc. Please do not casually use food and drinks in the refrigerator and storage cabinet in Dubai hotels. Once consumed, additional fees will be charged, and they are quite expensive. Additionally, telephone charges in Dubai hotels are also very high, so you can buy phone cards and make calls at public phone booths. The voltage in Egypt is uniformly 220 volts. Electrical sockets are generally British three-pin plugs. Most hotel front desks offer adapter rentals, but it is still recommended that guests purchase adapters domestically.

Dubai Drinking Water: As a city in the desert, Dubai's water resources are very precious, but domestic water supply is very sufficient. Generally, Dubai tourists prefer drinking mineral water, which is not much more expensive in supermarkets than in Yunnan. The tap water in hotels can be drunk directly. Hotels generally do not provide free hot water services. If you need to drink hot tea, please bring your own electric hot water cup. If requested from the hotel, the hotel may charge a certain fee.

Dubai Meals: Breakfast in Dubai is usually a Western buffet provided by the hotel. Lunch and dinner mainly feature Chinese cuisine (lunch and dinner do not include beverages and mineral water. If needed, guests can purchase them at their own expense). Foreign Chinese cuisine is not as authentic as domestic Chinese cuisine, please understand. In areas where there are no Chinese restaurants, we have arranged local specialty meals for you. Guests can bring instant noodles, pickled vegetables, biscuits, beef jerky, and other snacks according to their own taste.

Dubai Tips: Tips in Dubai are a sign of satisfaction with service. Tips are required for all services (paper money, not coins), based on the principle of exchanging for service, and should not be paid until the service is completed. According to local customs and affirmation of room cleaning staff services, please appropriately tip the room cleaning staff. It is suggested to give 1 USD per room per night (can also pay in local currency), and 1 USD per room for hotel bellboys.
