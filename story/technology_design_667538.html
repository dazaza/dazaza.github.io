How well a driver works for a particular golfer depends as much on the swing speed as it does on the size of the clubhead, with greater flex being required for a lower swing speed. Cobra has made it easier for golfers to match their swing speed to the appropriate driver flex by specifying which driver is most suitable. For example, with the L5V: if a golfer's swing speed is lower, specifically between 87-108 mph, resulting in a ball speed of 125-155 mph and an average drive distance of 220-275 yards, then the ideal driver would be the F speed. If you're new to golf, you should look for a driver with the largest clubhead possible, as this significantly increases a novice golfer’s chances of hitting the sweet spot.

If a golfer's swing speed is 104+ mph, with an average drive traveling 265+ yards and a ball speed of 150+ mph, that golfer should aim for an X speed driver. Selecting the correct golf clubs is the first step a novice golfer must take to master the game and improve, and Cobra drivers help golfers match their swing speed to the perfect driver, assisting them in getting the most out of the game. However, if the swing speed is even lower, at 76-97 mph (miles per hour), with a drive traveling 210-250 yards and an average ball speed of 110-150 mph, Cobra recommends choosing the M speed driver.
