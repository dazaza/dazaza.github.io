<；p>；Xuzhou, June 23 (Justice News) - On June 23, the verdict was announced in the bribery case of Zhang Hui, former deputy director of the Public Security Department of Xuzhou Municipal Public Security Bureau, investigated and prosecuted by Gulou District People's Procuratorate of Xuzhou City. The first-instance court sentenced defendant Zhang Hui to ten years and six months in prison for the crime of accepting bribes.<；/p>；

<；p>；Between 2001 and February 2010, while serving as the head of Yangzhuang Police Station, Tongpai Police Station, and deputy director of the municipal public security department of Xuzhou Municipal Public Security Bureau, Zhang Hui took advantage of his position to illegally accept bribes worth 190,000 yuan from Ma Mou, Zhang Mou, Chen Mou, and others. He also sought benefits for those who sought his help in matters such as household registration, public place security management, and case handling.<；/p>；

<；p>；According to Director Zhang of the Anti-Graft Bureau of Xuzhou Gulou District People's Procuratorate, which handled this case, Zhang Hui's bribery case came to light due to a report by his ex-wife. Originally, Zhang Hui had a happy family of three. Around the Mid-Autumn Festival in 2007, he developed feelings for a female police officer in his unit and began to disdain his own wife. After about a year of quarrels, their relationship deteriorated, leading to a divorce at the end of 2008.
