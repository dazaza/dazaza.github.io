**Must-read for bosses: Points to consider when selecting work phones for employees**

When using Master Enterprise Communication, some bosses may choose to let employees use their personal phones, and in return, provide a small subsidy for personal phone usage. Some companies opt to provide their employees with a unified set of phones. Both methods are good options. If you choose the first method, please pay attention to the following issues:

1. The differences between smartphones on the market can be significant. Although they are all smart devices, variations in smartphone operating system versions and manufacturers may result in different experiences when using Master Enterprise Communication. Please remind your employees that it is best to avoid using non-branded or low-quality phones. These phones may have quality issues, and if problems arise while using Master Enterprise Communication, it will be difficult to determine whether the issue lies with the phone or the software system provided.

2. Avoid using phones produced many years ago, such as smartphones with a resolution of 240*320. These phones may experience poor display effects and slow operation speeds when using Master Enterprise Communication.

If you choose the second method, where the company provides a unified set of phones for employees, sometimes companies will arrange mobile services with carriers, which offers several advantages:

1. The company does not need to pay for the phones directly; instead, by signing an agreement for consumption, the carrier will provide a free phone.

2. The phone numbers belong to the company, so when an employee leaves, the number can be reassigned to someone else.

3. Employees can make free calls to each other, and some carriers even offer free calls between mobile phones and the company's landline.

However, please also note the following:

1. The signal strength of carriers varies by region. In some areas, China Telecom has better signals, while in others, China Unicom or China Mobile may be stronger. Therefore, it is advisable to consult your employees' opinions.

2. China Mobile's phones often have slower internet speeds, with only 2G signals available in many regions and no 3G coverage. If you need to use Master Enterprise Communication, especially for uploading photos on-site, please test the speed beforehand. China Unicom's phones sometimes have weak signals in remote areas, so if your employees frequently work in rural areas, it is recommended to seek their input.

3. Try to choose a carrier's one-year or two-year contract package. Some carriers offer promotions where the company consumes for three years and receives a free phone. However, the industry consensus is that the lifespan of a smartphone is around two years. Choosing a three-year contract may result in a large number of phones becoming obsolete or damaged after two years.

Regardless of whether you procure phones through a carrier contract, there are additional considerations when purchasing phones:

1. What happens if a phone breaks or is stolen? This could directly affect business operations. For example, if orders cannot be placed or sales data cannot be reported for a day, this would be difficult for the company to accept. Therefore, preparations must be made. It is suggested that companies either keep spare phones on hand or encourage employees to use their personal phones if the company-issued phone is broken. Alternatively, the logistics department should be able to quickly purchase replacements.

2. Smartphones consume a lot of power. When selecting a phone, try to choose one with a longer standby time. Generally, phone batteries are measured in milliampere-hours (mAh), such as 1500 mAh or 2000 mAh. The higher the number, the longer the standby time. If the phone can be equipped with two batteries, that would be even better.

3. Finally, after providing phones to your employees, it is recommended to equip them with silicone protective cases, which can significantly extend the life of the phone!

After mobile phones have become inseparable from our daily lives, the influence of mobile internet has begun to permeate enterprises. It is now time to incorporate location information into corporate management considerations. Developed by Zhongtai Group, "Master Enterprise Communication" is a software that uses location information provided by mobile devices to increase the efficiency of employee management within a company.

In traditional models, it is difficult for company managers to monitor the real-time locations of employees working outside the office. Data collected by employees outside the office also needs to be entered via phone calls or returned to the office for manual entry. With the development of smartphones and mobile internet, more convenient location-based management allows companies to manage field employees as efficiently as office employees.

Through a mobile app, employees can check in more promptly and accurately, and transfer data collected during fieldwork to the company's internal network in real time. Such demands are concentrated in industries like fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) and logistics express, where there are many tasks conducted outside the office. According to statistics, more than 30 million company employees work outside the office nationwide. With the increasing penetration of mobile internet into daily life, the popularization of mobile office software is an inevitable trend.

In addition to basic attendance management, "Master Enterprise Communication" also provides comprehensive mobile office services, such as managing/updating customer information via a mobile phone, recording new business opportunities, uploading materials to the company's internal network, checking company notifications, receiving new tasks, writing work summaries, applying for expenses, and registering for business trips via a mobile phone.
