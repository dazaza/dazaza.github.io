Facebook Loses Loads of Users — But Why?

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 17:01:40

Regardless of whether users are fickle or have grown weary of online social networking, Facebook is no longer the darling of the young; it has become the dowdy wife.Data shows that the appeal of this social giant is gradually waning. Facebook loses millions of users every month. Many users have turned to other emerging social networks in search of more interesting ways and content for social interaction.In March 2013, the number of visitors to Facebook decreased in multiple regions. In the United States, they lost 6 million users (though this is only 4% of their total user base); in the UK, 1.4 million users did not visit Facebook, resulting in a 4.5% drop in traffic. Don't assume this is just a minor dip in one month. According to data provided by the social media analytics firm SocialBakers, over the past six months, Facebook has been losing an average of about 9 million visitors per month. And this trend is not limited to the UK and the US; it also appears in other parts of Europe, North America, and even Asia, including countries like Spain, France, Germany, Canada, and even Japan, all of which have recorded varying degrees of visitor decline.The main reasons for the loss of users are as follows:Saturation of user numbers and loss of appeal to younger users. According to the analysis consultancy EndersAnalysis, Facebook's user base has reached saturation: at least in developed areas like the UK and the US, those who wanted to register for Facebook have already done so.Despite rarely advertising itself, Facebook's early viral spread among young people allowed this social network to grow to an unstoppable size. The herd mentality triggered by this has already led a large number of people interested in the service to register and use it. Its penetration rate is quite high.However, for the even younger generation, Facebook has not been able to fully capture their hearts. An earlier article on BuzzFeed also revealed such a trend: growing teenagers prefer YouTube as their top choice for a social site.Other social websites offer more segmented and interesting options, and users have grown tired of Facebook's format. While Facebook's visitor numbers are declining, the photo-sharing community Instagram, which it acquired, is seeing a trend towards younger new users; Path, a social application with a limit on the maximum number of friends, adds over a million new users each week; Snapchat, the self-destructing picture app, is also very popular among the youth.It's therefore not hard to understand why Facebook quickly launched its mobile product, Facebook Home, and positioned the HTC First phone in the mid-range market. Their goal is to continue capturing users in the US and Europe who are seeking new experiences, thereby maintaining their stickiness. This move cleverly combines their inherent social service advantages with new mobile social experiences. And the mid-range price will ensure that this phone (also Facebook's promotion) remains within the budget of most users. Facebook's next target market should be developing regions, such as Africa and South America. Because Facebook's user visits in South Africa and Brazil are still growing, with Brazil recording a 6% increase in March, and India recording a 4% increase.Despite the decline in visitor numbers, its monetization capability remains impressive. Wall Street still has high hopes for Facebook's performance. In the upcoming Q1 2013 earnings report due out next Wednesday, Wall Street predicts Facebook's revenue to be $1.44 billion, representing a 36% increase compared to the same period last year.The title image is from the movie "A Chinese Odyssey".
