{Xiaoli Image Library} The Inferno of Human Nature in "The Gate of Wealth and Nobility"

by nomyliu996 on 2009-11-29 17:32:40

They all say love is selfish! And that's absolutely true. Originally, Dong Lingzi as a secret admirer could have garnered quite a bit of sympathy. This kind of love was somewhat selfless, pure, and something that couldn't help but make people want to protect it! But as she kept her secret admiration alive, watching the other couple being sweet and happy together made her uncomfortable inside, to the point where she would go through fire and water just to fight for him!

This is human nature! No one likes just standing by and guarding without owning! Since I am guarding, since I am giving, why can't I own? So, I must fight for it, regardless of how others' families may be torn apart! What was originally thought to be a pure and selfless love, in the end, remains a love for love's sake, unable to escape the crucible of human nature!

Dong Lingzi is sexy and emotional. A not-so-long relationship that cannot be cut or put down. She lingers on with thoughts, eventually even giving up the person right before her eyes, choosing instead to become a secret admirer. Secret admiration is one thing, but when she doesn't give up hope, wanting to step into the main wife's role, it becomes another.

It is said that Luo Jialiang is taking on his first major villainous role, and there's even a scene where he rapes his "sister." Of course, I haven't seen this part of the play yet. But whenever I see Luo Jialiang, I always feel there's a bit of wickedness in him, like someone who hides bad intentions in his heart, always ready to unexpectedly tease people. Back when he was Sha Fula, Luo Jialiang was an unrepentant little thug and big swindler, doing all sorts of bad things but still claiming, “I have principles; if I'm going to be a swindler, I won't harm women and children.” It’s precisely this complex humanity—revealing a bit of cuteness amidst the badness, giving hope in disappointment—that tugs at the heartstrings. That's why Zhuo Yixin (played by Maggie Cheung), who has met countless men, immediately fell for him upon their first romantic encounter!

Later, Sha Fula, for the sake of money, impersonated the illegitimate son of the Zhuo family, becoming Yixin's "brother." Torn between money and love, after much inner struggle and hesitation, Luo Jialiang chose money over love, abandoning Sha Fula to become Zhuo Yiming. Initially, Zhuo Yiming's motives were relatively simple: scam a large sum of money and then leave. Who knew that Sha Fula would get more and more entangled, gradually leaning towards the identity of Zhuo Yiming, slowly losing his original self. This was due to the most primal desire for money. However, deep down, the sense of being an imposter couldn't resist his inner feelings; he couldn't forget about his "sister"!

The wisdom and tolerance of He Juennian are most vividly displayed within her marriage. Twelve years ago, she already knew that her husband had once cheated on her, but because she deeply loved her husband, she endured for twelve years. Later, when the former mistress appeared, seeing the signs, He Juennian immediately hinted to the other woman, “What matters most for a woman is a relationship that leads to results.” When the mistress came aggressively, she didn’t back down either, calmly and objectively handling the situation, always thinking from her husband's and family's perspective. When Dong Lingzi might cause her husband to fall into the awkward position of being unfaithful to his wife, she finally couldn't help but step out as a wife to defend her position, giving the mistress a scolding!

He Juennian is one of the highlights of this drama. She comes from a prestigious family, elegant and graceful, intelligent and wise. Qi Meizhen is also a seasoned actress, and her acting skills are naturally impeccable. She firmly believes that through personal effort and intelligence, everyone can have a perfect life. He Juennian practices what she preaches, using her wisdom to paint her beautiful life.

If we can ignore He Juennian's beauty, if we can overlook her elegance, her wisdom will certainly move and impress, leaving a lasting impression. In her, we see the brilliance of humanity, yet it feels completely genuine and vivid. Her beauty lies in her wisdom, her grace, and her tolerance. In the drama, she often clashes with her mother-in-law, who is from humble origins. The mother-in-law always shouts at her, causing chaos wherever she goes. However, He Juennian always knows when to shut her mouth, enduring委屈with full grievance. As a result, every argument ends with the mother-in-law apologizing rather than He Juennian simply becoming a punching bag.

The friction between the desire for money and sincere emotions creates significant tension, and any initial good intentions gradually disappear in this struggle. The fortress of human nature slowly collapses, ultimately leading to irreversible consequences!

Rich families, triangular romances, illegitimate children—these plots are theoretically overused. If you're a TVB fan, after watching the first few episodes, you could logically guess the subsequent plot twists. But different actors bring different styles to their performances, especially when they're all seasoned veterans. The return of these old hands is itself a performance. It's no wonder that although "Fortune Gate" didn't match the ratings of "Palace Intrigues," it still tightly held the audience's attention. Because we're no longer captivated merely by the plot; we're more interested in how these veteran actors portray the allure of money and push the portrayal of the human crucible to its extreme!
