Kucher's conversation with Lee Kai-Fu: Stay away from Twitter

by anonymous on 2013-09-18 21:33:47

Li Kaifu and Path investor Ashton Kutcher were recently guests on Tencent Technology's program (photographed by Tencent Technology).

The social networking site Twitter has recently confirmed that it has submitted an S-1 filing to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC), indicating that Twitter is embarking on its Initial Public Offering (IPO) journey. This will be the most anticipated social network giant IPO since Facebook's listing, undoubtedly a crucial moment for Twitter.

It is estimated that Twitter's valuation could reach $15 billion to $20 billion, not only bringing substantial returns to Twitter investors but also potentially reigniting investors' enthusiasm for consumer application and internet company investments. As a result, companies like Airbnb, Dropbox, and Uber may collectively regain capital market attention regarding their IPO timelines.

In this sensitive period, Tencent Technology organized a special event, inviting "Jobs" actor Ashton Kutcher and Li Kaifu, Chairman of Innovation Works, to share stories about their interactions with Twitter and Weibo.

Mentioning Ashton Kutcher, those in the tech industry might be less familiar, but those who know him recognize him as a typical example of a Hollywood star investing in emerging enterprises. Besides portraying "Jobs" in the movie "Jobs: Get Inspired," he has invested in a series of internet companies, including Path, Airbnb, Flipboard, Fab, Foursquare, and Summly.

Many people in the industry claim to know Li Kaifu well; he is a prominent figure online, with 25 million followers on Tencent Weibo, significantly influencing domestic hot internet events. Li Kaifu also frequently publishes foreign news reports via Twitter. Both Ashton Kutcher and Li Kaifu expressed during their exchange that early Twitter was "cool."

However, amidst the strong anticipation for Twitter's IPO and favorable opinions from industry insiders, Ashton Kutcher, as an early user and beneficiary of Twitter, now expresses dissatisfaction with Twitter's current state. Ashton Kutcher told Tencent Technology that Twitter once completely transformed his life. However, with large corporations moving in, Twitter has become filled with noise.

Kutcher further criticized Twitter—retweeting severely disrupts Twitter, introducing a lot of "noise" into the system, burying some truly valuable content. His remarks indicate disappointment with the Twitter platform. As one of the first beneficiaries of Twitter and the first user to gain a million followers, Ashton Kutcher's shift is noteworthy.

Became the first celebrity with over a million followers after competing with CNN

As a new-generation American star, Ashton Kutcher has keen insight into social media and skillfully uses emerging platforms to boost his popularity, promote his brand, and invest in companies. In 2009, Ashton Kutcher bet with CNN to see whose followers would first reach the ten-million level. Ultimately, Ashton Kutcher defeated CNN within four days, becoming the first celebrity on the Twitter platform with over a million followers.

Recalling this experience, Ashton Kutcher revealed in his exchange with Li Kaifu that he initially loved Twitter because users provided timely feedback on products, akin to a massive focus group always available for use. He learned a great deal from these feedbacks, which represented the democratization of media.

"We no longer need media giants to tell us narrow or biased news and information, depending on the type of media. We can create our own news and have our own biases. I think this is absolutely brilliant."

After recognizing the power of Twitter's fan economy, Ashton Kutcher cleverly targeted one of the world's largest news organizations, CNN, declaring war on it and beginning a competition against the entire news network to see who would first reach a million followers.

"At the time, CNN had over 800,000 followers, and I only had over 500,000, so I was already behind by more than 300,000. But I knew how to operate the platform to make my follower growth exceed theirs. When CNN broadcasted the news asking people to follow their Twitter account, I was doing the same thing on online internet radio." Ashton Kutcher recalled, ultimately achieving success with his strategy.

Ashton Kutcher believed that the key reason he became the first Twitter user with over a million followers was challenging the entire network alone, gaining external favor, and using this power to surpass television media long trusted by the masses.

Another unexplained reason is that Ashton Kutcher has close personal ties with Twitter Chairman Jack Dorsey. The celebrity effect allowed Ashton Kutcher and Twitter to achieve mutually beneficial promotion. In March 2011, Ashton Kutcher collaborated with Twitter third-party client developer UberMedia to launch his exclusive Twitter client A.Plus.

This is similar to influential figures domestically, where social media allows them to gain both fame and fortune. For instance, through Sina and Tencent Weibo, Li Kaifu has enormous social influence. While maintaining his relevance through trending events, he also gains hidden monetization by promoting products under Innovation Works' investments. Internet tycoons Lei Jun and Zhou Hongyi mainly use Weibo to promote their products, saving significant advertising costs.

Now reducing Twitter usage and turning toward personal social networks

However, compared to his past battle with CNN, Ashton Kutcher now feels troubled and disappointed with Twitter. He frankly admitted to Li Kaifu that social networks easily disrupt life, causing him to use Twitter less frequently. He realized he was always busy either capturing or sharing moments, failing to fully live in the present.

Despite still personally managing his Twitter account, Ashton Kutcher has clearly reduced his frequency of using Twitter, instead opting for personal social networks. He pointed out that rather than following trends, it’s better to quietly absorb and consider fragmented information until forming real opinions or understanding, eventually integrating them into larger concepts.

"People don't need to know where I am or how I am. There are already enough media outlets capturing, stealing, and selling this information solely for their profit. So I don't need to broadcast every moment; I will post when I feel there is something important."

Possibly due to this reason, Ashton Kutcher subsequently invested in private or real-neighborhood social networks like Path, nextdoor, and Couple. He also uses the Couple app for communication with friends.

"Whenever I receive a message on Couple, I must check it immediately. I know it must be good news, and I'm not afraid to read it. After hearing too much 'noise' on other platforms, you realize 'ah, this is important.'"

In fact, beyond Twitter, Ashton Kutcher maintains a high level of attention to the tech industry. He told Tencent Technology that the first wave of computing was entirely reactive computation. Anytime you search or ask questions, you need to input your query beforehand to get an answer. In recent 3 to 4 years, real-time computing and interaction have become popular, which includes various platforms like Weibo and WeChat.

Ashton Kutcher believes that the next generation of technology will enhance proactive computation concepts. Using dual inputs and static inputs previously used, it can inform users of the weather, schedule, and traffic conditions today, allowing computers to proactively offer suggestions. If this technology can be precise enough, it will be very promising for investment.

Despite being in the entertainment industry, when meeting with industry insiders, Ashton Kutcher first looks at their phone's home screen, especially the apps at the forefront, using these as investment reference points and downloading them for use. As a "big account" on Twitter, Ashton Kutcher has repeatedly launched fierce criticisms against Twitter. During his visit to China, he even sought advice from Li Kaifu on how to maintain his Twitter account.

Li Kaifu also shared insights, stating that he spends 2 hours daily on social media, digesting what's happening outside, finding interesting things, sharing thoughts, and retweeting domestic amusing incidents. Among his tweets, 70% are related to social responsibility, 30% to entertainment and learning, and beyond that, making friends. In the last 4 years, 60 out of the top 100 friends on his list come from social networks.

Li Kaifu also revealed that he gets up at 5 o'clock every day, quickly checking what happened the previous night while scheduling the day's Weibo posts. Even criticizing Ashton Kutcher for being "indifferent" to Twitter, losing the fruits of his battle against CNN. Despite being diagnosed with cancer, Li Kaifu continues to use Weibo daily to comment on hot topics.

However, similar to Ashton Kutcher reducing his Twitter usage, the enthusiasm of domestic celebrities for Weibo is also declining. While Twitter's IPO has sparked heated discussions abroad on social media, this fervor has not been transmitted domestically. On that day, apart from Li Kaifu who was undergoing lymphoma treatment, domestic tech tycoons paid no attention to Twitter's IPO.

Twitter's IPO still faces challenges amid the rise of mobile applications

Twitter has been around for seven years, raising a total of $1.16 billion in venture capital, with over 200 million monthly active users. Market research company eMarketer predicts that Twitter's ad revenue will approach $1 billion in 2014.

Twitter is also encountering a good time: the IPO climate in the internet industry has significantly improved. In the months following Facebook's IPO, most people believed that with Facebook's stock price falling, Twitter's chances of going public were diminishing. However, in recent months, due to significant improvements in mobile business revenue, Facebook's stock price hit a historical high.

In fact, Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg recently stated in an interview that Twitter should not fear going public.

Twitter has also been preparing for its IPO, acquiring multiple companies so far and making some major decisions to increase its revenue. First investor of Twitter, Bill Tai, said: "Twitter can go public now."

Not only is Twitter popular, but its Chinese counterpart, Sina Weibo, is also favored. Investment bank Pacific Crest recently pointed out that Alibaba might strategically increase its stake in Sina Weibo, driving a significant rise in Sina's stock.

However, Sina Weibo is facing significant challenges. With the rise of WeChat and relevant policy impacts, valuable content on Weibo is decreasing, leading to a continuous decline in Sina Weibo's activity. Similarly, as a service, Twitter also shows signs of fatigue, with users like Ashton Kutcher gradually moving away, raising doubts about how far Twitter can go in the future.

Recently, foreign sources indicated that Twitter is actually below external expectations. These sources pointed out that the number of new active users added monthly by Twitter in 2013 was less than 4.5 million. If this growth rate continues, the number of active users by the end of the year will only reach 260 million. This means that the active user base has only grown by 30%, not the previously expected 100%.

Some Twitter employees reflected that many users just try out Twitter and then stop paying attention. Perhaps, Twitter's efforts to inform new users about what "retweet" is and how it differs from "edited retweet" according to its own set of rules and vision make it more of a niche social service product compared to Facebook.

Twitter's biggest challenge lies in the emergence of new applications. Foreign media sharply pointed out that services like Instagram and instant messaging service Snapchat have surfaced, becoming the main tools for people to stay connected with friends and discuss major events. For instance, Instagram recently claimed that its monthly users have reached 150 million, posing a severe challenge to Twitter's current position.
