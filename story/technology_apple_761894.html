YiXun responds to JD.com's four deliveries in one day: warehousing services will be open in the second half of the year

by geekzhang on 2013-05-14 18:16:07

On the afternoon of May 14th, YiXun responded to JD.com's trial run of four deliveries in a day in Beijing. YiXun stated that e-commerce logistics needs to be not only fast but also stable and punctual, and announced that it will officially open its warehousing services in the second half of this year.The following is the full content of YiXun's response:1. We are very glad to see more and more e-commerce companies start paying attention to logistics services. We believe this will better enhance the shopping experience for consumers;2. E-commerce logistics need to be not only fast but also stable and punctual, as well as maintaining low costs while ensuring efficiency. Currently, YiXun's pioneering three-deliveries-a-day service has covered multiple cities including Beijing, Shanghai, Shenzhen, Chongqing, and Wuhan. Consumers do not need to pay extra fees under normal shopping conditions;3. This year, YiXun will also build more than ten warehousing and distribution bases in Chengdu, Jinan, Fuzhou, Changchun, etc., and provide one-day-three-deliveries or one-day-two-deliveries delivery services within a 200-kilometer radius around the warehouse bases in major cities. YiXun will actively cooperate with third-party logistics to cover cash-on-delivery (COD) services in 1000 cities nationwide. With the continuous improvement of YiXun's own logistics and backend system upgrades, YiXun will continue to launch two-hour delivery and even time-limited delivery logistics services in the future;4. YiXun's logistics services will not only ensure timely delivery of self-operated goods but also open up to more third-party merchants, utilizing its own infrastructure construction to benefit more merchants and consumers. According to the plan, YiXun will be able to realize door-to-door collection + unified delivery services for third-party merchants in the East China region in the first half of this year, and will officially open warehousing services in the second half of the year, realizing unified door-to-door collection, warehousing, and delivery businesses in the East China and South China regions.
