How did Jack Ma use big data to transform e-commerce?

by anonymous on 2013-10-16 17:34:04

　　The e-commerce of the future will be big data e-commerce, Ma Yun once defined the future of electronic commerce in this way. However, what does big data e-commerce look like? The Alibaba Group has never given an accurate definition or demonstrated it at the practical operation level. Everything remains in the realm of imagination.

　　This year on Singles' Day (November 11), big data e-commerce will for the first time unveil its mysterious veil. On October 15, the Alibaba Group held a "Double 11" press conference in Hangzhou.

　　According to Wang Yulei, Vice President of Tmall, the big data e-commerce of the future at least includes the following three aspects: First, O2O, which is the combination of offline and online; second, real interaction, which is the active interaction between users and manufacturers; third, personalization, which means different experiences for each person. Wang Yulei stated that "Double 11" will focus on these three aspects.

　　In fact, the three major businesses of the Alibaba Group's future layout by Ma Yun also revolve around big data: one is big data e-commerce, namely Alibaba Group; the second is big data finance, namely Micro Financial Group; the third is big data logistics, namely CaiNiao Technology.

　　Alibaba Group and JD.com are racing on two tracks: one is the race to go public, where the company that goes public first will gain a financing advantage; the second is the race in ecological layout. The first track is a "100-meter sprint," while the second track is a "marathon."

　　Offline stores participate for the first time

　　In previous years, Singles' Day was all about creating an online shopping carnival. With the development of mobile internet, this year's Singles' Day will create a collective O2O online-offline carnival.

　　Wang Yulei told the "21st Century Business Herald" that this year's Tmall "Double 11" will break down the boundaries between online and offline commerce, integrating over 30,000 offline stores covering more than 1,000 cities and counties nationwide, fully connecting through the Tmall wireless client.

　　Wang Yulei revealed that more than 300 brands including Uniqlo, GAP, IT, Toys "R" Us, BaoDao Eyewear, YinTai, Haier, Erdos, and Hongtu Sanbao will simultaneously participate in Tmall's "Double 11" Shopping Carnival.

　　The premise of seamless O2O integration is the development of mobile internet technology, specifically embodied in the Tmall wireless client and AutoNavi Map. A relevant official introduced to the "21st Century Business Herald" that consumers can shop in three ways: one is to log into the Tmall client to select products and then navigate to the physical store via AutoNavi Map; two is to use the Tmall client to scan posters and obtain corresponding product coupons; three is to try on clothes in the physical store, scan the barcode of the item with the Tmall client, add it to the shopping cart, and purchase it on "Double 11" day to enjoy a 50% discount.

　　The prospect of the future retail industry form is O2O, which has become an industry consensus. The debate revolves around whether traditional retailers or internet retailers will come out on top. Traditional retailers include Suning, Gome, Walmart, etc., while internet retailers include Taobao, Tmall, JD.com, etc.

　　Representatives of traditional manufacturers such as Suning and Gome are arming themselves with internet technology, breaking through from offline to online; whereas Taobao, Tmall, and JD.com leverage their massive online user base to integrate physical stores into their ecosystem.

　　This time, more than 30,000 stores cover over 1,000 cities and counties, accounting for about half of the total number of cities and counties nationwide. This is just the beginning. Wang Yulei said that next year, more regions and more physical stores will join Tmall's carnival party, experiencing the thrill brought by the impact of the internet.

　　Big data transforms e-commerce

　　O2O integration is just part of the carnival. Tmall has prepared 200 million yuan in unrestricted cash red packets for consumers. Unlike previous years, this year Tmall will adopt a socialized method of distribution: consumers who grab red packets can share them with friends, and while friends receive shared red packets, they also have the chance to double their own red packets.

　　Socialized shopping is the basic logic of social e-commerce, and the foundation of achieving this is Sina Weibo, in which Alibaba Group holds shares. Wang Yulei stated that for this purpose, Tmall and Sina Weibo's user systems have been fully integrated, and the friendship relationships of consumers on Sina Weibo will serve as the basis for red packet circulation.

　　Wang Yulei stated that after "Double 11," Tmall will further develop more sharing, recommendation, and communication functions based on friendship relationships. More importantly, these sharing interactions will generate a large amount of commercial demand, promoting new models of future commerce such as C2B.

　　The foundation of socialization is big data technology: because only big data technology can accurately depict the different relationships between people. Wang Yulei said, "Using big data technology, the red packet game will connect everyone in the real-world social network."

　　This is not all that big data can achieve. The second point achieved by big data is personalized e-commerce, or "different faces for each person."

　　Wang Yulei said that this year, Tmall will base itself on big data operations to give each consumer their own exclusive event venue. The content of the personalized venue is mainly based on the data analysis of users' behaviors during the pre-event warm-up period, such as attention, browsing, adding to the shopping cart, and saving items. Additionally, all relevant information such as red packets grabbed by consumers and coupons received will be consolidated, ultimately forming a personalized venue exclusive to the consumer.

　　The third characteristic of big data e-commerce is big data logistics. Wang Yulei revealed that this year's logistics data radar will cooperate with the National Meteorological Bureau Data Center to provide weather forecasts and current conditions for national highways, guiding real-time road traffic situations.

　　Tmall's data shows that more than one million express delivery personnel across the country are preparing for Tmall's "Double 11." Domestic thirteen major courier companies have added more than 150 sorting centers, increased operational areas exceeding two million square meters, and the major courier companies have added 4,000 vehicles.
