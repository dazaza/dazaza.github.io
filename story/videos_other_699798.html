Gang Posing as Migrant Workers to Slack Off and Deceive for Road Fees Gets Arrested

by fd12yt9hgyi on 2012-03-09 16:42:44

A morning's work, and a few people didn't do a bit of it. They didn't eat little at lunch though. After the afternoon work began, Peng Wei and the others sat in their respective work places resting, and they all fell asleep. The foreman was worried because the schedule was already tight. Not only did these workers eat and live for free, but they also affected the enthusiasm of other workers, which was really infuriating.

On the first day of work, it was like this. The foreman saw it and was very angry, constantly urging them. But these 7 people acted as if they didn't hear anything.

□Fast reporter Zhang Chujie

(All characters in the article are pseudonyms)

Waiting for that sentence

This group of fake migrant workers consisted of 7 people, 6 from Henan and 1 from Sichuan. Peng Wei from Henan was the leader. He was responsible for contacting projects. Wherever laborers were needed, he immediately agreed and gathered the workers. On September 8, 2010, he led 6 people from Henan to a construction site in Xinghua, Jiangsu, with bedding rolls, settling down properly.

Facing dismissal, besides cursing and causing trouble, Peng Wei and his group had other ways to cheat money.

It turned out that the 7 people had already planned before setting out. They decided not to work well wherever they went, forcing the foreman to dismiss them. Then they would demand compensation. In this way and that, apart from earning enough for the road fare, they could earn a bit more without doing any work. This method of cheating money was Peng Wei's usual trick. As his accomplice said, "We all know that Peng Wei makes a living by this."

Collective non-working

On September 11, the foreman decided not to use these 7 people anymore. Upon hearing about being dismissed, the 7 people suddenly became spirited, "We came such a long way. You can't just say we can't work and then not let us work. No, you have to compensate us for our road fare and lost wages." Peng Wei said righteously.

"Without compensation, we won't leave." Peng Wei and his gang sat in the foreman's office, cursing and making a fuss. Both sides argued fiercely and even started fighting. With many people on his side, Peng Wei refused to compromise at all. The foreman was cursed and beaten. This group still wouldn't stop, running to the construction site to cause trouble, making other workers unable to work normally.

Cheaters come out to cheat, usually pretending to be an identity, either transforming into a certain bureau chief or pretending to be a section chief or director of some kind. But now in this era, even the identity of migrant workers is being impersonated. Not long ago, the police caught 7 fake migrant workers in Jiangning. They went through various construction sites in Nanjing, Lishui, Taizhou, Danyang, etc. Their way of making money wasn't by working hard, but by not working hard...

The next day, the foreman arranged the work, issued tools, and the 7 people started working. However, this group took their time, walking around with their tools, or squatting on the side smoking, playing cards, chatting idly. A one-hour job took them four hours to finish.

"You don't work and still want compensation? Impossible!" The foreman refused the request.

What do they want to do?

Cheating 6 times

On September 10, the foreman watched them all day, but this group became even more scattered. The 7 people were assigned to different positions to work, but soon they gathered together to play. In Peng Wei's words, they were "slacking off". While the surrounding workers were busy knocking and hammering, these 7 people were having a great time playing.

Every time they were dismissed, Peng Wei demanded compensation for road fare. In February 2011, this group rushed from a construction site in Shanghai to a construction site in Lishui County, but falsely claimed they came from their hometown in Henan, defrauding the difference in train tickets. Thus, in the two years between 2010 and 2011, Peng Wei and his gang cheated six times in various places in Jiangsu, succeeding every time. Tasting the sweetness of success, the seven people arrived at a construction site in Jiangning District on April 15, 2011, using the same old tricks. After being dismissed, they fought and caused trouble, demanding 6000 yuan in compensation. But this time, the foreman immediately reported it to the police, and Peng Wei and the seven others were caught on the spot. By then, the seven had defrauded nearly 50,000 yuan.

"Impossible!"

Yesterday morning, Qixia Court heard this case. The seven people admitted their illegal actions without hesitation. The case is still under further review.
