English games compilation

by kayaedu on 2009-08-28 16:59:59

(Kaya Education www.kakayaya.com)

1. English Quiz: Various English riddles and simple small puzzles are hung or posted indoors in card form. Participants can choose a card, read the riddle or requirement in English, and provide the correct answer.

Example: "It's deep and blue. There're many fishes in it. It sounds like the third letter. What is it?"

"What do you call your father’s father?"

2. English Actions: The organizer gives commands, and participants perform actions to demonstrate their understanding of the knowledge. Simple examples include: "Clap your hands," "A monkey." More complex ones might be: "Fly a kite," "Use computers," etc.

3. English Words:

a) Dart Tool Setup: Participants throw darts and answer questions based on the number they hit. Hitting the bullseye means lucky pass.

b) Spinning Wheel: Participants spin the wheel or pointer and answer related questions based on the final result. These activities can test vocabulary or language knowledge. For example, categories of words may include: vegetables, fruits, food, colors, school subjects, sports, stationery, animals, days of the week, etc. Examples could be: "Name ... kinds of vegetables." Language knowledge includes various cultural common sense, communication skills, or language points, such as: "Tell us how to greet others."

4. English Games: These activities require teamwork to complete.

a) Patchwork: Pre-design an animal or character outline on a blackboard or board paper. Participant A covers their eyes while Participant B gives instructions like "up/down/left/right" to guide A to stick or draw the missing parts.

b) Makeup Artist: Participant A wears a blank mask and sits in a fixed position. Participant B stands about 10 meters away with watercolor pens ready. The organizer gives instructions for Participant B to complete the makeup task. B can only carry one pen at a time, so they must run back and forth to choose different pens. Example: "Draw a red nose." "Draw a blue square mouth." Winners are determined by speed, accuracy, and realism.

c) Pick Fruits: The organizer gives a command: "Get a cat." "Get a telephone." Participant A gives English instructions like "up, down, right, left" to guide Participant B (who has their eyes covered) to touch pictures and pick fruits from the tree. The more fruits picked within a certain time, the better.

d) Maze: Participant A uses commands like "go ahead, turn left, turn right, back, stop" to guide Participant B (with their eyes covered) to walk out of the maze.

5. Word Chain:

a) Word Chain: The teacher provides a word, and students use the last letter of that word as the first letter of a new word, continuing in this manner. For example: "desk — kite — egg..."

b) Peacock Display: The teacher provides a word, and students create phrases or sentences starting with that word. For example: "have: have a cat, have a dog, have a pen..."

6. English Supermarket: Students play the role of shopkeepers or waiters serving customers dining or shopping in a restaurant or market, practicing regular service language or bargaining language.

7. Card Playing: Letters or words are presented in card form. Players randomly draw cards and connect their own or each other's letters or words to form meaningful words or sentences.
