Do the Hong Kong and mainland China versions of the iPhone 5s support China Mobile's 4G service?

by anonymous on 2013-10-02 17:15:52

Today, Guangdong Mobile has launched the reservation service and commercial package for 4G in Guangzhou and Shenzhen. With the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) set to issue 4G licenses, the mobile version of the iPhone is also imminent, and many users are looking forward to using TD-LTE on the next generation of iPhones.

Depending on the carrier and region, there are already five models of the iPhone 5s: A1533, A1528, A1530, A1453, and A1457. Since Apple's Hong Kong website clearly stated that the A1530 and A1529 models of the iPhone support the TD-LTE standard, many users have chosen this version for future use with Mobile 4G.

However, PCINLIFE posted a Weibo yesterday stating, "At today’s launch event for China Mobile's 4G phones in Hangzhou, inserting a normally functioning 4G SIM card into a Hong Kong edition A1530 iPhone 5s resulted in a 'no signal' prompt." PCINLIFE suggests that this might mean only the Mobile customized version of the iPhone supports Mobile 4G.

Nevertheless, such testing methods through SIM card insertion are evidently flawed. Theoretically, TD-LTE is backward compatible with GSM networks, so even if Mobile's 4G signal cannot be used, it should still show GSM signal. We speculate that this could be due to the APN preset in the Hong Kong edition device for Mobile's SIM card. After Mobile secures its 4G license, Apple will likely update the carrier information, at which point the Hong Kong edition iPhone 5s should be able to smoothly support Mobile 4G.

Another focal point is whether the A1528 (iPhone 5s) and A1526 (iPhone 5c) models purchased from the mainland Apple Store are completely out of luck with Mobile 4G?

It seems no one doubts this, as the information from the mainland official website shows that these two models only support GSM/EDGE/UMTS/HSPA+/DC-HSDPA.

Theoretically, different models of the iPhone differ in their baseband chips, but we were surprised to find that despite the clear labeling of model A1528 on the back cover, the "Settings" under "Regulation" is marked as A1530. This might imply that these two models do not differ in their baseband chips and are merely version-limited in their support for Mobile TD-LTE network.

If our guess is wrong, does this mean that after MIIT issues the 4G license, Apple will separately release a batch of A1530 devices supporting TD-LTE?

We previously received information from the carrier side indicating that the first batch of iPhones will later be upgraded via firmware to support TD-LTE networks. As China Unicom and China Telecom lean towards the LTE-FDD 4G network standard, their TD-LTE networks are still in the preliminary trial phase. They may provide 4G services by leasing China Mobile's TD-LTE network.

A “blue shirt” at the Sanlitun Apple Store was unable to clearly answer our aforementioned questions. He believes that the existing A1528 model in the domestic market should not be able to support TD-LTE through a firmware upgrade.

Before the official explanation, we can only make theoretical analyses. If you hope to reliably use Mobile 4G on an iPhone, you may need to wait a bit longer.
