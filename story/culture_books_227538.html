"《Holding Hands for a Thousand Years · Part I & II》 A laundry maid meets her present-day boyfriend"

by jqrxass on 2009-08-23 13:10:19

Modern girl Mo Xiaoyan, through a twist of fate, brings along an amulet that triggers time travel, transporting her back to ancient times. She arrives in Hangzhou and becomes a laundry maid at the Governor's residence.

Unintentionally, she encounters Prince Rui, Endmu Zixuan, who has infiltrated the Governor's residence in disguise. As they spend more time together, feelings develop between them. The protagonist discovers that Endmu Zixuan has come to deal with the Governor and also finds out that her past-life self, Mo Xiaoyan, was actually the Governor's biological daughter.

An unexpected turn of events leads to the Governor's suicide, and Mo Xiaoyan enters the palace with Zixuan. In the palace, she encounters a man who looks identical to her current boyfriend in this life - Endmu Linfeng, Zixuan's younger brother.

The powerful Sheng Prince intends to marry his daughter, An'an Princess, to Zixuan as a means to pressure the throne; to ensure Zixuan isn't harmed, Mo Xiaoyan chooses to let go and agrees to marry Endmu Linfeng...

Volume One • Hangzhou Chapter

"You know, if you've played this game, you might not be so devoted. This male lead is truly one of the best men around!" Xiao Q teased me, "It's not good to like someone who's cold as ice!" I smiled lightly. Perhaps this game will bring about some changes in me. The game started, and music suddenly played, making my heart tremble. I froze momentarily – this game actually uses this melody, "Connected, connected, we two have pledged for a hundred years, whoever dies at ninety-seven, we'll wait three years on the Bridge of Helplessness..." What kind of story could this be? Secretly anticipating, from this tune onwards, I will be immersed in the love and hatred of this game. Hopefully, the emotions in the game won't be too painful... I wish for a perfect ending – at this point, I can no longer endure any heartbreaking stories.
