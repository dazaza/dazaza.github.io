Computer radiation spots are a new term in skincare that has emerged recently. In the information age, people enjoy the efficiency and convenience brought by computers, but at the same time, our skin is also suffering from silent damage caused by computers. This includes various sizes of computer radiation spots, dry skin with fine lines, yellowish complexion, enlarged pores, small pimples appearing, dry eyes, and worsening dark circles... So, how can we easily deal with the troublesome "computer radiation spots"?

1. Pay attention to skin cleanliness. Apply an isolation cream before going online, and after finishing your internet session, the first task is to cleanse your face. Use warm water with facial cleanser to thoroughly clean your face, washing away all the dust attracted by static electricity, then apply gentle skincare products. Doing this regularly can reduce damage and nourish the skin.

2. For people who frequently use the internet, increasing nutrition is very important. Vitamin B is beneficial for mental workers. If you sleep late and the quality of sleep isn't good, you should eat more animal livers and fresh fruits and vegetables, which contain rich B vitamins. Additionally, meats, fish, and dairy products help enhance memory; chocolates, wheat rings, seafood, and dried fruits can improve the coordination of the nervous system, making them the best snacks while online.

3. Drinking wolfberry juice and carrot juice at irregular intervals has significant effects on eye care and skincare. If you are very concerned about your appearance, then discard carbonated drinks immediately and switch to carrot juice or other fresh fruit juices.

4. Drink 2-3 cups of green tea and eat one orange every morning. Tea leaves contain abundant vitamin A precursors, which, after being absorbed by the body, can quickly convert into vitamin A. Vitamin A not only synthesizes rhodopsin but also improves vision in dim light, so green tea not only eliminates the harm of computer radiation but also protects and enhances vision. If you're not used to drinking green tea, chrysanthemum tea can also resist computer radiation and regulate bodily functions.

5. You should buy a new computer if possible, and generally avoid using old computers. The radiation from old computers is usually more severe; under the same distance and model conditions, it is generally 1-2 times that of a new computer. When using a computer, adjust the screen brightness properly. Generally speaking, the brighter the screen, the stronger the electromagnetic radiation, and vice versa. However, don't make it too dim either, as it may affect the effect and cause eye fatigue. Also, maintain an appropriate distance from the screen. The closer you are to the screen, the greater the electromagnetic radiation exposure, so it's better to be more than half a meter away.

6. Remember to shut down your computer when sleeping face down, instead of just turning off the screen. Actually, the keyboard emits more radiation than the display. Turning off the screen alone cannot eliminate the radiation, and since we often sleep face down with our heads directly facing the keyboard, this is clearly a misconception. Correct this bad habit promptly!
