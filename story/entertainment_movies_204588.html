The story revolves around a character named Xin Yi, who is an embodiment of the "便利貼女孩" (Sticky Note Girl) trope: a mild-mannered, unassuming administrative assistant at a law firm, responsible for handling miscellaneous tasks—both others' and her own. She is gentle, simple in her desires, lacks assertiveness, and much like a sticky note, may seem insignificant but is indispensable in daily life. Coming from an average working-class family, she bears one of the most common names in the country—Xin Yi. Her small daily wishes include hoping her boss won't scold her, that colleagues will assign her less extra work, that someone might occasionally value her opinions, and that her boyfriend will remember her birthday.

Despite her non-threatening exterior, Xin Yi exudes an incredible sense of security, making her the first person people think of when they want to vent about their boss. However, the seemingly ordinary life of Xin Yi takes a dramatic turn when she unexpectedly has a one-night stand with Cun Xi, a universally admired prince charming figure, and ends up pregnant. Following this, Xin Yi marries into the Ji family under the circumstances of a shotgun wedding.

After marriage, Xin Yi discovers a more endearing, boyish side of Cun Xi and realizes he's the first person who genuinely gets jealous on her behalf, leading her to fall in love with him uncontrollably. However, complications arise when Cun Xi’s official girlfriend, Anna, returns to the country. Realizing the truth, Xin Yi awakens to the fact that she no longer wants to be the compliant, complaint-free person who simply goes along with fate. Determined to take control of her own destiny, Xin Yi decides to part ways with Cun Xi.

Following Dylan's encouragement, Xin Yi embarks on a transformative journey of self-discovery and empowerment—a path that marks the evolution and reinvention of the once meek "sticky note girl" into a confident and independent woman.
