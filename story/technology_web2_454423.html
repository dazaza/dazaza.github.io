Performance appraisal is one of the effective tools for companies to improve their performance actions and promote efficiency. The current appraisal tool used by the company is the KPI index. KPI, or Key Performance Index, is derived from the breakdown of organizational objectives and tasks analysis within the company. By introducing the KPI appraisal system, the company has already grasped the management ideas and methods in continuous improvement of performance actions, and combined with practical applications in various departments, it has been utilized quite sufficiently. Of course, there are still several key points we need to break through:

First, each department must further implement KPI appraisal as a fundamental part of departmental management. According to the company's requirements for departmental appraisal, the first step is to require self-assessment of KPI appraisal within the department. The aim is to strengthen the responsibility awareness of managers, understand and control the performance situation within their department, and make improvements accordingly. Therefore, self-assessment is an important part of the appraisal process for the units under the jurisdiction of the managers. Many managers may raise various doubts, such as whether self-assessment is just a formality, lack of detailed data making self-assessment difficult, or other reasons to avoid it. This clearly does not meet the requirements of the appraisal work.

Whether self-assessment is just a formality depends on the attitude towards self-assessment and the handling method. Excellent self-assessment can more truly reflect work performance and accurately describe job responsibilities and performance gaps. Therefore, adding this appraisal link is not simply about assigning a score; its art lies in providing services to more managers.

Second, the appraisal process and responsibilities need further clarification. In the appraisal work, the Human Resources Department plays a role in organizing, educating, and promoting improvements. The responsible departments are the main implementing units of the appraisal. In the past, people always thought that the HR Department would directly assign scores, and they would accept whatever score they receive. But this is not the case. During the implementation of the appraisal, the HR Department often faces the problem of fragmented and unsystematic data provided by data providers, leading to biased appraisal factors. In fact, the execution power of the appraisal lies in systems and processes. If we overly rely on manual efforts to push implementation, it is like using humans to plow fields in modern times—labor-intensive, time-consuming, and inefficient.

Third, the KPI appraisal should be effectively implemented in the work improvement and performance enhancement of every individual. The company holds KPI appraisal meetings in the middle of each month to inform all departments about the KPI appraisal results from the previous month. The purpose is to let managers clearly understand the performance situation. For example, regarding sales targets, if some individuals do not meet them, why didn't they meet them, what improvement measures will be taken in the future, and who will be responsible for their implementation. We don't expect superficial work to continue. After attending the appraisal meeting, whether specific work is reorganized, arranged, and planned accordingly is something we need to address.

Therefore, letting every employee understand the work objectives of their department (team objectives) and combining individual work performance with team objectives requires managers to clearly recognize the importance of performance feedback. Simply put, managers must act as coaches who help each individual continuously break through and achieve performance improvements.

Thus, some corporate officials always feel that corporate culture is for motivating and constraining employees.
