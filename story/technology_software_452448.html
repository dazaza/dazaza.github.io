99 Health Network Introduction: Drinking tea at the right time can have a certain health-preserving effect on the body. However, drinking tea at inappropriate times may instead be detrimental to one's health. Especially for women, there are certain considerations when it comes to drinking tea. It is advised to avoid drinking tea during five specific periods.

1. During the monthly menstrual period.

At this time, menstrual blood consumes a significant amount of iron within the body. Therefore, women should consume more iron-rich fruits and vegetables like spinach, grapes, and apples. However, if one has the habit of drinking tea after meals during this time, the tea contains up to 50% tannic acid, which interferes with the absorption of iron by the intestinal mucosa, greatly reducing the level of iron absorbed. This can easily lead to the formation of deposits with iron in the intestines or with iron from blood supplements.

2. Women who are pregnant should not drink tea.

Strong tea contains caffeine concentrations as high as 10%, which can increase the frequency of urination and heart rate in pregnant women, adding extra strain on the heart and kidneys. It may even lead to pregnancy-induced hypertension syndrome, so it’s best to avoid drinking too much tea during pregnancy.

3. Pregnant women nearing childbirth should also avoid drinking excessive amounts of tea.

If a pregnant woman drinks too much strong tea before giving birth, the caffeine in the tea can stimulate excitement and cause insomnia. If a pregnant woman does not get enough sleep before childbirth, it could result in exhaustion during labor and may even lead to difficult delivery.

4. Women who have just given birth and plan to breastfeed should avoid drinking too much tea.

If large amounts of tea are consumed during this period, the high concentration of tannic acid in tea can be absorbed by the mucous membranes, affecting the blood circulation of the mammary glands and inhibiting milk secretion, leading to insufficient milk production. Additionally, caffeine from tea consumed by the mother can pass into the breast milk and directly affect the health of the baby.

5. Women going through menopause.

Besides experiencing dizziness and general weakness, they may also experience an accelerated heartbeat, irritability, and poor sleep quality. Drinking too much tea can exacerbate these symptoms. Therefore, those who enjoy drinking tea but are going through these special stages should do so in moderation to avoid negatively impacting their health!
