I've been doing an independent blog for 3 years now, and many people feel that blogs are no longer as popular. However, after the hype dies down, what remains is quality content. Since it's quality content, it must have its value.

Famous bloggers like Yueguang are actually very thoughtful individuals. Therefore, their writings are very profound and precise. I believe readers of these quality blogs benefit greatly because they perceive the value of these blogs. Recently, I've been thinking about one question: What is the core value of a blog?

The internet never lacks novelty. Thus, bloggers enjoy constantly searching for new things and being the first to introduce them to their readers. But that's all there is to it. Although such blogs can attract a group of fashion-conscious readers, they lack high core value because anyone can do this. This has led to the emergence of "Geekr" - a group of tech enthusiasts who specialize in chasing the latest trends. So, our independent blogs cannot continue doing this.

So, how should we proceed? In the past, introductory articles merely described something using words or pictures. They may catch the eye, but they lack personal usage experiences, reviews, and analyses of their value. This is the core value we lack - thought.

A blog without thought is like a beautifully decorated restaurant with terrible food. It might attract many customers based on appearance alone, but no one will come back for a second time. A homely eatery with delicious food, on the other hand, leaves a lasting impression and makes you want to return for more.

If you tally up the pageviews (PV) of an independent blog, you can understand the stickiness of the content. Pay attention to the PV value! It directly and proportionally reflects the value of the blog. If your blog is relatively new, the number of unique visitors (IP) doesn't have to be high, but the average PV per visitor should be at least 5. Having stickiness indicates having value.

There's another standard to measure by: the activity level of comments. Please pay attention to this as well.

Finally, if you're managing an independent blog, please remember:

1. You can lack anything, but absolutely not thought.

2. Completely sever ties with hosted blogs (platform-based, shared hosting, space-based). Otherwise, why bother being independent?

3. You can introduce many novel things on your blog, but your visitors will likely just take a look and never return.

4. Don't expect writing a blog to bring you any value unless your traffic surpasses Yueguang's.

5. Leave room for others to add their thoughts.

That's all for now, everyone!
