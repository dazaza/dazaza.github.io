UT-developed system

by wgyuvs8a on 2012-02-24 14:13:50

T-shirts in drums are easy to arrange, save shop space, and the cylindrical packaging can prevent customers from separating clothes. Shop assistants don't have to follow behind and tidy up the shop all the time. (There are sample displays on the in-store display racks. When customers want to try on a size, they just need to pick the right one. On Uniqlo's Taobao flagship store, among the wall full of cans, find the desired one and unscrew the cap.) In April 2007, the UT flagship store in Japan fully embodying this design concept opened in Harajuku, a hub for trends. 500 types of T-shirts were displayed on the whole wall, and each floor was equipped with a "UT Search" retrieval machine. This system specially developed for UT allows customers to search for their favorite T-shirts from various angles such as color, graphics, theme, keywords, etc., and also to find the location of the product's display. The new T-shirt selection method made UT a topic of conversation in Japan as soon as it launched, and some products even sold out instantly. For several consecutive days, the store was crowded with a large number of customers.

The original meaning of UT is "Tee with printed patterns." All UT series products have two label plates. In addition to the usual composition and barcode plate found on other Uniqlo products, there is another label introducing the background of the designer of this product. The designers come from all walks of life: it could be a London graffiti artist or British supermodel Agyness Deyn. Disney's new movie "Alice in Wonderland" had just premiered globally, and UT immediately put up the Alice limited series.

Sometimes things that merely exist in people's memories may be reborn through UT: like the covers of "VOGUE" issued over the past hundred years, or the mobile suit Gundam which first hit the silver screen in 1979. UT has become a platform showcasing more designs.

UT equals influence. "To me, so-called trendy brands are those brand stores I want to visit whenever I have time," said Shi Yiwen, a 25-year-old office worker, "because they always tell me about new trends and new things."

In fact, Sato Kashiwa hopes that Uniqlo will always be a topic of conversation. He set the slogan for the new flagship store as "From Shanghai to the World," because "Shanghai is close to Tokyo, rather than emphasizing differences, we should face the world together." Previously in the New York store, Sato emphasized Tokyo, because "this would make young people in New York feel cool."

At the Uniqlo flagship store on Nanjing West Road in Shanghai, the company hired BCJ (Bohlin Cywinski Jackson), the same firm that designed Apple Stores and Bill Gates' mansion, for the store design. The triangular terrain at the intersection of Nanjing West Road and Wujia River focuses the eyes of passers-by on that completely transparent tall building, while the nearby pedestrian street and subway exit ensure a steady flow of people.
