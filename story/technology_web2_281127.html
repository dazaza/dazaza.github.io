Site Supervisor: Has many years of experience in managing and implementing various types of high-altitude operations on buildings, assists the project manager in scheduling personnel at the operation site and providing operational guidance.

II. Operating Procedures for Exterior Cleaning Staff

Staff must comply with all standards agreed upon by the company and client when performing tasks; during the operation process, safety operating procedures and quality standards must be strictly followed to ensure timely completion of tasks with high quality. Staff should be courteous, respectful, considerate, and friendly when communicating with clients, avoiding confrontation. Staff should actively cooperate with clients in supervising service quality, accepting clients' guidance and inspections. If the operation process may affect or damage them, written permission from the other party must be obtained before proceeding; otherwise, any damages caused by improper operation or without permission will result in personal liability.

III. Construction Plan

1. Exterior Cleaning Method: Currently, there are mainly two methods for exterior cleaning - hanging board and window wiping.

- Hanging Rope (high-altitude operation rope, academic name nylon rope, diameter 18mm, tensile force 240,000 Newtons (approximately 2.5 tons))

- Auxiliary Rope (safety protection rope): academic name nylon rope, diameter 16mm, tensile force 22,000 Newtons (approximately 2 tons)

- Hanging Board (made of ash wood)

- Steel Wire Rope (diameter 10mm)

- U-shaped Crab Buckle (steel made)

- Self-locker: Automatically activates the auxiliary rope as a safety measure if the working rope has issues.

- Safety Belt

These seven components form a seated high-altitude hanging board.

2. First, select a fixed place on the rooftop platform to tie the ropes, such as pipes, etc. The ropes should be tied several times, repeatedly tested under heavy loads to ensure no mistakes. All corners, edges, and eaves where the ropes pass should be padded with rubber or carpet blocks to prevent friction.

3. Tie a special knot in the hanging rope inside the U-shaped crab buckle on the seated hanging board. Attach a bucket to the board edge filled with half a bucket of cleaning agent (chemical solution). Operation personnel should carry glass scrapers, water removers, and cloths and can then start work. Due to a special knotting method, the hanging board will not slide down during work. Personnel control the rope freely, simply lifting it slightly causes the board to slide down accordingly until the task is completed.

4. Personnel stationed on the rooftop provide safety protection.

5. Personnel stationed at the base provide patrol safety protection.

Exterior Cleaning Conditions:

1. Climate Conditions: Exterior cleaning must be conducted under favorable climate conditions, with wind speed less than 5 levels. Work stops above level 5. Additionally, rain, snow, fog with poor visibility, high temperatures (above 38 degrees Celsius), and low temperatures (below 0 degrees Celsius) are unsuitable for exterior cleaning.

2. Personnel Conditions: Exterior cleaning requires good physical and psychological conditions, age under 40, weight around 60 kilograms, normal blood pressure, good eyesight, and no fear of heights.

3. Equipment Conditions: Exterior cleaning equipment must be in good condition, such as replacing hanging boards showing signs of wear or partial fiber breakage immediately.

Preparations for Using Hanging Boards:

(1) Weather situation.

(2) On-site reconnaissance.

(3) Tool preparation.

(4) Staff dressed neatly, wearing safety belts as per regulations, self-lockers hung separately on safety ropes.

(5) On-site safety supervisors present, with two people assigned—one at the top and one at the bottom. Place high-altitude operation warning signs, ensuring strict compliance and absolute safety.

Operational Process:

(1) Operation personnel sit in the designated position on the hanging board, securing all tools on the upper side of the board.

(2) Gradually lower the hanging board to reach the first working position.

(3) Use a water gun (or spray bottle) to rinse the area, preliminarily removing dust, then apply the cleaning agent to the glass or wall corner. Afterward, use a scraper or brush to wipe, finally drying the wall with a cloth.

(4) Once a position is complete, adjust to the next position and continue cleaning until the vertical position is finished, then move horizontally.

(5) Throughout the entire process, safety supervisors must continuously monitor the site and cannot leave their posts.

(6) It is strictly forbidden to use alkaline cleaning agents for glass curtain walls, as this could cause severe consequences like peeling and discoloration within half a month.

Post-Operation:

(1) Collect and inspect the hanging rope and safety rope for any damage. If any damage is found, the rope should be discarded.

(2) Clean other tools and apply lubricating oil to the spring part of the self-locker.

(3) Record the work performed during the shift, signed off by the safety officer and team leader.

IV. Construction Tools:

High-altitude cables, safety ropes, safety belts, and other related materials and accompanying tools.

V. Cleaning Agents:

The cleaning agents used by Qingdao Liangxin Cleaning Company are neutral-to-alkaline cleaning agents. After dilution, the pH value ranges between 8-10, which will not harm exterior tiles, glass, or other materials.

VI. Safety Assurance:

Under the premise of ensuring safety, exterior cleaning operations must guarantee quality, both complementing each other. All high-altitude operation employees of our company have undergone high-altitude operation training, obtaining corresponding certificates, with very rich practical operation experience. All high-altitude operation employees are insured by insurance companies for personal accidental injury insurance. The high-altitude operation team has accumulated rich experience through multiple actual battles, achieving 100% safe construction.
