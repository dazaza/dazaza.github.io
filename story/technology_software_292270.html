Advantages of Studying in Japan

by bridge2009 on 2009-12-04 14:18:30

① A world economic superpower, with cutting-edge technology in IT, engineering, automotive industries, and more. The business and financial sectors are highly developed, and there are many internationally renowned enterprises. Fashion and sports entertainment lead the entire Asia-Pacific region, making it a huge demand market for excellent professional and managerial talents.

② One of the most advanced countries in education, with over 600 institutions of higher learning. Teaching facilities and materials are top-notch and comprehensive. Faculty is strong, and teaching concepts are leading-edge, having produced multiple Nobel Prize winners throughout history. The government and private enterprises offer a variety of generous scholarships and internship opportunities for students. Overseas students enjoy national treatment, free medical care, and internationally recognized diplomas, with an enrollment rate as high as 93%.

③ Numerous local employment opportunities exist, with a high demand for specialized senior professionals. Additionally, with China's deepening reform and opening up, more Japanese companies are investing in China. Graduating students can rely on their fluent Japanese language skills and advanced professional knowledge to possess undeniable competitive strength in the domestic job market. They can choose to work in foreign enterprises, joint ventures, state-owned enterprises, or government agencies, achieving significant career development space.

④ While studying in Japan, international students are legally allowed to work part-time. Students can work a maximum of 28 hours per week (equivalent to 4 hours/day), with no limit during winter and summer breaks. Japan’s developed economy and service industry have a high demand for short-term workers and hourly workers, with a minimum wage of approximately 800 yen/hour.

⑤ After obtaining admission notification from a Japanese school, the school will process the reverse visa application for the student in Japan, resulting in an extremely high success rate for student visas.

⑥ Japan is a country that places great emphasis on etiquette and the rule of law, with legislation completeness comparable to that of the United States. The perfection of its legal system ensures good public security, with a much lower crime rate compared to North America. Over the past 30 years, Japan has continuously worked on environmental greening and governance, becoming one of the "world's cleanest and most environmentally friendly countries" and being selected multiple times as one of the "most suitable regions for human habitation."

⑦ Japan has convenient transportation. It takes only about 3 hours by plane from Beijing, with airfare consistently around RMB 2000. The domestic road network is well-developed, allowing international students to purchase Japanese-made cars at very low prices locally and travel to various cities via highways. The newly operational maglev Shinkansen not only makes travel faster, safer, and more comfortable but also serves as a practical example of the world's most advanced technology in this field. Tuition is approximately RMB 40,000/year, and living expenses (including accommodation) are about RMB 40,000/year.

⑧ Studying at a university in Japan costs about RMB 40,000/year in tuition, with main living and accommodation expenses around RMB 30,000/year. After graduating from a local junior college, students can find employment in Japan, obtain a work visa, and gain long-term residency if hired by a company. Alternatively, after graduation, students can pursue further studies or employment in North America or Europe, gaining greater upward mobility.

⑨ Following the Asian financial crisis, Japan's prices and exchange rates have been continuously declining for many years. The exchange rate between the yen and the renminbi remains relatively low, reducing the cost of studying abroad in Japan for current students.

⑩ To protect the rights of overseas students, Japan's Immigration Bureau has explicitly stipulated that schools are not allowed to collect tuition fees from overseas students until they have obtained a study visa for Japan. This ensures that students pay tuition only after safely and successfully obtaining their visas.
