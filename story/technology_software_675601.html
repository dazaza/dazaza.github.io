133 Zen Phrases for a Better State of Mind

by shunyuan on 2012-02-25 10:49:19

88. Do not alienate your closest friends over petty arguments, nor forget the great kindness of others due to small grievances.

89. Being open to criticism from others can help adjust one's own shortcomings.

90. Be thankful for what you have and grateful for what you do not.

91. When speaking, avoid being aggressive or hurtful; do not boast about your abilities or expose others' faults, and you may turn enemies into friends.

92. A person who constantly looks for flaws in others often has issues with themselves, as they lack the time to reflect on their own actions.

93. There will always be gossip; if you don't listen, it naturally disappears. If you do listen, it still exists. How you handle it is up to you.

94. If you truly love someone, you must tolerate some of their faults.

95. To overcome the fear of death, you must accept that everyone will die eventually.

96. Though you may dislike someone, if you can still recognize their strengths, such a cultivated person is rare in this world.

97. Honestly confront the conflicts and blemishes within yourself; do not deceive yourself.

98. Cause and effect owe us nothing, so please do not complain.

99. We indeed have certain merits, but we should also conceal some of them; this is called cultivation.

100. Most people only do three things in life: deceive themselves, deceive others, and be deceived by others.

101. Those who overly admire themselves will not appreciate the strengths of others.

103. When you cling to something, you can only possess that one thing. If you let go, you have the opportunity to choose something else. If your mind clings stubbornly to its own concepts, unwilling to let go, then your wisdom can only reach a certain level.

104. People fearing you is not a blessing, and people deceiving you is not a disgrace.

105. It is not someone else causing you distress, but rather you allowing their words and actions to trouble yourself.

106. Do not deliberately distort the good intentions of others; think positively about them.

107. In this world, most things do not go as we wish.

108. My wealth is not determined by how much I own, but by how little I need.

109. After eating, one must excrete; one must learn to let go with grace, otherwise, one might become "constipated."

110. Constantly thinking that others are paying attention to you, or hoping for their attention, leads to a more troubled life.

111. I can cook for you, but I cannot eat for you. Each person satisfies their own hunger, and each person resolves their own life and death issues.

112. Criticizing others is easy, but controlling oneself is difficult.

113. Just live your own life and do not concern yourself with the distortions and right/wrong judgments of others.

114. If you plan to get married, remember this important philosophy: you must endure and tolerate the other person's flaws. There is no absolutely happy marriage; happiness comes from infinite tolerance and mutual respect.

115. If you can safely spend a day, that is already a blessing. Many people today will not see tomorrow's sun, many will become disabled, many will lose their freedom, and many will experience family destruction.

116. Right and wrong, gain and loss, can only be judged after the final outcome.

117. You do not need to argue with cause and effect; cause and effect never misleads people. You also do not need to argue with fate; fate is the fairest judge.

118. You have your view of life, and I have mine. I do not interfere with you. If I can, I will influence you. If I cannot, then I accept my fate.

119. If you want to control eternity, you must control now.

120. Never let harsh words come out of your mouth, no matter how bad or evil the other person is. The more you scold them, the more your heart gets infected. Think of them as your good teacher.

121. When you start living tomorrow, if someone argues with you, just let them win. Winning and losing are just conceptual ideas. When you let the other side win, you do not lose anything. What does winning give them? What does losing take away from you?

123. Do not always feel like you are being forced. Instead, think that he treats me this way already quite well. This is the practice of meditation.

124. Others may violate cause and effect, harm us, hit us, slander us. But we should not therefore hate others. Why? We must maintain a pure nature and a calm mind.

125. When interacting with anyone, always ask yourself, "What can I offer that would benefit them?" If I cannot help others through my morality, knowledge, and practice, then I am in debt.

126. If a person does not feel suffering, it is not easy to sympathize with others. To learn the spirit of saving others from suffering, you must first endure suffering.

129. The world originally does not belong to you, so you do not need to abandon it. What you need to abandon is all attachments. All things are for my use, but they do not belong to me.

130. It is better to forgive others yourself than to wait for others to forgive you.

131. When you face things with a troubled heart, you will feel that everything is an obstacle, and the world will seem ugly and hateful.

133. Although we cannot change the surrounding world, we can change ourselves, using love and wisdom to face everything.

60. If you can discover your own faults as accurately as you find faults in others, then your life will be extraordinary.

61. Forgiving others creates space in your heart for maneuvering.

69. Those who praise us and marvel at us are not true teachers. Those who criticize us and guide us are good teachers; with them, we improve.

71. Wasting a day, getting up early and going to bed late, is like committing theft.

80. Do not often feel sorry for yourself; there are more people in the world who suffer more than we do.

81. Foolish people always want others to understand them. Wise people strive to understand themselves.

83. Only by facing reality can you transcend it.

85. Those who do not know how to love themselves are incapable of loving others.

86. Doing things is simply learning how to be a person.

1. People suffer because they pursue the wrong things.

2. Rather than blaming others for making you suffer, it is better to say that your own cultivation is insufficient.

3. If you do not create troubles for yourself, others can never give you troubles. Because in your own heart, you cannot let go.

4. Discipline yourself well, and do not bother about others.

5. Not forgiving others, not pardoning others, is tormenting yourself.

6. Do not say others are pitiful; you are even more pitiful. How is your own cultivation? How much do you understand about life?

15. Every wound is a form of maturity.

16. When you know confusion, it is not pitiful. When you do not know confusion, that is the most pitiful.

17. Proud people can be saved, but arrogant people cannot.

18. Do not always complain about others; you should always examine yourself. Complaining about others torments yourself.

19. You must accept those whose opinions differ from yours; this makes life easier. If you always try to change them, you will be very distressed. Learn to endure them. Learn to accept them.

49. A person full of jealousy, not straightforward in heart, and speaking improperly cannot be considered as having a correct appearance.

50. Tell more jokes and deal with matters in a humorous attitude; life will be a bit easier.

51. Living in others' applause is a sign of a person who cannot withstand tests.
