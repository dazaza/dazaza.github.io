Development of Polysilicon Standard Curve, MIIT is preparing for industrialization bidding

by jeans30197 on 2010-03-31 17:34:03

"Recently, it's impossible to achieve the target of 60 kilowatt-hours per kilogram," Zhang HuiGuo insisted. "During the investigation process, everyone vied to report super low electricity consumption indicators. Although experts proposed disapproval opinions, many first-line enterprises argued that there wasn't much to say about it," the person expressed helplessness. After the research group returned to Beijing, they initially set a target of 60 kilowatt-hours per kilogram for power consumption in the production of polysilicon. "From the reported data, this number is not high; it is still 10 kilowatt-hours per kilogram higher than the best level."

"However, the new power consumption indicator caused opposition from the National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC). The person revealed that 'this conflicts with the requirements of Document No. 38, which would be equivalent to denying the document just passed by the state.' According to the person, the indicators in Document No. 38 originated from a survey conducted by the NDRC and other ministries starting in February 2009."

Wu Chenggong, Vice Chairman of Henan Shangyu New Energy, which operates in the polysilicon business, also believed that the market should decide survival of the fittest. "For example, the original industry standards for trichlorosilane (a raw material for polysilicon production) no longer meet current market demands for products. If your product only meets the industry standard, you definitely won't survive. Moreover, it's hard to enforce industry standards, and there's always a risk of countermeasures below the policy."

Zhang Huiguo said that XinGuang Silicon Industry is implementing technical reforms to meet the new energy consumption targets. "China’s polysilicon industry has been developing domestically for more than 30 years without much progress, with total capacity never breaking through 100 tons. But since entering the 21st century, driven by photovoltaics, China’s polysilicon industry has achieved leapfrog development, with current capacity breaking through 20,000 tons." Cui Rongqiang believed that the market had nurtured the rapid development of domestic polysilicon.

The 80 kilowatt-hours per kilogram indicator received support from ministries represented by MIIT and the Ministry of Science and Technology, as well as related enterprises. The person involved in setting the standard said, "The new indicator is relatively feasible; it can be completely achieved by introducing complete sets of foreign equipment. Domestic equipment can also reach this level after reform."

Our reporter exclusively learned that the point of contention between the NDRC and MIIT, Ministry of Science and Technology, etc., was the polysilicon power consumption indicator. The NDRC requested a reduction in power consumption to 60 kilowatt-hours per kilogram, but MIIT and the Ministry of Science and Technology believed it should be set at 80 kilowatt-hours per kilogram. Due to difficulties in coordination, the standard was difficult to issue.

"When we surveyed Chongqing DaQuan, they said their reduction in power consumption was at 60 kilowatt-hours per kilogram. Although it might be exaggerated, it wouldn't be too far off. We estimated they could achieve 67-68 kilowatt-hours per kilogram because they used the best equipment imported from abroad. This raised questions," the person introduced. Subsequently, the survey group went to Yangzhou ShunDa, "They were the most exaggerated, with technical personnel reporting a reduction in power consumption to 50 kilowatt-hours per kilogram and comprehensive power consumption at 120 kilowatt-hours per kilogram."

"The power consumption problem was all due to exaggeration by enterprises and localities," the person said angrily.

Due to unresolved disputes over polysilicon standards, MIIT, Ministry of Science and Technology, and others who strongly promoted polysilicon development planned to launch industrialization bidding to approve new polysilicon projects under special circumstances.

The delayed issuance of the standard has already had an underestimated impact on the industry. On September 26, 2009, the NDRC, MIIT, and other ten ministries officially announced a notice [State Council Document (2009) No. 38] (hereinafter referred to as Document No. 38), pointing out that the polysilicon industry faced overcapacity issues.

According to our understanding, to promote the development of polysilicon, MIIT is implementing polysilicon industrialization bidding, using scientific research methods to start large-scale polysilicon projects with annual production exceeding 3,000 tons.

The inability of various ministries to agree on polysilicon threshold entry standards led to the delay in issuing these standards. Under this context, MIIT is planning to launch polysilicon industrialization bidding to promote the development of the polysilicon industry.

According to the requirements of Document No. 38, "solar-grade polysilicon reduction power consumption should be less than 60 kilowatt-hours per kilogram... by 2011, eliminate polysilicon capacities with comprehensive power consumption greater than 200 kilowatt-hours per kilogram."

Subsequently, major domestic banks were required to freeze loans for polysilicon projects, causing tight funding for ongoing polysilicon projects in the country and preventing new polysilicon projects from obtaining bank credit support. A person involved in formulating the standards revealed to our reporter, "Before the issuance of the polysilicon industry access standards, new polysilicon projects have almost stopped."

"The key debate is about power consumption," the person involved in formulating the polysilicon industry standards told reporters, with polysilicon reduction power consumption and comprehensive power consumption being the main controversial indicators.

In Document No. 38, polysilicon was pointed out as a high-energy-consuming and high-polluting product with overcapacity issues. However, Cui Rongqiang, Chairman of the Shanghai Solar Energy Society, told reporters that polysilicon does not have high energy consumption or pollution problems, nor should one worry about overcapacity.

"This tendering process was urged very urgently by MIIT," the drafting person said, requiring completion of reviews within one or two weeks and beginning external tenders.

According to Cui Rongqiang's investigation, the total electricity consumption for producing each kilowatt of photovoltaic power generation system in China is 2,620 kilowatt-hours, and this energy can be recovered in about 2 years, "Different regions have different light intensity levels, so recovery times may vary, but given the current situation, obtaining more than 10 times the energy benefits from photovoltaic batteries is not a problem."

Zhang KaiJun, General Manager of Ordos Polysilicon Co., Ltd., a newcomer to domestic polysilicon, told reporters, "Reducing energy consumption is an inevitable trend. Only by continuously reducing costs can one survive, and achieving 60 kilowatt-hours per kilogram is not a problem."

Under the influence of Document No. 38, recent approval and filing of new domestic polysilicon projects have stopped. According to our investigation, the industry is waiting for the issuance of polysilicon access standards to construct new polysilicon projects.

On March 29, 2010, Zhang HuiGuo, Chief Engineer of Sichuan XinGuang Silicon Industry, the owner of the first thousand-ton demonstration project in the country, told reporters that the reasonable level for reduction power consumption standards was 80 kilowatt-hours per kilogram. Jiang WenWu, General Manager of Jiangsu ZhongNeng, the largest domestic polysilicon manufacturer, said, "60 kilowatt-hours per kilogram is also possible, the key is to specify the standards, with different equipment corresponding to different standards."

"Industrialization tendering is something I heard for the first time," a person working in Emei Semiconductor Materials Factory, one of the earliest engaged in polysilicon production in the country, replied to our reporter.

"80 kilowatt-hours per kilogram is a relatively reasonable standard," Zhang HuiGuo also believed. XinGuang Silicon Industry uses domestically produced equipment, with its reduction power consumption level at 87-90 kilowatt-hours per kilogram. "We once achieved 62-3 kilowatt-hours per kilogram in one furnace, but this is not the average level and is not representative."

Scientific research funds are not the key. In fact, a polysilicon project with an annual production of 3,000 tons has a total investment of approximately 2.7 billion yuan. The Emei person said, "If the enterprise successfully bids, it will obtain permission for a polysilicon project, significantly enhancing the corporate image, while also receiving corresponding technical support, especially greatly aiding in obtaining bank financing, as this is a national project."

According to people involved in drafting the industrialization tender documents, the MIIT industrialization tender stipulated an annual production capacity of 3,000 tons or more per line, "basically a large-scale polysilicon project, with successful bidding enterprises sharing 100 million yuan in scientific research funds from MIIT."

"Once the polysilicon access standards are established, many distinctive polysilicon projects may be eliminated in their infancy," Cui Rongqiang said. In this round of polysilicon price cuts, many polysilicon companies closed down. "Those companies with poor technology and severe pollution will definitely be eliminated. Polysilicon can be screened through market competition."

"It can be achieved separately, but achieving a reduction in power consumption of 60 kilowatt-hours per kilogram under a comprehensive power consumption indicator of no more than 200 kilowatt-hours per kilogram is impossible," the person said. The best international level for reduction in power consumption is currently 70 kilowatt-hours per kilogram, therefore, "in the previous discussion draft, everyone set the reduction in power consumption at 80 kilowatt-hours per kilogram and comprehensive power consumption at 200 kilowatt-hours per kilogram."
