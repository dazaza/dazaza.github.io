There are tricks to quickly becoming proficient in buying and selling on Paitao online stores.

by anonymous on 2011-03-25 13:45:08

Starting a business on Paipai is indeed a very good entrepreneurial project because of its low starting point, simple operation, small start-up capital, and with the development of the network and the perfection of the platform, the number of online shoppers is gradually increasing, and the future development prospect is very bright. Precisely because of this, many people have chosen to go through this narrow bridge. Just this year alone, the number of sellers on Paipai has doubled compared to last year, which has无形中increased the pressure on each seller. Moreover, the goods on Paipai can be said to be dazzling, almost everything, and similar products in the same industry are everywhere. Due to the special environment of the Internet, compared with traditional industries, the price of products is very transparent, and one click will immediately reveal who is high and who is low. In such a big environment and fierce competition, many people come to Paipai with great enthusiasm, but eventually "come with high hopes and leave disappointed." Therefore, I would like to say to the sellers who have stayed: "You are doing a great job!" Let's work together, believe that our tomorrow will be better. The competition among many heroes has not yet been decided, right? Of course, if you want to make a difference on Paipai and carve out a career with your own abilities, it is not that easy. First, you must have good psychological quality and a certain business philosophy.

Many new sellers on Paipai come with full confidence to manage their small stores well, but after a month, the business is still cold and deserted. Where did the customers go? I think they all went to queue up at the diamond crown sellers, hehe~ Although some new sellers also made a lot of efforts, often reading the essence posts of their predecessors, learning from their experiences, being busy reading posts and replying, and many sellers worked hard to promote their shops and items. Frequently studying the essence posts of predecessors to avoid unnecessary detours is definitely worth affirming and encouraging, and promotion is indispensable. However, if these two aspects of effort still result in a cold store, you may need to seriously consider whether there are still things you haven't realized or done.

As the saying goes: "To repel external enemies, one must first stabilize internally." If your own shop isn't well managed and you rush into promoting it, no matter how much promotion you do, even if the store's popularity improves and the browsing volume increases, ultimately, there will only be more viewers but very few actual transactions. This might excite you for a while, but it seems to be futile. What is the reason? For example, something that happened to me before: I asked my friend on Paipai to take a look at my store and find out why my business was still cold after a month. After looking, she frankly told me, "Honestly, standing from a consumer's perspective, I didn't find anything attractive in your store. I doubt I would stay in your store for more than a minute." This is the truth. No matter how much promotion you do, if the customers entering your store feel this way, all your efforts will be wasted. So, before promoting, everyone must first manage their own store well.

1. Have your own characteristics

If you're from Shaoxing, you can sell pickled mustard greens; if you're from Fuzhou, you can sell the three treasures of Fuzhou; if you're from Xinjiang, you can sell jade; if you're from Northeast China, you can sell chestnuts, ginseng, etc. Of course, these are just examples. The main point is to make the products you operate distinctive, advantageous, able to speak for themselves, and become a striking landscape on Paipai. It's great if others don't have what you have. If you can't make your products distinctive, you can still focus on the points mentioned above, work hard on them, and I believe it will be very helpful to you.

2. Make the products fashionable and trendy:

Aim at the market hotspots, grab the products generally liked by consumers for procurement, and let the products speak for you, saving money and effort.

3. Make the pictures more realistic and clear:

Using the official website's advertising pictures may attract people and produce good effects, but when buyers receive the goods, the actual product may differ greatly from their expectations, leading to a significant psychological gap. I think neutral or poor reviews might easily visit your store. This is probably why many new sellers complain that although their products are so good, they still receive unsatisfactory feedback, while the items of diamond-level sellers aren't necessarily better. One reason might be related to this! Clearer pictures require sellers to be more patient and spend more time researching. My photos are also not presentable, and I share this with all sellers, hehe.

4. Higher quality, lower prices:

High-quality products are your best promotional tool and the most effective and fundamental guarantee to retain old customers. Imagine which buyer would remember buying a pair of shoes from your store that turned into crocodile skin after a week? And they certainly won't recommend to friends saying, "I bought these shoes from a certain store for ten dollars, very cheap, not bad, and they lasted a week." On one hand, people don't want others to know they're greedy for small benefits, even though your price may already be very low or even unprofitable, but they won't care about these. So, it's better to choose better products even if the price is higher, which is fine. Procurement is also very important! Lower prices: Not making money, losing money to gain reputation, I think it's not impossible depending on whether you treat it as a long-term career. If you only want to make a little money or see short-term gains, I don't recommend it. As Jack Ma said, "Making money is actually very easy, as long as you grow big and strong, places where you can make money and projects you can undertake now might be unknown or incomprehensible to you." Everything comes slowly. As long as your store's popularity is high enough and you have enough loyal customers, you won't worry about making money. At that time, just signing contracts with courier companies to get some discounts on courier fees; just negotiating lower prices with manufacturers by a few yuan compared to before, I think it will be enough. You can also apply for agency rights from big brands... Isn't that great? These are personal views, it all depends on how you view your Paipai store - whether you want to make a little money first or grow your business bigger, which is up to you.

5. More publicity, broader mindset:

Publicity is an indispensable and crucial part of online stores. Regarding methods of publicity, the posts of predecessors have explained them in great detail, so I won't elaborate here. However, I found that many new sellers seem a bit impatient, which is understandable, but not entirely commendable. Who doesn't hope that their hard-earned publicity will show results quickly? But I want to say that publicity should be treated as a long-term investment, whether in terms of manpower or financial resources. Its short-term benefits may not be obvious, but in the long run, it will definitely be very significant. Think about the most familiar and funny advertisement around us: Brain White Gold "This year, no gifts for the festival, only Brain White Gold." When I first saw the advertisement, I thought it was strange that such an advertisement could be aired on TV, hehe, but this is exactly the brilliance of Shi Yuzhu, the chairman of the Giant Group. Such publicity makes people remember it more easily. Due to the influence of such advertisements, when I go to large supermarkets to buy supplements for my parents, I tend to choose Brain White Gold over Brain Gold, because it sounds more familiar, and the advertisement airs every day, giving it a sense of class, hehe...

6. Be more serious, have a better attitude:

Here we talk about service. Everyone knows that service is important, but its implementation can vary from person to person. Since I used to work as a customer service specialist in a listed company, I deeply understand how important service is. Maybe your product isn't the best or the most cost-effective, but if your service is excellent, I believe customers will return. When I first joined this company, it was still an unknown small company, but it employed 400 to 500 customer service personnel, and even more staff to handle backend issues. Within less than a year, it successfully listed in Hong Kong. I think what attracted customers wasn't just the product itself, but more importantly, the subsequent service. Why is Haier famous? Because its after-sales service is famous.

On Paipai, many new sellers don't seem to pay enough attention to it or do enough. In our competition with diamond crown sellers, our greatest advantage and the area where we can fully play our strengths is service. We have more time and energy than diamond crown sellers. In today's era and social environment, I personally feel that service is our core competitiveness. In this era of rapid information network development, no product can be exclusively owned by one person. Stockpiling and cornering the market have become outdated, akin to suicide. Your smart opponents may discover and replicate your products and business models, but your thoughtful service and the deep bond established with buyers cannot be replicated. As Chen Anzhi, the greatest Chinese motivational master, said, "Selling products is not as good as selling yourself," which means that often what buyers purchase is not your product but the satisfactory service. Such emotional attacks are everywhere now. China Mobile sends blessing messages to its customers during festivals, and even government agencies send caring messages like "As the Spring Festival approaches, please take precautions against theft and fire, wishing you a happy Spring Festival." Even these large groups and state agencies know the importance of serving with heart, shouldn't we learn from them? Anyway, just follow the concept of "Customer First." Whether you want to or not, you must believe one thing: customers are your bread and butter. There is a very famous 20/80 rule in economics, which I believe everyone knows: 80% of your total income comes from the 20% of major customers and old customers in your customer base. How to maintain these 20% of old customers and let them continuously bring you new customers depends on everyone's efforts!
