Problems in health food industry need to be solved urgently

by zxyasdk60 on 2011-07-01 14:22:50

Health food has always been the focus of social attention, and integrity issues have always been the bottleneck hindering the rapid development of the health food industry. Since the issuance of the "Health Food Registration Management Measures" by the Ministry of Health in 1996, the state has approved more than ten thousand health food codes. However, before the introduction of the "Food Safety Law", whether health foods would still be retained in the new law became the biggest concern and unease for companies in the industry!

Why does the "sunshine industry" aimed at promoting and maintaining human health keep facing questions about its integrity? Why does the "people's livelihood industry" which should play a huge role in disease prevention and reducing medical burdens encounter the awkward issue of almost losing its "identity"? Although Article "Fifty-one" of the "Food Safety Law" clearly established the legal status of health foods, allowing health product enterprises to breathe a sigh of relief, the statement that "the state will strictly supervise foods claimed to have specific health functions, with specific management measures to be stipulated by the State Council" still made enterprises "nervous"!

The phrase "strict supervision" clearly shows the determination of the state to supervise and manage health foods more severely based on past practices. This fully reflects the government's attitude towards protecting consumer interests and valuing product quality safety. The yet-to-be-issued specific regulatory measures by the State Council are like the Sword of Damocles hanging over the heads of enterprises. Recently, many enterprises have been exploring and speculating about the possible contents of regulations related to the approval, production, and sales of health foods, and some have even made suggestions and recommendations to relevant departments through various channels.

The upcoming "Regulations on Health Foods" has become the most watched hotspot in the health industry recently.

In addition to paying attention to regulations, I believe that the health food industry must first resolve the long-term "integrity" crisis that has plagued the industry and face up to several existing issues:

1. Exaggerated and false advertising. This is the phenomenon that most troubles regulators and is most detested by consumers; if this problem is not corrected, the "integrity" of health products will never be established!

2. Adulteration in products, some even containing banned components. Almost every so often, there are incidents where products are penalized due to unauthorized addition of Western medicine ingredients or serious discrepancies between product compositions and registered filing content. Although these negative events are caused by individual enterprises, they occur repeatedly, damaging consumer confidence and seriously affecting the industry's reputation.

3. "Li Gui" dragging down "Li Kuai" ("Li Gui" refers to counterfeit health foods). While "Li Kuai" itself has unresolved issues, counterfeit health foods ("Li Gui") continue to create chaos. Many ordinary foods without health food approvals falsely advertise and sell themselves as health foods in the market. Due to regulatory gaps, this not only misleads consumers but also makes health foods bear undeserved blame. A considerable number of counterfeit products pose safety hazards, adding unnecessary salt to the already painful wounds of the health food industry, exacerbating industry disorder, urgently requiring effective regulation.

To change these phenomena and establish a good, standardized consumer market, making consumers truly healthier due to guaranteed quality and clear efficacy products, I think it is necessary to strengthen the supervision and scientific guidance of the health food industry.

Firstly, the situation of "multiple head management" needs to change. According to the characteristics of the product, health foods differ from ordinary foods and are not suitable for "segmented management". They should be managed uniformly by one administrative department, making it possible to have unified standards and requirements for product approval, production, and sales, possibly avoiding or reducing regulatory loopholes, preventing vacuums and repeated supervision, leaving no room for illegal products and unlawful behaviors.

At the same time, strengthen the management of production and distribution processes. Besides requiring production conditions to meet GMP standards, increase random inspections of products on the market. Corresponding to product codes, inspect the main raw materials, production process, quality inspection, and adverse reaction records of products on the market at any time to prevent "cutting corners" and "falsifying" behaviors. Of course, this requires increasing regulatory costs, but this work can be entirely entrusted to local relevant institutions or industry associations. Third-party inspections can not only save government regulatory costs but also expand the regulatory network, reducing the "passive" situations of post-event remedies.

Thirdly, increase the penalty for illegal activities. "Tenfold compensation" clearly cannot effectively stop illegal acts in the health food industry. Can we formulate measures such as "lifetime prohibition of production for severely illegal enterprises or punishing them to bankruptcy", making violators "hesitant to act", perhaps this is an effective measure to completely eliminate the above phenomena.

These issues and suggestions are problems within the industry itself and the level of government supervision. The increasing health demands of consumers and the lack of public health knowledge is the root cause of many problems in the health industry. Increasing science popularization education, advocating healthy lifestyles, and cultivating a market environment for scientific consumption and rational purchasing are essential market foundations for the long-term development of the health industry.

It is evident that since the "Food Hygiene Law" explicitly defined health foods in 1995, whether it's in the research, production, and market sales of raw materials and finished products, or international trade, a scale has been formed. With a market size exceeding 100 billion yuan annually, it shows that health foods have played an indispensable role in improving the physical health of residents, enhancing national health literacy, and reducing and preventing chronic diseases caused by dietary imbalance, nutrient deficiencies, and other factors.

Of course, the problems existing in health foods cannot be overlooked. As economic development levels continue to rise, along with increased income and changing consumption concepts among residents, the demand for health will become increasingly urgent. This requires the government to promote the orderly development of the health industry and formulate more scientific, reasonable, and effective laws and regulations, providing effective legal guarantees for consumers to purchase high-quality and qualified products. Enterprises should also strengthen self-discipline and standardization, gaining consumer trust through integrity and quality. This is the fundamental for the development of the health industry.
