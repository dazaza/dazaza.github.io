Introduction to the New Liang Shanbo and Zhu Yingtai:

In the late Eastern Jin Dynasty, more than 1600 years ago. It was an era where aristocrats were in opposition with the commoners, and romantic freedom could not coexist with traditional constraints.

Zhu Yingtai, a young lady from the Shangyu aristocratic family, is the ninth child and the only daughter of her parents. She is smart, beautiful, lively, spirited, pure, and tenacious. From a young age, she enjoyed reading poetry and books, excelling in music, chess, calligraphy, and painting. She refused to be bound by conventions, was as capable as any man, and managed to convince her parents through various means to disguise herself as a man and go to Hangzhou for studies. Not long after leaving, she encountered Liang Shanbo, a poor scholar from Yueyang, whom she had met before at the Matchmaker Temple.

Liang Shanbo comes from a commoner's background, his family having fallen on hard times. His father died in his youth while working on flood control, leaving him to be raised by his mother. He is straightforward in character, honest and sincere, devoted to inheriting his father's aspirations and serving the world. On the road to Ni Mountain Academy in Hangzhou, he met Zhu Yingtai, who was disguised as a man. The two shared similar ideals and became sworn brothers despite being of different surnames. For three years at the academy, they "sat at the same table during the day and slept in the same bed at night," supporting each other with deep and meaningful friendship.

Ni Mountain Academy is a prestigious place in southern China for aristocratic offspring to study and enter public service. The son of the Hangzhou governor, Ma Wencai, and Wang Langtian, a member of the Wang family from Taiyuan, also arrived here. Ma Wencai is suave and erudite, with exceptional equestrian and archery skills, making him the leader among his peers at the academy. Relying on his father's position as governor and his own advantages, he gathered a group of wealthy but spoiled students, often opposing Liang Shanbo and Zhu Yingtai. When Shanbo's academic ranking surpassed his, he attempted to kill Shanbo. Upon discovering that Yingtai was a woman and had fallen in love with Liang Shanbo, he resorted to all sorts of schemes, determined to make Yingtai his wife.
