Huang Guangyu Hong Kong Hearing

by dfdfffffffffffffffffffff007 on 2009-09-08 13:52:02

[News Introduction] Today, according to the interim mandatory order issued by the Hong Kong High Court in early August, the hearing of the case of freezing Huang's assets will be conducted in a non-public way.

It is understood that since last November, Huang Guangyu has been detained by the Beijing police on suspicion of economic crimes, and his wife Dujuan was also placed under residential surveillance afterwards. Relevant legal professionals believe that whether Huang Guangyu and Dujuan can attend mainly depends on the attitude of the mainland judicial department. However, Huang Guangyu's absence will not affect the judicial investigation and hearing of the other two corporate defendants. According to relevant regulations, Mr. and Mrs. Huang Guangyu can appoint agents to appear in court.

According to the summons document No. 1496 issued by the Hong Kong court on August 5th, the reason for Mr. and Mrs. Huang Guangyu being brought to court this time has no direct connection with the case currently under investigation by the mainland police. The reason why they were "targeted" by the relevant departments in Hong Kong this time was due to a stock repurchase incident in 2008. At that time, Huang Guangyu and his wife Dujuan planned to use the funds of Gome Electrical Appliances to repurchase the shares of Gome Electrical Appliances originally held by Huang Guangyu, enabling Huang Guangyu to use the proceeds from the sale of shares to repay a private loan of HK$2.4 billion.

Since the above-mentioned share repurchase had a negative impact on Gome Electrical Appliances' financial situation and did not conform to the best interests of Gome Electrical Appliances and its shareholders, it resulted in a loss of approximately HK$1.6 billion for Gome Electrical Appliances and its shareholders. Therefore, the assets frozen last month by Huang Guangyu were valued at HK$1.66 billion.

According to Hong Kong media reports, someone has offered HK$73 million to purchase the property at No. 56 Shallow Water Bay Road privately held by Dujuan, Huang Guangyu's wife, which may be concluded within a short period of time. It is claimed that the house was purchased in October 2005 for HK$55 million. If it is successfully sold in the end, it could make a profit of HK$18 million on paper, appreciating by 33% in four years. The property covers an area of 3,236 square feet (approximately 320 square meters) with 2 parking spaces. Calculated at HK$73 million, each square foot is approximately HK$23,000, which falls within the current market price level. According to the data from the Hong Kong Land Registry, the property is held under Dujuan's private name, and Hang Seng Bank handled the mortgage in February 2006.

However, because both Dujuan and Huang Guangyu are currently under investigation, it remains unknown whether the transfer of the aforementioned property is initiated by Dujuan's active sale or due to other reasons.

Further developments about this case will be revealed to you by the author at any time.
