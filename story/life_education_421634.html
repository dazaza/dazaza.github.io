How should children aged 7-8 learn English?

by thatseed123 on 2011-04-26 21:01:03

Student Age: 7-8 years old

1. Characteristics of Students

Children enter school for formal study, and with efforts in various aspects, they quickly adapt to this learning method and develop basic study habits. However, at this stage, they still possess the trait of being "active," as if they have endless energy. Therefore, whether at home or in school, smart parents and teachers know how to cleverly help students "release" their energy. Games, songs, and jumping around are all the best ways.

2. Expert Guidance

Memorizing Words: Remembering or not remembering is just one aspect; more importantly, it depends on what kind of method is used!

Perhaps your child doesn't like memorizing words at all, or perhaps your child has put in a lot of effort but still can't remember many words. Maybe your child is gradually losing interest in English learning because of the "terrible" words. Here, two points need to be clarified:

First: Rote memorization only works temporarily. No matter how smart the child is, mechanical memory methods won't allow these ever-changing "symbols" to stay in his mind for long.

Second: Memorizing words is actually not difficult. To remember accurately and firmly, one must find the pronunciation rules of letters (natural phonics rule). Therefore, whether parents or teachers, while paying attention to the child's dictation scores, more attention should be given to the way the child reads and memorizes words, systematically cultivating the child's ability to "defeat" words intelligently.

3. Expert Q&A

1) What should I do if my child doesn't like to memorize words?

There are two reasons why children don't like to memorize words:

First, they have tried before but failed or found it very difficult, so they are unwilling to try again.

Second, they think it's boring and simply don't bother.

A common solution: Teach the child scientific and simple methods, which means recognizing and memorizing words through letter pronunciation rules. This is also known as the natural phonics method that experts and teachers often mention. In fact, in the preparatory level and level one Cambridge Young Learners' English textbooks, there is already teaching content related to this, but it is not complete. Therefore, systematic study of natural phonics courses at different levels should be carried out at the appropriate time.

2) Why is it necessary to do recording homework?

Students aged 6-12 have very strong language imitation abilities. Recording can motivate students to study diligently, record their progress and growth process, and also help students develop good study habits. This is a quite effective learning method in the stage of children's English learning.

3) How many shields are required to pass the Cambridge Young Learners' English test? Is it mandatory to take the exam?

First of all, the Cambridge Young Learners' English testing system itself does not have a classification of passing or failing levels. It is just an English proficiency testing system that can truly reflect the student's English learning situation and intuitively show the development trend of listening, speaking, reading, and writing skills.

In addition, the exam is not mandatory. Parents can arrange according to the actual situation of the child, voluntarily sign up for the exam. But it must be emphasized that: English learning is a long-term, phased process, and timely testing of the student's learning situation is needed at different learning stages. While reviewing and consolidating the knowledge learned, preparation for the next stage of learning is also made. The Cambridge Young Learners' English examination system is one of the most scientifically authoritative English evaluation systems in the world, closely linked to the content of the Cambridge Young Learners' English textbook, with lively and diverse forms. It will intuitively reflect the child's English learning situation and give the child positive encouragement. Therefore, after the completion of each level of study, the corresponding examination evaluation should be participated in.

4) With so many types of courses, what are the benefits of attending a foreign teacher oral class?

English is an important communication tool for modern people. It has become an indispensable part of our lives, studies, and work. The premise of learning English well is to learn oral English well and strengthen listening and speaking abilities. Statistics show that in daily language practice activities, "listening" accounts for 45%, "speaking" accounts for 30%, "reading" accounts for 16%, and "writing" accounts for 9%. This shows the importance of listening and speaking in everyone's English learning process. The foreign teacher oral class at Juren School is aimed at solving this problem. All classes are taught by foreign teachers, focusing mainly on listening, speaking, learning, and practicing, aiming to improve the child's practical application ability in English.

5) If the child has no foundation at all and cannot understand what the foreign teacher says, what should be done?

Even if the child has no foundation, they can still learn oral English from foreign teachers. Our foreign teachers at Juren School are all carefully selected. They graduated from foreign universities and have years of teaching experience. On the podium, they are not only teachers but also actors. They have rich body language, and when the child encounters unfamiliar words, their vivid and lifelike explanations make it easy for the child to understand.

6) If the child hasn't attended Level 1 or 2 of Juren School's foreign teacher oral class, can they directly attend Level 3 or 4?

Yes. The "Foreign Dad Foreign Mom" program at Juren School is divided into Levels 1-12, and the transition between each level is very smooth. Moreover, we adopt a test-based admission method, providing the child with a suitable level to study, so the child will face no obstacles in studying the foreign teacher oral course.

[Expert Tip]

Parents can cooperate by letting children listen to more English tapes or related content. Children at this age may feel shy during English learning, but a good sense of language will make the child very confident. Parents can choose to play some English during the time from getting up in the morning to going to school, or listen before sleeping at night, allowing the child to listen naturally without emphasizing that they must sit straight or follow the reading as in school. Instead, naturally cultivate the child's sense of language and appropriately encourage them with words, making them full of confidence in English learning.
