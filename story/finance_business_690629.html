How to quit drugs

by sudag6o5 on 2012-03-05 11:15:53

How to quit drugs? Detoxing at home requires the patient's own willpower, but one can also go to a compulsory detox center; being there for a year should be enough. It depends on your patience. I hope you can enter a detox center soon and escape from the devil's world... become yourself again. Once you've made up your mind, you must persevere. Detox center, detox center, nothing else works!!

1) Do not attempt to detoxify on your own. Some drug users, for personal reputation and to avoid others knowing, try to detoxify themselves or with the help of their families. This is also known as the dry method (or cold turkey method). Those who are not severely addicted and have strong willpower can try this method. Severe addicts or those who are physically weak should preferably seek treatment in specialized detox institutions under the care of doctors; otherwise, they may not only suffer greatly but also risk accidents.

2) Choose a detox institution recognized by relevant national departments. Detoxification is a complex treatment process. One should not believe in any so-called miracle drugs that promise little pain and instant cure. Don't easily trust folk remedies or secret formulas, and definitely don't trust itinerant medicine vendors selling so-called detox formulas or drugs. Otherwise, it might not only be ineffective but could also lead to dangerous situations.

3) Be fully mentally prepared and have strong willpower. In general, detoxifiers can relieve physiological dependence during withdrawal treatment, but the psychological craving is hard to endure. Currently, China lacks sufficient rehabilitation and reintegration stages in its detox treatment processes, leading to very high relapse rates once detoxifiers return to society. Therefore, detoxifiers need strong willpower.

4) Obey hospital management and cooperate with medical treatment. Most detox institutions implement closed or semi-closed management and have strict treatment plans. If detoxifiers genuinely want to break free from their addiction, they should actively cooperate with doctors. Secretly using drugs during treatment not only fails to achieve the expected results but also poses significant risks if the original dosage is maintained during withdrawal, which could be life-threatening.

5) Auxiliary treatment, improving symptoms. During the withdrawal process, if there is unbearable pain, non-narcotic analgesics can be appropriately selected. For severe insomnia, *** can be used. For anxiety, anti-anxiety medications can be chosen, and for constipation, laxatives can be used. This can improve withdrawal symptoms, reduce suffering, and enhance detoxification effects.

6) Reasonable diet, strengthening nutrition. Long-term drug users often have gastrointestinal dysfunction, showing symptoms of indigestion and malnutrition. Their physical condition is very weak, and their disease resistance is low. Therefore, during detox treatment, nutrition should be strengthened to restore physical health as soon as possible.
