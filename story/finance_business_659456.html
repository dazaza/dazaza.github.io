In the face of flowers and applause, the three brothers, He Ruishi, remained very calm: "We just did what we should do. If there is such a thing in the future, we will not hesitate to do it."

On the morning of June 9, in front of the gate of Xia County High School in Henan Province, the sound of suona was heard, fireworks were set off, and the atmosphere was enthusiastic and extraordinary. Dozens of people held banners and delivered brocades and letters of thanks to the school leaders, including Principal Han Haodian.

"In the face of deep water over ten meters, they risked their lives to save others selflessly, which is admirable. This is the pride of our post-90s generation."

"He Sweden is a class officer who often teaches by words and deeds at school. He has a strong sense of responsibility, cares about his classmates, and is willing to help others. His grades have improved rapidly. Originally an average student, he now ranks 11th in the class. In life, he is also very frugal, with a strong sense of justice, and can set an example everywhere in the class," said Mr. Cheng Weifeng, the head teacher.

"The saviors, I will never forget your great kindness." The grandmother of one of the rescued children, Miao Miao (a pseudonym), shed tears as she held He Ruishi's hand. "We have only one grandson, if he were gone, how would our whole family survive!"

He Ruishi said that the day of the rescue was when he returned home for two days to prepare for the college entrance exam. He wanted to relieve some inner pressure before the exam, so he went fishing at the pit near his house with his younger brother. The three brothers, who grew up by the river, were all relatively familiar with swimming.

After hearing the cries for help, He Ruishi and He Shuoshi quickly ran to the edge of the pit to save the children. He Sweden said that at the time, they could see five children struggling on the water surface, one of whom was floating on the water and could not move. Due to the deep water and steep slope, He Ruishi drank several mouthfuls of water and had difficulty pushing the five children to the shore. Then the three combined their efforts to drag the children onto the shore. At this point, the three were already exhausted. Seeing that one boy's lips were blue and he was unconscious with rolling eyes, they used the drowning rescue knowledge they learned at school to save the child's life.

Talking about the heroic act of the three He brothers, Principal Han Haodian of Xia County High School said excitedly: "In many people's impressions, the post-80s and post-90s generations are more self-centered, but this incident is enough to prove that they are the future and hope of the country."

Miao Miao's father, Miao Zhanjun, spoke hoarsely: "My child was truly pulled back from the 'gate of hell' by them." It was understood that among the five children who were rescued, Miao Miao was the most dangerous one. His lips were blue, and he was unconscious and rolling his eyes. The three brothers used the drowning rescue knowledge they learned at school to provide timely assistance, thus saving Miao Ning's life.

Three hours later, the Shangqiu Water Duty Rescue Team retrieved the bodies of two drowned girls.

At this point, the children had been drowning for 20 minutes.

Half an hour later, the Party Secretary of Luozhuang Town, He Yongfeng, the town mayor Liu Shuhua, police officers, and medical personnel arrived at the scene. Several nearby villagers also bravely jumped into the water to participate in the search and rescue. Due to the depth of the water, it took a long time for everyone to find nothing.

Flags of gratitude were presented to thank the saviors.

The age of He Shuoshi is relatively young, being 14 years old, studying at Luozhuang Town No.1 Middle School, and is an eighth-grade student. According to Mr. Shi Chaowei, a teacher at Luozhuang Town No.1 Middle School, He Shuoshi's performance at school is also good, with excellent academic results. There is also a sister in the family. He is very willing to help others and leads a simple life.

When the families of the children saw the saviors who saved their children, they were moved to tears, tightly holding their hands and bowing their heads in gratitude. Seeing this scene, the surrounding citizens and teachers and students immediately teared up.

"At that time, I was only thinking of saving people, and didn't think of anything else," said He Ruishi when the reporter asked him if he was afraid while saving people. "After rescuing them, I started to feel a bit scared. The water was so deep, and if something unexpected happened, wouldn't these years of study be wasted?"

At around 5 p.m. on June 4, seven teenagers from Luozhuang Town in Xia County were playing by a pit formed by soil extraction at an abandoned brick factory and accidentally fell into the water-filled pit. Hearing the cries for help, He Ruishi, He Sweden, and He Shuoshi rushed over without hesitation and successfully saved five teenagers. Later, it was discovered that these three selfless heroes were students from different classes at Xia County High School and Xia County Luozhuang Town No.1 Middle School. He Ruishi and He Sweden are biological brothers, and He Shuoshi is their cousin. Their story of selflessness spread quickly in the local area.

"There are still two more!" cried one of the rescued children. He Sweden immediately called the emergency number, and then the three brothers jumped back into the water to continue searching.

Mr. He Ruishi's high school head teacher, Mr. Zhu Lianying, told reporters that He Ruishi's family was not wealthy, and he enjoyed national scholarships at school. He is an optimistic and confident person, someone who finds joy in helping others. Classmates with difficulties like to seek out He Ruishi, and He Ruishi never refuses, always doing his best to help, even during the final push for the college entrance exam.

On June 10, China News Agency reported that recently in Xia County, Shangqiu, Henan Province, seven teenagers accidentally slipped into deep water while playing by the water. Three local middle school students disregarded their own safety and resolutely jumped into the water to rescue them, saving the lives of five children. In recent days, parents of the rescued children and many strangers from society have visited the homes and schools of the three middle school students to pay tribute to the heroes in various forms, showing respect for their brave actions.

He Ruishi, He Sweden, and He Shuoshi were born in Hezhai Village, Luozhuang Town, Xia County. The three brothers are diligent, filial, polite, and caring. They have lived independently since childhood and often help their parents with household chores. Especially precious is that they share a unique trait: they have always been warm-hearted and enjoy helping others, earning praise from the elders in their hometown.

After the rescue, in order not to delay the college entrance exam, they returned to school to prepare the next afternoon. During this period, they did not tell their classmates or teachers, until yesterday morning when their head teacher, Mr. Zhu Lianying, heard about He Ruishi's courageous rescue.
