Speaking of the origin of the five rings, there was once an interesting story. In 1936, during the 11th Berlin Olympic Games, the torch relay event was held for the first time. The torch relay route started in Olympia, exited through northern Greece, followed the Danube River through Austria, and finally entered Germany. To highlight this symbolic activity, Karl Diem, the chairman of the Organizing Committee and his colleagues almost completely arranged the ancient Greek sites along the way according to the scenes of the ancient Olympics. When the torch reached the ancient stadium of Mount Parnassus in Delphi, a special ceremony was held. At this moment, Diem suddenly came up with an idea to design and carve the five-ring symbol of modern Olympic Games on a rectangular stone about 3 feet high on all four sides, and placed it at one end of the starting line of the ancient stadium. After the ceremony, the torch continued northward while the stone used as a prop was left in the ancient stadium.
