Human's first long voyage across the ocean with a non-fuel mechanical vessel: A Swiss solar boat will cross the Atlantic Ocean

by fight4what on 2006-11-26 13:13:36

A pure solar energy ship developed by Swiss scientists will set off from Spain this Sunday and cross the Atlantic to New York, USA. This will be the first time for mankind to drive a mechanized ship far across the ocean without depending on any fuel. The organizers have prepared to apply for the Guinness Record.

This solar energy ship is 14 meters in length and 6 meters in width, with a maximum of 6 crew members. It runs its motor through batteries. The top of the ship is covered with solar panels. After one full charge, it can sail continuously for 20 hours at a speed of 6 knots (9-12 kilometers per hour), which is the same as that of ordinary yachts.

Because of weather conditions, the ship failed to start on Tuesday as originally planned. If the weather is suitable, it will set off this Sunday. The total voyage is 12,900 kilometers. It is expected to arrive in New York next May. At that time, it will cruise from Florida to Boston along the east coast of the United States to promote solar power. (Source: Xinhua Telecom Broadband Network)
