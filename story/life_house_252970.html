How to select decoration and renovation materials for the bathroom

by longersing on 2009-10-28 14:39:14

How to choose decoration and renovation materials for the bathroom? Crafting bathroom cabinets with solid wood better complements a Chinese-style home decor. Versatile thin panels, coated mirrors, artificial stone, and resin glass are all excellent materials. Let's analyze their specific uses below.

If you want your bathroom space to be unconventional and eye-catching, you can also consider using the following materials to make bathroom cabinets, vanity countertops, etc. Using natural marble as the countertop for the bathroom cabinet should avoid getting colored cosmetics like lipstick on the surface, as these oily pigments can penetrate into the stone.

Crafting bathroom cabinets with solid wood better matches a traditional Chinese home style, but it’s best to design the space with a dry/wet separation layout.

Versatile thin panels — Currently, an advanced versatile thin panel material is popular internationally. This material is both waterproof and has pure natural wood grain textures, making it suitable for decorating bathroom walls and vanity countertops, creating a realistic wood effect that's hard to distinguish from real wood.

Coated mirrors — Unique coated mirror surfaces from high-tech optical and electronic fields have a light absorption rate of 50%, do not reflect glare, offer good visual effects, and are highly decorative. Even spotlight reflections become non-blinding after being reflected by a coated mirror, often used for wall styling designs in bathrooms.

Artificial stone — High-quality artificial stone can be cut into various shapes and sizes at will, allowing for small piece combinations or large-scale seamless wall splicing. Large floral and line patterns carved into long strips of artificial stone can create unique decorative effects.

Resin glass — Resin glass is lightweight, resistant to breaking, and transparent like crystal, making it the most fashionable and ideal material for creating partitions and sliding doors. Using resin glass in bathrooms to flexibly separate dry and wet areas not only looks delicate and beautiful but also maximizes the use of space.
