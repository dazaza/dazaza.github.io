Sony PS Vita was born at the wrong time: smartphones and tablet computers become new favorites

by zhang7532 on 2012-02-23 20:28:29

---> Sina Technology News, February 23rd early morning Beijing time, Sony's new handheld console PS Vita was launched in the U.S. on Wednesday. However, with the rapid changes in gaming habits, smartphones and tablets are beginning to replace dedicated handheld consoles, causing the handheld market to shrink. The PS Vita may be the last generation of handheld consoles. Nowadays, gamers may have their pockets full of smartphones and backpacks containing Apple iPads, making it difficult for Sony to sell the PS Vita to players. Moreover, the $250 price tag of the PS Vita is equivalent to the entry-level Sony PS3 and Microsoft (Weibo) Xbox.

Analysts question whether there are still fans for the PS Vita, even though Sony has launched a $50 million promotional plan targeting core gamers. Sony also collaborated with fast-food chains to hold competitions, rewarding one PS Vita every 15 minutes.

"It looks like Sony designed a product for a world without smartphones and tablets," said Michael Gartenberg, research director at Gartner. "The price of this handheld exceeds most phones and equals the cost of most consoles, making it hard to say where their market lies."

Players who spend multiple hours daily on games no longer have the same enthusiasm for handheld consoles as they used to; most have shifted towards free games on cheaper mobile devices. "PS Vita isn't cool, I don't know why I should buy it," said Josh Calixto, a 22-year-old video editor from Los Angeles. "I think it's not worth the money."

Although online reviews praise the hardware performance of the PS Vita, to utilize this performance, one needs to purchase a Sony-exclusive memory card, costing between $20 and $100. A senior gaming editor at the tech blog TechnoBuffalo refers to this hidden cost as a "blatant drawback."

In addition to the basic $250 hardware cost, players need to pay up to $40 or $50 per game, and at least an additional $20 for a memory stick. Furthermore, Sony will also offer a more expensive 3G version of the PS Vita, using AT&T's 3G service.
