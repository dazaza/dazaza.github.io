"Beauty and Sorrow of the Ten Extraordinary Women of the Republic of China" reveals the secret past of these peerless beauties.

by jqrxass on 2009-09-18 17:02:39

The Republic of China (ROC), a period in Chinese history with special significance.

During these chaotic years, there was a group of legendary women: some were born into wealthy and powerful families, incredibly prestigious; others possessed unparalleled beauty and charm that no one could match; some had immense talent and were fiercely independent; while others led dazzling lives with stories full of twists and turns.

In this book, we meticulously document the secret pasts of ten extraordinary women from the ROC era, recalling the joys, sorrows, ups, and downs of these remarkable beauties from 100 years ago. They say "life is like a play, and the play is like life." So, let's feel the splendor both on and off the stage through this historical warmth.

The ROC, a period in Chinese history with special significance. During these turbulent times, there was a group of legendary women: some were born into prosperous noble families, incredibly prestigious; others had unparalleled beauty and charm that no one could match; some had immense talent and were fiercely independent; while others led dazzling lives with stories full of twists and turns.

These extraordinary women pursued freedom, loved boldly, and hated openly. With their beauty and wisdom, they wrote an immortal legend for their generation.

The World-Renowned Performer: Mèng Xiǎodōng

Before Chen Kaige's movie *Mei Lanfang* came out, few people knew about her. Her name only occasionally appeared in biographies about Mei Lanfang or Du Yuesheng. But now, through this movie by Chen Kaige, people across China and even around the world have begun to notice this name shining with a strange brilliance.

Her unmatched singing voice, her touching love affairs with Mei Lanfang and Du Yuesheng, her genuine nature, and her stunning beauty destined her to be noticed by the world. She was the "Winter Emperor" of the opera world, a famous beauty, and she is the world-renowned performer — Mèng Xiǎodōng!

The Social Queen of Old Shanghai: Táng Yīng

Sixty years ago, the Paramount Dance Hall in Shanghai was known as the "First Music House of the Far East." A graceful woman often danced here, enjoyed leisure time, and spent her youth; she was Táng Yīng. At that time, she was the most prominent figure in social circles and was mentioned alongside Lù Xiǎomán as the "Southern Táng, Northern Lù." However, now Lù Xiǎomán is well-known due to poet Xú Zhìmó, while Táng Yīng's image has gradually faded behind the vicissitudes of life. One significant reason for this result is that although Táng Yīng was beautiful, she did not have a marriage history that caused too much of a stir.

Lonely Fireworks: Lù Xiǎomán

If Táng Yīng in the "Southern Táng, Northern Lù" pair is the red mole on men's hearts, then Lù Xiǎomán is the moonlight before the bed. She is not fireworks, yet she is lonelier than fireworks; she is not a rose, yet she is more beautiful and alluring than roses; she is a vast blue sea, clear and transparent yet deep and unfathomable. She can make waves in any era, and she can ignite all the hidden passion and desire in men.

Lù Xiǎomán became known because of Xú Zhìmó, and Xú Zhìmó also burned his entire brilliant light on Lù Xiǎomán's fragile youth, but at the same time overshadowed Lù Xiǎomán's extraordinary talent.

A Remarkable Woman in Literature: Líng Shūhuá

Nowadays, not many people know the name Líng Shūhuá, but she was one of the three renowned female writers of the 1920s, alongside Bīngxīn and Lín Huīyīn. Líng Shūhuá was well-versed, excelling in both writing and painting. In terms of writing, painting, and dealing with people, Líng Shūhuá was known for being peaceful, gentle, and elegant. She viewed the world with the unique kindness and warmth of a woman and treated those around her with the same mindset.

Líng Shūhuá's talent and artistic practice added important chapters to the history of modern Chinese literature. By getting closer to this artist who was once overlooked by literary history, we can experience a thought-provoking historical flavor.

She is the April Sky: Lín Huīyīn

Lín Huīyīn once wrote a poem for Xú Zhìmó, saying he was the April sky. In fact, in the hearts of the men of her time, she was their April sky. Her beauty, her talent, and her endearing little temperaments all made people's hearts flutter. Lín Huīyīn was a woman with remarkable talent but also ordinary emotions, and three outstanding and excellent men fulfilled her beauty.

Now, everything related to Lín Huīyīn has become an endless topic of conversation. People admire her beauty, intelligence, and talent and lament that she possessed the purest love in the world. But can one's life really be lived so perfectly?
