The biggest problem with Apple's iOS system is software functionality, not appearance design.

by geekzhang on 2013-05-05 10:38:11

May 5th, Beijing Time - According to foreign media reports, there are rumors that Apple's iOS appearance design will abandon realism and instead follow a minimalist route. However, this is not crucial. The biggest issue currently facing Apple's iOS is not its appearance design but rather the core functions of its operating system and Apple's localized applications. One manifestation of this is that Apple's built-in iPhone applications do not match up to third-party developed applications. Below is a summary of the article:The biggest rumor about Apple at present is that the company plans to redesign the appearance of its iOS operating system. We all know that the iOS operating system drives devices such as the iPhone, iPad, and iPod Touch.If Apple merely superficially updates the appearance of its iOS system, then this system risks being overtaken by Google's Android system.The new appearance of iOS will look simpler, possibly abandoning the design style that has achieved iPhone's brilliance and is familiar to us. Instead of mimicking real-life objects with icons and images, it will adopt a more simple and compact design.Jony Ive, Apple's head of hardware design, will be the leader in making these changes. Jony Ive helped revive Apple by creating stunning hardware like the iMac, iPod, and iPhone.In last year's personnel changes, Apple CEO Tim Cook assigned this person to oversee the changes in the iOS system's appearance, replacing Scott Forstall, who had been responsible for the iOS system for a long time.Jony Ive is a proponent of minimalism, which can be seen in every device he has developed for Apple. Reports indicate that Jony Ive's minimalism will now be applied to Apple's software.A fresh new hairstyle, a few pairs of new shoes, neatly trimmed nails, and a bath might make iOS appear sexier and more popular. However, what Apple truly needs to address right now is the way its operating system operates.Since its release, the Apple iOS operating system has undergone many updates. In most cases, these updates have led Android. However, the gap between Apple's iOS system and Google's Android system is gradually narrowing.Although it looks good for Apple's iOS appearance design to abandon realism and embrace minimalism, this is not critical. The real issues with Apple's iOS devices are unrelated to their appearance. The greatest flaws of Apple's iOS devices focus on the core functions of its operating system and Apple's localized applications.Making iOS look different isn't just about applying a new coat of paint. To make this system better, Apple needs to invest more effort. It's unclear whether Jony Ive will address these issues.Despite the overall good performance of iPhone software, there are some flaws. All built-in applications on Apple's iPhone are inferior to those developed by third parties, such as:1) Google Maps performs better than Apple Maps. Google Maps offers a better search experience, traffic guidance, and more accurate information.2) Evernote performs better than Apple's Notes. Evernote can synchronize on the web, save pictures and audio, and allow third-party integrations.3) Camera+ performs better than Apple's built-in camera. Camera+ offers more photography modes and superior picture editing experiences.4) Fantastical performs better than Calendar. Fantastical has a better layout and makes it easier to add events.5) Weather Channel performs better than Weather. Weather Channel updates faster.These examples aren't exhaustive; users can discover more. The reason third-party applications perform better than Apple's applications has nothing to do with the appearance of iOS devices; the problem lies in their functionality.At the same time, the flaws in Apple's operating system include:1) When temperatures are extremely high, the built-in Weather application in the iOS system fails. This application should be able to update temperatures in real-time and accurately reflect actual temperatures.2) When new notifications appear on the screen, they cover other content. Josh Topolsky of The Verge criticized how annoying these notifications are. For example, when checking emails, dozens of notifications appear at the top of the phone. When these notifications appear at the top, users cannot operate the top part of the phone.3) The settings button menu is too complex. If you want to turn Bluetooth or Wi-Fi on or off, you need to go through several steps in the settings. Adjusting the notification center also requires a lot of work.4) The notification center is good but needs improvement. If you want to cancel a notification from an app, you have to click twice. Furthermore, the notification center lacks a one-touch cancellation function, which Android does offer.The search function in Apple's iOS system is also a significant issue. Searching for emails on an iPhone is a terrifying experience. You cannot find your information, which lags far behind the Google Gmail app.The search feature in Apple Maps also needs substantial improvement. There are also content issues with Apple Maps.Search flaws are not something that someone with only design knowledge can fix. They require algorithms and data, years of effort, and possibly millions or even hundreds of millions of dollars invested to identify and resolve the problems.Siri is another function that Apple's operating system needs to improve; it requires a complete rethink. Google Now performs better than Apple's Siri; it is a true personal voice assistant. With Siri, you must ask for directions home. However, with Google Now, Google can make its best effort to provide you with pre-prepared routes home without asking. Even without asking, Google Now can provide sports scores and weather updates to users.The good news for Apple is that the company seems to already be aware of the issues with its iOS devices. Mark Gurman stated on Twitter: There are still many unresolved problems with Apple's iOS devices. I think iOS devices need functional updates rather than simple cosmetic improvements.Reports suggest that after Jony Ive takes over iOS, the iOS update may be delayed. Former President of Microsoft's Windows Division, Steven Sinofsky, said: If the iOS update release is postponed, it means Apple is currently improving the functionality of the iOS system rather than simply improving the design appearance.For Apple, increasing the megapixels of the camera is relatively easy, but changing core functions is not so straightforward.
