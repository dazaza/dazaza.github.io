New mothers eating too much monosodium glutamate and drinking more water is not good for babies.

by cshelive9520 on 2012-03-04 21:44:11

1. Avoid eating too much monosodium glutamate (MSG)

To prevent zinc deficiency in infants, new mothers should avoid consuming excessive MSG. Generally speaking, MSG is harmless for adults, but for infants, especially those within 12 weeks old, it can be detrimental if the breastfeeding mother consumes high-protein diets along with excessive MSG. This is because the sodium glutamate in MSG can enter the infant's body through breast milk.

Excessive sodium glutamate can severely impact the development of infants, particularly those within 12 weeks old. It can specifically bind with zinc in the infant's blood, forming glutamate that cannot be absorbed by the body, while zinc is excreted through urine. This leads to zinc deficiency in the infant, which may result in poor taste sensation, loss of appetite, reduced intelligence, and delayed physical development.

2. Avoid hurriedly taking ginseng

Some new mothers rush to take ginseng after childbirth, hoping to replenish their bodies. However, doing so can actually be more harmful than beneficial. Ginseng contains various active ingredients that can have a widespread stimulating effect on the body. The stimulation of the central nervous system can lead to insomnia, irritability, and restlessness. New mothers who have just given birth experience significant physical and mental exhaustion and need plenty of rest. Taking ginseng at this time can make it difficult to sleep due to overstimulation, affecting recovery.

Ginseng is a tonic that promotes blood circulation and accelerates blood flow. This is not favorable for new mothers who have just given birth. During delivery, many blood vessels in the internal and external reproductive organs are damaged. Consuming ginseng could hinder the natural healing of these damaged vessels, potentially causing continuous bleeding or even severe hemorrhage.

Therefore, new mothers should avoid taking ginseng within the first week after giving birth. After seven days postpartum, when the wounds have healed, taking a small amount of ginseng can help restore physical strength. However, excessive consumption should still be avoided. Ginseng is warming in nature and can cause heat-related issues for the mother or the baby. The best way for new mothers to replenish nutrients is by eating a variety of foods.

3. Avoid eating too many eggs

Medical research shows that within a few hours after delivery, new mothers should avoid eating eggs. During childbirth, new mothers expend a lot of energy, sweat profusely, and have insufficient bodily fluids, leading to decreased digestive capacity. If eggs are consumed immediately after childbirth, they may be hard to digest and increase the burden on the gastrointestinal tract. Within a few hours after delivery, semi-liquid or liquid diets are more appropriate. Throughout the postpartum period, according to national nutritional guidelines for pregnant and new mothers, about 100 grams of protein is needed daily. Therefore, eating 3-4 eggs per day is sufficient. Overeating eggs can also increase the burden on the gastrointestinal tract and may even lead to stomach problems.

4. Avoid drinking large amounts of plain water

Edema is common in the later stages of pregnancy for most new mothers. The postpartum confinement period is a golden time for the body to recover, during which all accumulated water should be expelled as much as possible. Drinking too much water during this time may hinder recovery.

If the mother has undergone a cesarean section and needs to take some medication, she should still drink an appropriate amount of water, but it should be consumed in smaller portions rather than large quantities at once.
