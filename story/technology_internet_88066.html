Operating Procedures for Importing Wine

by wqwq123 on 2008-08-29 17:09:58

I. Qualifications that wine import enterprises should have

In addition to the business and tax procedures required for general operating enterprises, Chinese enterprises that wish to engage in the wine import business must also include wine operation qualifications within their registered business scope. They must possess an import wine operation license and a health permit.

They also need to handle the following procedures with relevant departments: obtain the "Registration Form for Record-filing of Foreign Trade Operators" from the local commerce department. After registration, they will have the qualification for foreign trade. Foreign trade operators should take the stamped "Registration Form" to the local customs, inspection and quarantine, foreign exchange, taxation and other departments within 30 days to handle the necessary procedures for conducting foreign trade business.

II. Selection and determination of imported wine manufacturers and products

Choosing a high-quality, beautifully packaged, appropriately priced, and marketable imported wine is crucial for the market sales and profitability of an imported wine enterprise. How to find and select a good wine? First, determine the type of product you need to represent, the price range of the wine, and the annual purchase volume based on your own strengths, operating area, capital, and promotion capabilities. The specific search for manufacturers can be carried out through the following processes and methods:

1. Participating in wine exhibitions

Currently, there are many domestic and international professional wine exhibitions and product promotion meetings. Agents can negotiate face-to-face with many manufacturers through these conferences according to their needs, comparing quality, price, packaging, supply agency conditions, etc.

2. Online browsing search

You can search and browse foreign wine professional websites online, contact wine manufacturers via email, phone, and fax. After obtaining certain communication, choose suitable products and request the manufacturer to send sample wines for appearance inspection and wine quality tasting. If satisfied, proceed with price and supply condition negotiations, sign contracts, and handle related procedures such as registering a Chinese trademark.

3. Obtain information about foreign winemakers who wish to develop in China through professional consulting companies or foreign business institutions in China.

III. Import taxes on bottled wine

The current tax rate for importing bottled wine (taxes paid in Renminbi):

1. Tariff: 14% (Tariff: CIF × 14%);

2. Value-added tax: 17% (Value-added tax: (CIF + tariff amount) × 17%);

3. Consumption tax: 10% (Consumption tax: [(CIF + tariff amount) / (1-10%)] × 10%).

IV. Application for "Chinese Registered Trademark"

Import wine enterprises should obtain forms from the local commodity inspection and quarantine bureau and prepare the following application documents:

1. Business license of the enterprise;

2. Quality inspection and quarantine report of wine; the enterprise needs to send corresponding wine samples to the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine for inspection and issuance of inspection reports.

3. Original print and translation of the manufacturer's "Production License" (this certificate should be issued by relevant institutions or organizations in the country of origin);

4. Original print and translation of the manufacturer's "Health Permit" (this certificate should be issued by relevant institutions or organizations in the country of origin);

5. Original print and translation of the manufacturer's "Wine Production Process Flow" (only a simple explanation of the wine production process is required, with the company seal or responsible person's signature);

6. Chinese trademark design sample. Retain the original foreign text displayed on the front label, and ensure that there is corresponding translated text on the Chinese label. The Chinese product name font must be larger than the foreign product name font. The sample must be the original size and can be a color printout. After all the above materials are prepared, submit them to the National Commodity Inspection Bureau through the local commodity inspection bureau for review and approval, and issue the "Chinese Registered Trademark Approval Certificate". One wine corresponds to one trademark. If a wine applies for another trademark, the Chinese name of the wine cannot be the same. If the declared Chinese name is already repeated, it needs to be renamed.

V. Determination of Chinese names for imported wines

1. Named after grape varieties or manufacturers, named after the Chinese name of the grape or manufacturer;

2. Named after the place of origin, named after its Chinese translation;

3. Named after the brand, generally named after phonetic translation for correspondence between Chinese and foreign pronunciation.

VI. Precautions for receiving and sending sample wines

1. Courier Company: It is better to choose multinational courier companies for delivery, so that one company can deliver the whole process to reduce transshipment and allow full tracking. Moreover, large courier companies are familiar with customs due to their large business volume, and any import-related issues can be coordinated through them.

2. Damage Prevention Packaging: Long-distance international mailings of fragile sample wines require manufacturers to carefully package the sample wines to prevent damage.

3. Customs Taxation Issues for Sample Wines: Domestic customs generally still charge tariffs, value-added taxes, and consumption taxes on sample wines sent from abroad. Both parties should confirm before sending samples who will be responsible for paying the import tariffs on the sample wines. If the foreign merchant adds "shipper pays tax" on the shipping form, then the courier company will collect payment from the foreign merchant; otherwise, the consignee will pay. Many agents and manufacturers have experienced high tariffs imposed by customs. To avoid customs misjudging the value of the goods and imposing high tariffs, the following measures are recommended:

(1) Do not send too many sample wines at once, control the quantity to around 10 bottles or 3 bottles per type to avoid customs considering them as gifts or consumer goods.

(2) Clearly mark "sample" on the mailing form and outer packaging, and specify the wholesale price per bottle.

(3) Customs valuation personnel generally do not understand the actual value of wine and may consider imported wine as a high-end consumer product, judging the value of sample wines based on retail prices they see in stores. If the packaging is exquisite and comes from developed countries in Europe and America, the valuation will be higher. You can separate the wine bottle labels, gift box packaging, and sample wines, simply labeling the bottles with basic numbers and prices. In case of disagreement with customs, when they open the box for inspection and see this, they might change their opinion.

(4) Please leverage the business relationships of the courier company, providing them with original quotation letters to explain and coordinate with customs.

VII. Matters needing attention during transportation and import

1. Plan the transportation method in advance, book space, and choose a transportation company. The main mode of transportation for imported wine within the country is road transport, while intercontinental transport mainly relies on sea and air freight. Large quantities of wine with average value are mainly transported by sea, while small quantities of expensive or high-end wines are mainly transported by air. Before confirming the purchase contract, the transportation method and transportation agent should be examined and selected simultaneously, and space should be booked as early as possible. Avoid temporary selection or changes affecting loading, delaying capital turnover or cargo delays, especially during summer high temperatures, where cargo delays at the port yard can easily cause wine to age prematurely due to high temperatures.

2. Avoid high temperatures during transportation. Wine is an active fermented wine, and the most feared factor during storage is high temperature. Therefore, measures should be taken to avoid high temperature effects during land and sea transportation. When shipping, request the shipping agent to place the goods below the waterline away from heat sources (boilers or engine rooms). Choose regular direct flights if possible, avoiding irregular flights or those with more scattered cargo that need to stop and load/unload in hot regions.

3. Handle transportation insurance promptly. Bottled wine is fragile, and insurance is important, especially for high-value wine.

VIII. Procedures for customs clearance of imported wine upon arrival

The import procedures for food and beverages upon arrival are first commercial inspection followed by customs. If the commercial inspection fails, the goods are not allowed to enter the country and will be returned. Commercial inspections are conducted in the goods supervision area to verify the declared goods: whether the packaging meets standards and whether the Chinese registered trademark is complete. Additionally, the goods documents are reviewed, including health certificates, certificates of origin, and quality assurance documents issued by the exporting country. For wines packed in pallets, if wooden pallets are used, the "fumigation certificate" issued by the exporting country must also be reviewed. Furthermore, random inspections are conducted, typically at a rate of one-thousandth of the total quantity. Inspections check whether the goods meet national import food hygiene standards, and remaining samples are retained for record-keeping.

After the commercial inspection is completed, customs declaration and tax payment occur simultaneously. The process is the same as for other imported goods. Mainly, attention should be paid to the review by customs valuation personnel of the authenticity of the declared import wine cost. It is essential to prepare some convincing materials to avoid customs distrust and customs pricing (which would be much higher than the original declared price), causing a heavy burden on the enterprise.

IX. Precautions for foreign wine manufacturers recruiting agents in China

Since many foreign wine manufacturers see the enormous potential of China's future wine consumption market, they are trying various ways to find business opportunities in China and seek Chinese agents. However, many manufacturers return empty-handed after coming to China through various means. Therefore, foreign manufacturers recruiting agents in China should mainly pay attention to the following issues:

1. First, understand the characteristics of wine consumption in China;

2. Based on your own product structure, features, quality level, and production scale, determine the target consumers and then determine the type of agent and cooperation method;

3. Find a suitable agent;

4. Determine the steps and promotion methods for various levels of wine produced and sold by your factory to gradually enter the Chinese market;

5. Pay attention to whether the wine label design meets the determined consumer group;

6. Cooperate with the agent to carry out detailed sales promotion work;

7. Be patient, don't expect good wine at a low price to succeed in China.

X. Determining the cost-performance ratio of imported wine

The so-called "cost-performance ratio" is: wine quality / supply price. The higher this ratio, the better, indicating that the product is "worth the money" or even "exceeds expectations."

When receiving samples and quotes from foreign merchants, how to determine whether this wine is worth purchasing or has a "high cost-performance ratio"? Since wine cannot be simply quantitatively analyzed using scientific instruments, it can only be evaluated through sensory appraisal and comparison, and then conclusions can be drawn.

Cost-performance ratio is relative. For some agents unfamiliar with the supply prices or market prices of foreign wines, it is difficult to judge the cost-performance ratio. Therefore, when making judgments, it is generally best to consult the evaluations of some skilled tasters, compare them with familiar products on the market, and then calculate the tax-inclusive price, wholesale price, and retail price based on the manufacturer's quote. Check whether the retail price is lower than comparable products, and the greater the difference, the better the cost-performance ratio.

This work requires experience. The more appraisals and comparisons you make, the easier it is to draw conclusions. Generally speaking, Italian and Spanish wines have a better cost-performance ratio than French and German wines in mid-to-low-end products.

Additionally, when tasting some wine samples, pay attention to the production year and whether they are in the optimal drinking period. Also, adjust the wine for "decanting" as needed, otherwise, the general tasting experience may be biased.
