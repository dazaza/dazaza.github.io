Manchester United's acknowledged weakest link has left opponents despairing; a versatile defender with no transfer fee has become famous in one game.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-18 08:16:23

Manchester United's acknowledged weakest link has become a source of despair for opponents. A versatile defender with no transfer fee has become famous in one game. Due to the high-density fixtures and frequent injuries among the defenders, Ferguson had no choice but to use some inexperienced players to temporarily fill the gaps. As a result, Belgian player Riki Delle becomes an indispensable figure on Manchester United's defense line. Although Delle, who just turned 21, did not have a smooth career development before joining Manchester United, under Ferguson, he was reborn.

To be precise, Delle's first appearance this season was in the League Cup against Wolves. Today, once again against Wolves, but this time it is in the higher-level league and as a starter. The recent domino-like injuries in the central defense line indeed created today's opportunity for Delle. However, this is only superficial. Ferguson first used him as a right-back and then as a center-back in this match. His willingness to test a player with only two games of league experience in such a way indicates his strong confidence in Delle's abilities.

In this match, Delle performed quite impressively. The Belgian was not afraid of hard battles. To counter Wolves' plan to suppress Manchester United through strong tackles, Delle had to be even tougher. He made six successful tackles, ranking first among both teams, succeeded three times, and completed four successful clearances, which ranked first in Manchester United. This shows that although the opponent deliberately attacked his position, Wolves did not gain any consoling opportunities.

Passing the defensive test is just one aspect. Since Delle played as a right-back, he also had to push forward and assist in attack. Perhaps there were not many highlights in this regard for the Belgian, but in the first half, he successfully went down the right flank, forcing the ball directly towards the goal. In the ensuing melee, it was cleared by an opposing defender at the last moment. For Delle, who was playing in his first league game for Manchester United this season, you cannot ask for too much. Additionally, when he switched to play as a center-back at the 60-minute mark, his performance was even more impressive. His decisive and clean ball handling, combined with his ease in dealing with players over 1m90 despite being 1m86 himself, earned special praise from Ferguson after the match. Ferguson said: "Delle performed very well when he moved to the center-back position, showing great potential. He is a natural center-back."

Including today's match, Delle has played a total of five games for Manchester United, three in the League Cup and two in the league. The last game of the previous season against Hull City was his first league appearance. Although five games may seem insufficient to draw conclusions, Delle has already played as a left-back, right-back, and both sides of the center-back position, making him a standard versatile defender in the backfield. Considering the future, Delle has a complete chance to become a legendary figure for Manchester United or at least an indispensable all-rounder like O'Shea. Interestingly, when he joined Manchester United in January 2009, they didn't spend much money on him because Manchester United agreed to pay his former club Stoke City based on his performance. Clearly misjudging his potential, Stoke agreed at the time. Despite only passing a year, and despite Stoke possibly making a small profit, Manchester United is clearly the sole beneficiary. Stoke's limited capabilities are evident as they let go of a player who could have been an important addition to their squad.

It is unimaginable that if Delle had set entering Stoke City as his highest career goal and had not been noticed by Manchester United scouts, when would he have emerged? Transferring from Antwerp to Stoke City in 2007 was seen by many Belgians as a good opportunity, but being loaned out to Wrexham suppressed his professional aspirations significantly. It might have been better for him to stay in Antwerp and play in the Belgian Second Division. First, Manchester United scouts identified his talent, and then Ferguson recognized his potential, giving Riki Delle a real chance for development.

We must admire Ferguson's recognition of young players' abilities. The use of Macheda, Da Silva brothers, and Obertan has already achieved initial success. The key lies in the fact that the fees for bringing them in were really cheap. Obertan was the most expensive, costing no more than £3 million. Delle represents the latest success. And in August, the 16-year-old Paul Pogba brought in from Le Havre might be the next surprise. Recently, he helped Manchester United's U18 team achieve a four-game winning streak, scoring three goals in four matches, including one against Liverpool's U18 team. Just a few days ago, he scored within the first minute, helping Manchester United's U18 team smoothly advance to the fourth round of the Youth FA Cup. Moreover, in his debut for Manchester United's reserve team against Bolton on the 14th, he scored a goal and provided an assist. Born in 1993, his growth speed is astonishingly fast. At this rate, a call-up to the first team could come at any time. Ferguson's emphasis on young players, especially his unique and accurate eye for talent selection, lays a very solid foundation for Manchester United's future orderly development.
