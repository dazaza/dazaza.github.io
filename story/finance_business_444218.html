Methods of Detoxifying the Five Organs - Worth Collecting

What are the toxins of the five organs?

In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), there are many toxins in our bodies. Any substance that cannot be promptly expelled and has adverse effects on both our physical and mental well-being can be called "toxins." Examples include stagnant blood, phlegm-dampness, cold pathogen, food accumulation, qi stagnation, and heat-fire. When these toxins accumulate in the five organs, they accelerate their aging. Consequently, the skin, tendons, muscles, and nerves nourished by these organs also age. Although the toxins are deeply hidden, they leave traces on the surface of the body. Different appearances indicate where the toxins are stored. Now, we need to find out where the toxins hide and expel them as soon as possible.

If the liver has toxins

Manifestations:

1. The nail surface has raised ridges or is concave. TCM believes that “the liver governs the tendons,” and nails are part of the tendons. Therefore, when toxins accumulate in the liver, there will be obvious signals on the nails.

2. Breast hyperplasia occurs, with significantly increased breast pain before menstruation. The breasts are an important checkpoint along the liver meridian. Once there is "toxin" in the liver meridian, breast hyperplasia will immediately occur, especially during menstruation when the full flow of blood and qi causes noticeable swelling and pain.

3. Emotions become easily depressed. The liver is the organ responsible for regulating emotions within the body. Once the toxins in the liver cannot be expelled in time, blocking the flow of qi, it will lead to noticeable negative emotions.

4. Migraines, acne on the sides of the face, and dysmenorrhea. The sides of the face and the lower abdomen are the "territory" of the liver meridian and its partner, the gallbladder meridian. Once the liver's detoxification process is not smooth, its own "backyard" will catch fire first.

How to detox more smoothly

1. Eat green foods. According to TCM's Five Elements theory, green foods can promote liver qi and have excellent effects in soothing the liver, relieving depression, and improving mood, making them helpful foods for liver detoxification. TCM experts recommend green oranges or lemons, peeling them and making green orange juice or green lemon water, which can be consumed directly.

2. Goji berries enhance the liver's tolerance. Besides detoxifying, it is also necessary to improve the liver's ability to resist toxins. This type of food is primarily goji berries, which have good protective effects on the liver and can increase its tolerance to toxins. It’s best to eat them by chewing a small handful daily.

3. Press the liver detoxification acupoint. This refers to Taichong point, located in the depression between the first and second metatarsal bones on the top of the foot. Use your thumb to massage for 3-5 minutes until you feel slight soreness. Don't use too much force, and alternate between both feet.

4. Tears detox method. Compared to men who never cry, women live longer, which cannot be unrelated to tears. TCM has long recognized this, and Western medicine has also confirmed it. As an excretory fluid, tear contains some biochemically harmful toxins to the body. So, when you're upset, feel wronged, or under pressure, just cry it out. For those who are always cheerful, watching a sad movie on a weekend afternoon and letting your tears flow with the plot is also a proactive way to detox.

If the heart has toxins

Manifestations:

1. Tongue ulcers. TCM believes that the tongue is most closely related to the heart, so ulcers on the tongue are often considered to be due to internal heat or toxic heat in the heart.

2. Acne on the forehead. The forehead is a territory governed by the heart. When excessive heat becomes toxic heat, this area will also boil over, resulting in recurring acne.

3. Insomnia, palpitations. The heart works continuously, and when toxic heat lingers in the heart without being expelled, sleep will not be restful.

4. Chest tightness or stabbing pain. The presence of stagnant blood in the heart is also a form of toxin, similar to traffic congestion on a road. Mild cases result in chest tightness, while severe ones may cause stabbing pain.

How to detox more smoothly

1. Bitter-tasting foods detoxify. Lianzi Xin (Lotus Seed Heart) is recommended because it tastes bitter and can disperse heart fire. Although it has a cooling nature, it does not harm the body's yang energy, so it is considered the best food to resolve heart heat toxins. You can make tea with lotus seed hearts, possibly adding bamboo leaves or raw licorice to enhance its detoxifying effect.

2. Press the heart detoxification acupoint. This refers to Shaofu point, located in the palm, between the 4th and 5th metacarpal bones, at the base of the little finger and ring finger when making a fist. Pressing this point can be done with some force, alternating between both hands.

3. Mung beans diuretic detoxification. Mung beans help detoxify through diuresis and clearing heat. However, mung beans should be consumed in liquid form, such as mung bean soup or mung bean milk, as mung bean cakes will have a lesser effect.

Best time for heart detoxification

Between 11 am to 1 pm is the strongest time for the heart, so eating heart-nourishing and detoxifying foods like Poria, nuts, soybeans, black sesame seeds, jujubes, and lotus seeds is beneficial.

If the spleen has toxins

Manifestations:

1. Spots on the face. Women with spots usually have weaker digestive systems.

2. Excessive vaginal discharge. The spleen is responsible for removing dampness from the body. If dampness exceeds the spleen's capacity, it leads to excessive dampness in the body, one manifestation being increased vaginal discharge.

3. Fat accumulation. Fat, known in TCM as "phlegm-dampness," arises from poor digestion and inability to timely eliminate waste and toxins. Effective weight loss must focus on restoring normal metabolic functions of the spleen and stomach regarding phlegm-dampness; otherwise, it will rebound.

4. Obvious bad breath, acne or ulcers around the lips. The areas around the mouth and lips belong to the spleen. When toxins in the spleen cannot be expelled, accumulated toxins will find opportunities to erupt in these areas.

How to detox more smoothly

1. Eating sour foods helps the spleen detox. For example, dried plums (ume) and vinegar are optimal foods for resolving toxins from food. They enhance gastrointestinal digestion, allowing toxins in food to be expelled quickly. Additionally, sour-tasting foods strengthen the spleen, effectively acting as "anti-toxin foods."

2. Press the spleen detoxification acupoint. This refers to Shangqiu point, located in the depression anterior and inferior to the inner ankle. Use your fingers to press and rub this point until you feel a sensation of heaviness and soreness. Do this for about 3 minutes on each foot alternately.

3. Walk after meals. Exercise helps the spleen and stomach digest, accelerating the speed of toxin expulsion. However, consistency is key for better results.

Best time for spleen detoxification

After meals is the easiest time for toxins to accumulate. If food isn’t digested or absorbed promptly, toxins will build up. Besides walking after meals, since sweet flavors strengthen the spleen, eating one fruit an hour after eating can help strengthen the spleen and detoxify.

If the lungs have toxins

Manifestations:

1. Dull, rust-colored skin. TCM believes that the lungs manage the skin throughout the body. Whether the skin is moist and fair depends on the lungs functioning properly. When there are many toxins in the lungs, these toxins will deposit on the skin, making the complexion look dull.

2. Constipation. TCM considers the lungs and large intestine as one system. When the lungs contain toxins, there will also be abnormal accumulations in the intestines, leading to constipation.

3. Melancholy and sadness. Toxins in the lungs interfere with the circulation of qi and blood, preventing the lungs from relieving chest oppression adequately, causing feelings of melancholy and sadness.

How to detox more smoothly

1. Radishes are lung detoxifying foods. In TCM, the relationship between the large intestine and lungs is very close. The extent to which the lungs expel toxins depends on whether the large intestine is unobstructed. Radishes help the large intestine expel old stools, which can be eaten raw or mixed into cold dishes.

2. Lily bulbs enhance the lungs' anti-toxin capability. The lungs dislike dryness. In dry conditions, toxins tend to accumulate. Mushrooms and lily bulbs have excellent lung-nourishing and yin-nourishing effects, helping the lungs fight toxins. When cooking, processing time should not be too long, otherwise, the juice of the lily bulb will decrease, significantly reducing its detoxifying effect.

3. Press the lung detoxification acupoint. Hegu point is beneficial for the lungs. It is located on the back of the hand, between the 1st and 2nd metacarpal bones, specifically at the midpoint on the radial side of the 2nd metacarpal bone. You can pinch this area with your thumb and index finger and apply firm pressure.

4. Sweating detoxifies. Since the lungs manage the skin, sweating profusely allows sweat to carry away toxins from within the body, refreshing the lungs. In addition to exercise, another method to induce sweating is taking a hot bath. Adding ginger and mint essential oil to the water before bathing promotes even more profuse sweating, expelling deep-seated toxins from the body.

5. Deep breathing. During each breath, residual exhaust gases remain in the lungs. These exhaust gases, compared to fresh oxygen-rich air, are also considered toxins. A few deep breaths can reduce the residual exhaust gases in the body.

Best time for lung detoxification

The lungs are strongest from 7 am to 9 am. At this time, aerobic exercises like jogging are ideal for strengthening the lungs' detoxifying function.

If the kidneys have toxins

Manifestations:

1. Reduced menstrual flow, shorter periods, dark color. Menstrual generation and cessation reflect the vitality of kidney function. If there are many toxins in the kidneys, menstrual blood will decrease.

2. Edema. The kidneys manage the fluids in the body. Accumulation of toxins in the kidneys reduces their ability to remove excess fluids, leading to edema.

3. Acne on the jawline. The jawline area of the face is governed by the kidneys. Insufficient kidney detoxification leads to excess toxins manifesting in the jawline.

4. Easy fatigue. The toxins in the body consume the energy of the kidneys, reducing the energy provided by the kidneys, thus causing fatigue, lethargy, drowsiness, and weakness in the limbs.

How to detox more smoothly

1. Kidney detoxifying food: Winter melon. Winter melon is rich in juice, and once consumed, it stimulates the kidneys to increase urine output, expelling toxins from the body. Winter melon can be cooked in soups or stir-fried, with a preference for a lighter taste.

2. Kidney anti-toxin food: Yam. Although yam can nourish many organs simultaneously, it ultimately focuses on replenishing the kidneys. Regular consumption of yam can enhance the kidney's detoxifying function. Candy-pulled yam is an excellent way to consume it, as the caramelized sugar enhances its kidney-nourishing and anti-toxic properties.

3. Press the kidney detoxification acupoint: Yongquan point. This is the lowest acupoint in the human body. If the human body were a building, this acupoint would be the outlet of the sewage pipe. Regularly pressing it shows significant detoxifying effects. Yongquan point is located in the front third of the sole (excluding the toes). This point is relatively sensitive, so do not apply too much force. Simply feel it slightly, and combine pressing with rubbing for about 5 minutes.

Best time for kidney detoxification

The most suitable time for kidney detoxification is from 5 am to 7 am. After the body repairs itself overnight, toxins gather in the kidneys by morning. Drinking a glass of plain water upon waking flushes the kidneys and expels toxins from the body.
