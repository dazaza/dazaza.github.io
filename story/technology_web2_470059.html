Asian Games "Smiling Sister" goes viral online, she's a lucky ceremony volunteer

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-03 13:36:58

This log is from QQ12348 space, welcome to reprint.

Since the opening ceremony of Guangzhou Asian Games, the three words "smile sister" have quickly become popular on the Internet. What's going on? Who is the "Smiling Sister" of the Asian Games? In fact, it's very simple - Wu Yi, who always kept a bright smile during the opening ceremony of the Guangzhou Asian Games, was called "Smiling Sister" by a large number of netizens, and she might be one of the people with the highest on-screen rate that night.

At the opening ceremony of the Guangzhou Asian Games, who had the longest screen time on live TV? It could be her - during the speeches of the three leaders at the opening ceremony, there was a beautiful volunteer almost always on screen, whose bright and unchanging smile moved the netizens, and she was called the "Smiling Girl" by the netizens. We learned that this girl is named Wu Yi, from the Nanguo Business School of Guangdong University of Foreign Studies, despite being remembered by the audience for her smile, she didn't know on stage that the camera was filming her. More interestingly, her twin sister Wu Yue, who studies at the Southern Medical University, is also an etiquette volunteer for the Asian Games, and together with her sister, participated in yesterday's opening ceremony.

The "Smiling Girl" moved the netizens

As soon as the opening ceremony of the Guangzhou Asian Games ended, a netizen posted: "The speeches of the three leaders let the whole country know this most fortunate woman. Fortunate because she was on screen for such a long time during the live broadcast of the opening ceremony (as long as the total time of the three leaders' speeches); but she smiled until her face stiffened, her hands were persistently crossed in front of her abdomen, almost cramping. It was too difficult for her."

This etiquette volunteer, dressed in a white shirt and black coat, received much attention after the opening ceremony, and on the live video channel of the Asian Games, there was also a replay video titled "Beautiful etiquette miss, always smiling and charming."

Unaware of being filmed on stage

The question remains, who is she and where does she come from? Reporters confirmed first-hand that this girl is named Wu Yi, from the Exhibition Economy class of the Nanguo Business School of Guangdong University of Foreign Studies, a freshman student. After participating in the opening ceremony, Wu Yi accepted a phone interview from the reporter. Wu Yi said that she herself did not know that she was continuously filmed, "I thought the camera was only aimed at the speaking leaders." This shows that her charming smile was not deliberately maintained because of the presence of the camera. "This is the result of our professional training," she said.

Wu Yi said that there were three etiquette volunteers on stage at the time, each leading a leader to speak on stage, and Wu Yi was responsible for leading Prince Ahmed, the chairman of the Olympic Council of Asia. Wu Yi said that she was very excited to have successfully completed the tasks at the opening ceremony. She emphasized several times that whatever honor there was, it represented all the etiquette volunteers, "In fact, everyone is the same, just with slightly different responsibilities."

Reporter: Sorry for disturbing you so early.

Wu Yi: It's not early, we award presentation etiquette volunteers got up at 4:50 in the morning. Now we are in Nansha, and we will participate in the first gold medal awarding of men's long fist at nine o'clock, so we came over at six o'clock.

Reporter: At the opening ceremony, when several leaders of the Asian Games were speaking, you stood behind them, always smiling, and the camera was on you for more than ten minutes.

Wu Yi: I only knew this after the opening ceremony when I was backstage, I was very surprised, I thought the camera would only shoot the leaders. My classmates backstage told me they saw me on TV, I thought it was just a quick scan, I didn't expect to be on screen for so long.

Reporter: Before the opening ceremony ended, you were discussed online, saying that you showed the "Oriental Beauty", some even called you "Smiling Sister".

Wu Yi: Everyone is flattering me, I really don't dare to claim the "Oriental Beauty". Being selected into the etiquette team, everyone is very serious. If there is glory, it belongs to the collective glory of more than 30 award presenters, it's just that I'm lucky to be filmed by the camera for a longer time.

Reporter: Have you seen the online comments?

Wu Yi: I can't go online, I haven't even watched the replay of the opening ceremony on TV. But as soon as the opening ceremony ended, I received many calls backstage, from my parents, classmates, teachers, they all said they saw me on TV. I was very surprised.

Reporter: Your parents must be proud of you, right?

Wu Yi: My ancestral home is in Yangzhou, Jiangsu, my parents both work in Guangzhou, they were watching the live broadcast that night, but seeing me on TV was unexpected. They were very happy, saying I looked beautiful. I am also very satisfied. I have a younger sister Wu Yue, studying at Southern Medical University, we both were selected into the etiquette team and participated in the opening ceremony.

Reporter: Did you and your sister actively sign up to be volunteers for the Asian Games?

Wu Yi: With such a large event held in Guangzhou, we both wanted to participate. The teacher also recommended us. When recruitment was nationwide, we signed up. Finally, I was the only one from Nanguo Business School of Guangdong University of Foreign Studies to be selected. My younger sister at Southern Medical University also signed up, what a coincidence, we were both selected, both in the award presentation etiquette team, now we live together every day.

Reporter: On stage, you probably didn't think about it much?

Wu Yi: Once on stage, everything was done according to the etiquette requirements, everyone just had different position arrangements, no matter where, good posture must be maintained. At the time, I didn't think about being filmed by the camera, I didn't think about it much, I just required myself to do my best.

Reporter: The opening ceremony of the Asian Games was globally focused, standing on the podium, next to big leaders, were you nervous?

Wu Yi: Of course I was nervous, my whole body was tense. Under the gaze of ten thousand people, maintaining a smile all the time is quite challenging. I also felt that my smile was a bit stiff, but I was confident. We've trained for so long, I believe I can definitely do well, as long as I pay more attention to details.

Reporter: Did you know beforehand that you would participate in the opening ceremony and stand on the stage?

Wu Yi: We are very lucky, out of 550 award presentation professional volunteers, 30 people were selected to participate in the opening ceremony, and finally 3 people were drawn to go on stage. We three are the luckiest ones. However, the glory belongs to everyone, everyone is excellent. To have the opportunity to participate in the Asian Games, I am very proud and honored.

Twin sisters

Surprisingly, Wu Yue, the twin sister of Wu Yi, also became an Asian Games etiquette volunteer. At yesterday's opening ceremony, Wu Yue served as an etiquette volunteer on the pathway leading to the stage. Wu Yue studies at the College of Humanities and Management of Southern Medical University. After attending the unified training for the Asian Games award ceremony organized by the Organizing Committee, the two sisters successfully completed all courses and were selected by the Organizing Committee to participate in the torch collection and relay ceremony.
