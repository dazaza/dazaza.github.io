User's own words: How Microsoft persuaded me to buy an iPhone 5s

by anonymous on 2013-10-15 20:50:38

Not long ago, I was a loyal Windows Phone user. Ever since I switched from a feature phone to a smartphone, Windows Phone became my constant companion. I would turn to it for everything – even if a Windows PC was right in front of me. So, how did Apple manage to sway both my heart and actions, leading me to stand in line to purchase an iPhone 5s? No, Apple didn’t win me over—it was Microsoft that convinced me to buy the iPhone 5s.

**Recent Years of Inaction**

Windows Phone hasn’t been around for very long. After abandoning Windows Mobile, Microsoft launched Windows Phone 7, which received universal acclaim: finally, there was a software platform that embodied the future concept of mobile interfaces. Unfortunately, in the following three years, it seemed as though the design team behind Windows Phone had accomplished nothing.

Windows Phone 8 performed decently, and Live Tiles (dynamic windows) remained the most remarkable notification system I'd ever seen on a smartphone operating system. However, after watching videos showcasing the notification features in iOS 7, I realized that Live Tiles and pop-up windows aren't the only viable notification systems for mobile devices.

In addition to these issues, Microsoft had a fatal flaw: its inability to deliver updates to users in a timely manner. This left a sour taste in my mouth. Apple’s operating system not only looked attractive but also came with full functionality. The radio app that came pre-installed on my Lumia 920 and HTC 8X purchased a year ago still lacked a replay function, and I’ve been waiting for Microsoft’s update ever since. It's reported that Apple released their update back in June this year, yet I haven’t seen any sign of it even now.

**Microsoft Products Are Also Available on iOS**

Despite all the problems with Windows Phone, I still valued and cherished Microsoft’s other core products, such as Xbox Music, SkyDrive, Office 2013, and Bing. Until recently, you had to own a Windows Phone to fully enjoy all of Microsoft’s powerful products at once.

Fortunately, Microsoft has never hesitated to provide iOS versions of its various services and products. Therefore, my iPhone 5s now has Xbox Music, Office 2013, Bing, Xbox SmartGlass, OneNote, and SkyDrive installed. As you can probably guess, I’m still using Microsoft’s services rather than Apple’s corresponding ones.

If I decide to switch back to using Windows Phone or Android, I can take all the information from these services with me. Moreover, each application is compatible with the Windows 8.1 Dell XPS 13 and Microsoft Surface that I currently own.

The fact that Microsoft supports other mobile platforms with its other businesses is good news for consumers. However, this also means that the unique interface of Live Tiles and the top-tier SkyDrive cloud storage service are the only things distinguishing Windows Phone from other platforms.

**Where Are the Great Devices?**

I am a staunch Nokia supporter. From the moment I opened the package of my HTC 8X and realized it didn’t come close to matching the Lumia 920, I became a firm Nokia supporter. Nokia’s phones are truly impressive. While many Windows phones have fallen by the wayside in comparison to Apple’s iPhone 5s and Samsung’s Galaxy S4, the Lumia 920 continues to hold its ground.

However, Nokia’s devices lack visual appeal. At one point, the world began favoring colorful metal materials and polished surfaces, but Nokia remained indifferent, sticking to bulky polycarbonate materials. Don’t misunderstand me—I value the durability of a phone. The Lumia 920 can even withstand collisions. But I believe that placing the Lumia 1020 or Lumia 920 next to the iPhone 5s and asking people to choose isn’t fair to Nokia.

Of course, Nokia tried to address this issue with the Lumia 925, but this device hit the shelves way back in early spring this year. Now, the Lumia 925 could be replaced by another new Nokia model at any time.

I bought the iPhone 5s because Microsoft made it so easy for me to make that choice. One day, I might return to using Windows Phone because I like its interface and prefer Microsoft’s products. However, even from the perspective of a Microsoft fan, the iPhone 5s is clearly the best choice right now.
