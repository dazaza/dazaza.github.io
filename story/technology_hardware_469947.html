Cantonese Three-Character Classic

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-03 12:46:34

At the beginning of a person's life - Mouthy (Children speak without reserve, speaking without choosing words.)

Smilingly riding - Releasing venomous snakes (Hiding knives in smiles.)

Still taking photos - Girls still causing a fuss (After 1997, Hong Kong preserved the original owner system for fifty years unchanged.)

Dancing continues - Roosters continue to crow

Knowing little - Pretending to be a representative (Taking credit for being clever.)

How long - Pretending to be important (An important bag is a vulgar term in Hong Kong meaning a big shot; this sentence refers to someone who is ostentatious and overbearing.)

Teeth chattering - Dying miserably (Misfortune comes from the mouth.)

Eyes blinking - Pretending to be an actress (Even without committing offenses, they are detestable.)

Voice loud - Selling inferior goods (Overstating reality.)

Big eyes - Easily corrupted (Young and impressionable, easily led astray.)

Body restless - Pretending to be busy (More talk than action.)

Eyes moist - Pretending to be melancholy (Crying over spilt milk.)

Mouth boastful - Rarely beaten (Talkative, stirring up trouble.)

Quietly - Admitting defeat (Surrendering when at a disadvantage.)

If only I had known - Begging (Unpredictable worldly affairs.)

Early knowledge - Winning the Triple Trio (The Triple Trio is Hong Kong's horse racing pool with the highest jackpot.)

Bringing stools - Seizing the front row (Fearing to lose out.)

One ticket - Watching until laughter (In Hong Kong's adult movie theaters, one ticket allows you to watch all day.)

Fine, fine, fine - Not watching properly (Going through the motions.)

Cut corners - Leaving early (Learning laziness.)

Shaking - In deep trouble (Shaking, trembling.)

Troublemaking - Losing customers (Those who cause trouble and disrupt.)

Shoulder leaning - Few aspirations (Living day by day, seeking mediocrity.)

Foggy weather - No need to work (Taking advantage of confusion.)

Keeping a mistress - Listening to truth (It's dangerous for Hong Kong people to have affairs on the mainland.)

Too many Zhao - Worthless (Making oneself look bad.)

Having posture - Lacking substance (Appearances can be deceiving.)

Having transactions - Depending on strength (Not relying on unconventional methods.)

Seven or eight - Drinking until vomiting ("Seven or eight" is a guessing game in Karaoke nightclubs.)

Hard bones - Eating the Earth ("Earth" refers to cigarettes produced in China during the 60s and 70s.)

Tight funds - No real money (Working hard to earn money.)

Confused - Easy to be deceived (Being unclear makes it easy to be tricked.)

Calm and composed - Having surplus money (Remaining calm in any situation; "surplus" sounds like "quiet.")

Panicking - Hurting neighbors (Lack of patience ruins great plans.)

Unclear forest - Ascending to Qionglin (Qionglin is a famous Hong Kong nightclub.)

Dry mouth - Eating cough drops (No saliva left.)

Gambling opportunities - Feeling good (Addicted gamblers.)

Three short of one - Worse than beggars (Mahjong terminology.)

Winning victories - Eating double portions (Making mistakes amidst busyness.)

High fortune - Seizing opportunities (Capitalizing on timing.)

Smoke K J - Holding tightly (Poker terminology, very high chances of winning.)

Two bottom cards - Barely worth seeing ("Crab" terminology, more losses than wins.)

Talking nonsense - Treating as gospel (Speech that shocks unless it's extraordinary.)

What tricks - Lucky in the middle (Arrogant but lucky.)

Winning a candy - Losing a factory (Gambling leads to loss nine times out of ten.)

Winning an umbrella - Losing a car (Another version of the previous sentence.)

Face confused - Acting like Old Feng (Old Feng means acting under orders.)

Young and naive - Easily deceived (Inexperienced girls are easily deceived when pursuing the opposite sex.)

A woman's heart - A needle at the bottom of the sea (Women are fickle.)

For no reason - Playing the flute while curled up (Nonsensical.)

Eighteenth floor - Own friends (Neighborhoods help each other.)

Having a home - Early comfort (Wishing for millions of spacious homes.)

Less education - Being looked down upon (People with less knowledge are often disrespected.)

Face to face - Looking familiar (Feels like we've met before.)

Head bobbing - Looking around (Bored and restless.)

Corrupt - Irresponsible (Shameless individuals.)

Exhausted all strategies - Sending Long gifts (Meaning not following conventional wisdom, the whole sentence suggests forcing someone into a corner.)

Curry coffee - Pretending to be a sister (Fake stonefish pretending to be grouper.)

Repeatedly missing - Losing contact (Important people forgetting connections.)

Looking neat - Having environment (Revealing flaws.)

Everyone gathered - Waiting for distribution (United efforts.)

Taking advantage of illness - Taking lives (Eliminating all traces.)

Showing off power - Knowing how to grab the mic (Knowing how to seize opportunities.)

Not wanting to fail - Joining the masses (Strength in numbers.)

Having the opposite sex - Losing humanity (Seeing beauty and forgetting righteousness.)

Better to kill mistakenly - Not letting go (Better to wrongly accuse than let go.)

Having chest hair - Going out to party (Rough image.)

Hand hair showing - Sexual arousal (Just rhymes, no scientific basis.)

Not understanding - Pretending to be innocent (Putting on airs.)

Lazy to argue - Seeking pity (Acting.)

Big collapse - Eating oneself (Big Collapse, Hong Kong's popular night club where stall owners are self-reliant.)

Being stinky four - Not standing firm (Stinky four, those who betray friends will have no good end.)

Bright-eyed - Waiting for dawn (Insomnia, full of worries.)

Stepping on feet - Waiting for someone to invite (Idle.)

Not calculating - Not suffering (Sometimes ignorance is bliss.)

Birdcage - Eventually poor (Image of squandering wealth.)

High salary - Secretly working part-time (Secretly doing part-time jobs.)

Not skilled enough - Handling second-class situations (Not good enough.)

Following the boss - Gaining benefits (Boss, influential people; benefits, advantages.)

Wearing straw shoes - Hardship ahead (Straw shoes mean walking, delays.)

Walking fast - Good world (Childhood rhyme.)

Walking slowly - Losing nose (Slow walkers have no good end.)

Your old man - Big water buffalo (Childhood insult rhyme.)

Your mother - Big wind furnace (Another childhood insult rhyme.)

Big belly Dum - No conscience (Childhood rhyme.)

Big shrimp small - Given feces (Bullying, pronounced 'ha'.)

Having background - No difficulties (Background, personal connections.)

Having quality - Great dining history (Quality refers to qualifications and reputation; dining history, chance.)

Number One - Having tattoos (Number One, influential people.)

'Gangster' Fit person - Having scars ('Gangster' Fit, slang for top leaders.)

Frequent calls - Not letting go (Compensating for laziness with hard work.)

Pointing fingers - Pretending to be a fan (Pretending familiarity, fan meaning superfan.)

Building a big house - Relying on Uncle (Uncle, a senior official, meaning needing high-level support to acquire land.)

Guessing games - Guessing with cousins (Guessing games, played in bars, cousin referring to older girls.)

Ten, ten, shocking - Pulling the lead horse (Ten, meaning 'shocking,' a very funny term. This means a person is slow-witted but has dumb luck and gets rich unexpectedly.)

Middle parting - Riding strange creatures (Middle parting refers to hairstyles, riding strange creatures refers to odd and eccentric people.)

Not enough skin - No face to give (Skin, operating expenses. When there's not enough money, no one is accommodated.)

Second-tier - Hard to change (Second-tier refers to secondary roles. This sentence says bad things are hard to improve.)

Easygoing - Wearing gold watches (Indicating arrogance, gold watches refer to Rolex golden watches.)

Talking nonsense - Creating scars (Scars should be written as '＊'; '疤' is pronounced 'la'.)

Pure white - No sexually transmitted diseases (Complimenting a girl's beauty.)

People scare people - Easily dizzy (Cantonese saying: People scaring people can't be cured. It means being scared by people is worse than being scared by ghosts.)

Steady and secure - Doing half the job (Safety first, don't take unnecessary risks.)

Tight funds - Selling white powder (Desperate measures, not considering consequences.)

Blowing hot air - Not wiping the mouth (Talking nonsense, loving to brag.)

Taking advantage of your illness - Cancelling the deal (Taking advantage of someone's misfortune.)

Taking advantage of your illness - Taking your life (Same as above, taking advantage of someone's misfortune.)

Not using the brain - Waiting for luck (Criticism of those who rely on luck instead of hard work.)

Crazy behavior - Not knowing death (Not knowing one's place.)

Making quick money - Speculating in property (Speculating in high-risk investments, like gambling, which can make one rich or bankrupt.)

Buying blue chips - No need to worry (Blue chips, high-quality stocks.)

Crossing the sea - Gambling for good luck (Sea refers to Macau, where Hong Kong people like to gamble during holidays.)

Much saliva - No urine (Referring to people who love to pontificate. Hong Kong slang says: More saliva than tea, so much saliva that there's no urine, indicating how impressive it is.)

Surrounding alcohol - Competing with strong friends (Strong friends, muscle-bound back slang. Refers to robust and powerful people.)

When I pretend - Throwing waves (Pretend, meaning fake, not genuine material. Throwing waves means relying on boasting and intimidation.)

Out playing - No responsibility (Referring to carefree and irresponsible people.)

Selling large buns - Letting others copy (Not caring about gains and losses.)

Japanese ship - Eventually finished ("Maru" in Japanese means "number," hence Japanese ships often have "丸" written on them, "完" means "丸".)
