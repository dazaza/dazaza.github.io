When flowers bloom, the twilight years

The late writer Shi Tiesheng said: Death is something that does not need to be rushed, it is a festival that you will not miss no matter how you delay.

Facing death, I can't help but feel the fleeting nature of time. Yesterday and today seem to be just a snap of the fingers.

I remember once when I was chatting idly with my mother, she couldn't help but sigh and say, "Your grandmother is getting old, she probably won't be with us much longer." At this point, I really didn't know how to comfort my mother about the impending loss of a loved one. A person's life span is limited, good or bad, ultimately we are all but fleeting guests in this world.

My mother is an emotionally delicate and sensitive person. She recalled that in the year she turned eighteen, her father committed suicide by drinking pesticide for reasons unknown. This event had a profound impact on my mother who was in the prime of her youth. That year, she almost suffered a mental breakdown. Fortunately, she eventually managed to emerge from her sorrow with strength.

Many years have passed, and once again, my mother feels the pain of losing a loved one. My grandmother's passing will be inevitable. Moreover, she is nearly eighty years old, and the passage of time seems to have made her acceptance of death more evident. Although she is in her twilight years, she still holds hope and a tranquil peace towards life. One spring, my mother casually told me, "The flowers your grandmother planted have all bloomed, come with me next time!" Influenced by my grandmother during childhood, I have always been a lover of flowers since I was young. So I immediately agreed with my mother, saying, "That's great, I'll definitely go with you next time." Time passes quickly, amidst the hustle and bustle of life, I ultimately did not get to see the flowers my grandmother planted. I imagine my mother could only go alone. During my childhood, visiting my grandmother with my mother was what made her happiest. Who would have thought that in the blink of an eye, I wouldn't be able to accompany her to visit my grandmother as often. Time is merciless, may the wheels of time move slower, or may my grandmother in her twilight years live a few more years.

Time always flows silently. Last year, when I returned home for the Spring Festival, I suddenly realized that my relatives had aged significantly. Especially my elderly grandmother, her gait had become even more unsteady, her whole body almost bent like a bow about to break. Her silver hair was like snow in winter, but her kind face remained as kind as ever. Every time my mother visited my grandmother, she would offer my mother one of her carefully cultivated potted plants. But each time, my mother politely declined her kindness, because she knew that these flowers my grandmother loved most were her best companions in her later years. My mother certainly did not want to make my elderly grandmother endure the pain of parting with her beloved plants.

Sometimes I think, a person's later years are the happiest times. Without too many worldly entanglements, one can instead live naturally and peacefully in the remaining days. Without too much worry and care for children, one can instead enjoy the rare pleasures of old age contentedly.

As one reaches the last stop of life, even in the twilight years, it can still be the most beautiful scenery of one's lifetime. Time is like this, it doesn't allow for sadness or pain. The years flow like water, and the source of life will inevitably dry up one day. But life can continue, like the flowers my grandmother painstakingly planted. Perhaps she thinks, I might not be able to see her one last time. But the flowers she carefully planted will surely meet me in the long days to come.

The wind lifts the curtain of thoughts, and I once again think of my mother, thinking of her lamentation about the fleeting nature of life. May she find strength once more amidst the unavoidable pain, and may my grandmother in her twilight years live a little longer for the sake of those flowers she has so carefully tended.

As the wind blows through the dusk, yellow leaves fall like rain.

I think, autumn has arrived. My grandmother's flowers will likely only bloom fully when the next spring arrives. In the final years of her life, she will always be that industrious gardener, even if it means exhausting her twilight years. May the fragrant flowers bloom vigorously when the next spring comes, becoming increasingly lush while she is still alive.
