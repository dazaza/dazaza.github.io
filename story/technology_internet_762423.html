Yahoo's Stock Price Hits 5-Year High on Alibaba Profit Growth Impact

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 15:25:52

On July 18, Beijing time, according to foreign media reports, boosted by a sharp rise in Alibaba Group's first quarter results, Yahoo's stock price surged 10.34% during regular trading on the Nasdaq Stock Exchange, closing at $29.66, marking its highest stock price since May 2008.

Yahoo stated on Tuesday that Alibaba Group's net profit in the first quarter tripled year-on-year to reach $669 million; revenue in the first quarter increased sharply by 71% year-on-year, reaching $1.38 billion. After selling nearly half of its shares in Alibaba Group last year, Yahoo still holds approximately 24% of Alibaba Group's equity.

The robust performance of Alibaba Group has favorably supported Yahoo's stock price. One year after Marissa Mayer was appointed as Yahoo's CEO, the company is still in the early stages of transformation. By focusing on launching new products, Yahoo aims to attract advertisers and consumers from Google and Facebook. Analyst Mark Mahaney of RBC Capital Markets believes that the majority of Yahoo's stock price increase over the past year has been driven by the appreciation of its stakes in Alibaba Group and Yahoo Japan.

Riley &Co analyst Sameet Sinha said, "Alibaba Group is showcasing a great deal of operating leverage. This company has built a massive market in China and is beginning to charge users." Yahoo's second-quarter earnings report released on Tuesday showed that due to a 12% year-on-year decline in display advertising revenue, the company's revenue excluding traffic acquisition costs (TAC) was $1.071 billion, down 1% year-on-year; net profit was $331 million, up 46% year-on-year. In the second quarter, Yahoo gained $225 million in income through its stakes in Alibaba Group and Yahoo Japan, higher than $180 million in the same period last year.

Skyrocketing Results

Yahoo announced on its official website on Tuesday that Alibaba Group's net profit in the first quarter tripled, increasing from $220 million in the same period last year to $669 million. This Hangzhou-based company may conduct its initial public offering by the end of this year.

The Asian assets held by Yahoo also provided an opportunity for hedging against the depreciation of the Japanese yen exchange rate. Yahoo stated that due to hedging transactions, the company generated $243 million in cash income in the second quarter. Yahoo stated in May this year that due to hedging on its stake in Yahoo Japan, the company generated $270 million in pre-tax income in the first quarter and $3 million in pre-tax income last year.

Yahoo stated on Tuesday that excluding traffic acquisition costs, the company's revenue in the third quarter would be between $1.06 billion and $1.1 billion. Bloomberg data shows that market analysts expect Yahoo's third-quarter revenue to be $1.12 billion.

CEO's Efforts

Bloomberg Industries analyst Paul Sweeney said that while Mayer is striving to increase the number of Yahoo users, investors must have patience in waiting for growth in Yahoo's advertising revenue. Sweeney said, "Mayer's investment in products to enhance user engagement and ultimately achieve revenue growth is a long-term endeavor. Judging from Yahoo's current earnings forecast, these investments by Mayer are unlikely to significantly boost the company's performance in the short term."

Market research firm EMarketer estimates that the scale of the display advertising market reached $17.5 billion last year, with Yahoo's share declining from 9.2% last year to 7.9% this year. Google's share will increase by three percentage points to nearly 18%; Facebook's share is expected to grow by two percentage points to reach 17%.

Yahoo's financial report shows that the company's display advertising business revenue declined by 12% year-on-year in the second quarter, dropping to $472 million. Colin Gillis, an analyst at U.S. investment company BGC Partners, said, "In fact, Yahoo's troubles with its display advertising business are just beginning. We haven't yet seen what measures the Yahoo management team can take to stop their downward trend."

Chain Reaction

Mayer likened her revival plan to a "chain reaction," stating that it would eventually lead to revenue growth for the company. During a live-streamed analyst conference call on Tuesday, Mayer said, "Hiring and retaining a great team; they will develop inspiring products that attract users and increase traffic; the increase in traffic can spark advertiser interest and ultimately convert into revenue. First comes the people, then the products, followed by traffic, and finally the revenue."

Yahoo also revised down its revenue forecast for 2013 on Tuesday. Excluding traffic acquisition costs, Yahoo expects fiscal year revenue to be at most $4.55 billion, lower than the $4.6 billion forecast in April this year, and barely higher than last year's $4.47 billion. Analyst Sinha believed, "Yahoo will experience another year of zero revenue growth."

Crazy Acquisitions

Driven by the sale of its stake in Alibaba Group, Mayer began a spree of acquisitions after taking office. During her tenure as Yahoo's CEO, Yahoo has acquired at least 18 companies, including Tumblr for $1.1 billion, as well as mobile application developers Stamped, Jybe, and Summly. Earlier this month, Yahoo acquired address book application and plugin company Xobni for about $70 million.

Yahoo announced on Wednesday that the company had acquired Admovate, an innovative company providing technology for buying and selling mobile advertisements. Four technical personnel from Admovate will join Yahoo, helping the company generate revenue through ads displayed on smartphones and tablets.

In the second quarter, Yahoo spent $653 million to repurchase 25.3 million shares of the company's stock, with an average repurchase price of $2.576 billion.

Yahoo's current stock price is nearing $31, which was also the offer price when Microsoft made its initial acquisition offer in 2008. Although Microsoft later raised the acquisition price per share to $33, Yahoo still rejected Microsoft's acquisition offer. Affected by the reduction in the number of shares outstanding, Yahoo's current market capitalization is $31.8 billion, still far below the $47.5 billion acquisition price offered by Microsoft back then.
