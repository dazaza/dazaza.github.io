Encountering love through the chaotic times: Manman Qingluo

by jqrxass on 2009-09-02 10:58:07

She had neither the grand ambition to transform the ancient world and win the admiration of the ancients, nor the potential to become an all-knowing, charming KTV singing sensation. So why did she wake up one day to find herself transformed from a twenty-year-old modern college student into a six-year-old little girl in another space and time? Faced with her father, the prime minister who was determined to let his daughter associate with nobles for personal gains, all she wanted was to grow up quickly, take her beautiful mother away early, and avoid being sold off... What about Prince Liu Jue and Crown Prince Zi Li? She didn't want either of them! However, according to the rules of穿越 (time/space travel), running away would be useless. A big battle in Lin Nan City pulled her back to Liu Jue's side, and back into the intrigue-filled palace struggles... Surrounded by wolves and tigers, let's see how this non-omnipotent穿越 female protagonist manages to survive while also attracting handsome men in these chaotic times!

Mann Qing Luo Part One

Upon opening her eyes, Cheng Jing immediately closed them again, then opened them once more before closing them again. After repeating this several times, Cheng Jing concluded that she was definitely not dreaming. She wasn't lying in her bed at home but had somehow arrived in a strange place. She moved her hands and feet, finding them intact and unharmed. She shook her head slightly; it felt a bit light, yet she was still conscious. Sitting up, her body seemed fine. Cheng Jing sat on the bed lost in thought, wondering who could have silently moved her from her home to this place in the middle of the night without even waking her.

Mann Qing Luo Part Two

Prime Minister Li’s eyes sparkled with amusement; it seemed he also didn’t intend for Qing Lei to play the zither. It was clear that the Crown Prince’s heart was already set on Qing Lei, and the Empress was simply upset that her niece Wang Yan Hui had been outdone before even making an appearance. Prime Minister Li smiled and said, “This moon appreciation event was deliberately planned by the Empress so that Miss Yan Hui could turn the tables. The plan certainly wouldn’t involve playing the zither.”

Mann Qing Luo Part Three

Qing Lei glared at her, her eyes filled with anger and desolation: “My little sister who never shows off her talents, you are just like your mother, unable to break free from the habits of a courtesan, always trying to make a stunning impression! If you hadn’t played that piece for me, I wouldn’t have lived every day in fear and pain! The Crown Prince listened to your music, and what made him admire was your gracefulness – it was you! Do you know how the half-smile on Wang Yan Hui’s face whenever she heard me play the zither always made me tremble with fear? Do you know that the more the Crown Prince favored me, the more I feared losing it? Even if I had embarrassed myself at that moment, at least I wouldn’t have fallen in love with him! But now?” Qing Lei’s voice lowered, thin and sharp, filled with deep hatred.

Mann Qing Luo Part Four

Ming Wei brought A Luo down outside the courtyard wall and saw that Liu Jue and the Black Robe Riders had already arrived at Chang Le Wine Shop. Feeling relieved, Ming Wei fainted. A Luo stood there for a moment, not looking at Liu Jue, and turned to run back into the yard. Liu Jue, seeing A Luo alive in front of him, sighed with relief, and the anxiety and irritation from earlier vanished. Suddenly seeing A Luo running back into the yard, he frowned, his figure flickered as he blocked her path. A Luo looked up at him, tears streaming down her face: “My mother is still inside.”

Mann Qing Luo Part Five

Liu Ying blushed: “To test his martial arts skills, um, the two of them… they were just fooling around.” Liu Ying refused to say any more. He thought that if the King of Pingnan ever found out that he personally handed over his number one internal traitor, he would die very ungracefully. A Luo suspiciously glanced at him but decided not to trouble him further since he was Liu Jue’s confidant. She smiled faintly: “I understand, it’s hard to say, and I won’t trouble you anymore. Just remember, if you follow me, it’s best to forget your master as soon as possible. Otherwise, don’t follow me!”

Mann Qing Luo Part Six

When A Luo heard Liu Jue mention that the grass on the prairie was too short to hide ambushes, she suddenly remembered a movie she had seen before and asked, “If soldiers wear clothes similar in color to the grass and use grass to make camouflage clothing, painting green and brown stripes on their faces, hiding flags, and setting up ambushes on the prairie, wouldn’t it be hard to spot them?” Liu Jue was startled for a moment, then delighted, “You always come up with small ideas that turn out to be very useful.” He gave A Luo a passionate kiss and ran out immediately.

Mann Qing Luo Part Seven

Zi Li’s eyes softened as A Luo sweetly smiled at him. She earnestly pleaded with him, her wide eyes reflecting her request. They rode horses together on the prairie and watched stars... He preferred to believe that A Luo didn’t want to marry Liu Jue. He knew Liu Jue’s pride, a pride that couldn’t tolerate any woman rejecting him. But slowly, Liu Jue fell in love with A Luo, loving her without any logical reason. However, did A Luo truly love him? Zi Li’s heart was filled with mixed feelings, partly admiring Liu Jue’s deep affection, and partly doubting whether A Luo had feelings for him. Zi Li closed his eyes; would A Luo never open her crystal-clear eyes again?

Mann Qing Luo Part Eight

A Luo gradually regained consciousness, opening her eyes to see a black-clad masked person standing before her. She opened her mouth to cry out, but An Ye quickly covered her mouth: “I am An Ye. My lord has been waiting for a long time. Can you walk? If you can, change your clothes and leave with me!” An Ye? A Luo's heart surged with joy, taking the black clothes handed by An Ye and changing quickly. Still dressed in black and masked, An Ye took her hand: “Forgive me!”
