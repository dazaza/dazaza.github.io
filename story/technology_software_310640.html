Looking ahead - Barca vows not to repeat mistakes of 3 years ago, Messi rests and misses semi-final

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-16 12:30:51

Preview - Barcelona determined not to repeat mistakes of 3 years ago; Messi to sit out semi-final

Sina Sports - Barcelona will begin their FIFA Club World Cup campaign today, with their opening match against Atlante widely regarded as an easy encounter. As Guardiola put it: "In which competition is Barcelona not the favorite?" Besides having similar kits (both red and blue), the two teams share a resemblance in football philosophy. Atlante's coach José Cruz is a loyal follower of former Mexican national team coach Lavolpe, who is also a coach that Guardiola admires greatly.

In predictions, Barcelona is such a strong favorite that the organizers of the Club World Cup have allowed Barcelona to wear their traditional red and blue kit, while Atlante must wear indigo. Mexican media joked that this color isn't bad either, as it reminds one of the Caribbean Sea. In fact, both teams had originally agreed to decide on kit colors by drawing lots.

Atlante doesn't seem overly concerned about this, as they only brought three fans and one journalist. Atlante was founded in 1916 and for a long time represented the working class, but since the 1980s, the club has gradually fallen into the hands of big capitalists, drifting further away from ordinary people. Currently, the club is in dire straits; the team doesn't even have a locker room and trains on a golf course due to lack of facilities.

However, Atlante’s players are relatively young and play good football. If Barcelona underestimates them, it would be a grave mistake. Barcelona's Mexican central defender Rafael Márquez stated: "Atlante has a pretty solid squad, especially along the spine of the team. I'm very familiar with their goalkeeper and forwards. Interestingly, Atlante's forward is also named Rafael Márquez." He shares his name with the Barcelona defender. "The game won't be easy," Márquez reminded his teammates.

The best praise for Atlante comes from their opponent. Guardiola never takes any opponent lightly or undervalues any match. The Barcelona coach said: "If we don't get past the semi-final, there will be no final. You have to earn your place in the final on the pitch; everything else is empty talk."

Barcelona's trophy cabinet is filled with various cups, but it lacks one – the 'Club World Cup' trophy. Historically, Barcelona has participated twice in the Club World Cup (including its predecessor, the Toyota Cup). In 1993, they lost 1-2 to Brazil's São Paulo, and in 2007, Barcelona lost 0-1 to Brazil's Internacional. If Barcelona wins this time, it will fill a significant gap in the club's history. Moreover, Barcelona could achieve the extraordinary feat of becoming the sextuple champions. In 2009, Barcelona already won five major trophies: La Liga, Copa del Rey, Champions League, Spanish Super Cup, and UEFA Super Cup. Winning the Club World Cup would create an unprecedented miracle.

However, Guardiola is not interested in discussing the possibility of winning six titles at the moment. "I am more focused on figuring out how to beat Atlante on the football field. If we don't focus on the game itself, we'll make a mistake. If we can't prepare well for the semi-final, it's entirely possible that we won't reach the final."

Márquez also emphasized that Barcelona must not repeat the mistake of arrogance. In 2006, Barcelona defeated Mexico's América 4-0 in the semi-final, making the whole team overconfident, which led to their defeat by Brazil's Internacional in the final. Guardiola also referenced Barcelona's loss to São Paulo in 1992: "At that time, everyone in the team, including the coach, didn't know how to win this trophy." Psychological preparation began on Tuesday, with Guardiola looking serious, and the Barcelona players understanding the importance of preparing seriously even for matches against weaker opponents.

Finally, it's worth noting that Messi's absence will open a historical door for Pedro. Although the Argentine participated in the team's normal training yesterday, Guardiola is unlikely to start him in the semi-final. Pedro expressed: "I hope to participate in this important Club World Cup match. If I can score and be written into history, it would be perfect. I look forward to scoring a goal, but most importantly, the team must win." Currently, Pedro has scored in La Liga, Copa del Rey, Spanish Super Cup, UEFA Super Cup, and the Champions League. Scoring in the Club World Cup would create the legend of scoring in all six competitions the team participates in during a single season.

Marañón predicts Barcelona's starting lineup:

Valdés / Alves, Puyol, Piqué, Abidal / Xavi, Touré, Iniesta / Pedro, Ibrahimović, Henry.
