What are the symptoms of adolescent sinusitis?

Runny nose with pus is one of the important symptoms of sinusitis. Nasal mucus and secretions accumulate in the nasal cavity and sinuses for a long time, which may flow back to the throat and ear canal, expanding the range of inflammation infection and causing more ENT complications. Secondly, due to nasal obstruction, prolonged mouth breathing can lead to facial deformities in children who have not fully developed, resulting in "adenoid face". Parents sometimes find that teenagers may suddenly develop a high fever, which could also be caused by sinusitis. It is worth noting that many teenagers experience headaches due to chronic sinusitis, which often leads to a decline in the quality of their classes.

How should sinusitis be treated?

Some adolescents have undergone treatment before, but the effect was not ideal, and the condition kept recurring. This is because the anatomical structure of sinusitis is complex, with narrow openings that are prone to blockage and inflammation. Therefore, adolescent sinusitis has the characteristics of being easy to occur and difficult to cure. Especially during colds, adolescents are prone to sinusitis, which can become chronic if untreated for a long time.
