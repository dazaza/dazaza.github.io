Zhengzhou workers outside the assembly line

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 13:59:39

Labor costs are a major part of business expenses. In order to cut labor costs, profit-driven enterprises have two choices: lay off employees or relocate the company to a place with lower salary levels. Foxconn has done exactly that, moving from Shenzhen to Zhengzhou, where Zhengzhou is gradually becoming a new manufacturing hub. Around the clock, day and night, factories here produce international products like the iPhone. Here, young Chinese workers refuse to become worker ants.

Near the factory, there is a bar. At night, all sorts of people come to listen to energetic disco music while swaying their bodies. Lian Yulong (phonetic), a 19-year-old inspector working on the iPhone motherboard at Foxconn, is a regular visitor to the bar. "Dancing helps me vent my anger and stress. Whenever I'm here, I forget everything else." He uses dancing to forget the monotonous and stifling reality of his days.

An increasing number of leisure spaces attract workers back to the assembly line. These workers manufacture goods desired by others, yet they endure hardship themselves. Because opportunities are rare, their rest time is precious, and now they endure the present for a better future. Every worker looks forward to the weekend with excitement, even though some of their weekend plans may be simple. For example, Bai Si Hai (phonetic), a 24-year-old worker, plans to indulge in gaming online and then call his girlfriend far away.

Now, the foremen in the factory begin to see the benefits of taking vacations. In recent years, "worker suicides" have become an unsettling shadow over companies, whether it's Foxconn or Samsung's factories. After several reports of "sweatshops," facing external pressure, Foxconn improved workers' benefits and promised not to make them work overtime. However, after another suicide incident at the Zhengzhou factory, Foxconn initiated a "silent mode," prohibiting workers from discussing non-work topics during work hours. Despite Foxconn’s claim that this rule has been canceled under external scrutiny, the workers say everything remains the same as before.

The environment in domestic factories cannot compare to foreign ones. Overseas workers enjoy high allowances and various welfare and entertainment options such as haircuts, climbing walls, and table football. As for the offices, they are luxurious. Domestically, workers wear uniform uniforms and listen to routine scolding and shouting from their supervisors. As for the dormitories, rooms contain eight hard metal bed frames and a combined shower and toilet room, with nothing else.

Perhaps because of this, there is a large market in front of the factory gates. When the sun sets, Mandarin pop music drifts out from the hair salons, and DVDs are sold on the street along with sliced watermelons and roses glittering in silver light. A flatbed truck full of colorful teddy bears attracts the attention of young girls. Some boyfriends, at the request of their girlfriends, immediately take out 10 RMB to buy one. In this market, there are artfully painted vans and large video games. Young workers drink light beer and smoke continuously.

Sometimes, elaborately dressed singers mount the nearby stage. As for the youths, they find sufficient entertainment in strong liquor. Luo Haojie (phonetic) left the factory in May, quitting his job making iPhone 5 components, saying, "Our factory supervisor is vicious." Talking to a circle of friends drinking with him, he complained about the unbearable food in the factory cafeteria.

Ultimately, he will look for another job, but for now, he enjoys his youth, drinking with friends and dating girls. Luo said, "I stayed because of my brothers. Without them, I would be miserable."

Summer is the off-season for Chinese factories, allowing many workers to enjoy holidays on weekends, sometimes even double days off. The variety of entertainment near the factory satisfies their needs. At night, mobile food stalls are set up with names resembling KFC. Acrobats perform, blowing fireballs. Workers say it would be better if you came a few months earlier when the acrobatic troupe had real lions and tigers.

On Saturday, another iPhone 5 motherboard tester, Zhou Peng Zheng (phonetic), wearing roller skates worth thousands of RMB, carefully avoided plants and started spinning. These skates amount to a third of his monthly salary. He said, "It feels like I'm flying." Before the crowd grew, he quickly skated another lap.

Roller skating and inline skating have gradually formed a cultural atmosphere among those striving at Foxconn. Several teams have been formed, and on weekends, they put on their skates and glide through the city. Fang Ti Ma (phonetic), 17 years old, joined a hundred-person skating team named "Shadow" shortly after starting work at Foxconn. However, due to her age, she was unable to work overtime and thus left the factory. She said, "I used to come to the skating rink twice a week, now I come every night."

At 11 PM, the performers have gone, and the hourly hotels start to get busy. "We work every day, so why not go on a date?"
