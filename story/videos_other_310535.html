England striker Owen regrets missing Derby interview; the aging Golden Boy may struggle to impress Coach Capello.

by yinding999 on 2009-12-16 10:55:59

England's striker audition: Owen leaves regret in Derby, and the aging Golden Boy may not catch Capello's eye. In the match between Manchester United and Villa, there was an audition for England strikers: Rooney and Owen were from United, while Heskey and Agbonlahor were from the visiting team. Judging by performance, the combination of Rooney and Heskey as the main strikers for the Three Lions remains unshakable. However, Owen, who just scored a hat-trick in the Champions League in midweek, might fall into disappointment again: Agbonlahor is younger, better at scoring, and more energetic... If you were Capello, who would you choose?

The only goal of this match came from Agbonlahor's header. Gabby Logan, the beautiful BBC presenter who was filling in for Lineker, joked about her shared name with Agbonlahor: "For Villa fans, the most important thing is that Gabby made history by scoring the winning goal at Old Trafford." Although their names are spelled slightly differently, they are both abbreviations of "Gabriel," which is the nickname of the archangel well-known in the West.

This goal first demonstrated Agbonlahor's individual combat ability to control the ball in the front field and wait for reinforcements. In Villa's last 3 matches, Heskey has started every game. But Heskey's main job is actually with the England national team, and Villa is just a side gig. Why does Capello insist on using a striker who doesn't play much for his club? It's simply because of Heskey's ability to control the ball in the front field and create space for teammates. This goal by Agbonlahor also came from receiving a pass from Warnock in the front field, first using his body to block Wes Brown who tried to disrupt, then forcibly controlling the ball and waiting for Ashley Young's support from the left wing before passing it out wide. At 1.80 meters tall, Agbonlahor can also serve as a bridgehead like Heskey.

Next, Agbonlahor showcased his off-the-ball movement ability to find space. After Ashley Young went down the flank, Agbonlahor used a beautiful running curve to shake off Brown, then inserted himself into the gap between Brown and Vidic towards the small penalty area... By the time the cross came in, he was already unmarked, 1-0! For a player who comes from a near-top-tier team, what could be better than scoring against one of the top four teams to grab Capello's attention? This was his 9th goal of the season (8th in the league), and he has now scored against United in each of the last 4 seasons.

In the 30th minute, Agbonlahor capitalized on Fletcher's mistake when receiving the goalkeeper's pass, breaking through after intercepting the ball. Facing the advancing Kuszczak, Agbonlahor did not seek glory but instead passed to Ashley Young in the center, fortunately, the Polish goalkeeper defused the crisis. In the final moments, Villa began to defend stubbornly, and Agbonlahor, standing alone up front, was their only outlet.

In contrast, Owen had few highlights after coming on for Giggs in the 45th minute. Even after scoring a hat-trick in midweek, he still didn't secure a Premier League start (this season Owen has only started 3 times in the league, while Agbonlahor has played all of Villa's games), which speaks volumes. Moreover, Bent increased his goal tally to 10 in this round, leaving no reason for Owen to be optimistic.

Owen is already 30 years old, an age that is a worrying watershed - unless your name is Giggs, the Peter Pan. For such an older player who yearns to participate in the World Cup, it must be tough to sit on the bench and watch young, ambitious opponents. This wasn't United's night, nor Owen's night. And young Gabriel, with an angelic face, left with a radiant smile.
