The earliest written record of Scottish whisky was in 1494 when monk John Cor purchased eight bushels of malt to produce 35 boxes of whisky. Of course, it is certain that the birth of whisky far predates 1494. Between 1534-1535, Protestant reformers from England destroyed almost all monasteries.
