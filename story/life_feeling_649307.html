Missing You, Like Tea...

In the wind that shifts with the seasons, a memory flows like water.

Flowing quietly, scattering all memories of the past. Once, softly whispering, swaying leaves filled with poetry, asking ceaselessly, feeling deeply, blooming in a pool of tender affection, you far away, do you know?

I remember that night, when you blossomed with all your youthful charm, a pale green liquid gently dyed the entire cup of gentle water.

As the tea smoke swirls faintly, I seem to see you smiling and walking towards me. In the time it takes to drink a cup of tea, a beautiful encounter was decided.

In the exquisite blue and white porcelain, the clear green tea juice sways in the clear water, calm, warmly embracing each other. The green leaves gradually sink and float, the first brew comes with a fresh and light taste, as the tea smoke curls around, your smile becomes indistinct.

When I think of those times, I smile to myself quietly. On a windless night, gazing at the moon by the window, the moonlight spreads its gentle warmth. Drinking tea alone, under soft light, in a gentle and peaceful pavilion, I remember you smiling and saying, "I like Biluochun the most. Do you?"

In the quiet night, I imagine your former appearance, under the hazy moonlight, savoring the unique tenderness of Biluochun, light green and pure. Taking a sip, the tea is in my heart, my heart is in the tea, a bit sweet, a bit bitter, these memories twist and turn endlessly.

That night had not yet brightened, I walked along the beautiful West Lake, stepping through the night's colors, with silent steps, slowly approaching a touch lighter than dew. Your graceful singing voice attracted a group of tea-picking girls with laughter like silver bells, thus you gained a light tea fragrance, serene and gentle.

If there were no attachment, remember, a cup of tea and water, neither too close nor too distant, once so near, yet so far.

The tea color, dim and bright, in still water, the second brew brings aftertaste, coiled in the heart, gut-wrenching.

The sound of rain patters, outside the wooden lattice window, a gust of wind disturbs the thoughts of the rain, the raindrops are so tiny, the rain curtain so long and dense, blown into a cup of tea, I seem to hear the tea whispering secretly.

With the moon bright and the breeze clear, leaning alone on the railing, the distant flute music wavers faintly. Closing my eyes, I vaguely see your blurred face.

Suddenly, I feel the tea has cooled, the taste has faded. I put it down, letting the raindrops fall one by one into the tea, splashing up water flowers, finally merging together. The white porcelain, the green leaves, by the third brew, the tea tastes of sorrowful winds and bitter rains.

If it were just a cup of tea, even if it fades, it can still be savored. Tonight, I yearn for you with the longing of a cup of tea, repeatedly, endlessly, as the tenderness of a cup of water slowly fades.

Missing you... like tea...
