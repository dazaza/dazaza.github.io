Female passenger injured while escaping from taxi driver's threats in the middle of night

by zxyhszzfp on 2011-08-02 16:24:29

Ms. Li, who came to Baiyin from Shenzhen to visit relatives, took a taxi. The driver made her get out before reaching her doorstep. After Ms. Li opposed this, the driver actually drove off towards the suburbs with her still in the car. On the way, the driver threatened Ms. Li: "Do you not want to live anymore?" In shock, Ms. Li jumped out of the moving taxi, after which the driver drove away...

Nighttime taxi ride ends with her being asked to get out before reaching home

On the morning of August 17th, I met Ms. Li in Baiyin District. Ms. Li had visible external injuries on her face and arms. She said that she hadn't been hospitalized for treatment these past two days so as not to worry her parents.

Ms. Li stated that she had recently come from Shenzhen to Baiyin to visit her parents. Around 9:40 PM on the 15th, after parting ways with a friend at the south gate of Wansheng Garden in Baiyin District, she took a taxi with the number plate Gan D・31775 back to her parents' home in Jianyin Neighborhood. When the car arrived at the entrance of Jianyin Neighborhood, the driver asked her to get out. It was pitch black at night, she was alone and carrying many items, so she requested the driver to drive to the building's entrance. However, the driver refused her request and asked her to get out.

When the two were arguing, the driver suddenly started the car, leaving Ms. Li no time to get out as she was pulled back onto the road.

Faced with threats, single woman jumps out of the car to escape

Ms. Li recalled that at the time, the driver said to her: "What do you want to do tonight? Do you not want to live anymore?" Then he turned the car around and sped eastward towards the Nanshan Road. When the car reached the crossroads of the 109 National Highway Shuichuan Road, the driver again threatened her: "Do you want to die tonight?" She was very scared and asked the driver to stop, but the driver not only didn't stop, but also drove extremely fast. Ms. Li told the reporter that the driver was heading towards the suburbs in the direction of Shuichuan. Because she was afraid the driver might do something unexpected, Ms. Li said to the driver: "If you don't stop now, I will jump out of the car." But the driver paid no attention. In utter helplessness, Ms. Li had no choice but to jump out of the car to escape.

After Ms. Li jumped out of the moving taxi, the driver did not stop but drove away. Ms. Li endured the severe pain and called her friend, who subsequently arrived at the scene. Ms. Li's clothes, pants, shoes, mobile phone, watch, and backpack were all broken, and she had multiple injuries on her body. Afterwards, Ms. Li reported the incident to the police.

Severe handling results in the involved driver being dismissed

On the afternoon of the 17th, I met the car owner and the driver who were undergoing investigation and handling at the Baiyin City Transport Management Office. It was understood that the driver was privately employed by the car owner and did not have a taxi industry practitioner's license. The driver admitted that he realized his mistake; he didn't want to enter because the car couldn't turn around in the neighborhood. As for why he drove towards the Shuichuan intersection, the driver claimed: "I had diarrhea at the time and wanted to find a secluded place to relieve myself."

On August 17th, after an investigation, Baiyin City Transport Management Office handled the incident caused by the poor service quality of the taxi driver. They decided to criticize and educate the car owner, ordering him to write a profound self-examination, and ordered the driver to permanently exit the taxi industry, apologize to Ms. Li, and compensate for her losses amounting to 3600 yuan.
