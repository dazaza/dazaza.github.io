Pregnant woman finds fetus lacking right hand before delivery, though it showed normal in four prenatal examinations

by yqwdfkmt on 2010-05-29 11:39:19

The day after the hospital confirmed the diagnosis, a disabled baby was born. An appeal for a new medical review was initiated.

"How should we answer when people ask about our child's condition and how it happened?" Mr. Yang asked his wife. Mr. Yang believed that the inspection when the fetus was seven months old was not thorough enough, which was related to the negligence of the medical staff. He felt that the hospital's actions violated his rights as a father to be informed and to choose, and they should bear corresponding responsibility.

The hospital suggested going through legal procedures.

It was already 9:30 PM, and after enduring severe pain for three hours, Ms. Xu finally lay down in the obstetrics and gynecology ward of the Qingdao Maternity Hospital. It was too late; the next afternoon at 2:00, little Hao Hao, who was born without a right hand, came into the world.

Four prenatal inspections during pregnancy all showed normal results, yet a baby with a deformed left hand was delivered, making it difficult for them to seek justice from the hospital. Qingdao's Mr. Yang and Ms. Xu (both pseudonyms) are currently facing one of life's toughest challenges. Father Mr. Yang believed that this inspection when the fetus was seven months old was inadequate due to the negligence of relevant medical personnel, and the hospital had violated his rights as a father to be informed and to choose.

When the nurse brought the baby Hao Hao to Ms. Xu (pseudonym), she felt dizzy - the child lacked a left hand! On the evening of the 14th, while looking at her child waving its deformed hand, Ms. Xu couldn't help but shed tears again.

On the evening of the 14th, Mr. and Mrs. Yang expressed that they had not yet decided whether to file a complaint with the local health bureau. If no results were obtained, they would consider getting relevant evaluations and legal intervention.

Ms. Xu recalled seeing the report, and the B-ultrasound department of Jiaozhou People's Hospital did not answer who conducted the examination when the fetus was seven months old. She said, "This was done by a female doctor at Jiaozhou People's Hospital, and the inspection process was very quick. Afterwards, it was always the male doctor conducting the checks, except for this time when it was changed to a female doctor."

In April 2010, the report stated, "The baby's right hand is not detected."

In August, September, November 2009, February, and April 2010, Mr. Yang (pseudonym) showed the reporter five B-ultrasound inspection reports. The first four all indicated normal fetal development, except the April 2010 report, which noted "the baby's right hand is not detected."

"Limbs checking indeed is not a mandatory item for routine B-ultrasound. Therefore, hospitals generally recommend pregnant women undergo detailed ultrasound prenatal diagnosis around the 25th week of pregnancy, and this kind of diagnosis can certainly confirm if the fetus has any defects," said Vice President of Qingdao Maternal and Child Health Care Hospital, Chi Hebai. He suggested that the best course of action would be to apply to the local medical association for a new medical evaluation.

Relevant doctors at Jiaozhou People's Hospital informed her that the baby’s right hand might have defects and recommended immediate confirmation through further testing. Upon hearing this news, the couple immediately panicked.

On the afternoon of the 14th, the reporter went with the couple to the South Campus of Jiaozhou People's Hospital. "Checking limbs is not a mandatory item for B-ultrasound, so only normal fetuses can be detected, but abnormalities cannot be completely ruled out," said Sun Li from the B-ultrasound department. The baby's fists are clenched, and due to the baby's habitual curling, many factors can affect the B-ultrasound results. All prenatal examinations at the hospital are carried out according to standard procedures.
