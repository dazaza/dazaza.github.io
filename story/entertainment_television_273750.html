Plot Introduction of "The War for Custody":

After completing her three-year mission aiding Tibet, Chen Xiu returned to her hometown - the mountain city - with her four-year-old son, Gangbeng.

For three years, Zhao Xuepeng had been waiting patiently for Chen Xiu; thus, under the encouragement of their friends and family, Chen Xiu decided to rekindle her relationship with Zhao Xuepeng!

The wedding was both simple and grand, with everyone raising their glasses to congratulate the newlyweds. Suddenly, a car sped into the wedding venue, and Ye Jiajia appeared in black clothing with a black armband.

Ye Jiajia told Chen Xiu that Wang Juntao had been in a car accident in Australia, their child Tongtong died on the spot, and Wang Juntao was now in a coma. In his hand, he tightly held a photo of him with Chen Xiu and Gangbeng, softly calling out "Gangbeng." The doctors were at a loss, and now the only hope was for Gangbeng to meet Wang Juntao, perhaps a miracle would happen!

The people around did not support Chen Xiu taking Gangbeng to Australia. The first to oppose was Zhao's mother, who always felt this matter was too suspicious. She even suspected it might be a conspiracy by the Wang family. If Chen Xiu went abroad, she might not return so easily! Zhao Xuepeng also opposed Chen Xiu going abroad. Even if everything was true, there was still the possibility that Gangbeng’s call could wake up Wang Juntao, which would mean Gangbeng needed to stay and cooperate with the treatment... until Wang Juntao wakes up. What if Wang Juntao doesn't wake up for ten years?
