Many friends may have this concern: tattoos that were once considered beautiful and individualistic now have to be removed due to work or love reasons. So what methods can remove these tattoos? For this, we consulted experts from Han's Plastic Surgery in Jinan. Let's take a look at the relevant introductions from the experts.

To remove tattoos, you can first choose laser treatment based on the tattoo color. If it is ineffective, then consider surgery. Generally speaking, if laser treatment has no effect after four to five sessions, it should not be continued. Someone once did research on this, finding that there was almost no effect after five sessions. After five sessions, surgery must be considered. However, the downside of surgery is that it may leave scars.

If you choose surgical treatment, it depends on the size, range, and shape of your tattoo. If it is long and narrow or relatively small, it can be directly excised and sutured. After removing the sutures, external use of Conrui Bao can prevent scarring. Three months to half a year later, the incision scar can be treated with dermabrasion. Dermabrasion can be performed every three months, two to four times until the scar becomes less noticeable.

If the range is large, a skin excision and grafting procedure, staged excision, or skin expansion can be considered. Of course, this method leaves more obvious scars. Therefore, you should first try laser treatment, with surgery being the last option. If the tattoo is shallow, dermabrasion can also be tried.

Common methods for laser tattoo removal:

(1) For those who need removal for aesthetic reasons, surgical excision with a scalpel can be used. If the area is large, a skin graft or plastic surgery can be performed.

(2) For those who do not need removal for aesthetic reasons, electrolysis or chemical corrosion methods can be used for smaller areas.

(3) Currently, dermabrasion or its improved methods are also commonly used, using salt as an abrasive agent. The disadvantage is that it generally cannot remove all pigments and may leave deforming scars and/or pigmentation. However, within a period of time after the operation, the pigmentation may fade spontaneously.

(4) Laser therapy also has good therapeutic effects. Laser therapy for pigmentation, tattoos, and eyebrow tattoos shows significant efficacy, minimal side effects, short treatment time, and simple postoperative care for the affected area.
