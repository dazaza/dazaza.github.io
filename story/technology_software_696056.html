Property prices in 100 cities drop for six consecutive months, marking the start of a downward cycle in the property market

by df1iaiw7 on 2012-03-07 19:47:00

According to the survey data published by China Real Estate Index System on 1 March, in February, the average price of residential property in 100 cities across the country was RMB8767 per square metre, down 0.3% from January. Of these cities, prices rose in 27 cities and fell in 72 cities, with one city remaining steady. Thus, property prices in these 100 cities have fallen for six consecutive months, while the fall is the highest level since September 2011 when prices began to fall. Xie Yifeng, Dean of China Real Estate Economics Research Institute, believed that the main reason for falling housing prices was that the State Council did not relax regulation, developers' funds became increasingly strained and the Spring Festival holiday had an impact. Experts believed that the continued fall in property prices in February was also closely related to real estate companies' intensive promotions. After the Spring Festival, the property market entered a new round of launching and promotion. Large well-known property developers led by "China Resources Land, Vanke, Country Garden and Poly" offered obvious price cuts, while the number of projects offering price cuts in key cities continued to increase. Recently, a development by Poly Group in Shanghai cut its unit price from RMB19,000 per square metre to RMB17,000 per square metre. The delivery standard was upgraded from unfinished to fully renovated. If decoration costs were taken into account, this would be equivalent to an 80% discount. According to World Union Real Estate's earlier investigation into the extent of expected house price falls among home buyers in 28 cities across the country, 35% of buyers expected a price drop of over 30%. By the end of last year, 75% of developments offered price cuts of less than 15%. The low trading volume reflects buyers' dissatisfaction with current developers' price cuts. Regarding future housing prices, Zhang Dawei, Director of Beijing Centaline Property Market Research Department, believed that based on current data, the national property market has already entered a clear downward adjustment cycle. As the cities where regulation policies have been most strictly enforced, first- and second-tier cities lead the nation in terms of the number of projects and extent of price drops. However, Mo Tianquan, Chairman of SouFun Holdings, held a different view. He believed that given the current situation, housing prices will not fall dramatically. In the coming year, housing prices should not rise sharply and there will not be a dramatic fall either. They will remain relatively stable. Launching new developments at lower prices in third- and fourth-tier cities is gradually becoming a "habit". In the past 13 years, national land revenues have reached nearly RMB13 trillion, with pure profits accounting for more than half. The trend of falling prices in Beijing's property market is spreading from suburban areas to central urban areas. "China Resources Land, Vanke, Country Garden and Poly" are sparking a new round of price cuts in the property market. South China property developers such as Evergrande and Country Garden have sparked the second wave of price cuts.
