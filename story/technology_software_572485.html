The Wolf is Coming! A Brief Experience of Tencent's Synchronized Network Disk - Q Pan (QBox)

by xdash on 2011-11-20 12:30:29

QBox, also known as Q Disk, is a synchronized cloud storage product under Tencent. It is currently included in QQ PC Manager 6.0 (QDisk Edition) and provided in the form of an independent component.

It makes perfect sense for Tencent to launch a synchronized cloud storage tool. From the early network hard drives offered in the QQ chat panel, to the well-received QQ email attachments, then to group sharing and offline files, all the major product lines have been permeated with Tencent's understanding and grasp of the basic need for file transfer, while continuously accumulating technology.

This time, QDisk was quietly embedded in QQ PC Manager 6.0 to provide services, continuing to follow Tencent's habitual rhythm of product release: before the market prospects are explored, first launch the basic product; when the market gradually matures, quickly improve it, and first provide a small-scale public beta test through associated products on the product line, then promote it on a large scale through powerful pop-up windows in the lower right corner after the product gradually matures.

I simply downloaded and experienced QDisk, here are some personal feelings:

Firstly, you need to download a 48MB QQ PC Manager 6.0 installation package to experience it. Secondly, you need to log in to the designated website (http://guanjia.qq.com/act/qpan/) to check if you have the qualification to experience it. Some friends reported that they didn't get the qualification even after checking for three days. I succeeded on my first try. I'm not sure if it has anything to do with the QQ level strategy (I am currently VIP4 level);

The initial space is 2GB, and I haven't yet learned whether there is an expansion strategy. After installation, by default, it will create a QBox folder at the last disk symbol of the system and add a "My QDisk" shortcut entry in "My Computer";

The current version has relatively simple functions, except for basic file synchronization, direct login with QQ number, and space capacity statistics, features such as pausing/resuming synchronization and speed limitation are no longer new features at this stage;

But being a Tencent product, its strength lies in the details. In addition to maintaining Tencent's consistently high UI standards, the beginner help, official website guidance, bubble pop-ups, help pages, etc., all reflect the attention to detail (by the way, the tray icon in the lower right corner looks very much like an inverted blue iPod, doesn't it?);

There is currently no web version, no cross-platform support, no mobile device end; selective synchronization is not supported; no external link or contribution function;

The transfer speed is ideal, basically equivalent to the rate of QQ file transfer;

Like group sharing, it supports automatic detection of files on the server side. If it finds that the file already exists, it will not upload it again, but directly appear in your cloud, saving upload speed (so theoretically speaking, the more people use it, the more time everyone saves, Tencent's scary human sea tactic~~);

Operations performed when QDisk is offline will automatically synchronize when connected to the Internet.

Overall, the current functions of Tencent QDisk are simple, without the flashy features of similar competitive products on the market, taking a steady and methodical approach. However, given Tencent's strong background and the mutual promotion between product lines, there won't be any problems in future market popularization and functional expansion. For example, providing the function of saving QQ chat records to the cloud (currently a paid membership feature), uploading user-defined emoticon animations, publishing external link sharing through QZone or Tencent Weibo, combining with mobile devices such as WeChat to achieve synchronization and sharing with mobile phones/Pads, etc., the imagination space is vast.

Perhaps the road ahead for domestic small and medium-sized teams is one of several paths: 1) watch as Tencent enters the market, educates it, and then forcibly takes it away; 2) take a differentiated path, providing vertical or special services, winning with rapid iteration and good openness; 3) connect with Q+, becoming a junior member.

Anyway, the wolf is here.
