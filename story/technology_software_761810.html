How Firefox OS can gain a foothold in the mobile market

by geekzhang on 2013-05-10 20:42:45

In the mobile operating system market dominated by iOS and Android, how can Mozilla's Firefox OS secure its position? Competing directly in iOS and Android territory clearly won't work; Firefox OS must adopt targeted strategies to leverage its strengths while avoiding its weaknesses.Firstly, the market is clearly defined. The first batch of Firefox OS phones targets emerging markets such as Latin America, focusing on mid-to-low-end users rather than directly challenging high-end smartphones like the iPhone 5 or Galaxy S4. Chris Lee, the product manager for Firefox OS, said they have no plans to enter the U.S. market before 2014. Secondly, compared with iOS and Android, one significant advantage of Firefox OS is its ease of updates, which is appealing to carriers and OEMs. The Firefox OS software consists of three layers: Gonk, Gecko, and Gaia. Gecko is the platform part of the system, including the rendering engine; Gonk includes the kernel and drivers; Gaia represents the user interface. Firefox OS allows for updating parts of the system instead of requiring a full system update. In contrast, iOS and Android updates must be deployed across the entire phone, which may explain why some carriers and manufacturers don't update their systems as frequently as possible. Thirdly, balancing security and user experience: system-level applications are unrestricted, but regular applications are only allowed access to specific APIs. Mozilla has submitted 32 Web APIs to W3C, most of which are awaiting standardization.
