Care and space encourage the baby to explore actively.

by childrenstory126 on 2012-02-24 17:36:54

During the process of a child's growth [http://www.126bb.com/category-27-b0.html], actively exploring the external world is an extremely important quality, as one spends their entire life establishing their sense of self. The moment a baby is born marks the beginning of its journey of exploration. How can we cultivate in our children the excellent quality of courageously exploring the outside world? You can start from the following two aspects.

One, Give your baby enough care

At any time, a child needs to know that mom will be happy for him - simply because he is mom's treasure. Therefore, when the baby engages in exploratory activities, the baby often pays attention to mom's expressions; mom's satisfied smile allows the baby to experience a sense of fulfillment and affirmation, which encourages the child to explore the external world more positively.

When the baby is playing, it will occasionally turn around to find mom, checking if mom is still nearby. To alleviate the baby's concerns, mom can frequently greet the baby from afar, allowing the baby to confirm that mom is paying attention to him, thus enabling the baby to play with peace of mind.

When the baby has played for a long time and starts to feel tired and uncomfortable; or, when the child is in an unsafe situation, what the child needs mom to do first is to "welcome me back." Mom should warmly embrace the baby back into her arms, caress him, kiss him, look at him, and tell him that mom has always been here... This kind of skin contact, verbal communication, and eye contact gives the baby immense comfort.

Two, Give your baby enough space

Sometimes, the baby's exploration requires mom's help to continue. Provide the baby with necessary assistance, but let him complete it independently - mom's role is to act as a "scaffold," to "assist" rather than "replace" the child in completing the exploration. This requires mom to have sufficient patience, not rushing to finish things for the child.

Some moms particularly like holding their babies tightly in their arms, not trusting them to explore independently. When the baby explores, mom repeatedly warns and overprotects. Babies who are overly protected this way tend to cling to mom, not leaving her side even for a moment, and crying uncontrollably during brief separations.

Some mothers give their children a lot of love but neglect giving them space, while others give their children enough space but fail to respond sensitively to their signals, both of which negatively impact the child's exploratory behavior. As long as these two aspects are balanced, it is not difficult to cultivate in the baby a character that dares to explore.
