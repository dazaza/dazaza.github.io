The Li Zhuang case rises again

by edivista on 2012-02-24 14:52:05

In February 2012, the Li Zhuang case resurfaced, and as the curtain slowly drew back, the third season of this legal drama began to heat up. On the morning of February 7th, Ma Xiaojun, Li Zhuang's former assistant, and his wife Han Huijuan filed a lawsuit against the Chongqing police, accusing them of illegally restricting their personal freedom during the investigation of the Li Zhuang case. After verification, the Jiangbei District Court of Chongqing has received relevant litigation materials and entrustment procedures, but has not yet commented on whether to file the case.

This move was interpreted by the outside world as a counterattack by Li Zhuang and his assistants against the Chongqing police. The timing of the counterattack is quite thought-provoking.

Five days earlier, on February 2nd, Wang Lijun, the famous anti-gang hero and vice mayor of Chongqing, was officially announced to have stepped down from his position as director of the Public Security Bureau and no longer in charge of police affairs.

Three days later, the prediction came true, and there were reports that Wang Lijun (latest news of being dismissed) entered the U.S. Consulate General in Chengdu on February 6th and left after staying for one day.

On the evening of February 7th, in Chengdu, many police officers were urgently notified to return to their posts. Some special police units received orders to go to the front of the U.S. Consulate General in Chengdu located in the southern part of the city to perform duty.

On February 8th, Victoria Nuland, the spokesperson for the U.S. State Department, confirmed at a regular press conference in Washington that Wang Lijun (latest news of being dismissed) went to the U.S. Consulate General in Chengdu to meet with the U.S. side as the vice mayor of Chongqing, and this meeting was scheduled in advance. However, she did not disclose the details of the meeting, only claiming not to discuss refugee or asylum issues.

At the same time, she emphasized that after the meeting, Wang Lijun (latest news of being dismissed) left the consulate voluntarily (of his own volition), and both sides have had no further contact since then.

Cui Tiankai, Deputy Minister of China's Ministry of Foreign Affairs, told Reuters in an interview on February 9th that Wang Lijun's visit to the U.S. consulate was an isolated incident, and this issue has been peacefully resolved.

To this day, neither side has disclosed who Wang Lijun (latest news) met with or what topics were discussed inside the U.S. consulate.
