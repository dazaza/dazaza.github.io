Zhangjiang Independent Innovation: Building a World-class Science and Technology Park by 2020

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 16:28:33

Zhangjiang National New Area, which has incorporated 80% of the knowledge economy intensive areas in Shanghai, has once again become a focus at the planning level after obtaining delegated power from the city.

Recently, the Ministry of Science and Technology issued the "Development Plan Outline of Zhangjiang National Independent Innovation Demonstration Zone (2013-2020)" (hereinafter referred to as the "Zhangjiang Plan"). The plan clearly focuses on building a world-class science park. So far, Beijing Zhongguancun, Wuhan Donghu, and Shanghai Zhangjiang, the three national independent innovation demonstration zones, have all released outlines with plans up to 2020. However, relevant individuals from Zhangjiang believe that the relationship among these three locations in many industrial layouts is more about cooperation than competition.

Cooperation outweighs competition

According to the outline, in the future, Zhangjiang will focus on developing five major leading industries: next-generation information technology, high-end equipment manufacturing, biomedicine, energy conservation and environmental protection, new materials, as well as two leading industries: new energy and new energy vehicles. After reviewing the planning outlines of the three major independent innovation demonstration zones by this reporter, it was found that electronic information, biomedicine, and energy conservation and environmental protection industries are all included in the industrial layout of the three demonstration zones, and both Beijing's and Wuhan's plans were formulated earlier than Zhangjiang’s.

In terms of regional strategic positioning, Zhangjiang is “an important engine driving the innovative development of Shanghai, the Yangtze River Delta region, and even the entire eastern region.” Donghu will become “a growth pole for the rise of central China,” while Zhongguancun will “radiate across the whole country.”

Therefore, regarding their respective development scales by 2020, the three independent innovation demonstration zones differ significantly. Zhangjiang's announced development goal states that by 2020, the total corporate revenue will reach approximately 6 trillion yuan, while Wuhan Donghu will reach 3 trillion yuan, and Zhongguancun will reach 10 trillion yuan.

Mawen Gang, Director of the Comprehensive Coordination Department of the Zhangjiang High-tech Zone Management Committee, told this reporter that compared with other independent innovation zones, Zhangjiang also has its own characteristics at the planning level. Wuhan Donghu Demonstration Zone adopts a complete urban development model, which is completely different from Zhangjiang.

Compared with Zhongguancun, the lack of large-scale research institutes is considered a "weakness" in Zhangjiang's exploration of independent innovation. Mawen Gang stated that the 18 parks and over 90 points of Zhangjiang are laid out along the axis of Shanghai's urban development, thereby integrating into the overall layout of Shanghai and radiating outward from point to area across Shanghai.

In terms of overlapping industrial layouts, Mawen Gang expressed that the three locations have a cooperative rather than competitive relationship in industry. "For example, Huawei has R&D institutions nationwide, utilizing the different resources of various regions to integrate and participate in international competition."

Promoting Shanghai's transformation

The planning of Zhangjiang High-tech Zone plays a significant role in bearing the responsibility of Shanghai's innovation ecosystem. In terms of spatial layout, the area of Zhangjiang High-tech Zone has increased from 63 square kilometers to 294 square kilometers, and then to over 470 square kilometers. A characteristic of Zhangjiang High-tech Zone's planning is that its various blocks are distributed in a dot-like manner across multiple regions in the city.

"The scientific and technological innovation resources gathered in each region not only have a strong radiating and driving effect locally, but also promote the overall transformation and development of Shanghai through multi-point collaboration," said Hou Jin, Deputy Director of the Shanghai Zhangjiang High-tech Industry Development Zone Management Committee.

Besides spatial-level promotion, pioneering and pilot trials at the institutional level are more important for Shanghai. "In some issues that may not be fully resolved or achieved breakthroughs yet, or where mature experience has not been obtained, Zhangjiang can play a leading and exemplary role, providing us with very good experiences and references for subsequent follow-ups," said Hou Jin.

To match this, the Shanghai municipal government requires Zhangjiang Demonstration Zone to achieve breakthroughs in five aspects: equity incentives, science and technology finance, talent experimental zone, fiscal and taxation support, and institutional mechanism innovation.

For instance, in terms of institutional mechanism innovation, one month before the release of the plan, the Shanghai municipal government piloted the delegation of municipal administrative approval powers in Zhangjiang. Currently, the pilot work in the park is ongoing, and the ten offices of the Zhangjiang Management Committee are formulating detailed implementation rules, expected to be issued by the end of August.

"Each park will have承接权and审批权, and establish a service center. Once the detailed implementation rules are issued, the two can be connected," Mawen Gang revealed to this reporter.
