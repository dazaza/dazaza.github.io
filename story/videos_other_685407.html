Micro film about a girl taking care of her boyfriend suffering from leukemia draws heated discussions (Photo)

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-01 15:08:16

After months of treatment, in order to transfer her boyfriend to a hospital in Beijing for a bone marrow transplant surgery, Genxiu came to Beijing alone to contact hospitals, find a bone marrow donor, and raise money. The doctor introduced that the cost of the bone marrow transplant would be at least 800,000 yuan, "If there are any accidents, it will be more than this amount."

Three months ago, the two were happily living in Fuzhou. 27-year-old Genxiu worked at a small company, and her boyfriend was an art designer at an IT company. The two were preparing for their wedding. However, during a routine physical examination on September 2nd, Ke Peizong was found to have abnormal blood, and after further examination, he was diagnosed with acute leukemia.

Every day, the figure of a girl could be seen shuttling between the PLA Air Force General Hospital and a nearby rented apartment. The girl's name is Ding Genxiu. After her boyfriend, Ke Peizong, was diagnosed with leukemia, she quit her job to take care of him. Before they set off for Beijing, a micro-film called "Genxiu and Peizong" was widely shared online, touching tens of thousands of netizens with its real-life records.

This micro-film has been clicked over 20,000 times by netizens, and its genuine and warm details have moved countless people. Many strangers donated money. Genxiu said that since being hospitalized, all the savings originally intended for their wedding had been used up. The company where Peizong worked also donated 200,000 yuan. Peizong's colleagues helped establish a "Life Redemption" link on Taobao. "Some loving netizens buy 100 copies at once, saying they want to make love more complete," Genxiu said. Talking about everything Genxiu has done, Ke Peizong revealed his feelings to the reporter: "After I get better, I want to marry Genxiu and have a cute baby."

"Facing misfortune, there is a sense of unreality, as if in a dream, hoping someone would slap me to wake up." After the diagnosis, Genxiu cried constantly and didn't sleep all night. The next day, she resolutely quit her job, "Both our parents are farmers, illiterate, and cannot speak Mandarin. Only I can take good care of him." Due to the critical condition, Ke Peizong was admitted to the sterile isolation ward of Fujian Provincial Hospital and underwent three phases of chemotherapy.

In order to take better care of her boyfriend, Genxiu bought books to study on her own, looked up information online, and consulted with doctors. Now, she has become a semi-expert and is very familiar with leukemia. Under her care, Peizong's condition has improved. Seeing her girlfriend working hard for him, Peizong choked up: "When a person is on the brink of a cliff, it is the moment that truly tests one's choices. The more so, the less I can give up."

This kind of mutual support and true affection has recently been made into a micro-film called "Genxiu and Peizong." In just 3 minutes and 48 seconds, it records a day in which Genxiu takes care of Peizong. There are such details in the film: after getting up early and busying herself, Genxiu stood in front of the mirror to comb her hair before leaving, took a photo with her mobile phone, and sent it to Peizong: "It's raining again today, do I look good dressed like this?" Peizong replied: "As always, beautiful."

After chatting with the reporter, Ke Peizong kept coughing. "Leukemia patients have low immunity, and they don't adapt well to Beijing, accidentally catching a cold," said Genxiu sympathetically looking at her boyfriend. Genxiu said her boyfriend had entered the fourth phase of chemotherapy and was extremely uncomfortable.

Yesterday morning, standing in the ward of the Air Force General Hospital, 26-year-old Ke Peizong sent out a Weibo post: "Taking a picture from the ward, the first snow I encountered in Beijing..." It was the 10th day he left Fujian to come to Beijing for leukemia treatment.
