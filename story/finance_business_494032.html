Series Four of Regional Brand Marketing:

The Secret Behind the Sky-High Price of "Golden Brand" Jinhua Ham

Lao Xiangpeng, Chairman of Flai Brand Marketing Consulting Institution

On the morning of December 3, 2010, Golden Ham Co., Ltd. was officially listed on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange, raising a total of 583 million yuan. The funds will mainly be used for the construction of a production line capable of producing one million low-salt hams and 5,000 tons of chilled meat products annually, upgrading traditional ham products to create premium meat products.

Golden Ham Co., Ltd. has rejuvenated the ancient category of Jinhua Ham, bringing it onto the capital stage. Although China has countless regional specialties, only a few companies have truly succeeded in scaling up. Can we uncover the secrets behind building a prestigious brand by studying Golden Ham?

Innovative Products: Prestigious Brands Still Need Upgrades

Jinhua Ham has a history of over 1,200 years and is one of the few traditional Chinese specialties with global renown. It ranks first among China's "Three Great Hams." However, since Spanish and Italian ready-to-eat fermented hams entered mainland China, despite being priced more than ten times higher than traditional Chinese hams, their sales quickly increased, primarily in high-end hotels. This significant price difference greatly irked Shi Yanjun, chairman of Golden Ham.

Shi Yanjun noticed that the European hams were very different from the Jinhua Ham he had been making for decades. Traditional Jinhua Ham was hard, while European hams were soft. In Europe, wherever mineral water was sold, so too was ham, which could be eaten directly without further processing. On the other hand, Jinhua Ham was always found in kitchens, requiring preparation before consumption. Moreover, Jinhua Ham contained much more salt than its European counterparts, making it less healthy.

Shi Yanjun realized that as living standards in China improved, consumer demands for hams would shift towards more nutritious, healthier, convenient, and delicious options. Traditional Jinhua Ham required processing and was typically used as an ingredient in dishes, unable to meet the changing consumer needs. Consequently, he immediately began researching low-salt and fermented hams.

To make hams more nutritious and flavorful, they needed to be made "lighter." In 2005, Golden Ham developed a low-salt fermented ham with about 40% less salt content than traditional Jinhua Ham. This technology received the first domestic patent in the industry, effectively catering to consumers' demand for healthier eating habits. It also resolved the growth bottleneck of traditional hams due to their high salt content, expanding the consumer base in hotels and households and quickly gaining market recognition.

To tap into the high-end market, improve gross margins, and enhance brand image, Golden Ham did not limit itself to tradition but invested heavily in developing Western-style Parma fermented hams. They were the first to pass GMP certification and adhered strictly to the purest Italian traditional process to produce authentic Italian-style fermented hams. The Parma ham debuted at a sky-high price of 2,000 yuan per piece, and now serves as a certain level of import substitution, successfully entering this high-margin niche market.

The successful development of low-salt ham and Parma ham has allowed Golden Ham to accumulate a wealth of leading-edge technologies in the industry, leaving competitors far behind. At the same time, it has strengthened Golden Ham's position in the ham industry, significantly increasing brand value and driving the traditional ham market. Additionally, since Parma ham requires three years just for fermentation, by the time competitors catch up, Golden Ham will already be mass-producing, making the product itself a natural barrier.
