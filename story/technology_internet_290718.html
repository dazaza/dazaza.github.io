Tracking the Farmer's Spring Arsenic Scandal: The Director of Haikou Industry and Commerce Bureau has been transferred away

by sns22001 on 2009-12-03 20:31:40

On November 24, the Haikou Administration for Industry and Commerce announced to consumers that three types of beverages from Nongfu Spring and Uni-President Enterprises had arsenic content exceeding standards. About a week later, at around 10:00 PM on December 1, the same administration again informed reporters of an identical news item, stating that re-inspection results showed all products from Nongfu Spring and Uni-President were qualified. Wang Xiulu, Director of the Haikou Administration for Industry and Commerce, also stated, "We recognize the re-inspection results."

Such administrative actions have left both consumers and producers confused. To clarify the matter, journalists subsequently interviewed relevant individuals involved.

The Administration for Industry and Commerce Admits Mistakes

On December 1, after the re-inspection results were released, regarding doubts about the propriety of the initial sampling procedures, Director Wang Xiulu explained: "As a government department, we neither will nor dare take any action on the sampled items. After the re-inspection results are out, we will disclose all the work procedures and related records of this sampling inspection to the public."

Also, Director He Yunjie of the Haikou Administration for Industry and Commerce said: "We can take responsibility for the legality of the sampling inspections and welcome media and public supervision of the inspection procedures."

After the re-inspection results were out, Wang Xiulu told reporters in an interview on the evening of December 1: "After the re-inspection results come out, we also recognize the re-inspection results. There is no longer any need to announce the details of the initial sampling inspections concerning Nongfu Spring and Uni-President products."

On December 2, the Hainan Provincial Health Department believed that the Haikou Administration for Industry and Commerce had made a "procedural error" in handling this matter. Ma Yongjian, a senior official from the Disease Control Department of the provincial health department, stated that the method used by the Haikou Administration for Industry and Commerce to release the initial inspection results did not conform to the procedures stipulated in the "Regulations on Supervision and Management of Food Safety in Circulation."

Ma Yongjian mentioned that the administrative body responsible for commerce should inform the inspected entities of "non-qualified" inspection results. According to regulations, if the inspected entity disagrees with the initial inspection results, they may legally request a re-inspection. The quality inspection department retains samples during the initial inspection, and the re-inspection must be conducted on the same batch or the retained sample from the initial inspection. The re-inspection conclusions issued by the inspection agency are the final inspection conclusions.

Wang Jianlu admitted on December 2 that the major mistake in handling this incident was not promptly informing the manufacturers of the test results.

Inspection and Quarantine Departments Do Not Acknowledge Errors

Before the re-inspection results were out, Wu Shuliang, Director of the Testing Technology Center of the Hainan Entry-Exit Inspection and Quarantine Bureau, stated on November 30 that the center holds certificates of testing qualifications issued by relevant authoritative national departments, laboratory certification, and equipment calibration certificates, with the capability to conduct "detection of arsenic and organic arsenic in food." Moreover, the center's testing equipment had just passed its annual instrument calibration, ensuring it was in excellent working condition. The personnel conducting these tests were senior engineers with over ten years of testing experience. "Before accepting any testing requests, we check whether the sample quantity is sufficient and whether the samples are properly sealed. There are no issues with our testing procedures, and we can fully take responsibility for the results of the tested samples," Wu Shuliang said.

After the re-inspection results came out, when journalists attempted to interview officials at the Hainan Entry-Exit Inspection and Quarantine Bureau late on the evening of December 2, they were stopped by security guards at the entrance. Wu Shuliang, from the technical center, also failed to answer phone calls. After waiting for a long time, Kang Li from the bureau’s office came out and said, "You reporters have the right to interview, but we also have the right not to accept interviews." Kang Li further stated that the technical center, upon receiving normal commissioning tasks, conducts product inspections following regular procedures, which falls within their normal business scope.

Journalists questioned why there were such differing inspection results? Kang Li responded, "Our relevant personnel are currently understanding the situation and conducting investigations. If there are any findings, we will notify the media."

Who Will Bear the Responsibility for the Conflicting Benefits?

The two conflicting inspection results have led to significant losses for consumers, enterprises, and the government. Should such administrative actions bear responsibility?

On December 2 at the Haikou Administration for Industry and Commerce, Director Wang Jianlu told reporters that he personally could not comment and would need to consult with the leadership team before making a statement.
