With the title of "Buddhist Incense Connection", you might think that I am a Buddhist believer, but I am not. For more than thirty years, I have always followed the principle of "&逢庙必进,见佛不拜" (enter every temple, but do not worship Buddha). However, when I face Buddha, I am certainly devout and respectful. Regarding this unknown world, all I can do is to respect it. The so-called "Buddhist connection" also has its reasons: My hometown is in Chizhou, Anhui Province, where one of the four major Buddhist holy sites, "Mount Jiuhua," belongs to my hometown region. Mount Jiuhua naturally became our destination for distant travels during childhood. I still clearly remember that as a child, because I was carrying the Young Pioneers flag and the Communist Youth League flag, I often stood outside the temple unwilling to go in.

As I grew older, I learned that the reason why Mount Jiuhua is so prosperous with incense offerings is due to Bodhisattva Ksitigarbha's saying: "I will not become a Buddha until the hell is empty." What a profound realm... Among many Buddhist temples and mountains, only Mount Jiuhua has numerous mummified bodies. Strangely, those who could achieve mummification were mostly ordinary low-ranking monks or nuns similar to servants, which seems like a kind of irony.

Later, in order to learn English, I dealt with Christian missionaries in Haikou, seeing how Western believers conflicted with each other, leading to various wars and massacres. Comparing the war history of the East and West, the East mostly had "a beauty's anger causing war," while the West had more wars among believers to protect their faith.
