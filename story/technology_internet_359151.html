[Daily Focus: Guo Tai-ming quietly laying out in real estate]

The world's largest processing enterprise Foxconn is expected to shift from the least profitable contract manufacturing to the lucrative real estate industry. According to media reports, Guo Tai-ming has long intended to establish a development company, with Hong Kong Xuan Sheng Investment Co., Ltd. as Foxconn's real estate business platform, registered capital of 3 billion Hong Kong dollars. From existing projects, most of Foxconn's developments rely on the location advantages of science parks, such as Chongqing. On August 4, 2009, Foxconn Technology Group signed a project with the Chongqing municipal government to produce 20 million notebook computers, and the company also plans to invest 10 billion yuan in developing commercial real estate in Chongqing.

Analysts: Foxconn's investment in real estate goes beyond "small-scale operations," establishing a real estate company to pilot in central and western regions. The only smart move Guo Tai-ming has shown is that all his land acquisitions rarely go through the "bidding" process. From Xuan Sheng Investment's targets, Chengdu and Langfang could be its next goals. The only puzzling thing is that during the shareholders' meeting on June 8th this year, when a shareholder suggested Foxconn invest in real estate and criticized Guo Tai-ming for losing hundreds of millions of yuan per year by not investing in real estate, Guo Tai-ming still clearly stated that he would not invest in real estate and would stick to manufacturing.

China Real Estate Information Group analyst Jian Xiao said that wherever Foxconn goes, it can boost the development of local secondary industries, solve employment issues, and promote the development of tertiary industries. Therefore, local governments are willing to offer land at low premiums. Using industrial advantages to acquire land for real estate is both easy and cost-effective for Foxconn. "Foxconn headquarters tower, doing commercial projects is more realistic, part for self-use, part for rent, can be rented to suppliers, suppliers are willing to work together, business is easier. This model is what many companies are doing as 2.5 generation industry."
