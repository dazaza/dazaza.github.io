Knowledge of proximity switches

by lwp0013 on 2009-04-23 10:23:40

**I. Performance Characteristics of Proximity Switches**

In various types of switches, there is a component with the ability to "sense" objects approaching it — the displacement sensor. By utilizing the sensitivity characteristics of the displacement sensor to nearby objects, the switch can be controlled to turn on or off. This is known as a proximity switch.

When an object moves toward the proximity switch and approaches to a certain distance, the displacement sensor will "sense" it, causing the switch to actuate. This distance is typically referred to as the "detection distance." Different proximity switches have different detection distances.

Sometimes, the object being detected moves toward the proximity switch at regular intervals, one after another, and then leaves, repeating this process continuously. Different proximity switches have varying response capabilities to the detected objects. This response characteristic is called the "response frequency."

---

**II. Types of Proximity Switches**

Since displacement sensors can be made based on different principles and methods, and because different displacement sensors use different methods to "sense" objects, common proximity switches include the following:

1. **Eddy Current Proximity Switch**

This type of switch is sometimes also called an inductive proximity switch. It works by generating an electromagnetic field that induces eddy currents within a conductive object when it approaches. The eddy current then reacts back on the proximity switch, altering the internal circuit parameters, which allows the switch to identify whether a conductive object is nearby and control its on/off state. This type of proximity switch can only detect conductive materials.

2. **Capacitive Proximity Switch**

In this type of switch, the measurement component usually acts as one plate of a capacitor, while the other plate is the switch's housing. During operation, the housing is typically grounded or connected to the equipment casing. When an object moves close to the proximity switch, regardless of whether it is conductive, its approach will change the dielectric constant of the capacitor, altering its capacitance. This change causes the circuit connected to the measurement head to alter its state, thereby controlling the switch's on/off state. This type of proximity switch can detect not only conductors but also non-conductive liquids or powdered substances.

3. **Hall Effect Proximity Switch**

A Hall effect switch is a magnetic-sensitive element. When a magnetic object approaches the Hall effect switch, the Hall elements on the switch's detection surface produce a Hall effect, changing the internal circuit state of the switch. This allows the switch to recognize the presence of a magnetic object nearby and control its on/off state. The detection object for this type of proximity switch must be a magnetic material.

4. **Infrared Proximity Switch**

A switch made using the photoelectric effect is called a photoelectric switch. The light-emitting device and the photosensitive device are installed in the same detection head in a specific direction. When a reflective surface (the object being detected) approaches, the photosensitive device receives the reflected light and outputs a signal, allowing the system to "sense" the presence of an object.

5. **Pyroelectric Proximity Switch**

A switch made using temperature-sensitive elements is called a pyroelectric proximity switch. This type of switch has pyroelectric components installed on its detection surface. When an object with a temperature different from the environment approaches, the output of the pyroelectric component changes, enabling the detection of an approaching object.

6. **Other Types of Proximity Switches**

When the distance between an observer or system and a wave source changes, the frequency of the received wave shifts. This phenomenon is called the Doppler effect. Sonar and radar systems are based on this principle. Using the Doppler effect, ultrasonic proximity switches, microwave proximity switches, etc., can be created. When an object approaches, the reflected signal received by the proximity switch will exhibit a Doppler frequency shift, allowing the system to identify the presence of an object.

---

**III. Main Applications of Proximity Switches**

Proximity switches are widely used in aviation, aerospace technology, industrial production, and daily life. For example, they are commonly found in automatic doors at hotels, restaurants, and garages, as well as in automatic heat fans. In security and anti-theft systems, such as archives, finance offices, museums, and vaults, various proximity switches are often combined to form anti-theft devices. In measurement technology, they are used for length and position measurements; in control technology, they are used for displacement, speed, and acceleration measurements and controls.

---

**IV. Precautions for Selecting Proximity Switches**

In general industrial production environments, eddy current proximity switches and capacitive proximity switches are typically chosen because they have relatively low environmental requirements. When the object being detected is a conductive material or can be fixed on a metal object, eddy current proximity switches are generally selected due to their high response frequency, strong resistance to environmental interference, wide range of applications, and lower cost.

If the object being detected is non-metallic (or metallic), liquid levels, powder heights, plastics, tobacco, etc., then a capacitive proximity switch should be selected. This type of switch has a lower response frequency but better stability. Environmental factors should be considered during installation.

If the object being detected is a ferromagnetic material or if a magnet is embedded in the object being detected to distinguish it from other moving objects, a Hall effect proximity switch should be selected as it is the most cost-effective option.

In clean environments without dust pollution, photoelectric proximity switches can be used. These switches have almost no impact on the object being detected, so they are widely used in high-precision applications such as fax machines and tobacco machinery.

In anti-theft systems, automatic doors often use pyroelectric proximity switches, ultrasonic proximity switches, and microwave proximity switches. Sometimes, several types of proximity switches are combined to improve recognition reliability.

Regardless of which type of proximity switch is selected, attention should be paid to parameters such as operating voltage, load current, response frequency, and detection distance.
