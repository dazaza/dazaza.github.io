Netizens reposted that the prosecutor was detained for 10 days for soliciting prostitution.

by wls297077866 on 2012-02-29 12:42:36

Another [mystery case] triggered by an online post.

Recently, a well-known local netizen in Foshan named [Qin Shi Huangyue] was suddenly taken away by the police after work and disappeared from the online world for ten consecutive days. In reality, few people knew his exact whereabouts. Several days later, his hometown in Shaanxi received a criminal detention notice.

The Chancheng police confirmed yesterday that they had arrested [Qin Shi Huangyue], stating the reason was the publication of an inaccurate online post about a prosecutor visiting a sauna,涉嫌诬告陷害罪 (suspected of making false accusations).

The police also stated that upon investigation, it was found that the online post was not originally written by [Qin Shi Huangyue], and due to the minor nature of the crime, the criminal detention was changed to administrative detention, and he could be released as early as today.

[Disappearance] for Ten Days

Suddenly Taken Away by Police After Work

[Qin Shi Huangyue], active on various large online forums in Foshan, is familiar to many netizens. His real name is Shang Lai Cheng, a 35-year-old native of Shaanxi, who currently works as a network editor for the local website [Tiantianxin] forum in Foshan.

Outside of work, [Qin Shi Huangyue] often publishes current affairs comments in newspapers and magazines and organizes public welfare activities with other netizens. He also established a [Tiantianxin Reporter Photo Group], maintaining close ties with local media.

An unnamed person from the [Tiantianxin] website told Southern Metropolis Daily that at 5 p.m. on February 17 after work, Shang Lai Cheng was taken away by several police officers from the Zhumiao Police Station in Chancheng. The sudden action by the police left his colleagues at a loss, and they could only patiently wait for news.

The next day, Shang Lai Cheng did not come to work, and the website's responsible person tried to inquire about his situation everywhere. Then for a full ten days, [Qin Shi Huangyue] abnormally disappeared from the internet, causing much discussion among netizens. Since Shang Lai Cheng worked alone in Foshan without any relatives nearby and his mobile phone was always off, others found it difficult to know his exact situation.

Bad News from Hometown

Criminal Detention for Suspected False Accusations

Almost ten days after Shang Lai Cheng's disappearance, on the morning of February 27 at 9 o'clock, his home deep in the Qinling Mountains in Shangluo, Shaanxi, received a criminal detention notice sent by the Chancheng Branch of the Foshan Public Security Bureau.

The notice wrote: [According to Article 61 of the Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China, our bureau has criminally detained Shang Lai Cheng, suspected of making false accusations, at 6 a.m. on February 18, 2012. He is currently detained in the Chancheng Detention Center in Foshan.] The stamp at the bottom was from the [Chancheng Branch of the Foshan Public Security Bureau].

Yesterday afternoon, Shang Lai Cheng's second brother, Shang Heng Ming, informed Southern Metropolis Daily of this news. Shang Heng Ming excitedly said on the phone: [My mother is already in her seventies, and I am just a farmer. I don't know what to do now.]

Incident Triggered by Online Post

Post Claims Two Prosecutors Were Caught Soliciting Prostitutes

Journalists confirmed yesterday from police sources that [Qin Shi Huangyue]'s arrest was due to an online post.

At 9:06 a.m. on February 17, a post titled "Chancheng District Prosecutor Had Fun at Sauna Tonight Got Arrested Released Tomorrow Nothing Happened" appeared on the [Tiantianxin] website. The content roughly stated: [After the Spring Festival, two prosecutors from the Chancheng District Procuratorate of Foshan City enjoyed a full-service massage provided by my sister at a sauna place and were caught naked by the police officers of the Zhumiao Police Station! However, the next day, they were leisurely driving around on the streets without any consequences. Wailing, but my sister is still suffering!]

After the post was published, it quickly drew attention, with 1,281 views and 27 replies, but less than two hours later, it was deleted by the cyber police. Notably, the post did not specifically name the two prosecutors or provide detailed information about other related individuals.

Upon investigation, it was found that this post was published by Shang Lai Cheng under another account [Voice of Justice] registered temporarily on the same day. At 5 p.m. on the day the post was deleted, Shang Lai Cheng was taken away by the police.

Official [Clarification]

Claiming Serious Damage to Judicial Authority Reputation

Was the content of the post true? On the day the post was deleted, at 18:53, the official Weibo account of the Chancheng District Procuratorate issued a clarification statement titled "[True or False Situation of 'Chancheng District Prosecutor Had Fun at Sauna Tonight Got Arrested Released Tomorrow Nothing Happened' Post]" stating: [On February 17, after investigating the posts on Tiantianxin and Shi Erqian Forum regarding the prosecutor visiting a sauna, we confirmed with the Chancheng District Public Security Bureau that the content reflected in the post was purely fabricated. Given that this post seriously damaged the reputation of judicial authorities, we have requested the public security organs to further investigate the incident according to law.]

However, some netizens questioned the procedural rationality of the Chancheng District Procuratorate's clarification: [As two involved public prosecution departments, the authenticity of the post content should at least be verified by their superior level.]

Regarding the origin of the post, the [Tiantianxin] website staff stated that it did not originate from the [Tiantianxin] forum but was first published on another local online forum [Shi Erqian] at 17:43 on February 16. [As a website editor, Shang Lai Cheng has the function of re-posting information from other websites.] The [Tiantianxin] website side also pointed out that when Shang Lai Cheng forwarded the post, he temporarily registered the same username [Voice of Justice] as the original post and did not indicate the source or mark it as a repost.

Outcome

Police: Forwarding Was Not Original Content, Changed Criminal Detention to Administrative Detention

Southern Metropolis Daily reported that according to the criminal detention notice issued by the Chancheng Branch of the Foshan Public Security Bureau, the police determined that Shang Lai Cheng was suspected of making false accusations. However, at around 6 p.m. last night, the message from the Chancheng Branch of the Foshan Public Security Bureau stated that since Shang Lai Cheng illegally posted false information but was not the original author and the crime was minor, the criminal detention has been changed to an administrative detention of 10 days.

According to this police source, the police discovered the post through online monitoring in the morning of the 17th, immediately deleted the post, and verified its content, confirming that the content did not exist. [He fabricated facts, spread false information, and in order to evade police investigation, he asked someone to change the IP address, which is suspected of being illegal.]

The police also investigated and found that Shang Lai Cheng was not the original author of the post. [In order to attract attention, he posted false information, with minor illegal circumstances, and the decision has now been changed to an administrative detention of 10 days.] Accordingly, Shang Lai Cheng could be free that evening, but the police source said that it was already late that day, so he should be able to leave the next day.

The police also stated that the original poster is still under investigation.

Understanding

[Haven't Named Anyone, How Can There Be Accusation?]

Legal Experts Believe That This Case's Online Post Does Not Contain Any Personal Real Names, Thus Does Not Constitute the Crime of Making False Accusations

Southern Metropolis Daily reports that considering the absence of any real-name information in the online post and the fact that the alleged prostitution behavior does not constitute a crime, multiple legal experts believe that according to criminal law, [Qin Shi Huangyue]'s actions in this case do not constitute the crime of making false accusations. Even if it is confirmed that the content of the post is untrue, public power agencies should not arbitrarily impose criminal penalties but should present evidence to refute the allegations head-on.

Nor Constitutes Defamation

Guangzhou public interest lawyer Liao Jianxun pointed out that according to the criminal law, the crime of making false accusations must involve fabricating certain criminal facts about others. If the fabricated facts are not aimed at subjecting others to criminal responsibility, then it does not constitute this crime. [Judging from the existing circumstances of this case, prostitution is not a criminal act, and releasing a prostitute does not constitute a crime either. At most, one might face public security penalties, party disciplinary actions, or administrative punishments.]

Liao Jianxun further pointed out that in terms of objectivity, the crime of making false accusations also involves reporting fabricated criminal facts to state organs or relevant units. Posting online is clearly different from such reporting behavior, and reporting is generally targeted at specific individuals. [If no names are mentioned, how can there be any so-called reporting?]

A well-known legal scholar within the province analyzed that the crime of making false accusations falls under the category of [no complaint, no action], requiring a report from the victim; otherwise, the public security organs have no authority to intervene proactively. From the available information, it is unclear which victim reported the matter to the public security organs. [It cannot be an individual because no personal real names appeared in the post; nor should it be the procuratorate or the public security bureau, as they are merely supervisory objects here.]

In recent years, cases where ordinary netizens were arrested or held accountable for posting online have been frequently seen across the country, and most have been categorized by the police as defamation. Renowned criminal defense lawyer Zhou Ze, who once represented the [Weinan Book Case], pointed out that to constitute the crime of defamation, there must be acts of fabricating and disseminating certain facts, targeting specific individuals, and the circumstances must be particularly serious. [(In this case), the online post did not mention any names specifically, and based solely on the content, it is impossible to determine who the victim is, thus it naturally cannot constitute defamation. State organs cannot possibly become victims of defamation crimes.]

Can Be Considered Citizen Supervision

The aforementioned well-known legal scholar believes that regardless of the content or tone of the post, the posting behavior in this case can be regarded as citizen supervision. [The Constitution grants all citizens the right to supervise state organs. If exercising the right to supervision requires absolute correctness, then the right to supervision would lose its meaning.] He believes that if it is considered that the online post reflects false information, public power agencies should present conclusive evidence to refute the alleged facts and actively respond to more inquiries from the public.

Zhou Ze also proposed that morally, posters should verify the authenticity of the information. If it is subsequently proven that the information is false but does not harm specific individuals or public interests and does not cause serious consequences, it is generally a matter of moral condemnation. Even if public power intervenes, it can only be criticism, education, or even admonishment, and criminal penalties must not be abused. [In addition to the objective consequences caused, the subjective motives of the poster must be considered more.] Zhou Ze believes that often, the release of false information occurs due to information asymmetry.

Moreover, legal experts pointed out obvious procedural flaws in this case. Specifically, the Zhumiao Police Station of Chancheng District involved in the online post served as the investigative body in this case. If a crime is suspected, the approval for arrest must be sought, and the Chancheng District Procuratorate involved in the online post will serve as the review body or even the prosecution body. [There is no need to elaborate on the conflict of interest here; the investigating body should automatically recuse itself without waiting for the parties to apply for recusal.]

Link

Crime of False Accusation Refers to deliberately fabricating facts to falsely accuse others with the intention of subjecting them to criminal prosecution, and the circumstances are serious. Objectively, this crime manifests as fabricating facts about others committing crimes, reporting them to state organs or relevant units, or taking other methods sufficient to trigger investigations by judicial organs.

Crime of Defamation Refers to deliberately fabricating and disseminating fictitious facts, sufficient to damage another person's personality and destroy their reputation, and the circumstances are serious. Objectively, this crime manifests as the perpetrator carrying out acts of fabricating and disseminating certain fictitious facts, sufficient to damage another person's personality and reputation, and the circumstances are serious. The target of the crime is a natural person.
