The Voice of the CPPCC: How should Zhongying Street reposition itself in a new era of development, and what does its future hold? These questions have drawn the attention of the Shenzhen Committee of the Revolutionary Committee of the Kuomintang (RCCK). At this year's "Two Sessions" in Shenzhen, the RCCK proposed a suggestion titled "Proposal on Emphasizing the Reform and Development of 'Zhongying Street'". The committee members suggested lowering the threshold for people entering the street, strengthening the management of duty-free goods, enhancing cooperation with Hong Kong, and jointly planning the development of Zhongying Street.

Vigorously Developing Cultural Tourism Industry

The RCCK pointed out that Zhongying Street, being less than 0.5 kilometers long and only 7 meters wide, is tasked with multiple functions such as history, commerce, tourism, and culture, which is clearly too demanding. Due to historical reasons, there are only two channels for tourists to enter and exit the checkpoint, without any sunshade or rainproof facilities for public convenience. The time spent at the checkpoint is long, and visitors need to apply for a pass, pay fees, and go through cumbersome procedures. Moreover, the current Sha Tou Jiao Port has already become severely saturated, making it difficult to maintain order at the checkpoint, thus complicating entry and exit.

In response to this, the committee members suggested leveraging Zhongying Street's traditional role as a symbol of Shenzhen-Hong Kong cooperation, expanding the influence of Zhongying Street, using the Sha Tou Jiao area as a buffer zone for border defense and commercial activities, and providing space and policy direction for commercial activities. Developing a Sha Tou Jiao commercial buffer zone centered around Zhongying Street not only provides greater room for commercial development but also uses the Sha Tou Jiao area as a buffer, moving the port inspection function outward, expanding the scale of the port, enabling large-scale, high-quality tourism and commercial activities, and driving the popularity of commercial activities in the Hong Kong side of the Sha Tou Jiao area.

Secondly, they suggested integrating Hong Kong and Yantian's surrounding logistics resources (Yantian Port), tourism resources (Eastern Overseas Chinese Town, Sha Tou Jiao Port region), cultural resources (Hakka ancestral traditions), service industry resources, transforming from a single commercial function to a complete six-element tourism function — eating, lodging, touring, shopping, entertainment, and transportation themes — using tourism to drive regional commercial activities. On one hand, this can not only vigorously protect the traditional national culture within Zhongying Street, building it into a "living museum" continuously innovating, but also further explore cultural connotations, using culture to drive tourism, tourism to drive commerce, catering, accommodation, and other service industries, achieving coordinated industrial development. On the other hand, improving the quality of the population within Zhongying Street, ensuring subsequent development, developing it into an open thematic tourist district with historical and cultural connotations.

Strengthening the Management of Duty-Free Goods

For the commercial development of Zhongying Street, increasing popularity is a prerequisite. For Shenzhen residents who can now apply for multiple trips to Hong Kong under the Individual Visit Scheme, the threshold of Zhongying Street undoubtedly puts it at a competitive disadvantage. The committee members pointed out that if the threshold for entering the street could be lowered and combined with the multiple-entry visa of the Home Return Permit, it would greatly attract tourists from Shenzhen and even Guangdong Province. Zhongying Street can be more open, expanding the scope of tourist shopping, allowing tourists from all over the country to visit Zhongying Street.

Moreover, while mainland tourists can shop in the New Territories' Sha Tou Jiao area, Hong Kong residents can also enter the mainland via Zhongying Street. Lowering the threshold for entering the street must simultaneously strengthen the management of duty-free goods; otherwise, it will inevitably lead to a further rampant increase in "water guests." They also proposed that the Hong Kong side of Zhongying Street focus on checking people, while the mainland side focuses on checking goods. "IoT technology can be used to enhance the management of duty-free goods in Zhongying Street, thereby preventing tax evasion of excess goods and the clearance of non-Zhongying Street goods requiring customs declaration." Additionally, measures such as separate cargo and passenger clearance and collecting duty-free goods outside the checkpoint can be adopted.

Incorporating into the High-Level Consultation Mechanism of Guangdong-Hong Kong

The development of Zhongying Street cannot be separated from Guangdong-Hong Kong tourism cooperation and needs to be incorporated into the high-level consultation mechanism of Guangdong-Hong Kong, striving for joint development and construction by Shenzhen and Hong Kong. The committee members believe that the development and reform of Zhongying Street should be led by the district government, gathering various forces to formulate reform plans, reporting to the Shenzhen Municipal Government, and ultimately forming a mature plan. They proposed that plans could be submitted to Guangdong Province in the name of Shenzhen City and Yantian District, seeking greater support, and following the Qianhai model to cooperate with Hong Kong in developing Zhongying Street.

They pointed out that the Sha Tou Jiao border area should relax restrictions on "uphill and downhill" tourism in some areas and waters under new border policies, fully utilizing these policies, doing more and talking less in ambiguous areas, acting first and speaking later. Through local, governmental, and civilian channels, pushing forward separately, gradually gaining policy support. In terms of strictly governing commercial order and rebuilding a good image, deep cooperation between Shenzhen and Hong Kong is also needed, strengthening collaboration, sharing information, and jointly managing. This is the foundation for attracting tourists, ensuring that travelers eat safely and shop confidently.
