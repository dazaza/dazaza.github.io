Rational stop-loss in stock trading

by guolian1798 on 2011-07-12 18:39:15

What is known as stop loss, is cutting your losses. To survive in the stock market, you need to learn how to cut your losses. When the trend goes downward, only those who know how to stop their losses will not be trapped deeply and will not let the losses expand. Since the day the stock market was established, the concept of stop loss has existed. On the surface, it doesn't seem difficult to stop losses in the stock market. You just need to determine if the market conditions are wrong, then set a stop loss price. However, many people can't do this, leading them to make mistakes repeatedly and eventually lose everything. In fact, the reason why people don't know how to stop their losses is partly due to technical factors, but the fundamental reason lies in psychological factors.

Many investors believe that if they don't stop their losses, the number of stocks remains unchanged, and the stock price will rise again in the future. Once they stop their losses, the losses on the stocks become a reality. Therefore, even though they know that the stock price will continue to fall, they are unwilling to stop their losses and exit. In fact, does stopping losses mean losing money? Does not stopping losses mean not losing money? Let's take an example. If you have a sum of equivalent cash in your hand, would you rather use it to buy a falling stock or a rising one? That is to say, if the stocks in your hand have already been wrongly traded, you might as well stop your losses immediately, convert the stocks into cash, and then look for a stock with an upward trend. This way, the previous losses can be quickly recovered. But if you know the stock trade was wrong and still hesitate to sell off, by the time the stock price falls drastically, it may be too late to regret.

Since there are no absolute things in the stock market, sometimes after stopping losses, the stock price does not fall but rises instead. This gives some people a false impression, thinking that such lucky events will also happen to them. Therefore, after the stock market trend turns bad, they watch the stock price continue to fall, but they are still unwilling to cut their losses, waiting for a miracle to happen. In fact, this idea is wrong. After the downward trend forms, very few stocks will rise instead of falling. The vast majority of individual stocks will continue to fall. Chasing extremely small probabilities while taking huge risks to bet against trends is both laughable and pitiful. Some investors know that a downward trend has formed, and they have thought about stopping their losses. They keep saying they will stop their losses, but in action, they delay again and again. In the end, being deeply trapped is inevitable.

In the stock market, there is also a common situation where when their own stocks abnormally fall, some investors think about stopping their losses and escaping. But when the whole market forms a downward trend, they exhibit a collective unconscious state: everyone is trapped, so it doesn't matter if they are trapped too. At this point, they inquire everywhere about how much others have lost and how much they are trapped, seeking a bit of psychological balance, and start hoping for a rebound without anyone willing to cut their losses and exit. This is a typical case of collective unconscious inaction. As such, the history of losing money will continue to repeat itself.

From this, we can see that although everyone understands the importance of stop losses and the operation is very simple, knowing and operating are two different things, and there are very few investors who can strictly enforce this operational discipline.
