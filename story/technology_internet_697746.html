JIAYAN Management Interpretation of Financial Statements: Wireless Registered Users Reach 2 Million

by bingo123 on 2012-03-08 17:24:26

Sina Tech News, March 6th - CenturyMatch (Nasdaq: DATE) has announced its unaudited financial results for the fourth quarter and full year ended December 31, 2011. The report shows that CenturyMatch's net revenue reached RMB 88.6 million (USD 14.1 million), representing a year-over-year increase of 53.6%; net loss was RMB 1.2 million (USD 0.19 million), compared to a net profit of RMB 10 million in the same period last year.

Following the release of the earnings report, CEO Ms. Haiyan Gong and CFO Mr. Shangxiu Gu attended an analyst conference call to interpret the quarterly results and answer analysts' questions.

Below are the highlights from the Q&A session:

Stifel Nicolaus Analyst George Ask: What is your advertising strategy? Is it mainly online or offline advertising?

Haiyan Gong: Our advertising strategy focuses on online channels with offline as supplementary. Meanwhile, we will strengthen our brand communication efforts. This year, we have a major strategy which is to increase investment in wireless internet.

Barclays Capital Analyst Alicia Yap: New registered users grew faster in the fourth quarter of last year, but expectations for the first quarter are relatively lower. Can you explain why growth is slowing down?

Haiyan Gong: The faster registration growth in the fourth quarter of last year was due to the significant amount of advertising we did during that quarter. The relatively low forecast for the first quarter of this year is because January is typically the lowest point of the year, and it accounts for one-third of the first quarter. Looking at the data for February and March, they are good, primarily pulled down by January.

Alicia Yap: How about the competitive landscape? Was there a higher user churn rate in the fourth quarter, and were users moving to competitors?

Haiyan Gong: The decline in active users in the fourth quarter last year was mainly due to seasonal reasons. The registration user data for the first quarter of this year is very good. In our view, although the competitive environment is more complex, there is mutual dilution and influence among competitors. As the market leader, CenturyMatch's position remains unshaken. The gap between us and our nearest competitor is actually widening.

Citigroup Analyst Jack Li: How is the distribution of your user base? What is the breakdown between PC platform and mobile platform users?

Haiyan Gong: We began focusing on wireless internet in July last year, and within half a year, we have effectively rebuilt another CenturyMatch. In terms of growth in the wireless segment, it took us three years to register 1 million users when CenturyMatch was first established. By the end of the fourth quarter last year, we already had 2 million registered users via wireless, and this number continues to grow rapidly, which is a very impressive result.

There is no significant difference between PC and wireless users essentially. I believe their characteristics are basically similar. Currently, the main difference is that making payments on mobile devices is still relatively difficult for users on wireless platforms. However, as technology upgrades and mobile payment experiences improve, there won't be much distinction between these two platforms.

One noticeable difference is the severe imbalance in gender ratio on the wireless platform. For CenturyMatch, both wireless and PC users share a common database, meaning wireless users can also search for internet users. Therefore, the user experience for wireless users, especially male users, is much better compared to other competitors entering from the wireless side.
