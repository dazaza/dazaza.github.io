Record your own thoughts by yourself.

by lk0gamef on 2009-07-18 00:07:03

Sichuan, my second hometown, has witnessed my growth. Here, I have come to know Sichuan, this "ancient city". A thousand years of history have made this land of Sichuan so beautiful and magical. But I also have my own opinions. This so-called leisure capital has made the young people today so realistic and their thinking so open that it is difficult for me, born in an ordinary family, to understand what I should adapt to in the social circle here. It's not that I think too highly of myself, nor that I feel complacent. But as someone born into a teacher's family, how can I adhere to my own thoughts, not betray the hard work of my parents' education, and more importantly, not violate my own beliefs?

To be honest, young people all have a kind of vitality, a fighting spirit, and are full of passion and desire for the future. It is precisely because of this that our imagination is rich. If we say that human nature is bad, then it might as well be said that the prevailing风气of this society is like this, which makes young people easily confused and lost ...

Everyone has the experience of growing up, but how many truly understand the meaning of their own growth? I don't know, maybe this is also a puzzle. Everyone has their own imagination, insight, comprehension and other abilities. Facing this colorful world, young people always want to live more freely and comfortably. There is a sentence that shouldn't be said (which is to let oneself go too far). How many people can give themselves a satisfactory answer when they are young? How can parents see you as someone who can take care of the family? How can your wife and children take you as their support? Faced with these series of problems, what do we young people think? I think, no matter how society changes, and no matter how humanity may seem to be lost, principles should always remain within ourselves. We feel sad and hurt because we haven't walked the path according to the goals we expected. That is to say, the principle no longer exists, and the only thing left is the negative impact society brings to us. I think we cannot fight against society...
