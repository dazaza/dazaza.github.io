● Huang Juntin ● Leadership expert: Pan Sanyin (associate of the Sleep Center, First Affiliated Hospital of Jinan University, professor) Case study

Recently, there was a patient named Ms. Liu in a newspaper who consulted about her sleep issues. She has been taking sleeping pills for quite some time and always wakes up in the middle of the night, unable to fall back asleep. She asked the doctor whether she should take another sleeping pill.

The doctor's response: Due to modern life stress and various physical and mental factors, insomnia is quite common. For treating insomnia, sedative hypnotics are not the only tool that doctors rely on. Many insomnia patients also seek and consume "sleeping pills." Additionally, the idea that "taking sleeping pills can lead to dependency" is deeply ingrained in many people, causing many patients to refuse medication out of fear, leading to worsening conditions. In fact, the treatment of insomnia is a scientific and rigorous issue. Insomnia patients should take medication correctly under the guidance of sleep specialists and receive standardized treatment.

Insomnia can be both a primary disease and a clinical symptom of other diseases. The key first step in treating insomnia is finding the root cause. There are multiple reasons that can lead to insomnia:

1. Various emotional or mental disorders (depression, anxiety, mania, schizophrenia, etc.), which are relatively common, accounting for about 50%;

2. Various physical illnesses (pain, restless leg syndrome, heart failure, brain damage, etc.), accounting for about 20%;

3. Sleep apnea syndrome (commonly known as snoring), accounting for about 15%;

4. Primary or complex insomnia, accounting for about 15%.

Insomnia is not a simple problem. When insomnia symptoms appear, one cannot assume it is primary insomnia and must not use medication casually. Instead, professional diagnosis and testing are needed to identify the cause, type of insomnia, and assess the severity of insomnia before receiving standardized treatment.

How to take sleeping pills for insomnia?

As mentioned earlier, there are many causes of insomnia such as physical illness or pain, psychiatric diseases like depression, side effects of drugs, circadian rhythm disorders, or obstructive sleep apnea syndrome. Therefore, the best method for treating insomnia is to find and treat the underlying cause, i.e., causal treatment. For example, if the cause of sleep disturbance or insomnia is found to be depression, then using antidepressant medications would be the best choice. Similarly, if the reason preventing the patient from falling asleep is arthritis-induced pain, then the best treatment drug would be a painkiller.

Currently, sedative-hypnotic drugs are an important clinical means for treating insomnia. Clinically, sedative-hypnotic drugs should be chosen based on different forms of insomnia:

① Difficulty falling asleep. Use drugs that induce sleep quickly, such as zolpidem and zaleplon, which have short half-lives.

② Waking up at night, light sleep. Choose hypnotic drugs that can extend sleep time, such as ramelteon.

③ Early waking. Can choose medium- or long-acting sedative-hypnotic drugs. Early waking is often due to depression-caused insomnia, so while treating insomnia, attention should be paid to treating the primary disease.

④ Insomnia caused by sleep apnea. Emphasize the treatment of sleep apnea, which can be treated with CPAP or surgery.

⑤ If the patient’s sleep disorder is accompanied by anxiety or depression, anti-anxiety or antidepressant drugs should be used for treatment.

⑥ If the patient's sleep disorder is caused by mental abnormalities, antipsychotic drugs should be used for treatment. Combination therapy with sedative-hypnotic drugs may be necessary when required.

Can you add more sleeping pills if you wake up in the middle of the night?

All sedative-hypnotic drugs have similar effects on insomnia symptoms, but choosing the right drug clinically is very important. Some patients initially respond well to a certain sedative-hypnotic drug, but after some time, the effect diminishes or even disappears, as seen in Mr. Liu's case. In such situations, the clinician should reassess the patient's condition and adjust the treatment plan accordingly.

Mr. Liu recently wakes up in the middle of the night and cannot fall back asleep. Possible reasons include:

1. Concurrent other diseases:

① Most commonly, depressive or anxiety disorders. Acute insomnia is one of the causes of depressive or anxiety disorders. If primary insomnia is not treated early and properly, it may lead to mood disorders such as depression or anxiety. Conversely, depressive or anxiety disorders can cause secondary insomnia symptoms such as daytime awakening, early waking, and difficulty falling asleep.

② Concurrent sleep apnea hypopnea syndrome (SAHS). When SAHS occurs or worsens, symptoms such as nocturnal breath holding, difficulty breathing, inability to fall back asleep after waking, difficulty waking up after falling asleep, and fatigue during the day may occur. If the insomnia patient has a family history of snoring or snored as a child, or is obese, or has a short neck, or has recently gained weight, combined with insomnia symptoms, SAHS should be highly suspected.

③ Various physical illnesses. Chronic insomnia can lead to decreased physical fitness, endocrine imbalance, and impaired bodily functions, making patients prone to various physical illnesses. Poorly managed sleep can exacerbate existing physical illnesses, creating a vicious cycle.

2. Drug tolerance. Long-term use of a certain sedative-hypnotic drug, especially benzodiazepines, particularly when used improperly, may lead to drug tolerance.

Ms. Liu should come to a sleep specialist for re-evaluation and examination to clarify her condition. If diagnosed with concurrent other diseases, comprehensive treatment targeting the specific disease (e.g., adding anti-anxiety treatment for anxiety disorders and adjusting the dosage and type of sedative-hypnotic drugs accordingly) is necessary. If drug tolerance has occurred, adjustments can be made as follows:

① Change medication. Gradually reduce the original drug while switching to another suitable drug for the patient.

② Add sedative-hypnotic drugs. Only applicable when using short-acting drugs like zaleplon or zolpidem. Different drugs have different half-lives, meaning the time it takes for the drug to metabolize and clear from the body varies. For instance, zolpidem has a half-life of 1.5-4.5 hours; for slow metabolizers, it takes 4.5 hours for the drug to wear off. If the patient needs to get up at 7 p.m. the next day, they should not take additional zolpidem after 2:30 a.m., otherwise, they may feel drowsy the next morning due to the drug not being fully metabolized. Therefore, if considering adding medication during daytime awakenings, patients must clearly understand the half-life of the drug they are taking and determine if it is appropriate to add more after a certain time.

③ Increase bedtime medication dosage. If the patient's current dosage is low, they can choose to increase the dose before bedtime. However, if the patient is already taking the maximum safe dosage, further increases are not recommended.

④ Add another drug. Clinical doctors can strictly follow indications to use two or more drugs in combination after scientifically evaluating the patient's condition.

Are you afraid of side effects or addiction from sleeping pills?

After reading about Ms. Liu's medication adjustment methods, many people might wonder: how much side effects could there be from taking so many drugs? What if I become addicted? In response to some people's outdated views and concerns, sleep medicine experts strongly advocate: seek correct medical advice, follow standardized treatment, and take sleeping pills with peace of mind!

"All medicines have side effects," this is true. Any medication (even vitamin supplements) has certain side effects. However, having side effects is not scary as long as these side effects are minor and within the range that our bodies can tolerate. New-generation sedative-hypnotic drugs, mainly benzodiazepines (like diazepam), if misused, could potentially lead to drug tolerance or dependence. Abrupt cessation can make insomnia rebound worse. However, the toxic side effects of these drugs on the human body are not significant. With advancements in geriatric medicine, many new types of sedative-hypnotic drugs have been introduced and applied clinically. These drugs are mostly non-benzodiazepine class, with fewer side effects, generally do not cause dependence, and are safer, bringing good news to many insomnia patients.

Here, there is one issue that insomnia patients need to pay high attention to: not taking medication for insomnia can be more harmful to physical health than taking medication! Sleep is a basic physiological need for humans. Lack of sufficient rest due to insomnia can lead to endocrine imbalance, physical function damage, weight loss, daytime fatigue, and anxiety or depressive disorders affecting work and life. Please manage your sleep scientifically!

Scientific management of sleep is very important. Good sleep habits can effectively prevent many sleep disorders. Insomnia patients forming good sleep habits will help with early recovery. Here are a few points to note for managing sleep scientifically:

① Maintain regular routines. Avoid irregular schedules, staying up late sometimes and oversleeping others.

② Exercise appropriately. Moderate exercise in the afternoon or evening is beneficial, but exercising before bed can affect sleep. Morning vigorous exercise can make you feel tired and sleepy during the day.

③ Healthy diet. Balanced nutrition is essential. Avoid caffeine, tea, and other stimulants, especially in the evening or before bed.

④ Correct attitude towards sleep. Do not overly focus on or worry about sleep. Occasional one or two nights of insomnia or poor sleep quality does not require excessive concern. Consult professional doctors to understand sleep knowledge.

⑤ Seek medical help early if problems arise. Do not delay seeking treatment! Many sleep issues can be quickly resolved if addressed early.
