Synopsis:

Main Cast: Ryu Kasai / Masaki Okada / Fumiyo Kohinata / Yuriko Takagi / Atsuro Watabe / Yoshinori Okada / Kyoko Suzuki

Izumi (played by Ryu Kasai) and Haru (played by Masaki Okada) are brothers two years apart in age. In a high school campus covered in cherry blossoms, Haru takes a baseball bat from Izumi and says, "Let's go!" "Where?" "To teach them a lesson." Some boys were planning to attack a conceited girl in the class. The screams of a girl came from the sports warehouse, and Haru rushed in and drove them away. But when the girl thanked him, he tapped her on the head and coldly said, "I didn't come here to save you." Izumi watched this scene and was stunned.

Seven years later, the brothers return home on their mother Rieko's (played by Kyoko Suzuki) memorial day and sit down to eat with their father Masashi (played by Fumiyo Kohinata). Izumi has become a graduate student studying genetics at university; Haru has entered society and is engaged in erasing graffiti. Upon hearing that his father had been hospitalized for food poisoning, Izumi returned home to visit him. Masashi calmly told him that he had cancer. Izumi was worried, but Haru said, "Shouldn't serious things be said straightforwardly?" This relieved the heavy atmosphere.

A series of arson cases occurred in Sendai City. Haru noticed that the fires always happened near where he erased graffiti. From the photos he took, the words "God", "Can", "Talk", "Ants", "go to", "America" were written on the walls, and Haru believed this was a message left by the arsonist. He sought Izumi's advice on the matter. He pulled the skeptical Izumi out to ambush the culprit at night, but another fire broke out nearby while they were separated.

What is the relationship between the arson cases and the graffiti? Izumi is racking his brains. His friend Yamauchi (played by Yoshinori Okada) tells him that Goto (played by Atsuro Watabe), who is related to a case twenty-four years ago, has returned here.

One day, Masashi called his sons back home and revealed a family secret that had been hidden for many years.

Haru discovered new graffiti and sought Izumi's help, vowing to catch the criminal this time. An irritated Izumi said he had no time for such trivial matters. He ran off and met the mysterious beauty Natsuko (played by Yuriko Takagi) again. Natsuko revealed the astonishing truth about the arson cases...

Behind the Scenes:

Junichi Mori Tackles Isaka Kotaro's Debut Masterpiece

Isaka Kotaro is one of the most popular genre novelists in Japan today and also one of the most favored writers by the film industry. Starting with "Happy Gangsters" in 2006, his novels have been adapted into films one after another, and "Gravity Clown" is the fifth movie adapted from Isaka's works. By last February, the novel had sold about 500,000 copies. After the news of the movie adaptation spread, sales doubled within a year, reaching 1.19 million copies currently.

Among Isaka's numerous works, the 2003 published "Gravity Clown" holds a unique significance and can be considered a "watershed" work in his writing career. With this book, he was nominated for the Akutagawa Prize and the Booksellers Award for the first time, drawing widespread attention from critics and significantly increasing his popularity among ordinary readers. The story narrates the tale of two brothers with vastly different personalities who grew up in the warmth of their parents' love. A series of consecutive arson cases unraveled a family secret buried for twenty-four years.

Isaka stated, "For me, 'Gravity Clown' is the most passionate work. However, I had a concern that once it was adapted into a film, it would become an ordinary family story. But there's no need to worry anymore; this movie is excellent."

The film version, affirmed by the original author, is directed by Junichi Mori. Mori had previously directed numerous advertisements and PVs for famous artists. In 2000, his debut feature film "Stay With Me" won the SUNDANCE·NHK International Filmmaker Award, bringing him instant fame.

Mori expressed that Isaka conveys many heavy themes in "Gravity Clown," but reading the novel leaves one with a light feeling. He particularly appreciates this lightness, which allows readers to resonate with the incredible story and characters. Mori desires to narrate people's lives through cinematic language, "Life isn't just happiness; there's always sadness. I want to make a movie with both light and darkness, and many parts of this novel resonated with me. I deeply believe that without light and darkness, humans cannot move forward."

Acting Veteran Ryu Kasai Collaborates with New Idol Masaki Okada

Ryu Kasai and Masaki Okada portray the two brothers in the Ogino family.

Ryu Kasai, playing the elder brother Izumi, has collaborated with many renowned directors such as Yukisada Isao, Kiyoshi Kurosawa, and Isshin Inudo, making him one of the leading young actors active in the Japanese film industry today. His lead role in the film "Even If This Were Not My Doing" garnered numerous awards in 2007, topping the annual Movie旬best ten list.

Masaki Okada, playing the younger brother Haru, is a tall and handsome emerging idol. In 2006, Okada was selected through five rounds to play an important role in the film "Natural Structure," gaining recognition for his outstanding performance. Following this, he played major roles in the hit TV dramas "Boys Over Flowers" and "Classroom of the Sea," significantly boosting his popularity. Okada is one of the hottest new stars in the entertainment industry, with five films he starred in being released consecutively from winter 2008 to 2009.

The supporting cast of the film is also impressive, including renowned actors such as Fumiyo Kohinata, Kyoko Suzuki, and Yuriko Takagi.

Fun Facts:

- Before getting his role, Masaki Okada coincidentally read the original novel. Ryu Kasai deliberately avoided reading the novel to avoid affecting his performance.

- The cherry blossom-blossomed campus is an important scene in the film and was the primary consideration in choosing the exterior location. The staff visited many schools, finding many beautiful cherry blossoms, but couldn't find any cherry trees extending branches towards the teaching buildings. As filming approached, someone suggested using CG to handle the cherry blossoms. Director Junichi Mori wanted to capture a "living" town and did not want to use artificial CG for the opening scenes, so he rejected the suggestion and continued searching. Eventually, they found a high school in Miyagi Prefecture that perfectly matched their vision.

- During filming, Ryu Kasai and Masaki Okada quickly became familiar with each other. They often discussed movies together and even went out to eat, like real brothers. Okada expressed that Kasai is an excellent older brother. Kasai revealed that if his own younger brother heard this praise, he would likely shake his head in disagreement.

- Director Junichi Mori revealed that during filming in Sendai, Yuriko Takagi frequently spent her free time alone in manga cafes.
