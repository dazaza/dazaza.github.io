Indifferent officials are held criminally liable for subordinates' embezzlement

by zxyasdg40 on 2011-07-28 16:24:26

Our reporter (Wang Qiushi) - Due to loopholes in the financial system of her department, a cashier from the Planning and Finance Department of the Tongzhou District Health Bureau was able to embezzle public funds over a period of four years, accumulating a total amount close to 2 million yuan. Yesterday, it was reported that after the cashier was sentenced, the head of the department, Mr. Yang, was also sentenced by the Tongzhou Court for dereliction of duty, receiving a six-month detention sentence, suspended for six months. According to reports, this is the first time that a court in our city has held a leader criminally responsible for neglecting subordinates' official crimes.

Li Minglu, aged 24, was originally a cashier in the Planning and Finance Department of the Tongzhou District Health Bureau. To please her boyfriend Zhao Kai, she began taking money from the unit starting in 2004, giving it to her boyfriend for various expenses. When someone in the unit needed reimbursement, she would overdraw amounts using cash checks, withdrawing extra cash from the unit's basic account, or use methods such as cash check withdrawals without recording them, or not recording received payments. According to the financial system implemented by the Health Bureau, the checks and seals were all managed by her alone, so her actions went undetected. Encouraged by Zhao Kai with the idea of "not taking it would be foolish," the two used public funds to buy luxury goods, eat out, gamble, and indulge in extravagant spending. In December 2006, Li Minglu broke up with Zhao Kai and started dating a new boyfriend, Mr. Li. She continued to embezzle public funds to enjoy lavish dining, entertainment, and purchasing expensive items with her new boyfriend. It wasn't until last July that, due to the growing deficit and unbearable pressure, Li Minglu confessed to her family, and under their persuasion, turned herself in. By the time the case was uncovered, she had embezzled over 1.9 million yuan, most of which was spent extravagantly.

In August this year, the court sentenced Li Minglu to 13 years and 6 months in prison for embezzlement, and Zhao Kai to 11 years in prison. After the case broke out, the prosecution intervened and criminally detained Yang on January 20th this year. The prosecution charged that from 2005 to last July, during his tenure as deputy director and then director of the Planning and Finance Department of the Tongzhou District Health Bureau, Yang failed to properly perform his duties, violating financial laws, regulations, and management systems by entrusting all bank reserved stamps, including checks and payment-related financial seals and personal name seals, to Li Minglu alone. Moreover, he did not conscientiously fulfill his supervisory responsibilities, such as checking and reconciling accounts, allowing Li Minglu to withdraw cash from the opening bank without any approval procedures, resulting in her embezzling over 1.9 million yuan over more than four years, causing direct economic losses of over 1.7 million yuan to public property.

After reviewing the case, the court determined that as a state official, Yang neglected his duties, causing significant loss to public property, constituting the crime of dereliction of duty. Given his confession and remorse during the trial, he was legally given a mitigated punishment.
