How to get pregnant

by h0945910 on 2009-11-27 14:40:25

One morning a few months later, I, who was lonely but fulfilled, couldn't resist the temptation of an online friend and ended up meeting her in person. What followed was as natural as water flowing downhill - we checked into a room and went to bed. Even as I lay on the bed at home, I thought it was all a dream! Who would have known that a few days later, I suddenly started feeling nauseous in the morning, had a loss of appetite, yet craved sour foods. My mind raced with thoughts: Could I be pregnant? I wanted to cry but no tears came. I quickly examined my stomach, and seeing it made me even more tense - my once flat stomach was now slightly protruding. Oh, I thought, I must be showing signs of pregnancy. But I didn't dare consult anyone else, so I turned to the internet and sought advice from a close older sister.

She had successfully given birth before! Me: Sister, I might be pregnant. Her: Ah, congratulations to you! Me: 5555 (crying), but I'm not married. Her: Ah? Then you should get married quickly. Me: That person is just an online friend, and I can't find them anymore. Her: Sigh, young people these days don't know how to cherish themselves. Her: How are you feeling now? Me: Nausea, craving sour foods, and it seems like my stomach has grown too. Her: You should quickly buy a pregnancy test stick and check. They're available at pharmacies.

I immediately rushed out to find a secluded pharmacy and bought the best pregnancy test stick available. In the bathroom at work, I followed the instructions and peed on the stick, but there was no reaction. At that moment, I almost fainted. How could there be no response at all? I quickly went online and asked my sister again: Why isn't the famous test strip showing anything? Sister: Maybe you have a special physique. You should go to the hospital for a check-up. Your health is important.

I became even more panicked. How strange! One-night stands can make your belly grow, and the accuracy rate is incredibly high. I sprinted to the best hospital, registered, waited patiently in line. Finally, it was my turn. I asked: "Doctor, could I be pregnant?" The doctor laughed and said: "Get out of here! You're a man, how could you possibly be pregnant?"

Relieved, I replied: "Then why am I feeling nauseous?" The doctor explained: "It's probably because you've been smoking less." Frustrated, I asked further: "Then why is my stomach getting bigger?" The doctor replied: "That's due to a lack of exercise!" All my worries were instantly relieved.
