Article publisher, source, save this article. Yang Qiang refers to the condition where the penis becomes hard and does not soften, also known as "strong middle disease" or "yang not falling". It describes a condition where the penis easily becomes erect and remains so without decline. The author will now introduce the Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) differential diagnosis and treatment for Yang Qiang disease.

1. Excessive fire ascending Yang Qiang: The penis remains hard, enlarged, painful, with thirst, red face, restlessness, yellowish and scanty urine, glossy tongue without coating, and wiry pulse. Treatment should focus on nourishing yin and reducing fire, guiding fire back to its origin. Prescription: 18 grams each of raw oyster shell (pre-boiled), 15 grams of raw rehmannia, 12 grams each of anemarrhena, phellodendron, moutan bark, cornus fruit, ligustrum, seaweed, and kelp. Decocted in water and taken warm in three doses.

2. Kidney deficiency and liver exuberance Yang Qiang: Dizziness, mental fatigue, dryness and bitterness in mouth, yellowish urine, red tip of tongue, fine rapid pulse. Treatment should focus on nourishing yin and enriching water to control liver yang. Prescription: 30 grams (pre-boiled), 15 grams of raw rehmannia, 12 grams each of anemarrhena, phellodendron, moutan bark, ligustrum, cornus fruit, dried lonicera, white peony, and 6 grams of licorice; decocted in water and taken warm in three doses.

3. Kidney yin deficiency Yang Qiang: If engaging in sexual activity, premature ejaculation occurs, but after ejaculation, the penis softens momentarily before quickly becoming erect again, remaining hard without softening, red tongue with little coating, fine rapid pulse. Treatment should focus on tonifying kidney and enriching water. Prescription: 30 grams each of radix adenosae, 15 grams of cortex lycii, 12 grams each of ligustrum and cornus fruit, 6 grams of licorice, decocted in water and taken warm in three doses.

4. Damp-heat descending Yang Qiang: Long-lasting hardness without softening, swelling and dark coloration of the penis, lower back pain, irritability, insomnia, red tongue with yellow coating, slippery pulse. Treatment should focus on clearing damp-heat and activating blood circulation to resolve stasis. Prescription: 15 grams, 10 grams each of bupleurum, gentian, gardenia, coix seed, alisma, scutellaria, 8 grams each of peach kernel and safflower, 6 grams of licorice. Decocted in water and taken warm in three doses.
