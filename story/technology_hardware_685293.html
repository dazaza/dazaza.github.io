Nutritional Value of Radishes

Besides containing thiocyanates, radishes are rich in vitamin A and vitamin C, and also contain a significant amount of amylase. However, when heated, the vitamin C and enzymes in radishes are easily damaged. Eating them raw effectively avoids the loss of these active ingredients!

Weight Loss Benefits of Eating Raw Radishes

Radishes contain a spicy component called thiocyanate, which has antioxidant properties. Consuming thiocyanate can remove reactive oxygen species from the body, boost metabolism, help you develop an easy-to-lose constitution that resists fat accumulation, and simultaneously provide anti-aging and beauty health benefits!

Speaking of thiocyanate, it isn't simply stored in radishes. When we slice, shred, or chop radishes, cell damage occurs, and thiocyanate is automatically generated. In other words, cutting radishes can produce this substance—how amazing is that!

Here’s another tip for you: after eating raw radishes, you’ll find that the parts near the radish leaves have a sweeter taste, while the tips become increasingly spicy. This indicates that the tips contain more thiocyanate!

Precautions for Eating Raw Radishes

When talking about eating raw radishes, don’t forget that the juice lost when chopping the radishes also contains large amounts of active components. Therefore, when making raw radish weight-loss meals, remember not to waste the juice and make full use of it!

Raw Radish Weight-Loss Recipes

Menu 1: Raw Radish Kombu Seaweed Onion Salad

Ingredients: Raw radish, kombu seaweed, onion (each as needed).

Wash and peel the raw radish and onion, then shred them. Soak the kombu seaweed in water and shred it as well. Then mix all three ingredients together. Depending on personal preference, you can add some corn kernels and enjoy.

Menu 2: Raw Radish Bacon Roll

Ingredients: Radish (6 pieces as needed), perilla leaves (4), bacon (4 slices), olive oil (1 teaspoon), chicken powder (1 teaspoon).

Slice the radish horizontally into thin pieces. Slightly dry-fry the bacon in a non-stick pan, then wrap the perilla leaves inside the bacon, and finally wrap everything with the thin radish slices on the outside layer. Sprinkle black pepper for seasoning before serving.

Menu 3: Perilla Kombu Tea-soaked Raw Radish

Ingredients: Radish (6 cm), perilla powder (a little), kombu tea (a little), nori flakes (a little).

Shred the raw radish. Soak the kombu tea in water, mix it with perilla powder, then pour it over the shredded radish. Don’t add too much; sprinkle some nori flakes on top and enjoy it mixed together.
