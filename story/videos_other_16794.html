I am planning to go on a self-guided tour for May Day, collect some routes for comparison, and hopefully help other friends who are preparing to travel.

"The land of six dynasties' gold and powder, the imperial city of Jinling." Nanjing, with more than 6000 years of civilization history and over 2400 years of city construction history, is known as one of the "Four Great Ancient Capitals of China" alongside Beijing, Xi'an, and Luoyang. Since Sun Quan of Eastern Wu moved the capital to Nanjing in 229 AD, historically there have been ten dynasties that established their capitals here, hence it is called the "Capital of Ten Dynasties."

Nanjing self-guided tour - Nanjing, anciently known as Jinling and abbreviated as Ning, is the provincial capital of Jiangsu. It is located in the lower reaches of the Yangtze River, looking eastward to the sea, reaching Jingchu to the west, bordering Anhui and Zhejiang to the south, and connecting to the Huai and Jiang regions to the north. The current jurisdiction of Nanjing includes six urban districts: Xuanwu, Baixia, Qinhuai, Jianye, Gulou, and Xiaguan; five suburban districts: Yuhuatai, Qixia, Pukou, Dachang, and Jiangning; and four counties: Jiangpu, Liuhe, Lishui, and Gaochun. The total area of the city is 6516 square kilometers, with a total population of 5.2982 million people. The mountains and rivers surrounding Nanjing form a famous scenic area integrated with mountains, water, city, and forest.

Nanjing self-guided tour - Nanjing is one of the four great ancient capitals of China, with a long history. The discovery of the Nanjing ape man skull in Tangshan in the eastern suburbs shows that this place was inhabited by humans 300,000 years ago. In 472 BC, after King Goujian of Yue destroyed Wu, he built Yuecheng in what is now the southwestern corner of Zhonghua Gate, starting the history of Nanjing's city walls, which has continued for two thousand four hundred seventy years. Since the third century AD, successively, Eastern Wu, Eastern Jin, and the Southern Dynasties of Song, Qi, Liang, and Chen (historically referred to as the Six Dynasties), as well as Southern Tang, Ming, Taiping Heavenly Kingdom, and the Republic of China, established their capitals here, leaving behind rich national cultural heritage.

The ancient and enduring cultural heritage, modern civilized economic metropolis, combined with magnificent natural landscapes, constitute Nanjing's unique garden city style.
