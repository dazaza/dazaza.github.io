Seven Approaches to Online Marketing Analytics

by net6612822 on 2009-11-29 19:37:18

Currently, online marketing has become one of the hottest topics in the industry. An increasing number of businesses and individuals are recognizing the necessity of conducting online marketing, and a large number of online marketing service companies have emerged accordingly. For both enterprises or individuals, as well as online marketing service institutions, it is necessary to conduct targeted online marketing analysis before engaging in online marketing. By analyzing online marketing, using data and reality to guide the steps of your online marketing can effectively improve its efficiency. Here, I will briefly introduce seven directions of online marketing analysis. Combining various directions can lead to a relatively objective and accurate online marketing analysis.

1. Domain Analysis: A good website first needs a good domain name. As part of online marketing analysis, it starts with the target object's domain name. The reason why domain analysis is placed at the forefront of online marketing analysis is that the domain plays an important role in online marketing activities. Once a domain is modified after being chosen, it becomes a major undertaking, requiring many related online marketing tasks to be redone. Therefore, when choosing a domain, it is crucial to carefully compare and analyze options. We can conveniently query various domains through the domain query function provided by onlinenic (http://www.onlinenic.com/chinese). This includes two main functions: checking if a domain is already registered and querying domain registration information. It can simultaneously check the registration status of three domain prefixes and various domain suffixes such as .com, .net, .org, .中国, .公司, .网络, .tv, .cc, .info, .com.cn, .net.cn, .org.cn, .cn, .us, .biz, etc. Domain analysis mainly includes two tasks: querying related domains and determining the website domain. By querying related domains such as candidate domains, creative domains, and competitors' domains, you can understand their registration status and registrant information, thereby deciding whether to choose an unregistered domain or purchase a registered domain through domain trading.

2. Website Analysis: A website is the foundation for conducting online marketing. Generally speaking, if online marketing is done well, the website must be relatively ideal. How do we analyze whether a website is ideal? If there are deficiencies, how should they be improved? These questions need to be answered through website analysis. I would like to introduce a good website analysis platform: World Network (http://www.linkwan.com/gb/). This is a professional website that provides website testing and analysis services. Through World Network, you can use various website analysis tools to analyze your website, including network connection speed tests, network path node tracebacks, website response speed tests, webpage characteristic tests, www server probes, webtrends website structure analyses, and more. Website analysis mainly focuses on aspects such as website connectivity, response speed, and internal structure, which are technically demanding. Conducting website analysis allows effective control of the website's operational data, thereby reinforcing or improving it for in-depth engagement in various forms of online marketing.

3. Google Analysis: Google is currently the most authoritative intelligent search engine globally. A website's performance on Google can directly reflect the extent and quality of its online marketing efforts. Querying relevant keywords to find out the website’s ranking on Google and its recognized PageRank value are the two main criteria for Google analysis. Websites that conduct online marketing effectively usually have several related keywords with desirable natural rankings in Google search results. Facts have proven that websites with comprehensive online marketing generally have higher Google PageRank assessment values. If a corporate website has a PageRank of 4 or above, or a comprehensive website has a PageRank of 5 or above, it can be considered that the applied online marketing effect is relatively ideal. By downloading and installing the free toolbar browser tool provided by Google, you can easily view the PageRank value of any website page you browse. Additionally, querying the number of pages indexed by Google and the number of external links to the website are also important reference data for online marketing analysis. Being adept at utilizing Google has become an essential skill for those involved in online marketing analysis.

4. Alexa Analysis: Alexa analysis is one of the important methods for competitor online marketing analysis and online marketing comparative analysis. Alexa (http://www.alexa.com) is a globally renowned website for comprehensive statistical analysis. Its world rankings have become an important reference standard. People from various fields, including online marketers, investors, and SEO professionals, all value a website's performance on Alexa. Alexa analysis mainly includes fluctuations in world rankings and comparisons with similar websites. By examining a website's performance on Alexa rankings and comparing it with similar websites, relatively objective conclusions can be drawn. The advantage of Alexa is that it can provide Alexa data for any website, which is extremely useful for those who need to analyze competitors but lack data. Except for a few websites that engage in malicious Alexa cheating, Alexa has a relatively unified and independent evaluation standard for various websites, making Alexa data referable and becoming one of the popular methods for online marketing analysis today.

5. Keyword Analysis: Keyword analysis is an important content of online marketing analysis. Different websites have different keywords. Keywords are essentially a summary of the core content of online marketing, and all online marketing activities revolve around certain keywords. To conduct online marketing based on a website, keyword analysis must first be performed. Keyword analysis includes two aspects: keyword selection and keyword queries. Keyword selection can follow the principle of "clarifying major and minor categories, grasping the core and focus, and perfecting connotation and extension." For example, for the online marketing manual website (http://www.tomx.com), the major and minor categories could be online marketing and marketing resources, i.e., a marketing resource site under the category of online marketing. For such a website, what are its core and key keywords for online marketing? Undoubtedly, the core keyword is online marketing, and the key keywords are business plans and online marketing consultants, which are closely related to the nature of the website. After determining this direction, we can grasp the connotation and extension of the website's keywords, identifying keywords such as website planning, marketing tutorials, management books, and consulting training. Using this principle, we can determine the website's keywords and easily distinguish between primary and secondary keywords. Querying the ranking of keywords can help you fully understand the online marketing situation under that keyword and identify your competitors. In most cases, simply performing natural ranking queries for keywords on Google and Baidu can allow you to understand the basic online marketing situation of that keyword. This serves as an important reference for keyword selection.

6. Traffic Analysis: Traffic analysis is one of the important methods for measuring the effectiveness of online marketing. By loading website traffic analysis software onto a website or specific content, it can effectively monitor website visits and provide reference data for online marketing decisions. Here, I recommend WWA4.0 - Full Process Statistical Analysis System (http://wwa.hotsales.net), independently developed by Shanghai Huosu Network Information Technology Co., Ltd. This is a practical network software used for website visit statistics and analysis. This system can perform data analysis on the traffic of any page or even the entire site, providing detailed visit reports, including visitor IP addresses, visit times, visit sources, search keywords, search engine sources, etc. Additionally, it offers comparison functions such as best visit source comparisons and best visit keyword comparisons. This allows you to always understand the traffic trends and popularity of pages and make relevant adjustment strategies accordingly. More uniquely, it is clear what proportion of users come via search engines, network real names, or advertising links, allowing you to clearly see the best way to promote your website and provide real and effective analysis data for market decision-making.

7. Advertising Analysis: For large-scale online projects conducting online marketing, online advertising placement is indispensable. Online advertising placement represents a relatively significant investment in online marketing and should be approached cautiously to ensure substantial returns. This requires monitoring and analyzing online advertisements. Typically, most online advertisements recommended by service providers are priced based on time slots and positions. However, this does not allow advertisers to fully grasp the effectiveness of the advertisement placement and can only assess it based on the provider's promotion. Moreover, advertisers want to understand the trends and current state of competitors' online advertising placements to formulate corresponding strategies. In such cases, it is necessary to conduct online advertising analysis through other channels and methods, which is also an important aspect of online marketing analysis. I recommend iAdTracker - Online Advertising Query System (http://www.iadtracker.com), independently developed by Shanghai iResearch Market Consulting Co., Ltd. All data in the iAdTracker database is collected through manual browsing of fixed website channels and web pages, as well as through web robots grabbing fixed website page files, capturing the latest commercial online advertising formats appearing on the website and scientifically inputting them into the database. iAdTracker provides multiple convenient query functions for online advertisers, allowing them to better understand the situation of their competitors. The main functions of iAdTracker include overall plan analysis for advertisers, media count analysis for advertisers, ad creative count analysis for advertisers, ad record count analysis for advertisers, comparison analysis of ad placements for advertisers, cost analysis of ad placements for advertisers, and more. It can comprehensively monitor online advertisements and analyze the advertiser's online advertising placement activities, providing data support and suggestion analysis for advertisers to evaluate the effectiveness of their online advertising placements and improve their online advertising placement decisions.
