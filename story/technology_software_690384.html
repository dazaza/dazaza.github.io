Many media outlets came to the filming location in Xiangshan, enduring temperatures close to 40 degrees Celsius, trying every method possible to sneak a peek at the "Hedong Lion Roar 2" filming site through the cracks.

Now, "Hedong Lion Roar 2" has officially been released, let's take a look at how various stars showcase their different acting styles amidst the beautiful scenery of Xiangshan!

Xiangshan is located in the west of Jingmen City in Hubei Province, facing Dongbao Mountain in the east, forming a famous landscape of Jingmen City. Xiangshan was called Mengshan during the Tang Dynasty; the name "Xiangshan" is said to be in memory of Lu Jiuyuan from the Southern Song Dynasty. Coincidentally, there is another "Xiangshan" in Guixi, Jiangxi today, also named in memory of Lu Jiuyuan. Hence, due to the emergence of Master Lu in the past, we have two Xiangshans today.

"Hedong Lion Roar 2" Filming Location: Xiangshan Film City

The early architecture of the Xiangshan Film City was constructed based on the story content of Jin Yong's novel "The Return of the Condor Heroes." The main attractions such as the majestic Xiangyang City, the mysterious tomb of the living dead, the elegant Guiyun Villa, the mystical Valley of Absolute Feelings, and the lush banyan forest are uniquely styled and creatively designed. Within the Xiangshan Film City, there are picturesque lakes and mountains, pavilions and terraces, ravines and caves, as well as ancient streets from the Song Dynasty, making one involuntarily immerse themselves in the martial arts atmosphere. "The Return of the Condor Heroes" was the first TV drama filmed in Xiangshan Film City. Subsequently, classic martial arts dramas like "The Blood Sword," "The Deer and the Cauldron," and "The Wanderers of Tang" were also filmed here, along with war films like "Jinggang Mountain." Here, you can visit film arenas, attend celebrity meet-and-greets, experience eerie ancient tombs, watch plays at the Yichun Teahouse, or even make a cameo appearance in a county court trial.

"Hedong Lion Roar 2" Filming Location: China Fishing Village

China Fishing Village, the filming location of "Hedong Lion Roar 2," is a national AAAA-level tourist area, one of the most popular tourist areas in the Yangtze River Delta, and one of the top ten leisure and summer retreat destinations in Zhejiang Province. It is the largest original ecological seaside leisure and vacation resort on China's eastern coast, a large-scale leisure seaside tourism destination themed around "Fish Culture Folklore Tour" and "Seaside Leisure Vacation," and has been rated as one of the "Top Ten Summer Retreat Destinations in Zhejiang Province." Divided into two major scenic areas, Sunshine Coast and Fish Mountain Island, as China's largest comprehensive marine cultural leisure and vacation resort, it features Mediterranean-style villas, Caribbean-style entertainment zones, high-quality beaches, authentic and delicious seafood snacks, verdant islands, and abundant forest resources. Whether it's the main building of the theme park, fishing village theme villas, three-mast ancient ships, fisherman stalls, tent villages, or the prominently displayed fishing village wind direction markers, fishing boats, and mast lights, they all comprehensively display the life atmosphere of the fishing area.

"Hedong Lion Roar 2" Filming Location: Shippu Ancient City

Shippu Ancient City, the filming location of "Hedong Lion Roar 2," was built along the mountain, by the sea, and people call it "the city above the port, the mountain within the city." One end connects to the fishing port, while the other hides deep within the mountain valley. The city walls follow the terrain, rising and falling with the hills, and the city gates are constructed according to the shape of the land, controlling the harbor from a higher elevation, which is the main characteristic of the "Maritime Defense Stronghold" of Shippu Ancient City. The old houses are built in tiers, the streets ascend step by step, twisting and turning. Here, you can visit unique venues that blend fishing culture, merchant culture, and maritime defense culture, weaving fish nets and making fishing lamps.

"Hedong Lion Roar 2" Filming Location: Wengcheng

Wengcheng, the filming location of "Hedong Lion Roar 2," is located at the intersection of Wanxing Street, Fujian Street, and Zhong Street. It was originally built during the Ming Dynasty and rebuilt during the Guangxu period of the Qing Dynasty. The ancient city walls are now well-preserved. The newly reconstructed Wengcheng covers an area of 240 square meters, with entrances set as city gates, door lintels placed above, and exits as double-eaved city towers, consisting of a gatehouse. Shippu uses Wengcheng as its symbol, dividing it into inside and outside the city, hence the name Ancient City Shippu. The various venues within the city allow visitors to experience the prosperity of shops in the past and the lively lives of the common people.
