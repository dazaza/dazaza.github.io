myGoya: Online operating system from Germany!

by tttbbb on 2008-11-27 09:49:09

The online operating system (WebOS) has flourished since its inception to make our work easy. The development of the web2.0 project has also prompted WebOS to integrate and provide more services and features for people. What people want from WebOS is no longer a simple network hard drive, online FEED subscription and other traditional features. And in terms of visual effects, the requirements are getting higher and higher. As a result, many of the original WebOS projects in China have become old and yellow. Foreign WebOS, however, keep coming up with new ones. As a result, these WebOS became the focus of Sweet IT.Net. Such as webbr, OOdesk, Desktoptwo, atoolo, jooce, etc.

Today we will introduce WebOS is an online operating system from Germany myGoya!

image

WebOS isn't that mysterious. WebOS is not a replacement for the operating system we have on our computers. WebOS is web-based. Then, it must rely on the real operating system to run. But we still want WebOS to be powerful enough to move us. Click here to charge: What is WebOS?

All features of myGoya are completely web-based and can be used anywhere in the world without downloading any software. myGoya's desktop includes a Linux-like DOCK bar effect that integrates common applications such as file management, contact management, chat, PM tools, share manager, book creator, Flickr client, etc. It is very easy to get started.

image

The main menu of myGoya provides us with more than 20 application services in several categories such as service, communication, media, office and management. All of the apps have a nice MacOS interface and support for virtual desktops, allowing you to have several different desktops to work on.
