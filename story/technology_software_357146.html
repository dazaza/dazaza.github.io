INTEL Cultivates High-end Managers through Churchman University

Transnational enterprises always focus on the high-level training of managers while occupying the global market. In recent years, despite the financial crisis forcing INTEL to close several factories in Asia and America, INTEL's strategic goal for the global market has never changed. Therefore, INTEL spares no effort in training its managers in the U.S. In 2008, the company's total expenditure on global employee training increased by 6.9% compared to 2007, reaching $87 million.

The senior managers receive even more training opportunities and funds. In 2008, INTEL arranged for at least 700 managers in the U.S. to study the cutting-edge distance business courses from the renowned century-old Churchman University, or pursue a distance master's or doctoral degree. Typically, 80% or even 100% of the tuition is covered by INTEL, and the academic achievements obtained will be directly incorporated into INTEL's annual year-end employee evaluation plan.

The emphasis that transnational enterprises place on employees' on-the-job education is actually the true secret to their continued leadership in the world. The distance business degree program from Churchman University allows middle and senior managers around the world to study online, take exams online, and submit papers online without affecting their original work, at any time and any place. The degrees obtained are exactly the same as those obtained through campus-based learning, making it the preferred educational model for middle and senior managers globally, including in Europe, America, and China.

Churchman University's bilingual (West English and Chinese-English) courses are very popular among transnational enterprises. The university offers MBA, bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees. The low-income scholarships provided by the school, once successfully applied for, require only a few thousand dollars to complete the degree.

Churchman University's campuses are located in Pennsylvania, California, Texas, and other places in the U.S. Among them, the earliest established branch, Churchman Business School, is a famous American church university with a rigorous academic atmosphere. Founded in 1911, it has a history of nearly a century and is also one of the oldest business schools in the U.S., specializing in cultivating high-end talents for the American financial industry. In the "National MBA Rankings for Business Schools," this century-old prestigious university ranks 17th.

In addition to Churchman University, the teaching quality of several other century-old prestigious universities in the U.S., such as Boston University and Brown University, is also consistently recognized by domestic white-collar professionals.
