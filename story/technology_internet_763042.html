Adobe Launches Cloud Marketing System, Targets Asia

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 12:41:08

On July 20, according to foreign media reports, Adobe launched its cloud marketing system Adobe Social 3.0 in Singapore, signaling that Adobe will focus on the Asian marketing market.

Adobe Social 3.0 includes a publishing engine that uses historical data to predict potential social reactions to social marketing strategies. The new features help marketers determine the revenue generated by social campaigns and convert social marketing data into more traditional business data. Social 3.0 is also compatible with mobile devices for the first time.

This move by Adobe indicates a greater focus on the Asian market. Brad Rencher, Vice President of Adobe's Digital Marketing Business Unit, believes that the Asian market has growth potential. In the Asian region, Adobe's digital marketing products are popular in the tourism, entertainment, and financial services markets.

S&P Equity analyst Barbara Coffey stated that due to the acquisition of many small U.S. startups, Adobe's digital marketing services have a higher penetration rate in the U.S. market than overseas. Over the past three years, Adobe has acquired digital marketing solutions including Neloane, Omniture, Day Software, Demdex, Auditude, and Efficient Frontier.

A report from the Chief Marketing Officer Council at Citigroup noted that in the Asian market, social media, mobile, and internet penetration rates are extremely high, but digital advertising still lags behind the European and North American markets.

Citigroup analyst Walter Pritchard said, "In terms of digital marketing, the Asian market is somewhat behind the U.S. market."

However, the Asian market has tremendous potential. According to market research company eMarketer, digital advertising spending in the Asia-Pacific region was approximately $33 billion in 2012.

Although Adobe is well-known for design products such as Photoshop and InDesign, the company is increasingly focusing on marketing tools like Adobe Marketing Cloud. In the second quarter of this year, this tool contributed $229.6 million in revenue to Adobe.

Merrill Lynch analyst Jay Vleeschhouwer believes that digital marketing revenue accounts for 22% of Adobe's total revenue, but the scale of Adobe Social is still smaller compared to tools like Analytics and CQ.

Currently, Google, Oracle, IBM, and Salesforce are competing with Adobe in the marketing systems market.

Coffey noted that Google's products are completely free, but Google retains the data. While IBM has made progress, Adobe currently still holds an advantage.

Pritchard believes that compared to Oracle, Adobe focuses heavily on front-end development and maintains a lead in content marketing activities, but falls short in back-end development areas such as marketing activity management. (Edward)
