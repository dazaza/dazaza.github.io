Without saying a word, he ate when it was time to eat and slept when it was time to sleep. He would say thank you when the medical staff shaved him. On the 28th day, they discharged him from the hospital.

Grey Becker expressed such feelings in his comments: It's very difficult for a normal person to prove their normalcy. Perhaps only those who don't try to prove it can be considered truly normal.

Later, many people left messages on the online version of the article.

One message was particularly moving: Those who use certain methods to prove they hold the truth, those who use certain methods to show off their extensive knowledge, and even those who flaunt their wealth, may all be considered crazy, just that they themselves don't realize it! So I think the best way to prove you're normal is not to do too much.

I believe that trying too hard to prove oneself often has the opposite effect.

For example, doing something different to prove you're unique might get you labeled as insane.

To prove how deeply you love your girlfriend, no matter how much you say, it might not work; only time can prove everything.

Life is a process, so take it slow, don't rush things.
