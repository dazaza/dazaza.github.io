In view of this problem, many women consulted experts from Xi'an Gynecology Hospital. What are the adverse reactions after women put a ring? 1. Uterine perforation

The mechanism of uterine perforation caused by putting a ring is not clear. It is generally believed that it may be caused by the size of the uterus or improper operation by the doctor. In addition, when women exercise vigorously, the intrauterine device in the body may cause damage to the uterus due to physical inertia. Unexplained uterine contraction may also squeeze the intrauterine device and cause uterine perforation. 2. Damage to other organs

In rare cases, after the intrauterine device causes uterine perforation, it will enter the abdominal cavity, thus causing displacement of the intrauterine device. The intrauterine device may be displaced to the bladder, pelvic cavity, and mesentery. 3. Excessive menstruation

Some intrauterine devices contain copper ions, and copper ions have cytotoxicity and hemolytic effect, resulting in excessive menstruation, which is a normal phenomenon. 4. Ectopic pregnancy

If the embryo implants in the fallopian tube and does not reach the uterus, the result will be ectopic pregnancy. The principle of contraception of the intrauterine device is to change the environment inside the uterus to cause miscarriage, which only plays a local role in the uterus, so it can only prevent normal pregnancy in the uterus and cannot avoid ectopic pregnancy. 5. Fibrotic changes in the endometrium

Intrauterine devices are essentially foreign bodies in the human body, and foreign bodies in the human body will cause mechanical damage and chronic inflammation as well as fibrotic changes to local tissues. This has already become a common sense in medicine. After wearing a ring for 10-12 years, fibrotic changes will occur in the endometrium. 6. Pregnancy with ring

Pregnancy with ring is also called pregnancy with device. Because the contraceptive success rate of intrauterine devices is lower than oral contraceptives and sterilization surgery, therefore, women should still take preventive measures after wearing a ring to prevent pregnancy with a ring. 7. Pain

After putting on a ring, some women will feel soreness and discomfort in the lower abdomen or waist back. This is because the contraceptive ring placed in the uterus is a foreign body to the uterus, especially when it is too large or in a lower position. The uterus contracts to expel this foreign body, causing uterine contraction pain. When is it better to put on a ring? Experts point out that every menstrual period of women will shed the upper layer of the endometrium and discharge it outside the body; then the endometrial basal layer cells split and regenerate the endometrium. Therefore, after putting on a ring, women during the menstrual period will have the upper layer of the endometrium shed, and the endometrial basal layer directly contacts the contraceptive ring. The contraceptive ring directly kills the endometrial basal layer through mechanical scraping and the cytotoxic effect of copper ions. Therefore, after women wear a ring for 10-12 years, fibrotic changes will occur in the entire endometrium.
