Today, someone asked me about the quality of the Beijing Military Region General Hospital? Can it treat male diseases? In fact, I have some knowledge about this military hospital, and now I will answer this friend's question.

The Beijing Military Region General Hospital was built in 1913. After nearly a century of development, it now has four fully functional ward buildings with more than 1300 beds available at the same time; it has 47 specialties and more than 70 professions. It is the Beijing Clinical Medical College of the Second Military University of Medicine, as well as the clinical teaching hospital of the Third and Fourth Military Medical Universities. In May 1995, it was awarded the title of "Grade A, Level Three Hospital" by the General Logistics Department. In 2001, it was designated by Beijing as one of the first hospitals for medical insurance. The outpatient building invested by the hospital was completed at the end of 2003, with a usable area of more than 20,000 square meters. It is a modern outpatient clinic that integrates general outpatient services, emergency care, auxiliary examinations, laboratory tests, and more functions. Its daily capacity can reach over 4,000 patients. In December 2005, during the national health week selection activity hosted by Sohu.com, ten hospitals were selected as the most trusted hospitals in Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou, and our hospital ranked first. In the China Health Annual Review selection jointly organized by 39 Health Network and Sina.com in 2007 and 2008, our hospital consecutively ranked first among the most popular top three hospitals in Beijing. At the end of 2006, in the selection activity of the "Moving China" Top Ten Figures, Comrade Hua Yiwei was honorably elected. In 2007, our hospital’s chief nurse, Chen Haihua, won the 25th "Florence Nightingale" Award.

The hospital has a number of specialized centers with strong technical capabilities. There are six centers for the entire army: the Orthopedic Center of the entire army, the Liver Disease Treatment Center of the entire army, the Family Planning Technology Guidance Center of the entire army, the General Surgery Research and Treatment Center of the entire army, the Dermatology Research and Treatment Center of the entire army, and the Key Laboratory of Clinical Drug Research of the entire army. One institute of the military region: Cardio-pulmonary Vascular Disease Research Institute. Seven centers of the military region: Gastroenterology Research and Treatment Center, Thoracic Surgery Research and Treatment Center, Tumor Research and Treatment Center, Neurology Research and Treatment Center, Pathology Research and Treatment Center, Respiratory Medicine Research and Treatment Center, and the Key Department of Burns and Plastic Surgery of the military region.

There are more than 1,900 health professionals of all types in the hospital, including 500 senior technical professionals, and more than 400 master and doctoral graduates. The hospital has a doctoral and post-doctoral scientific research workstation approved by the state; there are 20 doctoral supervisors and 34 master supervisors; 200 clinical medicine professors and associate professors, and 50 well-known experts who enjoy government allowances.

The Urology Center is a key specialty under construction at the Beijing Military Region General Hospital, led by Academician Guo Yinglu of the Chinese Academy of Engineering, who is also the leading figure in urology in our country. Academician Guo has been engaged in urological clinical work for more than fifty years, with rich clinical experience and exquisite surgical skills. He is one of the founders of minimally invasive urology and andrology in China, and has achieved remarkable achievements in the diagnosis and treatment of urological tumors and adrenal diseases.

The center has gathered a group of experts and scholars who are knowledgeable, highly accomplished, and have long been engaged in urological clinical work. They have participated in writing 11 professional books, published more than 100 papers in top-tier journals, and won two third prizes and six fourth prizes for military science and technology progress, as well as one clinical achievement award.

The center has developed into a department that meets the standards of the "Medical Excellence Project" and has a certain influence domestically. It has formed certain advantages in the specialized treatment of adrenal diseases, urological tumors (kidney cancer, bladder cancer, prostate cancer, testicular tumors), urolithiasis, prostatic diseases, urogenital infections, and male diseases. In recent years, minimally invasive techniques and laparoscopic techniques have been widely applied, significantly improving the technical level. The center was one of the earliest in the country to carry out kidney transplant technology and has completed hundreds of kidney transplants, achieving survival rates that lead both domestically and within the military.
