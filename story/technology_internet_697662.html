**Conditions for Registering a Hong Kong Company**

1. Provide a new company name → Name check → Results available in 15 minutes, informing whether the name can be registered in Hong Kong.

2. Provide copies of shareholders' ID cards or passports (at least one shareholder aged 18 or above, any nationality or enterprise is acceptable).

3. Registered capital (no capital verification required; if the registered capital exceeds HKD 10,000, a 0.1% stamp duty will be charged by the government, equivalent to domestic stamp tax).

For example: HKD 100,000 requires HKD 100 stamp duty; HKD 1,000,000 requires HKD 1,000 stamp duty (calculated accordingly).

4. Confirm the share allocation ratio of the company's chairman and shareholders.

5. Fill in the commonly used contact address, telephone number, mobile phone, and email of the shareholders and directors in mainland China.

Note: If there are more than two company shareholders, each additional shareholder from the third onward will incur a document processing fee of HKD 200.

**Options for Choosing a Hong Kong Company Name**

According to Hong Kong company law regulations, regardless of the size of the registered capital, companies registered in Hong Kong are allowed to include names such as Federation, Promotion Association, Foundation, Association, Society, Chamber of Commerce, Research Institute, Center, Travel Agency, Publishing House, Business Association, Group, Enterprise, Holding, Investment, Stock, Trade, Import & Export, Culture, Film & Television, Design Institute, College, University, etc. The scope of business operations is unrestricted (except for industries regulated by the Hong Kong government).

Note: Registration of chambers of commerce, associations, foundations, or other social organizations is divided into two types: private nature and partnership nature. The former requires only one person, while the latter requires at least two people. Social organizations are non-profit institutions but must bear unlimited liability. Only residents of Hong Kong or subsidiaries of Hong Kong limited companies can establish chambers of commerce, associations, and foundations. The registration process for these social organizations is based on Chapter 310 of the Hong Kong Government's Social Organization Registration Ordinance. **Registration Process for a Hong Kong Company**

1. Sign the power of attorney and agreement for the registration of the Hong Kong company.

2. Prepay 70% of the total fees for registering the Hong Kong company.

3. RuiFeng Accountants will collect statutory form data from the Companies Registry and the Inland Revenue Department, and draft the company's articles of association.

4. Shareholders personally sign the statutory documents and related materials for company registration. Return the statutory documents to RuiFeng Institution.

5. RuiFeng Institution submits the statutory application documents to the Hong Kong Government Companies Registry and the Hong Kong Inland Revenue Department for registration.

6. Upon providing the above materials, we will complete the new company within 10 working days and provide the following documents to the client:

a. Certificate of Incorporation: Issued by the Hong Kong Companies Registry.

b. Business Registration Certificate: Issued by the Hong Kong Inland Revenue Department.

c. Articles of Association: Including the Memorandum and Articles of Association.

d. Company Seal: Steel seal, atomic round seal, director's signature seal (used for bank check endorsements).

e. Company Stock: A book of internally issued stock certificates.

f. Minutes Record: Recording the major events of the company's shareholders and directors.

g. Green Hard Box: Used for storing company documents.

7. Collect the full set of registered company documents and pay the remaining 30% balance.
