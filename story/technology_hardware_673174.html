Nutritional Value of Peaches

The nutritional content of peaches is as follows: in every 100 grams of edible portion of a peach, the energy is 117.2-7.7 kilojoules, with approximately 0.8 grams of protein; 0.1 grams of fat; various sugars at 10.7 grams, calcium at 8 milligrams, phosphorus at 20 milligrams, iron at 10 milligrams, vitamin A precursor (carotene) at 60 micrograms, vitamin B1 at 30 micrograms, vitamin B2 at 20 micrograms, vitamin C at 6 milligrams, niacin at 0.7 milligrams, and it also contains various vitamins, malic acid, and citric acid, etc.

The main component of peaches is sucrose, with relatively less vitamins and minerals. However, there is quite a lot of pectin which belongs to the fiber component and has the function of regulating the intestines. The use of peach blossoms as traditional Chinese medicine is due to the glycosides they contain, which are effective for diuresis or constipation. Peach leaves are said to be used for bathing and can remove heat rash.

Peaches have a warm nature, sweet and sour taste, can eliminate summer heat and quench thirst, clear heat and moisten the lungs, and are known as the "fruit of the lungs," suitable for consumption by those with lung diseases. Peach fruits are rich in nutrients, especially iron, making them an ideal therapeutic fruit for patients with iron-deficiency anemia. In addition, peaches contain more potassium and less sodium, making them suitable for edema patients. Eating peaches during hot summer days can nourish yin and generate body fluids, and moisturize the intestines. If paired with other foods creatively, it can greatly benefit health.

1. Blood-nourishing and Moisturizing Peach Jam: Take four ripe peaches (about 500 grams), peel and remove the pits, scrape the flesh into a pot, add washed mulberries (about 250 grams), then add sugar (about 250 grams) and water (about 500 milliliters), boil together, then simmer over low heat until it becomes a paste. Stir into a pulp, then add pine nuts, walnuts, and black sesame seeds (each about 100 grams) and cook for another 10 minutes or so. Once warm, it can be consumed. This multi-fruit peach jam has the effect of nourishing blood, moisturizing dryness, and promoting bowel movements.

2. Heat-clearing Crispy Peach Watermelon Cup: Take a small watermelon weighing about 1000 grams, cut it in half, scoop out the flesh, remove the seeds and place them aside, leaving the watermelon shell as the cup. Wash two crisp peaches (about 250 grams), remove the fuzz, and slice them (remove the pits). Peel and slice two bananas (about 250 grams), wash eight plums (about 150 grams), slice them and remove the pits. Mix peach slices, banana slices, plum slices, and appropriate amounts of watermelon flesh, add sugar and stir evenly, divide into two watermelon cups, freeze in the refrigerator for 30 minutes, and then consume. This watermelon cup has the effects of eliminating summer heat, clearing heat, generating body fluids, and quenching thirst, and can treat symptoms such as summer heat injury, irritability, thirst, short red urine, etc.

3. Qi-supplementing and Deficiency-supplementing Peach Skin Candy: Take 2000 grams of fresh peaches, wash and remove the pits, cut into pieces and mix with 500 grams of sugar, dry in the sun to remove moisture. Eat daily, suitable for those with physical weakness, insufficient qi and blood.

4. Honey Peach Juice Drink: 20 grams of honey, one fresh peach. First, peel the fresh peach, remove the pit, press it into juice, then add honey and an appropriate amount of warm water. Consume once or twice daily, each time 100 milliliters. It can treat acute gastritis.

Peaches are rich in various vitamins, minerals, and fruit acids, and their iron content ranks first among fruits. Iron is the main raw material for human blood production, which is quite beneficial to health. Peaches have an important position among many herbal plants. Peaches have a mild and harmonious nature, containing various vitamins and fruit acids, as well as calcium, phosphorus, and other inorganic salts. Their iron content is 4-6 times that of apples and pears. Peaches have the functions of replenishing qi and blood, nourishing yin, generating body fluids, quenching thirst, eliminating accumulation, and moistening the intestines. They are ideal auxiliary food for people with iron-deficiency anemia.

In Traditional Chinese Medicine, peaches are considered to have a warm nature and sweet-sour taste, with the effects of replenishment, nourishing the heart, generating body fluids, quenching thirst, eliminating accumulation, moistening the intestines, and relieving labor-induced heat. They are referred to as the "fruit of the lungs" and are suitable for people with hypoglycemia, lung disease, and chronic cough as auxiliary therapeutic food. Peach kernels have the effects of removing stagnant blood, moistening dryness, lubricating the intestines, and suppressing coughs, and can be used to treat stagnant blood, amenorrhea abdominal pain, hypertension, and constipation (used as a single herb, not for raw consumption); young green peaches (unripe smaller peaches) soaked and brewed with tea as a drink have the effects of stopping sweating and bleeding, and can be used to treat night sweats due to yin deficiency and hemoptysis; peach leaves also have insecticidal effects, and washing the perineum with decocted peach leaves can treat vaginal trichomoniasis; peach blossoms have the effects of reducing swelling and promoting urination, and can be used to treat edema ascites, hard stools, oliguria, and foot swelling due to foot qi. Although peaches are good, there are some taboos: firstly, unripe peaches should not be eaten, otherwise they may cause bloating or carbuncles; secondly, even ripe peaches should not be consumed excessively, excessive consumption may cause heat and fire; thirdly, rotten peaches must not be eaten; fourthly, peaches should not be eaten with soft-shelled turtles; fifthly, diabetic patients with high blood sugar should eat fewer peaches.
