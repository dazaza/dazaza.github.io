Baidu takes over the Chinese fiction website Zongheng for about RMB 4 billion

by anonymous on 2013-11-21 14:22:38

Reliable industry insiders revealed that Baidu has confirmed to take over the entire ZhangWo Chinese Literature Network from Perfect World, but the final acquisition amount is unknown. According to what I previously heard, Baidu's takeover price may be around 400 million yuan.

Another insider from Baidu then said that the cooperation negotiations with ZhangWo Chinese Literature Network suddenly stopped recently, the reason being that Baidu's investment department confirmed the acquisition of ZhangWo Chinese Literature Network, so there was no longer any talk of cooperation negotiations.

After acquiring ZhangWo Chinese Literature Network, Baidu now has three online literature businesses: Baidu DuKu, 91 Panda Reading, and ZhangWo Chinese Literature Network. Among them, DuKu is strong in Wap end, 91 Panda Reading is strong in mobile client, and ZhangWo Chinese Literature Network is a first-class online literature website on the PC end. Although the three businesses overlap, they indeed have their own strengths at present.

ZhangWo Chinese Literature Network’s other strength lies in original copyrights. Before Tencent's Chongshi Chinese Literature Network appeared, ZhangWo Chinese Literature Network was considered the second-largest original online literature website after Qidian Chinese Literature Network: ZhangWo Chinese Literature Network has many "big god" authors, and the quality of its original works and the number of copyrights are recognized as top-notch in the industry.

Obviously, Baidu's acquisition of ZhangWo Chinese Literature Network is not for the latter's traffic on the PC end, but for ZhangWo's original platform. By continuously outputting exclusive online literature works from ZhangWo to DuKu and 91 Panda, it aims to expand its market share and make mobile reading an entry point for Baidu on the mobile end. Baidu's strategy is actually very easy to understand.

However, the issue is that investing in online literature copyrights is similar to video websites buying copyrights, which is actually a money-burning matter.

Industry insiders revealed that ZhangWo Chinese Literature Network used to introduce two big-name authors every year, and each recruitment cost was in the millions. However, ZhangWo Chinese Literature Network performed poorly in monetization, resulting in annual losses of tens of millions, posing a constant risk of collapse. This is why Perfect World was eager to find a buyer.

Therefore, Baidu's acquisition of ZhangWo Chinese Literature Network is beneficial to all three parties. For Baidu, it found a high-quality original platform, and the acquisition price was not expensive;

For ZhangWo, there will be no profit pressure, and there might even be substantial investments coming in every year, with the only evaluation criterion being how many excellent works can be output to Baidu's mobile end;

For Perfect, selling ZhangWo not only avoids large annual losses but also brings in a significant amount of funds.
