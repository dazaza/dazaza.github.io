**Boss Must-Read: Points to Consider When Selecting Work Phones for Employees**

When using the Master Enterprise app, some bosses choose to let employees use their personal phones, of course providing a small subsidy for using personal devices. Some companies opt to provide employees with unified company phones. Both methods are good. If you choose the first method, please pay attention to the following issues:

1. The differences between smartphones on the market can be significant. Although they are all smartphones, variations in operating system versions and manufacturers may lead to different performance when using Master Enterprise. Please remind your employees that some lesser-known or low-quality brands should be avoided if possible. These phones may have inherent quality issues, making it harder to determine whether any problems encountered while using Master Enterprise stem from the phone itself or the software system we provide.

2. Avoid using very old phones, such as smartphones with a resolution of 240x320. These phones may experience poor display effects and slow operation speeds when using Master Enterprise.

If you choose the second method, i.e., the company provides unified phones to employees, sometimes businesses will work with carriers to handle bulk phone plans. This approach has certain advantages, such as:

1. The company doesn't need to directly pay for the phones; instead, agreeing to a consumption plan allows the carrier to provide phones for free.

2. The phone numbers belong to the company, so when an employee leaves, the number can be reassigned to someone else.

3. Calls between employees can be free, and some carriers even offer free calls between mobile phones and the company's landline.

However, please also note the following:

1. Mobile signal quality varies by region and carrier. In some areas, China Telecom has better signals, while in others, China Unicom or China Mobile may be stronger. It is advisable to consult your employees' preferences.

2. China Mobile phones often have slower internet speeds and only 2G signals in many regions, without 3G coverage. If you need to use Master Enterprise, especially for uploading photos on-site, please test the speed beforehand. China Unicom phones may have weaker signals in remote areas, so if your employees frequently work in rural areas, consider their input.

3. Opt for carriers' 2-year or 1-year contract packages. Some carriers offer deals where purchasing 3 years of service earns you a free phone. However, the industry consensus is that smartphones last around 2 years. Choosing a 3-year agreement could result in many phones becoming obsolete or damaged after 2 years.

Regardless of whether you procure phones through a carrier or not, here are additional factors to consider when purchasing phones:

1. What happens if a phone breaks or gets stolen? This could directly impact business operations. For instance, missing orders or sales data for a day would be unacceptable for most companies. Therefore, it’s essential to prepare accordingly. Either keep some backup phones available, encourage employees to temporarily use their personal phones if the company-provided one malfunctions, or ensure the logistics department can quickly replace phones.

2. Smartphones consume a lot of power. When selecting phones, prioritize those with longer battery life. Typically, phone batteries are measured in milliampere-hours (mAh), such as 1500 mAh or 2000 mAh. Larger numbers generally indicate longer standby times. Having two batteries per phone would be even better.

3. Finally, after equipping your employees with phones, consider providing silicone protective cases, which can significantly extend the lifespan of the devices!

After mobile phones became indispensable, the influence of mobile internet began to penetrate enterprises. It's time to incorporate location information into corporate management considerations. Developed by Zhongtai Group, "Master Enterprise" is a software application that leverages location information provided by mobile devices to enhance the efficiency of managing employees.

In traditional models, it was difficult for company managers to monitor the real-time locations of employees working outside the office. Additionally, data collected by employees had to be reported via phone or recorded upon returning to the office. With the development of smartphones and mobile internet, more convenient location-based management enables companies to manage field employees just like office workers.

Through a mobile app, employees can sign in more promptly and accurately, transmitting data collected during fieldwork directly to the company's internal network. Such needs are common in industries like fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) and logistics, where employees frequently work outside the office. Statistics show that over 30 million company employees work out of the office nationwide. As mobile internet continues to permeate daily life, the popularization of mobile office software is inevitable.

Beyond basic attendance management, "Master Enterprise" offers comprehensive mobile office services, such as managing/updating customer information via phone, recording new business opportunities, uploading materials to the company's internal network, checking company announcements, receiving new tasks, writing work summaries, applying for expenses, and registering for business trips—all through the phone.
