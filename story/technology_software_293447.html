Affected by the international financial crisis, industry insiders generally expect that the profits of central enterprises will decrease year-on-year this year. However, Huang Shuhua, Vice Chairman of the State-owned Assets Supervision and Administration Commission of the State Council, said at the Seventh Transnational Corporation Enterprise Annual Meeting held on December 3rd that the profits of central enterprises this year would be slightly higher than last year, achieving positive year-on-year growth. Analysts believe that there is no longer much doubt about positive year-on-year profit growth for the whole year. On one hand, this is due to the low base of last year, and on the other hand, it reflects the great success of a series of economic stimulus policies implemented by our country in response to the financial crisis. However, once these unconventional policies exit, the current profitability status of our country's central enterprises remains unknown. The withdrawal of central enterprises from some industries and encouragement of private capital development may be more conducive to enhancing the vitality of our country's economy.

Their stock prices not only need to reflect their own investment value, but also need to reflect their asset allocation value as super-large-cap stocks, which are indispensable core configuration assets for institutional investors. Moreover, institutional investors need to configure many large-cap blue-chip stocks for hedging purposes and strategically hold them as basic positions.

The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. This commission is only valid for the day it is issued. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock you want to buy or sell. Stock abbreviations are usually three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is six digits. The code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent when entrusting a trade. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks being traded: the quantity of stocks being bought must be a multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks being sold does not have to be a multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place a commission: counter submission, telephone automatic commission, computer automatic commission, and remote terminal commission.
