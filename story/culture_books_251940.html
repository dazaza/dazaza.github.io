Identifying Constitution for Better Health Preservation Related Content

The phenomenon of constitution is an important manifestation of human life activities. It refers to the comprehensive and relatively stable inherent traits formed in the process of human life, based on innate endowment and postnatal acquisition, including morphology, physiological function, and mental state. When the concept of health preservation according to TCM constitution had not yet taken root in the hearts of ordinary people, "constitution" was like a scale in the heart of every TCM practitioner. They would use this "scale" to measure their diagnostic standards when diagnosing diseases and prescribing medicine for patients.

Identifying Constitution for Better Health Preservation Part One

As the saying goes: born with it, cultivated by postnatal factors. Especially, postnatal factors play a decisive role in determining what kind of constitution each person has. The constitution needs to be nurtured through postnatal influences, which include rich content such as age, lifestyle, living environment, mental activities, diseases, drugs, etc. These "postnatal" factors are like busybodies who interfere here and there, repeatedly acting on the human body, thus forming various constitutions.

Identifying Constitution for Better Health Preservation Part Two

During the Spring Festival, my son and niece came back to Beijing together, and I happily went to the airport to pick up the two children. As soon as we got out of the station, my niece found an opportunity to tell me secretly: "Auntie, adjust my menstruation for me. My period has lasted for 30 days without stopping!" Hearing this, I was greatly shocked and asked her about the details. It turned out that she was dieting to lose weight. I seriously told her: "I can't adjust your condition. Your dieting has caused qi deficiency, which is bad for your health. You should eat normally and stop dieting!" She listened well and ate two large bowls of rice for lunch on the first day at my house.

Identifying Constitution for Better Health Preservation Part Three

"Controlling the mouth" means staying away from rich and greasy foods, maintaining a light diet, and ensuring a balanced nutrition intake. I told him that he must control his food intake, develop the habit of eating at regular times and in fixed amounts, and avoid overeating. He should not only control the intake of sugar but also the intake of salt. In fact, it's hard to imagine how a diabetic patient who exercises regularly but doesn't control his diet can manage his condition. "Exercising" means sticking to physical exercise. I advised him to reduce the time spent in front of the computer, get moving, and stick to a certain amount of exercise every day. Since he likes traveling, he could appropriately replace driving with hiking, achieving both travel and fitness at the same time. Why not take advantage of this dual benefit?
