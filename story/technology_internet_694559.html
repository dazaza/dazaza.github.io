#### Lip Modification

**I. Anatomical Structure of the Lips**

Lips consist of the upper and lower lips, with the area between them called the philtrum. The upper lip has two raised areas known as the cupid's bow, and the valley between these two peaks is referred to as the center of the lips. The sides of the lips are called the corners of the mouth.

**II. Definition of Standard Lip Shape**

In a standard lip shape:

- The peak of the lips lies on the vertical extension line from the outer edge of the nostrils.

- The corners of the mouth align with the vertical extension line from the inner side of the eyeballs when looking straight ahead.

- The lower lip is slightly thicker than the upper lip, with its middle thickness being twice that of the upper lip’s center. The outline of the lips is clear, with slightly upturned corners, giving the lips a three-dimensional appearance.

1. **Steps and Methods**

1. **Designing the Lip Shape** - Design an ideal lip shape based on the customer's natural conditions.

2. **Determining Key Points** - Identify the positions of the lip peaks on the upper lip and corresponding points on the lower lip.

3. **Drawing the Lip Line** - Connect the determined points to draw the lip line. There are two methods for drawing the lip line:

- Start from the corner of the mouth and draw towards the center.

- Start from the center and draw towards the corners.

4. **Applying Lipstick** - Apply lipstick in the same direction as the lip line was drawn.

5. **Highlighting** - Use a bright shade of lipstick or lip gloss to highlight the center of the lower lip.

**IV. Precautions**

1. The color of the lip line should match the tone of the lipstick but be slightly darker.

2. The lines of the lip contour should be smooth and symmetrical on both sides.

3. The color gradient of the lipstick should follow the rule: the upper lip is darker than the lower lip, and the corners are darker than the center.

4. The application of lipstick should be full to enhance the three-dimensional effect of the lips.

**V. Common Lip Shapes and Their Styling Styles**

- **Standard Lip Shape**: The peak is located at one-third of the distance from the center of the lips to the corners. This shape gives a friendly and natural impression.

- **Full Lip Shape**: The peak is located halfway between the center of the lips and the corners. This shape has a balanced contour with the height of the peak matching the thickness of the lower lip, creating a fuller appearance.

- **Sensual Lip Shape**: The peak is located two-thirds of the way from the center to the corners. This shape is round, plump, and evokes a sense of a beautiful smile, giving a passionate impression.

- **Sharp Lip Shape**: The peak is more pronounced and slightly pointed, with the corners slightly raised, giving a stern and serious impression.

- **Cute Lip Shape**: The upper lip is heart-shaped, while the lower lip is fuller, creating a small and adorable appearance.

**VI. Techniques for Correcting Lip Shapes**

1. **Overly Large Lips**

- **Characteristics**: A lip shape that is too wide can cause facial disproportion.

- **Correction Method**: Cover the original lip edges with foundation, then use a lip liner to slightly reduce the size of the lips by about 1-2mm. Draw the lip line smoothly and roundly. The upper lip line should be square-shaped, while the lower lip line should be boat-shaped. Use a red shade for the lips, with the inner contour slightly darker than the outer contour to create a slimming effect.

2. **Overly Small Lips**

- **Characteristics**: A lip shape that is too short and small can also cause facial disproportion.

- **Correction Method**: Use a lip liner to slightly expand the lips outward along the original lip edges, but not excessively to avoid an unnatural look. Choose warm pastel shades like pink or light orange, emphasizing the saturation of the color.

3. **Overly Thin Lips**

- **Characteristics**: Both the upper and lower lips are too thin, lacking a three-dimensional appearance.

- **Correction Method**: Use a lip liner slightly darker than the lipstick to draw the lip line outside the original lip edges. The upper lip line can be drawn rounder, while the lower lip line should be boat-shaped. Use a slightly darker shade of lipstick to blend inward from the lip line edges. Highlight the center of the lips with a pale pearl-colored lipstick or lip gloss for a fuller look.

4. **Overly Thick Lips**

- **Characteristics**: While thick lips can appear sensual and full, overly thick lips may lack femininity.

- **Correction Method**: Cover the original lip contour with foundation, then use a lip liner to draw a slightly smaller lip line inside the original lips. Avoid making it too small to prevent distortion. Use a matte deep-colored lipstick without pearl effects.

5. **Protruding Lips**

- **Characteristics**: The center of the lips protrudes outward, resembling a pout.

- **Correction Method**: Avoid using dark lip lines; instead, make them slightly blurred to create a receding effect. Do not use bright or pearl-colored lipsticks; choose neutral tones. Enhance eye makeup to divert attention from the lips.

6. **Drooping Corners**

- **Characteristics**: Drooping corners can make one appear serious and less cheerful.

- **Correction Method**: Use a lip liner to slightly lift the lower lip line and raise the position of the corners. Slightly lower the peaks of the upper lip. Make the lower lip color darker than the upper lip, applying a pearl highlighter in the center of the upper lip but avoiding it on the lower lip.

7. **Flat Lips**

- **Characteristics**: Flat lips lack visible peaks and curves, making the face expressionless.

- **Correction Method**: Use a lip liner to appropriately draw the peaks of the upper lip and shape the lower lip into a boat form. Then fill in with lipstick, choosing according to preference.
