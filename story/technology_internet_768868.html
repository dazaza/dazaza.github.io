Apple fans, face the reality! Apple has really been beaten by Samsung.

by anonymous on 2013-11-28 11:09:02

According to an article by Business Insider, recent reports on global smartphone sales in the third quarter reveal that Apple's sales growth has slowed down significantly. In contrast, Samsung is riding high and has claimed the crown by surpassing Apple.

Recently, the report on global smartphone sales in the third quarter was released. The report stated:

Samsung has overtaken Apple to become the number one global smartphone manufacturer. According to the third-quarter report, Samsung holds a market share of 32%, consistent with last year. Meanwhile, Apple's market share has dropped from 14% last year to 12% this year.

Although both companies (Samsung and Apple) have seen sales growth compared to last year, the smartphone market has expanded significantly, with more brands emerging in the competition!

Compared to last year, Apple's sales grew by 23%, while Samsung's grew by 46%. However, the overall sales growth of the global smartphone market also increased by 46%.

In other words, Apple's sales growth is only half of the overall market growth rate. Fans of Apple argue that the iPhone is designed for the high-end market, and Apple isn't a producer of "cheap products"! Or you could say that Apple is like BMW, not at the level of Ford!

However, it might be precisely this "high-end" philosophy that is hindering Apple's development. Take the iPad as an example; although Apple launched the iPad Mini, its price is still significantly higher than similar tablets from other brands. As a result, non-hardcore Apple fans or those with budget constraints may not choose Apple's products.

Perhaps it can be said that Apple is somewhat shortsighted. If it were to lower its stance slightly, capturing a larger share of the mobile device market wouldn't be difficult.
