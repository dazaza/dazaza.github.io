Dunk Champion Scores 42 Points as Suns Dominate Away Game, Trailblazers Collapse at Half-Time, Now Trailing 1-2 in Basketball - NBA - NIKE - Sina Weibo

by abcad123f on 2012-02-05 16:34:26

(Click to watch photo gallery) Sina Sports News, on April 23 Beijing time, the Suns won a big victory over the Trail Blazers with a score of 108-89 in the away game, leading the series 2-1.

Jason Richardson scored 42 points and grabbed 8 rebounds, shooting 13 out of 19 from the field, including 8 out of 12 three-pointers, becoming the hero for the Suns' victory. Amar'e Stoudemire scored 20 points, while Steve Nash contributed 13 points and 10 assists.

The Trail Blazers collapsed in the first half. LaMarcus Aldridge scored 17 points and grabbed 7 rebounds, Rudy Fernández scored 12 points, while Matt Webster and Jared Bayless each scored 14 points.

Nicolas Batum was injured in the previous game, which was like adding insult to injury for the Trail Blazers. Coach Nate McMillan was very worried, even bringing his phone to meetings, something rarely seen in the past. Fortunately, he didn't hear the worst news, as Batum was still able to play.

Not long after the first quarter began, Batum scored with a break-through dunk. However, his performance was affected by his injury. The Suns, on the other hand, launched a fierce attack. With 3 minutes and 48 seconds left in the first quarter, after Nash hit a three-pointer, they completed a 16-4 run, taking a 24-10 lead. The Trail Blazers managed to reduce the gap to 9 points, but Richardson quickly responded with an alley-oop dunk, followed by a reverse layup and then another three-pointer. The Suns ended the quarter with a 9-0 run, leading 34-16.

The Suns made the Trail Blazers collapse in the second quarter. The substitutes kept hitting three-pointers. With 6 minutes and 18 seconds left in the quarter, Dragic's three-pointer gave the Suns a 49-24 lead. After the Trail Blazers responded with 4 points, Richardson hit two more three-pointers within a minute. The Suns hit 5 three-pointers in this quarter, winning the quarter 32-21 and at one point had a 31-point advantage, leading 66-37 at halftime.

With the situation already difficult to control, the Trail Blazers rested Batum in the second half. Although they were trailing by a large margin, the Trail Blazers still fought hard, winning the quarter 23-15, reducing the gap to 60-81. Despite the large deficit, holding the Suns to just 15 points in the quarter showed that the Trail Blazers' defense was effective.

At the 5 minute and 24 second mark of the fourth quarter, Fernández hit 3 three-pointers within a minute, reducing the gap to 80-91. The Suns could no longer afford to be careless. Richardson and Nash continuously hit free throws, while the Trail Blazers' Webster missed multiple free throws. Richardson hit another three-pointer, and the Suns went on an 11-0 run, extending their lead to 102-80, making the game's outcome certain.
