Real Estate Developers' Deteriorating Financial Conditions and Rising Residential Inventory: 2012 May Become the Year of Property Market Consolidation

by yangliu435sh on 2012-02-19 11:29:45

As the regulatory policies deepen step by step, real estate companies are having an increasingly tough time. Issues such as broken capital chains and difficulties in financing are following one after another, leaving most real estate companies stuck in a difficult situation. On the other hand, land prices for new deals in the Year of the Dragon are generally lower than the levels of the same period in the previous two years. The vacancy rate of residential properties is also continuously increasing. From empty houses to ghost cities, home buyers are becoming more rational, and the atmosphere of waiting and seeing is growing stronger. Where will the property market head in 2012?

Regulatory Policies in the Year of the Dragon Hard to Relax

Since last year, the "strong medicine" of property market regulation has been remarkably effective. Data shows that in December last year, in 70 large and medium-sized cities across the country, the scope of newly built commercial housing and second-hand housing prices either declining or remaining stable continued to expand. Regulation methods dominated by purchase restrictions, loan restrictions, and price caps effectively controlled housing prices in various regions nationwide. Relevant experts thus judged: property market regulation in the Year of the Dragon will not be easily relaxed, and commercial housing prices will continue to decline.

On January 31st, Premier Wen Jiabao presided over the sixth plenary meeting of the State Council, discussing the "Draft Government Work Report (for soliciting opinions)" which would soon be submitted for review at the Fifth Session of the Eleventh National People's Congress. Among the six current key tasks, the third was "to consolidate the achievements of property market regulation, continue to strictly implement and gradually improve measures to suppress speculative and investment demand, promote reasonable regression of housing prices. Take effective measures to increase the supply of ordinary commercial housing. Do a good job in the construction and management of affordable housing." On February 9th, the Beijing Municipal Commission of Housing and Urban-Rural Development held a system-wide work conference, setting the tone for maintaining purchase restrictions within the year. The conference emphasized: "In 2012, we will continue to maintain the continuity and stability of macro-regulation policies in the real estate sector, resolutely implement existing regulation policies, promote reasonable regression of housing prices, prevent major fluctuations in the real estate market, and promote steady and healthy development of the real estate market."

"In general, the Central Economic Work Conference has made it very clear that we must adhere to the real estate regulation policies without wavering, promote reasonable regression of housing prices, and promote healthy development of the real estate market. Property market regulation will definitely not relax in the short term," industry insiders analyzed. If the construction of affordable housing does not keep up, and reforms in real estate taxation and land policies are not thorough, the effects of property market regulation will not be significant. Therefore, regulation policies in the Year of the Dragon are hard to relax, and the policies still need to be implemented further.

Financial Conditions Will Further Deteriorate

Under the pressure of a series of policies, real estate enterprises are beginning to face many problems, among which the most important is capital. Currently, peers within the real estate industry no longer avoid discussing the "capital dilemma." Song Pengyu, chairman of Orient Yongfeng Real Estate Company, stated that this situation is indeed a difficult period for real estate enterprises. Most companies suffer from capital hunger, but for small and medium-sized real estate companies, "hard times always have those who survive and those who starve." Problems faced by small and medium-sized real estate companies are varied and complex, both real and deceptive, involving both strategies and worsening actual conditions.

Relevant statistical data shows that 2012 will see a peak in debt repayment for real estate enterprises. Various offshore private bonds, real estate trusts, real estate funds, and private credits due for repayment in 2012 total several trillion yuan. Some institutions predict that the peak in debt repayment for real estate enterprises coinciding with the low point in sales recovery funds will lead to a further deterioration in financial conditions for real estate companies starting from the second quarter of 2012, possibly even worse than the worst level in 2008. In this situation, selling inventory remains the primary goal for real estate companies in the first half of 2012. Meanwhile, project transfers, equity transfers, and other corporate cooperation restructurings will still be the main events in the real estate industry in 2012.

Although many real estate tycoons like Yu Liang, president of Vanke, have stated that massive bankruptcies in the industry are impossible, large-scale reorganizations are also unlikely, the fact is that many small and medium-sized real estate companies have already begun choosing to raise funds quickly through equity transactions, project sales, or private funds. Industry insiders expressed that under the current situation where financing costs are increasingly high, whether real estate companies can hold on is clearly a severe issue.

The Land Market Becomes Colder, Land Acquisition More Cautious

With intensified regulation, developers' enthusiasm for acquiring land has sharply declined, leading to stagnant land prices. For example, in 2011, land prices in some areas of Hangzhou fell by half. In the ranks of land acquirers, once prominent players like Greentown, Binjiang, Songdu, Xizi, etc., were nowhere to be found. Also absent were aggressive foreign brand companies like Longfor, Wharf Holdings, Poly, and China Overseas that had previously performed strongly in Hangzhou's land market.

Beijing's land market in January 2012 was also significantly colder compared to the same period last year. In the first two months of 2011, land sale revenues reached 6.145 billion yuan and 7.09 billion yuan respectively, while in January 2012, Beijing's land market only saw the transaction of 19 plots, with total land sale revenue amounting to 2.061 billion yuan. The coldness of Beijing's land market is not unique; the latest statistical data from the China Urban Land Price Dynamic Monitoring System shows that in the fourth quarter of 2011, the national residential land price showed zero growth. Zhao Song, director of the Land Price Office of the China Land Survey and Planning Institute, expressed that the residential land price might further decline in the first quarter of 2012, indicating possible negative growth.

Zhang Dawei, director of the Beijing Centaline Market Research Department, also believes that first, blocked new home sales, skyrocketing inventories, and frequent delays in the commencement of already sold lands force real estate developers to respond helplessly. Second, there is great financial pressure. Currently, most of the financing channels available to developers have almost all been blocked by policy. The entire national real estate industry has entered winter. In this situation, the rule of cash being king makes the land market quiet. Third, land prices have not significantly decreased. Although the current market land transaction prices have slightly dropped, in some regions where housing prices have dropped by more than 10%, the decrease in land prices is still not significant. Moreover, the high starting prices of some lands are also one of the reasons for land auctions failing. Fourth, policy expectations remain tight. The currently enforced purchase restrictions in 46 cities are basically determined to continue implementing regulations, which clearly affects developers' market expectations. Developers are gradually reducing their investments in hot cities. Under the extremely pessimistic circumstances of developers, their enthusiasm for acquiring land is low, leading to a cooling down of the national land market.

Residential Inventory Continuously Climbs

Accompanying the sharp decline in trading volume, residential inventory continues to climb. According to data from the National Bureau of Statistics, the unsold area of commercial housing nationwide is 271.94 million square meters, an increase of 26.1%, with the growth rate accelerating by 18 percentage points compared to the previous year. Additionally, GF Securities also pointed out in its monthly report that in January of the Year of the Dragon, each company's inventory increased. Companies with more serious inventory issues currently have salable values reaching twice their actual sales figures in 2011.

Regarding this, UBS real estate analyst Li Zhiying believes that in the next six months, real estate policies will remain unchanged. In the first half of 2012, if construction does not slow down, there may be an oversupply. According to UBS calculations, construction volume must decrease by 28% to rebalance market supply and demand. "In 2012, if the decline in developers' sales exceeds 30%, most companies will face cash flow pressure," said Li Zhiying. In that case, more small and medium-sized developers may exit the market.

Wu Zhihui, director of the Market Research Department of Shenzhen World Union Real Estate Group, judged that the keyword for the real estate industry in 2012 is "de-stocking." Under the pressures of inventory impairment and capital turnover, rapidly digesting inventory will become the top priority for real estate companies.

2012 Could Be the Year of Real Estate Market Consolidation

It is difficult to say that regulatory policies will relax. Real estate companies will continue to face enormous pressure on various fronts, with decreasing trading volumes, climbing inventory, and homebuyers observing. 2012 is bound to be a year when the real estate industry undergoes significant changes.

Renowned economist Ye Tan analyzed and expressed: "The real estate industry is at a crossroads. In the future, the government will implement different financing policies for different enterprises, while ensuring the stable growth of fixed asset investment through affordable housing. Large enterprises' counter-cyclical land acquisitions and mergers and acquisitions of project companies indicate that the real estate industry has entered the deep-water zone of regulation. In 2012, we can witness the great reshuffle of the real estate industry."

"From the perspective of real estate companies, in 2012, real estate companies will place trading volume in a prominent position," Tongce Consulting Research Center Director Zhang Hongwei also believed that from the current market pattern, major real estate companies adopt a high-speed turnover market strategy to quickly recover funds. "Cash is king" becomes inevitable. Industry development experience and actual market sales data show that under the tightening of real estate regulation policies and market liquidity, it is better for real estate companies to "run fast" rather than "hold on stubbornly." A "one-step drop" and reasonable pricing become the key to "volume running" under current real estate regulation. Many analysts believe that the property market will still face downward price pressure in the first half of the year.

Moreover, corporate mergers and acquisitions will frequently occur in 2012. Monita Real Estate Industry Analyst Shi Qi believed that under the continuous tension of the capital chain, small real estate companies, due to insufficient operating and financing capabilities, may face significant capital risks. Large and medium-sized real estate companies with healthy capital chains and operations may benefit from land and property rights transactions, as well as the upcoming wave of mergers and acquisitions. In summary, a more thorough industry reshuffle may be coming.
