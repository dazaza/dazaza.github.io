Introduction to Car Paint and Maintenance Knowledge

Article Theme Tags: Car Beauty Knowledge, Car Polishing Methods, Car Maintenance Knowledge, Car Paint Knowledge, Car Waxing Methods

I. Introduction by Paint Manufacturers:

Due to the continuous and rapid changes in car paint technology, it is difficult for manufacturers of car care products to keep up. In the past, a paint system was usually completed through various conversions along the entire production line, but today, it is more often completed through the synchronized production of multiple lines. Moreover, according to market needs, they may frequently change different paint systems within a year;

This pressure comes from two sources: government regulations because volatile compounds in paints gradually release, exceeding air quality standards requires changes; customers also demand brighter and more durable paint surfaces. This pressure makes paint companies continuously adjust their production lines until they meet the requirements of automobile manufacturers.

II. The Development History of Paints:

With the development of the industrial era, products must constantly be changed and improved, especially in terms of product quality and packaging. Today's more advanced and mature car paint applications are marked by approximately three aspects. First is color selection. Since Henry Ford introduced to his customers "you can choose any other color just like choosing black," from then on, car colors have become increasingly diverse and dazzling. Over the years, an even more competitive market has left car manufacturers uncertain whether they should lean towards "traditional" or "trendy";

Second is the development of paint technology. Early car paints lacked durability, with common problems such as powder spots, fading, oiling, cracking, premature corrosion, chemical reactions, tar stains, etc. However, from 1900 to the present day, after extensive use, research, and testing, paint technology has been continuously improved. Today's paint technology is purer, and current car paints can ensure that vehicles last for over a decade.

The third factor affecting the development of paint technology is that the amount of solvent contained in the product must be reduced to meet air quality standards. This can be achieved in two ways: one is to research more advanced paint technology, and the other is to reduce the amount of solvent and change the paint formula;

Air quality standards and EPA regulations have prompted paint manufacturers to pay more attention to improving low-solvent products than ever before.

III. Structure of Paint:

The composition of the paint layer mainly includes the following three structures:

First, single-layer structure; second, clear-coat layer structure; third, multi-layer structure.

Single-layer Structure ("Standard" or "Traditional")

A single-layer structure refers to applying a layer of paint on top of the car body substrate, or the most primitive layer structure without a clear coat protection.

The majority of cars produced before the 1980s were coated with a single-layer paint.

The thickness of the top pigment coating is approximately 2.0 to 2.5 millimeters.

The entire paint structure (including the primer layer and pigment coating) is roughly 3.5 to 5.0 millimeters thick.

Clear-coat Layer Structure

The clear coat refers to the layer applied over the paint base layer. Because the paint surface is often exposed to environmental pollution such as industrial dust, paint manufacturers in the U.S. must apply a bright, metallic luster layer on top of the paint base to provide longer-lasting protection for the car body compared to a single-layer structure.

The clear coat uses new, high-tech products applied as a protective outer layer on top of the paint.

The clear coat has excellent isolation capabilities, preventing damage to the paint layer from ultraviolet rays.

It also has anti-stain and anti-mild scratch functions similar to silicon.

When polishing the car surface, the clear coat does not remove any color (it only works on the clear coat layer). Today's clear coats have a certain thickness and many metallic-like, pearl-like, astonishingly attractive glosses.

Today, 95% of car manufacturers use clear coats.

Compared to the single-layer structure of the past, the clear coat is more durable, sturdy, and long-lasting.

Since the car paint surface is a clear coat, operators can detect scratches through it (the clear coat is somewhat like a prism), making scratches more visible on the clear coat than on a single layer. The clear coat can magnify some swirl marks and scratches.

The total thickness of the clear coat structure (base layer, substrate, clear coat) is approximately 4.5 to 6.0 millimeters. The clear coat alone is 1.5 to 2.0 millimeters thick.

Multi-layer Structure

As customer requirements continue to increase, original equipment manufacturers must produce a better paint system, which is the multi-layer structure.

The multi-layer structure includes: base layer or substrate, base coat or "color coat" (approximately 15 microns).
