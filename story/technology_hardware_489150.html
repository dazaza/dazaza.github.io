Ten years

by nlghaihaie on 2011-09-14 14:36:22

Ten years, a total of three thousand six hundred days, sounds long when counted; but passes by in the blink of an eye.

In these ten years, he once told me "I like you," and I remember it clearly. It was a gloomy afternoon, the sunlight shone through the glass, illuminating the bed beneath us. My smile became soft yet cruel amidst the golden glow filling the room.

Even now, it remains the happiest day of my ten years.

Chapter One

Your name is Gao Yu? Is that the 'Yu' for melancholy?

No, it's the 'Yu' for lush greenery.

Ten years ago, I had just started high school. The first familiar person I met in class was Zhao Pingchuan, a former elementary schoolmate. Since all the other classmates were strangers, we quickly became friends within a few days. This guy always had a large group of buddies around him, and his closest friend was Li Weisen, a rather civilized-sounding name. But during the start of school, this guy got there first, and it wasn't until after a week of classes that I finally saw his face.

The moment I saw him, I felt inexplicable disgust. He looked like a little thug, smiling as if he knew me well. Not to mention, he started talking about how I looked. I immediately didn’t like him and only refrained from dealing with him due to Pingchuan’s face. In my heart, I already listed him on my blacklist, and my replies to him carried a bit of gunpowder. So, even though our group always hung out together, I never really paid attention to him.

Speaking of troubles, Pingchuan was somewhat sentimental. Despite having a lot of friends, he often exaggeratedly lamented the fleeting seasons. I just thought his act was pretty funny. His little pretense and deceitfulness especially attracted girls. Rumor had it that his romantic history began at the age of thirteen. Of course, he didn't shy away from fights either. In our group, no matter whose trouble it was, we would solve it together, whether it was a one-on-one fight or a gang brawl, it didn't matter. At that age, our blood was boiling almost excessively, and our looks weren't bad either. Aside from our chaotic grades, we could be considered quite confident and invincible, whether it was on the battlefield or the love field.

Generally speaking, we all had some interaction with girls. We didn't dare to do anything too extreme, but hugging and cuddling were inevitable. Gathering together to discuss experiences or secretly watching adult films were the most enjoyable things. Our mouths were dirty, especially during these times. Jokes among each other were uninhibited and chaotic, and the three of us were the best talkers. However, no matter how nonsensical Pingchuan was, I always felt a connection with him. But whenever Li Weisen opened his mouth, I would immediately shut up. The sight of him spitting everywhere was disgusting enough. He always exaggerated wildly, saying things like he had already gotten the girl, how big her chest was, how slim her waist was, how white her skin was... Finally, we completely clashed. The girl I went to see a movie with was the one he was pursuing. He insisted on fighting me outside the classroom, and after Pingchuan tried to mediate a few words, he stopped his hands and cursed me as crazy, intentionally picking on him. Although I can swear to heaven that I didn't know about his relationship with that girl, I jumped up and cursed him: "I just don't like you, what can you do! She asked me out herself, otherwise, I wouldn't even look at her!"

As soon as these words came out, he pushed Pingchuan aside and pounced on me fiercely. After a fierce battle, we were both injured. Even those who tried to stop us couldn't avoid being hit by mistake. In the end, his zipper or copper button scratched a long bloody wound on my face, and while I was in excruciating pain, he pinned me down and beat me mercilessly. The result was obvious—he won, and I was almost unrecognizable. When he walked away satisfied, he didn't forget to leave a harsh warning: "If you want to win me, forget about it for this lifetime!"

The teacher, hearing the news, forced me to explain what happened. I stuck to the rule of "gangster affairs handled by gangsters" and said it was an accident. Of course, this couldn't fool anyone, but with the limited intelligence of the teacher, they eventually ran out of tricks.

I refused Pingchuan's support and directly skipped class to go home. Lying in bed, I got angrier the more I thought about it. What kind of hero is Li Weisen to win by using despicable weapons!

While I was cursing his ancestors in my mind, my dad came home from work. After lingering in the living room for a while, he finally entered my room. Seeing my battered state, he surprisingly didn't scold me. Instead, his expression was unusually strange and lost, completely losing his usual demeanor. He sat in my room silently for a long time.

It wasn't until I cautiously touched him that he snapped back to reality and then told me something unexpected: "Your mom... she's gone."

At the time, I didn't understand, so I casually asked: "Where did she go?"

"I don't know..." he said, "She's not coming back, not returning to this house anymore... She took everything, clothes, documents, cosmetics... Only leaving a letter... But yesterday, she was fine..."

My father's self-talk made me inexplicably afraid, let alone the shock in my heart. I didn't continue listening and rushed into their room, pulling open the wardrobe. Sure enough... Mom's clothes were all gone, and the bottles and jars on the bedside table had disappeared completely.

The truth was clear—my beautiful mother had left us, without any warning... No, maybe the branded sneakers she bought me yesterday were the only sign. How clean-cut! She used a pair of shoes to conclude my...

I don't hate her, even now, I still feel this way. I just calmly understood a truth... This world changes so easily, so impermanently. Nothing stays constant, not even the family that gave birth to me and raised me, which had every condition to create that illusion.

Afterward, I took three consecutive days off. At home, I kept sleeping. If the wounds on my body woke me up, I would get up and randomly read some old books. Any phone calls were ignored. During these three days, I didn't shed a single tear, but aside from drinking water, I didn't eat anything because Dad didn't cook. He went to work every day, but when he came home, he just spaced out. Facing him was worse than starving—it was almost suffocating. So on the morning of the fourth day, I took a shower, changed into clean clothes, and directly took money from the drawers at home to go to school.

When I arrived at school, Pingchuan anxiously asked where I had been for the past few days since no one was home. I indifferently said I hadn't gone anywhere and then pulled him to have breakfast with me.

That day, my appetite was really good. I ate two large bowls of noodles and two buns, stopping Pingchuan's excessive complaints: "Heaven, how many days haven't you eaten?"

Three days.

What! Did you take medicine?

No need, I'm fine, I just wanted to sleep.

That... Wei Sen... he...

If you're a real brother, don't mention him. I want to eat more, don't help me lose weight.

Not... He agreed to apologize to you, that guy really went too far...
