Baidu Joins the QR Code Battle, Launches Dynamic "Dream QR Codes"

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 17:03:34

Baidu is also set to join the battle for QR codes. If WeChat's "Scan" feature has propelled QR codes into the spotlight, then Baidu hopes to revolutionize the past with its small but innovative "image QR code." As shown in the demo image, this is Baidu's dream QR code service currently under internal testing, which includes both static and dynamic forms.

The static image version of the QR code differs from the traditional black-and-white ones as it combines QR codes with color images, making a single image into a QR code. Users can customize their personalized QR codes based on the images they select. If this technology is integrated into advertising displays and industrial design, it will change the situation where black-and-white QR codes do not blend well with advertising images, enhancing users' willingness to scan. The dynamic GIF version of the QR code refers to a GIF animation being a QR code. For example, a movie trailer could be made into a few seconds of GIF, and after users scan the dynamic QR code, they can obtain information related to the movie and even purchase tickets online, bridging the gap between users' offline viewing intentions and actual viewing behavior.

QR codes have always been seen as a crucial tool for connecting online and offline experiences. The "Scan" feature of WeChat 5.0 can scan barcodes, covers, street views, and translations, allowing functions such as price comparison shopping, O2O discount information, mobile e-commerce, and scenic area discount information to be completed through scanning QR codes within WeChat. Dianping recently also launched its new business model via QR codes - the "New O2O Shopping Model," where users who buy shoes at a certain specialty store can try them on in-store and then scan the QR code inside the store to enter the Dianping group-buying page and purchase at a discounted price, aiming to create a Taobao-like platform for local life services. This time, Baidu is finding an entry point from the form of the QR code itself.

According to Baidu, the current dream QR code demo is the 1.0 version, utilizing deep image recognition technology to achieve adaptive grayscale at the basic level, automatically distinguishing the overlapping parts between the image and the black-and-white areas of the QR code. The 2.0 version with color adaptation will be released later, weakening the sense of separation between the picture and the QR code except for the three scanning points. In fact, Baidu's dream QR code is not the first attempt in the industry to beautify QR codes. Back in 2011, we introduced "15 QR Codes That Will Blow Your Mind," but most of these QR codes still only designed patterns within the QR code dot matrix rather than combining them with images.

Currently, Baidu has not yet confirmed whether it will integrate this technology into the existing QR code recognition function in the Baidu Mobile app or launch a separate dream QR code tool. However, existing QR code generation tools and scanning tools on the market can all recognize this form of QR code.
