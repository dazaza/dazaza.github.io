Research on Problems and Countermeasures in the Manufacturing of PCB Copper-Clad Laminate

by famang5911 on 2010-04-14 14:08:36

I. Tracing the Root Causes

Quality issues often arise during the actual manufacturing process of PCBs. How to determine the causes leading to production process problems and how to conduct necessary project tests? Here, we list some of the most commonly encountered PCB laminate problems and methods to confirm them.

Once a PCB laminate problem occurs, it should be considered for inclusion in the PCB laminate material specifications. Usually, if this technical specification enhancement work is not carried out, it will lead to continuous quality variations, followed by product scrapping. Typically, material issues caused by changes in PCB laminate quality occur in products made from different batches of raw materials used by manufacturers or produced with different pressing loads. Very few users can maintain sufficient records that would allow them to distinguish specific pressing loads or material batches at the processing site. As a result, it frequently happens that PCBs are continuously produced, components are mounted, and warping occurs continuously in the solder bath, wasting a great deal of labor and expensive components. If the batch number of the raw materials can be immediately traced, the PCB laminate manufacturer can verify the resin batch number, copper foil batch number, curing cycle, etc. In other words, if the user cannot provide continuity with the quality control system of the PCB laminate manufacturer, it will cause long-term losses for the user. Below, we introduce general issues related to substrate materials in the PCB manufacturing process.

II. Surface Issues

Symptoms: Poor adhesion of printing materials, poor adhesion of plating, certain parts cannot be etched away, and certain parts cannot be soldered.

Inspection Methods Available: Usually visual inspection by forming visible water patterns on the board surface:

Possible Causes:

- Extremely dense and smooth surfaces due to release films, causing overly glossy uncovered copper surfaces.

- Typically, the laminate manufacturer does not remove the release agent from the uncovered copper side of the laminate.

- Pinholes in the copper foil cause resin to flow out and accumulate on the copper foil surface, usually occurring in copper foils thinner than 3/4 ounce weight specifications.

- The copper foil manufacturer applies excessive antioxidant on the copper foil surface.

- The laminate manufacturer has changed the resin system, release film, or cleaning method.

- Many fingerprints or grease stains due to improper handling.

- Contaminated with machine oil during punching, blanking, or drilling operations.

Possible Solutions:

- Suggest that the laminate manufacturer use fabric-like films or other release materials.

- Contact the laminate manufacturer regarding mechanical or chemical removal methods.

- Contact the laminate manufacturer to inspect each batch of defective copper foil; request recommended solutions for removing resin.

- Request removal methods from the laminate manufacturer. Commonly, hydrochloric acid is recommended, followed by mechanical brushing.

- Collaborate with the laminate manufacturer before any changes are made to their processes, ensuring professional service and defining the user's test items.

- Educate all personnel across all processes to wear gloves when handling copper-clad laminates. Ensure that appropriate padding paper or packaging bags are used during transportation, with low sulfur content in the padding paper and no dirt in the packaging bags. Ensure that no one is using silicone-containing detergents while handling copper foils.

- Perform degreasing treatment on all laminates before plating or image transfer processes.
