Create a false model market

by njubaxgk on 2011-07-13 17:01:34

The competition in the cosmetics market has reached an almost fierce level. If a cosmetics distributor can find a good product line, it's almost equivalent to discovering a gold mine. Consequently, large cosmetics merchants go crazy searching for good products all over the world. However, what kind of product is considered a "good" product and how to find one varies greatly among merchants. Despite these differences, their original mindset—making money—is entirely the same.

Understanding this merchant psychology, targeting this group of frenzied potential customers, specialized cosmetic packaging companies have emerged, as well as specialized cosmetic recruitment companies and professional cosmetic recruitment managers. As a result, phenomena such as distributors hoarding good products in bulk, good products not selling at good prices, and the tragic failures of those who produce good products have appeared... Some in the cosmetics business say that the market competition is too intense and they can't continue; others blame their bad luck for not seizing opportunities; still others attribute their failure to low marketing management standards... But most merchants fail to fully realize that from the moment they 'marry' a good product, they have already fallen into the carefully disguised traps set by the product promoters! Therefore, how to truly find a good product and how to discover and avoid the traps in the process of cosmetics recruitment has become a crucial condition for successful agency.

Step One in laying the trap: Concealing Product Concept Packaging

The quality of beauty and cosmetic products entering the market varies greatly. Some ordinary drugs or health foods spare no effort to package themselves to sell at a good price.

Packaging generally falls into two levels: product shape packaging and product concept packaging.

Product shape packaging usually improves the product's class through exquisite printing of outer packaging or making larger outer packaging. The saying goes, "An item sells on its cover," creating the sensory impression that the product is worth the price. In reality, many products are far from satisfactory but priced several times higher. Product concept (mechanism) packaging generally enhances the so-called technological content of the product or caters to consumers' fears of disease by proposing new concepts or trendy terms.

Step Two in laying the trap: Creating False Model Markets

To make someone faithfully pursue a belief or ideal, you must first let your followers see a bright future or let facts speak for themselves. In other words, the power of a role model is infinite. Therefore, smart hunters always place bait near the trap. Creating a superficially prosperous model market, when you're still hesitating, the recruitment team will present examples like a certain market only requiring a 100,000 yuan investment to break even in the first month, earning 200,000 yuan profit in the second month, and so on, making you feel that not acting on this opportunity would be a sin against God! However, whether the model market is real or not remains unclear as the deposit or initial payment has already been transferred to the manufacturer's account. Therefore, before becoming an agent, it is essential to personally investigate the so-called model market and observe firsthand if the realities promoted by the manufacturer are indeed true!

Step Three in laying the trap: Aggressive Advertising to Attract Attention

Once packaging and model markets are ready, manufacturers or marketing companies with resources begin aggressively placing recruitment advertisements on national television, local television, or specialized media. They heavily promote the "new product with high technology, enormous market demand, excellent opportunity for quick wealth, sincerely seeking dealers nationwide!" However, there is currently no supply available at the terminal market. Want to be an agent? Want to get rich? Quickly call the recruitment hotline for consultation! Soon after, you'll receive an invitation to a product recruitment seminar. The invitation will enthusiastically invite you to listen to the gospel of wealth, an opportunity rarely seen in a hundred years!

Step Four in laying the trap: On-site Contract Signing and Payment at the Seminar

The venue for the recruitment product seminar must be chosen in an authoritative location. For example, a weight-loss product's recruitment seminar was held at the Great Hall of the People. According to professionals, an authoritative venue significantly increases the success rate of recruitment. During the seminar, the host or main recruiter's eloquence is very important. Can they convincingly explain the product's supposed advantages and vast market opportunities? Can they devalue similar products to nothing? Can they fully mobilize the emotions of the merchants? All this depends on an excellent professional recruitment manager! At a critical moment during the meeting, the prearranged decoys are pushed forward—strongly demanding exclusive agency rights for a certain market, signing contracts on the spot, and paying large deposits! As a result, most merchants, already excited, sign contracts and pay deposits without much thought! Within the shortest possible time after the recruitment seminar, the first batch of purchase orders from agents across the country will continuously flow into the recruitment party's account!

Step Five in laying the trap: Unfulfilled Promises Lead to Confrontation

When agents nationwide transfer large sums of purchase money into the recruitment party's account, simultaneously transferring massive quantities of goods from the factory warehouse to the agent warehouses, agents look at the products as if they've seen a gold mountain. They quickly expand their sales teams, rigorously train in product and marketing knowledge, and rapidly blanket the market with their products... A series of actions occur swiftly, fearing any delay in market sales. However, after the aggressive initial market coverage, various promises made by the factory remain unfulfilled! Promised CCTV advertisements disappear, local advertisement support gradually cools down, and eventually vanishes altogether. When agents request returns and refunds due to poor product efficacy, unfulfilled advertising support, heavy inventory accumulation, aging product batches, etc., the factory has a thousand reasons to refuse! Returning goods is harder than climbing to heaven! Legal action? Who's afraid of whom! By this point, agents regret their decisions, but it's too late!

The hidden traps in daily chemical recruitment fundamentally stem from three issues: First, the short-sightedness and lack of agency knowledge among distributors; Second, the short-term profit-focused mentality of daily chemical manufacturers disregarding long-term growth; Third, the greedy actions of speculative marketing companies and professional recruitment managers seeking personal gain through recruitment commissions. Regardless, increasing knowledge about cosmetic agency in this semi-transparent, moon-on-the-water commercial era is akin to gaining a pair of wise eyes, making it easier to identify and avoid traps.

Note: The last sentence refers to supervisors or regional managers having no decision-making power during visits or supervision.
