China Unicom business hall deploys new iPhone sales: the new model may soon be on sale

by anonymous on 2013-09-08 19:52:25

On the evening of September 6th, Sina Technology learned that China Unicom has already begun deploying sales of the new generation iPhone in its service halls, signaling that the official release of the new Unicom-version iPhone is not far off.

Recently, many domestic media outlets have received invitations to an Apple event scheduled for September 11th in Beijing. Industry insiders speculate that this event will be the Chinese version of the new iPhone launch. However, the new generation of iPhones seems to no longer be a secret, with numerous speculations and leaks about the iPhone 5S and 5C flooding the internet. Some replicas of the iPhone 5C are even being sold on Taobao. Therefore, more people are concerned with the question: "When exactly will the new generation of iPhones go on sale domestically?"

"Definitely within September, though the exact date hasn't been finalized yet. The contract plans will also be released soon. We just received notice from higher-ups, and the iTunes on our service hall computers has already been upgraded to the latest version 11.0.5, specifically to align with the new generation iPhone," revealed a Unicom service hall employee, exposing the news that the Unicom-version iPhone 5S will be launched in the domestic market. This seems to indicate that all sales channels of China Unicom have made various preparations before the launch of the new generation iPhone, which means it can be sold in China shortly after its official release.

According to internal sources at Unicom, the contract versions of the iPhone 5S and 5C will be available for purchase by mid-September.

In comparison with the upgrade version of the iPhone 5, the iPhone 5S, the iPhone 5C has garnered more attention. According to rumors, the 5C is equipped with a 64-bit A7 chip, 1GB RAM, an 8-megapixel camera, a 326PPI screen, and an F2.0 aperture, with options for 16GB, 32GB, and 64GB storage. It is speculated that the "C" stands for Color, offering at least five different color choices including black, white, green, blue, and yellow. Another interpretation suggests that "C" stands for Cheap, meaning a "low-cost version." However, based on Apple's usual style, the initial pricing should not be less than $400.

The iPhone event will be held on September 10th.
