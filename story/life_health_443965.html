Is it okay to get orthodontic treatment at the age of 20?

by 454kq on 2011-06-10 13:05:19

Can braces work at 20 years old? This is a question that many adults ask. We all know that the ideal age for orthodontic treatment is generally between 4 and 15 years old because during this time, baby teeth are being replaced by permanent ones and the dental bones are forming. But after this period, can one still undergo orthodontic treatment?

Nowadays, many college students in their early twenties start to pay attention to the aesthetics of their teeth. Today, experts from the Department of Stomatology at the Nanjing Air Force 454 Hospital will discuss whether braces can work at 20 years old.

Orthodontics involves adjusting the balance and coordination among facial bones, teeth, and the jaw-facial nerves and muscles through various corrective devices. In other words, orthodontics aims to correct abnormal relationships between the upper and lower jaws, upper and lower teeth, as well as between teeth and the jawbone and the associated neural muscular connections. Simply put: our main goals of orthodontics include improving face shape, aligning teeth, and enhancing chewing efficiency.

In general, the earlier the permanent teeth have fully grown in, the better it is to start orthodontic treatment. The younger the age, the looser the bone density, the faster the tooth movement, and thus the quicker the correction process. As age increases, bone density becomes harder. Therefore, young people at 20 years old can also choose orthodontic treatment, though it may take longer than in childhood. For adults, the face and bones have largely set into shape. To achieve a thorough correction, orthodontic treatment must be combined with maxillofacial surgery to correct developmental deviations in adult jawbones. Functional orthodontic appliances used for correcting children’s jaw structures usually have little effect on adults.

Can braces work at 20 years old? Actually, as long as you maintain a persistent attitude and actively cooperate with your doctor's treatment, you will definitely end up with a set of neat and beautiful teeth.
