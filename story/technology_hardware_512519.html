Call for more "micro-angels" and "micro-investments"_7611

Hongyi Zhou

Here is my understanding of angel investing: when you were an entrepreneur, others helped you; now that you have the ability, you can actually help more young people.

In China, I am certainly not the one who has invested in the most projects among angel investors, nor am I the one with the largest investment amount. Here is my understanding of angel investing: when you were an entrepreneur, others helped you; now that you have the ability, you can actually help more young people. Today, what China lacks the most are angel investors. This is also the reason why I chose to do angel investing.

When doing angel investing, I feel that one should have a mindset of supporting young people and be down-to-earth. When investing money, you should be mentally prepared, knowing that this investment may make a profit, but it is even more likely to incur a loss. Since the investment is made at the early stage of a company, its risk is bound to be the greatest. Therefore, when investing this money, whether it's one million yuan or two million yuan, you should consider this money as already gone in your heart. If he succeeds, it's good luck for you; if he fails, then it's like paying tuition fees.

Angel investors must pass on their experience to entrepreneurs. Many young people have courage, sharpness, and insights into new markets and new users, but they lack experience, network connections, and some resources. I found that in Silicon Valley, USA, many senior executives of large companies are active angel investors; they have experience, and while investing money, they also provide some assistance and guidance to young entrepreneurs. In China, there are more and more such angel investors because there are more and more people who have successfully listed or sold their companies. To grow a company from small to large is truly a history of blood and tears, with many lessons learned from failure, many detours, and many shoals and hidden reefs. Those who have walked this path before can, if they pass on their knowledge without reservation to young entrepreneurs, accelerate the pace of entrepreneurship and innovation across the entire industry.

China should have more "micro-angels" and "micro-investments." Currently, there is a kind of misleading notion: if an angel investor doesn't invest several million yuan in a case, it seems embarrassing to call oneself an angel investor. I oppose this value concept; we should encourage more micro-angels and micro-investments. Don't refrain from doing good just because it's small; if you can't invest one million yuan, you can invest five hundred thousand, or even one hundred thousand. There's no need to always compare who has invested more money or who has invested in more projects; such comparisons are meaningless.

In the United States, there are also many micro-angels who invest ten thousand US dollars. However, this amount of money can change the fate of some entrepreneurs and give some young people some assistance. When Charles Zhang of Sohu started his business, Nicholas Negroponte gave him less than ten thousand US dollars, which was a typical case of micro-angel and micro-investment. It changed the fate of Charles Zhang and also altered the landscape of China's Internet.

The most important thing in angel investing is to identify the right person. To identify the right person, more often than not, it involves making a few phone calls, doing some background checks, and then having a conversation with the entrepreneurial team. Based on your feelings, whether you like them or not, whether you get along well or not, after a few conversations, the impression will naturally come out.

Moreover, I have two additional ideas:

Firstly, due to the high risk, I suggest that the amount of angel investment should not be too large. Regardless of how good the project is, it is best to set a maximum limit when investing.

Secondly, the percentage you own should not be too high, otherwise, it might bring some adverse effects to the company's future financing. For example, if some traditional angel investors hold 70% of the shares, later investors would have quite a lot of concerns. Moreover, if you own a relatively large proportion of shares, the risk is actually on your shoulders. Therefore, don't invest too much in one project, don't own too much equity, but you can continuously invest in multiple projects.
