In the early years of the Song Dynasty, Emperor Renzong of the Song Dynasty was fond of inkstones. Every year, he would select inkstones with outstanding texture and exquisite craftsmanship from all over the country as tribute to the court. Dingzhou in Guangdong Province has always been famous for producing Duan inkstones. The old pits of Duanzhou were sealed as the royal rock tribute pits, and the stones mined there would be carved into inkstones by the best craftsmen. The best ones would be selected as tribute inkstones and presented to the court.

The annual inspection and delivery of tribute inkstones to the capital was always a big event in Duanzhou, a great day for the people of Duanzhou. Therefore, those lucky enough to be appointed to make tribute inkstones were often called "the best in the world." There are many inkstone studios in Duanzhou, and countless inkstone craftsmen. Among them, Jimo Studio is the most famous. The owner of Jimo Studio, Xiao Jingzhu, has an impeccable carving technique, and almost all the inkstones offered by Duanzhou every year come from his hands, making Duanzhou famous. However, being so prominent also invites envy, and many inkstone officials have harbored resentment towards the Xiao family. Moreover, Xiao Jingzhu's only son Xiao Fei, his adopted son Le Feiyan, and Yang Feng are known as the three young masters of Ruizhou, notorious wastrels known far and wide.

On this day, it happened to be the grand occasion when Jimo Studio presented the tribute inkstone. Xiao Jingzhu respectfully presented the tribute inkstone, but the censor supervisor Changxiaou Hou of Shezhou pointed out that this inkstone was a forgery, and Xiao Jingzhu was arrested. The governor of Duanzhou, Bao Zheng, held a court session to investigate the case, and the expert invited to authenticate the inkstone was none other than Xiao Jingzhu's long-lost younger sister, Lu Min. Lu Min firmly stated that the inkstone could only belong to Xiao Jingzhu, thus inexplicably involving the Xiao family in a prison disaster. Although Xiao Fei appeared indifferent on the surface, he was deeply worried about his father, even rashly entering the courtroom to take the blame for his father, but failed to save him. Xiao Fei was grief-stricken. Just before Xiao Jingzhu was escorted to the execution ground, an imperial decree from the emperor pardoning Xiao Jingzhu arrived, ordering him to carve an unparalleled inkstone within half a year for use in peace negotiations. It turned out that Xiao Jingzhu's good friend Zhuo Tianyi had once been the emperor's teacher and secretly helped him. At this time, Xiao Fei met Lu Min's mute daughter Ju Ban, and they got along well.

Xiao Jingzhu arranged for Xiao Fei to marry Zhuo Tianyi's daughter Zhuo Yuer, who had a pre-arranged marriage contract, in order to help Xiao Fei overcome his impatience. Xiao Fei refused. Xiao Jingzhu worked tirelessly night and day carving the tribute inkstone, but unfortunately damaged the imperial inkstone stone, resulting in the confiscation of the Xiao family's property and the sealing of Jimo Studio. Gradually, they developed feelings for each other.

Upon returning from his studies, the inkstone carved by Xiao Fei received high praise from the emperor, allowing Jimo Studio to reopen. At this point, Xiao Fei became arrogant.

Bao Zheng timely exposed Chang Xiaou Hou's conspiracy, leading to his demotion and confiscation of his property.

When Xiao Fei learned that Le Feiyan liked Zhuo Yuer, he sent a letter of resignation and a forged proposal letter on behalf of Le Feiyan to Zhuo Tianyi, fulfilling their wishes. Le Feiyan and Zhuo Yuer finally became a couple. Xiao Fei swore to win the Mid-Autumn Inkstone Meeting and asked the emperor for permission to marry Rongrong, which the emperor readily agreed to. At this time, however, war broke out again at the borders between Song and Liao.
