Man wins 900 yuan from lottery, suffers mental breakdown and contemplates suicide (Picture)

by caodnedrper on 2011-11-10 10:55:22

A man in Chengdu was saved after being in a standoff with the police for nearly 4 hours; his wife prepared to send him to a psychiatric hospital.

On the morning of May 5, a 44-year-old man living in Xiaoguanmiao, Chengdu, used a single-edged razor blade to press against his throat. After nearly 4 hours of stand-off with the police and relatives, he said "the drill is over!" and attempted suicide by slashing his neck but failed. He was then rushed to the hospital for emergency treatment. His wife stated that after winning the lottery twice in succession recently, he suddenly suffered from mental breakdown. The family had already contacted a psychiatric hospital to prepare for his admission and treatment.

The man held a razor blade to his throat and stood off with the police for 3 hours.

At 9 o'clock in the morning on May 5, the atmosphere at No. 24 Xiaoguanmiao was tense. Several police cars were parked at the entrance of a small alley. Inside the alley, the police and the crowd were anxiously watching the movements inside a small door. From the conversations among the police, it was learned that at 6 o'clock in the morning, a man on the loft inside the house used a sharp single-edged razor blade to press against his neck artery, intending to commit suicide. He had been standing off with his relatives who tried to dissuade him and the police who arrived later for 3 hours. However, he was still emotionally agitated, showing no intention of putting down the knife and kept looking at the clock.

According to the introduction, the loft where the incident occurred was very small. The main participants in the negotiation were one of the man's friends, an undercover police officer posing as a friend, and his wife holding their three-month-old daughter, who was pleading with him desperately. However, the man continued to refuse cooperation.

Constantly looking at the clock, there was suspicion that he had set a specific time for his suicide.

" At 6 o'clock in the morning, I received a call saying that he suddenly pressed a razor blade to his neck and wanted to commit suicide, so I hurried over," Mr. Zhao, a friend involved in the persuasion, told reporters: "The man preparing to commit suicide is surnamed Wang, 44 years old, and is a native of Chengdu."

Mr. Zhao said that he and Wang have been friends for over ten years, often drinking together, which is why Wang's family sought his help first. However, even Wang, who usually maintained a good relationship with everyone, refused to let him approach this time, not even allowing his wife near him. "His eyes were always filled with fear, trusting no one, making the rescue extremely difficult." At 7:40 in the morning, seeing that persuasion as a friend was ineffective, Mr. Zhao called 110 for help. Soon, the 110 patrol police, station officers, and 120 medical staff arrived at the scene. After deliberation, it was decided to send an undercover police officer posing as Mr. Zhao's friend upstairs to negotiate, but Wang still showed no intention of putting down the knife.

"We have been looking for an opportunity to save him, but it seemed like he knew our intentions and refused to drop the knife," In order to make him put down the knife, the rescuers first chatted idly with him, hoping to ease his emotions. When this proved ineffective, the undercover police officer noticed a pack of cigarettes next to him and asked for a cigarette. The man threw the cigarettes with his other hand; when they offered water, he warned them not to approach and just threw the water bottle; when they offered food, he said he didn't want to eat.

During the prolonged standoff, the meticulous police noticed that Wang kept glancing at a clock placed in the room. "Could he have planned a specific time in his mind?" To be safe, the undercover police officer and Mr. Zhao worked together to divert Wang's attention, quietly taking the small clock and setting it back by an hour.

Avoiding the police, he slashed his neck and was saved.

At 9:50 in the morning, Wang suddenly said, "The drill is over, you can leave now." When the police asked him to put down the knife, he said he would only do so if everyone left the loft. Under repeated requests, he agreed to let his wife and Mr. Zhao stay, while all other personnel must leave the room. With no choice, the policemen stepped out and decided immediately to rush in!

A minute later, three policemen rushed into the small door, hearing cries and shouts coming from upstairs. When the police rushed upstairs, Wang had already fallen to the ground, bleeding from his neck, with the razor blade already in Mr. Zhao's hands. After performing a simple hemostasis on Wang, who had fainted, the medical staff immediately sent him to the nearest hospital for emergency treatment.

"After the police went out, seeing that he still wouldn't drop the knife, Sister Three (Wang's wife) knelt before him with her child, begging him," Mr. Zhao recounted the scene just happened: "I told him, if you drop the knife, she will get up. Unexpectedly, after letting go of his wife, he started cutting his own neck with the knife. I thought this was the last chance, so I rushed forward to grab his knife."

After examination by the hospital, Wang had two ten-centimeter-long cuts on his neck. Fortunately, these were superficial wounds, not deep enough to reach the arteries and veins. After stitching, he was no longer in danger of life. According to Chengdu Evening News,

The collapse was due to winning the lottery twice.

Mr. Zhao told reporters that since three days ago when Wang won the lottery twice in succession, his mental state suddenly deteriorated. It was learned that Wang had been laid off more than ten years ago, without work, and his wife also had no job, leading to a rather tight life. However, Wang's personality has always been relatively introverted, rarely confiding his difficulties to friends. Three months ago, after the birth of his daughter, friends noticed that Wang became increasingly worried.

Three days ago, Wang bought a lottery ticket and won 400 yuan on his first try, and the next day, he won another 500 yuan. Normally, such consecutive wins should bring joy, but Wang began to "lose it", constantly worrying and becoming suspicious. Subsequently, he started thinking that all his friends wanted to harm him. Even walking on the street, he would run into cars, fortunately being stopped by his friends. Mr. Zhao said, "Wang dared not use the phone, fearing eavesdropping, and communicated with people by writing notes." "They are deliberately trying to kill me," the reporter saw Wang write such words on a note.

Friends then took him to consult a psychologist. After understanding the situation, the psychologist indicated that this was no longer within the scope of psychological intervention but belonged to mental illness, requiring specialized treatment at a psychiatric hospital. Mr. Zhao told reporters that to help this friend, several friends had already discussed observing him for two more days. If things didn't improve, they would send Wang to a psychiatric hospital, but unexpectedly, the incident occurred early on May 5.

Preparing to send him to a psychiatric hospital

In the hospital, Wang, who had calmed down, was receiving an infusion and occasionally reaching out to touch his daughter's smiling face as if nothing had happened. On the doctor's advice, Wang's wife had already arranged with the psychiatric hospital to prepare for her husband's treatment.

Medical staff and police officers carried the injured person to the ambulance.
