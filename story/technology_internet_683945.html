In 2001, through Ruifeng Accounting Firm, she registered [XX International Apparel Co., Ltd.] in Hong Kong [http://www.rf.hk/company/hongkong/] and simultaneously registered trademarks in Hong Kong and mainland China. Under the name of the Hong Kong company [http://www.rf.hk/company/hongkong/], she commissioned relevant factories for private label processing. Now, Miss Lin's family has invested in advanced equipment in Hangzhou, establishing a large-scale garment factory with assembly lines.

Case: International Trade

1. [Tax Optimization] Mr. Wang from Fujian: Mainly engaged in project engineering investment bidding, due to business expansion needs, he wanted to establish a reputable group company in mainland China. However, tens of millions of registered capital could not be secured, and the desired company name was already taken by others. Later, through friends' introductions, he established the group company in the Cayman Islands [http://www.rf.hk/company/cayman/introduction.html], and also set up holding subsidiaries in Hong Kong, Samoa [http://www.rf.hk/company/samoa/13235.html], the Bahamas, and mainland China, becoming a true multinational group. After careful planning by accountants, the company’s taxes were reasonably arranged.

2. [Import & Export] Ms. Du from Hefei: Long-term engagement in international import trade. Once, a French chemical raw material supplier refused to sign a contract with her and insisted on signing through an overseas intermediary company and issuing a letter of credit [http://www.rf.hk/exp/xinyongzheng/], citing the reason that overseas intermediary companies have high credibility and timely reliable payments. Since this was a long-term business relationship, Ms. Du had no choice but to establish a company in Delaware, USA [http://www.rf.hk/company/usa/], and open an offshore account for the Delaware company at a foreign bank in Shanghai (this account has all kinds of settlement functions in international trade). This way, a long-term good cooperative relationship with the customer was established.

3. [Trade Settlement] Mr. Jin from Tianjin: Engaged in international textile product export trade, he always had to export through intermediaries, but was concerned about business secrets and settlement security. To ensure long-term stability and avoid exchange rate differences, he wanted to register an offshore company [http://www.rf.hk/company/offshore/], conduct independent exports, and directly settle accounts. The premise was that the offshore company should not have complicated financial reporting procedures. With the help of Ruifeng professionals, Mr. Jin registered a BVI offshore company in the British Virgin Islands [http://www.rf.hk/company/bvi/bvijr.html] (which does not need to provide annual financial reports to the government), and simultaneously opened an offshore account at a foreign bank in Beijing, fully meeting Mr. Jin's offshore trade planning requirements and allowing for greater international development.

4. [Export Tax Rebate [http://www.rf.hk/exp/ckts/]] Mr. Lin from Xiamen: Long-term engagement in stone import and export trade. Moreover, the speed of receiving overseas payments became much faster than before.

5. [Payment Settlement] Mr. Chen from Shenzhen: Long-term supply of electronic components to Hong Kong manufacturers, most of which had factories in Shenzhen, Dongguan, Zhuhai, etc. Sometimes, Hong Kong manufacturers paid with Hong Kong bank [http://www.rf.hk/bank/hongkongbank.html] checks.
