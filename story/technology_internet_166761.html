Just 24 hours after the PR value update of Google, China's network encountered another network paralysis incident. A national network crash occurred frequently in just a few months. QQ could not be logged in, large communities were inaccessible, personal websites also showed signs of being unable to access, and Google could not be accessed normally either. Just the day before, Google had carried out a PR update, namely: on June 24, 2009, PR was updated again. Why did this happen just 24 hours later? What is the reason for all this? The SEO Tribe continues to pay attention.

Google Experiences Largest Paralysis "Accident" in History

Yesterday morning, Google gave many company employees a "shocking moment". "Gmail suddenly couldn't be opened when I started work in the morning," an employee of an IT company told CBN reporter that he has been using Google's Gmail as his work email because it has much more storage capacity than the company's email and is convenient to use.

Behind this, however, is the longest-lasting and most severe website access paralysis "accident" since Google officially entered China in 2005.

In fact, from the evening of the 24th to the morning of the 25th, the international site Google.com of the search engine site Google could not be accessed normally domestically. Multiple Google services, including Gmail, Google Reader, ADwords advertising, etc., were unavailable. Only the Google.cn specifically set up for the Chinese market could still be accessed normally.

"We are investigating," said a Google spokesperson, who learned that in the past 24 hours, most Chinese users could not access Google.com, and at the same time, they could not use related services such as Google Docs and Gmail. Google has received a large number of user reports, but the cause is still under investigation, and no more comments will be made currently. In order to soothe netizens' dissatisfaction, Li Kaifu, President of Greater China of Google, personally posted a message calling on Chinese netizens to be patient.

It wasn't until near noon yesterday that the relevant paralyzed services of Google gradually returned to normal, but some users still reported that Google Maps and others could not be accessed.

Some analysts said that if Google's related services are blocked, or even intermittently blocked, it will deal a substantial blow to Google in China. For example, Gmail has a large number of users in China. Email requires very high service reliability. If the service cannot be guaranteed, it will cause a great deal of dissatisfaction among users, and even loss. The ADwords advertising platform also has a large number of advertisers in China using this service to utilize the online platform for keyword advertising services.

Previously, Google's website was criticized by relevant departments for containing pornographic and vulgar information content.

Subsequently, Google's overseas webpage search business and联想词search business were suspended by the relevant authorities. Google China stated that it is working hard to clear the relevant content, and most of the problems pointed out by the relevant government departments have been resolved.

Tencent QQ Suspected of Being Attacked, Large-Scale Disconnection Nationwide

"QQ suddenly went offline. At first, I thought it was a network problem or one of those very short disconnections, but when I checked a webpage, I found that the network was fine, and this time it lasted for a long time." At around 5 pm today (the 25th), Tencent QQ experienced a long-term disconnection. Meanwhile, our website reporters received a large number of messages from netizens, saying that QQ in their cities had disconnected, causing a lot of trouble at work.

Netizen "Yimianwai" said that QQ first intermittently disconnected, and soon could not log in at all. Netizens from Shanghai, Xiamen, Gansu, Beijing, and other places all followed up saying that the same problem occurred in their cities.

Some portals and enterprise/personal websites could not be opened

The SEO Tribe received feedback from some customers, stating that their websites could not be opened after 18:00 in the evening. If you're lucky after refreshing multiple times, you might see a few words on the website. At this time, the network across the country was almost in a state of paralysis. Websites relying on domestic domain name resolution to foreign space were also difficult to open domestically for a time.

A few speculations about this incident: 1. Network machine failure, this is a possibility, because such a large-scale network crash within a short period of time would not be so serious if only considered from a software perspective. 2. Malicious attack, the previous network crash was caused by disputes between private servers, and this time it is not entirely ruled out that such a possibility exists, after all, interests can drive people to do incomprehensible things. 3. Business competition conflicts.
