A female student of Shenzhen University exposes on Weibo that she was raped outside the school gate.

by edivista on 2011-10-31 10:21:58

Click to enter the Weibo of "Bangbangxiaonvzi"

Please everyone, you must help me. I was raped by a man near the south gate. I recognize his appearance, but I don't dare to report it to the police. Please give me a solution, please help me. Netizens, Bangbangxiaonvzi sent out this Weibo post yesterday at noon. She claimed that the previous night, while walking alone near the south gate of Shenzhen University, she was raped. Due to not having the courage to report it to the police and tell her boyfriend, she could only seek comfort and advice from netizens, which immediately caused great attention online. Subsequently, several female students revealed they had been molested in the Guimiao New Village bus stop area, near the Bai Shi Road south gate of Shenzhen University.

The reporter visited the scene at the south gate and Bai Shi Road of Shenzhen University yesterday and found that during the day, there were few pedestrians on Bai Shi Road outside the south gate of Shenzhen University, with tall trees and dense forests. At night, it appeared even darker and more secluded.

A self-identified female student of Shenzhen University claimed to have been raped at night.

Bangbangxiaonvzi's first Weibo post was sent out around 1 PM yesterday: "Please everyone, you must help me. I was raped by a man near the south gate. I recognize his appearance, but I don't dare to report it to the police. Please give me a solution, please help me." She also said that the reason for posting this Weibo was, on one hand, to seek comfort and advice from classmates, and on the other hand, to warn others, telling female students not to go out alone and hoping that boyfriends do not look down upon women who have been raped.

After the Weibo post was made, it immediately drew the attention of many netizens. Although some people were skeptical, most netizens still strongly advised Bangbangxiaonvzi and urged her to preserve evidence and seek help from the police as soon as possible.

From the interactive dialogue between Bangbangxiaonvzi and multiple netizens, the reporter roughly understood the course of events: On the evening of September 29th, Bangbangxiaonvzi, who often did not return home, encountered a rape by an off-campus man on the road near Yuehai Gate Village next to the south gate of Shenzhen University. She resisted but failed. Although she remembered the appearance of the suspect, she did not want to recall every detail. She indicated that the incident was unrelated to campus security personnel and the responsibility lay with local police officers.

Bangbangxiaonvzi wrote on Weibo: "I don't dare to answer my boyfriend's phone calls. I've been bathing all night long. I feel so dirty, what should I do? I don't have the courage to face others and tell them what happened to me. I really don't want to report it to the police, I really don't want to. Actually, if this happens to you now, you would just want to die."

After about an hour of counseling from netizens, Bangbangxiaonvzi finally decided to tell her boyfriend about the incident. Afterward, she would no longer use this Weibo account and hoped that everyone would change their comments to protecting themselves well and not going out late at night. "I am sorry to my parents and boyfriend. I don't want to make Shenzhen University difficult. The incident happened off-campus, I am handling it myself with other Shenzhen University students."

At around 8 PM last night, Bangbangxiaonvzi changed her name to "Thank You Every Person at Shenzhen University" and deleted all blog posts. In the signature section, she also wrote: "I will report to the police and later tell you what that person looks like, but I kindly ask everyone not to forward anymore, just comment is fine, don't forward anymore."

Several female students revealed they had been molested

While carefully examining the interactions among netizens, the reporter discovered that several self-identified female students of Shenzhen University revealed they had been molested in the South Campus and near the south gate of Shenzhen University.

A netizen named QXXXX, claiming to be a student of Shenzhen University, introduced her recent experience to the reporter: "Around 11 AM on Saturday, October 15th, while waiting for the bus at the Guimiao Village entrance bus stop on Bai Shi Road heading east, a man suddenly approached me, grabbed my hand, took off his pants, and forced my hand to touch his private parts. I struggled fiercely and screamed for help, but there was no one around at the time. After struggling for about ten seconds, I finally broke free. That man did not run away immediately; instead, he continued to handle his own private parts. A while later, he walked to an electric bike parked beside the bus stop and drove away, clearly premeditated." She stated that due to being in a hurry to go home, she did not report it until the 17th.

Netizen WangXX's Cat also said: "One time around 9 PM, on the road from Yuehai Gate Village to the south gate of Shenzhen University, I encountered a pervert riding an electric bike. I didn't know that road was unsafe before."

Netizen hxxXuan also posted a Weibo with pictures on the 17th: "Pervert! Pervert! Pervert! Today, after class, I was walking back alone on the small road from the south campus, when this seemingly refined but depraved man suddenly came up, pushed aside my hand, and touched my private area (although he didn't actually touch). I screamed loudly, and after stopping, I noticed the girl behind me was also touched. Then I chased after him and shouted, 'Pervert, stand still!' He actually dared to stand still and then pretended to sincerely apologize."

Reporter Investigation

Few Pedestrians on Incident Road

The reporter conducted an on-site investigation at the south gate of Shenzhen University yesterday. The south campus of Shenzhen University is under construction, and Bai Shi Road divides the south campus and north campus into two parts. There are multiple construction sites along the road, and the dormitories of the south campus are far away, about 400 meters along Gang Yuan Road.

The reporter observed that the south gate of Shenzhen University is adjacent to Bai Shi Road, which is a new six-lane road. The pedestrian path on the side close to the Shenzhen University wall is densely planted with vegetation, and in the middle, there are shrubs one or two meters high, dividing the pedestrian path into two. Standing on the outer bus stop, the shrubs block the view, making it difficult to see what happens inside the pedestrian path. Since nearby areas are all construction sites without residential zones, even during the day, there are very few pedestrians on the road. Occasionally, a few buses pass by, and there are not many passengers waiting at the bus stops.

At around 8 PM last night, the reporter revisited Bai Shi Road, where there were even fewer pedestrians at this time. Through the roadside streetlights and shrubs, the lights of distant lamps appear dimmer. Occasionally, students walk by, and some girls walk alone. Since there are often no passengers waiting at the bus stop, many buses speed past without stopping, and taxis are rarely seen.

Several students of Shenzhen University told the reporter that the pedestrian path on the east-to-west side of the road in front of the school has more people during the day but fewer at night, while the west-to-east side close to the south gate of Shenzhen University has very few people regardless of the time of day or night, and there are also many construction workers coming and going. Usually, one doesn't dare to walk alone. They all believe that the female student who was raped might be a freshman unfamiliar with the surrounding environment.

The reporter saw that not far from the road frequently pointed out by Shenzhen University students as the location of frequent incidents is the Yuehai Police Station, but there were no security officers patrolling the road.

Police Statement

Further Investigation Needed to Verify

Last night, a police officer from the Public Relations Department of Nanshan Public Security Bureau confirmed to the reporter that they indeed received a report from QXXXX. The officer stated that cases of female students from Shenzhen University being molested are occasional incidents. Since the incident occurred on a remote road with multiple ongoing construction sites and a large number of workers, it is not practical for the police to station many officers there. Currently, the police plan to strengthen cooperation with campus security personnel to increase patrols on the Bai Shi Road section near the south gate of Shenzhen University to prevent similar incidents from happening again.

Meanwhile, Nanshan police stated that whether what Bangbangxiaonvzi said is true still requires further investigation. The police will find Bangbangxiaonvzi as soon as possible to restore the truth of the event.
