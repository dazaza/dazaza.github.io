Killing star refuses 25 million to join AC Milan, but the Rossoneri have already got a stronger one

by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-09 08:22:06

The Star Refuses £25 Million for AC Milan, But Rossoneri Already Have a Stronger Option

Sina Sports News - Manchester United and Arsenal have publicly announced their interest in Edin Dzeko, while Manchester City quietly offered Wolfsburg a staggering £25 million bid. However, this Bosnian striker who has already scored 6 goals in the Bundesliga this season and 3 in the Champions League rejected the invitation from Manchester City. The UK's Daily Star revealed: Dzeko told Wolfsburg that his preferred transfer target is AC Milan, and he would only consider other teams if AC Milan is not an option.

Meanwhile, Italian media reported further developments. In the next three weeks, possibly during Christmas, Galliani will meet with Dzeko and his father again to discuss future matters. They had a successful meeting in Sarajevo last summer and even signed a contract at the time. Ultimately, it didn't come to fruition because Wolfsburg coach Armin Veh refused to let him go. However, if AC Milan wants Dzeko, the contract still stands. Dzeko stated that money isn't an issue; the annual salary AC Milan offers is similar to what Wolfsburg currently pays him. Moreover, Dzeko has been a die-hard fan of AC Milan since childhood and also appreciates their playing style—this is the key reason for his choice.

Given Dzeko’s enthusiasm, what stance will AC Milan take? On one hand, when Dzeko previously openly expressed hope to join AC Milan next summer, and Italian media hyped the story, AC Milan remained silent. At least for the winter transfer window, AC Milan has no non-EU spots available, so things can calm down temporarily. The club definitely doesn't want transfer market rumors to affect the morale of players like Borrello and Huntelaar. What about the future?

Firstly, it's important to understand that despite receiving substantial sponsorship from Emirates Airlines, AC Milan still needs to carefully consider each deal as many areas require reinforcement. Within limited funds, is the central forward position the most urgent need for AC Milan? The answer is no. If Dzeko comes, what happens to the outstanding Borrello, or the improving Huntelaar? And what about Paroschi, co-owned with Parma? Not to mention that Inzaghi may not leave AC Milan in the summer. In comparison, although Antonini and Abate have eased the full-back issues, with players like Oddo, Janu, and Zambrotta getting older, AC Milan needs at least one more full-back. In the center-back position, someone needs to replace Kaladze as the first-choice substitute. In midfield, the likelihood of Gattuso leaving is much higher than Inzaghi on the forward line. On the wings, AC Milan lacks sufficient depth, and Seedorf also needs a successor...

From a technical perspective, Dzeko's scoring ability is better than Borrello, but Borrello moves laterally faster and holds the ball better. Dzeko excels in tight spaces within the penalty area, while Borrello helps push the team's offense into the opponent's box and keeps the ball there, creating opportunities for teammates. Both have their strengths, but Borrello has played in Serie A for many years, whereas Dzeko's adaptability remains uncertain.

In such a scenario, if AC Milan makes a rational decision, Galliani will inform Dzeko during their Christmas meeting that with Borrello's recovery, AC Milan's recruitment needs have changed. While giving up on Dzeko is regrettable, using the money saved from not signing Dzeko to buy players for more urgently needed positions would be a wise choice from a global perspective.
