How to reduce server procurement costs

by now on 2009-02-20 12:02:14

Currently, the world is in the midst of the most severe economic crisis in a decade. In this crisis, whose end cannot yet be foreseen, many small and medium-sized enterprise (SME) managers are beginning to meticulously calculate expenditures. Naturally, server procurement in IT infrastructure has become one of the key areas where many SMEs aim to cut costs. How to enhance resilience, improve survival capabilities, and truly enable users to achieve business growth through IT systems is a question that requires more rational thought amidst this economic crisis.

In the past, during the process of enterprise informatization, IT equipment was considered merely an auxiliary facility for business operations. In recent years, enterprises have gradually moved away from this erroneous mindset. For businesses today, informatization has become a very important topic, representing one of the most significant changes across enterprises in recent years. Servers have always been the foundation of informatization construction. Their overall performance greatly influences the progress of enterprise informatization and affects the entire operation of the company. How can we achieve a win-win situation, reducing investment while still meeting enterprise needs?

Reducing investment directly means cutting the budget when purchasing servers. What methods can meet daily application needs? The best solution is to purchase products with higher cost-effectiveness. During the process of server procurement, SMEs should consider various factors comprehensively to make full use of limited resources. SMEs not only need a server that meets their requirements but must also consider multiple comprehensive factors, choosing a server with high availability, high reliability, ease of management and maintenance, as well as a high level of service capability.
