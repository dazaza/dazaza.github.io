Car Beauty Knowledge: Car Maintenance Answers

Car beauty knowledge in car maintenance, answering that cars are like people, they also need care and more importantly, maintenance. Below, we will answer several common questions that car owners encounter during car maintenance or washing.

A. What to do if the car freezes while washing?

Answer: Use warm water to wash the car. In cold weather, water often freezes immediately after dripping, so the biggest taboo when washing the car is having water droplets freeze on the vehicle, which directly leads to paint layer cracking. Therefore, it's best to use warm water when washing, instead of cold water. If possible, wash the car indoors. After the vehicle enters the workstation, let it sit for 5 to 10 minutes until the engine cools down before washing. Sudden temperature changes are not good for the paint surface.

B. The difference between polymers and silicon resin?

Answer: Car wax contains different types of polymers. Polymers usually enhance the durability of the product and/or seal the car paint, avoiding corrosion caused by environmental pollution to the car paint. Silicon resin components are used to enhance the brightness of the paint surface.

C. How to wash your car in snowy winter days?

Answer: Several days of continuous snowfall led Mr. Li to keep delaying, and by the time he wiped his car, he found that the paint was not as glossy as before? Wiping the car should be timely. Even if the weather forecast predicts snow for the next few days, don't delay wiping the car. As long as the car is covered with snow, you should immediately rinse off the snow with clear water. Snow contains corrosive components. Whether it's the paint surface, chassis, tires, or rims, being covered by snow for a long time will cause the car paint to corrode, which is why it is not as glossy as before.

D. Are the dashboard and car doors made of genuine leather?

Answer: No (very expensive cars might have genuine leather). They are leather products, so they only need surface gloss enhancement, without the need for penetration nourishing liquid.

E. The difference between leather and synthetic leather?

Answer: Synthetic leather is fake leather, it has no pores, it's just shiny on the surface. Genuine leather is similar to our skin; the internal structure of genuine leather is actually very similar to a dense bundle of silk. They are connected by protein chains. Once the protein chain shrinks and dries out, the genuine leather begins to crack.
