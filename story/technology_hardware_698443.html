How to reduce the accident rate of lifting machinery safety

by anonymous on 2012-03-09 09:31:15

As one of the eight categories of special equipment, the accident rate of lifting machinery has remained high in recent years. The reasons are multifaceted and mainly include lagging standards, insufficient design foresight, backward manufacturing process levels, inadequate quality control during installation, and poor management during use. To reduce the occurrence of lifting machinery accidents, efforts can be made from revising relevant regulations and standards, strengthening supervision and management, among other approaches.

Lifting machinery is categorized as one of the eight types of special equipment according to national regulations. Whether it's the equipment itself or its usage, from a safety perspective, it is less dangerous compared to the other types of special equipment. However, based on the statistics of accidents by the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection, and Quarantine (AQSIQ) over the past few years, the incidence of lifting machinery accidents and the absolute number of casualties caused by these accidents have consistently ranked first among the eight categories of special equipment. In 2009, the AQSIQ prioritized the safety of lifting machinery as part of the annual "three actions," hoping that rectification would lead to a reduction in lifting machinery accidents.

### Complex Accident Causes

Why has the accident rate of lifting machinery remained persistently high? Through our extensive experience in conducting safety inspections on lifting machinery, we identify several main causes:

1. **Lagging Standards**: The delay in standard revisions has led to a weakening of innovation in lifting machinery design. The basis for China's lifting machinery design is the "Crane Design Code" GB3811-1983, with safety requirements primarily set out in the "Safety Regulations for Lifting Machinery" GB6067-1986. These two general technical standards were introduced in the 1980s and played a decisive role in regulating lifting machinery at the time. However, with the large-scale production and use of lifting machinery, improvements in manufacturing processes, and the use of new materials, the standards have become inapplicable in some situations. The "Safety Regulations for Lifting Machinery" is the main theoretical basis for the inspection of lifting machinery. A new version of the inspection regulations was released in 2009 and implemented starting April 1, 2010. However, the revised version of the "Safety Regulations for Lifting Machinery" has yet to be issued. If there are significant differences between the new inspection regulations and the new safety regulations, inspection agencies may find themselves uncertain about how to proceed with inspections.

2. **Insufficient Design Foresight**: Some manufacturers of lifting machinery fail to fully consider the load conditions of stressed components under various operating environments when designing new products. The reliability of the hoisting mechanism braking system is a key factor in determining the safety performance of lifting machinery. The majority of safety accidents involving lifting machinery during use are related to the hoisting mechanism. The hoisting mechanism typically uses electromagnetic brake systems, where the key to reliable operation lies in controlling the electromagnetic coil. Electrical control system design for lifting machinery has been a weak point for Chinese manufacturers for over 20 years.

3. **Backward Manufacturing Process Levels**: There exists a gap between China's lifting machinery standards and those of Europe and America, making it difficult for Sino-foreign joint ventures producing lifting machinery to gain traction in China. Some medium and small-sized lifting machinery devices still need to improve their technical content. Additionally, due to market competition, manufacturers' profits are limited, restricting the funds available for new product research and development.

4. **Inadequate Installation Quality Control**: For a long time, the acceptance inspection of lifting machinery installations has relied on final completion inspections, with the entire installation process quality control entirely dependent on the skills and technical proficiency of the workers. The installation process can be seen as an extension of the manufacturing process, but the on-site working conditions are often worse than those during manufacturing. Lifting machinery manufacturing and construction standards lack clear specifications and requirements for the process control of construction techniques, leading to low-level construction in the installation phase. Non-compliant operations and deficiencies in some construction techniques introduce hidden and structural defects that increase the risk of accidents. Starting from April 1, 2010, the new inspection regulations stipulate that the final acceptance inspection of lifting machinery should be revised to supervise the entire installation process, providing a strong guarantee for the installation quality of lifting machinery.

5. **Poor Management During Use**: Safety accidents occurring during use are the focus of lifting machinery accidents, with poor management by the user being the primary cause. Here, management mainly refers to the management of people and equipment. With the restructuring of state-owned enterprises and the development of private enterprises, it is difficult to ensure that every lifting machine is operated by qualified personnel. Moreover, the weak awareness of special equipment safety among private enterprise owners is also an important reason affecting lifting machinery accidents. Additionally, equipment management includes daily inspections and planned maintenance. Daily inspections mainly involve checking the safety performance of the equipment, which is often neglected. Some companies do not even schedule annual maintenance plans for lifting machinery. Violations during operation are also one of the main reasons for accidents occurring during the use of lifting machinery.

Other factors contributing to lifting machinery accidents include usage environment, load classification, illegal or beyond-capability production of lifting machinery, etc.

### Addressing the Root Causes

To reduce the occurrence of lifting machinery accidents, as lifting machinery inspectors, we believe action should be taken in the following areas:

1. **Issue (Revise) Current Technical Standards**: Quickly issue (revise) general technical standards that meet the current production, installation, and usage requirements of the lifting machinery industry, while fully considering international synchronization. For example, the revision of the "Safety Regulations for Lifting Machinery" should focus on the control of hoisting mechanism brakes, utilizing the auxiliary braking function of electrical braking to protect the brakes.

2. **Encourage Sino-Foreign Cooperation**: Promote the cooperative production of lifting machinery within China to introduce advanced technology, thereby increasing the technical content and safety performance of lifting machinery and enhancing the overall manufacturing level of China's lifting machinery.

3. **Strengthen Industry Supervision**: Intensify the supervision and management of the lifting machinery industry, severely cracking down on illegal and out-of-scope production of lifting machinery. Encourage lifting machinery manufacturers to introduce high-tech talent, especially in control technology, to redesign lifting machinery with a completely new concept.

4. **Strict Legal Training and Assessment**: Quality supervision departments at all levels should strictly enforce legal training and assessment of operators, especially reinforcing operational management skill training within user organizations. Emphasize the responsibility system for corporate safety production, strengthen the safety awareness of the main responsible persons of enterprises regarding special equipment, standardize the operational procedures of operators, enhance the safety awareness of employees engaged in special operations, and advocate daily inspection management models for lifting machinery users.

5. **Change Concepts Among Inspectors**: Due to the long-standing practice of supervising lifting machinery after installation completion, adopting the new inspection regulations to supervise the entire installation process requires a change in mindset and improved professional skills among all inspectors. Theoretically, they should strengthen their understanding of the inspection regulations, understand the weak links in the manufacturing and installation process, and formulate practical inspection operation guidance documents to ensure inspection quality. On this basis, in future regular inspections of previously installed lifting machinery, apart from routine inspections according to the inspection regulations, targeted checks should be conducted on potential safety hazards and defects in installation and manufacturing, adding necessary inspection items using advanced detection methods to prevent accidents.

6. **Advocate Seamless Communication Between Supervisory Inspection and Management**: Advocate managing lifting machinery used in construction sites through seamless communication between supervisory inspection and management, ensuring that building lifting machinery with safety hazards does not continue to be used until corrections are properly implemented.
