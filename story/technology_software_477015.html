Channel.me - Multi-person collaboration to comment on a webpage simultaneously

by xdash on 2011-08-15 14:43:21

Channel.me is a multi-user collaborative online service based on web browsers, allowing multiple users to access a specific webpage simultaneously and post comments in real-time on the page.

Specifically, if a user creates an invitation link B pointing to a target URL A through Channel.me, other users can click on B to access A. They can then plaster their own comment sticky notes all over the screen, much like sticking plasters everywhere, and everyone else can see them.

What's the use of this thing? For example, behind every hardworking designer, there's always a group of people who think they know best. With this tool, no matter if this group of people is sitting in the Alps, or in the president's office next door, whether they're having breakfast across the street, or leisurely fishing — as long as they have internet access, they can always give orders and make suggestions to the web designer: "This should be rounded corners, this should be shifted 2px to the right, this font needs to be larger and bolder, change this title to something more clickbaity"... (Designers, are you feeling so overwhelmed that you want to pay to delete posts - -+)

As for team meetings for analyzing competitor websites, collective testing before new product launches, remote assistance, or group bulletin boards, Channel.me can handle all these tasks.
