Harbin gang forcibly pulled passers-by to be slave workers at knifepoint

by king2z9o on 2012-02-24 11:39:02

Recently, the Daoli Public Security Bureau of Harbin City successfully dismantled two criminal gangs suspected of illegal detention and forced labor. Eight gang members were criminally detained, and 18 homeless individuals and migrant workers were successfully rescued.

Four people were dragged into a minivan in front of a bank

At around 3 a.m. on the morning of February 17, Harbin taxi driver Jiang reported to the 110 emergency number: while driving past the Agricultural Bank on Xiangzheng Street in the Daoli District, three men armed with wooden sticks and knives dragged four people from inside the ATM room into a Chang'an minivan (license plate number Hei ARL109). One of them refused to get into the van and was immediately beaten to the ground by two men with sticks and knives. Another managed to escape, while the remaining two were forcibly dragged into the minivan and taken away.

After receiving instructions from the Harbin Municipal Public Security Bureau's 110 command center, the police officers of the Xinhua Police Station immediately rushed to the scene for handling, and the Major Case Squad of the Criminal Investigation Team No. 2 was ordered to organize forces to rush to the scene for investigation. Through reviewing the surveillance footage from the bank and along the route, the investigators quickly locked onto this Chang'an minivan and determined that the victim Yang Fu, aged 52, with a registered address at Group 49, Baishan Village, Longjiang County, was beaten for refusing to get into the van, resulting in his right leg being broken and cuts and stab wounds on his leg and face. He was sent to the hospital by the police for treatment; Victim Li Xuechun, aged 40, with a registered address at No. 353, Gongnong First Street, Daoli District, Harbin City, escaped during the struggle; Victim Lu Senmin, aged 52, with a registered address at No. 97, Xiangli Street, Daoli District, Harbin City; Tan Xuewu, aged 46, with a registered address at Committee 6, Guoxing Village, Pinggang Township, Shuangcheng City, Harbin City. The two were taken away in the Chang'an minivan.

Police [Tianyan]

Locked down four suspects

Based on [Tianyan] tracking and investigation visits, the police ultimately locked onto four criminal suspects. The involved criminal suspects include Dong Ansheng, aged 37, with a registered address in Yian County, Qiqihar City, currently residing in Shuangyang Town, Yian County, unemployed. In 1999, he was sentenced to one year and eight months in prison for theft by the Longfeng District Court of Daqing City. Lu Fengzhong, aged 26, nicknamed "Erlong", currently residing and registered in Changchun Town, Baiquan County, unemployed. Dong Fengbin, aged 37, with a registered address in Shuangyang Town, Yian County, Qiqihar City, currently residing in Fushan Village, Wanggang Town, Nangang District, Harbin City, unemployed. Guo Jiliang, aged 27, with a registered address in Xidi Village, Lianhua Village, Yonghe Township, Bin County, currently residing in Xifeng Town, Daowai District, Harbin City, unemployed.

On February 19, the Major Case Squad of the Criminal Investigation Team No. 2 of the Daoli Branch swiftly acted, arresting criminal suspect Dong Ansheng in a mahjong parlor in Fushan Village, Wanggang Town, Nangang District; arresting criminal suspect Lu Fengzhong in a residential area on Haping Road in Xiangfang District; and arresting criminal suspect Dong Fengbin in a bathhouse on Xuefu Fourth Street in Nangang District. At the same time, five victims who were illegally detained and forced to work were rescued. On the evening of February 20, criminal suspect Guo Jiliang surrendered to the Daoli Public Security Bureau. This criminal gang suspected of illegal detention and forced labor was successfully dismantled.

Following clues

Rescued another 13 people

During the interrogation of the gang members, the police obtained leads on another criminal gang suspected of illegal detention and forced labor. Following the leads and acting swiftly, the police arrested the criminal suspects Jia Huichun (male, aged 29, with a registered address at Building 836, Tihigh Street, Daowai District, Harbin City, currently residing at Building 18, Yuxuan Community, Xiongwei Road, Daowai District, unemployed), Liu Yujin (male, aged 22, with a registered address at Gangou Village, Chenggong Village, Piaohe Town, Jilin Province's Jiaohe City, currently residing at Dao Kou Fifth Street, Daowai District, unemployed), Kou Guobo (male, aged 31, with a registered address at Committee 2, Yongzhi Village, Happy Town, Shuangcheng City, Harbin City, currently residing at Dao Kou Fifth Street, Daowai District, unemployed), and Su Zhongli (male, aged 32, with a registered address at Wujia Village, Wujia Town, Shuangcheng City, Harbin City, currently residing at Building 5, Yuqiang New Village, Limin Development Zone, Hulan District, Harbin City, unemployed) in two houses on Dao Kou Fifth Street in Daowai District, successfully rescuing 13 victims.

Work without pay

Escape attempt results in beating

According to the police, these two gangs are suspected of illegally detaining and forcing homeless individuals and migrant workers to work for their own maximum benefit, intentionally harming them, and possessing characteristics of an evil force. The criminal suspects used deceptive, coercive, and even threatening and violent means to successively detain homeless individuals and migrant workers. They locked the doors every day and assigned specific personnel to manage them, promising each person about 100 yuan per day as wages, settling accounts daily, but in reality only demanding they work without ever paying them. If anyone tried to escape or disobeyed orders while working, they would be brutally beaten with electric batons and rubber hoses.

Currently, the eight criminal suspects have been criminally detained, and the 18 victims have been rescued by the police and repatriated to their registered places of residence. The case is still under further review.
