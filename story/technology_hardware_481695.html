One-to-one assistance for children of migrant workers at Dongwuli Primary School

by cdbdks2012 on 2011-08-26 01:40:41

Event Planning

Event Theme: The transmission of love, the exchange of hearts, wonders gather here, and ideals set sail from here.

Event Background: With the development of the economy, cities require more and more labor resources. Many farmers choose to work in cities, making their own ordinary but extraordinary contributions to the construction and development of cities. Accompanying this social trend are a series of social issues, among which the education and living conditions of migrant workers' children stand out prominently. Due to the instability of their parents' jobs, children have to move frequently following their parents' workplaces, leading to feelings of loneliness and lack of mental stability. In daily life and study, they receive less care and encouragement. Additionally, since most of these children come from rural areas with poor family backgrounds, they are prone to being looked down upon by urban peers and often live on the fringes of the city. These factors can be extremely detrimental during the formative years of their personalities, often resulting in arrogance, introversion, and unwillingness to communicate with others.

Event Objectives:

1. To encourage children to establish correct values and great ideals, actively guiding them to make arduous efforts for their dreams.

2. To help students learn some simple knowledge and specialties outside textbooks, discovering their interests through extracurricular activities and providing basic cultivation.

3. To strengthen children's confidence and pride in life, discovering the joy of life, and loving life.

Event Overview: The Youth Volunteer Association of Huaihua College at Hebei Normal University actively responds to the call of the municipal committee of the Communist Youth League, taking Wuli Zhuang Primary School in Shijiazhuang as the base to practice the "One-to-One Assistance for Migrant Workers' Children" activity.

Fully utilizing the role of volunteers to make a humble contribution to the construction of a harmonious society.

The event is generally divided into two parts:

1. In-school activities. Mainly conducted within Wuli Zhuang Primary School, including organizing volunteers to interact closely with the children and teach them some simple specialties to enrich their extracurricular cultural lives.

2. Out-of-school extensions. According to the needs of the event, inviting the children to participate in artistic and voluntary activities organized by Huaihua College at Hebei Normal University, giving them opportunities to perform on stage and interact with society.

The event cycle is preliminarily scheduled to occur once every 1-2 months.

Event Time: March 5th

Event Location: Wuli Zhuang Primary School, Shijiazhuang

Detailed Process:

First Part: In-school Activities

1. Preparations:

- Organize volunteers. Huaihua College at Hebei Normal University is responsible for contacting college student organizations: Sign Language Society, Fitness Society, Handicraft Society, forming three extracurricular groups. Each group consists of about five members who will teach the children simple skills. About five event organizers will supervise the interaction and teaching process, ensuring overall direction control.

- Organize students. Wuli Zhuang Primary School is responsible for organizing students, with each group consisting of 20-30 students (third grade and above).

2. Detailed Process:

- November 6th, at 9:00 AM, a simple launch ceremony takes place at the school (within 30 minutes).

1) Host opening remarks.

2) Talent show by the societies, led by the host, followed by initial exchanges with the children.

3) Students share their impressions, further exchanging with the children.

4) Everyone recites the oath together until memorized.

5) The host announces the start of free activities, with each society leading their respective groups in skill-based interactions.

- Around 9:30 AM, group activities begin (lasting 1 hour and 20 minutes).

1) Under the guidance of small teachers, students learn the unique talents of their respective groups.

2) Simple exchanges occur during learning, offering timely encouragement to give children confidence.

- At 10:50 AM, group performances take place (within 30 minutes).

1) Host leads the way, each group showcasing their results.

2) Students share their feelings, followed by final exchanges with the children.

3) Final recitation of the oath, encouraging children to remain strong and strive hard.

4) Leave contact information, becoming their long-term trusted older brothers or sisters.

- Around 11:30 AM, the event concludes, with volunteers responsible for tidying up the venue.

3. Post-event Organization:

- Create display boards for post-event promotion within Huaihua College at Hebei Normal University, expanding the event's influence and encouraging more volunteers to join.

- Each participant writes an event summary, reflecting on experiences.

- Maintain continuous contact with Wuli Zhuang Primary School, understanding changes in the children's psychological states and summarizing event outcomes. Further improve future event plans accordingly.

Second Part: Out-of-School Extensions

- Depending on actual circumstances, selectively participate in various activities hosted by the Youth Volunteer Association of Huaihua College at Hebei Normal University, such as the "Rui Xiang Elderly Apartment" activity, the Youth Volunteer Elegance Competition, Campus Guinness events, and other college-level artistic performances.

Required Items: Handicraft materials, paper, pens, mineral water, sound equipment, volunteer flags, podium setup.

Personnel Deployment:

- Societies: Sign Language Society, Fitness Society, Handicraft Society, with about five members per society.

- Youth Association (Qingxie): One person responsible for equipment, ten people maintaining order and interacting with students while teaching them skills.

- Hosts: Two people.

Actual Event Record:

- Volunteers arrived at the Wuli Zhuang Migrant Workers' Children School under the morning sun. Greeted warmly by all teachers and students, the event officially began. Under the leadership of various societies, the students started engaging in serious yet fun learning sessions.

- Members of the Sign Language Society patiently taught the students gestures like "hello," "goodbye," and "home," which were the children's favorite words. Members of the Fitness Society taught the students simple dances like the Bonfire Dance and "Arabian Nights," allowing the children to enjoy happiness and increase their self-confidence, gradually becoming brave enough to showcase themselves in front of others. The Handicraft Society brought many handicraft materials, carefully teaching the students to create simple crafts, letting them enjoy the pleasure of creation and the joy of success.

- The event concluded with exchanges between volunteers and students. The volunteers left their contact information, hoping to help these children as much as possible through future exchanges.

Event Summary:

- This event aimed to encourage children to establish correct values and great ideals, actively guiding them to make arduous efforts for their dreams. It also helped students learn some simple knowledge and specialties outside textbooks, discovering their interests through extracurricular activities and providing basic cultivation. Furthermore, it strengthened children's confidence and pride in life, discovering the joy of life, and fostering a love for life.
