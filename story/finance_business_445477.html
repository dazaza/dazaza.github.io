NBA stars PK Slam Dunk

A "Slam Dunk" has made countless teenagers fall in love with basketball. Frankly speaking, up to now, no film or TV reflecting basketball stories can reach or even exceed the height of this comic. At that time, it was also the time when Stern and his NBA entered the Chinese market. The perfect combination of comics and reality made basketball in China reach an unprecedented prosperity.

Nowadays, there are still many people who love this comic and look for the shadow of "Slam Dunk" on the actual NBA court. In fact, Inoue started creating this comic in 1990 and serialized it in a Japanese comic magazine until it stopped in 1996, which lasted for six years. Based on this situation, it can be speculated that the comic should reflect the NBA at the end of the 1980s and the beginning of the 1990s. We cannot see from the story of the comic that Shohoku did not win the championship in the final national tournament, and at that time, the Bulls also continued to stumble in the Eastern Conference playoffs until 1991, winning their first championship and starting the Bulls dynasty.

Almost everyone who has read this comic will find a template for those characters in the NBA, but unfortunately, we were unable to see at least five NBA games every week like today, so it is difficult to give these characters a definite position. Therefore, for a while, there was a "hundred schools of thought" scenario, and everyone had their own ideas in their hearts. So, instead of saying that we are looking for prototypes for these characters, it's better to find a player in reality who is most similar to the character.

Hikaru Rui mm Michael Jordan Similarity Index:

Careful observation will find that Hikaru's face shape and Jordan's face shape are extremely similar. Basically, Inoue imitated Jordan's image when he created this character. The same shooting posture, the same dunking action, both wearing arm guards on the left forearm, and even, Hikaru once wore No. 23 jersey during training. Of course, don't forget the shoes under Hikaru's feet - Air Jordan 5. Jordan is the absolute core of the Bulls, and so it has been since he entered the league. After joining the Shohoku basketball team, Hikaru quickly became an important offensive point for the team. In addition, Hikaru and Jordan have many similarities, such as relying more on mid-range penetration and jump shots, not liking to pass the ball, and being highly competitive. Even their opponents' evaluations of them are surprisingly similar. Seita Shincho from Hainan said Hikaru Rui is: "The only one in the world!" Larry Bird said Jordan is: "God in Bulls uniform!"

Hanamichi Sakuragi mm Dennis Rodman Similarity Index:

The same rebounding maniac, the same off-court problem factor, Hanamichi and Rodman are almost carved out of the same mold, especially after Hanamichi cut his hair short, they look even more alike. Hanamichi indeed started playing basketball after high school, but his excellent physical quality and tenacious personality allowed him to quickly become an indispensable force for Shohoku, although the team only needed his rebounds. Rodman's amazing jumping speed allowed him to control the rebounds well, and Hanamichi is certainly no less than Rodman in this regard. In addition, both of them play very hard on the court. In the match against Mountain King, Hanamichi risked everything to save the ball, almost ruining his basketball career, and Rodman would often fly out of bounds several times per game. The only difference is that Rodman's offensive ability is more outstanding. This guy was also averaging 20+10 in college (NCAA Division II).

Kaede Rukawa mm Patrick Ewing Similarity Index:

Both have the same nickname. The tall and strong Kaede uses excellent post moves and soft touch, which is similar to Ewing. Whether on offense or defense, both can intimidate their opponents. Kaede's signature move is a "fly swatter"-like block, and Ewing's career average block number also reached 2.4. Kaede is determined to become a national champion, and Ewing also repeatedly launched attacks on the championship, but unfortunately, they both eventually became tragic heroes, missing the championship.

Takeshi Miyagi mm Peja Stojakovic Similarity Index:

To be honest, when "Slam Dunk" came out, Peja was just a 13-year-old kid, completely from a different era, but looking across the entire league, it seems that Peja is more similar to Takeshi. Takeshi was once the MVP of the junior high school league, and Peja also won the Most Valuable Player title in the Greek League; due to personal reasons, Takeshi once abandoned basketball, and although Peja was selected by the Kings in the first round at No. 14 in 1996, he didn't join the team until 1998. There is no need to mention their technical aspects, both are known for their three-point shooting and have some individual attack capabilities. Many times, we consider Takeshi as Reggie Miller, but in fact, their technical characteristics differ greatly. Miller is good at positioning and needs the help of teammates' screens to create space, then receive the ball and shoot. On the other hand, Takeshi belongs to the type of定点投手, does not require too much screen from teammates to take shots, and Peja is also this type of player. Another point, despite his outstanding skills, Takeshi's personality is not suitable to be the leader of a team, and Peja is the same. When the Kings tried to establish him as the leader, they even sent Chris Webber away, but in reality, Peja was not qualified to be the leader. The Kings, eager to rebuild, had to send him to Indiana.

Ryota Miyagi mm Tyrone Bogues Similarity Index:

At 5 feet 3 inches, Bogues is the shortest player in league history. Nicknamed "Little Bug," Bogues is fast, has good passing awareness, but his offensive ability is average, possibly limited by his height. In his best year, Bogues scored only 11.1 points, but his assists reached 10.7. In Shohoku, Ryota, Kaede, and Rukawa are the team's three main offensive points, so Ryota focuses more on organizing the offense. His scoring segment is mainly limited to counterattacks after steals, and rarely does he hold the ball for personal attacks. Of course, the short point guard will inevitably become the opponent's breakthrough point. In the match against Hainan, Ryota basically could not stop the tall point guard Murata. Bogues is often the first choice for opponents to break through, so any team will arrange their point guard to single out Bogues.

Akira Sendoh mm Tracy McGrady Similarity Index:

Putting Sendoh and McGrady together might seem mismatched, but if you carefully observe, the similarities between the two are actually quite numerous. McGrady is an outstanding swingman with strong personal offensive capabilities and great passing awareness, and Sendoh is similarly the offensive core and organizational center of his team. Of course, the most similar point between the two is that they always play on the field with a smile, their overly relaxed mindset leading to the result that the teams they belong to, although having strong power, are fundamentally without a championship. When McGrady retracts his mysterious smile, it results in the Rockets' 22-game winning streak; when Sendoh exerts 100% of his strength, Ryonan would surely become a super-strong team. Unfortunately, such moments, whether in reality or in the comic, we have only seen once.

Jun Fish Column mm David Robinson Similarity Index:

The same tall center, the same lack of toughness, both have a powerful opponent. Fish Column needs to face Kaede, and Robinson needs to resist Ewing. The only difference is that Robinson defeated Ewing to win the championship, while Fish Column could never surpass the mountain of Kaede.

Shinichi Maki mm "Magic" Johnson Similarity Index:

Just based on the yellow jerseys and letters on the chest of Hainan, we can immediately see the shadow of the Lakers. This team is exactly a replica of the Lakers. Amu is tall and strong, yet plays as a point guard, with outstanding skills, able to play inside and outside, and excellent passing awareness, just like a comic version of Magic Johnson. Magic Johnson is 2 meters 06 tall, yet serves as a point guard. These two not only have excellent skills, but also have very strong leadership qualities, and are qualified leaders of their teams.

Kazuhiro Moro mm Shaquille O'Neal Similarity Index:

In fact, Moro is only a minor role in "Slam Dunk" created by Inoue, but he is the one with the most ink among the minor roles, and also one of the few non-technical centers in the comic. From his dunks and strength, he is obviously a copy of the big shark, O'Neal, who entered the league in 1992, right in the middle of the comic's serialization period, so his appearance order is also relatively late. The most shocking moment in the comic for Moro was when he lightly bumped into Hanamichi who challenged him. Hanamichi came intentionally, while Moro was unaware and lightly touched him, knocking down Hanamichi, who is known for his strength and physical quality, to the ground. The terrifying power is undeniable. To quote a line from the author in the comic: "After this national tournament, Moro's name will resonate throughout Japan."
