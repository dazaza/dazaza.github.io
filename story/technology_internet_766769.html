UC Vice President Chen Shitong said that the Analysys Mobile Browser Report was bribed again.

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 21:00:42

Last week, Tencent took a stake in Sogou. After the merger, Sogou and SouSuo's market share in domestic search engines will rise to around 14%, approaching Qihoo 360’s 18%. Three days later, Zhou Hongyi quickly launched an aggressive attack, beginning to "block" Sogou through the 360 Security Guard. The official statement from 360 read: "Sogou Pinyin Input Method has the behavior of inducing users to bundle the Sogou browser. Therefore, it is entirely reasonable and legal for us to warn users of this risk."

Around the same time, another fierce battle related to Tencent broke out in the mobile browser sector. Before the Mid-Autumn Festival, data released by domestic consulting firm Analysys International on July 2013 mobile browser usage showed that in terms of “coverage population” and “total practical usage duration”, Tencent's Mobile QQ Browser surpassed UCWeb’s UC Browser.

Yesterday morning, Chen Shi, vice president of UCWeb, retaliated via Weibo, accusing Analysys International of being bribed again, and stating that "fabricating reports is an insult to all entrepreneurs and a crime against Chinese companies listed overseas. Ultimately, the victims are China's Internet industry." He also cited share statistics from various sources including iResearch, Frost & Sullivan, IMedia Research, Baidu, Taobao, as well as download volume figures from mainstream app distribution platforms such as the 360 Mobile Assistant, 91 Wireless, and豌豆荚 (Wandoujia), to prove that UC still holds the top position in China's mobile browser market.

Looking back at the third-party statistics over the past two years, this isn't the first time UC has "taken issue" with Analysys. In a report from April 2012, Analysys placed Mobile QQ Browser ahead of UC Browser under the standard of "market brand share". At that time, UC officially expressed strong opposition, angrily condemning the Analysys report as inaccurate.

In fact, among domestic third-party statistical reports related to mobile browsers, QQ Browser only appears ahead of UC in Analysys' data. Since this is a one-sided claim, why does UC react so strongly each time, even appearing to be "furious"? In my view, what Chen Shi refers to as "an insult to entrepreneurs" is certainly one aspect, but this emotional factor is merely used for "showing strength." More importantly, in today's Internet world, when two armies face off, any misstep could lead to total defeat, especially given that QQ Browser is backed by the vast Tencent empire.

UC's condemnation of Analysys and 360's move against Sogou are based on the same principle —— Tencent is too big, and any move it makes could potentially cause its competitors to crumble. However, due to management style and saving face, UC only points the finger at the third-party institutions publishing the reports. But someone like Zhou Hongyi, known for his decisive actions, always directly takes action, doing everything he can to minimize what his opponents can do.

Looking back at the 3Q War, it appeared that 360's "QQProtector" forced Tencent to make the decision to ask users to choose between the two. However, the real trigger was Tencent's "QQ Doctor." Back then, 360 Security Guard did not have its current scale. If "QQ Doctor" had grown rapidly through Tencent's channels, 360 would have been in serious trouble. Later came 360's "QQProtector." This method may indeed seem a bit dirty, but 360 survived and grew stronger. Now, with Tencent's investment in Sogou, the combination of "Tencent's strong channel + Sogou's strong product" has made 360 uneasy again. So Zhou Hongyi chose to use the same old tactics once more, regardless of moral condemnation.

As for UC's "nervousness," its conflict with Analysys is actually a covert war with Tencent —— Tencent doesn't always succeed in wiping out its competitors when entering a new field. A simple example is that before the merger of SouSuo and Sogou, 360 never attacked SouSuo. It is said that Tencent invested a cumulative 2 billion yuan in SouSuo, but clearly, this product never posed a "threat" to 360. This also proves that if the product isn't good enough, no matter how excellent the channel, it is futile.

However, "SouSuo" cannot be compared with "Mobile QQ Browser," which is a quite competitive product with clear advantages in the channel. Not to mention Tencent's own channels, according to my understanding, just in the brush machine market, UC cannot match QQ in terms of investment. Moreover, although Tencent owns super apps like WeChat, it has always struggled to monetize traffic effectively in the mobile end. In contrast, mobile browsers have a complete and mature business model for traffic monetization, and Tencent's focus on this area is increasing. Thus, it is quite reasonable for UC to think about potential risks while maintaining stability.

Conversely, despite having a great "father," the Mobile QQ Browser has failed to completely overthrow UC, which is relatively grassroots, over the years. According to some consulting agencies other than Analysys, the market shares of the two have remained relatively close. This shows that even without resources comparable to Tencent's, UC, which is transitioning towards a platform, has built some defensive systems using its own methods. Just how long it can defend depends crucially on UC's growth rate and Tencent's pursuit speed.
