With the recent large-scale temperature drop, while we are adding more clothes, we should also conduct a vehicle inspection and maintenance for our beloved cars. As the saying goes, "three parts driving, seven parts maintenance." If a car is not properly maintained, its performance will be greatly affected, and it can also create safety hazards!

Vehicle exterior inspection and maintenance:

1. If your car has obvious scratches, timely external spray painting treatment should be done. Because the function of paint is not only for aesthetics, but more importantly, it prevents rust.

2. Choose a sunny day to give the whole car a waxing treatment to maintain the paint surface of the car. For already damaged areas, do not polish multiple times; it’s best to repaint them.

3. In winter, tires become harder and more brittle due to the cold, which reduces the friction coefficient and makes them more prone to leaks or punctures. Check if there are any obvious external injuries or scratches on the tires, and whether the tire pressure is standard. Pay attention to regularly cleaning debris from the tire treads and replace heavily worn tires. In snowy regions, using winter-specific tires is a good solution.

4. Don't forget to check the headlights. If the headlight bulbs have dimmed, they need to be replaced promptly. Fog lights should not be neglected either. Brake lights are prone to damage and need to be checked and replaced in a timely manner.

5. When using windshield washer fluid, pay attention as well. In colder areas, it's best to purchase winter-specific products.

6. If the wipers have been used for too long, they must be replaced. Winter sees more rain and snow, and visibility is not always good, so the windshield view must be carefully attended to.

Engine compartment inspection items:

1. As the temperature changes, the viscosity of regular lubricating oil also decreases with the drop in temperature, causing increased friction resistance during the movement of car components. This leads to difficulties in cold starting and a decrease in engine power. Severe cases may even cause accidents such as seized bearings. It's best to change the oil in winter.

2. With the increase in difficulty of starting a car in winter, the battery is more likely to lose charge. Oxidation on the battery terminals should be cleared to ensure good contact and eliminate potential issues with starting. For owners using liquid-filled batteries, check the level of the battery electrolyte. If it's below the mark, distilled water needs to be added. Cars with insufficient power should be sent to the repair shop for charging in a timely manner.

3. Antifreeze under normal circumstances should be replaced every two years. In winter, it's best to go to a repair shop for an ice point test to determine if the antifreeze needs to be replaced.

4. The heating system should be checked thoroughly, and any problems should be repaired promptly.

5. Brake fluid relates to safety and cannot be taken lightly. High-quality winter brake fluid must be used.

Chassis performance testing and maintenance:

1. Carefully inspect the suspension and front half-shaft for leaks, etc. On flat ground, press down on the four wheels of the car with your body to check for any unusual noises.

2. Inspect the brake pads, brake discs, and brake drums. Also include the handbrake system. If there are no issues, use a blow gun at the repair shop to clean dust from the drum brakes. Uneven brake pads should be sanded down to make them smooth.

Actually, many of these maintenance tasks can be regularly performed by the owner themselves. Perhaps many owners are too busy or neglect taking care of their cars. People say, "If you don't love your car, the car won't love you." All the aforementioned precautions are actually quite simple and are not just targeted at winter. The Car Beauty Management Software Network still hopes that every car owner develops the habit of regularly checking their vehicle.
