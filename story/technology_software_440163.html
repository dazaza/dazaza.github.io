Nanfang Daily: What does "too famous" refer to?

Nanfang Daily: Did you prepare to resort to legal action at first?

Nanfang Daily: Have you heard of Zhang Jiangnan's claim that you owe his film remuneration?

Director Zhang Jiangnan

Nanfang Daily: The revenue from "Midnight Taxi" should be considered pretty good.

Zhang Jiangnan: He kept saying he would pay the last installment. Including before the New Year, he said that after the March box office settlement, as soon as the money came in, he would pay me the last installment. But later, he stopped answering my calls and text messages. It wasn't until yesterday that we reconnected.

Nanfang Daily: Besides this last payment, you mentioned that the other party also owes you additional compensation?

Nanfang Daily: But you still went ahead with the post-production work?

Lü Jianmin at the Shanghai Film Festival Industry Forum

Southern Metropolitan Daily: You said on Weibo that you have been holding back for quite some time.

Special Interview with Lü Jianmin: Bringing a Scourge to the Film Circle

Lü Jianmin: Yes, it is Zhu Wen's script. The basic quality of the script is very good, and many people in the industry have read it. The quality of what he (Zhang Jiangnan) shot is far below the level of the script. You can ask China Film Group how much we invested in this movie. He actually boasts about choosing the release date himself. I think this is a very strange phenomenon: what qualifications do you, a young director, have to choose the release date? We spent a lot of effort deciding the release date.

Zhang Jiangnan: I've been holding back for a year, reaching my limit of tolerance. Now that he attacked me yesterday, I must defend my rights and seek fairness for myself and my team. His defamation of me and other young directors is extremely unfair. Before, when the film received bad reviews, I took the blame silently, being responsible to the investors and the film, which is my self-discipline as a professional director.

Zhang Jiangnan: First, there was the incident where Simon Yam stopped participating, due to concerns about being scammed by Hong Kong staff. I was worried too. Chen Guanghong, the cinematographer, who is over 50 years old, couldn't possibly come asking for his salary. At that point, everyone had only received a deposit while the film was almost finished shooting.

Zhang Jiangnan: Lü Jianmin’s production team has always been his own. The production manager is someone from his company. The assistant director of "Midnight Taxi" was fired just because he made a mistake in scheduling Deng Ziyi (the lead actress), making her get up a few hours earlier. I learned about this afterward. This is his producer-centered system. Cheng Yusu (a young director)'s "Attic", which was supervised by French director Olivier Assayas, ended up being shelved after Lü Jianmin severely criticized the director. There's another film, "The Rooftop", starring Huang Bo and Xue Cun, where the director was kicked down the stairs. These incidents are all verifiable through physical evidence from the crew, and he will definitely not deny them. They were all done by the production department.

Party A

Threatened by text message

Zhang Jiangnan: Let's prepare both hands. I'm speaking up not because of the money, but because he accused me of wrongdoing first, taking advantage of young directors who are cheap, obedient, dedicated, and easy to bully, getting away with it and then turning around to bite us. My delayed payment is a small matter; I'm just standing up for a group of young directors who have been viciously attacked. I'm ready to continue voicing my opinions. He says we're unreliable?! I'll wait and see!

Nanfang Daily: In fact, such incidents are not uncommon in the film and television circle.

I want to ask them: Are you really prepared to enter the film industry? Are you sincere about movies? Do you think that watching hundreds of thousands of DVDs makes you capable of directing films? We've made around 20 films, 16 of which were collaborations with young directors. I will never collaborate with young directors again in the future.

I'll wait and see

Party B

Nanfang Daily: How was the payment schedule agreed upon at the time?

Zhang Jiangnan: He has always been like this. If he doesn't give, that's it, no way around it. Except for not going to court because I know it wouldn't help. For over 10 years, he has been too famous in the industry.

Nanfang Daily: Did they threaten you during the shutdown period?

Nanfang Daily: How much remuneration does he still owe you?

Zhang Jiangnan: That might be the TV series circle, I haven't encountered it in films. Unless the movie project failed and wasn't filmed. For the movies I participated in, I haven't encountered any cases of non-payment or delayed payment. Because the money isn't really much. When it reaches hundreds of thousands one day, it might be different. You can offer less, like this time, I could forego the screenwriter's fee, and the director's fee is also very low. But even so, there's still delay in payment, which is speechless.

Lü Jianmin: I think he is completely talking nonsense. By the end of this movie, I had already told him to leave. Due to his unprofessionalism, approximately 7 scenes were left unshot. Later, we had to find new actors and teams to reshoot, and for post-production, we specifically hired editor Yang Hongyu from "Red Cliff" to salvage the situation. Even at the end of filming, he told me, "I didn't know today was the wrap-up," those were his exact words. I really don't want to say more. Of course, I think this phenomenon is not unique to him, and I am not entirely targeting him.

Zhang Jiangnan: The TV series "Great Changes" is missing 180,000 yuan in screenwriting fees, only receiving a deposit. This project has been ongoing for more than two years.

Nanfang Daily: Always unable to get paid?

Nanfang Daily: You mentioned in your Weibo post that the Hong Kong staff and actors stopped working because they were worried about not getting paid.

Delay in payment due to missing 7-8 scenes

Zhang Jiangnan: Yes, threatened via text message. Records have been preserved all along. I won't mention the dirty words. Clean ones, for example: "If you provoke me, I'll teach you what it means to play with fire." Before the shutdown, there were production staff waiting for me with bricks due to a wrong route I took, but I missed them. After the shutdown, he continued sending text messages to me, saying, "I represent the crew to dismiss you from your director position, you don't need to participate in post-production." Then he said, "I won't retaliate against you. Go do the post-production."

Special Interview with Zhang Jiangnan: Accusing others first, I'll wait and see.

Zhang Jiangnan: To put it plainly, the last payment is less than ten thousand, just for the principle. Even a single cent is what I deserve.
