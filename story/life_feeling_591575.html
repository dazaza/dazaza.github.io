Birthday flower language

by liwuyou on 2011-11-28 12:06:49

Special Topic: Birthday Gifts

Birthday flowers, each birth represents a kind of flower. So which floral language do you belong to? Let's take a look together.

January 1st - Hydrangea (Laurustinus)

Floral Language: Hope (Hope)

The hydrangea, native to the Mediterranean, is renowned worldwide for blooming in the dead of winter as an evergreen tree. During the cold season, when one sees the pink buds and white flowers, it seems to be telling people that spring is near. Therefore, the floral language of hydrangeas is "hope." People born under this blessing possess great endurance and tolerance. They bring hope to many, and their own lives are very rich.

February 1st - Laurel Tree (Bay Laurel)

Floral Language: Pride (Pride)

In ancient Greece, the crowns worn by emperors and Olympic champions were made from laurel leaves, symbolizing honor. Thus, the floral language of laurels is "pride." Those blessed by this flower are both intelligent and respected, naturally becoming proud, sometimes even unintentionally hurting others. It is necessary to learn humility; only by understanding the principle of humility will the honor brought by the laurel tree continue to surround you.

March 1st - Leek Flower (Leak)

Floral Language: Service (Service)

The stem of the leek flower resembles an onion, with its primary function being edible. It is a favorite on the tables of the Scottish and Welsh. Additionally, Roman Emperor Nero used it as medicine to improve his voice. This plant, so useful, has the floral language of "service." People born under the blessing of this flower are good at social activities and are active leaders both in school and society. In matters of love, they also take the initiative!

April 1st - Mountain Blue (Annual Mercury)

Floral Language: Reform (Reform)

Mountain blue is a flower dedicated to Bishop Fugue of Cornoburg, who was involved in the church reform movement in the 8th century. When it blooms, it produces green house-like flowers, and its floral language originates from the spirit of reform consistent with Fugue's principles. People born under the blessing of this flower are extremely serious and straightforward, unable to ignore anything unreasonable. If even a lover's small mistake is criticized, it would be difficult to cultivate feelings. Sometimes, one must turn a blind eye!

May 1st - Red Campion

Floral Language: Friendship (Friendship)

Red campion is a dioecious plant, having male plants with only stamens and female plants with only pistils. To reproduce, it must have both male and female plants. Moreover, white campions often bloom alongside red ones, and their crossbreeding produces soft pink flowers. Hence, its floral language is "friendship." People born under the blessing of this flower usually have good interpersonal relationships and plenty of friends of the opposite sex. However, choosing a lifelong partner among many friends is wise.

June 1st - Yellow Rose

Floral Language: Good Fortune (Good Fortune)

Roses have played a significant role in horticulture since ancient times. The yellow rose is considered the best among flowers. Therefore, its floral language is "good fortune." People born under the blessing of this flower usually have good luck and can create a path to success without much effort. However, applying this attitude to relationships doesn't work. So, remember to put in extra effort when facing someone you like!

July 1st - Agrimony

Floral Language: Baby-like Cuteness (Dolikeababy)

Agrimony is a plant with apricot-yellow flowers, and its fruit is covered with a layer of velvet. Anyone or any animal passing by will stick to its seeds and spread them far and wide. Its reproductive method depends on others, so the floral language of agrimony is "baby-like cuteness." People born under the blessing of this flower have strong dependency and lack confidence. However, many opposite-sex friends like this kind of personality, proving that baby-like cuteness is indeed a winning weapon in love.

August 1st - White Datura (Thorn-Apple)

Floral Language: Moderation (Moderately)

White datura contains anesthetic components. Greek botanists' research reports indicate that patients taking appropriate doses quickly regain energy; double doses start producing hallucinations; triple doses drive people mad. This plant has completely different effects depending on the amount. Therefore, the floral language of white datura is "moderation." People born under the blessing of this flower are very serious and committed in their approach to things; when encountering a crush, they pursue relentlessly. But remember, everything should be done in moderation, otherwise it could lead to undesirable results.

September 1st - Sedum Plumbizincicola (Livelong Sedum)

Floral Language: Saving (Store)

Judging from how Sedum Plumbizincicola stores water in its leaves or stems to survive longer during dry seasons or when picked, this flower possesses the healthy characteristic of preparing for the future. Therefore, its floral language is "saving." People born under the blessing of this flower are planned individuals. They work diligently on a daily basis and carefully design future plans, never wasting their lives. However, life also requires moments of relaxation and fun. Occasionally allowing yourself to relax can add color to life and enrich your existence!

October 1st - Autumn Snowflake

Floral Language: Sea Wind (SeaWind)

Autumn snowflakes grow along the Mediterranean coast or are scattered across islands in the Mediterranean Sea, cultivated by the refreshing sea breeze. Therefore, the floral language of autumn snowflakes is "sea wind." People born under the blessing of this flower, like the refreshing sea breeze brushing against the face, have a cheerful personality and are also much loved by everyone. Such personalities suit clear and gentle love, overly passionate or unethical romances are less likely to happen to you.

November 1st - Bay Laurel (Laurustinus)

Floral Language: Skillfulness (Skillfulness)

Laurel trees are favored by carpenters due to their very solid wood and beautiful grain patterns; that is to say, they are suitable for making handicrafts. Therefore, its floral language is "skillfulness." People born under the blessing of this flower not only have dexterous fingers but also a clever mind. These people can overcome the pain brought by setbacks and heartbreak without being defeated by fate, living fully utilizing their clever minds.

December 1st - Carrion Flower

Floral Language: Self-assertiveness (Self-contention)

The petals are tea-brown with a yellow base, covered with a layer of red velvet. Moreover, the flowers are large, with diameters reaching up to twelve centimeters, quite eye-catching, just like a stubborn self-centered individual. Therefore, its floral language is "selfishness." People born under the blessing of this flower have strong selfish tendencies. Due to such obvious possession of oneself, there is a high possibility of breaking up with lovers after arguments, so it is important to note that sometimes appropriate compromise is crucial.
