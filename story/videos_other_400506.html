The development of online shopping

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 12:53:50

I think e-commerce is divided into four stages of development, and the combination is easy to remember.

The first stage is that "non-mainstream people" buy non-mainstream products on the Internet. Children aged 17 or 18 buy some game cards, decorations, and other non-mainstream products online.

The second stage is still "non-mainstream people" start to buy mainstream products, such as clothing, mobile phones and other mainstream products.

The third stage is that the mainstream people in our society begin to buy some non-mainstream products online because at the beginning of online shopping, we are used to buying some products that cannot be bought in offline stores.

The fourth stage is that the "main force population" begins to buy mainstream products.

Why does it reach the fourth stage? It's not us who will change our shopping habits. The first batch of non-mainstream people who started online shopping five years ago or ten years ago, from the age of 17 or 18 to 27 or 28, naturally become the consumer population of society.

Now there are 400 million Internet users in China, and almost 300 million of the 400 million Internet users are game Internet users, but the game Internet users in China have reached a saturated state. If China has 1 billion game Internet users, then China will be doomed. I think that the number of Chinese Internet users who play games is about 300 million to 400 million, which is already the top. There are less than 200 million online shopping people on the Internet, and most of the 200 million only occasionally shop online. Therefore, in the next few years, we will enter the fourth stage described earlier.
