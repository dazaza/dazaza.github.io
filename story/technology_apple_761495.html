New手机, Old iPhone, Apple's Charm Sparks Controversy

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 14:40:05

For a smartphone at 2500 yuan, there are many options available: gray market Android phones, legitimate domestic brand phones, or perhaps an iPhone 4? Compared with other smartphones in the same price range, the cost-performance ratio of the iPhone 4 remains very "Apple-like." However, when compared to the newer iPhone 5, it appears to offer much better value for money. For consumers who do not have high demands on phone performance but prefer the iOS system and reliable build quality, purchasing an iPhone 4 is still a good choice.However, this has sparked concerns among Wall Street analysts. They believe that this indicates that the latest iPhones are no longer as appealing for people to buy at a high premium. The Los Angeles Times introduced their perspectives.Vicki Macchiavello is an ordinary consumer who previously used a BlackBerry. Recently, she acquired an AT&T iPhone 4 contract phone almost for free. This might be hard for gadget enthusiasts to accept, but for Vicki:I am not one of those people who need to get the coolest thing immediately. After a short while, the coolest thing stops being cool, and then it becomes much cheaper.In recent years, Apple's business has thrived because it could continuously release new versions of products, especially iPhones, enticing consumers to willingly pay a higher premium. In the past few months, more and more consumers like Vicki have opted to buy older versions of the iPhone.When the iPhone 5 was launched last September, Apple announced that its sales reached 5 million units in the first three days, surpassing the launch figures of both the iPhone 4S and the original iPhone 4. However, the sales growth rate has gradually slowed down. In January, Apple executives stated that if the company had the capacity to produce more older model phones, iPhone sales would be even higher.This has led analysts to question Apple's ability to create compelling new products. Analyst Brian Colello from Morningstar said: I am not surprised that the current sales of the iPhone 5 are below expectations.Analyst Ben Reitzes from Barclays believes that last year’s prolonged iPhone 5 launch drained people's patience.Moreover, these older versions of the iPhone are eroding Apple's profit margins. During Tuesday's earnings call, several analysts pointed out that the average selling price of the iPhone dropped from $641 in the previous fiscal quarter to $613. Although Apple did not disclose the specific numbers of different iPhone models sold, a recent survey by a research company in Chicago found that in the first three months of 2013, only 53% of respondents bought the iPhone 5, compared to 73% who bought the iPhone 4S in its launch year.However, some analysts have refuted these claims. Apple stated that Apple consumers have high loyalty; once people buy an Apple product, they tend to continue buying Apple products. Carl Howe from Yankee Group believes that Apple's "black hole" effect will capture these users, and purchasing a cheaper older version of the iPhone will bring long-term benefits to Apple, such as in the competition against Android on the platform level.Speaking of platforms, some analysts worry that consumers who buy older versions of the iPhone may have a lower likelihood of purchasing music and apps. After buying the iPhone 4, Vicki downloaded only 11 apps. This is indeed a problem for Apple, but even if a cheaper iPhone were launched, this issue would persist.In my view, consumers buying the iPhone 4 will not become a major concern for Apple. Sure, the iPhone 5 is flashier than the iPhone 4, and Apple can obtain a higher premium from it. But if there were no iPhone 4, how likely would these consumers be to choose the iPhone 5? If they instead opt for other brands of smartphones at around 2500 yuan, the existence of the iPhone 4 would hold much greater significance.The featured image comes from: i-phone.kiev.ua
