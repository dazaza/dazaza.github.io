For the first time, an exoplanet with water was discovered outside the solar system, which is of great significance to the search for alien life.

by cacard on 2007-07-13 18:10:51

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, July 12th - According to comprehensive reports from Xinhua News Agency and others: British scientists disclosed in the journal "Nature" on July 12th that they had discovered the existence of water molecules for the first time on a planet named "HD189733b" outside the solar system. They claimed that this discovery marked a "genuine significant step forward" in humanity's attempts to survive on other planets.

Scientists confirmed the existence of "HD189733b" through changes in the starlight spectrum and obtained information about its atmospheric characteristics by analyzing the mutual interference between its spectrum and the starlight spectrum. They "without any doubt" proved the presence of water.

"HD189733b" is located 63 light-years away from Earth in the constellation Vulpecula. With current human technology, it is impossible to reach this "water world beyond the stars." Its volume is approximately 15% larger than Jupiter, and it has no solid surface, being a gas giant. It orbits extremely close to its host star, sharing similarities with Jupiter in these two aspects but having much higher temperatures, hence earning the nickname "hot Jupiter."

Dr. Giovanna Tinetti, leader of the team of scientists from University College London, stated that although the environment on "HD189733b" is not suitable for human habitation, this new discovery suggests that planets might possess more water than previously anticipated.

The mass of visible matter in the universe accounts for only 4%, while the remaining 96% consists of dark matter and dark energy. The temperature of stars generally reaches several thousand degrees Celsius, making it impossible for life to exist on them; life can only possibly exist on planets. To find extraterrestrial life, the first step is to locate water on planets, and then we need to determine if the planet has an appropriate temperature.

There are no "indigenous" aliens in the solar system, so people have placed their hopes on planets outside the solar system. In 1995, humanity discovered the first planet outside the solar system. Over the past decade, more than 200 such planets have been identified. However, among those where water has been definitively detected, this "HD189733b" is the most promising candidate. Scientists achieved this in just 12 years, indicating that progress in humanity's search for extraterrestrial life is faster than expected.

Since the launch of the first artificial satellite by the Soviet Union in 1957, humanity has entered the space age, marking its 50th anniversary. With the advancement of science and technology, it is foreseeable that the possibility of finding Earth's "distant siblings" outside the solar system will continue to increase.

I optimistically predict that within the next 50 years, humanity may be able to make contact with intelligent extraterrestrial life.
