Be Cautious When Choosing Collagen! Beware of Side Effects...

Collagen is great for the skin, bones, and even helps with anti-aging. I won't go into too much detail as you might know this better than me... However, I think it's still important to be cautious when choosing a brand because if there are side effects, it can be troublesome. Although my experience isn't particularly extreme, I'll share it anyway.

I started taking collagen when I was assigned to work in the U.S., coincidentally a good colleague at the time recommended it to me... As women, we tend to pay more attention to these kinds of anti-aging products as we get older. After taking it for a while, I did feel that it worked well (at least wrinkles didn't increase:P, and my figure was well maintained...), so I kept taking it for over two years.

After returning to China last year, I noticed that taking collagen was also becoming popular here, with quite a few famous brands available, which made me happy. The collagen I took in the U.S. was from an old American dietary supplement brand called Nature’s Sunshine, which should translate to 'Natural Sunshine' in Chinese. You might not know it as it's rarely seen in China. I'm the type of person who, once accustomed to a brand, doesn't like changing, but the supply I brought back only lasted two months. It wasn't practical to bring more or keep asking colleagues to bring it for me. So, thinking there were now many good brands available, I decided to try switching to another brand for convenience. After hearing some friends' recommendations and doing some research... I carefully weighed my options and finally chose FANCL's collagen drink.

After taking it for three months, I actually gained weight and started getting acne on my face!!! ORZ!

If I had indulged in eating and drinking after coming back, I would have accepted it, but I was just eating and sleeping normally. Moreover, the collagen I used to take served a dual purpose: it not only beautified but also helped reduce fat. By taking the daily dose all at once in the evening, it could burn fat, keeping my weight stable... I never expected this change upon returning... So, I wondered if it was due to the collagen. After searching online, Google showed a bunch of results about FANCL collagen's side effects... Allergies, acne, people saying it contained hormones, and even some reports showing negative protein levels during medical check-ups... It was terrifying!!! I was scared out of my wits, fortunately, my reaction wasn't too severe.

Of course, it could also be due to other reasons, but regardless, I dared not take FANCL collagen anymore and got rid of the remaining one-month supply... After stopping for over half a month, the acne on my face improved, but the weight I gained didn't come off... At that moment, I really missed the weight-loss effect of NS collagen, so I shamelessly asked a colleague going on a business trip to the U.S. to bring back a few bottles for me... I persisted with the weight-loss regimen for about two months, combined with irregular exercise, and finally managed to lose the weight again... What a hassle!

In short, I will no longer randomly switch brands. If I get used to one, it's better to stick with it, otherwise, it's really inconvenient. Fortunately, NS products are now available in China, translated into Chinese as 'Natural Sunshine'... Perhaps because they've just entered the market, I haven't seen them much on the shelves, but I know they're always available in Hong Kong... If sisters who are just starting to take collagen are interested, you can try my brand. After all, it's an old American herbal dietary supplement brand, and its formulation might be more natural than general health supplements? But honestly, I'm not entirely sure, shrug...

If you already have a brand you're comfortable with and it works well for you, then don't switch...

To be honest, aside from the possibility of side effects in a few cases, collagen itself is still pretty good... Um, my skin elasticity is still well maintained... Don't ask me how old I am &%#@%￥
