Actions without intent and actions with intent... when viewed from the results of the behavior... are there really significant differences...? Here's an example that isn't particularly interesting: those who intentionally continue reading versus those who unintentionally continue reading... how do their experiences differ...? The survey is ongoing... hehe.

1. Waterhead

On a Saturday morning, it was overcast in Shenzhen. Without any particular intention, I set off for the end of Dapeng Peninsula. The usual route is through Dapeng and Nanao, entering Dongchong or Xichong (not Xiyong). On the way, I passed near Waterhead. Because I didn't like the dining environment there, I didn't leave the main road and continued forward, planning to have lunch in Nanao, even though my stomach was starting to feel a bit hungry.

Suddenly on the road, I saw a newly revised sign indicating "To go to Dongchong or Xichong, turn left at the next intersection." Following the sign, I eventually found myself back at Waterhead. With no other choice, I had lunch at an unplanned location. Was this good fortune or misfortune...?

2. Nail

The most annoying thing about Waterhead is the seafood vendors and restaurant staff who solicit customers and block the way. But unexpectedly, after returning, I discovered that the right rear tire of the car had been punctured by a nail. The location was suspicious, leading me to suspect that it was the malicious act of a young waiter from the outermost restaurant in Waterhead. Fortunately, two days later, while washing the car, I noticed it and safely drove to the 4S store to resolve the issue. Was this good fortune or misfortune...?

3. Road Construction

While angrily criticizing the "incorrect" road signs, I continued heading towards Nanao. Unexpectedly, construction was underway ahead. The road sign indicated "To go to Dongchong or Xichong, you need to make a U-turn and detour via Waterhead." Cursing the previous road sign, I realized "self-righteousness" comes at a cost. At the fork leading to Dongchong and Xichong, I unintentionally chose Dongchong. The road was smooth, but soon enough, it turned into mountain road reconstruction. The left lane was dirt, the right lane sandy. At that moment, I immediately felt I should have chosen Xichong. Was this good fortune or misfortune...?

4. China Surfing Open

After navigating the rough roads, passing through the village, I arrived at the seaside. There were parking lots 30 meters to the left and 50 meters to the right. Someone opted for the closer one, only to find that neither parking nor going to the beach incurred a fee. Was this good fortune or misfortune...?

Lazily strolling along the beach, I spotted a group of black-clad individuals floating on the sea surface in the distance. Upon approaching, I could clearly see they were surfers. Strangely, there were foreign children and adults among them. Some were surfing in the sea, others drinking wine on the shore. I thought Dongchong had attracted Western tourists residing in Thailand.

The wooden houses by the shore were beautiful (the other buildings were horrifying). Unintentionally, I decided to stay overnight and visit Sanmen Island the next day. The hotel informed me all the rooms in the wooden houses were fully booked. As I dejectedly left, I suddenly noticed a whiteboard at the entrance prominently displaying in English "2006 China Dongchong Surfing Open." What a coincidence! Hehe, after all the bumps on the journey, it wasn't wasted. Was this good fortune or misfortune...?

5. Sunshine

The wind was strong by the sea, but unlike what I said on the road, it wasn't sunny at the coast despite being overcast in the city. Feeling a bit down, while fetching the kite, the heavens seemed to hear my confident remarks earlier and began clearing up. The temperature rose, feeling like a warm early summer day. Was this good fortune or misfortune...?

The kite took off. After the wind shifted, I became angry—why was the sun always shining directly into my eyes...?

6. Kite

Under unfavorable sunlight, the kite headed westward, vigorously dragging the reel. Nearly 2000 meters of string was almost fully released. The orange bat looked like a small dot, occasionally disappearing into the clouds. An eagle approached, observed, then flew away. Staring blankly at the kite, I pondered the implications hidden in the clouds. Was this good fortune or misfortune...?

Suddenly, I noticed the bat tilting left and right, showing a tendency to spin (later estimated that rain in the distant high altitude wet the edges of the wings, causing uneven wind-receiving areas on both sides of the kite; a similar case had occurred before). Reeling in the line required skill and was physically demanding—absolutely exhausting. It was more intense than a gym workout. Reeling in thousands of meters of line meant turning the reel thousands of times. In critical situations, rapid reeling was torturous—or perhaps a trial. Was this good fortune or misfortune...?

More troubling than the kite tilting was the sudden drop in wind strength. The reeling motion had already tortured (or trained) my shoulders, back, deltoids, upper arms, forearms, fingers, and palms until they were sore and weak. Clenching my teeth, I continued rapidly turning the reel, continuing to torture (or train) myself.

Who says positive, optimistic stories generally have happy endings? After the kite spun, it fell into the crown of a distant tree. After struggling fruitlessly for a while, just like fishing, it's best not to struggle unnecessarily when it's time to cut the line. I only managed to retrieve over a thousand meters of string—the worst personal experience I've ever had. Goodbye, Batman, who had accompanied me for over three years. Was this good fortune or misfortune...?

Looking for the next kite...
