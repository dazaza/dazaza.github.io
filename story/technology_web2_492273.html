Director Yan looked forward to all college graduates participating in the training to cherish this opportunity, seriously study entrepreneurial knowledge, and proactively engage in entrepreneurial practical activities. Meanwhile, Director Yan hoped that all the college graduates participating in the training could seek employment and start businesses in Yangzhou, thereby promoting employment and re-employment for more urban and rural workers.

On the afternoon of June 8th, the launch ceremony of the special campaign of entrepreneurship services entering universities organized by the Municipal Human Resources and Social Security Bureau was grandly held in the academic lecture hall of Yifu Library at Yangzhou University. Attending the event were Rui Hongyan, Deputy Secretary and Vice President of Yangzhou University, Yan Jun, Deputy Director of the Municipal Human Resources and Social Security Bureau, and Li Dejiang, Director of the Municipal Labor Employment Service Center.

It is understood that the number of graduates from general colleges and universities nationwide this year has reached 6.6 million people, an increase of 300,000 compared to last year, significantly increasing the employment pressure on college graduates, leading to a relatively severe employment situation. In order to implement the spirit of the national video conference on the employment work of ordinary college graduates and the requirements of the municipal Party committee and municipal government's key leaders, the Municipal Human Resources and Social Security Bureau (MHRSS) quickly took action, placing the promotion of college graduate employment and entrepreneurship high on the agenda, adopting positive and effective measures, and establishing a "one-stop" entrepreneurship system including entrepreneurship training, policy consultation, project recommendations, expert analysis, financing services, and business guidance. This encourages and supports graduates to start their own businesses and pursue self-employment. The first ten-day Start Your Business (SYB) entrepreneurship skills training class with 168 students simultaneously commenced at five campuses including Hehuachi and Yangzi River Estuary of Yangzhou University.
