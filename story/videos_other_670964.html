The瘦肉精issue keeps haunting, while a "crackdown" on瘦肉精is carried out in various places

by uefbp54187 on 2012-02-21 14:05:54

If nothing goes wrong, the 220 fattening lambs raised by Han Lirong from Xidiao Tuocun Village of Changli County in Hebei Province will be ready for market in two months, and she will earn more than 10,000 yuan. In February this year, Han Lirong found that 15 sheep "did not eat grass well and became thin." At this time, a white bread van appeared in front of her house. A man with a plastic bag containing 20 bags of white tablets, each bag with 100 tablets. Later, the sheep in Han Lirong's home ate the "symptomatic good medicine" which made them "eat grass and gain weight quickly". On March 22, personnel from the Animal Husbandry Bureau of Changli County tested the sheep at Han Lirong's home, and the results were positive. Facing the test results, Han Lirong admitted that the tablets she bought were clenbuterol hydrochloride, commonly known as "lean meat powder". Eating this drug does not significantly affect the weight of the sheep, but it can produce more lean meat. Lean mutton is valuable, while fat is very cheap. However, lean meat powder may leave residues. According to media reports, if people ingest a certain amount of lean meat powder residue, they may experience palpitations, headaches, vomiting, etc. Long-term consumption may even induce heart disease, and severe cases can lead to death. Among them, clenbuterol hydrochloride, as the first generation of lean meat powder products, is highly toxic and has more residues in animal offal. On July 29, due to "production and sale of toxic and harmful food", Han Lirong was sentenced to five years in prison and fined 70,000 yuan. Five years is the upper limit of this sentence. Han Lirong's husband Ji Gengliang said he had been working and did not know about this matter. But he also said he could not understand why the sentence was so heavy. In 2009, he also fed "lean meat powder" to his sheep, and after being caught, he was only fined a few thousand yuan. The reason why Han Lirong was not as "lucky" as her husband was because she got caught during a "crackdown". After the Shuanghui "lean meat powder" incident in March this year, Hebei Province and other regions across the country began a one-year special governance action. The political and legal system of Hebei Province required that food safety cases be handled "quickly and severely". Reporters found in their investigation in Changli and Ludong counties of Hebei Province that local farmers have been feeding animals such as sheep with lean meat powder for many years, and the scale is very wide. Drug sellers come to your door. Drug sellers know who raises sheep and can judge which old customers are out of drugs, and they will come to your door. Han Lirong never managed to say who the drug seller was. She just said the drug seller had a Ludong accent. In a material, Han Lirong claimed that these lean meat powders were purchased from Ludong County. The data shows that the police of Changli County once visited Ludong according to the clues provided by Han Lirong, but without success. And the animal husbandry bureau did not find those white small tablets at Han Lirong's home, and the clue to track down the lean meat powder was interrupted. In the local sheep-raising households, the drug seller driving a minivan was not mysterious and used to be a familiar customer of the breeders. Their selling of lean meat powder was not a secret either. Xizhang Village in Changli County is a large-scale sheep-raising village, where most of the villagers have sheep pens, mostly family-style breeding with one or two hundred sheep. According to Zhang Min's account, drug sellers often drove minivans to visit actively, holding bags and directly asking sheep raisers if they wanted to buy lean meat powder. One bag with 100 tablets costs two yuan. If you don't buy, they turn around and leave without saying another word. Drug sellers generally do not contact breeders, but they know who raises sheep and can judge which old customers are out of drugs. "You don't need to look for them, they will naturally come to your door," Zhang Min said. He once wrote down several drug sellers' phone numbers, and when he called to buy, he found they were all empty numbers. Zhang Min never heard where they were from, he judged they were from Ludong County based on their accents. After Han Lirong's case broke out, the drug sellers disappeared. Ludong County mentioned by Han Lirong and Zhang Min is a county under Qinhuangdao City, adjacent to Changli County. Ludong is a major livestock county, among which Haibo Township is a concentrated area for meat sheep. Every day, there are sheep collection vehicles gathering from places like Xianghe in Hebei, Jiangsu, Shanghai, Henan, etc. In early August, the purchase price of meat sheep in Ludong has risen to around 10.5 yuan per catty, nearly 1 yuan higher than last year. Comprehensive media reports show that since the beginning of this year, the supply of mutton from Inner Mongolia and Xinjiang has obviously decreased, resulting in a phenomenon where mutton enterprises and dealers rush to buy sheep at the breeding source. Hebei is among them. According to current statistics, lean meat powder includes more than ten varieties such as clenbuterol hydrochloride, ractopamine, salbutamol, etc. However, in the local population of Ludong, clenbuterol hydrochloride tablets are almost synonymous with lean meat powder. Investigations show that feeding lean meat powder by breeders is no longer a secret in Changli County and Ludong County. The Hebei farmer Han Lirong who fed lean meat powder to sheep was sentenced to five years in prison on July 29. Journalists found in their investigation that in Changli County where Han is located and the neighboring Ludong County, it is not uncommon for breeders to feed lean meat powder to sheep, cattle, etc. These sheep are sold to Henan, Jiangsu, Shanghai, and other places. Some sheep raisers said that sheep that haven't eaten lean meat powder couldn't be sold. Lean meat powder has formed a chain that binds together buyers, brokers, farmers, lean meat powder producers, and drug peddlers. The Han Lirong case occurred at the forefront of Hebei's special campaign against lean meat powder. Some farmers said the strict crackdown made villagers dare not "feed drugs" anymore. Some villagers said that after the "windfall" weakened, some farmers resumed feeding. Local grassroots law enforcement departments are considering how to make supervision effective in the long term beyond the strict crackdown. Public Secrets Breeders can get wind from brokers about when the regulations are loose and when the news is tight to stop taking drugs For Sun Guozhong, a sheep raiser in Haibo Township of Ludong County, feeding lean meat powder allows him to sell each catty of sheep for an extra dime or two. With more than 100 sheep, he can make an additional profit of about 2000 yuan. Sun Guozhong learned about lean meat powder five or six years ago. He heard that it was originally a human medicine used to treat asthma, called cough and wheeze essence. When pigs eat it, they grow more lean meat. Later, sheep raisers started feeding this drug too. He said back then buying the drug wasn't difficult; any pharmacy could sell it, and no one checked. Over time, "almost no one didn't feed the drug." Some people even put the drug directly into the feed. In Li Guangezhuang of Ludong County, a sheep raiser claimed that his feed was supplied by a nearby feed factory, "whatever you want to add, how much money, just call and they deliver." On August 7, a staff member of a nearby feed processing point said that they belong to a commissioned processing type and do not provide raw materials. "What you want to add is your business." A cattle raiser who was processing feed said that when people used to feed lean meat powder, they just crushed the tablets into the grass feed. He also guided reporters to see which pharmacies used to sell this drug before. Sun Guozhong said that in recent years, these tablets have become harder to buy in pharmacies. Drug peddlers, sheep collectors, and brokers have started doing the business of selling lean meat powder. Moreover, Sun Guozhong and others can get wind from brokers about when the regulations are loose and when the news is tight to stop taking drugs. Brokers are an important link between sheep raisers and sheep collectors. For sheep raisers, brokers are people they must trust and rely on. They control the market trend of meat sheep and can even decide how much profit the breeder makes. When problems arise, brokers sometimes can step in to resolve them, including messages like "the authorities are coming to check," which are mostly leaked by brokers. On August 5, local broker Gu Xiao Zeng said that if you concentrate on feeding the drug for a few days and stop five days before the sheep go to market, then feed glucose with diuretic effects, the urine test will be normal. Sun Guozhong said that his drugs are still bought from the broker in his village. Sheep raiser Sun Tian said that well-packaged drugs with trademarks mostly claim to come from Shanghai or Hubei. There are also unpackaged tablets without trademarks and bags of original powder. Besides, there are specialized drug peddlers, namely the person who sold drugs to Han Lirong. They said that now the quality of the drugs is also poor, and hundreds of tablets need to be fed to a sheep before it goes to market. "Originally, the tablets bought from pharmacies worked with just 10 tablets per day for a sheep, now it takes 50." Sun Guozhong lamented that the drug makers lack credibility. Underground Factories Produce Lean Meat Powder? The manufacturer on the packaging said it could be confirmed as counterfeit. Some villagers said that there were underground factories producing lean meat powder locally. On August 7, after much consideration, Sun Guozhong pulled out a bag of 1000-clenbuterol hydrochloride tablets from his feed storage. Knowing about the crackdown, he dared not give the drug to his sheep. This was what was left over from feeding a pen of sheep. The package indicated the manufacturer as "Shanghai Quanyu Biotechnology Suiping Pharmaceutical Co., Ltd.", with a production date of March 2010. It had a trademark and batch number. Following the phone number on the package, the reporter found the company's sales department. A responsible person said that the company stopped producing clenbuterol hydrochloride as early as September-October 2009 and no longer sells it. Another responsible person from the company said that according to national regulations, every sale and flow of such stimulant drugs must be registered. They could confirm that this bag of drugs was counterfeit. They also mentioned that they had previously received leads about counterfeiting their trademark and had reported it in Tianjin, "heard that the people were arrested, but later it ended without further action." Sun Tian said that although the drugs claim to come from Shanghai and Hubei, he does not believe that large quantities of drugs could be transported long distances to Hebei. He said that the lean meat powder he uses comes from underground factories. "At least the original powder is locally produced." Sun Tian once called the phone number on the package, which turned out to be a disconnected number. The factory address and name on the package could not be found. He heard from some local brokers selling lean meat powder that these drugs were locally produced, though the brokers did not know where the underground factory was. Broker Gu Xiao Zeng said that there was once an underground factory producing lean meat powder locally. The owner made millions in a few years and had "retired." He does not know where the current drugs come from. In March-April this year, Qinhuangdao City launched a strict crackdown on lean meat powder. A vice mayor said at a scheduling meeting that after "secret visits," the use of lean meat powder by individual breeders has increased. The lean meat powder network has gone underground, and its use has expanded from pigs to cattle and sheep. Director Gao Yunfang of the Animal Product Safety Office of the Animal Husbandry Bureau of Changli County said that after months of cracking down on lean meat powder, they have not yet found clues to the sale and production of lean meat powder. He is concerned that the source of lean meat powder might be in the circulation of human medicines. Deputy Director Tian Zhiyong of the Food and Drug Administration of Ludong County said that several medical institutions and pharmacies in Ludong County have long banned the sale of such drugs. The drug administration department had previously received reports, but no problems were found during inspections at pharmacies. Captors in the Chain "The problem is, if others feed and you don't, your sheep won't sell." said breeder Sun Guozhong. Sheep breeder Sun Guozhong said that he increasingly feels that the "lean meat powder" bomb will explode sooner or later, endangering his life and property at any time. He said that breeders invest too much, and almost all their savings are used for each batch of sheep. Once found problematic, not only will they suffer significant losses, but they may also end up in jail. Sun Guozhong believes that breeders like him are actually "captured" by brokers and sheep buyers, making it hard to get rid of lean meat powder. Sun Guozhong calculated that feeding lean meat powder allows sheep to sell for an extra dime or two per pound, meaning an additional profit of 10 to 20 yuan per sheep. Raising a hundred sheep, spending three or four hundred yuan on lean meat powder, can yield an additional thousand or two yuan. Sheep breeders said that a 100-pound sheep, fed with lean meat powder, can produce an extra 4 to 5 pounds of lean meat. The big money is earned by the sheep buyers. Sun Guozhong admitted that he willingly fed lean meat powder initially. He said that in the past five or six years, earning an extra thousand or two per batch of sheep was not a small amount. There was a time when lean meat powder tablets were unavailable at pharmacies, and sheep buyers would proactively provide them to farmers. "If the quantity needed is small, sometimes they give them away for free." Sun Guozhong said that five or six years ago, almost no one cared, and even if someone came to check, there would always be a leak. Later, people realized that the residue of this drug in animals was harmful to health. "But the problem is, if others feed and you don't, your sheep won't sell," Sun Guozhong said.
