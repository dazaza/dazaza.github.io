An Inner Monologue Letter

Dear Mom and Dad,

By the time you read this letter, I may no longer be in this world. I want to thank you both for everything you've done for me, especially mom for all the teachings and the silent tears we've shared during my times of helplessness. As I write this letter, I find myself shedding tears alone. The vicissitudes of life and emotions, above all, family affection, are expressed most genuinely.

Perhaps because I've been suppressing my feelings for too long, I've come to increasingly believe in the weakness of human nature. Mom, you should understand me. I've always been someone who dislikes talking, but gradually became more outgoing as I grew up. That was just a facade; in reality, my inner self is fragile and in need of help. Later on, I developed a love for writing, channeling all my emotions into my writings without anyone to confide in. Over time, this became my personality. I hoped to bear everything on my own, thinking that I had grown up. But nothing escapes your sharp eyes; eventually, everything comes out.

Mom and Dad, I also hope that I can grow up a bit more, become more mature, and make some decisions on my own. Perhaps it's because I've experienced life-and-death situations before that I now view worldly affairs with indifference. When I was seven, I almost lost my life, and you were terrified, yet you didn't scold me. However, I didn't take life seriously enough and lost many things. Now, I realize I was wrong.

Mom and Dad, please look at things more lightly. When I leave this world, I will leave behind much spiritual wealth: my novels and articles which once made you proud. These will remain as beautiful memories for you.

Mom and Dad, leaving this world is inevitable. Because of the increasing mental pressure, I feel like I can't bear it anymore. In fact, I really want to be a bird flying freely, but the higher I fly, the harder I fall. I wish to be completely free, though I might not even graduate. I don't blame anyone; I blame myself for being overly curious and getting involved in the homosexual circle. Initially, it was for my writings, hoping to experience things firsthand to understand certain truths, but this only added more pressure on myself. Only when I lost something did I realize how much I regretted it. Mom and Dad, please don't blame me. My inner world is extremely complicated, filled with so much emotion that has left me scarred and exhausted. When I can no longer bear it, it's time to say goodbye to this world.

I haven't left you with anything good. In your eyes, among relatives and friends, and even the village's grandpas and grandmas, I seem to have a good impression, but that's just appearances. Despite making mistakes, I don't seek your forgiveness; I'm grateful for your deep love.

Mom and Dad, I know what it means not to graduate — breaking the promises and goals I once set for you. In my current state, I can't achieve anything. Everything signifies an end. Mom and Dad, your son is very competitive, but this year, I might lose everything: scholarships, articles... everything has fallen into decline, disappointing you greatly. Hopefully, there will be another life where I can win scholarships and publish books.

A classmate drowned themselves, and a teacher passed away from illness. I don't know when I'll be freed, but I think it should be soon, moving on to another paradise. I'll watch over you from there. Mom, please take care of yourself better and don't be so frugal. You and Dad still have a life to live well. Truly, I feel unworthy of your kindness. I've made mistakes, and there's no chance to start over. Losing things makes one realize what's truly important. Mom, do you remember? My first article was about you, helping me get into the Young Writers Class. I know all the encouragement you've given me, but it hasn't replaced the overwhelming feelings inside. I am truly exhausted and wish to be free.

Son has made mistakes, irreparably. Really, the emotions I can't resist, the blooming and falling of flowers, people will die eventually, sooner or later. Mom and Dad, please don't be sad when I'm gone. Although your son is reluctant to leave you, staying would mean enduring pain. Take good care of yourselves and wish me a smooth journey. Let everything go with the wind. There are three things troubling me as I leave: one is your future lives, two is whether my writings after I'm gone will bring back something meaningful, and three is whether you can check my diaries on my computer and notebook. Can you understand them?

Mom and Dad, I'm leaving. Please take care of yourselves. Even if this unfilial son couldn't withstand the inner suppression and lived painfully in this world, I'm now free...

Your unfilial son
