The era of charging individual members has arrived! Is your website ready?

Today, I saw a report on iResearch titled "Three major portal sites test online book reading charges with a starting fee of 2 yuan per book." Based on years of internet experience, the author speculates that China's free era is coming to an end, and the era of websites charging individual members has arrived. Before this, some sites like QQ and Love Apartment have already started charging individual members. So, should the site operated by the author (www.coko365.com) also consider charging individuals? The author has always believed that the site would take the free route, but it is difficult to sustain the normal operation and development of the site through free services alone. Therefore, Caike Online (www.coko365.com) has developed relatively slowly.

If China's internet really has matured and begun implementing a personal membership fee system, there will be another thorough revolution in China's internet landscape. As internet professionals, we must consider many issues:

First: Is your website worth paying for?

The fact that the reading channels of three major portal sites have begun implementing a fee system indicates what? It shows that the content of the reading channel is now worth paying for, that the reading channel has gathered many members, gained recognition from many members, and possesses a group of loyal members. At the same time, copyright issues can develop towards regularization. Therefore, when China's internet industry begins implementing personal membership fees, websites must be prepared and adjust their thinking; otherwise, they will not be able to share in this pie because to make individual members pay, there must be value worthy of payment.

Second: Can free still defeat paid?

When a website considers charging members, it will definitely consider whether free can disrupt the market. For example, Taobao's free policy seized eBay's paid market. Regarding this issue, the author believes that if Chinese netizens truly become mature, or if the website is positioned as high-end, or if the website's content truly has value, uniqueness, originality, depth, etc., then one does not need to worry too much about the free policies of later entrants because any website's development requires market sedimentation. Websites without market sedimentation will have few visitors even if they are free.

Third: Enhancing member loyalty and stickiness

If a website implements personal membership fees, it will definitely lose some individual members. However, to reduce the loss of individual members, the dependence, loyalty, and stickiness of members to the website must be greatly increased compared to before. Free and paid represent a threshold for individual members, but this threshold is not very high. For example, 2 yuan per month, everyone can accept it as long as the member truly has a need. If there is this low threshold and the website itself lacks solid charm and value, then even without the threshold, members will likely be lost quickly.

Fourth: How should the website prepare for charging?

For a website to charge individual members, its uniqueness, irreplaceability, originality, etc., in terms of value and charm must be strong. For example, for news websites, timeliness and authenticity are crucial. The most important thing for a website is content, and content lies in originality, readability, etc., and irreplaceability. For technical application websites, technology must be strengthened. For example, Caike Online (www.coko365.com), which provides free online accounting and financial management software, is mainly based on technology. For Caike Online, content is secondary; the key is technology. The online accounting and financial management functions must be powerful, complete, and convenient to attract members and possibly charge them!
