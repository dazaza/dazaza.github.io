Will the former leading stock in the non-ferrous metal sector have a good performance this year?

by anonymous on 2012-02-27 19:29:04

It is new. Although due to the downward trend in the global economy, China's economic growth in the Year of the Dragon (Ren-Chen) will also slow down, and the non-ferrous metals industry will inevitably be affected, with great uncertainty about the industry's prospects. However, "great uncertainty" also means there is much room for maneuverability. If we can guide by circumstances and adapt to changes, the overall industry's prosperity still has the possibility of "standing out". Recently, there is a tendency for the non-ferrous metals sector in both the Shanghai and Shenzhen stock markets to rise prominently and become the core force driving the market upward. After the guidance of macroeconomic regulation policies last year, the structural contradictions in China's non-ferrous metals industry are gradually easing. The extensive development model that relies on the expansion of smelting output is shifting towards balanced development along the industrial chain. The production of raw materials for non-ferrous metal mines has significantly increased, and resource security capabilities have improved somewhat. The most prominent example is that rare earths are re-establishing their "precious" value. In April last year, the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation uniformly adjusted the tax amount standard for rare earth ore, increasing it by more than 10 times. This means that the phenomenon of cheap selling of rare earths, which has been criticized by many Chinese people, will become history. In the terminal fields of non-ferrous metals, current metal consumption demand remains stable. Except for the slowing growth rate of automobile production and a significant decline in year-on-year growth rate, other downstream demands such as power and home appliances still maintain prosperity. The year-on-year growth rates of production of power generation equipment, power cables, household appliances, etc., all reached around 20% in the first half of last year. However, China's non-ferrous metals industry still faces many heart-wrenching bottleneck factors. First, pollution occurs frequently, making it hard to support alone. On January 15th, environmental protection departments in Yizhou, Hechi, Guangxi, found dead fish inside the Lalong Hydropower Station on the Long River. Subsequent investigations revealed that the cadmium content in the water quality 200 meters before the Lalong wharf on the Long River in Yizhou exceeded standards. This cadmium pollution incident has sparked widespread doubts from public opinion about the development model of the non-ferrous metals industry. In recent years, due to excessive reliance on mining and smelting, and the lack of an old non-ferrous metals recycling system, pollution incidents have occurred frequently, sounding a warning bell for the healthy development of China's non-ferrous metals industry.
