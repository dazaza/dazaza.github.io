Things don't go as one wishes.

by dm0oz2808kj on 2012-02-18 22:26:57

On the day the woman entered the bridal chamber, she had put away her own shoes early. When the man took off his shoes to get into bed, the woman stepped on the man's shoes with both feet. Seeing this, the man chuckled and said it was quite scientific. The woman, however, said seriously that her mother had told her: if you step on a man's shoes, you will never be upset by him for the rest of your life. The man replied, "My mother also said that if a woman steps on a man's shoes, it means she will endure hardship with the man all her life."

The woman started to test the man, beginning with small things in daily life. She ordered the man to take out the chamber pot, and he did everything she asked. Whatever crops the woman wanted to plant in the fields, the man planted them. Neighbors whom the woman wanted to get closer to or keep a distance from, the man followed her wishes entirely. When the man was chatting idly with others, the woman called out once, and the man went back like an ox being led by the nose. When the man was drinking with others, the woman just pulled his ear, and he was dragged home.

Some people provoked the man, saying, "If you don't beat this woman every three days, she'll tear off the roof tiles." "You call yourself a man, how can you let a woman control you without any sign of masculinity?" "If she were my woman, I'd slap her with a shoe!" The man calmly replied, "Bring your woman here, and I'll be willing to slap her too." That person got angry and said, "Do you understand good and bad talk? You're like a monk from the previous life who's never seen a woman! You really don't seem like your father's son—afraid of your wife!"

Whenever there was something important to discuss in the village, when the man showed up, people would say, "You can't make decisions on big matters either. Why don't you bring your wife instead?" And the man really called the woman over.

The woman felt proud that she could control the man until one day she started criticizing the mother-in-law near his ear. The man's eyes turned red, and he shouted, "Do you want to know why I don't hit you? It's because of my old mother. My mother had a hard life; my father had a fiery temper. At the slightest dissatisfaction, he would curse and hit. He broke sticks as thick as arms and scattered chairs. My mother endured it all for us children. Every time I saw my mother beaten, I swore that I would never touch my wife. It's not that I'm afraid of you, but I can't forget what my mother said: women are to be cherished by men, not beaten by men."

The woman was stunned; she never expected the man to have such a broad mind.

No matter how much the man bragged and drank outside afterward, the woman no longer shouted or pulled his ears. Sometimes, she would even offer him a bowl of water. Someone asked the man, "How did you train her?" The man replied solemnly, "A woman’s mouth may yield through beating, but her heart only yields through love."

Have you understood the simple truth from this story? A woman who is loved... is happy and blessed.
