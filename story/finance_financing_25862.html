Monthly financial management insights for a couple with an income of 4000 yuan [Repost]

by fenqu on 2007-07-27 14:39:05

The financial management experience of a two-person world with a monthly budget of 4000 yuan [repost]

I am a college girl. Since I started university, I have been managing my own finances and have achieved some small success. Here, I would like to share a little bit of my experience with everyone. Currently, I am also just starting to learn about financial management, and I have been using a free online accounting and finance website (www.coko365.com) which can help us manage our finances and keep accounts. It is a powerful free online accounting book.

Step one: Subdivide income types and formulate a financial plan:

My boyfriend's and my salary belong to fixed income, totaling around 3000 yuan per month. Personally, I quite like the "three-three-three" financial management method, which means using 1/3 for savings, 1/3 for investment, and 1/3 for household expenses.

My extra income from working as a coach at the club is floating income, averaging around 1500 yuan per month, used for rent payments.

My boyfriend's bonus, since it is not fixed, is considered additional income, used to pay other unexpected expenses.

Step two: Financial Plan:

1. Savings. After comparison, I choose to deposit a fixed-term deposit slip every month. I have also thought about making lump sum deposits, but the interest rate is not suitable (the whole deposit interest rate is discounted by 70%). Although depositing a deposit slip every month is a bit troublesome, there are many benefits. Besides the interest income, after a year if we do not need to use this money, we can add the interest together and deposit it again, and the compound interest income is quite astonishing. Moreover, if we need to withdraw early, the loss of interest will be relatively small. This money is basically deposited as soon as I receive my monthly salary, so that it cannot be spent unknowingly. Many people think saving is unnecessary, but I think the role of saving is still very important. At least you will have a bottom line amount of money for emergencies, and you will feel very reassured. At the same time, if you need to take out a loan, study abroad, or apply for a high-limit credit card, it will also be useful.

2. Investment. One-third of the fixed income is used for fund regular investment. I personally highly recommend this type of fund business. Fund regular investment means using a fixed amount each month at a fixed time based on the previous trading day's net asset value to purchase funds. For people like us who do not have high income, it is difficult to use a large amount of money at once to buy funds. However, the threshold for fund regular investment is very low, 200 yuan can be used to buy, and it saves time and effort. Both Industrial and Commercial Bank of China and Bank of Communications offer this service, and it can be handled through online banking. After logging into online banking, all transaction information is clearly visible, which is very convenient. Personally, I have a relatively strong ability to bear risks, so my fund portfolio has selected three funds, two stock-type funds, and one stable-type fund. The reason for separately selecting stock-type funds is mainly considering the different investment portfolios of the funds, hoping to improve the yield rate through mutual compensation. Personally, I believe that when choosing stock funds, one must look at their long-term performance, at least more than 2 years, and not overly focus on the highs and lows of the net value. Funds with low net values (except those that have just distributed dividends) generally have low yields. At the same time, investing in funds is not a one-time effort; one should regularly check the quarterly reports and investment portfolios of the funds. For funds with low yields, one can consider switching to other varieties. When investing in funds, one must look at long-term returns. In the beginning, I was constantly checking the net value of the funds, always feeling that they were growing too slowly. Later, after not paying attention to them for a while, I found that their returns were actually quite good, hehe. So I think that investing in funds is a long-term accumulation process, and one must have persistence. Another point to note is that if you hope to hold long-term, you can choose back-end fees, because fund companies will reduce or even exempt handling fees for fund holders who hold funds for more than two years (the accumulated handling fees are not insignificant). Last year, I started using leftover money to trade stocks, and my luck was pretty good, hehe. I prefer medium and long-term investments, investing in some high-quality companies, and I am not very speculative. I won't share here, but I warmly welcome experts in stock trading to give some help and guidance (very grateful...).

3. Household expenses. My boyfriend is a person who knows how to spend money reasonably, and under his influence, I have improved a lot in terms of wasting money (thankful...). I suggest that everyone must keep accounts, because only by keeping accounts can one have a clear understanding of one's expenses. Let me give an example: many people, like me, may feel that they haven't spent much money, but it's gone. Through keeping accounts, I found that at first, I was spending more than 500 yuan each month on snacks, and more than 200 yuan on chocolate alone (quite scary). Later, I consciously controlled this aspect, changing some snacks to fruits, which not only saved money but also benefited health, achieving two goals at once, hehe. Through keeping accounts, one can specifically understand one's expenditure situation. Generally, after persisting for three months, one can summarize one's basic monthly expenses and start formulating a financial plan and budget suitable for oneself. Through keeping accounts and budgeting, now we not only live comfortably every day, but also have a small surplus in the household expenses part every month. Saving money and being happy is actually quite simple, hehe.

As for the remaining part plus the floating income, after deducting necessary fixed expenses, it serves as my and my boyfriend's entertainment and travel fund. Normally, it is kept as a current account. During holidays, we go out to play according to plans. I think going out to travel, as long as it is not crazy shopping, the general expenses will not be too large. Do not impulsively buy everything that looks like local specialties, that will definitely lead to bankruptcy, hehe (personal opinion). We prefer independent travel, where the travel agency is responsible for transportation and accommodation, and we play ourselves or join local groups upon arrival at the destination. This way, it will save some compared to directly joining a group, and it is more flexible in terms of time.

My boyfriend's bonus was used by me to buy insurance, buying him health insurance, accident insurance, and medical insurance, and I bought myself a type of insurance targeted at women. I am just starting to learn about insurance and don't know much about it, so I hope everyone here can give some advice (thank you in advance...). However, I personally think that buying insurance is buying a guarantee, and one should not overly consider the returns (if considering returns, it is better to choose funds and stocks).

Here is another experience about using credit cards. Nowadays, many credit cards offer rewards such as points exchanged for air miles, which is also a good way to save money if used well. My attempt is to fully utilize the longest repayment period of credit cards, using credit cards to buy daily necessities (such as going to the supermarket, paying phone bills, coal, electricity, water fees, etc.), and using the saved household money to buy money market funds (many money market funds can be used for short-term purposes), then redeeming the funds before the repayment date to repay the overdrawn limit. This way, not only can points be accumulated, but the annual fee of the credit card can also be waived (many cards waive the annual fee if swiped a certain number of times), and if operated well, there can be some small gains. This May Day, I will use the mileage redeemed by points to go to Shanghai with my boyfriend, saving the cost of air tickets, and feeling quite accomplished, hehe. Everyone can try, but there are two points that must be remembered: 1 is to remember the repayment time clearly to avoid being charged interest, and most importantly, swiping the card should also be done with moderation and without overspending! 2 is to suggest that everyone try to use only one credit card, as分散using them will not accumulate points effectively.

These are some of my experiences. Personally, I think the "three-three-three" financial management method is quite good. Everyone can formulate their own "three-three-three" plan according to their financial situation. As long as it is persisted in the long term, it will certainly make one's life comfortable. I hope it will be helpful to everyone. I also hope to discuss with everyone.

Finally, let me say something to the girls reading this post. We absolutely have the ability to make our lives comfortable through our own efforts. Financial independence will make us confident, and confident women will surely be more charming. Therefore, sisters, let's work hard together!
