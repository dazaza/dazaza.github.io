Whose face does the iPhone 5c's 45% "fire sale" discount slap?

by anonymous on 2013-10-10 18:30:13

Although Cook does not believe that Apple would produce "junk products," the market does not necessarily treat all iPhones as high-end items. The iPhone 5c, for example, is not a universally loved premium product because carriers have already started "liquidating" the iPhone 5c. It has been reported that just two weeks after the launch of the iPhone 5c, the three major U.S. retailers simultaneously began large-scale promotional activities, with the lowest price for a two-year contract model being only $45.

Why is it the iPhone 5c and not the iPhone 5s? Even less so the gold version of the iPhone 5s? Is this something worth Cook's consideration? In fact, although Cook may be stubborn in his statements, he knows the Achilles' heel of the iPhone 5c. Simply adding a flashy shell cannot change its predetermined fate. With the introduction of the iPhone 5s, it was destined that the iPhone 5c would play a secondary role.

The retail price of the iPhone 5c on a two-year contract through official Apple channels is $99. However, just two weeks after its release, Walmart, Radio Shack, and Best Buy, the three major electronics retail channels, all began discount promotions. Why? How could Apple not know the underlying reasons? The significant price drop of the iPhone 5c also placed Cook in an awkward position.

Best Buy announced that starting immediately, customers purchasing the iPhone 5c on a two-year contract would receive a $50 gift card that can be used immediately, effectively reducing the price of the iPhone 5c from $99 to $49. However, there are no similar discounts for the iPhone 5s. The electronics retail giant Radio Shack also announced almost identical promotional measures. The largest discount comes from Walmart, which directly reduced the price to $45, with the promotion lasting until the end of the "holiday season."

Although it is the contract model that is being discounted, this is partly due to locking users into two years of usage and network time. However, the fate of the iPhone 5c is far inferior to that of the iPhone 5s, indicating that its appeal to users is far from sufficient. Operators can only rely on greater discount strategies to stimulate consumer purchases. Without more incentives to boost consumption, the sales of the iPhone 5c are likely to be poor.

In fact, it's not just the "liquidation" of the iPhone 5c; even the popularity of the gold version of the iPhone 5s has cooled down compared to before. Although Apple still hopes to gain more market attention and user preference through scarcity marketing strategies, with the increasing number of competing products and improved supply capabilities, the price of the gold version of the iPhone 5s has gradually decreased. Currently, the price of the gold iPhone 5s in Zhongguancun is around 7500 yuan, falling nearly 4000 yuan from its peak.

The domestic market price of the iPhone 5c has also weakened significantly, with quotes in Zhongguancun's electronic markets around 4100 yuan, about 300-400 yuan lower than the official price. Neither operators nor sellers take the iPhone 5c seriously, and liquidation is a last resort because while sales are one aspect, profit margins and the length of time funds are tied up are more critical. Clearly, the iPhone 5c has a low rate of capital recovery, leading to price cuts.

Cook stubbornly claims that Apple does not produce junk products, but what is the significance of launching an out-of-tune iPhone 5c? I speculate that Cook had a few considerations: First, since Apple has run out of ideas, introducing some out-of-tune products is not entirely unreasonable. Since there is the gold version of the iPhone 5s as the foundation, introducing the iPhone 5c serves to highlight the premium nature of the iPhone 5s.

Second, Apple needs to maintain continuous market attention and growth in market share. However, the gradual saturation of the high-end market makes Apple lack confidence in relying on the high-end market to gain more market share. Therefore, it needs to target slightly lower markets to seize opportunities. However, the price difference is too small to capture the hearts of ordinary users. Whether in mature or emerging markets, the response to the iPhone 5c has been consistently lukewarm. Changes in market share mainly depend on the efforts of the iPhone 5s, which was probably unexpected by Cook. Not all Apple fans will continue to support an Apple iPhone that lacks innovation.

Third, is Apple showing weakness? Since its innovation strength has weakened, introducing a mediocre product could serve to highlight the uniqueness of the iPhone 5s. This might also be a strategy to indirectly boost sales growth. Regardless, the iPhone 5c will quickly be forgotten as a short-lived transitional product. Even the iPhone 5s, with the emergence of more competitors' products, will gradually see price drops and possibly more promotional activities. After all, the development of LTE determines that the iteration and follow-up of 4G products may faster influence user choices, especially in the Chinese market.
