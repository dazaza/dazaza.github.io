External Strategy: "All-encompassing"

I don't recommend product-type corporate sites focusing solely on rankings every day. Such friends, I think, would be better off going for traffic sites. Corporate sites need to learn the strategy of "surrounding cities from the countryside". Why? Because there are countless tools available outside the site that we can use, such as B2B business websites, free information publishing platforms, blogs, forums, microblogs, etc. We need to utilize all these resources to achieve comprehensive coverage, naturally ensuring no shortage of customers. Let me elaborate on how to use these:

B2B Business Websites and Information Publishing Platforms: For instance, the Website Builder Box provided by Kaiyue Interconnect includes website promotion. Many centers have shops, which you must take full advantage of. Once you open a shop, whether it's free or paid, you should manage and decorate it properly, making it look official. Finally, leave the main site link so that people viewing your published information might enter these shops. A regular shop makes clients willing to delve deeper and visit your main site, bringing both relevant external links to your main site and potential customers.

Blogs: Although the role of blogs in external linking isn't as obvious as before, there are still ample reasons for us to utilize them for corporate sites. Blog marketing is a method many friends have heard of, which promotes through blogs. Product-type corporate sites also need to leverage this point. Establish blogs in high-authority areas, preferably related ones, then post authoritative articles related to our products daily, strong industry-related articles. This will bring many customers who want to understand this aspect. Then find ways to attract them to the main site.

Forums: We can post soft articles on relevant forums, those related to our industry. This not only creates external links but also directly brings customer traffic. Remember, external links must be precise rather than excessive. Even if there are fewer relevant forums, we shouldn't post on irrelevant ones, wasting time with poor results.
