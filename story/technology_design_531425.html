My marriage story

by funwalker520 on 2011-11-03 12:41:41

Since a mature woman will not be attracted only by the nominal halo of a man and abandon a careful examination of his inner qualities, then when it comes to shoes, she would also adhere to a principle: even if a pair of shoes looks fashionable and appealing, she would never neglect whether its internal structure is comfortable and reasonable. What she needs to do is: First, make a practical judgment based on its appearance: Is he a practical good man who can share happy times with me or just a man with a gorgeous appearance but too many personalities? Or a man with good temperament and character but not suitable for public occasions? Then try them on like you would understand and interact with a boyfriend, feel if he is the one you want. This is very important. Someone said that marriage is like shoes; whether they are "suitable" can only be known after wearing them. Conversely, the result of wearing wrong shoes is almost as tragic as a faulty marriage. They will torment your delicate feet bit by bit, making you unable to take a step without pain.

On average, a person walks 200,000 kilometers in their lifetime, equivalent to circling the Earth's equator five times. And with every step, the body generates several hundred kilograms of impact force against the ground. The soles of our feet are the main receivers of this force.

To adapt to such an important function, the structure of the foot is more intricate and flexible than we imagine. Each foot has a total of 26 bones, 33 joints, and over 100 muscles, tendons, and ligaments. Under normal circumstances, we can walk, run, and jump freely. But there is a cruel punishment method —— wearing the wrong shoes. No matter how prominent a pair of shoes looks on the surface, as long as there is a slight error in its internal structure, it will cause our feet to ache, swell, or even deform.

Speaking of which, faulty shoes generally commit three major sins:

1. Cause unreasonable weight distribution on the feet.

Flat shoes place more weight on the back of the foot, accounting for about 60% of body weight, easily leading to heel pain; while high heels, exceeding 7 centimeters, increase the load on the front of the foot, reaching over 80% of body weight, thus causing forefoot pain. The fair heel height is 2-4 centimeters, at which time the weight distribution between the front and back of the foot is approximately 50% each, making the feet feel more comfortable.

Then look at the sole, draw a vertical line forward from the midpoint of the heel, it should equally divide the area of the sole, so that the forces on the inside and outside of the foot are balanced. Otherwise, one side will suffer from excessive pressure, leading to pain and other problems.

2. Weak energy cushioning effect of the shoes.

When the sole is hard and thin, the impact force from the ground directly affects the feet, making the feet prone to fatigue and damage.

3. Compression and friction of the feet caused by the shoes.

If the length or width of the shoe is insufficient, it will compress and rub against the toes, creating an unbearable situation. Over time, the stressed areas may develop diseases.

If you think that foot pain is too individual and normal, then you're wrong. Pain is telling you that something is wrong with your feet, and you need to change your shoes to eliminate the oppression on your feet. If the pain persists for more than a week without relief, you should still see a doctor, maybe you have already developed a relatively serious foot condition, and the doctor will tell you what to do. It is clear that the causes of foot diseases are obvious and easy to prevent, the most critical step is choosing the right shoes, which is as serious as choosing a marriage!
