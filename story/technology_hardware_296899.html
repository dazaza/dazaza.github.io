Choosing and Taking Care of Your Men's Suits

by baeyayljio on 2009-12-06 16:17:38

Every man should own at least one suit in his lifetime. There will always be occasions when a man needs to be formally attired, such as attending a job interview or a wedding function. When it comes to buying and choosing a suit, you should invest in something of good quality. You cannot afford to be cheap, as the common saying goes. Therefore, knowing the right suit is important. Being smart in choosing the right fabric for your suit is an important factor you must consider. Suits can be made from the following fabrics: polyester, rayon, merino wool, silk, cotton, seersucker, worsted wool, gabardine, and linen. A suit made of wool remains a common and great choice these days. However, different types of fabrics vary in terms of quality and durability. Tailored suits are customized to fit an individual's frame and are a better choice, though they tend to be quite expensive. It’s up to the wearer to decide if it's worth the investment. A less pricey option is Off The Rack Suits, which are designed with the latest trends and are always readily available. However, the main issue is that they may not fit as well as a tailored suit. Other options include single-breasted and double-breasted suits. Of the two, double-breasted suits are more formal, featuring overlapping front panels with two sets of buttons. Single-breasted suits are extremely versatile and come with options of two or three buttons, depending on the height of the wearer.

Properly caring for your suit ensures that you always look professional and feel good about yourself. Here are a few things you can do to keep your suit in top shape. First, check the instructions on how to properly clean the suit. Dry clean your suit about once or twice a year, ensuring you find a reliable dry cleaning service that won’t make your suit look worn. If you want to remove wrinkles, use a steamer. Steam helps remove wrinkles from suits, keeping them crisp and fresh. Before storing your suit in the wardrobe, let it rest in a shaded and airy place for some time. Avoid putting too much pressure on your suit. Don't put bulging items like keys, watches, and pens in your pockets. Avoid keeping fountain pens in your pocket as they may leak and cause hard-to-remove stains. Choose hangers specifically designed to maintain the shape of your suit. Ensure your suit isn't stuffed into your closet so it doesn’t get wrinkled. Some people brush their suits to remove lint, which is generally a good practice before storing it in the closet. Make sure you unbutton your jacket before sitting down, and pull your pants up slightly so you don’t stretch the fabric, especially at the knees. Adding a crotch liner to the suit pants prevents tearing from abrasion over time. Proper care of your suit is essential to protect your investment and ensure your suits last for a few years. It’s crucial for projecting a well-groomed appearance.
