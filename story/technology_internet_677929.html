Baby girl suffers from a strange disease and got fracture just after birth (Picture)

by anonymous on 2012-02-27 10:44:02

Xinru loves singing and dancing, but she can only achieve this wish while lying semi-reclined on her bed. This often causes her elderly grandmother to cry with deep concern. The reporter Chen Zhuo took photos, and the intern Gao Long assisted. The article is written by reporter Zhou Yuan.

In apartment 16 of building 24 on Street 119 in Qingshan District, when we knocked on their door, a little girl with two braids warmly responded. She was not shy and instead "invited" us to sit closer to her. During our conversation, when she became excited, she twisted her body, prompting her grandmother to quickly correct her, saying "Don't move around" – because even slight movement could cause her to fracture. She is a "porcelain doll," named Lin Xinru, who is 5 years and 10 months old. When she wrote her name on the interview notebook, her grandmother bitterly smiled – though she shares a name with a famous star, she is a pitiful child. Because they were worried she might not grow up, they gave her a nickname – Mi Mi.

**Fractured at Birth**

On January 9, 2004, the birth of granddaughter Mi Mi brought immense joy to Grandma Wang Tian'e.

However, as soon as the baby was taken out of the operating room, she wouldn’t stop crying. Upon examination, it was discovered that her thigh was fractured. Initially, it was thought to be an accident during delivery, but test results showed that the child suffered from osteogenesis imperfecta, meaning fractures would become a regular occurrence.

Though the baby in swaddling clothes was adorable, none of the visiting relatives dared to hold her, fearing any accidental harm. To provide nutrition for the child, her father, who was then working at Wuhan Iron and Steel Group, saved every penny he could to buy the best formula for her.

**Father Murdered, Mother Remarried and Left**

Time passed slowly, filled with agony as they treated Mi Mi’s fractures. On July 28, 2006, an unexpected tragedy struck, leaving the rest of their days filled with painful memories.

As her father was leaving work, he encountered a group of bandits trying to snatch his phone. In the struggle, he was stabbed and died after unsuccessful attempts at resuscitation. To this day, the main culprit remains at large, and no compensation has been settled.

Not long after, her mother remarried and left home, cutting off all contact.

Suddenly, what had once been a happy family now consisted of only an elderly woman and a child – every night, Grandma Wang Tian'e cried countless tears holding her granddaughter Mi Mi, becoming increasingly frail.

Sometimes, the thoughtful Mi Mi would ask her grandmother, “Where did Daddy go?” The elder could only tell a kind lie – Dad went abroad to work; once he earns money, he can treat your illness…

**Grandma Overloaded as the Sole Relative**

Due to frequent fractures, Mi Mi endured immense suffering – even during the hottest days of summer, her entire body was encased in plaster, unable to move. To save money, the air conditioner at home remained unused despite its availability; she underwent two major surgeries just to salvage her deformed legs.

Every four months, her grandmother had to carry Mi Mi alone, dragging luggage, to take her to Tianjin for injections, costing 3000 yuan each time. With only a monthly pension of 1000 yuan, the grandmother had to live frugally for four months to afford each treatment.

“Last June, when I took Mi Mi for a check-up, I found out she had a compression fracture of the thoracic and lumbar vertebrae. She needed a special lumbar support, which cost over 1000 yuan, but I simply couldn’t scrape together enough money,” said the grandmother, tears streaming down her face.

To treat Mi Mi, Grandma borrowed money from all the relatives she knew. Now, they are all afraid, as these debts are impossible for her to repay. Currently, Mi Mi can only rely on calcium tablets for treatment. The grandmother often eats just a bit of cabbage or plain noodles to fill her stomach.

Because of Mi Mi and her "son" who left, the grandmother suffers sleepless nights – no matter how hard life gets, she isn't afraid. However, she has suffered from coronary heart disease for many years, fainting twice while caring for Mi Mi. Her granddaughter is still so young, and she worries deeply about what will happen to this child in the future.

**Mi Mi: Yearning for Friends' Help**

To let Mi Mi learn like normal children, her grandmother carried her around, begging kindergartens to accept her. However, most of them politely refused upon hearing about Mi Mi's condition. Eventually, a private kindergarten not far from home kindly accepted Mi Mi, allowing her to sit aside and listen to lessons.

Mi Mi loves singing and dancing, but she cannot stand up. Every time the other children exercise, she sits and imitates them. Through her education at the kindergarten, Mi Mi learned to write and recognize numbers. She hopes to study abroad one day to see her "dad." Every day after returning home from kindergarten, Mi Mi diligently "does homework."

The grandmother says that the only thing she can teach Mi Mi now is to make her learn self-care and independence. Due to this disease, many children her age are afraid to touch her. Therefore, Mi Mi rarely has friends. In her eyes, every visitor to their home is her friend.

In Mi Mi's heart, her grandmother is the only relative she can see and touch.
