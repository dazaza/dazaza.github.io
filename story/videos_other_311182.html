Lee Chang-soo gives farewell blessing to Guoan and thanks the fans, frankly says that making friends with Chinese fans is a fortune

by yinding999 on 2009-12-16 18:11:26

Lee Chang-soo bids farewell to Beijing Guoan and thanks the fans, frankly stating that befriending Chinese fans is a treasure.

Yesterday morning, Lee Chang-soo bid farewell to Beijing and returned to South Korea. On this cold winter day, the most beloved iron coach of Beijing fans left Chinese football with a lonely figure.

According to reports (by reporter Zheng Xiaolong), Lee Chang-soo originally planned to leave Beijing last week, but due to his son handling school affairs, he had to delay his departure. This made the Iron Coach feel somewhat bitter because on the 14th, it was exactly Guoan's championship celebration day, so he chose to avoid it.

At 8:30 yesterday morning, Lee Chang-soo left the Guoan Hotel where he had lived for three years. Room 1803 had already been emptied. Mr. Lee had a lot of luggage, and Guoan sent a driver in a business car to take him to the airport. No one from the club came to see him off, and there were not many fans at the scene either. However, the former Guoan goalkeeper coach Tomić appeared, seemingly sharing a mutual appreciation.

At the airport, besides the media, what moved the Iron Coach the most was that some fans held up banners saying "Lee Chang-soo, we will always support you" and waited patiently. Despite the cold weather, the Iron Coach insisted on taking photos and signing autographs with each fan, without minding that he was only wearing a gray suit. Facing familiar fans, he could even call out their nicknames in Chinese and warmly invited them, "If you have the chance, come to South Korea for a visit, I will definitely treat you."

Facing interviews, Mr. Lee said, "Now I am going back to South Korea. I will definitely not leave football. Next, I will go to Europe for investigation and learning, and also watch the World Cup. If everything goes smoothly, I will continue my coaching career in South Korea." Would he return to China? Lee Chang-soo did not want to talk much about it, "I don't know, let's see the opportunity. If there is an edge, we will meet again." Subsequently, the Iron Coach waved his hand and walked towards the boarding gate, temporarily drawing a conclusion to his relationship with Chinese football.

Before leaving, Lee Chang-soo expressed his blessings to Guoan and thanked the fans.

"Thank you to the fans, thank you everyone!" Yesterday morning, just before leaving China, what Lee Chang-soo said the most was gratitude, thanking the fans who cared about him.

Lee Chang-soo said, "The time in China was unforgettable, whether it was Chongqing, Qingdao, or Beijing. For so long, I have regarded China as my second hometown. My fame in Beijing is greater than in Seoul, which is all thanks to the Beijing fans. I fully understand their feelings towards the club. When the team plays well, they cheer; when they play poorly, they get angry. I understand that this is a sincere love for the team. My biggest gain in China is making so many fans, which is a treasure for my life."

Not leading Guoan to win the championship became a thorn in Lee Chang-soo's heart, "It's not true that I have no regrets, but that's football. Being dismissed halfway through my tenure in Beijing was the biggest setback in my coaching career, but I will absolutely never say anything bad about Guoan. Guoan is a pretty good club. I hope they can continue to win championships in both the CSL and AFC Champions League. Wishing Guoan good luck."

The team arrived late and missed each other.

Just as Lee Chang-soo boarded the flight to South Korea, Beijing Guoan also arrived at the airport, preparing to return to Kunming for continued winter training. However, due to time constraints, they and Lee Chang-soo passed each other by.

According to arrangements, Guoan would return to Hai Gen yesterday to continue physical training and prepare for the YOYO test organized by the Football Association in two weeks. Head coach Hong Yuanshuo hopes the entire team can pass at once, then the team will return to Beijing on the 30th for a short rest. Guoan is currently actively contacting locations for overseas training, followed by starting recruitment work and tactical drills.

The recruitment issue that Beijing fans are most concerned about has already taken shape. Ryan Griffiths (Younger Griffiths) has basically been determined to continue playing for Guoan next season, Maji also will return to China to report soon, Jinny from Chongqing Lifan, Otto from Zhejiang Greentown, and Ramirez from Guangzhou Pharmaceutical are all in contact with Guoan. The club will decide based on the specific situation of the coaching group and negotiations.

In fact, Lee Chang-soo knew that the Guoan team would arrive at the airport shortly after, but he clearly avoided this question, "Now the relationship with the players is different. I am no longer the head coach, so it is actually more relaxed. We are still friends, and we can still make calls later, so whether to send off is not important."

Winning the championship also has Old Li's contribution

After Lee Chang-soo left China, whether it was the Guoan Tieba on Baidu or other forums, a large number of fans posted messages expressing their longing for the Iron Coach.

From being dismissed from Guoan to today's departure from Beijing, in these three months, Lee Chang-soo stayed in China to accompany his son to complete his studies. During this period, every move of his touched the hearts of Guoan fans. At the Guoan championship thank-you banquet the other night, fans were still shouting Lee Chang-soo's name at the scene. In recent days, many fans called hoping to see Lee Chang-soo off, but most were politely declined. It was not that Lee Chang-soo didn't want to, but recently he was too busy to handle everything, "I can only apologize to the fans. There are many things to be sorted out, and there are also a lot of miscellaneous affairs, really no time. If there is an opportunity in the future, I will gather with everyone."

In many fan forums, many fans stated that Guoan's championship victory was due to Lee Chang-soo's contributions. A Beijing fan named "Guoan Iron Rod" said, "Guoan fans will not forget Lee Chang-soo, will not forget the foundation he laid for the championship. Although there were some stubbornness and shortcomings in personnel use and tactics, this cannot erase Mr. Lee's contributions."
