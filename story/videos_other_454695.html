Sudden bus brake sends 5 passengers to hospital, 2 with waist fracture (Picture)

by jyq2011668 on 2011-07-01 10:45:36

The injured passengers are receiving medical treatment at the hospital.

At around 5:20 PM on February 15th, a bus No. 320 was driving near the intersection of Jiaozhou Road and Zhongshan Road when it was suddenly cut off by a car that changed lanes abruptly. The bus had to brake sharply causing many passengers on board to fall down like dominoes. Subsequently, five passengers were sent to the Orthopedic Hospital by the 120 emergency ambulance for examination and treatment, among whom two female passengers suffered from lumbar fractures and have been hospitalized for treatment.

Five passengers were rushed to the hospital

At around 5:50 PM on the 15th, our newspaper received a report from a citizen stating that due to sudden braking, many passengers on a No. 320 bus were shaken off their feet, and five injured passengers had already been taken to the hospital. In order to direct traffic, the two involved vehicles had been towed away from the scene.

Upon receiving the report, the journalist then rushed to the Orthopedic Hospital where in the emergency room, Mr. Yan lying on a stretcher preparing for an X-ray examination was seen. "My left knee hurts so much that I can't walk," Mr. Yan said, "It feels like being pricked by needles as soon as it touches the ground."

In the X-ray room of the hospital, the journalist also met three other citizens who were injured in the accident, all waiting for their X-ray examinations.

Passengers fell all over the bus

"My waist now hurts terribly, and my stomach also feels very painful," Ms. Zhang told reporters that at around 5:20 PM, there were many passengers on the No. 320 bus she was riding. She was standing at the back of the bus, and the sudden sharp braking prevented her from grabbing the handrail, causing her entire body to fall forward onto Mr. Yan. Meanwhile, a passenger next to her directly collided with her stomach, and her waist hit the ground while Mr. Yan's knee knelt on the bus, resulting in injury.

"At that moment, the situation was like collapsing dominoes, people piled up and fell together," a female passenger who did not want to disclose her name told the reporter. At that time, she and her boyfriend collided with Ms. Zhang. Next to her, another female passenger was more seriously injured and seemed to have been transferred to another department for examination.

Car driver lost focus

At the hospital, the journalist met the insurance officer of the bus company who came to handle this traffic accident. It was introduced that at that time, a car suddenly cut in front of the bus, forcing the bus driver to brake urgently, which led to such an unexpected incident. The car driver who came along with him was there to pay the examination fees for the injured passengers.

This unnamed car driver said that at the time of the incident, he was driving parallel to the bus. Since his child had eczema and was seeing a doctor at the hospital, he was about to go to the city hospital. However, preoccupied with thoughts, he suddenly turned the steering wheel to the right to change lanes, which caused the bus to be blocked. "I was too anxious at the time, otherwise such an incident wouldn't have happened," the man said.

Two seriously injured need hospitalization

At around 6:20 PM that evening, a woman was pushed into the emergency surgery room on a hospital bed, her face showing extreme pain. When the on-duty doctor examined her, the woman kept groaning in pain. According to her family, she is surnamed Jiang, 27 years old. During the sudden braking of the bus, her waist hit the seat of the bus, and many people fell on top of her. After an X-ray examination, it was found that her fourth lumbar spinous process was fractured, requiring hospitalization for treatment.

The journalist subsequently learned that the female passenger surnamed Zhang was found to have a lumbar fracture during the examination and needed to be hospitalized for observation and treatment. The other three injured passengers had soft tissue injuries and could rest at home after taking some medication.

Reporters Lin Gang and Bao Fuyu
