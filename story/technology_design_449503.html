Student organizations Anime Character World Cup, Sun Wukong as the playmaker and The Lion King as the referee.

by zzf0zxyx80 on 2011-06-21 13:02:47

"Goal!" someone exclaimed outside the court. The attacking midfielder was Monkey King, the referee was Lion King, and the goalkeeper was Kung Fu Panda with Hulk. Yesterday morning, an intense "World Cup of Anime Characters" took place at Changsha Normal School, leaving people momentarily unsure where they were. This special event was organized by 100 university students majoring in Fine Arts Animation. All participating players were anime characters, crafted over a month using clay by the students.

Reported by Wu Ke from this newspaper in Changsha,

Regret: Too few local Chinese anime characters

The game started as the goalkeeper, Kung Fu Panda Po, passed the ball to the defender, female hippo Gloria from "Madagascar". She swayed her big hips and headed the ball to the striker, Hongmao. The cat grinned as he "split" the ball to the attacking midfielder, Monkey King. Just as Monkey King was about to pass the ball, it was intercepted by Po's senior sister "Tigress" who slid to tackle. The referee, Lion King, proudly blew his whistle. Avatar Na'vi and Popeye charged ahead, facing off against the opposing goalkeeper, Hulk. The fans in the stands were relatives, friends, or enemies of the on-field "players", including "Japanese fans" like Chihiro from "Spirited Away" and Doraemon, but more were "American fans", some with football-adorned hats and others waving team banners.

According to student Jiang studying Fine Arts Animation, "At the beginning of this semester, the teacher assigned a task, giving each of the 100 students one role to create within a month. We chose 100 roles from 20 animated films and TV shows. Then we selected the currently popular 'World Cup' as the theme and designed each character accordingly."

The students felt slightly regretful after completing their "World Cup of Anime Characters" - there were too few local Chinese anime characters to choose from. Student Jiang said, "After picking and choosing, we found that there were only one or two with distinct personalities."

Insight: The hardest to sculpt was "Hulk"

Student Jiang looked at "defender" Gloria on the field with satisfaction. "I'm very satisfied with her!" she said.

The reason Jiang chose Gloria, the adorable female hippo from "Madagascar," was because "her belly is so big and round." However, sculpting the smooth belly of the hippo proved not as easy as imagined. "There would be gaps between the clay, so new clay had to be used to fill them, but then it wouldn't be smooth, requiring a lot of effort to 'smooth out the belly.'" After much smoothing, Gloria's round belly finally came into being, and Jiang expressed satisfaction.

The character unanimously considered the hardest to sculpt was Hulk. "Since the character is realistic, knowledge of anatomy is required, but the real challenge wasn't just that." As the goalkeeper, Hulk's pose with arms spread wide needed to display his powerful side, thus requiring a design that conveyed the "bulging muscles" feel, which troubled the creator. "We talked most about texture, texture, and then coloring, all detailed work," explained a participating student squatting by the "field."

Reason: Doing this to cultivate interest

Teacher Qian, who planned this "World Cup," explained that the reason for having the students do such a project was to stimulate their interest and achieve the purpose of cultivating three-dimensional spatial thinking and practical skills. "We were given a two-dimensional image of a character, and we needed to restore it into three dimensions while sculpting it," one student introduced. Through participating in this production, he felt quite accomplished and indicated that next time, he would spend more time making something even more complex.
