The scene was in disarray. The world remained silent. A man was pouring water onto the car.

"This is suspicious," said the car owner, "I received a call from the parking lot saying 'your car is on fire.'"

The enthusiastic one said: "Your car caught fire first, it was due to a short circuit." The one assigned to put out the fire said: "I'm just temporarily helping out at the parking lot."

The person collecting the parking fee said: "I'm just here for fun."

The police said: "We are currently in contact with the parking lot manager and investigating the nature of this parking lot."

The flames rose to over 20 meters high, accompanied by continuous 'bang! bang!' explosion sounds. Many people ran out in their pajamas. At around 11 p.m. on the evening of the 7th, a big fire broke out at the Jinali Parking Lot in Jinhua No.2 Group, Tianhui Town. Three trucks were destroyed, one of which was carrying 144 brand new Yamaha motorcycles, all of which were burned to bare frames. The total loss of the three vehicles was estimated to be close to 3 million RMB. Currently, Chengdu Fire Department and Tianhui Town Police Station are investigating the cause of the accident.

Incident

A midnight fire with flames rising 20 meters

The villagers' residential area is less than 100 meters away from the Jinali Parking Lot in Chengdu, "It was terrifying, many people ran out in their pajamas, the flames were over 20 meters high. There were constant 'bang! bang!' explosion sounds, it felt like the building was shaking." said villager Li Bencheng, "Fire trucks kept coming, it wasn't until about 1 a.m. (the next morning) that the fire was extinguished."

"We arrived when the perimeter was already cordoned off with caution tape," another villager, Li Haiqing introduced, "We didn't dare to get too close, the fire was intense, and there were still explosion sounds."

Extinguishing the Fire

Flammable materials, lack of water, the fire took 2 hours to extinguish

After receiving the public's alarm that night, Fire Special Operations Team 4 dispatched 5 fire trucks to the scene. Three trucks were on fire, covering an area of approximately 150 square meters.

Each motorcycle contained a small amount of gasoline, and each motorcycle had a wooden frame fixed on it, thus forming a three-dimensional fire.

Due to the remote location of the fire site and the lack of nearby water sources, more fire trucks arrived at the scene. These emergency support fire trucks were mainly responsible for providing water sources. After 2 hours, the fire was finally extinguished.

Scene

144 new motorcycles burned down to bare frames

At noon yesterday, inside the Jinali Parking Lot in Chengdu, the three completely destroyed trucks were still parked in place.

The truck parked at the front was a Liberation brand truck, originally carrying 144 brand new Yamaha motorcycles, which were now all burned down to bare frames.

To the left of the Liberation truck was a Howo A7 truck, whose cab had been burned into a hollow frame, and the cargo loaded at the rear was also burned down to just the iron wire mesh. Another truck's cab was also destroyed by the fire, but its rear seemed to have not been significantly affected.

"This matter hasn't yet dared to tell the family, if they knew, they definitely wouldn't be able to eat." - Truck Owner

Both owners bought their trucks on loan

Several anxious-looking men were guarding the already destroyed trucks at the parking lot.

"I am the owner of the Liberation truck, we transported goods from Chongqing to Chengdu yesterday. Since we couldn't unload in the afternoon, we parked the car here at around 6 p.m. yesterday. We planned to unload at Shuangliu early in the morning today." said Henan owner Wu Chengliang, he and his nephew Wu Hongwei co-bought this truck on loan in 2006. The truck itself is worth 4.3 million yuan, and there were also 144 brand new Yamaha motorcycles on board, valued at 1.1 million yuan. "This matter hasn't yet dared to tell the family, if they knew, they definitely wouldn't be able to eat."

Not long after, Zhang Wenjie, the owner of the Howo A7 truck, also appeared at the parking lot. "My car is worth 5.7 million yuan, I bought it last year on loan. The cargo on the car was environmental protection equipment transported from Zhejiang, valued at over 4 million yuan."

According to the estimates of the truck drivers, the value of the other burned truck was also around 5 million yuan. Thus calculated, the losses of the three vehicles plus the already destroyed cargo have approached 3 million yuan.

Some Suspicious Points > >>

The car owner claims to have received a phone call from the parking lot notifying them

"The car is going to catch fire"

"At around 11 p.m. (on the 7th), I suddenly received a call from the parking lot, saying our car is going to catch fire. In about 5 minutes, we arrived at the parking lot, and the car was already burned like this." recalled the owner of the Liberation truck, Wu Chengliang.

However, Zhang Wenjie, the owner of the Howo A7 truck, said: "I only received the call from the parking lot at 1:30 a.m. (on the 8th), and by the time I arrived at the parking lot, it was already completely burned." Zhang Wenjie said: "(The parking lot) also said my car caught fire first, so I asked him, why did you notify me last?"

At this moment, a red tank-top clad man who claimed to be a bystander suddenly spoke with certainty to Zhang Wenjie: "It was your car that caught fire first, it was due to a short circuit causing the fire." For this, Zhang Wenjie completely could not accept, "My car is only one year old, and diesel cars are not easy to spontaneously combust. My car automatically disconnects power once the key is removed."

However, Yang Shiquan, the security guard at the parking lot, stated that he was not on duty that night, nor did he have the telephone number of the parking lot manager or the on-duty security guard.

At the accident scene, a police officer from the Tianhui Town Police Station said: "The police are currently in contact with the parking lot manager and investigating the nature of this parking lot."

Sketch:

The already burned-out motorcycles were still emitting white smoke continuously. On-site, two or three men who were not the car owners voluntarily picked up buckets to continue putting out the fire. "We are just temporarily helping out at the parking lot," the men denied being employees of the parking lot. A woman responsible for collecting parking fees at the parking lot also expressed: "I'm just here for fun."

News Extension > >>

Insurance Company: Not within the scope of compensation Lawyer: The parking lot should bear responsibility

Wu Chengliang and Zhang Wenjie both indicated that their trucks had purchased insurance. Zhang Wenjie even called the insurance company staff, but disappointingly, after the staff inspected the scene, they said: "It's not a traffic accident, it's not within the scope of compensation."

The truck owners believed that the parking lot also bore certain responsibilities, and showed the reporter the parking lot's dedicated parking receipts, clearly marked with a parking fee of 40 yuan. Regarding this, the reporter consulted lawyer Sun Shunfa from Sichuan Haoda Law Firm, "Regardless of the reason for the fire, as long as the car owner paid the parking lot, the parking lot must bear corresponding responsibilities."
