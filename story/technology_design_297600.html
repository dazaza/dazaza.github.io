2009.01.19 (Shanzhai)

by bjseeprof on 2009-12-07 11:26:15

Tonight, a program on Phoenix TV is themed around "Shanzhai" culture. In the past half year, we've seen Shanzhai Spring Festival Gala, Shanzhai mobile phones, and Shanzhai laptops, all of which have been making shocking appearances.

The term "Shanzhai" was originally coined for domestic mobile phones from Huaqiangbei. Since the poverty alleviation-style alliance of the MTK platform, the entire mobile phone industry has set a precedent. Over the course of three bustling years, in 2008, some big shot came up with the elegant name: Shanzhai mobile phones. Perhaps because this word was so flavorful, it became an overnight sensation, widely applied across various industries under socialism. Then came the Shanzhai Spring Festival Gala, with rumors spreading online. Even if you haven't seen it, it's believed that in terms of entertainment, it won't be much worse than the official gala. After all, self-entertainment aligns more with public taste. We no longer want to sit neatly in front of the TV like we did ten years ago, waiting to be re-educated.

Some people say that Shanzhai always copies and never innovates. Well, wasn't it said by some great man that one should stand on the shoulders of giants... Meaning that logos and the shoulders of giants are meant to be stood upon, used as a foundation. Those fortunate shoulders that get stood upon should indeed feel honored.

These days, everyone talks about innovation daily, but is innovation really that easy? Can we expect someone to pick up a shoe and claim it's actually a moustache shaver like Stephen Chow did? No matter how much innovation happens, at home, our TVs, washing machines, and refrigerators remain separate entities, never fusing together into one.

Regardless of how much Chinese people love copying and disregarding intellectual property rights, over these many years, foreign products have also been copied back and forth. These days, what is truly original? The iPhone may be innovative, but it's still just a phone; it can't turn into an airplane.

One crucial aspect of Shanzhai culture is its deep connection with the hearts of the masses, providing whatever they desire. As for highbrow culture, that's not something ordinary people can easily perceive. Take iOrgane, which at a price of over 800 RMB and with high quality, completely overturned the iPhone's pricing system of several thousand RMB. Numerous dual-SIM dual-standby phones have already satisfied the public's need for two SIM cards for two years, while the brand giants of highbrow culture have only recently launched a few models, priced nearly ten times higher than Shanzhai phones.

Thus, from the bottom of my heart, I genuinely support Shanzhai culture with all four limbs, hoping it becomes increasingly powerful, striking you with awe. Only when it grows stronger will its quality and service improve further. Why should we let foreign giants change the soup but not the medicine, altering designs slightly to make huge profits?

Let Shanzhai phones and other forms of Shanzhai come even more fiercely!!!
