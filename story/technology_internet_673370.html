How should patients with anal fistulas eat after surgery? This is a common question in the minds of many patients and their families. Regarding this issue, experts from the Nanjing Armed Police Hospital's Anorectal Center state that the dietary habits of anal fistula patients after surgery are crucial for the complete cure of their condition. Therefore, patients should pay attention to the following three points after surgery:

1. Diet must be light

After surgery, anal fistula patients should consume more foods rich in protein and vitamins C and E. Additionally, it's important to drink plenty of water, eat fresh fruits and vegetables, and avoid spicy, stimulating foods and foods that cause heatiness, especially chili peppers and white liquor. In terms of diet, one should also minimize consumption of beef, mutton, and seafood. It’s also essential to maintain anal hygiene and ensure soft stools, which are critical for wound healing.

2. Eat more vitamin-rich foods

After surgery, anal fistula patients should consume more light foods rich in vitamins, such as mung beans, radishes, winter melon, and other fresh vegetables and fruits. Establishing a normal dietary habit is important because the occurrence of anal fistulas is related to damp-heat. For greasy diets, they can generate internal damp-heat, so it's not advisable to consume too much. For chronic non-healing anal fistulas, which are often due to deficiency symptoms, one should consume more protein-rich foods like lean meat, beef, and mushrooms.

3. Avoid greasy foods

After surgery, anal fistula patients should be cautious about consuming too many fried fast foods or foods that easily cause heatiness. Drinking plenty of water is important, and it’s recommended to eat fiber-rich grains and vegetables to regulate intestinal function. This enhances the coordination of intestinal motility and peristalsis, promotes contraction of the intestinal wall, regulates the microecology of the intestine, corrects intestinal dysfunction, and improves constipation while nurturing the intestines. Avoid eating stimulating foods like alcohol, chili peppers, and garlic, as these can easily cause damp-heat in the gastrointestinal tract, leading to a recurrence of the condition due to turbid qi and blood stagnation in the anus.

In addition, what health care tips should anal fistula patients pay attention to after surgery?

Patients should maintain smooth bowel movements after surgery, taking mild laxatives like paraffin oil or honey if necessary, and taking painkillers when in pain. Start sitting baths on the second day after surgery, performing them once in the morning, once in the evening, and after each bowel movement. After bathing, dry the area and apply 2% coptisine ointment. Visit the hospital every 5-7 days to tighten the thread until the rubber band falls off and the anal fistula opens. After the thread falls off, one can apply granulation powder or anti-inflammatory ointment locally, and the wound generally heals within about three weeks.

Experts at the Nanjing Armed Police Hospital's Anorectal Center remind patients that the primary principle of treating anal fistulas is clearing the internal opening, meaning that only surgical treatment can lead to recovery. Many people neglect dietary habits after surgery, resulting in a recurrence of the condition, which not only worsens the disease but also poses health risks.
