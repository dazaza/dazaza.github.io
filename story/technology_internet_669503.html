Fault Phenomenon: No warm air is blown out by the indoor unit when the system is in heating mode.

Analysis and Inspection: When starting the air conditioner in heating mode, KM1 in the control circuit is energized. Upon inspection, no internal wiring was found to be loose, indicating that the main circuit was functioning properly. The deformed air delivery grille was removed, and the electric heater EH was tested with a multimeter and found to be normal. However, the temperature fuses FT1~FT4 were all found to be open-circuited.

Repair Method: After replacing the temperature fuses, the air conditioner resumed heating operation.

Experience and Insights: During the use of an air conditioner, if the indoor fan is set at any speed (high, medium, or low) and there is no airflow from the outlet, the machine should be immediately shut down and a professional technician should be called for repair. Especially during summer cooling operations, it is strictly prohibited to run the machine while the fan is not turning, as this could damage equipment within the system and cause unnecessary fault escalation.

During heating operations, some air conditioners are equipped with thermal protectors near the electric heaters. Once the specified temperature is exceeded, the thermal protector will automatically cut off the power supply to the electric heater EH. After the temperature drops, the heating circuit will reconnect automatically. Some air conditioners have temperature fuses or other protective components installed near the electric heaters. When the temperature exceeds the specified value, they will melt to protect the air conditioner. Therefore, when the electric heater protection element of the air conditioner is damaged, different specifications or models of thermal protection elements should not be replaced arbitrarily, nor should wires be used to directly short-circuit them as substitutes. Instead, identical specification elements must be used for replacement.

Additionally, the installation position and height distance of the internal thermal protection element should not be altered arbitrarily to avoid adverse consequences.
