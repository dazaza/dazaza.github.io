The online anti-counterfeiting group launches a public welfare plan to educate the whole country in online shopping.

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:13:58

On the afternoon of December 20, 2009, a civilian online anti-counterfeiting group will hold a press conference at the Jin Waitan Hotel in Shanghai. At the same time, the first phase of the National Online Shopping Education Plan titled "Teach You to Distinguish Between Genuine and Fake Goods, Smart and Happy Shopping Public Welfare Forum" will also be launched. At that time, lawyers from the E-commerce Committee of the Shanghai Bar Association, doctoral students from East China University of Political Science and Law, brand enterprise managers, e-commerce fraud prevention experts, and netizen volunteers will gather together to discuss how to stay vigilant and identify fake goods while enjoying the convenience brought by online shopping.

According to Huang Xiangru, the leader of the online anti-counterfeiting group, the national online shopping education plan includes three steps:

1. Distribute to netizens the self-compiled "National Online Shopping Anti-counterfeit Fraud Prevention Handbook" by the online anti-counterfeiting group. The handbook involves teaching people how to search for and filter online stores, transaction communication skills, as well as complaint channels if they buy counterfeit goods.

2. Establish an online anti-counterfeiting expert forum (www.cheatbbs.net) and a counterfeiting report email (dajiatuan@sohu.com), inviting experts such as Gao Bingtian and Shang Jian Gang to answer netizens' questions about online shopping through MSN, QQ, and forum formats, as well as provide legal consultation assistance.

3. Encourage netizens to forward various types of "anti-counterfeit fraud prevention" science popularization articles, so more netizens can understand fraud prevention knowledge and stay away from counterfeit goods.

He stated that he hopes more netizens will take spontaneous action, learn anti-counterfeit fraud prevention techniques, and learn to protect their rights. Therefore, he hopes that consumers who have bought counterfeit goods online and want to complain will actively participate in this event.
