Online fake reviews keep upgrading, gray interest chain emerges

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-08-31 16:34:01

Last week, the Fengtai Court heard a case involving a wedding celebration company suing Dazhong Dianping (Mass Pointing Review). The case was triggered by a negative review on the website. With the development of the internet and the growth of Dazhong Dianping, reviews increasingly influence people's consumption choices. A single bad review could lead to a decline in business for merchants; conversely, a good review might bring them a flood of wealth. Investigations by reporters revealed that some veteran members' reviews on review sites have become tools for merchants to enhance their credibility. According to insiders, on Dazhong Dianping, gray-market intermediaries pay 10 yuan for veteran members to post one fake review.

Gray-market Intermediaries Offer Prices for Fake Reviews

Netizen Vivian is a four-star member of Dazhong Dianping. Yesterday, Vivian disclosed to reporters that a user named linglinganna claiming to be part-time for Dianping appeared in her Dianping QQ group, stating they could easily earn hundreds of yuan.

After inquiry, she found out that this person was actually a gray-market intermediary for a certain merchant, specializing in seeking out star-rated users on Dianping to write false positive reviews, or what is known as "stirring up reviews."

According to Vivian, participating part-time reviewers only need to go to the designated merchant page on Dianping and leave a review. Although there are no explicit requirements for the content, it must be positive with a rating of four stars or above. Additionally, only two-star members or higher on Dianping can participate. Vivian said that once such a review passes the audit, the money can be collected, priced at 10 yuan per review.

Reporters contacted linglinganna and learned that these stirred-up reviews generally have no format restrictions, but the word count cannot be too short; wage settlements are flexible and can be deposited into the reviewer's Alipay or bank account.

In principle, there is a limit of two drafts per day. I will give you the URL and some brief introductions about the store, allowing members to create freely, but reviews from the same username, identical IP addresses, or repeated reviews will not be accepted," she said.

Fake Reviews Continuously Upgrade

In fact, due to the lack of thresholds for online reviews, this kind of 'internet water army'-style fake review has existed for a long time.

Dazhong Dianping has made considerable efforts to combat stirred-up reviews. When reporters opened a merchant page on Dianping, they discovered that user reviews were categorized into all reviews, default reviews, friend reviews, and group-buying reviews. According to explanations from Dianping, default reviews are filtered through Dianping's newly developed review integrity system to screen netizens' reviews.

Default reviews filter out new-user reviews and sock puppet accounts, playing a significant role in curbing general water army behavior. Reporters randomly opened the merchant review page for Yizuo Yiwang Lijiang-themed restaurant and found that the total number of reviews was 3,358, while the number of default reviews was only 1,744, with a filtering ratio reaching 48%.
