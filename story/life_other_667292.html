I love you but I can't love you, do you know?

by huiming on 2012-02-16 17:15:05

I love you but cannot be with you, do you know that?

I love you but cannot be with you, my heart feels like it's bleeding, yet you don't know. Perhaps you simply don't want to know!

I am a woman who has been hurt, once having my own warm family. I never thought there would come a day when I would leave the home I cherished so much, but I was still driven out by my husband. With longing for my home and guilt towards my child, I left. In those few days, I felt as if I had aged ten years. I couldn't believe why feelings could be so fragile?

Even more, I couldn't believe why my usually honest husband could be so heartless. I didn't want to leave home, but the undeniable fact of divorce forced me to go. At that time, I was so desperate, so helpless, as if my entire world turned dark!

I gave my husband two years hoping he would change his mind, but when he handed our son over to me, I was utterly devastated. I told myself it was time to let go. Looking at my adorable son, my heart was filled with such happiness. It wasn't until two years after the divorce that I realized what I truly couldn't let go of was my son, not the husband who hurt me the most!

Time passed quickly, and my mood gradually became happier. However, the idea of finding another husband never changed. I was afraid, truly afraid. I gradually lost confidence in men. Upon reflection, I realized how irresponsible I had been to myself. From childhood to adulthood, I hadn't truly loved anyone. Even if I did, it was love born from being moved, not deep, unforgettable love. I always thought finding someone good to me was enough. The meticulous care of my former husband moved me to marry him, but the "seven-year itch" made us rush apart. So, I began no longer trusting men.

In life, I face all difficulties alone, but watching my child grow every day warms my heart. I deeply understand what it means to raise a child. Maybe I will spend the rest of my life alone, but I remain optimistic. Almost everyone sees my smile; I believe heaven will see it too.

Until that day, I met him: a man I only knew superficially. Only then did I believe in love at first sight. I never believed in this concept before, thinking it was something intangible, but when I saw him, I couldn't control my emotions. I met him by chance when I sought help for a problem, introduced by someone else.

When he learned about my situation, he showed great concern. After a few phone calls, he invited me to his office to discuss the matter. I gladly accepted. When I arrived, he was sitting quietly. Seeing me, he immediately got up and prepared fine tea for me. His manner of drinking tea was very particular. For some reason, I felt incredibly nervous. We talked about marriage and life. I knew he was very tired, with many troubles. We seemed to have much in common. During our conversation, I often stole glances at him. When I saw his small eyes, I found him incredibly cute.

When I saw his slightly upturned corners of his mouth when he smiled, I found him incredibly charming. When I listened carefully to his voice, I found it incredibly magnetic. I stopped myself from thinking further because I knew I shouldn't. He is a few years older than me. He said he had no sister and asked me to call him "Brother." I agreed. I was grateful for his help and happy to have a brother-like figure to confide in.

For a while afterward, he called me every day. If he missed a day, I felt extremely disheartened. From then on, I changed: I started dressing up and seriously losing weight. In just one month, I lost a full ten pounds! In my heart, this feeling was beautiful, until one day he asked me to accompany him. I refused. Perhaps I overthought it. I told him that during our time together, I had developed feelings for him. I knew I shouldn't act this way. I didn't want to hurt another woman or play games with life. Gradually, he mentioned it a few times and then stopped. In the following days, I sent him text messages, hoping he would be well, but he always told me I didn't understand him. Later, when I sent messages, I received no reply.

After just one meeting, he was deeply etched in my heart. These days, I think of him every day. I try hard to control myself. I deleted his number but can't delete the memories. On my computer, I check every day if he is online. What's wrong with me? Why am I acting so childish at my age?

Nowadays, we occasionally talk on the phone for certain matters. He tells me what to say and what not to say, but his attitude is always cold. Every day, I hope to receive his call, but I also fear it. I'm afraid of his complaints and his indifference. I once vowed never to find another partner, but since I met him, I've never forgotten him.

In my heart, I silently call out to Brother Hu. Do you know how I feel? Can you understand the feelings of the person who says they like you but refuses to meet you? Brother Hu, I really like you. I know I don't have the right to say this anymore, nor should I!

I dare not interact with you anymore because I like you so much. I'm afraid I'll fall too deep, hurting not only myself but also another woman. In my heart, I know you may not lack anything. I just want you to be well, to treat your family well. I believe you can do that because I sense you are a good man. I'm just afraid of loving you too much because from the moment I saw you, my world changed! That's why I refuse to see you again, even though I think of you every moment in my heart!

Brother Hu, do you know? These days, I cry every day. Every time I hear your voice, it feels like my heart is being cut by a knife. Your coldness makes me too scared to speak, yet you continue to help me. But I no longer dare to call you because I fear disturbing you, I fear your indifference!

Maybe you misunderstood me, but I don't want to explain too much. I hope your coldness will make me stop thinking about you. I hope that no longer contacting you will make me forget you!

I truly love you, but I don't know why I feel this way. I always feel you are my sixth sense in life, but I don't dare to love you because I'm unworthy. I don't want to hurt anyone. Because of love, I swear to walk away silently, despite all the desires and sorrows. I still choose to do so because I love you, which is why I leave you, fearing that no one can bear the pain of getting hurt!

Brother Hu, will you remember, years from now, that there was someone who liked you at first sight? Will you know the tears I shed for you?

I am just like the song says: I am a small leaf that only gains freedom when blown by the wind. Wherever the wind blows, I drift. Day after day, year after year, I don't look back. I am a small leaf, always drifting with the wind. Wherever the wind stops, I lie on the ground. Day after day, year after year, I don't look back. The north wind blows and breaks my heart. Drifting with the wind has made me so weary!

I know you won't love me because I'm unworthy!

Brother Hu, I love you but cannot be with you, do you know that?
